Word: νηστευομεν

Etymology:
The word νηστευομεν (nesteuomen) is a present participle form of the Greek verb νηστεύω (nesteuo), which means "to fast" or "to abstain from food". The word is derived from the noun νηστεία (nesteia), which means "fasting" or "abstinence from food".

Grammar:
νηστευομεν is a present participle form of the verb νηστεύω (nesteuo), which is in the present tense and active voice. The word is in the first person plural form, indicating that the subject is "we". The word is also in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently.

Description:
νηστευομεν is a Greek word that means "we are fasting" or "we are abstaining from food". The word is often used in religious contexts, particularly in Christianity, where fasting is a common practice. The word can also be used more generally to describe any period of abstinence from food or other indulgences.
