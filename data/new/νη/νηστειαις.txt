Word: νηστειαις

Etymology:
The word "νηστειαις" is derived from the Greek word "νηστεία" (nēsteía), which means "fasting" or "abstinence from food". The suffix "-αις" indicates that the word is in the dative plural form.

Grammar:
νηστειαις is a feminine noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
νηστειαις refers to a state of fasting or abstinence from food. It can be used to describe a period of time during which one refrains from eating, such as during religious observances or as a form of self-discipline. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a period of deprivation or hardship.
