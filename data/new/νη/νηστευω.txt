Word: νηστεύω

Etymology:
The word νηστεύω (nēsteuō) comes from the Greek root word νηστεία (nēsteia), which means "fasting" or "abstinence from food." The verb form νηστεύω means "to fast" or "to abstain from food."

Grammar:
νηστεύω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: νηστεύω (nēsteuō) - I fast
- Plural: νηστεύομεν (nēsteuomen) - we fast
- Singular: νηστεύεις (nēsteueis) - you fast
- Plural: νηστεύετε (nēsteuete) - you all fast
- Singular: νηστεύει (nēsteuei) - he/she/it fasts
- Plural: νηστεύουσι(ν) (nēsteuousi(n)) - they fast

Description:
νηστεύω is a verb that describes the act of abstaining from food for a period of time, usually for religious or spiritual reasons. It is a common practice in many religions, including Christianity, Islam, and Judaism. Fasting is often seen as a way to purify the body and mind, and to demonstrate devotion and discipline. In addition to religious fasting, some people also fast for health reasons, such as to detoxify the body or to lose weight.
