Word: νηστευσας

Etymology:
The word νηστευσας (nesteusas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word νηστεία (nesteia), which means "fasting" or "abstinence from food." The verb form means "to fast" or "to abstain from food."

Grammar:
νηστευσας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed from the root word νηστεία (nesteia) and the suffix -σας (-sas), which is the second-person singular aorist active indicative ending. The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
νηστευσας is a verb that means "you fasted" or "you abstained from food." It is used to describe a specific action that someone took in the past. The word is often used in a religious context to describe a period of fasting or abstinence from food as a form of spiritual discipline.
