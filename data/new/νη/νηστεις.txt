The word "νηστεις" (nēsteis) is a Greek noun that means "fasters" or "those who fast." It is derived from the verb "νηστεύω" (nēsteuō), which means "to fast" or "to abstain from food." In Greek Orthodox Christianity, fasting is an important spiritual practice, and "νηστεις" refers to those who observe the fasts prescribed by the church. 

In terms of grammar, "νηστεις" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It can also be spelled as "νηστείς" with an accent on the last syllable. 

Overall, "νηστεις" is a word that is closely associated with Greek Orthodox religious practices and has a specific cultural and historical context.
