Word: νηστευσουσιν

Etymology:
The word νηστευσουσιν (nēsteusousin) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word νηστεία (nēsteia), which means "fasting" or "abstinence from food." The verb form means "they will fast" or "they will abstain from food."

Grammar:
νηστευσουσιν is a third-person plural future active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σουσιν (-sousin) to the stem νηστευ- (nēsteu-). The verb is conjugated in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of fasting.

Description:
νηστευσουσιν is a verb that describes the act of fasting or abstaining from food. It is often used in a religious context, particularly in Christianity, where fasting is a common practice during certain times of the year. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any period of abstinence or self-denial.
