The word "νηστειων" (nesteion) is a Greek noun that means "fasting" or "abstinence from food". It is derived from the verb "νηστεύω" (nesteuo), which means "to fast" or "to abstain from food". In Greek Orthodox Christianity, fasting is an important spiritual practice, and "νηστειων" is often used to refer to the fasting periods throughout the year. 

In terms of grammar, "νηστειων" is a third declension noun in the genitive case. It can also be spelled as "νηστείων" with an accent on the last syllable. 

Overall, "νηστειων" is a word that is closely associated with the practice of fasting in Greek Orthodox Christianity.
