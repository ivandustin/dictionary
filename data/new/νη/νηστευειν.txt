Word: νηστεύειν

Etymology:
The word νηστεύειν (nēsteúein) comes from the Greek root word νηστεία (nēsteía), which means "fasting" or "abstinence from food." The verb form νηστεύειν means "to fast" or "to abstain from food."

Grammar:
νηστεύειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the present tense ending -ειν to the stem νηστευ-. The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of fasting.

Description:
νηστεύειν is a common word in Greek Orthodox Christianity, where fasting is an important spiritual practice. It is also used in medical contexts to refer to fasting for health reasons. The word can be used in a literal sense to describe abstaining from food, or in a figurative sense to describe abstaining from other things, such as social media or television.
