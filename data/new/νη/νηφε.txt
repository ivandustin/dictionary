In Greek mythology, the god Dionysus was often associated with drunkenness and excess, so being "nepho" was seen as a virtue and a way to resist his influence. 

In terms of grammar, "νηφε" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "νήφω". This means that it is a command directed at one person, telling them to be sober or clear-headed. 

Overall, "νηφε" is a word that emphasizes the importance of being sober and level-headed, especially in the face of temptation or excess.
