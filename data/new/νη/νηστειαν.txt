The word "νηστειαν" (nēsteian) is a Greek noun that means "fasting" or "abstinence from food". It is derived from the verb "νηστεύω" (nēsteuō), which means "to fast" or "to abstain from food". In Greek Orthodox Christianity, fasting is an important spiritual practice, and "νηστειαν" is often used to refer to the period of fasting before Easter. 

In terms of grammar, "νηστειαν" is a third declension feminine noun. Its nominative singular form is "νηστεία" (nēsteia), and it can also be declined in the genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative cases. 

Overall, "νηστειαν" is a word that is closely associated with Greek Orthodox religious practices and has a specific meaning related to fasting and abstinence from food.
