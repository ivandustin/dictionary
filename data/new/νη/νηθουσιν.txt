The word "νηθουσιν" (nēthousin) is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form, in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the root "νηθ-" (nēth-), which means "to spin" or "to twist". 

Grammar: The verb "νηθουσιν" is in the present tense, which indicates that the action is happening currently. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things. 

Description: The word "νηθουσιν" can be translated to mean "they spin" or "they twist". It is often used in the context of spinning or twisting fibers to make thread or yarn. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where something is being spun or twisted, such as a story or a web of lies.
