Etymology: The word "μυλικος" (mýlikos) comes from the Greek word "μυλος" (mýlos), meaning "mill". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *melh₂- meaning "to grind".

Grammar: "Μυλικος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description: "Μυλικος" is used to describe something that is related to or associated with a mill. It can refer to the mill itself, the process of grinding, or anything else connected to milling.
