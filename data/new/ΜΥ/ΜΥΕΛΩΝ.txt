Etymology: The word "μυελών" (myelon) comes from the Greek word "μυελός" (myelos), meaning "marrow" or "spinal cord".

Grammar: "Μυελών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description: "Μυελών" refers to the marrow, which is the soft, fatty tissue found inside bones. It is responsible for producing blood cells and storing fat. Marrow can be found in various types, such as red marrow (responsible for blood cell production) and yellow marrow (composed mainly of fat cells).
