Etymology: The word "μυωπαζων" comes from the Greek word "μυωπάζων" (myōpazōn), which is derived from "μύωψ" (myōps) meaning "short-sighted" or "nearsighted".

Grammar: "μυωπαζων" is a noun in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case in Greek.

Description: "μυωπαζων" refers to a person who is nearsighted or has myopia, a condition where distant objects appear blurry. It is used to describe someone who has difficulty seeing things clearly at a distance.
