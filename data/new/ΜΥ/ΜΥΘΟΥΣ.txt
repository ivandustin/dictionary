Etymology: The word "μυθος" (mythos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb "μυθεύω" (mytheuō), which means "to tell a story" or "to narrate." 

Grammar: In Greek, "μυθος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form and a plural form. The plural form is "μύθοι" (mythoi). 

Description: "Μυθος" refers to a traditional story or a myth. It can also mean a fable, legend, or tale. In ancient times, myths were used to explain natural phenomena, customs, and beliefs. They often involved gods, heroes, and supernatural beings. Myths were passed down orally from generation to generation and played a significant role in ancient Greek culture and religion.
