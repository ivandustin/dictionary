Etymology: The word "μυριων" (myrion) comes from the Greek word "μύριος" (myrios), meaning "countless" or "innumerable".

Grammar: "Μυριων" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description: "Μυριων" refers to a large number or a multitude of things. It is used to describe an extremely large quantity or an indefinite number that is too great to be counted.
