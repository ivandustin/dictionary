Etymology: The word "μυρίσαι" comes from the Greek verb "μυρίζω" (myrizo), which means "to smell" or "to emit a fragrance." 

Grammar: "Μυρίσαι" is the aorist tense, imperative mood, and second person singular form of the verb "μυρίζω" (myrizo). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to "smell" or "take a whiff."

Description: "Μυρίσαι" is a Greek word that means "smell" or "take a whiff." It is used to instruct someone to use their sense of smell to perceive a fragrance or odor.
