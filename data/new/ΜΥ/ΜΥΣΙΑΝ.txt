Etymology: The word "μυσιαν" comes from the Greek word "μυσία" (mysia), which means "defilement" or "pollution". 

Grammar: "μυσιαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. 

Description: "μυσιαν" refers to the act of defiling or polluting something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a stain or blemish on someone's character or reputation.
