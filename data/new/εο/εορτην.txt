The word "εορτην" (eorten) is a noun in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "ἑορτή" (heortē), which means "festival" or "holiday".

Grammar: "Εορτην" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Description: The word refers to a celebration or festival, typically a religious one. It can also refer to a holiday or a day of observance.
