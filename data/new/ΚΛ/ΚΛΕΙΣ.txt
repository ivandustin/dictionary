Etymology: The word "κλεις" (klēis) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *klei- meaning "to close" or "to lock."

Grammar: In Greek, "κλεις" (klēis) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form of "κλειδος" (kleidos).

Description: "Κλεις" (klēis) means "key" in Greek. It refers to a small metal or wooden device used to unlock or lock doors, gates, or other mechanisms. Keys have been used for centuries as a means of securing and controlling access to various spaces or objects.
