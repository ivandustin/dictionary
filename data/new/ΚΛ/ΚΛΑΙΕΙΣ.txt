Etymology: The word "κλαίεις" (kláeis) comes from the Greek verb "κλαίω" (kláio), which means "to cry" or "to weep". 

Grammar: "Κλαίεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense in Greek. It is used when referring to one person who is crying or weeping.

Description: "Κλαίεις" is a word in the Greek language that describes the action of crying or weeping. It is used to express the act of shedding tears due to sadness, pain, or any other strong emotion.
