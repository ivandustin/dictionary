Etymology: The word "κλαίν" (klain) comes from the Greek verb "κλαίω" (klaio), which means "to weep" or "to cry." 

Grammar: "Κλαίν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Κλαίνω (klaino) - I weep
- Κλαίνεις (klainis) - You weep
- Κλαίνει (klaini) - He/she/it weeps
- Κλαίνουμε (klainoume) - We weep
- Κλαίνετε (klainete) - You (plural) weep
- Κλαίνουν (klainoun) - They weep

Description: "Κλαίν" is a verb that describes the action of weeping or crying. It is used to express sadness, grief, or strong emotions.
