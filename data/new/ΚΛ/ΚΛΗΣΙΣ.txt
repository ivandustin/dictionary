Etymology: The word "κλησις" comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-.

Grammar: "Κλησις" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "κλήσεως" (kléseos). In the plural, it becomes "κλήσεις" (kléseis).

Description: In Greek, "κλησις" refers to a call, summons, or invitation. It can also denote a vocation or a divine calling. In a religious context, it can refer to a divine invitation to serve in a particular role or function.
