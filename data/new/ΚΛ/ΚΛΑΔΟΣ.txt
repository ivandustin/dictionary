Etymology: The word "κλαδος" (klados) comes from the Greek word "κλάω" (klao), which means "to break" or "to split." It is related to the Latin word "clades," meaning "branch" or "twig."

Grammar: In Greek, "κλαδος" (klados) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case, and its genitive form is "κλάδου" (kladou).

Description: "Κλαδος" (klados) refers to a branch or a twig of a tree. It is a part of a tree that grows out from the trunk or a larger branch and bears leaves, flowers, or fruits. Branches play a crucial role in the overall structure and growth of a tree, as they provide support for leaves and help distribute nutrients and water throughout the tree.
