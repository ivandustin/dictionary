Etymology: The word "κλαιουσαν" (klaiousan) comes from the Greek verb "κλαίω" (klaio), which means "to weep" or "to cry". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *klei-, meaning "to lament" or "to cry out".

Grammar: "κλαιουσαν" is the present participle form of the verb "κλαίω" (klaio) in the third person singular feminine. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The feminine form indicates that the subject of the verb is a female.

Description: "κλαιουσαν" describes a female subject who is currently weeping or crying. It can be used as a standalone word or as part of a sentence to convey the action of crying by a female.
