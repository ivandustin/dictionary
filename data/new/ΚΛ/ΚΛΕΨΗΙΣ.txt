Etymology: The word "κλεψης" (klépsis) comes from the Greek verb "κλέπτω" (kléptō), which means "to steal". 

Grammar: "Κλεψης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender. 

Description: "Κλεψης" refers to a thief or a person who steals. It is used to describe someone who takes someone else's property without permission or unlawfully.
