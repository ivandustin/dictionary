Etymology: The word "κληθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-.

Grammar: "κληθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo) in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "κληθηναι" is used to express the action of being called or summoned in the past. It is often used in Greek to describe events or actions that have already taken place.
