Etymology: The word "κληρονομος" (klēronomos) comes from the Greek roots "κληρος" (klēros) meaning "inheritance" and "νομος" (nomos) meaning "law". Therefore, "κληρονομος" can be translated as "one who receives an inheritance" or "heir".

Grammar: In Greek, "κληρονομος" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: κληρονομος (klēronomos)
- Genitive: κληρονομου (klēronomou)
- Accusative: κληρονομο (klēronomo)
- Vocative: κληρονομε (klēronome)

Plural:
- Nominative: κληρονομοι (klēronomoi)
- Genitive: κληρονομων (klēronomōn)
- Accusative: κληρονομους (klēronomous)
- Vocative: κληρονομοι (klēronomoi)

Description: "κληρονομος" refers to a person who inherits property, assets, or rights from a deceased relative or ancestor. It is commonly used in legal and inheritance contexts.
