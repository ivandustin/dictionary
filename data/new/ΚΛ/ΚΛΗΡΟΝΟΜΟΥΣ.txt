Etymology: The word "κληρονόμος" comes from the Greek roots "κληρο-" meaning "inheritance" and "-νόμος" meaning "law" or "rule". 

Grammar: In Greek, "κληρονόμος" is a noun. It is masculine in gender and singular in number. 

Description: "Κληρονόμος" refers to a person who inherits or is entitled to inherit property, assets, or rights from someone who has died. It is commonly used in legal and inheritance contexts.
