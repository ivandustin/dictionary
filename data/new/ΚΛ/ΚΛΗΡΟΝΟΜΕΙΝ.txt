Etymology: The word "κληρονομείν" comes from the Greek roots "κληρονόμος" (klēronómos), meaning "heir" or "inheritance," and "εἶναι" (eînai), meaning "to be."

Grammar: "κληρονομείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood.

Description: The word "κληρονομείν" means "to inherit" in English. It refers to the act of receiving property, rights, or qualities from a predecessor or ancestor.
