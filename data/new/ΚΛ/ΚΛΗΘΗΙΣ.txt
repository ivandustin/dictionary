Etymology: The word "κληθείς" (klētheis) comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleō), which means "to call" or "to summon". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-.

Grammar: "Κληθείς" is the aorist passive participle of the verb "καλέω" (kaleō). In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to modify nouns or function as verbs. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Κληθείς" is often translated as "having been called" or "having been summoned". It is used to describe someone who has received a call or summons from someone else. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in legal or religious contexts, to indicate that someone has been officially called or summoned to appear or perform a specific task.
