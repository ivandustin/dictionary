Etymology: The word "κλεπτης" (kleptēs) comes from the Greek verb "κλέπτω" (kléptō), which means "to steal". 

Grammar: "Κλεπτης" is a noun in the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension in Greek, and its nominative singular form is "κλεπτης". 

Description: "Κλεπτης" means "thief" in Greek. It refers to a person who steals or takes someone else's property without permission.
