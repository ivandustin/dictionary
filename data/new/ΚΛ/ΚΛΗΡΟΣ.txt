Etymology: The word "κληρος" comes from the Ancient Greek word "κλῆρος" (klêros), which means "lot" or "inheritance." It is derived from the verb "κληρόω" (klêroô), meaning "to assign by lot."

Grammar: In Greek, "κληρος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form ("κληρος") and a plural form ("κληροι").

Description: "Κληρος" is a term used in various contexts. In ancient Greece, it referred to a lot or portion of land that was assigned to an individual or a family through a process of drawing lots. It could also refer to an inheritance or a share of property. In a broader sense, it can be used to describe any kind of allocation or assignment by chance or fate.
