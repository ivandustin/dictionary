Etymology: The word "κληθεντος" comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to name." The suffix "-θεντος" (-thentos) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the action of calling or naming has been done to the subject.

Grammar: "κληθεντος" is the masculine singular form of the passive participle of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo). It is in the nominative case, indicating that it is the subject of a sentence or clause.

Description: "κληθεντος" can be translated as "having been called" or "having been named." It is used to describe something or someone that has received a name or a title.
