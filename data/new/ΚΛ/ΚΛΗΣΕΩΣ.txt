Etymology: The word "κλησεως" (klēseōs) comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleō), which means "to call" or "to summon."

Grammar: "Κλησεως" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Κλησεως" refers to the act of calling or summoning. It can also be translated as "invitation" or "summons."
