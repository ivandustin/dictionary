τυφλούς (typhlous) is a Greek adjective that means "blind". It is derived from the Greek word τυφλός (typhlos), which also means "blind". In terms of grammar, τυφλούς is in the accusative case and is used to describe a noun that is the object of an action. For example, in the sentence "Ο Ιάσονας έβλεπε τους τυφλούς" (O Iasonas eblepe tous typhlous), which means "Jason saw the blind", τυφλούς describes the noun "blind" and is in the accusative case.
