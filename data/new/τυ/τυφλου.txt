τυφλός (typhlós) is a Greek adjective that means "blind". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "typh-" which means "smoke" or "mist". It is believed that the word "typhlós" originally referred to someone who was "smoked" or "blinded" by the sun or other bright light.

Grammar: τυφλός is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: τυφλός is a common word in Greek literature and mythology, often used to describe characters who are physically or metaphorically blind. In modern Greek, it is used to describe someone who is visually impaired.
