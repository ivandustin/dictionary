Etymology:
The word τυπτεσθαι comes from the Greek verb τύπτω (týpto), which means "to strike" or "to hit". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) indicates that the verb is in the middle or passive voice, meaning "to be struck" or "to be hit".

τυπτεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb τύπτω. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is the receiver of the action.

Description:
τυπτεσθαι is a verb that describes the action of being struck or hit. It can be used in various contexts, such as in physical combat, sports, or accidents. In Greek literature, it is often used metaphorically to describe being struck by emotions or ideas.
