The word "τυφλων" (typhlon) is a Greek adjective that means "blind" or "unable to see". It is derived from the Greek word "τυφλός" (typhlos), which also means "blind". In terms of grammar, "τυφλων" is in the genitive case and is used to modify a noun. For example, "τυφλών ανθρώπων" (typhlon anthropon) means "of blind people". Overall, "τυφλων" is a descriptive word used to indicate a lack of sight.
