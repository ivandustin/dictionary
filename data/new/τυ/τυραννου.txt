τυραννου (tyrannou) is a Greek word that can be translated to "tyrant" or "despot". It comes from the Greek word τύραννος (tyrannos), which originally referred to a ruler who seized power by force and ruled without legal authority. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to describe oppressive or cruel leaders. 

In terms of grammar, τυραννου is the genitive form of τύραννος. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship, and in this case, it indicates that the noun it modifies (such as a person's name) is in some way connected to or associated with a tyrant. 

Overall, τυραννου is a word that has a rich history and carries a strong meaning in Greek culture.
