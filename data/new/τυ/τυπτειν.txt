τυπτειν (typtein) is a Greek verb that means "to strike" or "to hit". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *tewp- which means "to beat" or "to strike". In terms of grammar, τυπτειν is a regular verb in the present tense, with the stem τυπτ- and the ending -ειν. It is conjugated in the active voice, and can take both direct and indirect objects. In the past tense, the stem changes to ἔτυψ- (etyps-) and the ending changes depending on the tense and voice. Overall, τυπτειν is a common and versatile verb in Greek, used in a variety of contexts to describe physical actions of striking or hitting.
