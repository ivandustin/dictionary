Word: τυπτοντες

Etymology: τυπτοντες is a present active participle of the Greek verb τυπτω (typtō), which means "to strike" or "to hit". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *tewp- which means "to beat" or "to strike".

Grammar: τυπτοντες is a masculine plural nominative present active participle in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently striking or hitting something.

Description: τυπτοντες can be translated to "those who are striking" or "those who are hitting". It is often used in Greek literature and texts to describe warriors or soldiers who are engaged in battle. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe anyone who is actively striking or hitting something.
