Etymology: The word "ηυρισκον" (pronounced "eef-ris-kon") comes from the Greek verb "εὑρίσκω" (pronounced "eef-ree-skoh"), which means "to find" or "to discover." 

Grammar: "ηυρισκον" is the present active participle form of the verb "εὑρίσκω" in Ancient Greek. It is in the masculine, singular, and nominative case. 

Description: "ηυρισκον" is used to describe someone or something that is currently finding or discovering. It can also be translated as "finding" or "discovering" in English.
