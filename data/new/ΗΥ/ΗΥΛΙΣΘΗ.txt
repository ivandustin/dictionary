Etymology: The word "ηυλισθη" (pronounced "eulisithi") comes from the Greek verb "ηυλίζομαι" (pronounced "eulizomai"), which means "to slide" or "to slip". 

Grammar: "ηυλισθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive form of the verb "ηυλίζομαι". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "ηυλισθη" describes an action that happened in the past, where the subject of the verb was sliding or slipping. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone who slipped and fell, or an object that slid down a slope.
