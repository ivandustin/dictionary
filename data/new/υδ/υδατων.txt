Word: υδατων

Etymology:
The word υδατων (udaton) is derived from the Greek word ύδωρ (udor) which means "water". The suffix -ων (-on) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to "waters" or "of waters".

Grammar:
υδατων (udaton) is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, υδατων (udaton) means "of waters" or "belonging to waters".

Description:
υδατων (udaton) is a word used in Greek to refer to "waters" or "bodies of water". It can be used to describe any type of water, such as rivers, lakes, oceans, or even rainwater. The genitive plural form υδατων (udaton) is often used in phrases such as "the waters of the sea" or "the waters of the river".
