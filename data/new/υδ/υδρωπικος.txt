Word: υδρωπικος

Etymology: The word υδρωπικος comes from the Greek words υδρο (hydro) meaning water and ωπικος (opikos) meaning related to or resembling. Therefore, υδρωπικος means related to or resembling water.

Grammar: υδρωπικος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined in different cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: υδρωπικος is used to describe something that is related to or resembles water. It can be used to describe physical characteristics of water, such as its color, texture, or movement, as well as things that are associated with water, such as aquatic animals or plants. It can also be used metaphorically to describe things that are fluid, adaptable, or constantly changing.
