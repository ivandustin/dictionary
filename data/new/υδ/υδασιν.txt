Word: υδασιν

Etymology:
The word υδασιν (hydasin) is a Greek word that comes from the root word υδωρ (hydor), which means "water". The suffix -ασιν (-asin) is a common suffix used in Greek to form adjectives.

Grammar:
υδασιν is a feminine singular adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe a feminine singular noun.

Description:
The word υδασιν (hydasin) means "watery" or "full of water". It is often used to describe things that are wet or moist. For example, it can be used to describe a wet sponge or a moist piece of cake.
