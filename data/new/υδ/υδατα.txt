Word: υδατα

Etymology: The word υδατα (udata) is derived from the Greek word ύδωρ (hydor), which means water. The plural form of ύδωρ is ύδατα (udata).

Grammar: υδατα is a neuter noun in Greek and is used in the plural form. It is declined as follows:

Singular: ύδωρ (hydor)
Plural: ύδατα (udata)

Description: υδατα refers to water in Greek. It is a common noun used in everyday language to describe any form of water, whether it is in a river, lake, or ocean. The word is often used in scientific contexts as well, such as in the study of hydrology or the analysis of water samples.
