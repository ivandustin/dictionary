Word: αχειροποίητον

Etymology:
The word αχειροποίητον comes from the Greek roots α- (a-), meaning "not," χείρ (cheir), meaning "hand," and ποιέω (poieo), meaning "to make." Therefore, the word literally means "not made by hand."

Grammar:
αχειροποίητον is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description:
The word αχειροποίητον is used to describe something that is not made by human hands, but rather is natural or created by some other means. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is not contrived or artificial, but rather is genuine or authentic.
