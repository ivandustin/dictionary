The word "αχαια" (achaea) is a feminine noun in Greek and refers to the region of Achaea in ancient Greece. The etymology of the word is uncertain, but it is believed to have originated from the name of the mythical hero Achaeus. In terms of grammar, it is declined in the first declension and has a genitive form of "αχαιας" (achaeas). The word can also be used as an adjective to describe things related to the region of Achaea.
