The word "αχειροποίητος" (acheiropoietos) is a Greek adjective that means "not made by human hands". It is derived from the words "α-" (a-), which means "not", "χείρ" (cheir), which means "hand", and "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make". The word is often used in religious contexts to describe images or objects that are believed to have been miraculously created without human intervention. In terms of grammar, "αχειροποίητος" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case.
