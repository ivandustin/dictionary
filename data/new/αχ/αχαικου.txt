The word "αχαικου" (achai̱kou) is a Greek adjective that means "Achaean" or "pertaining to the Achaeans". The Achaeans were an ancient Greek tribe that inhabited the region of Achaea in the northern Peloponnese. The word is derived from the name of the tribe, which in turn is believed to have been derived from the Greek word "αχος" (achos), meaning "pain" or "distress". In terms of grammar, "αχαικου" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship.
