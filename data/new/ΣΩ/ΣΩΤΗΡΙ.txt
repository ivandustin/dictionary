Etymology: The word "σωτηρι" (sōtēri) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to deliver."

Grammar: "Σωτηρι" is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. It is a masculine noun.

Description: "Σωτηρι" is a Greek word that means "savior" or "deliverer." It is often used to refer to Jesus Christ as the savior of humanity in Christian theology.
