Etymology: The word "σωτήρ" (sōtēr) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to deliver." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₁- ("to protect").

Grammar: In Greek, "σωτήρ" is a masculine noun of the third declension. It can be declined in various cases, numbers, and genders to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Σωτήρ" is a Greek word that means "savior" or "deliverer." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to a person or deity who saves or delivers others from harm or danger. In Christianity, it is commonly used to refer to Jesus Christ as the savior of humanity.
