Etymology: The word "σωσατω" (sōsatō) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue."

Grammar: "Σωσατω" is the third person singular imperative form of the verb "σῴζω" (sōzō). It is used to give a command or instruction for someone to save or rescue.

Description: "Σωσατω" is a Greek word that is used to express the command "Let him/her/it save" or "Let him/her/it rescue." It is often used in religious or moral contexts to urge someone to take action and save or rescue someone or something.
