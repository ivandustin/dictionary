Etymology: The word "σωιζεται" (sōizetai) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sṓzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue."

Grammar: "Σωιζεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "σῴζω" (sṓzō) in Greek. It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: "Σωιζεται" means "he/she/it is saved" or "he/she/it is being saved" in English. It is used to express the action of saving or rescuing someone or something.
