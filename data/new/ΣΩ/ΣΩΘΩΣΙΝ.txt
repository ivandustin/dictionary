Etymology: The word "σωθωσιν" (sōthōsin) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₂- ("to save, protect").

Grammar: "Σωθωσιν" is the third person plural, aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "σῴζω" (sōzō). In Greek grammar, the subjunctive mood is used to express possibility, potentiality, or doubt. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past.

Description: "Σωθωσιν" is a verb form that means "they might be saved" or "they could be rescued" in English. It implies a potential or hypothetical action of saving or rescuing in the past.
