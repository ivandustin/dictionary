Etymology: The word "σωτήριος" (sōtērios) comes from the Greek root "σωτήρ" (sōtēr), meaning "savior" or "deliverer."

Grammar: In Greek, "σωτήριος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Σωτήριος" is an adjective in Greek that means "saving" or "salvific." It is often used to describe something that brings salvation, deliverance, or rescue. It can also refer to something that is beneficial or advantageous.
