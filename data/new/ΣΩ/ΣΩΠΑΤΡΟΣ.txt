Etymology: The word "σωπατρος" (sōpatros) comes from the Greek roots "σῶς" (sōs) meaning "safe" or "sound" and "πατήρ" (patēr) meaning "father". It can be translated as "savior" or "deliverer".

Grammar: In Greek, "σωπατρος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "σωπατρου" (sōpatrou).

Description: "Σωπατρος" is a Greek word that refers to a person who saves or delivers others. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe a savior or a divine figure who rescues people from danger or harm.
