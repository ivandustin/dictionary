Etymology: The word "σωφρονως" (sophronos) comes from the Greek adjective "σώφρων" (sophron), which means "of sound mind" or "self-controlled." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "σώ" (so), meaning "safe" or "sound," and the noun "φρήν" (phrēn), meaning "mind" or "heart."

Grammar: "Σωφρονως" is an adverb in Greek. It is the adverbial form of the adjective "σώφρων" (sophron), which means "self-controlled" or "moderate." Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, and in this case, "σωφρονως" describes the manner in which an action is performed.

Description: "Σωφρονως" is used to describe an action that is done in a self-controlled, moderate, or balanced manner. It implies acting with wisdom, prudence, and restraint. This word is often associated with the concept of temperance and is used to express the idea of acting in a way that is reasonable and avoids excess or extremes.
