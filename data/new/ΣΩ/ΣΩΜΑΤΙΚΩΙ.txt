Etymology: The word "σωματικοί" (sōmatikoi) comes from the Greek word "σῶμα" (sōma), meaning "body". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *sṓmeh₁, which means "body" or "corpse".

Grammar: "Σωματικοί" is the plural form of the adjective "σωματικός" (sōmatikos) in Greek. It is used to describe things or people that are related to the body or physical aspects.

Description: "Σωματικοί" refers to individuals or things that are connected to the body or physical aspects. It can be used to describe physical activities, bodily functions, or anything related to the physical form.
