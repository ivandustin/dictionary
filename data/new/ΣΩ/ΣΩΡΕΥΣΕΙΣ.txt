Etymology: The word "σωρευσεις" (sōreusēs) comes from the Greek verb "σωρεύω" (sōreúō), which means "to accumulate" or "to gather together."

Grammar: "Σωρευσεις" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Σωρευσεις" refers to accumulations or gatherings of things. It can be used to describe physical or abstract collections, such as piles, heaps, or accumulations of objects, ideas, or data.
