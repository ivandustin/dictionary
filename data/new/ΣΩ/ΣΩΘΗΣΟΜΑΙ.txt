Etymology: The word "σωθήσομαι" (sōthēsomai) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₂- ("to hold firmly").

Grammar: "Σωθήσομαι" is a first-person singular future passive indicative form of the verb "σῴζω" (sōzō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action, and the indicative mood is used for statements of fact.

Description: "Σωθήσομαι" means "I will be saved" or "I will be rescued" in English. It implies that the subject will be delivered from danger or harm by someone or something else.
