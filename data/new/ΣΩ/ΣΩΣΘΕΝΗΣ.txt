Etymology: The word "σωσθενης" (sōsthenēs) comes from the Greek roots "σωζω" (sōzō), meaning "to save" or "to heal," and "θενος" (thenos), meaning "strength" or "power." Therefore, "σωσθενης" can be translated as "strong" or "healthy."

Grammar: In Greek, "σωσθενης" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Σωσθενης" is used to describe someone or something that is strong, healthy, or vigorous. It can refer to physical strength, mental fortitude, or overall well-being.
