Etymology: The word "σωσαί" (sōsai) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue."

Grammar: "Σωσαί" (sōsai) is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "σῴζω" (sōzō). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to save or rescue someone or something.

Description: "Σωσαί" (sōsai) is a word in the Greek language that is used to express the command "save" or "rescue." It is often used in situations where immediate action is required to prevent harm or danger.
