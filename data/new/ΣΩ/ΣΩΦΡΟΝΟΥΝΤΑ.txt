Etymology: The word "σωφρονούντα" comes from the Greek verb "σωφρονέω" (sophronéo), which means "to have a sound mind" or "to be of sound mind." It is derived from the combination of the words "σῶς" (sos), meaning "safe" or "sound," and "φρήν" (phrén), meaning "mind" or "heart."

Grammar: "Σωφρονούντα" is the present participle form of the verb "σωφρονέω" in the masculine gender, accusative case, and singular number. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description: "Σωφρονούντα" can be translated as "having a sound mind" or "being of sound mind." It describes someone who possesses mental clarity, self-control, and moderation in their thoughts, emotions, and actions. This word is often associated with wisdom, prudence, and rationality.
