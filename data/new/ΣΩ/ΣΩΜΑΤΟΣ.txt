Etymology: The word "σώματος" (sōmatos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "σῶμα" (sōma), meaning "body".

Grammar: "Σώματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Σώματος" refers to the body. It can be used to describe the physical body of a person or an animal. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the main part or substance of something.
