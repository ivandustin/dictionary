Etymology: The word "σωσθενην" comes from the Greek verb "σώζω" (sózo), which means "to save" or "to rescue."

Grammar: "σωσθενην" is the aorist passive participle form of the verb "σώζω" (sózo). It is in the masculine accusative singular form.

Description: "σωσθενην" is used to describe someone or something that has been saved or rescued. It indicates that the action of saving or rescuing has been done to the subject.
