Etymology: The word "σωθήναι" (sōthēnai) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₂- ("to hold firmly").

Grammar: "Σωθήναι" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "σῴζω" (sōzō) in Greek. The aorist tense generally expresses a completed action in the past, without specifying its duration or repetition. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for person or number.

Description: "Σωθήναι" is used to express the idea of "to be saved" or "to be rescued" in Greek. It can refer to physical salvation from danger or harm, as well as spiritual or moral salvation. The word carries a sense of deliverance or liberation from a negative or threatening situation.
