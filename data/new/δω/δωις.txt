The word "δωις" (dōis) is a Greek noun that means "gift" or "present". It is derived from the verb "δίδωμι" (dídōmi), which means "to give". In terms of grammar, "δωις" is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases and numbers, such as the genitive singular "δώρου" (dṓrou) or the accusative plural "δῶρα" (dōra). Overall, "δωις" is a common word in Greek literature and is often used to describe offerings or presents given to gods or important individuals.
