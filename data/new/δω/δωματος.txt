The word "δωματος" (domatos) is a Greek noun that means "room" or "chamber". It is derived from the Greek word "δόμος" (domos), which means "house" or "building". 

In terms of grammar, "δωματος" is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It can be used to indicate possession or relationship, such as "the room of the king" (ὁ δωμάτων τοῦ βασιλέως).

In description, "δωματος" can refer to any enclosed space within a building, such as a bedroom, living room, or study. It can also be used in a metaphorical sense to refer to a person's inner thoughts or emotions, as in "the chambers of the heart".
