Word: δωσιν

Etymology: δωσιν is a present active subjunctive form of the Greek verb δίδωμι (dídōmi), which means "to give". The verb is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-, which means "to give".

Grammar: δωσιν is a third-person plural present active subjunctive form of the verb δίδωμι. In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations.

Description: δωσιν is a verb form that indicates the action of giving in the present tense. It is used in situations where the speaker is uncertain or doubtful about the action of giving. For example, "Είθε δωσιν τα δώρα τους στους φίλους τους." (May they give their gifts to their friends.)
