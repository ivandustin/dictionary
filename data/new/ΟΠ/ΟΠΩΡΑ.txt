Etymology: The word "ὁπωρα" (opōra) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ὁπώρα" (opṓra), which means "autumn" or "harvest time". It is derived from the combination of the words "ὀπός" (opós), meaning "fruit", and "ὥρα" (hṓra), meaning "time" or "season".

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁπωρα" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the first declension and has a singular form. The word can be declined to indicate different cases, numbers, and genders.

Description: "ὁπωρα" refers to the season of autumn or harvest time. It is often associated with the abundance of fruits and vegetables that are harvested during this time of the year. In Greek culture, autumn is considered a time of harvest and celebration of the bountiful crops.
