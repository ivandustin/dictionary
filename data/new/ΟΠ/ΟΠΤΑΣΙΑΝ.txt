Etymology: The word "οπτασιαν" comes from the Greek verb "οπτανομαι" (optanomai), which means "to see" or "to perceive". 

Grammar: "Οπτασιαν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. It is the first person singular form of the verb "οπτανομαι" (optanomai), which means "I see" or "I perceive".

Description: "Οπτασιαν" is used to express the action of seeing or perceiving something in the present tense. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing in the Greek language.
