Etymology: The word "οπως" (opos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the preposition "οπως" (opos), meaning "as" or "like," and the conjunction "ως" (os), meaning "that" or "how."

Grammar: "Οπως" (opos) is a conjunction in Greek grammar. It is used to introduce a subordinate clause that expresses a purpose, result, or comparison. It is often translated as "so that," "in order that," "that," or "as."

Description: "Οπως" (opos) is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to connect two clauses or sentences. It is used to indicate the purpose or result of an action, or to compare two things. It is an important conjunction that helps to express relationships between ideas in a sentence.
