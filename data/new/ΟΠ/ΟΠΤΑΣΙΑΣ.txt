Etymology: The word "ὀπτασίας" (optasías) comes from the Greek verb "ὀπτάνομαι" (optánomai), which means "to see" or "to behold." It is derived from the noun "ὄψ" (ops), meaning "eye" or "sight."

Grammar: "ὀπτασίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.
