Etymology: The word "οποιος" (opios) comes from the combination of the Greek words "οπος" (opos) meaning "which" and "εις" (eis) meaning "one". 

Grammar: "Οποιος" (opios) is a pronoun in the Greek language. It is used to refer to a person or thing in an indefinite way, similar to the English word "whoever" or "whichever". It is declined according to gender, number, and case.

Description: "Οποιος" (opios) is a versatile pronoun that can be used in various contexts. It is often used to express an indefinite choice or selection among a group of people or things. It can also be used to introduce a relative clause, indicating that the following information is related to a specific person or thing.
