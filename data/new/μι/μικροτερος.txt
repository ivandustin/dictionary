Word: μικρότερος

Etymology: μικρότερος is a Greek word that comes from the root word μικρός (mikrós), meaning "small" or "little". The suffix -τερος (-teros) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "less" than something else.

Grammar: μικρότερος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is the comparative form of μικρός, meaning "smaller" or "less big" than something else. It can also be used as a noun to refer to someone who is younger or smaller than someone else.

Description: μικρότερος is a word used to compare the size or age of two things or people. It can be used to describe physical size, age, or importance. For example, "Ο αδελφός μου είναι μικρότερος από εμένα" (My brother is younger than me) or "Η πόλη μας είναι μικρότερη από την Αθήνα" (Our city is smaller than Athens).
