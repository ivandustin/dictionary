Word: μισθωσασθαι

Etymology:
The word μισθωσασθαι comes from the Greek verb μισθόω (misthóō), which means "to hire" or "to rent". The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a middle voice ending, indicating that the action is being performed by the subject on themselves.

Grammar:
μισθωσασθαι is a verb in the aorist tense, middle voice, infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The infinitive mood is used to express the idea of the verb without specifying a subject or tense.

Description:
μισθωσασθαι means "to hire oneself out" or "to rent oneself". It is often used in the context of labor or services, where someone offers their skills or abilities in exchange for payment. The word can also be used in a broader sense to describe any situation where someone is selling their time or effort for compensation.
