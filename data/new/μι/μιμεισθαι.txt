Word: μιμεισθαι

Etymology:
The word μιμεισθαι is derived from the Greek verb μιμέομαι (mimeomai), which means "to imitate" or "to mimic". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) is a middle/passive voice ending, indicating that the action is being done to oneself or is being done passively.

Grammar:
μιμεισθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb μιμέομαι. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is the receiver of the action. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing or habitual.

Description:
μιμεισθαι means "to be imitated" or "to be mimicked". It is often used in the context of art, where an artist may imitate or mimic the style or techniques of another artist. It can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any kind of imitation or mimicry.
