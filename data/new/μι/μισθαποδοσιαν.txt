Word: μισθαποδοσιαν

Etymology: The word μισθαποδοσιαν comes from the Greek words μισθός (misthós) meaning "wages" or "payment" and ἀπόδοσις (apódosis) meaning "giving back" or "retribution". Together, they form the compound word μισθαποδοσιαν, which means "payment of wages" or "reward".

Grammar: μισθαποδοσιαν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. For example, in the sentence "Ο Πέτρος έλαβε μισθαποδοσιαν" (O Pétrοs élave misthapodosian), which means "Peter received his wages", μισθαποδοσιαν is the direct object of the verb έλαβε (élave).

Description: μισθαποδοσιαν refers to the payment of wages or reward for work done. It is often used in the context of employment or labor, where an employer pays their employees for their work. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of compensation or reward for a service rendered.
