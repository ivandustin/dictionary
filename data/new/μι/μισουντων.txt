Word: μισουντων

Etymology:
μισουντων is the genitive plural form of the Greek verb μισέω (miseo), which means "to hate" or "to detest". The verb comes from the noun μῖσος (misos), which means "hatred" or "enmity".

Grammar:
μισουντων is a third-person plural genitive form of the verb μισέω (miseo). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, μισουντων indicates that the subject of the sentence possesses or is related to something that is hated or detested.

Description:
μισουντων can be translated as "of those who hate" or "of those who detest". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a group of people who share a common feeling of hatred or enmity towards something or someone.
