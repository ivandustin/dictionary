Word: μισούντες

Etymology: μισούντες is the present participle of the Greek verb μισέω (miseo), which means "to hate". The word comes from the ancient Greek root μισ- (mis-), which means "hatred" or "dislike".

Grammar: μισούντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb μισέω (miseo), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description: μισούντες is a Greek word that means "hating" or "those who hate". It is often used to describe a group of people who have a strong dislike or animosity towards someone or something. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in literature, politics, or everyday conversation.
