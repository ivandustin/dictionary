Word: μικροι

Etymology:
μικροι is the plural form of the Greek adjective μικρός (mikrós), which means "small" or "little". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *smik- meaning "small".

Grammar:
μικροι is a masculine plural form of the adjective μικρός. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, μικροι is used to describe plural masculine nouns.

Description:
μικροι is used to describe a group of small or little things. It can also be used to describe people who are physically small in size or young in age. In Greek mythology, the term μικροί (mikroi) was used to refer to the lesser gods or minor deities.
