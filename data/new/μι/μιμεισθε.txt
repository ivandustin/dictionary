Word: μιμεισθε

Etymology:
The word μιμεισθε is derived from the Greek verb μιμέομαι (mimeomai), which means "to imitate" or "to mimic". The suffix -σθε (-sthe) is a second person plural ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and addressing a group of people.

Grammar:
μιμεισθε is a present tense, second person plural form of the verb μιμέομαι. It is a passive voice form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action. In this case, the subject is a group of people who are being imitated or mimicked.

Description:
μιμεισθε can be translated as "you are being imitated" or "you are being mimicked". It is often used in a negative context, implying that someone is being mocked or ridiculed. However, it can also be used in a positive context, indicating that someone is being admired or emulated. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and drama, where characters often imitate or mimic each other's actions and speech.
