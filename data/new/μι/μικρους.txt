Word: μικρούς

Etymology: μικρούς is a Greek word that comes from the root word μικρός (mikrós), meaning "small" or "little."

Grammar: μικρούς is the accusative plural form of the adjective μικρός (mikrós), which means "small" or "little." In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, μικρούς is used to describe plural masculine nouns in the accusative case.

Description: μικρούς is used to describe multiple small or little things in the accusative case. For example, "Οι μικρούς κήποι είναι όμορφοι" (Oi mikroús kípoi eínai ómorfoi) means "The small gardens are beautiful."
