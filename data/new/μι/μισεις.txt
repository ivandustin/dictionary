The word "μισεις" (misseis) is a verb in Greek that means "you hate" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "μισέω" (miseo), which means "to hate" or "to detest". 

Grammar: "μισεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "μισέω". It is conjugated as follows: 

- Εγώ μισώ (ego miso) - I hate 
- Εσύ μισείς (esi miseis) - You hate 
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό μισεί (aftos/afti/afto misei) - He/She/It hates 
- Εμείς μισούμε (emeis misoume) - We hate 
- Εσείς μισείτε (eseis miseite) - You hate (plural) 
- Αυτοί/Αυτές μισούν (aftoi/aftes misoun) - They hate 

Description: "μισεις" is a strong and negative emotion that refers to a feeling of intense dislike or hostility towards someone or something. It can be used to express a personal feeling or a general attitude towards a particular person, group, or thing.
