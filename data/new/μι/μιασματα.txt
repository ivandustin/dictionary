Word: μιασματα

Etymology: The word μιασματα comes from the Greek word μίασμα (miasma), which means "pollution" or "defilement". It is derived from the verb μιαίνω (miano), which means "to pollute" or "to defile".

Grammar: μιασματα is a plural noun in Greek. It is the plural form of μίασμα (miasma).

Description: μιασματα refers to things that are considered impure or contaminated. In ancient Greek culture, miasma was believed to be a spiritual pollution that could cause harm or misfortune. It was often associated with death, disease, and other forms of suffering. Today, the word μιασματα is still used in Greek to refer to things that are considered unclean or contaminated, such as polluted air or water.
