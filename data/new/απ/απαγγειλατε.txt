Word: απαγγειλατε

Etymology:
The word απαγγειλατε (apangelate) is derived from the Greek verb απαγγελλω (apangello), which means "to announce" or "to declare". It is formed by adding the suffix -ατε (-ate) to the imperative form of the verb.

Grammar:
απαγγειλατε is the second person plural imperative form of the verb απαγγελλω (apangello). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

The word απαγγειλατε is often used in the context of public speaking or formal announcements. It is a command to a group of people to make a declaration or announcement. The word can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to proclaim" or "to report".
