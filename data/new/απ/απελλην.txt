The word "απελλην" (apellēn) is a Greek verb that means "to call out" or "to summon". It is derived from the noun "απελλα" (apella), which referred to the assembly of citizens in ancient Greek city-states. The verb form is used in various contexts, such as calling out a name, summoning someone to appear in court, or inviting someone to a gathering. 

In terms of grammar, "απελλην" is a second-person singular present active imperative verb. This means that it is used to give a command or instruction to someone in the present tense. 

Overall, "απελλην" is a versatile verb that has been used in Greek language and culture for centuries.
