Word: αποστελλειν

Etymology:
The word αποστελλειν (apostellein) is derived from the Greek roots από (apo) meaning "away from" and στέλλω (stello) meaning "to send". Therefore, the word means "to send away".

Grammar:
αποστελλειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the third person singular form. The verb stem is αποστελ-, and the present tense suffix for the third person singular is -λλειν.

Description:
αποστελλειν is a Greek verb that means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is often used in the context of sending someone or something to a particular destination. The verb can also be used in a figurative sense, such as sending a message or sending someone on a mission. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the sending of Jesus Christ by God to save humanity.
