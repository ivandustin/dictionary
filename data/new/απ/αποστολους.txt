Word: αποστόλους

Etymology: The word αποστόλους (apostolous) comes from the Greek word απόστολος (apostolos), which means "messenger" or "envoy". In the New Testament, the word is used to refer to the twelve apostles chosen by Jesus Christ to spread his teachings.

Grammar: αποστόλους is a plural noun in the accusative case. It is the direct object of a sentence and refers to a group of messengers or envoys.

Description: αποστόλους is a Greek word that is commonly used in the context of Christianity. It refers to the twelve apostles chosen by Jesus Christ to spread his teachings and establish the Christian church. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to messengers or envoys in general.
