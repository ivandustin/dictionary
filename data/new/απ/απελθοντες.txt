Word: απελθοντες

Etymology:
The word απελθοντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απέρχομαι (apérchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart". The verb is in the present participle form, which means "going away" or "departing".

Grammar:
απελθοντες is a present participle verb in the Greek language. It is in the masculine plural form, which means it refers to a group of males who are currently departing or going away. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -οντες (-ontes) to the root verb απέρχομαι (apérchomai).

Description:
απελθοντες is a Greek verb that describes a group of males who are currently departing or going away. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of soldiers leaving a battlefield or a group of travelers leaving a city. The present participle form of the verb emphasizes the ongoing nature of the action, suggesting that the departure is currently taking place.
