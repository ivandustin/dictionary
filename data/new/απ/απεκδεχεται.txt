Word: απεκδεχεται

Etymology:
The word απεκδεχεται (apekdechetai) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb εκδεχομαι (ekdechomai), meaning "to wait for" or "to expect." Therefore, απεκδεχεται can be translated as "to await eagerly" or "to anticipate."

Grammar:
απεκδεχεται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb απεκδέχομαι (apekdechomai). It is a regular verb in the Greek language, and it belongs to the first conjugation group. The verb is in the indicative mood, which is used to make statements or ask questions.

Description:
απεκδεχεται is a verb that expresses the idea of eagerly waiting for something to happen. It can be used in various contexts, such as waiting for a person, an event, or a result. The verb implies a sense of anticipation and excitement, as the person who is waiting is looking forward to the outcome. The word can be used in both formal and informal settings, and it is commonly used in everyday conversations.
