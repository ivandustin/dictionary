Word: αποστελλω

Etymology:
The word αποστελλω comes from the Greek roots από (apo) meaning "away from" and στέλλω (stello) meaning "to send". Therefore, αποστελλω means "to send away".

Grammar:
αποστελλω is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is a regular verb. It is used in the present tense, past tense, and future tense, as well as in the imperative mood and the subjunctive mood.

Description:
αποστελλω means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is often used in the context of sending a message or a letter, but it can also refer to sending a person or an object. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the sending of messengers or apostles to spread the word of God.
