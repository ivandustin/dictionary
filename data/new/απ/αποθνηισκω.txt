Word: αποθνήσκω

Etymology: αποθνήσκω comes from the combination of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb θνήσκω (thnēskō), meaning "to die." Therefore, αποθνήσκω means "to die away from" or "to perish."

Grammar: αποθνήσκω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ αποθνήσκω (Egó apothnḗskō) - I die
- Εσύ αποθνήσκεις (Esý apothnḗskeis) - You die
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό αποθνήσκει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó apothnḗskei) - He/She/It dies
- Εμείς αποθνήσκουμε (Emeís apothnḗskoume) - We die
- Εσείς αποθνήσκετε (Eseís apothnḗskete) - You die
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά αποθνήσκουν (Aftoí/Aftés/Aftá apothnḗskoun) - They die

Description: αποθνήσκω is a verb that describes the act of dying or perishing. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to the death of a person or animal, or in a figurative sense, such as when referring to the end of a relationship or the failure of a business. The verb is commonly used in Greek literature and is an important part of the language's vocabulary.
