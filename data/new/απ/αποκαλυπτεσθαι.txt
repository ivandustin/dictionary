Word: αποκαλυπτεσθαι

Etymology: απο- (apo-) meaning "away from" and καλυπτω (kalupto) meaning "to cover". Together, αποκαλυπτεσθαι (apokalyptesthai) means "to uncover" or "to reveal".

Grammar: αποκαλυπτεσθαι is a Greek verb in the present passive infinitive form. It is derived from the root verb αποκαλυπτω (apokalypto) which means "to reveal" or "to disclose". The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: αποκαλυπτεσθαι is a verb that is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the act of revealing or uncovering something that was previously hidden or unknown. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of uncovering or revealing something that was previously concealed or unknown.
