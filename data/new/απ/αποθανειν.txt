Word: αποθανειν

Etymology: αποθανειν is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: από (apo) meaning "away from" and θανείν (thanein) meaning "to die". Therefore, αποθανειν means "to die away from" or "to die completely".

Grammar: αποθανειν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation. The verb stem is θανειν (thanein), and the prefix από (apo) is added to indicate the sense of "away from".

Description: αποθανειν is a strong and powerful word that refers to the act of dying completely or away from something. It can be used in a literal sense, to describe the physical death of a person or an animal, or in a figurative sense, to describe the end of something, such as a relationship or a project. The word carries a sense of finality and permanence, and it is often used in serious or solemn contexts.
