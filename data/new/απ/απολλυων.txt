The word "απολλυων" (apollyon) is a Greek noun that means "destroyer" or "the destroyer". It is derived from the verb "απόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". In Greek mythology, Apollyon was also the name of the Greek god of destruction and death. 

In terms of grammar, "απολλυων" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive "απολλυοντος" (apollyontos) or the accusative "απολλυοντα" (apollyonta). 

Overall, "απολλυων" is a powerful and ominous word that conveys a sense of destruction and chaos.
