The word "αποστολοις" (apostolois) is a Greek noun in the dative plural form. It is derived from the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". 

In the context of Christianity, "αποστολοις" refers to the apostles, the twelve disciples chosen by Jesus to spread his teachings and establish the Christian church. 

In terms of grammar, "αποστολοις" is a third declension noun, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. In the dative plural form, it indicates the indirect object of a verb, such as "to the apostles". 

Overall, "αποστολοις" is a significant word in Christian history and theology, representing the group of individuals who were instrumental in spreading the message of Jesus and establishing the early Christian church.
