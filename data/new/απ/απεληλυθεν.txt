Word: απεληλυθεν

Etymology:
απεληλυθεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb απέρχομαι (aperchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart". The word is formed by adding the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from", and the perfect tense ending -ηλυθεν (-eluthen).

Grammar:
απεληλυθεν is a third-person singular form of the verb απέρχομαι (aperchomai) in the perfect tense. The perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. In this case, απεληλυθεν describes someone who has gone away or departed and is still away.

Description:
απεληλυθεν can be translated as "has gone away" or "has departed". It is often used in a religious context to describe the ascension of Jesus Christ into heaven. It can also be used more generally to describe someone who has left or gone away from a place or situation.
