Word: απαντών

Etymology: απαντών is the genitive plural form of the Greek word απαντώ (apantó), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". The word is derived from the prefix α- (a-), which means "not" or "without", and the verb παντεύω (pantevo), which means "to go everywhere" or "to wander".

Grammar: απαντών is a masculine noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple objects or people being referred to.

Description: απαντών is often used in Greek literature and poetry to refer to a group of people who have gathered together or who are united in a common purpose. It can also be used to describe a meeting or encounter between two or more individuals.
