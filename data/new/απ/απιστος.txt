Word: απιστος

Etymology:
The word απιστος (apistos) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the word "pistos" meaning "faithful" or "trustworthy". Therefore, απιστος means "unfaithful" or "untrustworthy".

Grammar:
απιστος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. For example, απιστος can modify a masculine singular noun in the nominative case, such as "ο άντρας απιστος" (the unfaithful man).

Description:
απιστος is a word that describes someone or something that cannot be trusted or relied upon. It can refer to a person who is disloyal, deceitful, or unfaithful, or to something that is unreliable or untrustworthy. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, business dealings, or political situations.
