The word "απολωλως" is a Greek adverb that means "utterly, completely, entirely, wholly." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "ἀπόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy, to perish, to be lost." The adverb form "απολωλως" is formed by adding the suffix "-ως" (-ōs) to the past participle stem "απολωλ-" (apolōl-).

Grammar: "απολωλως" is an adverb that modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It does not change form based on gender, number, or case.

Description: The word "απολωλως" emphasizes the completeness or totality of an action or state. It can be used to express a sense of finality or irreversibility. For example, "He was utterly defeated" could be translated as "Ήταν απολωλως ηττημένος" (Itan apololos ittimenos) in Greek.
