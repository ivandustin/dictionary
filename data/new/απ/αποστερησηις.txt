Word: αποστερησηις

Etymology:
The word αποστερησηις (aposteresis) is derived from the Greek verb αποστερεω (apostereo), which means "to deprive" or "to take away". The suffix -ησηις (-esis) is a nominalizing suffix that is used to form nouns from verbs.

Grammar:
αποστερησηις is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word is pronounced as "apostereesis".

Description:
αποστερησηις refers to the act of depriving or taking away something from someone. It can also refer to the state of being deprived or the thing that has been taken away. The word is often used in legal contexts to describe the act of confiscating property or assets. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the feeling of loss or deprivation.
