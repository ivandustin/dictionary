Word: αποφθεγγεσθαι

Etymology:
The word αποφθεγγεσθαι comes from the Greek verb αποφθέγγομαι (apophthengomai), which means "to speak out, to utter, to declare". It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb φθέγγομαι (phthengomai), meaning "to utter, to speak".

αποφθεγγεσθαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive mood is used to express the idea of the verb without specifying any subject or tense.

Description:
αποφθεγγεσθαι means "to speak out, to utter, to declare" in Greek. It is a formal and literary verb that is often used in philosophical and rhetorical contexts.
