Word: απεκαλυφθη

Etymology:
The word απεκαλυφθη (apekalufthi) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words από (apo) meaning "from" and καλύπτω (kalupto) meaning "to cover". The word is derived from the ancient Greek word αποκάλυψις (apokalypsis) which means "uncovering" or "revelation".

Grammar:
απεκαλυφθη is a third person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb αποκαλύπτω (apokalupto) which means "to reveal" or "to uncover". The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb received the action.

Description:
The word απεκαλυφθη is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe a revelation or an unveiling of something hidden or unknown. It can also be used more generally to describe the act of revealing or uncovering something that was previously hidden or secret.
