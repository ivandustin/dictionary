Word: απεκατεσταθη

Etymology:
The word απεκατεσταθη is a Greek word that comes from the verb αποκαθιστάνω (apokathistano), which means "to restore" or "to re-establish". It is formed by the prefix απο- (apo), which means "away from" or "off", and the verb καθιστάνω (kathistano), which means "to set up" or "to establish".

απεκατεσταθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it is implied that someone or something was restored or re-established.

Description:
απεκατεσταθη is a powerful and meaningful word that conveys the idea of restoration and renewal.
