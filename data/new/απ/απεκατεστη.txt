Word: απεκατέστη (apokatesti)

Etymology: The word απεκατέστη comes from the Greek verb αποκαθιστώ (apokathistó), which means "to restore" or "to bring back to its original state". The word is formed by adding the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from" or "off", and the verb καθιστώ (kathistó), which means "to establish" or "to set up".

Grammar: απεκατέστη is a verb in the past tense, third person singular. It is formed by adding the augment α- (a-) to the stem κατέστη (katesti) and the suffix -ε (e) to indicate the past tense. The verb is transitive, meaning it takes a direct object.

Description: απεκατέστη is a word that describes the act of restoring or bringing something back to its original state. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as restoring a building, a relationship, or a political system. The word implies a sense of renewal and rejuvenation, as well as a return to a previous state of order or stability.
