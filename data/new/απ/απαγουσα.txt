Word: απαγουσα

Etymology: The word απαγουσα comes from the Greek verb απάγω (apago), which means "to lead away" or "to take away". The suffix -ούσα (-ousa) is a feminine participle ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to a female subject.

Grammar: απαγουσα is a present participle in the feminine singular form. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently leading or taking away something/someone.

Description: απαγουσα can be translated to English as "leading away" or "taking away". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a female figure who is leading or guiding someone or something. In modern Greek, it can also be used in a more literal sense to describe someone who is physically leading or taking away something/someone.
