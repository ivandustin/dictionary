Word: απολυθησεσθε

Etymology: απολυθησεσθε is a Greek word that comes from the verb απολύω (apolyo), which means "to release" or "to dismiss". The suffix -θησεσθε (-thesesthe) is a passive voice ending that indicates that the action of the verb is being done to the subject.

Grammar: απολυθησεσθε is a second-person plural passive verb in the future tense. It is the future passive indicative form of the verb απολύω (apolyo).

Description: απολυθησεσθε means "you will be released" or "you will be dismissed". It is a command or a prediction that someone or something will be set free or let go. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a legal or employment setting, where someone is being released from a contract or agreement. It can also be used in a spiritual or religious context, where someone is being released from sin or guilt.
