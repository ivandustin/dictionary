Word: απιστον

Etymology:
The word απιστον (apiston) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the word "pistos" meaning "faithful" or "trustworthy". Therefore, απιστον means "unfaithful" or "untrustworthy".

Grammar:
απιστον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is often used as an adjective to describe a person or thing that is not reliable or trustworthy.

Description:
απιστον is a word that is commonly used in Greek to describe someone or something that is not trustworthy. It can be used to describe a person who is not faithful in a relationship or a business partner who is not reliable. It can also be used to describe an object or situation that is not dependable. Overall, απιστον is a negative term that implies a lack of trust or reliability.
