Word: απολλυνται

Etymology: The word απολλυνται comes from the Greek verb απόλλυμι (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". It is derived from the name of the Greek god Apollo, who was associated with destruction and death.

Grammar: απολλυνται is a third-person plural present passive indicative form of the verb απόλλυμι. This means that it is used to describe an action that is happening to a group of people or things in the present, and that they are not actively doing the action themselves. In this case, it means "they are being destroyed" or "they are perishing".

Description: απολλυνται is a strong and dramatic word that conveys a sense of destruction and loss. It can be used to describe a wide range of situations, from physical destruction to the loss of a way of life or a culture. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was often associated with destruction and death, and the word απολλυνται reflects this association.
