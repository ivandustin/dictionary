Etymology:
The word απεδεχετο (apedecheto) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb δεχομαι (dechomai), meaning "to receive." Therefore, απεδεχετο can be translated as "he/she/it refused" or "he/she/it rejected."

απεδεχετο is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in the indicative mood of the verb δεχομαι (dechomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.
