Etymology:
The word απειθησαντες (apeithēsantes) is a Greek word that is derived from the root words α- (a-) meaning "not" and πειθω (peithō) meaning "to persuade". The word is a verb form of the Greek word απειθεια (apeitheia) which means "disobedience" or "unbelief".
