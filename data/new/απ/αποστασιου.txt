The word "αποστασιου" (apostasiou) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb "αποστατέω" (apostateo), which means "to revolt" or "to desert." The noun "αποστασιου" refers to a revolt or rebellion, particularly in a political or religious context.

In terms of grammar, "αποστασιου" is a genitive singular noun, meaning it is used to indicate possession or relationship to another noun. It can be translated as "of the revolt" or "of the rebellion."

Overall, "αποστασιου" is a word that describes a specific type of uprising or rebellion, and is often used in historical or political contexts.
