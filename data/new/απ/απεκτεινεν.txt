Word: απεκτεινεν

Etymology:
The word απεκτεινεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κτείνω (kteinó), which means "to kill." The prefix απο- (apo-) means "away from" or "off," so απεκτεινεν can be translated as "to kill off" or "to slay."

Grammar:
απεκτεινεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is in the past tense and indicates that the action of killing has been completed. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
απεκτεινεν is a strong and forceful word that conveys the idea of killing or slaying with finality. It is often used in the context of warfare or violent conflict, but can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of killing any living thing. The word has a sense of finality and permanence, suggesting that the subject of the sentence has killed something or someone completely and without any chance of revival or recovery.
