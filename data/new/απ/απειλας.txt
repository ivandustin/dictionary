Word: απειλας

Etymology:
The word απειλας (apeilas) is derived from the Greek verb απειλέω (apeileō), which means "to threaten" or "to menace". The noun απειλας refers to a threat or a menace.

Grammar:
απειλας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
απειλας is a word that is commonly used in Greek to refer to a threat or a menace. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in politics, business, or personal relationships. For example, one might say "Δέχτηκα απειλές από τον αντίπαλο μου στις εκλογές" (I received threats from my opponent in the elections). The word can also be used in a more general sense, such as "Η απειλή της βροχής μας ανάγκασε να ακυρώσουμε την εκδήλωση" (The threat of rain forced us to cancel the event).
