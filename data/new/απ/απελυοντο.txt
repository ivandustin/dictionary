Word: απελυοντο

Etymology:
The word απελυοντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απολυω (apoluo), which means "to release" or "to set free". The verb is in the middle voice, indicating that the subject is acting upon itself.

Grammar:
απελυοντο is the third person plural, imperfect middle/passive indicative form of the verb απολυω (apoluo). In this form, the verb indicates an ongoing action in the past, and the subject is either acting upon itself or being acted upon by an outside force.

Description:
The word απελυοντο can be translated as "they were being released" or "they were setting themselves free". It implies a process of liberation or freedom that was taking place in the past. The context in which the word is used would determine the exact meaning and significance of the action.
