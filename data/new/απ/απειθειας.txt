The word "απειθειας" (apeitheias) is a Greek noun that means disobedience or noncompliance. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" or "without" and the noun "πείθω" (peitho) meaning "to persuade". 

Grammar: "απειθειας" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. 

Description: The word is often used in a negative context to describe someone who refuses to obey authority or follow rules. It can also refer to a general lack of compliance or adherence to a set of guidelines or principles.
