Word: αποτολμαι

Etymology:
The word αποτολμαι is derived from the Greek prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from" or "off", and the verb τολμάω (tolmao), meaning "to dare" or "to be bold". Therefore, αποτολμαι can be translated as "to be discouraged" or "to lose one's courage".

Grammar:
αποτολμαι is a first-person singular present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It belongs to the conjugation of the verb τολμάω (tolmao), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description:
The word αποτολμαι describes a state of mind where one loses their courage or becomes discouraged. It can be used to describe a person who is hesitant or reluctant to take action due to fear or lack of confidence. The word can also be used to describe a situation where someone has lost their nerve or is no longer willing to take risks.
