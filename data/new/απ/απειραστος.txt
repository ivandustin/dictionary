Word: απειραστος

Etymology:
The word απειραστος comes from the Greek prefix "α-" meaning "not" and the word "πειραστός" meaning "tested" or "experienced". Therefore, απειραστος means "untested" or "inexperienced".

Grammar:
απειραστος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender and number of the noun it modifies.

Description:
απειραστος is used to describe something that has not been tested or experienced. It can be used in a literal sense, such as an untested theory or an inexperienced person, or in a figurative sense, such as an untested idea or an untried method. It can also be used to describe a lack of limits or boundaries, as in the phrase "απειραστος ουρανός" meaning "limitless sky".
