Word: αποκριθεντες

Etymology: αποκριθεντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb αποκρίνομαι (apokrinomai), which means "to answer" or "to reply". The word is formed by adding the suffix -τες (-tes) to the present participle stem αποκριν- (apokrin-).

Grammar: αποκριθεντες is a plural masculine participle in the aorist tense. It is derived from the verb αποκρίνομαι (apokrinomai), which means "to answer" or "to reply". The participle form indicates that the action of answering or replying has already taken place in the past.

Description: αποκριθεντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (22:33) and the Gospel of Mark (12:24).
