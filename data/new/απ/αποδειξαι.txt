The word "αποδείξαι" (apodeíxai) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "απόδειξη" (apódeixi), which means "proof" or "demonstration". 

Grammar: "Αποδείξαι" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "αποδείκνυμι" (apodeíknymi), which means "to prove" or "to demonstrate". It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to prove or demonstrate something. 

Description: The word "αποδείξαι" is often used in formal or academic contexts, such as in scientific research or legal proceedings. It is a strong and assertive word that implies a need for evidence or proof to support a claim or argument.
