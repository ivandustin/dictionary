Word: απωλειαν

Etymology: απωλειαν is a Greek word that comes from the verb απολλύω (apollyo), which means "to destroy" or "to lose". The suffix -αν (-an) is added to the end of the verb to form a noun, indicating the act or result of the verb.

Grammar: απωλειαν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is singular and feminine in gender.

Description: απωλειαν refers to the act of losing or destruction. It can be used to describe the loss of something physical, such as a possession or a life, or something abstract, such as a relationship or an opportunity. The word carries a sense of finality and permanence, suggesting that the loss is irreversible.
