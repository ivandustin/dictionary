Word: απεστεγασαν

Etymology:
The word απεστεγασαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word στέγω (stego), which means "to cover" or "to protect". The prefix απο- (apo) means "away from" or "off", so απεστεγασαν can be translated as "uncovered" or "unprotected".

Grammar:
απεστεγασαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb αποστεγάζω (apostegazo), which means "to uncover" or "to remove the roof". In this form, it indicates that the action of uncovering or removing the roof was completed in the past by a group of people.

Description:
απεστεγασαν is a verb that describes an action of removing the roof or uncovering something. It is a past tense form, indicating that the action was completed in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in construction, demolition, or even in a metaphorical sense to describe the exposure of something that was previously hidden or protected.
