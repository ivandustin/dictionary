Word: απεσταλκαμεν

Etymology: απο- (apo-) meaning "away from" and στελλω (stello) meaning "to send". Therefore, απεσταλκαμεν (apestalkamen) is the first person plural perfect active indicative form of the Greek verb αποστελλω (apostello), which means "we have sent away".

Grammar: απεσταλκαμεν is a verb in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action in the past with ongoing relevance to the present. It is in the first person plural, meaning "we have sent away".

Description: απεσταλκαμεν is a Greek verb that expresses the action of sending something or someone away. It can be used in various contexts, such as sending a message, a package, or a person to a different location. The perfect tense indicates that the action has been completed, but it may still have an impact on the present.
