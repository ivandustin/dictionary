The word "απλοτητι" (aplotiti) is a Greek noun that means simplicity or plainness. 

Etymology: The word comes from the adjective "απλός" (aplos), which means simple or plain. The suffix "-τητα" (-tita) is added to the adjective to form the noun, which indicates the quality or state of being simple.

Grammar: "Απλοτητι" is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender and number, and their endings change depending on their function in a sentence.

Description: The word "απλοτητι" is often used to describe a quality or characteristic of something that is straightforward, uncomplicated, and easy to understand. It can also refer to a lack of complexity or ornamentation in design, art, or language. The concept of simplicity is highly valued in many cultures and is often associated with elegance, clarity, and efficiency.
