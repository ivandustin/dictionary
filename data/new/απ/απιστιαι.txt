Word: απιστιαι

Etymology:
The word απιστιαι (apistiai) is derived from the Greek word απιστία (apostia), which means "unbelief" or "faithlessness". It is formed by adding the suffix -αι (-ai) to the end of the word.

Grammar:
απιστιαι is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word απιστιαι is feminine and plural, which means it refers to multiple instances of unbelief or faithlessness.

Description:
απιστιαι is a word that describes a lack of belief or faith. It can refer to a lack of faith in a religious context, or a lack of trust or confidence in a person, idea, or institution. The word is often used in a negative context, implying that the lack of belief or faith is a negative trait.
