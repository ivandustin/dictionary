Word: απροσιτον

Etymology: 
The word απροσιτον (aprositon) is derived from the Greek prefix "α-" (a-) meaning "not" or "without" and the Greek word "προσιτος" (prositos) meaning "accessible" or "approachable". Therefore, απροσιτον means "inaccessible" or "unapproachable".

Grammar:
απροσιτον is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is not accessible or cannot be reached.

Description:
απροσιτον is a word that describes something that is difficult or impossible to reach or access. It can be used to describe physical objects or abstract concepts. For example, a mountain peak that is too steep to climb or a person who is emotionally distant can be described as απροσιτον.
