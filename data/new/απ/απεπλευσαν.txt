Word: απεπλευσαν

Etymology:
The word απεπλευσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πλεύω (plevo), which means "to sail." The prefix απο- (apo) means "away from" or "off," so απεπλευσαν can be translated as "they sailed away from."

Grammar:
απεπλευσαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb πλέω (pleo), which means "to sail." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (they) performed the action of sailing.

Description:
απεπλευσαν means "they sailed away from" in Greek. It is a verb that describes a completed action in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of sailors leaving a port or a ship sailing away from a storm.
