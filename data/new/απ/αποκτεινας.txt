Word: αποκτείνας

Etymology:
The word αποκτείνας (apokteinas) is a verb in the Greek language, which means "to kill" or "to slay". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from" or "off", and the verb κτείνω (kteino), meaning "to kill".

Grammar:
αποκτείνας is a second-person singular aorist active participle of the verb αποκτείνω (apokteino), which means "to kill". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The participle form is used to modify a noun or pronoun, and in this case, it means "having killed" or "having slain".

Description:
αποκτείνας is a strong and forceful word that conveys the act of taking someone's life intentionally. It is often used in the context of warfare, murder, or execution. The word carries a sense of finality and irreversibility, as the act of killing cannot be undone. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was known as the "apokteinas" or "slayer" of the Python, a monstrous serpent.
