Word: απεκδυσαμενος

Etymology:
The word απεκδυσαμενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb απεκδύομαι (apekdyomai), which means "to undress" or "to take off one's clothes". The word is formed by adding the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from", to the verb κδύω (kdyo), which means "to dress" or "to put on clothes".

Grammar:
απεκδυσαμενος is the past participle form of the verb απεκδύομαι (apekdyomai). In Greek, the past participle is used to form the perfect tense, which is used to describe completed actions in the past. The word is masculine, singular, and nominative case.

Description:
απεκδυσαμενος describes someone who has undressed or taken off their clothes. It can also be used figuratively to describe someone who has stripped themselves of something, such as their pride or their ego. The word is often used in the context of sports, where athletes may undress or change their clothes after a game or competition.
