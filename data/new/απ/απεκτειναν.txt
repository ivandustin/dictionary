Word: απεκτείναν (apekteínan)

Etymology: The word απεκτείναν comes from the Greek verb αποκτείνω (apokteíno), which means "to kill" or "to slay". The prefix απο- (apo-) means "away from" or "off", and the root κτείνω (kteíno) means "to kill". The -αν (-an) ending indicates the past tense, third person plural.

Grammar: απεκτείναν is a verb in the past tense, third person plural. It is the aorist tense of the verb αποκτείνω (apokteíno), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past. The verb is active, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of killing.

Description: απεκτείναν is a Greek verb that means "they killed" or "they slayed". It is often used in historical or mythological contexts to describe acts of violence or warfare. The word has a strong connotation of finality, indicating that the killing was completed and the victim is dead.
