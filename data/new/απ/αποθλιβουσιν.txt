The word "αποθλιβουσιν" is a verb in Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the root word "θλίβω" which means "to press, to squeeze, to afflict". The prefix "απο-" means "away from" or "off". Therefore, "αποθλιβουσιν" means "they are being pressed away from" or "they are being squeezed off".

Grammar: The word is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person plural form of the verb "αποθλίβω".

Description: The word "αποθλιβουσιν" is used to describe a situation where someone or something is being pressed or squeezed away from a particular place or situation. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a feeling of being oppressed or overwhelmed.
