The word "αποκαλυψιν" (apokalypsin) is a Greek noun that means "revelation" or "unveiling". It is derived from the verb "αποκαλυπτω" (apokalypto), which means "to reveal" or "to uncover". In Greek mythology, the term was used to describe the revealing of hidden knowledge or secrets by the gods.

In terms of grammar, "αποκαλυψιν" is a third declension noun in the accusative case. It is the singular form of the noun, with the plural form being "αποκαλυψεις" (apokalypseis).

Overall, "αποκαλυψιν" is a word that conveys the idea of uncovering or revealing something that was previously hidden or unknown. It has been used in various contexts throughout history, including in religious texts such as the Book of Revelation in the Bible.
