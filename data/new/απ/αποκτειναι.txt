Word: αποκτείναι

Etymology: The word αποκτείναι comes from the Greek verb αποκτείνω (apokteino), which means "to kill" or "to slay". The word is composed of two parts: από (apo), which means "from" or "away", and κτείνω (kteino), which means "to kill".

Grammar: αποκτείναι is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb αποκτείνω (apokteino).

Description: αποκτείναι is a strong and forceful word that refers to the act of killing or slaying someone or something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as in the context of murder or warfare, or in a figurative sense, such as in the context of defeating an opponent or overcoming an obstacle. The word carries a sense of finality and permanence, as the act of killing is irreversible.
