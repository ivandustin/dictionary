Word: αποθνήσκει (apothnēskei)

Etymology: The word αποθνήσκει comes from the Greek verb αποθνήσκω (apothnēskō), which means "to die" or "to perish". It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb θνήσκω (thnēskō), meaning "to die".

Grammar: αποθνήσκει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb αποθνήσκω. It is used to describe an action that is currently happening or is habitual in nature. In this case, it means "he/she/it dies" or "he/she/it is dying".

Description: αποθνήσκει is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of dying or perishing. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in medical settings, literature, and everyday conversation. It is a regular verb, meaning that it follows a predictable conjugation pattern.
