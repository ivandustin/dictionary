Word: αποτασσεται

Etymology: 
The word αποτασσεται comes from the Greek verb αποτάσσω (apotássō), which means "to arrange, to put in order, to dispose of". It is derived from the prefix από (apó), meaning "away from", and the verb τάσσω (tássō), meaning "to arrange, to put in order".

Grammar:
αποτασσεται is a third person singular present indicative active form of the verb αποτάσσω (apotássō). It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present and is being performed by a single person or thing.

Description:
The word αποτασσεται is used to describe the act of arranging or putting something in order. It can also be used to describe someone who is taking a stand or making a decision. In Greek, it is often used in military contexts to describe the act of deploying troops or arranging them in a specific formation.
