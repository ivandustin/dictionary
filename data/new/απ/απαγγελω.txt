Word: απαγγελω

Etymology:
The word απαγγελω (apangelō) is derived from the Greek prefix απο- (apo-) meaning "away from" or "off" and the verb αγγελλω (angelō) meaning "to announce" or "to report". Therefore, απαγγελω means "to report away from" or "to announce off".

Grammar:
απαγγελω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: απαγγελω (apangelō) - I report away from
- Plural: απαγγελουμεν (apangeloumen) - We report away from

Description:
απαγγελω is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can mean "to announce", "to report", "to declare", "to proclaim", "to recite", or "to deliver". It is often used in legal or formal settings to indicate the delivery of a message or the announcement of a decision. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of communicating information to others.
