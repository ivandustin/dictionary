Word: απελθων

Etymology:
The word απελθων (apelthōn) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απελθειν (apelthein), which means "to go away" or "to depart". It is formed from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb ελθειν (elthein), meaning "to come".

Grammar:
απελθων is the aorist participle of the verb απελθειν (apelthein), which means "having gone away" or "having departed". As a participle, it can function as an adjective or a noun in a sentence. It is declined like a regular adjective in Greek, with different forms for masculine, feminine, and neuter genders, and for singular and plural numbers.

Description:
απελθων is a word that describes someone or something that has gone away or departed. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to describe a person who has left a place, a thing that has been removed or taken away, or a situation that has ended or changed. In Greek literature, it is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe the passing of time or the transience of life.
