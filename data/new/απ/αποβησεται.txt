Word: αποβησεται

Etymology: αποβησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb αποβαίνω (apovéno), which means "to come to land" or "to disembark". The word is formed by adding the suffix -σεται (-setai), which is the third person singular future tense form of the verb.

Grammar: αποβησεται is a third person singular future tense form of the verb αποβαίνω (apovéno). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future.

Description: αποβησεται means "he/she/it will come to land" or "he/she/it will disembark". The word is often used in the context of ships or boats arriving at a port or shore. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone arriving at a destination or achieving a goal.
