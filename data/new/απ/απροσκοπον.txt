The word "απροσκοπον" is a Greek adjective that means "unobstructed" or "unimpeded". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "α-" which means "not" or "un-", and the word "προσκοπή" which means "obstacle" or "hindrance". 

Grammar: "απροσκοπον" is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: The word is often used to describe something that is free from obstacles or hindrances, and can be used in both literal and figurative contexts. For example, one might use the word to describe a clear path or a smooth process, or to describe a situation that is free from complications or difficulties.
