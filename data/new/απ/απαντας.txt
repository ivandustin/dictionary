Word: απαντας

Etymology:
The word απαντας (apantas) is derived from the Greek verb απαντάω (apantao), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb αντάω (antao), meaning "to meet".

Grammar:
απαντας is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple subjects.

Description:
απαντας can be translated to "everyone" or "all". It is often used to refer to a group of people or things collectively. For example, "απαντας οι φίλοι μου" (apantas oi filoi mou) means "all my friends". It can also be used as an adjective to describe a group of people or things, such as "απαντας οι μαθητές" (apantas oi mathites), meaning "all the students".
