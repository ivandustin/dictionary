Word: αποχωρείτε

Etymology: αποχωρείτε is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from," and the verb χωρείτε (choreite), meaning "you go." Therefore, αποχωρείτε means "you go away from."

Grammar: αποχωρείτε is the second person plural form of the present tense of the verb αποχωρώ (apochoro), which means "to depart" or "to leave." It is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of the verbs that end in -ω.

Description: αποχωρείτε is a formal way of saying "you leave" or "you depart" in Greek. It is often used in official or polite contexts, such as when addressing a group of people or when giving instructions.
