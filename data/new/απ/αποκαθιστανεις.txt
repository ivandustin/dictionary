Word: αποκαθιστάνεις

Etymology: The word αποκαθιστάνεις comes from the Greek verb αποκαθιστώ (apokathistó), which means "to restore" or "to bring back to its original state". The word is formed by adding the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from" or "off", to the verb καθιστώ (kathistó), which means "to place" or "to establish".

Grammar: αποκαθιστάνεις is a second person singular present indicative active verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ανείς (-aneis) to the stem αποκαθιστά- (apokathistá-). The verb is used to describe the action of restoring or bringing something back to its original state.

Description: αποκαθιστάνεις is a Greek verb that describes the act of restoring or bringing something back to its original state. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as repairing a broken object, fixing a damaged relationship, or restoring a historical monument. The verb is commonly used in everyday speech and writing in Greece.
