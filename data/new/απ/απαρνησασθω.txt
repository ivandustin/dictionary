Word: απαρνησασθω

Etymology: απαρνησασθω is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απαρνεομαι (aparneomai), which means "to deny" or "to disown". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: απαρνησασθω is a second person singular aorist middle imperative verb in Greek. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves, and the imperative mood indicates a command or request.

Description: απαρνησασθω is a strong and forceful command to someone to deny or disown something. It is often used in a religious context, such as when Jesus tells Peter to deny him three times before the rooster crows (Matthew 26:34). The word can also be used in a more general sense to mean renouncing or rejecting something.
