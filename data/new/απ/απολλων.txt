Word: απολλων

Etymology:
The word απολλων (Apollo) is derived from the Greek word ἀπόλλυμι (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to kill". In Greek mythology, Apollo was the god of music, poetry, prophecy, and healing.

Grammar:
απολλων is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case (απόλλωνος) or the accusative case (απόλλωνα).

Description:
In Greek mythology, Apollo was one of the twelve Olympian gods and was considered one of the most important. He was the son of Zeus and Leto, and the twin brother of Artemis. Apollo was associated with the sun, light, and music, and was often depicted playing a lyre. He was also known for his healing abilities and was worshipped as a god of medicine. Apollo was a complex figure in Greek mythology, and his stories and attributes varied depending on the region and time period.
