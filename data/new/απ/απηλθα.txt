Word: απηλθα

Etymology: απηλθα is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb ερχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come." Therefore, απηλθα means "I went away" or "I departed."

Grammar: απηλθα is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb απέρχομαι (aperchomai), which means "to depart" or "to go away." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: απηλθα is a simple and straightforward verb that expresses the action of going away or departing. It can be used in various contexts, such as when someone leaves a place or situation, or when someone ends a relationship or connection with someone else. It is a common verb in Greek language and is used in both formal and informal settings.
