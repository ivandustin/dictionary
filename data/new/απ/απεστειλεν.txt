Word: απεστειλεν

Etymology:
The word απεστειλεν (apostelen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αποστελλω (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch." This verb is composed of two parts: απο (apo), which means "away from," and στελλω (stello), which means "to send."

Grammar:
απεστειλεν is the aorist tense of the Greek verb αποστελλω (apostello). The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, it means "he sent away" or "he dispatched."

Description:
απεστειλεν is a verb that describes an action of sending or dispatching someone or something away from a particular location. It is often used in the context of sending a message or a messenger to a specific destination.
