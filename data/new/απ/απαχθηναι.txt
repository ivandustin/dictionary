The word "απαχθηναι" is a Greek verb in the aorist passive infinitive form. Its etymology is derived from the prefix "απο-" meaning "away from" and the verb "αχθεσθαι" meaning "to bear, carry, or endure". Therefore, "απαχθηναι" can be translated to mean "to be carried away from" or "to be removed from".

In terms of grammar, "απαχθηναι" is a second aorist passive infinitive verb, which means that it describes an action that was completed in the past and was done to the subject of the verb. 

Overall, "απαχθηναι" is a word that describes a passive action of being removed or carried away from something.
