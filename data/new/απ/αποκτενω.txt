Word: αποκτενω

Etymology: απο- (apo-) meaning "away from" and κτείνω (kteinō) meaning "to kill". Therefore, αποκτενω (apoktenō) means "to kill off" or "to slay".

Grammar: αποκτενω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ αποκτενώ (Egō apoktenō) - I kill off
- Εσύ αποκτενείς (Esý apokteneís) - You kill off
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό αποκτενεί (Aftós/Aftē/Aftó apokteneí) - He/She/It kills off
- Εμείς αποκτενούμε (Emeís apoktenoúme) - We kill off
- Εσείς αποκτενείτε (Eseís apokteneíte) - You all kill off
- Αυτοί/Αυτές αποκτενούν (Aftoí/Aftés apoktenoún) - They kill off

Description: αποκτενω is a strong and forceful verb that implies a deliberate and intentional act of killing or slaying. It can be used in a literal sense, such as killing an animal for food or killing an enemy in battle, or in a figurative sense, such as killing off a bad habit or a negative emotion. It is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts, but it always conveys a sense of finality and irrevocability.
