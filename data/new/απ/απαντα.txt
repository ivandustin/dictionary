Word: απαντα

Etymology:
The word απαντα (apanta) is derived from the Greek verb απαντώ (apantó), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". It is a combination of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb ανταμώνω (antamóno), meaning "to meet".

Grammar:
απαντα is a noun in the neuter gender and is in the plural form. It is often used in the phrase "όλα τα απαντα" (óla ta apanta), which means "everything" or "all things".

Description:
απαντα refers to all things or everything. It is a comprehensive term that encompasses all aspects of existence. It can be used in a literal sense to refer to all physical objects or in a figurative sense to refer to all ideas, concepts, or experiences.
