Word: απελθοντων

Etymology:
The word απελθοντων is a present participle form of the Greek verb απέρχομαι (aperchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart". The word is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb ερχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar:
απελθοντων is the genitive plural form of the present participle of απέρχομαι. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the present participle is a verb form that describes an ongoing action.

Description:
απελθοντων can be translated as "of those who are departing" or "of those who are going away". It is often used in the context of a group of people who are leaving a place or situation. For example, in the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used in the phrase "οι απελθοντες ευηγγελισαντο" (hoi apelthontes euangelisanto), which means "those who went away preached the good news".
