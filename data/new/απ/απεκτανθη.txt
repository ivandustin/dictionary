The prefix απο- (apo-) means "away from" or "off", and the root κτείνω (ktíno) means "to kill". The suffix -θη (-thi) is a passive ending, indicating that the action was done to the subject.

The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The verb is conjugated in the indicative mood, which is used to make statements or ask questions.
