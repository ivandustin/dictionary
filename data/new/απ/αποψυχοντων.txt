The word "αποψυχοντων" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "αποψύχω" (apopsychó), which means "to cool down" or "to become cold". The prefix "απο-" (apo-) indicates separation or departure, while the root "ψυχ-" (psych-) refers to coldness or chilliness.

In terms of grammar, "αποψυχοντων" is the present participle form of the verb, which means it describes an ongoing action or state of being. It is also in the genitive case, indicating possession or association with another noun.

Overall, "αποψυχοντων" can be translated as "of those who are cooling down" or "of those who are becoming cold".
