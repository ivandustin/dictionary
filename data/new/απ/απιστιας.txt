The word "απιστιας" (apistias) is a Greek noun that means "infidelity" or "unfaithfulness". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek adjective "άπιστος" (apistos), which means "unbelieving" or "faithless". The suffix "-ιας" (-ias) is added to form the noun.

Grammar: "Απιστιας" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: The word "απιστιας" is often used to describe a breach of trust or loyalty in a personal or romantic relationship. It can also refer to disloyalty in a political or business context. The word has a negative connotation and is often associated with betrayal and deceit.
