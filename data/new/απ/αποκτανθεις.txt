The word "αποκτανθεις" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means "to be killed" or "to die". The word is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" which means "away from" or "off", and the verb "κταννυμι" which means "to kill". Therefore, "αποκτανθεις" literally means "to be killed off" or "to die away from". 

In terms of grammar, "αποκτανθεις" is a second person singular aorist passive participle. This means that it is used to describe an action that happened to the subject (you) in the past. 

Overall, "αποκτανθεις" is a word that describes a past event of being killed or dying.
