Word: απεκεφαλίσα (apekefalisa)

Etymology: The word απεκεφαλίσα comes from the Greek prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb κεφαλίζω (kefalizo), meaning "to behead." Therefore, απεκεφαλίσα means "I beheaded" or "I cut off the head."

Grammar: απεκεφαλίσα is the first person singular past tense of the verb αποκεφαλίζω (apokefalizo), which means "to behead" or "to decapitate." It is a regular verb in the active voice and belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs.

Description: απεκεφαλίσα is a strong and violent word that describes the act of cutting off someone's head. It is often used in historical or mythological contexts, such as the beheading of Medusa by Perseus or the execution of criminals in ancient Greece. The word carries a sense of finality and irreversibility, as the act of beheading is often seen as a definitive end to someone's life.
