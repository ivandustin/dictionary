Word: απαιτεί (apaití)

Etymology:
The word απαιτεί (apaití) comes from the Greek verb απαιτώ (apaitó), which means "to demand" or "to require". It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb αίτεω (aíteo), meaning "to ask" or "to request".

Grammar:
απαιτεί (apaití) is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is a regular verb and follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -ω (-o).

Description:
απαιτεί (apaití) means "demands" or "requires" in English. It is often used in the context of expressing a need or necessity for something. For example, "Αυτή η εργασία απαιτεί πολλή προσοχή" (Aftí i ergasía apaití pollí prosóchi) means "This work requires a lot of attention".
