The word "απεστη" (apesti) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "απέστησα" (apestisa), which means "I caused to stand away" or "I removed". 

Grammar: "απεστη" is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "απέστησα". It is a transitive verb, meaning it requires a direct object. 

Description: The word "απεστη" is used to describe an action of causing something or someone to stand away or to remove something or someone from a particular place or situation. It can also be used figuratively to describe a person's withdrawal or departure from a group or situation.
