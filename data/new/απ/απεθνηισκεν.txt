The word "απεθνησκεν" (apethnesken) is a Greek verb in the past tense, which means "he/she/it died" or "was dying". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "θνησκω" (thnesko), which means "to die". The prefix "απο" (apo) in "απεθνησκεν" indicates a completed action, so the word can be translated as "he/she/it died" or "was dying".

Grammar: "απεθνησκεν" is in the past tense and the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb "θνησκω" (thnesko) in the aorist tense.

Description: "απεθνησκεν" is a simple and straightforward verb that describes the action of dying or being in the process of dying. It is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday language to express the idea of death or mortality.
