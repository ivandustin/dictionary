The word "απολουσαι" (apoloosai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the second person singular, present tense, middle voice form of the verb "απολούω" (apoloouo), which means "to wash off" or "to cleanse." 

Etymology: The word "απολούω" comes from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and the verb "λούω" (louo), meaning "to wash." 

Grammar: "απολουσαι" is a present tense verb in the middle voice, which means that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The second person singular form indicates that the subject is "you." 

Description: The word "απολουσαι" can be translated to "you are washing off/cleansing yourself" in English. It is often used in a religious context, referring to the act of ritual purification or cleansing.
