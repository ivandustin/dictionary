Word: απεκεφαλίσεν

Etymology: The word απεκεφαλίσεν is derived from the Greek words από (apo) meaning "from" and κεφαλή (kephalē) meaning "head". It is a compound word formed by combining the prefix από with the verb κεφαλίζω (kephalízo) which means "to behead".

Grammar: απεκεφαλίσεν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the aorist form of the verb αποκεφαλίζω (apokephalízo) which means "to behead".

Description: απεκεφαλίσεν is a Greek word that means "he beheaded". It is commonly used to describe the act of cutting off someone's head, usually as a form of execution.
