Word: απατων

Etymology:
The word απατων (apaton) is derived from the Greek verb απαταω (apatao), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". It is related to the noun απατη (apate), which means "deception" or "trickery".

Grammar:
απατων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description:
απατων refers to someone who deceives or cheats others. It can also refer to a trickster or a fraudster. The word is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is dishonest or untrustworthy.
