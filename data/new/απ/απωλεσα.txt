Etymology: The word "απολέω" comes from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb "λέω" (leo), meaning "to say". The combination of these two words gives the meaning of "to say away from", which evolved to mean "to destroy" or "to lose".

Grammar: "Απωλεσα" is a past tense verb in the first person singular. The verb is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description: The word "απωλεσα" describes an action that has already happened in the past, specifically an action of destruction or loss. It can be used in various contexts, such as losing a game, destroying a building, or losing a loved one.
