Word: απεκδεχομενοι

Etymology: απεκδέχομαι (apekdechomai) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb εκδέχομαι (ekdechomai), meaning "to wait for" or "to expect." Therefore, απεκδέχομαι (apekdechomai) means "to wait for eagerly" or "to anticipate."

Grammar: απεκδεχομενοι (apekdechomenoi) is the present participle, middle voice, masculine plural form of the verb απεκδέχομαι (apekdechomai). The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The present participle indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Description: απεκδεχομενοι (apekdechomenoi) can be translated as "awaiting eagerly" or "anticipating." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as waiting for the coming of a savior or the fulfillment of a prophecy. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is eagerly waiting for something to happen.
