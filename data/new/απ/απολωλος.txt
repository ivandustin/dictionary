The word "απολωλος" (apollolos) is a Greek adjective that means "lost" or "destroyed". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the verb "απόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". 

Grammar: "απολωλος" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

Description: The word is often used in Greek mythology to describe heroes who have been lost or destroyed, such as the hero Achilles who was killed in battle. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe anything that has been lost or destroyed.
