Word: αποστασία (apostasia)

Etymology: The word αποστασία comes from the Greek verb αποστατέω (apostateo), which means "to revolt" or "to desert." It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb στατιζω (statizo), meaning "to stand."

Grammar: αποστασία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is αποστασίες (apostasies).

Description: αποστασία refers to a state of rebellion or defection from a religious or political belief or system. It can also refer to a physical distance or separation between two objects or individuals. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a break or separation in a relationship or friendship.
