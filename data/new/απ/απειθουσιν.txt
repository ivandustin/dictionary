Word: απειθουσιν

Etymology: The word απειθουσιν comes from the Greek verb απειθέω (apeitheo), which means "to disobey" or "to be disobedient". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates the present participle form, meaning "they are disobeying" or "they are being disobedient".

Grammar: απειθουσιν is a present participle form of the verb απειθέω (apeitheo), which is in the third person plural. It is used to describe a continuous action of disobedience in the present tense. 

Description: απειθουσιν is a Greek word that means "they are disobeying" or "they are being disobedient". It is often used in a negative context to describe people who are not following rules or orders. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, religion, or personal relationships.
