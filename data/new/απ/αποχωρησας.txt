Word: αποχωρησας

Etymology: 
The word αποχωρησας comes from the Greek verb αποχωρώ (apochoró), which means "to depart" or "to withdraw". The verb is formed by combining the prefix από (apó), meaning "from", and the verb χωρώ (choró), meaning "to go" or "to move".

Grammar:
αποχωρησας is the second person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb αποχωρώ (apochoró). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
αποχωρησας is a Greek verb that means "you departed" or "you withdrew". It is used to describe a completed action of leaving or going away from a place or situation. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as leaving a party, withdrawing from a conversation, or departing from a job. The word is commonly used in everyday speech and writing in Greece.
