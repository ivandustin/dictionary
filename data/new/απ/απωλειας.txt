Word: απωλειας

Etymology: απωλειας is a Greek word that comes from the verb απολέω (apoleo), which means "to destroy" or "to lose". The noun απωλειας (apoleias) means "loss" or "destruction".

Grammar: απωλειας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is the genitive singular form of the noun απώλεια (apoleia).

Description: απωλειας refers to the state of losing something or experiencing destruction. It can be used to describe the loss of material possessions, as well as the loss of intangible things like relationships, opportunities, or time. The word can also be used in a metaphorical sense to describe the decline or decay of something.
