Word: απελυσεν

Etymology: απελυσεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb απολύω (apolyo), which means "to release" or "to set free". It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb λύω (lyo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie".

Grammar: απελυσεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb απολύω (apolyo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: απελυσεν can be translated as "he/she/it released" or "he/she/it set free". It is a verb that describes an action of freeing someone or something from a constraint or obligation. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action.
