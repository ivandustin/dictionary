Word: αποστρέφειν

Etymology:
The word αποστρέφειν (apostrephein) is derived from the Greek roots από (apo) meaning "away from" and στρέφειν (strephein) meaning "to turn". Therefore, the word means "to turn away from".

Grammar:
αποστρέφειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν to the stem αποστρεφ-.

Description:
αποστρέφειν is a Greek verb that means "to turn away from". It can be used in various contexts, such as turning away from someone or something physically, or turning away from a belief or opinion. The word is often used in a negative sense, implying rejection or disapproval.
