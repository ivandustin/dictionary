Word: απελθωσιν

Etymology: απελθωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb απέρχομαι (aperchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart."

Grammar: απελθωσιν is a third-person plural aorist active subjunctive form of the verb απέρχομαι (aperchomai). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations.

Description: απελθωσιν can be translated as "they may depart" or "let them depart." It is a form of the verb απέρχομαι (aperchomai), which is a common verb in Greek and is used to express the idea of going away or departing from a place. The subjunctive mood in this form suggests that the action of departing is uncertain or hypothetical.
