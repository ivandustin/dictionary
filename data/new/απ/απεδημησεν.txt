Word: απεδημησεν

Etymology:
The word απεδημησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "πεδημία" (pedimia), which means "residence" or "sojourn." The prefix "απο-" (apo-) means "away from," so the word απεδημησεν means "to go away from one's residence" or "to emigrate."

Grammar:
απεδημησεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
απεδημησεν is a verb that describes the act of leaving one's residence or homeland to settle in a new place. It can be used to describe both voluntary and involuntary migration, such as emigrating for economic or political reasons, or being forced to flee due to war or persecution. The word has a sense of finality to it, as it implies a permanent departure from one's previous home.
