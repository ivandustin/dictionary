Word: απεσπασθη

Etymology:
The word απεσπασθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σπαω (spao), which means "to pull" or "to tear." The prefix απο- (apo) means "away from," so απεσπασθη can be translated as "to be pulled away from" or "to be torn away from."

Grammar:
απεσπασθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
απεσπασθη can be translated as "was taken away" or "was removed." It implies a forceful or sudden separation from something or someone. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as when describing the removal of an object, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing the emotional separation of two people.
