The word "απαιτουσιν" (apaitousin) is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically the present active indicative third person plural of the verb "απαιτέω" (apaitéō). 

Etymology: The word comes from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb "αιτέω" (aitéō), meaning "to ask, demand." Together, "απαιτέω" means "to demand, require, claim."

Grammar: As mentioned, "απαιτουσιν" is the present active indicative third person plural form of the verb. This means that it is describing an action that is currently happening, being done by a group of people (the third person plural). 

Description: The word "απαιτουσιν" can be translated to mean "they demand" or "they require." It implies a sense of urgency or necessity in the action being taken by the group.
