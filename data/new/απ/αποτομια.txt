Word: αποτομία

Etymology: The word αποτομία comes from the Greek word απότομος (apotomos), which means "sharp" or "abrupt". The suffix -ία (-ia) is added to form a noun, indicating the state or quality of being sharp or abrupt.

Grammar: αποτομία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is αποτομίες (apotomies).

Description: αποτομία refers to the quality of being sharp or abrupt, both in a physical and a metaphorical sense. In a physical sense, it can refer to a sharp edge or point, such as that of a knife or a cliff. In a metaphorical sense, it can refer to a sudden change or a blunt manner of speaking. It can also be used to describe a person who is blunt or abrupt in their behavior or speech.
