Word: αποβαλων

Etymology:
The word αποβαλων comes from the Greek verb αποβάλλω (apobállō), which means "to throw away" or "to cast off". The word is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from", and the verb βάλλω (bállō), which means "to throw".

Grammar:
αποβαλων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the present participle of the verb αποβάλλω (apobállō), which means "casting off" or "throwing away". The word can be used as an adjective to describe something that is being cast off or thrown away.

Description:
αποβαλων refers to something that is being cast off or thrown away. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as trash or waste, or abstract concepts, such as bad habits or negative emotions. The word implies a deliberate and intentional act of getting rid of something that is no longer needed or wanted.
