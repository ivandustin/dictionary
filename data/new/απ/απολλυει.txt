The word "απολλυει" (apollyei) is a verb in the present tense in Greek. It comes from the root word "απόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". The verb form "απολλυει" means "he/she/it destroys" or "he/she/it is perishing". 

In terms of grammar, "απολλυει" is a third-person singular verb in the present tense. It is conjugated from the stem "απολλυ-", with the ending "-ει" indicating the present tense. 

Overall, "απολλυει" is a word that describes destruction or perishing, and is used in the context of something or someone being destroyed or coming to an end.
