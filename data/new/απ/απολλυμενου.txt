Word: απολλυμενου

Etymology:
The word απολλυμενου is derived from the Greek verb απόλλυμι (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". The word is in the present participle form, which means "being destroyed" or "being perished".

Grammar:
απολλυμενου is a present participle in the genitive case, masculine gender, and singular number. It is used to describe a noun or pronoun in a state of being destroyed or perished.

Description:
The word απολλυμενου is often used in Greek literature to describe a person or thing that is in the process of being destroyed or perished. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or idea that is failing or coming to an end. The word has a sense of urgency and finality, as it implies that the destruction or perishing is inevitable and cannot be stopped.
