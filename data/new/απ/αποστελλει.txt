Word: αποστέλλει (apostellei)

Etymology: αποστέλλει comes from the Greek verb αποστέλλω (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb στέλλω (stello), meaning "to send".

Grammar: αποστέλλει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb αποστέλλω. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of sending or dispatching.

Description: αποστέλλει is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physically sending something or someone away, as in "He sent the package by mail" (Απέστειλε το πακέτο με ταχυδρομείο). It can also refer to sending a message or communication, as in "She sent an email to her boss" (Απέστειλε ένα email στον αφεντικό της). Additionally, it can be used in a figurative sense to mean "to inspire" or "to motivate", as in "The coach sent the team out onto the field with a pep talk" (Ο προπονητής απέστειλε την ομάδα στο γήπεδο με μια ενθαρρυντική ομιλία).
