The word "απαταις" (apatais) is a Greek noun in the plural form, which means "deceptions" or "frauds". It is derived from the verb "απατάω" (apatao), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". In Greek mythology, the goddess Apate was the personification of deceit and deception. The word "απαταις" is commonly used in modern Greek to refer to scams or fraudulent activities. In terms of grammar, "απαταις" is a feminine noun in the nominative case.
