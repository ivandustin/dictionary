Word: απολλυμενην

Etymology: απολλυμενην is the feminine singular form of the Greek verb απόλλυμι (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb ολλυμι (ollymi), meaning "to destroy".

Grammar: απολλυμενην is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is also in the feminine gender and singular number, indicating that it refers to a single female object or person.

Description: απολλυμενην can be translated as "destroyed" or "perishing". It is often used in Greek literature to describe a woman who is in a state of distress or suffering. In the context of ancient Greek tragedies, it is often used to describe the tragic fate of a female character.
