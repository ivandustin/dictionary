Word: αποκρινεσθαι

Etymology: απο- (apo-, meaning "away from") + κρινω (krinō, meaning "to judge") + -εσθαι (-esthai, a middle voice suffix indicating reflexive action). Therefore, αποκρινεσθαι means "to answer for oneself" or "to defend oneself".

Grammar: αποκρινεσθαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. It is a compound verb, consisting of the prefix απο- and the verb κρινω.

Description: αποκρινεσθαι is a Greek verb that is commonly used in legal contexts, where it refers to the act of defending oneself in court. It can also be used more broadly to mean "to answer" or "to reply". In the New Testament, it is often used to describe Jesus' responses to his accusers.
