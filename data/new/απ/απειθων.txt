The word "απειθων" (apeithōn) is a Greek adjective that means "disobedient" or "unruly". It is derived from the verb "απειθέω" (apeithéō), which means "to disobey" or "to be disobedient". In terms of grammar, it is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can be used to describe a person who refuses to follow rules or authority, or to describe an action that goes against what is expected or required.
