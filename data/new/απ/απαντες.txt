Word: απαντες

Etymology:
The word απαντες (apantes) is derived from the Greek word απαντάω (apantao), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". It is a combination of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb ανταω (antao), meaning "to meet".

Grammar:
απαντες is a Greek adjective that means "all" or "every". It is in the masculine plural form, and is often used to describe a group of people or things. It can be used as a subject, object, or predicate adjective in a sentence.

Description:
απαντες is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. For example, it can be used to describe a group of people, such as "all the students in the class" (οι απαντες οι μαθητές στην τάξη), or a group of things, such as "all the books on the shelf" (τα απαντα βιβλία στο ράφι). It can also be used to express a universal truth, such as "all men are mortal" (οι απαντες οι άνθρωποι είναι θνητοί). Overall, απαντες is a useful and commonly used word in the Greek language.
