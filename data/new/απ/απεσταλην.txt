Word: απεσταλην

Etymology:
The word απεσταλην (apestalēn) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αποστέλλω (apostellō), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch." It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb στέλλω (stellō), meaning "to send."

Grammar:
απεσταλην is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb αποστέλλω (apostellō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or asking a question.

Description:
The word απεσταλην (apestalēn) can be translated to English as "I was sent" or "I have been sent." It implies that the speaker was sent by someone else to a particular place or on a particular mission. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the sending of Jesus Christ by God to save humanity.
