Word: αποστελλομενα

Etymology: αποστέλλω (apostellō) is a Greek verb meaning "to send away" or "to dispatch". The word is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb στέλλω (stellō), meaning "to send".

Grammar: αποστελλόμενα (apostellomena) is the present participle passive form of the verb αποστέλλω (apostellō). It is a neuter plural adjective that can function as a noun, meaning "the things being sent" or "the ones being dispatched".

Description: αποστελλόμενα (apostellomena) is a Greek word that refers to things or people that are being sent or dispatched. It can be used in various contexts, such as in business, politics, or religion. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe the letters that the apostle Paul sent to various churches.
