Word: απελθοντι

Etymology:
The word απελθοντι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "απέρχομαι" (aperchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart".

Grammar:
απελθοντι is the aorist participle of the verb απέρχομαι. In Greek, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. The aorist participle indicates a completed action in the past, and it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
απελθοντι can be translated as "having gone away" or "having departed". It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of Jesus or his disciples, such as "απελθοντες εκηρυξαν" (having gone away, they preached). The word emphasizes the completed nature of the action, and can also imply a sense of finality or separation.
