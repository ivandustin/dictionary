The word "αποκτενειτε" is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be broken down into two parts: "από" meaning "from" or "away" and "κτείνω" meaning "to kill". Therefore, "αποκτενειτε" can be translated to "to kill off" or "to exterminate".

In terms of grammar, "αποκτενειτε" is a second person plural present active imperative verb. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to perform the action of killing off or exterminating.

In description, "αποκτενειτε" is a strong and forceful word that conveys a sense of urgency and severity. It is often used in contexts where there is a need to eliminate a threat or danger quickly and decisively.
