Word: απαλλάσσεσθαι

Etymology:
The word απαλλάσσεσθαι comes from the Greek verb απαλλάσσω (apallássō), which means "to release" or "to free". It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb αλλάσσω (allássō), meaning "to change".

Grammar:
απαλλάσσεσθαι is a reflexive verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. It is often translated as "to be released" or "to be freed". The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description:
απαλλάσσεσθαι is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of being released or freed from something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as being released from a debt, being freed from captivity, or being relieved of a burden. The reflexive form of the verb emphasizes that the subject is the one being released or freed.
