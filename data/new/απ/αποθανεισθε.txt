Word: αποθανεισθε

Etymology:
The word αποθανεισθε is derived from the Greek verb αποθνῄσκω (apothnēskō), which means "to die" or "to pass away". The word is formed by adding the suffix -εισθε (-eisthe) to the stem αποθαν- (apothan-).

Grammar:
αποθανεισθε is a second person plural verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and imperative mood. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people to "die" or "pass away".

Description:
αποθανεισθε is a strong and direct command that is not commonly used in everyday language. It is often found in religious or philosophical texts, where it is used to convey the idea of letting go of the physical world and embracing the spiritual realm. The word carries a sense of finality and inevitability, emphasizing the idea that death is an unavoidable part of life.
