Word: απηγαγον

Etymology:
The word απηγαγον (apēgagon) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb ἄγω (agō), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." Therefore, απηγαγον (apēgagon) means "to lead away from" or "to take away."

Grammar:
απηγαγον (apēgagon) is the aorist tense of the Greek verb ἀπάγω (apagō), which means "to lead away" or "to take away." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
απηγαγον (apēgagon) is a verb that describes the act of taking someone or something away from a particular place or situation. It can also be used figuratively to mean "to remove" or "to withdraw." In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the act of taking someone away by force or kidnapping.
