The word "αποκτενουσιν" is a verb in the present active participle form in Greek. It is derived from the root word "αποκτείνω" which means "to kill" or "to slay". The suffix "-ουσιν" indicates that the action is being performed by a group of people in the present tense. 

In terms of grammar, "αποκτενουσιν" is a third-person plural verb in the present tense. It is often translated as "they are killing" or "they are slaying". 

Overall, "αποκτενουσιν" is a word that describes a violent action being carried out by a group of people in the present moment.
