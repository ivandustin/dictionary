Word: απησπασαμεθα

Etymology:
The word απησπασαμεθα is a conjugated form of the Greek verb απασπάζω (apaspázo̱), which means "to draw away, to take away, to remove". The verb is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb σπάω (spáo̱), meaning "to draw, to pull".

Grammar:
απησπασαμεθα is a first-person plural, past tense, middle voice form of the verb απασπάζω. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. In this case, the subject is plural, meaning that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description:
απησπασαμεθα can be translated to "we were drawn away" or "we were taken away". The context in which the word is used will determine the exact meaning.
