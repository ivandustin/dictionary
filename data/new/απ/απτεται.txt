Word: απτεται

Etymology: The word απτεται comes from the Greek verb απτομαι (aptomai), which means "to touch" or "to grasp".

Grammar: απτεται is a third person singular present indicative passive form of the verb απτομαι. It is used to indicate that the subject is being touched or grasped by someone or something else.

Description: απτεται is a Greek verb that is commonly used in everyday language. It is often used to describe physical contact between two objects or people. For example, one might say "Το χέρι μου απτεται του τοίχου" (My hand is touching the wall). The passive form of the verb is often used to describe a state of being touched or grasped, rather than an action being taken by the subject.
