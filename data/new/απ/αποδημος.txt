Word: αποδημος

Etymology:
The word αποδημος comes from the Greek roots από (apo) meaning "away from" and δήμος (demos) meaning "people" or "community". Therefore, αποδημος literally means "away from the community" or "away from home".

Grammar:
In Greek, αποδημος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person who is living away from their homeland or community. It can also be used to describe something that is foreign or unfamiliar.

The word αποδημος is often used to describe the experience of being an immigrant or living in a foreign country. It can also be used to describe something that is not native to a particular place or culture. For example, a plant or animal species that is not native to Greece could be described as αποδημος.
