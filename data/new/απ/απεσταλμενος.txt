Word: απεσταλμένος

Etymology:
The word απεσταλμένος (apostalmenos) is derived from the Greek verb αποστέλλω (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice, meaning "sent" or "dispatched".

Grammar:
απεσταλμένος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this case, it could modify a masculine singular noun such as άνθρωπος (anthropos) meaning "man" or άγγελος (angelos) meaning "angel".

Description:
The word απεσταλμένος is often used in a religious context to describe someone who has been sent by God or a higher power to carry out a specific mission or task. It can also be used more generally to describe someone who has been sent on a mission or assignment. The word carries a sense of purpose and authority, as the person who is sent is seen as having a specific role to fulfill.
