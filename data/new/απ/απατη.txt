The word "απατη" (apati) is a Greek noun that means "deception" or "fraud". It comes from the Greek verb "απαταω" (apatao), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". In terms of grammar, "απατη" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It can be used in various contexts, such as in legal or financial matters, or in personal relationships. Overall, "απατη" refers to an act of dishonesty or trickery that is intended to mislead or harm someone.
