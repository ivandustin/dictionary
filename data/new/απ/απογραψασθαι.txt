Word: απογραψασθαι

Etymology: The word απογραψασθαι comes from the Greek prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb γραφω (grapho), meaning "to write." Together, απογραφω (apographo) means "to write off" or "to register."

Grammar: απογραψασθαι is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. It is derived from the verb απογραφω (apographo), which means "to register" or "to enroll."

Description: απογραψασθαι refers to the act of being registered or enrolled in a list or census. It can also mean to be written off or removed from a list. In ancient Greece, it was used to refer to the registration of citizens for military service or taxation purposes. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to refer to the process of registering for various official documents or services.
