The word "απελθουσα" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "departing" or "leaving". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" (apérchomai), which means "to depart" or "to go away". The present participle form "απελθουσα" (apelthousa) is formed by adding the suffix "-ούσα" (-ousa) to the stem of the verb.

Grammar: The word "απελθουσα" is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently departing or leaving.

Description: The word "απελθουσα" is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday language to describe someone who is leaving a place or situation. It can be used in various contexts, such as a person leaving a party, a traveler departing from a city, or a soldier leaving the battlefield.
