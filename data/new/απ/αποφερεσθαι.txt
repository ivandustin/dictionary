Word: αποφερεσθαι

Etymology:
The word αποφερεσθαι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from" or "off", and the verb φερω (phero), meaning "to carry" or "to bear". Therefore, αποφερεσθαι means "to carry away" or "to remove".

Grammar:
αποφερεσθαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, infinitive form. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. Therefore, αποφερεσθαι can be translated as "to carry oneself away" or "to remove oneself".

Description:
αποφερεσθαι is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to physically carrying something away, such as removing a piece of furniture from a room. It can also refer to removing oneself from a situation or a place, such as leaving a party or a city. In addition, it can be used metaphorically to indicate getting rid of something, such as removing a bad habit or a negative thought.
