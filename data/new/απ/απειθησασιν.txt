Word: απειθησασιν

Etymology: The word απειθησασιν comes from the Greek root words απειθέω (apeitheo) meaning "to disobey" and the suffix -σασιν (-sasin) which is the third person plural aorist active participle ending. Therefore, απειθησασιν means "they disobeyed" in Greek.

Grammar: απειθησασιν is a third person plural aorist active participle in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who disobeyed in the past. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: απειθησασιν is a Greek word that means "they disobeyed". It is often used in religious contexts to describe those who have disobeyed God or religious authorities. The word has a strong negative connotation and implies a willful and deliberate act of disobedience.
