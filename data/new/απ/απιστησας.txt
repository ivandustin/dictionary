Word: απιστησας

Etymology:
The word απιστησας (apistēsas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απιστια (apisteia), which means "unbelief" or "distrust." The verb form means "to disbelieve" or "to be faithless."

Grammar:
απιστησας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb απιστια (apisteia) and the aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
The word απιστησας (apistēsas) is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Mark 16:11, where it describes the disciples' initial disbelief in the resurrection of Jesus. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is faithless or disbelieving.
