The word "αποκοψον" is a verb in the imperative mood in Greek. It is derived from the verb "αποκόπτω" which means "to cut off" or "to sever". The word is formed by adding the imperative ending "-ον" to the stem "αποκόψ-". 

In terms of grammar, "αποκοψον" is a second person singular command, meaning it is used to give a direct order to someone. 

Example: "Αποκόψτον το νήμα" (Cut off the thread).
