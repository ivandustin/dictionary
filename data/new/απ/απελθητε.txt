The word "απελθητε" is a verb in the Greek language, which means "you (plural) go away" or "you (plural) depart". It is derived from the Greek word "απέρχομαι" (apérchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart". 

In terms of grammar, "απελθητε" is the second person plural aorist imperative form of the verb "απέρχομαι". The aorist tense in Greek refers to a completed action in the past, while the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Overall, "απελθητε" is a formal way of telling a group of people to leave or depart from a place.
