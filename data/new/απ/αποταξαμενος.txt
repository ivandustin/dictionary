The word "αποταξαμένος" (apotaxamenos) is a Greek verb in the past participle form. It comes from the verb "αποτάσσομαι" (apotassomai), which means "to withdraw" or "to resign". The prefix "απο-" (apo-) means "away from" or "off", while the root "τάσσω" (tasso) means "to arrange" or "to put in order". 

In terms of grammar, "αποταξαμένος" is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be used as an adjective to describe a person who has resigned or withdrawn from a position or responsibility. 

Example: Ο αποταξάμενος υπάλληλος δεν ήθελε να σχολιάσει την απόφασή του. (The resigned employee did not want to comment on his decision.)

Overall, "αποταξαμένος" is a word that describes someone who has voluntarily stepped down from a role or duty.
