Word: αποστολων

Etymology: The word αποστολων (apostolon) is derived from the Greek word αποστολή (apostolē), which means "mission" or "sending forth". In the context of Christianity, it refers to the twelve apostles who were chosen by Jesus to spread his teachings.

Grammar: αποστολων is the genitive plural form of απόστολος (apostolos), which means "apostle" or "messenger". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: αποστολων is a Greek word that refers to the apostles, the twelve disciples of Jesus who were chosen to spread his teachings and establish the Christian church. The word is often used in the context of the New Testament and early Christian history.
