Word: απεθανομεν

Etymology:
The word απεθανομεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θανατος (thanatos), which means "death". The prefix απο (apo) means "away from" or "separation". Therefore, απεθανομεν means "we died" or "we have died".

Grammar:
απεθανομεν is a first-person plural aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
απεθανομεν is a powerful and solemn word that refers to the act of dying. It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to reflect on the nature of mortality and the human condition. In Greek literature, it is used to describe the deaths of heroes and other important figures. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to talk about death and dying.
