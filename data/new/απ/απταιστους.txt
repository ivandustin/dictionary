Word: απταιστους

Etymology:
The word απταιστους (aptai̱stous) is derived from the Greek word "απταίστως" (aptáistos), which means "without fault" or "blameless". It is formed from the prefix "α-" (a-), which means "not", and the word "πταίω" (ptai̱o̱), which means "to stumble" or "to err".

Grammar:
απταιστους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, απταιστους is used to describe masculine plural nouns that are in the nominative, accusative, or vocative case.

Description:
The word απταιστους is used to describe someone or something that is faultless, blameless, or without error. It is often used in a moral or ethical sense to describe a person who is virtuous or righteous. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe God and Jesus Christ.
