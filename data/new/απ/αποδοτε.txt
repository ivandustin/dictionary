The word "αποδοτε" is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "ἀποδίδωμι" (apodidomi), which means "to give back, to return, to restore." 

In terms of grammar, "αποδοτε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "αποδίδωμι" (apodidomi). This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people, telling them to give back or return something. 

In terms of description, "αποδοτε" is a strong and direct command that is often used in formal or authoritative settings. It is commonly used in legal or business contexts, where there is a need to demand the return of something that is owed or to enforce a contractual obligation.
