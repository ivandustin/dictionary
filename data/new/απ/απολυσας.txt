Word: απολυσας

Etymology: απολυσας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απολυω (apoluo), which means "to release" or "to let go". The suffix -ας (-as) indicates that it is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar: απολυσας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb απολυω (apoluo). It is used to describe a completed action in the past, specifically when the subject (you) released or let go of something or someone.

Description: απολυσας can be translated to "you released" or "you let go". It is a verb that implies a deliberate action of releasing or letting go of something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as releasing a prisoner, letting go of a grudge, or dismissing an employee.
