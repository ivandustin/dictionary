Word: αποθεσθε

Etymology:
The word αποθεσθε comes from the Greek verb αποτιθημι (apotithemi), which means "to put away" or "to deposit". The prefix απο- (apo-) means "away from" or "off", while the root word τιθημι (tithemi) means "to put" or "to place".

Grammar:
αποθεσθε is a second person plural imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is derived from the verb αποτιθημι (apotithemi), which means "to put away" or "to deposit". The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests, and the aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
αποθεσθε is a command to a group of people to "put away" or "deposit" something. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly is being referred to.
