Word: απεριτμητοι

Etymology: The word απεριτμητοι comes from the Greek prefix α- (meaning "not") and the word περιτμητός (peritmitos) which means "circumcised". Therefore, απεριτμητοι means "uncircumcised".

Grammar: απεριτμητοι is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are not circumcised.

Description: απεριτμητοι is a term used in the Bible to describe non-Jewish people who were not circumcised. In the New Testament, it is used to refer to Gentiles who were not part of the Jewish faith. The term is also used in modern Greek to describe someone who is not circumcised.
