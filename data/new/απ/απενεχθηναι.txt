Word: απενεχθηναι

Etymology:
The word απενεχθηναι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from" or "off", and the verb ενεχθηναι (enechthēnai), meaning "to be accused" or "to be charged". Therefore, απενεχθηναι means "to be cleared of charges" or "to be acquitted".

Grammar:
απενεχθηναι is a passive verb in the aorist tense, indicating that the action of being cleared of charges has already taken place. The verb is in the infinitive form, which means that it is not conjugated to agree with a specific subject.

Description:
απενεχθηναι is a legal term that refers to the act of being cleared of charges or acquitted in a court of law. It is often used in the context of criminal trials, where a defendant is accused of a crime and must prove their innocence. The word implies that the accused was previously under suspicion or facing charges, but has since been exonerated.
