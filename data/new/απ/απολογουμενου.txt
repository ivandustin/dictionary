The word "απολογουμενου" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "απολογούμαι" (apologoumai), which means "to defend oneself" or "to make a defense." 

The word is formed by adding the present participle suffix "-μενος" (-menos) to the verb stem "απολογού" (apologou). The suffix "-μενος" indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or in progress.

In terms of grammar, "απολογουμενου" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. It can be translated as "of defending oneself" or "of making a defense."

Overall, "απολογουμενου" is a word that describes the ongoing action of defending oneself or making a defense.
