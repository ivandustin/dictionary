The word "απολλως" (apollōs) is a Greek adverb that means "completely" or "wholly." It is derived from the name of the Greek god Apollo, who was associated with music, poetry, prophecy, and healing. In terms of grammar, "απολλως" is an adverb that modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It can be used to emphasize the degree or extent of an action or quality. For example, "He was completely exhausted" could be translated as "Ήταν απολλως εξαντλημένος" (Ītan apollōs exantlēmenos) in Greek.
