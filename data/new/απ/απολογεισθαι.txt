Word: απολογεισθαι

Etymology: The word απολογεισθαι comes from the Greek verb απολογέομαι (apologeomai), which means "to defend oneself" or "to make a defense." It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb λογος (logos), meaning "word" or "reason."

Grammar: απολογεισθαι is a Greek verb in the present tense, middle or passive voice, and infinitive mood. It is a compound verb, consisting of the prefix απο- and the verb λογεισθαι (logeisthai), which means "to reason" or "to calculate."

Description: απολογεισθαι is a word that refers to the act of making a defense or justification for oneself. It is often used in legal contexts, where a person may need to defend themselves against accusations or charges. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any situation where a person needs to explain or justify their actions or beliefs.
