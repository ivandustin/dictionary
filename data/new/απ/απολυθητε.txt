Word: απολυθητε

Etymology: απολυθητε is a Greek word that comes from the verb απολύω (apolyo), which means "to release" or "to dismiss". The word is formed by adding the suffix -θητε (-thete) to the verb stem.

Grammar: απολυθητε is the second person plural form of the aorist passive imperative of the verb απολύω (apolyo). In other words, it is a command in the past tense, passive voice, and directed to a group of people.

Description: απολυθητε is a command that can be translated as "you all were released" or "you all should be released". It implies that a group of people were or should be set free from something. The context in which the word is used will determine the exact meaning.
