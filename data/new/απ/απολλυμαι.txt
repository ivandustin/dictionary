Word: απολλύμαι

Etymology:
The word απολλύμαι (apollymai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απόλλυσις (apollysis), which means "destruction" or "ruin". The verb απολλύμαι is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In other words, it is a reflexive verb.

Grammar:
απολλύμαι is a present tense verb in the middle voice. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Απολλύομαι (apollyomai) - I am being destroyed
- Απολλύσαι (apollysai) - You are being destroyed
- Απολλύται (apollytai) - He/She/It is being destroyed

Plural:
- Απολλυόμαστε (apollyomaste) - We are being destroyed
- Απολλύσθε (apollysthe) - You are being destroyed
- Απολλύνται (apollyntai) - They are being destroyed

Description:
The word απολλύμαι is often used in Greek mythology to describe the destruction or downfall of a hero or god. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of ruin or destruction. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a person who is in a state of despair or hopelessness.
