Word: απαντησιν

Etymology: The word απαντησιν comes from the Greek verb απαντάω (apantáo), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a common ending in Greek nouns that denotes action or result.

Grammar: απαντησιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: απαντησιν refers to the act of meeting or encountering someone or something. It can also be used to describe a response or answer to a question or statement. In modern Greek, the word απάντηση (apántisi) is more commonly used to mean "answer" or "reply".
