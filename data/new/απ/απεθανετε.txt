Word: απεθανετε

Etymology: απεθανετε is a Greek word that comes from the verb αποθνῄσκω (apothnēskō), which means "to die" or "to pass away". The word is formed by adding the suffix -τε (-te) to the verb stem.

Grammar: απεθανετε is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is a verb form that indicates a completed action in the past. The word is often translated as "you died" or "you have died".

Description: απεθανετε is a word that is used to describe the act of dying or passing away. It is a formal and solemn word that is often used in religious or philosophical contexts. In Greek culture, death was seen as a natural part of life, and the word απεθανετε reflects this belief. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe the act of dying.
