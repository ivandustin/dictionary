The word "απεδετο" is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology is derived from the prefix "απο-" meaning "away from" and the verb "δένω" meaning "to tie". Therefore, "απεδετο" means "untied" or "unfastened". 

In terms of grammar, "απεδετο" is in the past tense and indicative mood. It is conjugated from the verb "αποδένω" which means "to untie" or "to unfasten". 

In description, "απεδετο" can be used to describe the state of something that was previously tied or fastened but is now untied or unfastened. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or problem that has been resolved or untangled.
