Word: απηλθον

Etymology:
The word απηλθον (apēlthon) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ἔρχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come." The prefix απο- (apo-) means "away from" or "off," so απηλθον (apēlthon) can be translated as "I went away" or "I departed."

Grammar:
απηλθον (apēlthon) is the aorist tense of the Greek verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past. It is the first person singular form, meaning "I went away" or "I departed."

Description:
απηλθον (apēlthon) is a Greek verb that describes an action of leaving or departing from a place. It is often used in the New Testament to describe the departure of Jesus or his disciples from a particular location. The word can also be used in a metaphorical sense to describe a departure from a particular way of life or belief system.
