The word "αποστολον" (apostolon) is a Greek noun that means "apostle" or "messenger". It is derived from the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". In Greek grammar, "αποστολον" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive "αποστόλου" (apostolou) or the accusative "απόστολον" (apostolon). In Christian theology, the term "apostle" refers to the twelve disciples of Jesus Christ who were chosen to spread his teachings and establish the early Christian church.
