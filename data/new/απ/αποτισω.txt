The word "αποτισω" (apotiso) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "away from" or "off", and the verb "τίνω" (tino), meaning "to pay". Therefore, "αποτισω" means "to pay off" or "to settle a debt".

Grammar: "αποτισω" is a first-person singular present tense verb, meaning "I pay off" or "I settle a debt". It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs that end in "-ω" (o).

Description: The word "αποτισω" is commonly used in financial contexts, such as paying off a loan or debt. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to settling a score or fulfilling a duty.
