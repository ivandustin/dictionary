Word: απειθειαν

Etymology: απειθειαν is a Greek word that comes from the root word απειθεία (apeitheia), which means disobedience or lack of compliance. The suffix -αν (-an) is added to form the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a sentence.

Grammar: απειθειαν is a noun in the accusative case, which means it is used as the direct object of a sentence. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their function in a sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive.

Description: απειθειαν refers to disobedience or noncompliance with authority or rules. It can be used to describe a person's behavior or attitude, or to refer to a specific act of disobedience. The word is often used in a negative context, as disobedience is generally seen as a negative trait.
