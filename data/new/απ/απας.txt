Word: απας

Etymology:
The word απας (apas) is a Greek word that means "all" or "every". It is derived from the Greek word "πᾶς" (pas), which means "all" or "every".

Grammar:
In Greek, απας is an adjective that is used to describe a noun. It is declined like any other adjective in Greek, and its form changes depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
απας is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe a group of people or things, or to indicate that something applies to all members of a group. For example, απας ο λαος (apas o laos) means "all the people", while απας ο ουρανος (apas o ouranos) means "the whole sky". It can also be used to emphasize the completeness or totality of something, as in απας ο κοσμος (apas o kosmos), which means "the entire world".
