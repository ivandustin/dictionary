Etymology: The word "απέχω" comes from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb "έχω" (echo), meaning "to have". 

Grammar: "απεχουσι" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs. It is formed by adding the present tense endings to the stem "απεχ-". 

Description: The word "απεχουσι" is used to describe the action of a group of people who are keeping away from or abstaining from something. For example, "Οι μοναχοί απεχουσι από τα κρέατα" (Oi monachoi apechousi apo ta kreatα) means "The monks abstain from meat".
