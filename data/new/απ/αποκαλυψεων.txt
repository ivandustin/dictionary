The word "αποκαλυψεων" (apokalypseon) is a Greek noun that means "revelation" or "unveiling". It is derived from the verb "αποκαλυπτω" (apokalypto), which means "to reveal" or "to uncover". In grammar, it is a genitive plural noun, which means it is used to indicate possession or relationship to another noun. 

In the context of religion, "αποκαλυψεων" is often used to refer to the biblical book of Revelation, which is also known as the Apocalypse. This book is believed to contain prophecies about the end of the world and the second coming of Jesus Christ. 

Overall, "αποκαλυψεων" is a word that conveys a sense of uncovering or revealing something that was previously hidden or unknown.
