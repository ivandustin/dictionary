Word: απεστραφησαν

Etymology: απεστράφησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix από (apó), which means "away from," and the verb στρέφω (stréfo), which means "to turn." Therefore, απεστράφησαν means "they turned away from."

Grammar: απεστράφησαν is the third person plural, aorist tense, passive voice of the verb αποστρέφω (apostréfo), which means "to turn away from." In this form, the subject (they) receives the action of the verb (turned away from).

Description: απεστράφησαν is a verb that describes an action of turning away from something or someone. It implies a deliberate decision to distance oneself from a person, situation, or idea. The aorist tense indicates that the action was completed in the past, and the passive voice suggests that the subject did not initiate the action but rather received it.
