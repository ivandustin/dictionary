The word "απεκοψεν" (apekopsen) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb "κοψω" (kopso), meaning "to cut." 

Grammar: "απεκοψεν" is in the aorist tense, third person singular, indicative mood. 

Description: The word means "he/she/it cut off" or "he/she/it severed." It is often used in a literal sense, such as cutting off a limb or a piece of clothing, but can also be used figuratively to mean ending a relationship or connection.
