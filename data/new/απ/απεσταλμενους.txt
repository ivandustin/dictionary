Word: απεσταλμένους

Etymology:
The word απεσταλμένους (apostalmenous) is a Greek word that comes from the verb αποστέλλω (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to send forth". The word is derived from the prefix από (apo), which means "away from", and the verb στέλλω (stello), which means "to send".

Grammar:
απεσταλμένους is the plural form of the adjective απεσταλμένος (apostalmenos), which means "sent" or "dispatched". It is in the masculine gender and accusative case, and agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description:
The word απεσταλμένους is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the apostles, who were sent by Jesus to spread his teachings. In Greek, the word is often used to describe messengers or envoys who are sent on a mission. The word implies a sense of purpose and urgency, as those who are sent are expected to carry out a specific task or message.
