Word: απηλασεν

Etymology: απηλασεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απαλλάσσω (apallasso), which means "to set free" or "to release". The prefix απο (apo) means "away from" or "off", so απηλασεν can be translated as "he/she/it set free" or "he/she/it released".

Grammar: απηλασεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb απαλλάσσω (apallasso). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, voice, and mood. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: απηλασεν can be translated as "he/she/it set free" or "he/she/it released". The verb implies that someone or something was previously held captive or restrained, and has now been set free. The context of the sentence would determine who or what was released.
