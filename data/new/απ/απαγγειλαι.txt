Word: απαγγειλαι

Etymology: απαγγειλαι is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb αγγελλω (angello), meaning "to announce" or "to report." Therefore, απαγγειλαι means "to report away from" or "to announce from a distance."

Grammar: απαγγειλαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to report or announce something.

Description: απαγγειλαι is a verb that is often used in formal or official contexts, such as in legal or political settings. It is used to instruct someone to report or announce something, usually from a distance or to a specific audience. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to declare" or "to proclaim."
