The word "απαγετε" (apagete) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "απάγω" (apago), which means "to lead away" or "to take away". 

Grammar: "απαγετε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "απάγω". This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people, telling them to "lead away" or "take away" something. 

Description: The word "απαγετε" is often used in a figurative sense, meaning to remove or eliminate something negative or unwanted. It can also be used in a literal sense, such as telling someone to physically remove an object or person from a certain location.
