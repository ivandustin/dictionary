Word: απεχεσθαι

Etymology: απεχεσθαι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb εχειν (echein), meaning "to have." Therefore, απεχεσθαι means "to be away from" or "to abstain from."

Grammar: απεχεσθαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive mood indicates that the verb is not being used to express a specific tense or subject, but rather as a verbal noun.

Description: απεχεσθαι is a Greek verb that means "to abstain from" or "to keep away from." It is often used in the context of avoiding something that is harmful or undesirable. For example, one might use απεχεσθαι to describe abstaining from alcohol or unhealthy foods. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to describe keeping a distance from someone or something.
