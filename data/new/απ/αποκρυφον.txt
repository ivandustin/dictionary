Word: αποκρυφον

Etymology: The word αποκρυφον comes from the Greek word απόκρυφος (apokryphos), which means "hidden" or "obscure". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb κρύπτω (krypto), meaning "to hide".

Grammar: αποκρυφον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is often used as an adjective to describe something that is hidden or obscure.

Description: αποκρυφον refers to something that is hidden or secret, often with a negative connotation. It can also refer to something that is not widely known or understood. In some contexts, it can be used to describe something that is mysterious or mystical.
