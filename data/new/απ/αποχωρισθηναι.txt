Word: αποχωρισθηναι

Etymology: απο- (apo-, prefix meaning "away from") + χωρίζω (chorizo, "to separate") + -θηναι (-thenai, suffix indicating passive voice). Therefore, αποχωρισθηναι means "to be separated from" or "to depart from".

Grammar: αποχωρισθηναι is a verb in the aorist passive infinitive form. It is derived from the ancient Greek verb χωρίζω (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". The prefix απο- (apo-) adds the meaning of "away from", making the verb mean "to be separated from" or "to depart from".

Description: αποχωρισθηναι is a word that describes the act of being separated from something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as when someone leaves a place or when two things are separated from each other. The word is commonly used in religious contexts, such as when referring to the separation of the soul from the body after death.
