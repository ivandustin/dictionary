Word: αποστειλας

Etymology:
The word αποστειλας (aposteilas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αποστελλω (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". The word is formed by adding the suffix -ας (-as) to the stem of the verb.

Grammar:
αποστειλας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb αποστελλω (apostello). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action, and the indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
αποστειλας can be translated to English as "you sent" or "you dispatched". It is a specific past action that has been completed. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as sending a message, a package, or a person. The verb is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is often used to describe the sending of messengers or apostles.
