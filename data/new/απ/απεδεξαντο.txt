Word: απεδεξαντο

Etymology: απεδεξαντο is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb δεχομαι (dechomai), meaning "to receive." Therefore, απεδεξαντο means "they refused" or "they rejected."

Grammar: απεδεξαντο is the third person plural aorist middle voice form of the verb αποδεχομαι (apodechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept." The aorist tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject was both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description: απεδεξαντο is a Greek verb that means "they refused" or "they rejected." It is a strong and decisive word that implies a deliberate and conscious decision to not accept something. The middle voice form suggests that the subject was actively involved in the decision-making process and had control over the outcome. απεδεξαντο can be used in a variety of contexts, from personal relationships to political negotiations, to express a clear and final rejection of an offer, proposal, or idea.
