Word: απεβλεπεν

Etymology:
The word απεβλεπεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βλεπω (blepo), which means "to see". The prefix απο (apo) means "away from" or "off", so απεβλεπεν can be translated as "to look away" or "to turn one's gaze from".

απεβλεπεν is a third-person singular imperfect active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is in the indicative mood, which indicates a statement of fact, and in the imperfect tense, which indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

απεβλεπεν can be translated as "he/she was looking away" or "he/she turned their gaze from". It describes an action of someone intentionally looking away from something or someone.
