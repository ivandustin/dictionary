Word: απωθεισθε

Etymology:
The word απωθεισθε (apothesthe) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απωθεω (apothéō), which means "to push away" or "to repel." It is formed by adding the suffix -σθε (-sthe) to the stem of the verb.

Grammar:
απωθεισθε is a second-person plural present passive imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to be pushed away or repelled. The passive voice indicates that the action is being done to the subject, rather than the subject doing the action.

Description:
The word απωθεισθε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Acts (13:46). In this context, it is used by Paul and Barnabas to rebuke the Jews who had rejected their message of salvation through Jesus Christ.
