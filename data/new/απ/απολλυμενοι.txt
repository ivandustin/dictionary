Word: απολλυμενοι

Etymology: απολλυμενοι is the present participle of the Greek verb απόλλυμι (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". The word is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb ολλυμι (ollymi), meaning "to destroy".

Grammar: απολλυμενοι is a masculine plural nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently in the process of being destroyed or perishing.

Description: απολλυμενοι is a word that conveys a sense of imminent destruction or doom. It is often used in Greek literature and mythology to describe characters who are facing a tragic fate. In modern usage, the word can be used to describe any situation or group of people that is in danger of being destroyed or wiped out.
