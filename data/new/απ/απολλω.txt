The word "απολλω" (apollō) is a Greek verb that means "to destroy" or "to kill". It is derived from the name of the Greek god Apollo, who was associated with death and destruction in addition to his more positive attributes such as music, poetry, and healing. 

In terms of grammar, "απολλω" is a first-conjugation verb in the present tense. Its stem is "απολλυ-", and it follows the regular pattern of first-conjugation verbs in Greek. 

Overall, "απολλω" is a word with a strong and somewhat ominous connotation, reflecting its association with the destructive aspects of the god Apollo.
