Word: απωλλυντο

Etymology:
The word απωλλυντο (apollynto) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word απόλλυμι (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". The suffix -ντο (-nto) is a passive ending, indicating that the action of the verb is being done to the subject.

Grammar:
απωλλυντο (apollynto) is a third-person plural, passive, indicative, aorist verb in the Greek language. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is receiving the action.

Description:
The word απωλλυντο (apollynto) means "they were destroyed" or "they perished" in English. It is often used in the context of a catastrophic event or a great loss. The word can be found in ancient Greek literature, such as in the works of Homer and Herodotus.
