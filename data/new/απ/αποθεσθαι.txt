Word: αποθεσθαι

Etymology:
The word αποθεσθαι comes from the Greek verb ἀποτίθημι (apotithemi), which means "to put away" or "to deposit". It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix ἀπό (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb τίθημι (tithemi), meaning "to put" or "to place".

Grammar:
αποθεσθαι is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and middle voice. It is the infinitive form of the verb, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
αποθεσθαι is a Greek verb that means "to put away" or "to deposit". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as depositing money in a bank, putting away clothes in a closet, or setting aside food for later.
