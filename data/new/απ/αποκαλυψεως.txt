The word "αποκαλυψεως" (apokalypseos) is a Greek noun that means "revelation" or "unveiling". It is derived from the verb "αποκαλυπτω" (apokalypto), which means "to reveal" or "to uncover". In Greek mythology, the word was used to describe the revealing of hidden knowledge or secrets by the gods.

In terms of grammar, "αποκαλυψεως" is a genitive singular noun, meaning it is used to indicate possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun. It is often used in the context of the Christian Bible, specifically the Book of Revelation, which is also known as the "Apocalypse".

Overall, "αποκαλυψεως" is a significant word in Greek language and culture, and has been used in various contexts throughout history.
