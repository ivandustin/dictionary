Word: απειρος

Etymology:
The word απειρος (apeiros) comes from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" or "without" and the word "peira" meaning "experience" or "trial". Therefore, απειρος means "without experience" or "untested".

Grammar:
απειρος is an adjective in Greek, and it can be used to describe a person, an object, or a situation. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
απειρος can be used to describe someone who lacks experience or knowledge in a particular field. It can also be used to describe something that is untested or untried. In mathematics, απειρος is used to represent infinity.
