Word: απεστησαν

Etymology:
The word απεστησαν is derived from the Greek verb αποσταίνω (apostaino), which means "to revolt" or "to rebel". It is formed from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb σταίνω (staino), meaning "to stand".

Grammar:
απεστησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb αποσταίνω (apostaino). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
απεστησαν means "they revolted" or "they rebelled". It is a verb that describes a group of people rising up against a government or authority. The word can be used in a historical context, such as the Greek War of Independence, or in a contemporary context, such as a political uprising.
