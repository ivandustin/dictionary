Word: απατης

Etymology:
The word απατης (apates) comes from the Greek verb απαταω (apatao), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb αταω (atao), meaning "to deceive".

Grammar:
απατης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender.

Description:
απατης refers to deception or fraud. It can also mean a trick or a deceitful act. The word is often used in legal contexts to describe fraudulent behavior or actions. In everyday language, it can refer to any kind of deception or trickery.
