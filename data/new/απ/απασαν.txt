The word "απασαν" (apasan) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It comes from the root word "πασχω" (pascho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." 

In its aorist form, "απασαν" can be translated as "suffered" or "experienced." It is often used in the context of describing a past event or experience, such as "I suffered a great loss" or "He experienced a moment of clarity." 

In terms of grammar, "απασαν" is a third-person singular verb in the aorist tense. It can be conjugated to match the subject of the sentence, such as "απασαν εγώ" (apasan ego) meaning "I suffered" or "απασαν αυτός" (apasan aftos) meaning "he suffered." 

Overall, "απασαν" is a versatile verb that can be used to describe a wide range of past experiences or events.
