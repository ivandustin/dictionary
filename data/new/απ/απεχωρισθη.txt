Word: απεχωρισθη

Etymology: απεχωρισθη is a Greek word that comes from the verb αποχωρίζομαι (apochorizomai), which means "to separate" or "to divide".

Grammar: απεχωρισθη is a third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb αποχωρίζομαι (apochorizomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon.

Description: απεχωρισθη can be translated to English as "was separated" or "was divided". It is often used in a religious context to describe the separation of the righteous from the wicked, or the separation of the soul from the body after death.
