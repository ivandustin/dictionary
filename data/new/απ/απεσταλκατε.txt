Word: απεσταλκατε

Etymology: απο- (apo-) meaning "away from" and στελλω (stello) meaning "to send". Therefore, απεσταλκατε (apestalkate) means "you have been sent away".

Grammar: απεσταλκατε is the second person plural perfect indicative active form of the Greek verb αποστελλω (apostello), which means "to send away". The perfect tense indicates that the action was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description: απεσταλκατε is a verb in the Greek language that is used to describe the act of sending someone or something away. It is a formal and polite way of expressing this action, and is often used in official or religious contexts. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a sense of being removed or separated from a particular situation or group.
