Word: απηντησαν

Etymology: The word απηντησαν is derived from the Greek verb απαντώ (apantó), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". The prefix απ- (ap-) means "away from" or "off", while the root word αντί (antí) means "against" or "opposite". Therefore, απηντησαν can be translated as "they met" or "they encountered".

Grammar: απηντησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb απαντώ (apantó). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: απηντησαν is a Greek verb that describes a past action of meeting or encountering. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and in literature. The word can be conjugated in different tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different meanings and nuances.
