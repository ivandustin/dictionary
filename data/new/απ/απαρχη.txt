Word: απαρχή

Etymology:
The word απαρχή (aparchi) comes from the ancient Greek verb απαρχω (aparchō), which means "to begin" or "to initiate". In ancient times, απαρχή referred to the first fruits or crops that were offered to the gods as a sign of gratitude and respect.

Grammar:
απαρχή is a feminine noun in Greek, and its plural form is απαρχές (aparches). It is often used in the singular form, and it can be used with definite or indefinite articles, as well as with adjectives and other modifiers.

Description:
απαρχή is a term that is often used to refer to the beginning or origin of something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as the first fruits of a harvest, or in a figurative sense, such as the beginning of a new project or the start of a new relationship.
