Word: αποδοκιμασθηναι

Etymology: απο- (apo-, prefix meaning "away from") + δοκιμάζω (dokimazo, "to test, to approve") + -σθηναι (-sthenai, passive infinitive suffix). Therefore, αποδοκιμασθηναι means "to be disapproved, rejected, or condemned".

Grammar: αποδοκιμασθηναι is a passive infinitive verb form in Ancient Greek. It belongs to the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: αποδοκιμασθηναι is a word that expresses the idea of being rejected or disapproved. It is often used in a moral or ethical sense, to describe a person or action that has been condemned by society or a group of people. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to describe a situation where someone is being booed or jeered at.
