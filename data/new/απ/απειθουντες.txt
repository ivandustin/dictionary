The word "απειθουντες" (apeithountes) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "disobeying" or "being disobedient". It is derived from the Greek word "απειθεια" (apeitheia), which means "disobedience" or "unwillingness to obey". In terms of grammar, it is a present participle verb, which means it is used to describe an ongoing action or state of being.
