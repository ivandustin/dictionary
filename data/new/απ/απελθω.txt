Word: απελθω

Etymology:
The word απελθω (apeltho) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απο (apo) which means "away from" and the verb ερχομαι (erchomai) which means "to come". Therefore, απελθω means "to go away" or "to depart".

Grammar:
απελθω is a first aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Απηλθον (apelthon) - I went away
- Απηλθες (apelthes) - You went away
- Απηλθεν (apelthen) - He/she/it went away

Plural:
- Απηλθομεν (apelthomen) - We went away
- Απηλθετε (apelthete) - You (all) went away
- Απηλθον (apelthon) - They went away

Description:
απελθω is a common Greek verb that is used to describe someone leaving or departing from a place. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone physically leaves a location, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone leaves a job or a relationship. The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus leaving a place or departing from his disciples.
