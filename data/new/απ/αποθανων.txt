Word: αποθανων

Etymology:
The word αποθανων is derived from the Greek verb αποθνῄσκω (apothnēskō), which means "to die" or "to pass away". The word is formed from the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from", and the verb θνῄσκω (thnēskō), which means "to die".

Grammar:
αποθανων is the present participle of the verb αποθνῄσκω (apothnēskō). As a participle, it functions as an adjective and describes a noun or pronoun. In this case, it describes a person who is in the process of dying or passing away.

Description:
αποθανων is a word that describes the state of being in the process of dying or passing away. It is often used in a medical context to describe a patient who is terminally ill or in critical condition. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the process of decay or decline, such as the death of a language or culture.
