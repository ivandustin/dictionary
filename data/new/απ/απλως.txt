Word: απλως

Etymology: απλως is a Greek adverb that comes from the adjective απλός (aplos), meaning "simple" or "plain."

Grammar: απλως is an adverb that modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is invariable and does not change form.

Description: απλως is used to indicate simplicity, plainness, or straightforwardness. It can be translated as "simply," "just," or "plainly." For example, "Απλώς το κάνω" (aplos to kano) means "I'm just doing it." It can also be used to indicate a lack of complexity or sophistication, as in "Αυτό το φόρεμα είναι απλό" (afto to forema einai aplo), meaning "This dress is simple."
