Word: απων

Etymology:
The word απων (apōn) is derived from the Greek prefix α- (a-) meaning "not" and the verb πων (pōn) meaning "working" or "doing". Therefore, απων (apōn) means "not working" or "inactive".

Description:
απων (apōn) is an adjective that describes something or someone that is not working or inactive. It can be used to describe a person who is lazy or unproductive, or an object that is broken or not functioning properly. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was sometimes referred to as απων (apōn) because he was associated with the sun, which is inactive during the night.
