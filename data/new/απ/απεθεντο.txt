Word: απεθεντο

Etymology:
The word απεθεντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αποτιθημι (apotithemi), which means "to put away" or "to deposit". The prefix απο (apo) means "away from" or "off", and the verb ending -εντο (-ento) indicates the past tense.

Grammar:
απεθεντο is the third person plural past tense form of the verb αποτιθημι (apotithemi). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is acting upon itself or is affected by the action.

Description:
απεθεντο can be translated to English as "they put away" or "they deposited". It describes a completed action in the past where a group of people put something away or deposited something. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was put away or deposited.
