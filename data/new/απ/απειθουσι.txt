The word "απειθουσι" (apeithousi) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "απειθέω" (apeitheo), which means "to disobey" or "to be disobedient." 

In terms of grammar, "απειθουσι" is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" in Greek. 

Overall, "απειθουσι" can be translated to mean "they disobey" or "they are disobedient."
