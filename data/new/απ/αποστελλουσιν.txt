Word: αποστελλουσιν

Etymology: 
The word αποστελλουσιν (apostellousin) is derived from the Greek verb αποστελλω (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to send forth". The word is formed from the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from", and the verb στελλω (stello), which means "to send".

Grammar:
αποστελλουσιν is a present tense verb in Greek, which means "they send" or "they are sending". It is in the active voice and the indicative mood. The verb is conjugated in the third person plural, which means that it refers to a group of people who are doing the action of sending.

Description:
αποστελλουσιν is a common Greek verb that is used in various contexts. It can refer to the act of sending a message, a letter, or a package. It can also refer to the act of sending someone on a mission or a task. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the sending of the apostles by Jesus to preach the gospel. Overall, the word conveys the idea of sending something or someone away from one place to another.
