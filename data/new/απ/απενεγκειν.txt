Word: απενεγκειν

Etymology:
The word απενεγκειν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: από (apo) meaning "away from" and φέρω (fero) meaning "to carry". Therefore, απενεγκειν means "to carry away from".

Grammar:
απενεγκειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to a specific tense or person. It is often used in combination with auxiliary verbs to form different tenses and moods.

Description:
απενεγκειν is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to physically carrying something away from a place, as well as metaphorically carrying away something abstract, such as a burden or a problem. It can also be used in a legal context to mean "to bring a case to court". Overall, απενεγκειν is a useful verb that can convey a range of meanings depending on the context in which it is used.
