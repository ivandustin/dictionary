Word: απειθειαι

Etymology: The word απειθειαι (apeitheiai) is derived from the Greek word απειθής (apeithēs), which means disobedient or rebellious. The suffix -εια (-eia) is added to form a plural noun, meaning disobediences or rebellions.

Grammar: απειθειαι is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word απειθειαι is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: απειθειαι refers to acts of disobedience or rebellion. It can be used to describe a person's behavior or actions, as well as a group's collective resistance to authority or rules. The word has a negative connotation and is often associated with defiance and insubordination.
