Word: απορεισθαι

Etymology:
The word απορεισθαι comes from the Greek verb απορεω (aporeo), which means "to be at a loss, to be perplexed, to be in doubt". It is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), which means "away from" or "off", and the verb ρεω (reo), which means "to flow".

Grammar:
απορεισθαι is a verb in the passive voice, present tense, infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -σθαι (-sthai) to the stem απορει- (aporei-).

Description:
απορεισθαι is a Greek verb that describes a state of being perplexed or in doubt. It is often used to express a feeling of uncertainty or confusion about a particular situation or problem. The verb is commonly used in philosophical and intellectual contexts, as well as in everyday conversation.
