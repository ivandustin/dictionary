Word: αποθανοντι

Etymology: αποθανοντι is a Greek word that comes from the verb αποθνῄσκω (apothnēskō), which means "to die" or "to pass away". The word is in the dative case, which indicates the indirect object of the verb.

Grammar: αποθανοντι is a third-person singular form of the aorist active participle of the verb αποθνῄσκω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the active participle describes the subject as performing the action.

Description: αποθανοντι can be translated as "to the one who died" or "to the deceased". It is often used in Greek Orthodox funeral services as a way of referring to the person who has passed away. The word carries a sense of respect and reverence for the deceased.
