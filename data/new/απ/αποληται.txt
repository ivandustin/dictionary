The word "αποληται" (apoleitai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "απολύω" (apoluo), which means "to release" or "to set free". 

In terms of grammar, "αποληται" is a passive voice verb, indicating that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it. 

In modern Greek, "αποληται" is often used in legal contexts to refer to someone who has been acquitted or released from custody. 

Overall, the word "αποληται" carries a sense of freedom and release, both in its etymology and its modern usage.
