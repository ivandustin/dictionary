Word: απολυσον

Etymology: απολυσον is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απολύω (apolýo), which means "to release" or "to dismiss". It is formed by adding the suffix -σον (-son) to the stem of the verb.

Grammar: απολυσον is a second person singular aorist imperative active form of the verb απολύω (apolýo). This means that it is a command directed at one person to perform an action in the past. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed, while the imperative mood indicates that it is a command.

Description: απολυσον is a command to release or dismiss someone or something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a legal or employment setting, or in a personal relationship. The word carries a sense of finality and authority, as it is a direct order to take action.
