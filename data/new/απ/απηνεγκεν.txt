The word "απηνεγκεν" is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "ἀποφέρω" (apophérō), which means "to carry away, to bear off". In modern Greek, "απηνεγκεν" is the aorist tense of the verb "αποφέρω" and it means "he/she/it carried away, bore off".

In terms of grammar, "απηνεγκεν" is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, completed at a specific point in time.

In terms of description, "απηνεγκεν" can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone carrying away an object, or figuratively, to describe someone taking away a burden or responsibility.
