The word "αποθνηισκομεν" (apothnēiskomen) is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, first person plural form. It is derived from the root word "θνησκω" (thnēskō), which means "to die". The prefix "απο" (apo) means "away from" or "off". Therefore, "αποθνηισκομεν" can be translated to mean "we are dying off" or "we are perishing". 

In terms of grammar, "αποθνηισκομεν" is a regular verb in the present tense, meaning it follows the standard conjugation patterns for verbs in Greek. 

Overall, "αποθνηισκομεν" is a word that conveys a sense of mortality and impermanence.
