Word: απαρνησομαι

Etymology:
The word απαρνησομαι (aparnesomai) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix απο- (apo-) meaning "away from" and the verb αρνεομαι (arneomai) meaning "to deny". Therefore, απαρνησομαι means "to deny or disown something or someone".

Grammar:
απαρνησομαι is a first-person singular present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It belongs to the aorist tense, which refers to an action that has been completed in the past. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
απαρνησομαι is a strong verb that is used in the New Testament to describe Peter's denial of Jesus three times before the rooster crowed. It is also used in other contexts to describe the act of denying or disowning someone or something. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to describe the act of renouncing or rejecting something.
