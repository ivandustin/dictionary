Word: απηνεγκαν

Etymology: The word απηνεγκαν is derived from the Greek verb αποφέρω (apoféro), which means "to carry away" or "to bear off". The prefix απο- (apo-) means "away from" or "off", while the verb φέρω (féro) means "to carry" or "to bear".

Grammar: απηνεγκαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb αποφέρω (apoféro). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact or reality, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: απηνεγκαν means "they carried away" or "they bore off" in English. It describes a past action where a group of people carried something away from a particular location. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was carried away.
