Word: απολογουμαι

Etymology: απολογουμαι comes from the Greek word απολογία (apologia), which means "defense" or "justification". The word is derived from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb λόγος (logos), meaning "word" or "reason". Therefore, απολογουμαι literally means "to speak away from" or "to give a reason for".

Grammar: απολογουμαι is a reflexive verb in Greek, meaning that the subject performs the action on itself. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

Εγώ απολογούμαι (I defend myself)
Εσύ απολογείσαι (You defend yourself)
Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό απολογείται (He/She/It defends himself/herself/itself)
Εμείς απολογούμαστε (We defend ourselves)
Εσείς απολογείστε (You defend yourselves)
Αυτοί/Αυτές απολογούνται (They defend themselves)

Description: απολογουμαι is a verb that is commonly used in legal or formal contexts to express the act of defending oneself or justifying one's actions. It can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to apologize" or "to make excuses".
