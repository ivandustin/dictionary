The word "απηγγελη" (apengeli) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "απαγγέλλω" (apangello), which means "to announce" or "to report". 

Grammar: "απηγγελη" is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "απαγγέλλω". In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. 

Description: "απηγγελη" means "he/she/it announced" or "he/she/it reported". It is used to describe a past action of someone making an announcement or report. For example, "Ο διευθυντής απηγγελε την απόφαση" (O diethyntis apengeli tin apofasi) means "The director announced the decision".
