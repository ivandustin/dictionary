Word: αποταξασθαι

Etymology: απο- (apo-, prefix meaning "away from") + ταξις (taxis, "arrangement, order") + -ασθαι (-asthai, suffix indicating a middle voice verb form). Therefore, αποταξασθαι means "to arrange oneself away from" or "to withdraw oneself from an arrangement/order".

Grammar: αποταξασθαι is a middle voice verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description: αποταξασθαι is a Greek verb that describes the act of withdrawing oneself from an arrangement or order. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as military orders, social obligations, or personal commitments. The verb emphasizes the idea of actively removing oneself from a situation, rather than simply passively not participating.
