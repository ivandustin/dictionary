Word: απαρασκευαστους

Etymology: The word απαρασκευαστους comes from the Greek prefix α- (a-), meaning "not," and the word παρασκευή (paraskeví), meaning "preparation" or "readiness." The suffix -ος (-os) is added to make it an adjective.

Grammar: απαρασκευαστους is a Greek adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are unprepared or not ready for something.

Description: απαρασκευαστους is a word that describes a lack of preparation or readiness. It can be used to describe a group of people who are unprepared for a task or situation, or who have not made the necessary arrangements or plans. The word can also be used to describe a lack of resources or equipment needed for a particular task.
