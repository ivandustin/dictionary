Word: αποκτανθωσιν

Etymology: The word αποκτανθωσιν is derived from the Greek verb αποκτείνω (apokteínō), which means "to kill" or "to slay". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a third person plural ending, indicating that the word is in the subjunctive mood.

Grammar: αποκτανθωσιν is a third person plural present subjunctive form of the verb αποκτείνω (apokteínō). The subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. In this case, the word could be translated as "they may be killed" or "let them be killed".

Description: αποκτανθωσιν is a Greek word that refers to the act of killing or slaying in a subjunctive form. It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to express the idea of sacrifice or martyrdom.
