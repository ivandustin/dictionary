Word: απολλύται

Etymology: απολλύται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word απόλλυμι (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". It is derived from the name of the Greek god Apollo, who was associated with destruction and death.

Grammar: απολλύται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb απόλλυμι. It is in the middle or passive voice, indicating that the subject is either performing the action on itself or is being acted upon by an external force.

Description: απολλύται can be translated to mean "is being destroyed" or "is perishing". It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe the gradual decay or decline of something, such as a civilization or a relationship. In Greek mythology, the word is associated with the downfall of heroes and the destruction of cities.
