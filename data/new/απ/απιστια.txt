Word: απιστία (apostía)

Etymology: The word απιστία comes from the Greek prefix α- (a-) meaning "not" and the word πίστη (písti) meaning "faith" or "trust". Therefore, απιστία means "lack of faith" or "distrust".

Grammar: απιστία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is απιστίες (apostíes).

Description: απιστία refers to the lack of trust or faith in someone or something. It can also refer to infidelity or unfaithfulness in a romantic relationship. The word is often used in a negative context, as it implies a betrayal of trust.
