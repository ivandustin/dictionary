Word: απεσπάσεν

Etymology:
The word απεσπάσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σπάω (spao), which means "to tear" or "to pull apart". The prefix απο- (apo) means "away from" or "off", so απεσπάσεν can be translated as "to tear away" or "to pull off".

Grammar:
απεσπάσεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb αποσπάω (apospao), which means "to tear away" or "to detach". In this form, it indicates that the action of tearing away or detaching has been completed in the past.

Description:
απεσπάσεν is a verb that describes an action of forcefully separating something from its original position. It can be used in a literal sense, such as tearing a piece of paper or pulling a plant out of the ground, or in a figurative sense, such as separating oneself from a group or breaking away from a habit. The word has a strong connotation of force and effort, as the action of tearing or pulling requires physical strength.
