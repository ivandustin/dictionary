The word "απολω" (apolo) is a Greek verb that means "to destroy" or "to perish". 

Etymology: The word comes from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), which means "away from" or "off", and the verb "λω" (lo), which means "to destroy". 

Grammar: "Απολω" is a first conjugation verb in the present tense, active voice. Its conjugation in the present tense is as follows:

- Απολώ (apolo) - I destroy
- Απολείς (apoleis) - You destroy
- Απολεί (apolei) - He/she/it destroys
- Απολούμε (apoloume) - We destroy
- Απολείτε (apoleite) - You (plural) destroy
- Απολούν (apoloun) - They destroy

Description: "Απολω" is a strong and forceful verb that is often used to describe the destruction of something or someone. It can be used in a literal sense, such as the destruction of a building or a city, or in a figurative sense, such as the destruction of a relationship or a reputation.
