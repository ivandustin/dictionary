Word: απειθης

Etymology: The word απειθης comes from the Greek word απειθής (apeithēs), which is derived from the prefix α- (a-) meaning "not" and the word πείθω (peithō) meaning "to persuade". Therefore, απειθης means "disobedient" or "unwilling to obey".

Grammar: απειθης is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: απειθης is a word that describes someone who is disobedient or unwilling to obey. It can be used to describe a person who refuses to follow rules or orders, or who is rebellious in nature. The word can also be used in a broader sense to describe anything that goes against established norms or expectations.
