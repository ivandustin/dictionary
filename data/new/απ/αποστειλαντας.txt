Word: αποστειλαντας

Etymology: αποστείλαντας is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from," and the verb στέλλω (stello), meaning "to send." Therefore, αποστείλαντας means "to send away."

Grammar: αποστείλαντας is the aorist participle of the verb αποστέλλω (apostello), which means "to send away." The aorist tense in Greek refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, completed at a specific point in time. The participle form is used to modify a noun or pronoun, and it can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description: αποστείλαντας is a Greek verb that means "having sent away." It is often used in the context of sending someone or something away to a different location. The aorist participle form of the verb is commonly used in Greek literature and is often translated as "sent" or "having sent."
