Word: απεχεσθε

Etymology: απεχεσθε is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix απο- (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb εχω (echo), meaning "to have" or "to hold." Together, απεχεσθε means "to hold oneself away from" or "to keep oneself distant from."

Grammar: απεχεσθε is a second person plural present imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to keep themselves away from something.

Description: απεχεσθε is a strong and direct command in Greek, telling a group of people to keep themselves away from something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as warning people to stay away from a dangerous area or telling someone to keep their distance from a particular person or situation.
