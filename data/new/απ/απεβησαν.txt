Word: απεβησαν

Etymology:
The word απεβησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: από (apo) meaning "away from" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go". Therefore, απεβησαν means "they went away from".

Grammar:
απεβησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb βαίνω (baino) in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
απεβησαν is a verb that describes an action of a group of people going away from a certain place. It is a past tense verb that indicates that the action has already happened. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in a historical narrative or in a description of a scene where people are leaving a place.
