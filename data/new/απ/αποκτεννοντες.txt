Word: αποκτεννοντες

Etymology: The word αποκτεννοντες comes from the Greek verb αποκτείνω (apokteino), which means "to kill" or "to slay". The suffix -ντες (-ntes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently killing or slaying.

Grammar: αποκτεννοντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb αποκτείνω (apokteino), which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense. The word functions as an adjective, describing people who are currently killing or slaying.

Description: αποκτεννοντες describes people who are actively killing or slaying. It can be used to refer to soldiers in battle, hunters pursuing prey, or murderers committing crimes. The word has a sense of immediacy and action, indicating that the killing is happening right now.
