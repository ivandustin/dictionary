Word: ηιτησας

Etymology: The word ηιτησας is a verb in the Greek language, which comes from the ancient Greek verb "αιτέω" (aiteo), meaning "to ask" or "to request".

Grammar: ηιτησας is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "αιτέω" (aiteo). It is used to indicate a past action of asking or requesting something.

Description: ηιτησας is a verb that describes the act of asking or requesting something in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as asking for a favor, requesting information, or seeking permission.
