Etymology: The word "ζυμης" (zymēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ζύμοω" (zymoō), which means "to ferment" or "to leaven."

Grammar: In Greek, "ζυμης" (zymēs) is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "ζυμη" (zymē).

Description: "ζυμης" (zymēs) refers to leaven or yeast, which is used in baking to make dough rise. It is a crucial ingredient in the fermentation process, where it breaks down sugars and produces carbon dioxide gas, causing the dough to expand and become light and fluffy.
