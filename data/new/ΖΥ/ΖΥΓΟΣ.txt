Etymology: The word "ζυγος" (zygos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *yeug- meaning "to join" or "to yoke."

Grammar: In Greek, "ζυγος" (zygos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be declined as follows:

- Singular: ο ζυγος (o zygos) - the yoke
- Plural: οι ζυγοι (oi zygoi) - the yokes

Description: A yoke is a wooden beam used to join two animals, typically oxen, together for pulling heavy loads. It is placed across the necks of the animals and attached to the load. The word "ζυγος" (zygos) can also be used metaphorically to refer to any kind of burden or constraint.
