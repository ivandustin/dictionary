Etymology: The word "μολυσμού" (molysmou) comes from the Greek verb "μολύνω" (molyno), which means "to pollute" or "to contaminate."

Grammar: "Μολυσμού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Μολυσμού" refers to the act or state of being polluted or contaminated. It can be used to describe the contamination of something with impurities, toxins, or harmful substances.
