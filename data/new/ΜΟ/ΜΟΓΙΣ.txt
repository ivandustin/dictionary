Etymology: The word "μογις" (mogis) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb "μογέω" (mogeō), which means "to toil" or "to labor."

Grammar: In Greek, "μογις" is an adverb. It is used to express the idea of "with difficulty" or "with great effort." It is often used to describe actions or tasks that require a lot of physical or mental exertion.

Description: "Μογις" is a word that conveys the sense of struggle or hardship. It implies that something is not easy to accomplish and requires considerable effort. It can be used to describe various situations, such as difficult tasks, challenging circumstances, or demanding activities.
