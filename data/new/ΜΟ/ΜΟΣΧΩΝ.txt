Etymology: The word "μοσχων" (moschon) comes from the Greek word "μόσχος" (moschos), which means "calf" or "young bull."

Grammar: "Μοσχων" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: "Μοσχων" refers to a young male bovine, specifically a calf or a young bull. It is commonly used to describe a young male animal that has not yet reached maturity.
