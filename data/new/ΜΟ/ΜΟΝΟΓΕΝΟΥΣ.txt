Etymology: The word "μονογενούς" comes from the Greek roots "μόνος" (monos) meaning "only" or "single" and "γένος" (genos) meaning "kind" or "type". 

Grammar: "Μονογενούς" is an adjective in the masculine genitive singular form. It is used to describe something as "unique", "one of a kind", or "singular".

Description: "Μονογενούς" is a Greek word that is often used in religious contexts to refer to Jesus Christ as the "only begotten" or "unique" Son of God. It emphasizes his special and exclusive relationship with God.
