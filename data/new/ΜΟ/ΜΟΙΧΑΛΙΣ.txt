Etymology: The word "μοιχαλίς" (moichalis) comes from the Greek word "μοιχεία" (moicheia), which means "adultery". 

Grammar: "Μοιχαλίς" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "μοιχαλίδος" (moichalidos). 

Description: "Μοιχαλίς" refers to a woman who commits adultery, a woman who cheats on her husband or partner. It is a term that carries a negative connotation and is often used to criticize or condemn such behavior.
