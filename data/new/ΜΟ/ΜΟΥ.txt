Etymology: The word "μου" (mou) is a Greek pronoun that means "my" or "mine". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἐμοῦ" (emoû).

Grammar: "Μου" (mou) is a possessive pronoun in Greek. It is used to indicate ownership or possession. It is the first person singular possessive pronoun and is equivalent to the English pronoun "my" or "mine".

Description: "Μου" (mou) is a very common word in the Greek language and is used in various contexts. It is used to indicate possession or ownership of something. For example, "Αυτό είναι το βιβλίο μου" (Aftó eínai to vivlío mou) means "This is my book". It can also be used to indicate relationships, such as "Αυτή είναι η φίλη μου" (Aftí eínai i fíli mou) meaning "She is my friend".
