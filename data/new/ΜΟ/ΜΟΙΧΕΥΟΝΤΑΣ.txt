Etymology: The word "μοιχευοντας" (moicheuontas) comes from the Greek verb "μοιχεύω" (moicheuō), which means "to commit adultery". 

Grammar: "Μοιχευοντας" is the present participle form of the verb "μοιχεύω" in the active voice. It is used to describe someone who is currently committing adultery. In Greek grammar, the present participle is formed by adding the suffix "-οντας" to the verb stem.

Description: "Μοιχευοντας" refers to individuals who are engaging in the act of adultery, which involves having sexual relations with someone other than one's spouse while being married. Adultery is generally considered a breach of trust and a violation of the commitment made in a marriage or committed relationship.
