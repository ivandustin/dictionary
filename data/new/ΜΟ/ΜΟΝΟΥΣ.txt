Etymology: The word "μονούς" (monous) comes from the Greek word "μόνος" (monos), meaning "alone" or "single."

Grammar: "Μονούς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it means "a single person" or "a solitary individual."

Description: "Μονούς" refers to someone who is alone or solitary, emphasizing their singularity or individuality. It can also be used to describe someone who prefers to be alone or who is not part of a group.
