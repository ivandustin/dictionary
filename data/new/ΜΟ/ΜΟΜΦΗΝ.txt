Etymology: The word "μομφήν" (mompheen) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "μομφή" (momphee), which means "to blame" or "to criticize."

Grammar: "Μομφήν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Μομφήν" refers to the act of blaming or criticizing someone or something. It can also be used to describe a complaint or an accusation.
