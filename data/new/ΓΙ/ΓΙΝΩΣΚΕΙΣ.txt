Etymology: The word "γινωσκεις" (pronounced "ginoskeis") comes from the Greek verb "γινώσκω" (ginósko), which means "to know" or "to understand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃-.

Grammar: "Γινωσκεις" is the second person singular form of the present indicative tense of the verb "γινώσκω" (ginósko). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The second person singular form is used when addressing one person.

Description: "Γινωσκεις" means "you know" in English. It is used to express knowledge or understanding of something.
