Etymology: The word "γινώσκω" (ginósko) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "γιγνώσκειν" (gignóskein), which means "to know" or "to understand."

Grammar: "Γινώσκω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ γνωρίζω (Egó gnorízo) - I know
- Εσύ γνωρίζεις (Esý gnorízeis) - You know (singular)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό γνωρίζει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó gnorízei) - He/She/It knows
- Εμείς γνωρίζουμε (Emeís gnorízoume) - We know
- Εσείς γνωρίζετε (Eseís gnorízete) - You know (plural)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές γνωρίζουν (Aftoí/Aftés gnorízoun) - They know

Description: "Γινώσκω" is a versatile verb that can be used to express various meanings related to knowledge, understanding, recognition, or awareness. It can refer to acquiring knowledge about something or someone, recognizing or identifying something, or understanding a concept or situation.
