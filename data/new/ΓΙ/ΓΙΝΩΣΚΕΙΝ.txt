Etymology: The word "γινωσκειν" (ginōskein) comes from the Greek verb "γινώσκω" (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃-.

Grammar: "Γινώσκειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb "γινώσκω" in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs, which is characterized by the ending "-ω" in the present tense.

Description: "Γινώσκειν" is a Greek verb that means "to know" or "to understand". It is used to express the act of acquiring knowledge or awareness about something. The verb can be used in various tenses, moods, and voices to convey different shades of meaning related to knowledge and understanding.
