Word: ηυφρανθη

Etymology:
The word ηυφρανθη is a past tense form of the Greek verb ευφραίνω (euphraino), which means "to make happy" or "to cheer up". The prefix ηυ- (eu-) means "well" or "good", so ηυφρανθη can be translated as "was made happy" or "was cheered up".

Grammar:
ηυφρανθη is a third person singular past tense form of the verb ευφραίνω. It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is passive, indicating that the subject of the sentence received the action of being made happy or cheered up.

Description:
ηυφρανθη is a word that describes a state of happiness or joy that was experienced in the past. It can be used to describe a person who was made happy by someone or something, or a situation that brought joy to someone. The word has a positive connotation and is often used in a celebratory context.
