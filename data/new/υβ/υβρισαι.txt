The word "υβρισαι" (hybrisai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "hubris," which refers to excessive pride or arrogance. The verb "υβρισαι" means to act with hubris, to show arrogance or insolence towards others. 

In terms of grammar, "υβρισαι" is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. 

Overall, "υβρισαι" is a word that describes a negative behavior and attitude towards others, often resulting in harm or offense.
