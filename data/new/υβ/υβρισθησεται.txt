Word: υβρισθησεται

Etymology: The word υβρισθησεται is derived from the Greek verb υβρίζω (hybrizo), which means "to insult" or "to treat with violence and arrogance". The suffix -θησεται (-thesetai) is a passive form indicating that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Grammar: υβρισθησεται is a third-person singular, passive, future indicative form of the verb υβρίζω (hybrizo). It is conjugated from the root υβρισθ-, with the addition of the future tense suffix -ησεται (-esetai) and the passive voice marker -θη- (-the-).

Description: υβρισθησεται is a Greek verb form that means "he/she/it will be insulted" or "he/she/it will be treated with violence and arrogance". It is a passive form, indicating that the subject of the verb is receiving the action rather than performing it. This verb form is often used in the context of moral or ethical wrongdoing, where someone is being punished for their arrogance or mistreatment of others.
