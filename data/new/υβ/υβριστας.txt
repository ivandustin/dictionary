Word: υβριστας

Etymology:
The word υβριστας (hybristas) comes from the Greek word ύβρις (hubris), which means "excessive pride" or "arrogance". The suffix "-της" (-tas) is added to the end of the word to indicate a person who exhibits this behavior.

Grammar:
υβριστας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word can be declined in different cases to indicate its role in a sentence.

Description:
υβριστας refers to a person who displays excessive pride or arrogance. In ancient Greek culture, hubris was considered a serious offense and was often punished by the gods. The term is still used today to describe someone who behaves in a haughty or disrespectful manner.
