Etymology: The word "ιδιον" (idion) comes from the Greek adjective "ιδιος" (idios), meaning "one's own" or "personal".

Grammar: "Ιδιον" (idion) is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Description: In Greek, "ιδιον" (idion) refers to something that is unique, specific, or characteristic of a particular person or thing. It can also mean "property" or "possession".
