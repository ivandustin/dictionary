Etymology: The word "ἰδηῖος" (idēios) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the noun "ἴδος" (idos), meaning "form" or "appearance."

Grammar: "ἰδηῖος" is an adjective in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and has different forms depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: The word "ἰδηῖος" is used to describe something as "peculiar," "distinctive," or "characteristic." It implies that the thing being described has unique qualities or features that set it apart from others.
