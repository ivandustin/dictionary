Etymology: The word "ιδοντες" (idontes) comes from the Greek verb "εἶδον" (eidon), which means "to see" or "to perceive." It is the aorist tense of the verb, indicating a completed action in the past.

Grammar: "Ιδοντες" is the plural form of the verb "εἶδον" (eidon) in the aorist tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have seen or perceived something in the past.

Description: "Ιδοντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having seen" or "those who have seen." It is often used in narratives or descriptions to refer to a group of people who have witnessed an event or observed something.
