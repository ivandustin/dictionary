Etymology: The word "ἰδιοῦς" (idious) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root "ἴδιος" (idios), meaning "one's own" or "personal."

Grammar: In Greek, "ἰδιοῦς" is an adjective that agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: The word "ἰδιοῦς" is used to describe something that belongs to or is characteristic of a specific person or thing. It conveys the idea of ownership, possession, or uniqueness. It can also be used to express the concept of individuality or personal identity.
