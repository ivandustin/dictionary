Word: ωφελείται

Etymology: The word ωφελείται comes from the Greek verb ωφελέω (opheléō), which means "to benefit" or "to help". The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates that the verb is in the present tense and passive voice.

Grammar: ωφελείται is a third-person singular present passive indicative form of the verb ωφελέω. It is used to describe an action that is being done to the subject of the sentence. In this case, the subject is receiving the benefit or help.

Description: ωφελείται is a Greek word that means "is being benefited" or "is being helped". It is often used in a positive context to describe someone or something that is receiving a benefit or advantage. For example, one might say "Η υγεία μας ωφελείται από την άσκηση" (Our health is benefited by exercise).
