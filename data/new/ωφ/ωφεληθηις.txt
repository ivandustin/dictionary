The word "ωφεληθηις" (ophelēthēis) is a Greek verb that means "to be helped" or "to be benefited." It is derived from the root word "οφελος" (ophelos), which means "help" or "benefit." 

In terms of grammar, "ωφεληθηις" is a second person singular passive aorist indicative verb. This means that it is addressing one person (second person singular), the action is in the past (aorist), and the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it (passive).

Overall, "ωφεληθηις" is a word that describes the state of being helped or benefited, and is used in a passive sense to indicate that the subject is receiving the help or benefit rather than actively seeking it out.
