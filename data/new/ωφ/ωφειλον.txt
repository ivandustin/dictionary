Word: ωφειλον

Etymology:
The word ωφειλον (ophelon) is a Greek word that comes from the verb ωφειλω (opheilō), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". It is derived from the noun οφειλη (opheilē), which means "debt" or "obligation".

Grammar:
ωφειλον is a noun in the neuter gender and singular number. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
ωφειλον refers to a debt or an obligation that someone owes. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a financial debt, or in a figurative sense, such as a moral obligation. The word implies that the debt or obligation is not yet fulfilled and still needs to be paid or fulfilled.
