Word: ωφελουμαι

Etymology:
The word ωφελουμαι (opheloumai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ωφελειν (ophelin), which means "to help" or "to benefit". The suffix -ουμαι (-oumai) indicates that the verb is in the middle or passive voice, meaning that the subject is being acted upon or is acting on itself.

Grammar:
ωφελουμαι is a present tense, middle or passive voice verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ ωφελούμαι (ego opheloumai) - I am helped/benefited
- Συ ωφελείσαι (su ophelisai) - You are helped/benefited
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό ωφελείται (aftos/afti/afto ophelitai) - He/She/It is helped/benefited

Plural:
- Εμείς ωφελούμαστε (emeis opheloumaste) - We are helped/benefited
- Εσείς ωφελείστε (eseis opheliste) - You are helped/benefited
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά ωφελούνται (aftoi/aftes/afta ophelountai) - They are helped/benefited

Description:
The word ωφελουμαι (opheloumai) means "to be helped" or "to benefit oneself". It is often used in a passive sense, indicating that the subject is receiving help or benefit from someone or something else. However, it can also be used in a reflexive sense, indicating that the subject is taking action to help or benefit themselves. The word is commonly used in Greek philosophy and ethics, where it is associated with the idea of eudaimonia, or human flourishing.
