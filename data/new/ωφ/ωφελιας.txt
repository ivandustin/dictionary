Word: ωφελιας

Etymology: The word ωφελιας comes from the Greek word "ωφέλεια" (ophéleia), which means "benefit" or "advantage". The suffix "-ας" is added to form the noun.

Grammar: ωφελιας is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form is ωφελία (ophélia).

Description: ωφελιας refers to a benefit or advantage that one gains from something. It can also refer to usefulness or profitability. In modern Greek, it is commonly used in the context of business and economics.
