Word: ατιμασθηναι

The word ατιμασθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ατιμος (atimos), which means "dishonored" or "disgraced". The suffix -σθηναι (-sthenai) is a passive form of the verb, indicating that the action is being done to the subject rather than by the subject.

ατιμασθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive verb in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "to be dishonored" or "to be disgraced". ατιμασθηναι is a strong word that conveys a sense of shame and disgrace.
