Word: ατιμοι

Etymology:
The word ατιμοι (atimoi) is derived from the Greek word ατιμος (atimos), which means "dishonored" or "disgraced". It is formed by adding the prefix "a-" (meaning "not" or "without") to the word τιμη (time), which means "honor" or "respect".

ατιμοι is a plural adjective in Greek, used to describe a group of people who are dishonored or disgraced. It is in the masculine gender and nominative case.

The word ατιμοι is often used in Greek literature and language to describe individuals or groups who have lost their honor or reputation due to their actions or circumstances.
