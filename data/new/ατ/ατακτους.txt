Word: ατακτους

Etymology:
The word ατακτους (ataktous) is derived from the Greek word α- (a-) which means "not" and τακτός (taktos) which means "ordered" or "arranged". Therefore, ατακτους means "disordered" or "unruly".

Grammar:
ατακτους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are not in order or are behaving in a disorderly manner.

Description:
ατακτους is a word that is often used to describe a group of people who are not following rules or behaving in a chaotic manner. It can also be used to describe things that are disorganized or not arranged in a proper manner. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of students who are not following classroom rules, or a messy room that is not organized.
