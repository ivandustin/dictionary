Word: ατιμία

Etymology:
The word ατιμία (atimía) comes from the Greek root words α- (a-) meaning "not" and τιμή (timí) meaning "honor" or "respect". Therefore, ατιμία can be translated to mean "dishonor" or "disrespect".

Grammar:
ατιμία is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the first declension and has the following forms:

Singular:
- Nominative: ατιμία
- Genitive: ατιμίας
- Accusative: ατιμίαν
- Vocative: ατιμία

Plural:
- Nominative: ατιμίαι
- Genitive: ατιμιών
- Accusative: ατιμίας
- Vocative: ατιμίαι

Description:
ατιμία is a word that describes a lack of honor or respect. It can refer to a person who has been dishonored or disgraced, or to an action or behavior that is considered shameful or disrespectful. In Greek culture, honor and respect are highly valued, and ατιμία is seen as a serious offense. The word can also be used in a legal context to refer to a loss of civil rights or social status.
