Word: ατιμαζεσθαι

Etymology: The word ατιμαζεσθαι comes from the Greek verb ατιμαζω (atimazo), which means "to dishonor" or "to treat with contempt". The suffix -εσθαι (-esthai) indicates that it is a passive form, meaning "to be dishonored" or "to be treated with contempt".

Grammar: ατιμαζεσθαι is a passive present infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -εσθαι to the stem ατιμαζ-.

Description: ατιμαζεσθαι refers to the act of being dishonored or treated with contempt. It is often used in a moral or social context, such as when someone is shamed or disgraced for their actions or behavior. The word can also be used in a religious context, such as when someone is considered to have sinned or acted against the will of the gods.
