Word: ατμίδα (atmida)

Etymology: The word ατμίδα comes from the ancient Greek word ατμός (atmos), meaning vapor or steam.

Grammar: ατμίδα is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is ατμίδες (atmides).

Description: ατμίδα refers to a visible gas or vapor that is suspended in the air. It can be used to describe the mist that rises from a hot cup of coffee or the steam that comes out of a boiling pot of water. In scientific contexts, ατμίδα can refer to atmospheric gases or the gaseous state of matter.
