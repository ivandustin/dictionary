Word: ατιμασαντες

Etymology: ατιμασαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb ατιμαζω (atimazo), which means "to dishonor" or "to treat with contempt". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: ατιμασαντες is a present active participle in the masculine plural form. It is derived from the verb ατιμαζω (atimazo), which is in the aorist tense. The participle form indicates that the action of dishonoring or treating with contempt is ongoing or continuous.

Description: ατιμασαντες can be translated to "dishonoring" or "treating with contempt". It is often used in a religious or moral context to describe those who disrespect or dishonor God or other sacred things. In a broader sense, it can also refer to those who show disrespect or contempt towards others.
