Word: ατιμίαι

Etymology:
The word ατιμίαι (atimiai) is derived from the Greek word ατιμία (atimia), which means "dishonor" or "disgrace". The suffix -αι (-ai) is added to the end of the word to indicate that it is plural.

Grammar:
ατιμίαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
ατιμίαι refers to acts or situations that bring dishonor or disgrace. It can be used to describe a person who has lost their honor or reputation, or to refer to actions that are considered shameful or disgraceful. In ancient Greece, atimia was a legal term that referred to the loss of citizenship rights and social status as a result of certain crimes or offenses. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe situations or behaviors that are considered dishonorable or shameful.
