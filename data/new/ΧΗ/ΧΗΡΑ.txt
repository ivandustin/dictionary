Etymology: The word "χηρα" (chira) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "χηρός" (cheros), meaning "widow."

Grammar: In Greek, "χηρα" (chira) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a singular form.

Description: "Χηρα" (chira) refers to a widow, a woman whose spouse has died and has not remarried. It is used to describe a woman who has lost her husband and is living without a partner.
