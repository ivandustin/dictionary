Etymology: The word "χηρας" (chēras) comes from the Greek word "χηρός" (chēros), which means "widow."

Grammar: In Greek, "χηρας" (chēras) is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular number.

Description: "Χηρας" (chēras) refers to a widower, a man whose spouse has died and who has not remarried.
