Etymology: The word "χηραίς" (chēraís) comes from the Greek word "χήρα" (chēra), which means "widow". 

Grammar: In Greek, "χηραίς" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is the plural form of "χήρα" (chēra).

Description: "Χηραίς" refers to a group of widows. In ancient Greece, widows were often considered vulnerable members of society and were sometimes provided with support or assistance. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people who share a common experience or situation.
