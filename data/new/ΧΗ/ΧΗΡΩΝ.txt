Etymology: The word "χηρων" (chiron) comes from the Greek mythology. Chiron was a centaur, known for his wisdom and knowledge in medicine and astrology.

Grammar: "Χηρων" (chiron) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description: In Greek mythology, Chiron was a wise and knowledgeable centaur. He was known for his teaching abilities and was considered to be a mentor to many heroes, including Achilles and Jason. Chiron was also skilled in medicine and astrology. He was known for his kindness and wisdom, and he played a significant role in many mythological stories.
