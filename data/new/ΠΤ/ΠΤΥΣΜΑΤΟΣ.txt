Etymology: The word "πτυσματος" (ptysmatos) comes from the Greek verb "πτύσσω" (ptyssō), which means "to fold" or "to bend." 

Grammar: "Πτυσματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Πτυσματος" refers to something that is folded or bent. It can be used to describe a physical object that has been folded, such as a piece of paper or a garment. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or concept that is complex or intricate.
