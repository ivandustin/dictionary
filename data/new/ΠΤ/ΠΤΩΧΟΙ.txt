Etymology: The word "πτωχοί" (ptōchoí) comes from the Greek adjective "πτωχός" (ptōchós), which means "poor" or "needy". It is derived from the verb "πίπτω" (píptō), meaning "to fall" or "to be brought low".

Grammar: "Πτωχοί" is the plural form of the noun "πτωχός" (ptōchós), which means "poor people" or "the poor" in Greek. It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns.

Description: "Πτωχοί" refers to individuals or a group of people who lack material wealth or resources. It can also imply a state of poverty or neediness. The term is often used to describe those who are economically disadvantaged or have limited access to basic necessities.
