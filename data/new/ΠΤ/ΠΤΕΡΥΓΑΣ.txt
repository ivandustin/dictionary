Etymology: The word "πτερυγας" (pterygas) comes from the Greek word "πτερόν" (pteron), meaning "wing". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, meaning "to fly".

Grammar: "Πτερυγας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and belongs to the third declension. In the singular form, it is "πτέρυγα" (pteryga).

Description: "Πτερυγας" refers to "wings" in Greek. It is used to describe the anatomical structures that enable flight in birds, insects, and other animals. Wings are typically composed of feathers, membranes, or other specialized structures that allow for aerial movement and control.
