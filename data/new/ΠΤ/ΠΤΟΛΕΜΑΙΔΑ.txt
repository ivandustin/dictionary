Etymology: The word "πτολεμαίδα" comes from the Greek word "Πτολεμαίς" (Ptolemais), which was the name of several ancient cities founded by or named after Ptolemy, a common name among the Ptolemaic dynasty of Egypt.

Grammar: "Πτολεμαίδα" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "Πτολεμαίδας" (Ptolemaidas).

Description: "Πτολεμαίδα" refers to a city or a place named after Ptolemy. It can be used to describe any location that bears the name Ptolemais.
