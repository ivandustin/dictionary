Etymology: The word "πτώμα" (ptoma) comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pipto), which means "to fall". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, meaning "to fall".

Grammar: In Greek, "πτώμα" is a neuter noun of the second declension. It has the plural form "πτώματα" (ptomata). It can function as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Πτώμα" is a Greek word that means "corpse" or "dead body". It refers to the lifeless remains of a human or animal. It is often used in the context of death, funerals, and forensic investigations.
