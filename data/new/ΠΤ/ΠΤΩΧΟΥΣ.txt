Etymology: The word "πτωχούς" (ptochous) comes from the Greek word "πτωχός" (ptochos), which means "poor" or "needy".

Grammar: In Greek, "πτωχούς" is the plural form of the adjective "πτωχός" (ptochos), which means "poor" or "needy". It is used to describe multiple individuals who are lacking in material possessions or resources.

Description: "Πτωχούς" is a word that refers to a group of people who are poor or in a state of need. It can be used to describe individuals who have limited financial resources, lack basic necessities, or are socially disadvantaged. The word carries a connotation of poverty and vulnerability.
