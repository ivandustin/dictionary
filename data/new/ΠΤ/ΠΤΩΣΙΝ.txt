Etymology: The word "πτωσιν" (ptōsin) comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (piptō), which means "to fall". 

Grammar: "Πτωσιν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative form of the noun "πτῶσις" (ptōsis), which means "falling" or "fall". 

Description: "Πτωσιν" refers to the act of falling or the state of being fallen. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a physical fall or a decline in status or reputation.
