Etymology: The word "πτυσας" (ptysas) comes from the Greek verb "πτύω" (ptyo), which means "to fold" or "to bend." 

Grammar: "Πτυσας" is the aorist active participle of the verb "πτύω" (ptyo). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: "Πτυσας" describes someone or something that has folded or bent. It can be used as an adjective to modify a noun, or as a noun itself.
