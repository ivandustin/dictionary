Etymology: The word "πτωχείαν" (ptōkheían) comes from the Greek word "πτωχός" (ptōkhós), meaning "poor" or "needy". It is derived from the verb "πτώσσω" (ptṓssō), which means "to fall" or "to become destitute".

Grammar: "Πτωχείαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Πτωχείαν" refers to the state or condition of being poor or impoverished. It can also be translated as "poverty" or "destitution". The word is often used to describe a lack of material wealth or resources.
