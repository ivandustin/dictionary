Etymology: The word "ιππος" (ippos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἵππος" (hippos), which means "horse".

Grammar: "Ιππος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "ιππου" (ippou).

Description: "Ιππος" refers to a horse in Greek. It is a common word used to describe the large, four-legged animal with a mane, tail, and hooves that is often used for riding, racing, or working.
