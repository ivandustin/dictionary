Etymology: The word "ιππος" (ippos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ekwo- meaning "horse."

Grammar: In Greek, "ιππος" (ippos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: ιππος (ippos) - horse
- Genitive: ιππου (ippou) - of a horse
- Dative: ιππῳ (ippō) - to/for a horse
- Accusative: ιππον (ippon) - horse
- Vocative: ιππε (ippe) - O horse

Plural:
- Nominative: ιπποι (ippoi) - horses
- Genitive: ιππων (ippōn) - of horses
- Dative: ιπποις (ippois) - to/for horses
- Accusative: ιππους (ippous) - horses
- Vocative: ιπποι (ippoi) - O horses

Description: "Ιππος" (ippos) is the Greek word for "horse." It refers to a large, four-legged mammal with a flowing mane and tail, often used for riding, racing, or pulling vehicles. Horses have been domesticated for thousands of years and have played a significant role in human history and culture.
