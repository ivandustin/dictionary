Etymology: The word "λυστραν" (lystran) comes from the Greek word "λύω" (lyo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie". 

Grammar: "Λυστραν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the third declension. 

Description: "Λυστραν" refers to a type of instrument used in ancient Greece for loosening or untying knots. It was commonly used in various tasks such as untying ropes, unfastening knots, or unraveling tangled objects.
