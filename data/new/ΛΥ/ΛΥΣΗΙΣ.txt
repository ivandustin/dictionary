Etymology: The word "λυσηις" (lysis) comes from the Greek verb "λυω" (luo), which means "to loosen" or "to dissolve."

Grammar: "Λυσηις" (lysis) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Λυσηις" (lysis) refers to the process of breaking down or dissolving something. In biology, it specifically refers to the disintegration or destruction of cells, tissues, or organs. Lysis can occur naturally or be induced artificially through various methods.
