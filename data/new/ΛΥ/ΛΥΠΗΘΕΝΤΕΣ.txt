Etymology: The word "λυπηθεντες" comes from the Greek verb "λυπέω" (lypéo), which means "to grieve" or "to be sad." It is derived from the noun "λύπη" (lýpē), meaning "grief" or "sorrow."

Grammar: "Λυπηθεντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "λυπέω" (lypéo) in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the participle form functions as an adjective. In this case, "λυπηθεντες" translates to "having grieved" or "having been saddened."

Description: "Λυπηθεντες" is used to describe a group of people who have experienced grief or sadness in the past. It implies that they have gone through a period of sorrow or mourning.
