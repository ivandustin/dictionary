Etymology: The word "λυπώ" (lypo) comes from the Greek verb "λυπέω" (lypeo), which means "to grieve" or "to cause sorrow." It is derived from the noun "λύπη" (lypi), meaning "sorrow" or "grief."

Grammar: "Λυπώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ λυπώ (ego lypo) - I grieve
- Εσύ λυπάσαι (esi lypase) - You grieve (singular, informal)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό λυπάται (aftos/afti/afto lypate) - He/She/It grieves
- Εμείς λυπόμαστε (emeis lypomaste) - We grieve
- Εσείς λυπάστε (eseis lypaste) - You grieve (plural, formal)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές λυπούνται (aftoi/aftes lypounte) - They grieve

Description: "Λυπώ" is a verb that expresses the action of grieving or causing sorrow. It is used to describe the feeling of deep sadness or sorrow that one experiences. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing personal grief, empathizing with someone's sorrow, or describing the act of causing sorrow to someone else.
