Etymology: The word "λυσον" (lyson) comes from the Greek verb "λύω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". 

Grammar: "Λυσον" is the imperative form of the verb "λύω" (lyo) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or request someone to loosen or untie something.

Description: "Λυσον" is a Greek word that specifically instructs someone to loosen or untie something. It is commonly used in contexts where there is a need to release or free something from being tied or bound.
