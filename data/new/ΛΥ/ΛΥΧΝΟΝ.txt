Etymology: The word "λυχνον" (lychnon) comes from the Ancient Greek word "λύχνος" (lychnos), which means "lamp" or "lantern".

Grammar: In Greek, "λυχνον" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension and is classified as a neuter noun.

Description: "Λυχνον" refers to a lamp or lantern, which is a portable light source typically fueled by oil or gas. It is used to provide illumination in various settings, such as homes, streets, or outdoor areas.
