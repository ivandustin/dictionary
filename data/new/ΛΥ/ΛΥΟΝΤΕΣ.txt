Etymology: The word "λυοντες" (lyontes) comes from the Greek verb "λύω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". 

Grammar: "Λυοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "λύω" (lyo) in the active voice. It is used to describe an ongoing action of loosening or untying. 

Description: "Λυοντες" refers to a group of people who are currently in the process of loosening or untying something. It can be used in various contexts, such as untying knots, unfastening ropes, or unraveling a problem.
