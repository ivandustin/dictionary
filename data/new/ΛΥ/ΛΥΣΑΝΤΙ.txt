Etymology: The word "λυσαντι" (lysanti) comes from the Greek verb "λυω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". 

Grammar: "Λυσαντι" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "λυω" (lyo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Λυσαντι" is a word in the Greek language that means "he/she/it loosened" or "he/she/it untied". It refers to the action of untying or loosening something.
