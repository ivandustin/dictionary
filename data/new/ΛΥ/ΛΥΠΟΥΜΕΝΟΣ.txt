Etymology: The word "λυπούμενος" (lypoumenos) comes from the Greek verb "λυποῦμαι" (lypoumai), which means "to be grieved" or "to be saddened."

Grammar: "Λυπούμενος" is the present participle form of the verb "λυποῦμαι" (lypoumai), which is in the middle voice. It is used to describe someone who is currently experiencing grief or sadness.

Description: "Λυπούμενος" is an adjective that describes a person who is feeling sorrowful or distressed. It can be used to refer to someone who is grieving over a loss, feeling sad about a situation, or experiencing emotional pain.
