Etymology: The word "λυσαι" (lysai) comes from the Greek verb "λύω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie."

Grammar: "Λυσαι" (lysai) is the aorist tense, active voice, imperative mood form of the verb "λύω" (lyo). It is used to give a command or make a request.

Description: "Λυσαι" (lysai) is a Greek word that means "loosen" or "untie." It is often used metaphorically to mean "release" or "set free."
