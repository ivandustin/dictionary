Etymology: The word "λυχνιών" (lychniṓn) comes from the Greek word "λύχνος" (lychnos), meaning "lamp" or "lantern".

Grammar: "Λυχνιών" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Λυχνιών" refers to lamps or lanterns. In ancient times, these were typically oil lamps made of clay or metal, with a wick that was lit to provide light. They were commonly used in households, temples, and other spaces that required illumination.
