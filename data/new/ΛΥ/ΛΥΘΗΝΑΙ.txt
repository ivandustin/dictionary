Etymology: The word "λυθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "λύω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". 

Grammar: "λυθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "λύω" (lyo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "λυθηναι" is used to express the action of being loosened or untied. It can be used in various contexts, such as untying a knot, releasing something that was held tightly, or figuratively, to indicate the resolution or release from a problem or burden.
