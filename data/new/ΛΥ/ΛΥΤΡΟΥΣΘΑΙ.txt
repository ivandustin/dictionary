Etymology: The word "λυτρούσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "λυτρόω" (lytroo), which means "to redeem" or "to ransom."

Grammar: "Λυτρούσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "λυτρόω" (lytroo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the voice (active, middle, or passive), tense, mood, and person.

Description: "Λυτρούσθαι" is an ancient Greek verb that conveys the idea of redemption or ransom. It is used to describe the act of freeing or delivering someone or something from captivity or a difficult situation by paying a price or offering a substitute.
