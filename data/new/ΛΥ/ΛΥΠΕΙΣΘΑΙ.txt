Etymology: The word "λυπείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "λυπέω" (lypeo), which means "to grieve" or "to be sad." It is derived from the noun "λύπη" (lypē), meaning "sorrow" or "grief."

Grammar: "Λυπείσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "λυπέω" (lypeo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the subject and voice (active, middle, or passive). The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Λυπείσθαι" is a Greek verb that expresses the action of feeling sorrow, grief, or sadness. It is used to describe the state of being emotionally affected by something negative or distressing.
