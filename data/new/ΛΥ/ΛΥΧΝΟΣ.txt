Grammar: In Greek, "λυχνος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be used as the subject of a sentence or as a predicate nominative.

Description: "Λυχνος" refers to a lamp or a lantern. It is an object that produces light, typically using a flame or an electric bulb. In ancient times, oil lamps were commonly used, while modern lamps use electricity. Lamps are used for illumination in various settings, such as homes, streets, and public spaces.
