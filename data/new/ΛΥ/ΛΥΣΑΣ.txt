Etymology: The word "λυσας" (lysis) comes from the Greek verb "λυω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". 

Grammar: "Λυσας" (lysis) is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Λυσας" (lysis) refers to the act of loosening or untying. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the resolution or solution of a problem or conflict.
