Etymology: The word "λυτρωσιν" (lytrosin) comes from the Greek verb "λυτρόω" (lytroo), which means "to redeem" or "to ransom."

Grammar: "Λυτρωσιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description: "Λυτρωσιν" refers to the act of redeeming or ransoming someone or something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the deliverance or liberation from a difficult or oppressive situation.
