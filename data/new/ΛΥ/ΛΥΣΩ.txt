Etymology: The word "λυσω" (luso) comes from the Greek verb "λύω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". 

Grammar: "Λυσώ" (lyso) is the first person singular future tense of the verb "λύω" (lyo). It is used to express an action that will happen in the future, specifically when the subject is "I" or "me".

Description: "Λυσώ" (lyso) is a Greek word that means "I will loosen" or "I will untie". It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing to indicate a future action of untying or loosening something.
