Etymology: The word "νοησαί" (noesai) comes from the Greek verb "νοέω" (noeo), which means "to perceive" or "to understand."

Grammar: "Νοησαί" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "νοέω" (noeo). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to perceive or understand something.

Description: "Νοησαί" is a Greek word that is used to express the command "perceive" or "understand." It is often used in a figurative sense, urging someone to grasp the meaning or significance of something.
