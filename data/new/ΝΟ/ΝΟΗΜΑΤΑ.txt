Etymology: The word "νοηματα" (noimata) comes from the Greek word "νόημα" (noima), which means "meaning" or "significance". 

Grammar: "Νοηματα" is a noun in the plural form in Greek. It belongs to the neuter gender and is declined as such. 

Description: "Νοηματα" refers to meanings or significances. It is used to describe the ideas, concepts, or messages conveyed by words, symbols, or actions. In the context of language, "νοηματα" can refer to the semantic content or the intended interpretations of words or sentences. It is a fundamental concept in linguistics and communication studies.
