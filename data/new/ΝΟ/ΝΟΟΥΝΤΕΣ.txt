Etymology: The word "νοούντες" comes from the Greek verb "νοέω" (noeo), which means "to think" or "to perceive." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *new- meaning "to perceive" or "to know."

Grammar: "Νοούντες" is the plural form of the noun "νοούς" (noous), which means "mind" or "intellect" in Greek. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Νοούντες" refers to individuals who possess the ability to think, perceive, or have intellect. It is often used in philosophical or metaphysical contexts to describe beings with higher consciousness or spiritual awareness.
