Etymology: The word "νουθετώ" (nouthetó) comes from the Greek roots "νοῦς" (nous), meaning "mind" or "intellect," and "θέτω" (theto), meaning "to place" or "to set." It literally means "to set the mind."

Grammar: "Νουθετώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ νουθετώ (ego nouthetó) - I admonish, εσύ νουθετείς (esý noutheteís) - you admonish, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό νουθετεί (aftós/aftí/aftó noutheteí) - he/she/it admonishes, εμείς νουθετούμε (emeís nouthetoúme) - we admonish, εσείς νουθετείτε (eseís noutheteíte) - you admonish, αυτοί/αυτές νουθετούν (aftoí/aftés nouthetoún) - they admonish.

Description: "Νουθετώ" is a Greek verb that means "to admonish" or "to warn." It refers to the act of giving advice, guidance, or correction to someone in order to help them improve their behavior or actions. It implies a gentle and constructive approach rather than harsh criticism.
