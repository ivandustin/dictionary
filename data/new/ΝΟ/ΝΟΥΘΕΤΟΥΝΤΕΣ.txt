Etymology: The word "νουθετούντες" comes from the Greek verb "νουθετέω" (noutheteo), which means "to admonish" or "to warn." It is derived from the combination of the words "νοῦς" (nous), meaning "mind" or "intellect," and "θέτω" (theto), meaning "to place" or "to set."

Grammar: "Νουθετούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "νουθετέω" (noutheteo) in Greek. It is used to describe the action of admonishing or warning someone in the present tense. In Greek grammar, the present participle is formed by adding the suffix "-ούντ-" to the verb stem.

Description: "Νουθετούντες" refers to those who admonish or warn others. It can be used to describe individuals who give advice, guidance, or cautionary messages to others in order to correct their behavior or prevent them from making mistakes.
