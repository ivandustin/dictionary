Etymology: The word "νοσσιά" comes from the Greek word "νοσσός" (nossos), which means "nestling" or "young bird". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nes-, meaning "to return to the nest".

Grammar: "Νοσσιά" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "νοσσιές" (nossies). In the singular, it can be used with definite articles like "η νοσσιά" (the nestling) or indefinite articles like "μια νοσσιά" (a nestling).

Description: "Νοσσιά" refers to a nestling or a young bird that has not yet left the nest. It is commonly used to describe the offspring of birds, especially during the period when they are still dependent on their parents for food and protection.
