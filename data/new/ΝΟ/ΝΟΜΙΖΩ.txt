Etymology: The word "νομίζω" (nomízo) comes from the Greek verb "νομίζω" (nomízo), which means "to think" or "to believe".

Grammar: "Νομίζω" (nomízo) is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ νομίζω (Egó nomízo) - I think
- Εσύ νομίζεις (Esý nomízeis) - You think
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό νομίζει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó nomízei) - He/She/It thinks
- Εμείς νομίζουμε (Emeís nomízoume) - We think
- Εσείς νομίζετε (Eseís nomízete) - You think
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά νομίζουν (Aftoí/Aftés/Aftá nomízoun) - They think

Description: "Νομίζω" (nomízo) is a versatile verb that is used to express the act of thinking or believing. It can be used in various contexts and can convey different shades of meaning depending on the context in which it is used.
