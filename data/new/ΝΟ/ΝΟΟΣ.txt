Etymology: The word "νοος" (noos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *new- meaning "to think" or "to perceive."

Grammar: In Greek, "νοος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and can also be declined in other cases such as genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative.

Description: "Νοος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "mind" or "intellect." It refers to the faculty of thinking, understanding, and reasoning. In philosophy, it is often used to describe the higher cognitive functions of the human mind, such as perception, consciousness, and rationality.
