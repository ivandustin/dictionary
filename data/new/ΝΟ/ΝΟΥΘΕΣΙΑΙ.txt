Etymology: The word "νουθεσία" comes from the Greek roots "νοῦς" (nous), meaning "mind" or "intellect," and "θέσις" (thesis), meaning "placing" or "setting." It literally translates to "setting the mind straight" or "admonition."

Grammar: "Νουθεσία" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form.

Description: "Νουθεσία" refers to the act of giving advice, admonition, or reproof in order to correct someone's behavior or thinking. It implies a gentle or friendly approach to guiding someone towards the right path. The term is often associated with moral or ethical guidance.
