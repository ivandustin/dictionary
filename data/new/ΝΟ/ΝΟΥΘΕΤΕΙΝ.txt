Etymology: The word "νουθετείν" (nouthetein) comes from the Greek roots "νοῦς" (nous), meaning "mind" or "intellect," and "θέτης" (thetis), meaning "one who places" or "one who sets." 

Grammar: "Νουθετείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "Νουθετείν" means "to admonish" or "to warn" in Greek. It refers to the act of advising or cautioning someone in order to correct their behavior or actions.
