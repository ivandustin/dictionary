Etymology: The word "νοείτω" (noeitō) comes from the Greek verb "νοέω" (noeō), which means "to think" or "to perceive."

Grammar: "Νοείτω" is the third person singular imperative form of the verb "νοέω" (noeō). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to think or perceive something.

Description: "Νοείτω" is a Greek word that is often used in philosophical and spiritual contexts. It is often translated as "let it be understood" or "let it be known." It is used to emphasize the importance of thinking or perceiving something, particularly in a deeper or more profound way.
