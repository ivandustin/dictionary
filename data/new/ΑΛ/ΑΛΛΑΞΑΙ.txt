Etymology: The word "αλλαξαι" (allaxai) comes from the Greek verb "αλλάσσω" (allasso), which means "to change" or "to alter".

Grammar: "Αλλαξαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "αλλάσσω" (allasso). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Αλλαξαι" is a verb in Greek that means "to change" or "to alter". It is used to describe the act of transforming or modifying something.
