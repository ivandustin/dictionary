Etymology: The word "αλεξανδρος" (aleksandros) comes from the Greek name Αλέξανδρος (Alexandros), which is composed of the elements "αλέξω" (alexo) meaning "to defend" and "ἀνήρ" (aner) meaning "man". It is commonly translated as "defender of man" or "protector of men".

Grammar: In Greek, "αλεξανδρος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can be declined in different cases and forms depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Αλεξανδρος" is a Greek name that has been used since ancient times. It is famously associated with Alexander the Great, the Macedonian king who conquered a vast empire in the 4th century BC. The name has remained popular throughout history and is still used today.
