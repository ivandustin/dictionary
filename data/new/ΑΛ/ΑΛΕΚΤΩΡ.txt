Etymology: The word "αλεκτωρ" (alektor) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂el- meaning "to crow" or "to shout". 

Grammar: In Greek, "αλεκτωρ" (alektor) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be declined as follows:

- Singular: αλεκτωρ (alektor)
- Plural: αλεκτορες (alektores)

Description: "Αλεκτωρ" (alektor) is the Greek word for "rooster" or "cock". It refers to the male chicken, known for its distinctive crowing sound. Roosters are often associated with the break of dawn and are considered symbols of vigilance and courage in many cultures.
