Etymology: The word "αληθιναί" (alithinai) comes from the Greek adjective "αληθινός" (alithinos), which means "true" or "genuine".

Grammar: "Αληθιναί" is the plural form of the feminine adjective "αληθινή" (alithini), which means "true" or "genuine". It agrees with plural feminine nouns in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Αληθιναί" is used to describe multiple feminine nouns as being true or genuine. It can be used to emphasize the authenticity or accuracy of something.
