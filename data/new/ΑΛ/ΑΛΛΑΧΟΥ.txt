Etymology: The word "αλλαχού" (allachou) comes from the Greek phrase "αλλού χωρίς" (allou choris), which means "elsewhere without." It is a colloquial expression used in Greek to indicate that something or someone is absent or missing.

Grammar: "Αλλαχού" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe a location or situation where something or someone is not present. It is often used in negative sentences or questions to express the absence of something.

Description: "Αλλαχού" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used in everyday speech to indicate that something or someone is not present in a particular place or situation. It can also be used metaphorically to express the absence or lack of something in a broader sense.
