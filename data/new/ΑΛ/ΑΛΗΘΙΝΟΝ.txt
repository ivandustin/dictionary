Etymology: The word "αληθινόν" (alithinon) comes from the Greek adjective "αληθής" (alithis), meaning "true" or "real". 

Grammar: "Αληθινόν" is the neuter form of the adjective, used to describe nouns of neuter gender. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αληθινόν" is an adjective in Greek that means "true" or "real". It is used to describe something that is genuine, authentic, or accurate. It can be used to describe a person, an object, or an idea.
