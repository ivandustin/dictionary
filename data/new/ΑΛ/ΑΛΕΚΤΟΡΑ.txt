Etymology: The word "αλεκτορα" (alektora) comes from the Greek word "αλέκτωρ" (alektor), which means "rooster" or "cock". 

Grammar: "Αλεκτορα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Αλεκτορα" refers to a female rooster or hen. It is commonly used to describe the female counterpart of the male rooster.
