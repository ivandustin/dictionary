Etymology: The word "αληθευων" (alētheuōn) comes from the Greek verb "αληθεύω" (alētheuō), which means "to speak the truth" or "to be true." It is derived from the noun "αλήθεια" (alētheia), meaning "truth."

Grammar: "Αληθευων" is the present participle form of the verb "αληθεύω" (alētheuō). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Αληθευων" is a Greek word that can be translated as "speaking the truth" or "being true." It describes someone or something that is actively engaged in the act of speaking or embodying the truth. The word carries connotations of honesty, sincerity, and accuracy.
