Etymology: The word "αλληλοίς" (allēlois) comes from the Greek root "ἄλλος" (allos), meaning "other" or "different". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂élyos.

Grammar: "Αλληλοίς" is a Greek noun in the dative plural form. It is used to indicate reciprocity or mutual action between two or more entities. In English, it can be translated as "to/for each other" or "mutually".

Description: "Αλληλοίς" is a word that expresses the concept of mutual or reciprocal action. It is often used to describe relationships or interactions between individuals or groups. It emphasizes the idea of give and take, where both parties are involved in the same action or experience.
