Etymology: The word "αλλοτριεπίσκοπος" comes from the Greek roots "άλλος" (allos), meaning "other" or "different," and "επίσκοπος" (episkopos), meaning "bishop." 

Grammar: "Αλλοτριεπίσκοπος" is a noun in the masculine gender. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension. 

Description: "Αλλοτριεπίσκοπος" is a term used in the Greek Orthodox Church to refer to a bishop who interferes or intervenes in the affairs of another diocese without proper authorization. It implies an unauthorized intrusion into the jurisdiction of another bishop.
