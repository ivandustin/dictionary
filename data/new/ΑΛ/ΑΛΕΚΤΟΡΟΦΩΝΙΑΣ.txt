Etymology: The word "αλεκτοροφωνίας" comes from the Greek words "αλέκτωρ" (alektor), meaning "rooster" or "cock", and "φωνή" (phoni), meaning "voice" or "sound". 

Grammar: "αλεκτοροφωνίας" is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of the noun "αλεκτοροφωνία" (alektorophonia), which means "cockcrow" or "the sound of a rooster crowing". 

Description: "αλεκτοροφωνίας" refers to the act or sound of a rooster crowing. It is often used metaphorically to describe someone who speaks loudly or forcefully, similar to the way a rooster crows to assert its dominance.
