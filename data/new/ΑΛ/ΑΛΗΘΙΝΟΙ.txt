Etymology: The word "αληθινοί" (alithinoi) comes from the Greek adjective "αληθής" (alithis), which means "true" or "real". It is derived from the Greek noun "αλήθεια" (aletheia), meaning "truth".

Grammar: "Αληθινοί" is the plural form of the adjective "αληθινός" (alithinos), which means "true" or "real". It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αληθινοί" is used to describe multiple things or people that are genuine, authentic, or sincere. It indicates that something or someone is not fake or false, but rather corresponds to reality or truth.
