Etymology: The word "αλοης" (aloēs) comes from the Greek word "ἀλόη" (alóē), which means "aloe" in English. 

Grammar: In Greek, "αλοης" (aloēs) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form of "αλοητος" (aloētos).

Description: "Αλοης" (aloēs) refers to the aloe plant, a succulent plant species that is native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is known for its thick, fleshy leaves that contain a gel-like substance with various medicinal properties. The aloe plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and skincare products.
