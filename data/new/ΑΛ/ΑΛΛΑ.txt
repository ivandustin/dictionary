Etymology: The word "αλλα" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἀλλά" (alla), which means "but" or "however".

Grammar: "Αλλα" is a conjunction in Greek grammar. It is used to introduce a contrasting or opposing idea in a sentence. It is similar to the English word "but" or "however".

Description: "Αλλα" is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to express contrast or contradiction. It is often used to connect two clauses or phrases that present opposing ideas. It helps to create a balance in the sentence and highlight the difference between the two ideas being expressed.
