Etymology: The word "αλειψαι" (aleipsai) comes from the Greek verb "αλείφω" (aleípho), which means "to anoint" or "to smear". 

Grammar: "αλειψαι" is the aorist active imperative form of the verb "αλείφω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "αλειψαι" is a command form that translates to "anoint" or "smear" in English. It is used to instruct someone to apply oil or another substance onto a surface or body part.
