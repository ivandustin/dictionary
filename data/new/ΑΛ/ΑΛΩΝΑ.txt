Etymology: The word "αλωνα" comes from the Greek word "αλων" (alon), which means "threshing floor" or "place where grain is separated from the chaff". 

Grammar: "Αλωνα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Αλωνα" refers to a specific location or area where the process of threshing or separating grain from the chaff takes place. It is typically an open space or a flat surface where the harvested crop is spread out and beaten or trampled to separate the edible grain from the inedible parts.
