Etymology: The word "αληθούς" (alithoús) comes from the Greek adjective "ἀληθής" (alēthḗs), meaning "true" or "real".

Grammar: "Αληθούς" is the genitive form of the adjective "ἀληθής" (alēthḗs) in Greek. It is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "Αληθούς" is a Greek word that means "of truth" or "of reality". It is often used in philosophical or metaphysical contexts to refer to something that is genuine, authentic, or in accordance with reality.
