Etymology: The word "αλατι" (alati) comes from the Greek word "άλας" (alas), which means "salt". 

Grammar: "Αλατι" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a neuter noun and belongs to the second declension. 

Description: "Αλατι" refers to salt, which is a crystalline mineral substance composed primarily of sodium chloride (NaCl). It is commonly used as a seasoning and preservative in food. Salt is also essential for the human body as it helps maintain proper fluid balance and nerve function.
