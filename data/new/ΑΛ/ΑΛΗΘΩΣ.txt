Etymology: The word "αληθως" (alēthōs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "αληθης" (alēthēs), meaning "true," and the suffix "-ως" (-ōs), which is used to form adverbs.

Grammar: "Αληθως" (alēthōs) is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate that something is done or happening in a true or genuine manner.

Description: The word "αληθως" (alēthōs) is often translated as "truly," "genuinely," or "really" in English. It is used to emphasize the truth or reality of a statement or action.
