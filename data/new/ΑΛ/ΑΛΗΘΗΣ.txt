Etymology: The word "αληθης" (alithis) comes from the Greek word "ἀληθής" (alēthēs), which means "true" or "real".

Grammar: In Greek, "αληθης" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Αληθης" is used to describe something that is true, genuine, or real. It is often used to contrast with something that is false or deceptive. It can also be used to describe a person who is honest or sincere.
