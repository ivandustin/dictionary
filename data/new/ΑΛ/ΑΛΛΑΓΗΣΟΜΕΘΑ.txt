Etymology: The word "αλλαγησομεθα" comes from the Greek verb "αλλάσσω" (allasso), which means "to change" or "to alter". The suffix "-σομεθα" (-sometha) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning "we will change ourselves".

Grammar: "αλλαγησομεθα" is a first-person plural future tense verb form in Greek. It is used to express the action of changing or altering that we (the speaker and others) will do in the future.

Description: "αλλαγησομεθα" is a word that emphasizes the idea of self-change or self-alteration. It implies that the subject (we) will actively participate in the process of transformation.
