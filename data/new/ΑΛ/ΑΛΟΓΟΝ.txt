Etymology: The word "αλογον" (alogon) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "λόγος" (logos), meaning "reason" or "logic." Therefore, "αλογον" can be translated as "illogical" or "unreasonable."

Grammar: In Greek, "αλογον" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αλογον" is used to describe something that lacks reason or logic. It can refer to an action, argument, or situation that is irrational, nonsensical, or without justification.
