Etymology: The word "αλλος" (allos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *al- meaning "other" or "different".

Grammar: In Greek, "αλλος" (allos) is an adjective. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three numbers (singular, plural, and dual). It declines according to the case, number, and gender of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αλλος" (allos) means "other" or "different" in Greek. It is used to indicate something or someone that is distinct from what has been mentioned or known before. It can also be used to express contrast or comparison.
