Word: πτωχόν

Etymology:
The word πτωχόν (ptochon) is derived from the Greek word πτωχός (ptochos), which means "poor" or "beggar". The suffix -όν (-on) is added to the end of the word to indicate the neuter gender.

Grammar:
πτωχόν is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description:
πτωχόν is an adjective that describes something as poor or impoverished. It can be used to describe a person, place, or thing. For example, one might say "Ο πτωχός άντρας ζητάει ελεημοσύνη" (The poor man is asking for charity) or "Η πτωχή γειτονιά δεν έχει πολλά πόρισματα" (The poor neighborhood doesn't have many resources).
