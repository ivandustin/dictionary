Word: πτωμα

Etymology:
The word πτωμα (ptoma) comes from the Greek verb πίπτω (pipto), which means "to fall." It is related to the noun πτῶμα (ptoma), which means "a fallen body" or "a corpse."

Grammar:
In Greek, πτωμα is a neuter noun. Its plural form is πτώματα (ptomata).

Description:
πτωμα refers to a dead body or a corpse. It is often used in the context of death and mourning. In ancient Greek culture, the proper burial of a πτωμα was considered very important, as it was believed that the soul of the deceased could not rest until the body was properly interred. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a corpse.
