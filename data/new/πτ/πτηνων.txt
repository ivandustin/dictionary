Word: πτηνων

Etymology:
The word πτηνων (ptēnōn) is derived from the Greek word πτηνός (ptēnós), which means "winged" or "flying". It is related to the Greek word πτέρυξ (ptéryx), which means "wing".

Grammar:
πτηνων is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive plural form of the noun πτηνός (ptēnós).

Description:
πτηνων refers to "winged creatures" or "birds" in Greek. It is often used in the context of mythology and literature, where it is associated with the gods and their ability to transform into birds. In scientific contexts, it can be used to refer to any flying animal, including birds, bats, and insects.
