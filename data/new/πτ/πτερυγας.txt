Word: πτερυγας

Etymology: The word πτερυγας comes from the Greek word πτέρυξ (ptéryx), which means "wing". 

Grammar: πτερυγας is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a sentence. 

Description: πτερυγας refers to the wings of a bird or other flying creature. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to something that gives a sense of freedom or the ability to soar.
