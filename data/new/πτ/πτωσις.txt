Word: πτωσις

Etymology: πτωσις comes from the Greek verb πίπτω (pípto), which means "to fall". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, which means "to fly, rush, fall".

Grammar: πτωσις is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the third declension and has a genitive singular form of πτώσεως (ptóseos).

Description: πτωσις is a Greek word that means "fall" or "falling". It can refer to a physical fall, such as falling from a height, or a metaphorical fall, such as a decline in status or reputation. In Greek literature, πτωσις is often used to describe the tragic downfall of a hero or protagonist.
