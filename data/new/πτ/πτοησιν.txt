Word: πτοησιν

Etymology:
The word πτοησιν (ptoisin) is derived from the Greek verb πτοέω (ptoéo), which means "to terrify" or "to frighten." The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a noun ending that indicates the action or result of the verb.

Grammar:
πτοησιν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is a third declension noun, which means that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description:
πτοησιν refers to the act of frightening or terrifying someone or something. It can also be used to describe the state of being frightened or terrified. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the fear or panic experienced by soldiers in battle.
