Word: πτοηθέντες

Etymology:
The word πτοηθέντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb πτοέω (ptoéo), which means "to terrify" or "to frighten". The suffix -θέντες (-théntes) is a participle suffix that indicates the action of the verb has been completed.

Grammar:
πτοηθέντες is a participle in the aorist passive voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb πτοέω (ptoéo), which means "to terrify" or "to frighten". The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, the implied "they") received the action of the verb.

Description:
πτοηθέντες can be translated as "having been terrified" or "having been frightened". It is often used in the New Testament to describe the emotional state of people who have encountered something frightening or awe-inspiring. For example, in Mark 16:5, the women who come to the tomb of Jesus are described as πτοηθεῖσαι μεγάλως (ptoētheisai megalōs), which means "greatly frightened".
