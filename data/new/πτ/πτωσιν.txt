Word: πτωσιν

Etymology:
The word πτωσιν (ptōsin) is a noun in the accusative singular form of the Greek word πτῶσις (ptōsis), which means "falling" or "fall." It is derived from the verb πίπτω (piptō), which means "to fall."

Grammar:
πτωσιν is a noun in the accusative singular form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "falling" or "fall."

Description:
πτωσιν is a term used in Greek to describe the act of falling or the state of being fallen. It can be used in a literal sense, such as the falling of an object, or in a figurative sense, such as the falling of a person from grace or power. The word is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the concept of the fall of man or the fall of the soul.
