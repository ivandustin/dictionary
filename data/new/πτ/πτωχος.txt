Word: πτωχος

Etymology:
The word πτωχος (ptochos) comes from the Greek root word πτωσις (ptosis), which means "falling" or "fallen." It originally referred to someone who had fallen from a higher social or economic status and was now poor or destitute.

Grammar:
In Greek, πτωχος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person or thing that is poor, needy, or lacking in resources. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
πτωχος is a Greek word that is often translated as "poor" or "needy." It can refer to someone who is financially poor, but it can also be used more broadly to describe someone who is lacking in any kind of resources or support.
