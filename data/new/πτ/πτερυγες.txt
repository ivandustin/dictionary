Word: πτερυγες

Etymology: The word πτερυγες comes from the Greek word πτέρυξ (ptéryx), which means "wing". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, which means "to fly".

Grammar: πτερυγες is a noun in the Greek language. It is plural and refers to wings.

Description: πτερυγες are the structures that enable birds, insects, and some other animals to fly. They are typically composed of feathers or membranes stretched over a framework of bones. In birds, the wings are also used for balance and steering during flight. Insects have two pairs of wings, which are often used for display or to regulate body temperature.
