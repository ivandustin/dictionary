Word: πτυρομενοι

Etymology: 
The word πτυρομενοι is a present participle form of the Greek verb πτύω (ptyo), which means "to spit". The suffix -μενοι (-menoi) indicates that the action is being performed by a group of people. Therefore, πτυρομενοι means "spitting together" or "spitting in unison".

Grammar:
πτυρομενοι is a present participle form of the verb πτύω (ptyo), which is in the middle voice. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, the subject is a group of people who are spitting together.

Description:
πτυρομενοι is a rare word that appears only once in the New Testament (Mark 14:65). In this passage, it describes how the people who were mocking Jesus spat on him. The word conveys a sense of contempt and disrespect towards the person being spat upon.
