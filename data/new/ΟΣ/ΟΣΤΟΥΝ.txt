Etymology: The word "οστούν" (ostoun) comes from the Greek word "οστό" (osto), which means "bone". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃óst(h₁)-.

Grammar: "Οστούν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the neuter gender. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "οστό".

Description: "Οστούν" refers to a bone in Greek. It is a hard, rigid organ that forms part of the skeletal system in vertebrates. Bones provide support, protect internal organs, and facilitate movement.
