Etymology: The word "οστεων" (osteōn) comes from the Greek word "ὀστέον" (ostéon), which means "bone".

Grammar: In Greek, "οστεων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "οστον" (oston), which means "bone". The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "Οστεων" is a Greek word that refers to bones. It is the plural form of "οστον" and is used to describe multiple bones. Bones are rigid organs that make up the skeletal system of vertebrates, providing support, protection, and movement.
