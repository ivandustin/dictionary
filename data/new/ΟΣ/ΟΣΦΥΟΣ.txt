Etymology: The word "οσφυος" (osphyos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃esp-, meaning "to breathe" or "to exhale."

Grammar: In Greek, "οσφυος" (osphyos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case, singular number.

Description: "Οσφυος" (osphyos) refers to the lower back or the lumbar region of the human body. It is the area between the ribs and the pelvis.
