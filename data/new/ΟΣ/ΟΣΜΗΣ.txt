Etymology: The word "οσμης" (osmēs) comes from the Greek word "ὀσμή" (osmē), meaning "smell" or "odor".

Grammar: In Greek, "οσμης" (osmēs) is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of the word "οσμη" (osmē), which means "smell" or "odor".

Description: "Οσμης" (osmēs) refers to the sense of smell or the perception of odors. It is used to describe the ability to detect and recognize different scents or aromas.
