Etymology: The word "οσφρησις" comes from the Greek word "οσφραίνομαι" (osphrainomai), which means "to smell" or "to perceive by the sense of smell." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "οσ-" (os-), meaning "towards," and the root "φρήν" (phrēn), meaning "mind" or "soul."

Grammar: In Greek, "οσφρησις" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension and has the genitive form "οσφρησεως" (osphrēseōs).

Description: "Οσφρησις" refers to the sense of smell or the act of smelling. It is the ability to perceive odors and scents through the olfactory system. The sense of smell plays an important role in our perception of the world and can evoke strong emotions and memories.
