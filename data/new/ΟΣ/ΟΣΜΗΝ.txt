Etymology: The word "οσμην" (osmēn) comes from the Greek word "οσμή" (osmē), meaning "smell" or "odor".

Grammar: "Οσμην" (osmēn) is a noun in the accusative case. It is the direct object of a sentence and refers to a specific smell or odor.

Description: "Οσμην" (osmēn) is used to describe a particular scent or fragrance. It can refer to both pleasant and unpleasant smells. The word is often used in the context of perfumes, aromas, or any kind of olfactory sensation.
