The word "εζητει" (ezetei) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "ζητώ" (zito), which means "to seek" or "to search for". The prefix "ε-" is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the present tense.

Grammar: "Εζητεί" is the third person singular present tense form of the verb. It is conjugated as follows: 

- Εγώ ζητώ (ego zito) - I seek
- Εσύ ζητάς (esi zitas) - You seek
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό ζητεί (aftos/afti/afto ziti) - He/She/It seeks
- Εμείς ζητούμε (emeis zitoume) - We seek
- Εσείς ζητάτε (eseis zitate) - You (plural) seek
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά ζητούν (aftoi/aftes/afta zitoun) - They seek

Description: The verb "εζητεί" is used to describe the act of seeking or searching for something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as searching for information, looking for a lost item, or trying to find a solution to a problem.
