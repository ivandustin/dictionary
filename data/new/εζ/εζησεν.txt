Word: εζησεν

Etymology: εζησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ζωή (zoē), which means "life". The verb εζησεν is the aorist tense of the verb ζῶ (zō), which means "to live".

Grammar: εζησεν is a third person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: εζησεν means "he/she/it lived" in Greek. It is often used in the context of describing someone's life or the duration of someone's life. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that has survived or endured.
