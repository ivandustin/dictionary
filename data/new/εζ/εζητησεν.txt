Word: εζητησεν

Etymology: εζητησεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ζητέω (zeteo), which means "to seek" or "to search for". The prefix ε- (e-) is a past tense marker, indicating that the action of seeking has already been completed.

Grammar: εζητησεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb ζητέω (zeteo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εζητησεν can be translated as "he/she/it sought" or "he/she/it searched for". It is a past tense form, indicating that the action of seeking or searching has already been completed. The context of the sentence would determine the specific meaning and usage of the word.
