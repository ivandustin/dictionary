Word: εζησα

Etymology: εζησα is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ζω (zo), which means "to live". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the first person singular past tense.

Grammar: εζησα is a first person singular past tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb ζω (zo), which means "to live". The verb is in the indicative mood, which is used to make statements or ask questions.

Description: εζησα means "I lived" in Greek. It is used to describe a past action of living or existing. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as to describe a personal experience or to talk about a historical event.
