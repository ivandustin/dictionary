Word: εζων

Etymology: The word εζων comes from the Greek word ζώνη (zoni), which means "belt" or "girdle". 

Grammar: εζων is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. It is declined in the first declension and has the genitive form εζωνος (ezonos). 

Description: εζων refers to a belt or girdle worn around the waist. In ancient Greece, it was commonly worn by men and women as a functional accessory to hold clothing in place or to carry weapons. It was also used as a symbol of status and authority, worn by soldiers, officials, and even gods in art and mythology.
