Etymology: The word "ζηλωσαντες" comes from the Greek verb "ζηλόω" (zēloō), which means "to be jealous" or "to envy". The suffix "-σαντες" (-santes) is a participle ending, indicating that the action of jealousy or envy has been performed.

Grammar: "ζηλωσαντες" is the third person plural form of the aorist active participle of the verb "ζηλόω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "ζηλωσαντες" describes a group of people who have experienced jealousy or envy. It can be translated as "they were jealous" or "they envied" in English.
