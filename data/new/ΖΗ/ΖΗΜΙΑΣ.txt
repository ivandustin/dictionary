Etymology: The word "ζημιας" (zēmias) comes from the Greek verb "ζημιόω" (zēmióō), which means "to damage" or "to harm". 

Grammar: "ζημιας" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "ζημιας" refers to the concept of damage or harm. It can be used to describe physical, financial, or emotional harm.
