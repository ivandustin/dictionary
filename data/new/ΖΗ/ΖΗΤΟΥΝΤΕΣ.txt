Etymology: The word "ζητούντες" comes from the Greek verb "ζητέω" (zeteo), which means "to seek" or "to search". The suffix "-ούντες" is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "ζητούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "ζητέω" (zeteo) in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently seeking or searching for something. In terms of grammar, it is a masculine plural form.

Description: "ζητούντες" refers to a group of people who are actively seeking or searching for something. It can be used to describe individuals who are looking for answers, solutions, or information. The word implies a sense of purpose and determination in the act of seeking.
