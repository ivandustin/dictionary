Etymology: The word "ζηλωτήν" (zēlōtēn) comes from the Greek verb "ζηλόω" (zēlóō), which means "to be jealous" or "to be zealous." It is derived from the noun "ζῆλος" (zēlos), meaning "zeal" or "jealousy."

Grammar: "ζηλωτήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, masculine gender. It is the accusative form of the noun "ζηλωτής" (zēlōtēs), which means "zealot" or "one who is zealous."

Description: "ζηλωτήν" refers to a person who is zealous or passionate about a particular cause, belief, or ideology. It can also be used to describe someone who is jealous or envious.
