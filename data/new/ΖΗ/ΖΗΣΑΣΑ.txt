Etymology: The word "ζησασα" (zēsasa) is a Greek verb form that comes from the root word "ζάω" (zaō), meaning "to live" or "to be alive."

Grammar: "ζησασα" (zēsasa) is the aorist participle form of the verb "ζάω" (zaō) in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the participle form functions as an adjective.

Description: "ζησασα" (zēsasa) describes a female subject who has lived or was alive at some point in the past. It can be used to indicate that the subject was alive for a specific period of time or that she experienced a particular event.
