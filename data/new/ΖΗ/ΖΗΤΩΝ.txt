Etymology: The word "ζητών" (zētōn) comes from the Greek verb "ζητέω" (zēteō), which means "to seek" or "to search for."

Grammar: "ζητών" is the present participle form of the verb "ζητέω" (zēteō) in the masculine singular nominative case. It is used to describe someone who is currently seeking or searching for something.

Description: "ζητών" refers to a person who is actively seeking or searching for something. It can be used to describe someone who is looking for answers, solutions, or information.
