Etymology: The word "ζηλού" (zēlou) comes from the Greek verb "ζηλώ" (zēlō), which means "to be jealous" or "to envy." 

Grammar: "ζηλού" is the present tense, second person singular form of the verb "ζηλώ" (zēlō). It is used when addressing one person and expressing jealousy or envy.

Description: The word "ζηλού" is used to describe the action of feeling jealous or envious towards someone or something. It is often used in the context of desiring what someone else has or feeling resentful towards their success or advantages.
