Etymology: The word "ζησω" (zēsō) comes from the Greek verb "ζάω" (záō), which means "to live" or "to be alive."

Grammar: ζησω (zēsō) is a first-person singular future tense verb in Greek. It is derived from the verb ζάω (záō) and is conjugated in the future tense to indicate that the action of living will happen in the future.

Description: ζησω (zēsō) is used to express the idea of "I will live" or "I will be alive" in Greek. It is commonly used to talk about future plans or expectations regarding one's life or existence.
