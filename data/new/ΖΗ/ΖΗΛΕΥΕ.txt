Etymology: The word "ζηλευε" (zēleue) comes from the Greek verb "ζηλεύω" (zēleuō), which means "to be jealous" or "to envy." 

Grammar: "ζηλευε" is the present tense, third person singular form of the verb "ζηλεύω" (zēleuō). It is used to describe someone who is currently jealous or envious.

Description: "ζηλευε" is a word in the Greek language that expresses the action of being jealous or envious. It is commonly used to describe the feeling of wanting what someone else has or feeling resentful towards their success or possessions.
