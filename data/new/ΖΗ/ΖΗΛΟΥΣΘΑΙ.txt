Etymology: The word "ζηλούσθαι" (zēloústhai) comes from the Greek verb "ζηλόω" (zēlóō), which means "to be jealous" or "to envy." It is derived from the noun "ζῆλος" (zêlos), meaning "zeal" or "ardor."

Grammar: "ζηλούσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "ζηλόω." In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "ζηλούσθαι" is a verb that expresses the action of feeling jealous or envious. It is used to describe the emotion of wanting what someone else has or feeling resentful towards their success or possessions.
