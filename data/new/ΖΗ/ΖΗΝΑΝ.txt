Etymology: The word "ζηναν" (zenan) comes from the Greek verb "ζῆν" (zen), which means "to live" or "to be alive."

Grammar: "ζηναν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "ζῆν" (zen).

Description: "ζηναν" is used to describe the action of living or being alive. It can refer to both physical and metaphorical existence.
