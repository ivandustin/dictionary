Etymology: The word "ζητησον" (zētēson) comes from the Greek verb "ζητέω" (zēteō), which means "to seek" or "to search for". 

Grammar: "ζητησον" is the imperative form of the verb "ζητέω" in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or request someone to seek or search for something.

Description: "ζητησον" is a word in the Greek language that instructs someone to seek or search for something. It is commonly used in commands or requests to ask someone to find or look for a specific object, information, or solution.
