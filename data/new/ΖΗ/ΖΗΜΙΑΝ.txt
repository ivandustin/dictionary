Etymology: The word "ζημιαν" (zēmian) comes from the Greek verb "ζημιόω" (zēmióō), which means "to damage" or "to harm."

Grammar: "ζημιαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "ζημιαν" refers to the concept of "damage" or "harm." It can be used to describe physical, emotional, or financial harm caused by an action or event.
