Etymology: The word "ζητούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "ζητέω" (zeteo), which means "to seek" or "to search for". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʰed-.

Grammar: "ζητούσιν" is the present active participle, third person plural, of the verb "ζητέω" (zeteo). In Greek grammar, the present active participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people.

Description: "ζητούσιν" can be translated as "they are seeking" or "they are searching for" in English. It describes a group of people who are currently engaged in the act of seeking or searching for something.
