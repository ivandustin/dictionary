Etymology: The word "ηκει" (pronounced "ekei") comes from the Greek verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". It is the third person singular form of the present tense.

Grammar: "Ηκει" is a verb in the indicative mood, present tense, third person singular. In Greek, verbs change their form based on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The verb "ηκει" indicates that someone or something is coming or arriving at a specific location.

Description: "Ηκει" is a simple verb that describes the action of coming or arriving. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing to indicate movement towards a particular place.
