Etymology: The word "ηκουσθη" (ēkousthē) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouō), which means "to hear." It is formed by adding the prefix "ε-" (e-) and the suffix "-θη" (-thē) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "ηκουσθη" is the third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb "ακούω" (akouō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "ηκουσθη" means "he/she/it was heard" in English. It describes a past event where someone or something was listened to or perceived by someone else.
