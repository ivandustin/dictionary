Word: ηθελησας

Etymology:
The word ηθελησας is derived from the Greek verb θέλω (thélo), which means "to want" or "to wish". The prefix η (i) is added to the verb to indicate the past tense, making ηθελησας mean "you wanted" or "you wished".

Grammar:
ηθελησας is a second-person singular past tense form of the verb θέλω (thélo). It is used to indicate that the subject (you) wanted or wished for something in the past. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, person, and number of the subject.

Description:
ηθελησας is a word that expresses a past desire or wish. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing regret for not having done something in the past or expressing satisfaction for having achieved something that was desired. The word is commonly used in everyday conversation and is an important part of the Greek language.
