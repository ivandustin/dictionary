Word: ηθελησεν

Etymology: ηθελησεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb θέλω (thélo), which means "to will" or "to want". The prefix η- (e-) is a past tense marker, so ηθελησεν means "he/she/it wanted" or "he/she/it willed".

Grammar: ηθελησεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb θέλω (thélo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the third person singular indicates that the subject is he/she/it.

Description: ηθελησεν is a word that expresses the idea of wanting or willing something in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing a desire, a decision, or a preference. For example, "Ηθελησεν να φάει πίτσα" (He/she/it wanted to eat pizza) or "Ηθελησεν να πάει στην παραλία" (He/she/it wanted to go to the beach).
