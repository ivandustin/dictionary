Word: ηθελησα

Etymology:
The word ηθελησα is derived from the Greek verb θέλω (thélo), which means "to want" or "to wish". The prefix η (i) is added to the verb to indicate the past tense, making ηθελησα (ithelisa) mean "I wanted" or "I wished".

Grammar:
ηθελησα is a first-person singular past tense form of the verb θέλω (thélo). It is used to express a past desire or wish. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, person, and number of the subject. The past tense is formed by adding the prefix η (i) to the verb stem, and the first-person singular ending -σα (-sa) is added to indicate the subject.

Description:
ηθελησα is a common Greek word that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express a past desire or wish, such as "I wanted to go to the beach yesterday, but it was raining".
