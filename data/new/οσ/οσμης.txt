Word: οσμης

Etymology:
The word οσμης (osmes) is derived from the Greek word "οσμή" (osmē), which means "smell" or "odor". It is related to the verb "οσμέω" (osmeō), which means "to smell".

Grammar:
In Greek, οσμης is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its genitive form is οσμης, and its accusative form is οσμην.

Description:
Οσμης refers to the sense of smell or the quality of a scent or odor. It can also refer to a fragrance or perfume. The word is commonly used in scientific and medical contexts to describe the sense of smell or the chemical compounds that produce a particular odor.
