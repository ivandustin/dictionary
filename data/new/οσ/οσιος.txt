Word: οσιος

Etymology:
The word οσιος (osios) comes from the Greek adjective ὅσιος (hosios), which means "pious", "holy", or "devout". It is derived from the noun ὅσιος (hosios), which means "piety" or "holiness".

Grammar:
In Greek, οσιος (osios) is an adjective that can be used to describe a person or thing as pious, holy, or devout. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
Οσιος (osios) is a term used in the Eastern Orthodox Church to describe a person who has lived a life of exceptional holiness and devotion to God. It is often used to refer to monks and nuns who have dedicated their lives to prayer and asceticism. In the Orthodox tradition, the lives of the saints are celebrated and venerated as examples of how to live a life of faith and devotion.
