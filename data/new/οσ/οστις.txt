Word: οστις

Etymology:
The word οστις (ostis) is a Greek pronoun that means "whoever" or "anyone who". It is derived from the Greek word "οστις" (hostis), which means "anyone" or "whoever".

Grammar:
In Greek, οστις is a pronoun that is used to refer to a person or thing in an indefinite way. It is declined like a regular noun, with different forms for the nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases. It can be used as a subject, object, or possessive pronoun.

Description:
Οστις is a versatile pronoun that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to refer to an unknown or unspecified person or thing, as in "οστις θελει να μιλησει;" (whoever wants to speak?). It can also be used to express a general idea or concept, as in "οστις αγαπα την μουσικη" (anyone who loves music). Overall, οστις is a useful pronoun for expressing indefinite or general ideas in Greek.
