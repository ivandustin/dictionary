Word: οσους

Etymology: οσους is a Greek word that comes from the combination of the pronoun "ος" (who, which) and the particle "ους" (some, any).

Grammar: οσους is a pronoun in the Greek language that is used to refer to people or things in the plural form. It is used as an indirect object or as the object of a preposition.

Description: οσους is a versatile pronoun that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to refer to a group of people or things that are not specifically identified. For example, "Οσους φίλους έχεις;" (How many friends do you have?) or "Δεν ξέρω οσους ανθρώπους ήρθαν στο πάρτι" (I don't know how many people came to the party).
