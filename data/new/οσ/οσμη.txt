Word: οσμη

The word οσμη (osmē) comes from the Ancient Greek ὀσμή (osmḗ), which means "smell" or "odor". It is derived from the verb ὀζέω (ozéō), which means "to smell".

In Greek, οσμη is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is οσμη, and its genitive singular form is οσμης. The word can be declined in all cases and numbers.

Description:
Οσμη refers to the sense of smell or the quality of a scent. It can also refer to a fragrance or aroma.
