Word: οστούν

Etymology: The word οστούν comes from the Ancient Greek word "ὀστοῦν" (ostoun), which means "bone".

Grammar: οστούν is a neuter noun in Greek. It is in the nominative singular form. The genitive singular form is οστούνος (ostounos), the accusative singular form is οστούν (ostoun), and the nominative plural form is οστά (osta).

Description: οστούν refers to a bone, which is a hard, dense, and rigid connective tissue that forms the skeleton of vertebrates. Bones provide support and protection for the body, as well as serve as a site for muscle attachment and blood cell production. In Greek mythology, bones were believed to be the last part of the body to decay after death, and were therefore considered to be a symbol of immortality.
