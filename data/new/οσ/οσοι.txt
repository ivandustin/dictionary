Word: οσοι

Etymology: οσοι is a Greek word that comes from the combination of the adverb οσο (oso) meaning "as much as" and the pronoun οι (oi) meaning "those". 

Grammar: οσοι is a plural pronoun in Greek that is used to refer to a group of people or things. It is often translated as "those who" or "whoever" in English. 

Description: οσοι is a versatile pronoun that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is commonly used in Greek to introduce a group of people or things that share a common characteristic or quality. For example, "οσοι αγαπούν τη μουσική" (those who love music). It can also be used to express a condition or requirement, such as "οσοι έχουν διαβατήριο" (whoever has a passport).
