Grammar: γης is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship, and is often translated as "of" in English.

Description: γης is a word that refers to the earth or land. It can be used in a literal sense to describe the physical planet we live on, or in a metaphorical sense to describe the ground or soil. In Greek mythology, γης was also the name of the goddess of the earth.
