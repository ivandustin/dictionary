The word "γηρει" (geri) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "γηράσκω" (gerasko), which means "to grow old" or "to age". 

In terms of grammar, "γηρει" is a third person singular present indicative active verb. It is often used in the context of describing the process of aging or the state of being old. 

For example, one might say "Ο παππούς μου γηρει" (O pappous mou geri), which translates to "My grandfather is aging" or "My grandfather is growing old". 

Overall, "γηρει" is a word that is closely associated with the concept of aging and the passage of time.
