The word "γηρασηις" (gerasēs) is a Greek noun that means "aging" or "old age". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "γηράσκω" (geraskō), which means "to grow old" or "to age". 

Grammar: "γηρασηις" is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of describing the physical and mental changes that occur as a person grows older. It can also refer to the process of aging itself. In Greek mythology, the goddess of youth, Hebe, was believed to have the power to reverse the effects of aging.
