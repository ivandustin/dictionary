Word: γη

Etymology:
The word γη (gē) comes from the Ancient Greek language and is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʰem- meaning "earth" or "land". It is related to the Latin word "humus" which means "soil" or "earth".

Description:
The word γη refers to the planet Earth, the ground, or the soil. In Greek mythology, it was also the name of the goddess of the earth. The word is used in various contexts, such as in scientific discussions about the planet, in poetry to describe the beauty of nature, and in religious texts to symbolize the creation of the world.
