Word: ιδιοις

Etymology: ιδιοις is a Greek word that comes from the root word "ιδιος" (idios), which means "one's own" or "private". The suffix "-ις" (-is) is added to indicate the dative plural form, meaning "to/for one's own".

Grammar: ιδιοις is a dative plural form of the adjective ιδιος (idios), which means "own" or "private". In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: ιδιοις is used in Greek to indicate possession or ownership. It can be translated as "to/for one's own" or "to/for themselves". For example, "Οι φίλοι μου μίλησαν με ιδιοις λόγοις" (Oi filoi mou milisan me idiois logois) means "My friends spoke to me in their own words".
