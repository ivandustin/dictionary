Word: ιδου

Etymology:
The word ιδου (idou) is a Greek word that is derived from the verb εἶδον (eidon), which means "to see" or "to behold". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible as an interjection to draw attention to something important.

Grammar:
Ιδου is a particle in Greek that is used to draw attention to something. It is often translated as "behold" or "look". It is used to introduce a new idea or to emphasize something that has already been said.
