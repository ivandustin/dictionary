Word: ιδετε

Etymology: ιδετε is a Greek word that comes from the verb ιδείν (idein), which means "to see" or "to perceive."

Grammar: ιδετε is the second person plural form of the present tense of the verb ιδείν (idein). It is used to address a group of people and means "you all see" or "you all perceive."

Description: ιδετε is a common word in modern Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to draw attention to something or to indicate that something is visible or noticeable. For example, "Ιδετε τον ήλιο που ανατέλλει;" (Idete ton iliο pou anatellei?) means "Do you all see the sun rising?"
