The word "ιδιας" (idias) is a Greek adjective that means "own" or "personal". It is derived from the Greek noun "ιδιος" (idios), which means "one's own" or "private". In terms of grammar, "ιδιας" is in the feminine singular form, and it agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. For example, "της ιδιας μου ζωης" (tis idias mou zois) means "of my own life" in Greek.
