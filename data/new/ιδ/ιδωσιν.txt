Word: ιδωσιν

Etymology: ιδωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb οραω (orao), which means "to see". It is in the third person plural, aorist tense, and subjunctive mood.

Grammar: ιδωσιν is a verb in the Greek language. As mentioned above, it is in the third person plural, aorist tense, and subjunctive mood. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations.

Description: ιδωσιν is a verb that means "they might see" or "they may see" in English. It is often used in Greek literature and religious texts to express the possibility of seeing something in the future.
