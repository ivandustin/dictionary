Word: ιδουσα

Etymology:
The word ιδουσα (idousa) is a form of the Greek verb εἶδον (eidon), which means "to see" or "to perceive". It is the aorist participle feminine singular form of the verb, which means it describes a completed action in the past. 

Grammar:
In Greek, verbs change form depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The form ιδουσα (idousa) is in the aorist tense, which describes a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, which means the subject is performing the action. The participle form is used to modify a noun or pronoun, and in this case, it is feminine singular to agree with the gender and number of the subject it is modifying.

Description:
The word ιδουσα (idousa) can be translated as "having seen" or "having perceived" in English.
