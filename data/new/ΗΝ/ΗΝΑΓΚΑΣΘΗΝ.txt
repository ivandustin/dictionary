The word "ηναγκασθην" (enagkasithin) is a Greek verb in the aorist passive tense. Here is the breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "ηναγκασθην" is derived from the Greek verb "αναγκάζω" (anagkazo), which means "to force" or "to compel". The passive form "ηναγκασθην" indicates that the subject of the verb is being forced or compelled to do something.

Grammar: "ηναγκασθην" is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe completed actions in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it. In this case, the subject is being forced or compelled to do something.

Description: The verb "ηναγκασθην" describes a situation where someone is being forced or compelled to do something against their will. It implies a lack of choice or control in the matter.
