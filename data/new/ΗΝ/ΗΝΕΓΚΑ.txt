Etymology: The word "ηνεγκα" (eneka) comes from the Greek verb "φέρνω" (ferno), which means "to carry" or "to bring". It is formed by adding the prefix "εν-" (en-) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "Ηνεγκα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this case, "ηνεγκα" indicates that the action of carrying or bringing has been completed by the speaker.

Description: "Ηνεγκα" is a verb in Greek that means "I carried" or "I brought". It is used to describe an action of carrying or bringing something in the past.
