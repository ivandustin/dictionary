Etymology: The word "ηνεγκαν" (enekan) comes from the Greek verb "φέρνω" (ferno), which means "to carry" or "to bring". It is formed by adding the prefix "εν-" (en-) and the suffix "-καν" (-kan) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "Ηνεγκαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense of the verb "φέρνω" (ferno). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description: "Ηνεγκαν" is a verb in Greek that means "they brought" or "they carried". It is used to describe an action of bringing or carrying something in the past.
