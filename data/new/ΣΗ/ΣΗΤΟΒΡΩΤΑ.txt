Etymology: The word "σητοβρωτα" (sētovrōta) comes from the Greek words "σητα" (sēta), meaning "moth," and "βρωτα" (vrōta), meaning "eater." 

Grammar: "Σητοβρωτα" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Σητοβρωτα" refers to moths, which are insects belonging to the order Lepidoptera. Moths are known for their distinctive scales on their wings and their nocturnal habits. They are often attracted to light sources and can be found in various habitats worldwide. Some species of moths are considered pests as their larvae feed on fabrics, stored grains, and other organic materials.
