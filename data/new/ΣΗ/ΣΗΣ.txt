Etymology: The word "σης" (sēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "σῆς" (sês), which means "your" or "yours".

Grammar: In Greek, "σης" (sēs) is a possessive pronoun in the second person singular. It is used to indicate ownership or possession. It is equivalent to the English word "your" or "yours".

Description: "σης" (sēs) is used to refer to something that belongs to the person being addressed. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts in Greek.
