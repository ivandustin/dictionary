Etymology: The word "σημειοις" (sēmeiois) comes from the Greek word "σημεῖον" (sēmeion), meaning "sign" or "mark."

Grammar: "Σημειοις" is the plural form of the noun "σημεῖον" (sēmeion) in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Σημειοις" refers to "signs" or "marks" in Greek. It can be used to describe physical signs, symbols, or indicators that convey information or meaning. In a broader sense, it can also refer to any kind of evidence or indication of something.
