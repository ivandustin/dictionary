Etymology: The word "ψυχαί" (pronounced "psyche") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "ψυχή" (pronounced "psuche"), which means "soul" or "spirit."

Grammar: "Ψυχαί" is the plural form of the noun "ψυχή" in Greek. It is used to refer to multiple souls or spirits.

Description: In Greek mythology and philosophy, the concept of "ψυχή" (soul) is central. It is believed to be the immaterial essence or life force that animates living beings. The soul is often associated with the mind, emotions, and consciousness. It is considered to be the seat of one's thoughts, feelings, and identity. The plural form "ψυχαί" is used to refer to multiple souls or spirits.
