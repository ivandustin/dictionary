Etymology: The word "ψυχικός" (psychikós) comes from the Greek word "ψυχή" (psychḗ), meaning "soul" or "mind." It is derived from the verb "ψύχω" (psýchō), which means "to breathe" or "to blow."

Grammar: In Greek, "ψυχικός" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. For example, "ψυχικός άνθρωπος" (psychikós ánthropos) means "psychic person" in the masculine form, while "ψυχική δύναμη" (psychikí dýnami) means "psychic power" in the feminine form.

Description: The word "ψυχικός" is used to describe something related to the mind, soul, or psychic abilities. It can refer to mental or psychological aspects, such as emotions, thoughts, or behaviors. In a broader sense, it can also refer to paranormal or supernatural phenomena, such as extrasensory perception or telepathy.
