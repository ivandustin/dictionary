Word: ανθυπατος

Etymology:
The word ανθυπατος (anthypatos) is derived from the Greek words αντί (anti), meaning "instead of" or "in place of," and υπατος (hypatos), meaning "consul" or "governor." Therefore, ανθυπατος originally referred to a person who acted as a substitute or deputy for a governor or consul.
