Word: ανθρωπινων

Etymology: The word ανθρωπινων is derived from the Greek word "άνθρωπος" (anthropos) which means "human" and the suffix "-ινων" (-inon) which is used to form adjectives indicating possession or belonging. Therefore, ανθρωπινων means "of humans" or "belonging to humans".

Grammar: ανθρωπινων is a plural genitive form of the adjective ανθρώπινος (anthropinos) which means "human" or "related to humans". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description: ανθρωπινων is often used in Greek to describe things that are related to or belong to humans. For example, ανθρωπινων δικαιωμάτων (anthropinon dikaiomaton) means "human rights" and ανθρωπινων πόρων (anthropinon poron) means "human resources". It can also be used to describe characteristics or qualities that are typically associated with humans, such as ανθρωπινων αδυναμιών (anthropinon adynamion) which means "human weaknesses".
