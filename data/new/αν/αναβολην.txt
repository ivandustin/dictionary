The word "αναβολην" (anabolēn) is a Greek noun that means "delay" or "postponement". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "αναβάλλω" (anaballō), which means "to postpone" or "to defer". The noun form "αναβολην" is formed by adding the suffix "-ην" (-ēn) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "αναβολην" is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: The word "αναβολην" is commonly used in Greek to refer to a delay or postponement of an event, action, or decision. It can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a hesitation or procrastination in taking action. The word is often used in legal or bureaucratic contexts to refer to the extension of a deadline or the postponement of a hearing or trial.
