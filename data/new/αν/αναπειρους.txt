The word "αναπειρους" (anapeiros) is a Greek adjective that means "inexperienced" or "unskilled". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "ana-" meaning "up" or "again" and the adjective "peiros" meaning "experienced" or "skilled". 

Grammar: "αναπειρους" is in the masculine plural form, and it agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who lacks experience or skill in a particular field or activity. It can also be used to describe something that is poorly done or executed.
