Word: ανεχωρησεν

Etymology: ανεχωρησεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb αναχωρώ (anachoró), which means "to withdraw" or "to retreat."

The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: ανεχωρησεν means "he/she/it withdrew" or "he/she/it retreated" in English. It is a verb that describes an action of moving away from a place or situation. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in military or political situations, or in personal relationships.
