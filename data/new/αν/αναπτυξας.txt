Word: αναπτυξας

Etymology: αναπτυξας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ανάπτυξη (anaptixi), which means "development" or "growth". The verb form αναπτύσσω (anaptysso) means "to develop" or "to unfold".

Grammar: αναπτυξας is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb αναπτύσσω (anaptysso). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: αναπτυξας can be translated to English as "you developed" or "you unfolded". It describes a completed action in the past where the subject was actively involved in the development or unfolding of something. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was being developed or unfolded.
