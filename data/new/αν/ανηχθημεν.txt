Word: ανηχθημεν

Etymology: ανηχθημεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ανάγω (anago), which means "to lead up" or "to bring up". The word is formed by adding the prefix αν- (an-) which means "up" or "again", and the suffix -ημεν (-emen) which is a past participle ending.

Grammar: ανηχθημεν is a past participle in the passive voice, which means that the action of the verb is done to the subject. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it is implied that someone or something was led up or brought up.

Description: ανηχθημεν can be translated as "having been led up" or "having been brought up". It is a passive verb form that describes an action that was done to the subject. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action.
