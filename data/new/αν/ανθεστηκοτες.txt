Word: ανθεστηκοτες

Etymology: The word ανθεστηκοτες comes from the Greek verb ανθίστημι (anthistemi), which means "to stand up against" or "to resist". The suffix -κοτες (-kotes) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of people who have stood up against something.

Grammar: ανθεστηκοτες is a plural noun in the masculine gender. It is declined like the third declension noun κριτής (kritis), with the genitive singular form being ανθεστηκότων (anthistekoton).

Description: ανθεστηκοτες refers to a group of people who have stood up against something, often used in the context of political or social resistance. It can also be used to describe someone who is defiant or rebellious. The word has a strong connotation of courage and determination in the face of opposition.
