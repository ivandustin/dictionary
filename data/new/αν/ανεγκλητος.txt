Word: ανεγκλητος

Etymology:
The word ανεγκλητος comes from the Greek prefix αν- (an-) meaning "not" and the word εγκλητος (enklitos) meaning "accused" or "charged". Therefore, ανεγκλητος means "not accused" or "not charged".

Grammar:
ανεγκλητος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, ανεγκλητος can modify a masculine singular noun in the nominative case, such as άνδρας (man), to form the phrase ανεγκλητος άνδρας (innocent man).

Description:
ανεγκλητος is a Greek word that means "innocent" or "not guilty". It is often used in legal contexts to describe someone who has not been accused or charged with a crime. The word can also be used more broadly to describe someone who is blameless or free from wrongdoing.
