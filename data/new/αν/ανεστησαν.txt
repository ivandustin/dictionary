Word: ανεστησαν

Etymology: ανεστησαν is a Greek word that comes from the verb αναστήθηκα (anastithika), which means "to rise" or "to stand up". It is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "again", and the verb στήθηκα (stithika), which means "to stand".

Grammar: ανεστησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb αναστήθηκα (anastithika). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: ανεστησαν means "they rose" or "they stood up". It is often used in the context of the resurrection of Jesus Christ in the New Testament of the Bible.
