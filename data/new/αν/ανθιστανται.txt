Word: ανθιστανται

Etymology:
The word ανθιστανται is derived from the Greek verb ανθίστημι (anthistemi), which means "to resist" or "to oppose". It is formed by adding the prefix αντι- (anti-) meaning "against" to the verb ιστημι (istemi) meaning "to stand".

Grammar:
ανθιστανται is a third-person plural present indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb ανθίστημι (anthistemi) and is used to describe an action of resistance or opposition that is currently happening in the present.

Description:
ανθιστανται is a Greek verb that describes an action of resistance or opposition. It can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, social movements, or personal relationships. The word implies a sense of strength and determination in the face of adversity.
