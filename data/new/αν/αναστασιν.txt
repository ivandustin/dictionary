The word "αναστασιν" (anastasin) is a Greek noun that means "resurrection" or "rising up". It is derived from the verb "ανίστημι" (anistemi), which means "to rise up" or "to stand up". In Greek Orthodox Christianity, the word is often used to refer to the resurrection of Jesus Christ. 

In terms of grammar, "αναστασιν" is a third declension noun in the accusative case. Its singular form is "ανάσταση" (anastasi) and its plural form is "αναστάσεις" (anastaseis). 

Overall, "αναστασιν" is a significant word in Greek culture and religion, representing the idea of rising up and overcoming obstacles.
