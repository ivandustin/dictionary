Word: αναιρεθηναι

Etymology: αναιρεθηναι is a Greek word that comes from the verb αναιρεω (an-aire-o), which means "to take away" or "to abolish". The word is formed by adding the suffix -θηναι (-thenai) to the verb stem.

Grammar: αναιρεθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not being used in a specific tense or person, but rather as a verbal noun.

Description: αναιρεθηναι can be translated as "to be abolished" or "to be taken away". It is often used in legal or philosophical contexts to refer to the nullification or cancellation of a law or argument. In Christian theology, the word is used to describe the concept of "atonement", or the idea that Jesus' death on the cross abolished the power of sin and death.
