Word: αναστας

Etymology: αναστας is a Greek word that comes from the verb ανίστημι (anístimi), which means "to rise up" or "to stand up". It is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "again", and the verb ίστημι (ístimi), which means "to stand".

Grammar: αναστας is the aorist participle of the verb ανίστημι (anístimi), which means "having risen up" or "having stood up". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: αναστας is a word that describes someone who has risen up or stood up. It can be used in a literal sense, such as someone who has stood up from a seated position, or in a figurative sense, such as someone who has risen up to face a challenge or overcome an obstacle.
