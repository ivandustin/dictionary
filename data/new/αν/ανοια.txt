Word: ανοια

Etymology:
The word ανοια (anoia) comes from the ancient Greek word ἄνοια (anoia), which means "madness" or "insanity". It is derived from the prefix ἀν- (an-), which means "not" or "without", and the noun νόος (noos), which means "mind" or "reason".

Grammar:
In Greek, ανοια is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is ανοια, and its genitive singular form is ανοιας. The word can be used in the singular or plural form, depending on the context.

Description:
ανοια is a Greek word that refers to a state of madness or insanity. It can also be used to describe a lack of reason or common sense. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe irrational behavior or thinking. In modern Greek, the word ανοια is also used to describe a mental disorder or illness.
