The word "αντεστη" (antesti) is a Greek verb that means "to resist" or "to oppose". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), which means "against", and the verb "στη-" (sti-), which means "to stand". 

Grammar: "Αντεστη" is a first-person singular present tense verb in the Greek language. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and persons, such as "αντιστέκομαι" (antistekomai) in the present continuous tense or "αντιστάθηκα" (antistathika) in the past tense. 

Description: The word "αντεστη" is often used in the context of political or social resistance, such as resisting oppression or injustice. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of opposition or defiance.
