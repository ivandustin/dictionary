Word: αναβαινειν

Etymology:
The word αναβαινειν (anabainein) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: ανα (ana) meaning "up" or "again" and βαινειν (bainein) meaning "to go". Therefore, αναβαινειν means "to go up" or "to ascend".

Grammar:
αναβαινειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used in the present tense, but can also be used in other tenses depending on the context.

Description:
αναβαινειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physically going up or ascending, such as climbing stairs or a mountain. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a rise in status or importance, or an increase in intensity or emotion. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is often used to describe Jesus ascending to heaven.
