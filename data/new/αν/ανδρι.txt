The word "ανδρι" (andri) is a Greek word that means "to a man" or "for a man". It is derived from the Greek word "άνδρας" (andras), which means "man" or "male". 

In terms of grammar, "ανδρι" is a preposition that is used to indicate direction or purpose. It is often used in conjunction with other words to form phrases that describe actions or relationships between people.

For example, "ανδρι γυναικειος" (andri gynaikios) means "for the sake of women", while "ανδρι αντιθετος" (andri antithetos) means "opposite to men".

Overall, "ανδρι" is a versatile word that is used in a variety of contexts to describe relationships and actions between men and women.
