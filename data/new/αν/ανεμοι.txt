Word: ανεμοι

Etymology:
The word ανεμοι (anemoi) is derived from the Greek word ἄνεμος (anemos), which means "wind". In Greek mythology, the Anemoi were the gods of the winds, each associated with a particular direction.

Grammar:
ανεμοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative form of the word, which means it is used as the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, and accusative, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
ανεμοι refers to winds or breezes. In Greek mythology, the Anemoi were personified as gods who controlled the winds and were associated with different directions. The four main Anemoi were Boreas (north wind), Notus (south wind), Zephyrus (west wind), and Eurus (east wind). The Anemoi were often depicted as winged men, each carrying a different type of wind. The word ανεμοι can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any type of wind or breeze.
