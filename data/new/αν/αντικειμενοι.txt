Word: αντικειμενοι

Etymology: The word αντικειμενοι comes from the Greek word αντικείμενος (antikeímenos), which means "opposed" or "contrary". The suffix -οι (-oi) is added to make it plural.

Grammar: αντικειμενοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: αντικειμενοι refers to people or things that are opposed to each other or in conflict. It can also mean "enemies" or "adversaries". The word is often used in a political or social context to describe opposing groups or factions.
