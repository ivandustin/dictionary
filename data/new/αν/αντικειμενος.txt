Word: αντικείμενος

Etymology:
The word αντικείμενος comes from the Greek roots αντί (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and κείμενος (keimenos), meaning "lying" or "placed." Together, the word means "opposed" or "contrary."

Grammar:
In Greek, αντικείμενος is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe a person or thing that is opposed to or contrary to something else.

Description:
The word αντικείμενος can be used to describe a person or thing that is opposed to or contrary to something else.
