Word: ανεκειτο

Etymology: ανεκειτο is a Greek word that comes from the verb ανακειμαι (anakeimai), which means "to lie down" or "to recline". It is composed of the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "back", and the verb κειμαι (keimai), which means "to lie".

Grammar: ανεκειτο is a third-person singular past tense form of the verb ανακειμαι (anakeimai). It is in the indicative mood and the imperfect tense. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: ανεκειτο can be translated as "he/she/it was lying down" or "he/she/it used to lie down". It is a descriptive term that can be used to indicate a person's or an object's position or posture. In ancient Greek literature, it is often used to describe the way people would recline on couches during meals or gatherings.
