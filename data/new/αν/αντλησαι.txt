Word: αντλησαι

Etymology:
The word αντλησαι comes from the Greek verb αντλέω (antleó), which means "to draw water from a well or a cistern". The verb is derived from the noun αντλία (antlía), which means "a bucket or a pitcher used for drawing water".

Grammar:
αντλησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb αντλέω (antleó), which means "to draw water from a well or a cistern". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The infinitive mood indicates that the verb is not conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description:
αντλησαι is a Greek verb that means "to draw water from a well or a cistern". It is a common verb used in everyday life, especially in rural areas where people still rely on wells for their water supply. The verb is often used in the context of fetching water for household use or for watering crops and animals. The act of drawing water from a well or a cistern requires physical effort, and the verb αντλησαι reflects this by conveying a sense of exertion and hard work.
