Word: αντιδικος

Etymology:
The word αντιδικος comes from the Greek roots αντι- (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and δικη (dikē), meaning "justice" or "judgment." Therefore, αντιδικος can be translated as "opposing justice" or "against judgment."

Grammar:
In Greek, αντιδικος is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, αντιδικος can be used to describe a person who is against justice (αντιδικος της δικαιοσύνης) or a situation that is in opposition to a legal decision (αντιδικος απόφασης).

Description:
The word αντιδικος is often used in legal contexts to describe a party who is opposing another party in a legal dispute. It can also be used more broadly to describe someone who is against justice or fairness in general. Additionally, the word can be used metaphorically to describe a situation or circumstance that is in opposition to what is considered right or just.
