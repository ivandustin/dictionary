Word: ανεβαλετο

Etymology: ανεβαλετο is a Greek word that comes from the verb αναβάλλω (anaválo), which means "to postpone" or "to defer". The word is formed by adding the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "again", to the verb βάλλω (válo), which means "to throw" or "to put".

Grammar: ανεβαλετο is the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb αναβάλλω (anaválo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description: ανεβαλετο means "he/she/it postponed" or "he/she/it deferred" in English. It is a past tense form, indicating that the action of postponing or deferring has already taken place.
