Word: ανηγγειλεν

Etymology:
The word ανηγγειλεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αγγελος (angelos), which means "messenger" or "angel". The prefix αν- (an-) means "up" or "back", so ανηγγειλεν can be translated as "reported back" or "announced".

Grammar:
ανηγγειλεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is in the past tense and indicates a completed action. The verb is formed by adding the prefix αν- to the stem of the verb αγγελος and adding the appropriate endings for the aorist tense.

Description:
ανηγγειλεν means "reported back" or "announced" in English. It is often used in the context of delivering news or information to someone. The word is commonly found in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the actions of messengers who bring news of important events.
