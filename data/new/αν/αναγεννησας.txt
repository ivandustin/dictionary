Word: αναγεννησας

Etymology:
The word αναγεννησας is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix ανα- (meaning "again" or "re-") and the verb γεννάω (meaning "to beget" or "to give birth"). Therefore, αναγεννησας can be translated as "to be born again" or "to regenerate".

Grammar:
αναγεννησας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb αναγεννάω, which means "to regenerate" or "to be born again". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
αναγεννησας is a powerful and meaningful word in Greek, often used in religious contexts to describe a spiritual rebirth or renewal. It is also used in secular contexts to describe a physical or emotional transformation. The word carries a sense of starting anew, of being given a second chance, and of being transformed into something better.
