Word: αναστατούντες

Etymology: The word αναστατούντες comes from the Greek verb αναστατόω (anastatoó), which means "to disturb, unsettle, agitate". The suffix -ούντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently in the act of disturbing or unsettling.

Grammar: αναστατούντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is formed by adding the present participle suffix -ούντες to the stem αναστατούν- (anastatoún-), which is derived from the verb αναστατόω.

Description: αναστατούντες refers to people who are currently disturbing or unsettling something or someone. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as political unrest, social upheaval, or personal turmoil. It implies a sense of agitation or disruption, and can be used to describe both intentional and unintentional actions.
