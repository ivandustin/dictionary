Word: αναντιρρητως

Etymology: The word αναντιρρητως (anantirritos) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix ανα- (ana-), meaning "not" or "un-", and the adjective αντιρρητος (antirritos), meaning "irrefutable" or "indisputable". Therefore, the word αναντιρρητως means "unquestionably" or "indisputably".

Grammar: αναντιρρητως is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is often used to emphasize the certainty or truthfulness of a statement. For example, "Αυτό είναι αναντιρρητως αλήθεια" (Afto einai anantirritos alitheia) means "This is unquestionably true".

Description: αναντιρρητως is a strong and emphatic word in Greek, used to express absolute certainty or truthfulness. It is often used in formal or academic contexts, such as in legal or scientific documents, to emphasize the indisputable nature of a fact or argument.
