Word: αντεχεσθε

Etymology: αντεχεσθε is a Greek word that comes from the verb αντεχομαι (antechomai), which means "to hold out against" or "to endure". The word is formed by combining the prefix αντι- (anti-), which means "against", and the verb εχω (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar: αντεχεσθε is a second person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to hold out against something or endure it. The middle/passive voice indicates that the action is being done to or for the subject, rather than by them.

Description: αντεχεσθε is a strong and powerful word that conveys the idea of endurance and perseverance in the face of adversity. It is often used in a motivational or inspirational context, encouraging people to keep going and not give up. The word can be applied to a wide range of situations, from physical challenges to emotional struggles, and is a reminder that we are capable of overcoming even the toughest obstacles if we stay strong and hold out against them.
