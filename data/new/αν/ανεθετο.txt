Word: ανεθετο

Etymology: The word ανεθετο comes from the Greek verb αναθέτω (anatheto), which means "to set up" or "to appoint". The prefix ανα- (ana-) means "up" or "again", while θέτω (theto) means "to place" or "to set".

Grammar: ανεθετο is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is often used to describe something that has been set up or appointed.

Description: ανεθετο can be translated to English as "appointed" or "established". It is often used in the context of official appointments or designations, such as an appointed official or an established policy. It can also be used more broadly to describe something that has been set up or established, such as an established tradition or an appointed time.
