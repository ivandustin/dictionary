Word: αναστροφην

Etymology: The word αναστροφην comes from the Greek word αναστροφή (anastrophí), which means "turning back" or "reversal". The suffix -ν (-n) is added to the end of the word to indicate the accusative case.

Grammar: αναστροφην is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: αναστροφην refers to the act of turning back or reversing something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as turning a car around, or in a figurative sense, such as reversing a decision or changing one's mind. The word can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as the reversal of a trend or the undoing of a situation.
