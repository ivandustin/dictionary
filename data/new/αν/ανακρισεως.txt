The word "ανακρισεως" (anakriseos) is a Greek noun that means "interrogation" or "examination". It is derived from the verb "ανακρίνω" (anakrino), which means "to examine" or "to investigate". In terms of grammar, it is a genitive singular noun in the Greek language. The word is often used in legal contexts, referring to the process of questioning witnesses or suspects in a trial or investigation.
