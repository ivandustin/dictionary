Word: αντιτασσομενων

Etymology: The word αντιτασσομενων is derived from the Greek verb αντιτάσσομαι (antitássomai), which means "to oppose" or "to resist". The suffix -μενων (-menon) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to something that is currently opposing or resisting.

Grammar: αντιτασσομενων is a present participle in the genitive plural form. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently opposing or resisting something.

Description: αντιτασσομενων can be translated to "opposing" or "resisting". It is often used in political or social contexts to describe groups or individuals who are actively opposing a particular policy or idea. For example, one might say "the opposing party" or "the resisting group" in reference to those who are against a certain political agenda.
