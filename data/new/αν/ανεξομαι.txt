The word "ανεξομαι" (anexomai) is a Greek verb that means "to be exempted" or "to be released from". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "αν-" (an-), which means "not" or "without", and the verb "εξομαι" (exomai), which means "to be released". 

Grammar: "ανεξομαι" is a middle/passive voice verb in the present tense. It is conjugated as follows: 

- Singular: ανεξομαι
- Plural: ανεξομεθα

Description: The word "ανεξομαι" is often used in legal or bureaucratic contexts to refer to being exempted from a requirement or obligation. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe being released from something, such as a burden or responsibility.
