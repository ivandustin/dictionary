Word: αντιλεγοντα

Etymology:
The word αντιλεγοντα comes from the Greek root words αντι (anti) meaning "against" or "opposite" and λεγω (lego) meaning "to speak" or "to say". Therefore, αντιλεγοντα can be translated to mean "speaking against" or "opposing speech".

Grammar:
αντιλεγοντα is the present participle form of the verb αντιλέγω (antilégo) which means "to contradict" or "to argue against". The present participle form indicates that the action of contradicting or arguing against is currently happening.

Description:
αντιλεγοντα is a Greek word that describes someone or something that is currently speaking or arguing against something else. It can be used to describe a person who is actively disagreeing with another person's opinion or argument, or it can be used to describe a situation where two opposing viewpoints are being presented.
