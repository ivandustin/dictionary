Word: αναστρεφόμενους

Etymology: The word αναστρεφόμενους comes from the Greek verb αναστρέφω (anastrefo) which means "to turn back" or "to return". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: αναστρεφόμενους is the accusative plural form of the present participle of the verb αναστρέφω. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently turning back or returning.

Description: αναστρεφόμενους can be translated to "those who are turning back" or "those who are returning". It is often used in a religious context to describe individuals who are repenting or turning away from sin. In a more general sense, it can also be used to describe individuals who are changing their behavior or returning to a previous state.
