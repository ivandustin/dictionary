The word "ανηγγελη" (anengeli) is a Greek verb that means "to announce" or "to declare". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "ανα-" (ana-), which means "up" or "again", and "αγγελος" (angelos), which means "messenger". Therefore, "ανηγγελη" literally means "to send a message up" or "to send a message again".

Grammar: "ανηγγελη" is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: The verb "ανηγγελη" is commonly used in Greek literature and the Bible to describe the act of announcing or declaring something important. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as announcing news, proclaiming a message, or declaring a victory.
