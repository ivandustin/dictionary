The word "αντιοχειας" (Antiocheias) is a Greek noun that refers to the ancient city of Antioch, which was located in modern-day Turkey. The word is derived from the Greek words "αντί" (anti), meaning "opposite" or "against," and "Ιωνία" (Ionia), which was a region in ancient Greece. The city of Antioch was founded in the 4th century BC by Seleucus I Nicator, one of Alexander the Great's generals, and it became an important center of Hellenistic culture and commerce. In terms of grammar, "αντιοχειας" is in the genitive case, indicating possession or origin.
