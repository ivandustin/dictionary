Word: αναγομενοις

Etymology: αναγομενοις is the present participle of the Greek verb αναγω (anago), which means "to lead up" or "to bring up". The word is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), meaning "up" or "again", and the verb γω (go), meaning "to go" or "to lead".

Grammar: αναγομενοις is a masculine, plural, nominative, present participle in the Greek language. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently being led up or brought up.

Description: αναγομενοις can be translated to "being led up" or "being brought up". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe people who are being led up to Jerusalem for religious purposes.
