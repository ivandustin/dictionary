Word: ανυδροι

Etymology:
The word ανυδροι (anudroi) is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and υδρος (hydros) meaning "water". Therefore, the literal meaning of ανυδροι is "not watered" or "without water".

Grammar:
ανυδροι is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, ανυδροι is used to describe masculine plural nouns that are without water.

Description:
ανυδροι is a term used to describe areas or regions that are dry or arid, lacking in water. It can also be used to describe plants or crops that are not watered and are therefore withered or dried up. In a figurative sense, ανυδροι can be used to describe a person or situation that is lacking in vitality or energy.
