Word: αναμενειν

Etymology:
The word αναμενειν (anamenein) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix ανα- (ana-) meaning "up" or "again" and the verb μενω (meno) meaning "to stay" or "to remain". Therefore, αναμενειν means "to wait" or "to expect".

Grammar:
αναμενειν is a present tense verb in Greek. Its conjugation in the present tense is as follows:

- Εγώ αναμένω (ego anaméno) - I wait
- Εσύ αναμένεις (esý anaméneis) - You wait
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό αναμένει (aftós/aftí/aftó anaménei) - He/She/It waits
- Εμείς αναμένουμε (emeís anaménoyme) - We wait
- Εσείς αναμένετε (eseís anaménete) - You wait (plural)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά αναμένουν (aftoí/aftés/aftá anaménoyn) - They wait

Description:
αναμενειν is a common Greek verb that is used to express the act of waiting or expecting something. It can be used in various contexts, such as waiting for someone to arrive, waiting for a response, or waiting for a specific event to happen. It is a versatile verb that can be used in both formal and informal situations.
