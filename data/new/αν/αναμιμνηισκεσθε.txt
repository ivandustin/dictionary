Word: αναμιμνηισκεσθε

Etymology:
The word αναμιμνηισκεσθε is derived from the Greek word αναμιμνήσκω (anamimnisko), which means "to remind, to recall to mind". The suffix -σθε (-sthe) is added to the verb to indicate the second person plural imperative form, which means "you all remember".

Grammar:
αναμιμνηισκεσθε is a verb in the second person plural imperative form in the Greek language. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people to remember or recall something.

Description:
The word αναμιμνηισκεσθε is a command to a group of people to remember or recall something. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, where people are encouraged to remember important teachings or events. The word can also be used in a more general sense, to remind people of something important or to bring something to their attention.
