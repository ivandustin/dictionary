Etymology: 

The word "ανακυψας" (anakyptas) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix "ana-" meaning "up" or "again" and the verb "kypto" meaning "to hide". Therefore, "ανακυψας" means "to rise up" or "to emerge".

"ανακυψας" is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ας" to the stem "ανακυπτ-". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.
