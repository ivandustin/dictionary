The word "αντιλογιαι" (antilogiai) is a Greek noun that can be translated to "contradictions" or "disputes". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite", and the noun "λογια" (logia), meaning "words" or "speech". 

Grammar: "Αντιλογιαι" is a plural noun in the feminine gender. 

Description: The word refers to disagreements or arguments between two or more people, where each person presents opposing viewpoints or arguments. It can also refer to contradictions within a single argument or statement.
