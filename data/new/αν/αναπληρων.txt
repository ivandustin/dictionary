Word: αναπληρων

Etymology: ανα- (ana-, prefix meaning "again" or "back") + πληρων (pliron, present participle of πληρόω, meaning "to fill" or "to complete"). Therefore, αναπληρων means "refilling" or "replenishing".

Grammar: αναπληρων is a present participle in Greek, which means it is used to describe an ongoing action. It can be used as an adjective or a verb, depending on the context.

Description: αναπληρων can be used to describe the act of refilling or replenishing something that has been emptied or depleted. It can also be used to describe the act of completing or fulfilling something that was lacking or incomplete. In modern Greek, it is often used in the context of job positions, where someone is filling in for someone else who is absent or on leave.
