Word: αναιρεισθαι

Etymology:
The word αναιρεισθαι comes from the Greek verb αναιρεω (an-aire-o), which means "to take away" or "to remove". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) is a reflexive suffix that indicates that the action is being done to oneself. Therefore, αναιρεισθαι means "to remove oneself" or "to withdraw".

Grammar:
αναιρεισθαι is a passive verb in the present tense, third person singular. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Present tense:
αναιρεισθαι (an-aire-isthai) - to remove oneself

Description:
αναιρεισθαι is a Greek verb that is often used in legal contexts to refer to the act of withdrawing or revoking a claim or accusation. It can also be used more generally to refer to the act of withdrawing or removing oneself from a situation or relationship. The verb is often used in the passive voice, indicating that the action is being done to oneself.
