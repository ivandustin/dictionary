Word: ανεμους

Etymology: The word ανεμους (anemous) is derived from the Greek word ανεμος (anemos), which means "wind". The suffix -ους (-ous) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: ανεμους is a plural noun in Greek, which means "winds". It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: ανεμους is a word that refers to the winds, which are the movement of air in the atmosphere. In Greek mythology, the winds were often personified as gods, such as Boreas (the north wind) and Zephyrus (the west wind). The winds were also associated with different seasons and weather conditions. In modern Greek, ανεμους is still used to refer to the winds, both in a literal and figurative sense.
