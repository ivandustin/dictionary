Word: ανθρωπους

Etymology: The word ανθρωπους (anthropous) is derived from the Greek word ἄνθρωπος (anthropos), which means "human being" or "man". It is a combination of the words ἄνθρωπος (anthropos) and οὖς (ous), which means "ear". The word originally referred to a person who had the ability to listen and understand.

Grammar: ανθρωπους is the plural form of the Greek noun ανθρωπος (anthropos), which means "human being" or "man". It is a third declension noun, and its nominative singular form is ἄνθρωπος (anthropos). In the plural form, the ending -ους (-ous) is added to the stem.

Description: ανθρωπους is a Greek word that refers to human beings or men in the plural form. It is a common word used in everyday language and can be found in various contexts, such as literature, philosophy, and religion.
