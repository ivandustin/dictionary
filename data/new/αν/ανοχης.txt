Word: ανοχης

Etymology:
The word ανοχης (anochis) is derived from the Greek word ανοχή (anochi), which means "tolerance" or "indulgence". It comes from the verb ανέχομαι (anechomai), which means "to tolerate" or "to endure".

Grammar:
ανοχης is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
ανοχης refers to the act of tolerating or indulging someone or something. It can also refer to the state of being tolerant or indulgent. The word is often used in a negative context, implying that someone is being too lenient or permissive. It can also be used in a positive context, to describe someone who is patient and understanding.
