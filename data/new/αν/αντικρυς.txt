Word: αντικρυς

Etymology:
The word αντικρυς comes from the Greek words αντί (anti) meaning "opposite" or "against" and κρυς (krys) meaning "cold". The word originally referred to the opposite direction of the cold wind, but over time it came to mean "opposite" or "facing".

Grammar:
In Greek, αντικρυς is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). For example, αντικρυς can be used to describe a person, object, or location that is facing or opposite to another.

Description:
The word αντικρυς is often used to describe something that is directly facing or opposite to something else. It can be used to describe a physical location, such as two buildings that are facing each other, or a person who is standing directly in front of another. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as two opposing viewpoints or ideas.
