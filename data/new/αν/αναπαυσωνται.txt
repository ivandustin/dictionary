Word: αναπαυσωνται

Etymology:
The word αναπαυσωνται is derived from the Greek verb αναπαύω (anapauo), which means "to rest" or "to take a break". The suffix -νται (-ntai) is added to the verb stem to indicate the present tense and the passive voice.

Grammar:
αναπαυσωνται is a present tense, passive voice verb in Greek. It is the third person plural form of the verb αναπαύω (anapauo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, voice, and person.

Description:
αναπαυσωνται is a verb that describes the action of resting or taking a break. It is often used to describe physical rest, but can also be used to describe mental or emotional rest. The passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb, rather than performing it. Therefore, αναπαυσωνται can be translated as "they are being rested" or "they are taking a break".
