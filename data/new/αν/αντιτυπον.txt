Word: αντιτυπον

Etymology: The word αντιτυπον comes from the Greek words αντί (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and τύπος (typos), meaning "impression" or "mark." Together, αντιτυπον means "counterpart" or "opposite impression."

Grammar: αντιτυπον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its singular form is αντίτυπο, and its plural form is αντίτυπα. It can be used as a subject, object, or predicate in a sentence.

Description: αντιτυπον refers to a counterpart or a duplicate of something. It can be used to describe a copy of a document, a replica of a work of art, or a matching set of objects. It can also be used figuratively to describe a person who is similar to another in personality or behavior.
