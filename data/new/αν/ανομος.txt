Word: ανομος

Etymology:
The word ανομος (anomos) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the Greek word "nomos" meaning "law". Therefore, ανομος can be translated as "lawless" or "without law".

Grammar:
In Greek, ανομος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person or thing that is without law or outside the bounds of the law. It can also be used as a noun to refer to a lawless person.

Description:
The word ανομος is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is disobedient or rebellious. It can also be used to describe a situation or behavior that is considered to be outside the norms of society or the law. In some cases, it can be used to describe a person or group who is actively working against the law or established order.
