Word: ανακρινων

Etymology:
The word ανακρινων comes from the Greek verb ανακρίνω (anakríno), which means "to examine" or "to investigate". It is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "back", and the verb κρίνω (kríno), which means "to judge" or "to decide".

Grammar:
ανακρινων is the present participle of the verb ανακρίνω (anakríno), which means "examining" or "investigating". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
ανακρινων is a word that describes someone who is currently in the process of examining or investigating something. It can refer to a person who is conducting a formal inquiry, such as a police officer or a judge, or to someone who is simply trying to gather information about a particular topic. The word implies a sense of diligence and thoroughness in the pursuit of knowledge or truth.
