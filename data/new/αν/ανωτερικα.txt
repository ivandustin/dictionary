Word: ανωτερικα

Etymology: The word ανωτερικα is derived from the Greek word ανώτερος (anóteros), which means "higher" or "upper". The suffix -ικα (-ika) is added to form an adjective, meaning "related to the upper part".

Grammar: ανωτερικα is an adjective in the feminine plural form, used to describe a noun in the same form. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: ανωτερικα is used to describe something that is related to the upper part of something else. It can refer to physical location, such as the upper floors of a building, or to a higher level of importance or status. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is superior or better than something else.
