Word: ανοητοι

Etymology:
The word ανοητοι (anoitoi) is derived from the Greek word ανόητος (anoitos), which means "foolish" or "senseless". It is formed by adding the suffix -τος (-tos) to the prefix αν- (an-), which means "not" or "without".

Grammar:
ανοητοι is a plural noun in Greek, which means "fools" or "senseless people". It is used to refer to a group of people who are considered foolish or lacking in common sense.

Description:
ανοητοι is a word that is often used in Greek to describe people who are considered foolish or lacking in common sense. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in everyday conversation, literature, or even in political discourse. The word carries a negative connotation and is often used to criticize or ridicule someone's behavior or actions.
