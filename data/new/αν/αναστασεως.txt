The word "αναστασεως" (anastaseos) is a Greek noun that means "resurrection" or "rising again". It is derived from the verb "αναστησις" (anastasis), which means "to rise up" or "to stand up again". In Greek grammar, "αναστασεως" is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship to something else. 

In Christian theology, "αναστασεως" is often used to refer to the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, which is a central tenet of the faith. The word can also be used more generally to refer to any kind of resurrection or revival. 

In terms of pronunciation, "αναστασεως" is pronounced "an-ah-stah-SEH-os" with the stress on the second syllable.
