Word: αντιλεγει

Etymology: The word αντιλεγει comes from the Greek roots αντι- (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and λεγειν (legein), meaning "to speak" or "to say." Therefore, αντιλεγει means "to speak against" or "to contradict."

Grammar: αντιλεγει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb αντιλέγω (antilégo), which means "to contradict" or "to argue against." The verb is formed by adding the prefix αντι- to the verb λέγω (légo), meaning "to say."

Description: αντιλεγει is a Greek verb that means "to contradict" or "to argue against." It is often used in debates or discussions when one person disagrees with another's statement or opinion. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, philosophy, or everyday conversations.
