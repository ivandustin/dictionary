Word: ανελαβετε

Etymology:
The word ανελαβετε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαμβάνω (lamvano), which means "to take" or "to receive". The prefix ανε- (ane-) means "up" or "back", so the word ανελαβετε can be translated as "to take up" or "to take back".

Grammar:
ανελαβετε is a second person plural aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people, telling them to perform an action in the past. The verb is in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
ανελαβετε is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to mean "to take up" or "to take back", as in taking up a task or taking back something that was previously given. It can also be used to mean "to assume" or "to undertake", as in assuming responsibility for a task or undertaking a new project.
