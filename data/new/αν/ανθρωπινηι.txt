The word "ανθρωπινηι" (anthropini) is a Greek adjective that means "human" or "related to humans." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek noun "άνθρωπος" (anthropos), which means "human being."

Grammar: "ανθρωπινηι" is in the feminine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "ανθρωπινηι" is often used to describe characteristics or qualities that are specific to humans, such as emotions, behavior, or physical attributes. It can also be used to describe things that are made or created by humans, such as art, technology, or culture.
