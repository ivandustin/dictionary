Word: αναγκασον

Etymology:
The word αναγκασον comes from the Greek verb αναγκάζω (anankazo), which means "to force" or "to compel". The suffix -ον (-on) is a common ending for verbs in the aorist tense in Greek.

Grammar:
αναγκασον is the aorist tense of the verb αναγκάζω (anankazo) in the second person singular. It is used to express a completed action in the past, specifically an action of forcing or compelling someone to do something.

Description:
αναγκασον can be translated to English as "you forced" or "you compelled". It implies that the person being addressed had a strong influence over someone or something, and used that influence to make them do something against their will. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from personal relationships to political situations.
