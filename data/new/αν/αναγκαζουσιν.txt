Word: αναγκαζουσιν

Etymology: The word αναγκαζουσιν comes from the Greek verb αναγκάζω (anankazo), which means "to force" or "to compel". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: αναγκαζουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of the verb αναγκάζω.

It is often used in a negative context to describe situations where someone is being coerced or pressured into doing something against their will.
