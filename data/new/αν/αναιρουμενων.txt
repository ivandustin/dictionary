Word: αναιρουμενων

Etymology: The word αναιρουμενων comes from the Greek verb αναιρέω (anairéō), which means "to cancel" or "to annul". The suffix -μενων (-menōn) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to something that is currently being cancelled or annulled.

Grammar: αναιρουμενων is the genitive plural form of αναιρούμενος (anairoumenos), which is a present participle of the verb αναιρέω. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple things being cancelled or annulled.

Description: αναιρουμενων can be translated to "of those being cancelled" or "of those being annulled". It refers to a group of things or people that are currently being cancelled or annulled. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in legal or political settings where decisions or actions are being revoked or invalidated.
