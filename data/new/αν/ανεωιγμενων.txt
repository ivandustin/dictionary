Word: ανεωιγμενων

Etymology: The word ανεωιγμενων is a Greek verb form that comes from the root word ανοίγω (anoigo), which means "to open". The suffix -μενων (-menon) indicates a present passive participle, meaning "being opened".

Grammar: ανεωιγμενων is a present passive participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as standalone verbs. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon.

Description: ανεωιγμενων can be translated to mean "of the ones being opened". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe doors or gates that are being opened, both literally and metaphorically. The word conveys a sense of ongoing action and passive reception, suggesting that the opening is happening to the subject rather than being actively done by them.
