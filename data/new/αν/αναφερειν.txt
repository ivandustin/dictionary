Word: αναφέρειν

Etymology:
The word αναφέρειν (anaferein) comes from the Greek prefix ανα- (ana-), meaning "up" or "again," and the verb φέρω (fero), meaning "to carry." Therefore, αναφέρειν means "to carry up" or "to report."

Grammar:
αναφέρειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is an active verb in the present tense, and its infinitive form is αναφέρειν. The verb is conjugated in all tenses and moods, and it can be used in both the active and passive voice.

Description:
The verb αναφέρειν is commonly used in Greek to mean "to report" or "to mention." It is often used in formal or official contexts, such as in news reports or legal documents. The verb can also be used to mean "to bring up" or "to refer to" something or someone. In addition, it can be used in a more literal sense to mean "to carry up" or "to lift up."
