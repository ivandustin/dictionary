Word: αναστρέφω (anastrefo)

Etymology: The word αναστρέφω comes from the Greek prefix ανα- (ana-), meaning "up" or "back," and the verb στρέφω (strefo), meaning "to turn." Together, αναστρέφω means "to turn back" or "to reverse."

Grammar: αναστρέφω is a regular Greek verb that belongs to the first conjugation. It is an active verb that can be used in all tenses and moods. Its stem is αναστρεφ-, and its endings change depending on the tense and person.

Description: αναστρέφω means "to turn back," "to reverse," or "to invert." It can be used in a literal sense, such as turning a car around or reversing a decision, or in a figurative sense, such as changing one's opinion or attitude. It is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts.
