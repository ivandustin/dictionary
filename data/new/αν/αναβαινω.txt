Word: αναβαινω

Etymology:
The word αναβαινω (anabaino) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: ανα (ana) meaning "up" or "again" and βαινω (baino) meaning "to go". Therefore, αναβαινω means "to go up" or "to ascend".

Grammar:
αναβαινω is a first conjugation verb in Greek, which means that it follows a regular pattern of conjugation. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence performs the action of going up. It can be used in all tenses, moods, and voices.

Description:
αναβαινω is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used literally to describe physical movement upwards, such as climbing stairs or a mountain. It can also be used metaphorically to describe progress or improvement, such as in the phrase "to rise to the occasion". Additionally, it can be used in a spiritual or religious context to describe ascension to a higher plane of existence.
