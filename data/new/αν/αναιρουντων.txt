The word "αναιρουντων" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "αναιρέω" (anairéō), which means "to abolish" or "to annul". The present participle form indicates that the action of abolishing or annulling is currently ongoing or in progress. In terms of grammar, it is a masculine plural form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a group of males who are currently in the process of abolishing or annulling something.
