The word "ανακραξας" (anakraksas) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), which means "up" or "again," and the verb "κράζω" (krazo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out." 

Grammar: "ανακραξας" is the aorist tense, active voice, second person singular form of the verb "ανακράζω" (anakrazo), which means "to shout out" or "to cry out loudly." 

Description: The word "ανακραξας" describes a sudden and loud cry or shout, often used to express strong emotions such as anger, fear, or excitement. It can also be used to describe a call for help or attention.
