Word: ανευρον

Etymology:
The word ανευρον (aneuron) is derived from the Greek prefix αν- (an-) meaning "not" and the verb ευρίσκω (eurisko) meaning "to find". Therefore, ανευρον means "not found" or "unfound".

Grammar:
ανευρον is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Description:
ανευρον is a rare and obscure word in the Greek language. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe something that is missing or lost, or something that cannot be found or discovered. It can also be used to describe a feeling of emptiness or a sense of something being absent.
