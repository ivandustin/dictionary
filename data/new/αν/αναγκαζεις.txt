Word: αναγκάζεις

Etymology: The word αναγκάζεις comes from the Greek verb αναγκάζω (anankazo), which means "to force" or "to compel". It is derived from the noun ανάγκη (ananki), meaning "necessity" or "need".

Grammar: αναγκάζεις is the second person singular form of the present tense of the Greek verb αναγκάζω. It is used to indicate that the subject (you) is currently forcing or compelling someone or something to do something.

Description: αναγκάζεις is a verb that expresses the act of forcing or compelling someone or something to do something against their will. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a sentence like "Με αναγκάζεις να πάω στο γιατρό" (You're forcing me to go to the doctor), where the subject is being compelled to do something they may not want to do.
