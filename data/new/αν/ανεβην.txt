Word: ανεβην

Etymology: ανεβην is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αναβαίνω (anabaino), which means "to go up" or "to ascend."

Grammar: ανεβην is the aorist tense of the Greek verb αναβαίνω (anabaino), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past. It is also in the third person singular form.

Description: ανεβην is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it went up" or "he/she/it ascended." It is often used in the context of physical movement, such as climbing a mountain or ascending a staircase. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the ascension of Jesus Christ into heaven.
