The word "ανεντες" (anentes) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It comes from the root word "ανίημι" (aniemi), which means "to let go" or "to release". 

In terms of grammar, "ανεντες" is the third person singular aorist active participle of "ανίημι". It can be translated as "having let go" or "having released". 

Overall, the word "ανεντες" suggests an action of releasing or letting go of something in the past.
