Word: ανακρινουσιν

Etymology: The word ανακρινουσιν is derived from the Greek verb ανακρίνω (anakríno), which means "to examine" or "to investigate". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates that the word is in the present active participle form, meaning "they are examining" or "they are investigating".

Grammar: ανακρινουσιν is a present active participle in Greek, which means that it is a verb form that describes an ongoing action in the present tense. It is in the third person plural, indicating that the subject is a group of people who are currently examining or investigating something.

Description: ανακρινουσιν is a Greek word that describes the act of examining or investigating. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in legal proceedings, scientific research, or medical diagnoses.
