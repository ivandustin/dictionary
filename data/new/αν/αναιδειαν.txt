The word "αναιδειαν" (an-a-i-dei-an) is a Greek noun that means shamelessness or impudence. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "an-" meaning without or not, and the noun "αἰδώς" (aidos) meaning shame or modesty. 

Grammar: "αναιδειαν" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is shameless or lacks modesty. It can also be used to describe an action or behavior that is considered impudent or audacious.
