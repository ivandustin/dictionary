Word: ανυποτακτον

Etymology:
The word ανυποτακτον (anypotakton) is derived from the Greek words αν- (an-) meaning "not" and υποτακτος (ypotaktos) meaning "submissive" or "obedient". 

Grammar:
ανυποτακτον is a neuter noun in the singular form. 

Description:
The word ανυποτακτον is used to describe something or someone that is not submissive or obedient. It can refer to a person who is rebellious or defiant, or to a situation or thing that is difficult to control or manage. In modern Greek, the word is often used to describe a person who is unruly or disobedient.
