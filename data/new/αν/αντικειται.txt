Word: αντικείται

Etymology:
The word αντικείται (antikeítai) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: αντί (antí), which means "against" or "opposite," and κείμαι (keímai), which means "to lie" or "to be situated." Therefore, αντικείται can be translated as "to be opposed" or "to be situated against."

Grammar:
αντικείται is a third-person singular present indicative active verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation of verbs, which means that its stem remains the same throughout all its tenses. The verb is formed by adding the present tense suffix -εται (-etai) to the stem αντικει- (antikei-).

Description:
αντικείται is a verb that expresses opposition or resistance to something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, philosophy, or everyday life. For example, one can say "Η πολιτεία αντικείται στη διαφθορά" (I politía antikeítai sti diafthorá), which means "The state opposes corruption." Another example is "Η θέση του επιστήμονα αντικείται στην παράδοση" (I thési tou epistímona antikeítai stin parádosi), which means "The scientist's position is opposed to tradition."
