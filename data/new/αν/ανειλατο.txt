The word ανειλατο (aneilato) is a past tense form of the Greek verb αναιρέω (anaireo), which means "to take up, lift up, remove, or destroy." The verb is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "again," and the verb αίρω (airo), which means "to lift up" or "to take away."

ανειλατο (aneilato) is a third-person singular past tense form of the verb αναιρέω (anaireo). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

ανειλατο (aneilato) can be translated as "he/she/it lifted up," "he/she/it removed," or "he/she/it destroyed." The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning.
