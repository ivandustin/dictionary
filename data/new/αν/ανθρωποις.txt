Word: ανθρωποις

Etymology: ανθρωποις is the dative plural form of the Greek word ανθρωπος (anthropos), which means "human being" or "man". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: ανθρωπος (anthropos) and οικος (oikos), which means "house" or "dwelling". Therefore, the etymology of ανθρωποις can be traced back to the idea of humans being the inhabitants of a dwelling or house.

Grammar: ανθρωποις is a noun in the dative plural form in Greek grammar. It is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. In English, it can be translated as "to/for humans" or "by humans".

Description: ανθρωποις is a word that refers to human beings in Greek. It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to discuss the nature and essence of humanity. In ancient Greek literature, it was used to describe the common people or the masses, as opposed to the elite or the gods. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to refer to humans in general.
