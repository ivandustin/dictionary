Word: αντιλεγοντας

Etymology: The word αντιλεγοντας comes from the Greek root words αντι- (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and λεγω (lego), meaning "to speak" or "to say." Therefore, αντιλεγοντας can be translated as "speaking against" or "objecting."

Grammar: αντιλεγοντας is the present participle form of the verb αντιλέγω (antilégo), which means "to contradict" or "to argue against." It is in the masculine, plural, accusative case, and it functions as the direct object of a verb or as the object of a preposition.

Description: αντιλεγοντας is a word that describes someone or something that is speaking or arguing against a particular idea or statement. It can be used to describe a person who is actively disagreeing with someone else's opinion or to describe a statement or argument that contradicts another. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a debate, a legal proceeding, or a casual conversation.
