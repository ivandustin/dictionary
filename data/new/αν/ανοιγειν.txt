The word "ανοιγειν" (anoigein) is a Greek verb that means "to open". It is derived from the root word "ανοιγω" (anoigo), which also means "to open". In terms of grammar, it is a present tense verb in the infinitive form. It can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects and meanings. For example, in the past tense, it becomes "ανοιξα" (anoixa), which means "I opened". 

In Greek mythology, the god Hermes was known as the "opener of the ways" and was often associated with the act of opening doors and gates. The word "anoigein" is also used in various idiomatic expressions, such as "ανοίγω τα φτερά μου" (anoigo ta ftera mou), which means "to spread one's wings" or "to take flight". 

Overall, "ανοιγειν" is a versatile and commonly used verb in the Greek language, with a rich history and cultural significance.
