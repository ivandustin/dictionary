Word: ανενεγκειν

Etymology: The word ανενεγκειν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix αν- (meaning "up" or "away") and the verb ενεγκειν (meaning "to carry" or "to bring"). Therefore, ανενεγκειν can be translated as "to carry up" or "to bring away".

Grammar: ανενεγκειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means that it is not conjugated to match a specific tense, person, or number. It is often used in combination with auxiliary verbs to form compound tenses.

Description: ανενεγκειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physically carrying or bringing something up or away, as well as metaphorically carrying or bringing something to a higher level or removing it from a situation. It can also be used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to offering or sacrificing something to a higher power.
