The word "ανελωσιν" (anelōsin) is a Greek noun that means "waste, destruction, ruin." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "ανέλω" (anelō), which means "to destroy, to waste."

Grammar: "Ανελωσιν" is a third-person singular noun in the accusative case.

Description: The word is often used in the context of describing the aftermath of war or natural disasters, where there is widespread destruction and waste. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the ruin or downfall of a person or organization.
