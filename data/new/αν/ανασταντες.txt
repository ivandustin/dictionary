Word: ανασταντες

Etymology:
The word ανασταντες (anastantes) is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It is derived from the verb ανίστημι (anistemi), which means "to rise up" or "to stand up". The prefix ανα- (ana-) in ανασταντες (anastantes) means "up" or "again", so the word can be translated as "rising up" or "standing up again".

Grammar:
ανασταντες (anastantes) is a present participle verb in the masculine plural form. It is formed by adding the suffix -ντες (-ntes) to the present stem of the verb ανίστημι (anistemi). The present participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
ανασταντες (anastantes) can be translated as "rising up" or "standing up again". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a person who is standing up for their beliefs or principles, or who is overcoming a difficult situation. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe someone who is physically standing up or rising from a seated or prone position.
