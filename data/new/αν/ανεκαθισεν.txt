Word: ανεκάθισεν (anekathisen)

Etymology: ανεκάθισεν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix αν- (an-) meaning "up" or "back" and the verb κάθισεν (kathisen) meaning "to sit". Therefore, ανεκάθισεν means "to sit up" or "to rise from a sitting position".

Grammar: ανεκάθισεν is a third person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is conjugated from the verb κάθισεν (kathisen) in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: ανεκάθισεν is a verb that describes the action of sitting up or rising from a sitting position. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone getting up from a chair or bed, or someone rising from a kneeling or prostrate position.
