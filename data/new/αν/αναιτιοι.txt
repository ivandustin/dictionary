Word: αναιτιοι

Etymology:
The word αναιτιοι (anaitioi) is derived from the Greek prefix "αν-" (an-) meaning "not" and the Greek word "αιτιος" (aitios) meaning "responsible" or "guilty". Therefore, αναιτιοι (anaitioi) means "not responsible" or "not guilty".

Grammar:
αναιτιοι (anaitioi) is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are not responsible or guilty for a particular action or situation.

Description:
The word αναιτιοι (anaitioi) is often used in legal contexts to describe individuals who are not responsible for a crime or offense. It can also be used in a broader sense to describe individuals who are not to blame for a particular situation or problem.
