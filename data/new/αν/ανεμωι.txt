The word "ανεμωι" (anemoi) is a Greek noun that refers to the four wind gods in Greek mythology. The word is derived from the Greek word "anemos," which means wind. In terms of grammar, "ανεμωι" is a plural noun, indicating that it refers to multiple wind gods. The word can be translated to English as "winds" or "wind gods." In Greek mythology, the anemoi were believed to be responsible for the different winds that blew across the earth. Each of the four anemoi was associated with a specific cardinal direction and had their own unique characteristics and personalities.
