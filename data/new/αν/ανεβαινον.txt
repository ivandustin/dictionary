Word: ανεβαινον

Etymology:
The word ανεβαινον (anevainon) is a present participle form of the Greek verb αναβαινω (anabaino), which means "to go up" or "to ascend". The word is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "again", and the verb βαινω (baino), which means "to go".

Grammar:
ανεβαινον is a present participle form of the verb αναβαινω (anabaino), which is in the present tense and active voice. The participle form indicates that the action of going up or ascending is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
ανεβαινον can be translated as "going up" or "ascending". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the ascension of Jesus Christ into heaven. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of upward movement or ascent.
