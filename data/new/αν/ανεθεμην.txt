Word: ανεθεμην

Etymology:
The word ανεθεμην (anethemen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τιθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The prefix ανε (ane) is a form of the verb ανα (ana), which means "up" or "again". Therefore, ανεθεμην (anethemen) can be translated as "to set up" or "to place again".

Grammar:
ανεθεμην (anethemen) is a first-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
ανεθεμην (anethemen) is a verb that describes the act of setting something up or placing it again. It can be used in a literal sense, such as setting up a tent or placing a book on a shelf, or in a figurative sense, such as setting up a business or placing trust in someone again. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and can be found in various contexts.
