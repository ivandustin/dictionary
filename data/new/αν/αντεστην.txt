The word "αντεστην" (antesten) is a Greek verb that means "to resist" or "to oppose". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), which means "against", and the verb "εστην" (esten), which means "to stand". 

Grammar: "Αντεστην" is a first-person singular present indicative form of the verb. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and persons, such as "αντιστέκομαι" (antistekomai) in the present tense, or "αντιστάθηκα" (antistathika) in the past tense. 

Description: The word "αντεστην" is often used in the context of political or social resistance, such as resisting oppression or injustice. It can also be used in a more general sense, such as resisting temptation or temptation to do something wrong.
