Word: ανωφελες

Etymology:
The word ανωφελες (anopheles) is derived from the Greek words ανω (ano) meaning "useless" or "pointless" and φελη (phele) meaning "profit" or "benefit". It is a compound word that describes something that is of no use or benefit.

Grammar:
ανωφελες is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular form. It is often used to describe a person, thing, or situation that is useless or pointless.

Description:
The word ανωφελες is used to describe something that has no value or benefit. It can be used to describe a person who is lazy or unproductive, or a situation that is futile or pointless. It is often used in a negative context to express disappointment or frustration.
