Word: ανηγγελλον

Etymology:
The word ανηγγελλον (anengellon) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix αν- (an-) meaning "up" or "back" and the verb αγγελλω (angello) meaning "to announce" or "to report". Therefore, ανηγγελλον means "to report back" or "to announce again".

Grammar:
ανηγγελλον is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is ανηγγελλ- and it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs of this type.

Description:
ανηγγελλον is a word that is used to describe the act of reporting back or announcing again. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a military or political setting where a messenger is sent to report back to their superiors, or in a religious context where a prophet is announcing a message from a deity. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of repeating or reiterating a message or idea.
