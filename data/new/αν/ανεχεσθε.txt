The word "ανεχεσθε" (anechesthe) is a Greek verb in the present tense, middle voice, and imperative mood. It is derived from the root word "ανεχομαι" (anechomai), which means "to endure" or "to bear with patience." 

In its imperative form, "ανεχεσθε" is often translated as "be patient" or "put up with." It is a command to endure a difficult situation or person without complaining or becoming angry. 

In terms of grammar, "ανεχεσθε" is a second-person plural verb, meaning it is directed towards a group of people. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb. 

Overall, "ανεχεσθε" is a word that emphasizes the importance of patience and endurance in difficult situations.
