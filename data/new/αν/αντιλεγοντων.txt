Word: αντιλεγοντων

Etymology: The word αντιλεγοντων comes from the Greek roots αντι- (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and λεγω (lego), meaning "to speak" or "to say." Therefore, αντιλεγοντων can be translated as "those who speak against" or "those who contradict."

Grammar: αντιλεγοντων is the present participle of the verb αντιλέγω (antilégo), which means "to contradict" or "to argue against." It is in the masculine plural form, indicating that it refers to a group of people who are currently contradicting or arguing against something.

Description: αντιλεγοντων is a word that describes a group of people who are actively opposing or contradicting something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in politics, philosophy, or everyday conversation. The word implies a sense of disagreement or conflict, and suggests that the individuals in question are actively engaged in a debate or argument.
