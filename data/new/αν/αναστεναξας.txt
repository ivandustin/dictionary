Word: αναστενάξας

Etymology: The word αναστενάξας comes from the Greek verb αναστενάζω (anastenázo), which means "to sigh deeply" or "to groan". The prefix ανα- (ana-) means "again" or "back", while the root στενάζω (stenázo) means "to groan" or "to moan".

Grammar: αναστενάξας is the aorist participle of the verb αναστενάζω (anastenázo), which means that it describes an action that was completed in the past. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: αναστενάξας describes someone who has sighed deeply or groaned in the past. It can be used to describe someone who is feeling sad, frustrated, or tired. The word has a somewhat poetic or dramatic connotation, and is often used in literature or in artistic contexts.
