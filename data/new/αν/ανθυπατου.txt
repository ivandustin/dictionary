Word: ανθυπατου

Etymology: The word ανθυπατου is derived from the Greek words αντί (anti) meaning "against" or "opposite" and υπατος (ypatos) meaning "governor" or "ruler". Therefore, ανθυπατου can be translated as "vice-governor" or "deputy governor".

Grammar: ανθυπατου is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is ανθυπάτου.

Description: ανθυπατου is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to a deputy or vice-governor who acted on behalf of the governor in a province or region. This term was commonly used in the Roman Empire, where the governor of a province would appoint a deputy to assist him in his duties. The ανθυπατου would have had significant responsibilities and authority in the province, and would have been responsible for maintaining order and enforcing the law.
