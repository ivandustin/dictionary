Word: ανεγκλητον

Etymology:
The word ανεγκλητον comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the prefix αν- (an-), which means "not," and the word εγκλητος (enklitos), which means "accused" or "charged." Therefore, ανεγκλητον means "not accused" or "not charged."

Grammar:
ανεγκλητον is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description:
ανεγκλητον is a word that describes someone or something that has not been accused or charged with a crime. It can also be used to describe a situation or circumstance that is free from blame or guilt. The word is often used in legal contexts to describe a person who has been acquitted of a crime or a case that has been dismissed due to lack of evidence.
