Word: αναστησω

Etymology:
The word αναστησω (anastiso) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αναστασις (anastasis), which means "resurrection" or "rising up". The verb αναστησω means "to raise up" or "to resurrect".

Grammar:
αναστησω is a first-person singular future tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb αναστησ-, which means "to raise up". The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
αναστησω is a powerful and evocative word in Greek, with strong religious and spiritual connotations. It is often used in the context of the resurrection of Jesus Christ, as well as in other religious and mythological contexts. The word can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to raise up" or "to lift up", both literally and figuratively.
