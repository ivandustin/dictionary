Word: ανοητούς

Etymology: The word ανοητούς comes from the Greek adjective ανόητος (anóitos), which means "foolish" or "stupid". It is derived from the negative prefix α- (a-) and the noun νόηση (nóisi), which means "understanding" or "perception".

Grammar: ανοητούς is the plural form of the adjective ανόητος (anóitos). It is used to describe multiple people or things that are foolish or stupid. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, ανοητούς is the masculine plural accusative form of the adjective.

Description: ανοητούς is a Greek adjective that is used to describe people or things that lack intelligence or common sense. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone's behavior, actions, or decisions. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize or insult someone.
