Word: αναμνησις

Etymology:
The word αναμνησις (anamnesis) comes from the Greek verb αναμιμνησκω (anamimnesko), which means "to remember" or "to recall". It is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "again" or "back", and the verb μιμνησκω (mimnesko), which means "to remember".

Grammar:
αναμνησις is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case and its plural form is αναμνησεις (anamneseis).

αναμνησις is a Greek word that means "remembrance" or "recollection". In ancient Greek culture, it was used to refer to the act of remembering or recalling something from the past, especially in the context of religious rituals and ceremonies. In Christian theology, αναμνησις is used to refer to the commemoration of the Last Supper, which is also known as the Eucharist or Holy Communion.
