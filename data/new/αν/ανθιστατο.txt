Word: ανθιστατο

Etymology: The word ανθιστατο comes from the Greek verb ανθίστημι (anthistemi), which means "to resist" or "to oppose". The word is formed from the prefix αντι- (anti-), meaning "against", and the verb ιστημι (istemi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: ανθιστατο is the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb ανθίστημι (anthistemi). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and person. The middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon itself or is affected by the action.

Description: ανθιστατο describes an action that took place in the past, where the subject resisted or opposed something. The middle voice indicates that the subject was affected by the action in some way. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, social movements, or personal relationships.
