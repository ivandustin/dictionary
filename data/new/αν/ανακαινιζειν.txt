Word: ανακαινιζειν

Etymology: The word ανακαινιζειν (anakainizein) comes from the Greek roots ανα- (ana-), meaning "again" or "anew," and καινός (kainos), meaning "new." Therefore, the word means "to renew" or "to make new again."

Grammar: ανακαινιζειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem ends in -νυ- (ny) and its endings are -ω (o), -εις (eis), -ει (ei), -ομεν (omen), -ετε (ete), and -ουσι (ousi).

Description: ανακαινιζειν is a Greek verb that means "to renew," "to renovate," or "to restore." It is often used in the context of renovating or restoring buildings, but it can also be used in a metaphorical sense to describe the process of renewing or revitalizing something that has become old or worn out. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the renewal of the mind and spirit.
