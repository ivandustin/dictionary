The word "ανομιαι" (anomiai) is a plural noun in Greek that means "lawlessness" or "disobedience to the law". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek word "ανομος" (anomos), which means "lawless" or "without law". The suffix "-ιαι" (-iai) is added to form the plural.

Grammar: "ανομιαι" is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: The word "ανομιαι" is often used in a negative context to describe actions or behaviors that go against the law or moral standards. It can refer to both individual acts of disobedience and larger societal issues of lawlessness. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe sin and the rejection of God's law.
