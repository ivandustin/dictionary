The word "αναστροφαις" (anastrophais) is a Greek noun in the plural form. It is derived from the verb "αναστρέφω" (anastrepho), which means "to turn upside down" or "to invert." 

In terms of grammar, "αναστροφαις" is a feminine noun in the dative case. It is often used in the context of rhetoric or literature to refer to a figure of speech in which the normal word order is reversed for emphasis or poetic effect. 

Overall, "αναστροφαις" can be translated to mean "inversions" or "reversals."
