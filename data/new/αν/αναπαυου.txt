The word "αναπαυου" (anapauou) is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and imperative mood. It is derived from the root word "αναπαυω" (anapauo), which means "to rest" or "to take a break". 

In terms of grammar, "αναπαυου" is a second-person singular command, meaning it is used to give a direct order to someone. It can be translated to English as "rest" or "take a break". 

Overall, "αναπαυου" is a simple and straightforward word that is commonly used in everyday conversation in Greece. It is often used as a polite way to tell someone to relax or take a break.
