Word: αναπαυσω

Etymology:
The word αναπαυσω (anapauso) comes from the Greek root words ανα (ana) meaning "up" or "again" and παυω (pauo) meaning "to stop" or "to rest". Together, they form the verb αναπαυσω (anapauso) which means "to rest" or "to take a break".

Grammar:
αναπαυσω (anapauso) is a first-person singular present active indicative verb in Greek. It can be conjugated in different tenses and moods to indicate different actions and attitudes towards rest.

Description:
αναπαυσω (anapauso) is a common Greek verb that is used to express the idea of taking a break or resting. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as taking a break from work, resting after a long journey, or simply relaxing after a busy day. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the rest that believers find in God. Overall, αναπαυσω (anapauso) is a versatile and important word in the Greek language that conveys the idea of rest and relaxation.
