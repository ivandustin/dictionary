Word: αναβας

Etymology:
The word αναβας comes from the Greek verb αναβαίνω (anabaino), which means "to go up" or "to ascend". It is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), meaning "up" or "again", and the verb βαίνω (baino), meaning "to go".

Grammar:
αναβας is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is in the third person singular form.

Description:
αναβας can be translated to English as "he/she/it went up" or "he/she/it ascended". It is often used in a literal sense to describe physical movement upwards, such as climbing a mountain or stairs. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe progress or advancement in a non-physical sense.
