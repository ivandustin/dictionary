Word: ανεχομενοι

Etymology:
The word ανεχομενοι (anechomenoi) is a Greek word that comes from the verb ανέχομαι (anechomai), which means "to endure" or "to tolerate". The word is derived from the prefix αν- (an-), which means "not" or "without", and the verb εχω (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar:
ανεχομενοι is the plural form of the present participle of the verb ανέχομαι (anechomai). It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. The word can be translated to English as "enduring" or "tolerating".

Description:
ανεχομενοι is a word that describes people who are enduring or tolerating something. It can be used to describe someone who is putting up with a difficult situation or person, or someone who is persevering through a challenging task. The word has a connotation of strength and resilience, as it implies that the person is able to withstand something that might be difficult or unpleasant.
