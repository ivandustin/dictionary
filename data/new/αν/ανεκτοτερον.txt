Word: ανεκτοτερον

Etymology:
The word ανεκτοτερον is derived from the Greek word ανεκτος (anektos) which means "endurable" or "tolerable". The suffix -τερον (-teron) is a comparative suffix, which means "more" or "greater". Therefore, ανεκτοτερον means "more endurable" or "more tolerable".

Grammar:
ανεκτοτερον is an adjective in the comparative degree. It is used to compare two things, indicating that one is more endurable or tolerable than the other. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, ανεκτοτερον can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
ανεκτοτερον is a word that describes something that is more endurable or tolerable than something else. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a situation, a person's behavior, or a physical condition. For example, one could say "Η κατάσταση είναι ανεκτοτερη από ό,τι περίμενα" (The situation is more tolerable than I expected) or "Ο πόνος είναι ανεκτοτερος σήμερα" (The pain is more endurable today).
