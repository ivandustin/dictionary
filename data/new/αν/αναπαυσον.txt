Word: αναπαυσον

Etymology: αναπαυσον is a Greek word that comes from the verb αναπαύω (anapauo), which means "to rest" or "to take a break". The word is in the imperative form, which means it is a command or request to someone to rest.

Grammar: αναπαυσον is a second-person singular imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or request to someone to rest. The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: αναπαυσον is a word that is often used in Greek literature and poetry to convey a sense of rest or relaxation. It is a command or request to take a break from work or activity and to find peace and tranquility. The word can also be used metaphorically to suggest a need for spiritual or emotional rest.
