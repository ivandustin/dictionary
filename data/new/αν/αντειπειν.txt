The word "αντειπειν" (anteipein) is a Greek verb that means "to contradict" or "to oppose". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), which means "against" or "opposite", and the verb "ειπειν" (eipein), which means "to say" or "to speak".

Grammar: "αντειπειν" is a regular verb in Greek, belonging to the first conjugation. Its present tense stem is "αντιλέγω" (antilégo), and its past tense stem is "αντέλεγα" (antélega).

Description: The verb "αντειπειν" is often used in Greek to express disagreement or opposition to someone's opinion or statement. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a debate, an argument, or a discussion.
