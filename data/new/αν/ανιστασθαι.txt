Word: ανιστασθαι

Etymology:
The word ανιστασθαι (anistasthai) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix αν- (an-) meaning "up" or "against" and the verb ιστημι (histemi) meaning "to stand". Therefore, ανιστασθαι means "to stand up" or "to rise up".

Grammar:
ανιστασθαι is a middle voice verb in Greek, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is in the present tense and the infinitive form.

Description:
ανιστασθαι is a strong and powerful verb that conveys the idea of standing up against something or someone. It can be used in a literal sense, such as standing up from a seated position, or in a figurative sense, such as standing up for one's beliefs or rights. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
