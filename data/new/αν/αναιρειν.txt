The word "αναιρειν" (an-airein) is a Greek verb that means "to cancel", "to annul", "to revoke", or "to abolish". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "αν-" (an-), which means "not" or "without", and the verb "αιρειν" (airein), which means "to take" or "to choose". 

Grammar: "αναιρειν" is an active verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs. Its conjugation in the present tense is as follows:

- Αναιρώ (anairó) - I cancel
- Αναιρείς (anaireís) - You cancel
- Αναιρεί (anaireí) - He/She/It cancels
- Αναιρούμε (anairóume) - We cancel
- Αναιρείτε (anaireíte) - You (plural) cancel
- Αναιρούν (anairoún) - They cancel

Description: "αναιρειν" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts, such as legal, political, or personal. It is often used to describe the act of revoking a decision, canceling a contract, or annulling a law. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of negating or undoing something that has been done before.
