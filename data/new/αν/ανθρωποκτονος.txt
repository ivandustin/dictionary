Word: ανθρωποκτονος

Etymology: The word ανθρωποκτονος comes from the Greek words ανθρωπος (anthropos) meaning "human" and κτονος (ktonos) meaning "killing" or "murder". Therefore, ανθρωποκτονος means "murderer" or "killer of humans".

For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun in the nominative case, it becomes ανθρωποκτονος. If it modifies a feminine singular noun in the accusative case, it becomes ανθρωποκτονη.

Description: ανθρωποκτονος is a strong and negative word that describes someone who has committed the crime of murder. It is often used in legal contexts or in discussions about violent crimes. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has caused great harm or destruction.
