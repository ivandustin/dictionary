The word "αναστηθι" (anastethi) is a Greek verb in the imperative mood, which means "stand up" or "rise". It is derived from the root word "ανασταίνω" (anastaino), which means "to raise up" or "to cause to stand". 

In terms of grammar, "αναστηθι" is a second person singular imperative verb, which means it is a command directed towards one person. 

In Greek culture, the word "αναστηθι" is often used in religious contexts, such as during the Easter season when the phrase "Χριστός ανέστη" (Christ has risen) is commonly heard. It can also be used in everyday language to encourage someone to stand up or rise from a seated or lying position.
