The word "αναβητε" (anavite) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), which means "up" or "again," and the verb "βαίνω" (vaino), which means "to go." Therefore, "αναβητε" means "to go up again" or "to ascend."

Grammar: "αναβητε" is the second person plural of the present tense of the verb. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all go up again" or "you all ascend."

Description: The word "αναβητε" is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to ascending to a higher level of consciousness or to a higher place. It can also be used in a literal sense to describe physically going up or ascending a hill, mountain, or staircase.
