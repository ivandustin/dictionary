The word ανηχθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ανάγω (anago), which means "to lead up" or "to bring up". The prefix αν- (an-) means "up" or "back", and the suffix -ηχθησαν (-ichthisan) is the third person plural aorist passive indicative form of the verb.

Grammar:
ανηχθησαν is a third person plural aorist passive indicative form of the Greek verb ανάγω (anago). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.
