Word: ανθρωπον

Etymology: The word ανθρωπον (anthropon) is derived from the Greek word ἄνθρωπος (anthrōpos), which means "human being" or "man". It is a combination of the words ἀνήρ (anēr), meaning "man", and ὤψ (ōps), meaning "face" or "appearance".

Grammar: ανθρωπον is a noun in the singular form. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: ανθρωπον is a Greek word that means "man" or "human being". It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to refer to the nature of humanity. In ancient Greek literature, it was used to describe both men and women, but in modern Greek, it is often used to refer specifically to men.
