Word: αντιδιατιθεμενους

Etymology:
The word αντιδιατιθεμενους is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: αντι (anti) meaning "against" or "opposite" and διατίθεμαι (diatithemai) meaning "to arrange" or "to dispose". The word is derived from the ancient Greek verb τίθημι (tithemi) meaning "to put" or "to place".

Grammar:
αντιδιατιθεμενους is a plural adjective in the masculine gender, accusative case, and present participle tense. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently in a state of opposition or contrast.
