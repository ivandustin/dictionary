Word: ανελεος

The word ανελεος comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of two words: αν-, which means "not," and ελεος, which means "mercy" or "compassion." Therefore, ανελεος means "merciless" or "uncompassionate."

ανελεος is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes ανελεοι.

Description:
ανελεος is a word that describes someone who is lacking in mercy or compassion. It is often used to describe a person who is cruel, heartless, or unfeeling. The word can also be used to describe a situation or event that is harsh or unforgiving.
