Word: ανηχθη

Etymology:
The word ανηχθη (anixthi) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ανάγω (anago), which means "to lead up" or "to bring up". The prefix αν- (an-) means "up" or "back", and the suffix -η (thi) indicates the past tense.

Grammar:
ανηχθη is the third person singular past tense form of the verb ανάγω (anago). It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is passive, meaning that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
The word ανηχθη (anixthi) can be translated to English as "was led up" or "was brought up". It is often used in the context of someone being taken up to a higher place, such as a mountain or a platform. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone being elevated to a higher position or status.
