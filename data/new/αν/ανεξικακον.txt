The word "ανεξικακον" (anexikakon) is a Greek adjective that means "patient" or "enduring." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "ανεξίκακος" (anexikakos), which means "patient" or "enduring," and "αν-" (an-), which is a prefix that means "not" or "without." 

Grammar: "Ανεξικακον" is in the neuter gender and singular form. 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is able to endure difficult situations or hardships without complaining or becoming angry. It is a positive trait that is valued in Greek culture.
