Word: αντιλεγεται

Etymology: The word αντιλεγεται comes from the Greek roots αντι- (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and λεγω (lego), meaning "to speak" or "to say." Therefore, αντιλεγεται can be translated as "to speak against" or "to contradict."

Grammar: αντιλεγεται is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb αντιλέγω (antilégo), which means "to contradict" or "to argue against." The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: αντιλέγει (antilégei) - he/she/it contradicts
- Imperfect tense: αντελέγετο (antelégato) - he/she/it was contradicting
- Future tense: θα αντιλέγει (tha antilégei) - he/she/it will contradict

Description: αντιλεγεται is a Greek verb that means "to contradict" or "to argue against." It is often used in discussions or debates when one person disagrees with another person's statement or opinion. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, philosophy, or everyday conversations.
