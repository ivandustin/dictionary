Word: ανατειλαντος

Etymology: The word ανατειλαντος comes from the Greek verb ανατέλλω (anatéllo), which means "to rise" or "to appear". The suffix -αντος (-antos) is a participle ending, indicating that the word is describing someone or something that is rising or appearing.

Grammar: ανατειλαντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of the participle ανατειλαντος, which means "rising" or "appearing". In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between nouns.

Description: ανατειλαντος can be translated as "of the one who is rising" or "of the one who is appearing". It is often used in a poetic or metaphorical sense to describe the rising of the sun or the appearance of a new day. In Christian theology, it is used to describe the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
