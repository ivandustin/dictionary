Word: ανακειμένους

Etymology:
The word ανακειμένους (anakeimenous) is a Greek word that comes from the verb ανακείμαι (anakeimai), which means "to recline" or "to lie down". In ancient Greece, people would recline on couches while eating or socializing, and this verb was used to describe that action.

Grammar:
ανακειμένους is the plural form of the adjective ανακείμενος (anakeimenos), which means "reclining" or "lying down". It is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of the sentence.

Description:
ανακειμένους is used to describe a group of people who are reclining or lying down. It can be used in a literal sense, such as describing people who are lounging on couches or beds, or in a figurative sense, such as describing people who are relaxed or at ease. The word is often used in the context of social gatherings or meals, where people might be reclining while eating or conversing.
