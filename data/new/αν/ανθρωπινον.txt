Word: ανθρωπινον

Etymology: ανθρωπινον is a Greek word that comes from the root word ανθρωπος (anthropos), meaning "human being" or "man". The suffix -ινον (-inos) is added to the root word to form an adjective, meaning "related to humans" or "human-like".

Grammar: ανθρωπινον is a neuter singular adjective in the Greek language. It is declined as follows:

- Nominative: ανθρωπινον
- Genitive: ανθρωπινου
- Accusative: ανθρωπινον
- Vocative: ανθρωπινον
- Dative: ανθρωπινω

Description: ανθρωπινον is an adjective that describes something as being related to humans or human-like. It can be used to describe physical characteristics, emotions, behaviors, or anything else that is associated with human beings. For example, ανθρωπινον μυαλο (anthropinon myalo) means "human brain", ανθρωπινον συναισθημα (anthropinon synaisthima) means "human emotion", and ανθρωπινον δικαιωμα (anthropinon dikaioma) means "human right".
