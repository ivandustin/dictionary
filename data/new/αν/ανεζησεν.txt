Word: ανεζησεν

Etymology: ανεζησεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ζῆν (zen), which means "to live". The prefix ανε- (ane-) is an intensifier, so ανεζησεν means "to live fully" or "to live intensely".

Grammar: ανεζησεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is in the third person singular form, which means that it refers to a single person or thing that lived intensely.

Description: ανεζησεν can be translated as "he/she/it lived intensely" or "he/she/it lived fully". It implies that the person or thing in question lived with great passion and energy, making the most of their time on earth. It is a powerful and evocative word that suggests a life well-lived.
