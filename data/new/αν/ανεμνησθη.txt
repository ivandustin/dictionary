Word: ανεμνησθη

Etymology:
The word ανεμνησθη is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: ανα (ana) meaning "up" or "again" and εμνησθη (emnēsthē) meaning "remembered". Therefore, ανεμνησθη means "remembered again" or "recalled to memory".

Grammar:
ανεμνησθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the prefix ανα to the verb εμνησθη, which means "to remember". The aorist tense indicates a past action that is completed, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description:
ανεμνησθη is a verb that describes the act of remembering something that was previously forgotten or recalling something to memory. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe a sudden recollection or a moment of nostalgia. The word has a strong emotional connotation and is often associated with feelings of longing, regret, or joy.
