Word: αντιχριστος

Etymology:
The word αντιχριστος is derived from the Greek words αντι (anti) meaning "against" or "opposite" and Χριστός (Christos) meaning "Christ". Therefore, the word αντιχριστος means "anti-Christ" or "opposed to Christ".

Grammar:
In Greek, αντιχριστος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive αντιχρίστου (anti-christou) or accusative αντίχριστον (anti-christon).

Description:
The term αντιχριστος is used in Christian theology to refer to a figure who opposes or is against Christ. In the New Testament, the term is used in the First and Second Epistles of John to describe those who deny that Jesus is the Christ. The concept of the anti-Christ has been interpreted in various ways throughout history, with some seeing it as a specific individual or group, while others view it as a symbol of evil or opposition to Christ.
