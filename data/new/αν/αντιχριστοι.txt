Word: αντιχριστοι

Etymology: The word αντιχριστοι (antichristoi) is derived from the Greek words αντι (anti) meaning "against" or "opposite" and χριστός (christos) meaning "Christ". It is used in the New Testament to refer to those who oppose or deny the teachings of Christ.

Grammar: αντιχριστοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is declined as follows: 

Nominative: αντιχριστοι (antichristoi)
Genitive: αντιχριστων (antichriston)
Dative: αντιχριστοις (antichristois)
Accusative: αντιχριστους (antichristous)

It is often used in a religious context to refer to individuals or groups who are seen as enemies of Christianity.
