Word: αντοφθαλμειν

Etymology:
The word αντοφθαλμειν is derived from the Greek words αντί (anti) meaning "against" or "opposite" and οφθαλμός (ophthalmos) meaning "eye". Therefore, the literal meaning of αντοφθαλμειν is "to look someone in the eye".

Grammar:
αντοφθαλμειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is an infinitive form of the verb and is not conjugated to match any specific tense, person, or number.

Description:
αντοφθαλμειν is a term used to describe the act of looking someone directly in the eye, often as a sign of respect, honesty, or sincerity. It can also be used to convey a sense of challenge or defiance, depending on the context in which it is used. In ancient Greek culture, maintaining eye contact was considered an important aspect of communication and was often used as a way to establish trust and authority. Today, the term αντοφθαλμειν is still used in modern Greek to describe the act of making eye contact with someone.
