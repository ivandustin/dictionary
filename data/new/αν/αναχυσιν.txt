Word: αναχυσιν

Etymology:
The word αναχυσιν (anachysin) is derived from the Greek verb αναχύνω (anachyno), which means "to pour out" or "to shed." The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the action or result of the verb.

Grammar:
αναχυσιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
αναχυσιν refers to the act of pouring out or shedding something, such as tears or blood. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the release of emotions or feelings. The word is not commonly used in modern Greek, but it can be found in literature and poetry.
