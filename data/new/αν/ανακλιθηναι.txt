Word: ανακλιθηναι

Etymology:
The word ανακλιθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: ανα (ana) meaning "up" or "back" and κλινω (klinō) meaning "to lean" or "to recline". Therefore, ανακλιθηναι means "to lean back" or "to recline".

Grammar:
ανακλιθηναι is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive mood is used to express the action of the verb without specifying the subject or the time of the action.

Description:
ανακλιθηναι is a Greek verb that means "to lean back" or "to recline". It is often used in the context of resting or relaxing, such as reclining on a couch or leaning back in a chair. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is taking a break or stepping back from a situation.
