Word: ανεγκλητους

Etymology:
The word ανεγκλητους (anegkletous) is derived from the Greek prefix αν- (an-) meaning "not" and the adjective εγκλητος (enkletos) meaning "accused" or "charged". Therefore, ανεγκλητους means "not accused" or "not charged".

Grammar:
ανεγκλητους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who have not been accused or charged with a crime.

Description:
The word ανεγκλητους is often used in legal contexts to describe individuals who have not been accused or charged with a crime. It can also be used more broadly to describe individuals who are free from blame or guilt.
