The word "αναπολογητος" (anapologitos) is derived from the Greek prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "not" or "un-", and the verb "πολογέομαι" (pologeomai), meaning "to defend oneself" or "to make excuses". Therefore, "αναπολογητος" can be translated as "unapologetic" or "unrepentant".

"αναπολογητος" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, "αναπολογητη" (anapologiti) is the feminine singular form, "αναπολογητο" (anapologito) is the neuter singular form, and "αναπολογητοι" (anapologitoi) is the masculine plural form.

Description:

"αναπολογητος" is used to describe someone who is not sorry for their actions or behavior, and does not feel the need to apologize or make excuses.
