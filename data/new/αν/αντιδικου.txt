The word "αντιδικου" (antidikou) is a Greek adjective that means "adversarial" or "opposing". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "anti-" meaning "against" or "opposite", and the noun "dikē" meaning "justice" or "right". 

Grammar: "Αντιδικου" is in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word "αντιδικου" is often used in legal contexts to describe a party that is opposing or in conflict with another party. It can also be used more broadly to describe any situation or relationship that involves opposition or conflict.
