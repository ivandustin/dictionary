Word: ανακαινουται

Etymology:
The word ανακαινουται (anakainoutai) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: ανα (ana) meaning "again" or "anew" and καινός (kainos) meaning "new" or "fresh". Therefore, the word ανακαινουται can be translated as "to renew" or "to renovate".

Grammar:
ανακαινουται is a third-person singular present tense verb in Greek. It belongs to the passive voice and the indicative mood. The verb is conjugated from the root word καινός (kainos) and the prefix ανα (ana) which indicates repetition or renewal.

Description:
The word ανακαινουται is commonly used in modern Greek to describe the process of renovating or renewing something, such as a building, a house, or a piece of furniture. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of refreshing or revitalizing something, such as a relationship or a business. The word is often used in the context of construction and home improvement, as well as in everyday conversation.
