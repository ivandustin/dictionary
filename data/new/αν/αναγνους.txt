The word "αναγνους" (anagnous) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "γινώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to recognize". The prefix "ανα" (ana) in this case adds the meaning of "again" or "back".

In terms of grammar, "αναγνους" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense. It can be translated to "you recognized" or "you knew".

Overall, "αναγνους" refers to the act of recognizing or knowing something again or back in the past.
