Word: αναθεμασιν

Etymology:
The word αναθεμασιν (anathemasin) is derived from the Greek word ανάθεμα (anathema), which means "a thing devoted to evil" or "a curse". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is added to form the accusative case.

Grammar:
αναθεμασιν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
αναθεμασιν is a strong word used to express a curse or condemnation. It is often used in religious contexts to refer to something that is considered to be accursed or condemned by God. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe those who are cut off from God's grace and are subject to eternal punishment.
