Word: ανεκλειπτον

Etymology:
The word ανεκλειπτον is derived from the Greek words αν- (an-) meaning "not" and εκλειπτω (ekleipto) meaning "to fail, to cease, to disappear". Therefore, the word ανεκλειπτον means "unceasing, never failing, perpetual".

Grammar:
ανεκλειπτον is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case in Greek grammar. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
ανεκλειπτον is a word that describes something that never fails or ceases to exist. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a perpetual flame or an unending love. The word has a positive connotation and is often used to describe something that is desirable or admirable.
