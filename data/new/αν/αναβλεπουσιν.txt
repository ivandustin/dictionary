The word "αναβλεπουσιν" (anablepousin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "αναβλεπω" (anablepo), which means "to look up" or "to raise one's eyes." 

In terms of grammar, "αναβλεπουσιν" is a regular present tense verb in the indicative mood, which means it is used to make a statement or ask a question. The "-ουσιν" ending indicates that it is in the third person plural form, meaning "they look up" or "they raise their eyes."

Overall, the word "αναβλεπουσιν" can be translated to mean "they are looking up" or "they are raising their eyes." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone who is seeking guidance or inspiration from a higher power.
