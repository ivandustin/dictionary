The word "ανερ" (aner) is a Greek noun that means "man" or "male". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἀνήρ" (anḗr), which also means "man". In terms of grammar, it is a third-declension masculine noun. It can be used to refer to a specific man or to men in general. In ancient Greek literature, it was often used to describe heroic or virtuous men.
