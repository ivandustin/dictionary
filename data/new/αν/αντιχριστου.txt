Word: αντιχριστου

Etymology: The word αντιχριστου comes from the Greek words αντι (anti) meaning "against" or "opposite" and χριστός (christos) meaning "Christ". Therefore, αντιχριστου means "against Christ" or "opposite of Christ".

Grammar: αντιχριστου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is often used in Christian theology to refer to the concept of an opposing force to Christ.

Description: αντιχριστου is a term used in Christian theology to refer to the idea of an opposing force to Christ. It is often associated with the concept of the Antichrist, a figure who is believed to appear before the end of the world and oppose Christ. The term is also used more broadly to refer to any person or force that is opposed to the teachings of Christ or Christianity.
