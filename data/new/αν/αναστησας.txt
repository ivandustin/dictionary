Word: αναστησας

Etymology: αναστησας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ανασταίνω (anastaíno), which means "to raise up" or "to resurrect." It is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "again," and the verb σταίνω (staíno), which means "to stand."

Grammar: αναστησας is the aorist active participle of the Greek verb ανίστημι (anístemi), which means "to rise up" or "to stand up." In this form, it is used to describe an action that has been completed in the past. It is also in the second person singular, indicating that the action was performed by one person.

Description: αναστησας can be translated to English as "you raised up" or "you resurrected." It is a powerful verb that is often used in the New Testament to describe the resurrection of Jesus Christ. In this context, it is a reminder of the power of God to bring life out of death.
