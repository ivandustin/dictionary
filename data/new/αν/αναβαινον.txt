Word: αναβαινον

Etymology:
The word αναβαινον (anabainon) is a present participle form of the Greek verb αναβαινω (anabaino), which means "to go up" or "to ascend". The word is derived from the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "up" or "again", and the verb βαινω (baino), which means "to go".

Grammar:
αναβαινον is a present participle form of the verb αναβαινω (anabaino). In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ων (-on) to the stem of the verb. Present participles are used to describe ongoing actions or states, and can function as adjectives or nouns.

Description:
αναβαινον (anabainon) can be translated as "going up" or "ascending". It describes an ongoing action or state of something or someone that is moving upwards. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as climbing stairs or a mountain, or in a figurative sense, such as rising in status or importance.
