Word: ανεωιξεν

Etymology: The word ανεωιξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ανοίγω (anoigo), which means "to open". The prefix ανε- (ane-) adds emphasis to the action of opening.

Grammar: ανεωιξεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is in the past tense and indicates a completed action. The verb is conjugated from the root word ανοίγω (anoigo) and the augment ε- (e-).

Description: ανεωιξεν means "he/she/it opened" in Greek. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the opening of doors, eyes, and other objects. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the opening of one's mind or heart.
