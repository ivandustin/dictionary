Word: ανταποκριθηναι

Etymology: ανταποκριθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix αντι- (anti-), which means "against" or "opposite," and the verb αποκρινομαι (apokrinomai), which means "to answer" or "to reply." Therefore, ανταποκριθηναι means "to answer back" or "to reply in turn."

Grammar: ανταποκριθηναι is an aorist infinitive passive verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for person, number, or tense. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description: ανταποκριθηναι is a verb that describes the act of responding or replying to someone or something. It implies a sense of reciprocity or a back-and-forth exchange of communication. The passive voice suggests that the subject is not actively initiating the response but rather receiving it from someone else. Overall, ανταποκριθηναι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe different types of responses or replies.
