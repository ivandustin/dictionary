Word: ανεκραγον

Etymology:
The word ανεκραγον is a Greek verb that comes from the root words αν- (an-) meaning "up" or "back" and κραζω (krazo) meaning "to shout" or "to cry out". Therefore, ανεκραγον means "I shouted out" or "I cried out".

Grammar:
ανεκραγον is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb κραζω (krazo) in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
ανεκραγον is a strong and powerful word that conveys a sense of urgency and intensity. It suggests that the speaker was shouting or crying out with great force and emotion. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to express joy, pain, fear, anger, or surprise. It is often used in literature and poetry to create a vivid and dramatic effect.
