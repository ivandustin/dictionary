The word "αντλειν" (antlein) is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "ἄντλημα" (antlema), which means "a bucket or a vessel used for drawing water." 

In terms of grammar, "αντλειν" is a present tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated as follows: 

- Αντλώ (antlo) - I draw
- Αντλείς (antleis) - You draw
- Αντλεί (antlei) - He/She/It draws
- Αντλούμε (antloume) - We draw
- Αντλείτε (antleite) - You (plural) draw
- Αντλούν (antloun) - They draw

The verb "αντλειν" means "to draw" or "to pump." It is often used in the context of drawing water from a well or pumping fluids from one place to another.
