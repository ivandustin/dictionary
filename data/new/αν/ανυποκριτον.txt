Word: ανυποκριτον

Etymology: The word ανυποκριτον comes from the Greek prefix αν- (an-), meaning "not," and the Greek word υποκρισις (hypokrisis), meaning "hypocrisy" or "acting." Therefore, ανυποκριτον means "not hypocritical" or "sincere."

Grammar: ανυποκριτον is a neuter noun in the nominative singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can also be used as an adjective, meaning "sincere" or "honest."

Description: ανυποκριτον is a word that describes someone or something that is genuine, honest, and sincere. It is often used to describe a person who speaks or acts without pretense or hypocrisy. In Greek philosophy, the concept of ανυποκριτον was highly valued as a virtue, representing a person's ability to live in accordance with their true nature and values.
