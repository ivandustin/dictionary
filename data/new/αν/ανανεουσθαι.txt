Word: ανανεουσθαι

Etymology:
The word ανανεουσθαι is derived from the Greek verb ανανεοω (ananeoō), which means "to renew" or "to revive". It is formed by adding the prefix ανα- (ana-), which means "again" or "anew", to the verb νεοω (neoō), which means "to make new" or "to refresh".

ανανεουσθαι is a reflexive verb in the present tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. It is conjugated from the root verb ανανεοω (ananeoō), which is a first-conjugation verb. In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows:

- Ανανεομαι (ananeomai) - I am renewed
- Ανανεοσαι (ananeosai) - You are renewed
- Ανανεοται (ananeotai) - He/She/It is renewed

Plural:
- Ανανεομαστε (ananeomaste) - We are renewed
- Ανανεοστε (ananeoste) - You are renewed
- Ανανεονται (ananeontai) - They are renewed

Description:
The word ανανεουσθαι refers to the act of being renewed or revived. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to the renewal of a contract or the revival of a tradition. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as when referring to the renewal of one's spirit or energy. The word conveys a sense of freshness, vitality, and newness.
