Word: ανηλθεν

Etymology: ανηλθεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb αναβαίνω (anabaino), which means "to go up" or "to ascend". The prefix ανα- (ana-) means "up" or "again", and βαίνω (baino) means "to go".

Grammar: ανηλθεν is the aorist tense of the verb αναβαίνω (anabaino), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past. It is in the third person singular form.

Description: ανηλθεν means "he/she/it went up" or "he/she/it ascended". It is often used in a spiritual or religious context, such as in the Bible when referring to Jesus ascending to heaven. It can also be used in a literal sense, such as when describing someone climbing a mountain or stairs.
