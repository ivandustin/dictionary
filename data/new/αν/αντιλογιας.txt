The word "αντιλογιας" (antilogias) is a Greek noun that can be translated to "argumentation" or "disputation". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "anti-" meaning "against" or "opposite", and the noun "λογια" (logia) meaning "words" or "speech". 

Grammar: "Αντιλογιας" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It can be translated to "of argumentation" or "of disputation". 

Description: The word refers to the act of presenting arguments or engaging in a debate or dispute. It can also refer to a written or spoken work that presents arguments on a particular topic.
