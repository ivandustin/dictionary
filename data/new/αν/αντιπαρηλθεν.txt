Word: αντιπαρηλθεν

Etymology: The word αντιπαρηλθεν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix αντι- (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and the verb παρηλθεν (parēlthen), meaning "passed by" or "went past." 

Grammar: αντιπαρηλθεν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular, in the Greek language. 

Description: The word αντιπαρηλθεν describes an action that has already taken place, specifically the act of passing by or going past something or someone in an opposing or opposite direction. It can be used in a literal sense, such as passing by a person on the street, or in a figurative sense, such as passing by an opportunity or a problem without addressing it.
