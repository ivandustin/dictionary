Etymology: The word "γερουσιαν" comes from the Greek word "γερουσία" (gerousía), which means "senate" or "council of elders". 

Grammar: "Γερουσιαν" is a noun in the singular form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Γερουσιαν" refers to a member or a characteristic of the "γερουσία" (gerousía), which was an important political institution in ancient Greece. The gerousía was a council of elders that played a significant role in the decision-making process in various city-states. The members of the gerousía were typically older, experienced individuals who provided advice and guidance to the ruling body.
