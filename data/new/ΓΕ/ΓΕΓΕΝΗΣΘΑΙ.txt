Etymology: The word "γεγενησθαι" comes from the Greek verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". It is a compound word formed by adding the prefix "γε-" (ge-) to the verb stem "γεν-" (gen-).

Grammar: "γεγενησθαι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action or event that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "γεγενησθαι" can be translated as "to have been born" or "to have become". It is used to describe a completed action of being born or becoming something.
