Etymology: The word "γεωργός" (georgos) comes from the Greek roots "γῆ" (ge) meaning "earth" and "ἔργον" (ergon) meaning "work". Therefore, "γεωργός" literally means "earth-worker" or "farmer".

Grammar: In Greek, "γεωργός" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "γεωργοί" (georgoi).

Description: "Γεωργός" refers to a person who cultivates the land, engages in agricultural activities, and is involved in farming. This term is commonly used to describe someone who grows crops, raises livestock, or manages agricultural operations.
