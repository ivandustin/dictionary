Etymology: The word "γενησεται" (genēsetai) comes from the Greek verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁-.

Grammar: "γενησεται" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.
