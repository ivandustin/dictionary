Etymology: The word "γεδεών" (gedeōn) comes from the Greek name Γεδεών (Gedeōn), which is derived from the Hebrew name גִּדְעוֹן (Gid'on). 

Grammar: "Γεδεών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine name and is declined according to the Greek noun declension patterns.

Description: In Greek mythology, Gedeon (Γεδεών) was a hero and a judge of Israel. He is best known for leading the Israelites to victory against the Midianites with a small army of 300 men. Gedeon's story is described in the Book of Judges in the Hebrew Bible.
