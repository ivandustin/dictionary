Etymology: The word "γενομένης" (genomenes) is derived from the Greek verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". It is in the genitive case form, indicating possession or origin.

Grammar: "Γενομένης" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Γενομένης" can be translated as "of becoming" or "of being born". It is often used in philosophical or theological contexts to refer to the process of coming into existence or the act of being created.
