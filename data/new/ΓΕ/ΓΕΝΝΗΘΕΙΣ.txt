Etymology: The word "γεννηθείς" (gennithis) comes from the Greek verb "γεννιέμαι" (genniémai), which means "to be born" or "to come into existence."

Grammar: "Γεννηθείς" is the aorist participle form of the verb "γεννιέμαι" (genniémai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The participle form indicates that the action of being born has already taken place.

Description: "Γεννηθείς" is a Greek word that means "having been born" or "having come into existence." It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to refer to the act of being born or created.
