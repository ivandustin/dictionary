Etymology: The word "γενομενα" comes from the Greek verb "γίνομαι" (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". 

Grammar: "Γενομενα" is the plural form of the noun "γενομενον" (genomenon), which is the neuter participle of the verb "γίνομαι". In Greek grammar, neuter nouns ending in "-ον" form their plural by replacing the "-ον" ending with "-α".

Description: "Γενομενα" is a Greek word that can be translated as "events" or "occurrences". It refers to things that have happened or come into existence.
