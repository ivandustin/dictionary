Etymology: The word "γεννησαντα" comes from the Greek verb "γεννάω" (gennáo), which means "to give birth" or "to generate." The suffix "-σαντα" is the aorist participle form of the verb, indicating past action.

Grammar: "Γεννησαντα" is the aorist participle form of the verb "γεννάω" in the masculine singular accusative case. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as independent verbs.

Description: "Γεννησαντα" refers to a male subject who has given birth or generated something in the past. It can be used as an adjective to describe a noun or as a verb in a sentence.
