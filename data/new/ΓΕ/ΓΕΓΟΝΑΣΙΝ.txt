Etymology: The word "γεγονασιν" comes from the Greek verb "γίγνομαι" (gínomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁-.

Grammar: "γεγονασιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "γίγνομαι" in the perfect tense. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate completed actions or states that have a continuing relevance to the present.

Description: "γεγονασιν" can be translated as "they have become" or "they have been born." It implies that a certain action or state has occurred in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.
