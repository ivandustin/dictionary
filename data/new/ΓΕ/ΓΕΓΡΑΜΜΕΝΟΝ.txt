Etymology: The word "γεγραμμένον" comes from the Greek verb "γράφω" (grapho), which means "to write". The prefix "γε-" (ge-) indicates completion or perfect tense, so "γεγραμμένον" can be translated as "written" or "having been written".

Grammar: "Γεγραμμένον" is the neuter form of the adjective, used to describe a noun. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this form, it is in the nominative case, singular number.

Description: "Γεγραμμένον" is an adjective that describes something as having been written. It can be used to refer to a written document, a piece of literature, or any other form of written text.
