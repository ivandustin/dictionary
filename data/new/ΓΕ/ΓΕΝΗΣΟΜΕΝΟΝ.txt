Etymology: The word "γενησομενον" comes from the Greek verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁-.

Grammar: "γενησομενον" is the future middle participle of the verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai). In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to modify nouns or function as independent verbs. The future middle participle indicates an action that will happen to the subject in the future and is in the middle voice, which implies that the subject is performing the action on itself.

Description: "γενησομενον" can be translated as "being born" or "about to be born". It refers to the state of something or someone that is in the process of coming into existence or being brought forth.
