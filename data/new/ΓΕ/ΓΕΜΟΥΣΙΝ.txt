Etymology: The word "γεμούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "γεμίζω" (gemízo), which means "to fill" or "to be full". It is derived from the noun "γέμος" (gémos), meaning "fullness" or "abundance".

Grammar: "Γεμούσιν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "γεμίζω" (gemízo). In Greek grammar, it belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. It is used to describe an ongoing action of filling or being full.

Description: "Γεμούσιν" describes the action of filling or being full. It can be used to refer to physical objects being filled with something, as well as metaphorical concepts such as emotions or ideas being filled or overflowing.
