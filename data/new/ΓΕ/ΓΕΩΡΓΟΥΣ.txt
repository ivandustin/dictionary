Etymology: The word "γεωργούς" (geōrgoús) comes from the Greek words "γῆ" (gē), meaning "earth" or "land," and "ἔργον" (érgon), meaning "work" or "task." Therefore, "γεωργούς" can be translated as "worker of the land" or "farmer."

Grammar: In Greek, "γεωργούς" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Γεωργούς" refers to a person who cultivates and works on the land, typically engaged in agricultural activities such as farming, gardening, or crop cultivation. This term is commonly used to describe someone who is involved in the agricultural industry or practices farming as a profession.
