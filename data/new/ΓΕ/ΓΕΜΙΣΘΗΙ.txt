Etymology: The word "γεμισθηι" (gemisthēi) comes from the Greek verb "γεμίζω" (gemízō), which means "to fill" or "to stuff". It is derived from the noun "γέμος" (gémos), meaning "fullness" or "abundance".

The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, the active voice shows that the subject performs the action, and the subjunctive mood expresses a possibility or potentiality.
