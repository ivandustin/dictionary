Etymology: The word "γεγονα" (gegona) comes from the Greek verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". 

Grammar: "Γεγονα" is the first person singular perfect tense of the verb "γίγνομαι". In Greek, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance to the present. 

Description: "Γεγονα" is a word in the Greek language that means "I have become" or "I have happened". It is used to express a state of being or an action that has been completed in the past.
