Etymology: The word "γεγραπται" (gegraptai) comes from the Greek verb "γράφω" (grapho), which means "to write". It is in the perfect tense and passive voice form.

Grammar: "γεγραπται" is the third person singular form of the verb "γράφω" (grapho) in the perfect tense and passive voice. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "γεγραπται" is a Greek word that means "it has been written" or "it is written". It implies that something has been written in the past and the written text still has relevance or importance in the present.
