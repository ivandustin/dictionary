Etymology: The word "γενεσιος" (geneseios) comes from the Greek word "γένεσις" (genesis), meaning "origin" or "birth".

Grammar: In Greek, "γενεσιος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It can also be declined to match the gender and number of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Γενεσιος" is used to describe something that is related to birth, origin, or creation. It can refer to the beginning or starting point of something, or the qualities and characteristics associated with the beginning or origin of a person, thing, or concept.
