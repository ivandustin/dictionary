Etymology: The word "γευση" (geusi) comes from the Greek verb "γεύομαι" (geuomai), which means "to taste" or "to experience." 

Grammar: "Γευση" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Γευση" refers to the sense of taste. It is the ability to perceive different flavors and distinguish between them. The sense of taste is closely related to the taste buds on the tongue, which can detect sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami (savory) flavors. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the enjoyment or appreciation of something, such as the "taste" of music or art.
