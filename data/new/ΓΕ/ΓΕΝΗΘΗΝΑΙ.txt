Etymology: The word "γενηθῆναι" (genēthēnai) comes from the Greek verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born."

Grammar: "Γενηθῆναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "γίγνομαι" (ginomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Γενηθῆναι" is used to express the concept of "to be born" or "to come into existence." It is often used in the context of birth or creation.
