Etymology: The word "γεννηθηί" (gennithi) comes from the Greek verb "γεννιέμαι" (genniémai), which means "to be born" or "to come into existence."

Grammar: "Γεννηθηί" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and subjunctive mood. It is the third person singular form.

Description: "Γεννηθηί" is used to express a hypothetical or potential action in the past. It is often translated as "would have been born" or "might have come into existence."
