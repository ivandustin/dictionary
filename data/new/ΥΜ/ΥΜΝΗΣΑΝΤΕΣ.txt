Etymology: The word "υμνησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "υμνέω" (hymnéo), which means "to sing a hymn" or "to praise." The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending, indicating the past tense and plural form.

Grammar: "Υμνησαντες" is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the verb "υμνέω" (hymnéo). In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as verbs. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: "Υμνησαντες" translates to "having praised" or "having sung hymns" in English. It is used to describe a group of people who have already praised or sung hymns.
