Etymology: The word "υμετεραι" (umeterai) comes from the Greek word "υμετερος" (umeteros), which means "yours" or "belonging to you". It is derived from the pronoun "υμεις" (umeis), meaning "you" in the plural form.

Grammar: "Υμετεραι" is the feminine plural form of the adjective "υμετερος" (umeteros). In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "υμετεραι" is used to describe feminine plural nouns.

Description: "Υμετεραι" is an adjective that means "yours" or "belonging to you" in the feminine plural form. It is used to indicate possession or ownership by a group of people addressed as "you" in the plural form.
