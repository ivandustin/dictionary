Etymology: The word "υμετεραν" comes from the Greek word "υμετερος" (umeteros), meaning "yours" or "belonging to you". It is derived from the pronoun "υμεις" (umeis), which means "you" in the plural form.

Grammar: "Υμετεραν" is a Greek adjective in the accusative case. It is used to modify a noun and agrees with it in gender, number, and case. In this form, it is used to indicate possession or ownership, similar to the English word "your" or "yours".

Description: "Υμετεραν" is used to refer to something that belongs to or is associated with the person or people being addressed. It is commonly used in formal or polite speech to show respect or deference.
