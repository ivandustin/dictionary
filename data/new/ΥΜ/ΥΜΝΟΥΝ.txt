Etymology: The word "υμνούν" (pronounced "imnoon") comes from the Greek verb "υμνέω" (imneo), which means "to praise" or "to hymn." It is derived from the noun "ύμνος" (imnos), meaning "hymn" or "ode."

Grammar: "Υμνούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "υμνέω" (imneo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject and tense. The "-ούν" ending indicates that the subject is third person plural (they) and the action is happening in the present.

Description: "Υμνούν" is a Greek verb that means "they praise" or "they hymn." It is used to describe the action of praising or singing hymns.
