Word: τοπου

Etymology:
The word τοπου (topou) is derived from the Greek word τόπος (topos), which means "place" or "location". The suffix -ου (-ou) is a genitive ending, indicating possession or relationship.

Grammar:
τοπου (topou) is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to show possession, origin, or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
τοπου (topou) refers to a place or location that is possessed or related to something else. It can be used to describe a physical location, such as a city or a building, or a more abstract concept, such as a position or a role. The word is often used in combination with other words to create compound nouns, such as τοποθεσία (topothesia), which means "location" or "placement".
