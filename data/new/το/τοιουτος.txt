Word: τοιουτος

Etymology:
τοιουτος is a Greek word that is composed of two parts: τοιος (meaning "such") and ουτος (meaning "this"). When combined, τοιουτος means "such as this" or "of this kind".

Grammar:
In Greek, τοιουτος is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used as a predicate adjective or as an attributive adjective. For example, in the sentence "Τοιουτος άνθρωπος είναι αξιόλογος" (Such a person is admirable), τοιουτος is a predicate adjective modifying the noun άνθρωπος.

Description:
τοιουτος is a versatile word that can be used to describe a wide range of things. It can be used to describe people, objects, or situations that are similar to something else. For example, it can be used to describe a person who is similar to another person in terms of their character or behavior. It can also be used to describe an object that is similar to another object in terms of its shape or function. Additionally, it can be used to describe a situation that is similar to another situation in terms of its outcome or consequences.
