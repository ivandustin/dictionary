τοσουτων (tosoutōn) is a Greek word that means "so many" or "such a great number". 

Etymology: The word is a combination of two Greek words, τόσος (tósos) meaning "so much" and τοῦτος (toûtos) meaning "this". 

Grammar: τοσουτων is a plural form of the adjective τοσοῦτος (tosoutos), which agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. 

Description: The word is commonly used in Greek to express a large or indefinite number of things or people. It can be used as an adjective or a pronoun, and is often followed by a genitive noun or pronoun to indicate the specific group or category being referred to.
