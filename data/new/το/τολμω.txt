τολμώ (tolmó) is a Greek verb that means "to dare" or "to have the courage to do something". 

Etymology: The word τολμώ comes from the ancient Greek word τόλμα (tólma), which means "boldness" or "courage". 

Grammar: τολμώ is a first-person singular present tense verb in Greek. Its conjugation is as follows: 

- Εγώ τολμώ (I dare)
- Εσύ τολμάς (You dare)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό τολμάει (He/She/It dares)
- Εμείς τολμάμε (We dare)
- Εσείς τολμάτε (You dare)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές τολμάνε (They dare)

Description: τολμώ is a strong and active verb that implies taking risks and facing challenges. It is often used in contexts where someone is encouraged to step out of their comfort zone and try something new or difficult. It can also be used to express admiration for someone who has the courage to do something that others might find intimidating or impossible.
