Word: τολμησας

Etymology:
The word τολμησας (tolmēsas) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It is derived from the root word τολμάω (tolmáō), which means "to dare" or "to be bold". The suffix -σας (-sas) indicates the second person singular form of the aorist tense.

Grammar:
τολμησας (tolmēsas) is a verb in the aorist tense, second person singular form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The second person singular form indicates that the action was performed by the person being addressed.

Description:
τολμησας (tolmēsas) can be translated to English as "you dared" or "you were bold". It implies that the person being addressed took a risk or acted with courage in the past. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a person's actions in a dangerous situation or their willingness to speak out against injustice.
