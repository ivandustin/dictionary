τομωτερος (tomōteros) is a comparative adjective in Greek, meaning "sharper" or "keener". It is derived from the root word τομός (tomos), which means "sharp" or "cutting". The suffix -ωτερος (-ōteros) is used to form comparative adjectives in Greek, similar to the -er suffix in English. 

Example sentence: Ο ξυραφάκος είναι τομώτερος από το μαχαίρι. (O xyrafakos einai tomōteros apo to machairi.) - The razor is sharper than the knife.
