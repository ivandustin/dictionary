Word: τοιουτων

Etymology: τοιουτων is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words, τοιοῦτος (toioutos) and οὗτος (houtos). τοιοῦτος means "such" or "of such a kind," while οὗτος means "this." Therefore, τοιουτων means "of such a kind as this."

Grammar: τοιουτων is a pronoun in the genitive plural form. It is used to refer to a group of things or people that are of the same kind or type as the one previously mentioned.

Description: τοιουτων is a word that is commonly used in Greek literature and language. It is often used to emphasize the similarity or sameness of a group of things or people. For example, in the sentence "Οἱ μαθηταὶ τοιούτων ἀνθρώπων εἰσίν," (The disciples are of such men), τοιουτων is used to describe the disciples as being of the same kind or type as the men previously mentioned.
