Word: τοις

Etymology: τοις is a Greek word that is the dative plural form of the definite article "ο" (ho). It is used to indicate that the noun it modifies is plural and definite.

Grammar: τοις is a definite article in Greek that is used before a noun to indicate that the noun is specific and known to both the speaker and the listener. It is in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: τοις is a common word in Greek that is used to indicate a specific group of people or things. It is often used in conjunction with a noun to provide more information about it. For example, "τοις ανθρώποις" (tois anthrōpois) means "to the people" or "for the people."
