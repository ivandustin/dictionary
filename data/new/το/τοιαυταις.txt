Word: τοιαυταις

Etymology: τοιαυταις is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words, τοιαῦτα (toiauta) meaning "such things" and the suffix -αις (-ais) indicating the feminine plural form.

Grammar: τοιαυταις is a feminine plural form of the Greek adjective τοιοῦτος (toioutos) which means "such" or "of this kind". It is used to describe or refer to a group of feminine nouns.

Description: τοιαυταις is used in Greek language to refer to a group of feminine nouns that are similar or of the same kind. It is often used in literature and poetry to create vivid descriptions and imagery.
