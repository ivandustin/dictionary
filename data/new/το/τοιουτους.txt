Word: τοιουτους

Etymology: τοιουτους is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words, τοιοῦτος (toioutos) and οὗτος (houtos). τοιοῦτος means "such" or "of such a kind" and οὗτος means "this". Therefore, τοιουτους means "such as these" or "of this kind".

Grammar: τοιουτους is a pronoun in Greek that is used to refer to a group of people or things that have been previously mentioned or are known to the speaker and the listener. It is in the masculine plural form and is declined as follows:

Nominative: τοιοῦτοι (toioutoi)
Genitive: τοιούτων (toiouton)
Dative: τοιούτοις (toioutois)
Accusative: τοιούτους (toioutous)

Description: τοιουτους is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to refer to a group of people or things that share similar characteristics or qualities. For example, in the sentence "τοιουτους ανθρώπους δεν μπορώ να αντέξω" (toioutous anthropous den borō na antexō), which means "I can't stand people like these", τοιουτους is used to refer to a group of people who the speaker finds intolerable.
