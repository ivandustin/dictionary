Word: τουτων

Etymology: τουτων is a Greek word that is derived from the demonstrative pronoun τοῦτος (this).

Grammar: τουτων is a genitive plural form of τοῦτος. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns.

Description: τουτων is used to refer to a group of things or people that are related or possessive of something. It can be translated to "of these" or "of this group." For example, in the sentence "τουτων οἱ πολλοί," it means "of these, the majority."
