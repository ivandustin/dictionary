Word: το

Etymology:
The word "το" is a neuter definite article in the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "τό" (to), which also means "the". 

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, "το" is used as a definite article to indicate a specific noun that is neuter in gender. It is used before a noun to indicate that the noun is definite and specific. For example, "το σπίτι" (to spiti) means "the house".

Description:
"Το" is one of the most commonly used words in the Greek language. It is used to indicate a specific noun that is neuter in gender. It is an essential part of Greek grammar and is used in everyday conversation and writing.
