Word: τοιουτον

Etymology: τοιουτον is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words, τοιοῦτος (such) and τοῦτο (this).

Grammar: τοιουτον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe a noun that is of the same kind or quality as something previously mentioned.

Description: τοιουτον is often translated as "such" or "like this" in English. It is commonly used in Greek literature and philosophy to refer to something that is similar or comparable to something else. For example, in Plato's Republic, Socrates uses τοιουτον to describe the ideal city-state, saying that it should be "such a city as we have described."
