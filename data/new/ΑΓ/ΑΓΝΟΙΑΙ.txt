Etymology: The word "αγνοια" (agnia) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the adjective "αγνός" (agnos), meaning "pure" or "innocent".

Grammar: "Αγνοια" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the first declension and has the plural form "αγνοίες" (agnies).

Description: In Greek, "αγνοια" refers to ignorance or lack of knowledge. It can also imply innocence or naivety. It is often used to describe a state of not knowing or being unaware of something.
