Etymology: The word "αγιωσύνη" (agiōsynē) comes from the Greek root words "άγιος" (ágios), meaning "holy" or "sacred," and "σύνη" (synē), meaning "union" or "togetherness." 

Grammar: "Αγιωσύνη" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. 

Description: "Αγιωσύνη" refers to the concept of holiness or sanctity. It is often used to describe the state of being holy or the quality of being sacred. In religious contexts, it can refer to the purity and righteousness associated with divine beings or objects. It can also be used more broadly to describe moral or ethical goodness.
