Etymology: The word "αγαλλιαθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "αγαλλιάομαι" (agalliaomai), which means "to exult" or "to rejoice". 

Grammar: "αγαλλιαθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "αγαλλιάομαι". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "αγαλλιαθηναι" is used to express the action of rejoicing or exulting in a passive sense. It implies a state of being filled with joy or happiness.
