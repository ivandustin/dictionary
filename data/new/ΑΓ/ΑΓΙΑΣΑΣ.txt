Etymology: The word "αγιασας" comes from the Greek verb "αγιάζω" (agiázo), which means "to sanctify" or "to make holy". It is derived from the adjective "άγιος" (ágios), meaning "holy" or "sacred".

Grammar: "Αγιασας" is the second person singular form of the verb "αγιάζω" (agiázo) in the past tense. It is used when addressing one person who performed the action of sanctifying or making something holy.

Description: "Αγιασας" is a word used in Greek to address someone directly and inform them that they have sanctified or made something holy. It is often used in religious contexts to acknowledge and praise someone for their actions in making something sacred or consecrated.
