Etymology: The word "αγαπητα" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "αγαπώ" (agapó), which means "to love" or "to cherish."

Grammar: "Αγαπητα" is the plural form of the adjective "αγαπητός" (agapitos), which means "beloved" or "dear." In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "αγαπητα" is used to describe plural nouns that are dear or beloved.

Description: "Αγαπητα" is an adjective used to express affection or endearment towards multiple people or objects. It is often used to address a group of individuals in a warm and affectionate manner.
