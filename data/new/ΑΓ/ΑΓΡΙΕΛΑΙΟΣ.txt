Etymology: The word "αγριελαίος" comes from the Greek words "αγριος" (agrios) meaning "wild" and "ελαιος" (elaio) meaning "olive". 

Grammar: "Αγριελαίος" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "αγριελαίοι".

Description: "Αγριελαίος" refers to a wild olive tree. It is a species of olive tree that grows in the wild and is not cultivated. The wild olive tree is known for its hardiness and ability to survive in harsh conditions. Its fruits are smaller and more bitter compared to the cultivated olive tree. The wood of the wild olive tree is also highly valued for its durability and unique grain pattern.
