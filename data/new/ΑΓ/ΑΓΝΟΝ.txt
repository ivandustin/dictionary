Etymology: The word "αγνον" (agnon) comes from the Greek adjective "αγνός" (agnos), which means "pure" or "chaste".

Grammar: "Αγνον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the singular number.

Description: "Αγνον" refers to something or someone that is pure, innocent, or untainted. It can be used to describe a person's character, actions, or intentions. In a broader sense, it can also refer to something that is clean, unadulterated, or free from impurities.
