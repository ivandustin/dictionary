Etymology: The word "αγραν" (agran) comes from the Greek word "αγράνα" (agrana), which means "wild" or "savage". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἄγραυλος" (agraulos), meaning "wild" or "uncultivated".

Grammar: "Αγραν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number.

Description: "Αγραν" is used to describe something or someone as wild, untamed, or uncultivated. It can refer to a person's behavior, character, or appearance. It is often used metaphorically to describe someone who is unruly, uncontrolled, or rebellious.
