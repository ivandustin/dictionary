Etymology: The word "αγαπώσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αγαπάω" (agapao), which means "to love". The suffix "-σιν" is added to the verb to indicate the third person plural present tense. 

Grammar: "αγαπώσιν" is a verb form in the Greek language. It is the third person plural present tense of the verb "αγαπάω". In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person. 

Description: "αγαπώσιν" is used to express the action of loving in the present tense by a group of people. It can be translated as "they love" or "they are loving".
