Grammar: "Αγιον" (agion) is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "αγιον" (agion), the genitive singular form "αγιου" (agiou), and the accusative singular form "αγιον" (agion).

Description: "Αγιον" (agion) refers to something that is holy or sacred. It can be used to describe a place, an object, or a person that is considered to be of great religious significance or purity.
