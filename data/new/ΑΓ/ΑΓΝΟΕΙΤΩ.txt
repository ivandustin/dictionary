Etymology: The word "αγνοείτω" (agnoeitō) comes from the Greek verb "αγνοώ" (agnoō), which means "to ignore" or "to be unaware of". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the verb "γινώσκω" (ginōskō), meaning "to know".

Grammar: "Αγνοείτω" is a verb in the present imperative form, third person singular. In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The present tense indicates that the action is happening at the present time.

Description: The word "αγνοείτω" is used to tell someone to ignore or disregard something. It is a command to not pay attention to or be unaware of a particular thing or situation.
