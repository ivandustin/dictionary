Etymology: The word "αγνων" (agnon) comes from the Greek adjective "αγνός" (agnos), which means "pure" or "chaste". 

Grammar: "Αγνων" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. 

Description: "Αγνων" is a term used in Greek mythology to describe a hero or a character who is pure, virtuous, and free from any moral or physical impurities. It is often associated with individuals who possess noble qualities and act with integrity.
