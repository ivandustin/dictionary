Etymology: The word "αγιωσύνης" (agiōsynēs) comes from the Greek root words "αγιος" (agios), meaning "holy" or "sacred," and "σύνη" (synē), meaning "union" or "communion." 

Grammar: "Αγιωσύνης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αγιωσύνης" refers to the concept of holiness or sanctity. It can be used to describe the state of being holy or sacred, as well as the quality or attribute of being holy. In religious contexts, it often refers to the divine or spiritual purity and righteousness.
