Etymology: The word "αγνοείν" (agnoein) comes from the Greek verb "αγνοώ" (agnoo), which means "to not know" or "to be ignorant". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the verb "γινώσκω" (ginosko), meaning "to know".

Grammar: "Αγνοείν" is the present tense infinitive form of the verb. It belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs, which means it follows a regular pattern of conjugation. In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows:

- Αγνοώ (agnoo) - I do not know
- Αγνοείς (agnoeis) - You do not know (singular)
- Αγνοεί (agnoei) - He/she/it does not know
- Αγνοούμε (agnooume) - We do not know
- Αγνοείτε (agnoeite) - You do not know (plural)
- Αγνοούν (agnooun) - They do not know

Description: "Αγνοείν" is a Greek verb that means "to not know" or "to be ignorant". It is used to express a lack of knowledge or awareness about something. It can be used in various contexts, such as when someone is unaware of a fact, does not understand a concept, or is ignorant about a particular subject.
