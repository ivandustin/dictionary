Etymology: The word "αγιοις" (pronounced "agios") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the adjective "ἅγιος" (hagios), which means "holy" or "sacred."

Grammar: In Greek, "αγιοις" is the dative plural form of the adjective "ἅγιος" (hagios). It is used to describe or refer to multiple holy or sacred things or beings.

Description: "Αγιοις" is commonly used in religious contexts to refer to saints or holy persons. It can also be used to describe sacred places, objects, or rituals.
