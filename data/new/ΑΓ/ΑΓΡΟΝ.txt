Etymology: The word "αγρον" (agron) comes from the Greek word "ἀγρός" (agros), meaning "field" or "land".

Grammar: In Greek, "αγρον" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it refers to a field or a piece of land.

Description: "Αγρον" is a term used in Greek to describe a specific area of land that is used for agricultural purposes. It can refer to a cultivated field, a farm, or any piece of land that is used for growing crops or raising livestock.
