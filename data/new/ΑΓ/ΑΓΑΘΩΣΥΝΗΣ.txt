Etymology: The word "αγαθωσύνης" (agathosynis) comes from the Greek words "αγαθός" (agathos), meaning "good," and "σύνη" (syni), meaning "disposition" or "nature." It refers to the quality or state of being good-natured or kind-hearted.

Grammar: "Αγαθωσύνης" is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this form, it means "of kindness" or "of good-naturedness."

Description: "Αγαθωσύνης" is a Greek word that represents the concept of kindness or good-naturedness. It refers to the quality of being benevolent, generous, and considerate towards others. It is often associated with acts of compassion, empathy, and goodwill.
