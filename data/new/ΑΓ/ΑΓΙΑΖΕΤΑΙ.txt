Etymology: The word "αγιαζεται" comes from the Greek verb "αγιάζω" (agiázo), which means "to sanctify" or "to consecrate". 

Grammar: "Αγιάζεται" is the third person singular form of the verb "αγιάζω" in the present tense, passive voice. It is used to describe the action of something being sanctified or consecrated.

Description: "Αγιάζεται" is a Greek word that refers to the act of making something holy or sacred. It is often used in religious contexts to describe the process of consecrating objects, places, or individuals.
