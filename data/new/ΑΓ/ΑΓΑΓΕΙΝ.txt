Etymology: The word "αγαγειν" (agagein) comes from the Greek verb "αγω" (ago), which means "to lead" or "to bring". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eǵ-.

Grammar: "αγαγειν" is the infinitive form of the verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is a regular verb. In the present tense, it conjugates as follows: αγαγώ, αγάγεις, αγάγει, αγαγούμε, αγάγετε, αγάγουν.

Description: "αγαγειν" means "to lead" or "to bring" in Greek. It is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts, such as leading someone or something physically, guiding or directing someone's actions, or bringing something to a certain place.
