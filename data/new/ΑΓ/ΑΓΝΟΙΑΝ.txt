Etymology: The word "αγνοιαν" (agnian) comes from the Greek word "αγνοια" (agnia), which means "ignorance" or "lack of knowledge". It is derived from the Greek word "αγνοεω" (agnoeo), meaning "to not know" or "to be unaware".

Grammar: "Αγνοιαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.
