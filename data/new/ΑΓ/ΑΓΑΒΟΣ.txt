Etymology: The word "αγαβος" (agavos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἀγαπητός" (agapētos), which means "beloved" or "dear."

Grammar: "Αγαβος" (agavos) is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has a singular and plural form. In the singular, it is declined as follows: ο αγαβος (o agavos) - the beloved, τον αγαβο (ton agavo) - the beloved (accusative case), του αγαβου (tou agavou) - of the beloved (genitive case), τον αγαβο (ton agavo) - to the beloved (dative case). In the plural, it is declined as follows: οι αγαβοι (oi agavoi) - the beloved (nominative case), τους αγαβους (tous agavous) - the beloved (accusative case), των αγαβων (ton agavon) - of the beloved (genitive case), τοις αγαβοις (tois agavois) - to the beloved (dative case).

Description: "Αγαβος" (agavos) is a term used to describe someone or something that is beloved, dear, or cherished. It conveys a sense of affection and endearment.
