Etymology: The word "αγιασαι" comes from the Greek verb "αγιάζω" (agiázo), which means "to sanctify" or "to make holy". It is derived from the adjective "άγιος" (ágios), meaning "holy" or "sacred".

Grammar: "Αγιάσαι" is the second person singular form of the verb "αγιάζω" in the present tense. It is used when addressing one person and means "you sanctify" or "you make holy".

Description: "Αγιάσαι" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of sanctifying or making something holy. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to the act of consecrating or purifying something or someone.
