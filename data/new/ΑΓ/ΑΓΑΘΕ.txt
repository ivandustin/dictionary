Etymology: The word "αγαθε" (agathe) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek adjective "ἀγαθός" (agathos), meaning "good" or "virtuous".

Grammar: "αγαθε" (agathe) is the vocative form of the adjective "ἀγαθός" (agathos). In Greek grammar, the vocative case is used to address or call someone or something.
