Etymology: The word "αγρος" (agros) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀγρός" (agros), meaning "field" or "land".

Grammar: In Greek, "αγρος" (agros) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and can be used as both the subject and the object of a sentence.

Description: "Αγρος" (agros) refers to a field or a piece of land, typically used for agricultural purposes. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a wide, open space or a rural area.
