Etymology: The word "αγκιστρον" comes from the Greek word "αγκίστρι" (ankístri), which means "hook" or "fishhook". 

Grammar: "Αγκιστρον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. 

Description: "Αγκιστρον" refers to a fishing hook, which is a curved piece of metal or other material with a sharp point at one end and a loop or barb at the other. It is used for catching fish by impaling them through the mouth or body. Fishing hooks come in various sizes and designs, depending on the type of fish being targeted and the fishing method employed.
