Grammar: "αγιασθητω" is the third person singular, aorist passive imperative form of the verb "αγιάζω" (agiázo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "αγιασθητω" is a command or instruction in Greek, specifically telling someone in the third person singular to be sanctified or made holy. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, urging individuals to purify themselves or dedicate themselves to a higher purpose.
