Etymology: The word "αγριον" (agrio) comes from the Greek adjective "αγριος" (agrios), meaning "wild" or "savage".

Grammar: In Greek, "αγριον" (agrio) is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases.

Description: "Αγριον" (agrio) refers to something that is wild or untamed. It can be used to describe animals, plants, or even people who exhibit wild or untamed behavior.
