Etymology: The word "αγοραί" (agorai) comes from the Greek word "αγορά" (agora), which means "marketplace" or "assembly". It is derived from the verb "αγοράζω" (agorazo), meaning "to buy" or "to purchase".

Grammar: "Αγοραί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form is used to refer to multiple marketplaces or assemblies.

Description: "Αγοραί" refers to marketplaces or assemblies in ancient Greece. In the context of ancient Greek city-states, the agora was a central public space where people gathered for various purposes, including commerce, socializing, and political discussions. It was an important hub of economic, social, and political activity.
