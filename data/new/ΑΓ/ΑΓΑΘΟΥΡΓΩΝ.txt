Etymology: The word "αγαθουργών" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the words "αγαθός" (agathos), meaning "good," and "έργο" (ergo), meaning "work" or "deed."

Grammar: "Αγαθουργών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: "Αγαθουργών" refers to someone who performs good deeds or acts of kindness. It is often used to describe a person who is benevolent, helpful, or charitable.
