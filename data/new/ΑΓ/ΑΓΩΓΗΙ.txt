Etymology: The word "αγωγή" (agogi) comes from the Greek verb "αγω" (ago), which means "to lead" or "to guide". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eǵ-.

Grammar: "Αγωγή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "αγωγής" (agogis). In the plural, it becomes "αγωγές" (agoges).

Description: "Αγωγή" is a Greek word that can be translated as "guidance" or "education". It refers to the act of leading or guiding someone, especially in a moral or educational sense. It can also refer to the process of educating or training individuals, as well as the methods and practices used in education.
