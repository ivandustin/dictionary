Etymology: The word "αγαθην" (agathin) comes from the Greek adjective "ἀγαθός" (agathos), which means "good" or "virtuous".

Grammar: "αγαθην" (agathin) is the accusative singular form of the adjective "ἀγαθός" (agathos). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "αγαθην" (agathin) is used to modify a feminine singular noun in the accusative case.

Description: "αγαθην" (agathin) is used to describe something or someone as good or virtuous. It can be used to express a positive quality or attribute.
