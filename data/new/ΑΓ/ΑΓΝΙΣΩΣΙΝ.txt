Etymology: The word "αγνισωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αγνίζω" (agnízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It is derived from the adjective "αγνός" (agnós), meaning "pure" or "clean."

Grammar: "Αγνισωσιν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case and is the plural form of the word "αγνισμός" (agnismós), which means "purification" or "cleansing."

Description: "Αγνισωσιν" refers to the act of purifying or cleansing. It can be used in various contexts, such as religious rituals, spiritual practices, or the removal of impurities from something.
