Etymology: The word "αγκυραν" (ankuran) comes from the Greek verb "αγκυρώνω" (ankorono), which means "to anchor". It is derived from the noun "άγκυρα" (ankura), meaning "anchor".

Grammar: "Αγκυραν" is the accusative form of the noun "άγκυρα" (ankura) in Greek. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Αγκυραν" refers to the anchor, a heavy object that is dropped from a boat or ship to the bottom of a body of water to prevent the vessel from drifting. It is an essential tool in maritime navigation and is used to secure a ship in place.
