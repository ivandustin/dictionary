Etymology: The word "αγαθοποιητε" comes from the Greek words "αγαθός" (agathos) meaning "good" and "ποιητής" (poiitis) meaning "maker" or "doer". 

Grammar: "αγαθοποιητε" is a verb in the imperative mood, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb "αγαθοποιώ" (agathopoio), which means "to do good" or "to perform acts of kindness".

Description: "αγαθοποιητε" is a command or request for a group of people to do good or perform acts of kindness. It is used to encourage or instruct others to engage in positive actions or behaviors.
