Etymology: The word "αγαθωσύνη" (agathosýni) comes from the Greek roots "αγαθός" (agathós) meaning "good" and "σύνη" (sýni) meaning "quality" or "state of being." 

Grammar: "Αγαθωσύνη" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. 

Description: "Αγαθωσύνη" refers to the virtue of goodness or kindness. It represents the quality of being morally upright, benevolent, and virtuous. It is often associated with acts of charity, generosity, and compassion towards others.
