Etymology: The word "αγενής" (agénis) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "γένος" (génos), meaning "race" or "kind." Therefore, "αγενής" (agénis) literally means "not of a specific race or kind."

Grammar: In Greek, "αγενής" (agénis) is an adjective that is used to describe someone or something that is without origin, race, or kind. It is declined according to gender, number, and case.
