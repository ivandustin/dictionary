Etymology: The word "αγανακτησιν" comes from the Greek word "αγανακτέω" (aganakteo), which means "to be indignant" or "to be angry".

Grammar: "Αγανακτησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender.

Description: "Αγανακτησιν" refers to the feeling of indignation or anger. It is used to describe a strong emotional response to a perceived injustice or unfairness.
