Etymology: The word "αγαπητού" comes from the Greek word "αγαπητός" (agapitos), which means "beloved" or "dear". It is derived from the verb "αγαπάω" (agapao), meaning "to love".

Grammar: "Αγαπητού" is the genitive form of the word "αγαπητός" (agapitos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. It is often used to show ownership or to express the idea of "of" or "belonging to".

Description: "Αγαπητού" is an adjective in Greek that is used to describe something or someone as beloved or dear. It is often used as a term of endearment or affectionate address.
