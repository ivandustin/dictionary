Etymology: The word "αγγελώ" (aggeló) comes from the Greek verb "αγγέλλω" (angéllo), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim". It is derived from the noun "άγγελος" (ángelos), meaning "messenger" or "angel".

Grammar: "Αγγελώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Αγγέλω (aggélo) - I announce
- Αγγέλεις (aggéleis) - You announce (singular)
- Αγγέλει (aggélei) - He/she/it announces
- Αγγελούμε (aggeloúme) - We announce
- Αγγελείτε (aggeléite) - You announce (plural)
- Αγγέλουν (aggéloun) - They announce

It primarily means "to announce" or "to proclaim", but it can also be used to express the act of reporting, communicating, or delivering a message.
