Etymology: The word "αγνος" (agnos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂egʷnos, meaning "pure" or "clean."

Grammar: In Greek, "αγνος" (agnos) is an adjective. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative) to agree with the noun it modifies. It can also be declined for number (singular and plural).

Description: The word "αγνος" (agnos) means "pure" or "clean" in Greek. It is often used to describe something that is free from impurities, contamination, or moral corruption. It can also refer to something that is innocent, chaste, or untouched.
