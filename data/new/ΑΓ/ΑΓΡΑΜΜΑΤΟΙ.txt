Etymology: The word "αγραμματοι" (agrámmatoi) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "without," and "γράμματα" (grámmata), meaning "letters." It literally translates to "illiterate" or "unlettered."

Grammar: "Αγραμματοι" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is used to refer to a group of people who are illiterate or lack formal education.

Description: "Αγραμματοι" is a term used to describe individuals who are unable to read or write. It can also be used more broadly to refer to people who lack knowledge or education in a particular field or subject.
