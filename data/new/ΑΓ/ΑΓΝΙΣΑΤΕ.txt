Etymology: The word "αγνισατε" comes from the Greek verb "αγνίζω" (agnízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It is derived from the adjective "αγνός" (agnós), meaning "pure" or "clean."

Grammar: "αγνισατε" is the imperative form of the verb "αγνίζω" in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. In this form, it is addressing multiple individuals and is translated as "purify" or "cleanse."

Description: "αγνισατε" is a Greek verb that is used to instruct or command a group of people to purify or cleanse something. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, where purification rituals or practices are performed to cleanse oneself or a place from impurities or negative energies.
