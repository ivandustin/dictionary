Etymology: The word "αγοντες" (agontes) comes from the Greek verb "αγω" (ago), which means "to lead" or "to bring". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eǵ- ("to lead, drive").

Grammar: "Αγοντες" (agontes) is the plural form of the noun "αγων" (agon), which means "contest" or "struggle" in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Αγοντες" (agontes) refers to a group of people who are engaged in a contest, competition, or struggle. It can be used to describe athletes competing in a sports event, participants in a battle or war, or individuals involved in any kind of competitive activity.
