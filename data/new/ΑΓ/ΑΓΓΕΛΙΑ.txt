Etymology: The word "αγγελία" (aggelia) comes from the Greek word "αγγέλλω" (angello), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ang- meaning "to bend" or "to curve".

Grammar: In Greek, "αγγελία" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular and can be used in both the nominative and accusative cases. The plural form is "αγγελίες" (aggelies).

Description: "Αγγελία" refers to an advertisement or a notice that is publicly displayed or published to inform or promote something. It can be found in various forms such as printed ads, online ads, or announcements in newspapers, magazines, or websites.
