Etymology: The word "αγνισμού" (agnismou) comes from the Greek verb "αγνίζω" (agnizo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It is derived from the adjective "αγνός" (agnos), meaning "pure" or "clean."

Grammar: "Αγνισμού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Αγνισμού" refers to the act or process of purifying or cleansing. It can be used in various contexts, such as religious rituals, spiritual practices, or even metaphorically to describe the purification of thoughts or emotions.
