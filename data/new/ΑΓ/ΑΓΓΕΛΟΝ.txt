Etymology: The word "αγγελον" (aggelon) comes from the Greek word "ἄγγελος" (angelos), which means "messenger" or "angel". 

Grammar: "Αγγελον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender. 

Description: "Αγγελον" refers to an angel or a messenger in Greek mythology and religion. In Christianity, angels are believed to be spiritual beings who act as messengers of God. They are often depicted as winged figures with divine powers and are associated with protection, guidance, and the delivery of important messages.
