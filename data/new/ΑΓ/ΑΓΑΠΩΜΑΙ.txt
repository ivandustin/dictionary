Etymology: The word "αγαπώμαι" (agapomai) comes from the Greek verb "αγαπάω" (agapao), which means "to love". The suffix "-μαι" (-mai) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the action is being performed on oneself. Therefore, "αγαπώμαι" can be translated as "I love myself".

Grammar: "αγαπώμαι" is a reflexive verb in the present tense, first person singular form. In Greek, reflexive verbs are used to express actions that someone does to themselves. The verb is conjugated as follows:
- Present tense: αγαπώμαι (agapomai) - I love myself
- Past tense: αγαπήθηκα (agapithika) - I loved myself
- Future tense: θα αγαπηθώ (tha agapitho) - I will love myself

Description: The word "αγαπώμαι" refers to the act of loving oneself. It implies self-acceptance, self-care, and self-appreciation. It is an important concept in promoting self-esteem and mental well-being.
