Etymology: The word "αγαπητήν" (agapitin) comes from the Greek verb "αγαπώ" (agapo), which means "to love". It is derived from the noun "αγάπη" (agapi), meaning "love".

Grammar: "Αγαπητήν" is the accusative form of the feminine adjective "αγαπητή" (agapiti), which means "beloved" or "dear". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "αγαπητήν" is used to modify a feminine noun in the accusative case.

Description: "Αγαπητήν" is used to address someone in a formal or affectionate manner. It is often used to address a female person, expressing love, affection, or respect.
