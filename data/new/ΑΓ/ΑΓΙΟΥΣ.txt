Etymology: The word "αγιος" (agios) comes from the Greek language and means "holy" or "saint". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eǵ- ("to be sacred").

Grammar: In Greek, "αγιος" (agios) is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe a person, place, or thing as holy or saintly.
