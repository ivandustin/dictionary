Etymology: The word "αγωνίζου" (agonízou) comes from the Greek verb "αγωνίζομαι" (agonízomai), which means "to struggle" or "to compete." It is derived from the noun "αγών" (agón), meaning "contest" or "competition."

Grammar: "Αγωνίζου" is the second person singular form of the present tense in Greek. It is used when addressing someone informally and means "you struggle" or "you compete."

Description: "Αγωνίζου" is a verb that expresses the action of struggling or competing. It can be used in various contexts, such as sports, personal challenges, or any situation where there is a competition or a difficult task.
