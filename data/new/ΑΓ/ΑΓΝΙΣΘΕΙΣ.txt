Etymology: The word "αγνισθείς" (agnistheis) comes from the Greek verb "αγνίζω" (agnizo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It is derived from the adjective "αγνός" (agnos), meaning "pure" or "clean."

Grammar: "Αγνισθείς" is the plural form of the verb "αγνίζω" in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It is used to describe multiple individuals or objects that have been purified or cleansed.

Description: "Αγνισθείς" is a Greek word that refers to a group of individuals or objects that have undergone a process of purification or cleansing. It implies that they have been made pure or clean from impurities or sins.
