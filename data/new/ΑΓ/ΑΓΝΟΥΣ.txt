Etymology: The word "αγνούς" (agnous) comes from the Greek adjective "ἁγνός" (hagnos), which means "pure" or "chaste".

Grammar: In Greek, "αγνούς" is the masculine form of the adjective, in the nominative case. It is used to describe a singular masculine noun.

Description: "Αγνούς" is an adjective in Greek that describes something or someone as pure, chaste, or innocent. It can be used to describe a person's character, actions, or intentions.
