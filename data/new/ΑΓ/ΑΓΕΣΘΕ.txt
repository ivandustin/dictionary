Etymology: The word "αγεσθε" (agesthe) comes from the Greek verb "αγω" (ago), which means "to lead" or "to bring". It is conjugated in the imperative form, addressing a group of people and instructing them to "go" or "proceed".

Grammar: "αγεσθε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "αγω" (ago). In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The second person plural form is used to address a group of people.

Description: "αγεσθε" is a command in Greek, instructing a group of people to go or proceed. It is commonly used in situations where someone wants to direct or lead a group to a specific location or action.
