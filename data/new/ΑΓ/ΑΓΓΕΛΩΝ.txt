Etymology: The word "αγγελών" (aggelon) comes from the Greek verb "αγγέλλω" (angello), which means "to announce" or "to bring news". It is derived from the noun "άγγελος" (angelos), meaning "messenger" or "angel".

Grammar: "Αγγελών" is the present participle form of the verb "αγγέλλω" (angello) in the masculine gender. It is used to describe someone who is currently announcing or bringing news.

Description: "Αγγελών" refers to a male messenger or announcer. In Greek mythology, angels were divine beings who served as messengers between the gods and humans. In modern usage, the term can also refer to a person who delivers news or messages.
