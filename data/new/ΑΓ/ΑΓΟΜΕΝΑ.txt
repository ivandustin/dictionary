Etymology: The word "αγορά" comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀγορά" (agorá), which referred to a public gathering place or marketplace.

Grammar: In Greek, "αγορά" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the first declension and has the plural form "αγορές" (agorés).

Description: "Αγορά" is a common Greek word that means "market" or "bazaar." It refers to a physical place where goods are bought and sold, or a general concept of commercial activity. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a place or situation where ideas or opinions are exchanged.
