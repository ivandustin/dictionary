Etymology: The word "αγαμοις" (agamois) comes from the Greek word "α" (a), meaning "without," and "γαμος" (gamos), meaning "marriage." 

Grammar: "Αγαμοις" is a noun in the plural form. It is a masculine noun in the nominative case.

Description: "Αγαμοις" refers to individuals who are unmarried or without a spouse. It can also be used to describe a group of people who have chosen to remain single or celibate.
