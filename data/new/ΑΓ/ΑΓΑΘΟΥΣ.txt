Etymology: The word "αγαθούς" (agathous) comes from the Greek adjective "ἀγαθός" (agathos), which means "good" or "virtuous".

Grammar: "Αγαθούς" is the masculine form of the adjective in the nominative case, singular number, and is used to describe a singular masculine noun.

Description: "Αγαθούς" is used to describe something or someone as good, virtuous, or morally upright. It can be used to describe a person's character, actions, or qualities.
