Etymology: The word "αγανακτών" comes from the Greek verb "αγανακτώ" (aganaktó), which means "to be indignant" or "to be angry". It is derived from the noun "αγανάκτηση" (aganáktisi), meaning "indignation" or "anger".

Grammar: "Αγανακτών" is the plural form of the present participle of the verb "αγανακτώ". In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to form the continuous aspect of verbs. It can also function as an adjective.

Description: "Αγανακτών" refers to a group of people who are indignant or angry about something. It can be used to describe individuals who feel a strong sense of dissatisfaction or frustration towards a particular situation or injustice.
