Etymology: The word "αγαπησατε" (agapisate) comes from the Greek verb "αγαπάω" (agapao), which means "to love". It is conjugated in the second person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood.

Grammar: "αγαπησατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "αγαπάω" (agapao) in the aorist tense, indicative mood. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they loved or have loved someone or something in the past.

Description: "αγαπησατε" is a verb form in Greek that expresses the action of loving in the past. It is commonly used in various contexts to talk about past experiences, feelings, or actions related to love.
