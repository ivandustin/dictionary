Etymology: The word "αγαθοεργείν" comes from the Greek roots "αγαθός" (agathos), meaning "good," and "εργεία" (ergeia), meaning "work" or "action." 

Grammar: "αγαθοεργείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive form. 

Description: "αγαθοεργείν" means "to do good" or "to perform good deeds" in Greek. It refers to the act of engaging in actions that are morally right, beneficial, or helpful to others.
