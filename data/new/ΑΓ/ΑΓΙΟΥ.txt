Etymology: The word "αγιού" (agiou) comes from the Greek word "άγιος" (agios), which means "holy" or "saint". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eǵ- ("to be sacred").

Grammar: "Αγιού" (agiou) is the genitive form of the noun "άγιος" (agios), which means "holy" or "saint" in Greek. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αγιού" (agiou) is a word used in Greek to indicate possession or origin. It is commonly used in phrases such as "του αγίου" (tou agiou), meaning "of the saint" or "belonging to the saint". It is often used in religious contexts to refer to holy objects, places, or individuals.
