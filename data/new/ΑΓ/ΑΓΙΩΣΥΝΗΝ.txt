Etymology: The word "αγιωσύνην" (agiōsynēn) comes from the Greek root words "αγιος" (agios), meaning "holy" or "sacred," and "σύνη" (synē), meaning "union" or "communion." 

Grammar: "Αγιωσύνην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Αγιωσύνην" refers to the concept of holiness or sanctity. It can be used to describe the state of being holy or sacred, as well as acts or qualities associated with holiness. In religious contexts, it often refers to the divine or spiritual purity and righteousness.
