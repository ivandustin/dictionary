Etymology: The word "αγελην" (agelen) comes from the Greek word "αγελη" (agelē), which means "herd" or "flock". It is derived from the verb "αγελαζω" (agelazō), meaning "to gather into a herd".

Grammar: "Αγελην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Αγελην" refers to a herd or a flock of animals, typically domesticated animals such as sheep, goats, or cattle. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people or things that are gathered or grouped together.
