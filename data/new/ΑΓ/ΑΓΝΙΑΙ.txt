Etymology: The word "αγνιαί" (agniaí) comes from the Greek adjective "αγνός" (agnós), which means "pure" or "clean".

Grammar: "Αγνιαί" is the plural form of the noun "αγνιά" (agniá), which is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: η αγνιά (i agniá) - the purity
- Genitive: της αγνιάς (tis agniás) - of the purity
- Accusative: την αγνιά (tin agniá) - the purity
- Vocative: αγνιά (agniá) - purity

Plural:
- Nominative: οι αγνιαί (i agniaí) - the purities
- Genitive: των αγνιών (ton agnión) - of the purities
- Accusative: τις αγνιαίς (tis agniaís) - the purities
- Vocative: αγνιαί (agniaí) - purities

Description: "Αγνιαί" refers to multiple instances of purity. It can be used to describe physical cleanliness, moral purity, or the absence of impurities.
