Etymology: The word "αγιωτατηι" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the adjective "αγιος" (agios), meaning "holy" or "sacred," and the superlative suffix "-τατηι" (-tatēi), which indicates the highest degree of a quality.

Grammar: "Αγιωτατηι" is a feminine adjective in the nominative singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The superlative form indicates that the noun it describes possesses the highest degree of the quality expressed by the adjective.

Description: "Αγιωτατηι" is used to describe something as the "most holy" or "most sacred." It emphasizes the exceptional level of holiness or sacredness attributed to a person, place, object, or concept.
