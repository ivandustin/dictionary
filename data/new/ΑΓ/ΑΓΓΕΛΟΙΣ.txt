Etymology: The word "αγγελος" (angelos) comes from the Greek word ἄγγελος (angelos), which means "messenger" or "one who announces". It is derived from the verb ἀγγέλλω (angello), meaning "to announce" or "to bring news".

Grammar: In Greek, "αγγελος" (angelos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, numbers, and genders to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Αγγελος" (angelos) refers to an angel in Greek mythology and religious traditions. Angels are supernatural beings believed to act as messengers or intermediaries between the divine and human realms. They are often depicted as celestial beings with wings and are associated with various roles and attributes in different religious contexts.
