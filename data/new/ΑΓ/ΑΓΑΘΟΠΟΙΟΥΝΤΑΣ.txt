Etymology: The word "αγαθοποιούντας" comes from the Greek root words "αγαθός" (agathos) meaning "good" and "ποιώ" (poio) meaning "to do/make." The suffix "-ούντας" (-ountas) is added to form the present participle, indicating the action of doing/making good.

Grammar: "Αγαθοποιούντας" is a verb in the present participle form in Greek. It is used to describe the ongoing action of doing/making good.

Description: "Αγαθοποιούντας" refers to the act of doing good or performing acts of kindness. It implies engaging in actions that benefit others or contribute to the well-being of society.
