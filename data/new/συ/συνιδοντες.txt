Word: συνιδοντες

Etymology:
συνιδοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb συνοιδοντες (sunoidontes), which is a compound of the prefix συν- (sun-), meaning "together," and the verb οιδα (oida), meaning "to know." Therefore, συνιδοντες means "knowing together" or "having a shared knowledge."

Grammar:
συνιδοντες is the present participle of the verb συνοιδοντες (sunoidontes), which means that it is a verb form that functions as an adjective. In Greek, the present participle is formed by adding the suffix -ντ- (-nt-) to the stem of the verb. The ending -ες (-es) indicates that the participle is masculine, plural, and nominative.

Description:
συνιδοντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Hebrews (10:34), where it is translated as "having compassion on me in my chains, and joyfully accepting the plundering of your goods." The word implies a sense of shared knowledge or understanding among a group of people, and in this context, it suggests that the readers of the letter to the Hebrews were aware of the author's situation and were empathetic towards him.
