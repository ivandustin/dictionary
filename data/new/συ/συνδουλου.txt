The word "συνδουλου" (syndoulou) is a Greek word that is composed of two parts: "συν" (syn) meaning "together" and "δοῦλος" (doulos) meaning "slave". Therefore, "συνδουλου" means "fellow slave" or "co-slave". 

In terms of grammar, "συνδουλου" is a noun in the genitive case, which is often used to indicate possession or relationship. 

Overall, "συνδουλου" is a word that describes a shared experience of enslavement or servitude.
