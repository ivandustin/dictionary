Word: συροντες

Etymology:
The word συροντες is derived from the Greek verb σύρω (sýro), which means "to drag" or "to pull". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the action of dragging or pulling is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar:
συροντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe the subject of a sentence as currently engaged in the action of dragging or pulling.

Description:
συροντες can be translated to English as "dragging" or "pulling". It is often used in the context of physical movement, such as dragging a heavy object or pulling a cart. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone is being forced or compelled to do something against their will.
