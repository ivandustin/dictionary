Word: συμβαινοντος

Etymology:
The word συμβαινοντος comes from the Greek verb συμβαίνω (sumbaínō), which means "to happen" or "to occur". The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb βαίνω (baínō), meaning "to go".

Grammar:
συμβαινοντος is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb συμβαίνω. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action.

Description:
συμβαινοντος can be translated to mean "of happening" or "of occurring". It is often used in Greek literature to describe events or situations that are currently taking place. The word can also be used to describe a person who is present at a particular event or situation.
