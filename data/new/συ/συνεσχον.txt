Word: συνεσχον

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with"
εσχον (eschon) meaning "I held" or "I took"

Therefore, συνεσχον (syneschon) means "they held together" or "they took together".

Grammar:
συνεσχον is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb συνέχω (synecho), which means "to hold together" or "to contain".

Description:
συνεσχον is a verb that describes an action of holding or taking something together. It is used in the past tense to indicate that the action has already happened. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action.
