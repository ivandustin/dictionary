Word: συνεκλείσαν (synekleisan)

Etymology: συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and εκλείω (ekleio) meaning "to shut in" or "to enclose". Therefore, συνεκλείσαν (synekleisan) means "they shut in together" or "they enclosed together".

Grammar: συνεκλείσαν (synekleisan) is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb εκλείω (ekleio) which means "to shut in" or "to enclose". The prefix συν- (syn-) adds the meaning of "together" to the verb.

Description: συνεκλείσαν (synekleisan) is a Greek verb that describes the action of shutting in or enclosing something together. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the closing of a door or the enclosing of a space. The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.
