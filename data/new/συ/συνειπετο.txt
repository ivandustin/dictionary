Word: συνειπετο

Etymology:
The word συνειπετο is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn) meaning "together" and εἶπον (eipon) meaning "to say". Therefore, the literal meaning of συνειπετο is "to say together".

Grammar:
συνειπετο is a third-person singular past tense verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the conjugation of the verb εἶπον (eipon), which is an irregular verb in Greek.

Description:
The word συνειπετο is used to describe an action that was said or spoken together with someone else. It can also be used to describe an action that was said in agreement with someone else. The word is often used in a narrative context to describe a conversation or dialogue between two or more people.
