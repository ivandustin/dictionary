The word συνευδοκων is a Greek verb that means "to agree with, to consent to, to approve of." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix συν- (meaning "together with") and the verb ευδοκω (meaning "to be well pleased, to approve of").

Grammar: συνευδοκων is the present participle form of the verb συνευδοκεω (syneudokeo), which means that it describes an ongoing action or state of being. It is in the masculine, singular, nominative case.

Description: The word συνευδοκων is often used in a religious context, particularly in the New Testament of the Bible, to describe the agreement or approval of God or Jesus Christ. It can also be used more generally to describe agreement or approval between individuals or groups.
