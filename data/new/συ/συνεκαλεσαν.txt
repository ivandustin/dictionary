Word: συνεκάλεσαν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
καλέω (kaleo) means "to call"

Therefore, συνεκάλεσαν (synekalesan) means "they called together" or "they summoned together."

Grammar:
συνεκάλεσαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
συνεκάλεσαν is a word in the Greek language that describes an action of calling or summoning people together. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a political or social gathering, a religious ceremony, or a family meeting. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels and the Acts of the Apostles.
