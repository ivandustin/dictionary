Word: συναγαγετε

Etymology:
The word συναγαγετε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather together." The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
συναγαγετε is a second-person plural aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to perform a specific action in the past. The verb is composed of the prefix συν- (syn-), which means "together," and the verb stem αγαγ- (agag-), which means "to lead" or "to bring."

Description:
The word συναγαγετε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 18:20, where Jesus says, "For where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I in the midst of them." The verb is used to emphasize the importance of gathering together in the name of Jesus. The command to gather together is not just a suggestion, but a vital part of Christian fellowship and worship.
