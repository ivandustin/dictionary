Word: συνεζωοποιησεν

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + ζωοποιέω (zōopoieō, meaning "to give life to") + -σεν (-sen, a past tense ending). Therefore, the word συνεζωοποιησεν means "he/she/it gave life together with" or "he/she/it revived together with".

Grammar: συνεζωοποιησεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is composed of the prefix συν- (syn-, meaning "together"), the verb ζωοποιέω (zōopoieō, meaning "to give life to"), and the past tense ending -σεν (-sen).

Description: συνεζωοποιησεν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (27:52-53). It describes the resurrection of some saints who had died and were buried, but who came back to life after Jesus' crucifixion. The word emphasizes the idea that these people were brought back to life together with Jesus, and that their resurrection was a result of his sacrifice.
