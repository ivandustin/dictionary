Word: συνοντων

Etymology:
The word συνοντων is derived from the Greek words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and ὀντός (ontos) meaning "being" or "existing". The word is a present participle of the verb συνοντάω (synontao), which means "to be present together".

Grammar:
συνοντων is a present participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ων (-on) to the present stem of the verb. The genitive plural form indicates that the word is describing a group of people or things that are present together.

Description:
The word συνοντων describes a group of people or things that are present together. It can be translated as "those who are present together" or "those who exist together". The word is often used in a religious context to describe a group of people who are gathered together for worship or prayer.
