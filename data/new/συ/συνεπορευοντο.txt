Word: συνεπορευοντο

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
ἐπορεύοντο (eporeuonto) means "they were going"

Therefore, συνεπορευοντο (syneporeuonto) means "they were going together" or "they were traveling together."

Grammar:
συνεπορευοντο is a verb in the imperfect tense, third person plural form. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description:
συνεπορευοντο is a Greek verb that describes a group of people traveling together. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a group of people walking together on a journey, or in a figurative sense, such as a group of people working together towards a common goal. The verb is in the imperfect tense, indicating that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past.
