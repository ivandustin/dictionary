The word "συνκαθισαντων" is a Greek verb that is derived from two words: "συν" meaning "together" and "καθίζω" meaning "to sit". The verb is in the aorist tense and active voice, indicating that the action was completed in the past and the subject performed the action.

In terms of grammar, "συνκαθισαντων" is a third-person plural form of the verb, which means that it refers to a group of people who sat together. The word can be translated to "they sat together" or "they were sitting together".

Overall, "συνκαθισαντων" is a compound word that describes an action of sitting together in the past.
