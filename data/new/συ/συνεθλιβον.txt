Word: συνεθλιβον

Etymology:
The word συνεθλιβον is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix συν- (meaning "together") and the verb θλίβω (meaning "to press, squeeze, or oppress"). The prefix συν- indicates that the action of the verb is done together with someone or something else.

Grammar:
συνεθλιβον is the third person plural, imperfect tense, active voice form of the verb συνθλίβω (synthlibo). The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
The word συνεθλιβον describes an action of pressing or squeezing together with someone or something else. It can be used in a literal sense, such as pressing grapes together to make wine, or in a figurative sense, such as feeling oppressed or overwhelmed by a situation. The imperfect tense suggests that this action was ongoing or repeated in the past.
