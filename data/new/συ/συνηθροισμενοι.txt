Word: συνηθροισμένοι

Etymology:
The word συνηθροισμένοι is derived from the Greek root words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ηθροίζω (ethroizo), meaning "to gather." The suffix -μένοι (-menoi) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the word describes a group of people who have been gathered together.

Grammar:
συνηθροισμένοι is a plural masculine passive participle in the nominative case. It is used to describe a group of people who have been gathered together. The word can be translated to mean "gathered together" or "assembled."

Description:
συνηθροισμένοι is a word that describes a group of people who have come together for a specific purpose or event. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of protesters who have gathered in a public space, or a group of friends who have assembled for a party. The word emphasizes the idea of people coming together as a collective, rather than as individuals.
