Word: συνεχεον

Etymology:
The word συνεχεον is derived from the Greek verb συνέχω (synechō), which means "to hold together" or "to unite". The suffix -εον (-eon) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing an ongoing action.

Grammar:
συνεχεον is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is the present participle form of the verb συνέχω (synechō), which means "to hold together" or "to unite". As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action of holding together or uniting.

Description:
συνεχεον can be translated as "holding together" or "uniting". It describes an ongoing action of bringing things together and keeping them connected. This word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the physical properties of a material that holds its shape, or the social bonds that connect people together.
