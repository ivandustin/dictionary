Word: συναντησας

Etymology:
The word συναντησας is derived from the Greek verb συναντάω (synantáo), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". The verb is composed of two parts: συν- (syn-), which means "together" or "with", and αντάω (antáo), which means "to meet" or "to encounter".

Grammar:
συναντησας is the second person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb συναντάω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
συναντησας is a Greek verb that means "you met" or "you encountered". It is used to describe a past event in which the subject met or encountered someone or something. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal conversations, in literature, or in historical accounts.
