Word: συνειχετο

Etymology:
The word συνειχετο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word συνέχω (synechō), which means "to hold together" or "to compress." The verb is in the middle/passive voice and is derived from the present tense stem συνεχ- (synech-).

Grammar:
συνειχετο is a third-person singular, imperfect, middle/passive indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. The imperfect tense indicates that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past.

Description:
The word συνειχετο can be translated as "was being held together" or "was being compressed." It describes an ongoing or repeated action in the past where something or someone was being held together or compressed. The context of the sentence would determine the specific meaning and usage of the word.
