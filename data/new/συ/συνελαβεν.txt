Word: συνελαβεν

Etymology:
συνελαβεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb συλλαμβάνω (syllambanō), which means "to take hold of, seize, arrest". The prefix συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with", so συνελαβεν can be translated as "he/she/it took hold of together" or "he/she/it arrested together".

Grammar:
συνελαβεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb συλλαμβάνω (syllambanō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, voice, and mood. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a whole, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

συνελαβεν is a word that describes an action of taking hold of or arresting someone or something. It is a past tense form, so it refers to an action that has already happened. The context in which this word is used can provide more information about who or what was arrested and why.
