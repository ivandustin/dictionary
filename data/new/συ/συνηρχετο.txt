Word: συνηρχετο

Etymology:
The word συνηρχετο is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix συν- (meaning "together") and the verb ἔρχομαι (meaning "to come"). Therefore, the literal meaning of συνηρχετο is "to come together".

Grammar:
συνηρχετο is a third-person singular imperfect middle/passive indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It belongs to the verb group ἔρχομαι, which is a deponent verb, meaning that it has a passive form but an active meaning. The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description:
The verb συνηρχετο is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Mark 1:33, where it describes the people who came together to see Jesus. The verb implies a sense of unity and gathering, as people came together for a common purpose. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe any group of people coming together.
