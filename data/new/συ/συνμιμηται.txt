Word: συνμιμηται

Etymology:
The word συνμιμηται is derived from the Greek words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and μιμητής (mimētēs) meaning "imitator". 

Grammar:
συνμιμηται is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word συνμιμηται is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description:
The word συνμιμηται refers to "imitators who act together". It can be used to describe a group of people who imitate or mimic each other's actions or behavior. The word can also be used in a broader sense to refer to people who work together to achieve a common goal or purpose.
