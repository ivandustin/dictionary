Word: συνιωσιν

Etymology:
The word συνιωσιν is derived from the Greek verb συνίημι (syníēmi), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend." The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a third-person plural ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to a group of people.

Grammar:
συνιωσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative form of the verb συνίημι. In Greek, the present tense is used to describe actions that are currently happening or are habitual. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
συνιωσιν can be translated as "they understand" or "they comprehend." It is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the understanding or comprehension of spiritual truths. The word emphasizes not just intellectual understanding, but also a deep, intuitive grasp of the meaning and significance of something.
