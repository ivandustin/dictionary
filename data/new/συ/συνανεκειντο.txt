Word: συνανεκειντο

Etymology:
The word συνανεκειντο is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (meaning "together") and the verb ἀνακεῖντο (meaning "to recline"). It is derived from the ancient Greek language.

Grammar:
συνανεκειντο is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is a compound verb, as mentioned earlier, and is formed by adding the prefix συν- to the verb ἀνακεῖντο. The verb ἀνακεῖντο is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description:
The word συνανεκειντο is used in ancient Greek literature to describe a group of people reclining together at a meal or banquet. It is often used in the context of describing the seating arrangements at such events. The word is also used metaphorically to describe a group of people who are closely associated or united in some way.
