Word: συναθλουντες

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together"
αθλεω (athleo) means "to contend, to struggle"
-ουντες (-ountes) is a present participle suffix indicating ongoing action

Therefore, συναθλουντες (synathlountes) means "those who are contending together" or "those who are struggling together."

Grammar:
συναθλουντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number.

Description:
συναθλουντες is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Philippians 1:27. It is used to describe the unity and cooperation that should exist among believers in Christ as they strive to live out their faith. The word emphasizes the idea of working together towards a common goal, much like athletes who compete together as a team.
