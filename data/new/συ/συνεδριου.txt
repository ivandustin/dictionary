Word: συνεδριου

Etymology: The word συνεδριου (synedriou) comes from the Greek root words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ἐδρα (edra), meaning "seat" or "assembly." Together, they form the word συνέδριον (synedrion), which means "a sitting together" or "a council."

Grammar: συνεδριου is a genitive singular form of the noun συνέδριον (synedrion). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, συνεδριου indicates that something belongs to or is related to a council or assembly.

Description: συνεδριου refers to a council or assembly, typically used in a political or religious context. It can also refer to a group of people who come together to discuss and make decisions on a particular issue. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the Sanhedrin, the Jewish council that tried Jesus.
