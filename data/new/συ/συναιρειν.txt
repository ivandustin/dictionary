Word: συναιρειν

Etymology:
The word συναιρειν comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and αἱρέω (haireo), meaning "to take, choose." Therefore, the literal meaning of συναιρειν is "to take together."

Grammar:
συναιρειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present active infinitive form, which means that it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. Instead, it is used to express the action of taking something together in a general sense.

Description:
The word συναιρειν is often used in a figurative sense to mean "to infer" or "to deduce." It can also be used to describe the act of combining or adding things together. In philosophy, it is used to describe the process of drawing conclusions from a set of premises. Overall, συναιρειν is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe the act of taking things together or drawing conclusions.
