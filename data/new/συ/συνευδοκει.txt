Word: συνευδοκει

Etymology:
The word συνευδοκει is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (meaning "together" or "with") and the verb ευδοκεω (meaning "to be well pleased" or "to approve"). Therefore, the literal meaning of συνευδοκει is "together well pleased" or "together approve".

Grammar:
συνευδοκει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb ευδοκεω, which is a compound verb consisting of the prefix ευ- (meaning "good" or "well") and the verb δοκεω (meaning "to seem" or "to appear"). The verb ευδοκεω is conjugated in the present tense as follows: ευδοκω, ευδοκεις, ευδοκει, ευδοκουμεν, ευδοκειτε, ευδοκουσι(ν).

Description:
The word συνευδοκει is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 1:32, where it is translated as "they not only do them but give approval to those who practice them" (ESV). The word conveys the idea of not only participating in sinful behavior but also approving of it and encouraging others to do the same.
