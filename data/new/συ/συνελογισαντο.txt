Word: συνελογισαντο

Etymology:
The word συνελογισαντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and λογίζομαι (logizomai), meaning "to reason, to calculate." Therefore, the word συνελογισαντο means "they reasoned together" or "they calculated together."

Grammar:
συνελογισαντο is a third-person plural aorist middle indicative verb in ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
συνελογισαντο is a verb that describes an action of reasoning or calculating together. It implies a collaborative effort to come to a conclusion or make a decision. The word is often used in a philosophical or intellectual context, where people are working together to solve a problem or understand a concept.
