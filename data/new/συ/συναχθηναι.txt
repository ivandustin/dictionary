Word: συναχθηναι

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together"
ἄγω (agō) meaning "to lead, bring"

συναχθηναι (synachthēnai) is a Greek verb in the aorist passive infinitive form, which means "to be gathered together" or "to be assembled together". It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and the verb ἄγω (agō) meaning "to lead, bring".

Grammar:
συναχθηναι is a verb in the aorist passive infinitive form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
συναχθηναι is a verb that describes the act of being gathered or assembled together. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people coming together for a meeting or a collection of objects being brought together in one place. The passive voice of the verb suggests that the gathering is happening to the subject, rather than being initiated by them.
