Word: συνεχομενους

Etymology:
The word συνεχομενους is a present participle form of the Greek verb συνέχω (synechō), which means "to hold together" or "to bind together". The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb ἔχω (echō), meaning "to hold".

Grammar:
συνεχομενους is a present participle form of the verb συνέχω (synechō) in the masculine accusative plural. In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action or state. The masculine accusative plural form indicates that the participle is referring to a group of people who are being held together or bound together.

Description:
The word συνεχομενους can be translated as "being held together" or "being bound together". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a group of people who are united or connected in some way. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the unity of believers in Christ, as in Ephesians 4:16: "From whom the whole body, joined and held together by every joint with which it is equipped, when each part is working properly, makes the body grow so that it builds itself up in love."
