Word: συγγενειας

Etymology:
The word συγγενειας comes from the Greek root words συν (syn) meaning "together" and γένος (genos) meaning "race" or "family". Together, they form the word συγγένεια (syngeneia) which means "kinship" or "relationship".

Grammar:
συγγενειας is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of kinship" or "of relationship".

Description:
συγγενειας refers to the concept of kinship or relationship between individuals or groups. It can refer to blood relations, such as family members, or to more distant relationships, such as those between members of the same community or social group. The word is often used in a broader sense to describe the connections and relationships between different cultures, nations, or ethnic groups.
