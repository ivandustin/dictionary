Word: συμμορφους

Etymology:
The word συμμορφους is derived from the Greek word συμμορφός (summorphos), which means "conforming to, in harmony with". It is a combination of the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and μορφή (morphe), meaning "form" or "shape".

Grammar:
συμμορφους is an adjective in the masculine, plural form. It is used to describe something or someone that is in conformity with or in harmony with something else.

Description:
The word συμμορφους is often used in a religious context to describe someone who is conforming to the teachings or principles of a particular faith. It can also be used more generally to describe something that is in harmony with something else, such as a piece of music that is in conformity with a particular style or genre.
