The word "συνκαλουσιν" (synkalousin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the root word "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon," and the prefix "συν" (syn), which means "together" or "with." 

In terms of grammar, "συνκαλουσιν" is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of the verb "καλέω." It is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. The third person plural form suggests that the subject of the verb is a group of people.

In terms of meaning, "συνκαλουσιν" can be translated as "they are calling together" or "they are summoning together." It implies that a group of people are gathering or assembling for a specific purpose. 

Overall, "συνκαλουσιν" is a Greek verb that describes the action of a group of people coming together for a specific purpose.
