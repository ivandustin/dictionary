Word: συνεχαιρον

Etymology:
The word συνεχαιρον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn), meaning "together," and χαιρω (chairō), meaning "to rejoice." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνεχαιρον is "to rejoice together."

Grammar:
συνεχαιρον is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person plural form of the verb συνχαιρω (synchairō), which means "to rejoice together." 

Description:
The word συνεχαιρον is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 12:15, where it is translated as "rejoice with those who rejoice." It conveys the idea of sharing in someone else's joy and celebrating with them. It is a call to empathy and compassion, encouraging people to be happy for others and to share in their happiness.
