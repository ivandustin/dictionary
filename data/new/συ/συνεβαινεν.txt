Word: συνεβαινεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with"
ἔβαινεν (ébainen) meaning "he/she/it was going"

Therefore, συνεβαινεν (synebainen) means "he/she/it was going together/with".

Grammar:
συνεβαινεν is a verb in the imperfect tense, third person singular form. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description:
συνεβαινεν is a Greek verb that is commonly used in the New Testament. It is often translated as "was happening" or "was occurring". It is used to describe events that were taking place at a particular time in the past.
