Word: συνχαιρετε

Etymology:
The word συνχαιρετε is derived from the Greek words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and χαίρω (chairō) meaning "to rejoice" or "to be glad". Therefore, the literal meaning of συνχαιρετε is "rejoice together" or "be glad together".

Grammar:
συνχαιρετε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form in Greek. It is often used as a greeting or farewell, similar to the English phrase "hello" or "goodbye". 

Description:
συνχαιρετε is a common Greek expression used to greet someone or bid them farewell. It is often used in formal settings, such as in business or academic settings, but can also be used in informal situations. The word conveys a sense of joy and happiness, as it literally means "rejoice together". It is a polite and friendly way to acknowledge someone's presence or departure.
