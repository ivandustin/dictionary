Word: συναρπασαντες

Etymology:
The word συναρπασαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αρπαζω (arpazo), which means "to snatch away" or "to seize". The prefix συν (syn) means "together" or "with", so συναρπασαντες can be translated as "having snatched away together" or "having seized together".

Grammar:
συναρπασαντες is the aorist participle form of the verb αρπαζω (arpazo) in the active voice. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The participle form is used to modify a noun or pronoun, and in this case, it is used to describe the subject of the sentence.

Description:
συναρπασαντες is a word that describes a group of people who have snatched or seized something together. The context in which this word is used will determine what exactly has been snatched or seized. It could refer to a group of thieves who have stolen something, or a group of soldiers who have captured an enemy. The word conveys a sense of forceful action and cooperation among the group.
