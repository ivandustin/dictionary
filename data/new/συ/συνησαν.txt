Word: συνησαν

Etymology:
συνησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word συν- (syn-), which means "together" or "with," and the verb ἥσσω (hēssō), which means "to be inferior" or "to be weaker." The prefix συν- indicates that the action of the verb is done together with someone or something else.

Grammar:
συνησαν is a third-person plural aorist indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb happened in the past, and the indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
συνησαν means "they were inferior together" or "they were weaker together." It is a verb that describes a joint or collective weakness or inferiority. The context in which this verb is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action.
