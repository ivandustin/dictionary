Word: συνηχθησαν

Etymology:
The word συνηχθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather together." The prefix συν- (syn-) means "together," and the verb άγω (agō) means "to lead" or "to bring." Therefore, συνηχθησαν means "they were gathered together" or "they were brought together."

Grammar:
συνηχθησαν is a third-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
συνηχθησαν is a verb that describes the action of a group of people being gathered or brought together. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people coming together for a meeting, a group of soldiers being assembled for battle, or a group of animals being herded together. The word has a sense of unity and cooperation, as it implies that the individuals involved are working together towards a common goal.
