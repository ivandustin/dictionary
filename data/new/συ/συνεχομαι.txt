Word: συνεχομαι

Etymology:
The word συνεχομαι comes from the Greek root words συν (syn) meaning "together" and εχω (echo) meaning "to hold". Therefore, the word συνεχομαι means "to be held together" or "to be united".

Grammar:
συνεχομαι is a verb in the present tense, middle or passive voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Συνέχομαι (I am held together)
- Συνέχεσαι (You are held together)
- Συνέχεται (He/She/It is held together)

Plural:
- Συνεχόμαστε (We are held together)
- Συνέχεστε (You are held together)
- Συνέχονται (They are held together)

Description:
The word συνεχομαι is often used to describe a state of being united or held together. It can refer to physical objects that are joined together, as well as abstract concepts such as ideas or emotions that are closely connected. In addition, συνεχομαι can also be used to describe a person who is emotionally or mentally stable, as if they are being held together by their own strength.
