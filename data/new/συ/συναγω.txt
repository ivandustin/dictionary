Word: συναγω (synagō)

Etymology:
The word συναγω (synagō) is derived from the Greek root words συν (syn) meaning "together" and αγω (agō) meaning "to lead". The word originally referred to a gathering or assembly of people, and later came to specifically refer to a Jewish place of worship.

Grammar:
συναγω (synagō) is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular:
  - 1st person: συνάγω (synagō)
  - 2nd person: συνάγεις (synageis)
  - 3rd person: συνάγει (synagei)

- Plural:
  - 1st person: συνάγομεν (synagomen)
  - 2nd person: συνάγετε (synagete)
  - 3rd person: συνάγουσι(ν) (synagousi(n))

Description:
συναγω (synagō) is a Greek word that refers to a gathering or assembly of people, and specifically to a Jewish place of worship. The word is used in the New Testament to refer to the Jewish synagogues where Jesus and his disciples preached and taught. The word is also used in modern Greek to refer to a synagogue or Jewish community center.
