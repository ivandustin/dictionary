Word: συναγουσιν

Etymology:
The word συναγουσιν (synagousin) is a present participle form of the Greek verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather together" or "to assemble." The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb ἄγω (agō), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar:
συναγουσιν is a present participle form of the verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "they are gathering together" or "they are assembling." The word is in the third person plural, present tense, active voice, and indicative mood.

Description:
συναγουσιν is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (12:30) and the Gospel of Mark (3:27). In both instances, the word is used to describe a group of people who are gathering together or assembling. In the context of these passages, the word refers to those who are aligned against Jesus and his teachings. The word is also related to the term "synagogue," which is a place of worship and assembly for Jewish people.
