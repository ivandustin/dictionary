Word: συνεκεράσεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with"
ἐκεράσθη (ekerasthe) meaning "was mixed" or "was blended"

Therefore, συνεκεράσεν (synekerasen) means "he/she/it mixed together" or "he/she/it blended together."

Grammar:
συνεκεράσεν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb κεράννυμι (kerannymi), which means "to mix" or "to blend."

Description:
συνεκεράσεν is a Greek word that describes the act of mixing or blending together. It is often used in the context of cooking or preparing food, but can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of combining different elements or substances. The word is commonly found in ancient Greek texts, particularly in the works of Aristotle and other philosophers.
