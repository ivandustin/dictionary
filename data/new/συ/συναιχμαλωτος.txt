Word: συναιχμαλωτος

Etymology: The word συναιχμαλωτος comes from the Greek words σύν (syn), meaning "together," αἰχμάλωτος (aichmálotos), meaning "prisoner of war," and the suffix -ος (-os), which is used to form adjectives. Therefore, the word συναιχμαλωτος means "fellow prisoner of war" or "co-captive."

Grammar: συναιχμαλωτος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined in different cases, genders, and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: The word συναιχμαλωτος refers to someone who is a prisoner of war along with others. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is in a difficult situation with others, such as being trapped in a bad relationship or a difficult project.
