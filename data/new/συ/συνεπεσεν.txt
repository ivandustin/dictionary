Word: συνεπεσεν

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + ἔπεσεν (épesen, meaning "fell"). The word is derived from the Greek verb συμπίπτω (sympípto), which means "to fall together" or "to coincide".

Grammar: συνεπεσεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the third person singular form, meaning "he/she/it fell together" or "he/she/it coincided".

Description: συνεπεσεν is a Greek word that describes the act of falling together or coinciding. It can be used in a literal sense, such as two objects falling together, or in a figurative sense, such as two events happening at the same time. The word is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the idea of events or circumstances aligning in a meaningful way.
