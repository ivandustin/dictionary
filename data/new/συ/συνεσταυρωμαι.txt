Word: συνεσταυρωμαι

Etymology:
The word συνεσταυρωμαι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (meaning "together" or "with") and the verb εσταυρωθη (meaning "was crucified"). Therefore, συνεσταυρωμαι means "I have been crucified together with".

Grammar:
συνεσταυρωμαι is a first-person singular passive indicative verb in Greek. It is in the perfect tense, indicating that the action of being crucified together with someone else has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description:
The word συνεσταυρωμαι appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Galatians 2:20, where the apostle Paul writes, "I have been crucified with Christ and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me." This verse is often interpreted to mean that Paul has died to his old self and now lives a new life in Christ. The word συνεσταυρωμαι emphasizes the idea of being united with Christ in his death and resurrection.
