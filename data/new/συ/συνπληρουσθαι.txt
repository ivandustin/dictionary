Word: συνπληρουσθαι

Etymology:
The word συνπληρουσθαι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (meaning "together" or "with") and the verb πληρουσθαι (meaning "to fill" or "to complete"). Therefore, the literal meaning of συνπληρουσθαι is "to be filled together" or "to be completed together".

Grammar:
συνπληρουσθαι is a passive verb in the present tense, middle or passive voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ουσθαι to the stem πληρο-, which is derived from the verb πληρουσιν (they fill). The prefix συν- is added to indicate that the action is done together with someone or something else.

Description:
The word συνπληρουσθαι is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the process of being filled or completed together with others in a community or group. It is often used in the context of spiritual growth and development, as believers are encouraged to work together to build up the body of Christ. The word emphasizes the importance of unity and cooperation in achieving a common goal.
