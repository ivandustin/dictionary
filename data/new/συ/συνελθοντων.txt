Word: συνελθοντων

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
ἔρχομαι (erchomai) means "to come"
The suffix -οντ- (-ont-) is a present participle ending, indicating ongoing action.

Therefore, συνελθοντων (synelthonton) means "coming together" or "gathered together."

Grammar:
συνελθοντων is the present participle form of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is in the genitive plural form, indicating that the subject is plural and in the possessive case.

Description:
συνελθοντων is a Greek word that describes a group of people or things coming together or assembling. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as a group of friends gathering for a meal or a team of workers assembling for a project. The word emphasizes the idea of unity and cooperation, as the individuals are coming together for a common purpose.
