The word συνεξουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they will eat together" or "they will dine together". 

Etymology: 
The word is derived from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and ἐξουσία (exousia), meaning "power" or "authority". 

Grammar: 
συνεξουσιν is a third-person plural future active indicative verb in Greek. 

Description: 
The word συνεξουσιν is used to describe a group of people who will eat or dine together in the future. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a group of friends planning to have a meal together, or in a figurative sense, such as a group of people coming together to discuss or negotiate something over a meal.
