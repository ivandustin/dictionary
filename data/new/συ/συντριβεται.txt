Word: συντριβεται

Etymology:
The word συντριβεται comes from the Greek verb συντρίβω (syndrivó), which means "to break into pieces" or "to crush". It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb τρίβω (trivó), meaning "to rub" or "to wear away".

Grammar:
συντριβεται is a third-person singular present indicative form of the verb συντρίβω (syndrivó), which means "he/she/it is being broken into pieces" or "he/she/it is being crushed". It is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it.

Description:
συντριβεται is a Greek verb that describes the process of something being broken into pieces or crushed. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a vase being dropped and shattered, or figurative concepts, such as a person's spirit being crushed by a difficult situation. The passive voice of the verb emphasizes that the subject is being acted upon, rather than actively participating in the action.
