Word: συνετεθειντο

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together"
-τεθειντο (-tetheinto) is the third person plural aorist middle indicative form of the verb τίθημι (tithemi) meaning "to place, set, establish"

Therefore, συνετεθειντο (synetetheinto) means "they established together" or "they set up together".

Grammar:
συνετεθειντο is a third person plural aorist middle indicative form of the verb τίθημι (tithemi). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon themselves or for their own benefit.

Description:
συνετεθειντο is a Greek verb that describes a group of people coming together to establish or set up something. The word has a sense of collaboration and cooperation, as the action is done together for the benefit of all involved. The word is commonly used in historical and philosophical texts to describe the actions of groups working towards a common goal.
