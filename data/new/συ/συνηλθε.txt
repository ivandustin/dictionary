Word: συνηλθε (synelthe)

Etymology: συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, συνηλθε (synelthe) means "came together".

Grammar: συνηλθε (synelthe) is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is a compound verb, consisting of the prefix συν- (syn-) and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai).

Description: συνηλθε (synelthe) is a Greek verb that describes the action of coming together. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people coming together for a meeting or a family gathering. The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates that the action was completed in the past.
