Word: συνισταντες

Etymology:
The word συνισταντες comes from the Greek verb συνίστημι (synístēmi), which means "to place together, to set in order, to recommend." It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb ἵστημι (hístēmi), meaning "to stand, to place."

Grammar:
συνισταντες is the present participle of the verb συνίστημι in the active voice, third person plural. It is often translated as "they recommend" or "they present."

Description:
συνισταντες is a Greek word that refers to a group of people who are recommending or presenting something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in business, politics, or personal relationships. The word implies that the group is working together to make a recommendation or presentation, and that they have a shared goal or purpose.
