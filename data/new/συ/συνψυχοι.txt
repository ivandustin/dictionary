The word "συνψυχοι" is a Greek term that is derived from the combination of two words: "σύν" (syn) meaning "together" and "ψυχή" (psyche) meaning "soul" or "spirit". The word is a plural noun that refers to people who are united in spirit or soul, or who share a common purpose or goal. 

In terms of grammar, "συνψυχοι" is a third-person plural noun in the nominative case. It can be translated into English as "fellowship" or "companionship". 

Overall, "συνψυχοι" is a word that emphasizes the importance of unity and shared purpose, and it is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe a community of believers who are united in their faith.
