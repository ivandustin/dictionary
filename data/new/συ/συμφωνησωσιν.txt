Word: συμφωνησωσιν

Etymology: συμφωνησωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb συμφωνέω (symphonéo), which means "to agree" or "to be in harmony". The suffix -σωσιν (-sōsin) is the third person plural aorist subjunctive active ending, indicating that the action of agreeing or being in harmony is in the subjunctive mood and happened in the past.

Grammar: συμφωνησωσιν is a third person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb συμφωνέω. In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: συμφωνησωσιν can be translated as "they might have agreed" or "they may have been in harmony". The subjunctive mood and aorist tense suggest that the action of agreeing or being in harmony is hypothetical and completed in the past.
