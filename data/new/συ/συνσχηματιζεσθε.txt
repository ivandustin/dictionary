Word: συνσχηματιζεσθε

Etymology: 
The word συνσχηματιζεσθε is derived from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and σχηματίζω (schēmatízō), meaning "to form or shape." The word is a verb form in the present tense, middle voice, and second person plural.

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, συνσχηματιζεσθε is a present tense verb in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb. The second person plural form indicates that the action is being performed by a group of people. 

Description:
The word συνσχηματιζεσθε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Colossians 3:10. The verse reads, "and have put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge after the image of its creator." The word συνσχηματιζεσθε is translated as "being renewed" in this context, indicating that the process of renewal is ongoing and involves a collective effort.
