The word "συνπολιται" is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix "συν-" meaning "together" or "with", and the noun "πολίτης" meaning "citizen". Therefore, "συνπολιται" can be translated as "fellow citizens" or "co-citizens". 

In terms of grammar, "συνπολιται" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It can be used to refer to a group of people who share the same citizenship or nationality. 

The etymology of "πολίτης" can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "πόλις" meaning "city". Therefore, "πολίτης" originally referred to a person who lived in a city-state and had certain rights and privileges as a member of that community. 

Overall, "συνπολιται" is a word that emphasizes the idea of community and shared identity among citizens.
