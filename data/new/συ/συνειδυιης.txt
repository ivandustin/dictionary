The word "συνειδυιης" (syneiduia) is a Greek noun that means "conscience" or "consciousness." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εἶδος" (eidos), meaning "form" or "appearance." 

Grammar: "Συνειδυιης" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. 

Description: The word refers to the inner sense of right and wrong that guides a person's behavior. It is often associated with moral and ethical decision-making. In Greek philosophy, the concept of "syneiduia" was central to the idea of living a virtuous life.
