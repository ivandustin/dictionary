Word: συνεφωνησας

Etymology:
The word συνεφωνησας is derived from the Greek words συν (syn) meaning "together" and εφωνησας (ephonēsas) meaning "spoke". The prefix συν indicates a sense of collaboration or unity, while εφωνησας is the aorist tense of the verb φωνέω (phonéō) which means "to speak" or "to sound".

Grammar:
συνεφωνησας is a verb in the aorist tense, second person singular, indicative mood, active voice. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
The word συνεφωνησας can be translated to mean "you agreed" or "you spoke in agreement". It is often used in the context of a conversation or discussion where two or more people are expressing their opinions and ideas. The word implies a sense of harmony and unity, as the prefix συν suggests collaboration and working together towards a common goal.
