Word: συνεδριον

Etymology:
The word συνεδριον (synedrion) is derived from the Greek words συν (syn) meaning "together" and εδρα (edra) meaning "seat" or "assembly". It originally referred to a council or assembly of people who came together to discuss and make decisions on important matters.

Grammar:
In Greek, συνεδριον is a neuter noun. Its nominative singular form is συνεδριον, and its genitive singular form is συνεδριου. The word can also be declined in the plural form, with the nominative plural being συνεδρια and the genitive plural being συνεδριων.

Description:
συνεδριον is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to a council or assembly of people who came together to discuss and make decisions on important matters. It was often used in the context of political or religious gatherings, and was an important part of the democratic process in ancient Greece. The word has also been used in modern times to refer to similar types of assemblies or councils.
