The word "συνστρατιωτηι" (synstratioti) is a Greek word that means "fellow soldier" or "comrade in arms". It is derived from the words "σύν" (syn), meaning "together", and "στρατιώτης" (stratiotis), meaning "soldier". 

In terms of grammar, "συνστρατιωτηι" is a noun in the singular form. It is a compound word made up of two separate words, which are joined together with the connecting vowel "ι" (iota). 

This word is often used in military contexts to refer to someone who serves alongside another soldier or is part of the same military unit. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of ally or partner in a shared endeavor.
