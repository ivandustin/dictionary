Word: συναπηχθη

Etymology:
The word συναπηχθη is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb απηχθη (apichthi), meaning "to be drawn away" or "to be carried off." The prefix συν- indicates that the action of the verb is done together with someone or something else.

Grammar:
συναπηχθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb happened in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
The word συναπηχθη describes a situation where someone or something was carried away or drawn away together with someone or something else. The context of the sentence would determine the exact meaning of the word.
