The word συνβασιλευσομεν (synbasileusomen) is a Greek verb that means "we will reign together" or "we will be joint rulers." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb βασιλεύω (basileuo), meaning "to reign" or "to rule."

Grammar: συνβασιλευσομεν is a first-person plural future indicative active verb, meaning it refers to an action that will be performed by the speaker and others in the future.

Description: The word is often used in a political or royal context, referring to joint rulership or co-regency. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where two or more people share power or authority.
