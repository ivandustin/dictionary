Word: συνυπουργουντων

Etymology: The word συνυπουργουντων is derived from the Greek words συν (syn) meaning "together" or "with", υπουργός (ypourgos) meaning "minister" or "servant", and the present participle suffix -ουντων (-ounton).

Grammar: συνυπουργουντων is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, plural number, and genitive case. It is formed by adding the suffix -ουντων (-ounton) to the present stem of the verb υπουργέω (ypourgeo), which means "to serve as a minister".

Description: συνυπουργουντων refers to a group of people who are serving as ministers together or working collaboratively as ministers. It can be used to describe a group of ministers in a government or a team of leaders in an organization who are working towards a common goal.
