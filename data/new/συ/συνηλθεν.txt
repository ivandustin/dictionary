Word: συνηλθεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with"
ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come"

Therefore, συνηλθεν (synelthen) means "came together" or "gathered together".

Grammar:
συνηλθεν is the aorist tense of the Greek verb συνέρχομαι (synerchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
συνηλθεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Mark 3:20, where it describes a crowd that gathered around Jesus. It can also be used in a general sense to describe any group of people who come together or assemble.
