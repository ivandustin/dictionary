Word: συνεργός

Etymology: The word συνεργός comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ἔργον (ergon), meaning "work." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνεργός is "working together."

Grammar: In Greek, συνεργός is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive συνεργοῦ (synergou) or the accusative συνεργόν (synergon).

Description: συνεργός is a word that refers to someone who works together with others towards a common goal. It can be used to describe a collaborator, a partner, or a team member. In modern Greek, it is also used to refer to a colleague or a coworker. The word has a positive connotation, as it implies cooperation and mutual support.
