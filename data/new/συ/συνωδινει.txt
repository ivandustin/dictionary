Word: συνωδινει

Etymology:
The word συνωδινει (synodinei) is derived from the Greek words σύν (syn), meaning "together," and ὀδύνη (odyni), meaning "pain." Therefore, the word συνωδινει means "to suffer together" or "to experience pain together."

Grammar:
συνωδινει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb συνωδίνω (synodino), which means "to suffer together" or "to experience pain together." The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: συνωδινει (synodinei)
- Plural: συνωδινουν (synodinoun)

Description:
The word συνωδινει is used to describe the act of experiencing pain or suffering together with someone else. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when two people are experiencing physical pain at the same time, or in a figurative sense, such as when two people are going through a difficult situation together. The word emphasizes the idea of shared experience and solidarity in the face of hardship.
