Word: συνιστημι

Etymology:
The word συνιστημι comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ἵστημι (histēmi), meaning "to stand." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνιστημι is "to stand together."

Grammar:
συνιστημι is a Greek verb that belongs to the first conjugation. It is an active verb that can be used in the indicative, subjunctive, and imperative moods. The present tense stem is συνιστα-, and the present indicative active forms are:

- συνίστημι (I stand together)
- συνίστης (you stand together)
- συνίστησι(ν) (he/she/it stands together)
- συνίσταμεν (we stand together)
- συνίστατε (you all stand together)
- συνίστασι(ν) (they stand together)

Description:
συνιστημι is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can mean "to recommend," "to introduce," "to present," "to establish," "to prove," "to unite," and "to stand together." In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the act of introducing someone to another person or group, as in Romans 16:1-2: "I commend to you our sister Phoebe, a servant of the church at Cenchreae, that you may welcome her in the Lord in a way worthy of the saints and help her in whatever she may need from you, for she has been a patron of many and of myself as well."
