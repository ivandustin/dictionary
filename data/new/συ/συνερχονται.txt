Word: συνερχονται

Etymology:
συνερχονται is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn), meaning "together," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνερχονται is "they come together."

Grammar:
συνερχονται is a present tense, third-person plural verb in Greek. It belongs to the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In English, the equivalent verb would be "they are coming together."

Description:
συνερχονται is a verb that expresses the action of people or things coming together in a group or gathering. It can be used in various contexts, such as social events, meetings, or even natural phenomena like clouds gathering in the sky. The verb implies a sense of unity and collaboration among the subjects that are coming together.
