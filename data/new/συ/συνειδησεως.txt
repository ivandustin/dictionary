Word: συνειδησεως

Etymology:
The word συνειδησεως comes from the Greek root words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and εἶδος (eidos) meaning "appearance" or "form". The word συνείδηση (syneídisi) means "conscience" or "consciousness" in modern Greek.

Grammar:
συνειδησεως is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun. In this case, it is the genitive form of the noun συνείδηση (syneídisi), which means "conscience" or "consciousness".

Description:
συνειδησεως refers to the state of being conscious or aware of one's thoughts, feelings, and actions. It is often used in the context of moral or ethical decision-making, as one's conscience is seen as a guide to making choices that align with one's values and beliefs. The word can also refer to a sense of guilt or remorse that arises when one violates their own moral code.
