Word: συνεληλυθυιαι

Etymology:
The word συνεληλυθυιαι is a Greek word that is derived from the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." The word is formed by adding the prefix συν- (syn-), which means "together," to the verb ελαύνω (elaúno), which means "to drive" or "to push."

συνεληλυθυιαι is a third-person plural perfect indicative active verb in the Greek language. The perfect tense indicates that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact. Description:
The word συνεληλυθυιαι can be translated to mean "they have come together" or "they have assembled." It is often used in the context of a group of people coming together for a specific purpose or event. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the coming together of ideas or concepts.
