Word: συνηγμένοι

Etymology:
The word συνηγμένοι (synēgmenoi) is a Greek word that comes from the verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather together." The suffix -μένοι (-menoi) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is ongoing or completed.

Grammar:
συνηγμένοι is the masculine plural nominative form of the participle συνάγω. It is used to describe a group of people who have gathered together. In this form, it is the subject of a sentence.

Description:
The word συνηγμένοι describes a group of people who have come together for a common purpose. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as a group of friends gathering for a social event, a team of workers collaborating on a project, or a congregation assembling for worship. The word implies a sense of unity and cooperation among the individuals who have gathered.
