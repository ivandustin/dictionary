Word: συνεργων

Etymology:
The word συνεργων comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ἔργον (ergon), meaning "work." Therefore, the word συνεργων literally means "working together."

Grammar:
συνεργων is a present participle in Greek, which means it is a verb form that functions as an adjective. It is derived from the verb συνεργέω (synergeō), which means "to work together." The present participle form indicates that the action of working together is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
συνεργων is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe individuals who work together for a common purpose, particularly in the context of spreading the gospel. It can also be used more generally to describe any kind of cooperative effort.
