Word: συνηθειαν

Etymology:
The word συνηθειαν comes from the Greek word συνήθεια (synḗtheia), which means "habit" or "custom". It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the noun ἦθος (êthos), meaning "character" or "habit".

Grammar:
συνηθειαν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence.

Description:
συνηθειαν refers to a habit or custom that is regularly practiced by an individual or a group of people. It can also refer to a tradition or a way of life that is passed down from generation to generation. The word implies a sense of familiarity and comfort with a particular behavior or practice.
