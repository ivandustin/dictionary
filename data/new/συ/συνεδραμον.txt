Word: συνεδραμον

Etymology:
The word συνεδραμον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn) meaning "together" and εδραμον (edramon) meaning "I ran". Therefore, the literal meaning of συνεδραμον is "I ran together".

Grammar:
συνεδραμον is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb συντρέχω (syntrecho), which means "to run together". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
συνεδραμον is a verb that describes an action of running together with someone or something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as running together with a group of people, or in a figurative sense, such as working together towards a common goal. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels and the Book of Acts.
