Word: συνανακειμενους

Etymology:
The word συνανακειμενους is derived from the Greek words συν (syn) meaning "together" or "with", ανα (ana) meaning "up" or "again", and κειμενους (keimenous) meaning "lying down" or "reclining". It is a compound word formed by combining these three words.

Grammar:
συνανακειμενους is a Greek adjective in the masculine, plural, accusative case. It is used to describe a group of people who are reclining or lying down together, typically at a banquet or feast.

Description:
The word συνανακειμενους is often used in the context of ancient Greek culture and literature, particularly in descriptions of banquets and feasts. It is used to describe the position of guests who are reclining on couches or cushions while dining. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any group of people who are lying down or reclining together.
