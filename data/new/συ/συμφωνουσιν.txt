The word "συμφωνουσιν" (symphonousin) is a verb in Greek that means "they agree" or "they are in harmony". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "συμφωνέω" (symphōneō), which means "to agree" or "to be in harmony". 

Grammar: "συμφωνουσιν" is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb "συμφωνέω". 

Description: The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe people or things that are in agreement or harmony with each other. It can also be used in a musical context to describe instruments or voices that are in harmony with each other.
