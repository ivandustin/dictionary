Word: συνπαθησαι

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + παθησαι (pathēsai, meaning "to suffer"). The word is derived from the Greek verb πάσχω (paschō, meaning "to suffer").

Grammar: συνπαθησαι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is composed of the prefix συν- and the verb παθησαι.

Description: συνπαθησαι means "to suffer together" or "to sympathize with." It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe the idea of sharing in the suffering of others. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the letters of Paul.
