Word: συνελάλουν

Etymology:
The word συνελάλουν is derived from the Greek root words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and λαλέω (laleo), meaning "to speak." The word is a present tense, plural form of the verb συνελαλέω (synelaleo), which means "to speak together" or "to converse."

Grammar:
συνελάλουν is a present tense, plural form of the verb συνελαλέω (synelaleo). In Greek, verbs change form based on the tense, voice, mood, and person/number of the subject. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, the plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people, and the verb is in the active voice.

Description:
συνελάλουν is a Greek verb that means "they speak together" or "they converse." It implies a group of people engaged in a conversation or discussion. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a social setting, a business meeting, or a religious gathering.
