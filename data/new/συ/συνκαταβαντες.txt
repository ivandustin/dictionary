The word "συνκαταβαντες" is a Greek verb that is derived from two words: "συν" meaning "together" and "καταβαίνω" meaning "to descend" or "to go down". Therefore, "συνκαταβαντες" means "to go down together" or "to descend together". 

In terms of grammar, "συνκαταβαντες" is a present participle verb form, indicating an ongoing action. It is also in the masculine plural form, indicating that the subject of the verb is a group of males. 

Overall, "συνκαταβαντες" is a descriptive word that paints a picture of a group of people descending together, possibly in a coordinated or synchronized manner.
