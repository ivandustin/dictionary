Word: συμβεβηκεν

Etymology:
συμβεβηκεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb συμβαίνω (sumbaínō), which means "to happen" or "to occur". The word is a perfect tense form of the verb, which indicates that the action has been completed in the past.

συμβεβηκεν is a third-person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb συμβαίνω. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or expressing a fact.

συμβεβηκεν can be translated to English as "has happened" or "has occurred". It is often used to describe a past event that has ongoing effects in the present. For example, "Συμβεβηκεν κάτι σημαντικό" (sumbebekan káti simantikó) can be translated as "Something important has happened".
