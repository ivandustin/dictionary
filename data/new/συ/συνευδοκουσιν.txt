Word: συνευδοκουσιν

Etymology: The word συνευδοκουσιν is derived from the Greek words συν (syn) meaning "together" and ευδοκέω (eudokeo) meaning "to be well pleased". 

Grammar: συνευδοκουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is a compound word made up of the prefix συν (syn) and the verb ευδοκέω (eudokeo). 

Description: The word συνευδοκουσιν means "they are well pleased together" or "they agree together". It is often used in a religious context to describe a group of people who are in agreement with each other and with God. The word is found in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 8:28, which reads: "And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose." In this verse, the word συνευδοκουσιν is translated as "work together".
