The word "συναγαγηι" (synagagei) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "συνάγω" (synagō), which means "to gather together" or "to bring together." 

In terms of grammar, "συναγαγηι" is a third-person singular form of the verb, which means it refers to a single subject who performed the action in the past. 

Overall, "συναγαγηι" can be translated to mean "he/she/it gathered together" or "he/she/it brought together."
