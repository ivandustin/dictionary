Word: συναποθανειν

Etymology: συν- (together) + ἀποθανεῖν (to die)

Grammar: συναποθανειν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist infinitive form. It is composed of the prefix συν- which means "together" and the verb ἀποθανεῖν which means "to die". 

Description: συναποθανειν means "to die together" or "to die at the same time". It is often used to describe a group of people who die together, such as soldiers in battle or passengers in a plane crash. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a shared fate or destiny.
