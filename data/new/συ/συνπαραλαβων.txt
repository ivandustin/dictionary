Word: συνπαραλαβων

Etymology:
The word συνπαραλαβων is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (meaning "together" or "with") and the verb παραλαμβάνω (meaning "to receive" or "to take"). Therefore, the literal meaning of συνπαραλαβων is "receiving together" or "taking with".

συνπαραλαβων is a present participle in Greek, which means that it is a verb form that functions as an adjective. It is declined according to gender, number, and case, and agrees with the noun it modifies. In this case, it is masculine, singular, and nominative, modifying an implied subject.

συνπαραλαβων is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark (15:21). It is used to describe Simon of Cyrene, who was compelled by the Roman soldiers to carry the cross of Jesus.
