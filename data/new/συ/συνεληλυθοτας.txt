Word: συνεληλυθότας

Etymology:
The word συνεληλυθότας is a Greek word that is derived from the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." The word is formed by combining the prefix συν- (syn-), which means "together," and the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), which means "to come."

Grammar:
συνεληλυθότας is a plural past participle of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai). It is in the masculine gender and accusative case. The word is used to describe a group of people who have come together or assembled.

Description:
The word συνεληλυθότας is used in Greek literature to describe a group of people who have gathered together for a specific purpose. It can refer to a group of soldiers who have assembled for battle, a group of citizens who have gathered for a meeting, or a group of friends who have come together for a social event. The word implies a sense of unity and purpose among the group.
