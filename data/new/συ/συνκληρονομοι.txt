Word: συνκληρονομοι

Etymology: 
The word συνκληρονομοι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: συν (syn) meaning "together" or "with", and κληρονομοι (klēronomoi) meaning "heirs" or "inheritors". Therefore, the literal meaning of συνκληρονομοι is "co-heirs" or "joint heirs".

Grammar:
συνκληρονομοι is a plural noun in Greek, and it is declined as follows:

Nominative: συνκληρονομοι (synklēronomoi)
Genitive: συνκληρονομων (synklēronomōn)
Dative: συνκληρονομοις (synklēronomois)
Accusative: συνκληρονομους (synklēronomous)
Vocative: συνκληρονομοι (synklēronomoi)

Description:
The word συνκληρονομοι is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 8:17, where it is translated as "joint heirs" in English. The passage reads: "And if children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ; if so be that we suffer with him, that we may be also glorified together." The word emphasizes the idea of sharing in the inheritance of Christ, and being co-heirs with him.
