Word: συντριβον

Etymology:
The word συντριβον (syntribon) is derived from the Greek verb συντρίβω (syntribō), which means "to crush" or "to break into pieces". The suffix -ον (-on) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word describes something that is currently happening.

Grammar:
συντριβον is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is used to describe something that is currently happening or ongoing. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives or as part of a verb phrase.

Description:
συντριβον can be translated as "crushing" or "breaking into pieces". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe something that is causing great destruction or harm. For example, it could be used to describe a powerful storm that is causing buildings to crumble or a disease that is ravaging a population. In a more literal sense, it could be used to describe the act of crushing grapes to make wine or breaking rocks to build a road.
