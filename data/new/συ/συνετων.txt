Word: συνετων

Etymology: συνετων is a Greek word that comes from the root word συνετός (synetós), which means "intelligent" or "wise". The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a masculine plural ending, so συνετων means "of the wise" or "of the intelligent".

Grammar: συνετων is a genitive plural form of the adjective συνετός (synetós). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, συνετων modifies a masculine plural noun.

Description: συνετων is used in Greek literature to describe a group of wise or intelligent people. It can refer to a group of philosophers, scholars, or any other group of people who are known for their intelligence and wisdom. The word is often used in a positive sense, to praise the intelligence and wisdom of a particular group.
