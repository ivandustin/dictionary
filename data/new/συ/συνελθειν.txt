Word: συνελθειν

Etymology:
The word συνελθειν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (meaning "together") and the verb ἔρχομαι (meaning "to come"). Therefore, the literal meaning of συνελθειν is "to come together".

Grammar:
συνελθειν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is also in the infinitive form, which means that it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
The word συνελθειν is often used in the context of people coming together for a specific purpose or meeting. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as ideas or concepts coming together to form a new understanding. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the gathering of people for religious purposes, such as in Matthew 18:20 where it says "For where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I in the midst of them."
