Word: συνεδρια

Etymology: The word συνεδρια (synedria) comes from the Greek root words συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and εδρα (edra) meaning "seat" or "place of sitting". Together, they form the word συνεδρια, which means "a sitting together" or "a meeting".

Grammar: συνεδρια is a noun in the Greek language. It is a neuter noun and its plural form is συνεδρια.

Description: συνεδρια refers to a meeting or assembly of people who come together to discuss and make decisions on a particular topic. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe the meetings of the Athenian council, which was responsible for making important decisions for the city-state. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to any type of meeting or conference.
