Word: συνδεσμων

Etymology:
The word συνδεσμων comes from the Greek root words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and δεσμός (desmos) meaning "bond" or "tie". Therefore, the word συνδεσμων literally means "something that ties together".

Grammar:
In Greek, συνδεσμων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is συνδεσμοι (syndesmoi).

Description:
The word συνδεσμων is commonly used in Greek to refer to a conjunction or a connector. It is a word that is used to link two or more words, phrases, or clauses together in a sentence. In this sense, it is similar to the English words "and", "or", "but", and "because". 

For example, in the sentence "Ο ήλιος λάμπει και ο ουρανός είναι γαλάζιος" (The sun is shining and the sky is blue), the word συνδεσμων is used to link the two independent clauses "Ο ήλιος λάμπει" (The sun is shining) and "ο ουρανός είναι γαλάζιος" (the sky is blue). 

Overall, συνδεσμων is an important word in Greek grammar that is used to connect ideas and create more complex sentences.
