Word: συνεχομενον

Etymology:
συνεχομενον is a present participle of the Greek verb συνέχω (synechō), which means "to hold together, to bind, to unite". The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb ἔχω (echō), meaning "to hold".

Grammar:
συνεχομενον is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb συνέχω (synechō), which is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice.

Description:
συνεχομενον describes something that is being held together or bound. It can also refer to something that is continuous or uninterrupted. In English, it can be translated as "being held together" or "being continuous".
