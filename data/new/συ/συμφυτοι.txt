Word: συμφυτοι

Etymology:
The word συμφυτοι (symphytoi) is derived from the Greek words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and φυτόν (phyton) meaning "plant". The word literally means "grown together" or "joined together".

Grammar:
συμφυτοι is a plural noun in the nominative case in Greek. It is often used to describe plants that have grown together or are intertwined.

Description:
συμφυτοι is a term used in botany to describe plants that have grown together or are intertwined. This can occur naturally or through intentional cultivation. In some cases, the plants may share a common root system or have grown together in a way that allows them to support each other. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe people or things that are closely connected or intertwined.
