Word: συνιστησιν

Etymology:
The word συνιστησιν comes from the Greek verb συνίστημι (synistēmi), which means "to place together, to set in order, to recommend." The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb ἵστημι (histēmi), meaning "to stand, to place."

Grammar:
συνιστησιν is a noun in the nominative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description:
συνιστησιν refers to a recommendation or a commendation. It is often used in the context of introducing someone to another person or group, or in the context of giving a positive evaluation or endorsement of someone or something. The word can also refer to a letter of recommendation or a reference. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the act of commending oneself or others to God.
