Word: συνλυπούμενος

Etymology: The word συνλυπούμενος comes from the Greek prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb λυπούμενος (lypoúmenos), meaning "to grieve." Therefore, the word συνλυπούμενος means "grieving together" or "sympathizing."

Grammar: συνλυπούμενος is a present participle in the passive voice, masculine gender, and singular number. It is derived from the verb λυπέω (lypéo), meaning "to grieve," and the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together." The word is used to describe someone who is experiencing grief or sorrow along with someone else.

Description: συνλυπούμενος is a word that describes the act of sharing in someone else's grief or sorrow. It is often used to describe a person who is empathetic and compassionate towards others who are going through a difficult time. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, in the workplace, or in a community setting. It is a powerful word that conveys a sense of solidarity and support for those who are struggling.
