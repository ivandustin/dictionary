Word: συναθροισας

Etymology:
The word συναθροισας is a Greek verb that comes from the root words συν (syn) meaning "together" and αθροίζω (athroizo) meaning "to gather". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
συναθροισας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb αθροίζω (athroizo) and the prefix συν (syn), which means "together". The verb indicates that the action of gathering together has been completed in the past.

Description:
συναθροισας is a Greek verb that means "gathered together". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the gathering of people or things. For example, in Matthew 18:20, Jesus says, "For where two or three are gathered together (συναθροισας) in my name, there am I in the midst of them." The verb can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of bringing things together or collecting them in one place.
