Word: συνπαραλαβοντες

Etymology: συν- (together) + παραλαμβάνω (to receive, take) + -οντες (present participle suffix) = "taking together"

Grammar: συνπαραλαβοντες is a present participle in Greek, which means it functions as an adjective or adverb describing an ongoing action. It is in the masculine plural form, indicating that the subject is a group of males.

Description: συνπαραλαβοντες is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Acts (20:34). It describes a group of people who are taking something together, likely referring to the apostle Paul and his companions receiving support from the Ephesian elders. The word emphasizes the idea of unity and cooperation in the act of receiving.
