Word: συντελων

Etymology:
The word συντελων (syntelon) is derived from the Greek verb συντελέω (synteleo), which means "to complete" or "to finish." It is composed of the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb τελέω (teleo), meaning "to complete" or "to finish."

Grammar:
In Greek, συντελων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the noun συντελεστής (syntelestis), which means "co-worker" or "co-operator." 

Description:
The word συντελων is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to a co-worker or fellow-laborer in the work of spreading the gospel. It is used in several passages, including Philippians 4:3 and Colossians 4:11. In these contexts, it refers to individuals who worked alongside the apostle Paul in his missionary efforts. The word suggests a sense of partnership and collaboration in a common goal.
