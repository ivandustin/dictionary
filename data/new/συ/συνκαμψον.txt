The word "συνκαμψον" (synkamphon) is a Greek verb that means "to bend together" or "to fold together." It is derived from the prefix "syn-" meaning "together" and the verb "kamptō" meaning "to bend." 

In terms of grammar, "συνκαμψον" is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests.

Overall, "συνκαμψον" is a specific command to bend or fold something together.
