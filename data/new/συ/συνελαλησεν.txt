Word: συνελαλησεν

Etymology: συν- (together) + ἐλαλήσεν (spoke) → συνελάλησεν (spoke together)

Grammar: συνελάλησεν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular, indicative mood, active voice. It is derived from the verb ἐλαλέω (to speak) with the prefix συν- (together).

Description: συνελάλησεν means "he/she/it spoke together" or "he/she/it conversed" in Greek. It implies a conversation or dialogue between two or more people. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Luke 24:14, where it describes two disciples walking together and discussing the events surrounding Jesus' crucifixion and resurrection.
