Word: συνιστανειν

Etymology:
The word συνιστανειν comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ἱστάναι (histánai), meaning "to cause to stand." The word originally meant "to set together" or "to place together," but later came to mean "to recommend" or "to introduce."

Grammar:
συνιστανειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It is derived from the root verb συνίστημι (synístēmi), which means "to place together" or "to recommend." The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Συνιστάνω (synistánō) - I recommend
- Συνιστάνεις (synistáneis) - You recommend
- Συνιστάνει (synistánei) - He/she/it recommends
- Συνιστανούμε (synistanoúme) - We recommend
- Συνιστάνετε (synistánete) - You (plural) recommend
- Συνιστάνουν (synistanoun) - They recommend

Description:
συνιστανειν is a Greek verb that means "to recommend" or "to introduce." It is often used in the context of introducing someone to another person or recommending someone for a job or position. The verb is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the introduction of one person to another or the recommendation of one person to a group. The word can also be used more broadly to describe the act of bringing together or uniting different things or people.
