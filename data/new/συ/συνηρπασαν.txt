Word: συνηρπασαν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
ἁρπάζω (harpazō) means "to seize" or "to snatch"

Therefore, συνηρπασαν (synērpasan) means "they seized together" or "they snatched together."

Grammar:
συνηρπασαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people.

Description:
συνηρπασαν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Acts 23:10. In this passage, it describes a group of Jews who seized the apostle Paul and attempted to kill him. The word conveys a sense of sudden and forceful action, as if the group acted quickly and decisively.
