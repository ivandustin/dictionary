The word συνπασχομεν (synpaschomen) is a Greek verb that means "to suffer together" or "to share in suffering." It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb πάσχω (pascho), meaning "to suffer." 

In terms of grammar, συνπασχομεν is a first-person plural present indicative active verb. This means that it is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present, and that the subject of the verb is "we" (as in "we are suffering together").

Overall, συνπασχομεν is a word that emphasizes the idea of shared experience and solidarity in difficult times.
