The word "συνεπεμψαμεν" (synepempsamen) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, first person plural form. 

Etymology: 
The word is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb "ἐπέμπω" (epempo), meaning "to send". Therefore, "συνεπεμψαμεν" can be translated as "we sent together" or "we accompanied".

Grammar: 
The word is a regular aorist active indicative verb, which means it describes a completed action in the past. The first person plural form indicates that the subject is "we". 

Description: 
The word "συνεπεμψαμεν" is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Acts 15:27. In this context, it is used to describe the action of sending representatives from the church in Jerusalem to Antioch to deliver a letter regarding the decision of the council of Jerusalem.
