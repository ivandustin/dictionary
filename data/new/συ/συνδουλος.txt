Word: συνδούλος

Etymology: The word συνδούλος comes from the Greek roots σύν (syn), meaning "together," and δοῦλος (doulos), meaning "slave." Therefore, the word συνδούλος means "fellow slave" or "co-slave."

Grammar: συνδούλος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes συνδοῦλοι. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case συνδούλου, which means "of a fellow slave."

Description: συνδούλος refers to a person who is a slave along with others. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is in a similar situation or condition as others. In ancient Greece, slavery was a common practice, and many people were forced into servitude. The word συνδούλος was used to describe the relationships between slaves who worked together and shared the same fate.
