Word: συνεκδημους

Etymology:
The word συνεκδημους is derived from two Greek words: σύν (syn) meaning "together" and εκδημος (ekdemos) meaning "resident in a foreign country". Therefore, the literal meaning of συνεκδημους is "co-resident in a foreign country".

Grammar:
In Greek, συνεκδημους is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it would be συνεκδημος. If it modifies a feminine plural noun, it would be συνεκδημουσαι.

Description:
The word συνεκδημους is used to describe someone who is a fellow resident in a foreign country. It can also be used to describe someone who is a fellow expatriate or immigrant. The word has a sense of camaraderie and shared experience, as it implies that the person being described is going through similar challenges and experiences as the speaker.
