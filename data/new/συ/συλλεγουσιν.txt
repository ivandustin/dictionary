Word: συλλεγουσιν

Etymology:
The word συλλεγουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word συλλέγω (syllego), which means "to gather" or "to collect". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates that the verb is in the present tense and third person plural form.

Grammar:
συλλεγουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person of the subject. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
The word συλλεγουσιν describes the action of a group of people gathering or collecting something. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly is being gathered or collected. For example, if the sentence is "They are gathering flowers", συλλεγουσιν would mean "they are collecting flowers".
