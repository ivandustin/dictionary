Word: συνηθειαι

Etymology:
The word συνηθειαι (synētheiai) is derived from the Greek root words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and ἦθος (ēthos) meaning "habit" or "custom". Therefore, the word συνηθειαι refers to the collective habits or customs of a group of people.

Grammar:
συνηθειαι is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or the predicate nominative.

Description:
συνηθειαι is a term used to describe the customs, traditions, and habits of a particular group of people. It can refer to the shared practices of a community, such as religious rituals, social norms, or cultural traditions. The word implies a sense of continuity and stability, as these customs are often passed down from generation to generation. In modern usage, the term can also refer to personal habits or routines that an individual has developed over time.
