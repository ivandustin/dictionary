Word: συνεθεντο

Etymology:
συνεθεντο is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn), which means "together," and τιθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνεθεντο is "they placed together" or "they set together."

Grammar:
συνεθεντο is a third-person plural aorist middle indicative verb in ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a past action that is viewed as a whole, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon itself or for its own benefit.

Description:
συνεθεντο is used in the New Testament of the Bible in Luke 22:66, where it describes the action of the council of the Jewish leaders who gathered together to question Jesus. The verb implies a deliberate and intentional action of bringing together a group of people for a specific purpose.
