Word: συνηγμένων

Etymology:
The word συνηγμένων is derived from the Greek verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather" or "to collect". The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a masculine plural ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of people who are gathering or collecting something.

Grammar:
συνηγμένων is a genitive plural form of the verb συνάγω (synagō). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, συνηγμένων indicates that the noun it modifies is in a state of being gathered or collected by a group of people.

Description:
συνηγμένων can be translated as "of those who are gathered" or "of those who are collected". It is often used in the context of a group of people who have come together for a specific purpose, such as a meeting or a religious gathering. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any group of people who are gathered or collected for any reason.
