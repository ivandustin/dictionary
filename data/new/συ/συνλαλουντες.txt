Word: συνλαλουντες

Etymology:
The word συνλαλουντες is derived from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and λαλεω (laleo), meaning "to speak." Therefore, the word συνλαλουντες means "speaking together" or "conversing."

Grammar:
συνλαλουντες is a present participle in Greek, which means that it describes an ongoing action. It is in the masculine gender, plural number, and nominative case. 

Description:
The word συνλαλουντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe a group of people who are speaking together or conversing. Specifically, it is used in Acts 2:4 to describe the disciples of Jesus who were filled with the Holy Spirit and began speaking in different languages. The word can also be used more generally to describe any group of people who are speaking together or having a conversation.
