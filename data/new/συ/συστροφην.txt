The word "συστροφην" (systrophēn) is a Greek noun that means "a turning or twisting together, a knot or tangle." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "συστρέφω" (systrephō), which means "to twist together, to turn around." The suffix "-ην" (-ēn) is a common ending for nouns in Greek.

Grammar: "Συστροφην" is a singular, feminine noun in the accusative case.

Description: The word can be used to describe a physical knot or tangle, as well as a metaphorical one, such as a difficult situation or problem that is hard to unravel. It can also refer to the act of twisting or turning something together, such as the twisting of two ropes to make a stronger cord.
