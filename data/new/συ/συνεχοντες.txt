Word: συνεχοντες

Etymology:
The word συνεχοντες is derived from the Greek verb συνέχω (synécho), which means "to hold together" or "to bind together". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently holding or binding something together.

Grammar:
συνεχοντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is formed by adding the present participle suffix -οντες to the stem συνεχ- of the verb συνέχω. The word functions as an adjective, describing people who are currently holding or binding something together.

Description:
συνεχοντες can be translated to "those who are holding together" or "those who are binding together". The word implies a sense of unity and cooperation, as the people described by the word are working together to achieve a common goal. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people who are physically holding something together, or a group of people who are working together to maintain a relationship or organization.
