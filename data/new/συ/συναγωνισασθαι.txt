Word: συναγωνισασθαι

Etymology: The word συναγωνισασθαι comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and αγωνιζομαι (agonizomai), meaning "to struggle" or "to contend." Together, συναγωνισασθαι means "to struggle together" or "to contend together."

Grammar: συναγωνισασθαι is a verb in the aorist tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. The aorist tense indicates a past action that is viewed as a whole, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive mood indicates that the verb is being used as a verbal noun.

Description: συναγωνισασθαι is a word that describes the act of struggling or contending together with someone else. It can be used in a literal sense, such as in a physical competition or battle, or in a figurative sense, such as in a collaborative effort to achieve a common goal. The word implies a sense of unity and cooperation, as the struggle is undertaken together with others.
