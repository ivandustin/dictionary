Word: συναιχμαλωτους

Etymology: The word συναιχμαλωτους comes from the Greek words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and αἰχμάλωτος (aichmálotos) meaning "prisoner of war". Therefore, the word συναιχμαλωτους means "fellow prisoner of war" or "co-captive".

Grammar: συναιχμαλωτους is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the masculine gender and in the accusative case. The singular form of the word is συναιχμαλώτης (synaichmálōtēs).

Description: The word συναιχμαλωτους refers to a group of people who are prisoners of war together. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people who are in a difficult situation together and are united in their struggle. The word has a strong connotation of solidarity and shared experience.
