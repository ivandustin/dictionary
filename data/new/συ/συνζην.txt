Word: συνζην

Etymology: The word συνζην (synzēn) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb ζην (zēn), meaning "to live." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνζην is "to live together."

Grammar: συνζην is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ συνζώ (egō synzō) - I live together
- Εσύ συνζείς (esý synzeís) - You live together
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό συνζεί (af̱tós/af̱tí/af̱tó synzeí) - He/She/It lives together
- Εμείς συνζούμε (emeís synzoúme) - We live together
- Εσείς συνζείτε (eseís synzeíte) - You (plural) live together
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά συνζουν (af̱toí/af̱tés/af̱tá synzoun) - They live together

Description: συνζην is a verb that describes the act of living together with someone or something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to people or animals living together in the same place, or in a figurative sense, such as when referring to ideas or concepts that are closely related or interconnected. The word can also be used to describe the act of coexisting or collaborating with others towards a common goal.
