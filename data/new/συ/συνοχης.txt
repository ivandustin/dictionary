Word: συνοχης

Etymology:
The word συνοχης comes from the Greek root words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and οχή (oché) meaning "a holding, a keeping". Together, they form the word συνοχης which means "a holding together, cohesion".

Grammar:
συνοχης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description:
συνοχης refers to the state of being united or held together. It can be used to describe physical objects that are held together, such as a building or a bridge, as well as abstract concepts such as a community or a team. The word implies a sense of strength and stability that comes from being united and working together towards a common goal.
