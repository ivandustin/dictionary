συνιεις is a Greek word that comes from the verb συνίημι (syníēmi), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend." The word is formed by combining the prefix συν- (syn-), which means "together," and the verb ἴημι (íēmi), which means "to send" or "to go."

Grammar:
συνιεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the verb συνίημι (syníēmi). It is translated as "you understand" or "you comprehend" in English.

συνιεις is a verb that expresses the action of understanding or comprehending something. It is often used in the context of gaining knowledge or insight into a particular subject or situation.
