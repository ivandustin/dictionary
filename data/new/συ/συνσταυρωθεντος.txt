Word: συνσταυρωθεντος

Etymology: συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with" and σταυρός (stauros) meaning "cross". The word is derived from the Greek verb συνσταυρόω (synstauróō) which means "to crucify together with".

Grammar: συνσταυρωθεντος is the passive participle form of the verb συνσταυρόω (synstauróō) in the aorist tense. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: συνσταυρωθεντος is a Greek word used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. It is translated as "crucified with" or "co-crucified" in English. The word emphasizes the idea that Jesus was not crucified alone, but that he was crucified together with others. This word is used in Galatians 2:20, where the apostle Paul writes, "I have been crucified with Christ and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me."
