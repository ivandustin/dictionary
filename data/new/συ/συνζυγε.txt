The word "συνζυγε" (synzyge) is a Greek verb that means "to yoke together" or "to join together." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ζυγεῖν" (zygein), meaning "to yoke."

Grammar: "Συνζυγε" is a second person singular present active imperative form of the verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to yoke or join something together.

Description: The word "συνζυγε" is often used in a figurative sense to describe the act of bringing together two or more things or people. It can be used to describe the joining of forces, the formation of alliances, or the uniting of different ideas or concepts.
