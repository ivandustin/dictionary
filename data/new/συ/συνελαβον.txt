Word: συνελαβον

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with"
ἔλαβον (élabon) meaning "they took"

Therefore, συνελαβον (synelabon) means "they took together" or "they arrested together".

Grammar:
συνελαβον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb συλλαμβάνω (sullambanō), which means "to seize" or "to arrest". It is in the third person plural, indicating that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
συνελαβον is a Greek word that describes the act of arresting or seizing someone. It is often used in legal or law enforcement contexts. The word is formed by combining the prefix συν- (syn-) with the verb ἔλαβον (élabon), which means "they took". The resulting word, συνελαβον, conveys the idea of a group of people working together to apprehend someone.
