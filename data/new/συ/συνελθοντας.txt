Word: συνελθοντας

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
ἔρχομαι (érchomai) means "to come"

Therefore, συνελθοντας (synelthontas) is the present participle form of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble."

Grammar:
συνελθοντας is the present participle form of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which is in the active voice and the masculine gender. It is declined in the nominative case and the plural number.

Description:
συνελθοντας is used to describe a group of people who are coming together or assembling for a specific purpose. It can also be used to describe a group of things that are coming together or combining to form a whole.
