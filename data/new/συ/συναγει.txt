The word "συναγει" (synagei) is a Greek verb that means "to gather together" or "to assemble." 

Etymology: The word comes from the combination of the prefix "syn-" meaning "together" and the verb "agei" meaning "to lead."

Grammar: "συναγει" is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It can be conjugated to match the subject of the sentence.

Description: The word is often used in the context of religious gatherings or meetings, such as in the New Testament when Jesus "gathered together" his disciples. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of gathering or assembly.
