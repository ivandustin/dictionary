Etymology: The word "σύρω" (syro) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *swer- which means "to turn, to twist". 

Grammar: "συρει" (syrei) is a present tense verb in the indicative mood, which means it is used to describe actions that are currently happening or are habitual. It is also in the third person singular form, which means it refers to a single subject who is performing the action.
