Word: συναγωγη

Etymology:
The word συναγωγη (synagoge) comes from the Greek roots συν (syn), meaning "together," and αγωγη (agogi), meaning "leading" or "bringing." The word originally referred to a gathering or assembly of people, and later came to specifically refer to a Jewish place of worship and study.

Grammar:
συναγωγη is a feminine noun in Greek, and its nominative singular form is συναγωγή. In the plural form, it becomes συναγωγές. It is declined like other first-declension feminine nouns in Greek.

Description:
συναγωγη is a term used in Greek to refer to a synagogue, which is a Jewish place of worship and study. Synagogues were established as centers for Jewish prayer, study, and community life, and were often the focus of Jewish life in a particular area. The term συναγωγη is still used in modern Greek to refer to a synagogue.
