Word: συνμαθηταίς

Etymology: συν- (together) + μαθητής (disciple, student) = συνμαθητής (fellow student)

Grammar: συνμαθηταίς is a plural noun in the feminine gender, derived from the singular form συνμαθητής. It is declined in the first declension, with the nominative plural ending in -αις.

Description: συνμαθηταίς refers to fellow female students or disciples who are learning together. It can be used in both a literal and figurative sense, such as referring to classmates in a school setting or fellow learners in a spiritual or philosophical context.
