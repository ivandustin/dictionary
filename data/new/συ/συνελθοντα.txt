Word: συνελθοντα

Etymology: συνελθοντα is a Greek word that comes from the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." The word is formed by adding the prefix συν- (syn-), which means "together," to the verb stem ἔρχομαι (érchomai), which means "to come."

Grammar: συνελθοντα is the present participle form of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "coming together" or "assembling." In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. It is declined according to gender, number, and case, and can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description: συνελθοντα can be translated as "those who are coming together" or "those who are assembling." It is often used in a religious or political context to describe a group of people who are gathering for a specific purpose, such as a meeting or a ceremony.
