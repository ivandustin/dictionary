Word: συνπαραλαμβανειν

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + παραλαμβάνω (paralambanō, meaning "to take along") + -ειν (-ein, a suffix indicating the infinitive form of a verb). Therefore, the word συνπαραλαμβανειν means "to take along together".

Grammar: συνπαραλαμβανειν is a Greek verb in the present active infinitive form. It is composed of the prefix συν- and the verb παραλαμβάνω, which means "to take along". The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: συνπαραλαμβάνω (I take along together), συνπαραλαμβάνεις (you take along together), συνπαραλαμβάνει (he/she/it takes along together), συνπαραλαμβάνομεν (we take along together), συνπαραλαμβάνετε (you all take along together), συνπαραλαμβάνουσι(ν) (they take along together)

Description: συνπαραλαμβανειν is a verb that describes the action of taking something or someone along with another person or thing. It can be used in a literal sense, such as taking a friend along on a trip, or in a figurative sense, such as taking someone's opinion or perspective into consideration. The word emphasizes the idea of doing something together, rather than alone.
