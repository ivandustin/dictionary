The word "συνηρπακει" (synērpakei) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular form of the verb "συναρπάζω" (synarpazō), which means "to snatch away, to seize, to carry off". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb "αρπάζω" (arpazō), meaning "to snatch". 

Grammar: "συνηρπακει" is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: The word describes an action of forcefully taking something away, often quickly and unexpectedly. It can be used in a literal sense, such as snatching an object from someone's hand, or in a figurative sense, such as seizing an opportunity.
