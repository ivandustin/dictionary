Word: συνελθοντες

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
ἔρχομαι (erchomai) means "to come"

Therefore, συνελθοντες (synelthontes) means "coming together" or "having come together".

Grammar:
συνελθοντες is the present participle of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together". It is in the masculine plural form, indicating that the subject is a group of males who are coming together.

Description:
συνελθοντες is a Greek word that describes a group of people who are gathering or coming together for a specific purpose. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as a group of friends meeting up, a team gathering for a meeting, or a group of people assembling for a protest. The word emphasizes the idea of unity and collaboration, as the individuals are coming together to achieve a common goal.
