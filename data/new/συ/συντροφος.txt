Word: συντροφος

Etymology:
The word συντροφος comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and τροφος (trophos), meaning "nourisher" or "feeder." The word originally referred to someone who shared a meal with another person, but over time it came to mean a companion or partner.

Grammar:
In Greek, συντροφος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive συντροφου (sintrofou) or the accusative συντροφον (sintrofon).

Description:
The word συντροφος refers to a companion or partner, someone who shares experiences and supports another person. It can be used to describe a friend, a spouse, or a business partner. The word implies a close relationship based on mutual trust and respect.
