The word "συνεδριωι" (synedrioi) is a Greek noun that refers to a council or assembly of people who come together to discuss and make decisions on important matters. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "syn" meaning "together" and "hedra" meaning "seat" or "chair". 

Grammar: "συνεδριωι" is a plural noun in the dative case, which means it is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. 

Description: In ancient Greece, synedrioi were important political and judicial bodies that were responsible for making decisions on matters such as laws, taxes, and foreign policy. They were composed of elected officials or representatives from various regions or social groups. The most famous synedrion was the Great Sanhedrin of Jerusalem, which was the highest Jewish council during the Second Temple period. Today, the word "synedrioi" is still used in modern Greek to refer to various types of councils or committees.
