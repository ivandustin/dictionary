Word: συνεργουντες

Etymology: συνεργουντες is a Greek word that comes from the root words συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and εργον (ergon) meaning "work". The word is a present participle form of the verb συνεργέω (synergeō), which means "to work together".

Grammar: συνεργουντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb συνεργέω (synergeō), which means "to work together". The present participle form indicates that the action of working together is ongoing or continuous.

Description: συνεργουντες refers to a group of people who are working together towards a common goal or objective. The word can be used to describe a team of coworkers, collaborators, or partners who are working together to achieve a specific outcome. The word implies a sense of cooperation and collaboration, and suggests that the individuals involved are working together in a coordinated and effective manner.
