Word: συνκαλεσασθαι

Etymology:
The word συνκαλεσασθαι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: συν- (meaning "together") and καλεσασθαι (meaning "to call"). Therefore, the literal meaning of the word is "to call together".

Grammar:
συνκαλεσασθαι is a verb in the aorist middle infinitive form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
The word συνκαλεσασθαι is used to describe the act of calling people together for a meeting or gathering. It implies a sense of collaboration and cooperation, as the prefix συν- suggests working together towards a common goal. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of bringing together different ideas or perspectives in order to reach a consensus or agreement.
