Word: συνεβη

Etymology: συνεβη is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn) meaning "together" and εβη (ebē) meaning "it happened". Therefore, the literal meaning of συνεβη is "it happened together".

Grammar: συνεβη is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question. In this case, συνεβη is in the third person singular form, meaning "it happened".

Description: συνεβη is a word that is often used in Greek literature to describe a sudden or unexpected event that occurred in the past. It can be translated to English as "it happened", "it occurred", or "it took place". It is commonly used in historical accounts, narratives, and storytelling.
