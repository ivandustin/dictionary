Word: συνηγαγετε

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together"
ἀγαγεῖν (agagein) meaning "to lead, bring"

Therefore, συνηγαγετε (synēgagete) means "you all brought together" or "you all gathered".

Grammar:
συνηγαγετε is the second person plural aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather, collect, assemble". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
συνηγαγετε is a verb in the Greek language that is used to describe the act of gathering or collecting something. It is often used in a literal sense, such as gathering a group of people or collecting objects, but can also be used figuratively to describe gathering information or ideas. The word is commonly found in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the gathering of people for religious purposes.
