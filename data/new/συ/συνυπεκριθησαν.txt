Word: συνυπεκριθησαν

Etymology:
The word συνυπεκριθησαν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of three parts: συν (syn), υπο (hypo), and κρινω (krino). συν means "together," υπο means "under," and κρινω means "to judge." Therefore, the word συνυπεκριθησαν means "together-under-judged," or "to be judged together."

Grammar:
συνυπεκριθησαν is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person plural form of the verb συνυποκρινομαι (synypokrinomai), which means "to act together with someone," "to pretend," or "to play a part." In this form, it means "they were judged together" or "they pretended together."

Description:
The word συνυπεκριθησαν appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Mark 14:56. In this passage, it describes the false witnesses who testified against Jesus during his trial. The word implies that these witnesses were working together to deceive the court and falsely accuse Jesus. The word can also be used more generally to describe any situation where people are working together to deceive or mislead others.
