Word: συνοχη

Etymology: The word συνοχη comes from the Greek root words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and οχή (ochi) meaning "a holding, a keeping". Together, they form the word συνοχη which means "a holding together, a cohesion".

Grammar: συνοχη is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is συνοχές (synoches).

Description: συνοχη refers to the state of being united or held together. It can be used to describe physical objects that are held together, such as a building or a bridge, as well as abstract concepts such as a community or a society. In a figurative sense, it can also refer to the unity or coherence of an argument or a piece of writing.
