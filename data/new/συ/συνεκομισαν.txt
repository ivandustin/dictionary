Word: συνεκομισαν

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + ἐκομίσαντο (ekomísanto, meaning "they brought")

Grammar: συνεκομισαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. It is a compound verb formed by adding the prefix συν- to the verb κομίζω (komízo, meaning "to bring").

Description: συνεκομισαν means "they brought together" or "they collected". It is often used in the context of gathering or collecting things or people. The verb is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels, to describe the gathering of people or things by Jesus and his disciples.
