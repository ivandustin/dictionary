Word: συναγαγειν

Etymology: συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and ἀγαγεῖν (agagein) meaning "to lead" or "to bring". Therefore, συναγαγεῖν (synagagein) means "to bring together" or "to gather".

Grammar: συναγαγεῖν (synagagein) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, meaning the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is formed by adding the prefix συν- (syn-) to the verb ἀγαγεῖν (agagein).

Description: συναγαγεῖν (synagagein) is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of gathering or bringing together people or things. It can also be used in a more abstract sense to describe the gathering of ideas or thoughts. For example, in Matthew 18:20, Jesus says "For where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I in the midst of them." Here, συναγαγεῖν (synagagein) is used to describe the act of gathering people together in the name of Jesus.
