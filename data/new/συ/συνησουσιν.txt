The word "συνησουσιν" is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "συνίημι" which means "to understand, comprehend, or perceive." The prefix "συν-" means "together" or "with," so the word "συνησουσιν" can be translated as "they will understand together" or "they will comprehend with one another." In terms of grammar, it is a regular future tense verb in the active voice.
