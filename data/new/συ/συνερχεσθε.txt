Word: συνερχεσθε

Etymology:
συνερχεσθε is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn), meaning "together," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνερχεσθε is "to come together."

Grammar:
συνερχεσθε is a second-person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed to a group of people, telling them to come together. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb.

Description:
συνερχεσθε is a verb that appears several times in the New Testament of the Bible, usually in the context of Jesus calling his disciples to gather together. It is often translated as "come together" or "assemble." The word carries a sense of unity and community, emphasizing the importance of people coming together for a common purpose.
