Word: συνηρχοντο

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with"
ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come"

Therefore, συνηρχοντο (synērchonto) means "they were coming together" or "they were assembling."

Grammar:
συνηρχοντο is the third person plural, imperfect indicative middle/passive form of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai).

Description:
συνηρχοντο is a Greek verb that describes a group of people coming together or assembling. It is often used in a religious or political context, such as a gathering of believers or a meeting of leaders. The verb is in the imperfect tense, indicating that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past.
