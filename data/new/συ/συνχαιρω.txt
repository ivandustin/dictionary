Word: συνχαιρω

Etymology: συν- (together) + χαιρω (to rejoice) = to rejoice together

Grammar: συνχαιρω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ συνχαίρω (I rejoice together)
- Εσύ συνχαίρεις (you rejoice together)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό συνχαίρει (he/she/it rejoices together)
- Εμείς συνχαιρούμε (we rejoice together)
- Εσείς συνχαιρείτε (you all rejoice together)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές συνχαίρουν (they rejoice together)

Description: συνχαιρω means to rejoice together or to share in someone else's joy. It is often used in a congratulatory sense, such as when someone has achieved something or received good news. It can also be used in a more general sense of expressing happiness for someone else's good fortune.
