Word: συνεσθιειν

Etymology:
The word συνεσθιειν comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ἐσθίω (esthiō), meaning "to eat." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνεσθιειν is "to eat together."

Grammar:
συνεσθιειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It is derived from the present tense stem συνεσθι-, which means "to eat together." The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: συνεσθίω (synesthiō)
- Plural: συνεσθίομεν (synesthiomen)

Description:
συνεσθιειν is a Greek verb that means "to eat together." It is often used in the context of sharing a meal or dining together. The word emphasizes the communal aspect of eating and highlights the importance of socializing and sharing food in Greek culture. In addition, the word can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of sharing experiences or participating in a common activity with others.
