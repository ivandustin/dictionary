Word: συναπαχθεντες

Etymology:
The word συναπαχθεντες is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix συν- (meaning "together" or "with") and the verb απαχθεντες (meaning "taken away" or "carried off"). The word is derived from the ancient Greek language.

Grammar:
συναπαχθεντες is a participle in the passive voice, masculine gender, plural number, and nominative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -εντες to the aorist stem of the verb απάγω (apago), which means "to lead away" or "to carry off". The prefix συν- indicates that the action was done together with someone else.

Description:
The word συναπαχθεντες can be translated as "having been carried away together" or "having been taken away in company with others". It is often used in the context of war or captivity, where a group of people is taken away together as prisoners. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people who are united in a common cause or experience.
