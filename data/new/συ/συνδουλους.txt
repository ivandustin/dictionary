Word: συνδούλους

Etymology: The word συνδούλους comes from the Greek words σύν (syn), meaning "together," and δοῦλος (doulos), meaning "slave." Therefore, the word συνδούλους means "fellow slaves" or "co-slaves."

Grammar: συνδούλους is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -ους to the stem δουλο-.

Description: συνδούλους is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a group of people who are slaves together. It can also be used metaphorically to describe people who are in a similar situation or who share a common bond. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe fellow workers in the gospel.
