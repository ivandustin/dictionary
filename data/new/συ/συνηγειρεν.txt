Word: συνηγειρεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together"
ἀγείρω (ageirō) meaning "to gather"

Therefore, συνηγειρεν (synēgeiren) means "to gather together" or "to assemble".

Grammar:
συνηγειρεν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb συναγείρω (synageirō).

Description:
συνηγειρεν is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to describe the action of Jesus gathering together his disciples or followers. The word emphasizes the idea of unity and togetherness, as Jesus brings people together for a common purpose.
