Etymology: 
The word "συμφωνησας" (symphonēsas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words "syn" meaning "together" and "phone" meaning "sound". The word originally meant "to make a sound together" or "to agree".

Grammar:
"Συμφωνησας" is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb "συμφωνέω" (symphonéō), which means "to agree" or "to be in harmony". In this form, it means "you agreed" or "you have agreed".

Description:
"Συμφωνησας" is a past tense verb that indicates that the subject (you) agreed to something in the past. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as agreeing to a proposal, a decision, or a plan. The word implies that the agreement was made willingly and voluntarily.
