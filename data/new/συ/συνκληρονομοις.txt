Word: συνκληρονομοις

Etymology: 
The word συνκληρονομοις is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: συν (syn) meaning "together" and κληρονόμοις (klēronómois) meaning "heirs". Therefore, the word συνκληρονομοις means "co-heirs" or "joint heirs".

Grammar:
In Greek, συνκληρονομοις is a noun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. It is the plural form of the noun συνκληρονόμος (synklēronómos), which means "co-heir" or "joint heir".

Description:
The word συνκληρονομοις is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 8:17, where it is translated as "joint heirs" in English. The verse reads: "And if children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ; if so be that we suffer with him, that we may be also glorified together."
