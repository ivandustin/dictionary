Word: συνλαμβάνω (synlamváno)

Etymology: The word συνλαμβάνω comes from the Greek prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb λαμβάνω (lamváno), meaning "to take." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνλαμβάνω is "to take together."

Grammar: συνλαμβάνω is a Greek verb that belongs to the first conjugation. It is a compound verb, which means that it is formed by combining a prefix (συν-) with a base verb (λαμβάνω). The verb is conjugated in all tenses and moods, and its stem changes in some of them. For example:

- Present indicative: συνλαμβάνω, συνλαμβάνεις, συνλαμβάνει, συνλαμβάνουμε, συνλαμβάνετε, συνλαμβάνουν
- Aorist indicative: συνέλαβα, συνέλαβες, συνέλαβε, συνελάβαμε, συνελάβατε, συνέλαβαν
- Imperative: συνλαμβάνε, συνλαμβάνετε

Description: συνλαμβάνω is a versatile verb that can have different meanings depending on the context. Some of its common meanings are:

- To take together, to take with: Συνέλαβα τον φίλο μου στο σπίτι μου. (I took my friend with me to my house.)
- To understand, to comprehend: Δεν συνελαβαίνεις τι σου λέω; (Don't you understand what I'm telling you?)
- To arrest, to apprehend: Η αστυνομία συνέλαβε τον δράστη του εγκλήματος. (The police arrested the perpetrator of the crime.)

Overall, συνλαμβάνω is a useful verb that can express different actions and ideas related to taking, understanding, and arresting.
