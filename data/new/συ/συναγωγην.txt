The word συναγωγην (synagogen) is derived from the Greek word συναγωγή (synagoge), which means "assembly" or "gathering." It is composed of two parts: συν (syn), which means "together," and αγωγή (agoge), which means "leading" or "bringing."

συναγωγην is a noun in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb in a sentence. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their function in a sentence, and the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.
