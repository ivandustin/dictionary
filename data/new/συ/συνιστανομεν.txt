Word: συνιστανομεν

Etymology:
συνιστανομεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: σύν (syn), meaning "together," and ἱστάναι (histánai), meaning "to cause to stand." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνιστανομεν is "to cause to stand together."

Grammar:
συνιστανομεν is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the first person plural, meaning "we cause to stand together."

Description:
συνιστανομεν is a verb that implies the act of bringing together different elements or parts to form a cohesive whole. It can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, where it refers to the formation of a coalition or alliance, or in philosophy, where it refers to the combination of different ideas or concepts to form a coherent theory. In general, συνιστανομεν conveys the idea of unity and cooperation towards a common goal.
