Word: συνεισηλθεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
εἰσελθεῖν (eiselthein) means "to enter"

Therefore, συνεισηλθεν (syneisēlthen) means "to enter together" or "to come in with someone."

Grammar:
συνεισηλθεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the third person singular form.

Description:
συνεισηλθεν is a Greek word found in the New Testament of the Bible (Mark 14:66). It describes Peter following Jesus into the courtyard of the high priest after Jesus was arrested. The word emphasizes that Peter entered with Jesus, indicating his loyalty and commitment to him.
