Word: συνωσιν

Etymology:
The word συνωσιν (synōsin) is derived from the Greek root words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and ὠστός (ōstos) meaning "a push, a thrust". The word συνωσιν (synōsin) literally means "a pushing together" or "a joining together".

Grammar:
συνωσιν (synōsin) is a noun in the feminine gender and in the accusative case. It is the accusative singular form of the noun σύνοδος (synodos), which means "a meeting, an assembly".

Description:
συνωσιν (synōsin) is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to a gathering or assembly of people. It is often used in reference to the gathering of believers in Christ, such as in Hebrews 10:25 which states, "not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another, and all the more as you see the Day drawing near." The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any kind of gathering or assembly.
