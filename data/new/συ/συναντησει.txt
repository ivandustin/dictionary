Word: συναντησει

Etymology:
The word συναντησει comes from the Greek verb συναντάω (synantáō), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb ανταω (antáō), meaning "to meet".

Grammar:
συναντησει is the third person singular future tense of the verb συναντάω (synantáō). In Greek, verbs are highly inflected, meaning that their form changes depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future.

Description:
συναντησει is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it will meet" or "he/she/it will encounter". It is often used in everyday conversation to describe future plans or meetings. For example, "Θα συναντηθούμε αύριο στις 5 το απόγευμα" (Tha synanthithoume avrio stis 5 to apogevma) means "We will meet tomorrow at 5 pm".
