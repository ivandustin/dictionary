Word: συγγενειαν

Etymology:
The word συγγενειαν (syngeneian) is derived from the Greek word συγγενής (syngenes), which means "related by blood" or "of the same kin". The suffix -ειαν (-eian) is a noun-forming suffix that denotes a state or quality.

Grammar:
συγγενειαν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence.

Description:
συγγενειαν refers to the state or quality of being related by blood or of the same kin. It can also refer to a family or a group of people who are related to each other. The word is often used in the context of genealogy or family history.
