Word: συνλημφθεντα

Etymology: The word συνλημφθεντα is derived from the Greek prefix συν- (meaning "together") and the verb λαμβάνω (lamvano) which means "to take". The word is in the passive voice and the perfect tense, indicating that the action of taking has been completed in the past and the subject has been affected by it.

Grammar: συνλημφθεντα is a neuter plural adjective in the nominative case. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. In this form, it can be used to describe a group of things that have been taken together.

Description: συνλημφθεντα can be translated as "taken together" or "collected". It is often used in medical contexts to describe lymph nodes that have been removed and examined together as a group.
