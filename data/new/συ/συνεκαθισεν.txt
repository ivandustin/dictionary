Word: συνεκαθισεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
εκαθίζω (ekathizō) means "to sit down"

Therefore, συνεκαθισεν (synekaqhisēn) means "he/she/it sat down together with" or "he/she/it sat down with."

Grammar:
συνεκαθισεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the third person singular form, meaning it refers to "he/she/it" sitting down.

Description:
συνεκαθισεν is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Mark 14:3. In this passage, a woman anoints Jesus with expensive perfume while he is reclining at a table. The word συνεκαθισεν is used to describe how Jesus was sitting down with his disciples when this happened.
