Word: συνειδησει

Etymology:
The word συνειδησει (syneidēsei) is derived from the Greek root words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and εἶδος (eidos) meaning "appearance" or "form". The word συνείδηση (syneidēsē) means "conscience" or "consciousness" in Greek.

Grammar:
συνειδησει is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. It is the future active indicative form of the verb συνείδω (syneidō), which means "to be conscious of" or "to have a conscience".

Description:
The word συνειδησει refers to the act of being conscious of something or having a conscience. It can also refer to the act of recognizing or acknowledging one's own actions or thoughts. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to refer to the feeling of guilt or remorse.
