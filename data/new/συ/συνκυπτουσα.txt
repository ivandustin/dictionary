The word "συνκυπτουσα" is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It is derived from the combination of two words: "σύν" meaning "together" and "κύπτω" meaning "to bend or stoop down". 

In terms of grammar, "συνκυπτουσα" is a feminine singular form, indicating that it refers to a female subject who is currently bending or stooping down together with someone else. 

Overall, the word "συνκυπτουσα" can be translated to mean "bending down together" or "stooping down together". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe two or more people who are working together or cooperating towards a common goal.
