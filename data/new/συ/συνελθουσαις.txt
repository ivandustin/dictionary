Word: συνελθουσαις

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
ἔρχομαι (erchomai) means "to come"

Therefore, συνελθουσαις (synelthousais) is the feminine dative plural form of the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble."

Grammar:
συνελθουσαις is a verb form in the Greek language. It is in the feminine gender, indicating that the subject of the verb is female. It is in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of the sentence.

Description:
συνελθουσαις can be translated as "having come together" or "having assembled." It is often used in the context of a group of people coming together for a specific purpose or event.
