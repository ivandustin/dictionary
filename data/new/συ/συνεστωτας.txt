Word: συνεστωτας

Etymology:
The word συνεστωτας is derived from the Greek verb συνίστημι (synistēmi), which means "to set together, to place together, to bring together." The suffix -τας (-tas) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of people or things that have been brought together.

Grammar:
συνεστωτας is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or the predicate nominative, which renames the subject. 

Description:
συνεστωτας refers to a group of people who are present or assembled together. It can also refer to a group of things that are combined or united in some way. The word is often used in a religious context to refer to the congregation or assembly of believers. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the gathering of Christians for worship or other religious purposes.
