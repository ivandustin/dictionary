Word: συνακολουθουσαι

Etymology:
The word συνακολουθουσαι is derived from the Greek words συν (syn) meaning "together" and ακολουθέω (akolouthéo) meaning "to follow". The word is a present participle form of the verb ακολουθέω.

Grammar:
συνακολουθουσαι is a feminine plural form of the present participle of the verb ακολουθέω. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description:
The word συνακολουθουσαι describes a group of people or things that are following or accompanying someone or something else. It can be translated to English as "following together" or "accompanying together". The word is often used in a religious context to describe a group of people who are following a particular religious leader or doctrine.
