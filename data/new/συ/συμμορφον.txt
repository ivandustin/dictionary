Word: συμμορφον

Etymology:
The word συμμορφον comes from the Greek roots σύν (syn) meaning "together" and μορφή (morphē) meaning "form" or "shape". Together, they form the word συμμορφον, which means "conforming" or "in agreement with".

Grammar:
συμμορφον is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
συμμορφον is a word that describes something that is in agreement with or conforms to a certain standard or expectation. It can be used to describe a person, thing, or idea that is compliant or obedient to a particular set of rules or guidelines. In a broader sense, it can also refer to something that is harmonious or consistent with a larger system or structure.
