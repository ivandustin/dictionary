Word: συνηλθον

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together"
ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come"

Therefore, συνηλθον (synelthon) means "they came together" or "they assembled".

Grammar:
συνηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
συνηλθον is a third-person plural form of the verb συνέρχομαι. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the gathering of people, such as in Matthew 18:20 where Jesus says, "For where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I in the midst of them."
