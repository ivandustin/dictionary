Word: συνεργον

Etymology: 
The word συνεργον comes from the Greek root words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ἔργον (ergon), meaning "work." Therefore, the word συνεργον literally means "working together."

Grammar:
συνεργον is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in the Greek language. It is declined in the first declension, and its nominative singular form is συνεργός (synergos).

Description:
συνεργον is a term used to describe someone who works together with others towards a common goal. It can refer to a collaborator, a partner, or a team member. In the context of ancient Greek philosophy, συνεργον was used to describe the concept of synergy, where the whole is greater than the sum of its parts. Today, the word συνεργον is still used in modern Greek to describe someone who works well with others and contributes to a team effort.
