Word: συστροφης

Etymology:
The word συστροφης comes from the Greek root words συν (syn) meaning "together" and στροφή (strophē) meaning "turning, twisting". Together, they form the word συστροφης, which means "a twisting or turning together".

Grammar:
In Greek, συστροφης is a feminine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is συστροφές.

Description:
συστροφης can refer to a physical twisting or turning, such as the twisting of a rope or the turning of a key in a lock. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a turning point or a change in direction. In music, συστροφης refers to a musical ornamentation technique where two notes are played in quick succession, creating a twisting or turning sound.
