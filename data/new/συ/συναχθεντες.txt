Word: συναχθεντες

Etymology:
The word συναχθεντες is a Greek word that is derived from the verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather together." The word is formed by adding the prefix συν- (syn-) which means "together" and the verb ἄγω (agō) which means "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar:
συναχθεντες is a participle in the passive voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb συνάγω (synagō) and the suffix -εντες (-entes), which is used to form participles in Greek. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
The word συναχθεντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 18:20, where it is translated as "gathered together" in English. The verse reads, "For where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I in the midst of them." The word conveys the idea of people coming together for a common purpose or goal.
