Word: συνμαρτυρει

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + μαρτυρει (martyrei, meaning "testifies"). Therefore, the word συνμαρτυρει means "testifies together" or "bears witness together".

Grammar: συνμαρτυρει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the Greek verb μαρτυρεω (martyreo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The prefix συν- (syn-) adds the meaning of "together" or "with".

Description: συνμαρτυρει is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the letters of Paul. It is often used to describe the Holy Spirit bearing witness with believers, or believers bearing witness together about their faith. The word emphasizes the idea of unity and cooperation in bearing witness to the truth.
