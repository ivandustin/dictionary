Word: συνησθιεν

Etymology:
The word συνησθιεν is derived from the Greek verb συναισθάνομαι (synaisthanomai), which means "to perceive together" or "to feel together." The prefix συν- (syn-) means "together," and the verb αἰσθάνομαι (aisthanomai) means "to perceive" or "to feel."

Grammar:
συνησθιεν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb συναισθάνομαι (synaisthanomai).

Description:
The word συνησθιεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Mark 7:2-5 and Acts 10:14. In both instances, it is translated as "eat together" or "eat with." The word implies a sense of community and fellowship, as it refers to the act of sharing a meal with others.
