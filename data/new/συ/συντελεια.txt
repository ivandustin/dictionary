Word: συντελεία

Etymology: The word συντελεία comes from the Greek root words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and τέλος (telos), meaning "end" or "goal." Therefore, συντελεία can be translated as "completion" or "culmination."

Grammar: συντελεία is a feminine noun in Greek, and its plural form is συντελείες. It is often used in the context of the completion of a task or the end of a process.

Description: συντελεία refers to the final stage or completion of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as the completion of a project, the end of a performance, or the conclusion of a story. In Greek grammar, it is often used as the object of a verb or as the subject of a sentence.
