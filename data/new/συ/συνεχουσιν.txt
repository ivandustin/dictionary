Word: συνεχουσιν

Etymology:
The word συνεχουσιν is a present active participle of the Greek verb συνέχω (synechō), which means "to hold together" or "to press together". The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb ἔχω (echō), meaning "to hold".

Grammar:
συνεχουσιν is a present active participle in the third person plural form. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as independent verbs. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
The word συνεχουσιν can be translated as "they are holding together" or "they are pressing together". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a group of people who are united or working together towards a common goal. In the New Testament, the word is used in several passages to describe the unity of believers in Christ, such as in Colossians 2:19: "And not holding fast to the Head, from whom all the body, nourished and knit together by joints and ligaments, grows with the increase that is from God."
