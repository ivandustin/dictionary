Word: συνεκλείσεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together" or "with"
εκλείσεν (ekleisen) meaning "closed"

Therefore, συνεκλείσεν (synekleisen) means "closed together" or "closed with".

Grammar:
συνεκλείσεν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the Greek verb κλείω (kleio), meaning "to close". The prefix συν- (syn-) adds the meaning of "together" or "with".

Description:
συνεκλείσεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Luke 4:20, where it describes how Jesus closed the book of Isaiah after reading from it in the synagogue. The word conveys the idea of a deliberate and intentional action of closing something.
