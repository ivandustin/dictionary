Word: συναγαγοντες

Etymology: συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and ἄγω (agō) meaning "to lead" or "to bring". The word is a combination of the prefix and the verb in the aorist participle form.

Grammar: συναγαγοντες is the aorist participle form of the Greek verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather together" or "to assemble". The word is in the masculine plural nominative case, indicating that the subject of the verb is a group of men.

Description: συναγαγοντες is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (18:20) and the Acts of the Apostles (20:7). In both instances, it refers to a group of people coming together for a specific purpose, such as for prayer or to hear a message. The word emphasizes the idea of unity and cooperation among the members of the group.
