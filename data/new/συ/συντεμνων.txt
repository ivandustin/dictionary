Word: συντεμνων

Etymology:
The word συντεμνων is derived from the Greek verb συντέμνω (syntémnō), which means "to cut off" or "to sever". It is formed by combining the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together" or "with", and the verb τέμνω (témnō), meaning "to cut".

Grammar:
συντεμνων is the present participle form of the verb συντέμνω (syntémnō). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description:
συντεμνων can be translated as "cutting off" or "severing". It describes someone who is currently engaged in the act of cutting or severing something. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as cutting off a branch from a tree, or in a figurative sense, such as severing ties with someone.
