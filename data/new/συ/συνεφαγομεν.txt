Word: συνεφαγομεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) meaning "together"
ἐφάγομεν (ephagomen) meaning "we ate"

Therefore, συνεφαγομεν (synephagomen) means "we ate together" or "we shared a meal".

Grammar:
συνεφαγομεν is a verb in the aorist tense, indicative mood, and first person plural form. It is derived from the verb ἐσθίω (esthio) meaning "to eat".

Description:
συνεφαγομεν is a Greek word that describes the act of sharing a meal with others. It implies a sense of community and togetherness, as well as the act of nourishing oneself. The word is commonly used in religious contexts, particularly in reference to the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with his disciples.
