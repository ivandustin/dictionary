Word: συνηκατε

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + ἥκω (hēkō, meaning "to have come"). The word is a compound of the prefix συν- and the verb ἥκω.

Grammar: συνηκατε is a second person plural verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It is derived from the verb συνίημι (syníēmi), which means "to understand together" or "to comprehend jointly". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: συνηκατε is a verb that means "you understood together" or "you comprehended jointly". It implies a shared understanding or comprehension among a group of people.
