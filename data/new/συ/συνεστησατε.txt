Word: συνεστησατε

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + ἵστημι (hístēmi, meaning "to stand") + -ατε (-ate, a suffix indicating second person plural aorist indicative active). Therefore, συνεστησατε means "you all stood together" or "you all established together".

Grammar: συνεστησατε is a second person plural aorist indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. It is composed of the prefix συν- (syn-) indicating "together", the root ἵστημι (hístēmi) meaning "to stand", and the suffix -ατε (-ate) indicating second person plural. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: συνεστησατε is a verb that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Philippians 1:27. It is used by the apostle Paul to encourage the Philippians to stand firm in their faith and to work together in unity. The word emphasizes the importance of unity and cooperation among believers.
