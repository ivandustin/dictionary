Word: συνομορουσα

Etymology: The word συνομορουσα is derived from the Greek words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and ὁμορρέω (homorrheo) meaning "to flow together". 

Grammar: συνομορουσα is a feminine singular participle in the present active voice. It is formed by adding the prefix συν- to the verb stem ὁμορρέω and the ending -ουσα. 

Description: συνομορουσα is a Greek word that means "flowing together". It is often used in the context of rivers or streams that merge into one. In grammar, it is a participle that describes an action that is happening at the same time as the main verb.
