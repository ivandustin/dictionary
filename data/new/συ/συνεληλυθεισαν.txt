Word: συνεληλυθεισαν

Etymology:
The word συνεληλυθεισαν is a Greek word that is derived from the verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." The word is formed by adding the prefix συν- (syn-), which means "together," to the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), which means "to come."

Grammar:
συνεληλυθεισαν is a verb in the perfect tense, passive voice, and plural number. The perfect tense indicates that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The plural number indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description:
The word συνεληλυθεισαν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Acts (Acts 15:22). It refers to a group of people who have come together or assembled for a specific purpose. In this context, the word is used to describe a group of leaders who have gathered to discuss and make decisions about the requirements for Gentiles who want to become followers of Jesus Christ.
