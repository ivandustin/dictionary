Word: συνερχομενοι

Etymology: συνερχομενοι is a present participle of the Greek verb συνέρχομαι (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble". The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: συνερχομενοι is a present participle in the masculine plural form. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντ- (-ont-) to the stem of the verb. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description: συνερχομενοι is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe a group of people who are coming together for a specific purpose, such as for prayer or worship. It is often translated as "assembling" or "gathering". The word emphasizes the communal aspect of the action, highlighting the importance of coming together as a group.
