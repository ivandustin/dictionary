Word: συνεχει

Etymology: The word συνεχει comes from the Greek root words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and εχει (echei), meaning "to have." Together, they form the verb συνεχω (synecho), which means "to hold together" or "to keep together."

Grammar: συνεχει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is often used in the context of describing something that is continuous or ongoing.

Description: συνεχει can be translated to mean "he/she/it continues" or "he/she/it is continuing." It is often used to describe an action or process that is ongoing or persistent. For example, one might say "ο καρκίνος συνεχει να εξαπλώνεται" (o karkinos synechei na exaplonei), which means "cancer continues to spread." 

Overall, συνεχει is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe ongoing actions or processes.
