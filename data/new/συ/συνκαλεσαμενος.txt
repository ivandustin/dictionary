Word: συνκαλεσαμενος

Etymology:
The word συνκαλεσαμενος is a compound word in Greek, formed by combining the prefix συν- (meaning "together") with the verb καλέω (meaning "to call"). The suffix -μενος is a participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and passive voice.

Grammar:
συνκαλεσαμενος is a past participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb καλέω, which means "to call." The prefix συν- indicates that the action of calling was done together with someone else.

Description:
The word συνκαλεσαμενος can be translated to mean "having been called together." It is often used in the context of a group of people being summoned or called together for a specific purpose. The word implies a sense of collaboration and cooperation, as the action of calling was done together with others.
