Word: συνερχησθε

Etymology:
συνερχησθε is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: συν (syn), meaning "together," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come." Therefore, the literal meaning of συνερχησθε is "to come together."

Grammar:
συνερχησθε is a second-person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to come together.

Description:
συνερχησθε is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels and the Book of Acts. It is used to describe the gathering of people, either for a specific purpose or simply to be together. In the context of the Bible, it is often used to describe the gathering of believers for worship or prayer. However, it can also be used in a more general sense to describe any group of people coming together for a common purpose.
