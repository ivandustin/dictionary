Word: συνηλθαν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) means "together" or "with"
ἔρχομαι (erchomai) means "to come"

Therefore, συνηλθαν (synelthan) means "they came together" or "they assembled."

Grammar:
συνηλθαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
συνηλθαν is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to describe a group of people coming together or assembling for a specific purpose. The word is often used in a religious context to describe the gathering of believers for worship or prayer.
