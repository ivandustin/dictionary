Word: συντριμμα

Etymology:
The word συντριμμα (syntimma) comes from the Greek verb συντρίβω (syntribō), which means "to break into pieces" or "to shatter." The suffix -μμα (-mma) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the result or product of an action. Therefore, συντριμμα (syntimma) means "a broken piece" or "a fragment."

Grammar:
In Greek, συντριμμα (syntimma) is a neuter noun. It is declined like other neuter nouns, with the nominative singular form being συντριμμα (syntimma) and the genitive singular form being συντρίμματος (syntimmatos).

Description:
συντριμμα (syntimma) refers to a broken piece or fragment of something. It can be used to describe physical objects that have been broken into pieces, such as a broken vase or a shattered window. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person or thing that has been broken or destroyed, such as a shattered dream or a broken heart.
