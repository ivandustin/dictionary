Word: συνιστανοντες

Etymology: συνιστανοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb συνίστημι (synístēmi), which means "to place together, to set in order, to recommend." The word is formed by combining the prefix συν- (syn-), which means "together," and the verb ἵστημι (hístēmi), which means "to stand."

Grammar: συνιστανοντες is the present participle of the verb συνίστημι (synístēmi) in Greek. It is in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. In English, it can be translated as "those who recommend" or "those who are recommending."

Description: συνιστανοντες is a word that refers to people who recommend or endorse something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in business, politics, or personal relationships. The word implies that the people who are recommending something have a certain level of authority or expertise in the matter.
