Word: συνηγαγομεν

Etymology:
συν- (syn-) = together
ἀγαγεῖν (agagein) = to lead, bring

συνηγαγομεν (synēgagomen) is the first person plural form of the verb συναγαγεῖν (synagagein), which means "to gather together" or "to collect." It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb ἀγαγεῖν (agagein), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar:
συνηγαγομεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The first person plural form indicates that the subject is "we" and that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description:
συνηγαγομεν can be translated as "we gathered together" or "we collected." It implies that a group of people came together to gather or collect something. The context of the sentence would determine what was being gathered or collected.
