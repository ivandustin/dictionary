Word: συνεζευξεν

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + εζευξεν (ezeuxen, meaning "yoked" or "joined"). The word comes from the Greek verb ζεύγνυμι (zeugnumi, meaning "to yoke" or "to join").

Grammar: συνεζευξεν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular, active voice, indicative mood. It is formed by adding the prefix συν- to the verb εζευξεν.

Description: συνεζευξεν means "he/she/it yoked together" or "he/she/it joined together" in Greek. It is often used in a literal sense, such as yoking animals together, but can also be used metaphorically to describe the joining of people or things.
