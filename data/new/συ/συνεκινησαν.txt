Word: συνεκινησαν

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + εκινησαν (ekinēsan, meaning "they moved"). The word is derived from the Greek verb κινέω (kineō, meaning "to move").

Grammar: συνεκινησαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. It is a compound verb, formed by adding the prefix συν- to the verb εκινησαν.

Description: συνεκινησαν means "they moved together" or "they were moved together". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a group of people who are moved emotionally or spiritually in the same way. The word is commonly used in religious contexts, particularly in the New Testament of the Bible.
