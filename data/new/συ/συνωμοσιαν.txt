Word: συνωμοσίαν (synomosian)

Etymology:
The word συνωμοσίαν is derived from the Greek words σύν (syn) meaning "together" and ὁμός (homos) meaning "same" or "equal". The word συνωμοσίαν literally means "conspiracy" or "plotting together".

Grammar:
συνωμοσίαν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
συνωμοσίαν refers to a secret agreement or plan made by a group of people to do something unlawful or harmful. It can also refer to a group of people who are involved in such a plan. The word is often used in a political context to describe a group of people who are plotting to overthrow a government or to gain power.
