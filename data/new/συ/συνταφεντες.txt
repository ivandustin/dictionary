Word: συνταφεντες

Etymology: The word συνταφεντες is derived from the Greek words συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and ταφή (tafí), meaning "burial." The word is a compound of these two words and means "buried together."

Grammar: συνταφεντες is a plural past participle of the verb συντάσσω (syntássō), which means "to bury together." The word is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: συνταφεντες refers to a group of people who were buried together in the same grave or tomb. This word is often used in reference to soldiers who died in battle and were buried together. It can also be used more generally to describe any group of people who were buried together, such as family members or friends.
