Word: συμβιβαζων

Etymology: συμβιβαζων is the present participle of the Greek verb συμβιβαζω (sumbibazō), which means "to reconcile, to bring together, to harmonize." The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb βιβαζω (bibazō), meaning "to cause to stand, to set up."

Grammar: συμβιβαζων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe a person or thing that is currently reconciling, bringing together, or harmonizing.

Description: συμβιβαζων can be translated as "reconciling" or "harmonizing" in English. It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe the process of bringing together opposing ideas or beliefs. In modern Greek, the word can also be used to describe a mediator or peacemaker.
