Word: συντηρούνται

Etymology: The word συντηρούνται comes from the Greek verb συντηρέω (syntereo), which means "to preserve, maintain, keep in good condition". It is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb τηρέω (tireo), meaning "to keep, guard".

Grammar: συντηρούνται is a present passive indicative verb in Greek. It is the third person plural form, which means "they are being preserved/maintained". The verb stem is συντηρ-, and the ending -ούνται indicates the passive voice and the present tense.

Description: συντηρούνται is a verb that describes the action of preserving or maintaining something in good condition. It can be used in various contexts, such as the preservation of historical monuments, the maintenance of machinery, or the upkeep of personal health. The passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb, rather than performing it themselves.
