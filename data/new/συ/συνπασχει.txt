Word: συνπασχει

Etymology: συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and πάσχειν (paschein) meaning "to suffer". Therefore, συνπασχει (synpaschei) means "to suffer together".

Grammar: συνπασχει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the Greek verb πάσχειν (paschein) which means "to suffer". The prefix συν- (syn-) adds the meaning of "together" to the verb.

Description: συνπασχει is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of sharing in someone else's suffering or pain. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the idea of sharing in the suffering of Christ or other religious figures. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the idea of empathy or compassion for others who are suffering.
