Word: συντυχειν

Etymology:
The word συντυχειν comes from the Greek roots συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and τυχειν (tychein), meaning "to happen" or "to chance." Therefore, the literal meaning of συντυχειν is "to happen together" or "to chance upon together."

Grammar:
συντυχειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The present active infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb in a general sense, without specifying the tense or the subject.

Description:
συντυχειν is a Greek verb that can be translated as "to happen together," "to coincide," or "to meet by chance." It is often used to describe unexpected or coincidental events that occur simultaneously or in close proximity to each other. For example, if two friends happen to run into each other at a coffee shop, they could say συντυχειν to describe the chance encounter. The word can also be used in a more abstract sense to describe the convergence of different ideas or events.
