Word: συνπαθεις

Etymology:
The word συνπαθεις is derived from two Greek words: συν (syn) meaning "together" and παθεις (patheis) meaning "suffering" or "passion". Therefore, the literal meaning of συνπαθεις is "together suffering" or "co-suffering".

Grammar:
συνπαθεις is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine, plural form, and it is in the nominative case. The word is used to describe a group of people who share the same feelings or emotions.

The word συνπαθεις is often used to describe a group of people who share a common experience or emotion. It is often used in a positive sense to describe a group of people who are united in their feelings of empathy or compassion towards others.
