Word: συναξω

Etymology:
The word συναξω (synaxō) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb ἄγω (agō), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." Therefore, συναξω means "to gather together" or "to assemble."

συναξω is a Greek verb that describes the act of gathering together or assembling. It is often used in a religious context to refer to the gathering of believers for worship or prayer.
