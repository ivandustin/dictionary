Word: συνηγμένα

Etymology:
The word συνηγμένα is derived from the Greek verb συνάγω (synagō), which means "to gather together" or "to collect." The suffix -μένα (-mena) is a participle ending that indicates a passive voice, meaning that the word συνηγμένα can be translated as "gathered together" or "collected."

Grammar:
συνηγμένα is a neuter plural adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In Greek, adjectives usually follow the noun they modify.

Description:
συνηγμένα is an adjective that describes a group of things that have been gathered together or collected. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as books or papers, or abstract concepts, such as ideas or data. The word implies a sense of organization or order, as the things that have been gathered together are likely to be related in some way.
