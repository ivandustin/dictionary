Word: συνδοξασθωμεν

Etymology: συν- (syn-, meaning "together") + δοξάζω (doxazo, meaning "to glorify") + -θωμεν (-thomen, a first person plural present subjunctive ending). The word is a compound of these three elements and means "let us be glorified together".

Grammar: συνδοξασθωμεν is a first person plural present subjunctive form of the verb συνδοξάζω (syndoxazo), which means "to glorify together". The subjunctive mood is used to express a wish, a suggestion, or a hypothetical situation. In this case, the form συνδοξασθωμεν expresses a wish or a suggestion that we should be glorified together.

Description: συνδοξασθωμεν is a Greek verb that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 8:17. The verse reads: "And if children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ; if so be that we suffer with him, that we may be also glorified together."
