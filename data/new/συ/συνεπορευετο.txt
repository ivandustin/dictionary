Word: συνεπορευετο

Etymology: συν- (together) + ἐπορεύετο (he/she/it was traveling)

Grammar: συνεπορευετο is a verb in the imperfect tense, third person singular, active voice, indicative mood in Ancient Greek.

Description: συνεπορευετο means "he/she/it was traveling together" in Ancient Greek. The verb is composed of the prefix συν- which means "together" and the verb ἐπορεύετο which means "he/she/it was traveling". The imperfect tense indicates that the action was ongoing in the past, without specifying whether it was completed or not.
