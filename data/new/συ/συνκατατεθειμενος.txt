The word "συνκατατεθειμενος" is a Greek adjective that is derived from the verb "συνκατατίθημι" (synkatatithemi), which means "to agree together" or "to arrange together." The prefix "συν" (syn) means "together," and the root "κατατίθημι" (katatithemi) means "to place" or "to set."

In terms of grammar, "συνκατατεθειμενος" is a masculine singular participle in the perfect tense. It is often used as an adjective to describe someone who has agreed or arranged something together with others.

Overall, "συνκατατεθειμενος" can be translated to mean "having been agreed together" or "having been arranged together."
