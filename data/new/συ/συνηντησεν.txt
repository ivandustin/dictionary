Word: συνηντησεν

Etymology: συν- (syn-) meaning "together" and ἀντι- (anti-) meaning "against" or "opposite", combined with the verb ἐντυγχάνω (entugchano) meaning "to meet". Therefore, συνηντησεν (synentēsen) means "he/she/it met together with".

Grammar: συνηντησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb συναντάω (synantáō), which means "to meet together with". In this form, it refers to a single past action performed by a third person subject.

Description: συνηντησεν is a Greek verb that describes the act of meeting together with someone or something. It is often used in a literal sense, such as meeting someone in person, but can also be used in a figurative sense, such as meeting a challenge or obstacle. The aorist tense indicates that the action was completed in the past, without specifying its duration or repetition.
