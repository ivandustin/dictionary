The word "συνκακοπαθησον" is a compound Greek verb that combines the prefix "συν-" meaning "together" or "with" and the verb "κακοπαθέω" meaning "to suffer evil, to be ill". Therefore, "συνκακοπαθησον" means "suffer together" or "endure hardship together". 

In terms of grammar, "συνκακοπαθησον" is a second person singular aorist imperative verb, which means it is a command directed towards one person to perform a specific action in the past. 

Overall, "συνκακοπαθησον" is a word that emphasizes the importance of solidarity and mutual support in times of hardship.
