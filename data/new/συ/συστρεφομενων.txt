Word: συστρεφομενων

Etymology: συστρεφομενων is a present participle form of the Greek verb συστρέφω (systrepho), which means "to twist together, to turn around, to wind up". The word is derived from the prefix συν- (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb τρέφω (trepho), meaning "to nourish, to feed".

Grammar: συστρεφομενων is a present participle in the passive voice, masculine, plural, genitive case. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the present stem of the verb συστρέφω (systrepho).

Description: συστρεφομενων can be translated as "of those who are being twisted together" or "of those who are being turned around". The word is often used in the context of physical twisting or turning, but it can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where people or things are being brought together or united in some way.
