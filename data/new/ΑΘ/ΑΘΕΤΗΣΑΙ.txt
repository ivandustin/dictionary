Etymology: The word "αθετησαι" (athetēsai) comes from the Greek verb "αθετώ" (athetō), which means "to reject" or "to invalidate."

Grammar: "Αθετησαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "αθετώ" (athetō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition.

Description: "Αθετησαι" is a verb in the Greek language that means "to reject" or "to invalidate." It is used to indicate the act of refusing to accept or acknowledge something, or declaring something as invalid or null.
