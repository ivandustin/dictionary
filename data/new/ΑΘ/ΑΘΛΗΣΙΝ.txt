Etymology: The word "αθλησιν" (athlēsin) comes from the Greek word "αθλησις" (athlēsis), which means "contest" or "competition". It is derived from the verb "αθλεω" (athleō), meaning "to contend" or "to strive".

Grammar: "Αθλησιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Αθλησιν" refers to the act of competing or participating in a contest or competition. It can also be used to describe the overall concept of sports or athletic activities.
