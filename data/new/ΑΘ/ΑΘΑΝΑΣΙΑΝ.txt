Etymology: The word "αθανασίαν" comes from the Greek word "αθάνατος" (athanatos), meaning "immortal" or "deathless". The suffix "-ίαν" (-ian) is added to form the noun "αθανασίαν" (athanasian), which means "immortality" or "eternity".

Grammar: "αθανασίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "αθανασίαν" refers to the concept of immortality or eternal life. It is often used in a philosophical or religious context to describe the state of being free from death or the ability to live forever.
