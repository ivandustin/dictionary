Etymology: The word "αθεσμων" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (meaning "without" or "not") and the noun "θεσμοί" (meaning "laws" or "rules").

Grammar: "αθεσμων" is a Greek adjective in the masculine singular form. It belongs to the third declension and is used to describe something or someone as "lawless" or "without rules".

Description: The word "αθεσμων" is used to describe a person or a situation that does not conform to established laws or rules. It implies a lack of order, discipline, or adherence to societal norms.
