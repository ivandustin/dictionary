Etymology: The word "αθετων" (atheton) comes from the Greek verb "αθετώ" (atheto), which means "to reject" or "to invalidate." 

Grammar: "Αθετων" is the plural form of the noun "αθετον" (atheton) in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a neuter noun. 

Description: "Αθετων" is a term used in legal contexts to refer to documents or contracts that have been declared null and void, or invalidated. It signifies that something has been rejected or set aside due to legal reasons.
