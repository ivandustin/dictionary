Etymology: The word "αθετησις" comes from the Greek verb "αθετώ" (athetó), which means "to reject" or "to invalidate". 

Grammar: "Αθετησις" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Αθετησις" refers to the act of rejecting or invalidating something, particularly in a legal or formal context. It can also be used to describe the act of disregarding or disowning something that was previously accepted or acknowledged.
