Etymology: The word "μετεπειτα" (metepeita) comes from the Greek words "μετά" (meta), meaning "after," and "επειτα" (epeita), meaning "then." 

Grammar: "Μετεπειτα" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate that something happened or will happen after a certain point in time.

Description: "Μετεπειτα" is used to refer to something that occurs or will occur in the future, after a specific event or time. It is often used to express a sequence of events or to indicate a change that will happen later.
