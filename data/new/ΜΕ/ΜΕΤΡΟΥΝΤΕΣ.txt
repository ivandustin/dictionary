Etymology: The word "μετρούντες" comes from the Greek verb "μετρώ" (metró), which means "to measure" or "to count". It is derived from the Greek noun "μέτρον" (métron), meaning "measure" or "meter".

Grammar: "Μετρούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "μετρώ" (metró) in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action of measuring or counting in the present tense. In English, it can be translated as "measuring" or "counting".

Description: "Μετρούντες" refers to a group of people or objects that are currently engaged in the act of measuring or counting something. It implies an ongoing action or process of determining quantities or dimensions.
