μεμβρανας (membranas) is a Greek word that can be translated to "membrane" in English. Here is the information you requested:

Etymology: The word "μεμβρανας" comes from the Greek word "μέμβρανα" (membra) which means "membrane" or "thin layer".

Grammar: In Greek, "μεμβρανας" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: A membrane is a thin, flexible layer that separates two spaces or substances. It can be found in various biological structures, such as cells, organs, and tissues, where it acts as a barrier or a selective filter. Membranes can be composed of different materials, including lipid bilayers, proteins, and polysaccharides. They play a crucial role in various physiological processes, such as transport, signaling, and protection.
