Etymology: The word "μετοικιώ" (metoikió) comes from the Greek root "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "οἰκία" (oikía), meaning "house" or "dwelling." It refers to the act of moving or resettling to a new place or country.

Grammar: "μετοικιώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and persons to indicate different actions related to moving or resettling.

Description: "μετοικιώ" describes the action of relocating or resettling to a new place or country. It can refer to both individuals or groups of people who move from one location to another for various reasons, such as seeking better opportunities, escaping conflict or persecution, or simply for a change of environment.
