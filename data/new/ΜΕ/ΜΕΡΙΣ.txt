Etymology: The word "μερις" (meris) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "μερίζω" (merizo), which means "to divide" or "to share."

Grammar: In Greek, "μερις" (meris) is a noun. It is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative, to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Μερις" (meris) refers to a part, portion, or division of something. It can also mean a share or a portion that is distributed or allocated among individuals. The word is often used in contexts related to dividing or sharing resources, responsibilities, or tasks.
