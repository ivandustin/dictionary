μετακαλέσαι (metakalésai) is a Greek verb that means "to call back" or "to recall." It is derived from the combination of the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "back" or "again," and the verb καλέω (kaléo), meaning "to call." In Greek grammar, μετακαλέσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.
