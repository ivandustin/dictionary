μεταβεβηκέν (metabebeken) is a Greek word that means "to have passed through" or "to have gone beyond." It is derived from the combination of the prefix μετα- (meta-), meaning "beyond" or "through," and the verb βαίνω (baino), meaning "to go" or "to pass." 

In terms of grammar, μεταβεβηκέν is the perfect tense form of the verb μεταβαίνω (metabaino), which means "to pass over" or "to go beyond." The perfect tense indicates that the action of passing through or going beyond has been completed in the past.

Overall, μεταβεβηκέν is a word that describes the act of surpassing or going beyond a certain point or limit.
