Etymology: The word "μετεστησεν" comes from the Greek verb "μεταστέλλω" (metastellō), which means "to change" or "to alter."

Grammar: "μετεστησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist active indicative verb "μεταστέλλω" (metastellō) in Greek. It indicates that the action of changing or altering was performed by a third person in the past.

Description: "μετεστησεν" is a Greek word that describes the act of changing or altering something. It can refer to a physical change or a change in circumstances or conditions.
