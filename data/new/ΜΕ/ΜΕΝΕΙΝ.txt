Etymology: The word "μενειν" (menein) comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar: "Μενειν" is the present tense form of the verb "μένω" (meno) in the Greek language. It is the third person singular form, which means it is used when referring to a single subject that is staying or remaining.

Description: "Μενειν" is a Greek word that expresses the action of staying or remaining in a particular place or condition. It can be used to describe physical or metaphorical situations where someone or something remains in a specific state or location.
