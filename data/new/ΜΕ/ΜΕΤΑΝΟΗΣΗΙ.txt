Etymology: The word "μετανόηση" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "νόος" (noos), meaning "mind" or "thought." Therefore, "μετανόηση" can be translated as "change of mind" or "repentance."

Grammar: "Μετανόηση" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Μετανόηση" refers to the act of changing one's mind, thoughts, or attitudes, particularly in the context of regret or remorse for past actions. It implies a deep sense of reflection, acknowledgement of wrongdoing, and a sincere desire to make amends or improve oneself. In various religious and philosophical contexts, "μετανόηση" is often associated with seeking forgiveness, redemption, or spiritual transformation.
