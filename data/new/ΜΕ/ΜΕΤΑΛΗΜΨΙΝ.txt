Etymology: The word "μεταλημψιν" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "λημψις" (lēmpsis), meaning "reception" or "taking." 

Grammar: "μεταλημψιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "μεταλημψιν" refers to the act of receiving or taking something after or beyond a certain point. It can also imply the concept of acquiring or obtaining something.
