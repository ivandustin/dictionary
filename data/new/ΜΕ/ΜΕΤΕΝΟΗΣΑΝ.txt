μετενοησαν (metenoisan) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix "μετα-" (meta-), meaning "change" or "after," and the verb "νοέω" (noeo), meaning "to think" or "to perceive." 

In terms of grammar, μετενοησαν is the third person plural, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a whole, without any specific duration. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

As for its meaning, μετενοησαν translates to "they repented" or "they changed their mind." It is often used in a religious or moral context to describe a change of heart or a turning away from previous beliefs or actions.
