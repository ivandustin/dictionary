Etymology: The word "μερος" (meros) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *mer- meaning "to divide" or "to share". 

Grammar: In Greek, "μερος" (meros) is a noun. It is a neuter noun of the second declension. It can be used in both singular and plural forms. 

Description: "Μερος" (meros) means "part" or "portion" in Greek. It refers to a piece or a section of something that is separated or divided from the whole. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate a role, a share, or a contribution in a broader context.
