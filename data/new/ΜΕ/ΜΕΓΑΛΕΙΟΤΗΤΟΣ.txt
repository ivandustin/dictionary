μεγαλειότητα (megaleiotita)

Etymology:
The word μεγαλειότητα comes from the Greek root words μεγάλος (megalos) meaning "great" or "big" and εἶδος (eidos) meaning "form" or "shape". The suffix -τητα (-tita) is used to form abstract nouns in Greek. Therefore, μεγαλειότητα can be translated as "greatness" or "grandeur".

Grammar:
μεγαλειότητα is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. Adjectives and other modifiers must agree with the noun in gender, number, and case.

Description:
μεγαλειότητα refers to the quality or state of being great, magnificent, or majestic. It is often used to describe something that is impressive, awe-inspiring, or of significant importance. This word can be used to describe physical objects, events, achievements, or even abstract concepts.
