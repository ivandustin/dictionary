Etymology: The word "μενητε" (menete) comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar: "μενητε" is the second person plural form of the verb "μένω" (meno) in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all stay" or "you all remain".
