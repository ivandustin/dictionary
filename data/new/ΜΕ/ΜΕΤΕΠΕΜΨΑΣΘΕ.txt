Etymology: The word "μετεπεμψάσθε" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by combining the prefix "μετα-" (meaning "after" or "beyond") and the verb "ἐπεμψάσθαι" (meaning "to send" or "to dispatch").

Grammar: "μετεπεμψάσθε" is a verb in the second person plural, aorist middle voice form in Greek. The aorist tense generally refers to a past action, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit.

Description: The word "μετεπεμψάσθε" can be translated as "you sent yourselves after" or "you dispatched yourselves beyond." It suggests an action of sending or dispatching oneself to a location or situation that is beyond or after the current one.
