Etymology: The word "μεμαθηκως" (memathēkōs) comes from the Greek verb "μανθανω" (manthanō), which means "to learn" or "to study". The prefix "μεμα" (mem-) indicates that the action of learning has been completed or perfected.

Grammar: "Μεμαθηκως" is the adverbial form of the verb "μανθανω" (manthanō). It is used to describe the manner in which something is learned or studied.

Description: "Μεμαθηκως" is an adverb that means "learnedly" or "with knowledge". It is used to indicate that someone has learned or studied something thoroughly and has a deep understanding of it.
