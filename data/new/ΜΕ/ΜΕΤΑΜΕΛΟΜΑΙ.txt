Etymology: The word "μεταμελομαι" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "μέλος" (melos), meaning "limb" or "part." It literally translates to "to feel remorse after."

Grammar: "μεταμελομαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and persons to express different meanings and nuances.

Description: "μεταμελομαι" is a Greek word that describes the feeling of remorse or regret that arises after an action has been taken. It implies a sense of sorrow or repentance for something that has been done. This word is often used to express a deep emotional response to a past event or decision.
