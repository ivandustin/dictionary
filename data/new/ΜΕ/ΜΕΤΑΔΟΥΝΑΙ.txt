Etymology: The word "μεταδουναί" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "δούναι" (dounai), meaning "to give." 

Grammar: "μεταδουναί" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "μεταδουναί" refers to gifts or offerings that are given after a sacrifice or religious ceremony in ancient Greek culture. These gifts were typically presented to the gods or to the priests as a form of gratitude or supplication.
