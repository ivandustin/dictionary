Etymology: The word "μενουσαν" (menousan) is a form of the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men-.

Grammar: "μενουσαν" is the present participle form of the verb "μένω" (meno) in the feminine singular accusative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action. The feminine singular accusative case is used when the participle is functioning as a direct object.

Description: "μενουσαν" can be translated as "staying" or "remaining" in English. It describes a female subject who is currently in the state of staying or remaining.
