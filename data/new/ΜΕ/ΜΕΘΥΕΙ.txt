Etymology: The word "μεθυεί" (methyee) comes from the Greek verb "μεθύω" (methyo), which means "to get drunk" or "to be intoxicated". 

Grammar: "Μεθυεί" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "μεθύω" and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Εγώ μεθύω (methyo) - I get drunk
- Εσύ μεθύεις (methyees) - You get drunk
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό μεθυεί (methyee) - He/She/It gets drunk
- Εμείς μεθυούμε (methyoome) - We get drunk
- Εσείς μεθύετε (methyete) - You (plural) get drunk
- Αυτοί/Αυτές μεθυούν (methyooun) - They get drunk
