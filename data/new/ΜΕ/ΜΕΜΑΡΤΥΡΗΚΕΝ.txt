Grammar: "μεμαρτυρηκέν" is the perfect active participle form of the verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureo). In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate an action that has been completed in the past with ongoing results in the present. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "μεμαρτυρηκέν" can be translated as "having testified" or "having borne witness." It is used to describe someone or something that has given testimony or provided evidence in the past.
