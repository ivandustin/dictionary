Etymology: The word "μεταμορφούμεθα" comes from the Greek root words "μετά" (meta), meaning "change" or "beyond," and "μορφή" (morphē), meaning "form" or "shape." The suffix "-ούμεθα" (-oumeta) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning "we are transformed."

Grammar: "Μεταμορφούμεθα" is a verb in the present tense, first person plural form. It is the reflexive form of the verb "μεταμορφόνω" (metamorphóno), which means "to transform" or "to change shape." The reflexive form indicates that the subject of the verb is also the object of the action, in this case, "we" are being transformed.

Description: "Μεταμορφούμεθα" describes the act of undergoing a transformation or a change in form. It implies a process of becoming something different or assuming a new shape. This word is often used metaphorically to describe personal growth, development, or a profound change in one's life or circumstances.
