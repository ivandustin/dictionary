Etymology: The word "μεταλαβείν" (metalevein) comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "λαβείν" (lavein), meaning "to take" or "to receive." 

Grammar: "μεταλαβείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: The word "μεταλαβείν" means "to understand" or "to comprehend" in Greek. It refers to the act of grasping or comprehending something intellectually.
