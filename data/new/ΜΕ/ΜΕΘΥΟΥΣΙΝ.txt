Etymology: The word "μεθυούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "μεθύω" (methyo), which means "to get drunk" or "to be intoxicated."

Grammar: "Μεθυούσιν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "μεθύω" (methyo). It is used to describe the action of getting drunk or being intoxicated by a group of people.

Description: "Μεθυούσιν" is a Greek word that specifically refers to a group of people getting drunk or being intoxicated. It is commonly used in literature or poetry to depict scenes of revelry or excessive drinking.
