μερισμός (merismos) is a Greek word that can be translated as "division" or "partition" in English. It is derived from the verb μερίζω (merizo), which means "to divide" or "to share." In grammar, μερισμός refers to the division or separation of words or phrases into smaller units for analysis or classification. It is often used to describe the process of breaking down sentences or linguistic structures into their constituent parts.
