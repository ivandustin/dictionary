Etymology: The word "μετριοπαθείν" (metriopathin) comes from the Greek roots "μέτρον" (metron) meaning "measure" and "πάθος" (pathos) meaning "suffering" or "passion". 

Grammar: "μετριοπαθείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive form. 

Description: "μετριοπαθείν" is a Greek word that can be translated as "to moderate one's passions" or "to maintain a balanced emotional state". It refers to the act of controlling or restraining one's emotions and desires, avoiding extremes and finding a middle ground. It is often used in the context of self-control and emotional balance.
