Etymology: The word μετοικεσίας comes from the Greek root words μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and οἰκέω (oikeó), meaning "to dwell" or "to inhabit." 

Grammar: μετοικεσίας is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. It is derived from the noun μετοικεσία (metoikesía), which means "migration" or "relocation" in the nominative case. 

Description: μετοικεσίας refers to the act or process of moving from one place to another, typically with the intention of settling in the new location. It can be used to describe both human migration and the movement of animals or plants.
