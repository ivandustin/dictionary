Grammar: "μεμαρτυρηκας" is the second person singular form of the perfect tense in Greek. It is used to address someone directly and indicates that the action of testifying or bearing witness has been completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present.

Description: "μεμαρτυρηκας" is a word that emphasizes the act of testifying or bearing witness. It suggests that the person being addressed has already given their testimony or witnessed something important and that their testimony or witness has significance or impact.
