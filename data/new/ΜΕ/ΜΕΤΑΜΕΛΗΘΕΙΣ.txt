Etymology: The word "μεταμεληθείς" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "μέλος" (melos), meaning "limb" or "part." It is derived from the verb "μεταμελέομαι" (metameléomai), which means "to regret" or "to repent."

Grammar: "Μεταμεληθείς" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It is used to describe someone who has experienced regret or repentance.

Description: "Μεταμεληθείς" refers to the state of feeling remorse or regret for one's actions or decisions. It implies a sense of sorrow or repentance after the fact.
