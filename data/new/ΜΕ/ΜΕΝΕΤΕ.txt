Etymology: The word "μενετε" (menete) comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar: "μενετε" is the second person plural form of the present tense of the verb "μένω" (meno). It is used to address a group of people and means "you all stay" or "you all remain".

Description: "μενετε" is a verb in the Greek language that is used to express the action of staying or remaining. It is commonly used in various contexts to indicate that a group of people should continue to stay in a particular place or maintain a certain state.
