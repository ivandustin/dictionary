Etymology: The word "μεθυσκεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "μεθύω" (methyo), which means "to get drunk" or "to be intoxicated". 

Grammar: "μεθυσκεσθαι" is an infinitive verb form in the Greek language. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

Description: "μεθυσκεσθαι" refers to the act of getting drunk or becoming intoxicated. It is commonly used to describe the state of being under the influence of alcohol or other substances that impair one's mental and physical faculties.
