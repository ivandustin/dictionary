Etymology: The word "μετεχουσιν" comes from the Greek word "μετέχω" (metécho), which means "to participate" or "to share". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "μετά" (meta), meaning "with" or "among", and the verb "έχω" (echo), meaning "to have" or "to possess".

Grammar: "μετεχουσιν" is the present participle form of the verb "μετέχω" (metécho) in the third person plural. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "μετεχουσιν" is used to describe a group of people or entities who are currently participating or sharing in something. It implies an active involvement or engagement in a particular activity, event, or state.
