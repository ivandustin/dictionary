Etymology: The word "μεθυούσαν" (methyoúsan) comes from the Greek verb "μεθύω" (methýo), which means "to get drunk" or "to be intoxicated."

Grammar: "Μεθυούσαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "μεθύω" in the past continuous tense. It is used to describe an ongoing action of getting drunk or being intoxicated in the past.

Description: "Μεθυούσαν" is a Greek word that describes the action of multiple individuals getting drunk or being intoxicated in the past. It implies a continuous or ongoing state of intoxication.
