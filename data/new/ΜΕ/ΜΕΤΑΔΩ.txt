Etymology: The word "μεταδώ" (metadó) comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "δίδωμι" (dídomi), meaning "to give." Therefore, "μεταδώ" can be translated as "to give after" or "to transmit."

Grammar: "μεταδώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Εγώ μεταδίδω (ego metadído) - I transmit
- Εσύ μεταδίδεις (esý metadídeis) - You transmit
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό μεταδίδει (aftós/aftí/aftó metadídi) - He/She/It transmits
- Εμείς μεταδίδουμε (emeís metadídoume) - We transmit
- Εσείς μεταδίδετε (eseís metadídate) - You transmit
- Αυτοί/Αυτές μεταδίδουν (aftoí/aftés metadídoun) - They transmit

Description: "μεταδώ" refers to the act of transmitting or passing something from one person or place to another. It can be used in various contexts, such as transmitting information, knowledge, or a message.
