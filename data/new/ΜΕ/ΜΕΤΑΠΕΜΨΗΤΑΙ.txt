Etymology: The word "μεταπεμψηται" comes from the Greek language. It is a verb form derived from the root word "μεταπέμπομαι" (metapémpomai), which means "to send after" or "to dispatch."

Grammar: "μεταπεμψηται" is the third person singular, aorist middle/passive subjunctive form of the verb "μεταπέμπομαι" (metapémpomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on themselves (middle voice) or receiving the action (passive voice). The subjunctive mood suggests a hypothetical or potential action.

Description: "μεταπεμψηται" can be translated as "he/she/it might have been sent after" or "he/she/it might have been dispatched." It implies a possibility or potentiality of someone being sent or dispatched in the past.
