Etymology: The word "μεθυσος" (methysos) comes from the Greek word "μεθυω" (methyo), which means "to get drunk" or "to be intoxicated". 

Grammar: In Greek, "μεθυσος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "μεθυσου" (methysou). 

Description: "Μεθυσος" refers to a person who is drunk or intoxicated. It can also be used as an adjective to describe something related to drunkenness or intoxication.
