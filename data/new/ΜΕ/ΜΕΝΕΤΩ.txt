Etymology: The word "μενετω" (menetō) comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (menō), which means "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar: "μενετω" is a verb in the imperative mood, present tense, and active voice. It is the third person singular form of the verb "μένω" (menō).

Description: "μενετω" is used to give a command or instruction for someone to stay or remain in a certain place or state. It is often used in a figurative sense to encourage someone to persevere or endure in a particular situation.
