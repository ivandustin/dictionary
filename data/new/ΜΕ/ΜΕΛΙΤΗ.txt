Etymology: The word "μελίτη" (melítē) comes from the Greek word for "honey". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *mélit, meaning "honey".

Grammar: "Μελίτη" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "μελίτης" (melítēs). In the plural form, it becomes "μελίτες" (melítes).

Description: "Μελίτη" refers to honey in the Greek language. It is a sweet, viscous substance produced by bees from the nectar of flowers. Honey has been used as a food and sweetener for thousands of years and has various culinary and medicinal uses.
