Etymology: The word "μεριστην" comes from the Greek word "μερίζω" (merízo), which means "to divide" or "to share". It is derived from the noun "μέρος" (méros), meaning "part" or "portion".

Grammar: "μεριστην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "μεριστην" refers to someone who divides or shares something. It can be used to describe a person who distributes or allocates resources, responsibilities, or tasks among others.
