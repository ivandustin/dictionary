Etymology: The word "μετεκαλεσατο" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "καλέω" (kaleo), meaning "to call" or "to name." 

Grammar: "μετεκαλεσατο" is a verb in the third person singular form of the aorist tense in the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "μετακαλέω" (metakaleo), which means "to call by another name" or "to rename."

Description: "μετεκαλεσατο" is used to describe the action of renaming or calling something or someone by a different name. It implies a change in the name or designation of the subject.
