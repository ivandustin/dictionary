Etymology: The word "μεριμνών" comes from the Greek verb "μεριμνάω" (merimnao), which means "to be anxious" or "to worry". It is derived from the noun "μέριμνα" (merimna), meaning "care" or "concern".

Grammar: "Μεριμνών" is the present participle form of the verb "μεριμνάω" (merimnao) in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently worrying or being anxious.

Description: "Μεριμνών" refers to a person who is actively engaged in worrying or being anxious. It describes someone who is preoccupied with concerns, cares, or anxieties.
