Etymology: The word "μετασταθω" (metastatho) comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "στάθη" (stathi), meaning "to stand." 

Grammar: "μετασταθω" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: "μετασταθω" means "I change" or "I move" in Greek. It is used to express the action of moving or changing from one state or position to another.
