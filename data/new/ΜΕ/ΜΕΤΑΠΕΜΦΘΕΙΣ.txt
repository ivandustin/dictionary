Etymology: The word "μεταπεμφθείς" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "πέμπω" (pempo), meaning "to send." 

Grammar: "μεταπεμφθείς" is a verb form in the aorist passive participle tense in Greek. It is derived from the verb "μεταπέμπομαι" (metapempomai), which means "to be sent after" or "to be dispatched."

Description: "μεταπεμφθείς" refers to someone or something that has been sent or dispatched after a certain event or action. It indicates a passive action of being sent or summoned by someone else.
