Etymology: The word "μετανοιαν" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "μετανοέω" (metanoeo), which means "to change one's mind" or "to repent."

Grammar: "μετανοιαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "μετανοιαν" refers to the act of repentance or the change of mind and heart. It is often associated with remorse for past actions and a desire to change one's behavior or beliefs. In religious contexts, it is often used to describe the process of turning away from sin and seeking forgiveness.
