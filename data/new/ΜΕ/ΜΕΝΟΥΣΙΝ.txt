Etymology: The word "μενουσιν" (menousin) comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men-.

Grammar: "μενουσιν" is the present active participle, third person plural form of the verb "μένω" (meno). In Greek grammar, the present active participle is used to indicate ongoing action or state.

Description: "μενουσιν" is a Greek word that means "they stay" or "they remain". It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently staying or remaining in a particular place or state.
