Etymology: The word "μενομεν" comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". The suffix "-μεν" is added to the verb stem to form the present tense first person plural form.

Grammar: "μενομεν" is the present tense first person plural form of the verb "μένω" (meno). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated to indicate the subject, tense, and mood of the action. The first person plural form is used when the subject is "we" and the action is happening in the present.

Description: "μενομεν" is used to express the action of staying or remaining in the present tense. It is commonly used in sentences where the subject is "we" and the action is ongoing or continuous.
