Etymology: The word "μεταστησας" comes from the Greek verb "μεταστέλλω" (metastellō), which means "to change, to alter, to transform."

Grammar: "μεταστησας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "μεταστέλλω" (metastellō). It is used to describe an action that has been completed in the past.

Description: "μεταστησας" is a Greek word that means "having changed" or "having altered." It is used to indicate that something or someone has undergone a transformation or a change.
