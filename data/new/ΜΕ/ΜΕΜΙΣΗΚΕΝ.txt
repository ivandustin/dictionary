Etymology: The word "μεμισηκεν" comes from the Greek verb "μισέω" (miseo), which means "to hate" or "to detest". The prefix "με-" (me-) indicates a completed action, so "μεμισηκεν" can be translated as "has hated" or "has detested".

Grammar: "μεμισηκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "μισέω" in the perfect tense. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance in the present.

Description: "μεμισηκεν" describes the state of someone who has hated or detested something in the past and continues to hold that feeling in the present. It implies a strong and lasting dislike or aversion towards the object of hatred.
