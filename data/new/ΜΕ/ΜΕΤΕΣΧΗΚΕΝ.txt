μετεσχηματισμός (meteschimatismos) is a Greek word that can be translated as "transformation" in English. 

Etymology: The word μετεσχηματισμός is derived from the Greek roots μετά (meta), meaning "beyond" or "change," and εσχηματίζω (eschimatizo), meaning "to form" or "to shape." 

Grammar: μετεσχηματισμός is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns can have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: μετεσχηματισμός refers to the act or process of transforming or changing something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in science, mathematics, or even in everyday life, to describe a significant alteration or conversion.
