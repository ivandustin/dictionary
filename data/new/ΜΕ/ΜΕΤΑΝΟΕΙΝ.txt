Etymology: The word "μετανοείν" (metanoein) comes from the Greek roots "meta-" meaning "change" and "noos" meaning "mind". It literally translates to "change of mind".

Grammar: "μετανοείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive form.

Description: The word "μετανοείν" is often translated as "to repent" or "to change one's mind". It refers to a change in one's thinking or attitude, particularly in the context of regret or remorse for past actions. In religious or spiritual contexts, it can also refer to a change of heart or turning away from sin.
