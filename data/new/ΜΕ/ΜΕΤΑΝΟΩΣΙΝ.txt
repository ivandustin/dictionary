Etymology: The word "μετανοείν" (metanoein) comes from the Greek roots "meta-" meaning "change" and "noos" meaning "mind". It literally translates to "change of mind".

Grammar: "μετανοείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive form.

Description: The word "μετανοείν" is often translated as "to repent" or "to change one's mind". It refers to a change in one's thoughts, attitudes, or actions, particularly in the context of remorse or regret for past behavior. In religious or spiritual contexts, it can also imply a turning away from sin or wrongdoing and a commitment to a new way of life.
