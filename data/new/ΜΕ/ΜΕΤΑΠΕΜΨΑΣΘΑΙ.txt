Etymology: The word "μεταπεμψασθαι" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "πέμπω" (pempo), meaning "to send." 

Grammar: "μεταπεμψασθαι" is a verb in the aorist middle infinitive form of the Greek language. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description: "μεταπεμψασθαι" means "to send back" or "to refer" in English. It is used to describe the act of sending someone or something back to a previous location or referring them to someone else.
