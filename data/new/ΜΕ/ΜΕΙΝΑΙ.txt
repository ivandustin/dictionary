Etymology: The word "μειναί" comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men-.

Grammar: "μειναί" is the 3rd person singular aorist active subjunctive form of the verb "μένω" (meno). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the subjunctive mood indicates possibility or potentiality.

Description: "μειναί" is used to express the idea of "let him/her/it stay" or "may he/she/it remain". It is often used in commands, wishes, or hypothetical situations.
