Etymology: The word "μεταδοτω" (metadotō) comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "δίδωμι" (didōmi), meaning "to give." 

Grammar: "μεταδοτω" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and imperative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb.

Description: "μεταδοτω" means "give after" or "give beyond" in Greek. It is used to instruct or command someone to give or transmit something after a certain point or to a specific recipient.
