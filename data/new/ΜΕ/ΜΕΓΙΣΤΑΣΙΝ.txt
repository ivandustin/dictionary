Etymology: The word "μεγιστασιν" comes from the Greek word "μέγας" (megas), meaning "great" or "big", and the suffix "-στασιν" (-stasin), which denotes a state or condition. 

Grammar: "μεγιστασιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "μεγιστασιν" refers to a state or condition of greatness or bigness. It can be used to describe something that is very large or significant in size, importance, or influence.
