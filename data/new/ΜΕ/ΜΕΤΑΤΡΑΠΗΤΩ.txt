Etymology: The word "μετατραπητω" comes from the Greek verb "μετατρέπω" (metatrepo), which means "to transform" or "to change". It is derived from the prefix "μετά" (meta), meaning "beyond" or "after", and the verb "τρέπω" (trepo), meaning "to turn" or "to convert".

Grammar: "μετατραπητω" is the imperative form of the verb "μετατρέπω" (metatrepo) in the Greek language. The imperative form is used to give commands or make requests. In this form, it is addressing someone in the second person singular, instructing them to transform or change something.
