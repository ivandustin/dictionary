Etymology: The word "μετασχηματιζόμενοι" comes from the Greek word "μετασχηματίζω" (metaschēmatizō), which means "to transform" or "to change form". The suffix "-μενοι" (-menoi) indicates the plural form of the verb, indicating that the subject is multiple individuals who are transforming or changing form.

Grammar: "Μετασχηματιζόμενοι" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is used to describe multiple individuals who are currently undergoing a process of transformation or change.

Description: "Μετασχηματιζόμενοι" refers to a group of people or objects that are in the process of transforming or changing form. It implies a continuous action or state of transformation.
