Etymology: The word "μετακαλεσομαι" comes from the Greek roots "μετά" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "καλέω" (kaleo), meaning "to call." 

Grammar: "Μετακαλεσομαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and persons to express different meanings and nuances.

Description: "Μετακαλεσομαι" means "to call after" or "to summon." It can also be translated as "to invite" or "to call upon." It implies the act of calling someone to come or join, often for a specific purpose or gathering.
