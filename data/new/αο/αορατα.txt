Word: αορατα

Etymology:
The word αορατα (aorata) is derived from the Greek word αόρατος (aoratos), which means "invisible" or "unseen". The suffix -α at the end of the word indicates that it is plural.

Grammar:
αορατα is a neuter plural adjective in Greek. It is used to describe things that cannot be seen or are invisible.

Description:
αορατα is often used in a figurative sense to describe things that are not physically visible, such as emotions, thoughts, or spiritual entities. It can also be used to describe things that are hidden or obscured from view, such as secrets or mysteries. In Greek mythology, αορατα is often used to describe the realm of the gods, which is invisible to mortals.
