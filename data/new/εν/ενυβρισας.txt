Word: ενυβρισας

Etymology:
The word ενυβρισας is derived from the Greek verb "νυβρίζω" (nybrizo), which means "to insult" or "to treat with contempt". The prefix "εν" (en) in this word means "in" or "into", so ενυβρισας can be translated as "having insulted" or "having treated with contempt".

Grammar:
ενυβρισας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "νυβρίζω" (nybrizo).

Description:
ενυβρισας is a strong word that conveys a sense of disrespect and insult towards someone. It implies that the subject has intentionally and deliberately insulted or treated someone with contempt. The word is often used in a negative context and can be considered offensive.
