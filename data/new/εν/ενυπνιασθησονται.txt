Word: ενυπνιασθησονται

Etymology: The word ενυπνιασθησονται is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix ενυπνιασ- (enypniasthai) meaning "to dream" and the suffix -σονται (-sontai) indicating the future tense. Therefore, the word means "they will dream".

Grammar: ενυπνιασθησονται is a third-person plural future passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σονται to the passive form of the verb ενυπνιαζομαι (enypniazomai) which means "to dream". The verb is conjugated as follows:

ενυπνιασθησονται (enypniasthēsontai) - they will dream

Description: ενυπνιασθησονται is a Greek verb that means "they will dream". It is a future tense verb in the passive voice, indicating that the subject will receive the action of dreaming. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the act of dreaming or having a vision while asleep.
