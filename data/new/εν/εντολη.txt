Word: εντολη

Etymology:
The word εντολη (entolē) comes from the Greek verb εντελλω (entellō), which means "to order" or "to command". It is derived from the prefix εν- (en-), meaning "in" or "on", and the verb τελλω (tellō), meaning "to accomplish" or "to perform".

In Greek, εντολη is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is declined as follows:

Nominative: η εντολη (i entolē)
Genitive: της εντολης (tēs entolēs)
Accusative: την εντολην (tēn entolēn)
Vocative: εντολη (entolē)

Plural:

Genitive: των εντολων (tōn entolōn)
Accusative: τις εντολες (tis entoles)
Vocative: εντολες (entoles)

Description:
The word εντολη can be translated as "command", "order", "instruction", or "mandate". It is often used in the context of military or governmental orders, but can also refer to any kind of directive or request. In the New Testament, it is frequently used to refer to the commandments of God or Jesus Christ.
