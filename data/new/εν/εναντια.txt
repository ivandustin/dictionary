Word: εναντια

Etymology: The word εναντια comes from the Greek preposition "εναντίος" (enantios), which means "opposite" or "contrary".

Grammar: εναντια is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It is often used in the plural form, εναντία, which means "oppositions" or "contraries".

Description: εναντια is a word that describes something that is opposite or contrary to something else. It can be used to describe opposing viewpoints, conflicting ideas, or contrasting characteristics. In Greek, it is often used in political or philosophical contexts to describe opposing ideologies or beliefs.
