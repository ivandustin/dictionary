Word: ενεδραν

Etymology:
The word ενεδραν comes from the Greek word ενέδρα (enedra), which means "ambush" or "trap". It is derived from the verb ενεδρεύω (enedreuō), which means "to lie in wait" or "to ambush".

Grammar:
ενεδραν is a noun in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb. It is a singular noun, and its gender is feminine.

Description:
ενεδραν refers to an ambush or a trap that is set to catch someone or something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as in military tactics or hunting, or in a figurative sense, such as in a deceitful or cunning plan. The word implies a sense of danger and surprise, as the person or thing being ambushed is caught off guard.
