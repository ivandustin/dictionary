Word: εντολης

Etymology: The word εντολης comes from the Greek root word εντολή (entolé), which means "command" or "order". It is derived from the verb εντέλλω (entélo), which means "to command" or "to order".

Grammar: εντολης is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case in this form, which means it is used to show possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in a sentence.

Description: εντολης refers to a command or order given by someone in authority. It can also refer to a directive or instruction given by a higher power or deity. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to refer to the commandments of God.
