The word "ενταφιασμός" (entafiásmos) is a Greek noun that refers to the act of burying someone who has died. 

Etymology: The word comes from the combination of the prefix "εντα-" (enta-), which means "in" or "into," and the noun "τάφος" (táfos), which means "grave" or "tomb." 

Grammar: "Ενταφιασμός" is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "ενταφιασμοί" (entafiásmoi). 

Description: The word is commonly used in the context of funerals and mourning. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the end of something or the closure of a chapter in one's life.
