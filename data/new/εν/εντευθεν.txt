Word: εντευθεν

Etymology:
The word εντευθεν comes from the Greek words εν (en) meaning "in" or "within" and τευθεν (teuthen) meaning "from there" or "from that place". Therefore, εντευθεν literally means "from within there" or "from that place".

Grammar:
εντευθεν is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate the origin or starting point of something. It is often translated as "from here" or "from this point".

Description:
εντευθεν is a useful word in Greek for indicating the starting point or origin of something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in giving directions or describing the source of a problem. Its etymology reflects its meaning, as it literally means "from within there" or "from that place".
