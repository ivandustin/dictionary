Word: ενιψαμην

Etymology: ενιψαμην is a Greek verb that belongs to the aorist tense and middle voice. It is derived from the root word "νίπτω" (nipto), which means "to wash". The prefix "εν" (en) means "in" or "on", and the suffix "-μην" (-men) indicates the middle voice, which means that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Grammar: ενιψαμην is a first-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Greek. It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and was performed by the subject on themselves. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: ενιψαμην can be translated to "I washed myself" or "I bathed". It is a reflexive verb that emphasizes the action of washing oneself. It is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday speech to describe personal hygiene activities such as taking a shower or washing one's face.
