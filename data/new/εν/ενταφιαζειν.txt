The word "ενταφιαζειν" (entafiázein) is a Greek verb that means "to bury" or "to inter". 

Etymology: The word comes from the combination of the prefix "εντα-" (enta-), which means "in" or "within", and the verb "ταφιάζω" (tafiázo), which means "to bury". 

Grammar: "Ενταφιαζειν" is an infinitive verb in the present tense. In Greek, verbs are conjugated according to the tense, mood, and person of the subject. 

Description: The word is commonly used in the context of funerals and burials. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of putting something to rest or ending something.
