Word: ενδυσασθαι

Etymology:
The word ενδυσασθαι comes from the Greek verb ενδύω (endýō), which means "to put on" or "to dress". The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar:
ενδυσασθαι is a reflexive verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In English, it can be translated as "to dress oneself" or "to put on clothes".

Description:
ενδυσασθαι is a common verb in Greek, used to describe the act of getting dressed or putting on clothes. It can be used in a literal sense, as in "I dressed myself in the morning", or in a figurative sense, as in "I dressed myself in confidence before the interview". The word can also be used in a religious context, such as "to put on the armor of God" (Ephesians 6:11).
