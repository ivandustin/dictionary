Word: εντιμότερος

Etymology: The word εντιμότερος comes from the Greek root word "εντίμως" (entimos), which means "honorable" or "respectable". The suffix "-τερος" (-teros) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "comparatively".

Grammar: εντιμότερος is an adjective in the comparative form, meaning "more honorable" or "more respectable". It is declined like other Greek adjectives, with different forms for masculine, feminine, and neuter genders, as well as for singular and plural numbers.

Description: εντιμότερος is used to compare two things or people in terms of their level of honor or respectability. It implies that one thing or person is more honorable or respectable than the other. For example, one might say "Ο Πέτρος είναι εντιμότερος από τον Γιάννη" (O Petros einai entimoteros apo ton Gianni), which means "Petros is more honorable than Giannis".
