The word "εναντιας" (enan-tias) is a Greek adjective that means "opposite" or "contrary". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek preposition "εναντίος" (enan-tios), which means "facing" or "opposite". 

Grammar: "Εναντιας" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, "εναντια" (enan-tia) is the feminine singular form, and "εναντιοι" (enan-tioi) is the masculine plural form. 

Description: The word "εναντιας" is often used to describe something that is the opposite or contrary of something else. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in politics, philosophy, or everyday conversation. For example, "Οι απόψεις μας είναι εντελώς εναντίον η μία της άλλης" (Oi apopseis mas einai entelos enantion i mia tis allis) means "Our opinions are completely opposite of each other".
