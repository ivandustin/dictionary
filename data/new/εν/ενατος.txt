Word: ενατος

Etymology:
The word ενατος (enatos) is derived from the Greek word εννέα (ennea) which means "nine". It is used to describe the ninth in a series or sequence.

Grammar:
In Greek, ενατος is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it would be ενάτος (enatos), if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it would be ενάτη (enati), and so on.

Description:
The word ενατος is commonly used in Greek to describe the ninth item in a series or sequence. It can also be used to describe the ninth day of the month or the ninth year of a decade. In addition, it can be used in a figurative sense to describe something that is the "ninth" or last in a series of events or actions.
