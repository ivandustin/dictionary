Word: ενδυμα

Etymology:
The word ενδυμα (endyma) comes from the Greek verb ενδύω (endyo), which means "to put on" or "to dress". It is derived from the prefix εν- (en-), which means "in" or "on", and the verb δύω (dyo), which means "to put on" or "to clothe".

Grammar:
ενδυμα is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is ενδύματα (endymata). It is declined like other neuter nouns in Greek, with the nominative and accusative forms being the same.

Description:
ενδυμα refers to a garment or clothing item. It can be used to describe any type of clothing, from everyday wear to formal attire. The word is often used in the context of dressing up or putting on clothes for a special occasion.
