The word "εντολων" (entolon) is a Greek noun that can be translated to "commandments" or "orders". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "εντέλλω" (entello), which means "to command" or "to order". 

Grammar: "Εντολων" is the genitive plural form of the noun. 

Description: The word is often used in a religious context, referring to the commandments given by God in the Bible. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any type of command or order given by someone in authority.
