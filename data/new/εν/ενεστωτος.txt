Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be", and the suffix "-τος" (-tos), which is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: "Ενεστωτος" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: The word is commonly used in Greek to describe something that is happening or existing at the current moment. It can also be used in a grammatical sense to describe the present tense of a verb.
