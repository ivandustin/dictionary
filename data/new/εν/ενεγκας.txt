Word: ενεγκας

Etymology:
The word ενεγκας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φέρω (fero) which means "to carry" or "to bear". The prefix εν- (en-) means "in" or "into", so ενεγκας can be translated as "to bring in" or "to carry in".

Grammar:
ενεγκας is the aorist tense of the Greek verb φέρω (fero), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past. It is in the second person singular, which means that it is addressing one person. The verb is in the active voice, which means that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
ενεγκας can be translated as "you brought in" or "you carried in". It is a verb that describes an action of bringing or carrying something into a place. The context of the sentence would determine what was brought in or carried in.
