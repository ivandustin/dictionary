Word: ενωτισασθε

Etymology:
The word ενωτισασθε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ενωτίζω (enotizo), which means "to unite" or "to join together." The suffix -σθε (-sthe) indicates that the verb is in the second person plural form, meaning "you all unite" or "you all join together."

Grammar:
ενωτισασθε is a second person plural aorist passive imperative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action of the verb. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The word ενωτισασθε is a call to unity or togetherness. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, encouraging people to come together in harmony and work towards a common goal. The word can also be used more generally to encourage cooperation and collaboration among individuals or groups.
