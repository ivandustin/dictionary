Word: ενκοπτεσθαι

Etymology:
The word ενκοπτεσθαι is derived from the Greek word "ενκόπτω" (enkopto) which means "to hinder" or "to impede". The suffix "-σθαι" (-sthai) is a reflexive suffix that indicates that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar:
ενκοπτεσθαι is a verb in the present middle/passive participle form. It is a compound word consisting of the prefix "εν-" (en-) which means "in" or "into", and the verb "κόπτω" (kopto) which means "to cut" or "to strike". The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
The word ενκοπτεσθαι refers to the act of hindering oneself or impeding one's own progress. It can also mean to obstruct or block oneself from achieving a goal or completing a task. This word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as personal development, self-improvement, or overcoming obstacles.
