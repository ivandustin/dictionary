Word: ενδεδυμένον

Etymology:
The word ενδεδυμένον comes from the Greek verb ενδύω (endyō), which means "to put on" or "to dress". The word is formed from the prefix εν- (en-), which means "in" or "on", and the verb δύω (dyō), which means "to put on".

Grammar:
ενδεδυμένον is a past participle in the neuter gender, singular number, and accusative case. It is derived from the verb ενδύω (endyō), which is a first conjugation verb in the active voice. The word is used to describe something that has been dressed or put on.

Description:
ενδεδυμένον is an adjective that describes something that has been dressed or put on. It can be used to describe a person who is wearing clothes, or an object that has been covered or adorned with something.
