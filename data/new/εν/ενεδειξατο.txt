Word: ενεδειξατο

Etymology: The word ενεδειξατο comes from the Greek verb ενδεικνύω (endeiknyo), which means "to show" or "to demonstrate". The prefix εν- (en-) means "in" or "within", and the root word δεικνύω (deiknyo) means "to show" or "to point out".

Grammar: ενεδειξατο is a third person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb ενδεικνύω (endeiknyo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon itself or is affected by the action.

Description: ενεδειξατο can be translated as "he/she/it showed" or "he/she/it demonstrated". It is a verb that is often used in legal or formal contexts to indicate the presentation of evidence or proof.
