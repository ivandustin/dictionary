Word: ενδοξον

Etymology: The word ενδοξον comes from the Greek roots ενδο- (endo-), meaning "within" or "inside," and δοξα (doxa), meaning "glory" or "honor." Therefore, ενδοξον can be translated as "glorious within" or "honorable inside."

Grammar: ενδοξον is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: ενδοξον is used to describe something that is glorious or honorable from within. It can be used to describe a person, place, or thing that possesses an inner beauty or greatness. In Greek mythology, the term was often used to describe heroes or gods who possessed great inner strength and honor. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe something that is highly esteemed or respected.
