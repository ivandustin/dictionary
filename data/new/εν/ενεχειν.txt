The word "ενεχειν" (enechein) is a Greek verb that means "to accuse" or "to charge with a crime." It is derived from the prefix "en-" meaning "in" or "within," and the verb "echō" meaning "to have" or "to hold." In Greek mythology, the Furies were often depicted as enecheis, or accusers, who punished those who had committed crimes. In grammar, ενεχειν is a present active infinitive verb.
