Word: εναντίος

Etymology: The word εναντίος comes from the Greek root words εν (en) meaning "in" or "inside" and αντίος (antios) meaning "opposite" or "contrary". 

Grammar: εναντίος is an adjective in Greek, meaning "opposite" or "contrary". It can be used to describe a person, thing, or situation that is opposite or contrary to another. It can also be used as an adverb to describe an action that is done in an opposite or contrary way.

Description: εναντίος is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe opposing viewpoints, contrasting colors, or even conflicting emotions. It is a useful word to know in Greek, as it can help you express yourself more clearly and effectively.
