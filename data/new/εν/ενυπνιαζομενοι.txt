Word: ενυπνιαζομενοι

Etymology: The word ενυπνιαζομενοι comes from the Greek root words ενυπνιον (enypnion) meaning "dream" and αζω (azo) meaning "to cause to be". Therefore, ενυπνιαζομενοι means "to be caused to dream" or "to be put to sleep and dream".

Grammar: ενυπνιαζομενοι is the present participle form of the Greek verb ενυπνιαζω (enypniazo), which means "to dream". The word is in the masculine plural form, indicating that it refers to a group of people who are being caused to dream.

Description: ενυπνιαζομενοι is a word that describes a state of being in which someone is asleep and dreaming. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of being lost in thought or imagination. The word is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe visions or prophetic dreams.
