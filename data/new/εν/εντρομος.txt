Word: εντρομος

Etymology: The word εντρομος comes from the Greek word ἔντρομος (entromos), which means "terrified" or "frightened". It is derived from the prefix ἐν- (en-), meaning "in" or "within", and the root τρόμος (tromos), meaning "trembling" or "fear".

Grammar: εντρομος is an adjective in Greek, which means it modifies a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). The word can be declined according to the gender and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: εντρομος is an adjective that describes a state of being terrified or frightened. It can be used to describe a person, an animal, or a situation that causes fear or anxiety. The word is often used in literature and poetry to create a sense of tension or suspense.
