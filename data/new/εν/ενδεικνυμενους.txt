Word: ενδεικνυμενους

Etymology:
The word ενδεικνυμενους is derived from the Greek verb ενδείκνυμαι (endeiknymai), which means "to show, to demonstrate, to prove". The suffix -ους (-ous) is added to the verb stem to form the adjective ενδεικνυμενους (endeiknymenos), which means "demonstrated, proven, shown".

Grammar:
ενδεικνυμενους is a masculine plural adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
The word ενδεικνυμενους is used to describe something that has been demonstrated or proven. It can be used to describe a person, an object, or an idea that has been shown to be true or valid. In Greek literature, the word is often used in a legal context to describe evidence that has been presented in court.
