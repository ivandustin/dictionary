Word: ενεδυσασθε

Etymology:
The word ενεδυσασθε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ενδυω (enduo), which means "to put on" or "to clothe oneself." The suffix -σασθε (-sasthe) indicates that the verb is in the second person plural form, meaning "you all put on" or "you all clothe yourselves."

Grammar:
ενεδυσασθε is a second person plural aorist middle imperative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The imperative mood is used to give a command or instruction.

Description:
ενεδυσασθε is a command to a group of people to put on or clothe themselves. The context in which this word is used would determine what exactly they are being instructed to put on. It could be clothing, armor, or some other type of garment. The word is often used in a metaphorical sense as well, such as putting on the "armor of God" in Christian theology.
