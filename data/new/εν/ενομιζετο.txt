In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, voice, and person/number of the subject. The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. So, ενομιζετο can be translated as "he/she/it was being believed" or "he/she/it was thought to be".
