Word: εναντιον

Etymology:
The word εναντιον (enan-tion) is derived from the Greek preposition εναντι (enan-ti), which means "opposite" or "against". It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix εν- (en-), meaning "in" or "inside", and the adjective αντιος (antios), meaning "opposite" or "contrary".

Grammar:
In Greek, εναντιον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is often used as an adverb, meaning "opposite" or "against". It can also be used as a preposition, meaning "in front of" or "facing".

Description:
The word εναντιον is commonly used in Greek to describe something that is opposite or contrary to something else. It can be used to describe a physical location, such as "the store is opposite the park", or a more abstract concept, such as "his opinion is opposite to mine". It can also be used to describe a conflict or opposition between two parties, such as "there is a disagreement between the two groups".
