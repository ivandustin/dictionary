Word: ενεφάνισας

Etymology:
The word ενεφάνισας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φαίνω (phaino), which means "to show" or "to make visible". The prefix εν- (en-) means "in" or "into", so ενεφάνισας can be translated as "made visible" or "revealed".

Grammar:
ενεφάνισας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb φαίνω (phaino) and means "you made visible" or "you revealed". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
ενεφάνισας is a verb that describes an action of making something visible or revealing it. It can be used in various contexts, such as revealing a hidden object, exposing a truth, or making something known to others. The word has a strong connotation of bringing something to light or making it clear and visible.
