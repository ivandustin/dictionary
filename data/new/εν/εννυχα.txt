The word "εννυχα" (ennycha) is a Greek verb that means "to stay awake all night". 

Etymology: The word comes from the combination of the prefix "εν-" (en-), which means "in" or "inside", and the noun "νύξ" (nyx), which means "night". 

Grammar: "Εννυχα" is a first-person singular present indicative form of the verb. 

Description: The word describes the act of staying awake throughout the night, often for a specific purpose such as studying, working, or keeping watch. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of heightened awareness or vigilance.
