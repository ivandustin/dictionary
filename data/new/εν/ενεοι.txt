Word: ενεοι

Etymology:
The word ενεοι (eneoi) is a Greek word that comes from the root word "νέος" (neos), which means "new" or "young". The suffix "-οι" (-oi) is a plural ending, so ενεοι (eneoi) means "the new ones" or "the young ones".

Grammar:
In Greek, ενεοι (eneoi) is a plural noun. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence. It can also be used as an adjective to describe a group of new or young things.

Description:
ενεοι (eneoi) is a word that refers to a group of new or young things. It can be used to describe people, animals, or objects that are recently born or created. The word has a positive connotation, suggesting freshness, vitality, and potential.
