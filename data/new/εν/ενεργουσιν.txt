The word "ενεργουσιν" (energousin) is a Greek verb in the present active indicative form, which means "they are working" or "they are active". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "ενεργός" (energos), which means "active" or "energetic". 

Grammar: "ενεργουσιν" is a third-person plural verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. 

Description: The word is commonly used in Greek to describe a group of people who are actively engaged in some kind of work or activity. It can also be used to describe a state of being active or energetic.
