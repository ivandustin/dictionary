Word: ενδεκατος

Etymology:
The word ενδεκατος comes from the Greek words ενδέκα (endeka) meaning "eleven" and -τος (-tos) which is a suffix used to form ordinal numbers. Therefore, ενδεκατος means "eleventh" in English.

Grammar:
In Greek, ενδεκατος is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if we want to say "the eleventh day" in Greek, we would say "η ενδέκατη μέρα" (i endekati mera) for feminine nouns, "ο ενδέκατος μήνας" (o endekatos minas) for masculine nouns, and "το ενδέκατο παιδί" (to endekato paidi) for neuter nouns.

Description:
ενδεκατος is a word that is used to indicate the position of something in a sequence, specifically the eleventh position. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as the eleventh day of the month, the eleventh month of the year, or the eleventh child in a family. It is a common word in Greek and is used in everyday conversation and writing.
