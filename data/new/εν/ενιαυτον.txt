Word: ενιαυτον

Etymology: The word ενιαυτον comes from the Greek words ενιαυτός (eniautos) meaning "year" and αὐτός (autos) meaning "self". It literally means "self-year" or "year by year".

Grammar: ενιαυτον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its nominative singular form is ενιαυτον and its genitive singular form is ενιαυτου. In the plural form, it is ενιαυτα.

Description: ενιαυτον refers to a period of one year. It is often used in the context of annual events or occurrences. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to refer to the New Year's Eve celebration.
