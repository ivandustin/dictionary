The word "ενκεκαινισται" is a Greek verb that means "to renew" or "to make new again". It is derived from the prefix "εν-" which means "in" or "into", and the verb "καινίζω" which means "to make new". 

In terms of grammar, "ενκεκαινισται" is a perfect passive participle in the third person singular form. This means that it describes an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present, and that the subject of the verb is the recipient of the action rather than the doer. 

Overall, "ενκεκαινισται" is a word that conveys the idea of renewal and restoration, and is often used in a spiritual or religious context to describe the process of being made new or born again.
