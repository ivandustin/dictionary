Word: ενδυσαμενος

Etymology:
The word ενδυσαμενος comes from the Greek verb ενδύω (endýo), which means "to put on" or "to dress". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending, indicating that the action of putting on clothes has been completed.

Grammar:
ενδυσαμενος is a participle in the aorist tense, middle voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. The aorist tense indicates that the action of putting on clothes was completed in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject (in this case, a masculine noun) performed the action on himself. The nominative case indicates that the participle is functioning as a subject in the sentence.

Description:
ενδυσαμενος can be translated as "having put on" or "having dressed oneself". It is often used in Greek literature to describe someone who has just put on new clothes or armor, or who has changed into a different outfit. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has taken on a new role or identity.
