Word: ενεδυναμουτο

Etymology: The word ενεδυναμουτο comes from the Greek verb ενδυναμόω (endynamóo), which means "to empower" or "to strengthen". The prefix εν- (en-) means "in" or "within", and the root word δυναμόω (dynamóo) means "to make strong" or "to give power".

Grammar: ενεδυναμουτο is a verb in the past tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb ενδυναμόω.

Description: ενεδυναμουτο means "was empowered" or "was strengthened". It is used to describe a past action in which someone or something was given power or strength. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence received the action rather than performed it.
