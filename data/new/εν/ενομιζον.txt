Word: ενομιζον

Etymology: The word ενομιζον comes from the Greek verb "νομίζω" (nomizo), which means "to think" or "to believe". The prefix "εν-" (en-) adds the meaning of "in" or "within", so ενομιζον can be translated as "I was thinking" or "I was believing".

Grammar: ενομιζον is the first person singular, imperfect tense of the verb "νομίζω" (nomizo). In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past. So, ενομιζον can be translated as "I was thinking" or "I used to think".

Description: ενομιζον is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Luke 2:49. In this passage, Jesus is speaking to his parents after they found him in the temple, and he says, "Why were you searching for me? Didn't you know I had to be in my Father's house?" The Greek text reads: "καὶ εἶπεν πρὸς αὐτοὺς· Τί ὅτι ἐζητεῖτέ με; οὐκ ᾔδειτε ὅτι ἐν τοῖς τοῦ πατρός μου δεῖ εἶναί με;" (kai eipen pros autous: Ti hoti ezeteite me? ouk edeite hoti en tois tou patros mou dei einai me?).
