The word "ενεβλεψεν" (eneblepsen) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "βλέπω" (blepo), which means "to see". The prefix "εν" (en) adds the meaning of "into" or "upon", so "ενεβλεψεν" can be translated as "he/she/it looked into" or "he/she/it gazed upon". 

In terms of grammar, "ενεβλεψεν" is a third-person singular verb in the aorist tense. It can be conjugated for different persons and tenses, such as "ενέβλεψα" (eneblepsa) for first-person singular in the aorist tense, or "θα ενεβλέπω" (tha eneblepo) for future tense.

Overall, "ενεβλεψεν" is a specific form of the verb "βλέπω" that indicates a completed action of looking into or gazing upon something in the past.
