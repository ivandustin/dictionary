Word: ενεδυσαν

Etymology:
The word ενεδυσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "ενδύω" (enduo), which means "to put on" or "to clothe". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
ενεδυσαν is a third-person plural aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
ενεδυσαν means "they put on" or "they clothed" in English. The word is often used in the context of putting on clothing or garments. It can also be used metaphorically to describe putting on a particular attitude or behavior. For example, one might say "they put on a brave face" using this verb.
