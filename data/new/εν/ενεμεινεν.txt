Word: ενεμεινεν

Etymology:
The word ενεμεινεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb μένω (meno), which means "to remain" or "to stay". The prefix εν- (en-) means "in" or "within". Therefore, ενεμεινεν means "to remain within" or "to stay in".

Grammar:
ενεμεινεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the third person singular form, which means that it refers to a single person or thing that performed the action.

Description:
ενεμεινεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 1:32. It is used to describe the Holy Spirit "remaining" or "staying in" Jesus after he was baptized by John the Baptist. The word emphasizes the idea of the Holy Spirit dwelling within Jesus and remaining with him throughout his ministry.
