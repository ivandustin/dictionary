Word: ενοχοι

Etymology: The word ενοχοι comes from the Greek word "ένοχος" (enochos), which means guilty or culpable.

Grammar: ενοχοι is the plural form of the adjective "ένοχος" (enochos), which means guilty. It is used to describe multiple people who are guilty of something.

Description: ενοχοι is a Greek word that is commonly used in legal contexts to refer to individuals who have been found guilty of a crime. It can also be used more broadly to describe individuals who are responsible for a wrongdoing or mistake. In Greek culture, there is a strong emphasis on personal responsibility and accountability, and the concept of guilt is an important part of this.
