Word: ενεμειναν

Etymology:
The word ενεμειναν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μένω (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". The prefix εν- (en-) means "in" or "within". Therefore, ενεμειναν can be translated as "to remain within" or "to stay in".

Grammar:
ενεμειναν is a third-person plural aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
ενεμειναν is used in the New Testament of the Bible in Acts 1:4, where it describes the disciples waiting in Jerusalem for the Holy Spirit to come upon them. The verb emphasizes their patient endurance and steadfastness in remaining in one place until the promised event occurred.
