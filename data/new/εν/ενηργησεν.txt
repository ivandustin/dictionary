Word: ενηργησεν

Etymology: The word ενηργησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ενεργός (energos), which means "active" or "effective". The verb form ενηργησεν is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: ενηργησεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the prefix εν- (en-) to the root word εργον (ergon), which means "work" or "action". The suffix -σεν (-sen) indicates the past tense and the active voice.

Description: The word ενηργησεν means "he/she/it acted" or "he/she/it worked" in Greek. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of God or Jesus. For example, in the Gospel of John, Jesus says "My Father is working (ενηργει) until now, and I am working (ενεργω) " (John 5:17). The word emphasizes the idea of effective action or work that produces results.
