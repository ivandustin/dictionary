Word: ενδειξασθαι

Etymology:
The word ενδειξασθαι comes from the Greek verb ενδεικνύω (endeiknyo), which means "to show, to demonstrate". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) is a reflexive suffix, which means that the action is performed on oneself. Therefore, ενδειξασθαι means "to demonstrate oneself, to prove oneself".

Grammar:
ενδειξασθαι is a verb in the aorist middle/passive infinitive form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
ενδειξασθαι means "to demonstrate oneself, to prove oneself" in Greek. It is often used in a legal context, where a person needs to prove their innocence or their right to something. It can also be used in a more general sense, to show one's abilities or qualities.
