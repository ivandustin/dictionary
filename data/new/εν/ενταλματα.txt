Word: ενταλματα

Etymology: The word ενταλματα comes from the Greek verb εντάλλω (entállō), which means "to command" or "to order". The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a plural noun ending, which indicates that ενταλματα refers to multiple commands or orders.

Grammar: ενταλματα is a plural noun in Greek. It is declined in the third declension and has a neuter gender. The nominative singular form is ενταλμα (entálma).

Description: ενταλματα refers to commands or orders given by someone in authority. It can also refer to a legal injunction or a court order. In modern Greek, ενταλματα is often used in the context of financial transactions, such as bank transfers or payment orders.
