Word: εντυγχανειν

Etymology: The word εντυγχανειν comes from the Greek roots εν (en) meaning "in" or "within" and τυγχανω (tygchano) meaning "to happen" or "to occur". Therefore, εντυγχανειν means "to happen within" or "to occur in the presence of".

Grammar: εντυγχανειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often translated as "to meet", "to encounter", or "to come across".

Description: εντυγχανειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to chance encounters or meetings, as well as intentional meetings or appointments. It can also be used in a more abstract sense to describe the occurrence of events or situations.
