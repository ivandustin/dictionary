Word: εναντίους

Etymology:
The word εναντίους (enan-ti-ous) is derived from the Greek word εναντίος (enan-ti-os), which means "opposite" or "contrary". It is formed by combining the prefix εν- (en-), meaning "in" or "inside", and the adjective αντίος (anti-os), meaning "opposite" or "contrary".

Grammar:
εναντίους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe something or someone that is opposite or contrary to something else. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, if we want to describe a group of men who are opposed to something, we would use the word εναντίους.

Description:
The word εναντίους is often used in Greek to describe people or things that are in opposition to each other. It can be used to describe political opponents, conflicting ideas, or opposing forces. For example, if two political parties have opposing views on a particular issue, they can be described as εναντίους. Similarly, if two armies are fighting against each other, they can be described as εναντίους. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe anything that is opposite or contrary to something else.
