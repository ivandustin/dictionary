Word: ενοχος

Etymology:
The word ενοχος comes from the Greek word "ένοχος" which means guilty or culpable. It is derived from the prefix "εν-" which means "in" or "inside" and the word "οχος" which means "guilt".

Grammar:
ενοχος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can also be used in the feminine gender as ενοχη or in the neuter gender as ενοχο.

Description:
ενοχος is a word used to describe someone who is guilty or responsible for a wrongdoing. It can also be used to describe something that is responsible for a negative outcome or consequence. The word is commonly used in legal contexts to refer to a person who has been found guilty of a crime.
