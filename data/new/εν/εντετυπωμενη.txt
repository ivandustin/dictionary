Word: εντετυπωμένη

Etymology:
The word εντετυπωμένη comes from the Greek verb "τυπώνω" (typóno), which means "to print". The prefix "εντε-" means "in" or "into", so εντετυπωμένη literally means "printed into" or "impressed into".

Grammar:
εντετυπωμένη is the feminine form of the past participle of the verb εντυπώνω (entypóno), which means "to impress" or "to print". It is used to describe something that has been printed or impressed, and agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
εντετυπωμένη is an adjective that describes something that has been printed or impressed. It can be used to describe a wide range of things, from a book that has been printed to a design that has been stamped onto a piece of metal. The word implies a sense of permanence, as something that has been printed or impressed is not easily erased or removed.
