Word: εντος

Etymology:
The word εντος (entos) is a Greek word that means "inside" or "within". It is derived from the Greek preposition "en" which means "in" or "within".

Grammar:
In Greek, εντος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). It can also be used as an adverb to modify a verb.

Description:
The word εντος is commonly used in Greek to describe something that is inside or within something else. For example, it can be used to describe the inside of a building, the interior of a car, or the contents of a container. It can also be used figuratively to describe something that is internal or inherent to a person or thing.
