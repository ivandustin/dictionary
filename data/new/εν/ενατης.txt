Word: ενατης

Etymology: The word ενατης comes from the Greek word εννέα (ennéa), meaning "nine". It is used to describe something that is ninth in a series or sequence.

Grammar: ενατης is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: ενατης is a term used to describe something that is ninth in a series or sequence. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in sports rankings, musical scales, or historical events. For example, in ancient Greek mythology, the nine Muses were often referred to as the ενατης Μουσα (enatis Mousa), or "ninth Muse".
