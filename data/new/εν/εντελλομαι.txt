Word: εντελλομαι

Etymology:
The word εντελλομαι comes from the Greek root words εν (en) meaning "in" or "into" and τελλω (tellō) meaning "to order" or "to command". Therefore, εντελλομαι means "to give an order" or "to command".

Grammar:
εντελλομαι is a verb in the present tense, middle or passive voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ εντέλλομαι (I give an order)
- Συ εντέλλεσαι (You give an order)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό εντέλλεται (He/She/It gives an order)

Plural:
- Εμείς εντελλόμαστε (We give an order)
- Εσείς εντέλλεστε (You give an order)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά εντέλλονται (They give an order)

Description:
εντελλομαι is a Greek verb that means "to give an order" or "to command". It is often used in the context of military or authoritative orders. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to indicate a strong request or instruction. The middle or passive voice of the verb can also indicate that the subject is receiving an order or command.
