The word "ενεστωσαν" is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the present tense and third person plural form. Its etymology can be traced back to the Greek word "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be". 

In terms of grammar, "ενεστωσαν" is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" (omega). It is also a transitive verb, meaning it requires a direct object to complete its meaning. 

As for its description, "ενεστωσαν" can be translated to English as "they are". It is commonly used to describe a current state or condition of a subject. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου ενεστώταν στο σπίτι" (Oi filoi mou enestotan sto spiti) translates to "My friends are at home".
