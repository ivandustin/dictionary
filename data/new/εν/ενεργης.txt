Word: ενεργης

Etymology: The word ενεργης comes from the Greek word ενέργεια (energeia), which means "activity" or "operation". It is derived from the words εν (en), meaning "in" or "within", and εργον (ergon), meaning "work" or "action".

Grammar: ενεργης is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: ενεργης is a Greek word that means "active" or "energetic". It is often used to describe a person who is lively, dynamic, and full of energy. It can also be used to describe a situation or event that is full of activity or excitement. In Greek philosophy, the concept of energeia refers to the actualization of potentiality, or the realization of a thing's inherent capacity for action.
