Word: κενοις

Etymology:
The word κενοις (kenois) is derived from the Greek word κενός (kenos), which means "empty" or "void". It is a plural form of κενός.

Grammar:
In Greek, κενοις is a masculine noun in the dative case. It is the plural form of κενός, which means "empty" or "void". The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
The word κενοις is used in Greek literature to describe empty or void spaces. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a lack of substance or meaning. In philosophy, the concept of κενός (kenos) is often associated with the idea of emptiness or nothingness, and is used to describe the nature of reality or the ultimate reality of existence.
