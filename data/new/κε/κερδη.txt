Word: κερδη

Etymology:
The word κερδη (kerdē) is derived from the Greek word κέρδος (kérdos), which means "gain" or "profit". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *ker- which means "to grow" or "to increase".

Grammar:
κερδη is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and is often used to refer to profits or gains.

Description:
κερδη is a term used in finance and economics to refer to profits or gains. It is often used in the context of business or investment, where it represents the amount of money earned after deducting expenses. The term can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any kind of benefit or advantage gained from a particular action or decision.
