Word: κερδησας

Etymology:
The word κερδησας (kerdēsas) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It comes from the root word κέρδος (kérdos), which means "gain" or "profit". The suffix -ας (-as) is added to the root word to indicate the past tense.

Grammar:
κερδησας is a second-person singular verb in the aorist tense. It is conjugated from the verb κερδίζω (kerdízo), which means "to gain" or "to profit". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
κερδησας can be translated to "you gained" or "you profited" in English. It is often used in a financial context to describe a person or entity that has made a profit. It can also be used more broadly to describe any situation where someone has gained something of value.
