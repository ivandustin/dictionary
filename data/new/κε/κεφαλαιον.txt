Word: κεφαλαιον

Etymology:
The word κεφαλαιον (kephalaion) comes from the Greek word κεφαλή (kephalē), which means "head" or "capital". In ancient Greece, κεφαλαιον referred to the main or principal city of a region or state.

Grammar:
In Greek, κεφαλαιον is a neuter noun. Its plural form is κεφάλαια (kephalaia).

Description:
In modern Greek, κεφαλαιον is commonly used to refer to a chapter or section of a book. It can also refer to a main point or topic of discussion. In finance, κεφαλαιον is used to refer to capital or assets.
