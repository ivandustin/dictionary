Word: κεκλεισται

Etymology:
The word κεκλεισται is a form of the Greek verb κλείω (kleiō), which means "to close" or "to shut." The prefix κε- (ke-) indicates that the verb is in the perfect tense, which means that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Grammar:
κεκλεισται is a third-person singular form of the perfect passive indicative tense of the verb κλείω. This form indicates that the subject (which is not specified in this case) has been closed or shut in the past and remains closed or shut in the present.

Description:
The word κεκλεισται can be translated as "has been closed" or "has been shut." It is often used to describe doors, windows, or other objects that have been closed and remain closed. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is closed off or secretive.
