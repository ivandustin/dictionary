Word: κεῖμαι (keimai)

Etymology:
The word κεῖμαι (keimai) is a Greek verb that means "to lie down" or "to be laid". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ḱey- which means "to lie down" or "to rest".

Grammar:
κεῖμαι (keimai) is a present tense verb in the middle voice. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- ἔχω (echo) - I lie down
- ἔχεις (echeis) - you lie down
- ἔχει (echei) - he/she/it lies down

Plural:
- ἔχομεν (echomen) - we lie down
- ἔχετε (echete) - you all lie down
- ἔχουσι (echousi) - they lie down

Description:
κεῖμαι (keimai) is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of lying down or being laid. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a person lying down to rest, a body being laid to rest in a tomb, or an object being placed in a certain position. The verb is often used in combination with other verbs to describe more complex actions, such as "to lie down and sleep" or "to lie down and rest".
