Word: κειται

Etymology: κειται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κεῖμαι (keimai), which means "to lie down" or "to be placed." 

Grammar: κειται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb κεῖμαι (keimai). It is in the active voice and indicative mood. 

Description: κειται means "he/she/it lies" or "he/she/it is placed" in English. It is often used to describe the position or location of an object or person. For example, "Το βιβλίο κειται πάνω στο τραπέζι" (To vivlio keitai pano sto trapezi) means "The book lies on the table."
