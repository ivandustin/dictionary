The word "κελευσματι" (keleusmati) is a noun in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the verb "κελεύω" (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order". The suffix "-ματι" (-mati) is added to the verb stem to form a noun, indicating the result or product of the action.

Grammar: The word is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is declined as follows: 

- Nominative: το κελευσμα (to keleusma)
- Genitive: του κελευσματος (tou keleusmatos)
- Accusative: το κελευσμα (to keleusma)
- Vocative: κελευσμα (keleusma)

Description: The word "κελευσματι" refers to a command, order, or directive given by someone in authority. It can also refer to a signal or a call to action. In modern Greek, the word is often used in the context of military commands or orders given by a superior officer.
