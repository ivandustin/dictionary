Word: κειμενην

Etymology: κειμενην is a Greek word that comes from the verb κεῖμαι (keimai), which means "to lie down" or "to be placed". The suffix -ην (-en) is a feminine accusative singular ending.

Grammar: κειμενην is a feminine accusative singular form of the adjective κεῖμενος (keimenos), which means "lying down" or "placed". It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: κειμενην is used to describe a feminine noun that is lying down or placed in a certain position. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is stagnant or inactive.
