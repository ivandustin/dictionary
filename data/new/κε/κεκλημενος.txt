Word: κεκλημένος

Etymology:
The word κεκλημένος is a past participle of the Greek verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to name". The prefix κεκ- (kek-) indicates that the action of calling or naming has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
In Greek, past participles are used to form the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The word κεκλημένος is masculine, singular, and nominative, which means it is used to describe a male subject who has been called or named in the past.

Description:
The word κεκλημένος can be translated as "called" or "named". It is often used in Greek literature and mythology to describe characters who have been given a specific name or title. For example, in Homer's Odyssey, the hero Odysseus is often referred to as "κεκλημένος Λαερτιάδης" (keklemenos Laertiades), which means "the son of Laertes who has been called by name".
