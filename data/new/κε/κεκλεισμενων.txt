Word: κεκλεισμένων

Etymology:
The word κεκλεισμένων is derived from the Greek verb κλείω (kleiō), which means "to close" or "to shut". The prefix κεκλεισ- (kekleis-) indicates that the action of closing has already been completed in the past.

Grammar:
κεκλεισμένων is the genitive plural form of the adjective κεκλεισμένος (kekleismenos), which means "closed". The genitive plural form is used to indicate possession or association with a group of people or things.

Description:
The word κεκλεισμένων is often used to describe a group of closed or locked objects, such as doors, windows, or gates. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people who are isolated or cut off from the rest of society. In Greek Orthodox Christianity, the term is used to refer to the "closed" or "sealed" doors of the sanctuary during certain parts of the liturgy.
