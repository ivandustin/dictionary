The word "κεις" (keis) is a Greek verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It comes from the verb "κεῖμαι" (keimai), which means "to lie down" or "to be situated". In its present tense form, "κεις" means "you lie down" or "you are situated". 

In terms of grammar, "κεις" is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-μαι". It is also a second person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is "you".

Overall, "κεις" is a simple verb that describes a state of being or position. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and writing in Greek.
