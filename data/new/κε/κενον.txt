Word: κενον

Etymology:
The word κενον (kenon) is derived from the Greek word κενός (kenos), which means "empty" or "void". It is related to the Latin word "vacuus", which has a similar meaning.

Grammar:
κενον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive, dative, and vocative.

Description:
κενον refers to an empty space or void. It can also be used to describe something that is lacking or deficient. In philosophy, the term κενον is used to describe the concept of emptiness or nothingness. The word is often used in scientific contexts, such as in physics to describe a vacuum or in mathematics to describe an empty set.
