Word: κεχρηματισται

Etymology: The word κεχρηματισται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word χρηματίζω (chrēmatízō), which means "to transact business" or "to make money." The prefix κε- (ke-) indicates that the action is completed or has already happened.

Grammar: κεχρηματισται is a third-person singular perfect passive indicative verb in the Greek language. The perfect tense indicates that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action. 

Description: κεχρηματισται can be translated to mean "has been made rich" or "has been enriched." It describes a state of having acquired wealth or resources through business transactions or other means. The word implies a sense of permanence or ongoing benefit from the wealth that has been acquired.
