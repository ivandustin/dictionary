Word: κεφαλης

Etymology: κεφαλης is a Greek word that comes from the root word κεφαλή (kephalē), which means "head."

Grammar: κεφαλης is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive form of κεφαλή.

Description: κεφαλης refers to "of the head" or "belonging to the head." It can be used to describe various things related to the head, such as hair, a headache, or a head injury. In Greek mythology, κεφαλης is also the name of a monster with multiple heads, known as the Hydra.
