Word: κεκερασμένου

Etymology:
The word κεκερασμένου is derived from the Greek verb κεκεράσθαι (kekerasthai), which means "to be drunk" or "to be intoxicated". The suffix -μένου (-menou) is a genitive form that indicates possession or belonging.

Grammar:
κεκερασμένου is a genitive singular form of the adjective κεκερασμένος (kekerasmenos), which means "drunk" or "intoxicated". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, κεκερασμένου modifies a masculine singular noun in the genitive case.

Description:
κεκερασμένου describes someone or something that is drunk or intoxicated. It can be used to describe a person who has consumed too much alcohol or a thing that has been soaked in alcohol. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize or condemn behavior that is considered irresponsible or reckless.
