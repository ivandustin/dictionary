The word "κενοδοξιαν" (kenodoxian) is a Greek noun that combines the words "κενός" (kenos) meaning "empty" or "void" and "δόξα" (doxa) meaning "glory" or "praise". 

Etymology: The word "κενοδοξιαν" is derived from the combination of the two Greek words mentioned above.

Grammar: "κενοδοξιαν" is a feminine noun in the accusative case.

Description: The word "κενοδοξιαν" refers to the act of seeking empty or vain glory, or the desire for praise or recognition without any real merit or accomplishment. It can also refer to the act of boasting or showing off in a way that is excessive or unwarranted.
