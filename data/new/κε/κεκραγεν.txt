Word: κεκραγεν

Etymology:
The word κεκραγεν is a perfect tense form of the Greek verb κράζω (krázō), which means "to cry out" or "to shout". The prefix κεκ- indicates that the action of shouting has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
κεκραγεν is a third-person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb κράζω. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or expressing a fact.

Description:
κεκραγεν can be translated as "he/she/it has shouted" or "he/she/it shouted". The word implies that the shouting has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The context in which the word is used will determine the exact meaning and implications of the shouting.
