Word: κεκρικα

Etymology: κεκρικα is the perfect tense form of the Greek verb κρινω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix κεκρικα (kekrika) indicates that the action of judging or deciding has been completed in the past.

Grammar: κεκρικα is a perfect tense verb in the active voice. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: κεκρικα can be translated as "I have judged" or "I have decided". It is a strong and decisive verb that implies a finality to the action taken. The perfect tense form suggests that the decision or judgment made in the past continues to have an impact on the present.
