Word: κεκληρονομηκεν

Etymology: κεκληρονομηκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root words κληρονομέω (klēronomeō) and κλῆρος (klēros), which mean "to inherit" and "lot" or "inheritance" respectively. The prefix κε- (ke-) indicates that the action is completed or perfect.

Grammar: κεκληρονομηκεν is a third-person singular perfect indicative active verb in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the perfect tense suffix -κε (ke) to the stem κληρονομέω (klēronomeō). The verb is conjugated as follows:

Present: κληρονομῶ (klēronomō) - I inherit
Perfect: κεκληρονόμηκα (keklēronomēka) - I have inherited
Future: κληρονομήσω (klēronomēsō) - I will inherit

Description: κεκληρονομηκεν means "he/she/it has inherited" in English. It is a past action that has been completed in the present. The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the inheritance of believers in Christ.
