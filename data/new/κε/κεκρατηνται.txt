Word: κεκρατηνται

Etymology:
The word κεκρατηνται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κρατέω (kratéō), which means "to rule" or "to have power over." The prefix κεκρα- (kekra-) indicates that the action of the verb is in the perfect tense, meaning that it has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Grammar:
κεκρατηνται is a third-person plural verb in the perfect tense. It is in the indicative mood, which means that it is a statement of fact. The verb is passive, indicating that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
The word κεκρατηνται can be translated to mean "they have been ruled" or "they have been dominated." It implies that the subject has been subjected to the power or authority of another. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in politics, where one country may have dominated another, or in personal relationships, where one person may have power over another.
