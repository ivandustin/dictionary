Word: κενοφωνίας

Etymology: The word κενοφωνίας is derived from two Greek words, κενός (kenos) meaning "empty" and φωνή (phone) meaning "voice". Therefore, κενοφωνίας can be translated as "empty voice" or "hollow sound".

Grammar: κενοφωνίας is a feminine noun in Greek. Its genitive form is κενοφωνίαδος (kenophoníados) and its accusative form is κενοφωνίαν (kenophonían).

Description: κενοφωνίας refers to a sound or voice that is empty, hollow, or lacking in substance. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who speaks without saying anything meaningful or substantial.
