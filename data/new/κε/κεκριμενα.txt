Word: κεκριμενα

Etymology: The word κεκριμενα is a perfect passive participle of the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix κε- (ke-) indicates the perfect tense, and the suffix -μενα (-mena) indicates the passive voice.

Grammar: κεκριμενα is a neuter gender noun in the nominative case. As a perfect passive participle, it functions as an adjective and describes a state resulting from a completed action. In this case, it describes something that has been judged or decided upon.

Description: κεκριμενα can be translated as "judged", "decided", or "determined". It is often used in legal or judicial contexts to describe a decision that has been made. It can also be used more broadly to describe any situation where a decision or judgment has been reached.
