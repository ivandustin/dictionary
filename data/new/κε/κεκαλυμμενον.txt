Word: κεκαλυμμένον

Etymology:
The word κεκαλυμμένον comes from the Greek verb καλύπτω (kalýpto), which means "to cover" or "to conceal". The prefix κεκα- indicates that the action is in the perfect tense, meaning that it has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Grammar:
κεκαλυμμένον is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is in the perfect passive participle form, which means that it describes a state resulting from a completed action. In this case, the state is one of being covered or concealed.

Description:
κεκαλυμμένον describes something that has been covered or concealed in the past and remains covered or concealed in the present. It can be used to describe physical objects that are hidden from view, as well as abstract concepts that are not immediately apparent. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe hidden motives or intentions.
