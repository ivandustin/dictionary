Word: κελευσον

Etymology:
The word κελευσον (keleuson) is derived from the Greek verb κελεύω (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order." It is a second-person singular aorist imperative form of the verb.

Grammar:
In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. Therefore, κελευσον (keleuson) is a command or request in the past tense.

Description:
The word κελευσον (keleuson) is a command or request to someone in the second person singular form. It is often used in ancient Greek literature and mythology, where gods and heroes give commands to each other. For example, in Homer's Iliad, Zeus commands the god Apollo to go to the battlefield and help the Trojans. The word can also be used in modern Greek to give commands or make requests.
