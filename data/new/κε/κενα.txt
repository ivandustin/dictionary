Word: κενα

Etymology:
The word κενα (kena) is derived from the Greek word κενός (kenos), which means "empty" or "void". It is related to the Latin word "vacuus", which has a similar meaning.

Grammar:
κενα is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "emptiness" or "voids". It is often used in a philosophical or metaphysical context to refer to the concept of emptiness or nothingness.

Description:
κενα is a word that is often used in Greek philosophy and metaphysics to describe the concept of emptiness or nothingness. It is used to describe the absence of something, whether it be physical or abstract. In some contexts, it can also refer to a state of mind or a feeling of emptiness. The word has a deep philosophical significance and is often used in discussions about the nature of reality and existence.
