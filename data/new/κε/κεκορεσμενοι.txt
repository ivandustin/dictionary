Word: κεκορεσμενοι

Etymology:
The word κεκορεσμενοι is derived from the Greek verb κορέω (koreo), which means "to satiate" or "to fill up". The prefix κεκο- (keko-) indicates that the action is completed or perfect.

Grammar:
κεκορεσμενοι is a plural adjective in the masculine gender, nominative case, and perfect tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have been fully satisfied or satiated.

Description:
The word κεκορεσμενοι is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe people who have had their fill of something, such as food, drink, or pleasure. It can also be used to describe a feeling of contentment or satisfaction. In ancient Greek literature, the term was often used to describe the gods, who were believed to be completely satisfied and fulfilled in their existence.
