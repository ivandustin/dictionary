Word: κεκοσμημενοι

Etymology: κεκοσμημενοι is a Greek word that comes from the verb κοσμέω (kosmeo), which means "to arrange" or "to adorn". The prefix κεκο- (keko-) indicates that the action is completed or perfect.

Grammar: κεκοσμημενοι is the plural form of the past participle of the verb κοσμέω (kosmeo). It is used to describe a group of people who have been arranged or adorned in some way. In this form, it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: κεκοσμημενοι can be translated as "adorned", "arranged", or "decorated". It is often used to describe people who are dressed up or wearing fancy clothing. It can also be used more broadly to describe anything that has been embellished or made more attractive.
