Word: κερδος

Etymology:
The word κερδος (kerdos) comes from the Greek root κερδ- (kerd-), which means "gain" or "profit". It is related to the verb κερδίζω (kerdizo), which means "to gain" or "to profit".

Grammar:
In Greek, κερδος is a neuter noun. Its nominative singular form is κέρδος (kerdos), and its genitive singular form is κέρδους (kerdous).

Description:
κερδος (kerdos) refers to gain or profit, especially in a financial sense. It can also refer to advantage or benefit. In ancient Greek philosophy, the pursuit of κερδος was often contrasted with the pursuit of virtue or wisdom. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to financial gain or profit.
