Word: κερδηθησονται

Etymology:
The word κερδηθησονται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κέρδος (kerdos), which means "gain" or "profit". The suffix -θησονται (-thesontai) is a passive voice ending that indicates that the subject of the verb is receiving the action.

Grammar:
κερδηθησονται is a third-person plural passive present indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb κερδίζω (kerdizo), which means "to gain" or "to profit". The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. The passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action, rather than performing it.

Description:
The word κερδηθησονται describes a passive action of gaining or profiting. It can be translated to English as "they are being gained/profited" or "they are receiving gain/profit". The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and interpretation.
