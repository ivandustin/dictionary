Word: κελευσαντες

Etymology:
The word κελευσαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κελευω (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is in the past tense and the subject is plural.

Grammar:
κελευσαντες is a third-person plural aorist active participle of the verb κελευω. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb is completed in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
κελευσαντες can be translated as "having commanded" or "having ordered". It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of Jesus or his disciples. For example, in Matthew 28:19, Jesus commands his disciples to "go and make disciples of all nations". The word κελευσαντες is used to describe this command.
