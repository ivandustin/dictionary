Word: κερδους

Etymology:
The word κερδους (kerdous) is derived from the Greek word κέρδος (kerdos), which means "gain" or "profit". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *kerd-, which means "to acquire" or "to gain".

Grammar:
In Greek, κερδους is a noun of the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and its nominative and genitive forms are as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: κερδους (kerdous)
Genitive: κερδου (kerdou)

Plural:
Nominative: κερδοι (kerdoi)
Genitive: κερδων (kerdon)

Description:
κερδους is a Greek word that refers to "gain" or "profit". It is often used in the context of business and finance, and can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any kind of benefit or advantage. The word has a positive connotation, and is often associated with success and prosperity.
