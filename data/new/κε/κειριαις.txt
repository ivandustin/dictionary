The word "κειριαις" (keiriais) is a Greek noun in the dative plural form. It is derived from the verb "κείρω" (keirō), which means "to cut" or "to shear". The noun refers to the act of cutting or shearing, and can also be used to describe a tool or instrument used for cutting or shearing. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used in the context of sacrificial rituals, where animals were cut or sheared as part of the offering.
