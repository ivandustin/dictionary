Word: κερδησω

Etymology:
The word κερδησω (kerdēsō) is derived from the Greek root word κέρδος (kérdos), which means "gain" or "profit". It is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form.

Grammar:
κερδησω (kerdēsō) is a regular verb in Greek, belonging to the first conjugation. It is a transitive verb, meaning it takes a direct object. In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: κερδησω (kerdēsō) - I gain, I profit
- Plural: κερδησουμε (kerdēsoume) - we gain, we profit

Description:
The word κερδησω (kerdēsō) refers to the act of gaining or profiting, usually in a financial sense. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of benefit or advantage gained from a particular action or situation. In Greek culture, the concept of κέρδος (kérdos) was highly valued, and was often associated with wisdom and prudence in managing one's affairs.
