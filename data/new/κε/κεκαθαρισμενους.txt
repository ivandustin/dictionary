Word: κεκαθαρισμένους

Etymology: The word κεκαθαρισμένους is derived from the Greek verb καθαρίζω (katharízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse". The prefix κεκα- indicates that the action has been completed in the past.

Grammar: κεκαθαρισμένους is a participle in the masculine gender, accusative case, and plural number. It is derived from the verb καθαρίζω in the perfect tense, passive voice, and participle form.

Description: κεκαθαρισμένους is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe individuals who have been purified or cleansed. Specifically, it is used in Hebrews 10:14 to describe those who have been sanctified through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ. The word conveys a sense of completeness and finality in the act of purification.
