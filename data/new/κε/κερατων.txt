Word: κερατων

Etymology:
The word κερατων comes from the Greek word κέρας (keras) which means "horn". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form nouns.

Grammar:
κερατων is a noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. κερατων is a masculine noun in the plural form.

Description:
κερατων refers to "horns" in Greek. It can be used to describe the horns of animals or the horns of objects such as musical instruments. In Greek mythology, the god Pan is often depicted with horns on his head. The word κερατων can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is sharp or pointed.
