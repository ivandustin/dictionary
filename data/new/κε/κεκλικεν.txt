Word: κεκλικεν

Etymology:
The word κεκλικεν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb κλίνω (klinó), which means "to lean" or "to incline". The prefix κεκ- indicates that the action of leaning or inclining has already been completed in the past.

Grammar:
κεκλικεν is a third-person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb κλίνω. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or assertion about the action.

Description:
The word κεκλικεν can be translated as "he/she/it has leaned/inclined". It describes an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.
