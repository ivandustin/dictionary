Word: κελευσας

Etymology:
The word κελευσας (keleusas) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It is derived from the root word κελευω (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order". The suffix -ας (-as) is added to the root word to indicate the past tense.

Grammar:
κελευσας (keleusas) is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is used to describe a specific action that was completed in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: κελευσας (keleusas) - "you commanded"
- Plural: κελευσατε (keleusate) - "you all commanded"

Description:
The word κελευσας (keleusas) is used to describe an action of commanding or ordering someone to do something. It is often used in the context of military or authoritative situations. The word implies a sense of authority and power, as the person who is doing the commanding is in a position of control.
