Word: κεφαλαι (kephalai)

Etymology:
The word κεφαλαι (kephalai) comes from the Greek word κεφαλή (kephalē), which means "head" or "capital". In ancient Greece, κεφαλαι referred to the principal sum of money in a loan or investment.

Grammar:
κεφαλαι is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative plural form.

Description:
In modern Greek, κεφαλαι (kephalai) means "chapters" or "headings". However, in ancient Greek, it referred to the principal sum of money in a loan or investment. The word is also used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the "chief cornerstone" or "foundation stone" of a building.
