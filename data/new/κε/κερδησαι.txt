Word: κερδησαι

Etymology:
The word κερδησαι comes from the Greek verb κερδίζω (kerdízo), which means "to gain, to win, to profit". It is derived from the noun κέρδος (kérdos), which means "gain, profit, advantage".

Grammar:
κερδησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the infinitive mood is used to express the idea of the verb without specifying any subject or tense.

Description:
κερδησαι means "to gain" or "to profit" in Greek. It can be used in various contexts, such as in business or personal relationships. The word implies the acquisition of something valuable or beneficial, whether it be material or non-material.
