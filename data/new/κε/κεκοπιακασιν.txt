Word: κεκοπιακασιν

Etymology: The word κεκοπιακασιν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb κοπιάζω (kopiázo), which means "to toil" or "to work hard". The prefix κεκοπια- (kekopiá-) indicates that the action has been completed in the past.

Grammar: κεκοπιακασιν is a third-person plural form of the verb κοπιάζω in the perfect tense. It is in the indicative mood and the active voice. The suffix -σιν (-sin) indicates that the action was completed by a group of people.

Description: κεκοπιακασιν can be translated as "they have toiled" or "they have worked hard". It implies that a group of people have put in a significant amount of effort to complete a task or achieve a goal. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing the hard work of a team of athletes, the efforts of a group of workers to complete a project, or the dedication of a group of volunteers to a cause.
