Word: κενος

Etymology:
The word κενος (kenos) is derived from the Greek language. It comes from the root word κενός (kenos), which means "empty" or "void". The word has been used in Greek literature since ancient times.

Grammar:
In Greek, κενος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). The declension of the word depends on the gender and case of the noun it is describing.

Description:
The word κενος has several meanings in Greek. It can mean "empty", "void", "vacant", "desolate", "barren", or "worthless". It is often used to describe physical spaces or objects that are empty or devoid of something. It can also be used to describe a person's emotional state, such as feeling empty or hollow inside. In philosophy, the term κενος is used to describe the concept of emptiness or nothingness.
