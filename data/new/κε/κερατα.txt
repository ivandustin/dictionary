Word: κερατα

Etymology: The word κερατα (kerata) is derived from the Greek word κέρας (keras), which means "horn". It is a plural form of κέρας.

Grammar: κερατα is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative plural form.

Description: κερατα refers to "horns" in Greek. It can be used to describe the horns of animals or the horns of objects. In Greek mythology, many creatures were depicted with horns, such as the Minotaur and the satyrs. The word κερατα can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is sharp or pointed.
