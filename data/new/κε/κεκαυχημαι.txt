Word: κεκαυχημαι

Etymology:
The word κεκαυχημαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καυχαομαι, which means "to boast" or "to brag". The prefix κε- indicates that the action is completed or perfect, so κεκαυχημαι means "I have boasted" or "I have bragged".

Grammar:
κεκαυχημαι is a first-person singular perfect indicative active verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the perfect tense suffix -κα to the present tense stem καυχα- and then adding the personal ending -ημαι, which indicates that the subject is the first-person singular.

Description:
κεκαυχημαι is a strong word that conveys a sense of pride and self-promotion. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is arrogant or boastful. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe people who boast about their own accomplishments or abilities, rather than giving credit to God.
