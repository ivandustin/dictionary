Etymology: The word "αετοί" (aetoi) comes from the Greek word "αετός" (aetos), which means "eagle". 

Grammar: "Αετοί" is the plural form of the noun "αετός" (aetos). It is a masculine noun in Greek and belongs to the second declension. 

Description: "Αετοί" refers to eagles in Greek. Eagles are large birds of prey known for their powerful build, keen eyesight, and soaring flight. They are often associated with strength, power, and freedom.
