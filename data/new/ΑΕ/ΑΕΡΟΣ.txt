Etymology: The word "αερος" (aeros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂éh₁os, meaning "air" or "wind."

Grammar: In Greek, "αερος" (aeros) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form of "αερου" (aerou). It can be used in the singular or plural form, depending on the context.

Description: "Αερος" (aeros) means "air" in Greek. It refers to the invisible, odorless, and tasteless substance that surrounds the Earth and is essential for the survival of living organisms. In Greek mythology, "Aer" was also the personification of the air.
