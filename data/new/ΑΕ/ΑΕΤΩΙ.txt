Etymology: The word "αετών" (aeton) comes from the Greek word "αετός" (aetos), which means "eagle". 

Grammar: "Αετών" is the genitive plural form of the noun "αετός" (aetos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. The plural form indicates that there are multiple eagles.

Description: "Αετών" refers to "of eagles" or "belonging to eagles". It is used to describe something that is related to or associated with eagles.
