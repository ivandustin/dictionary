Etymology: The word "εναντίους" comes from the Greek word "εναντίος" (enantíos), which means "opposite" or "contrary".

Grammar: "Εναντίους" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe multiple nouns that are opposite or contrary to something else.

Description: "Εναντίους" is used to describe things or people that are in opposition or contrary to something else. It indicates a contrast or a difference in characteristics, opinions, or actions.
