Etymology: The word "εντρεπομαι" (entrepomai) comes from the Greek root "εντροπή" (entropi), which means shame or embarrassment.

Grammar: "Εντρέπομαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ εντρέπομαι (egó entrepomai) - I am ashamed
- Εσύ εντρέπεσαι (esý entrepesai) - You are ashamed
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό εντρέπεται (aftós/aftí/aftó entrepetai) - He/She/It is ashamed
- Εμείς εντρεπόμαστε (emeís entrepómaste) - We are ashamed
- Εσείς εντρέπεστε (eseís entrepéste) - You are ashamed
- Αυτοί/Αυτές εντρέπονται (aftoí/aftés entrepóntai) - They are ashamed

Description: "Εντρέπομαι" is a Greek verb that expresses the feeling of shame or embarrassment. It is used to describe the state of being ashamed or feeling guilty about something. It can also imply a sense of modesty or self-consciousness.
