Etymology: The word "ενοχοι" (enochi) comes from the Greek word "ενοχος" (enochos), which means "guilty" or "culpable". 

Grammar: "Ενοχοι" is the plural form of the word "ενοχος" (enochos), which is an adjective in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. 

Description: "Ενοχοι" is used to describe multiple individuals who are guilty or culpable for a certain action or offense. It implies that these individuals are responsible for their actions and should be held accountable.
