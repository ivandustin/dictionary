Etymology: The word "εντεταλται" comes from the Greek verb "ενταλλω" (entallō), which means "to command" or "to order". It is derived from the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "within", and the verb "ταλλω" (tallō), meaning "to accomplish" or "to bring about".

Grammar: "Εντεταλται" is the third person singular form of the verb "ενταλλω" in the present tense. It is in the indicative mood and the active voice. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on person, number, tense, mood, and voice.

Description: "Εντεταλται" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it commands" or "he/she/it orders". It is used to express the action of giving a command or an order.
