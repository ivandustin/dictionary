Etymology: The word "εντολήν" (entolēn) comes from the Greek verb "ἐντέλλω" (entellō), which means "to order" or "to command."

Grammar: "Εντολήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in Greek.

Description: "Εντολήν" refers to a command, order, or instruction given to someone. It can also be used to describe a directive or a mandate.
