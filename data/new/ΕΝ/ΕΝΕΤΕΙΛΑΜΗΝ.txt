Etymology: The word "ενετειλαμην" comes from the Greek verb "εντέλλω" (entéllō), which means "to order" or "to command". It is derived from the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into", and the verb "τέλλω" (téllō), meaning "to accomplish" or "to bring about".

Grammar: "ενετειλαμην" is the first person singular, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "εντέλλω" (entéllō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The middle voice indicates that the subject of the verb is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "ενετειλαμην" can be translated as "I ordered" or "I commanded myself". It implies that the speaker gave an order or command to themselves, indicating a self-imposed obligation or directive.
