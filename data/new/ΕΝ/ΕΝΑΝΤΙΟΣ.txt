Etymology: The word "εναντίος" (enantios) comes from the Greek prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "opposite" or "against," and the word "αντίος" (antios), meaning "opposite" or "contrary."

Grammar: "Εναντίος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Εναντίος" is used to describe something or someone that is opposite or contrary to another thing or person. It can refer to opposing ideas, opinions, actions, or characteristics.
