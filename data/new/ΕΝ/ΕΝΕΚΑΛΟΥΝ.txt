Etymology: The word "ενεκαλούν" comes from the Greek verb "ενεκαλέω" (enekaleo), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke." It is derived from the preposition "εν" (en), meaning "in" or "on," and the verb "καλέω" (kaleo), meaning "to call."

Grammar: "Ενεκαλούν" is the third person plural form of the verb "ενεκαλέω" (enekaleo) in the present tense. It is used to describe an action that is being performed by a group of people or things.

Description: "Ενεκαλούν" is a Greek verb that is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts. It is used to describe the act of calling upon or invoking a deity, a higher power, or a sacred entity. It implies a sense of reverence, respect, and supplication.
