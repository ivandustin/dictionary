Etymology: The word "ενιαυτος" (eniautos) comes from the combination of the Greek words "ενια" (enia), meaning "year," and "αυτος" (autos), meaning "self." 

Grammar: "Ενιαυτος" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "ενιαυτοι" (eniautoi). 

Description: "Ενιαυτος" refers to the concept of a year, which is the period of time it takes for the Earth to complete one revolution around the sun. It is commonly used to measure the passage of time and is divided into months, weeks, and days.
