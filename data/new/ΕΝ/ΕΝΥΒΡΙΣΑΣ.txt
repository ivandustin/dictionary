Etymology: The word "ενυβρισας" (enubrisas) comes from the Greek verb "ενυβριζω" (enubrizo), which means "to insult" or "to abuse". 

Grammar: "ενυβρισας" is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb "ενυβριζω" (enubrizo), which means "you insulted" or "you abused".

Description: "ενυβρισας" is a Greek word that describes an action of insulting or abusing someone. It is used to refer to the act of verbally or emotionally attacking someone in a disrespectful or offensive manner.
