Etymology: The word "εντολή" (entolí) comes from the Greek verb "εντέλλω" (entélo), which means "to order" or "to command". It is derived from the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into", and the verb "τέλλω" (téllo), meaning "to accomplish" or "to fulfill".

Grammar: "Εντολή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its plural form is "εντολές" (entolés). In the singular, it can be used with definite articles like "η εντολή" (the command) or indefinite articles like "μια εντολή" (a command).

Description: "Εντολή" refers to a command, order, or instruction given by someone in authority. It can also refer to a directive, a decree, or a mandate. In various contexts, it can be used to describe a legal or military order, a religious commandment, or a general instruction.
