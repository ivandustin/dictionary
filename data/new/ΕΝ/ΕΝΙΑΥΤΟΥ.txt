Etymology: The word "ενιαυτού" (eniautou) comes from the Greek words "ενιαυτός" (eniautos), meaning "year," and "-ου" (-ou), a genitive ending. 

Grammar: "Ενιαυτού" is a noun in the genitive case, singular number. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical case, number, and gender. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Ενιαυτού" refers to the genitive form of the Greek word for "year." It is used to indicate possession or origin in relation to a year. For example, "το τέλος του ενιαυτού" (to telos tou eniautou) means "the end of the year."
