Etymology: The word "ενορκίζω" (enorkízo) comes from the Greek root "ορκίζω" (orkízo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath." The prefix "εν-" (en-) adds the meaning of "in" or "within," so "ενορκίζω" can be translated as "to swear in" or "to administer an oath."

Grammar: "Ενορκίζω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ ενορκίζω (Egó enorkízo) - I swear in
- Εσύ ενορκίζεις (Esý enorkízeis) - You swear in
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό ενορκίζει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó enorkízei) - He/She/It swears in
- Εμείς ενορκίζουμε (Emeís enorkízoume) - We swear in
- Εσείς ενορκίζετε (Eseís enorkízete) - You (plural) swear in
- Αυτοί/Αυτές ενορκίζουν (Aftoí/Aftés enorkízoun) - They swear in

Description: "Ενορκίζω" refers to the act of administering an oath or making someone swear to tell the truth or fulfill a promise. It is commonly used in legal or official contexts, such as in courtrooms or during the swearing-in of public officials.
