Etymology: The word "ενεδρευούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "ενεδρεύω" (enedrevo), which means "to lie in wait" or "to ambush". It is derived from the noun "ενέδρα" (enedra), meaning "ambush" or "trap".

Grammar: "Ενεδρευούσιν" is the present tense, indicative mood, active voice, third person plural form of the verb "ενεδρεύω" (enedrevo). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "Ενεδρευούσιν" is a Greek verb that describes the action of lying in wait or ambushing. It indicates that a group of people or individuals are currently engaged in this activity.
