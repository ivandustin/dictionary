Etymology: The word "ενκατοικών" comes from the Greek roots "εν" (en), meaning "in," and "κατοικώ" (katoikó), meaning "to dwell" or "to inhabit." 

Grammar: "Ενκατοικών" is a present participle in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ών" to the verb stem "κατοικ-". The present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description: "Ενκατοικών" can be translated as "dwelling" or "inhabiting" in English. It refers to the act of residing or living in a particular place.
