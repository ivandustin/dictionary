Etymology: The word "ενκρατειαν" comes from the Greek word "ἐγκράτεια" (enkrateia), which means self-control or self-restraint. It is derived from the prefix "ἐν" (en), meaning "within," and the root "κράτος" (kratos), meaning "power" or "strength."

Grammar: "ενκρατειαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "ενκρατειαν" refers to the concept of self-control or self-restraint. It is the ability to control one's desires, impulses, or actions. In a broader sense, it can also refer to having mastery over oneself or having discipline in various aspects of life, such as emotions, habits, or temptations.
