Etymology: The word "ενγεγραπται" comes from the Greek verb "ενγράφω" (engráfo), which means "to write" or "to record." The suffix "-ται" indicates the passive voice, so "ενγεγραπται" can be translated as "it is written" or "it is recorded."

Grammar: "Ενγεγραπται" is a third-person singular form of the verb "ενγράφω" in the present tense, passive voice. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, voice, and mood.

Description: "Ενγεγραπται" is a verb form that indicates an action being done to the subject. It implies that something is being written or recorded by someone or something else. The exact meaning and context of the word depend on the surrounding sentence or discourse.
