Etymology: The word "εντιμοτερος" comes from the Greek word "εντιμος" (entimos), meaning "honorable" or "esteemed", and the suffix "-τερος" (-teros), which is used to form the comparative degree in Greek.

Grammar: "Εντιμοτερος" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is used to compare the degree of honor or esteem between two things or people. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: "Εντιμοτερος" describes something or someone as more honorable or esteemed compared to another. It implies a higher level of respect or value.
