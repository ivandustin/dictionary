Grammar: "ενιψαμην" is the first person singular aorist middle voice form of the verb "νίπτω" (nipto). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on or for itself.

Description: "ενιψαμην" translates to "I washed myself" or "I bathed" in English. It is used to describe the action of washing oneself or taking a bath in the past.
