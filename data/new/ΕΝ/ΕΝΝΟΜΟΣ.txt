Etymology: The word "εννομος" (ennomos) comes from the Greek roots "εν" (en), meaning "in" or "within," and "νομος" (nomos), meaning "law." Therefore, "εννομος" can be translated as "within the law" or "lawful."

Grammar: In Greek, "εννομος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if used to describe a masculine singular noun, it would be "εννομος," while for a feminine singular noun, it would be "εννομη," and for a neuter singular noun, it would be "εννομον." In the plural form, it would be "εννομοι" for masculine, "εννομες" for feminine, and "εννομα" for neuter.

Description: The word "εννομος" is often used to describe something or someone that is in accordance with the law, legal, or lawful. It implies that the subject is following the rules and regulations set by a governing authority.
