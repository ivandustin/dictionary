Etymology: The word "ενικησεν" comes from the Greek verb "νικάω" (nikao), which means "to conquer" or "to defeat". The prefix "εν-" (en-) is added to indicate the past tense, resulting in "ενικησεν" (enikesen), which means "he/she/it conquered" or "he/she/it defeated".

Grammar: "ενικησεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "νικάω" in the aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the sentence is a singular third person (he/she/it).

Description: "ενικησεν" is a word in the Greek language that describes an action of conquering or defeating that has already been completed in the past. It can be used to refer to a person, group, or entity that has achieved victory over someone or something else.
