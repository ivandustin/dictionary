Etymology: The word "ενκαλούμαι" (enkaloúmai) comes from the Greek verb "καλώ" (kaló), which means "to call" or "to invite." The prefix "εν-" (en-) adds the meaning of "in" or "into," so "ενκαλούμαι" can be translated as "to be called in" or "to be invited in."

Grammar: "Ενκαλούμαι" is a reflexive verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, it means "I call myself in" or "I invite myself in."

Description: "Ενκαλούμαι" is a Greek verb that is used to express the action of calling or inviting oneself in. It can be used in various contexts, such as when someone invites themselves to a gathering or when someone calls themselves into a situation or conversation.
