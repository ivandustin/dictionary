Etymology: The word "εννομω" comes from the Greek verb "νομίζω" (nomízo), which means "to think" or "to believe". The prefix "εν-" (en-) adds the meaning of "in" or "within", so "εννομω" can be translated as "to think within" or "to understand".

Grammar: "Εννομω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Εγώ εννοώ (ennoó) - I understand
- Εσύ εννοείς (ennoís) - You understand
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό εννοεί (aftós/aftí/aftó ennoí) - He/She/It understands
- Εμείς εννοούμε (emís ennooúme) - We understand
- Εσείς εννοείτε (esís ennoéte) - You understand
- Αυτοί/Αυτές εννοούν (aftí/aftés ennooún) - They understand

Description: "Εννομω" is a versatile verb that can be used to express various meanings related to understanding, comprehending, or interpreting something. It can also be used to convey the idea of intending or meaning something. The context in which it is used determines its precise meaning.
