Etymology: The word "ενικησαν" comes from the Greek verb "νικάω" (nikao), which means "to conquer" or "to defeat". The prefix "εν-" (en-) is added to indicate the past tense, resulting in "ενικησαν" (enikisan), which means "they conquered" or "they defeated".

Grammar: "Ενικησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "νικάω" in the past tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject, tense, and mood. The "-αν" (-an) ending is used for the third person plural in the past tense.

Description: "Ενικησαν" is a Greek verb that describes an action of conquering or defeating. It is used to refer to a group of people or entities who have achieved victory over someone or something.
