Etymology: The word "εντευθεν" (entefthen) comes from the Greek roots "εν" (en), meaning "in" or "within," and "θεν" (then), meaning "there" or "from there." It can be translated as "from within" or "from here."

Grammar: "Εντευθεν" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate a location or origin, similar to the English words "from here" or "from within."

Description: "Εντευθεν" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is often used to indicate the origin or starting point of something, whether it is a physical location or a figurative concept. It can also be used to express the idea of "from this point forward" or "from this moment on."
