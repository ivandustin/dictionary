The word "ενκακωμέν" (enkakomen) is a Greek verb in the passive voice, which means "to be entangled" or "to be ensnared." Here is a breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "ενκακωμέν" comes from the combination of the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into," and the verb "κάκωση" (kakose), meaning "to entangle" or "to snare."

Grammar: "Ενκακωμέν" is the present participle form of the verb, which is used to describe a continuous action or state. In this case, it describes the state of being entangled or ensnared.

Description: The word "ενκακωμέν" is often used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone is caught up in a complex or difficult situation, unable to escape or find a solution. It can also be used to describe a physical entanglement, such as being caught in a web or tangled in ropes.
