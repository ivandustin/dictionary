Etymology: The word "ενκυωι" (enkuōi) comes from the Greek verb "ενκυειν" (enkyein), which means "to be pregnant" or "to conceive." 

Grammar: "Ενκυωι" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the third person singular form. 

Description: The word "ενκυωι" is used to describe the action of being pregnant or conceiving. It refers to the state of a female organism carrying a developing offspring within her body.
