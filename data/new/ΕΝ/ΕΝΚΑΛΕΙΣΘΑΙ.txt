The word "ενκαλείσθαι" is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "within," and the verb "καλείσθαι" (kaleisthai), meaning "to call" or "to summon." 

In terms of grammar, "ενκαλείσθαι" is an infinitive verb form in the passive voice. It is used to express the action of being called or summoned from within or by someone else.

In a broader sense, "ενκαλείσθαι" can also be used metaphorically to describe being influenced or motivated by something or someone. It implies a sense of being compelled or drawn towards a particular action or state.

Overall, "ενκαλείσθαι" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to convey the idea of being called, summoned, or influenced.
