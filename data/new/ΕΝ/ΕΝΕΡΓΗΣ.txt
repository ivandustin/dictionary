Etymology: The word "ενεργης" (energēs) comes from the Greek verb "ενεργεω" (energeō), which means "to work" or "to be active". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "within", and the root "εργον" (ergon), meaning "work" or "action".

Grammar: In Greek, "ενεργης" (energēs) is an adjective that is used to describe someone or something that is active, energetic, or effective. It can be used to describe a person who is actively engaged in work or a task, or to describe something that is in a state of action or operation.

Description: "Ενεργης" (energēs) is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can describe a person who is actively involved in their work or activities, showing enthusiasm and productivity. It can also describe something that is in a state of action or operation, indicating effectiveness and efficiency. Overall, "ενεργης" (energēs) conveys the idea of being active, dynamic, and engaged.
