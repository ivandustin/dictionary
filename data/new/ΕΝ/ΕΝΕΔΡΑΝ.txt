Etymology: The word "ενεδραν" comes from the Greek word "ενέδρα" (enédra), which means "ambush" or "trap".

Grammar: "Ενεδραν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender in the Greek language.

Description: "Ενεδραν" refers to an ambush or a trap. It is used to describe a situation where someone or something is lying in wait to surprise or attack others.
