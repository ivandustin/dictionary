Etymology: The word "ενιαυτον" (eniauton) comes from the Greek words "ενιαυτός" (eniautos), meaning "year," and "-ον" (-on), a suffix used to form neuter nouns. 

Grammar: "Ενιαυτον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Ενιαυτον" refers to a year, a period of time consisting of 365 or 366 days, depending on whether it is a leap year or not. It is commonly used in Greek to indicate the duration of time or to refer to a specific calendar year.
