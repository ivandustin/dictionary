Etymology: The word "ενεργειαν" (energeian) comes from the Greek word "ενέργεια" (energeia), which means "activity" or "operation". It is derived from the verb "ενεργέω" (energeo), meaning "to work" or "to act".

Grammar: "Ενεργειαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ενεργειαν" refers to the concept of energy. It is used to describe the capacity of a system or object to do work or produce an effect.
