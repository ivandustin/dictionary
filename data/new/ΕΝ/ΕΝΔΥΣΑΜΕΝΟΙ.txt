It is derived from the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into", and the verb "δύω" (dyo), meaning "to sink" or "to dive". The combination of these two elements gives the sense of "putting on" or "covering oneself with".

The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past. The participle mood indicates that the verb is functioning as an adjective.

Description: "Ενδυσαμένοι" describes a group of people who have dressed themselves or put on clothing. It implies that they have completed the action of dressing and are now in a state of being dressed.
