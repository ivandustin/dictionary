Etymology: The word "εωρακως" (eōrakōs) comes from the Greek verb "εωρακα" (eōraka), which means "I have seen." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "ε-" (e-), indicating completion, and the verb "οραω" (oraō), meaning "I see."

Grammar: "Εωρακως" (eōrakōs) is the third person singular, perfect active indicative form of the verb "εωρακα" (eōraka). In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance in the present.

Description: "Εωρακως" (eōrakōs) can be translated as "he/she/it has seen" in English. It is used to express the idea that someone or something has seen or observed something in the past, and that this action has a continuing effect or relevance in the present.
