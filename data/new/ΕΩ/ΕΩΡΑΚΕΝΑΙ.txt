Etymology: The word "εωρακεναι" (eōrakenai) comes from the Greek verb "οραω" (oraō), which means "to see". It is in the present active infinitive form.

Grammar: "εωρακεναι" (eōrakenai) is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The "-εναι" (-enai) ending is the infinitive form of the verb.

Description: "εωρακεναι" (eōrakenai) can be translated as "to have seen" or "to have looked". It expresses the idea of having experienced or witnessed something in the past, with the implication that the effects or consequences of that experience continue in the present.
