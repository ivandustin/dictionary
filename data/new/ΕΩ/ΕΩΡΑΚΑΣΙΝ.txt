Etymology: The word "εωρακασιν" (eōrakasin) comes from the Greek verb "ὁράω" (horaō), which means "to see" or "to perceive". It is in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "εωρακασιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "ὁράω" in the perfect tense. In Greek, verbs change their form based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The perfect tense is used to express a completed action with present relevance.

Description: "εωρακασιν" is used to describe an action that has been completed in the past but has ongoing effects in the present. It can be translated as "they have seen" or "they have perceived" in English.
