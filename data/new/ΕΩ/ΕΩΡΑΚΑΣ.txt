Etymology: The word "εωρακας" (eōrakas) comes from the Greek verb "οραω" (oraō), which means "to see". It is in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action with ongoing results.

Grammar: "Εωρακας" is the second person singular form of the verb "οραω" (oraō) in the perfect tense. It is used to address someone directly, indicating that the person being spoken to has seen something.

Description: "Εωρακας" is a Greek word that translates to "you have seen" in English. It is commonly used in everyday conversation to express the idea that someone has witnessed or observed something.
