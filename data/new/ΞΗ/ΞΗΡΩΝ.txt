Etymology: The word "ξηρων" (xērōn) comes from the Greek verb "ξηραίνω" (xērainō), which means "to dry up" or "to make dry."

Grammar: ξηρων is a noun in the nominative case, singular form.

Description: ξηρων refers to something that is dry or dried up. It can be used to describe various objects or substances that lack moisture or are in a state of dehydration.
