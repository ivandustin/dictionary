Etymology: The word "ξηρας" (xeras) comes from the Greek word "ξηρός" (xeros), meaning "dry".

Grammar: ξηρας (xeras) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number.

Description: ξηρας (xeras) refers to something that is dry. It can be used to describe the state or condition of an object or a place that lacks moisture or is not wet.
