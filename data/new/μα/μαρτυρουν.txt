Word: μαρτυρουν

Etymology: μαρτυρουν comes from the Greek word μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". It is derived from the noun μάρτυς (martys), which means "witness" or "martyr".

Grammar: μαρτυρουν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is the active voice of the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo).

Description: μαρτυρουν means "they testify" or "they bear witness" in Greek. It is often used in a legal or religious context, where someone is giving testimony or bearing witness to something. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is standing up for a particular belief or cause.
