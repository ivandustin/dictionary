The word is derived from the name of the ancient Macedonian kingdom, which was ruled by Alexander the Great and his successors. 

In terms of grammar, μακεδονος is a masculine noun in the singular form. It can be declined in various cases, such as the nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative. 

The word can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is related to Macedonia, such as Macedonian culture, Macedonian cuisine, or Macedonian history. 

Overall, μακεδονος is a significant word in Greek history and culture, representing the ancient kingdom of Macedonia and its influence on the world.
