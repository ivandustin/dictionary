Word: μανιαν

Etymology:
The word μανιαν is derived from the Greek word μανία (mania), which means "madness" or "insanity". The suffix -an is added to the end of the word to indicate a person or thing related to or possessing the quality of the root word.

Grammar:
μανιαν is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is masculine.

Description:
μανιαν refers to a person who is affected by mania, a mental illness characterized by periods of extreme excitement, euphoria, or irritability, often followed by periods of depression or sadness. The term can also be used more broadly to describe someone who is excessively enthusiastic or obsessed with something.
