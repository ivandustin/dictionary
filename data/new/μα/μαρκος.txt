Word: μαρκος

Etymology:
The name μαρκος (Markos) is of Greek origin and is derived from the Latin name Marcus. It is believed to have originated from the Roman god of war, Mars.

Grammar:
μαρκος is a masculine noun in Greek. It is declined in the nominative case as Μάρκος (Markos) and in the genitive case as Μάρκου (Markou).

Description:
μαρκος is a common Greek name, often given to boys. It has been used since ancient times and is still popular today. People with this name are often described as strong, brave, and determined. In Greek culture, the name is associated with the Christian evangelist Saint Mark, who is believed to have written the Gospel of Mark.
