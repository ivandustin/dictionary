Word: μαγους

Etymology:
The word μαγους (magous) is derived from the Greek word μάγος (magos), which means "magician" or "sorcerer". The word has its roots in the ancient Persian language, where it referred to a priestly caste of the Zoroastrian religion.

Grammar:
μαγους is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and is singular. The word is masculine in gender.

Description:
μαγους refers to a magician or sorcerer, someone who practices magic or sorcery. In ancient times, magicians were often associated with religious practices and were believed to have supernatural powers. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is skilled at manipulating or influencing others.
