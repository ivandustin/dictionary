Word: μακροθυμίας

Etymology:
The word μακροθυμίας comes from the Greek words μακρός (makrós) meaning "long" and θυμός (thymós) meaning "temper" or "passion". Therefore, μακροθυμίας literally means "long-tempered" or "patient".

Grammar:
μακροθυμίας is a noun in the genitive case in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "patience" or "long-suffering". It is a singular noun and has no plural form.

Description:
μακροθυμίας refers to the ability to endure difficult circumstances or people without becoming angry or upset. It is a virtue that is highly valued in many cultures and religions. In Christianity, for example, it is listed as one of the fruits of the Holy Spirit. The concept of μακροθυμίας is often associated with forgiveness, compassion, and empathy. It is considered a key component of emotional intelligence and is often seen as a sign of maturity and wisdom.
