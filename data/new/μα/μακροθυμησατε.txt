Word: μακροθυμησατε

Etymology:
The word μακροθυμησατε is derived from the Greek words μακρός (makrós) meaning "long" and θυμός (thymós) meaning "temper" or "passion". The suffix -σατε (-sate) is a second person plural imperative ending, which means "be patient" or "endure".

Grammar:
μακροθυμησατε is a verb in the second person plural imperative form. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people to be patient or endure a situation.

Description:
μακροθυμησατε is a word that encourages patience and endurance in difficult situations. It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to encourage people to persevere through trials and tribulations. The word emphasizes the importance of having a long temper and not giving in to anger or frustration.
