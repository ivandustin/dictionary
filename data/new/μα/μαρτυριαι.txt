Word: μαρτυριαι

Etymology: The word μαρτυριαι (marturiai) is derived from the Greek word μαρτυρία (marturia), which means testimony or witness. It is a plural form of the word, which means testimonies or witnesses.

Grammar: μαρτυριαι is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative plural case, which means it is the subject of a sentence or clause.

Description: μαρτυριαι is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the testimonies or witnesses of Jesus Christ. It is often used in the context of the martyrdom of early Christian believers who were persecuted for their faith.
