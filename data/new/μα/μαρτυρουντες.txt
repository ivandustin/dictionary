Word: μαρτυρουντες

Etymology:
The word μαρτυρουντες (marturountes) is derived from the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The suffix -ουντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to those who are currently bearing witness or testifying.

Grammar:
μαρτυρουντες is a plural form of the present participle of the verb μαρτυρέω. It is in the active voice and in the present tense. The word can function as a noun, meaning "witnesses" or "testifiers".

Description:
μαρτυρουντες is a Greek word that refers to those who are currently bearing witness or testifying. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the apostles and other early Christians who testified to the truth of Jesus Christ and his teachings. The word can also be used more generally to refer to anyone who bears witness or testifies to something.
