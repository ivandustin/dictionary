Word: μαστιγοι

Etymology:
The word μαστιγοι (mastigoi) is derived from the Greek word μάστιξ (mastix), which means "whip" or "scourge". The suffix -οι (-oi) is a plural ending in Greek.

μαστιγοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
μαστιγοι refers to whips or scourges used for punishment or torture. In ancient Greece, they were often used as a form of punishment for slaves and criminals.
