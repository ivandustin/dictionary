Word: ματαιος

Etymology:
The word ματαιος (mataios) comes from the Greek root word ματαιος (mataia), which means "vain" or "empty." It is derived from the word ματαιοτης (mataiotēs), which means "futility" or "vanity."

Grammar:
ματαιος is an adjective in Greek, which means it modifies a noun. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
ματαιος is used to describe something that is futile, empty, or vain. It can refer to something that is without purpose or meaning, or something that is worthless or insignificant. It can also be used to describe someone who is foolish or lacking in wisdom. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the fleeting nature of worldly possessions and pursuits, and the need to focus on eternal things.
