Word: μαργαριτην

The word μαργαριτην (margaritēn) is a Greek word that comes from the noun μαργαρίτης (margaritēs), which means "pearl". The word is derived from the Old Persian word "margārīta", which means "pearl".

Grammar:
μαργαριτην is the accusative singular form of the noun μαργαριτης. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

μαργαριτην refers to a single pearl. Pearls are formed inside the shells of certain mollusks, such as oysters and mussels, and are highly valued for their beauty and rarity.
