Word: μαλακιαν

Etymology: The word μαλακιαν comes from the Greek word μαλακία (malakía), which means "softness" or "weakness". It is derived from the adjective μαλακός (malakós), which means "soft" or "mild".

Grammar: μαλακιαν is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. The genitive form of the word is μαλακιανού (malakianou).

Description: μαλακιαν is a derogatory term used in Greek to describe someone who is weak, cowardly, or lacking in courage. It is often used as an insult to belittle someone's masculinity or strength. The word is considered vulgar and offensive, and its use is not recommended in polite conversation.
