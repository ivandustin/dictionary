The word μαρτυρω (marturo) is a Greek verb that means "to bear witness" or "to testify". It comes from the root word μαρτυς (martys), which means "witness" or "martyr". In terms of grammar, μαρτυρω is a first-person singular present active indicative verb, meaning "I bear witness" or "I testify". It can also be conjugated in other tenses and persons to indicate different forms of testimony. Overall, μαρτυρω is a powerful word that conveys the act of giving testimony or bearing witness to something important.
