Word: μαθθαιον

Etymology:
The word μαθθαιον (mathaion) is derived from the Greek word μαθητής (mathētēs), which means "disciple" or "learner". It is also related to the Greek word μαθηματικός (mathēmatikos), which means "mathematical".

Grammar:
μαθθαιον is a noun in the neuter gender. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is μαθθαια (mathaia).

Description:
μαθθαιον refers to a place or a thing where learning or teaching takes place. It can be translated as "school", "academy", or "place of learning". In ancient Greece, it was used to refer to a place where philosophers, mathematicians, and other scholars gathered to teach and learn. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to refer to a school or an educational institution.
