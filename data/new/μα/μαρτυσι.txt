Word: μαρτυσι

Etymology: The word μαρτυσι (martysi) is a Greek word that comes from the root word μάρτυρας (martyras), which means "witness" or "martyr". 

Grammar: μαρτυσι is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender. 

Description: μαρτυσι refers to a female witness or martyr. In Greek culture, martyrs were highly respected and revered for their bravery and dedication to their beliefs. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to someone who stands up for their beliefs or principles, even in the face of adversity.
