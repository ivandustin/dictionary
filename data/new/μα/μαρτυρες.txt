μαρτυρες (martures) is a Greek word that means "witnesses" or "testifiers". It is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". In Greek grammar, μαρτυρες is a plural noun in the nominative case. It can be used to refer to people who have witnessed or testified about something, or to describe someone who is a reliable or trustworthy witness. In Christian theology, the term is often used to refer to the apostles and other early followers of Jesus who testified to his life, teachings, and resurrection.
