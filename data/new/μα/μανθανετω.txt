Word: μανθανετω

Etymology:
The word μανθανετω comes from the Greek verb μανθάνω (manthánō), which means "to learn" or "to understand". The suffix -ετω (-etō) is the third person singular imperative form, which means "let him/her/it learn".

Grammar:
μανθανετω is a verb in the present tense, imperative mood, and active voice. It is the third person singular form, which means "let him/her/it learn". The verb belongs to the first conjugation class in Greek, which means that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs that end in -ω.

Description:
μανθανετω is a command or request for someone to learn or understand something. It is often used in educational or instructional contexts, such as in a classroom or when giving advice.
