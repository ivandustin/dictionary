The word "μανθανουσιν" (manthanousin) is a present active indicative verb form in Greek, which means "they learn" or "they are learning". 

Etymologically, the word comes from the Greek root "μανθάνω" (manthano), which means "to learn" or "to understand". 

In terms of grammar, "μανθανουσιν" is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. It is also in the active voice, meaning that the subject is performing the action of learning. The indicative mood suggests that the speaker is making a statement of fact. 

Overall, "μανθανουσιν" is a versatile verb form that can be used to describe ongoing learning or the act of acquiring knowledge.
