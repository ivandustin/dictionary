Word: μαγος

Etymology:
The word μαγος (magos) comes from the ancient Persian word maguš, which referred to a member of the priestly caste of the Medes and Persians. The term was later adopted by the Greeks to refer to a wise man, magician, or sorcerer.

Grammar:
In Greek, μαγος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is μαγος, and its genitive singular form is μαγου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
μαγος is a term that can have different meanings depending on the context. In ancient Greece, it was often used to refer to a magician or sorcerer who claimed to have supernatural powers. In the New Testament, the term is used to describe the wise men who visited the baby Jesus, and in modern Greek, it can refer to a magician or a wizard. Overall, the word μαγος is associated with the idea of someone who possesses special knowledge or abilities that are beyond the ordinary.
