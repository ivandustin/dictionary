Word: μαρτυς

Etymology:
The word μαρτυς (martys) comes from the Greek root word μαρτυρέω (martyreo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". In ancient Greece, a μαρτυς was someone who witnessed a crime or event and was called upon to testify in court.

Grammar:
In Greek, μαρτυς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is μαρτυρες (martures). It is often used in the New Testament to refer to those who witnessed the life and teachings of Jesus Christ.

Description:
μαρτυς is a word that carries a strong sense of testimony and witness. It is often used in religious contexts to refer to those who bear witness to their faith or to the truth of a particular doctrine. In modern Greek, the word has taken on a broader meaning and can refer to anyone who testifies or gives evidence in a legal or political context.
