Word: μαρτυρησας

Etymology: μαρτυρησας is a Greek verb that comes from the noun μαρτυρία (marturia), which means testimony or witness. The verb μαρτυρησας means to bear witness or testify.

Grammar: μαρτυρησας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed from the stem μαρτυρ- (martur-) and the suffix -ησας (-isas). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: μαρτυρησας is a powerful word that conveys the act of bearing witness or testifying to something. It is often used in legal or religious contexts, where a person is called upon to give testimony about an event or experience. The word carries a sense of responsibility and truthfulness, as the act of testifying requires a person to speak honestly and accurately about what they have seen or heard. In Christian theology, the word is often used to describe the act of bearing witness to one's faith in Jesus Christ.
