μαρτυρουσης (marturousēs) is a Greek word that is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martureō), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The word μαρτυρουσης is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb, which means "of one who is testifying" or "of a witness". 

In terms of grammar, μαρτυρουσης is a feminine noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. It is often used in the New Testament to refer to witnesses of Christ or God.

Overall, μαρτυρουσης is a word that is closely associated with the concept of testimony and witness, and is often used in religious contexts.
