Word: μαθθαῖος

Etymology: The word μαθθαῖος comes from the Greek word μαθητής (mathētēs), which means "disciple" or "learner". The suffix -αῖος (-aios) is added to indicate someone who is associated with or belongs to a particular group or profession.

Grammar: μαθθαῖος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (μαθθαίου), dative (μαθθαίῳ), and accusative (μαθθαῖον).

Description: μαθθαῖος refers to a disciple or follower of a teacher or master. In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to the disciples of Jesus Christ. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to any student or learner.
