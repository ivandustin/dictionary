Word: μαρτυρας

Etymology: μαρτυρας comes from the Greek word μάρτυρας (martyras), which means "witness" or "testifier". It is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martyreo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify".

Grammar: μαρτυρας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: μαρτυρας is a word that is commonly used in the Greek language to refer to a witness or someone who testifies in a legal or religious context. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to refer to those who witnessed the life and teachings of Jesus Christ.
