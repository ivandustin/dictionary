The word μαχεσθαι (machesethai) is a Greek verb that means "to fight" or "to engage in battle". It is derived from the noun μάχη (mache), which means "battle" or "fight". 

In terms of grammar, μαχεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive, which means that it can be translated as "to be fighting" or "to be engaged in battle". It can also be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects and attitudes towards the action of fighting.

Overall, μαχεσθαι is a versatile and important word in Greek, reflecting the culture's emphasis on martial prowess and the valorization of heroic deeds in battle.
