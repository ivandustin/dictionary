Word: μαθων

Etymology:
The word μαθων (mathon) is a Greek noun that means "learner" or "student". It is derived from the verb μανθανω (manthano), which means "to learn" or "to understand".

Grammar:
μαθων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
μαθων is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a student or learner. It is often used in the context of philosophical or educational discussions. The word emphasizes the idea of active learning and seeking knowledge, rather than simply receiving information passively. In some contexts, it can also refer to a disciple or follower of a particular teacher or school of thought.
