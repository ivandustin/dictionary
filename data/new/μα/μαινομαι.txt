Word: μαινομαι

Etymology:
The word μαινομαι (mainomai) comes from the Greek root word μαίνομαι (mainomai), which means "to be mad" or "to rage." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men-, which means "to think" or "to remember."

μαινομαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Μαίνομαι (mainomai) - I am mad
- Μαίνεσαι (mainesai) - You are mad
- Μαίνεται (mainetai) - He/She/It is mad

Plural:
- Μαίνομεθα (mainometha) - We are mad
- Μαίνεσθε (mainesthe) - You are mad
- Μαίνονται (mainontai) - They are mad

Description:
μαινομαι is a Greek verb that means "to be mad" or "to rage." It is often used to describe someone who is acting in an irrational or frenzied manner. In Greek mythology, the god Dionysus was often associated with madness and was said to have the power to drive people insane.
