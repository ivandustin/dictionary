Etymology:
The word μακροθυμησας comes from the Greek words μακρός (makrós) meaning "long" and θυμός (thymós) meaning "temper" or "passion". The suffix -ησας (-isas) is a past tense ending, indicating that the word is a verb in the past tense.

Grammar:
μακροθυμησας is a verb in the past tense, third person singular. It is derived from the verb μακροθυμέω (makrothyméō), which means "to be patient" or "to endure patiently". The verb is formed by adding the suffix -ησας (-isas) to the stem μακροθυμη- (makrothymē-).

Description:
μακροθυμησας describes an action that was done in the past, specifically an action of enduring patiently or being patient for a long time. It is often used in a religious or moral context, referring to the patience and endurance required to follow a particular path or belief system.
