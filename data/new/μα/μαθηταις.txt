Word: μαθηταῖς

Etymology: μαθηταῖς is a Greek word that comes from the root word μανθάνω (manthánō), which means "to learn." It is the plural form of μαθητής (mathētēs), which means "disciple" or "student."

Grammar: μαθηταῖς is a noun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. In this form, it means "to/for the students."

Description: μαθηταῖς is a word that refers to a group of students or disciples. It is often used in the context of religious or philosophical teachings, such as in the New Testament where it is used to refer to the disciples of Jesus.
