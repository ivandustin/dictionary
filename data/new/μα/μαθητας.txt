Word: μαθητάς

Etymology:
The word μαθητάς (mathētás) is derived from the Greek verb μανθάνω (manthánō), which means "to learn." The suffix -τάς (-tás) is added to the verb stem to form the noun μαθητάς, which means "learner" or "disciple."

Grammar:
In Greek, μαθητάς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive μαθητοῦ (mathētoú) or the accusative μαθητήν (mathētḗn).

Description:
μαθητάς is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to a student or disciple who is learning from a teacher or master. The term is often used in the New Testament to refer to the disciples of Jesus Christ. In modern Greek, the term is still used to refer to a student or learner.
