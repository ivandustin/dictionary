Word: μακρα

Etymology:
The word μακρα (makra) is derived from the Greek adjective μακρός (makrós), which means "long" or "tall". It is related to the Latin word macer, which means "lean" or "thin".

Grammar:
In Greek, μακρα is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It is often used in the plural form μακρές (makrés).

Description:
μακρα can refer to a variety of things that are long or tall, such as a long piece of cloth or a tall building. It can also be used to describe a long period of time or a long distance. In modern Greek, it is often used to refer to a long and narrow street or alleyway.
