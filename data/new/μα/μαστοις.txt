Word: μαστοις

Etymology: μαστοις is a Greek word that comes from the root word μαστός (mastós), which means "breast" or "mammary gland".

Grammar: μαστοις is a noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: μαστοις refers to the mammary glands or breasts of a female mammal. It is often used in medical or scientific contexts to describe the anatomy or physiology of these glands. In ancient Greek mythology, the goddess Hera was often depicted nursing her son, which is why the word μαστοις is sometimes associated with motherhood and nurturing.
