Word: μαρτυριας

Etymology: The word μαρτυριας comes from the Greek word μαρτυρία (marturia), which means testimony or witness. It is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means to bear witness or testify.

Grammar: μαρτυριας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of testimony" or "of witness."

Description: μαρτυριας is a Greek word that refers to testimony or witness. It is often used in legal contexts to refer to evidence given in court. In the Christian faith, it is also used to refer to the act of bearing witness to one's faith. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of testimony or evidence given to support a claim or argument.
