Word: μαρτυρειν

Etymology:
The word μαρτυρειν (marturein) comes from the Greek root word μαρτυρία (marturia), which means "testimony" or "witness." The verb form μαρτυρειν means "to bear witness" or "to testify."

Grammar:
μαρτυρειν is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often used in the New Testament to describe the act of bearing witness to the truth of Jesus Christ.

Description:
μαρτυρειν is a powerful word that conveys the idea of giving testimony or bearing witness to something important. In the context of the New Testament, it is often used to describe the act of testifying to the truth of Jesus Christ and his teachings. The word carries a sense of conviction and commitment, as those who bear witness are often willing to suffer persecution or even death for their beliefs. Overall, μαρτυρειν is a word that speaks to the importance of standing up for what one believes in, even in the face of opposition.
