Word: μακαριζομεν

Etymology: The word μακαριζομεν comes from the Greek verb μακαρίζω (makarízo), which means "to bless" or "to make happy". It is derived from the adjective μακάριος (makários), which means "blessed" or "happy".

Grammar: μακαριζομεν is a present tense verb in the first person plural form. It is conjugated from the root verb μακαρίζω (makarízo) and the present tense suffix -ομεν (-omen).

Description: μακαριζομεν is a Greek verb that means "we bless" or "we make happy". It is often used in religious contexts, such as in prayers or blessings. It can also be used in a more general sense to express good wishes or congratulations.
