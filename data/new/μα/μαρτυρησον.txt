Word: μαρτυρησον

Etymology:
The word μαρτυρησον (marturēson) is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular form of the Greek word μαρτυρέω (martureō), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The word is derived from the noun μάρτυς (martys), which means "witness" or "martyr".

Grammar:
μαρτυρησον is a second person singular verb in the imperative mood. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. In this case, the speaker is commanding the listener to bear witness or testify.

Description:
μαρτυρησον is a strong and powerful word that conveys a sense of urgency and importance. It is often used in religious or legal contexts, where bearing witness or testifying is crucial. The word emphasizes the importance of speaking the truth and standing up for what is right, even in the face of adversity.
