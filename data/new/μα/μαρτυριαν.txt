Word: μαρτυριαν

Etymology:
The word μαρτυριαν (marturian) is derived from the Greek word μαρτυρία (marturia), which means testimony or witness. It is formed from the root word μάρτυς (martys), which means witness or martyr.

Grammar:
μαρτυριαν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
μαρτυριαν refers to the act of giving testimony or bearing witness. It can also refer to the evidence or testimony given in a legal or religious context. In Christian theology, the term is often used to refer to the testimony of the apostles and other early Christians about the life and teachings of Jesus Christ.
