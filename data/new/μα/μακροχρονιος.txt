Word: μακροχρονιος

Etymology: The word μακροχρόνιος (makrochronios) is derived from the Greek words μακρός (makrós) meaning "long" and χρόνος (chrónos) meaning "time". 

Grammar: μακροχρόνιος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined in the following cases: 

Nominative: μακροχρόνιος 
Genitive: μακροχρόνιου 
Accusative: μακροχρόνιο 
Vocative: μακροχρόνιε 

Description: μακροχρόνιος is used to describe something that lasts for a long time or has been going on for a long time. It can also refer to a person who has been in a certain position or job for a long time. For example, "Η εταιρεία αντιμετωπίζει μακροχρόνιες δυσκολίες" (The company is facing long-term difficulties) or "Ο μακροχρόνιος διευθυντής αποφάσισε να παραιτηθεί" (The long-serving director decided to resign).
