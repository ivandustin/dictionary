The word "ματαιων" (mataiōn) is a Greek adjective that means "vain" or "empty." It is derived from the noun "ματαιότης" (mataiotēs), which means "futility" or "vanity." In terms of grammar, "ματαιων" is in the genitive case, singular form, masculine gender. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe worldly pursuits or actions that are ultimately meaningless or futile.
