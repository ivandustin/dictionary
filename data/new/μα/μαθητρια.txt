Word: μαθήτρια

Etymology: The word μαθήτρια (mathitria) comes from the Greek word μαθητής (mathitis), which means "student" or "disciple". The suffix -τρια (-tria) is a feminine suffix used to indicate a female person or object.

Grammar: μαθήτρια is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The plural form of μαθήτρια is μαθήτριες (mathitries).

Description: μαθήτρια refers to a female student or disciple. It is commonly used in Greek schools to refer to a girl who is studying. The word can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any female who is learning or studying a particular subject.
