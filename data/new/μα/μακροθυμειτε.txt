Word: μακροθυμείτε

Etymology: The word μακροθυμείτε comes from the Greek words μακρός (makrós) meaning "long" and θυμός (thymós) meaning "temper" or "anger". The suffix -είτε (-eíte) is added to the verb stem to form the present tense plural imperative, meaning "be patient" or "endure".

Grammar: μακροθυμείτε is a verb in the present tense plural imperative form. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people to be patient or endure a situation.

Description: μακροθυμείτε is a word that encourages patience and endurance in difficult situations. It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to encourage people to persevere through trials and tribulations. The word emphasizes the importance of having a long temper and not giving in to anger or frustration.
