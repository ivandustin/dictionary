Word: μαρτυρία (marturia)

Etymology: The word μαρτυρία comes from the Greek root word μάρτυρ (martur), which means "witness." The suffix -ία (-ia) is added to form a noun, giving the word its meaning of "testimony" or "witnessing."

Grammar: μαρτυρία is a feminine noun in Greek, and its plural form is μαρτυρίες (marturies). It can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: μαρτυρία refers to the act of giving testimony or bearing witness to something. In legal contexts, it can refer to the testimony given in a court of law. In religious contexts, it can refer to the act of bearing witness to one's faith or beliefs. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of testimony or evidence given to support a claim or argument.
