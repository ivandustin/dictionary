Word: μαρτυρουσιν

Etymology: The word μαρτυρουσιν is derived from the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb.

Grammar: μαρτυρουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is an active indicative form of the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo).

Description: μαρτυρουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they bear witness" or "they testify". It is often used in a legal or religious context to describe the act of giving testimony or bearing witness to something. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who speaks out in support of a particular cause or belief.
