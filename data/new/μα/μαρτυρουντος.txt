Word: μαρτυρουντος

Etymology: μαρτυρουντος is the present participle of the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martyreo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The word comes from the noun μάρτυς (martys), which means "witness" or "martyr".

Grammar: μαρτυρουντος is a masculine singular genitive form of the present participle of the verb μαρτυρέω. In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship.

Description: μαρτυρουντος can be translated as "of the one bearing witness" or "of the one testifying". It is often used in the New Testament to describe someone who is giving testimony or bearing witness to the truth of something.
