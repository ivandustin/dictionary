Word: μαθητων

Etymology:
The word μαθητων (mathētōn) is a Greek word that is derived from the verb μανθάνω (manthanō), which means "to learn." The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a genitive plural ending, which means that μαθητων (mathētōn) translates to "of the learners" or "of the disciples."

Grammar:
μαθητων (mathētōn) is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, μαθητων (mathētōn) indicates that the noun it modifies is in the possession of or related to the learners or disciples.

Description:
μαθητων (mathētōn) is a word that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the disciples of Jesus Christ. It is also used in other contexts to refer to students or learners in general. The word carries connotations of being a follower or a student of a particular teacher or philosophy.
