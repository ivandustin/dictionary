Word: μαλιστα

Etymology:
μαλιστα (malista) is an adverb in Greek that means "most" or "especially". It is derived from the adjective μάλα (mala), which means "very" or "much".

Grammar:
μαλιστα is an adverb and does not have any inflections. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate emphasis or degree.

Description:
μαλιστα is a common word in modern Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express agreement or confirmation, similar to the English phrase "indeed". It can also be used to indicate that something is particularly true or important. For example, "Μαλιστα, η Ελλάδα είναι μια όμορφη χώρα" (Malista, i Ellada einai mia omorfi hora) means "Indeed, Greece is a beautiful country".
