Word: μαρτυρησαι

Etymology: μαρτυρησαι is a Greek verb that comes from the noun μάρτυς (martyr), which means witness. The verb μαρτυρησαι means to bear witness or testify.

Grammar: μαρτυρησαι is a first-person singular aorist active subjunctive form of the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means to bear witness or testify. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the subjunctive mood expresses doubt, uncertainty, or a hypothetical situation.

Description: μαρτυρησαι is a verb that is often used in legal or religious contexts to describe the act of bearing witness or testifying to the truth. It can also be used more broadly to describe any situation where someone is giving evidence or speaking out about something they have seen or experienced. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was often referred to as the "witness" or "testifier," and the verb μαρτυρέω was used to describe his role as a witness to the truth.
