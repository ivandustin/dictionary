Word: μακροθυμεί (makrothymeí)

Etymology:
The word μακροθυμεί (makrothymeí) is derived from two Greek words: μακρός (makrós) meaning "long" and θυμός (thymós) meaning "temper" or "passion". Therefore, the literal meaning of μακροθυμεί is "having a long temper" or "being patient".

Grammar:
μακροθυμεί is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb μακροθυμέω (makrothyméō), which means "to be patient" or "to endure patiently". 

Description:
μακροθυμεί is a word that describes the quality of being patient and having the ability to endure difficult situations without becoming angry or frustrated. It is often used to describe a person who is able to remain calm and composed in the face of adversity or provocation. In Greek philosophy, μακροθυμία (makrothymía) is considered a virtue, and is associated with self-control, wisdom, and inner strength.
