Word: ματην

Etymology:
The word ματην (maten) is derived from the Greek word μάταιος (mataios), which means "vain" or "useless". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *mad-, which means "to be confused" or "to be foolish".

Grammar:
ματην is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is often translated as "in vain" or "without purpose". It can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is useless or futile.

Description:
ματην is a word that is often used in Greek to describe something that is done without any purpose or result. It can be used to describe actions, thoughts, or words that are pointless or ineffective. It is often used in a negative context, to express disappointment or frustration with a situation. For example, "Έκανες ματην όλη αυτή τη δουλειά" (Ekaneς maten oli afti ti douleia) means "You did all this work for nothing".
