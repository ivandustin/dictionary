Word: μαθειν

Etymology:
The word μαθειν (mathein) is derived from the Greek root word μανθάνω (manthano), which means "to learn" or "to understand." It is also related to the Greek word μάθημα (mathema), which means "lesson" or "subject of study."

Grammar:
μαθειν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the present active infinitive form. It is often translated to mean "to learn" or "to understand."

Description:
μαθειν is a fundamental concept in Greek philosophy and education. It represents the process of acquiring knowledge and understanding through study and experience. The word is often used in the context of learning about oneself, others, and the world around us. In modern Greek, the word is still used to refer to the act of learning or studying.
