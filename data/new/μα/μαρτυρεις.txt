Word: μαρτυρεις

Etymology: μαρτυρεις comes from the Greek word μάρτυρας (mártiras), which means "witness" or "testifier". It is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martyréō), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify".

Description: μαρτυρεις is a verb that is used to describe the act of bearing witness or testifying. It is often used in legal or religious contexts, where someone is called upon to give testimony or evidence. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the role of the apostles and other witnesses who testified to the life and teachings of Jesus Christ.
