Word: μαρτυρηθεντες

Etymology:
The word μαρτυρηθεντες is derived from the Greek word μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The suffix -θεντες (-thentes) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the word refers to those who have been witnessed or testified about.

Grammar:
μαρτυρηθεντες is a plural masculine passive participle in the Greek language. It is declined in the nominative case and can function as a subject or predicate in a sentence. The word is formed by adding the suffix -θεντες to the stem of the verb μαρτυρέω.

Description:
μαρτυρηθεντες refers to those who have been witnessed or testified about. In a religious context, it can refer to the martyrs who have given their lives for their faith. In a legal context, it can refer to witnesses who have testified in a trial or hearing. The word emphasizes the passive nature of being witnessed or testified about, rather than actively bearing witness or testifying.
