Word: μαθηται

Etymology:
The word μαθηται (mathētai) is a Greek word that means "disciples" or "students". It is derived from the verb μανθανω (manthanō), which means "to learn" or "to understand".

Grammar:
μαθηται is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
μαθηται is a term used in the New Testament to refer to the disciples of Jesus Christ. The word is used to describe those who followed Jesus and learned from him.
