Word: μακραν

Etymology:
The word μακραν (makran) is derived from the Greek adjective μακρός (makrós), which means "long" or "far". It is related to the Latin word "longus" and the English word "macro".

Grammar:
μακραν is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate distance or extent. It can be translated as "far", "distant", "by far", or "greatly".

Description:
μακραν is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to indicate physical distance, as in "Το σπίτι μου είναι μακριά από εδώ" (My house is far from here). It can also be used to indicate a large difference or degree, as in "Αυτός είναι μακράν ο καλύτερος παίκτης στην ομάδα" (He is by far the best player on the team). Additionally, it can be used to indicate time, as in "Περπάτησα μακριά για δύο ώρες" (I walked far for two hours).
