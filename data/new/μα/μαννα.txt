Word: μαννα

Etymology: The word μαννα (manna) comes from the Hebrew word "man" which means "what is it?" or "bread from heaven". In the Bible, manna was the food that God provided for the Israelites during their journey in the desert.

Grammar: In Greek, μαννα is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular and its nominative form is μαννα. The genitive form is μαννας, the accusative form is μανναν, the dative form is μαννῃ, and the vocative form is μαννα.

Description: μαννα is a term used in the New Testament to refer to the bread that God provided for the Israelites in the desert. It is also used metaphorically to refer to spiritual nourishment or sustenance. In John 6:31-35, Jesus says, "I am the bread of life. Whoever comes to me will never go hungry, and whoever believes in me will never be thirsty." This passage is often interpreted as a reference to the spiritual nourishment that Jesus provides.
