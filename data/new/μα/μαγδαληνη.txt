μαγδαληνη (Magdalene)

Etymology: The name Magdalene is derived from the Hebrew name "Magdala," which means "tower" or "elevated, great, magnificent." In the New Testament, Mary Magdalene was a follower of Jesus Christ and was present at his crucifixion and resurrection.

Grammar: Magdalene is a proper noun and is typically used as a name for a person.

Description: Magdalene is a name that has been used for centuries and is often associated with religious significance. It is a popular name for girls and has been used in literature, art, and music. Mary Magdalene is a prominent figure in Christianity and is often depicted in art and literature as a symbol of repentance and devotion.
