Word: μαθητήν

Etymology:
The word μαθητήν (mathitín) is derived from the Greek verb μανθάνω (mantháno), which means "to learn." The suffix -τήν (-tín) is added to the verb stem to form the noun μαθητήν, which means "learner" or "student."

Grammar:
μαθητήν is a masculine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. For example, in the sentence "Ο δάσκαλος διδάσκει τον μαθητήν" (The teacher teaches the student), μαθητήν is in the accusative case because it is the direct object of the verb διδάσκει (teaches).

Description:
μαθητήν refers to a male student or learner. It can be used to describe someone who is studying in a formal educational setting, such as a school or university, or someone who is learning a new skill or subject on their own. The word can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is open to learning and willing to be taught.
