μαχας (máchas) is a Greek noun that means "battle" or "fight". 

Etymology: The word derives from the ancient Greek verb μάχομαι (máchomai), which means "to fight" or "to engage in battle".

Grammar: μαχας is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes μαχαι (machai).

Description: The word μαχας is often used in Greek mythology and literature to describe battles between gods, heroes, and mortals. It can also refer to any kind of conflict or struggle, whether physical or metaphorical.
