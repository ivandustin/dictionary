Etymology: The word "τηρώμεν" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to keep" or "to guard."

Grammar: "τηρώμεν" is the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice form of the verb "τηρέω" (tēréō) in Greek. It is the first person plural form, meaning "we keep" or "we guard."

Description: "τηρώμεν" is a Greek verb that expresses the action of keeping or guarding something. It can be used in various contexts, such as keeping a promise, guarding a secret, or preserving something valuable.
