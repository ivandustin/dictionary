Etymology: The word "τηρησης" (pronounced tirisēs) comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tireō), which means "to keep" or "to preserve."

Grammar: "Τηρησης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Τηρησης" refers to the act of keeping, preserving, or maintaining something. It can also be used to describe the state or condition of being kept or preserved.
