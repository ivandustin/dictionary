Etymology: The word "τηρών" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to keep" or "to guard."

Grammar: "Τηρών" is the present participle form of the verb "τηρέω" (tēréō) in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: "Τηρών" is used to describe a person or thing that is currently keeping or guarding something. It indicates an ongoing action or state of keeping or guarding.
