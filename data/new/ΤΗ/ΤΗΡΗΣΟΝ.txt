Etymology: The word "τηρησον" (tērēson) comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēreō), which means "to keep" or "to guard."

Grammar: "τηρησον" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "τηρέω" in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "keep" or "guard" something.

Description: "τηρησον" is a word in the Greek language that is used to express a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "keep" or "guard" something. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as giving directions, setting rules, or emphasizing the importance of preserving or protecting something.
