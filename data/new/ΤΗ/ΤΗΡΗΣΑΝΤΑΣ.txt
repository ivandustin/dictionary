Etymology: The word "τηρησαντας" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tereo), which means "to keep" or "to observe". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter-, meaning "to watch" or "to guard".

Grammar: "τηρησαντας" is the accusative plural form of the verb "τηρέω" (tereo) in the active voice. It is in the aorist tense, indicating a completed action in the past. The -ας ending indicates the masculine gender and accusative case, while the -ντας ending indicates the participle form.

Description: "τηρησαντας" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having kept" or "having observed". It is often used as a participle to describe an action that has been completed in the past.
