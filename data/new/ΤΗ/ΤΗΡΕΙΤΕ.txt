Etymology: The word "τηρείτε" (pronounced "tireite") comes from the Greek verb "τηρώ" (pronounced "tiro"), which means "to keep" or "to observe."

Grammar: "Τηρείτε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "τηρώ." It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to keep or observe something.

Description: "Τηρείτε" is a command in Greek that translates to "Keep" or "Observe" in English. It is often used in instructions or rules to tell people to follow or adhere to certain guidelines or regulations.
