Etymology: The word "τηρούντων" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to keep" or "to observe". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter-, meaning "to watch" or "to guard".

Grammar: "τηρούντων" is the present active participle, masculine genitive plural form of the verb "τηρέω" (tēréō). In Greek grammar, the present active participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The genitive plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is masculine and there are multiple individuals or objects being referred to.

Description: "τηρούντων" is used to describe a group of individuals or objects that are currently keeping or observing something. It implies an ongoing action or state of keeping or observing.
