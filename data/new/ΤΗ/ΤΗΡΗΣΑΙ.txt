Etymology: The word "τηρησαί" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tireo), which means "to keep, to guard, to observe." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter- meaning "to watch over, to guard."

Grammar: "τηρησαί" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "τηρέω" (tireo) in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for a specific subject or tense.

Description: "τηρησαί" is used to express the idea of "to keep, to guard, to observe" in Ancient Greek. It is often used in contexts where someone is instructed or commanded to keep or observe something.
