Etymology: The word "τηνδε" (tende) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the definite article "την" (ten) and the demonstrative pronoun "δε" (de).

Grammar: "τηνδε" is a feminine singular form in Greek. It is used as a demonstrative pronoun to indicate something that is close to the speaker or the person being addressed.

Description: In Greek, "τηνδε" is used to point out or refer to something that is nearby or in close proximity. It is similar to the English demonstrative pronoun "this" or "these".
