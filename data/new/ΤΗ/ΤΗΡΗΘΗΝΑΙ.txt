Grammar: "τηρηθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "τηρέω" (tēréō) in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb receives the action. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description: "τηρηθηναι" is used to express the idea of "to be kept, to be guarded, to be observed" in Greek. It implies that the subject of the verb is being watched over or protected by someone or something.
