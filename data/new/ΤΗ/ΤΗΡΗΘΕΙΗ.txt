Etymology: The word "τηρηθείη" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to guard" or "to keep". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter- meaning "to watch over" or "to observe".

Grammar: "τηρηθείη" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the third person singular, aorist tense, subjunctive mood, and passive voice. The subjunctive mood indicates a possibility or potentiality, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "τηρηθείη" is used to express the idea of something being guarded, observed, or kept. It implies a sense of protection or preservation. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action is uncertain or hypothetical.
