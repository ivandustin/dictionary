Etymology: The word "τηλικούτος" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by combining the words "τηλέ" (tele), meaning "far" or "distant," and "κούτος" (koutos), meaning "such" or "this." 

Grammar: "Τηλικούτος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is of such a kind or of such a nature. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: "Τηλικούτος" is used to indicate the quality or characteristic of something being of a particular kind or nature. It can be used to describe a person, an object, or a situation. For example, if someone says "Αυτός είναι ένας τηλικούτος άνθρωπος," it means "He is such a person" or "He is that kind of person."
