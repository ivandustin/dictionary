Etymology: The word "τηρώ" (pronounced "tiro") comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (pronounced "tireo"), which means "to keep" or "to guard."

Grammar: "Τηρώ" is the first person singular present tense form of the verb "τηρέω." It is used to express the action of keeping or guarding something in the present.

Description: "Τηρώ" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of keeping or guarding something. It can be used in various contexts, such as keeping a promise, guarding a secret, or preserving something valuable. The verb emphasizes the act of actively maintaining or protecting something.
