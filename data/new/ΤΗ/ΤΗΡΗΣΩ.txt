Etymology: The word "τηρησω" (pronounced "tiriso") comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (pronounced "tireo"), which means "to keep" or "to observe."

Grammar: "τηρησω" is the first person singular future tense form of the verb "τηρέω." It is used to express an action that will happen in the future, specifically when the subject "I" will keep or observe something.

Description: "τηρησω" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of keeping or observing something in the future. It can be used in various contexts, such as keeping a promise, observing a rule, or maintaining a certain behavior.
