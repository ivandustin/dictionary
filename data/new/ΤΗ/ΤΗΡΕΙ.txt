Etymology: The word "τηρεί" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tireo), which means "to keep" or "to guard."

Grammar: "Τηρεί" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "τηρέω" (tireo) in Greek. It is used to express the action of keeping or guarding something in the present.

Description: "Τηρεί" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of keeping or guarding something. It can be used in various contexts, such as keeping a promise, guarding a secret, or preserving something valuable.
