Etymology: The word "τηρείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tereo), which means "to keep, to guard, to observe". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter- meaning "to watch, to guard".

Grammar: "τηρείσθαι" is the present passive infinitive form of the verb "τηρέω" (tereo) in Greek. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description: "τηρείσθαι" is used to express the idea of "to be kept, to be guarded, to be observed" in Greek. It is often used in contexts where something needs to be preserved, protected, or followed.
