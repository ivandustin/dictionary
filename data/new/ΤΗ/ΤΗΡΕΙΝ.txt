Etymology: The word "τηρείν" (tērein) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēreō), which means "to guard" or "to keep". 

Grammar: "Τηρείν" is the present tense form of the verb in Greek. It is the third person singular form, which means it is used when referring to a single person or thing that is currently performing the action of guarding or keeping.

Description: "Τηρείν" is a verb in Greek that conveys the idea of guarding, keeping, or preserving something. It can be used in various contexts, such as guarding a place, keeping a secret, or preserving a tradition.
