Etymology: The word "τηρησις" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to guard" or "to keep". 

Grammar: "τηρησις" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular number. 

Description: "τηρησις" refers to the act of guarding, keeping, or observing something. It can also mean preservation, protection, or maintenance. In a broader sense, it can be used to describe the act of following or adhering to rules, laws, or principles.
