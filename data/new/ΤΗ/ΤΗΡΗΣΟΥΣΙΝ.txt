Etymology: The word "τηρησουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to keep" or "to observe." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter-, meaning "to watch" or "to guard."

Grammar: "τηρησουσιν" is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "τηρησουσιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to keep" or "to observe." It implies the action of preserving, guarding, or maintaining something.
