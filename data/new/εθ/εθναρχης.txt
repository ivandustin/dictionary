Word: εθναρχης

Etymology: The word εθναρχης comes from the Greek words εθνος (ethnos) meaning "nation" or "people" and αρχης (arches) meaning "ruler" or "leader". Therefore, εθναρχης can be translated as "ruler of a nation" or "leader of a people".

Grammar: εθναρχης is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in Greek. It is declined according to the second declension, with the genitive form being εθναρχου.

Description: εθναρχης is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to a leader or ruler of a nation or people. It was often used to describe the leaders of various Greek city-states or tribes. The role of the εθναρχης was to govern and protect their people, and to represent them in matters of diplomacy and warfare. The term is not commonly used in modern Greek, but it remains an important part of ancient Greek history and culture.
