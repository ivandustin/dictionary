The word "εθνεσιν" (ethnesin) is a Greek noun that means "nations" or "peoples." It is derived from the Greek word "εθνος" (ethnos), which means "nation" or "people." 

In terms of grammar, "εθνεσιν" is a plural noun in the accusative case. It can be used as the direct object of a verb or as the object of a preposition.

In description, "εθνεσιν" refers to a group of people who share a common culture, language, and history. It can also refer to a political entity or a geographical region inhabited by a particular group of people.
