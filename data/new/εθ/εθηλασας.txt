Word: εθηλάσας

Etymology: The word εθηλάσας comes from the Greek verb θηλάζω (thilazo), which means "to suckle" or "to nurse". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εθηλάσας means "you nursed" or "you suckled".

Grammar: εθηλάσας is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject (you) performed the action. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: εθηλάσας is a verb that describes the act of nursing or suckling. It is used in the second person singular form, so it refers specifically to the action of "you" nursing or suckling. The word can be used in a literal sense, to describe the act of a mother nursing her child, or in a figurative sense, to describe nurturing or caring for someone or something.
