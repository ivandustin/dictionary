Word: εθαυμασαν

Etymology: The word εθαυμασαν comes from the Greek verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that it is in the past tense.

Grammar: εθαυμασαν is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb θαυμάζω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the sentence is a group of people.

Description: εθαυμασαν can be translated to English as "they marveled" or "they wondered". It is often used to describe a group of people who are amazed or surprised by something. For example, in the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the reaction of the people who witnessed Jesus healing a paralyzed man (Mark 2:12).
