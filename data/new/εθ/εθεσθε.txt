Word: εθεσθε

Etymology: 
The word εθεσθε is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The prefix "ε-" is added to the root word to indicate the second person plural form of the verb, which means "you all place" or "you all set".

εθεσθε is a second person plural form of the verb in the aorist tense in the Greek language. It is used to address a group of people and indicates an action that was completed in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εθεσθε (etheste) - you all placed/you all set
