Word: εθυσεν

Etymology: The word εθυσεν comes from the Greek verb θύω (thýo), which means "to sacrifice." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰewh₁- ("to offer, give").

Grammar: εθυσεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb θύω (thýo). In this form, it indicates that the action of sacrificing was completed in the past.

Description: εθυσεν is a Greek word that means "he/she/it sacrificed." It is often used in the context of religious or ritual sacrifices, as well as in a metaphorical sense to describe giving up something valuable or important. The word has a rich cultural and historical significance in Greek mythology and religion, as well as in ancient Greek society.
