Word: εθνους

Etymology: The word εθνους (ethnous) comes from the Greek word ἔθνος (ethnos), which means "nation" or "people". It is derived from the verb ἔθω (etho), which means "to be accustomed to" or "to be used to".

Grammar: εθνους is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is the genitive singular form of the word ἔθνος (ethnos).

Description: εθνους refers to a group of people who share a common culture, language, and history. It can also refer to a nation or a people. In ancient Greece, the term was used to describe non-Greek people or barbarians. Today, it is used more broadly to refer to any group of people who share a common identity.
