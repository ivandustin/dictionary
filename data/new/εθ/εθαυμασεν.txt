Word: εθαυμασεν

Etymology: The word εθαυμασεν comes from the Greek verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: εθαυμασεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject (he/she/it) performed the action.

Description: εθαυμασεν means "he/she/it marveled" or "he/she/it wondered" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the reactions of people to the miracles performed by Jesus. For example, in Mark 5:20, it is written, "And he went away and began to proclaim in the Decapolis how much Jesus had done for him, and everyone marveled (εθαυμασεν)."
