Word: εθρηνησαμεν

Etymology: The word εθρηνησαμεν comes from the Greek verb θρηνέω (threnéo), which means "to lament" or "to mourn." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, and the suffix -σαμεν (-samen) indicates the first person plural.

Grammar: εθρηνησαμεν is the first person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood, active voice form of the Greek verb θρηνέω (threnéo). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (we) performed the action.

Description: εθρηνησαμεν means "we lamented" or "we mourned" in Greek. It is a verb that describes an action that was completed in the past. The word can be used to describe a group of people mourning or lamenting together, such as at a funeral or during a time of national tragedy.
