Word: εθαυμασας

Etymology: The word εθαυμασας comes from the Greek verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel, wonder, admire". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense, making εθαυμασας (ethaumasas) mean "you marveled, wondered, admired".

Grammar: εθαυμασας is a second person singular past tense verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated from the verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo) and is used to describe an action that was completed in the past by the person being addressed.

Description: εθαυμασας is a word that describes a feeling of amazement or wonder. It is often used to express admiration for something that is impressive or awe-inspiring. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a beautiful sunset, an incredible performance, or a remarkable achievement.
