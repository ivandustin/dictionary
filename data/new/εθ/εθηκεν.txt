The word "εθηκεν" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It comes from the root word "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". 

In terms of grammar, "εθηκεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "τίθημι". 

As for its meaning, "εθηκεν" can be translated as "he/she/it placed" or "he/she/it set". It is often used in the context of placing or setting something in a specific location or position. 

Example sentence: Ο πατέρας εθηκε το βιβλίο στο γραφείο. (O pateras ethike to vivlio sto grafeio.) - "The father placed the book on the desk."
