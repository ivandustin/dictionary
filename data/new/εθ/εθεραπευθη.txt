Word: εθεραπευθη

Etymology:
The word εθεραπευθη is derived from the Greek verb θεραπεύω (therapevo), which means "to heal" or "to cure". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the action of the verb is in the past tense, and the suffix -θη (-the) indicates that the action was performed on the subject.

Grammar:
εθεραπευθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb θεραπεύω (therapevo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is the recipient of the action.

Description:
The word εθεραπευθη describes a past action of being healed or cured. It can be translated to English as "was healed" or "was cured". The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action.
