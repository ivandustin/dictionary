Word: εθαυμασα

Etymology: The word εθαυμασα comes from the Greek verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel, wonder, admire". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: εθαυμασα is the first person singular past tense form of the verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo). It is formed by adding the suffix -σα (-sa) to the stem of the verb. The verb is a regular verb in the Greek language and belongs to the first conjugation.

Description: εθαυμασα is a Greek verb that means "I marveled, wondered, admired". It is used to express a feeling of amazement or admiration for something. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as expressing admiration for a person, an object, or an event. For example, "Εθαυμασα την ομορφιά του τοπίου" (I marveled at the beauty of the landscape).
