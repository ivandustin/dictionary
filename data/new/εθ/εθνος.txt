Word: εθνος

Etymology:
The word εθνος (ethnos) comes from the Greek root word ἔθνος (ethnos), which means "nation" or "people". It is derived from the verb ἔθω (etho), which means "to be accustomed to" or "to be used to".

Grammar:
In Greek, εθνος is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is typically translated as "nation" or "people". The plural form is εθνη (ethni).

Description:
εθνος is a term used to describe a group of people who share a common culture, language, history, and identity. It can refer to a nation, tribe, or ethnic group. In ancient Greece, the term was used to describe non-Greek people, while in modern times it is used to describe any group of people who share a common identity. The concept of εθνος has played an important role in the development of nationalism and the formation of nation-states.
