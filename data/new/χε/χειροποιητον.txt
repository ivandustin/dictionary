Word: χειροποίητον

Etymology: The word χειροποίητον comes from the Greek words χείρ (cheir) meaning "hand" and ποιέω (poieo) meaning "to make". Therefore, χειροποίητον literally means "made by hand".

Grammar: χειροποίητον is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description: χειροποίητον is used to describe something that is made by hand, rather than by a machine or mass-produced. It implies a level of craftsmanship and individuality that cannot be replicated by a machine. This word is often used to describe handmade crafts, artwork, or clothing.
