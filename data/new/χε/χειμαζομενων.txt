The word "χειμαζομενων" (cheimazomenon) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "χειμάζω" (cheimazo), which means "to winter" or "to spend the winter". The word is often used in reference to animals that hibernate or migrate during the winter months. 

In terms of grammar, "χειμαζομενων" is in the present tense and active voice, and it is declined in the genitive case, indicating possession or association. 

Overall, "χειμαζομενων" can be translated as "of those who are wintering" or "belonging to those who spend the winter".
