Word: χειροτονησαντες

Etymology: The word χειροτονησαντες comes from the Greek words χείρ (cheir) meaning "hand" and τονός (tonos) meaning "tension" or "strain". The word is derived from the verb χειροτονέω (cheirotonéo) which means "to stretch out the hand" or "to vote by show of hands".

Grammar: χειροτονησαντες is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. It is the third person plural form of the verb χειροτονέω (cheirotonéo).

Description: χειροτονησαντες means "having voted by show of hands" or "having elected by raising hands". It is often used in the context of political or democratic processes where people vote by raising their hands. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people who have collectively made a decision or taken a particular action.
