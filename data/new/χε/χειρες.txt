Word: χειρες

The word χειρες (cheires) is derived from the Greek word χείρ (cheir), which means "hand". It is a plural form of the word, meaning "hands".

In Greek, χειρες is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is a third declension noun, which means that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive form of χειρες is χειρῶν (cheirōn), the dative form is χερσί (chersi), and the accusative form is χεῖρας (cheiras).

Description:
χειρες is a Greek word that refers to hands. It can be used to describe the physical body part, as well as metaphorically to refer to actions or deeds performed by a person's hands. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday conversation.
