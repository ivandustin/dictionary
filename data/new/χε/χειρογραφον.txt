Word: χειρογραφον

Etymology: The word χειρογραφον comes from the Greek words χείρ (cheir) meaning "hand" and γράφω (grapho) meaning "to write". Therefore, χειρογραφον literally means "handwritten".

Grammar: χειρογραφον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is χειρόγραφα (cheirografa).

Description: χειρογραφον refers to a handwritten document or manuscript. It can be used to describe any type of written work that has been produced by hand, such as a letter, a diary, or a book. In modern times, the term is often used to refer specifically to historical or literary manuscripts that have been preserved over time.
