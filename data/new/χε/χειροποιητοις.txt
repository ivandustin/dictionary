The word "χειροποιητοις" (cheiropoiētois) is a Greek adjective that means "handmade" or "handcrafted". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand", and "ποιέω" (poiéō), meaning "to make" or "to create".

Grammar: "χειροποιητοις" is in the plural form and in the dative case, which indicates that it is used to describe something that is made by hand. 

Description: The word is often used to describe products that are made by skilled artisans or craftsmen, such as handmade jewelry, pottery, or clothing. It emphasizes the uniqueness and quality of the product, as well as the care and attention to detail that went into its creation.
