The word "χωλους" (cholous) is a Greek adjective that means "lame" or "crippled". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "χωλός" (cholos), which means "lame" or "crippled". 

Grammar: "χωλους" is the masculine accusative plural form of the adjective. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. 

Description: The word is often used to describe a person or animal that has difficulty walking due to a physical disability. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is flawed or imperfect.
