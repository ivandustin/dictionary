Word: χωριων

Etymology:
The word χωριων comes from the Greek word χωριός (chorios), which means "village" or "hamlet". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives and nouns.

Grammar:
χωριων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of the village" or "belonging to the village".

Description:
χωριων is a word that is used to describe something that is related to or comes from a village. It can be used to describe people, animals, or objects that are associated with village life. For example, one might say "τα χωριωνικά προϊόντα" (ta chorionika proionta) to refer to products that are made in a village.
