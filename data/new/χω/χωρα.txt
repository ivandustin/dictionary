Word: χωρα

Etymology:
The word χωρα (khora) comes from the Ancient Greek word χώρα (khṓra), which means "region," "territory," or "country." It is derived from the verb χωρέω (khōréō), which means "to make room," "to give space," or "to allow."

Grammar:
In Greek, χωρα is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is χωρα, and its genitive singular form is χωρας. The word can also be declined in the plural.

χωρα is a Greek word that refers to a region, territory, or country. It can be used to describe a specific geographic area or a broader political entity.
