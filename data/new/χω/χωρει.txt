Word: χωρει

Etymology: χωρει comes from the Greek verb χωρέω (choréo), which means "to make room," "to give way," or "to move aside." It is derived from the noun χώρα (chora), which means "space," "place," or "region."

Grammar: χωρει is a present tense verb in Greek. It is the third person singular form of the verb χωρώ (choró), which means "to contain," "to hold," or "to fit." The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ χωρώ (ego choró) - I contain
- Εσύ χωρείς (esý choreís) - You contain
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό χωρεί (aftós/aftí/aftó choreí) - He/She/It contains
- Εμείς χωρούμε (emeís choroúme) - We contain
- Εσείς χωρείτε (eseís choreíte) - You (plural) contain
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά χωρούν (aftoí/aftés/aftá choroún) - They contain

Description: χωρει is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physical space, as in "Η αίθουσα χωρεί πολλούς ανθρώπους" (The room can hold many people). It can also refer to abstract concepts, such as emotions or ideas, as in "Η αγάπη χωρεί στην καρδιά μας" (Love resides in our hearts). Additionally, χωρει can be used in idiomatic expressions, such as "Δεν χωρεί αμφισβήτηση" (There is no room for doubt).
