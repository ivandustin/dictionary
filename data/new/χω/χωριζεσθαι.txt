Word: χωριζεσθαι

Etymology: The word χωριζεσθαι comes from the Greek verb χωρίζω (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning "to separate oneself" or "to divide oneself".

Grammar: χωριζεσθαι is a reflexive verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, reflexive verbs indicate that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: χωριζεσθαι means "to separate oneself" or "to divide oneself". It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone physically separates themselves from a group or situation, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone emotionally or mentally separates themselves from a person or situation. The word can also be used in a theological context to refer to the concept of "separation" or "division" from God.
