Word: χωρισθεις

Etymology: χωρισθεις is a Greek word that comes from the verb χωρίζω (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". The suffix -θεις (-theis) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of the verb has been completed in the past.

Grammar: χωρισθεις is a past participle in the passive voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is the receiver of the action. In Greek, the verb agrees with the subject in gender, number, and case. The word χωρισθεις is masculine, singular, and in the nominative case.

Description: χωρισθεις can be translated as "separated" or "divided". It is often used in a religious context to refer to the separation of the soul from the body after death. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of separation or division.
