Word: χωρησαι

Etymology: The word χωρησαι comes from the Greek verb χωρέω (choréo), which means "to contain" or "to hold". It is derived from the noun χώρα (chora), which means "space" or "place".

Grammar: χωρησαι is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and the active voice. It is the second person singular form of the verb χωρέω (choréo), which means "you contained" or "you held".

Description: χωρησαι is a Greek verb that describes the action of containing or holding something. It is often used in a physical sense, such as holding an object or containing a substance. However, it can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as containing emotions or thoughts. The word is commonly used in everyday conversation and is an important part of the Greek language.
