Word: χωρισθηι

The word χωρισθηι is derived from the Greek verb χωρίζω (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". The suffix -θηι (-thii) is a passive aorist ending, indicating that the action of separating or dividing has been completed in the past.

χωρισθηι is a third-person singular passive aorist form of the verb χωρίζω (chorizo). In Greek, the passive voice is used to indicate that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

χωρισθηι can be translated as "was separated" or "was divided".
