Word: χωρισαι

Etymology:
The word χωρισαι comes from the Greek verb χωρίζω (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". It is derived from the noun χώρα (chora), which means "space" or "place".

Grammar:
χωρισαι is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the second person singular form, which means it is addressing one person. The verb is in the active voice, which means the subject is performing the action.

Description:
χωρισαι means "you separated" or "you divided" in Greek. It is a verb that is often used in the context of relationships, such as a couple separating or divorcing. It can also be used in a more general sense, such as separating objects or dividing a group of people.
