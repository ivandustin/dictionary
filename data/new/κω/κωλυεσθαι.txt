The word "κωλυεσθαι" (kōluesthai) is a Greek verb that means "to be hindered" or "to be prevented." It is derived from the noun "κωλυμα" (kōluma), which means "hindrance" or "obstacle." 

In terms of grammar, "κωλυεσθαι" is a passive form of the verb "κωλυω" (kōluō), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent." The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action themselves. 

Overall, "κωλυεσθαι" is a useful word for describing situations where someone or something is being prevented from doing something due to an obstacle or hindrance.
