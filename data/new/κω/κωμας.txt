Word: κωμας

Etymology:
The word κωμας (komas) is derived from the Greek word κῶμος (kōmos), which means "revel, carousal, or festivity." In ancient Greece, a kōmos was a procession of revelers who sang, danced, and played music during festivals and celebrations.

Grammar:
In Greek, κωμας is a masculine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is κωμας, and its genitive singular form is κωμου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
κωμας can refer to a group of revelers or a festive procession. It can also refer to a specific type of ancient Greek comedy that was performed during festivals. In this context, κωμας refers to a satirical play that mocked the social and political issues of the time.
