Word: κωλυθέντες

Etymology:
The word κωλυθέντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κωλύω (kōlýō), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent." The suffix -θέντες (-théntes) is a past participle ending that indicates the action of the verb has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
κωλυθέντες is a past participle in the passive voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is the receiver of the action. In this case, the subject is not explicitly stated, but it is implied to be a group of people who have been hindered or prevented from doing something.

Description:
The word κωλυθέντες can be translated to mean "having been hindered" or "having been prevented." It implies that some action or event has taken place that has stopped a group of people from doing something they intended to do. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the hindrance or prevention.
