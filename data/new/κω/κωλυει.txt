Word: κωλυει

Etymology: κωλυει comes from the Greek verb κωλύω (kōlúō), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent."

Grammar: κωλυει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb κωλύω. It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: κωλυει means "he/she/it hinders" or "he/she/it prevents" in Greek. It is often used in a negative context, such as when someone is preventing something from happening or someone else from doing something. For example, "Ο κακός καιρός κωλύει τους ανθρώπους από το να βγουν έξω" (The bad weather prevents people from going outside).
