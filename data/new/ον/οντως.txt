Word: οντως

Etymology:
The word οντως (ontos) is derived from the Greek verb εἰμί (eimi), which means "to be." It is the adverbial form of the participle ὤν (on), which means "being" or "existing."

Grammar:
Οντως is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is often translated as "truly," "really," or "indeed."

Description:
Οντως is a common Greek adverb that is used to emphasize the truth or reality of something. It is often used in conversation to express agreement or confirmation, similar to the English expressions "indeed" or "absolutely." It can also be used to express surprise or disbelief, depending on the context.
