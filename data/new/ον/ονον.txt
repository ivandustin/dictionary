The word "ονον" (onon) is a Greek noun that means "donkey" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "ὄνος" (ónos), which also means "donkey". 

Grammar: "Ονον" is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "ονοι" (onoi). 

Description: Donkeys are domesticated animals that are known for their long ears, short mane, and braying sound. They are often used for transportation of goods and people, especially in rural areas. Donkeys are also known for their stubbornness and intelligence.
