Word: ονομαζομενος

Etymology: 
The word ονομαζομενος is a present participle of the Greek verb ονομαζω (onomazo), which means "to name" or "to call". It is derived from the noun ονομα (onoma), which means "name".

Grammar:
Ονομαζομενος is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently being named or called. In English, it can be translated as "being called" or "named".

Description:
Ονομαζομενος is a word that is often used in Greek to introduce someone or something by their name. For example, if someone is introducing themselves, they might say "Εγω ειμαι ονομαζομενος Πανος" (I am called Panos). It can also be used to describe a person or thing that is known by a particular name, such as "ονομαζομενος Αλεξανδρος" (the one called Alexander).
