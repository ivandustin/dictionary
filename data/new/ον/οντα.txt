Grammar: οντα is a neuter noun in the nominative case, singular form. It is often used as a predicate noun, meaning it follows a linking verb and describes the subject of the sentence.

Description: οντα can be translated to English as "being" or "existence". It is often used in philosophical or metaphysical contexts to refer to the concept of existence itself. In Greek grammar, it is considered a participle noun, which means it has both verbal and nominal characteristics.
