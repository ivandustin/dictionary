Word: οναιμην

Etymology:
The word οναιμην (onaimēn) is a Greek verb that means "to lament" or "to wail." It comes from the prefix ον- (on-), which means "woe" or "grief," and the verb αἰμάζω (aimazō), which means "to shed blood" or "to be wounded."

Grammar:
Οναιμην is a first-person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb οναιμάζω (onaimazō), which means "to lament" or "to wail." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
Οναιμην is a word that expresses intense grief and mourning. It is often used in the context of funerals or other tragic events. The word conveys a sense of deep sorrow and lamentation, and is often accompanied by weeping and wailing. In ancient Greek literature, the word is used to describe the mourning of characters who have suffered great losses or tragedies.
