Grammar: οντες is a masculine plural nominative form of the present participle of the verb εἰμί. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently existing or being.

Description: οντες can be translated to "being" or "existing". It is often used in philosophical and metaphysical contexts to describe the nature of existence.
