The word ονειδισμοι (oneidismoi) comes from the Greek root word ονειδος (oneidos), which means "reproach" or "shame." The suffix -ισμοι (-ismoi) is a plural noun ending that indicates the action or state of the root word.

Ονειδισμοι is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is masculine in gender. The nominative singular form is ονειδισμός (oneidismos).

Description:
Ονειδισμοι refers to verbal or written insults, reproaches, or shaming directed towards someone. It can also refer to the act of reproaching or shaming someone.
