Word: οντας

Etymology: οντας is the present participle of the Greek verb είμαι (eimai), which means "to be". It comes from the ancient Greek word ὤν (on), which also means "being" or "existing".

Grammar: οντας is a masculine noun in the nominative case, singular number. As a present participle, it is used to describe a continuous action or state of being. In Greek, it can function as a verb or an adjective, depending on the context.

Description: οντας can be translated as "being" or "existing". It is often used in philosophical and metaphysical contexts to refer to the nature of existence and reality. In everyday language, it can be used to describe someone or something that is present or currently existing.
