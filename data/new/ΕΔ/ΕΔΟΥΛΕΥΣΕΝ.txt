Etymology: The word "εδουλευσεν" comes from the Greek verb "δουλεύω" (doulevo), which means "to work" or "to serve". The prefix "ε-" (e-) indicates the past tense, and the suffix "-εν" (-en) indicates the third person singular form.

Grammar: "Εδουλευσεν" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened.

Description: "Εδουλευσεν" means "he/she/it worked" or "he/she/it served" in Greek. It is used to talk about someone's past actions or employment.
