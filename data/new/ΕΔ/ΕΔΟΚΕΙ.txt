Etymology: The word "εδοκεί" (edokei) comes from the Greek verb "δοκεί" (dokei), which means "to seem" or "to appear." The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, resulting in "εδοκεί," which means "it seemed" or "it appeared."

Grammar: "Εδοκεί" is a verb in the third person singular form of the past tense. It is commonly used in Greek to express an opinion or a subjective judgment about something that appeared or seemed to be a certain way in the past.

Description: "Εδοκεί" is a word that is often used in Greek to express personal impressions or subjective opinions. It is commonly used in sentences like "Εδοκεί μου ότι..." (It seemed to me that...) or "Εδοκεί τους αρμόζει..." (It seemed appropriate to them...). It is a versatile word that allows speakers to express their thoughts and perceptions about past events or situations.
