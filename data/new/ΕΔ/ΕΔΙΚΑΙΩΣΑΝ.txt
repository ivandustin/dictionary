Etymology: The word "εδικαιώσαν" comes from the Greek verb "δικαιώνω" (dikaióno), which means "to justify" or "to vindicate." The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: "Εδικαιώσαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "δικαιώνω" in the past tense. It is used to refer to a group of people who justified or vindicated something in the past.

Description: "Εδικαιώσαν" is a Greek word that describes an action of justifying or vindicating. It indicates that a group of people have successfully defended or proven the righteousness or validity of something in the past.
