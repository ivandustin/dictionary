Etymology: The word "εδεσμευετο" comes from the Greek verb "δεσμεύω" (desmevo), which means "to confiscate" or "to seize". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the passive voice, so "εδεσμευετο" can be translated as "it was confiscated" or "it was seized".

Grammar: "εδεσμευετο" is the third person singular form of the verb "δεσμεύω" in the past tense, passive voice. In Greek grammar, verbs can be conjugated in different tenses, moods, and voices to indicate the subject, time, and manner of the action.

Description: "εδεσμευετο" is a Greek word that describes an action of confiscation or seizure that happened in the past. It is used when referring to a third person singular subject.
