Etymology: The word "εδεηθη" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "εδεήθηκα" (edeithika), which means "I have needed" or "I have been in need."

Grammar: "Εδεηθη" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb.

Description: The word "εδεηθη" is used to express the action of being in need or requiring something. It indicates a state of lacking or requiring assistance or support.
