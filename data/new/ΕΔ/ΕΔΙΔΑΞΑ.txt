Etymology: The word "εδιδαξα" comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, so "εδιδαξα" translates to "I taught" or "I instructed".

Grammar: "Εδιδαξα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "διδάσκω" (didasko). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate different tenses, moods, and persons. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened in the past.

Description: "Εδιδαξα" is a word used to express the action of teaching or instructing that has been completed in the past. It is commonly used in sentences to talk about educational activities or experiences.
