Etymology: The word "εδησεν" comes from the Greek verb "δένω" (deno), which means "to bind" or "to tie". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-.

Grammar: "Εδησεν" is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "δένω" (deno) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εδησεν" means "he/she/it bound" in English. It is used to describe an action of binding or tying that was completed in the past.
