Etymology: The word "εδιδαξας" (edidaxas) comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, making "εδιδαξας" mean "you taught" or "you instructed".

Grammar: "Εδιδαξας" is the second person singular form of the verb "διδάσκω" in the past tense. It is used to address someone directly and indicate that they taught or instructed someone or something in the past.

Description: "Εδιδαξας" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the action of teaching or instructing in the past. It is used to describe the act of imparting knowledge or skills to others.
