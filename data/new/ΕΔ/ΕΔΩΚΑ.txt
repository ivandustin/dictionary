Etymology: The word "εδωκα" (edoka) comes from the Greek verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), which means "to give". It is conjugated in the first person singular, aorist tense, indicative mood.

Grammar: "Εδωκα" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense, which is used to express completed actions in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement or a fact.

Description: "Εδωκα" is the first person singular form of the verb "δίδωμι" (didomi) in the aorist tense. It translates to "I gave" in English. This verb is commonly used to express the action of giving something to someone.
