Etymology: The word "εδιδασκον" (edidaskon) comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, meaning "to show" or "to point out".

Grammar: "Εδιδασκον" is the third person singular, imperfect tense form of the verb "διδάσκω" (didasko) in Greek. The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: "Εδιδασκον" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was teaching" or "he/she/it used to teach". It refers to the action of instructing or imparting knowledge to others.
