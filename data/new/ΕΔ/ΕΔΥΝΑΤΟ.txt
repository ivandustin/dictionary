Etymology: The word "εδυνατο" comes from the Greek word "δυνατός" (dynatos), which means "possible" or "capable". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, making "εδυνατο" mean "was possible" or "was capable".

Grammar: "Εδυνατο" is the third person singular form of the verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have the power". It is in the past tense, indicating that the action of being able or having the power occurred in the past.

Description: "Εδυνατο" is a Greek word that describes the past ability or power of someone or something. It indicates that at a certain point in the past, the subject had the capability or capacity to do something.
