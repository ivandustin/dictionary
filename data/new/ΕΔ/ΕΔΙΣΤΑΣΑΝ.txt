Etymology: The word "εδιστασαν" comes from the Greek verb "εδιστασα" (edistasa), which means "to hesitate" or "to be uncertain." It is derived from the prefix "ε-" (e-), meaning "out" or "away," and the verb "δισταζω" (distazo), meaning "to doubt" or "to waver."

Grammar: "Εδιστασαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "εδιστασα" (edistasa) in the past tense. It is used to describe an action or state of hesitation or uncertainty performed by a group of people.

Description: "Εδιστασαν" is a Greek word that translates to "they hesitated" or "they were uncertain" in English. It is used to express a collective hesitation or uncertainty among a group of individuals.
