Word: ημισυ

Etymology: Ημισυ comes from the ancient Greek word "ἥμισυς" (hēmisus), which means "half". 

Grammar: Ημισυ is a noun in the Greek language, and it is always used in the neuter gender. It is declined like a second-declension noun, and its plural form is "ημίσεις" (hemiseis).

Description: Ημισυ is used to refer to half of something, or a portion that is equal to half of the whole. It can be used in various contexts, such as in measurements, fractions, or in expressions like "half of the day" or "half of the year".
