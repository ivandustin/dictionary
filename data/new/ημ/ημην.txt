Word: ημην

Etymology: ημην is the first person singular imperfect indicative form of the Greek verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁es-.

Grammar: ημην is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the imperfect tense. It is the first person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is "I". The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: ημην is a word that means "I was" in Greek. It is used to describe a past action that was ongoing or repeated. For example, "ημην στο σχολείο" (I was at school) describes a past action that was ongoing, as opposed to "πήγα στο σχολείο" (I went to school), which describes a past action that was completed.
