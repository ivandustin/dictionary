The word "ημετεραν" (hēmeteran) is a Greek adverb that means "ourselves" or "our own." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek pronoun "ἡμεῖς" (hēmeis), which means "we" or "us," and the suffix "-τερος" (-teros), which is used to form adjectives and adverbs indicating possession or relationship. 

Grammar: "Ημετεραν" is an adverb in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is often used with verbs of motion or action to indicate that the action is directed towards oneself or one's own group. 

Description: The word "ημετεραν" is commonly used in Greek to express a sense of ownership or belonging. It can be used to refer to oneself or to a group of people, and is often used in a positive or affectionate way.
