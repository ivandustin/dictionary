The word "ημελλεν" (emellen) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "μέλλω" (mello), which means "to be about to" or "to intend to". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate a past tense form.

Grammar: "ημελλεν" is a third-person singular past tense form of the verb "μέλλω". It is in the indicative mood and the active voice.

Description: The word "ημελλεν" is used to indicate that someone or something was about to do something in the past. It can be translated as "was going to" or "intended to". For example, "Ημελλεν να φύγει αλλά άλλαξε γνώμη" (emellen na fygei alla allaxe gnomi) means "He was going to leave but changed his mind".
