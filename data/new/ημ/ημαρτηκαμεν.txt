The word "ημαρτηκαμεν" (emartekamen) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "αμαρτάνω" (amartano), which means "to sin" or "to make a mistake".

Grammar: "ημαρτηκαμεν" (emartekamen) is the first person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood of "αμαρτάνω" (amartano). The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. 

Description: "ημαρτηκαμεν" (emartekamen) can be translated as "we sinned" or "we made a mistake". It implies that the speaker and others have committed a wrongdoing in the past.
