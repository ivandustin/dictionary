Word: ημεραις

Etymology:
The word ημεραις (hemerais) is derived from the Greek word ἡμέρα (hēméra), which means "day". It is a plural form of the word, which means "days".

Grammar:
In Greek, ημεραις is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is in the plural form and is used to refer to multiple days.

Description:
Ημεραις is a word that is commonly used in Greek to refer to multiple days. It can be used in various contexts, such as in the phrase "τις ημέρες" (tis hēmeres), which means "these days". It is also used in the phrase "καθημερινές ημέρες" (kathimerinés hēmeres), which means "everyday days". Overall, ημεραις is a versatile word that is used frequently in Greek language and culture.
