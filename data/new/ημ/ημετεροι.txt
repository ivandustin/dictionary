Word: ημετεροι

Etymology: ημετεροι is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: ημεις (we) and ετερος (other). Therefore, ημετεροι means "our others" or "our different ones".

Grammar: ημετεροι is a plural adjective in the masculine gender. It agrees with the noun it modifies in number, gender, and case.

Description: ημετεροι is used to refer to people or things that are different from us but still belong to our group or community. It implies a sense of ownership and belonging. For example, in the sentence "Οι ημετεροι φίλοι είναι πολλοί" (Our other friends are many), ημετεροι emphasizes that there are other friends besides the ones we usually hang out with, but they are still part of our group.
