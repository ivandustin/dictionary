The word "ημελλον" is a Greek verb in the imperfect tense, which means "I was about to" or "I was going to." Its etymology is derived from the Greek word "μέλλω" (méllō), which means "to be about to" or "to intend." In terms of grammar, "ημελλον" is conjugated in the first person singular, and its stem is "μελλ-." 

In usage, "ημελλον" is often used to describe an action that was planned or intended to happen in the past but did not actually occur. For example, "I was about to go to the store, but then I got a phone call and had to stay home." 

Overall, "ημελλον" is a useful verb for expressing past intentions or plans that were not fulfilled.
