Word: θελησωσιν

Etymology: θελησωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb θέλω (thelō), which means "to will" or "to desire". The suffix -σωσιν (-sōsin) is a third-person plural aorist subjunctive ending, indicating a wish or desire.

Grammar: θελησωσιν is a verb form in the aorist subjunctive mood, third-person plural. In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or a wish. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past.

Description: θελησωσιν can be translated as "they might desire" or "let them desire". It is a wish or desire expressed in the third-person plural form. This word is often used in religious or philosophical contexts, expressing a desire for something to happen according to the will of a higher power.
