Word: θεασαμενοις

Etymology: θεασαμενοις is a Greek word that comes from the verb θεάομαι (theaomai), which means "to see, to behold, to observe." The suffix -μενοις (-menois) is a participle ending that indicates the present passive voice, meaning "being seen, being observed."

Grammar: θεασαμενοις is a present passive participle in the dative plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are being seen or observed.

Description: θεασαμενοις can be translated as "to those who are being seen" or "to those who are being observed." It is often used in Greek literature to describe a group of people who are watching a spectacle or event. In English, it can be translated as "spectators" or "onlookers."
