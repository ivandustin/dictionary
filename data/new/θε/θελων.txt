Word: θελων

Etymology:
The word θελων is derived from the Greek verb θέλω (thélō), which means "to will" or "to want". It is a present participle form of the verb, which means "willing" or "wanting".

Grammar:
In Greek, θελων is a masculine singular nominative present participle. It is used to describe someone who is currently willing or wanting something. It can also be used as a noun to refer to someone who is characterized by their willingness or desire.

Description:
The word θελων is often used in philosophical and religious contexts to describe someone who is willing to follow a certain path or belief system. It can also be used in everyday language to describe someone who is determined or motivated to achieve a particular goal. In both cases, the word conveys a sense of active desire and intention.
