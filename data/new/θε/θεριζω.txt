Word: θεριζω

Etymology: θεριζω comes from the Greek word θεριζειν (therizein), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". It is derived from the noun θερις (theris), which means "harvest" or "crop".

Grammar: θεριζω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- θερίζω (I harvest)
- θερίζεις (you harvest)
- θερίζει (he/she/it harvests)
- θερίζομεν (we harvest)
- θερίζετε (you all harvest)
- θερίζουσι(ν) (they harvest)

Description: θεριζω means "to harvest" or "to reap" in Greek. It is often used in the context of agriculture, referring to the act of cutting and gathering crops from the fields. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of gathering or collecting something, such as information or ideas.
