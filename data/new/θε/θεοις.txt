Word: θεοις

Etymology:
The word θεοις is derived from the Greek word θεός (theos), which means "god" or "deity". The suffix -ις (-is) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
θεοις is a masculine noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
θεοις refers to "gods" or "deities" in Greek mythology. It is often used to describe the various gods and goddesses worshipped by the ancient Greeks, such as Zeus, Hera, Apollo, Athena, and many others. In Greek mythology, the gods were believed to have control over various aspects of the natural world and were often worshipped through rituals and sacrifices. The concept of the gods played a significant role in ancient Greek culture and religion.
