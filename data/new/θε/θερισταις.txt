Word: θερισταις

Etymology: θερισταις is a Greek word that comes from the root word θερίζω (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". The suffix "-ταις" (-tais) is a plural ending used for feminine nouns.

Grammar: θερισταις is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: θερισταις refers to female harvesters or reapers. In ancient Greece, agriculture was a vital part of the economy, and harvesting was an important task that required a lot of labor. Women were often involved in the harvesting process, and θερισταις would have been a common term to describe them. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to female harvesters or farm workers.
