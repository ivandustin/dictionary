The word θεωρω (theorō) comes from the Greek root word θεωρέω (theōréō), which means "to observe" or "to contemplate". It is derived from the combination of the words θεός (theos), meaning "god", and ὁράω (horáō), meaning "to see".

- Singular: θεωρώ (theorō)
- Plural: θεωρούμε (theoroúme)

The word θεωρω is often translated as "to see" or "to observe". However, it can also mean "to contemplate", "to consider", or "to perceive".
