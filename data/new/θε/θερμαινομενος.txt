Word: θερμαινομενος

Etymology: θερμαίνω (thermaíno) means "to heat" in Greek, and the suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle suffix indicating ongoing action. Therefore, θερμαινομενος (thermainomenos) means "heating" or "being heated".

Grammar: θερμαινομενος is a present participle in Greek, which means it is a verb form that describes ongoing action. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: θερμαινομενος is a Greek word that describes something that is currently being heated or warmed up. It can be used to describe a person, object, or environment that is undergoing a heating process.
