Word: θεριζειν

Etymology: θεριζειν is a Greek verb that comes from the noun θερισμός (therismos), which means "harvesting" or "reaping". The verb θεριζειν means "to harvest" or "to reap".

Grammar: θεριζειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used in combination with other verbs to form compound verbs, such as θερίζων (therizon), which means "harvesting" or "reaping".

Description: θεριζειν is a common verb in Greek, especially in agricultural contexts. It refers to the act of cutting and gathering crops, such as wheat or barley, during the harvest season.
