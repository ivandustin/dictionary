Word: θεριζεις

Etymology: θεριζεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θερίζω (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". 

Grammar: θεριζεις is a second person singular present active indicative form of the verb θερίζω. It is used to describe the action of harvesting or reaping in the present tense, and specifically refers to the action being performed by the person being addressed. 

Description: θεριζεις is a verb that describes the act of harvesting or reaping. It can be used in a literal sense, to describe the act of gathering crops from a field, or in a figurative sense, to describe the act of gathering or collecting something more abstract, such as knowledge or information. As a present tense verb, θεριζεις suggests that the action is currently taking place or is ongoing.
