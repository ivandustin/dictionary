Word: θεληματος

Etymology: θεληματος is a Greek word that comes from the root word θέλημα (thélēma), which means "will" or "desire". The suffix -τος (-tos) is a genitive ending, indicating possession or relationship.

Grammar: θεληματος is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of θέλημα (thélēma).

Description: θεληματος refers to the will or desire of someone or something. It can also refer to a decision or choice made by someone. In some contexts, it can have a religious or spiritual connotation, referring to the will of a deity or higher power.
