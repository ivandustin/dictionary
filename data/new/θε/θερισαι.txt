Word: θερισαι

Etymology: θερισαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θερίζω (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". The verb θερισαι is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: θερισαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards one person to perform a completed action in the past. The verb is conjugated from the root word θερίζω, which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description: θερισαι is a command to harvest or reap. It is often used in the context of agriculture or farming, where it is necessary to gather crops at the appropriate time. The verb can also be used metaphorically to mean "to gather" or "to collect" something, such as information or resources.
