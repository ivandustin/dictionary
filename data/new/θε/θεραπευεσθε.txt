Word: θεραπευεσθε

Etymology: θεραπευεσθε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θεραπεία (therapeia), which means "healing" or "cure". The verb form θεραπευεσθε is in the present passive imperative tense, which means it is a command to be healed or cured.

Grammar: θεραπευεσθε is a second-person plural verb in the present passive imperative tense. This means that it is a command directed at a group of people to be healed or cured. The passive voice indicates that the action of being healed or cured is being done to the subject (in this case, the group of people being commanded).

Description: θεραπευεσθε is a command to be healed or cured. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the Bible where it appears in the New Testament book of Luke (Luke 5:31) and is spoken by Jesus to a group of Pharisees who were questioning why he was eating with tax collectors and sinners.
