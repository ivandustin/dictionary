Word: θεραπευσον

Etymology: θεραπευσον is a verb in the Greek language, derived from the root word θεραπεία (therapeia), which means "healing" or "cure". The suffix -σον (-son) is a second person singular imperative ending, which means "you (singular) do" or "you (singular) perform".

Grammar: θεραπευσον is a second person singular imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, specifically to perform an action related to healing or curing.

Description: θεραπευσον is a command to an individual to perform an action related to healing or curing. It can be used in a medical context, such as a doctor instructing a patient to take medication or undergo a certain treatment. It can also be used in a metaphorical sense, such as a friend encouraging another friend to take care of themselves and prioritize their health.
