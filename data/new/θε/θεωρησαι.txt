Word: θεωρησαι

Etymology: θεωρησαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θεωρέω (theoreo), which means "to observe" or "to contemplate". The suffix -σαι (-sai) indicates the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar: θεωρησαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed at one person to perform a completed action in the past. The verb is conjugated from the root word θεωρέω (theoreo) and the suffix -σαι (-sai) for the aorist tense.

Description: θεωρησαι can be translated to English as "observe" or "contemplate". It is a command directed at one person to perform the action of observing or contemplating something in the past. The verb is often used in a philosophical or intellectual context, such as in the works of Plato or Aristotle.
