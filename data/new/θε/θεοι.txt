Word: θεοι

Etymology:
The word θεοι (theoi) is the plural form of the Greek word θεός (theos), which means "god" or "deity". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰéh₁s, which means "to set, place, do". The word has been used in Greek mythology to refer to the gods and goddesses.

Grammar:
In Greek, θεοι is the nominative plural form of θεός. It is used to refer to multiple gods or deities. The word is declined as follows:

Nominative: θεοί (theoi)
Genitive: θεών (theon)
Dative: θεοίς (theois)
Accusative: θεούς (theous)
Vocative: θεοί (theoi)

Description:
Θεοι is a term used in Greek mythology to refer to the gods and goddesses. The ancient Greeks believed in a pantheon of gods and goddesses who controlled various aspects of life, such as love, war, wisdom, and the sea. The gods were often depicted as having human-like qualities and were worshipped through various rituals and sacrifices. The most well-known Greek gods include Zeus, Poseidon, Hera, Demeter, Athena, Apollo, Artemis, Ares, Aphrodite, Hephaestus, Hermes, and Dionysus. The Greek gods and their stories have had a significant impact on Western culture and continue to be studied and celebrated today.
