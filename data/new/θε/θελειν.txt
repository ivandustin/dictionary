Word: θελειν

Etymology:
The word θελειν (thelein) is a Greek verb that means "to will" or "to want". It comes from the Greek root word θελ-, which means "to wish" or "to desire".

Grammar:
θελειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is the infinitive form of the verb, which means that it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. Instead, it is used to express the idea of "to will" or "to want" in a general sense.

Description:
θελειν is a common verb in Greek and is used to express a variety of desires or wishes. It can be used to express a simple desire, such as "I want to eat" (θελω να φαω) or a more complex desire, such as "I want to travel the world" (θελω να ταξιδεψω στον κοσμο). It can also be used to express a willingness to do something, such as "I am willing to help" (ειμαι διαθετημενος να βοηθησω). Overall, θελειν is a versatile verb that is used frequently in Greek conversation and writing.
