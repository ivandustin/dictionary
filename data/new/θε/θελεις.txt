Word: θελεις

Etymology: θελεις is a Greek word that comes from the verb θέλω (thélo), which means "to want" or "to wish".

Grammar: θελεις is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb θέλω (thélo). It is used to address one person and means "you want" or "you wish".

Description: θελεις is a common word in Greek and is used in everyday conversations. It is often used to ask someone if they want something or to express one's own desires. It can also be used in a more formal context, such as in business or academic settings.
