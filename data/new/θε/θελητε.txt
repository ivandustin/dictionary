Word: θελητε

Etymology:
The word θελητε (thelete) is derived from the Greek verb θέλω (thelō), which means "to will" or "to want". The suffix -τε (-te) is added to the verb to indicate the second person plural form of the present tense.

Grammar:
θελητε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is used to address a group of people and means "you want" or "you will". In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person of the subject. The present tense is used to describe actions that are happening now or are habitual.

Description:
θελητε is a common verb in Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express desires or wishes, such as "Do you want to go to the movies?" or "Would you like some coffee?". It can also be used to give commands or make requests, such as "Please, want to help me with this?" or "Want to come with me to the store?".
