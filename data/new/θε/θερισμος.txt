Word: θερισμός

Etymology: θερισμός comes from the Greek word θερίζω (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". 

Grammar: θερισμός is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. 

Description: θερισμός refers to the act of harvesting crops, particularly grains such as wheat or barley. It can also refer to the time of year when this harvesting takes place. In Greek culture, the harvest season was an important time of year, and was often celebrated with festivals and rituals. Today, θερισμός is still an important part of agriculture in Greece and other countries with similar climates.
