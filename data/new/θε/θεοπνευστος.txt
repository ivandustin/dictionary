Word: θεοπνευστος

Etymology:
The word θεοπνευστος comes from the Greek words θεός (theos) meaning "God" and πνεῦμα (pneuma) meaning "breath" or "spirit". Therefore, θεοπνευστος literally means "God-breathed" or "God-inspired".

Grammar:
In Greek, θεοπνευστος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The word can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it is describing.

Description:
The word θεοπνευστος is most commonly used to describe the Bible, specifically the Christian Bible. It is used to convey the belief that the Bible is not just a human creation, but rather a divinely inspired text. The concept of θεοπνευστος is central to many Christian beliefs about the nature and authority of the Bible.
