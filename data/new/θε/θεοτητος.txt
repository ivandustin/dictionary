Word: θεοτητος

Etymology: θεοτητος is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: θεός (theos) meaning "god" and -τητα (-tēta) meaning "quality of being". Therefore, θεοτητος means "the quality of being god" or "divinity".

Grammar: θεοτητος is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, singular number, and feminine gender.

Description: θεοτητος is a term used in theology to refer to the divine nature or essence of God. It is often used in discussions about the Trinity, where it is used to describe the nature of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as being one in essence or substance. In Christian theology, θεοτητος is considered an essential attribute of God, along with other attributes such as omniscience, omnipotence, and omnipresence.
