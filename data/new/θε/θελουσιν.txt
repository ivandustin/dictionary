Word: θελουσιν

Etymology:
The word θελουσιν is a present active participle of the Greek verb θέλω (thelō), which means "to will" or "to desire". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *wel-, which means "to wish" or "to will".

Grammar:
In Greek, θελουσιν is the third person plural present active participle of θέλω. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently willing or desiring something. The word can be translated as "they are willing" or "they desire".

Description:
The word θελουσιν is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the willingness or desire of individuals or groups to follow God's will. For example, in Matthew 26:41, Jesus tells his disciples to "watch and pray, that ye enter not into temptation: the spirit indeed is willing (θέλων), but the flesh is weak." The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone's willingness or desire to do something, such as in the sentence "They are willing to help us with the project."
