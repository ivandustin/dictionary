Word: θελω

Etymology:
The word θελω (thelō) comes from the Ancient Greek verb θέλω (thélō), which means "to will" or "to wish". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *wel-, which means "to wish" or "to desire".

Grammar:
θελω is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject (in this case, "I") is performing the action of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated to indicate the tense, mood, voice, and person of the subject.

Description:
θελω is a common Greek verb that is used to express a desire or a wish. It can be translated as "I want" or "I wish". It is often used in everyday conversation to express a preference or a request. For example, "θελω ένα καφέ" (thelō éna kafé) means "I want a coffee". The verb can also be used to express a willingness or a readiness to do something. For example, "θελω να βοηθήσω" (thelō na voiθíso) means "I am willing to help".
