Etymology:
The word θεις comes from the Greek verb θέω (theo), which means "to place" or "to set". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰeh₁- ("to put, place, set").

Grammar:
Θεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the Greek verb θέω (theo). It is used to address one person and means "you place" or "you set".
