Word: θεραπευων

Etymology:
The word θεραπευων comes from the Greek verb θεραπεύω (therapevo), which means "to heal" or "to cure". The suffix -ων (-on) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently performing the action of healing or curing.

Grammar:
θεραπευων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is a present participle, which means that it describes someone who is currently performing the action of healing or curing. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ων to the stem of the verb.

Description:
θεραπευων refers to someone who is currently healing or curing. It can be used to describe a doctor, a nurse, or any other healthcare professional who is actively engaged in the process of healing. The word emphasizes the ongoing nature of the healing process, suggesting that the person is not just providing a one-time cure, but is actively working to restore the patient's health over time.
