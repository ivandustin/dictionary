Word: θεραπειαν

Etymology: θεραπειαν is a Greek word that comes from the verb θεραπεύω (therapevo), which means "to heal" or "to cure". The suffix -αν (-an) is a common ending for nouns in the Greek language.

Grammar: θεραπειαν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their endings depending on their function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: θεραπειαν refers to the act of healing or curing. It can also be used to describe a treatment or therapy that is used to cure an illness or injury. The word is commonly used in the medical field to refer to various forms of therapy, such as physical therapy, occupational therapy, or speech therapy.
