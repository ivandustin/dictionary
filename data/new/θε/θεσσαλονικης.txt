Word: Θεσσαλονίκης

Etymology: The name Thessaloniki comes from the ancient Greek word Θεσσαλός (Thessalos), who was the son of the mythological hero Jason and the princess Medea. The city was named after him by King Cassander of Macedon in 315 BC.

Grammar: Θεσσαλονίκης is a feminine noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or origin. It is the genitive form of the noun Θεσσαλονίκη (Thessaloniki), which is the name of the second-largest city in Greece.

Description: Thessaloniki is a city located in northern Greece, on the Thermaic Gulf of the Aegean Sea. It is the capital of the region of Central Macedonia and has a population of over one million people. Thessaloniki is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and vibrant nightlife. It is home to numerous museums, ancient ruins, and Byzantine churches, as well as modern shopping centers, restaurants, and cafes. The city is also a major transportation hub, with an international airport, a seaport, and a railway station connecting it to other parts of Greece and Europe.
