Word: θελει

Etymology:
The word θελει (thelei) is derived from the Greek verb θέλω (thélō), which means "to want" or "to desire". It is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb.

Grammar:
In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. θελει is a present tense, indicative mood, active voice, third person singular form of the verb θέλω.

Description:
Θελει is a common Greek word that is used to express someone's desire or want. It can be translated to English as "he/she/it wants" or "he/she/it desires". It is often used in everyday conversations and can be found in various contexts, such as expressing a preference for a certain food or expressing a desire for a particular outcome.
