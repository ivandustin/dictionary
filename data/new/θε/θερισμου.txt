The word "θερισμου" (therismou) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb "θερίζω" (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". It is derived from the noun "θέρος" (theros), which means "summer" or "harvest season". 

In terms of grammar, "θερισμου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It can be translated as "of the harvest" or "of the reaping".

Overall, "θερισμου" refers to the act of harvesting or reaping crops during the summer season.
