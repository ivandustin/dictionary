Word: θελοντες

Etymology: θελοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb θέλω (thélo), which means "to will" or "to want". Θελοντες is the present participle form of the verb, which means "those who want" or "those who are willing".

Grammar: θελοντες is a masculine plural noun in the nominative case. It is often used as a participle, meaning it functions as both a verb and an adjective. In this form, it can be translated as "those who want" or "those who are willing".

Description: θελοντες is a word that describes a group of people who are willing or eager to do something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a religious or philosophical context to describe those who are willing to follow a certain path or belief system. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe people who are motivated and enthusiastic about a particular activity or goal.
