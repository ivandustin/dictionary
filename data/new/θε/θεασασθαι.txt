Word: θεασασθαι

Etymology: θεασασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θεαομαι (theaomai), which means "to see, to behold, to observe." The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a middle voice ending that indicates the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Grammar: θεασασθαι is a second aorist middle infinitive verb in Greek. It is a non-finite verb form that does not indicate tense, person, or number. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: θεασασθαι means "to look at oneself, to observe oneself" in Greek. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to mean "to reflect on oneself, to examine oneself." The word is commonly used in philosophical and spiritual contexts to describe the process of self-reflection and self-awareness.
