Word: θεαθηναι

Etymology: The word θεαθηναι is derived from the Greek word θεά (thea) meaning "goddess" and the verb θεάομαι (theaomai) meaning "to see, to behold". The suffix -θηναι (-thenai) is a passive aorist infinitive ending, indicating that the action of seeing or beholding is being done to the subject.

Grammar: θεαθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the suffix -θηναι to the stem of the verb θεάομαι. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb.

Description: θεαθηναι can be translated to mean "to be seen, to be beheld". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the act of seeing or beholding a deity or divine presence. In ancient Greek mythology, the gods and goddesses were often described as being visible to mortals, and the act of seeing them was considered a great privilege. Today, the word is still used in Greek Orthodox religious texts to describe the act of beholding the divine.
