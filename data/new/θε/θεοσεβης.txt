Word: θεοσεβης

Etymology:
The word θεοσεβης comes from the Greek words θεός (theos) meaning "god" and σέβομαι (sevomai) meaning "to revere" or "to worship". Therefore, θεοσεβης means "god-fearing" or "pious".

In Greek, θεοσεβης is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it would be θεοσεβής (theosévēs), while if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it would be θεοσεβή (theosévē). In the plural form, it would be θεοσεβεῖς (theoséveis) for masculine and mixed gender nouns, and θεοσεβεῖσαι (theoséveisai) for feminine nouns.

Description:
The word θεοσεβης is often used in Greek literature and religious texts to describe individuals who have a deep respect and reverence for God. It is a term that is associated with piety, devotion, and a strong sense of faith. In modern usage, it can also be used to describe someone who is deeply religious or spiritual.
