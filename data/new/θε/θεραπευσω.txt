Word: θεραπεύω

Etymology: The word θεραπεύω (therapeuo) comes from the Greek root word θεράπων (therapon), which means "attendant" or "servant." It is derived from the verb θεράπω (therapo), which means "to serve" or "to attend to."

Grammar: θεραπεύω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- θεραπεύω (I serve/attend to)
- θεραπεύεις (you serve/attend to)
- θεραπεύει (he/she/it serves/attends to)
- θεραπευόμενοι (we serve/attend to)
- θεραπεύετε (you all serve/attend to)
- θεραπεύουσι(ν) (they serve/attend to)

Description: θεραπεύω is a verb that means "to heal" or "to cure." It is often used in the context of medical treatment or therapy. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of service or care provided to someone in need.
