Word: θερος

Etymology: θερος is a Greek word that comes from the root word θερμός (thermós), which means "hot" or "warm". It is related to the English word "thermal".

Grammar: θερος is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative singular form. In the plural form, it becomes θεροί (theroi).

Description: θερος refers to the season of summer in Greek. It is a time when the weather is hot and dry, and the days are long. In ancient Greek mythology, θερος was associated with the god Apollo, who was the god of the sun and light. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to the summer season.
