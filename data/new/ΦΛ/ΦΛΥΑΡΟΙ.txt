Etymology: The word "φλυαροι" comes from the Greek word "φλυαρώ" (phlyaró), which means "to talk excessively or idly". 

Grammar: "Φλυαροι" is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is masculine in gender. 

Description: "Φλυαροι" refers to people who talk excessively or engage in idle chatter. It can be used to describe individuals who talk a lot without saying anything meaningful or important.
