Etymology: The word "φλογος" (phlogos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "φλέγω" (phlego), which means "to burn" or "to ignite."

Grammar: In Greek, "φλογος" (phlogos) is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "φλογου" (phlogou).

Description: "Φλογος" (phlogos) refers to fire or flame. It can also be used metaphorically to describe passion, intensity, or fervor.
