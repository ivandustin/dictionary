Word: ογδοον

Etymology:
The word ογδοον (ogdoon) is derived from the Greek word ογδοος (ogdoos), which means "eighth". It is formed by adding the suffix -ον (-on) to the end of the word.

Grammar:
Ογδοον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form of the word is ογδοου (ogdoou).

Description:
Ογδοον refers to the number eight in Greek. It is used in various contexts, such as counting, mathematics, and time. For example, in the Greek Orthodox Church, the eighth day after a person's death is considered an important day for memorial services. In music, the octave is the eighth note in a musical scale.
