Word: σωσατω

Etymology:
The word σωσατω is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σῴζω (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₁- ("to protect").

Grammar:
σωσατω is a third-person singular aorist imperative active verb in Greek. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The word σωσατω is a command to "save" or "rescue." It is often used in a religious context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to command believers to save themselves or others. The word can also be used in a more general sense to urge someone to take action to save or rescue someone or something in danger.
