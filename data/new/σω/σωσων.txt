Word: σωσων

Etymology: σωσων is a Greek word that comes from the verb σῴζω (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue." 

Grammar: σωσων is the present active participle of the verb σῴζω (sōzō). It is declined in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: σωσων can be translated as "the one who saves" or "the one who rescues." It is often used in reference to Jesus Christ, who is seen as the ultimate savior in Christianity. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to anyone who saves or rescues someone else.
