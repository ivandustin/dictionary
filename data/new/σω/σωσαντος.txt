Word: σωσαντος

Etymology: σωσαντος is the genitive form of the Greek verb σῴζω (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₁- ("to protect").

Grammar: σωσαντος is a masculine singular genitive form of the verb σῴζω (sōzō). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the masculine gender is used to refer to male beings or objects.

Description: σωσαντος can be translated as "of the one who saved" or "of the one who rescued". It is often used in reference to Jesus Christ, who is believed by Christians to have saved humanity from sin and death through his death and resurrection. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to anyone who has saved or rescued someone else from danger or harm.
