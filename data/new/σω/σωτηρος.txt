Word: σωτηρος

The word σωτηρος (sōtēros) is derived from the Greek word σωτήρ (sōtēr), which means "savior" or "deliverer". It is formed from the verb σῴζω (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to deliver".

In Greek, σωτηρος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive σωτηρος (sōtērou) or the accusative σωτηρον (sōtēron).

Description:
The word σωτηρος is often used in a religious context to refer to Jesus Christ as the savior of humanity. It can also be used more generally to refer to anyone who saves or delivers others from harm or danger.
