The word σωτηριαν (soterian) is derived from the Greek word σωτηρία (soteria), which means "salvation" or "deliverance". The suffix "-an" is added to the end of the word to indicate that it is a noun.

σωτηριαν is a singular noun in the accusative case in Greek. It is often used in the context of salvation or deliverance.

σωτηριαν is a word that is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to salvation or deliverance. It is often used in the context of the salvation that is offered through Jesus Christ.
