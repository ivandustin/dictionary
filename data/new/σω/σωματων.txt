Word: σωμάτων

Etymology: The word σωμάτων comes from the Greek word σῶμα (sōma), which means "body" or "corpse". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *som-, which means "together" or "whole".

Grammar: σωμάτων is the genitive plural form of σῶμα (sōma). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple bodies being referred to.

Description: σωμάτων is a Greek word that refers to bodies, either living or dead. It can be used to describe the physical bodies of humans, animals, or other organisms, as well as the bodies of celestial objects such as planets or stars. In a figurative sense, it can also refer to a group of people or things that are united or connected in some way.
