The word "σωιζεται" (sōizetai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue". 

In terms of grammar, "σωιζεται" is a present indicative active verb, meaning that it describes an action that is currently happening in the present. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the sentence is singular and not the speaker or the person being spoken to.

Overall, "σωιζεται" can be translated to mean "he/she/it is being saved" or "he/she/it is being rescued".
