Word: σωθηναι

Etymology:
The word σωθηναι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. It is derived from the root word σῴζω (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue." 

Grammar:
As mentioned, σωθηναι is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive mood is used to express the idea of an action without specifying who is performing it. 

Description:
The word σωθηναι can be translated to mean "to be saved" or "to be rescued." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to salvation or deliverance from sin or danger. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used frequently in this sense, particularly in the writings of the apostle Paul.
