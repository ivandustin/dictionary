Word: σωπατρος

Etymology:
The word σωπατρος is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the words σῶς (sos) meaning "safe" or "sound" and πατήρ (pater) meaning "father". Therefore, the literal meaning of σωπατρος is "safe father" or "sound father".

Grammar:
In Greek, σωπατρος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case σωπάτρου (sopatrou) or the accusative case σωπάτρον (sopatron).

Description:
The word σωπατρος is not commonly used in modern Greek, but it appears in ancient Greek literature. It is often used to refer to a father who is reliable, trustworthy, and protective. In some contexts, it can also be used to refer to a god or a deity who is considered a protector or a savior.
