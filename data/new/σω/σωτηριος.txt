Word: σωτηριος

Etymology:
The word σωτηριος (soterios) is derived from the Greek word σωτήρ (soter), which means "savior" or "deliverer". It is also related to the Greek word σῶς (sos), which means "safe" or "sound".

Grammar:
In Greek, σωτηριος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person or thing as "saving" or "delivering". It can also be used as a noun to refer to a "savior" or "deliverer".

Description:
The word σωτηριος is often used in a religious context to refer to Jesus Christ as the "savior" or "deliverer" of humanity. It can also be used more generally to describe someone or something that saves or delivers, such as a hero or a life-saving medicine.
