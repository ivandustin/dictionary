Word: σωσω

Etymology: The word σωσω (sōsō) is a Greek verb that means "to save" or "to rescue". It comes from the Greek word σῴζω (sōzō), which has the same meaning.

Grammar: σωσω is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: σῴζω (sōzō) - I save
- σῴζεις (sōzeis) - You save
- σῴζει (sōzei) - He/she/it saves

- Plural: σῴζομεν (sōzomen) - We save
- σῴζετε (sōzete) - You (plural) save
- σῴζουσι(ν) (sōzousi(n)) - They save

Description: σωσω is a powerful and important word in Greek, as it represents the act of saving or rescuing someone or something from danger or harm. It is often used in religious contexts, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the salvation of humanity through Jesus Christ. In everyday language, it can also be used to describe the act of saving someone from a difficult situation or helping them out of trouble.
