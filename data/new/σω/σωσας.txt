Word: σωσας

Etymology: σωσας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σῴζω (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue." 

Grammar: σωσας is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb σῴζω (sōzō). It is translated as "you saved" or "you rescued." 

Description: σωσας is a verb that describes an action of saving or rescuing someone or something from danger or harm. It is often used in a religious context to refer to salvation or redemption. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the actions of Jesus Christ in saving humanity from sin and death.
