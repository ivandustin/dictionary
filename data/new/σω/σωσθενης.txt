Word: σωσθενης

Etymology:
The word σωσθενης comes from the Greek root words σῶς (sos) meaning "safe" or "sound" and θενής (thenes) meaning "strength" or "power". Therefore, the word σωσθενης can be translated to mean "strong and healthy".

Grammar:
In Greek, σωσθενης is an adjective that is used to describe a person or thing that is strong and healthy. It is declined in the masculine, feminine, and neuter genders, and in the nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases.

Description:
The word σωσθενης is often used in medical contexts to describe a person who is physically strong and healthy. It can also be used more broadly to describe someone who is mentally or emotionally strong. In ancient Greek literature, the word was often used to describe heroes or warriors who were known for their strength and bravery.
