The word "σωιζειν" (sōizein) is a Greek verb that means "to save" or "to rescue." It comes from the root word "σως" (sōs), which means "safe" or "sound." In terms of grammar, "σωιζειν" is a present active infinitive, meaning it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of saving or rescuing in the present tense. Overall, "σωιζειν" is a word that conveys the idea of delivering someone or something from harm or danger.
