Etymology: The word "μωριαν" comes from the Greek word "μωρία" (moría), which means "foolishness" or "stupidity". 

Grammar: "μωριαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "μωριαν" refers to the concept of foolishness or stupidity. It is used to describe someone or something that lacks intelligence or common sense.
