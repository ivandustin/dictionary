Etymology: The word "μωρία" (mōria) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "μωρός" (mōros), meaning "foolish" or "stupid".

Grammar: In Greek, "μωρία" is a feminine noun. It is usually used in the singular form, but it can also be used in the plural form "μωρίες" (mōries).

Description: "Μωρία" refers to foolishness, stupidity, or silliness. It is used to describe someone's lack of intelligence or common sense. It can also be used to refer to absurd or nonsensical behavior or ideas.
