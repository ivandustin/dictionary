Etymology: The word "μωροί" (mōroi) comes from the Greek language. It is the plural form of the word "μωρός" (mōros), which means "foolish" or "silly".

Grammar: "Μωροί" (mōroi) is a noun in the nominative case, plural number. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension.

Description: "Μωροί" (mōroi) refers to a group of people who are considered foolish or silly. It can be used to describe individuals who lack intelligence, wisdom, or common sense.
