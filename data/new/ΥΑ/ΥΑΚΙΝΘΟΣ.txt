Etymology: The word "υακινθος" (pronounced "yakinthos") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the name of a flower called "hyacinth" in English.

Grammar: "Υακινθος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "υακινθου" (yakinthou).

Description: In Greek mythology, Hyacinth was a beautiful Spartan youth loved by the god Apollo. According to the myth, Hyacinth was accidentally killed by a discus thrown by Apollo during a friendly competition. From his blood, Apollo created a flower, which was named after him. The hyacinth flower is known for its vibrant colors and pleasant fragrance. In modern Greek, "υακινθος" refers to the hyacinth flower.
