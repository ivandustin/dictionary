Word: ιατρων

Etymology:
The word ιατρων (iatron) is derived from the Greek word ιατρός (iatros), which means "physician" or "healer". The suffix -ων (-on) is a masculine plural ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of physicians.

Grammar:
Ιατρων is a masculine noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. It is the genitive plural form of ιατρός (iatros), which means "physician" or "healer". In Greek, nouns decline according to their gender, number, and case.

Description:
Ιατρων is a word that refers to a group of physicians or healers. It is often used in medical contexts, such as in the names of medical associations or organizations. The word emphasizes the importance of collaboration and teamwork among healthcare professionals.
