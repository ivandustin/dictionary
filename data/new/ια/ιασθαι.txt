Word: ιασθαι

Etymology:
The word ιασθαι (iasthai) is derived from the Greek verb ιάομαι (iaomai), which means "to heal" or "to cure". This verb is also the root of the word ιατρός (iatros), which means "doctor" or "physician".

Grammar:
ιασθαι is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the middle voice. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
ιασθαι means "to be healed" or "to be cured" in Greek. It is often used in the context of physical healing, but can also refer to emotional or spiritual healing. The middle voice emphasizes that the healing is happening to the subject, rather than being done by an external agent.
