The word "ιατροις" (iatrois) is a plural form of the Greek noun "ιατρός" (iatros), which means "doctor" or "physician". The word has its roots in the Greek verb "ιάομαι" (iaomai), which means "to heal" or "to cure". In terms of grammar, "ιατροις" is a third declension noun in the masculine gender. It is declined like the singular form "ιατρός", but with the addition of the plural ending "-οις" (-ois).
