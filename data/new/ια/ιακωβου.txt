Grammar: Ιακώβου is a Greek word in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of Jacob" or "belonging to Jacob".

Description: Ιακώβου is a name that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the genealogy of Jesus Christ in the Gospel of Matthew. It is the Greek form of the name Jacob, which is a common name in the Old Testament. The name has a rich history and is associated with the patriarch Jacob, who was the son of Isaac and the father of the twelve tribes of Israel.
