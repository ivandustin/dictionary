Word: ιαματων

Etymology: The word ιαματων comes from the Greek word ιαμα, which means "healing" or "cure". The suffix -ων is a plural ending, so ιαματων means "healings" or "cures".

Grammar: ιαματων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, nouns change their endings depending on their function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple healings or cures.

Description: ιαματων refers to healings or cures, often in a medical or spiritual context. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the miraculous healings performed by Jesus. It can also be used more generally to refer to any kind of healing or remedy.
