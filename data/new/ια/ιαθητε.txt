The word "ιαθητε" (iathete) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and second person plural form. It is derived from the root word "ιαομαι" (iaomai), which means "to heal" or "to cure". 

In terms of grammar, "ιαθητε" is a passive verb, indicating that the action of healing or curing is being done to the subject rather than by the subject. The second person plural form suggests that the action is being directed towards a group of people.

Overall, "ιαθητε" can be translated to mean "you were healed" or "you were cured". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the healing of the soul or spirit.
