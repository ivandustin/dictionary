Etymology: The word "χοιρούς" (choiroús) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "χοῖρος" (choîros), which means "pig" or "swine".

Grammar: In Greek, "χοιρούς" (choiroús) is a noun. It is in the masculine gender and singular number. It belongs to the second declension class of nouns.
