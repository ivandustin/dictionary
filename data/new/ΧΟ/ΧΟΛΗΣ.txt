Etymology: The word "χολής" (cholēs) comes from the Greek word "χολή" (cholē), which means "bile" or "gall." In ancient Greek medicine, bile was believed to be one of the four humors that influenced a person's temperament.

Grammar: The word "χολής" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Χολής" is a Greek word that refers to "bile" or "gall." Bile is a digestive fluid produced by the liver and stored in the gallbladder. It plays a crucial role in the digestion and absorption of fats. In ancient Greek medicine, an imbalance of the humors, including an excess of bile, was believed to cause various physical and mental conditions.
