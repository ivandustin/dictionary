Etymology: The word "χοιρων" (choiron) comes from the Greek word "χοῖρος" (choiros), which means "pig" or "swine".

Grammar: In Greek, "χοιρων" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular number.

Description: "Χοιρων" is the Greek word for "pig" or "swine". It refers to a domesticated mammal of the Suidae family, typically having a stout body, short legs, a snout for rooting in the ground, and a thick skin covered in bristles. Pigs are commonly raised for their meat, and their products are used in various industries. In Greek culture, pigs were also associated with certain religious rituals and festivals.
