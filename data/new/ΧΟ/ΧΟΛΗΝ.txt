Etymology: The word "χολήν" (cholēn) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ghel- meaning "to shine, be yellow or green." In ancient Greek, it referred to the yellowish-green bile produced by the liver.

Grammar: "Χολήν" (cholēn) is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Χολήν" (cholēn) refers to bile, a bitter yellowish-green fluid produced by the liver and stored in the gallbladder. In ancient Greek medicine, it was believed to be one of the four humors that influenced a person's temperament and health. Bile plays a role in the digestion and absorption of fats in the small intestine.
