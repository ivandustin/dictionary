Etymology: The word "χορτασθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "χορτάζω" (chortázo), which means "to be satisfied" or "to be full". The suffix "-σθηναι" (-sthenai) is a reflexive form indicating that the action is performed on oneself.

Grammar: "Χορτασθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "χορτάζω" (chortázo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action.

Description: "Χορτασθηναι" is used to describe the state of being satisfied or full. It implies that one has consumed enough food or has had enough of something. It can also be used metaphorically to express being content or fulfilled in a broader sense.
