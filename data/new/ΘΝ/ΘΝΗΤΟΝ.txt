Grammar: "Θνητόν" (thneton) is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "θνητόν" (thneton) is used to describe a noun that is also neuter and singular.

Description: "Θνητόν" (thneton) refers to something that is mortal or subject to death. It is often used to describe human beings or other living creatures that have a limited lifespan.
