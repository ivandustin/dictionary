Etymology: The word "θνητηί" (pronounced "thniti") comes from the Greek word "θνητός" (pronounced "thnitos"), which means "mortal" or "mortal being". 

Grammar: "Θνητηί" is a noun in the singular form. It is a feminine noun, and its gender is indicated by the ending "-ηί". 

Description: "Θνητηί" refers to a mortal being or a person who is subject to death. It is often used in Greek mythology to describe humans, as opposed to gods or immortals.
