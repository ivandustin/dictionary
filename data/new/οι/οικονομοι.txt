Word: οικονομοι

Etymology: The word οικονομοι comes from the Greek words οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and νομος (nomos) meaning "law". Therefore, οικονομοι originally referred to someone who managed the affairs of a household.

Grammar: οικονομοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative form of the word, which means it is used as the subject of a sentence. The singular form of the word is οικονόμος (oikonomos).

Description: οικονομοι is a term used in ancient Greece to refer to officials who were responsible for managing the finances of a city-state or a temple. They were in charge of collecting taxes, managing public funds, and overseeing the distribution of resources. In some cases, they also had judicial powers and were responsible for enforcing laws related to financial matters. The role of οικονομοι was highly respected in ancient Greek society, and they were often chosen from the wealthiest and most influential families.
