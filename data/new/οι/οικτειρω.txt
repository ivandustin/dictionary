Word: οικτειρω

Etymology: The word οικτειρω comes from the Greek root word οικτος (oiktos), which means "pity" or "compassion". The suffix -ειρω (-eiro) is a verb-forming suffix that indicates the action of the root word.

Grammar: οικτειρω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: οικτειρω (oikteiro) - I pity/feel compassion
- Plural: οικτειρουμε (oikteiroume) - We pity/feel compassion

Description: οικτειρω is a Greek verb that means "to pity" or "to feel compassion for". It is often used in a religious or moral context, to describe the feeling of compassion that one has for others who are suffering or in need. The word can also be used in a more general sense, to describe any feeling of sympathy or empathy towards another person.
