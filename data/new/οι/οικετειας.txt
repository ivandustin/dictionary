Word: οικετείας

Etymology:
The word οικετείας comes from the Greek root word οἶκος (oikos) which means "house" or "home". The suffix -τείας (-teias) is a genitive singular ending that denotes possession or ownership. Therefore, οικετείας means "of the house" or "belonging to the household".

Grammar:
Οικετείας is a feminine noun in the genitive singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to show possession or ownership. 

Description:
Οικετείας is a word that refers to something that belongs to the household or is related to the management of a household. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as household goods, servants, or the management of a household. In ancient Greece, the term was often used to refer to the duties and responsibilities of a wife in managing the household. Today, the word is less commonly used, but it still carries the connotation of something related to the household or domestic life.
