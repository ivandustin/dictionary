The word "οικειν" (oikein) is a Greek verb that means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". It comes from the root word "οικος" (oikos), which means "house" or "home". In terms of grammar, "οικειν" is a first conjugation verb in the present tense, and it can be used in various tenses and moods to express different meanings related to dwelling or inhabiting. Overall, "οικειν" is a versatile and important word in Greek language and culture.
