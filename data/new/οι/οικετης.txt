Word: οικέτης

Etymology:
The word οικέτης (oikétēs) comes from the Greek root word οἶκος (oîkos), meaning "house" or "home." Οικέτης originally referred to a household servant or slave who worked within the home.

Grammar:
Οικέτης is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and its nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases are as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: οικέτης (oikétēs)
Genitive: οικέτου (oikétou)
Dative: οικέτῃ (oikéti)
Accusative: οικέτην (oikéten)

Plural:
Nominative: οικέται (oikétai)
Genitive: οικετῶν (oiketôn)
Dative: οικέταις (oikétais)
Accusative: οικέτας (oikétas)

Description:
Οικέτης is a term that has evolved over time. In ancient Greece, it referred to a household servant or slave who worked within the home. Today, the term is used more broadly to refer to someone who works in a domestic capacity, such as a housekeeper or caretaker. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is subservient or obedient to another person or group.
