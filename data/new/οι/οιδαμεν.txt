Word: οιδαμεν

Etymology:
The word οιδαμεν (oidamen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word οἶδα (oida), which means "to know." It is a perfect tense form of the verb, which means that it indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present.

Οιδαμεν is a first-person plural perfect indicative active verb in Greek. It is formed by combining the stem οἶδα (oida) with the perfect tense ending -μεν (-men). The verb means "we have known" or "we know."

Οιδαμεν is a common verb in Greek and is used to express knowledge or understanding of something. It can be translated as "we know," "we have known," or "we understand."
