Word: οικονομειν

Etymology: Οικονομείν comes from the Greek word οἰκονομέω (oikonoméō), which means "to manage a household" or "to manage resources". It is derived from the words οἶκος (oîkos), meaning "house", and νέμω (némō), meaning "to manage".

Grammar: Οικονομείν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the present infinitive form of the verb οἰκονομέω.

Description: Οικονομείν means "to manage" or "to administer". It is often used in the context of managing resources, such as money or property. In ancient Greece, it was also used to refer to the management of a household or estate. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to refer to the management of finances or resources.
