Word: οικονομος

Etymology: The word οικονομος comes from the Greek words οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and νομος (nomos) meaning "law". Therefore, οικονομος originally meant "household manager" or "steward".

Grammar: οικονομος is a masculine noun in Greek. Its plural form is οικονομοι (oikonomoi).

Description: οικονομος refers to a person who manages the financial affairs of a household, business, or organization. In ancient Greece, οικονομοι were responsible for managing the estates of wealthy families. Today, the term is used to refer to financial managers, accountants, and other professionals who are responsible for managing the financial affairs of businesses and organizations.
