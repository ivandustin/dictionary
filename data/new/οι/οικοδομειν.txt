Word: οικοδομειν

Etymology:
The word οικοδομειν comes from the Greek roots οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομειν (domein) meaning "to build". Therefore, οικοδομειν means "to build a house" or "to construct".

Grammar:
Οικοδομειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is οικοδομε- and it follows the regular pattern of conjugation.

Description:
Οικοδομειν is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to the physical act of building a house or any other structure, as well as to the metaphorical act of building something abstract, such as a relationship, a career, or a reputation. It is often used in the New Testament to describe the building of the church or the spiritual growth of believers. Overall, οικοδομειν conveys the idea of creating something new and valuable through hard work and dedication.
