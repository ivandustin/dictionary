The word "οικοδεσπότου" (oikodespotou) is a Greek noun that refers to the head of a household or the master of a house. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "οίκος" (oikos) meaning "house" and "δεσπότης" (despotis) meaning "master" or "lord".

Grammar: Οικοδεσπότου is a singular noun in the genitive case. 

Description: The term is often used to refer to the person who is responsible for the management and organization of a household, including its finances, maintenance, and hospitality. In modern Greek, the word can also be used to refer to the owner or manager of a hotel or other lodging establishment.
