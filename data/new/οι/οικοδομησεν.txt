Word: οικοδομησεν

Etymology: οικοδομησεν is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομη (dome) meaning "building". Therefore, οικοδομησεν means "he/she/it built a house".

Grammar: οικοδομησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb οικοδομέω (oikodomeo), which means "to build". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: οικοδομησεν is a verb that describes the action of building a house. It can be used in various contexts, such as in the Bible where it is used to describe the building of the temple in Jerusalem. It is a common word in modern Greek and is used to describe the construction of buildings and houses.
