The word "οικετην" (oiketēn) is a Greek noun that means "servant" or "household slave". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root word "οἶκος" (oikos), which means "house" or "home". The suffix "-την" (-tēn) is a common ending for nouns denoting a person who performs a certain action or function.

Grammar: "Οικετην" is a singular noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: In ancient Greek society, household slaves were an integral part of the economy and daily life. They were owned by the head of the household and performed various tasks such as cooking, cleaning, and farming. Slavery was a common practice in ancient Greece, and slaves were considered property rather than people with rights. The word "οικετην" reflects this social reality and is often used in literature and historical texts to describe the role of household slaves in ancient Greek society.
