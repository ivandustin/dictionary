Word: οικετων

Etymology:
The word οικετων (oiketon) is derived from the Greek word οἶκος (oikos), which means "house" or "home". Οικετων is the genitive plural form of οἰκέτης (oiketes), which means "household servant" or "slave".

Grammar:
Οικετων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. The plural form indicates that there are multiple household servants or slaves.

Description:
Οικετων refers to the household servants or slaves who worked in ancient Greek households. They were responsible for various tasks such as cooking, cleaning, and taking care of the children. In some cases, they were treated well and considered part of the family, while in others they were mistreated and abused. The role of οικετων was an important one in ancient Greek society, and their labor was essential for the functioning of households.
