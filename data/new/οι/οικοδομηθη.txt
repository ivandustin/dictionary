Word: οικοδομηθη

Etymology:
The word οικοδομηθη comes from the Greek root words οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομη (domi) meaning "building". The word is derived from the verb οικοδομέω (oikodomeo) which means "to build" or "to construct".

Grammar:
Οικοδομηθη is a past tense passive voice form of the verb οικοδομέω (oikodomeo). It is in the third person singular form and refers to a single action that was done to the subject of the sentence.

Οικοδομηθη is a Greek word that means "was built" or "was constructed". It is often used to describe the process of building or constructing something, such as a house or a building.
