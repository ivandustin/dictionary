Word: οικονομους

Etymology: Οικονομους is a Greek word that comes from the root words οἶκος (oikos) meaning "house" and νόμος (nomos) meaning "law". Together, they form the word οἰκονόμος (oikonomos) which means "steward" or "manager". 

Grammar: Οικονομους is the plural form of οικονόμος (oikonomos) and is used to refer to multiple stewards or managers. It is a noun in the accusative case. 

Description: Οικονομους is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to stewards or managers of household affairs. It is often used in parables to illustrate the importance of being a faithful and responsible manager of the resources entrusted to us. In modern usage, the term can also refer to financial managers or accountants.
