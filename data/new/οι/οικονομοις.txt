Word: οικονομοις

Etymology: οικονομοις is a Greek word that comes from the root words οἶκος (oikos) meaning "house" and νόμος (nomos) meaning "law". Together, they form the word οἰκονομία (oikonomia) which means "management of a household" or "economy".

Grammar: οικονομοις is a noun in the dative plural form in Greek. It is often translated as "stewards" or "managers" in English.

Description: οικονομοις is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the stewards or managers of a household or estate. It is often used metaphorically to refer to those who are responsible for managing the resources and affairs of a community or organization. In modern usage, the term can refer to financial managers or administrators in various contexts.
