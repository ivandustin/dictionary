Word: οικοδομώ

Etymology: Οικοδομώ comes from the Greek words οίκος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομή (domi) meaning "structure" or "building". Therefore, οικοδομώ literally means "to build a house".

Grammar: Οικοδομώ is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω. The verb stem is οικοδομ-, and the ending -ώ indicates the first person singular present tense.

Description: Οικοδομώ means "to build" or "to construct". It is a common verb in Greek and is used in various contexts, such as in construction, architecture, and engineering. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of building or developing something, such as a relationship or a business.
