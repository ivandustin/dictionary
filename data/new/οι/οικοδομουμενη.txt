Word: οικοδομουμενη

Etymology: 
The word οικοδομουμενη comes from the Greek root words οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομεω (domeo) meaning "to build". The word is derived from the present participle form of the verb οικοδομέω (oikodomeo), which means "to build a house" or "to construct".

Grammar:
Οικοδομουμενη is the present participle form of the verb οικοδομέω (oikodomeo) in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently building or constructing something.

Description:
Οικοδομουμενη is a Greek word that describes the action of building or constructing something, specifically a house or a structure. It is often used in the context of construction or architecture, and can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of building or developing something abstract, such as a relationship or a community. The word has a positive connotation, suggesting growth, progress, and improvement.
