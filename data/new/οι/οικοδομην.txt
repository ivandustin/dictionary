Word: οικοδομην

Etymology:
The word οικοδομην comes from the Greek root words οἶκος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομή (domē) meaning "building". Therefore, οικοδομην literally means "house building" or "construction".

Grammar:
Οικοδομην is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. For example, in the sentence "Οικοδομώ μιαν καινήν οικίαν" (I am building a new house), οικοδομην is the direct object of the verb οικοδομώ (I am building).

Description:
Οικοδομην refers to the act of building or constructing a house or other structure. It can also be used figuratively to refer to the process of building or developing something, such as a relationship or a business. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the building of the temple or the church.
