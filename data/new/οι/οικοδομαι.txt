Word: οικοδομαι

Etymology:
The word οικοδομαι comes from the Greek roots οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομη (dome) meaning "building". Therefore, οικοδομαι literally means "to build a house".

Grammar:
Οικοδομαι is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of adding the suffix -ω to the stem of the verb.

Description:
Οικοδομαι is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to the physical act of building a house or any other structure, as well as to the metaphorical act of building something abstract, such as a relationship, a community, or a character. In the New Testament, οικοδομαι is often used in a spiritual sense, referring to the process of building up the church or the individual believer's faith.
