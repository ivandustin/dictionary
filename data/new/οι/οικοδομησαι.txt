Word: οικοδομησαι

Etymology:
The word οικοδομησαι comes from the Greek root words οικος (oikos) meaning "house" and δομη (dome) meaning "to build". Therefore, οικοδομησαι means "to build a house" or "to construct".

Grammar:
Οικοδομησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb οικοδομεω (oikodomeo), which means "to build" or "to construct". 

Description:
Οικοδομησαι is a Greek word that refers to the act of building or constructing a house or other structure. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the process of building or developing something, such as a relationship or a community. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the building of the church or the spiritual growth of believers.
