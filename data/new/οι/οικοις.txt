Word: οικοις

Etymology:
The word οικοις (oikois) is derived from the Greek word οἶκος (oikos), which means "house" or "home". It is a plural form of the word, which means "houses" or "homes".

Grammar:
In Greek, οικοις (oikois) is a noun in the dative case. It is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
Οικοις (oikois) is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to the households or families of individuals. It is often used in the context of describing the social structure of ancient Greek society, where the household was considered the basic unit of society. The term can also be used to refer to the physical structures of houses or homes.
