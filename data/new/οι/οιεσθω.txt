Word: οιεσθω

Etymology: The word οιεσθω comes from the ancient Greek verb οἴομαι (oíomai), which means "to think, suppose, imagine". 

Grammar: οιεσθω is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and imperative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb οἴομαι (oíomai). 

Description: οιεσθω is a command form of the verb οἴομαι (oíomai), which means "to think, suppose, imagine". The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. Therefore, οιεσθω can be translated as "imagine" or "suppose" in English.
