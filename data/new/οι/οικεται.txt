Word: οικεται

Etymology: οικεται is a Greek word that comes from the root word οἶκος (oikos), meaning "house" or "home." Οικεται is the plural form of οἰκέτης (oiketēs), which means "household servant" or "domestic slave."

Grammar: οικεται is a third-person plural present active indicative form of the Greek verb οἰκέω (oikeō), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit." In this form, it means "they dwell" or "they inhabit."

Description: οικεται is a word that refers to a group of people who dwell or inhabit a particular place. In ancient Greece, it was often used to refer to household servants or domestic slaves who lived and worked in the homes of their masters. Today, it can be used more broadly to refer to any group of people who live together in a particular place, such as a community or a family.
