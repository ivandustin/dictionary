The word "ερωταν" (erotan) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "ἐρωτάω" (erotao), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". 

Grammar: "Ερωταν" is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb. 

Description: The word "ερωταν" means "he/she/it asks" or "he/she/it inquires". It is commonly used in everyday conversation and can be used in a variety of contexts, such as asking for information, seeking clarification, or expressing curiosity.
