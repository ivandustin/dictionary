The word "εριθειαν" (eritheian) is a Greek noun that means "strife" or "contention". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "erithos" which means "strife" or "discord". 

Grammar: "εριθειαν" is a singular accusative noun in the Greek language. 

Description: The word is often used in a negative context to describe conflicts or disagreements between individuals or groups. It can also refer to a state of constant arguing or bickering.
