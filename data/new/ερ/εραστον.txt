Word: εραστόν

Etymology:
The word εραστόν (eraston) comes from the Greek word εραστής (erastes), which means "lover" or "admirer". It is derived from the verb εράω (erao), which means "to love".

Grammar:
εραστόν (eraston) is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form of the word is εραστού (erastou).

Description:
εραστόν (eraston) refers to a male lover or admirer. In ancient Greece, it was commonly used to describe the younger partner in a pederastic relationship, where an older man would take a younger man as his lover and mentor. The term has also been used in a broader sense to refer to any male lover or admirer, regardless of age or sexual orientation.
