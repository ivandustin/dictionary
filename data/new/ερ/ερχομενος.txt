Word: ερχομενος

Etymology: 
The word ερχομενος (erchomenos) is a present participle of the Greek verb ερχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ- (to move, to go).

Grammar:
In Greek, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action. The word ερχομενος (erchomenos) is in the masculine singular nominative case, which means it is used to describe a male subject who is currently coming or arriving.

Description:
The word ερχομενος (erchomenos) can be translated to English as "coming" or "arriving". It is often used in Greek to describe someone who is in the process of arriving at a location or coming to a particular event. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or event that is approaching or imminent.
