Word: ερημωσις

Etymology:
The word ερημωσις (erēmōsis) comes from the Greek verb ερημόω (erēmóō), which means "to make desolate" or "to lay waste". The suffix -σις (-sis) is a common Greek noun ending that denotes the action or process of the verb.

Grammar:
ερημωσις is a feminine noun in Greek, and its nominative singular form is ερημωσις. In the genitive singular, it is ερημωσεως (erēmōseōs), and in the accusative singular, it is ερημωσιν (erēmōsin). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
ερημωσις refers to the act of making something desolate or uninhabitable. It can also refer to a state of desolation or abandonment. In the context of the Bible, ερημωσις is often used to describe the destruction of cities or the punishment of sinners.
