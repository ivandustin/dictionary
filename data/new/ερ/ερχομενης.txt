Word: ερχομενης

Etymology: 
The word ερχομενης (erchomenes) is the feminine form of the present participle of the Greek verb ερχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ- (to move, to go).

Grammar:
ερχομενης is a feminine singular form of the present participle of the verb ερχομαι (erchomai). It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of the sentence. The word can be translated as "coming" or "arriving".

Description:
ερχομενης is a Greek word that describes a female subject who is currently in the process of coming or arriving at a certain place. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Η ερχομενης του τρένου είναι στις 5 το απόγευμα" (The arrival of the train is at 5 pm).
