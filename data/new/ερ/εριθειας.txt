The word "εριθειας" (eritheias) is a Greek noun that means "strife" or "contention". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "erithos", which means "strife" or "discord". 

Grammar: "Εριθειας" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. 

Description: The word is often used in a negative context to describe conflicts or disagreements between people. It can also refer to a competitive or argumentative nature.
