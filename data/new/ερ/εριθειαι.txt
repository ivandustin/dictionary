Word: εριθειαι

Etymology: The word εριθειαι (eritheiai) comes from the Greek word εριθεία (eritheia), which means "strife" or "contention". It is derived from the verb εριθεύω (eritheuo), which means "to contend" or "to strive".

Grammar: εριθειαι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: εριθειαι refers to acts of strife or contention. It can be used to describe any kind of conflict or disagreement, whether it is between individuals, groups, or nations. In ancient Greece, the term was often used to describe political rivalries and power struggles. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to describe any kind of conflict or disagreement.
