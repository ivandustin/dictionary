Word: ερμηνία

Etymology: The word ερμηνία comes from the Greek verb ερμηνεύω (erminevo), which means "to interpret" or "to explain". It is derived from the noun ερμηνεία (ermeneia), which means "interpretation" or "explanation".

Grammar: ερμηνία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is ερμηνίες (ermeneies).

Description: ερμηνία refers to the act of interpreting or explaining something. It can also refer to the interpretation or explanation itself. In Greek mythology, Hermes was the god of interpretation and communication, and his name is related to the word ερμηνεία. In modern Greek, ερμηνία is commonly used in legal and religious contexts, as well as in literary criticism and philosophy.
