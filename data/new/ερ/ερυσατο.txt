Word: ερυσατο

Etymology: The word ερυσατο comes from the Ancient Greek verb ερυσάμην (erysámen), which means "I drew water". It is derived from the noun ερυσίπτω (erysíptō), which means "I draw water".

Grammar: ερυσατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb ερυσάμην (erysámen). In Ancient Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: ερυσατο means "he/she/it drew water for himself/herself/itself" in Ancient Greek. It is a verb that describes the action of drawing water from a well or other source. This word is commonly found in Ancient Greek literature, particularly in descriptions of daily life and agricultural practices.
