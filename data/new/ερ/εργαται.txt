The word "εργαται" (ergatai) is a Greek noun in the plural form, which means "workers" or "laborers". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "εργάζομαι" (ergazomai), which means "to work" or "to labor". 

Grammar: "Εργαται" is the nominative plural form of the noun, which means it is used to refer to a group of workers or laborers. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: The word "εργαται" is commonly used in Greek to refer to people who work in various fields, such as construction, agriculture, manufacturing, and services. It can also be used in a broader sense to refer to the working class or the labor force in general.
