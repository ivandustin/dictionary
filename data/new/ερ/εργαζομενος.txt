Word: εργαζόμενος

Etymology: The word εργαζόμενος comes from the Greek verb εργάζομαι (ergázomai), which means "to work" or "to labor". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, which means "working" or "being employed".

Grammar: εργαζόμενος is a masculine noun in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence. It is also a present participle, which means it describes an ongoing action or state of being. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives, describing the subject of the sentence.

Description: εργαζόμενος refers to a person who is currently employed or working. It can be used to describe someone who works in any field or industry, from manual labor to office work. The word emphasizes the ongoing nature of the work, rather than a specific job or task.
