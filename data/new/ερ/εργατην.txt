The word "εργατην" (ergatin) is a Greek noun that means "worker" or "laborer". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "εργάζομαι" (ergazomai), which means "to work". 

Grammar: "Εργατην" is a singular noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. 

Description: The word is commonly used in Greek to refer to someone who works in manual labor, such as a construction worker or a factory worker. It can also be used more broadly to refer to anyone who works for a living.
