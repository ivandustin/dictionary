Word: ερωτησαι

Etymology:
The word ερωτησαι comes from the Greek verb ερωτάω (erotao), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". It is derived from the noun ερωτήμα (erotima), which means "question".

Grammar:
ερωτησαι is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb stem is ερωτη-, and the ending -σαι indicates the second person singular form.

Description:
ερωτησαι is a common Greek verb that means "to ask" or "to inquire". It is often used in everyday conversation and in formal settings, such as in interviews or meetings. The verb can be used to ask a question or to request information from someone. It can also be used to express curiosity or interest in a particular topic. The verb can be conjugated in different tenses, moods, and persons to match the subject and context of the sentence.
