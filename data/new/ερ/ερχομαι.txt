Word: ερχομαι

Etymology:
The word ερχομαι (erchomai) is a Greek verb that means "to come" or "to go". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁regʷ- which means "to move in a straight line".

Grammar:
ερχομαι (erchomai) is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ έρχομαι (ego erchomai) - I come
- Εσύ έρχεσαι (esi erchesai) - You come (singular, informal)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό έρχεται (autos/afti/afto erchetai) - He/She/It comes

Plural:
- Εμείς έρχομαστε (emeis erchomaste) - We come
- Εσείς έρχεστε (eseis ercheste) - You come (plural, formal or informal)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά έρχονται (autoi/aftes/afta erchontai) - They come

Description:
ερχομαι (erchomai) is a common Greek verb that is used to describe movement towards a destination. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to describe physical movement, emotional states, or the passage of time. The verb is often used in combination with prepositions to indicate the direction of movement, such as "προς" (pros) which means "towards", or "από" (apo) which means "from".
