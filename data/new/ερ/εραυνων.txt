The word "εραυνων" (eraunon) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "εραυνάω" (eraunaō), which means "to search" or "to investigate". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek root "εραυν-" (eraun-), which means "to search" or "to explore". 

Grammar: As a present participle, "εραυνων" functions as an adjective and describes a continuous action of searching or investigating. It can be used to modify a noun or pronoun, or it can be used as a noun itself. 

Description: "Εραυνων" can be translated as "searching" or "investigating". It implies a persistent and thorough effort to find or discover something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as scientific research, criminal investigations, or personal exploration.
