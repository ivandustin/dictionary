The word "εριθεια" (eritheia) is a Greek noun that can be translated to mean "ambition," "striving," or "competition." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "εριθεύω" (eritheuo), which means "to contend" or "to strive." 

Grammar: "Εριθεια" is a feminine noun in the singular form. 

Description: The word is often used in a positive sense to describe someone who is ambitious and driven to succeed. However, it can also have negative connotations when used to describe someone who is overly competitive or willing to do whatever it takes to win. In ancient Greek philosophy, "εριθεια" was seen as a virtue when balanced with other virtues such as wisdom and justice.
