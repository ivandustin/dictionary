Word: εργατης

Etymology: The word εργατης (ergatis) comes from the ancient Greek word ἐργάτης (ergatēs), which means "worker" or "laborer". It is derived from the verb ἐργάζομαι (ergazomai), which means "to work".

Grammar: εργατης is a masculine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural, and can be used as the subject or object of a sentence. For example, "Ο εργάτης δουλεύει σκληρά" (The worker works hard).

Description: εργατης refers to a person who performs physical or manual labor, such as a construction worker, factory worker, or farmer. It is a common term used in Greek society to describe individuals who work in various industries and sectors. The word can also be used in a broader sense to refer to anyone who works for a living.
