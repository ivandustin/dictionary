Word: εραυνησον

Etymology:
The word εραυνησον (eraunēson) is a verb in the aorist imperative mood in Ancient Greek. It comes from the root word εραυνάω (eraunáō), which means "to search, investigate, examine thoroughly". The word is derived from the noun εραυνή (eraunē), which means "search, inquiry, investigation".

Grammar:
εραυνησον is a second-person singular aorist imperative verb in Ancient Greek. It is used to give a command or order to someone to perform an action in the past. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed or finished.

Description:
The word εραυνησον is often translated as "search" or "investigate" in English. It is a strong command to someone to thoroughly examine or investigate something. The word is often used in a figurative sense, such as searching for knowledge or truth. In the New Testament, the word is used in the context of searching for God's will or understanding the mysteries of God.
