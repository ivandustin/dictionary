Word: ερχομενον

Etymology: 
The word ερχομενον (erchomenon) is the present participle of the Greek verb ερχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ- which means "to move, to go".

Grammar:
ερχομενον is a neuter gender noun in the singular form. It is in the present participle tense, which means it describes an ongoing action. In Greek, the present participle is formed by adding the suffix -οντας (-ontas) to the stem of the verb.

Description:
ερχομενον can be translated as "coming" or "in the act of coming". It is often used in Greek literature to describe a person or thing that is currently arriving or approaching. For example, in the New Testament, it is used to describe the coming of Jesus Christ. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or event that is imminent or about to happen.
