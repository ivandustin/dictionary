The word "εραυνατε" (eraunate) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "ἐραυνάω" (eraunaō), which means "to search, to explore, to investigate". 

Grammar: "εραυνατε" is the second person plural present imperative form of the verb "ἐραυνάω". This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people, telling them to search, explore or investigate something. 

Description: The word "εραυνατε" is often used in a figurative sense, meaning to search for knowledge or understanding. It can also be used in a more literal sense, such as when searching for something lost or hidden. Overall, the word conveys a sense of curiosity and a desire to discover new things.
