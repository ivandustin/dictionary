The word "ερριπται" (erriptai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "ρίπτω" (ripto), which means "to throw" or "to cast". The prefix "ερ-" (er-) adds emphasis to the action of throwing.

In terms of grammar, "ερριπται" is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" (o). It is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of throwing.

As for its meaning, "ερριπται" can be translated as "he/she/it throws" or "he/she/it is throwing". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone throwing a ball or casting a fishing line.
