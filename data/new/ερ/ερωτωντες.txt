Word: ερωτωντες

Etymology: The word ερωτωντες comes from the Greek verb ερωτάω (erotao), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". The suffix -ντες (-ntes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently asking or inquiring.

Grammar: ερωτωντες is a plural noun in the present participle form. It is often used as a descriptive term for a group of people who are currently asking or inquiring about something.

Description: ερωτωντες can be translated to "inquirers" or "questioners". It is often used in a philosophical or intellectual context to describe individuals who are seeking knowledge or understanding. In ancient Greece, the term was used to refer to those who engaged in philosophical discussions and debates. Today, it can be used to describe anyone who is actively seeking information or answers to their questions.
