Word: ερχομενου

Etymology: 
The word ερχομενου (erchomenou) is the present participle of the Greek verb ερχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come". The verb ερχομαι is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ- which means "to move, to go".

Grammar:
ερχομενου is a masculine singular genitive form of the present participle of the verb ερχομαι. In Greek, the present participle is used to indicate an ongoing action or state. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship to another noun.

Description:
The word ερχομενου can be translated as "of the one who is coming" or "of the coming one". It is often used in Greek literature and language to describe someone who is currently in the process of coming or arriving at a particular place.
