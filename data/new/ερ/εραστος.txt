Word: εραστός

Etymology:
The word εραστός (erastos) comes from the Greek verb εράω (erao), which means "to love". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁er- ("to move, stir").

Grammar:
In Greek, εραστός is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: εραστός (erastos)
- Genitive: εραστού (erastou)
- Accusative: εραστό (erasto)
- Vocative: εραστέ (eraste)

Plural:
- Nominative: εραστοί (erastoi)
- Genitive: εραστών (eraston)
- Accusative: εραστούς (erastous)
- Vocative: εραστοί (erastoi)

Description:
The word εραστός (erastos) refers to a male lover or a suitor. In ancient Greece, it was commonly used to describe a man who was in love with another man. The term was also used in a broader sense to refer to anyone who was passionately devoted to someone or something. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a male lover or a boyfriend.
