The word "εργατων" (ergaton) is a Greek noun in the plural form, which means "workers" or "laborers". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "εργα-" (erga-), which means "work" or "labor". The suffix "-των" (-ton) is a common plural ending in Greek.

Grammar: "Εργατων" is a third-declension noun in Greek, which means that its endings change depending on its grammatical case. For example, in the nominative case (subject of a sentence), it is "εργάτες" (ergates).

Description: The word "εργατων" is commonly used in Greek to refer to people who work in various fields, such as construction, manufacturing, agriculture, and services. It can also be used in a broader sense to refer to the working class or labor force in general.
