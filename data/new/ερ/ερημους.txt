Word: ερημους

Etymology:
The word ερημους (erēmous) is derived from the Greek word ερημία (erēmia), which means "desert" or "wilderness". It is related to the word ερημίτης (erēmitēs), which means "hermit" or "recluse".

Grammar:
ερημους is a masculine noun in the accusative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
ερημους is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the wilderness or desert. It is often used to describe the place where John the Baptist preached and where Jesus was tempted by Satan. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to a desolate or uninhabited place.
