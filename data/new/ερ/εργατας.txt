Word: εργατας

Etymology: The word εργατας (ergatas) comes from the ancient Greek word ἐργάτης (ergatēs), which means "worker" or "laborer". It is derived from the verb ἐργάζομαι (ergazomai), which means "to work".

Grammar: εργατας is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes εργάτες (ergates). It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns and its genitive form is εργάτα (ergata).

Description: εργατας refers to a person who works for a living, usually in manual labor or in a blue-collar job. It can also refer to a member of the working class. The word is commonly used in Greek society to describe people who work in factories, construction sites, or other physically demanding jobs.
