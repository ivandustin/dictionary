The word "εργα" (erga) is a Greek noun that means "works" or "tasks". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "ἔργον" (érgon), which also means "work" or "task". 

Grammar: "Εργα" is a plural noun in Greek, and it is usually used with a definite article, such as "τα εργα" (ta erga), which means "the works" or "the tasks". 

Description: The word "εργα" can refer to any kind of work or task, whether it is physical labor or mental effort. It can also refer to a specific project or job that needs to be completed. In Greek culture, the concept of "erga" is highly valued, as it represents the idea of hard work and productivity.
