The word "ερχομενα" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". The present participle form indicates that the action of coming is currently happening or ongoing. It can be translated to English as "coming" or "arriving". 

In terms of grammar, the present participle in Greek is formed by adding the suffix "-οντας" (-ontas) to the stem of the verb. The stem of "έρχομαι" is "ερχ-", so the present participle form is "ερχόμενος" (erchomenos) for masculine singular, "ερχόμενη" (erchomeni) for feminine singular, and "ερχόμενο" (erchomeno) for neuter singular. "Ερχόμενα" (erchomena) is the present participle form for neuter plural.

Overall, "ερχομενα" is a Greek word that describes something that is currently coming or arriving.
