The word "ερχεσθε" (erchesthe) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the present tense and second person plural form. It is derived from the root verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to go". 

In terms of grammar, "ερχεσθε" is a conjugated form of the verb and is used to address a group of people, instructing them to come or go somewhere. 

Etymologically, the root verb "έρχομαι" is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ-, which means "to move" or "to go". 

Overall, "ερχεσθε" is a common verb in Greek and is used in everyday conversation to give directions or instructions.
