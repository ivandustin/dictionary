Etymology: The word "φοβηθησομαι" (phobēthēsomai) comes from the Greek verb "φοβέω" (phobeō), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid". The suffix "-θησομαι" ("-thēsomai") is a future tense ending, indicating that the action of fearing will happen in the future.

Grammar: "Φοβηθησομαι" is a first-person singular future tense form of the verb "φοβέω" (phobeō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "Φοβηθησομαι" means "I will be afraid" or "I will fear" in English. It expresses the idea of experiencing fear or being afraid at some point in the future.
