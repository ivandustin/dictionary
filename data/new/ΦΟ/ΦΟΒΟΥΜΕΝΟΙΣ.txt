Grammar: "Φοβούμενοις" is the present participle form of the verb "φοβούμαι" in the dative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to express an ongoing action or state. The dative case indicates the indirect object or the recipient of the action.

Description: "Φοβούμενοις" can be translated as "to those who are afraid" or "for those who fear". It refers to a group of people who are currently experiencing fear or being afraid.
