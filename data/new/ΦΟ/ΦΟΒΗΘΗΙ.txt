Etymology: The word "φοβηθη" (phobēthē) comes from the Greek verb "φοβέω" (phobeō), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid". It is derived from the noun "φόβος" (phobos), meaning "fear" or "terror".

Grammar: "Φοβηθη" is the third person singular aorist passive subjunctive form of the verb "φοβέω" (phobeō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The subjunctive mood suggests a possibility or potentiality.

Description: "Φοβηθη" can be translated as "he/she/it was feared" or "he/she/it might have been feared" depending on the context. It implies that someone or something was the object of fear or caused fear in others.
