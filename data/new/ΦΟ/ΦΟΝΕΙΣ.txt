Etymology: The word "φονεις" (pronounced "foneis") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "φονεύω" (foneuo), which means "to murder" or "to kill."

Grammar: In Greek, "φονεις" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Φονεις" refers to "murderers" or "killers" in Greek. It is used to describe individuals who commit acts of murder or take someone's life intentionally.
