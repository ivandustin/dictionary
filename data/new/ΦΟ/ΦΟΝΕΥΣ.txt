Etymology: The word "φονευς" (phonēus) comes from the Greek root "φονεύω" (phoneuō), which means "to murder" or "to kill." 

Grammar: In Greek, "φονευς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "φονεῖς" (phoneis). 

Description: "Φονευς" refers to a murderer or a killer. It is used to describe someone who intentionally takes the life of another person.
