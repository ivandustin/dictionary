Etymology: The word "φοβηθείς" (pronounced "foveethis") comes from the Greek verb "φοβούμαι" (pronounced "fovoomeh"), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid". It is derived from the noun "φόβος" (pronounced "fovos"), meaning "fear".

Grammar: "Φοβηθείς" is the aorist tense, passive voice, second person singular form of the verb "φοβούμαι". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Φοβηθείς" can be translated to English as "you were afraid" or "you got scared". It describes a state of fear or being afraid that occurred in the past.
