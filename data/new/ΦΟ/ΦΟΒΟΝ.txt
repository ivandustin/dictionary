Etymology: The word "φοβον" (phobon) comes from the Greek word "φόβος" (phobos), which means fear or dread.

Grammar: "Φοβον" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Φοβον" refers to fear or terror. It is often used to describe a strong feeling of apprehension or anxiety in response to a perceived threat or danger.
