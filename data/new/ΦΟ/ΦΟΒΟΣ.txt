Etymology: The word "φοβος" (phobos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "φοβεω" (phobeo), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid".

Description: "Φοβος" (phobos) refers to fear or dread. It can also be used to describe a feeling of terror or anxiety. In Greek mythology, Phobos was the personification of fear and was often depicted as a companion of Ares, the god of war.
