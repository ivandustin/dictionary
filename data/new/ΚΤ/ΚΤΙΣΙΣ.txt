Etymology: The word "κτίσις" comes from the Greek verb "κτίζω" (ktízo), which means "to create" or "to build."

Grammar: In Greek, "κτίσις" is a feminine noun of the third declension. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Κτίσις" refers to the act of creating or building something. It can also be used to describe the result or product of this act, such as a creation, a structure, or an establishment. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the world or the universe as a whole, emphasizing the idea of creation.
