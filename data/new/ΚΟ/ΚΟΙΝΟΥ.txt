Etymology: The word "κοινού" (koynoú) comes from the Greek adjective "κοινός" (koinós), which means "common" or "shared".

Grammar: "Κοινού" is the genitive form of the word "κοινός" (koinós). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Κοινού" is a Greek word that can be translated as "of the common" or "of the shared". It is often used in phrases or expressions to refer to something that is common to a group of people or shared by multiple individuals.
