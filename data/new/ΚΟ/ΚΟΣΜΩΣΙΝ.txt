Etymology: The word "κοσμωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "κοσμέω" (kosmeo), which means "to order" or "to arrange". It is derived from the noun "κόσμος" (kosmos), meaning "world" or "order".

Grammar: "κοσμωσιν" is the present active participle of the verb "κοσμέω" (kosmeo) in the third person plural. It is used to describe an ongoing action of ordering or arranging.

Description: "κοσμωσιν" refers to the act of ordering or arranging something. It can be used in various contexts, such as organizing objects, arranging events, or establishing order in general.
