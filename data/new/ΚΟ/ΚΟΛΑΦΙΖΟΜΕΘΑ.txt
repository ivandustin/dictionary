Etymology: The word "κολαφιζομέθα" comes from the Greek verb "κολαφίζω" (kolaphízo), which means "to slap" or "to strike with an open hand." The suffix "-μεθα" indicates the reflexive form, meaning "we are slapped" or "we strike ourselves."

Grammar: "Κολαφιζόμεθα" is a verb in the present tense, indicative mood, and passive voice. It is conjugated in the first person plural, indicating that "we" are the ones being slapped or striking ourselves.

Description: The word "κολαφιζόμεθα" describes an action of slapping or striking oneself. It can be used metaphorically to express self-criticism or self-punishment for a mistake or wrongdoing.
