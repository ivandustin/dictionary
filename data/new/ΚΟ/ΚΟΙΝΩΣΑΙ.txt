Etymology: The word "κοινωσαί" comes from the Greek verb "κοινόω" (koinóo), which means "to make common" or "to share." It is derived from the adjective "κοινός" (koinós), meaning "common" or "shared."

Grammar: "κοινωσαί" is the 2nd person singular, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb "κοινόω" (koinóo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: The word "κοινωσαί" is used to describe the act of making something common or shared. It implies the action of sharing or making something available to others.
