Etymology: The word "κολυμβηθρα" comes from the Greek word "κολυμβάω" (kolymbáo), which means "to swim". The suffix "-θρα" is added to form the noun "κολυμβηθρα" (kolymbēthra), meaning "swimming pool".

Grammar: In Greek, "κολυμβηθρα" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "κολυμβηθρες" (kolymbēthres).

Description: "Κολυμβηθρα" refers to a swimming pool, a man-made structure filled with water for the purpose of swimming and other water-related activities. It is a common term used in Greek to describe both indoor and outdoor swimming pools.
