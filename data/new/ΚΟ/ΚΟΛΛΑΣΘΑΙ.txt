Etymology: The word "κολλασθαι" (kollasthai) comes from the Greek verb "κολλάω" (kollaō), which means "to glue" or "to stick together". 

Grammar: "κολλάσθαι" is a reflexive verb in the middle voice form. It is derived from the verb "κολλάω" and is conjugated in the present tense, middle voice, indicative mood, and third person singular. 

Description: The word "κολλάσθαι" is used to express the action of sticking or adhering to something. It implies a passive or involuntary action, as the subject is being stuck or glued to something.
