Etymology: The word "κόσμος" (kosmos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb "κοσμέω" (kosmeo), which means "to order" or "to arrange." In Greek philosophy, "κόσμος" refers to the ordered and harmonious universe.

Grammar: In Greek, "κόσμος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form and a plural form. The plural form is "κόσμοι" (kosmoi).

Description: "Κόσμος" has multiple meanings and uses in Greek. It can refer to the physical world or universe, as well as the order and arrangement of things within it. It can also mean "world" in the sense of the human world or society. Additionally, "κόσμος" can be used to describe the overall appearance or style of something, such as in the phrase "κόσμος της μόδας" (fashion world).
