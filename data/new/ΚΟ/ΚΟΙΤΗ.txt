Etymology: The word "κοιτη" (koiti) comes from the Ancient Greek word "κοίτη" (koítē), meaning "bed" or "couch". It is derived from the verb "κείμαι" (keímai), which means "to lie down" or "to recline".

Grammar: "Κοιτη" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "κοιτης" (koitēs).

Description: In modern Greek, "κοιτη" (koiti) refers to a bed or a sleeping place. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a resting place or a location where someone or something is settled or positioned.
