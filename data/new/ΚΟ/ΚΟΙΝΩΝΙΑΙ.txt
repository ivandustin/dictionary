Etymology: The word "κοινωνία" comes from the Greek root "κοινός" (koinos), meaning "common" or "shared". It is derived from the verb "κοινόω" (koinoo), which means "to make common" or "to share".

Grammar: In Greek, "κοινωνία" is a feminine noun. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can take different cases depending on its role in a sentence.

Description: "Κοινωνία" is a Greek word that has multiple meanings. It can refer to the concept of community, fellowship, or social interaction. It implies a sense of sharing, participation, and mutual support among individuals or groups. It can also be used to describe a society or a group of people who share common interests, beliefs, or goals. Additionally, "κοινωνία" can be used in a religious context to refer to the communion or fellowship among believers.
