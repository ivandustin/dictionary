Etymology: The word "κοσμος" (kosmos) comes from the Greek language and has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European word "ḱes-," meaning "to arrange" or "to adorn." It is related to other words such as "cosmetic" and "cosmopolitan."

Grammar: In Greek, "κοσμος" (kosmos) is a masculine noun. It is in the nominative case and can function as the subject or the predicate of a sentence.

Description: "Κοσμος" (kosmos) has multiple meanings in Greek. It can refer to the world or the universe, encompassing everything that exists. It can also mean order, harmony, or beauty, as in the well-arranged and balanced nature of the world. Additionally, it can denote the people or society, representing the organized and structured aspects of human civilization.
