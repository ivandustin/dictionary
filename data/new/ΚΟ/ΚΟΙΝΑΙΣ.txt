Etymology: The word "κοιναίς" (koineis) comes from the Greek word "κοινός" (koinos), meaning "common" or "shared." It refers to the common dialect of the Greek language that emerged during the Hellenistic period.

Grammar: "Κοιναίς" is the feminine form of the adjective "κοινός" (koinos), which means "common" or "shared." In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "κοιναίς" is used to modify feminine nouns in the dative or genitive case.

Description: "Κοιναίς" is an adjective that describes something as common or shared. It can be used to refer to shared ideas, beliefs, customs, or language. In the context of the Greek language, "κοιναίς" specifically refers to the common dialect that developed during the Hellenistic period and became the lingua franca of the eastern Mediterranean.
