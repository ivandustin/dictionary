Etymology: The word "κοινωνίαν" (ko̱no̱nían) comes from the Greek word "κοινωνία" (ko̱no̱nía), which means "community" or "fellowship". It is derived from the root word "κοινός" (ko̱nós), meaning "common" or "shared".

Grammar: "κοινωνίαν" is the accusative form of the noun "κοινωνία" (ko̱no̱nía) in Greek. In Greek grammar, the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "κοινωνίαν" refers to the concept of community or fellowship. It can also be used to describe the act of sharing or participating in something together. In a broader sense, it encompasses the idea of social interaction, cooperation, and mutual support among individuals or groups.
