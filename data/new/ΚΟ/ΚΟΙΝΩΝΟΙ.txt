Etymology: The word "κοινωνοι" (koinōnoi) comes from the Greek word "κοινωνία" (koinōnia), which means "community" or "fellowship". It is derived from the root word "κοινός" (koinos), meaning "common" or "shared".

Grammar: "Κοινωνοι" (koinōnoi) is the plural form of the noun "κοινωνός" (koinōnos), which means "companion" or "partner". In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their number (singular or plural), gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter), and case (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, or vocative).

Description: "Κοινωνοι" (koinōnoi) refers to a group of companions or partners who share a common purpose or interest.
