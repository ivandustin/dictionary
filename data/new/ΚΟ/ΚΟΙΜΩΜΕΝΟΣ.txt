Etymology: The word "κοιμώμενος" (koimómenos) comes from the Greek verb "κοιμάομαι" (koimáomai), which means "to sleep". It is derived from the noun "κοῖμος" (koîmos), meaning "sleep" or "slumber".

Grammar: "Κοιμώμενος" is the present participle form of the verb "κοιμάομαι" (koimáomai), which means "sleeping" or "being asleep". It is used to describe someone or something that is currently in a state of sleep.

Description: "Κοιμώμενος" is an adjective that describes the act of sleeping or being asleep. It can be used to refer to a person, an animal, or even an inanimate object that is in a state of sleep.
