Etymology: The word "κοινωνούντες" comes from the Greek verb "κοινωνέω" (koinonéo), which means "to share" or "to participate in." It is derived from the noun "κοινωνία" (koinonía), meaning "community" or "fellowship."

Grammar: "Κοινωνούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "κοινωνέω" in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action of sharing or participating in something. In this form, it agrees with the subject in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Κοινωνούντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "sharing" or "participating." It is often used to describe individuals who are actively involved in a community or who engage in mutual sharing and cooperation.
