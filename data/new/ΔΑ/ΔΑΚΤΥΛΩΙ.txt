Etymology: The word "δακτυλοι" (daktyloi) comes from the Greek word "δάκτυλος" (daktulos), meaning "finger". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deḱ- meaning "to show, point out".

Grammar: "Δακτυλοι" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and belongs to the third declension. In Greek grammar, nouns are declined based on gender, number, and case. The word "δακτυλοι" is masculine and plural.

Description: "Δακτυλοι" refers to "fingers" in Greek. It is used to describe the five digits on the hand that are used for gripping, touching, and manipulating objects. Fingers play a crucial role in various activities such as writing, typing, playing musical instruments, and more.
