Etymology: The word "δακτυλον" (daktylon) comes from the Greek word for "finger" or "toe". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deḱ- meaning "finger" or "toe".

Grammar: In Greek, "δακτυλον" (daktylon) is a noun of neuter gender. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is "δακτυλα" (daktyla).

Description: "Δακτυλον" (daktylon) refers to a finger or a toe. It is a body part that is used for grasping, touching, and manipulating objects. Fingers and toes are essential for various activities such as writing, playing musical instruments, and performing delicate tasks.
