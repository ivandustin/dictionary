Etymology: The word "δακρυον" (dakryon) comes from the Greek word "δάκρυ" (dakry), meaning "tear". 

Grammar: In Greek, "δακρυον" is a neuter noun. It is the singular form, and the plural form is "δάκρυα" (dakrya). 

Description: "Δακρυον" refers to a tear, the salty liquid that is secreted by the lacrimal glands in the eyes. Tears are often associated with emotions such as sadness, joy, or pain, and they serve to lubricate and protect the eyes.
