Etymology: The word "δαμαλεως" (damaleōs) comes from the Greek verb "δαμάζω" (damazō), which means "to tame" or "to subdue". 

Grammar: "Δαμαλεως" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Δαμαλεως" refers to a person who tames or subdues something or someone. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has control or mastery over a particular skill or domain.
