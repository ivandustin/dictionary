Etymology: The word "δαρησεται" comes from the Greek verb "δαρέω" (daréo), which means "to beat" or "to strike". It is conjugated in the future tense, third person singular form.

Grammar: "δαρησεται" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated to indicate the tense, mood, voice, and person of the action. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person singular form refers to a singular subject that is not the speaker or the person being spoken to.

Description: "δαρησεται" means "he/she/it will be beaten" or "he/she/it will be struck" in English. It describes a future action of someone or something being physically hit or struck.
