Etymology: The word "δαιμονες" (daimones) comes from the ancient Greek language. It is derived from the word "δαίμων" (daimōn), which means "divine power" or "spirit." In ancient Greek mythology, daimones were considered to be supernatural beings or spirits that could be either good or evil.

Grammar: In Greek, "δαιμονες" is the plural form of the noun "δαίμων" (daimōn). It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun. In the singular form, it would be "δαίμων" (daimōn).

Description: In Greek mythology, daimones were believed to be intermediate beings between gods and humans. They were often associated with specific places, activities, or natural phenomena. Daimones could be protective or malevolent, and they were thought to have the power to influence human lives.
