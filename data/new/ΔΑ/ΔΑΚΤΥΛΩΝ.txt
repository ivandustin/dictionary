Etymology: The word "δακτυλων" (daktylon) comes from the Greek word "δάκτυλος" (daktylos), meaning "finger" or "toe".

Grammar: In Greek, "δακτυλων" is a noun in the genitive case. It is the plural form of "δάκτυλος" (daktylos), which means "finger" or "toe".

Description: "Δακτυλων" refers to multiple fingers or toes. It is commonly used to describe the digits on the hands or feet of humans and animals.
