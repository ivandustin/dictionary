Etymology: The word "δανισασθαι" comes from the Greek verb "δανίζω" (danízo), which means "to lend" or "to borrow". It is derived from the noun "δάνειον" (dáneion), meaning "loan" or "debt".

Grammar: "δανισασθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "δανίζω" (danízo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense generally refers to a past action, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description: "δανισασθαι" is an ancient Greek verb form that means "to lend" or "to borrow" in the aorist tense and middle voice. It is used to describe the action of lending or borrowing money or other items.
