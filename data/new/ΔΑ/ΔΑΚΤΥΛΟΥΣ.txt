Etymology: The word "δακτυλος" (daktylos) comes from the Greek language and means "finger". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dekm̥t, which also gave rise to the Latin word "digitus" and the English word "digit".

Grammar: In Greek, "δακτυλος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form (δακτυλος) and a plural form (δακτυλοι). It can be used in the nominative, accusative, genitive, dative, and vocative cases.

Description: "Δακτυλος" refers to the anatomical structure of the finger. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is finger-like or resembling a finger.
