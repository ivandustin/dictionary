The word "δαιμονιζόμενος" (daimonizomenos) is a Greek word that can be translated as "possessed by a demon" or "demon-possessed." It is derived from the Greek word "δαίμων" (daimon), which means "demon" or "spirit." In Greek mythology, daimons were supernatural beings that could be either good or evil. In the context of the New Testament, the word "δαιμονιζόμενος" is often used to describe individuals who were believed to be under the influence or control of evil spirits.
