Word: αζυμοις

Etymology: αζυμοις is a Greek word that comes from the combination of the prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the word "ζυμος" meaning "leavened". Therefore, αζυμοις means "unleavened".

Grammar: αζυμοις is a plural noun in the dative case in Greek. It is often used in the context of the Christian celebration of Passover, where unleavened bread is traditionally eaten.

Description: αζυμοις refers to bread that is made without yeast or any other leavening agent. It is often associated with Jewish and Christian religious traditions, where it is eaten during Passover and other religious holidays. Unleavened bread is also commonly used in various cuisines around the world, such as in Indian roti and Mexican tortillas.
