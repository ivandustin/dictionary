Word: αζυμα

Etymology: The word αζυμα comes from the Greek word α- (a-), which means "not," and ζύμη (zymē), which means "leavened dough." Therefore, αζυμα literally means "unleavened."

Grammar: αζυμα is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative singular case.

Description: αζυμα is a term used in the Greek Orthodox Church to refer to unleavened bread that is used during the Eucharist. It is made without yeast or any other leavening agent and is usually round and flat. The use of unleavened bread during the Eucharist is based on the biblical account of the Last Supper, where Jesus is said to have used unleavened bread. In Greek cuisine, αζυμα is also used to refer to a type of bread that is made without yeast and is similar to a flatbread.
