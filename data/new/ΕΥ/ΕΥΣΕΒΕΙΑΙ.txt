Etymology: The word "ευσεβεία" (eusebeia) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "σέβομαι" (sebomai), meaning "to revere" or "to worship." Therefore, "ευσεβεία" can be translated as "piety" or "devotion."

Grammar: "Ευσεβεία" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. In the plural form, it becomes "ευσεβείες" (eusebeies).

Description: "Ευσεβεία" refers to the quality or state of being pious or devout. It is often associated with religious devotion, reverence, and respect towards gods, deities, or higher powers. In a broader sense, it can also refer to a general sense of righteousness, moral uprightness, and dutifulness.
