Etymology: The word "ευεργεσίας" (euergēsías) comes from the Greek root words "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "εργεσία" (ergēsía), meaning "work" or "deed." Therefore, "ευεργεσίας" can be translated as "good work" or "benefaction."

Grammar: "ευεργεσίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "ευεργεσίας" refers to an act of doing good or performing a beneficial action. It can be used to describe acts of kindness, charity, or any positive action that brings benefits or improvements to others.
