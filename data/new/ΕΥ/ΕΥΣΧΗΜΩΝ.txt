Etymology: The word "ευσχημων" (euschēmōn) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "σχημα" (schēma), meaning "form" or "shape." 

Grammar: "Ευσχημων" is an adjective in the masculine form. It can also be used as a noun to refer to a person who is well-behaved or has good manners.

Description: "Ευσχημων" is used to describe someone or something that is well-mannered, decent, or proper in behavior and appearance. It implies a sense of dignity, grace, and respectability.
