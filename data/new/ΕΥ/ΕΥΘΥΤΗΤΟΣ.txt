Etymology: The word "ευθυτητος" (euthytetos) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "θυτης" (thytēs), meaning "arranged" or "ordered." 

Grammar: "Ευθυτητος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ευθυτητος" describes something that is well-arranged, well-ordered, or well-organized. It implies a sense of correctness, precision, or straightness.
