Etymology: The word "ευαγγελίζεσθαι" (pronounced: ev-angel-iz-esthai) comes from the Greek root words "ευ" (eu) meaning "good" and "αγγελία" (angelia) meaning "message" or "announcement". It is derived from the Greek verb "ευαγγελίζω" (ev-angel-izo) which means "to bring good news" or "to proclaim".

Grammar: "Ευαγγελίζεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "ευαγγελίζω" in Greek. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, it means "to be evangelized" or "to be proclaimed the good news".

Description: "Ευαγγελίζεσθαι" is a Greek word that is often used in religious contexts, particularly in Christianity, to refer to the act of spreading or proclaiming the good news of the Gospel. It signifies the sharing of the message of salvation and redemption.
