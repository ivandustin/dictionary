Etymology: The word "ευδοκησα" (eudokēsa) comes from the Greek verb "ευδοκέω" (eudokeō), which means "to be well pleased" or "to approve." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "ευ-" (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and the verb "δοκέω" (dokeō), meaning "to think" or "to seem."

Grammar: "Ευδοκησα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "ευδοκέω" (eudokeō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The first person singular form indicates that the action was performed by the speaker.

Description: "Ευδοκησα" is a Greek word that translates to "I was well pleased" or "I approved" in English. It expresses a state of being satisfied or content with something. The word can be used in various contexts, such as expressing approval of a decision, action, or outcome.
