Etymology: The word "ευλαβείς" (eulabeis) comes from the Greek root "ευ-" (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and the verb "λαμβάνω" (lambano), meaning "to take" or "to receive." The combination of these elements gives the word its meaning of "being cautious" or "taking care."

Grammar: "Ευλαβείς" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The singular form of this adjective is "ευλαβής" (eulabeis), and the feminine form is "ευλαβή" (eulabe).

Description: The word "ευλαβείς" is used to describe someone who is cautious, careful, or reverent. It implies a sense of respect and attentiveness towards others or towards a specific situation.
