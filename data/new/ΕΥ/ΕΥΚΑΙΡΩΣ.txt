Etymology: The word "ευκαιρως" (eukairos) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "καιρος" (kairos), meaning "opportunity" or "right moment." 

Grammar: "Ευκαιρως" is an adverb in Greek. It is the adverbial form of the adjective "ευκαιρος" (eukairos), which means "timely," "opportune," or "convenient." 

Description: "Ευκαιρως" is used to describe an action or event that happens at the right time or is well-timed. It implies that something is done or occurs when it is most advantageous or suitable.
