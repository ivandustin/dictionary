Etymology: The word "ευδοκησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "ευδοκέω" (eudokeo), which means "to be pleased" or "to approve." The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and refers to a group of people who have performed the action.

Grammar: "Ευδοκησαντες" is the third person plural form of the verb "ευδοκέω" in the aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The participle form "ευδοκησαντες" functions as an adjective, describing a group of people who have been pleased or have approved of something.

Description: "Ευδοκησαντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having been pleased" or "having approved." It is often used in a religious or formal context to describe a group of people who have expressed their approval or satisfaction with a particular event, decision, or action.
