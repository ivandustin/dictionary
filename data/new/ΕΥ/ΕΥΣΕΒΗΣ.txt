Etymology: The word "ευσέβης" (eusebes) comes from the combination of the Greek words "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "σέβομαι" (sebomai), meaning "to worship" or "to revere." Therefore, "ευσέβης" can be translated as "pious" or "devout."

Grammar: In Greek, "ευσέβης" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms depending on the context.

Description: "Ευσέβης" is a term used to describe someone who is deeply religious, devout, or pious. It refers to a person who shows reverence and respect towards the gods, religious practices, or moral principles. The term can also be used to describe someone who is dutiful, loyal, or respectful in general.
