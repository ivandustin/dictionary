Etymology: The word "ευσχημονας" (euschēmonas) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "σχημα" (schēma), meaning "form" or "shape." 

Grammar: "Ευσχημονας" is a noun in the masculine gender. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension. 

Description: "Ευσχημονας" is an ancient Greek word that can be translated as "well-formed" or "well-shaped." It is often used to describe someone who has a good physical appearance or someone who carries themselves with grace and dignity.
