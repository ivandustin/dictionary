Etymology: The word "ευγενείς" (eugenéis) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "γένος" (genos), meaning "race" or "birth." It refers to someone who is of noble or high birth.

Grammar: "Ευγενείς" is the plural form of the adjective "ευγενής" (eugenís), which means "noble" or "well-born" in Greek. It agrees in gender and number with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ευγενείς" is used to describe individuals who come from a distinguished or noble lineage. It can also be used more broadly to refer to people who possess qualities associated with nobility, such as refinement, grace, or good manners.
