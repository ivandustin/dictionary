Etymology: The word "ευτυχος" (eutychos) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu) meaning "good" and "τυχη" (tyche) meaning "luck" or "fortune". Therefore, "ευτυχος" can be translated as "fortunate" or "lucky".

Grammar: In Greek, "ευτυχος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it becomes "ευτυχος" (eutychos), if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it becomes "ευτυχης" (eutychis), and so on.

Description: "Ευτυχος" is used to describe someone or something that is fortunate, lucky, or happy. It can be used to express well-being, success, or positive circumstances.
