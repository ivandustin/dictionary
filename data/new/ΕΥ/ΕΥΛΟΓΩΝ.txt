Etymology: The word "ευλογων" (eulogon) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "λογος" (logos), meaning "word" or "speech." 

Grammar: "Ευλογων" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular number. 

Description: "Ευλογων" refers to someone who gives blessings or speaks well of others. It can also be used to describe a person who is praised or admired for their good qualities or actions.
