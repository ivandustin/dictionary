Etymology: The word "ευοδουσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "ευοδέω" (euodeo), which means "to prosper" or "to succeed". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "ευ-" (eu-), meaning "good" or "well", and the verb "οδέω" (odeo), meaning "to go" or "to travel".

Grammar: "ευοδουσθαι" is the present passive infinitive form of the verb "ευοδέω" in Greek. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description: "ευοδουσθαι" is used to express the idea of "to be prospered" or "to be successful" in Greek. It is often used in contexts related to achieving goals, accomplishing tasks, or attaining desired outcomes.
