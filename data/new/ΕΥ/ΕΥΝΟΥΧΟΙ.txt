Etymology: The word "ευνοχοι" (eunochoi) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu) meaning "good" and "νοχος" (nochos) meaning "to hold back" or "to restrain." 

Grammar: "Ευνοχοι" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Ευνοχοι" refers to eunuchs, which are individuals who have been castrated, typically males. In ancient times, eunuchs often served as servants or attendants in royal courts or religious institutions. They were known for their loyalty and ability to be trusted with sensitive tasks.
