Etymology: The word "ευτραπελία" (eutrapelia) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "τραπεζα" (trapeza), meaning "table" or "banquet." It originally referred to the skill of entertaining guests at a banquet or social gathering.

- Genitive: της ευτραπελίας (tis eutrapelias)
- Accusative: την ευτραπελία (tin eutrapelia)
- Vocative: ευτραπελία (eutrapelia)

Plural:
- Nominative: οι ευτραπελίες (i eutrapelies)
- Genitive: των ευτραπελιών (ton eutrapelion)
- Accusative: τις ευτραπελίες (tis eutrapelies)
- Vocative: ευτραπελίες (eutrapelies)

Description: "Ευτραπελία" is a Greek word that can be translated as "wittiness," "playfulness," or "humor." It refers to the ability to make clever and amusing remarks or to entertain others with lightheartedness and wit. It is often associated with a quick and sharp sense of humor.
