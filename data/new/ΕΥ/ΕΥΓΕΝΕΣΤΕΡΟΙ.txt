Etymology: The word "ευγενεστεροι" (eugenesteroi) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "γενεστεροι" (genesteroi), meaning "born" or "descendants." It literally translates to "well-born" or "noble."

Grammar: "Ευγενεστεροι" is the plural form of the adjective "ευγενής" (eugenis), which means "noble" or "well-born" in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who come from a distinguished or high-ranking lineage.

Description: "Ευγενεστεροι" refers to individuals who belong to a noble or aristocratic class. It implies a social status that is associated with privilege, refinement, and a prestigious lineage. The term is often used to describe families or individuals who have a long history of wealth, power, and influence.
