Etymology: The word "ευχρηστος" (euchrēstos) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "χρηστος" (chrēstos), meaning "useful" or "fit for use." 

Grammar: In Greek, "ευχρηστος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Ευχρηστος" is used to describe something that is easy to use, convenient, or user-friendly. It implies that the object or system is well-designed and efficient, making it simple and enjoyable for people to interact with.
