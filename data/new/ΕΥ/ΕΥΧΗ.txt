Etymology: The word "ευχή" (eukhí) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "εὔχομαι" (eúkhomai), which means "to pray" or "to wish." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ewk- ("to speak, to say").

Grammar: "Ευχή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "ευχής" (eukhís). In the plural form, it becomes "ευχές" (eukhés).

Description: "Ευχή" is commonly translated as "wish" or "prayer" in English. It refers to a desire or request for something, often expressed in a religious or spiritual context. It can also refer to a blessing or a vow. In Greek culture, making a wish or offering a prayer is often associated with expressing hope or seeking divine intervention.
