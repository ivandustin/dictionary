Etymology: The word "ευαγγέλιον" (evangelion) comes from the Greek words "ευ" (eu), meaning "good," and "αγγέλιον" (angelion), meaning "message" or "news." It originally referred to the "good news" or "gospel" of Christianity.

Grammar: "Ευαγγέλιον" is a neuter noun in Greek. It is the singular form, and its plural form is "ευαγγέλια" (evangelia). In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: "Ευαγγέλιον" is a term used in Christianity to refer to the four books of the New Testament that contain the life, teachings, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ. These books are known as the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to the message of salvation and the teachings of Jesus Christ.
