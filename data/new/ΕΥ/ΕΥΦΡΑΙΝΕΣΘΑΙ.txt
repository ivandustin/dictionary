Etymology: The word "ευφραίνεσθαι" (euphrainesthai) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu) meaning "well" or "good" and "φρήν" (phrēn) meaning "mind" or "heart". 

Grammar: "ευφραίνεσθαι" is a verb in the present middle/passive infinitive form. It is derived from the verb "ευφραίνω" (euphrainō), which means "to make happy" or "to rejoice". 

Description: "ευφραίνεσθαι" refers to the act of experiencing joy, happiness, or delight. It can also imply a sense of being uplifted or comforted.
