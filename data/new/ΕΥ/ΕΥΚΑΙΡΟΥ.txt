Etymology: The word "ευκαιρου" (eukairou) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "καιρός" (kairos), meaning "opportunity" or "right moment." 

Grammar: "Ευκαιρου" is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive.
