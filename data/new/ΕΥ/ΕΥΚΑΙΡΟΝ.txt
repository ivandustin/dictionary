Etymology: The word "ευκαιρον" comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "καιρος" (kairos), meaning "opportunity" or "right moment." 

Grammar: "Ευκαιρον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the neuter gender and singular number. 

Description: "Ευκαιρον" refers to a favorable or opportune moment, a time when conditions are right for something to happen or be done. It can also be used to describe a convenient or suitable occasion.
