Etymology: The word "ευθεία" (euthéia) comes from the Greek adjective "εὐθύς" (euthús), meaning "straight" or "direct."

Grammar: "Ευθεία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the first declension and has the nominative singular form "ευθεία."

Description: In Greek, "ευθεία" means "straight line" or "straight." It is used to describe a line that does not curve or bend. In mathematics, it is often used to refer to a line segment that extends infinitely in both directions.
