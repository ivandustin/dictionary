Etymology: The word "ευθυς" (euthys) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ewdʰ- meaning "straight" or "direct."

Grammar: In Greek, "ευθυς" (euthys) is an adjective. It is in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined to match different genders, numbers, and cases.

Description: The word "ευθυς" (euthys) means "straight" or "direct" in Greek. It is often used to describe something that is not curved or bent. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person or their behavior as honest, straightforward, or sincere.
