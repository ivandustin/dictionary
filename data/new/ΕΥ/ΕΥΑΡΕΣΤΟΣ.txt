Etymology: The word "ευαρεστος" (euaresos) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu) meaning "good" and "αρεστος" (arestos) meaning "pleasing" or "agreeable". 

Grammar: "Ευαρεστος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, in the feminine form it becomes "ευαρεστη" (euaresi), and in the neuter form it becomes "ευαρεστο" (euaresi).

Description: "Ευαρεστος" is used to describe something or someone that is pleasing, agreeable, or enjoyable. It can be used to express satisfaction or approval.
