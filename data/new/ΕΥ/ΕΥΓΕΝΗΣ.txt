Etymology: The word "ευγενής" (eugenēs) comes from the Greek roots "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "γένος" (genos), meaning "race" or "birth." Therefore, "ευγενής" can be translated as "of noble birth" or "well-born."

Grammar: In Greek, "ευγενής" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. For example, "ευγενής άνδρας" (eugenēs andras) means "noble man," while "ευγενής γυναίκα" (eugenēs gynaika) means "noble woman."

Description: The word "ευγενής" is used to describe someone who comes from a distinguished or high-ranking family. It implies qualities such as nobility, refinement, and good breeding. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who possesses admirable qualities or behaves in an honorable manner.
