Etymology: The word "ευλαβηθείς" (eulabētheis) comes from the Greek verb "ευλαβέομαι" (eulabeomai), which means "to be cautious" or "to take care." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "ευ-" (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and the verb "λαβέομαι" (labeomai), meaning "to take" or "to seize."

Grammar: "Ευλαβηθείς" is the aorist participle form of the verb "ευλαβέομαι" (eulabeomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The participle form indicates that the action of being cautious or taking care has been completed.

Description: "Ευλαβηθείς" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having been cautious" or "having taken care." It describes someone who has acted with caution or care in the past. This word emphasizes the importance of being careful and attentive in one's actions.
