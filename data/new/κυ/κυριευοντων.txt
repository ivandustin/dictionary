Word: κυριευοντων

Etymology:
The word κυριευοντων is derived from the Greek verb κυριεύω (kyrieuō), which means "to rule" or "to dominate". The suffix -οντων (-ontōn) is the present participle form of the verb, indicating that the action of ruling or dominating is currently taking place.

Grammar:
κυριευοντων is a present participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, the genitive plural form indicates that the ruling or dominating is being done by multiple individuals or entities.

Description:
κυριευοντων can be translated to mean "of those who are ruling" or "of those who are dominating". It is often used in political or social contexts to describe those who hold power or authority over others. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any situation where one entity has control or influence over another.
