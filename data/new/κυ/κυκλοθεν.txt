Word: κυκλοθεν

Etymology:
The word κυκλοθεν is derived from two Greek words: κύκλος (kuklos) meaning "circle" and θέν (then) meaning "from". Therefore, κυκλοθεν means "from a circle" or "circularly".

Grammar:
κυκλοθεν is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe the manner in which something is done or the origin of something.

Description:
κυκλοθεν is a Greek adverb that is used to describe something that is done in a circular manner or originates from a circle. It can be used in various contexts, such as in mathematics, where it can describe a circular motion or a circular path. It can also be used in everyday language to describe something that is done in a circular way, such as walking around in circles or driving in circles.
