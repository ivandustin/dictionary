Word: κυλισμόν

Etymology:
The word κυλισμόν (kyllismón) is derived from the Greek verb κυλίω (kylió), which means "to roll" or "to tumble." The suffix -σμόν (-smón) is a nominalizing suffix that is used to form nouns from verbs. Therefore, κυλισμόν (kyllismón) can be translated as "a rolling" or "a tumbling."

Grammar:
In Greek, κυλισμόν (kyllismón) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the accusative case, which means that it is the direct object of a verb. The nominative form of the word is κυλισμός (kyllismós).

Description:
κυλισμόν (kyllismón) refers to the act of rolling or tumbling. It can be used to describe the movement of objects, such as rocks or balls, as well as the movement of people or animals. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the movement of ideas or events. For example, one might say that a political campaign gained momentum and began to "roll" towards victory.
