Word: κυναριοις

Etymology: The word κυναριοις is derived from the Greek word κυνάριον (kynarion), which means "little dog". It is a diminutive form of the word κύων (kyon), which means "dog".

Grammar: κυναριοις is a noun in the dative plural form in the Greek language. It is used to refer to "little dogs" or "puppies".

Description: κυναριοις is a term used in ancient Greek literature to describe small dogs or puppies. It is often used in a playful or affectionate manner to refer to these animals. In some contexts, it may also be used to describe someone who is small or insignificant.
