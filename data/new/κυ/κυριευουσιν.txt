Word: κυριευουσιν

Etymology: κυριευουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κυριεύω (kyrieuō), which means "to rule" or "to dominate". The verb form κυριευουσιν is in the present tense and third person plural.

Grammar: κυριευουσιν is a present tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated from the root word κυριεύω (kyrieuō) and the present tense ending -ουσιν (-ousin), which is used for the third person plural. The verb means "they rule" or "they dominate".

Description: κυριευουσιν is a Greek verb that describes the action of ruling or dominating. It is often used in a political or social context to describe those who hold power or authority over others. The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the action is currently happening.
