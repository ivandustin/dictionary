Word: κυριότητος

Etymology:
The word κυριότητος (kyriotitos) comes from the Greek word κύριος (kyrios), which means "lord" or "master". The suffix -τητος (-titos) is a nominalizing suffix that is used to form abstract nouns from adjectives or verbs. Therefore, κυριότητος can be translated as "lordship" or "mastery".

Grammar:
κυριότητος is a noun in the singular form. It is a feminine noun, which means that it takes feminine articles and adjectives. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case. For example, the genitive case of κυριότητος is κυριότητας (kyriotitas).

Description:
κυριότητος refers to the state or quality of being a lord or master. It can be used to describe someone who has authority or control over others, or to describe a particular domain or area of expertise in which someone has mastery. The word is often used in a religious context to refer to the lordship of God or Jesus Christ.
