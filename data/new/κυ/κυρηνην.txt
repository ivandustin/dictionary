The word "κυρηνην" (kyreneen) is a Greek noun that refers to a person or thing from the ancient city of Cyrene, which was located in modern-day Libya. 

Etymology: The word "κυρηνην" comes from the Greek word "Κυρήνη" (Kyrene), which was the name of the city in ancient times.

Grammar: "κυρηνην" is a singular noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: The word "κυρηνην" can be used to refer to a person or thing that is from or associated with the city of Cyrene. For example, one could say "Ο Πυθαγόρας ήταν κυρηνηνός" (O Pythagoras itan kyreneenos), which means "Pythagoras was from Cyrene."
