Word: κυριότητες

Etymology: The word κυριότητες comes from the Greek word κύριος (kyrios) which means "lord" or "master". The suffix -τητες (-tites) is used to form abstract nouns, so κυριότητες means "lordship" or "dominion".

Grammar: κυριότητες is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative case form, which means it is the subject of a sentence. In English, it can be translated as "dominions" or "lordships".

Description: κυριότητες refers to the concept of having power or authority over something or someone. It can be used to describe a person's dominion over a territory or a ruler's authority over their subjects. In modern Greek, it is also used to refer to property ownership or possession.
