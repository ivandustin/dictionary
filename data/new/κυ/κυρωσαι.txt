Word: κυρωσαι

Etymology: κυρωσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κυρόω (kyroo), which means "to confirm" or "to ratify." It is derived from the noun κύρος (kyros), which means "authority" or "power."

Grammar: κυρωσαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb κυρόω. In English, it would be translated as "ratify" or "confirm."

Description: κυρωσαι is a command or request to confirm or ratify something. It is often used in legal or political contexts to refer to the process of approving or validating a decision or agreement.
