The word "κυλλους" (kyllous) is a Greek noun that means "lame" or "crippled". It is derived from the Greek verb "κυλλοω" (kyllóō), which means "to make crooked" or "to cripple". In terms of grammar, "κυλλους" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive "κυλλου" (kyllou) or the accusative "κυλλον" (kyllon).
