Word: κυριοι

Etymology:
The word κυριοι (kyrioi) is a plural form of the Greek word κύριος (kyrios), which means "lord" or "master". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *keuə-, which means "to swell" or "to be strong".

Grammar:
In Greek, κυριοι is the nominative plural form of κύριος. It is used to refer to multiple lords or masters. The word is masculine in gender.

Description:
κυριοι is a term used in Greek to address or refer to multiple lords or masters. It can be used in both formal and informal settings. In ancient times, it was commonly used to address people of high social status or authority. Today, it is still used in religious contexts to refer to God or Jesus Christ as the ultimate lord or master.
