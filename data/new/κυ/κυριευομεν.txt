Word: κυριευομεν

Etymology:
The word κυριευομεν (kyrieuomen) is a present participle form of the Greek verb κυριεύω (kyrieuo), which means "to dominate" or "to rule over". The verb is derived from the noun κύριος (kyrios), which means "lord" or "master".

Grammar:
κυριευομεν is a present participle form of the verb κυριεύω (kyrieuo), which is in the present tense and active voice. The participle form indicates that the action of dominating or ruling over is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
The word κυριευομεν (kyrieuomen) describes the act of dominating or ruling over something or someone. It can be used to describe a person or group who has control or authority over others, or it can be used to describe a situation where one thing is overpowering or dominating another. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a strong emotion or feeling that is dominating or controlling a person's thoughts or actions.
