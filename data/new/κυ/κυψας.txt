Etymology: 
The word κυψας (kypsas) is derived from the Greek verb κύπτω (kypto), which means "to bend down" or "to stoop". 

Grammar: 
κυψας is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. 

Description: 
κυψας is translated to "stooped" or "bent down" in English. It is often used to describe a physical action, such as bending down to pick something up. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is humble or submissive.
