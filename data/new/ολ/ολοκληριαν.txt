Word: ολοκληριαν

Etymology: The word ολοκληριαν comes from the Greek word ολοκληρία (olokliría), which means completeness or entirety.

Grammar: ολοκληριαν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender.

Description: ολοκληριαν refers to the act of completing or finishing something in its entirety. It can also refer to the state of being complete or whole. The word is often used in legal or financial contexts to refer to the full payment or settlement of a debt or obligation.
