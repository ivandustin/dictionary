Word: ολοτελείς

Etymology: The word ολοτελείς comes from the Greek words ολος (olos) meaning "whole" and τελείς (teleis) meaning "complete" or "perfect". 

Grammar: ολοτελείς is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe something that is complete or perfect in every way.

Description: ολοτελείς is a word that is often used to describe a work of art or a piece of literature that is considered to be flawless or perfect. It can also be used to describe a person who is considered to be complete or perfect in their abilities or character.
