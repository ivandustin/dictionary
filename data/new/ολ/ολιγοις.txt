Word: ολιγοις

Etymology: ολιγοις is a Greek word that comes from the adjective ολίγος (oligos), meaning "few" or "little".

Grammar: ολιγοις is a plural form of the noun ολίγος (oligos), which means "few" or "little". It is a masculine noun in the third declension, and its nominative plural form is ολιγοις.

Description: ολιγοις is used to describe a small number of people or things. It can also be used to describe a small amount of something. For example, "ολιγοις άνθρωποι ήρθαν στο πάρτι" (oligis anthropoi irthan sto parti) means "few people came to the party".
