Word: ολους

Etymology: ολους is a Greek word that comes from the root word ολος (olos), which means "whole" or "entire". 

Grammar: ολους is the accusative plural form of the Greek adjective ολος (olos), which means "whole" or "entire". It is used to describe a group of people or things as a whole. 

Description: ολους is commonly used in Greek to refer to a group of people or things as a whole. For example, "Ολους οι φίλοι μου" (olous oi filoi mou) means "All my friends" in English. It is often used in combination with other words to describe a specific group, such as "ολους οι Έλληνες" (olous oi Ellines), which means "All Greeks".
