Word: ολοκληρον

Etymology: The word ολοκληρον comes from the Greek root words ολος (holos) meaning "whole" and κληρος (kleros) meaning "lot" or "inheritance". 

Grammar: ολοκληρον is a noun in the neuter gender. It is singular and in the accusative case. 

Description: ολοκληρον refers to something that is complete or whole. It can also refer to a sum of money or property that is inherited. In modern Greek, it is often used to mean "entirely" or "completely".
