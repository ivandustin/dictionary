Word: ολιγωρει

Etymology: Ολιγωρεί is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: "ολίγος" (oligos) meaning "few" or "little" and "ὁράω" (horaō) meaning "to see". Therefore, ολιγωρεί means "to see little" or "to pay little attention".

Grammar: Ολιγωρεί is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in "-ω".

Description: Ολιγωρεί is a verb that describes the act of paying little attention or neglecting something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, work, or politics. For example, one can say "Ο φίλος μου ολιγωρεί στις υποχρεώσεις του" (My friend neglects his responsibilities) or "Οι πολιτικοί ολιγωρούν στα προβλήματα της χώρας" (Politicians pay little attention to the country's problems).
