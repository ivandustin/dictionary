Word: ολιγοπιστοι

Etymology:
The word ολιγοπιστοι (oligopistoi) is a Greek word that is derived from two words: ολίγος (oligos) meaning "few" and πιστεύω (pistevo) meaning "believe". Therefore, ολιγοπιστοι means "those who believe little" or "the faithless".

Grammar:
Ολιγοπιστοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -οι (-oi) to the stem ολιγοπιστ- (oligopist-). The word is declined according to the second declension in Greek.

Description:
Ολιγοπιστοι is a term used to describe people who have little faith or trust in something or someone. It can refer to individuals who are skeptical or doubtful about religious beliefs, political ideologies, or even personal relationships. The term can also be used to describe someone who is unreliable or untrustworthy.
