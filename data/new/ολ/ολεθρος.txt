Word: ολεθρος

Etymology:
The word ολεθρος (olethros) comes from the Greek root word ὄλεθρος (ólethros), which means "destruction" or "ruin". It is derived from the verb ὄλλυμι (óllumi), which means "to destroy" or "to ruin".

Description:
Ολεθρος is a Greek word that refers to complete destruction or ruin. It can be used to describe the devastation caused by natural disasters, wars, or other catastrophic events. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the destruction of a person's reputation or the downfall of a political regime. The word carries a sense of finality and irreversibility, suggesting that the destruction is complete and irreversible.
