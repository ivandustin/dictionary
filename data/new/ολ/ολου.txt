Word: ολου

Etymology: ολου is a Greek word that comes from the root word ολος (olos), which means "whole" or "entire". 

Grammar: ολου is a genitive singular form of the Greek adjective ολος (olos), which means "whole" or "entire". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns. 

Description: ολου can be translated to English as "of the whole" or "of the entire". It is often used in Greek phrases and expressions to indicate completeness or totality. For example, ολου του κοσμου (olou tou kosmou) means "of the whole world".
