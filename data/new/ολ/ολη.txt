Word: ολη

Etymology:
The word ολη (olē) is derived from the Greek adjective ὅλος (holos), which means "whole" or "complete". 

Grammar:
Ολη is a feminine singular form of the adjective ὅλος (holos). It is used to describe a feminine singular noun, indicating that it is complete or whole. 

Description:
Ολη is a Greek adjective that is commonly used to describe a feminine singular noun. It can be translated to English as "whole", "complete", or "entire". For example, ολη η πόλη (olē i póli) means "the whole city" or "the entire city". It can also be used to describe a person, such as ολη η οικογένεια (olē i oikogéneia), which means "the whole family".
