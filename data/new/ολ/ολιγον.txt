Word: ολιγον

Etymology:
The word ολιγον (oligon) is derived from the Greek word "ὀλίγος" (oligos), which means "few" or "little". It is a combination of the prefix "ὀλιγ-" (olig-) meaning "few" and the suffix "-ον" (-on) which is used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
In Greek, ολιγον (oligon) is a neuter singular adjective. It is used to describe a noun that is masculine, feminine, or neuter and is in the nominative, accusative, or vocative case. For example, ολιγον κρασί (oligon krasí) means "a little wine".

Description:
Ολιγον (oligon) is a Greek word that means "little" or "few". It is often used to describe a small amount of something, such as a little bit of food or a few coins. It can also be used to describe a small number of people or things. In modern Greek, it is often used as an adverb to mean "slightly" or "a little".
