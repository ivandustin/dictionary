Etymology: The word "ορκωτής" (orkotis) comes from the Greek verb "ορκίζω" (orkizo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath". It is derived from the noun "όρκος" (orkos), meaning "oath".

Grammar: "Ορκωτής" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and its plural form is "ορκωτές" (orkotes).

Description: "Ορκωτής" refers to a person who has taken an oath or made a solemn promise. It can also be used to describe someone who is bound by an oath or is obligated to fulfill a promise.
