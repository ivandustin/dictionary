Etymology: The word "ὁργής" (orgēs) comes from the Greek verb "ὀργάω" (orgaō), which means "to be angry" or "to be enraged."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁργής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension and has a stem ending in -η.

Description: "ὁργής" refers to anger or wrath. It is often used to describe intense and passionate anger, typically associated with a strong emotional response to a perceived injustice or offense.
