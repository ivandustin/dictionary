Etymology: The word "ὁργή" (orgē) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "ὀργάω" (orgaō), which means "to be angry" or "to be enraged."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁργή" is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows:

- Singular: ἡ ὀργή (hē orgē) - the anger
- Plural: αἱ ὀργαί (hai orgai) - the angers

Description: "ὁργή" refers to the intense feeling of anger or rage. It can be used to describe a strong emotional reaction to a perceived injustice, offense, or provocation. In Greek mythology, the gods were often depicted as experiencing "ὁργή" when they were angered by mortals or other gods.
