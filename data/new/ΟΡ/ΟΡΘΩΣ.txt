Etymology: The word "ὁρθως" (orthos) comes from the Greek root "ὀρθός" (orthos), meaning "straight" or "correct."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁρθως" (orthos) is an adverb that means "correctly" or "rightly." It is used to describe the manner in which an action is performed.

Description: "ὁρθως" (orthos) is a word that emphasizes the idea of doing something in a correct or proper manner. It implies accuracy, precision, and adherence to established standards or principles.
