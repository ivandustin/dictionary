Etymology: The word "οργιλον" comes from the Greek word "οργή" (orgi), meaning anger or wrath.

Grammar: In Greek, "οργιλον" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can also be declined to match different genders, numbers, and cases.

Description: "Οργιλον" is used to describe someone or something that is angry or wrathful. It denotes a strong feeling of anger or intense rage.
