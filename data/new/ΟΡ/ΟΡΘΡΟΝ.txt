Etymology: The word "ορθρον" (orthron) comes from the Greek word "ορθρος" (orthros), meaning "dawn" or "daybreak". 

Grammar: "Ορθρον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is classified as a masculine noun. 

Description: "Ορθρον" refers to the period of time just before sunrise, commonly known as dawn or daybreak. It is the moment when the sky starts to lighten up and the first light of the day appears.
