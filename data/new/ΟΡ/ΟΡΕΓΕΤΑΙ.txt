Etymology: The word "ὁρεγεται" (oregetai) comes from the Greek verb "ὁρέγομαι" (oregomai), which means "to desire" or "to long for."

Grammar: "ὁρεγεται" is a present middle/passive indicative form of the verb "ὁρέγομαι" in the third person singular. It indicates that the subject desires or longs for something in the present.

Description: "ὁρεγεται" is a word used in ancient Greek to express the act of desiring or longing for something. It can refer to both physical desires and aspirations. The word implies a strong and intense longing or craving for something.
