Etymology: The word "ορκωμοσίας" comes from the Greek word "ορκομοσία" (orkomosía), which is derived from the combination of "όρκος" (órkos) meaning "oath" and "μοσία" (mosía) meaning "act of swearing".

Grammar: "Ορκωμοσίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Ορκωμοσίας" refers to the act of taking an oath or making a solemn declaration. It is commonly used in legal and official contexts, such as in courtrooms or during the inauguration of public officials.
