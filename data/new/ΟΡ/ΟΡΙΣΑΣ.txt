Etymology: The word "ορισας" comes from the Greek verb "ορίζω" (orizo), which means "to define" or "to determine". 

Grammar: "Ορισας" is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "ορίζω" (orizo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Ορισας" is a word that can be translated as "you defined" or "you determined" in English. It is used to refer to the act of setting boundaries, establishing limits, or making a decision.
