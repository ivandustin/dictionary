Etymology: The word "ὁρθός" (orthos) comes from the Greek language and means "straight" or "correct". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃er- which means "to set in order" or "to straighten".

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁρθός" (orthos) is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "ὁρθός" (orthos) is used to describe something that is straight, upright, or correct. It can refer to physical objects that are not bent or twisted, as well as abstract concepts that are accurate or true. In a figurative sense, it can also describe a person who is morally upright or honest.
