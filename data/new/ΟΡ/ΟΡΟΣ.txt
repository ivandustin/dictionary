Etymology: The word "ορος" (oros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂er- meaning "to rise, to be high". 

Grammar: In Greek, "ορος" (oros) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and can also be declined in other cases such as genitive (ορου), dative (ορῳ), accusative (ορον), and vocative (ορε).

Description: "Ορος" (oros) means "mountain" in Greek. It refers to a large landform that rises above the surrounding land, typically with steep sides and a peak. Mountains are an important part of the natural landscape and can have significant cultural, ecological, and geological importance.
