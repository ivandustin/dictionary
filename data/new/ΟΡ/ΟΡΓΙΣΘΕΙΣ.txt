Etymology: The word "ὁργισθεῖς" (orgistheis) comes from the Greek verb "ὀργίζω" (orgizo), which means "to be angry" or "to be enraged."

Grammar: "ὁργισθεῖς" is the second person singular form of the verb "ὀργίζω" in the aorist tense. It is used to address someone directly and means "you were angry" or "you became enraged."

Description: "ὁργισθεῖς" is a word used in ancient Greek literature to describe someone's state of anger or rage. It implies a sudden or intense outburst of anger.
