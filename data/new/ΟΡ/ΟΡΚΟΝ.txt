Etymology: The word "ορκον" (orkon) comes from the Greek word "ορκος" (orkos), which means "oath" or "sworn statement". 

Grammar: "Ορκον" (orkon) is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ορκον" (orkon) refers to an oath or a sworn statement. It is a formal declaration made by a person, often in a legal or religious context, in which they pledge to tell the truth or fulfill a promise. Oaths are commonly used in courts, during ceremonies, or in official documents to ensure the credibility and commitment of the person making the oath.
