Etymology: The word "ορφανος" (orphanos) comes from the Greek word "ορφανός" which means "bereaved" or "deprived of parents". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃erbʰ- which means "to be or become orphaned".

Grammar: In Greek, "ορφανος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases such as genitive (ορφανού), accusative (ορφανό), and vocative (ορφανέ).

Description: "Ορφανος" refers to a person, usually a child, who has lost one or both parents. It is commonly used to describe someone who is without the care, support, or protection of parents. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is lacking or deprived of something essential.
