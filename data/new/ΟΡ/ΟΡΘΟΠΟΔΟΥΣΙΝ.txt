Etymology: The word "ορθοποδουσιν" comes from the Greek roots "ορθος" (orthos) meaning "straight" or "correct" and "ποδος" (podos) meaning "foot". 

Grammar: "ορθοποδουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb "ορθοποδεω" (orthopodeo), which means "to walk straight" or "to walk correctly". 

Description: "ορθοποδουσιν" describes the action of walking straight or walking correctly. It implies a proper and balanced way of walking, without any deviation or irregularity.
