Etymology: The word "ορατα" comes from the Greek word "ορατός" (oratos), which means "visible" or "able to be seen".

Grammar: "Ορατα" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the neuter gender and is in the nominative case.

Description: "Ορατα" refers to things that are visible or can be seen. It can be used to describe objects, phenomena, or any other entities that are perceptible to the human eye.
