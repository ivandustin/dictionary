Etymology: The word "όρκος" (orkos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ὅρκος" (horkos), which means "oath" or "sworn statement".

Grammar: In Greek, "όρκος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Όρκος" refers to a solemn or formal promise or declaration made under oath. It is often used in legal or official contexts, where someone swears to tell the truth or fulfill a certain obligation.
