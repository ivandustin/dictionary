Etymology: The word "ὀρωντες" (orontes) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ὀράω" (orao), which means "to see" or "to behold."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὀρωντες" is the plural form of the noun "ὀρωντής" (orontes). It belongs to the third declension and is of masculine gender.

Description: "ὀρωντες" refers to a group of people who have the ability to see or behold. It can also be used metaphorically to describe individuals who possess insight or understanding.
