Etymology: The word "ορνις" (ornis) comes from the Greek language and means "bird".

Grammar: In Greek, "ορνις" is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "ορνιθος" (ornithos).

Description: "Ορνις" refers to a bird, which is a warm-blooded vertebrate animal characterized by feathers, beak, and the ability to fly. Birds are found in various habitats worldwide and exhibit a wide range of sizes, shapes, and colors. They are known for their ability to produce melodious sounds and are often admired for their beauty and grace. Birds play important roles in ecosystems as pollinators, seed dispersers, and indicators of environmental health.
