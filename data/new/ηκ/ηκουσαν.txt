Word: ηκουσαν

Etymology: ηκουσαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The verb comes from the ancient Greek word "ἀκούω" (akouo), which has the same meaning.

Grammar: ηκουσαν is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood of the verb ακούω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: ηκουσαν can be translated to English as "they heard". It describes a past action where a group of people listened to something. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly they heard.
