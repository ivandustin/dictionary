Word: ηκουσεν

Etymology: ηκουσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: ηκουσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: ηκουσεν means "he/she/it heard" in Greek. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe someone hearing the word of God or a message from a prophet. It can also be used in everyday conversation to describe someone hearing any kind of sound or message.
