Word: ηκουεν

Etymology: ηκουεν is the aorist tense of the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The verb comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ḱews-.

Grammar: ηκουεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb ακούω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: ηκουεν can be translated to English as "he/she/it heard" or "he/she/it listened". It is a simple past tense form that describes a completed action of hearing or listening in the past.
