Word: ηκουσας

Etymology:
The word ηκουσας (ekousas) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix "ε-" (e-) in front of the root word indicates the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
ηκουσας (ekousas) is a second-person singular form of the aorist tense in the indicative mood. It is used to describe a completed action in the past that was performed by the person being addressed. In English, it can be translated as "you heard".

Description:
ηκουσας (ekousas) is a verb that describes the act of hearing or listening to something in the past. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of hearing the word of God. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any act of listening or hearing in the past.
