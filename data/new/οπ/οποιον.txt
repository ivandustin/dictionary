Word: οποιον

Etymology: οποιον is a Greek word that comes from the combination of the words "οποιος" (which means "whoever") and "ν" (which is a particle that indicates negation). Therefore, οποιον means "whoever not".

Grammar: οποιον is a pronoun in the Greek language that is used to express negation and uncertainty. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it refers to. For example, in the nominative case, it can be οποιον (masculine), οποιαν (feminine), or οποιο (neuter).

Description: οποιον is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to express negation and uncertainty. It is often used in combination with other words to form complex phrases and sentences. For example, "οποιον και να είναι" (whoever it may be) or "δεν ξέρω οποιον να επιλέξω" (I don't know whom to choose).
