Word: οπτανομενος

Etymology: The word οπτανομενος comes from the Greek verb οπτάνομαι (optanomai), which means "to see" or "to observe". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: οπτανομενος is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb οπτάνομαι (optanomai), which is in the middle voice. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: οπτανομενος describes someone who is currently observing or watching something. It can also be translated as "being seen" or "being watched". The word is often used in a passive sense, as in "being observed by someone or something".
