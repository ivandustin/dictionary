Etymology: οπως is a Greek word that comes from the combination of the preposition "όπως" (meaning "as, like") and the adverb "ήδη" (meaning "already"). 

Grammar: οπως is a conjunction that is used to introduce a subordinate clause expressing a comparison or a manner in which something is done. It is often translated as "as" or "like" in English. 

Description: οπως is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to express comparisons and manners. It is often used in everyday speech and writing, and is an important part of the Greek language.
