Word: δρασσόμενος

Etymology: δρασσόμενος comes from the Greek verb δράσκω (drasko), which means "to do, to act, to accomplish." The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: δρασσόμενος is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb δράσκω (drasko), which is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood.

Description: δρασσόμενος describes someone who is currently doing or accomplishing something. It can be translated as "doing," "acting," or "accomplishing." The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in literature, philosophy, or everyday conversation.
