Etymology: The word "ζαχαριας" (zacharias) is derived from the Hebrew name "Zechariah" which means "Yahweh has remembered" or "God has remembered". It is a biblical name that appears in both the Old Testament and the New Testament.

Grammar: In Greek, "ζαχαριας" (zacharias) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is declined as follows:
- Singular: ζαχαριας (zacharias)
- Plural: ζαχαριαδες (zachariades)

Description: Zacharias is a name that is commonly used in Greek-speaking countries. It is often given to boys and has biblical origins. In the New Testament, Zacharias is the name of the father of John the Baptist. The name carries a sense of divine remembrance and is associated with faith and devotion.
