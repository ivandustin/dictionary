Etymology: The word "κυρηναίων" comes from the Greek word "Κυρηναῖος" (Kyrenaîos), which means "of Cyrene." Cyrene was an ancient Greek city located in present-day Libya.

Grammar: "κυρηναίων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "Κυρηναῖος" (Kyrenaîos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "κυρηναίων" refers to multiple things that belong to or are associated with the people of Cyrene. It can be used to describe various aspects of the culture, history, or characteristics of the people from Cyrene.
