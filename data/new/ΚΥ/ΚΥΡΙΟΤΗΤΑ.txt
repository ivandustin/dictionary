Etymology: The word "κυριότητα" comes from the Greek word "κύριος" (kyrios), meaning "lord" or "master", and the suffix "-τητα" (-tita), which denotes the state or quality of being. Therefore, "κυριότητα" can be translated as "lordship" or "mastery".

Grammar: In Greek, "κυριότητα" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a noun of quality.

Description: "Κυριότητα" refers to the state or quality of being a lord or master. It can also be used to describe the authority, control, or ownership that someone has over something.
