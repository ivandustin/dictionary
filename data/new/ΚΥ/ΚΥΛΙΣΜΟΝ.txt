Etymology: The word "κυλισμόν" (kylismon) comes from the Greek verb "κυλίω" (kylío), which means "to roll" or "to turn around." It is derived from the noun "κύλισις" (kýlisis), meaning "rolling" or "rotation."

Grammar: "Κυλισμόν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender in Greek. It belongs to the second declension.

Description: "Κυλισμόν" refers to the act of rolling or the process of turning something around. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a change or transformation.
