Etymology: The word "κυρηναῖος" (kyrenaîos) comes from the Greek city of Cyrene, which was located in present-day Libya. The term was used to refer to someone from Cyrene or something related to the city.

Grammar: In Greek, "κυρηναῖος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Κυρηναῖος" is used to describe someone or something that is from or related to the city of Cyrene. It can also refer to the ancient Greek dialect spoken in Cyrene.
