Etymology: The word "κυψας" (kypas) comes from the Greek verb "κύπτω" (kypto), which means "to bend" or "to stoop". 

Grammar: "Κυψας" is the aorist participle form of the verb "κύπτω" (kypto), which means "having bent" or "having stooped". It is used to describe an action that has been completed in the past.

Description: "Κυψας" is a Greek word that refers to the act of bending or stooping. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone physically bending down or figuratively referring to someone humbling themselves or showing respect.
