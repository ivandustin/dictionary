Etymology: The word "κυμινον" (kuminon) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "kuminon" which means "cumin".

Grammar: In Greek, "κυμινον" (kuminon) is a noun. It is a neuter noun of the second declension.

Description: "κυμινον" (kuminon) refers to the spice known as cumin. Cumin is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is widely used as a spice in cooking. It has a warm, earthy, and slightly bitter taste, and is commonly used in various cuisines around the world. Cumin is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in spice blends, curry powders, and other savory dishes.
