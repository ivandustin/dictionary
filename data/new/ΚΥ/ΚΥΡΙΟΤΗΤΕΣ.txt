Etymology: The word "κυριότητες" comes from the Greek word "κύριος" (kyrios), meaning "lord" or "master", and the suffix "-τητες" (-tites), which is used to form abstract nouns indicating a state or quality.

Grammar: "κυριότητες" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is feminine in gender.

Description: "κυριότητες" refers to the plural form of the Greek word for "possessions" or "properties". It is used to describe the things that someone owns or has control over.
