Etymology: The word "κυβερνητης" (kybernētēs) comes from the Greek verb "κυβερνάω" (kybernáō), which means "to steer" or "to govern." It originally referred to a helmsman or a pilot who steered a ship.

Grammar: "Κυβερνητης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number.

Description: In ancient Greece, a "κυβερνητης" (kybernētēs) was a person who had the responsibility of steering and guiding a ship. Over time, the term evolved and came to be used metaphorically to refer to someone who governs or leads a group or organization. In modern usage, "κυβερνητης" can be translated as "governor" or "leader."
