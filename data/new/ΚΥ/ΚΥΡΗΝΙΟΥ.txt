Etymology: The word "κυρηνίου" (kyrēniou) comes from the Greek word "Κυρήνη" (Kyrenē), which was the name of an ancient Greek city in present-day Libya.

Grammar: "κυρηνίου" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "κυρηνίου" refers to something that is related to or originates from Kyrenē, the ancient Greek city in Libya.
