Etymology: The word "κυκλοθεν" comes from the Greek words "κύκλος" (kyklos) meaning "circle" and "θέν" (then) meaning "around". 

Grammar: "κυκλοθεν" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate movement or action in a circular or revolving manner.

Description: "κυκλοθεν" is used to describe something that is happening or moving in a circular or revolving pattern. It can be used to describe physical movement, such as walking in circles, or metaphorical movement, such as going around in circles in a conversation or thought process.
