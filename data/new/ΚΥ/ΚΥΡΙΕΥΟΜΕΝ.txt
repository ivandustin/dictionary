Etymology: The word "κυριευόμενος" comes from the Greek verb "κυριεύω" (kyrievo), which means "to dominate" or "to rule over." The suffix "-μενος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice, so "κυριευόμενος" can be translated as "being dominated" or "being ruled over."

Grammar: "κυριευόμενος" is a masculine singular participle in the present tense, middle voice, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb "κυριεύω" (kyrievo), which is a first conjugation verb. The participle form indicates that the subject is being acted upon or affected by the verb.

Description: "κυριευόμενος" describes something or someone that is being dominated, ruled over, or controlled by another entity. It implies a state of subjugation or subordination.
