Etymology: The word "κυρώσαι" comes from the Greek verb "κυρώνω" (kyrónō), which means "to ratify" or "to confirm." It is derived from the noun "κύρος" (kýros), meaning "authority" or "power."

Grammar: "κυρώσαι" is the second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb "κυρώνω" (kyrónō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The imperative mood is used to give a command or make a request.

Description: The word "κυρώσαι" is a command form that can be translated as "ratify" or "confirm." It is used to instruct someone to officially approve or endorse something, typically a law, agreement, or decision.
