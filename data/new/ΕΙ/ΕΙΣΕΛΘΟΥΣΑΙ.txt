Etymology: The word "εισελθουσαι" comes from the Greek verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". It is formed by combining the prefix "εις" (eis), meaning "into" or "in", with the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: "εισελθουσαι" is the feminine plural form of the aorist participle of the verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai). In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or adverbs. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past. The feminine plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of female entities.

Description: "εισελθουσαι" can be translated as "having entered" or "having gone in" in English. It describes a group of female entities who have completed the action of entering or going into a place.
