Etymology: The word "εισελεύσονται" comes from the Greek verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". It is formed by combining the prefix "εις" (eis), meaning "into" or "in", with the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: "Εισελεύσονται" is the third person plural form of the future tense of the verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai). In Greek grammar, the future tense is used to express actions or states that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: "Εισελεύσονται" describes the action of a group of people or things entering or going into a place. It implies movement from outside to inside. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing the entrance of a group of individuals into a building, a room, or any other enclosed space.
