Etymology: The word "εισιασιν" comes from the Greek word "εἰσίασιν" (eisiasin), which is the third person plural form of the verb "εἰσίημι" (eisiēmi), meaning "they go in" or "they enter."

Grammar: "εισιασιν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on tense, voice, mood, and person.

Description: "εισιασιν" is used to describe the action of multiple people going in or entering a place. It indicates that the subject (third person plural) is currently in the process of entering or going into a location.
