Etymology: The word "εικονα" (eikona) comes from the Greek word "εἰκών" (eikṓn), which means "image" or "icon".

Grammar: "Εικονα" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "εικονες" (eikones).

Description: In Greek, "εικονα" means "image" or "picture". It refers to a visual representation or depiction of something, often in the form of a painting, photograph, or drawing. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a representation or reflection of something in a more abstract sense.
