Etymology: The word "εισαγαγείν" (eisagagein) comes from the Greek roots "εἰς" (eis), meaning "into," and "ἄγειν" (agein), meaning "to lead." 

Grammar: "Εισαγαγείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "Εισαγαγείν" means "to introduce" or "to bring in" in English. It refers to the action of bringing something or someone into a particular place or situation.
