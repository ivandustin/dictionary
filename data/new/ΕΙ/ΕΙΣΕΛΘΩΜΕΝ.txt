Grammar: "εισελθώμεν" is the first person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the subjunctive mood is used to express possibility, potentiality, or uncertainty. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "εισελθώμεν" is a word that is used to express the idea of "let us enter" or "we should enter" in Greek. It is often used as a command or an invitation to go into a place or to participate in an activity.
