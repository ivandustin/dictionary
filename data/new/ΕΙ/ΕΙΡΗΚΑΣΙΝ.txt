Etymology: The word "ειρηκασιν" comes from the Greek verb "εἴρηκα" (eirēka), which is the perfect tense of the verb "εἶπον" (eipon), meaning "to say" or "to speak".

Grammar: "ειρηκασιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "εἴρηκα" (eirēka), which is in the perfect tense. The perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has continuing relevance in the present.

Description: "ειρηκασιν" is used to refer to a group of people who have said or spoken something. It can be translated as "they have said" or "they have spoken" in English.
