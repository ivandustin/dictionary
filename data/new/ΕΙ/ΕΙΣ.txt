Etymology: The word "εἶς" (eis) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ésti, meaning "is" or "to be."

Grammar: "Εἶς" (eis) is a Greek pronoun that means "one" or "someone." It is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender.

Description: "Εἶς" (eis) is used to refer to a single person or thing. It can be used as a pronoun or an adjective. In Greek, it is often used to emphasize singularity or uniqueness.
