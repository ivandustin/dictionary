Etymology: The word "εισακουσθείς" comes from the Greek verb "εισακούω" (eisakoúo), which means "to listen to" or "to hear." The suffix "-θεις" (-theis) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, indicating that the action of listening has been completed.

Grammar: "Εισακουσθείς" is the masculine singular form of the past participle of the verb "εισακούω." In Greek grammar, past participles are used to form compound tenses and passive voice constructions.

Description: "Εισακουσθείς" means "having been listened to" or "having been heard." It is used to describe something or someone that has undergone the action of listening or hearing.
