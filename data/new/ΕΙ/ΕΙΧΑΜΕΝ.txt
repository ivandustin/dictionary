Etymology: The word "ειχαμεν" comes from the Greek verb "εχω" (echo), which means "to have". It is conjugated in the past tense, first person plural form.

Grammar: "Ειχαμεν" is a verb in the past tense, first person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated to indicate the person, number, tense, mood, and voice of the action. The past tense indicates that the action took place in the past, and the first person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is "we".

Description: "Ειχαμεν" is used to express an action that "we" had in the past. It can be translated as "we had" in English.
