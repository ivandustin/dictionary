Etymology: The word "ειδέα" (eidéa) comes from the Greek word "εἶδος" (eîdos), meaning "form" or "shape."

Grammar: "Ειδέα" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Ειδέα" is a Greek word that can be translated as "idea" in English. It refers to a concept, thought, or mental image that represents something. In philosophy, an idea is often considered as a fundamental unit of thought or a building block of knowledge.
