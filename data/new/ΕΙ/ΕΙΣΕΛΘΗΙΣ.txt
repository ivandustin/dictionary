Etymology: The word "εισελθης" comes from the Greek verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "εις" (eis), meaning "into", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εισελθης" is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Εισελθης" is a word used in Greek to address someone directly and command them to enter or go in. It is commonly used in instructions, invitations, or when giving directions.
