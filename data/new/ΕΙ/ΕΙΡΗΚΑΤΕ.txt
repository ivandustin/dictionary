Etymology: The word "ειρηκατε" (eirēkate) comes from the Greek verb "εἴρηκα" (eirēka), which is the perfect tense of the verb "εἶπον" (eipon), meaning "to say" or "to speak".

Grammar: "ειρηκατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "εἴρηκα" (eirēka). It is in the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing relevance to the present. In this form, it is used to address a group of people and means "you all have said" or "you all have spoken".

Description: "ειρηκατε" is a word used in Greek to refer to the act of speaking or saying something. It is commonly used in various contexts to express that a group of people have communicated a message or expressed their thoughts.
