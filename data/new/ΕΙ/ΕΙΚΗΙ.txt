Etymology: The word "εικη" (eikē) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "εἰκῆ" (eikē), meaning "in vain" or "without cause".

Grammar: "Εικη" (eikē) is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or situation that is done or happens without reason, purpose, or justification.

Description: "Εικη" (eikē) is used to express the idea of something being done or happening without a valid reason or purpose. It implies that the action or situation is unnecessary, futile, or without any benefit.
