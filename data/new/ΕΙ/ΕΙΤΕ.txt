Etymology: The word "εἶτε" (eite) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the verb "εἶμι" (eimi), which means "to go" or "to come". 

Grammar: "Εἶτε" is a conjunction in Greek grammar. It is used to introduce alternative options or choices. It is similar to the English conjunction "either...or".

Description: "Εἶτε" is used to present two or more options or alternatives. It is often used to express a choice between two possibilities. For example, "Εἶτε πάμε σινεμά εἶτε μείνουμε σπίτι" (Either we go to the cinema or we stay at home).
