Etymology: The word "ειωθος" (eiōthos) comes from the Greek verb "εἴωθα" (eiōtha), which means "to be accustomed" or "to be accustomed to doing something."

Grammar: "Ειωθος" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "ειωθου" (eiōthou).

Description: In Greek, "ειωθος" refers to a habit, custom, or usual practice. It can also denote a traditional or established way of doing something.
