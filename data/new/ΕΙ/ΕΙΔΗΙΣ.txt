Etymology: The word "ειδης" (eidēs) comes from the Greek root "εἶδος" (eidos), meaning "form" or "shape."

Grammar: "Ειδης" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "ειδους" (eidous).

Description: "Ειδης" is used to describe something that has a specific form or appearance. It can also refer to a type or kind of something. In English, it can be translated as "species," "kind," or "sort."
