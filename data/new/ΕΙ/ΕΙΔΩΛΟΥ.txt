Etymology: The word "ειδολου" (eidolou) comes from the Greek word "εἶδος" (eidos), meaning "form" or "appearance," and the suffix "-λου" (-lou), which indicates possession. Therefore, "ειδολου" can be translated as "of form" or "of appearance."

Grammar: "Ειδολου" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Ειδολου" refers to something that belongs to or is associated with a form or appearance. It can be used to describe an attribute, characteristic, or quality of an object or person.
