Etymology: The word "ειποντες" (eipontes) comes from the Greek verb "εἶπον" (eipon), which is the aorist tense of the verb "λέγω" (lego), meaning "to say" or "to speak".

Grammar: "Ειποντες" is the plural form of the verb "εἶπον" (eipon), which is in the aorist tense. It is used to refer to a group of people who said or spoke something in the past.

Description: "Ειποντες" is a Greek word that means "they said" or "they spoke". It is commonly used in Greek texts to indicate that a group of people made a statement or expressed something verbally.
