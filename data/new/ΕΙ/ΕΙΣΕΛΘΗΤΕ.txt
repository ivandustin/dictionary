Etymology: The word "εισελθητε" comes from the Greek verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". It is formed by combining the prefix "εις" (eis), meaning "into" or "in", with the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: "Εισελθητε" is the second person plural, aorist tense, subjunctive mood form of the verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai). It is used to address a group of people and expresses a command or a request for them to enter or go in.

Description: "Εισελθητε" is a verb form that is commonly used in Greek to give instructions or directions. It is often used in signs, invitations, or any situation where someone wants to tell others to enter a place or go inside.
