Etymology: The word "ειρηκεν" (eirēken) comes from the Greek verb "εἶπον" (eipon), which means "I said" or "I have said." It is the perfect tense form of the verb.

Grammar: "Ειρηκεν" is the third person singular perfect tense form of the verb "εἶπον" (eipon). In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past with ongoing relevance to the present.

Description: "Ειρηκεν" is a word used in Greek to express the idea of "he/she/it has said" or "he/she/it said." It is commonly used in narratives or to report someone's speech or statement.
