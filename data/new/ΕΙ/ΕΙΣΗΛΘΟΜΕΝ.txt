Etymology: The word "εισηλθομεν" comes from the Greek verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in." It is formed by combining the prefix "εις" (eis), meaning "into," and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Εισηλθομεν" is the first person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood form of the verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "Εισηλθομεν" translates to "we entered" or "we went in" in English. It describes an action of a group of people entering or going into a place.
