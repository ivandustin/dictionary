Etymology: The word "υδροποτει" (udropotei) comes from the Greek words "υδρο" (hydro) meaning "water" and "ποτείν" (potein) meaning "to drink". 

Grammar: "υδροποτει" is a verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 
- Εγώ υδροποτώ (ego udropoto) - I drink water
- Εσύ υδροποτείς (esi udropoteis) - You drink water
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό υδροποτεί (aftos/afti/afto udropotei) - He/She/It drinks water
- Εμείς υδροποτούμε (emeis udropotoume) - We drink water
- Εσείς υδροποτείτε (eseis udropoteite) - You (plural) drink water
- Αυτοί/Αυτές υδροποτούν (aftoi/aftes udropotoun) - They drink water

Description: "υδροποτει" refers to the act of drinking water. It is a common verb used in everyday language to express the action of consuming water.
