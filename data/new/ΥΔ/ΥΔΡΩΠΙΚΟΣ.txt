Etymology: The word "υδρωπικός" (hydropikos) comes from the Greek words "ύδωρ" (hydor) meaning "water" and "πικρός" (pikros) meaning "bitter". 

Grammar: "Υδρωπικός" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Υδρωπικός" is a Greek word that means "hydroptic" in English. It is used to describe something or someone that is affected by or related to dropsy, a condition characterized by an abnormal accumulation of fluid in the body.
