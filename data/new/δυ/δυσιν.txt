Word: δυσιν

Etymology:
δυσιν (dysin) is a Greek word that comes from the root word δυσ- (dys-), which means "bad" or "difficult". It is often used as a prefix in Greek words to indicate something negative or difficult.

Grammar:
δυσιν is a noun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb. It can also be used as an adverb to indicate difficulty or hardship.

Description:
δυσιν is a word that is often used in Greek literature to describe a difficult or challenging situation. It can also be used to describe a negative or unpleasant experience. In modern Greek, it is often used in the phrase "δυσίνοια" (dysínoia), which means "anxiety" or "worry".
