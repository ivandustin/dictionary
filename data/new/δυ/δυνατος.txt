Word: δυνατος

Etymology: δυνατος comes from the Greek word δύναμις (dynamis), meaning "power" or "ability". It is derived from the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able".

Grammar: δυνατος is an adjective in Greek, meaning "powerful" or "able". It is declined in the masculine, feminine, and neuter genders, and in the nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases.

Description: δυνατος is a word that describes someone or something that has the power or ability to do something. It can refer to physical strength, mental ability, or any other kind of power or capability. In Greek mythology, the god Zeus was often described as δυνατος, meaning powerful or mighty. In modern Greek, the word is commonly used to describe someone who is strong or capable.
