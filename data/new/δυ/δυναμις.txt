Word: δυναμις

Etymology: The word δυναμις (dynamis) comes from the Greek root word δυναμη (dynamē), which means "power" or "ability". It is related to the English word "dynamic".

Grammar: δυναμις is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as both a subject and object in a sentence.

Description: δυναμις can be translated to mean "power", "ability", "strength", or "force". In ancient Greek philosophy, it was used to describe the inherent power or potential of something. In modern usage, it is often used in scientific and technical contexts to describe energy or force.
