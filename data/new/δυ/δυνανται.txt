Word: δυνανται

Etymology: δυνανται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δύναμη (dynamē), which means power or strength. The verb form δυνανται is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power."

Grammar: δυνανται is a third person plural present indicative active form of the verb δύναμαι. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The present indicative active form indicates that the action is happening in the present and that the subject is performing the action.

Description: δυνανται is a Greek verb that means "they are able" or "they have power." It is often used to describe the ability or power of a group of people or entities. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in discussions of physical strength, political power, or intellectual ability.
