Word: δυνατοί

Etymology: δυνατός (dunatos) is a Greek word that means "powerful" or "able". It comes from the root word δύναμις (dynamis), which means "power" or "force".

Grammar: δυνατοί is the plural form of the adjective δυνατός (dunatos), which means "powerful" or "able". It is used to describe a group of people or things that possess strength or capability.

Description: δυνατοί is a Greek word that is often used to describe a group of people who are strong, capable, or powerful. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a team of athletes, a group of soldiers, or a community of individuals who possess a particular skill or talent. The word conveys a sense of strength and ability, and is often used in a positive and admiring way.
