Word: δυνάσθαι

Etymology:
The word δυνάσθαι (dunasthai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δύναμις (dynamis), which means "power" or "ability". The verb form δυνάσθαι means "to be able to" or "to have the power to".

Grammar:
δυνάσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive verb in Greek. It is often translated as "to be able to" or "to have the power to" in English. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
δυνάσθαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to express ability or capability, as in "I am able to run fast" or "She has the power to lift heavy weights". It can also be used to express permission or authority, as in "You are allowed to enter the building" or "He has the authority to make decisions". Additionally, δυνάσθαι can be used to express possibility or potential, as in "It is possible to find a solution" or "There is potential for growth in this industry".
