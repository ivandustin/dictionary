Word: δυνατά

Etymology: δυνατά comes from the Greek word δυνατός (dunatos), which means "powerful" or "able". It is derived from the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able".

Grammar: δυνατά is the plural form of the adjective δυνατός (dunatos), which means "powerful" or "able". It is used to describe plural nouns.

Description: δυνατά is an adjective that is commonly used in Greek to describe things that are strong, powerful, or capable. It can be used to describe physical strength, as well as mental or emotional strength. It is often used in expressions such as δυνατά χέρια (strong hands) or δυνατός χαρακτήρας (strong character).
