Word: δυνησεσθε

Etymology:
δυνησεσθε (dunēsesthe) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δύναμις (dunamis), which means power or ability. The verb form δυνησεσθε is in the future tense and passive voice, meaning "you will be able" or "you will have power."

Grammar:
δυνησεσθε is a second-person plural verb in the future tense and passive voice. It is conjugated from the root word δύναμις (dunamis) and the suffix -σθε (-sthe), which is used to form the passive voice in Greek.

Description:
δυνησεσθε is a verb that expresses the idea of having power or ability in the future. It is often used in a positive sense, indicating that someone will be able to accomplish something or have the strength to overcome a challenge. In the New Testament, it is used in various contexts, such as when Jesus tells his disciples that they will receive power from the Holy Spirit (Acts 1:8) or when Paul encourages the Corinthians to be strong and steadfast in their faith (1 Corinthians 16:13).
