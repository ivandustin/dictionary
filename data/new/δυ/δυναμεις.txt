Word: δυναμεις

Etymology: δυναμεις is a Greek word that comes from the root word δύναμη (dynamē), which means power or strength. It is derived from the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power."

Grammar: δυναμεις is the plural form of the noun δύναμις (dynamis), which means power, strength, or ability. It is a third-declension feminine noun in Greek.

Description: δυναμεις can refer to physical or mental powers, abilities, or strengths. In Greek mythology, the term is often used to describe the powers of the gods and goddesses. In modern usage, it can refer to military or political power, as well as personal or spiritual strength.
