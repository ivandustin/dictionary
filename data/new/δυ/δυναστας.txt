Word: δυναστας

Etymology:
The word δυναστας (dynastas) comes from the Greek word δυναστης (dynastes), which means "ruler" or "lord". It is derived from the verb δυναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power".

Grammar:
δυναστας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word is singular, meaning it refers to one person.

Description:
δυναστας is a term used to describe a ruler or a person who holds great power and authority. In ancient Greece, it was used to refer to a monarch or a tyrant. In modern times, it can refer to a political leader or a wealthy and influential person. The word carries connotations of strength, dominance, and control.
