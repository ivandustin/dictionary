Word: δυναστής

Etymology:
The word δυναστής (dynastis) comes from the Greek word δύναμις (dynamis), meaning "power" or "ability". It originally referred to a ruler or leader who had the power to control a particular region or people.

Grammar:
δυναστής is a masculine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence. For example, "Ο δυναστής κυβερνά τον λαό" (The ruler governs the people).

Description:
δυναστής refers to a powerful ruler or leader who has control over a particular region or people. It can also be used to describe someone who has a great deal of influence or power in a particular field or industry. In modern Greek, the word is often used to refer to a wealthy or influential person.
