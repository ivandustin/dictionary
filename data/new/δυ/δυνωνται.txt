Word: δυνωνται

Etymology: δυνωνται is a present tense, third person plural form of the Greek verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able to" or "to have power". The word δύναμη (dynamē) from which δύναμαι is derived, means "power" or "ability".

Grammar: δυνωνται is a present tense, indicative mood, third person plural form of the verb δύναμαι. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an action that is happening now, the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact, and the third person plural indicates that the subject is a group of people or things.

Description: δυνωνται is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of ability or power. It is often used to describe the ability of a person or group of people to do something. For example, "οι άνθρωποι δυνώνται να μάθουν πολλά" (oi ánthropoi dynóntai na máthoun pollá) means "people can learn a lot". The verb δύναμαι is also used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the power of God.
