Word: δυσνοητα

Etymology: δυσ- (dys-) meaning "difficult" or "bad" and νοητά (noita) meaning "perceptible" or "understandable" in Greek. Therefore, δυσνοητα means "difficult to understand" or "hard to comprehend".

Grammar: δυσνοητα is a neuter plural adjective in Greek.

Description: δυσνοητα is used to describe something that is hard to understand or comprehend. It can refer to a complex idea, a difficult text, or a confusing situation. It is often used in academic or intellectual contexts to describe challenging concepts or theories.
