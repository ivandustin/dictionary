Word: δυναμενον

Etymology: δυναμενον is the present participle of the Greek verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". The word comes from the root δυνα- (dyna-), which means "power" or "ability".

Grammar: δυναμενον is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is declined as follows:

- Nominative: δυναμενον (dynamenon)
- Genitive: δυναμενου (dynamenou)
- Dative: δυναμενῳ (dynamenōi)
- Accusative: δυναμενον (dynamenon)
- Vocative: δυναμενε (dynamene)

Description: δυναμενον is a word that describes someone or something that is currently able to do something or has the power to do something. It is often used in the context of Greek philosophy and theology to describe the power or ability of a deity or divine being. In modern Greek, the word is often used in the sense of "potential" or "capability".
