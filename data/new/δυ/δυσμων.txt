Word: δυσμων

Etymology:
The word δυσμων (dysmon) is derived from the Greek words δυσ- (dys-), meaning "bad" or "difficult," and μωνη (mone), meaning "dwelling" or "abode." Therefore, the word δυσμων can be translated as "difficult dwelling" or "unpleasant abode."

Grammar:
δυσμων is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the nominative case, it is spelled as δυσμων. In the genitive case, it is spelled as δυσμωνος. In the accusative case, it is spelled as δυσμωνα.

Description:
The word δυσμων is not commonly used in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek literature, where it is used to describe a difficult or unpleasant place to live in. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a difficult or unpleasant situation.
