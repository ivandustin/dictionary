Word: δυναμενος

Etymology: δυναμενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able to" or "to have power". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the present passive voice, meaning "being able to" or "having power".

Grammar: δυναμενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is often used as an adjective to describe a person or thing that has the ability or power to do something.

Description: δυναμενος can be translated as "able", "capable", "powerful", or "strong". It is often used in the New Testament to describe Jesus Christ as the one who has the power to heal, perform miracles, and save people from sin. In secular Greek, it can also be used to describe a person who has physical or mental strength, or who is skilled in a particular area.
