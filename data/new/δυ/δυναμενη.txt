Word: δυναμενη

Etymology: δυναμενη is the feminine form of the Greek verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". It comes from the root word δύναμις (dynamis), which means "power" or "ability".

Grammar: δυναμενη is a feminine singular form of the present participle of the verb δύναμαι (dynamai). In Greek, present participles are used to describe ongoing actions or states. The feminine form indicates that the subject of the verb is female.

Description: δυναμενη can be translated as "being able" or "having power". It is often used in the New Testament to describe the power of God or the ability of believers to do good works through the Holy Spirit. In secular Greek, it can also be used to describe physical strength or ability.
