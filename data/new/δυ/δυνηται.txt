Word: δυνηται

Etymology: δυνηται is a Greek word that comes from the root word δύναμη (dynamē), which means power or strength. The suffix -ται (-tai) is a third person singular present indicative ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and referring to a single person or thing.

Grammar: δυνηται is a third person singular present indicative form of the Greek verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able to" or "to have power." It is conjugated from the present stem δυνα- (duna-) and the present tense ending -ται (-tai). 

Description: δυνηται is a verb that expresses the idea of having power or ability. It is often used in the context of someone being capable of doing something, or having the strength or resources to accomplish a task. For example, "Ο άνθρωπος δυνηται να κάνει πολλά πράγματα" (O ánthropos dynetai na kánei pollá prágmata) means "A person is capable of doing many things."
