Word: δυναμενους

Etymology: δυναμενους is the present participle of the Greek verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". The word comes from the root δυνα- (dyna-), which means "power" or "ability".

Grammar: δυναμενους is a masculine plural form of the present participle in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence.

Description: δυναμενους can be translated to mean "able" or "capable". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe individuals who have the power or ability to do something. For example, in the book of Romans, it is used to describe those who are able to withstand temptation and live a righteous life.
