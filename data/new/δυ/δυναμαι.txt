Word: δυναμαι

Etymology: δυναμαι comes from the Greek word δύναμις (dynamis), which means power or ability.

Grammar: δυναμαι is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It is a deponent verb, which means that it has a passive form but an active meaning. In other words, δυναμαι is translated as "I am able" or "I have power," even though it is in the passive voice.

Description: δυναμαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to express physical or mental ability, as in "I am able to run fast" or "I am able to solve complex problems." It can also be used to express permission or authority, as in "I am allowed to enter the building" or "I have the power to make decisions." Overall, δυναμαι is a powerful and important verb in the Greek language.
