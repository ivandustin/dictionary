Word: δυνησονται

Etymology: δυνησονται is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form of the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able, to have power, to be strong". The word comes from the root word δύναμις (dynamis), which means "power, strength, ability".

Grammar: δυνησονται is a future tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: δυνησονται is a verb that expresses the idea of ability or power. It can be translated as "they will be able" or "they will have power". The context in which the word is used will determine the exact meaning. For example, if the word is used in a sentence like "they will be able to lift the heavy object", it would mean that the group of people referred to in the sentence will have the ability to lift the heavy object.
