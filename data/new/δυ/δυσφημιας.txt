The word δυσφημίας (dysphēmías) is a Greek noun that can be translated as "slander" or "defamation". It is derived from the prefix δυσ- (dys-), meaning "bad" or "difficult", and the noun φήμη (phēmē), meaning "rumor" or "reputation". In terms of grammar, δυσφημίας is a genitive singular noun, indicating possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun.
