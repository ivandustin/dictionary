Word: δυνησομεθα

Etymology: δυνησομεθα is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δύναμις (dynamis), meaning power or ability. The suffix -σομεθα (-sometha) indicates the future tense, so δυνησομεθα means "we will be able to".

Grammar: δυνησομεθα is a first-person plural future indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. It is conjugated from the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able to". The verb is composed of the stem δυν- (dun-) and the ending -ησομεθα (-esometha).

Description: δυνησομεθα is a verb that expresses the idea of future ability or power. It can be translated as "we will be able to" or "we will have the power to". The verb is often used in a positive sense, indicating that the subject will be capable of achieving something.
