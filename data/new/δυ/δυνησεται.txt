Word: δυνησεται

Etymology: δυνησεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δύναμη (dynamē), meaning power or strength. The verb form δυνησεται is in the future tense and means "will be able to" or "will have the power to".

Grammar: δυνησεται is a third-person singular future indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb δύναμη (dynamē) and the future tense suffix -σεται (-setai). The verb is used to express the ability or power to do something in the future.

Description: δυνησεται is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical strength or ability, as well as mental or emotional strength. It can also be used to express the potential for success or achievement in a particular task or endeavor. In Greek literature and philosophy, the concept of δύναμη (dynamē) and its related verb forms are often used to explore the nature of power, agency, and human potential.
