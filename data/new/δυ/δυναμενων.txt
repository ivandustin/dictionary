Word: δυναμενων

Etymology: δυναμενων is the present participle of the Greek verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". The word comes from the root δυνα- (dyna-), which means "power" or "ability".

Grammar: δυναμενων is a present participle in the genitive case, which means it is used to describe a noun and indicates possession or association. In this case, it is describing a group of people who have the ability or power to do something.

Description: δυναμενων can be translated as "of those who are able" or "of those who have power". It is often used in the context of a group of people who have a particular ability or power, such as in the phrase "δυναμενων των πολεμειν" (of those who have the power to fight).
