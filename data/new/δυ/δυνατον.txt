Word: δυνατόν

Etymology: δυνατόν comes from the Greek word δυνατός (dunatos), which means "able, powerful, strong". It is derived from the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able, to have power".

Grammar: δυνατόν is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is possible or capable of happening.

Description: δυνατόν is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to express the idea of something being possible or capable of happening. For example, one might say "It is possible that it will rain tomorrow" using δυνατόν. It can also be used to express the idea of something being strong or powerful, as in "He is a strong and capable leader". Overall, δυνατόν is a useful word for expressing the concept of possibility and capability in Greek.
