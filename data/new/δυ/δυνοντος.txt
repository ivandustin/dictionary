The word δυνόντος (dunontos) is a Greek adjective in the genitive case, meaning "able" or "capable". It comes from the verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". The root of the word is δυνα- (duna-), which means "power" or "ability". 

In terms of grammar, δυνόντος is in the masculine gender, singular number, and genitive case. It is often used to modify a noun, indicating that the noun has the ability or power to do something. 

Overall, δυνόντος is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe someone or something as capable or powerful.
