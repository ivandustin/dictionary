Word: δυναμεως

Etymology: δυναμεως is a Greek word that comes from the root word δύναμις (dynamis), which means power or strength. The suffix -εως (-eos) is a genitive singular ending, indicating possession or belonging.

Grammar: δυναμεως is a noun in the genitive singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description: δυναμεως can be translated as "of power" or "of strength" in English. It is often used in the context of describing someone's or something's ability or capacity to do something. For example, in the New Testament, the phrase "δύναμις θεοῦ" (dynamis theou) is used to describe the power of God. In modern Greek, the word δύναμη (dynamē) is used to refer to physical or mental strength.
