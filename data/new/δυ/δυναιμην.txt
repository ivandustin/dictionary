The word δυναιμην (dunaimēn) is a Greek verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root word δύναμις (dynamis), which means power or ability. 

In its present tense, middle voice form, δυναιμην means "I am able" or "I have power." It is often used in the context of physical or mental ability, but can also refer to social or political power. 

In terms of grammar, δυναιμην is a first-person singular verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. 

Overall, δυναιμην is a versatile verb that conveys a sense of power and ability in various contexts.
