Word: φημι

Etymology:
The word φημι (phēmi) is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰeh₂- which means "to speak, say". It is related to the Latin word fari and the English word fame.

Grammar:
φημι is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It means "I say" or "I speak".

Description:
φημι is a common verb in Greek and is used to express a wide range of meanings related to speaking, telling, and expressing opinions. It can also be used to indicate a rumor or a report. In ancient Greek literature, φημι is often used in the context of prophecy or divine communication.
