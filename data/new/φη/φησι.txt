Word: φησι

Etymology: φησι comes from the Greek verb φημί (phēmí), which means "to say" or "to declare."

Grammar: φησι is a third-person singular present indicative active form of the verb φημί. It is often translated as "he/she/it says" or "he/she/it declares."

Description: φησι is a common word in Greek literature and is often used to introduce a quotation or to indicate that someone is speaking. It is also used in philosophical and religious texts to indicate a statement of truth or belief.
