Word: φηστος

Etymology:
The word φηστος (phēstos) is a Greek adjective that means "spoken, said, or declared." It is derived from the verb φημί (phēmí), which means "to say" or "to declare."

Grammar:
φηστος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description:
φηστος is a word that describes something that has been spoken or declared. It can be used to describe a statement, a rumor, or a piece of information that has been communicated verbally. The word can also be used to describe a person who is known for speaking or declaring things, such as a prophet or an orator.
