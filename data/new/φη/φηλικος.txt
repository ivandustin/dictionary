Word: φηλικος

Etymology:
The word φηλικος (phēlikos) is derived from the Greek word φίλος (phílos), which means "friend" or "beloved". The suffix -ικος (-ikos) is used to form adjectives, indicating "related to" or "pertaining to".

Grammar:
φηλικος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
φηλικος is an adjective that describes something as friendly or amicable. It can be used to describe a person, a relationship, or a situation. For example, one might use the word φηλικος to describe a friendly conversation or a warm greeting between friends.
