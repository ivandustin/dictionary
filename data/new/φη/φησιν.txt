Word: φησιν

Etymology: φησιν comes from the Greek verb φημί (phēmí), which means "to say" or "to declare."

Grammar: φησιν is a third-person singular present indicative active form of the verb φημί (phēmí). It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: φησιν is used to indicate that someone is saying or declaring something in the present tense. It can be translated as "he/she/it says" or "he/she/it declares." It is commonly used in Greek literature and is an important verb in the Greek language.
