Word: φημη

Etymology:
The word φημη (phēmē) is derived from the Greek verb φημί (phēmí), which means "to say" or "to speak." It is related to the Greek word φωνή (phōnē), which means "voice" or "sound."

Grammar:
In Greek, φημη is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is φημη, and its genitive singular form is φημης. The word can be declined in all cases, singular and plural.

Description:
φημη is a Greek word that can be translated as "rumor," "report," or "news." It refers to information that is spread by word of mouth, rather than through official channels. In ancient Greek literature, φημη often plays a significant role in the plot, as characters act on rumors that turn out to be false or true. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any kind of talk or conversation.
