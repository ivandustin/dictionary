Word: αισχυνας

Etymology:
The word αισχυνας (aischynas) is derived from the Greek verb αισχύνομαι (aischynomai), which means "to feel shame or disgrace." The word is composed of two parts: αισχύνη (aischynē), which means "shame," and the verb suffix -νομαι (-nomai), which indicates the action of feeling or doing something.

Grammar:
In Greek, αισχυνας is a noun in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb. The word is masculine and singular, indicating that it refers to a single male person or thing. 

Description:
The word αισχυνας is often used to describe a feeling of shame or disgrace. It can also refer to a person who is shameful or disgraceful in some way. For example, one might say "Ο αισχυνας δεν μπορεί να κοιτάξει τους ανθρώπους στα μάτια" (The shameful person cannot look people in the eye). The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe something that is embarrassing or shameful, such as "Η κατάσταση στη χώρα είναι αισχυνας" (The situation in the country is shameful).
