Word: αιμορροουσα

Etymology: The word αιμορροουσα comes from the Greek words αιμορραγία (hemorrhage) and ρέω (to flow), which means "bleeding" or "flowing of blood".

Grammar: αιμορροουσα is a feminine adjective in the present participle form, which means "bleeding" or "hemorrhaging". It is used to describe a female subject who is currently experiencing bleeding.

Description: αιμορροουσα is a medical term used to describe a female who is currently experiencing bleeding, particularly in the context of menstruation or postpartum bleeding.
