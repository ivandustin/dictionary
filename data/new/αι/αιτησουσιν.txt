Word: αιτησουσιν

Etymology:
The word αιτησουσιν is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form of the verb αἰτέω (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request". The root of the word is αἴτημα (aitēma), which means "request" or "petition".

Grammar:
The word αιτησουσιν is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people.

Description:
The word αιτησουσιν describes an action that a group of people will take in the future. Specifically, it means "they will ask" or "they will request". The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and purpose of the request.
