The word "αιδους" (aidous) is a Greek noun that means "Hades" or "the underworld". It is derived from the Greek word "αἰδώς" (aidos), which means "shame" or "modesty". In Greek mythology, Hades was the god of the underworld and the ruler of the dead. The word "αιδους" is often used in reference to the realm of the dead or the afterlife. 

In terms of grammar, "αιδους" is a second declension noun in the genitive case. Its singular form is "αἰδοῦς" (aidous) and its plural form is "αἰδοῦν" (aidoun). 

Overall, "αιδους" is a word that is deeply rooted in Greek mythology and culture, and is still used today to refer to the underworld or the afterlife.
