The word αιτησας (aitēsas) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It comes from the root word αἰτέω (aitéō), which means "to ask" or "to request". The suffix -ας (-as) is added to the root word to indicate the past tense.

αιτησας (aitēsas) is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence (the person who performed the action) is the one who made the request. The word is also in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject is a single person.
