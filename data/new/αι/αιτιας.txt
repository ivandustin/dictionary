Word: αιτιας

Etymology:
The word αιτιας (aitias) comes from the Greek word αἰτία (aitia), which means "cause" or "reason". It is derived from the verb αἰτέω (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to demand".

Grammar:
In Greek, αιτιας is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive singular form of αἰτία (aitia).

Description:
The word αιτιας is used to refer to the cause or reason for something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in legal proceedings, philosophical discussions, or everyday conversations. For example, one might say "Η αιτία του προβλήματος είναι η έλλειψη χρημάτων" (The cause of the problem is the lack of money).
