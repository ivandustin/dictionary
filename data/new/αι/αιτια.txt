Word: αιτια

Etymology:
The word αιτια (aitia) comes from the Ancient Greek word αἰτία (aitía), which means "cause" or "reason". It is derived from the verb αἰτέω (aitéō), which means "to ask" or "to demand".

Grammar:
In Greek, αιτια is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is usually translated as "cause", "reason", or "blame". It can be used in the singular or plural form, depending on the context.

Description:
The word αιτια is commonly used in Greek to refer to the cause or reason for something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in legal proceedings, philosophical discussions, or everyday conversations. For example, one might say "Η αιτία του προβλήματος είναι η έλλειψη χρημάτων" (The cause of the problem is the lack of money). Alternatively, one might say "Δεν φέρω αιτία για το λάθος σου" (I am not to blame for your mistake).
