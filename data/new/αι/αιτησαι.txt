Word: αιτησαι

Etymology:
The word αιτησαι (aitēsai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word αἰτέω (aitéō), which means "to ask for, to request, to demand". 

Grammar:
The word αιτησαι is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. 

Description:
The word αιτησαι is commonly used in modern Greek to mean "to ask for, to request". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as asking for a favor, requesting information, or making a demand. The word is often used in formal situations, such as in business or legal settings.
