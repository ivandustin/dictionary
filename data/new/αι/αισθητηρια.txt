Word: αισθητηρια

Etymology:
The word αισθητηρια (aisthitiria) comes from the Greek word αισθητήριον (aisthitirion), which means "sense organ" or "sensor". It is derived from the Greek word αισθητής (aisthetes), which means "perceiver" or "sensitive".

Grammar:
In Greek, αισθητηρια is a plural noun, which means "sensors" or "sense organs". It is a feminine noun and belongs to the second declension.

Description:
The word αισθητηρια is used to refer to the organs or devices that detect and respond to stimuli, such as light, sound, touch, taste, and smell. Examples of αισθητηρια include the eyes, ears, nose, tongue, and skin, as well as electronic sensors used in various fields such as medicine, engineering, and robotics. The study of αισθητηρια is known as sensory physiology.
