Word: αιρετικόν

Etymology:
The word αιρετικόν comes from the Greek word αἱρετικός (hairetikos), which means "able to choose" or "one who chooses." It is derived from the verb αἱρέω (haireo), which means "to choose" or "to take."

Grammar:
In Greek, αιρετικόν is an adjective that agrees with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe a person or thing that is characterized by choosing or selecting. For example, αιρετικόν δόγμα (hairetikon dogma) means "heretical doctrine."

Description:
The word αιρετικόν is often used in a religious context to describe someone who holds beliefs that are contrary to the official doctrine of a particular faith. In Christianity, for example, a heretic is someone who denies one or more of the fundamental teachings of the church. The term can also be used more broadly to describe anyone who holds unconventional or controversial beliefs.
