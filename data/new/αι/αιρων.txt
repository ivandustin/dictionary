The word "αιρων" (airon) is a Greek verb that means "to lift up" or "to take away". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "αίρω" (airo), which has the same meaning. In terms of grammar, "αιρων" is the present participle form of the verb, which means it describes an ongoing action in the present tense. 

In ancient Greek literature, "αιρων" was often used in a metaphorical sense to describe the act of taking away or removing something, such as a burden or a problem. It was also used in a more literal sense to describe lifting up objects or people. 

Overall, "αιρων" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts, both literal and metaphorical.
