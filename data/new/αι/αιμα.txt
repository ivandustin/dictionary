Word: αιμα

Etymology:
The word αιμα (aima) comes from the Ancient Greek word αἷμα (haîma), which means "blood". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ésh₂r̥ ("blood").

Grammar:
In Greek, αιμα is a neuter noun. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: το αίμα (to aima) - the blood
Genitive: του αίματος (tou aimatos) - of the blood
Accusative: το αίμα (to aima) - the blood

Plural:
Nominative: τα αίματα (ta aimata) - the bloods
Genitive: των αιμάτων (ton aimaton) - of the bloods
Accusative: τα αίματα (ta aimata) - the bloods

Description:
Αιμα is a very important substance in the human body, as it carries oxygen and nutrients to all parts of the body and removes waste products. It is also a symbol of life and vitality in many cultures. In Greek mythology, the gods were said to have "immortal blood", which gave them their power and strength. The word αιμα is often used metaphorically to refer to the essence or core of something, as in the phrase "the blood of the matter".
