The word "αιματων" (aimatōn) is a Greek word that means "of blood" or "blood-related". It is derived from the Greek word "αἷμα" (aima), which means "blood". In terms of grammar, "αιματων" is the genitive plural form of "αἷμα". This means that it is used to indicate possession or relationship to multiple instances of blood. 

In Greek mythology, the term "αιματων" is often used to refer to the descendants of a particular god or goddess, such as the "αιματα Ζευς" (aimata Zeus), which means "the bloodline of Zeus". 

Overall, "αιματων" is a word that is primarily used in scientific or mythological contexts to describe blood or blood-related concepts.
