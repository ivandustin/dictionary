Word: αιματι

Etymology:
The word αιματι (aimati) is derived from the Greek word αίμα (aima), which means "blood". The suffix -τι (-ti) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adverbs.

Grammar:
In Greek, αιματι (aimati) is an adverb. It is used to describe an action that is done with blood or in a bloody manner.

Description:
The adverb αιματι (aimati) is used to describe actions that involve blood or are done in a bloody manner. For example, one might say "Οι δολοφόνοι έσφαξαν το θύμα αιματι" (The murderers slaughtered the victim in a bloody manner). The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe something that is violent or brutal.
