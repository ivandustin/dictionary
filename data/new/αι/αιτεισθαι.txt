The word "αιτεισθαι" (aiteisthai) is a Greek verb that means "to ask for, to request, to demand". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "αιτε-" (aite-), which means "to ask, to request". The suffix "-σθαι" (-sthai) is a middle voice ending that indicates the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Grammar: "αιτεισθαι" is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb. It can also be conjugated in various tenses and moods to match the subject and the context of the sentence.

Description: The word "αιτεισθαι" is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday language to express the act of asking for something, whether it is a favor, a permission, or a material object. It can also imply a sense of urgency or insistence in the request.
