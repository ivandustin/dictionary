Word: αινων

Etymology:
The word αινων (ainon) is derived from the Greek verb αινέω (aineo), which means "to praise" or "to extol". It is related to the noun αινος (ainos), which means "praise" or "commendation".

Grammar:
αινων (ainon) is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description:
αινων (ainon) is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a type of song or hymn of praise. It was often sung in honor of a god or hero, and was typically accompanied by a lyre or other musical instrument. The word is also used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to a type of prayer or thanksgiving.
