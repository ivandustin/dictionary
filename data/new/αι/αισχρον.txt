Word: αισχρον

Etymology:
The word αισχρον (aischron) is derived from the Greek word αἰσχρός (aischros), which means "ugly" or "disgraceful". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁eyd- ("to see"), which is also the root of the English word "hideous".

Grammar:
In Greek, αισχρον is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is ugly, shameful, or disgraceful.

Description:
The word αισχρον is often used to describe something that is considered to be morally or aesthetically repugnant. It can refer to physical ugliness, as well as to behavior or actions that are considered to be shameful or disgraceful. In modern Greek, the word is often used to describe something that is vulgar or indecent.
