Word: αιτησωμεν

Etymology:
The word αιτησωμεν is derived from the Greek verb αἰτέω (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request". It is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb.

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, αιτησωμεν is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb αἰτέω (aiteo). The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, while the subjunctive mood expresses a wish, doubt, or possibility. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
The word αιτησωμεν is a verb form that expresses a wish or possibility of a completed action of asking or requesting in the past. It is often used in Greek literature and language to express a polite request or a desire for something.
