Word: αιτιωματα

Etymology:
The word αιτιωματα (aitiomata) is derived from the Greek verb αιτιάομαι (aitiaomai), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a plural noun ending in Greek.

Grammar:
In Greek, αιτιωματα is a neuter plural noun. It is declined as follows:

Nominative: αιτιωματα
Genitive: αιτιωματων
Accusative: αιτιωματα
Vocative: αιτιωματα

Description:
The word αιτιωματα refers to accusations or charges brought against someone. It can also refer to the evidence or proof used to support these accusations. In modern Greek, the word is often used in legal contexts.
