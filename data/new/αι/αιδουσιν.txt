The word "αιδουσιν" (aidousin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "αίδω" (aido), which means "to be ashamed" or "to feel reverence." 

In terms of grammar, "αιδουσιν" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it is conjugated in the third person plural form. 

In terms of description, "αιδουσιν" can be translated to mean "they are ashamed" or "they feel reverence." The context in which it is used will determine the exact meaning.
