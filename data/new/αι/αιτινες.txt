The word "αιτινες" (aitines) is a Greek pronoun that means "who, which, that". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "αἵτιος" (aitios), which means "responsible, guilty, cause". 

Grammar: "Αιτινες" is a plural form of the pronoun "αιτινα" (aitina), which is used to refer to feminine nouns. It can also be used as a relative pronoun to introduce a subordinate clause.

Description: "Αιτινες" is a versatile pronoun that can be used in various contexts to refer to people, animals, or things. It is commonly used in written and spoken Greek to connect clauses and sentences, and to provide additional information about a noun or a subject.
