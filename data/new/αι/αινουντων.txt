Word: αινουντων

Etymology:
The word αινουντων is derived from the Greek verb αινέω (aineo), which means "to praise" or "to extol". The suffix -ουντων (-ounton) is the present participle form of the verb, indicating that the action of praising is currently taking place.

Grammar:
αινουντων is a present participle form of the Greek verb αινέω (aineo), which means "to praise". It is in the genitive case, indicating possession or association with a noun in the sentence.

Description:
The word αινουντων is used to describe a group of people who are currently praising or extolling something or someone. It can be translated as "praising" or "those who praise". The word is often used in religious or poetic contexts to describe the act of praising a deity or a hero.
