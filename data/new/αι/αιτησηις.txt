The word "αιτησηις" (aitēsis) is a Greek noun that means "request" or "petition". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "αιτέω" (aitéō), which means "to ask" or "to request". The suffix "-ησηις" (-ēsis) is a nominalizing suffix that turns the verb into a noun.

Grammar: "Αιτησηις" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. Its genitive form is "αιτήσεως" (aitēseōs), and its plural form is "αιτήσεις" (aitēseis).

Description: "Αιτησηις" is a formal word that is often used in legal or bureaucratic contexts to refer to a written or verbal request or petition. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any kind of request or demand.
