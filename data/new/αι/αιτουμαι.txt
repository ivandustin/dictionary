Word: αιτούμαι

Etymology:
The word αιτούμαι (aitoumai) comes from the ancient Greek verb αἰτέω (aiteo), which means "to ask for, to request, to demand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid- ("to ask, to request").

Grammar:
αιτούμαι is a first-person singular present indicative form of the verb αἰτέω (aiteo). It is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. In English, it can be translated as "I ask for myself, I request for myself, I demand for myself".

Description:
αιτούμαι is a common verb in Greek, used in various contexts to express a request or a demand. It can be used in formal or informal situations, and it is often accompanied by the preposition για (gia) to indicate the object of the request. For example, "Αιτούμαι ένα ποτήρι νερό για παρακαλώ" (Aitoumai ena potiri nero gia parakalo) means "I ask for a glass of water, please".
