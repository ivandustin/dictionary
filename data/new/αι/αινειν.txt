Word: αινειν

Etymology:
The word αινειν (ainein) comes from the Greek verb αἰνέω (aineo), which means "to praise" or "to commend". It is derived from the Greek noun αἶνος (ainos), which means "praise" or "commendation".

αινειν (ainein) is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Present tense:
- Singular: αινεί (ainei)
- Plural: αινοῦμεν (ainoumen)

Active voice:
- The subject of the sentence performs the action of the verb.

Infinitive mood:
- The verb is not conjugated to show tense, person, or number. It is often used to express purpose or intention.

The word αινειν (ainein) means "to praise" or "to commend" in Greek. It is often used in the context of expressing admiration or approval for someone or something.
