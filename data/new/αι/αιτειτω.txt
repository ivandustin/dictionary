Word: αιτειτω

Etymology:
The word αιτειτω comes from the Greek verb αἰτέω (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid-.

Grammar:
αιτειτω is a verb in the present imperative form, which is used to give commands or make requests. It is the third person singular form of the verb αἰτέω (aiteo).

Description:
The word αιτειτω is used to express a command or request in the third person singular form. It is often translated as "let him/her ask" or "let him/her request". It is commonly used in religious or formal contexts, such as in prayers or official documents.
