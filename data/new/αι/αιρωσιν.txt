Word: αιρωσιν

Etymology: The word αιρωσιν comes from the Greek verb αίρω (airō), which means "to lift up" or "to take away". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a third person plural ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to multiple people.

Grammar: αιρωσιν is a present tense verb in Greek, specifically in the third person plural form. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of lifting up or taking away.

Description: αιρωσιν can be translated to English as "they lift up" or "they take away". It is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone physically lifting an object, taking something away from someone, or even metaphorically lifting someone's spirits or taking away their worries.
