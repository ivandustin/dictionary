The word "αινουντα" (ainounta) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "αίνεω" (aineo), which means "to praise" or "to commend". The participle form indicates that the action of praising is ongoing or continuous. 

In terms of grammar, "αινουντα" is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be used as a subject or a predicate nominative in a sentence.

Etymologically, the verb "αίνεω" comes from the ancient Greek word "αἴνησις" (ainesis), which means "praise" or "commendation". The word has roots in Proto-Indo-European language and is related to the English word "encomium".

Overall, "αινουντα" refers to someone who is continuously praising or commending.
