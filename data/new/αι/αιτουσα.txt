The word "αιτουσα" (aitousa) is a feminine noun in Greek that means "petitioner" or "suppliant". 

Etymology: The word comes from the verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request". 

Grammar: In Greek, "αιτουσα" is in the nominative case, singular number, and feminine gender. 

Description: The word "αιτουσα" is often used in legal or formal contexts to refer to someone who makes a request or a petition. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is asking for something, whether it be a favor, assistance, or information.
