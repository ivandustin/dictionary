Word: αιρεται

Etymology:
The word αιρεται (airetai) is derived from the Greek verb αίρω (airō), which means "to lift up" or "to take away". It is a present tense, middle or passive voice form of the verb.

Grammar:
In Greek, αιρεται is a present tense, middle or passive voice form of the verb αίρω (airō). It is the third person singular form, which means "he/she/it is lifted up" or "he/she/it is taken away".

Description:
The word αιρεται is used to describe the action of lifting up or taking away something or someone. It can be used in a literal sense, such as lifting up an object, or in a figurative sense, such as taking away someone's freedom or rights.
