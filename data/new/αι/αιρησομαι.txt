Word: αιρησομαι

Etymology:
The word αιρησομαι (airēsomai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αἴρω (airō), which means "to lift up" or "to take away." It is a compound verb formed from the prefix αἱρέω (haireō), which means "to choose" or "to take," and the suffix -σομαι (-somai), which indicates a future tense.

Grammar:
αιρησομαι (airēsomai) is a first-person singular future middle deponent verb in Greek. The middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon themselves, and the deponent voice means that the verb is passive in form but active in meaning. In other words, the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

The verb αιρησομαι (airēsomai) means "I will take for myself" or "I will choose." It is often used in a spiritual or religious context, such as choosing to follow a particular path or belief system.
