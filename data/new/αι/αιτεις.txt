Word: αιτεις

Etymology:
The word αιτεις (aitēs) comes from the Greek verb αἰτέω (aitéō), which means "to ask" or "to request". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid-.

Grammar:
In Greek, αιτεις is a noun in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence. It is also a masculine noun, which means it refers to a male person. The word has a plural form, αιτείς (aitéis).

Description:
The word αιτεις refers to someone who asks for something, a petitioner or a supplicant. In ancient Greece, it was often used to refer to someone who made a request to a god or goddess. In modern Greek, it is used more generally to refer to someone who asks for something, such as a favor or a loan.
