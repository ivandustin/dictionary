Word: αισχυνθωμεν

Etymology:
The word αισχυνθωμεν is derived from the Greek verb αισχύνομαι (aishunomai), which means "to feel shame or disgrace." The verb is formed by adding the prefix αισχυν- (aishun-) to the root verb ομαι (omai), which means "to be."

Grammar:
αισχυνθωμεν is a first-person plural present passive subjunctive form of the verb αισχύνομαι (aishunomai). The subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or a hypothetical situation. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
The word αισχυνθωμεν is used to express the idea of "let us be ashamed." It is often used in a religious or moral context, to express a sense of guilt or remorse for one's actions. The subjunctive mood suggests that the speaker is uncertain or doubtful about whether they will actually feel ashamed, but is expressing a desire or intention to do so.
