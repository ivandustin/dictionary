Word: αιτωμεν

Etymology:
The word αιτωμεν (aitomen) is a present participle form of the Greek verb αιτέω (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request". The verb αιτέω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid- which means "to ask" or "to request".

Grammar:
αιτωμεν (aitomen) is a present participle form of the verb αιτέω (aiteo) in the first person plural. It is in the active voice and the indicative mood. The present participle form indicates that the action of asking or requesting is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
The word αιτωμεν (aitomen) can be translated as "we are asking" or "we are requesting". It is a verb form that indicates an ongoing or continuous action of asking or requesting. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a formal request, a plea, or a demand.
