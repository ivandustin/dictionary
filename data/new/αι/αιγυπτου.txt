The word "αιγυπτου" (aigyptou) is the genitive form of the Greek word "Αίγυπτος" (Aigyptos), which means "Egypt" in English. 

In terms of grammar, "αιγυπτου" is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun. 

As for its etymology, "Αίγυπτος" is believed to have originated from the ancient Egyptian name "Hwt-ka-Ptah" (House of the ka of Ptah), which was the name of a temple in Memphis dedicated to the god Ptah. The Greeks later adapted this name as "Aigyptos" and used it to refer to the entire country of Egypt. 

In terms of description, Egypt is a country located in North Africa, bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the north, Libya to the west, Sudan to the south, and the Red Sea to the east.
