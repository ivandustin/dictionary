Word: αισχυνη

Etymology:
The word αισχυνη (aishunē) comes from the ancient Greek word αἰσχύνη (aischunē), which means "shame" or "disgrace". It is derived from the verb αἰσχύνομαι (aischynomai), which means "to feel shame" or "to be ashamed".

Description:
The word αισχυνη refers to the feeling of shame or embarrassment that one experiences when they have done something wrong or inappropriate. It can also refer to the state of being shameful or disgraceful. In Greek culture, shame was considered a powerful force that could motivate people to behave in a socially acceptable manner. The concept of shame was also closely tied to the idea of honor, as a person's reputation and social standing were closely linked to their ability to avoid shameful behavior. Today, the word αισχυνη is still used in modern Greek to refer to the feeling of shame or embarrassment.
