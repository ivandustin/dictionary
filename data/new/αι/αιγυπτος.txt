Word: αιγυπτος

Etymology:
The word αιγυπτος (aiguptos) comes from the ancient Greek word Αἴγυπτος (Aigyptos), which was the name of the country we now know as Egypt. The origin of the word is uncertain, but it is believed to have been derived from the ancient Egyptian phrase "Hwt-ka-Ptah" which means "temple of the soul of Ptah".

Grammar:
In Greek, αιγυπτος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is αιγυπτος, and its genitive singular form is αιγυπτου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
αιγυπτος is the Greek word for Egypt, a country located in North Africa. It is known for its ancient civilization, including the pyramids, temples, and hieroglyphics. Today, Egypt is a modern country with a rich cultural heritage and a diverse population.
