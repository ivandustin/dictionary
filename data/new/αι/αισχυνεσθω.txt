Word: αισχυνεσθω

Etymology:
The word αισχυνεσθω comes from the Greek verb αἰσχύνομαι (aischýnomai), which means "to feel shame or disgrace." It is derived from the noun αἰσχύνη (aischýnē), which means "shame" or "disgrace."

Grammar:
αισχυνεσθω is a present passive imperative verb in Greek. It is in the third person singular form and is used to give a command or instruction. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
The word αισχυνεσθω is often translated as "let him be ashamed" or "let him feel shame." It is a command or instruction given to someone to feel ashamed or to acknowledge their wrongdoing. It is often used in a moral or ethical context, encouraging individuals to take responsibility for their actions and to feel remorse for any harm they may have caused.
