Word: αιτησωνται

Etymology: The word αιτησωνται is derived from the Greek verb αιτέω (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request". The suffix -ωνται (-ontai) is the third person plural present middle/passive form of the verb.

Grammar: αιτησωνται is a verb in the present tense, third person plural, middle/passive voice. It is a form of the verb αιτέω (aiteo), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: αιτησωνται means "they are being asked/requested" or "they are asking/requesting for themselves". The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either receiving the action or performing the action for their own benefit.
