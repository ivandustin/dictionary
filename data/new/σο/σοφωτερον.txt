Word: σοφωτερον

Etymology: σοφωτερον is a comparative form of the Greek adjective σοφός (sophos), which means "wise" or "clever". The suffix -τερον (-teron) is added to the stem of the adjective to form the comparative degree, meaning "wiser" or "more clever".

Grammar: σοφωτερον is a neuter singular form of the comparative degree of the adjective σοφός. It is used to compare the degree of wisdom or cleverness between two things or persons.

Description: σοφωτερον is a word that describes something or someone as being wiser or more clever than another thing or person. It is often used in philosophical or intellectual contexts to compare the level of knowledge or understanding between different individuals or ideas.
