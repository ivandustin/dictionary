Word: σοφούς

Etymology:
The word σοφούς (sophous) is derived from the Greek word σοφός (sophos), which means wise or clever. It is a masculine plural form of the adjective σοφός.

Grammar:
In Greek, σοφούς is a masculine plural accusative form of the adjective σοφός. It is used to describe a group of wise or clever men.

Description:
The word σοφούς is often used in ancient Greek literature to describe philosophers, scholars, and other learned men. It is a term of respect and admiration, and is often used to describe individuals who possess great wisdom and knowledge. In modern Greek, the word σοφούς is still used to describe wise and knowledgeable individuals.
