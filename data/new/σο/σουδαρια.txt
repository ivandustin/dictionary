Word: σουδαρια

Etymology:
The word σουδαρια (soudaria) is derived from the Greek word σουδάριον (soudarion), which means "a handkerchief or a cloth used for wiping the face." The word is also related to the Latin word sudarium, which has the same meaning.

Grammar:
σουδαρια is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative plural form.

Description:
σουδαρια is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the cloth that was used to wrap the head of Jesus after his crucifixion.
