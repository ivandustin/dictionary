Word: σοφισαι

Etymology:
The word σοφισαι (sophisai) is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood in the Greek language. It is derived from the noun σοφία (sophia), which means "wisdom" or "knowledge."

Grammar:
In Greek, verbs have different tenses, voices, and moods that indicate the time, subject, and purpose of the action. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The infinitive mood is used to express the action as a verbal noun, without specifying the subject or time.

Description:
The word σοφισαι (sophisai) means "to make wise" or "to become wise." It is often used in the context of gaining knowledge or understanding, and can refer to both intellectual and moral wisdom. In ancient Greek philosophy, the pursuit of wisdom was considered the highest goal of human life, and the word σοφία (sophia) was used to describe the ideal of wisdom and knowledge.
