Word: σοφιαν

Etymology:
The word σοφιαν (sophian) is derived from the Greek word σοφία (sophia), which means "wisdom" or "knowledge". It is a noun in the accusative singular form.

Grammar:
In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their function in a sentence. The accusative singular form is used when the noun is the direct object of a verb. In this case, σοφιαν is the direct object of an implied verb.

Description:
σοφιαν is a Greek word that refers to wisdom or knowledge. It is often used in philosophical and religious contexts to describe a deep understanding of the world and its mysteries. The word has a rich history in Greek literature and philosophy, and is still used today in modern Greek.
