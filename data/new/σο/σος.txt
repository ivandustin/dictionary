Word: σος

Etymology:
The word "σος" is a Greek word that means "your" or "yours". It is derived from the ancient Greek language and is commonly used in modern Greek as well.

Grammar:
In Greek, "σος" is a possessive pronoun that is used to indicate possession or ownership. It is the second person singular form of the possessive pronoun and is used to refer to something that belongs to the person being addressed.

Description:
"Σος" is a simple but important word in the Greek language. It is used in everyday conversation to indicate possession or ownership. For example, if someone asks "Πού είναι το βιβλίο σου;" (Where is your book?), the word "σος" is used to indicate that the book belongs to the person being addressed.
