In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, voice, and mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or is affected by the action.

Description: "ηδυναντο" is a verb form that expresses the idea of ability or power in the past. It can be translated as "they were able" or "they had the power". The imperfect tense suggests that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past.
