Etymology: The word "ηδυνατο" (idynato) comes from the Greek verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". The prefix "η-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, making it mean "was able" or "could".

Grammar: "ηδυνατο" is the third person singular form of the verb "δύναμαι" in the past tense. It is used to express the ability or power of someone or something in the past.

Description: "ηδυνατο" is a Greek word that describes the past ability or power of someone or something. It indicates that someone or something had the capability to do something in the past.
