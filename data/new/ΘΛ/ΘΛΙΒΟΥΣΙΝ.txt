Etymology: The word "θλιβούσιν" (thlivousin) comes from the Greek verb "θλίβω" (thlivó), which means "to press, squeeze, or afflict." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *telh₂-.

Grammar: "Θλιβούσιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "θλίβω" in the present tense. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb belongs to the first conjugation class in Greek.

Description: "Θλιβούσιν" is used to describe an action or state of pressing, squeezing, or afflicting that is being performed by a third person plural subject. It can be translated as "they press," "they squeeze," or "they afflict" in English.
