Etymology: The word "θλιβεσθαι" (thlibesthai) comes from the Greek verb "θλίβω" (thlibo), which means "to press, to squeeze, to afflict." 

Grammar: "Θλιβεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "θλίβω" (thlibo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and mood. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive form expresses the action of the verb without specifying any subject or tense.

Description: "Θλιβεσθαι" is a Greek verb that can be translated as "to be afflicted, to be distressed, to be oppressed." It describes a state of being under pressure, experiencing distress, or feeling oppressed.
