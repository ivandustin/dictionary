Grammar: "Θλίψις" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and has a stem ending in -ιψι-. In the nominative singular form, it is spelled as "θλίψις". The genitive singular form is "θλίψεως" (thlipseos), and the accusative singular form is "θλίψιν" (thlipsin).

Description: "Θλίψις" refers to the state of being distressed, afflicted, or troubled. It can also mean suffering, hardship, or anguish. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe emotional or psychological distress.
