Word: δημος

Etymology:
The word δημος (demos) comes from the ancient Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰem- which means "people". It is related to the English word "democracy" which also comes from the same root.

Grammar:
In ancient Greek, δημος is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a nominative singular form of δημος and a genitive singular form of δημου. The plural forms are δημοι (nominative) and δημων (genitive).

Description:
The word δημος has several meanings in ancient Greek. It can refer to the people of a city or state, the common people, or the citizens of a democracy. It can also refer to a district or region within a city or state. In ancient Athens, the δημος referred to the lower classes of citizens who were not part of the aristocracy. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to the people or population of a place.
