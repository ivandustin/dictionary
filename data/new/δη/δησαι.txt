The word "δησαι" (dēsai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the aorist tense, imperative mood, and second person singular form of the verb "δέω" (deō), which means "to bind" or "to tie up". 

Etymology: The word "δέω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₁- which means "to tie, to fasten". 

Grammar: As mentioned earlier, "δησαι" is the aorist tense, imperative mood, and second person singular form of the verb "δέω". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The second person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is "you" (singular). 

Description: "δησαι" is a command to tie or bind something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as tying up an animal or binding a book, or in a figurative sense, such as being bound by a promise or obligation.
