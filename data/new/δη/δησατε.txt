The word "δησατε" (desate) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the imperative mood and second person plural form. It is derived from the root word "δεσ-" (des-), which means "to bind" or "to tie up". 

In terms of grammar, "δησατε" is a regular verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or orders, so "δησατε" can be translated as "bind" or "tie up" in English.

Overall, "δησατε" is a word that instructs someone to bind or tie something up, and it has its roots in the Greek language's rich history and vocabulary.
