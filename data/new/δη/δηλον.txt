Word: δηλον

Etymology:
The word δηλον (dēlon) is a Greek word that comes from the verb δηλόω (dēlóō), which means "to make clear" or "to manifest." It is derived from the adjective δῆλος (dêlos), which means "clear" or "evident."

Grammar:
δηλον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case.

Description:
δηλον is used to describe something that is clear, evident, or obvious. It can also be used to indicate certainty or assurance. In some contexts, it can be translated as "certainly" or "undoubtedly." The word is often used in philosophical and theological discussions to express the idea of something being self-evident or indisputable.
