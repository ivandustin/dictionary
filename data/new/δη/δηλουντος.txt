Word: δηλουντος

Etymology:
δηλουντος is the genitive form of the Greek verb δηλόω (delóo), which means "to make clear, to show, to indicate". The word comes from the root δηλ-, which is related to the adjective δῆλος (dêlos), meaning "clear, evident, manifest".

Grammar:
δηλουντος is a masculine singular genitive form of the verb δηλόω (delóo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person/number of the subject. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns.

Description:
δηλουντος can be translated as "of the one who shows" or "of the one who indicates". It is a form of the verb δηλόω (delóo), which is used to express the idea of making something clear or evident. The genitive case indicates that δηλουντος is related to or possessed by another noun or pronoun.
