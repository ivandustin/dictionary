The word "ηλειου" (eleiou) is a Greek noun that means "oil" or "olive oil". It is derived from the Greek word "ελαία" (elaia), which means "olive tree". In terms of grammar, "ηλειου" is a singular noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or origin. 

In Greek cuisine, olive oil is a staple ingredient and is used in a variety of dishes, from salads to main courses. It is also used in cosmetics and skincare products due to its moisturizing and nourishing properties. 

Overall, "ηλειου" is a versatile and important word in Greek culture and language.
