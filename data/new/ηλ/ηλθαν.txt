Word: ηλθαν

Etymology: ηλθαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb έρχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ- which means "to move, to go".

Grammar: ηλθαν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: ηλθαν is a Greek verb that means "they came". It is used to describe a group of people arriving at a location. The word is commonly used in everyday conversation and in literature.
