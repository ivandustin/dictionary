Word: ηλεηθητε

Etymology: The word ηλεηθητε is a conjugated form of the Greek verb ηλεηθώ (eleitho), which means "to have mercy" or "to show compassion". The root of the word is ηλε-, which means "mercy" or "pity".

Grammar: ηλεηθητε is the second person plural, aorist passive indicative form of the verb ηλεηθώ. This form indicates that the action of having mercy or showing compassion was done to you (plural) in the past.

Description: ηλεηθητε is a verb form that expresses a past action of receiving mercy or compassion. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, where the concept of mercy is important. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe a situation where someone has been shown kindness or forgiveness.
