Word: ηλεημένος

Etymology: The word ηλεημένος comes from the Greek verb ηλεέω (eleéo), which means "to have mercy" or "to pity". The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word describes someone who has received mercy or pity.

Grammar: ηλεημένος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: ηλεημένος describes someone who has received mercy or pity. It can also be used to describe someone who is blessed or fortunate. In a religious context, it can refer to someone who has been shown divine mercy or grace.
