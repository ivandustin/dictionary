The word "ηλεησα" (eleisa) is a verb in the Greek language, which means "I had mercy" or "I showed compassion". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "ελεέω" (eleéo), which means "to have mercy" or "to show compassion". The suffix "-σα" (-sa) is the first person singular past tense ending.

Grammar: "ηλεησα" is a first person singular past tense verb. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. 

Description: The word "ηλεησα" is often used in a religious context, referring to God's mercy and compassion towards humanity. It can also be used in a general sense to describe an act of kindness or forgiveness towards someone in need.
