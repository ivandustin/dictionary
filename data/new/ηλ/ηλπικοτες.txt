Word: ηλπικότες

Etymology: ηλπικότες is the past participle of the Greek verb "ελπίζω" (elpizo), which means "to hope". The word comes from the ancient Greek word "ἐλπίς" (elpis), which means "hope" or "expectation".

Grammar: ηλπικότες is a masculine plural form of the past participle of the verb "ελπίζω". It is used to describe a group of people who have hoped or had hope in the past.

Description: ηλπικότες can be translated to "those who have hoped" or "those who had hope". It is a word that describes a group of people who have had a positive outlook on a situation or outcome. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in literature, history, or everyday conversation.
