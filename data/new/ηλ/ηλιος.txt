Word: ηλιος

Etymology: 
The word ηλιος (helios) comes from the Ancient Greek word ἥλιος (hēlios), which means "sun". In Greek mythology, Helios was the god of the sun.

Grammar:
In Greek, ηλιος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is ηλιοι (helioi).

Description:
Ηλιος refers to the star that is the center of the solar system and provides light and heat to the Earth. In Greek mythology, Helios was often depicted as a handsome god driving a chariot across the sky, pulling the sun behind him. The word ηλιος is also used in everyday language to refer to the sun, as well as to describe sunny weather.
