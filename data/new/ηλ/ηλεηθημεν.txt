Word: ηλεηθημένος (eleithemenos)

Etymology: The word ηλεηθημένος comes from the Greek verb ηλεέω (eleéo), which means "to have mercy" or "to pity". The suffix -θημένος (-themenos) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the subject has received the action of the verb.

Grammar: ηλεηθημένος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: ηλεηθημένος is an adjective that describes someone who has received mercy or pity. It can also be used to describe someone who is compassionate or merciful towards others. In Christian theology, the term is often used to describe the state of being forgiven or saved by God's mercy.
