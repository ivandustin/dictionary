Word: βημα

Etymology:
βημα (bēma) comes from the Ancient Greek βῆμα (bêma), which means "step" or "platform". In Ancient Greece, it referred to a raised platform in a public space where speakers could address a crowd.

Grammar:
βημα is a neuter noun in Greek. Its nominative singular form is βημα, and its genitive singular form is βηματος (bēmatos).

Description:
βημα can refer to a step, a pace, or a platform. In modern Greek, it is often used to refer to a podium or a stage. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a significant or decisive step in a process or journey.
