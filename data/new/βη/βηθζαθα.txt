βηθζαθα (pronounced "beth-za-tha") is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible. It is mentioned in the Gospel of John, chapter 5, verse 2, where it is described as the location of a pool in Jerusalem where people would gather to be healed. The etymology of the word is uncertain, but it is believed to be a transliteration of a Hebrew or Aramaic word. In terms of grammar, βηθζαθα is a proper noun and is declined in the nominative case.
