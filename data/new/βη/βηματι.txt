The word βηματι (bēmati) is a Greek noun that means "step" or "pace". It is derived from the verb βαίνω (baínō), which means "to walk" or "to step". In terms of grammar, βηματι is a singular noun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. 

In description, βηματι can refer to a physical step or pace taken while walking or running, or it can be used metaphorically to refer to progress or advancement in a process or situation. It is a common word in Greek literature and is often used in poetry and philosophical texts.
