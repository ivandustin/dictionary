βηθσαιδα (Bethsaida) is a Greek word that means "house of fishing" or "place of fishing". It is a biblical place name that appears in the New Testament, specifically in the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, and Luke. In the Bible, Bethsaida is described as a small fishing village located on the northern shore of the Sea of Galilee. The town is also associated with several miracles performed by Jesus, including the feeding of the 5,000 and the healing of a blind man. In terms of grammar, βηθσαιδα is a feminine noun in the nominative case.
