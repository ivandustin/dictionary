Word: βηθανιαι

Etymology: βηθανιαι is a Greek word that comes from the Aramaic word "beth anya" which means "house of figs". It is a place name mentioned in the New Testament.

Grammar: βηθανιαι is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, it is spelled βηθανία and pronounced "vee-tha-NEE-ah".

Description: βηθανιαι is a biblical place name that refers to a village located on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives, near Jerusalem. It is mentioned several times in the New Testament, including as the place where Jesus raised Lazarus from the dead.
