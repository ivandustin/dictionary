Word: βηθανιαν

Etymology: βηθανιαν (Bethany) is a Greek word that comes from the Aramaic word "bet 'anya," which means "house of the poor" or "house of affliction." It is a village located on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives, about two miles from Jerusalem.

Grammar: βηθανιαν is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, it is spelled with the letters βηθανίαν and pronounced as "vay-tha-nee-an."

Description: βηθανιαν is a biblical place name that appears in the New Testament. It is most famously known as the village where Jesus raised Lazarus from the dead (John 11:1-44). It is also mentioned in other passages, such as Matthew 21:17 and Mark 11:1, as the place where Jesus stayed during his final week before his crucifixion. Today, Bethany is a Palestinian village located in the West Bank.
