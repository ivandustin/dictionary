Word: βηθανιας

Etymology:
The word βηθανιας (Bethany) is of Aramaic origin, meaning "house of dates" or "house of figs". It is derived from the Hebrew word "bet-ha'aniya", which means "house of affliction" or "house of misery". Bethany is a village located on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives, about two miles from Jerusalem.

Description:
βηθανιας is a proper noun that refers to a village located on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives. It is mentioned several times in the New Testament, particularly in the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John. It is most famous for being the home of Mary, Martha, and Lazarus, who were close friends of Jesus. According to the Gospels, Jesus performed several miracles in Bethany, including raising Lazarus from the dead. Today, Bethany is a popular pilgrimage site for Christians visiting Jerusalem.
