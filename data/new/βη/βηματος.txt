Word: βηματος

Etymology:
The word βηματος (bēmatos) is derived from the Greek word βῆμα (bēma), which means "step" or "platform". In ancient Greece, the βῆμα was a raised platform or podium from which public speakers addressed the crowd.

Grammar:
βηματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive form of βῆμα (bēma), which is a neuter noun.

Description:
βηματος refers to a step or a platform, and is often used in the context of a raised platform or podium from which a speaker addresses an audience. In modern Greek, the word can also refer to a step or a stair.
