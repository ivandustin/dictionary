The word "θωμαι" (thōmai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is derived from the root word "θέω" (theō), which means "to run" or "to rush." 

In terms of grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact or reality.

The verb "θωμαι" can be translated to mean "I rush" or "I hasten." It is often used in the context of urgency or eagerness, such as in the phrase "θωμαῖς ἐπὶ τὸν πόλεμον" (thōmais epi ton polemon), which means "I hasten to war." 

Overall, "θωμαι" is a dynamic verb that conveys a sense of urgency and action.
