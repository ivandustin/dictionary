Word: θω

Etymology:
The word θω is a Greek verb that means "to rush" or "to hasten". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "θέω" (theo), which means "to run".

Grammar:
Θω is a first conjugation verb in the present tense. Its conjugation is as follows:

Singular:
- θω (I rush)
- θεις (you rush)
- θει (he/she/it rushes)

Plural:
- θομεν (we rush)
- θετε (you all rush)
- θουσι (they rush)

Description:
Θω is a verb that describes a quick and sudden movement. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the movement of a person or an object. For example, "Ο ανεμος θεριζει τα δεντρα" (The wind rushes through the trees). It can also be used figuratively to describe a sudden action or decision, such as "Θω να παω στην Αθηνα" (I decided to rush to Athens).
