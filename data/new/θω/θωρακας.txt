Word: θωρακας

Etymology:
The word θωρακας (thorakas) comes from the ancient Greek word θώραξ (thorax), which means "breastplate" or "chest armor". The word was used to describe the protective armor worn by soldiers in ancient Greece.

Grammar:
In Greek, θωρακας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is θωρακες (thorakes). It is often used in the context of military or protective gear.

Description:
Θωρακας refers to a type of armor worn to protect the chest and torso. In ancient Greece, it was typically made of bronze or other metals and was an essential part of a soldier's equipment. Today, the word can also be used to describe modern body armor worn by law enforcement or military personnel.
