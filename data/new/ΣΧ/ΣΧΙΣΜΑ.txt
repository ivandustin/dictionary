Etymology: The word "σχίσμα" (schisma) comes from the Greek verb "σχίζω" (schizo), which means "to split" or "to divide". 

Grammar: "Σχίσμα" is a neuter noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "σχίσματος" (schismatos). 

Description: "Σχίσμα" refers to a division or a split, usually in a group or an organization. It can also be used to describe a disagreement or conflict that leads to a separation.
