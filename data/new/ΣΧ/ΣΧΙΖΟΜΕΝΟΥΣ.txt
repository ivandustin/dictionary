Etymology: The word "σχιζομένους" (schizomenous) comes from the Greek verb "σχίζω" (schizo), which means "to split" or "to divide." The suffix "-μένους" (-menous) indicates the present participle form of the verb.

Grammar: "Σχιζομένους" is the masculine accusative plural form of the present participle of the verb "σχίζω" (schizo). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions.

Description: "Σχιζομένους" can be translated as "splitting" or "dividing" in English. It refers to multiple masculine subjects who are currently engaged in the action of splitting or dividing something.
