Etymology: The word "σχολή" (skholē) comes from the Ancient Greek word "σχολή" (skholḗ), which means "leisure" or "free time". In ancient times, it referred to a place where people gathered for intellectual pursuits and discussions.

Grammar: In Greek, "σχολή" is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows:

- Singular: η σχολή (i skholí) - the school
- Plural: οι σχολές (i skholés) - the schools

Description: In modern Greek, "σχολή" primarily refers to a school or an educational institution. It can also refer to a specific field of study or a department within a university. Additionally, "σχολή" can be used metaphorically to describe a group of people who share a common interest or profession.
