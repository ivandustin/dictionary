Etymology: The word "σχω" (skhō) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seĝh-, meaning "to hold" or "to have."

Grammar: In Greek, "σχω" (skhō) is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the second conjugation class and is a regular verb.

Description: "Σχω" (skhō) means "I have" or "I hold" in Greek. It is used to express possession or ownership of something.
