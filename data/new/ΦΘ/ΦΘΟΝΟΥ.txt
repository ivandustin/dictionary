Etymology: The word "φθονού" (pronounced "fthonou") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "φθόνος" (pronounced "fthonos"), which means "envy" or "jealousy".

Grammar: "Φθονού" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person singular form of the verb "φθονώ" (pronounced "fthono"), which means "I envy" or "I am jealous".

Description: "Φθονού" is used to express the action of feeling envy or jealousy towards someone or something. It indicates a negative emotion of wanting what someone else has or feeling resentful towards their success or possessions.
