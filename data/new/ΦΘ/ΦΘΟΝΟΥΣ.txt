Etymology: The word "φθονος" (pronounced "phthonos") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "φθονεω" (pronounced "phthoneo"), which means "to envy" or "to be jealous."

Grammar: In Greek, "φθονος" is a noun of masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "φθονου" (pronounced "phthonou").

Description: "φθονος" refers to the feeling of envy or jealousy. It is the state of being resentful or covetous of someone else's possessions, qualities, or achievements. In Greek mythology, "φθονος" was personified as the spirit of envy.
