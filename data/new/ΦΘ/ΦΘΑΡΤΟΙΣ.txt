Etymology: The word "φθαρτοίς" (pronounced "fthartois") comes from the Greek word "φθαρτός" (pronounced "fthartos"), which means "perishable" or "corruptible". It is derived from the verb "φθείρω" (pronounced "ftheiro"), meaning "to destroy" or "to corrupt".

Grammar: "Φθαρτοίς" is the plural form of the adjective "φθαρτός" in Greek. It is used to describe multiple things or people that are perishable or corruptible.
