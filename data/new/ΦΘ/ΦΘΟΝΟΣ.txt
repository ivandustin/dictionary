Etymology: The word "φθονος" (pronounced "phthonos") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "φθονεω" (pronounced "phthoneo"), which means "to envy" or "to be jealous."

Grammar: In Greek, "φθονος" is a noun. It is classified as a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be used in both the singular and plural forms.

Description: "Φθονος" refers to the feeling of envy or jealousy. It is the emotion experienced when one desires what someone else possesses or achieves and feels resentment or bitterness towards them as a result.
