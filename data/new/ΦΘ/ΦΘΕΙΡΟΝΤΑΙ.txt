Etymology: The word "φθείρονται" comes from the Greek verb "φθείρω" (phtheíro), which means "to corrupt" or "to destroy".

Grammar: "Φθείρονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense in the passive voice of the verb "φθείρω". It is used to indicate that a subject is being corrupted or destroyed by someone or something.

Description: "Φθείρονται" is a Greek word that describes the action of something or someone being corrupted or destroyed. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the decay of a physical object, the moral corruption of a person, or the destruction of a system or organization.
