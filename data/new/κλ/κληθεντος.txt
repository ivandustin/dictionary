Word: κληθεντος

The word κληθεντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The word κληθεντος is the passive participle form of καλέω, which means "called" or "summoned".

In Greek grammar, κληθεντος is a masculine singular nominative or accusative form of the passive participle of the verb καλέω. It can be translated as "the one who has been called" or "the one who has been summoned".

Description:
The word κληθεντος is often used in Greek literature and mythology to describe heroes or gods who have been called or summoned to perform a task or duty. For example, in Homer's Iliad, Achilles is often referred to as κληθεντος, as he was called by Agamemnon to fight in the Trojan War.
