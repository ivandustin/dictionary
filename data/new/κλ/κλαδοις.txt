Word: κλαδοις

Etymology:
The word κλαδοις (kladois) is a noun in the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word κλάδος (klados), which means "branch" or "twig". 

Grammar:
κλαδοις is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form of κλαδοις indicates that there are multiple branches or twigs.

Description:
κλαδοις refers to branches or twigs that grow out of a tree or plant. It is often used in the context of gardening, agriculture, or forestry. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the different branches or aspects of a larger concept or idea.
