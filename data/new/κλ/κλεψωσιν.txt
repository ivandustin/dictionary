Word: κλεψωσιν

Etymology: The word κλεψωσιν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb κλέπτω (kleptó), which means "to steal". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *klep- which means "to steal" or "to take secretly".

Grammar: κλεψωσιν is the third person plural, aorist subjunctive active form of the verb κλέπτω. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: κλεψωσιν can be translated as "they might steal" or "let them steal" depending on the context. It is a form of the verb κλέπτω, which is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday speech. The word has a negative connotation as it refers to an illegal and immoral act.
