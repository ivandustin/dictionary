Word: κλαδούς

Etymology:
The word κλαδούς (kladoús) is derived from the Greek word κλάδος (kládos), which means "branch" or "twig". It is a noun in the plural form.

Grammar:
In Greek, κλαδούς is a masculine noun in the accusative case. It is the direct object of a sentence and can be used with verbs that require a direct object.

Description:
κλαδούς refers to branches or twigs of a tree or plant. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to branches of knowledge or fields of study. For example, one might say "οι κλαδούς της επιστήμης" (oi kladoús tis epistímis) to refer to the branches of science.
