Word: κληθηναι

Etymology:
The word κληθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The verb κληθηναι is the aorist passive infinitive form of καλέω, which means "to be called" or "to be summoned".

Grammar:
κληθηναι is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist passive infinitive form, which means that it describes an action that was completed in the past and was done to the subject of the sentence. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it would be the person or thing that was called or summoned.

Description:
The word κληθηναι is used to describe the act of being called or summoned. It is often used in the context of someone being called to a specific place or for a specific purpose. For example, if someone is summoned to court, they might say "Κλήθηναι στο δικαστήριο" (Klithenai sto dikastirio) which means "I was called to court". The word can also be used in a more general sense, such as "Κληθηναι για μια συνάντηση" (Klithenai gia mia synantisi) which means "I was called for a meeting".
