Word: κλαιοντας

Etymology:
The word κλαιοντας is derived from the Greek verb κλαίω (klaiō), which means "to weep" or "to cry". The suffix -οντας (-ontas) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently weeping or crying.

Grammar:
κλαιοντας is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently weeping or crying. In Greek, present participles are often used to form compound tenses or to describe ongoing actions.

Description:
κλαιοντας can be translated to "weeping" or "crying" in English. It is often used in literature or poetry to describe a scene of mourning or sadness. The word can also be used to describe a group of people who are expressing their emotions through tears.
