Word: κλεπτουσιν

Etymology:
The word κλεπτουσιν is derived from the Greek verb κλέπτω (kléptō), which means "to steal". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to someone who is currently stealing.

Grammar:
κλεπτουσιν is a present participle in the third person plural form. It is used to describe people who are currently stealing. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ουσα (-ousa), -ον (-on), -οντας (-ontas), or -ουσιν (-ousin) to the stem of the verb.

Description:
κλεπτουσιν is a Greek word that means "they are stealing" or "they steal". It is a present participle that describes an ongoing action of stealing. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Οι κλεπτουσιν κλέβουν χρήματα από το σούπερ μάρκετ" (The ones who are stealing are stealing money from the supermarket).
