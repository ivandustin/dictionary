Word: κλεις

The word κλεις (kleis) is derived from the Ancient Greek verb κλείω (kleiō), which means "to close" or "to lock". It is related to the English word "key", which also has the same meaning.

In Greek, κλεις is a feminine noun of the third declension. Its nominative singular form is κλεις, and its genitive singular form is κλειδός (kleidós). The word can also be declined in the plural, with the nominative plural form being κλεῖδες (kleides) and the genitive plural form being κλειδῶν (kleidōn).

Description:
κλεις refers to a key, which is a small metal object used to unlock or lock doors, locks, or other devices. In Ancient Greece, keys were often made of bronze or iron and were used to secure homes, temples, and other important buildings.
