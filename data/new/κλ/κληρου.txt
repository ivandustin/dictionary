The word "κληρου" (klērou) is a Greek noun that means "lot" or "inheritance". It comes from the Greek verb "κληρόω" (klēroō), which means "to cast lots" or "to allot". In ancient Greece, lots were often cast to determine the outcome of events or to divide property among heirs. The word "κληρου" can also refer to a portion of land or property that is inherited. 

In terms of grammar, "κληρου" is a second declension neuter noun in the genitive case. Its singular form is "κλῆρος" (klēros) and its plural form is "κλῆροι" (klēroi). 

Overall, "κληρου" is a word that is closely associated with the concept of inheritance and the division of property.
