Word: κλαιουσαν

Etymology: κλαιουσαν is a Greek verb in the present participle form, derived from the root word κλαίω (kláio), which means "to weep" or "to cry."

Grammar: κλαιουσαν is the present participle form of the verb κλαίω (kláio), which means "weeping" or "crying." It is in the feminine gender, plural number, and active voice.

Description: κλαιουσαν describes a group of females who are currently weeping or crying. It can be used to describe a scene of mourning or sadness.
