Word: κληθησονται

Etymology:
The word κληθησονται is a Greek verb in the future passive tense. It is derived from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The addition of the prefix κλη- (kle-) intensifies the meaning of the root word, resulting in the meaning "to be called" or "to be summoned".

Grammar:
κληθησονται is a third-person plural verb in the future passive tense. In Greek grammar, the passive voice is used to indicate that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The future tense indicates that the action will take place at some point in the future.

Description:
κληθησονται can be translated to mean "they will be called" or "they will be summoned". The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning. The word is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, such as in the Bible, where it is used to refer to the calling of individuals to a higher purpose or to the afterlife.
