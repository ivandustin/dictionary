Word: κλαιουσα

Etymology:
The word κλαιουσα (klaiousa) is the present participle of the Greek verb κλαίω (klaio), which means "to weep" or "to cry". The verb is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *klei-, which means "to lament" or "to cry out".

Grammar:
κλαιουσα is a feminine singular form of the present participle in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence. The word can be translated to English as "weeping" or "crying".

Description:
κλαιουσα is a word that describes a female who is weeping or crying. It can be used to describe a person who is experiencing sadness, grief, or pain. The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a situation or event that is causing emotional distress. In literature, the word is often used to create a sense of pathos or to evoke sympathy from the reader.
