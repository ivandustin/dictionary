Word: κλαιε

Etymology:
The word κλαιε (klaié) is derived from the Greek verb κλαίω (klaíō), which means "to weep" or "to cry." The verb is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *klei-, which means "to lament" or "to cry out."

Grammar:
κλαιε is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb κλαίω (klaíō).

Description:
κλαιε is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it weeps" or "he/she/it cries." It is often used to describe the act of shedding tears in response to sadness, grief, or pain. The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or event that is emotionally distressing or difficult to bear.
