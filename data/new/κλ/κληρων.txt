Word: κληρων

Etymology:
The word κληρων comes from the Greek verb κληρόω (klēróō), which means "to allot" or "to assign by lot". It is derived from the noun κλῆρος (klêros), which means "lot" or "portion".

Grammar:
κληρων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. The word has a singular form and a plural form.

Description:
κληρων refers to a person who draws lots or a lot itself. In ancient Greece, lots were used to determine the outcome of various events, such as the allocation of land or the selection of officials. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to someone who is lucky or fortunate.
