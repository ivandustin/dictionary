The word "κλαιοντων" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "κλαίω" (klaió), which means "to weep" or "to cry". The participle form indicates that the action of weeping or crying is ongoing or continuous. In English, it can be translated as "weeping" or "crying". 

In terms of grammar, "κλαιοντων" is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship. It can be translated as "of those who are weeping" or "belonging to those who are crying". 

Overall, "κλαιοντων" is a descriptive word that paints a picture of people who are in a state of sadness or grief, continuously weeping or crying.
