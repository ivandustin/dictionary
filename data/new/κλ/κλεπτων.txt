Word: κλεπτων

Etymology:
The word κλεπτων (kleptōn) is the present participle of the Greek verb κλέπτω (kléptō), which means "to steal". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *klep- which means "to steal" or "to take secretly".

Grammar:
In Greek, κλεπτων is a masculine noun in the nominative case, singular form. As a present participle, it indicates an ongoing action of stealing. It can also function as an adjective, modifying a noun to indicate that it is stolen or related to theft.

Description:
κλεπτων is a word that describes someone who is stealing or engaged in the act of stealing. It can also be used to describe stolen goods or property. In Greek mythology, the god Hermes was often associated with thievery and was sometimes referred to as "the god of thieves". The word κλεπτων is still used in modern Greek to refer to a thief or someone who steals.
