Word: κλητοις

Etymology:
The word κλητοις (klētois) is derived from the Greek verb καλέω (kaleō), which means "to call." The suffix -τοις (-tois) is a dative plural ending, indicating that the word is referring to something or someone that is being called.

Grammar:
In Greek, κλητοις is a noun in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of a verb or a preposition. It is also in the plural form, indicating that it refers to more than one thing or person.

Description:
The word κλητοις can be translated as "to the called ones" or "to those who are called." It is often used in a religious context, referring to those who have been called by God or chosen for a particular purpose. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to refer to the followers of Jesus Christ, who are called to be his disciples.
