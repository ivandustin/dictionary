Word: κλινης

Etymology:
The word κλινης (klinēs) is derived from the Greek verb κλίνω (klinō), which means "to lean" or "to incline." It is related to the English word "clinic," which originally referred to a place where patients would recline or lie down.

Grammar:
κλινης is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative singular case. The genitive singular form is κλίνης (klinēs).

Description:
κλινης refers to a bed or a couch, typically one that is used for sleeping or resting. In ancient Greece, it was common for people to eat and sleep on couches rather than sitting in chairs or sleeping in beds. The word can also refer to a hospital bed or a medical examination table.
