Word: κλασμάτων

Etymology:
The word κλασμάτων (klasmáton) is derived from the Greek verb κλάω (kláō), which means "to break" or "to shatter". The suffix -ματων (-matōn) is a plural form indicating "pieces" or "fragments". Therefore, κλασμάτων (klasmáton) can be translated as "fragments" or "broken pieces".

Grammar:
κλασμάτων (klasmáton) is a plural noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive plural form of κλάσμα (klásma), which means "fragment" or "piece". In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description:
κλασμάτων (klasmáton) is a word that refers to broken pieces or fragments of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in mathematics to refer to fractions or in medicine to refer to broken bones or tissues. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or a relationship that is fragmented or broken into pieces.
