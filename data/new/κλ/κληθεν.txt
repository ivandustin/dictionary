The word "κληθεν" is a past participle form of the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". In this form, it indicates that the action of calling or summoning has already taken place in the past. 

In terms of grammar, "κληθεν" is a third-person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is singular and in the third person. It is also in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Overall, "κληθεν" can be translated as "called" or "summoned" in English, depending on the context in which it is used.
