Word: κληρονομησητε

Etymology: κληρονομησητε is a Greek word that comes from the verb κληρονομέω (klēronomeō), which means "to inherit" or "to receive by inheritance". The word is formed by adding the suffix -σητε (-sēte) to the stem κληρονομη- (klēronomē-).

Grammar: κληρονομησητε is a second person plural aorist active imperative verb form in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to inherit or receive something.

Description: κληρονομησητε is a verb that is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Matthew (5:5) and in the book of Romans (8:17). In both instances, it is used in the context of inheriting the kingdom of God. The word emphasizes the idea of receiving something as an inheritance, rather than earning it through one's own efforts.
