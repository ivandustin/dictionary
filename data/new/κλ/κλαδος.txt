Word: κλαδος

Etymology:
The word κλαδος (klados) comes from the Greek root κλάω (klao), which means "to break off" or "to branch out." It is related to the English word "clad," which means "covered with branches or foliage."

Grammar:
In Greek, κλαδος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is κλαδός (klados), and its genitive singular form is κλάδου (kladou). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
κλαδος refers to a branch or a twig of a tree or plant. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a subdivision or a branch of a larger organization or system.
