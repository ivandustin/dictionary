Word: κλαιετε

Grammar: κλαιετε is the present tense, second person plural form of the verb κλαίω (klaiō). It is used to address a group of people and means "you all are weeping" or "you all are crying". 

Description: κλαιετε is a verb that expresses a strong emotion of sadness or grief. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe a scene of mourning or lamentation. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the mourning of the disciples after the death of Jesus.
