Word: κλασας

Etymology: The word κλασας is derived from the Greek verb κλάω (kláō), which means "to break" or "to shatter". 

Grammar: κλασας is a noun in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender. 

Description: κλασας is not a commonly used word in modern Greek. However, it can be found in ancient Greek texts and refers to a fragment or piece that has been broken off from a larger object. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is broken or shattered emotionally or mentally.
