Word: κληθησεται

Etymology:
The word κληθησεται is a Greek verb in the future passive indicative tense. It is derived from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The addition of the prefix κλη- (kle-) intensifies the meaning of the root word, giving it a sense of being "called upon" or "summoned".

Grammar:
As mentioned, κληθησεται is a Greek verb in the future passive indicative tense. This means that it describes an action that will happen in the future, and that the subject of the verb will receive the action rather than perform it. The verb is conjugated for the third person singular, indicating that the subject is a singular entity.

Description:
The word κληθησεται can be translated to English as "will be called" or "will be summoned". It implies that the subject of the verb will be given a name or title, or that they will be called upon to perform a task or duty. The word has a sense of formality and importance, as it suggests that the subject is being recognized or acknowledged in some way.
