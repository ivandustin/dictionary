Word: κλεισθωσιν

Etymology: κλεισθωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb κλείω (kleiō), which means "to close" or "to shut". The suffix -θωσιν (-thōsin) is a third-person plural aorist passive subjunctive ending, indicating that the action of closing is being done to them.

Grammar: κλεισθωσιν is a third-person plural aorist passive subjunctive form of the verb κλείω (kleiō). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb.

Description: κλεισθωσιν can be translated as "let them be closed" or "may they be closed". It is a form of the verb κλείω (kleiō), which means "to close" or "to shut".
