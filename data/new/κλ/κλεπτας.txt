Word: κλέπτας

Etymology:
The word κλέπτας (kleptas) is derived from the Greek verb κλέπτω (klepto), which means "to steal". It is a present participle form of the verb, meaning "stealing" or "thief".

Grammar:
In Greek, κλέπτας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the noun, meaning "thief". The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (κλέπτα), accusative (κλέπτη), and dative (κλέπτῃ).

Description:
κλέπτας is a common Greek word that refers to a person who steals or commits theft. The word can be used in a literal sense to describe someone who steals physical objects, or in a figurative sense to describe someone who takes something that does not belong to them, such as ideas or credit. The word can also be used in a broader sense to describe someone who is dishonest or untrustworthy.
