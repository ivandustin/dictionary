Word: κληρονόμους

Etymology: The word κληρονόμους comes from the Greek words κλῆρος (klēros) meaning "lot" or "inheritance" and νόμος (nomos) meaning "law". Together, κληρονόμους means "inheritors" or "heirs".

Grammar: κληρονόμους is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. κληρονόμους is a masculine noun in the accusative case.

Description: κληρονόμους refers to people who inherit something, usually property or money, from a deceased person. In Greek culture, inheritance was an important aspect of family life and was often regulated by law. The term can also be used metaphorically to refer to someone who inherits a particular trait or characteristic from their ancestors.
