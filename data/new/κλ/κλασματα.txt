Word: κλασματα

Etymology:
The word κλασματα (klasmata) is derived from the Greek verb κλάω (klao), which means "to break" or "to shatter". The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a plural noun ending, which indicates that the word refers to multiple broken pieces.

Grammar:
κλασματα is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a neuter noun. In the nominative case, the word is spelled as κλάσματα (klasmata).

Description:
The word κλασματα refers to broken pieces or fragments of something. It can be used to describe physical objects that have been shattered or broken into smaller pieces, as well as abstract concepts that have been fragmented or divided. The word is often used in the context of mathematics to refer to fractions or ratios, which can be thought of as broken pieces of a whole.
