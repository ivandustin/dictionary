Word: κλαυδιον

The word κλαυδιον is derived from the Greek word κλαυδιος (kladios), which means "broken off" or "cut off". It is believed to have originated from the verb κλαω (klao), which means "to break" or "to cut".

κλαυδιον is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the second declension. The genitive form of κλαυδιον is κλαυδιου (kladiou).

κλαυδιον is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a small branch or twig that has been cut off from a tree.
