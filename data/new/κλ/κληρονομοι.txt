Word: κληρονομοι

Etymology:
The word κληρονομοι (klēronomoi) is derived from the Greek words κλῆρος (klēros) meaning "lot" or "inheritance" and νομός (nomos) meaning "law" or "custom". Therefore, κληρονομοι literally means "inheritance law" or "inheritance custom".

Grammar:
κληρονομοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative case of the word, which means it is the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (κληρονόμων), dative (κληρονόμοις), and accusative (κληρονόμους).

Description:
κληρονομοι refers to heirs or inheritors, people who receive an inheritance from a deceased person. In ancient Greece, inheritance was an important aspect of society, and the laws and customs surrounding it were highly regulated. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to people who inherit or receive something from a previous generation or era.
