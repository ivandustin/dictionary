Word: κληρονομει

Etymology:
The word κληρονομει (klēronomei) is derived from the Greek words κλῆρος (klēros) meaning "lot" or "inheritance" and νέμω (nemō) meaning "to distribute" or "to allot". The word κληρονομει is a present tense verb form of the word κληρονομέω (klēronomeō), which means "to inherit" or "to receive an inheritance".

Grammar:
κληρονομει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb κληρονομέω. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Present tense:
- Κληρονομώ (klēronomō) - I inherit
- Κληρονομείς (klēronomeis) - You inherit
- Κληρονομεί (klēronomei) - He/she/it inherits
- Κληρονομούμε (klēronomoúme) - We inherit
- Κληρονομείτε (klēronomeíte) - You all inherit
- Κληρονομούν (klēronomoún) - They inherit

Description:
The word κληρονομει refers to the act of inheriting or receiving an inheritance. It can be used in a literal sense, such as inheriting property or money from a family member, or in a figurative sense, such as inheriting a talent or trait from a parent. The word is often used in legal contexts, such as in wills and estate planning.
