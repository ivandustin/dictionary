Word: κλινειν

The word κλινειν comes from the Ancient Greek verb κλίνω (klinō), which means "to lean", "to incline", or "to bend". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ḱley- ("to lean").

κλινειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. In Ancient Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present active infinitive is used to express an action that is ongoing or habitual.

Description:
κλινειν is a verb that describes the action of leaning or inclining. It can be used in a literal sense, such as leaning against a wall or inclining one's head, or in a figurative sense, such as inclining towards a particular opinion or belief.
