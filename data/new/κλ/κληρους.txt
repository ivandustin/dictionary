The word "κληρους" (klērous) is a Greek noun that means "lot" or "portion". It is derived from the verb "κληρόω" (klēroō), which means "to cast lots" or "to allot". In ancient Greece, lots were often cast to determine the outcome of events or to distribute property or other goods. 

In terms of grammar, "κληρους" is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases and numbers, such as the genitive singular "κλήρου" (klērou) or the accusative plural "κλήρους" (klērous).

Overall, "κληρους" is a word that is closely associated with the concept of chance or fate, as well as with the idea of distribution or allocation.
