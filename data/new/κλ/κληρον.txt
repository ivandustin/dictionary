Word: κληρον

Etymology:
The word κληρον (klēron) is derived from the Greek word κλῆρος (klēros), which means "lot" or "inheritance". In ancient Greece, a κλῆρος was a piece of land that was distributed by lot to citizens.

Grammar:
κληρον is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun in the singular form. The plural form of κληρον is κληρονομίαι (klēronomiai).

Description:
κληρον is a term that is often used in the context of inheritance or legacy. It refers to something that is passed down from one generation to the next, such as property, wealth, or knowledge. In ancient Greece, the concept of κλῆρος was closely tied to the idea of citizenship and the distribution of land. Today, the term κληρον is still used in modern Greek to refer to inheritance or legacy.
