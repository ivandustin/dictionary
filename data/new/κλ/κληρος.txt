Word: κληρος

Etymology:
The word κληρος comes from the Ancient Greek κλῆρος (klêros), which means "lot" or "portion". In Ancient Greece, a κλῆρος was a small piece of land that was distributed by lot to citizens.

Grammar:
In Greek, κληρος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is κληρος, and its genitive singular form is κληρου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
κληρος can refer to a variety of things, including a lot or portion of land, an inheritance, or a share of something. In Ancient Greece, the concept of κλῆρος was important in the distribution of land and property. Today, the word is still used in Greek to refer to an inheritance or a share of something.
