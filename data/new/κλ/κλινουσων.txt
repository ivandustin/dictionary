The word "κλινουσων" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "κλίνω" (klinó), which means "to lean" or "to incline". The participle form indicates ongoing action, so "κλινουσων" would translate to "leaning" or "inclining". 

In terms of grammar, "κλινουσων" is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship to another noun in the sentence. It can also function as an adjective, modifying a noun to indicate that it is "leaning" or "inclining". 

Overall, "κλινουσων" is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe ongoing action or possession/relationship.
