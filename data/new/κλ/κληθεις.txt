Word: κληθεις

Etymology:
The word κληθεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The verb κληθεις is the aorist passive participle of καλέω, which means "having been called" or "having been summoned".

The word κληθεις is used to describe someone or something that has been called or summoned. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a legal setting where a person is summoned to appear in court, or in a religious context where a person is called to serve a higher purpose.
