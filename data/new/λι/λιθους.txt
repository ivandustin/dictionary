Word: λιθους

Etymology:
The word λιθους (lithous) is a Greek word that means "stone" or "rock". It is derived from the Greek word λίθος (lithos), which also means "stone".

Grammar:
λιθους is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender.

Description:
λιθους is a common Greek word that refers to a solid, naturally occurring substance composed of minerals or mineral-like substances. It can refer to a small stone or a large rock. In ancient times, stones were used for building, as weapons, and for various other purposes. Today, stones are still used for construction, decoration, and as a source of minerals.
