The word "λιθιναις" (lithinais) is a feminine plural form of the Greek adjective "λιθινός" (lithinos), which means "made of stone" or "stone-like". The word is derived from the noun "λίθος" (lithos), which means "stone". 

In terms of grammar, "λιθιναις" is in the feminine gender, indicating that it modifies a feminine noun. The plural form suggests that it is describing multiple objects or entities that are made of stone or have a stone-like quality.

Overall, "λιθιναις" is a descriptive word that can be used to characterize various things, such as buildings, sculptures, or even natural formations like rocks or cliffs.
