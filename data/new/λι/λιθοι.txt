Word: λιθοι

Etymology: 
The word λιθοι (lithoi) is a plural form of the Greek word λίθος (lithos), which means "stone" or "rock". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European language, where it was derived from the root word *leit-, meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar:
In Greek, λιθοι is a plural noun, which means it refers to more than one stone or rock. It belongs to the second declension, and its nominative singular form is λίθος (lithos). The word can be declined in various cases, such as genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
λιθοι is a common word in Greek, and it is used to refer to stones or rocks of various sizes and shapes. It can be used in a literal sense, to describe natural formations such as mountains, cliffs, or boulders, or in a figurative sense, to describe something that is hard, unyielding, or unchanging.
