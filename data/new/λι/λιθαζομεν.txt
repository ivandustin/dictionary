Word: λιθαζομεν

Etymology: λιθαζομεν is a Greek verb that comes from the noun λίθος (lithos), meaning "stone". The verb λιθαζομεν (lithazomen) means "to stone" or "to pelt with stones".

Grammar: λιθαζομεν is a present tense, first person plural verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb λιθάζω (lithazo), which means "to stone".

Description: λιθαζομεν is a verb that describes the act of throwing stones at someone or something. It is often used in the context of punishment or retribution, and is commonly associated with the practice of stoning in ancient times. Today, the verb is still used in modern Greek to describe the act of throwing stones, but it is also used metaphorically to describe verbal attacks or criticism.
