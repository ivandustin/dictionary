Word: λιπαρά

Etymology:
The word λιπαρά (lipará) is derived from the Greek word λίπος (lípos), which means "fat" or "grease". The suffix -άρα (-ára) is a feminine suffix used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
λιπαρά is an adjective in the feminine plural form. It is used to describe a noun as "fatty" or "greasy".

Description:
λιπαρά is a Greek adjective that is commonly used to describe food that is high in fat or oil. It can also be used to describe a person's skin or hair as oily. In general, the word has a negative connotation and is often used to describe something that is unhealthy or unappealing.
