Word: λιμοι

Etymology:
The word λιμοι (limoi) is derived from the Greek noun λιμός (limos), which means "hunger" or "famine". The word is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₁- ("to be weak, sick").

Grammar:
λιμοι is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is classified as a masculine noun. The nominative singular form of the word is λιμός (limos).

Description:
λιμοι refers to a state of extreme hunger or famine. In ancient Greece, famines were common due to poor agricultural practices and natural disasters such as droughts. The word is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a lack or scarcity of something, such as resources or opportunities. In modern Greek, the word is still used to refer to hunger or famine, but it can also be used to describe a strong desire or craving for something.
