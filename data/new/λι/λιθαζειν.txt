Word: λιθαζειν

Etymology: The word λιθαζειν comes from the Greek word λιθάζειν (lithazein), which means "to stone" or "to pelt with stones". It is derived from the word λίθος (lithos), which means "stone".

Grammar: λιθαζειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It is a compound word made up of the prefix λιθο- (litho-) meaning "stone" and the verb αζειν (azein) meaning "to hurl" or "to throw". The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Singular: λιθάζω (lithazo) - I stone, you stone, he/she/it stones
- Plural: λιθάζομεν (lithazomen) - we stone, you stone, they stone

Description: λιθαζειν refers to the act of throwing stones at someone or something. It is often used in the context of punishment or execution, as stoning was a common form of punishment in ancient times. The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone is being criticized or attacked by a group of people.
