Word: λιτραν

Etymology: The word λιτραν comes from the Greek word λίτρα (lítra), which means a measure of weight or capacity. It is derived from the Latin word libra, which means pound.

Grammar: λιτραν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description: λιτραν refers to a unit of measurement used in ancient Greece, equivalent to approximately 327 grams or 11.5 ounces. It was commonly used to measure dry goods such as grain or flour. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a liter, a unit of volume measurement.
