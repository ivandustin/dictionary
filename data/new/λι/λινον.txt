Word: λινον

Etymology:
λινον (linon) is a Greek word that means "flax" or "linen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *lenH- which means "flax, linen".

Grammar:
λινον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its nominative singular form is λινον and its genitive singular form is λινου.

Description:
λινον refers to the plant flax and the fabric linen that is made from it. Flax is a tall, slender plant with blue flowers that is grown for its fiber. The fiber is used to make linen, which is a lightweight, breathable fabric that is often used for clothing and household textiles. In ancient Greece, linen was a highly prized fabric and was often used for clothing worn by the wealthy.
