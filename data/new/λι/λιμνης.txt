Word: λιμνης

Etymology: λιμνης is a Greek word that means "lake". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *lak-, which means "water".

Grammar: λιμνης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender.

Description: λιμνης refers to a large body of water that is surrounded by land. It is a natural or artificial basin that is filled with water and can be used for various purposes such as fishing, swimming, and boating. In Greek mythology, there are several lakes that are associated with different gods and goddesses.
