Word: λιθινά

Etymology: The word λιθινά (lithina) comes from the Greek word λίθος (lithos), which means "stone". The suffix -ινα (-ina) is added to indicate that something is made of or resembles the root word.

Grammar: λιθινά is an adjective in the feminine plural form. It is used to describe something that is made of stone or has the appearance of stone.

Description: λιθινά is often used to describe buildings, statues, or other structures that are made of stone. It can also be used to describe the texture or appearance of something that resembles stone, such as a piece of pottery or a painting. In modern Greek, the word λιθινά can also be used to describe something that is very hard or unyielding, similar to the qualities of stone.
