Word: λιμον

Etymology:
The word λιμον (limon) is derived from the ancient Greek word λείμων (leimon), which means "meadow" or "pasture". It is believed that the word λιμον was originally used to refer to a meadow or pasture that was particularly rich in grass and other vegetation.

Grammar:
In Greek, λιμον is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is λιμον, and its genitive singular form is λιμονος. The word can be used in both the singular and plural forms.

Description:
λιμον is a Greek word that refers to a meadow or pasture. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe a peaceful and idyllic setting. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to a place of abundance or plenty.
