Etymology: The word "τοξον" (toxon) comes from the Ancient Greek word meaning "bow". 

Grammar: "Τοξον" is a neuter noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its nominative singular form is "τοξον". 

Description: "Τοξον" refers to a bow, a weapon used for shooting arrows. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the shape of a curve or arc.
