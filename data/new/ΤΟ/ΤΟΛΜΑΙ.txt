Etymology: The word "τολμάω" (tolmao) comes from the ancient Greek verb "τολμάω" (tolmao), which means "to dare" or "to have courage". 

Grammar: "Τολμάω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ τολμάω (I dare), εσύ τολμάς (you dare), αυτός/αυτή/αυτό τολμάει (he/she/it dares), εμείς τολμάμε (we dare), εσείς τολμάτε (you dare), αυτοί/αυτές τολμάνε (they dare).

Description: "Τολμάω" is a verb that expresses the act of daring or having the courage to do something. It implies taking risks or facing challenges without fear. It can be used in various contexts to describe someone's bravery or willingness to do something that others might find difficult or dangerous.
