Etymology: The word "τοιούτοι" (toioútoi) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the demonstrative pronoun "τοιοῦτος" (toioûtos), meaning "such" or "like this," and the plural form of the definite article "οἱ" (hoi), meaning "the." 

Grammar: "Τοιούτοι" is the plural form of the word "τοιοῦτος" (toioûtos). It is used as a pronoun or an adjective to refer to things or people that are similar or of the same kind. In Greek grammar, it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.
