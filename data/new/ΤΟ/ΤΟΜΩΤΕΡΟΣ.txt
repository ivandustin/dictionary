Etymology: The word "τομωτερος" comes from the Greek word "τομή" (tomē), meaning "cut" or "section". It is derived from the verb "τέμνω" (temnō), which means "to cut".

Grammar: "τομωτερος" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine form and in the nominative case. It is used to describe a noun as being "more cut" or "more sectioned".

Description: "τομωτερος" is used to compare the degree of being cut or sectioned. It implies that something is more cut or sectioned than something else. It can be used to describe physical objects that have been divided into parts or abstract concepts that have been analyzed or broken down.
