Etymology: The word "τοσαυτήν" (tosautēn) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the words "τόσος" (tosos), meaning "so much," and "αὕτη" (autē), meaning "this." 

Grammar: "Τοσαυτήν" is a feminine singular accusative form of the adjective "τοσαύτης" (tosautēs). In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "τοσαυτήν" is used to modify a feminine singular accusative noun.

Description: "Τοσαυτήν" is used to indicate a specific amount or degree of something. It can be translated as "so much" or "such." It is often used in comparison or to emphasize the extent of something.
