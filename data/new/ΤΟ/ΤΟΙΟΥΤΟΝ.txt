Etymology: The word "τοιούτον" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the words "τοιοῦτος" (toioutos) and "ὅτι" (hoti), which mean "such" and "that" respectively.

Grammar: "τοιούτον" is a neuter singular adjective in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "τοιούτον" is modifying a neuter noun.

Description: "τοιούτον" is used to describe something that is of a similar kind or nature. It is often translated as "such" or "like this" in English. It is commonly used in Greek literature and speech to refer to something previously mentioned or to introduce an example or comparison.
