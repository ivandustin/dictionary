Etymology: The word "τοιούτους" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the words "τοιοῦτος" (toioutos) and the suffix "-ους" (-ous). "Τοιοῦτος" means "such" or "this kind of" in Greek.

Grammar: "Τοιούτους" is a masculine plural form of the adjective "τοιοῦτος" (toioutos). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the nouns they modify. In this case, "τοιούτους" is used to describe masculine plural nouns.

Description: "Τοιούτους" is used to refer to a group of people or things that are of a similar kind or nature. It implies that the objects or individuals being referred to possess certain qualities or characteristics that make them similar or comparable.
