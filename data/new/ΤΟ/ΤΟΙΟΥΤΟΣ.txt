Etymology: The word "τοιούτος" (toioútos) comes from the combination of the Greek words "τοιοῦτος" (toioûtos), meaning "such," and "οὗτος" (hoûtos), meaning "this." 

Grammar: "Τοιούτος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

Description: "Τοιούτος" is used to describe something or someone that is of a similar kind or nature. It is often translated as "such," "like this," or "of this kind." It is commonly used in Greek to refer to a specific person or thing that has been previously mentioned or is known to the speaker and listener.
