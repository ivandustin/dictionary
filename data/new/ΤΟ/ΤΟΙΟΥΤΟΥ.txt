Etymology: The word "τοιούτου" (toiotou) is a Greek term that comes from the combination of the demonstrative pronoun "τοιοῦτος" (toioutos), meaning "such" or "like this," and the genitive form of the pronoun "οὗτος" (houtos), meaning "this." 

Grammar: "Τοιούτου" is the genitive singular form of the word "τοιοῦτος" (toioutos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Τοιούτου" is used to describe something that is of a similar nature or quality to something previously mentioned or implied. It is often used in formal or literary contexts to refer to a specific type or kind of thing.
