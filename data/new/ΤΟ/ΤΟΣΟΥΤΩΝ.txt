Etymology: The word "τοσούτων" (tosouton) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the words "τόσος" (tosos), meaning "so much," and "τον" (ton), which is a form of the definite article "ο" (o) meaning "the." 

Grammar: "Τοσούτων" is a neuter plural form of the adjective "τόσος" (tosos), which means "so much" or "so many." In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "τοσούτων" is used to modify a plural neuter noun.

Description: "Τοσούτων" is used to express a large or significant quantity or number of something. It can be used to describe both tangible and intangible things. For example, "τοσούτων χρόνων" (tosouton chronon) means "so many years," and "τοσούτων ανθρώπων" (tosouton anthropon) means "so many people."
