Etymology: The word "τολμησαί" comes from the Greek verb "τολμάω" (tolmao), which means "to dare" or "to have courage". 

Grammar: "τολμησαί" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "τολμάω" (tolmao). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that is not conjugated for tense, mood, or person.

Description: "τολμησαί" is used to express the idea of daring or having the courage to do something. It can be translated as "to dare" or "to have the courage" in English.
