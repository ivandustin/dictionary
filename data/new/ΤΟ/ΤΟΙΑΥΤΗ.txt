Etymology: The word "τοιαύτη" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the demonstrative pronoun "τοιαύτη" (toiaúti), meaning "such" or "like this," and the feminine form of the definite article "η" (i), meaning "the." 

Grammar: "τοιαύτη" is a feminine singular form of the adjective in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this form, it is used to describe a feminine singular noun.

Description: "τοιαύτη" is used to indicate that something or someone is of a particular kind or quality, similar to what has been mentioned or implied before. It is often translated as "such" or "like this" in English.
