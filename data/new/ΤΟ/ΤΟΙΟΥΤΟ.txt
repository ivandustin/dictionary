Etymology: The word "τοιούτο" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the words "τοιοῦτος" (toioutos) and "οὗτος" (houtos), which mean "such" and "this" respectively.

Grammar: "τοιούτο" is a pronoun in Greek. It is used to refer to something or someone that has been previously mentioned or is known to the speaker. It is declined according to gender, number, and case.

Description: "τοιούτο" is used to describe something or someone that is of a similar kind or nature to what has been mentioned before. It can be translated as "such," "like this," or "of this kind." It is often used to make comparisons or to give examples.
