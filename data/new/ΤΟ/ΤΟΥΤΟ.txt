Etymology: The word "τουτο" (tuto) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "τοῦτο" (tuto), which means "this" or "that."

Grammar: In Greek, "τουτο" (tuto) is a pronoun that is used to refer to something that is close to the speaker or has been previously mentioned. It is the neuter form of the demonstrative pronoun "οὗτος" (outos).

Description: "τουτο" (tuto) is a versatile word that can be used to point out or refer to objects, people, or ideas. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing to indicate something specific or to draw attention to a particular thing.
