Etymology: The word "τουτου" (pronounced "toutou") comes from the Greek language. It is a demonstrative pronoun that means "this" or "this one."

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "τουτου" is the genitive form of the pronoun "αυτός" (pronounced "aftos"), which means "this" or "this one." The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "τουτου" is used to refer to something that is close to the speaker or the person being addressed. It is often used to point out or identify a specific object or person. In English, it can be translated as "this" or "this one."
