Word: εφαγεν

Etymology:
The word εφαγεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φαγεῖν (phagein), which means "to eat." The prefix ε- (e-) is a past tense marker, indicating that the action of eating has already taken place. Therefore, εφαγεν means "he/she/it ate" in the past tense.

Grammar:
εφαγεν is a third-person singular past tense verb in the active voice. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of the verb.

Description:
εφαγεν is a simple and common verb in Greek that is used to describe the act of eating in the past tense. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Εφαγεν ένα μήλο" (Efagen ena milo), which means "He/she/it ate an apple." The verb can also be conjugated in different tenses, moods, and voices to express different meanings and nuances.
