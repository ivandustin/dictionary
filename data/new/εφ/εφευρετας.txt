Word: εφευρετας

Etymology:
The word εφευρετας (efevretas) comes from the Greek verb εφευρίσκω (efevriskó), which means "to invent" or "to discover". The suffix -της (-tis) is added to the verb stem to form the noun εφευρετας, which means "inventor" or "discoverer".

Grammar:
εφευρετας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or the predicate nominative.

Description:
εφευρετας refers to a person who invents or discovers something new. It can be used to describe someone who creates a new device, process, or idea. The word implies a certain level of creativity and ingenuity, as well as a willingness to take risks and explore new possibilities. In Greek mythology, there were many famous εφευρετας, such as Daedalus, who invented wings to escape from the labyrinth, and Hephaestus, the god of fire and metalworking.
