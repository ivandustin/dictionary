The word "εφοβουμην" (efoboumen) is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root word "φοβέομαι" (phobeomai), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid." 

The prefix "ε-" (e-) indicates the present tense, while the suffix "-μην" (-men) indicates the passive voice and indicative mood. Therefore, "εφοβουμην" can be translated as "I am being feared" or "I am afraid." 

In terms of grammar, "εφοβουμην" is a first-person singular verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.
