Word: εφαγομεν

Etymology: εφαγομεν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb φάγω (phago), which means "to eat". The word comes from the ancient Greek verb ἔφαγον (ephagon), which is the aorist tense of φάγω.

Grammar: εφαγομεν is the first person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood of the verb φάγω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: εφαγομεν means "we ate" in English. It is a past tense verb that describes a completed action of eating. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "εφαγομεν μαζί το βράδυ" (we ate together in the evening).
