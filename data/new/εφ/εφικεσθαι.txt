The word "εφικεσθαι" (epikesesthai) is a Greek verb that means "to arrive" or "to come to". It is derived from the prefix "epi-" which means "upon" or "to" and the verb "keimai" which means "to lie" or "to be situated". 

In terms of grammar, "εφικεσθαι" is a middle voice verb, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is also in the present tense and the infinitive form.

In terms of description, "εφικεσθαι" is often used in Greek literature and mythology to describe the arrival of a hero or a god. It can also be used in everyday language to describe someone arriving at a destination or reaching a goal.
