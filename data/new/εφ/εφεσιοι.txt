Word: εφεσιοι

Etymology: The word εφεσιοι comes from the Greek word Εφέσιος (Ephesios), which means "belonging to Ephesus". Ephesus was an ancient Greek city located in modern-day Turkey.

Grammar: εφεσιοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is the plural form of εφεσιος (Ephesios). In English, it can be translated as "Ephesians".

Description: εφεσιοι refers to the people who lived in the ancient Greek city of Ephesus. The Ephesians were known for their devotion to the goddess Artemis and their impressive temple dedicated to her. The Apostle Paul also wrote a letter to the Ephesians, which is now a book in the New Testament of the Bible. Today, the ruins of Ephesus are a popular tourist attraction in Turkey.
