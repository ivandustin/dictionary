Word: εφυλαξεν

Etymology: εφυλαξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φυλάσσω (phylássō), which means "to guard" or "to protect." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εφυλαξεν means "he/she/it guarded" or "he/she/it protected."

Grammar: εφυλαξεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb φυλάσσω (phylássō). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The -εν (-en) ending indicates the past tense, and the ε (e) at the beginning indicates the third person singular.

Description: εφυλαξεν means "he/she/it guarded" or "he/she/it protected." It is a past tense verb, indicating that the action of guarding or protecting has already happened. The context in which the word is used will determine the exact meaning and implications of the action.
