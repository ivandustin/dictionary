Word: εφυγεν

Etymology:
The word εφυγεν (efygen) is a past tense form of the Greek verb φεύγω (fevgo), which means "to flee" or "to escape". The verb comes from the ancient Greek word φυγή (fygi), which means "flight" or "escape".

Grammar:
εφυγεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb φεύγω (fevgo). It is used to describe an action that happened in the past and was completed at a specific time. In Greek, the past tense is formed by adding specific endings to the stem of the verb.

Description:
εφυγεν is a verb that describes an action of fleeing or escaping that happened in the past. It can be used to describe a physical escape, such as running away from danger, or a metaphorical escape, such as avoiding a difficult situation. The word is commonly used in everyday Greek language and is an important part of the Greek vocabulary.
