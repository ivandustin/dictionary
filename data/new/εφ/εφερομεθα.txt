Word: εφερομεθα

Etymology:
The word εφερομεθα is derived from the Greek verb φέρω (fero), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the verb is in the passive voice, meaning "we are carried" or "we are borne".

Grammar:
εφερομεθα is a first-person plural passive form of the verb φέρω (fero). It is in the present tense and indicative mood. The suffix -μεθα (-meta) indicates that the verb is in the middle voice, which is a type of passive voice that indicates the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description:
εφερομεθα is a Greek verb that means "we are carried" or "we are borne". It is often used to describe a passive action, where the subject is being carried or transported by someone or something else. This verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a physical journey or a metaphorical journey through life.
