Word: εφανησαν

Etymology:
The word εφανησαν is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word φαίνω (phaino), which means "to appear" or "to shine." The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the root word to indicate the past tense, making εφανησαν (efanisan) mean "they appeared" or "they shone."

Grammar:
εφανησαν is a third-person plural past tense verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated from the root word φαίνω (phaino) and the past tense marker ε- (e-). The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. 

Description:
εφανησαν is a verb that describes an action of appearing or shining in the past. It can be used to describe the appearance of a person, object, or event. The verb is often used in the context of religious or supernatural events, such as the appearance of angels or divine beings. It can also be used in a more literal sense, such as the appearance of the sun or stars in the sky.
