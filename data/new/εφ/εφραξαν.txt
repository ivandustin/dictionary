Word: εφραξαν

Etymology: The word εφραξαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb φράσσω (phrasso), which means "to block", "to obstruct", or "to close off". The verb comes from the ancient Greek noun φραγμός (phragmos), which means "a fence" or "a barrier".

Grammar: εφραξαν is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood of the verb φράσσω (phrasso). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: εφραξαν describes a past action where a group of people blocked or obstructed something. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was blocked or obstructed.
