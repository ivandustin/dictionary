Word: εφερον

Etymology:
The word εφερον (ef-er-on) is a Greek verb in the past tense, third person plural form of the verb φέρω (fero), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The root of the word is φερ-, which is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰer- meaning "to carry" or "to bear".

Grammar:
εφερον is a past tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description:
The word εφερον is used to describe an action that a group of people or things carried or bore in the past. For example, "Οι πολίτες εφερον τα βαρέλια στο λιμάνι" (The citizens carried the barrels to the port). The word can also be used metaphorically to describe carrying or bearing a burden or responsibility.
