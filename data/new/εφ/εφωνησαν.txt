Word: εφωνησαν

Etymology:
The word εφωνησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φωνή (phoné), which means "voice" or "sound". The prefix ε- (e-) is a common Greek prefix that can indicate "out" or "away". Therefore, εφωνησαν can be translated as "they called out" or "they shouted".

Grammar:
εφωνησαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb φωνέω (phonéo), which means "to call out" or "to shout". The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
εφωνησαν is a verb that describes a loud and forceful action of calling out or shouting. It can be used in various contexts, such as to get someone's attention, to express excitement or urgency, or to make a public announcement. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is still used in modern Greek language.
