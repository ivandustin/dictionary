Word: εφεισάτο

Etymology:
The word εφεισάτο is a verb in the aorist middle voice tense of the Greek verb "φείδομαι" (feidomai), which means "to spare" or "to have mercy". The word comes from the ancient Greek root "φειδος" (feidos), which means "pity" or "mercy".

Grammar:
εφεισάτο is a third-person singular aorist middle voice form of the verb "φείδομαι" (feidomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon itself or is being affected by the action.

The word εφεισάτο is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Luke (23:34), where it is translated as "he prayed" or "he begged".
