Word: εφυτευσα

Etymology: The word εφυτευσα comes from the Greek verb "φυτεύω" (fytevo), which means "to plant". The prefix "ε-" (e-) in this word indicates the past tense, so εφυτευσα means "I planted".

Grammar: εφυτευσα is a first-person singular past tense verb in Greek. It belongs to the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb stem is "φυτευσ-" (fytevs-), and the suffix "-α" (-a) indicates the first-person singular.

Description: εφυτευσα is a Greek verb that means "I planted". It is used to describe the action of planting something in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as gardening, agriculture, or even metaphorically to describe the act of establishing something new.
