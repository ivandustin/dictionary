Word: εφαγον

Etymology: εφαγον is a Greek verb that comes from the aorist tense of the verb φάγω (fago), which means "I eat". 

Grammar: εφαγον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb φάγω (fago), which means "I ate". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description: εφαγον is a past tense verb that describes the action of eating in the past. It is commonly used in Greek literature and conversation to describe a specific instance of eating that has already occurred.
