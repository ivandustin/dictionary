Word: εφρονειτε

Etymology: The word εφρονειτε comes from the Greek verb φρονέω (phroneo), which means "to think" or "to have an opinion". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the second person plural form, making εφρονειτε (efroneite) mean "you all think" or "you all have an opinion".

Grammar: εφρονειτε is a second person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are currently thinking or have an opinion about something.

Description: εφρονειτε is a command or exhortation to a group of people to think or have an opinion about something. It is often used in a religious or philosophical context, encouraging people to reflect on their beliefs and values.
