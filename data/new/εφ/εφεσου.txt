The word "εφεσου" (ephesou) is a Greek noun that refers to the city of Ephesus, an ancient Greek city located on the coast of modern-day Turkey. 

Etymology: The word "εφεσου" is derived from the Greek word "Εφέσιος" (Ephesios), which means "of Ephesus". The origin of the name Ephesus is uncertain, but it is believed to have been derived from the Hittite language.

Grammar: "Εφεσου" is a singular noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, it indicates that something belongs to or is related to Ephesus.

Description: Ephesus was an important city in ancient Greece, known for its impressive architecture, bustling port, and rich cultural heritage. It was home to one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, the Temple of Artemis, and was also a center of early Christianity, with the Apostle Paul visiting and writing to the Ephesian church. Today, the ruins of Ephesus are a popular tourist destination and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
