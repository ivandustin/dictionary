Word: εφθασεν

Etymology: The word εφθασεν comes from the Greek verb φθάνω (phthánō), which means "to arrive" or "to reach". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εφθασεν means "he/she/it arrived" or "he/she/it reached".

Grammar: εφθασεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εφθασεν is a simple and straightforward verb that describes the act of arriving or reaching a destination. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone's arrival at a location, the completion of a journey, or the attainment of a goal. The word has a sense of finality to it, as it implies that the action has been completed and the subject has reached their intended destination.
