Word: ουσαι

Etymology: ουσαι is the plural form of the Greek verb ουσα, which comes from the ancient Greek verb εἰμί (eimí), meaning "to be". 

Grammar: ουσαι is a third-person plural present active participle of the verb ουσα. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or act as independent verbs. The present active participle indicates that the action is currently happening and that the subject is actively performing the action.

Description: ουσαι can be translated as "being" or "existing". As a participle, it can be used to modify a noun, such as in the phrase "the existing trees". It can also be used as a verb, such as in the sentence "They are being".
