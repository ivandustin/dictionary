Word: ουσία (ousia)

Etymology: The word ουσία (ousia) comes from the ancient Greek word οὐσία (ousia), which means "substance" or "essence". It is derived from the verb εἶναι (einai), which means "to be".

Grammar: ουσία (ousia) is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural, and its nominative, genitive, and accusative forms are all the same.

Description: ουσία (ousia) can be translated as "substance", "essence", or "being". In philosophy, it refers to the fundamental nature of something, or what makes something what it is. In theology, it is used to describe the nature of God. In everyday language, it can refer to the material or physical components of something.
