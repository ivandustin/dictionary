Word: ουσίας

Etymology: Ουσίας comes from the Greek word "οὐσία" (ousía), which means "essence" or "substance".

Grammar: Ουσίας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in a sentence.

Description: Ουσίας refers to the substance or essence of something. It can also refer to the material or physical matter that makes up an object. In philosophy, it is often used to describe the fundamental nature of reality or existence. In chemistry, it can refer to the chemical composition of a substance.
