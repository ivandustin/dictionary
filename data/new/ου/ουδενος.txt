Word: ουδενος

Etymology:
The word ουδενος (oudenos) is derived from the Greek words ου (ou) meaning "not" and δεν (den) meaning "anything". Therefore, ουδενος literally means "not anything" or "nothing".

Grammar:
Ουδενος is a pronoun in the Greek language, which means it is used to replace a noun. It is in the genitive case, which means it is used to show possession or relationship to another noun in the sentence.

Description:
Ουδενος is a word that is used to express the absence of something or someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a sentence like "Δεν υπάρχει ουδενός είδους πρόβλημα" (Den yparchei oudenos eidous problima), which means "There is no kind of problem". It can also be used to express the idea of "no one" or "nobody", as in the sentence "Ουδενός δεν ξέρω το όνομα" (Oudenos den xero to onoma), which means "I don't know the name of anyone".
