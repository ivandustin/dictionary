Word: ουρανων

Etymology:
The word ουρανων (ouranon) comes from the Greek word ουρανός (ouranos), which means "sky" or "heaven". In Greek mythology, Ouranos was the god of the sky and the father of the Titans.

Grammar:
Ουρανων is the genitive form of ουρανός. It is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated as "of the sky" or "of heaven". 

Description:
Ουρανων is a word that is often used in Greek mythology and in religious contexts to refer to the sky or heaven. It is a poetic and evocative word that conveys a sense of awe and wonder at the vastness and beauty of the natural world. In modern Greek, it is also used in a more literal sense to refer to the physical sky or atmosphere.
