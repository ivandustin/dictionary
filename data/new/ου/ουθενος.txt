Word: ουθενος

Etymology:
The word ουθενος (outhenos) is derived from the Greek words οὐ (ou) meaning "not" and θένος (thenos) meaning "something". Therefore, the word ουθενος means "nothing" or "none".

Grammar:
In Greek, ουθενος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also used as an adjective to describe something that is non-existent or lacking.

Description:
Ουθενος is a word that is commonly used in Greek to express the absence of something or someone. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to the lack of physical objects or people, or in a figurative sense, such as when referring to the absence of a feeling or emotion. It is a word that is often used in philosophical and existential discussions, as it represents the concept of nothingness.
