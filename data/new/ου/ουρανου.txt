Word: ουρανός (ouranos)

Etymology: The word ουρανός comes from the ancient Greek word ουρανός (ouranos), which means "sky" or "heaven". In Greek mythology, Ouranos was the god of the sky and the father of the Titans.

Grammar: ουρανός is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case in this form. The genitive form is ουρανού (ouranou), the accusative form is ουρανό (ourano), and the vocative form is ουρανέ (ourane).

Description: ουρανός refers to the sky or the heavens, and is often used in Greek mythology to refer to the realm of the gods. It can also refer to the atmosphere or the celestial sphere. In modern Greek, it is also used to refer to the weather.
