Word: ουδεις

Etymology:
The word ουδεις (oudeis) is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words, ου (ou) meaning "not" and δεις (deis) meaning "anyone". Therefore, ουδεις (oudeis) means "no one" or "nobody".

Grammar:
Ουδεις (oudeis) is a pronoun in Greek that is used to refer to a person or thing that does not exist or is not present. It is declined in the same way as the masculine singular form of the adjective "no one" or "nobody".

Description:
Ουδεις (oudeis) is a word that is commonly used in Greek to express the absence of someone or something. It is often used in negative sentences to indicate that there is no one or nothing present. For example, "Ουδεις ήρθε στο πάρτι" (oudeis irthe sto parti) means "No one came to the party".
