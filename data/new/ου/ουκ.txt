Word: ουκ

Etymology: ουκ is a Greek word that comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ne, which means "not."

Grammar: ουκ is an adverb that means "not" or "no." It is often used in negative sentences to negate the verb. It is the negative counterpart to the Greek word "ναι" (yes).

Description: ουκ is a common word in the Greek language and is used in everyday conversation. It is an important word to know for anyone learning Greek, as it is used frequently in negative sentences.
