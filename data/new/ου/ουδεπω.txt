Word: ουδεπω

Etymology: ουδεπω is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the negative particle ου (ou) meaning "not" and the adverb δεπω (depō) meaning "yet" or "still". 

Grammar: ουδεπω is an adverb in Greek, meaning "not yet" or "still not". It is often used in the New Testament to describe events that have not yet occurred or situations that have not yet been resolved. 

Description: ουδεπω is a word that expresses a sense of anticipation or expectation. It suggests that something has not yet happened, but may happen in the future. It is often used in a hopeful or optimistic context, as it implies that a desired outcome is still possible.
