Word: ουθεν

Etymology:
The word ουθεν (outhen) is derived from the Greek words οὐ (ou) meaning "not" and θέν (then) meaning "something". Therefore, ουθεν (outhen) means "nothing" or "not anything".

Grammar:
Ουθεν (outhen) is a neuter noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. It can also be used as an adverb, meaning "in no way" or "not at all".

Description:
Ουθεν (outhen) is a common word in Greek, used to express the absence of something or the lack of anything. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a sentence like "Δεν έχω ουθεν να πω" (Den eho outhen na po), which means "I have nothing to say". It can also be used in a negative sentence, such as "Ουθεν δεν έγινε" (Outhen den egine), which means "Nothing happened".
