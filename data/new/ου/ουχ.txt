Word: ουχ (ouch)

Etymology: ουχ is a Greek word that comes from the ancient Greek word οὐχί (ouchi), which means "no" or "not."

Grammar: ουχ is an adverb that is used to negate a statement or question. It is often used in conjunction with other words to form negative phrases, such as ουχί μόνον (not only) or ουχ ὅτι (not that).

Description: ουχ is a common word in the Greek language and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express disagreement or to correct a statement. For example, if someone says "Αυτό είναι αλήθεια" (This is true), someone else might respond with "Ουχί, δεν είναι αλήθεια" (No, it's not true).
