The word "ουρανε" (ourane) is a Greek noun that means "heaven" or "sky". It is derived from the Greek word "οὐρανός" (ouranos), which also means "heaven" or "sky". In Greek mythology, Ouranos was the god of the sky and the father of the Titans. 

In terms of grammar, "ουρανε" is a singular noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case "ουρανού" (ouranou) or the accusative case "ουρανό" (ourano).

Overall, "ουρανε" is a simple yet significant word in Greek language and culture, representing the vast and infinite expanse of the sky and the divine realm of the gods.
