Word: ουδαμως

Etymology: ουδαμως comes from the Greek words ου (not) and δαμων (at all), meaning "not at all."

Grammar: ουδαμως is an adverb in Greek, meaning "not at all" or "by no means." It is often used to emphasize a negative statement.

Description: ουδαμως is a common word in Greek that is used to express a strong negative sentiment. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in response to a question or to express disagreement. It is often used in combination with other negative words, such as ουδε (neither) or μηδε (nor).
