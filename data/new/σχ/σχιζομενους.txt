Word: σχιζομένους

Etymology: The word σχιζομένους comes from the Greek verb σχίζω (skhizō), which means "to split" or "to divide". The suffix -μένους (-menous) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word describes an action that is currently happening.

Grammar: σχιζομένους is a present participle in the masculine accusative plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently splitting or dividing something. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives or as part of a verb tense.

Description: σχιζομένους can be translated to "those who are splitting/dividing". It is often used in the context of a group of people who are in the process of dividing something, such as property or resources.
