Word: σχισμα

Etymology:
The word σχισμα (schisma) comes from the Greek verb σχίζω (schizo), which means "to split" or "to divide." It is related to the English word "schism."

Grammar:
σχισμα is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is σχίσματα (schismata).

Description:
σχισμα refers to a division or split, often used in a religious or political context. It can also refer to a tear or a rift in a physical object. In Christian theology, the Great Schism refers to the split between the Eastern Orthodox Church and the Roman Catholic Church in 1054.
