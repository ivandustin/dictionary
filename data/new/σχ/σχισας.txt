Word: σχισας

Etymology:
The word σχισας (skhizas) is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word σχίζω (skhizo), which means "to split" or "to tear apart." 

Grammar:
As mentioned, σχισας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The participle mood is used to describe a verb as an adjective or noun. 

Description:
The word σχισας can be translated to mean "having split" or "having torn apart." It is often used in the context of physical objects being split or torn, such as clothing or paper. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe a relationship or situation that has been torn apart or broken. 

Example sentence:
Ο άνεμος σχίζει τα ρούχα μου. (O anemos skhizei ta roukha mou.) - The wind is tearing apart my clothes.
