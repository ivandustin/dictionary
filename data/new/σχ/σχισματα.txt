Word: σχισματα

Etymology:
The word σχισματα (schismata) is derived from the Greek verb σχίζω (schizo), which means "to split" or "to divide." The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a plural noun ending, which indicates that the word refers to multiple instances of splitting or division.

Grammar:
σχισματα is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word can be translated to mean "splits" or "divisions."

Description:
σχισματα is a word that refers to splits or divisions. It can be used in a literal sense to describe physical splits or breaks, such as in a piece of wood or a rock. It can also be used in a figurative sense to describe divisions between people, groups, or ideas. In the context of religion, σχισματα is often used to describe schisms or divisions within a church or religious community.
