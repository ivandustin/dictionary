The word "οθονιων" (othonion) is a Greek noun that refers to a curtain or a screen. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek word "οθονη" (othonē), which means a piece of cloth or a sheet.

Grammar: "Οθονιων" is a masculine noun in the genitive plural form. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of theater or cinema, referring to the screen on which the images are projected. It can also refer to a curtain used to separate different areas or to provide privacy.
