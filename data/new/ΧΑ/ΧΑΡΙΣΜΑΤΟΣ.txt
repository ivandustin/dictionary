Etymology: The word "χαρισματος" (charismatos) comes from the Greek word "χαρισμα" (charisma), which means "gift" or "favor". It is derived from the verb "χαριζομαι" (charizomai), meaning "to bestow" or "to grant".

Grammar: "Χαρισματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Χαρισματος" refers to a gift or talent that someone possesses. It can also be used to describe a special ability or quality that sets a person apart and makes them unique. In a religious context, it can refer to a spiritual gift or divine favor bestowed upon an individual.
