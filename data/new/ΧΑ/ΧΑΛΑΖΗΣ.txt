Etymology: The word "χαλαζης" (chálazis) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "χαλάζω" (chalázo), which means "to hail" or "to pelt with hailstones."

Grammar: In Greek, "χαλαζης" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be used as both a subject and a predicate in a sentence.

Description: "Χαλαζης" refers to the meteorological phenomenon of hail. It is the term used to describe the formation of ice pellets that fall from the sky during thunderstorms. Hailstones are typically larger than raindrops and can cause damage to crops, buildings, and vehicles.
