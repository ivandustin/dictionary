Grammar: In Greek, "χαριτα" is a noun in the plural form. It is the accusative case of the noun "χάρις" (charis). The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Χαριτα" is often translated as "graces" or "charms" in English. In Greek mythology, the Charites (also known as the Graces) were goddesses of charm, beauty, and grace.
