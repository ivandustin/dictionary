Etymology: The word "χαρισθηναι" (charisthenai) comes from the Greek verb "χαρίζομαι" (charizomai), which means "to grant, to bestow, to give freely." It is derived from the noun "χάρις" (charis), meaning "grace, favor, kindness."

Grammar: "Χαρισθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "χαρίζομαι" (charizomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Χαρισθηναι" is used to express the action of receiving or being granted something freely or as a favor. It implies a sense of generosity or kindness from the giver.
