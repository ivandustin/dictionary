Etymology: The word "χαρισμα" (charisma) comes from the Greek word "χάρις" (charis), which means "grace" or "favor". It is derived from the verb "χαίρω" (chairō), meaning "to rejoice" or "to be glad".

Grammar: In Greek, "χαρισμα" is a neuter noun of the first declension. It is singular and can be used in both the nominative and accusative cases. The plural form is "χαρίσματα" (charismata).

Description: "Χαρισμα" refers to a special gift or talent that someone possesses. It is often associated with a natural ability or skill that is considered exceptional or extraordinary. In a religious context, it can also refer to a spiritual gift or divine favor bestowed upon an individual by God.
