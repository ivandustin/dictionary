Etymology: The word "χασμα" (chasma) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "χαίνω" (chaino), which means "to gape" or "to open wide."

Grammar: In Greek, "χασμα" is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "χάσματα" (chas mata).

Description: "Χασμα" is a Greek word that translates to "chasm" in English. It refers to a deep, narrow opening or gap in the ground or a solid object. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a significant division or separation between two things, such as beliefs, opinions, or social groups.
