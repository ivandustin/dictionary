Etymology: The word "χαρησονται" (charēsontai) is derived from the Greek verb "χαιρω" (chairō), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad."

Grammar: "Χαρησονται" is the third person plural form of the future tense of the verb "χαιρω" (chairō). It is used to express that a group of people will rejoice or be glad in the future.

Description: "Χαρησονται" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of joy, happiness, or gladness. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to express the joy that comes from a positive outcome or a sense of fulfillment.
