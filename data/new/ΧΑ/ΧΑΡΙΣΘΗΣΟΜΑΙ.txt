Etymology: The word "χαρισθήσομαι" comes from the Greek verb "χαρίζομαι" (charízomai), which means "to be gracious" or "to show favor". It is derived from the noun "χάρις" (charis), meaning "grace" or "favor".

Grammar: "Χαρισθήσομαι" is a first-person singular future passive indicative form of the verb "χαρίζομαι" (charízomai). In Greek grammar, the future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action, and the indicative mood states a fact or reality.
