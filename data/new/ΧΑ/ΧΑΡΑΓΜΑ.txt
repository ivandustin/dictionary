Etymology: The word "χαραγμα" (charagma) comes from the Greek verb "χαράσσω" (charasso), which means "to engrave" or "to mark." It is derived from the noun "χάραξ" (charax), meaning "a pointed stake" or "a scratch."

Grammar: In Greek, "χαραγμα" is a neuter noun of the first declension. It is singular and can be used in both the nominative and accusative cases.

Description: "Χαραγμα" refers to a mark, engraving, or inscription made on a surface. It can also denote a stamp, seal, or brand. In a figurative sense, it can represent a distinguishing mark or characteristic.
