Etymology: The word "χαρισματων" (charismaton) comes from the Greek word "χαρισμα" (charisma), which means "gift" or "grace." It is derived from the verb "χαριζομαι" (charizomai), meaning "to give freely" or "to bestow."

Grammar: "Χαρισματων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "χαρισμα" (charisma). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. The plural form indicates that there are multiple gifts or graces being referred to.

Description: "Χαρισματων" is a Greek word that translates to "of gifts" or "of graces" in English. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to special abilities or talents that are believed to be given by a higher power.
