Etymology: The word "χαρησομαι" (charisomai) comes from the Greek root "χαρις" (charis), meaning "grace" or "favor". It is derived from the verb "χαιρω" (chairō), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad".

Grammar: "Χαρησομαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first person singular form of the future tense. It is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: χαίρω (chairō) - I rejoice
- Future tense: χαρήσομαι (charēsomai) - I will rejoice

Description: "Χαρησομαι" is a Greek verb that expresses the action of rejoicing or being glad. It conveys a sense of happiness, delight, or satisfaction. It is often used to express joy or pleasure in response to a positive event or situation.
