Etymology: The word "χαριζεσθαι" (pronounced: harizesthai) comes from the Greek verb "χαρίζομαι" (pronounced: harizomai), which means "to show favor" or "to grant." It is derived from the noun "χάρις" (pronounced: charis), meaning "grace" or "favor."

Grammar: "Χαριζεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "χαρίζομαι." In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that expresses the action or state without specifying any tense, mood, or person.
