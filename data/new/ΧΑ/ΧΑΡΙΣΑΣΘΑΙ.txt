Etymology: The word "χαρισασθαι" (charisasthai) comes from the Greek verb "χαρίζομαι" (charizomai), which means "to show favor" or "to grant." It is derived from the noun "χάρις" (charis), meaning "grace" or "favor."

Grammar: "Χαρισασθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "χαρίζομαι" (charizomai). The aorist tense indicates a past action, and the middle voice suggests that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Χαρισασθαι" is used to express the act of showing favor, granting something, or being gracious. It implies an act of kindness or generosity towards someone.
