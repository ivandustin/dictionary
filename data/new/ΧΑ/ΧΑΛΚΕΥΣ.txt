Etymology: The word "χαλκεύς" (chálkeus) comes from the Greek word "χαλκός" (chalkós), meaning "copper" or "bronze." In ancient Greece, a chálkeus was a craftsman who worked with copper or bronze.

Grammar: In Greek, "χαλκεύς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension, which means its ending changes depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Χαλκεύς" refers to a person who works with copper or bronze, such as a coppersmith or a bronze worker. In ancient times, chálkeus were highly skilled craftsmen who created various objects using these metals, including tools, weapons, and decorative items.
