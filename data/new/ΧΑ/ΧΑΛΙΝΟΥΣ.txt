Etymology: The word "χαλινούς" comes from the Greek word "χαλινός" (khalinós), which means "bridle" or "rein". 

Grammar: In Greek, "χαλινούς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "χαλινού" (khalinoú). 

Description: "Χαλινούς" refers to a type of bridle or rein used to control a horse or other animals. It is a device that is placed over the head of the animal and is used by the rider or handler to guide and control its movements.
