Etymology: The word "εκαστου" (ekastou) comes from the Greek word "εκαστος" (ekastos), which means "each" or "every".

Grammar: "Εκαστου" is a genitive form of the word "εκαστος" (ekastos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Εκαστου" is a Greek word that is commonly used to express the possessive form of "each" or "every". It is often used in phrases or sentences to indicate that something belongs to or is associated with each or every individual or item in a group.
