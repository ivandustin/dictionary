Etymology: The word "εκβολην" comes from the Greek word "εκβολή" (ekvoli), which means "outflow" or "discharge". It is derived from the verb "εκβάλλω" (ekvallo), meaning "to throw out" or "to expel".

Grammar: "Εκβολην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Εκβολην" refers to the act of flowing out or discharging, often used in the context of rivers or streams. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the release or expression of emotions or ideas.
