Etymology: The word "εκδικείς" (ekdikeis) comes from the Greek verb "εκδικέω" (ekdikeo), which means "to avenge" or "to seek justice". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "from", and the verb "δίκη" (diki), meaning "justice" or "judgment".

Grammar: "Εκδικείς" is the plural form of the adjective "εκδικητικός" (ekdikitikos), which means "vengeful" or "retributive". It is used to describe someone or something that seeks revenge or justice.
