Etymology: The word "εκτενως" (ektenos) comes from the Greek word "εκτεινω" (ekteino), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "away", and the verb "τεινω" (teino), meaning "to stretch".

Grammar: "Εκτενως" is an adverb in Greek. It is the adverbial form of the adjective "εκτενης" (ektenis), which means "extensive" or "thorough". It is used to describe the manner or extent of an action or state.

Description: "Εκτενως" is used to indicate that something is done in a comprehensive or thorough manner. It implies that a task or activity is carried out with great attention to detail and completeness. It can also be used to describe a state or condition that is extensive or far-reaching.
