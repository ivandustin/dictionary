Etymology: The word "εκρινεν" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (kríno), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the completion or the result of the action.

Grammar: "Εκρινεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "κρίνω" (kríno). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εκρινεν" can be translated as "he/she/it judged" or "he/she/it decided" in English. It refers to the act of making a judgment or decision.
