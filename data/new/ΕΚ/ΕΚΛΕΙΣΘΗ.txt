Etymology: The word "εκλεισθη" (ekleisthe) comes from the Greek verb "κλείνω" (kleino), which means "to close" or "to shut". The prefix "εκ" (ek) indicates the completion or result of the action, so "εκλεισθη" can be translated as "it was closed" or "it was shut".

Grammar: "εκλεισθη" is the third person singular form of the aorist passive tense of the verb "κλείνω" (kleino). The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a whole, without any specific duration. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.
