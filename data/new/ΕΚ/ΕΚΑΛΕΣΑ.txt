Etymology: The word "εκάλεσα" (ekalesa) comes from the Greek verb "καλώ" (kalo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the completion or result of the action.

Grammar: "Εκάλεσα" is the first person singular past tense of the verb "καλώ" (kalo). It is used to indicate that the speaker called or summoned someone in the past.

Description: "Εκάλεσα" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the action of calling or summoning someone in the past. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts.
