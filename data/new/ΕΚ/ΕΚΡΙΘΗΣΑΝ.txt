Etymology: The word "εκριθησαν" (ekrithisan) comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix "εκ" (ek) indicates a completed action, so "εκριθησαν" can be translated as "they were judged" or "they were decided".

Grammar: "εκριθησαν" is the third person plural, past tense, passive voice form of the verb "κρίνω" (krino). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, voice, and person. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon.

Description: "εκριθησαν" is a verb form that describes an action that was completed in the past and was done to a group of people or things. It implies that a judgment or decision was made about them by someone or something else.
