Etymology: The word "εκλεισθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "κλείνω" (kleíno), which means "to close" or "to shut". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates a completed action or a state of being. The suffix "-σθησαν" (-sthēsan) is a passive voice ending, indicating that the action of closing was done to the subject.

Grammar: "εκλεισθησαν" is the third person plural, past tense, passive voice form of the verb "κλείνω" (kleíno). It is used to describe an action of closing that was done to a plural subject.

Description: "εκλεισθησαν" means "they were closed" in English. It is used to indicate that a group of objects or entities were shut or closed by someone or something else.
