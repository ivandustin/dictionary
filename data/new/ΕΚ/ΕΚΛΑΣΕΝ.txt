Etymology: The word "εκλασεν" (eklasen) comes from the Greek verb "κλάω" (klao), which means "to break" or "to shatter". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kelh₂-.

Grammar: "εκλασεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "κλάω" (klao) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "εκλασεν" is a verb form that indicates that someone or something broke or shattered in the past. It can be used to describe physical objects breaking or metaphorical situations falling apart.
