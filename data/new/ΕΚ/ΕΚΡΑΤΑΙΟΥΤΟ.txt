Etymology: The word "εκραταιούτο" comes from the Greek verb "κραταιοῦν" (krataioún), which means "to strengthen" or "to make strong." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the action of "coming out" or "emerging." Therefore, "εκραταιούτο" can be translated as "it was made strong" or "it emerged strengthened."

Grammar: "εκραταιούτο" is the third person singular, past tense, passive voice form of the verb "κραταιοῦν" (krataioún). In Greek grammar, the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description: "εκραταιούτο" is a word in the Greek language that describes an action or state of being in the past. It suggests that something or someone was made strong or emerged strengthened. The exact context and meaning of the word would depend on the sentence or phrase in which it is used.
