Etymology: The word "εκλελησθε" comes from the Greek verb "εκλελησθαι" (eklelēsthai), which is a compound word formed by the prefix "εκ" (ek) meaning "out" or "from," and the verb "λησθαι" (lēsthai) meaning "to forget." Therefore, "εκλελησθε" can be translated as "you have forgotten" or "you have been forgotten."

Grammar: "εκλελησθε" is the second person plural form of the verb "εκλελησθαι" in the aorist tense. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they have forgotten something or have been forgotten.

Description: "εκλελησθε" is a verb form in the Greek language that expresses the action of forgetting or being forgotten. It is commonly used in various contexts to describe the act of not remembering or being overlooked.
