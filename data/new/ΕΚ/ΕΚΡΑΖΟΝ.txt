Etymology: The word "εκραζον" (ekrazon) comes from the Greek verb "κράζω" (krazo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in Greek often indicates an action that is done forcefully or intensively.

Grammar: "Εκραζον" is the present participle form of the verb "κράζω" (krazo) in the active voice. It is used to describe an ongoing action of shouting or crying out in the present tense.

Description: "Εκραζον" is a word that describes the act of shouting or crying out forcefully or intensively. It can be used to depict someone who is loudly expressing their emotions, calling for attention, or making a strong statement.
