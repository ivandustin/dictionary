Etymology: The word "εκεφαλιωσαν" comes from the Greek verb "κεφαλιώνω" (kefalióno), which means "to behead" or "to decapitate". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in Greek indicates "out" or "away", so "εκεφαλιώνω" literally means "to behead and remove the head".

Grammar: "εκεφαλιώσαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb "εκεφαλιώνω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence performs the action.

Description: "εκεφαλιώσαν" is a Greek word that describes the act of beheading or decapitating. It refers to the action of cutting off someone's head from their body. This word is often used in historical or literary contexts to describe violent acts or executions.
