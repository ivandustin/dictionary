Etymology: The word "εκρυψας" (ekrypsas) comes from the Greek verb "κρύπτω" (krypto), which means "to hide" or "to conceal." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the action of doing something outwards or away. 

Grammar: "Εκρυψας" is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "κρύπτω" (krypto). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence performs the action. The second person singular form is used when addressing one person.

Description: "Εκρυψας" translates to "you hid" or "you concealed" in English. It refers to the action of hiding or concealing something in the past.
