Etymology: The word "εκοινωνησαν" (ekoinonisan) is derived from the Greek verb "κοινωνέω" (koinoneo), which means "to communicate" or "to share." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the past tense, so "εκοινωνησαν" translates to "they communicated" or "they shared."

Grammar: "εκοινωνησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "κοινωνέω" in the past tense. It is used to refer to a group of people who communicated or shared something in the past.

Description: "εκοινωνησαν" is a Greek word that describes an action of communication or sharing that took place in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as interpersonal communication, sharing of information, or participating in a common activity.
