Etymology: The word "εκλασα" (eklasa) comes from the Greek verb "κλάω" (klao), which means "to break" or "to shatter". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kleh₂-.

Grammar: "Εκλασα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "κλάω" (klao) in Greek. It is used to indicate that the action of breaking or shattering occurred in the past and was performed by the speaker.

Description: "Εκλασα" is a verb in the Greek language that specifically refers to the act of breaking or shattering something. It is used to describe the action of causing an object to separate into pieces or fragments.
