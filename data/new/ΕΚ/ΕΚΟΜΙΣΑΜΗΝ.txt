Etymology: The word "εκομισαμην" comes from the Greek verb "κομίζω" (komízo), which means "to bring" or "to carry". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the completion or result of an action, so "εκομισαμην" can be translated as "I have brought" or "I have carried".

Grammar: "εκομισαμην" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in the middle voice in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description: "εκομισαμην" is a verb form that indicates that the speaker (first person singular) has completed the action of bringing or carrying something in the past. It is commonly used in Greek to express past actions or events.
