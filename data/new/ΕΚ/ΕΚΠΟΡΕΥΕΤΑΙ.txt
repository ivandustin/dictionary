Etymology: The word "εκπορευεται" (ekporevetai) comes from the Greek verb "εκπορεύομαι" (ekporevomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of", and the verb "πορεύομαι" (porevomai), meaning "to go" or "to travel".

Grammar: "Εκπορεύεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "εκπορεύομαι" (ekporevomai). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Εκπορεύεται" is a Greek word that can be translated to English as "he/she/it goes out" or "he/she/it comes forth". It describes the action of someone or something leaving or exiting a place.
