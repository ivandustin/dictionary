Etymology: The word "εκακωσαν" (ekakosan) comes from the Greek verb "κακόω" (kakoo), which means "to harm" or "to do evil". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates a completed action or a result. Therefore, "εκακωσαν" can be translated as "they harmed" or "they did evil".

Grammar: "εκακωσαν" is the third person plural, past tense form of the verb "κακόω" (kakoo). It is used to describe an action that was done by a group of people in the past.

Description: "εκακωσαν" is a Greek word that describes an action of causing harm or doing evil. It can be used in various contexts to indicate acts of wrongdoing or malicious behavior.
