Etymology: The word "εκτος" (ektos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the preposition "εκ" (ek), meaning "out of" or "from," combined with the adverb "τος" (tos), meaning "the." 

Grammar: In Greek, "εκτος" is an adverb. It is used to indicate exclusion or exception, meaning "except" or "apart from." It is often used in combination with the preposition "απο" (apo), meaning "from," to express the idea of "apart from" or "excluding."

It is used to indicate that something or someone is not included in a particular group, category, or action.
