Etymology: The word "εκρατησεν" (ekratēsen) comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (krateō), which means "to hold" or "to possess." The prefix "εκ" (ek) indicates the completion or accomplishment of the action.

Grammar: "εκρατησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "κρατέω" (krateō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "εκρατησεν" is a verb form that indicates an action that was completed in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it held" or "he/she/it possessed" in English.
