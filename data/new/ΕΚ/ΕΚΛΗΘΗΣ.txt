Etymology: The word "εκληθης" (eklēthēs) comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleō), which means "to call" or "to summon." The prefix "εκ" (ek) indicates the action of calling out or summoning from within.

Grammar: "Εκληθης" is the second person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleō). It is used to describe an action that was done to you in the past, specifically the action of being called or summoned.

Description: "Εκληθης" is a Greek word that means "you were called" or "you were summoned." It refers to the passive form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleō), indicating that the action of calling or summoning was done to you.
