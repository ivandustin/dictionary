Etymology: The word "εκοιμηθησαν" (ekimithisan) comes from the Greek verb "κοιμάμαι" (koimamai), which means "to sleep". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates a completed action, so "εκοιμηθησαν" (ekimithisan) can be translated as "they fell asleep".

Grammar: "εκοιμηθησαν" (ekimithisan) is the third person plural, aorist tense, passive voice form of the verb "κοιμάμαι" (koimamai). The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "εκοιμηθησαν" (ekimithisan) is a verb form that describes the action of falling asleep. It is used to refer to a group of people or things that fell asleep in the past.
