Etymology: The word "εκδυσαντες" comes from the Greek verb "εκδύω" (ekdyo), which means "to undress" or "to take off clothes". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "away", and the verb "δύω" (dyo), meaning "to put on" or "to wear".

Grammar: "Εκδυσαντες" is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the verb "εκδύω" (ekdyo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Εκδυσαντες" can be translated as "having undressed" or "having taken off clothes". It describes a group of people who have completed the action of undressing or removing their clothes.
