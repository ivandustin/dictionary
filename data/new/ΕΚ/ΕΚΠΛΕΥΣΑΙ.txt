Etymology: The word "εκπλευσαι" (ekpleusai) comes from the Greek verb "εκπλέω" (ekpleo), which means "to sail out" or "to set sail." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out," and the verb "πλέω" (pleo), meaning "to sail."

Grammar: "Εκπλευσαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "εκπλέω" (ekpleo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action.

Description: "Εκπλευσαι" is a verb form that expresses the action of sailing out or setting sail. It can be used to describe the act of leaving a port or starting a journey by sea.
