Etymology: The word "εκουφιζον" (ekoufizon) comes from the Greek verb "κουφίζω" (koufizo), which means "to lighten" or "to make something lighter." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the action of making something lighter or relieving it from weight.

Grammar: "Εκουφιζον" is the present participle form of the verb "κουφίζω" (koufizo) in the third person singular. It is used to describe an ongoing action of lightening or relieving something.

Description: "Εκουφιζον" describes the act of lightening or relieving something from weight. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically lightening a load or metaphorically relieving someone from a burden or stress.
