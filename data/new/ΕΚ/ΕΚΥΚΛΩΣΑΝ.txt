Etymology: The word "εκυκλωσαν" comes from the Greek verb "κυκλώνω" (kyklóno), which means "to encircle" or "to surround". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in Greek indicates "out" or "from", so "εκυκλωσαν" can be translated as "they encircled" or "they surrounded".

Grammar: "εκυκλωσαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "κυκλώνω" in the past tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings based on the tense, mood, and person. The "-αν" ending indicates the past tense and the third person plural.

Description: "εκυκλωσαν" is a verb in the Greek language that describes an action of encircling or surrounding. It can be used to express the act of forming a circle or enclosing something or someone.
