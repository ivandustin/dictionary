Etymology: The word "εκυλιετο" comes from the Greek verb "κυλιω" (kylio), which means "to roll" or "to turn." The prefix "εκ" (ek) indicates the action of rolling or turning outwards.

Grammar: "Εκυλιετο" is the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb "κυλιω" (kylio). In Greek grammar, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Εκυλιετο" describes the action of rolling or turning outwards. It can be used metaphorically to describe the movement or rotation of objects or concepts.
