Etymology: The word "εκελευον" (ekeloun) comes from the Greek verb "εκελευω" (ekeléo), which means "to command" or "to order".

Grammar: "Εκελευον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word "εκελευον" is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: "Εκελευον" refers to a command or an order given by someone in authority. It can also be used to describe an authoritative instruction or directive.
