Etymology: The word "εκληθητε" comes from the Greek verb "καλώ" (kaló), which means "to call" or "to summon". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the completion or fulfillment of the action.

Grammar: "Εκληθητε" is the second person plural form of the aorist passive tense of the verb "καλώ" (kaló). It is used to address a group of people and means "you all were called" or "you all were summoned".

Description: "Εκληθητε" is a verb form that indicates a past action of being called or summoned. It is often used in religious or formal contexts to refer to a group of people being called or invited to a specific event or gathering.
