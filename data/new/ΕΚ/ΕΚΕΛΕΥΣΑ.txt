Etymology: The word "εκελευσα" (ekelēsa) comes from the Greek verb "κελεύω" (keleuō), which means "to command" or "to order". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kelh₁-.

Grammar: "εκελευσα" is the first person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "κελεύω" (keleuō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "εκελευσα" is a Greek word that translates to "I commanded" or "I ordered" in English. It is used to express an action of commanding or giving an order that has been completed in the past.
