Etymology: The word "εκλαυσατε" comes from the Greek verb "κλαίω" (kláio), which means "to weep" or "to cry". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in Greek often indicates a sense of "out" or "away", so "εκλαυσατε" can be understood as "to cry out" or "to weep aloud".

Grammar: "εκλαυσατε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "κλαίω" (kláio). In Greek, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The second person plural form is used when addressing a group of people.

Description: "εκλαυσατε" is a command or request for a group of people to weep or cry out. It is often used in a figurative sense to express deep sorrow, grief, or lamentation.
