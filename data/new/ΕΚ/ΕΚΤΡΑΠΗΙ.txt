Etymology: The word "εκτραπη" (ektrapi) comes from the Greek verb "εκτρέπω" (ektrepo), which means "to divert" or "to turn aside". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "away", and the verb "τρέπω" (trepo), meaning "to turn".

Grammar: "Εκτραπη" is the third person singular form of the aorist passive tense of the verb "εκτρέπω" (ektrepo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Εκτραπη" is a Greek word that can be translated as "he/she/it was diverted" or "he/she/it turned aside". It describes an action in the past where someone or something was redirected or deviated from its original course or path.
