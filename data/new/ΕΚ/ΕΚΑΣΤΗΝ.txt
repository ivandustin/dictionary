Etymology: The word "εκαστην" (ekastin) comes from the Greek word "εκαστος" (ekastos), which means "each" or "every".

Grammar: "Εκαστην" is a feminine singular form of the adjective "εκαστος" (ekastos), which agrees with a feminine singular noun in the accusative case.

Description: "Εκαστην" is used to refer to each or every individual item or person in a group. It emphasizes the individuality or uniqueness of each item or person.
