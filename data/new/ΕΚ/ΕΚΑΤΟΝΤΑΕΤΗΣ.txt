Etymology: The word "εκατονταετης" comes from the Greek words "εκατόν" (ekatón), meaning "hundred," and "έτος" (étos), meaning "year." 

Grammar: "Εκατονταετης" is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Εκατονταετης" means "centenarian" or "person who is one hundred years old" in English. It is used to describe someone who has reached the age of one hundred.
