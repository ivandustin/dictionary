Etymology: The word "εκαθεζομην" comes from the Greek verb "καθέζομαι" (kathézomai), which means "to sit down" or "to take a seat." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the action of sitting down completely or fully.

Grammar: "Εκαθεζομην" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in the middle voice of the verb "καθέζομαι." The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: "Εκαθεζομην" describes the action of sitting down or taking a seat in the past. It specifically refers to the speaker (first person singular) sitting down or taking a seat.
