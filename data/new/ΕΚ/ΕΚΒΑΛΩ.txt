Etymology: The word "εκβαλω" (ekbalō) comes from the Greek roots "εκ" (ek), meaning "out," and "βαλλω" (ballō), meaning "to throw." Therefore, "εκβαλω" literally means "to throw out."

Grammar: "Εκβαλω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ εκβάλλω (ego ekballo) - I throw out
- Εσύ εκβάλλεις (esy ekballeis) - You throw out
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό εκβάλλει (autos/auti/auto ekballei) - He/She/It throws out
- Εμείς εκβάλλουμε (emeis ekballoume) - We throw out
- Εσείς εκβάλλετε (eseis ekballete) - You (plural) throw out
- Αυτοί/Αυτές εκβάλλουν (autoi/aftes ekballoun) - They throw out

Description: "Εκβαλω" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It generally means "to throw out" or "to expel." It can refer to physically throwing something out of a place or removing it forcefully. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of expelling or removing someone or something from a group, organization, or situation.
