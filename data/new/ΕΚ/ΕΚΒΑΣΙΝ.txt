Etymology: The word "εκβασιν" comes from the Greek verb "εκβαίνω" (ekbaino), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out", and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go".

Grammar: "Εκβασιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Εκβασιν" refers to the act of going out or exiting. It can be used to describe physical movement, such as leaving a place or coming out of a building. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the conclusion or outcome of a situation or event.
