Etymology: The word "εκτισεν" comes from the Greek verb "κτίζω" (ktízo), which means "to build" or "to create". The prefix "εκ" (ek) in Greek denotes "out" or "from", so "εκτισεν" can be translated as "to build out" or "to create from".

Grammar: "εκτισεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "κτίζω" (ktízo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "εκτισεν" is a verb that describes the act of building or creating something. It indicates that the action has been completed in the past.
