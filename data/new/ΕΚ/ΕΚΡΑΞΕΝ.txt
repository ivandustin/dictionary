Etymology: The word "εκραξεν" (ekraksen) comes from the Greek verb "κράζω" (krazo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates an action that is done outwards or away from something.

Grammar: "Εκραξεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "κράζω" (krazo) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εκραξεν" can be translated as "he/she/it shouted" or "he/she/it cried out" in English. It is used to describe a single action of shouting or crying out that happened in the past.
