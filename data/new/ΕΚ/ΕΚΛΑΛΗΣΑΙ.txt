Etymology: The word "εκλαλησαι" (eklalēsai) comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleō), which means "to call" or "to summon." The prefix "εκ" (ek) adds the meaning of "out" or "away," so "εκλαλησαι" can be translated as "to call out" or "to summon forth."

Grammar: "εκλαλησαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.
