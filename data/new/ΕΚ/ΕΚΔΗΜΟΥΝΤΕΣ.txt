Etymology: The word "εκδημούντες" comes from the Greek verb "εκδημώ" (ekdimo), which means "to go abroad" or "to emigrate." The suffix "-ούντες" is added to the verb stem to form a present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Εκδημούντες" is the plural form of the present participle of the verb "εκδημώ." In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Εκδημούντες" refers to a group of people who are currently abroad or living outside their home country. It can be used to describe individuals who have emigrated or are temporarily residing in another country.
