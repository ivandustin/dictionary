Etymology: The word "εκείτο" comes from the Greek word "εκεί" which means "there". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "εκ" meaning "out of" and the adverb "εί" meaning "there is". 

Grammar: "Εκείτο" is a Greek adverb that is used to indicate a location or position. It is often used to refer to a place that is not close to the speaker or the person being addressed. It can also be used to express the idea of "there" in a more abstract sense, such as in expressions like "εκείτο που θέλω" meaning "there where I want".

Description: "Εκείτο" is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday Greek conversations. It helps to provide clarity and specificity when referring to a particular location or position. It is important to note that the word can change its form depending on the gender and number of the noun it modifies.
