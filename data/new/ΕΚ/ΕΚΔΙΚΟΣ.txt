Etymology: The word "εκδικος" (ekdikos) comes from the Greek roots "εκ" (ek), meaning "out" or "from," and "δικη" (dike), meaning "justice" or "judgment." Therefore, "εκδικος" can be translated as "out of justice" or "from judgment."

Grammar: In Greek, "εκδικος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: The word "εκδικος" is often translated as "vengeful" or "avenging" in English. It describes someone or something that seeks revenge or retribution for a perceived wrong or injustice. It can also refer to a punishment or act of vengeance.
