Etymology: The word "εκδικησαί" comes from the Greek verb "εκδικέω" (ekdikeo), which means "to avenge" or "to take revenge". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "from", and the verb "δίκη" (dike), meaning "justice" or "judgment".

Grammar: "Εκδικησαί" is the 2nd person singular imperative form of the verb "εκδικέω" (ekdikeo). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to avenge or take revenge.

Description: "Εκδικησαί" is a Greek word that specifically instructs someone to seek revenge or take vengeance. It implies a desire for justice or retribution for a perceived wrong or harm done to oneself or others.
