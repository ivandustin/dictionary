Etymology: The word "εκερδησεν" comes from the Greek verb "κερδίζω" (kerdízo), which means "to gain" or "to profit". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in Greek denotes "out" or "from", so "εκερδησεν" can be translated as "he/she/it gained" or "he/she/it profited".

Grammar: "εκερδησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "κερδίζω" (kerdízo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "εκερδησεν" is a word in the Greek language that describes the action of gaining or profiting. It can be used to refer to someone or something that has achieved a financial or non-financial gain.
