Etymology: The word "εκείνα" (ekena) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek demonstrative pronoun "εκείνος" (ekinos), meaning "that" or "those."

Grammar: "Εκείνα" is a neuter plural form of the Greek demonstrative pronoun. It is used to refer to objects or things that are distant in space or time. In Greek grammar, it functions as a pronoun or an adjective.

Description: "Εκείνα" is used to point out or refer to specific objects or things that are not close to the speaker or the listener. It can be used to indicate objects that have been previously mentioned or are known to both parties in a conversation.
