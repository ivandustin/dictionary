Etymology: The word "εκαμμυσαν" (ekammysan) comes from the Greek verb "καμμύω" (kammýo), which means "to close the eyes" or "to blink." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the action of doing something completely or intensively.

Grammar: "Εκαμμύσαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense of the verb "καμμύω" (kammýo). The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εκαμμύσαν" is a Greek verb that means "they closed their eyes" or "they blinked." It refers to the action of closing the eyes momentarily or rapidly.
