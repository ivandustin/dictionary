Etymology: The word "εκαθερισθη" comes from the Greek verb "εκαθεριζω" (ekatherizo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "away," and the verb "αθεριζω" (atherizo), meaning "to remove impurities."

Grammar: "Εκαθερισθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive form of the verb "εκαθεριζω" (ekatherizo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Εκαθερισθη" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was purified" or "he/she/it was cleansed." It describes a past action of someone or something being purified or cleansed.
