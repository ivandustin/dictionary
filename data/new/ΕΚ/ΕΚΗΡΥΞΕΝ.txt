Etymology: The word "εκηρυξεν" (ekēryxen) comes from the Greek verb "κηρύσσω" (kērýssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce."

Grammar: "Εκηρύξεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "κηρύσσω" (kērýssō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition.

Description: "Εκηρύξεν" is a verb form that indicates that someone or something proclaimed or announced something in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it proclaimed" or "he/she/it announced" in English.
