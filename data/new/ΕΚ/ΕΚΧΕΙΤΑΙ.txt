Etymology: The word "εκχειρίται" (ekcheirítai) comes from the Greek verb "εκχειρίζομαι" (ekcheirízomai), which means "to execute by hand" or "to perform manually." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "from," and the noun "χείρ" (cheír), meaning "hand."

Grammar: "Εκχειρίται" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action on itself. The verb is conjugated from the base form "εκχειρίζομαι" (ekcheirízomai) by adding the appropriate endings for the present tense.

Description: "Εκχειρίται" is a Greek verb that can be translated as "he/she/it executes by hand" or "he/she/it performs manually." It implies the act of carrying out a task or action using one's hands.
