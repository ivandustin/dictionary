Etymology: The word "εκαυματισθη" comes from the Greek verb "καυματίζω" (kaumatízo), which means "to burn" or "to scorch." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the action of going out or away from something. Therefore, "εκαυματισθη" can be translated as "to be burned" or "to be scorched."

Grammar: "εκαυματισθη" is the third person singular form of the aorist passive tense of the verb "καυματίζω." In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "εκαυματισθη" describes the state of being burned or scorched. It implies that someone or something has undergone the action of being burned.
