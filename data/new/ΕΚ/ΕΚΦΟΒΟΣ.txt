Etymology: The word "εκφοβος" comes from the Greek roots "εκ" (ek), meaning "out" or "from," and "φοβος" (phobos), meaning "fear." Therefore, "εκφοβος" can be translated as "out of fear" or "fearful."

Grammar: In Greek, "εκφοβος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms depending on the gender and number of the noun it describes.

Description: "Εκφοβος" is used to describe someone or something that is fearful or scared. It can refer to a person who is easily frightened or intimidated, or to a situation or environment that induces fear.
