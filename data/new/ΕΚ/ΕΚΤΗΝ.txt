Etymology: The word "εκτην" (ektin) comes from the Greek verb "εκτίνω" (ektino), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend."

Grammar: "Εκτην" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the neuter gender.

Description: "Εκτην" refers to the act of stretching or extending something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the expansion or elongation of an idea, concept, or situation.
