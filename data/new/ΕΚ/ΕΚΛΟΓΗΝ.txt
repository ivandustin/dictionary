Etymology: The word "εκλογην" (eklogēn) comes from the Greek root "εκλογή" (eklogē), which means "choice" or "selection". 

Grammar: "Εκλογην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Εκλογην" refers to the act of choosing or selecting something or someone. It can also be used to describe an election or the process of voting.
