Etymology: The word "εκωλυομεν" comes from the Greek verb "κωλύω" (kōlýō), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent." The verb is conjugated in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.

Grammar: "Εκωλυομεν" is the first person plural form of the verb "κωλύω" in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It indicates that "we" are being hindered or prevented from doing something.

Description: The word "εκωλυομεν" describes an action of being hindered or prevented from doing something. It implies that there are obstacles or barriers preventing the subject from carrying out a particular action.
