Etymology: The word "εκδοτον" (ekdoton) comes from the Greek roots "εκ" (ek), meaning "out" or "from," and "δοτον" (doton), meaning "given" or "granted." It can be translated as "that which is given out" or "that which is published."

Grammar: "Εκδοτον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" in the nominative case.

Description: "Εκδοτον" refers to something that is published or issued, such as a book, magazine, or other printed material. It can also be used more broadly to describe any form of publication or release, including digital media.
