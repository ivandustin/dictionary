Etymology: The word "εκλεισεν" comes from the Greek verb "κλείνω" (kleíno), which means "to close" or "to shut". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in Greek often indicates a completion or a result, so "εκλεισεν" can be translated as "it closed" or "it shut".

Grammar: "εκλεισεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe completed actions in the past. In this case, "εκλεισεν" indicates that someone or something closed or shut something in the past.

Description: "εκλεισεν" is a verb in Greek that describes the action of closing or shutting something. It can be used to refer to physical objects being closed, such as doors or windows, or metaphorical actions, such as closing a chapter in one's life or shutting out distractions.
