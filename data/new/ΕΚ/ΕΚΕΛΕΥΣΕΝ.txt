Etymology: The word "εκελευσεν" comes from the Greek verb "κελεύω" (keleúo), which means "to command" or "to order". It is conjugated in the third person singular aorist active indicative form.

Grammar: "εκελευσεν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense, which is used to express past actions that are completed. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood suggests that the action is a statement of fact.

Description: "εκελευσεν" means "he/she/it commanded" in English. It is used to describe an action of commanding or ordering that was done in the past.
