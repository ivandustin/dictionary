Etymology: The word "εκτείνον" (ekténon) comes from the Greek verb "εκτείνω" (ekteíno), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend."

Grammar: "Εκτείνον" is the present participle form of the verb "εκτείνω" (ekteíno). It is used to describe an ongoing action of stretching or extending.

Description: "Εκτείνον" is an adjective that describes something as being stretched out or extended. It can be used to describe physical objects that are elongated or stretched, as well as abstract concepts that are expanded or prolonged.
