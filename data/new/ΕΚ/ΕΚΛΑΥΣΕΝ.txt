Etymology: The word "εκλαυσεν" (eklausen) comes from the Greek verb "κλαίω" (kláio), which means "to weep" or "to cry". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates an intensification or completion of the action.

Grammar: "Εκλαυσεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "κλαίω" (kláio) in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εκλαυσεν" is a verb form that describes the act of weeping or crying in the past. It indicates that someone (third person singular) cried or wept at a specific point in time.
