Etymology: The word "εκβαλειν" comes from the Greek roots "εκ" (ek), meaning "out," and "βαλλειν" (ballein), meaning "to throw." 

Grammar: "Εκβαλειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "Εκβαλειν" means "to throw out" or "to expel" in English. It is used to describe the action of forcefully removing or ejecting something or someone from a place or a group.
