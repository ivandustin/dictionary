Etymology: The word "εκολληθησαν" (ekollithisan) is derived from the Greek verb "κολλάω" (kollao), which means "to stick" or "to glue." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates a completed action or a change of state.

Grammar: "Εκολληθησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "κολλάω" (kollao) in the past tense. It is in the indicative mood and the aorist tense, indicating a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εκολληθησαν" is a Greek verb that means "they stuck" or "they glued." It describes an action where multiple objects or substances are joined together by adhesive or by sticking closely to each other.
