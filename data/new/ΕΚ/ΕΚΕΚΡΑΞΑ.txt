Etymology: The word "εκεκραξα" comes from the Greek verb "κράζω" (krázo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the completion or intensity of the action.

Grammar: "Εκεκραξα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this case, it indicates that the shouting or crying out has already taken place.

Description: "Εκεκραξα" is a verb that describes the act of shouting or crying out loudly. It implies a strong and intense expression of emotion or a sudden burst of sound.
