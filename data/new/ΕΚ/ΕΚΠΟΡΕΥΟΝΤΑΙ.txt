Etymology: The word "εκπορευονται" (ekporeuontai) comes from the Greek verb "εκπορευομαι" (ekporeuomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of", and the verb "πορευομαι" (poreuomai), meaning "to go" or "to travel".

Grammar: "Εκπορευονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "εκπορευομαι" (ekporeuomai). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Εκπορευονται" is a Greek verb that means "they go out" or "they come forth". It is used to describe the action of multiple individuals or objects leaving a place or emerging from a source.
