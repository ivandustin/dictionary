Etymology: The word "εκφερειν" comes from the Greek verb "φέρω" (fero), which means "to carry" or "to bring out". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) adds the meaning of "out" or "from". Therefore, "εκφερειν" can be translated as "to bring out" or "to carry out".

Grammar: "εκφερειν" is an infinitive verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is formed by adding the suffix "-ειν" (-ein) to the verb stem "φερ-" (fer-).

Description: "εκφερειν" is a versatile verb that can have various meanings depending on the context. It can refer to physically carrying or bringing something out, as well as expressing the idea of expressing or uttering something. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate the manifestation or display of a quality or characteristic.
