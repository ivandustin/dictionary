Etymology: The word "εκριζωθητι" comes from the Greek verb "εκριζόω" (ekrizóo), which means "to uproot" or "to eradicate". The suffix "-θητι" (-thiti) is added to the verb stem to form the imperative mood, giving the meaning "uproot!" or "eradicate!".

Grammar: "Εκριζωθητι" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "εκριζόω" (ekrizóo). In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or express requests. The second person singular form is used when addressing one person directly.

Description: The word "εκριζωθητι" is a command or request to uproot or eradicate something. It is used to instruct someone to remove or eliminate something completely, often referring to plants or weeds being pulled out from the ground.
