Etymology: The word "εκρινας" (ekrinas) comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the completion or result of an action, so "εκρινας" can be translated as "you have judged" or "you have decided".

Grammar: "Εκρινας" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this case, "εκρινας" indicates that the action of judging or deciding has been completed by the second person singular subject.

Description: "Εκρινας" is a verb form that specifically addresses the action of judging or deciding. It is used to refer to the act of making a judgment or decision in the past.
