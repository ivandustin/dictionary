Etymology: The word "εκηρυχθη" (ekērychthē) comes from the Greek verb "κηρύσσω" (kērýssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce."

Grammar: "εκηρυχθη" is the aorist passive indicative form of the verb "κηρύσσω" (kērýssō). The aorist tense indicates a past action, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb receives the action.

Description: "εκηρυχθη" is a Greek word that means "was proclaimed" or "was announced." It is used to describe an action that was announced or proclaimed in the past.
