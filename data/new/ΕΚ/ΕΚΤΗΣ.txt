Etymology: The word "εκτης" (ektēs) comes from the Greek verb "εκτείνω" (ekteínō), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend." 

Grammar: "Εκτης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Εκτης" refers to the sixth in a series or the sixth item in a sequence. It can also be used to indicate the sixth day of the month.
