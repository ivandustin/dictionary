Etymology: The word "εκομισαντο" comes from the Greek verb "κομίζω" (komízo), which means "to bring" or "to carry". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the completion or result of the action.

Grammar: "Εκομισαντο" is the third person plural, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "κομίζω" (komízo). The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: "Εκομισαντο" is a Greek verb form that can be translated as "they brought" or "they carried" in English. It refers to a completed action of bringing or carrying something in the past, with the subject being the recipients of the action.
