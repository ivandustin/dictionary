Etymology: The word "εκοιμηθη" (ekoi̱mīthē) comes from the Greek verb "κοιμάω" (koimáō), which means "to sleep". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates a completed action, so "εκοιμηθη" (ekoi̱mīthē) can be translated as "he/she/it fell asleep" or "he/she/it was put to sleep".

Grammar: "εκοιμηθη" (ekoi̱mīthē) is the third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb "κοιμάω" (koimáō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "εκοιμηθη" (ekoi̱mīthē) is a word used in Greek to describe the act of falling asleep or being put to sleep. It is commonly used in narratives or descriptions to indicate that someone or something has entered a state of sleep.
