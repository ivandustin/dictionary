Etymology: The word "εκταρασσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "εκταρασσω" (ektarasso), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". The suffix "-ουσιν" indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "Εκταρασσουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The present tense indicates an action happening in the current moment.

Description: "Εκταρασσουσιν" describes the action of disturbing or agitating. It is used to express that a group of people is currently causing disturbance or agitation.
