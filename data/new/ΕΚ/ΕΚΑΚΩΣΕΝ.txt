Etymology: The word "εκακωσεν" comes from the Greek verb "κακόω" (kakóo), which means "to harm" or "to afflict". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates an action that is completed or brought to an end.

Grammar: "εκακωσεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "κακόω" (kakóo). The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition.

Description: "εκακωσεν" means "he/she/it harmed" or "he/she/it afflicted" in Greek. It is used to describe an action of causing harm or affliction to someone or something in the past.
