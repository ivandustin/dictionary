Etymology: The word "εκζητών" comes from the Greek verb "ζητώ" (zito), which means "to seek" or "to search". The prefix "εκ" (ek) adds the meaning of "out" or "from", so "εκζητών" can be translated as "one who seeks out" or "one who searches for".

Grammar: "Εκζητών" is the present participle form of the verb "ζητώ" (zito), which means "to seek" or "to search". In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Εκζητών" refers to someone who is actively seeking or searching for something. It implies a persistent and determined effort to find or discover. The word can be used in various contexts, such as someone seeking knowledge, truth, answers, or solutions.
