Etymology: The word "εκαθισαν" comes from the Greek verb "καθίζω" (kathízo), which means "to sit down" or "to settle". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the completion or result of the action.

Grammar: "Εκαθισαν" is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb "καθίζω" (kathízo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εκαθισαν" means "they sat down" or "they settled" in English. It refers to a group of people or things that have taken a seated position or have established themselves in a particular place.
