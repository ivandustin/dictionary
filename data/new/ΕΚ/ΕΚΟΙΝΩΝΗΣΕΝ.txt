Etymology: The word "εκοινωνησεν" comes from the Greek verb "κοινωνέω" (koinonéo), which means "to communicate" or "to share". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the completion or accomplishment of the action.

Grammar: "εκοινωνησεν" is the third person singular aorist tense form of the verb "κοινωνέω" (koinonéo) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "εκοινωνησεν" means "he/she/it communicated" or "he/she/it shared" in English. It refers to a single action of communication or sharing that took place in the past.
