Etymology: The word "εκμασσειν" (ekmassein) comes from the Greek roots "εκ" (ek), meaning "out" or "from," and "μασσειν" (massein), meaning "to knead" or "to mold." 

Grammar: "εκμασσειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εκμάσσω (ekmasso), εκμάσσεις (ekmasseis), εκμάσσει (ekmassei), εκμάσσομεν (ekmassomen), εκμάσσετε (ekmassete), εκμάσσουσι (ekmassousi).

Description: "εκμασσειν" is a Greek verb that means "to knead" or "to mold." It refers to the action of working dough or clay with the hands to mix and shape it. This verb is commonly used in the context of baking or pottery.
