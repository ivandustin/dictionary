Etymology: The word "εκπορευσονται" comes from the Greek verb "εκπορευομαι" (ekporeuomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of", and the verb "πορευομαι" (poreuomai), meaning "to go" or "to travel".

Grammar: "Εκπορευσονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "εκπορευομαι" (ekporeuomai). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Εκπορευσονται" is a Greek verb that means "they go out" or "they come forth". It is used to describe the action of a group of people or things leaving a place or emerging from a certain location.
