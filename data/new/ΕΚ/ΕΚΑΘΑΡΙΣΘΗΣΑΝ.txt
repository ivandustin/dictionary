Etymology: The word "εκαθαρισθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates completion or intensification of the action.

Grammar: "Εκαθαρισθησαν" is the third person plural, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action.

Description: "Εκαθαρισθησαν" means "they were purified" or "they were cleansed" in English. It refers to a group of people or things that underwent a process of purification or cleansing.
