Etymology: The word "εκαθαρισθη" comes from the Greek verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this word indicates the completion or result of the action.

Grammar: "Εκαθαρισθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb receives the action.

Description: "Εκαθαρισθη" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was purified" or "he/she/it has been cleansed." It describes the action of purifying or cleansing that has been completed in the past.
