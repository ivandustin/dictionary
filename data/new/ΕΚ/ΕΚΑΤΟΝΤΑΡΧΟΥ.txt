Etymology: The word "εκατονταρχου" (ekatontarchou) comes from the Greek words "εκατόν" (ekaton), meaning "hundred," and "αρχος" (archos), meaning "ruler" or "leader." It refers to a military rank in ancient Greece, equivalent to a centurion in the Roman army.

Grammar: "Εκατονταρχου" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Εκατονταρχου" refers to a military officer who commanded a century, a unit of soldiers in ancient Greek and Roman armies. The century typically consisted of around 100 soldiers, hence the name. The role of the εκατονταρχου was to lead and manage the soldiers under their command.
