The word "εκκολύμβησας" is a Greek verb in the past tense, which means "I swam out" or "I emerged from the water". Here is the breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "εκκολύμβησας" is derived from the Greek root words "εκ" (ek), meaning "out", and "κολυμβώ" (kolymbó), meaning "I swim".

Grammar: "Εκκολύμβησας" is the second person singular form of the verb in the past tense. It is used when referring to the action of swimming out or emerging from the water performed by the speaker or someone addressed directly.

Description: The word describes the act of swimming out or emerging from the water. It implies that the subject was previously in the water and has now come out or surfaced.
