Etymology: The word "εκοπτοντο" comes from the Greek verb "κοπτω" (kopto), which means "to cut." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the action of cutting out or cutting off.

Grammar: "Εκοπτοντο" is the third person plural form of the verb "κοπτω" (kopto) in the imperfect tense. In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: "Εκοπτοντο" can be translated as "they were cutting" or "they used to cut" in English. It indicates a continuous or repeated action of cutting in the past.
