Etymology: The word "εκαθισα" (ekathisa) comes from the Greek verb "καθίζω" (kathizo), which means "to sit down". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the completion or result of an action, so "εκαθισα" can be translated as "I sat down" or "I have sat down".

Grammar: "εκαθισα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this case, it indicates that the action of sitting down has been completed.
