Etymology: The word "εκβαλλειν" comes from the Greek roots "εκ" (ek), meaning "out," and "βαλλειν" (ballein), meaning "to throw." 

Grammar: "Εκβαλλειν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs.

Description: "Εκβαλλειν" means "to throw out" or "to expel" in Greek. It is often used to describe the act of forcefully removing something or someone from a place or a group.
