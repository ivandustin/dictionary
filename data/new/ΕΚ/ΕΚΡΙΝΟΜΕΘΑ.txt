Etymology: The word "εκρινομεθα" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix "εκ" (ek) indicates the action of judging or deciding from within oneself.

Grammar: "Εκρινομεθα" is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person plural form of the verb "κρίνομαι" (krinomai), which is a reflexive verb meaning "to judge oneself" or "to make a decision for oneself".

Description: "Εκρινομεθα" expresses the idea of self-judgment or self-decision. It implies that the subject is actively involved in the process of judging or deciding for themselves.
