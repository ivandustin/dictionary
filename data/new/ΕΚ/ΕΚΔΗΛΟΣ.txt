Etymology: The word "εκδηλος" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εκ-" (meaning "out" or "from") and the adjective "δηλος" (meaning "clear" or "evident"). 

Grammar: In Greek, "εκδηλος" is an adjective. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter, which agree with the gender of the noun it modifies. It can also be declined in different cases (nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.) to match the grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Εκδηλος" is used to describe something that is clearly visible, evident, or obvious. It implies that there is no doubt or ambiguity about the subject being discussed. It can be used to describe a physical appearance, a characteristic, or a situation that is easily perceivable.
