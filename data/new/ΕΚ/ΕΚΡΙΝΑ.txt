Etymology: The word "εκρινα" (ekrina) comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this case indicates a completed action or a result.

Grammar: "Εκρινα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition.
