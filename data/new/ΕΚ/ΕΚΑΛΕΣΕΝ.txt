Etymology: The word "εκαλεσεν" (ekalesen) comes from the Greek verb "καλεί" (kalei), which means "to call" or "to summon." The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) indicates the completion or fulfillment of the action.

Grammar: "Εκάλεσεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in the indicative mood of the verb "καλεί" (kalei). The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "Εκάλεσεν" means "he/she/it called" in Greek. It refers to the act of summoning or inviting someone.
