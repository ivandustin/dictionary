Etymology: The word "εκωλυσατε" comes from the Greek verb "κωλύω" (kolýo), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent". It is derived from the noun "κῶλον" (kôlon), meaning "limb" or "member".

Grammar: "εκωλυσατε" is the second person plural, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb "κωλύω" (kolýo). In Greek, verbs change their form based on tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, the active voice shows that the subject performs the action, and the second person plural refers to a group of people being addressed.

Description: "εκωλυσατε" means "you hindered" or "you prevented" in English. It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past, where the subject (a group of people) hindered or prevented something or someone from happening or progressing.
