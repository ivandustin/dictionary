Etymology: The word "εκστασις" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "εκσταίνω" (ekstaino), which means "to stand outside" or "to be in a state of ecstasy." 

Grammar: "Εκστασις" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun of the third declension. In its nominative form, it is spelled as "εκστασις." 

Description: "Εκστασις" refers to a state of being outside oneself, often associated with intense emotions, excitement, or trance-like experiences. It can also refer to a state of ecstasy or rapture. In a broader sense, it can describe a state of extreme happiness, joy, or spiritual transcendence.
