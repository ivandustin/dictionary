Etymology: The word "εκαστος" (ekastos) comes from the Greek word "εκατος" (ekatos), meaning "each" or "every". It is derived from the Greek prefix "εκ" (ek), meaning "out of" or "from", and the Greek word "ατος" (atos), meaning "individual" or "person".

Grammar: "Εκαστος" is a masculine adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe individual objects or people, emphasizing that something applies to each or every one of them. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "εκαστος" can change its form depending on the gender and number of the noun it accompanies.

Description: "Εκαστος" is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to express the idea of "each" or "every". It is often used to indicate that something applies to every individual or object in a group. For example, "εκαστος μαθητής" (ekastos mathitis) means "each student" or "every student". It can also be used in phrases like "εκαστος ανθρωπος" (ekastos anthropos), meaning "every person" or "each individual".
