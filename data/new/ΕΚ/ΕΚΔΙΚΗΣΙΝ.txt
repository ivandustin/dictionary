Etymology: The word "εκδικησιν" (ekdikēsin) comes from the Greek verb "εκδικέω" (ekdikeō), which means "to avenge" or "to take revenge". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out" or "from", and the verb "δίκη" (dikē), meaning "justice" or "judgment".

Grammar: "Εκδικησιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Εκδικησιν" refers to the act of seeking revenge or taking vengeance. It can also be used to describe the desire or motive for revenge. The word carries a sense of justice being served or retribution being exacted for a perceived wrongdoing.
