Word: νυξ

Etymology: νυξ (nyx) is a Greek word that means "night". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nókʷts.

Grammar: In Greek, νυξ is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its genitive form is νυκτός (nyktós) and its accusative form is νύκτα (nýkta).

Description: νυξ is a significant concept in Greek mythology, often personified as a goddess. She is the daughter of Chaos and represents the darkness and mystery of the night. In literature, νυξ is often associated with death, dreams, and the unknown. The word is also used in everyday Greek language to refer to the period of time between sunset and sunrise.
