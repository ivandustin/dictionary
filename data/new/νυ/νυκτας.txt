Word: νυκτας

Etymology:
The word "νυκτας" is derived from the Greek word "νύξ" (nyx), which means "night". The suffix "-ας" is added to indicate the accusative case, making the word mean "nights".

Grammar:
νυκτας is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, the accusative case is also used to indicate the duration of time for which an action takes place. 

Description:
νυκτας refers to multiple nights. It can be used to describe a period of time that spans across several nights, or to refer to a specific number of nights. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in literature, poetry, or everyday conversation.
