Word: νυν

Etymology:
νυν (nun) is a Greek adverb that means "now" or "at present". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nu, which means "now" or "here".

Grammar:
νυν is an adverb in Greek and does not have any inflections. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate the present time.

Description:
νυν is a common word in Greek and is used to indicate the present time. It is often used in combination with other words to form phrases such as "νυν καιρός" (now is the time) or "νυν είμι" (I am here now). It is also used in religious contexts, such as in the phrase "Κύριε, νυν απολύεις τον δούλον σου εν ειρήνη" (Lord, now you are letting your servant depart in peace).
