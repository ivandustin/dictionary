Etymology: The word "υστεροις" (ysterois) comes from the Greek word "υστερος" (ysteros), meaning "later" or "after." 

Grammar: "Υστεροις" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Υστεροις" refers to something that comes later or is subsequent in time or order. It can also be used to describe something that is behind or inferior in quality or importance.
