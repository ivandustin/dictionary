Etymology: The word "υστερημα" (ustērēma) comes from the Greek verb "υστερέω" (ustereō), which means "to lack" or "to be deficient".

Grammar: "Υστερημα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a neuter noun of the first declension, and its plural form is "υστερηματα" (ustēremata).

Description: "Υστερημα" refers to something that is lacking or missing. It can be used to describe a deficiency or a shortage in various contexts, such as in knowledge, skills, resources, or qualities.
