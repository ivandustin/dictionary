Etymology: The word "υς" (pronounced "oos") is a Greek word that means "you" in English. It is the second person singular pronoun in Greek.

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "υς" is the nominative case form of the second person singular pronoun. It is used when referring to one person as the subject of a sentence.

Description: "υς" is a pronoun that is used to address or refer to one person directly. It is equivalent to the English pronoun "you" when used to address someone individually.
