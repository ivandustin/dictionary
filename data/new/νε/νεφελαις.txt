Word: νεφέλαις

Etymology:
The word νεφέλαις (nephelais) is derived from the Greek word "νέφος" (néfos), which means "cloud". The suffix "-αις" (-ais) is a feminine plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple clouds.

Grammar:
νεφέλαις is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
νεφέλαις refers to a group or collection of clouds. In Greek mythology, the god Zeus was said to create clouds to hide himself and his actions from mortals. The word is often used in literature and poetry to describe the beauty and majesty of the sky.
