Word: νεωτερας

Etymology: 
The word νεωτερας (neōteras) is a Greek adjective that means "newer" or "more recent". It is derived from the Greek word νέος (neos), which means "new" or "young".

Grammar:
νεωτερας is a masculine singular form of the adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description:
νεωτερας is used to describe something that is newer or more recent than something else. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in history, technology, or fashion. For example, one might use the word νεωτερας to describe a more recent version of a software program or a newer model of a car.
