Word: νεκροις

Etymology:
The word νεκροις (nekrois) is a Greek word that comes from the root word νεκρός (nekros), which means "dead" or "lifeless". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nek- which means "death".

Grammar:
νεκροις (nekrois) is a plural form of the adjective νεκρός (nekros), which means "dead" or "lifeless". It is in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
νεκροις (nekrois) is used to describe a group of dead or lifeless things or beings. In Greek mythology, the word is often used to describe the dead in the underworld. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is no longer useful or relevant.
