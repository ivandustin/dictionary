Word: νεφρούς

Etymology: The word νεφρούς comes from the Greek word "νεφρός" (nephros), which means "kidney". 

Grammar: νεφρούς is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is the genitive plural form of the word "νεφρός". 

Description: νεφρούς refers to the kidneys, which are a pair of organs located in the back of the abdomen. They are responsible for filtering waste products from the blood and producing urine. The kidneys also play a role in regulating blood pressure, producing hormones, and maintaining electrolyte balance in the body. Kidney disease can lead to serious health problems, so it is important to take care of these vital organs.
