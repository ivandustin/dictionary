The word "νεκρου" (nekrou) is a Greek adjective that means "dead" or "lifeless". It is derived from the Greek word "νεκρός" (nekros), which also means "dead". 

In terms of grammar, "νεκρου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. It is often used to describe a dead person or animal, or to refer to something that is no longer alive or active.

Overall, "νεκρου" is a descriptive word that is commonly used in Greek to refer to death or lifelessness.
