Word: νεανίσκος

Etymology:
The word νεανίσκος (neaniskos) is derived from the Greek word νέος (neos) which means "young" or "new". The suffix -ίσκος (-iskos) is a diminutive suffix, which means "little" or "small". Therefore, νεανίσκος can be translated as "little young man" or "young lad".

Grammar:
νεανίσκος is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes νεανίσκοι (neaniskoi). It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description:
νεανίσκος is a term used to describe a young man who is not yet an adult. It is often used to refer to a teenager or a young adult who is still in the process of growing up. In ancient Greece, νεανίσκος was used to describe a young man who was not yet a citizen and had not yet completed his military training. Today, the term is still used in modern Greek to refer to a young man who is not yet fully mature.
