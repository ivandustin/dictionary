Word: νεωτεροι

Etymology:
The word νεωτεροι (neōteroi) is derived from the Greek word νέος (neos), meaning "new" or "young". It is the comparative form of νέος, which means "newer" or "younger". 

Grammar:
νεωτεροι is a masculine plural adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are newer or younger than another group. 

Description:
νεωτεροι is often used in Greek literature to describe a younger generation or a group of people who are more modern or progressive than their predecessors. It can also be used to describe something that is more recent or up-to-date. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to refer to young people or a new generation.
