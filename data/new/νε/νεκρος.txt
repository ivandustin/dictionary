Word: νεκρός

Etymology:
The word νεκρός (nekros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nek- which means "death". The word has been used in Greek literature since ancient times to refer to a dead person or a corpse.

Grammar:
In Greek, νεκρός is an adjective that is used to describe a person or an object that is dead. It is declined like any other Greek adjective, with different forms for masculine, feminine, and neuter genders, as well as for singular and plural numbers.

Description:
νεκρός is a word that is commonly used in Greek to describe a person or an object that is no longer alive. It can be used to refer to a dead body, a deceased person, or even a lifeless object. The word is often used in a solemn or serious context, such as in funeral or mourning rituals. It is also used in medical contexts to describe a person who has died or is in a state of clinical death.
