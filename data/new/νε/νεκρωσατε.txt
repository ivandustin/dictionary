Word: νεκρωσατε

Etymology: The word νεκρωσατε comes from the Greek root word νεκρός (nekros), meaning "dead," and the verb ωσάτω (osato), meaning "to make." Therefore, νεκρωσατε means "to make dead" or "to kill."

Grammar: νεκρωσατε is a second-person plural aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to perform an action in the past. In this case, the action is to kill or make dead.

Description: νεκρωσατε is a strong and forceful word that is often used in a violent or aggressive context. It is not a word that is commonly used in everyday conversation, but rather in situations where there is a need for urgency or a call to action. The word carries a sense of finality and permanence, as it refers to the act of taking a life. It is important to use this word with caution and sensitivity, as it can be emotionally charged and potentially harmful.
