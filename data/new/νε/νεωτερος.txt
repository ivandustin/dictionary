Word: νεωτερος

Etymology: νεωτερος is a Greek word that comes from the root word νέος (neos), meaning "new" or "young". The suffix -τερος (-teros) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "greater". Therefore, νεωτερος means "newer" or "more recent".

Grammar: νεωτερος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: νεωτερος is used to describe something that is newer or more recent in comparison to something else. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as technology, ideas, or events. For example, "Η νεωτερη τεχνολογία είναι πιο αποτελεσματική" (The newer technology is more effective).
