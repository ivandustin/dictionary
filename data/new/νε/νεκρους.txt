Word: νεκρούς

Etymology: The word νεκρούς (nekroús) comes from the Greek word νεκρός (nekros), which means "dead" or "lifeless".

Grammar: νεκρούς is the accusative plural form of the adjective νεκρός (nekros), which means "dead" or "lifeless". It is used to describe multiple dead or lifeless things or people.

Description: νεκρούς is an adjective that is commonly used in Greek to describe dead or lifeless things or people. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as dead animals, plants, or people. In Greek mythology, the word is often used to describe the dead in the underworld.
