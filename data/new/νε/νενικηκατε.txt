Word: νενικηκατε

Etymology: νενικηκατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word νικάω (nikao), which means "to conquer" or "to be victorious". The prefix νενικη- (nenike-) indicates that the action of conquering or being victorious has already been completed.

Grammar: νενικηκατε is the second person plural perfect indicative active form of the verb νικάω (nikao). This means that it is used to address a group of people who have already conquered or been victorious in the past. The perfect tense indicates that the action was completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present.

Description: νενικηκατε can be translated to mean "you have conquered" or "you have been victorious". It is often used in a celebratory or triumphant context, such as after a battle or competition. The word carries a sense of accomplishment and pride in one's achievements.
