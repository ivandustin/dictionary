Word: νεφελης

Etymology:
The word νεφελης (nephelēs) is derived from the Greek word νέφος (néphos), which means "cloud". In Greek mythology, Nephelē was a nymph who was transformed into a cloud by Zeus.

Grammar:
νεφελης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and is singular in number. The word is feminine in gender.

Description:
νεφελης refers to a cloud or a mass of clouds. In Greek literature, it is often used metaphorically to describe something that is hazy, unclear, or uncertain. The word is also used in the context of meteorology to describe different types of clouds, such as cumulus, stratus, and cirrus clouds. In ancient Greek mythology, the god Zeus was often depicted as appearing in the form of a cloud or a mist, and the word νεφελης was used to describe this phenomenon.
