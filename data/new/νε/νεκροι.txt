Word: νεκροί

Etymology: The word νεκροί (nekroi) is derived from the Greek word νεκρός (nekros), which means "dead" or "lifeless". It is a plural form of the word, meaning "dead people" or "corpses".

Grammar: νεκροί is a masculine plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. It can also be used as the object of a verb or preposition.

Description: νεκροί is a word that refers to deceased individuals. It can be used to describe a group of dead people or corpses. In Greek mythology, the underworld was often referred to as the realm of the νεκροί. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is lifeless or inactive.
