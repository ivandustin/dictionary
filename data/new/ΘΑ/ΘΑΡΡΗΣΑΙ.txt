Etymology: The word "θαρρησαί" comes from the Greek verb "θαρρέω" (tharreō), which means "to be bold" or "to have courage."

Grammar: "Θαρρησαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "θαρρέω" (tharreō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Θαρρησαί" is a Greek verb that means "to be bold" or "to have courage." It is often used to describe someone who is fearless, confident, or daring.
