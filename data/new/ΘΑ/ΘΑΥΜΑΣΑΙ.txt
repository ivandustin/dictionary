Etymology: The word "θαυμάσαι" (thavmásai) comes from the Greek verb "θαυμάζω" (thavmázo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". It is derived from the noun "θαῦμα" (thávma), meaning "wonder" or "marvel".

Grammar: "Θαυμάσαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "θαυμάζω" (thavmázo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb, which does not indicate tense, mood, or person.

Description: "Θαυμάσαι" is a verb in Greek that means "to marvel" or "to wonder". It is used to express a sense of awe, admiration, or astonishment towards something. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing wonder at a beautiful sight, an impressive achievement, or a surprising event.
