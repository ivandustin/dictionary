Etymology: The word "θανατηφόρος" comes from the Greek roots "θάνατος" (thanatos) meaning "death" and "φορός" (phoros) meaning "bearing" or "carrying". 

Grammar: "θανατηφόρος" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: "θανατηφόρος" is a Greek word that translates to "deadly" or "lethal" in English. It is used to describe something or someone that causes or is capable of causing death.
