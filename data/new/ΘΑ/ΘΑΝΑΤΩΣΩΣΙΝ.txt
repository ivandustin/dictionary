Etymology: The word "θανατώσωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "θανατόω" (thanatoó), which means "to kill" or "to put to death." It is derived from the noun "θάνατος" (thanatos), meaning "death."

Grammar: "Θανατώσωσιν" is the third person plural, aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "θανατόω" (thanatoó). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The subjunctive mood indicates possibility, potentiality, or uncertainty. The active voice indicates that the subject performs the action.

Description: "Θανατώσωσιν" is a verb form that expresses the action of killing or putting to death in the third person plural. It suggests that multiple individuals or entities are performing the action.
