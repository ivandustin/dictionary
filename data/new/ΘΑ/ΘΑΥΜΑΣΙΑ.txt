Etymology: The word "θαυμασία" (thavmasía) comes from the Greek verb "θαυμάζω" (thavmázo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". It is derived from the noun "θαῦμα" (thávma), meaning "wonder" or "marvel".

Grammar: "Θαυμασία" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "θαυμασίες" (thavmasíes).

Description: "Θαυμασία" refers to the state or feeling of being amazed, astonished, or in awe of something. It can also be used to describe something extraordinary, remarkable, or impressive. The word is often used to express admiration or wonder towards something that is considered exceptional or surprising.
