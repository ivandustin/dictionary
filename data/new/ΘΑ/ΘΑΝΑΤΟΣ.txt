Etymology: The word "θανατος" (thanatos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek verb "θνῄσκω" (thnēskō), which means "to die."

Grammar: "Θανατος" (thanatos) is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "θανάτου" (thanatou).

Description: "Θανατος" (thanatos) means "death" in Greek. It is often used to refer to the state or act of dying, the end of life, or the cessation of vital functions. In Greek mythology, Thanatos was also the personification of death.
