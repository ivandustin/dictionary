Etymology: The word "θάλασσης" (thalassēs) comes from the Greek word "θάλασσα" (thalassa), meaning "sea".

Grammar: θάλασσης (thalassēs) is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. It is the genitive form of the noun θάλασσα (thalassa), which means "sea" in Greek.

Description: θάλασσης (thalassēs) refers to "of the sea" or "belonging to the sea" in Greek. It is used to describe something that is related to or associated with the sea.
