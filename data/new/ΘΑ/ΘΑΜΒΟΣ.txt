Etymology: The word "θαμβος" (thambos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "θαμβέω" (thambeo), which means "to amaze" or "to astonish."

Grammar: "Θαμβος" (thambos) is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "θαμβου" (thambou).

Description: "Θαμβος" (thambos) refers to a state of astonishment or amazement. It can describe the feeling of being overwhelmed or stunned by something unexpected or extraordinary.
