Etymology: The word "θαρρούντας" (tharrountas) comes from the Greek verb "θαρρώ" (tharro), which means "to be bold" or "to have courage". The suffix "-ούντας" (-ountas) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating the action of being bold or having courage.

Grammar: "Θαρρούντας" is the present participle form of the verb "θαρρώ" (tharro) in the masculine singular nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Θαρρούντας" describes someone who is bold, courageous, or confident. It implies a sense of fearlessness and determination in the face of challenges or adversity. The word can be used to describe a person's character or behavior.
