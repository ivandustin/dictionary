Etymology: The word "θαρρούντες" comes from the Greek verb "θαρρώ" (tharró), which means "to be bold" or "to have courage". The suffix "-ούντες" is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating a group of people who are currently exhibiting the action or quality of being bold or courageous.

Grammar: "Θαρρούντες" is the plural form of the present participle of the verb "θαρρώ". In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or qualities. The plural form indicates that there are multiple individuals who are currently exhibiting the action or quality of being bold or courageous.

Description: "Θαρρούντες" is an adjective that describes a group of people who are currently displaying bravery, courage, or boldness. It can be used to describe individuals who are fearless, confident, or willing to take risks.
