Etymology: The word "θαμβούς" (thambous) comes from the Greek verb "θαμβέω" (thambeo), which means "to amaze" or "to astonish". 

Grammar: "Θαμβούς" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "θαμβού". 

Description: "Θαμβούς" refers to a state of astonishment or amazement. It is often used to describe a feeling of being overwhelmed or stunned by something unexpected or extraordinary.
