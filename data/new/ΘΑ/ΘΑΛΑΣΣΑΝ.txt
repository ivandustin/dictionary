Etymology: The word "θαλασσαν" (thalassan) comes from the Greek word "θάλασσα" (thalassa), which means "sea".

Grammar: "Θαλασσαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Θαλασσαν" refers to the sea in Greek. It is a common word used to describe the large body of saltwater that covers most of the Earth's surface. The sea is an essential part of the Earth's ecosystem and plays a crucial role in climate regulation, transportation, and recreation.
