Etymology: The word "θανατου" (thanatou) comes from the Greek word "θάνατος" (thanatos), which means "death". 

Grammar: "Θανατου" (thanatou) is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Θανατου" (thanatou) refers to "of death" or "belonging to death" in Greek. It is commonly used in phrases or expressions to describe something related to death or associated with the concept of death.
