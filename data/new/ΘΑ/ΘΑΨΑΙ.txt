Etymology: The word "θάψαι" (thapsai) comes from the Greek verb "θάπτω" (thapto), which means "to bury". 

Grammar: "Θάψαι" is the aorist tense of the verb "θάπτω" (thapto) in the 2nd person singular. It is used to express a completed action in the past, specifically the act of burying.

Description: "Θάψαι" is a Greek word that means "to bury" in English. It is used to describe the action of placing a dead body in the ground or in a tomb as a final resting place.
