Etymology: The word "θανατε" (thánate) comes from the Greek word "θάνατος" (thánatos), which means "death".

Grammar: "θανατε" (thánate) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the first person singular form of the verb "θανατεύω" (thanatévo), which means "I kill" or "I cause death".

Description: "θανατε" (thánate) is used to express the action of killing or causing death. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the end or destruction of something.
