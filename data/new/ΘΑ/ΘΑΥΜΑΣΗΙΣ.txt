Etymology: The word "θαυμασης" (thavmasis) comes from the Greek verb "θαυμάζω" (thavmazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". 

Grammar: "Θαυμασης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Θαυμασης" refers to the act of marveling or wondering. It can also be translated as "admiration" or "amazement".
