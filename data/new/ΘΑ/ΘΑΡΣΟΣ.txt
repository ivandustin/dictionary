Etymology: The word "θαρσος" (tharsos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "θαρσεῖν" (tharsein), which means "to be bold" or "to have courage."

Grammar: In Greek, "θαρσος" (tharsos) is a noun. It is a masculine noun of the second declension, and its genitive form is "θαρσου" (tharsou).

Description: "Θαρσος" (tharsos) refers to courage, boldness, or confidence. It is the state of being brave or fearless in the face of danger or adversity. This word is often used to describe someone's inner strength and determination.
