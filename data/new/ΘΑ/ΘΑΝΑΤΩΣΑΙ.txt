Etymology: The word "θανατώσαι" comes from the Greek verb "θανατόω" (thanatoó), which means "to kill" or "to put to death". It is derived from the noun "θάνατος" (thanatos), meaning "death".

Grammar: "θανατώσαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, subjunctive mood, and second person singular form of the verb "θανατόω" (thanatoó). It is used to express a hypothetical action or possibility of killing someone.
