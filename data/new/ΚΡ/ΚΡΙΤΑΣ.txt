Etymology: The word "κριτας" (kritas) comes from the Greek verb "κρινω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *krei-, meaning "to separate" or "to distinguish".

Grammar: In Greek, "κριτας" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "κριτου" (kritou). The plural form is "κριτες" (krites).

Description: "Κριτας" is the Greek word for "judge". In ancient Greece, judges played a crucial role in the legal system, making decisions and resolving disputes. They were responsible for interpreting and applying the law, as well as ensuring justice. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to someone who has the authority to make judgments or decisions in various contexts.
