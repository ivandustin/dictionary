Etymology: The word "κρινώμεν" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *krei-, meaning "to separate" or "to sift".

Grammar: "κρινώμεν" is the present tense, indicative mood, and first person plural form of the verb "κρίνω" (krino). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, and person. The present tense indicates an action happening in the present time, the indicative mood expresses a statement or a fact, and the first person plural refers to a group of people including the speaker.

Description: "κρινώμεν" is a Greek verb that means "we judge" or "we decide". It is used to express the action of making a judgment or decision in the present time. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as legal proceedings, personal opinions, or evaluations.
