Etymology: The word "κριθώσιν" comes from the Greek verb "κριθώ" (kritho), which means "to separate" or "to sift." It is derived from the noun "κρίθη" (krithi), meaning "barley."

Grammar: "Κριθώσιν" is the third person plural, aorist active subjunctive form of the verb "κριθώ" (kritho). It is used to express a hypothetical action or condition in the past.

Description: "Κριθώσιν" is a verb form that indicates an action of separating or sifting in the past. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the process of separating grains of barley from the chaff.
