Etymology: The word "κριθήσονται" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (kríno), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The suffix "-θησονται" is a future passive indicative ending, indicating that the action of judging will be done to the subject.

Grammar: "κριθήσονται" is a third-person plural future passive indicative form of the verb "κρίνω" (kríno). In Greek grammar, the future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "κριθήσονται" means "they will be judged" in English. It refers to a future event where a group of people or things will be subjected to judgment or evaluation.
