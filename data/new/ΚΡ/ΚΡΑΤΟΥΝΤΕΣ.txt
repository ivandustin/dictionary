Etymology: The word "κρατούντες" comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (kratéo), which means "to rule" or "to govern". The suffix "-ούντες" is a plural participle ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of people who are ruling or governing.

Grammar: "Κρατούντες" is the plural form of the noun "κρατών" (kratón), which means "ruler" or "governor" in Greek. It is used to refer to a group of people who hold power or authority.

Description: "Κρατούντες" is a Greek word that describes a group of individuals who are in a position of power or authority, specifically in the context of ruling or governing. It can be used to refer to political leaders, government officials, or any individuals who have control over a certain area or population.
