Etymology: The word "κριτής" (kritēs) comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *krei-, meaning "to separate" or "to sift".

Grammar: In Greek, "κριτής" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case, and its genitive form is "κριτοῦ" (kritou). It can be declined in various cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Κριτής" is the Greek word for "judge". It refers to a person who has the authority to make legal decisions and judgments in a court of law. In ancient Greece, judges played a crucial role in the legal system and were responsible for resolving disputes and administering justice.
