Etymology: The word "κραβαττού" comes from the Greek word "κρεβάτι" (kreváti), which means "bed". It is a diminutive form of "κρεβάτι", indicating a small or child's bed.

Grammar: "Κραβαττού" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. In the plural form, it becomes "κραβαττάκια" (kravatákia).

Description: "Κραβαττού" refers to a small bed or crib, typically used for infants or young children. It is often used to describe a cozy and comfortable sleeping space for babies.
