Etymology: The word "κρινεται" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (kríno), which means "to judge" or "to decide". 

Grammar: "Κρινεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "κρίνω" (kríno). It is used to describe an action or state that is happening in the present and is performed by a third person.

Description: "Κρινεται" can be translated as "he/she/it judges" or "he/she/it decides" in English. It is used to indicate that someone or something is making a judgment or decision in the present.
