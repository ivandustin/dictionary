Etymology: The word "κραζοντας" (krazontas) comes from the Greek verb "κράζω" (krazo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out".

Grammar: "Κραζοντας" is the present participle form of the verb "κράζω" (krazo), which is used to describe an ongoing action of shouting or crying out. It is in the masculine singular form.

Description: "Κραζοντας" is a Greek word that refers to someone who is shouting or crying out. It can be used to describe someone who is raising their voice in a loud and forceful manner to express their emotions or to get attention.
