Etymology: The word "κρινεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". 

Grammar: "κρινεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "κρίνω" (krinō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The present middle/passive infinitive form indicates that the action of judging or deciding is being done by the subject (middle voice) or is being done to the subject (passive voice) in the present tense.

Description: "κρινεσθαι" is a verb form that expresses the action of judging or deciding. It can be used in various contexts and can be conjugated to match different subjects and tenses.
