Etymology: The word "κρατείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (kratéō), which means "to rule" or "to have power over". The suffix "-σθαι" indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning "to rule oneself" or "to exercise self-control".

Grammar: "κρατείσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "κρατέω". In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the subject and voice (active, middle, or passive). The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "κρατείσθαι" is a Greek verb that can be translated as "to exercise self-control" or "to govern oneself". It implies the ability to restrain one's desires, emotions, or impulses and to maintain discipline or self-discipline. It is often used in a moral or ethical context, emphasizing the importance of self-control and self-mastery.
