Etymology: The word "κριθινων" (krithinon) comes from the Greek word "κριθή" (krithi), meaning "barley". 

Grammar: "κριθινων" is a noun in the genitive plural form. It is used to describe or refer to multiple things that are related to or made of barley.

Description: "κριθινων" is often used in Greek to describe various products or items that are made from or contain barley. It can refer to barley-based foods, drinks, or other products.
