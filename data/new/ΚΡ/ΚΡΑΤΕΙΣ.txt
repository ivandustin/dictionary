Etymology: The word "κρατείς" (krateis) comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (krateo), which means "to rule" or "to have power over." 

Grammar: "Κρατείς" is the second person singular form of the verb "κρατέω" in the present tense. It is used to address someone directly and means "you rule" or "you have power over."

Description: "Κρατείς" is a Greek word that refers to the act of ruling or having power over something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, leadership, or personal relationships.
