Etymology: The word "κρατος" (kratos) comes from the Greek language and means "power" or "rule". It is derived from the verb "κρατεω" (krateo), which means "to rule" or "to have power over".

Grammar: In Greek, "κρατος" (kratos) is a noun of neuter gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "κρατους" (kratous).

Description: "Κρατος" (kratos) refers to power, authority, or dominion. It can also denote strength, might, or control. In ancient Greek, it was often used to describe the power or rule of a king or ruler. In modern usage, it can be used more broadly to refer to any form of power or authority.
