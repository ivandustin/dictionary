Etymology: The word "κρατών" (kratón) comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (krateo), which means "to hold" or "to possess."

Grammar: "Κρατών" is the present participle form of the verb "κρατέω" (krateo) in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: "Κρατών" is used to describe someone or something that is currently holding or possessing something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has power, control, or influence over others.
