Etymology: The word "κρατησαί" comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (kratéo), which means "to hold" or "to possess". The suffix "-σαι" indicates the aorist tense and the middle voice, giving the meaning of "I held for myself" or "I possessed".

Grammar: "κρατησαί" is the aorist tense, middle voice, subjunctive mood form of the verb "κρατέω" (kratéo). The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon itself, and the subjunctive mood expresses possibility or potentiality.

Description: "κρατησαί" is a verb form that can be translated as "to have held" or "to have possessed" in English. It is used to describe a completed action of holding or possessing something in the past. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action may or may not have actually occurred.
