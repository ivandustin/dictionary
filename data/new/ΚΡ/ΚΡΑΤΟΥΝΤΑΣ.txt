Etymology: The word "κρατούντας" comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (kratéo), which means "to rule" or "to govern". It is derived from the noun "κράτος" (krátos), meaning "power" or "authority".

Grammar: "Κρατούντας" is the plural form of the present participle of the verb "κρατώ" (krató), which means "to hold" or "to possess". In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description: "Κρατούντας" can be translated as "ruling" or "governing" in English. It refers to a group of people who are currently in a position of power or authority, actively exercising control over others.
