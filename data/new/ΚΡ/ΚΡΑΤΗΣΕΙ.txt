Etymology: The word "κρατήσει" comes from the Greek verb "κρατώ" (krató), which means "to hold" or "to grasp". The suffix "-σει" (-sei) indicates the future tense, so "κρατήσει" means "he/she/it will hold" or "he/she/it will grasp".

Grammar: "κρατήσει" is the third person singular form of the future tense of the verb "κρατώ" (krató). In Greek grammar, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood.

Description: "κρατήσει" is a verb form that expresses an action that will happen in the future. It can be used to talk about someone or something holding or grasping something in the future.
