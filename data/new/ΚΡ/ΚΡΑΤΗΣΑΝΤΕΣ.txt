Etymology: The word "κρατησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (kratéo), which means "to hold" or "to seize". The suffix "-σαντες" (-santes) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of holding or seizing has already been completed.

Grammar: "κρατησαντες" is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the verb "κρατέω" (kratéo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The participle form indicates that the action of holding or seizing has been completed by a group of people.
