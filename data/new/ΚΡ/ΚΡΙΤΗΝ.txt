Etymology: The word "κριτήν" (kritēn) comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *krei-, meaning "to separate" or "to sift".

Grammar: "Κριτήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.
