Etymology: The word "κρατεῖτε" (krateite) comes from the Greek verb "κρατέω" (krateo), which means "to hold" or "to possess."

Grammar: "Κρατεῖτε" is the second person plural present active indicative form of the verb "κρατέω" (krateo). It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are currently holding or possessing something.

Description: "Κρατεῖτε" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of holding or possessing. It can be used in various contexts, such as physical possession, control, or power.
