Etymology: The word "κραζομεν" (krázomen) comes from the Greek verb "κράζω" (krázo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". 

Grammar: "κραζομεν" is the present participle form of the verb "κράζω" (krázo) in the first person plural. It is used to describe an ongoing action of shouting or crying out.

Description: "κραζομεν" refers to the act of shouting or crying out. It can be used to describe a loud and forceful vocal expression of emotions, such as anger, excitement, or distress.
