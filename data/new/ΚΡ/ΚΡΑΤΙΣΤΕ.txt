Etymology: The word "κρατιστε" (kratiste) comes from the Greek verb "κρατώ" (krato), which means "to hold" or "to possess". The suffix "-στε" (-ste) is added to the verb stem to form the imperative mood, giving the meaning "hold" or "possess".

Grammar: "κρατιστε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "κρατώ" (krato). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to hold or possess something.

Description: "κρατιστε" is a word used in the Greek language to command or instruct a group of people to hold or possess something. It is commonly used in situations where a group of people need to collectively hold or possess an object or concept.
