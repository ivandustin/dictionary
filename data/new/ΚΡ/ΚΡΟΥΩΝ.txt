Etymology: The word "κρούων" (krouon) comes from the Greek verb "κρούω" (krouo), which means "to strike" or "to knock." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kreu-.

Grammar: "Κρούων" is the present participle form of the verb "κρούω" (krouo) in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate an ongoing action or state.

Description: "Κρούων" refers to a person who is currently striking or knocking. It can be used to describe someone who is actively hitting or tapping on something.
