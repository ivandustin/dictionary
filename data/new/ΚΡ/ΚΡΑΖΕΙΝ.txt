Etymology: The word "κραζειν" (krazein) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ger- meaning "to cry out" or "to shout".

Grammar: "Κραζειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is a regular verb. In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows: εγώ κράζω (krazo) - I shout, εσύ κράζεις (krazeis) - you shout, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό κράζει (krazei) - he/she/it shouts, εμείς κράζουμε (krazoume) - we shout, εσείς κράζετε (krazete) - you shout, αυτοί/αυτές κράζουν (krazoun) - they shout.

Description: "Κραζειν" is a verb in Greek that means "to shout" or "to cry out". It is used to express the act of making a loud and sharp vocal sound to attract attention, express strong emotions, or communicate a message forcefully.
