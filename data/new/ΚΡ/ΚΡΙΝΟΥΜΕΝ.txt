Etymology: The word "κρινούμεν" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The suffix "-ούμεν" is added to the verb stem to form the present passive participle, indicating that the subject is being judged or decided upon.

Grammar: "κρινούμεν" is the present passive participle of the verb "κρίνω" (krino). In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as nouns themselves. The present passive participle indicates an ongoing action or state in the passive voice.

Description: "κρινούμεν" can be translated as "being judged" or "being decided upon". It describes the subject as the recipient of the action of judging or deciding. The word can be used in various contexts, such as legal proceedings, evaluations, or assessments.
