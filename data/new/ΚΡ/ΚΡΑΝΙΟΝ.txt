Etymology: The word "κρανιον" (krânion) comes from the Greek word "κρανίον" (kraníon), which means "skull".

Grammar: In Greek, "κρανιον" (krânion) is a neuter noun.

Description: "Κρανιον" (krânion) refers to the skull, which is the bony structure that forms the head and protects the brain. It is composed of several bones, including the cranium and the facial bones. The skull plays a crucial role in providing support and protection for the brain and sensory organs.
