Etymology: The word "κραζοντων" (krazontōn) comes from the Greek verb "κράζω" (krázō), which means "to shout" or "to cry out".

Grammar: "κραζοντων" is the present active participle form of the verb "κράζω" (krázō). It is in the masculine, plural, genitive case. In Greek grammar, the present active participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description: "κραζοντων" can be translated as "of those shouting" or "of those crying out". It refers to a group of people who are currently shouting or crying out.
