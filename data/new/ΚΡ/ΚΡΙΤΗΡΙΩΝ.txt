Etymology: The word "κριτηριών" comes from the Greek word "κριτήριον" (kritérion), which means "criterion" or "standard of judgment." 

Grammar: "κριτηριών" is the plural form of the noun "κριτήριον" (kritérion) in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is of neuter gender. 

Description: "κριτηριών" refers to multiple criteria or standards of judgment. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as decision-making, evaluation, or assessment, where multiple factors are considered to determine or measure something.
