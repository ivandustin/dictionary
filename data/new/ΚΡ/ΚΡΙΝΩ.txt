Etymology: The word "κρινω" (krino) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "κρίνω" (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". 

Grammar: In Greek, "κρινω" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in various forms to match the subject and tense of the sentence. 

Description: "κρινω" is a versatile verb that can have several meanings depending on the context. It can mean "to judge", "to decide", "to discern", "to separate", or "to distinguish". It is often used to express the act of making a judgment or decision based on careful consideration or evaluation.
