Etymology: The word "κραζοντες" comes from the Greek verb "κράζω" (krázo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out".

Grammar: "Κραζοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "κράζω" (krázo) in Greek. It is used to describe a continuous action of shouting or crying out in the present tense.

Description: "Κραζοντες" refers to a group of people who are shouting or crying out. It can be used to describe a protest, a demonstration, or any situation where people are raising their voices to express their opinions or emotions.
