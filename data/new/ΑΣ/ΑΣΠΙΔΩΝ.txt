Etymology: The word "ασπιδων" (aspidon) comes from the Greek word "ασπις" (aspis), meaning "shield". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ep-, meaning "to protect".

Grammar: "Ασπιδων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "ασπις" (aspis), which means "shield" in Greek. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Ασπιδων" refers to multiple shields. In ancient Greece, shields were an essential part of a warrior's equipment, providing protection in battle. The word can also be used metaphorically to represent defense or protection in a broader sense.
