Etymology: The word "ασεβείαν" comes from the Greek word "ασέβεια" (asebeia), which means "impiety" or "lack of reverence." It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "σέβομαι" (sevomai), meaning "to revere" or "to worship."

Grammar: "Ασεβείαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ασεβείαν" refers to the act or state of being impious or lacking reverence. It is used to describe behavior or attitudes that show disrespect or disregard for religious or moral principles.
