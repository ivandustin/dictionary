Etymology: The word "ασπονδοι" (aspondoi) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "without," and the word "σπονδοι" (spondoi), meaning "libations" or "offerings." 

Grammar: "Ασπονδοι" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word "ασπονδοι" is used to describe something or someone as "without libations" or "without offerings." It can be used in a literal sense to refer to a lack of offerings made to the gods, or in a figurative sense to describe someone who is ungrateful or unappreciative.
