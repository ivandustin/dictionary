Etymology: The word "ασεβείς" comes from the Greek word "α-" (a-), which means "not," and "σέβομαι" (sevomai), which means "to revere" or "to worship." Therefore, "ασεβείς" can be translated as "not revering" or "not worshiping."

Grammar: "Ασεβείς" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe people who do not show reverence or respect towards gods, religious beliefs, or sacred things.

Description: "Ασεβείς" is a term used in Greek to refer to individuals who lack piety or show disrespect towards religious or sacred matters. It can also be used more broadly to describe those who disregard moral or ethical principles.
