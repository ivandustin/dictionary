Etymology: The word "ασαλευτον" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-) meaning "not" or "without," and the word "σαλευτον" (saleuton) meaning "shaken" or "disturbed."

Grammar: "Ασαλευτον" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description: "Ασαλευτον" describes something that is unshaken, undisturbed, or steady. It can be used to describe a physical object or a state of mind.
