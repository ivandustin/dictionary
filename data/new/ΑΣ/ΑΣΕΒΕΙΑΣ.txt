Etymology: The word "ασεβείας" comes from the Greek word "ασέβεια" (asebeia), which means "impiety" or "irreverence". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the word "σέβομαι" (sevomai), meaning "to respect" or "to revere".

Grammar: "ασεβείας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "ασεβείας" refers to the concept of impiety or irreverence. It is the state or quality of being disrespectful or lacking in proper reverence, especially towards religious or sacred things.
