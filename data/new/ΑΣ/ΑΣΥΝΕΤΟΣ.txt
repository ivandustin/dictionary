Etymology: The word "ασυνετος" (asynetos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "συνετος" (synetos), meaning "intelligent" or "wise." Therefore, "ασυνετος" can be translated as "unintelligent" or "foolish."

Grammar: In Greek, "ασυνετος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it becomes "ασυνετος" (asynetos), while if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it becomes "ασυνετη" (asyneti), and so on.

Description: "ασυνετος" is used to describe someone or something that lacks intelligence, wisdom, or good judgment. It implies a lack of understanding or awareness. It can be used to criticize someone's actions, decisions, or behavior as foolish or irrational.
