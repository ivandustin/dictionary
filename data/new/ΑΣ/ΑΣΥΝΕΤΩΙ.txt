Etymology: The word "ασυνετως" (asynetos) comes from the Greek prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the adjective "συνετος" (synetos), meaning "intelligent" or "wise." Therefore, "ασυνετως" can be translated as "foolishly" or "stupidly."

Grammar: "ασυνετως" is an adverb in the Greek language. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate the manner in which an action is performed.

Description: "ασυνετως" is used to describe actions or behaviors that are done in a foolish or unintelligent manner. It implies a lack of wisdom, understanding, or good judgment.
