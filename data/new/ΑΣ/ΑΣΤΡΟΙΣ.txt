Etymology: The word "αστρος" (astros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂stḗr, meaning "star."

Grammar: In Greek, "αστρος" (astros) is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "αστρου" (astrou).

Description: "Αστρος" (astros) means "star" in Greek. It refers to a celestial body that emits light and is visible in the night sky. In Greek mythology, stars were often associated with gods and heroes. Stars have been studied and admired by humans for thousands of years, and they play a significant role in astronomy and astrology.
