Etymology: The word "ασεβής" (asevís) comes from the Greek prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "σεβής" (sevís), meaning "pious" or "reverent." Therefore, "ασεβής" (asevís) can be translated as "impious" or "irreverent."

Grammar: "Ασεβής" (asevís) is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ασεβής" (asevís) is used to describe someone who lacks respect or reverence towards religious or moral principles. It refers to a person who is disrespectful, irreverent, or impious.
