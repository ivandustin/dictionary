Etymology: The word "αστερος" (asteros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂stḗr, meaning "star."

Grammar: In Greek, "αστερος" (asteros) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "αστερος" (asteros), the genitive singular form "αστερου" (asterou), and the accusative singular form "αστερα" (astera).

Description: "Αστερος" (asteros) means "star" in Greek. It refers to the celestial object that emits light and is visible in the night sky. In Greek mythology, stars were often associated with gods and heroes. Stars have been a subject of fascination and study throughout human history.
