Etymology: The word "ασαλευτος" (asaleutos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "σαλευτος" (saleutos), meaning "shaken" or "disturbed." 

Grammar: "Ασαλευτος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It is used to describe something or someone that is unshaken, undisturbed, or steady.

Description: "Ασαλευτος" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of stability, calmness, and steadfastness. It is often used to describe a person who remains composed and unaffected by external circumstances or emotions. It can also be used to describe an object or situation that is unchanging or unwavering.
