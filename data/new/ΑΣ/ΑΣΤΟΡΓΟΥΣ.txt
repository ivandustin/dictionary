Etymology: The word "αστοργούς" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not") and the word "στοργή" (meaning "affection" or "love"). 

Grammar: "Αστοργούς" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word "αστοργούς" describes someone or something that lacks affection or love. It can be used to refer to individuals who are cold, indifferent, or unloving.
