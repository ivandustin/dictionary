Etymology: The word "ασεβείς" (aseveis) comes from the Greek root "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "σέβομαι" (sevomai), meaning "to respect" or "to revere." Therefore, "ασεβείς" (aseveis) can be translated as "not reverent" or "irreverent."

Grammar: "ασεβείς" (aseveis) is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe people or actions that lack respect or reverence towards something, often referring to religious or moral principles.

Description: "ασεβείς" (aseveis) is a term used in Greek to describe individuals who do not show proper respect or reverence towards religious beliefs, customs, or moral values. It implies a disregard for established norms and principles.
