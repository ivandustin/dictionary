Etymology: The word "αστραπή" (astrape) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀστραπή" (astrapē), meaning "lightning". 

Grammar: "Αστραπή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "αστραπής" (astrapēs). 

Description: "Αστραπή" refers to the natural phenomenon of lightning, which is a sudden and powerful electrical discharge that occurs during a thunderstorm. It is characterized by a bright flash of light and a loud sound. Lightning is caused by the buildup and discharge of electrical energy in the atmosphere.
