Etymology: The word "ασοφοι" (asophoi) comes from the Greek word "α" (a), which means "not," and "σοφός" (sophos), which means "wise" or "knowledgeable." Therefore, "ασοφοι" (asophoi) can be translated as "unwise" or "ignorant."

Grammar: "Ασοφοι" (asophoi) is the plural form of the adjective "ασοφος" (asophos). In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. So, if the noun is plural and masculine, the adjective will also be plural and masculine.

Description: "Ασοφοι" (asophoi) is an adjective in Greek that describes someone or something as lacking wisdom or knowledge. It can be used to refer to individuals who are ignorant or uninformed about a particular subject.
