Etymology: The word "αστηρ" (astēr) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*h₂stḗr" which means "star".

Grammar: In Greek, "αστηρ" (astēr) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be declined as follows:

- Singular: αστηρ (astēr)
- Plural: αστερες (asteres)

Description: "Αστηρ" (astēr) means "star" in Greek. It refers to the celestial body that emits light and is visible in the night sky. In Greek mythology, stars were often associated with gods and represented divine beings.
