Etymology: The word "ασχημοσύνην" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "ασχήμων" (aschimon), meaning "ugly" or "disgraceful," and the suffix "-σύνην" (-synen), which denotes the state or quality of something.

Grammar: "Ασχημοσύνην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ασχημοσύνην" refers to the state or quality of being ugly or disgraceful. It can be used to describe physical ugliness or moral disgrace.
