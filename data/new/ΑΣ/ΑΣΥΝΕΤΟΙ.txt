Etymology: The word "ασυνετοι" (asynetoi) comes from the Greek prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the adjective "συνετοι" (synetoi), meaning "wise" or "intelligent." Therefore, "ασυνετοι" can be translated as "unwise" or "foolish."

Grammar: "Ασυνετοι" is the plural form of the adjective "ασυνετος" (asynetos) in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people or things that lack wisdom or intelligence.

Description: "Ασυνετοι" is an adjective in Greek that is used to describe individuals or groups who are considered foolish or lacking in wisdom. It is often used to criticize someone's actions, decisions, or behavior.
