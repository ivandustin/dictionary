Etymology: The word "ασυνθετους" (asynthetous) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "συνθετος" (synthetos), meaning "compound." 

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "ασυνθετους" is an adjective in the masculine accusative plural form. It is used to describe nouns that are not compound or complex.

Description: "ασυνθετους" is used to describe something that is simple or not composed of multiple parts. It indicates that the noun it modifies is not a compound or a combination of different elements.
