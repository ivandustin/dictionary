Etymology: The word "ασεβης" (asevēs) comes from the Greek prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "σεβης" (sebēs), meaning "pious" or "reverent." Therefore, "ασεβης" can be translated as "impious" or "irreverent."

Grammar: In Greek, "ασεβης" is an adjective that agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "ασεβης" is used to describe someone who lacks piety, reverence, or respect towards the gods, religious practices, or sacred things. It can also refer to someone who disrespects authority, laws, or moral principles.
