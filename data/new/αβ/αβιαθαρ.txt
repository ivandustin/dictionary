Etymology: The name Abiathar is of Hebrew origin and is composed of two words: "Abi" meaning "my father" and "athar" meaning "remnant". Therefore, Abiathar means "my father is a remnant".

Grammar: Abiathar is a proper noun and is typically used as a masculine name.

Description: Abiathar was a high priest in the Old Testament of the Bible, serving during the reigns of King David and King Solomon. He was the son of Ahimelech, who was also a high priest. Abiathar is known for his loyalty to David during a time of political turmoil and for his role in the anointing of Solomon as king. He was eventually banished by Solomon for supporting Adonijah's claim to the throne.
