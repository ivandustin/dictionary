Word: αβελ

Etymology:
The word αβελ (Abel) is a Greek transliteration of the Hebrew name הֶבֶל (Hevel), which means "breath" or "vapor". In the Bible, Abel was the second son of Adam and Eve, who was murdered by his brother Cain.

Grammar:
In Greek, αβελ is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It has no plural form.

Description:
αβελ is a proper noun that refers to the biblical character Abel. He is known for being a righteous man who was murdered by his jealous brother Cain. The story of Cain and Abel is often used as an example of the consequences of jealousy and the importance of brotherly love. The name Abel has been used in various cultures and languages throughout history, and is still used as a given name today.
