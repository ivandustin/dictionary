The word "χηρας" (chiras) is a Greek noun that means "widow" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek adjective "χηρός" (chirós), which means "bereft" or "deprived". 

Grammar: "χηρας" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: The word "χηρας" refers to a woman whose husband has died and who has not remarried. In ancient Greek society, widows were often considered vulnerable and in need of protection. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a widow.
