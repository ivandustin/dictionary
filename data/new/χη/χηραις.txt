The word "χηραις" (cherais) is a Greek noun in the feminine plural form, which means "widows". It is derived from the Greek word "χήρα" (chira), which means "widow". In Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was known as "χήραις πατρώοις" (cherais patrois), which means "protectress of widows". The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to widows who were supported by the early Christian community.
