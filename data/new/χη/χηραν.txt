The word "χηραν" (khēran) is a Greek noun that means "widow" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek adjective "χηρός" (khēros), which means "bereft" or "deprived". 

Grammar: "χηραν" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Description: The word refers to a woman whose husband has died and who has not remarried. In ancient Greek society, widows were often vulnerable and dependent on their families or the community for support. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a widow.
