Etymology: The word "τραπεζείταις" comes from the Greek word "τράπεζα" (trapeza), meaning "table" or "bank". It is derived from the verb "τραπέζειν" (trapezein), which means "to eat at a table" or "to transact business at a table".

Grammar: "Τραπεζείταις" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Τραπεζείταις" refers to the bankers or money-changers in ancient Greece. They were responsible for conducting financial transactions, exchanging currencies, and lending money.
