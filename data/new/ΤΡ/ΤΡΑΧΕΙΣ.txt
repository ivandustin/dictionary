Etymology: The word "τραχείς" (tracheis) comes from the Greek word "τραχύς" (trachys), meaning rough or harsh.

Grammar: In Greek, "τραχείς" is the plural form of the adjective "τραχύς" (trachys). It is used to describe nouns in the masculine or feminine gender.

Description: "Τραχείς" is an adjective in Greek that means rough, harsh, or rugged. It is often used to describe surfaces, textures, or voices that are not smooth or gentle.
