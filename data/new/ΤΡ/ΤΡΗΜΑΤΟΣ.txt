Etymology: The word "τρηματος" (trēmatos) comes from the Greek verb "τρηματίζω" (trēmatizō), which means "to bore" or "to pierce". 

Grammar: "Τρηματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Τρηματος" refers to something that is pierced or bored. It can be used to describe a physical hole or opening, as well as a metaphorical or abstract concept of penetration or perforation.
