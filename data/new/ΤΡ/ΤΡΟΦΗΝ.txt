Etymology: The word "τροφήν" (trophēn) comes from the Greek verb "τρέφω" (trephō), which means "to nourish" or "to feed."

Grammar: In Greek, "τροφήν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative form of the noun "τροφή" (trophē), which means "nourishment" or "food."

Description: "Τροφήν" refers to food or nourishment. It is used to describe any substance that is consumed by living organisms to sustain life, provide energy, and promote growth and development.
