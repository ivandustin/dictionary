Etymology: The word "τριβούς" comes from the Greek word "τρίβω" (trívo), which means "to rub" or "to wear away". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *terh₃-, meaning "to rub" or "to turn".

Grammar: In Greek, "τριβούς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "τριβοῦ" (triboû).

Description: "Τριβούς" refers to a person who rubs or wears away something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is persistent, tenacious, or hardworking.
