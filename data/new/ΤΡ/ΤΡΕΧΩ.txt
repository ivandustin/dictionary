Etymology: The word "τρέχω" (trecho) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "τρέχειν" (trechein), meaning "to run". 

Grammar: "Τρέχω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is in the present tense. It is used to express the action of running in the first person singular form.

Description: "Τρέχω" is a common Greek verb that means "to run". It is used to describe the action of moving quickly on foot, typically with a rapid pace. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing physical running or metaphorical running, such as running errands or running a race.
