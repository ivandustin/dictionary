Etymology: The word "τροφής" (trofis) comes from the Greek verb "τρέφω" (trefo), which means "to nourish" or "to feed". 

Grammar: "Τροφής" is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or origin. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence.

Description: "Τροφής" refers to food or nourishment. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that provides sustenance or support.
