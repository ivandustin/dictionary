Etymology: The word "τραυματισαντες" comes from the Greek verb "τραυματίζω" (traumatízo), which means "to injure" or "to wound". The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates past tense and active voice.

Grammar: "Τραυματισαντες" is the plural form of the verb "τραυματίζω" in the past tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have performed the action of injuring or wounding.

Description: "Τραυματισαντες" is a Greek word that refers to a group of individuals who have caused injuries or wounds. It can be used to describe a specific incident or event where multiple people were involved in causing harm.
