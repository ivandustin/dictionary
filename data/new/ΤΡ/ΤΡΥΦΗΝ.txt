Etymology: The word "τρυφην" (tryphein) comes from the Greek verb "τρέφω" (trepho), which means "to nourish" or "to feed". 

Grammar: "Τρυφην" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: The word "τρυφην" means "to nourish" or "to feed" in Greek. It refers to the act of providing sustenance or nourishment to someone or something.
