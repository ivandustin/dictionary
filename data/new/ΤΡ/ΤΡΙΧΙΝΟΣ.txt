Etymology: The word "τριχινός" (trichinos) comes from the Greek word "τρίχα" (tricha), meaning "hair". It refers to the microscopic hair-like structures found in the muscle tissue of animals infected with the parasitic worm Trichinella.

Grammar: In Greek, "τριχινός" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Τριχινός" is used to describe something that is related to or infected with trichinosis, a disease caused by the ingestion of undercooked meat containing Trichinella larvae. It is commonly used to describe infected meat or the parasitic worms themselves.
