Etymology: The word "τρεις" (treis) comes from the Ancient Greek word "τρεῖς" (treîs), which means "three".

Grammar: In Greek, "τρεις" is the nominative form of the number three. It is used to indicate the quantity of three items or individuals.

Description: "Τρεις" is a cardinal number in Greek, representing the quantity of three. It is used in various contexts, such as counting objects, expressing age, or indicating a specific number.
