Etymology: The word "τροφος" (trofos) comes from the Greek word "τρέφω" (trefo), which means "to nourish" or "to feed". 

Grammar: In Greek, "τροφος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "τροφου" (trofou).

Description: "Τροφος" is a Greek word that means "nourisher" or "provider of food". It refers to someone or something that provides sustenance or nourishment to others. It can be used to describe a person who feeds or takes care of others, or it can refer to food itself.
