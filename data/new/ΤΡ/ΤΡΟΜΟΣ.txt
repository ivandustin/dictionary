Etymology: The word "τρομος" (tromos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "τρέμω" (tremo), which means "to tremble" or "to shake". 

Grammar: In Greek, "τρομος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. 

Description: "Τρομος" refers to the feeling of fear, terror, or dread. It is often used to describe a strong and intense fear that can cause trembling or shaking.
