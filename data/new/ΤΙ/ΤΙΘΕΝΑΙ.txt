Etymology: The word "τιθέναι" (tithenai) comes from the Greek verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put". 

Grammar: "Τιθέναι" is the present active infinitive form of the verb "τίθημι". In Greek grammar, infinitives are non-finite verb forms that function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The present active infinitive form indicates the action of placing or putting in the present tense.

Description: "Τιθέναι" is a Greek word that refers to the act of placing or putting something. It can be used in various contexts, such as placing objects, arranging things, or even metaphorically referring to establishing or setting something in motion.
