Etymology: The word "τίτον" comes from the Greek language.

Grammar: "τίτον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the accusative case.

Description: "τίτον" is a Greek word that means "titan" in English. In Greek mythology, the Titans were a race of powerful deities who ruled during the Golden Age. They were the children of Uranus (the sky) and Gaia (the earth). The Titans were eventually overthrown by the Olympian gods led by Zeus.
