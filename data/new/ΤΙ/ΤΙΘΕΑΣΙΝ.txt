Etymology: The word "τιθεασιν" (pronounced "tith-e-a-sin") comes from the Greek verb "τιθημι" (tithēmi), which means "to place" or "to set". 

Grammar: "τιθεασιν" is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb "τιθημι" (tithēmi). In Greek grammar, it belongs to the thematic conjugation class and is used to express actions or states that are happening in the present and are performed by a third person plural subject.

Description: "τιθεασιν" is used to describe an action or state of placing or setting something in the present tense. It indicates that a third person plural subject is currently placing or setting something.
