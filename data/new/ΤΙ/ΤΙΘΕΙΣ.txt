Etymology: The word "τιθεις" (pronounced "tithis") comes from the Greek verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put". 

Grammar: "Τιθεις" is the second person singular form of the present indicative active of the verb "τίθημι". It is used when addressing someone directly and means "you place" or "you put".

Description: "Τιθεις" is a Greek word that refers to the action of placing or putting something in a specific location. It can be used in various contexts, such as placing objects, arranging things, or positioning oneself.
