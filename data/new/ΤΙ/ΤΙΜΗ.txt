Etymology: The word "τιμή" (timē) comes from the Ancient Greek word "τιμάω" (timáō), which means "to honor" or "to value."

Grammar: In Greek, "τιμή" is a feminine noun. It is in the singular form, and its plural form is "τιμές" (timés).

Description: "Τιμή" is a Greek word that translates to "honor" or "value" in English. It refers to the respect or esteem given to someone or something. It can also mean the price or cost of something. In Greek culture, "τιμή" is highly valued and is associated with dignity and worth.
