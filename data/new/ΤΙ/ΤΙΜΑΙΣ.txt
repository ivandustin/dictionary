Etymology: The word "τιμαίς" (timais) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "τιμάω" (timao), which means "to honor" or "to value."

Grammar: In Greek, "τιμαίς" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a feminine noun.

Description: "Τιμαίς" is a Greek word that can be translated as "honors" or "values." It refers to the recognition or esteem given to someone or something. It can also denote the price or worth of something.
