The word "κοπιασαντες" is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically the aorist active participle of the verb "κοπιάζω" (kopiázō). 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "κόπος" (kópos), meaning "labor" or "toil". 

Grammar: As a participle, "κοπιασαντες" functions as an adjective, describing the subject of the sentence as having completed the action of the verb. In this case, it would mean "having labored" or "having toiled". 

Description: The word "κοπιασαντες" is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the disciples of Jesus who worked hard to spread his message. It can also be used more generally to describe anyone who has worked hard or toiled to achieve something.
