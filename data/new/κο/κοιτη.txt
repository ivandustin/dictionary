The word "κοιτη" (koitē) is a Greek noun that means "bed" or "couch". It is derived from the verb "κείμαι" (keimai), which means "to lie down" or "to recline". In terms of grammar, "κοιτη" is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in various cases, such as the genitive case "κοιτης" (koitēs) or the accusative case "κοιτην" (koitēn). In ancient Greek literature, the word "κοιτη" is often used to refer to the bed of a god or goddess, such as the bed of Zeus or Hera.
