The word "κοιταις" (koitais) is a Greek noun in the plural form, which means "beds" or "couches". It is derived from the Greek verb "κείμαι" (keimai), which means "to lie down" or "to recline". In Greek mythology, the god of sleep, Hypnos, is often depicted as lying on a bed of feathers or on a couch made of ebony and ivory. The word "κοιταις" is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels, to describe the places where people reclined during meals or where Jesus healed the sick. In terms of grammar, "κοιταις" is a feminine noun in the dative or accusative case.
