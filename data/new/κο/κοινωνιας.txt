The word "κοινωνιας" (koinonias) is a Greek noun that can be translated to mean "fellowship" or "community." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "κοινός" (koinos), which means "common" or "shared." 

Grammar: "κοινωνιας" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of the noun "κοινωνία" (koinonia). 

Description: The word is often used in a religious context to refer to the fellowship or community of believers in a church or religious group. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of shared community or partnership.
