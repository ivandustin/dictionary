The word "κοινωσαι" (koinosai) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "κοινός" (koinos), which means "common" or "shared". The suffix "-σαι" (-sai) is a conjugation ending for the aorist tense, indicating past action.

Grammar: "Κοινωσαι" is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb, meaning it is a command directed towards one person to perform a specific action in the past. 

Description: The word "κοινωσαι" can be translated to mean "to make common" or "to share". It is often used in a religious context, referring to the sharing of communion or the act of making something holy or sacred. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the act of sharing or making something common among a group of people.
