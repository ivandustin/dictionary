Word: κοσμειν

Etymology:
The word κοσμειν (kosmein) is derived from the Greek word κόσμος (kosmos), which means "order" or "world." The verb κοσμειν (kosmein) means "to order," "to arrange," or "to adorn."

Grammar:
κοσμειν (kosmein) is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the basic form of the verb and does not indicate any specific subject or tense.

Description:
κοσμειν (kosmein) is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to the act of arranging or organizing something, such as a room or a collection of objects. It can also refer to the act of adorning or beautifying something, such as a person or a piece of art. In a broader sense, κοσμειν (kosmein) can refer to the act of bringing order or harmony to a situation or environment.
