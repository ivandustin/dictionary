Word: κοιναις

Etymology: κοιναις is a Greek word that comes from the root word κοινός (koinós), which means "common" or "shared". The suffix -αις (-ais) is a feminine plural ending, indicating that the word is referring to multiple common or shared things.

Grammar: κοιναις is a feminine plural adjective in the dative case. It is used to describe or modify a noun in the dative case, indicating that the noun is the recipient of the action or is in a state of being affected by something.

Description: κοιναις can be translated to mean "common" or "shared". It is often used to describe things that are held in common or shared by a group of people. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe things like customs, traditions, or beliefs that are shared by a community.
