Word: κοπος

Etymology:
The word κοπος (kopos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb κόπτω (kopto), which means "to cut" or "to strike". The noun κοπος (kopos) originally referred to the act of striking or cutting, but later came to mean "toil" or "hard work".

In Greek, κοπος (kopos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is declined as follows:

Nominative: κοπος (kopos)
Genitive: κοπου (kopou)
Dative: κοπῳ (kopōi)
Accusative: κοπον (kopon)
Vocative: κοπε (kope)

Plural:
Nominative: κοποι (kpoi)
Genitive: κοπων (kopōn)
Dative: κοποις (kpois)
Accusative: κοπους (kopous)
Vocative: κοποι (kpoi)

Description:
κοπος (kopos) refers to hard work, toil, or laborious effort. It can also refer to physical exhaustion or fatigue resulting from such work. The word is often used in a negative sense, implying that the work is difficult or unpleasant. However, it can also be used in a positive sense, to describe the satisfaction that comes from accomplishing a difficult task.
