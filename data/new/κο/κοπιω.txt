The word "κοπιω" (kopio) is a Greek verb that means "to toil" or "to work hard". It comes from the Greek word "κόπος" (kopos), which means "labor" or "hard work". 

In terms of grammar, "κοπιω" is a first-conjugation verb in the present tense. Its stem is "κοπι-" and it is conjugated as follows:

- Κοπιώ (kopió) - I toil
- Κοπιάζεις (kopiázis) - You toil
- Κοπιάζει (kopiázi) - He/she/it toils
- Κοπιάζουμε (kopiázoume) - We toil
- Κοπιάζετε (kopiázete) - You (plural) toil
- Κοπιάζουν (kopiázoun) - They toil

Overall, "κοπιω" is a word that describes the act of working hard and putting in effort to achieve a goal.
