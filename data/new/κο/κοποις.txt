Word: κοποις

Etymology:
The word κοποις (kopois) is a noun in Greek language. It is derived from the verb κοπος (kopos) which means "toil" or "labour". The suffix -οις (-ois) is a common ending for nouns in Greek.

Grammar:
κοποις (kopois) is a plural noun in the dative case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
κοποις (kopois) refers to the toils or labours that someone has undergone. It can also refer to the hardships or difficulties that someone has faced. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe the struggles and challenges of life.
