Word: κολποις

Etymology:
The word κολποις (kolpois) is derived from the Greek word κόλπος (kolpos), which means "bosom" or "fold." It is often used in the context of a bay or gulf, which is a natural fold or indentation in the coastline.

Grammar:
κολποις is a noun in the dative plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "in the folds" or "in the bays."

Description:
κολποις is a term used in Greek literature to describe the folds or bays of a coastline. It is often used in poetry and prose to evoke a sense of natural beauty and tranquility. In modern usage, it can also refer to the folds or creases of clothing or fabric.
