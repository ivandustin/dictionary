Word: κοινην

Etymology: κοινην is a Greek word that comes from the root word κοινός (koinós), which means "common" or "shared". 

Grammar: κοινην is the accusative singular form of the adjective κοινός (koinós), which means "common" or "shared". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: κοινην is used to describe something that is common or shared. It can be used to describe a common language, a shared experience, or a general concept that is widely accepted. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the common people or the general public.
