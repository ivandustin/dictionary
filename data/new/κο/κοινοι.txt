The word "κοινοι" (koinoi) is a Greek adjective that means "common" or "shared". It comes from the Greek word "κοινός" (koinos), which has the same meaning. 

In terms of grammar, "κοινοι" is the masculine plural form of the adjective. The feminine plural form is "κοινές" (koines), and the neuter plural form is "κοινά" (koina). 

In ancient Greek, "κοινοι" was often used to describe things that were shared by a group of people, such as common property or shared beliefs. It could also be used to describe things that were ordinary or unremarkable. 

Overall, "κοινοι" is a versatile adjective that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe things that are common or shared.
