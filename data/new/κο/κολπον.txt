Word: κολπον

The word κολπον (kolpon) is derived from the Greek word κόλπος (kolpos), which means "bosom" or "fold." It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-, which means "to bend" or "to turn."

In Greek, κολπον is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form κόλπου (kolpou).

Description:
The word κολπον refers to a fold or a recess in a surface, such as a bay or a gulf. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a hidden or secret place. In ancient Greek literature, the word was often used to describe the folds of a garment or the bosom of a woman. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a bay or a gulf.
