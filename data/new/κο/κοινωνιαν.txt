The word "κοινωνιαν" (koinōnian) is a Greek noun that means "fellowship" or "communion." It is derived from the root word "κοινωνός" (koinōnos), which means "partner" or "sharer." In Greek, the word is spelled κοινωνίαν with an accent on the second to last syllable.

In terms of grammar, "κοινωνιαν" is a singular accusative noun in the feminine gender. It is often used in Christian contexts to refer to the sharing of spiritual experiences and beliefs among believers.

Overall, "κοινωνιαν" is a word that emphasizes the importance of community and sharing in both spiritual and secular contexts.
