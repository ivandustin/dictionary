Word: κολακιας

Etymology:
The word κολακιας (kolakias) comes from the Greek word κόλαξ (kolax), which means "flatterer" or "sycophant". The suffix -ιας (-ias) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns indicating a person who performs a certain action or has a certain quality.

Grammar:
κολακιας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the singular and plural, and its nominative, genitive, accusative, vocative, and dative forms are all the same: κολακιας.

Description:
κολακιας is a word used to describe a person who flatters or sucks up to others in order to gain favor or advantage. It has a negative connotation and is often used to describe someone who is insincere or manipulative. The word can also be used to describe the act of flattery itself.
