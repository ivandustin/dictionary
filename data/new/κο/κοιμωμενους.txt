The word "κοιμωμενους" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "sleeping" or "being asleep". It is derived from the root word "κοιμάω" (koimao), which means "to put to sleep" or "to cause to sleep". In terms of grammar, it is in the masculine, plural, accusative case, which means it is used to describe multiple male subjects who are the direct object of the sentence.
