The word "κοινωνειτω" (koinōneitō) is a Greek verb that means "let him/her/it share/participate". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root word "κοινωνός" (koinōnos), which means "partner" or "sharer". 

Grammar: "κοινωνειτω" is a third person singular present active imperative verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to share or participate in something. 

Description: The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the Bible where it is used to encourage believers to share with others and participate in the community. It can also be used in a more general sense to encourage people to work together and share resources for the common good.
