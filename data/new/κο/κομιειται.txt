Word: κομιείται

Etymology: κομιείται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κομίζω (komizo), which means "to bring" or "to carry". The suffix -είται (-eitai) is a third-person singular present passive indicative ending, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of the verb.

Grammar: κομιείται is a present tense, passive voice verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word κομίζω (komizo) and the present tense ending -είται (-eitai). The verb is used to describe an action that is being done to the subject, rather than the subject doing the action.

Description: κομιείται means "is being brought" or "is being carried" in English. It is often used to describe the passive action of someone or something being transported or delivered.
