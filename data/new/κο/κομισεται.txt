The word "κομισεται" is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "κομίζω" which means "to bring, to carry, to convey". The suffix "-εται" indicates that the verb is in the passive voice, meaning that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it.

In terms of grammar, "κομισεται" is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ίζω". It can be translated to English as "is brought" or "is carried".

Overall, "κομισεται" is a simple verb that describes the action of something being brought or carried, and is commonly used in everyday Greek language.
