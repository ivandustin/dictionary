Word: κομαι

Etymology:
The word κομαι (komai) is derived from the Greek verb κόπτω (kopto), which means "to cut" or "to strike." It is related to the word κόμη (kome), which means "hair," and is thought to refer to the practice of cutting or trimming hair.

Grammar:
κομαι (komai) is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person singular form of the verb κόπτω (kopto), which means "I cut" or "I strike." In the middle voice, the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description:
κομαι (komai) can be translated as "I cut myself" or "I strike myself." It is often used in the context of self-flagellation or self-mutilation, and is also used metaphorically to describe intense emotional or mental anguish. The word has a strong connotation of pain and suffering.
