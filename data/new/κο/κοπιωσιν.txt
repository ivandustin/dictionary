Word: κοπιωσιν

Etymology: κοπιωσιν is the present active indicative third person plural form of the Greek verb κοπιάζω (kopiázō), which means "to toil" or "to work hard". The word comes from the root κόπος (kópos), which means "labor" or "toil".

Grammar: κοπιωσιν is a verb form in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. It is in the active voice, meaning that the subject is performing the action. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description: κοπιωσιν can be translated to "they are toiling" or "they are working hard". It is a word that conveys the idea of physical or mental exertion, often in the context of labor or work. The word can be used to describe a group of people who are putting in a lot of effort to achieve a goal or complete a task.
