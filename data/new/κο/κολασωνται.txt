Word: κολασώνται

Etymology: The word κολασώνται comes from the Greek verb κολάζω (kolazo), which means "to punish" or "to chastise". The suffix -νται (-ntai) indicates that the verb is in the passive voice, meaning "they are being punished" or "they are being chastised".

Grammar: κολασώνται is a third-person plural present passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -νται to the stem κολάζω and conjugating it in the present tense. The verb is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of the verb.

Description: κολασώνται is a Greek verb that means "they are being punished" or "they are being chastised". It is often used in a religious or moral context, referring to the punishment of sinners or wrongdoers. The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the punishment is ongoing or repeated.
