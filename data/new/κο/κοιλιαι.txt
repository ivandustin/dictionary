The word "κοιλιαι" (koilai) is a plural noun in Greek that means "hollows" or "cavities". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek adjective "κοῖλος" (koilos), which means "hollow" or "concave".

Grammar: "Κοιλιαι" is the nominative plural form of the noun. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: The word can refer to physical hollows or cavities, such as those found in the body or in rocks. It can also be used metaphorically to describe empty spaces or voids.
