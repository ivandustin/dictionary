The word "κοινου" (koinou) is a Greek adjective that means "common" or "shared". It is derived from the noun "κοινός" (koinos), which also means "common" or "shared". In terms of grammar, "κοινου" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. 

In ancient Greek, "κοινου" was often used to describe things that were shared by a group of people, such as a common meal or a communal property. It could also be used to describe a common language or culture. In modern Greek, "κοινου" is still used in a similar way, but it can also be used to describe something that is ordinary or average. 

Overall, "κοινου" is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe things that are shared, common, or ordinary.
