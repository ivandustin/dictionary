Word: κοσμος

Etymology:
The word κοσμος (kosmos) comes from the Greek language and has its roots in the verb κοσμεῖν (kosmein), which means "to order" or "to arrange." In ancient Greek philosophy, the term was used to refer to the universe as an ordered and harmonious whole.

Grammar:
In Greek, κοσμος is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: ὁ κόσμος (ho kosmos)
Genitive: τοῦ κόσμου (tou kosmou)
Dative: τῷ κόσμῳ (tō kosmō)
Accusative: τὸν κόσμον (ton kosmon)
Vocative: ὦ κόσμε (ō kosme)

Plural:
Nominative: οἱ κόσμοι (hoi kosmoi)
Genitive: τῶν κόσμων (tōn kosmōn)
Dative: τοῖς κόσμοις (tois kosmois)
Accusative: τοὺς κόσμους (tous kosmous)
Vocative: ὦ κόσμοι (ō kosmoi)

Description:
κοσμος is a Greek word that has a variety of meanings depending on the context in which it is used. It can refer to the universe, the world, or the order and arrangement of things. In ancient Greek philosophy, it was used to describe the harmonious and ordered nature of the universe. In modern Greek, it is often used to refer to the world or society as a whole.
