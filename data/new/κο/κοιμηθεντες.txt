The word "κοιμηθεντες" is a Greek verb that is in the aorist participle passive voice. It comes from the root word "κοιμάω" which means "to put to sleep" or "to cause to sleep". The participle form "κοιμηθεντες" means "having been put to sleep" or "having been caused to sleep". 

In terms of grammar, "κοιμηθεντες" is a participle, which is a type of verbal adjective that describes the action of the verb. It is in the aorist tense, which refers to a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action themselves.

Overall, "κοιμηθεντες" is a word that describes a past action of being put to sleep or caused to sleep.
