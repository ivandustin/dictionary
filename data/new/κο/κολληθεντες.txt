Word: κολληθεντες

Etymology: κολληθεντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb κολλάω (kollao), which means "to glue" or "to stick together". The suffix -θεντες (-thentes) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the action of gluing or sticking together has been done to the subject.

Grammar: κολληθεντες is a passive participle in the nominative plural form. It is derived from the verb κολλάω (kollao), which is in the aorist tense. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action themselves. In this case, the subject is plural and nominative, meaning that it is the one being glued or stuck together.

Description: κολληθεντες can be translated to mean "having been glued/stuck together". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe people who have become closely united or attached to each other, such as in a friendship or alliance. The word can also be used in a more literal sense to describe objects that have been glued or stuck together.
