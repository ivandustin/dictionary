Etymology: The word "υβρισαν" (ubrisan) comes from the Greek verb "υβρίζω" (ubrizo), which means "to insult" or "to treat with violence." It is derived from the noun "υβρις" (ubris), meaning "outrage" or "insolence."

Grammar: "Υβρισαν" is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb "υβρίζω" (ubrizo). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Υβρισαν" is a Greek verb that means "they insulted" or "they treated with violence." It indicates that a group of people performed the action of insulting or treating someone with violence in the past.
