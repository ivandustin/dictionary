Grammar: "Υβριν" (ubrin) is a noun in the accusative case. It is the direct object of a sentence and refers to the action of committing hybris.

Description: "Υβριν" (ubrin) refers to the act of committing hybris, which is an ancient Greek concept. Hybris is the excessive pride or arrogance that leads to disrespect, insolence, or violence towards others.
