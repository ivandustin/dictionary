Etymology: The word "μισώ" (miso) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "μίσος" (misos), which means "hatred" or "dislike".

Grammar: "Μισώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ μισώ (ego miso) - I hate
- Εσύ μισείς (esi miseis) - You hate
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό μισεί (aftos/afti/afto misei) - He/She/It hates
- Εμείς μισούμε (emeis misoume) - We hate
- Εσείς μισείτε (eseis miseite) - You hate
- Αυτοί/Αυτές μισούν (aftoi/aftes misoun) - They hate

Description: "Μισώ" is a strong verb that expresses a strong feeling of dislike or hatred towards someone or something. It is commonly used to describe intense negative emotions or strong aversions.
