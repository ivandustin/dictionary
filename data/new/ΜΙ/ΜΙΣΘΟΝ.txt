Etymology: The word "μισθόν" (miston) comes from the Ancient Greek word "μισθός" (misthos), which means "wage" or "payment".

Grammar: In Greek, "μισθόν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it is used to refer to a wage or salary.

Description: "Μισθόν" is a term used to describe the payment or compensation received for work or services rendered. It can refer to the wages earned by an employee, the salary of a professional, or any other form of monetary compensation for work.
