Etymology: The word "μισθιοι" (mysthioi) comes from the Greek word "μισθός" (misthós), which means "wage" or "salary". It is derived from the verb "μισθόω" (misthóo), meaning "to hire" or "to pay wages".

Grammar: "Μισθιοι" (mysthioi) is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun. In the singular form, it is "μισθιος" (mysthios).

Description: "Μισθιοι" (mysthioi) refers to hired workers or employees who receive wages or salaries for their services. It can be used to describe individuals who work for someone else in exchange for payment.
