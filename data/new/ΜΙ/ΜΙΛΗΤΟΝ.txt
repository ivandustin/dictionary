Grammar: "Μιλητον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "μιλητον". It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (μιλητου), dative (μιλητῳ), accusative (μιλητον), and vocative (μιλητε).

Description: "Μιλητον" refers to a soldier or warrior in Greek. It is often used to describe someone who is actively engaged in military service or warfare. In ancient Greece, soldiers played a crucial role in defending their city-states and participating in battles.
