Etymology: The word "μισθαποδοτης" (mysthapodotis) comes from the Greek roots "μισθός" (mysthos) meaning "wage" or "salary" and "ἀποδίδωμι" (apodidomi) meaning "to give back" or "to pay." 

Grammar: "μισθαποδοτης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number. 

Description: "μισθαποδοτης" refers to a person who pays or gives wages or salaries. It is commonly used to describe an employer or someone who is responsible for compensating others for their work or services.
