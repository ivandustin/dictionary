Etymology: The word "μικροτερον" comes from the Greek word "μικρός" (mikrós), meaning "small" or "little", and the suffix "-τερον" (-teron), which is a comparative suffix indicating "smaller" or "lesser".

Grammar: "Μικροτερον" is a neuter adjective in the singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The neuter form is used when referring to a neutral or inanimate object.

Description: "Μικροτερον" means "smaller" or "lesser" in Greek. It is used to compare the size or importance of two things, indicating that one is smaller or lesser than the other.
