Etymology: The word "μικραν" (mikran) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the adjective "μικρός" (mikrós), meaning "small" or "little".

Grammar: "Μικραν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Μικραν" refers to a small or little thing. It can be used to describe an object, a person, or a concept that is physically or metaphorically small in size or importance.
