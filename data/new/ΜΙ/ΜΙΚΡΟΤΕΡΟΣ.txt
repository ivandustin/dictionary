Etymology: The word "μικρότερος" (mikroteros) comes from the Greek root "μικρός" (mikros), meaning "small" or "little," and the comparative suffix "-τερος" (-teros), which indicates a comparison between two things.

Grammar: "Μικρότερος" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to compare the size or degree of something to another thing, indicating that the subject is "smaller" or "lesser" in comparison.

Description: "Μικρότερος" is a Greek word that means "smaller" or "lesser" in English. It is used to describe the comparative degree of something, indicating that it is smaller or lesser in size, quantity, importance, or degree compared to something else.
