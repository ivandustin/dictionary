Etymology: The word "μισείν" (misein) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *mey- meaning "to hate."

Grammar: "μισείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ μισώ (ego miso) - I hate, εσύ μισείς (esi miseis) - you hate, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό μισεί (aftos/afti/afto misei) - he/she/it hates, εμείς μισούμε (emeis misoume) - we hate, εσείς μισείτε (eseis miseite) - you hate, αυτοί/αυτές μισούν (aftoi/aftes misoun) - they hate.

Description: "μισείν" is a verb that means "to hate" in Greek. It expresses a strong feeling of dislike or aversion towards someone or something.
