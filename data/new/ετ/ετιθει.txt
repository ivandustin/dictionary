Word: ετιθει

Etymology: The word ετιθει comes from the Greek verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate a present tense form.

Grammar: ετιθει is a present tense form of the verb τίθημι (tithemi) in the third person singular. It is a regular verb in the present tense, and follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -ω (-o).

Description: ετιθει means "he/she/it puts" or "he/she/it places" in Greek. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing, and can be used in a variety of contexts.
