Word: ετελεσαν

Etymology: The word ετελεσαν comes from the Greek verb τελεσαν, which means "they completed" or "they finished". The prefix ε- is a common augment used in ancient Greek to indicate past tense.

Grammar: ετελεσαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb τελεω (teleo), which means "to complete" or "to finish". It is a regular verb in the active voice and belongs to the first conjugation class in ancient Greek.

Description: ετελεσαν is a verb form that indicates an action that was completed in the past by a group of people. It can be translated as "they completed" or "they finished". The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action.
