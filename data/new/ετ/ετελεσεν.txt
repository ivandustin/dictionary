Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "τελε-" (tele-), which means "to complete" or "to finish". The suffix "-σεν" (-sen) is the third person singular aorist indicative active ending, indicating that the action was completed in the past.

Grammar: "ετελεσεν" is a third person singular aorist indicative active verb. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: The word "ετελεσεν" is commonly used in Greek literature and the New Testament to describe the completion of a task or accomplishment of a goal. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the fulfillment of a prophecy or the completion of a divine plan.
