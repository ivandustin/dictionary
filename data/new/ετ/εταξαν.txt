Word: εταξαν

Etymology: The word εταξαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb τάσσω (tasso), which means "to arrange" or "to put in order". The verb τάσσω comes from the ancient Greek word τάξις (taxis), which means "order" or "arrangement".

Grammar: εταξαν is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood of the verb τάσσω. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: εταξαν describes a past action of arranging or putting something in order. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was being arranged or put in order.
