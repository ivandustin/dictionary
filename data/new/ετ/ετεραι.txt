In ancient Greek society, "eterai" referred to a group of women who were not married and were available for sexual relationships with men. These women were often associated with prostitution and were considered to be of lower social status. 

In terms of grammar, "eterai" is a third declension noun, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.
