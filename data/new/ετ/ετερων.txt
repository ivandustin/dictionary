The word "ετερων" (eteron) is a Greek adjective that means "other" or "different". It is derived from the Greek word "ετερος" (eteros), which also means "other" or "different". 

In terms of grammar, "ετερων" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. It can be used to modify a noun or to indicate the noun that possesses something.

For example, "των ετερων ανθρώπων" (ton eteron anthropōn) means "of the other people" or "of the different people".

Overall, "ετερων" is a versatile adjective that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe something as different or other than what is expected or known.
