Word: ετρεχον

Etymology: The word ετρεχον comes from the Greek verb τρέχω (trecho), which means "to run". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: ετρεχον is the third person plural past tense form of the verb τρέχω (trecho). It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past by a group of people. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, person, and number of the subject.

Description: ετρεχον can be translated to English as "they ran". It is a simple past tense form that describes an action that took place in the past and has already been completed.
