The word ετηρησας (etirisas) is a verb in the Greek language, which comes from the root word τηρέω (tireo), meaning "to keep, guard, or observe." The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the root word to indicate the past tense, making ετηρησας (etirisas) mean "you kept" or "you guarded."

ετηρησας (etirisas) is a second-person singular verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question.
