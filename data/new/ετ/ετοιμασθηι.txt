The word "ετοιμασθηι" is a Greek verb in the aorist passive tense, which means "to be prepared" or "to be made ready". Its etymology comes from the combination of the prefix "ετοιμασ-" meaning "to prepare" and the passive ending "-θηι". In terms of grammar, it is a second aorist passive form of the verb "ετοιμάζω" (etoimázō), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready".
