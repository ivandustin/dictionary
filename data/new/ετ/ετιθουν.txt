The word "ετιθουν" (etithoun) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put". The prefix "ετι" (eti) means "still" or "yet", so "ετιθουν" can be translated as "they were still placing" or "they were yet putting".

Grammar: "Ετιθουν" is the third person plural, past continuous tense of the verb "τίθημι". It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: The word "ετιθουν" describes an ongoing action in the past, where someone or something was still placing or putting something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a construction site where workers were still placing bricks or a kitchen where someone was yet putting ingredients into a recipe.
