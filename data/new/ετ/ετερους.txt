Word: ετερους

Etymology: 
The word ετερους comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "ἕτερος" (héteros), which means "other" or "different". 

Grammar: 
ετερους is a masculine adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe a noun that is masculine and singular. In Greek, adjectives must agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. 

Description: 
ετερους is often translated as "other" or "different". It is used to describe something that is not the same as something else. For example, if you were to say "ετερους άνθρωπος" (héteros ánthropos), it would mean "another person" or "a different person". It can also be used to describe something that is not the same as what was previously mentioned.
