Word: ετεροις

Etymology: 
The word ετεροις comes from the Greek language. It is the dative plural form of the adjective ετερος (heteros), which means "other" or "different". 

Grammar: 
In Greek, ετεροις is a dative plural form of the adjective ετερος. It is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. 

Description: 
The word ετεροις is used to describe a group of people or things that are different or distinct from another group. It can also be used to indicate a contrast or comparison between two or more things.
