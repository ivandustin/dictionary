Word: ετερον

Etymology: 
The word ετερον comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "heteros" which means "other" or "different". 

Grammar: 
In Greek, ετερον is a neuter noun. It is in the singular form and is used to refer to something that is different or other than what has been previously mentioned. 

Description: 
The word ετερον is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe something that is different or distinct from what is considered normal or expected. It can also be used in everyday language to describe something that is different or unusual.
