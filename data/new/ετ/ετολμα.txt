Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "τολμάω" (tolmao), which also means "to dare". The prefix "ε-" is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate a present tense form.

Grammar: "Ετολμα" is a present tense form of the verb, which means it describes an action that is currently happening or ongoing. It is in the first person singular form, meaning it refers to the speaker themselves.

Description: The word "ετολμα" is often used to describe someone who is brave or courageous, willing to take risks or face challenges. It can also be used in a negative sense, to describe someone who is reckless or foolish in their actions.
