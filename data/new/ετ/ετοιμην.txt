The word "ετοιμην" (etimēn) is a Greek adjective that means "ready" or "prepared". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "ετοιμάζω" (etoimazō), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready". 

Grammar: "Ετοιμην" is the feminine accusative singular form of the adjective. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of being prepared for something, such as being ready for an event or a task. It can also be used to describe someone who is mentally or emotionally prepared for a situation.
