Word: ετελειωθη

Etymology: ετελειωθη is a Greek word that comes from the verb τελειώνω (teleiōnō), which means "to complete" or "to finish."

Grammar: ετελειωθη is the third person singular aorist passive indicative form of τελειώνω (teleiōnō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: ετελειωθη can be translated to English as "it was completed" or "it was finished." It is often used in the context of completing a task or reaching a goal. The word carries a sense of finality and accomplishment.
