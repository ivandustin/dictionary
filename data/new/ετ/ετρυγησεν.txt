Word: ετρυγησεν

Etymology: The word ετρυγησεν comes from the Greek verb τρυγάω (trygao), which means "to harvest" or "to gather crops". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so ετρυγησεν means "he/she/it harvested" or "he/she/it gathered crops".

Grammar: ετρυγησεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (he/she/it) performed the action of harvesting or gathering crops.

Description: ετρυγησεν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in John 4:36. In this passage, Jesus is speaking to his disciples about the harvest of souls, using the metaphor of a farmer harvesting crops. The word ετρυγησεν is used to describe the action of harvesting, emphasizing the completion of the task.
