The word "ετεροζυγουντες" is a Greek verb that means "to yoke together different kinds of animals." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "ετερος" meaning "different" and "ζυγος" meaning "yoke."

Grammar: "ετεροζυγουντες" is a present participle verb in the active voice, third person plural form. 

Description: The word describes the act of yoking together animals of different kinds, such as a horse and an ox, to work together in the fields. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of combining or uniting different things or ideas.
