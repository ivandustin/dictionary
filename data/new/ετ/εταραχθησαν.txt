Word: εταραχθησαν

Etymology: The word εταραχθησαν is derived from the Greek verb εταράχθη, which means "to be disturbed" or "to be agitated". It is formed by adding the prefix ε- (meaning "out" or "away") to the verb ταράσσω (meaning "to disturb" or "to agitate").

Grammar: εταραχθησαν is a third-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The third-person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: εταραχθησαν describes a state of being disturbed or agitated. It can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as a group of people being upset or a body of water being turbulent. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe emotional or mental turmoil.
