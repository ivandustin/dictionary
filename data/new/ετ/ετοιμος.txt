Word: ετοιμος

Etymology: The word ετοιμος comes from the Greek root words ετος (etos) meaning "year" and μελλω (mello) meaning "to be about to". Therefore, ετοιμος originally meant "about to be a year old" or "ripe for harvest".

Grammar: ετοιμος is an adjective in Greek, meaning "ready" or "prepared". It can be used to describe a person, object, or situation that is ready or prepared for something.

Description: ετοιμος is a commonly used word in Greek, often used in everyday conversation. It can be used to describe someone who is ready for a task or event, or to describe an object that is prepared for use.
