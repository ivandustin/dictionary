Word: ετοιμους

Etymology: The word ετοιμους comes from the Greek root word "ετοιμία" which means readiness or preparedness. The suffix "-ους" is added to indicate the plural masculine form.

Grammar: ετοιμους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. In this form, it is used to describe a group of people who are ready or prepared.

Description: ετοιμους is a Greek adjective that describes a group of people who are ready or prepared. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing soldiers who are ready for battle, athletes who are prepared for a competition, or students who are ready for an exam. It conveys a sense of readiness and willingness to take action.
