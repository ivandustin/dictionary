Word: ετεροι

Etymology: 
The word ετεροι (heteroi) is derived from the Greek word ἕτερος (heteros), which means "other" or "different". It is a plural form of the adjective, which means "others" or "different ones".

Grammar:
In Greek, ετεροι is a masculine plural adjective. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are different from another group. It can be used as a subject, object, or predicate adjective in a sentence.

Description:
The word ετεροι is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe the concept of "the other". It is used to refer to people or things that are different from oneself or one's own group. In modern Greek, it is still used in this sense, but it can also be used more generally to mean "others" or "other people".
