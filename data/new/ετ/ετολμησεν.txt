Word: ετολμησεν

Etymology: The word ετολμησεν comes from the Greek verb τολμάω (tolmáo), which means "to dare" or "to be bold". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so ετολμησεν means "he/she/it dared" or "he/she/it was bold".

Grammar: ετολμησεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb τολμάω (tolmáo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a he, she, or it.

Description: ετολμησεν is a word that describes an action of daring or boldness that happened in the past. It can be used to describe a person who took a risk or did something brave. The word has a strong connotation of courage and fearlessness.
