The word "ετεθην" is a Greek verb that is in the aorist tense and passive voice. It comes from the root word "τίθημι" which means "to place" or "to set". The prefix "ε-" indicates a completed action, while the suffix "-θην" indicates that the action was done to the subject of the verb. 

In terms of grammar, "ετεθην" is a third-person singular verb, meaning that it refers to a single subject. The passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb rather than performing it themselves. 

Overall, "ετεθην" can be translated to mean "he/she/it was placed" or "he/she/it was set".
