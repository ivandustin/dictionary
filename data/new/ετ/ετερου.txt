The word "ετερου" (eterou) is a Greek adjective that means "other" or "different". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "ετερ-" (eter-), which means "other" or "different". 

Grammar: "Ετερου" is the masculine genitive singular form of the adjective. It is used to describe a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. For example, "του ετέρου αδελφού" (tou eterou adelfou) means "of the other brother". 

Description: The word "ετερου" is often used to contrast or compare two things that are different from each other. It can also be used to describe something that is not the same as what was previously mentioned. For example, "Ο ένας αδελφός είναι ψηλός και ο ετερου είναι κοντός" (O enas adelfos einai psilos kai o eterou einai kontos) means "One brother is tall and the other is short".
