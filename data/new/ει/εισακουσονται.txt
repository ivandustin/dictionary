Word: εισακουσονται

Etymology:
The word εισακουσονται is derived from the Greek verb εισακούω (eisakouo), which means "to listen to" or "to hear". The suffix -ονται (-ontai) is added to the verb stem to indicate the passive voice, meaning "they are listened to" or "they are heard".

Grammar:
εισακουσονται is a third-person plural passive present indicative form of the verb εισακούω. In Greek, verbs are inflected to indicate tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person/number. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb rather than performing it.

Description:
εισακουσονται is a Greek verb form that means "they are listened to" or "they are heard". It is often used in the context of music or other forms of audio, indicating that something is being listened to or heard by someone. The word can also be used metaphorically to indicate that someone is paying attention to or taking heed of something.
