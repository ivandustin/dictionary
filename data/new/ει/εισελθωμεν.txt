Word: εισελθωμεν

Etymology:
The word εισελθωμεν is derived from the Greek verb εισέρχομαι (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". It is formed by combining the prefix εις (eis), meaning "into", and the verb ερχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar:
εισελθωμεν is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb εισέρχομαι (eiserchomai). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
εισελθωμεν is a Greek verb that means "let us enter" or "let us go in". It is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts, such as in the Bible when Jesus tells his disciples to "enter" or "go into" a certain place.
