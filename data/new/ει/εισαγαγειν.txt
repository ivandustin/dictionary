Word: εισαγαγειν

Etymology:
The word εισαγαγειν comes from the Greek root words εις (eis) meaning "into" and αγω (ago) meaning "to lead". The suffix -ειν (-ein) is added to form the infinitive verb form.

Grammar:
εισαγαγειν is a Greek verb in the infinitive form. It is a compound verb, consisting of the prefix εις (eis) and the verb αγω (ago). It is a regular verb and follows the conjugation pattern of the verb αγω (ago).

Description:
The word εισαγαγειν means "to introduce" or "to bring in". It is often used in the context of introducing someone or something into a new environment or situation. For example, "Ο διευθυντής εισήγαγε τον νέο υπάλληλο στην ομάδα" (The manager introduced the new employee to the team). It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as "Η εταιρεία εισήγαγε μια νέα πολιτική για την αειφορία" (The company introduced a new policy for sustainability).
