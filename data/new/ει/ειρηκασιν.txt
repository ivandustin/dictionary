Word: ειρηκασιν

Etymology: ειρηκασιν is the third person plural perfect active indicative form of the Greek verb εἴρω (eirō), which means "to say" or "to speak."

Grammar: ειρηκασιν is a verb form in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description: ειρηκασιν can be translated as "they have said" or "they have spoken." It is often used in Greek literature and scripture to indicate a statement or declaration made by a group of people.
