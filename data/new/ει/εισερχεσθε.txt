The word "εισερχεσθε" (eiserchesthe) is a Greek verb that means "to enter" or "to go in". It is derived from the root words "εις" (eis), meaning "into", and "ερχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come". 

In terms of grammar, "εισερχεσθε" is the second person plural present middle or passive imperative form of the verb. This means that it is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to enter or go in. 

Overall, "εισερχεσθε" is a useful and common word in Greek, often used in everyday conversation and in various contexts such as in religious texts or in directions.
