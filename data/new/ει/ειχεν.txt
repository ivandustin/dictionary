Word: ειχεν

Etymology: ειχεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar: ειχεν is the third person singular, past tense form of the verb "έχω" (echo). It is in the indicative mood and the active voice.

Description: ειχεν is a verb that indicates an action that took place in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it had" or "he/she/it held". The context in which it is used will determine the exact meaning. For example, "ειχεν ένα βιβλίο στα χέρια του" (eichen ena vivlio sta heria tou) means "he had a book in his hands".
