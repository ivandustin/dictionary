Word: εισελθηις

Etymology:
The word εισελθηις comes from the Greek verb εἰσέρχομαι (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". It is a compound word made up of the prefix εἰς (eis), meaning "into", and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar:
εισελθηις is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb εἰσέρχομαι (eiserchomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
εισελθηις can be translated as "you entered" or "you went in". It is a form of the verb εἰσέρχομαι (eiserchomai), which is used to describe physical entry into a place or space. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe entering into a state of mind or a new situation.
