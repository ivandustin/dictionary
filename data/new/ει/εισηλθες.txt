The word εισηλθες (eiselthes) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εις (eis) meaning "into" and ερχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, εισηλθες means "you came into" or "you entered".

εισηλθες is the second person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb εισερχομαι (eiserchomai), which means "to enter". In this form, it refers to a single action that was completed in the past by the person being addressed.

εισηλθες is a verb that describes an action of entering or coming into a place. It is often used in the context of entering a building, a room, or a specific location. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples.
