Word: εισοδος

Etymology:
The word εισοδος (eisodos) comes from the Greek root words εις (eis), meaning "into," and οδος (odos), meaning "way" or "path." Therefore, εισοδος literally means "way in" or "entrance."

Grammar:
In Greek, εισοδος is a feminine noun. Its plural form is εισοδοι (eisodoi).

Description:
εισοδος refers to the entrance or way in to a place or building. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the beginning or start of something. For example, "Η εισοδος του καλοκαιριου σημαινει παραλιες και ηλιο" (The start of summer means beaches and sun).
