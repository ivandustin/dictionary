The word "ειπηις" (eipēis) is a Greek noun that means "speech" or "utterance". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "εἶπον" (eipon), which means "I said" or "I spoke". 

Grammar: "Ειπηις" is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. 

Description: The word is often used in Greek literature and mythology to refer to a powerful or prophetic speech. For example, in Homer's Iliad, the goddess Athena inspires the hero Diomedes with a "divine eipēis" that gives him the courage to fight against the Trojans.
