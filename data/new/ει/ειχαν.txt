Word: ειχαν

Etymology: The word ειχαν comes from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have". It is the third person plural past tense form of the verb.

Grammar: ειχαν is a regular verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation group and is conjugated in the indicative mood, past tense. It is used to describe an action that took place in the past and was completed.

Description: ειχαν is a Greek verb that means "they had" in English. It is used to describe possession or ownership of something in the past. For example, "Είχαν ένα σπίτι στην πόλη" (They had a house in the city). It can also be used to describe a state of being in the past, such as "Είχαν πολλούς φίλους" (They had many friends).
