Word: ειδους

Etymology:
The word ειδους (eidous) is derived from the Greek word εἶδος (eidos), which means "form" or "shape". It is related to the Greek verb εἶδον (eidon), which means "to see".

Grammar:
In Greek, ειδους is a noun of the neuter gender. It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. The singular form of the word is εἶδος (eidos).

Description:
The word ειδους (eidous) is often used in Greek to refer to the nature or character of something. It can also be used to describe the appearance or form of something. In philosophy, the term εἶδος (eidos) is used to refer to the abstract concept of a particular thing or idea.
