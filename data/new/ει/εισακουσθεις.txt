Word: εισακουσθεις

Etymology:
The word εισακουσθεις is derived from the Greek verb εισακούω (eisakouo), which means "to listen to" or "to hear". The suffix -θεις (-theis) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, which means "having been listened to" or "having been heard".

Grammar:
εισακουσθεις is a past participle in the passive voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is the receiver of the action. In Greek, the past participle is used to form the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with a continuing effect in the present. For example, the sentence "Έχω εισακουστεί" (Eho eisakousti) means "I have been listened to" or "I have been heard".

Description:
εισακουσθεις is a word that describes the state of having been listened to or heard. It can be used to indicate that someone has paid attention to what was said or that a message has been received. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone has been acknowledged or recognized for their achievements or contributions.
