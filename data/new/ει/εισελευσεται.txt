The word "εισελευσεται" (eiseleusetai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". 

In terms of grammar, "εισελευσεται" is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of the present tense in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix "-εται" to the stem "εισελευσ-". 

As for its meaning, "εισελευσεται" translates to "he/she/it enters" or "he/she/it goes in". It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone entering a building or a room, or figuratively referring to someone joining a group or a conversation.
