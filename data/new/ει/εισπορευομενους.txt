Word: εισπορευομενους

Etymology: The word εισπορευομενους comes from the Greek verb εἰσπορεύομαι (eis-poreuomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". The word is formed from the prefix εἰς (eis), meaning "into", and the verb πορεύομαι (poreuomai), meaning "to go".

Grammar: εισπορευομενους is the present participle form of the verb εἰσπορεύομαι (eis-poreuomai). It is in the masculine, plural, accusative case. In Greek, the present participle is often used to describe an action that is ongoing or in progress.

Description: The word εισπορευομενους describes a group of people who are entering or going into a place. It can be translated as "those who are entering" or "those who are going in". The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people entering a building, a group of soldiers entering a battlefield, or a group of students entering a classroom.
