Word: εισελθωσιν

Etymology: εισελθωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb εισέρχομαι (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". The suffix -ωσιν (-osin) is the third person plural present subjunctive ending, indicating a hypothetical action or possibility.

Grammar: εισελθωσιν is a third person plural present subjunctive form of the verb εισέρχομαι (eiserchomai). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now or is ongoing.

Description: εισελθωσιν means "they may enter" or "let them enter" in English. It is often used in commands or requests, indicating a desire or permission for someone to enter a place or situation.
