Word: εισελθε (eiselthe)

Etymology: 
The word εισελθε (eiselthe) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εις (eis) meaning "into" and ερχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, εισελθε (eiselthe) means "to come into" or "to enter".

Grammar:
εισελθε (eiselthe) is a second person singular aorist imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to enter into a place or situation.

εισελθε (eiselthe) is a verb that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is often used in the context of Jesus instructing his disciples to enter into a certain place or situation. For example, in Matthew 26:18, Jesus tells his disciples, "Go into the city to a certain man and say to him, 'The Teacher says, My time is at hand.
