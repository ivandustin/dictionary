It is derived from the verb "εισέρχομαι" (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to come in". The word is formed by adding the present participle suffix "-μενος" (-menos) to the verb stem "εισερχ-". 

In terms of grammar, "εισερχομενου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. It can be used as an adjective to describe a noun, such as "ο εισερχόμενος κόσμος" (o eiserchomenos kosmos), meaning "the entering crowd".

Overall, "εισερχομενου" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to describe the act of entering or coming in.
