Word: εισελθειν

Etymology:
The word εισελθειν comes from the Greek roots εις (eis) meaning "into" and ελθειν (elthein) meaning "to come". Therefore, εισελθειν means "to come into".

Grammar:
εισελθειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
εισελθειν is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the action of entering or coming into a place or space. It can be used in a literal sense, such as entering a building or room, or in a figurative sense, such as entering into a conversation or situation. The verb is often used in religious contexts, such as entering into a temple or holy place.
