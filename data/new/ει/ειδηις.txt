The word "ειδηις" (eidēis) is a Greek adjective that means "visible" or "apparent". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "εἶδος" (eidos), which means "form" or "appearance". The suffix "-ις" (-is) is a common ending for Greek adjectives.

Grammar: "Ειδηις" is a masculine nominative singular adjective. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "ειδηις" is often used in philosophical and scientific contexts to describe something that is observable or perceivable by the senses. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe something that is obvious or apparent.
