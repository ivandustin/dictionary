The word εισενεγκωσιν is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words: εις (eis) meaning "into" or "in" and ενεγκω (enenko) meaning "to bring in" or "to carry in". Therefore, εισενεγκωσιν means "to bring in" or "to carry in" something into a particular place or situation.

εισενεγκωσιν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense and in the third person plural form. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.
