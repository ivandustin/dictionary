Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "οἶδα" (oida), which means "to know". 

Grammar: "Ειδοτα" is the masculine plural form of the adjective, which agrees with the gender and number of the noun it modifies. 

Description: The word "ειδοτα" is commonly used in Greek to describe something that is known or recognized by someone. It can also be used to describe a person who is well-known or famous. For example, "Οι ειδωμένοι του κόσμου" (Oi eidomenoi tou kosmou) means "The well-known people of the world".
