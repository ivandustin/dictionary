Word: εισκαλεσαμενος

Etymology: The word εισκαλεσαμενος is derived from the Greek verb εισκαλέω (eiskaleo), which means "to invite in" or "to welcome". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and that the subject was the one who performed the action.

Grammar: εισκαλεσαμενος is a past participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe a person who has been invited or welcomed in the past.

Description: The word εισκαλεσαμενος can be translated to English as "having been invited" or "having been welcomed". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a person who has been invited to a special event or occasion, such as a wedding or a banquet. The word carries a sense of honor and respect, as being invited to such events was considered a privilege in ancient Greek society.
