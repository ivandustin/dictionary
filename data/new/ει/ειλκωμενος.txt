The word "ειλκωμενος" (eilkoménos) is a Greek verb in the passive participle form. It comes from the verb "ελκύω" (elkýo), which means "to draw" or "to pull". The passive participle form indicates that the subject is being pulled or drawn, rather than doing the pulling or drawing.

In terms of grammar, "ειλκωμενος" is in the masculine, nominative, singular form. It can be translated to English as "being drawn" or "being pulled".

Overall, "ειλκωμενος" is a word that describes a state of being pulled or drawn, and is often used in a figurative sense to describe being influenced or compelled by something.
