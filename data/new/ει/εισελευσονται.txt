Word: εισελευσονται

Etymology: 
The word εισελευσονται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εισέρχομαι (eiserchomai), which means "to enter" or "to go in". The verb is in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.

Grammar:
εισελευσονται is a third-person plural verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, voice, and mood. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
The word εισελευσονται is used to describe the action of a group of people entering or going into a place. The passive voice indicates that the subject is not performing the action themselves, but rather the action is being done to them. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now. Overall, the word can be translated to mean "they are being entered" or "they are going in".
