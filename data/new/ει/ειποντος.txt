The word "ειποντος" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means "having said" or "having spoken". It is derived from the verb "εἶπον" (eipon), which means "I said" or "I spoke". In terms of grammar, "ειποντος" is the genitive singular form of the participle of "εἶπον". 

The word is commonly used in Greek literature, including the New Testament, to indicate that someone has spoken or said something. It is often used in conjunction with a direct quote or reported speech. 

Overall, "ειποντος" is a useful word for describing past actions or events that involve speaking or communication.
