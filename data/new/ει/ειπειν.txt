The word "ειπειν" (eipein) is a Greek verb that means "to say" or "to speak". It is derived from the ancient Greek verb "εἶπον" (eipon), which has the same meaning. 

In terms of grammar, "ειπειν" is an infinitive verb form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used in combination with other verbs to form compound verb forms, such as "λέγω ειπειν" (lego eipein), which means "to say, for example".

Overall, "ειπειν" is a common and versatile verb in the Greek language, used in a variety of contexts and situations.
