Word: ειχες

Etymology: ειχες is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess". The verb comes from the ancient Greek word "ἔχω" (ekho), which has the same meaning.

Grammar: ειχες is the second person singular past tense of the verb "έχω" (echo). It is used to indicate that the subject (you) had something in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

είχα (I had)
είχες (you had)
είχε (he/she/it had)
είχαμε (we had)
είχατε (you had)
είχαν (they had)

Description: ειχες is a common Greek word that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to talk about past experiences or possessions. For example, "Είχες ένα καλό βιβλίο;" (Did you have a good book?) or "Είχαμε μια ωραία διακοπή στην παραλία." (We had a nice break at the beach).
