Word: εισφερεις

Etymology: The word εισφερεις comes from the Greek verb εισφέρω (eisfero), which means "to bring in" or "to contribute". It is derived from the prefix εις (eis), meaning "into", and the verb φέρω (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bring".

Grammar: εισφερεις is a second person singular present indicative active form of the verb εισφέρω (eisfero). It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present and is being performed by the person being addressed.

Description: εισφερεις is a Greek verb that means "you contribute" or "you bring in". It is often used in the context of financial contributions, such as paying taxes or making donations. The verb can also be used more broadly to describe any kind of contribution or input, such as bringing in ideas or resources to a project or team.
