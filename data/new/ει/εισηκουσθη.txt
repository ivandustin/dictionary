Word: εισηκουσθη

Etymology:
The word εισηκουσθη (eisēkousthē) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εις (eis) meaning "into" and ακουω (akouō) meaning "to hear". Therefore, εισηκουσθη means "to be heard" or "to listen to".

Grammar:
εισηκουσθη is a passive verb in the aorist tense, which means that the action of being heard or listened to has already taken place in the past. It is also in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is singular and in the past.

Description:
εισηκουσθη is a word that describes the act of being heard or listened to. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a conversation, a speech, or a lecture. The word emphasizes the importance of being attentive and actively listening to what is being said.
