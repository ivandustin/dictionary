The word "ειτα" (eita) is a conjunction in the Greek language, which means "then" or "afterwards". It is commonly used to connect two events or actions in a sequence. 

Etymology: The word "ειτα" is derived from the ancient Greek word "εἶτα" (eita), which has the same meaning. 

Grammar: "Ειτα" is a conjunction and is usually placed between two clauses. It is often used with the aorist tense in Greek, which indicates a completed action in the past. 

Example: "Πήγα στο σούπερμαρκετ και ειτα πήγα στο σπίτι" (Piga sto super market kai eita piga sto spiti) - "I went to the supermarket and then I went home."
