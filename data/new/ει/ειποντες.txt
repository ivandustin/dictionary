Word: ειποντες

Etymology: ειποντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb εἶπον (eipon), which means "to say" or "to speak". It is the aorist participle of the verb, which means it describes an action that has been completed in the past.

Grammar: ειποντες is a participle in the nominative plural form. It is often used as an adjective to modify a noun or pronoun. In this form, it can be translated as "those who said" or "having said".

Description: ειποντες is a common word in the Greek language and is often used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to introduce direct speech or to describe a group of people who have spoken. It is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts.
