Word: ειρημενον

Etymology:
The word ειρημενον comes from the Greek verb εἴρω (eirō), which means "to say" or "to speak". The word is the perfect passive participle of the verb, which means "having been said" or "having been spoken".

Grammar:
In Greek, ειρημενον is a neuter noun in the nominative singular form. It is a perfect passive participle, which means it describes an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The word can function as an adjective, modifying a noun, or as a noun itself.

Description:
The word ειρημενον is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to refer to something that has been said or spoken. It can be used to describe a statement, a command, or a piece of advice that has been given. The word implies that the thing that has been said has a certain weight or authority, and that it should be taken seriously.
