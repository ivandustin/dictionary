Word: εισεληλυθαν

Etymology: The word εισεληλυθαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εις (eis) meaning "into" and ελθω (eltho) meaning "to come". Therefore, εισεληλυθαν means "they came into".

Grammar: εισεληλυθαν is a third-person plural aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is in the past tense and indicates that a group of people came into a place.

Description: εισεληλυθαν is a biblical term that appears in the New Testament, specifically in the Gospel of John 10:9. In this passage, Jesus says "I am the gate; whoever enters through me will be saved. They will come in and go out, and find pasture." The word εισεληλυθαν is used to describe the act of entering through the gate.
