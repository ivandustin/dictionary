Word: εισελθατε

Etymology: 
The word εισελθατε comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of two words: εις (eis) meaning "into" and ελθετε (elthete) meaning "you come". Therefore, εισελθατε means "you come into" or "enter".

Grammar:
εισελθατε is a verb in the second person plural form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all entered" or "you all came in".

Description:
εισελθατε is a command or an imperative form of the verb εισερχομαι (eiserchomai) which means "to enter" or "to come in". It is often used in formal or official settings, such as in religious ceremonies or in official announcements. It can also be used in everyday conversations to invite someone to enter a place or to join a group.
