Word: εισελευσομαι

Etymology:
The word εισελευσομαι comes from the Greek roots εις (eis), meaning "into," and ελευσομαι (eleusomai), meaning "to come." Therefore, εισελευσομαι means "to come into" or "to enter."

Grammar:
εισελευσομαι is a verb in the Greek language, and it belongs to the middle voice. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

εισερχομαι (I enter)
εισερχεσαι (you enter)
εισερχεται (he/she/it enters)
εισερχομεθα (we enter)
εισερχεσθε (you all enter)
εισερχονται (they enter)

Description:
εισελευσομαι is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of entering or coming into a place. It can be used in a literal sense, such as entering a building or a room, or in a figurative sense, such as entering into a conversation or a relationship. The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of entering into the kingdom of God or into eternal life.
