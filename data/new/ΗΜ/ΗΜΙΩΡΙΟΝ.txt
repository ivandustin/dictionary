Etymology: The word "ημιωριον" (imiōrion) comes from the Greek roots "ημι-" (hemi-), meaning "half," and "ωρα" (ōra), meaning "hour." It literally translates to "half an hour."

Grammar: "Ημιωριον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. Neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" in the singular form.

Description: "Ημιωριον" refers to a period of time equal to half an hour. It is commonly used to indicate a short duration or a specific time interval.
