Etymology: The word "ημεραίς" (pronounced "imeres") comes from the Greek word "ημέρα" (pronounced "imera"), which means "day". The suffix "-αις" is added to indicate the plural form.

Grammar: "Ημεραίς" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is the plural form of "ημέρα". In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: "Ημεραίς" refers to "days" in Greek. It is used to indicate multiple days, as opposed to the singular form "ημέρα" which refers to a single day.
