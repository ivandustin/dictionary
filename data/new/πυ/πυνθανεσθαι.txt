Word: πυνθανεσθαι

Etymology:
The word πυνθανεσθαι comes from the Ancient Greek verb πυνθάνομαι (punthánomai), which means "to inquire" or "to ask". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pent- meaning "to tread, go" and the suffix -θανειν (-thanein) which indicates a reflexive or middle voice.

Grammar:
πυνθανεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb πυνθάνομαι (punthánomai). In Ancient Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit. The passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is receiving the action. The present tense indicates that the action is happening currently or habitually.

Description:
πυνθανεσθαι is a verb that means "to inquire" or "to ask". It is often used in the middle voice to indicate that the subject is asking for information for its own benefit. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as inquiring about someone's health, asking for directions, or seeking advice. In Ancient Greek literature, the verb is often used in dialogue to indicate characters asking questions of each other.
