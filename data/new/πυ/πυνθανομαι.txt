Word: πυνθανομαι

Etymology:
The word πυνθανομαι (punthanomai) is derived from the Greek verb πυνθάνομαι (punthánomai), which means "to inquire" or "to ask". It is a compound verb formed from the prefix πυνθ- (punth-) meaning "to learn" and the verb stem -ανομαι (-anomai) meaning "to ask".

Grammar:
πυνθανομαι is a middle voice verb in Greek, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

Singular:
- Πυνθάνομαι (punthanomai) - I inquire
- Πυνθάνῃ (punthanē) - You inquire (singular)
- Πυνθάνεται (punthanetai) - He/she/it inquires

Plural:
- Πυνθανόμεθα (punthanometha) - We inquire
- Πυνθάνεσθε (punthanesthe) - You inquire (plural)
- Πυνθάνονται (punthanontai) - They inquire

Description:
πυνθανομαι is a verb that is commonly used in Greek to express the act of asking questions or seeking information. It can be used in a variety of contexts, from casual conversations to more formal settings. The verb is often used in combination with other verbs or nouns to form compound expressions that convey more specific meanings. For example, πυνθανομαι μαθητείαν (punthanomai matheteian) means "to inquire about a disciple" or "to ask about a student". Overall, πυνθανομαι is a versatile verb that is essential to the Greek language and its communication.
