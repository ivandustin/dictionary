Word: πυργον

Etymology:
The word πυργον (pyrgon) is derived from the Greek word πύργος (pyrgos), which means "tower" or "fortress". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European language, where it is believed to have originated from the root word *bhergh-, which means "high" or "elevated".

Grammar:
In Greek, πυργον is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form πύργου (pyrgou). The word is also used in the plural form πύργοι (pyrgoi).

Description:
πυργον refers to a tower or a fortress, typically made of stone or brick, that is used for defensive purposes. In ancient Greece, towers were often built as part of city walls to protect against invaders. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to towers or tall buildings.
