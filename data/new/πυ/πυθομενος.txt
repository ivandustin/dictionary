Word: πυθόμενος

Etymology:
The word πυθόμενος (pythomenos) is a present participle of the Greek verb πύθομαι (pythomai), which means "to inquire" or "to ask". The verb is derived from the name of the Greek god Apollo's sanctuary at Delphi, which was called Pytho.

Grammar:
πυθόμενος is a present participle in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb. In this case, the subject is implied to be masculine and singular. The verb πύθομαι (pythomai) is a deponent verb, which means that it has a passive form but an active meaning.

Description:
πυθόμενος can be translated as "inquiring" or "asking". It is often used to describe someone who is actively seeking information or knowledge. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe characters who are on a quest for truth or wisdom.
