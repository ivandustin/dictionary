Word: πυλην

Etymology:
The word πυλην (pylen) is derived from the Greek word πύλη (pyle), which means "gate" or "entrance". It is related to the Latin word "porta" and the English word "portal".

Grammar:
πυλην is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. πυλην is a masculine noun in the accusative case.

Description:
πυλην refers to the gate or entrance of a building or structure. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to an entrance or gateway to a new experience or opportunity. In ancient Greek mythology, the gates of the underworld were guarded by the three-headed dog Cerberus. The gates of the city of Troy were famously breached by the Trojan Horse.
