Word: πυρετοις

Etymology: The word πυρετοις comes from the Greek word πυρετός (pyretos), which means fever. The suffix -οις (-ois) is a plural ending in Greek, indicating that the word refers to multiple fevers.

Grammar: πυρετοις is a noun in the dative plural case in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "fevers" or "to fevers."

Description: πυρετοις refers to multiple instances of fever, which is a medical condition characterized by an elevated body temperature. Fevers can be caused by a variety of factors, including infections, inflammation, and other medical conditions. Symptoms of fever can include sweating, chills, headache, and muscle aches. Treatment for fever typically involves addressing the underlying cause and managing symptoms with medication and other supportive measures.
