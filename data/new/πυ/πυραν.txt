Word: πυραν

Etymology:
The word πυραν comes from the Greek word πῦρ (pyr), which means "fire". The suffix -αν (-an) is a common suffix used in Greek to form nouns.

Grammar:
πυραν is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. πυραν is a feminine noun in the accusative case.

Description:
πυραν refers to a firebrand or a burning stick. It is often used in Greek mythology to describe the torches carried by the gods or heroes. In modern Greek, the word πυραν is also used to refer to a torch or a flaming stick used for lighting fires or for signaling.
