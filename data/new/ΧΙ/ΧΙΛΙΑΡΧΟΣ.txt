Etymology: The word "χιλιαρχος" (chiliarchos) comes from the Greek words "χίλιοι" (chilioi), meaning "thousand," and "ἄρχων" (archon), meaning "ruler" or "commander." It originally referred to a military officer in ancient Greece who commanded a thousand soldiers.

Grammar: In Greek, "χιλιαρχος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "χιλιάρχοι" (chiliarchoi).

Description: In modern Greek, "χιλιαρχος" is used to refer to a military rank equivalent to the rank of major in the army. It is also used in a broader sense to describe a person who holds a high-ranking position or has authority over a large group.
