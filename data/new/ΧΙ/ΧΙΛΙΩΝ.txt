Etymology: The word "χιλιών" (chiliōn) comes from the Greek word "χίλιοι" (chilioi), which means "thousand."

Grammar: "Χιλιών" is the genitive form of the noun "χίλιοι" (chilioi), which is the plural form of "χίλιος" (chilios), meaning "thousand." In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "Χιλιών" is used to describe or quantify a noun that is in the genitive case. It indicates that there are "thousands" of something. For example, "χιλιών ανθρώπων" (chiliōn anthrōpōn) means "thousands of people."
