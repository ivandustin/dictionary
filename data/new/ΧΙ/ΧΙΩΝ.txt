Etymology: The word "χιων" (chiōn) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*ḱewh₁-" meaning "snow".

Grammar: In Greek, "χιων" is a noun of feminine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The plural form of "χιων" is "χιονες" (chiones).

Description: "Χιων" (chiōn) means "snow" in Greek. It refers to the frozen precipitation that falls from the atmosphere in the form of ice crystals. Snow is commonly associated with winter and is known for its white color and soft texture.
