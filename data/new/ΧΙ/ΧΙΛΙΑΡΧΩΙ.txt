Etymology: The word "χιλιαρχώι" (chiliarchói) comes from the Greek words "χίλιοι" (chílioi), meaning "thousand," and "ἄρχω" (árcho), meaning "to rule." It originally referred to a military officer in ancient Greece who commanded a thousand soldiers.

Grammar: "Χιλιαρχώι" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Χιλιαρχώι" is a term used in the Greek military to refer to a rank equivalent to the modern-day rank of major. It denotes a military officer who is in charge of a battalion or a similar unit consisting of around a thousand soldiers.
