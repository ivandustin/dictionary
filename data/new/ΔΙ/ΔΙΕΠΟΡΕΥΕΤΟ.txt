Etymology: The word "διεπορεύετο" comes from the Greek verb "διεπορεύομαι" (diaporevomai), which is a compound word consisting of "δια-" (dia-), meaning "through" or "across," and "πορεύομαι" (porevomai), meaning "to pass" or "to go." 

Grammar: "Διεπορεύετο" is the third person singular, imperfect tense form of the verb "διεπορεύομαι" in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past. 

Description: "Διεπορεύετο" means "he/she/it was passing through" or "he/she/it used to pass through" in English. It indicates that someone or something was moving or traveling through a place.
