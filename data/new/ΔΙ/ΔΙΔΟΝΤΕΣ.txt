Etymology: The word "διδαντες" comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct".

Grammar: "Διδαντες" is the plural form of the noun "διδάσκαλος" (didaskalos), which means "teacher" or "instructor" in Greek. It is used to refer to multiple teachers or instructors.

Description: "Διδαντες" is a Greek word that specifically refers to more than one teacher or instructor. It is commonly used in educational contexts to describe a group of individuals who are responsible for teaching or instructing others.
