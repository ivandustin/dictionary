Etymology: The word "διηλθον" (diēlthon) comes from the Greek verb "διέρχομαι" (diérchomai), which means "to pass through" or "to go across". It is derived from the prefix "δι-" (di-), meaning "through", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to go" or "to come".

Grammar: "Διηλθον" is the aorist tense of the verb "διέρχομαι" (diérchomai) in the first person singular. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, it indicates that the speaker has passed through or gone across something.
