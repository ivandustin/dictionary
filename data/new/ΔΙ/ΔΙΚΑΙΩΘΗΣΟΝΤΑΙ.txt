Etymology: The word "δικαιωθεί" comes from the Greek verb "δικαιόω" (dikaioo), which means "to justify" or "to vindicate." It is derived from the noun "δίκη" (diki), meaning "justice" or "righteousness."

Grammar: "Δικαιωθεί" is the third person singular form of the aorist passive subjunctive mood of the verb "δικαιόω." In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The subjunctive mood expresses possibility, potentiality, or doubt.

Description: "Δικαιωθεί" can be translated as "he/she/it may be justified" or "he/she/it might be vindicated." It suggests the possibility or potentiality of someone or something being justified or vindicated in a specific context or situation.
