Etymology: The word "διαστολή" comes from the Greek verb "διαστέλλω" (diastéllō), which means "to spread out" or "to expand". It is derived from the prefix "δια-" (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the verb "στέλλω" (stéllō), meaning "to send" or "to place".

Grammar: "Διαστολή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "διαστολής" (diastolís). In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: "Διαστολή" refers to the act of spreading out or expanding something. It can also be used to describe the dilation or widening of a space or an object. In medical terminology, it specifically refers to the expansion or relaxation of blood vessels, especially the arteries, during the diastolic phase of the cardiac cycle.
