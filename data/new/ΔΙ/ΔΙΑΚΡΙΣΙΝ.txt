Etymology: The word "διακρισιν" comes from the Greek verb "διακρίνω" (diakríno), which means "to distinguish" or "to separate". 

Grammar: "Διακρισιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence (case) and the number (singular or plural).

Description: "Διακρισιν" refers to the act of distinguishing or differentiating between things. It can also mean discrimination or discernment.
