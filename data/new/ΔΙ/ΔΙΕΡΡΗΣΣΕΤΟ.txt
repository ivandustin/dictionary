Etymology: The word "διερρησσετο" comes from the Greek verb "διαρρήσσω" (diarrhḗssō), which means "to tear apart" or "to split open". It is derived from the prefix "δια-" (dia-), meaning "through" or "apart", and the verb "ῥήσσω" (rhḗssō), meaning "to tear" or "to split".

Grammar: "διερρησσετο" is the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb "διαρρήσσω" (diarrhḗssō). In Greek grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or is affected by the action.

Description: "διερρησσετο" describes an action that occurred in the past, where the subject tore or split itself apart. It can be used metaphorically to describe a situation or object that experienced a significant rupture or division.
