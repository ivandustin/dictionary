Etymology: The word "διαφορωτερον" comes from the Greek word "διαφορώτερον" (diaphoróteron), which is the neuter form of the comparative adjective "διάφορος" (diáphoros), meaning "different" or "distinct."

Grammar: "Διαφορωτερον" is the neuter form of the comparative adjective "διάφορος" (diáphoros). In Greek grammar, adjectives change their form based on the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. The neuter form is used when the adjective refers to a neutral or inanimate object.

Description: "Διαφορωτερον" is an adjective in Greek that means "more different" or "more distinct." It is used to compare two or more things and indicate that one is more different or distinct than the other(s).
