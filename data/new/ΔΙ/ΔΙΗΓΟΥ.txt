Etymology: The word "διηγού" (diēgou) comes from the Greek verb "διηγούμαι" (diēgoumai), which means "to narrate" or "to tell a story." It is derived from the prefix "δι-" (di-), meaning "through" or "across," and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuō), meaning "to speak" or "to address."

Grammar: "Διηγού" (diēgou) is the present tense, first person singular form of the verb "διηγούμαι" (diēgoumai). It is used when the speaker wants to narrate or tell a story in the present tense.

Description: "Διηγού" (diēgou) is a Greek verb that is commonly used to express the act of narrating or telling a story. It can be used in various contexts, such as when recounting personal experiences, describing events, or sharing anecdotes. The verb emphasizes the act of storytelling and is often used to engage listeners or readers in a narrative.
