Etymology: The word "διεταξεν" (dietaxen) comes from the Greek verb "διατάσσω" (diatasso), which means "to arrange" or "to order".

Grammar: "Διεταξεν" is the third person singular, aorist tense of the verb "διατάσσω" (diatasso) in the Greek language. It is used to describe an action that occurred in the past.

Description: "Διεταξεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it arranged" or "he/she/it ordered". It is commonly used to refer to someone giving instructions or organizing something.
