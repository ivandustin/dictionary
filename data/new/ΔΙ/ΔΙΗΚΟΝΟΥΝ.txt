Etymology: The word "διηκονούν" (diēkonoun) comes from the Greek verb "διακονέω" (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister." It is derived from the noun "διάκονος" (diákonos), meaning "servant" or "minister."

Grammar: "Διηκονούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "διακονέω" (diakonéō). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time, and the third person plural form refers to multiple individuals or entities performing the action.

Description: "Διηκονούν" is a Greek verb that means "they serve" or "they minister." It is used to describe the action of serving or ministering to others. This verb can be used in various contexts, such as serving in a religious or ceremonial capacity, providing assistance or support, or fulfilling a role of service in a specific organization or community.
