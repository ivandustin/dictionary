Etymology: The word "δικαιοσύνη" (dikaiosýni) comes from the Greek roots "δίκη" (díki), meaning "justice" or "judgment," and "σύνη" (sýni), meaning "quality" or "state of being." 

Grammar: In Greek, "δικαιοσύνη" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is typically used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form "δικαιοσύνες" (dikaiosýnes). 

Description: "Δικαιοσύνη" is a concept that encompasses the principles of fairness, righteousness, and moral integrity. It refers to the quality or state of being just, equitable, and in accordance with the law. In Greek philosophy, "δικαιοσύνη" is often associated with the idea of virtue and the harmonious balance of individual and societal interests. It is a fundamental concept in ethics, law, and social justice.
