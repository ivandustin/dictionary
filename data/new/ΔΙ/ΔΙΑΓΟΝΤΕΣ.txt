Grammar: "Διαγοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "διαγω" (diago) in the active voice. It is used to describe ongoing or continuous action. In Greek grammar, the present participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Διαγοντες" can be translated as "crossing" or "going through" in English. It is often used to describe someone or something that is in the process of crossing or going through a particular place or situation.
