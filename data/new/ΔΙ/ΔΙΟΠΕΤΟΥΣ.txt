Etymology: The word "διοπετούς" comes from the Greek roots "διο-" meaning "through" and "πέτομαι" meaning "to fly". It is derived from the verb "διοπετέω" which means "to fly through".

Grammar: "Διοπετούς" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe something or someone that can fly through or traverse the air.

Description: "Διοπετούς" refers to something or someone that is capable of flying through the air. It can be used to describe birds, insects, or any other flying creatures.
