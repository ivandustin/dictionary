Etymology: The word "διαστρέψαι" comes from the Greek verb "διαστρέφω" (diastrepho), which means "to distort" or "to twist". 

Grammar: "Διαστρέψαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "διαστρέφω" (diastrepho). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that is not conjugated to show tense, mood, or person.

Description: "Διαστρέψαι" is a verb form that expresses the action of distorting or twisting. It can be translated as "to distort" or "to twist" in English.
