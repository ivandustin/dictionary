Etymology: The word "διελαλεῖτο" comes from the Greek verb "διαλέγομαι" (dialégomai), which means "to converse" or "to speak through." It is derived from the prefix "δια-" (dia-), meaning "through," and the verb "λέγω" (légō), meaning "to speak" or "to say."

Grammar: "Διελαλεῖτο" is the third person singular, imperfect, middle voice form of the verb "διαλέγομαι" (dialégomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Διελαλεῖτο" can be translated as "he/she/it was speaking through" or "he/she/it was conversing." It suggests that someone or something was engaged in a conversation or speaking through a medium.
