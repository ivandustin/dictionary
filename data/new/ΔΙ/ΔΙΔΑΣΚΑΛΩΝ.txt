Etymology: The word "διδασκαλών" comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct." The suffix "-ών" is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, which indicates someone who is currently teaching.

Grammar: "Διδασκαλών" is the present participle form of the verb "διδάσκω" in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently teaching or instructing.

Description: "Διδασκαλών" refers to a person who is actively engaged in the act of teaching or instructing others. It can be used to describe a teacher, instructor, or educator.
