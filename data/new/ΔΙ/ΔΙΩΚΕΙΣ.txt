Etymology: The word "διωκείς" (diōkeis) comes from the Greek verb "διώκω" (diōkō), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". 

Grammar: "Διωκείς" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "διώκω" (diōkō), which means "you pursue" or "you chase". It is used when addressing one person.

Description: "Διωκείς" is a Greek word that describes the action of pursuing or chasing someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in sports, hunting, or figuratively to express the idea of actively seeking or going after a goal or objective.
