Etymology: The word "διψυχος" (dipsychos) comes from the Greek roots "δι" (di), meaning "two," and "ψυχή" (psyche), meaning "soul" or "mind." It literally translates to "having two souls" or "dual-minded."

Grammar: In Greek, "διψυχος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Διψυχος" is used to describe someone who has conflicting or contradictory thoughts, feelings, or desires. It refers to a person who is torn between two opposing choices or has a divided mind. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or concept that exhibits duality or ambivalence.
