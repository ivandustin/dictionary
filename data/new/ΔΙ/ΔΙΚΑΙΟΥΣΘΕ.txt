Etymology: The word "δικαιοσύνη" (dikaiosýni) comes from the Greek roots "δίκη" (díki), meaning "justice" or "righteousness," and "σύνη" (sýni), meaning "state" or "quality." 

Grammar: "Δικαιοσύνη" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in various cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Δικαιοσύνη" refers to the concept of justice, righteousness, or fairness. It encompasses the idea of moral and ethical correctness, as well as the proper treatment of individuals and the maintenance of a just society. In Greek philosophy, "δικαιοσύνη" is often associated with the virtue of living in accordance with reason and the natural order.
