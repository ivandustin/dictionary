Etymology: The word "δικαιώματα" comes from the Greek word "δίκαιος" (dikaios), meaning "just" or "righteous". It is derived from the root word "δίκη" (dikē), which means "justice" or "right".

Grammar: "Δικαιώματα" is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a neuter noun. In the singular form, it is "δικαίωμα" (dikaíoma).

Description: "Δικαιώματα" refers to rights or entitlements. It is commonly used to describe legal or moral rights that individuals possess, such as human rights, civil rights, or legal rights. These rights are typically protected by laws or regulations and are considered fundamental to the well-being and freedom of individuals in a society.
