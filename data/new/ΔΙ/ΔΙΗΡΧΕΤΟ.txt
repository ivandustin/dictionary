Etymology: The word "διηρχετο" comes from the Greek verb "διέρχομαι" (diérchomai), which means "to pass through" or "to go through". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "δι-" (di-), meaning "through", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to go" or "to come".

Grammar: "διηρχετο" is the third person singular, past tense form of the verb "διέρχομαι" (diérchomai). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated according to person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The past tense indicates that the action of passing through or going through has already happened.

Description: "διηρχετο" describes an action of passing through or going through something in the past. It can be used to indicate physical movement from one place to another or metaphorical movement through a process or experience.
