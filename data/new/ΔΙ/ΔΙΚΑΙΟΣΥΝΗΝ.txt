Etymology: The word "δικαιοσύνην" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the word "δίκη" (dike), meaning "justice" or "judgment," and the suffix "-σύνην" (-synēn), which denotes the abstract noun form. 

Grammar: "Δικαιοσύνην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Δικαιοσύνην" refers to the concept of justice or righteousness. It encompasses the idea of fairness, moral rightness, and the proper treatment of individuals. In Greek philosophy, δικαιοσύνην is often associated with the harmonious balance of the individual and society, where each person receives what they deserve and contributes to the common good.
