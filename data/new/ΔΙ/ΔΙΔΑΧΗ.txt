Etymology: The word "διδαχή" (didachē) comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didaskō), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, meaning "to show" or "to point out".

Grammar: "Διδαχή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the first declension and its genitive form is "διδαχής" (didachēs). In the plural form, it becomes "διδαχές" (didachés).

Description: "Διδαχή" refers to the act of teaching or instruction. It can also be used to describe a lesson, a teaching method, or a body of teachings. In a broader sense, it can refer to the entire field of education or the body of knowledge that is taught.
