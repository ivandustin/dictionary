Etymology: The word "διαιρούν" (diaroun) comes from the Greek verb "διαιρέω" (diareo), which means "to divide" or "to separate".

Grammar: "Διαιρούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "διαιρέω" (diareo). It is used to describe an ongoing action of dividing or separating that is being performed by a group of people or things.

Description: "Διαιρούν" is a Greek verb that is commonly used to express the act of dividing or separating something into parts. It can be used in various contexts, such as dividing numbers, dividing objects, or dividing tasks among a group of people.
