Etymology: The word "διαταγείς" comes from the Greek verb "διατάσσω" (diatássō), which means "to arrange" or "to command."

Grammar: "Διαταγείς" is the plural form of the noun "διαταγή" (diatagí), which means "order" or "command" in Greek. It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns.

Description: "Διαταγείς" refers to a group of people who give orders or commands. It can be used to describe military commanders, officers, or any individuals in positions of authority who have the power to issue orders.
