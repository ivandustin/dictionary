Etymology: The word "δικαιοσύνη" (dikaiosýni) comes from the Greek roots "δίκη" (díki), meaning "justice" or "judgment," and "σύνη" (sýni), meaning "quality" or "state of being." 

Grammar: In Greek, "δικαιοσύνη" is a feminine noun. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used as the subject or object of a sentence.

Description: "Δικαιοσύνη" is a concept that encompasses the principles of fairness, righteousness, and moral correctness. It refers to the quality or state of being just, equitable, and lawful. In Greek philosophy, "δικαιοσύνη" is often associated with the idea of virtue and the proper balance between individual rights and societal obligations. It is a fundamental concept in legal systems and ethical frameworks.
