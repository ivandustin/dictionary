Etymology: The word "διαυγής" comes from the Greek roots "δια-" meaning "through" and "αυγή" meaning "dawn". It literally means "through the dawn" or "transparent like the dawn".

Grammar: "Διαυγής" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine form and can also be declined to match the gender and number of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Διαυγής" is used to describe something that is transparent, clear, or bright. It is often used metaphorically to describe a person's character or a situation that is clear and easily understood.
