Etymology: The word "διδαχήν" (didachēn) comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didaskō), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, meaning "to show" or "to point out".

Grammar: "Διδαχήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case (nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.), number (singular or plural), and gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter).

Description: "Διδαχήν" refers to the act of teaching or instruction. It can also be used to describe a lesson, a teaching, or a doctrine.
