Etymology: The word "διχοστασία" comes from the Greek roots "δίχα" (dicha), meaning "in two" or "apart," and "στάσις" (stasis), meaning "standing" or "position." 

Grammar: "Διχοστασία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Διχοστασία" refers to a state of division, discord, or conflict between two opposing factions or groups. It can also describe a disagreement or dispute that leads to a split or separation.
