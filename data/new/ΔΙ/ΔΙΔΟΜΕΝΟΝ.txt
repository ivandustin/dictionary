Etymology: The word "διδομένον" comes from the Greek verb "δίδωμι" (didōmi), which means "to give". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃- ("to give").

Grammar: "Διδομένον" is the neuter singular form of the present participle of the verb "δίδωμι" (didōmi). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to form verbal adjectives and can also function as a noun.

Description: "Διδομένον" can be translated as "being given" or "that which is given". It refers to something that is currently being given or bestowed upon someone or something.
