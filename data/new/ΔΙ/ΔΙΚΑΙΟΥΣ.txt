Etymology: The word "δικαίους" (dikaious) comes from the Greek word "δίκαιος" (dikaios), which means "just" or "righteous".

Grammar: "Δικαίους" is the plural form of the adjective "δίκαιος" (dikaios) in Greek. It is used to describe multiple people or things that are just or righteous.

Description: "Δικαίους" is a Greek word that refers to multiple individuals or things that are considered just, fair, or righteous. It is often used in moral or ethical contexts to describe people who act in accordance with what is right or who possess qualities of fairness and justice.
