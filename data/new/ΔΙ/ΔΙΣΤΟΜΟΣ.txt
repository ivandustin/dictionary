Etymology: The word "διστομος" (distomos) comes from the Greek roots "δις" (dis), meaning "twice," and "στόμα" (stoma), meaning "mouth." 

Grammar: In Greek, "διστομος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms depending on the context.

Description: "Διστομος" is used to describe something that has two mouths or openings. It can be used in a literal sense to refer to organisms or objects that have two distinct openings, such as certain types of animals or plants. It can also be used metaphorically to describe situations or concepts that have two opposing aspects or characteristics.
