Etymology: The word "διανυσάντες" comes from the Greek verb "διανύω" (dianýo), which means "to traverse" or "to cross through". The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) indicates the past participle form of the verb.

Grammar: "Διανυσάντες" is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the verb "διανύω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Διανυσάντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having crossed" or "having traversed". It is often used to describe a group of people who have completed a journey or crossed a distance.
