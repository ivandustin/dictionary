Etymology: The word "διαρπασαί" comes from the Greek verb "διαρπάζω" (diarpázo), which means "to plunder" or "to seize by force". 

Grammar: "Διαρπασαί" is the plural form of the noun "διαρπασαί" (diarpasai), which is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Διαρπασαί" refers to a group of people or things that have been plundered or seized by force. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where something has been taken or stolen forcefully.
