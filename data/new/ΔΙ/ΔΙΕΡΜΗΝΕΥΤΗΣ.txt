Etymology: The word "διερμηνευτής" (di̱ermēneutḗs) comes from the Greek verb "διερμηνεύω" (di̱ermēneúō), which means "to interpret" or "to translate."

Grammar: In Greek, "διερμηνευτής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "διερμηνεῖς" (di̱ermēneîs).

Description: "Διερμηνευτής" refers to an interpreter or translator, someone who translates spoken or written language from one language to another. This term is commonly used in various fields, such as diplomacy, business, and language services.
