Etymology: The word "δικαστην" comes from the Greek word "δικαστής" (dikastís), which means "judge". It is derived from the verb "δικάζω" (dikázō), meaning "to judge" or "to pass judgment".

Grammar: "Δικαστην" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender. In the plural form, it becomes "δικαστές" (dikastés).

Description: "Δικαστην" refers to a judge in the Greek legal system. It is someone who presides over a court of law, listens to the evidence presented by both parties, and makes a decision based on the law and the facts of the case. Judges play a crucial role in ensuring justice and upholding the rule of law.
