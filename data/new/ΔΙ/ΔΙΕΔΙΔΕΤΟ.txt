Etymology: The word "διεδιδετο" comes from the Greek verb "διδίδωμι" (dididomi), which means "to give" or "to distribute". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃- ("to give").

Grammar: "διεδιδετο" is the third person singular, imperfect middle/passive indicative form of the verb "διδίδωμι" (dididomi). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on itself (middle voice) or receiving the action (passive voice).

Description: "διεδιδετο" is a verb form that describes an ongoing or repeated action in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it was giving" or "he/she/it was distributing" in English.
