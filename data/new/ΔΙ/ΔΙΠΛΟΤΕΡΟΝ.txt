Etymology: The word "διπλοτερον" comes from the Greek word "διπλός" (diplós), meaning "double" or "twofold".

Grammar: "Διπλοτερον" is a noun in the neuter gender and singular number in Greek. It is the accusative case form of the word.

Description: "Διπλοτερον" refers to something that is double or twofold. It can be used to describe an object, a concept, or a situation that has two parts or aspects.
