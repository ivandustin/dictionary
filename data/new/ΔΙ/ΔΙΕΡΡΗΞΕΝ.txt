Etymology: The word "διερρηξεν" comes from the Greek verb "διαρρήσσω" (diarrhḗssō), which means "to tear apart" or "to rip open."

Grammar: "Διερρηξεν" is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "διαρρήσσω" (diarrhḗssō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition.

Description: "Διερρηξεν" describes an action that occurred in the past, where something was torn apart or ripped open.
