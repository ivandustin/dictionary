Etymology: The word "διδασκαλος" (didaskalos) comes from the Greek verb "διδασκω" (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, meaning "to show" or "to point out".

Grammar: In Greek, "διδασκαλος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "διδασκαλοι" (didaskaloi). It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive "διδασκαλου" (didaskalou), dative "διδασκαλῳ" (didaskalō), and accusative "διδασκαλον" (didaskalon).

Description: "Διδασκαλος" is the Greek word for "teacher" or "instructor". It refers to someone who imparts knowledge or skills to others, typically in an educational setting. In ancient Greece, the role of a διδασκαλος was highly respected, and they were responsible for teaching various subjects, including philosophy, mathematics, and rhetoric. Today, the term can be used to describe a teacher in any field or discipline.
