Etymology: The word "διηνοιγμένους" comes from the Greek verb "διανοίγω" (dianoigo), which means "to open" or "to unfold". The suffix "-μένους" (-menous) is added to the verb stem to form the adjective form.

Grammar: "Διηνοιγμένους" is the masculine accusative plural form of the adjective. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, the adjective is modifying a masculine plural noun in the accusative case.

Description: The word "διηνοιγμένους" describes something or someone that is "opened" or "unfolded" in the masculine plural accusative form.
