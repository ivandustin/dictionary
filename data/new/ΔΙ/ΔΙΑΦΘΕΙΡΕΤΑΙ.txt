Etymology: The word "διαφθείρεται" comes from the Greek verb "διαφθείρω" (diaphtheíro), which means "to corrupt" or "to destroy". It is derived from the prefix "δια-" (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the verb "φθείρω" (pheíro), meaning "to ruin" or "to spoil".

Grammar: "Διαφθείρεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense in the passive voice of the verb "διαφθείρω". In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, voice, mood, and person. The passive voice is used when the subject of the sentence is being acted upon or affected by the verb.

Description: "Διαφθείρεται" describes the action of something or someone being corrupted, destroyed, or spoiled. It implies a negative change or deterioration.
