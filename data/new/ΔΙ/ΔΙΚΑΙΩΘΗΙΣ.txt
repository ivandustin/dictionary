Etymology: The word "δικαιοθητης" comes from the Greek word "δίκαιος" (dikaios), meaning "just" or "righteous," and the suffix "-θητης" (-thētēs), which denotes the agent or doer of a particular action.

Grammar: "Δικαιοθητης" is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in Greek. It belongs to the second declension class of nouns.

Description: "Δικαιοθητης" refers to a person who administers justice or acts as a judge. In Greek, it is commonly used to describe a judge in a court of law or a person who upholds justice and fairness.
