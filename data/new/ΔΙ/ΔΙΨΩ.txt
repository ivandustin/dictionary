Etymology: The word "διψώ" (dipsó) comes from the Greek verb "διψάω" (dipsáo), which means "to thirst". 

Grammar: "Διψώ" is a first-person singular present tense verb in Greek. It belongs to the class of verbs that end in "-ώ" and follow the pattern of conjugation known as the "ω-verbs". 

Description: "Διψώ" is used to express the action of being thirsty or feeling thirst. It is commonly used in everyday speech to indicate the need for water or any other liquid.
