Etymology: The word "διεμερίσθη" comes from the Greek verb "διαιρέω" (diareo), which means "to divide" or "to separate". The prefix "δι-" (di-) indicates a separation or division, and the root "μερίζω" (merizo) means "to divide" or "to distribute".

Grammar: "διεμερίσθη" is the third person singular, aorist tense, passive voice form of the verb "διαιρέω" (diareo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "διεμερίσθη" describes an action of dividing or separating that was performed in the past. It implies that something was divided or separated by someone or something else.
