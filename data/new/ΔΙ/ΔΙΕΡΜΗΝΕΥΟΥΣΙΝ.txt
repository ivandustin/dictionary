Etymology: The word "διερμηνευσην" comes from the Greek verb "διερμηνεύω" (di-er-mi-ne-vo), which means "to interpret" or "to translate."

Grammar: "Διερμηνευσην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Διερμηνευσην" refers to the act of interpreting or translating. It can be used to describe the process of conveying the meaning of one language into another, or the act of explaining or clarifying something.
