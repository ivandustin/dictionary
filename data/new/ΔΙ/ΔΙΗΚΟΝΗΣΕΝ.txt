Etymology: The word "διηκονησεν" (diēkonēsen) comes from the Greek verb "διακονέω" (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister." It is derived from the noun "διάκονος" (diákonos), meaning "servant" or "minister."

Grammar: "Διηκονησεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "διακονέω" (diakonéō). In Greek, verbs change their form based on tense, voice, mood, person, and number. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, the active voice shows that the subject performs the action, and the indicative mood states a fact or reality. The third person singular form refers to a singular subject.

Description: "Διηκονησεν" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it served" or "he/she/it ministered." It describes an action of serving or providing assistance to others.
