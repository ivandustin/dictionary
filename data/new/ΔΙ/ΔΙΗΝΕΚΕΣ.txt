Etymology: The word "διηνεκες" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "δι-" (di-), meaning "through" or "across," and the adjective "ἀείνεκες" (aeinekes), meaning "continuous" or "unceasing."

Grammar: "Διηνεκες" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine, feminine, and neuter gender, and it can be declined to match the case, number, and gender of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Διηνεκες" is used to describe something that is continuous, uninterrupted, or perpetual. It implies that the described object or action is ongoing without any breaks or interruptions.
