Etymology: The word "δικαίε" (dikaíe) comes from the Greek word "δίκαιος" (díkaios), which means "just" or "righteous".

Grammar: "Δικαίε" is the vocative form of the adjective "δίκαιος" (díkaios) in the masculine singular. It is used to directly address or call someone who is just or righteous.

Description: "Δικαίε" is a term used in Greek to address someone who is considered just or righteous. It is often used in literature, poetry, or in formal settings to show respect or admiration towards the person being addressed.
