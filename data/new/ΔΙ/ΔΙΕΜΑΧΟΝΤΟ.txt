Etymology: The word "διεμαχοντο" comes from the Greek verb "διαμαχομαι" (diamachomai), which means "to fight" or "to quarrel". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "δια-" (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the verb "μαχομαι" (machomai), meaning "to fight".

Grammar: "διεμαχοντο" is the third person plural, past tense, middle voice form of the verb "διαμαχομαι" (diamachomai). In Greek grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The past tense form indicates that the action took place in the past.

Description: "διεμαχοντο" describes an action that occurred in the past, where multiple parties were involved in fighting or quarreling. It can be translated as "they were fighting" or "they were quarreling".
