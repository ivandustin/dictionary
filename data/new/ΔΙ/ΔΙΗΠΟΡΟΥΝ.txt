Etymology: The word "διηπορούν" comes from the Greek verb "διηπορέω" (diēporéō), which means "to be in doubt" or "to be perplexed." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "δι-" (di-), meaning "through" or "across," and the verb "ἀπορέω" (aporéō), meaning "to be at a loss" or "to be without resources."

Grammar: "διηπορούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "διηπορέω" (diēporéō). It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on tense, person, number, voice, and mood.

Description: "διηπορούν" is a Greek verb that describes the action of being in doubt or being perplexed. It indicates a state of uncertainty or confusion.
