Etymology: The word "διατεταγμένος" comes from the Greek verb "διατάσσω" (diatássō), which means "to arrange" or "to order". The suffix "-μένος" (-menos) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, indicating a state of being or having been arranged or ordered.

Grammar: "Διατεταγμένος" is the masculine singular form of the past participle of the verb "διατάσσω" in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Διατεταγμένος" is an adjective in Greek that means "arranged" or "ordered". It describes something that has been put in a specific order or arrangement.
