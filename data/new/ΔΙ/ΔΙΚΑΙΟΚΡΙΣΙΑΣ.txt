Etymology: The word "δικαιοκρισίας" comes from the Greek words "δίκαιος" (dikaios), meaning "just" or "righteous," and "κρίση" (krisi), meaning "judgment" or "decision." 

Grammar: "Δικαιοκρισίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Δικαιοκρισίας" refers to the act or process of judging or making legal decisions based on justice and fairness. It can also refer to the judicial system or the administration of justice.
