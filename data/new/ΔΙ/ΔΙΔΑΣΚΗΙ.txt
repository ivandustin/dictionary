Etymology: The word "διδασκην" (didaskēn) comes from the Greek verb "διδάσκω" (didaskō), which means "to teach" or "to instruct."

Grammar: "Διδασκην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Διδασκην" refers to the act of teaching or instruction. It can also be used to describe the process of imparting knowledge or skills to others.
