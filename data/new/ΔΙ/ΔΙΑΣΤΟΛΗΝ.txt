Etymology: The word "διαστολήν" (diastolḗn) comes from the Greek verb "διαστέλλω" (diastéllō), which means "to separate" or "to stretch out."

Grammar: "Διαστολήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive.

Description: "Διαστολήν" refers to the act of stretching or expanding something, or the state of being stretched or expanded. It can also be used in a medical context to describe the dilation or expansion of blood vessels, particularly the relaxation phase of the cardiac cycle.
