Etymology: The word "δικαιοσύνη" (dikaiosýni) comes from the Greek roots "δίκη" (díki), meaning "justice" or "righteousness," and "σύνη" (sýni), meaning "quality" or "state of being." 

Grammar: In Greek, "δικαιοσύνη" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is in the nominative case and singular number. 

Description: "Δικαιοσύνη" is a concept that encompasses the idea of justice, fairness, and righteousness. It refers to the quality or state of being just or morally right. In Greek philosophy, "δικαιοσύνη" is often associated with the idea of virtue and the proper balance between individual rights and societal obligations.
