Etymology: The word "διωκομαι" (diōkomai) comes from the Greek verb "διώκω" (diōkō), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". 

Grammar: "Διωκομαι" is a first person singular present middle/passive indicative form of the verb "διώκω". In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

Description: "Διωκομαι" is a Greek verb that can be translated as "I am pursued" or "I am chased". It is used to express the action of being pursued or chased by someone or something.
