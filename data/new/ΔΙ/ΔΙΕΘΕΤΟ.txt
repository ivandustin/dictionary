Etymology: The word "διεθνές" (diethnes) comes from the Greek roots "δια-" (dia-), meaning "through" or "across," and "εθνικός" (ethnikos), meaning "national." It literally translates to "through nations" or "across nations."

Grammar: "Διεθνές" is an adjective in the Greek language. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, "διεθνές συμβόλαιο" (diethnes symvolaio) means "international agreement," where "συμβόλαιο" (symvolaio) is the noun being modified.

Description: "Διεθνές" is used to describe something that is related to or involves multiple nations or countries. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as international relations, diplomacy, trade, and organizations. It indicates that something has a global or cross-border scope or significance.
