Etymology: The word "ατενισαντες" comes from the Greek verb "ατενίζω" (atenízo), which means "to gaze intently" or "to fix one's eyes upon". The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending, indicating that the action of gazing intently has been performed.

Grammar: "Ατενισαντες" is the plural form of the verb "ατενίζω" in the aorist tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have gazed intently or fixed their eyes upon something.

Description: "Ατενισαντες" is a Greek word that describes a collective action of people gazing intently or fixing their eyes upon something. It implies a focused and concentrated observation or scrutiny.
