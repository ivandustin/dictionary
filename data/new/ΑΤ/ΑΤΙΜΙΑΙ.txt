Etymology: The word "ατιμιαί" comes from the Greek word "ατιμία" (atimía), which means "dishonor" or "disgrace".

Grammar: "ατιμιαί" is a noun in the plural form. It is the feminine form of the adjective "ατιμιαίος" (atimiaíos), which means "dishonorable" or "disgraceful".

Description: "ατιμιαί" refers to actions, behaviors, or situations that are considered dishonorable or disgraceful. It is used to describe acts that bring shame or discredit to someone or something.
