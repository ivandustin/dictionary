Etymology: The word "ατακτους" (ataktoús) comes from the Greek adjective "ατάκτης" (atáktis), which means "disorderly" or "unruly". It is derived from the negative prefix "α-" and the noun "τάξη" (táxi), meaning "order" or "arrangement".

Grammar: "Ατακτους" is the masculine plural form of the adjective, used to describe multiple nouns that are disorderly or unruly. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the nouns they modify.

Description: "Ατακτους" is an adjective in Greek that describes something or someone as disorderly, unruly, or undisciplined. It can be used to describe a group of people, objects, or actions that lack order or organization.
