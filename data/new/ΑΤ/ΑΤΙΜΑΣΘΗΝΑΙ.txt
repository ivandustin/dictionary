Etymology: The word "ατιμασθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "ατιμαζω" (atimazo), which means "to dishonor" or "to disgrace". The suffix "-θηναι" (-thenai) is a passive form ending, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "ατιμασθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "ατιμαζω" (atimazo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "ατιμασθηναι" is used to express the idea of being dishonored or disgraced. It implies that someone or something has been subjected to a loss of honor, reputation, or dignity.
