Etymology: The word "ατιμος" (atimos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), which means "not," and the word "τιμη" (time), which means "honor" or "esteem." Therefore, "ατιμος" can be translated as "without honor" or "dishonorable."

Grammar: In Greek, "ατιμος" is an adjective. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it becomes "ατιμος" (atimos). If it modifies a feminine singular noun, it becomes "ατιμη" (atimi). In the plural form, it becomes "ατιμοι" (atimoi) for masculine nouns and "ατιμες" (atimes) for feminine nouns.

Description: The word "ατιμος" is used to describe someone or something that lacks honor, respect, or dignity. It can refer to a person who is considered dishonorable, shameful, or unworthy. It can also be used to describe actions, behaviors, or qualities that are deemed disgraceful or disreputable.
