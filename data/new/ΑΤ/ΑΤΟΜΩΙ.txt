Etymology: The word "ατομοι" (atomoi) comes from the Greek word "ατομος" (atomos), which means "indivisible" or "uncuttable". It was originally used in ancient Greek philosophy to refer to the smallest possible unit of matter.

Grammar: "Ατομοι" (atomoi) is the plural form of the noun "ατομος" (atomos) in Greek. It is used to refer to multiple atoms.

Description: In modern science, an atom is the basic unit of matter. It consists of a nucleus, which contains protons and neutrons, and is surrounded by electrons. Atoms combine to form molecules, which are the building blocks of all substances. The study of atoms and their properties is known as atomic physics or chemistry.
