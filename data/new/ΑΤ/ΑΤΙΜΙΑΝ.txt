Etymology: The word "ατιμιαν" comes from the Greek word "ατιμία" (atimía), which means "dishonor" or "disgrace".

Grammar: "ατιμιαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "ατιμιαν" refers to the state of being dishonored or disgraced. It can also be used to describe an act or behavior that brings shame or disgrace.
