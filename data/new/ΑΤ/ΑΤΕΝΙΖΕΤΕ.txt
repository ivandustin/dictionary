Etymology: The word "ατενίζετε" comes from the Greek verb "ατενίζω" (atenízo), which means "to gaze intently" or "to fix one's eyes upon." It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), which denotes intensity, and the verb "τενίζω" (tenízo), meaning "to stretch" or "to extend."

Grammar: "Ατενίζετε" is the second person plural form of the verb "ατενίζω" in the present tense. It is used when addressing a group of people and means "you all gaze intently" or "you all fix your eyes upon."

Description: "Ατενίζετε" describes the action of looking at something with great concentration or focus. It implies a deliberate and intense observation, often with the intention of understanding or analyzing what is being observed.
