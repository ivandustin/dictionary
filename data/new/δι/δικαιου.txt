The word δικαιου (dikaiou) is a genitive form of the Greek word δίκαιος (dikaios), which means "just" or "righteous". It is derived from the noun δίκη (dikē), which means "justice" or "judgment". In Greek grammar, δικαιου is the genitive singular form of δίκαιος, indicating possession or relationship to something else.

In terms of usage, δικαιου can be found in various contexts in ancient Greek literature, including legal and philosophical texts. It is often used to describe individuals who are morally upright and adhere to principles of justice and fairness.
