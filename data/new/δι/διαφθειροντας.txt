Word: διαφθειρόντας

Etymology:
The word διαφθείρω (diaphthéirō) comes from the Greek roots διά (diá), meaning "through," and φθείρω (phtheírō), meaning "to corrupt" or "to destroy." The suffix -όντας (-óntas) is the present participle form of the verb, indicating ongoing action.

Grammar:
διαφθειρόντας is the present participle form of the Greek verb διαφθείρω, which means "to corrupt" or "to destroy." It is in the active voice and the masculine gender, indicating that the subject is a male who is currently corrupting or destroying something.

Description:
The word διαφθειρόντας describes an ongoing action of corruption or destruction being carried out by a male subject. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as politics, business, or personal relationships, to describe someone who is actively causing harm or damage.
