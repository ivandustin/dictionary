Word: διακονησαντες

Etymology:
The word διακονησαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διακονέω (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister." The suffix -σαντες (-santes) is the aorist active participle ending, indicating that the action of serving or ministering has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
διακονησαντες is a third-person plural aorist active participle in the Greek language. In English, it can be translated as "having served" or "having ministered." The word can be used as a verb or a noun, depending on the context.

Description:
διακονησαντες is a word that describes an action that has been completed in the past. It is often used in the context of Christian ministry, where it refers to those who have served or ministered in some capacity. The word can also be used more generally to describe any act of service or ministry.
