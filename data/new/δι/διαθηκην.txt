Word: διαθήκην

Etymology:
The word διαθήκην (diathēkēn) is derived from the Greek word διαθήκη (diathēkē), which means "a will, testament, or covenant." It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across," and the noun θήκη (thēkē), meaning "a receptacle or container."

Grammar:
διαθήκην is a noun in the accusative case, indicating that it is the direct object of the sentence. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
διαθήκην is a word that refers to a will, testament, or covenant. In ancient Greek culture, a διαθήκη was a legal document that outlined the distribution of a person's property after their death. It was often used to ensure that the person's wishes were carried out and that their property was distributed fairly among their heirs. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word διαθήκη is used to refer to the covenant between God and humanity, particularly in the context of Jesus Christ's death and resurrection.
