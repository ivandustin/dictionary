Word: διαφορα

Etymology:
The word διαφορα (diaphora) comes from the Greek root διαφέρω (diapherō), which means "to differ" or "to distinguish." It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across," and the verb φέρω (pherō), meaning "to carry" or "to bear."

Grammar:
In Greek, διαφορα is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. Its plural form is διαφορές (diaphores). It is often used in the plural form to refer to differences or distinctions between things.

Description:
διαφορα is a Greek word that can be translated to mean "difference" or "distinction." It is often used to describe the ways in which things are different from one another, or to highlight the unique qualities or characteristics of something. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from discussing the differences between two ideas or concepts, to describing the unique features of a particular object or person.
