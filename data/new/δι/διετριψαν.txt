Word: διετριψαν

Etymology: διετριψαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix δι- (meaning "through" or "across") and the verb ετριψαν (meaning "they rubbed" or "they wore away"). 

Grammar: διετριψαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb διατρίβω (diatribō), which means "to spend time" or "to pass time." In this form, it indicates that a group of people spent time doing something in the past.

Description: διετριψαν is a verb that describes an action that took place in the past, specifically an action where a group of people spent time doing something. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly they were doing.
