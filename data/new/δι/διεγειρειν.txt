Word: διεγειρειν

Etymology:
The word διεγειρειν comes from the Greek roots δια- (dia-, meaning "through" or "across") and εγείρω (egeirō, meaning "to wake up" or "to rouse"). Therefore, διεγειρειν means "to wake up thoroughly" or "to arouse completely".

Grammar:
διεγειρειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is εγειρ-. The prefix δια- adds the meaning of "thoroughly" or "completely" to the verb.

Description:
διεγειρειν is a strong verb that implies a forceful awakening or arousal. It can be used in a literal sense, such as waking up from sleep, or in a figurative sense, such as arousing someone's interest or enthusiasm. The verb is often used in the context of religious or spiritual awakening, as well as in medical contexts to describe the process of reviving someone who has fainted or lost consciousness.
