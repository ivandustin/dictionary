Word: δικαιως

Etymology:
The word δικαιως (dikaios) is derived from the Greek word δίκαιος (dikaios), which means "just" or "righteous". It is formed from the root word δίκη (dikē), which means "justice" or "righteousness".

Grammar:
δικαιως is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate the manner or degree of an action or quality. In English, it can be translated as "justly", "righteously", or "fairly".

Description:
δικαιως is a word that conveys the idea of justice, righteousness, and fairness. It is often used in the context of legal or moral matters, to indicate that something is being done in accordance with what is right and just. It can also be used to describe a person who is just and fair in their dealings with others.
