The word δικαιων (dikaiōn) is a Greek adjective that means "just" or "righteous". It is derived from the Greek word δίκη (dikē), which means "justice" or "righteousness". In terms of grammar, δικαιων is in the masculine nominative singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

In ancient Greek philosophy, the concept of δικαιων was central to the idea of living a virtuous life. It was believed that by acting justly and righteously, one could achieve a state of eudaimonia, or happiness and fulfillment. 

Overall, δικαιων is a word that emphasizes the importance of justice and righteousness in both personal and societal contexts.
