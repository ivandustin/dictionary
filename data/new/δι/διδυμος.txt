Word: διδυμος

Etymology:
The word διδυμος (didymos) comes from the Greek language and means "twin". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *duwo, which means "two".

Grammar:
In Greek, διδυμος is an adjective that can be used to describe a pair of twins or anything that comes in pairs. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative).

Description:
διδυμος is a word that is commonly used in Greek mythology to describe famous twins such as Castor and Pollux, the Dioscuri. It is also used in everyday language to describe things that come in pairs, such as shoes or gloves. The word has a rich history and is still used today in various contexts.
