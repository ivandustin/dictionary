Word: διηρχετο

Etymology:
διηρχετο is a Greek verb that comes from the root words δια- (dia-) meaning "through" and ἔρχομαι (érchomai) meaning "to go". Therefore, διηρχετο means "he/she/it went through".

Grammar:
διηρχετο is a third-person singular, past tense, indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. It is conjugated from the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai) in the aorist tense.

Description:
διηρχετο is a verb that describes an action of going through or passing by something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person walking through a street or a river flowing through a valley. It is commonly used in Ancient Greek literature and is still used in modern Greek language.
