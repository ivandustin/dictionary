Word: διδαξον

Etymology: διδαξον is a verb in the aorist imperative active form of the Greek word διδάσκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The word is derived from the root word διδαχή (didache), which means "teaching" or "instruction".

Grammar: διδαξον is a second person singular aorist imperative active form of the verb διδάσκω (didasko). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give a command or make a request.

Description: διδαξον is a command to teach or instruct someone. It is a call to action, urging the listener to take on the role of a teacher and impart knowledge to others. The word carries a sense of urgency and importance, emphasizing the value of education and the responsibility of passing on knowledge to future generations.
