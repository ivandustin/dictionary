Word: δικαιωθεντες

Etymology:
The word δικαιωθεντες is derived from the Greek verb δικαιόω (dikaioó), which means "to justify" or "to declare righteous". The suffix -θεντες (-thentes) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the word is in the passive voice and refers to those who have been justified or declared righteous.

Grammar:
δικαιωθεντες is a passive participle in the nominative plural form. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or verbs depending on the context. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, the people referred to by δικαιωθεντες) is the recipient of the action rather than the doer.

Description:
δικαιωθεντες is a term used in Christian theology to refer to the concept of justification by faith. It is used in the New Testament to describe those who have been made righteous through faith in Jesus Christ. The word emphasizes that the righteousness of the individual is not earned through good works or personal merit, but is a gift of God's grace.
