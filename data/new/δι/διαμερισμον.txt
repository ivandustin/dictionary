Word: διαμερισμόν

Etymology: δια- (dia-, prefix meaning "through" or "across") + μερισμός (merismós, "division" or "partition"). The word διαμερισμόν comes from the Greek language.

Grammar: διαμερισμόν is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, it is in the accusative case.

Description: διαμερισμόν refers to the act of dividing or partitioning something into parts or sections. It can also refer to the resulting parts or sections themselves. The word is often used in legal or political contexts, such as the division of land or the partitioning of a country.
