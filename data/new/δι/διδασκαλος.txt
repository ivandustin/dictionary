Word: διδασκαλος

Etymology:
The word διδασκαλος (didaskalos) is a Greek word that means "teacher" or "instructor". It is derived from the verb διδασκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct".

Grammar:
διδασκαλος is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive, dative, and accusative.

Description:
διδασκαλος is a term used to refer to a teacher or instructor. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe someone who taught a particular subject, such as philosophy, mathematics, or music. The word is also used in the New Testament to refer to Jesus Christ as a teacher. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a teacher or professor.
