Word: διεμαρτυρω

Etymology:
The word διεμαρτυρω comes from the Greek words δια (dia) meaning "through" or "across" and μαρτυρέω (martyreo) meaning "to testify" or "to bear witness". The prefix δια (dia) emphasizes the thoroughness or completeness of the action of testifying.

Grammar:
διεμαρτυρω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The verb stem is μαρτυρ- (martur-) and the prefix δια (dia) has been added to it to form the compound verb διεμαρτυρω.

Description:
The word διεμαρτυρω means "to testify thoroughly" or "to bear witness completely". It is often used in legal or religious contexts to describe the act of giving a complete and truthful testimony. The word emphasizes the importance of giving a thorough and complete testimony, without leaving out any important details.
