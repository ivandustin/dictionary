Word: διακρινόμενος

Etymology:
The word διακρινόμενος comes from the Greek verb διακρίνω (diakrino), which means "to distinguish" or "to separate". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word describes an ongoing action or state.

Grammar:
διακρινόμενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb διακρίνω, which is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice.

Description:
The word διακρινόμενος describes someone or something that is in the process of being distinguished or separated from others. It can also be used to describe someone who is discerning or able to make careful distinctions. In English, it can be translated as "distinguishing" or "discerning".
