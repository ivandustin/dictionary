Word: διπλοτερον

Etymology:
The word διπλοτερον comes from the Greek root words διπλός (diplós) meaning "double" and -τερος (-teros) meaning "comparative suffix". Therefore, διπλοτερον means "more double" or "doubly".

Grammar:
διπλοτερον is a neuter singular adjective in the comparative degree. It is used to compare two things, indicating that one is more double than the other.

Description:
διπλοτερον is a word that is not commonly used in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek texts and is used to describe something that is twice as much or twice as many as something else. For example, if someone says "this book is διπλοτερον than that book", they mean that the first book has twice as many pages as the second book.
