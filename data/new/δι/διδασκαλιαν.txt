Word: διδασκαλίαν (didaskalian)

Etymology: The word διδασκαλίαν comes from the Greek verb διδάσκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The suffix -ιαν (-ian) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the action or state of the verb.

Grammar: διδασκαλίαν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. It is the direct object of a verb or a preposition in a sentence.

Description: διδασκαλίαν refers to the act of teaching or instruction. It can also refer to a specific teaching or doctrine. In the New Testament, it is often used to refer to the teachings of Jesus or the apostles.
