The word διακονιας (diakonias) is a Greek noun that means "service" or "ministry". It is derived from the verb διακονεω (diakoneo), which means "to serve" or "to minister". In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to the service or ministry of the early Christian church, particularly in the context of caring for the poor and needy. The word is also used to describe the role of deacons in the church, who are responsible for serving and assisting the needs of the congregation. In terms of grammar, διακονιας is a feminine noun in the genitive case.
