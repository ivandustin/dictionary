The word "διανεμηθηι" (dianemethēi) is a Greek verb in the aorist passive tense. It comes from the root word "διανέμω" (dianemō), which means "to distribute" or "to divide among." The passive form indicates that the action of distributing or dividing is being done to the subject of the sentence rather than by the subject.

In terms of grammar, "διανεμηθηι" is a third-person singular verb in the aorist passive tense. The aorist tense in Greek generally indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it.

Overall, "διανεμηθηι" means "it was distributed" or "it was divided among."
