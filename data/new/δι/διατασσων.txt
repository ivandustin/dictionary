Word: διατασσων

Etymology:
The word διατασσων comes from the Greek verb διατάσσω (diatássō), which means "to arrange" or "to order". It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb τάσσω (tássō), which means "to arrange" or "to put in order".

Grammar:
διατασσων is the present participle form of the verb διατάσσω (diatássō), which means "arranging" or "ordering". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
διατασσων is used to describe someone or something that is currently arranging or ordering something. It can also be used as an adjective to describe a person who is orderly or well-organized. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe military leaders who are skilled at organizing their troops.
