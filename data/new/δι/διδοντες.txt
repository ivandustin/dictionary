Word: διδόντες

Etymology:
διδόντες is the present participle of the Greek verb δίδωμι (didōmi), which means "to give". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-, which means "to give" or "to put".

Grammar:
διδόντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb δίδωμι (didōmi), which means "to give". As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action of giving.

Description:
διδόντες is used in Greek to describe someone who is currently giving something. It can be translated to English as "giving" or "those who give". The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone who is giving a gift, or someone who is giving advice or instruction.
