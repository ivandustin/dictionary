Word: διαδοχον

The word διαδοχον comes from the Greek word διάδοχος (diádokhos), which means "successor" or "heir". The word is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the verb δέχομαι (dékhomai), meaning "to receive" or "to accept".

διαδοχον is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the noun that is directly affected by the action of the verb.

διαδοχον refers to a successor or heir, someone who takes over a position or role from someone else.
