Word: διαφθειρεται

Etymology:
The word διαφθείρω (diaphthéirō) comes from the Greek roots διά (diá), meaning "through," and φθείρω (phtheírō), meaning "to corrupt" or "to destroy." The word διαφθειρεται (diaphtheiretai) is the third person singular present passive indicative form of the verb διαφθείρω (diaphthéirō).

Grammar:
διαφθειρεται (diaphtheiretai) is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated from the root verb διαφθείρω (diaphthéirō), which means "to corrupt" or "to destroy." The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
The word διαφθειρεται (diaphtheiretai) means "is being corrupted" or "is being destroyed." It can be used to describe a physical object that is deteriorating or a person who is being morally corrupted. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe the decay or decline of something.
