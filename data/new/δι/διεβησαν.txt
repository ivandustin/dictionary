Word: διεβησαν

Etymology:
The word διεβησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βαίνω (baino), which means "to walk" or "to go". The prefix δι- (di-) in this word means "through" or "across". Therefore, διεβησαν means "they went through" or "they crossed over".

Grammar:
διεβησαν is a third-person plural aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performed the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
διεβησαν is a verb that describes an action of going through or crossing over something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as crossing a river or a bridge, or in a figurative sense, such as overcoming an obstacle or a challenge. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the crossing of the Jordan River by the Israelites or the disciples crossing the Sea of Galilee.
