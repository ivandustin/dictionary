Word: διαστασης

Etymology:
The word διαστασης (diastasis) comes from the Greek verb διαστατικός (diastatikos), which means "to separate" or "to divide." The noun form διαστασης (diastasis) means "separation" or "division."

Grammar:
διαστασης (diastasis) is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
διαστασης (diastasis) can refer to physical separation or distance between two objects or points. It can also refer to a separation or division in a non-physical sense, such as a separation between two ideas or concepts. In medical terminology, diastasis can refer to a separation of muscles or other tissues in the body.
