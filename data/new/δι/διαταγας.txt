Word: διαταγας

Etymology:
The word διαταγας (diatagas) is derived from the Greek verb διατάσσω (diatasso), which means "to arrange" or "to command." The suffix -ας (-as) is a masculine nominative singular ending.

Grammar:
διαταγας is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
διαταγας refers to a command or an order given by someone in authority. It can also refer to an arrangement or a disposition of things. The word is often used in a military context, but it can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any kind of authoritative instruction or arrangement.
