Word: διακονηθεισα

Etymology: διακονηθεισα is a Greek word that comes from the verb διακονέω (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister". The suffix -θεισα (-theisa) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the word is in the passive voice and refers to a female subject.

Grammar: διακονηθεισα is a passive participle in the aorist tense, feminine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb διακονέω, which is a first conjugation verb in the active voice.

Description: διακονηθεισα can be translated as "having been served" or "having been ministered to". It is often used in the New Testament to describe women who served or ministered to Jesus and his disciples. In modern Greek, the word is still used to refer to someone who serves or works in a helping profession, such as a nurse or a social worker.
