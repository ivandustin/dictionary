Word: διωκτην

Etymology:
The word διωκτην (diōktēn) is derived from the Greek verb διώκω (diōkō), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". The suffix -την (-tēn) is a common suffix used to form nouns in Greek.

Grammar:
διωκτην is a noun in the singular form. It is a masculine noun in the third declension in Greek.

Description:
The word διωκτην (diōktēn) refers to a hunter or a pursuer. It is often used in the context of hunting animals or chasing after someone or something. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the god Apollo, who was known as a skilled hunter and pursuer.
