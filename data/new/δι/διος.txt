Word: διος

Etymology:
The word διος (dios) is derived from the Greek word Διός (Dios), which is the genitive form of the name of the Greek god Zeus. The word is often used in Greek mythology to refer to Zeus or anything related to him.

Grammar:
In Greek, διος (dios) is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is often used as an adjective to describe something that is related to Zeus or has the qualities of Zeus.

Description:
The word διος (dios) is a significant term in Greek mythology, as it is used to refer to the powerful god Zeus. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe the strength, power, and authority of Zeus. The word can also be used to describe anything that is related to Zeus, such as his temple or his followers.
