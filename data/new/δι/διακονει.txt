The word διακονει (diakonei) is a Greek verb that means "to serve" or "to minister." It comes from the root word διάκονος (diakonos), which means "servant" or "minister." In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the work of deacons in the early Christian church, who were responsible for serving the needs of the community. The verb form can also be used more generally to describe any act of service or ministry. In terms of grammar, διακονει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form.
