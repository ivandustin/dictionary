Word: διαστησάντες

Etymology: δια- (dia-, prefix meaning "through" or "across") + στησάντες (stesantes, aorist participle of σταίνω, meaning "to stand")

Grammar: διαστησάντες is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. It is masculine, plural, and nominative in form.

Description: διαστησάντες means "having stood apart" or "having separated." It is often used in a military context to describe soldiers who have broken formation or separated from their unit. It can also be used more generally to describe any group of people who have become separated or scattered.
