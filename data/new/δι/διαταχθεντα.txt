Word: διαταχθεντα

Etymology:
The word διαταχθεντα is derived from the Greek verb διατάσσω (diatássō), which means "to arrange" or "to command." The word is formed from the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across," and the verb τάσσω (tássō), which means "to arrange" or "to put in order."

Grammar:
διαταχθεντα is the plural neuter form of the past participle of the verb διατάσσω (diatássō). It is used as an adjective to describe things that have been arranged or commanded.

Description:
διαταχθεντα is an adjective that describes something that has been arranged or commanded. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as an army that has been ordered into battle, a list of tasks that has been organized, or a group of people who have been assigned specific roles. The word implies a sense of order and structure, and suggests that the thing being described has been carefully planned and executed.
