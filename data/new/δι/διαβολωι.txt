The word "διαβολωι" (diaboloi) is a Greek noun that means "slanderer" or "accuser". It is derived from the verb "διαβάλλω" (diaballo), which means "to slander" or "to accuse falsely". In grammar, it is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. In ancient Greek mythology, "diaboloi" referred to evil spirits or demons. Today, the word is commonly used in Greek to refer to someone who spreads false rumors or gossip.
