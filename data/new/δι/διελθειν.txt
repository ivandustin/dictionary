Word: διελθειν

Etymology:
The word διελθειν comes from the Greek roots δια- (dia-), meaning "through," and ἐλθεῖν (elthein), meaning "to come." Therefore, διελθειν means "to go through" or "to pass through."

Grammar:
διελθειν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The word is in the infinitive form, meaning that it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
διελθειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used literally to describe physically passing through a space or figuratively to describe overcoming a challenge or obstacle. It can also be used in a temporal sense to describe the passage of time. Overall, διελθειν is a useful and commonly used verb in the Greek language.
