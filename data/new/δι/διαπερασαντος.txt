Word: διαπερασαντος

Etymology: δια- (dia-, prefix meaning "through") + περασαντος (perasantos, aorist participle of περαίνω, meaning "to pierce through"). Therefore, διαπερασαντος means "having pierced through".

Grammar: διαπερασαντος is a masculine singular genitive participle in Greek. It modifies a noun and indicates possession or association. In English, it can be translated as "of the one who pierced through".

Description: διαπερασαντος is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 19:34. It refers to the soldier who pierced the side of Jesus with a spear while he was on the cross. The word emphasizes the force and completeness of the action, indicating that the spear went all the way through Jesus' body.
