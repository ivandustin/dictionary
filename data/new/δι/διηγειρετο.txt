Word: διηγειρετο

Etymology:
The word διηγειρετο comes from the Greek verb διεγείρω (diēgeirō), which means "to wake up" or "to arouse". It is derived from the prefix δι- (di-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb εγείρω (egeirō), which means "to wake up" or "to rouse".

Grammar:
διηγειρετο is a third-person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb διεγείρω (diēgeirō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and person. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description:
διηγειρετο can be translated as "he/she/it was awakened" or "he/she/it woke up". It is a past tense form, indicating that the action of waking up has already happened. The middle voice suggests that the subject woke up on their own or for their own benefit. The word can be used in a literal sense, to describe someone waking up from sleep, or in a figurative sense, to describe someone becoming alert or active.
