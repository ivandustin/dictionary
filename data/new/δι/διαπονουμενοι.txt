Word: διαπονουμενοι

Etymology: δια- (dia-) meaning "through" or "across" and πονέω (ponéo) meaning "to suffer" or "to be in pain". Therefore, διαπονουμενοι (diaponoumenoi) means "suffering through" or "enduring".

Grammar: διαπονουμενοι is the present participle form of the verb διαπονέω (diaponéo), which means "to suffer through" or "to endure". It is in the masculine plural form, indicating that the subject is a group of males who are suffering or enduring.

Description: διαπονουμενοι is a Greek word that describes a group of people who are enduring or suffering through something. It can be used to describe physical or emotional pain, hardship, or difficulty. The word emphasizes the idea of perseverance and endurance through difficult circumstances.
