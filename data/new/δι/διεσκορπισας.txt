Word: διεσκορπίσας

Etymology:
The word διεσκορπίσας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σκορπίζω (skorpízo), which means "to scatter" or "to disperse." The prefix δι- (di-) adds the meaning of "thoroughly" or "completely," so διεσκορπίσας means "to scatter completely" or "to disperse thoroughly."

Grammar:
διεσκορπίσας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb σκορπίζω (skorpízo) and the prefix δι- (di-). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject (you) performed the action. 

Description:
διεσκορπίσας is a verb that describes the act of scattering or dispersing something completely or thoroughly. It can be used in a literal sense, such as scattering seeds in a field, or in a figurative sense, such as dispersing a crowd or breaking up a group.
