The word "διελευσεται" is a Greek verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root word "διέρχομαι" which means "to pass through" or "to cross over". 

In terms of grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact or reality.

Therefore, "διελευσεται" can be translated as "he/she/it is passing through/crossing over (something) for themselves". 

Overall, the word has a sense of movement and self-benefit.
