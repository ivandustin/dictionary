Word: διασκορπισθησονται

Etymology: δια- (dia-) meaning "through" or "across", σκορπίζω (skorpízo) meaning "scatter", and -θησονται (-thesontai) which is a future passive indicative ending. Therefore, the word means "they will be scattered throughout".

Grammar: διασκορπισθησονται is a third-person plural future passive indicative form of the verb διασκορπίζω (diaskorpízo), which means "to scatter throughout". The word is in the future tense, indicating that the action of scattering will happen in the future. It is also in the passive voice, indicating that the subject will receive the action of scattering rather than performing it.

Description: διασκορπισθησονται is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 26:31 and Mark 14:27. In both instances, Jesus predicts that his disciples will be scattered throughout when he is arrested. The word is used to describe the physical scattering of people or things throughout a space.
