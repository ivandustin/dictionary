Word: διελθωμεν

Etymology:
διελθωμεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb διέρχομαι (diérchomai), which means "to pass through" or "to go through". The word is formed by adding the prefix δι- (di-), which means "through", to the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), which means "to come" or "to go".

Grammar:
διελθωμεν is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb διέρχομαι (diérchomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the subjunctive mood is used to express a possibility, a wish, or a hypothetical situation. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
διελθωμεν can be translated as "let us pass through" or "may we go through". It is often used in the context of a journey or a difficult situation that needs to be overcome. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action is not certain, but rather a possibility or a wish. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe overcoming obstacles or challenges in life.
