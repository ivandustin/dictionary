Word: διηλθον

Etymology:
διηλθον is a Greek verb that comes from the root words δια- (dia-) meaning "through" and ἔρχομαι (érchomai) meaning "to go". Therefore, διηλθον means "I went through" or "I passed through".

Grammar:
διηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb διέρχομαι (diérchomai), which means "to pass through" or "to go through". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
διηλθον is a verb that describes the action of passing through or going through something. It is often used in a literal sense, such as passing through a city or a tunnel, but can also be used figuratively, such as passing through a difficult time or situation. The aorist tense indicates that the action of passing through has been completed in the past.
