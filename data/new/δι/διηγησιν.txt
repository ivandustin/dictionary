Word: διηγησιν

Etymology:
The word διηγησιν (diēgēsin) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb διηγέομαι (diēgéomai), which means "to narrate" or "to tell a story." The noun form διηγησιν refers to a narrative or a story.

Grammar:
In Greek, διηγησιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. It is the direct object of a verb or a preposition in a sentence.

Description:
The word διηγησιν is often used in Greek literature to refer to a narrative or a story. It can also be used to describe the act of storytelling or narrating. In modern Greek, the word is still used in the same way, and it is often used in the context of literature or film to refer to a plot or storyline.
