Word: διεθετο

Etymology:
διεθετο is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix δι- (di-), meaning "through" or "across," and the verb ἔθετο (etheto), meaning "he placed" or "he set." Therefore, διεθετο can be translated as "he placed through" or "he set across."

Grammar:
διεθετο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb διατίθημι (diatithemi), which means "to place," "to set," or "to arrange." The aorist tense indicates that the action took place in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject acted upon himself or herself.

Description:
διεθετο can be translated as "he arranged for himself" or "he set aside for himself." It implies that the subject took deliberate action to prepare or reserve something for his or her own use. The verb is often used in the context of making provisions or preparations, such as setting aside food or supplies for a journey.
