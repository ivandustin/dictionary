Word: διωγμόν (diogmon)

Etymology:
The word διωγμόν (diogmon) comes from the Greek verb διώκω (dioko), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". The suffix -μόν (-mon) is a nominal suffix that is used to form nouns from verbs. Therefore, διωγμόν (diogmon) means "the act of pursuing" or "the act of chasing".

Grammar:
διωγμόν (diogmon) is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description:
διωγμόν (diogmon) refers to the act of pursuing or chasing someone or something. It can also be used to describe the persecution of a person or group of people. In ancient Greece, it was often used in the context of hunting or chasing animals. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to describe the persecution of minorities or political opponents.
