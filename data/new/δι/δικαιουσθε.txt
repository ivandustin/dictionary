Word: δικαιουσθε

Etymology:
The word δικαιουσθε (dikaiousthe) is a verb form in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word δικαιόω (dikaióō), which means "to justify" or "to declare righteous". The suffix -ουσθε (-ousthe) is a conjugation ending that indicates the present passive voice, meaning that the subject is being acted upon.

Grammar:
δικαιουσθε is a present passive indicative verb form in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word δικαιόω (dikaióō) and the present passive ending -ουσθε (-ousthe). The verb form indicates that the subject is being justified or declared righteous in the present tense.

Description:
The word δικαιουσθε (dikaiousthe) is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 3:24. In this context, it is used to describe the act of being justified or declared righteous by God through faith in Jesus Christ. The word emphasizes the passive nature of this justification, indicating that it is not something that the subject does, but rather something that is done to them.
