Word: διδάσκαλον

Etymology:
The word διδάσκαλον (didaskalon) is derived from the Greek verb διδάσκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The suffix -ον (-on) is a neuter noun ending in Greek.

Grammar:
διδάσκαλον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In the nominative case, it is spelled διδάσκαλον. In the genitive case, it is spelled διδασκάλου (didaskalou). In the accusative case, it is spelled διδάσκαλον (didaskalon).

Description:
διδάσκαλον refers to a teacher or instructor. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe someone who taught a specific subject or skill, such as music, philosophy, or rhetoric. The term was also used to refer to a mentor or guide who provided moral or spiritual instruction. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a teacher or professor.
