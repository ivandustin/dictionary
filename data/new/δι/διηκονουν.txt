Word: διηκονουν

The word διηκονουν (diakonoun) is a Greek verb that comes from the noun διακονος (diakonos), which means "servant" or "minister." The verb form means "to serve" or "to minister."

διηκονουν is a third-person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is in the indicative mood, which indicates a statement of fact, and the present tense, which indicates an ongoing action in the present.

Description:
διηκονουν is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the service or ministry of individuals in the early Christian church. It is often translated as "to serve" or "to minister."
