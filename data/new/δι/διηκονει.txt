The word διηκονει (diakonei) is a Greek verb that means "to serve" or "to minister." It is derived from the noun διάκονος (diakonos), which means "servant" or "minister." In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to those who serve in the church, such as deacons or ministers. 

In terms of grammar, διηκονει is a third-person singular present active indicative verb. It is conjugated from the root διακον- (diakon-), with the -ει (-ei) ending indicating the present tense and the active voice. 

Overall, διηκονει is a word that emphasizes the importance of service and ministry, particularly in a religious context.
