Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "διώκω" (diōkō), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". 

Grammar: "διωκεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "διώκω". It is conjugated from the root "διωκ-" and the present tense suffix "-εις". 

Description: The word "διωκεις" is often used in the context of chasing or pursuing someone or something, either literally or figuratively. It can also be used to describe a persistent or determined effort to achieve a goal or overcome an obstacle.
