Word: διαταγμα

Etymology:
The word διαταγμα (diatagma) comes from the Greek verb διατάσσω (diatasso), which means "to arrange" or "to order". It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb τάσσω (tasso), which means "to arrange" or "to put in order".

Grammar:
διαταγμα is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is διατάγματα (diatagmata). In English, it can be translated as "decree", "order", or "command".

Description:
διαταγμα refers to an official order or decree issued by a person in authority. In ancient Greece, it was often used to refer to orders issued by military commanders or rulers. In modern Greek, it can refer to any type of official order or decree issued by a government or other authority.
