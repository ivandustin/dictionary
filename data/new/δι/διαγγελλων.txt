The word διαγγελλων (diaggellon) is a Greek verb that means "to announce" or "to proclaim." It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across," and the verb ἀγγέλλω (angellō), which means "to tell" or "to report." 

In terms of grammar, διαγγελλων is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is often used in the New Testament to describe someone who is announcing or proclaiming something, such as the good news of Jesus Christ. 

Overall, διαγγελλων is a powerful word that conveys the idea of spreading an important message or news to others.
