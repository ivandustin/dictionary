Word: διδασκάλους

Etymology:
The word διδάσκαλος (didaskalos) comes from the Greek verb διδάσκω (didasko), which means "to teach." The suffix -ους (-ous) is added to form the plural form of the noun, which means "teachers."

Grammar:
διδασκάλους is a plural noun in the accusative case. In Greek, the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. So, διδασκάλους would be used to refer to a group of teachers who are the direct object of a verb.

Description:
διδασκάλους is a Greek word that means "teachers" or "instructors." It is often used in the New Testament to refer to those who teach the word of God.
