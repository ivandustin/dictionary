Word: διαπερωσιν

Etymology:
The word διαπερωσιν is derived from the Greek verb διαπερνώ (diapernó), which means "to pierce through" or "to penetrate". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a nominalizing suffix that is used to form abstract nouns from verbs.

Grammar:
διαπερωσιν is a feminine noun in the accusative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
διαπερωσιν refers to the act of piercing through or penetrating something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as piercing through a piece of fabric with a needle, or in a figurative sense, such as penetrating the meaning of a difficult text. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is able to see through deception or understand a complex situation.
