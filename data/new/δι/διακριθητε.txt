Word: διακριθητε

Etymology:
The word διακριθητε comes from the Greek verb διακρίνω (diakríno), which means "to distinguish" or "to separate". It is formed by combining the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb κρίνω (kríno), which means "to judge" or "to decide".

διακριθητε is the second person plural aorist passive imperative form of the verb διακρίνω (diakríno). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

διακριθητε is a command that means "be distinguished" or "be separated". It is often used in a figurative sense to encourage someone to stand out or be different from others.
