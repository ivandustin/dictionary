Word: διαστελλομενον

Etymology: διαστελλομενον is a present participle of the Greek verb διαστέλλω (diastéllō), which means "to separate, to distinguish, to set apart". The word is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through, across", and the verb στέλλω (stéllō), meaning "to set, to place".

Grammar: διαστελλομενον is a present participle in the middle or passive voice, masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενον (-menon) to the present stem διαστελλο- (diastello-).

Description: διαστελλομενον refers to something that is being separated or distinguished. It can also mean something that is being set apart or marked off from something else. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe a person or thing that stands out or is exceptional in some way.
