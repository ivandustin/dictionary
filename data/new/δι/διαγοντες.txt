Word: διαγοντες

Etymology: διαγοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb διαγω (diago), which means "to pass through" or "to cross over."

Grammar: διαγοντες is the present participle of the verb διαγω (diago), which means "crossing over" or "passing through." It is in the masculine plural form.

Description: διαγοντες can be translated as "those who are crossing over" or "those who are passing through." It can be used to describe people who are traveling from one place to another, or who are going through a difficult situation. In ancient Greek literature, it was often used to describe heroes who were on a journey or quest.
