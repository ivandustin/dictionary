Word: διεταξεν

Etymology: διεταξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τάσσω (tasso), which means "to arrange" or "to put in order." The prefix δι- (di-) adds the meaning of "through" or "thoroughly," so διεταξεν can be translated as "he thoroughly arranged" or "he put in complete order."

Grammar: διεταξεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb διατάσσω (diatasso), which means "to arrange thoroughly" or "to put in complete order." In this form, it refers to a single action that was completed in the past.

Description: διεταξεν is a verb that implies a deliberate and thorough action of arranging or putting things in order. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as military tactics, organizational management, or personal organization. In the New Testament, it is used to describe how Jesus arranged the disciples before feeding the multitude (Mark 6:39) and how God arranged the parts of the body in the church (1 Corinthians 12:18).
