The word διακονων (diakonōn) is a Greek word that means "servant" or "minister". It is derived from the verb διακονέω (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister". In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to those who serve in the church, such as deacons or ministers. 

In terms of grammar, διακονων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, with the plural form being διακονοι (diakonoi). 

Overall, διακονων is a word that emphasizes the importance of service and ministry, particularly in a religious context.
