Word: διατριβόντες

Etymology: διατριβόντες comes from the Greek verb διατρέχω (diatrécho), which means "to spend time, to pass one's life." The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently engaged in the action of spending time or passing their lives in a certain way.

Grammar: διατριβόντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is declined like a regular first-declension noun, with the stem διατριβοντ- (diatribont-). The word can function as an adjective, modifying a noun, or as a noun itself, referring to a group of people who are currently engaged in a certain activity.

Description: διατριβόντες refers to people who are currently spending their time or passing their lives in a certain way. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, depending on the activity in question. For example, it could refer to scholars who are currently engaged in research and writing (i.e. "dissertating"), or to monks who are currently living in a monastery and dedicating their lives to prayer and contemplation. The word carries a connotation of ongoing, continuous activity, rather than a one-time or occasional action.
