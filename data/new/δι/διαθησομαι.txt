Word: διαθησομαι

Etymology:
The word διαθησομαι comes from the Greek verb διατίθημι (diatithemi), which means "to place, to arrange, to dispose". It is a compound word, formed by the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set".

Grammar:
διαθησομαι is a future tense verb in the Greek language. It is a first-person singular form of the verb διατίθημι (diatithemi), which means "I will arrange" or "I will dispose". The verb belongs to the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
διαθησομαι is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of arranging or disposing something. It is often used in the context of making a will or testament, where the testator arranges the distribution of their property after their death.
