Word: διεκρινομεν

Etymology:
διεκρινομεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κρίνω (krinó), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix δι- (di-) adds the meaning of "separating" or "distinguishing". Therefore, διεκρινομεν means "we were distinguishing" or "we were making a distinction".

Grammar:
διεκρινομεν is a first-person plural verb in the imperfect tense. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject (we) is performing the action.

Description:
διεκρινομεν is a verb that describes the act of distinguishing or making a distinction. It implies that the subject is able to differentiate between two or more things, and is actively doing so. The use of the imperfect tense suggests that this action was ongoing or repeated in the past.
