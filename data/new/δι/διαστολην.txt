Word: διαστολην

Etymology: διαστολην comes from the Greek word διαστολή (diastolḗ), which means "distinction" or "difference". It is derived from the verb διαστέλλω (diastéllō), which means "to separate" or "to distinguish".

Grammar: διαστολην is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: διαστολην refers to the act of distinguishing or differentiating between two or more things. It can also refer to the difference or distinction itself. The word is often used in medical contexts to refer to the expansion or dilation of a blood vessel or other tubular structure in the body.
