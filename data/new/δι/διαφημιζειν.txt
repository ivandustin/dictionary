Word: διαφημίζειν

Etymology:
The word διαφημίζειν (diaphēmízein) comes from the Greek root words διά (diá), meaning "through" or "across," and φημίζω (phēmízō), meaning "to make known" or "to proclaim." Together, διαφημίζειν means "to advertise" or "to promote."

Grammar:
διαφημίζειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and mood. The present active infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb in a general sense, without specifying who is performing the action or when it is taking place.

Description:
διαφημίζειν is a common Greek verb that means "to advertise" or "to promote." It is often used in the context of marketing and business, but can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any kind of promotion or publicity. The verb can be conjugated in different tenses, voices, and moods to express different nuances of meaning.
