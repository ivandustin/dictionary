Word: διηγουμενον

Etymology:
The word διηγουμενον (diēgoumenon) is a present participle of the Greek verb διηγέομαι (diēgéomai), which means "to narrate" or "to tell a story". The word is derived from the prefix δι- (di-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb ἀγορεύω (agoreuō), which means "to speak in public".

Grammar:
διηγουμενον is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently narrating or telling a story. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντ- (-ont-) to the stem of the verb.

Description:
διηγουμενον is a word that describes someone or something that is currently narrating or telling a story. It is often used in Greek literature to introduce a character who is telling a story or to describe the act of storytelling itself. The word conveys a sense of immediacy and action, suggesting that the story being told is unfolding in real-time.
