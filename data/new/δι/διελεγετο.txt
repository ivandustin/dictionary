Word: διελεγετο

Etymology:
The word διελεγετο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διαλέγομαι (dialégomai), which means "to converse, to discuss, to debate." It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across," and the verb λέγω (légō), which means "to speak" or "to say."

Grammar:
διελεγετο is a third-person singular, imperfect, middle-passive indicative form of the verb διαλέγομαι (dialégomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past, while the middle-passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
διελεγετο is a verb that describes an ongoing or repeated conversation, discussion, or debate in the past. It implies that the subject was actively engaged in the conversation, but it does not provide any information about the content or the outcome of the discussion. The word can be used in various contexts, such as politics, philosophy, or everyday life, to describe a situation where people exchange ideas, opinions, or arguments.
