Word: διατίθεμαι

Etymology:
The word διατίθεμαι comes from the Greek verb διατίθημι (diatithemi), which means "to place, to arrange, to dispose". It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put".

Grammar:
διατίθεμαι is a reflexive verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ διατίθεμαι (ego diatithemai) - I place myself, I arrange myself
- Σύ διατίθεσαι (su diatithesai) - You place yourself, You arrange yourself
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό διατίθεται (autos/afti/afto diatithetai) - He/She/It places himself/herself/itself

Plural:
- Εμείς διατιθόμαστε (emeis diatithomaste) - We place ourselves, We arrange ourselves
- Εσείς διατίθεσθε (eseis diatithesthe) - You place yourselves, You arrange yourselves
- Αυτοί/Αυτές διατίθενται (autoi/aftes diatithentai) - They place themselves, They arrange themselves

Description:
διατίθεμαι is a Greek verb that means "to dispose oneself", "to offer oneself", or "to make oneself available". It is often used in the context of making oneself available for a particular task or purpose. For example, one might say "Διατίθεμαι για βοήθεια" (Diatithemai gia voithia) which means "I am available to help". The verb can also be used in a more general sense, to indicate a willingness to do something or to be of service.
