The word "διαμεινηι" (diamenei) is a Greek verb that means "to remain" or "to stay". It is derived from the prefix "δια-" (dia-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to abide". In terms of grammar, "διαμεινηι" is a present active infinitive, meaning it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of remaining or staying in an ongoing or continuous sense.
