Word: διετριβεν

Etymology:
διετριβεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: διά (diá), which means "through" or "across," and τρίβω (trívo), which means "to rub" or "to wear away." Therefore, διετριβεν can be translated as "to spend time" or "to pass through."

Grammar:
διετριβεν is a third-person singular verb in the past tense of the Greek language. It belongs to the indicative mood and the active voice. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -εν (-en) to the stem διετριβ- (dietrib-).

Description:
διετριβεν is a verb that describes an action that took place in the past. It indicates that someone spent time doing something or passed through a particular period. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's daily routine, a period of study or work, or a particular experience.
