The word διακονιαν (diakonian) is a Greek noun that means "service" or "ministry". It is derived from the verb διακονεω (diakoneo), which means "to serve" or "to minister". In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to the service or ministry of the early Christian church, particularly in the context of caring for the poor and needy. The word is also used to describe the work of deacons, who were appointed to assist the apostles in their ministry. In terms of grammar, διακονιαν is a third declension noun in the accusative case.
