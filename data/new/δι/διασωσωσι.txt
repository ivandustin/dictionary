Word: διασωσωσι

Etymology: δια- (dia-) meaning "through" or "across" and σῴζω (sōzō) meaning "to save" or "to rescue". The suffix -σι (-si) indicates the third person plural subjunctive mood.

Grammar: διασωσωσι is a verb in the third person plural subjunctive mood, meaning "they might save" or "they may rescue". It is derived from the verb σῴζω (sōzō) in the aorist tense.

Description: διασωσωσι is a Greek verb that expresses the possibility of a group of people saving or rescuing someone or something. It is often used in contexts where there is uncertainty or doubt about whether the action will actually take place.
