Word: διαφέρει

Etymology: διαφέρει comes from the Greek verb διαφέρω (diapherō), which means "to differ" or "to be different". It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the verb φέρω (pherō), meaning "to carry" or "to bear".

Grammar: διαφέρει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is often used in the indicative mood to express a fact or a statement of truth. It can also be used in the subjunctive mood to express a possibility or a hypothetical situation.

Description: διαφέρει is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to describe differences between two or more things, such as differences in opinion, appearance, or behavior. It can also be used to describe changes or variations in a particular situation or circumstance. Overall, διαφέρει is a useful and commonly used verb in the Greek language.
