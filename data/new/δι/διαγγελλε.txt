The word διαγγελλε (diaggelle) is a Greek verb that means "to announce" or "to proclaim." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through" or "across," and the verb ἀγγέλλω (angello), which means "to tell" or "to report."

Grammar: διαγγελλε is a second person singular present imperative verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to announce or proclaim something.

Description: The word is often used in the context of making an official announcement or declaration, such as in a public speech or a legal document. It can also be used in a religious context, such as in the Bible, where it is used to describe the act of proclaiming the good news of Jesus Christ.
