The word "διακονουμενηι" (diakonoumenēi) is a Greek verb in the present passive participle form. It comes from the root word "διακονέω" (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister." 

In terms of grammar, the word is in the present tense, indicating that the action is currently happening. The passive voice suggests that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it. The participle form indicates that the word is functioning as an adjective, describing the subject of the sentence.

Overall, "διακονουμενηι" can be translated as "being served" or "being ministered to."
