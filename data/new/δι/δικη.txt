Word: δικη

Etymology:
The word δικη (dikē) comes from the Ancient Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, meaning "to show, to point out". In Ancient Greek, δικη originally meant "custom, usage, law", and later came to mean "justice, judgment, trial".

Grammar:
In Ancient Greek, δικη is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is δικη, and its genitive singular form is δικης. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
In Ancient Greek, δικη had a wide range of meanings related to law and justice. It could refer to a legal case or trial, a judgment or sentence, or the concept of justice itself. The word was also used in philosophical discussions about ethics and morality. In modern Greek, δικη still means "trial" or "lawsuit".
