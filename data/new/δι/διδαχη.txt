The word διδαχη (didache) is a Greek noun that means "teaching" or "instruction". It comes from the verb διδάσκω (didasko), which means "to teach". In terms of grammar, διδαχη is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive διδαχης (didaches) or the accusative διδαχην (didachen). 

In early Christianity, the term διδαχη was used to refer to the teachings of the apostles and the early church. The Didache, also known as The Teaching of the Twelve Apostles, is a Christian document from the first century that provides instructions on various aspects of Christian life and worship.
