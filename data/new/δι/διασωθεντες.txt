Word: διασωθέντες

Etymology:
The word διασωθέντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb διασώζω (diaso̱zo̱), which means "to save, rescue, or deliver." The suffix -τες (-tes) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, which means "having been saved or rescued."

Grammar:
διασωθέντες is the past participle of the verb διασώζω (diaso̱zo̱). It is in the masculine plural nominative case, which means it is used to refer to a group of people who have been saved or rescued.

Description:
διασωθέντες is a word that describes a group of people who have been saved or rescued from a dangerous or difficult situation. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a historical account of a battle or in a religious text to describe those who have been saved by divine intervention. The word carries a sense of relief and gratitude, as those who have been saved are no longer in danger and can continue with their lives.
