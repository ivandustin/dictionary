Word: διεχειρισασθε

Etymology: The word διεχειρισασθε is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix δι- (meaning "through" or "across"), the root word χείρ (meaning "hand"), and the verb ending -σασθε (indicating a second person plural aorist middle voice imperative form). Therefore, the word can be translated as "handle with your own hands" or "manage with your own hands".

Grammar: διεχειρισασθε is a second person plural aorist middle voice imperative form of the verb διαχειρίζομαι (diacheirizomai), which means "to handle with one's own hands" or "to manage with one's own hands". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: διεχειρισασθε is a command or instruction to a group of people to handle or manage something with their own hands. It implies a sense of personal responsibility and ownership over the task at hand. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a work setting where employees are instructed to handle a project themselves, or in a personal context where someone is encouraged to take control of their own situation.
