The word διψώ (dipsó) is a Greek verb that means "to thirst" or "to be thirsty". It comes from the ancient Greek word δίψα (dípsa), which also means "thirst". 

In terms of grammar, διψώ is a first-person singular present active indicative verb. It can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects and nuances of thirst.

Overall, διψώ is a common and important word in Greek, especially in the context of hot and dry climates where thirst is a frequent experience.
