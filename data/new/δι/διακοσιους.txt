Word: διακοσιους

Etymology:
The word διακοσιους (diakosious) is a Greek word that means "two hundred". It is derived from the combination of the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the cardinal number κόσιοι (kosioi), meaning "hundred".

Grammar:
In Greek, διακοσιους is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it would be διακόσιος (diakosios), if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it would be διακόσια (diakosia), and if it modifies a neuter singular noun, it would be διακόσιο (diakosio). In the plural form, it would be διακόσιοι (diakosioi) for masculine and mixed gender nouns, διακόσιες (diakosies) for feminine nouns, and διακόσια (diakosia) for neuter nouns.

Description:
The word διακοσιους is used to describe a quantity of two hundred of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in counting money, measuring distance, or describing a group of people. For example, διακόσιοι ευρώ (diakosioi evro) means "two hundred euros", διακόσια μέτρα (diakosia metra) means "two hundred meters", and διακόσιοι άνθρωποι (diakosioi anthropoi) means "two hundred people".
