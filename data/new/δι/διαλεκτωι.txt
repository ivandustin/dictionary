The word "διαλεκτωι" (dialektoi) is a Greek noun that refers to dialects or regional variations of a language. It comes from the Greek word "διάλεκτος" (dialektos), which means speech or language. In terms of grammar, it is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It can be used to describe the different dialects spoken in Greece, such as the Attic dialect or the Doric dialect.
