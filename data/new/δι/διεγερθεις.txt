Word: διεγερθεις

Etymology:
διεγερθεις is a Greek word that is derived from the verb διεγείρω (di-e-gei-ro), which means "to arouse" or "to wake up". The word is formed by adding the prefix δι- (di-), which means "through" or "thoroughly", to the verb εγείρω (e-gei-ro), which means "to wake up" or "to rouse".

Grammar:
διεγερθεις is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The participle mood is used to describe a verb as an adjective.

Description:
διεγερθεις can be translated to mean "having been aroused" or "having been awakened". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus' actions of waking people up from sleep or death. The word can also be used in a metaphorical sense to describe being awakened or stirred up emotionally or spiritually.
