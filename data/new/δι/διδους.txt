Word: διδους

Etymology:
διδους is the present participle of the Greek verb διδωμι (didōmi), which means "to give". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-, which means "to give".

Grammar:
διδους is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb διδωμι (didōmi), which is an irregular verb in Greek.

Description:
διδους is used to describe someone who is currently giving or in the act of giving. It can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is given or being given. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the actions of gods or heroes who give gifts or blessings to mortals.
