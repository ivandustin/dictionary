Word: διηπορουν

Etymology:
διηπορουν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: δια (dia) meaning "through" or "across" and απορεω (aporeo) meaning "to be at a loss" or "to be in doubt". Therefore, διηπορουν means "to be perplexed" or "to be in a state of uncertainty".

Grammar:
διηπορουν is a third-person plural verb in the present tense of the indicative mood. It is conjugated from the verb διαπορεω (diaporeo), which is a compound of δια (dia) and απορεω (aporeo). The verb is in the active voice and indicative mood, indicating that the subject is performing the action of being perplexed.

διηπορουν is a verb that describes a state of confusion or uncertainty. It is often used to describe a situation where someone is unsure of what to do or how to proceed.
