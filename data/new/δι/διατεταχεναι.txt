Word: διατεταγμένος (diatetagmenos)

Etymology: δια- (dia-) meaning "through" or "across" and τάσσω (tasso) meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order". The word διατεταγμένος (diatetagmenos) is the perfect participle passive form of διατάσσω (diatasso) which means "to be arranged" or "to be put in order".

Grammar: διατεταγμένος (diatetagmenos) is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: διατεταγμένος (diatetagmenos) describes something that has been arranged or put in order. It can also be used to describe someone who is disciplined or well-organized.
