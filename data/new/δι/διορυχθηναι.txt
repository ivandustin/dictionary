Word: διορυχθηναι

Etymology:
The word διορυχθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διορύσσω (dioryssō), which means "to dig through" or "to excavate." The suffix -θηναι (-thenai) is a passive aorist form, indicating that the action of digging through has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
διορυχθηναι is a passive aorist form of the verb διορύσσω (dioryssō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is the recipient of the action, rather than the doer of the action.

Description:
διορυχθηναι means "to have been dug through" or "to have been excavated." It is often used in reference to digging tunnels or canals, or excavating archaeological sites. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of uncovering or revealing hidden information or secrets.
