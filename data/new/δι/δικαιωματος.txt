The word δικαιωματος (dikaiōmatos) is a Greek noun that means "right" or "entitlement". It is derived from the verb δικαιόω (dikaióō), which means "to justify" or "to declare righteous". In terms of grammar, δικαιωματος is a genitive singular noun, indicating possession or association with another noun. 

In ancient Greek society, δικαιωματος referred to legal rights and privileges granted to citizens, such as the right to vote or hold public office. In modern usage, it can also refer to moral or ethical rights, such as the right to free speech or the right to a fair trial. 

Overall, δικαιωματος is a word that conveys the idea of entitlement or justifiable claim to something, whether it be legal, moral, or otherwise.
