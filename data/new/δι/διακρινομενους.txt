Word: διακρινομένους

Etymology:
The word διακρινομένους comes from the Greek verb διακρίνω (diakrino), which means "to distinguish" or "to separate". The suffix -μένους (-menous) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to something that is currently being distinguished or separated.

Grammar:
διακρινομένους is a masculine plural accusative form of the present participle of the verb διακρίνω. In Greek, present participles are often used to describe ongoing actions or states. The accusative case indicates that the word is the direct object of a verb or preposition.

Description:
διακρινομένους can be translated as "distinguishing" or "separating". As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action or state of something that is being distinguished or separated. In context, it could refer to people who are being distinguished or separated based on some characteristic or quality.
