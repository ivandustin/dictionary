The word διηπορει (diēporei) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel." The prefix δι- (di-) adds the meaning of "through, across, thoroughly."

In terms of grammar, διηπορει is a present indicative active verb, which means that it describes an action that is currently happening in the third person singular. It can be translated to "he/she/it is thoroughly perplexed" or "he/she/it is at a loss."

Overall, διηπορει is a word that describes a state of confusion or uncertainty. It is often used in philosophical or intellectual contexts to describe a person who is struggling to understand a complex idea or problem.
