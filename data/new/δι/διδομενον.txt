Word: διδομενον

Etymology:
διδομενον is the present participle of the Greek verb δίδωμι (didōmi), which means "to give". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-, which means "to give".

Grammar:
διδομενον is a present participle in the Greek language. Present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντας (-ontas) to the stem of the verb. They function as adjectives and describe an ongoing action. In this case, διδομενον describes something that is currently being given.

Description:
διδομενον is a Greek word that describes an ongoing action of giving. It can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as a gift being given, a task being assigned, or information being shared. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples.
