The word "δικαιοι" (dikaiōi) is a plural form of the Greek adjective "δίκαιος" (dikaios), which means "just" or "righteous". It is derived from the noun "δίκη" (dikē), which means "justice" or "righteousness". In terms of grammar, "δικαιοι" is the nominative plural form of the adjective, used to describe multiple people or things as just or righteous.
