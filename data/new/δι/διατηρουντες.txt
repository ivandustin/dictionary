Word: διατηρουντες

Etymology: διατηρουντες is a present participle form of the Greek verb διατηρέω (diatēréō), which means "to maintain, preserve, keep, or retain." The word is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through," and the verb τηρέω (tēréō), meaning "to guard or keep."

Grammar: διατηρουντες is a present active participle in the masculine gender, nominative case, and plural number. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently maintaining or preserving something.

Description: διατηρουντες can be translated as "those who are maintaining" or "those who are preserving." It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe individuals or groups who are keeping the teachings of Jesus Christ or the commandments of God. The word emphasizes the ongoing and active nature of the action being described.
