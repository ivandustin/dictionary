Word: διηνεκες

Etymology:
The word διηνεκες (diēnekes) is derived from the Greek words διά (diá) meaning "through" and ἀεί (aeí) meaning "always". The combination of these two words gives the meaning of "continuously" or "perpetually".

Grammar:
διηνεκες is an adjective in the masculine gender, nominative case, and singular number. It is often used to describe something that is continuous or unceasing.

Description:
The word διηνεκες is used in Greek literature to describe something that is always or continuously present. It can be used to describe a person's character or behavior, as well as a physical object or natural phenomenon. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe something that is persistent or unrelenting.
