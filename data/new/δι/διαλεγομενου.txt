Word: διαλεγομενου

Etymology:
δια- (dia-) - prefix meaning "through" or "across"
λέγω (lego) - verb meaning "to speak" or "to say"
-μενος (-menos) - suffix indicating a present participle

Therefore, διαλεγομενου (dialegomenou) is a present participle form of the verb διαλέγομαι (dialegomai), which means "to converse" or "to discuss".

Grammar:
διαλεγομενου is a present participle in the genitive case, masculine gender, and singular number. It is used to modify a noun or pronoun and functions as an adjective in a sentence.

Description:
διαλεγομενου can be translated as "of the one who is conversing" or "of the one who is discussing". It is often used in Greek literature to describe a person who is engaged in a conversation or debate.
