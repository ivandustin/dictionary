The word διακονιων (diakoniōn) is a Greek noun that means "service" or "ministry." It is derived from the verb διακονέω (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister." In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to the service or ministry of the early Christian church, particularly in the context of caring for the poor and needy. The word is also used to describe the role of deacons in the church, who are responsible for serving and ministering to the needs of the congregation. In terms of grammar, διακονιων is a masculine noun in the nominative case.
