Word: διωγμούς

Etymology:
The word διωγμούς (diogmous) is derived from the Greek verb διώκω (dióko), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". The suffix -μούς (-mous) is added to the verb stem to form the accusative plural form of the noun, which means "persecutions" or "pursuits".

Grammar:
διωγμούς is a masculine noun in the accusative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In this case, διωγμούς is the direct object of a verb that refers to persecutions or pursuits.

Description:
διωγμούς refers to persecutions or pursuits, usually in a negative sense. It can be used to describe the act of chasing or pursuing someone or something with the intention of causing harm or damage.
