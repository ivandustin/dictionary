The word διχοστασίας (dichostasias) is a Greek noun that means "division" or "schism". It is derived from the Greek words δίχα (dicha), meaning "in two" or "apart", and στάσις (stasis), meaning "standing" or "position". In grammar, it is a feminine noun in the genitive case. The word is often used in political or religious contexts to describe a split or disagreement within a group or organization.
