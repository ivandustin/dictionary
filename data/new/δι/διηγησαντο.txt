Word: διηγησαντο

Etymology:
διηγησαντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διηγέομαι (diēgéomai), which means "to narrate" or "to tell a story". The suffix -σαντο (-santo) indicates that the verb is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
διηγησαντο is a third-person plural aorist indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
διηγησαντο can be translated to English as "they narrated" or "they told a story". It is a verb that describes the act of recounting a story or narrative. The word is often used in the context of oral storytelling or recounting events from the past.
