Word: διισχυριζετο

Etymology: The word διισχυριζετο comes from the Greek verb διισχυρίζομαι (diischyrízomai), which means "to affirm with an oath" or "to make a solemn declaration". The prefix δι- (di-) means "through" or "across", while the root word ἰσχυρίζομαι (ischyrízomai) means "to make strong" or "to strengthen".

Grammar: διισχυριζετο is a third-person singular, middle voice, imperfect tense form of the verb διισχυρίζομαι (diischyrízomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and person. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, and the imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description: διισχυριζετο can be translated as "he/she/it was affirming with an oath" or "he/she/it was making a solemn declaration". The word implies a strong and serious affirmation, often made in a formal or legal context. It suggests that the speaker is willing to back up their words with a pledge or promise, and that they consider their statement to be of great importance.
