Word: διαγνωσιν

Etymology:
The word διαγνωσιν (diagnosis) comes from the Greek verb διαγιγνώσκω (diagnosko), which means "to distinguish, to discern, to recognize." The word is composed of two parts: δια (dia), which means "through," and γιγνώσκω (ginosko), which means "to know."

Grammar:
διαγνωσιν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
διαγνωσιν is a Greek word that means "diagnosis" in English. It refers to the process of identifying a disease or medical condition based on its symptoms and other diagnostic tests. The word is commonly used in the medical field to describe the process of diagnosing a patient's illness.
