The word "διψώντα" (dipsonta) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "διψάω" (dipsao), which means "to thirst". The word can be translated as "thirsting" or "being thirsty". It is often used in the New Testament to describe a person who is physically or spiritually thirsty for something, such as water or righteousness. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a strong desire or longing for something.
