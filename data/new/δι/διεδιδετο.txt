Word: διεδιδετο

Etymology:
The word διεδιδετο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διδομαι (didomai), which means "to give." The prefix δι- (di-) in this word indicates a repetition or distribution of the action of giving.

Grammar:
διεδιδετο is a third-person singular, imperfect, middle-passive indicative form of the verb διδομαι (didomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past, while the middle-passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
διεδιδετο can be translated as "he/she/it was distributing/sharing/giving out." The middle-passive voice suggests that the subject was both giving and receiving something, or that the action was being done to or for the subject. The imperfect tense indicates that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past. 

Overall, διεδιδετο is a complex verb that conveys the idea of repeated or distributed giving, with the subject being both the giver and the receiver of the action.
