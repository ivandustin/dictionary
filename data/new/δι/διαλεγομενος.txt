Word: διαλεγομενος

Etymology:
διαλεγομενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb διαλέγομαι (dialégomai), which means "to converse, to discuss, to argue". The word is formed by combining the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through, across", and the verb λέγω (légō), which means "to speak, to say".

Grammar:
διαλεγομενος is the present participle of the verb διαλέγομαι (dialégomai), which means "conversing, discussing, arguing". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
διαλεγομενος describes someone who is currently engaged in a conversation, discussion, or argument. It implies an active and ongoing exchange of ideas and opinions. The word can be used to describe both friendly and hostile conversations, depending on the context.
