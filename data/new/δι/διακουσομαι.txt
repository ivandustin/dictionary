Word: διακουσομαι

Etymology:
The word διακουσομαι comes from the Greek root words δια (dia) meaning "through" or "across" and ακουω (akouo) meaning "to hear". Therefore, the word διακουσομαι means "to hear through" or "to listen attentively".

Grammar:
διακουσομαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ διακούομαι (I hear through)
- Συ διακούεσαι (You hear through)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό διακούεται (He/She/It hears through)

Plural:
- Εμείς διακούομαστε (We hear through)
- Εσείς διακούεστε (You all hear through)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά διακούονται (They hear through)

Description:
The word διακουσομαι refers to the act of listening attentively or hearing through something. It implies a sense of focus and concentration, as opposed to simply hearing something passively. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to understanding or comprehending something deeply.
