Word: διεξοδους

Etymology: διεξοδους is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: διά (dia) meaning "through" and έξοδος (exodos) meaning "exit". Therefore, διεξοδους means "through exit" or "way out".

Grammar: διεξοδους is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form of διεξοδους indicates that there are multiple ways out or exits.

Description: διεξοδους refers to the ways out or exits of a place, such as a building, a city, or a country. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a solution or a way out of a difficult situation. In ancient Greece, διεξοδους was also used to refer to a narrow passage between two mountains or cliffs.
