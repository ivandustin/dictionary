Word: διαγινωσκειν

Etymology: The word διαγινωσκειν comes from the Greek roots δια- (dia-, meaning "through") and γινωσκειν (ginoskein, meaning "to know"). Therefore, the word means "to know thoroughly" or "to discern".

Grammar: διαγινωσκειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It is derived from the ancient Greek language and is used in the New Testament of the Bible.

Description: διαγινωσκειν is a word that describes the act of knowing or discerning something thoroughly. It implies a deep understanding of a subject or situation, and the ability to make accurate judgments based on that understanding. In the New Testament, the word is often used in the context of spiritual discernment, referring to the ability to distinguish between good and evil, truth and falsehood, and right and wrong.
