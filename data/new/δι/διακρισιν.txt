Word: διακρισιν

Etymology: διακρισιν comes from the Greek verb διακρίνω (diakrino), which means "to distinguish" or "to separate". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the action or result of the verb.

Grammar: διακρισιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: διακρισιν refers to the act of distinguishing or separating one thing from another. It can also refer to the result of this action, such as a distinction or difference between two things. In philosophy, διακρισιν is often used to describe the process of making a distinction between two concepts or ideas.
