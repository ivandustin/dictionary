The word διελθοντα (dielthonta) is a Greek verb in the aorist participle form. It comes from the verb διέρχομαι (dierchomai), which means "to pass through" or "to go through." The aorist tense indicates that the action of passing through or going through has been completed in the past.

In terms of grammar, the word is in the masculine, accusative, singular form. This means that it is referring to a singular masculine noun that is the object of the verb.

Overall, διελθοντα can be translated as "having passed through" or "having gone through." It is often used in the context of describing a journey or a process that has been completed.
