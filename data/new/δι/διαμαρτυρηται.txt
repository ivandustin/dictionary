Word: διαμαρτυρηται

Etymology: διαμαρτυρηται is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: δια (dia) meaning "through" or "across" and μαρτυρέω (martyreo) meaning "to testify" or "to bear witness". Therefore, διαμαρτυρηται means "to testify thoroughly" or "to bear witness completely".

Grammar: διαμαρτυρηται is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martyreo) and has the prefix δια (dia) added to it. The verb is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of the verb.
