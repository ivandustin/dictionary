Word: διψησουσιν

Etymology: διψησουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δίψος (dipsos), meaning "thirst." The verb form διψησουσιν (dipsēsousin) is in the future tense and means "they will thirst."

Grammar: διψησουσιν is a third-person plural future active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense ending -σουσιν (-sousin) to the stem διψη- (dipsē-).

Description: διψησουσιν is a verb that expresses the future action of thirsting or being thirsty. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 5:6, where Jesus says, "Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they will be filled." The verb emphasizes the strong desire or need for something, in this case, righteousness.
