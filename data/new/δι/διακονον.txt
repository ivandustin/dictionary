The word διακονον (diakonon) is a Greek noun that means "servant" or "minister". It is derived from the verb διακονεῖν (diakonein), which means "to serve" or "to minister". In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to those who serve in the church, such as deacons or ministers. 

In terms of grammar, διακονον is a masculine noun in the accusative case. It can also be spelled διάκονον with an accent on the second syllable. 

Overall, διακονον is a word that emphasizes the importance of service and ministry, particularly in a religious context.
