Word: διαδος

Etymology:
The word διαδος comes from the Greek word διάδοσις (diádosis), which means "a passing on" or "a transmission". It is derived from the verb διαδίδωμι (diadídōmi), which means "to give or pass on to another".

Grammar:
διαδος is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative singular form, it is spelled as διαδος.

Description:
διαδος refers to a succession or a series of things that are passed on from one person or generation to another. It can also refer to a tradition or a custom that is handed down from one generation to another. The word is often used in the context of cultural or religious practices that are passed on from one generation to another.
