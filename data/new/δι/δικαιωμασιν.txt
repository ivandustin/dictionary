The word δικαιωμασιν (dikaiōmasin) is a plural noun in Greek that means "rights" or "justifications." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root word δικαιόω (dikaióō), which means "to justify" or "to declare righteous." The suffix -μασιν (-masin) is a plural ending that indicates the word refers to multiple instances of the root concept.

Grammar: δικαιωμασιν is a third declension noun in Greek, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. In the nominative case (used for the subject of a sentence), the word is spelled δικαιώματα (dikaiómata). 

Description: The word δικαιωμασιν is often used in legal or political contexts to refer to the rights or entitlements that individuals or groups have under the law. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any justifications or reasons for a particular action or decision.
