The word "διψαι" (dipsai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the second person singular, present tense, indicative mood of the verb "διψάω" (dipsao), which means "to thirst" or "to be thirsty". 

Etymology: The word "διψάω" comes from the ancient Greek word "δίψα" (dipsa), which means "thirst". 

Grammar: "Διψαι" is conjugated in the present tense, indicative mood, and second person singular. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person. 

Description: "Διψαι" is used when addressing someone directly and asking if they are thirsty. For example, "Διψάς;" (Dipsas?) means "Are you thirsty?"
