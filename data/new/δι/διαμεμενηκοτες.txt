Word: διαμεμενηκότες

Etymology:
The word διαμεμενηκότες is a Greek word that is derived from the verb διαμένω (diaméno), which means "to remain" or "to abide". The suffix -κότες (-kótes) is a plural participle ending, which means "those who have remained" or "those who have abided".

Grammar:
διαμεμενηκότες is a plural participle in the perfect tense. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past but has ongoing effects in the present. The participle form is used to describe the subject of the sentence.

Description:
διαμεμενηκότες can be translated to mean "those who have remained" or "those who have abided". It is often used in a religious context to describe those who have remained faithful to their beliefs or have abided by the laws and teachings of their religion. It can also be used more generally to describe those who have stayed in a particular place or situation for a long period of time.
