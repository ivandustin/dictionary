Word: διδάσκεις

Etymology:
The word διδάσκεις (didáskeis) is a present tense verb in Greek, which means "you teach" in English. It is derived from the root word διδάσκω (didásko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The word has its origins in the ancient Greek language and has been used in various forms throughout history.

διδάσκεις is a second-person singular present active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word διδάσκω (didásko), which means "to teach". The verb is used to indicate that the subject (you) is currently teaching or instructing someone or something.

Description:
διδάσκεις is a verb that is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of teaching or instructing. It is often used in educational settings, such as schools or universities, but can also be used in other contexts, such as coaching or mentoring.
