Word: διθαλασσον

Etymology:
The word διθαλασσον is derived from the Greek words δι- (di-), meaning "two," and θάλασσα (thalassa), meaning "sea." Therefore, the literal meaning of διθαλασσον is "two seas."

Grammar:
διθαλασσον is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative singular form. The genitive singular form is διθαλασσου.

Description:
διθαλασσον is not a commonly used word in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek texts and literature. In ancient Greek mythology, it was used to describe a place where two seas met. It is also used metaphorically to describe a place where two different cultures or ideas come together.
