The word διεστειλατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word στέλλω (stello), which means "to send" or "to order." The prefix δι- (di-) adds the meaning of "apart" or "thoroughly," so διεστειλατο can be translated as "he thoroughly ordered" or "he commanded completely."

διεστειλατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon themselves or is affected by the action. Description:
διεστειλατο is a strong verb that conveys a sense of authority and control.
