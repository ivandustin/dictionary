Word: διεστελλετο

Etymology:
διεστελλετο is a Greek word that comes from the verb διαστέλλω (diastellō), which means "to separate, to distinguish, to arrange." The word is composed of the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through, across," and the verb στέλλω (stellō), which means "to arrange, to set in order."

Grammar:
διεστελλετο is a third-person singular, middle voice, imperfect tense form of the verb διαστέλλω (diastellō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and person. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description:
διεστελλετο can be translated as "he/she/it was arranging, was separating, was distinguishing." The imperfect tense indicates that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past. The middle voice suggests that the subject was arranging or separating something for its own benefit or interest.
