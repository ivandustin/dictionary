The word "δικαια" (dikaiā) is a feminine noun in Greek that means "justice" or "righteousness". It is derived from the adjective "δίκαιος" (díkaios), which means "just" or "righteous". In terms of grammar, it is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as a subject, object, or possessive noun. In Greek mythology, the goddess Dike was the personification of justice and was often depicted holding scales.
