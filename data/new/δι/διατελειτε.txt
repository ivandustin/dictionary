Word: διατελείτε

Etymology:
The word διατελείτε (diatelite) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: διά (dia) meaning "through" or "across" and τελείτε (teleite) meaning "you complete" or "you accomplish". Therefore, the word διατελείτε can be translated as "you continue to accomplish" or "you persist in completing".

Grammar:
διατελείτε is a second-person plural present indicative active verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the prefix διά to the present tense of the verb τελειώνω (teleiōnō), which means "to complete" or "to finish". The verb διατελείτε is used to describe a continuous action or state of completing or accomplishing something.

Description:
The word διατελείτε is often used in a positive sense to describe someone who is persistent and dedicated in completing a task or achieving a goal. It can also be used in a negative sense to describe someone who is stubborn or unyielding in their actions or beliefs. In both cases, the word emphasizes the idea of persistence and continuity in completing or accomplishing something.
