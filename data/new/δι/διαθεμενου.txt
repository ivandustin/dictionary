Word: διαθεμένου

Etymology:
The word διαθεμένου comes from the Greek verb διατίθημι (diatithemi), which means "to place, to arrange, to dispose". The word is formed from the prefix δια- (dia-), which means "through, across", and the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place, to put".

Grammar:
διαθεμένου is the genitive singular form of the participle διατιθέμενος (diatithemenos), which means "being disposed, being arranged, being placed". The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and in this case, it is used to show that the participle is related to a noun in the genitive case.

Description:
διαθεμένου is a word that describes something that is being disposed, arranged, or placed. It is often used in a passive sense, indicating that something is being done to the subject of the sentence. For example, "το βιβλίο διαθεμένου στο τραπέζι" (to vivlio diathemenou sto trapezi) means "the book being placed on the table". The word can also be used in a more active sense, indicating that the subject is doing the disposing or arranging. For example, "ο διαθεμένος των ανθρώπων στον κόσμο" (o diathemenos ton anthropon ston kosmo) means "the disposition of people in the world".
