The word διοπετους (diopetous) is a Greek adjective that means "having two wings". It is derived from the Greek words δις (dis), meaning "twice" or "two", and πτερόν (pteron), meaning "wing". In grammar, it is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is often used in Greek mythology to describe various winged creatures, such as Pegasus, the winged horse.
