Word: δικαιωσαι

The word δικαιωσαι is derived from the Greek verb δικαιόω (dikaioó), which means "to justify" or "to declare righteous". It is formed from the root word δίκη (díkē), which means "justice" or "righteousness".

δικαιωσαι is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated from the root word δικαιόω (dikaioó) and the suffix -σαι (-sai), which is the second person singular ending for the present tense.

Description:
δικαιωσαι is a Greek verb that means "you justify" or "you declare righteous". It is often used in a legal or religious context to describe the act of declaring someone innocent or righteous.
