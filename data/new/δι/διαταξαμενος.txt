Word: διαταξαμένος

Etymology: διατάσσω (diatássō) + -μένος (-ménos), from the verb διατάσσω meaning "to arrange, to put in order" and the suffix -μένος indicating the passive voice and the state of being.

Grammar: διαταξαμένος is a participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb διατάσσω in the perfect tense, middle voice, and participle form.

Description: διαταξαμένος describes someone who has been arranged or put in order, or someone who has arranged or put something in order. It can also mean someone who has been appointed or assigned a certain task or position. In English, it can be translated as "arranged," "ordered," "appointed," or "assigned."
