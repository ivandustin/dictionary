Word: διερρηξεν

Etymology: διερρηξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix δι- (meaning "through") and the verb ῥήγνυμι (meaning "to break, to tear apart"). Therefore, διερρηξεν means "to tear apart completely, to rend asunder".

Grammar: διερρηξεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is composed of the prefix δι- and the verb ῥήγνυμι, both in their aorist forms. The aorist tense in Ancient Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: διερρηξεν is a strong verb that conveys a sense of force and violence. It is often used in the context of physical tearing or breaking, but can also be used metaphorically to describe emotional or social ruptures.
