Word: διδακτικόν

Etymology:
The word διδακτικόν comes from the Greek word διδακτικός (didaktikos), which means "teaching" or "instructive". The suffix -όν (-on) is a neuter noun ending in Greek.

Grammar:
διδακτικόν is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive διδακτικού and the dative διδακτικῷ.

Description:
διδακτικόν refers to something that is related to teaching or instruction. It can be used to describe a person, a method, or a material that is used for teaching. The word can also be used in a broader sense to refer to anything that is educational or informative.
