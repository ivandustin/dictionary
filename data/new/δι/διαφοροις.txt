Word: διαφοροις

Etymology: διαφοροις is a Greek word that comes from the root word διαφέρω (diapherō), which means "to differ" or "to be different."

Grammar: διαφοροις is a plural form of the Greek adjective διάφορος (diáphoros), which means "different" or "various." It is in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of a verb or a preposition.

Description: διαφοροις is used to describe a group of things or people that are different or diverse from each other. It can be used to indicate a variety of characteristics, such as color, size, shape, or quality. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the different types of people or animals that exist in the world.
