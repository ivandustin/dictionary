Word: διαπονηθείς

Etymology:
The word διαπονηθείς is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words: δια (dia) meaning "through" or "across" and πονέω (ponéo) meaning "to suffer" or "to toil". Therefore, the word διαπονηθείς can be translated to mean "to suffer through" or "to toil through".

Grammar:
διαπονηθείς is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The participle mood indicates that the verb is being used as an adjective.

Description:
διαπονηθείς is a word that describes the act of enduring or suffering through a difficult or challenging situation. It can be used to describe physical or emotional pain, as well as the effort required to accomplish a task. The word conveys a sense of perseverance and determination in the face of adversity.
