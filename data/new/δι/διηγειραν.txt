Word: διηγειραν

Etymology:
The word διηγειραν is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "εγείρω" (egeiró), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". The prefix "δι-" (di-) in this word means "thoroughly" or "completely". Therefore, διηγειραν can be translated as "to thoroughly wake up" or "to completely rise".

Grammar:
διηγειραν is a third-person plural aorist active indicative verb in the Greek language. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, and it is a completed action. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
διηγειραν is a strong and powerful verb that conveys the idea of waking up or rising completely. It can be used in a literal sense, such as waking up from sleep, or in a figurative sense, such as rising to a challenge or overcoming an obstacle. The word has a sense of urgency and intensity, suggesting that the action of waking up or rising is not a passive or gradual process, but rather a sudden and forceful one.
