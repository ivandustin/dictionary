Word: διδάσκων

Etymology:
The word διδάσκων comes from the Greek verb διδάσκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, which means "to show" or "to point out".

Grammar:
διδάσκων is the present participle of the verb διδάσκω (didasko), which means "teaching" or "instructing". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
διδάσκων is a word that describes someone who is currently teaching or instructing. It can refer to a teacher, instructor, or anyone who is imparting knowledge or skills to others. The word is often used in the context of education, but it can also be used more broadly to describe someone who is sharing their expertise or experience with others.
