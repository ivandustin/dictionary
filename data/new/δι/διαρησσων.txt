The word διαρησσων (diarēssōn) is a present participle form of the Greek verb διαρήσσω (diarēssō), which means "to tear apart" or "to rend asunder". It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the verb ῥήσσω (rhēssō), meaning "to tear" or "to break". 

In terms of grammar, διαρησσων is a masculine singular nominative present participle, indicating that the action of tearing apart is currently happening and being performed by a single male subject. 

Overall, διαρησσων describes someone or something that is actively tearing apart or rending asunder.
