διωκοντων is a present participle of the Greek verb διώκω (diókō), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". The word is derived from the combination of the prefix δι- (di-), which means "through" or "across", and the verb ὀκνέω (oknéō), which means "to hesitate" or "to be reluctant".

διωκοντων is a present participle in the genitive case, masculine gender, and plural number. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ων (-ōn) to the present stem of the verb. διωκοντων can be translated as "of those who are pursuing" or "of those who are chasing". It is used to describe a group of people who are actively pursuing or chasing something or someone.
