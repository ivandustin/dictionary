Word: δικαιας

Etymology:
The word δικαιας comes from the Greek word δίκαιος (dikaios), which means "just" or "righteous". It is derived from the root word δίκη (dikē), which means "justice" or "right".

Grammar:
δικαιας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of the word δίκαιος (dikaios).

Description:
δικαιας is a Greek word that means "of the just" or "of the righteous". It is often used in reference to God or to people who are considered to be just or righteous. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe the righteousness of God and the righteousness that comes through faith in Jesus Christ.
