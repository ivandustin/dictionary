Word: διαπαρατριβαι

Etymology:
The word διαπαρατριβαι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix δια- (meaning "through" or "across") and the noun παρατριβή (meaning "friction" or "rubbing against"). The word can be translated to mean "mutual friction" or "conflict".

Grammar:
διαπαρατριβαι is a plural noun in the nominative case in Greek. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe conflicts or disputes between individuals or groups.

The word διαπαρατριβαι is used to describe situations where there is friction or conflict between individuals or groups.
