Word: διαμεριζονται

Etymology: δια- (dia-, prefix meaning "through" or "across") + μερίζω (merizo, "to divide") + -ονται (-ontai, third person plural present middle/passive voice ending). The word comes from Ancient Greek.

Grammar: διαμεριζονται is a present tense, middle/passive voice verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb διαμερίζω (diamerizo), which means "to divide into parts" or "to distribute". The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on themselves or is being acted upon by the verb.

Description: διαμεριζονται is a Greek verb that describes the act of dividing or distributing something into parts. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as dividing up land, distributing resources, or separating groups of people. The present tense indicates that the action is currently happening or is a habitual action.
