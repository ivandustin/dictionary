Word: διερχόμενον

Etymology: διερχόμενον is a present participle of the verb διέρχομαι (diérchomai), which means "to pass through" or "to go through". It is derived from the prefix δι- (di-), meaning "through", and the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: διερχόμενον is a neuter singular form of the present participle, which is used to describe an ongoing action. In this case, it describes something that is currently passing through or going through something else.

Description: διερχόμενον can be translated as "passing through" or "going through". It is often used in a literal sense, such as describing a person or object passing through a physical space. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as describing a thought or emotion passing through someone's mind.
