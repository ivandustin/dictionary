Word: δις

Etymology:
δις (dis) is a Greek word that means "twice" or "two times". It is derived from the Greek word δύο (dyo), which means "two".

Grammar:
δις is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate that something has happened twice or that there are two of something.

Description:
δις is a common word in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. For example, it can be used to indicate that someone has been to a place twice, or that they have done something twice. It can also be used to indicate that there are two of something, such as two doors or two windows. In addition, δις is often used in mathematical contexts to indicate multiplication by two.
