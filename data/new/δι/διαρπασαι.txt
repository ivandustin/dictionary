Word: διαρπάσαι

Etymology:
διαρπάσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across," and the verb ἁρπάζω (harpazō), meaning "to snatch" or "to seize." Therefore, διαρπάσαι means "to snatch through" or "to seize across."

Grammar:
διαρπάσαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -σαι (-sai) to the stem διαρπα- (diarpa-). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the imperative mood expresses a command or request.

Description:
διαρπάσαι is a strong and forceful verb that conveys the idea of forcefully seizing or snatching something. It is often used in a military or violent context, such as seizing a city or capturing an enemy. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the violent persecution of Christians (Acts 8:3) and the snatching away of believers in the rapture (1 Thessalonians 4:17).
