Word: διελάλουν

Etymology:
διελάλουν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The prefix δι- (di-) in this word indicates a repeated or continuous action, so διελάλουν means "they were speaking" or "they were talking".

Grammar:
διελάλουν is a third-person plural imperfect indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
διελάλουν is used in the New Testament of the Bible in Luke 24:14, where it describes two disciples who were walking on the road to Emmaus and discussing the events surrounding Jesus' crucifixion and resurrection. The word conveys a sense of ongoing conversation and dialogue between the two disciples.
