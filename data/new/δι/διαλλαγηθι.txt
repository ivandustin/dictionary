Word: διαλλαγηθι

Etymology:
The word διαλλαγηθι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διαλλάσσω (diallasso), which means "to change, exchange, or reconcile." The suffix -θι (-thi) is a second-person singular imperative ending, which means that the word is a command to someone to do something.

Grammar:
διαλλαγηθι is a second-person singular imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is a command directed at one person to perform an action. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -θι to the stem διαλλαγη-, which is the aorist stem of the verb διαλλάσσω.

Description:
The word διαλλαγηθι is a command to someone to "be reconciled" or "make peace." It is a call to action, urging someone to change their behavior or attitude towards another person in order to resolve a conflict or disagreement. The word carries a sense of urgency and importance, emphasizing the need for reconciliation and harmony.
