Word: διαταγεις

Etymology: διαταγή (diatagé) is a Greek word meaning "order" or "command". The suffix -εις (-eis) is a plural ending, so διαταγεις (diatageis) means "orders" or "commands".

Grammar: διαταγεις is a plural noun in the nominative case in Greek. It can be translated to English as "orders" or "commands".

Description: διαταγεις refers to a set of orders or commands given by someone in authority. It can be used in a military context, where a commander gives orders to his troops, or in a civilian context, where a boss gives orders to his employees. The word implies a sense of authority and obedience.
