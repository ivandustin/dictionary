The word διεληλυθοτα (dielēlythota) is a Greek verb that is in the perfect tense and active voice. It is derived from the root word λυω (luō), which means "to loosen" or "to release." The prefix δι- (di-) adds the meaning of "completely" or "thoroughly."

In terms of grammar, the perfect tense in Greek indicates a completed action with ongoing results or effects. The active voice means that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Overall, διεληλυθοτα can be translated as "having completely passed through" or "having thoroughly gone through." It implies that the subject has gone through a process or experience and has come out on the other side.
