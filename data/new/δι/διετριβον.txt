Word: διετριβον

Etymology:
διετριβον is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix δι- (meaning "through" or "across") and the verb ετριβον (meaning "I spent time" or "I lived"). The prefix δι- emphasizes the duration or intensity of the action, so διετριβον means "I spent a long time" or "I lived for a while".

Grammar:
διετριβον is the first person singular form of the verb διατριβω (diatribō), which is in the aorist tense. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating its duration or repetition. Therefore, διετριβον means "I spent a long time" or "I lived for a while" in the past.
