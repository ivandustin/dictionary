The word "δικαιοκρισίας" (dikaiokrisías) is a Greek noun that combines the words "δίκαιος" (díkaios), meaning "just" or "righteous," and "κρίση" (krísi), meaning "judgment" or "decision." 

Etymology: The word can be broken down into "δίκαιος" and "κρίση," both of which have ancient Greek roots. "Δίκαιος" comes from the Greek word "δίκη" (díkē), meaning "justice," while "κρίση" comes from the verb "κρίνω" (krínō), meaning "to judge" or "to decide."

Grammar: "Δικαιοκρισίας" is a feminine noun in the genitive case, which means it is used to indicate possession or origin. It can be translated to mean "of justice" or "pertaining to justice."

Description: The word "δικαιοκρισίας" refers to the act of judging or making decisions based on what is just or right. It can be used in legal contexts to refer to the administration of justice, or in a more general sense to refer to any situation where a decision is made based on principles of fairness and righteousness.
