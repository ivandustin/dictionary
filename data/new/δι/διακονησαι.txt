Word: διακονησαι

Etymology:
The word διακονησαι comes from the Greek verb διακονέω (diakonéō), which means "to serve" or "to minister." It is derived from the noun διάκονος (diákonos), which means "servant" or "minister."

Grammar:
διακονησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb διακονέω.

Description:
διακονησαι is a Greek verb that means "to serve" or "to minister." It is often used in the New Testament to describe the work of deacons and other church leaders who serve the needs of the community. The word emphasizes the idea of humble service and selflessness, and is often associated with acts of kindness and charity.
