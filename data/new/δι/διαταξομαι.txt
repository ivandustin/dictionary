Word: διατάσσομαι (diatássomai)

Etymology:
The word διατάσσομαι (diatássomai) comes from the Greek root words διά (diá), meaning "through" or "across," and τάσσω (tássō), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order." The prefix διά (diá) emphasizes the idea of arranging or putting things in order in a thorough or comprehensive way.

Grammar:
διατάσσομαι (diatássomai) is a passive verb in the present tense. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ διατάσσομαι (Egó diatássomai) - I am arranged/ordered
- Εσύ διατάσσεσαι (Esý diatásseai) - You are arranged/ordered
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό διατάσσεται (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó diatássetai) - He/She/It is arranged/ordered

Plural:
- Εμείς διατάσσομαστε (Emeís diatássomaste) - We are arranged/ordered
- Εσείς διατάσσεστε (Eseís diatásseste) - You are arranged/ordered
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά διατάσσονται (Aftoí/Aftés/Aftá diatássontai) - They are arranged/ordered

Description:
διατάσσομαι (diatássomai) means "to be arranged" or "to be ordered." It can refer to physical objects being arranged in a particular order, or to people being given orders or instructions. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or set of circumstances that is arranged or ordered in a particular way.
