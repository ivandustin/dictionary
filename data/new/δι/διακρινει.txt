Word: διακρινει

Etymology:
The word διακρινει comes from the Greek verb διακρίνω (diakrino), which means "to distinguish" or "to separate". It is derived from the prefix δια- (dia-), meaning "through" or "across", and the verb κρίνω (krino), meaning "to judge" or "to decide".

Grammar:
διακρινει is the third person singular present tense form of the verb διακρίνω (diakrino) in Greek. It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description:
διακρινει is a verb that means "he/she/it distinguishes" or "he/she/it separates" in Greek. It is often used in contexts where a distinction or separation needs to be made between two or more things. For example, it can be used to describe the process of distinguishing between different colors, flavors, or sounds. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as when making a distinction between two ideas or concepts.
