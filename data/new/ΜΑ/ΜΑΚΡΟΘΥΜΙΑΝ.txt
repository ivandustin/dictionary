Etymology: The word "μακροθυμίαν" (makrothymían) comes from the Greek roots "μακρός" (makrós), meaning "long," and "θυμός" (thymós), meaning "temper" or "passion." It literally translates to "long temper" or "long-suffering."

Grammar: "Μακροθυμίαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension.

Description: "Μακροθυμίαν" refers to the quality or state of being patient, forbearing, or long-suffering. It is the ability to endure difficult or challenging situations without becoming easily angered or frustrated. It is often associated with a calm and composed demeanor in the face of adversity.
