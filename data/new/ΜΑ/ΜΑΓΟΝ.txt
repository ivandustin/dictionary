Etymology: The word "μαγον" (magos) comes from the Old Persian word "maguš" which means "sorcerer" or "magician". It was later adopted into Greek as "μάγος" (magos).

Grammar: In Greek, "μάγος" (magos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: In ancient Greek, "μάγος" (magos) referred to a person who practiced magic or sorcery. They were often associated with religious rituals and were believed to have supernatural powers. In later usage, the term "μάγος" (magos) also came to refer to wise men or scholars, particularly in the context of the biblical Magi who visited Jesus after his birth.
