Etymology: The word "μακελλώ" comes from the Greek verb "μακελλεύω" (makelleúo), which means "to butcher" or "to slaughter." It is derived from the noun "μάκελλον" (makellon), meaning "butcher's shop" or "slaughterhouse."

Grammar: "Μακελλώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ μακελλώ (ego makelló) - I butcher
- Εσύ μακελλεύεις (esý makelleúeis) - You butcher
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό μακελλεύει (aftós/aftí/aftó makelleúei) - He/She/It butchers
- Εμείς μακελλούμε (emeís makelloúme) - We butcher
- Εσείς μακελλεύετε (eseís makelleúete) - You (plural) butcher
- Αυτοί/Αυτές μακελλούν (aftoí/aftés makelloún) - They butcher

Description: "Μακελλώ" refers to the action of butchering or slaughtering animals, typically for the purpose of obtaining meat. It is commonly used in the context of the meat industry or culinary practices.
