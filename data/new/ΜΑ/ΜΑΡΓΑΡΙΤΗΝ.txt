Etymology: The word "μαργαριτην" (margaritēn) comes from the Greek word "μαργαρίτης" (margaritēs), which means "pearl". 

Grammar: "Μαργαριτην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. 

Description: "Μαργαριτην" refers to a pearl, which is a hard, lustrous spherical object produced within the soft tissue of a living shelled mollusk. Pearls are often used in jewelry and are valued for their beauty and rarity.
