μαρτυρούμενος (marturoumenos) is a Greek word that can be translated as "testifying" or "bearing witness." Here is the breakdown of the word:

- Etymology: The word μαρτυρούμενος (marturoumenos) comes from the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to testify" or "to bear witness." The suffix -ούμενος (-oumenos) is a present participle ending that indicates continuous action.

- Grammar: μαρτυρούμενος (marturoumenos) is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo) in the present tense.

- Description: The word describes someone who is currently testifying or bearing witness to something. It implies an ongoing action or state of being.
