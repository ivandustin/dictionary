Etymology: The word "μακροθυμών" comes from the Greek roots "μακρός" (makrós), meaning "long" or "patient," and "θυμός" (thymós), meaning "temper" or "anger." 

Grammar: "Μακροθυμών" is the masculine singular form of the adjective in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is patient or long-suffering.

Description: "Μακροθυμών" refers to a person who has the ability to endure difficult or frustrating situations without becoming easily angered or impatient. It describes someone who has a calm and patient temperament.
