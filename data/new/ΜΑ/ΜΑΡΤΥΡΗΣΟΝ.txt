Etymology: The word "μαρτυρησον" (marturēson) comes from the Greek verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureō), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify." It is derived from the noun "μάρτυς" (martys), meaning "witness."

Grammar: "Μαρτυρησον" is the imperative form of the verb "μαρτυρέω" in Greek. It is used to give a command or request someone to bear witness or testify.

Description: "Μαρτυρησον" is a Greek word that is used to instruct or urge someone to bear witness or testify. It is often used in legal or religious contexts, where someone is called upon to provide evidence or give testimony about a particular event or situation.
