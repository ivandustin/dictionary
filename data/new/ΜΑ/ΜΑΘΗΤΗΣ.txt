Etymology: The word "μαθητης" (mathētēs) comes from the Greek verb "μανθανω" (manthanō), which means "to learn" or "to study". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*men-" meaning "to think" or "to understand".

Grammar: In Greek, "μαθητης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "μαθητου" (mathētou). The plural form is "μαθηται" (mathētai).

Description: "Μαθητης" is the Greek word for "student" or "disciple". It refers to someone who is learning or studying under the guidance of a teacher or mentor. In ancient Greece, it was commonly used to describe the followers of famous philosophers or religious leaders. Today, it is still used to refer to students in various educational contexts.
