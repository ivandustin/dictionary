μακροθυμησας (makrothymēsas) is a Greek word that can be broken down into two parts: "μακρός" (makrós) meaning "long" or "patient" and "θυμός" (thymós) meaning "temper" or "anger". Therefore, the word μακροθυμησας can be translated as "long-tempered" or "patient". In terms of grammar, μακροθυμησας is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb μακροθυμέω (makrothyméō), which means "to be patient" or "to have patience".
