Etymology: The word "μαχαιραν" (makhairan) comes from the Greek word "μάχαιρα" (makhaira), which means "knife" or "dagger". 

Grammar: "Μαχαιραν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Μαχαιραν" refers to a knife or a dagger. It is a sharp-edged tool or weapon with a handle and a blade used for cutting or stabbing.
