Etymology: The word "μαργαριταί" comes from the Greek word "μαργαρίται" (margarítai), which means "pearls". 

Grammar: "μαργαριταί" is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative form of the word, used to refer to pearls.

Description: "μαργαριταί" refers to pearls, which are hard, lustrous spherical objects produced within the soft tissue of a living shelled mollusk. They are valued as gemstones and are often used in jewelry. Pearls have been treasured for their beauty and rarity throughout history and are associated with elegance and luxury.
