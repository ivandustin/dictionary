Etymology: The word "μακραν" (makran) comes from the Greek word "μακρός" (makrós), meaning "long" or "far". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *mak-.

Grammar: "Μακραν" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe something that is far away or distant in space or time. It can also be used to express a great difference or extent.

Description: "Μακραν" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used to emphasize the distance or difference between two things. For example, "Το σπίτι μου είναι μακράν από εδώ" (My house is far away from here) or "Είναι μακράν ο καλύτερος παίκτης" (He is by far the best player).
