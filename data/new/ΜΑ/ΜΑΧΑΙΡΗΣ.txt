Etymology: The word "μαχαιρης" (makhairēs) comes from the Greek word "μάχαιρα" (mákhaíra), meaning "knife". 

Grammar: In Greek, "μαχαιρης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "μαχαιρη" (makhairē). 

Description: "Μαχαιρης" refers to a person who uses or carries a knife. It can also be used to describe a knife-wielder or someone skilled in using a knife.
