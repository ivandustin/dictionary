Etymology: The word "μαρτυριαί" comes from the Greek word "μαρτυρία" (marturia), which means "testimony" or "witness."

Grammar: "Μαρτυριαί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple testimonies or witnesses.

Description: "Μαρτυριαί" refers to testimonies or witnesses. It can be used to describe the statements or evidence given by individuals who have witnessed an event or have knowledge about a particular situation. In a legal context, testimonies are often presented as evidence in court proceedings.
