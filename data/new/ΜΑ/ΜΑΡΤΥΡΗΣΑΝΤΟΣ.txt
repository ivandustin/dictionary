μαρτυρησαντος (marturēsantos) is a Greek word that is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martureō), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify." The word μαρτυρησαντος is in the genitive case and is the present active participle form of the verb. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. The word μαρτυρησαντος can be translated as "of the one testifying" or "of the one bearing witness."
