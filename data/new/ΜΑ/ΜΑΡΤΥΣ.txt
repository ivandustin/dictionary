Etymology: The word "μαρτυς" (martyrs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "μαρτυρέω" (martyreo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify."

Grammar: In Greek, "μαρτυς" is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "μάρτυρος" (marturos). It can be declined in various cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: The word "μαρτυς" refers to a martyr, someone who willingly suffers or dies for their beliefs or principles. In Christian context, it specifically refers to those who were persecuted or killed for their faith. The term can also be used more broadly to describe anyone who sacrifices their life or well-being for a cause they strongly believe in.
