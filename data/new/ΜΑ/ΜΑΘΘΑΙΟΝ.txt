Etymology: The word "μαθθαῖον" (maththaîon) comes from the Greek word "μανθάνω" (manthánō), which means "to learn" or "to understand." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*mendh-" meaning "to learn" or "to think."

Grammar: "Μαθθαῖον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension. It can be used in both the singular and plural forms.

Description: "Μαθθαῖον" refers to a place or institution where learning or teaching takes place. It can be translated as "school," "academy," or "learning center." In ancient Greece, it specifically referred to the philosophical schools of Plato and his followers.
