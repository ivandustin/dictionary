Etymology: The word "ματαια" comes from the Greek word "ματαιος" (mataios), meaning "vain" or "futile".

Grammar: "ματαια" is an adjective in the feminine form, used to describe something as vain, futile, or pointless. It can also be used as an adverb to modify verbs.

Description: "ματαια" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of something being in vain or without purpose. It is often used to describe actions, efforts, or pursuits that are futile or have no real value. It can also be used to express the concept of emptiness or meaninglessness.
