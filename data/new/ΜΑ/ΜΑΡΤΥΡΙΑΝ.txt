Etymology: The word "μαρτυριαν" (marturian) comes from the Greek word "μαρτυρία" (marturia), which means "testimony" or "witness."

Grammar: "Μαρτυριαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender in Greek grammar.

Description: "Μαρτυριαν" refers to the accusative form of the noun "μαρτυρία" (marturia), which means "testimony" or "witness" in Greek. It is used to describe the act of giving evidence or testimony in a legal or formal context.
