Etymology: The word "μαθητής" (mathitis) comes from the Greek verb "μανθάνω" (manthano), which means "to learn" or "to study". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*men-" meaning "to think" or "to understand".

Grammar: "Μαθητής" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its nominative singular form is "μαθητής". In the plural, it becomes "μαθητές" (mathites). It can also have different forms depending on the case, number, and gender.

Description: "Μαθητής" means "student" in Greek. It refers to a person who is studying or learning, typically in an educational institution. It can be used to describe students of any age, from primary school to university.
