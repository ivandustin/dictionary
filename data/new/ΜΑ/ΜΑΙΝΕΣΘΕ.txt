Etymology: The word "μαινεσθε" (mainesthe) comes from the Greek verb "μαίνομαι" (mainomai), which means "to be mad" or "to be insane". 

Grammar: "μαινεσθε" is the second person plural present middle/passive imperative form of the verb "μαίνομαι". In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "μαινεσθε" is a command in Greek, specifically addressing a group of people. It can be translated as "be mad" or "go mad". It is used to instruct or encourage others to act in a wild or frenzied manner.
