Etymology: The word "μακροθυμεῖ" comes from the Greek roots "μακρός" (makrós), meaning "long" or "patient," and "θυμός" (thymós), meaning "temper" or "anger." 

Grammar: "μακροθυμεῖ" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "μακροθυμέω" (makrothyméō), which means "to be patient" or "to have long-suffering."

Description: "μακροθυμεῖ" describes the action of being patient, enduring, or showing forbearance in difficult or challenging situations. It implies the ability to remain calm and composed in the face of provocation or delay.
