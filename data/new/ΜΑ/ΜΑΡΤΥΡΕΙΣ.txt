Etymology: The word "μαρτυρείς" (martureis) comes from the Greek verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". It is derived from the noun "μάρτυς" (martys), meaning "witness".

Grammar: "Μαρτυρείς" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureo) in Greek. It is used when addressing one person who is bearing witness or testifying.

Description: "Μαρτυρείς" is a Greek word that means "you bear witness" or "you testify". It is used to address someone directly and ask them to provide evidence or testimony about something.
