Etymology: The word "μαρτυρω" (marturo) comes from the Greek verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". 

Grammar: "μαρτυρω" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: "μαρτυρω" is used to express the action of bearing witness or testifying. It can be used in various contexts, such as legal proceedings, religious contexts, or simply to describe the act of giving evidence or supporting a claim.
