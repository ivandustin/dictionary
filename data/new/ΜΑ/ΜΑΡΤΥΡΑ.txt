Etymology: The word "μαρτυρα" comes from the Greek word "μάρτυρας" (martyras), which means "witness".

Grammar: "μαρτυρα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form.

Description: "μαρτυρα" refers to a witness, someone who has firsthand knowledge or evidence about an event or situation and can provide testimony or information about it. In legal contexts, a "μαρτυρα" is someone who testifies in court. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to someone who has observed or experienced something and can provide information or evidence about it.
