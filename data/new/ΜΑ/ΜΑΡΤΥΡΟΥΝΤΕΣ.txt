Etymology: The word "μαρτυρούντες" comes from the Greek verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify." The suffix "-ούντες" is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Μαρτυρούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "μαρτυρέω" in Greek. It is used to describe people or things that are currently bearing witness or testifying. In terms of grammar, it functions as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Description: "Μαρτυρούντες" refers to individuals or a group of people who are actively giving testimony or bearing witness to something. It can be used in various contexts, such as legal proceedings, religious settings, or even in everyday conversations to describe someone who is providing evidence or supporting a claim.
