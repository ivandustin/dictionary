Etymology: The word "μαθητού" comes from the Greek word "μαθητής" (mathitis), which means "student" or "disciple". It is derived from the verb "μανθάνω" (manthano), meaning "to learn" or "to study".

Grammar: "Μαθητού" is the genitive form of the noun "μαθητής" (mathitis). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. It is commonly used to express the relationship of "of" or "belonging to".

Description: "Μαθητού" refers to a male student or disciple. It is used to describe someone who is learning or studying a particular subject or following a teacher or mentor.
