Etymology: The word "μαρτυρησας" (marturēsas) comes from the Greek verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureō), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". It is derived from the noun "μάρτυς" (martys), meaning "witness".

Grammar: "Μαρτυρησας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureō) in Greek. It is used to describe someone who has testified or borne witness to something in the past.
