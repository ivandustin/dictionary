Etymology: The word "μαρτυριον" (marturion) comes from the Greek word "μαρτυρία" (marturia), which means "testimony" or "witness."

Grammar: In Greek, "μαρτυριον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "μαρτυρίου" (marturiou).

Description: "Μαρτυριον" is a Greek word that refers to a testimony, evidence, or proof given by a witness. It is often used in legal contexts to describe a statement or evidence provided by someone who has witnessed an event or has knowledge about it.
