Etymology: The word "ματαιότητος" (mataiotētos) comes from the Greek root "ματαιο-" (mataio-), meaning "vain" or "futile," and the suffix "-τητος" (-tētos), which denotes a state or quality. 

Grammar: "Ματαιότητος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or description.

Description: "Ματαιότητος" refers to the state or quality of being vain, futile, or pointless. It can describe actions, thoughts, or endeavors that lack purpose or significance. The word is often used to express the idea of emptiness, worthlessness, or the fleeting nature of worldly pursuits.
