Etymology: The word "μαργαριτών" (margaritōn) comes from the Greek word "μαργαρίτης" (margaritēs), which means "pearl". 

Grammar: "μαργαριτών" is a noun in the genitive case, singular number, masculine gender. It is the genitive form of the noun "μαργαριτής" (margaritēs).

Description: "μαργαριτών" refers to a pearl or pearls. It is used to describe the hard, lustrous spherical objects that are formed within the shells of certain mollusks, especially oysters, and are highly valued as gemstones. Pearls are often used in jewelry and have been prized for their beauty and rarity throughout history.
