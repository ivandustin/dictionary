The word "θανατου" (thanatou) is a Greek noun that means "of death" or "pertaining to death". It is derived from the Greek word "θάνατος" (thanatos), which means "death". In terms of grammar, "θανατου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. 

In Greek mythology, Thanatos was the personification of death and was often depicted as a winged figure carrying a sword or a butterfly net. The word "thanatophobia" is derived from "thanatos" and refers to the fear of death. 

Overall, "θανατου" is a word that is closely associated with the concept of death and mortality in Greek culture and language.
