Word: θαυμαζοντων

Etymology: θαυμαζοντων is a present active participle in Greek, derived from the verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". The suffix -οντ- (-ont-) indicates the present tense and active voice.

Grammar: θαυμαζοντων is a masculine, plural, genitive form of the present active participle. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently marveling or wondering about something. In English, it would be translated as "of those who are marveling/wondering".

Description: θαυμαζοντων is a word that describes a specific group of people who are currently experiencing a sense of wonder or amazement. It is often used in literature and poetry to convey a sense of awe or admiration.
