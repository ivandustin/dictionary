Word: θανατουμενοι

Etymology: The word θανατουμενοι is derived from the Greek word θανατός (thanatos) which means "death" and the verb μένω (meno) which means "to remain". Therefore, θανατουμενοι means "those who remain in death" or "the dead".

Grammar: θανατουμενοι is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενοι (-menoi) to the stem of the verb θανατός (thanatos).

Description: θανατουμενοι refers to the deceased or the dead. It is often used in a solemn or poetic context, such as in funeral or mourning rituals. In Greek mythology, the god of death was also named Thanatos.
