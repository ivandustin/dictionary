Word: θαυμαζετε

Etymology: θαυμαζετε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". The suffix -ετε (-ete) indicates that the verb is in the present tense and second person plural form.

Grammar: θαυμαζετε is a present tense verb in the second person plural form. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all marvel" or "you all wonder". The verb is formed by adding the suffix -ετε to the root word θαυμάζω.

Description: θαυμαζετε is a Greek verb that expresses the act of marveling or wondering. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the awe and wonder that people feel when they encounter something divine or miraculous. The verb is commonly used in the Greek Orthodox Church during religious services and prayers.
