Word: θαυμασαντες

Etymology: θαυμασαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the present active participle, meaning "those who are marveling" or "those who are wondering".

Grammar: θαυμασαντες is a present active participle in the masculine gender, nominative case, and plural number. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently marveling or wondering about something.

Description: θαυμασαντες is a word that describes a group of people who are in a state of wonder or amazement.
