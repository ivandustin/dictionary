Word: θαυμαζω

Etymology: The word θαυμαζω (thav-ma-zo) comes from the Greek root word θαῦμα (thauma), which means "wonder" or "marvel." The suffix -ζω (-zo) is a verb ending that indicates the action of the word.

Grammar: θαυμαζω is a Greek verb in the present tense, which means "I wonder" or "I am amazed." It is in the first person singular form. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: θαυμάζω (thav-ma-zo)
- Imperfect tense: θαύμαζα (thav-ma-za)
- Future tense: θαυμάσω (thav-ma-so)
- Aorist tense: ἐθαύμασα (e-thav-ma-sa)

Description: θαυμαζω is a Greek verb that expresses a feeling of wonder or amazement. It can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as being amazed by a beautiful sunset, a talented musician, or a remarkable achievement. The word is often used in a positive context, but it can also be used to express surprise or disbelief.
