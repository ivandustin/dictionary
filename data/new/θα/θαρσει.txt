Word: θαρσει

Etymology: θαρσει is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θάρρος (tharros), which means "courage" or "boldness". 

Grammar: θαρσει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb θαρσέω (tharseo), which means "to be courageous" or "to be bold". 

Description: θαρσει is a verb that describes someone who is showing courage or boldness in a particular situation. It can be used to encourage someone to be brave or to describe someone who is already exhibiting bravery. For example, "Θαρσείς, δεν υπάρχει λόγος να φοβάσαι" (Tharseis, den yparxei logos na fovasai) means "Be brave, there is no reason to be afraid".
