Word: θαυμασονται

Etymology: θαυμασονται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θαυμάζω (thaumazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". The suffix -ονται (-ontai) indicates that the verb is in the third person plural, passive voice, and present tense.

Grammar: θαυμασονται is a third person plural, passive voice, and present tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb θαυμάζω (thaumazo) by adding the suffix -ονται (-ontai) to indicate the passive voice and present tense.

Description: θαυμασονται is a Greek verb that means "they are being marveled at" or "they are being wondered at". It is often used to describe something that is impressive or awe-inspiring.
