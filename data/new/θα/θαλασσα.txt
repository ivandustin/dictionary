Word: θαλασσα

Etymology:
The word θαλασσα (thalassa) comes from the ancient Greek word θάλασσα (thalassa), which means "sea" or "ocean". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *tel-, which means "to lift up" or "to bear".

Grammar:
In Greek, θαλασσα is a feminine noun, and its article is η (i). It is declined in the singular and plural, and its declension follows the first declension pattern. For example:

Singular:
- η θαλασσα (i thalassa) - the sea
- μια θαλασσα (mia thalassa) - a sea
- τη θαλασσα (ti thalassa) - the sea (accusative case)

Plural:
- οι θαλασσες (oi thalasses) - the seas
- κάποιες θαλασσες (kapoies thalasses) - some seas
- τις θαλασσες (tis thalasses) - the seas (accusative case)

Description:
Θαλασσα is a common word in Greek, and it refers to the vast body of saltwater that covers most of the Earth's surface. It is an important element of Greek culture and history, as Greece is a country surrounded by the Aegean, Ionian, and Mediterranean Seas. The sea has played a significant role in Greek mythology, literature, and art, and it continues to be a major source of food, transportation, and recreation for Greeks today.
