Etymology: The word "υιοθεσίας" (yióthesías) comes from the Greek roots "υιός" (yiós), meaning "son," and "θέση" (thési), meaning "placement" or "position." It refers to the legal act of adopting a child.

Grammar: "Υιοθεσίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence.

Description: "Υιοθεσίας" is a term used to describe the process of legally adopting a child. It involves the transfer of parental rights and responsibilities from the biological parents to the adoptive parents. Adoption can provide a loving and stable home for children who are unable to be raised by their biological parents.
