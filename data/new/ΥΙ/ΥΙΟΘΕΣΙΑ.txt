Etymology: The word "υιοθεσία" comes from the Greek roots "υιός" (uios), meaning "son" or "child," and "θέση" (thesi), meaning "placement" or "position." 

Grammar: "Υιοθεσία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Υιοθεσία" refers to the legal process of adopting a child and becoming their legal parent. It involves the transfer of parental rights and responsibilities from the biological parents to the adoptive parents. Adoption can be a complex and emotional process, and it is often done to provide a stable and loving home for children who are unable to be raised by their biological parents.
