Word: μωρίας

Etymology:
The word μωρίας (morias) is derived from the Greek word μωρός (moros), which means "foolish" or "stupid". The suffix -ίας (-ias) is added to the end of the word to form a noun, indicating a state or condition of being foolish.

Grammar:
μωρίας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated into English using the preposition "of".

Description:
μωρίας refers to the state or condition of being foolish or stupid. It can also be used to describe an action or behavior that is foolish or senseless. The word is often used in a negative context, and is not typically used as a compliment.
