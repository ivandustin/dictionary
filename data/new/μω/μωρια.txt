Word: μωρία (moria)

Etymology: The word μωρία comes from the Greek word μωρός (moros), which means "foolish" or "stupid". It is related to the English word "moron".

Grammar: μωρία is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case and can be translated as "foolishness" or "stupidity".

Description: μωρία refers to the state or quality of being foolish or stupid. It can also refer to foolish or stupid behavior or actions. In Greek philosophy, μωρία was seen as a negative quality that should be avoided in order to achieve wisdom and virtue.
