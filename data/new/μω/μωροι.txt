Word: μωροί

Etymology:
The word μωροί (moroi) is derived from the Greek word μωρός (moros), which means "foolish" or "stupid". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *meh₁- ("to be bewildered, confused").

Grammar:
μωροί is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "fools" or "stupid people". It is declined in the second declension, with the nominative singular form being μωρός (moros).

Description:
μωροί is a derogatory term used to describe people who are foolish or lacking in intelligence. It can be used in a variety of contexts, from playful teasing to serious insults. The word is often used in Greek literature and mythology to describe characters who make foolish decisions or act in a foolish manner.
