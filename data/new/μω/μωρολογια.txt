Word: μωρολογια

Etymology: The word μωρολογια comes from the Greek words μωρός (moros) meaning "foolish" and λογία (logia) meaning "discourse" or "study of". Therefore, μωρολογια can be translated as "foolish talk" or "nonsense".

Grammar: μωρολογια is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is μωρολογίες (morologies).

Description: μωρολογια refers to speech or writing that is foolish, nonsensical, or lacking in intelligence. It can also refer to the act of speaking or writing in such a manner. The term is often used to criticize someone's ideas or arguments as being illogical or absurd.
