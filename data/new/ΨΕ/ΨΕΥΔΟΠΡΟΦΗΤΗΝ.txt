Etymology: The word "ψευδοπροφητην" (pronounced "psev-doh-pro-fee-teen") is derived from the Greek words "ψευδος" (pseudos) meaning "false" and "προφητης" (prophētēs) meaning "prophet". 

Grammar: "ψευδοπροφητην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "ψευδοπροφητην" refers to a false prophet. It is used to describe someone who claims to have divine or supernatural knowledge or powers, but is actually deceptive or misleading in their teachings or predictions.
