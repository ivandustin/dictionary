Etymology: The word "ψευσασθαι" (pronounced "pseusasthai") comes from the Greek verb "ψεύδομαι" (pseudomai), which means "to lie" or "to deceive." It is derived from the Greek noun "ψεῦδος" (pseudes), meaning "falsehood" or "lie."

Grammar: "ψευσασθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "ψεύδομαι" (pseudomai). The aorist tense indicates a past action, and the middle voice suggests that the subject is performing the action on themselves. In English, it can be translated as "to have lied" or "to have deceived oneself."

Description: "ψευσασθαι" refers to the act of deceiving oneself or intentionally telling oneself a falsehood. It implies a deliberate act of self-deception or self-delusion.
