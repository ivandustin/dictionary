Etymology: The word "ψευδοπροφητης" (pronounced "psev-doh-pro-fee-tis") is derived from the Greek words "ψευδος" (pseudos) meaning "false" and "προφητης" (prophētēs) meaning "prophet". 

Grammar: In Greek, "ψευδοπροφητης" is a compound word consisting of the noun "ψευδος" (pseudos) which means "false" and the noun "προφητης" (prophētēs) which means "prophet". It is a masculine noun in the nominative case.

Description: "ψευδοπροφητης" is a Greek word that refers to a person who claims to be a prophet but is not genuine or truthful in their prophecies. It is often used to describe someone who falsely claims to have divine or supernatural knowledge or insight into the future.
