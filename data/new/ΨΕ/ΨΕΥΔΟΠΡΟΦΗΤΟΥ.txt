Etymology: The word "ψευδοπροφητου" comes from the Greek roots "ψευδο-" meaning "false" and "προφήτης" meaning "prophet". 

Grammar: "ψευδοπροφητου" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. 

Description: "ψευδοπροφητου" refers to a false prophet, someone who claims to have divine knowledge or insight but is actually deceiving others.
