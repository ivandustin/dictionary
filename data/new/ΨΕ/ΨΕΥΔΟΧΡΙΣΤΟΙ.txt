Etymology: The word "ψευδοχριστοι" comes from the Greek words "ψευδής" (pseudēs) meaning "false" and "Χριστός" (Christos) meaning "Christ". 

Grammar: "ψευδοχριστοι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the suffix "-οι" to the word "ψευδοχριστος" (pseudochristos), which means "false Christ". 

Description: "ψευδοχριστοι" refers to individuals or groups who claim to be Christ or the Messiah, but are considered false or deceptive by others. This term is often used in religious contexts to describe impostors or charlatans who falsely claim to have divine authority or to be the second coming of Christ.
