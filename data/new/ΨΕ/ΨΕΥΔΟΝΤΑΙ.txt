Etymology: The word "ψευδονται" (pronounced "psev-don-tai") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "ψευδος" (psev-dos), which means "false" or "deceptive."

Grammar: "ψευδονται" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is conjugated from the verb "ψεύδομαι" (psev-dom-ai), which means "to lie" or "to deceive." The "-νται" (-ntai) ending indicates that the action is being performed by multiple people.

Description: "ψευδονται" is used to describe the act of lying or deceiving. It refers to the action of multiple individuals engaging in falsehood or deception.
