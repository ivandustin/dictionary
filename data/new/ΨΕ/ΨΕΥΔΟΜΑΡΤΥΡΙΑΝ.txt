Etymology: The word "ψευδομαρτυρίαν" comes from the Greek roots "ψευδο-" meaning "false" and "μαρτυρία" meaning "testimony". 

Grammar: "ψευδομαρτυρίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is derived from the base form "ψευδομαρτυρία" which is a compound word consisting of "ψευδο-" as a prefix meaning "false" and "μαρτυρία" meaning "testimony". The suffix "-ν" indicates the accusative case.

Description: "ψευδομαρτυρίαν" refers to the act of giving false testimony or bearing false witness. It is used to describe the act of intentionally providing untrue information or making false statements under oath or in a legal context.
