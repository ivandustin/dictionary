Etymology: The word "ψευδαδέλφους" comes from the Greek roots "ψευδής" (pseudēs) meaning "false" or "deceptive" and "ἀδελφός" (adelphos) meaning "brother". 

Grammar: "Ψευδαδέλφους" is a Greek noun in the masculine plural form. It is composed of the root "ψευδαδελφ-" and the plural ending "-ους". 

Description: "Ψευδαδέλφους" is a term used in Greek to refer to someone who pretends to be a friend or a brother but is actually deceitful or treacherous. It describes a person who presents themselves as trustworthy or loyal, but their true intentions are dishonest or harmful.
