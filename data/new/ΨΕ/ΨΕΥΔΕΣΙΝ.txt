Grammar: "Ψευδεσίν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ψευδεσίν" refers to something that is false or deceptive. It can be used to describe a person, an idea, or any other thing that is not genuine or truthful.
