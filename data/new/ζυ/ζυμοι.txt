Word: ζυμοι

Etymology:
ζυμοι (zymoi) is a Greek word that comes from the verb ζέω (zeo), which means "to boil" or "to ferment." ζυμοι refers to the foam or froth that forms on the surface of fermenting liquids, such as beer or wine.

ζυμοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
ζυμοι is a term used in the context of brewing and fermentation. It refers to the foam or froth that forms on the surface of fermenting liquids, such as beer or wine. This foam is a result of the carbon dioxide gas that is produced during the fermentation process. The presence of ζυμοι is an indication that the fermentation is active and progressing.
