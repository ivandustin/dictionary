Etymology: The word "ακουσας" (akousas) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ekʷeh₂-, meaning "to hear".

Grammar: "ακουσας" is the aorist participle form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The participle form is used to modify a noun or pronoun, and it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "ακουσας" can be translated as "having heard" or "after hearing" in English. It is used to indicate that someone has heard or listened to something in the past. It is commonly used in Greek literature and can be found in various contexts, such as storytelling, historical accounts, or personal narratives.
