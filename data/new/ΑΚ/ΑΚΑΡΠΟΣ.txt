Etymology: The word "ακαρπος" (akarpos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "καρπος" (karpos), meaning "fruit." Therefore, "ακαρπος" (akarpos) literally means "not fruitful" or "unfruitful."

Grammar: In Greek, "ακαρπος" (akarpos) is an adjective. It can be used to describe something that does not produce fruit or is unproductive.

Description: "Ακαρπος" (akarpos) is often used metaphorically to describe something that is barren, unproductive, or lacking in results. It can refer to a person, a situation, or an idea that fails to yield positive outcomes or benefits.
