Etymology: The word "ακουομεν" (akouomen) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is formed by adding the suffix "-μεν" (-men) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "ακουομεν" is the present tense, indicative mood, active voice, first person plural form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The first person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is "we" and the action is happening in the present.

Description: "ακουομεν" is used to express the action of hearing or listening in the present tense. It can be translated as "we hear" or "we are listening" in English.
