Etymology: The word "ακουοντι" (akounti) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ḱous-.

Grammar: "Ακουοντι" is the present participle form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo) in the third person singular. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Ακουοντι" describes someone or something that is currently hearing or listening. It indicates that the action of hearing is happening at the present moment.
