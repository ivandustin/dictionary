Etymology: The word "ακοή" (akoí) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akoúo), which means "to hear". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ekʷeh₂-, meaning "to perceive".

Grammar: "Ακοή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "ακοής" (akoís). In the plural, it becomes "ακοές" (akoés).

Description: "Ακοή" refers to the sense of hearing or the act of hearing. It is used to describe the ability to perceive sound through the ears. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the act of listening or paying attention to something.
