Etymology: The word "ακρατεις" (akrateis) comes from the Greek word "ακρατης" (akrates), which means "unrestrained" or "lacking self-control". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "without", and the root "κρατος" (kratos), meaning "power" or "control".

Grammar: In Greek, "ακρατεις" is the plural form of the adjective "ακρατης" (akrates), which means "unrestrained" or "lacking self-control". It is used to describe multiple individuals who exhibit this characteristic.

Description: "ακρατεις" is an adjective that describes individuals who lack self-control or are unable to restrain themselves. It can be used to describe various behaviors or actions that are impulsive, excessive, or indulgent.
