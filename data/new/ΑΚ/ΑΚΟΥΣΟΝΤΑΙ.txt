Etymology: The word "ακουσονται" (akousontai) is derived from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix "-νται" (-ntai) is the third person plural passive ending in Greek.

Grammar: "ακουσονται" (akousontai) is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of hearing or listening.

Description: "ακουσονται" (akousontai) describes an action of hearing or listening that is being done by a group of people or things. It implies that the subject is not actively performing the action, but rather the action is happening to them.
