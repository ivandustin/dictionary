Grammar: "ακουσασα" (akousasa) is a participle form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo). In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. The form "ακουσασα" (akousasa) indicates that the action of hearing or listening has been completed in the past.

Description: The word "ακουσασα" (akousasa) describes a female subject who has heard or listened to something in the past. It can be used to indicate that she has heard a specific sound, a piece of information, or someone's words.
