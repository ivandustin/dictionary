Etymology: The word "ακαθαρσία" (akatharsía) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "καθαρός" (katharós), meaning "clean" or "pure." 

Grammar: "Ακαθαρσία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. 

Description: "Ακαθαρσία" is a Greek word that translates to "impurity" or "uncleanness" in English. It is often used to describe something that is morally or spiritually impure, dirty, or contaminated. In various contexts, it can refer to physical dirtiness, moral corruption, or religious impurity.
