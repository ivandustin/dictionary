Etymology: The word "ακατακριτον" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the noun "κριτον" (meaning "judge" or "critic"). 

Grammar: "ακατακριτον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" in the nominative case.

Description: "ακατακριτον" is a word that can be translated as "unjudged" or "uncriticized" in English. It refers to something that has not been evaluated or criticized by a judge or critic.
