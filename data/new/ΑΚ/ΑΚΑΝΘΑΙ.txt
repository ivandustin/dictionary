Etymology: The word "ακανθαί" (akanthai) comes from the Greek word "ακανθα" (akantha), meaning "thorn" or "spine". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ek- ("sharp").

Grammar: "Ακανθαί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple thorns or spines.

Description: "Ακανθαί" refers to thorns or spines. It can be used to describe the sharp, pointed structures found on plants, such as those on a rose bush or a cactus. Thorns and spines serve as a defense mechanism for plants, deterring animals from eating them or touching them.
