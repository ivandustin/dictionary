Etymology: The word "ακαθαρτος" (akathartos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "καθαρτος" (kathartos), meaning "clean" or "pure." 

Grammar: In Greek, "ακαθαρτος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Ακαθαρτος" is a Greek word that means "unclean" or "impure." It is often used to describe something that is dirty, contaminated, or morally impure.
