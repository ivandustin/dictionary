Etymology: The word "ακολουθείν" (akolouthein) comes from the Greek roots "ακόλουθος" (akolouthos) meaning "follower" and "-είν" (-ein) which is a verb ending. 

Grammar: "Ακολουθείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: "Ακολουθείν" means "to follow" in English. It is used to describe the action of going after or accompanying someone or something. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate the act of adhering to a belief, principle, or course of action.
