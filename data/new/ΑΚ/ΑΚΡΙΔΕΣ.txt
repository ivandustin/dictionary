Etymology: The word "ακρίδες" (akrides) comes from the Greek word "ακρίς" (akris), which means "locusts". 

Grammar: "ακρίδες" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "ακρίδες" refers to locusts, which are insects belonging to the family Acrididae. They are known for their ability to swarm and cause significant damage to crops. Locusts have long hind legs for jumping and are typically green or brown in color. They are found in various parts of the world and are known for their distinctive buzzing sound.
