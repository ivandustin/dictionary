Etymology: The word "ακρων" (akron) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word "ακρος" (akros), meaning "extreme" or "highest point."

Grammar: In Greek, "ακρων" (akron) is a noun. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: "Ακρων" (akron) refers to the highest or outermost point of something. It can be used to describe the top or apex of a mountain, the tip of a spear, or the summit of a building. It generally signifies the extreme or farthest point of an object or concept.
