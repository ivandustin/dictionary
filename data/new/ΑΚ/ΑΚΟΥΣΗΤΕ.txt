Etymology: The word "ακουσητε" (akousite) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix "-τε" (-te) is added to the verb stem to form the imperative mood, giving the meaning "listen" or "hear".

Grammar: "ακουσητε" (akousite) is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to listen or hear something.

Description: "ακουσητε" (akousite) is a word used in Greek to command or instruct a group of people to listen or hear something. It is often used in contexts where someone wants to get the attention of a group or wants them to pay close attention to what is being said or played.
