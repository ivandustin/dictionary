Etymology: The word "ακολουθησαι" (akolouthēsai) comes from the Greek verb "ακολουθέω" (akoloutheō), which means "to follow" or "to accompany."

Grammar: "ακολουθησαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "ακολουθέω" (akoloutheō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action.

Description: "ακολουθησαι" is a verb form that expresses the action of following or accompanying someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as following a person's instructions, following a path or route, or following a particular course of action.
