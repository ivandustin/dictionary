Etymology: The word "ακανθινον" (akanthinon) comes from the Greek word "ακανθα" (akantha), meaning "thorn" or "spine". It is derived from the Greek word "ακη" (ake), meaning "sharp point".

Grammar: "Ακανθινον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" in the nominative case.

Description: "Ακανθινον" refers to something that is thorny or spiny. It can be used to describe plants or objects that have sharp, pointed projections or thorns.
