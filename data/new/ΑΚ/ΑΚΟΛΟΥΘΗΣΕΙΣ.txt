Etymology: The word "ακολουθήσεις" comes from the Greek verb "ακολουθώ" (akolouthó), which means "to follow" or "to accompany". The suffix "-εις" (-eis) is added to the verb stem to form the noun "ακολουθήσεις" (akolouthísis), which means "followings" or "followers".

Grammar: "Ακολουθήσεις" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns can have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. In this case, "ακολουθήσεις" is in the masculine or feminine gender, plural number, and nominative case.

Description: "Ακολουθήσεις" refers to the act of following or the group of people who follow someone or something. It can be used to describe a group of followers, disciples, or supporters.
