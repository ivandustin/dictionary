Etymology: The word "ακαταλυτου" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not" or "un-") and the noun "κατάλυτος" (meaning "dissolvable" or "soluble"). 

Grammar: "ακαταλυτου" is an adjective in the masculine genitive singular form. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word "ακαταλυτου" describes something that cannot be dissolved or broken down. It implies a state of permanence or stability.
