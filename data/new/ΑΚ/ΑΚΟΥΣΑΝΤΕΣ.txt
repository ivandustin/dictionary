Etymology: The word "ακουσαντες" (akousantes) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending, indicating that the action of hearing has been completed.

Grammar: "Ακουσαντες" is the masculine plural form of the aorist active participle of the verb "ακούω" (akouo). It is used to describe a group of people who have heard or listened to something. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to modify nouns or function as independent verbs.
