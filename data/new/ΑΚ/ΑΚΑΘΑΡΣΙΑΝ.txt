Etymology: The word "ακαθαρσιαν" comes from the Greek word "ακάθαρσος" (akatharos), meaning "impure" or "unclean".

Grammar: "ακαθαρσιαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. It is derived from the adjective "ακάθαρτος" (akathartos), meaning "impure" or "unclean".

Description: "ακαθαρσιαν" refers to impurity or uncleanness. It can be used to describe physical impurity or moral impurity. In a religious context, it can refer to ritual impurity or spiritual impurity.
