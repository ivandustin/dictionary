Etymology: The word "ακουσετε" (akousete) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is formed by adding the suffix "-σετε" (-sete) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "Ακουσετε" (akousete) is the second person plural form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo) in the imperative mood. It is used to give a command or make a request to a group of people, telling them to listen or hear something.

Description: "Ακουσετε" (akousete) is a Greek word that is commonly used in everyday speech. It is often used to get someone's attention or to ask them to pay attention to something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in conversations, instructions, or public announcements.
