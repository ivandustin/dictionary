Etymology: The word "ακριδων" (akridon) comes from the Greek word "ακρίς" (akris), which means "locust". 

Grammar: "ακριδων" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "ακριδων" refers to locusts, which are a type of insect belonging to the family Acrididae. They are known for their ability to swarm and cause significant damage to crops. Locusts have long hind legs for jumping and are typically found in warm and arid regions.
