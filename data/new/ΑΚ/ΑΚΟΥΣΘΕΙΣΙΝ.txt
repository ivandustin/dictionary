Etymology: The word "ακουσθείσιν" comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix "-σθεισιν" is a passive participle ending, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "ακουσθείσιν" is a passive participle in the dative plural form. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the recipient of an action.

Description: "ακουσθείσιν" can be translated as "having been heard" or "having been listened to". It is used to describe something that has been heard or listened to by someone.
