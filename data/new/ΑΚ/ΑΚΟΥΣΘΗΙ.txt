Etymology: The word "ακουσθηι" (akousthēi) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouō), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ekʷeh₂-, meaning "to hear".

Grammar: "ακουσθηι" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and subjunctive mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "ακούω" (akouō), which means "to hear" or "to listen".

Description: "ακουσθηι" is used to express an action of hearing or listening in the past. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action is uncertain or hypothetical. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is the receiver of the action.
