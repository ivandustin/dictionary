Etymology: The word "ακουσομεθα" comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix "-μεθα" (-metha) is a reflexive ending that indicates the action is performed by the subject upon itself. Therefore, "ακουσομεθα" can be translated as "we hear ourselves" or "we listen to ourselves".

Grammar: "ακουσομεθα" is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and first person plural form. It is used to express an action that is being done to us or by us. In this case, it indicates that we are hearing or listening to ourselves.

Description: "ακουσομεθα" is a word that emphasizes the act of self-listening or self-awareness. It suggests the importance of paying attention to our own thoughts, feelings, and experiences. It can be used in various contexts, such as personal growth, introspection, or self-reflection.
