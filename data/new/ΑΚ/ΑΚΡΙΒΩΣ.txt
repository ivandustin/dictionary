Etymology: The word "ακριβώς" comes from the Greek adjective "ακριβής" (akrivis), which means "precise" or "accurate". It is derived from the noun "άκρος" (akros), meaning "extreme" or "highest point".

Grammar: "Ακριβώς" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate precision, accuracy, or exactness.

Description: "Ακριβώς" is commonly translated as "exactly" or "precisely" in English. It is used to emphasize that something is done or said with great accuracy or without any deviation. It can also be used to express agreement or confirmation.
