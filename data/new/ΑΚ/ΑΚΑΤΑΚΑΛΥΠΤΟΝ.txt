Etymology: The word "ακατακαλυπτον" comes from the Greek roots "ακατα-" meaning "not" and "καλυπτω" meaning "to cover". 

Grammar: "ακατακαλυπτον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "ακατακαλυπτον" is a Greek word that translates to "unveiled" or "uncovered" in English. It is used to describe something that is not hidden or concealed.
