Etymology: The word "ακουσαι" (akousai) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". 

Grammar: "ακουσαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that does not indicate tense, mood, or person.

Description: "ακουσαι" is used to express the action of hearing or listening in the past. It can be translated as "to have heard" or "to have listened".
