Etymology: The word "ακρασίας" (akrasias) comes from the Greek word "ακρασία" (akrasia), which means "lack of self-control" or "weakness of will." It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "without," and the noun "κράσις" (krasis), meaning "mixture" or "blending."

Grammar: "Ακρασίας" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Ακρασίας" refers to the state or condition of lacking self-control or having a weakness of will. It is often used to describe situations where a person knows what they should do, but they act against their better judgment due to their inability to resist temptation or overcome their desires.
