Etymology: The word "ακούω" (akouo) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "ἀκούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ekʷeh₂-, meaning "to perceive" or "to hear".

Grammar: "Ακούω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Ακούω (akouo) - I hear
- Ακούς (akous) - You hear (singular)
- Ακούει (akouei) - He/she/it hears
- Ακούουμε (akououme) - We hear
- Ακούετε (akouete) - You hear (plural)
- Ακούουν (akououn) - They hear

Description: "Ακούω" is a common Greek verb that means "to hear" or "to listen". It is used to describe the act of perceiving sound through the ears. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "to understand" or "to pay attention to".
