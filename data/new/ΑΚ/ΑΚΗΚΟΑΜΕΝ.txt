Etymology: The word "ακηκοαμεν" comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear". It is in the perfect tense, indicative mood, and first person plural form.

Grammar: "ακηκοαμεν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The indicative mood suggests a statement of fact. The first person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is "we".

Description: "ακηκοαμεν" translates to "we have heard" in English. It implies that the act of hearing has been completed in the past, and the result of that action is still relevant in the present.
