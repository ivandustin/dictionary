Etymology: The word "ακανθας" (akanthas) comes from the Greek word "ακανθα" (akantha), meaning "thorn" or "spine". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ek- ("sharp").

Grammar: "Ακανθας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes "ακανθες" (akanthes).

Description: "Ακανθας" refers to a thorn or a spine, typically found on plants. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is sharp, prickly, or difficult to handle.
