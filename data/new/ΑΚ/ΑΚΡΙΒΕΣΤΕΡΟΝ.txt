Etymology: The word "ακριβεστερον" comes from the Greek word "ακριβής" (akribis), meaning "precise" or "accurate", and the suffix "-τερον" (-teron), which is a comparative suffix. 

Grammar: "Ακριβεστερον" is an adverb in the Greek language. It is the comparative form of the adverb "ακριβώς" (akribos), which means "precisely" or "exactly". 

Description: "Ακριβεστερον" is used to indicate a higher degree of precision or accuracy compared to something else. It is often used to compare two or more things and express that one is more precise or accurate than the other(s).
