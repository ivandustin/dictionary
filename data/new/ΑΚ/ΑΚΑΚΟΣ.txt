Etymology: The word "ακακος" (akakos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), which means "not," and the word "κακος" (kakos), which means "bad" or "evil." Therefore, "ακακος" can be translated as "not bad" or "not evil."

Grammar: In Greek, "ακακος" is an adjective. It is in the masculine form and singular number. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: The word "ακακος" is used to describe someone or something that is not bad or evil. It can be used to express the absence of negative qualities or to describe someone who is virtuous, kind, or good-natured.
