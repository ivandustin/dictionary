Etymology: The word "ακαρποι" (akarpoi) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "καρπός" (karpos), meaning "fruit." Therefore, "ακαρποι" (akarpoi) literally means "not fruitful" or "unfruitful."

Grammar: "ακαρποι" (akarpoi) is the plural form of the adjective "ακαρπος" (akarpos) in Greek. It is used to describe something or someone that does not produce fruit or results.

Description: "ακαρποι" (akarpoi) is often used metaphorically to describe situations, actions, or efforts that do not yield any positive outcomes or results. It can refer to unproductive or unsuccessful endeavors, relationships, or endeavors that do not bear any fruit or achieve their intended purpose.
