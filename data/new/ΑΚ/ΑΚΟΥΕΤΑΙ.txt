Etymology: The word "ακουεται" comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ḱous-.

Grammar: "ακουεται" is the third person singular form of the verb "ακούω" in the present tense passive voice. It is used to indicate that something is being heard or listened to by someone or something.

Description: "ακουεται" is a Greek verb that describes the action of hearing or listening. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing to express the act of perceiving sound through the ears. The passive voice form suggests that the subject is the receiver of the action, rather than the doer.
