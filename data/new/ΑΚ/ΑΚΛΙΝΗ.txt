Etymology: The word "ακλινη" (aklini) comes from the Greek word "α" (a) meaning "not" and "κλίνη" (klini) meaning "bed" or "couch". It literally translates to "not bed" or "not couch".

Grammar: "Ακλινη" (aklini) is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form.

Description: "Ακλινη" (aklini) is a term used in Greek to describe a condition or state of being unable to sleep or rest. It refers to a state of restlessness or insomnia.
