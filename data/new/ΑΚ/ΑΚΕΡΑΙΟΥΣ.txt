Etymology: The word "ακεραιος" (akeraios) comes from the Greek word "α" (a), meaning "not," and "κεραιος" (keraios), meaning "mixed" or "impure." It can be translated as "pure" or "unmixed."

Grammar: In Greek, "ακεραιος" (akeraios) is an adjective. It is declined according to gender, number, and case to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ακεραιος" (akeraios) is used to describe something that is pure, unadulterated, or untainted. It can refer to physical objects, qualities, or concepts.
