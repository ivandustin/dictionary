Etymology: The word "ακούε" (akoue) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". 

Grammar: "ακούε" is the imperative form of the verb "ακούω" (akouo) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to listen or hear something.

Description: "ακούε" is a word used in Greek to tell someone to listen or pay attention to something. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and can be used in various contexts, such as when someone wants to get someone's attention or when someone wants to emphasize the importance of listening.
