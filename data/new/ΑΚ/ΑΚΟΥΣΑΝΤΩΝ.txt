Etymology: The word "ακουσαντων" is derived from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix "-των" (-ton) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action of hearing is ongoing or in progress.

Grammar: "ακουσαντων" is the genitive plural form of the present participle of the verb "ακούω" (akouo). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. The plural form indicates that there are multiple individuals or objects possessing or related to the action of hearing.

Description: "ακουσαντων" can be translated as "of those who hear" or "of the ones listening". It refers to a group of people who are currently engaged in the act of hearing or listening.
