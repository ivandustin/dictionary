Etymology: The word "ακαίρως" (akairos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "καιρός" (kairos), meaning "time." 

Grammar: "Ακαίρως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or event that occurs at an inappropriate or untimely moment.

Description: "Ακαίρως" is used to express the idea of something happening at the wrong time or in an untimely manner. It implies that the action or event is not suitable or opportune given the circumstances.
