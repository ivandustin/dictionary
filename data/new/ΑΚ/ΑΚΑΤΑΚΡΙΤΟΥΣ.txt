Etymology: The word "ακατακρίτους" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the noun "κρίτης" (meaning "judge"). 

Grammar: "ακατακρίτους" is a Greek adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who cannot be judged or criticized. 

Description: "ακατακρίτους" refers to individuals who are beyond reproach or criticism. It implies that they are so exceptional or faultless that they cannot be judged or found fault with.
