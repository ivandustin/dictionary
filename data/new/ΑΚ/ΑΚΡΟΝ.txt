Etymology: The word "ακρον" (akron) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "ἄκρος" (akros), meaning "highest" or "topmost."

Grammar: In Greek, "ακρον" (akron) is a noun. It is a neuter gender noun, and its plural form is "ακρα" (akra).

Description: "Ακρον" (akron) refers to the highest or topmost part of something. It can be used to describe the peak or summit of a mountain, the tip of a spear, or the top of a building, among other things.
