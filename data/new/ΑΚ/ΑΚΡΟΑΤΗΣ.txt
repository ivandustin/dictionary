Etymology: The word "ακροατης" (akroatis) comes from the Greek roots "ακρο-" (acro-), meaning "at the top" or "highest," and "ακροαω" (acroao), meaning "to hear" or "to listen." 

Grammar: In Greek, "ακροατης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "ακροατες" (akroates). 

Description: "Ακροατης" refers to a listener or an audience member. It is used to describe someone who listens attentively or actively participates in a lecture, speech, or performance.
