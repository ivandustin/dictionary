Etymology: The word "ακουσατε" (akousate) comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is conjugated in the second person plural, aorist tense, imperative mood.

Grammar: "ακουσατε" is a verb form in Greek. It is in the second person plural, which means it is addressing a group of people. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give a command or make a request.

Description: "ακουσατε" is a command in Greek, meaning "Listen!" or "Hear!". It is used to direct someone or a group of people to pay attention and listen to what is being said.
