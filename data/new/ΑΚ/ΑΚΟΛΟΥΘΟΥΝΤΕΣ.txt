Etymology: The word "ακολουθούντες" comes from the Greek verb "ακολουθώ" (akolouthó), which means "to follow". The suffix "-ουντες" (-ountes) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the subject is "following" or "being followers".

Grammar: "Ακολουθούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "ακολουθώ" (akolouthó) in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently "following" or "being followers" of someone or something. In terms of grammar, it is a masculine plural form.

Description: "Ακολουθούντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "followers" or "those who follow". It is often used to refer to a group of people who are following a leader, a teacher, a philosophy, or a particular path. The word implies a sense of loyalty, obedience, and adherence to the beliefs or teachings of the person or thing being followed.
