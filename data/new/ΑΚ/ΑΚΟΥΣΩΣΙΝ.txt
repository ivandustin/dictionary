Etymology: The word "ακουσωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix "-σιν" is the third person plural present subjunctive ending, indicating that the action is being done by multiple people.

Grammar: "ακουσωσιν" is a verb form in the Greek language. It is in the third person plural present subjunctive tense. This tense is used to express actions that are uncertain or hypothetical, often used in subordinate clauses.

Description: "ακουσωσιν" is used to express the idea of "they may hear" or "they might hear" in Greek. It implies a possibility or uncertainty about whether the action of hearing will occur.
