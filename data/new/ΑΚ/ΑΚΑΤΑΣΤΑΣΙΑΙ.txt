Etymology: The word "ακαταστασία" (akatastasía) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "κατάσταση" (katástasi), meaning "state" or "condition." Therefore, "ακαταστασία" can be translated as "instability" or "disorder."

Grammar: "ακαταστασία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the first declension and has the plural form "ακαταστασίες" (akatastasíes).

Description: "ακαταστασία" refers to a state of disorder, chaos, or instability. It can be used to describe a lack of organization or control in various contexts, such as politics, society, or personal life.
