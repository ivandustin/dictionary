Word: ηδη

Etymology: ηδη is a Greek adverb that means "already" or "now". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἤδη" (ēdē), which has the same meaning.

Grammar: ηδη is an adverb in Greek and is used to modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs. It does not change form based on the gender, number, or case of the noun it modifies.

Description: ηδη is a common word in modern Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to indicate that something has already happened or that something is happening now. For example, "Ηδη έφυγες;" (ēdē éfyges?) means "Have you already left?" or "Ηδη είσαι εδώ;" (ēdē eísai edó?) means "Are you already here?"
