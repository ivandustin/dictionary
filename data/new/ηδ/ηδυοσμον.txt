Word: ηδυοσμον

Etymology: The word ηδυοσμον comes from the Greek words ηδύς (sweet) and οσμή (smell), meaning "sweet smell".

Grammar: ηδυοσμον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can be declined as follows:

- Nominative/Accusative: ηδυοσμον
- Genitive: ηδυοσμου
- Dative: ηδυοσμῷ
- Vocative: ηδυοσμον

Description: ηδυοσμον refers to a sweet smell or fragrance. It can be used to describe the pleasant aroma of flowers, perfumes, or other scented objects. The word is often used in poetry and literature to evoke a sense of beauty and pleasure.
