The word "ηδιστα" (idista) is a Greek adverb that means "pleasantly" or "delightfully". 

Etymology: The word comes from the adjective "ήδιος" (idios), which means "pleasant" or "sweet". The suffix "-στα" (-sta) is a common adverbial suffix in Greek.

Grammar: "ηδιστα" is an adverb and does not change form based on gender or number.

Description: The word is often used to describe something that is enjoyable or pleasing to the senses. For example, one might say "Η μουσική ακούγεται ηδιστα" (I musiki akougetai idista) which means "The music sounds pleasantly".
