Word: ηδυνηθην

Etymology:
The word ηδυνηθην is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ηδυνω, which means "to be able" or "to have power." The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
ηδυνηθην is a first-person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb ηδυνάμην (ēdynámēn), which means "I was able" or "I had power." The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, the speaker) is the recipient of the action rather than the doer.

Description:
ηδυνηθην is a word that expresses the idea of having power or being able to do something. It is a past tense form, indicating that the speaker had this ability at some point in the past. The word is often used in a spiritual or religious context, referring to the power of a deity or divine force.
