Word: αμφοτερα

Etymology: αμφοτερα is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: αμφί (amphi) meaning "both" or "on both sides" and τέρας (teras) meaning "monster" or "beast". Therefore, αμφοτερα literally means "both monsters" or "both beasts".

Grammar: αμφοτερα is a neuter plural form of the adjective αμφότερος (amphoteros), which means "both" or "either". It is used to describe two things or people together, and it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: αμφοτερα is a word that is commonly used in Greek to refer to two things or people together. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Αμφότερα τα παιδιά είναι πολύ έξυπνα" (Both children are very smart). It is a versatile word that can be used in both formal and informal settings.
