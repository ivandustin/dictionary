Word: αμαρτησαντων

Etymology: The word αμαρτησαντων is derived from the Greek verb αμαρτάνω (amartano), which means "to sin" or "to miss the mark". The suffix -των (-ton) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple people who have sinned.

Grammar: αμαρτησαντων is the genitive plural form of αμαρτάνω (amartano). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, αμαρτησαντων indicates that the word is referring to "those who have sinned" or "the sinners".

Description: αμαρτησαντων is a Greek word that refers to a group of people who have sinned. It is often used in a religious or moral context to describe those who have committed a wrongdoing or transgression. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to anyone who has made a mistake or error in judgment.
