Word: αμεταμελητον

Etymology: The word αμεταμελητον comes from the Greek roots α- (a-), meaning "not," μεταμέλομαι (metamélomai), meaning "to regret," and the suffix -τον (-ton), which forms adjectives. Therefore, αμεταμελητον means "not to be regretted" or "irremediable."

Grammar: αμεταμελητον is a neuter adjective in the singular form.

Description: αμεταμελητον is used to describe something that cannot be changed or undone, something that is irreversible or final. It can also refer to a decision or action that cannot be regretted or undone. The word is often used in a negative context, emphasizing the finality of a situation or outcome.
