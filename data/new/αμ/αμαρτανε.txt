Word: αμαρτανε

Etymology:
The word αμαρτανε (amartane) is derived from the Greek verb αμαρτάνω (amartano), which means "to miss the mark" or "to err". It is also related to the noun αμαρτία (amartia), which means "sin".

Grammar:
αμαρτανε (amartane) is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb αμαρτάνω (amartano), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description:
The word αμαρτανε (amartane) is used to describe the act of making a mistake or committing a sin. It can be used in a religious context to refer to the act of disobeying God's laws or in a more general sense to refer to any kind of error or mistake. The word is often used in the imperative form as a command to avoid making mistakes or committing sins.
