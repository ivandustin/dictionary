Word: αμωμος

Etymology:
The word αμωμος (amomos) is derived from the Greek word "α" (a) which means "not" and "μωμος" (mōmos) which means "blemish" or "defect". Therefore, αμωμος means "without blemish" or "flawless".

Grammar:
αμωμος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
αμωμος is used to describe something that is perfect, faultless, or without any defects. It can be used to describe a person's character, behavior, or physical appearance. In the Bible, it is used to describe the sacrificial animals that were offered to God, which had to be without blemish or defect.
