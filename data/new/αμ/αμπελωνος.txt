Word: αμπελώνος

Grammar: αμπελώνος is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive (αμπελώνα), which is used to show possession or origin.

Description: αμπελώνος refers to a vineyard, which is an area of land where grapevines are grown. It is a common word in Greek, especially in areas where wine production is a significant industry. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to any place where something is cultivated or grown.
