Word: αμαρτησαντας

Etymology:
The word αμαρτησαντας is derived from the Greek verb αμαρτάνω (amartano), which means "to sin" or "to miss the mark". The suffix -αντας (-antas) is a participle ending that indicates the past tense and active voice. Therefore, αμαρτησαντας means "having sinned" or "having missed the mark".

Grammar:
αμαρτησαντας is a past participle in the active voice. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, αμαρτησαντας can modify a masculine singular noun in the nominative case, such as άνδρας (man), to form the phrase αμαρτησαντας άνδρας (a man who has sinned).

Description:
αμαρτησαντας is a word that describes someone who has committed a sin or missed the mark. It is often used in a religious or moral context to refer to a person who has violated a moral or ethical code. The word can also be used more broadly to describe someone who has made a mistake or failed to achieve a goal.
