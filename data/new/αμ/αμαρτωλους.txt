Word: αμαρτωλούς

Etymology: The word αμαρτωλούς (amartolous) comes from the Greek word αμαρτία (amartia) which means "sin" or "error". The suffix -λούς (-lous) is added to form the plural form of the word.

Grammar: αμαρτωλούς is a plural adjective in the masculine gender, agreeing with the noun it modifies. It is in the accusative case, indicating that it is the object of the sentence.

Description: αμαρτωλούς is a Greek word that means "sinners" or "wrongdoers". It is often used in religious contexts to refer to those who have committed sins or transgressions. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used frequently to describe those who are in need of redemption or forgiveness.
