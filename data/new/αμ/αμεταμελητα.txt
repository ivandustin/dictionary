Word: αμεταμελητα

Etymology: The word αμεταμελητα is derived from the Greek word α- (a-) meaning "not" and μεταμέλομαι (metamélomai) meaning "to regret". Therefore, αμεταμελητα means "unregretted" or "unrepentant".

Grammar: αμεταμελητα is an adjective in the neuter gender, plural form.

Description: αμεταμελητα is used to describe something that is not regretted or repented. It can be used to describe a person's actions or a situation. For example, "He left the job without any regrets" can be translated as "Έφυγε από τη δουλειά αμεταμελητα". It can also be used in a negative context to describe someone who is unrepentant or stubborn.
