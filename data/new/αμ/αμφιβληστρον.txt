Word: αμφιβληστρον

Etymology:
The word αμφιβληστρον comes from the Greek words αμφί (amphi) meaning "on both sides" and βλήστρον (blēstron) meaning "dart". It literally means "dart thrown on both sides" and was used to describe a type of ancient Greek weapon.

Grammar:
αμφιβληστρον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is αμφίβληστρα (amphiblastra). In English, it is often used as a loanword and does not change form in the plural.

Description:
αμφιβληστρον is a term that has been used in various contexts throughout history. In ancient Greece, it referred to a type of weapon that was thrown with both hands. It was made of two pointed darts connected by a cord, and was used for hunting or in battle. In modern times, the term has been used metaphorically to describe situations where someone is caught between two opposing forces or ideas.
