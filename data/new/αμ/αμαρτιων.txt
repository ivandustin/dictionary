Word: αμαρτιων

Etymology: The word αμαρτιων comes from the Greek word "ἁμαρτία" (hamartia), which means "sin" or "error". The suffix "-ων" is added to indicate the plural form.

Grammar: αμαρτιων is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the plural form of αμαρτία (hamartia).

Description: αμαρτιων refers to sins or transgressions committed by individuals. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the sins of humanity and the need for redemption through Jesus Christ. The word is also used in the Septuagint, the Greek translation of the Old Testament, to describe various sins and transgressions.
