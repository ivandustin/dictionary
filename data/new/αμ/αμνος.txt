Word: αμνός

Etymology:
The word αμνός (amnos) is a Greek word that means "lamb". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ónHmos, which means "sheep".

Grammar:
In Greek, αμνός is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is αμνός, and its genitive singular form is αμνοῦ. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
αμνός is a term used to refer to a young sheep, specifically one that is less than a year old. In ancient Greece, lambs were often used as sacrificial animals in religious ceremonies. The word is also used metaphorically to refer to someone who is innocent or pure, like a lamb.
