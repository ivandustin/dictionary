Word: αμαραντινον

Etymology: The word αμαραντινον comes from the Greek word αμαραντος (amarantos), which means "unfading" or "immortal". The suffix -ινον (-inos) is added to indicate that something is made of or resembles the root word.

Grammar: αμαραντινον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is declined like a second declension noun, with the genitive form being αμαραντινου (amarantinou).

Description: αμαραντινον is an adjective that describes something as being unfading or immortal. It can also be used as a noun to refer to something that is made of or resembles the amaranth plant, which is known for its bright, unfading flowers. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a deep shade of red.
