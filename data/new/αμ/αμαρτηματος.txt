Word: αμαρτηματος

Etymology:
The word αμαρτηματος (amartematos) is derived from the Greek word αμαρτία (amartia), which means "sin" or "error". The suffix -ματος (-matos) is a genitive ending, indicating possession or relationship.

Grammar:
αμαρτηματος is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. It is a singular noun, meaning "sin" or "error".

Description:
αμαρτηματος is a Greek word that refers to a sin or an error. It is often used in a religious or moral context to describe a wrongdoing or transgression. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any mistake or fault.
