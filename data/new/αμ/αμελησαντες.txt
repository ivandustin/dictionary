Word: αμελησαντες

Etymology:
The word αμελησαντες is derived from the Greek verb αμελέω (ameleo), which means "to neglect" or "to disregard". The suffix -σαντες (-santes) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and refers to a group of people who neglected something.

Grammar:
αμελησαντες is a past participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who neglected something in the past. The word can be used as an adjective or a verb, depending on the context.

Description:
αμελησαντες is a Greek word that describes a group of people who have neglected something in the past. The word can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as neglecting one's duties, responsibilities, or obligations. It can also be used to describe a group of people who have failed to take action when action was needed. The word has a negative connotation and implies that the neglect was intentional or careless.
