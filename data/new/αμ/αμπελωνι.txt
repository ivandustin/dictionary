Word: αμπελώνι (ampelóni)

Etymology: The word αμπελώνι comes from the Greek word αμπέλι (ampéli), which means "vineyard". The suffix -ώνι (-óni) is a common suffix used to indicate a place or location.

Grammar: αμπελώνι is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and its nominative case is αμπελώνι. In the plural form, it becomes αμπελώνες (ampelónes).

Description: αμπελώνι refers to a piece of land where grapevines are cultivated. It is a common word in Greek, especially in areas where wine production is a significant industry. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to a source of wealth or abundance.
