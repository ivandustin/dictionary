Word: αμετρα

Etymology:
The word αμετρα (ametra) is a Greek word that comes from the prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the word "metron" meaning "measure". Therefore, αμετρα means "immeasurable" or "without measure".

Grammar:
αμετρα is an adjective in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined in different cases and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
αμετρα is used to describe something that cannot be measured or is beyond measure. It can be used to describe abstract concepts such as love, beauty, or happiness, as well as physical objects that are too large or too small to be measured accurately. It can also be used to describe actions or behaviors that are excessive or immoderate.
