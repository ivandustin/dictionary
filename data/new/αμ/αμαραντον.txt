Word: αμαραντον

The word αμαραντον comes from the Greek word αμαραντος (amarantos), which means "unfading" or "immortal". It is derived from the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and μαραινω (maraino), which means "to fade".

Grammar:
αμαραντον is a neuter noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and the nominative case.

αμαραντον is a word that is often used to describe something that is everlasting or eternal. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe things like love, beauty, or truth.
