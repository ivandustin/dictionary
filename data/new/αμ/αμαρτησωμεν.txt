Word: αμαρτησωμεν

Etymology: αμαρτησωμεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb αμαρτάνω (amartano), which means "to sin" or "to miss the mark."

Grammar: αμαρτησωμεν is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb αμαρτάνω (amartano). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations.

Description: αμαρτησωμεν can be translated as "let us sin" or "may we sin" in English. It is often used in religious contexts to express the idea of asking for forgiveness for one's sins or to acknowledge one's own fallibility.
