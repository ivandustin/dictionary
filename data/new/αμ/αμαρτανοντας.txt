Word: αμαρτανοντας

Grammar: αμαρτανοντας is the present participle form of the verb αμαρτάνω (amartano), which means "sinning" or "missing the mark". It is used to describe an ongoing action of sinning or missing the mark. In Greek grammar, the present participle can function as an adjective, noun, or adverb.
