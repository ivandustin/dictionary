Word: αμεμπτος

Etymology:
The word αμεμπτος (amemptos) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the verb "memphomai" meaning "to blame". Therefore, αμεμπτος means "blameless" or "without fault".

Grammar:
αμεμπτος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
αμεμπτος is a word used to describe someone or something that is faultless, blameless, or without reproach. It is often used in a moral or ethical context to describe a person's character or actions. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe something that is perfect or flawless.
