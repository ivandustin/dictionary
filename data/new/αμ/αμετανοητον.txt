Word: αμετανοητον

Etymology: The word αμετανοητον comes from the Greek roots α- (a-), meaning "not," μετανοέω (metanoeo), meaning "to repent," and the suffix -τον (-ton), which forms adjectives. Therefore, αμετανοητον means "not repentant" or "unrepentant."

Grammar: αμετανοητον is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: αμετανοητον is a word used to describe someone who is unwilling or unable to repent for their actions or behavior. It can also be used to describe a situation or condition that is unchangeable or irreversible. In a religious context, it can refer to someone who is unrepentant of their sins.
