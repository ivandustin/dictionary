Word: αμιαντον

Etymology:
The word αμιαντον (amianton) is derived from the Greek word αμίαντος (amiantos), which means "undefiled" or "pure". It is composed of the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the word μίανος (mianos), which means "defiled" or "polluted".

Grammar:
αμιαντον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. Therefore, αμιαντον can be used as both the subject and object of a sentence.

Description:
αμιαντον is an adjective that describes something that is pure, undefiled, or uncorrupted. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a pure substance or a clean surface, as well as abstract concepts, such as a pure heart or a pure intention. The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe a state of purity or holiness.
