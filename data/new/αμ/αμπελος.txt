Word: αμπελος

Etymology:
The word αμπελος (ampelos) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂m̥bʰi- meaning "vine". It is related to the Latin word "vitis" which also means "vine".

Grammar:
αμπελος is a masculine noun in Greek and is declined in the first declension. The nominative singular form is αμπελος and the genitive singular form is αμπελου.

Description:
αμπελος refers to a vine or grapevine, a plant that produces grapes. In Greek mythology, the god Dionysus was often associated with the vine and wine, and the cultivation of grapes was an important part of ancient Greek agriculture. Today, Greece is known for its wine production and has many vineyards throughout the country.
