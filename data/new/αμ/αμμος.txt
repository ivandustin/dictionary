Word: αμμος

Etymology:
The word αμμος (ammos) comes from the Ancient Greek ἄμμος (ámmos), which means "sand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ébʰos, which means "dust, sand".

Grammar:
In Greek, αμμος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is αμμος, and its genitive singular form is αμμου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
αμμος refers to sand, which is a granular material composed of rock fragments, minerals, shells, and other organic materials. Sand is found in various environments, such as beaches, deserts, and riverbeds. It is often used in construction, as a filtration medium, and in the manufacturing of glass and ceramics. In Greek mythology, the god of the sea, Poseidon, was often depicted carrying a trident and riding a chariot pulled by horses that had hooves made of sand.
