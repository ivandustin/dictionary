Word: αμεμπτως

Etymology: The word αμεμπτως comes from the Greek word α- (a-) meaning "not" and μεμπτός (memptos) meaning "blameworthy" or "faulty". Therefore, αμεμπτως means "blamelessly" or "faultlessly".

Grammar: αμεμπτως is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate that something is done without fault or blame.

Description: αμεμπτως is a word that describes the act of doing something without fault or blame. It is often used to describe a person's behavior or actions, indicating that they have acted in a faultless or blameless manner. This word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in legal or ethical discussions, or in everyday conversations to describe someone's behavior.
