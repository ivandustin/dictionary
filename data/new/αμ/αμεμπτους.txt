Word: αμεμπτους

Etymology:
The word αμεμπτους (amemptous) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the adjective "memptos" meaning "blamed" or "reproached". Therefore, αμεμπτους means "unblamed" or "blameless".

Grammar:
αμεμπτους is an adjective in the masculine gender, nominative case, and singular number in Greek.

Description:
αμεμπτους is a word used to describe someone or something that is faultless, innocent, or beyond reproach. It is often used in a moral or ethical context to describe a person who has not committed any wrongdoing or a situation that is free from blame.
