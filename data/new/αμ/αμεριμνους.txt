Word: αμεριμνους

Etymology: The word αμεριμνους comes from the Greek roots α- (a-) meaning "not" and μεριμναω (merimnao) meaning "to worry" or "to be anxious". Therefore, αμεριμνους means "carefree" or "unworried".

Grammar: αμεριμνους is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: αμεριμνους is used to describe someone who is carefree, unconcerned, or not worried about something. It can also be used to describe a situation or state of being that is free from worry or anxiety. The word has a positive connotation and is often used to describe a desirable state of mind or situation.
