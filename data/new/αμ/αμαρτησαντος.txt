Word: αμαρτησαντος

Etymology: The word αμαρτησαντος is derived from the Greek verb αμαρτάνω (amartano), which means "to sin" or "to miss the mark". The suffix -σαντος (-santos) is the aorist participle form of the verb, indicating past action.

Grammar: αμαρτησαντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive singular form of the aorist participle of the verb αμαρτάνω (amartano). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: αμαρτησαντος can be translated as "of the one who sinned" or "of the one who missed the mark". It is often used in religious contexts to refer to someone who has committed a sin or fallen short of a moral standard. The word can also be used more broadly to describe someone who has made a mistake or failed to achieve a goal.
