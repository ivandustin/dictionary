Word: αμαρτιαις

Etymology: αμαρτιαις is a Greek word that comes from the root word αμαρτία (amartia), which means "sin" or "error". The suffix -αις (-ais) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple sins or errors.

Grammar: αμαρτιαις is a noun in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of a verb or the object of a preposition. In English, it can be translated as "sins" or "errors".

Description: αμαρτιαις refers to sins or errors that one has committed. It is often used in a religious context, particularly in Christianity, to refer to the sins that one must confess and seek forgiveness for. In Greek literature, it can also refer to mistakes or errors in judgment.
