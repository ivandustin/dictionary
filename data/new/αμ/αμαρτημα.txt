Word: αμαρτημα (amartima)

Etymology: The word αμαρτημα (amartima) comes from the Greek verb αμαρτανω (amartano), which means "to miss the mark" or "to err". It is also related to the noun αμαρτια (amartia), which means "sin".

Grammar: αμαρτημα (amartima) is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is declined like other neuter nouns in the second declension, with the genitive form being αμαρτηματος (amartimatos).

Description: αμαρτημα (amartima) refers to a mistake, error, fault, or sin. It can be used in both a literal and figurative sense. In the context of religion, it is often used to refer to a sin or wrongdoing. In everyday language, it can refer to any kind of mistake or error.
