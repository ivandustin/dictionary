Word: αμφοτερους

Etymology:
The word αμφοτερους is derived from the Greek words αμφί (amphi) meaning "both" and τερος (teros) meaning "of two". It is a compound word that means "both of two".

Grammar:
αμφοτερους is a masculine accusative plural adjective in Greek. It is used to describe two masculine nouns in the accusative case.

Description:
αμφοτερους is used to refer to two things or people that are both of the same kind or category. It is often used to express a sense of equality or balance between the two things being described. For example, in the sentence "Οι αδελφοί αγόρασαν αμφότεροι ένα αυτοκίνητο" (The brothers both bought a car), αμφοτερους emphasizes that both brothers bought a car, rather than just one of them.
