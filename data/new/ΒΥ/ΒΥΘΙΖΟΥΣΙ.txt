Etymology: The word "βυθιζουσι" comes from the Greek verb "βυθίζω" (vuthizo), which means "to sink" or "to submerge."

Grammar: "βυθιζουσι" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "βυθίζω" (vuthizo). It is used to describe an action being performed by a group of people or things.

Description: "βυθιζουσι" describes the act of sinking or submerging. It can be used in various contexts, such as sinking a ship, submerging an object in water, or metaphorically referring to being overwhelmed or engulfed by something.
