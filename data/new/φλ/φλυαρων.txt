The word φλυαρών (phlyarón) is derived from the Greek verb φλυαρέω (phlyaréō), which means "to talk excessively or idly." The suffix -ών (-ón) is added to the present participle form of the verb to create an adjective that describes someone who is engaging in the action of the verb.

φλυαρών is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it were used to describe a feminine singular noun, it would be φλυαρούσα (phlyaroúsa).

Description:
φλυαρών is used to describe someone who talks excessively or idly. It can have a negative connotation, implying that the person is talking too much without saying anything of substance. It can also be used in a more neutral or even positive way, to describe someone who is talkative or sociable.
