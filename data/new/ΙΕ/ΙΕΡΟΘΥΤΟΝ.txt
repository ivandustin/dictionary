Etymology: The word "ιεροθυτον" comes from the Greek words "ιερός" (hieros), meaning "sacred" or "holy," and "θύτης" (thytes), meaning "sacrificer" or "offerer." 

Grammar: "Ιεροθυτον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is in the singular form.

Description: "Ιεροθυτον" refers to a sacrificial offering or a victim that is offered in a religious ceremony. It is commonly used in the context of ancient Greek religious practices and rituals.
