Grammar: "Ιερωσυνην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ιερωσυνην" refers to the concept of sacredness or holiness. It can be used to describe the state or quality of something being considered sacred or holy.
