Etymology: The word "ιεροσυλείς" comes from the Greek words "ιερός" (holy) and "συλλαμβάνω" (to take, to seize), meaning "those who seize/take what is holy."

Grammar: "Ιεροσυλείς" is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is masculine in gender.

Description: "Ιεροσυλείς" refers to individuals who commit the act of sacrilege, which involves violating or disrespecting something that is considered sacred or holy.
