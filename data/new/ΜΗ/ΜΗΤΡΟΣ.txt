Etymology: The word "μητρος" (mētros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *méh₂tēr, meaning "mother."

Grammar: In Greek, "μητρος" (mētros) is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of the noun "μήτηρ" (mētēr), which means "mother." The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "μητρος" (mētros) refers to the mother of someone. It is used to indicate the relationship between a person and their mother.
