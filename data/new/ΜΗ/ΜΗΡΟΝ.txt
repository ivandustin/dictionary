Etymology: The word "μηρον" (mēron) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*mēr-", meaning "thigh" or "upper leg".

Grammar: In Greek, "μηρον" (mēron) is a noun of neuter gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in various grammatical cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative.

Description: "Μηρον" (mēron) refers to the thigh or upper leg of a human or animal. It is the part of the leg between the hip and the knee. In anatomy, the term is often used to describe the muscles, bones, and other structures in this region.
