Etymology: The word "μηνυθείς" (mēnytheís) comes from the Greek verb "μηνύω" (mēnýō), which means "to accuse" or "to denounce."

Grammar: "Μηνυθείς" is the past participle form of the verb "μηνύω" (mēnýō), and it is used in the passive voice. In Greek grammar, the past participle is often used to form compound verb tenses or to express actions that have already been completed.

Description: "Μηνυθείς" is a Greek word that refers to someone who has been accused or denounced. It describes a person who has been the subject of an accusation or a formal charge.
