Etymology: The word "μητέρα" (mētéra) comes from the Ancient Greek word "μήτηρ" (mḗtēr), which means "mother".

Grammar: In Greek, "μητέρα" is a noun in the singular form. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Μητέρα" is the Greek word for "mother". It refers to a female parent who gives birth to or raises a child. The word is used to address or refer to one's own mother or any woman who fulfills the role of a mother.
