Etymology: The word "μηδεπω" (mēdepō) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the negative particle "μή" (mē) and the adverb "ἔπω" (epō), which means "yet" or "still." 

Grammar: "Μηδεπω" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to express negation or denial, indicating that something has not yet happened or is not yet the case. It is often translated as "not yet" or "still not."

Description: "Μηδεπω" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday speech to convey the idea of something not happening or being the case at a particular time. It is often used to express anticipation or expectation for something to happen in the future.
