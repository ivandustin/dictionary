Etymology: The word "μητρας" (mētras) comes from the Greek word "μήτηρ" (mētēr), meaning "mother."

Grammar: "Μητρας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship.

Description: "Μητρας" refers to the genitive form of the Greek word "μήτηρ" (mētēr), which means "mother." In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. Therefore, "μητρας" can be translated as "of the mother" or "mother's."
