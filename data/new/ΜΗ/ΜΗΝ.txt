Etymology: The word "μην" (mēn) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *mḗh₁n̥s, meaning "moon" or "month".

Grammar: In Greek, "μην" (mēn) is a noun that means "month". It is in the singular form. The plural form is "μήνες" (mēnes).

Description: "Μην" (mēn) refers to a unit of time in the Greek calendar, specifically a lunar month. It is used to measure the passage of time and is equivalent to approximately 29.5 days. In Greek mythology, the moon was associated with the goddess Selene.
