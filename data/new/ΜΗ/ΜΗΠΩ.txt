Etymology: The word "μηπω" (mēpō) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the negative particle "μή" (mē) and the adverb "πω" (pō), which means "yet" or "still." 

Grammar: "Μηπω" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to express negation or denial of an action that has not yet occurred. It is often translated as "not yet" or "still not" in English.

Description: "Μηπω" is used to indicate that something has not happened or is not the case at the present moment, but there is a possibility that it may happen in the future. It is commonly used in Greek to express anticipation or expectation.
