Etymology: The word "μηδε" (mēde) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the negative particle "μή" (mē) and the conjunction "δέ" (de), which means "but" or "and." 

Grammar: "Μηδε" is a conjunction in Greek grammar. It is used to connect two negative statements or clauses, indicating that both statements are true. It is often translated as "neither...nor" in English.

Description: "Μηδε" is used to express a negative correlation between two elements or actions. It is commonly used in Greek literature and speech to emphasize the absence or denial of multiple things or ideas.
