Etymology: The word "φωτισαντος" comes from the Greek verb "φωτίζω" (fotízo), which means "to illuminate" or "to enlighten". It is derived from the noun "φῶς" (fós), meaning "light".

Grammar: "Φωτισαντος" is the genitive form of the verb "φωτίζω" in the third person singular, aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Φωτισαντος" can be translated as "of the one who illuminated" or "of the one who enlightened" in English. It is used to refer to someone or something that has brought light or enlightenment to others.
