Etymology: The word "φωναί" (pronounced "fonai") comes from the Greek word "φωνή" (pronounced "foní"), which means "voice" or "sound".

Grammar: "Φωναί" is a noun in the plural form. It is the plural of "φωνή" and refers to voices or sounds.

Description: "Φωναί" is used to describe multiple voices or sounds. It can refer to the sounds made by humans, animals, or objects. In Greek mythology, the term "φωναί" is also used to describe the voices of the Muses, who were the goddesses of inspiration in the arts and sciences.
