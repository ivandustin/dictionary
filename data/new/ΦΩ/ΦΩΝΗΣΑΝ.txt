Etymology: The word "φωνησαν" (pronounced "fonisan") comes from the Greek verb "φωνέω" (pronounced "fono"), which means "to speak" or "to sound". 

Grammar: "Φωνησαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense of the verb "φωνέω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe past actions that are viewed as a whole, without specifying the duration or completion of the action.

Description: "Φωνησαν" is a verb in Greek that means "they spoke" or "they sounded". It refers to an action performed by a group of people in the past.
