Etymology: The word "φωτιζηι" comes from the Greek verb "φωτίζω" (fotízo), which means "to illuminate" or "to enlighten". It is derived from the noun "φῶς" (phṓs), meaning "light".

Grammar: "φωτιζηι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: φωτίζω (fotízo) - I illuminate
- Plural: φωτίζουμε (fotízoume) - We illuminate

Description: "φωτιζηι" refers to the act of illuminating or shedding light on something. It can be used both literally, to describe the action of providing light, and metaphorically, to describe the act of enlightening or bringing understanding to a subject or situation.
