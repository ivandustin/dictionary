Etymology: The word "φωνησαί" (pronounced "fonise") comes from the Greek verb "φωνέω" (pronounced "foneo"), which means "to speak" or "to sound". 

Grammar: "Φωνησαί" is the aorist tense, imperative mood, and second person singular form of the verb "φωνέω". It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to speak or make a sound.

Description: "Φωνησαί" is a Greek word that specifically instructs someone to speak or make a sound. It is a command form of the verb and is used to tell someone to use their voice or produce a sound.
