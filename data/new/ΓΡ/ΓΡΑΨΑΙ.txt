Etymology: The word "γραψαί" (grapsai) comes from the Greek verb "γράφω" (grapho), which means "to write". 

Grammar: "Γραψαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, subjunctive mood, third person singular form of the verb "γράφω" (grapho). The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The subjunctive mood is used to express possibility, necessity, or doubt.

Description: "Γραψαί" is a verb form that can be translated as "he/she/it wrote" or "he/she/it has written" in English. It is used to describe a completed action of writing in the past.
