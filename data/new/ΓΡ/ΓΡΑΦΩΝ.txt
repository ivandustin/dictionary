Etymology: The word "γραφων" (graphein) comes from the Greek verb "γραφω" (grapho), which means "to write". 

Grammar: "Γραφων" is the present participle form of the verb "γραφω" (grapho), which means "writing" or "the one who writes". It is used to describe an ongoing action of writing in the present tense.

Description: "Γραφων" refers to the act of writing or the person who is currently writing. It can be used to describe someone who is engaged in the process of writing something, such as a letter, a book, or any other form of written communication.
