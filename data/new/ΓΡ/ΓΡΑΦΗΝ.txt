Etymology: The word "γραφήν" (graphēn) comes from the Greek verb "γράφειν" (graphein), which means "to write". It is derived from the Greek noun "γράμμα" (gramma), meaning "letter" or "writing".

Grammar: "Γραφήν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case (nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.), number (singular, plural), and gender (masculine, feminine, neuter).

Description: "Γραφήν" refers to the act of writing or the written text itself. It can also be used to describe a piece of writing, such as a letter, document, or inscription. In a broader sense, it can refer to any form of written communication or expression.
