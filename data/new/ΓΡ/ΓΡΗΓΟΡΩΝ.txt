Etymology: The word "γρηγορών" comes from the Greek verb "γρηγορέω" (grēgoreō), which means "to be awake" or "to be watchful." It is derived from the noun "γρηγορία" (grēgoria), meaning "wakefulness" or "vigilance."

Grammar: "Γρηγορών" is the present participle form of the verb "γρηγορέω" in the masculine singular nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description: "Γρηγορών" can be translated as "awake" or "watchful" in English. It describes someone who is alert, vigilant, or attentive. It can also refer to someone who is physically awake or not sleeping.
