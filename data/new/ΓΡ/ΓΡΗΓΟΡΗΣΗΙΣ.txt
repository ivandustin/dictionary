Γρηγορησηίς (grēgorēsēis) is a Greek word that can be translated as "wakefulness" or "vigilance." It is derived from the verb γρηγορέω (grēgoreō), which means "to be awake" or "to be watchful." In Greek mythology, the goddess Nike was often depicted with wings and described as being in a state of γρηγορησηίς, symbolizing her constant vigilance and readiness for action. In grammar, γρηγορησηίς is a noun in the nominative case, singular number, and feminine gender.
