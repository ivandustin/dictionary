Etymology: The word "γρηγορουντας" (pronounced "grigorountas") comes from the Greek verb "γρηγορώ" (pronounced "grigoró"), which means "to be awake" or "to be vigilant". The suffix "-ουντας" (-ountas) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating continuous action.

Grammar: "Γρηγορουντας" is the present participle of the verb "γρηγορώ" in the masculine singular form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Γρηγορουντας" describes someone who is awake or vigilant. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is alert, attentive, or quick to respond.
