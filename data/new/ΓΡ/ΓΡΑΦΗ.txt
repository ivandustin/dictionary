Etymology: The word "γραφή" (graphē) comes from the Greek verb "γράφω" (graphō), which means "to write". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *gerbh- meaning "to scratch" or "to carve".

Grammar: In Greek, "γραφή" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has a genitive form of "γραφῆς" (graphēs). It can be used in the singular or plural form, depending on the context.

Description: "Γραφή" refers to the act of writing or the written word. It can also refer to a piece of writing, such as a book, document, or inscription. In a broader sense, it can encompass various forms of written communication, including literature, poetry, and even calligraphy.
