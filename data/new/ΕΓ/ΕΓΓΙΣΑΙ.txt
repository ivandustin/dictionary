Etymology: The word "εγγίσαι" comes from the Greek verb "εγγίζω" (engízo), which means "to approach" or "to draw near." It is derived from the noun "εγγύς" (engýs), meaning "near" or "close."

Grammar: "Εγγίσαι" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "εγγίζω" (engízo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for a specific subject or tense.

Description: "Εγγίσαι" is used to express the action of approaching or drawing near to something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as physical proximity, emotional closeness, or figurative meanings. The aorist tense suggests that the action of approaching has been completed in the past.
