Etymology: The word "εγηγερται" comes from the Greek verb "εγείρω" (egéiro), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". It is in the perfect tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.

Grammar: "Εγηγερται" is the third person singular form of the verb "εγείρω" in the perfect tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. The perfect tense indicates a completed action with ongoing results, the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "Εγηγερται" means "he/she/it has been awakened" or "he/she/it has risen" in English. It describes the state of someone or something that has been awakened or has risen from a lying or sleeping position.
