Etymology: The word "εγνω" (egno) comes from the Greek verb "γινώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand".

Grammar: "Εγνω" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "γινώσκω" (ginosko). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εγνω" is a verb form that indicates that someone or something "knew" or "understood" something in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it knew" or "he/she/it understood" in English.
