Etymology: The word "εγραφη" (egraphi) comes from the Greek verb "γράφω" (grapho), which means "to write". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, so "εγραφη" means "was written".

Grammar: "Εγραφη" is the third person singular past tense form of the verb "γράφω" (grapho). It is used to indicate that something was written in the past.

Description: "Εγραφη" is a verb form in the Greek language. It is used to talk about something that was written in the past.
