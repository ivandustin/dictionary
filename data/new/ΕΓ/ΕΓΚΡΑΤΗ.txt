Etymology: The word "εγκρατης" (egkrates) comes from the Greek roots "εν" (en), meaning "in" or "within," and "κρατος" (kratos), meaning "power" or "rule." Therefore, "εγκρατης" can be translated as "having power within oneself" or "self-controlled."

- Nominative: εγκρατης (egkrates)
- Genitive: εγκρατους (egkratous)
- Dative: εγκρατει (egkratei)
- Accusative: εγκρατη (egkrate)
- Vocative: εγκρατα (egkrata)

Plural:
- Nominative: εγκρατεις (egkrateis)
- Genitive: εγκρατων (egkraton)
- Dative: εγκρατεσι (egkratesi)
- Accusative: εγκρατεις (egkrateis)
- Vocative: εγκρατεις (egkrateis)

Description: "Εγκρατης" is a Greek word that refers to someone who has self-control, restraint, or mastery over their desires and passions. It is often used to describe a person who can resist temptations and maintain discipline in various aspects of life, such as food, drink, or other pleasures.
