Etymology: The word "εγκατελίπες" comes from the Greek verb "εγκαταλείπω" (egkataleípo), which means "to abandon" or "to leave behind". It is formed by combining the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "within", and the verb "καταλείπω" (kataleípo), meaning "to leave" or "to forsake".

Grammar: "Εγκατελίπες" is the second person singular form of the verb "εγκαταλείπω" in the aorist tense. It is used to address someone directly and means "you abandoned" or "you left behind".

Description: "Εγκατελίπες" is a Greek word that expresses the action of abandoning or leaving something or someone behind. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone who has abandoned a place, a person, or a responsibility.
