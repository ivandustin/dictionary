Etymology: The word "εγηγερμένον" comes from the Greek verb "εγείρω" (egíro), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". The word is formed by adding the prefix "εν-" (en-) and the suffix "-μένον" (-menon) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "Εγηγερμένον" is the neuter singular form of the adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The neuter form is used when the adjective refers to a neuter noun.

Description: "Εγηγερμένον" means "awakened" or "aroused" in Greek. It is used to describe something or someone that has been woken up or stirred from a state of rest or inactivity.
