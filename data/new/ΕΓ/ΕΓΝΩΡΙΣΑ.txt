Etymology: The word "εγνωρισα" (egnorisa) comes from the Greek verb "γνωρίζω" (gnorizo), which means "to introduce" or "to make known". It is derived from the noun "γνώριμος" (gnorimos), meaning "known" or "familiar".

Grammar: "Εγνωρισα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "γνωρίζω" (gnorizo). In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The past tense indicates that the action of introducing or making known happened in the past.

Description: "Εγνωρισα" is a Greek word that translates to "I introduced" or "I made known" in English. It is used to express the action of introducing someone or something to others or making someone or something known to others.
