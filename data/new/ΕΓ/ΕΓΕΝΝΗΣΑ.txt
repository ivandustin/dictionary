Etymology: The word "εγέννησα" comes from the Greek verb "γεννώ" (gennó), which means "to give birth" or "to beget." The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the first person singular past tense form.

Grammar: "Εγέννησα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "γεννώ" (gennó), which means "I gave birth" or "I begot." It belongs to the aorist tense, which is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εγέννησα" is a verb in Greek that specifically refers to the act of giving birth or begetting. It is used to describe the action of a female giving birth to a child or an animal giving birth to offspring.
