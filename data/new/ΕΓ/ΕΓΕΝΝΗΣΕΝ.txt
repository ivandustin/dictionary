Etymology: The word "εγεννησεν" comes from the Greek verb "γεννάω" (gennáo), which means "to give birth" or "to beget." The prefix "ε-" (e-) indicates the past tense, and the suffix "-εν" (-en) indicates the third person singular form.

Grammar: "εγεννησεν" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: "εγεννησεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "γεννάω" (gennáo) in the aorist tense. It is commonly translated as "he/she/it gave birth" or "he/she/it begot." This verb is often used in the context of childbirth or the act of bringing forth offspring.
