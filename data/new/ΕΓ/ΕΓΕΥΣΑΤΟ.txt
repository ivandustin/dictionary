Etymology: The word "εγευσατο" (egyso) comes from the Greek verb "γεύομαι" (geuomai), which means "to taste" or "to try." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʰew- meaning "to taste" or "to enjoy."

Grammar: "Εγευσατο" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, person, and number. The aorist tense is used to describe completed actions in the past.

Description: "Εγευσατο" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "γεύομαι" (geuomai). It means "he/she/it tasted" or "he/she/it tried" in English. This verb is commonly used to describe the act of experiencing the taste of something or trying something for the first time.
