Etymology: The word "εγνωκότες" comes from the Greek verb "γινώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand". The suffix "-κότες" is a participle ending that indicates past tense and active voice.

Grammar: "Εγνωκότες" is the plural form of the verb "γινώσκω" in the past perfect tense. It is used to refer to a group of people who have known or understood something in the past.

Description: "Εγνωκότες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "those who have known" or "those who have understood". It is often used in a philosophical or intellectual context to describe individuals who have gained knowledge or insight.
