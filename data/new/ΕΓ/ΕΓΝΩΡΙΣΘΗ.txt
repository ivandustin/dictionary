Etymology: The word "εγνωρισθη" (egnoristhe) comes from the Greek verb "γνωρίζω" (gnorizo), which means "to make known" or "to inform". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: "Εγνωρισθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive form of the verb "γνωρίζω" (gnorizo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Εγνωρισθη" is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and in which the subject was made known or informed about something. It is often translated as "he/she/it was made known" or "he/she/it was informed".
