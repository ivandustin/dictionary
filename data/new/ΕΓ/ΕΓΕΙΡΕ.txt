Etymology: The word "εγείρε" comes from the Greek verb "εγείρω" (egeíro), which means "to wake up" or "to raise up."

Grammar: "Εγείρε" is the imperative form of the verb "εγείρω" (egeíro) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to wake up or raise up.

Description: "Εγείρε" is a word used to command or instruct someone to wake up or raise up. It is commonly used in situations where someone needs to be awakened from sleep or needs to be lifted or raised from a lying or sitting position.
