Etymology: The word "εγεννηθης" (egennithis) comes from the Greek verb "γεννιέμαι" (genniémai), which means "to be born" or "to come into existence."

Grammar: "Εγεννηθης" is the second person singular form of the aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. It is used to refer to someone who was born or came into existence.

Description: "Εγεννηθης" is a Greek word that specifically means "you were born" or "you came into existence." It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to refer to the beginning of someone's existence or the act of being born.
