Etymology: The word "εγραψα" (egrapsa) comes from the Greek verb "γράφω" (grapho), which means "to write". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, so "εγραψα" (egrapsa) translates to "I wrote" in English.

Grammar: "Εγραψα" (egrapsa) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "γράφω" (grapho). In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The past tense is used to indicate an action that has already happened in the past.

Description: "Εγραψα" (egrapsa) is a simple verb form that expresses the action of writing in the past. It can be used to talk about something that you wrote or completed in the past.
