Grammar: "εγερθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "εγείρω" (egero). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "εγερθηναι" is used to express the action of being awakened or being raised. It can be used in various contexts, such as waking up from sleep, rising from a seated or lying position, or being raised from a lower to a higher position.
