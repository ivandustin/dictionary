Etymology: The word "εγείραι" (pronounced "egire") comes from the Greek verb "εγείρω" (pronounced "egiro"), which means "to wake up" or "to raise". 

Grammar: "Εγείραι" is the imperative form of the verb "εγείρω" in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to wake up or raise something.

Description: "Εγείραι" is a word that is used to command or instruct someone to wake up or raise something. It is commonly used in everyday speech and can be used in various contexts, such as waking someone up from sleep or asking someone to raise an object.
