Etymology: The word "εγνωρίσαν" comes from the Greek verb "γνωρίζω" (gnorízo), which means "to get to know" or "to become acquainted with". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, making it "εγνωρίσαν" (egnorísan), which translates to "they got to know" or "they became acquainted with".

Grammar: "Εγνωρίσαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "γνωρίζω" in the past tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings based on the subject and tense. The "-αν" ending indicates the past tense for the third person plural.

Description: "Εγνωρίσαν" is a verb in the Greek language that describes the action of getting to know or becoming acquainted with someone or something. It is used to indicate that a group of people (third person plural) got to know or became acquainted with someone or something in the past.
