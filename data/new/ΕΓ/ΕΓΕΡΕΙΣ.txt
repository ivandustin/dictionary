Etymology: The word "εγερείς" (egereis) comes from the Greek verb "εγείρω" (egeiro), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". 

Grammar: "Εγερείς" is the second person singular form of the verb "εγείρω" (egeiro) in the present tense. It is used when addressing someone directly and means "you wake up" or "you rise".

Description: "Εγερείς" is a Greek word that is used to command or urge someone to wake up or rise. It is commonly used in situations where someone needs to be awakened from sleep or needs to get up from a sitting or lying position.
