Etymology: The word "εγεννησαν" comes from the Greek verb "γεννώ" (gennó), which means "to give birth" or "to beget." The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, making it "εγέννησαν" (egenísan), which translates to "they gave birth" or "they begot."

Grammar: "Εγέννησαν" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "γεννώ" (gennó). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. In this case, "εγέννησαν" indicates that the action of giving birth or begetting was performed by a group of people in the past.

Description: "Εγέννησαν" is a verb in the Greek language that describes the action of giving birth or begetting. It is used to indicate that a group of people performed this action in the past.
