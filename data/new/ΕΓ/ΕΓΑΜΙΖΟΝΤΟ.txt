Etymology: The word "εγαμιζοντο" comes from the Greek verb "γαμίζω" (gamízo), which means "to marry." The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, so "εγαμιζοντο" translates to "they were getting married."

Grammar: "Εγαμιζοντο" is the third person plural form of the verb "γαμίζω" in the past tense. It is used to describe an action that multiple people were doing in the past, specifically getting married.

Description: "Εγαμιζοντο" is a Greek word that describes the action of multiple people getting married in the past. It is commonly used in narratives or historical contexts to talk about marriages that took place in the past.
