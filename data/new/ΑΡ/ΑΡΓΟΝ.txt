Etymology: The word "αργον" (argon) comes from the Greek word "αργός" (argos), meaning "lazy" or "inactive". It was named by the British scientist Lord Rayleigh and Scottish chemist Sir William Ramsay in 1894.

Grammar: In Greek, "αργον" (argon) is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the same form in both the nominative and accusative cases.

Description: Argon is a chemical element with the symbol Ar and atomic number 18. It is a noble gas and is the third-most abundant gas in the Earth's atmosphere, making up about 0.934% by volume. Argon is colorless, odorless, and tasteless. It is used in various applications, such as welding, lighting, and as a protective gas in the production of metals.
