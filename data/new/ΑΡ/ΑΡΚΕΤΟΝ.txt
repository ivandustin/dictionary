Etymology: The word "αρκετον" (arketon) comes from the Greek word "αρκετός" (arketos), meaning "enough" or "sufficient".

Grammar: "Αρκετον" (arketon) is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The neuter gender is used for objects or concepts that are neither masculine nor feminine. The singular form indicates that there is only one of the noun.

Description: "Αρκετον" (arketon) is used to describe something that is enough or sufficient. It can be used to express that there is an adequate amount of something or that a certain condition has been met.
