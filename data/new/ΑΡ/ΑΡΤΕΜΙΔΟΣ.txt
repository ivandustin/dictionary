Etymology: The word "αρτεμιδος" (artemidos) comes from the Greek mythology, specifically from the name of the goddess Artemis. 

Grammar: "Αρτεμιδος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αρτεμιδος" refers to something that belongs to or is related to Artemis, the Greek goddess of the hunt, wilderness, and childbirth. It can be used to describe various things associated with Artemis, such as her attributes, symbols, or places dedicated to her.
