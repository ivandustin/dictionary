Etymology: The word "αριστου" (aristou) comes from the Greek adjective "αριστος" (aristos), which means "excellent" or "best". 

Grammar: "Αριστου" is the genitive form of the word "αριστος" (aristos), which is used to indicate possession or origin. It is commonly used in Greek grammar to show the possessive relationship between nouns.

Description: "Αριστου" is a Greek word that is often used in formal or literary contexts. It is used to indicate possession or origin, similar to the English word "of". For example, "το βιβλίο του αριστου φίλου" (to vivlio tou aristou filou) means "the book of his best friend".
