Etymology: The word "αρχηί" (archēi) comes from the Greek word "ἀρχή" (archē), which means "beginning" or "origin".

Grammar: "Αρχηί" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Αρχηί" refers to the concept of "principle" or "rule" in Greek. It signifies the fundamental or initial element from which something originates or is derived. It can also denote the first cause or source of something.
