Etymology: The word "αρκετός" (arketos) comes from the Greek word "ἀρκετός" (arketos), which means "sufficient" or "enough."

Grammar: In Greek, "αρκετός" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used in the masculine, feminine, or neuter gender, and in the singular or plural form.

Description: "Αρκετός" is used to describe a quantity or amount that is sufficient or enough. It can also be used to express the idea of adequacy or satisfaction. It is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to convey the concept of "enough" or "sufficient."
