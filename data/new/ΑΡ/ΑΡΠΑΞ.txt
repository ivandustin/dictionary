Etymology: The word "αρπαξ" (ar-paks) comes from the Greek verb "αρπάζω" (ar-pazo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize". 

Grammar: "Αρπαξ" is a noun in the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension in Greek, and its genitive form is "αρπακτός" (ar-pak-tos). 

Description: "Αρπαξ" refers to a person who snatches or seizes something forcefully and quickly. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is greedy or opportunistic.
