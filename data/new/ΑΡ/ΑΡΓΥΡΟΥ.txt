Etymology: The word "αργυρού" (argyroú) comes from the Greek word "άργυρος" (árgyros), which means "silver". 

Grammar: "Αργυρού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αργυρού" refers to the genitive form of the noun "αργυρός" (argyrós), which means "silver" in Greek. It is used to indicate possession or origin related to silver.
