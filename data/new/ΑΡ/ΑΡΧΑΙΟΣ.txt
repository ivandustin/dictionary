Etymology: The word "αρχαίος" (archaios) comes from the Greek root "αρχή" (arche), meaning "beginning" or "origin". It is related to the word "αρχαίολογος" (archaiologos), which means "archaeologist" and refers to the study of ancient civilizations.

Grammar: In Greek, "αρχαίος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. For example, "αρχαίος" is the masculine form, "αρχαία" is the feminine form, and "αρχαίο" is the neuter form. These forms change depending on the noun they describe.

Description: The word "αρχαίος" is commonly translated as "ancient" in English. It is used to describe something that is old, from a distant past, or belonging to a previous era. It can refer to ancient civilizations, artifacts, traditions, or any other aspect of the past.
