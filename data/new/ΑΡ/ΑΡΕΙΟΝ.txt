Etymology: The word "αρειον" comes from the Greek word "Ἄρης" (Ares), which is the name of the Greek god of war.

Grammar: "Αρειον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the neuter gender and singular number.

Description: "Αρειον" refers to the Areopagus, which is a prominent rock outcropping in Athens, Greece. In ancient times, it served as a meeting place for the city's council and court. The Areopagus was known for its role in the administration of justice and the deliberation of important matters. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of ancient Athenian democracy.
