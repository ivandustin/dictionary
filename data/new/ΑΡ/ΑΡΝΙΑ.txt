Etymology: The word "αρνία" (arnia) comes from the Greek word "αρνί" (arni), which means "lamb". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erh₃- ("to move, to wander"), which is also the origin of the English word "errant".

Grammar: "Αρνία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "αρνίας" (arnias). In the plural form, it becomes "αρνίες" (arnies).

Description: "Αρνία" refers to a female lamb, a young sheep. It is commonly used to describe a lamb that is less than one year old. Lambs are often associated with innocence, purity, and gentleness. In Greek culture, lamb is also a traditional food consumed during Easter celebrations.
