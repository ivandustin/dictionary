Etymology: The word "αρης" (aris) comes from the Greek mythology, where it is the name of the Greek god of war, Ares. 

Grammar: "Αρης" (aris) is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form "Αρεως" (areos). 

Description: In Greek mythology, Ares is the son of Zeus and Hera. He is known as the god of war, violence, and bloodshed. Ares is often depicted as a fierce and brutal warrior, and he is associated with the chaos and destruction of war. He is not as revered as some of the other gods in Greek mythology, as he represents the negative aspects of war.
