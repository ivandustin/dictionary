Etymology: The word "αρτεμις" (artemis) comes from the Greek mythology, where Artemis was the goddess of the hunt, wilderness, and childbirth.

Grammar: In Greek, "αρτεμις" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "αρτεμιδος" (artemidos).

Description: "Αρτεμις" is a name commonly used for girls in Greece. It is associated with qualities such as strength, independence, and nature. In Greek mythology, Artemis was known for her skill in archery and her connection to the natural world.
