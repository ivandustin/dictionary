Etymology: The word "αργοί" (argoi) comes from the Greek word "αργός" (argos), which means "lazy" or "idle". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "without", and the root "ἔργον" (ergon), meaning "work".

Grammar: "Αργοί" is the plural form of the adjective "αργός" (argos), which means "lazy" or "idle". It is used to describe multiple people or things that are characterized by a lack of activity or motivation.

Description: "Αργοί" is an adjective in Greek that describes individuals or things that are lazy or idle. It is often used to criticize or express disapproval towards someone's lack of productivity or motivation.
