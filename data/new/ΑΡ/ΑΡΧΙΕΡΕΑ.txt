Etymology: The word "αρχιερεα" (archierea) comes from the Greek words "αρχι" (archi), meaning "chief" or "high-ranking," and "ερεα" (erea), meaning "priest." It refers to a high-ranking priest in the Eastern Orthodox Church.

Grammar: "Αρχιερεα" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. 

Description: "Αρχιερεα" is a title given to a high-ranking priest in the Eastern Orthodox Church. The archierea is responsible for performing important religious ceremonies, administering sacraments, and overseeing the clergy. They hold a position of authority and are often involved in the governance of the church.
