Etymology: The word "αρχιτελώνης" comes from the Greek roots "αρχή" (arche), meaning "chief" or "leader," and "τελώνης" (telónis), meaning "tax collector." 

Grammar: "Αρχιτελώνης" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Αρχιτελώνης" refers to a chief or head tax collector. In ancient Greece, tax collectors were responsible for collecting taxes from the citizens on behalf of the government. The prefix "αρχι-" indicates that the person holds a higher position or has authority over other tax collectors.
