Etymology: The word "αρπαζειν" (arpaizein) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "ἁρπάζειν" (harpazein), which means "to snatch" or "to seize".

Grammar: "Αρπαζειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an action happening in the present, the active voice shows that the subject performs the action, and the infinitive form expresses the verb in its most basic form.

Description: "Αρπαζειν" is a Greek verb that means "to snatch" or "to seize". It implies a quick and forceful action of taking something or someone by force or with great speed. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing physical actions, capturing someone's attention, or acquiring something in a swift manner.
