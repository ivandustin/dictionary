Etymology: The word "αρχιποιμένος" comes from the Greek roots "αρχή" (arche), meaning "chief" or "leader," and "ποιμένος" (poimenos), meaning "shepherd." 

Grammar: "Αρχιποιμένος" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Αρχιποιμένος" is a Greek word that refers to the highest-ranking bishop or archbishop in the Orthodox Church. This title is typically given to the head of an autocephalous (self-governing) church or a metropolitan bishop who oversees multiple dioceses. The αρχιποιμένος is responsible for the spiritual leadership and administration of the church under their jurisdiction.
