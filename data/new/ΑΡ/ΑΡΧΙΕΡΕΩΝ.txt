Etymology: The word "αρχιερεών" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the words "αρχή" (arche), meaning "chief" or "ruler," and "ἱερεύς" (hiereus), meaning "priest." 

Grammar: "Αρχιερεών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes "αρχιερείς" (archiereis).

Description: "Αρχιερεών" refers to the highest-ranking priests in the Greek Orthodox Church. They hold significant religious authority and are responsible for performing sacred rituals and administering sacraments. The title is often translated as "archbishop" or "high priest" in English.
