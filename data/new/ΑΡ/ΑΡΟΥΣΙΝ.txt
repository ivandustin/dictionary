Etymology: The word "αρουσιν" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "αρουσω" (arouso), which means "to plow" or "to till the soil."

Grammar: "Αρουσιν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the third declension. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "αρουσιν."

Description: "Αρουσιν" refers to the act of plowing or tilling the soil. It is commonly used in the context of agriculture and farming.
