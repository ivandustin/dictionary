Etymology: The word "αρπαξιν" (arpanxin) comes from the Greek verb "αρπάζω" (arpazo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize". 

Grammar: "Αρπαξιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence (nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.), as well as their number (singular or plural).

Description: "Αρπαξιν" refers to a specific instance or act of snatching or seizing. It can be used to describe the action of forcefully taking something away from someone or grabbing something quickly.
