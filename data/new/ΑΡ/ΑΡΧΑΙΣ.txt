Etymology: The word "αρχαίς" (archaís) comes from the Greek word "ἀρχαῖος" (archaîos), which means "ancient" or "old".

Grammar: "Αρχαίς" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the first declension and has the nominative singular form "αρχαίς". In the plural form, it becomes "αρχαίς".

Description: "Αρχαίς" refers to something ancient or old. It can be used to describe objects, buildings, or any other things that have been around for a long time.
