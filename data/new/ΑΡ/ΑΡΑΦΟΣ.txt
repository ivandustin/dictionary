Etymology: The word "αραφος" (araphos) comes from the Greek word "αραφη" (araphe), which means "seam" or "stitch". It is derived from the verb "αρασσω" (arasso), meaning "to sew" or "to stitch".

Grammar: "Αραφος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "αραφος". In the plural, it becomes "αραφοι" (araphoi).

Description: "Αραφος" refers to a seam or stitch in sewing. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is well-constructed or tightly knit.
