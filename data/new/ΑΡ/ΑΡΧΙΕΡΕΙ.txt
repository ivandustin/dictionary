Etymology: The word "αρχιερεί" comes from the Greek words "αρχή" (archi), meaning "chief" or "principal," and "ιερεία" (iereia), meaning "priesthood" or "sacred office." 

Grammar: "Αρχιερεί" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Αρχιερεί" refers to the high priesthood or the office of the high priest in the Greek Orthodox Church. The high priest is the highest-ranking clergy member and holds significant religious authority.
