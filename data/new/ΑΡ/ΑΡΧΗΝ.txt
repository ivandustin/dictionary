Etymology: The word "αρχην" (archēn) comes from the Greek word "ἀρχή" (archē), which means "beginning" or "origin".

Grammar: "Αρχην" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative form of the noun "αρχή" (archē), which is a feminine noun in Greek.

Description: "Αρχην" refers to the beginning or the origin of something. It can also mean the first cause or the principle from which something originates. In Greek philosophy, "αρχην" is often used to describe the ultimate source or the fundamental principle of the universe.
