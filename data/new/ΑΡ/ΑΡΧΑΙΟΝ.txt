Etymology: The word "αρχαίον" (archaion) comes from the Greek adjective "ἀρχαῖος" (archaios), meaning "ancient" or "old."

Grammar: In Greek, "αρχαίον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative case ending "-ον."

Description: "Αρχαίον" refers to something ancient or old. It can be used to describe objects, buildings, artifacts, or any other thing that has existed for a long time.
