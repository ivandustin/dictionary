Etymology: The word "αριθμησαί" comes from the Greek verb "αριθμέω" (arithmeo), which means "to count" or "to number". The suffix "-σαι" is the second person singular imperative form, which gives the meaning "you count" or "you number".

Grammar: "αριθμησαί" is a verb in the second person singular imperative form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood. The second person singular imperative is used to give a command or instruction to someone.

Description: The word "αριθμησαί" is used to tell someone to count or number something. It is a command form of the verb and is used when addressing a single person.
