Etymology: The word "αρνας" (arnas) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἀρνός" (arnos), which means "lamb".

Grammar: In Greek, "αρνας" is a noun of masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "αρνα".

Description: "Αρνας" refers to a male lamb, a young sheep. It is commonly used to describe a young sheep that has not yet reached adulthood. In Greek culture, lambs are often associated with innocence and purity.
