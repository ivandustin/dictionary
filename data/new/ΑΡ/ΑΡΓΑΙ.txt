Etymology: The word "αργαί" (argai) comes from the Greek word "ἀργός" (argos), meaning "lazy" or "idle". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "ἀ-" (a-), meaning "not", and the root "ἔργον" (ergon), meaning "work".

- Nominative: αργή (argi)
- Genitive: αργῆς (argēs)
- Dative: αργῇ (argēi)
- Accusative: αργήν (argin)
- Vocative: αργή (argē)

Plural:
- Nominative: αργαί (argai)
- Genitive: αργῶν (argōn)
- Dative: αργαῖς (argais)
- Accusative: αργάς (argas)
- Vocative: αργαί (argai)

Description: "Αργαί" is an adjective in Greek that means "lazy" or "idle". It is used to describe someone or something that is not active or productive.
