Etymology: The word "αρχηται" comes from the Greek word "αρχή" (arche), meaning "beginning" or "origin".

Grammar: "αρχηται" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "αρχηται" refers to the rulers or leaders in ancient Greek city-states. They were typically elected or appointed to govern and make decisions on behalf of the community. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of authority figure or leader.
