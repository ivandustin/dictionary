Etymology: The word "αρχείν" (archein) comes from the Greek verb "αρχω" (archo), which means "to begin" or "to rule". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂erǵʰ-.

Grammar: "Αρχείν" is an infinitive verb form in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is classified as a thematic verb. It is commonly used in the present tense to express the action of beginning or ruling.

Description: "Αρχείν" is a versatile verb in Greek that can have various meanings depending on the context. It can refer to the act of starting or commencing something, as well as the act of ruling or governing. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the initiation or leadership of a particular action or process.
