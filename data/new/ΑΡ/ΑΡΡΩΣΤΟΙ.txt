Etymology: The word "αρρωστοι" (arrōstoi) comes from the Greek word "ἄρρωστος" (arrhōstos), which means "sick" or "ill."

Grammar: "αρρωστοι" is the plural form of the noun "αρρωστος" (arrhōstos), which means "sick person" or "patient" in Greek. It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns.

Description: "αρρωστοι" is used to refer to multiple sick or ill individuals. It can be used in various contexts, such as in medical settings or when discussing a group of people who are unwell.
