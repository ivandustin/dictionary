Etymology: The word "αρχής" (archēs) comes from the Greek word "ἀρχή" (archē), which means "beginning" or "origin".

Grammar: "Αρχής" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Αρχής" is a Greek word that can be translated as "of the beginning" or "of the origin". It is often used in phrases or expressions to refer to the starting point or the source of something.
