Etymology: The word "αροτρον" (arotron) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἄροτρον" (árotron), which means "plow". 

Grammar: In Greek, "αροτρον" is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and its nominative singular form is "αροτρον". 

Description: "Αροτρον" refers to a plow, which is a farming tool used for turning over soil in preparation for planting crops. It typically consists of a strong blade or blades that cut into the ground and a handle or handles for guiding and controlling the plow. The plow has been used for centuries in agriculture to break up and loosen the soil, making it easier to sow seeds and promote plant growth.
