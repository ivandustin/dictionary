Etymology: The word "αρειού" (areiou) comes from the Greek word "αρειος" (areios), which means "of Ares." Ares is the Greek god of war.

Grammar: "Αρειού" (areiou) is a genitive form of the word "αρειος" (areios). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αρειού" (areiou) is an adjective that describes something as being related to or belonging to Ares. It can be used to describe various things associated with war or the god Ares.
