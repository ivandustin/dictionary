Etymology: The word "αρτος" (artos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂erh₃- meaning "to bake" or "bread". 

Grammar: In Greek, "αρτος" (artos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case when used as the subject of a sentence. The plural form of "αρτος" is "αρτοι" (artoi).

Description: "Αρτος" (artos) means "bread" in Greek. It refers to a staple food made from flour, water, and yeast or sourdough starter, which is typically baked. Bread is a common food in many cultures and is often consumed as a part of meals or used as a base for sandwiches.
