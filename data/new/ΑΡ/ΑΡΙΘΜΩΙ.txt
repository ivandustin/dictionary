Etymology: The word "αριθμώ" (arithmó) comes from the Greek verb "αριθμέω" (arithméo), which means "to count" or "to number." It is derived from the Greek noun "ἀριθμός" (arithmós), meaning "number."

Grammar: "Αριθμώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Αριθμώ (I count)
- Αριθμείς (You count)
- Αριθμεί (He/She/It counts)
- Αριθμούμε (We count)
- Αριθμείτε (You count)
- Αριθμούν (They count)

Description: "Αριθμώ" is used to express the action of counting or numbering. It can be used in various contexts, such as counting objects, people, or occurrences. It is a versatile verb that is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts.
