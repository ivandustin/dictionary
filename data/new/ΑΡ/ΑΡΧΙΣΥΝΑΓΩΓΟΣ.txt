Etymology: The word "αρχισυναγωγος" comes from the Greek roots "αρχι-" meaning "chief" or "leader" and "συναγωγη" meaning "synagogue". 

Grammar: "αρχισυναγωγος" is a noun in the masculine gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their case, number, and gender. 

Description: "αρχισυναγωγος" refers to the leader or chief of a synagogue. In Jewish tradition, the archisynagogos is responsible for leading the congregation in prayer and overseeing the activities of the synagogue.
