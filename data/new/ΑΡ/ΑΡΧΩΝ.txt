Etymology: The word "αρχων" (archon) comes from the Greek verb "αρχω" (archo), which means "to rule" or "to govern". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂erǵ- which means "to lead" or "to command".

Grammar: In Greek, "αρχων" (archon) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "αρχοντες" (archontes).

Description: In ancient Greece, an "αρχων" (archon) was a high-ranking magistrate or ruler. They were elected officials who held various administrative and judicial responsibilities. The archons played a significant role in the government and legal system of Athens and other Greek city-states.
