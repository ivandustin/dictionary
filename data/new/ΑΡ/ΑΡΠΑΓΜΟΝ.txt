Etymology: The word "αρπαγμόν" (ar-pag-mon) comes from the Greek verb "αρπάζω" (ar-pa-zo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂rebʰ-.

Grammar: "Αρπαγμόν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender in Greek. It belongs to the second declension class of nouns.

Description: "Αρπαγμόν" refers to an act of snatching or seizing something forcefully or violently. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a sudden and aggressive action or behavior.
