Etymology: The word "αρχή" (archē) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀρχή" (archē), meaning "beginning" or "origin".

Grammar: In Greek, "αρχή" (archē) is a feminine noun. It is in the nominative case and singular form.

Description: "Αρχή" (archē) has multiple meanings in Greek. It can refer to the beginning or starting point of something, such as the beginning of a story or the origin of a concept. It can also mean power, authority, or leadership, as in being in charge or having control over something. Additionally, "αρχή" (archē) can refer to a principle, rule, or fundamental concept in various fields, such as philosophy, mathematics, or science.
