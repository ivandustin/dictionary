Etymology: The word "αρχιτεκτον" (architekton) comes from the Greek words "αρχή" (arche), meaning "chief" or "master," and "τέκτων" (tekton), meaning "builder" or "craftsman." Therefore, "αρχιτεκτον" can be translated as "master builder" or "chief craftsman."

Grammar: In Greek, "αρχιτεκτον" is a noun of masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The plural form is "αρχιτέκτονες" (architektones).

Description: "Αρχιτεκτον" refers to an architect, someone who designs and oversees the construction of buildings or other structures. Architects are responsible for creating functional and aesthetically pleasing designs, considering factors such as safety, sustainability, and the needs of the users. They work closely with clients, engineers, and construction teams to bring their designs to life.
