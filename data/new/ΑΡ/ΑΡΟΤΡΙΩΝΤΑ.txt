Etymology: The word "αροτριωντα" comes from the Greek verb "αροτριώνω" (arotrióno), which means "to plow" or "to till the land." 

Grammar: "Αροτριωντα" is the present participle form of the verb "αροτριώνω" in the plural masculine gender. It is used to describe multiple subjects who are currently plowing or tilling the land.

Description: "Αροτριωντα" refers to a group of individuals who are actively engaged in the act of plowing or tilling the land. It can be used to describe farmers or agricultural workers who are using a plow to prepare the soil for planting crops.
