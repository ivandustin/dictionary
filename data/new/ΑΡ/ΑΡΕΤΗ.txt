Etymology: The word "αρετή" (aretē) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the root "αρ-" (ar-), which means "excellence" or "virtue".

Grammar: In Greek, "αρετή" (aretē) is a feminine noun. It is in the singular form.

Description: "Αρετή" (aretē) is a concept that refers to moral excellence or virtue. It encompasses qualities such as courage, wisdom, justice, and self-control. In Ancient Greek philosophy, "αρετή" (aretē) was considered a fundamental aspect of living a good and fulfilling life. It was believed that cultivating virtues would lead to personal happiness and the well-being of society.
