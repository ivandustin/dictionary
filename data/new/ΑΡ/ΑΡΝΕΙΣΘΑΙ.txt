Etymology: The word "αρνεισθαι" comes from the Greek verb "αρνέομαι" (arneomai), which means "to deny" or "to refuse." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂er- ("to be, exist").

Grammar: "αρνεισθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "αρνέομαι" (arneomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the voice (active, middle, or passive), tense, mood, and person. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "αρνεισθαι" is a Greek verb that means "to deny" or "to refuse." It is used to express the act of rejecting or refusing something, often in a deliberate or intentional manner. The middle/passive form suggests that the subject is denying or refusing something for their own benefit or because they are being acted upon.
