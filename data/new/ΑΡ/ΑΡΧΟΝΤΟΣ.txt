Grammar: "Αρχοντος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Αρχοντος" refers to someone who holds a position of authority or leadership. It can also be used to describe a ruler, governor, or chief. In ancient Greece, an "αρχοντος" was a magistrate or a high-ranking official who had power and influence in the city-state.
