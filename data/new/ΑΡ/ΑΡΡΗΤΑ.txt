Etymology: The word "αρρητα" (arreta) comes from the Greek word "αρρητος" (arretos), which means "unspoken" or "unsaid". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the verb "ρητος" (retos), meaning "spoken" or "said".

Grammar: "αρρητα" (arreta) is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple unsaid things.

Description: "αρρητα" (arreta) refers to things that are left unsaid or unspoken. It can be used to describe thoughts, feelings, or information that is not expressed verbally.
