Etymology: The word "αρχιτρικλίνος" comes from the Greek words "αρχή" (archi), meaning "chief" or "principal," and "τρίκλινος" (triklinos), meaning "triclinium" or "dining room." 

Grammar: "Αρχιτρικλίνος" is a noun in the masculine gender. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension. 

Description: "Αρχιτρικλίνος" refers to the chief or principal person who presides over a triclinium or dining room. In ancient Greece, the triclinium was a room where guests would recline on couches while dining. The "αρχιτρικλίνος" would have been responsible for overseeing the dining arrangements and ensuring that everything ran smoothly.
