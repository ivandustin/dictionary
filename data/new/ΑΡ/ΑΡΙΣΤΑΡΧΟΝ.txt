Etymology: The word "αρισταρχον" comes from the Greek words "αριστος" (aristos) meaning "best" and "αρχον" (archon) meaning "ruler" or "leader". 

Grammar: "αρισταρχον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "αρισταρχον" refers to a person who is considered the best or the most excellent ruler or leader. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is highly skilled or talented in a particular field.
