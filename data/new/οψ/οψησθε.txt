Word: οψησθε

Etymology:
The word οψησθε is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and second person plural form. It is derived from the root word οπτομαι (optomai), which means "to see" or "to observe."

Grammar:
As mentioned, οψησθε is a verb in the aorist tense and second person plural form. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The second person plural form indicates that the action is being directed towards a group of people.

Description:
The word οψησθε can be translated to mean "you saw" or "you observed" in English. It is often used in the context of recounting a past event or experience. For example, one might say "οψησθε τον ήλιον στην παραλία;" which translates to "did you see the sun at the beach?"
