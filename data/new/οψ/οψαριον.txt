Word: οψαριον

Etymology:
The word οψαριον comes from the Greek word οψαρός (opsaros), which means "fish". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peys- which means "fish".

Grammar:
In Greek, οψαριον is a neuter noun. Its plural form is οψάρια (opsaria). It is often used in the singular form to refer to a small fish or a fish dish.

Description:
Οψαριον is a Greek word that refers to a small fish or a fish dish. It is a popular food item in Greece and is often served fried or grilled. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to something small or insignificant.
