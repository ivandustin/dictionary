Word: οψωνιοις

Etymology: The word οψωνιοις comes from the Greek word "ὀψώνιον" (opsōnion), which means "a relish or delicacy eaten with bread." The suffix "-οις" (-ois) indicates the dative plural case, which means "to/for the delicacies."

Grammar: οψωνιοις is a noun in the dative plural case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: οψωνιοις refers to the delicacies or relishes that are eaten with bread. It can also refer to the money or provisions that are given to soldiers for the purchase of food.
