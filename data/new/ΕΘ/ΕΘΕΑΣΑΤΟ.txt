Etymology: The word "εθεασατο" (ethēasato) comes from the Greek verb "θεάομαι" (theaomai), which means "to see" or "to behold". It is in the aorist tense, indicative mood, and middle voice.

Grammar: "εθεασατο" is the third person singular form of the verb "θεάομαι" in the aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself.

Description: "εθεασατο" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it saw" or "he/she/it beheld". It is used to describe an action of seeing or observing something in the past.
