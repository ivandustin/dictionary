Etymology: The word "εθνους" (ethnous) comes from the Greek word "ἔθνος" (ethnos), which means "nation" or "people".

Grammar: "Εθνους" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "εθνους".

Description: "Εθνους" refers to a group of people who share a common cultural, linguistic, or historical background. It can also be used to describe a nation or a people.
