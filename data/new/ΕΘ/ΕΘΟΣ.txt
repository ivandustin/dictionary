Etymology: The word "εθος" (ethos) comes from the Greek language and has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European word "swedh-", meaning "custom" or "habit".

Grammar: In Greek, "εθος" is a neuter noun of the second declension. It is the nominative singular form, and its genitive form is "εθους" (ethous).

Description: "Εθος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "custom", "habit", or "character". It refers to the customary behavior, values, and beliefs of a particular group or society. It encompasses the traditions, practices, and moral principles that shape the collective identity and behavior of a community.
