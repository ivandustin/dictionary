Etymology: The word "εθεασαντο" comes from the Greek verb "θεάομαι" (theaomai), which means "to see" or "to behold". It is derived from the noun "θέα" (thea), meaning "a view" or "a sight".

Grammar: "εθεασαντο" is the third person plural, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "θεάομαι" (theaomai). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the middle voice suggests that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit.

Description: "εθεασαντο" is a Greek verb that means "they saw" or "they beheld". It is used to describe an action of seeing or observing something in the past.
