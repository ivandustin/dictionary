Etymology: The word "εθαυμασαν" (ethaumasan) comes from the Greek verb "θαυμάζω" (thaumazo), which means "to marvel" or "to wonder". It is derived from the noun "θαῦμα" (thauma), meaning "wonder" or "marvel".

Grammar: "εθαυμασαν" is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb "θαυμάζω" (thaumazo). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "εθαυμασαν" is a Greek word that translates to "they marveled" or "they wondered" in English. It is used to describe the action of a group of people expressing astonishment or admiration for something.
