Etymology: The word "πιπτοντες" comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pipto), which means "to fall". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, meaning "to fall".

Grammar: "πιπτοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "πίπτω" (pipto) in Greek. It is in the masculine plural nominative case. The present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions.

Description: "πιπτοντες" describes a group of people or objects that are currently falling. It can be translated as "falling" or "those who are falling" in English.
