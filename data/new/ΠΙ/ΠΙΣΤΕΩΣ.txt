Etymology: The word "πιστεύω" (pistevo) comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pistevo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". It is derived from the noun "πίστις" (pistis), meaning "faith" or "belief".

Grammar: "Πιστεύω" is a first-person singular present tense verb in Greek. It belongs to the class of verbs that end in "-ω" and follow the regular conjugation pattern. In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows:

- Πιστεύω (pistevo) - I believe
- Πιστεύεις (pisteveis) - You believe (singular)
- Πιστεύει (pistevei) - He/she/it believes
- Πιστεύουμε (pistevoyme) - We believe
- Πιστεύετε (pistevete) - You believe (plural)
- Πιστεύουν (pistevoyn) - They believe

Description: "Πιστεύω" is a verb that expresses the act of believing or having faith in something or someone. It is commonly used in religious or spiritual contexts, but can also be used in a general sense to indicate trust or confidence in a person, idea, or concept.
