Etymology: The word "πιστοίς" (pistois) comes from the Greek adjective "πιστός" (pistos), which means "faithful" or "loyal". 

Grammar: "Πιστοίς" is the plural form of the adjective "πιστός" (pistos) in the dative case. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Πιστοίς" is a Greek word that can be translated as "to the faithful" or "to the loyal ones". It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to those who are faithful or devoted to a particular belief or cause.
