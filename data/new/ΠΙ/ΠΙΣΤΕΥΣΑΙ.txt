Etymology: The word "πιστεύσαι" (pisteúsai) comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pisteúō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". It is derived from the noun "πίστις" (pístis), meaning "faith" or "belief".

Grammar: "Πιστεύσαι" is the second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb "πιστεύω" (pisteúō). In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: "Πιστεύσαι" is a command form that translates to "Believe!" or "Have faith!" in English. It is used to urge someone to believe or have faith in something.
