Etymology: The word "πιστευοντες" (pisteuontes) comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pisteuō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The suffix "-οντες" (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to those who are currently believing or having faith.

Grammar: "Πιστευοντες" is the plural form of the present participle of the verb "πιστεύω" (pisteuō). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Πιστευοντες" is a Greek word that means "believers" or "those who have faith". It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to individuals who have a strong belief in a particular religion or doctrine.
