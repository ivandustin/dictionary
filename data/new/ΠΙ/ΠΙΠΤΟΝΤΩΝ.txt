Etymology: The word "πιπτοντων" comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pipto), which means "to fall". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, meaning "to fall".

Grammar: "πιπτοντων" is the present participle form of the verb "πίπτω" (pipto) in Greek. It is in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number.

Description: "πιπτοντων" is used to describe a group of people or objects that are currently falling. It indicates ongoing action in the present tense.
