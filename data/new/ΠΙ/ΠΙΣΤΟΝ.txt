Grammar: In Greek, "πιστον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is the accusative form of the word, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Πιστον" refers to a piston, which is a cylindrical component that moves up and down inside a cylinder in an engine or pump. It is typically used to transfer force from expanding gas in the cylinder to a rotating crankshaft. Pistons are commonly found in internal combustion engines, such as those used in cars and motorcycles.
