Etymology: The word "πιθανολογία" comes from the Greek words "πιθανός" (pithanós), meaning "probable," and "λογία" (logía), meaning "study" or "discourse." 

Grammar: "Πιθανολογία" is a noun in the Greek language. It is feminine in gender and singular in number.

Description: "Πιθανολογία" refers to the study or discourse of probability. It is the branch of mathematics that deals with the likelihood of events occurring. In everyday language, it can also refer to the act of making educated guesses or predictions based on available information.
