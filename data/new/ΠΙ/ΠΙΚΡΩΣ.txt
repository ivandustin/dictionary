Etymology: The word "πικρός" (pikros) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πικρός" (pikros), meaning "bitter".

Grammar: In Greek, "πικρός" (pikros) is an adjective. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and declines accordingly in different cases and numbers.

Description: "Πικρός" (pikros) is used to describe something that has a bitter taste or flavor. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person or situation that is unpleasant, harsh, or causing feelings of sadness or disappointment.
