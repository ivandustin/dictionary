Etymology: The word "πινητε" (pinete) comes from the Greek verb "πίνω" (pino), which means "to drink". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃- ("to drink").

Grammar: "Πινητε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "πίνω" (pino), which means "you all drink". In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the subject and tense.

Description: "Πινητε" is a command or instruction addressed to a group of people, telling them to drink. It is used when the speaker wants to express the idea of drinking as an action that should be performed by multiple individuals.
