Etymology: The word "πιστεύειν" (pisteúein) comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pisteúō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". 

Grammar: "Πιστεύειν" is the present tense form of the verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular form, which means it is used when referring to a single person or thing that believes or has faith.

Description: "Πιστεύειν" is a Greek word that expresses the act of believing or having faith. It is commonly used in religious or philosophical contexts to refer to the act of placing trust or confidence in something or someone.
