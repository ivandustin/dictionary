Etymology: The word "πιστεύεις" (pisteúeis) comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pisteúō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith." It is derived from the noun "πίστις" (pístis), meaning "faith" or "belief."

Grammar: "Πιστεύεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense in Greek. It is used when addressing one person and means "you believe" or "you have faith."

Description: "Πιστεύεις" is a verb in Greek that expresses the act of believing or having faith. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing one's belief in a religious or philosophical sense, trusting someone or something, or having confidence in a particular idea or concept.
