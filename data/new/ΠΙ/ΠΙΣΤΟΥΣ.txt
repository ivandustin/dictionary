Etymology: The word "πιστούς" (pistous) comes from the Greek word "πίστη" (pistē), meaning "faith" or "belief". It is derived from the verb "πιστεύω" (pisteuō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith".

Grammar: "Πιστούς" is the plural form of the adjective "πιστός" (pistos), which means "faithful" or "loyal". In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "πιστούς" is used to describe plural masculine nouns.

Description: "Πιστούς" is an adjective that is used to describe a group of people who are faithful, loyal, or trustworthy. It can be used to describe individuals who are reliable and can be trusted to fulfill their commitments or obligations.
