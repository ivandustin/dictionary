Etymology: The word "πιστωτής" (pistotis) comes from the Greek verb "πιστόω" (pisto-o), which means "to trust" or "to believe." It is derived from the noun "πίστις" (pistis), meaning "faith" or "trust."

Grammar: "Πιστωτής" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "πιστωτές" (pistotes).

Description: "Πιστωτής" refers to a person or entity that lends money or extends credit to others. It can be translated as "creditor" or "lender" in English.
