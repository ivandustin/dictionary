Etymology: The word "πινουσιν" (pinousin) comes from the Greek verb "πίνω" (pino), which means "to drink". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃- ("to drink").

Grammar: "Πινουσιν" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "πίνω" (pino), which means "they drink" in Greek. It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: "Πινουσιν" is a verb form used to describe the action of drinking performed by a group of people or things. It indicates that multiple individuals are currently engaged in the act of drinking.
