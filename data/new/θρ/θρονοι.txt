Word: θρονοι

Etymology:
The word θρονοι (thronoi) is a plural form of the Greek noun θρόνος (thronos), which means "throne" or "seat of power". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dher- meaning "to support" or "to hold".

Grammar:
In Greek, θρονοι is a plural noun of the second declension. It is masculine in gender and nominative in case. The singular form of the word is θρόνος (thronos).

Description:
Θρονοι refers to multiple thrones or seats of power. In ancient Greece, thrones were often used as symbols of authority and power, and were reserved for rulers and other high-ranking officials. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to positions of power or influence in general.
