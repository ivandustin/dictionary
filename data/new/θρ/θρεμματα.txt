Word: θρεμματα

Etymology:
The word θρεμματα (thremmata) is a Greek word that comes from the verb θρέμμα (thremma), which means "to break" or "to crush". It is derived from the ancient Greek word θράω (thrao), which means "to shatter" or "to break into pieces".

Grammar:
In Greek, θρεμματα is a plural noun that belongs to the neuter gender. It is declined according to the second declension, and its nominative singular form is θρέμμα (thremma).

Description:
Θρεμματα refers to broken pieces or fragments of something. It can be used to describe physical objects that have been shattered or broken into pieces, as well as abstract concepts that have been fragmented or broken down into smaller parts. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or relationship that has been damaged or broken beyond repair.
