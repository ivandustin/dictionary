Word: θρησκος

Etymology: The word θρησκος comes from the Greek word θρησκεία (threskeia), which means "religion" or "worship". It is derived from the verb θρησκεύω (threskeuo), which means "to worship" or "to perform religious rites".

Grammar: θρησκος is a Greek adjective that means "religious" or "pious". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: θρησκος is a word that describes someone who is devoted to religion or religious practices. It can also be used to describe something that is related to religion or has a religious connotation. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe someone who is overly pious or hypocritical in their religious beliefs.
