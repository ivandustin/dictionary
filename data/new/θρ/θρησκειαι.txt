Word: θρησκείαι

Etymology:
The word θρησκείαι (threskeiai) is derived from the Greek word θρησκεία (threskeia), which means "religion" or "worship." It is a combination of the words θρησκός (threskos), meaning "religious," and εἶδος (eidos), meaning "form" or "appearance."

Grammar:
Θρησκείαι is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is the nominative case of the word, which means it is used as the subject of a sentence or clause. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case (θρησκειῶν) or the accusative case (θρησκείας).

Description:
Θρησκείαι refers to religious practices or beliefs. It can be used to describe a particular religion or the general concept of religion. In ancient Greece, the word was often used to refer to the various cults and rituals associated with the worship of the gods. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to religion in general.
