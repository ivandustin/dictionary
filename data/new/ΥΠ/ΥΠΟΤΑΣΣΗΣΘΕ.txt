Etymology: The word "υποτασσεθε" comes from the Greek verb "υποτασσω" (hypotasso), which means "to submit" or "to subject oneself". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (hypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "τασσω" (tasso), meaning "to arrange" or "to place".

Grammar: "υποτασσεθε" is the second person plural form of the verb "υποτασσω" (hypotasso) in the present tense, passive voice, and imperative mood. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to submit or subject themselves to something.

Description: The word "υποτασσεθε" is used to express the idea of willingly placing oneself under the authority or control of someone or something else. It implies a sense of obedience, compliance, or surrender.
