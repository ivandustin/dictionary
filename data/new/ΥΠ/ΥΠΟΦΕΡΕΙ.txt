Etymology: The word "υποφερει" (ypoferei) comes from the Greek verb "υποφέρω" (ypoféro), which means "to endure" or "to suffer". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "φέρω" (féro), meaning "to bear" or "to carry".

Grammar: "Υποφέρει" (ypoferei) is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "υποφέρω" (ypoféro). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Υποφέρει" (ypoferei) is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it endures" or "he/she/it suffers" in English. It describes the act of enduring or suffering through a difficult or challenging situation.
