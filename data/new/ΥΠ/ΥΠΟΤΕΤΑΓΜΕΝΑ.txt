Etymology: The word "υποτεταγμένα" (ypotetagmena) comes from the Greek roots "υπό" (ypo), meaning "under," and "τάγμα" (tagma), meaning "order" or "arrangement." 

Grammar: "Υποτεταγμένα" is the plural form of the adjective "υποτεταγμένος" (ypotetagmenos), which means "subordinate" or "submissive." It is used to describe something or someone that is under the authority or control of another.

Description: "Υποτεταγμένα" refers to things or individuals that are in a subordinate position or have a lower rank or status. It can be used in various contexts, such as in organizational structures, military hierarchies, or social relationships.
