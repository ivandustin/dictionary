Etymology: The word "υπακούειν" (ypakouein) comes from the Greek roots "υπό" (ypo), meaning "under," and "ακούειν" (akouein), meaning "to hear." It literally means "to hear under" or "to listen to."

Grammar: "Υπακούειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is in the present tense. It is commonly used in the active voice.

Description: "Υπακούειν" means "to obey" or "to comply" in English. It refers to the act of following instructions or commands willingly and without resistance. It implies a sense of submission and respect towards authority or rules.
