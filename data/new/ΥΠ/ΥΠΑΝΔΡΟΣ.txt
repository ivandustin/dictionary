Etymology: The word "υπανδρος" (ypandros) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "below," and "ανδρος" (andros), meaning "man." It literally translates to "under man" or "subordinate to man."

Grammar: "Υπανδρος" is a masculine adjective in the Greek language. It declines according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Υπανδρος" is used to describe a man who is married or in a marital relationship. It refers to a person who is under the authority or control of a husband.
