Etymology: The word "υπομενειτε" (ypomeneite) comes from the Greek verb "υπομένω" (ypomeno), which means "to endure" or "to bear". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "μένω" (meno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar: "Υπομενείτε" is the second person plural form of the verb "υπομένω" (ypomeno) in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you endure" or "you bear".

Description: "Υπομενείτε" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of enduring or bearing something. It can be used in various contexts to describe the act of staying strong, persevering, or tolerating difficult situations or challenges.
