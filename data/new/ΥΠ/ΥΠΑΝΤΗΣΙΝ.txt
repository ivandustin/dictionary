It is derived from the combination of the preposition "υπό" (ypó), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "ανταω" (antáō), meaning "to meet" or "to come face to face with".

Grammar: "Υπαντησιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.
