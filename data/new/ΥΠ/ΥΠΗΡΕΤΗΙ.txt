Etymology: The word "υπηρετης" (yperetis) comes from the Greek verb "υπηρετεω" (ypereteo), which means "to serve" or "to be in service". It is derived from the noun "υπηρεσια" (yperesia), meaning "service" or "assistance".

Grammar: "Υπηρετης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number. In the plural form, it becomes "υπηρετες" (yperetes).

Description: "Υπηρετης" refers to a person who serves or assists others. It can be used to describe various types of service or assistance, such as a servant, a waiter, a helper, or an employee. The word implies a role of providing support or fulfilling tasks for someone else.
