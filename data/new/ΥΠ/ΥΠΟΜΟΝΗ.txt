Etymology: The word "υπομονη" (ypomonē) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "below," and "μονη" (monē), meaning "abode" or "dwelling." Together, they form the concept of "remaining under" or "enduring."

Grammar: "Υπομονη" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. In the plural form, it becomes "υπομονες" (ypomones).

Description: "Υπομονη" is a Greek word that translates to "patience" or "endurance" in English. It refers to the ability to remain calm and composed in difficult or challenging situations, without giving in to frustration or anger. It is often associated with perseverance, self-control, and the ability to wait for something without becoming anxious or restless. "Υπομονη" is considered a virtue in many cultures and is valued for its positive impact on personal growth and relationships.
