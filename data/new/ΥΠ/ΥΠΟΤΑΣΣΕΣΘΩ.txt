Etymology: The word "υποτασσω" (ypotasso) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "below," and "τασσω" (tasso), meaning "to arrange" or "to place." 

Grammar: "Υποτασσω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different grammatical aspects.

Description: "Υποτασσω" means "to submit" or "to subject" in English. It refers to the act of willingly placing oneself under the authority or control of someone or something else. It can also imply obedience, compliance, or surrendering to a higher power or a set of rules.
