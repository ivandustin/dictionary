Etymology: The word "υπεταγησαν" (υπετάγησαν) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (upo-), meaning "under," and the verb "τάσσω" (tasso), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order."

Grammar: "Υπετάγησαν" is the third person plural, past tense, indicative mood form of the verb "υποτάσσομαι" (υποτάσσομαι). It is used to express that a group of people or things were subjected to or placed under someone's authority or control.

Description: The word "υπετάγησαν" describes an action of submission or obedience. It implies that a group of individuals willingly or unwillingly accepted someone's authority or control over them.
