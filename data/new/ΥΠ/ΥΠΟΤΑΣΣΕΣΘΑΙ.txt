Etymology: The word "υποτασσεσθαι" (ypotassesthai) comes from the Greek verb "υποτασσω" (ypotasso), which means "to submit" or "to subject oneself to". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "τασσω" (tasso), meaning "to arrange" or "to place".

Grammar: "Υποτασσεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "υποτασσω" (ypotasso). In Greek grammar, the infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb without specifying the subject or tense. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Υποτασσεσθαι" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of willingly submitting oneself to someone or something. It implies a voluntary act of obedience, surrender, or subordination.
