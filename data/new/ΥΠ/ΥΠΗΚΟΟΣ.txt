Etymology: The word "υπηκοος" (ypikos) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "sub-", and "ηκω" (eko), meaning "to come". It literally translates to "one who comes under" or "one who submits".

Grammar: "Υπηκοος" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number.

Description: In Greek, "υπηκοος" refers to a person who is subject to the authority or rule of another. It can be used to describe a citizen or a subject of a country or a person who is obedient and submissive to someone else's authority.
