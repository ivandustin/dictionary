Etymology: The word "υπαγοντες" (ypagontes) comes from the Greek verb "υπαγω" (ypago), which means "to lead away" or "to bring under." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under," and the verb "αγω" (ago), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar: "Υπαγοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "υπαγω" (ypago) in Greek. The present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. In this case, "υπαγοντες" means "leading away" or "bringing under" in a continuous sense.

Description: "Υπαγοντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "leading away" or "bringing under." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the act of guiding or directing someone or something towards a particular destination or outcome.
