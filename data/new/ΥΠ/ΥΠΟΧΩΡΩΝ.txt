Etymology: The word "υποχωρών" comes from the Greek verb "υποχωρώ" (ypochoró), which means "to retreat" or "to withdraw." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below," and the verb "χωρώ" (choró), meaning "to go" or "to move."

Grammar: "Υποχωρών" is the present participle form of the verb "υποχωρώ" (ypochoró) in the masculine singular form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Υποχωρών" describes someone or something that is currently retreating or withdrawing. It can be used to refer to a person physically moving backward or figuratively stepping back from a situation or position.
