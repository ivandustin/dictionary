Etymology: The word "υπερηφανους" (yperifanous) comes from the Greek word "υπερήφανος" (yperifanos), which is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπερ-" (hyper-), meaning "above" or "beyond," and the adjective "ήφανος" (ifanos), meaning "proud" or "arrogant."

Grammar: "Υπερηφανους" is the plural form of the adjective "υπερήφανος" (yperifanos) in the nominative case. It is used to describe multiple people or things that are proud or arrogant.

Description: "Υπερηφανους" is an adjective in Greek that describes a state of being proud or arrogant. It is used to express a sense of excessive self-importance or superiority.
