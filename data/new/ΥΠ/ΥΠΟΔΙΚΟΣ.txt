Etymology: The word "υποδικος" (ypodikos) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "below," and "δικος" (dikos), meaning "judgment" or "trial." Therefore, "υποδικος" can be translated as "under judgment" or "under trial."

Grammar: In Greek, "υποδικος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Υποδικος" is used to describe someone who is under legal judgment or trial. It refers to a person who is accused of a crime and is awaiting a verdict or undergoing legal proceedings.
