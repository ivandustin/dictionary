Etymology: The word "υπηκουσαν" (ypikousan) comes from the Greek verb "υπακούω" (ypakouo), which means "to obey" or "to listen to". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "ακούω" (akouo), meaning "to hear".

Grammar: "Υπηκουσαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "υπακούω" (ypakouo) in the past tense. It is used to describe an action of obedience or listening that was performed by a group of people.

Description: "Υπηκουσαν" is a Greek word that can be translated as "they obeyed" or "they listened to". It indicates that a group of individuals followed instructions or complied with someone's commands. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing historical events, narratives, or personal experiences.
