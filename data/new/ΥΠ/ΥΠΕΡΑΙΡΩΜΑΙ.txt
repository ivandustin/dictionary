Etymology: The word "υπεραίρωμαι" (yperairōmai) comes from the Greek prefix "υπερ-" (hyper-), meaning "above" or "beyond," and the verb "αίρω" (airō), meaning "to lift" or "to raise." 

Grammar: "Υπεραίρωμαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and persons to express different meanings and nuances.

Description: "Υπεραίρωμαι" means "to surpass," "to exceed," or "to excel" in English. It implies going beyond a certain limit or standard, often in a positive or superior manner. This verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's achievements, abilities, or performance that goes above and beyond expectations.
