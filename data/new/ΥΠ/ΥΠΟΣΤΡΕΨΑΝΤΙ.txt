Etymology: The word "υποστρεψαντι" comes from the Greek verb "υποστρέφω" (ypostrefó), which means "to return" or "to go back". It is formed by combining the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "στρέφω" (strefó), meaning "to turn".

Grammar: "υποστρεψαντι" is the third person singular, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb "υποστρέφω" (ypostrefó). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "υποστρεψαντι" describes the action of someone or something returning or going back in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it returned" or "he/she/it went back".
