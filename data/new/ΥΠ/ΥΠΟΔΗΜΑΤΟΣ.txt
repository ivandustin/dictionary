Etymology: The word "υποδηματος" (ypodimatos) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "below," and "δημα" (dima), meaning "foot." Therefore, "υποδηματος" literally means "under the foot" or "footwear."

Grammar: "Υποδηματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. It is commonly used to express the relationship between nouns, showing that one noun belongs to or is associated with another noun.

Description: "Υποδηματος" refers to footwear or shoes in Greek. It is a general term that encompasses all types of shoes, including sandals, boots, sneakers, and more.
