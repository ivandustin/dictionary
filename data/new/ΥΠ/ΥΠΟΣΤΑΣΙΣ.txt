Etymology: The word "υποστασις" (ypostasis) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "sub-", and "στασις" (stasis), meaning "standing" or "state". It originally referred to a physical or concrete substance or essence.

Grammar: "Υποστασις" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in various grammatical cases depending on its function in a sentence.

Description: In philosophy, "υποστασις" refers to the underlying reality or substance that supports or constitutes something. It can also be translated as "essence", "substance", or "hypostasis". In Christian theology, it is used to describe the three distinct persons of the Holy Trinity (Father, Son, and Holy Spirit) as different hypostases sharing the same essence.
