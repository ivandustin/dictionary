Etymology: The word "υπακούσαι" (ypakousai) comes from the Greek verb "υπακούω" (ypakouo), which means "to obey" or "to listen to." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under," and the verb "ακούω" (akouo), meaning "to hear."

Grammar: "Υπακούσαι" is the second person singular form of the verb "υπακούω" (ypakouo) in the present tense. It is used when addressing one person and means "you obey" or "you listen to."

Description: "Υπακούσαι" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of obeying or listening to someone. It is used to indicate compliance or submission to authority or instructions.
