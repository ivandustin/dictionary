Etymology: The word "υπεδεξατο" (υποδέχομαι in modern Greek) comes from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (meaning "under" or "below") and the verb "δέχομαι" (meaning "to receive" or "to accept"). 

Grammar: "Υπεδέξατο" is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "υποδέχομαι" in ancient Greek. It is used to express the past tense and active voice. 

Description: "Υπεδέξατο" means "he/she/it received" or "he/she/it accepted" in English. It is a verb that indicates the action of receiving or accepting something or someone.
