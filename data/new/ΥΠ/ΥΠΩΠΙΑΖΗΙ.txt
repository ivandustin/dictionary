Etymology: The word "υπωπιαζηι" (ypopiazī) comes from the Greek verb "υπωπιάζω" (ypopiazō), which means "to examine closely" or "to scrutinize". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb "ωπιάζω" (opiazō), meaning "to look at" or "to observe".

- Εγώ υπωπιάζω (ego ypopiazō) - I examine closely
- Εσύ υπωπιάζεις (esý ypopiazeis) - You examine closely
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό υπωπιάζει (aftós/aftí/aftó ypopiazei) - He/She/It examines closely
- Εμείς υπωπιάζουμε (emeís ypopiazoume) - We examine closely
- Εσείς υπωπιάζετε (eseís ypopiazete) - You (plural) examine closely
- Αυτοί/Αυτές υπωπιάζουν (aftoí/aftés ypopiazoun) - They examine closely

Description: "Υπωπιαζηι" refers to the act of examining or scrutinizing something closely. It implies a careful and detailed observation of an object, situation, or person. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as scientific research, detective work, or simply paying close attention to details.
