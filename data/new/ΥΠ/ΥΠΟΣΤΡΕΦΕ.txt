Etymology: The word "υποστρέφε" (υποστρέφεις in its infinitive form) comes from the Greek verb "στρέφω" (strefó), which means "to turn" or "to return". The prefix "υπο-" (ypo-) adds the meaning of "under" or "below", indicating a movement or action that goes back or returns to a lower position or state.

Grammar: "Υποστρέφε" is the second person singular form of the present tense in Greek. It is used to address someone directly and indicates that the action of returning or turning back is being performed by the person being spoken to.

Description: "Υποστρέφε" is a verb in Greek that means "return" or "turn back". It is used to describe the action of going back to a previous position, state, or direction. It can be used in various contexts, such as returning to a place, changing one's mind, or reverting to a previous condition.
