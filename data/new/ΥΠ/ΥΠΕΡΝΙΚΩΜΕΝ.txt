Etymology: The word "υπερνικώμεν" comes from the Greek verb "υπερνικάω" (ypernikao), which means "to overcome" or "to conquer." It is derived from the prefix "υπερ-" (hyper-), meaning "above" or "beyond," and the verb "νικάω" (nikaο), meaning "to conquer" or "to prevail."

Grammar: "Υπερνικώμεν" is the present tense, first person plural form of the verb "υπερνικάω" (ypernikao). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated to indicate the person, number, tense, and mood of the action. The present tense indicates an action that is happening in the present time.

Description: "Υπερνικώμεν" can be translated to English as "we overcome" or "we conquer." It expresses the action of overcoming or prevailing over something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as overcoming challenges, obstacles, or difficulties.
