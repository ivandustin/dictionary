Etymology: The word "υπηρετησαν" (ypiretisan) comes from the Greek verb "υπηρετώ" (ypiretó), which means "to serve" or "to assist". It is derived from the noun "υπηρέτης" (ypirétis), meaning "servant" or "attendant".

Grammar: "Υπηρέτησαν" is the third person plural, past tense form of the verb "υπηρετώ" (ypiretó). It is used to describe an action that was performed by multiple people in the past. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "Υπηρέτησαν" is a Greek word that translates to "they served" or "they assisted" in English. It indicates that a group of people performed a service or provided assistance in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing the actions of employees, volunteers, or individuals helping others.
