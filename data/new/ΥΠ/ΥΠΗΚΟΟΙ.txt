Etymology: The word "υπηκοοι" (ypikoee) comes from the Greek word "υπήκοος" (ypikos), which means "subject" or "obedient". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "sub-", and the noun "οικος" (oikos), meaning "house" or "family". Therefore, "υπηκοοι" can be translated as "those under the same house" or "those belonging to the same family".

Grammar: "Υπηκοοι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is the plural form of "υπηκοος" and refers to multiple subjects or obedient individuals.

Description: "Υπηκοοι" is a term used to describe individuals who are subjects or obedient to a higher authority, such as a ruler, government, or leader. It implies a sense of loyalty, obedience, and subordination.
