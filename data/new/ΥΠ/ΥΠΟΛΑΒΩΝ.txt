Etymology: The word "υπολαβών" comes from the Greek verb "υπολαμβάνω" (ypolambáno), which means "to assume" or "to undertake." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below," and the verb "λαμβάνω" (lambáno), meaning "to take" or "to receive."

Grammar: "Υπολαβών" is the present participle form of the verb "υπολαμβάνω" (ypolambáno). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Υπολαβών" refers to someone who is currently assuming or undertaking something. It can be used to describe a person who is taking on a task, responsibility, or role. The word implies an ongoing or continuous action of assuming or undertaking.
