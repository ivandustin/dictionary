Etymology: The word "υπερβαίνειν" (yperbainein) comes from the Greek roots "υπέρ" (yper), meaning "beyond" or "above," and "βαίνειν" (bainein), meaning "to go." 

Grammar: "υπερβαίνειν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb. 

Description: "υπερβαίνειν" means "to surpass" or "to exceed" in Greek. It is used to describe going beyond a certain limit or surpassing expectations.
