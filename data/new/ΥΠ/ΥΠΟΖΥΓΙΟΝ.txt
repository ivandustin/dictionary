Etymology: The word "υποζυγιον" (ypozygion) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "below," and "ζυγιον" (zygion), meaning "yoke." It literally translates to "under the yoke."

Grammar: "Υποζυγιον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension. The plural form of this word is "υποζυγια" (ypozygia).

Description: "Υποζυγιον" refers to a yoke, which is a wooden beam used to connect two animals, usually oxen, together for pulling heavy loads. It is a common tool in agriculture and transportation. The yoke is placed on the necks of the animals, and they work together to move the load.
