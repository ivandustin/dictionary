Etymology: The word "υπερηφανία" (yperifanía) comes from the Greek roots "υπέρ" (yper), meaning "above" or "beyond," and "ηφανία" (ifanía), meaning "pride" or "arrogance." 

Grammar: "Υπερηφανία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined as follows: 

- Singular: η υπερηφανία (i yperifanía) 
- Plural: οι υπερηφανίες (i yperifaníes) 

Description: "Υπερηφανία" refers to the feeling of excessive pride or arrogance. It is the state of considering oneself superior to others and often involves an inflated sense of self-importance.
