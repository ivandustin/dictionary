Etymology: The word "υπομενω" (ypomeno) comes from the Greek roots "υπο" (ypo), meaning "under" or "below," and "μενω" (meno), meaning "to remain" or "to endure." 

Grammar: "Υπομενω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is in the present tense. It can be translated as "I endure" or "I remain."

Description: "Υπομενω" is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of enduring or remaining under difficult circumstances. It implies patience, perseverance, and the ability to withstand challenges or hardships. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's ability to endure physical pain, emotional distress, or difficult situations in general.
