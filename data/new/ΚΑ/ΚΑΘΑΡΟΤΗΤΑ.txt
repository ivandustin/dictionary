Etymology: The word "καθαροτητα" (katharotita) comes from the Greek root "καθαρός" (katharos), meaning "clean" or "pure," and the suffix "-τητα" (-tita), which denotes the quality or state of being.

Grammar: "Καθαροτητα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Καθαροτητα" refers to the quality or state of being clean, pure, or free from impurities. It can be used to describe physical cleanliness, moral purity, or clarity of thought or expression.
