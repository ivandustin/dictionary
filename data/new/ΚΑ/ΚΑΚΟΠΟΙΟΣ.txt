Etymology: The word "κακοποιός" comes from the Greek roots "κακός" (kakós), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "ποιέω" (poiéo), meaning "to make" or "to do." Therefore, "κακοποιός" can be translated as "one who does evil" or "wrongdoer."

Grammar: In Greek, "κακοποιός" is a noun. It is masculine and singular. The plural form is "κακοποιοί" (kakopoioí).

Description: "Κακοποιός" is a term used to describe someone who engages in evil or harmful actions. It refers to a wrongdoer, a person who intentionally causes harm or engages in malicious behavior.
