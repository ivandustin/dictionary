Etymology: The word "κακουργούς" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the words "κακός" (kakos), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "έργο" (ergo), meaning "work" or "deed." 

Grammar: In Greek, "κακουργούς" is a noun in the masculine gender and the accusative case. It is the plural form of the word "κακουργός" (kakourgos), which means "evil-doer" or "criminal." 

Description: "Κακουργούς" refers to a group of individuals who engage in evil or criminal activities. It can be used to describe people who commit crimes, engage in malicious behavior, or intentionally cause harm to others.
