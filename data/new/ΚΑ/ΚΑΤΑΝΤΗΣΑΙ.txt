Etymology: The word "καταντησαί" comes from the Greek verb "καταντώ" (katanó), which means "to arrive at" or "to reach." It is derived from the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "towards," and the verb "ἀντι" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite."

Grammar: "καταντησαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, indicative mood, and third person singular form of the verb "καταντάω" (katanó), which means "to arrive at" or "to reach." It is used to describe the action of someone or something reaching a certain point or outcome.

Description: "καταντησαί" is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of reaching a certain point or outcome. It can be used in various contexts to describe the act of arriving at a destination, achieving a goal, or reaching a certain state or condition.
