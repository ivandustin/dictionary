Etymology: The word "κακοποιησαί" comes from the Greek verb "κακοποιώ" (kakopoió), which is a compound word formed by the combination of "κακός" (kakós) meaning "bad" or "evil" and "ποιώ" (poió) meaning "to do" or "to make". 

Grammar: "κακοποιησαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, subjunctive mood, second person singular form of the verb "κακοποιέω" (kakopoiéo), which means "to mistreat" or "to harm". The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, the active voice indicates that the subject performs the action, the subjunctive mood expresses a possibility or potentiality, and the second person singular form is used when addressing one person.

Description: "κακοποιησαί" is a verb form that specifically addresses the action of mistreating or harming someone. It implies the intention or act of causing harm or distress to another person.
