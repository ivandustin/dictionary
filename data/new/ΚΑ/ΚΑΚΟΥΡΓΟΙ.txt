Etymology: The word "κακουργοι" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the words "κακός" (kakos), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "έργο" (ergo), meaning "work" or "deed." Therefore, "κακουργοι" can be translated as "evil-doers" or "wrongdoers."

Grammar: In Greek, "κακουργοι" is a plural noun. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun. It is used to refer to a group of people who engage in evil or criminal activities.

Description: "Κακουργοι" is a term used to describe individuals who commit wicked or unlawful acts. It is often used to refer to criminals, wrongdoers, or individuals who engage in immoral behavior.
