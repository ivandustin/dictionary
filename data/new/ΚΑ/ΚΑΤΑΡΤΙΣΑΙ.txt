Etymology: The word "καταρτίσαι" comes from the Greek verb "καταρτίζω" (katartízo), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to," and the verb "ἀρτίζω" (artízo), meaning "to fit" or "to arrange."

Grammar: "καταρτίσαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "καταρτίζω" (katartízo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that expresses the action without specifying the subject or tense.

Description: "καταρτίσαι" is a verb form that expresses the action of preparing or making something ready. It can be translated as "to prepare," "to make ready," or "to arrange." The word can be used in various contexts, such as preparing a meal, organizing an event, or getting ready for a task.
