Etymology: The word "καθαιρείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "καθαιρέω" (kathaireo), which means "to remove" or "to take down." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "αίρω" (airo), meaning "to lift" or "to take away."

Grammar: "καθαιρείσθαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present passive infinitive form, which indicates that the action of removing or taking down is being done to the subject of the sentence.

Description: The word "καθαιρείσθαι" describes the act of being removed or taken down. It can be used in various contexts, such as removing an object from a place or taking down a structure.
