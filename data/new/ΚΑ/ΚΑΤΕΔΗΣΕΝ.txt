Etymology: The word "κατηγέομαι" (katēgéomai) comes from the Greek verb "κατά" (katá), meaning "down" or "against," and "ἡγέομαι" (hēgéomai), meaning "to lead" or "to consider." 

Grammar: "κατηγέομαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the middle voice and is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and persons to indicate different actions and states of being.

Description: "κατηγέομαι" means "to lead down" or "to consider as." It is often used to express the act of considering someone or something as inferior or of lesser value. It can also imply the act of leading or guiding someone or something in a downward direction.
