Etymology: The word "κατηντήσαμεν" comes from the Greek verb "καταντώ" (katanó), which means "to arrive" or "to reach." It is formed by combining the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "towards," and the verb "αντι" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite."

Grammar: "κατηντήσαμεν" is the first person plural form of the verb "καταντώ" in the aorist tense. The aorist tense is used to describe completed actions in the past. The "-σαμεν" ending indicates that the subject of the verb is "we."

Description: "κατηντήσαμεν" can be translated as "we arrived" or "we reached" in English. It implies that the subject has successfully reached a destination or achieved a goal.
