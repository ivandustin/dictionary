Etymology: The word "καίνης" (kainēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the adjective "καινός" (kainos), meaning "new" or "fresh."

Grammar: In Greek, "καίνης" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension, which means its form changes depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Καίνης" is a Greek word that can have multiple meanings depending on the context. It can refer to something new, recent, or fresh. It can also be used to describe something innovative or different from what is commonly known or expected.
