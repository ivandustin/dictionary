Etymology: The word "καθαροί" (katharoi) comes from the Greek word "καθαρός" (katharos), meaning "clean" or "pure".

Grammar: "Καθαροί" is the plural form of the adjective "καθαρός" (katharos) in Greek. It is used to describe multiple nouns that are clean or pure.

Description: "Καθαροί" is an adjective in Greek that is used to describe things or people that are clean, pure, or free from impurities. It can be used to describe physical cleanliness, moral purity, or even abstract concepts.
