Etymology: The word "κατακαυχώ" (katakaucho) comes from the Greek verb "καυχώμαι" (kauchomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The prefix "κατά" (kata) adds the meaning of "down" or "against", giving the word a sense of excessive or arrogant boasting.

Grammar: "κατακαυχώ" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb "καυχώμαι" (kauchomai), which is a deponent verb, meaning it has a passive form but an active meaning.

Description: "κατακαυχώ" is a Greek word that describes the act of boasting or bragging excessively and arrogantly. It implies a sense of self-importance and superiority.
