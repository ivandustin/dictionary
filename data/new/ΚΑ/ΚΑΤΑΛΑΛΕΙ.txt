Etymology: The word "καταλαλεῖ" comes from the Greek verb "καταλαλέω" (katalaleo), which is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata) meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "λαλέω" (laleo) meaning "to speak." 

Grammar: "καταλαλεῖ" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It belongs to the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-εω" in Greek. 

Description: The word "καταλαλεῖ" means "he/she/it speaks against" or "he/she/it slanders" in Greek. It refers to the act of speaking negatively or maliciously about someone or something.
