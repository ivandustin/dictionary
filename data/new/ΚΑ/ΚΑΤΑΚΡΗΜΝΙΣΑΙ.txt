Etymology: The word "κατακρημνίζαι" comes from the Greek verb "κατακρημνίζω" (katakrēmnizō), which means "to precipitate" or "to fall headlong". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "κρημνίζω" (krēmnizō), meaning "to fall" or "to tumble".

Grammar: "κατακρημνίζαι" is the present tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb. In Greek grammar, the infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb without specifying the subject or the time of the action. The present tense indicates that the action is happening in the present.

Description: "κατακρημνίζαι" describes the action of falling headlong or precipitating. It can be used to refer to a physical fall or a metaphorical fall, such as a sudden decline or downfall.
