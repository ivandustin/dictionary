Etymology: The word "καθαρός" (katharos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb "καθαίρω" (kathairo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify."

Grammar: In Greek, "καθαρός" is an adjective. It is used to describe something that is clean, pure, or free from impurities. It can be used to describe both physical objects and abstract concepts.

It can also describe a person who is morally pure or free from guilt.
