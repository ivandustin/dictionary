Etymology: The word "καθησθαι" comes from the Greek verb "καθίζω" (kathizō), which means "to sit down" or "to be seated."

Grammar: "καθησθαι" is the present infinitive form of the verb "καθίζω" (kathizō). In Greek grammar, the infinitive is a non-finite verb form that expresses the action or state of the verb without specifying any subject or tense.

Description: "καθησθαι" is used to describe the action of sitting down or being seated. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone taking a seat, resting in a sitting position, or assuming a seated posture.
