Etymology: The word "καλεσεις" (kaleseis) comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon".

Grammar: "Καλεσεις" is the second person singular form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo) in the future tense. It is used when addressing one person and means "you will call" or "you will summon".

Description: "Καλεσεις" is a word in the Greek language that refers to the action of calling or summoning someone in the future. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts.
