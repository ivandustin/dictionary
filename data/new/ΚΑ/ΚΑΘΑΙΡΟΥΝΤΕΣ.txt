Etymology: The word "καθαιρούντες" comes from the Greek verb "καθαιρέω" (kathaireo), which means "to remove" or "to take away". It is derived from the prefix "κατ-" (kat-), meaning "down" or "away", and the verb "αίρω" (airo), meaning "to lift" or "to take".

Grammar: "καθαιρούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "καθαιρέω" (kathaireo) in Greek. It is in the active voice, masculine gender, plural number, and present tense. The participle form indicates ongoing action or state.

Description: "καθαιρούντες" is used to describe a group of people who are currently removing or taking away something. It can refer to physical removal or metaphorical removal, depending on the context.
