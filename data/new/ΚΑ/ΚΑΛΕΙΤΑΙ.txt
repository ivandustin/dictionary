Etymology: The word "καλειται" (kaleitai) comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to name."

Grammar: "Καλείται" (kaleitai) is the third person singular form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo) in the present tense. It is in the indicative mood and the passive voice.

Description: "Καλείται" (kaleitai) means "he/she/it is called" or "he/she/it is named" in Greek. It is used to refer to someone or something that has a specific name or title.
