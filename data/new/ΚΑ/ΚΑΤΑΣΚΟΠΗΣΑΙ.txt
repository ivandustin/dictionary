Etymology: The word "κατασκοπεία" comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "σκοπός" (skopos), meaning "watcher" or "observer." It literally translates to "watching from below" or "spying."

Grammar: "Κατασκοπεία" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Κατασκοπεία" refers to the act of spying or espionage. It involves gathering information secretly and covertly, often for political, military, or intelligence purposes. Spying can involve various methods such as surveillance, infiltration, and gathering classified or sensitive information.
