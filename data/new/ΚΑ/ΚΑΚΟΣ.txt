Etymology: The word "κακός" (kakos) comes from the ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kakka-, which means "bad" or "evil."

Grammar: In Greek, "κακός" is an adjective that is used to describe something or someone as "bad," "evil," or "wicked." It can be used to describe a person's character, behavior, or the quality of something.

Description: "Κακός" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can describe a person who is morally corrupt, someone who is unpleasant or harmful, or something that is of poor quality. It is often used to express disapproval or criticism.
