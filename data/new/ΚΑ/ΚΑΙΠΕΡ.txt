Etymology: The word "καίπερ" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by combining the conjunction "καί" (meaning "and") and the adverb "περ" (meaning "even, though"). 

Grammar: "Καίπερ" is a conjunction in Greek grammar. It is used to introduce a concessive clause, indicating a contrast or contradiction between two ideas. It is similar in meaning to the English word "although" or "even though".

Description: "Καίπερ" is used to express a concession or a contradiction. It is often used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or contradicts a previous statement. It emphasizes that despite the contradiction, the following statement is still true.
