Etymology: The word "καταστήσω" (katastíso) comes from the Greek verb "καταστήνυμι" (katastínumi), which means "to establish" or "to set up." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to," and the verb "ἵστημι" (ístimi), meaning "to stand" or "to place."

Grammar: "Καταστήσω" is a verb in the future tense, first person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The first person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is the speaker.

Description: "Καταστήσω" means "I will establish" or "I will set up" in English. It is used to express the speaker's intention or plan to create or establish something in the future.
