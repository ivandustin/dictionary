Etymology: The word "καταβαίνων" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend." The suffix "-ων" (-on) indicates the present participle form of the verb.

Grammar: "Καταβαίνων" is the present participle form of the verb "καταβαίνω" in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action of "going down" or "descending" in the present tense.

Description: "Καταβαίνων" is an adjective that describes someone or something that is currently "going down" or "descending." It can be used to describe a person physically descending from a higher place to a lower place, or metaphorically to describe a decrease or decline in something.
