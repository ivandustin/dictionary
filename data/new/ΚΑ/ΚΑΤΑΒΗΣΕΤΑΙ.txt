Etymology: The word "καταβησεται" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down", and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go".

Grammar: "καταβησεται" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person singular form refers to a single subject.

Description: "καταβησεται" means "he/she/it will go down" or "he/she/it will descend" in English. It is used to express a future action of going down or descending.
