Etymology: The word "καταφρονείς" comes from the Greek verb "καταφρονέω" (kataphroneo), which means "to despise" or "to look down upon". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "φρονέω" (phroneo), meaning "to think" or "to have an opinion".

Grammar: "καταφρονείς" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "καταφρονέω" in Greek. It is used to address someone directly and indicates that the subject is despising or looking down upon someone or something.

Description: "καταφρονείς" is a Greek word that describes the act of despising or looking down upon someone or something. It conveys a sense of disdain, contempt, or disregard towards the object of one's scorn.
