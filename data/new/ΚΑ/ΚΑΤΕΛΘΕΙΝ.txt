Etymology: The word "κατελθειν" comes from the Greek verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "κατελθειν" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for a specific subject or tense.

Description: "κατελθειν" is a Greek verb that means "to come down" or "to descend". It is often used in a literal sense to describe the act of physically moving from a higher position to a lower position.
