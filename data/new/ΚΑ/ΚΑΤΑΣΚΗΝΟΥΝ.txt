Etymology: The word "κατασκηνούν" comes from the Greek verb "κατασκηνόω" (kataskeeno), which means "to camp" or "to set up a tent". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to", and the verb "σκηνόω" (skeeno), meaning "to pitch a tent".

Grammar: "κατασκηνούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "κατασκηνόω". In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time. The third person plural form refers to a group of people or things.

Description: "κατασκηνούν" describes the action of a group of people setting up a camp or pitching tents. It implies that the group is currently engaged in this activity.
