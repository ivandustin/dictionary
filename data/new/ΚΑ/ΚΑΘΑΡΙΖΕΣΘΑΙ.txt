Etymology: The word "καθαριζεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse."

Grammar: "καθαριζεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo). In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "καθαριζεσθαι" is a Greek verb that means "to purify oneself" or "to cleanse oneself." It implies the act of making oneself clean or free from impurities, both physically and metaphorically. This verb is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, referring to the purification of the soul or the cleansing of sins.
