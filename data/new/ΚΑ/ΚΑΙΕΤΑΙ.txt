Etymology: The word "καῖται" (kaitai) comes from the Greek verb "καίω" (kaio), which means "to burn." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kay-.

Grammar: "Καῖται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "καίω" (kaio) in the indicative mood. It is used to describe an action or state of burning that is happening in the present.

Description: "Καῖται" is a Greek verb that specifically refers to the act of burning. It can be used to describe the burning of objects, substances, or even people. The verb is conjugated according to the subject and tense of the sentence.
