Etymology: The word "κατακοπτων" comes from the Greek verb "κατακόπτω" (katakopto), which means "to cut down" or "to strike down". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "κόπτω" (kopto), meaning "to cut" or "to strike".

Grammar: "κατακοπτων" is the present participle form of the verb "κατακόπτω" (katakopto) in Greek. Present participles are used to form verb phrases and can function as adjectives or adverbs. In this form, "κατακοπτων" means "cutting down" or "striking down".

Description: "κατακοπτων" describes the action of cutting down or striking down. It can be used to refer to someone or something that is actively engaged in cutting or striking down.
