Etymology: The word "καταβηναι" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go."

Grammar: "καταβηναι" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino). In Greek grammar, the infinitive is the basic form of the verb that expresses the action or state without indicating any specific tense, mood, or person. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion.

Description: "καταβηναι" is used to describe the action of going down or descending. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically descending from a higher place to a lower place, or metaphorically referring to a decrease or decline in something.
