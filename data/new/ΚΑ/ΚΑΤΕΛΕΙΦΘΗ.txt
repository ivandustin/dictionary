Etymology: The word "κατελείφθη" comes from the Greek verb "καταλείπω" (kataleípo), which means "to leave behind" or "to abandon." It is formed by combining the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "λείπω" (leípo), meaning "to leave" or "to be left."

Grammar: "κατελείφθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "καταλείπω" (kataleípo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "κατελείφθη" is a Greek verb form that means "he/she/it was left behind" or "he/she/it was abandoned." It describes a past action where someone or something was left or abandoned by someone else.
