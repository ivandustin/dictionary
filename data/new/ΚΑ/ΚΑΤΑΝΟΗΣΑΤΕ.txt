Etymology: The word "κατανοησατε" comes from the Greek verb "κατανοέω" (katanoeo), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "through," and the verb "νοέω" (noeo), meaning "to think" or "to perceive."

Grammar: "κατανοησατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "κατανοέω" in the aorist tense. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they have understood or comprehended something.

Description: "κατανοησατε" is a Greek verb that expresses the action of understanding or comprehending. It implies a deep level of understanding or insight into a particular subject or situation.
