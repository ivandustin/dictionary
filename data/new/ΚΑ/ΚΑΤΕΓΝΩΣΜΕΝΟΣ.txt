Etymology: The word "κατεγνωσμένος" comes from the Greek verb "καταγινώσκω" (kataginósko), which means "to condemn" or "to find guilty". The suffix "-μένος" (-menos) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, indicating that the action of condemning or finding guilty has been completed.

Grammar: "Κατεγνωσμένος" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The masculine form is used when the noun being described is masculine, singular, and in the nominative case.

Description: The adjective "κατεγνωσμένος" describes someone or something that has been condemned or found guilty. It implies that the person or thing has been judged and deemed to be at fault or deserving of punishment.
