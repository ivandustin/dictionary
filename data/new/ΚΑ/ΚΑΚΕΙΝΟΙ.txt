Etymology: The word "κακείνοι" (kakeinoi) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κακ-" (kak-), meaning "bad" or "evil," and the pronoun "εἶναι" (eînai), meaning "to be." 

Grammar: "Κακείνοι" is a plural form of the adjective "κακός" (kakós), which means "bad" or "evil." It is used to describe multiple people or things that are considered negative or of poor quality. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: "Κακείνοι" is often used to refer to a group of people who are seen as morally corrupt, wicked, or undesirable. It can also be used more generally to describe things that are considered bad or negative. The word carries a negative connotation and is typically used in a critical or disapproving manner.
