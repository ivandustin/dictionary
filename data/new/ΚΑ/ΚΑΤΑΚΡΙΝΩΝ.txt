Etymology: The word "κατακρινων" comes from the Greek verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno), which means "to condemn" or "to judge against." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "κρίνω" (kríno), meaning "to judge."

Grammar: "κατακρινων" is the present participle form of the verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "κατακρινων" describes someone or something that is currently condemning or judging against others. It implies a negative judgment or criticism. The word can be used to describe a person who is expressing disapproval or passing judgment on someone or something.
