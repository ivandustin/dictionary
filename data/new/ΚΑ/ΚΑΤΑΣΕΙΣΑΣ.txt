Etymology: The word "κατασείσας" comes from the Greek verb "κατασείω" (kataseio), which means "to shake violently" or "to cause an earthquake". The suffix "-ας" (-as) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle.

Grammar: "κατασείσας" is the masculine singular form of the past participle of the verb "κατασείω". In Greek grammar, the past participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "κατασείσας" is an adjective that means "having shaken violently" or "having caused an earthquake". It is used to describe something or someone that has caused a strong shaking or trembling motion, typically referring to earthquakes.
