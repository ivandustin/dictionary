Etymology: The word "καταπατείν" comes from the Greek verb "καταπατώ" (katapato), which means "to trample down" or "to tread upon". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "upon", and the verb "πατέω" (pateo), meaning "to tread" or "to step on".

Grammar: "καταπατείν" is the present tense, active voice, indicative mood form of the verb "καταπατώ" in Greek. It is used to describe the action of trampling down or treading upon something in the present time.

Description: "καταπατείν" is a Greek verb that describes the act of stepping on or trampling down something forcefully. It implies a strong and forceful action of exerting pressure or crushing something underfoot.
