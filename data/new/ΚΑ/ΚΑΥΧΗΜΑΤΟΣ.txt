Etymology: The word "καυχηματος" (kaukhēmatos) comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaukhaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag."

Grammar: "Καυχηματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Καυχηματος" refers to a boast or a brag. It is used to describe a statement or an action in which someone proudly talks about their achievements, abilities, or possessions.
