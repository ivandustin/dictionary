Etymology: The word "κατανοούντι" comes from the Greek verb "κατανοέω" (katanoeo), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "through", and the verb "νοέω" (noeo), meaning "to perceive" or "to think".

Grammar: "κατανοούντι" is the present participle form of the verb "κατανοέω" in the third person singular. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "κατανοούντι" describes someone or something that is currently understanding or comprehending. It indicates an ongoing action or state of understanding.
