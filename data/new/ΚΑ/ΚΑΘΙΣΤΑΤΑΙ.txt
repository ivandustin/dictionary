Etymology: The word "καθισταται" comes from the Greek verb "καθίστημι" (kathistēmi), which means "to establish" or "to appoint."

Grammar: "καθισταται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "καθίστημι" (kathistēmi) in Greek. It is in the indicative mood and active voice.

Description: "καθισταται" is used to describe an action of establishing or appointing something or someone. It can also imply the act of making something or someone stand or sit in a particular position.
