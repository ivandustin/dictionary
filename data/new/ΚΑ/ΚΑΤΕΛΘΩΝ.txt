Etymology: The word "κατελθων" (katelthōn) comes from the Greek verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "κατελθων" is the present participle form of the verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "κατελθων" is often translated as "coming down" or "descending." It can be used to describe someone or something that is currently in the process of coming down or descending from a higher place to a lower place. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a decrease or decline in something.
