Etymology: The word "καταχθέντες" comes from the Greek verb "καταχθείν" (katachthein), which means "to be brought down" or "to be descended." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "χθείν" (chthein), meaning "to bring" or "to descend."

Grammar: "καταχθέντες" is the plural form of the participle of the verb "καταχθείν." In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to describe a noun or indicate a state or action. The participle "καταχθέντες" is in the masculine gender, plural number, and nominative case.

Description: "καταχθέντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "those who have been brought down" or "those who have descended." It is often used in a figurative sense to refer to people who have experienced a downfall or a decline in status or reputation.
