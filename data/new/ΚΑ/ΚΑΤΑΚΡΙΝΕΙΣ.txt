Etymology: The word "κατακρινείς" comes from the Greek verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno), which means "to condemn" or "to judge against." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "κρίνω" (kríno), meaning "to judge."

Grammar: "Κατακρινείς" is the second person singular form of the present tense indicative mood of the verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno). It is used when addressing one person and indicates that the subject is currently condemning or judging against someone or something.

Description: "Κατακρινείς" is a Greek word that translates to "you condemn" or "you judge against" in English. It is used to express the act of passing judgment or expressing disapproval towards someone or something.
