Etymology: The word "καυχησιν" (kaukhēsin) comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaukhaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag."

Grammar: "Καυχησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. It is derived from the verb "καυχάομαι" and is used to express the act of boasting or bragging.

Description: "Καυχησιν" refers to the act of boasting or bragging. It is used to describe the action of excessively praising oneself or one's achievements in order to gain attention or admiration from others.
