Etymology: The word "καμνοντα" comes from the Greek verb "καμνω" (kamno), which means "to be ill" or "to be weak."

Grammar: "καμνοντα" is the present participle form of the verb "καμνω" (kamno), which indicates ongoing action or state of being. It is in the masculine singular accusative case.

Description: "καμνοντα" describes a person or thing that is currently ill or weak. It can be used as an adjective to modify a noun, or as a noun itself to refer to a sick person.
