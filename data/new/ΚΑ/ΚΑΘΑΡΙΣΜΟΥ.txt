Etymology: The word "καθαρισμός" (katharismos) comes from the Greek verb "καθαρίζω" (katharizo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify."

Grammar: "Καθαρισμός" is a noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the masculine gender and is in the singular form.

Description: "Καθαρισμός" refers to the act of cleaning or purifying something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of removing impurities or negative elements from a situation or a person's character.
