Etymology: The word "κατεστραμμένα" comes from the Greek verb "καταστρέφω" (katastréfo), which means "to destroy" or "to ruin". The suffix "-μένα" (-mena) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, indicating that the action of destruction has been completed.

Grammar: "κατεστραμμένα" is the neuter plural form of the past participle of the verb "καταστρέφω". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, the adjective agrees with a neuter plural noun.

Description: "κατεστραμμένα" is an adjective in Greek that means "destroyed" or "ruined". It is used to describe something that has been damaged or completely destroyed.
