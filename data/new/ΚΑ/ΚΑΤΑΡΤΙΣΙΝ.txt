Etymology: The word "καταρτισιν" comes from the Greek verb "καταρτίζω" (katartízo), which means "to prepare" or "to put in order."

Grammar: "καταρτισιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and neuter gender in the Greek language.

Description: "καταρτισιν" refers to the act of preparing or putting something in order. It can also be used to describe the state of being well-prepared or well-organized.
