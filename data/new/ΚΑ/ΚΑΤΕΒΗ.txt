Etymology: The word "κατεβή" (kateví) comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaíno), which means "to descend" or "to go down". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down", and the verb "βαίνω" (baíno), meaning "to go".

Grammar: "κατεβή" is the past tense form of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaíno) in the 3rd person singular. It is used to indicate that someone or something descended or went down in the past.

Description: "κατεβή" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the action of descending or going down. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone physically descending a staircase or a mountain, or metaphorically referring to a decrease or decline in something.
