Etymology: The word "καταβαντος" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend." The word is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go."

Grammar: "Καταβαντος" is the masculine form of the participle of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino). In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. The participle "καταβαντος" indicates that the action of going down or descending has been completed in the past.

Description: "Καταβαντος" can be translated as "the one who has gone down" or "the one who has descended." It refers to someone or something that has moved from a higher position to a lower position. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person who has descended from a mountain or a spiritual being who has come down to Earth.
