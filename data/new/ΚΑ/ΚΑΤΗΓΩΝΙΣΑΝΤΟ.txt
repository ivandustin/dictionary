It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreúō), meaning "to speak in public" or "to address".

Grammar: "κατηγωνισαντο" is the third person plural, aorist middle indicative form of the verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoréō). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice suggests that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit.
