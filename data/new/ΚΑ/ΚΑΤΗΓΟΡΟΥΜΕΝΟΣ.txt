Etymology: The word "κατηγορούμενος" comes from the Greek verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoreō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The suffix "-μενος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice, so "κατηγορούμενος" can be translated as "being accused" or "being charged".

Grammar: "Κατηγορούμενος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the present passive participle of the verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoreō), which means that it describes someone or something that is currently being accused or charged.

Description: "Κατηγορούμενος" refers to a person who is being accused or charged with a crime or wrongdoing. It is often used in legal contexts to describe the defendant or the person against whom an accusation has been made.
