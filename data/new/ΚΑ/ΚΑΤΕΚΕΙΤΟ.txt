Etymology: The word "κατεκείτο" comes from the Greek verb "κατακείμαι" (katakeimai), which means "to lie down" or "to be situated." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "κείμαι" (keimai), meaning "to lie."

Grammar: "κατεκείτο" is the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb "κατακείμαι" (katakeimai). In Greek grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or is affected by the action in some way.

Description: "κατεκείτο" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was lying down" or "he/she/it was situated." It is used to describe the action of someone or something being in a reclining or resting position.
