Etymology: The word "καμπτω" (kampto) comes from the Greek verb "κάμπτω" (kampto), which means "to bend" or "to curve". 

Grammar: "Καμπτώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ κάμπτω (ego kampto) - I bend, εσύ κάμπτεις (esi kampteis) - you bend, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό κάμπτει (aftos/afti/afto kamptei) - he/she/it bends, εμείς κάμπτουμε (emeis kamptoume) - we bend, εσείς κάμπτετε (eseis kamptete) - you bend, αυτοί/αυτές/αυτά κάμπτουν (aftoi/aftes/afta kamptoun) - they bend.

Description: "Καμπτώ" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It generally refers to the action of bending or curving something physically, but it can also be used metaphorically to describe the bending or changing of opinions, rules, or situations.
