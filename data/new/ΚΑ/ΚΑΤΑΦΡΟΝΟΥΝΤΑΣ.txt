Etymology: The word "καταφρονούντας" comes from the Greek verb "καταφρονέω" (kataphroneo), which means "to despise" or "to look down upon." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "φρονέω" (phroneo), meaning "to think" or "to have an opinion."

Grammar: "καταφρονούντας" is the present participle form of the verb "καταφρονέω" in the third person plural. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "καταφρονούντας" describes a group of people who are currently despising or looking down upon someone or something. It implies a sense of disdain or contempt towards the subject.
