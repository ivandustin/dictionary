Etymology: The word "κακοπαθησον" comes from the Greek roots "κακός" (kakos), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "πάθος" (pathos), meaning "suffering" or "affliction." 

Grammar: "κακοπαθησον" is a verb in the aorist imperative form, second person singular, of the Greek verb "κακοπαθέω" (kakopathéo), which means "to suffer" or "to experience hardship."

Description: "κακοπαθησον" is a command or request for someone to suffer or endure hardship. It is used in a figurative sense to express the idea of enduring difficulties or facing challenges.
