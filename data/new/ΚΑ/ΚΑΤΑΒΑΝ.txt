Etymology: The word "καταβαν" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go."

Grammar: "καταβαν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is the present tense, indicative mood, active voice, and third person singular form of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino). It is used to describe the action of going down or descending.

Description: "καταβαν" is a word that specifically refers to the act of going down or descending. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing physical movement from a higher place to a lower place or metaphorical descent in a figurative sense.
