Etymology: The word "καταλυσω" (katalyso) comes from the Greek verb "καταλύω" (katalyo), which means "to dissolve" or "to destroy." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "λύω" (lyo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie."

Grammar: "καταλύω" is a first-conjugation verb in Greek, which means it follows a regular pattern of conjugation. It is an active verb, meaning it describes an action performed by the subject. It can be conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different aspects and nuances of the action.
