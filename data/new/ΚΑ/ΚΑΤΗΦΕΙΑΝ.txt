Etymology: The word "κατηφειαν" (katēpheian) comes from the Greek verb "κατηφείαν" (katēpheian), which means "to be downcast" or "to be dejected." It is derived from the prefix "κατ-" (kat-), meaning "down," and the noun "ἥψυχ" (hēpsykh), meaning "soul" or "spirit."

Grammar: "κατηφειαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "κατηφειαν" refers to a state of being downcast, dejected, or disheartened. It describes a feeling of sadness, gloom, or low spirits.
