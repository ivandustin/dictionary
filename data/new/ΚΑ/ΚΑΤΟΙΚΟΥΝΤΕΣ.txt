Etymology: The word "κατοικούντες" comes from the Greek verb "κατοικέω" (katoikeo), which means "to inhabit" or "to dwell". The suffix "-ούντες" (-ountes) is added to the verb stem to form a present participle, indicating that the action of inhabiting is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Κατοικούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "κατοικέω" in Greek. It is used to describe people or things that are currently inhabiting or dwelling in a specific place. In terms of grammar, it functions as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Description: "Κατοικούντες" refers to the people who are currently living or residing in a particular location. It can be used to describe the inhabitants of a city, a country, or any other place. The word implies a continuous or ongoing state of inhabiting, suggesting that the people mentioned are not just temporary visitors, but rather have established a more permanent presence in the place.
