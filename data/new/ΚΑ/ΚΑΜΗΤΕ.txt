Etymology: The word "καμητε" comes from the Greek word "κάμνω" (kamno), which means "to work" or "to toil."

Grammar: "καμητε" is a verb in the imperative mood, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb "κάμνω" (kamno), which means "to work" or "to toil."

Description: "καμητε" is a command or request for a group of people to work or toil. It is used to instruct or encourage multiple individuals to engage in labor or exertion.
