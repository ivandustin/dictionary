Etymology: The word "καταβηθι" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go."

Grammar: "καταβηθι" is the imperative form of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "go down" or "descend."
