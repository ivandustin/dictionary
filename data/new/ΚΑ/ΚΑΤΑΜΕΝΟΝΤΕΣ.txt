Etymology: The word "καταμενοντες" comes from the Greek verb "καταμένω" (kataméno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". The suffix "-ντες" (-ntes) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing.

Grammar: "καταμενοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "καταμένω" (kataméno) in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action or state. In English, it can be translated as "staying" or "remaining".

Description: "καταμενοντες" is a word in Greek that describes the action of staying or remaining. It can be used to refer to people who are staying in a particular place or situation.
