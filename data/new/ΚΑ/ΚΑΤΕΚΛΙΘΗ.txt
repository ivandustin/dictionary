Etymology: The word "κατεκλιθη" comes from the Greek verb "κατακλίνω" (kataklíno), which means "to recline" or "to lie down." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "κλίνω" (klíno), meaning "to lean" or "to incline."

Grammar: "κατεκλιθη" is the third person singular, aorist tense, passive voice form of the verb "κατακλίνω" (kataklíno). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "κατεκλιθη" describes an action that occurred in the past, where the subject was reclined or laid down. It can be translated as "he/she/it was reclined" or "he/she/it lay down."
