Etymology: The word "κατεκαίον" comes from the Greek verb "κατεκαίω" (katekaio), which means "to burn down" or "to consume by fire." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "καίω" (kaio), meaning "to burn."

Grammar: "κατεκαίον" is a Greek noun in the neuter gender. It is in the nominative case and singular number. In Greek grammar, nouns decline according to gender, number, and case.

Description: "κατεκαίον" refers to something that has been burned down or consumed by fire. It can be used to describe a building, a forest, or any other object that has been destroyed by fire.
