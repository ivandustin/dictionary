Etymology: The word "κατακεκριται" comes from the Greek verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno), which means "to condemn" or "to judge against". It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "κρίνω" (kríno), meaning "to judge".

Grammar: "κατακεκριται" is a verb in the perfect tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. The perfect tense indicates a completed action with ongoing results. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood is used to state a fact or make a statement.

Description: "κατακεκριται" means "has been condemned" or "has been judged against" in English. It is used to describe a situation where someone or something has been officially declared guilty or deserving of punishment.
