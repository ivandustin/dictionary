Etymology: The word "καταβατώ" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend". The suffix "-ω" is added to form the present tense of the verb.

Grammar: "καταβατώ" is a first-person singular present tense form of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino). It is used to express the action of going down or descending in the present tense.

Description: "καταβατώ" is a Greek verb that describes the act of going down or descending. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically descending from a higher place to a lower place or metaphorically descending in terms of status or rank.
