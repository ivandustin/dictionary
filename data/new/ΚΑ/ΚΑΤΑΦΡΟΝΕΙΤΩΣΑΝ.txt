It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "φρονέω" (phroneo), meaning "to think" or "to have an opinion".

Grammar: "καταφρονείτωσαν" is a verb in the third person plural, present tense, imperative mood, and active voice. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to despise or look down upon something or someone.
