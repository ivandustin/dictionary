Etymology: The word "καλεσατε" (kalesate) comes from the Greek verb "καλείν" (kalein), which means "to call" or "to invite."

Grammar: "Καλεσατε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "καλείν" (kalein). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to call or invite someone.

Description: "Καλεσατε" is a Greek verb form that is used to tell a group of people to call or invite someone. It is commonly used in situations where multiple people need to be called or invited, such as when organizing an event or gathering.
