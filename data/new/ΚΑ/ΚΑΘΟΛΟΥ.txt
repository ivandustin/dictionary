Etymology: The word "καθολου" comes from the Greek word "καθόλου" which means "at all" or "altogether". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (meaning "according to" or "throughout") and the adverb "ὅλος" (meaning "whole" or "entire").

Grammar: In Greek, "καθολου" is an adverb. It is used to express the idea of "completely" or "entirely". It is often used in negative sentences to indicate the absence or lack of something.

Description: "καθολου" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used in everyday speech to emphasize the absence or lack of something. For example, "Δεν έχω καθόλου χρόνο" (I don't have any time at all) or "Δεν ξέρω καθόλου την πόλη" (I don't know the city at all).
