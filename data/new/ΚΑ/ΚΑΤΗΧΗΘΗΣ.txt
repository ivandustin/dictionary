Etymology: The word "κατηχηθης" comes from the Greek verb "κατηχέω" (katēcheō), which means "to instruct" or "to teach." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to," and the verb "ἠχέω" (ēcheō), meaning "to sound" or "to resound." 

Grammar: "Κατηχηθης" is the masculine form of the adjective in the nominative singular. It is used to describe someone who has been instructed or taught.
