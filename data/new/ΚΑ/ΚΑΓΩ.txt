Etymology: The word "καγω" (kago) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the words "καί" (kai) meaning "and" and "ἐγώ" (ego) meaning "I". 

Grammar: "καγω" is a first-person singular pronoun in Greek. It is used to refer to oneself, similar to the English pronoun "I".

Description: "καγω" is a personal pronoun that is used to indicate the speaker or writer of a sentence. It is commonly used in Greek to express one's identity or to take responsibility for an action.
