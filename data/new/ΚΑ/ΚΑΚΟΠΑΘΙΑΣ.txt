Etymology: The word "κακοπαθίας" comes from the Greek roots "κακός" (kakos), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "πάθος" (pathos), meaning "suffering" or "disease." 

Grammar: "κακοπαθίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. It is derived from the noun "κακοπάθεια" (kakopatheia), which means "suffering" or "affliction." 

Description: "κακοπαθίας" refers to the state or condition of suffering or experiencing affliction. It can be used to describe physical or mental suffering, hardship, or distress.
