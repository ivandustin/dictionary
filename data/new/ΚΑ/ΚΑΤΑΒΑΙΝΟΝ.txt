Etymology: The word "καταβαίνον" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go."

Grammar: "καταβαίνον" is the present participle form of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "καταβαίνον" can be translated as "going down" or "descending." It describes the action of someone or something moving from a higher position to a lower position. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing physical movement, metaphorical descent, or the act of descending in rank or status.
