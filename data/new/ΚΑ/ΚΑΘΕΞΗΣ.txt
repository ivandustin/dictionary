Etymology: The word "καθεξής" comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "through," and "ἕξις" (hexis), meaning "state" or "condition." 

Grammar: "καθεξής" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe actions or events that occur in a consecutive or uninterrupted sequence.

Description: "καθεξής" is often translated as "successively," "in a row," or "one after another." It is used to indicate that something is happening or being done continuously or without interruption.
