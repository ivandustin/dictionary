Etymology: The word "καλεσούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to invite." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-.

Grammar: "καλεσούσιν" is the third person plural, future indicative active form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo) in Greek. It indicates that the subject will call or invite someone in the future.

Description: "καλεσούσιν" is a Greek verb form that expresses an action of calling or inviting that will happen in the future. It is used when referring to a third person plural subject.
