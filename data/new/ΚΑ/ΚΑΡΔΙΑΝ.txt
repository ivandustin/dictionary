Etymology: The word "καρδιαν" comes from the Greek word "καρδία" (kardia), meaning "heart". 

Grammar: "καρδιαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. 

Description: "καρδιαν" refers to the heart in Greek. It is used to describe the organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body. The heart is vital for the circulatory system and is often associated with emotions and feelings in various cultures.
