Etymology: The word "καινού" (kainou) comes from the Greek adjective "καινός" (kainos), which means "new" or "fresh."

Grammar: "Καινού" is the genitive form of the word "καινός" (kainos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Καινού" is used to describe something that belongs to or originates from something new or fresh. It is often used in phrases like "του καινού έτους" (tou kainou etous), which means "of the new year."
