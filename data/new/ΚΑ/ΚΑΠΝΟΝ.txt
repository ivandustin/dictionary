Etymology: The word "καπνόν" (kapnon) comes from the Ancient Greek word "καπνός" (kapnos), meaning "smoke". 

Grammar: "Καπνόν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the neuter gender and singular number. 

Description: "Καπνόν" refers to "smoke" in Greek. It is the visible gas and particles that are released when a substance undergoes combustion or pyrolysis. Smoke is often associated with fire and can be produced by various sources such as cigarettes, chimneys, or forest fires.
