Etymology: The word "κατηλθον" (katēlthon) comes from the Greek verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend." It is derived from the prefix "κατ-" (kat-), meaning "down," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "κατηλθον" is the aorist tense of the Greek verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai). The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or completion.

Description: "κατηλθον" is a Greek word that means "I came down" or "I descended." It is often used in a literal sense to describe the act of physically coming down from a higher place to a lower place. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe a descent in status, rank, or moral character.
