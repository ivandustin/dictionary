Etymology: The word "καθαρισμός" (katharismos) comes from the Greek verb "καθαρίζω" (katharizo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify."

Grammar: "Καθαρισμός" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "καθαρισμού" (katharismou).

Description: "Καθαρισμός" refers to the act or process of cleaning, purifying, or making something free from impurities or contaminants. It can be used in various contexts, such as cleaning physical objects, purifying substances, or even metaphorically referring to the purification of one's thoughts or actions.
