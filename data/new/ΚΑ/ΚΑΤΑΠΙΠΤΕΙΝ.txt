Etymology: The word "καταπιπτειν" (katapiptein) comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "πίπτειν" (piptein), meaning "to fall." 

Grammar: "καταπιπτειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. 

Description: "καταπιπτειν" means "to fall down" or "to collapse" in Greek. It is used to describe the action of something or someone falling or collapsing downward.
