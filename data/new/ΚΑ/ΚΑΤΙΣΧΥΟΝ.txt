Etymology: The word "κατισχύων" comes from the Greek verb "κατισχύω" (katischýo), which means "to prevail" or "to have power over." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "ἰσχύω" (ischýo), meaning "to be strong" or "to have power."

Grammar: "κατισχύων" is the present participle form of the verb "κατισχύω" in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate an ongoing action or state. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "κατισχύων" describes something or someone that is prevailing or having power over others. It can be used to refer to a person who is dominant or influential in a particular situation. As a present participle, it implies an ongoing or continuous action of prevailing or having power.
