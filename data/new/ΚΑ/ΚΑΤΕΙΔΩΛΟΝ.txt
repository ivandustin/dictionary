Etymology: The word "κατειδωλον" (kateidolon) comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "εἴδωλον" (eidolon), meaning "image" or "idol." 

Grammar: "Κατειδωλον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is in the singular form.

Description: "Κατειδωλον" refers to an idol or an image that is worshipped or revered. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is idolized or held in high regard.
