Etymology: The word "και" (kai) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷe, meaning "and" or "also."

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "και" is a conjunction. It is used to connect words, phrases, or clauses, indicating addition, continuation, or similarity. It is equivalent to the English word "and."

Description: "και" is one of the most commonly used words in the Greek language. It is a simple and versatile conjunction that plays a crucial role in connecting ideas and expressing relationships between different elements in a sentence.
