Etymology: The word "κατακεισθαι" comes from the Greek verb "κατακείμαι" (katakeimai), which is a compound of the preposition "κατά" (kata) meaning "down" or "according to," and the verb "κεῖμαι" (keimai) meaning "to lie" or "to be situated."

Grammar: "κατακεισθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "κατακείμαι" in Greek. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action or is involved in the action in some way.

Description: "κατακεισθαι" means "to lie down" or "to be situated" in Greek. It is used to describe the action of someone or something being in a reclining or resting position.
