Etymology: The word "καταλυσαι" comes from the Greek verb "καταλύω" (katalýo), which means "to dissolve" or "to destroy." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "λύω" (lýo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie."

Grammar: "καταλυσαι" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in the active voice of the Greek verb "καταλύω." It is used to address one person and indicates an action that occurred in the past.

Description: "καταλυσαι" is a verb form that expresses the command "dissolve" or "destroy" in Greek. It is used when instructing or ordering someone to break down or dismantle something.
