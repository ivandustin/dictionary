Etymology: The word "κατηγορείν" (katēgorein) comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuō), meaning "to speak in public." 

Grammar: "κατηγορείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: The word "κατηγορείν" means "to accuse" or "to charge" in English. It refers to the act of making an accusation or bringing charges against someone in a legal or formal context.
