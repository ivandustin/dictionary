Etymology: The word "καταστησομεν" comes from the Greek verb "καταστήνω" (katasténo), which means "to establish" or "to set up". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to", and the verb "ἵστημι" (histēmi), meaning "to stand" or "to place".

Grammar: "καταστησομεν" is a verb in the future tense, first person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The first person plural form indicates that the action is performed by a group of people, in this case, "we".

Description: "καταστησομεν" is a Greek verb that translates to "we will establish" or "we will set up" in English. It implies the act of creating or organizing something in the future.
