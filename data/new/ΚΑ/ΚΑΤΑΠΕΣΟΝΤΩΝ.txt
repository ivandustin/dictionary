Grammar: "καταπεσοντων" is the present participle form of the verb "καταπίπτω" in the genitive plural masculine case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin, and the plural form indicates multiple subjects.

Description: "καταπεσοντων" can be translated as "of those falling down" or "of those descending." It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently in the process of falling down or descending.
