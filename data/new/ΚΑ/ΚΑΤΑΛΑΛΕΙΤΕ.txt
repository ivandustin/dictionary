Etymology: The word "καταλαλεῖτε" comes from the Greek verb "καταλαλέω" (katalaleo), which means "to speak against" or "to slander." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "against," and the verb "λαλέω" (laleo), meaning "to speak."

Grammar: "καταλαλεῖτε" is the second person plural present active indicative form of the verb "καταλαλέω" in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are currently speaking against or slandering someone.

Description: The word "καταλαλεῖτε" is used to describe the act of speaking negatively or maliciously about someone, often with the intention of damaging their reputation. It implies gossiping, spreading rumors, or engaging in slanderous speech.
