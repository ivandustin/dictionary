Etymology: The word "κατευθύναι" comes from the Greek verb "κατευθύνω" (kateuthyno), which means "to direct" or "to guide." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "towards," and the verb "ευθύνω" (euthyno), meaning "to straighten" or "to make straight."

Grammar: "κατευθύναι" is the third person singular, present tense, indicative mood form of the verb "κατευθύνω" in Greek. It is used to describe an action that is happening in the present and is performed by a third person.

Description: The word "κατευθύναι" refers to the act of directing or guiding someone or something towards a specific destination or goal. It implies the action of providing instructions or showing the way to someone or something.
