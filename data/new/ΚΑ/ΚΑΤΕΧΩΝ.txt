Etymology: The word "κατεχων" comes from the Greek verb "κατέχω" (katechō), which means "to hold down" or "to possess." It is derived from the prefix "κατ-" (kat-), meaning "down," and the verb "έχω" (echō), meaning "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar: "Κατεχων" is the present participle form of the verb "κατέχω" (katechō) in the masculine singular form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Description: "Κατεχων" can be translated as "holding down" or "possessing" in English. It describes someone or something that is currently holding or possessing something.
