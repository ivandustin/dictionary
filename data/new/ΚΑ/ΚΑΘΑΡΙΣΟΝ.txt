Etymology: The word "καθαρισμόν" (katharismón) comes from the Greek verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify."

Grammar: "Καθαρισμόν" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word "καθαρισμόν" is in the neuter gender and the accusative case.

Description: "Καθαρισμόν" refers to the act of cleansing or purifying. It can also be used to describe the state of being clean or pure.
