Etymology: The word "καυχημα" (kaukhēma) comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaukhaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag."

Grammar: "Καυχημα" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word "καυχημα" is neuter and singular.

Description: "Καυχημα" refers to something that is boasted about or bragged about. It can be used to describe an accomplishment, a possession, or any other thing that someone takes pride in and wants to show off.
