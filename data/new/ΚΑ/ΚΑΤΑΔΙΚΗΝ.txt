Etymology: The word "καταδικην" comes from the Greek word "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the word "δίκη" (diki), meaning "judgment" or "trial." 

Grammar: "καταδικην" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative form of the noun "καταδίκη" (katadiki), which means "condemnation" or "sentence."

Description: "καταδικην" refers to the act of condemning or sentencing someone. It is used to describe the legal judgment or punishment imposed on a person who has been found guilty of a crime.
