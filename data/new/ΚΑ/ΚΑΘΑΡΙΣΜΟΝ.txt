Etymology: The word "καθαρισμόν" (katharismón) comes from the Greek verb "καθαρίζω" (katharízo), which means "to clean" or "to purify."

Grammar: "Καθαρισμόν" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the neuter gender and the accusative case.

Description: "Καθαρισμόν" refers to the act of cleaning or purifying something. It can also be used to describe the state of being clean or pure.
