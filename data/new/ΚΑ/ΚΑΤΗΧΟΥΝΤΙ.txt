Etymology: The word "κατηχούντι" comes from the Greek verb "κατηχέω" (katēchéō), which means "to instruct" or "to teach." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to," and the verb "ἠχέω" (ēchéō), meaning "to sound" or "to resound."

Grammar: "Κατηχούντι" is the present participle form of the verb "κατηχέω" (katēchéō) in the third person singular. It is used to describe someone who is currently instructing or teaching.

Description: "Κατηχούντι" refers to a person who is actively engaged in the act of instructing or teaching. It can be used to describe someone who imparts knowledge or guidance to others, particularly in a religious or educational context.
