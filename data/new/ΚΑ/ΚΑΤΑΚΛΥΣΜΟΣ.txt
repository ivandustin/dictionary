Etymology: The word "κατακλυσμός" comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "κλύζω" (klyzo), meaning "to wash" or "to flood." 

Grammar: "Κατακλυσμός" is a masculine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Κατακλυσμός" refers to a flood or deluge, typically caused by excessive rainfall or the overflow of bodies of water. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a large amount or overwhelming quantity of something.
