Etymology: The word "καταλαλεῖσθε" comes from the Greek verb "καταλαλέω" (katalaleo), which means "to speak against" or "to slander." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "against," and the verb "λαλέω" (laleo), meaning "to speak."

Grammar: "καταλαλεῖσθε" is a verb form in the present tense, passive voice, and second person plural form. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are being spoken against or slandered.

Description: The word "καταλαλεῖσθε" is found in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in 1 Peter 2:12. It is used to encourage believers to live honorable lives and to not engage in slander or speaking against others. The word carries a negative connotation and emphasizes the importance of speaking positively and avoiding harmful speech.
