Etymology: The word "κατακαυθήσεται" comes from the Greek verb "κατακαίω" (katakaiō), which means "to burn down" or "to consume by fire". The suffix "-σεται" (-setai) indicates the future tense, so "κατακαυθήσεται" means "it will be burned down" or "it will be consumed by fire".

Grammar: "κατακαυθήσεται" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "κατακαίω" (katakaiō) and is conjugated in the future tense to indicate that the action of burning down or consuming by fire will happen in the future.

Description: "κατακαυθήσεται" describes the action of something being burned down or consumed by fire in the future. It can be used to refer to the destruction of a building, object, or any other entity by fire.
