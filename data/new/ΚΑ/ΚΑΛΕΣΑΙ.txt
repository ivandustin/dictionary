Etymology: The word "καλεσαί" comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". 

Grammar: "καλεσαί" is the third person singular, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo). It is used to indicate that someone or something was called or summoned in the past.

Description: "καλεσαί" is a Greek verb form that specifically refers to the act of calling or summoning someone or something in the past. It is commonly used in narratives or descriptions to indicate that someone was called or summoned at a certain point in time.
