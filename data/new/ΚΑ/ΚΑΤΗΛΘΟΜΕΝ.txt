Etymology: The word "κατηλθομεν" comes from the Greek verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "κατηλθομεν" is the first person plural form of the verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai) in the aorist tense. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The first person plural form indicates that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description: "κατηλθομεν" is a Greek verb that means "we came down" or "we descended." It is used to describe the action of a group of people moving from a higher position to a lower position. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as descending from a mountain, descending from a higher level of authority, or descending from a higher spiritual realm.
