Etymology: The word "καλαμος" (kalamos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kelh₂- meaning "to strike, cut". 

Grammar: In Greek, "καλαμος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form (καλαμος) and a plural form (καλαμοι). 

Description: "Καλαμος" refers to a reed or a stalk, typically of a plant like bamboo or papyrus. It can also refer to a pen made from such a reed. In ancient times, reeds were commonly used as writing instruments.
