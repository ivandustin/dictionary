Etymology: The word "καθού" (kathou) comes from the Greek verb "καθέζομαι" (kathézomai), which means "to sit down" or "to be seated."

Grammar: "Καθού" is a second person singular imperative verb form of the verb "καθέζομαι." It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to sit down.

Description: "Καθού" is a Greek word that is used to tell someone to sit down. It is a direct and straightforward command, often used in situations where someone needs to be seated or when giving an order to sit down.
