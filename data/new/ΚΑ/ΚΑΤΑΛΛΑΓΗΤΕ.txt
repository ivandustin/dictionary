Etymology: The word "καταλλαγήτε" comes from the Greek verb "καταλλάσσω" (katallasso), which means "to reconcile" or "to make peace." The suffix "-τε" is added to the verb stem to form the imperative mood, giving the meaning "reconcile" or "make peace."

Grammar: "καταλλαγήτε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "καταλλάσσω" (katallasso). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to reconcile or make peace.

Description: "καταλλαγήτε" is a Greek word that is used to command or instruct a group of people to reconcile or make peace. It is often used in a context where there is conflict or disagreement, and the speaker is urging the group to resolve their differences and come to a peaceful resolution.
