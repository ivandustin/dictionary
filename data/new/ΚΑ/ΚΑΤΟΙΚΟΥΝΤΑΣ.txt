Etymology: The word "κατοικούντας" comes from the Greek verb "κατοικώ" (katoikó), which means "to inhabit" or "to dwell". The suffix "-ας" (-as) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Κατοικούντας" is the present participle form of the verb "κατοικώ" in Greek. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently inhabiting or dwelling in a place. In terms of grammar, it functions as an adjective or a noun modifier.

Description: "Κατοικούντας" can be translated as "inhabiting" or "dwelling" in English. It is often used to describe people or creatures who are currently living in a specific location. For example, "Οι κατοικούντες της πόλης" (Oi katoikúntes tis pólis) means "the inhabitants of the city".
