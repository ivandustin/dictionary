Etymology: The word "καταντήσαντες" comes from the Greek verb "καταντάω" (katan-tao), which means "to arrive at" or "to reach." The suffix "-σαντες" is a participle ending, indicating that the action of reaching or arriving has been completed.

Grammar: "καταντήσαντες" is the masculine plural form of the verb "καταντάω" in the aorist tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have reached a certain point or achieved a particular state.

Description: "καταντήσαντες" can be translated as "having reached" or "having arrived at." It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a group of individuals who have successfully accomplished something or have reached a desired outcome.
