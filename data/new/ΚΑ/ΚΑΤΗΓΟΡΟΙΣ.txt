Etymology: The word "κατηγοροις" comes from the Greek word "κατηγορέω" (katēgoreō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuō), meaning "to speak in public" or "to address".

Grammar: "κατηγοροις" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple accusers or chargers.

Description: "κατηγοροις" refers to a group of people who accuse or charge someone with a wrongdoing. It can be used in legal contexts to describe the prosecutors or accusers in a trial.
