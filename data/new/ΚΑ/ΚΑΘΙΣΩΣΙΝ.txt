Etymology: The word "καθισωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "καθίζω" (kathizō), which means "to sit down" or "to seat". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down", and the verb "ἵζω" (hizō), meaning "to sit".

Grammar: "καθισωσιν" is a verb form in the Greek language. It is the third person plural, aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "καθίζω" (kathizō). The aorist tense indicates a past action, and the subjunctive mood expresses possibility, potentiality, or doubt. The active voice indicates that the subject performs the action.

Description: "καθισωσιν" is used to express a hypothetical or potential action in the past. It can be translated as "they might have sat down" or "they could have seated". The subjunctive mood suggests uncertainty or doubt about the action actually taking place.
