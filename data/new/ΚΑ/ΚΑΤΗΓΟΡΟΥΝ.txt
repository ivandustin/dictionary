Etymology: The word "κατηγορούν" comes from the Greek verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreúō), meaning "to speak in public".

Grammar: "κατηγορούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoréō). In Greek grammar, it belongs to the class of verbs that end in "-ω" and follow the pattern of the verb "λύω" (lyō), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie".

Description: "κατηγορούν" is a verb that is used to express the action of accusing or charging someone with a wrongdoing. It is commonly used in legal contexts or when discussing allegations or charges against someone.
