Etymology: The word "καταλυθησεται" comes from the Greek verb "καταλύω" (katalýo), which means "to destroy" or "to dismantle". It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "λύω" (lýo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie".

Grammar: "καταλυθησεται" is a verb in the future tense, passive voice, and third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, voice, and person.

Description: The word "καταλυθησεται" describes an action that will happen in the future and will be done to the subject. It implies that something will be destroyed or dismantled.
