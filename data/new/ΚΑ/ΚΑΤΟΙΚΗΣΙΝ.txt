It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kat-a), meaning "down" or "through," and the verb "οἰκέω" (ee-keh-o), meaning "to dwell" or "to live."

Grammar: "κατοικησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. In Greek grammar, nouns decline based on their case, number, and gender to indicate their role in a sentence.

Description: "κατοικησιν" refers to the act of inhabiting or dwelling in a place. It can also be used to describe a settlement or a place where people live.
