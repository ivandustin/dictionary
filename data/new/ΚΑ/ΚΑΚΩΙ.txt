Etymology: The word "κακώς" (kakós) comes from the Greek adjective "κακός" (kakós), meaning "bad" or "evil".

Grammar: "Κακώς" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate that something is done or happening in a bad or wrong way.

Description: "Κακώς" is used to express the manner in which an action is performed, indicating that it is done poorly, incorrectly, or with negative consequences. It can also be used to express disapproval or criticism.
