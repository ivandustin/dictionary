Etymology: The word "καταπατείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "καταπατέω" (katapateo), which means "to trample upon" or "to tread on". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "upon", and the verb "πατέω" (pateo), meaning "to tread" or "to step".

Grammar: "καταπατείσθαι" is a verb in the present passive infinitive form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The present passive infinitive form indicates that the action of trampling upon or treading on is being done to the subject of the sentence.

Description: "καταπατείσθαι" is a Greek verb that describes the action of trampling upon or treading on something. It implies a forceful or aggressive action of stepping on or crushing something underfoot. The passive form suggests that the subject is being acted upon, rather than actively performing the action.
