Etymology: The word "καρδιών" comes from the Greek word "καρδία" (kardia), meaning "heart".

Grammar: "καρδιών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, singular form.

Description: "καρδιών" refers to the heart, the organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body. In Greek mythology and literature, the heart is often associated with emotions, feelings, and the seat of one's innermost thoughts.
