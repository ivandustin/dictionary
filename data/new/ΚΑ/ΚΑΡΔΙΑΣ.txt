Etymology: The word "καρδιάς" (kardias) comes from the Greek word "καρδία" (kardia), which means "heart". 

Grammar: "Καρδιάς" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and belongs to the second declension. 

Description: "Καρδιάς" refers to the heart, the organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body. In a figurative sense, it is also used to describe emotions, feelings, and the center of one's being.
