Etymology: The word "καταλαλών" comes from the Greek verb "καταλαλέω" (katalaleo), which means "to speak against" or "to slander." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "against," and the verb "λαλέω" (laleo), meaning "to speak."

Grammar: "καταλαλών" is the present participle form of the verb "καταλαλέω" (katalaleo). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "καταλαλών" is a Greek word that can be translated as "slanderer" or "one who speaks against." It refers to someone who spreads false or damaging information about another person with the intention of harming their reputation.
