Etymology: The word "καλεσάντες" comes from the Greek verb "καλείν" (kalein), which means "to call" or "to invite". The suffix "-αντες" is added to the verb stem to form the participle form, indicating a group of people who have been called or invited.

Grammar: "Καλεσάντες" is the plural masculine form of the participle. In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives. They agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "καλεσάντες" modifies a plural masculine noun.

Description: "Καλεσάντες" can be translated as "those who have been called" or "those who have been invited" in English. It refers to a group of people who have received an invitation or have been called to a specific event or gathering.
