Etymology: The word "κατατομήν" comes from the Greek verb "κατατέμνω" (katatemnō), which means "to cut down" or "to divide". It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "through", and the verb "τέμνω" (temnō), meaning "to cut".

Grammar: "κατατομήν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "κατατομήν" refers to the act of cutting down or dividing something. It can be used to describe the action of physically cutting or dividing an object, or it can be used metaphorically to describe the process of analyzing or dissecting a concept or idea.
