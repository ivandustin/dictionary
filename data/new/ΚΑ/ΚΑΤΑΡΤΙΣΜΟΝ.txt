Etymology: The word "καταρτισμόν" comes from the Greek verb "καταρτίζω" (katartízo), which means "to prepare" or "to equip." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "according to," and the verb "ἀρτίζω" (artízo), meaning "to fit" or "to arrange."

Grammar: "καταρτισμόν" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension and is classified as a neuter noun.

Description: "καταρτισμόν" refers to the act or process of preparing, equipping, or arranging something. It can also denote the state of being well-prepared or well-equipped. The word is often used in contexts related to training, organization, or the development of skills.
