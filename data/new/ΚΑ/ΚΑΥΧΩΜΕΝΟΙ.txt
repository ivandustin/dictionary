Etymology: The word "καυχώμενοι" (kaukhómenoi) comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaukháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag."

Grammar: "Καυχώμενοι" is the present participle form of the verb "καυχάομαι" in the masculine plural. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently boasting or bragging.

Description: "Καυχώμενοι" refers to a group of people who are actively boasting or bragging about themselves or their achievements. It implies a sense of pride and self-promotion.
