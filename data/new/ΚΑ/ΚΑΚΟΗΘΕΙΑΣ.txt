Etymology: The word "κακοηθείας" comes from the Greek roots "κακός" (kakos), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "ἠθέω" (ētheō), meaning "to be accustomed to" or "to be inclined to." 

Grammar: "κακοηθείας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. It is derived from the base form "κακοηθεία" (kakoētheia), which means "malice" or "ill-will." 

Description: "κακοηθείας" refers to the state or quality of being malicious or having ill-will towards others. It is often used to describe someone's tendency or inclination to act in a harmful or malevolent way.
