Etymology: The word "κατηγορησω" (katēgorēsō) comes from the Greek verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuō), meaning "to speak in public".

Grammar: "κατηγορησω" is a first-person singular future active indicative form of the verb "κατηγορέω". In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "κατηγορησω" is a Greek word that means "I will accuse" or "I will charge". It is used to express the act of making an accusation or bringing charges against someone.
