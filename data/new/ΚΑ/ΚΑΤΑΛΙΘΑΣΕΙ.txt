Etymology: The word "καταλιθάσει" comes from the Greek verb "καταλιθάζω" (katalithazo), which means "to stone" or "to pelt with stones". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the noun "λίθος" (lithos), meaning "stone".

Grammar: "καταλιθάσει" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: The word "καταλιθάσει" describes the act of stoning or pelting someone or something with stones. It implies a violent action of throwing stones at a target.
