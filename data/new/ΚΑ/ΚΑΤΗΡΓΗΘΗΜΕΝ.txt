Etymology: The word "κατηργηθήμεν" comes from the Greek verb "καταργέω" (katargeo), which means "to abolish" or "to nullify". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "ἀργέω" (argeo), meaning "to be idle" or "to be inactive".

Grammar: "κατηργηθήμεν" is the aorist passive subjunctive form of the verb "καταργέω" (katargeo) in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The subjunctive mood suggests a possibility or potentiality.

Description: "κατηργηθήμεν" is used to express the idea of something being abolished, nullified, or rendered inactive. It implies that a certain action or state has been completed in the past, and it often carries a sense of finality or permanence.
