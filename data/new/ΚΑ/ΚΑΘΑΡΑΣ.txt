Etymology: The word "καθαρας" (katharas) comes from the Greek adjective "καθαρός" (katharos), meaning "clean" or "pure."

Grammar: "Καθαρας" is the nominative singular form of the word. It is a masculine noun in Greek.

Description: "Καθαρας" is a Greek word that can be translated as "clean" or "pure." It is often used to describe something that is free from dirt, impurities, or contamination. In a figurative sense, it can also refer to something that is morally pure or untainted.
