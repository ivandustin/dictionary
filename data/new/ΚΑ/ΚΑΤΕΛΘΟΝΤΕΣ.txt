Etymology: The word "κατελθοντες" comes from the Greek verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend." It is derived from the prefix "κατ-" (kat-), meaning "down," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "κατελθοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai) in the active voice. It is used to describe an ongoing action of coming down or descending in the present tense.

Description: "κατελθοντες" is a Greek word that describes the act of coming down or descending. It can be used to refer to physical descent, such as descending a mountain or descending from a higher place to a lower place. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a decrease or decline in a non-physical sense.
