Etymology: The word "καινοτερον" comes from the Greek word "καινός" (kainos), meaning "new" or "fresh."

Grammar: "καινοτερον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "καινοτερον" refers to something that is newer or more recent. It can be used to describe an object, idea, or concept that is considered to be of a more recent origin or development.
