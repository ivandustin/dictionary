Etymology: The word "κατισχύσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "κατισχύω" (katischýo), which means "to prevail" or "to overcome." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "ισχύω" (ischýo), meaning "to be strong" or "to have power."

Grammar: "κατισχύσουσιν" is a verb form in the future tense, third person plural, active voice, and indicative mood. It indicates that the subject (third person plural) will prevail or overcome in the future.

Description: "κατισχύσουσιν" is a Greek word that expresses the idea of prevailing or overcoming. It can be used in various contexts to describe situations where someone or something gains the upper hand, succeeds, or triumphs over others.
