Etymology: The word "κατειχον" (kateichon) comes from the Greek verb "κατέχω" (katecho), which means "to hold back" or "to restrain."

Grammar: "κατειχον" is the third person singular, past tense form of the verb "κατέχω" (katecho) in the Greek language.

Description: "κατειχον" is a word that describes an action that occurred in the past. It indicates that someone or something was holding back or restraining something or someone else.
