Etymology: The word "καταντήσωμεν" comes from the Greek verb "καταντάω" (katan-tao), which means "to arrive at" or "to reach." It is formed by combining the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "towards," and the verb "αντάω" (antao), meaning "to come" or "to go."

Grammar: "καταντήσωμεν" is a verb in the first person plural, aorist subjunctive mood, and active voice. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the subjunctive mood expresses possibility, necessity, or doubt. The active voice indicates that the subject performs the action.

Description: "καταντήσωμεν" is a Greek verb that can be translated as "let us arrive at" or "let us reach." It is often used to express the idea of reaching a certain state or condition.
