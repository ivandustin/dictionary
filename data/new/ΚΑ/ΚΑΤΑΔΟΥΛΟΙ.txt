Etymology: The word "καταδούλοι" comes from the Greek words "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "δούλος" (doulos), meaning "slave." It literally translates to "down-slaves" or "against-slaves."

Grammar: "καταδούλοι" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple slaves.

Description: "καταδούλοι" refers to slaves who are oppressed or treated poorly. It can also be used metaphorically to describe people who are subjugated or controlled by others.
