Etymology: The word "κατηλθαμεν" comes from the Greek verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend." It is formed by combining the prefix "κατ-" (kat-), meaning "down," and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "κατηλθαμεν" is the first person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "κατηλθαμεν" can be translated as "we came down" or "we descended" in English. It implies a movement from a higher position to a lower position. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing physical descent from a mountain or figurative descent in a metaphorical sense.
