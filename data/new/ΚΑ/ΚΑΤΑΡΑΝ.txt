Etymology: The word "καταραν" comes from the Greek word "κατάρα" (katára), which means "curse" or "malediction".

Grammar: "καταραν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "καταραν" refers to a curse or a malediction. It is used to describe a spoken or written expression of a wish for harm or misfortune to befall someone or something. Curses can be found in various cultures and are often believed to have supernatural or magical powers.
