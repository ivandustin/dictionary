Etymology: The word "καυματισαί" comes from the Greek verb "καυματίζω" (kaumatízo), which means "to burn" or "to scorch." It is derived from the noun "καῦμα" (kauma), meaning "burn" or "heat."

Grammar: "καυματισαί" is a plural noun in the feminine gender. It belongs to the first declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "καυματισαί" refers to women who have been burned or scorched. It can be used metaphorically to describe women who have experienced hardship or suffering.
