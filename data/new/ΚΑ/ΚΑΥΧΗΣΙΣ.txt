Etymology: The word "καυχησις" (kaukhēsis) comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaukhaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag."

Grammar: In Greek, "καυχησις" is a noun of feminine gender. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Καυχησις" refers to the act of boasting or bragging about one's achievements, abilities, or possessions. It can also imply excessive pride or arrogance.
