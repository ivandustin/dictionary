Etymology: The word "κατεναρκησα" comes from the Greek verb "καταναρκάω" (katanarkao), which means "to numb" or "to put to sleep". It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "through", and the verb "ναρκάω" (narkao), meaning "to numb" or "to make numb".

Grammar: "κατεναρκησα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "καταναρκάω" (katanarkao). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "κατεναρκησα" describes the action of numbing or putting to sleep. It indicates that the speaker (first person singular) has performed this action in the past.
