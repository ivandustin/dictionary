Etymology: The word "καταψυξη" comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "ψύξη" (psyxi), meaning "cooling" or "freezing." 

Grammar: "καταψυξη" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender.

Description: "καταψυξη" refers to the process of freezing or the state of being frozen. It is commonly used to describe the solidification of a substance due to a decrease in temperature.
