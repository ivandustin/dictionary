Etymology: The word "καταπατήσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "καταπατέω" (katapateo), which means "to trample upon" or "to tread on". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "upon", and the verb "πατέω" (pateo), meaning "to tread" or "to step".

Grammar: "καταπατήσουσιν" is a verb form in the future tense, third person plural, active voice, and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated to indicate the subject, tense, mood, and voice of the action.

Description: The word "καταπατήσουσιν" describes an action that will be performed in the future by a third person plural subject. It conveys the idea of trampling upon or treading on something or someone.
