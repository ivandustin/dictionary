Etymology: The word "καταφαγε" comes from the Greek verb "καταφάγω" (katafago), which is a compound of the preposition "κατά" (kata) meaning "down" or "through" and the verb "φάγω" (fago) meaning "to eat". 

Grammar: "καταφαγε" is the aorist tense, third person singular form of the verb "καταφάγω" (katafago). The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description: "καταφαγε" means "he/she/it ate up" in English. It is used to indicate that someone or something completely consumed or devoured something.
