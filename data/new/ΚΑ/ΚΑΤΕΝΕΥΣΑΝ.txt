Etymology: The word "κατενεύσαν" comes from the Greek verb "κατενεύω" (katenévo), which means "to nod in agreement" or "to approve." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (katá), meaning "down" or "according to," and the verb "νεύω" (névo), meaning "to nod."

Grammar: "κατενεύσαν" is the third person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "κατενεύω" (katenévo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "κατενεύσαν" describes the action of a group of people nodding in agreement or approval. It implies that the subject(s) acknowledged or accepted something by nodding their heads.
