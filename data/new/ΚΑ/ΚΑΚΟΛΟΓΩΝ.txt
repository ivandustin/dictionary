Etymology: The word "κακολογών" comes from the Greek root words "κακός" (kakos) meaning "bad" or "evil" and "λέγω" (lego) meaning "to speak". 

Grammar: "κακολογών" is the present participle form of the verb "κακολογώ" (kakologo), which means "to speak ill of" or "to slander". It is used to describe someone who is currently engaging in the act of speaking negatively about someone else.

Description: "κακολογών" refers to a person who is speaking ill of others or spreading negative gossip. It is often used to describe someone who engages in malicious or slanderous speech.
