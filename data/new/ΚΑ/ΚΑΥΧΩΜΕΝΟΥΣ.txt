Etymology: The word "καυχώμενος" (kaukhómenos) comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaukháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag."

Grammar: "Καυχώμενος" is the present participle form of the verb "καυχάομαι" in the masculine singular nominative case. It is used to describe someone who is currently boasting or bragging.

Description: "Καυχώμενος" refers to a person who is actively boasting or bragging about their achievements, qualities, or possessions. It implies a sense of pride and self-importance.
