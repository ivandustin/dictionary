Etymology: The word "κατηγορίαν" (katēgorían) comes from the Greek word "κατηγορέω" (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to speak against". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreúō), meaning "to speak in public".

Grammar: "κατηγορίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "κατηγορίαν" refers to the act of accusing or making a formal statement against someone. It can also be used to describe a category or classification of things. In a legal context, it refers to the formal charge or accusation brought against a person in a court of law.
