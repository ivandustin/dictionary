Etymology: The word "καθισαντος" comes from the Greek verb "καθίζω" (kathízo), which means "to sit down" or "to seat". The suffix "-αντος" (-antos) is a participle ending that indicates the past tense and masculine gender.

Grammar: "καθισαντος" is the genitive form of the word, which is used to indicate possession or origin. It is declined according to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "καθισαντος" can be translated as "of the one who sat down" or "of the one who was seated" in English. It is commonly used in Greek texts to refer to someone who has sat down or taken a seat.
