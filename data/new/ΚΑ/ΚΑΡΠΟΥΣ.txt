Etymology: The word "καρπούς" (karpoús) comes from the Greek word "καρπός" (karpos), meaning "fruit" or "produce."

Grammar: In Greek, "καρπούς" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the plural form of "καρπός" (karpos), which is a masculine noun.

Description: "Καρπούς" refers to fruits or produce. It can be used to describe the edible parts of plants that contain seeds, such as apples, oranges, or tomatoes. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the result or outcome of someone's efforts or actions.
