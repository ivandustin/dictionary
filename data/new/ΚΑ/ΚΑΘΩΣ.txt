Etymology: The word "καθώς" comes from the Greek word "καθώς" (kathōs), which means "just as" or "in the same way". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "according to" or "in accordance with", and the adverb "ὡς" (hōs), meaning "as" or "like".

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "καθώς" is a conjunction. It is used to introduce a subordinate clause that expresses a comparison or similarity to the main clause. It is often translated as "as", "just as", or "like" in English.

Description: "καθώς" is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to establish a comparison or similarity between two actions, events, or situations. It helps to provide additional information or clarify the relationship between the main clause and the subordinate clause.
