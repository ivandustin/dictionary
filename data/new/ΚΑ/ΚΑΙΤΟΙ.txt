Etymology: The word "καίτοι" comes from the Greek language. It is a conjunction that combines the words "καί" (meaning "and") and "τοι" (meaning "though" or "although").

Grammar: "Καίτοι" is a conjunction used to introduce a concessive clause, indicating a contrast or contradiction to the main clause. It is often translated as "although" or "though" in English.

Description: "Καίτοι" is used to express a concession or contradiction in a sentence. It is commonly used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or contradicts the main idea of the sentence. It adds a sense of unexpectedness or surprise to the sentence.
