Etymology: The word "κακωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "κακόω" (kakóo), which means "to harm" or "to injure."

Grammar: "κακωσιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender. It is the accusative form of the noun "κακωσις" (kakósis), which means "injury" or "harm."

Description: "κακωσιν" refers to a specific instance or act of causing harm or injury. It can be used to describe physical, emotional, or psychological harm inflicted upon someone or something.
