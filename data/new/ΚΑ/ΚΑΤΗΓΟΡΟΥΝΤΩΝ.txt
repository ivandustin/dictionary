Etymology: The word "κατηγορούντων" comes from the Greek verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreúō), meaning "to speak in public".

Grammar: "κατηγορούντων" is the present active participle, masculine genitive plural form of the verb "κατηγορέω" (katēgoréō). In Greek grammar, the present active participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The genitive plural form indicates that the noun it modifies is in the genitive case and refers to multiple individuals or things.

Description: "κατηγορούντων" can be translated as "of those accusing" or "of the accusers" in English. It is often used in legal or judicial contexts to refer to the group of people who are making accusations or charges against someone.
