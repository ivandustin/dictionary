Etymology: The word "κατεσταθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "κατασταθῆναι" (katastathēnai), which is a combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata) meaning "down" or "according to" and the verb "ἵστημι" (histēmi) meaning "to stand". 

Grammar: "κατεσταθησαν" is the third person plural form of the aorist passive indicative tense of the verb "κατασταθῆναι". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "κατεσταθησαν" can be translated as "they were established" or "they were set up" in English. It refers to a group of people or things being put in a certain state or position.
