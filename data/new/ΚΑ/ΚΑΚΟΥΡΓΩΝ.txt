Etymology: The word "κακουργών" comes from the Greek root "κακός" (kakos), meaning "bad" or "evil," and the verb "εργάζομαι" (ergazomai), meaning "to work." Therefore, "κακουργών" can be translated as "one who works evil" or "evildoer."

Grammar: "κακουργών" is a noun in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is the present participle form of the verb "κακουργώ" (kakourgó), which means "to commit evil deeds" or "to engage in wickedness."

Description: "κακουργών" refers to a person who is actively involved in doing evil or engaging in wicked actions. It is often used to describe someone who commits crimes or engages in immoral behavior.
