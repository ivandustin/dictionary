Etymology: The word "καππαδοκιαν" comes from the Greek word "Καππαδοκία" (Kappadokia), which is the name of a historical region in central Anatolia, Turkey.

Grammar: "Καππαδοκιαν" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is used to describe something or someone related to the region of Cappadocia.

Description: "Καππαδοκιαν" refers to anything or anyone that is associated with Cappadocia, a region known for its unique geological formations, ancient cave dwellings, and rich history.
