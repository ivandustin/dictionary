Etymology: The word "καθαρώι" (katharói) comes from the Greek adjective "καθαρός" (katharós), meaning "clean" or "pure."

Grammar: "Καθαρώι" is the plural form of the adjective "καθαρός" (katharós) in the nominative case. It is used to describe multiple nouns that are clean or pure.

Description: "Καθαρώι" is a Greek word that refers to multiple things or people that are clean or pure. It can be used to describe physical cleanliness or purity, as well as moral or spiritual purity.
