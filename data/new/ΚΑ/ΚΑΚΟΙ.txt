Etymology: The word "κακοί" (kakoi) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "κακός" (kakos), which means "bad" or "evil".

Grammar: "Κακοί" is the plural form of the adjective "κακός" (kakos). In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "κακοί" is used to describe plural nouns of masculine gender.

Description: "Κακοί" is an adjective in Greek that means "bad" or "evil". It is used to describe people, actions, or things that are morally wrong, harmful, or of poor quality.
