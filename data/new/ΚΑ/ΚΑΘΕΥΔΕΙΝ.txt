Etymology: The word "καθευδειν" (kathudein) comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and "εὖ" (eu), meaning "well," combined with the root "ὕπνος" (hupnos), meaning "sleep." 

Grammar: "καθευδειν" is an infinitive verb form in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation and is used to express the action of sleeping. 

Description: "καθευδειν" is a Greek verb that means "to sleep." It is commonly used to describe the state of being in a state of rest or unconsciousness during which the body and mind rejuvenate.
