Etymology: The word "κατακαυσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "κατακαίω" (katakaiō), which means "to burn down" or "to consume by fire." The suffix "-ουσιν" (-ousin) indicates the third person plural present tense form of the verb.

Grammar: "κατακαυσουσιν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the third person plural present tense form, indicating that the action is being performed by a group of people. The verb is in the active voice, suggesting that the subject is performing the action of burning down or consuming by fire.

Description: "κατακαυσουσιν" describes the act of burning down or consuming something by fire. It implies a destructive action, where the subject is causing something to be completely destroyed or reduced to ashes.
