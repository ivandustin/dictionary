Etymology: The word "καθαροίς" (katharois) comes from the Greek adjective "καθαρός" (katharos), meaning "clean" or "pure."

Grammar: "Καθαροίς" is the dative plural form of the adjective "καθαρός" (katharos). In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Καθαροίς" is an adjective in Greek that describes a plural noun in the dative case. It is used to indicate that something is clean or pure in a grammatical context.
