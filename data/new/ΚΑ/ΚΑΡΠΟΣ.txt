Etymology: The word "καρπός" (karpos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kerp- meaning "to gather, pluck, harvest." 

Grammar: In Greek, "καρπός" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form (καρπός) and a plural form (καρποί). It can function as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Καρπός" primarily means "fruit" in Greek. It refers to the edible part of a plant that develops from the flower and contains seeds. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the result or outcome of an action or effort.
