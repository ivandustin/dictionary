Etymology: The word "καταισχύνηι" comes from the Greek verb "καταισχύνω" (kataischyno), which means "to disgrace" or "to shame."

Grammar: "καταισχύνηι" is a noun in the dative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description: "καταισχύνηι" refers to the state or act of feeling ashamed, disgraced, or humiliated. It is used to describe the feeling of embarrassment or shame that one experiences due to a particular event or situation.
