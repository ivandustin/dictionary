Etymology: The word "κατανοείς" comes from the Greek verb "κατανοέω" (katanoeo), which is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "through," and the verb "νοέω" (noeo), meaning "to perceive" or "to understand." Therefore, "κατανοείς" can be translated as "you understand" or "you comprehend."

Grammar: "κατανοείς" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "κατανοέω" in Greek. It is used when addressing one person and indicates that the subject (you) understands or comprehends something.

Description: "κατανοείς" is a Greek word that expresses the action of understanding or comprehending. It is often used in conversations or written texts to acknowledge someone's understanding of a concept, idea, or situation.
