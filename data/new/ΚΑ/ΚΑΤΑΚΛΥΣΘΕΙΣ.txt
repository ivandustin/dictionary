Etymology: The word "κατακλυσθείς" comes from the Greek verb "κατακλύζω" (kataklýzo), which means "to flood" or "to inundate." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "κλύζω" (klýzo), meaning "to wash" or "to surge."

Grammar: "κατακλυσθείς" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle form. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action, and the participle form functions as an adjective.

Description: "κατακλυσθείς" describes something or someone that has been flooded or overwhelmed by water. It can also be used metaphorically to describe being overwhelmed or inundated by something non-physical, such as emotions or information.
