Etymology: The word "κατεκριναν" comes from the Greek verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno), which means "to condemn" or "to judge against." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "κρίνω" (kríno), meaning "to judge."

Grammar: "κατεκριναν" is the third person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action.

Description: "κατεκριναν" is a Greek verb that means "they condemned" or "they judged against." It refers to the act of expressing a negative judgment or disapproval towards someone or something.
