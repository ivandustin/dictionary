Etymology: The word "κατεκλιναν" comes from the Greek verb "κατακλίνω" (kataklíno), which means "to recline" or "to lie down." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and the verb "κλίνω" (klíno), meaning "to lean" or "to incline."

Grammar: "κατεκλιναν" is the third person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "κατακλίνω" (kataklíno). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action.

Description: "κατεκλιναν" describes the action of a group of people reclining or lying down in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing people reclining at a meal or resting in a relaxed position.
