Etymology: The word "καταρας" (kataras) comes from the Greek verb "καταράομαι" (kataráomai), which means "to curse" or "to damn." 

Grammar: "Καταρας" is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Καταρας" refers to a curse or a malediction. It is used to describe a solemn utterance or invocation that is intended to bring harm or misfortune upon someone or something.
