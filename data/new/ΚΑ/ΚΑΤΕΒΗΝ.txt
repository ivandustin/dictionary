Etymology: The word "κατεβήν" comes from the Greek verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down", and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go".

Grammar: "κατεβήν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "κατεβήν" is used to describe the action of going down or descending. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically descending from a higher place to a lower place, or metaphorically descending in status or rank.
