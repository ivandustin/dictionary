Etymology: The word "κατακλυσμόν" (kataklusmon) comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "κλύζω" (kluzo), meaning "to wash" or "to flood." 

Grammar: "κατακλυσμόν" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "κατακλυσμόν" refers to a flood or deluge, typically caused by heavy rainfall or the overflow of water bodies. It describes a situation where a large amount of water submerges land areas, causing damage and destruction.
