Etymology: The word "καθιστησιν" comes from the Greek verb "καθίστημι" (kathistēmi), which means "to place, to set up, to establish."

Grammar: "καθιστησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. It is derived from the verb "καθίστημι" and is formed by adding the suffix "-σιν" to the stem of the verb.

Description: "καθιστησιν" refers to the act of placing or establishing something. It can also be used to describe the appointment or installation of someone to a position or role.
