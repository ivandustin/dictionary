Etymology: The word "κατωτερα" comes from the Greek word "κατώτερος" (katóteros), which means "lower" or "inferior". It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "below", and the comparative suffix "-τερος" (-teros).

Grammar: "κατωτερα" is the neuter plural form of the adjective "κατώτερος" (katóteros). In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "κατωτερα" is used to describe plural neuter nouns.

Description: "κατωτερα" is an adjective in Greek that means "lower" or "inferior". It is used to compare two or more things, indicating that one is of a lower or inferior quality, position, or rank than the other.
