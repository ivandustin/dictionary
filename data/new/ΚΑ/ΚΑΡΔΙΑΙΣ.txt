Etymology: The word "καρδιαίς" comes from the Greek word "καρδία" (kardia), meaning "heart". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kerd- which also gave rise to words like "cardiac" in English.

Grammar: "καρδιαίς" is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple hearts.

Description: "καρδιαίς" refers to hearts in Greek. It can be used to describe the anatomical organ or symbolize emotions, feelings, or the center of one's being.
