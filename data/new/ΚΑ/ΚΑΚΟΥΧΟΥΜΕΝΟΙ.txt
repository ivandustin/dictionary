Etymology: The word "κακουχούμενοι" comes from the Greek verb "κακουχώ" (kakoucho), which means "to torment" or "to afflict." The suffix "-μενοι" (-menoi) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Grammar: "Κακουχούμενοι" is the plural form of the verb "κακουχώ" in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is used to describe a group of people who are being tormented or afflicted.

Description: "Κακουχούμενοι" is an adjective that describes individuals or groups who are experiencing suffering, torment, or hardship. It implies that they are enduring some form of physical, emotional, or mental distress.
