Etymology: The word "κακολογησαί" comes from the Greek verb "κακολογώ" (kakologo), which means "to speak ill of" or "to slander." It is derived from the combination of the Greek words "κακός" (kakos), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "λόγος" (logos), meaning "word" or "speech."

Grammar: "κακολογησαί" is the third person singular, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb "κακολογέω" (kakologeo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "κακολογησαί" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it spoke ill of" or "he/she/it slandered." It refers to the act of saying negative or harmful things about someone or something.
