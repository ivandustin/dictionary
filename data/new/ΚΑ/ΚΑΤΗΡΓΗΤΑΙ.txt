Etymology: The word "κατηργηται" comes from the Greek verb "καταργέω" (katargeo), which means "to abolish" or "to nullify".

Grammar: "κατηργηται" is the third person singular form of the verb "καταργέω" in the present tense, middle voice, indicative mood. It is used to describe an action being performed by the subject on itself.

Description: "κατηργηται" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it abolishes" or "he/she/it nullifies". It is used to express the act of completely eliminating or rendering something ineffective or useless.
