Etymology: The word "κατεχεεν" comes from the Greek verb "κατέχω" (katechō), which means "to hold back" or "to restrain."

Grammar: "κατεχεεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "κατέχω" in the aorist tense. It is used to describe an action that happened in the past and is completed.

Description: "κατεχεεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it held back" or "he/she/it restrained." It is used to indicate that someone or something prevented or stopped something from happening.
