Etymology: The word "κατενώπιον" comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "ἐνώπιον" (enōpion), meaning "in front of" or "before." 

Grammar: "κατενώπιον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is neuter in gender. 

Description: "κατενώπιον" refers to something that is situated or located in front of or facing someone or something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or circumstance that one is confronted with or facing.
