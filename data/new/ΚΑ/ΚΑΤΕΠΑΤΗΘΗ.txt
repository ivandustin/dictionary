Etymology: The word "κατεπατηθη" comes from the Greek verb "καταπατώ" (katapató), which means "to trample upon" or "to tread on." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "upon," and the verb "πατώ" (pató), meaning "to tread" or "to step."

Grammar: "κατεπατηθη" is the passive voice form of the verb "καταπατώ" (katapató) in the past tense. It is conjugated in the third person singular. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "κατεπατηθη" describes an action that happened in the past where someone or something was trampled upon or treaded on. It implies a forceful or aggressive action of stepping on or crushing something or someone.
