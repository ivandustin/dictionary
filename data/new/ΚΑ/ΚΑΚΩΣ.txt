Etymology: The word "κακως" (kakos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kakka, meaning "bad" or "evil."

Grammar: "Κακως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe actions or states in a negative or unfavorable manner. It can be translated as "badly" or "poorly" in English.

Description: "Κακως" is used to express the manner in which something is done or the quality of an action. It indicates that something is done in a negative or unsatisfactory way. It can be used to describe various aspects such as behavior, performance, or condition.
