Etymology: The word "κατασυρην" comes from the Greek word "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the word "σύρω" (sýro), meaning "drag" or "pull." 

Grammar: "κατασυρην" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the third declension. 

Description: "κατασυρην" refers to the act of dragging or pulling something down or against. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone or something is being forcefully brought down or hindered.
