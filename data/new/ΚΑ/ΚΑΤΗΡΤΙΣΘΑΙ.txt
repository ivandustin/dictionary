Grammar: "κατηρτισθαί" is the third person singular, aorist passive form of the verb "καταρτίζω" (katartízo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "κατηρτισθαί" is a verb form that describes the action of being prepared, equipped, or made ready. It implies that someone or something has been made suitable or fit for a particular purpose or situation.
