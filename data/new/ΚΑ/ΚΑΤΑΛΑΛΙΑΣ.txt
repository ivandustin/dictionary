Etymology: The word "καταλαλιάς" comes from the Greek word "καταλαλέω" (katalaleo), which means "to speak against" or "to slander". 

Grammar: "Καταλαλιάς" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender. 

Description: "Καταλαλιάς" refers to the act of speaking ill of someone, spreading rumors, or engaging in slanderous speech. It is often used to describe malicious gossip or defamation.
