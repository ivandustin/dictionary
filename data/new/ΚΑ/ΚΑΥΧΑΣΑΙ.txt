Etymology: The word "καυχάσαι" comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". 

Grammar: "καυχάσαι" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "καυχάομαι" (kaucháomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on tense, mood, and voice. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb and does not indicate tense or person.

Description: "καυχάσαι" is used to express the action of boasting or bragging in the past. It is often used in narratives or descriptions to indicate that someone boasted about something at a specific point in the past.
