Etymology: The word "κατηργηκα" comes from the Greek verb "καταργέω" (katargéo), which means "to abolish" or "to nullify". 

Grammar: "κατηργηκα" is the first person singular form of the verb "καταργέω" in the perfect tense. It indicates that the action of abolishing or nullifying has been completed in the past.

Description: "κατηργηκα" is a Greek word that expresses the idea of having abolished or nullified something in the past. It is commonly used in various contexts to indicate the completion of an action or the termination of something.
