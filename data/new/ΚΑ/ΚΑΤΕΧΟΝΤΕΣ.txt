Etymology: The word "κατεχοντες" comes from the Greek verb "κατέχω" (katecho), which means "to hold" or "to possess." The suffix "-οντες" is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to those who are currently holding or possessing something.

Grammar: "Κατεχοντες" is the plural form of the word, referring to multiple individuals who are holding or possessing something. It is a present participle in the active voice.

Description: "Κατεχοντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "those who hold" or "those who possess." It is often used to describe individuals who have control or ownership over something. The exact meaning of the word can vary depending on the context in which it is used.
