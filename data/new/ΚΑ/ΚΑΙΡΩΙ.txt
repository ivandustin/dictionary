Etymology: The word "καιροί" (kairoi) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "καιρός" (kairos), which means "time" or "opportunity" in Greek.

Grammar: "καιροί" is the plural form of "καιρός" in Greek. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: "καιροί" refers to "times" or "seasons" in Greek. It can be used to indicate specific periods or moments in time, as well as the right or opportune time for something to happen. The word is often used in a metaphorical sense to express the idea of seizing opportunities or making the most of a particular moment.
