Etymology: The word "καρποφόρος" comes from the Greek roots "καρπός" (karpos), meaning "fruit," and "φέρω" (phero), meaning "to bear" or "to carry." 

Grammar: In Greek, "καρποφόρος" is an adjective. It is in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: "Καρποφόρος" is used to describe something or someone that bears or produces fruit. It can be used in a literal sense to refer to plants or trees that bear fruit, or in a figurative sense to describe a person or thing that is productive or fruitful.
