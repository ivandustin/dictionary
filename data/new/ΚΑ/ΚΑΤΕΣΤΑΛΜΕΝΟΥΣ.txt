Etymology: The word "κατεσταλμένους" comes from the Greek verb "κατασταλμένος" (katastalmenos), which is the past participle form of the verb "κατασταλώ" (katastalo). 

Grammar: "κατεσταλμένους" is the masculine accusative plural form of the adjective "κατεσταλμένος" in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: The adjective "κατεσταλμένος" in Greek means "settled" or "calmed." It is used to describe something or someone that has become calm or tranquil after a period of disturbance or agitation.
