Etymology: The word "κατενέχθεις" comes from the Greek verb "κατέχω" (katecho), which means "to hold down" or "to possess." The word is formed by adding the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," to the verb "έχω" (echo), meaning "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar: "κατενέχθεις" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and second person singular form. It is used to address someone directly and indicates an action that was completed in the past.

Description: The word "κατενέχθεις" can be translated as "you were possessed" or "you were held down." It implies that someone or something had control or power over the person being addressed.
