Etymology: The word "κατελείπον" comes from the Greek verb "καταλείπω" (kataleípo), which means "to leave behind" or "to abandon." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "λείπω" (leípo), meaning "to leave" or "to forsake."

Grammar: "Κατελείπον" is the aorist active participle of the verb "καταλείπω" (kataleípo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Κατελείπον" can be translated as "having left behind" or "having abandoned." It is used to describe a past action of leaving or forsaking something or someone.
