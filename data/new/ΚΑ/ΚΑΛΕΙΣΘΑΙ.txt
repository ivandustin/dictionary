Etymology: The word "καλεισθαι" comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to name."

Grammar: "καλεισθαι" is the present passive infinitive form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo). In Greek grammar, the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "καλεισθαι" is used to express the action of being called or named in the present tense and in a passive voice. It is often used in sentences where the subject is the recipient of the action rather than the doer.
