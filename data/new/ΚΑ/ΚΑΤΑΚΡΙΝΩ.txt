Etymology: The word "κατακρινω" (katakrino) comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "κρίνω" (krino), meaning "to judge." Therefore, "κατακρινω" can be translated as "to judge against" or "to condemn."

Grammar: "κατακρινω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to express different actions and states of judgment or condemnation.

Description: "κατακρινω" is a strong word that denotes the act of judging someone or something negatively, often with a sense of condemnation or disapproval. It can be used in various contexts, such as legal proceedings, moral judgments, or personal opinions.
