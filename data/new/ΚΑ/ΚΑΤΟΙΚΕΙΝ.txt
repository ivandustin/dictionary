Etymology: The word "κατοικείν" comes from the Greek verb "οἰκέω" (oikeo), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit." The prefix "κατ-" (kat-) adds the sense of "down" or "through," giving the word the meaning of "to settle" or "to reside."

Grammar: "Κατοικείν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb in a general or abstract sense, without specifying the subject or the time of the action.

Description: "Κατοικείν" is a Greek verb that means "to dwell," "to reside," or "to inhabit." It is used to describe the act of living or staying in a particular place.
