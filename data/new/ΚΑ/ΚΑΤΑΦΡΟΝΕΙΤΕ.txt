Etymology: The word "καταφρονείτε" comes from the Greek verb "καταφρονέω" (kataphroneo), which means "to despise" or "to look down upon". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "φρονέω" (phroneo), meaning "to think" or "to have an opinion".

Grammar: "καταφρονείτε" is the second person plural form of the present tense in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and means "you despise" or "you look down upon". The verb is conjugated in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood.

Description: "καταφρονείτε" is a strong word that expresses a negative attitude towards someone or something. It implies a sense of disdain, contempt, or disregard. It is often used to describe a behavior or attitude of superiority or arrogance towards others.
