Etymology: The word "κατηγοροι" comes from the Greek word "κατηγορέω" (katēgoreō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuō), meaning "to speak in public".

Grammar: "κατηγοροι" is the plural form of the noun "κατηγόρος" (katēgoros) in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun. In Greek grammar, nouns are declined according to gender, number, and case.

Description: In ancient Greece, a "κατηγόρος" referred to a person who accused or charged someone in a legal context, particularly in a court of law. They would present evidence and arguments against the defendant. The role of the κατηγόρος was crucial in the Athenian legal system, where trials were conducted in public and involved both the accuser and the accused presenting their cases before a jury.
