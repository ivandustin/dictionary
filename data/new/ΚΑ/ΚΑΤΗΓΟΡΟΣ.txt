Etymology: The word "κατηγορος" comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuo), meaning "to speak in public." Therefore, "κατηγορος" can be translated as "one who speaks against" or "accuser."

Grammar: In Greek, "κατηγορος" is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "κατηγορος." The genitive singular form is "κατηγορου," the accusative singular form is "κατηγορον," and so on.

Description: "Κατηγορος" is a term used in various contexts, but it is most commonly associated with the legal field. In ancient Greece, a "κατηγορος" referred to a person who accused someone of a crime in a court of law. The role of the κατηγορος was to present evidence and arguments against the defendant in order to prove their guilt. In modern usage, the term can also refer to someone who makes accusations or brings charges against another person outside of a legal setting.
