Etymology: The word "κατακλιθης" comes from the Greek word "κατακλίνω" (kataklíno), which means "to recline" or "to lie down." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb "κλίνω" (klíno), meaning "to lean" or "to incline."

Grammar: "κατακλιθης" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a person or thing that is reclining or lying down.
