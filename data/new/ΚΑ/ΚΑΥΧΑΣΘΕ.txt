Etymology: The word "καυχάσθε" comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *keuk-, meaning "to swell" or "to puff up".

Grammar: "καυχάσθε" is the second person plural present middle/passive imperative form of the verb "καυχάομαι" (kaucháomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the subject, tense, mood, and voice. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on themselves (middle voice) or receiving the action (passive voice).

Description: "καυχάσθε" is a command or instruction addressed to a group of people, telling them to boast or brag. It is used to encourage or prompt others to express pride or confidence in themselves or their achievements.
