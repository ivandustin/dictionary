Etymology: The word "κατεκαη" comes from the Greek verb "κατακαίω" (katakaiō), which means "to burn down" or "to consume by fire." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "through," and the verb "καίω" (kaiō), meaning "to burn."

Grammar: "κατεκαη" is the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood form of the verb "κατακαίω" (katakaiō). The aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood is used to state a fact or ask a question.

Description: "κατεκαη" describes an action that occurred in the past, where something was burned down or consumed by fire. It can be used to refer to the destruction of a building, forest, or any other object or area by fire.
