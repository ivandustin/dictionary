Etymology: The word "καθωσπερ" (kathosper) comes from the Greek words "καθώς" (kathos), meaning "as" or "just as," and "περ" (per), meaning "through" or "by." 

Grammar: "Καθώσπερ" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to introduce a comparison or similarity between two things or situations. It is often translated as "just as," "as," or "like."

Description: "Καθώσπερ" is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to draw comparisons or highlight similarities between different elements. It is often used to emphasize a point or make a comparison more vivid.
