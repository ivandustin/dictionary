Etymology: The word "καταδηλών" comes from the Greek roots "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and "δηλόω" (deloo), meaning "to show" or "to indicate." 

Grammar: "καταδηλών" is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb "καταδηλόω" (katadeloo), which means "to indicate" or "to demonstrate." 

Description: "καταδηλών" is used to describe someone or something that is indicating or showing something clearly or explicitly. It can be translated as "indicating," "showing," or "demonstrating."
