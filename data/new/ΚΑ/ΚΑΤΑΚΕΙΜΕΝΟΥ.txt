Etymology: The word "κατακειμένου" comes from the Greek verb "κατακείμαι" (katakeimai), which means "to lie down" or "to recline". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down", and the verb "κείμαι" (keimai), meaning "to lie".

Grammar: "κατακειμένου" is the genitive form of the noun "κατακείμενος" (katakeimenos), which is a participle of the verb "κατακείμαι". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "κατακειμένου" is a Greek word that can be translated as "of the one lying down" or "of the one reclining". It is often used in contexts related to resting, reclining, or being in a lying position.
