Etymology: The word "καυχησεως" (kaukhēseōs) comes from the Greek verb "καυχάομαι" (kauchaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag."

Grammar: "καυχησεως" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "καυχησεως" refers to the act of boasting or bragging. It can also be translated as "pride" or "boastfulness."
