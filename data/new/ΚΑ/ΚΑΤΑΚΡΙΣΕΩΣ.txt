Etymology: The word "κατακρισεως" comes from the Greek verb "κατακρίνω" (katakríno), which means "to condemn" or "to judge against". It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "κρίνω" (kríno), meaning "to judge".

Grammar: "κατακρισεως" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.
