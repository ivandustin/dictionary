Etymology: The word "κατηγορία" comes from the Greek word "κατηγορέω" (katēgoreō), which means "to accuse" or "to speak against". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuō), meaning "to speak in public".

Grammar: In Greek, "κατηγορία" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is typically used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form "κατηγορίες" (katēgories). It can function as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Κατηγορία" is a Greek word that translates to "category" in English. It refers to a classification or grouping of things based on shared characteristics or attributes. In various fields such as philosophy, linguistics, and logic, "κατηγορία" is used to organize and classify concepts, objects, or ideas into distinct groups or classes.
