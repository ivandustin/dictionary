Etymology: The word "καθημενου" comes from the Greek verb "καθέζομαι" (kathézomai), which means "to sit down" or "to be seated". It is derived from the preposition "κατά" (kata), meaning "down", and the verb "ἕζομαι" (hézomai), meaning "to sit".

Grammar: "καθημενου" is the genitive form of the word "καθημενος" (kathémenos), which is the present participle of the verb "καθέζομαι" (kathézomai). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "καθημενου" can be translated as "of the one sitting" or "of the seated one". It is often used in Greek literature and religious texts to refer to someone who is sitting or seated in a specific context.
