Word: θυρίδα (θυριδος in genitive case)

Etymology: The word θυρίδα comes from the ancient Greek word θύρα (thura), which means "door". The suffix -ίδα (-ida) is a diminutive suffix, which means "small door".

Grammar: θυρίδα is a feminine noun in Greek. In the genitive case, it becomes θυριδος. It is declined like a first-declension noun, with the stem θυριδ-.

Description: θυρίδα refers to a small door or a hatch. It can be used to describe a small opening in a larger structure, such as a ship or a building. In modern Greek, it is also used to refer to a small window or a peephole.
