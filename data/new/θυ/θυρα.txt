Word: θυρα

Etymology:
The word θυρα (thura) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰwer- meaning "door". The word has been used in Greek language since ancient times and has been passed down to modern Greek.

In Greek, θυρα is a feminine noun. It is declined in the first declension and its nominative singular form is θυρα. The genitive singular form is θυρας, the accusative singular form is θυραν, and the vocative singular form is θυρα.

Description:
Θυρα is the Greek word for "door". It refers to a movable barrier used to block off an entrance or exit to a building or room. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to an opportunity or a gateway to something.
