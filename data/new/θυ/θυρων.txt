The word "θυρων" (thuron) is a Greek noun that means "door" or "gate". It is derived from the Greek word "θύρα" (thyra), which also means "door". In terms of grammar, "θυρων" is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, and accusative. 

In description, "θυρων" can refer to any type of door or gate, whether it is a physical entrance to a building or a metaphorical gateway to something else. It is a common word in Greek literature and is often used in idiomatic expressions, such as "κλείνω τις θύρες" (kleino tis thyres), which means "to close the doors" or "to shut oneself off from the world".
