Word: θυμομαχων

Etymology: The word θυμομαχων is derived from the Greek words θυμός (thymos) meaning "passion" or "anger" and μάχη (mache) meaning "battle" or "fight". 

Grammar: θυμομαχων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is a compound word made up of two nouns, and it is in the singular form. 

Description: θυμομαχων refers to someone who is engaged in a battle or fight fueled by passion or anger. It can also be used to describe a person who is prone to getting into fights or arguments due to their hot-headed nature.
