Word: θυμος

Etymology:
The word θυμος (thumos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb θύω (thuō), which means "to sacrifice". In ancient times, the word was used to refer to the smoke that rose from a sacrifice, and later came to mean "spirit" or "soul". In Greek mythology, θυμος was also the name of the god of anger and passion.

Θυμος is a complex word that can have different meanings depending on the context. It can refer to a strong feeling of anger or passion, as well as to the courage and spirit of a person. In ancient Greek literature, it was often used to describe the emotions of heroes and warriors.
