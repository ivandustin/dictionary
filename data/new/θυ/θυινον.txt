Word: θυινον

Etymology:
The word θυινον (thuinos) is derived from the Greek word θυω (thuo), which means "to sacrifice." It is related to the word θυσια (thusia), which means "sacrifice" or "offering."

Θυινον is a term used in ancient Greek religion to refer to a type of incense made from the resin of the terebinth tree. The use of θυινον was also associated with the worship of Dionysus, the god of wine and fertility. Today, the word is rarely used outside of academic or historical contexts.
