Word: ψυχικός

Etymology: The word ψυχικός (psychikos) comes from the Greek word ψυχή (psyche), which means "soul" or "mind". The suffix -ικός (-ikos) is used to form adjectives, meaning "related to" or "pertaining to".

Grammar: ψυχικός is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender and number of the noun it modifies.

Description: ψυχικός is used to describe something that is related to the mind or the soul. It can refer to mental or emotional states, such as psychological disorders or emotional trauma. It can also refer to spiritual or metaphysical concepts, such as psychic abilities or the soul's journey after death.
