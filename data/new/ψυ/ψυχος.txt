Word: ψυχος

Etymology:
The word ψυχος (psukhós) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word ψυχή (psukhḗ), which means "soul" or "spirit".

Grammar:
In Greek, ψυχος is a noun of the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms. The singular form is ψυχός (psukhós) and the plural form is ψυχοί (psukhoí).

Description:
ψυχος is a term used in psychology to refer to the mind or the psyche. It is also used in philosophy to refer to the soul or the spirit. In ancient Greek mythology, ψυχος was believed to be the essence of a person's being, and it was thought to be immortal. Today, the word is commonly used in scientific and medical contexts to describe mental and emotional states.
