Word: ψυχρος

Etymology:
The word ψυχρος (psychros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word ψυχή (psyche), which means "soul" or "spirit". The word ψυχρος (psychros) means "cold" or "chilly".

Grammar:
In Greek, ψυχρος (psychros) is an adjective. It can be used to describe a person, place, or thing. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative).

Description:
ψυχρος (psychros) is a word that describes something that is cold or chilly. It can be used to describe the temperature of a place or the feeling of a person. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person's personality or behavior as cold or distant.
