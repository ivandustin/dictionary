The word "ψυχει" (psychei) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "ψυχή" (psyche), which means "soul" or "spirit". 

In terms of grammar, "ψυχει" is in the present tense and active voice. It is the third person singular form of the verb, meaning "he/she/it breathes" or "he/she/it is alive". 

The word "ψυχει" is often used in philosophical and religious contexts to refer to the concept of the soul or the breath of life. It can also be used in a more literal sense to describe the act of breathing or being alive.
