Etymology: The word "αποδεκατεύω" (apodekatevó) comes from the combination of the prefix "από" (apó), meaning "from" or "away," and the verb "δεκατεύω" (dekatevó), meaning "to tithe" or "to give a tenth." 

Grammar: "Αποδεκατεύω" is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows: 

- Εγώ αποδεκατεύω (egó apodekatevó) - I tithe
- Εσύ αποδεκατεύεις (esý apodekatevéis) - You tithe
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό αποδεκατεύει (aftós/aftí/aftó apodekatevéi) - He/She/It tithes
- Εμείς αποδεκατεύουμε (emeís apodekatevéoume) - We tithe
- Εσείς αποδεκατεύετε (eseís apodekatevéte) - You tithe
- Αυτοί/Αυτές αποδεκατεύουν (aftoí/aftés apodekatevéoun) - They tithe

Description: "Αποδεκατεύω" is a Greek verb that means "to tithe" or "to give a tenth." It refers to the act of giving a portion of one's income or possessions, typically one-tenth, to a religious or charitable cause. Tithing is a practice that exists in various religions and cultures around the world.
