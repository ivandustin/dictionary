Etymology: The word "απελθηι" (apeltēi) comes from the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" (aperchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart."

Grammar: "απελθηι" is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and subjunctive mood. It is the third person singular form.

Description: "απελθηι" is used to express a command or wish for someone to go away or depart. It is often used in a polite or formal context.
