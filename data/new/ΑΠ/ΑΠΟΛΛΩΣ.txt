Etymology: The word "απολλων" (apollōn) comes from the Greek god Apollo, who was associated with music, poetry, and prophecy. 

Grammar: "Απολλων" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Απολλων" refers to Apollo, the Greek god of music, poetry, and prophecy. He was one of the twelve Olympian gods and was often depicted as a young man with a lyre. Apollo was also associated with healing and was believed to bring about plagues and diseases. He was highly revered in ancient Greek culture and had many temples and sanctuaries dedicated to him.
