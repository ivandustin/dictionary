Etymology: The word "αποστολήν" (apostolēn) comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostellō), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch." It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from," and the verb "στέλλω" (stellō), meaning "to send."

Grammar: "Αποστολήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Αποστολήν" refers to the act of sending or dispatching something or someone. It can also be used to describe a mission, task, or assignment. The word is commonly used in various contexts, such as in business, military operations, and religious contexts.
