Etymology: The word "αποστελεί" (apostelei) comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away", and the verb "στέλλω" (stello), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Αποστέλλει" (apostellei) is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, and tense. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Αποστέλλει" (apostellei) means "he/she/it sends" in Greek. It is used to describe the action of sending something or someone away or dispatching them to a specific destination.
