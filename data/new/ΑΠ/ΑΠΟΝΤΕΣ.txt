Etymology: The word "αποντες" (apontes) comes from the Greek verb "απονταω" (apontao), which means "to be absent" or "to be away". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "ειμι" (eimi), meaning "to be".

Grammar: "Αποντες" is the plural form of the noun "απων" (apon), which means "absent" or "missing". It is used to describe multiple people or things that are not present or are away.
