Etymology: The word "απειθειαν" comes from the Greek word "απειθεια" (apeitheia), which means "disobedience" or "unwillingness to obey." It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "πειθω" (peitho), meaning "to persuade" or "to convince."

Grammar: "Απειθειαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Απειθειαν" refers to the act or state of being disobedient or unwilling to obey. It can be used to describe a person's refusal to follow rules, orders, or authority. It is often associated with rebellion, defiance, or noncompliance.
