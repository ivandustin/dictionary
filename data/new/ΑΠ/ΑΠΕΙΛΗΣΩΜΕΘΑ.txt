Etymology: The word "απειλησωμεθα" comes from the Greek verb "απειλέω" (apeiléo), which means "to threaten" or "to menace". The suffix "-σωμεθα" (-sometha) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the action is performed by the subject upon itself.

Grammar: "απειλησωμεθα" is a first-person plural form of the verb "απειλέω" in the future middle voice. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, voice, and mood. The future middle voice indicates that the subject will perform the action upon itself in the future.

Description: "απειλησωμεθα" can be translated as "we will threaten ourselves" or "we will menace ourselves". It suggests that the subject will be the one causing harm or danger to themselves in the future.
