Etymology: The word "απειρος" (apeiros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "πειρα" (peira), meaning "limit" or "boundary." Therefore, "απειρος" (apeiros) can be translated as "without limit" or "infinite."

Grammar: In Greek, "απειρος" (apeiros) is an adjective. It can be used to describe something that is boundless, limitless, or infinite. It can also be used to describe something that is endless or immeasurable.
