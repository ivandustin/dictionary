Etymology: The word "απειμι" (apeimi) comes from the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" (aperchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Απειμι" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It is used to express the action of going away or departing.

Description: "Απειμι" is a Greek verb that describes the act of physically moving away from a location or departing from a place. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate a departure from a situation, idea, or state of being.
