Etymology: The word "απαραβατον" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not" or "un-") and the word "παραβατον" (meaning "transgressible" or "violable").

Grammar: In Greek, "απαραβατον" is an adjective. It is in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that cannot be transgressed or violated.

Description: "απαραβατον" refers to something that is inviolable, unable to be transgressed, or cannot be violated. It is often used to describe rules, laws, or principles that are absolute and cannot be broken.
