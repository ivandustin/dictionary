Etymology: The word "αποστρέψον" comes from the Greek verb "αποστρέφω" (apostrefo), which means "to turn away" or "to divert." It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away," and the verb "στρέφω" (strefo), meaning "to turn."

Grammar: "Αποστρέψον" is the imperative form of the verb "αποστρέφω" in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to turn away or divert.

Description: "Αποστρέψον" is a Greek word that is used to command someone to turn away or divert. It is often used in a figurative sense, suggesting the need to change direction or avoid something.
