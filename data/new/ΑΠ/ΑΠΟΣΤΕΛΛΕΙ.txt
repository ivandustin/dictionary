Etymology: The word "αποστελλει" comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "στέλλω" (stello), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "αποστελλει" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "αποστελλει" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it sends" or "he/she/it is sending". It is used to describe the action of sending something or someone away or dispatching them to a specific destination.
