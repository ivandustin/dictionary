Etymology: The word "απόδημος" comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and "δήμος" (demos), meaning "people" or "community." Therefore, "απόδημος" can be translated as "away from the community" or "foreign."

Grammar: In Greek, "απόδημος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Απόδημος" is used to describe someone or something that is away from their homeland or community. It refers to a person who is living or residing in a foreign country or a place that is not their place of origin. It can also be used to describe something that is foreign or unfamiliar.
