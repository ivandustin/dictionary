Etymology: The word "απελευθερος" (apelytheros) comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and "ελευθερία" (eleftheria), meaning "freedom." It literally translates to "one who is free from" or "liberated."

Grammar: In Greek, "απελευθερος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Απελευθερος" is used to describe someone or something that is free, liberated, or independent. It can refer to a person who is free from physical or mental constraints, or to a country that has gained independence.
