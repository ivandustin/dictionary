Etymology: The word "απολελυσθαι" (apolelysthai) comes from the Greek verb "απολυω" (apolyo), which means "to release" or "to set free". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "λυω" (lyo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie".

Grammar: "απολελυσθαι" is a verb in the passive voice and the perfect tense. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The perfect tense indicates that the action of the verb has been completed in the past and has ongoing relevance to the present.

Description: "απολελυσθαι" is a Greek word that means "to have been released" or "to have been set free". It implies that someone or something has been freed from a previous state of captivity, constraint, or obligation.
