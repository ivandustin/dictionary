Etymology: The word "απατη" (apati) comes from the Greek verb "απατώ" (apato), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". 

Grammar: "Απατη" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun and belongs to the second declension. 

Description: "Απατη" refers to deception, fraud, or trickery. It is used to describe an act of misleading or deceiving someone with the intention of gaining an unfair advantage or causing harm.
