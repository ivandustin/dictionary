Etymology: The word "αποσπαν" (apospan) comes from the Greek verb "αποσπάω" (apospao), which means "to detach" or "to separate". 

Grammar: "Αποσπαν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: "Αποσπαν" means "to detach" or "to separate" in English. It is used to describe the action of removing or taking something away from a larger whole.
