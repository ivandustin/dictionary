Etymology: The word "αποβολή" (apovoli) comes from the Greek verb "αποβάλλω" (apovallo), which means "to cast away" or "to reject". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "βάλλω" (vallo), meaning "to throw".

Grammar: "Αποβολή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "αποβολής" (apovolis). In the plural form, it becomes "αποβολές" (apoveles).

Description: "Αποβολή" refers to the act of casting away or rejecting something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as the expulsion of a person from a group or organization, the rejection of an idea or proposal, or the elimination of something unwanted or unnecessary.
