Etymology: The word "απαταίς" (apatais) comes from the Greek verb "απατάω" (apatao), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". 

Grammar: "Απαταίς" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Απαταίς" refers to a group of people who deceive or cheat others. It can be translated as "deceivers" or "cheaters" in English.
