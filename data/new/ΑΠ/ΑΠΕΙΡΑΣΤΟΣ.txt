Etymology: The word "απειραστος" (apeirastos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "πειραστος" (peirastos), meaning "tempted" or "tested." 

Grammar: "Απειραστος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It can also be declined to match the gender and number of the noun it modifies. For example, in the feminine form, it becomes "απειραστη" (apeirasti), and in the neuter form, it becomes "απειραστο" (apeirasto).

Description: "Απειραστος" is a Greek word that means "untouched" or "untempted." It is used to describe something or someone that has not been influenced, affected, or tempted by external factors. It can also refer to something that is pure, pristine, or unspoiled.
