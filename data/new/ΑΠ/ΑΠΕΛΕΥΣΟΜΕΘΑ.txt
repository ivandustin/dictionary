Etymology: The word "απελευθέρωση" (apelytherosi) comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away," and "ελευθερία" (eleftheria), meaning "freedom." 

Grammar: "Απελευθέρωση" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Απελευθέρωση" refers to the act of liberating or setting free. It can be used to describe the process of gaining freedom or releasing someone or something from captivity, oppression, or constraints.
