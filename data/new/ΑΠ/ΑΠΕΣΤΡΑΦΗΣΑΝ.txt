Etymology: The word "απεστραφησαν" comes from the Greek verb "αποστρέφω" (apostrefo), which means "to turn away" or "to divert". The suffix "-σαν" indicates the past tense, so "απεστραφησαν" translates to "they turned away" or "they diverted".

Grammar: "απεστραφησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "αποστρέφω" in the past tense. It is used to describe an action performed by a group of people or things in the past. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, person, and number of the subject.

Description: "απεστραφησαν" is a Greek word that describes an action of turning away or diverting. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people who changed their direction or shifted their attention from something.
