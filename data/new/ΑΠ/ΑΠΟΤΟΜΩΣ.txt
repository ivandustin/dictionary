Etymology: The word "απότομος" (apotomos) comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and "τομή" (tomi), meaning "cut." It literally translates to "cut away" or "cut off."

Grammar: In Greek, "απότομος" is an adjective. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter, which agree with the gender of the noun it modifies. It can also be declined in different cases, such as nominative, genitive, accusative, etc., to match the grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Απότομος" is used to describe something that is abrupt, sudden, or sharp. It can refer to physical objects or abstract concepts. For example, it can describe a steep cliff, a quick change in temperature, or an unexpected change in behavior or mood.
