Etymology: The word "απαγγελώ" (apangélō) comes from the Greek verb "απαγγέλλω" (apangéllō), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "αγγέλλω" (angéllō), meaning "to tell" or "to report".

Grammar: "Απαγγελώ" is a first-person singular present tense verb in Greek. It belongs to the class of verbs that end in "-ώ" (-ō) in the present tense. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Απαγγέλω (apangélō) - I announce
- Απαγγέλεις (apangéleis) - You announce
- Απαγγέλει (apangélei) - He/she/it announces
- Απαγγελούμε (apangeloúme) - We announce
- Απαγγέλετε (apangélete) - You (plural) announce
- Απαγγέλουν (apangéloun) - They announce
