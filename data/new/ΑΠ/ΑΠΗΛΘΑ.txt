Etymology: The word "απηλθα" (apiltha) comes from the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" (aperchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart". It is formed by combining the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "απηλθα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this case, "απηλθα" indicates that the action of "going away" or "departing" has been completed by the speaker.

Description: "απηλθα" is a word used to describe the act of someone going away or departing from a place. It can be used in various contexts, such as when someone leaves a location, ends a conversation, or departs from a situation.
