Etymology: The word "αποκεκυλισται" comes from the Greek verb "αποκεκυλίζομαι" (apokekylízomai), which means "to unroll" or "to unwind". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "off", and the verb "κυλίζω" (kylízo), meaning "to roll" or "to turn".

Grammar: "αποκεκυλισται" is the third person singular form of the verb "αποκεκυλίζομαι" in the present tense. It is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is being acted upon. The verb belongs to the first conjugation class in Greek.

Description: "αποκεκυλισται" describes the action of something being unrolled or unwound. It can be used to refer to the physical unrolling of an object or the metaphorical unraveling of a situation or problem.
