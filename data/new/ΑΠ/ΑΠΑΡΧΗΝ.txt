Etymology: The word "απαρχήν" (aparchin) comes from the Greek verb "απαρχίζω" (aparchizo), which means "to begin" or "to initiate". It is derived from the noun "απαρχή" (aparche), meaning "first fruits" or "first offering".

Grammar: "Απαρχήν" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate that something is done or happening at the beginning or as a first step.

Description: "Απαρχήν" is often used to express the idea of doing something initially or as a first attempt. It can also imply that something is done with enthusiasm or dedication. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as in conversations, writing, or formal speeches.
