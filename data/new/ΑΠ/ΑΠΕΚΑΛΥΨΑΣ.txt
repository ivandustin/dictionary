Etymology: The word "απεκαλυψας" (apekalypsas) comes from the Greek verb "αποκαλύπτω" (apokalypto), which means "to reveal" or "to uncover". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "off", and the verb "καλύπτω" (kalypto), meaning "to cover" or "to hide".

Grammar: "Απεκαλυψας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "αποκαλύπτω" (apokalypto) in Greek. It is used to describe an action that has been completed in the past. The word can be translated as "having revealed" or "having uncovered".

Description: "Απεκαλυψας" is a Greek word that refers to the act of revealing or uncovering something. It can be used in various contexts, such as revealing a secret, disclosing information, or uncovering the truth. The word carries a sense of discovery and bringing something hidden into light.
