Etymology: The word "απεσταλη" (apostalē) comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostellō), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "στέλλω" (stellō), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "απεσταλη" is the past tense form of the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostellō) in the passive voice. It is used to indicate that someone or something was sent or dispatched by someone else.

Description: "απεσταλη" is a Greek word that describes the action of being sent or dispatched. It can be used in various contexts, such as when referring to a message, a package, or a person being sent to a specific location.
