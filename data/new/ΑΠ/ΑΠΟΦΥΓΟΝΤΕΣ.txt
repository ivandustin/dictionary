Etymology: The word "αποφυγοντες" comes from the Greek verb "αποφεύγω" (apofevgo), which means "to avoid" or "to escape". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "φεύγω" (fevgo), meaning "to flee" or "to run away".

Grammar: "αποφυγοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "αποφεύγω" (apofevgo) in Greek. The present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. In this case, it describes the action of avoiding or escaping.

Description: "αποφυγοντες" is a Greek word that can be translated to English as "avoiding" or "escaping". It refers to the act of intentionally staying away from something or someone, or the act of successfully getting away from a dangerous or unwanted situation.
