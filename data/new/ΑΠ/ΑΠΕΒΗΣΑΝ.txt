Etymology: The word "απεβησαν" (apevisan) comes from the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" (aperchomai), which means "to depart" or "to go away". It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: "Απεβησαν" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "απέρχομαι" (aperchomai). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action of departing or going away happened in the past, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: "Απεβησαν" is a Greek verb that describes the action of a group of people or things departing or going away from a certain place. It can be used to indicate physical movement or figurative departure, such as leaving a location, ending a relationship, or discontinuing an activity.
