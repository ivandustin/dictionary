Etymology: The word "αποστολος" (apostolos) comes from the Greek verb "αποστελλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". In ancient Greece, an "αποστολος" referred to a messenger or envoy who was sent on a mission.

Grammar: "Αποστολος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "αποστολου" (apostolou). In the plural form, it becomes "αποστολοι" (apostoloi).

Description: In modern Greek, "αποστολος" is commonly used to refer to an apostle, particularly in the context of Christianity. In the New Testament, the term "αποστολος" is used to describe the twelve disciples chosen by Jesus to spread his teachings. These apostles played a crucial role in the establishment and early development of the Christian church.
