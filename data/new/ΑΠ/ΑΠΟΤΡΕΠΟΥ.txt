Etymology: The word "αποτρέπου" (apotrepou) comes from the Greek verb "αποτρέπω" (apotrepō), which means "to turn away" or "to avert". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "τρέπω" (trepō), meaning "to turn".

Grammar: "αποτρέπου" is the present tense, second person singular form of the verb "αποτρέπω" (apotrepō). It is used when addressing someone directly and telling them to turn away or avert something.

Description: "αποτρέπου" is a command form of the verb "αποτρέπω" (apotrepō), which is used to instruct someone to turn away or avert something. It is commonly used in Greek to give directions or warnings.
