Etymology: The word "απεχωρίσθη" comes from the Greek verb "αποχωρίζομαι" (apochorizomai), which means "to separate" or "to depart from". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "χωρίζω" (chorizo), meaning "to separate" or "to divide".

Grammar: "απεχωρίσθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive form of the verb "αποχωρίζομαι" (apochorizomai). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "απεχωρίσθη" is a Greek verb form that describes an action of separation or departure that was completed in the past. It is used when referring to a third person singular subject.
