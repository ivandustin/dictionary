Etymology: The word "απταιστους" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "απτομαι" (aptomai), which means "to touch" or "to grasp."

Grammar: "Απταιστους" is an adjective in the masculine form. It is in the nominative case and singular number. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "απταιστους" describes something or someone that cannot be touched or grasped. It implies that the object or person is intangible or elusive.
