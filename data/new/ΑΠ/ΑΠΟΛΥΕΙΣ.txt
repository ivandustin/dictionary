Etymology: The word "απολυεις" (apolyeis) comes from the Greek verb "απολύω" (apolyo), which means "to release" or "to set free". 

Grammar: "απολυεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "απολύω" (apolyo). It is used when addressing one person and means "you release" or "you set free".

Description: "απολυεις" is a Greek word that is used to express the action of releasing or setting free someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as freeing a person from a commitment or letting go of something.
