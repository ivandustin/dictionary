Grammar: "αποθεσθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "αποτίθημι" (apotithemi). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The middle voice indicates that the subject of the verb is both the doer and the receiver of the action.
