Etymology: The word "αποθνήσκων" comes from the Greek verb "θνήσκω" (thnēskō), which means "to die." The prefix "απο-" (apo-) in Greek indicates separation or completion, so "αποθνήσκων" can be translated as "one who has died" or "the deceased."

Grammar: "αποθνήσκων" is the present participle form of the verb "θνήσκω" (thnēskō) in Greek. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently in the process of dying or has recently died. In Greek grammar, the present participle can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description: "αποθνήσκων" refers to someone who is dying or has already died. It can be used to describe a deceased person or to refer to the concept of death in general. The word is commonly used in legal or formal contexts, such as obituaries, death certificates, or discussions about mortality.
