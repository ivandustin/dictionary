Etymology: The word "αποτίσω" (apotiso) comes from the Greek verb "αποτίνω" (apotino), which means "to pay off" or "to compensate." It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away," and the verb "τίνω" (tino), meaning "to pay."

Grammar: "Αποτίσω" is a verb in the future tense, first person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The first person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is the speaker.

Description: "Αποτίσω" is a Greek verb that means "I will pay off" or "I will compensate." It is used to express the future action of paying off a debt or compensating for something.
