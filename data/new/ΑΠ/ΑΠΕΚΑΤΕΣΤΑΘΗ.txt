It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "καθίστημι" (kathístimi), meaning "to place" or "to establish".

Grammar: "απεκατεστάθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "αποκαθιστώ" (apokathistó). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.
