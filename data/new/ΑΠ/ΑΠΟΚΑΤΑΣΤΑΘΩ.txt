Etymology: The word "αποκατασταθώ" comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away," and "κατασταθώ" (katastatho), meaning "to establish" or "to restore." 

Grammar: "Αποκατασταθώ" is a verb in the first person singular form of the future tense in Greek. It is derived from the verb "κατασταθώ" (katastatho), which means "to establish" or "to restore." The prefix "από" (apo) adds the meaning of "from" or "away," indicating a restoration or reestablishment of a previous state.

Description: "Αποκατασταθώ" is a Greek verb that means "I will restore" or "I will establish again." It implies the action of bringing something back to its original or previous state. It can be used in various contexts, such as restoring a relationship, rebuilding a structure, or recovering from an illness.
