Etymology: The word "αποχωρησας" comes from the Greek verb "αποχωρώ" (apochoró), which means "to depart" or "to withdraw." The suffix "-ας" (-as) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle.

Grammar: "Αποχωρησας" is the past participle form of the verb "αποχωρώ" (apochoró) in the second person singular. It is used to describe an action that has been completed in the past.

Description: "Αποχωρησας" means "you departed" or "you withdrew" in English. It refers to the act of leaving or moving away from a place or situation.
