Etymology: The word "απελυοντο" comes from the Greek verb "απελυω" (apeluo), which means "to release" or "to set free". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "λυω" (luo), meaning "to loose" or "to untie".

Grammar: "Απελυοντο" is the third person plural, past tense, middle voice form of the verb "απελυω" (apeluo). In Greek grammar, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Απελυοντο" is a Greek word that means "they were released" or "they were set free". It is used to describe an action in the past where a group of people or things were freed or released from a certain condition or situation.
