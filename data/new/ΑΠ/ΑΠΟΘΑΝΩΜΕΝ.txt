Etymology: The word "αποθανώμεν" comes from the Greek verb "αποθνήσκω" (apothnḗskō), which means "to die". It is formed by adding the suffix "-μεν" (-men) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "αποθανώμεν" is a first-person plural present subjunctive form of the verb "αποθνήσκω" (apothnḗskō). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express actions that are uncertain, hypothetical, or desired.

Description: "αποθανώμεν" translates to "let us die" in English. It is a form used to express a suggestion, wish, or command for a group of people to die.
