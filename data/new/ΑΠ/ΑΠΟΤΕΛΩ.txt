Etymology: The word "αποτελώ" (apotelo) comes from the combination of the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away," and the verb "τελώ" (telo), meaning "to accomplish" or "to complete." 

Grammar: "Αποτελώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ αποτελώ, εσύ αποτελείς, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό αποτελεί, εμείς αποτελούμε, εσείς αποτελείτε, αυτοί/αυτές αποτελούν.

Description: The verb "αποτελώ" means "to constitute," "to form," or "to make up." It is used to describe the process of something being composed or comprised of certain elements. It can also be used to express the idea of achieving or accomplishing something.
