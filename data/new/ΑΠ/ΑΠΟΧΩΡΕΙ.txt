Etymology: The word "αποχωρεί" comes from the Greek verb "χωρώ" (choreo), which means "to go, to depart." The prefix "απο-" (apo-) in Greek indicates separation or movement away from something. Therefore, "αποχωρεί" can be translated as "he/she/it departs" or "he/she/it goes away."

Grammar: "Αποχωρεί" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "αποχωρώ" (apochoro). In Greek, verbs change their endings based on the subject and tense. The present tense indicates an action happening in the present time.

Description: "Αποχωρεί" is a verb in the Greek language that describes the action of someone or something departing or going away. It can be used to express physical departure or leaving a place, as well as metaphorical departure or separation from a situation or relationship.
