Etymology: The word "απεπλευσαν" comes from the Greek verb "αποπλέω" (apopleo), which means "to sail away" or "to set sail." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away," and the verb "πλέω" (pleo), meaning "to sail."

Grammar: "Απεπλευσαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense of the verb "αποπλέω" (apopleo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe completed actions in the past. The third person plural refers to a group of people or things.

Description: "Απεπλευσαν" describes the action of a group of people or things sailing away or setting sail. It indicates that the action has been completed in the past.
