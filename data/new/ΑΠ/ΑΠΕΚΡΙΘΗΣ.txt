Etymology: The word "απεκριθης" (apekrithēs) comes from the Greek verb "αποκρίνομαι" (apokrinomai), which means "to answer" or "to reply". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "off", and the verb "κρίνω" (krinō), meaning "to judge" or "to decide".

Grammar: "Απεκρίθης" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is used when addressing someone directly and means "you answered" or "you replied".

Description: "Απεκρίθης" is a Greek word that refers to the act of answering or replying to someone. It is often used in the context of a conversation or dialogue, indicating that someone has responded to a question or statement.
