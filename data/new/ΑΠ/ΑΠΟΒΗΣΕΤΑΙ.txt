Etymology: The word "αποβησεται" comes from the Greek verb "αποβαίνω" (apováino), which means "to result in" or "to end up". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "από" (apó), meaning "from" or "away", and the verb "βαίνω" (váino), meaning "to go".

Grammar: "αποβησεται" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: The word "αποβησεται" describes an action or event that will result in something or have a certain outcome in the future. It implies that there will be a specific consequence or conclusion.
