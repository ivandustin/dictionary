Etymology: The word "απολυειν" (apolyein) comes from the Greek roots "απο" (apo), meaning "away" or "from," and "λυειν" (lyein), meaning "to loosen" or "to release." 

Grammar: "απολυειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "απολυειν" means "to release" or "to dismiss" in English. It is often used to describe the act of setting someone or something free, or letting them go.
