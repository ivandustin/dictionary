Etymology: The word "αποδημών" comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and "δήμος" (demos), meaning "people" or "community." It refers to someone who is away from their homeland or living in a foreign country.

Grammar: "Αποδημών" is a noun in the masculine gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case. For example, in the nominative case, it is "αποδημών," but in the accusative case, it becomes "αποδήμονα" (apodimon-a).

Description: "Αποδημών" is used to describe someone who has left their homeland and is living in a foreign country. It can refer to immigrants, expatriates, or anyone who is residing outside their native land. The term carries the connotation of being away from one's community or familiar surroundings.
