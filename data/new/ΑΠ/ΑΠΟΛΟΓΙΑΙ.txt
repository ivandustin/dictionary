Etymology: The word "απολογία" comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away," and "λόγος" (logos), meaning "word" or "speech." It can be translated as "a speech from" or "a speech in defense of."

Grammar: "Απολογία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form, and its plural form is "απολογίες" (apologies).

Description: "Απολογία" refers to a formal speech or written statement made by someone in defense or justification of their actions, beliefs, or opinions. It is often used in legal or philosophical contexts, where individuals present arguments to explain or defend themselves against accusations or criticism.
