Etymology: The word "αποκαθιστάνεις" comes from the Greek verb "αποκαθιστώ" (apokathistó), which means "to restore" or "to bring back to its original state". The suffix "-εις" indicates the second person singular form of the verb.

Grammar: "Αποκαθιστάνεις" is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It is used to address someone directly and ask if they are restoring or bringing something back to its original state.

Description: "Αποκαθιστάνεις" is a Greek word that means "are you restoring" or "are you bringing back to its original state". It is commonly used in conversations to inquire about someone's actions or intentions of restoring or fixing something.
