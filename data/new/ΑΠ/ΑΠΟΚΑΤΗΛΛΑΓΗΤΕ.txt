Etymology: The word "αποκατηλλαγητε" comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away" or "from," and "καταλλαγή" (katallagí), meaning "reconciliation" or "restoration." 

Grammar: "αποκατηλλαγητε" is a verb in the imperative mood, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb "αποκαταλλάσσομαι" (apokatallássomai), which means "to reconcile" or "to restore a relationship."

Description: "αποκατηλλαγητε" is a command or request for a group of people to reconcile or restore a relationship. It is used to urge or encourage others to make peace or resolve conflicts.
