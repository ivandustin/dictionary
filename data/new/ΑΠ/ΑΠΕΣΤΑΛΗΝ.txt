Etymology: The word "απεσταλην" (apostalēn) comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostellō), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "στέλλω" (stellō), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Απεσταλην" is the aorist tense, passive voice, first person singular form of the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostellō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Απεσταλην" can be translated as "I was sent" or "I have been sent" in English. It implies that the speaker was sent by someone else to a particular place or on a specific mission. The word suggests a sense of being commissioned or delegated to carry out a task or fulfill a purpose.
