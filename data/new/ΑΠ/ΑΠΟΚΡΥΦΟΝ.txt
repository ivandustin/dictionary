Etymology: The word "αποκρυφον" (apokryphon) comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away" or "off," and "κρύπτω" (krypto), meaning "to hide" or "to conceal." It literally translates to "hidden" or "concealed."

Grammar: "Αποκρυφον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative, genitive, accusative, etc., to indicate its role in a sentence.

Description: "Αποκρυφον" refers to something that is hidden, concealed, or secret. It can be used to describe hidden knowledge, secret teachings, or mysterious texts. In a broader sense, it can also refer to anything that is kept hidden or not easily accessible.
