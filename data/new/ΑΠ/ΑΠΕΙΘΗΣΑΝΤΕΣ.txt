Etymology: The word "απειθησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "απειθέω" (apeitheo), which means "to disobey" or "to be disobedient". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the verb "πείθω" (peitho), meaning "to persuade" or "to convince".

Grammar: "Απειθησαντες" is the plural form of the verb "απειθέω" in the aorist tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have disobeyed or refused to follow orders or instructions.

Description: "Απειθησαντες" is a Greek word that refers to individuals or a group of people who have shown disobedience or a lack of willingness to comply with authority or rules. It implies a deliberate act of defiance or resistance against authority.
