Etymology: The word "απελευσομαι" (apeleusomai) comes from the Greek verb "απελευθεροω" (apeleutheroo), which means "to set free" or "to liberate". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the root "ελευθερος" (eleutheros), meaning "free".

Grammar: "Απελευσομαι" is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the first person singular form. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on or for itself.

Description: "Απελευσομαι" means "I set myself free" or "I liberate myself" in Greek. It implies an act of freeing oneself from a situation, constraint, or limitation.
