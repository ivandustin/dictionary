Etymology: The word "αποδους" (apodous) comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and "πούς" (pous), meaning "foot." It refers to a creature or organism that lacks or has no feet.

Grammar: In Greek, "αποδους" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined according to the case, number, and gender of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αποδους" is used to describe organisms or creatures that do not have feet. It can refer to various animals, such as snakes, worms, or certain types of insects, that move without the use of legs or feet.
