Etymology: The word "απελθειν" (apellein) comes from the Greek roots "apo" meaning "away" and "ellein" meaning "to go." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "h₁elh₁-" meaning "to go" or "to wander."

Grammar: "Απελθειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, suggesting that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Απελθειν" means "to go away" or "to depart" in Greek. It implies physically moving away from a location or leaving a place. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate the act of departing from a situation, relationship, or state of mind.
