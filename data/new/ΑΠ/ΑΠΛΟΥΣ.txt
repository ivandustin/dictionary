Etymology: The word "απλούς" (aploús) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἁπλοῦς" (haploûs), meaning "simple" or "plain."

Grammar: In Greek, "απλούς" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: απλούς (masculine), απλή (feminine), and απλό (neuter).

Description: "Απλούς" is used to describe something that is straightforward, uncomplicated, or without any unnecessary complexity. It can also mean "honest" or "sincere."
