Etymology: The word "απέχεις" comes from the Greek verb "απέχω" (apecho), which means "to be distant" or "to be far away". It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb "έχω" (echo), meaning "to have" or "to possess".

Grammar: "Απέχεις" is the second person singular form of the verb "απέχω" in the present tense. It is used to address someone directly and means "you are distant" or "you are far away".

Description: "Απέχεις" is a Greek word that describes the state of being distant or far away. It can be used in various contexts, such as physical distance, emotional distance, or figurative distance.
