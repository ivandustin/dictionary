Etymology: The word "απολελυμένην" comes from the Greek verb "απολύω" (apolýo), which means "to release" or "to set free." The suffix "-μένην" (-ménin) is added to the verb stem to indicate the past participle form.

Grammar: "Απολελυμένην" is the feminine singular form of the past participle of the verb "απολύω" (apolýo). It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Απολελυμένην" is an adjective in Greek that means "released" or "set free." It is used to describe something or someone that has been freed or liberated from a certain condition or situation.
