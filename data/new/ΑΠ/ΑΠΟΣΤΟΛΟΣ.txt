Etymology: The word "αποστολος" (apostolos) comes from the Greek verb "αποστελλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". In ancient Greece, an "αποστολος" referred to a messenger or an envoy who was sent to deliver a message or carry out a mission.

Grammar: "Αποστολος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "αποστολου" (apostolou). In the plural form, it becomes "αποστολοι" (apostoloi).

Description: In modern Greek, "αποστολος" is commonly used to refer to an apostle, particularly in the context of Christianity. In the New Testament of the Bible, the term "αποστολος" is used to describe the twelve disciples chosen by Jesus to spread his teachings. These apostles were considered the foundation of the early Christian Church.

Outside of religious contexts, "αποστολος" can also be used more broadly to refer to a messenger, envoy, or ambassador. It can denote someone who is sent on a mission or tasked with a specific duty.
