Etymology: The word "απομασσομέθα" (apomassometha) comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and "μάσσω" (masso), meaning "to knead" or "to squeeze." It is derived from the ancient Greek practice of kneading or squeezing out impurities from substances.

Grammar: "απομασσομέθα" is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person plural form of the verb "απομάσσομαι" (apomassomai), which means "we knead" or "we squeeze out."

Description: "απομασσομέθα" refers to the action of kneading or squeezing out impurities from a substance. It can be used in a literal sense, such as kneading dough to remove air bubbles, or in a figurative sense, such as purifying or refining something.
