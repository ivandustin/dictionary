Etymology: The word "αποστολής" (apostolēs) comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostellō), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away", and the verb "στέλλω" (stellō), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Αποστολής" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "from" in English.

Description: "Αποστολής" refers to the act of sending or dispatching someone or something. It can also refer to a mission, task, or assignment. In a religious context, it can specifically refer to the apostles, who were the early followers of Jesus Christ and were sent out to spread his teachings.
