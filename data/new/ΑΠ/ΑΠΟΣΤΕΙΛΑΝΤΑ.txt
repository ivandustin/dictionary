Etymology: The word "αποστειλαντα" comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello), which means "to send" or "to dispatch". The suffix "-αντα" is added to the verb stem to indicate the past participle form.

Grammar: "Αποστειλαντα" is the past participle form of the verb "αποστέλλω" in the masculine gender, singular number, and accusative case. In Greek grammar, the past participle is used to form compound tenses and passive voice constructions.

Description: "Αποστειλαντα" refers to a singular masculine noun or pronoun that has been sent or dispatched. It can be used as a verb complement or as a standalone noun in a sentence.
