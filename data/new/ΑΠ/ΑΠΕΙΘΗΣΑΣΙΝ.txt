Etymology: The word "απειθησασιν" comes from the Greek verb "απειθέω" (apeitheo), which means "to disobey" or "to be disobedient". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the verb "πείθω" (peitho), meaning "to persuade" or "to convince".

Grammar: "απειθησασιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "απειθέω" (apeitheo) in the aorist tense. It is used to describe an action of disobedience or refusal to obey in the past.
