Etymology: The word "αποσυναγωγος" comes from the Greek roots "απο" (apo), meaning "away from," and "συναγωγη" (synagoge), meaning "gathering" or "assembly." 

Grammar: "Αποσυναγωγος" is a Greek adjective in the masculine form. It is used to describe someone or something that is "away from the gathering" or "excluded from the assembly."

Description: "Αποσυναγωγος" is a term used in Greek to refer to someone who has been expelled or excluded from a religious or social gathering, particularly from a synagogue. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is isolated or alienated from a group or community.
