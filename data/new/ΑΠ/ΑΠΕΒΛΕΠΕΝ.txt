Etymology: The word "απεβλέπεν" comes from the Greek verb "βλέπω" (blepo), which means "to see". The prefix "απε-" (ape-) indicates a sense of separation or departure. Therefore, "απεβλέπεν" can be translated as "to look away" or "to turn one's gaze".

Grammar: "απεβλέπεν" is the third person singular form of the verb in the imperfect tense. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: "απεβλέπεν" is an ancient Greek verb that describes the act of looking away or turning one's gaze. It implies a deliberate action of diverting one's attention from something or someone. This word can be used metaphorically to express the idea of intentionally ignoring or disregarding something.
