Etymology: The word "αποστελλω" (apostello) comes from the Greek roots "απο" (apo), meaning "away" or "from," and "στελλω" (stello), meaning "to send." 

Grammar: "Αποστελλω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different actions and states of sending.

Description: "Αποστελλω" means "to send" in Greek. It is used to express the action of sending something or someone from one place to another. It can also be used metaphorically to convey the idea of transmitting or delivering a message or information.
