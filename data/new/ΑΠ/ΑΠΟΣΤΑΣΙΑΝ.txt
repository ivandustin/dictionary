Etymology: The word "αποστασίαν" comes from the Greek word "αποστασία" (apostasía), which means "apostasy" or "defection". It is derived from the verb "αποστατέω" (apostatéo), meaning "to revolt" or "to desert".

Grammar: "αποστασίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "αποστασίαν" refers to the act of apostasy or defection, which is the abandonment or renunciation of one's religious or political beliefs. It can also be used more broadly to describe any act of rebellion, betrayal, or abandonment of a cause or allegiance.
