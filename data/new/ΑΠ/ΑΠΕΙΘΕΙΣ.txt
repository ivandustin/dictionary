Etymology: The word "απειθείς" (apeitheis) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "πείθω" (peitho), meaning "to persuade." Therefore, "απειθείς" can be translated as "disobedient" or "unpersuaded."

Grammar: "Απειθείς" is the plural form of the adjective "απειθής" (apeithis), which means "disobedient" or "unpersuaded." It is used to describe a group of people who do not follow orders or refuse to be convinced.

Description: "Απειθείς" is a term used to describe individuals or groups who do not comply with rules, instructions, or authority. It implies a lack of obedience or willingness to be persuaded.
