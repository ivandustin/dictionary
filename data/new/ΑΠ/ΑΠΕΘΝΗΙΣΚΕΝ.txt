Etymology: The word "απεθνήσκεν" comes from the Greek verb "αποθνήσκω" (apothnēskō), which means "to die". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "off", and the verb "θνήσκω" (thnēskō), meaning "to die".

Grammar: "Απεθνήσκεν" is the third person singular, past tense form of the verb "αποθνήσκω" (apothnēskō). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense form indicates that the action of dying happened in the past and was performed by a third person.

Description: "Απεθνήσκεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it died" in English. It is used to describe the action of someone or something dying in the past.
