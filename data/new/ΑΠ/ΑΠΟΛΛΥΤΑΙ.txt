Etymology: The word "απολλυται" comes from the Greek verb "απολλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish".

Grammar: "απολλυται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "απολλυμι" (apollymi) in Greek. It is in the indicative mood and the middle voice.

Description: "απολλυται" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it is destroyed" or "he/she/it perishes". It is used to describe the action of something being destroyed or coming to an end.
