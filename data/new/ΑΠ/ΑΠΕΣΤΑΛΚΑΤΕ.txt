Etymology: The word "απεσταλκατε" comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostéllo), which means "to send" or "to dispatch". It is conjugated in the perfect tense, indicative mood, and second person plural form.

Grammar: "απεσταλκατε" is a verb form in Greek. It belongs to the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The indicative mood suggests a statement or a fact. The second person plural form indicates that the action is being performed by a group of people.

Description: "απεσταλκατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostéllo) in Greek. It translates to "you all have sent" or "you all have dispatched" in English.
