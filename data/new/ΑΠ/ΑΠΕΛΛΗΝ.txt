Etymology: The word "απελλην" comes from the Greek word "Ἀπελλην" (Apellēn), which refers to a member of the Apella, the popular assembly in ancient Sparta.

Grammar: "Απελλην" is a noun in the singular form.

Description: "Απελλην" is a term used to describe a citizen of ancient Sparta who was a member of the Apella, the popular assembly. The Apella was responsible for making important decisions and passing laws in Sparta. The term "απελλην" is often used to refer to someone who actively participated in the political life of Sparta and had the right to vote in the assembly.
