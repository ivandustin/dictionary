Etymology: The word "απολυσης" (apolyseis) comes from the Greek verb "απολυω" (apolyo), which means "to release" or "to dismiss". 

Grammar: "Απολυσης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Απολυσης" refers to the act of releasing or dismissing someone from a position or job. It can also be used to describe the termination of a contract or the end of a relationship.
