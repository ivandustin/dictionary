Etymology: The word "απηλγηκότες" comes from the Greek roots "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from," and "ἄλγος" (algos), meaning "pain." It is derived from the verb "απαλγέω" (apalgeo), which means "to be free from pain."

Grammar: "Απηλγηκότες" is the plural form of the adjective "απηλγηκός" (apilgikos), which means "pain-free" or "free from pain." It is used to describe multiple people or things that are without pain.
