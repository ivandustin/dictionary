Etymology: The word "απονεμοντες" comes from the Greek verb "απονεμω" (aponomo), which means "to distribute" or "to allot". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "νεμω" (nemo), meaning "to distribute" or "to assign".

Grammar: "Απονεμοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "απονεμω" (aponomo) in the active voice. It is used to describe the action of distributing or allotting in the present tense. In Greek grammar, the present participle can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Απονεμοντες" refers to individuals or a group of people who are currently engaged in the act of distributing or allotting something. It implies an ongoing action or activity.
