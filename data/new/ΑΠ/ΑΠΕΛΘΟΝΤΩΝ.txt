Grammar: "απελθοντων" is the present participle form of the verb "απέρχομαι" (aperchomai) in the masculine plural genitive case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship.

Description: "απελθοντων" can be translated as "of those who are going away" or "of those who are departing". It refers to a group of people who are currently in the process of leaving or going away from a certain place.
