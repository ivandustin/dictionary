Etymology: The word "απογραφή" (apografi) comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "out of," and "γράφω" (grapho), meaning "to write." It literally translates to "writing out" or "registering."

Grammar: "Απογραφή" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and its plural form is "απογραφές" (apografes).

Description: "Απογραφή" refers to the act of registering or recording information, particularly in an official or formal context. It can be used to describe the process of taking a census or conducting a survey to gather data about a population. In Greece, it is commonly used to refer to the population census that takes place every ten years.
