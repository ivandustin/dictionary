Etymology: The word "αποκτανθείναι" comes from the Greek verb "αποκτείνω" (apokteíno), which means "to kill" or "to slay". It is formed by adding the reflexive suffix "-θηναι" (-thenai) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "αποκτανθείναι" is an infinitive verb form in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense and passive voice. The "-θηναι" suffix indicates the passive voice, meaning that the action is performed on the subject.

Description: The word "αποκτανθείναι" refers to the act of being killed or slain. It is commonly used in Greek literature and language to describe the act of causing someone's death or being killed oneself.
