Etymology: The word "απενεγκειν" comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "away from," and "φέρω" (fero), meaning "to carry." 

Grammar: "απενεγκειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to a specific tense, mood, or person.

Description: "απενεγκειν" is a Greek verb that translates to "to carry away" or "to remove." It implies the action of taking something or someone from one place to another, often with the connotation of removing or transporting it away.
