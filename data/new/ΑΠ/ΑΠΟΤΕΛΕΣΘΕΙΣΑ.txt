Etymology: The word "αποτελεσθείσα" comes from the Greek verb "αποτελώ" (apoteło), which means "to accomplish" or "to achieve". The suffix "-σθεισα" (-stheisa) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "αποτελεσθείσα" is the feminine singular form of the passive participle of the verb "αποτελώ". It is used to describe a female subject who has undergone the action of accomplishing or achieving something.

Description: "αποτελεσθείσα" is a Greek word that describes a female subject who has been accomplished or achieved something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a woman who has achieved success in her career, accomplished a difficult task, or fulfilled a goal.
