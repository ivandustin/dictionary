Etymology: The word "αποθήκη" (apothíki) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀποθήκη" (apothḗkē), which means "storehouse" or "warehouse". It is derived from the verb "ἀποτίθημι" (apotíthēmi), meaning "to put away" or "to store".

Grammar: In Greek, "αποθήκη" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "αποθήκες" (apothíkes).

Description: "Αποθήκη" is commonly used in Greek to refer to a storage place or a warehouse where goods or supplies are kept. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a place where things are stored or accumulated, such as a repository of knowledge or information.
