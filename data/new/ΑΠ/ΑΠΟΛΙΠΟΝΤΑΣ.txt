Etymology: The word "απολιποντας" comes from the Greek verb "απολείπω" (apoleípo), which means "to leave behind" or "to abandon". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "λείπω" (leípo), meaning "to leave".

Grammar: "Απολιποντας" is the present participle form of the verb "απολείπω" (apoleípo). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Απολιποντας" describes someone or something that is leaving behind or abandoning something. It implies an ongoing or continuous action of leaving or abandoning.
