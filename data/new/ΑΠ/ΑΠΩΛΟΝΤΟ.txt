Etymology: The word "απολοντο" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "απολύω" (apolýo), which means "to release" or "to set free".

Grammar: "απολοντο" is the third person plural, past tense, middle voice form of the verb "απολύω" (apolýo). In Greek grammar, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "απολοντο" is a verb form that indicates an action of releasing or setting free in the past. It is used when referring to a group of people or things that were released or set free.
