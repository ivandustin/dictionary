Etymology: The word "απελθητε" (apélthete) comes from the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" (apérchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart". It is formed by combining the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "έρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "απελθητε" is the second person plural, aorist tense, imperative mood form of the verb "απέρχομαι" (apérchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: The word "απελθητε" is a command or request for a group of people to go away or depart. It is used to instruct or urge others to leave a place or move away from a certain location.
