Etymology: The word "απεκαλύπτεν" (apekalýpten) comes from the Greek verb "ἀποκαλύπτω" (apokalýptō), which means "to uncover" or "to reveal". It is derived from the prefix "ἀπό" (apó), meaning "away from", and the verb "καλύπτω" (kalýptō), meaning "to cover".

Grammar: "Απεκαλύπτεν" is the present active participle form of the verb "ἀποκαλύπτω" (apokalýptō) in Ancient Greek. It is in the third person singular form and indicates ongoing action.

Description: "Απεκαλύπτεν" is used to describe someone or something that is currently uncovering or revealing something. It implies an ongoing action of bringing to light or making known something that was previously hidden or unknown.
