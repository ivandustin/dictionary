Etymology: The word "απεναντι" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away," and the word "εναντίον" (enantion), meaning "opposite" or "against."

Grammar: In Greek, "απεναντι" is an adverb that is used to indicate the position or direction of something being opposite or facing someone or something else. It is often used in phrases or sentences to describe the location or orientation of objects or people in relation to each other.

Description: "Απεναντι" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used to describe the position of objects or people in relation to each other, such as "Ο σπίτι είναι απέναντι από το πάρκο" (The house is opposite the park). It can also be used metaphorically to describe opposing viewpoints or contrasting situations, such as "Οι απόψεις μας είναι απέναντι" (Our opinions are opposite).
