Etymology: The word "απαγουσα" (apagousa) comes from the Greek verb "απάγω" (apago), which means "to lead away" or "to take away". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away", and the verb "αγω" (ago), meaning "to lead" or "to carry".

Grammar: "Απαγουσα" is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb "απάγω" (apago). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to form compound tenses and to express ongoing actions or states. The feminine form indicates that the subject of the action is female.

Description: "Απαγουσα" is a Greek word that can be translated as "leading away" or "taking away" in English. It describes the action of leading or taking someone or something away from a certain place.
