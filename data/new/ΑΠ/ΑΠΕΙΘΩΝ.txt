Etymology: The word "απειθών" comes from the Greek roots "α-" (not) and "πειθώ" (to persuade). It is derived from the verb "πείθω" (to persuade) with the negative prefix "α-".

Grammar: "Απειθών" is the present participle form of the verb "απειθώ" in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is not obedient or does not follow orders.

Description: "Απειθών" is an adjective that describes someone who is disobedient, rebellious, or resistant to authority. It can also be used to describe actions or behaviors that go against rules or instructions.
