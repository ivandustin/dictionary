Etymology: The word "απολεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "ἀπόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish".

Grammar: "απολεσθαι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "ἀπόλλυμι" (apollymi). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "απολεσθαι" is a Greek verb form that expresses the idea of destruction or perishing. It is used to describe the action of something being destroyed or coming to an end.
