Etymology: The word "αποστρεψουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αποστρέφω" (apostrepho), which means "to turn away" or "to divert". The suffix "-ουσιν" indicates the third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "αποστρεψουσιν" is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "αποστρεψουσιν" means "they will turn away" or "they will divert" in English. It is used to express an action that will happen in the future, performed by a group of people.
