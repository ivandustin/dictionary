Etymology: The word "αποδεκτον" comes from the Greek word "αποδέχομαι" (apodechomai), which means "to accept" or "to receive".

Grammar: "αποδεκτον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" in the nominative case.

Description: "αποδεκτον" refers to something that is acceptable or agreeable. It can be used to describe an idea, a proposal, or a decision that is deemed acceptable or approved.
