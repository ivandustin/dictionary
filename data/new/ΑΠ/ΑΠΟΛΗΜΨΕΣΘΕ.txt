Etymology: The word "απολημψεσθε" comes from the Greek language. It is a verb form derived from the root word "απολαμβάνω" (apolambano), which means "to receive" or "to take".

Grammar: "απολημψεσθε" is the second person plural form of the verb "απολαμβάνω" in the future middle-passive tense. In Greek grammar, the future middle-passive tense is used to express actions that will be performed by the subject or actions that will happen to the subject in the future.

Description: "απολημψεσθε" can be translated to English as "you will be received" or "you will take". It indicates that a group of people (the second person plural) will be the recipients or takers of something in the future.
