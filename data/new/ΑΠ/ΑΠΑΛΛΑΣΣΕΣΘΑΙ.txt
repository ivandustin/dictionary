Etymology: The word "απαλλασσεσθαι" (apallassésthai) comes from the Greek verb "απαλλάσσω" (apallássō), which means "to free oneself from" or "to get rid of." It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb "αλλάσσω" (allássō), meaning "to change" or "to exchange."

Grammar: "απαλλασσεσθαι" is a reflexive verb in the middle voice form. In Greek grammar, reflexive verbs indicate that the subject of the verb is performing the action on itself. The "-σθαι" ending is a characteristic of middle voice verbs.

Description: "απαλλασσεσθαι" is an ancient Greek verb that conveys the idea of freeing oneself from something or getting rid of something. It implies an active effort to separate oneself from a particular situation, condition, or object.
