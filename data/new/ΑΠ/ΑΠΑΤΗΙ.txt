Etymology: The word "απατηί" (apati) comes from the Greek verb "απατώ" (apato), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". 

Grammar: "Απατηί" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the feminine gender and belongs to the second declension. 

Description: "Απατηί" refers to deceit or deception. It is used to describe an act of tricking or misleading someone with the intention of gaining an unfair advantage or causing harm.
