Etymology: The word "απελθουσα" (apélthousa) comes from the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" (apérchomai), which means "to go away" or "to depart."

Grammar: "απελθουσα" is the feminine form of the Greek verb "απέρχομαι" in the present participle tense. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently going away or departing.

Description: "απελθουσα" is a Greek word that describes a female subject who is in the process of going away or departing from a certain place. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone leaving a location or moving away from a situation.
