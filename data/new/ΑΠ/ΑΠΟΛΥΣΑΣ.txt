Etymology: The word "απολυσας" (apolyzas) comes from the Greek verb "απολυω" (apolyo), which means "to release" or "to set free". 

Grammar: "Απολυσας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "απολυω" (apolyo). It is used to describe someone who has released or set free something or someone. 

Description: "Απολυσας" is a Greek word that refers to the act of releasing or setting free. It can be used in various contexts, such as releasing a prisoner, setting free a captive, or letting go of something or someone.
