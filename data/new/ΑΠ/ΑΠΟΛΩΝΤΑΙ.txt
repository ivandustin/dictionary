Etymology: The word "απολωνται" (apollontai) comes from the Greek verb "απόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish."

Grammar: "απολωνται" is the third person plural form of the verb "απόλλυμι" in the present tense. It is used to describe actions or states of multiple subjects who are currently perishing or being destroyed.

Description: "απολωνται" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of destruction or perishing. It can be used in various contexts to describe the act of something or someone being destroyed or coming to an end.
