Etymology: The word "αποστραφής" comes from the Greek verb "αποστρέφω" (apostrefo), which means "to turn away" or "to avert". The suffix "-ης" (-is) is added to form the noun.

Grammar: "Αποστραφής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Αποστραφής" refers to the act of turning away or averting. It can also be used to describe someone who is distant or aloof.
