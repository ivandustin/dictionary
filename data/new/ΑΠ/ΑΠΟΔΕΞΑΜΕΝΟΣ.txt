Etymology: The word "αποδεξαμενος" comes from the Greek roots "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from," and "δεξαμενος" (dexamenos), meaning "receiving" or "accepting." 

Grammar: "Αποδεξαμενος" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word can be declined to match different cases, numbers, and genders.

Description: "Αποδεξαμενος" describes someone or something that is accepting, receptive, or open-minded. It can also refer to someone who is accommodating or willing to receive or take in something.
