Etymology: The word "απολεσω" (apoleso) comes from the Greek verb "απολλυμι" (apollumi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the root "λυ-" (lu-), meaning "to loose" or "to destroy".

Grammar: "απολεσω" (apoleso) is a verb in the future tense, first person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The first person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is the speaker.

Description: "απολεσω" (apoleso) can be translated to English as "I will destroy" or "I will perish". It expresses an action that will happen in the future, where the speaker is the one who will cause destruction or face destruction.
