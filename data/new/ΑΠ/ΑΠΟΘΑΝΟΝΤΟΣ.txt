Etymology: The word "αποθανοντος" comes from the Greek roots "απο" (apo), meaning "away" or "from," and "θανατος" (thanatos), meaning "death." 

Grammar: "Αποθανοντος" is the genitive form of the Greek noun "αποθανοντης" (apothanontes), which means "the one who dies" or "the deceased." In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αποθανοντος" is a Greek word that refers to someone who has died or is deceased. It is often used in formal or legal contexts to describe a person who has passed away.
