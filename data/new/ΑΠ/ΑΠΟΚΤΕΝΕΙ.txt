Etymology: The word "αποκτενεί" comes from the Greek verb "αποκτείνω" (apoktíno), which means "to kill" or "to murder". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "από" (apó), meaning "from" or "away", and the verb "κτείνω" (ktíno), meaning "to kill".

Grammar: "αποκτενεί" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice.

Description: The word "αποκτενεί" describes the action of killing or murdering someone. It implies the intentional act of causing someone's death.
