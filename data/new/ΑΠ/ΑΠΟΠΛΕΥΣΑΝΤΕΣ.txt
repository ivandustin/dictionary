Etymology: The word "αποπλευσάντες" comes from the Greek verb "αποπλέω" (apopleo), which means "to sail away" or "to set sail." The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates a group of people who have performed the action of the verb.

Grammar: "Αποπλευσάντες" is the plural form of the verb "αποπλέω" in the aorist tense. It is used to refer to a group of people who have sailed away or set sail.

Description: "Αποπλευσάντες" is a Greek word that describes a group of people who have embarked on a sailing journey or have departed by ship. It can be used to refer to sailors, travelers, or explorers who have set sail from a particular location.
