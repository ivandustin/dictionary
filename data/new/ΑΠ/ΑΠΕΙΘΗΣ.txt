Etymology: The word "απειθης" (apeithēs) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "πειθω" (peithō), meaning "to persuade." Therefore, "απειθης" can be translated as "disobedient" or "unpersuaded."

Grammar: In Greek, "απειθης" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "απειθης" is used to describe someone who does not obey or follow instructions, rules, or authority. It implies a lack of willingness to comply or be persuaded.
