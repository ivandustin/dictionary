Etymology: The word "απιστησας" comes from the Greek verb "απιστεω" (apistéo), which means "to disbelieve" or "to be faithless". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the verb "πιστευω" (pisteuo), meaning "to believe" or "to have faith".

Grammar: "Απιστησας" is the aorist tense, active voice, second person singular form of the verb "απιστεω" (apistéo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Απιστησας" can be translated as "you disbelieved" or "you were faithless" in English. It refers to the act of not believing or having faith in something or someone.
