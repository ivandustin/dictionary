Etymology: The word "απεστη" (apesti) comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "στέλλω" (stello), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Απεστη" (apesti) is the third person singular form of the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello) in the past tense. It is used to describe an action that was sent away or dispatched in the past.

Description: "Απεστη" (apesti) is a Greek word that refers to the act of sending something or someone away. It can be used in various contexts, such as sending a letter, a package, or a message. The word implies the action of physically or metaphorically separating something from its origin or source.
