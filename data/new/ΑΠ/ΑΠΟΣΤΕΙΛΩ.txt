Etymology: The word "αποστείλω" (aposteílo) comes from the combination of the prefix "από" (apó), meaning "from" or "away," and the verb "στέλλω" (stéllo), meaning "to send." Therefore, "αποστείλω" means "to send away" or "to dispatch."

Grammar: "Αποστείλω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the active voice, indicative mood, and aorist tense. It can be used in various forms to express actions of sending or dispatching something or someone.

Description: "Αποστείλω" is a versatile verb that can be used in different contexts. It can refer to physically sending or dispatching objects or people, as well as metaphorically sending or transmitting information, messages, or emotions. The verb implies a deliberate action of sending something away or transferring it to a different location or recipient.
