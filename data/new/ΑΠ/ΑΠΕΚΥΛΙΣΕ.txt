Etymology: The word "απεκυλίσε" comes from the Greek verb "απεκυλίνω" (apekylíno), which means "to unroll" or "to unwind". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "κυλίνω" (kylíno), meaning "to roll" or "to coil".

Grammar: "απεκυλίσε" is the imperative form of the verb "απεκυλίνω" (apekylíno) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to unroll or unwind something.

Description: "απεκυλίσε" is a Greek verb that specifically refers to the action of unrolling or unwinding something. It can be used in various contexts, such as unrolling a scroll, unwinding a thread, or unraveling a problem. The imperative form "απεκυλίσε" is used to directly command someone to perform this action.
