Etymology: The word "απολλυμενοι" (apollymenoi) comes from the Greek verb "απόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "away from", and the root "λύω" (luo), meaning "to loosen" or "to destroy".

Grammar: "Απολλυμένοι" (apollymenoi) is the plural form of the verb "απόλλυμι" (apollymi) in the passive voice. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are being destroyed or perishing.

Description: "Απολλυμένοι" (apollymenoi) can be translated as "those who are being destroyed" or "those who are perishing". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a state of ruin, loss, or destruction.
