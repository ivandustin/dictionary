Etymology: The word "απολελυσαι" comes from the Greek verb "απολύω" (apolýo), which means "to release" or "to set free". It is formed by combining the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from", and the verb "λύω" (lyo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie".

Grammar: "απολελυσαι" is the second person singular, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "απολύω" (apolýo). The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself.

Description: "απολελυσαι" can be translated as "you have been released" or "you have set yourself free". It implies that the subject has been freed or liberated from something or someone.
