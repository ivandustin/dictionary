Etymology: The word "απαρασκευαστους" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by the prefix "α-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the word "παρασκευαστούς" (meaning "prepared" or "ready"). 

Grammar: "Απαρασκευαστους" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word "απαρασκευαστους" describes something or someone that is unprepared, not ready, or lacking in preparation. It implies a state of being unorganized or not equipped for a particular task or situation.
