Etymology: The word "απεχοντος" (apexontos) comes from the Greek verb "απέχω" (apecho), which means "to be distant" or "to keep away". It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from", and the verb "εχω" (echo), meaning "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar: "απεχοντος" is the genitive form of the noun "απεχων" (apexon), which is the present participle of the verb "απέχω" (apecho). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "απεχοντος" can be translated as "of the one who is distant" or "of the one who keeps away". It is used to describe someone or something that is far away or keeps a distance.
