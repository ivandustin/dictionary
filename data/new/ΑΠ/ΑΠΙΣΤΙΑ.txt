Etymology: The word "απιστια" (apistia) comes from the Greek word "απίστευτος" (apistevtos), which means "unbelievable" or "incredible". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the word "πίστη" (pisti), meaning "faith" or "belief".

Grammar: "Απιστια" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. In the plural form, it becomes "απιστίες" (apisties).

Description: "Απιστια" refers to the act of being unfaithful or disloyal. It is commonly used to describe infidelity in romantic relationships, but it can also be used in a broader sense to describe betrayal or lack of trust in any type of relationship.
