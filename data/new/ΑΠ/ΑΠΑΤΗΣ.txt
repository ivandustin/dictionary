Etymology: The word "απατης" (apatis) comes from the Greek verb "απαταω" (apatao), which means "to deceive" or "to cheat". 

Grammar: "Απατης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. 

Description: "Απατης" refers to deception or fraud. It is used to describe an act of tricking or misleading someone with the intention of gaining an unfair advantage or causing harm.
