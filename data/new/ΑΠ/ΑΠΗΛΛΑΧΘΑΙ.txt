Grammar: "απηλλάχθαι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "ἀπαλλάσσω" (apallássō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "απηλλάχθαι" is used to describe the action of being separated or removed from something. It implies a complete or definitive separation.
