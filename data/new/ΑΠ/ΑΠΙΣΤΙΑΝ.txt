Etymology: The word "απιστιαν" comes from the Greek word "απιστία" (apistía), which means "infidelity" or "unfaithfulness". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not", and the word "πίστη" (písti), meaning "faith" or "trust".

Grammar: "Απιστιαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Απιστιαν" refers to the act or state of being unfaithful or disloyal. It is commonly used to describe infidelity in romantic relationships, but it can also be used more broadly to refer to any form of betrayal or breach of trust.
