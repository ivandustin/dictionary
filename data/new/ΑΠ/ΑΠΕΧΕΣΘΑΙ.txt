Etymology: The word "απεχεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "απέχω" (apecho), which means "to be distant" or "to keep away from." It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb "εχω" (echo), meaning "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar: "απεχεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "απέχω" (apecho). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present middle/passive infinitive form indicates that the action of "keeping away" or "being distant" is ongoing and either performed by the subject or happening to the subject.

Description: "απεχεσθαι" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of distance or separation. It can be translated as "to keep away," "to stay away," or "to be distant." The verb implies a deliberate action of maintaining a physical or metaphorical distance from something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's behavior, attitude, or physical location.
