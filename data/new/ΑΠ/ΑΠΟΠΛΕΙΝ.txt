Etymology: The word "αποπλεύσειν" comes from the Greek roots "από" (apo), meaning "from" or "away," and "πλεύσειν" (pleusein), meaning "to sail." It literally means "to sail away" or "to set sail."

Grammar: "Αποπλεύσειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to a specific tense, mood, or person. In Greek, verbs are highly inflected and can have various forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "Αποπλεύσειν" is a word that describes the action of sailing away or setting sail. It implies the act of leaving a place by boat or ship. It can be used in a literal sense, referring to the physical act of sailing away, or in a figurative sense, indicating the departure or separation from a situation or place.
