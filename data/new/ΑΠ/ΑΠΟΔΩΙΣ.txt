Etymology: The word "αποδωίς" (apodōís) comes from the Greek verb "αποδίδωμι" (apodídōmi), which means "to give back" or "to return." It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from," and the verb "δίδωμι" (dídōmi), meaning "to give."

Grammar: "Αποδωίς" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it is used to refer to someone who gives back or returns something.

Description: "Αποδωίς" is a term used in Greek to describe a person who gives back or returns something that they have received. It can be used in various contexts, such as returning a favor, repaying a debt, or giving back something that was borrowed.
