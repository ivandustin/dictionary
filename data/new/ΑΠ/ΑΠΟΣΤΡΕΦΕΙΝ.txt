Etymology: The word "αποστρεφειν" (apostrephein) comes from the Greek roots "apo" meaning "away" and "strephein" meaning "to turn". 

Grammar: "αποστρεφειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive form. 

Description: "αποστρεφειν" means "to turn away" or "to divert" in English. It is used to describe the action of physically or metaphorically turning something or someone away or diverting their attention.
