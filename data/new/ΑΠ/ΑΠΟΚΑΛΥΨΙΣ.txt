Etymology: The word "αποκαλυψις" (apokalypsis) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "αποκαλυπτω" (apokalypto), which means "to uncover" or "to reveal". 

Grammar: In Greek, "αποκαλυψις" is a feminine noun of the third declension. It is in the nominative case and singular number. 

Description: "Αποκαλυψις" is a Greek word that translates to "revelation" in English. It refers to the act of uncovering or revealing something that was previously hidden or unknown. In religious contexts, it is often used to describe the disclosure of divine or spiritual truths. The term is commonly associated with the biblical book of Revelation, which is also known as the Apocalypse.
