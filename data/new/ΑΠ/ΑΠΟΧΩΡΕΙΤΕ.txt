Etymology: The word "αποχωρείτε" comes from the Greek verb "αποχωρώ" (apochoró), which means "to depart" or "to leave." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "από" (apó), meaning "from," and the verb "χωρώ" (choró), meaning "to go" or "to move."

Grammar: "Αποχωρείτε" is the second person plural form of the verb "αποχωρώ" in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you (all) depart" or "you (all) leave."

Description: "Αποχωρείτε" is a command or an imperative form of the verb, instructing a group of people to depart or leave. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as giving directions, asking people to leave a place, or indicating the end of an event or gathering.
