Etymology: The word "απεκατεστηστη" comes from the Greek verb "αποκαθιστώ" (apokathistó), which means "to restore" or "to bring back to its original state."

Grammar: "απεκατεστηστη" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the aorist tense of the verb "αποκαθιστώ" (apokathistó), which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: "απεκατεστηστη" describes an action of restoring or bringing something back to its original state. It implies that something was damaged, broken, or lost, and has now been repaired or returned to its previous condition.
