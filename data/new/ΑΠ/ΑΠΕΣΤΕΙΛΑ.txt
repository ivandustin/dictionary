Etymology: The word "απεστείλα" (aposteíla) comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostéllo), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix "από" (apó), meaning "from" or "away", and the verb "στέλλω" (stéllo), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Απεστείλα" is the past tense form of the verb "αποστέλλω" (apostéllo) in the first person singular. It is used to indicate that the speaker sent or dispatched something in the past.

Description: "Απεστείλα" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the action of sending or dispatching something. It can be used in various contexts, such as sending a message, a package, or a person to a specific destination.
