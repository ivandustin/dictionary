Etymology: The word "αποθανοντες" comes from the Greek verb "αποθνῄσκω" (apothnēskō), which means "to die" or "to pass away." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away" or "from," and the verb "θνῄσκω" (thnēskō), meaning "to die."

Grammar: "Αποθανοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "αποθνῄσκω" (apothnēskō) in Greek. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. In this case, it means "dying" or "passing away."

Description: "Αποθανοντες" is a Greek word that refers to the act of dying or passing away. It can be used to describe the process of death or the state of being dead.
