Etymology: The word "απόλεια" (apoleia) comes from the Greek roots "apo" meaning "away" and "olethros" meaning "destruction". It can be translated as "ruin" or "loss".

Grammar: "Απόλεια" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "απωλείες" (apoleies).

Description: "Απόλεια" refers to the state or act of being lost, destroyed, or ruined. It can be used to describe the loss of something valuable, the destruction of a place or object, or the ruin of a person's life or reputation. It can also be used metaphorically to express a sense of hopelessness or despair.
