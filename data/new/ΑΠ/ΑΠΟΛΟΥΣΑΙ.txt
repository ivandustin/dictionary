Etymology: The word "απολουσαι" comes from the Greek verb "απολούω" (apolúo), which means "to wash off" or "to cleanse." It is derived from the prefix "απο-" (apo-), meaning "away from," and the verb "λούω" (lúo), meaning "to wash."

Grammar: "απολουσαι" is the present participle form of the verb "απολούω" (apolúo) in the feminine gender. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The feminine form indicates that the subject of the action is female.

Description: "απολουσαι" can be translated as "washing off" or "cleansing" in English. It refers to the ongoing action of washing or cleansing, specifically in the feminine form.
