Etymology: The word "απολλωνιαν" comes from the Greek word "Απόλλων" (Apollon), which is the name of the Greek god of music, poetry, and prophecy.

Grammar: "Απολλωνιαν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine.

Description: "Απολλωνιαν" refers to a female follower or devotee of the god Apollo. In ancient Greece, there were various religious festivals and rituals dedicated to Apollo, and women who participated in these events were often referred to as "απολλωνιαν". These women would engage in music, poetry, and dance as part of their worship of Apollo.
