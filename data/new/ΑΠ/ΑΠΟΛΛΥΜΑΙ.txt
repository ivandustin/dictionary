Etymology: The word "απολλυμαι" (apollymai) comes from the Greek verb "ἀπόλλυμι" (apollymi), which means "to destroy" or "to perish". It is derived from the prefix "ἀπό" (apo), meaning "away from", and the root "ὄλλυμι" (ollumi), meaning "to destroy".

Grammar: "απολλυμαι" is a first-person singular present middle/passive indicative form of the verb "ἀπόλλυμι" (apollymi). In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The present tense indicates that the action is happening in the present.

Description: "απολλυμαι" can be translated as "I am being destroyed" or "I am perishing". It expresses the idea of someone or something undergoing destruction or perishing.
