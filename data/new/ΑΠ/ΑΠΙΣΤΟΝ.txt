Etymology: The word "απιστον" comes from the Greek word "ἄπιστον" (apiston), which means "unbelievable" or "incredible". It is derived from the prefix "ἀ-" (a-), meaning "not", and the word "πιστός" (pistos), meaning "believable" or "trustworthy".

Grammar: "Απιστον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The neuter gender is used for objects, concepts, and abstract ideas. The singular form indicates that there is only one of the noun.

Description: "Απιστον" is a Greek word that can be translated as "unbelievable" or "incredible". It is used to describe something that is difficult to believe or accept as true. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing surprise, skepticism, or astonishment.
