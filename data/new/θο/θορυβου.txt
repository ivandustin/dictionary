Word: θορυβου

Etymology:
The word θορυβου comes from the Greek word θόρυβος (thorubos), which means noise, uproar, or disturbance. It is derived from the verb θορυβέω (thorubeo), which means to make noise or to disturb.

Grammar:
θορυβου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In Greek, the genitive case is often used to indicate the source or origin of something.

Description:
θορυβου refers to a noise or disturbance, often caused by a commotion or uproar. It can also refer to a state of confusion or chaos. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the tumultuous events of battle or the chaos of a storm.
