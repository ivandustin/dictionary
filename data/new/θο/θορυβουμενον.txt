Word: θορυβουμενον

Etymology:
The word θορυβουμενον is derived from the Greek verb θορυβέω (thorybeo), which means "to make noise" or "to disturb". The suffix -ούμενον (-oumenon) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word describes something that is currently happening.

Grammar:
θορυβουμενον is a present participle in the passive voice, meaning "being disturbed" or "being made noisy". It is in the neuter gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
The word θορυβουμενον describes something that is currently being disturbed or made noisy. It can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as a loud party, a construction site, or a busy street. The word has a negative connotation, implying that the noise is unwanted or disruptive.
