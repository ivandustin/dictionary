Etymology: The word "βαριησούς" (variesous) comes from the Greek word "βαρύς" (varis), meaning "heavy" or "burdensome."

Grammar: "Βαριησούς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Βαριησούς" refers to something or someone that is heavy or burdensome. It can be used to describe physical weight or metaphorical burdens.
