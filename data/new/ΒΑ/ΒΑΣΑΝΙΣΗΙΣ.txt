Etymology: The word "βασανισης" (basanisēs) comes from the Greek verb "βασανίζω" (basanizō), which means "to torment" or "to torture". 

Grammar: βασανισης is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: βασανισης refers to the act of tormenting or torturing someone. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of extreme suffering or distress.
