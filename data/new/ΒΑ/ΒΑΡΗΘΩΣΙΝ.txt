Etymology: The word "βαρηθώσιν" comes from the Greek verb "βαρηθώ" (varitho), which means "to be burdened" or "to be weighed down." It is derived from the adjective "βαρύς" (varus), meaning "heavy" or "burdensome."

Grammar: "Βαρηθώσιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "βαρηθώ" in the aorist tense. It is used to express an action that happened in the past and is completed.

Description: "Βαρηθώσιν" is a verb form that indicates an action of being burdened or weighed down. It can be used to describe a physical or metaphorical burden that someone or something has experienced in the past.
