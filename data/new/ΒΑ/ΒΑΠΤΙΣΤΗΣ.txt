Etymology: The word "βαπτιστης" (baptistes) comes from the Greek verb "βαπτίζω" (baptizo), which means "to dip" or "to immerse." It is derived from the noun "βάπτισμα" (baptisma), meaning "baptism."

Grammar: In Greek, "βαπτιστης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "βαπτισται" (baptistai).

Description: "Βαπτιστης" is the Greek word for "baptist." It refers to a person who baptizes others, particularly in the Christian religious context. In Christianity, John the Baptist is a prominent figure known for baptizing Jesus Christ. The term "baptist" is also used to describe various Christian denominations that emphasize the practice of baptism.
