Etymology: The word "βατούς" (batous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "βάτος" (batos), which means "bramble" or "thorny bush."

Grammar: In Greek, "βατούς" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular number.

Description: "Βατούς" refers to a type of thorny bush or bramble. It is often used metaphorically to describe something that is prickly or difficult to deal with.
