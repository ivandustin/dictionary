Etymology: The word "βασιλεύσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "βασιλεύω" (basileúo), which means "to reign" or "to rule." It is derived from the noun "βασιλεύς" (basileús), meaning "king" or "ruler."

Grammar: "Βασιλεύσουσιν" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb "βασιλεύω" (basileúo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The "-σουσιν" ending indicates the future tense and the third person plural.

Description: "Βασιλεύσουσιν" is a Greek word that means "they will reign" or "they will rule." It refers to a future action of multiple individuals ruling or reigning over something.
