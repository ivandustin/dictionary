Etymology: The word "βαστασασι" comes from the Greek verb "βαστάζω" (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The suffix "-σασι" is added to the verb stem to form the noun "βαστασασι", which means "carrying" or "bearing".

Grammar: In Greek, "βαστασασι" is a noun in the accusative case. It is used to indicate the action of carrying or bearing something.

Description: "βαστασασι" refers to the act of carrying or bearing something. It can be used to describe the physical action of carrying an object, as well as the metaphorical act of carrying a burden or responsibility.
