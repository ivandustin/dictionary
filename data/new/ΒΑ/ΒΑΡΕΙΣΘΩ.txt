Etymology: The word "βαρεισθω" (bareisthō) comes from the Greek verb "βαρείζω" (bareizō), which means "to burden" or "to weigh down." It is derived from the adjective "βαρύς" (barus), meaning "heavy" or "burdensome."

Grammar: "Βαρεισθω" is a verb in the imperative mood, present tense, and passive voice. It is the third person singular form of the verb "βαρείζω." The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The present tense indicates an action happening in the current moment. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Βαρεισθω" is a command or request for someone to be burdened or weighed down. It can be used metaphorically to express the idea of someone taking on a heavy responsibility or being overwhelmed by a task.
