Grammar: In Greek, "βαραββαν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is declined as follows:
- Singular: βαραββαν
- Plural: βαραββανς

Description: "βαραββαν" is a name that appears in the New Testament of the Bible. It refers to a prisoner who was released by Pontius Pilate instead of Jesus Christ, according to the Gospel accounts. The name has become synonymous with someone who is pardoned or released from punishment.
