Etymology: The word "βασιλεύς" (basileus) comes from the ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂wes- meaning "to dwell" or "to reside". In ancient Greece, the term referred to a king or ruler.

Grammar: In Greek, "βασιλεύς" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The word can also be declined in other cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Βασιλεύς" is the Greek word for "king" or "ruler". It is used to refer to a male monarch or a person who holds supreme authority and power in a kingdom or empire. In ancient Greece, the role of a king was significant, and they were often seen as the highest authority in the political and social structure.
