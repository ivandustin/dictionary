Etymology: The word "βαπτιστην" comes from the Greek verb "βαπτίζω" (baptizo), which means "to immerse" or "to dip". It is derived from the noun "βάπτισμα" (baptisma), meaning "baptism".

Grammar: "Βαπτιστην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Βαπτιστην" refers to a person who baptizes or performs baptisms. It is commonly used to describe John the Baptist, who is known as "Ιωάννης ο Βαπτιστής" (Ioannis o Vaptistis) in Greek.
