Etymology: The word "βαλαάμ" (pronounced bal-a-am) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the Old Testament, specifically from the story of Balaam, a diviner and prophet mentioned in the Book of Numbers.

Grammar: "Βαλαάμ" is a noun in Greek. It is a proper noun, referring to the biblical character Balaam.

Description: Balaam was a non-Israelite prophet who was hired by the Moabite king Balak to curse the Israelites. However, each time Balaam tried to curse them, blessings came out of his mouth instead. The story of Balaam is found in Numbers 22-24 in the Old Testament.

Please note that the provided information is specific to the Greek word "βαλαάμ" and its usage in the context of the biblical character.
