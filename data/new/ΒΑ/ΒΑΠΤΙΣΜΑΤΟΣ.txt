Grammar: "Βαπτισματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Βαπτισματος" refers to the act or process of baptizing. In Christian religious context, it specifically refers to the sacrament of baptism, which is the ritual of initiation and admission into the Christian Church.
