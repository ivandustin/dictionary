Etymology: The word "βασιλεία" (basileia) comes from the Greek word "βασιλεύς" (basileus), meaning "king" or "ruler". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃rḗǵs, which also gave rise to words like "rex" in Latin and "king" in English.

Grammar: "Βασιλεία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "βασιλείες" (basileies).

Description: "Βασιλεία" means "kingdom" or "realm" in Greek. It refers to a territory or domain ruled by a king or queen. In a broader sense, it can also refer to a monarchy or a form of government where a single ruler holds supreme power. Additionally, "βασιλεία" can be used metaphorically to describe a domain or sphere of influence in various contexts, such as the "kingdom of knowledge" or the "kingdom of heaven".
