Etymology: The word "βασανισμός" (basanismos) comes from the Greek verb "βασανίζω" (basanizo), which means "to torment" or "to torture". It is derived from the noun "βάσανος" (basanos), meaning "touchstone" or "test". In ancient times, a touchstone was used to test the purity of gold or silver by rubbing it against the metal. The verb "βασανίζω" later took on the metaphorical meaning of subjecting someone to a test or torment.

Grammar: "Βασανισμός" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "βασανισμοί" (basanismoi).

Description: "Βασανισμός" refers to the act or process of tormenting or torturing someone. It can also be used metaphorically to describe any form of severe or prolonged suffering, distress, or hardship.
