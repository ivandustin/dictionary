Etymology: The word "βασιλισσης" (basilissēs) comes from the Greek word "βασιλεύς" (basileús), meaning "king" or "ruler," with the suffix "-ισσης" (-issēs) denoting the feminine form.

Grammar: "Βασιλισσης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the feminine gender and singular number.

Description: "Βασιλισσης" means "queen" in Greek. It refers to a female monarch who holds the highest position of power and authority in a monarchy.
