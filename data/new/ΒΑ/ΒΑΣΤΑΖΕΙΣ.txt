Etymology: The word "βασταζεις" (vastazeis) comes from the Greek verb "βαστάζω" (vastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear". 

Grammar: "Βασταζεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "βαστάζω" (vastazo). It is used when addressing someone directly and means "you carry" or "you bear".

Description: "Βασταζεις" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of carrying or bearing something. It can be used in various contexts, both literally and metaphorically, to indicate physical or emotional carrying or bearing of a burden or responsibility.
