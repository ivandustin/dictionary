Etymology: The word "βασταζοντος" (bastazontos) comes from the Greek verb "βαστάζω" (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear."

Grammar: "Βασταζοντος" is the genitive form of the noun "βαστάζων" (bastazon), which is the present active participle of the verb "βαστάζω" (bastazo). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Βασταζοντος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "of the one carrying" or "of the one bearing." It is used to describe someone or something that is in the act of carrying or bearing something.
