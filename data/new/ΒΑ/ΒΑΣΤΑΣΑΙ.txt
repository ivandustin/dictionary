Etymology: The word "βαστασαί" comes from the Greek verb "βαστάζω" (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear". 

Grammar: "Βαστασαί" is the 2nd person singular imperative form of the verb "βαστάζω" (bastazo). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to carry or bear something.

Description: "Βαστασαί" is a Greek word that specifically addresses one person and instructs them to carry or bear something. It is a direct command, often used in situations where someone needs to physically lift or transport an object.
