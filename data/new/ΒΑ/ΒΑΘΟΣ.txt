Etymology: The word "βαθος" (bathos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "βαθύς" (bathys), meaning "deep" or "profound".

Grammar: In Greek, "βαθος" is a noun of neuter gender. It is in the singular form.

Description: "βαθος" refers to depth or deepness. It can be used to describe physical depth, such as the depth of water or a hole, as well as metaphorical depth, such as the depth of emotions or thoughts.
