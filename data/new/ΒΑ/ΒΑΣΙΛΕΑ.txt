Etymology: The word "βασιλεία" (basileia) comes from the Greek word "βασιλεύς" (basileus), meaning "king" or "ruler". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃rḗǵs, which also gave rise to words like "rex" in Latin and "king" in English.

Grammar: In Greek, "βασιλεία" is a feminine noun. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The word can also be declined to match different cases, numbers, and genders.

Description: "Βασιλεία" is commonly translated as "kingdom" or "realm" in English. It refers to a territory or domain ruled by a king or queen. In a broader sense, it can also represent the concept of sovereignty, power, or authority. The term is often used in historical, political, and religious contexts.
