Etymology: The word "βασιλείοις" comes from the Greek word "βασιλεία" (basileía), which means "kingdom" or "royal power". It is derived from the word "βασιλεύς" (basileús), meaning "king".

Grammar: "Βασιλείοις" is the dative plural form of the noun "βασίλειος" (basíleios), which means "kingly" or "royal". In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Βασιλείοις" is used to describe or refer to multiple things that are related to kings or royal power. It can be used to describe a group of kings, a kingdom, or anything associated with royalty.
