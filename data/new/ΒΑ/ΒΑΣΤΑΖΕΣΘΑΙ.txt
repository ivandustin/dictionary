Etymology: The word "βασταζεσθαι" (bastazesthai) comes from the Greek verb "βαστάζω" (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear". 

Grammar: "Βασταζεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "βαστάζω" (bastazo). In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description: "Βασταζεσθαι" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of carrying or bearing something. It can be used in a literal sense, referring to physically carrying or bearing an object, or in a figurative sense, referring to enduring or tolerating something.
