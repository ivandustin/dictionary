Etymology: The word "βασιλικήν" (basilikēn) comes from the Greek word "βασιλικός" (basilikos), which means "royal" or "kingly". It is derived from the word "βασιλεύς" (basileus), meaning "king".

Grammar: "Βασιλικήν" is the accusative form of the feminine adjective "βασιλικός" (basilikos). In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Βασιλικήν" is an adjective in Greek that means "royal" or "kingly". It is used to describe something or someone that is related to or characteristic of a king or queen.
