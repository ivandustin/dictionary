Etymology: The word "βασιλεία" (basileía) comes from the Greek word "βασιλεύς" (basileús), meaning "king" or "ruler". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃rḗǵs, which also gave rise to words like "rex" in Latin and "reich" in German.

Grammar: In Greek, "βασιλεία" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "βασιλείες" (basileíes).

Description: "Βασιλεία" is a Greek word that translates to "kingdom" or "realm" in English. It refers to a territory or domain ruled by a king or queen. In a broader sense, it can also represent sovereignty, power, or authority. The term is often used in historical and religious contexts, such as referring to the Kingdom of Greece or the Kingdom of Heaven in Christianity.
