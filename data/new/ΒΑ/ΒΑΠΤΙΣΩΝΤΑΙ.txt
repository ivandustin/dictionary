Grammar: "Βαπτίσωνται" is the third person plural, present tense, middle/passive voice form of the verb "βαπτίζω" (baptízo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject, tense, voice, and mood. The "-ωνται" ending indicates that the subject is plural and the action is being done to them or by them.

Description: "Βαπτίσωνται" is a Greek verb form that means "they are being baptized" or "they are baptizing themselves." It refers to a group of people undergoing the act of baptism or performing the act of baptism on themselves.
