Etymology: The word "βασανισταίς" (basanistais) comes from the Greek verb "βασανίζω" (basanizo), which means "to torment" or "to torture". The suffix "-ταις" (-tais) indicates the plural form.

Grammar: "Βασανισταίς" (basanistais) is a noun in the masculine gender and plural number. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Βασανισταίς" (basanistais) refers to individuals who inflict torment or torture on others. It can be used to describe people who engage in physical or psychological abuse.
