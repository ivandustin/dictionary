Etymology: The word "βασανού" (basanou) comes from the Greek verb "βάσανος" (basanos), which means "torture" or "torment". 

Grammar: "Βασανού" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Βασανού" refers to the act of tormenting or torturing someone. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or experience that is extremely difficult or painful.
