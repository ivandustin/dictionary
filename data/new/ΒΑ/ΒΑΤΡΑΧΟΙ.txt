Etymology: The word "βατραχοι" (batrachoi) comes from the Greek word "βάτραχος" (batrachos), which means "frog". 

Grammar: "Βατραχοι" is the plural form of the noun "βάτραχος" (batrachos). It is used to refer to multiple frogs. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Βατραχοι" is a word in the Greek language that specifically refers to more than one frog. Frogs are amphibians that typically have long hind legs, webbed feet, and smooth skin. They are known for their ability to jump and their unique life cycle, which involves metamorphosis from tadpoles to adult frogs.
