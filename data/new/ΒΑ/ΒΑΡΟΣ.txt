Etymology: The word "βαρος" (baros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷerə-, meaning "heavy" or "weight". 

Grammar: In Greek, "βαρος" is a noun of neuter gender. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Βαρος" is a Greek word that translates to "weight" in English. It refers to the measure of the heaviness of an object or a person. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is burdensome or difficult to bear.
