Etymology: The word "οδοντας" (odontas) comes from the Greek word "οδούς" (odous), which means "tooth". 

Grammar: "Οδοντας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. 

Description: "Οδοντας" refers to a tooth, which is a hard, calcified structure found in the jaws of many vertebrates. Teeth are used for biting and chewing food, as well as for other functions such as speech and aesthetic purposes.
