Etymology: The word "οδυνάσαι" comes from the Greek verb "οδυνάομαι" (odunáomai), which means "to suffer" or "to be in pain". It is derived from the noun "οδύνη" (odýni), meaning "pain" or "sorrow".

Grammar: "Οδυνάσαι" is the second person singular form of the verb "οδυνάομαι" in the present tense. It is used when addressing one person and means "you suffer" or "you are in pain".

Description: "Οδυνάσαι" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of suffering or being in pain. It is commonly used to describe physical or emotional pain.
