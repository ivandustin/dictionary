Etymology: The word "οδυνη" (odynē) comes from the Greek verb "οδυναω" (odynáō), which means "to cause pain" or "to grieve."

Grammar: In Greek, "οδυνη" (odynē) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has a genitive form of "οδυνης" (odynēs).

Description: "Οδυνη" (odynē) refers to physical or emotional pain, suffering, or distress. It can be used to describe both physical and emotional discomfort or anguish.
