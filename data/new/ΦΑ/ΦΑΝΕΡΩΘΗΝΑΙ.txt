Etymology: The word "φανερωθείναι" comes from the Greek verb "φανερόω" (faneróo), which means "to make visible" or "to reveal." The suffix "-θη-" indicates the passive voice, and the ending "-ναι" indicates the infinitive form.

Grammar: "Φανερωθείναι" is the infinitive form of the verb and is used to express the action of making something visible or revealing something. It belongs to the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The passive voice suggests that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Φανερωθείναι" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of bringing something into the open, making it visible, or revealing it. It is often used in contexts where something was previously hidden or unknown and is now being made known or evident.
