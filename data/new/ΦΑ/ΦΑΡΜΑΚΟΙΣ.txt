Etymology: The word "φαρμακοις" (pharmakois) comes from the Greek word "φάρμακον" (pharmakon), which means "drug" or "medicine."

Grammar: "Φαρμακοις" is a noun in the dative plural form in Greek. It is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Φαρμακοις" refers to drugs or medicines in Greek. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "poisons" or "potions."
