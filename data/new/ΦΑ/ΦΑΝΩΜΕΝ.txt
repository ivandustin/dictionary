Etymology: The word "φανωμέν" (pronounced "fanomen") comes from the Greek verb "φαίνομαι" (pronounced "fenome"), which means "to appear" or "to be seen". 

Grammar: "Φανωμέν" is the present participle form of the verb "φαίνομαι" in the masculine singular nominative case. It is used to describe something or someone that is currently appearing or being seen.

Description: "Φανωμέν" can be translated as "the one who is appearing" or "the one who is being seen". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a person or thing that is visible or present in a particular moment.
