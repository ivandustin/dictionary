Etymology: The word "φαρμακεία" comes from the Greek word "φάρμακο" (pharmako), meaning "drug" or "medicine". It is derived from the verb "φαρμακεύω" (pharmakeuo), which means "to practice pharmacy" or "to administer drugs".

Grammar: "Φαρμακεία" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "φαρμακείες" (pharmakeies).

Description: "Φαρμακεία" refers to a pharmacy or a drugstore. It is a place where medications, drugs, and other healthcare products are dispensed and sold. Pharmacies also provide services such as medication counseling, prescription filling, and health advice.
