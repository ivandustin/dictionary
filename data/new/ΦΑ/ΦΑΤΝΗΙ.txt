Grammar: "Φατνηι" (phatnēi) is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Φατνηι" (phatnēi) refers to a manger or feeding trough, typically used for animals to eat from. In the biblical context, it is famously associated with the birth of Jesus Christ, as he was laid in a manger after his birth in Bethlehem. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a place of humble or simple origins.
