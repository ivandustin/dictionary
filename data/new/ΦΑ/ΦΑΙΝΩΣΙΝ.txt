Etymology: The word "φαινωσιν" (pronounced "phainōsin") comes from the Greek verb "φαίνω" (phainō), which means "to show" or "to appear." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰeh₂- ("to shine, appear").

Grammar: "Φαινωσιν" is the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb "φαίνω" (phainō) in Greek. It indicates that the subject is performing the action of showing or appearing in the present tense.

Description: "Φαινωσιν" is a Greek word that means "they show" or "they appear." It is used to describe the action of multiple subjects showing or appearing in the present tense.
