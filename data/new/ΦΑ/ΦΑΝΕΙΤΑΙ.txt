Etymology: The word "φανείται" comes from the Greek verb "φαίνομαι" (phainomai), which means "to appear" or "to be seen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰeh₂- ("to shine, appear").

Grammar: "Φανείται" is the third person singular form of the verb "φαίνομαι" in the present tense. It is a passive verb, indicating that the subject is being acted upon or experiencing the action.

Description: "Φανείται" is used to express the idea of something appearing or being seen. It can be translated as "it seems", "it appears", or "it is evident". It is often used to express an opinion or a subjective judgment based on observation or evidence.
