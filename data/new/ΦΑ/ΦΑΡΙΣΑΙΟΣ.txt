Etymology: The word "φαρισαῖος" (pharisaios) comes from the Greek word "φαρισαῖος" (pharisaios), which is derived from the Aramaic word "פַּרְשַׁיָּא" (parshaya), meaning "separated" or "set apart."

Grammar: In Greek, "φαρισαῖος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "φαρισαῖοι" (pharisaioi).

Description: The term "φαρισαῖος" refers to a member of a Jewish religious sect known as the Pharisees. The Pharisees were known for their strict adherence to the Jewish law and traditions. They were often seen as self-righteous and hypocritical by Jesus in the New Testament.
