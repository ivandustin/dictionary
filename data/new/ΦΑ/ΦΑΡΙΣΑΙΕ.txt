Etymology: The word "φαρισαῖος" (pharisaios) comes from the Greek word "Φαρισαῖος" (pharisaios), which is derived from the Aramaic word "פַּרְשַׁיָּא" (parshaya), meaning "separated" or "set apart."

Grammar: In Greek, "φαρισαῖος" is a noun in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It belongs to the second declension.

Description: "Φαρισαῖος" is a term used in the New Testament to refer to a Jewish religious sect known for their strict adherence to the Mosaic law and traditions. They were often seen as self-righteous and hypocritical.
