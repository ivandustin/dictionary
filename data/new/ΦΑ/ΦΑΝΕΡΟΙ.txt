Etymology: The word "φανεροί" (phaneroi) comes from the Greek word "φανερός" (phaneros), which means "visible" or "apparent". 

Grammar: "Φανεροί" is the plural form of the adjective "φανερός" (phaneros), which is used to describe multiple things or people that are visible or apparent. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Φανεροί" is an adjective in Greek that is used to describe things or people that are visible, apparent, or obvious. It can be used to describe physical objects that can be seen with the eyes, as well as abstract concepts or qualities that are easily perceived or understood.
