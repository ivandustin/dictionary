Etymology: The word "φανερούς" (pronounced "fah-neh-roos") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "φάνερος" (pronounced "fah-neh-ros"), which means "visible" or "apparent."

Grammar: "Φανερούς" is the masculine form of the adjective in the nominative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The masculine form is used when the noun being described is masculine.

Description: "Φανερούς" is an adjective that means "visible" or "apparent" in Greek. It is used to describe something that can be seen or easily perceived.
