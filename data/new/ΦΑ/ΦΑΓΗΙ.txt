Etymology: The word "φαγηί" (phagēí) comes from the Greek verb "φαγεῖν" (phageîn), which means "to eat."

Grammar: "Φαγηί" (phagēí) is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the first declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Φαγηί" (phagēí) refers to a type of traditional Greek dish made with lentils. It is a popular vegetarian dish in Greek cuisine and is often served as a main course. The dish typically includes lentils, onions, garlic, and various herbs and spices. It is often enjoyed with bread or served over rice.
