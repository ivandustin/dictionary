Etymology: The word "φαρμακοι" (pharmakoi) comes from the Greek word "φάρμακον" (pharmakon), which means "drug" or "medicine."

Grammar: "Φαρμακοι" is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is masculine in gender.

Description: "Φαρμακοι" refers to a group of people who were used in ancient Greek rituals as scapegoats or sacrificial victims. They were chosen to bear the sins or misfortunes of the community and were often expelled or killed as a means of purification or atonement. The practice of using pharmakoi was believed to bring about healing or restoration to the community.
