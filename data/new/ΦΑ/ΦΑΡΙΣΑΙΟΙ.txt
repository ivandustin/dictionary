Etymology: The word "φαρισαῖοι" (pharisaioi) comes from the Greek word "Φαρισαῖος" (pharisaos), which is derived from the Aramaic word "פְּרִישַׁיָּא" (pərīšayyā), meaning "separated" or "set apart."

Grammar: "Φαρισαῖοι" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: The term "Φαρισαῖοι" refers to a Jewish religious sect that existed during the time of Jesus and the New Testament. The Pharisees were known for their strict adherence to the Jewish law and traditions. They were often seen as religious leaders and teachers, but were also criticized for their hypocrisy and legalism.
