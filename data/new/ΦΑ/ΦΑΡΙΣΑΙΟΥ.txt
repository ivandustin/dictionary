Etymology: The word "φαρισαίου" (pharisaíou) comes from the Greek word "Φαρισαῖος" (pharisaîos), which means "Pharisee." The term originated from the Hebrew word "פְּרוּשִׁים" (perushim), which refers to a Jewish sect during the Second Temple period.

Grammar: "Φαρισαίου" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "Φαρισαίου" refers to a Pharisee, a member of a Jewish sect that emerged during the Second Temple period. The Pharisees were known for their strict adherence to religious laws and traditions. They played a significant role in Jewish society and were often in conflict with other religious groups, such as the Sadducees and Jesus Christ.
