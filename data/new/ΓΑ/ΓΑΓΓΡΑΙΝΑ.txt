Etymology: The word "γαγγραίνα" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "γαγγραίνω" (gaggraino), which means "to cackle" or "to squawk."

Grammar: In Greek, "γαγγραίνα" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a plural form "γαγγραίνες" (gaggraines).

Description: "Γαγγραίνα" refers to a hen or a female chicken. It is commonly used in Greek to describe the domesticated bird that is raised for its eggs and meat.
