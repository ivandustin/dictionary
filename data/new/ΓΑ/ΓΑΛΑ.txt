Grammar: In Greek, "γαλα" (gala) is a noun. It is a neuter noun and its singular form is "το γάλα" (to gala). The plural form is "τα γάλατα" (ta galata).

Description: "Γαλα" (gala) refers to the white liquid produced by female mammals to nourish their young. In Greek, it is commonly used to refer to cow's milk. It is a staple food and is consumed in various forms, such as drinking it plain, using it in cooking and baking, or making dairy products like cheese and yogurt.
