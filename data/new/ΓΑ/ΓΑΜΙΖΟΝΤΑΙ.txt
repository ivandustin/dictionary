Etymology: The word "γαμιζονται" (gamizontai) comes from the Greek verb "γαμίζω" (gamizo), which means "to marry." The suffix "-νται" (-ntai) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the action is performed by the subject on themselves. 

Grammar: "Γαμιζονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "γαμίζω" (gamizo). It is used to describe the action of multiple people marrying each other.

Description: "Γαμιζονται" is a Greek verb that specifically refers to the act of multiple people getting married to each other. It implies a mutual agreement and consent among the individuals involved.
