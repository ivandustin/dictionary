Etymology: The word "οντες" (ontes) comes from the Greek verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be." It is the present participle form of the verb, meaning "being" or "existing."

Grammar: "Οντες" is the nominative plural form of the word, used to refer to multiple beings or entities. In Greek grammar, it is classified as a participle, which is a verb form that functions as an adjective or noun.

Description: "Οντες" is used to describe or refer to beings or entities that exist or are present. It can be used in various contexts, such as philosophy, theology, or general discussions about existence.
