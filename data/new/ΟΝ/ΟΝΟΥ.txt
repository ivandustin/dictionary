Etymology: The word "ονού" (onou) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ὄνος" (ónos), which means "donkey".

Grammar: "Ονού" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender.

Description: "Ονού" refers to a donkey in Greek. Donkeys are domesticated animals that are known for their long ears, short mane, and braying sound. They are often used for transportation or as working animals in various parts of the world.
