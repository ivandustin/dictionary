Word: βδελυσσομενος

Etymology: βδελυσσομενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb βδελύσσομαι (bdelyssomai), which means "to feel disgust or loathing."

Grammar: βδελυσσομενος is a present participle in the passive voice, which means "being disgusted" or "being loathed." It is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the present stem of the verb βδελύσσομαι (bdelyssomai).

Description: βδελυσσομενος is an adjective that describes someone or something that is being disgusted or loathed. It can be used to describe a person's feelings or reactions towards something that they find repulsive or offensive. For example, one might say "Ο άνθρωπος ήταν βδελυσσόμενος από την άσχημη μυρωδιά" (The man was disgusted by the bad smell).
