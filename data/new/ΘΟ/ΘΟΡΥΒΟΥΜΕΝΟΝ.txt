Etymology: The word "θορυβούμενον" comes from the Greek verb "θορυβέω" (thorýveo), which means "to make noise" or "to disturb". The suffix "-μενον" (-menon) is a present participle ending, indicating continuous action.

Grammar: "Θορυβούμενον" is the present participle form of the verb "θορυβέω" (thorýveo) in Greek. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: "Θορυβούμενον" describes something or someone that is currently making noise or causing disturbance. It can be translated as "noisy" or "disturbing" in English.
