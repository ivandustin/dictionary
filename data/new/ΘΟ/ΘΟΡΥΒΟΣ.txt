Etymology: The word "θορυβος" (thorubos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "θορυβέω" (thorubeo), which means "to make noise" or "to disturb". 

Grammar: "Θορυβος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "θορυβος". In the plural, it becomes "θορυβοι" (thoruboi). 

Description: "Θορυβος" refers to noise, disturbance, or commotion. It can be used to describe loud sounds, chaos, or any situation that disrupts peace and quiet.
