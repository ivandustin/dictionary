Word: κηρυγματι

Etymology:
The word κηρυγματι (kērygmati) is derived from the Greek word κήρυγμα (kērygma), which means "proclamation" or "preaching". It is derived from the verb κηρύσσω (kēryssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce".

Grammar:
κηρυγματι is a noun in the dative case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb, the object of certain prepositions, and to show possession.

Description:
κηρυγματι refers to the act of preaching or proclaiming a message. It can also refer to the content of the message being preached. In Christian theology, κηρυγματι is often used to refer to the preaching of the gospel or the message of salvation.
