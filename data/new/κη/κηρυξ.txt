Word: κηρυξ

Etymology:
The word κηρυξ (kerux) comes from the Greek verb κηρύσσω (kerusso), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". In ancient Greece, a kerux was a herald or a public crier who made official announcements and proclamations.

Grammar:
In Greek, κηρυξ is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is κηρυξ, and its genitive singular form is κηρύκος (kerukos). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
In ancient Greece, a kerux was an important figure who played a crucial role in public life. They were responsible for making official announcements and proclamations, and were often employed by the government or by wealthy individuals. The role of the kerux was also important in religious ceremonies, where they would announce the beginning and end of rituals. Today, the word κηρυξ is still used in Greek to refer to a preacher or a herald.
