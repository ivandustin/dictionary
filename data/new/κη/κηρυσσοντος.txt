The word "κηρυσσοντος" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "κηρύσσω" (kerusso), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". The participle form indicates that the action of proclaiming or announcing is ongoing or continuous. 

In terms of grammar, "κηρυσσοντος" is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or association. It can be translated as "of the one proclaiming" or "belonging to the one who announces".

Overall, "κηρυσσοντος" is a word that describes someone who is actively proclaiming or announcing something, and it emphasizes the ongoing nature of that action.
