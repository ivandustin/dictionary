Word: κηρυσσω

Etymology:
The word κηρυσσω (kerusso) comes from the Greek verb κηρυσσειν (kerussein), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". It is derived from the noun κηρυξ (kerux), which means "herald" or "messenger".

Grammar:
κηρυσσω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: κηρυσσω (kerusso) - I proclaim
- Plural: κηρυσσομεν (kerussomen) - we proclaim
- Singular: κηρυσσεις (kerusseis) - you proclaim
- Plural: κηρυσσετε (kerussete) - you all proclaim
- Singular: κηρυσσει (kerussei) - he/she/it proclaims
- Plural: κηρυσσουσι(ν) (kerussousin) - they proclaim

Description:
κηρυσσω is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of proclaiming or preaching the gospel. It is used to describe the work of John the Baptist, Jesus, and the apostles. The word implies a sense of urgency and importance in the message being proclaimed. It is also used in a broader sense to describe any kind of public announcement or proclamation.
