Word: κηρυγμα

Etymology:
The word κηρυγμα (kērygma) comes from the Greek verb κηρύσσω (kēryssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". It is derived from the noun κῆρυξ (kēryx), which means "herald" or "messenger".

Grammar:
In Greek, κηρυγμα is a neuter noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is κηρυγμα, and its genitive singular form is κηρυγματος. The word can be used in both the singular and plural forms.

Description:
κηρυγμα refers to a proclamation or announcement, often of a religious or spiritual nature. It can also refer to a sermon or a message delivered by a preacher or religious leader. In Christian theology, κηρυγμα is often used to describe the preaching of the gospel or the message of salvation.
