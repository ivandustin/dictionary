Word: κηρυσσων

Etymology:
The word κηρυσσων (kēryssōn) is a present participle form of the Greek verb κηρυσσω (kēryssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". The word is derived from the noun κηρυξ (kēryx), which means "herald" or "messenger".

Grammar:
κηρυσσων is a present participle form of the verb κηρυσσω. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The present participle form indicates that the action of proclaiming or announcing is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
κηρυσσων can be translated as "proclaiming" or "announcing" in English. It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of John the Baptist, who was a herald of the coming of Jesus Christ. The word can also be used more generally to describe anyone who is proclaiming or announcing a message or news.
