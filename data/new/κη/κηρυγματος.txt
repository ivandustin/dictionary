The word "κηρυγματος" (kerugmatos) is a Greek noun that means "preaching" or "proclamation." It is derived from the verb "κηρυσσω" (kerusso), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce." In Greek grammar, "κηρυγματος" is a genitive singular noun, indicating possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun. 

In Christian theology, "κηρυγματος" is often used to refer to the act of preaching the gospel or the message of salvation. It is also used in a broader sense to refer to any public proclamation or announcement. 

Overall, "κηρυγματος" is a word that emphasizes the importance of clear and bold communication, particularly in the context of sharing important messages with others.
