Word: κηρυχθεις

Etymology:
The word κηρυχθεις is derived from the Greek verb κηρύσσω (kērýssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". The suffix -θεις (-theis) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and passive voice.

Grammar:
κηρυχθεις is a past participle in the passive voice, indicating that the subject of the verb (the one being proclaimed or announced) is the receiver of the action. In Greek, past participles are often used as adjectives, and can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description:
κηρυχθεις can be translated as "having been proclaimed" or "having been announced". It is often used in a religious or political context, to describe someone who has been publicly declared or announced as a leader, messenger, or representative. The word carries a sense of authority and importance, as if the person has been chosen or appointed for a specific purpose.
