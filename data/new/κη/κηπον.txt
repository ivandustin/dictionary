The word "κηπον" (kēpon) is a Greek noun that means "garden" or "orchard". It is derived from the Greek word "κῆπος" (kēpos), which also means "garden". In terms of grammar, "κηπον" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

In ancient Greek literature, gardens were often associated with beauty, pleasure, and tranquility. They were also seen as a symbol of wealth and status, as only the wealthy could afford to maintain them. Gardens were typically filled with fruit trees, flowers, and other plants, and were often used as a place for relaxation and contemplation. 

Overall, "κηπον" is a simple yet important word in Greek vocabulary, representing the concept of a garden or orchard and all the cultural and symbolic associations that come with it.
