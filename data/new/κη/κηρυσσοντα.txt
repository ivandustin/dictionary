Word: κηρυσσοντα

Etymology: κηρυσσοντα is the present participle form of the Greek verb κηρύσσω (kerusso), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". The word has its roots in the ancient Greek language and is derived from the word κῆρυξ (kerux), which means "herald" or "messenger".

Grammar: κηρυσσοντα is a present participle form of the verb κηρύσσω (kerusso). In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verb form that describes an ongoing action or state. It is formed by adding the suffix -οντα (-onta) to the stem of the verb. The word κηρυσσοντα is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: κηρυσσοντα can be translated as "proclaiming" or "announcing". It describes someone who is currently engaged in the act of proclaiming or announcing something. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in religious or political settings, where someone is proclaiming a message or making an announcement.
