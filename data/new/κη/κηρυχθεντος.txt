Word: κηρυχθεντος

Etymology:
The word κηρυχθεντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb κηρύσσω (kērússō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce". The word is in the passive participle form, which means "having been proclaimed" or "having been announced".

Grammar:
κηρυχθεντος is a masculine singular form of the passive participle in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. In this case, the word κηρυχθεντος indicates possession or origin of the action of proclaiming or announcing.

Description:
The word κηρυχθεντος is used to describe something that has been proclaimed or announced. It can be used in various contexts, such as in religious or political settings, to refer to a message or announcement that has been made. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of proclamation or announcement.
