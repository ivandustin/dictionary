The word "αθετω" (athetó) is a Greek verb that means "to reject" or "to invalidate." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek prefix "a-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the verb "τιθέναι" (tithenai), which means "to place" or "to put." 

Grammar: "αθετω" is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the verb. 

Description: The word is often used in legal contexts to refer to the rejection or invalidation of a legal document or decision. It can also be used more generally to mean the rejection or dismissal of something as untrue or invalid.
