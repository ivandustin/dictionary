Word: αθλησιν

Etymology: The word αθλησιν (athlesin) is derived from the Greek word αθλητής (athletis), which means athlete. The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a common ending used to form nouns from verbs in ancient Greek.

Grammar: αθλησιν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is a singular noun, meaning it refers to one thing.

Description: αθλησιν refers to the act of competing in athletic games or contests. It can also refer to the prizes or rewards given to the winners of such contests. In ancient Greece, athletics were an important part of daily life and were often associated with religious festivals. The word αθλησιν is used in various ancient Greek texts, including the works of Homer and Pindar.
