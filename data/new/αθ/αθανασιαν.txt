Word: αθανασιαν

Etymology:
The word αθανασιαν (athanasian) is derived from the Greek word αθανασία (athanasia), which means immortality. The suffix -ιαν (-ian) is added to the end of the word to form a noun.

Grammar:
αθανασιαν is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender, and αθανασιαν is feminine. It is also in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
αθανασιαν refers to the state of being immortal or having eternal life. In Greek mythology, the gods and goddesses were often described as being immortal, while humans were mortal. The concept of immortality has also been explored in various religions and philosophical traditions. The word αθανασιαν is often used in a figurative sense to describe something that is timeless or enduring.
