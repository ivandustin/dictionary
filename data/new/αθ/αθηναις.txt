Word: αθηναις

Etymology: αθηναις is the plural form of the Greek word "Αθήνα" (Athina), which is the name of the ancient Greek goddess of wisdom, courage, inspiration, civilization, law and justice. The city of Athens was named after her.

Grammar: αθηναις is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form in Greek. It is declined as follows: αθήνα, αθήνας, αθηναί, αθηναίς.

Description: αθηναις refers to the city of Athens, the capital and largest city of Greece. It is one of the oldest cities in the world, with a history spanning over 3,400 years. Athens is known for its rich cultural heritage, including ancient monuments such as the Acropolis, the Parthenon, and the Temple of Olympian Zeus. It is also a modern city with a vibrant nightlife, museums, and art galleries. Athens is considered the birthplace of democracy, philosophy, and Western civilization.
