The word "αθετουσιν" is a Greek verb in the present active participle form. It comes from the root word "αθετέω" which means to reject, disapprove, or nullify. The present active participle form indicates ongoing action, so "αθετουσιν" means "they are rejecting" or "they are nullifying". 

In terms of grammar, "αθετουσιν" is a third-person plural verb in the present tense. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action. 

Overall, "αθετουσιν" is a word that describes an ongoing action of rejection or nullification.
