Word: αθηναιοι

Etymology:
The word αθηναιοι (athenians) is derived from the Greek word Αθήνα (Athina), which is the name of the Greek goddess of wisdom, courage, inspiration, civilization, law and justice, strategic warfare, mathematics, strength, strategy, the arts, crafts, and skill. The suffix "-οι" is a common Greek ending used to denote a group of people or inhabitants of a place.

Grammar:
αθηναιοι is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "Athenians" in English. It is declined according to the second declension of Greek nouns, with the nominative singular form being "Αθηναῖος" (Athinaeos) and the genitive singular form being "Αθηναίου" (Athinaeou).

Description:
αθηναιοι refers to the people who live in Athens, the capital city of Greece. Athens is known for its rich history, culture, and architecture, and is often referred to as the cradle of Western civilization. The Athenians played a significant role in the development of democracy, philosophy, literature, and the arts. Today, Athens is a bustling metropolis with a population of over 3 million people.
