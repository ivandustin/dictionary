Word: αθεσμων

Etymology:
The word αθεσμων (athesmon) is derived from the Greek word αθέσμιος (athesmios), which means "lawless" or "unlawful". It is composed of the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the word θέσμιος (thesmios), which means "legal" or "according to law".

Grammar:
αθεσμων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. The genitive singular form is αθεσμονος (athesmonos), the accusative singular form is αθεσμονα (athesmona), and the nominative plural form is αθεσμονες (athesmones).

Description:
αθεσμων is a Greek word that refers to someone who is lawless or who acts outside the bounds of the law. It can also be used to describe something that is illegal or against the law. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize or condemn someone's behavior.
