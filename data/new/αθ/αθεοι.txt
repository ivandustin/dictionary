Word: αθεοι

Etymology:
The word αθεοι (atheoi) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "without" or "not" and the Greek word "theos" meaning "god". Therefore, αθεοι means "without gods" or "godless".

Grammar:
αθεοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
αθεοι is a term used to describe people who do not believe in any gods or deities. It is often used to refer to atheists or those who reject the existence of a higher power. The term has been used throughout history to describe individuals or groups who have challenged or rejected traditional religious beliefs. In modern times, the term has become more widely used as atheism has become more accepted and visible in society.
