Word: αθυμωσιν

Etymology:
The word αθυμωσιν (athumosin) is derived from the Greek word αθυμία (athumia), which means "despondency" or "dejection". The suffix -ωσιν (-osin) is a verb ending that indicates the action of the word.

Grammar:
αθυμωσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb αθυμέω (athumeo), which means "to be despondent" or "to be dejected". The verb is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description:
αθυμωσιν describes the action of being despondent or dejected. It is often used to describe a state of mind or emotion, and can be translated as "they are despondent" or "they are dejected". The word is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to convey a sense of sadness or melancholy.
