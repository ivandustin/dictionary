Etymology: The word "ηιτησας" (pronounced "eetisas") comes from the Greek verb "ηιταω" (eetao), which means "to ask" or "to request." The suffix "-σας" (-sas) is added to the verb stem to form the past tense.

Grammar: "ηιτησας" is the past tense form of the verb "ηιταω" (eetao) in the second person singular. It is used to indicate that the action of asking or requesting has already been completed by the subject.

Description: "ηιτησας" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the act of asking or requesting in the past tense. It is used to describe an action that has already taken place.
