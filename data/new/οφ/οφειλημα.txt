Word: οφειλημα

Etymology:
The word οφειλημα (opheilēma) comes from the Greek verb οφείλω (opheilō), which means "to owe". The suffix -ημα (-ēma) is added to the verb to form a noun, indicating the action or result of the verb. Therefore, οφειλημα (opheilēma) means "something owed" or "debt".

Grammar:
Οφειλημα (opheilēma) is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The singular form of οφειλημα (opheilēma) is used to refer to a single debt or obligation.

Description:
Οφειλημα (opheilēma) refers to a debt or obligation that one person owes to another. It can be a financial debt, such as a loan or unpaid bill, or a moral obligation, such as a promise or duty. In Greek culture, the concept of οφειλημα (opheilēma) is important, as it emphasizes the importance of fulfilling one's obligations and responsibilities.
