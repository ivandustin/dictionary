The word οφειλομεν (opheilomen) is a present participle of the Greek verb οφείλω (opheilō), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". The word comes from the root word οφείλημα (opheilēma), which means "debt" or "obligation".

The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state, and in this case, it means "owing" or "being indebted".
