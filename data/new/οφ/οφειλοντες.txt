Word: οφειλοντες

Etymology:
The word οφειλοντες comes from the Greek verb οφείλω (opheílō), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently owing or indebted.

Grammar:
Οφειλοντες is the present participle form of the verb οφείλω (opheílō), which means "to owe". It is a masculine plural noun, indicating that it refers to a group of people who are currently owing or indebted.

Description:
Οφειλοντες is a Greek word that refers to people who are currently owing or indebted. It can be used in various contexts, such as in finance, law, or personal relationships. The word implies a sense of obligation or responsibility to repay a debt or fulfill a duty. In Greek culture, the concept of οφειλή (opheilé), or debt, is considered a serious matter and is often associated with honor and integrity.
