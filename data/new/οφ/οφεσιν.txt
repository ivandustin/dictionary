Word: οφεσιν

Etymology:
The word οφεσιν (ophesin) is derived from the Greek word οφις (ophis), which means "snake". It is a noun in the accusative case, and is used in the New Testament of the Bible.

In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their function in a sentence. οφεσιν is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of the verb in the sentence. It is also a singular noun.

Οφεσιν is a Greek word that means "snake" or "serpent". It is used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the book of Revelation, where it is used to describe the devil or Satan. The word is also used in other ancient Greek texts to refer to snakes or serpents.
