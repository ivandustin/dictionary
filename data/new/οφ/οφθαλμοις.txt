The word οφθαλμοις (ophthalmois) is derived from the Greek word "οφθαλμός" (ophthalmos), which means "eye". The suffix "-οις" (-ois) is a dative plural ending, indicating that the word is in the dative case and refers to multiple eyes.

In Greek, οφθαλμοις is a noun in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of a verb or a preposition. It is the plural form of οφθαλμός (ophthalmos), which is a masculine noun.
