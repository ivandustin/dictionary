Word: οφθαλμούς

Etymology: The word οφθαλμούς (ophthalmous) comes from the ancient Greek word ὀφθαλμός (ophthalmós), which means "eye".

Grammar: οφθαλμούς is a noun in the Greek language, and it is in the accusative case. It is the plural form of the word οφθαλμός (ophthalmós).

Description: οφθαλμούς refers to "eyes" in the Greek language. It is used to describe the organs of sight in humans and animals. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to the ability to see or perceive things.
