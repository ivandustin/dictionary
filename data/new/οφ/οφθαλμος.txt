Word: οφθαλμός

Etymology:
The word οφθαλμός (ophthalmos) comes from the Ancient Greek ὀφθαλμός (ophthalmós), which means "eye". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃ekʷ- ("to see").

Grammar:
In Greek, οφθαλμός is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is οφθαλμός, and its genitive singular form is οφθαλμού. The plural forms are οφθαλμοί (nominative) and οφθαλμών (genitive).

Description:
Οφθαλμός is a word that refers to the organ of sight in humans and animals. It is a vital part of the body that allows us to see the world around us. The word is often used metaphorically to refer to the ability to perceive or understand something, as in the phrase "the eye of the mind". In medical terminology, οφθαλμός is used to refer to various conditions and diseases of the eye, such as cataracts, glaucoma, and macular degeneration.
