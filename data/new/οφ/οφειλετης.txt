Word: οφειλέτης

Etymology:
The word οφειλέτης (opheilétis) comes from the Greek verb οφείλω (opheílo), which means "to owe". The suffix -της (-tis) is added to the verb stem to form a noun indicating the person who owes something.

Grammar:
Οφειλέτης is a masculine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and number (singular or plural). The word can be declined in different cases, such as nominative, genitive, accusative, etc., depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
Οφειλέτης refers to a person who owes something to someone else, usually money. It can also refer to a debtor or a borrower. In legal terms, it is used to describe a person who has a debt or an obligation to pay. The word is commonly used in everyday language in Greece, especially in financial and business contexts.
