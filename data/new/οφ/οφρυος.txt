Word: οφρυος

Etymology:
The word οφρυος (ophryos) comes from the Ancient Greek word ὀφρύς (ophrys), which means "eyebrow". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰer- ("brow, forehead").

Grammar:
In Greek, οφρυος is a masculine noun of the third declension. Its nominative singular form is οφρυς (ophrys), and its genitive singular form is οφρυος (ophryos). The word is declined like the noun λογος (logos).

Description:
Οφρυος refers to the eyebrow, the arch of hair above the eye. Eyebrows serve to protect the eyes from sweat and other debris, and also play a role in facial expression and communication. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was said to have thick, beautiful eyebrows, which were a symbol of his youth and beauty.
