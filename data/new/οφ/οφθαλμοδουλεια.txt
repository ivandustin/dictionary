Word: οφθαλμοδουλεια

Etymology: The word οφθαλμοδουλεια comes from the Greek words οφθαλμός (ophthalmos) meaning "eye" and δουλεία (douleia) meaning "slavery" or "servitude". 

Grammar: οφθαλμοδουλεια is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is written in the singular form and its plural form is οφθαλμοδουλείες. 

Description: οφθαλμοδουλεια refers to the condition of being a slave to one's eyes, or being excessively focused on appearances or material possessions. It can also refer to the act of being enslaved by one's own desires or passions. The term is often used in a negative context, implying that one is not living a fulfilling or meaningful life due to their preoccupation with superficial things.
