The word "ηλεημένος" (pronounced: e-lee-mé-nos) is a Greek adjective that comes from the verb "ηλεέω" (pronounced: e-le-é-o), which means "to have mercy" or "to show compassion." 

In terms of etymology, "ηλεημένος" is derived from the noun "ελεος" (pronounced: e-le-os), which means "mercy" or "compassion." The suffix "-μένος" (-mé-nos) is added to the noun to form the adjective, indicating the state or condition of having mercy or being compassionate.

In grammar, "ηλεημένος" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

In terms of description, "ηλεημένος" describes someone or something that has received mercy or compassion. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is blessed or fortunate.
