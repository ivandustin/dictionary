Etymology: The word "ηλιος" (pronounced "ilios") comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἥλιος" (hēlios), meaning "sun".

Grammar: In Greek, "ηλιος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The genitive form is "ηλιου" (ilíou), the accusative form is "ηλιο" (ilío), and the vocative form is also "ηλιε" (ilie).

Description: "Ηλιος" is the Greek word for "sun". It is a celestial body that is the central source of light and heat in our solar system. The sun plays a crucial role in sustaining life on Earth and is often associated with warmth, light, and energy. In Greek mythology, the sun was personified as the god Helios.
