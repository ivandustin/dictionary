Etymology: The word "ηλιον" (helion) comes from the Greek word "ἥλιος" (helios), meaning "sun".

Grammar: In Greek, "ηλιον" (helion) is a noun of neuter gender.

Description: "Ηλιον" (helion) refers to the sun, the star at the center of the solar system. It is the primary source of light and heat for the Earth. The sun plays a crucial role in sustaining life on our planet and is also a subject of scientific study.
