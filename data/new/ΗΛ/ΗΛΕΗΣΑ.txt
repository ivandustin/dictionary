Etymology: The word "ηλεησα" (pronounced "ileesa") comes from the Greek verb "ηλεεω" (pronounced "ileeo"), which means "to have mercy" or "to show compassion." 

Grammar: "Ηλεησα" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "ηλεεω." In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Ηλεησα" is a Greek word that means "I had mercy" or "I showed compassion." It is used to describe an action of showing kindness, pity, or forgiveness towards someone in the past.
