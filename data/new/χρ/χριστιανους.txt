Word: χριστιανούς

Etymology: The word χριστιανούς (christianoús) comes from the Greek word Χριστός (Christós), meaning "Christ," and the suffix -ανος (-anos), which denotes belonging or association. Therefore, χριστιανούς means "belonging to Christ" or "associated with Christ."

Grammar: χριστιανούς is a plural noun in the accusative case in Greek. It is used to refer to a group of people who are Christians.

Description: χριστιανούς is a term used to refer to Christians in the Greek language. It is commonly used in religious contexts, such as in the Bible or in church settings. The word emphasizes the association of Christians with Christ and their belief in him as their savior.
