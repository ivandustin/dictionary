Word: χρηστος

The word χρηστος (chrēstos) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the root word χράομαι (chráomai), which means "to use" or "to make use of." The word χρηστος (chrēstos) means "useful," "good," or "kind."

In Greek, χρηστος (chrēstos) is an adjective that can be used to describe a person, thing, or situation. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, χρηστος (chrēstos) can be used to describe a masculine singular noun in the nominative case, such as ἄνθρωπος (ánthrōpos), meaning "man," as in ὁ χρηστός ἄνθρωπος (ho chrēstós ánthrōpos), which means "the good man."
