Word: χριστιανός (christianos)

Etymology: The word χριστιανός (christianos) comes from the Greek word χριστός (christos), meaning "anointed one" or "messiah". The suffix -ιανός (-ianos) is a common Greek suffix used to denote a follower or believer of a particular person or ideology.

Grammar: χριστιανός (christianos) is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes χριστιανοί (christianoi). It is declined like other first-declension nouns in Greek.

Description: χριστιανός (christianos) refers to a person who follows the teachings of Jesus Christ and is a member of the Christian religion. The term was first used in Antioch in the first century AD to describe the followers of Jesus. Today, it is a widely used term to describe people who identify as Christians around the world.
