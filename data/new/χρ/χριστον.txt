Word: χριστόν

Etymology: The word χριστόν comes from the Greek word χριστός (christos), which means "anointed one" or "chosen one". In the New Testament, it is used to refer to Jesus Christ as the Messiah.

Grammar: χριστόν is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is declined as follows:

Nominative: τὸ χριστόν (to christon)
Genitive: τοῦ χριστοῦ (tou christou)
Dative: τῷ χριστῷ (to christo)
Accusative: τὸ χριστόν (to christon)
Vocative: χριστέ (christe)

Description: χριστόν is a term used in Christianity to refer to Jesus Christ as the Messiah and Savior. It is also used to refer to the consecrated bread used in the Eucharist. The word has significant religious and cultural importance for Christians around the world.
