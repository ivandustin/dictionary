Word: χρονους

Etymology:
The word χρονους (chronous) is derived from the Greek word χρόνος (chronos), which means time. The suffix -ous is added to the end of the word to indicate that it is an adjective.

Grammar:
χρονους is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case in the Greek language.

Description:
The word χρονους is used to describe something that is related to time or has a temporal quality. It can be used to describe a person, event, or object that is old, ancient, or has been around for a long time. It can also be used to describe something that is timely or occurring at a specific time.
