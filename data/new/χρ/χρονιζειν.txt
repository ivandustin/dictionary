The word "χρονιζειν" (chronizein) is a Greek verb that means "to delay" or "to procrastinate". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "χρόνος" (chronos), meaning "time", and the verb suffix "-ιζειν" (-izein), which indicates the action of making or doing something.

Grammar: "Χρονιζειν" is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. In English, it would be translated as "to delay" or "to procrastinate".

Description: The word "χρονιζειν" is often used to describe someone who is putting off doing something or taking action. It can also be used to describe a situation where progress is slow or delayed.
