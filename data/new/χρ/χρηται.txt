The word "χρηται" (chretaí) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "χράομαι" (chráomai), which means "to use" or "to make use of". 

Grammar: "χρηται" is a third-person singular present indicative active form of the verb "χράομαι". It is often used in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. 

Description: The word "χρηται" is commonly translated as "uses" or "makes use of". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing how someone uses a tool or resource, or how they employ a particular skill or ability. In Greek philosophy, the concept of "chresis" (χρῆσις) refers to the proper use or application of something, and "χρηται" can be used to describe this concept in action.
