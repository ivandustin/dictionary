The word χριστος (khristos) is a Greek word that means "anointed one" or "chosen one". It is derived from the verb χρίω (khriō), which means "to anoint" or "to smear with oil". In the Old Testament, the Hebrew word "Messiah" is used to refer to the anointed one, and in the New Testament, the Greek word χριστος is used to refer to Jesus Christ.
