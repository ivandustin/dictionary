Word: χριστου

Etymology:
The word χριστου (christou) is derived from the Greek word Χριστός (Christos), which means "anointed one" or "chosen one". It is a title given to Jesus Christ, who is considered the savior and messiah in Christianity.

Grammar:
χριστου (christou) is a genitive singular form of Χριστός (Christos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, χριστου (christou) indicates possession or relationship with Christ.

Description:
χριστου (christou) is a Greek word that is primarily used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to refer to Jesus Christ, who is considered the savior and messiah in Christianity. The word is often used in phrases such as "the son of God, Christ" or "the anointed one, Christ". It is a significant word in Christian theology and is central to the belief in the divinity of Jesus Christ.
