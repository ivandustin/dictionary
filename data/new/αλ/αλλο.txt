Word: αλλο

Etymology:
The word αλλο (allo) is derived from the Greek word ἄλλος (allos), which means "other" or "different". It is a common word in the Greek language and is used in various contexts.

Grammar:
αλλο is a neuter singular form of the adjective ἄλλος (allos). It is used to describe a noun that is different or other than the one previously mentioned. It can also be used as a pronoun to refer to something else.

Description:
αλλο is a versatile word in the Greek language that can be used in many different contexts. It is often used to express contrast or to indicate a change in topic. For example, if someone is talking about one thing and then suddenly changes the subject, they might say "αλλο τώρα" (allo tora), which means "something else now". It can also be used to describe something that is different or distinct from something else. For example, if someone is comparing two cars and says "αυτό είναι αλλο από αυτό" (auto einai allo apo auto), they mean "this is different from that". Overall, αλλο is a useful word for expressing variety, contrast, and change in the Greek language.
