Word: αληθειαι

Etymology: αληθειαι is a Greek word that comes from the root word αληθής (alithis), meaning "true" or "real". The suffix -εια (-eia) is a common Greek suffix that is used to form abstract nouns.

Grammar: αληθειαι is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "truths" or "realities". In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. αληθειαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form.

Description: αληθειαι refers to truths or realities that are objective and factual. It is often used in philosophical or religious contexts to refer to ultimate truths or principles. In everyday language, it can also refer to simple truths or facts.
