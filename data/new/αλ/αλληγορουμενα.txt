Word: αλληγορουμενα

Etymology: The word αλληγορουμενα comes from the Greek word αλληγορέω (allēgoreō), which means "to speak in allegories". The suffix -μενα (-mena) is a passive participle ending, which means that the word can be translated as "things being spoken in allegories".

Grammar: αλληγορουμενα is a neuter plural adjective in the present passive participle form. It is used to describe things that are being spoken in allegories.

Description: αλληγορουμενα is a term used to describe a literary device in which a story, poem, or other work of art is told through the use of symbols and metaphors. It is often used to convey complex ideas or emotions in a way that is more accessible to the reader or listener. The term can also be used to describe a work of art that is itself an allegory, such as John Bunyan's "The Pilgrim's Progress".
