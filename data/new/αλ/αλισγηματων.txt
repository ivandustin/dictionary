In terms of grammar, "αλισγηματων" is a second declension noun, with a stem of "αλισγηματ-". The genitive plural ending "-ων" indicates that the noun is referring to multiple instances of defilement or pollution. 

Overall, "αλισγηματων" is a word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to impurities or sins that need to be cleansed or purified.
