The word "αλεξανδρου" is a Greek noun that is derived from the name Alexander. It is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship to Alexander. In Greek grammar, it is a second declension noun, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. 

Etymologically, the name Alexander comes from the Greek words "alexo" meaning "to defend" and "aner" meaning "man", thus the name means "defender of men". 

In terms of description, "αλεξανδρου" can be used to indicate possession or relationship to someone named Alexander. For example, "το βιβλίο του αλεξανδρου" (to vivlio tou Alexandrou) means "the book of Alexander".
