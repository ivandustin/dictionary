The word "αληθευοντες" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "αληθεύω" (alithevo), which means "to speak the truth" or "to be truthful". The present participle form indicates that the action of speaking the truth is ongoing or continuous. 

In terms of grammar, "αληθευοντες" is in the present active participle form, which means that it is describing a subject that is currently performing the action of speaking the truth. 

Overall, "αληθευοντες" can be translated to "speaking the truth" or "being truthful".
