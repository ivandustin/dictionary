Word: αλλης

Etymology: αλλης is a Greek word that comes from the root word "άλλος" (allos), which means "other" or "different". αλλης is the genitive singular form of the feminine adjective αλλή (allee), which means "other" or "different".

Grammar: αλλης is a feminine adjective in the genitive singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship.

Description: αλλης is used to describe a feminine noun in the genitive case, indicating that the noun possesses or is related to something that is "other" or "different". For example, in the phrase "της αλλης γυναίκας" (tis allees gynaikas), which means "the other woman's", αλλης describes the woman as being different from the one previously mentioned.
