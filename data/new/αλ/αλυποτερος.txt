Word: αλυποτερος

Etymology: The word αλυποτερος comes from the Greek words α (a) meaning "not" and λυπη (lypi) meaning "grief" or "sorrow". The suffix -τερος (-teros) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "less".

Grammar: αλυποτερος is an adjective in the comparative form. It is used to compare two things, with the meaning "less sorrowful" or "more carefree".

Description: αλυποτερος is a word that describes a state of being free from sorrow or grief. It can be used to describe a person who is carefree, or a situation that is not burdened by sadness or worry. The word has a positive connotation, suggesting a sense of lightness and ease.
