Word: αλλους

Etymology: αλλους is a Greek word that comes from the root word "άλλος" (allos), which means "other" or "different". 

Grammar: αλλους is the accusative plural form of the adjective "άλλος" (allos), which means "other" or "different". It is used to describe a noun in the accusative case, indicating that the noun is the object of the sentence. 

Description: αλλους is used in Greek to describe multiple objects or people that are different or distinct from others. For example, "Οι αλλους άνθρωποι είναι πολύ διαφορετικοί από εμάς" (Oi allous anthropoi einai polu diaforetikoi apo emas) means "The other people are very different from us".
