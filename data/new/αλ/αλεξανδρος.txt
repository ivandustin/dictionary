Word: αλεξανδρος

Etymology: The name αλεξανδρος (Alexandros) is of Greek origin and is composed of two words: αλεξω (alexo) meaning "to defend" or "to protect" and ανηρ (aner) meaning "man". Therefore, the name αλεξανδρος can be translated as "defender of man" or "protector of man".

Grammar: αλεξανδρος is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the first declension and its nominative singular form is αλεξανδρος. In the genitive singular, it becomes αλεξανδρου.

Description: αλεξανδρος is a popular name in Greece and other countries with Greek influence. It is also the name of several historical figures, including Alexander the Great, the famous ancient Greek king who conquered much of the known world. The name is often associated with strength, courage, and leadership.
