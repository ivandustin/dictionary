Word: αληθινοι

Etymology: 
The word αληθινοι (alithinoi) is derived from the Greek word αληθής (alithis), which means "true" or "real". The suffix -νοι (-noi) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple individuals.

Grammar:
αληθινοι is a plural adjective in the masculine gender, agreeing with the noun it modifies. In Greek, adjectives typically follow the noun they modify.

Description:
αληθινοι is an adjective that describes something as true or genuine. It can be used to describe people, objects, or ideas that are authentic or real. For example, αληθινοι φίλοι (alithinoi filoi) means "true friends", while αληθινοι λόγοι (alithinoi logoi) means "true words" or "truthful statements".
