Word: αλλαγησομεθα

Etymology:
The word αλλαγησομεθα is derived from the Greek verb αλλάζω (allazo) which means "to change" and the reflexive pronoun μεθα (metha) which means "ourselves". Therefore, αλλαγησομεθα means "we will change ourselves".

Grammar:
αλλαγησομεθα is a first-person plural future passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by combining the future tense of the verb αλλάζω (allazo) with the reflexive pronoun μεθα (metha).

Description:
αλλαγησομεθα is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of changing oneself. It implies a deliberate and conscious decision to transform oneself in some way. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as personal growth, self-improvement, or social change. The passive voice suggests that the change is something that will happen to us, rather than something we will actively do.
