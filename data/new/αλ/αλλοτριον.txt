Word: αλλοτριον

Etymology:
The word αλλοτριον comes from the Greek word "άλλος" (allos) meaning "other" or "different" and "τρίον" (trion) meaning "property" or "possession". Therefore, αλλοτριον literally means "something belonging to another".

Grammar:
αλλοτριον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is αλλότρια (allotria).

Description:
αλλοτριον is used to describe something that belongs to someone else or is foreign to oneself. It can also refer to something that is strange or unfamiliar. In legal terms, αλλοτριον can refer to property that is owned by someone else.
