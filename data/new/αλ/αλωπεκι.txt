The word "αλωπεκι" (alopeki) is a Greek noun that means "fox". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "ἀλώπηξ" (alōpēx), which also means "fox". 

Grammar: In Greek, "αλωπεκι" is a neuter noun and its plural form is "αλώπεκα" (alopeka). 

Description: Foxes are small carnivorous mammals with a pointed snout, bushy tail, and a distinctive coat of fur. They are known for their intelligence and cunning behavior, and are often featured in folklore and mythology. In Greek mythology, the fox was associated with the god Dionysus and was considered a symbol of fertility and cunning.
