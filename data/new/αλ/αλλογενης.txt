Word: αλλογενης

Etymology:
The word αλλογενης comes from the Greek roots αλλος (allos) meaning "other" and γενος (genos) meaning "race" or "kind". Therefore, αλλογενης means "of another race" or "foreign".

Grammar:
αλλογενης is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined in different cases, such as αλλογενής (nominative), αλλογενούς (genitive), αλλογενεί (dative), and αλλογενή (accusative).

Description:
αλλογενης is a Greek word that is commonly used to describe someone or something that is foreign or of another race. It can be used in various contexts, such as in politics, sociology, and anthropology. For example, it can be used to describe immigrants, refugees, or people from different cultural backgrounds. It can also be used to describe plants or animals that are not native to a particular region.
