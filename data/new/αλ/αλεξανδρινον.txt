Word: αλεξανδρινον

Etymology: The word αλεξανδρινον comes from the Greek word Αλεξάνδρεια (Alexandria), which was the name of a city in ancient Egypt founded by Alexander the Great. The suffix -ινον (-inon) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives.

Grammar: αλεξανδρινον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: αλεξανδρινον is an adjective that describes something as being related to or characteristic of Alexandria. It can be used to describe anything from a person or place to a food or drink. For example, αλεξανδρινον κρασί (alexandrino krasi) means "Alexandrian wine" and αλεξανδρινή ποίηση (alexandrini poisi) means "Alexandrian poetry."
