Word: αληθές

Etymology: αληθές comes from the Greek word "αλήθεια" (alétheia), which means truth.

Grammar: αληθές is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something as true or genuine.

Description: αληθές is a word that is often used in philosophical and scientific contexts to describe something that is objectively true or accurate. It is also used in everyday language to describe something that is not false or misleading. In Greek mythology, the goddess of truth was named Aletheia.
