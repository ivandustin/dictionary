Word: αλλαξαι

Etymology: αλλαξαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αλλάσσω (allasso), which means "to change" or "to alter."

Grammar: αλλαξαι is a first-person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb αλλάσσω (allasso). It is a regular verb in Greek and follows the conjugation pattern of the first conjugation.

Description: αλλαξαι means "I changed" in English. It is a past tense form of the verb αλλάσσω (allasso), which is commonly used in Greek to express a change or alteration in something. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as changing one's mind, changing one's clothes, or changing the direction of a conversation.
