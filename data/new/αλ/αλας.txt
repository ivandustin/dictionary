Word: αλας

Etymology:
The word αλας (alas) comes from the Ancient Greek word ἅλς (háls), which means "salt". This word is also the root of the English word "halite", which is a type of rock salt.

Grammar:
αλας is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. In the genitive singular form, it is αλατος (alatos).

Description:
αλας refers to salt, which is a mineral substance that is commonly used as a seasoning and preservative in food. In Ancient Greece, salt was a valuable commodity and was used as a form of currency. Salt was also used in religious ceremonies and was believed to have purifying properties. Today, salt is still an important part of Greek cuisine and is used in many traditional dishes.
