In terms of grammar, "αλοωντα" is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be used as a subject or a predicate nominative in a sentence.

Etymologically, the verb "αλόω" comes from the ancient Greek word "ἄλων" (alon), which means "threshing floor". The word "αλοωντα" is also related to the noun "αλευρόν" (aleuron), which means "flour".

Overall, "αλοωντα" describes the ongoing action of threshing or beating grain, and can be used in various contexts related to agriculture or food production.
