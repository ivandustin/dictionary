The word "αλογον" (alogon) is a Greek adjective that means "senseless" or "foolish". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the noun "λόγος" (logos), which can mean "word", "reason", or "logic". 

Grammar: "αλογον" is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. 

Description: The word is often used to describe actions or ideas that lack reason or logic. It can also be used to describe someone who is foolish or lacking in common sense.
