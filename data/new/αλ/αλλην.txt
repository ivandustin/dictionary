Word: αλλην

Etymology: αλλην is a Greek word that comes from the root word "άλλος" (allos), which means "other" or "different". 

Grammar: αλλην is a feminine singular accusative form of the adjective "άλλος" (allos), which means "other" or "different". It is used to describe a feminine singular noun that is the object of the sentence. 

Description: αλλην is used in Greek to indicate something that is different or distinct from something else. It is often used in comparisons, such as "this is different from that" or "this is not the same as that". It can also be used to indicate a change or variation, such as "the weather is different today than it was yesterday".
