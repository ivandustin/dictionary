Word: αλλος

Etymology:
The word αλλος (allos) is a Greek word that means "other" or "different". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *al- meaning "beyond" or "other".

Grammar:
In Greek, αλλος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). It can also be declined for number (singular and plural).

Description:
αλλος is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe something that is different or distinct from something else. For example, "Ο άλλος άνθρωπος" (o allos anthropos) means "the other person". It can also be used to indicate a preference for one thing over another. For example, "Προτιμώ τον άλλο δρόμο" (protimo ton allo dromo) means "I prefer the other road". Additionally, it can be used to express surprise or disbelief. For example, "Τι άλλο θα δούμε;" (ti allo tha doume) means "What else will we see?".
