Word: αλλοτριων

Etymology:
The word αλλοτριων comes from the Greek word αλλότριος (allotrios), which means "belonging to another, foreign, strange, or unfamiliar." The suffix -ων (-on) is added to the end of the word to make it a noun.

Grammar:
αλλοτριων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns. In this case, it means "of strangers" or "of foreigners."

Description:
αλλοτριων is a Greek word that refers to something that belongs to strangers or foreigners. It can be used to describe anything that is unfamiliar or foreign to a particular group or individual.
