Word: αληθεις

Etymology: αληθεις is a Greek word that comes from the root word αληθής (alithis), which means "true" or "real."

Grammar: αληθεις is an adjective in Greek, and it is in the masculine nominative plural form. It is used to describe something that is true or genuine.

Description: αληθεις is a word that is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the nature of truth. It is also used in everyday language to describe something that is genuine or authentic. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe a person who is truthful and honest.
