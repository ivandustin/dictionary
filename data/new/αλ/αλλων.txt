Word: αλλων

Etymology: αλλων is a Greek word that comes from the verb ἀλλάσσω (allasso), which means "to change" or "to exchange". It is a form of the adjective ἄλλος (allos), which means "other" or "different".

Grammar: αλλων is the genitive plural form of ἄλλος (allos), which means "other" or "different". In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between nouns.

Description: αλλων is used to indicate "of others" or "belonging to others" in Greek. It is often used in phrases such as "των αλλων" (ton allon), which means "of the others" or "belonging to the others". It can also be used to compare or contrast different things, as in the phrase "ἕτερον αλλων" (heteron allon), which means "one thing different from another".
