Word: αληθειας

Etymology:
The word αληθειας (alētheias) is derived from the Greek word αληθεία (alētheia), which means "truth" or "verity". It is composed of the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the noun λήθη (lēthē), which means "forgetfulness" or "oblivion". Therefore, αληθεία (alētheia) literally means "not forgetting" or "not being forgotten".

Grammar:
αληθειας (alētheias) is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of truth" or "truth's".

Description:
αληθειας (alētheias) is a Greek word that refers to the concept of truth or verity. It is often used in philosophical and religious contexts to describe the ultimate reality or the fundamental nature of existence. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe the truth of God and the teachings of Jesus Christ. In modern Greek, it is also used in everyday language to mean "truth" or "reality".
