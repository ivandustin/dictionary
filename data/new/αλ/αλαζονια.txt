Word: αλαζονία (alazonia)

Etymology: The word αλαζονία comes from the Greek word αλαζών (alazon), which means "braggart" or "boaster". 

Grammar: αλαζονία is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and its nominative case is αλαζονία. 

Description: αλαζονία refers to the act of boasting or bragging about oneself or one's accomplishments. It can also refer to a person who is a braggart or a show-off. In Greek mythology, the character of Alazon was a stock character in comedy who was known for his exaggerated claims and boasts.
