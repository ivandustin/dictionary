Word: αλιείς

Etymology:
The word αλιείς (aliis) is derived from the Greek word αλιεύω (alievo), which means "to fish". It is related to the Latin word "alio", which means "to nourish" or "to support".

Grammar:
αλιείς is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "fishermen". It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
αλιείς is a term used to refer to people who fish for a living or as a hobby. In ancient Greece, fishing was an important industry, and fishermen were highly respected members of society. Today, fishing is still an important industry in Greece, and many people enjoy fishing as a recreational activity.
