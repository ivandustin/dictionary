Word: αλλοτριεπισκοπος

Etymology: The word αλλοτριεπισκοπος is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: αλλότριος (allotrios) meaning "foreign" or "strange" and επίσκοπος (episkopos) meaning "bishop". Therefore, the word αλλοτριεπισκοπος literally means "foreign bishop".

Grammar: αλλοτριεπισκοπος is a noun in the masculine gender in Greek. It is declined like other third-declension nouns, with the genitive form being αλλοτριεπισκόπου (allotriepiskopou).

Description: αλλοτριεπισκοπος is a term used in the Greek Orthodox Church to refer to a bishop who is not the bishop of a particular diocese or region, but rather has been appointed to oversee a specific task or area of responsibility. This can include overseeing a particular group of churches or monasteries, or serving as a representative of the church in a particular region or country. The term is also used in a broader sense to refer to any bishop who is not the bishop of a particular diocese or region.
