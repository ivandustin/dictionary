Word: αλισθησεται

Etymology:
The word αλισθησεται is a Greek verb in the future tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is derived from the root word αλισθάνω (alizthano), which means "to slip" or "to slide."

Grammar:
αλισθησεται is a third-person singular verb in the future tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, the future tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
The word αλισθησεται can be translated to English as "he/she/it will slip" or "he/she/it will slide." It is often used in a literal sense to describe someone or something losing their footing and falling. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe someone making a mistake or experiencing a setback.
