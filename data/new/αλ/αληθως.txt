Word: αληθως

Etymology: αληθως is a Greek adverb that means "truly" or "really". It is derived from the Greek word "αληθής" (alithis), which means "true" or "real".

Grammar: αληθως is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb. It is often used to emphasize the truth or reality of something.

Description: αληθως is a powerful word that conveys a sense of truth and authenticity. It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to express a deep conviction or belief. In everyday speech, it can be used to emphasize the truthfulness of a statement or to express agreement with someone else's statement.
