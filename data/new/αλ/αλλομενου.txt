Word: αλλομενου

Etymology:
The word αλλομενου is derived from the Greek verb αλλάσσω (allasso), which means "to change" or "to alter". The suffix -μενου (-menou) is a participle form that indicates the present passive voice, meaning "being changed" or "being altered".

Grammar:
αλλομενου is a present passive participle in the genitive case, masculine gender, and singular number. It is used to describe a noun or pronoun that is undergoing a change or alteration.

Description:
The word αλλομενου can be translated as "being changed" or "being altered". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe the concept of change or transformation. In grammar, it is used as an adjective to modify a noun or pronoun that is undergoing a change. For example, in the sentence "το αλλομενου πρόσωπο" (to allomenou prosopo), which means "the changing face", αλλομενου modifies the noun πρόσωπο (prosopo), meaning "face".
