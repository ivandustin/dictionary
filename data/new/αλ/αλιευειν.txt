Word: αλιεύειν

Etymology: αλιεύειν is a Greek verb that comes from the noun αλιεύς (alieus), meaning "fisherman". The verb αλιεύειν means "to fish" or "to catch fish".

Grammar: αλιεύειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the pattern of verbs that end in -ω. The verb stem is αλιευ-, and the present tense ending for the first person singular is -ω, giving us αλιεύω (alieuo), "I fish".

Description: αλιεύειν is a common verb in Greek, especially in the context of fishing and seafood. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "to search for something" or "to pursue something". The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where Jesus calls his disciples to become "fishers of men" (αλιείς ανθρώπων, alieis anthropon).
