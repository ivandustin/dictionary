Word: αλαζονας

Etymology:
The word αλαζονας (alazonas) comes from the Greek word αλαζων (alazon), which means "braggart" or "boaster". It is derived from the verb αλαζειν (alazein), which means "to boast" or "to exaggerate".

Grammar:
αλαζονας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word is singular, meaning it refers to one person or thing.

Description:
αλαζονας is a term used to describe someone who boasts or brags excessively. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is arrogant or conceited. In Greek mythology, the term was used to describe a type of character who was known for their excessive pride and self-importance.
