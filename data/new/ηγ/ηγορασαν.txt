Word: ηγοράσαν (pronounced ee-go-RA-san)

Etymology: ηγοράσαν comes from the Greek verb αγοράζω (agorazo), which means "to buy" or "to purchase". The prefix η- (e-) is a past tense marker, so ηγοράσαν means "they bought" or "they purchased".

Grammar: ηγοράσαν is the third person plural past tense form of αγοράζω. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The -ασαν (-asan) ending indicates the past tense and the third person plural (they).

Description: ηγοράσαν is a verb that describes an action that happened in the past. It specifically means "they bought" or "they purchased". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as talking about a group of people who bought something at a store or market.
