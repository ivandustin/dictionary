Word: ηγοράσθητε

Etymology:
The word ηγοράσθητε is a conjugated form of the Greek verb αγοράζω (agorazo), which means "to buy" or "to purchase". The verb is derived from the noun αγορά (agora), which means "marketplace" or "assembly".

Grammar:
Ηγοράσθητε is the second person plural aorist passive indicative form of αγοράζω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description:
Ηγοράσθητε can be translated to English as "you were bought" or "you have been purchased". The word is often used in a religious context to refer to the idea that believers have been bought or redeemed by God through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ. In this sense, the word carries a connotation of salvation and spiritual transformation.
