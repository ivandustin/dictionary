Word: ηγουμενων

Etymology: ηγουμενων is the present participle of the Greek verb ηγεομαι (hegeomai), which means "to lead" or "to guide". It comes from the root word ηγε-, which means "to lead" or "to go before".

Grammar: ηγουμενων is a masculine noun in the genitive case, which means it is used to show possession or relationship to another noun. It is often translated as "of leaders" or "of governors".

Description: ηγουμενων is a word that is often used in the context of leadership or governance. It can refer to leaders or governors of a country, region, or organization. In Greek Orthodox Christianity, it is also used to refer to the abbot or superior of a monastery.
