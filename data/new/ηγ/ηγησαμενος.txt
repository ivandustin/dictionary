Word: ηγησαμενος

Etymology: ηγησαμενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb ηγέομαι (ēgéomai), which means "to lead" or "to guide". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is completed.

Grammar: ηγησαμενος is the aorist participle of ηγέομαι (ēgéomai), which means "having led" or "having guided". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: ηγησαμενος describes someone who has led or guided others in the past. It implies a sense of authority and responsibility, as well as a certain level of expertise or knowledge in a particular area. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in politics, business, or education.
