Word: ηγιασθη

Etymology: ηγιασθη is a Greek word that comes from the verb ἁγιάζω (hagiazó), which means "to make holy" or "to sanctify."

Grammar: ηγιασθη is a verb in the aorist passive tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person.

Description: ηγιασθη is a word that describes an action of being made holy or sanctified. It is often used in a religious context to describe the process of becoming pure or righteous. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a person or thing that is considered sacred or revered.
