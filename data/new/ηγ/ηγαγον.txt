Word: ηγαγον

Etymology:
The word ηγαγον (hēgagon) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ἄγω (agō), which means "to lead" or "to bring". The prefix η (hē) is a past tense marker, so ηγαγον means "they led" or "they brought".

Grammar:
ηγαγον is the third person plural past tense form of the verb ἄγω (agō). It is an active voice verb, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. In this case, the subject is plural, meaning that "they" led or brought something.

Description:
ηγαγον is a verb that describes an action of leading or bringing something. It is often used in the context of leading or bringing someone or something to a particular place. In the New Testament of the Bible, ηγαγον is used to describe the actions of various individuals, such as the Pharisees who brought a woman caught in adultery to Jesus (John 8:3), or the soldiers who led Jesus to be crucified (Mark 15:16).
