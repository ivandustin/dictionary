Word: ηγοράσεν

Etymology: ηγοράσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αγοράζω (agorazo), which means "to buy" or "to purchase". The prefix η- (e-) is a past tense marker, indicating that the action of buying has already taken place.

Grammar: ηγοράσεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb αγοράζω (agorazo). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is conjugated in the active voice, indicating that the subject (he/she/it) performed the action of buying.

Description: ηγοράσεν means "he/she/it bought" in Greek. It is a past tense verb that describes a completed action of purchasing something. The context of the sentence would determine what was bought and who the subject is.
