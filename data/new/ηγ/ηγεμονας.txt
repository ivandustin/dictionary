Word: ηγεμόνας

Etymology:
The word ηγεμόνας (hegemonas) comes from the Greek word ηγεμών (hegemon), which means "leader" or "ruler". The suffix -ας (-as) is added to indicate the masculine gender.

Grammar:
Ηγεμόνας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as a subject, object, or possessive noun in a sentence.

Description:
Ηγεμόνας refers to a leader or ruler who has authority over a group of people or a territory. In ancient Greece, the term was used to describe the leader of a city-state or a military commander. Today, the word can be used to refer to a political leader, a CEO, or any person who holds a position of power and influence.
