Word: ηγιασμένον

Etymology: The word ηγιασμένον comes from the Greek verb ηγιάζω (hagiazo), which means "to make holy" or "to sanctify". The suffix -μένον (-menon) is a participle ending, indicating that the word is in the passive voice and describes something that has been made holy.

Grammar: ηγιασμένον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: ηγιασμένον is an adjective that describes something that has been made holy or sanctified. It can be used to describe a person, place, or thing that has been set apart for a sacred purpose. In Christian theology, it is often used to describe objects or places that are considered holy, such as the Eucharist or a church.
