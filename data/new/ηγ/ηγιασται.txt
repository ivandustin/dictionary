The word "ηγιασται" (pronounced "ee-yah-steh") is a Greek verb in the third person singular form of the aorist tense. It comes from the root word "ἁγιάζω" (hagiazó), which means "to make holy" or "to sanctify." 

In terms of grammar, "ηγιασται" is a transitive verb, meaning it requires a direct object to complete its meaning. It is also in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Overall, "ηγιασται" can be translated to mean "he/she/it was sanctified" or "he/she/it was made holy."
