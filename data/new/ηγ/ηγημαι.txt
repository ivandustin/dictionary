Word: ηγημαι

Etymology:
The word ηγημαι (hēgēmai) comes from the Greek verb ἡγέομαι (hēgéomai), which means "to lead, to guide, to think, to consider, to esteem, to regard as". It is derived from the noun ἡγεμών (hēgemṓn), which means "leader, ruler, governor".

Grammar:
ηγημαι is a verb in the present tense, indicative mood, active voice, and first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem ends in -α.

Description:
ηγημαι is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can mean "to lead, to guide" in a literal sense, as in "he led the army to victory". It can also mean "to think, to consider" in a more abstract sense, as in "I consider him to be a good friend". Additionally, it can mean "to esteem, to regard as" in a sense of respect or admiration, as in "he is highly esteemed in his field".
