Word: ηγγαρευσαν

Etymology: The word ηγγαρευσαν comes from the Greek verb ἡγεμονεύω (hēgemoneuō), which means "to lead, to rule, to govern". The word is derived from the noun ἡγεμών (hēgemōn), which means "leader, ruler, governor".

Grammar: ηγγαρευσαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb ἡγεμονεύω (hēgemoneuō). In this form, it indicates that the action of leading or ruling was completed in the past by a group of people.

Description: ηγγαρευσαν can be translated as "they led, they ruled, they governed". The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning. It is a verb that implies a sense of authority and control, and is often used in political or military contexts.
