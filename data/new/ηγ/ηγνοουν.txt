Word: ηγνοούν (pronounced ee-gno-OUN)

Etymology: ηγνοούν comes from the Greek verb άγνοια (agnia), which means "ignorance" or "lack of knowledge." The verb form ηγνοούν is the third person plural present tense of the verb άγνοια, meaning "they are ignorant" or "they do not know."

Grammar: ηγνοούν is a regular verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation group in Greek. It is formed by adding the present tense endings to the stem άγνοια. The verb is in the third person plural, which means it refers to a group of people who are ignorant or do not know something.

Description: ηγνοούν is a verb that describes a state of ignorance or lack of knowledge. It can be used to describe a group of people who are unaware of something or who do not have the information they need. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου ηγνοούν την αλήθεια" (My friends are ignorant of the truth) or "Οι μαθητές ηγνοούν τον κανόνα της γραμματικής" (The students are ignorant of the grammar rule).
