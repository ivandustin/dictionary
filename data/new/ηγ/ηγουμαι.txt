Word: ηγουμαι

Etymology:
The word ηγουμαι (pronounced as "ee-goo-mai") is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ἡγέομαι (hēgéomai), which means "to lead" or "to guide". It is derived from the noun ἡγεμών (hēgemṓn), which means "leader" or "ruler".

Grammar:
Ηγουμαι is a first-person singular present indicative active form of the verb. It is conjugated in the present tense and is used to express the act of leading or guiding. The verb can also be conjugated in other tenses, such as the past tense (ἡγησάμην, hēgēsamēn) or the future tense (ἡγήσομαι, hēgḗsomai).

Description:
Ηγουμαι is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to describe the act of leading or guiding a group of people, as well as the act of being in charge or having authority over something. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as leading a movement or guiding someone's thoughts or actions. Overall, ηγουμαι is a powerful verb that conveys a sense of leadership and authority.
