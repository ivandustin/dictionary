Word: ηγησασθε

Grammar: ηγησασθε is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in the middle voice. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The verb can be translated as "you led yourselves" or "you thought for yourselves".

Description: ηγησασθε is a verb that implies taking charge or taking responsibility for one's own actions or thoughts.
