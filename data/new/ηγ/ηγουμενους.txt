Word: ηγουμενους

Etymology:
The word ηγουμενους (pronounced "ee-goo-men-oos") is a Greek word that comes from the verb ηγέομαι (pronounced "ee-ye-o-mai"), which means "to lead" or "to guide". The word ηγουμενους is the present participle form of the verb, which means "those who lead" or "those who guide".

Grammar:
In Greek, ηγουμενους is a masculine noun in the accusative case. It is the plural form of the present participle of the verb ηγέομαι. The word can be translated to English as "leaders" or "guides".

Description:
Ηγουμενους is a word that refers to people who are in a position of leadership or guidance. In Greek history, the word was often used to refer to the leaders of monasteries or other religious communities. Today, the word can be used in a variety of contexts to refer to people who are in charge or who are guiding others in some way.
