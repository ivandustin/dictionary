The word "ηγεισθαι" (hegeisthai) is a Greek verb that means "to lead" or "to guide". It is derived from the root word "hegemon" which means "leader" or "commander". In terms of grammar, it is a present middle/passive participle, which means that it can be used as both a verb and an adjective. As a verb, it can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different actions and attitudes of leading or guiding. As an adjective, it can be used to describe someone who is a leader or guide.
