Word: ηγεμονία (hegemonía)

Etymology: The word ηγεμονία comes from the Greek word ηγέομαι (hegéomai), which means "to lead" or "to command". The suffix -ία (-ía) is added to indicate the abstract noun form, meaning "the state or condition of being a leader or ruler".

Grammar: ηγεμονία is a feminine noun in Greek, and its plural form is ηγεμονίες (hegemoníes). It is often used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural to refer to multiple instances of leadership or dominance.

Description: ηγεμονία refers to the state or condition of being a leader or ruler, particularly in a political or military context. It can also refer to dominance or control in other areas, such as economics or culture. The term is often used in discussions of power dynamics and international relations.
