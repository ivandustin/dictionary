Word: ηγαπησαν

Etymology: The word ηγαπησαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb αγαπάω (agapao), which means "to love". The prefix η- indicates the past tense, and the suffix -αν indicates the third person plural.

Grammar: ηγαπησαν is a past tense verb in the indicative mood, third person plural. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened. The indicative mood is used to make statements or ask questions. The third person plural refers to a group of people or things.

Description: ηγαπησαν describes an action that a group of people did in the past, which was to love. The context of the sentence would determine who or what they loved. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου ηγάπησαν την πόλη" (My friends loved the city).
