Word: ηγεμόνα (pronounced ee-ge-mo-na)

Etymology: ηγεμόνα comes from the Greek word ηγεμών (hegemon), which means leader or ruler. The suffix -α is added to make it feminine.

Grammar: ηγεμόνα is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence. In the plural form, it becomes ηγεμόνες (hegemones).

Description: ηγεμόνα refers to a female leader or ruler. It can be used to describe a woman who holds a position of power or authority, such as a queen, president, or CEO. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any woman who is in charge or takes charge of a situation.
