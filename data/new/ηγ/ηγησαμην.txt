It is a compound verb formed from the prefix ηγέ- (hēgé-), which means "to lead," and the suffix -σαμην (-samēn), which is a first-person singular aorist middle indicative ending.

Grammar:
ηγησαμην is a first-person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb ηγέομαι (hēgéomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon itself or is affected by the action in some way.

ηγησαμην can be translated to English as "I led myself" or "I considered myself."
