Word: ηγερθησαν

Etymology:
The word ηγερθησαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "εγείρω" (egero), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". The verb comes from the ancient Greek word "εγείρω" (egeiro), which has the same meaning.

Grammar:
ηγερθησαν is the third person plural aorist passive indicative form of the verb "εγείρω" (egero). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description:
ηγερθησαν can be translated to English as "they were raised" or "they were awakened". The word is often used in a religious context to describe the resurrection of Jesus Christ. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of waking up or rising from a state of sleep or inactivity.
