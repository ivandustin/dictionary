Word: ηγοράσα (pronounced ee-go-RA-sa)

Etymology: ηγοράσα comes from the Greek verb αγοράζω (agorazo), which means "to buy" or "to purchase". The prefix η- (e-) is a past tense marker, so ηγοράσα means "I bought" or "I purchased".

Grammar: ηγοράσα is the first person singular past tense form of αγοράζω. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The -ασα (-asa) ending indicates the past tense and the first person singular.

Description: ηγοράσα is a verb that means "I bought" or "I purchased". It is a past tense form, so it refers to a completed action in the past. The verb αγοράζω (agorazo) is a common verb in Greek, and it is used in a variety of contexts, such as buying goods or services, purchasing property, or acquiring something in general.
