Word: ηγιασεν

Etymology: ηγιασεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ἁγιάζω (hagiazó), which means "to make holy" or "to sanctify."

Grammar: ηγιασεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb ἁγιάζω (hagiazó). In this form, it indicates that the action of sanctifying was completed in the past.

Description: ηγιασεν is a verb that describes the act of making something holy or sanctifying it. It is often used in a religious context, such as when referring to the sanctification of a person or object for use in a religious ceremony. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe the act of Jesus Christ sanctifying himself for the sake of his followers.
