Word: ιερατευμα

Etymology:
The word ιερατευμα (ierateuma) comes from the Greek word ιερατεύω (ierateuo), which means "to serve as a priest" or "to perform priestly duties". The suffix -μα (-ma) is added to the verb to form a noun, which means "the act of serving as a priest" or "the office of a priest".

Grammar:
Ιερατευμα is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word ιερατευμα is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
Ιερατευμα refers to the act of serving as a priest or the office of a priest. In ancient Greece, priests played an important role in religious ceremonies and rituals. They were responsible for performing sacrifices, interpreting omens, and communicating with the gods. The word ιερατευμα can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any religious office or position of authority within a religious organization.
