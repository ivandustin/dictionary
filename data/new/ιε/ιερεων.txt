Word: ιερεων

Etymology:
The word ιερεων (iereon) comes from the Greek word ιερεύς (iereus), which means "priest" or "sacred person". The word is derived from the Greek word ιερός (ieros), which means "sacred" or "holy".

Grammar:
Ιερεων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and is in the nominative case. The plural form of ιερεων is ιερείς (iereis).

Description:
Ιερεων refers to a priest or a sacred person in the Greek language. In ancient Greece, priests played an important role in religious ceremonies and rituals. They were responsible for performing sacrifices, interpreting omens, and communicating with the gods. In modern times, the word ιερεων is still used to refer to priests in the Greek Orthodox Church.
