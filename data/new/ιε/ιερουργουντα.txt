Word: ιερουργουντα

Etymology: The word ιερουργουντα comes from the Greek words ιερός (hieros) meaning "sacred" and έργο (ergo) meaning "work" or "action". Therefore, ιερουργουντα can be translated as "performing sacred work" or "conducting religious rites".

Grammar: ιερουργουντα is a verb in the present participle form, which means it describes an ongoing action. It is in the active voice and in the third person plural form. 

Description: ιερουργουντα refers to the act of performing religious rituals or ceremonies, particularly in the context of the Greek Orthodox Church. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of sacred or religious work.
