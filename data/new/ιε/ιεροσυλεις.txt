Word: ιεροσυλεις

Etymology:
The word ιεροσυλεις comes from the Greek words ιερός (hieros) meaning "sacred" and συλλαμβάνω (sullambanō) meaning "to take away, to steal". Therefore, ιεροσυλεις literally means "to steal sacred things".

Grammar:
ιεροσυλεις is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. ιεροσυλεις is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form.

Description:
ιεροσυλεις refers to the act of stealing or desecrating sacred objects or places. In ancient Greece, this was considered a serious crime and was punishable by death. The term is still used today to describe the theft or desecration of religious artifacts or places.
