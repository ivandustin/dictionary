Word: ιερον

Etymology:
The word ιερον (hieron) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the word ιερός (hieros), which means "sacred" or "holy." It is often used to refer to a temple or a sacred place.

Grammar:
Ιερον is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and can be used as the subject or object of a sentence. In the plural form, it becomes ιερά (hiera).

Description:
Ιερον is a word that is often used in the context of religion and spirituality. It refers to a sacred place or a temple where religious rituals and ceremonies are performed. In ancient Greece, ιερον was used to refer to the temples of the gods and goddesses, such as the Parthenon in Athens.
