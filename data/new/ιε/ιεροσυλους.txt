Word: ιεροσυλους

Etymology: The word ιεροσυλους comes from the Greek words ιερός (holy) and συλλέω (to take away). Therefore, ιεροσυλους means "one who takes away holy things" or "a sacrilegious person".

Grammar: ιεροσυλους is a noun in the masculine gender and plural number in Greek. It is declined as follows: ο ιεροσύλος (singular), οι ιεροσύλοι (plural).

Description: ιεροσυλους is a term used to describe someone who steals or desecrates holy objects or places. In ancient Greece, this was considered a serious crime and was punishable by death. The term is still used today to describe someone who commits sacrilege or blasphemy.
