Word: κατεναντι

Etymology: κατεναντι is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἔναντι (enanti) meaning "opposite" or "facing". Therefore, κατεναντι can be translated as "facing down" or "opposite".

Grammar: κατεναντι is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe the position of something or someone in relation to another thing or person. It is often used in the expression "κατεναντι αλλήλων" (katenanti allilon) which means "facing each other".

Description: κατεναντι is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to describe the position of two people or objects facing each other, or the direction in which something is facing. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where two people or groups are in opposition to each other.
