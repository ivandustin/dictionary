Word: καταβηναι

Etymology:
The word καταβηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go". Therefore, καταβηναι means "to go down".

Grammar:
καταβηναι is an aorist infinitive active verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -αι to the stem καταβην-. The aorist tense in Greek refers to a past action that is completed, and the infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated to a specific subject.

Description:
καταβηναι is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to physically going down, such as descending a mountain or stairs. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a decrease in status or rank, or a decrease in intensity or quantity. For example, one might say "The sun went down" or "The stock market went down". Additionally, καταβηναι can be used in a religious context to describe the descent of a divine being or spirit.
