Word: κατηχηνται

Etymology:
The word κατηχηνται comes from the Greek verb κατηχέω (katecheo), which means "to instruct" or "to teach." The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "according to," and the verb ἠχέω (echeo), which means "to sound" or "to echo."

Grammar:
κατηχηνται is a third-person plural present passive indicative form of the verb κατηχέω (katecheo). In this form, the verb indicates that the subject (which is not specified in this case) is being instructed or taught by someone or something else. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing, and the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it.

Description:
κατηχηνται is a Greek verb that describes the act of being instructed or taught. It is often used in a religious context to refer to the process of catechesis, or religious instruction. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of teaching or instruction.
