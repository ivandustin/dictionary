Word: κακείνος

Etymology:
The word κακείνος (kakeinos) is a Greek adjective that means "that one" or "that person". It is derived from the pronoun κεῖνος (keinos), which means "that" or "that one". The prefix κακ- (kak-) means "bad" or "evil", so κακείνος can also be translated as "that bad/evil person".

Grammar:
In Greek, κακείνος is a masculine singular adjective that agrees with the gender and number of the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it would become κακείνη (kakeini). It can also be declined in different cases, such as the nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative.

Description:
The word κακείνος is often used to refer to a person who is disliked or considered to be bad or evil. It can also be used to refer to an object or situation that is unpleasant or undesirable. In literature, it is often used to describe villains or antagonists.
