Word: κατηγορούμενος (katēgoroúmenos)

Etymology: The word κατηγορούμενος comes from the Greek verb κατηγορέω (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a passive participle ending, which means that the word κατηγορούμενος can be translated as "the one who is accused" or "the one who is charged".

Grammar: κατηγορούμενος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is a present passive participle, which means that it describes an ongoing action that is being done to the subject of the sentence. In this case, the subject is masculine and singular.

Description: κατηγορούμενος is a word that is often used in legal contexts to refer to someone who is accused of a crime. It can also be used more broadly to refer to someone who is being blamed or criticized for something. The word has a negative connotation, as it implies that the person in question has done something wrong or is at fault in some way.
