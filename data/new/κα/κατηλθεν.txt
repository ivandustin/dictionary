Word: κατηλθεν

Etymology:
The word κατηλθεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κατα- (kata-) meaning "down" and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". It is the aorist tense of the verb καταβαίνω (katabainō) which means "to go down" or "to descend".

Grammar:
κατηλθεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb καταβαίνω (katabainō) in the Greek language. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
κατηλθεν is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it went down" or "he/she/it descended". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the descent of Jesus Christ from heaven to earth. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe the act of going down or descending from a higher place to a lower place.
