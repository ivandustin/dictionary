Word: κατηρτισθαι

Etymology:
The word κατηρτισθαι comes from the Greek verb καταρτίζω (katartizō), which means "to put in order" or "to prepare." It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down," and the verb ἀρτίζω (artizō), meaning "to fit" or "to join."

Grammar:
κατηρτισθαι is a passive verb in the aorist tense and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -θαι (-thai) to the stem καταρτισ- (katartis-).

Description:
κατηρτισθαι is a Greek verb that means "to be made complete" or "to be fully equipped." It is often used in a spiritual or moral sense, referring to the process of becoming whole or perfect. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the work of God in transforming believers into the image of Christ. The word implies a process of preparation and training, as well as a goal or end result.
