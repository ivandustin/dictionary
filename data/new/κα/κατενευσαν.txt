Word: κατενευσαν

Etymology: κατενευσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and the verb ενευω (eneuo) meaning "to nod". Therefore, κατενευσαν means "to nod down" or "to nod in agreement".

Grammar: κατενευσαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb κατανεύω (katanévo), which means "to nod in agreement". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: κατενευσαν describes a group of people nodding in agreement. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when a group of people are nodding their heads in agreement, or in a figurative sense, such as when a group of people are expressing their approval or support for something.
