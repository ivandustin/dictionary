Word: καλέσω

Etymology:
The word καλέσω (kaleso) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The verb καλέσω is formed by adding the suffix -σω (-so) to the root word καλέω.

Grammar:
καλέσω is a first-person singular future tense verb in the Greek language. It is used to indicate an action that will happen in the future. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: καλέσω (kaleso) - I will call
- Plural: καλέσουμε (kalesoume) - We will call

Description:
The verb καλέσω is commonly used in everyday conversation in the Greek language. It is used to express the act of calling or summoning someone or something. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as calling someone on the phone, calling someone to come over, or calling someone's attention to something.
