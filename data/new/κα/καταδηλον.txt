Word: καταδηλον

Etymology: The word καταδηλον comes from the Greek words κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and δηλόω (dēloō), meaning "to show" or "to make clear." 

Grammar: καταδηλον is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is clearly evident or obvious.

Description: καταδηλον is a word that is often used in legal or formal contexts to describe something that is clearly and unmistakably evident. It can be used to describe evidence that is incontrovertible or to describe a situation that is beyond dispute. The word is often used in a negative context, to describe something that is clearly wrong or unjust.
