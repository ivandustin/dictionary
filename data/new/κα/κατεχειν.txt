Word: κατεχειν

Etymology:
The word κατεχειν comes from the Greek verb κατέχω (katechō), which is a combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἔχω (echō) meaning "to hold" or "to possess". Therefore, κατέχω (katechō) means "to hold down" or "to possess".

Grammar:
κατεχειν is the infinitive form of the verb κατέχω (katechō). In Greek, infinitives are formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem of the verb. The verb κατέχω (katechō) belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs that end in -ω (-ō).

Description:
κατεχειν is a Greek verb that means "to hold down" or "to possess". It can be used in various contexts, such as to describe physical possession of an object or to express control over a situation or a person. The verb is often used in the passive voice, as in "to be held down" or "to be possessed". In addition, κατεχειν can also be used in a figurative sense, such as to describe a strong emotion or feeling that has taken hold of someone.
