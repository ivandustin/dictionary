Word: καρδια

Etymology:
The word καρδια (kardia) comes from the Ancient Greek καρδία (kardía), which means "heart". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kerd- which means "heart" or "core".

Grammar:
In Greek, καρδια is a feminine noun. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as the subject or object of a sentence. For example, "Η καρδιά μου χτυπάει δυνατά" (I kardia mou chtypaei dynata) means "My heart beats strongly".

Description:
καρδια is a vital organ in the human body that pumps blood throughout the circulatory system. It is also often used metaphorically to refer to the center or core of something, such as "η καρδιά της πόλης" (i kardia tis polis) meaning "the heart of the city". In Greek culture, the heart is also associated with emotions and feelings, such as love, courage, and compassion.
