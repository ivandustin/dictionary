Word: κατακαυσαι

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down" or "against") + καυσαι (kausai, aorist infinitive of καίω, meaning "to burn"). Therefore, κατακαυσαι means "to burn down" or "to destroy by fire".

Grammar: κατακαυσαι is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. 

Description: κατακαυσαι is a Greek verb that means "to burn down" or "to destroy by fire". It is often used in the context of a building or structure being destroyed by flames. The word is composed of the prefix κατα- (kata-, meaning "down" or "against") and the verb καίω (kaio, meaning "to burn"). The aorist tense indicates that the action of burning down or destroying by fire has been completed in the past.
