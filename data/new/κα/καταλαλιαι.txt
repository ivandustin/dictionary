Word: καταλαλιαι

Etymology:
The word καταλαλιαι is derived from the Greek verb καταλαλέω (katalaleo), which means "to speak against" or "to slander". The suffix -ιαι (-iai) is a feminine plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple instances of slander or malicious talk.

In Greek, καταλαλιαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. It is declined as follows:

- Nominative: καταλαλία (katalalia) 
- Genitive: καταλαλίας (katalalias) 
- Accusative: καταλαλίαν (katalalian) 

Plural:
- Nominative: καταλαλιαι (katalaliai) 
- Genitive: καταλαλιων (katalaliōn) 
- Accusative: καταλαλιας (katalalias) 

Description:
καταλαλιαι refers to the act of speaking maliciously or slanderously about someone. It can also refer to the words or speech used in such an act. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to describe harmful gossip or rumors. In Greek literature, καταλαλιαι is often associated with the sin of slander and is condemned as a moral failing.
