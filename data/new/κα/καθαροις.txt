In terms of grammar, "καθαροις" is in the dative plural form, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. 

In the New Testament, "καθαροις" is often used to describe those who are pure in heart or morally clean. For example, in Matthew 5:8, Jesus says, "Blessed are the pure in heart, for they shall see God."
