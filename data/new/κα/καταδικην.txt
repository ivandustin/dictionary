Word: καταδικην

Etymology:
The word καταδικην comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and δίκη (dikē) meaning "judgment" or "trial". Therefore, καταδικην means "to condemn" or "to sentence".

Grammar:
καταδικην is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense and active voice. The conjugation of the verb depends on the subject of the sentence.

Description:
καταδικην is a strong word that is used to describe the act of condemning or sentencing someone. It is often used in legal contexts, such as in courtrooms or in discussions of criminal justice. The word carries a sense of finality and severity, as it implies that the person being condemned or sentenced is guilty of a serious offense.
