Word: καθισαντες

Etymology:
The word καθισαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθίζω (kathizó), which means "to sit down." The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates past tense and active voice. Therefore, καθισαντες means "having sat down" or "having taken a seat."

Grammar:
καθισαντες is a past participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe a group of people who have sat down or taken a seat.

Description:
καθισαντες is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (26:36) and the Gospel of Mark (14:32). In both instances, it is used to describe Jesus and his disciples as they enter the Garden of Gethsemane. The phrase καθισαντες εκει ("having sat down there") is used to indicate that they have stopped and taken a seat in the garden. The word is also used in other contexts to describe people who have sat down or taken a seat.
