Word: καθισταται

Etymology: καθισταται comes from the Greek verb καθίστημι (kathistēmi), which means "to set up, establish, appoint, or make someone or something stand."

Grammar: καθισταται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb καθίστημι. It is in the indicative mood and the active voice.

Description: καθισταται is used to describe the action of setting up or establishing something or someone. It can also be used to describe the appointment of someone to a position of authority or responsibility. In modern Greek, it is often used in the context of politics or business to describe the appointment of a person to a high-ranking position.
