Word: κατελθων

Etymology:
The word κατελθων is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, κατελθων means "to come down".

Grammar:
κατελθων is a participle form of the verb κατέρχομαι (katerchomai), which means "to come down". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
κατελθων is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the descent of the Holy Spirit upon Jesus during his baptism in the Jordan River. It is also used to describe the descent of Jesus into Hades after his death. In both cases, the word emphasizes the idea of coming down from a higher place to a lower one.
