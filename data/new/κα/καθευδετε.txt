Word: καθευδετε

Etymology: καθευδετε is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and εὕδω (eúdo) meaning "to sleep". Therefore, καθευδετε means "to sleep down" or "to lie down to sleep".

Grammar: καθευδετε is a present tense verb in the second person plural form. It is derived from the verb καθεύδω (katheúdo) which means "to sleep". The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: καθευδετε (kath-eu-de-te)
- Imperfect tense: καθευδετε (kath-eu-de-te)
- Future tense: θα καθευδετε (tha kath-eu-de-te)

Description: καθευδετε is a verb that refers to the act of sleeping or lying down to sleep. It is commonly used in everyday language to indicate the need for rest or to express the act of falling asleep. The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of inactivity or lack of progress.
