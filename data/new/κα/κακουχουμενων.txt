Word: κακουχουμενων

Etymology:
The word κακουχουμενων is a present participle form of the Greek verb κακουχέομαι (kakoucheomai), which means "to be ill-treated" or "to suffer hardship". The verb is derived from the adjective κακοῦχος (kakouchos), which means "ill-treated" or "oppressed". The prefix κακο- (kako-) means "bad" or "evil", while the suffix -ουχος (-ouchos) means "bearing" or "having".

Grammar:
κακουχουμενων is a present participle form of the verb κακουχέομαι (kakoucheomai), which is in the passive voice. The present participle form indicates that the action of being ill-treated or suffering hardship is ongoing or continuous. The word is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or association with another noun or pronoun.

Description:
κακουχουμενων can be translated as "of those who are being ill-treated" or "of those who are suffering hardship".
