Word: κατηργηται

Etymology: κατηργηται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ἀργός (argos), meaning "inactive" or "lazy," and the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "against." The verb κατηργηται means "to be made inactive" or "to be abolished."

Grammar: κατηργηται is a third-person singular present passive indicative form of the verb καταργέω (katargeo), which means "to render inoperative" or "to abolish." In this form, the verb indicates that the subject is being made inactive or abolished by an external force.

Description: κατηργηται is a verb that describes the process of something being made inactive or abolished. It is often used in a legal or bureaucratic context to describe the termination of a law or regulation. The word can also be used more broadly to describe the end of a practice or tradition.
