Word: κατισχυσητε

Etymology: The word κατισχυσητε is a compound word in Greek, formed by the prefix κατα- (meaning "down" or "against") and the verb ἰσχύω (ischyō) which means "to be strong" or "to prevail". The suffix -σητε (-sēte) is the second person plural aorist subjunctive active ending, indicating a command or wish.

Grammar: κατισχυσητε is a second person plural aorist subjunctive active verb in Greek. It is a command form, indicating that the speaker is urging or commanding a group of people to do something. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, while the subjunctive mood suggests that the action is uncertain or hypothetical.

Description: κατισχυσητε can be translated as "you (all) prevailed" or "you (all) were victorious". It is a strong and forceful command, urging a group of people to overcome an obstacle or to succeed in a particular task. The word has a sense of urgency and determination, suggesting that the speaker believes that success is possible and that the group should do everything in their power to achieve it.
