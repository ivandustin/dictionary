Word: κατεσταλμένους

Etymology: The word κατεσταλμένους comes from the Greek verb καταστέλλω (katastellō), which means "to suppress" or "to restrain". The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: κατεσταλμένους is a masculine accusative plural participle in Greek. It is derived from the verb καταστέλλω in the perfect passive voice. The participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: κατεσταλμένους describes a group of people who have been suppressed or restrained. The word implies that they have been forcibly prevented from acting or expressing themselves. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of protesters who have been silenced by the authorities or a group of rebels who have been defeated by a government.
