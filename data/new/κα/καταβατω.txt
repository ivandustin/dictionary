Word: καταβατω

Etymology:
The word καταβατω comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go". Therefore, καταβατω means "to go down" or "to descend".

Grammar:
καταβατω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The conjugation of the verb is as follows:

- Singular: καταβαίνω (katabaino) - I go down, you go down, he/she/it goes down
- Plural: καταβαίνουμε (katabainoume) - we go down, you go down, they go down

Description:
καταβατω is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the action of going down or descending. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as descending a mountain, going down a staircase, or lowering oneself from a higher position. The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe a decrease in quantity or quality, such as a decrease in temperature or a decline in health.
