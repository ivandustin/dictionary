Word: καυχησις

Etymology:
The word καυχησις (kauchēsis) comes from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kauchaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The suffix -σις (-sis) is a nominalizing suffix that turns the verb into a noun, so καυχησις means "boasting" or "bragging".

Grammar:
In Greek, καυχησις is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its genitive form is καυχήσεως (kauchēseōs), its dative form is καυχήσει (kauchēsei), and its accusative form is καυχήσιν (kauchēsin).

Description:
καυχησις refers to the act of boasting or bragging about one's accomplishments, abilities, or possessions. It can also refer to the attitude or behavior of someone who is excessively proud or arrogant. In the New Testament, the word is often used in a negative sense, as a warning against pride and self-promotion.
