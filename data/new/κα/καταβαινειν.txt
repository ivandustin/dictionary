Word: καταβαινειν

Etymology:
The word καταβαινειν comes from the Greek roots κατά (kata) meaning "down" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go". Therefore, καταβαινειν means "to go down".

Grammar:
καταβαινειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is derived from the present stem καταβαίν- (katabain-) and the infinitive ending -ειν (-ein).

Description:
καταβαινειν is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the action of going down or descending. It can be used in a literal sense, such as going down a staircase or descending a mountain, or in a figurative sense, such as a decrease in value or status. The verb can be conjugated to match the tense, voice, and mood of the sentence.
