Word: κατεβη

Etymology:
The word κατεβη (katebē) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and βαίνω (bainō) meaning "to go". Therefore, κατεβη means "to go down" or "to descend".

Grammar:
κατεβη is a third-person singular aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb καταβαίνω (katabainō), which means "to go down" or "to descend". In this form, κατεβη indicates that the action of going down or descending has already been completed in the past.

Description:
κατεβη is a verb that describes an action of going down or descending. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person going down a staircase, a bird descending from the sky, or a river flowing down a mountain. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the descent of Jesus Christ from heaven to earth.
