Word: καθευδουσιν

Etymology:
The word καθευδουσιν (kath-eudousin) is a present active indicative verb form in Greek. It is derived from the root word εὕδω (eudō), which means "to sleep". The prefix κατά (kata) means "down" or "under", so καθευδουσιν literally means "they are sleeping down/under".

Grammar:
καθευδουσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative verb form in Greek. It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present tense. In this case, it means "they are sleeping".

καθευδουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they are sleeping".
