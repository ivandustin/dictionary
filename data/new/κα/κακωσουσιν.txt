Word: κακωσουσιν

Etymology: The word κακωσουσιν is a verb form in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word κακός (kakós), which means "bad" or "evil". The verb form κακωσουσιν is in the future tense and active voice, and it means "they will harm" or "they will injure".

The verb is conjugated in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of the verb.

Description: κακωσουσιν is a strong and forceful verb that conveys the idea of causing harm or injury. It is often used in contexts where there is a threat of violence or danger. The word is commonly found in ancient Greek literature, particularly in epic poetry and tragedy.
