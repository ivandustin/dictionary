Word: καθαριζων

Etymology: καθαριζων is a present participle form of the Greek verb καθαρίζω (katharízō), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse". The word comes from the root word καθαρός (katharós), which means "pure" or "clean".

Grammar: καθαριζων is a present participle form of the verb καθαρίζω. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ων (-ōn) to the present stem of the verb. The present participle functions as an adjective, describing the subject of the sentence as performing the action of the verb.

Description: καθαριζων can be translated as "purifying" or "cleansing". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the act of purifying oneself or one's surroundings. In the New Testament of the Bible, καθαριζων is used to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples in purifying people and places. The word can also be used in a more general sense, such as in the context of cleaning or purifying water or other substances.
