Word: κατηντησεν

Etymology: κατηντησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἵημι (himi) meaning "send" or "let go". Therefore, κατηντησεν means "to arrive" or "to come down to".

Grammar: κατηντησεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb καταντάω (katantao), which means "to arrive" or "to come down to". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: κατηντησεν is a verb that describes the act of arriving or coming down to a place. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the arrival of a person or an object. In Greek mythology, it is often used to describe the descent of gods or heroes to the mortal world.
