Word: καυχάσθω

Etymology:
The word καυχάσθω comes from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". It is derived from the noun καύχημα (kaúkhēma), which means "boasting" or "bragging".

Grammar:
καυχάσθω is a present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. It is in the second person singular form, which means that it is addressing one person. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, the subject is being commanded to boast or brag about something.

Description:
καυχάσθω is a strong and assertive word that is often used in a negative context. It is usually associated with arrogance and pride, and is often used to criticize someone who is boasting or bragging excessively. However, in some contexts, it can also be used in a positive way to express confidence and self-assurance. Overall, καυχάσθω is a powerful word that conveys a strong sense of self-importance and self-promotion.
