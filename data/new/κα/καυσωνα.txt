Word: καυσωνα

Etymology:
The word καυσωνα (kausōna) comes from the Greek word καῦσον (kauson), which means "heat" or "burning". It is derived from the verb καίω (kaio), which means "to burn".

Grammar:
καυσωνα is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is masculine in gender.

Description:
καυσωνα refers to a heatwave or a period of intense heat. It is often used to describe the hot and dry weather that occurs during the summer months in Greece. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or event that is intense or overwhelming.
