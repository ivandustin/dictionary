Word: καθου

Etymology:
The word καθου (kathou) is a Greek adverb that means "upstairs" or "above". It is derived from the preposition κατά (kata), which means "down" or "below", and the adverb ύψω (ypsō), which means "up" or "high".

Grammar:
In Greek, καθου is an adverb that modifies verbs or adjectives. It is often used to indicate a location or position that is higher than the speaker or the subject of the sentence.

Description:
The word καθου is commonly used in Greek to describe a location or position that is above or upstairs. For example, one might say "Οι γραφείο μου είναι καθου" (Oi grafeio mou einai kathou) to indicate that their office is located upstairs. The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a higher level of authority or importance, as in the phrase "καθου αξίωμα" (kathou axioma), which means a high-ranking position or office.
