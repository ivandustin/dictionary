Word: καταφρονειτε

Etymology:
The word καταφρονειτε comes from the Greek verb καταφρονέω (kataphroneo), which means "to despise" or "to look down upon". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down", and the verb φρονέω (phroneo), meaning "to think" or "to have an opinion".

Grammar:
καταφρονειτε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is used to address a group of people and means "you despise" or "you look down upon".

Description:
καταφρονειτε is a strong word that conveys a sense of contempt or disdain towards someone or something. It implies a negative judgment or opinion about the worth or value of a person or thing. The word is often used in a moral or ethical context, to express disapproval of behavior that is considered to be arrogant, disrespectful, or unjust.
