Word: κατηρασω

Etymology:
The word κατηρασω (katērasō) is derived from the Greek word κατάρα (katara), which means "curse" or "malediction". The suffix -σω (-sō) is a verb ending that indicates the action of cursing.

Grammar:
κατηρασω is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the Greek verb καταράομαι (kataráomai), which means "to curse" or "to imprecate". It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω (-ō).

Description:
κατηρασω is a verb that describes the act of cursing or invoking a curse upon someone or something. It is often used in a religious or supernatural context, such as in the Bible or in ancient Greek mythology. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe a strong expression of disapproval or condemnation.
