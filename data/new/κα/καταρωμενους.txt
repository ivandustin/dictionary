Word: καταρωμένους

Etymology:
The word καταρωμένους comes from the Greek verb καταράομαι (kataráomai), which means "to curse" or "to imprecate". The word is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), which means "down" or "against", and the verb ἀράομαι (aráomai), which means "to pray" or "to invoke".

Grammar:
καταρωμένους is the present participle passive form of the verb καταράομαι. It is in the masculine accusative plural form, which means it refers to a group of people who are being cursed or imprecated.

The word καταρωμένους is used to describe a group of people who are being cursed or imprecated. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone is cursing another person or group of people, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone is expressing their anger or frustration towards a particular situation or circumstance.
