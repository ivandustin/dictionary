Word: καρπων

Etymology:
The word καρπων (karpon) is derived from the Greek word καρπός (karpos), which means "fruit" or "produce". It is related to the Latin word "carpere", which means "to pluck" or "to harvest".

Grammar:
καρπων is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and is singular. The genitive form of the word is καρπού (karpoú).

Description:
καρπων refers to a fruit or produce that has been harvested. It can also refer to the result or outcome of an action. In the New Testament, the word is often used metaphorically to refer to the fruits of one's labor or the results of one's actions. For example, in Galatians 5:22-23, the "fruit of the Spirit" is listed as love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control.
