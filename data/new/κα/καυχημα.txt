Word: καυχημα

Etymology:
The word καυχημα (kauchēma) is derived from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kauchaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The suffix -μα (-ma) is added to the verb to form a noun, which means "a boast" or "a bragging".

Grammar:
In Greek, καυχημα is a neuter noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is καυχημα, and its genitive singular form is καυχηματος (kauchēmatos). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
The word καυχημα refers to a boast or a bragging. It is often used in a negative sense to describe someone who is excessively proud or arrogant. The word can also be used in a positive sense to describe someone who is confident and proud of their accomplishments.
