Word: κατακεκριται

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down" or "against") + κεκρίται (kekritai, perfect passive indicative of κρίνω (krinō), meaning "to judge" or "to condemn"). Therefore, κατακεκριται means "has been judged/condemned".

Grammar: κατακεκριται is a third person singular perfect passive indicative verb in Greek. It is composed of the prefix κατα- (kata-) and the perfect passive indicative form of the verb κρίνω (krinō). The perfect tense indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: κατακεκριται is a Greek verb that means "has been judged/condemned". It is often used in a legal or religious context to describe someone who has been found guilty or condemned for a wrongdoing. The word carries a strong negative connotation and implies a serious offense or wrongdoing.
