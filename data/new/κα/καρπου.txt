The word "καρπου" (kar-poo) is a Greek noun that means "fruit" or "produce". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "καρπός" (kar-pos), which means "fruit" or "result". 

Grammar: "Καρπου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It can be translated as "of the fruit" or "of the produce". 

Description: The word can refer to any type of fruit or produce, such as apples, oranges, vegetables, or grains. It is often used in the context of agriculture or gardening.
