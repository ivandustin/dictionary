Word: κατασκοπους

Etymology:
The word κατασκοπους (kataskopous) is derived from the Greek words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and σκοπός (skopos) meaning "watcher" or "observer". Therefore, κατασκοπους literally means "those who watch against".

Grammar:
κατασκοπους is a plural noun in the masculine gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word κατασκοπους is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
κατασκοπους refers to spies or secret agents who are sent to gather information about an enemy or a rival. In ancient Greece, spies were commonly used in warfare and politics. They would infiltrate the enemy's territory and report back to their own side with valuable information about the enemy's plans and movements. The word κατασκοπους is still used in modern Greek to refer to spies or undercover agents.
