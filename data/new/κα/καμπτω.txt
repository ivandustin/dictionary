Word: καμπτω

Etymology:
The word καμπτω (kamptō) comes from the Greek verb κάμπτω (kamptō), which means "to bend" or "to curve". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kamp- which means "to bend" or "to crook".

Grammar:
καμπτω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It can be conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different meanings.

Description:
καμπτω is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to physical bending or curving, as in "I bend the wire" or "The road curves to the left". It can also be used metaphorically to indicate a change in direction or course, as in "I changed my mind" or "The company changed its strategy". Additionally, καμπτω can be used in a figurative sense to indicate submission or yielding, as in "I bend to your will" or "The tree bends in the wind".
