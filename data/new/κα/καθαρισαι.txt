Word: καθαρισαι

Etymology: καθαρισαι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word καθαρός (katharos), which means "clean" or "pure."

Grammar: καθαρισαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed at one person to perform a completed action in the past. The verb is in the active voice, which means that the subject is performing the action.

Description: καθαρισαι means "cleanse" or "purify" in English. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the act of purifying oneself from sin. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of cleaning or purifying something physical, such as a room or a piece of clothing.
