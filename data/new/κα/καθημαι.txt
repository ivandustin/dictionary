Word: καθημαι

Etymology:
The word καθημαι (kathēmai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κατά (katá) which means "down" or "according to" and the verb ἧμαι (hēmai) which means "to sit". Therefore, καθημαι means "to sit down" or "to be seated".

Grammar:
καθημαι is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is a first conjugation verb, which means that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs that end in -ω (-ō). The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: κάθομαι (káthomai) - I sit down, you sit down, he/she/it sits down
- Plural: καθόμαστε (kathómaste) - we sit down, you (plural) sit down, they sit down

Description:
καθημαι is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the action of sitting down or being seated. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in describing someone's posture or position, or in giving instructions to sit down. The verb is often used in combination with other verbs to describe more complex actions, such as καθήστε (kathíste) which means "sit down" or καθίζω (kathízō) which means "to seat" or "to place in a seat".
