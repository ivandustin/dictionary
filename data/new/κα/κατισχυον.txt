Word: κατισχυον

Etymology: The word κατισχυον comes from the Greek verb κατισχύω (katischýo), which means "to prevail over" or "to overcome". It is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb ἰσχύω (ischýo), meaning "to be strong" or "to have power".

Grammar: κατισχυον is the present participle of the verb κατισχύω in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a person or thing that is currently prevailing over or overcoming something.

Description: κατισχυον can be translated as "prevailing" or "overcoming". It describes a person or thing that is currently in a position of power or dominance over something else. For example, in the sentence "The army was κατισχυον over the enemy", κατισχυον would describe the army as currently prevailing over the enemy.
