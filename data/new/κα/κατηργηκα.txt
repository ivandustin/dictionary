Word: κατηργηκα

Etymology: κατηργηκα is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ἀργός (argos), meaning "inactive" or "lazy". The prefix κατ- (kat-) means "down" or "against", so κατηργηκα can be translated as "to make inactive" or "to render useless".

Grammar: κατηργηκα is a first-person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb καταργέω (katargeo), which means "to abolish" or "to nullify". In this form, it indicates that the action of nullifying or abolishing has been completed in the past.

Description: κατηργηκα is a strong and decisive verb that conveys the idea of putting an end to something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, from legal or political matters to personal relationships or habits. Its aorist tense suggests that the action of nullifying or abolishing has been completed in the past, and it implies a sense of finality and closure.
