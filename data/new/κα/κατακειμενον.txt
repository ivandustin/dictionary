Word: κατακείμενον

Etymology:
The word κατακείμενον comes from the Greek verb κατακεῖμαι (katakeimai), which means "to lie down" or "to recline". The word is formed from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down", and the verb κεῖμαι (keimai), which means "to lie".

Grammar:
κατακείμενον is a neuter singular participle in the present tense, passive voice, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb κατακεῖμαι (katakeimai), which is a compound verb formed from the prefix κατά (kata) and the verb κεῖμαι (keimai). The participle form κατακείμενον is used as an adjective to describe a noun.

Description:
κατακείμενον describes something that is lying down or reclining. It can be used to describe a person, an animal, or an object. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the position of a person or an animal, such as a lion or a dog, who is resting or sleeping. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is inactive or at rest.
