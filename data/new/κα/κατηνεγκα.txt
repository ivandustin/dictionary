The word "κατηνεγκα" (katēneka) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root verb "φέρω" (pherō), which means "to carry" or "to bring". 

The prefix "κατα-" (kata-) in this word adds the meaning of "down" or "away", so "κατηνεγκα" can be translated as "I brought down" or "I carried away". 

In terms of grammar, "κατηνεγκα" is a first-person singular form of the aorist tense, which means it is used to describe an action that was completed by the speaker in the past. 

Overall, "κατηνεγκα" is a specific form of the Greek verb "φέρω" that describes a completed action of carrying or bringing something down or away.
