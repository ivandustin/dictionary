Word: καταπιπτειν

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, meaning "down") + πιπτειν (piptein, meaning "to fall"). Therefore, καταπιπτειν means "to fall down" or "to collapse".

Grammar: καταπιπτειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is πιπτ- (pipt-). In the present tense, it conjugates as follows:

- Singular: καταπίπτεις (katapípteis, "you fall down"), καταπίπτει (katapíptei, "he/she/it falls down")
- Plural: καταπίπτουμε (katapíptoume, "we fall down"), καταπίπτετε (katapíptete, "you all fall down"), καταπίπτουν (katapíptoun, "they fall down")

Description: καταπιπτειν is a Greek verb that means "to fall down" or "to collapse". It is a common verb used in everyday language and can be used in various contexts, such as describing a physical collapse or a metaphorical one, such as a business or a relationship. Its conjugation is relatively simple and follows the first conjugation of Greek verbs.
