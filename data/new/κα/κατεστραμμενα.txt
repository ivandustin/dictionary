Word: κατεστραμμένα

Etymology:
The word κατεστραμμένα is derived from the Greek verb καταστρέφω (katastréfo), which means "to destroy" or "to ruin". The word is formed by adding the suffix -μένα (-mena) to the verb stem κατεστραμμ- (katastramm-).

Grammar:
κατεστραμμένα is the plural form of the adjective κατεστραμμένος (katastrammenos), which means "destroyed" or "ruined". The word is in the feminine gender and in the nominative case, agreeing with a feminine plural noun.

Description:
κατεστραμμένα is an adjective that describes something that has been destroyed or ruined. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as buildings or roads, or abstract concepts, such as relationships or reputations. The word carries a negative connotation and implies that the thing being described is in a state of disrepair or irreparable damage.
