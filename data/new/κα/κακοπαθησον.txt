Word: κακοπαθησον

Etymology: κακο- (kako-) meaning "bad" or "evil" and πάθος (pathos) meaning "suffering" or "passion". Therefore, κακοπαθησον means "to suffer evil" or "to experience hardship".

Grammar: κακοπαθησον is a verb in the aorist imperative mood, second person singular form. It is derived from the verb κακοπαθέω (kakopathéo), which means "to suffer evil" or "to experience hardship".

Description: κακοπαθησον is a command or request to someone to endure or bear with a difficult situation or hardship. It can also be used as an encouragement to persevere through a challenging time.
