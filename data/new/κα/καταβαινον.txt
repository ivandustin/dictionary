Word: καταβαινον

Etymology:
The word καταβαινον (katabainon) is a present participle form of the Greek verb καταβαίνω (katabaino), which is derived from the prefix κατά (kata) meaning "down" and the verb βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go". Therefore, καταβαίνω (katabaino) means "to go down" or "to descend".

Grammar:
καταβαινον (katabainon) is a present participle form of the verb καταβαίνω (katabaino) in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. As a present participle, it indicates an ongoing action of "going down" or "descending".

Description:
καταβαινον (katabainon) can be translated as "the one who is going down" or "the one who is descending". It is often used in the New Testament to describe the descent of Jesus Christ from heaven to earth. For example, in John 3:13, it is written, "No one has ascended into heaven except the one who descended (καταβαίνων), the Son of Man."
