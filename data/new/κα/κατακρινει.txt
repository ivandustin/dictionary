Word: κατακρινει

Etymology:
The word κατακρινει (katakrinei) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb κρίνω (krino), meaning "to judge" or "to decide." Therefore, κατακρινει can be translated as "to condemn" or "to judge against."

Grammar:
κατακρινει is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the third person singular form. 

Description:
κατακρινει is a strong verb that is often used in legal contexts to express the act of condemning or judging someone or something as guilty or wrong. It can also be used in a more general sense to express disapproval or criticism of someone or something. 

Example sentence: Ο δικαστής κατακρίνει τον κατηγορούμενο για την εγκληματική πράξη. (The judge condemns the defendant for the criminal act.)
