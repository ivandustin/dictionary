Word: καυχησώνται

Etymology: The word καυχησώνται comes from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The suffix -σώνται (-sóntai) is the third person plural present middle/passive voice ending.

Grammar: καυχησώνται is a verb in the present tense, third person plural, middle/passive voice. It is derived from the verb καυχάομαι and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: καυχάμαι (kauchámai) - I boast, καυχάσαι (kauchásai) - you boast, καυχάται (kauchátai) - he/she/it boasts
- Plural: καυχόμαστε (kauchómaste) - we boast, καυχάσθε (kauchásthe) - you boast, καυχώνται (kauchóntai) - they boast

Description: καυχησώνται is a Greek verb that means "they boast" or "they brag". It is often used to describe someone who is excessively proud or arrogant. The word can be used in a positive or negative context, depending on the speaker's tone and intention.
