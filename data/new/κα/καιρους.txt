Word: καιρους

Etymology:
The word καιρους (kairous) is a Greek noun in the accusative plural form. It is derived from the Greek word καιρός (kairos), which means "time" or "season". The word καιρός is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *k̑er-, which means "to cut" or "to separate".

The word καιρους is used in Greek to refer to "times" or "seasons". It can be used to describe a specific period of time or a particular season of the year. The word is often used in a metaphorical sense to refer to a critical or opportune moment, or a moment of decision.
