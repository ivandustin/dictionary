Word: καθημενον

Etymology: καθημενον is a Greek word that comes from the verb καθέζομαι (kathézomai), which means "to sit down". The word καθημενον is the present participle of this verb, meaning "sitting" or "seated".

Grammar: καθημενον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. In this form, it can be used as a predicate adjective, describing the subject of a sentence.

Description: καθημενον is a word that describes something or someone that is in a seated position. It can be used to describe a person who is sitting down, or an object that is designed for sitting, such as a chair or a bench. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the posture of characters in a scene, conveying a sense of stillness or repose.
