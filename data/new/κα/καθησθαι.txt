Word: καθησθαι

Etymology:
The word καθησθαι (kathēsthai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κάθημαι (kathēmai), which means "to sit". It is derived from the combination of the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down", and the verb ἵστημι (histēmi), meaning "to stand". The prefix κατά (kata) in this case indicates a downward motion or position, hence the meaning "to sit".

Grammar:
καθησθαι (kathēsthai) is a present tense, passive voice, infinitive form of the verb κάθημαι (kathēmai). In Greek, the infinitive form is used to express the idea of an action without specifying the subject or the time of the action. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now.

Description:
καθησθαι (kathēsthai) means "to be seated" or "to sit down". It is a common verb in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts, such as in descriptions of physical posture, as well as in figurative expressions. For example, it can be used to describe someone sitting in a chair, or to describe a group of people sitting together. It can also be used in expressions such as "to sit in judgment" or "to sit at the feet of someone", which have a more figurative meaning.
