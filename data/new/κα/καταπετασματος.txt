Word: καταπετασματος

Etymology:
The word καταπετασματος comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and πέτασμα (petasma) meaning "something spread out" or "a covering". Together, καταπετασματος means "a covering that is spread out or hung down".

Grammar:
καταπετασματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun. In this case, it is the genitive form of καταπέτασμα (katapétasma), which means "curtain" or "veil". 

Description:
καταπετασματος refers to a hanging or covering that is used to conceal or separate something. In ancient Greek culture, it was often used to refer to the veil that separated the holy of holies in the temple from the rest of the sanctuary. In modern usage, it can refer to any type of curtain or hanging that is used for decorative or functional purposes.
