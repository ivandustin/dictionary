Word: καρδιαν

Etymology: The word καρδιαν comes from the Greek word καρδία (kardía), which means "heart". The suffix -αν (-an) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: καρδιαν is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: καρδιαν is an adjective that describes something as "related to the heart". It can be used to describe physical or emotional aspects of the heart. For example, καρδιαν πόνος (kardian ponos) means "heartache" or "heartbreak", while καρδιαν αρτηριακή νόσος (kardian arteriaki nosos) means "cardiovascular disease".
