Word: καιαφας

Etymology:
The origin of the word καιαφας is uncertain. It is believed to be a Greek transliteration of the Aramaic word "qayyafa," which means "deceitful" or "crooked." It is also possible that the word has a Hebrew origin, as it appears in the New Testament as the name of the high priest who presided over the trial of Jesus.

Grammar:
In Greek, καιαφας is a proper noun and is declined as such. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word does not have a plural form.

Description:
καιαφας is a name that appears in the New Testament, specifically in the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John. He is described as the high priest who presided over the trial of Jesus and played a significant role in his crucifixion. The name has become synonymous with betrayal and deceit.
