The word "κατακλυσμου" (kataklusmou) is a Greek noun that means "flood" or "deluge". It is derived from the verb "κατακλύζω" (kataklýzo), which means "to flood" or "to inundate". In Greek mythology, the most famous flood is the one caused by Zeus to punish humanity, which was survived only by Deucalion and Pyrrha. 

In terms of grammar, "κατακλυσμου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. It is the genitive singular form of the noun "κατακλυσμός" (kataklusmós).

Overall, "κατακλυσμου" is a word that refers to a catastrophic event of flooding, and is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe overwhelming or destructive situations.
