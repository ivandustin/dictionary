Word: κατηγοροις

Etymology:
The word κατηγοροις comes from the Greek word κατηγορέω (kategoreo), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The suffix -οις (-ois) is a plural ending in the dative case, indicating that the word is being used as the indirect object of a verb.

Grammar:
In Greek, κατηγοροις is a noun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb. It is a plural form, indicating that there are multiple objects being accused or charged.

Description:
The word κατηγοροις refers to the individuals or groups who are being accused or charged with a crime or wrongdoing. It is often used in legal contexts, such as in court proceedings or in discussions of criminal investigations. The word carries a negative connotation, as it implies that the accused individuals are guilty of some offense.
