Word: καυχασθε

Etymology:
The word καυχασθε (kaukhasthe) is a verb in the present imperative form in Greek. It comes from the root word καυχάομαι (kaukháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The word is derived from the noun καύχημα (kaúkhēma), which means "boasting" or "bragging".

Grammar:
καυχασθε is a second-person plural present imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -σθε (-sthe) to the root word καυχάομαι (kaukháomai).

Description:
The word καυχασθε (kaukhasthe) is used in Greek to tell a group of people to boast or brag. It is often used in a negative context, as boasting is generally seen as a negative trait. The word can also be used in a positive context, such as when encouraging someone to be proud of their accomplishments.
