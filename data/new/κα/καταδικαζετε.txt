Word: καταδικάζετε

Etymology: The word καταδικάζετε comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and δικάζω (dikazo) meaning "to judge" or "to condemn". 

Grammar: καταδικάζετε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is the formal or polite way of saying "you condemn" in Greek. 

Description: καταδικάζετε means "to condemn" or "to sentence" someone to punishment or disapproval. It is often used in legal contexts, such as in court cases or when discussing criminal behavior. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe disapproving or criticizing someone's actions or behavior.
