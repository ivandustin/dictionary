Word: καταισχυνθηι

The word καταισχυνθηι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αἰσχύνομαι (aischynomai), which means "to feel shame or disgrace." The prefix κατα- (kata-) adds the meaning of "down" or "against," so καταισχυνθηι can be translated as "to be put to shame" or "to be disgraced."

καταισχυνθηι is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. καταισχυνθηι is a strong verb that conveys a sense of shame or disgrace.
