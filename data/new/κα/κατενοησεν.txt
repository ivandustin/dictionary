Word: κατενοησεν

Etymology: κατενοησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "according to" and νοέω (noeo) meaning "to perceive" or "to understand". Therefore, κατενοησεν means "to understand thoroughly" or "to comprehend completely".

Grammar: κατενοησεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is he/she/it. The verb is also in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: κατενοησεν is a strong verb that implies a deep level of understanding or comprehension. It is often used in a spiritual or philosophical context to describe a profound realization or insight.
