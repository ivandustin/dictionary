The word "καιομενης" (kaiomenes) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "καίω" (kaio), which means "to burn". The participle form indicates that the burning action is ongoing or in progress. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *kewH- which means "to burn". 

Grammar: "καιομενης" is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: The word can be translated as "burning" or "being burned". It can be used to describe a physical object that is currently on fire, or a metaphorical situation that is intense or passionate. For example, "το κτίριο είναι καιομένο" (to ktirio einai kaiomeno) means "the building is burning", while "η καρδιά μου είναι καιομένη από πάθος" (i kardia mou einai kaiomeni apo pathos) means "my heart is burning with passion".
