Word: καινην

Etymology:
The word καινην (kainēn) is a feminine singular form of the Greek adjective καινός (kainos), which means "new" or "fresh". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kaino-, meaning "new" or "recent".

Grammar:
In Greek, καινην is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It can also be used as the object of a preposition.

Description:
καινην is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe various things, such as a new covenant (Luke 22:20), a new commandment (John 13:34), a new creation (2 Corinthians 5:17), and a new Jerusalem (Revelation 21:2). It emphasizes the idea of something being fresh, different, or renewed.
