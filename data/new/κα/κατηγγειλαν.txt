Word: κατηγγειλαν

Etymology:
The word κατηγγειλαν (katēngeilan) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἀγγελία (angelia) meaning "message" or "announcement". Therefore, κατηγγειλαν means "to accuse" or "to denounce".

Grammar:
κατηγγειλαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb κατηγγέλλω (katēngellō), which means "to accuse" or "to denounce". In this form, it refers to a past action that was performed by a group of people.

Description:
κατηγγειλαν is a strong and forceful word that is used to describe the act of accusing or denouncing someone or something. It implies a serious and formal accusation that is made with the intention of bringing attention to a wrongdoing or a crime. The word is often used in legal or political contexts, where accusations and denunciations are common.
