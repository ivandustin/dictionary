The word "κατεναρκησα" is a Greek verb that means "I made numb" or "I caused to be numb". It is derived from the prefix "κατά" meaning "down" or "against" and the verb "ναρκάω" meaning "I make numb". In terms of grammar, it is a first person singular aorist active indicative verb, indicating that the action was completed in the past by the speaker.
