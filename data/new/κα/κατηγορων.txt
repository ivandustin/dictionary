Word: κατηγορων

Etymology:
The word κατηγορων comes from the Greek verb κατηγορέω (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a present participle ending, indicating that κατηγορων is the present participle form of κατηγορέω.

Grammar:
In Greek, κατηγορων is the present participle form of the verb κατηγορέω. It is a masculine noun in the nominative case, and it means "accuser" or "prosecutor". 

Description:
κατηγορων is a term used in legal contexts to refer to someone who accuses or charges another person with a crime or offense. In ancient Greece, the role of the κατηγορων was an important one in the legal system, as they were responsible for presenting evidence and arguments against the accused. The term is still used in modern Greek to refer to a prosecutor in a court of law.
