Word: καταλυθησεται

Etymology: καταλυθησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb καταλύω (katalýo), which means "to destroy" or "to overthrow". The word is formed by adding the suffix -θησεται (-thésetai), which is a future passive indicative form.

Grammar: καταλυθησεται is a third-person singular future passive indicative form of the verb καταλύω (katalýo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon.

Description: καταλυθησεται can be translated as "it will be destroyed" or "it will be overthrown". The word implies a sense of finality and inevitability, as if the destruction or overthrow is certain to happen. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as the destruction of a building or the overthrow of a government, or in a figurative sense, such as the downfall of a person or an idea.
