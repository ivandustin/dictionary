Word: κατελθόντων

Etymology:
The word κατελθόντων is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". The verb form κατελθόντων is in the genitive plural, which means "of those who came down".

Grammar:
The word κατελθόντων is a verb in the genitive plural form. It is a participle, which means it functions as an adjective that describes the subject of the sentence. In this case, it describes a group of people who came down.

The word κατελθόντων is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Luke (10:30). The full sentence is "καταβαίνοντα τινὰ ἀπὸ Ἱερουσαλὴμ εἰς Ἰεριχώ, καὶ λῃσταῖς περιέπεσεν, οἳ καὶ ἐκδύσαντες αὐτὸν καὶ πληγὰς ἐπιθέντες ἀπῆλθον ἀφέντες ἡμιθανῆ" (katabainonta tina apo Hierousalēm eis Ierichō, kai lēstaīs periepesen, hoi kai ekdysantes auton kai plēgas epithentes apēlthon aphentes hēmithanē). The sentence describes a man who was traveling from Jerusalem to Jericho and was attacked by robbers who stripped him, beat him, and left him half dead.
