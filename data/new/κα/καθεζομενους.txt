Word: καθεζομενους

Etymology: καθεζομενους is a present participle form of the Greek verb καθέζομαι (kathézomai), which means "to sit down". The word is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), meaning "down", and the verb έζομαι (ézomai), meaning "to sit".

Grammar: καθεζομενους is a present participle in the masculine accusative plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently sitting down.

Description: καθεζομενους can be translated to "sitting" or "those who are sitting". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe a group of people who are gathered together, such as in Mark 2:2: "And many were gathered together, so that there was no more room, not even at the door. And he was preaching the word to them."
