Word: καιροις

Etymology:
The word καιροις (kairois) is a plural form of the Greek noun καιρός (kairos), which means "a moment in time" or "an opportune time". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *k̑er- which means "to cut" or "to separate".

Grammar:
In Greek, καιροις (kairois) is a masculine noun in the dative plural case. It is often used in the New Testament to refer to specific times or seasons.

Description:
The word καιροις (kairois) is used in the New Testament to refer to specific times or seasons that are significant in the context of God's plan for salvation. For example, in Mark 1:15, Jesus proclaims, "The time (καιρος) is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand; repent and believe in the gospel." In this context, καιρος refers to the specific moment in history when Jesus began his public ministry and announced the coming of the kingdom of God. The word is also used in other contexts to refer to specific seasons or periods of time, such as "the appointed time" or "the right time".
