The word "κακολογων" is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "κακός" (kakós), meaning "bad" or "evil," and "λέγω" (légo), meaning "to speak" or "to say." Therefore, "κακολογων" means "speaking badly" or "slandering." 

In terms of grammar, "κακολογων" is a present participle, which means it describes an ongoing action. It can be used as an adjective to describe someone who is constantly speaking badly about others. 

Overall, "κακολογων" is a negative term that implies someone who is not trustworthy or respectful towards others.
