Word: καταργουμενου

Etymology: καταργουμενου is a present participle of the Greek verb καταργέω (katargeo), which means "to abolish" or "to make ineffective". The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb ἀργέω (argeo), meaning "to be idle" or "to be inactive".

Grammar: καταργουμενου is a masculine singular genitive form of the present participle of the verb καταργέω. It is used to describe a noun in the genitive case, indicating that the noun is being abolished or made ineffective by the action of the verb.

Description: καταργουμενου can be translated as "being abolished" or "being made ineffective".
