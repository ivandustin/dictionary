Word: κατεποθη

Etymology:
The word κατεποθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πίνω (pino), which means "to drink." The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "against," so κατεποθη can be translated as "to drink down" or "to swallow."

Grammar:
κατεποθη is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
κατεποθη is a strong verb that is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of swallowing or consuming something. It is used in a metaphorical sense to describe the way that death "swallows up" or consumes life (1 Corinthians 15:54).
