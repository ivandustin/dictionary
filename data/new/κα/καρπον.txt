Word: καρπόν

Etymology:
The word καρπόν (karpon) is derived from the Greek word καρπός (karpos), which means "fruit" or "produce". It is related to the Latin word "carpere", which means "to pluck" or "to harvest".

Grammar:
In Greek, καρπόν is a masculine noun in the accusative case. It can also be spelled as καρπός in the nominative case.

Description:
καρπόν refers to a fruit or produce that is harvested from a plant. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the results or benefits of one's labor or efforts. In the New Testament, it is often used in a spiritual sense to refer to the fruits of the Holy Spirit.
