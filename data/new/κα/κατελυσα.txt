The word κατελυσα (katelusa) is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the root word λυω (luo), which means "to loosen" or "to release." The prefix κατα (kata) adds the meaning of "down" or "completely."

In terms of grammar, κατελυσα is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the first person singular form, meaning that the subject of the sentence is "I."

The word κατελυσα can be translated to mean "I completely loosened" or "I completely released." It is often used in a figurative sense to mean "I put an end to" or "I destroyed."
