Word: καταπατεισθαι

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down") + πατεῖν (patein, "to tread, step on")

Grammar: καταπατεῖσθαι is a passive infinitive verb form in Ancient Greek, meaning "to be trampled down". It is derived from the verb καταπατέω (katapateō), which means "to tread down, trample upon".

Description: καταπατεῖσθαι is a word that describes the action of being trampled down or stepped on. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to someone being trampled by a crowd, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing someone being oppressed or mistreated. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to describe a situation where someone is being treated unfairly or unjustly.
