Word: καλυμμα

Etymology:
The word καλυμμα (kalymma) comes from the Greek verb καλύπτω (kalypto), which means "to cover" or "to conceal". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-, which means "to cover" or "to hide".

Grammar:
In Greek, καλυμμα is a neuter noun. Its plural form is καλύμματα (kalymmata). It is declined like other neuter nouns in the second declension.

Description:
καλυμμα refers to a cover or a lid that is used to conceal or protect something. It can refer to a cover for a container, a lid for a pot or pan, or a cover for a book or document. It can also refer to a cover for a part of the body, such as a cap or a helmet. In modern Greek, it can also refer to a cover for a phone or tablet.
