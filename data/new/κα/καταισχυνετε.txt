Word: καταισχυνετε

Etymology: The word καταισχυνετε comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and αἰσχύνη (aischynē) meaning "shame" or "disgrace". Therefore, καταισχυνετε means "to shame" or "to disgrace".

Grammar: καταισχυνετε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb καταισχύνω (kataischýnō) which means "to put to shame" or "to disgrace".

Description: καταισχυνετε is a command form of the verb καταισχύνω, which means "to shame" or "to disgrace". It is used to tell a group of people to feel ashamed or to cause someone else to feel ashamed. The word is often used in a moral or ethical context, such as shaming someone for their behavior or actions.
