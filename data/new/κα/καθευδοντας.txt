Word: καθευδοντας

Etymology:
The word καθευδοντας (kath-eudontas) is a present participle form of the Greek verb καθεύδω (kath-eudō), which means "to sleep". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "under", and εὕδω (eudō), meaning "to sleep".

Grammar:
In Greek, καθευδοντας is a present participle form of the verb καθεύδω. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The present participle form indicates that the action of sleeping is ongoing or in progress.

Description:
καθευδοντας describes someone who is currently sleeping or in the act of sleeping. It can be used as an adjective to describe a person or as a verb to indicate an action. For example, "Ο άντρας καθευδώντας στο κρεβάτι" (O ánthras kath-eudóntas sto kreváti) means "The man sleeping in the bed".
