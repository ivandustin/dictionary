Word: κατηγορουσιν

Etymology:
The word κατηγορουσιν (katēgorousin) is a verb in the present active indicative form of the Greek word κατηγορέω (katēgoreō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: κατά (kata), which means "against", and ἀγορεύω (agoreuō), which means "to speak in public". Therefore, κατηγορέω literally means "to speak against in public".

Grammar:
The word κατηγορουσιν is a verb in the present active indicative form, third person plural. This means that it refers to a group of people who are currently accusing or charging someone or something. In Greek grammar, the present tense is used to describe actions that are currently happening or are habitual.
