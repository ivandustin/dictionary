Word: καταβαντες

Etymology:
The word καταβαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb καταβαίνω (katabainō), which means "to go down" or "to descend". The word is formed by adding the suffix -τες (-tes) to the present participle stem καταβαίν- (katabain-).

Grammar:
καταβαντες is a masculine plural participle in the nominative case. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to modify nouns or act as independent verbs. The present participle indicates an ongoing action or state, and the masculine plural form indicates that the subject is a group of males.

Description:
καταβαντες can be translated as "those who are going down" or "those who are descending". The word is used in various contexts in Greek literature, including the New Testament, where it is used to describe the descent of Jesus from heaven to earth. In modern Greek, the word is still used to describe someone who is going down a staircase or descending a hill.
