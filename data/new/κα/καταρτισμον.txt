Word: καταρτισμόν

Etymology: The word καταρτισμόν comes from the Greek verb καταρτίζω (katartizō), which means "to put in order" or "to prepare." The suffix -μόν (-mon) is a common ending in Greek that forms abstract nouns.

Grammar: καταρτισμόν is a masculine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: καταρτισμόν refers to the act of preparing or putting something in order. It can also refer to the state of being well-prepared or well-organized. The word is often used in a military context to describe the preparation and organization of troops or equipment for battle. In a broader sense, it can refer to any kind of preparation or organization, such as preparing a meal or organizing a project.
