Word: κατακρίσεως

Etymology: The word κατακρίσεως comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and κρίσις (krisis) meaning "judgment" or "decision". Together, κατακρίσεως means "condemnation" or "judgment against".

Grammar: κατακρίσεως is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun. In this case, it is used to indicate the possession of the quality of "condemnation" or "judgment against".

Description: κατακρίσεως refers to the act of condemning or judging someone or something as wrong or guilty. It can also refer to the sentence or punishment that is given as a result of this condemnation. The word is often used in legal or religious contexts, but can also be used in everyday language to describe a strong disapproval or criticism of someone or something.
