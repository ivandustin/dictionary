Word: καταγγελεύς

Etymology: The word καταγγελεύς comes from the Greek verb καταγγέλλω (katangello), which means "to accuse" or "to denounce". The suffix -εύς (-eus) is a common Greek suffix used to form agent nouns, indicating a person who performs a certain action.

Grammar: καταγγελεύς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word καταγγελεύς can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive καταγγελέως (katangeleos) or the accusative καταγγελέα (katangelea).

Description: καταγγελεύς refers to a person who accuses or denounces someone else, usually in a legal or political context. The word can also be used more broadly to describe someone who exposes or reveals something that was previously hidden or secret. In modern Greek, the word καταγγελεύς is often used in the context of journalism or activism, to describe someone who reports on or exposes corruption, injustice, or other wrongdoing.
