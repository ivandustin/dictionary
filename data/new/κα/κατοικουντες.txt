Word: κατοικούντες

Etymology: κατοικούντες is a present participle of the Greek verb κατοικέω (katoikeo), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "according to", and οἰκέω (oikeo), meaning "to dwell" or "to live in".

Grammar: κατοικούντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is formed by adding the suffix -ούντες (-ountes) to the stem κατοικ- (katoik-), which is the present tense stem of the verb κατοικέω (katoikeo).

Description: κατοικούντες refers to a group of people who are currently dwelling or inhabiting a particular place. It can be translated as "those who dwell" or "the inhabitants". The word is often used in a geographical or historical context to refer to the people who live in a specific region or city.
