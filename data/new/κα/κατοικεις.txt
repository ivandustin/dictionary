Word: κατοικεις

Etymology: κατοικεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word οἰκέω (oikeo), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". The prefix κατα- (kata-) adds the meaning of "down" or "below", so κατοικεις can be translated as "to dwell below" or "to inhabit a place".

Grammar: κατοικεις is a second-person singular present active indicative verb in Greek. It is often translated as "you dwell" or "you inhabit". The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: κατοικεις
- Imperfect tense: κατῴκεις (katōkeis)
- Future tense: κατοικήσεις (katoikēseis)
- Aorist tense: κατῴκησας (katōkēsas)
- Perfect tense: κατώκηκας (katōkēkas)

Description: κατοικεις is a verb that describes the act of dwelling or inhabiting a place. It can be used to refer to a physical location, such as a house or a city, or to a more abstract concept, such as a state of mind or a social group. In the New Testament, κατοικεις is often used in reference to the Holy Spirit dwelling within believers.
