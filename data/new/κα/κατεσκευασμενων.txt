Word: κατεσκευασμένων

Etymology: The word κατεσκευασμένων is derived from the Greek verb κατασκευάζω (kataskeuázō), which means "to construct" or "to build". The suffix -μένων (-menon) is a participle ending that indicates a passive voice and a plural number.

Grammar: κατεσκευασμένων is a masculine noun in the genitive plural form. It is used to describe something that has been constructed or built.

Description: κατεσκευασμένων is often used in technical or engineering contexts to refer to objects or structures that have been manufactured or assembled. It can also be used more broadly to describe anything that has been created or put together, such as a work of art or a piece of writing. In Greek, the word has a connotation of precision and careful craftsmanship.
