Word: καυχωμεθα

Etymology:
The word καυχωμεθα comes from the Greek verb καυχαομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". It is derived from the noun καυχησις (kauchēsis), which means "boasting" or "bragging".

Grammar:
καυχωμεθα is a first-person plural present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ομεθα (-ometha) to the stem καυχ- (kauch-), which is the first person plural present active indicative form of the verb καυχαομαι (kaucháomai).

Description:
καυχωμεθα is a Greek verb that means "we boast" or "we brag". It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is overly proud or arrogant. The verb is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the sin of boasting or bragging about one's own accomplishments or abilities.
