Word: καθαρισον

Etymology: καθαρισον is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθαρός (katharos), meaning "clean" or "pure". The verb form καθαρισον means "cleanse" or "purify".

Grammar: καθαρισον is a second person singular aorist imperative active verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed at one person to perform an action in the past. The verb is in the active voice, meaning that the subject is performing the action of cleansing or purifying.

Description: καθαρισον is a powerful word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts. It is a command to purify oneself or to cleanse oneself of impurities, whether physical or spiritual. The word can also be used in a more literal sense, such as cleaning a physical object or space. Overall, καθαρισον is a word that emphasizes the importance of purity and cleanliness in both a physical and spiritual sense.
