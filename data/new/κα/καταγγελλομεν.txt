Word: καταγγελλομεν

Etymology:
The word καταγγελλομεν (katangelomen) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἀγγέλλω (angello) meaning "to announce" or "to report". Therefore, καταγγελλομεν means "to accuse" or "to denounce".

Grammar:
καταγγελλομεν is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb καταγγέλλω (katangello) which means "to accuse" or "to denounce". The present participle form indicates that the action of accusing or denouncing is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
καταγγελλομεν is a Greek verb that is commonly used in legal contexts to refer to the act of accusing or denouncing someone of a crime or wrongdoing. It can also be used in a broader sense to refer to the act of reporting or exposing something that is considered wrong or unethical. The word is often used in the media to describe the act of whistleblowing or reporting on corruption or other illegal activities.
