Word: καταλειφθηναι

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down, against") + λείπω (leipō, "to leave") + -θηναι (-thēnai, a passive infinitive suffix). Therefore, καταλειφθηναι means "to be left behind, abandoned, or deserted."

Grammar: καταλειφθηναι is a passive infinitive verb in Ancient Greek. It belongs to the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: καταλειφθηναι is a word that describes a state of being left behind or abandoned. It can be used to describe a physical object or a person who has been deserted or neglected. In a figurative sense, it can also describe a feeling of being left out or forgotten. The word has a sense of finality to it, as if the abandonment is permanent and irreversible.
