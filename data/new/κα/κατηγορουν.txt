Word: κατηγορουν

Etymology:
The word κατηγορουν (katēgoroun) is derived from the Greek verb κατηγορέω (katēgoreō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". It is composed of two parts: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against", and ἀγορεύω (agoreuō), which means "to speak in public" or "to address a public assembly".

Grammar:
κατηγορουν is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb κατηγορέω (katēgoreō). It is an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of accusing or charging someone or something.

Description:
κατηγορουν is a verb that is often used in legal or formal contexts to describe the act of accusing or charging someone with a crime or wrongdoing. It can also be used more broadly to describe any situation in which someone is making a strong and specific accusation against someone or something. In Greek mythology, the word is often used to describe the accusations made against various gods and heroes, such as Prometheus and Oedipus.
