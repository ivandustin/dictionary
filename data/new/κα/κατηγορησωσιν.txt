Word: κατηγορησωσιν

Etymology:
The word κατηγορησωσιν is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form of the word κατηγορέω (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: κατά (katá), which means "against", and ἀγορεύω (agoreúō), which means "to speak in public". Therefore, κατηγορέω literally means "to speak against in public".

Grammar:
The word κατηγορησωσιν is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, the future tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the sentence is a group of people who will perform the action of accusing or charging.

Description:
The word κατηγορησωσιν describes the action of a group of people accusing or charging someone with a crime or wrongdoing. It implies a formal accusation made in a public setting, such as a court of law or a public hearing. The word has a negative connotation, as it suggests that the accused has done something wrong and is being held accountable for their actions.
