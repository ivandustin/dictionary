Word: κατηντησαν

Etymology:
The word κατηντησαν (katentisan) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The prefix κατά (kata) means "down" or "against". Therefore, κατηντησαν can be translated as "they arrived" or "they came down to".

Grammar:
κατηντησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb καταντάω (katantao), which means "to arrive" or "to come down to". In this form, it indicates that the action of arriving or coming down to was completed in the past.

Description:
κατηντησαν is a verb that describes the act of arriving or coming down to a place. It is often used in a literal sense, such as when someone arrives at a destination or comes down from a higher place. However, it can also be used in a figurative sense, such as when someone arrives at a conclusion or comes down from a position of power.
