Word: καλέσας

Etymology: καλέσας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". It is a past tense form of the verb, specifically the aorist tense.

Grammar: καλέσας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb καλέω. This means that it is used to describe an action that was completed in the past by the person being addressed. In English, it would be translated as "you called" or "you summoned".

Description: καλέσας is a verb that describes an action of calling or summoning someone or something. It is a past tense form, indicating that the action has already been completed. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action.
