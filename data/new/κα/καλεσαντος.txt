Word: καλεσαντος

Etymology: καλεσαντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The suffix -αντος (-antos) is a participle ending that indicates past action, so καλεσαντος means "having called" or "having summoned".

Grammar: καλεσαντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive singular form of the participle καλέσας (kalesas), which is the aorist active participle of the verb καλέω (kaleo). In this form, καλεσαντος is used to indicate possession or association with the action of calling or summoning.

Description: καλεσαντος can be translated as "of the one who called" or "of the one who summoned". It is often used in Greek literature to refer to a person who has called or summoned someone else, such as a leader or a god. In the New Testament, it is used to refer to Jesus Christ as the one who calls or summons his followers.
