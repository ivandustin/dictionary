Word: καταλυσωσιν

Etymology: καταλυσωσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καταλύω (katalyō), which means "to dissolve, to break up, to destroy." The suffix -σωσιν (-sōsin) is the third person plural present subjunctive active ending, indicating that the verb is in the subjunctive mood and refers to an action that may or may not happen.

Grammar: καταλυσωσιν is a third person plural present subjunctive active form of the verb καταλύω. In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the active voice means that the subject is performing the action.

Description: καταλυσωσιν can be translated as "they may dissolve, break up, or destroy." The verb is often used in a metaphorical sense, such as "to overthrow a government" or "to bring an end to something."
