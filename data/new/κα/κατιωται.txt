Word: κατιωται

Etymology:
The word κατιωται is derived from the Greek verb κατιόω (katioo), which means "to curse" or "to execrate". It is formed from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb ἰόω (ioo), meaning "to go". The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates a plural form.

Grammar:
κατιωται is a third-person plural present active indicative form of the verb κατιόω (katioo). It is used to describe a group of people who are currently cursing or execrating someone or something.

Description:
κατιωται can be translated as "they curse" or "they execrate". It is often used in the context of expressing strong disapproval or condemnation towards someone or something.
