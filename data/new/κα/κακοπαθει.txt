The word "κακοπαθει" is a Greek verb that means "to suffer evil or hardship". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "κακός" (kakós) meaning "bad" or "evil" and "πάθος" (páthos) meaning "suffering" or "passion".

Grammar: "κακοπαθει" is a third-person singular present active indicative verb in Greek.

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is experiencing hardship or misfortune. It can also be used to describe the act of enduring or persevering through difficult circumstances.
