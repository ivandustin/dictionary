Word: κατηλθον

Etymology:
The word κατηλθον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, κατηλθον means "to come down".

Grammar:
κατηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb καταβαίνω (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
κατηλθον is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the descent of Jesus Christ from heaven to earth. It is also used in other contexts to describe the act of coming down from a higher place to a lower place.
