Word: καθευδων

Etymology:
The word καθευδων (kath-eu-don) is a present participle of the Greek verb καθεύδω (kath-eu-dō), which means "to sleep". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down", and the verb εὕδω (eu-dō), meaning "to sleep". 

Grammar:
καθευδων is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe someone who is currently sleeping. 

Description:
καθευδων is a Greek word that means "sleeping" or "one who is sleeping". It is often used in literature and poetry to describe a peaceful or restful state. In Greek mythology, Hypnos, the god of sleep, is often depicted as καθευδων. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is inactive or dormant.
