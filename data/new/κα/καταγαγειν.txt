Word: καταγαγειν

Etymology:
The word καταγαγειν comes from the Greek roots κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἄγειν (agein) meaning "to lead" or "to bring". Therefore, καταγαγειν means "to bring down" or "to lead down".

Grammar:
καταγαγειν is an infinitive verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense, which indicates a past action that is completed. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
καταγαγειν is a Greek verb that means "to bring down" or "to lead down". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as bringing something or someone down from a higher place, or leading someone down a path or road. The verb is often used in a figurative sense as well, such as bringing down an opponent in an argument or debate.
