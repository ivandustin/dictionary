Word: κατακυριεύσας

Etymology: The word κατακυριεύσας comes from the Greek verb κατακυριεύω (katakyrieuo), which means "to subdue completely" or "to conquer". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "completely", and the verb κυριεύω (kyrieuo), meaning "to rule" or "to dominate".

Grammar: κατακυριεύσας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. It is the masculine singular form of the verb, which means "he who completely subdued" or "he who conquered completely". 

Description: κατακυριεύσας is a powerful and forceful word that conveys the idea of complete domination and subjugation. It is often used in a military or political context to describe the conquest of a territory or the defeat of an enemy. In a broader sense, it can also be used to describe the complete mastery or control of a situation or circumstance.
