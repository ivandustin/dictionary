Word: κακους

Etymology:
The word κακους (kakous) is a Greek adjective that means "bad" or "evil". It is derived from the Greek word κακός (kakós), which means "bad" or "evil".

Grammar:
In Greek, κακους is the masculine accusative plural form of the adjective κακός. It is used to describe a group of male nouns that are the direct object of a sentence.

Description:
The word κακους is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe evil or wickedness. It can also be used to describe something that is of poor quality or undesirable. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to describe a person who is unpleasant or difficult to deal with.
