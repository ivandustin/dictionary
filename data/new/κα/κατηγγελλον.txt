The word "κατηγγελλον" (katangelon) is a Greek verb that means "to accuse" or "to denounce". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and "ἀγγέλλω" (angello), meaning "to announce" or "to report". 

Grammar: "κατηγγελλον" is the present active participle form of the verb "κατηγγέλλω" (katangello), which means "I accuse" or "I denounce". It is in the masculine singular form. 

Description: The word "κατηγγελλον" is often used in legal contexts to describe the act of formally accusing someone of a crime or wrongdoing. It can also be used more broadly to describe any situation where someone is publicly calling out or condemning another person's actions or behavior.
