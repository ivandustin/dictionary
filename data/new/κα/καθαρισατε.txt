Word: καθαρισατε

Etymology: καθαρισατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθαρίζω (katharizo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse". It is derived from the adjective καθαρός (katharos), which means "clean" or "pure".

Grammar: καθαρισατε is the second person plural aorist imperative form of the verb καθαρίζω (katharizo). This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people, telling them to perform the action of purifying or cleansing. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, and the imperative mood indicates that it is a command.

Description: καθαρισατε is a strong and forceful command in Greek, urging a group of people to purify or cleanse something. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to instruct people to purify themselves or their surroundings. The word carries a sense of urgency and importance, emphasizing the need for immediate action.
