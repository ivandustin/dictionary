Word: κατενεχθεις

Etymology:
The word κατενεχθεις is a Greek word that is derived from the verb κατέχω (katechō), which means "to hold down" or "to possess." The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "against," and the verb εχω (echō) means "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar:
κατενεχθεις is a past participle in the passive voice. It is formed by adding the prefix κατα- (kata-) to the verb εχω (echō) and adding the suffix -θεις (-theis) to indicate the passive voice. The word is masculine, singular, and nominative in form.

Description:
The word κατενεχθεις can be translated as "held down" or "possessed." It is often used in a negative sense, to describe someone who is oppressed or burdened by something. In Christian theology, the word is used to describe the state of being possessed by demons or evil spirits.
