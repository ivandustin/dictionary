Word: καυχησασθαι

Etymology:
The word καυχησασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a reflexive ending that indicates the action is being done to oneself.

Grammar:
καυχησασθαι is a second aorist middle infinitive verb in Ancient Greek. The second aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description:
The word καυχησασθαι means "to boast about oneself" or "to brag about oneself". It is often used in a negative context, as boasting is generally seen as a negative trait. In Ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe characters who are arrogant or overconfident.
