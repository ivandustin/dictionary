The word κατηγορουντων is derived from the Greek verb κατηγορέω (katēgoréō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The suffix -ουντων (-ountōn) is the present active participle form of the verb, indicating that the action of accusing or charging is currently taking place.

κατηγορουντων is a present active participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek grammar, the present active participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action. κατηγορουντων can be translated as "accusing" or "charging" in English. It is often used in legal or formal contexts to describe the act of making an accusation or bringing charges against someone.
