Word: καθημενων

Etymology: καθημενων is the present participle of the Greek verb καθέζομαι (kathézomai), which means "to sit down". It is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), meaning "down", and the verb έζομαι (ézomai), meaning "to sit".

Grammar: καθημενων is a masculine, plural, genitive form of the present participle of the verb καθέζομαι. In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state, and the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: καθημενων can be translated as "of those who are sitting" or "of those who sit". It is often used in the New Testament to describe a group of people who are sitting down, such as in Mark 14:54: "And Peter followed him afar off, even into the palace of the high priest: and he sat with the servants, and warmed himself."
