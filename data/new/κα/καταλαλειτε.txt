Word: καταλαλείτε

Etymology: The word καταλαλείτε comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and λαλέω (laleo) meaning "to speak". Therefore, καταλαλείτε means "to speak against" or "to slander".

Grammar: καταλαλείτε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb καταλαλέω (katalaleo) which means "to speak against" or "to slander". The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Καταλαλείτε (katalaleite) - you speak against/slander (plural)

Description: καταλαλείτε is a Greek verb that means to speak against or to slander someone. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is spreading false or harmful information about another person. The verb is commonly used in legal contexts to describe the act of making false accusations or spreading rumors with the intent to harm someone's reputation.
