Word: κατεδραμεν

Etymology:
The word κατεδραμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δραμεν, which means "to run." The prefix κατ- in Greek means "down," so κατεδραμεν means "to run down."

Grammar:
κατεδραμεν is the aorist tense of the Greek verb κατατρέχω (katatrecho), which means "to run down." The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
κατεδραμεν is a word that describes the act of running down, or chasing after something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as running down a hill, or in a figurative sense, such as pursuing a goal or ambition. The word is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the pursuit of a lover or the chase of a prey.
