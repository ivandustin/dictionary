Word: κατεπλευσαν

Etymology: κατεπλευσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "towards" and πλέω (pleo) meaning "to sail". Therefore, κατεπλευσαν means "they sailed down" or "they sailed towards".

Grammar: κατεπλευσαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb καταπλέω (katapleo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: κατεπλευσαν is a verb that describes the action of sailing down or towards a destination. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a ship sailing towards a port or a group of people sailing down a river. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and history to describe naval expeditions and voyages.
