The word "κατασχωμεν" is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, and subjunctive mood. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "κατά" meaning "down" or "against" and the verb "σχέω" meaning "to hold" or "to possess". 

Grammar: The verb is conjugated in the first person plural, indicating that the subject is "we". The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action is hypothetical or uncertain. 

Description: The word "κατασχωμεν" can be translated to mean "let us be held down" or "let us be restrained". It implies a sense of being trapped or confined against one's will.
