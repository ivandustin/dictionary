The word καθισας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθίζω (kathizó), which means "to sit down". It is a past participle form of the verb καθίζω, which means "having sat down".

καθισας is a past participle form of the verb καθίζω, which is in the aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The past participle form is used to describe an action that has already been completed.

καθισας can be translated to English as "having sat down". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus or other characters.
