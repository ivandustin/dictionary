Word: κατακολουθησασαι

Etymology:
The word κατακολουθησασαι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "according to" and κολουθέω (kolouthéo) meaning "to follow". Therefore, κατακολουθησασαι means "she followed according to" or "she followed closely".

Grammar:
κατακολουθησασαι is a third-person singular aorist active indicative feminine form of the verb κολουθέω (kolouthéo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The feminine gender indicates that the subject is a female.

Description:
κατακολουθησασαι is a specific form of the verb κολουθέω (kolouthéo) that describes a female subject who followed closely or according to something or someone in the past. The context of the sentence would determine what or who she followed.
