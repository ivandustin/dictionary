Word: καταλειπόντες

Etymology:
The word καταλειπόντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and λείπω (leipo) meaning "to leave" or "to abandon". The verb is in the present participle form, which means "leaving behind" or "abandoning".

Grammar:
The word καταλειπόντες is a present participle verb in the masculine plural form. It is derived from the verb καταλείπω (kataleipo) which means "to leave behind" or "to abandon". The present participle form indicates an ongoing action, so καταλειπόντες means "those who are leaving behind" or "those who are abandoning".

Description:
The word καταλειπόντες is often used in a negative context to describe people who are abandoning something or someone. It can be used to describe people who are leaving behind their responsibilities, duties, or obligations. It can also be used to describe people who are abandoning their friends, family, or community. The word has a sense of finality to it, as if the action of leaving behind is complete and irreversible.
