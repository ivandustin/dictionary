Word: κατηχουντι

Etymology:
The word κατηχουντι comes from the Greek verb κατηχέω (katecheo), which means "to instruct" or "to teach." The suffix -ουντι (-ounti) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently instructing or teaching.

Grammar:
κατηχουντι is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and singular number. It is derived from the verb κατηχέω, which is a first-conjugation verb in Greek.

Description:
The word κατηχουντι refers to someone who is currently instructing or teaching. It can be used to describe a teacher or instructor in any subject or field. In Christian contexts, it is often used to refer to someone who is instructing others in the doctrines and teachings of the Christian faith.
