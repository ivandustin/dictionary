Word: κατειληφεναι

Etymology:
The word κατειληφεναι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and εἴληφα (eilēpha) meaning "to take hold of" or "to seize". Therefore, κατειληφεναι means "to seize down" or "to take hold of against".

Grammar:
κατειληφεναι is a verb in the Greek language, in the present perfect tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the prefix κατά (kata) to the verb εἴληφα (eilēpha), which means "to take hold of" or "to seize".

Description:
The word κατειληφεναι is used to describe the act of seizing or taking hold of something or someone in a forceful manner. It can also be used to describe the act of overpowering or subduing someone or something. The word is often used in legal contexts to describe the arrest or detention of a suspect.
