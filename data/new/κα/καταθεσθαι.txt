Word: καταθέσθαι

Etymology: καταθέσθαι comes from the Greek words κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "according to," and τίθημι (tithemi), meaning "to place" or "to put." Therefore, καταθέσθαι means "to place down" or "to deposit."

Grammar: καταθέσθαι is a verb in the Greek language, and it is in the present tense, middle or passive voice, and infinitive mood.

Description: καταθέσθαι is a Greek verb that means "to deposit" or "to place down." It can be used in various contexts, such as depositing money in a bank, placing an object on a surface, or submitting a document to an authority. The verb is often used in legal or financial contexts, but it can also be used in everyday language.
