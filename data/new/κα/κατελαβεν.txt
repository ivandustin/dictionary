Word: κατελαβεν

Etymology:
The word κατελαβεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to seize". The prefix κατα- (kata-) adds the meaning of "down" or "completely", so κατελαβεν can be translated as "to completely seize" or "to take down".

Grammar:
κατελαβεν is the aorist tense of the Greek verb καταλαμβάνω (katalambanō), which means "to seize" or "to overtake". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any indication of the duration or repetition of the action.

Description:
κατελαβεν is a strong and forceful verb that conveys the idea of taking something down or seizing it completely. It can be used in a literal sense, such as taking control of a city or capturing an enemy, or in a figurative sense, such as understanding a difficult concept or grasping a new idea.
