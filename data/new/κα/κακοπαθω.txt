Word: κακοπαθώ

Etymology: The word κακοπαθώ comes from the Greek words κακός (kakós) meaning "bad" or "evil" and πάθος (páthos) meaning "suffering" or "passion". 

Grammar: κακοπαθώ is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb κακοπαθέω (kakopathéo), which means "to suffer evil" or "to be ill-treated". 

Description: κακοπαθώ is a word that describes the act of suffering or enduring hardship. It can be used to describe physical or emotional suffering, and is often used in a negative context. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to express the idea of enduring pain or hardship.
