Word: κατακλιθηναι

Etymology:
The word κατακλιθηναι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "towards" and κλίνω (klinō) meaning "to lean" or "to recline". The word is derived from the ancient Greek language.

Grammar:
κατακλιθηναι is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive mood is used to express the idea of an action without specifying the subject or tense.

Description:
The word κατακλιθηναι means "to be laid down" or "to be reclined". It is often used in the context of a person lying down or reclining, such as on a bed or couch. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of rest or relaxation.
