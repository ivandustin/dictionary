Word: καλοί

Etymology: καλοί is a plural form of the Greek adjective καλός (kalós), which means "good" or "beautiful". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European root *kal-, which means "to be strong" or "to be beautiful".

Grammar: καλοί is a masculine plural form of the adjective καλός. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, καλοί is used to describe masculine plural nouns.

Description: καλοί is a Greek adjective that is commonly used to describe people or things that are good or beautiful. It can also be used to describe moral qualities, such as kindness or virtue. In modern Greek, καλοί is often used as a colloquial expression to mean "cool" or "awesome".
