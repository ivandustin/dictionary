Word: κατακρημνισαι

Etymology:
The word κατακρημνισαι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and κρημνός (kremnos) meaning "cliff" or "precipice". Therefore, κατακρημνισαι means "to fall down a cliff" or "to be thrown down a precipice".

Grammar:
κατακρημνισαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb in ancient Greek. It is derived from the verb κρημνίζω (kremnizo) which means "to hurl down a cliff". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the imperative mood is used to give commands or orders.

Description:
κατακρημνισαι is a strong and vivid verb that describes a violent and sudden action of falling down a cliff or being thrown down a precipice. It is often used in literature and poetry to create dramatic and intense scenes. The word conveys a sense of danger, fear, and helplessness, as the person falling down the cliff has no control over their fate.
