Word: κατεφθαρμένοι

Etymology:
The word κατεφθαρμένοι (katephtharménoi) is a Greek adjective that comes from the verb καταφθείρω (katafthéiro), which means "to corrupt" or "to destroy." The prefix κατά (kata) means "down" or "against," and the root word φθείρω (fthéiro) means "to ruin" or "to spoil."

Grammar:
κατεφθαρμένοι is a plural masculine adjective in the nominative case. It agrees with a masculine plural noun in gender, number, and case.

Description:
The word κατεφθαρμένοι is used to describe something or someone that has been corrupted or ruined. It can refer to physical decay or moral corruption. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe people who have been corrupted by false teachings or immoral behavior.
