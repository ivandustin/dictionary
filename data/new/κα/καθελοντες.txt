Word: καθελοντες

Etymology: καθελοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb καθαιρέω (kathaireo), which means "to remove" or "to take away".

Grammar: καθελοντες is the present participle form of καθαιρέω (kathaireo), which means "removing" or "taking away". It is in the masculine gender and plural number.

Description: καθελοντες is a word that describes a group of people who are currently in the process of removing or taking away something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a military operation where soldiers are removing obstacles or in a construction site where workers are taking away debris.
