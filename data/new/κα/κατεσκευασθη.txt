Word: κατεσκευασθη

Etymology:
The word κατεσκευασθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σκευαζω (skeuazo), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready." The prefix κατα (kata) means "down" or "according to," so κατεσκευασθη can be translated as "was prepared" or "was made ready."

Grammar:
κατεσκευασθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it can be inferred from the context.

Description:
κατεσκευασθη is a verb that describes the action of something being prepared or made ready. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as preparing a meal, constructing a building, or organizing an event. The word implies that the preparation or construction has been completed, and the subject is now ready for use or consumption.
