Word: καυχωμένοι

Etymology:
The word καυχωμένοι (kaukhomenoi) is the present participle of the verb καυχάομαι (kaukháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag" in Greek. The verb comes from the noun καύχημα (kaúkhēma), which means "boasting" or "bragging."

Grammar:
καυχωμένοι is a masculine plural nominative present participle in Greek. It is derived from the verb καυχάομαι, which means "to boast" or "to brag." The present participle form indicates that the action of boasting or bragging is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
καυχωμένοι is a Greek word that means "boasting" or "bragging." It is often used to describe people who are excessively proud or who like to show off their accomplishments. The word can have a negative connotation, implying that the person is arrogant or vain. In Greek literature, the word is often used in a moralizing context, warning against the dangers of excessive pride and boasting.
