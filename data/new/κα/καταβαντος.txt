Word: καταβαντός

Etymology:
The word καταβαντός is derived from the Greek verb καταβαίνω (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend". The suffix -τός (-tos) is added to the verb stem to form an adjective, which means "having gone down" or "having descended".

Description:
καταβαντός is an adjective that describes something or someone that has gone down or descended. It can be used to describe physical movement, such as going down a hill or descending a staircase, or it can be used metaphorically to describe a decline in status or reputation. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe heroes who have descended into the underworld or who have gone on a journey to the underworld.
