Word: καιρου

Etymology:
The word καιρου (kairou) is a genitive form of the Greek noun καιρός (kairos), which means "time" or "season". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *k̑er- which means "to cut" or "to separate". In ancient Greek, καιρός was used to refer to a specific moment in time, a critical point, or an opportune time.

Grammar:
In Greek, καιρου (kairou) is a genitive singular form of καιρός (kairos). The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and in this case, it indicates that something belongs to or is related to a specific time or season.

Description:
The word καιρου (kairou) refers to a specific moment in time or a season. It can be used to describe a particular point in time that is significant or opportune, or to refer to a specific season of the year. The word is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a critical moment or a turning point in a situation or event.
