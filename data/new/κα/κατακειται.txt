Word: κατακείται

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down") + κείται (keítai, present tense of κεῖμαι (keîmai), meaning "to lie down"). Therefore, κατακείται means "to lie down (in a downward position)".

Grammar: κατακείται is a present tense verb in Greek, third person singular. It belongs to the conjugation of verbs that end in -μαι (-mai), which are called "middle/passive voice" verbs. In this case, κατακείται is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is performing the action on itself.

Description: κατακείται is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of lying down in a downward position. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person or an animal lying down, or the position of an object that is lying flat on the ground. It is often used in combination with other verbs to describe a specific action, such as κατακείμενος (katakéimenos), which means "lying down" in a more general sense.
