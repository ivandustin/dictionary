Word: κατηλλαγημένος (katēllagēmenos)

Etymology: The word κατηλλαγημένος comes from the Greek verb καταλλάσσω (katallassō), which means "to reconcile" or "to make peace". The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice.

Grammar: κατηλλαγημένος is a masculine singular nominative participle in Greek. It is declined like an adjective, agreeing in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: κατηλλαγημένος is a word that describes someone who has been reconciled or made peace with someone else. It can also be used to describe something that has been restored to its original state or condition. In Christian theology, the word is often used to describe the reconciliation between God and humanity through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ.
