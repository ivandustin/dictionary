The word "καλην" (kalēn) is a Greek adjective in the feminine accusative singular form. It is derived from the root word "καλός" (kalos), which means "beautiful" or "good". 

In terms of grammar, "καλην" is used to describe a feminine noun in the accusative case. It can also be used as a predicate adjective, meaning it can be used to describe the subject of a sentence.

Example sentences:
- Η καλήν κόρην είδα στο πάρτι. (I saw a beautiful girl at the party.)
- Η καλήν μουσική με χαλαρώνει. (Good music relaxes me.)

Overall, "καλην" is a versatile adjective that can be used to describe a variety of feminine nouns and subjects.
