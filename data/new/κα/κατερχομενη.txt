The word "κατερχομενη" (katerchomenē) is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It is derived from the verb "κατέρχομαι" (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend". 

In terms of grammar, "κατερχομενη" is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently descending or coming down from a higher place.

Overall, "κατερχομενη" is a descriptive word that emphasizes the action of descending or coming down. Its etymology can be traced back to the ancient Greek language, where it was commonly used in various contexts.
