Word: καιρόν

The word καιρόν (kairón) comes from the Greek root καιρός (kairós), which means "time" or "season". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *k̑er- which means "to cut" or "to separate". The word καιρόν has been used in Greek literature since ancient times.

καιρόν refers to a specific point in time or a particular season. It can also refer to an opportune or decisive moment.
