Word: καθιστησιν

Etymology: The word καθιστησιν comes from the Greek verb καθίστημι (kathístēmi), which means "to set up" or "to establish."

Grammar: καθιστησιν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender.

Description: καθιστησιν refers to the act of setting up or establishing something. It can also refer to the result of this action, such as a foundation or a structure. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the establishment of a system or a government.
