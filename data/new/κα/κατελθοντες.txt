Word: κατελθόντες

Etymology: κατελθόντες is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata), which means "down," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come." Therefore, κατελθόντες means "coming down" or "descending."

Grammar: κατελθόντες is the present participle of the verb κατέρχομαι (katerchomai), which means "to come down" or "to descend." As a present participle, it functions as an adjective and describes a continuous action. In this case, it describes a group of people who are continuously coming down or descending.

Description: κατελθόντες is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (28:18). In this passage, it describes the disciples of Jesus who are coming down from a mountain after witnessing his transfiguration. The word can also be used more generally to describe any group of people who are descending from a higher place to a lower one.
