Word: καλείται

Etymology: καλείται is a conjugated form of the Greek verb καλώ (kaló), which means "to call" or "to name". The verb comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-, which means "to call" or "to cry out".

Grammar: καλείται is the third person singular present indicative form of the verb καλώ. It is used to refer to someone or something that is being called or named in the present tense. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ καλώ (I call)
- Εσύ καλείς (You call)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό καλείται (He/She/It is called)
- Εμείς καλούμε (We call)
- Εσείς καλείτε (You call)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά καλούν (They call)

Description: καλείται is a common verb in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to refer to someone's name, as in "Το όνομά του καλείται Νίκος" (His name is Nikos). It can also be used to refer to someone being called or summoned, as in "Τον καλούν για φαγητό" (They are calling him for dinner).
