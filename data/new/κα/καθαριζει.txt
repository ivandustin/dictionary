Word: καθαριζει

Etymology: καθαριζει is a verb in the Greek language, derived from the root word καθαρός (katharos), which means "clean" or "pure". The verb καθαριζει (katharizei) means "to clean" or "to purify".

Grammar: καθαριζει is a present tense, third person singular verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated from the root verb καθαρίζω (katharizo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify". The verb is often used in the context of religious or spiritual purification.

Description: καθαριζει is a verb that describes the act of cleaning or purifying something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as cleaning a physical object, or in a metaphorical sense, such as purifying one's thoughts or intentions.
