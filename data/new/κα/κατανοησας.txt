Word: κατανοησας

Etymology: κατανοησας is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "through," and νοέω (noeo), which means "to perceive" or "to understand." Therefore, κατανοησας means "to understand thoroughly" or "to comprehend."

Grammar: κατανοησας is the second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb κατανοέω (katanoeo). In Greek, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is completed, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. Therefore, κατανοησας means "you understood" or "you comprehended."

Description: κατανοησας is a verb that expresses the act of understanding or comprehending something thoroughly. It implies a deep level of understanding and insight into a particular subject or situation. The word is often used in philosophical and intellectual contexts, where a thorough understanding of complex ideas is required.
