Word: κατειργασατο

Etymology: κατειργασατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εργαζομαι (ergazomai), which means "to work" or "to labor." The prefix κατα (kata) adds the meaning of "down" or "completely," so κατειργασατο can be translated as "worked down" or "completely worked."

Grammar: κατειργασατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. 

Description: κατειργασατο can be translated as "he/she/it worked down" or "he/she/it completely worked." The verb implies a sense of thoroughness or completion in the work that was done. It can be used in a literal sense, such as someone working a piece of land until it is completely cultivated, or in a figurative sense, such as someone working through a difficult problem until it is completely solved.
