Word: κατελαβομην

Etymology:
The word κατελαβομην is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to seize". The prefix κατα- (kata-) in this word means "down" or "against". Therefore, κατελαβομην can be translated as "I took hold of" or "I seized".

Grammar:
κατελαβομην is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb καταλαμβάνω (katalambanō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or asking a question. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
κατελαβομην is a verb that describes an action of taking hold of or seizing something. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe various actions, such as taking hold of a person (Mark 9:27), seizing a city (Acts 17:5), or grasping a concept (Philippians 3:12). The word conveys a sense of urgency and determination, as if the subject is actively pursuing and grasping something that is important to them.
