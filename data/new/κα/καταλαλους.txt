Word: καταλαλους

Etymology:
The word καταλαλους is derived from the Greek word καταλαλέω (katalaleo), which means "to speak against" or "to slander". The suffix -ους (-ous) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
καταλαλους is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It modifies a noun and describes a person who speaks against or slanders others.

Description:
καταλαλους is a Greek word that describes a person who speaks against or slanders others. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who spreads false or harmful information about others. The word can be used to describe someone who is malicious, gossipy, or untrustworthy.
