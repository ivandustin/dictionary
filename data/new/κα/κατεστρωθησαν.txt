Word: κατεστρωθησαν

Etymology: κατεστρωθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against," and στρώνω (strono), which means "to spread" or "to lay out." Therefore, κατεστρωθησαν means "to be laid down" or "to be arranged against."

Grammar: κατεστρωθησαν is a passive verb in the aorist tense, third person plural. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon.

Description: κατεστρωθησαν is a verb that describes the action of something being laid down or arranged against. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the preparation of a ship for sailing or the arrangement of objects in a particular order. The passive voice of the verb suggests that the subject is not actively involved in the action, but rather is being acted upon by an external force.
