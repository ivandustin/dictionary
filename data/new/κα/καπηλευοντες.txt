Word: καπηλευοντες

Etymology: The word καπηλευοντες comes from the Greek verb καπηλεύω (kapēleúō), which means "to be a retail trader" or "to be a huckster". The word is derived from the noun κάπηλος (kápēlos), which means "a retail trader" or "a huckster".

Grammar: καπηλευοντες is the present participle form of the verb καπηλεύω (kapēleúō). It is in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. The present participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Description: καπηλευοντες refers to a group of people who are engaged in the activity of retail trading or huckstering. The word can be used to describe individuals who sell goods in a market or on the street, or those who engage in other forms of retail trade. The word has a slightly negative connotation, suggesting that the individuals in question may be engaged in shady or unethical business practices.
