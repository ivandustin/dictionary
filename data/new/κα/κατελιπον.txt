Word: κατελιπον

Etymology:
The word κατελιπον is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λείπω (leipo), which means "to leave" or "to abandon". The prefix κατα (kata) adds the meaning of "completely" or "thoroughly", so κατελιπον means "I left completely" or "I abandoned thoroughly".

Grammar:
κατελιπον is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb καταλείπω (kataleipo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
κατελιπον is a strong and decisive verb that conveys the idea of leaving something behind completely and without any intention of returning. It can be used in a literal sense, such as leaving a place or a person, or in a figurative sense, such as abandoning a project or a belief. The verb is often used in the New Testament to describe Jesus' disciples leaving everything behind to follow him.
