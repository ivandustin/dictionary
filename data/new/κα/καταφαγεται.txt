Word: καταφαγεται

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down" or "against") + φαγεῖν (phagein, "to eat"). Therefore, καταφαγεται means "to eat down" or "to devour".

Grammar: καταφαγεται is a verb in the third person singular form of the aorist tense in the Greek language. It is a passive voice verb, indicating that the subject is being acted upon.

Description: καταφαγεται is a strong verb that conveys the idea of consuming something completely or devouring it. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a situation where something is completely destroyed or consumed. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the destruction of the wicked in the final judgment.
