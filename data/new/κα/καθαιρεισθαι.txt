Word: καθαιρεισθαι

Etymology:
The word καθαιρεισθαι comes from the Greek verb καθαιρέω (kathaireo), which means "to remove, to take away, to cleanse." It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "away," and the verb αἱρέω (haireo), which means "to take, to choose."

Grammar:
καθαιρεισθαι is a passive verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is formed by adding the suffix -σθαι (-sthai) to the stem καθαιρει- (kathairei-). The verb is in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description:
καθαιρεισθαι means "to be removed, to be taken away, to be cleansed." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the purification of the soul or the removal of sin. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to the removal of something unwanted or undesirable.
