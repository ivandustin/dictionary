Word: κατοικείτε

Etymology: κατοικείτε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κατοικέω (katoikeo), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". The verb is in the present tense and second person plural form.

Grammar: κατοικείτε is a regular verb in Greek, and it belongs to the first conjugation. It is a second person plural form of the present tense, which means "you all dwell" or "you all inhabit". The verb can be used in various tenses, such as past, future, and subjunctive.

Description: κατοικείτε is a verb that is commonly used in everyday Greek language. It is used to describe the act of dwelling or inhabiting a place. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing where someone lives, where a group of people are located, or where a particular species of animal is found.
