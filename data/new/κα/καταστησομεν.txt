Word: καταστησομεν

Etymology:
The word καταστησομεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καταστήσω (katastéso), which means "to establish" or "to set up." It is formed by adding the future tense ending -σομεν (-somen) to the stem καταστη- (katasté-).

Grammar:
καταστησομεν is a first-person plural future active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by combining the future tense marker -σο- (-so-) with the stem καταστη- (katasté-) and the first-person plural ending -μεν (-men). The verb indicates that the subject (we) will establish or set up something in the future.

Description:
καταστησομεν is a Greek verb that means "we will establish" or "we will set up." It is a future tense verb that indicates an action that will take place in the future.
