Word: καταρασθε

Etymology:
The word καταρασθε (katarasthe) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word κατάρα (katará), which means "curse" or "malediction". The suffix -σθε (-sthe) is a second person plural ending, indicating that the verb is in the imperative mood and is directed towards a group of people.

Grammar:
καταρασθε is a second person plural imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is formed by adding the suffix -σθε to the stem καταρα-. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give a command or instruction.

Description:
The word καταρασθε is a command to a group of people to curse or damn something or someone. It is often used in a religious or supernatural context, such as in the Bible or in Greek mythology. The word carries a strong negative connotation and is not typically used in everyday conversation.
