Word: κατοικειν

Etymology:
The word κατοικειν (katoikein) comes from the Greek roots κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "according to" and οἰκέω (oikeo) meaning "to dwell" or "to inhabit". Therefore, κατοικειν means "to dwell down" or "to inhabit according to".

Grammar:
κατοικειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the pattern of verbs that end in -ω (omega). The verb stem is κατοικε-, and the ending -ιν (-in) indicates the infinitive form.

Description:
κατοικειν is a Greek verb that means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". It is often used in the context of describing where someone lives or where a particular group of people reside. For example, "Οι Έλληνες κατοικούν στην Ελλάδα" (Oi Ellines katoikoun stin Ellada) means "The Greeks dwell in Greece" or "The Greeks inhabit Greece". The verb can also be used in a figurative sense, such as "Η ελπίδα κατοικεί στην καρδιά μας" (I elpida katoikei stin kardia mas) which means "Hope dwells in our hearts".
