Word: κατακριμα

Etymology:
The word κατακριμα (katakrima) is derived from the Greek verb κατακρίνω (katakrino), which means "to condemn" or "to judge against". The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "against", while the root word κρίνω (krino) means "to judge".

Grammar:
In Greek, κατακριμα is a noun of neuter gender. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: το κατακριμα (to katakrima) - the condemnation
- Genitive: του κατακριματος (tou katakrimatos) - of the condemnation
- Accusative: το κατακριμα (to katakrima) - the condemnation

Plural:
- Nominative: τα κατακριματα (ta katakrimata) - the condemnations
- Genitive: των κατακριματων (ton katakrimaton) - of the condemnations
- Accusative: τα κατακριματα (ta katakrimata) - the condemnations

Description:
The word κατακριμα refers to the act of condemning or judging someone or something as guilty or wrong. It can also refer to the punishment or penalty that is imposed as a result of the condemnation. In a legal context, κατακριμα can refer to a sentence or verdict of guilt. In a religious context, it can refer to the concept of damnation or eternal punishment.
