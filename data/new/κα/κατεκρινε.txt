Word: κατεκρίνε

Etymology:
The word κατεκρίνε comes from the Greek verb κρίνω (krinó), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix κατα- (kata-) adds the meaning of "down" or "against", so κατεκρίνε means "to judge against" or "to condemn".

Grammar:
κατεκρίνε is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb κρίνω and the prefix κατα-. The verb is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
κατεκρίνε means "condemned" or "judged against". It is often used in a legal context to describe a verdict or sentence. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe a negative judgment or criticism.
