Word: καταντησαι

Etymology: καταντησαι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against," and ἱκανός (hikanos), which means "able" or "sufficient." Therefore, καταντησαι means "to become able" or "to arrive at a point."

Grammar: καταντησαι is a first-person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb καταντάω (katantao), which means "to arrive at," "to reach," or "to come to." In this form, καταντησαι means "I arrived at" or "I reached."

Description: καταντησαι is a verb that describes the action of arriving at a certain point or becoming able to do something. It can be used in various contexts, such as reaching a destination, achieving a goal, or acquiring a skill. The word has a sense of accomplishment and success, as it implies that the subject has overcome obstacles and reached a desired outcome.
