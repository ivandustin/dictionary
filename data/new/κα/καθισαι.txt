Word: καθίσαι

Etymology:
The word καθίσαι (kathisai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κάθημαι (kathemai), which means "to sit." The verb καθίσαι is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
καθίσαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to sit down. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: κάθομαι (kathomai) - I sit
- Aorist tense: κάθηκα (kathika) - I sat down
- Imperative mood: κάθισε (kathise) - Sit down!

Description:
καθίσαι is a simple and common verb in Greek that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to invite someone to sit down or to give a command to sit down. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in a formal meeting, at a dinner table, or in a casual conversation.
