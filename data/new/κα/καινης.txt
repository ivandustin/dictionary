The word "καινης" (kainēs) is an adjective in Greek that means "new" or "fresh". It is derived from the Greek word "καινός" (kainos), which also means "new". In terms of grammar, "καινης" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a new idea, a new product, or a new relationship.
