Word: καθηκαν

Etymology:
The word καθηκαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb καθίζω (kathízo), which means "to sit down" or "to seat". The verb is derived from the noun κάθισμα (káthisma), which means "seat" or "chair".

Grammar:
καθηκαν is the third person plural, aorist indicative active form of the verb καθίζω (kathízo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description:
καθηκαν describes the action of a group of people sitting down or taking a seat. It is a specific moment in time, rather than a continuous or ongoing action. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people sitting down for a meal, or taking their seats in a theater or classroom.
