Word: κατακαίεται

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down" or "against") + καίεται (kaíetai, present tense of καίω, meaning "to burn"). Therefore, κατακαίεται means "is burned down" or "is consumed by fire".

Grammar: κατακαίεται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb καίω (kaíō), which means "to burn". The prefix κατα- (kata-) adds the meaning of "down" or "against" to the verb.

Description: κατακαίεται is a Greek verb that describes the action of something being burned down or consumed by fire. It is often used in the context of buildings or forests being destroyed by fire. The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the burning is currently happening or ongoing. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.
