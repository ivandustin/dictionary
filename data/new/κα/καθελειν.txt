Word: καθελειν

Etymology:
The word καθελειν (kathelen) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down," and the verb ἕλω (helō), meaning "to take." Therefore, καθελειν means "to take down" or "to remove."

Grammar:
καθελειν is an active verb in the present tense, third person singular. Its infinitive form is καθελεῖν (kathelen) and its stem is καθελε-. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it has a thematic vowel -ε- in its stem.

Description:
καθελειν is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to physically taking something down or removing it, such as a building or a tree. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the removal of obstacles, barriers, or impediments. In addition, καθελειν can be used in a medical context to describe the removal of a tumor or a growth. Overall, καθελειν is a useful verb that conveys the idea of taking something down or removing it.
