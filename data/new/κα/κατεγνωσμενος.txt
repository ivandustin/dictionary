Word: κατεγνωσμένος

Etymology:
The word κατεγνωσμένος comes from the Greek verb καταγινώσκω (kataginósko), which means "to condemn" or "to accuse". The prefix κατά (katá) means "down" or "against", and the verb γινώσκω (ginósko) means "to know" or "to recognize". Therefore, κατεγνωσμένος literally means "known against" or "condemned".

Grammar:
κατεγνωσμένος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb καταγινώσκω and the suffix -μένος (-menos), which is used to form passive participles.

Description:
κατεγνωσμένος is used to describe someone who has been accused or condemned. It can also be used to describe something that has been discredited or disgraced. In modern Greek, it is often used in legal contexts to refer to someone who has been found guilty of a crime.
