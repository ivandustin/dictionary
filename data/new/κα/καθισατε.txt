Word: καθισατε

Etymology: καθισατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθίζω (kathizó), which means "to sit down". The verb form καθισατε is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: καθισατε is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is a command form, meaning "you all sit down". The verb is composed of the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down", and the verb ίζω (izo), which means "to sit".

Description: καθισατε is a command in Greek that instructs a group of people to sit down. It is often used in formal settings, such as in a lecture or a church service. The word is composed of two parts, the prefix κατά (kata) and the verb ίζω (izo), which together create the meaning of "sit down".
