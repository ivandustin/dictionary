The word "κακολογησαι" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means "to speak ill of" or "to slander". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "κακός" (kakos) meaning "bad" or "evil" and "λογέω" (logeo) meaning "to speak". 

Grammar: "κακολογησαι" is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb, which means it is a command directed towards one person to perform the action of speaking ill of someone. 

Description: The word has a negative connotation and is often used to describe someone who is speaking maliciously or spreading false rumors about another person. It is important to be mindful of the impact our words can have on others and to use language in a respectful and constructive manner.
