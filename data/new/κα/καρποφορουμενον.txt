Word: καρποφορουμενον

Etymology: The word καρποφορουμενον comes from the Greek words καρπός (karpos) meaning "fruit" and φορέω (phoreo) meaning "to bear or carry". Therefore, καρποφορουμενον means "bearing fruit".

Grammar: καρποφορουμενον is a present participle in the passive voice, which means it describes an ongoing action of bearing fruit that is being done to the subject. In this case, the subject is not specified.

Description: καρποφορουμενον is a Greek word that describes the act of bearing fruit. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a person or organization that is productive and successful in their endeavors. The word can also be used literally to describe a tree or plant that is currently producing fruit.
