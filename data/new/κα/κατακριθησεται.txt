Word: κατακριθησεται

Etymology: κατακριθησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb κατακρίνω (katakríno), which means "to condemn" or "to judge against". The word is formed by combining the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against", and the verb κρίνω (kríno), which means "to judge".

Grammar: κατακριθησεται is a third-person singular future passive indicative form of the verb κατακρίνω (katakríno). This form indicates that the subject of the sentence will be condemned or judged against in the future. The word is composed of the following elements: κατα- (prefix), κρι- (stem), -θη- (passive suffix), and -σεται (future tense ending).

Description: κατακριθησεται is a strong and powerful word that conveys a sense of judgment and condemnation. It is often used in legal or religious contexts to describe the punishment or condemnation of someone who has committed a wrongdoing.
