Word: καταργησαι

Etymology:
The word καταργησαι comes from the Greek verb καταργέω (katargeo), which means "to abolish" or "to make ineffective". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and the verb ἀργέω (argeo), meaning "to be idle" or "to be inactive".

Grammar:
καταργησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the infinitive mood is used to express the idea of an action without specifying who is performing it.

Description:
καταργησαι is a strong and forceful word that conveys the idea of completely abolishing or rendering something ineffective. It is often used in a legal or political context to describe the repeal of a law or the nullification of a decision. In a broader sense, it can also be used to describe the act of overcoming or defeating an obstacle or enemy.
