Word: κατάρας

Etymology:
The word κατάρας (kataras) comes from the Greek verb καταράομαι (kataráomai), which means "to curse" or "to imprecate". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against", and the verb ῥάομαι (rhaomai), which means "to speak".

Grammar:
κατάρας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of a curse" or "belonging to a curse". The word is masculine and singular.

Description:
κατάρας refers to a curse or imprecation, which is a spoken or written invocation of harm or misfortune upon someone or something. In Greek mythology, curses were often used as a means of revenge or punishment. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a persistent or inescapable problem or difficulty.
