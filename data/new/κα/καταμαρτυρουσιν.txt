Word: καταμαρτυρουσιν

Etymology:
The word καταμαρτυρουσιν is derived from the Greek words κατά (kata) meaning "against" and μαρτυρέω (martyreo) meaning "to testify". The word is a present active participle in the third person plural form.

Grammar:
The word καταμαρτυρουσιν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is in the third person plural form, which means that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things. The word is often translated as "they testify against" or "they bear witness against".

Description:
The word καταμαρτυρουσιν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (26:60) and the Acts of the Apostles (6:13). In both instances, it is used to describe the act of bearing false witness or giving testimony against someone. In Matthew 26:60, the word is used to describe the false witnesses who testified against Jesus during his trial. In Acts 6:13, it is used to describe the false witnesses who testified against Stephen, one of the first Christian martyrs.
