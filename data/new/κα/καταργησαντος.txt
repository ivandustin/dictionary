Word: καταργησαντος

Etymology: καταργησαντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb καταργέω (katargeo), which means "to abolish" or "to make ineffective". The word is formed by adding the suffix -αντος (-antos), which is the genitive form of the participle suffix -ας (-as).

Grammar: καταργησαντος is the genitive singular form of the participle καταργήσας (katargēsas), which is the aorist active participle of the verb καταργέω (katargeo). The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in the sentence.

Description: καταργησαντος is a Greek word that means "of the one who abolished" or "of the one who made ineffective". It is often used in the context of legal or political documents to refer to the person who has abolished or nullified something. In Greek grammar, it is a participle that functions as an adjective, modifying a noun in the sentence.
