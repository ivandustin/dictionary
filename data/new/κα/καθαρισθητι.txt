Word: καθαρισθητι

Etymology: καθαρισθητι is a Greek word that comes from the verb καθαρίζω (katharizo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify". It is a passive imperative form of the verb, meaning "be cleansed" or "be purified".

Grammar: καθαρισθητι is a second person singular passive imperative verb form in the Greek language. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to be cleansed or purified. The passive voice indicates that the action is being done to the subject, rather than the subject doing the action.

Description: καθαρισθητι is a word that is often used in religious contexts, particularly in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used in the context of spiritual cleansing or purification, and is often associated with the forgiveness of sins. The word is also used in a more general sense to refer to the process of cleaning or purifying something, such as a physical object or a space.
