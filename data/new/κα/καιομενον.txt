The word "καιομενον" (kaiomenon) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "καίω" (kaio), which means "to burn". The participle form indicates that the burning action is ongoing or in progress. 

In terms of grammar, "καιομενον" is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender. It can function as a subject, predicate nominative, or appositive in a sentence.

Etymologically, "καίω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *keh₂i- which means "to burn". The word has cognates in other Indo-European languages, such as the Latin "causus" and the English "caustic".

Overall, "καιομενον" describes something that is currently burning or in the process of being burned.
