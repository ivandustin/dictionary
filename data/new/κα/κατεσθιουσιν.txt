Word: κατεσθιουσιν

Etymology:
The word κατεσθιουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "εσθίω" (esthio), which means "to eat." The prefix "κατα" (kata) means "down" or "completely," so κατεσθιουσιν means "they completely eat" or "they devour."

Grammar:
κατεσθιουσιν is a present tense, third person plural verb in Greek. It is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of eating. The verb is also in the indicative mood, which means that it is a statement of fact.

Description:
κατεσθιουσιν is a strong and vivid verb that conveys the idea of consuming something completely or devouring it. It is often used in a negative context, such as when describing the actions of wild animals or people who are gluttonous or greedy. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the actions of the Pharisees, who were criticized for their hypocrisy and their tendency to focus on external rituals rather than internal righteousness.
