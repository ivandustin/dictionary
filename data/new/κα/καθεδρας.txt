Word: καθεδρας

Etymology:
The word καθεδρας (kathedras) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and ἕδρα (hedra) meaning "seat". The word καθεδρας originally referred to a seat or throne, especially one used by a bishop or other high-ranking church official.

Grammar:
In Greek, καθεδρας is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. Its genitive singular form is καθέδρας (kathedras) and its accusative singular form is καθέδραν (kathedran). The word is declined according to the second declension.

Description:
καθεδρας is a term that is used to describe a seat or throne, especially one used by a bishop or other high-ranking church official. It is often used in the context of religious ceremonies and rituals. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any type of seat or chair, although this usage is less common.
