Word: κατεφυγον

Etymology:
The word κατεφυγον (katephugon) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and φυγή (phugē) meaning "flight" or "escape". The verb form κατεφυγον is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
κατεφυγον is a first-person singular aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb καταφεύγω (katapheúgō), which means "to flee down" or "to take refuge". In this form, the verb indicates that the speaker (or writer) fled or took refuge in the past.

Description:
κατεφυγον is a word that describes an action of fleeing or taking refuge in the past. It is often used in a literal sense, such as when someone seeks shelter from danger or harm. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe seeking refuge from emotional or psychological distress. The word has a sense of urgency and desperation, as it implies that the person fleeing or seeking refuge is in a state of danger or distress.
