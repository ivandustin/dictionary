Word: καμινον

Etymology:
The word καμινον (kaminon) is derived from the Greek word κάμινος (kaminos), which means "furnace" or "chimney". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *kem- meaning "to cover" or "to hide".

Grammar:
In Greek, καμινον is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form καμίνου (kaminou).

Description:
καμινον refers to a chimney or a furnace used for heating or cooking. It is typically made of brick or stone and is used to vent smoke and gases from a fire. In ancient Greece, καμινον was commonly used in households for cooking and heating. Today, it is still used in many homes and buildings for heating and ventilation purposes.
