Word: καταδούλωση (kata-doúlosi)

Etymology: The word καταδούλωση comes from the Greek words κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "against," and δούλος (doúlos), meaning "slave." Therefore, καταδούλωση means "enslavement" or "subjugation."

Grammar: καταδούλωση is a noun in the feminine gender. Its plural form is καταδουλώσεις (kata-doúloses).

Description: καταδούλωση refers to the act of enslaving or subjugating someone or a group of people. It can also refer to the state of being enslaved or subjugated. The word is often used in the context of historical events, such as the enslavement of Africans during the transatlantic slave trade.
