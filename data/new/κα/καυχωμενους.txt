Word: καυχωμένους

Etymology:
The word καυχωμένους comes from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The suffix -μένους (-menous) is a participle ending that indicates the present tense and passive voice.

Grammar:
καυχωμένους is a present participle in the accusative case, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb καυχάομαι, which is a deponent verb, meaning that it has a passive form but an active meaning. The participle form καυχωμένους indicates that the subject is currently boasting or bragging.

καυχωμένους is a Greek word that describes someone who is currently boasting or bragging. It can be used to describe someone who is excessively proud or arrogant.
