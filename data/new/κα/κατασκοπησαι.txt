Word: κατασκοπησαι

Etymology: The word κατασκοπησαι comes from the Greek verb κατασκοπέω (katascopeo), which means "to spy on" or "to investigate". The suffix -σαι (-sai) indicates the first person singular aorist tense, which means that the word refers to an action that was completed in the past.

Grammar: κατασκοπησαι is a verb in the first person singular aorist tense. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, and voice. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The first person singular form indicates that the action was performed by the speaker.

Description: κατασκοπησαι means "I spied on" or "I investigated" in English. It is a verb that describes an action of observing or gathering information secretly. The word can be used in various contexts, such as espionage, surveillance, or research.
