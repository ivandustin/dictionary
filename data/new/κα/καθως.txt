Word: καθως

Etymology:
The word καθως (kathōs) is a Greek adverb that means "just as" or "in the same way". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words: κατά (kata), meaning "according to" or "in accordance with", and ὡς (hōs), meaning "as" or "like".

Grammar:
In Greek, καθως is an adverb that is used to introduce a comparison or similarity between two things. It is often translated as "just as" or "in the same way" in English. It is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospel of John, to emphasize the similarities between Jesus and God.

Description:
The word καθως is a versatile adverb that can be used in a variety of contexts to express similarity or comparison. It is often used in religious or philosophical texts to emphasize the similarities between two concepts or ideas. In everyday speech, it can be used to compare two things or to express agreement with a statement.
