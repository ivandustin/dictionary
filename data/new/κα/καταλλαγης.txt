Word: καταλλαγης

Etymology: καταλλαγης comes from the Greek verb καταλλάσσω (katallasso), which means "to reconcile" or "to bring back into favor." The suffix -ης (-es) indicates that it is a noun.

Grammar: καταλλαγης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: καταλλαγης refers to the act of reconciliation or the state of being reconciled. It can also mean "atonement" or "restoration of friendly relations." In Christian theology, it is used to describe the reconciliation between God and humanity through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ.
