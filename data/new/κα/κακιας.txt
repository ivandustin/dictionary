Word: κακιας

Etymology: κακιας comes from the Greek word κακία (kakía), which means "wickedness" or "malice". It is derived from the adjective κακός (kakós), which means "bad" or "evil".

Grammar: κακιας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this form, it means "of wickedness" or "of malice".

Description: κακιας is a Greek word that refers to the quality of being wicked or malicious. It is often used to describe someone who is intentionally harmful or cruel. The word can also be used to describe a situation or event that is characterized by wickedness or malice.
