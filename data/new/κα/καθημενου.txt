Word: καθημενου

Etymology: καθημενου is the genitive form of the Greek verb καθήμαι (kathēmai), which means "to sit". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down", and the verb ἧμαι (hēmai), meaning "to sit".

Grammar: καθημενου is a masculine singular genitive form of the verb καθήμαι. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns.

Description: καθημενου can be translated as "of the one who is sitting" or "of the sitting one". It is often used in the New Testament to describe someone who is sitting down, such as in Luke 22:55 where it is used to describe Peter sitting by the fire.
