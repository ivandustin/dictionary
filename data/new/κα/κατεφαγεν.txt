Word: κατεφαγεν

Etymology: κατεφαγεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and φαγεῖν (phagein) meaning "to eat". Therefore, κατεφαγεν means "to eat down" or "to devour".

Grammar: κατεφαγεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. It is composed of the prefix κατά (kata) and the verb φαγεῖν (phagein) in the aorist tense.

Description: κατεφαγεν is a strong verb that conveys the idea of consuming something completely or devouring it. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a situation where something or someone is completely destroyed or consumed. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the act of the devil devouring or destroying someone's faith or soul.
