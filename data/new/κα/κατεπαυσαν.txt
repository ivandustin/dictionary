Word: κατεπαυσαν

Etymology:
The word κατεπαυσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word παύω (pauo), which means "to stop" or "to cease." The prefix κατα (kata) means "down" or "completely," so κατεπαυσαν can be translated as "completely stopped" or "ceased."

Grammar:
κατεπαυσαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb καταπαύω (katapauo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
κατεπαυσαν can be translated as "they completely stopped" or "they ceased." The verb implies a sudden and complete cessation of an action or activity. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning.
