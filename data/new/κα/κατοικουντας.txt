Word: κατοικούντας

Etymology: κατοικούντας is the present participle of the Greek verb κατοικέω (katoikéo), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "according to", and οἰκέω (oikéo), meaning "to dwell" or "to live".

Grammar: κατοικούντας is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is used to describe an ongoing action of dwelling or inhabiting, and can function as an adjective or adverb in a sentence.

Description: κατοικούντας is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe people who are currently dwelling or inhabiting a particular place, such as a city or a house. It can also be used to describe animals or other living things that inhabit a particular environment.
