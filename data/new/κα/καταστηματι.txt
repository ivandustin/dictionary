Word: καταστηματι

Etymology:
The word καταστηματι (katastemati) is derived from the Greek verb καταστήσω (katastiso), which means "to establish" or "to set up". The suffix -ματι (-mati) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form nouns from verbs.

Grammar:
καταστηματι is a noun in the singular form. It is a neuter noun, which means that it does not have a gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, but neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases.

Description:
καταστηματι refers to a place of business or a shop. It can be used to describe any type of store, such as a grocery store, clothing store, or bookstore. The word is commonly used in modern Greek and is often used in everyday conversation.
