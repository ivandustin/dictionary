Word: καυχώμαι

Etymology:
The word καυχώμαι (kauchomai) comes from the Greek root word καυχάομαι (kauchaomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag." It is derived from the noun καύχημα (kauchēma), which means "boasting" or "bragging."

Grammar:
καυχώμαι is a first-person singular present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

Present: καυχώμαι
Future: θα καυχηθώ (tha kauchitho)
Aorist: ἐκαυχησάμην (ekaukhēsamēn)
Perfect: κεκαυχημαι (kekauchēmai)

Description:
καυχώμαι is a Greek verb that means "to boast" or "to brag." It is often used in a negative context, as boasting is generally seen as a negative trait. The verb can be used in both the active and passive voice, and can be conjugated in various tenses.
