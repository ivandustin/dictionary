The word "καρδιαις" (kardiais) is a Greek noun in the dative plural form, which means "hearts" in English. It is derived from the Greek word "καρδία" (kardia), which means "heart". In Greek mythology, the heart was considered the center of emotions and the seat of the soul. The word "καρδιαις" can be found in various ancient Greek texts, including the New Testament of the Bible.
