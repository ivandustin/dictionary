Word: κατεσκευασεν

Etymology:
The word κατεσκευασεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σκευαζω (skeuazo), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready." The prefix κατα (kata) means "down" or "against," so κατεσκευασεν can be translated as "to prepare against" or "to make ready for."

Grammar:
κατεσκευασεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
κατεσκευασεν can be translated as "he/she/it prepared" or "he/she/it made ready." The verb implies that someone has taken action to prepare for something, often in a deliberate and intentional way. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was being prepared or made ready.
