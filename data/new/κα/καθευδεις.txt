Word: καθευδεις

Etymology:
The word καθευδεις (kathudeis) is a present tense verb in Greek, which means "you are sleeping". It is derived from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and εὕδω (eúdo) meaning "to sleep". 

Grammar:
καθευδεις is a second person singular present active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb καθεύδω (kathévdo), which means "to sleep". The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now. The second person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is "you".
