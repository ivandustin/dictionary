Word: καθηκεν

Etymology:
The word καθηκεν (kathēken) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθίζω (kathizō), which means "to sit down" or "to settle." The verb καθηκεν is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
καθηκεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -εν to the stem καθηκ-. The verb is in the indicative mood, which indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
The verb καθηκεν means "he/she/it sat down" or "he/she/it settled" in English. It is a past tense verb, indicating that the action of sitting down or settling has already been completed.
