Word: και

Etymology:
The word και (kai) is a conjunction in the Greek language. It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷe, which means "and" or "also". The word has been used in Greek language since ancient times and has remained a common conjunction in modern Greek.

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, και is a coordinating conjunction that is used to connect words, phrases, or clauses of equal syntactic importance. It is often translated as "and" in English. It is a very common word in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts.
