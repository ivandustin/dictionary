Word: καθευδει

Etymology: καθευδει is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and εὕδω (eúdo) meaning "to sleep". Therefore, καθευδει means "he/she/it sleeps".

Grammar: καθευδει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. In Greek, verbs change their endings depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person.

Description: καθευδει is a common verb in Greek that describes the action of sleeping. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's sleeping habits or indicating that someone is currently sleeping. It is a regular verb, meaning that it follows the standard conjugation rules for present tense verbs in Greek.
