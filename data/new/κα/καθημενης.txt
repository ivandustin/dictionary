Word: καθημενης

Etymology:
The word καθημενης (kathēmenēs) is a feminine singular form of the Greek verb καθέζομαι (kathézomai), which means "to sit down" or "to be seated". It is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), meaning "down", and the verb ἕζομαι (hézomai), meaning "to sit".

Grammar:
In Greek, καθημενης is a feminine singular form of the present participle of the verb καθέζομαι (kathézomai). It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence or clause.

Description:
The word καθημενης can be translated as "sitting" or "seated". It is often used to describe a person or object that is in a seated position. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe Mary, the sister of Martha, who was "sitting at the Lord's feet" (Luke 10:39). The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of rest or stability, as in the phrase "a settled life" (καθημερινή ζωή, kathēmerinē zōē).
