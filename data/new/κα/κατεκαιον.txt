Word: κατεκαιον

Etymology:
The word κατεκαιον is derived from the Greek verb κατεκαίω (katakaio), which means "to burn down completely". The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "completely", while καίω (kaio) means "to burn".

Grammar:
κατεκαιον is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is the aorist tense of the verb κατεκαίω (katakaio).

Description:
κατεκαιον describes an action of burning down completely. It can be used to describe the destruction of a building or any other object by fire. The word has a strong connotation of complete destruction and devastation.
