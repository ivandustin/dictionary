The word "καταγγελλων" is a present participle in Greek, which means "accusing" or "denouncing". It is derived from the verb "καταγγέλλω" (katangello), which means "to accuse" or "to denounce". The word is composed of two parts: "κατά" (kata), which means "down" or "against", and "γγέλλω" (gello), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim". 

In terms of grammar, "καταγγελλων" is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a person who is currently accusing or denouncing someone or something.

Overall, "καταγγελλων" is a word that is commonly used in legal and political contexts, where accusations and denunciations are often made.
