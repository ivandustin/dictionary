Word: καλειν

Etymology:
The word καλειν (kalein) is derived from the Greek verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". It is related to the English words "call" and "vocational".

Grammar:
καλειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, and voice. The present active infinitive form is used to express an action that is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
καλειν is a Greek verb that means "to call" or "to summon". It is often used in the context of inviting someone to come or to request someone's presence. The word can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to name" or "to designate". In ancient Greek mythology, the god Hermes was often referred to as "the one who calls" because he was responsible for summoning the souls of the dead to the underworld.
