The word "κατηλθομεν" is a Greek verb that is in the aorist tense and indicative mood. It is the first person plural form of the verb "καταβαίνω" (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "κατά" (kata), which means "down" or "according to", and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), which means "to go". 

Grammar: As mentioned, "κατηλθομεν" is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The first person plural form means that the subject of the verb is "we".

Description: "κατηλθομεν" can be translated as "we went down" or "we descended". It is often used in a literal sense, such as when describing a physical descent down a mountain or staircase. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe a decline in status or a decrease in quality.
