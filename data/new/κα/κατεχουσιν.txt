Word: κατεχουσιν

Etymology: κατεχουσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb κατέχω (katechō), which means "to hold down" or "to possess."

Grammar: κατεχουσιν is a present tense, third person plural form of the verb κατέχω. It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: κατεχουσιν is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present moment. It can be translated to mean "they are holding down" or "they are possessing." The context in which it is used will determine the exact meaning.
