Word: κατοικει

Etymology: κατοικει comes from the Greek verb κατοικέω (katoikeo), which is a compound of κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "according to" and οἰκέω (oikeo) meaning "to dwell" or "to inhabit". Therefore, κατοικει means "he/she/it dwells" or "he/she/it inhabits".

Grammar: κατοικει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb κατοικέω. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. 

Description: κατοικει is a verb that describes the action of dwelling or inhabiting. It can be used to describe a person, animal, or even a place that is inhabited. For example, "Οι άνθρωποι κατοικούν στην πόλη" (Oi anthropoi katoikoun stin poli) means "People dwell in the city" or "People live in the city". 

Overall, κατοικει is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to describe the act of dwelling or inhabiting.
