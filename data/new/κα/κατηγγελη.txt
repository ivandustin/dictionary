The word "κατηγγελη" (katangelē) is a Greek verb that means "to accuse" or "to denounce". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "κατά" (kata), meaning "down" or "against", and "ἀγγελεῖν" (angelein), meaning "to announce" or "to proclaim".

Grammar: "Κατηγγελη" is a second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to accuse or denounce someone or something.

Description: The word is often used in legal contexts to describe the act of formally accusing someone of a crime or wrongdoing. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of publicly denouncing or criticizing someone or something.
