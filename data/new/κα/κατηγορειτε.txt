Word: κατηγορειτε

Etymology:
The word κατηγορειτε (katēgoreite) is derived from the Greek verb κατηγορέω (katēgoreō), which means "to accuse" or "to charge". The word is formed from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against", and the verb ἀγορεύω (agoreuō), which means "to speak in public".

- Singular:
  - First person: κατηγορώ (katēgorō)
  - Second person: κατηγορείς (katēgoreis)
  - Third person: κατηγορεί (katēgorei)

- Plural:
  - First person: κατηγορούμεν (katēgoroumen)
  - Second person: κατηγορείτε (katēgoreite)
  - Third person: κατηγορούν (katēgoroun)

Description:
κατηγορειτε is a verb that is used to accuse or charge someone with a crime or wrongdoing. It is often used in legal or formal contexts, and is typically followed by the accusative case of the person or thing being accused.
