Word: κατασφαξατε

Etymology: The word κατασφαξατε is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and σφάζω (sfazo) meaning "to slaughter" or "to kill". Therefore, κατασφαξατε means "to slaughter down" or "to kill against".

Grammar: κατασφαξατε is a second person plural aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed to a group of people to perform an action in the past. The verb is conjugated from the root word σφάζω (sfazo) and the prefix κατά (kata).

Description: κατασφαξατε is a strong and forceful word that is often used in a violent or aggressive context. It can be translated as "slaughter down" or "kill against" and is typically used in a military or battle context. The word is not commonly used in everyday conversation and is considered quite formal and archaic.
