Word: καυσων

Etymology:
The word καυσων (kausōn) is derived from the Greek verb καίω (kaíō), which means "to burn." It is related to the English word "caustic," which also comes from the same root.

Grammar:
καυσων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also known as a second-declension noun in Greek grammar. The word is often translated as "heat" or "summer."

Description:
καυσων is a Greek word that refers to the intense heat of summer. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe the oppressive heat of the season. In ancient Greece, the word was also used to describe the hot, dry winds that blew across the Mediterranean during the summer months. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to the summer heat.
