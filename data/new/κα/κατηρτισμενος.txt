Word: κατηρτισμένος

Etymology: The word κατηρτισμένος comes from the Greek verb καταρτίζω (katartízo), which means "to put in order" or "to prepare." The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates a state or condition.

Grammar: κατηρτισμένος is a masculine singular participle in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: κατηρτισμένος describes someone or something that has been prepared or made ready for a particular purpose. It can also mean "perfectly fitted" or "well-adjusted." In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the spiritual maturity of believers who have been equipped for good works (Ephesians 4:12-13).
