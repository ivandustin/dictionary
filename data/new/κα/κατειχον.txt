Word: κατείχον

Etymology:
The word κατείχον (kateichon) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and the verb εἶχον (eichon) meaning "to have" or "to hold". The prefix κατά adds the sense of "holding down" or "restraining".

Grammar:
κατείχον is the third person plural, imperfect active indicative form of the verb κατέχω (katecho), which means "to hold down", "to restrain", or "to possess". In this form, it indicates an ongoing action in the past, performed by a group of people.

Description:
κατείχον is a verb that describes the act of holding something down or restraining it. It can be used in a literal sense, such as holding down a person or an object, or in a figurative sense, such as restraining one's emotions or desires. In the context of ancient Greek philosophy, κατείχον was used to describe the concept of "holding back" or "restraining" the passions in order to achieve a state of inner peace and tranquility.
