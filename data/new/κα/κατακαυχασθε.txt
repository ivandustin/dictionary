Word: κατακαυχασθε

Etymology: The word κατακαυχασθε is derived from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The prefix κατα- (kata-) in this word means "down" or "against", so κατακαυχασθε can be translated as "boasting against" or "boasting down".

Grammar: κατακαυχασθε is a verb in the imperative mood, second person plural form. It is a command or an exhortation, telling a group of people to boast or brag against something or someone.

Description: κατακαυχασθε is a strong and forceful word, often used in a negative context. It implies a sense of arrogance and pride, and can be seen as a warning against boasting or bragging excessively. In the New Testament, the word is used in James 4:16, where it is translated as "you boast in your arrogance".
