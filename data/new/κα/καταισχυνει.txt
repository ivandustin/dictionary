Word: καταισχυνει

Etymology:
The word καταισχυνει (kataischynei) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against," and αἰσχύνη (aischynē), which means "shame" or "disgrace." Therefore, καταισχυνει means "to shame" or "to disgrace."

Grammar:
καταισχυνει is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the third person singular, which means that it refers to a single subject who is performing the action of shaming or disgracing someone or something.

Description:
καταισχυνει is a strong verb that conveys a sense of shame or disgrace. It is often used in a moral or ethical context, to describe actions or behaviors that are considered shameful or dishonorable. The verb can be used both transitively and intransitively, depending on the context. When used transitively, it takes a direct object, as in "He shamed his family by his actions." When used intransitively, it does not take a direct object, as in "She felt ashamed of her behavior."
