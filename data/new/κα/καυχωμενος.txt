Word: καυχώμενος

Etymology: The word καυχώμενος comes from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word describes someone who is currently boasting or bragging.

Grammar: καυχώμενος is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb καυχάομαι, which is in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. The word καυχώμενος functions as an adjective, describing someone who is currently boasting or bragging.

Description: καυχώμενος is a Greek word that describes someone who is boasting or bragging. It can be used to describe someone who is excessively proud or arrogant, or someone who is simply expressing confidence in their abilities or accomplishments. The word has a negative connotation, implying that the person is being boastful in a way that is annoying or offensive to others.
