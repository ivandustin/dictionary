Word: καθαρον

Etymology:
The word καθαρον (katharon) is a Greek word that means "clean" or "pure". It is derived from the Greek word καθαίρω (kathairō), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify".

Grammar:
In Greek, καθαρον is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun as clean or pure. It is in the masculine singular form, but it can also be declined to match the gender and number of the noun it is describing.

Description:
καθαρον is a word that is often used in a spiritual or moral sense to describe something that is pure or free from contamination. It can also be used in a literal sense to describe something that is physically clean or free from dirt or impurities. In the New Testament of the Bible, καθαρον is used to describe the pure heart of a believer and the cleansing power of Jesus Christ.
