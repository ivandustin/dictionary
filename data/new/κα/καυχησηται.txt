Word: καυχησηται

Etymology:
The word καυχησηται is derived from the Greek verb καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates that the verb is in the middle or passive voice.

Grammar:
καυχησηται is a third-person singular present middle/passive indicative form of the verb καυχάομαι. It is used to describe someone who is boasting or bragging about something in the present moment.

Description:
καυχησηται is a Greek verb that describes the act of boasting or bragging. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is overly proud or arrogant. The word can be used to describe someone who is boasting about their accomplishments, possessions, or abilities. In Greek literature, the word is often used to criticize characters who are seen as vain or conceited.
