Word: καταβαν

Etymology:
The word καταβαν (kataban) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go". Therefore, καταβαν means "to go down".

Grammar:
καταβαν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The conjugation of the verb is as follows:

- Εγώ καταβαίνω (ego katabaino) - I go down
- Εσύ καταβαίνεις (esi katabainis) - You go down
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό καταβαίνει (aftos/afti/afto katabainei) - He/She/It goes down
- Εμείς καταβαίνουμε (emeis katabainoume) - We go down
- Εσείς καταβαίνετε (eseis katabainete) - You go down
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά καταβαίνουν (aftoi/aftes/afta katabainoun) - They go down

Description:
καταβαν is a verb that describes the action of going down. It can be used in various contexts, such as descending a staircase, a mountain, or a hill. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a decrease in something, such as a decrease in temperature or a decrease in prices.
