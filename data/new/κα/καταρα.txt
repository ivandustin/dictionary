Word: κατάρα (ká-ta-ra)

Etymology: The word κατάρα comes from the Greek verb καταράομαι (kataráomai), which means "to curse" or "to imprecate". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against", and the verb ἀράομαι (aráomai), which means "to pray" or "to invoke".

Grammar: κατάρα is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is κατάρες (ká-ta-res).

Description: κατάρα refers to a curse or an imprecation that is believed to bring harm or misfortune to someone. In Greek mythology, curses were often used as a means of revenge or punishment. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or circumstance that is considered to be a source of misfortune or hardship.
