Word: κατηρτισμένοι

Etymology:
The word κατηρτισμένοι (katērtismenoi) is a Greek word that comes from the verb καταρτίζω (katartizō), which means "to put in order" or "to prepare." The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "according to," and the verb ἀρτίζω (artizō), which means "to make ready" or "to equip."

Grammar:
κατηρτισμένοι is the plural form of the past participle of the verb καταρτίζω (katartizō). It is used to describe a group of people who have been prepared or made ready for a particular task or purpose. In Greek, the word is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

The word κατηρτισμένοι is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe believers who have been equipped or prepared for ministry. For example, in Ephesians 4:12, it says that the role of apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers is to "equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ."
