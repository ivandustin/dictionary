The word "καταλλαγητω" (katallagētō) is a Greek verb that means "to reconcile" or "to make peace." It is derived from the root word "καταλλαγή" (katallagē), which means "reconciliation" or "restoration of friendly relations." 

In terms of grammar, "καταλλαγητω" is a second person singular aorist imperative active form of the verb "καταλλάσσω" (katallassō), which means "to change" or "to exchange." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the imperative mood is used to give a command or make a request.

Overall, "καταλλαγητω" is a powerful word that conveys the idea of restoring peace and harmony between individuals or groups who were previously at odds with each other.
