The word κατεκλασεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κλάω (klao), which means "to break" or "to shatter". The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "against", so κατεκλασεν can be translated as "he broke down" or "he shattered".

κατεκλασεν is a strong verb that conveys a sense of force and violence. It is often used in the context of physical destruction or breaking apart of something.
