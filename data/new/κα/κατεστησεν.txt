Word: κατεστησεν

Etymology:
The word κατεστησεν (katestesen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word στησεν (stesen), which means "to stand". The prefix κατε (kate) means "down" or "against". Therefore, κατεστησεν (katestesen) means "to establish", "to set up", or "to appoint".

Grammar:
κατεστησεν (katestesen) is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is formed by adding the prefix κατε (kate) to the root verb στησεν (stesen), and then conjugating it in the aorist tense. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
κατεστησεν (katestesen) is a powerful verb that conveys the idea of establishing or setting up something with authority. It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of God or Jesus, such as when Jesus appointed his disciples or when God established his covenant with Abraham. The word implies a sense of permanence and stability, as if the thing being established will endure for a long time.
