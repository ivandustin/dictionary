Word: καθαρος

Etymology:
The word καθαρος (katharos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb καθαίρω (kathairō), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse". The word has been used in various contexts throughout history, including in religious and philosophical texts.

Grammar:
In Greek, καθαρος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun as "clean", "pure", or "clear". It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, καθαρός (katharos) is the masculine singular form, while καθαρή (katharē) is the feminine singular form.

Description:
καθαρος is a word that is often associated with cleanliness and purity. It can be used to describe physical objects that are free from dirt or impurities, as well as abstract concepts such as thoughts or intentions that are morally pure. The word has also been used in religious contexts to describe a state of spiritual purity or innocence.
