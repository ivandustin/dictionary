Word: κατεχωμεν

Etymology:
The word κατεχωμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κατέχω (katechō), which means "to hold down" or "to possess." The verb is in the present tense and the first person plural form, which means "we hold down" or "we possess."

Grammar:
The word κατεχωμεν is a verb in the present tense and the first person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and voice of the action. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the first person plural form indicates that the subject is "we."

Description:
The word κατεχωμεν is used to describe the action of holding down or possessing something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as holding down a physical object, or in a figurative sense, such as possessing knowledge or power. The verb can also be used in a negative sense, such as holding someone down or oppressing them.
