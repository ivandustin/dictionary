Word: καθελώ

Etymology:
The word καθελώ (katheló) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and the verb ἕλω (heló) meaning "to take" or "to seize". Therefore, καθελώ means "to take down" or "to remove".

Grammar:
καθελώ is a first-person singular present active indicative verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation of verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs ending in -ω. The verb stem is καθελ-, and the present tense ending for the first-person singular is -ώ.

Description:
καθελώ is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can mean "to take down" or "to remove" something physically, such as a building or a tree. It can also mean "to remove" something abstract, such as a law or a custom. In addition, it can be used figuratively to mean "to overthrow" or "to defeat" someone or something.
