Word: καταγγέλλετε

Etymology:
The word καταγγέλλετε comes from the Greek verb καταγγέλλω (katangello), which means "to accuse" or "to denounce". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against", and the verb ἀγγέλλω (angello), which means "to announce" or "to report".

Grammar:
καταγγέλλετε is a present tense verb in the second person plural form. It is used to address a group of people and means "you accuse" or "you denounce". The verb is formed by adding the present tense ending -ετε (-ete) to the stem καταγγελλ- (katangel-).

Description:
καταγγέλλετε is a strong and forceful verb that is often used in legal or political contexts. It implies a serious accusation or denunciation of someone or something. The verb can be used in both a positive and negative sense, depending on the context. For example, it can be used to denounce corruption or injustice, or to accuse someone of a crime.
