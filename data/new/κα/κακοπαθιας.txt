Word: κακοπαθιας

Etymology: The word κακοπαθιας comes from the Greek words κακός (kakós) meaning "bad" or "evil" and πάθος (páthos) meaning "suffering" or "passion". 

Grammar: κακοπαθιας is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun. 

Description: κακοπαθιας refers to the state of being in a state of suffering or affliction. It can also refer to a person who is experiencing such suffering. The word is often used in a medical or psychological context to describe a condition or disorder that causes physical or emotional pain.
