Word: καλως

Etymology: καλως is an adverb in Greek that means "well" or "properly". It is derived from the adjective καλός (kalos), which means "beautiful" or "good".

Grammar: καλως is an adverb and does not change form. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs to indicate how well or properly something is done.

Description: καλως is a common word in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to express approval or satisfaction with something that has been done well, or to encourage someone to do something properly.
