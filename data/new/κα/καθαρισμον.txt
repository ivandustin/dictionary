Word: καθαρισμόν

Etymology: The word καθαρισμόν comes from the Greek verb καθαρίζω (katharízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse". The suffix -μόν (-mon) is a nominalizing suffix that turns the verb into a noun, giving the word the meaning of "the act of purifying" or "the process of cleansing".

Grammar: καθαρισμόν is a masculine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and number (singular or plural). The accent mark on the last syllable indicates that the stress falls on that syllable.

Description: καθαρισμόν refers to the act of purifying or cleansing something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as the purification of water or the cleansing of a wound, or in a figurative sense, such as the purification of the soul or the cleansing of one's conscience. The word can also be used in a religious context, such as the purification of a temple or the cleansing of sins through a religious ritual.
