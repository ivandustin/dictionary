Word: καταφαγων

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down" or "against") + φαγεῖν (phagein, "to eat"). Therefore, καταφαγων means "one who eats down" or "one who devours".

Grammar: καταφαγων is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in the Greek language.

Description: καταφαγων is not a commonly used word in modern Greek. It appears in the New Testament of the Bible (1 Corinthians 15:54) where it is used to describe the victory over death through Jesus Christ. The word conveys a sense of complete consumption or destruction.
