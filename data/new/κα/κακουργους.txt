Word: κακουργους

Etymology: The word κακουργους comes from the Greek words κακός (kakós) meaning "bad" or "evil" and ἔργον (érgon) meaning "work" or "deed". Therefore, κακουργους means "evil-doer" or "criminal".

Grammar: κακουργους is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the plural form of κακοῦργος (kakoûrgos).

Description: κακουργους is a term used to describe someone who commits evil or criminal acts. It can refer to a person who engages in illegal activities such as theft, fraud, or violence. In Greek literature, the term is often used to describe villains or antagonists in stories.
