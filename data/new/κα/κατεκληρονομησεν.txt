Word: κατεκληρονομησεν

Etymology: κατεκληρονομησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "according to" and κληρονομέω (klēronomeō) meaning "to inherit" or "to receive by lot". 

Grammar: κατεκληρονομησεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is composed of the prefix κατά (kata), the verb κληρονομέω (klēronomeō), and the aorist tense ending -σεν (-sen). 

Description: κατεκληρονομησεν means "he/she/it inherited" or "he/she/it received by lot" in Greek. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe how God gave the promised land to the Israelites as an inheritance (Acts 7:45).
