Word: κατεφαγον

Etymology:
The word κατεφαγον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἐσθίω (esthio) meaning "to eat". Therefore, κατεφαγον means "to eat down" or "to devour".

Grammar:
κατεφαγον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb κατεσθίω (katesthio), which means "to eat up" or "to devour completely". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
κατεφαγον is a strong verb that conveys the idea of consuming something completely or devouring it. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a situation where something is completely destroyed or consumed. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe a swarm of locusts devouring crops (Revelation 9:4) or a fire consuming a forest (James 5:3).
