The word "κατακοπτων" is a Greek verb that means "to cut down," "to chop," or "to strike down." It is derived from the prefix "κατά" (meaning "down") and the verb "κόπτω" (meaning "to cut"). In terms of grammar, it is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It describes someone or something that is currently cutting down or striking down.
