Word: κατειργασθη

Etymology:
The word κατειργασθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εργαζομαι (ergazomai), which means "to work" or "to labor". The prefix κατα (kata) in this word means "down" or "against". Therefore, κατειργασθη can be translated as "worked against" or "opposed".

Grammar:
κατειργασθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action. Therefore, κατειργασθη can be translated as "he/she/it was worked against" or "he/she/it was opposed".

Description:
κατειργασθη is a word that describes an action of opposition or resistance. It can be used to describe a situation where someone or something is actively working against another person or thing. The word has a negative connotation and implies a sense of conflict or struggle.
