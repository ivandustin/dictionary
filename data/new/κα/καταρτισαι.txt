Word: καταρτίσαι

Etymology:
The word καταρτίσαι comes from the Greek verb καταρτίζω (katartizō), which means "to put in order," "to prepare," or "to equip." It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down," and the verb ἀρτίζω (artizō), which means "to make ready" or "to complete."

Grammar:
καταρτίσαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the infinitive mood is used to express the idea of an action without specifying who is performing it.

Description:
καταρτίσαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to the act of preparing or equipping something, such as a ship or an army. It can also refer to the act of restoring or repairing something that has been damaged or broken. In a spiritual sense, it can refer to the act of reconciling or restoring a relationship between people or between people and God.
