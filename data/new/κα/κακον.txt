Word: κακόν

Etymology:
The word κακόν (kakon) is derived from the Greek adjective κακός (kakos), which means "bad" or "evil". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *kakka-, which means "to defecate" or "to soil".

Grammar:
In Greek, κακόν is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is declined as follows:

Nominative: κακόν
Genitive: κακοῦ
Dative: κακῷ
Accusative: κακόν
Vocative: κακόν

Description:
The word κακόν is used to describe something that is bad or evil. It can refer to a person, an action, or a situation. In Greek mythology, the goddess Eris (meaning "strife" or "discord") was often associated with the concept of κακόν. The word is also used in modern Greek to describe something that is of poor quality or undesirable.
