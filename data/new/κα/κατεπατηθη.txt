Word: κατεπατηθη

Etymology:
The word κατεπατηθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πατέω (pateo), which means "to tread" or "to step on". The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "against", so κατεπατηθη can be translated as "to be stepped on" or "to be trampled down".

Grammar:
κατεπατηθη is a passive verb in the aorist tense, which means that the action of being stepped on or trampled down has already happened in the past. The verb is in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is singular and in the third person.

Description:
κατεπατηθη is a powerful and vivid word that conveys a sense of violence and destruction. It is often used in the context of warfare or conflict, where armies or individuals are trampling over each other in battle. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe situations where someone or something is being oppressed or overwhelmed by external forces.
