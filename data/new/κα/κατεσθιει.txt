Word: κατεσθίει

Etymology: κατεσθίει is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σθίω (sthíō), which means "to eat." The prefix κατ- (kat-) adds the meaning of "completely" or "thoroughly," so κατεσθίει means "to eat up completely" or "to devour."

Grammar: κατεσθίει is a present tense, active voice verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the third person singular, meaning "he/she/it eats up completely" or "he/she/it devours."

Description: κατεσθίει is a strong and forceful verb that conveys the idea of consuming something completely and with great intensity. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when describing an animal devouring its prey, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing a person's insatiable appetite for power or wealth.
