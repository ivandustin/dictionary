Word: κατεργασαμενοι

Etymology: κατεργασαμενοι is a Greek word that comes from the verb κατεργάζομαι (katergazomai), which means "to work out" or "to accomplish."

Grammar: κατεργασαμενοι is the plural form of the aorist participle of κατεργάζομαι. It is a passive verb form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is the recipient of the action. In this case, the subject is plural and masculine, indicating that the action was performed by a group of men.

Description: κατεργασαμενοι can be translated as "having worked out" or "having accomplished." It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of the apostles or other early Christians who worked to spread the gospel and establish churches. The word implies a sense of diligence and perseverance in the face of difficulty or opposition.
