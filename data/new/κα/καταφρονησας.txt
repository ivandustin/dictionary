Word: καταφρονησας

Etymology: κατα- (kata-, prefix meaning "down" or "against") + φρονέω (phroneo, "to think" or "to have an opinion") + -σας (-sas, aorist active participle suffix). Therefore, καταφρονησας means "having thought down upon" or "having looked down upon".

Grammar: καταφρονησας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. It is derived from the verb φρονέω (phroneo) and the prefix κατα- (kata-).

Description: καταφρονησας is a Greek verb that means "to despise" or "to look down upon with contempt". It is often used to describe a feeling of superiority or disdain towards someone or something. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the attitude of the Pharisees towards Jesus.
