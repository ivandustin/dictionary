Word: κατακαησεται

Etymology:
The word κατακαησεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root words κατα- (kata-) meaning "down" or "against" and καίω (kaio) meaning "to burn". The verb form κατακαησεται is in the future tense and passive voice, meaning "it will be burned down".

Grammar:
κατακαησεται is a third-person singular future passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by combining the prefix κατα- (kata-) with the verb καίω (kaio) and adding the future tense ending -σεται (-setai) and the passive voice ending -ται (-tai).

Description:
κατακαησεται is a Greek verb that means "it will be burned down". It is often used to describe the destruction of buildings or other structures by fire. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the complete destruction or ruin of something.
