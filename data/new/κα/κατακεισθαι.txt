Word: κατακεισθαι

Etymology:
The word κατακεισθαι comes from the Greek roots κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "toward" and κεῖμαι (keimai) meaning "to lie down" or "to be placed". 

Grammar:
κατακεισθαι is a verb in the present passive infinitive form. It is derived from the verb κεῖμαι (keimai) which means "to lie down" or "to be placed". The prefix κατά (kata) adds the meaning of "down" or "toward". 

Description:
κατακεισθαι is a Greek verb that means "to lie down" or "to recline". It is often used in the context of dining or resting. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the posture of Jesus and his disciples at the Last Supper. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of rest or relaxation.
