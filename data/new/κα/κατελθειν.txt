Word: κατελθειν

Etymology:
The word κατελθειν comes from the Greek roots κατά (kata) meaning "down" and ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, κατελθειν means "to come down".

Grammar:
κατελθειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is an aorist active infinitive form of the verb κατέρχομαι (katerchomai), which means "to come down". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
κατελθειν is a word that is used to describe the action of coming down from a higher place to a lower place. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone descends a mountain or a staircase. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as when someone lowers their expectations or their status. The word is often used in religious contexts, such as when referring to the descent of Jesus Christ from heaven to earth.
