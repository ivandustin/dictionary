Word: καθηται

Etymology:
The word καθηται (kathētai) is a present tense form of the Greek verb καθίζω (kathizō), which means "to sit down". The verb is derived from the noun κάθεσις (kathesis), which means "sitting down" or "seat".

Grammar:
καθηται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb καθίζω (kathizō). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, voice, and person of the subject. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person singular form indicates that the subject is he/she/it.

Description:
