Word: κατανοησαι

Etymology:
The word κατανοησαι comes from the Greek verb κατανοέω (katanoeo), which is a compound of the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "through," and the verb νοέω (noeo), meaning "to perceive" or "to understand." Therefore, κατανοέω means "to understand thoroughly" or "to comprehend."

Grammar:
κατανοησαι is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in the active voice of the Greek verb κατανοέω. It is translated as "you understood" or "you comprehended" in English.

Description:
κατανοησαι is a verb that describes the act of understanding or comprehending something thoroughly. It implies a deep level of understanding and insight into a particular subject or situation. The word is often used in philosophical and intellectual contexts, where it is important to have a clear and complete understanding of complex ideas and concepts.
