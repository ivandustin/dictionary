The word "καταλυε" (katalye) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "κατα-" (kata-) meaning "down" or "against" and "λυε" (lye) meaning "to loosen" or "to dissolve". 

Grammar: "καταλυε" is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb. 

Description: The word "καταλυε" means "to dissolve" or "to break down". It can also mean "to overthrow" or "to destroy". It is often used in a figurative sense, such as "to dissolve a relationship" or "to break down barriers".
