Word: κατακρινω

Etymology:
The word κατακρινω comes from the Greek roots κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and κρίνω (krinō) meaning "to judge" or "to decide". Therefore, κατακρινω means "to condemn" or "to judge against".

Grammar:
κατακρινω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The conjugation of the verb in the present tense is as follows:

- Κατακρίνω (I condemn)
- Κατακρίνεις (You condemn)
- Κατακρίνει (He/She/It condemns)
- Κατακρίνουμε (We condemn)
- Κατακρίνετε (You all condemn)
- Κατακρίνουν (They condemn)

Description:
κατακρινω is a strong word that is used to express a negative judgment or condemnation towards someone or something. It can be used in a legal context to refer to a court's decision to find someone guilty of a crime. It can also be used in a more general sense to express disapproval or criticism of someone's actions or behavior.
