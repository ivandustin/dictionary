Word: καλον

Etymology:
The word καλον (kalon) is derived from the Greek word καλός (kalos), which means "beautiful" or "good". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *kal-, which means "to be beautiful" or "to be good".

Grammar:
In Greek, καλον is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun as "beautiful" or "good". It is in the neuter gender and singular number. It can also be used as a noun to refer to something that is beautiful or good.

Description:
καλον is a word that is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe something that is aesthetically pleasing or morally good. It can refer to physical beauty, such as a beautiful landscape or a work of art, or to moral goodness, such as a virtuous act or a noble character. In both cases, the word conveys a sense of admiration and appreciation for the object or person being described.
