Word: καθοραται

Etymology:
The word καθοραται comes from the Greek verb καθοράω (kathorao), which means "to observe closely" or "to examine thoroughly". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "through", and the verb ὁράω (horao), meaning "to see".

Grammar:
καθοραται is a third-person singular present middle/passive indicative form of the verb καθοράω (kathorao). In this form, the verb is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb.

Description:
καθοραται can be translated as "he/she/it is being examined closely" or "he/she/it is observing closely". The word implies a careful and thorough examination or observation, suggesting a deliberate and intentional act. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as scientific research, detective work, or personal introspection.
