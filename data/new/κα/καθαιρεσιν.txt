Word: καθαιρεσιν

Etymology:
The word καθαιρεσιν comes from the Greek verb καθαιρέω (kathaireo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "away", and the verb αἱρέω (haireo), meaning "to take" or "to choose".

Grammar:
καθαιρεσιν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description:
καθαιρεσιν refers to the act of cleansing or purifying. It can also refer to the removal of impurities or the elimination of something unwanted. In the context of ancient Greek religion, καθαιρεσιν was a ritual purification ceremony performed before certain religious rites or events. The word is also used in modern Greek to refer to the act of cleaning or sanitizing.
