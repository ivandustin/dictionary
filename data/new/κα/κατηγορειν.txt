Word: κατηγορειν

Etymology:
The word κατηγορειν (katēgorein) comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἀγορεύω (agoreuō) meaning "to speak in public". The word originally referred to the act of accusing someone in a public setting.

Grammar:
κατηγορειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often translated to English as "to accuse" or "to charge".

Description:
κατηγορειν is a strong and forceful word that is used to describe the act of formally accusing someone of a crime or wrongdoing. It is often used in legal contexts, such as in courtrooms or in the media when reporting on legal proceedings. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any situation in which someone is being accused or blamed for something.
