Word: κατελημφθην

Etymology:
The word κατελημφθην is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαμβάνω (lamvano), which means "to take" or "to seize". The prefix κατα (kata) means "down" or "against", so κατελημφθην can be translated as "I was seized" or "I was taken down".

Grammar:
κατελημφθην is the first person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb καταλαμβάνω (katalambano), which means "to seize" or "to take hold of". The aorist tense indicates a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject (in this case, "I") is the recipient of the action.
