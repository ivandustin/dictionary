Word: καταλλαξαντος

Etymology: καταλλαξαντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb καταλλάσσω (katallasso), which means "to reconcile" or "to exchange." The suffix -αντος (-antos) is a participle ending that indicates the past tense.

Grammar: καταλλαξαντος is the genitive singular form of the participle καταλλαξάντος (katallassantos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the participle is a verbal adjective that describes the action of the verb.

Description: καταλλαξαντος can be translated as "of the one who reconciled" or "of the one who exchanged." It is often used in Christian theology to refer to the reconciliation between God and humanity through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ. In Greek literature, it can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of reconciling or making peace between two parties.
