The word "καταλυθηι" (kataluthēi) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "καταλύω" (katalyō), which means "to dissolve, destroy, overthrow, or dismantle." 

In terms of grammar, "καταλυθηι" is in the third person singular form, which means it refers to a single subject who completed the action. 

Overall, the word "καταλυθηι" can be translated to mean "he/she/it was dissolved, destroyed, overthrown, or dismantled."
