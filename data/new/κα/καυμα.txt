Word: καυμα

Etymology: The word καυμα (kauma) comes from the Greek verb καίω (kaio), which means "to burn". It is related to the English word "caustic".

Grammar: καυμα is a noun in the neuter gender. It is singular and has no plural form.

Description: καυμα refers to a burning or scorching heat. It can also refer to a fever or inflammation. In ancient Greek literature, it was often used to describe the heat of the sun or the heat of battle. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to describe extreme heat or fever.
