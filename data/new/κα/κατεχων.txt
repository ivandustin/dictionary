Word: κατεχων

Etymology:
The word κατεχων comes from the Greek verb κατέχω (katechō), which means "to hold down" or "to possess". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down", and the verb έχω (echō), meaning "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar:
κατεχων is the present participle of the verb κατέχω (katechō) in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action or state of being. In this case, it would mean "the one who is holding down" or "the one who is possessing".

Description:
κατεχων is a word that describes someone who is holding down or possessing something. It could be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a military or political situation where one group is trying to hold down or control another group. It could also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is holding onto something tightly or possessing something with great determination.
