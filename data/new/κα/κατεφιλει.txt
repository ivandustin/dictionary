Word: κατεφίλει (katephilei)

Etymology: κατεφίλει is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "towards", and φιλέω (phileō), which means "to love" or "to kiss". Therefore, κατεφίλει can be translated as "to kiss passionately" or "to embrace with love".

Grammar: κατεφίλει is a third-person singular verb in the past tense of the indicative mood. It belongs to the aorist tense, which expresses a completed action in the past. The verb is formed by adding the prefix κατά to the verb φιλέω and conjugating it in the aorist tense.

Description: κατεφίλει is a verb that describes an intense and passionate act of kissing or embracing. It implies a deep affection and love between the two people involved. The word is often used in romantic contexts, but it can also be used to describe the love and affection between family members or close friends.
