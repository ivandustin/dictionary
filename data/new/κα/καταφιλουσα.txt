Word: καταφιλουσα

Etymology: The word καταφιλουσα is derived from the Greek words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and φιλέω (phileō) meaning "to love" or "to kiss". 

Grammar: καταφιλουσα is a feminine singular participle in the present tense, active voice, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb καταφιλέω (kataphileō) which means "to kiss fervently" or "to kiss repeatedly". 

Description: καταφιλουσα describes a female subject who is actively and passionately kissing someone or something repeatedly. It can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is showing excessive affection or devotion towards someone or something.
