Word: κατοικουσιν

Etymology:
The word κατοικουσιν comes from the Greek verb κατοικέω (katoikeo), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates that the word is in the present tense and third person plural form.

Grammar:
κατοικουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person of the subject. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
κατοικουσιν means "they dwell" or "they inhabit". It is often used to describe where a group of people live or reside. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of being or a condition. For example, one might say "they dwell in ignorance" to describe a group of people who are unaware of something.
