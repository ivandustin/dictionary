Word: καλεσαντα

Etymology: καλεσαντα is the aorist active participle of the Greek verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *kel-, which means "to call" or "to cry out".

Grammar: καλεσαντα is a masculine singular form of the aorist active participle in Greek. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. In this form, it modifies a masculine singular noun in the nominative case.

Description: καλεσαντα describes someone who has been called or summoned in the past. It can be translated as "the one who was called" or "the one who was summoned". The word is often used in the context of ancient Greek literature and mythology, where heroes and gods are frequently called upon to perform great deeds.
