Word: κακωσων

Etymology: κακωσων is a Greek word derived from the verb κακόω (kakóo), which means "to harm" or "to injure". The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a masculine noun ending that denotes the agent or doer of the action.

Grammar: κακωσων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form of κακωσων is κακωνος (kakōnos), the dative form is κακωνι (kakōni), and the accusative form is κακωνα (kakōna).

Description: κακωσων refers to someone who causes harm or injury to others. It can also be used to describe a person who is malicious or malevolent. In Greek mythology, the god Ares was often referred to as κακωσων because of his association with war and violence.
