Word: καινον

Etymology:
The word καινον (kainon) is derived from the Greek adjective καινός (kainos), which means "new" or "fresh". It is related to the Greek verb καινίζω (kainizo), which means "to make new" or "to renew".

Grammar:
In Greek, καινον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is often used as an adjective to describe something new or fresh.

Description:
καινον can be translated as "new thing" or "new creation". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the new life that believers receive through faith in Jesus Christ. It is also used in other contexts to describe something that is innovative or novel.
