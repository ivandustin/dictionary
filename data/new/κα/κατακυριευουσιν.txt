Word: κατακυριευουσιν

Etymology:
The word κατακυριευουσιν is derived from the Greek verb κατακυριεύω (katakyrieuō), which means "to subdue, conquer, or dominate." The word is composed of two parts: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against," and κυριεύω (kyrieuō), which means "to rule" or "to dominate."

Grammar:
κατακυριευουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is a compound verb, formed by adding the prefix κατά (kata) to the verb κυριεύω (kyrieuō). The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of subduing or conquering.

Description:
κατακυριευουσιν is a Greek verb that describes the act of subduing or conquering. It is often used in a military context, to describe the conquest of a city or territory. The word implies a sense of force or domination, and is often used to describe a violent or aggressive takeover. In modern usage, the word can also be used metaphorically to describe the domination or control of one person or group over another.
