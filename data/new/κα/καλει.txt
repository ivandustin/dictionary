Word: καλει

Etymology:
The word καλει (kalei) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The word has been used in Greek literature for thousands of years and has been passed down through generations.

καλει (kalei) is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular form of the verb καλέω (kaleo). The verb is conjugated as follows:

Καλώ (kalo) - I call
Καλείς (kaleis) - You call
Καλεί (kalei) - He/She/It calls
Καλούμε (kaloume) - We call
Καλείτε (kaletε) - You call
Καλούν (kaloun) - They call

Description:
The word καλει (kalei) is used to describe the act of calling or summoning someone or something. It can also be used to describe the act of inviting someone to an event or gathering. The word is often used in Greek literature and is an important part of the Greek language.
