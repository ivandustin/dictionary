Word: κατηρτισω

Etymology:
The word κατηρτισω (katērtisō) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ἀρτίζω (artizō), which means "to make ready" or "to prepare." The prefix κατά (katá) means "down" or "completely," so κατηρτισω can be translated as "to completely prepare" or "to make fully ready."

Grammar:
κατηρτισω is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the verb κατηρτίζω (katērtizō). It is a regular verb in the Greek language, and its conjugation follows the pattern of other verbs in the -ίζω (-izō) family.

Description:
κατηρτισω is a verb that means "to fully prepare" or "to make completely ready." It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as preparing a meal, organizing a space, or getting ready for an event. In the New Testament, it is often used in a spiritual sense, referring to the preparation of believers for their role in the kingdom of God.
