Word: κατοικιας

Etymology:
The word κατοικιας comes from the Greek root word κατοικία (katoikía), which means "dwelling" or "residence". The suffix -ας (-as) is a genitive singular ending, indicating possession or relationship.

Grammar:
κατοικιας is a noun in the genitive singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated into English as "of" or "belonging to".

Description:
κατοικιας refers to a dwelling or residence, and specifically indicates possession or ownership of a dwelling. It can also be used more broadly to refer to the concept of housing or the act of dwelling.
