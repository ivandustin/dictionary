Word: καλλιελαιον

Etymology: The word καλλιελαιον is derived from the Greek words καλλι (kalli) meaning "beautiful" and ελαιον (elaion) meaning "oil". 

Grammar: καλλιελαιον is a neuter noun in the singular form. 

Description: καλλιελαιον refers to a type of fragrant oil that was used in ancient Greece for various purposes such as perfuming the body, anointing the hair, and as a cosmetic. It was made from a variety of plants such as myrtle, rose, and lavender, and was highly valued for its pleasant aroma and therapeutic properties. The use of καλλιελαιον was considered a sign of luxury and refinement in ancient Greek society.
