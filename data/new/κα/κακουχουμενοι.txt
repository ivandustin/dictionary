Word: κακουχουμενοι

Etymology: The word κακουχουμενοι comes from the Greek verb κακούχειν (kakoúchein), which means "to maltreat" or "to mistreat". The suffix -ούμενοι (-oúmenoi) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Grammar: κακουχουμενοι is a plural masculine passive participle in the present tense. It is derived from the verb κακούχειν, which is in the present active indicative. The participle form indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action themselves.

Description: κακουχουμενοι describes a group of people who are being mistreated or maltreated. It implies that they are not in control of their situation and are suffering at the hands of others. The word has a negative connotation and suggests that the mistreatment is unjust or cruel.
