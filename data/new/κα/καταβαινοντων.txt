Word: καταβαινοντων

Etymology: καταβαινοντων is a present participle form of the Greek verb καταβαίνω (katabaino), which means "to go down" or "to descend". The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down", and the verb βαίνω (baino), meaning "to go".

Grammar: καταβαινοντων is a present participle in the genitive case, masculine gender, and plural number. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently descending or going down.

Description: καταβαινοντων can be translated as "of those who are descending" or "of those who are going down". It is often used in the context of physical movement, such as descending a mountain or going down a staircase. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the descent of angels or the Holy Spirit.
