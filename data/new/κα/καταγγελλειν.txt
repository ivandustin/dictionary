Etymology: 
The word καταγγελλειν (katangellein) comes from the Greek roots κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and ἀγγέλλειν (angellein) meaning "to announce" or "to report". Therefore, καταγγελλειν means "to accuse" or "to denounce".

Grammar:
καταγγελλειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is καταγγελλ-. The verb is composed of the prefix κατά and the verb ἀγγέλλειν.

Description:
καταγγελλειν is a Greek verb that means "to accuse" or "to denounce". It is often used in legal contexts to refer to the act of reporting a crime or wrongdoing. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of publicly criticizing or condemning someone or something.
