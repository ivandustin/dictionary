Word: καταδικασθητε

Etymology: The word καταδικασθητε comes from the Greek verb καταδικάζω (katadikazo), which means "to condemn" or "to sentence". The suffix -θητε (-thete) is a passive voice ending, indicating that the word is in the second person plural form.

Grammar: καταδικασθητε is a verb in the second person plural form of the aorist passive imperative tense in Greek. It is a command form, indicating that the speaker is instructing a group of people to be condemned or sentenced.

Description: καταδικασθητε is a strong and forceful word that carries a sense of judgment and condemnation. It is often used in legal or religious contexts to indicate a verdict or sentence that has been passed down. In everyday language, it can be used to express strong disapproval or condemnation of someone's actions or behavior.
