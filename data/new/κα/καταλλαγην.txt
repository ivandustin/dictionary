Word: καταλλαγην

Etymology:
The word καταλλαγην comes from the Greek verb καταλλάσσω (katallasso), which means "to reconcile" or "to bring back into a friendly relationship." The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against," and the verb ἀλλάσσω (allasso), which means "to change" or "to exchange."

Grammar:
καταλλαγην is a Greek noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative singular form of the noun καταλλαγή (katallage), which means "reconciliation" or "restoration to favor." The noun is feminine in gender.

Description:
καταλλαγην is a term used in Christian theology to refer to the reconciliation of humanity with God through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ. It is often translated as "atonement" or "propitiation" in English. The concept of καταλλαγην is central to the Christian faith, as it represents the idea that through Christ's death and resurrection, humanity can be restored to a right relationship with God.
