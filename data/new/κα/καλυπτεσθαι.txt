Word: καλυπτεσθαι

Etymology:
The word καλυπτεσθαι comes from the Greek verb καλύπτω (kalýpto), which means "to cover" or "to conceal". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning "to cover oneself" or "to hide oneself".

Grammar:
καλυπτεσθαι is a reflexive verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, reflexive verbs indicate that the subject is performing the action on itself. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
καλυπτεσθαι means "to cover oneself" or "to hide oneself". It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone covers themselves with a blanket, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone tries to conceal their emotions or intentions. The verb can also be used in a passive sense, meaning "to be covered" or "to be hidden".
