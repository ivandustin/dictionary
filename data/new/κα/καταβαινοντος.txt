Word: καταβαινοντος

Etymology: καταβαινοντος is a present participle of the Greek verb καταβαίνω (katabainō), which means "to go down" or "to descend". The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down", and the verb βαίνω (bainō), meaning "to go".

Grammar: καταβαινοντος is a masculine singular genitive form of the present participle of the verb καταβαίνω. In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: καταβαινοντος can be translated as "of the one going down" or "of the one descending". It is often used in the context of describing a person or object that is moving from a higher position to a lower one. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe the descent of the Holy Spirit upon Jesus at his baptism (Mark 1:10).
