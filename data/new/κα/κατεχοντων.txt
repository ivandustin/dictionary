Word: κατεχόντων

Etymology:
The word κατεχόντων is a present participle of the Greek verb κατέχω (katechō), which means "to hold down" or "to possess". The word is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "against", and the verb ἔχω (echō), which means "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar:
κατεχόντων is a present participle in the genitive case, masculine gender, and plural number. It is formed by adding the suffix -οντ- to the present stem κατεχ- and the ending -ων, which indicates the genitive plural.

Description:
The word κατεχόντων is used to describe a group of people who are currently holding down or possessing something. It can be translated as "those who are holding down" or "those who possess". The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning.
