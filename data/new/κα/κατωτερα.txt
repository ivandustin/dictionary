Word: κατωτερα

Etymology: κατωτερα is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: κάτω (káto) meaning "down" and τεράω (teráō) meaning "to be afraid". Therefore, κατωτερα can be translated as "lower" or "inferior".

Grammar: κατωτερα is the comparative form of the adjective κατώτερος (katóteros), which means "lower" or "inferior". It is used to compare two things, indicating that one is lower or inferior to the other. In Greek, the comparative form is formed by adding the suffix -τερος (-teros) to the stem of the adjective.

Description: κατωτερα is an adjective that is commonly used in Greek to describe something that is lower or inferior in comparison to something else. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a lower floor or a lower rank, as well as abstract concepts, such as a lower level of education or a lower quality of life. The word is often used in a negative context, implying that the thing being described is not as good as something else.
