Word: καλαμον

Etymology:
The word καλαμον (kalamon) is derived from the Greek word κάλαμος (kalamos), which means "reed" or "stalk." The word κάλαμος is also the root of the English word "calamus."

Grammar:
In Greek, καλαμον is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is καλάμου (kalamou).

Description:
καλαμον refers to a reed or a stalk, typically used for making writing pens, musical instruments, or baskets. In ancient Greece, reeds were also used for measuring liquids. The word καλαμον is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the reed that was given to Jesus as a mock scepter during his crucifixion.
