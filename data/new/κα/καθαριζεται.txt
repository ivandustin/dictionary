Word: καθαρίζεται

Etymology: καθαρίζεται comes from the Greek verb καθαρίζω (katharízo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse". It is derived from the adjective καθαρός (katharós), meaning "clean" or "pure".

Grammar: καθαρίζεται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is the third person singular form of the verb καθαρίζω.

Description: καθαρίζεται is a verb that describes the action of being purified or cleansed. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to the cleaning of a physical object, or in a figurative sense, such as when referring to the purification of the soul or mind. The passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb, rather than performing it.
