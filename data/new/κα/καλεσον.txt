Word: καλεσον

Etymology: καλεσον is a Greek word that comes from the verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon".

Grammar: καλεσον is a verb in the aorist imperative mood, second person singular. It is derived from the verb καλέω (kaleo), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: καλεσον is a command or an order to someone to call or summon someone else. It is often used in the context of inviting someone to come or to join in an activity. For example, "καλεσον τον φίλο σου να έρθει στο πάρτι" (kaleson ton filo sou na erthei sto parti) means "call your friend to come to the party".
