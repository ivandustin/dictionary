Word: κατεκαη

Etymology: κατεκαη comes from the Greek verb καίω (kaio), which means "to burn". The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "completely", so κατεκαη means "completely burned".

Grammar: κατεκαη is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, meaning the subject of the sentence is performing the action. κατεκαη is also in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: κατεκαη means "was completely burned" or "was consumed by fire". It can be used to describe a physical object that has been destroyed by fire, or a metaphorical situation that has been ruined or destroyed. For example, "The house was completely burned in the fire" or "His reputation was completely burned after the scandal".
