The word "καλης" (kalēs) is a Greek adjective in the feminine genitive singular form. It is derived from the root word "καλός" (kalos), which means "beautiful" or "good". 

In terms of grammar, "καλης" is used to describe a feminine noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. For example, "της καλης μητέρας" (tēs kalēs mēteras) means "of the good mother".

Overall, "καλης" is a versatile word that can be used to describe a variety of feminine nouns in Greek.
