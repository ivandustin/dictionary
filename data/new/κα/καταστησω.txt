Word: καταστησω

Etymology:
The word καταστησω comes from the Greek verb καταστήσω (katastésō), which is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), meaning "down" or "against," and the verb ἵστημι (hístēmi), meaning "to stand." Therefore, καταστήσω means "to cause to stand down" or "to establish."

Grammar:
καταστησω is a first-person singular future active indicative form of the verb καταστήσω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, the active voice indicates that the subject performs the action, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
καταστησω means "I will establish" or "I will cause to stand down." It is a verb that implies the act of setting up or founding something, or making something stable and secure. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as establishing a relationship or a reputation. The word has a strong sense of purpose and determination, as it implies a deliberate and intentional action towards a specific goal.
