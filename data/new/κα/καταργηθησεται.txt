Word: καταργηθησεται

Etymology: καταργηθησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb καταργέω (katargéō), which means "to abolish" or "to nullify". The word is formed by adding the suffix -θησεται (-thēsetai), which is a future passive indicative ending.

Grammar: καταργηθησεται is a third-person singular future passive indicative form of the verb καταργέω (katargéō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, while the passive voice indicates that the subject receives the action of the verb.

Description: καταργηθησεται means "it will be abolished" or "it will be nullified". The word is often used in legal or political contexts to refer to the cancellation or repeal of a law or regulation. It can also be used more broadly to describe the ending or termination of something.
