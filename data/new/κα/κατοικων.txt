Word: κατοικων

Etymology:
The word κατοικων comes from the Greek verb κατοικέω (katoikeo), which means "to dwell" or "to inhabit". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "according to", and the verb οἰκέω (oikeo), meaning "to dwell" or "to live in".

Grammar:
κατοικων is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb κατοικέω (katoikeo), which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense. The participle form indicates that the subject is currently dwelling or inhabiting.

Description:
κατοικων can be translated to "dwelling" or "inhabiting". It is often used in the context of describing a person or group of people who are currently residing in a particular place. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe the "dwelling" or "inhabiting" of God in believers (1 Corinthians 3:16). In other contexts, it can refer to the act of settling or establishing oneself in a new place.
