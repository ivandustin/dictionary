The word "καναναιον" (Kananaios) is a Greek word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to refer to the Canaanites, an ancient people who inhabited the land of Canaan, which is now modern-day Israel, Palestine, Lebanon, and Syria. The word is derived from the Hebrew word "Kena'an" which means "merchant" or "trader". In terms of grammar, "καναναιον" is a masculine noun in the nominative case.
