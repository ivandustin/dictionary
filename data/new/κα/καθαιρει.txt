Word: καθαιρει

Etymology:
The word καθαιρει (kathairei) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθαίρω (kathairō), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), which means "down" or "away", and the verb αἱρέω (haireō), which means "to take" or "to choose".

Grammar:
καθαιρει is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the third person singular form. The verb stem is καθαιρ-, and the ending -ει indicates the present tense, third person singular.

Description:
The word καθαιρει (kathairei) means "he/she/it cleanses" or "he/she/it purifies" in Greek. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the purification of the soul or the removal of sin. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to the act of cleaning or purifying something physical, such as a room or a piece of clothing.
