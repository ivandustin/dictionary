Word: κατεκλιναν

Etymology:
The word κατεκλιναν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κλίνω (klinó), which means "to lean" or "to incline". The prefix κατα- (kata-) adds the meaning of "down" or "toward the ground". Therefore, κατεκλιναν means "they leaned down" or "they inclined towards the ground".

Grammar:
κατεκλιναν is a third-person plural aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice shows that the subject is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
κατεκλιναν is a verb that describes a physical action of leaning or inclining towards the ground. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone leans down to pick something up, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone bows down in submission or reverence.
