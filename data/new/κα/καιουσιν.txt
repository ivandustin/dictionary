Word: καιουσιν

Etymology: The word καιουσιν (kaiousin) is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb καίω (kaio), which means "to burn" or "to set on fire". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *keh₂i- meaning "to burn".

Grammar: καιουσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative form of the verb καίω (kaio). This form indicates that the subject (whoever is doing the burning) is currently in the process of burning something. 

Description: καιουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they are burning" or "they are setting on fire". It can be used to describe a literal burning of something, such as a fire, or a metaphorical burning, such as a burning passion or desire. The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the burning is currently happening.
