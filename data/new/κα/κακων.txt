Word: κακων

Etymology:
The word κακων is derived from the Greek word κακός (kakós), which means "bad" or "evil". It is the genitive plural form of κακός.

Grammar:
In Greek, κακων is the genitive plural form of κακός. It is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated as "of the bad" or "of the evil".

Description:
κακων is a Greek word that is often used in philosophical and moral contexts to describe the concept of evil or badness. It is commonly used in discussions of ethics and morality, and is often contrasted with the concept of good or virtue. In literature and mythology, κακων is often associated with villains and antagonists, and is used to describe their wickedness or malevolence.
