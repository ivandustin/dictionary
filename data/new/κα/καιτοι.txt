Word: καιτοι

Etymology: καιτοι is a Greek conjunction that comes from the combination of καί (meaning "and") and τοι (meaning "certainly" or "indeed").

Grammar: καιτοι is a conjunction that is used to connect two clauses or phrases. It is often translated as "although" or "even though" in English. It is followed by a verb in the indicative mood.

Description: καιτοι is a conjunction that expresses a contrast between two ideas. It is used to introduce a subordinate clause that presents a fact that is contrary to what is stated in the main clause. For example, "καιτοι οὐκ ἔχω πολλοὺς φίλους, ἀλλὰ οἱ ὄντες εἰσὶν ἀληθῶς φίλοι" (although I do not have many friends, those who are my friends are truly friends).
