Word: καταναλισκον

Etymology: The word καταναλισκον comes from the Greek verb καταναλίσκω (katanalísko), which means "to consume completely" or "to waste away". It is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), meaning "down" or "completely", and the verb ἀναλίσκω (analísko), meaning "to use up" or "to spend".

Grammar: καταναλισκον is the present participle form of the verb καταναλίσκω (katanalísko), which means "consuming completely" or "wasting away". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: καταναλισκον describes something or someone that is in the process of being consumed completely or wasted away. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as food or resources, as well as abstract concepts, such as time or energy. The word carries a negative connotation, implying that the consumption or waste is excessive or unnecessary.
