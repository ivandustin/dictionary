Word: κατηισχυνοντο

Etymology:
The word κατηισχυνοντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αἰσχύνομαι (aischýnomai), which means "to feel shame or disgrace." The prefix κατά (katá) means "down" or "against," so κατηισχυνοντο can be translated as "they were shamed" or "they were disgraced."

Grammar:
κατηισχυνοντο is a third-person plural past tense verb in the Greek language. It is in the indicative mood, which means that it is a statement of fact. The verb is in the passive voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

κατηισχυνοντο is a word that describes a state of shame or disgrace. It is often used in the context of social or moral wrongdoing, where someone has done something that is considered shameful or dishonorable. The word can also be used to describe a feeling of embarrassment or humiliation.
