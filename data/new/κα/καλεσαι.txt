Word: καλεσαι

Etymology: καλεσαι is a verb in the Greek language, which comes from the root word καλέω (kaleo), meaning "to call" or "to summon".

Grammar: καλεσαι is a second person singular present active indicative form of the verb καλέω (kaleo). It is used to address someone directly and means "you call" or "you summon".

Description: καλεσαι is a common verb in Greek and is used in various contexts. It can be used to refer to calling someone by name, inviting someone to an event or gathering, or summoning someone for a specific purpose. It is often used in everyday conversation and is an important part of the Greek language.
