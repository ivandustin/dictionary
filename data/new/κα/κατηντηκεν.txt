Word: κατηντηκεν

Etymology:
The word κατηντηκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αντί (anti), which means "against" or "opposite." The prefix κατά (kata) means "down" or "against," and the verb ending -εν (-en) indicates the present perfect tense. Therefore, κατηντηκεν means "has come down against" or "has arrived at a destination."

Grammar:
κατηντηκεν is a third-person singular verb in the present perfect tense. It is formed by adding the prefix κατά (kata) to the root word αντί (anti) and the verb ending -εν (-en). The verb is active, indicating that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
κατηντηκεν is a Greek verb that describes the action of arriving at a destination or coming down against something. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a person's arrival at a particular state or condition. The verb is in the present perfect tense, indicating that the action has been completed in the recent past and has ongoing relevance to the present.
