Word: κατηφειαν

Etymology:
The word κατηφειαν comes from the Greek verb κατηφείαω (katēpheiaō), which means "to be downcast" or "to be dejected." It is derived from the prefix κατά (katá), meaning "down," and the verb ἤφεια (ēpheia), meaning "to speak softly" or "to whisper."

Grammar:
κατηφειαν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender. It is the accusative form of the noun κατηφεία (katēpheia), which means "dejection" or "downcastness."

Description:
κατηφειαν refers to a state of being downcast or dejected. It can be used to describe a person's mood or demeanor, or to refer to a general feeling of sadness or depression. The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context, to describe a feeling of spiritual or moral failure or unworthiness.
