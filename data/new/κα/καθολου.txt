Word: καθολου

Etymology: καθολου comes from the Greek word καθόλου (katholou), which means "entirely" or "completely". It is derived from the prefix κατά (kata), meaning "down" or "through", and the adjective ὅλος (holos), meaning "whole" or "entire".

Grammar: καθολου is an adverb in Greek, which means it modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is often used to indicate the completeness or totality of an action or state.

Description: καθολου is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to express a sense of completeness or totality, as in "I completely forgot about that" or "He thoroughly enjoyed the movie". It can also be used to indicate a lack of exception or distinction, as in "Everyone is welcome here, without exception" or "The rule applies universally, without distinction".
