Word: καταβολην

Etymology:
The word καταβολην comes from the Greek verb καταβάλλω (kataballō), which means "to throw down" or "to lay down". The suffix -ην (-en) is a common ending for nouns in Greek.

Grammar:
καταβολην is a singular feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
καταβολην can be translated as "deposit" or "payment". It is often used in financial contexts, such as in reference to a down payment on a loan or the initial deposit required to open a bank account. In the New Testament of the Bible, καταβολην is used to refer to the "foundation" or "laying down" of something, such as the foundation of the world or the foundation of a building.
