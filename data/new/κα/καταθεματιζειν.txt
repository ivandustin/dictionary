Word: καταθεματιζειν

Etymology: The word καταθεματιζειν comes from the Greek word κατάθεμα (katathema) which means "curse" or "anathema". The suffix -ιζειν (-izein) is added to the end of the word to indicate the action of cursing or anathematizing.

Grammar: καταθεματιζειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ιζειν to the stem καταθεματ-.

Description: καταθεματιζειν means "to curse" or "to anathematize" in Greek. It is a strong word that is often used in religious contexts to refer to the act of condemning someone or something as evil or sinful. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the act of pronouncing a curse on someone or something, often as a form of punishment or judgment.
