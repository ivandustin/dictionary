Word: κατακαυχάσαι

Etymology:
The word κατακαυχάσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καυχάομαι (kaucháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The prefix κατα- (kata-) in this word means "down" or "against", so κατακαυχάσαι can be translated as "to boast against" or "to boast down".

Grammar:
κατακαυχάσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in the Greek language. This means that it is a command directed towards one person, and it is in the past tense. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action, and the imperative mood indicates that the verb is a command.

Description:
κατακαυχάσαι is a strong word that conveys a sense of arrogance and pride. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is boasting or bragging excessively, or who is trying to put others down by boasting about their own accomplishments. The word can also be used to describe someone who is being overly confident or arrogant in a situation where they should be more humble.
