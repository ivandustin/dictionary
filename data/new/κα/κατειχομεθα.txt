The word "κατειχομεθα" is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root verb "εχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold". In terms of grammar, "κατειχομεθα" is a second-person plural form, meaning it refers to a group of people. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action themselves. The indicative mood suggests that the speaker is making a statement of fact, rather than expressing doubt or uncertainty.

Overall, "κατειχομεθα" is a word that describes a state of being held down or restrained, and is used in a passive voice to refer to a group of people.
