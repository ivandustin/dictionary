Word: καταντησαντες

Etymology: The word καταντησαντες is a compound word in Greek, formed by the prefix κατα- (meaning "down") and the verb ἀντάω (antao) which means "to meet face to face". The suffix -σαντες (-santes) is a participle ending indicating past tense and plural number. Therefore, καταντησαντες means "those who have met face to face down (with someone or something)".

Grammar: καταντησαντες is a past participle in the Greek language, indicating that the action of meeting face to face down has already taken place. It is in the masculine gender, plural number, and nominative case. 

Description: καταντησαντες is a word that describes a group of people who have met face to face down with someone or something. The context in which this word is used can vary, but it often implies a sense of defeat or surrender. It can also be used to describe a group of people who have reached a low point or a state of despair.
