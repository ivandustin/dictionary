Word: καιομενη

Etymology:
The word καιομενη is a present participle form of the Greek verb καίω (kaio), which means "to burn". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kewH- meaning "to burn, smoke, or glow".

Grammar:
In Greek, καιομενη is a feminine singular nominative present participle form of the verb καίω. As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action of burning, and as a feminine singular nominative, it agrees with a feminine singular subject.

Description:
The word καιομενη describes a feminine singular subject that is currently burning or on fire. It can be used to describe a literal fire or a metaphorical burning, such as a passionate love or a strong desire. The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a situation or emotion that is intense or overwhelming.
