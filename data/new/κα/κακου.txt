The word "κακου" (kakou) is a Greek noun in the genitive case, which means "of evil" or "of badness". It is derived from the adjective "κακός" (kakos), which means "bad" or "evil". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. 

Example sentence: Η συμπεριφορά του ήταν κακού (I simperifora tou itan kakou) - His behavior was bad.
