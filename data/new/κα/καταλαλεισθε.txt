Word: καταλαλεισθε

Etymology:
The word καταλαλεισθε is derived from the Greek verb καταλαλέω (katalaleo), which means "to speak against, to slander, to defame". It is composed of two parts: κατά (kata), which means "against" or "down", and λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak".

Grammar:
καταλαλεισθε is a second person plural present passive indicative form of the verb καταλαλέω (katalaleo). It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are being spoken against or defamed by someone else.

Description:
καταλαλεισθε is a verb form that describes the act of being slandered or defamed by someone else. It is often used in a negative context and implies that the subject is being unfairly criticized or attacked. The word can be found in various contexts, including religious texts and legal documents.
