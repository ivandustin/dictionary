Word: κατακρινεις

Etymology:
The word κατακρινεις comes from the Greek root words κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and κρίνω (krinō) meaning "to judge" or "to decide". Therefore, κατακρινεις means "to condemn" or "to judge against".

Grammar:
κατακρινεις is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It is derived from the verb κατακρίνω (katakrinō) which means "to condemn" or "to judge against". The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: κατακρίνω (katakrinō), κατακρίνεις (katakrineis), κατακρίνει (katakrinei)
- Plural: κατακρίνουμε (katakrinoume), κατακρίνετε (katakrinete), κατακρίνουν (katakrinoun)

Description:
κατακρινεις is a Greek verb that means "to condemn" or "to judge against". It is often used in legal or moral contexts to express disapproval or criticism of someone's actions or behavior. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to find fault with" or "to censure". In Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the judgment of gods or other divine beings.
