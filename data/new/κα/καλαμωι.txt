Word: καλαμωι

Etymology: The word καλαμωι (kalamo̱i) comes from the Greek word κάλαμος (kálamos), which means "reed" or "stalk". The suffix -ωι (-oi) is a common ending in Greek that denotes a group or collection of something.

Grammar: καλαμωι is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is masculine in gender.

Description: καλαμωι refers to a group or collection of reeds or stalks. It can be used to describe a bundle of reeds or a group of plants with long, slender stems. The word is often used in the context of weaving or basket-making, where reeds are commonly used as a material.
