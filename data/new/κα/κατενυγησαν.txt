Word: κατενυγησαν

Etymology: The word κατενυγησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and the verb ἐνύγχια (enyngchia) meaning "to press hard" or "to squeeze". 

Grammar: κατενυγησαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb κατενύγχω (katenyncho), which means "to press down" or "to subdue". 

Description: κατενυγησαν is a verb that describes an action of pressing down or subduing something or someone. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the defeat or suppression of an enemy or a rebellion.
