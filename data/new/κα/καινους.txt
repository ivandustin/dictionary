Word: καινους

Etymology: καινους comes from the Greek word καινός (kainos), which means "new" or "fresh".

Grammar: καινους is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also declined in the genitive singular form as καινοῦ (kainou).

Description: καινους is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe a new or renewed state of being. It is often used in reference to the new covenant between God and humanity, as well as the new life that believers receive through faith in Jesus Christ. In secular Greek, καινους can also refer to something that is new or fresh, such as a new garment or a new idea.
