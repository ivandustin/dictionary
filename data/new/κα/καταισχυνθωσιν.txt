Word: καταισχυνθωσιν

Etymology:
The word καταισχυνθωσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αἰσχύνη (aishunē), which means "shame" or "disgrace". The prefix κατα- (kata-) means "down" or "against", so καταισχυνθωσιν can be translated as "to be put to shame" or "to be disgraced".

Grammar:
καταισχυνθωσιν is a third-person plural present passive subjunctive verb in Greek. It is derived from the verb καταισχύνω (kataischynō), which means "to put to shame" or "to disgrace". The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action is uncertain or hypothetical.

Description:
καταισχυνθωσιν is a verb that describes a state of being in which someone or something is put to shame or disgraced. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to describe a person who has been publicly humiliated, or to express a sense of shame or embarrassment about one's own actions. The word has a strong negative connotation and is often used to express moral condemnation or disapproval.
