Word: κατανοεις

Etymology: κατανοεις is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata), which means "down" or "according to," and νοεω (noeo), which means "to perceive" or "to understand." Therefore, κατανοεις can be translated as "to understand thoroughly" or "to comprehend."

Grammar: κατανοεις is a second-person singular present indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb κατανοέω (katanoeo), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend." The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now. The second-person singular form indicates that the subject is "you."

Description: κατανοεις is a verb that expresses the act of understanding or comprehending something thoroughly. It implies a deep level of understanding and insight into a particular subject or situation. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in academic or philosophical discussions, personal relationships, or everyday conversations.
