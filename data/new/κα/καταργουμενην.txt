Word: καταργουμενην

Etymology: καταργουμενην is a Greek word that comes from the verb καταργέω (katargeo), which means "to abolish" or "to make ineffective". The word is in the present participle form, which means "being abolished" or "being made ineffective".

Grammar: καταργουμενην is a feminine singular accusative form of the present participle of the verb καταργέω. In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of the verb.

Description: καταργουμενην describes something that is currently being abolished or made ineffective. It can be used to describe a physical object, a law, a tradition, or an idea. The word implies that the process of abolishing or making something ineffective is ongoing and not yet complete.
