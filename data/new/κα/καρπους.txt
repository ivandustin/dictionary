Word: καρπούς

Etymology: The word καρπούς comes from the Greek word καρπός (karpos), which means "fruit" or "produce".

Grammar: καρπούς is a noun in the plural form. It is the accusative case of καρπός.

Description: καρπούς refers to fruits or crops that are harvested from trees or plants. It can also refer to the yield or profit from a business or investment. In the New Testament, it is used metaphorically to refer to the fruits of one's labor or the results of one's actions.
