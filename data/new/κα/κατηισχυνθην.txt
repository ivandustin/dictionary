Word: κατηισχυνθην

Etymology: The word κατηισχυνθην is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: κατά (kata) meaning "down" or "against" and αἰσχύνομαι (aischynomai) meaning "to be ashamed". Therefore, κατηισχυνθην means "to be ashamed down" or "to be ashamed against".

Grammar: κατηισχυνθην is a passive verb in the aorist tense, which means that the action of being ashamed was done to the subject of the sentence. In this case, the subject is not specified.

Description: κατηισχυνθην is a rare and archaic Greek verb that is not commonly used in modern Greek. It is often found in ancient Greek texts, such as the Bible and other religious texts. The verb conveys a strong sense of shame and humiliation, and is often used in a moral or ethical context.
