Word: καθαριζετε

Etymology: καθαριζετε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθαρός (katharos), meaning "clean" or "pure". The verb form καθαριζετε (katharizete) means "you (plural) clean" or "you (plural) purify".

Grammar: καθαριζετε is a second person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are currently cleaning or purifying something.

Description: καθαριζετε is a verb that is often used in religious contexts, particularly in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to describe the act of purifying oneself or one's surroundings, both physically and spiritually. In modern Greek, it is also used in everyday language to describe the act of cleaning or tidying up.
