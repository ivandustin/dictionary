Word: καιπερ

Etymology: καιπερ is a Greek adverb that is derived from the combination of two words, καί (kai) meaning "and" and πέρα (pera) meaning "beyond". The word καιπερ is used to express a concession or a contrast, similar to the English word "although".

Grammar: καιπερ is an adverb in Greek and is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is often used in conjunction with the conjunction καί (kai) to express a contrast or concession.

Description: καιπερ is a useful word in Greek that allows speakers to express a contrast or concession in a concise and effective way. It is commonly used in both written and spoken Greek and is an important part of the language's vocabulary.
