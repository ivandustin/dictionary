Word: κατεδίωξεν

Etymology: κατεδίωξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix κατά (meaning "down") and the verb διώκω (meaning "to pursue"). The prefix κατά adds the sense of "downward" or "completely" to the verb διώκω, resulting in the meaning "to pursue relentlessly" or "to chase down".

Grammar: κατεδίωξεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb καταδιώκω (katadióko), which means "to pursue relentlessly" or "to chase down". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject (he/she/it) performed the action.

Description: κατεδίωξεν is a strong verb that conveys the idea of pursuing or chasing down something or someone relentlessly. It implies a determined and persistent effort to catch or overcome an obstacle or opponent. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe the pursuit of a goal or the overcoming of a challenge.
