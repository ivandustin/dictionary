Word: καυχασαι

Etymology:
The word καυχασαι (kaukhasai) is a verb in the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the root word καυχάομαι (kaukháomai), which means "to boast" or "to brag". The word καυχάομαι is also the source of the English word "caucus".

Grammar:
καυχασαι is a second-person singular present middle/passive indicative form of the verb καυχάομαι. It is used to address someone directly and means "you are boasting" or "you are bragging". The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
καυχασαι is a word that expresses the act of boasting or bragging. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is excessively proud or arrogant. In Ancient Greek literature, the word is used to criticize individuals who are overly confident and boastful.
