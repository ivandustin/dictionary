Word: σφαγην

Etymology:
The word σφαγην (sphagēn) is derived from the Greek verb σφάζω (sphazō), which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher". It is related to the noun σφαγή (sphagē), which means "slaughter" or "butchery".

Grammar:
σφαγην is a noun in the singular form. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
σφαγην refers to a slaughter or butchery, where animals are killed and prepared for consumption. In ancient Greece, animal sacrifice was an important part of religious rituals, and the word σφαγην would have been used to describe the place where the sacrifices were performed. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a slaughterhouse or abattoir.
