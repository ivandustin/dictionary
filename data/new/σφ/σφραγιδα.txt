Word: σφραγίδα

The word σφραγίδα (sphragida) comes from the Greek verb σφραγίζω (sphragizo), which means "to seal" or "to stamp." The noun σφραγίδα refers to a seal or stamp used to mark or authenticate documents, letters, or other items.

In Greek, σφραγίδα is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is σφραγίς (sphragis), and its genitive singular form is σφραγίδος (sphragidos). The word can be declined in all cases and numbers.

σφραγίδα is a common word in Greek, used to refer to a seal or stamp used for various purposes. In ancient times, seals were used to authenticate documents, letters, and other items, and were often made of wax or clay.
