Word: σφραγίσον (sphragíson)

Etymology: The word σφραγίσον comes from the Greek verb σφραγίζω (sphragízo), which means "to seal" or "to stamp." It is derived from the noun σφραγίς (sphragís), which means "a seal" or "a stamp."

Grammar: σφραγίσον is a verb in the aorist imperative mood, second person singular. It is a command or request to someone to "seal" or "stamp" something.

Description: σφραγίσον is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Revelation. It is used in the context of sealing or marking something as belonging to God or being protected by God. In Revelation 7:3, an angel commands the four winds not to blow on the earth until the servants of God are sealed on their foreheads with the seal of the living God.
