Etymology: The word "ηθελησα" (ethelisa) comes from the Greek verb "θέλω" (thelo), which means "to want" or "to wish". The suffix "-σα" (-sa) is added to indicate the past tense, making "ηθελησα" (ethelisa) mean "I wanted" or "I wished".

Grammar: "Ηθελησα" (ethelisa) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "θέλω" (thelo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense is used to talk about actions or states that occurred in the past.

Description: "Ηθελησα" (ethelisa) is a Greek word that means "I wanted" or "I wished". It is used to express a desire or a wish that was present in the past.
