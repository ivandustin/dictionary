Etymology: The word "ηθετησαν" comes from the Greek verb "ηθετώ" (etheto), which means "to place" or "to set". It is formed by adding the suffix "-σαν" (-san) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "ηθετησαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb "ηθετώ" (etheto). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "ηθετησαν" is a verb form that indicates that a group of people (third person plural) placed or set something in the past. The specific meaning and context of the action would depend on the sentence or discourse in which it is used.
