Etymology: The word "αφοβως" (afobos) comes from the Greek word "αφόβως" (aphobos), which means "fearlessly" or "without fear". It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "without", and the word "φόβος" (phobos), meaning "fear".

Grammar: "Αφοβως" (afobos) is an adverb in Greek. Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, and they provide information about manner, time, place, degree, or frequency.

Description: "Αφοβως" (afobos) describes the action of doing something without fear or in a fearless manner. It implies a sense of courage, confidence, or bravery in the face of danger or uncertainty.
