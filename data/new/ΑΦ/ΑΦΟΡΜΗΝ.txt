Etymology: The word "αφορμήν" (aphormín) comes from the Greek verb "αφορμάω" (aphormáo), which means "to set in motion" or "to incite". It is derived from the noun "ὁρμή" (hormḗ), meaning "impulse" or "impetus".

Grammar: "Αφορμήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Αφορμήν" refers to a cause, motive, or stimulus that prompts or triggers something. It can also mean an occasion or opportunity that leads to a particular action or event.
