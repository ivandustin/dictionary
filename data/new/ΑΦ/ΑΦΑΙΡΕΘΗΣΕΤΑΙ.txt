Etymology: The word "αφαιρεθεται" comes from the Greek verb "αφαιρώ" (aphairo), which means "to remove" or "to take away". It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from", and the verb "αίρω" (airo), meaning "to lift" or "to take".

Grammar: "αφαιρεθεται" is the third person singular form of the verb "αφαιρείται" (aphaireitai) in Greek. It is in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated in this form to indicate that the action of removing or taking away is being done to a third person subject.

Description: "αφαιρεθεται" is a Greek word that means "it is removed" or "it is taken away". It is used to describe the action of something being taken away or removed from a certain place or situation.
