Etymology: The word "αφανιζουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αφανίζω" (afanízo), which means "to make disappear" or "to vanish." The suffix "-ουσιν" indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "Αφανιζουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The present tense indicates an action happening in the current moment.

Description: "Αφανιζουσιν" describes the action of making something disappear or vanish. It is used to refer to a group of people or things performing this action in the present tense.
