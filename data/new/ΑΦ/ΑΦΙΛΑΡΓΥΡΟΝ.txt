Etymology: The word "αφιλαργυρον" comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "φιλαργυρος" (philargyros), meaning "greedy" or "avaricious." 

Grammar: "αφιλαργυρον" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular form, and in the nominative case.

Description: "αφιλαργυρον" describes someone or something that is not greedy or avaricious. It refers to a person who is not driven by a strong desire for wealth or material possessions.
