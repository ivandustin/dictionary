Etymology: The word "αφωνος" (afonos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "without," and "φωνή" (phoni), meaning "voice." 

Grammar: "Αφωνος" is an adjective in Greek. It is used to describe something or someone that is voiceless or without a voice. 

Description: "Αφωνος" is often used to describe inanimate objects or animals that do not have the ability to produce sound. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is silent or unable to express themselves verbally.
