Etymology: The word "αφοριούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αφορίζω" (aphorízo), which means "to separate" or "to distinguish." It is derived from the prefix "από" (apo), meaning "from," and the verb "ορίζω" (orízo), meaning "to define" or "to set boundaries."

Grammar: "Αφοριούσιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "αφορίζω" (aphorízo) in the present tense. It is used to describe an action or state of separating or distinguishing performed by a group of people or entities.

Description: "Αφοριούσιν" is a Greek word that can be translated as "they separate" or "they distinguish." It is often used to express the act of setting boundaries, making distinctions, or separating one thing from another.
