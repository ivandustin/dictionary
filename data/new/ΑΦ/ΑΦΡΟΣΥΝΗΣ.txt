Etymology: The word "αφροσύνης" (aphrosynis) comes from the Greek root "αφρός" (aphros), meaning "foam," and the suffix "-σύνης" (-synis), which denotes a state or quality. It is derived from the ancient Greek myth of Aphrodite, the goddess of love and beauty, who was said to have been born from the foam of the sea.

Grammar: "Αφροσύνης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Αφροσύνης" refers to the state or quality of being foolish or senseless. It is often used to describe someone's lack of wisdom or rationality.
