Etymology: The word "αφιλαγαθοι" (aphilagathoi) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "φιλαγαθος" (philagathos), meaning "loving good." Therefore, "αφιλαγαθοι" can be translated as "not loving good" or "not inclined to do good."

Grammar: In Greek, "αφιλαγαθοι" is the plural form of the adjective "αφιλαγαθος" (aphilagathos). It is used to describe a group of people who are not inclined to do good or who lack a love for goodness.

Description: "αφιλαγαθοι" is a term used to describe individuals who are not motivated by or interested in doing good deeds or acting in a morally upright manner. It implies a lack of virtue or a disregard for ethical principles.
