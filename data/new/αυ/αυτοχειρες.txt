The word "αυτοχειρες" (autokheires) is a Greek term that literally translates to "self-hand" or "self-cutting." It refers to someone who commits suicide by their own hand. The word is derived from the Greek words "αυτός" (autos), meaning "self," and "χείρ" (kheir), meaning "hand." 

In terms of grammar, "αυτοχειρες" is a noun in the plural form. It is a compound word made up of two separate words, "αυτός" and "χείρ," which are joined together with the connecting vowel "ο." 

The word has a somber and serious connotation, as it refers to a tragic act. It is not commonly used in everyday conversation, but rather in more formal or academic contexts.
