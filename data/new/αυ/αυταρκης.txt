Word: αυταρκης

Etymology: The word αυταρκης comes from the Greek words αὐτός (autós) meaning "self" and ἀρκέω (arkéō) meaning "sufficient". Therefore, αυταρκης means "self-sufficient".

Grammar: αυταρκης is an adjective in Greek language. It is declined in three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and in three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative).

Description: αυταρκης is a word that describes someone or something that is self-sufficient and does not need external help or resources. It can be used to describe a person who is independent and self-reliant, or a system or process that is self-sustaining. In modern usage, it is often used in the context of sustainability and environmentalism, as a way to describe practices that are self-sufficient and do not rely on external resources.
