Word: αυτην

Etymology: αυτην is a Greek word that is derived from the pronoun αὐτός (autós), which means "self" or "same". It is the feminine accusative singular form of the pronoun.

Grammar: αυτην is a third-person singular feminine pronoun in the accusative case. It is used to refer to a female person or thing that is the direct object of a verb or preposition.

Description: αυτην is a pronoun that is commonly used in Greek to refer to a female person or thing. It is often used in sentences where the subject is doing something to or for the female object. For example, "Είδε την αδελφή του" (He saw his sister) or "Δώσε αυτήν την βιβλίο" (Give her the book).
