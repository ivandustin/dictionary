Word: αυτοι

Etymology:
The word αυτοι (autoi) is a Greek pronoun that means "they" or "them". It is derived from the Greek word αὐτός (autos), which means "self" or "same".

Grammar:
In Greek, αυτοι is the third person plural pronoun, used to refer to a group of people or things. It is often used as the subject of a sentence, but can also be used as the object of a verb or preposition.

Description:
αυτοι is a common word in Greek, used in everyday conversation and writing. It is often used to refer to a group of people or things, and can be used in a variety of contexts. For example, αυτοι μιλούν ελληνικά (autoi miloun ellinika) means "they speak Greek", while αυτοι είναι φίλοι μου (autoi einai filoi mou) means "they are my friends".
