Word: αυθαδεις

Etymology:
The word αυθαδεις (authadeis) comes from the Greek words αυτός (autos) meaning "self" and άδης (hades) meaning "pleasure" or "delight". Therefore, the word αυθαδεις means "self-pleasing" or "self-willed".

Grammar:
αυθαδεις is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case in Greek.

Description:
The word αυθαδεις is used to describe someone who is stubborn, self-willed, and unwilling to listen to others. It can also refer to someone who is arrogant and thinks highly of themselves. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize someone's behavior or attitude.
