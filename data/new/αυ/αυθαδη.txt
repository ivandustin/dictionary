Word: αυθαδη (authadi)

Etymology:
The word αυθαδη comes from the Greek word αυθάδεια (authadeia), which means arrogance or self-will. It is derived from the words αυτός (autos), meaning self, and θέλω (thelo), meaning to will or desire.

Grammar:
αυθαδη is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
The word αυθαδη is used to describe someone who is stubborn, self-willed, and unwilling to listen to others. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is difficult to work with or who refuses to compromise. The word can also be used to describe a situation or behavior that is characterized by arrogance or a lack of humility.
