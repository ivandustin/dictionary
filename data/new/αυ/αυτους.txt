Word: αυτους

Etymology: αυτους is a Greek word that comes from the pronoun αὐτός (autós), which means "self" or "same". It is a third-person plural pronoun, which means it refers to a group of people or things.

Grammar: αυτους is a pronoun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, which means it is used as the direct object of a verb or as the object of a preposition. In English, it can be translated as "them".

Description: αυτους is a pronoun that is commonly used in Greek to refer to a group of people or things. It is often used in sentences where the subject is doing something to or for the group.
