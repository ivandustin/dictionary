Word: αυτό (autó)

Etymology:
The word αυτό (autó) comes from the Ancient Greek word αὐτός (autós), which means "self" or "same". It is a pronoun that is used to refer to something that has already been mentioned or is known to both the speaker and the listener.

Grammar:
In Greek, αυτό (autó) is a neuter singular pronoun. It can be used as a subject, object, or possessive pronoun. For example:

- Αυτό είναι το βιβλίο μου. (Autó íne to vivlío mou.) - This is my book. (subject)
- Το βιβλίο αυτό είναι δικό σου. (To vivlío autó íne dikó sou.) - This book is yours. (object)
- Αυτό είναι δικό μου. (Autó íne dikó mou.) - This is mine. (possessive)

Description:
αυτό (autó) is a very common word in Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to avoid repetition of a noun or to refer to something that has already been mentioned. It is also used to emphasize a particular object or idea.
