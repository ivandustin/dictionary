The word "αυθεντειν" (authentein) is a Greek verb that means "to exercise authority" or "to have power over." It comes from the Greek word "αυθέντης" (authentēs), which means "one who acts on his own authority" or "a master." In terms of grammar, it is a first-person singular present active indicative verb. This word is often used in the context of power dynamics and can have negative connotations of abuse or misuse of authority.
