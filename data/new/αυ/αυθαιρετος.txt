Word: αυθαίρετος

Etymology:
The word αυθαίρετος (authaires) comes from the Greek words αυτός (autos) meaning "self" and αίρεση (airesi) meaning "choice". Therefore, αυθαίρετος means "self-chosen" or "self-determined".

Grammar:
αυθαίρετος is an adjective in Greek, and it can be used to describe a person or an action. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
αυθαίρετος is a word that describes someone who acts or behaves in a self-determined or arbitrary way, without regard for rules or authority. It can also be used to describe an action that is done without permission or outside of established norms. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe someone who is stubborn or difficult to work with.
