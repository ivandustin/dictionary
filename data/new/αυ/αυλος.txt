Word: αυλος

Etymology:
The word αυλος (aulos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ewh₁- meaning "to blow, breathe". In Ancient Greece, the aulos was a double-piped wind instrument made of reed or wood, which was played in pairs and used in various musical performances.

Grammar:
In Greek, αυλος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is αυλος, and its genitive singular form is αυλου. The word can also be declined in the plural form, with the nominative plural being αυλοι and the genitive plural being αυλων.

Description:
αυλος is a term that refers to the ancient Greek musical instrument known as the aulos. The aulos was a double-piped wind instrument that was played in pairs and used in various musical performances. It was an important instrument in ancient Greek music and was often used in religious ceremonies, theatrical performances, and athletic events. The aulos was also used in military settings to signal troops and to create a sense of unity and discipline. Today, the aulos is still played in some traditional Greek music and is considered an important part of the country's cultural heritage.
