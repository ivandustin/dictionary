Word: αυγουστου

Etymology: αυγουστου is the genitive form of the Greek word αύγουστος (augoustos), which means "venerable" or "majestic". It was originally used as a title for Roman emperors, and later became the name of the eighth month of the Roman calendar, which corresponds to August in the modern calendar.

Grammar: αυγουστου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is used to indicate that something belongs to or is related to the month of August.

Description: αυγουστου refers specifically to the month of August in the Greek language. It is commonly used in Greece and other Greek-speaking countries to refer to this month. August is the eighth month of the year in the Gregorian calendar, and is named after the Roman emperor Augustus. It is typically associated with summer, vacations, and warm weather.
