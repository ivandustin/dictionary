Word: αυτα

Etymology: αυτα is a Greek word that means "these" or "those". It is the plural form of the pronoun "αυτός" (autós), which means "this" or "that".

Grammar: αυτα is a pronoun in the Greek language. It is the plural form of the third person singular pronoun "αυτός" (autós). It is used to refer to things or people that are not present in the conversation.

Description: αυτα is a common word in the Greek language and is used in everyday conversations. It is often used to refer to objects or people that are not present in the conversation. For example, "Αυτά είναι τα βιβλία μου" (autá íne ta vivlía mou) means "These are my books".
