Etymology: The word "φησίν" (phēsín) comes from the Greek verb "φημί" (phēmí), which means "to say" or "to speak."

Grammar: "Φησίν" is the third person singular present indicative form of the verb "φημί" (phēmí). It is used to express that someone says or speaks something.

Description: "Φησίν" is a word in the Greek language that is used to indicate that someone says or speaks something. It is commonly used in written and spoken Greek to attribute speech or statements to a third person.
