Etymology: The word "φημι" (phēmi) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "bheh₂-" meaning "to speak, say."

Grammar: In Greek, "φημι" (phēmi) is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is classified as an irregular verb.

Description: "φημι" (phēmi) means "I say" or "I speak" in Greek. It is commonly used to express the act of verbal communication or making statements.
