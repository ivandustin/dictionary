Etymology: The word "φηστε" (pronounced "feste") comes from the Greek verb "φημί" (phēmí), which means "to say" or "to speak."

Grammar: "Φηστε" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "φημί" (phēmí). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, specifically telling them to speak or say something.

Description: "Φηστε" is a Greek word that is used to command someone to speak or say something. It is a direct and assertive way of telling someone to express their thoughts or opinions.
