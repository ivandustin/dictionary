Etymology: The word "φηλικός" (phēlikos) comes from the Greek root "φίλος" (philos), meaning "friend" or "loving." It is derived from the verb "φιλέω" (phileō), which means "to love" or "to be fond of."

Grammar: In Greek, "φηλικός" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. For example, "φηλικός φίλος" (phēlikos philos) means "friendly friend" in the masculine form, while "φηλική φίλη" (phēlikē philē) means "friendly friend" in the feminine form.

Description: The word "φηλικός" is used to describe someone or something that is friendly, amicable, or kind. It can refer to a person's demeanor, behavior, or attitude towards others. It is often used to describe relationships, interactions, or qualities that promote harmony and goodwill.
