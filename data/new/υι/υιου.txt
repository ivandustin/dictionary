Word: υιου

Etymology: υιου is a Greek word that comes from the noun υιος (huios), which means "son". 

Grammar: υιου is the genitive singular form of υιος (huios). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. 

Description: υιου is a word that is used to indicate the possession or relationship of a son. It is often used in genealogies or family trees to indicate the father-son relationship. For example, in the New Testament, the genealogy of Jesus Christ is traced through his father Joseph, who is referred to as "υιου Δαυιδ" (huiou David), meaning "son of David".
