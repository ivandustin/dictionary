Etymology: The word "αυταρκείαν" comes from the Greek word "αυτάρκεια" (autárkeia), which means self-sufficiency or self-reliance.

Grammar: "Αυταρκείαν" is the accusative form of the noun "αυταρκεία" in Greek. It is used when the noun is the direct object of a verb or when it is the object of a preposition.

Description: "Αυταρκείαν" refers to the state or quality of being self-sufficient or self-reliant. It describes the ability to meet one's own needs without relying on others. It can be applied to individuals, communities, or even countries.
