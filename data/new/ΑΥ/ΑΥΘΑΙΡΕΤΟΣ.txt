Etymology: The word "αυθαίρετος" comes from the Greek roots "αυτός" (autos), meaning "self," and "αίρεση" (airesi), meaning "choice" or "selection." It literally translates to "self-chosen" or "self-selected."

Grammar: In Greek, "αυθαίρετος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe a person, an action, or a characteristic that is independent, self-willed, or arbitrary.

Description: "Αυθαίρετος" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can describe someone who acts independently or makes decisions without considering others. It can also refer to actions or behaviors that are arbitrary or not based on any rules or principles. Additionally, it can describe something that is self-imposed or self-determined.
