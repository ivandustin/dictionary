Etymology: The word "αυτην" (autin) comes from the Greek language. It is the accusative form of the pronoun "αυτή" (auti), which means "she" in English.

Grammar: "Αυτην" (autin) is a feminine pronoun in the accusative case. In Greek, pronouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.
