Etymology: The word "αυθεντείν" comes from the Greek word "αυθέντης" (authentis), which means "author" or "originator". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "αυτός" (autos), meaning "self", and the verb "έχω" (echo), meaning "to have".

Grammar: "Αυθεντείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Αυθεντεύω (I authorize)
- Αυθεντείς (You authorize)
- Αυθεντεί (He/She/It authorizes)
- Αυθεντούμε (We authorize)
- Αυθεντείτε (You authorize)
- Αυθεντούν (They authorize)

Description: The verb "αυθεντείν" means "to authorize" or "to give authority". It is used to express the act of granting someone the power or permission to do something.
