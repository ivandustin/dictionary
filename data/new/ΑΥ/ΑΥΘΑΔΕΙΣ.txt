Etymology: The word "αυθαίρετος" (authaíretos) comes from the Greek roots "αυτός" (autos) meaning "self" and "αίρεση" (airesi) meaning "choice" or "selection". It literally translates to "self-chosen" or "self-selected".

Grammar: "Αυθαίρετος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It can also be used as a noun to refer to someone who acts or speaks without authority or without following established rules.

Description: "Αυθαίρετος" is used to describe something or someone that is arbitrary, capricious, or acting without justification or proper authority. It can refer to actions, decisions, or statements that are made without considering rules, regulations, or established norms.
