Etymology: The word "αυτης" (autis) comes from the Greek language. It is the genitive form of the pronoun "αυτη" (auti), which means "she" or "it".

Grammar: "Αυτης" (autis) is a feminine pronoun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αυτης" (autis) is used to refer to something that belongs to or is associated with a female person or object. It is commonly used in sentences to indicate possession, such as "το βιβλίο αυτης" (to vivlio autis) meaning "her book" or "the book of hers".
