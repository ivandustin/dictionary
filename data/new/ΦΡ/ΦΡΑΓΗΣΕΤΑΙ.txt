Etymology: The word "φραγησεται" comes from the Greek verb "φράσσω" (phrássō), which means "to fence" or "to block." It is derived from the noun "φραγμός" (phragmós), meaning "a fence" or "a barrier."

Grammar: "Φραγησεται" is a verb in the third person singular, aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is used to express the action of being blocked or fenced off.

Description: "Φραγησεται" describes the state of something being blocked or fenced off, indicating that access or passage is prevented. It can be used in various contexts, such as physical barriers, metaphorical obstacles, or figurative restrictions.
