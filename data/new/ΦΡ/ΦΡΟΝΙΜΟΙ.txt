Etymology: The word "φρονιμοι" (pronounced "phronimoi") comes from the Greek word "φρόνηση" (pronounced "fronisi"), which means "prudence" or "wisdom". It is derived from the verb "φρονέω" (pronounced "froneo"), meaning "to think" or "to have understanding".

Grammar: "Φρονιμοι" is the plural form of the adjective "φρόνιμος" (pronounced "fronimos"), which means "wise" or "prudent". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "φρονιμοι" is used to describe plural nouns.

Description: "Φρονιμοι" refers to a group of people who possess wisdom, prudence, or good judgment. It can be used to describe individuals who make thoughtful decisions, consider the consequences of their actions, and have a deep understanding of a particular subject or situation.
