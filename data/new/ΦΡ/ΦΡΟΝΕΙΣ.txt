Etymology: The word "φρονεῖς" (pronounced "phroneis") comes from the Greek verb "φρονέω" (pronounced "phroneo"), which means "to think" or "to have understanding." 

Grammar: "Φρονεῖς" is the second person singular form of the present indicative active of the verb "φρονέω." It is used to address someone directly and means "you think" or "you have understanding."

Description: "Φρονεῖς" is a Greek word that is often used in philosophical and ethical contexts. It implies a deeper level of thinking and understanding, suggesting a thoughtful and wise mindset. It can also refer to having a particular attitude or opinion about something.
