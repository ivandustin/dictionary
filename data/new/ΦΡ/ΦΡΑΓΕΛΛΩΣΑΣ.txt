Etymology: The word "φραγελλώσας" comes from the Greek verb "φραγελλόω" (phragellóo), which means "to whip" or "to flog." It is derived from the noun "φραγέλλιον" (phragéllion), meaning "a whip" or "a scourge."

Grammar: "Φραγελλώσας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "φραγελλόω" (phragellóo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Φραγελλώσας" describes someone who has whipped or flogged someone or something. It refers to the action of using a whip or a scourge to strike or beat someone as a form of punishment or torture.
