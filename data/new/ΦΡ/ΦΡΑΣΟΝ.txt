Etymology: The word "φρασον" (phrason) comes from the Greek word "φράση" (phrase), which means "a group of words that express a complete thought." 

Grammar: In Greek, "φρασον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is declined as follows: singular (το φρασον), plural (τα φρασον).

Description: "Φρασον" refers to a phrase or a group of words that form a meaningful unit in a sentence. It is used to express a complete thought or idea. In grammar, a phrase can consist of a single word or multiple words, and it can function as a noun, verb, adjective, or adverb.
