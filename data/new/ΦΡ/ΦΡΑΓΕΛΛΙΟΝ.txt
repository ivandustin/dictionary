Etymology: The word "φραγελλιον" (phragellion) comes from the Greek word "φραγελλος" (phragellos), which means "whip" or "scourge". 

Grammar: In Greek, "φραγελλιον" is a noun of neuter gender. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Φραγελλιον" refers to a whip or a scourge, which is a tool used for punishment or inflicting pain. It typically consists of a handle and multiple lashes or thongs. In ancient times, whips were commonly used for flogging or beating as a form of punishment or torture.
