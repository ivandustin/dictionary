Etymology: The word "φρισσουσιν" (pronounced "frissousin") comes from the Greek verb "φρίσσω" (frisso), which means "to shiver" or "to tremble."

Grammar: "Φρισσουσιν" is the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb "φρίσσω" (frisso). It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present and is performed by a group of people or things.

Description: "Φρισσουσιν" is a Greek word that means "they shiver" or "they tremble." It is used to describe the action of multiple individuals or objects shivering or trembling.
