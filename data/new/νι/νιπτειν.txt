The word "νιπτειν" (niptein) is a Greek verb that means "to wash" or "to cleanse". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "νίπτω" (niptō), which also means "to wash". In terms of grammar, "νιπτειν" is a present active infinitive verb, which means it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of washing or cleansing. The verb can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects of the action. Overall, "νιπτειν" is a common and important verb in the Greek language, used in a variety of contexts from personal hygiene to religious rituals.
