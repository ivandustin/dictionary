The word "νικαι" (nikai) is a plural noun in Greek, which means "victories" or "conquests". It is derived from the Greek verb "νικάω" (nikaō), which means "to conquer" or "to win". The word is often used in the context of ancient Greek mythology and history, where it refers to the victories of gods and heroes in battles and competitions. In modern Greek, the word is still used to refer to victories or triumphs in various contexts. In terms of grammar, "νικαι" is a third declension noun in Greek, with a stem ending in -κατ-.
