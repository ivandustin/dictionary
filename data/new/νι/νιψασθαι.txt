Word: νιψασθαι

Etymology: νιψασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word νίπτω (níptō), which means "to wash". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) indicates that the verb is in the middle or passive voice.

Grammar: νιψασθαι is an aorist middle infinitive verb in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "to wash oneself" or "to bathe oneself". The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.
