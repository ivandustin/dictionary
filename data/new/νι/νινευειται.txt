Word: νινευειται

Etymology:
The word νινευειται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word νεύω (neúō), which means "to nod" or "to beckon." The word is in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.

Grammar:
νινευειται is a third-person singular verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. In Greek, the passive voice is used to indicate that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
The word νινευειται can be translated to mean "he/she/it is being nodded to" or "he/she/it is being beckoned to." It implies that someone is gesturing or signaling to the subject of the sentence. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone is nodding their head to indicate agreement or beckoning someone to come closer. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as when someone is being drawn towards a particular idea or course of action.
