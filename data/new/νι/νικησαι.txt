Word: νικησαι

Etymology: νικησαι is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and imperative mood. It is derived from the root word νικάω (nikao), which means "to conquer" or "to overcome".

Grammar: νικησαι is a second person singular aorist imperative form of the verb νικάω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: νικησαι can be translated to mean "conquer" or "overcome" in English. As an imperative form, it is a command to someone to conquer or overcome something. It is often used in a motivational or inspirational context, encouraging someone to persevere and succeed in their endeavors.
