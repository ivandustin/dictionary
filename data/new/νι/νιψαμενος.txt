Etymology: 

The word "νιψαμενος" (nipsamenos) is a Greek verb in the aorist participle form. It comes from the root word "νίπτω" (nipto), which means "to wash" or "to cleanse." 

Grammar: 

"Νιψαμενος" is a masculine singular aorist participle in the nominative case. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The aorist tense indicates that the action of washing or cleansing has been completed in the past. 

Description: 

"Νιψαμενος" can be translated as "having washed" or "having cleansed." It is often used in the New Testament to describe the act of baptism, which symbolizes the washing away of sins. For example, in Acts 22:16, Ananias tells Saul (later known as Paul), "And now why do you wait? Rise and be baptized and wash away your sins, calling on his name." 

Overall, "νιψαμενος" is a word that emphasizes the importance of cleansing and purification, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
