Word: νιπτεις

Etymology:
The word νιπτεις (nippeis) is derived from the Greek verb νίπτω (níptō), which means "to wash". It is related to the Latin word "niveo", which means "to be snowy or white". 

Grammar:
νιπτεις is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. 

Description:
νιπτεις refers to a person who washes or cleanses something. In ancient Greece, it was often used to refer to a servant or slave who was responsible for washing the hands and feet of guests before a meal. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to anyone who washes or cleanses something.
