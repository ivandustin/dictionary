The word "νικωντας" is a present participle form of the Greek verb "νικάω" (nikao), which means "to conquer" or "to win". The word can be translated as "conquering" or "winning" in English. 

In terms of grammar, "νικωντας" is a masculine, nominative, singular form of the present participle. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ωντας" to the stem of the verb "νικάω". 

Etymologically, "νικάω" comes from the ancient Greek word "νίκη" (nike), which means "victory". The word has been used in various contexts throughout Greek history, including in mythology and in the Olympic Games. 

Overall, "νικωντας" is a word that describes someone or something that is currently in the process of conquering or winning.
