Word: νικησεις

Etymology:
The word νικησεις (nikēseis) is a Greek verb in the future tense, which means "you will win" or "you will conquer". It is derived from the Greek word νίκη (nikē), which means "victory" or "conquest".

Grammar:
νικησεις is a second-person singular future active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb νικάω (nikáō), which means "to conquer" or "to win". The verb is in the future tense, indicating that the action of winning or conquering will take place in the future.

Description:
νικησεις is a powerful and assertive word that conveys a sense of determination and confidence. It is often used in the context of sports, battles, or competitions, where the goal is to emerge victorious. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe overcoming obstacles or challenges in life. Overall, νικησεις is a word that embodies the spirit of perseverance and triumph.
