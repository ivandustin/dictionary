Etymology: The word "ισοτητος" (isotetos) comes from the Greek roots "ισος" (isos) meaning "equal" and "τητα" (teta) meaning "state or quality." 

Grammar: "Ισοτητος" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Ισοτητος" refers to the state or quality of being equal. It is used to describe a condition where two or more things have the same value, quantity, or characteristics.
