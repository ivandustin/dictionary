Etymology: The word "ισαι" (isai) comes from the Greek verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be." It is the present tense, indicative mood, active voice, third person singular form of the verb.

Grammar: "Ισαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicating an action happening in the present time. It is in the indicative mood, which is used to state a fact or ask a question. It is in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: "Ισαι" is used to describe a third person singular subject that is currently in a state of being. It can be translated as "he/she/it is" in English.
