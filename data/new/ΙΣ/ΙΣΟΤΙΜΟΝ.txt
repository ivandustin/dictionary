Etymology: The word "ισοτιμον" (isotimon) comes from the Greek roots "ισος" (isos) meaning "equal" and "τιμη" (time) meaning "value". 

Grammar: "Ισοτιμον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. 

Description: "Ισοτιμον" refers to something that has equal value or worth. It can also be used to describe a currency exchange rate where two currencies have the same value.
