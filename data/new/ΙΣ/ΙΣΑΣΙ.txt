Etymology: The word "ισασι" (isasi) comes from the Greek verb "ισάζω" (isazo), which means "to equalize" or "to make equal." 

Grammar: "Ισασι" is a verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 
- Εγώ ισάζω (ego isazo) - I equalize
- Εσύ ισάζεις (esi isazis) - You equalize
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό ισάζει (aftos/afti/afto isazi) - He/She/It equalizes
- Εμείς ισάζουμε (emeis isazoume) - We equalize
- Εσείς ισάζετε (eseis isazete) - You equalize
- Αυτοί/Αυτές ισάζουν (aftoi/aftes isazoun) - They equalize

Description: "Ισασι" is used to express the action of equalizing or making something equal. It can be used in various contexts, such as mathematics, physics, or even in a figurative sense to describe the act of making things balanced or fair.
