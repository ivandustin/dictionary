Etymology: The word "ισχυροτερον" comes from the Greek word "ισχυρός" (ischyros), meaning "strong" or "powerful", and the suffix "-τερον" (-teron), which is a comparative suffix indicating "more" or "stronger".

Grammar: "Ισχυροτερον" is the neuter form of the adjective, used to describe a noun of neuter gender. It is in the singular form and in the nominative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description: "Ισχυροτερον" means "stronger" or "more powerful" in English. It is used to compare the strength or power of two or more things.
