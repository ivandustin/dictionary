Ξενισάντες is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, third person plural, which means "they hosted" or "they accommodated". 

Etymology: The word ξενισάντες comes from the Greek root word ξενίζω (xenizo), which means "to host" or "to accommodate" someone who is a stranger or foreigner. The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a common ending for verbs in the aorist tense in Greek.

Grammar: Ξενισάντες is a regular verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the sentence is a group of people who hosted or accommodated someone.

Description: Ξενισάντες is a verb that describes an action of hospitality or kindness towards a stranger or foreigner. It is often used in the context of ancient Greek mythology, where gods and heroes would often be hosted by strangers during their travels.
