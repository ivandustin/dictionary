Word: εξηιεσαν

Etymology: The word εξηιεσαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb εξίημι (exiimi), which means "to send out" or "to let go". It is derived from the prefix εξ- (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb ἵημι (himi), meaning "to send".

Grammar: εξηιεσαν is the third person plural, past tense, indicative mood of the verb εξίημι. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, mood, and person of the subject. The past tense indicates that the action took place in the past, while the indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or asking a question.

Description: εξηιεσαν can be translated to English as "they sent out" or "they let go". The context in which the word is used will determine the exact meaning. It is a regular verb in Greek and follows the conjugation pattern of the verb είμαι (to be).
