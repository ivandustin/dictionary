Word: εξισταντο

Etymology: The word εξισταντο comes from the Greek verb εξίστημι (exístēmi), which means "to be amazed" or "to be astonished". It is derived from the prefix εξ- (ex-), meaning "out of" or "away from", and the verb ίστημι (ístēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: εξισταντο is the third person plural, imperfect middle/passive indicative form of the verb εξίστημι. In this form, it indicates that the subject (they) was being amazed or astonished, or that they were being caused to be amazed or astonished by something.

Description: εξισταντο is a Greek verb that describes a state of being amazed or astonished. It can be used to describe a wide range of emotions, from surprise and wonder to shock and disbelief. The word is often used in a figurative sense, to describe a state of mind that is overwhelmed or unable to comprehend something.
