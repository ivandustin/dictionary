Word: εξηραμμένην

Etymology: 
The word εξηραμμένην is a Greek word that comes from the verb εξαραίνω (exaraino), which means "to dry up" or "to wither." The word is formed by adding the suffix -μένην (-menin) to the past participle form of the verb, which means "having been dried up" or "having been withered."

Grammar:
εξηραμμένην is a feminine singular accusative form of the adjective εξηραμμένος (exarammenos), which means "dried up" or "withered." In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, εξηραμμένην modifies a feminine singular noun in the accusative case.

Description:
The word εξηραμμένην describes something that has been dried up or withered. It can be used to describe plants, fruits, or any other object that has lost its moisture and vitality. In a figurative sense, it can also be used to describe a person who is exhausted or drained of energy. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to describe something that is no longer useful or desirable.
