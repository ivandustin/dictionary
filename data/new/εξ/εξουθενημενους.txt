Word: εξουθενημένους

Etymology: The word εξουθενημένους comes from the Greek verb εξουθενώνω (exouthenóno), which means "to make someone or something powerless, to humiliate, to degrade". The word is formed by adding the suffix -μένος (-menos), which indicates the passive voice and the past participle tense.

Grammar: εξουθενημένους is a Greek adjective in the accusative case, masculine gender, and plural number. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: The word εξουθενημένους describes a group of people who have been made powerless, humiliated, or degraded. It can be used to refer to individuals who have been marginalized, oppressed, or discriminated against. The word carries a negative connotation and implies a sense of injustice or unfair treatment.
