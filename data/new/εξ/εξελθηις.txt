Word: εξελθηις

Etymology: εξελθηις is a Greek word that comes from the verb εξέρχομαι (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out."

Grammar: εξελθηις is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb εξέρχομαι (exerchomai). In this form, it means "you went out" or "you came out."

Description: εξελθηις is a specific form of the verb εξέρχομαι (exerchomai), which is used to describe movement out of a place or situation. In this form, it is addressing a single person and indicating that they have already gone out or come out of a place or situation. It is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels, to describe Jesus' actions and movements.
