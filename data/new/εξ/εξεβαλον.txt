Word: εξεβαλον

Etymology: εξεβαλον is a Greek word that comes from the verb εκβάλλω (ekballō), which means "to cast out" or "to expel". The prefix εξ- (ex-) means "out of" or "from".

Grammar: εξεβαλον is the aorist tense of the verb εκβάλλω (ekballō) in the first person plural. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: εξεβαλον means "we cast out" or "we expelled" in English. It is a verb that describes the act of forcefully removing someone or something from a place or situation.
