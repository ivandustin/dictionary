Word: εξεστησαν

Etymology: The word εξεστησαν comes from the Greek verb εξίστημι (exístimi), which means "to be out of one's mind" or "to be amazed". The prefix εξ- (ex-) means "out of" or "away from", while ίστημι (ístimi) means "to stand" or "to be in a certain state".

Grammar: εξεστησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb εξίστημι (exístimi). In this form, the verb indicates that the action of being out of one's mind or being amazed was completed in the past by a group of people.

Description: εξεστησαν describes a state of extreme surprise or shock that caused a group of people to be out of their minds or to lose their composure. It can be translated as "they were amazed", "they were astounded", or "they were beside themselves with wonder". The word conveys a sense of intense emotion and astonishment.
