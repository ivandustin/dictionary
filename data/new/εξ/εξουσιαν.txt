Word: εξουσίαν

Etymology: The word εξουσίαν comes from the Greek word εξουσία (exousia), which means power or authority. The suffix -αν (-an) is added to the end of the word to indicate the accusative case, which is used to show the direct object of a sentence.

Grammar: εξουσίαν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to show the direct object of a sentence. 

Description: εξουσίαν refers to power or authority, particularly the power to make decisions or take action. It can also refer to the right or permission to do something. The word is often used in political or legal contexts, such as when discussing the authority of a government or the power of a court to make decisions.
