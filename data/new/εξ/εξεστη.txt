The word "εξεστη" (exesti) is a Greek verb that means "to be possible" or "to be allowed". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "ex-" meaning "out" or "from" and the verb "esti" meaning "to be". 

Grammar: "Εξεστη" is a third person singular present indicative active verb. 

Description: The word is commonly used in modern Greek to express permission or possibility. For example, "Μου εξεστη να φύγω νωρίς" (Mou exesti na fygo noris) means "I am allowed to leave early". It can also be used in a more philosophical sense to express the idea of something being possible or feasible.
