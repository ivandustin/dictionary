Word: εξετιθετο

Etymology: The word εξετιθετο comes from the Greek verb "εξετάζω" (exetazo), which means "to examine" or "to investigate". The word is formed by adding the suffix "-το" (-to) to the stem of the verb.

Grammar: εξετιθετο is a third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb "εξετάζω" (exetazo). In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The past tense indicates that the action took place in the past.

Description: εξετιθετο can be translated as "he/she/it examined/investigated himself/herself/itself". The word implies a process of self-examination or self-investigation. It can be used in a literal sense, to describe someone who is examining themselves physically or mentally, or in a figurative sense, to describe someone who is reflecting on their own thoughts, feelings, or actions.
