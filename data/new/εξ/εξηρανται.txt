Word: εξηρανται

Etymology: The word εξηρανται comes from the Greek verb ξηραίνω (xeraino), which means "to dry up" or "to wither". The prefix εξ- (ex-) in this word means "out" or "away", so εξηρανται can be translated as "they are dried up" or "they are withered away".

Grammar: εξηρανται is a third person plural form of the verb ξηραίνω in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is used to describe a state of being, where something or someone has become dry or withered.

Description: εξηρανται is a word that describes a process of drying up or withering away. It can be used to describe plants that have lost their moisture and become dry, or to describe people or animals that have become weak or emaciated. In a figurative sense, it can also be used to describe a situation or relationship that has lost its vitality or energy.
