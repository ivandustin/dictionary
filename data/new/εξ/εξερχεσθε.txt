The word "εξερχεσθε" (exerchesthe) is a Greek verb that means "to go out" or "to depart". It is derived from the root word "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which is a compound of the preposition "εξ" (ex), meaning "out of", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come". 

In terms of grammar, "εξερχεσθε" is the second person plural present imperative form of the verb. This means that it is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to "go out" or "depart". 

Overall, "εξερχεσθε" is a versatile and commonly used verb in Greek, with a straightforward meaning and simple conjugation.
