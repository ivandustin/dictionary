Word: εξουθενησας

Etymology: The word εξουθενησας comes from the Greek verb εξουθενέω (exouthenéo), which means "to make nothing of, to despise, to treat as worthless." It is derived from the prefix εξού (exoú), meaning "out of," and the verb ουθενέω (outhenéo), meaning "to be nothing."

Grammar: εξουθενησας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb εξουθενέω.

Description: εξουθενησας means "you have despised" or "you have treated as worthless" in English. It is a strong word that conveys a sense of complete disregard or contempt for something or someone. It is often used in a moral or ethical context, to describe a person's attitude towards a particular action or behavior.
