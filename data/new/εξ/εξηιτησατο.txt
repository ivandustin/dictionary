Word: εξηιτησατο

Etymology: The word εξηιτησατο comes from the Greek verb "έξειμι" (eximi), which means "to go out" or "to depart". The verb is formed by adding the prefix "εξ-" (ex-) which means "out" or "away" to the verb stem "ειμι" (eimi) which means "to go".

Grammar: εξηιτησατο is a third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "έξειμι" (eximi). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εξηιτησατο means "he/she/it went out" or "he/she/it departed" in English. It is a past tense form of the verb "έξειμι" (eximi) and is used to describe a single event of someone leaving or departing from a place.
