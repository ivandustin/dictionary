Word: εξηλθαν

Etymology: εξηλθαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix "εξ-" (meaning "out of") and the verb "έρχομαι" (meaning "to come"). Therefore, εξηλθαν means "they came out of".

Grammar: εξηλθαν is the third person plural of the aorist tense of the Greek verb "έρχομαι". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, εξηλθαν means "they came out of" and refers to a group of people who left a place.

Description: εξηλθαν is a Greek verb that describes an action of a group of people leaving a place. It can be used in various contexts, such as a group of people leaving a building, a city, or a country. The verb is in the aorist tense, indicating that the action was completed in the past.
