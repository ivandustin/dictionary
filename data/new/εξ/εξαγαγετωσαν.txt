Word: εξαγαγετωσαν

Etymology: 
The word εξαγαγετωσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εξ (ex), meaning "out of" or "from," and αγαγειν (agagein), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." Therefore, εξαγαγετωσαν means "they led out" or "they brought out."

Grammar:
εξαγαγετωσαν is a third-person plural aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
εξαγαγετωσαν is a verb that describes an action of leading or bringing something out of a place. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly is being led out. It is a past tense verb, indicating that the action has already been completed.
