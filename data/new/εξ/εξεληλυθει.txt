Word: εξεληλυθει

Etymology: εξεληλυθει is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out". The word is formed by adding the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), which means "out of", and the perfect tense ending "-εληλυθει" (-eliluthei).

Grammar: εξεληλυθει is a third-person singular perfect tense verb in the active voice. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has continuing relevance in the present. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εξεληλυθει can be translated to English as "has gone out" or "has come out". The word implies that the action of going out or coming out has been completed in the past and has continuing relevance in the present. The context of the sentence will determine whether the subject has gone out or come out of something specific.
