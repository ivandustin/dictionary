Word: εξεπνευσεν

Etymology: 
The word εξεπνευσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εξ (ex), meaning "out of" or "from," and επνευσεν (epneusen), meaning "breathed." Therefore, εξεπνευσεν means "breathed out" or "exhaled."

Grammar:
εξεπνευσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is composed of the prefix εξ (ex), the root πνευ- (pneu-), meaning "to breathe," and the suffix -σεν (-sen), indicating the past tense. 

Description:
εξεπνευσεν is a verb that describes the action of exhaling or breathing out. It is commonly used in Ancient Greek literature to describe the last breath of a dying person or the release of air from a person's lungs. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the release of emotions or thoughts.
