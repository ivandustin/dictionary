Word: εξορκιζω

Etymology: The word εξορκιζω comes from the Greek prefix εξ (ex), meaning "out of," and the verb όρκιζω (orkizo), meaning "to swear" or "to take an oath." Therefore, εξορκιζω means "to exorcise" or "to drive out by oath."

Grammar: εξορκιζω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- εξορκίζω (exorkizo) - I exorcise
- εξορκίζεις (exorkizeis) - you exorcise
- εξορκίζει (exorkizei) - he/she/it exorcises
- εξορκίζουμε (exorkizoume) - we exorcise
- εξορκίζετε (exorkizete) - you (plural) exorcise
- εξορκίζουν (exorkizoun) - they exorcise

Description: εξορκιζω is a verb that means "to exorcise" or "to drive out by oath." It is often used in a religious context to describe the act of casting out demons or evil spirits. In modern usage, it can also refer to the act of getting rid of something unwanted or negative, such as bad habits or negative thoughts.
