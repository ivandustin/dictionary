The word "εξενισεν" (exenisen) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "ξεν" (xen), which means "foreigner" or "stranger". The prefix "εξ" (ex) means "out of" or "from". Therefore, "εξενισεν" can be translated as "to make someone a foreigner" or "to expel someone from their homeland".

Grammar: "Εξενισεν" is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a single person or thing.
