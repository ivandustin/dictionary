Word: εξεπλευσαμεν

Etymology: εξεπλευσαμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix εξ- (meaning "out of" or "from") and the verb πλέω (meaning "to sail"). Therefore, εξεπλευσαμεν means "we sailed out of" or "we departed from by ship".

Grammar: εξεπλευσαμεν is the first person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb πλέω in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (we) performed the action (sailed out of).

It is a word that is commonly used in historical and nautical contexts, as it refers to the act of departing from a location by ship.
