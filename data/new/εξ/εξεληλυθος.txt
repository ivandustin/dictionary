Word: εξεληλυθος

Etymology: The word εξεληλυθος comes from the Greek roots εξ (ex), meaning "out of," and εληλυθω (elēlythō), meaning "to come." Therefore, εξεληλυθος means "having come out of" or "having emerged from."

Grammar: εξεληλυθος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: εξεληλυθος is a word that describes something that has emerged or come out of a particular place or situation. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a plant that has grown out of the ground, or abstract concepts, such as an idea that has emerged from a discussion.
