Word: εξελθοντος

Etymology: 
The word εξελθοντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb εξέρχομαι (exérchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out". The word is formed by adding the suffix -οντος (-ontos) to the present participle stem of the verb.

Grammar:
εξελθοντος is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb εξέρχομαι. In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description:
The word εξελθοντος can be translated as "of the one going out" or "of the one coming out". It is often used in the New Testament to describe someone who is leaving a place or coming out of a situation. For example, in Mark 1:10, the word is used to describe Jesus coming out of the water after being baptized by John the Baptist.
