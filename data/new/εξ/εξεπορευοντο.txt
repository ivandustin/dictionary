Word: εξεπορευοντο

Etymology: 
The word εξεπορευοντο is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix εξ- (meaning "out of" or "from") and the verb πορεύομαι (meaning "to go" or "to travel"). Therefore, εξεπορευοντο means "they were going out" or "they were departing".

Grammar:
εξεπορευοντο is a third-person plural imperfect indicative active verb in ancient Greek. It is in the imperfect tense, which indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
εξεπορευοντο is a verb that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 8:59. In this passage, the verb is used to describe the action of the Jews who were trying to stone Jesus. The verb emphasizes the idea that the Jews were leaving or departing from the scene of the confrontation with Jesus. The word is not commonly used in modern Greek.
