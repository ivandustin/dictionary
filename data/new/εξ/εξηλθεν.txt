Word: εξηλθεν

Etymology: εξηλθεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ἐξέρχομαι (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out."

Grammar: εξηλθεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of ἐξέρχομαι (exerchomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: εξηλθεν means "he/she/it went out" or "he/she/it came out." It is often used in the New Testament to describe Jesus leaving a place or coming out of a tomb. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone leaving a building or a location.
