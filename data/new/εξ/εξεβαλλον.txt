Word: εξεβαλλον

Etymology: 
The word εξεβαλλον is derived from the Greek verb "ἐκβάλλω" (ekballō), which means "to throw out" or "to expel". The prefix "εξ-" (ex-) means "out of" or "from", so εξεβαλλον can be translated as "I was throwing out" or "I was expelling".

Grammar:
εξεβαλλον is the first person singular, imperfect tense of the Greek verb "ἐκβάλλω" (ekballō). In this form, it indicates that the action of throwing out or expelling was ongoing or repeated in the past. 

Description:
εξεβαλλον is a Greek verb that describes the act of throwing out or expelling something or someone. It is often used in a literal sense, such as throwing out garbage or expelling air from the lungs. However, it can also be used in a figurative sense, such as expelling negative thoughts or emotions. The imperfect tense indicates that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past, suggesting that the act of throwing out or expelling was not a one-time occurrence.
