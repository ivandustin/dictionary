Word: εξελεξω

Etymology: The word εξελεξω comes from the Greek prefix εξ- (ex-), meaning "out of" or "from," and the verb ελεγχω (elencho), meaning "to prove" or "to test." Therefore, εξελεξω can be translated as "to select out" or "to choose after testing."

Grammar: εξελεξω is a first-person singular future active indicative form of the verb εκλεγω (eklego), which means "to choose" or "to select." The prefix εξ- adds the meaning of "out of" or "from," indicating that the selection is made after careful consideration or testing.

Description: εξελεξω is a Greek verb that denotes a deliberate and thoughtful selection process. It implies that the person making the choice has carefully evaluated the options and has chosen the best one after testing or examination. The word can be used in various contexts, such as selecting a candidate for a job, choosing a product from a range of options, or picking a team for a sports competition.
