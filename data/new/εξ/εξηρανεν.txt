Word: εξηράνεν

Etymology: The word εξηράνεν comes from the Greek verb ξηραίνω (xeraino), which means "to dry up" or "to wither". The prefix εξ- (ex-) means "out of" or "from", so εξηράνεν can be translated as "he/she/it dried up" or "he/she/it caused to wither".

Grammar: εξηράνεν is a verb in the third person singular past tense of the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -εν (-en) to the stem εξηραν- (exeran-).

Description: εξηράνεν is a word that describes the action of drying up or causing something to wither. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to a plant that has dried up due to lack of water, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing a person's energy or enthusiasm that has diminished over time. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of something coming to an end or reaching a conclusion.
