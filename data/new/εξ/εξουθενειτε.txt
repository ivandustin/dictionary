Word: εξουθενειτε

Etymology: The word εξουθενειτε comes from the Greek root words εξουθενέω (exouthenéo) which means "to make nothing" or "to reduce to nothing" and εξουθενησις (exouthenesis) which means "contempt" or "disdain".

Grammar: εξουθενειτε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb εξουθενέω (exouthenéo) which means "to make nothing" or "to reduce to nothing". The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

εξουθενείτε (exouthenéite) - you make nothing, you reduce to nothing

Description: εξουθενειτε is a strong word that conveys a sense of contempt or disdain towards someone or something. It implies that the subject is worthless or insignificant. The word is often used in a negative context to express a strong disapproval or rejection of something.
