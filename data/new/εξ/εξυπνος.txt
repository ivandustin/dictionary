Word: εξυπνος

Etymology: The word εξυπνος comes from the Greek roots εξ (ex), meaning "out of," and υπνος (hupnos), meaning "sleep." Therefore, the literal translation of εξυπνος is "out of sleep," which refers to being awake or alert.

Grammar: εξυπνος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: εξυπνος is commonly translated as "smart," "intelligent," or "clever." It describes someone who is quick-witted and able to think on their feet. It can also refer to someone who is knowledgeable or well-informed. In some contexts, it can also mean "awake" or "alert."
