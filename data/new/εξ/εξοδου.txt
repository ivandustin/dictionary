The word "εξοδου" (exodou) is a Greek noun that means "exit" or "way out". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "έξειναι" (exine), which means "to go out" or "to exit". 

Grammar: "Εξοδου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is often used to indicate the exit of a place or a situation. 

Description: The word "εξοδου" is commonly used in everyday language to refer to a physical exit, such as a door or a gate, or to a figurative exit, such as a way out of a difficult situation. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as the "exit" of a person from a group or a relationship.
