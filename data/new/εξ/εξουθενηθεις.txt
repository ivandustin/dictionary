Word: εξουθενηθείς

Etymology: The word εξουθενηθείς comes from the Greek verb εξουθενώνω (exouthenóno), which means "to make nothing of," "to despise," or "to treat as worthless." The word is formed from the prefix εξ- (ex-), meaning "out of," and the noun ουθέν (outhen), meaning "nothing."

Grammar: εξουθενηθείς is a past participle in the passive voice, which means "being made nothing of" or "being treated as worthless." It is formed by adding the suffix -θεν (then) to the stem of the verb εξουθενώνω (exouthenóno).

Description: εξουθενηθείς is an adjective that describes someone or something that has been made to feel worthless or insignificant. It can be used to describe a person who has been humiliated or belittled, or an object that has been devalued or disregarded. The word carries a sense of powerlessness and vulnerability, and suggests a deep sense of shame or indignity.
