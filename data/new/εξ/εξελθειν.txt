Word: εξελθειν

Etymology: εξελθειν is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: εξ (ex), meaning "out of," and ελθειν (elthein), meaning "to come." Therefore, εξελθειν means "to come out of."

Grammar: εξελθειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific tense, person, or number. It is often translated into English as "to go out" or "to come out."

Description: εξελθειν is a common verb in Greek that is used in various contexts. It can refer to physically coming out of a place, such as a building or a room, or to figuratively coming out of a situation or a state of mind. It can also be used in a religious context, such as in the Bible, to refer to the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
