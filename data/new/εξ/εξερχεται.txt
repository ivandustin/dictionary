Word: εξερχεται

Etymology: εξερχεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "έξοδος" (exodos), which means "exit" or "departure". The verb εξερχεται means "to go out" or "to exit".

Grammar: εξερχεται is a present tense, third person singular verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb εξέρχομαι (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". The verb is often used in the present tense to describe ongoing actions or habits.

Description: εξερχεται is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical movements, such as leaving a building or exiting a vehicle. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as "to come out of one's shell" or "to break free from a bad habit". Overall, εξερχεται is a useful verb for describing movement and change.
