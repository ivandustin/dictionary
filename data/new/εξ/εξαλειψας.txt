The word "εξαλειψας" (exaleipsas) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "αλειφω" (aleifo), which means "to anoint" or "to smear". The prefix "εξ" (ex) in this case means "out" or "away from", so "εξαλειψας" can be translated as "having wiped away" or "having erased". 

In terms of grammar, "εξαλειψας" is a second person singular verb, which means it is addressing one person directly. It is also in the indicative mood, which means it is a statement of fact rather than a command or a question. 

Overall, "εξαλειψας" is a specific and precise verb that conveys the idea of completely wiping away or erasing something.
