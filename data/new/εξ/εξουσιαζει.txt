Word: εξουσιάζει

Etymology: The word εξουσιάζει comes from the Greek root word εξουσία (exousia), which means "power" or "authority". The suffix -άζω (-azo) is added to the root word to form the verb εξουσιάζω (exousiazo), which means "to exercise power" or "to have authority over".

Grammar: εξουσιάζει is a present tense, third person singular verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb εξουσιάζω (exousiazo) and means "he/she exercises power" or "he/she has authority over".

Description: εξουσιάζει is a verb that describes the act of having power or authority over something or someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in politics, business, or personal relationships. The word implies a sense of control or dominance, and can be used both positively and negatively depending on the situation.
