Word: εξετραπησαν

Etymology: The word εξετραπησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix εξ- (meaning "out of" or "away from") and the verb τρέπω (meaning "to turn" or "to change direction"). Therefore, εξετραπησαν means "they turned away" or "they changed direction".

Grammar: εξετραπησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb εξτρέπω (ex-tre-pó), which means "to turn aside" or "to divert". The aorist tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact.

Description: εξετραπησαν is a verb that describes a sudden change in direction or a diversion from a previous course. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when a person or object turns away from a particular direction, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone changes their mind or diverts from a previous plan.
