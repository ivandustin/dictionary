Word: εξωσεν

Etymology: εξωσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εξω, meaning "outside". The verb εξωσεν means "to push out" or "to expel".

Grammar: εξωσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is in the past tense and indicates that the action of pushing out or expelling has already been completed.

Description: εξωσεν is a strong and forceful verb that conveys the idea of forcefully pushing something or someone out. It can be used in a literal sense, such as pushing an object out of the way, or in a figurative sense, such as expelling someone from a group or organization. The verb is often used in the context of conflict or confrontation, and can be seen as a powerful tool for asserting one's authority or dominance.
