Word: εξουθενησατε

Etymology: The word εξουθενησατε comes from the Greek verb εξουθενέω (exouthenéo), which means "to make nothing of, to despise, to treat as worthless." It is derived from the prefix εξ (ex), meaning "out of," and the noun ουθέν (outhen), meaning "nothing."

Grammar: εξουθενησατε is a verb in the second person plural, aorist tense, active voice, indicative mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -σατε (-sate) to the stem εξουθενησα- (exouthenesa-).

Description: εξουθενησατε means "you have despised" or "you have treated as worthless" in English. It is a strong word that conveys a sense of contempt or disdain towards someone or something. It is often used in a moral or ethical context, to express disapproval of someone's actions or behavior.
