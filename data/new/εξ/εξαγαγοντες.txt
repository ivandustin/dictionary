Word: εξαγαγοντες

Etymology: 
The word εξαγαγοντες comes from the Greek verb "εξάγω" (exago), which means "to lead out" or "to bring out". The suffix "-οντες" (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the action of leading out is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar:
εξαγαγοντες is the present participle form of the verb εξάγω (exago) in the active voice. It is in the masculine plural form, indicating that the subject of the verb is a group of males who are currently leading out or bringing out something.

Description:
εξαγαγοντες can be translated to "leading out" or "bringing out" in English. It is often used in the context of a group of people who are leading or bringing out something, such as a group of soldiers leading prisoners out of a war zone or a group of workers bringing out goods from a warehouse. The word emphasizes the ongoing nature of the action, indicating that the group is actively engaged in the process of leading or bringing out.
