The word "εξεληλυθυιαν" (exelēlythyian) is a Greek verb that means "to have been sent forth" or "to have been released." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek roots "ex" (out) and "elēlythya" (to be sent). 

Grammar: "Εξεληλυθυιαν" is a past participle in the passive voice, indicating that the subject has received the action of being sent forth or released. 

Description: This word is often used in a religious context, particularly in the New Testament of the Bible, to describe the sending forth or release of individuals by God or other divine beings. It can also be used more generally to describe someone or something being sent out or released from a particular place or situation.
