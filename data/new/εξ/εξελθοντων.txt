The word "εξελθοντων" is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It is derived from the verb "εξέρχομαι" which means "to go out" or "to exit". The present participle form indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous.

In terms of grammar, "εξελθοντων" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It can be translated as "of those who are going out" or "of those who are exiting".

Overall, "εξελθοντων" is a word that describes an ongoing action of exiting or going out, and is used in the context of possession or relationship.
