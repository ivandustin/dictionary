Word: εξηλθον

Etymology: εξηλθον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix εξ- (meaning "out of") and the verb ηλθον (meaning "I came"). Therefore, εξηλθον means "I went out" or "I came out".

Grammar: εξηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating its duration or repetition.

Description: εξηλθον is a Greek verb that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to describe various actions, such as leaving a place, going out to meet someone, or coming out of a situation. The verb is often used in a figurative sense, to describe spiritual or moral actions, such as leaving a sinful lifestyle or coming out of darkness into light.
