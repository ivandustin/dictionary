Word: εξετεινατε

Etymology: The word εξετεινατε comes from the Greek verb "εκτείνω" (ektino), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend". The prefix "εξ-" (ex-) adds the meaning of "out" or "from", so εξετεινατε can be translated as "you stretched out" or "you extended".

Grammar: εξετεινατε is the second person plural, past tense, indicative mood of the verb εκτείνω. It is used to address a group of people who performed the action of stretching out or extending something in the past.

Description: εξετεινατε describes an action of stretching out or extending something in the past. It can refer to a physical action, such as stretching out a piece of fabric or extending a hand, or a metaphorical action, such as extending one's reach or influence. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe the expansion or growth of something, such as a business or an idea.
