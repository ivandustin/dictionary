The word "εξελθοντα" is a Greek verb in the aorist participle form. It comes from the root verb "εξέρχομαι" which means "to go out" or "to exit". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

In terms of grammar, "εξελθοντα" is in the masculine, accusative, singular form. This means that it is describing a singular masculine noun that is the object of the sentence.

Overall, "εξελθοντα" can be translated to mean "having gone out" or "having exited". It is often used in biblical or religious contexts to describe someone who has left a place or situation.
