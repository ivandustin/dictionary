The word "εξαρατε" is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology is derived from the prefix "εξ-" which means "out of" or "from" and the verb "αραίνω" which means "to lift up" or "to take away". 

In terms of grammar, "εξαρατε" is in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests. It is conjugated in the second person plural, meaning it is directed towards a group of people. 

The meaning of "εξαρατε" is "take away" or "remove". It is often used in a figurative sense, such as removing obstacles or negative influences. 

Example sentence: "Εξαρατε τον φόβο σας και προχωρήστε μπροστά" (Exarate ton fovos sas kai prochoriste mprosta) which translates to "Take away your fear and move forward".
