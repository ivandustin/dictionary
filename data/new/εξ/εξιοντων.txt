Word: εξιοντων

Etymology: The word εξιοντων comes from the Greek verb εξιέναι (exiéne), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". The suffix -των (-ton) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word is in the genitive case and refers to a group of people or things.

Grammar: εξιοντων is a genitive plural form of the verb εξιέναι. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. In this case, εξιοντων indicates that the word refers to a group of people who are going out or coming forth from somewhere.

Description: εξιοντων can be translated as "of those who are going out" or "of those who are coming forth". It is often used in the context of a group of people leaving a place or coming out of hiding.
