Word: εξηγοράσεν

Etymology: εξηγοράσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix "εξ-" (meaning "out of" or "from") and the verb "αγοράζω" (meaning "to buy"). Therefore, εξηγοράσεν means "to buy out" or "to redeem".

Grammar: εξηγοράσεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb εξαγοράζω (exagorazo), which means "to redeem" or "to buy back". In this form, the verb indicates that the action was completed in the past.

Description: εξηγοράσεν is a biblical term that appears in the New Testament, specifically in Galatians 3:13, where it is used to describe the act of Jesus Christ redeeming humanity from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us. The word emphasizes the idea of buying something back that was lost or taken away, and it is often used in a spiritual or religious context.
