Word: εξεψυξεν

Etymology: The word εξεψυξεν comes from the Greek verb εξύστερος (exysteros), which means "to dry up" or "to wither away". The prefix εξ- (ex-) means "out" or "away from", while ψυχή (psyche) means "soul" or "life". Therefore, εξεψυξεν can be translated as "it caused to wither away" or "it drained the life out of".

Grammar: εξεψυξεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is in the third person singular form, which means that it refers to a single subject that is not the speaker or the listener.

Description: εξεψυξεν is a strong and vivid word that conveys a sense of desolation and emptiness. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a situation or an event that has drained the life out of something or someone. For example, it can be used to describe a drought that has caused the crops to wither away, or a disease that has drained the vitality out of a person.
