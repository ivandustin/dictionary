The word "εξηχηται" (exēchētai) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "ἔχω" (echō), which means "to have" or "to hold", and the prefix "ἐξ-" (ex-), which means "out of" or "from". 

Grammar: The word is in the third person singular form of the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. 

Description: The word means "he/she/it is being held forth" or "he/she/it is being proclaimed". It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe the act of publicly declaring or preaching a message.
