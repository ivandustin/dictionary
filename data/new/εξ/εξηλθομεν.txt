Word: εξηλθομεν

Etymology: εξηλθομεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix εξ- (meaning "out of") and the verb ἔρχομαι (meaning "to come"). Therefore, εξηλθομεν means "we came out" or "we went out".

Grammar: εξηλθομεν is a first-person plural past tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb ἔρχομαι in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is composed of the prefix εξ- and the stem ἤλθομεν (meaning "we came").

Description: εξηλθομεν is a Greek verb that describes the action of a group of people going out or coming out of a place. It is often used in the context of leaving a building, a city, or a country. The verb is commonly found in ancient Greek literature, including the New Testament of the Bible.
