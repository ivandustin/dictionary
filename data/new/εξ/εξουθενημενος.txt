Word: εξουθενημένος

Etymology: The word εξουθενημένος comes from the Greek verb εξουθενώνω (exouthenóno), which means "to make someone or something powerless, to weaken, to exhaust". The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word describes someone or something that has been made powerless or weakened.

Grammar: εξουθενημένος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: εξουθενημένος describes someone or something that has been completely exhausted, weakened, or made powerless. It can be used to describe a person who has been emotionally or physically drained, or an object that has lost its strength or effectiveness. The word carries a sense of defeat or hopelessness, and is often used in a negative context.
