Word: εξελεξαμην

Etymology: The word εξελεξαμην is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εκλέγομαι (eklégomai), which means "to choose" or "to select". The prefix εξ- (ex-) adds emphasis to the action of choosing, indicating a deliberate and careful selection.

Grammar: εξελεξαμην is a first-person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb εκλέγομαι. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (in this case, "I") performed the action of choosing.

Description: εξελεξαμην can be translated to English as "I chose" or "I selected". It is a strong and deliberate verb that implies careful consideration and decision-making. The aorist tense indicates that the action of choosing is complete, and the indicative mood confirms that the speaker did, in fact, make a choice.
