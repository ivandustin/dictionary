The word "εξαγει" (exagei) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of" or "from", and the verb "αγω" (ago), meaning "to lead" or "to bring". Therefore, "εξαγει" can be translated as "to lead out" or "to bring out".

Grammar: "Εξαγει" is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation. 

Description: The verb "εξαγει" is used to describe the action of leading or bringing something out of a place or situation. It can be used in a literal sense, such as leading a person out of a building, or in a figurative sense, such as bringing out someone's hidden talents or qualities. 

Example sentence: "Ο δάσκαλος εξάγει το καλύτερο από τους μαθητές του." (O daskalos exagei to kalutero apo tous mathites tou.) - "The teacher brings out the best from his students."
