Word: εξεκλασθησαν

Etymology: The word εξεκλασθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κλάω (klao), which means "to break" or "to shatter". The prefix εξ- (ex-) means "out of" or "from", so εξεκλασθησαν can be translated as "they were shattered out of".

Grammar: εξεκλασθησαν is a third-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: εξεκλασθησαν can be translated as "they were shattered out of" or "they were broken out of". The word implies a violent or forceful breaking or shattering. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as glass or pottery, or figuratively to describe a situation or relationship that has been irreparably damaged.
