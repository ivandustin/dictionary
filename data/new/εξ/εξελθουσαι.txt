Word: εξελθουσαι

Etymology: 
The word εξελθουσαι is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It is derived from the root word "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out". The word is composed of two parts: "εξ" (ex), which means "out of", and "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to go".

Grammar:
εξελθουσαι is the present participle form of the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out". The present participle form indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous. In this case, εξελθουσαι means "going out" or "coming out".

Description:
εξελθουσαι is a Greek verb that describes the action of going out or coming out. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person leaving a building or a group of people emerging from a cave. The present participle form, εξελθουσαι, indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous.
