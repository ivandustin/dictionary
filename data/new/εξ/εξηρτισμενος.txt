Word: εξηρτισμένος

Etymology: The word εξηρτισμένος comes from the Greek verb "εξαρτώ" (exartó), which means "to equip" or "to prepare". The suffix "-μένος" (-menos) is added to the verb stem to form an adjective that means "equipped" or "prepared".

Grammar: εξηρτισμένος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: εξηρτισμένος is an adjective that describes someone or something that is well-equipped or well-prepared. It can refer to someone who is well-educated, well-trained, or well-prepared for a particular task or situation. It can also describe something that is well-equipped with the necessary tools or resources.
