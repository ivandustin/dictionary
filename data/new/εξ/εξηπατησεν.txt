Word: εξηπάτησεν

Etymology: 
The word εξηπάτησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εξ (ex), which means "out of" or "from," and ηπατάω (epatáo), which means "to deceive" or "to mislead." Therefore, εξηπάτησεν means "he/she/it deceived" or "he/she/it misled."

Grammar:
εξηπάτησεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is conjugated from the verb εξαπατάω (exapatáo), which means "to deceive" or "to mislead." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject performed the action.

Description:
εξηπάτησεν is a verb that describes an action of deceiving or misleading someone. It is often used in a negative context, as it implies that the subject intentionally misled someone for their own benefit. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in literature, politics, or personal relationships.
