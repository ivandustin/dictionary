Word: εξενεγκατε

Etymology: The word εξενεγκατε comes from the Greek verb εξενεγκώ (exenengo), which means "to carry out" or "to bring forth". It is formed by combining the prefix εξ- (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb φέρω (fero), meaning "to carry".

Grammar: εξενεγκατε is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in the active voice of the Greek verb εξενεγκώ. It is translated as "you (all) carried out" or "you (all) brought forth".

Description: εξενεγκατε is a verb that describes an action of carrying out or bringing forth something. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe bringing forth ideas, thoughts, or emotions. In the context of ancient Greek literature, it is often used to describe the act of bringing forth sacrifices or offerings to the gods.
