The word "εξομολογησηται" is a Greek verb that means "to confess" or "to acknowledge one's sins or faults". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek roots "εξομολογέομαι" (exomologeomai) which means "to confess" and "λόγος" (logos) which means "word" or "speech".

Grammar: "εξομολογησηται" is a third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb "εξομολογέομαι". In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person.

Description: The word is often used in a religious context, referring to the act of confessing one's sins to a priest or to God. It can also be used in a more general sense to mean admitting to one's mistakes or faults.
