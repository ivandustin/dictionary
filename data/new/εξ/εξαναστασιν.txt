Word: εξαναστασιν

Etymology: The word εξαναστασιν comes from the Greek root words εξ (ex), meaning "out of," and αναστασις (anastasis), meaning "resurrection" or "rising up." Together, εξαναστασιν means "to rise up out of" or "to revolt against."

Grammar: εξαναστασιν is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the prefix εξ to the verb ανασταίνω (anastaino), which means "to rise up" or "to stand up."

Description: εξαναστασιν is a word that describes the act of rising up or revolting against something. It can be used in a political or social context to describe a rebellion or uprising against a government or authority. It can also be used in a spiritual context to describe a resurrection or rising up from the dead.
