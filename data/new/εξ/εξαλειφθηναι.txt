Word: εξαλειφθηναι

Etymology: 
The word εξαλειφθηναι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix εξα- (meaning "out of" or "away from") and the verb αλείφω (meaning "to anoint" or "to smear"). The suffix -θηναι indicates the passive voice, meaning "to be anointed" or "to be smeared".

Grammar:
εξαλειφθηναι is a passive verb in the aorist tense, indicating a completed action in the past. It is also in the infinitive form, meaning it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
The word εξαλειφθηναι refers to the act of being anointed or smeared with oil or ointment. It can also be used metaphorically to describe being covered or coated with something, such as dirt or dust.
