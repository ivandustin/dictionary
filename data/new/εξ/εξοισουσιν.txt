The word εξοισουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word οἴσω (oiso), which means "to bring" or "to carry." The prefix εξ (ex) means "out of" or "from," so εξοισουσιν can be translated as "they will bring out" or "they will carry away."

εξοισουσιν is a verb that describes an action of bringing or carrying something out or away from a place. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a physical sense (e.g. carrying a box out of a room) or in a metaphorical sense (e.g. removing a problem or obstacle).
