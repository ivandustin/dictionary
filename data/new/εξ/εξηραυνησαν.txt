Word: εξηραυνησαν

Etymology: The word εξηραυνησαν comes from the Greek verb "ξηραίνω" (xeraino), which means "to dry up" or "to wither". The prefix "εξ-" (ex-) in this word means "out of" or "from", so εξηραυνησαν can be translated as "they dried up" or "they withered away".

Grammar: εξηραυνησαν is a verb in the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood, and active voice. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: εξηραυνησαν is a word that describes the action of something drying up or withering away. It can be used to describe the drying up of a river or a plant, or the withering away of a person's health or strength. The word has a sense of finality to it, as if the drying up or withering away is complete and irreversible.
