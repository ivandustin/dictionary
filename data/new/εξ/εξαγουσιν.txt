The word "εξαγουσιν" (exagousin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "εξαγω" (exago), which means "to lead out" or "to bring forth." 

In terms of grammar, "εξαγουσιν" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it is conjugated according to the third person plural subject. 

As for its usage, "εξαγουσιν" can be found in various contexts in Greek literature, such as in the New Testament of the Bible. For example, in the Gospel of Matthew, it is used to describe how Jesus led out or brought forth the disciples to preach and heal in his name. 

Overall, "εξαγουσιν" is a versatile verb that conveys the idea of leading out or bringing forth, and it is commonly used in Greek literature and language.
