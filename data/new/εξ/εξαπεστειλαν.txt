Word: εξαπεστείλαν (exapestilan)

Etymology: The word εξαπεστείλαν comes from the Greek verb "αποστέλλω" (apostello), which means "to send away" or "to dispatch". The prefix "εξ" (ex) in this word means "out of" or "from", so εξαπεστείλαν can be translated as "they sent out" or "they dispatched".

Grammar: εξαπεστείλαν is a verb in the past tense, third person plural. It is the aorist form of the verb αποστέλλω (apostello), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past. The verb is active, which means that the subject (they) performed the action of sending out.

Description: εξαπεστείλαν is a word that describes an action of sending out or dispatching something or someone. It is often used in a military or strategic context, to describe the sending out of troops or messengers. The word has a sense of urgency and purpose, as it implies that the action was necessary and important.
