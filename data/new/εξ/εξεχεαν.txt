Word: εξεχεαν

Etymology: The word εξεχεαν comes from the Greek verb εκχεω (ekcheo), which means "to pour out" or "to shed". The prefix εξ- (ex-) means "out of" or "from", so εξεχεαν can be translated as "they poured out" or "they shed".

Grammar: εξεχεαν is a verb in the third person plural, past tense, active voice, indicative mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -αν (-an) to the stem εξεχε- (ekche-).

Description: εξεχεαν is a word that describes an action of pouring out or shedding something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as pouring out water or shedding tears, or in a figurative sense, such as pouring out emotions or shedding light on a subject. The word has a poetic and expressive quality to it, and is often used in literature and art to convey a sense of release or catharsis.
