Word: εξεδικησεν

Etymology: The word εξεδικησεν comes from the Greek root words εξ (ex), meaning "out of" or "from," and δικαιοσύνη (dikaiosyni), meaning "justice" or "righteousness." Together, εξεδικησεν means "to avenge" or "to take revenge."

Grammar: εξεδικησεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a single person or thing.

Description: εξεδικησεν is a strong and powerful word that conveys a sense of justice and retribution. It is often used in the context of seeking revenge or avenging a wrong that has been done. The word has a sense of finality to it, suggesting that the action of revenge has been completed and justice has been served.
