Word: φιλητος

Etymology:
The word φιλητος (philētos) is derived from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "beloved" or "dear" and ἔτος (etos) meaning "year". Therefore, φιλητος can be translated as "dear to the year" or "beloved of the year".

Grammar:
φιλητος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
φιλητος is not a commonly used word in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek literature and poetry. In ancient Greek mythology, φιλητος was used to describe the beloved or favored year, which was often associated with a bountiful harvest or a successful military campaign.
