Word: φιλανθρωπίαν

Etymology:
The word φιλανθρωπίαν comes from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "loving" and ἄνθρωπος (anthropos) meaning "human being". Together, they form the word φιλανθρωπία (philanthropia) which means "love of humanity". The suffix -αν (-an) is added to form the accusative case of the noun.

Grammar:
φιλανθρωπίαν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
φιλανθρωπίαν refers to the act of showing kindness and concern for the welfare of others, especially those who are in need. It is often associated with charitable acts and philanthropy. The word has a positive connotation and is often used to describe individuals or organizations that are dedicated to helping others.
