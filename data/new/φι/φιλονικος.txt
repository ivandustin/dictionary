Word: φιλονικος

Etymology: The word φιλονικος comes from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "friend" and νίκη (nike) meaning "victory". Therefore, φιλονικος can be translated as "lover of victory" or "one who seeks victory".

Grammar: φιλονικος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined in different cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description: φιλονικος is a term used to describe a person who is competitive and driven to win. It can also refer to someone who is argumentative or quarrelsome, as they may be constantly seeking to prove their point or come out on top in a debate or disagreement. In ancient Greece, the term was often used to describe athletes who were passionate about winning in athletic competitions.
