Word: φιλησω

Etymology: φιλησω is a verb in the Greek language, which comes from the root word φιλέω (phileó), meaning "to love" or "to be fond of".

Grammar: φιλησω is a first-person singular future tense verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated from the root word φιλέω (phileó) and the future tense suffix -σω (-so).

Description: φιλησω means "I will love" or "I will be fond of" in English. It is a verb that expresses a future action of loving or being fond of someone or something. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and literature in the Greek language.
