Word: φιλων

Etymology: φιλων is a Greek word that comes from the verb φιλέω (phileō), which means "to love" or "to be fond of". It is derived from the noun φίλος (philos), which means "friend" or "lover".

Grammar: φιλων is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: φιλων can be translated as "lover" or "friend". In ancient Greek literature, it was often used to describe a close friend or companion, particularly in a romantic or sexual context. It can also be used to describe someone who is fond of or passionate about something, such as a lover of music or art.
