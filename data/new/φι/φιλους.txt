Word: φιλους

Etymology: φιλους is a Greek word that comes from the root word φίλος (philos), which means "friend" or "beloved". The suffix -ους (-ous) is a masculine plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple friends or beloved ones.

Grammar: φιλους is a noun in the masculine plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The masculine plural form is used to refer to a group of male friends or beloved ones.

Description: φιλους is a word that refers to a group of male friends or beloved ones. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the bonds of friendship and love between men. The word carries connotations of loyalty, trust, and affection.
