Word: φιλιππος

Etymology: 
The word φιλιππος (philippos) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: φίλος (philos) meaning "friend" and ἵππος (hippos) meaning "horse". Therefore, the word φιλιππος means "lover of horses" or "friend of horses".

Grammar:
In Greek, φιλιππος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative. The plural form of φιλιππος is φίλιπποι (philippoi).

Description:
φιλιππος is a name that was commonly used in ancient Greece. It was the name of several notable figures, including Philip II of Macedon, the father of Alexander the Great. The name is still used today in Greece and other countries.
