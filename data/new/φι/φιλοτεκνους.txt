Word: φιλοτεκνους

Etymology: The word φιλοτεκνους comes from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "loving" and τέκνον (teknon) meaning "child". Therefore, φιλοτεκνους means "loving children" or "fond of offspring".

Grammar: φιλοτεκνους is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined in different cases and numbers to agree with the gender and number of the noun it modifies.

Description: φιλοτεκνους is a term used to describe someone who loves children and is fond of raising them. It can refer to parents, caregivers, teachers, or anyone who has a strong affection for children and enjoys being around them. In ancient Greece, the term was often used to describe men who had many children and were proud of their offspring. Today, the term is still used to describe people who have a passion for children and their well-being.
