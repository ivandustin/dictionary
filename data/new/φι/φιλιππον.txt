The word "φιλιππον" (philippon) is a Greek noun that means "horseman" or "cavalryman". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "φίλος" (philos) meaning "beloved" or "dear" and "ἵππος" (hippos) meaning "horse". 

Grammar: "Φιλιππον" is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "φιλιπποι" (philippoi). 

Description: In ancient Greece, the cavalry was an important part of the military. The "φιλιππον" was a skilled horseman who fought in battles while riding a horse. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a horseman or equestrian.
