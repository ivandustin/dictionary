Word: φιλιππους

Etymology:
The word φιλιππους (philippos) is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words: φίλος (philos) meaning "friend" and ἵππος (hippos) meaning "horse". Therefore, the word φιλιππους means "lover of horses" or "friend of horses".

Grammar:
φιλιππους is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and is plural in form. The singular form of the word is φίλιππος (philippos).

Description:
In ancient Greece, horses were highly valued and were often used in warfare. Therefore, the word φιλιππους was a common name given to men who were skilled in horsemanship or who owned and bred horses. The most famous person with this name was Philip II of Macedon, the father of Alexander the Great, who was known for his love of horses and his skill in horsemanship.
