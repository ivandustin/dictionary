Word: φιλαργυρία

Etymology:
The word φιλαργυρία comes from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "love" and ἄργυρος (argyros) meaning "silver" or "money". Therefore, φιλαργυρία means "love of money" or "greed".

Grammar:
φιλαργυρία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is φιλαργυρίες. In English, it is often translated as "avarice" or "greediness".

Description:
φιλαργυρία refers to an excessive desire for wealth or material possessions. It is often associated with negative traits such as selfishness, dishonesty, and a lack of empathy for others. In many cultures and religions, φιλαργυρία is considered a sin or a moral failing.
