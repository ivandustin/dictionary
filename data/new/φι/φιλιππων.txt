Word: φιλιππων

Etymology:
The word φιλιππων is derived from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "friend" and ἵππος (hippos) meaning "horse". It is the genitive plural form of the word φίλιππος (philippos), which means "lover of horses".

φιλιππων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. The plural form indicates that there are multiple individuals or things that possess or are related to the noun.

φιλιππων is a word that refers to a group of individuals who are lovers of horses.
