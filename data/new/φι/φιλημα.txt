The word φιλημα (filima) comes from the Greek verb φιλέω (fileo), which means "to love" or "to kiss". The suffix -μα (-ma) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form nouns from verbs, indicating the result or action of the verb.

φιλημα is a neuter noun in Greek, and its plural form is φιληματα (filimata). It is declined like other neuter nouns in Greek, with the nominative and accusative forms being the same. The genitive form is φιληματος (filimatos), and the dative form is φιληματι (filimati).

φιλημα refers to a kiss, either on the cheek or on the lips. It is a common expression of affection in Greek culture, and is often used as a greeting or farewell between friends and family members. The word can also be used in a romantic context, to refer to a kiss between lovers.
