The word φιλονικια comes from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "friend" and νίκη (nike) meaning "victory". Therefore, φιλονικια can be translated as "love of victory" or "love of competition".

φιλονικια is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is φιλονικίες (filonikies).

φιλονικια refers to the desire or love of competition, especially in a combative or argumentative way. It can also refer to a tendency to engage in disputes or quarrels. In ancient Greece, φιλονικια was often seen as a negative trait, as it could lead to conflict and division within a community.
