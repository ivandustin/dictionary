Word: φιλω

Etymology:
The word φιλω (philo) is derived from the Greek root word φιλεω (phileō), which means "to love" or "to be fond of". It is a common verb in the Greek language and is used to express affection, friendship, and attachment.

Grammar:
φιλω (philo) is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the verb φιλεω (phileō). It is used to indicate that the subject (I) is currently loving or being fond of something or someone. The verb can be conjugated in different tenses, moods, and voices to express different meanings and nuances.

Description:
φιλω (philo) is a Greek verb that expresses the concept of love or fondness. It can be used to describe a wide range of emotions, from romantic love to platonic friendship.
