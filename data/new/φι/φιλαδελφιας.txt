Word: φιλαδελφιας

Etymology: The word φιλαδελφιας comes from the Greek words φίλος (phílos) meaning "friend" and ἀδελφός (adelphós) meaning "brother". Together, they form the compound word φιλαδελφία (philadelphía) which means "brotherly love" or "love between brothers".

Grammar: φιλαδελφιας is a noun in the genitive case, which means it is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive form of the noun φιλαδελφία (philadelphía).

Description: φιλαδελφιας is a Greek word that refers to the concept of brotherly love or affection between brothers. It is often used to describe the bond between siblings, but can also be used more broadly to refer to any close relationship between friends or family members.
