Word: φιλαργυροι

Etymology:
The word φιλαργυροι comes from the Greek words φίλος (philos) meaning "loving" and ἄργυρος (argyros) meaning "silver" or "money". Therefore, φιλαργυροι means "lovers of money" or "greedy".

Grammar:
φιλαργυροι is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or for a predicate nominative.

Description:
φιλαργυροι is a term used to describe people who are excessively fond of money or who prioritize wealth above all else. It can be used to criticize someone for being greedy or materialistic. The term has been used in various contexts throughout history, including in religious texts and literature.
