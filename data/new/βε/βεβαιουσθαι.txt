Word: βεβαιουσθαι

Etymology:
The word βεβαιουσθαι comes from the Greek verb βεβαιόω (bebaióo), which means "to confirm, establish, make sure". It is derived from the adjective βέβαιος (bébaios), which means "firm, secure, certain".

Grammar:
βεβαιουσθαι is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ουσθαι (-ousthai) to the stem βεβαιο- (bebaio-).

Description:
βεβαιουσθαι is a Greek verb that means "to be made sure, to be confirmed, to be established". It is often used in a legal or formal context to indicate that something has been verified or validated. The passive voice suggests that the action is being done to the subject, rather than the subject doing the action. In other words, the subject is being made sure or confirmed by someone or something else.
