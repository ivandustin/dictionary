Word: βεβηλους

Etymology:
The word βεβηλους (bebēlous) is derived from the Greek word βεβηλόω (bebēloō), which means "to profane" or "to desecrate." It is formed from the prefix βε- (be-), which means "thoroughly," and the verb βηλόω (bēloō), which means "to pollute" or "to defile."

Grammar:
βεβηλους is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence or clause.

Description:
βεβηλους is used to describe something that has been desecrated or profaned. It can refer to a sacred place or object that has been defiled, or to a person who has committed a sacrilege. The word is often used in a religious context, but it can also be used more broadly to describe any act of disrespect or irreverence.
