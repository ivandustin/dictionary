Word: βεβλημένον

Etymology:
The word βεβλημένον is derived from the Greek verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". The prefix βε- (be-) is a perfective prefix, indicating that the action of throwing has been completed. The suffix -μένον (-menon) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is the recipient of the action.

Grammar:
βεβλημένον is a neuter singular form of the passive participle of the verb βάλλω. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
βεβλημένον can be translated as "thrown" or "cast". It is often used in the New Testament to describe objects that have been thrown or cast, such as stones or nets. In some contexts, it can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has been rejected or cast aside.
