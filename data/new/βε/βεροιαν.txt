Word: βεροιαν

Etymology: βεροιαν is derived from the Greek word Βέροια (Beroia), which is the name of a city in northern Greece. The suffix -αν is added to indicate that something is related to or from that city.

Grammar: βεροιαν is a noun in the singular form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: βεροιαν refers to something that is related to or from the city of Beroia. It can be used to describe a person, place, or thing that has a connection to this city. For example, one could say "Αυτός είναι βεροιανός" (This person is from Beroia) or "Αυτό το φαγητό είναι βεροιανής κουζίνας" (This food is from the Beroia region).
