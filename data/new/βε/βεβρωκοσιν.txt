The word "βεβρωκοσιν" is a Greek verb in the perfect tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root verb "βρώσκω" which means "to gnaw" or "to eat away". The prefix "βε-" indicates completion or a state of being, so "βεβρωκοσιν" means "they have been completely gnawed away" or "they have been eaten up". 

In terms of grammar, "βεβρωκοσιν" is a regular perfect tense verb in the third person plural form. The perfect tense in Greek indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present. 

Overall, "βεβρωκοσιν" describes something that has been completely consumed or destroyed by gnawing or eating away.
