Word: βεβαιαν

Etymology: βεβαιαν is a Greek word that comes from the adjective βέβαιος (bébaios), meaning "firm, secure, certain". The suffix -αν (-an) is added to form the accusative singular feminine form of the adjective.

Grammar: βεβαιαν is the accusative singular feminine form of the adjective βέβαιος (bébaios). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, βεβαιαν is used to modify a feminine singular noun in the accusative case.

Description: βεβαιαν is an adjective that describes something as firm, secure, or certain. It can be used to describe a wide range of things, from physical objects to abstract concepts. For example, one might use βεβαιαν to describe a sturdy building, a reliable source of information, or a strong conviction.
