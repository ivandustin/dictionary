Word: βεβηλουσιν

Etymology:
The word βεβηλουσιν is a verb form in Greek, which comes from the root word βεβηλόω (bebēloō), meaning "to profane" or "to desecrate". The word is derived from the prefix βε- (be-), which means "thoroughly" or "completely", and the word βήλος (bēlos), meaning "sacred" or "holy".

Grammar:
βεβηλουσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative form of the verb βεβηλόω. This form indicates that the action of profaning or desecrating is being carried out by a group of people in the present time.

Description:
The word βεβηλουσιν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Revelation (11:2), where it describes the actions of those who will trample on the holy city of Jerusalem. The word conveys a sense of disrespect and irreverence towards something that is considered sacred or holy.
