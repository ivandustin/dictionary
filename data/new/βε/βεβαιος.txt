Word: βεβαιος

Etymology:
The word βεβαιος (bebaiōs) comes from the Greek verb βαινω (bainō), which means "to go" or "to walk". The prefix βε- (be-) means "well" or "thoroughly", so βεβαιος (bebaiōs) means "well-walking" or "steadfast".

Grammar:
βεβαιος (bebaiōs) is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
βεβαιος (bebaiōs) means "firm", "stable", "secure", or "certain". It is often used to describe something that is reliable or trustworthy. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the hope and faith that Christians have in God.
