Word: βεβαιωσαι

Etymology:
The word βεβαιωσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βεβαιόω (bebaióō), which means "to confirm" or "to make certain." The suffix -σαι (-sai) indicates that it is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
βεβαιωσαι is a second-person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb βεβαιόω. It is used to address one person and indicates that the action of confirming or making certain has been completed in the past.

Description:
βεβαιωσαι is a verb that means "you confirmed" or "you made certain" in English. It is often used in the context of affirming or verifying something that was previously uncertain or doubted. For example, in the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used in the book of Romans to describe how God confirmed his promises to Abraham.
