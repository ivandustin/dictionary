Word: βεβηλος

Etymology:
The word βεβηλος (bebēlos) is derived from the Greek word βέβηλος (bébelos), which means "profane" or "unholy." It is composed of the prefix βε- (be-), which means "thoroughly," and the root word βήλος (bēlos), which means "sacred" or "holy."

Grammar:
βεβηλος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, genders, and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
βεβηλος is used to describe something that is unholy, profane, or sacrilegious. It is often used in a religious context to describe actions or objects that are disrespectful or irreverent towards a deity or sacred place. The word can also be used more broadly to describe anything that is considered to be morally or socially unacceptable.
