Word: βεβληκότος

Etymology:
The word βεβληκότος (beblēkotos) is a past participle of the Greek verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw." The prefix βε- (be-) indicates that the action is completed, so βεβληκότος means "having been thrown."

Grammar:
βεβληκότος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a participle, which means it functions as an adjective that describes the subject of the sentence. In this case, it describes a masculine noun that has been thrown.

Description:
βεβληκότος is a word that describes something that has been thrown. It can be used to describe a variety of objects, such as a ball, a stone, or a spear. The word implies that the object has been thrown forcefully or with great speed. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who has been thrown into a situation or who has been thrust into a position of power or responsibility.
