Word: βιαν

Etymology:
βιαν (bian) is a Greek word that means "violently" or "forcibly". It is derived from the Greek word βία (bia), which means "force" or "violence".

Grammar:
βιαν is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate that something is done with force or violence.

Description:
βιαν is a word that describes an action or behavior that is done with force or violence. It can be used to describe physical actions, such as hitting or pushing, or non-physical actions, such as speaking forcefully or making demands. The word is often used in a negative context, to describe actions that are aggressive or harmful.
