Word: βιωσαι

Etymology: βιωσαι is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and active voice. It is derived from the root word βίος (bios), which means "life" or "livelihood."

Grammar: βιωσαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb form. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone in the second person singular form.

Description: βιωσαι is a Greek verb that means "to live" or "to experience life." It is often used in a philosophical or existential sense, referring to the way in which one lives their life or experiences the world around them. In the imperative form, it can be used as a command to someone to "live" or "experience life" in a certain way.
