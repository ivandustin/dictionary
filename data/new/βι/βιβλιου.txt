The word βιβλιου (bibliou) is a Greek noun in the genitive case, meaning "of a book" or "of books". It is derived from the Greek word βιβλίον (biblion), which means "book". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated in English as "of" or "'s". 

Example: Το βιβλίο του Ιωάννη (To vivlio tou Ioanni) - John's book. 

Overall, βιβλιου is a simple and common word in Greek, used to indicate the possession or relationship of books.
