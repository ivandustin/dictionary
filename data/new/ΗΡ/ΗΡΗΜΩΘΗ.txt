The word "ηρημωθη" (pronounced: irimothi) is a Greek verb in the passive voice, which means "to be deserted" or "to be abandoned." Here is a breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "ηρημωθη" comes from the Greek root "ερημος" (erimos), which means "deserted" or "abandoned." The suffix "-θη" (-thi) is added to form the passive voice.

Grammar: "ηρημωθη" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the third person singular.

Description: The verb "ηρημωθη" describes the state of being deserted or abandoned. It implies that someone or something has been left alone or neglected.
