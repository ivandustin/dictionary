Etymology: The word "ηρνειτο" (ernito) comes from the Greek verb "ερνάομαι" (ernáomai), which means "to deny" or "to refuse."

Grammar: "Ηρνειτο" is the third person singular form of the verb "ερνάομαι" in the imperfect tense. It is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description: "Ηρνειτο" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it was denying" or "he/she/it was refusing." It indicates that someone was consistently or repeatedly denying or refusing something in the past.
