Etymology: The word "ηρξατο" (pronounced "erxato") comes from the Greek verb "αρχίζω" (pronounced "archízo"), which means "to begin" or "to start".

Grammar: "ηρξατο" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "αρχίζω". In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "ηρξατο" is a verb in the Greek language that means "he/she/it began" or "he/she/it started". It is used to indicate the initiation or commencement of an action or event.
