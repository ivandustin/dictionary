Etymology: The word "ηρπασθη" (erpasthi) comes from the ancient Greek verb "ἁρπάζω" (harpazo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize".

Grammar: "ηρπασθη" (erpasthi) is the aorist passive form of the verb "ἁρπάζω" (harpazo). The aorist tense indicates a past action, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "ηρπασθη" (erpasthi) describes an action of being snatched or seized. It implies a sudden and forceful movement, often without the consent of the subject.
