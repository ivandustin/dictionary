ζεοντες (zeontes) is a Greek word that is derived from the verb ζέω (zeo), which means "to boil" or "to seethe". It is the present participle form of the verb, meaning "boiling" or "seething". 

In terms of grammar, ζεοντες is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. It is often used in ancient Greek literature to describe a group of people who are agitated or excited, as if they are boiling with emotion. 

Overall, ζεοντες is a vivid and expressive word that captures the intensity of a particular moment or situation.
