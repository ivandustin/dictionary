Ζευκτηρίας (zeukterías) is a Greek word that comes from the verb ζεύγνυμι (zeúgnymi), which means "to yoke" or "to join together." The suffix -τηρίας (-terías) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns indicating a profession or occupation. Therefore, ζευκτηρίας refers to a person who yokes or joins things together, such as a yoke maker or a harness maker.

In terms of grammar, ζευκτηρίας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case ζευκτηρίου (zeukteríou) or the accusative case ζευκτηρία (zeuktería).

Overall, ζευκτηρίας is a word that describes a specific profession or occupation related to joining or yoking things together.
