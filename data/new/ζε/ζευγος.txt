Word: ζευγός

Etymology: ζευγός comes from the Greek word ζεύγνυμι (zeúgnymi), which means "to yoke" or "to join together". It is related to the English word "yoke".

Grammar: ζευγός is a masculine noun in Greek. Its plural form is ζεύγη (zeúgi).

Description: ζευγός refers to a pair or a couple of things that are joined together. It can refer to a pair of animals that are yoked together, a pair of shoes, or a pair of people who are in a relationship. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to two things that are closely related or connected.
