Etymology: The word "θηρία" (pronounced "thiria") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "θηρίον" (pronounced "therion"), which means "wild animal" or "beast."

Grammar: In Greek, "θηρία" is a noun in the plural form. It is the nominative and accusative case of the word, used to refer to multiple wild animals or beasts.

Description: "Θηρία" is a term used to describe wild animals or beasts in general. It can refer to any non-domesticated creature, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and insects. The term is often used in a figurative sense to describe something savage, fierce, or untamed.
