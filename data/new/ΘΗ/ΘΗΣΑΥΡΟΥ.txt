Etymology: The word "θησαυρού" (thēsaurou) comes from the Greek word "θησαυρός" (thēsauros), which means "treasure" or "storehouse."

Grammar: "Θησαυρού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Θησαυρού" refers to the possessive form of the word "θησαυρός" (thēsauros), which means "treasure" or "storehouse." It is used to indicate that something belongs to or is associated with a treasure or storehouse.
