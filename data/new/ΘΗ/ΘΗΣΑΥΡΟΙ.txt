Etymology: The word "θησαυροί" (thēsaurói) comes from the Greek word "θησαυρός" (thēsauros), meaning "treasure" or "storehouse."

Grammar: "Θησαυροί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple treasures or storehouses.

Description: "Θησαυροί" refers to treasures or storehouses. It can be used to describe physical treasures or metaphorical treasures, such as valuable possessions, wealth, or valuable knowledge.
