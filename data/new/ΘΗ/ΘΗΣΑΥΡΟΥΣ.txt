Etymology: The word "θησαυρούς" (thēsauroús) comes from the Greek word "θησαυρός" (thēsauros), meaning "treasure" or "storehouse."

Grammar: "Θησαυρούς" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Θησαυρούς" refers to treasures or valuable possessions that are stored or kept in a safe place. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something of great value or importance.
