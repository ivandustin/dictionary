Etymology: The word "θηλαζούσαις" comes from the Greek verb "θηλάζω" (thēlazō), which means "to suckle" or "to nurse." It is derived from the noun "θηλή" (thēlē), meaning "breast" or "teat."

Grammar: "Θηλαζούσαις" is the present participle, feminine, dative, plural form of the verb "θηλάζω" (thēlazō). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action. The feminine form agrees with the gender of the noun it modifies, and the dative case indicates the indirect object of the action.

Description: "Θηλαζούσαις" describes a group of females who are currently nursing or suckling. It implies that these females are actively engaged in the act of breastfeeding or providing nourishment to their young.
