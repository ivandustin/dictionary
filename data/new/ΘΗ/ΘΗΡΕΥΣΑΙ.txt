Etymology: The word "θηρευσαί" comes from the Greek verb "θηρεύω" (thēreúō), which means "to hunt". It is derived from the noun "θήρα" (thḗra), meaning "wild animal" or "beast".

Grammar: "θηρευσαί" is the third person singular, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "θηρεύω" (thēreúō). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the middle voice suggests that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit.

Description: "θηρευσαί" is a Greek verb form that means "he/she/it hunted" or "he/she/it was hunting". It refers to the act of pursuing and capturing wild animals for food or sport.
