Etymology: The word "θηριών" comes from the Greek word "θηρίον" (thēríon), which means "wild animal" or "beast."

Grammar: "Θηριών" is the plural form of the noun "θηρίον" (thēríon) in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is of neuter gender.

Description: "Θηριών" refers to multiple wild animals or beasts. It can be used to describe a variety of creatures in the animal kingdom, such as lions, tigers, bears, and other predatory or dangerous animals.
