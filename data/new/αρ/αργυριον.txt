Word: αργυριον

Etymology:
The word αργυριον (argyrion) comes from the Greek word ἄργυρος (argyros), which means "silver". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂erǵ- ("white, shining").

Grammar:
In Greek, αργυριον is a neuter noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is αργυριον, and its genitive singular form is αργυριου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
αργυριον refers to a small amount of silver, usually in the form of a coin or a piece of jewelry. In ancient Greece, it was a common currency and was used for trade and commerce. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to silver coins or small amounts of silver.
