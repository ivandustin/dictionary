Word: αρπαζουσιν

Etymology: αρπαζουσιν is a present active indicative verb form in Greek, derived from the root word αρπάζω (harpazo), which means "to seize" or "to snatch away."

Grammar: αρπαζουσιν is the third person plural form of the present tense in Greek. It is an active verb, indicating that the subject is performing the action of seizing or snatching away.

Description: αρπαζουσιν is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of those who forcefully take something or someone. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the sudden and unexpected nature of an event or action.
