Word: αρκει

Etymology: αρκει comes from the Greek verb αρκέω (arkeo), which means "to suffice" or "to be enough".

Grammar: αρκει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is often used in the expression "αρκεί και περισσεύει" (arkei kai perissevei), which means "it is enough and more than enough".

Description: αρκει is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to express sufficiency or adequacy. It can be used to describe a situation where something is enough or sufficient, or to express the idea that something is more than enough. It is often used in everyday conversation and is an important part of the Greek language.
