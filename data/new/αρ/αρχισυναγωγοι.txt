Word: αρχισυναγωγοι

Etymology:
The word αρχισυναγωγοι (archisynagogoi) is derived from two Greek words: αρχι (archi) meaning "chief" or "leader" and συναγωγοι (synagogoi) meaning "synagogue". Therefore, the word αρχισυναγωγοι refers to the leaders or chiefs of a synagogue.

Grammar:
In Greek, αρχισυναγωγοι is a plural noun. The singular form is αρχισυναγωγός (archisynagogos). It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. 

Description:
The term αρχισυναγωγοι is used in the New Testament to refer to the leaders of the Jewish synagogues. They were responsible for the administration of the synagogue and the conduct of worship services. In some instances, they were also involved in the persecution of early Christians. The term is used in the Gospels and the Acts of the Apostles.
