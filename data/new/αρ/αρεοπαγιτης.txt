Word: αρεοπαγιτης

Etymology:
The word αρεοπαγιτης (areopagitēs) comes from the Greek word Άρειος Πάγος (Areios Pagos), which means "Hill of Ares". The Areopagus was a prominent rock outcropping in Athens, Greece, where the council of elders, or Areopagus, met to discuss important matters of state. The word αρεοπαγιτης (areopagitēs) refers to a member of this council.

Description:
αρεοπαγιτης (areopagitēs) refers to a member of the Areopagus council in ancient Athens. The Areopagus was a prestigious council of elders who were responsible for overseeing the administration of justice and other important matters of state. The word αρεοπαγιτης (areopagitēs) is still used today to refer to someone who is wise, knowledgeable, and respected in their field.
