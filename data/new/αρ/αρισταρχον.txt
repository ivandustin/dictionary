Word: αρισταρχον

Etymology: The word αρισταρχον comes from the Greek words αριστος (aristos) meaning "best" and αρχον (archon) meaning "ruler" or "leader". 

Grammar: αρισταρχον is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. 

Description: αρισταρχον refers to a person who is a ruler or leader, and is often used in a political context. It can also refer to someone who is considered the best or most excellent in a particular field or area of expertise.
