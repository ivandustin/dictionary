The word "αρχοντα" (archonta) is a Greek noun that means "ruler" or "leader". It comes from the Greek verb "αρχω" (archo), which means "to rule" or "to lead". In terms of grammar, "αρχοντα" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It can be used to refer to a person who holds a position of authority or power, such as a king, president, or CEO.
