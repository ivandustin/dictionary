Word: αρνίον

Etymology:
The word αρνίον (arnion) is a diminutive form of the Greek word αρνός (arnos), which means "lamb". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erh₃- ("woolly sheep").

Grammar:
In Greek, αρνίον is a neuter noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is αρνίον, and its genitive singular form is αρνίου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
αρνίον is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to a lamb or a young sheep. It is often used metaphorically to refer to Jesus Christ as the sacrificial lamb who takes away the sins of the world. The word is also used in other contexts to refer to a lamb or sheep in general.
