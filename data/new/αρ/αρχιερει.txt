Word: αρχιερει

Etymology:
The word αρχιερει (archierei) comes from the Greek words αρχι (archi) meaning "chief" or "principal" and ερεις (ereis) meaning "priest". Therefore, αρχιερει refers to the "chief priest" or "high priest" in Greek.

Grammar:
αρχιερει is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, it is declined as follows:

Nominative: αρχιερεύς (archiereus)
Genitive: αρχιερέως (archiereos)
Accusative: αρχιερέα (archierea)
Vocative: αρχιερεύ (archiereu)

Description:
αρχιερει is a term used in the Greek Orthodox Church to refer to the highest-ranking bishop or priest. The αρχιερει is responsible for overseeing the spiritual and administrative affairs of the church, and is often involved in the ordination of new priests and bishops. In the New Testament, the term is used to refer to Jesus Christ as the "high priest" who intercedes on behalf of his followers.
