Word: αρπαγης

Etymology: The word αρπαγης (arpages) comes from the Greek verb αρπαζω (arpazo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize". The suffix -ης (-es) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns indicating a person who performs a certain action or has a certain quality.

Grammar: αρπαγης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form is αρπαγου (arpagou), the dative form is αρπαγη (arpagi), and the accusative form is αρπαγην (arpagen).

Description: αρπαγης refers to a person who snatches or seizes something by force. It can also be used to describe a thief or a robber. In ancient Greek literature, the term was often used to describe a pirate or a marauder who raided ships and coastal towns. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a thief or a robber.
