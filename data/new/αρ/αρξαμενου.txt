Word: αρξαμενου

Etymology: αρξαμενου is the genitive form of the Greek verb αρχάομαι (archáomai), which means "to begin" or "to start". The verb comes from the noun αρχή (arché), which means "beginning" or "origin".

Grammar: αρξαμενου is a form of the verb αρχάομαι in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. In this case, αρξαμενου indicates that something belongs to or is related to the action of beginning or starting.

Description: αρξαμενου can be translated as "of the one who started" or "of the one who began". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the beginning of a story or a journey.
