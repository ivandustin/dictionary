Word: αριστου

Etymology: The word αριστου comes from the Greek adjective "αριστος" (aristos), which means "best" or "excellent". 

Grammar: αριστου is the genitive form of αριστος. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. 

Description: αριστου can be translated to "of the best" or "of the excellent". It is often used to describe someone or something that is of the highest quality or standard. For example, one might say "Αυτός είναι ένας αριστου φοιτητής" (This is an excellent student).
