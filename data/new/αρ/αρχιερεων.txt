Word: αρχιερεύς (archiereus)

Etymology: From the Greek words αρχι (archi), meaning "chief" or "highest," and ερευνάω (erevnao), meaning "to investigate" or "to examine."

Grammar: αρχιερεύς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In the genitive case, it becomes αρχιερέως (archiereos).

Description: In ancient Greece, αρχιερεύς referred to the high priest of a temple or religious cult. In the New Testament, it is used to refer to the Jewish high priest or to Jesus Christ as the high priest of the Christian faith. Today, the term is used in the Eastern Orthodox Church to refer to a bishop who holds a high rank within the church hierarchy.
