αργυροκοπος (argyrokopos) is a Greek word that literally means "silver cutter". It is derived from the words αργυρός (argyros), meaning "silver", and κόπτω (kopto), meaning "to cut". In ancient Greece, an argyrokopos was a craftsman who worked with silver, creating intricate designs and objects such as jewelry, utensils, and decorative items. Today, the word is rarely used and is mostly found in historical or cultural contexts. In terms of grammar, αργυροκοπος is a masculine noun in the nominative case.
