Word: αρρωστους

Etymology: 
The word αρρωστους (arrhōstoús) is derived from the Greek word αρρωστία (arrhōstía), which means sickness or illness. The suffix -ους (-oús) is added to the end of the word to indicate that it is in the accusative plural form.

Grammar:
αρρωστους is a Greek adjective in the accusative plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are sick or ill. In Greek, adjectives must agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. 

Description:
αρρωστους is a word that is commonly used in Greek to describe a group of people who are sick or ill. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in medical settings or in everyday conversation. The word is often used in combination with other words to provide more specific information about the type of illness or symptoms being experienced.
