Word: αριθμόν

Etymology: αριθμόν (arithmón) comes from the Greek word ἀριθμός (arithmós), meaning "number".

Grammar: αριθμόν is a masculine noun in the singular form in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: αριθμόν refers to a number or a numerical value. It is commonly used in mathematics and other fields that deal with numerical data. In Greek grammar, it is often used as the direct object of a verb, such as in the sentence "Πρέπει να προσθέσετε έναν αριθμόν στην εξίσωση" (You need to add a number to the equation).
