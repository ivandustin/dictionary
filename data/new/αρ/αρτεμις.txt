Description:
In Greek mythology, Artemis was the goddess of the hunt, the wilderness, childbirth, and virginity. She was the daughter of Zeus and Leto, and the twin sister of Apollo. Artemis was often depicted as a young woman carrying a bow and arrows, and accompanied by a pack of hunting dogs. She was also associated with the moon, and was sometimes called the "Lady of the Wild Things". Artemis was a virgin goddess, and was fiercely protective of her chastity. She was also known for her wrathful nature, and was said to punish those who offended her or her followers. Despite her fierce reputation, Artemis was also a nurturing goddess, and was often invoked by women in childbirth.
