Word: αργον

The word αργον (argos) is derived from the Greek word ἀργός (argos), which means "inactive" or "lazy". It is also related to the word ἀργός (argos), which means "bright" or "shining". In Greek mythology, Argus was a giant with a hundred eyes who was known for his vigilance.

Grammar:
αργον (argos) is a neuter noun in Greek. It is singular and nominative in form. In the plural form, it becomes αργά (arga).

Description:
αργον (argos) can be translated to mean "idle", "inactive", "lazy", or "slow". It can also be used to describe something that is dull or lacking in energy. In Greek mythology, the word is often associated with the god Hermes, who was known for his quickness and agility.
