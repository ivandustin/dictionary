Word: αρτους

Etymology: αρτους is a Greek word that comes from the root word ἄρτος (artos), which means "bread". 

Grammar: αρτους is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and is plural. 

Description: αρτους refers to loaves of bread or bread in general. It is a common word used in everyday language in Greece. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used in the story of the feeding of the 5,000, where Jesus miraculously multiplies five loaves of bread (πέντε ἄρτους) to feed the crowd.
