Word: αρχομενος

Etymology: αρχομενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb αρχω (archō), which means "to begin" or "to rule". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the present tense and passive voice.

Grammar: αρχομενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe someone who is currently beginning or starting something.

Description: αρχομενος can be translated as "beginning" or "starting". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe the beginning of a process or the start of a journey. In Christian theology, it is used to describe Jesus Christ as the "Alpha and Omega", the beginning and the end.
