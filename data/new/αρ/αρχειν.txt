Word: αρχειν

Etymology:
The word αρχειν (archein) comes from the Greek root word αρχή (arche), which means "beginning" or "origin." It is related to other Greek words such as αρχήγετος (archegētos) meaning "ruler" or "leader," and αρχαίος (archaios) meaning "ancient" or "old."

Grammar:
αρχειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It is a first conjugation verb, meaning that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω (omega) in the present tense. The verb stem is αρχ-, and the ending -ειν (-ein) indicates the infinitive form.

Description:
αρχειν means "to rule" or "to govern" in Greek. It is often used in the context of political or social leadership, and can also refer to the act of beginning or initiating something. The word is commonly used in ancient Greek literature, including the works of Plato and Aristotle.
