Word: αρχην

Etymology: αρχην is a Greek word that comes from the noun αρχή (arche), which means "beginning" or "origin". The suffix -ν (-n) is added to the end of the word to indicate the accusative case.

Grammar: αρχην is the accusative singular form of the noun αρχή (arche). In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: αρχην is a word that means "beginning" or "origin" in Greek. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the beginning of something, such as the beginning of the world or the beginning of a new era.
