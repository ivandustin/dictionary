Word: αρτι

Etymology: The word αρτι (arti) comes from the Ancient Greek word ἄρτι (arti), which means "just now", "at this moment", or "recently".

Grammar: αρτι is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the meaning of a verb, an adjective, or another adverb. It is often used to indicate that something has just happened or is happening now.

Description: αρτι is a common word in Greek that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express a sense of immediacy or urgency, such as when someone wants to say that something has just happened or is happening right now.
