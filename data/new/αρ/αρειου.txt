The word "αρειου" (areiou) is a Greek adjective that means "of Ares". It is derived from the name of the Greek god of war, Ares. In Greek mythology, Ares was known for his fierce and violent nature, and was often associated with bloodshed and destruction. 

In terms of grammar, "αρειου" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. It is often used in phrases such as "του Αρείου Πάγου" (tou Areiou Pagou), which means "of the Areopagus", a famous court in ancient Athens where trials were held.

Overall, "αρειου" is a word that is closely tied to Greek mythology and history, and is used to describe things that are related to Ares or his domain of war and conflict.
