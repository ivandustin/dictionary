Word: αρχισυναγωγου

Etymology: The word αρχισυναγωγου comes from the Greek words αρχι- (archi-), meaning "chief" or "leader," and συναγωγή (synagogi), meaning "assembly" or "gathering." Therefore, αρχισυναγωγου can be translated as "chief of the synagogue."

Grammar: αρχισυναγωγου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is used to indicate that the person in question is the chief of a synagogue.

Description: αρχισυναγωγου refers to the leader or chief of a synagogue, which is a Jewish place of worship and community gathering. The position of αρχισυναγωγου was an important one in Jewish communities, as they were responsible for overseeing the religious and social activities of the synagogue. In the New Testament, the term is used to refer to Jewish leaders who opposed Jesus and his teachings.
