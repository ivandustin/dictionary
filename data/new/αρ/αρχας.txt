Grammar: αρχας is a masculine noun in Greek, belonging to the first declension. Its nominative singular form is αρχας, and its genitive singular form is αρχα.

Description: αρχας is a Greek word that can be translated to "principles", "foundations", or "beginnings". It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to refer to the fundamental concepts or ideas that underlie a particular system of thought. In the Christian Bible, αρχας is used to refer to the "principalities and powers" of the spiritual realm.
