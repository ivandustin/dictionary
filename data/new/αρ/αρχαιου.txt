Word: αρχαιου

Etymology: The word αρχαιου comes from the Greek word αρχαίος (archaios), which means "ancient" or "old".

Grammar: αρχαιου is a Greek adjective in the masculine singular genitive case. It is used to describe something that is ancient or old.

Description: αρχαιου is often used in Greek literature and history to describe ancient artifacts, buildings, and civilizations. It can also be used to describe a person who is very old or has lived a long time. In modern Greek, it is sometimes used to describe something that is outdated or obsolete.
