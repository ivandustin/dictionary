Word: αροτρον

Etymology: αροτρον comes from the Greek word αροτρον (arotron), which means "plow". It is derived from the verb αροω (aroō), which means "to plow".

Grammar: αροτρον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its nominative singular form is αροτρον (arotron), and its genitive singular form is αροτρου (arotrou).

Description: αροτρον refers to a plow, which is a farming tool used to break up and turn over soil in preparation for planting crops. In ancient Greece, plows were typically made of wood and pulled by oxen. The use of the plow was a significant development in agriculture, allowing farmers to cultivate larger areas of land more efficiently.
