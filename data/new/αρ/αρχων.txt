Word: αρχων

Etymology:
The word αρχων (archon) comes from the Greek verb αρχω (archō), which means "to rule" or "to lead." In ancient Greece, an archon was a high-ranking official who held political power and was responsible for governing the city-state.

Grammar:
In Greek, αρχων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is αρχοντες (archontes). The word is declined like other first-declension nouns, with the stem αρχοντ-.

Description:
αρχων is a term that has been used throughout Greek history to refer to various types of leaders and officials. In ancient Athens, for example, the archons were the nine highest officials in the city-state, responsible for overseeing the administration of justice and other important functions. In the New Testament, the word is used to refer to rulers and authorities, both human and divine. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to leaders and officials in various contexts.
