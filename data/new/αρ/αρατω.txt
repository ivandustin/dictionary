Word: αρατω

Etymology: The word αρατω (aratō) is a Greek verb that comes from the ancient Greek word ἀράσσω (arassō), which means "to pluck" or "to pick."

Grammar: αρατω is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the verb ἀράσσω (arassō). It is used to indicate that the subject (I) is currently plucking or picking something.

Description: αρατω is a specific form of the verb ἀράσσω (arassō), which is used to describe the action of plucking or picking something. It is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the act of plucking flowers or fruits, or picking up objects from the ground.
