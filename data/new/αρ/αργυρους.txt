Word: αργυρους

Etymology: The word αργυρους (argyrous) is derived from the Greek word αργυρος (argyros), which means "silver". The suffix -ους (-ous) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: αργυρους is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is used to describe a noun that is masculine and singular.

Description: αργυρους is an adjective that means "silver" in Greek. It is often used to describe objects or materials that are made of silver, such as jewelry, coins, or utensils. It can also be used to describe the color silver. For example, one might say "Το αυτοκίνητο είναι αργυρους" (The car is silver).
