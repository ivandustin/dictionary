Word: αρωματων

Etymology: αρωματων is a Greek word that comes from the root word αρωμα (aroma), which means "fragrance" or "scent". The suffix -ων (-on) is a plural ending, so αρωματων means "fragrances" or "scents".

Grammar: αρωματων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, nouns change their endings depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple fragrances or scents.

Description: αρωματων refers to a collection or group of fragrances or scents. It can be used to describe a variety of different smells, such as perfumes, essential oils, or natural aromas.
