Word: αρχοντος

Etymology: αρχοντος is a Greek word that comes from the root word αρχων (archon), which means ruler or leader. It is derived from the verb αρχω (archo), which means to rule or to lead.

Grammar: αρχοντος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of the ruler" or "of the leader."

Description: αρχοντος is a term that is often used in Greek mythology and literature to refer to a powerful ruler or leader. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to anyone who holds a position of authority or power. In modern Greek, it is often used to refer to a government official or a person in a position of leadership.
