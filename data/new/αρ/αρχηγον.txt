The word "αρχηγον" (archegon) is a Greek noun that means "leader" or "chief". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "αρχή" (arche), meaning "beginning" or "origin", and "γωνία" (gonia), meaning "angle" or "corner". 

Grammar: "αρχηγον" is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "αρχηγοί" (archegoi). 

Description: The word "αρχηγον" is often used to refer to a person who holds a position of leadership or authority, such as a political leader, military commander, or CEO of a company. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is a pioneer or a trailblazer in their field.
