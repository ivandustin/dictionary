Word: αρνούμενος

Etymology: The word αρνούμενος comes from the Greek verb αρνούμαι (arnoumai), which means "to deny" or "to refuse". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the present tense and active voice.

Grammar: αρνούμενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb αρνούμαι (arnoumai), which is a deponent verb, meaning that it has a passive form but an active meaning. The present participle is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the verb stem.

Description: αρνούμενος is an adjective that describes someone who is currently denying or refusing something. It can also be used as a noun to refer to someone who habitually denies or refuses things. In English, it can be translated as "denying", "refusing", or "disavowing".
