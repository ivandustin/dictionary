Word: αρπαξ

Etymology:
The word αρπαξ (arpax) comes from the Greek verb αρπαζω (arpazo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁rebʰ- ("to grab, seize").

Grammar:
In Greek, αρπαξ is a masculine noun of the third declension. Its nominative singular form is αρπαξ, and its genitive singular form is αρπακτος (arpaktos). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
αρπαξ is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe a person who is caught up or snatched away suddenly. It is often used in reference to the rapture, a Christian belief that believers will be taken up to heaven before the end of the world. The word can also be used more generally to describe someone who is seized or taken away forcefully.
