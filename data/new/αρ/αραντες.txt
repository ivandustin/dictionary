Word: αραντες

Etymology:
The word αραντες (arantes) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It comes from the root word αίρω (airo), which means "to lift up" or "to take away."

Grammar:
αραντες is the aorist participle of the verb αίρω (airo), which means "having lifted up" or "having taken away." It is in the masculine gender, plural number, and nominative case.

Description:
αραντες is a word that describes a group of people who have lifted or taken away something in the past. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was lifted or taken away.
