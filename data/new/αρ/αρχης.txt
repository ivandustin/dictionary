Word: αρχης

Etymology: αρχης is derived from the Greek word "αρχή" (arche), which means "beginning" or "origin".

Grammar: αρχης is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of the noun αρχή.

Description: αρχης refers to the beginning or starting point of something. It can also refer to the source or origin of something. In Greek philosophy, αρχης is used to refer to the first principle or ultimate reality from which everything else is derived.
