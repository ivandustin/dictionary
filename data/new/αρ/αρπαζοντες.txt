Word: αρπαζοντες

Etymology: αρπαζοντες is a present participle form of the Greek verb αρπαζω (harpazō), which means "to seize, snatch, or carry off by force." The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *serp- which means "to seize or grasp."

Grammar: αρπαζοντες is a present participle form of the verb αρπαζω, which is in the active voice and present tense. It is used to describe an ongoing action of seizing or snatching.

Description: αρπαζοντες is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of those who forcefully take something or someone. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a sudden or unexpected event or action.
