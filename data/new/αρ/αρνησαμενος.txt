Word: αρνησαμενος

Etymology: αρνησαμενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb αρνέομαι (arnéomai), which means "to deny" or "to refuse."

Grammar: αρνησαμενος is the masculine nominative singular form of the participle αρνησάμενος (arnisámenos), which means "having denied" or "having refused." It is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the stem αρνησ- (arnis-), which is the aorist tense of the verb αρνέομαι.

Description: αρνησαμενος is a word that describes someone who has denied or refused something. It can be used to describe someone who has refused an offer, denied an accusation, or rejected a proposal. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe characters who have refused to comply with the wishes of others or who have denied their own beliefs or values.
