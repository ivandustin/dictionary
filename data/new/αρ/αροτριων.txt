The word "αροτριων" (arotriōn) is a Greek noun that means "plowshare" or "plow blade". It is derived from the verb "αροτριαω" (arotriao), which means "to plow". In terms of grammar, it is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible in Luke 9:62, where Jesus says, "No one who puts his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom of God."
