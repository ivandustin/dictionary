Word: αρξονται

Etymology: αρξονται is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically in the present tense, third person plural. It is derived from the root word αρχ-, which means "to begin" or "to rule."

The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person plural indicates that the subject is a group of people or things.

Description: αρξονται can be translated to English as "they begin" or "they start." It is a common verb in Greek and can be used in a variety of contexts, such as starting a conversation, beginning a project, or initiating an action.
