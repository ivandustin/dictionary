Word: αρνησασθαι

Etymology: αρνησασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αρνέομαι (arnéomai), which means "to deny" or "to refuse." The verb form αρνησασθαι is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

In this case, the subject is implied to be the speaker or a third person, and the object is also implied. The verb is in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: αρνησασθαι means "to deny oneself" or "to refuse for oneself." It can also be translated as "to renounce" or "to reject."
