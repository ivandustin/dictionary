Etymology: The word "αργώ" comes from the ancient Greek word "ἀργός" (argos), which means "lazy" or "inactive". 

Grammar: "Αργει" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation group in Greek. It is formed by adding the present tense endings to the stem "αργ-". 

Description: The verb "αργώ" and its various forms are commonly used in everyday Greek to describe situations where something is taking longer than expected or is delayed. For example, "Αργεί πολύ να έρθει ο καφές" (Argei poli na erthi o kafes) means "The coffee is taking a long time to come".
