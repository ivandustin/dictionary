Word: αραφος

Etymology: The word αραφος comes from the Greek word ἄραφος (aráphos), which means "unstitched" or "unsewn". It is derived from the prefix ἀ- (a-), which means "not", and the verb ῥάπτω (rháptō), which means "to sew".

Grammar: αραφος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: αραφος is used to describe something that is not stitched or sewn together. It can refer to clothing, fabric, or any other material that has not been sewn. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is incomplete or unfinished.
