Word: αρα

Etymology: The word αρα comes from the Greek language and is believed to have originated from the ancient Greek word ἄρα, which means "therefore" or "so".

Grammar: αρα is a conjunction in Greek grammar, which is used to introduce a conclusion or inference. It is often translated as "so", "therefore", or "then" in English.

Description: αρα is a common word in the Greek language and is used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to connect two clauses or sentences, indicating that the second clause is a logical conclusion or inference drawn from the first. For example, "Πήγε στο σπίτι, αρα ήταν κουρασμένος" (He went home, so he must have been tired).
