Word: αρεσαι

Etymology: αρεσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "αρέσω" (areso), which means "to please" or "to be pleasing".

Grammar: αρεσαι is a second-person singular present tense form of the verb "αρέσω" (areso), which means "you please" or "you are pleasing". It is used to express liking or preference for something or someone.

Description: αρεσαι is a verb that is commonly used in everyday Greek conversations. It is often used to express one's personal preferences or opinions about something or someone. For example, "Μου αρέσει η θάλασσα" (Mou aresi i thalassa) means "I like the sea". The verb can also be used in a negative form to express dislike or disapproval.
