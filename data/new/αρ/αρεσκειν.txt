Word: αρεσκειν

Etymology: The word αρεσκειν (areskein) is derived from the Greek verb αρέσκω (aresko), which means "to please" or "to be pleasing". It is a combination of the prefix αρ- (ar-), which means "fitting" or "suitable", and the verb εὐαρεστέω (euaresteo), which means "to be pleasing".

Grammar: αρεσκειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used in combination with other verbs to form compound verbs, such as αρεσκειν ποιεω (areskein poieo), which means "to please" or "to satisfy".
