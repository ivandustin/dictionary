The word "αρχουσιν" is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "αρχω" (archō), which means "to rule" or "to lead". It is in the present tense, third person plural form, indicating that "they rule" or "they lead". In terms of grammar, it is a regular verb in the present tense, with the stem "αρχ-" and the ending "-ουσιν". The etymology of the word can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "αρχή" (archē), which means "beginning" or "origin", and is also the root of words like "archaic" and "archetype".
