Word: αρχιτεκτων

Etymology:
The word αρχιτεκτων (architekton) comes from the Greek words αρχι (archi), meaning "chief" or "master," and τεκτων (tekton), meaning "builder" or "carpenter." Therefore, the word αρχιτεκτων refers to a master builder or architect.

Grammar:
In Greek, αρχιτεκτων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is αρχιτέκτονες (architektones).

Description:
αρχιτεκτων is a term used to describe a person who designs and supervises the construction of buildings, bridges, and other structures. In ancient Greece, architects were highly respected and considered to be artists. They were responsible for designing and overseeing the construction of some of the most iconic buildings in history, such as the Parthenon in Athens. Today, architects continue to play a vital role in shaping the built environment, designing structures that are both functional and aesthetically pleasing.
