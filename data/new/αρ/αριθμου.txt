Word: αριθμου

Etymology: The word αριθμου (arithmou) comes from the Greek word αριθμός (arithmos), which means "number". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁reh₁- which means "to count, number".

Grammar: αριθμου is a noun in the genitive case in Greek. It is the genitive singular form of αριθμός (arithmos), which means "number". In Greek, nouns decline based on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship.

Description: αριθμου is a Greek word that means "of a number" or "of numbers". It is often used in mathematical contexts to refer to the properties or characteristics of numbers. For example, one might say "τα χαρακτηριστικά του αριθμού 5 είναι πρώτος και πενταγωνικός" (the characteristics of the number 5 are prime and pentagonal).
