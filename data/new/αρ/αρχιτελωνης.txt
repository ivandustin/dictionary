Word: αρχιτελωνης

Etymology: The word αρχιτελωνης comes from the Greek words αρχή (archi) meaning "chief" or "leader" and τελώνης (telones) meaning "tax collector" or "customs officer". 

Grammar: αρχιτελωνης is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and its plural form is αρχιτελώνες (architelones). 

Description: αρχιτελωνης refers to a chief customs officer or tax collector. In ancient Greece, these officials were responsible for collecting taxes and duties on goods that were imported or exported. They were often appointed by the government and held a position of authority in their community. The term can also be used metaphorically to refer to someone who is in charge of overseeing a particular area or department.
