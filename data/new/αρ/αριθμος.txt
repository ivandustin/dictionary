Word: αριθμος

Etymology:
The word αριθμος (arithmos) comes from the Greek root ἀριθμός (arithmos), which means "number". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁reh₁- ("to count, number").

Grammar:
In Greek, αριθμος is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in the singular and plural, and its nominative, genitive, and accusative forms are all the same.

Description:
αριθμος is a Greek word that means "number". It is used to refer to a numerical value or quantity. In mathematics, αριθμος is used to describe the properties and relationships of numbers. It is also used in everyday language to describe quantities of objects or people.
