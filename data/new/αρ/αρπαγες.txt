Word: αρπαγες

Etymology: The word αρπαγες (arpages) comes from the Greek verb αρπαζω (arpazo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize". The suffix -γες (-ges) is a plural ending, so αρπαγες means "those who snatch" or "those who seize".

Grammar: αρπαγες is a noun in the masculine gender and plural number. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. αρπαγες is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: αρπαγες is a word that refers to people who snatch or seize things by force. It can be used to describe thieves, robbers, or other criminals who take things that do not belong to them. The word can also be used in a metaphorical sense to describe people who take advantage of others or exploit them for their own gain.
