Word: αρπαγην

Etymology:
The word αρπαγην (arpagin) is derived from the Greek verb αρπάζω (arpazo), which means "to snatch" or "to seize". The suffix -ην (-in) is a common ending for nouns in the Greek language.

Grammar:
αρπαγην is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is a singular noun, and its gender is feminine.

Description:
αρπαγην refers to something that has been snatched or seized. It can be used to describe a physical object that has been taken forcefully, or it can be used in a figurative sense to describe a situation where someone has taken advantage of another person or situation. The word can also be used to describe a person who is a thief or a robber.
