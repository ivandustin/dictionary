Word: αρχαια

Etymology: αρχαια comes from the Greek word αρχαίος (archaios), which means "ancient" or "old".

Grammar: αρχαια is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative singular form.

Description: αρχαια is often used to describe something that is ancient or old. It can refer to ancient artifacts, buildings, or even ideas. In modern Greek, it can also be used to describe something that is outdated or old-fashioned.
