Word: αργυριου

Etymology: The word αργυριου (argyriou) is derived from the Greek word αργυρος (argyros), which means "silver". The suffix -ιου (-iou) is a genitive ending, indicating possession or relationship.

Grammar: αργυριου is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship. It is a singular noun, meaning "of silver".

Description: αργυριου is a Greek word that means "of silver". It is often used in reference to objects or materials made of silver, such as jewelry or coins. In Greek mythology, silver was associated with the moon and was believed to have magical properties. Today, silver is still highly valued for its beauty and durability.
