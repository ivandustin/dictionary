Word: χιλιας

The word χιλιας (chilias) comes from the Greek word χίλιοι (chilioi), which means "thousand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵheslo-, which also gave rise to the Latin word mille, meaning "thousand".

χιλιας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative singular form. The genitive singular form is χιλιαδος (chiliados), the accusative singular form is χιλιαν (chilian), and the nominative plural form is χιλιαδες (chiliades).

χιλιας is a Greek word that means "thousand". It is often used in the context of counting large numbers, such as in the phrase "χιλιαδες ανθρωποι" (chiliades anthropoi), which means "thousands of people".
