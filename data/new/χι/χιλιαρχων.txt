Word: χιλιαρχων

Etymology:
The word χιλιαρχων comes from the Greek words χίλιοι (chilioi) meaning "thousand" and ἄρχων (archon) meaning "ruler" or "leader". Therefore, χιλιαρχων literally means "leader of a thousand".

Grammar:
χιλιαρχων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. The plural form of χιλιαρχων is χιλίαρχοι (chiliarchoi).

Description:
χιλιαρχων was a military rank in ancient Greece. It was used to refer to the commander of a thousand soldiers in the Greek army. The rank was also used in the Roman army, where it was known as the "tribunus militum". The χιλιαρχων was responsible for leading his troops into battle and ensuring their discipline and training. The rank was also used in the Byzantine Empire and in modern Greek military.
