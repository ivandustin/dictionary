Etymology: The word "γυμνάζε" (gymnaze) comes from the Greek verb "γυμνάζω" (gymnazo), which means "to exercise" or "to train." It is derived from the noun "γυμνός" (gymnos), meaning "naked" or "bare," as ancient Greek athletes used to exercise and compete in the nude.

Grammar: "Γυμνάζε" is the imperative form of the verb "γυμνάζω" in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction, specifically telling someone to exercise or train.

Description: "Γυμνάζε" is a Greek word that encourages physical activity and exercise. It is often used as a motivational phrase to encourage someone to stay active and maintain a healthy lifestyle.
