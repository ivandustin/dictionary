Etymology: The word "γυμνοτης" (gymnotis) comes from the Greek words "γυμνός" (gymnos), meaning "naked," and "της" (tis), meaning "one who is." It refers to someone who is naked or unclothed.

Grammar: In Greek, "γυμνοτης" is a noun of masculine gender. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Γυμνοτης" is a term used to describe someone who is naked or without clothing. It can also refer to the state of being naked.
