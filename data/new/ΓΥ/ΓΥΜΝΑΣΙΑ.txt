Etymology: The word "γυμνασία" comes from the Greek word "γυμνάζω" (gymnazo), which means "to exercise" or "to train". It is derived from the root word "γυμνός" (gumnos), meaning "naked" or "bare", as ancient Greek exercises were often performed in the nude.

Grammar: In Greek, "γυμνασία" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the first declension and has the plural form "γυμνασίες" (gymnasiés).

Description: "Γυμνασία" refers to physical education or gymnastics. It encompasses various exercises and activities that promote physical fitness, strength, flexibility, and coordination. In modern usage, it can also refer to a school or institution where physical education is taught.
