Etymology: The word "γυμνα" (gymna) comes from the Greek word "γυμνός" (gumnos), which means "naked." It is derived from the Indo-European root *gʷem- meaning "naked" or "bare."

Grammar: "Γυμνα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the feminine gender and is in the singular form.

Description: "Γυμνα" refers to a gymnasium or a place where physical exercises and sports are practiced. In ancient Greece, the gymnasium was a central part of education and physical training for young men. It was also a place for socializing and intellectual discussions. Today, the term "γυμνα" is still used in Greek to refer to a gym or fitness center.
