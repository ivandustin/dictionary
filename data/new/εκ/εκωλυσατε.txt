Word: εκωλυσατε

Etymology:
The word εκωλυσατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κωλύω (kōlyō), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent." The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from," so εκωλυσατε can be translated as "you prevented" or "you hindered."

Grammar:
εκωλυσατε is the second person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb κωλύω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The active voice indicates that the subject (in this case, "you") is performing the action of hindering or preventing.

Description:
εκωλυσατε is a verb that means "you prevented" or "you hindered" in Greek. It is used to describe an action that stopped something from happening or progressing. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as preventing someone from entering a building or hindering the progress of a project.
