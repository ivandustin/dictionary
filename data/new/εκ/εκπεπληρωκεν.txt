The word "εκπεπληρωκεν" is a Greek verb in the perfect tense, which means that the action it describes has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek roots "εκ" (out of) and "πληρόω" (to fill), with the perfect tense suffix "-κεν". 

Grammar: "εκπεπληρωκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb, which means "he/she/it has fulfilled". The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action. 

Description: The word "εκπεπληρωκεν" is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the fulfillment of a prophecy or a divine plan. It can also be used more generally to describe the completion of a task or obligation.
