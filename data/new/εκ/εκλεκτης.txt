In terms of grammar, "εκλεκτης" is a masculine singular nominative form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, "εκλεκτή" (eklekti) is the feminine singular nominative form, and "εκλεκτοί" (eklektoi) is the masculine plural nominative form.

In usage, "εκλεκτης" is often used to describe someone or something that has been carefully chosen or selected for a particular purpose. It can also be used in a religious context to refer to those who are chosen by God for salvation.
