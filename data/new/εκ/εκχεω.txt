Word: εκχεω

Etymology:
The word εκχεω (ekcheo) comes from the Greek prefix εκ (ek), meaning "out of," and the verb χεω (cheo), meaning "to pour." Therefore, εκχεω means "to pour out."

Grammar:
εκχεω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The conjugation of the verb is as follows:

εκχεω (ekcheo) - I pour out
εκχεις (ekcheis) - you pour out
εκχει (ekchei) - he/she/it pours out
εκχεομεν (ekcheomen) - we pour out
εκχετε (ekchete) - you all pour out
εκχεουσι(ν) (ekcheousi(n)) - they pour out

Description:
The verb εκχεω is used to describe the action of pouring out a liquid or substance from a container. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of expressing or releasing emotions or thoughts. The word is often used in the context of religious or spiritual practices, such as pouring out a libation or offering to a deity.
