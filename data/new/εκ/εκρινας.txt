The word "εκρινας" (ekrinas) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the root "κρίνω" (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix "εκ" (ek) means "out" or "from", so "εκρινας" can be translated as "you judged" or "you decided".

Grammar: "Εκρινας" is the second person singular past tense of the verb "κρίνω". It is in the indicative mood, which means it is a statement of fact. 

Description: The word "εκρινας" implies that the person being addressed made a decision or a judgment in the past. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as legal proceedings, personal relationships, or moral dilemmas.
