Word: εκταρασσουσιν

Etymology: The word εκταρασσουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ταράσσω (tarasso), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out" or "away", so εκταρασσουσιν can be translated as "they are being disturbed" or "they are being agitated".

Grammar: εκταρασσουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject is performing the action of the verb. The verb is also in the indicative mood, which indicates a statement of fact or reality.

Description: εκταρασσουσιν is a verb that describes a state of agitation or disturbance. It can be used to describe a physical or emotional state of being, such as a stormy sea or a person who is upset or anxious. The verb is often used in a figurative sense to describe a situation that is chaotic or unsettled.
