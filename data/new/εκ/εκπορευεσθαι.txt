The word "εκπορευεσθαι" (ekporeuesthai) is a Greek verb that means "to go out" or "to depart". It is derived from the prefix "ek-" which means "out" and the verb "poreuesthai" which means "to go". In terms of grammar, it is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action. 

In the New Testament, the word "εκπορευεσθαι" is used in various contexts, such as when Jesus "went out" to pray (Mark 1:35), when demons "went out" of people (Mark 1:34), and when the Holy Spirit "came out" of heaven (John 15:26). Overall, the word conveys the idea of movement or departure from a particular place or state.
