The word "εκδημησαι" (ekdimesai) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of" or "from," and the verb "δημητής" (dimetis), meaning "to inhabit." Therefore, "εκδημησαι" can be translated as "to leave one's homeland" or "to emigrate."

Grammar: "εκδημησαι" is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to leave their homeland or emigrate.

Description: The word "εκδημησαι" is often used in the context of migration or leaving one's homeland for various reasons such as work, education, or political instability. It is a common word in Greek literature and history, as Greeks have a long history of emigration and diaspora.
