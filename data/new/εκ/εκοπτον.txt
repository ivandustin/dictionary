Grammar: "εκοπτον" (ekopton) is a present participle form of the verb, which means it describes an ongoing action in the present. It is also in the active voice, which means the subject is performing the action.

Description: The word "εκοπτον" (ekopton) can be used in various contexts, such as in surgery, where a surgeon may "cut out" a tumor or a damaged organ. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as "cutting out" bad habits or negative people from one's life.
