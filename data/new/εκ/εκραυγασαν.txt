Word: εκραυγασαν

Etymology: The word εκραυγασαν comes from the Greek verb κραυγάζω (kraugázō), which means "to shout, to cry out". The prefix εκ- (ek-) in this word means "out of" or "from", so εκραυγασαν can be translated as "they shouted out" or "they cried out".

Grammar: εκραυγασαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb κραυγάζω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, "they") is performing the action.

Description: εκραυγασαν is a verb that describes a loud and sudden vocal expression of emotion or urgency. It can be translated as "they shouted out" or "they cried out". The context in which this word is used will determine the specific meaning and tone of the expression.
