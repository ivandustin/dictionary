Word: εκτενως

Etymology:
The word εκτενως (ekténos) is derived from the Greek word εκτενής (ektenees), which means "intensive" or "thorough." It is formed from the prefix εκ- (ek-), meaning "out" or "thoroughly," and the adjective τενής (tenees), meaning "stretched" or "tense."

Grammar:
εκτενως is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is often used to indicate that something is done thoroughly or extensively.

Description:
εκτενως is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. For example, it can be used to describe someone who is working diligently and thoroughly on a task, or to describe a detailed and comprehensive report or analysis. It can also be used to describe a conversation or discussion that is in-depth and thorough. Overall, εκτενως is a useful word for expressing the idea of thoroughness and intensity in a variety of situations.
