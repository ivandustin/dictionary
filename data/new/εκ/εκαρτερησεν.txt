Word: εκαρτερησεν

Etymology: The word εκαρτερησεν comes from the Greek verb καρτερέω (karteréo), which means "to be steadfast, to endure, to persevere". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκαρτερησεν can be translated as "he/she/it persevered" or "he/she/it endured".

Grammar: εκαρτερησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb καρτερέω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person/number. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: εκαρτερησεν is a word that describes an action of perseverance or endurance. It can be used to describe someone who has overcome a difficult situation or has persisted in a task despite obstacles. The word has a positive connotation and implies strength, determination, and resilience.
