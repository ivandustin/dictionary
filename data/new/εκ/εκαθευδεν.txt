The word "εκαθευδεν" (ekatheuden) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "καθεύδω" (katheudo), which means "to sleep". The prefix "εκ" (ek) in this case means "out" or "away from". Therefore, "εκαθευδεν" can be translated to "he/she/it is sleeping away from" or "he/she/it is sleeping apart from". 

In terms of grammar, "εκαθευδεν" is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of the verb "καθεύδω". 

Overall, "εκαθευδεν" is a specific form of the verb "καθεύδω" that indicates someone or something is sleeping apart from or away from something else.
