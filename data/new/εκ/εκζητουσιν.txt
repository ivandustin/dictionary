The word "εκζητουσιν" (ekzetousin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "ζητέω" (zetéo), which means "to seek" or "to search for". The prefix "εκ" (ek) adds the meaning of "out" or "away from", so "εκζητουσιν" can be translated as "they are seeking out" or "they are searching away from". 

In terms of grammar, "εκζητουσιν" is a present active indicative verb, which means it describes an action that is currently happening in the present. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Overall, "εκζητουσιν" is a word that describes an ongoing search or quest for something, with the added connotation of searching away from a particular location or direction.
