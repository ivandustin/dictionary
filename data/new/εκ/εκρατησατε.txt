Word: εκρατησατε

Etymology:
The word εκρατησατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κρατέω (krateo), which means "to hold, to grasp, to seize, to possess, to control." The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from," so εκρατησατε can be translated as "you held out of" or "you seized from."

Grammar:
εκρατησατε is the second person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb κρατέω (krateo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
εκρατησατε can be translated as "you held out of" or "you seized from." It implies a forceful action of taking something away from someone or something. The context in which the word is used can determine the exact meaning and connotation.
