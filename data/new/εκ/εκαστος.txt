Word: εκαστος

Etymology:
The word εκαστος (ekastos) is derived from the Greek words εκ (ek) meaning "out of" and αστος (astos) meaning "city". It originally referred to someone who was from a different city or town.

Grammar:
εκαστος is a masculine singular adjective in Greek. It is used to describe a noun in the sense of "each" or "every". It can also be used as a pronoun to mean "each one" or "everyone".

Description:
εκαστος is a common word in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It is often used in legal or official documents to refer to each individual or entity involved. It can also be used in everyday conversation to refer to each person or thing in a group.
