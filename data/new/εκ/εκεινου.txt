Word: εκείνου

Etymology: 
The word εκείνου (ekinou) is derived from the Greek word εκεί (ekei), which means "there". It is a combination of the preposition εκ (ek), meaning "out of" or "from", and the adverb εκεί (ekei), meaning "there". The suffix -νου (-nou) is a genitive ending, indicating possession.

Grammar:
εκείνου (ekinou) is a pronoun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession. It can be translated to English as "of that" or "of him/her/it".

εκείνου (ekinou) is a demonstrative pronoun in Greek, used to refer to something that is distant in space or time. It is often translated as "that" or "those" in English.
