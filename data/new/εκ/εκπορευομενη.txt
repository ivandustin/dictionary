Word: εκπορευομενη

Etymology: 
The word εκπορευομενη comes from the Greek verb εκπορευομαι (ekporeuomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". The word is formed from the prefix εκ- (ek-), which means "out of", and the verb πορευομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go" or "to travel".

Grammar:
εκπορευομενη is the present participle form of the verb εκπορευομαι (ekporeuomai). It is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
The word εκπορευομενη describes a female subject who is currently going out or coming forth from a particular place or situation. It can be used in a literal sense, such as someone leaving a building or exiting a room, or in a figurative sense, such as someone moving on from a difficult situation or leaving behind a negative experience.
