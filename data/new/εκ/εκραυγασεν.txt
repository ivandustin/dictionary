Word: εκραυγασεν

Etymology: The word εκραυγασεν is derived from the Greek verb κραυγάζω (kraugázō), which means "to shout, to cry out". The prefix εκ- (ek-) in this word means "out of" or "from".

Grammar: εκραυγασεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb κραυγάζω (kraugázō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: εκραυγασεν means "he/she/it shouted" or "he/she/it cried out" in English. It is a verb that describes a sudden and loud vocal expression of emotion or a message. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in literature, poetry, or everyday conversation.
