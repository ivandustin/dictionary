Word: εκαθισαν

Etymology:
The word εκαθισαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καθίζω (kathizo), which means "to sit down". The prefix εκα- (eka-) is a form of the verb είμαι (eimai), which means "to be". Therefore, εκαθισαν means "they were sitting down".

Grammar:
εκαθισαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb καθίζω (kathizo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

εκαθισαν is a verb that describes the action of a group of people sitting down. It is a past tense form, indicating that the action has already taken place.
