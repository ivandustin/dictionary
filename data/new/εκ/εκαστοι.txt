Word: εκαστοι

Etymology: The word εκαστοι (ekastoi) is derived from the Greek word εκάστος (ekastos), which means "each" or "every". It is a combination of the prefix εκ- (ek-), meaning "out of" or "from", and the word αυτός (autos), meaning "self" or "same".

Grammar: εκαστοι is a plural form of the Greek adjective εκάστος (ekastos), which means "each" or "every". It is used to refer to a group of people or things, emphasizing that each individual in the group is being considered separately.

Description: εκαστοι is often used in Greek to refer to a group of people or things, emphasizing that each individual in the group is being considered separately. For example, if someone says "εκαστοι από εσάς θα πρέπει να πληρώσετε το δικό σας εισιτήριο" (ekastoi apo esas tha prepei na plirosete to diko sas eisitirio), it means "each of you will have to pay for your own ticket".
