Word: εκτρεπομενος

Etymology:
The word εκτρεπομενος comes from the Greek verb εκτρεπω (ektrepo) which means "to turn aside" or "to deflect". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing an action that is currently happening.

Grammar:
εκτρεπομενος is a present participle in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb. It is masculine, singular, and nominative, which means that it is describing a singular male subject who is currently turning aside or deflecting.

Description:
The word εκτρεπομενος describes someone who is actively turning aside or deflecting from something. It could be used to describe someone who is avoiding a difficult situation, or someone who is intentionally steering clear of a particular topic or person. The word has a sense of movement and action, suggesting that the subject is actively engaged in the act of turning aside.
