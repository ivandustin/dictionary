Word: εκολαφίσαν (ekolafisan)

Etymology:
The word εκολαφίσαν (ekolafisan) is a past tense verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word κολαφίζω (kolafizo), which means "to slap" or "to hit with an open hand". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the action was completed in the past.

Grammar:
εκολαφίσαν (ekolafisan) is a third-person plural past tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated from the verb κολαφίζω (kolafizo), which is a regular verb in the Greek language. The verb is formed by adding the past tense suffix -αν (-an) to the stem of the verb.

Description:
The word εκολαφίσαν (ekolafisan) describes an action that was completed in the past, where someone or something was slapped or hit with an open hand.
