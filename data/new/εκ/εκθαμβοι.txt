The word "εκθαμβοι" (ekthamvoi) is a Greek adjective that means "astonished" or "dumbfounded". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "θαμβέω" (thambeo), which means "to be amazed" or "to be astonished". The prefix "εκ" (ek) means "out of" or "from", so "εκθαμβοι" literally means "out of astonishment".

Grammar: "εκθαμβοι" is the plural form of the adjective, used to describe a group of people who are all astonished. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, "εκθαμβοι γυναίκες" (ekthamvoi gynaikes) means "astonished women".

Description: The word "εκθαμβοι" is often used to describe a state of shock or surprise. It can be used to describe someone who is speechless or unable to react due to their astonishment. The word can also be used in a positive sense, to describe someone who is amazed by something wonderful or beautiful.
