Word: εκραζον

Etymology: The word εκραζον (ekrazon) is a verb in the ancient Greek language. It is derived from the root word κράζω (krazo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out".

Grammar: εκραζον is a verb in the imperfect tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person singular form of the verb κράζω (krazo).

Description: The word εκραζον is used to describe the action of shouting or crying out repeatedly in the past. It can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as calling out for help, expressing excitement or frustration, or simply making one's presence known.
