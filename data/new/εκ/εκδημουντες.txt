The word "εκδημούντες" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "those who are living abroad". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root words "εκ" (meaning "out of") and "δήμος" (meaning "people" or "community"). The suffix "-ούντες" is added to the root to form the present participle.

Grammar: The word is in the masculine gender and plural number, indicating that it refers to a group of men who are living abroad. It is also in the present tense, indicating that the action of living abroad is currently ongoing.

Description: The word "εκδημούντες" is commonly used in Greek to refer to people who have left their home country to live and work in another country. It can also be used to describe the state of being an expatriate or living abroad.
