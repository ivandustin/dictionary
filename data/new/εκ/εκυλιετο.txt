Word: εκυλιετο

Etymology:
The word εκυλιετο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κυλίω (kylio), which means "to roll." The prefix ε- (e-) in the word εκυλιετο indicates the action of rolling away from something or someone.

Grammar:
εκυλιετο is a third-person singular imperfect middle/passive indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb κυλίω (kylio) and the prefix ε- (e-). The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past, while the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
The word εκυλιετο describes the action of rolling away from something or someone. It can be used to describe a physical action, such as a ball rolling away from a person, or a metaphorical action, such as a person rolling away from a difficult situation. The word is often used in literature and poetry to create vivid imagery and convey a sense of movement and action.
