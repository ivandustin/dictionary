Word: εκαθητο

Etymology: 
The word εκαθητο (ekathito) is derived from the Greek verb καθίζω (kathizo), which means "to sit". The prefix εκα- (eka-) is a form of the preposition εκ (ek), which means "out of" or "from". Therefore, εκαθητο (ekathito) literally means "having been made to sit out of something".

Grammar:
εκαθητο (ekathito) is a past participle of the verb καθίζω (kathizo), which means "to sit". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
εκαθητο (ekathito) is an adjective that describes something or someone that has been made to sit out of something. It can be used to describe a person who has been seated or a thing that has been placed in a seated position. For example, one might say "ο εκαθητος άνδρας" (o ekathitos andras) to mean "the seated man".
