The word "εκκλεισαι" (ekkleisai) is a Greek verb that means "to lock up" or "to shut in." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of" or "from," and the verb "κλείω" (kleiō), meaning "to close" or "to shut." 

Grammar: "Εκκλεισαι" is the aorist tense of the verb, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, meaning the subject is performing the action. 

Description: The word can be used in a literal sense, such as locking a door or shutting a window, or in a figurative sense, such as shutting oneself off from the world or closing off one's emotions. It can also be used in a legal context, such as locking up a criminal or shutting down a business.
