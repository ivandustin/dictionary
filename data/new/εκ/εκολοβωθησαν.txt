Word: εκολοβωθησαν

Etymology: The word εκολοβωθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κολοβόω (kolobóō), which means "to beat with fists." The prefix εκολο- (ekolo-) indicates that the action was done thoroughly or completely.

Grammar: εκολοβωθησαν is a third-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: εκολοβωθησαν means "they were thoroughly beaten" or "they were completely pounded." It is a strong and forceful verb that conveys a sense of violence and aggression.
