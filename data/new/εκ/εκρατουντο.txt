Word: εκρατουντο

Etymology: The word εκρατουντο comes from the Greek verb κρατέω (krateo), which means "to hold, to possess, to rule". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the third person plural form of the verb in the imperfect tense.

Grammar: εκρατουντο is a third person plural form of the Greek verb κρατέω (krateo) in the imperfect tense. It is a passive voice form, indicating that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description: εκρατουντο can be translated as "they were being ruled" or "they were being held". The context of the sentence would determine the exact meaning. The word implies a sense of control or domination over the subject by another entity.
