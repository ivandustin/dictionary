Word: εκρινομεθα

Etymology:
The word εκρινομεθα is derived from the Greek verb κρίνω (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out" or "from", so εκρινομεθα can be translated as "we are judged" or "we are decided upon".

Grammar:
εκρινομεθα is a first-person plural passive form of the verb κρίνω (krino). The suffix -μεθα (-meta) indicates that the subject is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. The prefix εκ (ek) indicates that the action is directed outward from the subject.
