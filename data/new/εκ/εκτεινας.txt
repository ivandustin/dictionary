Word: εκτείνας

Etymology:
The word εκτείνας (ektinas) is derived from the Greek verb εκτείνω (ekteino), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend." It is formed by adding the suffix -ας (-as) to the present participle stem εκτειν- (ektei-n).

Grammar:
εκτείνας is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe the subject of the sentence as performing the action of stretching out or extending.

Description:
εκτείνας is a word that describes an action of stretching out or extending. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person stretching their arms or legs, or a material being stretched out to its full length. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the extension of an idea or concept.
