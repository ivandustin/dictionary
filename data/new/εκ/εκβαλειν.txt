Word: εκβαλειν

Etymology:
The word εκβαλειν comes from the Greek roots εκ (ek) meaning "out" and βαλλειν (ballein) meaning "to throw". Therefore, εκβαλειν means "to throw out".

Grammar:
εκβαλειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood.

Description:
εκβαλειν is a strong and forceful verb that is often used to describe the act of forcefully ejecting something or someone from a place or situation. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of removing something from one's mind or thoughts. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is often used to describe the casting out of demons or evil spirits.
