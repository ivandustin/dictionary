Word: εκελευσα

Etymology: The word εκελευσα comes from the Greek verb κελεύω (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκελευσα can be translated as "I commanded" or "I ordered".

Grammar: εκελευσα is the first person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb κελεύω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: εκελευσα is a Greek verb that means "I commanded" or "I ordered". It is a past tense form that describes a single event in the past.
