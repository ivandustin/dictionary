Word: εκτείνειν

Etymology:
The word εκτείνειν comes from the Greek verb "εκτείνω" (ektéino), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of" or "from", and the verb "τείνω" (téino), meaning "to stretch" or "to extend".

Grammar:
εκτείνειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

εκτείνω (1st person singular)
εκτείνεις (2nd person singular)
εκτείνει (3rd person singular)
εκτείνουμε (1st person plural)
εκτείνετε (2nd person plural)
εκτείνουν (3rd person plural)

Description:
The word εκτείνειν refers to the act of stretching out or extending something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as stretching out one's arms or legs, or in a figurative sense, such as extending one's reach or influence. The verb can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as extending one's thoughts or ideas.
