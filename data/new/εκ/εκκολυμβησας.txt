Word: εκκολυμβησας

Etymology:
The word εκκολυμβησας is derived from the Greek words εκ (ek) meaning "out" and κολυμβάω (kolymbáo) meaning "to swim". Therefore, the literal meaning of εκκολυμβησας is "to swim out".

Grammar:
εκκολυμβησας is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject (the person who swam out) performed the action.

Description:
εκκολυμβησας is a Greek word that means "to swim out". It can be used to describe someone who swam out of a body of water, such as a lake or the ocean. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has broken free from a difficult situation or overcome a challenge.
