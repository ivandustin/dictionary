Word: εκδίκηση (ekdíkisi)

Etymology:
The word εκδίκηση (ekdíkisi) comes from the Greek verb εκδικέω (ekdikéo), which means "to avenge" or "to take revenge". The word is derived from the prefix εκ- (ek-), which means "out of" or "from", and the verb δικαιόω (dikaióo), which means "to justify" or "to make right". Therefore, εκδίκηση (ekdíkisi) literally means "to make right from vengeance".

Grammar:
εκδίκηση (ekdíkisi) is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is εκδικήσεις (ekdikíseis).

Description:
εκδίκηση (ekdíkisi) refers to the act of seeking revenge or retribution for a wrong that has been done. It can also refer to the feeling of vindication or satisfaction that comes from getting revenge. In Greek mythology, the goddess Nemesis was often associated with the concept of εκδίκηση (ekdíkisi), as she was responsible for punishing those who showed hubris or arrogance.
