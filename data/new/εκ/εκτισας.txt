Word: εκτισας

Etymology: 
The word εκτισας comes from the Greek verb "κτίζω" (ktizo), which means "to build" or "to create". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) means "out of" or "from", so εκτισας can be translated as "having built" or "having created".

Grammar:
εκτισας is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence (the one who built or created) is performing the action. The word is also in the second person singular, meaning that it is addressing one person.

Description:
εκτισας is a word that describes an action of building or creating something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in reference to building a physical structure, creating a work of art, or establishing a new organization. The word implies a sense of accomplishment and achievement, as it refers to a completed action in the past.
