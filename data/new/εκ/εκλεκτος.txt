Word: εκλεκτος

Etymology:
The word εκλεκτος (eklektos) comes from the Greek verb εκλεγω (eklego), which means "to choose" or "to select". The word εκλεκτος is the past participle of this verb and means "chosen" or "selected".

Grammar:
In Greek, εκλεκτος is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it would be εκλεκτος (eklektos), but if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it would be εκλεκτη (eklekti). In the plural, it would be εκλεκτοι (eklekti) for masculine or mixed gender nouns, and εκλεκτες (eklektes) for feminine nouns.

Description:
The word εκλεκτος is used to describe something or someone that has been chosen or selected for a particular purpose. It can refer to a person who has been chosen for a special task or position, or to an object that has been selected for its quality or suitability. In religious contexts, it can also refer to those who are chosen by God for salvation. Overall, εκλεκτος conveys a sense of excellence, distinction, and specialness.
