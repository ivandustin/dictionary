Word: εκραταιουτο

Etymology:
The word εκραταιουτο is a conjugated form of the Greek verb κραταιόω (krataioo), which means "to strengthen" or "to make strong". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense and the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Grammar:
εκραταιουτο is a third-person singular past tense form of the verb κραταιόω in the middle voice. The verb belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is κραταιο- and it follows a regular pattern of inflection.

Description:
The word εκραταιουτο can be translated as "he/she/it was strengthened" or "he/she/it made himself/herself/itself strong". It implies an action of becoming stronger or more powerful, either through one's own efforts or through external means. The context in which the word is used can provide more information about the specific circumstances and motivations behind the action.
