Word: εκστασις

Etymology:
The word εκστασις comes from the Greek word "ekstasis" which means "standing outside oneself" or "being beside oneself". It is derived from the prefix "ek-" meaning "out" and the verb "histemi" meaning "to stand".

Grammar:
εκστασις is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is εκστάσεις.

Description:
εκστασις refers to a state of being outside oneself, often described as a trance or a state of ecstasy. It can also refer to a state of extreme emotion or excitement. In ancient Greek philosophy, εκστασις was seen as a state of transcendence, where one could achieve a higher level of consciousness or understanding. Today, the word is often used in a more general sense to describe a state of intense emotion or spiritual experience.
