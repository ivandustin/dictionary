Word: εκλαιεν

Etymology:
The word εκλαιεν is derived from the Greek verb κλαίω (klaió), which means "to weep" or "to cry". The prefix εκ- (ek-) means "out of" or "from", so εκλαιεν can be translated as "he/she/it was weeping" or "he/she/it was crying out".

Grammar:
εκλαιεν is a verb in the third person singular, past tense, indicative mood, active voice. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action took place in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
εκλαιεν is a word that describes an action of weeping or crying out. It can be used to describe a person who is crying due to sadness, pain, or other strong emotions. The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a situation or event that is causing emotional distress or pain.
