Word: εκπαλαι

Etymology:
The word εκπαλαι (ekpalai) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix εκ (ek) meaning "out of" or "from" and the word παλαι (palai) meaning "ancient" or "old". Therefore, the word εκπαλαι can be translated as "from ancient times" or "since olden times".

Grammar:
In Greek, εκπαλαι is an adverb, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is often used to describe something that has been happening or existing for a long time, or to emphasize the antiquity of something.

Description:
The word εκπαλαι is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the ancient origins of a place, tradition, or custom. It can also be used to describe the long-standing history of a family or lineage.
