Word: εκείνοις

Etymology: 
The word εκείνοις (ekinóis) is a Greek demonstrative pronoun that means "those" or "those ones". It comes from the Greek word "ekeinos" which means "that one" or "that thing". 

Grammar: 
In Greek, εκείνοις is a plural form of the demonstrative pronoun. It is used to refer to a group of people or things that are not close to the speaker or the listener. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

Description: 
εκείνοις is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to refer to a group of people or things that are far away from the speaker or the listener. For example, "Εκείνοις τοις ανθρώποις δεν αρέσει η βροχή" (Ekinóis tois anthrópois den arései i vrochí) means "Those people don't like the rain". It can also be used to emphasize a particular group of people or things. For example, "Εκείνοις τοις μαθητές είναι πολύ δύσκολο το μάθημα" (Ekinóis tois mathítes eínai polý dýskolo to mathíma) means "Those students find the lesson very difficult".
