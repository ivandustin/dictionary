Word: εκοπιάσεν

Etymology:
The word εκοπιάσεν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb κοπιάζω (kopiázō), which means "to toil" or "to work hard". The verb is derived from the noun κόπος (kópos), which means "labor" or "toil".

Grammar:
εκοπιάσεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of κοπιάζω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
The word εκοπιάσεν describes a completed action of someone working hard or toiling. It can be translated as "he/she/it toiled" or "he/she/it worked hard". The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing physical labor, mental exertion, or emotional strain.
