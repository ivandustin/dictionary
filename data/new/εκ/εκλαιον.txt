The word "εκλαιον" (eklaion) is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the root word "κλαίω" (klaio), which means "to weep" or "to cry." 

In terms of grammar, "εκλαιον" is in the past tense and indicative mood. It is the third person plural form of the verb "κλαίω." 

The word "εκλαιον" can be translated to English as "they wept" or "they cried." It is often used in the context of mourning or expressing deep sadness.
