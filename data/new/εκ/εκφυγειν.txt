The word "εκφυγειν" (ekfyegein) is a Greek verb that means "to escape" or "to flee". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), which means "out of" or "from", and the verb "φυγειν" (fygein), which means "to flee". 

Grammar: "Εκφυγειν" is an irregular verb in Greek, and its conjugation varies depending on the tense and mood. In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows: εκφεύγω, εκφεύγεις, εκφεύγει, εκφεύγουμε, εκφεύγετε, εκφεύγουν. 

Description: The verb "εκφυγειν" is often used to describe a sudden or urgent escape from danger or a difficult situation. It can also be used in a more metaphorical sense, to describe escaping from a difficult emotional or psychological state.
