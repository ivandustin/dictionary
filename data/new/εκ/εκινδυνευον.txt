Word: εκινδυνευον

Etymology: The word εκινδυνευον comes from the Greek verb κινδυνεύω (kindynevo), which means "to be in danger" or "to risk". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκινδυνευον can be translated as "being out of danger" or "not risking".

Grammar: εκινδυνευον is the present participle of the verb κινδυνεύω in the active voice. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: εκινδυνευον describes someone or something that is not in danger or not taking risks. It can also be used to describe a situation that is safe or secure.
