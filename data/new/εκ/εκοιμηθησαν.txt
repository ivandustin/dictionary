Word: εκοιμηθησαν

Etymology: 
The word εκοιμηθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κοιμάω (koimao), which means "to sleep". The prefix ε- (e-) in this word indicates a past tense, so εκοιμηθησαν means "they slept" or "they were sleeping".

Grammar:
εκοιμηθησαν is a third-person plural past tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated from the root verb κοιμάω (koimao) and the past tense prefix ε- (e-). The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
εκοιμηθησαν is a verb that describes an action of sleeping or being asleep. It is often used in the context of a group of people or animals sleeping together. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of inactivity or lack of progress.
