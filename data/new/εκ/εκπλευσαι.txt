The word "εκπλευσαι" (ekplefsai) is a Greek verb that means "to sail out" or "to set sail." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out," and the verb "πλεύω" (plevo), meaning "to sail."

Grammar: "Εκπλευσαι" is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb, meaning it is a command directed towards one person to perform the action of sailing out.

Description: The word "εκπλευσαι" is often used in the context of ships or boats leaving a port or harbor. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone leaving a situation or place.
