Word: εκατονταρχων

Etymology: The word εκατονταρχων comes from the Greek words εκατόν (ekatón) meaning "hundred" and αρχων (archon) meaning "ruler" or "leader". Therefore, εκατονταρχων literally means "ruler of a hundred".

Grammar: εκατονταρχων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and the plural form is εκατόνταρχοι (ekatóntarchoi).

Description: In ancient Greece, εκατονταρχων referred to a military officer who commanded a hundred soldiers. The position was similar to that of a modern-day captain. The term was also used in the Byzantine Empire to refer to a high-ranking military officer who commanded a hundred soldiers or more. Today, the word is rarely used outside of historical or military contexts.
