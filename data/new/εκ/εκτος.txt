Word: εκτος

Etymology: εκτος is a Greek word that means "outside" or "beyond". It is derived from the Greek preposition "εκ" (ek), which means "out of" or "from".

Grammar: εκτος is an adjective in Greek, which means it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can also be used as an adverb to mean "outside" or "beyond".

Description: εκτος is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to describe something that is outside or beyond a certain boundary or limit. For example, "εκτος της πολιτειας" (ektos tis politias) means "outside the state". It can also be used to describe something that is exceptional or extraordinary, such as "εκτος συνηθειας" (ektos synitheias) which means "out of habit".
