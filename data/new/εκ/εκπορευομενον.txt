Word: εκπορευομενον

Etymology:
The word εκπορευομενον is a present participle form of the Greek verb εκπορεύομαι (ekporeuomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". The word is derived from the prefix εκ (ek), meaning "out of", and the verb πορεύομαι (poreuomai), meaning "to go".

Grammar:
εκπορευομενον is a present participle in the neuter gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντ- (-ont-) to the present stem of the verb. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description:
The word εκπορευομενον describes something that is going out or coming forth. It can be used to describe a physical object or a non-physical concept. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe the Holy Spirit coming forth from God (John 15:26). In modern Greek, the word is also used to describe the act of leaving or departing from a place.
