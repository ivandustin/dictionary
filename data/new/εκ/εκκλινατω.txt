Word: εκκλινατω

Etymology:
The word εκκλινατω comes from the Greek verb εκκλίνω (ekklinó), which means "to bend aside" or "to turn away". It is derived from the prefix εκ- (ek-), meaning "out of" or "away from", and the verb κλίνω (klínō), meaning "to bend" or "to incline".

Grammar:
εκκλινατω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and imperative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb εκκλίνω (ekklinó).

Description:
The word εκκλινατω is used to give a command or instruction to someone to "turn away" or "bend aside". It can be used in a literal sense, such as telling someone to move out of the way, or in a figurative sense, such as telling someone to avoid a certain topic or situation.
