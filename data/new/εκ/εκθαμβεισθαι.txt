Word: εκθαμβεισθαι

Etymology:
The word εκθαμβεισθαι comes from the Greek roots εκ (ek) meaning "out" and θαμβεω (thambeo) meaning "to be amazed or astonished". Therefore, εκθαμβεισθαι means "to be amazed or astonished out of one's senses".

Grammar:
εκθαμβεισθαι is a verb in the passive voice, present tense, infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -σθαι (-sthai) to the stem εκθαμβε-. 

Description:
εκθαμβεισθαι is a strong verb that expresses a state of being overwhelmed with amazement or astonishment. It is often used in the New Testament to describe the reaction of people to the miracles of Jesus. The word conveys a sense of being so amazed that one is almost paralyzed or unable to think or act.
