Word: εκείνων

Etymology: 
The word εκείνων (ekinon) is a Greek pronoun that means "those" or "those ones". It is derived from the Greek word "ekeinos" which means "that one" or "that thing". 

Grammar: 
εκείνων is a plural form of the pronoun εκείνος (ekinos) which means "that" or "that one". It is used to refer to a group of people or things that are not close to the speaker or the listener. In Greek, pronouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. εκείνων is the genitive plural form of εκείνος.

Description: 
εκείνων is a Greek pronoun that is commonly used in everyday speech and writing. It is used to refer to a group of people or things that are not close to the speaker or the listener. For example, "εκείνων οι άνθρωποι" (ekinon oi anthropoi) means "those people". The word is often used in combination with other words to form phrases such as "εκείνων των ημερών" (ekinon ton imeron) which means "those days".
