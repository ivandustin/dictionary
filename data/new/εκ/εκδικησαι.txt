Word: εκδικησαι

Etymology:
The word εκδικησαι (ekdikisai) is a verb in the Greek language, which comes from the root word δίκη (diki), meaning "justice" or "judgment". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out" or "away", so the word εκδίκηση (ekdikisi) means "to take justice into one's own hands" or "to avenge".

Grammar:
εκδικησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The word is in the second person singular form, which means that it is addressing one person.

Description:
The word εκδικησαι means "to avenge" or "to take revenge". It implies a desire to seek justice or retribution for a perceived wrong or injustice. The word is often used in a negative context, as it suggests a desire for retaliation rather than forgiveness or reconciliation.
