Word: εκπεμφθεντες

Etymology: 
The word εκπεμφθεντες is derived from the Greek verb εκπέμπω (ekpémpō), which means "to send out" or "to dispatch". The word is formed by adding the suffix -εντες (-entes), which is a participle suffix indicating the present active participle.

Grammar:
εκπεμφθεντες is a plural form of the present active participle of the verb εκπέμπω. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. The word can be translated to English as "those who are being sent out" or "those who are dispatching".

Description:
εκπεμφθεντες is a Greek word that refers to a group of people who are being sent out or dispatching. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in military operations, diplomatic missions, or religious missions. The word emphasizes the action of sending out or dispatching, rather than the purpose or destination of the mission.
