Word: εκδυσαντες

Etymology:
The word εκδυσαντες is derived from the Greek verb εκδύω (ekdyo), which means "to undress" or "to strip off". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of undressing has already taken place.

Grammar:
εκδυσαντες is a past participle in the masculine plural form. It is used in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of the sentence. In Greek, the past participle is often used to form compound tenses, such as the perfect and pluperfect.

Description:
εκδυσαντες can be translated to "having undressed" or "having stripped off". It describes a group of people who have already taken off their clothes. The context in which this word is used will determine the exact meaning and implications of the action.
