Word: εκλαυσατε

Etymology: 
The word εκλαυσατε is derived from the Greek verb κλαίω (kláio), which means "to weep" or "to cry". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out" or "away", so εκλαυσατε can be translated as "to weep out" or "to cry away".

Grammar:
εκλαυσατε is the second person plural aorist active imperative form of the verb κλαίω (kláio). This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to weep or cry.

Description:
εκλαυσατε is a strong and emotional word that conveys a sense of deep sadness or grief. It is often used in religious or poetic contexts to express mourning or lamentation. The imperative form of the word suggests a call to action, urging listeners to express their sorrow through tears.
