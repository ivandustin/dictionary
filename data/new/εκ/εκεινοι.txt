Word: εκείνοι

Etymology:
The word εκείνοι (ekini) is derived from the Greek word "εκείνος" (ekinos), which means "that one" or "those ones". It is a demonstrative pronoun that is used to refer to something that is far away from the speaker or the listener.

Grammar:
In Greek, εκείνοι is a masculine plural form of the pronoun εκείνος. It is used to refer to a group of people or things that are far away from the speaker or the listener. It can be used as a subject, object, or possessive pronoun.

Description:
The word εκείνοι is commonly used in Greek to refer to a group of people or things that are far away from the speaker or the listener. It is often used in storytelling or in describing a scene or situation. For example, "εκείνοι οι άνθρωποι είναι πολύ φιλόξενοι" (ekini oi anthropoi einai poli filoxenoi) means "those people are very hospitable".
