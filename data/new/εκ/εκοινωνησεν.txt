Word: εκοινωνησεν

Etymology: The word εκοινωνησεν is derived from the Greek word κοινωνέω (koinonéo), which means "to share, to participate, to communicate". The prefix ε- (e-) in εκοινωνησεν indicates the past tense, making the word mean "shared, participated, communicated" in the past.

Grammar: εκοινωνησεν is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb κοινωνέω (koinonéo) in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: εκοινωνησεν means "he/she/it shared, participated, communicated" in the past. It is often used in the context of sharing or communicating something with others, such as information, ideas, or feelings. The word can also be used in a religious context to describe the act of partaking in communion or sharing in the body and blood of Christ.
