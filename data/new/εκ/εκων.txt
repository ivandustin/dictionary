Word: εκων

Etymology:
The word εκων (ekon) is derived from the Greek verb εκεινω (ekeino), which means "to be willing" or "to consent." It is also related to the Greek word εκος (ekos), which means "voluntary."

Grammar:
εκων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also used as an adjective, meaning "willing" or "voluntary." In Greek, it is declined according to the second declension.

Description:
εκων is a word that describes someone who is willing or consenting to do something. It can also be used to describe something that is done voluntarily or willingly. In ancient Greek literature, it is often used to describe heroes who willingly undertake dangerous tasks or sacrifices for the greater good.
