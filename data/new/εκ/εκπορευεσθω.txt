The word "εκπορευεσθω" (ekporeuomai) is a Greek verb that means "to go out" or "to depart". It is derived from the prefix "εκ-" (ek-) which means "out of" and the verb "πορευομαι" (poreuomai) which means "to go". 

In terms of grammar, "εκπορευεσθω" is a present passive imperative verb in the third person singular. This means that it is a command directed towards someone else to "let it go out" or "let it depart". 

Overall, "εκπορευεσθω" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in religious or philosophical texts to describe the departure of the soul from the body, or in everyday language to describe the act of leaving a place or situation.
