The word "εκραυγαζον" (ekraugazón) is a verb in the present participle form in Greek. It comes from the root word "κραυγάζω" (kraugázō), which means "to shout" or "to scream". The prefix "εκ-" (ek-) in this case means "out" or "outwardly". 

In terms of grammar, "εκραυγαζον" is in the present tense and active voice. It is also in the participle form, which means it can function as an adjective or adverb in a sentence. 

As for its description, "εκραυγαζον" describes someone who is currently shouting or screaming. It can be used to describe a person who is expressing strong emotions such as anger, fear, or excitement. 

Example sentence: Οι φίλοι μου εκραυγάζουν από τη χαρά τους για τη νίκη της ομάδας. (My friends are shouting with joy for the team's victory.)
