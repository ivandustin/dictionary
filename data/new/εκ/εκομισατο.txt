Word: εκομισατο

Etymology: εκομισατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κομίζω (komízo), which means "to bring" or "to carry". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar: εκομισατο is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb κομίζω (komízo) in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: εκομισατο means "he/she/it brought" or "he/she/it carried" in English. It is often used to describe a completed action in the past, such as "he brought the book to me" or "she carried the groceries home".
