The word "εκζητηθησεται" is a Greek verb in the passive voice, which means "to be thoroughly examined or investigated". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "εκ-" which means "out of" or "thoroughly", and the verb "ζητώ" which means "to seek" or "to search". The suffix "-θη-" is a common passive voice marker in Greek verbs, and the ending "-σεται" indicates the third person singular form in the future tense.

Grammar: The verb is in the passive voice, which means that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The tense is future, indicating that the action of being examined or investigated will take place at some point in the future.

Description: "εκζητηθησεται" is a formal and complex word that is often used in legal or academic contexts. It implies a thorough and rigorous examination or investigation, suggesting that the subject is being scrutinized in great detail.
