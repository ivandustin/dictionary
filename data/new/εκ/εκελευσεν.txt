Word: εκελευσεν

Etymology: The word εκελευσεν (ekelusen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κελευω (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order."

Grammar: εκελευσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is in the past tense and indicates that the subject (he/she/it) gave a command or order.

Description: εκελευσεν is a strong and authoritative word that implies a sense of power and control. It is often used in the context of military or political commands, but can also be used in everyday situations to indicate a strong directive or instruction. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the commands of Jesus or God.
