The word εκπορευόμενος comes from the Greek verb εκπορεύομαι (ekporevomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". The word is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos), which is a participle suffix indicating the present tense and active voice.

εκπορευόμενος is a present participle in the active voice. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενος to the present stem of the verb εκπορεύομαι. The word is declined like a regular adjective, with the masculine nominative singular form being εκπορευόμενος.

Description:
εκπορευόμενος describes someone or something that is going out or coming forth. It can be used in a literal sense, such as someone leaving a building, or in a figurative sense, such as an idea or emotion coming to the surface.
