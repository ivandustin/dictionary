The word "εκβαλλομενους" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "casting out" or "expelling". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "εκ" meaning "out" and "βαλλω" meaning "to throw". In terms of grammar, it is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and accusative case. It is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of casting out demons or evil spirits.
