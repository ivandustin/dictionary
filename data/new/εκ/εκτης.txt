The word "εκτης" (ektis) is a Greek adjective that means "sixth" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek prefix "εκ-" (ek-) meaning "out of" or "from" and the cardinal number "εξ" (ex) meaning "six". 

Grammar: "Εκτης" is a masculine, feminine, and neuter adjective in the nominative singular form. It declines like the first and second declension adjectives in Greek. 

Description: The word "εκτης" is used to describe the ordinal position of something in a series, indicating that it is the sixth in order. For example, "Η εκτή μέρα του ταξιδιού μας ήταν η πιο δύσκολη" (I ekti mera tou taxidiou mas itan i pio diskoli) means "The sixth day of our trip was the most difficult".
