Word: εκπορευομενοις

Etymology:
The word εκπορευομενοις is a present participle form of the Greek verb εκπορευομαι (ekporeuomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". The word is derived from the prefix εκ (ek), meaning "out of", and the verb πορευομαι (poreuomai), meaning "to go".

Grammar:
εκπορευομενοις is a present participle form of the verb εκπορευομαι. In Greek grammar, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντ- (-ont-) to the stem of the verb. The present participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
εκπορευομενοις is a Greek word that can be translated to English as "going out" or "coming forth". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the action of people or things leaving a particular place or situation. For example, in Mark 1:35, the phrase "εκπορευομενοις αυτοις" (ekporeuomenois autois) is translated as "while they were going out" or "as they were leaving". The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of movement or departure.
