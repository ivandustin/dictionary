Word: εκαμμυσαν

In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action, and the indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: εκαμμυσαν is a word that describes the action of closing one's eyes. It can be used in various contexts, such as to describe someone who is sleeping, meditating, or simply closing their eyes to rest. The word has a poetic quality to it and is often used in literature and poetry.
