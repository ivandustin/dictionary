The word "εκζητησας" (ekzetisas) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "εκ" (ek), meaning "out of" or "from", and the verb "ζητώ" (zito), meaning "to seek" or "to search". Therefore, "εκζητησας" can be translated as "to search out" or "to investigate thoroughly".

Grammar: "εκζητησας" is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the second person singular, meaning it is addressing one person.

Description: The word "εκζητησας" is often used to describe a thorough investigation or search for something. It can also be used to describe a person who is persistent in their search for knowledge or truth.
