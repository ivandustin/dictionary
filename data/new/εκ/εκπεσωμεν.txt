Etymology:
The word εκπεσωμεν is derived from the Greek verb εκπίπτω (ekpípto), which means "to fall out" or "to fall from". The word is formed by adding the suffix -ωμεν (-omen) to the stem εκπεσ- (ekpes-).

εκπεσωμεν is a first-person plural present active subjunctive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ωμεν (-omen) to the stem εκπεσ- (ekpes-) and conjugating it in the present tense. The subjunctive mood indicates that the action is uncertain or hypothetical.

The word εκπεσωμεν can be translated to English as "let us fall out" or "let us fall from". It is often used in a figurative sense to mean "to fail" or "to be defeated". The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe a physical falling out or falling from a place or position.
