Word: εκδικησον

Etymology:
The word εκδικησον (ekdikison) is derived from the Greek verb εκδικεω (ekdikeo), which means "to avenge" or "to take revenge". The word is formed by adding the suffix -σον (-son) to the imperative form of the verb, which gives it the meaning "avenge!" or "take revenge!".

Grammar:
εκδικησον is a second person singular imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is used to give a command or order to someone to take revenge or avenge something.

Description:
The word εκδικησον is a strong and powerful word that conveys a sense of justice and retribution. It is often used in the context of seeking revenge for a wrong that has been done, and can be seen as a call to action for someone to take matters into their own hands. The word has a sense of urgency and intensity, and is often used in dramatic or emotional situations.
