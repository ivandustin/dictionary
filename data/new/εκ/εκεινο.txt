Word: εκείνο (ekino)

Etymology:
The word εκείνο (ekino) comes from the Greek root word "εκεί" (ekei), which means "there". The suffix "-νο" (-no) is added to the end of the root word to form the pronoun "εκείνο" (ekino), which means "that" or "those".

Grammar:
εκείνο (ekino) is a pronoun in Greek. It is used to refer to something that has already been mentioned or is understood from the context. It can be used as a subject, object, or possessive pronoun.

Description:
εκείνο (ekino) is a versatile pronoun that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to refer to something that is distant in space or time, or to something that is not immediately present. For example, "Εκείνο το βιβλίο είναι πολύ καλό" (Ekino to vivlio einai poli kalo) means "That book is very good". It can also be used to emphasize a particular object or person, as in "Εκείνος ο άνθρωπος είναι πολύ έξυπνος" (Ekino o anthropos einai poli exypnos) which means "That man is very smart".
