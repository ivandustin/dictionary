The word "εκπορευομενα" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "coming out" or "emerging". It is derived from the root word "πορεύομαι" (poreúomai), which means "to go" or "to travel". The prefix "εκ" (ek) in this word means "out" or "from". 

In terms of grammar, "εκπορευομενα" is in the present tense and active voice. It is also in the neuter gender and plural number, which means it refers to multiple things that are coming out or emerging.

Overall, "εκπορευομενα" is a descriptive word that can be used to describe a variety of situations where something is coming out or emerging, such as the emergence of new ideas or the flow of water from a spring.
