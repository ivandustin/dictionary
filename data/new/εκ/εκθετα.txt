The word "εκθετα" (ektheta) is a Greek adjective that means "exposed" or "displayed". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "εκθέτω" (ektheto), which means "to expose" or "to display". 

Grammar: "Εκθετα" is the neuter plural form of the adjective. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word can be used to describe something that is openly displayed or exposed to view, such as an exhibit in a museum or a product in a store. It can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is open and honest about their thoughts or feelings.
