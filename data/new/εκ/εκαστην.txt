Word: εκαστην

Etymology: The word εκαστην comes from the Greek word "εκάστη" (ekásti), which means "each" or "every". The suffix "-ν" (-n) is added to the end of the word to indicate the accusative case.

Grammar: εκαστην is a feminine singular accusative form of the adjective εκάστος (ekástos), which means "each" or "every". It is used to modify a feminine singular noun in the accusative case.

Description: εκαστην is used in Greek to indicate "each" or "every" in reference to a feminine singular noun. For example, "εκαστην ημέρα" (ekásti iméra) means "each day" or "every day". It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing.
