Word: εκείνον

Etymology:
The word εκείνον (ekénon) is a Greek pronoun that means "that one" or "he/she/it." It comes from the Greek word "ekeinos," which means "that one" or "that person."

Grammar:
In Greek, εκείνον is a third-person singular pronoun that can be used to refer to a person, thing, or idea that has already been mentioned or is known to the speaker and listener. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it refers to.

Description:
εκείνον is a versatile pronoun that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to refer to a person or thing that has been previously mentioned or is known to the speaker and listener. For example, "Εκείνον τον άνθρωπο τον γνωρίζω" (Ekénon ton ánthropo ton gnorízo) means "I know that person." It can also be used to emphasize a particular person or thing, as in "Εκείνον τον άνθρωπο τον αγαπώ πολύ" (Ekénon ton ánthropo ton agapó polý), which means "I love that person very much."
