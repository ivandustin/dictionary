The word "εκειτο" (ekéito) is a Greek verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It comes from the verb "κείμαι" (keímai), which means "to lie down" or "to be situated". 

In its past tense form, "εκειτο" means "he/she/it lay" or "he/she/it was situated". It is often used in literature and storytelling to describe the position or location of a person or object. 

In terms of grammar, "εκειτο" is a regular verb in the past tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-μαι". 

Overall, "εκειτο" is a useful word for describing the position or location of something in the past tense.
