Word: εκαθεζετο

Etymology: The word εκαθεζετο comes from the Greek verb καθέζομαι (kathézomai), which means "to sit down". The prefix εκα- (eka-) is a form of the preposition εκ (ek), which means "out of" or "from". Therefore, εκαθεζετο can be translated as "he/she/it was sitting down".

Grammar: εκαθεζετο is a third person singular past tense verb in the indicative mood. It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb stem is καθεζ-, and the suffix -ετο indicates the third person singular.

Description: εκαθεζετο is a Greek verb that describes the action of sitting down. It can be used to describe a single instance of sitting down in the past. For example, "Ο άντρας εκαθεζετο στο πάρκο" (O ántras ekathézeto sto párko) means "The man sat down in the park".
