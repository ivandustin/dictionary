Word: εκαλούν

Etymology: The word εκαλούν (ekaloun) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb καλώ (kalo), which means "to call" or "to name". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the third person plural form of the verb in the past tense.

Grammar: εκαλούν is a regular verb in the Greek language, belonging to the first conjugation group. It is conjugated in the past tense, third person plural form. The verb stem is καλού- (kalou-), and the suffix -ν (-n) indicates the third person plural form.

Description: εκαλούν is a verb that describes an action in the past where someone or something was called or named by a group of people. It can be translated as "they called" or "they named". The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in storytelling, historical accounts, or everyday conversations.
