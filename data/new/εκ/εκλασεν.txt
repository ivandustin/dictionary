Word: εκλασεν

Etymology: The word εκλασεν comes from the Greek verb κλάω (kláo), which means "to break" or "to shatter". The prefix εκ- (ek-) means "out" or "away", so εκλασεν can be translated as "he/she/it broke away" or "he/she/it shattered".

Grammar: εκλασεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb κλάω (kláo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εκλασεν can be translated as "he/she/it broke away" or "he/she/it shattered". It describes a sudden and forceful action of breaking or shattering something. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and connotation.
