The word "εκλεκτον" (eklekton) is a Greek adjective that means "chosen" or "elect". It comes from the verb "εκλέγομαι" (eklegomai), which means "to choose" or "to select". In grammar, it is a masculine singular nominative form of the adjective. 

In the New Testament of the Bible, the word "εκλεκτον" is often used to refer to those who are chosen by God for salvation or for a specific purpose. For example, in 1 Peter 2:9, it says "But you are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession, that you may proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness into his marvelous light." 

Overall, the word "εκλεκτον" carries a sense of being specially chosen or selected for a particular purpose or honor.
