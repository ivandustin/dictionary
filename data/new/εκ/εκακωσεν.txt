Word: εκακωσεν

Etymology: εκακωσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κακός (kakos), which means "bad" or "evil". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκακωσεν can be translated as "he/she/it made bad" or "he/she/it caused evil".

Grammar: εκακωσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: εκακωσεν can be translated as "he/she/it made bad" or "he/she/it caused evil". It implies that someone or something has caused harm or damage. The context in which the word is used would determine the specific meaning and connotation.
