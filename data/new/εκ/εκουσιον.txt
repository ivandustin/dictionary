The word "εκουσιον" (ekousion) is a Greek adjective that means "voluntary" or "willing". It is derived from the Greek word "εκούσιος" (ekousios), which also means "voluntary". In terms of grammar, it is a neuter noun in the nominative singular form. It can be used to describe actions or behaviors that are done willingly or without coercion. For example, "Οι εκουσίες πράξεις του ήταν εμφανείς" (Oi ekousies praxeis tou itan emfaneis) means "His voluntary actions were evident".
