Word: εκαλεσεν

Etymology:
The word εκαλεσεν (ekalesen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to summon". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out" or "away", so εκαλεσεν can be translated as "called out" or "summoned away".

Grammar:
εκαλεσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
εκαλεσεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark 3:13. In this passage, Jesus "called out" or "summoned away" his disciples to him, choosing twelve of them to be apostles. The verb emphasizes the authority and power of Jesus in selecting his followers.
