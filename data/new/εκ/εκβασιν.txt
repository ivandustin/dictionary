The word "εκβασιν" (ekvasin) is a Greek noun that means "exit" or "way out". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "εκβαίνω" (ekvaino), which means "to go out" or "to exit". The noun form "εκβασιν" is formed by adding the suffix "-ιν" (-in) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "Εκβασιν" is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and number (singular or plural). 

Description: The word "εκβασιν" is often used to refer to a physical exit or way out of a building, room, or enclosed space. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a way out of a difficult situation or problem. 

Example sentence: "Η εκβασιν από το κτίριο βρίσκεται στο πίσω μέρος." (The exit from the building is located at the back.)
