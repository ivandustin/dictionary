Word: εκελευον

Etymology: The word εκελευον comes from the Greek verb κελεύω (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκελευον can be translated as "I ordered" or "I commanded".

Grammar: εκελευον is the first person singular past tense of the verb κελεύω. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb κελεύω is a regular verb in Greek, and its conjugation follows the pattern of the first conjugation.
