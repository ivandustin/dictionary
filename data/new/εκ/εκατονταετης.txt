Word: εκατονταετης

Etymology: The word εκατονταετης comes from the Greek words εκατόν (ekatón) meaning "hundred" and έτος (étos) meaning "year". Therefore, εκατονταετης literally means "hundred-year-old".

Grammar: εκατονταετης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is singular and refers to a person or thing that is one hundred years old.

Description: εκατονταετης is a term used to describe someone or something that has reached the age of one hundred years. It is often used to refer to centenarians, people who have lived for a century. In Greek culture, reaching the age of one hundred is considered a significant milestone and is often celebrated with family and friends.
