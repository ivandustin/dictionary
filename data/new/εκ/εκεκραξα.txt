Word: εκεκράξα (ekékraxa)

Etymology: The word εκεκράξα comes from the Greek verb κράζω (krázo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The prefix εκ- (ek-) means "out" or "from", so εκεκράξα can be translated as "I cried out loudly" or "I shouted".

Grammar: εκεκράξα is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb κράζω (krázo). The aorist tense in Greek refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: εκεκράξα is a strong and vivid verb that conveys a sense of urgency and intensity. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe a sudden outburst of emotion or a dramatic moment. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe someone shouting or crying out loudly.
