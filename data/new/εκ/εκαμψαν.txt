Word: εκαμψαν

Etymology: The word εκαμψαν comes from the Greek verb κάμπτω (kampto), which means "to bend" or "to curve". The prefix εκα- (eka-) indicates that the action was completed in the past.

Grammar: εκαμψαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb κάμπτω (kampto). In this form, it indicates that the action of bending or curving was completed in the past by a group of people.

Description: εκαμψαν can be translated to English as "they bent" or "they curved". It describes a physical action of bending or curving something, such as a piece of metal or a road. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and significance of the action.
