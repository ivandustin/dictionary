The word "εκφοβειν" (ekphobein) is a Greek verb that means "to intimidate" or "to frighten." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of" or "from," and the verb "φοβειν" (phobein), meaning "to fear" or "to be afraid." 

Grammar: "Εκφοβειν" is an infinitive verb form in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used in the present tense, such as "Οι εκφοβιστές τον εκφοβείουν" (The intimidators are intimidating him).

Description: The word "εκφοβειν" is often used to describe behavior that is intended to make someone feel afraid or powerless. It can refer to physical intimidation, such as threatening gestures or actions, or psychological intimidation, such as verbal threats or harassment. The word can also be used in a broader sense to describe situations or environments that are intimidating or frightening.
