Word: εκελευσε

Etymology: The word εκελευσε (ekeluse) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κελευω (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order."

Grammar: εκελευσε is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is often translated as "he commanded" or "he ordered."

Description: εκελευσε is a strong and authoritative word that implies a sense of power and control. It is often used in the context of military or political commands, but can also be used in everyday situations to convey a sense of urgency or importance. The word is commonly found in ancient Greek literature, including the works of Homer and Herodotus.
