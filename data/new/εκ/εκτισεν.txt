The word "εκτισεν" is a verb in the Greek language, which means "he/she/it built" in English. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root word "κτίζω" (ktizo), which means "to build" or "to create". The prefix "εκ" (ek) means "out of" or "from", so "εκτισεν" can be translated as "built out of" or "created from".

Grammar: "Εκτισεν" is the third person singular past tense form of the verb "κτίζω" (ktizo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense ending for the third person singular is "-εν" (-en).

Description: "Εκτισεν" is a simple verb that describes the action of building or creating something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the construction of a building, the creation of a work of art, or the establishment of an organization.
