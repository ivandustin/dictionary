Word: εκομισαμην

Etymology:
The word εκομισαμην is a Greek verb that belongs to the aorist tense. It is derived from the root word κομίζω (komízo), which means "to bring" or "to carry". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the first person singular form of the aorist tense.

Grammar:
εκομισαμην is a first person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is used to indicate a completed action in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εκομίσαμεν (ekomísame) - we brought
εκομίσατε (ekomísate) - you (plural) brought
εκομίσαν (ekomísan) - they brought

Description:
The word εκομισαμην is a past tense verb that means "I brought" or "I carried" in Greek. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and in written texts.
