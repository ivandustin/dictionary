Word: εκυκλωσαν

Etymology:
The word εκυκλωσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κύκλος (kyklos), which means "circle" or "ring". The prefix ε- (e-) means "out" or "away from", so εκυκλωσαν can be translated as "they encircled" or "they surrounded".

Grammar:
εκυκλωσαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb κυκλόω (kykloo), which means "to encircle" or "to surround". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice means that the subject (they) performed the action.

Description:
εκυκλωσαν is a verb that describes an action of surrounding or encircling. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a group of people encircling an object or person, or in a figurative sense, such as a feeling of being surrounded by a particular situation or circumstance. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and can be found in various contexts, including historical accounts, poetry, and prose.
