The word "εκτενειαι" (ekteniai) is a Greek noun that is derived from the verb "εκτείνω" (ekteinō), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend." It is commonly used in the context of religious worship, specifically in the Eastern Orthodox Church, to refer to a liturgical prayer that involves the congregation standing and making a series of petitions to God. 

In terms of grammar, "εκτενειαι" is a plural noun that is typically used in the feminine gender. It is often translated into English as "litany" or "supplication."

Overall, "εκτενειαι" refers to a specific type of prayer in the Eastern Orthodox Church that involves the congregation standing and making petitions to God.
