Word: εκολληθη (ekollithi)

Etymology:
The word εκολληθη (ekollithi) is a past tense form of the Greek verb κολλώ (kollo), which means "to stick" or "to glue". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar:
εκολληθη (ekollithi) is a third-person singular past tense form of the verb κολλώ (kollo). The verb is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject received the action of the verb. The word is also conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
The word εκολληθη (ekollithi) describes an action that happened in the past, where something was stuck or glued to something else. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a sticker that was stuck on a surface, or a piece of paper that was glued to a wall.
