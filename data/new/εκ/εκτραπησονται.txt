Word: εκτραπησονται

Etymology:
The word εκτραπησονται is derived from the Greek verb εκτρέπω (ektrepo), which means "to turn aside" or "to divert." The suffix -σονται (-sontai) is a third-person plural future passive indicative ending, indicating that the action of turning aside or diverting will be done to them.

Grammar:
εκτραπησονται is a third-person plural future passive indicative form of the Greek verb εκτρέπω (ektrepo). It is conjugated in the future tense, indicating that the action of turning aside or diverting will happen at some point in the future. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence will receive the action of the verb, rather than performing the action themselves.

Description:
εκτραπησονται can be translated to English as "they will be turned aside" or "they will be diverted." The word is often used in a figurative sense, to describe someone being led astray or distracted from their intended path or purpose. It can also be used in a more literal sense, to describe something being physically diverted or redirected.
