Word: εκτενεις

Etymology:
The word εκτενεις comes from the Greek verb εκτείνω (ekteíno), which means "to stretch out" or "to extend". The suffix -εις (-eis) is added to the verb stem to form the adjective εκτενεις (ektenis), which means "extended" or "prolonged".

Grammar:
εκτενεις is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a feminine singular noun, it would be εκτενής (ektenis).

Description:
The adjective εκτενεις is used to describe something that is extended or prolonged. It can refer to physical objects or abstract concepts. For example, one might use the word to describe a long speech, an extensive research project, or a prolonged illness. In general, εκτενεις implies that something is more extensive or prolonged than usual or expected.
