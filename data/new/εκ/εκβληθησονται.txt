Word: εκβληθησονται

Etymology:
The word εκβληθησονται is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: εκ (ek) meaning "out" and βαλλω (ballo) meaning "to throw". Therefore, the literal meaning of εκβληθησονται is "to be thrown out".

Grammar:
εκβληθησονται is a third-person plural future passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σονται to the passive voice stem εκβληθη-. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εκβληθησονται (ekvlithisontai) - they will be thrown out

Description:
εκβληθησονται is a strong and powerful verb that conveys the idea of forcefully ejecting something or someone out of a place or situation. It is often used in a negative context, such as in the Bible where it is used to describe the fate of sinners who will be cast out of the kingdom of God. In modern Greek, the verb is also used in a figurative sense to describe the rejection or expulsion of someone from a group or society.
