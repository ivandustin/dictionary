Word: εκενωσεν

Etymology:
The word εκενωσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κενός (kenos), which means "empty" or "void". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκενωσεν can be translated as "emptied out" or "made void".

Grammar:
εκενωσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb (he/she/it) performed the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
εκενωσεν is a verb that means "emptied out" or "made void". It can be used in a literal sense, such as emptying a container, or in a figurative sense, such as making something meaningless or insignificant. In the New Testament, the word is used in reference to Christ "emptying himself" of his divine nature to become human (Philippians 2:7).
