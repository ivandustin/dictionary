Word: εκερδησας

Etymology:
The word εκερδησας is derived from the Greek verb κερδίζω (kerdizo), which means "to gain, to profit". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκερδησας can be translated as "you gained" or "you profited".

Grammar:
εκερδησας is the second person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb κερδίζω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
εκερδησας is a word that describes a past action of gaining or profiting. It can be used in various contexts, such as in business, finance, or personal achievements. For example, if someone invested in the stock market and made a profit, they could say "εκερδησας" to express their success. Similarly, if someone won a competition or accomplished a goal, they could use this word to describe their achievement.
