The word "εκερδησα" is a verb in the Greek language, which means "I gained" or "I earned". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "κέρδος" (kerdos), which means "gain" or "profit". The prefix "εκ" (ek) means "out of" or "from", so "εκερδησα" can be translated as "I gained out of" or "I earned from".

Grammar: "Εκερδησα" is the first person singular past tense of the verb "κερδίζω" (kerdizo), which means "to gain" or "to earn". It is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: The word "εκερδησα" is commonly used in everyday Greek language to express the idea of gaining or earning something, such as money, a prize, or a reward. It can also be used metaphorically to describe gaining knowledge, experience, or wisdom.
