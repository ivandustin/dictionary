Word: εκλαλησαι

Etymology:
The word εκλαλησαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The prefix εκ (ek) means "out" or "from", so εκλαλησαι can be translated as "to speak out" or "to proclaim".

Grammar:
εκλαλησαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards one person to perform an action in the past. The verb is conjugated from the root word λαλέω (laleo) and the prefix εκ (ek).

Description:
εκλαλησαι is a strong and powerful word that implies a sense of urgency and importance. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe the act of proclaiming or speaking out a message or truth. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of speaking out or making a public statement.
