Word: εκδυσασθαι

Etymology:
The word εκδυσασθαι comes from the Greek verb "εκδύω" (ekdyo), which means "to undress" or "to take off". The suffix "-σασθαι" (-sasthai) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar:
εκδυσασθαι is a reflexive verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject.

Description:
The word εκδυσασθαι refers to the act of undressing oneself or taking off one's clothes. It can also be used figuratively to mean "to strip oneself of something", such as a title or a position. The word is commonly used in the context of changing clothes or getting undressed before going to bed or taking a shower.
