The word "εκερασεν" (ekerasen) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "κεράννυμι" (kerannymi), which means "to mix, mingle, or blend together." 

Etymology: The word "κεράννυμι" (kerannymi) comes from the ancient Greek word "κέρας" (keras), which means "horn." The verb originally referred to the act of mixing wine with water in a horn-shaped vessel.

Grammar: "Εκερασεν" (ekerasen) is a past tense form of the verb, specifically the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: "Εκερασεν" (ekerasen) can be translated as "he/she/it mixed" or "he/she/it blended." It is often used in the context of mixing ingredients together, such as in cooking or in the preparation of drinks.
