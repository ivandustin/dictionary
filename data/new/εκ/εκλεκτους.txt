Word: εκλεκτούς

Etymology: The word εκλεκτούς (eklektous) comes from the Greek verb εκλέγομαι (eklegomai), which means "to choose" or "to select". The word is derived from the prefix εκ- (ek-), meaning "out of", and the verb λέγω (lego), meaning "to say" or "to speak". Therefore, εκλεκτούς can be translated as "chosen ones" or "selected ones".

Grammar: εκλεκτούς is a masculine plural adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this case, it is modifying a masculine plural noun.

Description: εκλεκτούς is often used in the New Testament to describe the chosen or elect people of God. It is used to refer to those who have been selected by God for salvation. The word implies a sense of specialness or uniqueness, as those who are chosen are set apart from others.
