Word: εκνηψατε

Etymology: 
The word εκνηψατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "νήψις" (nepsis), which means "vigilance" or "alertness". The prefix "εκ" (ek) means "out of" or "from", so εκνηψατε can be translated as "wake up" or "be alert".

Grammar:
εκνηψατε is the second person plural aorist active imperative form of the verb "νηφάω" (nephao), which means "to be sober" or "to be watchful". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give a command or instruction.

Description:
εκνηψατε is a command to a group of people to wake up or be alert. It is often used in a spiritual or metaphorical sense, urging people to be vigilant and aware of their surroundings, both physically and spiritually. It can also be used in a literal sense, such as waking someone up from sleep.
