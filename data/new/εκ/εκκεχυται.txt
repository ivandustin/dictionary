Word: εκκεχυται

Etymology: The word εκκεχυται comes from the Greek verb εκχέω (ekcheo), which means "to pour out" or "to shed". The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates that it is in the third person singular present tense, passive voice.

Grammar: εκκεχυται is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the third person singular present tense, passive voice. The verb stem is εκχυτ-, and the suffix -αι (-ai) indicates that it is in the present tense.

Description: The word εκκεχυται means "is poured out" or "is shed". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe emotions or ideas that are expressed or released. For example, one might say "ο λυπητερός εκκεχυται στα δάκρυα" (the sorrowful one pours out in tears) to describe someone who is crying due to sadness. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe liquids that are poured out, such as "το νερό εκκεχύθη στο πάτωμα" (the water was spilled on the floor).
