Word: εκτησατο

Etymology: εκτησατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "κτάομαι" (ktáomai), which means "to acquire" or "to gain". The prefix "εκ" (ek) in εκτησατο indicates that the action of acquiring has been completed or achieved.

Grammar: εκτησατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates that the action of acquiring took place at a specific point in time in the past. The middle voice indicates that the subject of the verb is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description: εκτησατο means "he/she/it acquired" or "he/she/it gained" in English. It is often used in the context of acquiring or gaining possessions, property, or wealth. The middle voice indicates that the subject acquired something for themselves, rather than receiving it from someone else.
