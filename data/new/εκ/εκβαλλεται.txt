Word: εκβαλλεται

Etymology:
The word εκβαλλεται comes from the Greek verb εκβάλλω (ekbállō), which means "to throw out" or "to expel". It is derived from the prefix εκ- (ek-), meaning "out of", and the verb βάλλω (bállō), meaning "to throw".

Grammar:
εκβαλλεται is a third-person singular present indicative form of the Greek verb εκβάλλω (ekbállō). It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now.

Description:
The word εκβαλλεται describes the action of throwing or expelling something out of a place or situation. It can be used in a literal sense, such as throwing out garbage, or in a figurative sense, such as expelling negative thoughts or emotions. The word can also be used to describe the act of ejecting someone from a place or position, such as being expelled from school or being fired from a job.
