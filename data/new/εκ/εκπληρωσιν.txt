Word: εκπληρωσιν

Etymology: The word εκπληρωσιν comes from the Greek verb εκπληροω (ekplēroō), which means "to fulfill" or "to complete". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a noun ending that indicates the action or result of the verb.

Grammar: εκπληρωσιν is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: εκπληρωσιν refers to the act of fulfilling or completing something. It can also mean accomplishment or achievement. The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as fulfilling a prophecy or completing a divine mission. In modern Greek, it can also refer to the completion of a task or project.
