Word: εκβαλλεις

Etymology: 
The word εκβαλλεις comes from the Greek roots εκ (ek) meaning "out" and βαλλω (ballo) meaning "to throw". Therefore, the literal meaning of εκβαλλεις is "you throw out".

εκβαλλεις is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation of verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs ending in -ω. The verb stem is βαλλ-, and the prefix εκ- indicates the direction of the action.

Description:
εκβαλλεις is a strong and forceful verb that implies a deliberate and intentional action of throwing something out. It can be used in a literal sense, such as throwing out garbage or throwing someone out of a place, but it can also be used figuratively, such as throwing out negative thoughts or emotions. The verb is often used in the imperative form, as a command or instruction to someone to throw something out.
