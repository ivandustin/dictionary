Word: εκβαλλουσιν

Etymology:
The word εκβαλλουσιν is derived from the Greek verb εκβάλλω (ekballō), which means "to throw out" or "to expel". It is formed by combining the prefix εκ- (ek-), meaning "out of", and the verb βάλλω (ballō), meaning "to throw".

Grammar:
εκβαλλουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. It is conjugated from the base verb εκβάλλω (ekballō) and the present tense suffix -ουσιν (-ousin). The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
The word εκβαλλουσιν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark 1:34. It is translated to English as "they cast out" or "they expelled". In this context, it refers to Jesus and his disciples casting out demons from people who were possessed. The word conveys a sense of forceful removal or expulsion.
