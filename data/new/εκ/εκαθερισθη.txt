The word "εκαθερισθη" (ekatheristhe) is a Greek verb that means "to be cleansed" or "to be purified." It is derived from the root word "καθαρίζω" (katharizo), which means "to cleanse" or "to purify." 

In terms of grammar, "εκαθερισθη" is in the passive voice and past tense. It is the third person singular form of the verb, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a singular entity that has been cleansed or purified. 

Overall, "εκαθερισθη" is a word that connotes a sense of purification or cleansing, often in a spiritual or religious context.
