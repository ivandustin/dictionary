Word: εκρατησεν

Etymology: εκρατησεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb κρατέω (krateo), which means "to hold, to grasp, to seize, to possess, to control". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εκρατησεν means "he/she/it held, grasped, seized, possessed, controlled".

Grammar: εκρατησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb κρατέω (krateo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: εκρατησεν is a word that describes an action of holding, grasping, seizing, possessing, or controlling something or someone. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning. It is a past tense form, so it refers to an action that has already happened.
