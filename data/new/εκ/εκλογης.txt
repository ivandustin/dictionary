Word: εκλογης

Etymology: The word εκλογης comes from the Greek verb εκλέγομαι (eklégomai), which means "to choose" or "to select". The suffix -ης (-is) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form nouns from verbs.

Grammar: εκλογης is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated into English as "of" or "'s".

Description: εκλογης refers to the act of choosing or selecting. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as political elections, job interviews, or even choosing a flavor of ice cream. The word emphasizes the deliberate and intentional nature of the choice, rather than a random or haphazard selection.
