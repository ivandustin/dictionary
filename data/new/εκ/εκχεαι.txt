The word "εκχεαι" (ekcheai) is a Greek verb that means "to pour out" or "to spill." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "εκ-" (ek-), meaning "out of," and the verb "χεω" (cheo), meaning "to pour."

Grammar: "εκχεαι" is a second-person singular present active imperative verb. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and persons, such as "εκχεω" (ekcheo) in the present indicative or "εξεχυνν" (eksechynno) in the aorist indicative.

Description: The word is often used in a literal sense, such as pouring out a liquid from a container. It can also be used metaphorically, such as pouring out one's emotions or thoughts.
