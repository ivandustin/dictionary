Word: ραβδου

Etymology:
The word ραβδου (ravdou) is derived from the Greek word "ράβδος" (rávdos), which means "rod" or "staff". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *rebh- which means "to support" or "to straighten".

Grammar:
In Greek, ραβδου is a noun in the genitive case. It is often used in phrases such as "της ράβδου" (tis rávdos) which means "of the rod" or "of the staff".

Description:
ραβδου refers to a rod or staff, typically made of wood, that is used for support or as a symbol of authority. In ancient times, it was commonly used by shepherds to guide their flocks and by travelers to help them navigate difficult terrain. It was also used as a weapon in combat. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a walking stick or cane.
