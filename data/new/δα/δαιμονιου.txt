Word: δαιμονιου

Etymology: δαιμόνιον (daimónion) is a Greek word that means "divine power" or "divine spirit". It is derived from the word δαίμων (daímōn), which means "spirit" or "god". The suffix -ιου (-iou) is a genitive ending that indicates possession or relationship.

Grammar: δαιμονιου is a genitive singular form of the noun δαιμόνιον (daimónion). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. In this case, δαιμονιου indicates that something belongs to or is related to a divine power or spirit.

Description: δαιμονιου can be translated as "of a divine power" or "of a divine spirit". It is often used in Greek mythology to refer to supernatural beings or spirits that are neither entirely good nor entirely evil. In some contexts, it can also refer to a person's inner spirit or conscience.
