Word: δανιζουσιν

Etymology: δανιζουσιν is a present tense verb in Greek, derived from the root word δανείζω (daneizo), which means "to lend" or "to borrow". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates that the subject of the verb is third person plural.

Grammar: δανιζουσιν is a present tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated from the root word δανείζω (daneizo) in the third person plural. The verb is used to describe the action of lending or borrowing.

Description: δανιζουσιν is a Greek verb that describes the act of lending or borrowing. It is commonly used in financial contexts, such as loans or debts. The present tense form indicates that the action is currently taking place.
