Word: δαιμονισθεις

Etymology: The word δαιμονισθεις comes from the Greek word δαιμονίζομαι (daimonizomai), which means "to be possessed by a demon". The word is derived from the noun δαίμων (daimon), which means "demon" or "spirit".

Grammar: δαιμονισθεις is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It is the aorist passive participle of the verb δαιμονίζομαι (daimonizomai).

Description: δαιμονισθεις describes someone who has been possessed by a demon or evil spirit. In Greek mythology, demons were believed to be supernatural beings that could possess humans and cause them to act in strange or harmful ways. The word is often used in a religious context to describe someone who is believed to be under the influence of evil forces.
