Word: δανισασθαι

Etymology:
The word δανισασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δανείζω (daneizo), which means "to lend" or "to borrow". The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a middle voice ending that indicates the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Grammar:
δανισασθαι is a second aorist middle infinitive verb in Ancient Greek. The second aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The infinitive form means that the verb is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
δανισασθαι means "to borrow" in Ancient Greek. It is a verb that describes the action of taking something from someone else with the intention of returning it later. The middle voice ending indicates that the subject is borrowing something for themselves. This verb is commonly used in financial contexts, such as borrowing money or goods.
