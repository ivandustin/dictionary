Word: δανιηλ

Etymology:
The name δανιηλ (Daniel) is of Hebrew origin and means "God is my judge". It is derived from the Hebrew name דָּנִיֵּאל (Daniyyel), which is composed of two elements: "dan" meaning "judge" and "el" meaning "God".

Grammar:
In Greek, δανιηλ is spelled with the letters δανιήλ. It is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In the Greek language, nouns have gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and cases (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative) that indicate their function in a sentence.

Description:
Daniel is a biblical name that appears in the Old Testament. He was a prophet and advisor to the Babylonian king Nebuchadnezzar. The Book of Daniel tells the story of his life and prophecies. In Christianity, Daniel is considered a saint and his feast day is celebrated on July 21st. The name Daniel has been popular throughout history and is still commonly used today.
