Word: δακρυων

Etymology:
The word δακρυων (dakruon) is derived from the Greek word δάκρυ (dakry), which means "tear". The suffix -ων (-on) is a plural ending, so δακρυων means "tears" or "teardrops".

Grammar:
δακρυων is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is the genitive plural form of δάκρυ, meaning "of tears".

Description:
δακρυων is a word that refers to tears, the salty liquid that is secreted from the eyes as a response to emotions such as sadness, joy, or pain. Tears are an important part of human expression and can convey a wide range of emotions. In Greek mythology, the god of tears was named Lachrymos, and tears were often associated with mourning and grief. Today, tears are still an important part of human communication and can be a powerful way to express emotions and connect with others.
