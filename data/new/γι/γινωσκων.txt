Word: γινωσκων

Etymology:
The word γινωσκων (ginōskōn) is the present participle of the Greek verb γινώσκω (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand". The verb is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃- which means "to know".

Grammar:
γινωσκων is a present participle in the Greek language. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. In this form, it is masculine, singular, and nominative.

Description:
The word γινωσκων is used to describe someone who is currently knowing or understanding something. It can be translated as "knowing" or "understanding" in English. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the knowledge or understanding of God or spiritual matters.
