Word: γινομενοις

Etymology: γινομενοις is the present participle of the Greek verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁- which means "to give birth, to become".

Grammar: γινομενοις is a masculine, plural, dative case form of the present participle of the verb γίνομαι. In Greek, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb, or to show the recipient of an action.

Description: γινομενοις can be translated as "being made" or "being done". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe ongoing actions or events.
