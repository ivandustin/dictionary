Word: γινωσκομεν

Etymology:
γινωσκομεν (ginōskomen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand". The verb is in the present tense and the first person plural form, meaning "we know" or "we understand".

Grammar:
γινωσκομεν (ginōskomen) is a regular verb in Greek and follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -ω (-ō). In the present tense, the verb conjugates as follows:

- εγώ γνωρίζω (egō gnōrizō) - I know
- εσύ γνωρίζεις (esý gnōrízeis) - you know
- αυτός/αυτή/αυτό γνωρίζει (afós/afí/aftó gnōrízei) - he/she/it knows
- εμείς γνωρίζουμε (emeís gnōrízoume) - we know
- εσείς γνωρίζετε (eseís gnōrízete) - you know
- αυτοί/αυτές/αυτά γνωρίζουν (aftoí/afé/afá gnōrízoun) - they know

Description:
γινωσκομεν (ginōskomen) is a present tense verb that indicates knowledge or understanding. It is often used in philosophical or theological contexts to express a deeper level of comprehension or insight. In everyday conversation, it can be used to express familiarity with a person, place, or thing.
