Word: γινεσθε

Etymology:
The word γινεσθε (ginesthe) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". It is a present tense verb in the second person plural form, which means "you become" or "you happen".

Grammar:
γινεσθε (ginesthe) is a present tense verb in the second person plural form. It is a passive verb, which means that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action. In this case, the subject is "you" and the action is "becoming" or "happening".

Description:
The word γινεσθε (ginesthe) is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the teachings of Jesus Christ. It is used to convey the idea of transformation or change, both in a spiritual and a physical sense.
