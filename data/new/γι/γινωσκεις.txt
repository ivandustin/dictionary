Word: γινωσκεις

Etymology:
The word γινωσκεις (ginoskeis) is derived from the Greek verb γινώσκω (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand". The verb is formed from the root word γιγνώσκω (gignosko), which means "to come to know" or "to recognize".

Grammar:
γινωσκεις is a second person singular present indicative active form of the verb γινώσκω (ginosko). It is used to address one person and indicates that the action of knowing or understanding is currently happening in the present.

Description:
γινωσκεις is a Greek word that means "you know" or "you understand". It is often used in conversation to confirm that the listener has understood what has been said or to express agreement with a statement. The word can also be used in a more philosophical sense to refer to the process of gaining knowledge or understanding.
