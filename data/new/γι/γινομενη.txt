Word: γινομενη

Etymology: γινομενη is the present participle of the Greek verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". The word has its roots in the ancient Greek language and has been used in various forms throughout history.

Grammar: γινομενη is a feminine noun in the nominative case, singular form. As a present participle, it is used to describe an ongoing action or state of being. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the stem of the verb.

Description: γινομενη can be translated to English as "being done" or "being made". It is often used in Greek to describe an ongoing process or action, such as in the phrase "η δουλεια γινομενη" (i douleia ginomeni), which means "the work being done". The word can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as in the phrase "η ιστορια γινομενη" (i istoria ginomeni), which means "the history being made".
