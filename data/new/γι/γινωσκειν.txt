Word: γινωσκειν

Etymology:
The word γινωσκειν (ginōskein) is derived from the Greek root word γινώσκω (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand". This root word is also the source of the English words "gnosis" and "agnostic".

Grammar:
γινωσκειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb γινώσκω (ginōskō).

Description:
The word γινωσκειν is used to express the act of knowing or understanding something. It can also be used to describe the process of becoming acquainted with something or someone. In Greek philosophy, the term γνῶσις (gnōsis) refers to knowledge or understanding that is gained through direct experience or intuition, rather than through reason or logic. The verb γινώσκω and its derivatives, including γινωσκειν, are often used in this context to describe the pursuit of this kind of knowledge.
