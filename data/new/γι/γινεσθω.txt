Word: γινεσθω

Etymology:
The word γινεσθω (ginestho) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίγνομαι (gignomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." It is a present passive imperative form of the verb, which means that it is a command to someone to "let it be done" or "let it happen."

Grammar:
In Greek, verbs are inflected to show tense, mood, voice, and person. The verb γινεσθω is in the present tense, passive voice, and imperative mood. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The word γινεσθω is often translated as "let it be done" or "let it happen." It is a command that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, such as in the Lord's Prayer, where it is translated as "Thy will be done." It can also be used more generally to express a desire for something to happen or to give permission for something to occur.
