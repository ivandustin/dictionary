Word: γαρ

Etymology:
The word γαρ (gar) is a conjunction in the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵʰer- meaning "to grasp, enclose". The word has been used in Greek since ancient times and is still in use today.

Grammar:
In Greek, γαρ is a conjunction that is used to introduce a reason or explanation for something. It is often translated as "for" or "because". It is used to connect two clauses, with the first clause providing the reason or explanation for the second clause.

Description:
The word γαρ is a common conjunction in Greek and is used frequently in both written and spoken language. It is an important tool for expressing causality and providing explanations. Its usage is similar to the English conjunction "for" or "because".
