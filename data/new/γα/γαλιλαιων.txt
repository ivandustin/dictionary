Word: γαλιλαιων

Etymology: The word γαλιλαιων comes from the Greek word Γαλιλαία (Galilaia), which means "region of Galilee". The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a plural ending, so γαλιλαιων means "of the Galileans".

Grammar: γαλιλαιων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. So, γαλιλαιων can be translated as "of the Galileans".

Description: γαλιλαιων is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the people who lived in the region of Galilee. It is often used to distinguish them from the people of Judea or Jerusalem. The Galileans were known for their distinctive accent and were sometimes looked down upon by the Judeans. Jesus and many of his disciples were Galileans.
