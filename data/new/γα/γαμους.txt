Word: γαμους

Etymology: γαμους is a Greek word that comes from the verb γαμέω (gaméō), which means "to marry". 

Grammar: γαμους is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In this case, it is the object of the verb "to celebrate" or "to have" (as in "to have a wedding").

Description: γαμους refers to a wedding or marriage ceremony. It can also be used to describe the celebration or festivities that accompany a wedding. In Greek culture, weddings are often elaborate and involve many traditions and customs, such as the exchange of wedding crowns, the throwing of rice or flowers, and the breaking of plates.
