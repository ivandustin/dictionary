Word: γαλιλαῖοι (Galilaioi)

Etymology: The word γαλιλαῖοι (Galilaioi) is derived from the Greek word γαλιλαία (Galilaia), which means "region of Galilee." The suffix -οι (-oi) is a common Greek ending used to denote a group of people from a particular region.

Grammar: γαλιλαῖοι (Galilaioi) is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: γαλιλαῖοι (Galilaioi) refers to the people who lived in the region of Galilee in ancient Israel. Galilee was a region in the northern part of Israel, and it was known for its fertile land and its proximity to the Sea of Galilee.
