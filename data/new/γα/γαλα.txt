Word: γαλα

Etymology:
The word γαλα (gala) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵʰelh₃- which means "to shine, gleam, or be white". This root also gave rise to the Latin word "gala" which means "milk".

Grammar:
In Greek, γαλα is a neuter noun. Its singular form is γάλα (gala) and its plural form is γάλατα (galata). It is declined like a second-declension noun.

Description:
γαλα (gala) means "milk" in Greek. It is a staple food in many cultures and is used in a variety of dishes. Milk is also an important source of calcium and other nutrients. In Greek mythology, the goddess Hera was often depicted as a cow, and milk was seen as a symbol of her power and fertility.
