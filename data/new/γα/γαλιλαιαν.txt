Word: γαλιλαιαν

Etymology: The word γαλιλαιαν comes from the Greek word Γαλιλαία (Galilaia), which means "region of Galilee". Galilee was a region in northern Israel during the time of Jesus.

Grammar: γαλιλαιαν is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. It is the accusative form of the word Γαλιλαία (Galilaia).

Description: γαλιλαιαν is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the region of Galilee. It is often used in reference to Jesus and his disciples, who were from Galilee. The region of Galilee was known for its agriculture and fishing, and was a center of Jewish culture and learning during the time of Jesus.
