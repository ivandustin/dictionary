Word: γαμηθηναι

Etymology: The word γαμηθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γαμέω (gaméō), which means "to marry." The suffix -θηναι (-thēnai) is a passive aorist form, indicating that the action of marrying has been done to the subject.

Grammar: γαμηθηναι is a passive aorist form of the verb γαμέω (gaméō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is the recipient of the action, rather than the doer.

Description: γαμηθηναι means "to be married" or "to have been married" in Greek. It is a passive form, indicating that the subject has had the action of marrying done to them. This verb is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday conversation to describe the state of being married.
