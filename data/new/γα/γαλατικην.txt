The word "γαλατικην" (galatiken) is an adjective in Greek language. It is derived from the noun "Γαλατία" (Galatia), which was a region in central Anatolia (modern-day Turkey) during ancient times. The adjective "γαλατικην" means "Galatian" or "pertaining to Galatia". 

In terms of grammar, "γαλατικην" is in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. It is used to modify a feminine noun in the accusative case.

Example sentence: Η εκκλησία αναγνώρισε την γαλατικήν κοινότητα ως αυτόνομη. (I ekklisia anagnorise tin galatiken koinotita os autonomi.) - The church recognized the Galatian community as autonomous.
