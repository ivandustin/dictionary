The word "γαμειν" (gámein) is a Greek verb that means "to marry" or "to wed". It comes from the ancient Greek word "γάμος" (gámos), which also means "marriage". In terms of grammar, "γαμειν" is a present active infinitive verb, meaning it is the basic form of the verb and indicates ongoing action in the present tense. The verb can be conjugated to match the subject and tense of the sentence. Overall, "γαμειν" is a common and important word in Greek culture, as marriage has traditionally been a significant aspect of Greek society.
