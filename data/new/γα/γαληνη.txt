Word: γαληνη

Etymology: 
The word γαληνη (galene) comes from the Ancient Greek γαλήνη (galḗnē), which means "calmness" or "tranquility". It is derived from the verb γαληνόω (galēnóō), which means "to calm" or "to soothe".

Grammar:
In Greek, γαληνη is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular and nominative in form. 

Description:
γαληνη is a Greek word that refers to a state of calmness or tranquility. It can be used to describe a peaceful situation or a peaceful state of mind. The word is often used in literature and poetry to evoke a sense of serenity or stillness. It is also used in scientific contexts to describe a state of equilibrium or balance.
