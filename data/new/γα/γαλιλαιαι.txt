Word: γαλιλαιαι

Etymology: The word γαλιλαιαι is a plural form of the Greek word γαλιλαία (galilaia), which means "of Galilee". Galilee is a region in northern Israel.

Grammar: γαλιλαιαι is a feminine plural noun in Greek. It is declined in the first declension and has the nominative singular form γαλιλαία.

Description: γαλιλαιαι is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to a group of women who followed Jesus and witnessed his crucifixion and burial. The women are named in Mark 15:40 and 41, and include Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James the Less and Joses, and Salome. The word is also used in other ancient Greek texts to refer to the region of Galilee or its inhabitants.
