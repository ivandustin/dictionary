Word: γαλιλαῖος (galilaîos)

Etymology: The word γαλιλαῖος comes from the Greek word Γαλιλαία (Galilaía), which means "Galilee," a region in northern Israel. Therefore, γαλιλαῖος means "of Galilee" or "from Galilee."

Grammar: γαλιλαῖος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: γαλιλαῖος is used in the New Testament to describe Jesus and his disciples as being from Galilee. It is also used to describe the accent of Peter, who was recognized as a Galilean by his speech. The term can also be used more generally to describe anything or anyone from Galilee.
