Word: γωνιας

Etymology:
The word γωνιας (gonias) comes from the Greek word γωνία (gonia), which means "angle" or "corner". It is derived from the Greek word γωνίαζειν (goniazein), which means "to form an angle".

Grammar:
γωνιας is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is masculine. The genitive form of the word is γωνίας (gonias).

Description:
γωνιας refers to an angle or corner, typically in a geometric sense. It can also refer to a corner or angle in a building or structure. The word is commonly used in mathematics and geometry to describe the measurement of angles. In everyday language, it can be used to describe a sharp turn or a corner in a road.
