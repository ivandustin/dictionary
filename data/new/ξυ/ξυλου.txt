Ξυλου (xyloú) is a Greek word that means "of wood" or "wooden". It is derived from the Greek word ξύλο (xýlo), which means "wood". In terms of grammar, ξυλου is a genitive form of ξύλο. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. 

In Greek language, ξυλου is often used in phrases such as "ξυλουργός" (xyloúrgos), which means "woodworker" or "carpenter", and "ξυλόσομπα" (xylósompa), which means "wood-burning stove". 

Overall, ξυλου is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday Greek language to describe anything made of wood or related to wood.
