Word: ιππος

Etymology:
The word ιππος (hippos) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁éḱwos, which means "horse". This root is also the source of the English word "equine".

Grammar:
In Greek, ιππος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is ιππος, and its genitive singular form is ιππου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
Ιππος is the Greek word for "horse". Horses have played an important role in Greek culture and mythology, and were used for transportation, warfare, and sport. The ancient Greeks believed that horses were sacred animals, and they were often depicted in art and literature. Today, horses are still an important part of Greek culture, and equestrian sports are popular throughout the country.
