Word: ιππων

Etymology:
The word ιππων (ippon) is derived from the Greek word ἵππος (hippos), which means "horse". The suffix -ων (-on) is a masculine noun ending in Greek.

Grammar:
Ιππων (ippon) is a masculine noun in the nominative case in Greek. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is ίπποι (ippoi).

Description:
Ιππων (ippon) refers to a horse in Greek. It is a common word used in ancient Greek literature, particularly in epic poems such as the Iliad and the Odyssey. The word is also used in modern Greek to refer to horses.
