Etymology: The word "χρηστότητα" (chrestotitos) comes from the Greek root "χρηστός" (chrestos), meaning "good" or "useful," and the suffix "-τητα" (-tita), which denotes a quality or state. 

Grammar: "Χρηστότητα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Χρηστότητα" refers to the quality or state of being kind, good, or helpful. It is often used to describe someone's character or behavior, indicating their benevolence or willingness to assist others.
