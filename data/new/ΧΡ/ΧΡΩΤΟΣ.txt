Etymology: The word "χρωτός" (chrōtós) comes from the Greek word "χρῶμα" (chrōma), meaning "color." It is derived from the verb "χρῶ" (chrō), which means "to use" or "to employ."

Grammar: In Greek, "χρωτός" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. For example, the masculine form is "χρωτός" (chrōtós), the feminine form is "χρωτή" (chrōtē), and the neuter form is "χρωτό" (chrōtó).

Description: "Χρωτός" is an adjective that describes something as having color or being colorful. It can be used to describe objects, animals, or people. For example, if you say "μια χρωτή παλέτα" (mia chrōtē paleta), it means "a colorful palette." Similarly, if you say "ένα χρωτό αυτοκίνητο" (ena chrōtó autokinēto), it means "a colorful car."
