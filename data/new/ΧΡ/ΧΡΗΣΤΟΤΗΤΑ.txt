Etymology: The word "χρηστότητα" (chrestotita) comes from the Greek root "χρηστός" (chrestos), meaning "good" or "useful," and the suffix "-τητα" (-tita), which denotes a quality or state. 

Grammar: "Χρηστότητα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Χρηστότητα" refers to the quality or state of being kind, good, or helpful. It is often used to describe someone's character or behavior, indicating their benevolence, generosity, or willingness to assist others.
