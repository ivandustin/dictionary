Etymology: The word "χρεοφιλετών" comes from the Greek roots "χρέος" (chréos), meaning "debt," and "φιλέω" (philéo), meaning "to love." 

Grammar: "Χρεοφιλετών" is a noun in the plural form. It is a compound word formed by combining the noun "χρέος" (debt) and the verb "φιλέω" (to love) with the suffix "-τών" (-ton), which indicates a group or collection of people.

Description: "Χρεοφιλετών" refers to a group of people who love debt or have a fondness for being in debt. It can be used to describe individuals or entities that consistently borrow money or accumulate debt.
