Etymology: The word "χριστός" (chri-stos) comes from the Greek word meaning "anointed one" or "chosen one." It is derived from the verb "χρίω" (chri-o), which means "to anoint" or "to smear with oil." In Christian theology, it refers to Jesus Christ as the Messiah and Savior.

Grammar: In Greek, "χριστός" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive ("χριστοῦ"), dative ("χριστῷ"), and accusative ("χριστόν").

Description: "Χριστός" is a significant term in Christianity, referring to Jesus Christ as the anointed one, the Son of God, and the Savior of humanity. It is a central concept in Christian theology and plays a crucial role in the belief system of Christians worldwide.
