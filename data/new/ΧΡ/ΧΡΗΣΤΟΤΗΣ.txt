Etymology: The word "χρηστότης" (chrestotes) comes from the Greek root "χρηστός" (chrestos), meaning "useful" or "good," combined with the suffix "-της" (-tes), which denotes the quality or state of being. 

Grammar: "Χρηστότης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Χρηστότης" refers to the quality or characteristic of being kind, good-natured, or benevolent. It is often used to describe someone who is helpful, considerate, and well-intentioned towards others.
