Etymology: The word "χρονος" (chronos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ker- meaning "to cut" or "to separate." In Greek, "χρονος" originally referred to the concept of time as a measure or division.

Grammar: In Greek, "χρονος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form (χρονος) and a plural form (χρονοι). It can function as both the subject and the object of a sentence.

Description: "Χρονος" is the Greek word for "time." It refers to the continuous and irreversible progression of events from the past through the present to the future. In Greek mythology, "Χρονος" was also personified as the god of time.
