Etymology: The word "χρηματισμός" comes from the Greek verb "χρηματίζω" (chrēmatízō), which means "to transact business" or "to deal with money." It is derived from the noun "χρῆμα" (chrêma), meaning "money" or "property."

Grammar: In Greek, "χρηματισμός" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "χρηματισμοί" (chrēmatismoí).

Description: "Χρηματισμός" refers to the act or process of dealing with money, conducting financial transactions, or managing financial affairs. It can also refer to the study or theory of economics or finance.
