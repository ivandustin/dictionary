Etymology: The word "χρυσοδακτυλιος" (chrysodaktylios) comes from the Greek words "χρυσός" (chrysos) meaning "gold" and "δάκτυλος" (daktylos) meaning "finger". It literally translates to "gold-fingered".

Grammar: "Χρυσοδακτυλιος" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "χρυσοδακτυλίου" (chrysodaktyliou).

Description: "Χρυσοδακτυλιος" is a term used in Greek mythology to describe someone who has the ability to turn everything they touch into gold. This term is often associated with the mythical character King Midas, who was cursed with this power. The story of King Midas and his golden touch is a popular tale in Greek mythology.
