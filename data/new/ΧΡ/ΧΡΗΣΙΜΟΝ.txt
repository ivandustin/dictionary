Etymology: The word "χρησιμον" (chresimon) comes from the Greek word "χρησιμος" (chresimos), meaning "useful" or "helpful".

Grammar: In Greek, "χρησιμον" (chresimon) is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Description: "Χρησιμον" (chresimon) refers to something that is useful, beneficial, or advantageous. It can be used to describe objects, actions, or situations that serve a purpose or have practical value.
