Etymology: The word "χρονιζοντος" (chronizontos) comes from the Greek verb "χρονίζω" (chronizo), which means "to delay" or "to procrastinate." The suffix "-τος" (-tos) is added to the verb stem to form the noun "χρονιζοντος" (chronizontos), which means "one who delays" or "one who procrastinates."

Grammar: In Greek, "χρονιζοντος" (chronizontos) is a noun in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the present participle form of the verb "χρονίζω" (chronizo).

Description: "Χρονιζοντος" (chronizontos) refers to someone who delays or procrastinates. It can be used to describe a person who habitually puts off tasks or actions, often resulting in a delay or inefficiency.
