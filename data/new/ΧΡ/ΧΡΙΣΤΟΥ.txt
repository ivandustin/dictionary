Etymology: The word "χριστού" (chri-stou) comes from the Greek word Χριστός (Christos), which means "anointed one" or "messiah." It is derived from the verb χρίω (chrio), meaning "to anoint."

Grammar: In Greek, "χριστού" is the genitive form of the noun Χριστός (Christos). The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, it is used to indicate the possessive form of "Christ," as in "of Christ" or "belonging to Christ."

Description: "Χριστού" is a term used in Christian theology to refer to Jesus Christ. It is often used in religious texts, prayers, and hymns to express the relationship between Jesus and his followers.
