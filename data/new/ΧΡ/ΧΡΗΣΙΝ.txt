Etymology: The word "χρησιν" (chresin) comes from the Greek verb "χράομαι" (chraomai), which means "to use" or "to make use of." 

Grammar: "Χρησιν" (chresin) is a noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative form of the noun "χρῆσις" (chresis), which means "use" or "function."
