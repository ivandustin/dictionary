Etymology: The word "σφραγίδων" comes from the Greek word "σφραγίς" (sphragis), meaning "seal" or "stamp."

Grammar: "Σφραγίδων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "σφραγίς" (sphragis). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Σφραγίδων" refers to multiple seals or stamps. It can be used to describe physical seals used for authentication or to refer to metaphorical seals or marks of approval.
