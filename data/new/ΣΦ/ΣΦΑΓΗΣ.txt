Etymology: The word "σφαγής" (sphagis) comes from the Greek verb "σφάζω" (sphazo), which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher."

Grammar: In Greek, "σφαγής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has a stem ending in -η.

Description: "Σφαγής" is a Greek word that refers to someone or something that is related to or involved in the act of slaughter or butchery. It can be used to describe a person who slaughters animals for food or a place where animals are slaughtered.
