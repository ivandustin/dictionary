Etymology: The word "σφαγήν" (sphagín) comes from the Greek verb "σφάζω" (sfázo), which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher."

Grammar: In Greek, "σφαγήν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is a singular form.

Description: "Σφαγήν" is a Greek word that refers to the act of slaughtering or the place where animals are slaughtered. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a massacre or a bloodbath.
