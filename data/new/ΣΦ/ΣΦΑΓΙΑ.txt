Etymology: The word "σφαγία" (sphagia) comes from the Greek verb "σφάζω" (sfazo), which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher."

Grammar: In Greek, "σφαγία" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the first declension and has the plural form "σφαγίες" (sphagies).

Description: "Σφαγία" refers to the act of slaughtering animals for food. It can also be used to describe a massacre or a large-scale killing of people.
