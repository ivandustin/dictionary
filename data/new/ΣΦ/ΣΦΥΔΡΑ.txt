Etymology: The word "σφυδρα" (sphydra) comes from the Greek language. 

Grammar: "Σφυδρα" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the feminine gender in Greek grammar.

Description: "Σφυδρα" refers to a type of ancient Greek musical instrument. It is a percussion instrument that consists of a wooden frame with strings stretched across it. The strings are struck with a mallet or a stick to produce sound. The sphydra was commonly used in ancient Greek music and was often played during religious ceremonies and theatrical performances.
