Etymology: The word "σφαξουσιν" (sphaxousin) comes from the Greek verb "σφάζω" (sphazo), which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher."

Grammar: "Σφαξουσιν" is a verb form in the third person plural, present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is derived from the verb "σφάζω" (sphazo) and is used to express the action of slaughtering or butchering.

Description: "Σφαξουσιν" is a word in the Greek language that specifically refers to the act of slaughtering or butchering. It is commonly used in contexts related to the preparation of meat or the killing of animals for food.
