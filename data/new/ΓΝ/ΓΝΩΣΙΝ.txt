Etymology: The word "γνωσιν" (gnōsin) comes from the Greek verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand."

Grammar: "Γνωσιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive.

Description: "Γνωσιν" refers to knowledge or understanding. It can also be translated as "cognition" or "awareness." It represents the act of knowing or the state of being knowledgeable.
