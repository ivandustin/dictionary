Etymology: The word "γνωσομέθα" comes from the Greek verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand". The suffix "-μεθα" (-meta) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the action is performed by the subject upon itself.

Grammar: "Γνωσομέθα" is the first person plural, future tense, middle voice form of the verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginosko). The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, it means "we will know" or "we will understand".

Description: "Γνωσομέθα" is a Greek verb form that expresses the future tense and the idea of knowing or understanding. It implies that the subject will acquire knowledge or comprehension in the future.
