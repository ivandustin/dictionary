Etymology: The word "γνώστην" (gnostin) comes from the Greek verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃-.

Grammar: "Γνώστην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case, number, and gender. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Γνώστην" refers to a person who has knowledge or understanding of a particular subject. It can be translated as "knower" or "one who knows" in English.
