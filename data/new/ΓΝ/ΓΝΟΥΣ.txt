Etymology: The word "γνους" (gnous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand."

Grammar: In Greek, "γνους" (gnous) is a noun. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description: "Γνους" (gnous) refers to someone who possesses knowledge or understanding. It can be translated as "knower" or "one who knows."
