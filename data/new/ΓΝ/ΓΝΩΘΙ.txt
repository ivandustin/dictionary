Etymology: The word "γνωθι" (gnōthi) comes from the Greek verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand." The word "θι" (thi) is a particle that adds emphasis. Together, "γνωθι" (gnōthi) can be translated as "know" or "learn."

Grammar: "Γνωθι" (gnōthi) is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular form. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "know" or "learn" something.

Description: "Γνωθι" (gnōthi) is a word that encourages self-awareness and knowledge. It is often associated with the famous inscription "γνῶθι σεαυτόν" (gnōthi seauton) which means "know thyself." This phrase was inscribed on the Temple of Apollo at Delphi in ancient Greece and has been a guiding principle in philosophy and self-reflection.
