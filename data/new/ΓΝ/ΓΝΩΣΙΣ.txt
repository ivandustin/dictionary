Etymology: The word "γνῶσις" (gnōsis) comes from the Greek verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃-, meaning "to know" or "to perceive."

Grammar: In Greek, "γνῶσις" is a feminine noun of the third declension. It is typically used in the singular form, but it can also be used in the plural form "γνώσεις" (gnōseis). It can function as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Γνῶσις" refers to knowledge, understanding, or awareness. It encompasses the act of knowing, the process of acquiring knowledge, and the state of being knowledgeable. In philosophy, it often refers to a deeper, intuitive knowledge or insight. The term is commonly used in various fields, including philosophy, psychology, and education, to describe the acquisition and application of knowledge.
