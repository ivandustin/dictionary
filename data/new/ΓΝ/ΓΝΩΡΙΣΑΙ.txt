Etymology: The word "γνωρίσαι" (gnorísai) comes from the Greek verb "γνωρίζω" (gnorízo), which means "to get to know" or "to become acquainted with." It is derived from the noun "γνῶσις" (gnósis), meaning "knowledge" or "understanding."

Grammar: "Γνωρίσαι" is the aorist imperative form of the verb "γνωρίζω" (gnorízo) in the second person singular. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give a command or make a request.

Description: "Γνωρίσαι" is a verb form that instructs or requests someone to "get to know" or "become acquainted with" something or someone. It implies the action of initiating or establishing a relationship or familiarity with someone or something.
