Etymology: The word "γνησιως" (gnēsiōs) comes from the Greek adjective "γνήσιος" (gnēsios), which means "genuine" or "authentic."

Grammar: "Γνησιως" is an adverb in Greek. It is derived from the adjective "γνήσιος" (gnēsios) by adding the adverbial suffix "-ως" (-ōs).

Description: "Γνησιως" is used to describe something that is done or performed in a genuine or authentic manner. It implies that there is no deception or falseness involved.
