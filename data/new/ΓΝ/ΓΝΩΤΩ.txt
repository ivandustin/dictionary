Etymology: The word "γνωτω" (gnōtō) comes from the Greek verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginōskō), which means "to know" or "to understand."

Grammar: "Γνωτω" (gnōtō) is a verb in the imperative mood, present tense, and active voice. It is the second person singular form of the verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginōskō), which means "know" or "understand."

Description: "Γνωτω" (gnōtō) is a command form that instructs someone to know or understand something. It is used to express a direct order or request for knowledge or understanding.
