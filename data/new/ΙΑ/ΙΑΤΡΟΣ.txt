Etymology: The word "ιατρός" (iatros) comes from the Greek word "ἰατρός" (iatros), which means "physician" or "healer". It is derived from the verb "ἰάομαι" (iaomai), meaning "to heal" or "to cure".

Grammar: "Ιατρός" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "ιατρός". In the plural form, it becomes "ιατροί" (iatroi).

Description: "Ιατρός" is the Greek word for "doctor" or "physician". It refers to a person who is trained and licensed to practice medicine, diagnose illnesses, and provide medical treatment to patients. In Greek culture, the role of a doctor is highly respected and valued.
