Etymology: The word "ιατρος" (iatros) comes from the Greek word "ιατρεία" (iatreia), which means "healing" or "medical treatment". It is derived from the verb "ιάομαι" (iaomai), meaning "to heal" or "to cure".

Grammar: "Ιατρος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative form "ιατρος". In the plural form, it becomes "ιατροι" (iatroi).

Description: "Ιατρος" is the Greek word for "doctor" or "physician". It refers to a person who is trained and licensed to practice medicine, diagnose illnesses, and provide medical treatment to patients. In Greek culture, doctors have been highly respected throughout history for their knowledge and ability to heal.
