Etymology: The word "ιατρός" (iatrós) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἰατρός" (iatrós), which means "healer" or "physician". 

Grammar: In Greek, "ιατρός" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has a stem ending in -ο. 

Description: "Ιατρός" is the Greek word for "doctor" or "physician". It refers to a person who is trained and licensed to practice medicine, diagnose illnesses, and provide medical treatment.
