Etymology: The word "ιασονος" (iasónos) comes from the Greek mythological figure Jason, who was the leader of the Argonauts. 

Grammar: "Ιασονος" is a masculine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Ιασονος" refers to something that belongs to or is related to Jason. It can be used to describe his possessions, attributes, or characteristics.
