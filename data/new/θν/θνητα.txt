Word: θνητά

Etymology: θνητά is a Greek word that comes from the root word θνητός (thnitos), which means mortal or subject to death. The suffix -α (-a) is added to make it plural.

Grammar: θνητά is a plural neuter adjective in Greek. It is used to describe things that are mortal or subject to death.

Description: θνητά is a word that is often used in Greek mythology to describe mortals or humans. It is also used in a more general sense to describe anything that is subject to death or has a limited lifespan. In modern Greek, it is still used to describe mortal beings.
