Etymology: The word "βρῶμα" (brôma) comes from the Greek verb "βιβρώσκω" (bibrṓskō), which means "to eat" or "to devour."

Grammar: In Greek, "βρῶμα" is a neuter noun of the second declension. It is the nominative singular form, and its genitive singular form is "βρώματος" (brṓmatos).

Description: "Βρῶμα" is a Greek word that means "food" or "nourishment." It refers to any substance that is consumed to provide energy and sustain life.
