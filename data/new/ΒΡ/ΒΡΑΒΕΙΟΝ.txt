Etymology: The word "βραβείον" (brabeíon) comes from the Ancient Greek word "βραβεῖον" (brabeîon), which means "prize" or "reward." It is derived from the verb "βραβεύω" (brabeúō), meaning "to award a prize."

Grammar: In Greek, "βραβείον" is a neuter noun of the second declension. It has the plural form "βραβεῖα" (brabeîa).

Description: "Βραβείον" refers to a prize or an award given to someone as a recognition of their achievement or excellence in a particular field. It can be used to describe various types of awards, such as trophies, medals, certificates, or monetary rewards.
