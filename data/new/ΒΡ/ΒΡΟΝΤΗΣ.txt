Etymology: The word "βροντης" (brontēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "βροντάω" (brontáō), which means "to thunder."

Grammar: In Greek, "βροντης" (brontēs) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has a genitive form of "βροντητος" (brontētos).

Description: "Βροντης" (brontēs) refers to the concept of thunder. It is the sound produced by the rapid expansion and contraction of air surrounding a lightning bolt. In Greek mythology, Zeus, the king of the gods, was often associated with thunder and was believed to create it with his thunderbolts.
