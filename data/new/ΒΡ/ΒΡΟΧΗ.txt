Etymology: The word "βροχή" (vrochi) comes from the Ancient Greek word "βροχή" (brokhḗ), which means "rainfall" or "shower".

Grammar: In Greek, "βροχή" is a feminine noun. It is in the singular form, and its plural form is "βροχές" (vroches).

Description: "Βροχή" is the Greek word for "rain". It refers to the water droplets that fall from the atmosphere to the Earth's surface. Rain is an essential part of the water cycle and plays a crucial role in the Earth's climate and ecosystems.
