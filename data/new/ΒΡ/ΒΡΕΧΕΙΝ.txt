Etymology: The word "βρεχειν" (brechein) comes from the Greek verb "βρέχω" (brecho), which means "to rain". 

Grammar: "Βρεχειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb "βρέχω" (brecho). In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb in its most basic form, without specifying any tense, mood, or person.

Description: "Βρεχειν" is a Greek verb that means "to rain". It describes the action of water falling from the atmosphere in the form of droplets. Rain is an essential part of the water cycle and plays a crucial role in the Earth's climate and ecosystems.
