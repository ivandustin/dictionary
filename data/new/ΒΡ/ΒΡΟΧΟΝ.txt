Etymology: The word "βροχον" (vrochon) comes from the Greek word "βροχή" (vrochi), which means "rain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰreg- meaning "to break" or "to scatter".

Grammar: In Greek, "βροχον" is a noun of the neuter gender. It is in the singular form.

Description: "Βροχον" refers to a raindrop. It is the individual unit of water that falls from the sky during rainfall. Raindrops are formed when water vapor condenses in the atmosphere and falls to the ground due to gravity. They vary in size depending on the intensity of the rainfall.
