Etymology: The word "βρονταί" comes from the Greek verb "βροντάω" (brontáo), which means "to thunder". It is derived from the noun "βροντή" (bronté), meaning "thunder".

Grammar: "Βρονταί" is the plural form of the noun "βροντή" (bronté). It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Βρονταί" refers to thunders, the loud and rumbling sounds that occur during a thunderstorm. It is used to describe the noise produced by the rapid expansion and contraction of air surrounding a lightning bolt. Thunders are often associated with storms and can be heard as a deep, booming sound.
