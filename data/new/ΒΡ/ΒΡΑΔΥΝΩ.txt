Etymology: The word "βραδυνω" (bradyno) comes from the Greek verb "βραδύνω" (bradyno), which means "to slow down" or "to delay". It is derived from the adjective "βραδύς" (bradys), meaning "slow".

Grammar: "Βραδυνω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ βραδύνω (ego bradyno) - I slow down
- Εσύ βραδύνεις (esi bradynis) - You slow down
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό βραδύνει (aftos/afti/afto bradyni) - He/She/It slows down
- Εμείς βραδυνούμε (emeis bradynoume) - We slow down
- Εσείς βραδυνετε (esies bradynete) - You (plural) slow down
- Αυτοί/Αυτές βραδυνουν (aftoi/aftes bradynoun) - They slow down

Description: "Βραδυνω" is a Greek verb that describes the action of slowing down or delaying something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the act of reducing speed, taking longer to complete a task, or postponing an event.
