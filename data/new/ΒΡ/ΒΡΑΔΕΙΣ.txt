Etymology: The word "βραδείς" (bradeis) comes from the Greek adjective "βραδύς" (bradýs), which means "slow" or "sluggish".

Grammar: "Βραδείς" is the plural form of the adjective "βραδύς" in the masculine gender. It is used to describe multiple nouns that are slow or sluggish.

Description: "Βραδείς" is an adjective in the Greek language that is used to describe something or someone that is slow or sluggish. It can be used to describe the speed of movement, the pace of an action, or the overall behavior of a person or thing.
