Word: αετοί

Etymology: The word αετοί (aetoi) is derived from the Greek word αετός (aetos), which means "eagle". The plural form αετοί (aetoi) refers to multiple eagles.

Grammar: αετοί (aetoi) is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form in Greek. It is declined as follows: αετοί (nominative), αετών (genitive), αετοίς (dative), and αετούς (accusative).

Description: αετοί (aetoi) are large birds of prey with a wingspan of up to 2.5 meters. They are known for their keen eyesight and powerful talons, which they use to catch and kill their prey.
