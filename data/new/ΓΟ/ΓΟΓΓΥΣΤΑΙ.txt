Etymology: The word "γογγυσταί" comes from the Greek verb "γογγύζω" (gongyzō), which means "to grumble" or "to murmur."

Grammar: "Γογγυσταί" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Γογγυσταί" refers to a group of people who constantly complain or grumble about something. It can also be used to describe a chorus of voices murmuring in discontent.
