Etymology: The word "γονασιν" (gonasin) comes from the Greek word "γόνατο" (gonato), which means "knee". 

Grammar: "Γονασιν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the neuter gender. 

Description: "Γονασιν" refers to the knee, which is the joint between the thigh and the lower leg in humans and other mammals. It is a crucial joint that allows for movement and supports the body's weight during activities such as walking, running, and jumping.
