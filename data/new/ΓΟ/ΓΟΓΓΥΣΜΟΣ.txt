Etymology: The word "γογγυσμος" (gongysmos) comes from the Greek verb "γογγύζω" (gongyzō), which means "to grumble" or "to murmur."

Grammar: In Greek, "γογγυσμος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form.

Description: "Γογγυσμος" refers to the act of grumbling or murmuring. It can describe the low, indistinct sound made by someone who is complaining or expressing discontent. It can also be used metaphorically to describe any continuous, low-level noise or rumbling.
