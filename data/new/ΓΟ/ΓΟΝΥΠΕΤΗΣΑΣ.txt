Etymology: The word "γονυπετησας" (gonypetisas) comes from the Greek roots "γόνυ" (gonu), meaning "knee," and "πετάομαι" (petáomai), meaning "to fall on one's knees." 

Grammar: "Γονυπετησας" is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb.

Description: "Γονυπετησας" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it fell on his/her/its knees." It describes the action of someone or something kneeling down or prostrating themselves.
