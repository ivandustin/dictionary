Etymology: The word "γογγυζουσιν" (gongyzousin) comes from the Greek verb "γογγύζω" (gongyzo), which means "to grumble" or "to murmur."

Grammar: "Γογγυζουσιν" is the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb "γογγύζω" (gongyzo). It indicates that a group of people is currently grumbling or murmuring.

Description: "Γογγυζουσιν" is a Greek word that describes the action of multiple individuals grumbling or murmuring. It can be used to express discontent, dissatisfaction, or complaint.
