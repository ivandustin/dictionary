Etymology: The word "γονείς" (gonéis) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "γενεά" (geneá), meaning "generation" or "offspring."

Grammar: In Greek, "γονείς" is a noun in the plural form. It is the word used to refer to parents.

Description: "Γονείς" is a term used to describe the individuals who have given birth to or have raised a person. It refers to both the mother and the father collectively. The word emphasizes the role and importance of parents in a person's life.
