Etymology: The word "αμαρτιαί" comes from the Greek word "αμαρτία" (amartía), which means "sin" or "error". 

Grammar: "αμαρτιαί" is a noun in the plural form. It is the feminine form of the word "αμαρτία" (amartía).

Description: "αμαρτιαί" refers to sins or errors committed by someone. It is often used in a religious or moral context to describe actions that are considered morally wrong or against religious teachings.
