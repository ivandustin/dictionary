Etymology: The word "αμφοδου" (amphodou) comes from the Greek roots "αμφί" (amphi), meaning "around" or "on both sides," and "οδούς" (odous), meaning "tooth." It literally translates to "around the tooth."

Grammar: "Αμφοδου" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. It belongs to the masculine gender and is in the nominative case.

Description: "Αμφοδου" refers to the space between two teeth, commonly known as the interdental space. It is the area where the gums meet and where dental floss or interdental brushes are used for cleaning.
