Etymology: The word "αμφοτερους" (amphoteros) comes from the Greek roots "αμφι" (amphi), meaning "both" or "on both sides," and "τερος" (teros), meaning "one of two." 

Grammar: "Αμφοτερους" is a masculine accusative plural form of the adjective "αμφοτερος" (amphoteros), which means "both" or "either." It is used to refer to two people or things together.

Description: "Αμφοτερους" is used in Greek to indicate that something applies to or involves both of two people or things. It is often used in sentences where there is a choice between two options or when something is true for both alternatives.
