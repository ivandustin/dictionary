Etymology: The word "αμεθυστος" (amethystos) comes from the Greek words "α" (a), meaning "not," and "μεθυω" (methyo), meaning "to be drunk." It is derived from the belief in ancient Greece that amethyst had the power to prevent intoxication.

Grammar: "Αμεθυστος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description: "Αμεθυστος" is a Greek word that means "sober" or "not drunk." It is often used to describe someone who is not under the influence of alcohol or any other intoxicating substance.
