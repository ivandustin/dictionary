Etymology: The word "αμητωρ" (amitor) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "without," and the noun "μητωρ" (mitor), meaning "mother."

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "αμητωρ" is a noun in the masculine gender. It belongs to the third declension and has the genitive form "αμητορος" (amitoros).
