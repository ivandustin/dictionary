Etymology: The word "αμην" (amēn) comes from the Hebrew word "אָמֵן" (āmēn), which means "truly" or "so be it". It was adopted into Greek and then into other languages.

Grammar: In Greek, "αμην" (amēn) is an indeclinable word, meaning it does not change form based on gender, number, or case. It is used as an adverb or interjection.

Description: "Αμην" (amēn) is a word used to express agreement, affirmation, or confirmation. It is often used at the end of prayers or religious statements to indicate agreement with what has been said. It is also used in a similar way in other contexts to express agreement or emphasize a statement.
