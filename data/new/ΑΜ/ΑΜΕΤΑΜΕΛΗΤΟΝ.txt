Etymology: The word "αμεταμελητον" (ametamelēton) comes from the Greek roots "α" (a), meaning "not," "μετα" (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and "μελετητον" (meletēton), meaning "studied" or "considered." 

Grammar: "Αμεταμελητον" is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is not subject to change or regret.

Description: "Αμεταμελητον" refers to something that is unchangeable, immutable, or irrevocable. It describes a state or condition that cannot be altered or regretted.
