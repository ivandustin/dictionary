Etymology: The word "αμεμπτους" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not") and the word "μεμπτός" (meaning "blameworthy" or "faulty").

Grammar: "αμεμπτους" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "αμεμπτους" describes something or someone that is blameless, faultless, or without reproach. It implies that the subject is free from any wrongdoing or criticism.
