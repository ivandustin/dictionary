Etymology: The word "αμφοτερα" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the prefix "αμφι-" meaning "both" or "around" and the word "τερα" meaning "end" or "boundary".

Grammar: "αμφοτερα" is a neuter plural adjective in Greek. It is used to describe or modify plural nouns. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the nouns they modify. 

Description: "αμφοτερα" is used to indicate that something applies to or involves both of two things. It can be translated as "both" or "either" in English, depending on the context.
