Etymology: The word "αμετανοητον" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not") and the word "μετανοητον" (meaning "repentable" or "regrettable").

Grammar: "αμετανοητον" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In other cases, its form may change to agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "αμετανοητον" describes something that cannot be repented or regretted. It implies that a certain action or situation is irreversible or beyond the possibility of changing one's mind or feeling remorse.
