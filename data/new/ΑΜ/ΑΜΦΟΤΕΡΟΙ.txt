Etymology: The word "αμφοτεροι" (amphoteroi) comes from the Greek roots "αμφι" (amphi), meaning "both" or "on both sides," and "τερος" (teros), meaning "one of two." 

Grammar: "Αμφοτεροι" is a plural form of the adjective "αμφότερος" (amphoteros), which means "both" or "either." It is used to refer to two people or things together.

Description: "Αμφοτεροι" is used to indicate that something applies to or involves both of two people or things. It can be used to express a choice between two options or to describe a situation where both options are relevant or applicable.
