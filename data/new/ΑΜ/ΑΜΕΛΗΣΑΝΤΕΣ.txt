Etymology: The word "αμελησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "αμελέω" (ameléo), which means "to neglect" or "to disregard". The suffix "-σαντες" (-santes) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of neglecting has been completed.

Grammar: "αμελησαντες" is the third person plural form of the verb "αμελέω" in the aorist tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have neglected or disregarded something in the past.

Description: "αμελησαντες" is a Greek word that describes a group of people who have neglected or disregarded something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing individuals who have failed to take care of their responsibilities or who have ignored important tasks or duties.
