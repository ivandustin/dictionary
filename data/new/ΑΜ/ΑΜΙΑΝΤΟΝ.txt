Etymology: The word "αμιαντον" (amianton) comes from the Greek word "αμίαντος" (amiantos), which means "indestructible" or "unbreakable". 

Grammar: "Αμιαντον" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the neuter gender and is declined as such. 

Description: "Αμιαντον" refers to a material or substance that is resistant to heat, fire, and chemical reactions. It is commonly used in the context of asbestos, a mineral known for its heat resistance and durability. However, due to its harmful effects on health, the use of asbestos has been restricted or banned in many countries.
