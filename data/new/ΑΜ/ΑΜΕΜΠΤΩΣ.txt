Etymology: The word "αμεμπτως" (amemptos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "μεμπτός" (memptos), meaning "blameworthy" or "faulty."

Grammar: "Αμεμπτως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or state that is done or performed without any blame or fault.

Description: "Αμεμπτως" is used to express the idea of doing something in a faultless or blameless manner. It implies that the action or behavior is done perfectly, without any mistakes or wrongdoing.
