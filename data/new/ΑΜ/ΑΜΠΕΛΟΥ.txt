Etymology: The word "αμπελού" (ampelou) comes from the Greek word "αμπέλι" (ampeli), which means "vineyard" or "grapevine". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἄμπελος" (ampelos), which also means "vine" or "grapevine".

Grammar: "Αμπελού" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender. In the plural form, it becomes "αμπελοί" (ampeloi).

Description: "Αμπελού" refers to a vineyard or a grapevine. It is commonly used to describe the cultivated area where grapes are grown for winemaking. The word can also be used metaphorically to represent the wine industry or the process of winemaking.
