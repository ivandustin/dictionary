Etymology: The word "αμαρτανουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αμαρτάνω" (amartáno), which means "to sin" or "to make a mistake". The suffix "-ουσιν" (-ousin) indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "αμαρτανουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "αμαρτανουσιν" is used to describe the action of sinning or making mistakes. It refers to the act of committing a moral or religious offense, or making an error or wrongdoing.
