Etymology: The word "αμωμος" (amomos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "μωμος" (momos), meaning "blemish" or "defect."

Grammar: In Greek, "αμωμος" (amomos) is an adjective. It is used to describe something or someone that is without blemish, faultless, or perfect.

Description: "Αμωμος" (amomos) is a term often used in a moral or ethical context. It refers to a person who is blameless, innocent, or free from any wrongdoing. It can also be used to describe something that is flawless, pure, or uncorrupted.
