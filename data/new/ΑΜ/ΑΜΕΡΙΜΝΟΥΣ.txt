Etymology: The word "αμεριμνος" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (meaning "without" or "not") and the word "μεριμνη" (meaning "care" or "concern").

Grammar: In Greek, "αμεριμνος" is an adjective. It is in the masculine form and singular number. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: The word "αμεριμνος" describes someone or something that is carefree, without worries, or free from anxiety. It implies a state of being calm, relaxed, and not burdened by concerns or troubles.
