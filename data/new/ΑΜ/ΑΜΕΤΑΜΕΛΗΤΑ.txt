Etymology: The word "αμεταμελητα" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the word "μεταμέλητα" (meaning "regretted" or "repented").

Grammar: "αμεταμελητα" is an adjective in the neuter gender, plural form. It is used to describe nouns that are inanimate objects or concepts.

Description: The word "αμεταμελητα" is used to describe something that is not regretted or repented. It implies that there is no remorse or change of mind regarding a particular action or decision.
