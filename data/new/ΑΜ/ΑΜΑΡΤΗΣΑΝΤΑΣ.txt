Etymology: The word "αμαρτησαντας" comes from the Greek verb "αμαρτάνω" (amartáno), which means "to sin" or "to make a mistake". The suffix "-αντας" (-antas) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, indicating that the action of sinning or making a mistake has been completed.

Grammar: "αμαρτησαντας" is the masculine singular form of the past participle of the verb "αμαρτάνω". In Greek grammar, past participles are used to form compound verb tenses and can also function as adjectives.

Description: "αμαρτησαντας" describes someone who has sinned or made a mistake in the past. It can be used as an adjective to modify a noun or as part of a compound verb tense to indicate a completed action.
