Etymology: The word "αμφιεννυσιν" comes from the Greek roots "αμφί" (amphi), meaning "around" or "on both sides", and "εννύσσω" (ennýssō), meaning "to clothe" or "to dress". 

Grammar: "αμφιεννυσιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. 

Description: "αμφιεννυσιν" refers to the act of dressing or clothing oneself. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of adorning or embellishing something.
