Etymology: The word "αμφοτεροις" (amphoterois) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the prefix "αμφι-" (amphi-), meaning "both" or "on both sides," and the word "τερος" (teros), meaning "side" or "direction."

Grammar: "Αμφοτεροις" is a noun in the dative case, plural number, and masculine gender. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Αμφοτεροις" refers to a group of two people or things. It implies that both individuals or objects are involved or affected in a particular situation.
