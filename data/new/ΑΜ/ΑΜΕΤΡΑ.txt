Etymology: The word "αμετρα" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the word "μετρον" (meaning "measure"). 

Grammar: "αμετρα" is an adjective in Greek. It is in the feminine gender, singular form. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree with the noun they modify in gender, number, and case. 

Description: The word "αμετρα" means "immeasurable" or "boundless" in Greek. It is used to describe something that cannot be measured or has no limits.
