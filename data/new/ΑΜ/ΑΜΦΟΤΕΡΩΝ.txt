Etymology: The word "αμφοτερων" (amphoteron) comes from the Greek roots "αμφι" (amphi), meaning "both" or "on both sides," and "τερον" (teron), meaning "side" or "part." 

Grammar: "αμφοτερων" is the genitive plural form of the Greek adjective "αμφοτερος" (amphoteros), which means "both" or "either." In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "αμφοτερων" is used to refer to two or more things or people, indicating that they are both involved or affected. It can be translated as "of both" or "of either" in English.
