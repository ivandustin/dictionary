Etymology: The word "αμαραντινόν" comes from the Greek word "αμάραντος" (amarantos), meaning "unfading" or "immortal". It is derived from the negative prefix "α-" and the verb "μαραίνω" (maraino), meaning "to wither" or "to fade".

Grammar: "Αμαραντινόν" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is related to or resembles the amaranth plant, which is known for its vibrant and long-lasting flowers.
