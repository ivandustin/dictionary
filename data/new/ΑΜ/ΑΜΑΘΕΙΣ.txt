Etymology: The word "αμαθεις" (amathis) comes from the Greek word "α" (a), meaning "not," and "μαθεις" (mathis), meaning "learned" or "knowledgeable." Therefore, "αμαθεις" translates to "unlearned" or "ignorant."

Grammar: "Αμαθεις" is the plural form of the adjective "αμαθης" (amathis), which means "unlearned" or "ignorant" in Greek. It is used to describe multiple people who lack knowledge or education.

Description: "Αμαθεις" is a term used to describe individuals who lack knowledge or education in a particular subject or in general. It implies a lack of understanding or awareness and can be used to refer to someone who is uninformed or inexperienced.
