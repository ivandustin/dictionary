Etymology: The word "αμωμον" (amomon) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the word "μωμος" (mōmos), meaning "blemish" or "defect." 

Grammar: "Αμωμον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension. 

Description: "Αμωμον" refers to something that is without blemish, defect, or fault. It can be used to describe a person, object, or situation that is perfect or flawless.
