Etymology: The word "αμφιπολιν" (amfipolin) comes from the Greek roots "αμφί" (amphi), meaning "around" or "both sides", and "πόλις" (polis), meaning "city". 

Grammar: "Αμφιπολιν" is a noun in the singular form. It is a feminine noun, belonging to the third declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Αμφιπολιν" refers to a city that is situated on both sides of a river or a body of water. It is used to describe a city that is divided by a natural or man-made boundary, such as a river or a strait.
