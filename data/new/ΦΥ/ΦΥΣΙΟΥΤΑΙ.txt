Etymology: The word "φυσιουται" comes from the Greek verb "φυσιόω" (physioo), which means "to inflate" or "to blow up." It is derived from the noun "φύσις" (physis), meaning "nature" or "essence."

Grammar: "Φυσιουται" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action on itself.

Description: The word "φυσιουται" can be translated as "he/she/it inflates" or "he/she/it blows up." It describes the action of something expanding or becoming larger due to the internal pressure or force.
