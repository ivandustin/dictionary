Etymology: The word "φυλασσεσθε" comes from the Greek verb "φυλάσσω" (phylássō), which means "to guard" or "to protect."

Grammar: "Φυλάσσεσθε" is the present imperative form of the verb "φυλάσσω" in Greek. It is the second person plural form, used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. It translates to "guard yourselves" or "be on guard."

Description: "Φυλάσσεσθε" is a word that emphasizes the importance of being vigilant and cautious. It is often used in contexts where one needs to be watchful and protect oneself from potential dangers or threats.
