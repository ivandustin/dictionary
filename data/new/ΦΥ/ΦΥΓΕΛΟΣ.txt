Etymology: The word "φυγελός" (pronounced fygelos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "φεύγω" (pronounced fevgo), which means "to flee" or "to escape."

Grammar: "Φυγελός" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "φυγελοί" (pronounced fygeloi). In Greek grammar, nouns are declined according to gender, number, and case.

Description: "Φυγελός" is used to describe someone who is a fugitive or a runaway. It refers to a person who has escaped from a particular place or situation, often to avoid punishment or danger.
