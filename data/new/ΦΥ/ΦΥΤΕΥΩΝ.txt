Etymology: The word "φυτεύων" (pronounced "fytevón") comes from the Greek verb "φυτεύω" (fytevó), which means "to plant" or "to sow". It is derived from the noun "φυτόν" (fyton), meaning "plant".

Grammar: "Φυτεύων" is the present participle form of the verb "φυτεύω" in the masculine singular nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description: "Φυτεύων" refers to a person who is currently planting or sowing. It can be used to describe someone engaged in the act of planting seeds or young plants in the ground.
