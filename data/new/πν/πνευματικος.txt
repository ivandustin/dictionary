Word: πνευματικός

Etymology: The word πνευματικός comes from the Greek word πνεῦμα (pneuma), meaning "breath" or "spirit". It is derived from the verb πνέω (pneo), which means "to blow" or "to breathe".

Grammar: πνευματικός is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is related to the spirit or the mind, rather than the physical body.

Description: πνευματικός is a word that is often used in philosophical and religious contexts to describe concepts that are related to the spirit or the mind. It can be used to describe things like spiritual beliefs, intellectual pursuits, or artistic endeavors. In modern Greek, it is also used to describe things like books, music, and other cultural products that are not physical in nature.
