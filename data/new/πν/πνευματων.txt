Word: πνευματων

Etymology: πνεῦμα (pneuma) means "breath" or "spirit" in Greek. The suffix -των (-ton) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple spirits or breaths.

Grammar: πνευματων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple spirits or breaths.

Description: πνευματων is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Hebrews (1:14). It is translated as "spirits" or "angels" and refers to the heavenly beings who serve God. The word is also used in other contexts to refer to the breath of life or the Holy Spirit.
