Word: πνευματα

Etymology: πνευματα is a Greek word that comes from the root word πνεῦμα (pneuma), which means "breath" or "spirit". In Greek mythology, πνευματα referred to the spirits or ghosts of the dead.

Grammar: πνευματα is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative plural form of πνεῦμα (pneuma). In English, it can be translated as "spirits" or "ghosts".

Description: πνευματα is a word that has a rich history in Greek mythology and religion. It is often used to refer to the spirits or ghosts of the dead, and is associated with the idea of the afterlife. In modern Greek, it can also refer to the breath or wind.
