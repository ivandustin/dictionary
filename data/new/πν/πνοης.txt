The word "πνοης" (pnoes) is a Greek noun that means "breath" or "breathing." It is derived from the Greek verb "πνέω" (pneo), which means "to breathe." In terms of grammar, "πνοης" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It can be used to describe the act of breathing or the breath itself. In ancient Greek philosophy, "πνοης" was also used to refer to the vital force or spirit that animates living beings.
