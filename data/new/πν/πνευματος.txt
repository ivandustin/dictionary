Word: πνευματος

Etymology: πνεῦμα (pneuma) is a Greek word meaning "breath" or "spirit". The suffix -τος (-tos) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives from nouns.

Grammar: πνευματος is a genitive singular form of the Greek noun πνεῦμα (pneuma), which means "breath", "wind", or "spirit". In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: πνευματος is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the writings of the Apostle Paul. It is often translated as "spirit" or "Holy Spirit". In Christian theology, the Holy Spirit is considered to be the third person of the Trinity, along with God the Father and Jesus Christ. The word πνευματος is also used in Greek philosophy to refer to the breath or life force that animates living beings.
