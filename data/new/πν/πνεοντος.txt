The word "πνεοντος" (pneontos) is a Greek adjective in the genitive case, which means "breathing" or "blowing". It is derived from the verb "πνέω" (pneō), which means "to blow" or "to breathe". In grammar, it is classified as a third declension adjective, which means its endings change depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

Example usage: "ὁ πνεόντων ἀνέμων" (ho pneontōn anemōn) means "the blowing winds".
