Word: πνευματικάς

Etymology: The word πνευματικάς comes from the Greek word πνευματικός (pneumatikos), which means "spiritual" or "of the spirit". The suffix -άς (-as) is added to make the word masculine and singular.

Grammar: πνευματικάς is a masculine singular adjective in Greek. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. In this form, it modifies a masculine singular noun in the nominative case.

Description: πνευματικάς is an adjective that describes something related to the spirit or spiritual matters. It can be used to describe a person, an object, or an idea. For example, πνευματικάς διαδικασία (pneumatikas diadikasia) means "spiritual process", and πνευματικός άνθρωπος (pneumatikos anthropos) means "spiritual person".
