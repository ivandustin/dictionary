Word: πνευματικόν

Etymology: The word πνευματικόν comes from the Greek word πνεῦμα (pneuma), meaning "breath" or "spirit." The suffix -ικόν (-ikon) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives, meaning "related to" or "pertaining to."

Grammar: πνευματικόν is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: πνευματικόν is an adjective that describes something that is related to the spirit or the mind. It can be used to describe things like intellectual or spiritual pursuits, as well as things that are intangible or abstract. For example, πνευματικόν έργο (pneumatikon ergo) means "spiritual work" or "intellectual work," while πνευματικόν κλίμα (pneumatikon klima) means "spiritual atmosphere" or "intellectual atmosphere."
