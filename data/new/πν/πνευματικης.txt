Word: πνευματικής

Etymology: The word πνευματικής comes from the Greek word πνευματικός (pneumatikos), which means "spiritual" or "of the spirit". The root word is πνεῦμα (pneuma), which means "breath" or "spirit".

Grammar: πνευματικής is the genitive form of the adjective πνευματικός (pneumatikos), which means "spiritual" or "of the spirit". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, πνευματικής modifies a feminine noun in the genitive case.

Description: πνευματικής is an adjective that describes something that is related to the spirit or the soul. It can be used to describe a person, an idea, or a work of art that is spiritual or intellectual in nature. For example, πνευματικής δημιουργίας (pneumatikis dimiourgias) means "of spiritual creation" and can refer to a work of art or literature that is inspired by the spirit or the soul.
