Etymology: The word "ααρών" (aarón) comes from the Hebrew name "אַהֲרֹן" (Aharon), which means "mountain of strength" or "exalted one".

Grammar: "ααρών" is a masculine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in the nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases.

Description: In Greek, "ααρών" refers to Aaron, the older brother of Moses and the first high priest of the Israelites. He played a significant role in the Old Testament of the Bible and is considered a prophet and a leader.
