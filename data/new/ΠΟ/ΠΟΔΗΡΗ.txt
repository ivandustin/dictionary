Etymology: The word "ποδήρης" (podíris) comes from the combination of the Greek words "πούς" (poús) meaning "foot" and "ἥρως" (hḗrōs) meaning "hero." 

Grammar: "Ποδήρης" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and has the nominative singular form "ποδήρης." 

Description: In Greek mythology, a "ποδήρης" refers to a hero or warrior who is known for his bravery and strength. This term is often used to describe legendary figures such as Achilles or Hercules.
