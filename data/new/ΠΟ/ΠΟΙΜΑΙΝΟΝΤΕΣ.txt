Etymology: The word "ποιμαίνοντες" (poimainontes) comes from the Greek verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock." It is derived from the noun "ποιμήν" (poimen), meaning "shepherd."

Grammar: "Ποιμαίνοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino) in Greek. It is used to describe the action of shepherding or tending to a flock in the present tense.

Description: "Ποιμαίνοντες" is a Greek word that refers to the act of shepherding or tending to a flock of animals, typically sheep. It can also be used metaphorically to describe guiding or leading a group of people.
