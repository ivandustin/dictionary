Etymology: The word "ποιούμενος" (poioumenos) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to do." The suffix "-μενος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the present passive voice.

Grammar: "Ποιούμενος" is the present passive participle form of the verb "ποιέω" (poieo). It is used to describe someone or something that is being made or done by someone else. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as nouns themselves.

Description: "Ποιούμενος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "being made" or "being done." It is often used in contexts where an action is being performed by someone or something else. For example, in the sentence "Ο πίνακας ποιούμενος από τον καλλιτέχνη ήταν όμορφος" (The painting being made by the artist was beautiful), "ποιούμενος" describes the painting as being made by the artist.
