Etymology: The word "ποιείσθαι" (poieisthai) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to do". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey-.

Grammar: "ποιείσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "ποιέω" (poieo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the voice (active, middle, or passive), tense, mood, and person. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "ποιείσθαι" is an infinitive form of the verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to do". It is used to express the action of creating or performing something. In Greek, infinitives are non-finite verb forms that do not indicate tense or person. They are often used as the subject or object of a sentence, or to express purpose or result.
