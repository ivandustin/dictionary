Etymology: The word "πολλας" (pollas) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "πολύς" (polys), meaning "many" or "much".

Grammar: "Πολλας" (pollas) is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and has the genitive form "πολλοῦ" (pollou) and the accusative form "πολλᾶ" (polla).

Description: "Πολλας" (pollas) is used to describe a large quantity or number of something. It can also be used to express intensity or emphasis. In English, it can be translated as "many", "much", "a lot of", or "numerous".
