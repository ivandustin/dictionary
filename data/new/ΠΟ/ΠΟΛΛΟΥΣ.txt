Etymology: The word "πολλούς" (polloús) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πολύς" (polýs), meaning "many" or "much".

Grammar: "Πολλούς" is the accusative plural form of the adjective "πολύς" (polýs). It is used to describe or modify plural nouns in the accusative case.

Description: "Πολλούς" is an adjective in Greek that means "many" or "much". It is used to indicate a large quantity or number of something. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.
