Etymology: The word "ποιμνίου" (poimniou) comes from the Greek word "ποίμνη" (poimne), which means "flock" or "herd". It is derived from the verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino), meaning "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock".

Grammar: "Ποιμνίου" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Ποιμνίου" refers to something that belongs to or is associated with a flock or herd. It can be used to describe various things related to shepherding or tending to animals, such as a shepherd's staff, a shepherd's dog, or a pasture for grazing.
