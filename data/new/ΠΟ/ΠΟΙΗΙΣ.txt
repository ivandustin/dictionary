Etymology: The word "ποιητής" (poiitis) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to create." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey- meaning "to make" or "to build."

Grammar: In Greek, "ποιητής" is a masculine noun of the first declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "ποιητές" (poiites).

Description: "Ποιητής" is the Greek word for "poet." It refers to a person who writes poetry or creates literary works in verse. In ancient Greece, poets held a significant role in society and were often seen as inspired individuals who had a deep understanding of the world and its mysteries. They were known for their ability to express emotions, tell stories, and convey profound thoughts through their poetic compositions.
