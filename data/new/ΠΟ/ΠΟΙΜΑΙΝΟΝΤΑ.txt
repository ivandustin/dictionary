Etymology: The word "ποιμαίνοντα" comes from the Greek verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock." It is derived from the noun "ποιμήν" (poimen), meaning "shepherd."

Grammar: "Ποιμαίνοντα" is the present participle form of the verb "ποιμαίνω" in the Greek language. It is used to describe someone who is currently shepherding or tending to a flock.

Description: "Ποιμαίνοντα" refers to a person who is actively engaged in the act of shepherding or tending to a flock of animals, typically sheep. It implies the responsibility of guiding, protecting, and caring for the animals under their care.
