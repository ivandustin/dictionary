Etymology: The word "ποιούσιν" (poiousin) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to do" or "to make." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷeh₁-.

Grammar: "Ποιούσιν" is the third person plural form of the present active indicative of the verb "ποιέω" (poieo). In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third conjugation class.

Description: "Ποιούσιν" is used to express the action of doing or making in the present tense, referring to a third person plural subject. It can be translated as "they do" or "they make" in English.
