Etymology: The word "πορευθείς" (poreutheis) comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreuomai), which means "to go" or "to travel."

Grammar: "Πορευθείς" is the aorist participle form of the verb "πορεύομαι" (poreuomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The participle form is used to modify a noun or pronoun.

Description: "Πορευθείς" is a Greek word that means "having gone" or "after going." It is often used in biblical or religious contexts to refer to someone who has gone or traveled to a specific place.
