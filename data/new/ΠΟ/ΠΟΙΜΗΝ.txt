Etymology: The word "ποιμήν" (poimēn) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimainō), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock." 

Grammar: In Greek, "ποιμήν" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative (ποιμήν), genitive (ποιμένος), dative (ποιμένι), accusative (ποιμένα), and vocative (ποιμήν).

Description: "Ποιμήν" is the Greek word for "shepherd." In ancient times, shepherds played an important role in society as they were responsible for tending to and protecting flocks of sheep or goats. The term "ποιμήν" can also be used metaphorically to refer to someone who guides, leads, or takes care of others.
