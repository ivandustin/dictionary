Etymology: The word "πορευόμενοις" comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreuomai), which means "to go" or "to travel". The suffix "-μενοις" (-menois) indicates the present participle form, indicating that the action is ongoing or in progress.

Grammar: "Πορευόμενοις" is the present participle form of the verb "πορεύομαι" (poreuomai) in the masculine, plural, dative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. The dative case indicates the indirect object or the recipient of the action.

Description: "Πορευόμενοις" can be translated as "to those who are going" or "to those who are traveling" in English. It refers to a group of people who are currently in the process of going or traveling.
