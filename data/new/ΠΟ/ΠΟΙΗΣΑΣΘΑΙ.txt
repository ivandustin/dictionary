Etymology: The word "ποιησασθαι" comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to create". The suffix "-σασθαι" is a middle voice form indicating that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar: "ποιησασθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "ποιέω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on oneself.

Description: "ποιησασθαι" is an ancient Greek verb form that means "to make for oneself" or "to create for oneself". It implies an active involvement in the process of creation or making.
