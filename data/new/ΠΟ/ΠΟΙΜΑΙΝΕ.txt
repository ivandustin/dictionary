Etymology: The word "ποιμαίνε" (poimaine) comes from the Greek verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock." It is derived from the noun "ποιμήν" (poimen), meaning "shepherd."

Grammar: "Ποιμαίνε" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, specifically telling them to shepherd or tend to a flock.
