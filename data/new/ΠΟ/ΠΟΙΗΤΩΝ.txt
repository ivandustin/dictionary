Etymology: The word "ποιητών" (poiētōn) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to create." It is derived from the noun "ποίησις" (poiēsis), meaning "making" or "creation."

Grammar: "Ποιητών" is the plural form of the noun "ποιητής" (poiētēs), which means "poet" in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is of masculine gender.

Description: "Ποιητών" refers to a group of poets or individuals who engage in the art of poetry. In Greek literature and culture, poets were highly regarded for their ability to create and express themselves through verse.
