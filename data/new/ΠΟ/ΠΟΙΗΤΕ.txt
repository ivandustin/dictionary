Etymology: The word "ποιητής" (poiitis) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to create." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey- meaning "to make" or "to build."

Grammar: In Greek, "ποιητής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has a stem ending in -ητ-. The plural form is "ποιητές" (poiites).

Description: "Ποιητής" is the Greek word for "poet." It refers to a person who writes poetry or creates literary works in verse. In ancient Greece, poets held a significant role in society and were often seen as inspired individuals who could communicate with the gods through their art. Today, the term "ποιητής" is still used to describe someone who writes poetry or engages in creative writing.
