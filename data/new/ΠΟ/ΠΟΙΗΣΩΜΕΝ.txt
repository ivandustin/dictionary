Etymology: The word "ποιησωμεν" (poiēsōmen) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to create." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey- meaning "to make" or "to build."

Grammar: "ποιησωμεν" is the first person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "ποιέω" (poiéō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The subjunctive mood indicates possibility, potentiality, or uncertainty. The active voice indicates that the subject performs the action.

Description: "ποιησωμεν" is a verb form that expresses the idea of "let us make" or "let us create." It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to convey the sense of collaborative or collective action, inviting others to join in the act of creation.
