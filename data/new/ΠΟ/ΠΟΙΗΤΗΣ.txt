Etymology: The word "ποιητής" (poiitis) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to create." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey-.

Grammar: In Greek, "ποιητής" is a masculine noun of the first declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "ποιητές" (poiites).

Description: "Ποιητής" is the Greek word for "poet." It refers to a person who writes poetry or creates literary works in verse. In ancient Greece, poets held a significant role in society and were often seen as inspired individuals who could communicate with the gods through their art. Today, the term "ποιητής" is still used to describe someone who engages in poetic or creative writing.
