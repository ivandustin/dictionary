Etymology: The word "πολυτελούς" (polyteloús) comes from the Greek roots "πολύ" (polý), meaning "many" or "much," and "τέλος" (télos), meaning "end" or "goal." It originally referred to something that has reached its ultimate or highest level of excellence.

Grammar: In Greek, "πολυτελούς" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πολυτελούς" is an adjective that describes something as luxurious, extravagant, or of high quality. It is often used to refer to expensive or lavish items, experiences, or lifestyles.
