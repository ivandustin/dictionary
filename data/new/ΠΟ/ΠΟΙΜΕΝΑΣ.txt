Etymology: The word "ποιμένας" (poiménas) comes from the Greek word "ποιμήν" (poimḗn), which means "shepherd" or "herdsman". 

Grammar: "Ποιμένας" is a noun in the masculine gender and plural number. It is the nominative case form of the word. 

Description: "Ποιμένας" refers to shepherds or herdsman in Greek. It is used to describe those who take care of and guide sheep or other livestock. In a figurative sense, it can also refer to leaders or guides who take care of and protect a group of people.
