Etymology: The word "πορκίον" comes from the Latin word "porcium," which means "pig." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *porko-, meaning "young pig."

Grammar: In Greek, "πορκίον" is a neuter noun of the second declension. It has the plural form "πορκία" and the genitive form "πορκίου."
