Etymology: The word "ποιείς" (poieis) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to do". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey-.

Grammar: "Ποιείς" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "ποιέω" (poieo) in Greek. It is used when addressing one person and means "you make" or "you do".

Description: "Ποιείς" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of making or doing something. It is commonly used in various contexts to describe activities, actions, or creations.
