Etymology: The word "ποιμνιον" (poimnion) comes from the Greek word "ποιμήν" (poimēn), which means "shepherd". It is derived from the verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimainō), meaning "to tend or shepherd".

Grammar: "Ποιμνιον" is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "ποιμνιον". In the plural, it becomes "ποιμνια" (poimnia).

Description: "Ποιμνιον" refers to a flock or a herd of animals, typically sheep. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people or things that are under the care or guidance of someone.
