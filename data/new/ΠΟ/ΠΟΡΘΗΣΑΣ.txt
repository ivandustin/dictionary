Etymology: The word "πορθησας" (pronounced "por-thi-sas") comes from the Greek verb "πορθέω" (pronounced "por-the-o"), which means "to destroy" or "to ravage". 

Grammar: "Πορθησας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "πορθέω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Πορθησας" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having destroyed" or "having ravaged". It is often used to describe someone who has caused destruction or devastation.
