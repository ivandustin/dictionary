Etymology: The word "πολυλογία" comes from the Greek roots "πολύ" (poly), meaning "many," and "λόγος" (logos), meaning "word" or "speech." 

Grammar: "Πολυλογία" is a noun in the Greek language. It is feminine in gender and belongs to the first declension. 

Description: "Πολυλογία" refers to the act of speaking or writing excessively or using too many words. It can also describe someone who tends to be verbose or long-winded in their communication.
