Etymology: The word "ποιησαντι" (poiēsanti) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to create." The suffix "-σαντι" (-santi) is the aorist participle form of the verb, indicating past action.

Grammar: "Ποιησαντι" is the aorist participle form of the verb "ποιέω" (poiéō), which means "having made" or "having created." It is in the masculine singular form.

Description: "Ποιησαντι" is used to describe someone or something that has made or created something in the past. It can be used as an adjective or as a noun, depending on the context.
