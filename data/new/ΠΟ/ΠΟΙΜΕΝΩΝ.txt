Etymology: The word "ποιμένων" (poimenon) comes from the Greek verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock." It is derived from the noun "ποίμην" (poimen), meaning "shepherd."

Grammar: "Ποιμένων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "ποιμήν" (poimen), which means "shepherd" in Greek. In this form, it indicates possession or association with multiple shepherds.

Description: "Ποιμένων" is used to refer to a group of shepherds or individuals who tend to a flock of sheep or other animals. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who guides, protects, or takes care of a group of people.
