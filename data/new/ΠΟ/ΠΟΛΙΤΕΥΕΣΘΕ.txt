Etymology: The word "πολιτεύεσθε" (politeuesthe) comes from the Greek verb "πολιτεύομαι" (politeuomai), which means "to engage in politics" or "to be a citizen." It is derived from the noun "πόλις" (polis), meaning "city" or "state."

Grammar: "Πολιτεύεσθε" is the second person plural present middle/passive imperative form of the verb "πολιτεύομαι." In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.
