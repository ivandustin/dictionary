Etymology: The word "ποιμνης" (poimnēs) comes from the Greek word "ποιμήν" (poimēn), which means "shepherd". 

Grammar: "Ποιμνης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. 

Description: "Ποιμνης" refers to a shepherd, someone who tends to and takes care of sheep. In Greek mythology, shepherds were often associated with pastoral settings and were seen as guardians and protectors of their flocks. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who guides or leads others.
