Etymology: The word "ποτήριον" (potērion) comes from the Greek word "πίνω" (pinō), which means "to drink". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃-, meaning "to drink".

Grammar: In Greek, "ποτήριον" is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "ποτήρια" (potēria).

Description: "Ποτήριον" refers to a drinking vessel or a cup. It is typically used to hold and consume beverages, such as water, wine, or other liquids.
