Etymology: The word "ποιησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the past tense and active voice.

Grammar: "ποιησαντες" is the third person plural form of the aorist active participle of the verb "ποιέω". In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to modify nouns or function as verbs.

Description: "ποιησαντες" is a Greek word that means "having made" or "having done". It is used to describe a group of people who have completed an action in the past.
