Etymology: The word "πονηρών" comes from the Greek word "πονηρός" (ponērós), which means "evil" or "wicked".

Grammar: "Πονηρών" is the plural form of the adjective "πονηρός" (ponērós) in Greek. It is used to describe multiple evil or wicked things or people.

Description: "Πονηρών" is an adjective in Greek that is used to describe something or someone as evil, wicked, or morally corrupt. It can be used to describe various negative qualities or actions.
