Etymology: The word "ποσα" (posa) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "πόσος" (posos), meaning "how much" or "how many".

Grammar: "Ποσα" is a plural form of the Greek adjective "ποσός" (posos), which means "how much" or "how many". It is used to ask questions about quantity or number.

Description: "Ποσα" is commonly used in Greek to ask questions about the quantity or number of something. It can be used with both countable and uncountable nouns. For example, "Ποσα βιβλία έχεις;" (Posa vivlia ehis?) means "How many books do you have?", and "Ποσα νερά πίνεις;" (Posa nera pineis?) means "How much water do you drink?".
