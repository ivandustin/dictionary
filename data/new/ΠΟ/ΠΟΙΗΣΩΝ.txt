Etymology: The word "ποιήσων" (poiēsōn) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to create." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey-.

Grammar: "Ποιήσων" is the aorist active participle, masculine nominative singular form of the verb "ποιέω" (poiéō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Ποιήσων" is used to describe a male subject who has completed the action of making or creating something in the past. It can be translated as "having made" or "having created."
