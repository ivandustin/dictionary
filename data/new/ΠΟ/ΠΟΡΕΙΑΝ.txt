Etymology: The word "πορείαν" (poreian) comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreuomai), which means "to go" or "to travel."

Grammar: "Πορείαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Πορείαν" refers to the act of going or traveling. It can also be used to describe a journey, a course, or a route.
