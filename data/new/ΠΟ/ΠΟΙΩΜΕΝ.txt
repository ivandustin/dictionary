Etymology: The word "ποιώμεν" (poiomen) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to do" or "to make." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷeh₁-.

Grammar: "Ποιώμεν" is the present tense, first person plural form of the verb "ποιέω" (poieo). It is used to indicate that "we do" or "we make" something.

Description: "Ποιώμεν" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of doing or making something. It is used to describe the actions performed by a group of people, specifically the first person plural "we."
