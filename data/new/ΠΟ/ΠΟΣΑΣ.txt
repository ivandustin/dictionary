Etymology: The word "ποσας" (posas) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "πόσος" (posos), meaning "how much" or "how many".

Grammar: "Ποσας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the accusative case. It is used to ask about the quantity or number of something.

Description: "Ποσας" is commonly used in Greek to ask questions about quantity or number. It is often used in combination with other words to form questions such as "πόσα χρήματα;" (posa hrimata), meaning "how much money?" or "πόσα άτομα;" (posa atoma), meaning "how many people?".
