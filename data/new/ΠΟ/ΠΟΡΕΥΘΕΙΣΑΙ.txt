Etymology: The word "πορευθείσαι" comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreúomai), which means "to go" or "to travel". The suffix "-θεισα" (-theisa) is added to the verb stem to indicate the past tense and feminine gender.

Grammar: "Πορευθείσαι" is the third person singular, aorist tense, active voice, subjunctive mood form of the verb "πορεύομαι" (poreúomai). It is used to express an action that occurred in the past and is completed.

Description: "Πορευθείσαι" is a Greek verb form that means "she went" or "she traveled". It is used to describe a female subject who performed the action of going or traveling in the past.
