Etymology: The word "πορευθέντες" comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreúomai), which means "to go" or "to travel". The suffix "-θέντες" (-théntes) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of going or traveling has already taken place.

Grammar: "Πορευθέντες" is the masculine plural form of the verb "πορεύομαι" in the aorist tense. It is used to describe a group of people who have already gone or traveled.

Description: "Πορευθέντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having gone" or "having traveled". It is often used in a narrative context to indicate that a group of people has already completed a journey or a specific action.
