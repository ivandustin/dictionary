Etymology: The word "πολεμησαί" (polemesai) comes from the Greek verb "πολεμέω" (polemeo), which means "to wage war" or "to fight". 

Grammar: "πολεμησαί" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "πολεμέω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "πολεμησαί" is an infinitive verb form that expresses the idea of waging war or fighting. It can be used to describe the action of engaging in a battle or conflict.
