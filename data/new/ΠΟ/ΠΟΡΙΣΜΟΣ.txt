Etymology: The word "πορισμός" (porismos) comes from the Greek verb "πορίζω" (porizo), which means "to provide" or "to obtain". 

Grammar: "Πορισμός" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "πορισμού".

Description: "Πορισμός" refers to the act of obtaining or acquiring something, usually in a material or tangible sense. It can also be used to describe the process of providing or supplying something. In a broader sense, it can refer to the act of achieving or attaining a goal or objective.
