Etymology: The word "ποιούμεν" (poioumen) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to do" or "to make." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷeh₁-.

Grammar: "Ποιούμεν" is the present participle form of the verb "ποιέω" (poieo) in Greek. It is used to form the present continuous tense and indicates an ongoing action. In English, it can be translated as "doing" or "making."

Description: "Ποιούμεν" is a word that describes the act of doing or making something. It can refer to any kind of action or activity.
