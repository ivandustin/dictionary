Etymology: The word "ποιητής" (poiitis) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to create". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey- meaning "to make" or "to build".

Grammar: In Greek, "ποιητής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "ποιητές" (poiites).

Description: "Ποιητής" is the Greek word for "poet". It refers to a person who writes poetry or creates literary works in verse. In ancient Greece, poets were highly regarded and played an important role in society, often composing epic poems, hymns, and other forms of poetry. Today, the term "ποιητής" is still used to describe someone who engages in poetic writing.
