Etymology: The word "πονηροτερα" comes from the Greek word "πονηρός" (poniros), which means "evil" or "wicked". The suffix "-τερα" (-tera) is a comparative suffix, indicating that something is more evil or wicked than something else.

Grammar: "Πονηρότερα" is the feminine form of the comparative adjective "πονηρός" (poniros) in Greek. It is used to compare two or more things, indicating that one is more evil or wicked than the other(s).

Description: "Πονηρότερα" is an adjective that describes something as being more evil or wicked compared to something else. It is used to express a higher degree of evilness or wickedness.
