Etymology: The word "ποιμανατε" comes from the Greek verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock." It is derived from the noun "ποιμήν" (poimen), meaning "shepherd."

Grammar: "ποιμανατε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "ποιμαίνω" (poimaino). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to shepherd or tend to a flock.

Description: "ποιμανατε" is a Greek word that is used in the Bible, specifically in the New Testament. It appears in various passages, such as John 21:16, where Jesus instructs his disciples to "Feed my lambs." The word carries the connotation of caring for and guiding others, similar to how a shepherd takes care of his flock.
