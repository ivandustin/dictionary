Etymology: The word "ποτίζε" (potíze) comes from the Greek verb "ποτίζω" (potízo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate."

Grammar: "Ποτίζε" is the imperative form of the verb "ποτίζω" (potízo) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to water or irrigate something.

Description: "Ποτίζε" is a Greek word that specifically instructs someone to water or irrigate. It is commonly used in gardening or agricultural contexts, where it is important to provide water to plants or crops for their growth and nourishment.
