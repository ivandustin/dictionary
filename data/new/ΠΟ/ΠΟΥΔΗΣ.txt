Etymology: The word "πουδης" (poudēs) comes from the Greek word "πούς" (poús), meaning "foot". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*pṓds".

Grammar: "Πουδης" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "πουδη" (poudē). In the plural form, it becomes "ποδες" (podes).

Description: "Πουδης" refers to a foot, the lower extremity of the leg in humans and many animals. It is an essential part of the body used for walking, running, and maintaining balance.
