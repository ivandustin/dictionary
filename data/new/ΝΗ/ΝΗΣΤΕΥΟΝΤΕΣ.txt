Etymology: The word "νηστευοντες" comes from the Greek verb "νηστεύω" (nesteúo), which means "to fast" or "to abstain from food". The suffix "-οντες" (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently fasting.

Grammar: "Νηστευοντες" is the plural form of the word, used to refer to multiple people who are fasting. It is a present participle in the nominative case, indicating the subject of a sentence or clause.

Description: "Νηστευοντες" is a Greek word that specifically refers to individuals who are currently observing a period of fasting or abstaining from food. It is often used in religious contexts, such as during Lent in the Eastern Orthodox Church.
