Etymology: The word "νηφοντες" (nephontes) comes from the Greek verb "νήφω" (nepho), which means "to be sober" or "to be clear-headed."

Grammar: "Νηφοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "νήφω" (nepho) in Greek. It is used to describe a continuous action of being sober or clear-headed.

Description: "Νηφοντες" is a Greek word that describes the state of being sober or clear-headed. It can be used to refer to someone who is not under the influence of alcohol or drugs and is therefore able to think and act in a rational and controlled manner.
