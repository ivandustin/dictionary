Etymology: The word "νηστευτείς" (nisteuteis) comes from the Greek verb "νηστεύω" (nisteuo), which means "to fast" or "to abstain from food." The suffix "-τείς" (-teis) is added to the verb stem to form the adjective "νηστευτείς" (nisteuteis), meaning "fasting" or "abstaining from food."

Grammar: "Νηστευτείς" (nisteuteis) is the plural form of the adjective, used to describe multiple nouns. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the nouns they modify. Therefore, if the noun is masculine, feminine, or neuter, singular or plural, the adjective will change accordingly.

Description: "Νηστευτείς" (nisteuteis) refers to people who are fasting or abstaining from food, particularly in a religious or spiritual context. It can also be used to describe specific periods or practices of fasting.
