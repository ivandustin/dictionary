Etymology: The word "νηπιος" (nēpios) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *néh₂us, meaning "young" or "infant."

Grammar: In Greek, "νηπιος" (nēpios) is an adjective. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Νηπιος" (nēpios) means "young" or "infant" in Greek. It is often used to describe a child or a person who is inexperienced, naive, or lacking in knowledge or understanding.
