Etymology: The word "νηφαλιον" comes from the Greek word "νήφω" (nēphō), which means "to be sober" or "to be clear-headed".

Grammar: "Νηφαλιον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is classified as a neuter noun.

Description: "Νηφαλιον" refers to a state of sobriety or clear-headedness. It is used to describe a person who is not under the influence of alcohol or drugs and is mentally alert and aware.
