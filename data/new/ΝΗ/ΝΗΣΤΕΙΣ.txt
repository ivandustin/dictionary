Etymology: The word "νηστείς" (nisteis) comes from the Greek verb "νηστεύω" (nisteuo), which means "to fast" or "to abstain from food." 

Grammar: "Νηστείς" is the plural form of the adjective "νηστείς" (nisteis), which means "fasting" or "abstaining from food." It is used to describe multiple people who are observing a period of fasting.

Description: "Νηστείς" is a term used in Greek to refer to individuals who are observing a period of fasting, typically for religious or health reasons. During this time, they abstain from consuming certain foods or drinks.
