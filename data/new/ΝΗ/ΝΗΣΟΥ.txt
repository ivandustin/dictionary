Etymology: The word "νησού" (nēsoú) comes from the Greek word "νῆσος" (nêsos), which means "island". 

Grammar: "Νησού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Νησού" refers to the genitive form of the word "νῆσος" (nêsos), which means "island" in Greek. It is used to indicate that something belongs to or originates from an island.
