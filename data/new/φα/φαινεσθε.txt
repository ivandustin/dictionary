Word: φαινεσθε

Etymology:
The word φαινεσθε is derived from the Greek verb φαίνομαι (phainomai), which means "to appear" or "to seem". It is a passive form of the verb, indicating that the subject is being perceived or observed by someone else.

Grammar:
φαινεσθε is a second-person plural present passive indicative form of the verb φαίνομαι. In Greek, the passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The present tense indicates that the action is happening currently, and the plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
φαινεσθε can be translated to mean "you are being seen" or "you are appearing". It is often used in a figurative sense, to indicate that someone is presenting themselves in a certain way or giving a certain impression. In the context of Greek philosophy, the word is often used to describe the appearance or manifestation of something, such as an idea or a concept.
