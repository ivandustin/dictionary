Word: φανερωθωσιν

Etymology: 
The word φανερωθωσιν is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and subjunctive mood. It is derived from the root word φανερός (phaneros), which means "visible" or "manifest." The suffix -θωσιν (-thōsin) is added to the root word to indicate a passive action, meaning "to be made visible" or "to be revealed."

Grammar:
φανερωθωσιν is a third-person plural verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and subjunctive mood. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations.

φανερωθωσιν can be translated to mean "that they may be revealed" or "that they may become visible."
