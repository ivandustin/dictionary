Word: φασκόντες

Etymology: The word φασκόντες is derived from the Greek verb φάσκω (phasko), which means "to say" or "to declare". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently doing the action of saying or declaring.

Grammar: φασκόντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe people who are currently saying or declaring something.

Description: φασκόντες is a word that describes people who are currently making a statement or declaration. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a legal or political setting, or in everyday conversation. The word emphasizes the ongoing nature of the action, suggesting that the people in question are actively engaged in making their statement or declaration.
