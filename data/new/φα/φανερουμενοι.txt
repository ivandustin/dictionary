Word: φανερουμενοι

Etymology:
The word φανερουμενοι is a present participle form of the Greek verb φανερόω (phaneroó), which means "to make visible" or "to reveal". The word comes from the root word φανερός (phanerós), which means "visible" or "manifest".

Grammar:
φανερουμενοι is a present participle form of the verb φανερόω (phaneroó) in the masculine plural nominative case. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently making something visible or revealing something.

Description:
φανερουμενοι can be translated to "those who are being revealed" or "those who are making something visible". It is often used in a religious context to describe individuals who are being revealed as followers of a particular faith or as messengers of a divine message. In a broader sense, it can also be used to describe individuals who are making something previously hidden or unknown visible to others.
