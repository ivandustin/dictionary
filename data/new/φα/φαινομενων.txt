Word: φαινομενων

Etymology: 
The word φαινομενων is the genitive plural form of the Greek word φαινόμενος (phainómenos), which is the present participle of the verb φαίνω (phaínō), meaning "to appear" or "to seem". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰeh₂i- ("to shine, appear").

Grammar:
φαινομενων is a genitive plural form of the noun φαινόμενος (phainómenos), which is a present participle. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. The plural form indicates that there are multiple things being possessed or related to.

Description:
φαινομενων can be translated to mean "of the appearing things" or "of the things that seem". It is often used in philosophical and scientific contexts to refer to the observable phenomena or appearances of the world. The word is commonly used in the title of works, such as Aristotle's "Physics: On the Heavens and On Generation and Corruption" (Φυσικῶν βιβλίων Αʹ: Περὶ οὐρανοῦ καὶ κόσμου καὶ Περὶ γενέσεως καὶ φθορᾶς, "Physikōn biblíōn A': Perì ouranoû kaì kósmou kaì Perì genéseōs kaì phthorâs"), which translates to "Books of Physics: On the Heavens and the World and On Generation and Corruption of Things That Appear".
