Word: φαγω

Etymology:
The word φαγω (phagō) comes from the Greek verb φαγεῖν (phagein), which means "to eat." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bhag-, which means "to divide, apportion, eat."

Grammar:
φαγω (phagō) is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the Greek verb φαγεῖν (phagein). It is used to indicate that the speaker is currently eating.

Description:
φαγω (phagō) is a Greek verb that means "I eat." It is a present tense verb, indicating that the action is happening currently. The verb can be conjugated to match the subject of the sentence, such as "you eat" (φαγείς, phageis) or "they eat" (φαγοῦσιν, phagousin). The verb is commonly used in everyday conversation and is an essential part of the Greek language.
