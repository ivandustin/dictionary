Word: φανερα

Etymology:
The word φανερα comes from the Greek word φανερός (phanerós), which means "visible" or "apparent". It is derived from the verb φαίνω (phaínō), which means "to show" or "to appear".

Grammar:
φανερα is the feminine form of the adjective φανερός (phanerós), which means "visible" or "apparent". It is used to describe something that is easily seen or understood. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, if the noun is feminine, like in the case of φανερα, the adjective must also be in the feminine form.

Description:
φανερα is an adjective that is commonly used in Greek to describe something that is visible or apparent. It can be used to describe physical objects, emotions, or ideas. For example, one might say "Η φωτιά ήταν φανερή" (I fotia itan faneri), which means "The fire was visible". It can also be used to describe emotions, such as "Η χαρά της ήταν φανερή" (I hara tis itan faneri), which means "Her joy was apparent". Overall, φανερα is a versatile adjective that is used to describe a wide range of things in Greek.
