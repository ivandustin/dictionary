Word: φαινων

Etymology:
The word φαινων is derived from the Greek verb φαίνω (phaino), which means "to appear" or "to shine". It is a present participle form of the verb, which means "appearing" or "shining".

Grammar:
φαινων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a present participle, which means it is used to describe an ongoing action or state. In this case, it describes someone or something that is appearing or shining.

Description:
φαινων can be translated to "shining" or "appearing". It is often used in Greek mythology to describe various characters, such as the god Apollo, who is often referred to as "Phoebus Apollo" or "Apollo the Shining One". The word can also be used in a more literal sense to describe something that is physically shining or appearing, such as a star or a sunrise.
