The word "φατνης" (phatnēs) is a Greek noun that means "manger" or "feeding trough." It is derived from the verb "φατνοω" (phatnoō), which means "to feed in a manger." In terms of grammar, "φατνης" is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases and numbers, such as "φατνη" (phatnē) in the accusative singular form. The word is often used in the context of the Nativity story in the Bible, where Jesus is said to have been born in a manger.
