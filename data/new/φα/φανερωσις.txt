Word: φανερωσις

Etymology: 
The word φανερωσις comes from the Greek verb φανερόω (phaneroó), which means "to make visible" or "to reveal". The suffix -σις (-sis) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the action or process of the verb. Therefore, φανερωσις means "the act of revealing" or "the process of making something visible".

Grammar:
In Greek, φανερωσις is a feminine noun of the third declension. It is spelled with the Greek letters φανερώσις and is pronounced as "fah-neh-ROH-sis". 

Description:
φανερωσις is a term used in Christian theology to refer to the revelation of God to humanity. It is often translated as "manifestation" or "revelation". In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the revelation of Jesus Christ to the world. In philosophy, φανερωσις is used to describe the process of revealing or making something known that was previously hidden or unknown.
