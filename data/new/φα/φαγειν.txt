Word: φαγειν

Etymology:
The word φαγειν (phagein) is derived from the Greek verb φαγεῖν (phagein), which means "to eat." It is a common verb in the Greek language and is used in various contexts.

Grammar:
φαγειν (phagein) is an infinitive verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
φαγειν (phagein) is a simple verb that means "to eat" in Greek. It is used in various contexts, such as in everyday conversation, literature, and religious texts. The verb is often conjugated to match the subject and the tense of the sentence. For example, "I ate" would be translated as "ἔφαγον" (ephagon) in the past tense, while "he will eat" would be translated as "φάγει" (phagei) in the future tense.
