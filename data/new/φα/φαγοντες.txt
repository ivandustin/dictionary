Word: φαγόντες

Etymology: φαγόντες is the present participle of the Greek verb φάγω (phagó), which means "to eat". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bhag-, which also gave rise to the English word "food".

Grammar: φαγόντες is a masculine plural noun in the nominative case. It is the present participle of the verb φάγω (phagó), which means "to eat". As a participle, it functions as an adjective and describes the subject of the sentence as "eating".

Description: φαγόντες is a Greek word that means "eating". It is often used to describe a group of people who are currently eating or who have just finished eating. The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe someone who is consuming or devouring something, such as knowledge or power.
