Word: φασιν

Etymology:
The word φασιν (phasin) is a present tense form of the Greek verb φημί (phēmi), which means "to say" or "to speak." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bheh₂- which means "to speak" or "to say."

Grammar:
φασιν is a present tense, third person plural form of the verb φημί. It is used to indicate that a group of people are saying or speaking something. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number.

Description:
φασιν is a common Greek verb that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to report what someone else has said or to express an opinion. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου λένε ότι θα έρθουν στο πάρτι" (My friends say they will come to the party). The verb can also be used to express a belief or a fact, as in "Φασιν ότι ο ήλιος ανατέλλει στην ανατολή" (It is said that the sun rises in the east).
