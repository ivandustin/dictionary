Word: φανωσιν

Etymology: φανωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb φαίνω (phaino), which means "to show" or "to appear". The suffix -ωσιν (-osin) is a third person plural present subjunctive ending, indicating that the word is in the subjunctive mood and refers to a hypothetical action or state.

Grammar: φανωσιν is a third person plural present subjunctive form of the verb φαίνω (phaino). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now or is ongoing.

Description: φανωσιν can be translated as "they may appear" or "they might show". It suggests a hypothetical situation where something or someone may become visible or known. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "If they come out of hiding, they may appear to be innocent" or "The truth may show itself in unexpected ways".
