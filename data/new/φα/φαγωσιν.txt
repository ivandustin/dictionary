Word: φαγωσιν

Etymology: φαγωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb φαγεῖν (phagein), which means "to eat."

Grammar: φαγωσιν is a third-person plural aorist active subjunctive form of the verb φαγεῖν (phagein). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations.

Description: φαγωσιν is a verb form that can be translated as "they might eat" or "let them eat." It is often used in Greek literature and scripture to express a hypothetical or potential action.
