Word: φανερουμενον

Etymology: 
The word φανερουμενον is a present participle form of the Greek verb φανερόω (phaneroó), which means "to make visible, to reveal, to manifest". The root of the word is φανερός (phanerós), which means "visible, apparent, clear". 

Grammar: 
φανερουμενον is a present participle in the passive voice, masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb φανερόω (phaneroó), which is in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. 

Description: 
φανερουμενον describes something that is currently being revealed or made visible. It can also be translated as "being made known" or "being disclosed". The word is often used in a theological context to describe the revelation of God's will or the manifestation of divine truth.
