Word: φανησεται

Etymology:
The word φανησεται is a conjugated form of the Greek verb φαίνομαι (phainomai), which means "to appear" or "to seem". It comes from the root word φαίνω (phaino), which means "to shine" or "to bring to light".

Grammar:
φανησεται is a third-person singular future indicative form of the verb φαίνομαι. In Greek, verbs are highly inflected, meaning that their forms change depending on tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The future indicative tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future.

Description:
φανησεται can be translated to English as "he/she/it will appear" or "he/she/it will seem". It is a common verb in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the appearance of a person or object, or expressing an opinion or belief about something.
