Word: φαρισαῖοις (pharisaois)

Etymology:
The word φαρισαῖοις (pharisaois) is derived from the Greek word φαρισαῖος (pharisaios), which means "separated" or "set apart." The Pharisees were a Jewish sect that emerged during the Second Temple period and were known for their strict adherence to Jewish law and traditions.

Grammar:
φαρισαῖοις (pharisaois) is a noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
φαρισαῖοις (pharisaois) refers specifically to the Pharisees, a Jewish sect that emerged during the Second Temple period. The Pharisees were known for their strict adherence to Jewish law and traditions, and were often in conflict with Jesus and his followers in the New Testament. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to anyone who is overly concerned with religious legalism or adherence to tradition.
