Word: φαγεσαι

The word φαγεσαι is a verb form in the aorist tense of the Greek verb φαγεῖν (phagein), which means "to eat". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bhag-, which means "to share out, apportion, distribute". The word has been used in Greek since ancient times and is still used in modern Greek.

φαγεσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb φαγεῖν (phagein). The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. Therefore, φαγεσαι can be translated as "you (singular) ate" or "eat!".

φαγεσαι is a verb form that commands someone to eat.
