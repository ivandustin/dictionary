Etymology: The word "φαίνω" (phaino) comes from the ancient Greek root "φαίνειν" (phainein), which means "to show" or "to make visible". 

Grammar: "Φαινει" (phainei) is a present tense verb in the indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows: 

- Singular: φαινει (phainei) 
- Plural: φαινουν (phainoun) 

Description: The verb "φαινει" (phainei) is used to describe something that appears or seems to be a certain way. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the appearance of a person, the weather, or a situation.
