Word: φανερουται

Etymology: The word φανερουται comes from the Greek verb φανερόω (phaneroo), which means "to make visible" or "to reveal". It is derived from the noun φάνος (phanos), which means "light" or "brightness".

Grammar: φανερουται is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is a passive verb, meaning that the subject is receiving the action of the verb. In English, it can be translated as "is revealed" or "becomes visible".

Description: φανερουται is a Greek verb that describes the act of something becoming visible or being revealed. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when something that was previously hidden becomes visible, or in a figurative sense, such as when a truth or secret is revealed. It is often used in the context of religious or spiritual teachings, where it can refer to the revelation of divine truths or the manifestation of spiritual realities.
