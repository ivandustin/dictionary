Word: φαγος

Etymology:
The word φαγος comes from the Greek word "φαγεῖν" (phagein), which means "to eat". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "bhag-", which means "to share out, apportion, distribute".

Dative: τῷ φαγῷ (to phago)

Vocative: φαγέ (phage)

Description:
φαγος is a Greek word that means "eater" or "glutton". It is often used to describe someone who eats excessively or greedily. In Greek mythology, there is a character named φαγος who is known for his insatiable appetite. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe anything that consumes or devours, such as a fire or a disease.
