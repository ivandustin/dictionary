Word: φανερωθηναι

Etymology: φανερωθηναι is a Greek word that comes from the verb φανερόω (phaneroó), which means "to make visible, to reveal, to manifest." The suffix -θηναι (-thenai) is a passive aorist infinitive ending, indicating that the action of the verb was done to the subject.

Grammar: φανερωθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive form of the verb φανερόω (phaneroó). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: φανερωθηναι can be translated as "to be revealed, to be made known, to be manifested." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the revelation of divine truths or the manifestation of God's power. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the revelation of Jesus Christ to his disciples and the manifestation of his glory.
