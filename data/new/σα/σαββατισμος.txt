Word: σαββατισμος

Etymology: The word σαββατισμος comes from the Greek word σάββατο (sabbato), which means "Sabbath" or "day of rest". The suffix -ισμος (-ismos) is added to indicate the concept or practice of observing the Sabbath.

Grammar: σαββατισμος is a masculine noun in Greek. It is declined like other second declension nouns, with the genitive form being σαββατισμού (sabbatismou).

Description: σαββατισμος refers to the practice of observing the Sabbath, which is a day of rest and worship in many religions, including Judaism and Christianity. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the observance of the Sabbath as a religious duty. The concept of σαββατισμος is also related to the idea of keeping holy days and observing religious traditions.
