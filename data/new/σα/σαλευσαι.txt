Word: σαλεύσαι

Etymology: σαλεύσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σαλεύω (saleuo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". 

Grammar: σαλεύσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb σαλεύω. This means that it is a command directed towards one person to perform the action of shaking or moving back and forth. 

Description: σαλεύσαι is a verb that is often used in the context of physical movement or shaking. It can also be used metaphorically to describe emotional or mental agitation. In the imperative form, it is a direct command to someone to shake or move back and forth.
