In terms of grammar, "σαταναι" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels and the Book of Revelation.

Description-wise, "σαταναι" is often used to describe evil spirits or demons in Christian theology. It is also used to refer to individuals who are seen as adversaries or enemies. In some contexts, it can also be used to describe temptation or sin.
