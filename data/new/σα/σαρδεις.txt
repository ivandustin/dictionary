Word: σαρδεις

Sardis was an ancient city located in western Anatolia, in what is now Turkey. It was the capital of the ancient kingdom of Lydia and was known for its wealth and power. The city was situated on a hill and was surrounded by a defensive wall. Sardis was an important center for trade and commerce, and it was also known for its textiles and dyeing industry.
