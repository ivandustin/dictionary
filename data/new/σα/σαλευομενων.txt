Word: σαλευομενων

Etymology:
The word σαλευομενων is a present participle form of the Greek verb σαλεύω (saleuo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". The word is derived from the root word σαλος (salos), which means "tossing" or "agitation".

Grammar:
σαλευομενων is a present participle in the passive voice, which means that the subject is being acted upon. The word is in the masculine, plural, genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship to another noun in the sentence.

The word σαλευομενων is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Hebrews 12:27. The verse reads, "And this word, Yet once more, signifieth the removing of those things that are shaken, as of things that are made, that those things which cannot be shaken may remain."
