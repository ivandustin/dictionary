Word: σαββατα

Etymology: The word σαββατα (sabbata) comes from the Hebrew word שַׁבָּת (shabbat), which means "rest" or "cessation". It was adopted into Greek during the Hellenistic period.

Singular: N/A
Plural: Σάββατα (Sabbata)

Description: σαββατα is the Greek word for "Sabbath", which refers to the day of rest and worship in Judaism and Christianity. In the New Testament, it is often used to refer to the weekly day of rest observed by Jews and early Christians. It is also used metaphorically to refer to a time of rest or a period of time set aside for a specific purpose.
