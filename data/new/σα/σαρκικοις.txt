Word: σαρκικοις

Etymology: The word σαρκικοις comes from the Greek word σάρξ (sárks), which means "flesh". The suffix -ικος (-ikos) is used to form adjectives, and in this case, it means "related to" or "pertaining to".

Grammar: σαρκικοις is a plural dative form of the adjective σαρκικός (sarkikos), which means "fleshy" or "carnal". In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: σαρκικοις is used to describe something that is related to or characterized by flesh. It can refer to physical attributes, such as fleshy tissue or muscles, or to more abstract concepts, such as carnal desires or worldly pleasures. The word is often used in a negative sense, suggesting a focus on the material world rather than the spiritual or intellectual.
