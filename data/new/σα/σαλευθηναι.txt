Word: σαλευθηναι

Etymology:
The word σαλευθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σαλεύω (saleuo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth." The suffix -θηναι (-thenai) is a passive aorist infinitive ending, indicating that the action of shaking or moving back and forth is being done to the subject of the verb.

Grammar:
σαλευθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive verb in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "to be shaken" or "to have been shaken." The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
σαλευθηναι is a verb that describes the action of being shaken or moved back and forth. It can be used in a literal sense, such as describing the movement of an object in an earthquake or the shaking of a tree in the wind. It can also be used figuratively, to describe a person's emotional state or the instability of a situation.
