Word: σαρκινος

Etymology:
The word σαρκινος comes from the Greek word σαρκίνη (sarkine), which means "fleshy" or "of the flesh". It is derived from the Greek word σάρξ (sarx), which means "flesh".

Grammar:
σαρκινος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case in Greek. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
σαρκινος is used to describe something that is fleshy or meaty. It can also be used to describe something that is related to or made of flesh. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a person who is overweight or has a lot of flesh on their body.
