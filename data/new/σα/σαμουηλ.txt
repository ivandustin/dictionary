Word: σαμουηλ

Etymology:
The name Samuel is of Hebrew origin and is derived from the Hebrew name Shemu'el, which means "heard of God" or "asked of God". In the Old Testament, Samuel was a prophet and judge who anointed Saul as the first king of Israel and later anointed David as his successor.

Grammar:
In Greek, σαμουηλ (Samouēl) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is spelled with the Greek letters sigma (σ), alpha (α), mu (μ), omicron (ο), upsilon (υ), epsilon (η), and lambda (λ). 

Description:
Samuel is a name that has been used for centuries and is still popular today. It is often given to boys and has a strong biblical association. In the Bible, Samuel is known for his devotion to God and his prophetic abilities. He is also remembered for anointing the first two kings of Israel, Saul and David. The name Samuel has a timeless quality and is often chosen for its meaning and significance.
