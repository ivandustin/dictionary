Word: σαμαρειταις

Etymology:
The word σαμαρειταις (samareitais) is a Greek word that is derived from the name of a region in ancient Palestine called Samaria. The people who lived in this region were known as Samaritans.

Grammar:
σαμαρειταις is a plural noun in the dative case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
σαμαρειταις refers to the Samaritans, a group of people who lived in the region of Samaria in ancient Palestine. The Samaritans were a distinct ethnic and religious group who had their own version of the Torah and their own temple on Mount Gerizim. They were often at odds with the Jews, who considered them to be heretics. In the New Testament, the story of the Good Samaritan is a well-known parable that highlights the compassion and kindness of a Samaritan towards a stranger in need.
