Word: σακκος

Etymology:
The word σακκος (sakkos) is derived from the Hebrew word "sak" which means "sackcloth". It was originally used in the context of mourning or repentance, and was made from coarse, dark-colored cloth.

Grammar:
In Greek, σακκος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is σακκος, and its genitive singular form is σακκου. The word is also used in the plural form, σακκοι (sakkoí).

Description:
In modern Greek, σακκος refers to a bag or sack made of cloth or other material. It can be used to carry various items, such as groceries or personal belongings. In ancient times, the word was used to refer to a type of garment made from sackcloth, which was worn as a sign of mourning or repentance. The word is also used in the Bible to describe the clothing worn by prophets and ascetics.
