The word "σαρκιναις" (sarkinais) is a Greek adjective that means "fleshy" or "meaty". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek noun "σάρξ" (sárks), which means "flesh" or "meat". The suffix "-ιναις" (-inais) is a feminine plural ending used to form adjectives.

Grammar: "Σαρκιναις" is the feminine plural form of the adjective. It agrees in gender and number with the noun it modifies.

Description: The word "σαρκιναις" is often used to describe food that is rich in meat or flesh, such as a hearty stew or a juicy steak. It can also be used figuratively to describe something that is substantial or weighty.
