Word: σαλευθω

Etymology:
The word σαλευθω (saleutho) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σαλος (salos), which means "tossing" or "agitation." The verb σαλευθω (saleutho) means "to shake" or "to move back and forth."

σαλευθω (saleutho) is a first-person singular present passive indicative form of the verb σαλευω (saleuo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth." The verb is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

σαλευθω (saleutho) is a Greek verb that describes a shaking or moving back and forth motion. It can be used in a literal sense, such as shaking a tree or a person shaking with fear, or in a figurative sense, such as shaking someone's confidence or beliefs.
