Word: σαρκος

Etymology:
The word σαρκος (sarkos) is derived from the Greek word σάρξ (sárks), which means "flesh" or "meat". It is related to the Latin word "caro", which has a similar meaning.

Grammar:
In Greek, σαρκος is a noun in the genitive case. It is often used in conjunction with other words to describe the type of flesh or meat being referred to.

Description:
σαρκος is a term used to refer to flesh or meat, particularly that of animals. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in cooking or in religious or cultural practices. In medical terminology, it is often used to refer to the soft tissue of the body.
