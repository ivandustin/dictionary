Word: σαββατου

Etymology: The word σαββατου (sabbatou) is derived from the Hebrew word "shabbat" which means "rest" or "cessation". It was adopted into Greek during the Hellenistic period and was used to refer to the Jewish Sabbath day.

Grammar: σαββατου is a genitive singular form of the noun σάββατον (sabbaton) which means "Sabbath day" or "week". In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, σαββατου indicates that the noun it modifies is related to or belongs to the Sabbath day.

Description: σαββατου is a Greek word that is primarily used in the New Testament to refer to the Jewish Sabbath day. It is used in the Gospels to describe the day on which Jesus was crucified and buried, and in the book of Acts to describe the day on which the early Christians gathered for worship. The word can also be used to refer to a week, as in the phrase "σαββατου κατα σαββατον" (sabbaton kata sabbaton) which means "week after week".
