Etymology: The word "λιθαζετε" (lithazete) comes from the Greek word "λίθος" (lithos), meaning "stone," and the verb "αζέτε" (azete), meaning "to throw." It literally translates to "to throw stones."

Grammar: "Λιθαζετε" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and second person plural form. It is used to describe the action of throwing stones.

Description: "Λιθαζετε" is a Greek verb that specifically refers to the act of throwing stones. It can be used in a literal sense to describe physically throwing stones, or in a figurative sense to describe verbally attacking or criticizing someone.
