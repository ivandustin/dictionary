Etymology: The word "λιμνη" (limnē) comes from the Ancient Greek word "λίμνη" (límnē), which means "lake" or "pond".

Grammar: In Greek, "λιμνη" (limnē) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has the genitive form "λίμνης" (límnēs).

Description: "Λιμνη" (limnē) refers to a body of water that is smaller than a sea but larger than a pond. It is typically a natural or artificial lake.
