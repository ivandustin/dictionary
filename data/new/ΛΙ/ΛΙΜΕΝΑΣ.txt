Etymology: The word "λιμένας" (liménas) comes from the Ancient Greek word "λιμήν" (limḗn), which means "harbor" or "port".

Grammar: In Greek, "λιμένας" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "λιμένες" (liménes).

Description: "Λιμένας" is a Greek word that refers to a harbor or port, which is a place where ships can dock and load or unload cargo. It is an important location for trade and transportation, connecting land and sea routes. Harbors can be natural or man-made and are often protected by breakwaters or piers.
