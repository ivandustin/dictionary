Etymology: The word "λιθασωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "λιθάζω" (lithazo), which means "to stone" or "to pelt with stones". It is derived from the noun "λίθος" (lithos), meaning "stone".

Grammar: "Λιθασωσιν" is the third person plural, aorist active subjunctive form of the verb "λιθάζω" (lithazo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The subjunctive mood indicates possibility, potentiality, or hypothetical action.

Description: "Λιθασωσιν" is a verb form that can be translated as "they might stone" or "they could stone" in English. It suggests a hypothetical or potential action of stoning performed by a third person plural subject.
