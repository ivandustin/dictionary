Etymology: The word "λιμώι" (limōi) comes from the Greek word "λιμός" (limos), which means "hunger" or "famine".

Grammar: "Λιμώι" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the neuter gender.

Description: "Λιμώι" refers to the state of extreme hunger or famine. It describes a condition where there is a severe lack of food and people suffer from hunger and starvation.
