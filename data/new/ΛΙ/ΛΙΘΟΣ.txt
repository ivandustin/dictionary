Etymology: The word "λιθος" (lithos) comes from the Greek language and means "stone".

Grammar: In Greek, "λιθος" (lithos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Λιθος" (lithos) refers to a solid mineral substance that is naturally occurring and has a characteristic atomic structure. It is commonly used to describe rocks, stones, or pebbles. In a broader sense, it can also refer to any hard, non-metallic material.
