Etymology: The word "λιθιναίς" (lithinais) comes from the Greek word "λίθος" (lithos), meaning "stone". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "λίθινος" (lithinos), which means "made of stone" or "stone-like".

Grammar: "Λιθιναίς" is the feminine form of the adjective "λιθινός" (lithinos) in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this case, it is in the feminine plural form.

Description: "Λιθιναίς" is an adjective in Greek that describes something as being made of stone or having the characteristics of stone. It can be used to describe various objects or materials that are composed of or resemble stone.
