Etymology: The word "λιθούς" (lithous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "λίθος" (lithos), meaning "stone".

Grammar: In Greek, "λιθούς" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular number.

Description: "Λιθούς" is the Greek word for "stone". It refers to a solid, naturally occurring substance composed of minerals. Stones are commonly used in construction, as decorative elements, and for various other purposes.
