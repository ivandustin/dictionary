Etymology: The word "λιθιναί" (lithinai) comes from the Greek word "λίθος" (lithos), meaning "stone". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leit(h)-, which means "to throw" or "to cast". 

Grammar: "Λιθιναί" is the plural form of the adjective "λιθινός" (lithinos), which means "made of stone" or "stone-like". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "λιθιναί" is used to describe plural feminine nouns.

Description: "Λιθιναί" is used to describe multiple feminine nouns that are made of stone or have characteristics similar to stone. It can be used to describe various objects, structures, or materials that are composed of or resemble stone.
