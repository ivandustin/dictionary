Etymology: The word "λιμένα" (liména) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "λιμήν" (limḗn), which means "harbor" or "port".

Grammar: "Λιμένα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "λιμένας" (liménas).

Description: "Λιμένα" refers to a harbor or port, which is a place where ships can dock, load, and unload cargo. It is a crucial location for maritime trade and transportation. In addition to commercial purposes, harbors also serve as a shelter for boats and provide facilities for various maritime activities.
