Etymology: The word "σκιαν" (skian) comes from the Greek word "σκιά" (skia), meaning "shadow". 

Grammar: "Σκιαν" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Σκιαν" refers to a shadow, which is a dark area or shape produced by an object blocking the light. Shadows are formed when light is obstructed by an opaque object, creating an area of darkness on the opposite side. Shadows can vary in size, shape, and intensity depending on the angle and intensity of the light source.
