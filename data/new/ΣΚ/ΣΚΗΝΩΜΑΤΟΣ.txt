Etymology: The word "σκηνωματος" (skēnōmatos) comes from the Greek word "σκηνή" (skēnē), meaning "tent" or "stage". It is derived from the verb "σκηνόω" (skēnoō), which means "to set up a tent" or "to prepare a stage".

Grammar: "Σκηνωματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Σκηνωματος" refers to something that is related to or belongs to a tent or stage. It can be used to describe various aspects of theatrical productions, such as the set, scenery, props, or stage design.
