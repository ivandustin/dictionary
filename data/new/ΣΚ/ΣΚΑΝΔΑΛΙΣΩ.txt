Etymology: The word "σκανδαλίζω" (skandalízo) comes from the Greek root "σκάνδαλο" (skándalo), which means "scandal" or "offense". 

Grammar: "Σκανδαλίζω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ σκανδαλίζω (egó skandalízo) - I scandalize, εσύ σκανδαλίζεις (esí skandalízeis) - you scandalize, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό σκανδαλίζει (aftós/aftí/aftó skandalízei) - he/she/it scandalizes, εμείς σκανδαλίζουμε (emís skandalízoume) - we scandalize, εσείς σκανδαλίζετε (esís skandalízete) - you scandalize, αυτοί/αυτές σκανδαλίζουν (aftí/aftés skandalízoun) - they scandalize.

Description: "Σκανδαλίζω" is a verb that means "to scandalize" or "to cause offense" in Greek. It refers to the act of causing shock, outrage, or moral offense to others through one's words, actions, or behavior.
