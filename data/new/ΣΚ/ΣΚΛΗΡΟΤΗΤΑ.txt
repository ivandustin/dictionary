Etymology: The word "σκληροτητα" (sklērotēta) comes from the Greek root "σκληρός" (sklēros), meaning "hard" or "difficult," and the suffix "-τητα" (-tēta), which denotes a state or quality. 

Grammar: "Σκληροτητα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Σκληροτητα" refers to the quality or state of being hard or difficult. It can be used to describe physical hardness, as well as metaphorical hardness or difficulty in various contexts.
