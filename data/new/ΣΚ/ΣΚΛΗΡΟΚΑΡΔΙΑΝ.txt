Etymology: The word "σκληροκαρδιαν" comes from the Greek words "σκληρός" (skliros) meaning "hard" and "καρδιά" (kardia) meaning "heart". 

Grammar: "Σκληροκαρδιαν" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is used to describe someone who has a hard or unfeeling heart.

Description: "Σκληροκαρδιαν" is a term used to describe a person who lacks compassion, empathy, or sympathy towards others. It refers to someone who is cold-hearted, unkind, or indifferent.
