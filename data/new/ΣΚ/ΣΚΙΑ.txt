Etymology: The word "σκια" (skia) comes from the Ancient Greek word "σκιά" (skia), meaning "shadow".

Grammar: In Greek, "σκια" (skia) is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows:

- Singular: η σκιά (i skia) - the shadow
- Plural: οι σκιές (i skies) - the shadows

Description: "Σκια" (skia) refers to the dark area that is created when an object blocks the light. It is the absence of light caused by an object obstructing the path of light rays. Shadows are formed when an object is placed in front of a light source, and they change in size and shape depending on the position of the object and the direction of the light. Shadows play an important role in art, photography, and various scientific fields.
