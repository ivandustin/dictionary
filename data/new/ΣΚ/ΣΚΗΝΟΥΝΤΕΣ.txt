Etymology: The word "σκηνούντες" comes from the Greek verb "σκηνόω" (skēnoō), which means "to set up a tent" or "to pitch a tent." It is derived from the noun "σκηνή" (skēnē), meaning "tent" or "shelter."

Grammar: "Σκηνούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "σκηνόω" in the Greek language. It is used to describe an ongoing action of setting up a tent or pitching tents. In this form, it agrees with the subject in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Σκηνούντες" is a word used to describe individuals or a group of people who are currently setting up tents or pitching tents. It indicates an ongoing action in the present tense.
