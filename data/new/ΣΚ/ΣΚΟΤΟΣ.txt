Etymology: The word "σκοτος" (skotos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *skot-, meaning "darkness" or "shadow."

Grammar: In Greek, "σκοτος" (skotos) is a noun of neuter gender. It is in the nominative case, singular form. It can also be declined in other cases and numbers, such as the genitive case "σκοτου" (skotou) or the plural form "σκοτα" (skota).

Description: "Σκοτος" (skotos) means "darkness" in Greek. It refers to the absence or lack of light, resulting in a state of being dark. It can be used both literally and metaphorically to describe the absence of light or knowledge, as well as a state of gloom or obscurity.
