Etymology: The word "σκοτεινόν" (skoteinón) comes from the Greek adjective "σκοτεινός" (skoteinós), which means "dark" or "obscure". It is derived from the noun "σκότος" (skótos), meaning "darkness".

Grammar: "Σκοτεινόν" is the neuter form of the adjective "σκοτεινός" in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The neuter form is used when the adjective refers to a neuter noun.

Description: "Σκοτεινόν" is an adjective in Greek that describes something as dark or obscure. It can be used to describe the physical absence of light or metaphorically to describe something that is mysterious, hidden, or difficult to understand.
