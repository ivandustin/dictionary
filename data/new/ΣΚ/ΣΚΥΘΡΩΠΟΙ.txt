Etymology: The word "σκυθρωποι" (skuthropoi) comes from the Greek roots "σκυθρος" (skuthros), meaning "dark" or "gloomy," and "ποιεω" (poieo), meaning "to make." It literally translates to "those who make dark or gloomy."

Grammar: "Σκυθρωποι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is of masculine gender. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "σκυθρωποι."

Description: "Σκυθρωποι" is an adjective used to describe someone or something as dark, gloomy, or somber in appearance or demeanor. It can be used to describe a person's facial expression, clothing, or overall atmosphere.
