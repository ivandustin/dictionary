Etymology: The word "σκηνοπηγία" comes from the Greek roots "σκηνή" (skini), meaning "scene" or "stage", and "πηγή" (piyi), meaning "source" or "origin". 

Grammar: "Σκηνοπηγία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Σκηνοπηγία" refers to the art or technique of directing or staging a theatrical performance. It encompasses the planning, organizing, and coordinating of various elements such as actors, sets, costumes, lighting, and sound to bring a play or performance to life on stage. The director, or "σκηνοθέτης" (skinothetis), is responsible for overseeing the overall artistic vision and ensuring that the production is executed according to their creative vision.
