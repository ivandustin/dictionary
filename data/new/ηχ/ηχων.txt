Word: ηχων

Etymology:
The word ηχων (ichon) comes from the Greek verb ηχέω (icheo), which means "to sound" or "to echo." It is derived from the noun ήχος (ichos), which means "sound" or "noise."

Grammar:
ηχων is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
ηχων refers to a sound or noise, particularly an echo. It can also refer to a voice or a tone. In Greek mythology, ηχων was the son of the god Hermes and the nymph Dryope.
