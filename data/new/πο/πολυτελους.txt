Word: πολυτελούς

Etymology: The word πολυτελούς comes from the Greek words πολύ (polý) meaning "much" or "many" and τέλος (télos) meaning "end" or "goal". Together, they form the word πολυτελής (polýtelēs) which means "expensive" or "luxurious".

Grammar: πολυτελούς is an adjective in the masculine singular genitive case. It is used to describe a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship.

Description: πολυτελούς is used to describe something that is luxurious or expensive. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as clothing, jewelry, cars, or homes. It is often used in marketing and advertising to promote high-end products or services.
