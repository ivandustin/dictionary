Word: ποτίζει (potízei)

Etymology: The word ποτίζει comes from the Greek verb ποτίζω (potízo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate". It is derived from the noun πότος (pótos), which means "drink".

Grammar: ποτίζει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb ποτίζω. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ ποτίζω (egó potízo) - I water
- Εσύ ποτίζεις (esý potízeis) - You water
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό ποτίζει (afTÓS/afTÍ/afTÓ potízei) - He/She/It waters
- Εμείς ποτίζουμε (emeís potízoume) - We water
- Εσείς ποτίζετε (eseís potízete) - You water
- Αυτοί/Αυτές ποτίζουν (afTÍ/afTÉS potízoun) - They water

Description: ποτίζει is a verb that describes the action of watering or irrigating plants or crops. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of providing nourishment or support to something or someone. For example, one might say "Η μητέρα ποτίζει τα παιδιά της με αγάπη και φροντίδα" (I miterá potízei ta pediá tis me agápi kai frontída), which means "The mother nourishes her children with love and care".
