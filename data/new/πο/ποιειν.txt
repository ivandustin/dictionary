The word "ποιειν" (poiein) is a Greek verb that means "to make" or "to create". It is derived from the Greek word "ποιός" (poios), which means "what kind of" or "of what sort". In terms of grammar, "ποιειν" is a present active infinitive verb, meaning that it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of making or creating in the present tense. 

In ancient Greek philosophy, "ποιειν" was often used to describe the act of creation or bringing something into existence, and was associated with the concept of "poiesis" or "making". This concept was explored by philosophers such as Plato and Aristotle, who saw the act of creation as a fundamental aspect of human nature and the universe as a whole. 

Overall, "ποιειν" is a versatile and important word in the Greek language, with a rich history and a wide range of meanings and applications.
