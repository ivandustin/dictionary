Word: πολυτελές (politeles)

Etymology:
The word πολυτελές comes from the Greek words πολύς (polys) meaning "many" and τέλος (telos) meaning "end" or "goal". The combination of these two words gives the meaning of "having many goals" or "having many ends". Over time, the word came to be associated with luxury and extravagance.

Grammar:
πολυτελές is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is luxurious, extravagant, or high-end.

Description:
πολυτελές is a word that is commonly used in Greek to describe something that is of high quality and expensive. It can be used to describe anything from a luxurious hotel to an expensive car or a high-end fashion item. The word is often used in advertising and marketing to promote products that are considered to be of high quality and exclusive.
