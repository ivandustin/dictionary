Word: πορευομενου

Etymology:
The word πορευομενου comes from the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreúomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey". It is derived from the root word πόρος (póros), which means "passage, way, journey".

Grammar:
πορευομενου is the present participle form of the verb πορεύομαι. It is in the genitive case, masculine gender, and singular number. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and in this case, it indicates that the action of going or traveling belongs to a singular masculine subject.

Description:
πορευομενου can be translated as "of the one who is going/traveling/journeying". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus or his disciples as they traveled from place to place to preach and teach. The word conveys a sense of movement and purpose, as if the subject is on a mission or has a destination in mind.
