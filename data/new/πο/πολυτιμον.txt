The word "πολυτιμον" (polytimon) is a Greek adjective that means "very precious" or "highly valuable". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "πολύς" (polys) meaning "much" or "many" and "τιμή" (timi) meaning "honor" or "value".

Grammar: "πολυτιμον" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "πολυτιμον" is often used to describe something that is highly valued or esteemed, such as a precious gem or a valuable possession. It can also be used to describe a person who is highly respected or honored.
