Word: ποταμός

Etymology:
The word ποταμός (potamós) comes from the ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, meaning "to rush, to fly". The word has been used in Greek literature since ancient times to refer to a river or a stream.

In Greek, ποταμός is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: ο ποταμός (o potamós) - the river
Genitive: του ποταμού (tou potamoú) - of the river
Accusative: τον ποταμό (ton potamó) - the river

Plural:
Nominative: οι ποταμοί (oi potamoí) - the rivers
Genitive: των ποταμών (ton potamón) - of the rivers
Accusative: τους ποταμούς (tous potamoús) - the rivers
