Word: πορισμός

Etymology: The word πορισμός comes from the Greek verb πορίζω (porizo), which means "to provide" or "to furnish". The suffix -μός (-mos) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form nouns from verbs, indicating the action or result of the verb.

Grammar: πορισμός is a masculine noun in Greek, and its nominative singular form is πορισμός. Its genitive singular form is πορισμού and its accusative singular form is πορισμό. In the plural, its nominative form is πορισμοί, its genitive form is πορισμών, and its accusative form is πορισμούς.

Description: πορισμός refers to the act of providing or furnishing something, or the result of that action. It can also refer to the means or resources used to provide or furnish something. In modern Greek, it is often used in the context of business or finance, referring to the provision of funds or resources for a project or enterprise.
