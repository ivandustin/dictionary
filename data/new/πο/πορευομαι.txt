Word: πορευομαι

Etymology:
The word πορευομαι (poreuomai) is a Greek verb that means "to go" or "to travel". It comes from the root word πορος (poros), which means "passage" or "journey".

Grammar:
πορευομαι is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Πορεύομαι (poreuomai) - I go
- Πορεύεσαι (poreuesai) - You go
- Πορεύεται (poreuetai) - He/she/it goes

Plural:
- Πορευόμαστε (poreuomaste) - We go
- Πορεύεσθε (poreuesthe) - You go
- Πορεύονται (poreuontai) - They go

Description:
πορευομαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physical movement, such as walking or traveling, as well as more abstract concepts, such as progress or advancement. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples as they traveled from place to place. In modern Greek, it is still commonly used to mean "to go" or "to travel".
