Word: ποσακις

Etymology: ποσακις is a Greek adverb that means "how many times" or "often". It is derived from the combination of the words πόσος (posos) meaning "how much" and ἄκις (akis) meaning "time".

Grammar: ποσακις is an adverb in Greek and is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is often used in questions to ask how many times something has happened or how often it occurs.

Description: ποσακις is a useful word in Greek for asking about the frequency of events or actions. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as inquiring about how often someone goes to the gym or how many times a particular event has occurred. It is a common word in everyday conversation and is essential for anyone learning Greek.
