Word: πορρωτερον

Etymology:
The word πορρωτερον is derived from the Greek word πόρρω (porro), which means "far" or "distant". The suffix -τερον (-teron) is a comparative suffix, which means "more" or "greater". Therefore, πορρωτερον means "more distant" or "farther".

Grammar:
πορρωτερον is an adjective in the comparative degree. It is declined like a regular first/second declension adjective in the masculine, feminine, and neuter genders. For example, in the masculine singular, it is declined as follows:

Nominative: πορρωτερος
Genitive: πορρωτερου
Dative: πορρωτερῳ
Accusative: πορρωτερον

Description:
πορρωτερον is used to describe something that is more distant or farther away than something else. It can be used in a literal sense, such as describing the distance between two physical objects, or in a figurative sense, such as describing the difference between two ideas or concepts. For example, one might say "Το σπίτι μου είναι πορρωτερον από το σουπερμάρκετ" (My house is farther away from the supermarket) or "Η αντίληψή μου για το θέμα είναι πορρωτερη από τη δική σου" (My understanding of the issue is more distant than yours).
