Word: ποιηται

Etymology: The word ποιηται (poiētai) is derived from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to create." The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates that the word is a plural noun, meaning "makers" or "creators."

Grammar: ποιηται is a third-person plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: ποιηται refers to poets or makers of poetry. In ancient Greece, poetry was highly valued and considered a form of art. Poets were respected members of society and their works were often performed in public. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of creative artist or maker.
