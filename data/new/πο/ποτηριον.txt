Word: ποτήριον

Etymology:
The word ποτήριον (poterion) comes from the Greek word πίνω (pino) which means "to drink". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃- which means "to drink".

Grammar:
ποτήριον is a neuter noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and its nominative case is ποτήριον. In the genitive case, it is ποτηρίου (poteriou).

Description:
ποτήριον is a Greek word that means "cup" or "goblet". It is often used in religious contexts, such as in the Bible, where it is used to refer to the cup used in the Last Supper. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any type of drinking vessel.
