Word: πολλα

Etymology: πολλα is a Greek word that means "many" or "much". It is derived from the Greek word "πολύς" (polys), which also means "many" or "much".

Grammar: πολλα is an adjective in the Greek language. It is used to describe a noun or pronoun, indicating that there is a large quantity or number of something. It can be used in the masculine, feminine, or neuter gender, and in the singular or plural form.

Description: πολλα is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe a large number of people, objects, or ideas. For example, "πολλα άτομα" (polla atoma) means "many people", and "πολλα βιβλία" (polla vivlia) means "many books". It can also be used to describe a high degree or intensity of something, such as "πολλα χρήματα" (polla hrimata), which means "a lot of money".
