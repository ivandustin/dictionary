The word "ποιητε" (poiēte) is a verb in the Greek language, which means "to create" or "to make". It is derived from the Greek word "ποιέω" (poiéō), which also means "to make" or "to do". 

In terms of grammar, "ποιητε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "ποιέω". This means that it is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. For example, "ποιητε αυτό το έργο" (poiēte aftó to érgo) means "make this work".

Overall, "ποιητε" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to express the idea of creating or making something.
