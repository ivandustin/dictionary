Word: ποτηριων

Etymology:
The word ποτήριον (poterion) comes from the Greek word πίνω (pino) which means "to drink". It is a noun in the genitive plural form, which means "of cups".

Grammar:
ποτηριων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. In this case, ποτηριων indicates that the cups belong to someone or something.

Description:
ποτηριων refers to cups or drinking vessels. It is a plural form, indicating that there are multiple cups. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a restaurant or at home. It is commonly used in Greek Orthodox Church services, where it refers to the chalice used for Holy Communion.
