The word "ποντου" (pontou) is a Greek noun that refers to the Pontus region, which is located in the northeastern part of modern-day Turkey. The word is derived from the Greek word "πόντος" (pontos), which means "sea" or "ocean". In ancient times, the Pontus region was known for its rich natural resources, including timber, minerals, and agricultural products. 

In terms of grammar, "ποντου" is a second declension noun in the genitive case. It is often used in phrases such as "του Πόντου" (tou Pontou), which means "of the Pontus region". 

Overall, "ποντου" is a word that is closely associated with the history and culture of the Pontus region, and it continues to be used today to refer to this important part of the world.
