Word: ποιουμενοι

Etymology: The word ποιουμενοι comes from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The suffix -μενοι (-menoi) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to something that is currently being made or done.

Grammar: ποιουμενοι is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is derived from the verb ποιέω and is used to describe an ongoing action or state of being. In this form, it can function as an adjective, noun, or verb.

Description: ποιουμενοι can be translated to mean "those who are making" or "those who are doing". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe individuals who are actively engaged in a particular activity or pursuit. In modern Greek, it is commonly used in the context of work or labor, referring to those who are currently working or producing something.
