Word: πορευόμενον

Etymology:
The word πορευόμενον comes from the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey". The word is derived from the root πορεύω (poreuo), which means "to pass through, to traverse".

Grammar:
πορευόμενον is the present participle of the verb πορεύομαι. In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. It can function as an adjective, modifying a noun, or as a verb, taking on the tense and voice of the main verb in the sentence.

Description:
πορευόμενον describes something that is currently in the process of going or traveling. It can be used to describe a person, an animal, or an object that is moving from one place to another. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a process or a journey towards a goal or destination.
