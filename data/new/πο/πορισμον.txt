Word: πορισμόν

Etymology: The word πορισμόν comes from the Greek verb πορίζω (porizo), which means "to provide" or "to furnish". The suffix -μόν (-mon) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form nouns.

Grammar: πορισμόν is a masculine noun in the accusative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: πορισμόν refers to the act of providing or furnishing something. It can also refer to the thing that is provided or furnished. The word is often used in a financial or economic context, such as in the sense of "profit" or "gain". For example, one might say "ο πορισμός μου από αυτή τη συναλλαγή ήταν μεγάλος" (my profit from this transaction was large).
