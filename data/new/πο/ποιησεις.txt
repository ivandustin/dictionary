Word: ποιήσεις

Etymology: The word ποιήσεις comes from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to create". The suffix -σεις (-seis) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple creations or works.

Grammar: ποιήσεις is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. It is the plural form of ποίησις (poiēsis), which means "poetry" or "creation". In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: ποιήσεις refers to a collection of creative works or creations, such as poems, songs, or other artistic expressions. It can also refer to the act of creating or making something. The word is often used in the context of literature and the arts, and is commonly associated with poetry.
