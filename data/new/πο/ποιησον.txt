Word: ποιησον

Etymology:
The word ποιησον (poiēson) is a verb in the aorist imperative mood in Greek. It comes from the root word ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to do". The suffix -σον (-son) is added to the root to form the aorist imperative, which is used to give a command or request to someone to do something immediately.

Grammar:
ποιησον is a second person singular aorist imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or request to someone to do something immediately. In this case, the verb means "make" or "do", so the command is to "make" or "do" something.

Description:
ποιησον is a strong and direct command in Greek, urging someone to take immediate action. It is often used in the Bible and other ancient texts to convey urgency and importance. The word can be translated as "make" or "do", depending on the context. In modern Greek, the word is still used in the same way, as a command to take action.
