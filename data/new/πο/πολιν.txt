The word "πολιν" (polin) is a Greek noun that means "city" or "town". It is derived from the Greek word "πόλις" (polis), which also means "city". In terms of grammar, "πολιν" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

In ancient Greece, the city-state (polis) was the basic unit of political organization, and each polis had its own government, laws, and customs. The concept of the polis was central to Greek culture and philosophy, and it played a significant role in the development of Western civilization. 

Overall, "πολιν" is a fundamental word in Greek language and culture, representing the concept of the city as a political and social entity.
