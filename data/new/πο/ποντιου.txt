The word "ποντιου" (pontiou) is a Greek adjective that means "Pontic" or "of Pontus". Pontus was an ancient region located in the northeastern part of modern-day Turkey, along the coast of the Black Sea. The etymology of the word "Pontus" is uncertain, but it may have been derived from the Greek word "pontos", which means "sea". In terms of grammar, "ποντιου" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or origin.
