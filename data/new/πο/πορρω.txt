Word: πορρω

Etymology: πορρω is a Greek adverb that means "far away" or "distant". It is derived from the combination of the prefix πορ- (meaning "forward" or "ahead") and the adverb ῥῶ (meaning "far" or "distant").

Grammar: πορρω is an adverb in Greek and is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It does not change form based on the gender, number, or case of the noun it modifies.

Description: πορρω is a versatile adverb that can be used in a variety of contexts to indicate distance or remoteness. It can be used to describe physical distance, such as "the city is far away" (ἡ πόλις πορρω ἐστίν), or metaphorical distance, such as "his thoughts are far removed from reality" (αἱ διανοίαι αὐτοῦ πορρω τῆς ἀληθείας εἰσίν). It can also be used to indicate time, such as "that happened a long time ago" (τοῦτο πορρω ἐγένετο). Overall, πορρω is a useful and common adverb in Greek that can add depth and nuance to a variety of statements.
