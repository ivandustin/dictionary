The word "ποιμαινε" (poimaine) is a verb in Greek that means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock of sheep." It comes from the noun "ποιμήν" (poimēn), which means "shepherd." In terms of grammar, "ποιμαινε" is a present tense, active voice, imperative mood verb. It is often used in the Bible to describe the role of Jesus as the Good Shepherd.
