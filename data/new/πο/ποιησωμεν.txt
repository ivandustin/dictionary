Word: ποιησωμεν

Etymology:
The word ποιησωμεν is derived from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". It is formed by adding the suffix -σωμεν (-somen) to the stem ποιη- (poiē-), which indicates the first person plural subjunctive mood.

Grammar:
ποιησωμεν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the first person plural subjunctive mood. This mood is used to express a hypothetical or uncertain action, often in a subordinate clause. The verb is conjugated in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
ποιησωμεν can be translated to English as "let us make" or "let us do". It is often used as an imperative or a suggestion to take action. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in a command to start a project or in a call to action for a group of people.
