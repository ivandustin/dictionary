The word "ποιμανατε" (poimanate) is a Greek verb that means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock." It is derived from the noun "ποιμήν" (poimen), which means "shepherd." In terms of grammar, "ποιμανατε" is a second person plural present imperative verb, meaning it is a command directed towards a group of people to perform the action of shepherding or tending to a flock.
