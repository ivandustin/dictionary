Word: ποιμένας

Etymology:
The word ποιμένας (poimenas) is a Greek word that means "shepherd" or "herdsman". It is derived from the Greek word ποιμήν (poimen), which means "shepherd" or "pastor". The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to Jesus Christ as the "Good Shepherd" who cares for his flock.

ποιμένας is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word ποιμένας is masculine and plural, which means it refers to a group of male shepherds.

Description:
ποιμένας is a word that describes a person who takes care of sheep or other livestock. In ancient times, shepherds were an important part of the economy and culture of many societies. They were responsible for leading their flocks to pasture, protecting them from predators, and caring for their needs.
