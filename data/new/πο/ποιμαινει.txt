Word: ποιμαινει

Etymology: ποιμαινει comes from the Greek word ποιμαίνω (poimaino), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend a flock." It is derived from the noun ποιμήν (poimen), which means "shepherd."

Grammar: ποιμαινει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb ποιμαίνω (poimaino), which is a regular verb in the Greek language.

Description: ποιμαινει means "he/she/it shepherds" or "he/she/it tends a flock." It is often used in a literal sense to describe the act of tending to a group of animals, such as sheep or goats. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of guiding or leading a group of people.
