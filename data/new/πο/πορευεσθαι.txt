Word: πορευεσθαι

Etymology:
The word πορευεσθαι (poreuesthai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *per- which means "to lead, to pass over."

Grammar:
πορευεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb πορεύομαι. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is acting upon itself or is affected by the action. The passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is being acted upon. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing or habitual.

Description:
πορευεσθαι is a versatile verb that can be translated in a variety of ways depending on the context. It can mean "to go, to travel," "to proceed," "to march," "to journey," or "to conduct oneself." It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples as they traveled from place to place preaching and teaching. The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe the progress of an idea or a plan.
