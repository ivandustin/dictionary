The word "ποιουσιν" (poiousin) is a present active indicative verb in Greek, which means "they make" or "they do". It is derived from the root word "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to do". In terms of etymology, "ποιέω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root " *kʷey-" which means "to make" or "to build". 

In terms of grammar, "ποιουσιν" is in the third person plural form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a group of people or things that are making or doing something. It is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action rather than receiving it.

Overall, "ποιουσιν" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe actions being taken by a group of people or things.
