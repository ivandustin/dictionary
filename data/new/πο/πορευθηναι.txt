Word: πορευθηναι

Etymology: πορευθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πορεύομαι (poreúomai), which means "to go, to travel". The -θηναι (-thenai) ending is a passive aorist infinitive form.

Grammar: πορευθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive form of the verb πορεύομαι (poreúomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: πορευθηναι means "to be led, to be taken, to be conducted". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as being led by a higher power or being taken to a sacred place. The passive voice emphasizes the idea of being guided or directed by an external force.
