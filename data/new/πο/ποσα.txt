Word: ποσα

Etymology:
The word ποσα (posa) is derived from the Greek word πόσος (posos), which means "how much" or "how many". It is a combination of the prefix πο- (po-), which means "how" and the suffix -σος (-sos), which means "so much".

Grammar:
ποσα is a plural form of the Greek adjective ποσός (posos), which means "how much" or "how many". It is used to describe a quantity of something, and agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
ποσα is commonly used in Greek to ask about the quantity of something. For example, "πόσα βιβλία έχεις;" (posa vivlia ehis?) means "how many books do you have?". It can also be used to describe a quantity, such as "ποσά χρήματα έδωσες;" (posa hrimata edoses?) meaning "how much money did you give?".
