Word: ποικιλοις

Etymology: 
The word ποικιλοις (poikilois) is derived from the Greek word ποικίλος (poikilos), which means "variegated" or "multicolored". It is a combination of the prefix ποικιλ- (poikil-), meaning "many-colored" or "varied", and the suffix -οις (-ois), which is a dative plural ending.

Grammar:
ποικιλοις is a dative plural form of the adjective ποικίλος (poikilos), which means "variegated" or "multicolored". In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
ποικιλοις is used in Greek to describe something that is multicolored or variegated. It can be used to describe a wide range of things, from clothing and textiles to landscapes and natural phenomena.
