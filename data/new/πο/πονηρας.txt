Word: πονηρας

Etymology:
The word πονηρας (poneras) is derived from the Greek word πονηρός (poneros), which means wicked or evil. It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *pen- meaning "to suffer" or "to toil".

Grammar:
πονηρας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The word is often used as an adjective to describe a person or action as wicked or evil.

Description:
πονηρας is a word that is commonly used in Greek to describe someone who is wicked, evil, or malicious. It can also be used to describe an action or behavior that is deceitful or harmful. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to express disapproval or condemnation.
