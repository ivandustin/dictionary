Word: πολλακις

Etymology:
The word πολλακις (pollakis) is a Greek adverb that means "often" or "frequently". It is derived from the adjective πολύς (polys), meaning "many" or "much".

Grammar:
πολλακις is an adverb in Greek, and it is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is often used in the present tense, but can also be used in the past or future tenses.

Description:
πολλακις is a common word in Greek, and it is used to describe actions or events that occur frequently or repeatedly. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in everyday conversation, literature, or religious texts.
