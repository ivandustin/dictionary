Word: πορευσομαι

Etymology:
The word πορευσομαι (poreusomai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel." The verb is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σομαι (-somai) to the root.

Grammar:
πορευσομαι is a first-person singular future tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Πορευσομαι (poreusomai) - I will go/travel

Plural:
- Πορευσομεθα (poreusometha) - We will go/travel

Description:
πορευσομαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physical travel, as in "I will go to the store," or it can be used metaphorically to describe progress or advancement, as in "I will make progress in my studies." The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the travels of Jesus and his disciples.
