Word: ποιησουσιν

Etymology:
The word ποιησουσιν (poiēsousin) is a verb form in the future tense of the Greek word ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to do". The word is derived from the root word ποι- (poi-), which means "to create" or "to produce".

Grammar:
ποιησουσιν is a third-person plural future active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σ- (s) to the stem ποιη- (poiē-) and the personal ending -ουσιν (-ousin) for the third-person plural.

Description:
ποιησουσιν is a verb that indicates an action that will be done in the future by a third-person plural subject. It can be translated as "they will make" or "they will do". The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it appears in various contexts, such as in the phrase "they will make war" (Rev. 17:14) or "they will do good" (1 Tim. 6:18).
