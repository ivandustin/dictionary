Word: ποθεν

Etymology:
The word ποθεν (pothen) is a Greek adverb that means "from where" or "whence." It is derived from the combination of the preposition πό (po), meaning "from," and the adverb θέν (then), meaning "where."

Grammar:
ποθεν is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is used to ask about the origin or source of something or someone. For example, "ποθεν είσαι;" (pothen eisai?) means "Where are you from?"

Description:
ποθεν is a common word in Greek, used in everyday conversation to ask about someone's origin or to express curiosity about the source of something. It is a simple and straightforward word that is easy to use and understand.
