Word: ποιημα

Etymology:
The word ποιημα (poiēma) comes from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to create." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey- which means "to make" or "to build."

Grammar:
ποιημα is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is ποιήματα (poiēmata). It is declined like other neuter nouns in Greek, with the nominative and accusative forms being the same.

Description:
ποιημα refers to a poem or a piece of poetry. It is a literary work that uses language to evoke emotion, tell a story, or express an idea. In Greek literature, poetry was highly valued and considered a form of art. Many famous Greek poets, such as Homer, Sappho, and Hesiod, wrote ποιήματα that are still studied and admired today.
