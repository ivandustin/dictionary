Word: που

Etymology:
The word "που" comes from the Ancient Greek word "ποῦ" (pou), which means "where". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷo-, meaning "who, what, where".

Grammar:
"Που" is a Greek adverb that is used to indicate location or direction. It is often translated as "where" or "in which". It can also be used as a relative pronoun, meaning "who" or "which". In this case, it is often used to introduce a subordinate clause.

Description:
"Που" is a common word in the Greek language and is used in a variety of contexts. It is often used in questions to ask about location or direction, such as "που είσαι;" (where are you?). It can also be used to indicate a location or direction in a sentence, such as "Το βιβλίο είναι που το άφησα" (the book is where I left it). As a relative pronoun, it is used to introduce a subordinate clause that provides additional information about a noun or pronoun in the main clause, such as "Το σπίτι που μένω είναι μικρό" (the house where I live is small).
