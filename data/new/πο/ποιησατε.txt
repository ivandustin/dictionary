Word: ποιησατε

Etymology:
The word ποιησατε (poiēsate) is a verb in the aorist tense, second person plural form of the Greek word ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to do". The word comes from the ancient Greek root ποιέω (poiéō), which is related to the English word "poet".

Grammar:
ποιησατε (poiēsate) is a verb in the aorist tense, second person plural form of the Greek language. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The second person plural form indicates that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description:
ποιησατε (poiēsate) can be translated to mean "you made" or "you did". It is a command form of the verb ποιέω (poiéō), which means that it is used to give an order or instruction to a group of people. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a sentence like "ποιησατε την εργασία σας" (poiēsate tin ergasia sas), which means "you completed your work".
