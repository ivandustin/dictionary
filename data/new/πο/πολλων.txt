Word: πολλων

Etymology: πολλων is the genitive form of the Greek adjective πολύς (polys), which means "many" or "much". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pel-, which means "to fill" or "to be full".

Grammar: πολλων is a masculine, feminine, or neuter genitive plural form of the adjective πολύς. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: πολλων is commonly used in Greek to indicate a large quantity or number of something. It can also be used to express intensity or emphasis. For example, "πολλων ἐτῶν" (pollōn etōn) means "many years" or "a long time". In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the multitude of people who followed Jesus.
