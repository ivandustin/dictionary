Word: πορευθητε

Etymology:
The word πορευθητε (poreuthete) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey." The verb is in the imperative mood, which means it is a command or request to go or travel.

Grammar:
πορευθητε is a second-person plural verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or requests, so the word is telling a group of people to go or travel.

Description:
πορευθητε is a command to a group of people to go or travel. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as a military commander telling troops to move out, a teacher telling students to go to a certain location, or a religious leader telling followers to spread a message.
