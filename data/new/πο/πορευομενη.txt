Word: πορευομενη

Etymology:
The word πορευομενη (poreuomenē) is a present participle form of the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreúomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey". The verb is derived from the noun πόρος (póros), which means "passage, way, journey".

Grammar:
πορευομενη is a present participle form of the verb πορεύομαι. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ομενος (-omenos) to the stem of the verb. The present participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or in progress.

Description:
πορευομενη is a feminine singular form of the present participle, which means "going, traveling, journeying". It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of Jesus or his disciples as they traveled from place to place. For example, in Mark 6:6, it is written that Jesus "went (πορευόμενος) around the villages teaching". The word can also be used more generally to describe any ongoing action of going or traveling.
