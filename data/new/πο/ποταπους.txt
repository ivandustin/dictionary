Word: ποταπους

Etymology: The word ποταπους comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word made up of the words ποτέ (poté) meaning "ever" and από (apó) meaning "from". The suffix -ους (-ous) is added to form an adjective, meaning "of what kind".

Grammar: ποταπους is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a noun or pronoun, indicating that the thing being described is of an unknown or uncertain kind.

Description: The word ποταπους is often used to express uncertainty or vagueness about something. It can be translated as "what kind of" or "of what sort". For example, if someone asks "ποταπούς άνθρωπος είναι αυτός;" (potapús ánthropos íne aftós?) it means "what kind of person is this?" or "who is this person?". The word can also be used to describe an object or situation that is difficult to categorize or define.
