Word: ποτιζων

Etymology: 
The word ποτιζων comes from the Greek verb ποτίζω (potízo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate". It is derived from the noun πότος (pótos), which means "drink" or "beverage".

Grammar:
ποτιζων is the present participle form of the verb ποτίζω (potízo). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
ποτιζων refers to a person or thing that is currently watering or irrigating something. It can be translated as "watering" or "irrigating" in English. For example, in the sentence "Ο ποτιζων τον κήπον είναι ο γείτονας μου" (O potizón ton kípon eínai o geítonas mou), which means "The person watering the garden is my neighbor", ποτιζων refers to the person who is currently watering the garden.
