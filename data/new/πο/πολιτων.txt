Word: πολιτων

Etymology:
The word πολιτων (politon) is derived from the Greek word πόλις (polis), which means "city" or "city-state". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common ending for Greek nouns denoting a person or thing that is associated with the root word.

Grammar:
πολιτων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the plural form of πολίτης (politis), which means "citizen". In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description:
πολιτων refers to a group of citizens, or people who are members of a particular city-state or community. In ancient Greece, citizenship was a highly valued status that was granted to free-born men who met certain qualifications, such as owning property and serving in the military. Citizens had certain rights and privileges, such as the right to vote and participate in government, and were expected to fulfill certain duties, such as paying taxes and serving in the military.
