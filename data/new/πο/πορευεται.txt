Word: πορεύεται

Etymology: 
The word πορεύεται (poreuetai) is a present tense form of the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey". The verb is derived from the noun πόρος (poros), which means "passage, way, journey".

Grammar:
πορεύεται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb πορεύομαι. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now or regularly. The third person singular form indicates that the subject is he/she/it.

Description:
πορεύεται is a common Greek verb that is used to describe movement or travel. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone's physical movement, or describing the progress of a project or plan. The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples.
