Word: ποιησαντες

Etymology:
The word ποιησαντες is derived from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the past tense and active voice. Therefore, ποιησαντες means "having made" or "having done".

Grammar:
ποιησαντες is a past participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to modify a noun or pronoun and functions as an adjective in a sentence.

Description:
ποιησαντες is a word that describes an action that has already been completed in the past. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the actions of characters or historical figures. In English, it can be translated as "having made" or "having done".
