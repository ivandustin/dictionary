The word "ποδηρη" (podere) is a Greek noun that means "a foot soldier" or "an infantryman". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "πούς" (pous) meaning "foot" and "ἥρως" (heros) meaning "hero" or "warrior".

Grammar: In Greek, "ποδηρη" is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is "ποδηρη" and its genitive singular form is "ποδηρης".

Description: The word "ποδηρη" was commonly used in ancient Greece to refer to foot soldiers who fought in battles. These soldiers were typically armed with spears, shields, and swords. The term is still used in modern Greek to refer to infantry soldiers.
