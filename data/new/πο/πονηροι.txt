Word: πονηροί

Etymology: The word πονηροί comes from the Greek word πόνος (ponos), meaning "pain" or "hardship". The suffix -ρος (-ros) is a common Greek suffix that is used to form adjectives. Therefore, πονηροί can be translated as "painful" or "hardship-causing".

Grammar: πονηροί is the plural form of the adjective πονηρός (poniros), which means "wicked" or "evil". It is a masculine adjective and agrees with the gender and number of the noun it modifies.

Description: πονηροί is a strong and negative adjective that is used to describe people who are deceitful, cunning, and untrustworthy. It is often used to describe someone who is intentionally causing harm or trouble to others. In Greek mythology, the god Hermes was often described as πονηρός because of his trickster nature.
