Word: ποιησασαν

Etymology:
The word ποιησασαν is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form of the verb ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to do". The word is derived from the Greek root word ποιητής (poiētēs), which means "poet" or "maker".

Grammar:
ποιησασαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description:
ποιησασαν can be translated to English as "they made" or "they did". The word is often used in Greek literature to describe the actions of gods or heroes, such as in the Iliad and the Odyssey. In modern Greek, the word is still used in everyday language to describe the completion of a task or action.
