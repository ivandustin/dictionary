Word: πορευθέντες

Etymology:
The word πορευθέντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey." The suffix -θέντες (-thentes) is a participle ending that indicates past action, so πορευθέντες means "having gone, having traveled."

Grammar:
πορευθέντες is a participle in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the masculine plural form, which means it refers to a group of people who have gone or traveled. 

Description:
πορευθέντες is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples as they traveled from place to place to preach and teach. It can also be used more generally to describe any group of people who have gone or traveled somewhere.
