Word: πονηριαν

Etymology:
The word πονηριαν is derived from the Greek word πονηρία (poneria), which means wickedness or evil. It is formed by adding the suffix -αν (-an) to the end of the word.

Grammar:
πονηριαν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
πονηριαν refers to an act of wickedness or evil. It can be used to describe a person's behavior or actions that are considered immoral or unethical. The word is often used in a negative context and is associated with deceit, malice, and harm.
