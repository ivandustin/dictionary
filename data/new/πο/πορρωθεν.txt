Word: πορρωθεν

Etymology:
The word πορρωθεν (porrothen) is a Greek adverb that means "from afar" or "far away". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words: πόρρω (porro), meaning "far" or "distant", and the suffix -θεν (-then), meaning "from".

Grammar:
πορρωθεν is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is often used to indicate the distance or remoteness of an action or object. For example, "He saw the ship coming from far away" could be translated as "Είδε το πλοίο να έρχεται από πορρωθεν" (Eide to ploio na erchetai apo porrothen).

Description:
πορρωθεν is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used in literature and poetry to create a sense of distance or longing. It can also be used in everyday speech to describe something that is far away or out of reach. In addition, it can be used metaphorically to describe something that is difficult to understand or comprehend. Overall, πορρωθεν is a useful and expressive word in the Greek language.
