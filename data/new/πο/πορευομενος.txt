Word: πορευόμενος

Etymology:
The word πορευόμενος comes from the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreuomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey". The suffix -όμενος (-omenos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word describes an action that is currently happening.

Grammar:
πορευόμενος is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb πορεύομαι, which is in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. The participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing and is being performed by the subject of the sentence.

Description:
πορευόμενος can be translated as "going, traveling, journeying". It is often used to describe someone who is in the process of traveling from one place to another. For example, in the sentence "Ο πορευόμενος άνδρας είναι κουρασμένος" (The man who is traveling is tired), πορευόμενος describes the man who is currently traveling and is the subject of the sentence.
