The word "ποιουμεν" (poioumen) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "ποιέω" (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". 

Etymology: The word "ποιέω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey- which means "to make" or "to build". 

Grammar: As a present participle, "ποιουμεν" functions as an adjective and describes an ongoing action of making or doing. It is declined according to gender, number, and case, and can be used attributively or predicatively. 

Description: "ποιουμεν" can be translated as "making" or "doing" depending on the context. For example, in the sentence "Οι μαθητές ποιούν τις ασκήσεις" (Oi mathites poioun tis askiseis), it means "The students are doing the exercises".
