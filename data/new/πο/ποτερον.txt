Word: ποτερον

Etymology: ποτερον is a Greek adverb that means "which of two". It is derived from the Greek words πότε (pote) meaning "when" and ἕρπω (herpo) meaning "to creep or crawl".

Grammar: ποτερον is an adverb that is used to compare two things. It is often used in questions to ask which of two options is preferred or to express uncertainty between two choices.

Description: ποτερον is a useful word in Greek for expressing indecision or uncertainty between two options. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and can be found in literature and other written works.
