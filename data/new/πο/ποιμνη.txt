Word: ποιμνη

Etymology:
The word ποιμνη (poimnē) is derived from the Greek word ποιμήν (poimēn), which means "shepherd". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *peḱu-, which means "livestock".

Grammar:
In Greek, ποιμνη is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is ποιμνή, and its genitive singular form is ποιμνῆς. The word can be declined in all cases, singular and plural.

Description:
ποιμνη refers to a flock of sheep or other grazing animals that are tended by a shepherd. In ancient Greece, sheep were an important source of food, wool, and milk, and shepherding was a common occupation. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to a group of people who are under the care or guidance of a leader or mentor.
