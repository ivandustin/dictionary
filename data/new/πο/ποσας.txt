Word: ποσας

Etymology: ποσας is a Greek word that comes from the adjective ποσός (posos), meaning "how much" or "how many."

Grammar: ποσας is the feminine accusative plural form of the adjective ποσός (posos), which means "how much" or "how many." It is used to modify a feminine plural noun in the accusative case.

Description: ποσας is used to ask "how many" of something in the feminine plural form. For example, "ποσας φορές έχεις πάει στην Ελλάδα;" (posas fores ehis pei stin Ellada?) means "how many times have you been to Greece?"
