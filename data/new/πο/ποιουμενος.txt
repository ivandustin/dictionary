Word: ποιούμενος

Etymology: The word ποιούμενος comes from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the present passive voice, meaning "being made" or "being done".

Grammar: ποιούμενος is a present passive participle in Greek. It is declined like an adjective, agreeing in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun in the nominative case, it would be ποιούμενος. If it modifies a feminine singular noun in the accusative case, it would be ποιουμένην.

Description: ποιούμενος is a word that describes something that is currently being made or done. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in manufacturing, cooking, or construction. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the actions of gods or heroes, emphasizing their ongoing activity and power.
