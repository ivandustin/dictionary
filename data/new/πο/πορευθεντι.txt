Word: πορευθεντι

Etymology:
The word πορευθεντι is a form of the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreúomai), which means "to go, to travel, to journey". It is derived from the root word πόρος (póros), which means "passage, way, journey".

Grammar:
πορευθεντι is the aorist participle passive form of the verb πορεύομαι. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the participle form is used to describe an action that is ongoing or incomplete. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
πορευθεντι can be translated as "having gone, having traveled, having journeyed". It describes a completed action of going or traveling, with the subject being the recipient of the action. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a person who has gone on a journey, or a message that has been sent and received.
