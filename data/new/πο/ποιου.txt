Word: ποιου

Etymology: The word ποιου comes from the Greek word ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷey- which means "to make" or "to build".

Grammar: ποιου is a Greek pronoun that means "whose". It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is often used to ask about the owner of something or to indicate possession.

Description: ποιου is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is commonly used in questions to ask about the owner of something, such as "ποιου είναι αυτό το βιβλίο;" (Whose book is this?). It can also be used to indicate possession, such as "το σπίτι του ποιου;" (whose house is this?). In addition, it can be used in more abstract contexts, such as "ποιου είναι ο στόχος σου;" (what is your goal?).
