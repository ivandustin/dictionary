Word: ποιμνης

Etymology:
The word ποιμνης (poimnēs) is derived from the Greek word ποιμήν (poimēn), which means "shepherd". The suffix -νης (-nēs) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns indicating a person or thing that is associated with the root word.

Grammar:
ποιμνης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender. The word is declined in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description:
ποιμνης refers to a flock of sheep or a herd of cattle. It is often used in the context of pastoral life and agriculture. In ancient Greece, shepherds were an important part of the economy and society, and the care of flocks was considered a noble profession. The word is also used metaphorically to refer to a group of people who are under the care or guidance of a leader or authority figure.
