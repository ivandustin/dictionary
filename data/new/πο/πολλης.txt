Word: πολλης

Etymology: πολλης is a Greek word that comes from the adjective πολύς (polys), meaning "many" or "much". It is often used in conjunction with other words to indicate quantity or degree.

Grammar: πολλης is the genitive singular form of the feminine adjective πολλή (polle), which means "many" or "much". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: πολλης can be translated as "of many" or "of much" in English. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe abundance or excess. For example, in Homer's Odyssey, the phrase "πολλή δ' ἀλήθεια" (polle d' alētheia) means "much truth" or "great truth". In modern Greek, πολλης is also used as a standalone word to mean "a lot" or "many".
