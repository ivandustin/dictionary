Word: πονηριας

Etymology: The word πονηριας comes from the Greek word πονηρός (ponērós), which means "wicked" or "evil". The suffix -ιας (-ias) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns indicating a state or condition.

Grammar: πονηριας is a noun in the Greek language, and it is in the singular form. It is a feminine noun, and its gender is indicated by the suffix -ιας.

Description: πονηριας refers to the state or condition of being wicked or evil. It can also be used to describe someone who is deceitful or malicious. The word is often used in a negative context and is not typically used as a compliment.
