Word: πολεις

The word πολεις (poleis) is derived from the Greek word πόλις (polis), which means "city" or "city-state". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European word *pel-, which means "citadel" or "fortress".

πολεις is the plural form of πόλις. It is a third declension noun in Greek, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative singular form is πόλις, while the genitive singular form is πόλεως.

Description:
πολεις refers to multiple cities or city-states. In ancient Greece, each city-state had its own government, laws, and customs. The most famous city-states were Athens, Sparta, and Corinth.
