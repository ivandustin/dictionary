Word: ποιησαντι

Etymology: The word ποιησαντι comes from the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to create". The suffix -σαντι (-santi) is the aorist participle form of the verb, indicating past action.

Grammar: ποιησαντι is the aorist participle form of the verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "having made" or "having created". It is in the masculine singular nominative case, indicating that it refers to a male subject who has performed the action of making or creating.

Description: ποιησαντι is a Greek word that describes someone who has made or created something in the past. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe the actions of artists, craftsmen, and other creative individuals. In English, it can be translated as "the one who made" or "the one who created".
