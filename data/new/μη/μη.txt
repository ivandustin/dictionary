Word: μη

Etymology:
The word μη (mē) is a Greek particle that is used to indicate negation or prohibition. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *mē, which means "not" or "without".

Grammar:
In Greek, μη is a particle that is used to form negative statements. It is often used with the subjunctive mood to indicate a prohibition or a negative command. For example, "μη φεύγεις" (mē feugeis) means "do not flee".

Description:
μη is a small but important word in Greek, as it is used to indicate negation and prohibition. It is often used in conjunction with other words to form negative statements, and is an essential part of the Greek language.
