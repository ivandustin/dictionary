Word: μηδαμως

Etymology:
μηδαμως (mēdamōs) is a Greek adverb that means "not at all" or "by no means". It is derived from the negative particle μη (mē) and the adverb δαμως (damōs), which means "at all".

Grammar:
μηδαμως is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate the degree or extent of negation. It is often used in negative sentences to emphasize the negation.

Description:
μηδαμως is a common word in Greek that is used to express strong negation. It is often used in everyday conversation and in formal writing. It is a useful word to know for anyone learning Greek, as it is frequently used in a variety of contexts.
