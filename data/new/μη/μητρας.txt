μητρας (mētras) is a Greek word that can be translated to "of the mother" or "maternal". It is derived from the Greek word μήτηρ (mētēr), which means "mother". 

In terms of grammar, μητρας is the genitive singular form of μήτηρ. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship, and in this case, it indicates that something belongs to or is related to a mother.

In ancient Greek mythology, the word μητρας was often used to refer to the mother goddess, who was seen as the protector of women and childbirth. The word is also used in medical terminology to refer to the uterus or womb.

Overall, μητρας is a word that is closely associated with motherhood and maternal relationships.
