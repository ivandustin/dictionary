Word: μηκέτι

Etymology: μηκέτι is a Greek adverb that means "no longer" or "not anymore". It is derived from the combination of the negative particle μή (mē) and the adverb κέτι (kéti), which means "still" or "yet".

Grammar: μηκέτι is an adverb that modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is often used in negative sentences to indicate that something that was true or possible in the past is no longer true or possible.

Description: μηκέτι is a common word in Greek that is used in everyday speech and writing. It is often used to express a sense of finality or closure, as in "I am no longer interested" or "I can no longer do it". It can also be used to express a sense of relief or freedom, as in "I am no longer afraid" or "I am no longer bound by that obligation".
