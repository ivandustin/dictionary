μηλωταις (mēlōtais) is a Greek word that is the plural form of μηλωτή (mēlōtē), which means "apple tree". The word is derived from the Greek word μῆλον (mēlon), which means "apple". In terms of grammar, μηλωταις is a feminine noun in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of a sentence. 

Overall, μηλωταις refers to multiple apple trees, and can be used in various contexts such as in botany, agriculture, or even in literature and poetry.
