Word: μηδενος

Etymology:
The word μηδενος (mēdenos) is a Greek word that means "nothing" or "none". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words, μη (mē) meaning "not" and εδεν (eden) meaning "one".

Grammar:
μηδενος is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the nominative, accusative, and dative.

Description:
μηδενος is a word that is often used in Greek philosophy and mathematics to represent the concept of "nothingness" or "non-existence". It is also used in everyday language to indicate the absence of something or someone. For example, if someone asks for your opinion on a particular topic and you have no opinion, you might say "μηδενος" to indicate that you have no thoughts on the matter.
