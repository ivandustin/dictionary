Word: μηνας

Etymology:
The word μηνας (mēnas) is derived from the Greek word μήν (mēn), which means "month". It is related to the Sanskrit word māsa, the Latin word mensis, and the English word "month".

Grammar:
In Greek, μηνας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is also declined in the genitive case as μηνός (mēnos) and in the accusative case as μήνα (mēna).

Description:
μηνας is a Greek word that refers to a month, a period of time that is approximately 30 days long. In ancient Greece, the lunar calendar was used, and each month was named after the full moon that occurred during that time. The word μηνας is still used in modern Greek to refer to a month.
