Word: μηκυνηται

Etymology:
The word μηκυνηται is derived from the Greek word μηκύνω (mekyno), which means "to lengthen" or "to extend". The suffix -ται (-tai) is a third person plural ending, indicating that the word is in the passive voice.

Grammar:
μηκυνηται is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and third person plural form. It is conjugated from the verb μηκύνω (mekyno), which is a first conjugation verb in Ancient Greek.

Description:
μηκυνηται means "they are being lengthened" or "they are being extended". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the process of something becoming longer or more drawn out. For example, it could be used to describe the lengthening of a piece of fabric or the extension of a deadline.
