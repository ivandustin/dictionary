The word "λυουσιν" (lyousin) is a present active indicative third person plural form of the Greek verb "λύω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". The etymology of the word can be traced back to the Proto-Indo-European root *leu- which means "to loosen, divide, cut apart". In terms of grammar, "λυουσιν" is a regular verb form in the present tense, indicating that the action of loosening or untying is currently happening and being performed by a third person plural subject.
