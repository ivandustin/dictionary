Word: λυθησεται

Etymology:
λυθησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb λύω (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". The word is in the future tense and passive voice, meaning "it will be loosened" or "it will be untied".

λυθησεται is a third-person singular future passive indicative form of the verb λύω (lyo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The future tense indicates that the action will happen in the future, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
λυθησεται is a word that describes a future event where something will be loosened or untied. It can be used in a literal sense, such as untying a knot, or in a figurative sense, such as resolving a problem or releasing someone from a burden.
