Word: λυοντων

Etymology: λυοντων is the present participle of the Greek verb λύω (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leu- which means "to loosen, divide, cut apart".

Grammar: λυοντων is a masculine genitive plural form of the present participle of the verb λύω (lyo). In Greek, present participles are formed by adding -ων (-on) to the present stem of the verb. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: λυοντων can be translated as "of those who are loosening" or "of those who are untying". It is often used in the context of untying knots or unfastening something. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the disciples who were untying a colt for Jesus to ride on during his triumphal entry into Jerusalem (Mark 11:5).
