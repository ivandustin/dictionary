Word: λυδιαν

Etymology: The word λυδιαν (lydian) comes from the ancient Greek region of Lydia, which was located in what is now modern-day Turkey. The Lydians were known for their wealth and their use of gold and silver coins.

Grammar: λυδιαν is an adjective in the Greek language, which means "of or pertaining to Lydia." It is declined like other first and second declension adjectives in Greek.

Description: λυδιαν can be used to describe anything that is related to Lydia, such as its people, culture, language, or geography. It can also be used to describe something that is characteristic of Lydia or its people, such as their wealth or their use of coins.
