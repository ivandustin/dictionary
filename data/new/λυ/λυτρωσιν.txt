Word: λυτρωσιν

Etymology: λυτρωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb λυτρόω (lytroo), which means "to redeem" or "to ransom." It is derived from the noun λύτρον (lytron), which means "ransom" or "redemption price."

Grammar: λυτρωσιν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. It is the direct object of a verb in a sentence.

Description: λυτρωσιν refers to the act of redeeming or ransoming someone or something. It can also refer to the price paid for redemption or ransom. In Christian theology, it is often used to refer to the redemption of humanity through the sacrifice of Jesus Christ.
