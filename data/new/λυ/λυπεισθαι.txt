Word: λυπεισθαι

Etymology: λυπεισθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λυπη (lypē), which means "pain" or "grief". The verb form λυπεισθαι (lypeisthai) means "to be grieved" or "to be distressed".

Grammar: λυπεισθαι is a present passive infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by combining the present tense of the verb λυπεω (lypeō), which means "to grieve" or "to distress", with the passive voice ending -σθαι (-sthai).

Description: λυπεισθαι is a verb that describes a state of being grieved or distressed. It is often used in a passive sense, indicating that the subject is experiencing grief or distress rather than actively causing it. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the emotional state of characters who are mourning the loss of a loved one or experiencing some other form of emotional pain.
