Word: λυτρωσηται

Etymology: λυτρωσηται is a Greek word derived from the verb λυτρόω (lytróo), which means "to redeem" or "to ransom". The suffix -σηται (-setai) is a third person singular aorist middle/passive subjunctive form, indicating a potential action of redemption or ransom.

Grammar: λυτρωσηται is a third person singular aorist middle/passive subjunctive form of the verb λυτρόω (lytróo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on themselves or receiving the action. The subjunctive mood suggests a potential or hypothetical action.

Description: λυτρωσηται can be translated as "he/she/it might be redeemed" or "he/she/it might be ransomed". The word implies a sense of hope or possibility for redemption or rescue from a difficult situation. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the redemption of a soul or the salvation of a person.
