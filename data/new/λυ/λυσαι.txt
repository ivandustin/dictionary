The word λυσαι (lysai) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and active voice. It is the second person singular form of the verb λύω (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". The etymology of the word can be traced back to the Proto-Indo-European root *leu- which means "to loosen, divide, cut apart". In terms of grammar, λυσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative, which means it is a command directed towards one person to perform the action of "loosening" or "untying".
