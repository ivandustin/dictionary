Word: λυσω

Etymology:
λυσω (lysō) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the future tense. It is derived from the root word λυ- (lu-), which means "to loosen" or "to release."

Grammar:
λυσω (lysō) is a first-person singular future active indicative form of the verb λυω (lyō), which means "to loosen" or "to release." In Greek grammar, the future tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future.

Description:
λυσω (lysō) is a verb that describes the act of loosening or releasing something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as untying a knot, releasing a prisoner, or solving a problem. The future tense indicates that the action of loosening or releasing will happen at some point in the future.
