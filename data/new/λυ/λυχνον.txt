Word: λυχνον

Etymology:
The word λυχνον (lychnon) is derived from the Greek word λύχνος (lychnos), which means "lamp" or "lantern". The word is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *lewk-, which means "light".

Grammar:
λυχνον is a noun in the singular form in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. The genitive form of the word is λυχνου (lychnou).

Description:
λυχνον refers to a lamp or a lantern that is used to provide light. In ancient Greece, lamps were made of clay or bronze and were fueled by olive oil. They were used in homes, temples, and public places. The word is also used metaphorically to refer to something that provides guidance or illumination, such as knowledge or wisdom.
