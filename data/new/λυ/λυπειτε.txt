Word: λυπεῖτε (lypeite)

Etymology: λυπεῖτε is a present tense verb form in Greek, derived from the root word λυπέω (lypeo), which means "to cause pain or sorrow." It is formed by adding the present tense ending -εῖτε (-eite) to the stem.

Grammar: λυπεῖτε is a second-person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are currently causing pain or sorrow.

Description: λυπεῖτε is a verb that expresses the action of causing pain or sorrow. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as expressing sympathy or apologizing for causing harm. In the New Testament, it is often used in the context of repentance and seeking forgiveness.
