The word "λυσαντι" (lysanti) is a form of the Greek verb "λύω" (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". It is in the aorist tense and the active voice, indicating that the action of loosening or untying has already been completed by the subject of the sentence. 

In terms of grammar, "λυσαντι" is the third person singular form of the aorist active participle of "λύω". The aorist tense in Greek generally indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The participle form can function as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Etymologically, "λύω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leu- which means "to loosen, divide, cut apart". This root is also the source of words like "loose" and "release" in English.

Overall, "λυσαντι" is a specific form of the Greek verb "λύω" that indicates a completed action of loosening or untying in the past.
