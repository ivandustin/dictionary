The word λυπηθεντες (lypēthentes) is a Greek verb in the aorist participle form. It comes from the root word λυπέω (lypéō), which means "to grieve" or "to cause sorrow." The suffix -θεντες (-thentes) is a common ending for aorist participles in Greek, indicating past action.

In terms of grammar, λυπηθεντες is a participle, which is a verbal adjective that describes the action of the verb. It can function as an adjective, adverb, or noun in a sentence. In this case, it is used as an adjective to describe the subject of the sentence.

The word λυπηθεντες can be translated as "having been grieved" or "having been sorrowful." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe a state of mourning or repentance.
