Word: λυσάντες

Etymology:
λυσάντες is the aorist participle of the Greek verb λύω (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leu- which means "to loosen, divide, cut apart".

Grammar:
λυσάντες is a masculine plural nominative aorist participle in Ancient Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -αντ- (-ant-) to the stem λυ- (ly-) and the tense marker -α- (-a-). The participle indicates that the action of the verb has been completed in the past.

Description:
λυσάντες can be translated as "having loosened" or "having untied". It is often used in the New Testament to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples, who are said to have "loosened" or "untied" people from various afflictions, such as sickness or demonic possession. The word can also be used more generally to describe the act of releasing or freeing someone or something from a constraint or obligation.
