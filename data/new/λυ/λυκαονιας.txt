λυκαονιας (lykaonias) is a Greek word that is derived from the name of the mythical figure Lycaon, who was a king of Arcadia. The word is a feminine noun in the genitive case, and it refers to something that belongs to or is associated with Lycaon. In Greek mythology, Lycaon was known for his cruelty and was eventually turned into a wolf by the god Zeus as punishment.
