The word λυπην (lypēn) is a noun in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the root word λυπεω (lypeō), which means "to grieve" or "to cause pain." 

In terms of grammar, λυπην is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

As for its description, λυπην refers to a feeling of sadness or sorrow. It can also be translated as "grief" or "sorrow." In ancient Greek literature, it is often used to describe the emotional state of characters who are experiencing loss or hardship.
