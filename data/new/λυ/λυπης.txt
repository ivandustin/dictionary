Word: λυπης

Etymology:
The word λυπης (lypēs) is derived from the Greek verb λυπέω (lypéō), which means "to cause pain or sorrow." It is related to the Greek noun λύπη (lýpē), which means "pain, grief, or sorrow."

Grammar:
λυπης is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
λυπης is a Greek word that refers to the feeling of sadness or grief. It can also be used to describe a state of mourning or sorrow. In ancient Greek literature, the concept of λυπης was often associated with the idea of tragedy and the inevitability of suffering in life. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe feelings of sadness or grief.
