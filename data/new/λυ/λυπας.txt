The word "λυπας" (lypas) is a Greek verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It comes from the root word "λυπέω" (lypeo), which means "to cause pain, to grieve, to distress". 

In terms of grammar, "λυπας" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: λυπάς (lypas) - you cause pain/grieve/distress
- Plural: λυπάτε (lypate) - you all cause pain/grieve/distress

As for its meaning, "λυπας" refers to the act of causing pain, grief, or distress to someone or something. It can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a feeling of sadness or sorrow. 

Overall, "λυπας" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe emotional pain or distress.
