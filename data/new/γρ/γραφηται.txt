The word "γραφηται" (graphetai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "γράφω" (grapho), which means "to write". The suffix "-ται" (-tai) indicates that the verb is in the passive voice.

In terms of grammar, "γραφηται" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" (-o). In the passive voice, the subject of the sentence receives the action of the verb, rather than performing the action themselves.

In terms of description, "γραφηται" can be translated to mean "it is written" or "it is being written". It is often used in contexts where something is being recorded or documented, such as in legal or historical documents.
