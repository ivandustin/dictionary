Word: γρηγορησατε

Etymology: The word γρηγορησατε comes from the Greek verb γρηγορέω (grēgoreō), which means "to be awake, to be watchful, to be vigilant." The suffix -σατε (-sate) is the second person plural imperative ending, which means "you all" or "you guys."

Grammar: γρηγορησατε is a second person plural imperative verb form in the aorist tense. It is used to give a command or order to a group of people, telling them to be watchful or vigilant.

Description: γρηγορησατε is a strong and urgent command in Greek, urging people to be alert and watchful. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to encourage believers to be vigilant and ready for the return of Jesus Christ. The word emphasizes the importance of being alert and aware of one's surroundings, and is often used in contexts where there is a sense of danger or urgency.
