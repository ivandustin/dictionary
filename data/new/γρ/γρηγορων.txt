Word: γρηγορων

Etymology: 
The word γρηγορων (grēgorōn) is derived from the Greek verb γρηγορέω (grēgoreō), which means "to be awake, to be watchful, to be vigilant". The word is formed from the prefix γρη- (grē-), which means "watchful", and the verb ἐγείρω (egeirō), which means "to wake up".

Grammar:
γρηγορων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is in the present active participle tense, which means it describes an ongoing action. The word is declined according to the second declension, which means it follows a specific pattern of endings depending on its grammatical case.

Description:
γρηγορων is often translated as "watchful" or "vigilant". It is used to describe someone who is alert and attentive, especially in a spiritual or moral sense. In Christian theology, the word is often used to describe the state of being spiritually awake and aware of one's surroundings.
