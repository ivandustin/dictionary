Word: γραψαντες

Etymology: γραψαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb γράφω (grapho), which means "to write." The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb has been completed.

Grammar: γραψαντες is the aorist active participle of the verb γράφω (grapho). It is in the masculine plural form, which means it refers to a group of male subjects who have completed the action of writing.

Description: γραψαντες can be translated to "having written" or "having written down." It is often used in Greek literature to describe a group of people who have written something, such as a letter or a document. In English, it can be translated as "they wrote" or "they have written."
