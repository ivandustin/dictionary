Word: γραφαι

Etymology: The word γραφαι comes from the Greek word γραφή (graphē), which means "writing" or "scripture". The suffix -αι (-ai) is a plural ending, so γραφαι means "writings" or "scriptures".

Grammar: γραφαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: γραφαι refers to writings or scriptures, particularly those that are considered sacred or religious. The word can be used to refer to the Bible or other religious texts. In ancient Greece, γραφαι could also refer to legal documents or official records.
