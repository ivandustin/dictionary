Word: γρηγορουντας

Etymology: The word γρηγορουντας comes from the Greek verb γρηγορέω (grēgoreō), which means "to be awake, to be watchful, to be vigilant". The suffix -ουντας (-ountas) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently in the act of being vigilant or watchful.

Grammar: γρηγορουντας is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb γρηγορέω, which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense. The word can function as an adjective, describing someone who is currently vigilant or watchful.

Description: γρηγορουντας describes someone who is currently awake, alert, and watchful. It can be used to describe someone who is keeping watch over something, or someone who is paying close attention to their surroundings.
