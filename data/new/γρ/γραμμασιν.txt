Word: γραμμασιν

Etymology: The word γραμμασιν comes from the Greek word γράμμα (gramma), which means "letter" or "writing". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a plural ending, so γραμμασιν means "letters" or "writings".

Grammar: γραμμασιν is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: γραμμασιν refers to letters or writings, usually in the context of written communication. It can also refer to the alphabet or the art of writing. In ancient Greece, γραμμασιν was an important skill, as writing was used for record-keeping, literature, and communication. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to letters or writing.
