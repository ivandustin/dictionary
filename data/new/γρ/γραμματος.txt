Word: γραμματος

Etymology: The word γραμματος comes from the Greek word γράμμα (gramma), which means "letter" or "writing". The suffix -τος (-tos) is a common Greek suffix that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: γραμματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of the letter" or "of the writing".

Description: γραμματος is a Greek word that refers to a letter or writing. It can be used to describe any kind of written communication, including letters, documents, or books. In ancient Greece, writing was an important skill, and many people were trained in the art of writing. The word γραμματος is still used in modern Greek to refer to letters or writing, and it is also used in scientific terms, such as in the word "grammatology", which is the study of writing systems and their cultural significance.
