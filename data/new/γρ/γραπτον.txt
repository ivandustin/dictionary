Word: γραπτον

Etymology:
The word γραπτον comes from the Greek word γραπτός (graptós), which means "written" or "inscribed". It is derived from the verb γράφω (grapho), which means "to write".

Grammar:
γραπτον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word γραπτον is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
γραπτον refers to something that is written or inscribed. It can be used to describe a written document, a piece of literature, or any other written material. The word can also be used to describe something that is engraved or etched, such as an inscription on a monument or a piece of jewelry.
