Word: γραμματεων

Etymology: The word γραμματεων comes from the Greek word γραμματεύς (grammateus), which means "scribe" or "secretary". It is derived from the word γράμμα (gramma), which means "letter" or "writing".

Grammar: γραμματεων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship to another noun. In this case, it means "of the scribes" or "belonging to the scribes".

Description: γραμματεων is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the scribes, who were highly educated individuals responsible for copying and interpreting religious texts. They were considered experts in Jewish law and were often consulted by the people for legal and religious advice. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to any professional writer or secretary.
