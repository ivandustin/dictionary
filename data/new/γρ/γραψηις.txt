Word: γραψηις

Etymology: The word γραψηις comes from the Greek word γραφή (graphḗ), which means "writing" or "scripture". The suffix -ηις (-ēis) is a nominalizing suffix, which means that it turns a verb into a noun. Therefore, γραψηις can be translated as "a writing" or "a written work".

Grammar: γραψηις is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the first declension and has the genitive form γραφῆς (graphēs). In English, it can be translated as "writing", "scripture", or "written work".

Description: γραψηις refers to any kind of written work, including books, letters, documents, or inscriptions. In ancient Greece, it was used to refer to the works of famous writers, such as Homer or Hesiod. In the context of Christianity, it is often used to refer to the Holy Scriptures or the Bible. Overall, γραψηις is a versatile word that can be used to describe any kind of written work, regardless of its content or purpose.
