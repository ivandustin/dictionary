Etymology: The word "τυχοι" (tychoi) comes from the Greek word "τύχη" (tyche), which means "luck" or "fortune". 

Grammar: "Τυχοι" is the plural form of the noun "τυχή" (tyche), which is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: η τυχή (i tyche) 
- Genitive: της τυχής (tis tyches)
- Accusative: την τυχή (tin tyche)
- Vocative: ω τυχή (o tyche)

Plural:
- Nominative: οι τυχοι (i tychoi)
- Genitive: των τυχών (ton tyhon)
- Accusative: τους τυχούς (tous tyhous)
- Vocative: ω τυχοί (o tychoi)

Description: "Τυχοι" refers to "luck" or "fortunes" in Greek. It can be used to describe both positive and negative outcomes or events that occur by chance or without control.
