Etymology: The word "τυφλού" (tyflou) comes from the Greek adjective "τυφλός" (tyflos), which means "blind". 

Grammar: "Τυφλού" is the genitive form of the word "τυφλός" (tyflos), which is used to indicate possession or relationship. It is commonly used in phrases like "το βλέμμα τυφλού" (the blind person's gaze) or "η ανάγκη τυφλού" (the blind person's need).

Description: "Τυφλού" refers to something that belongs to or is related to a blind person. It can be used to describe various aspects of blindness, such as physical characteristics, abilities, or experiences.
