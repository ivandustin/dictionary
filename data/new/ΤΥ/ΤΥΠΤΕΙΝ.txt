Etymology: The word "τυπτειν" (typtein) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "τύπτω" (typto), which means "to strike" or "to beat".

Grammar: In Greek, "τυπτειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. It belongs to the first conjugation class of verbs, which have the ending "-ειν" in the infinitive form.
