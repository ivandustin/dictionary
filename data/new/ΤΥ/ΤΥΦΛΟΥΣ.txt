Etymology: The word "τυφλούς" (typhlous) comes from the Greek word "τυφλός" (typhlos), meaning "blind."

Grammar: In Greek, "τυφλούς" is the plural form of the adjective "τυφλός" (typhlos), which means "blind." It is used to describe multiple blind individuals.

Description: "Τυφλούς" is an adjective in Greek that specifically refers to multiple blind people. It is used to describe individuals who have a complete or partial loss of vision.
