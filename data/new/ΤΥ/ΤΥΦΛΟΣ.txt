Etymology: The word "τυφλός" (typhlós) comes from the Ancient Greek word "τυφλός" (tuphlós), meaning "blind".

Grammar: In Greek, "τυφλός" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: τυφλός (masculine), τυφλή (feminine), and τυφλό (neuter).

Description: "Τυφλός" is used to describe someone or something that is unable to see or lacks the sense of sight. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a lack of understanding or insight.
