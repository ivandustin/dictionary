Etymology: The word "ικανός" (ikanós) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἱκανός" (hikanós), meaning "sufficient" or "capable."

Grammar: In Greek, "ικανός" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used in the masculine, feminine, or neuter gender, and in the singular or plural form.

Description: "Ικανός" is used to describe someone or something that is capable, competent, or sufficient for a particular task or purpose. It can also mean "enough" or "adequate."
