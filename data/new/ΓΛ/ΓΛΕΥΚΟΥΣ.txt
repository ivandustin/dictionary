Etymology: The word "γλευκούς" (gleukous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "γλεῦκος" (gleûkos), meaning "sweet wine" or "must." 

Grammar: In Greek, "γλευκούς" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Γλευκούς" refers to a type of sweet wine or must. It is often used to describe a specific variety of wine that is sweet and has a high sugar content. This term is commonly used in the context of Greek wine-making and wine-tasting.
