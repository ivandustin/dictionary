Etymology: The word "ηχος" (ichos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἦχος" (ēchos), which means "sound" or "noise".

Grammar: In Greek, "ηχος" (ichos) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "ηχου" (ichou).

Description: "Ηχος" (ichos) refers to the physical phenomenon of sound. It can also be used to describe a specific sound or noise. In a broader sense, it can be used metaphorically to represent the impact or influence of something.
