Etymology: The word "ηχώ" (ichó) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἠχώ" (ēkhṓ), which means "echo" or "sound."

Grammar: "Ηχώ" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form of "ηχού" (ichú).

Description: "Ηχώ" refers to the phenomenon of sound reflection, specifically the repetition of a sound caused by the reflection of sound waves off a surface. It is commonly used to describe the sound that bounces back to the listener after hitting a solid object, such as a wall or a mountain.
