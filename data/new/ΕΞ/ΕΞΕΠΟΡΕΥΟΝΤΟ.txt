Etymology: The word "εξεπορευοντο" comes from the Greek verb "εξερχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to depart". The word is formed by adding the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", to the verb "ερχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξεπορευοντο" is the third person plural, imperfect, indicative active form of the verb "εξερχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: "Εξεπορευοντο" can be translated as "they were going out" or "they were departing". It describes a group of people or things in the past who were in the process of leaving or departing from a certain place.
