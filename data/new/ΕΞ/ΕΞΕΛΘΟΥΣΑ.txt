Etymology: The word "εξελθουσα" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit."

Grammar: "Εξελθουσα" is the feminine form of the participle of the verb "εξέρχομαι" in the present tense. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently going out or exiting.

Description: "Εξελθουσα" is a Greek word that describes a female subject who is in the process of going out or exiting from a place. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone leaving a building, a room, or a situation.
