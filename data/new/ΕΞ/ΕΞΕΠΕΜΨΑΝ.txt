Etymology: The word "εξεπεμψαν" comes from the Greek verb "εξεπέμπω" (exepémpo), which means "to send out" or "to dispatch". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of" or "from", and the verb "πέμπω" (pémpo), meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Εξεπέμπαν" is the third person plural, past tense form of the verb "εξεπέμπω" (exepémpo). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on tense, person, and number. The past tense indicates that the action of sending out or dispatching occurred in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: "Εξεπέμπαν" describes the action of a group of people or things sending out or dispatching something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of messengers sending out messages, a group of soldiers being deployed, or a group of items being distributed.
