Etymology: The word "εξεπορευετο" comes from the Greek verb "εξερχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to depart". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb "ερχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξεπορευετο" is the third person singular, imperfect tense form of the verb "εξερχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action.

Description: "Εξεπορευετο" can be translated as "he/she/it was going out" or "he/she/it was departing". It describes an action of someone or something leaving or going out from a particular place.
