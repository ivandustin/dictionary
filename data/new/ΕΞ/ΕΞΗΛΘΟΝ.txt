Grammar: "Εξηλθον" is the aorist tense form of the verb "ἐξέρχομαι" (exerchomai) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition.

Description: "Εξηλθον" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek, and it translates to "I went out" or "I came out" in English. It is commonly used in narratives or descriptions to indicate a specific action of going out or coming out from a place.
