Etymology: The word "εξεβαλον" comes from the Greek verb "εξεβάλλω" (exevállo), which means "to cast out" or "to expel". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb "βάλλω" (vállo), meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar: "Εξεβαλον" is the aorist tense form of the verb "εξεβάλλω" (exevállo) in the first person singular. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εξεβαλον" is a Greek word that translates to "I cast out" or "I expelled" in English. It is used to express the act of forcefully removing or driving something or someone away.
