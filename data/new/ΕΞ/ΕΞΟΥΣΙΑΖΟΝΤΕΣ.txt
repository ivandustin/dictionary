Etymology: The word "εξουσιαζοντες" comes from the Greek verb "εξουσιάζω" (exousiazo), which means "to exercise authority" or "to have power over." The suffix "-ντες" (-ntes) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Εξουσιαζοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "εξουσιάζω" in Greek. It is used to describe people who are currently exercising authority or having power over others. In terms of grammar, it functions as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Description: "Εξουσιαζοντες" refers to individuals who are in a position of authority or power, and are actively exercising that authority. It can be used to describe people in various contexts, such as political leaders, rulers, or those who have control over others in a specific domain.
