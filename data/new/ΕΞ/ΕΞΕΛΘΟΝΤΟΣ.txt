Etymology: The word "εξελθοντος" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb "έρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξελθοντος" is the genitive form of the verb "εξέρχομαι" in the masculine singular. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. It is often translated as "of" or "from" in English.

Description: "Εξελθοντος" is a word that describes someone or something that is going out or exiting from a place. It can be used to indicate physical movement or metaphorical departure.
