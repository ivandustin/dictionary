Etymology: The word "εξελθουσαν" (exelthousan) is derived from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit."

Grammar: "Εξελθουσαν" is the third person singular, aorist active participle of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εξελθουσαν" is a Greek word that can be translated as "she/he/it went out" or "she/he/it exited." It is used to describe the action of someone or something leaving or going out from a particular place.
