Grammar: "Εξαγει" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "εξάγω" (exago). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the sentence is a singular third person (he/she/it) and the action is happening in the present.

Description: "Εξαγει" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it leads out" or "he/she/it brings out". It is used to describe the action of leading or bringing something or someone out from a place or situation.
