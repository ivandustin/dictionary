Etymology: The word "εξεβληθη" comes from the Greek verb "εξεβάλλω" (exevállo), which means "to throw out" or "to expel." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out," and the verb "βάλλω" (vállo), meaning "to throw."

Grammar: "Εξεβληθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "εξεβάλλω" (exevállo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Εξεβληθη" can be translated as "he/she/it was thrown out" or "he/she/it was expelled" in English. It describes a past action of someone or something being forcefully removed or ejected from a place or position.
