Etymology: The word "εξεπλευσαμεν" comes from the Greek verb "εξεπλέω" (exepléo), which means "to sail out" or "to set sail." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out," and the verb "πλέω" (pléo), meaning "to sail."

Grammar: "Εξεπλευσαμεν" is the first person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood form of the verb "εξεπλέω" (exepléo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "Εξεπλευσαμεν" can be translated to English as "we sailed out" or "we set sail." It describes a past action of a group of people leaving or starting a journey by boat.
