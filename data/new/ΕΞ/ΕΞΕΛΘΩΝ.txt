Etymology: The word "εξελθων" (exelthōn) comes from the Greek verb "ἐξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". It is derived from the prefix "ἐξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: "Εξελθων" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "ἐξέρχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Εξελθων" can be translated as "having gone out" or "having come forth". It is often used in Greek texts, including the New Testament, to describe someone or something that has exited or emerged from a particular place or situation.
