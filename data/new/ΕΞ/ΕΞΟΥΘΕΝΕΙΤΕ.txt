Etymology: The word "εξουθενείτε" comes from the Greek verb "εξουθενώ" (exouthenó), which means "to despise" or "to consider as nothing." The suffix "-είτε" is added to the verb stem to form the second person plural present tense imperative form, giving the meaning "you all despise" or "you all consider as nothing."

Grammar: "Εξουθενείτε" is the second person plural present tense imperative form of the verb "εξουθενώ" (exouthenó). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to despise or consider something as nothing.

Description: "Εξουθενείτε" is a Greek verb form that expresses the command or instruction for a group of people to despise or consider something as nothing. It is used in situations where the speaker wants to convey a strong sense of disregard or insignificance towards a particular thing or idea.
