Etymology: The word "εξεβησαν" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is formed by combining the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb "έρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξεβησαν" is the third person plural, past tense form of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The "-αν" ending in "εξεβησαν" indicates that it is in the past tense and refers to a third person plural subject.

Description: "Εξεβησαν" is a Greek verb that means "they went out" or "they exited". It is used to describe an action of a group of people leaving a place or moving away from a certain location.
