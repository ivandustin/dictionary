Etymology: The word "εξενέγκαντες" comes from the Greek verb "εξενέγκω" (exenéngo), which means "to carry out" or "to bring out". It is formed by combining the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb "φέρω" (féro), meaning "to carry".

Grammar: "Εξενέγκαντες" is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the verb "εξενέγκω". In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Εξενέγκαντες" describes a group of people who have carried out or brought out something. The exact meaning and context of the word would depend on the specific sentence or context in which it is used.
