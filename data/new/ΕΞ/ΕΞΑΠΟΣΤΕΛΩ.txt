Etymology: The word "εξαποστελω" (exapostelo) comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out" or "from," and "αποστελλω" (apostello), meaning "to send." 

Grammar: "Εξαποστελω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εξαποστέλλω, εξαποστέλλεις, εξαποστέλλει, εξαποστέλλουμε, εξαποστέλλετε, εξαποστέλλουν.

Description: "Εξαποστελω" means "to send out" or "to dispatch" in English. It is used to describe the action of sending someone or something away or to a specific destination.
