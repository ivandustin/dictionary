Etymology: The word "εξεληλυθοτας" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". The word is formed by adding the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out" or "from", to the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξεληλυθοτας" is the masculine singular form of the past participle of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek grammar, the past participle is used to form compound tenses and passive voice. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Εξεληλυθοτας" can be translated as "having gone out" or "having come forth". It describes someone or something that has completed the action of going out or coming forth.
