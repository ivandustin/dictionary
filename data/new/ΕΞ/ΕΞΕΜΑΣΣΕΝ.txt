Etymology: The word "εξεμασσεν" comes from the Greek verb "εξεμασσω" (eksemasso), which means "to wipe out" or "to erase." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out," and the verb "μασσω" (masso), meaning "to wipe" or "to erase."

Grammar: "Εξεμασσεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "εξεμασσω" in the aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the action was performed by a singular subject.

Description: "Εξεμασσεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it wiped out" or "he/she/it erased." It is used to describe the action of completely removing or eliminating something.
