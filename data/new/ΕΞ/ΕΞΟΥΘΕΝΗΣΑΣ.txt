Etymology: The word "εξουθενησας" (exouthenēsas) comes from the Greek verb "εξουθενέω" (exoutheneō), which means "to despise" or "to consider as nothing." It is derived from the prefix "εξ" (ex), meaning "out" or "from," and the noun "ουθενός" (outhenos), meaning "nothing" or "worthless."

Grammar: "Εξουθενησας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "εξουθενέω" (exoutheneō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Εξουθενησας" can be translated as "having despised" or "having considered as nothing." It describes an action that was completed in the past, where the subject of the verb despised or regarded something or someone as worthless.
