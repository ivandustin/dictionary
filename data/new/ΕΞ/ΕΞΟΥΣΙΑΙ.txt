Etymology: The word "εξουσία" comes from the Greek word "ἐξουσία" (exousía), which means "power" or "authority". It is derived from the verb "ἔχω" (échō), meaning "to have" or "to possess".

Grammar: "Εξουσία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case and singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "εξουσίες" (exousíes).

Description: "Εξουσία" refers to the concept of power, authority, or control. It can be used to describe political power, legal authority, or the ability to influence or command others. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the freedom or right to do something.
