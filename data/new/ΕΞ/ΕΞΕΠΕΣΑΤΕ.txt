Etymology: The word "εξεπέσατε" comes from the Greek verb "εξέπιπτω" (exépipto), which means "to fall out" or "to fall from." It is formed by combining the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out" or "from," and the verb "έπιπτω" (épipto), meaning "to fall."

Grammar: "Εξεπέσατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "εξέπιπτω" (exépipto) in the past tense. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they fell out or fell from something.

Description: "Εξεπέσατε" is a Greek verb that describes the action of falling out or falling from something. It can be used in various contexts, such as falling out of a group, falling from grace, or falling from a position of power.
