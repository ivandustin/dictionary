Etymology: The word "εξαρτίσαι" comes from the Greek verb "εξαρτώ" (exartó), which means "to depend on" or "to be contingent upon". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of" or "from", and the verb "αρτίζω" (artízo), meaning "to equip" or "to furnish".

Grammar: "Εξαρτίσαι" is the third person singular form of the verb "εξαρτώμαι" (exartóme), which is a reflexive verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the present tense and indicates that the subject depends on or is contingent upon something or someone.

Description: "Εξαρτίσαι" is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of dependence or contingency. It is used to describe a situation where something or someone relies on or is influenced by another thing or person.
