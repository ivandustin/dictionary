Etymology: The word "εξελθης" (exelthēs) comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξελθης" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εξελθης" is a Greek word that translates to "you went out" or "you exited" in English. It is used to refer to the action of someone leaving or going out from a place.
