Etymology: The word "εξελθοντι" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb "έρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξελθοντι" is the aorist participle form of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai) in the masculine singular dative case. In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εξελθοντι" can be translated as "having gone out" or "having exited" in English. It is used to describe a person or thing that has completed the action of going out or exiting.
