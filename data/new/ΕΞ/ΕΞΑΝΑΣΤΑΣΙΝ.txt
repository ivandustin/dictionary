Etymology: The word "εξαναστασιν" comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out" or "from," and "αναστασιν" (anastasin), meaning "resurrection" or "rising up." 

Grammar: "Εξαναστασιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Εξαναστασιν" refers to a resurrection or a rising up from a previous state. It can be used in a literal sense to describe a physical resurrection, or in a metaphorical sense to describe a revival or reawakening.
