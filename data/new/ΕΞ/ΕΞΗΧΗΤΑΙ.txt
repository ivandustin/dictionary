Etymology: The word "εξηχηται" comes from the Greek verb "εξηχέω" (exēchéō), which means "to resound" or "to echo." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out" or "from," and the verb "ηχέω" (ēchéō), meaning "to sound" or "to make a noise."

Grammar: "Εξηχηται" is the third person singular form of the verb "εξηχέω" in the present tense. It is in the indicative mood and the active voice. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice.

Description: "Εξηχηται" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it resounds" or "he/she/it echoes." It describes the action of producing a sound that is reflected or repeated back from a surface.
