Etymology: The word "εξουθενείτω" comes from the Greek verb "εξουθενέω" (exouthenéo), which means "to despise" or "to treat as nothing." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out" or "away," and the noun "ούθεν" (outhen), meaning "nothing."

Grammar: "Εξουθενείτω" is a verb in the imperative mood and the third person singular form. It is used to give a command or instruction, specifically telling someone to despise or treat something as nothing.

Description: "Εξουθενείτω" is a strong and forceful word that conveys the idea of completely disregarding or devaluing something. It implies a sense of contempt or disdain towards the object or concept being referred to.
