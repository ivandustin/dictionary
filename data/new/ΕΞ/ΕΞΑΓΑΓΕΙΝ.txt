Etymology: The word "εξαγαγείν" (exagagein) comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out," and "αγαγείν" (agagein), meaning "to lead." 

Grammar: "Εξαγαγείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to a specific tense, mood, or person.

Description: "Εξαγαγείν" means "to lead out" or "to bring out" in English. It implies the action of taking something or someone out of a particular place or situation.
