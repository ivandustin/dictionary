Etymology: The word "εξεψύχεν" comes from the Greek verb "εξεψύχω" (exepsýcho), which means "to exhale" or "to breathe out." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out," and the verb "ψύχω" (psýcho), meaning "to breathe" or "to blow."

Grammar: "Εξεψύχεν" is the third person singular, present tense form of the verb "εξεψύχω" in the active voice. It is used to describe an action performed by a singular subject in the present time.

Description: The word "εξεψύχεν" describes the act of exhaling or breathing out. It can be used to refer to the physical act of exhaling air from the lungs or metaphorically to express the release or expression of emotions, thoughts, or ideas.
