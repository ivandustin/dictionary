Etymology: The word "εξεπετασα" comes from the Greek verb "εξεπετάω" (exepetáo), which means "to fly out" or "to take off". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb "πετάω" (petáo), meaning "to fly".

Grammar: "Εξεπετάσα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "εξεπετάζω" (exepetázo), which means "I flew out" or "I took off". It belongs to the regular conjugation pattern of Greek verbs.

Description: "Εξεπετάσα" is a verb form that describes an action of flying out or taking off. It is used to indicate that the subject (in this case, the first person singular) performed the action of flying out or taking off in the past.
