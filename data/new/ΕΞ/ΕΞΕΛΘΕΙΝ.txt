Etymology: The word "εξελθείν" (exelthein) comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out," and "ελθείν" (elthein), meaning "to come." 

Grammar: "Εξελθείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, suggesting that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Εξελθείν" means "to go out" or "to exit" in English. It is commonly used to describe physically leaving a place or situation. It can also be used metaphorically to express the idea of moving away from a certain state or condition.
