Etymology: The word "εξηρχοντο" (exērkhonto) comes from the Greek verb "ἐξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come out". It is derived from the prefix "ἐξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: "Εξηρχοντο" is the third person plural, imperfect tense form of the verb "ἐξέρχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is multiple people or things.
