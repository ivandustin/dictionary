Etymology: The word "εξηρανθη" (exeranthi) comes from the Greek verb "ξηραίνω" (xeraino), which means "to dry up" or "to wither". The suffix "-θη" (-thi) indicates that the verb is in the aorist tense, which refers to a completed action in the past.

Grammar: "Εξηρανθη" (exeranthi) is the third person singular form of the verb "ξηραίνω" (xeraino) in the aorist tense. It is used to describe an action that happened in the past and is completed.

Description: "Εξηρανθη" (exeranthi) can be translated as "it dried up" or "it withered". It is often used to describe the process of something losing moisture or vitality, becoming dry or wilted.
