Etymology: The word "εξεπνευσεν" comes from the Greek verb "εξεπνέω" (exepnéo), which means "to breathe out" or "to exhale." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out," and the verb "πνέω" (pnéo), meaning "to breathe."

Grammar: "Εξεπνευσεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "εξεπνέω" (exepnéo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Εξεπνευσεν" can be translated as "he/she/it breathed out" or "he/she/it exhaled." It describes the action of someone or something expelling air from their lungs.
