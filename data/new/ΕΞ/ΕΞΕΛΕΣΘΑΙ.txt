Etymology: The word "εξελεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to come forth". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out of", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar: "Εξελεσθαι" is the present infinitive form of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek grammar, the infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb without specifying the subject or tense. It can be translated as "to go out" or "to come forth".

Description: "Εξελεσθαι" is a Greek verb that describes the action of going out or coming forth. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically leaving a place, emerging from a situation, or expressing a figurative sense of liberation or progress.
