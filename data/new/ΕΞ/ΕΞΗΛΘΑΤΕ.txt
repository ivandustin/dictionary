Etymology: The word "εξηλθατε" (exēlthate) is derived from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is formed by combining the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξηλθατε" is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they have gone out or exited from a place.

Description: "Εξηλθατε" is a verb form that expresses the action of going out or exiting. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as giving instructions, describing past events, or expressing commands.
