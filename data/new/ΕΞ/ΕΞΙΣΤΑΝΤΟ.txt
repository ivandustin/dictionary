Etymology: The word "εξισταντο" comes from the Greek verb "εξίστημι" (exístēmi), which means "to astonish" or "to amaze". It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out" or "away", and the verb "ίστημι" (ístēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: "Εξισταντο" is the third person singular, past tense form of the verb "εξίστημι" (exístēmi). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense form indicates that the action of astonishing or amazing happened in the past and was performed by a third person.

Description: "Εξισταντο" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it astonished" or "he/she/it amazed". It describes a state of being surprised, amazed, or astounded.
