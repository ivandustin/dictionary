Etymology: The word "εξοδος" (exodos) comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out," and "οδος" (odos), meaning "way" or "path." It literally translates to "way out" or "exit."

Grammar: "Εξοδος" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is classified as a feminine noun.

Description: "Εξοδος" refers to an exit or a way out. It can be used to describe physical exits, such as doors or gates, as well as metaphorical exits, such as leaving a place or situation. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the act of departing or going away from a particular location or situation.
