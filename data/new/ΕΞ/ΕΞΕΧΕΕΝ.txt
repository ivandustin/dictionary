Etymology: The word "εξεχείν" (exekheín) comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out" or "from," and "εχείν" (ekheín), meaning "to have" or "to hold." 

Grammar: "Εξεχείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. 

Description: "Εξεχείν" means "to excel" or "to surpass" in English. It is used to describe the act of being superior or outstanding in a particular field or aspect.
