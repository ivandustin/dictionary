Etymology: The word "εξωτερον" (exōteron) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εξω-" (exo-), meaning "outside," and the noun "τερον" (teron), meaning "part." 

Grammar: "Εξωτερον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. 

Description: "Εξωτερον" refers to something external or outside. It can be used to describe physical objects or abstract concepts that are located or exist outside of a particular place or context.
