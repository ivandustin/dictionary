Etymology: The word "εξεπεσεν" (exepesen) comes from the Greek verb "εξέπιπτω" (exepipto), which means "to fall out" or "to fall from." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out," and the verb "πίπτω" (pipto), meaning "to fall."

Grammar: "Εξεπέσεν" is the third person singular past tense form of the verb "εξέπιπτω" (exepipto). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense form indicates that the action of falling out or falling from has already happened in the past.

Description: "Εξεπέσεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it fell out" or "he/she/it fell from." It is used to describe an action of something or someone falling out or falling from a certain place or position.
