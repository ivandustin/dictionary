Etymology: The word "εξερχόμενων" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit." The suffix "-ων" (-on) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or in progress.

Grammar: "Εξερχόμενων" is the present participle form of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai) in the plural masculine genitive case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The plural masculine genitive case indicates possession or association with a group of males.

Description: "Εξερχόμενων" can be translated as "of those going out" or "of those exiting" in English. It refers to a group of people or things that are currently in the process of leaving or going out from a particular place.
