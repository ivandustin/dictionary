Etymology: The word "εξηγείρα" (exēgeira) comes from the Greek verb "εξηγέομαι" (exēgeomai), which means "to explain" or "to interpret." It is derived from the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out" or "from," and the verb "ηγέομαι" (ēgeomai), meaning "to lead" or "to guide."

Grammar: "Εξηγείρα" is the first person singular form of the verb "εξηγείρω" (exēgeirō) in the past tense. It is used to indicate that the speaker explained or interpreted something in the past.

Description: "Εξηγείρα" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the act of explaining or interpreting something. It implies the process of making something clear or understandable to others by providing information, details, or context.
