Etymology: The word "εξουθενείς" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "εξουθενώ" (exouthenó), which means "to despise" or "to consider as worthless."

Grammar: "Εξουθενείς" is a verb in the second person singular form of the present tense in Greek. It is used to address someone directly and means "you despise" or "you consider as worthless."

Description: "Εξουθενείς" is a strong word that expresses a strong negative sentiment towards someone or something. It implies a deep sense of disregard, contempt, or devaluation.
