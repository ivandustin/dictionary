Etymology: The word "εξαναστηση" (exanastisi) comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out" or "from," and "αναστατω" (anastato), meaning "to disturb" or "to unsettle." It literally translates to "to unsettle out" or "to disturb from."

Grammar: "Εξαναστηση" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Εξαναστηση" refers to the act of unsettling or disturbing something or someone. It can be used to describe a state of unrest, rebellion, or revolution.
