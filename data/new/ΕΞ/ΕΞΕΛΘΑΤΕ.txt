Etymology: The word "εξελθατε" comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is formed by combining the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb "έρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "εξελθατε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exérchomai). In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The second person plural form is used when addressing a group of people.

Description: "εξελθατε" is a command or request for a group of people to go out or exit from a certain place. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as giving directions, instructing a group to leave a location, or urging a group to move forward.
