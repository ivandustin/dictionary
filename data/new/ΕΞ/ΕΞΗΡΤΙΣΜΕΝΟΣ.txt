The word "εξηρτισμένος" is a Greek adjective that can be translated as "educated" or "cultivated" in English. Here is the breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "εξηρτισμένος" comes from the Greek verb "εξηρτώ" (exartó), which means "to train" or "to educate." The suffix "-μένος" (-menos) is added to the verb stem to form the adjective.

Grammar: "Εξηρτισμένος" is the masculine singular form of the adjective. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The adjective "εξηρτισμένος" describes someone who has received a good education or has cultivated knowledge and refinement. It implies that the person is well-informed, sophisticated, and has a broad understanding of various subjects.
