Etymology: The word "εξωθεν" (exōthen) comes from the Greek roots "εξω" (exo), meaning "outside," and "θεν" (then), meaning "from." 

Grammar: "Εξωθεν" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate that something is happening or originating from the outside or external source.

Description: "Εξωθεν" is used to describe actions, events, or influences that come from an external source or occur externally. It implies that something is happening or originating from outside of a particular situation or context.
