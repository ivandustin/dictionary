Etymology: The word "εξορκίζω" (exorkízo) comes from the Greek prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out" or "away," and the verb "ορκίζω" (orkízo), meaning "to swear" or "to bind with an oath." Therefore, "εξορκίζω" can be translated as "to exorcise" or "to drive out by swearing."

- Εγώ εξορκίζω (ego exorkízo) - I exorcise
- Εσύ εξορκίζεις (esý exorkízeis) - You exorcise (singular, informal)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό εξορκίζει (aftós/aftí/aftó exorkízei) - He/She/It exorcises
- Εμείς εξορκίζουμε (emeís exorkízoume) - We exorcise
- Εσείς εξορκίζετε (eseís exorkízete) - You exorcise (plural, formal)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές εξορκίζουν (aftoí/aftés exorkízoun) - They exorcise
