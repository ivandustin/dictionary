Etymology: The word "εξηλθες" (exēlthes) comes from the Greek verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai), which means "to go out" or "to exit". It is formed by combining the prefix "εξ-" (ex-), meaning "out", and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Εξηλθες" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "εξέρχομαι" (exerchomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εξηλθες" is a Greek word that translates to "you went out" or "you exited" in English. It is used to refer to the action of someone leaving or going out from a place.
