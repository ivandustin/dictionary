Etymology: The word "εξοδον" (exodon) comes from the Greek roots "εξ" (ex), meaning "out," and "οδος" (odos), meaning "way" or "path." It literally translates to "outway" or "exit."

Grammar: "Εξοδον" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Εξοδον" refers to an exit or a way out. It can be used to describe a physical exit, such as a door or a gate, or a metaphorical exit, such as an escape from a difficult situation or a departure from a place.
