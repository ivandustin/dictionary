Etymology: The word "ελιθοβολησαν" comes from the Greek roots "ελιθος" (stone) and "βολη" (throwing). It literally means "they threw stones".

Grammar: "Ελιθοβολησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "ελιθοβολώ" (eli-tho-vo-LO), which is in the aorist tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate different grammatical aspects, such as tense, mood, and voice.

Description: "Ελιθοβολησαν" is a verb in the Greek language that describes the action of throwing stones. It is used to indicate that a group of people threw stones at someone or something.
