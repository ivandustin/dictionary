Etymology: The word "ελαλησαν" comes from the Greek verb "λαλώ" (laló), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, making it "ελάλησαν" (elálisan), which translates to "they spoke" or "they talked".

Grammar: "Ελάλησαν" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "λαλώ" (laló). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. In this case, "ελάλησαν" indicates that the action of speaking or talking was performed by a group of people in the past.

Description: "Ελάλησαν" is a word in the Greek language that specifically refers to the past action of speaking or talking by a group of people. It is commonly used in narratives or descriptions to indicate that a conversation or dialogue took place in the past.
