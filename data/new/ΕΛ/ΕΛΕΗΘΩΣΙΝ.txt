Etymology: The word "ελεηθωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "ελεηθω" (eleēthō), which means "to have mercy" or "to show compassion."

Grammar: "Ελεηθωσιν" is a noun in the accusative case, plural form. It is derived from the verb "ελεηθω" and is used to express the act of showing mercy or compassion.

Description: "Ελεηθωσιν" refers to the act of showing mercy or compassion towards others. It implies a sense of kindness, empathy, and understanding towards those who are in need or suffering.
