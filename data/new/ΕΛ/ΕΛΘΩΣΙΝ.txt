Etymology: The word "ελθωσιν" (elthōsin) is a form of the Greek verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to go." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ-.

Grammar: "Ελθωσιν" is the third person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the subjunctive mood is used to express possibility, potentiality, or uncertainty. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Ελθωσιν" is used to express the idea of "they might come" or "they might go" in a past context. It suggests a hypothetical or uncertain action that could have taken place in the past.
