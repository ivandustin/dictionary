Etymology: The word "ελυπηθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "λυπάομαι" (lypáomai), which means "to be grieved" or "to be saddened." The verb is derived from the noun "λύπη" (lýpi), meaning "grief" or "sorrow."

Grammar: "Ελυπηθησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "λυπάομαι" in the aorist tense. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The "-θη-" suffix indicates the passive voice, meaning that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Ελυπηθησαν" is a Greek word that translates to "they were grieved" or "they were saddened" in English. It describes a state of being in which a group of people experienced sorrow or sadness.
