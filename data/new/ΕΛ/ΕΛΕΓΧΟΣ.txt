Etymology: The word "ελεγχος" (eleghos) comes from the Greek verb "ελεγχω" (elegho), which means "to examine" or "to test". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg-, meaning "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: "Ελεγχος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "ελεγχος". In the plural, it becomes "ελεγχοι".

Description: "Ελεγχος" refers to the act of examining, testing, or verifying something. It can also mean scrutiny, investigation, or inspection. In various contexts, it can be used to describe the process of checking or confirming the accuracy, validity, or correctness of something.
