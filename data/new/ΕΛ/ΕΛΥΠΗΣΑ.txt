Etymology: The word "ελυπησα" (elypisa) comes from the Greek verb "λυπάομαι" (lypáomai), which means "to grieve" or "to be sad." The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the first person singular past tense form of the verb.

Grammar: "Ελυπησα" (elypisa) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "λυπάομαι" (lypáomai). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, person, and number. The past tense indicates an action that has already happened.

Description: "Ελυπησα" (elypisa) can be translated to English as "I grieved" or "I was sad." It describes a past action of feeling sorrow or sadness.
