Etymology: The word "ελθε" (elthe) comes from the Greek verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". It is conjugated in the aorist tense, second person singular.

Grammar: "ελθε" is a verb in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests. In this form, it is addressing a single person and instructing them to come.

Description: "ελθε" is a simple and direct command, urging someone to come to a specific location or join the speaker. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and can be seen as a polite way to invite someone or ask them to join an activity.
