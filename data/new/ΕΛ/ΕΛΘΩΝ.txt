Etymology: The word "ελθων" (elthon) comes from the Greek verb "ερχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". It is the aorist participle form of the verb.

Grammar: "Ελθων" is a masculine singular form of the aorist participle in Greek. It is used to indicate past action and is often translated as "having come" or "having arrived".

Description: "Ελθων" is used to describe someone or something that has come or arrived at a certain place. It can be used as an adjective or as a noun in a sentence. For example, "Ο ελθων" (o elthon) means "the one who has come" or "the one who arrived".
