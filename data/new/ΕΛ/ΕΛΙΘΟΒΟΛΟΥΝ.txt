Etymology: The word "ελιθοβολούν" comes from the Greek roots "ελιθος" (stone) and "βολή" (throwing). It literally means "stone-throwing".

Grammar: "Ελιθοβολούν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb "ελιθοβολώ", which means "to throw stones".

Description: "Ελιθοβολούν" refers to the action of throwing stones. It can be used in a literal sense to describe the act of physically throwing stones, or in a figurative sense to describe verbally attacking or criticizing someone.
