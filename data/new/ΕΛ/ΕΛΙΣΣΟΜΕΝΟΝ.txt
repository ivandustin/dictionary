Etymology: The word "ελισσομενον" comes from the Greek verb "ελίσσω" (elisso), which means "to roll" or "to turn around". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *wel- meaning "to turn" or "to wind".

Grammar: "Ελισσόμενον" is the present participle form of the verb "ελίσσω" (elisso) in the passive voice. It is used to describe an ongoing action of rolling or turning.

Description: "Ελισσόμενον" can be translated as "rolling" or "turning". It is often used to describe objects or movements that involve rotation or circular motion.
