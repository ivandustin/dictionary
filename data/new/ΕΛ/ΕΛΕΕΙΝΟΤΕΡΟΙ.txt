Etymology: The word "ελεεινότεροι" comes from the Greek word "ελεεινός" (eleinos), which means "pitiful" or "miserable". The suffix "-τεροι" (-teroi) is added to the adjective to form the comparative degree, meaning "more pitiful" or "more miserable".

Grammar: "Ελεεινότεροι" is the plural form of the comparative adjective "ελεεινός" (eleinos). It is used to compare two or more nouns, indicating that one is more pitiful or miserable than the other(s).

Description: "Ελεεινότεροι" is an adjective that describes something or someone as being more pitiful or miserable compared to others. It can be used to express sympathy or to emphasize the unfortunate state of someone or something.
