Etymology: The word "ελεηθεντες" comes from the Greek verb "ελεεω" (eleéo), which means "to have pity" or "to show mercy". The suffix "-θεντες" is a participle ending that indicates the past tense and passive voice.

Grammar: "Ελεηθεντες" is the plural form of the past participle of the verb "ελεεω". It is used to describe a group of people who have been shown mercy or have received pity.

Description: The word "ελεηθεντες" refers to individuals who have been the recipients of mercy or compassion. It implies that these individuals have been shown kindness or forgiveness by someone else.
