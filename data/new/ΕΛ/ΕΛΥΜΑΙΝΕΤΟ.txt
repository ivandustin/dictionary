Etymology: The word "ελυμαινετο" comes from the Greek verb "λυμαίνομαι" (lymainomai), which means "to defile" or "to pollute". It is derived from the noun "λύμη" (lyme), meaning "filth" or "pollution".

Grammar: "ελυμαινετο" is the third person singular, imperfect middle/passive indicative form of the verb "λυμαίνομαι" (lymainomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "ελυμαινετο" describes an ongoing or repeated action of defiling or polluting oneself. It implies a state of being contaminated or stained.
