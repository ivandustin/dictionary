Etymology: The word "ελληνική" (ellinikí) comes from the Greek word "Έλληνες" (Éllines), which means "Greeks." It is derived from the name of the ancient Greek tribe "Hellenes," who were the ancestors of the modern Greeks.

Grammar: "Ελληνική" is the feminine form of the adjective "ελληνικός" (ellinikós), which means "Greek." It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, "ελληνική γλώσσα" (ellinikí glóssa) means "Greek language," where "ελληνική" agrees with the feminine noun "γλώσσα" (glóssa).

Description: "Ελληνική" refers to anything related to Greece or the Greek people. It can be used to describe the Greek language, culture, traditions, or anything else associated with Greece.
