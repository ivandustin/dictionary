Etymology: The word "ελεγχω" (elecho) comes from the Greek verb "έλεγχω" (elencho), which means "to examine" or "to test". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *legh-, meaning "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: "Ελέγχω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Εγώ ελέγχω (ego elecho) - I examine
- Εσύ ελέγχεις (esi elecheis) - You examine
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό ελέγχει (aftos/afti/afto elechei) - He/She/It examines
- Εμείς ελέγχουμε (emeis elechoume) - We examine
- Εσείς ελέγχετε (eseis elechete) - You (plural) examine
- Αυτοί/Αυτές ελέγχουν (aftoi/aftes elechoun) - They examine

Description: "Ελέγχω" is a versatile verb that is used to express the action of examining, testing, or checking something. It can refer to various contexts, such as verifying information, inspecting objects, or assessing the accuracy of a statement. The verb is commonly used in everyday speech and writing in Greek.
