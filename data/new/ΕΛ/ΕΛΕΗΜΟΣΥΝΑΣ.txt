Etymology: The word "ελεημοσύνης" (eleēmosýnēs) comes from the Greek root words "ελεήμων" (eleēmōn), meaning "merciful" or "compassionate," and "σύνα" (sýna), meaning "act" or "practice." 

Grammar: "Ελεημοσύνης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Ελεημοσύνης" refers to the act or practice of showing mercy, compassion, or generosity towards those in need. It is often associated with acts of charity, giving, or helping others who are less fortunate.
