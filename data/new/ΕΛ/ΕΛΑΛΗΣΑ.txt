Etymology: The word "ελαλησα" comes from the Greek verb "λαλώ" (laló), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, so "ελαλησα" translates to "I spoke" or "I talked".

Grammar: "Ελαλησα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "λαλώ" (laló). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened in the past.

Description: "Ελαλησα" is a word in the Greek language that specifically refers to the action of speaking or talking in the past tense. It is used to indicate that someone spoke or talked at a previous point in time.
