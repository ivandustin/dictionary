Etymology: The word "ελθον" (elthon) comes from the Greek verb "ερχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". It is the aorist tense of the verb, indicating a completed action in the past.

Grammar: "Ελθον" is the aorist tense, active voice, first person singular form of the verb "ερχομαι" (erchomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Ελθον" is used to indicate that the action of coming has been completed in the past. It is often translated as "I came" or "I have come" in English.
