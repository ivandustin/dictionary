Etymology: The word "ελθοντι" (elthonti) comes from the Greek verb "ερχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". It is in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle form.

Grammar: "Ελθοντι" (elthonti) is a masculine singular dative form of the participle. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Ελθοντι" (elthonti) can be translated as "to the one who came" or "to the coming one". It is used to refer to someone who has arrived or is about to arrive.
