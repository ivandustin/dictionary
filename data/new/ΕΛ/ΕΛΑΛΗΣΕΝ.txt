Etymology: The word "ελαλησεν" comes from the Greek verb "λαλέω" (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, so "ελαλησεν" translates to "he/she/it spoke" or "he/she/it talked".

Grammar: "Ελαλησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe completed actions in the past. In this case, it indicates that someone spoke or talked at a specific point in the past.

Description: "Ελαλησεν" is a verb form that describes an action of speaking or talking that occurred in the past. It can be used to refer to someone speaking in a specific situation or context.
