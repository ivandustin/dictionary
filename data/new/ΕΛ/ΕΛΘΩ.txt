Etymology: The word "ελθω" (elthō) comes from the Greek verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to go". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ-.

Grammar: "Ελθω" is the first person singular aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The subjunctive mood is used to express possibility, potentiality, or hypothetical situations. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Ελθω" is a verb form that can be translated as "I may come" or "I might go" in English. It expresses a hypothetical or potential action in the past.
