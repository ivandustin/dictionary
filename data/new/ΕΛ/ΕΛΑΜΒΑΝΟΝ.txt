Etymology: The word "ελαμβανον" comes from the Greek verb "λαμβάνω" (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to receive". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to indicate the present tense.

Grammar: "Ελαμβανον" is the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice form of the verb "λαμβάνω" (lambanō) in Greek. It is the third person plural form, meaning "they take" or "they receive".

Description: "Ελαμβανον" is a verb form used to describe an action of taking or receiving performed by a group of people or things. It is commonly used in Greek sentences to express actions in the present tense.
