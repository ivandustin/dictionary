Etymology: The word "ελαιονος" (elaionos) comes from the Greek word "ελαιος" (elaion), which means "olive tree" or "olive oil". 

Grammar: "Ελαιονος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Ελαιονος" refers to something that is related to or belongs to an olive tree or olive oil. It can be used to describe various things such as olive groves, olive oil production, or products made from olives.
