Word: ηρνειτο

Etymology: ηρνειτο is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the imperfect middle/passive indicative tense. It comes from the root word ἀρνέομαι (arnéomai), which means "to deny" or "to refuse."

Grammar: ηρνειτο is a third-person singular verb in the imperfect middle/passive indicative tense. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past, while the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: ηρνειτο can be translated to English as "was denying" or "was refusing." It describes an ongoing or repeated action of denying or refusing in the past. The context of the sentence would determine the exact meaning and usage of the word.
