ηρωιδιαδα (heroidiada) is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: ηρωίς (herois) meaning "heroine" and εποποιία (epopoiia) meaning "epic poetry". It is a term used to describe a type of literary work that consists of a series of fictional letters exchanged between a heroine and her absent lover, usually a hero. The letters are written in verse and often express the heroine's longing, sorrow, and devotion to her lover. In terms of grammar, ηρωιδιαδα is a feminine noun in the singular form.
