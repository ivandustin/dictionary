The word "ηρημωμενην" (herēmōmenēn) is a Greek verb in the passive participle form. It comes from the root word "ἐρημόω" (erēmóō), which means "to make desolate" or "to lay waste." The passive participle form indicates that the action of making desolate has been done to the subject of the verb.

In terms of grammar, "ηρημωμενην" is in the feminine accusative singular form, indicating that the subject of the verb is a feminine noun and is the direct object of the sentence.

Overall, the word "ηρημωμενην" can be translated to mean "having been made desolate" or "having been laid waste."
