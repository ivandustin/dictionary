The word αδελφη (adelfi) is derived from the Greek word αδελφός (adelfos), which means "brother". The suffix -η (-i) is added to the end of the word to indicate that it is feminine.

αδελφη is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive, dative, and accusative.
