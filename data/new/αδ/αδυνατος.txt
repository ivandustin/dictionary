Word: αδυνατος

Etymology: 
The word αδυνατος (adynatos) comes from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the word "dynatos" meaning "able, powerful". Therefore, αδυνατος means "not able, impossible".

Grammar:
αδυνατος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can also be declined in other genders and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
αδυνατος is a Greek word that describes something that is impossible or not able to be done. It can also be used to describe someone who is weak or powerless. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe someone who is thin or skinny.
