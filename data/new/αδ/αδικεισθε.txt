The word "αδικεισθε" is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and second person plural form in Greek. It comes from the root word "αδικέω" which means "to do wrong" or "to act unjustly". The suffix "-σθε" indicates that the action is being done to the subject, making it a passive form. Therefore, "αδικεισθε" means "you are being wronged" or "you are being treated unjustly".
