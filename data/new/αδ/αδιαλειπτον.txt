Word: αδιαλειπτον

Etymology: The word αδιαλειπτον comes from the Greek roots α- (a-), meaning "not," δια- (dia-), meaning "through," and λειπτός (leiptós), meaning "left behind" or "omitted." Therefore, αδιαλειπτον means "unceasing" or "continuous."

Grammar: αδιαλειπτον is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case in Greek. It can also be declined in other cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: αδιαλειπτον is used to describe something that is uninterrupted, continuous, or incessant. It can refer to a wide range of things, such as a sound, a process, or a state of being. In Greek philosophy, αδιαλειπτον is often used to describe the eternal and unchanging nature of the universe.
