The word "αδικουσιν" (adikousin) is a verb in Greek that means "they unjustly treat" or "they act unfairly towards." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "adikia" which means "injustice" or "unfairness."

Grammar: "αδικουσιν" is the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb "αδικέω" (adikeo), which means "to act unjustly" or "to treat unfairly."

Description: The word "αδικουσιν" is often used in the context of social justice and human rights, to describe situations where people are treated unfairly or unjustly. It can also be used in legal contexts to describe actions that are illegal or against the law.
