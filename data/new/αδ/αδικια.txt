Word: αδικία (adikeea)

Etymology:
The word αδικία comes from the Greek root word "δίκη" (dikee), which means justice. The prefix "α-" (a-) in Greek is a negation, so αδικία means "injustice" or "unfairness".

Grammar:
αδικία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is αδικίες (adikees).

Description:
αδικία refers to any act or situation that is unjust or unfair. It can be used to describe a wide range of injustices, from small personal slights to large-scale societal inequalities. The concept of justice is central to Greek philosophy and culture, and the word αδικία is often used in discussions of ethics and morality.
