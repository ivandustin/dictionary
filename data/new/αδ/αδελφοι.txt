Word: αδελφοι

Etymology:
The word αδελφοι (adelphoi) is derived from the Greek word ἀδελφός (adelphos), which means "brother". It is a plural form of the word, which means "brothers" or "siblings".

Grammar:
In Greek, αδελφοι is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is a plural form, which means it refers to more than one brother or siblings. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (αδελφών), dative (αδελφοίς), accusative (αδελφούς), and vocative (αδελφοί).

Description:
The word αδελφοι is commonly used in Greek to refer to brothers or siblings. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a group of people who share a common bond or identity, such as members of a fraternity or a religious community. The word is often used in literature, poetry, and religious texts, and is an important part of Greek culture and tradition.
