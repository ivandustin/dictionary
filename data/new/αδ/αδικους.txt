Word: αδικους

Etymology:
The word αδικους (adikous) is derived from the Greek word αδικία (adikia), which means injustice or wrongdoing. The suffix -ους (-ous) is a common ending in Greek adjectives, indicating the masculine nominative singular form.

Grammar:
αδικους is an adjective in the masculine nominative singular form. It is used to describe a person or thing that is unjust or unfair. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, αδικους can be used to describe a person (ο αδικους άνθρωπος - o adikous anthropos) or a thing (η αδικους κατανομή - i adikous katanomi).

Description:
αδικους is a word that describes something that is unjust or unfair. It can be used to describe a person who treats others unfairly or a situation that is not right. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to express disapproval or criticism. In Greek literature and philosophy, the concept of justice and injustice is a recurring theme, and the word αδικους is frequently used to describe situations where justice is not served.
