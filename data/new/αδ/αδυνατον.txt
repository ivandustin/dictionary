Word: αδυνατον

Etymology: 
The word αδυνατον (adunaton) is derived from the Greek word "α-" (a-) which means "not" and "δυνατόν" (dynaton) which means "possible". Therefore, αδυνατον means "impossible" or "incapable".

Grammar:
αδυνατον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is impossible or incapable.

Description:
αδυνατον is a word that is commonly used in Greek to express the idea of something that cannot be done or achieved. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in everyday conversation, literature, and philosophy. The word is often used to describe situations that are beyond human control or ability.
