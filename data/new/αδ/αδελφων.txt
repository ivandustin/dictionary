Word: αδελφων

Etymology:
The word αδελφων (adelphon) is derived from the Greek word αδελφός (adelphos), which means "brother". It is a combination of the prefix α- (a-), which means "not" or "without", and the word δελφύς (delphys), which means "womb". Therefore, αδελφός (adelphos) literally means "one born from the same womb".

Grammar:
αδελφων (adelphon) is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of brothers".

Description:
αδελφων (adelphon) is a Greek word that refers to the relationship between brothers. It is often used in the context of family, but can also be used more broadly to refer to a group of male siblings or a fraternity. The word emphasizes the bond and connection between brothers, and is often used to express feelings of loyalty, love, and support.
