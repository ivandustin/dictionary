Word: αδηλον

Etymology:
The word αδηλον (adēlon) is derived from the Greek word α- (a-) which means "not" and the word δηλος (dēlos) which means "clear" or "evident". Therefore, αδηλον means "unclear" or "not evident".

Grammar:
αδηλον is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Description:
αδηλον is used to describe something that is not clear or evident. It can refer to a situation, a statement, or an object that is difficult to understand or explain. The word is often used in legal contexts to describe a situation where the facts are not clear or where there is a lack of evidence. It can also be used in everyday language to describe a confusing or ambiguous situation.
