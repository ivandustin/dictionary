The word "αδελφοτητι" (adelphotiti) is a Greek noun that means "brotherhood" or "fraternity". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "αδελφός" (adelphos) meaning "brother" and "-τητα" (-tita) which is a suffix used to form abstract nouns indicating a state or quality.

Grammar: "Αδελφοτητι" is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender and number, and their endings change depending on their function in a sentence.

Description: The word "αδελφοτητι" is often used to describe a group of people who share a common bond or purpose, such as a fraternity or brotherhood. It can also refer to the concept of brotherhood or solidarity in a broader sense.
