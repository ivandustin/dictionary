Word: αδελφού (adelfou)

Etymology: αδελφού comes from the Greek word αδελφός (adelfos), which means "brother". The suffix -ού (-ou) indicates the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship.

Grammar: αδελφού is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case. The genitive case is often used to indicate possession, as in "the brother's house" (το σπίτι του αδελφού).

Description: αδελφού is a Greek word that means "of the brother" or "brother's". It is often used to indicate possession or relationship, as in "the car of my brother" (το αυτοκίνητο του αδελφού μου) or "the sister of my brother" (η αδελφή του αδελφού μου). The word can also be used in a broader sense to refer to a group of brothers, as in "the brothers' business" (το επιχείρημα των αδελφών).
