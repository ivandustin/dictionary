Word: αδυνατων

Etymology: 
The word αδυνατων comes from the Greek root words α- (a-) meaning "not" and δυνατός (dynatós) meaning "able, capable". Therefore, αδυνατων means "not able, incapable".

Grammar:
αδυνατων is the genitive plural form of the adjective αδύνατος (adýnatos), which means "impossible, incapable". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, αδυνατων is used to modify plural nouns in the genitive case.

Description:
αδυνατων is a Greek adjective that describes something or someone as incapable or impossible. It is often used to express the idea of something being beyond one's ability or power. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in describing a difficult task or a challenging situation.
