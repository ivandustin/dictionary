The word αδικημα (adikima) comes from the Greek root words α- (a-) meaning "not" and δίκη (diki) meaning "justice". Therefore, αδικημα (adikima) means "injustice" or "wrongdoing".

αδικημα (adikima) is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word αδικημα (adikima) can be declined in different cases, such as the nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.

αδικημα (adikima) refers to an act of injustice or wrongdoing. It can be used to describe a variety of actions that are considered unfair or unjust, such as theft, fraud, or discrimination.
