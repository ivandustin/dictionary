The word αδημονων (ademonon) is a Greek adjective that means "distressed" or "anxious". It is derived from the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the noun δημος (demos), which means "people" or "community". The suffix -ων (-on) indicates the present participle form of the verb αδημονεω (ademonoeo), which means "to be distressed" or "to be anxious". 

In terms of grammar, αδημονων is a masculine singular nominative form of the adjective. It can also be declined in other cases and genders depending on the context of the sentence.

Overall, αδημονων describes a state of emotional distress or anxiety, often related to personal or societal issues.
