Word: αδικουμενον

Etymology: The word αδικουμενον comes from the Greek verb αδικέω (adikéo), which means "to do wrong" or "to act unjustly". The suffix -ουμενον (-oumeno) is a present passive participle ending, which means that the word is describing something that is being acted upon or affected by the action of the verb.

Grammar: αδικουμενον is a neuter singular form of the present passive participle of the verb αδικέω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The present tense describes actions that are happening now, and the passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon. The participle form is used to describe the subject of the sentence in more detail.

Description: αδικουμενον can be translated as "being wronged" or "being treated unjustly". It is often used in legal contexts to describe someone who has been wronged or treated unfairly. The word emphasizes the passive nature of the subject, highlighting that they are not the ones doing the wrong, but rather are the ones being acted upon.
