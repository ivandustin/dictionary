Word: αδικησαι

Etymology: The word αδικησαι comes from the Greek root word αδικία (adikia), which means injustice or wrongdoing. The suffix -σαι (-sai) is the second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb αδικέω (adikeo), which means to do wrong or to act unjustly.

Grammar: αδικησαι is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the second person singular aorist active imperative form, which means it is a command directed towards one person to perform an action in the past. The verb stem is αδικέω (adikeo), which means to do wrong or to act unjustly.

Description: αδικησαι is a command to someone to act unjustly or to do wrong. It is a strong word that implies a deliberate and intentional act of injustice. The word is often used in legal contexts to describe a violation of someone's rights or a breach of the law. In everyday language, it can be used to describe any action that is unfair or unjust.
