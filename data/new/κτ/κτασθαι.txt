Word: κτασθαι

Etymology:
The word κτασθαι comes from the Ancient Greek verb κτάομαι (ktáomai), which means "to acquire" or "to obtain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷeh₂- ("to get, acquire").

Grammar:
κτασθαι is a middle voice verb in Ancient Greek, which means that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and middle voice. The conjugation of κτασθαι is as follows:

Present Indicative Middle:
Singular:
1st person: κτάομαι (ktáomai)
2nd person: κτάσαι (ktásai)
3rd person: κτάται (ktátai)

Plural:
1st person: κτασόμεθα (ktasómetha)
2nd person: κτάσθε (ktásthe)
3rd person: κτάνται (ktántai)

Description:
κτασθαι means "to acquire" or "to obtain" in Ancient Greek. It is often used in the context of acquiring something through effort or struggle. The middle voice emphasizes the subject's involvement in the action of acquiring, suggesting that the acquisition is not passive but rather an active pursuit. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the acquisition of knowledge or skills.
