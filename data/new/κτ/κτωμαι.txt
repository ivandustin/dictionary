Word: κτωμαι

Etymology:
The word κτωμαι (ktomai) is a Greek verb that means "to acquire" or "to possess." It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷeh₁- ("to take, seize"), which is also the source of the English words "get" and "catch."

Grammar:
κτωμαι is a middle voice verb in Greek, which means that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

Singular:
- Κτώμαι (któmai) - I acquire/possess
- Κτᾶσαι (ktâsai) - You acquire/possess
- Κτᾶται (ktâtai) - He/she/it acquires/possesses

Plural:
- Κτώμεθα (któmetha) - We acquire/possess
- Κτᾶσθε (ktâsthe) - You all acquire/possess
- Κτᾶνται (ktântai) - They acquire/possess

Description:
κτωμαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to express possession or acquisition. It can refer to physical possessions, such as property or wealth, as well as abstract concepts like knowledge or skills. In addition, it can be used in a figurative sense to describe the acquisition of virtues or qualities, such as courage or wisdom. Overall, κτωμαι is a fundamental verb in Greek that is essential for expressing ownership and acquisition.
