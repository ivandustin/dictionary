Word: κτηματα

Etymology:
The word κτηματα (ktimata) is derived from the Greek word κτῆμα (ktēma), which means "possession" or "property". It is related to the verb κτάομαι (ktáomai), which means "to acquire" or "to possess".

Grammar:
κτηματα is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a neuter noun. In the nominative case, it is spelled as κτήματα.

Description:
κτηματα refers to possessions or properties that are owned by someone. It can refer to both tangible and intangible assets, such as land, buildings, money, stocks, and intellectual property. In modern Greek, the word is commonly used to refer to real estate or immovable property.
