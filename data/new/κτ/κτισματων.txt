Word: κτισμάτων

Etymology:
The word κτισμάτων comes from the Greek word κτίσμα (ktisma), which means "creation" or "structure". The suffix -ων (-on) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple creations or structures.

Grammar:
κτισμάτων is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "creations" or "structures".

Description:
κτισμάτων refers to multiple creations or structures, and can be used to describe a variety of things, such as buildings, monuments, or works of art. The word implies a sense of craftsmanship and creativity, as well as the idea of something being built or constructed.
