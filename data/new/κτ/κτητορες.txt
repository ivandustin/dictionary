The word "κτητορες" (ktetores) is a Greek noun in the masculine gender, which means "founders" or "builders". It is derived from the verb "κτίζω" (ktizo), which means "to found" or "to establish". In ancient Greece, ktetores were individuals or groups who were responsible for the construction of public buildings, such as temples, theaters, and stadiums. They were highly respected members of society and often received special privileges and honors for their work. Today, the word ktetores is still used in modern Greek to refer to builders or contractors. In terms of grammar, ktetores is a plural noun in the nominative case.
