The word "κτισεως" (ktiseos) is a Greek noun that means "creation" or "foundation." It is derived from the verb "κτίζω" (ktizo), which means "to create" or "to found." In terms of grammar, "κτισεως" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. 

In Christian theology, "κτισεως" is often used to refer to the act of God creating the world or the universe. It is also used in reference to the establishment or foundation of something, such as a city or a building. 

Overall, "κτισεως" is a word that conveys the idea of creation, foundation, and establishment.
