Word: φθόνος

Etymology:
The word φθόνος (phthonos) comes from the Greek root word φθείρω (phtheirō), which means "to waste away" or "to destroy." In ancient Greek, φθόνος referred to envy or jealousy, which were seen as destructive emotions that could eat away at a person's soul.

Grammar:
In Greek, φθόνος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is φθόνος (phthonos), and its genitive singular form is φθόνου (phthonou). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
φθόνος (phthonos) is a complex emotion that involves feelings of envy, jealousy, and resentment towards others. It is often associated with a desire to possess what others have, or to see them fail or suffer. In ancient Greek culture, φθόνος was seen as a negative emotion that could lead to destructive behavior and harm both the person experiencing it and those around them. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to envy or jealousy.
