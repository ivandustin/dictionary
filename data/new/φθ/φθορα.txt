Word: φθορά

Etymology:
The word φθορά comes from the Greek verb φθείρω (phtheirō), which means "to destroy" or "to corrupt". The noun form φθορά (phthorá) means "destruction" or "corruption".

Grammar:
φθορά is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as the subject or object of a sentence.

Description:
φθορά is a term that refers to the process of decay, destruction, or corruption. It can be used to describe physical decay, such as the decay of organic matter, or moral decay, such as the corruption of a society or individual. In Greek philosophy, the concept of φθορά was often used to describe the impermanence and transience of all things.
