Word: φθαρτόν

Etymology: The word φθαρτόν comes from the Greek root word φθαίρω (phthairō), which means "to destroy" or "to corrupt." The suffix -τόν (-ton) is a neuter adjective ending that means "able to be."

Grammar: φθαρτόν is a neuter adjective in the nominative singular form. It is used to describe a noun that is able to be destroyed or corrupted.

Description: φθαρτόν is a word that describes something that is perishable or subject to decay. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as food or clothing, as well as abstract concepts, such as morals or values. In philosophy, the term is often used to describe the impermanence of the material world and the transience of human existence.
