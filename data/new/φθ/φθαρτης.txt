Word: φθαρτης

Etymology:
The word φθαρτης comes from the Greek word φθαρτός (phthartós), which means "perishable" or "corruptible". It is derived from the verb φθείρω (phtheírō), which means "to destroy" or "to corrupt".

Grammar:
φθαρτης is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word φθαρτης is declined according to the second declension, which means that its stem ends in -της (-tēs).

Description:
φθαρτης is a word that describes something that is perishable or corruptible. It is often used in a philosophical or theological context to refer to the impermanence of material things and the transience of life. In Christian theology, φθαρτης is used to describe the mortal body, which is subject to decay and death. The word can also be used more generally to describe anything that is temporary or fleeting, such as beauty, wealth, or power.
