The word φθονουντες is derived from the Greek verb φθονέω (phthonéō), which means "to envy" or "to be jealous". The suffix -ουντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently experiencing envy or jealousy.

φθονουντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is the plural form of the participle, indicating that it refers to multiple people who are currently experiencing envy or jealousy. The word can function as a noun, adjective, or verb in a sentence, depending on the context.
