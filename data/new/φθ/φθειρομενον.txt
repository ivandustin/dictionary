Word: φθειρομενον

Etymology:
The word φθειρομενον is a present participle form of the Greek verb φθείρω (phtheírō), which means "to destroy" or "to corrupt". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰwer- which means "to damage" or "to injure".

Grammar:
φθειρομενον is a present participle in the passive voice, which means that the subject is being acted upon. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it is implied that something or someone is being destroyed or corrupted.

Description:
φθειρομενον can be translated as "being destroyed" or "being corrupted". It is a word that conveys a sense of ongoing action, as if something is in the process of being ruined or damaged.
