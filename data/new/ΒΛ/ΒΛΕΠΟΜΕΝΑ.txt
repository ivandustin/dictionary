Etymology: The word "βλεπομενα" comes from the Greek verb "βλέπω" (vlepó), which means "to see" or "to look". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰleh₃p-.

Grammar: "Βλεπομενα" is the plural form of the present participle of the verb "βλέπω" (vlepó). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to form the continuous aspect of verbs. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Βλεπομενα" can be translated as "the things being seen" or "the things that are seen". It refers to objects or entities that are currently being observed or perceived.
