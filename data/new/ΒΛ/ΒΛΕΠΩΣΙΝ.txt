Etymology: The word "βλεπωσιν" (blepōsin) comes from the Greek verb "βλέπω" (blepō), which means "to see" or "to look". The suffix "-σιν" (-sin) is added to the verb stem to indicate the third person plural form.

Grammar: "Βλεπωσιν" is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb "βλέπω" (blepō). In Greek grammar, this form is used to describe an action or state of being that is happening in the present and is performed by a group of people or things.

Description: "Βλεπωσιν" is used to express the idea of "they see" or "they look" in Greek. It indicates that a group of people or things is currently engaged in the act of seeing or looking at something.
