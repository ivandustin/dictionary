Etymology: The word "βλεπουσιν" (blepousin) comes from the Greek verb "βλέπω" (blepo), which means "to see" or "to look". It is conjugated in the present tense, third person plural form.

Grammar: "Βλεπουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an action happening in the present time.

Description: "Βλεπουσιν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "βλέπω" (blepo) in Greek. It means "they see" or "they look". This form is used when referring to a group of people or things who are currently seeing or looking at something.
