Etymology: The word "βλεπομεν" (blepomen) comes from the Greek verb "βλέπω" (blepo), which means "to see" or "to look". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰleh₃p-.

Grammar: "Βλεπομεν" is the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice form of the verb "βλέπω" (blepo) in Greek. It is the first person plural form, meaning "we see" or "we look".

Description: "Βλεπομεν" is a verb form used to express the action of seeing or looking in the present tense. It is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of visually perceiving something.
