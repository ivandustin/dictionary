Etymology: The word "βλέπω" (pronounced "vlepo") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰleh₃p- which means "to see" or "to look".

Grammar: "Βλέπω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Βλέπω (vlepo) - I see
- Βλέπεις (vlepeis) - You see
- Βλέπει (vlepei) - He/She/It sees
- Βλέπουμε (vlepoume) - We see
- Βλέπετε (vlepete) - You see (plural)
- Βλέπουν (vlepoun) - They see

Description: "Βλέπω" means "to see" or "to look" in Greek. It is a common verb used to describe the action of perceiving something visually with the eyes. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "to understand" or "to comprehend".
