Etymology: The word "βληθείς" (pronounced "vleetheis") comes from the Greek verb "βάλλω" (pronounced "vallo"), which means "to throw" or "to cast". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷʰel-, meaning "to throw" or "to hit".

Grammar: "Βληθείς" is the aorist passive participle of the verb "βάλλω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Βληθείς" is used to describe something or someone that has been thrown or cast. It can be translated as "having been thrown" or "having been cast". It is often used in contexts where an object or a person has been forcefully propelled or launched.
