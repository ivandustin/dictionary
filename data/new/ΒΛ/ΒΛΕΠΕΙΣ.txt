Etymology: The word "βλέπεις" (pronounced "vlepeis") comes from the Greek verb "βλέπω" (vlepo), which means "to see" or "to look". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰleh₁p-.

Grammar: "Βλέπεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense in Greek. It is used when addressing one person and means "you see" or "you are seeing".

Description: "Βλέπεις" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of seeing or looking. It is commonly used in everyday conversations to refer to the act of visually perceiving something.
