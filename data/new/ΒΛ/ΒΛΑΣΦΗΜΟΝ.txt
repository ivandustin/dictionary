Etymology: The word "βλασφημον" (blasphēmon) comes from the Greek roots "βλάπτω" (blaptō), meaning "to harm" or "to injure," and "φήμη" (phēmē), meaning "reputation" or "speech." It originally referred to someone who spoke ill or slandered others.

Grammar: In Greek, "βλασφημον" is a noun in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: "βλασφημον" is an ancient Greek word that is commonly translated as "blasphemer" or "slanderer." It refers to someone who speaks disrespectfully or irreverently about sacred or holy things, often with the intention of insulting or offending others. In religious contexts, it is used to describe someone who speaks against God or religious beliefs.
