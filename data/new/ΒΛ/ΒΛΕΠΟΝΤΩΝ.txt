Etymology: The word "βλεποντων" (blepontōn) is the present active participle of the Greek verb "βλέπω" (blepō), which means "to see" or "to look". 

Grammar: "Βλεποντων" is the genitive plural form of the participle, indicating possession or association. It is used to describe a group of people or things who are currently seeing or looking.

Description: "Βλεποντων" can be translated as "of those who see" or "of those who are looking". It refers to a collective group of individuals who are currently engaged in the act of seeing or looking at something.
