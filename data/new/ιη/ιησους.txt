Word: Ιησούς

Etymology: Ιησούς (Iesous) is the Greek form of the Hebrew name Yeshua (ישוע), which means "salvation" or "deliverance". It is also the name of Jesus Christ in the New Testament.

Grammar: Ιησούς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the subject of a sentence and can also be used as a predicate nominative.

Description: Ιησούς is a name that is highly revered in Christianity. It refers to Jesus Christ, the central figure of the Christian religion. Jesus is believed to be the son of God and the savior of humanity. He is known for his teachings, miracles, and his sacrificial death on the cross for the forgiveness of sins. The name Ιησούς is used in the New Testament to refer to Jesus and is also commonly used in Christian prayers and hymns.
