Etymology: The word "υετός" (pronounced "yetos") comes from the Ancient Greek word "ὕετος" (huetos), which means "rain." It is derived from the verb "ὑείν" (huein), meaning "to rain."

Grammar: "Υετός" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "υετού" (yetou). In the plural form, it becomes "υετοί" (yetoi).

Description: "Υετός" refers to rain in Greek. It is used to describe the precipitation of water droplets from the atmosphere, usually in the form of raindrops. Rain is an essential part of the water cycle and plays a crucial role in the Earth's climate and ecosystems.
