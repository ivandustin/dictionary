Etymology: The word "υετόν" (pronounced "yeton") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ὕετος" (huetos), meaning "rain."

Grammar: "Υετόν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number.

Description: "Υετόν" refers to rain in Greek. It is used to describe the water that falls from the atmosphere in the form of droplets. Rain is an essential part of the Earth's water cycle and plays a crucial role in the distribution of water resources.
