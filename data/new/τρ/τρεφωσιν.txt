Word: τρεφωσιν

Etymology: τρεφωσιν is the third person plural present active indicative form of the Greek verb τρέφω (trepho), which means "to nourish" or "to feed".

Grammar: τρεφωσιν is a verb in the present tense, indicating that the action of nourishing or feeding is currently happening. It is in the third person plural, meaning that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: τρεφωσιν can be translated as "they nourish" or "they feed". It is a common verb in Greek, used to describe the act of providing sustenance to someone or something. In a broader sense, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of supporting or nurturing someone or something.
