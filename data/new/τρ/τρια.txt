Word: τρια

Etymology:
The word τρια (tria) is a Greek word that means "three". It is derived from the Greek word τρίας (trias), which also means "three".

Grammar:
τρια is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. It is also in the singular form, which means it refers to one thing.

Description:
τρια is a simple and straightforward word that means "three" in Greek. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and is an essential part of the Greek language. It can be used to describe the number of objects, people, or things. For example, if someone wants to say "I have three apples", they would say "έχω τρία μήλα" (echo tria mila) in Greek.
