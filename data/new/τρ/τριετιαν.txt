The word "τριετιαν" (tri-et-ian) is a Greek noun that refers to a period of three years. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "τρία" (tria) meaning "three" and "έτος" (etos) meaning "year".

Grammar: In Greek, "τριετιαν" is a feminine noun in the accusative case. Its singular form is "τριετία" (tri-et-ia) and its plural form is "τριετίες" (tri-et-ies).

Description: The word "τριετιαν" is often used in historical and academic contexts to refer to a three-year period, such as the term of office for certain political positions or the duration of a specific event or project.
