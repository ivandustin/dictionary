The word "τρισιν" is a Greek adverb that means "thrice" or "three times." It is derived from the Greek word "τρίς" (tris), which means "three." In terms of grammar, "τρισιν" is an adverb of frequency, indicating how often something occurs. It can be used to describe actions that are repeated three times, or to indicate that something happens three times in a certain period of time.
