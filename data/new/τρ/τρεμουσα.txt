τρεμουσα (tremousa) is a Greek word that is the feminine present participle of the verb τρέμω (tremo), which means "to tremble" or "to shake". The word can be translated as "trembling" or "shaking" and is often used to describe a person or object that is physically shaking due to fear, anxiety, or excitement. In grammar, τρεμουσα is a participle, which is a type of verb that functions as an adjective or adverb in a sentence.
