Word: τριμηνον

Etymology:
The word τριμηνον comes from the Greek words τρία (tria) meaning "three" and μήνας (mēnas) meaning "months". Therefore, τριμηνον literally means "three months".

Grammar:
τριμηνον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The genitive form of τριμηνον is τριμήνου (trimēnou) and the dative form is τριμήνῳ (trimēnōi).

Description:
τριμηνον is a term used to refer to a period of three months. It can be used in various contexts, such as in finance, medicine, and education. For example, in finance, a company may report its earnings on a quarterly basis, which means every three months. In medicine, a pregnancy is divided into three trimesters, each lasting approximately three months. In education, a school year may be divided into three terms, each lasting three months.
