Word: τρις

Etymology:
The word τρις (tris) is a Greek word that means "three times" or "thrice". It is derived from the Greek root word τρι- (tri-), which means "three".

Grammar:
In Greek, τρις is an adverb that is used to indicate repetition or frequency. It is often used in combination with other words to express the idea of doing something three times.

Description:
The word τρις is commonly used in Greek literature and mythology to describe actions that are repeated three times. For example, in the story of the Labors of Hercules, Hercules had to complete twelve tasks, one of which was to capture the three-headed dog Cerberus. He had to do this by overpowering the dog and bringing it back to the surface of the earth, and he had to do it three times.
