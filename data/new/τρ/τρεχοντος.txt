Word: τρεχοντος

Etymology:
The word τρεχοντος is derived from the Greek verb τρέχω (trecho), which means "to run". It is in the present participle form, which indicates an ongoing action of running.

Grammar:
τρεχοντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb τρέχω. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
τρεχοντος can be translated to "of the one who is running" or "of the running one". It is often used in Greek literature to describe a person or animal that is in the act of running. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or event that is fast-paced or rapidly changing.
