Word: τρυφωσαν

Etymology: The word τρυφωσαν is derived from the Greek verb τρυφάω (tryphao), which means to live luxuriously or indulge in pleasure.

Grammar: τρυφωσαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. It is a passive voice verb, indicating that the subject of the sentence is receiving the action.

Description: τρυφωσαν is used to describe a state of indulgence or luxury. It can be translated as "they were living in luxury" or "they were indulging in pleasure." The word carries a connotation of excess and extravagance. It is often used in a negative context to criticize those who are living in luxury while others are suffering.
