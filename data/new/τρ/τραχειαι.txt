τραχειαι (tracheiai) is a Greek word that can be translated to "rough" or "harsh". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "trachys" which means rough or harsh.

Grammar: τραχειαι is a feminine plural adjective in the nominative case.

Description: The word is often used to describe rough or harsh surfaces, textures, or sounds. It can also be used to describe a person's voice or demeanor as rough or harsh. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the sea or the wind as rough or stormy.
