τριβολους (trivoulos) is a Greek word that means "three-pronged" or "trident". It is derived from the combination of the words "tri" meaning "three" and "bolos" meaning "prong". In Greek mythology, the god Poseidon is often depicted holding a trident, which is a symbol of his power over the sea. In grammar, τριβολους is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form.
