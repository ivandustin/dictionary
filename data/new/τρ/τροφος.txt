Word: τροφός

Etymology:
The word τροφός (trophos) comes from the Greek verb τρέφω (trepho), which means "to nourish" or "to feed". The suffix -ός (-os) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
τροφός is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. In the genitive singular form, it is τροφοῦ (trophou). In the accusative singular form, it is τροφόν (trophon). In the plural form, it is τροφοί (trofoi) in the nominative and accusative cases, and τροφῶν (trofon) in the genitive case.

Description:
τροφός is a Greek word that means "nourisher" or "feeder". It is often used to refer to someone who provides food or sustenance, such as a parent, caregiver, or provider. In Greek mythology, the goddess Demeter was known as the τροφός of the earth, as she was responsible for the growth and nourishment of crops. The word can also be used in a metaphorical sense, to refer to something that provides nourishment or sustenance, such as knowledge or spiritual guidance.
