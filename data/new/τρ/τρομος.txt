τρομος (tromos) is a Greek noun that means "trembling, shaking, or fear." It is derived from the verb τρέμω (tremo), which means "to tremble or shake." In Greek mythology, Tromos was also the name of a god who personified trembling and fear. 

In terms of grammar, τρομος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases, such as τρόμον (tromon) in the accusative singular or τρόμοι (tromoi) in the nominative plural. 

Overall, τρομος is a word that conveys a sense of unease or anxiety, often in response to a perceived threat or danger.
