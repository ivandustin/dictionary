τροφην (trophen) is a Greek noun that means "nourishment" or "food". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb τρέφω (trefo), which means "to nourish" or "to feed". 

Grammar: τροφην is a singular feminine noun in the accusative case. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of providing sustenance or nourishment to living beings, such as infants, animals, or plants. It can also refer to the food or nourishment that is consumed by humans. In Greek mythology, the goddess Demeter was associated with τροφην as she was the goddess of agriculture and fertility.
