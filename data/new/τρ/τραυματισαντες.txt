τραυματισαντες (traumatizantes) is a Greek verb in the aorist participle form, which means "having wounded" or "having injured". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root word τραύμα (trauma), which means "wound" or "injury". The suffix -τισαντες (-tisantes) is added to the root word to form the aorist participle form of the verb.

Grammar: τραυματισαντες is a third-person plural aorist participle form of the verb τραυματίζω (traumatizo), which means "to wound" or "to injure". The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, and the participle form indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Description: τραυματισαντες is used to describe a group of people who have wounded or injured someone in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as in medical reports, legal documents, or news articles.
