τροχιας (trochias) is a Greek word that comes from the root word τροχός (trochos), meaning "wheel" or "circle". In ancient Greek poetry, trochias refers to a metrical foot consisting of a stressed syllable followed by an unstressed syllable. In modern Greek, the word is used to describe the orbit or trajectory of a celestial body, such as a planet or satellite. In terms of grammar, τροχιας is a feminine noun in the genitive case.
