τραπεζας (trapezas) is a Greek noun that means "table" or "bank". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "τράπεζα" (trapeza), which meant "table" or "counter". Over time, the meaning of the word evolved to include the table or counter used for money transactions, which eventually led to the modern meaning of "bank".

Grammar: τραπεζας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: τραπεζας can refer to a physical table or counter, or to a financial institution such as a bank. In modern Greek, the word is primarily used to refer to banks and banking services.
