τρυμαλιας (trumalias) is a Greek word that means "a person who causes trouble or turmoil". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "τρυμαλίζω" (trumalizo), which means "to cause trouble or turmoil". 

Grammar: τρυμαλιας is a noun in the masculine gender. 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is causing chaos or disruption in a situation. It can also be used to describe someone who is a troublemaker or instigator. The word has a negative connotation and is not typically used in a positive context.
