The word "τρεμουσιν" (tremousin) is a present active participle in Greek, derived from the verb "τρέμω" (tremo), which means "to tremble" or "to shake". The participle form indicates that the action of trembling is ongoing or continuous. 

In terms of etymology, "τρέμω" is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *trem-, which means "to shake" or "to tremble". This root is also the origin of the English word "tremble".

In terms of grammar, "τρεμουσιν" is in the present tense, indicating that the action of trembling is happening now. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of trembling. 

Overall, "τρεμουσιν" can be translated as "they are trembling" or "they tremble".
