Word: τρωγόντες

Etymology: τρωγόντες is a present participle of the Greek verb τρώγω (trógo), which means "to eat". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *terə-, which means "to rub, grind, crush".

Grammar: τρωγόντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is used to describe people who are currently eating or in the act of eating.

Description: τρωγόντες is a word that describes people who are eating. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people having a meal together or individuals snacking on something. The word emphasizes the action of eating and can be used to convey a sense of immediacy or activity.
