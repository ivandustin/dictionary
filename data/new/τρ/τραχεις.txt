τραχείς (tracheis) is a Greek adjective that means rough, harsh, or rugged. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "trachys" which means rough or rugged.

Grammar: τραχείς is the masculine plural form of the adjective. 

Description: The word can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a rough terrain, a harsh voice, or a rugged appearance. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a difficult or challenging situation.
