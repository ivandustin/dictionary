τρωιαδι (troiadi) is a Greek word that refers to a woman from Troy, the ancient city in modern-day Turkey that was famously besieged by the Greeks in the Trojan War. The word is derived from the name of the city, Troy (Τροία in Greek), and the suffix -άδι, which is used to indicate a female inhabitant of a place. 

In terms of grammar, τρωιαδι is a feminine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek, which means that its stem ends in -α. 

Overall, τρωιαδι is a descriptive term that is used to identify a woman from Troy. It has historical and literary significance due to its association with the Trojan War and the epic poems of Homer.
