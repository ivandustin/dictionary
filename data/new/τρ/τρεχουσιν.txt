Word: τρεχουσιν

Etymology:
The word τρεχουσιν is derived from the Greek verb τρέχω (trecho), which means "to run". It is a present active participle, indicating that the action of running is currently happening.

Grammar:
τρεχουσιν is a third-person plural present active participle in the Greek language. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently running. In English, it can be translated as "they are running".

Description:
τρεχουσιν is a word that describes an action of running that is currently happening. It can be used to describe a group of people who are running, such as in the sentence "τρεχουσιν προς τον αγώνα" (they are running towards the race). The word can also be used in other contexts, such as in poetry or literature, to describe the act of running in a more metaphorical sense.
