τραπεζείτης (trapezeítis) is a Greek noun that refers to a banker or a money-changer. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek word τράπεζα (trápeza), which means "table" or "bank", and the suffix -είτης (-eítis), which denotes a profession or occupation.

Grammar: τραπεζείτης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is τραπεζείτες (trapezeítes).

Description: In ancient Greece, τραπεζείταις were individuals who exchanged coins and provided loans. They were often found in the agora, the marketplace, and were an important part of the economy. The profession of τραπεζείτης still exists in modern Greece, although their role has evolved to include banking and financial services.
