The word "τρυφην" (tryphēn) is a Greek noun that means "luxury" or "delicacy". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "τρυφάω" (trypháō), which means "to live luxuriously" or "to indulge in pleasure".

Grammar: "Τρυφην" is a feminine noun in the singular form.

Description: The word is often used to describe luxurious or delicate foods, such as fine wines or rich desserts. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of indulgence or extravagance. In ancient Greek literature, the concept of "tryphē" (luxury) was often associated with excess and moral decay, and was seen as a threat to the stability of society.
