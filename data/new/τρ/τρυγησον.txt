τρυγησον (trygeson) is a Greek verb in the aorist imperative mood, second person singular. It comes from the root word τρυγάω (trygao), which means "to harvest" or "to gather grapes." The imperative form τρυγησον is a command to someone to "harvest" or "pick" grapes. It is often used metaphorically in the New Testament to mean "to gather souls" or "to evangelize."
