Etymology: The word "βηθανιαν" comes from the Greek word "Βηθανίαν" (Bethania), which is derived from the Hebrew word "בֵּית־עָנִיָּה" (Beit-Anyah), meaning "house of affliction" or "house of poverty."

Grammar: "Βηθανιαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender in Greek.

Description: "Βηθανιαν" refers to the biblical town of Bethany, which is mentioned in the New Testament as the place where Jesus raised Lazarus from the dead. It is located on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives, about two miles east of Jerusalem.
