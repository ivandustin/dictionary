Etymology: The word "οικετων" (oiketon) comes from the Greek word "οικετης" (oiketes), which means "servant" or "household member". It is derived from the word "οικος" (oikos), meaning "house" or "home".

Grammar: "Οικετων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "οικετης" (oiketes). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. The plural form indicates that there are multiple servants or household members.

Description: "Οικετων" refers to servants or household members in Greek. It is used to describe individuals who work in someone's house, assisting with various tasks and chores. In ancient Greece, oiketons played an important role in maintaining the household and supporting the family.
