Etymology: The word "οικούσα" (oikousa) comes from the Greek verb "οικέω" (oikeo), which means "to dwell" or "to live in." It is derived from the noun "οίκος" (oikos), meaning "house" or "dwelling."

Grammar: "Οικούσα" is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb "οικέω" (oikeo). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The feminine form indicates that the subject of the action is female.

Description: "Οικούσα" is a Greek word that can be translated as "dwelling" or "living in." It describes the action of someone, specifically a female, residing or inhabiting a place.
