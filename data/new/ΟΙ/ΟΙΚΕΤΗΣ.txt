Etymology: The word "οικετης" (oiketēs) comes from the Greek root "οικος" (oikos), meaning "house" or "home." It refers to someone who serves or works in a household.

Grammar: In Greek, "οικετης" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Οικετης" is a term used in ancient Greek to describe a servant or a household worker. It refers to someone who performs various tasks and duties within a household, such as cleaning, cooking, and serving the family members. The role of an "οικετης" was important in ancient Greek society, as they played a crucial role in maintaining the functioning of a household.
