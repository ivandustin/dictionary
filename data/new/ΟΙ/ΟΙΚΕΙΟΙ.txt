Grammar: "Οἱκεῖοι" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is used to refer to a group of people who are related or connected in some way, such as family members, household members, or people who share a common origin or purpose.

Description: "Οἱκεῖοι" is often translated as "relatives," "kinsmen," or "kinsfolk" in English. It can also be used more broadly to refer to a group of people who are familiar or closely associated with each other. In ancient Greek society, the concept of "οἱκεῖοι" was important for defining social relationships and obligations within a community.
