Etymology: The word "οικτιρμοί" (oiktirmoi) comes from the Greek verb "οικτίρω" (oiktiro), which means "to have pity" or "to feel compassion". 

Grammar: "Οικτιρμοί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Οικτιρμοί" refers to feelings of pity, compassion, or sympathy. It is used to describe the emotions one feels when witnessing someone else's suffering or misfortune.
