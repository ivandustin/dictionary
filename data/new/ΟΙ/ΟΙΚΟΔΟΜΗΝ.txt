Etymology: The word "οικοδομην" (oikodomēn) comes from the Greek roots "οἶκος" (oikos) meaning "house" and "δέμω" (dēmō) meaning "to build." 

Grammar: "Οικοδομην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. It is the accusative form of the noun "οικοδομή" (oikodomē), which means "building" or "construction" in Greek.

Description: "Οικοδομην" refers to the act of building or constructing something, typically a physical structure such as a house, building, or monument. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of developing or improving something, such as relationships, skills, or knowledge.
