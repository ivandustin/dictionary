Etymology: The word "οικετην" (oiketēn) comes from the Greek word "οἰκέτης" (oiketēs), which means "servant" or "household member." It is derived from the word "οἶκος" (oikos), meaning "house" or "home."

Grammar: "Οικετην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Οικετην" refers to a servant or a household member. It can be used to describe someone who works in a household, assisting with various tasks and chores. In ancient Greece, oiketēs played an important role in maintaining the household and supporting the family.
