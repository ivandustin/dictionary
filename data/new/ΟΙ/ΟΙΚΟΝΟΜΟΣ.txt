Etymology: The word "οικονομος" comes from the Greek roots "οικος" meaning "house" and "νομος" meaning "law." It originally referred to a person who managed the affairs of a household.

Grammar: In Greek, "οικονομος" is a noun of masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular form.

Description: "Οικονομος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "economist" in English. It refers to a person who studies and manages the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. Economists analyze economic systems, trends, and policies to provide insights and make recommendations for improving economic efficiency and welfare.
