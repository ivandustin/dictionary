Etymology: The word "οινοφλυγίαις" comes from the Greek words "οἶνος" (oinos) meaning "wine" and "φλυγία" (phlygia) meaning "fleeing" or "running away". 

Grammar: "οινοφλυγίαις" is a noun in the dative plural form. It is used to indicate the manner or means by which something is done. 

Description: "οινοφλυγίαις" refers to the act of fleeing or running away from wine. It can be used metaphorically to describe someone who avoids or abstains from drinking alcohol.
