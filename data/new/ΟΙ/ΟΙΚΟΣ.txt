Etymology: The word "οἶκος" (oikos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *weik-, meaning "house" or "dwelling."

Grammar: In Greek, "οἶκος" (oikos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has various forms depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Οἶκος" (oikos) primarily means "house" or "dwelling" in Greek. It can refer to a physical structure where people live or to a household in a broader sense, including the family, property, and possessions associated with it. In a figurative sense, it can also represent a family lineage or a social unit.
