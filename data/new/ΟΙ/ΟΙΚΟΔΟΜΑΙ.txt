Etymology: The word "οικοδομαι" comes from the Greek roots "οικος" meaning "house" and "δομη" meaning "building". It literally means "to build a house".

Grammar: "Οικοδομώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Εγώ οικοδομώ (I build)
- Εσύ οικοδομείς (You build)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό οικοδομεί (He/She/It builds)
- Εμείς οικοδομούμε (We build)
- Εσείς οικοδομείτε (You build)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές οικοδομούν (They build)

Description: "Οικοδομώ" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It primarily refers to the physical act of constructing or building a house or any other structure. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of building or developing something abstract, such as relationships, knowledge, or skills.
