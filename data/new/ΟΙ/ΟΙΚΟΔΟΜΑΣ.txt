Etymology: The word "οικοδομας" (oikodomas) comes from the Greek roots "οικος" (oikos) meaning "house" and "δομη" (dome) meaning "building". It literally translates to "house builder" or "builder".

Grammar: In Greek, "οικοδομας" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Οικοδομας" refers to a person who constructs or builds houses or buildings. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who contributes to the development or improvement of something, such as a project or an idea.
