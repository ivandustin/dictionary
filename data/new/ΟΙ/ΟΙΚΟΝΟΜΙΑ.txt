Etymology: The word "οικονομία" comes from the Greek words "οίκος" (oikos) meaning "house" and "νόμος" (nomos) meaning "law". It originally referred to the management and administration of a household.

Grammar: In Greek, "οικονομία" is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows:
- Singular: η οικονομία (i oikonomía)
- Plural: οι οικονομίες (i oikonomíes)

Description: "Οικονομία" is a term used to describe the management, organization, and allocation of resources within a society or a system. It encompasses various aspects such as production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. It is also used to refer to the overall state of the economy of a country or region.
