Etymology: The word "οικονομείν" comes from the Greek word "οίκος" (oikos), meaning "house" or "home," and "νόμος" (nomos), meaning "law" or "rule." Together, "οικονομείν" means "to manage a household" or "to administer."

Grammar: "οικονομείν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs.

Description: "οικονομείν" refers to the act of managing or administering a household or resources. It can also be used more broadly to describe the management of any kind of organization or system. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the study or practice of economics.
