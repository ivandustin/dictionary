Grammar: In Greek, "οικος" (oikos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, numbers, and genders to indicate different grammatical functions and meanings.

Description: "Οικος" (oikos) refers to a house or dwelling place. It can also be used metaphorically to represent a household, family, or the entire domestic sphere. In ancient Greece, the concept of "οικος" (oikos) was central to the social structure and economy, as it encompassed not only the physical house but also the family, slaves, and property associated with it.
