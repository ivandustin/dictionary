Etymology: The word "οικονομον" comes from the Greek word "οικονομία" (oikonomía), which means "management of a household" or "economy". It is derived from the words "οίκος" (oíkos) meaning "house" and "νόμος" (nómos) meaning "law".

Grammar: "Οικονομον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Οικονομον" refers to a person who manages or oversees the financial and administrative aspects of a household, business, or organization. It can also refer to an economist or someone who studies and analyzes economic systems and theories.
