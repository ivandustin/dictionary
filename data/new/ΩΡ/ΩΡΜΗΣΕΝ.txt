Etymology: The word "ωρμησεν" (ormēsen) comes from the Greek verb "ὁρμάω" (hormaō), which means "to rush" or "to urge forward."

Grammar: "ωρμησεν" is the aorist tense, third person singular form of the verb "ὁρμάω" (hormaō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "ωρμησεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it rushed" or "he/she/it urged forward." It is used to describe a sudden or forceful movement or action.
