Etymology: The word "κηπουρος" (kēpouros) comes from the Greek roots "κῆπος" (kēpos) meaning "garden" and "οὐρά" (oura) meaning "tail". It literally translates to "garden tail" or "garden guardian".

Grammar: In Greek, "κηπουρος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. The plural form is "κηπουροι" (kēpouroi).

Description: "Κηπουρος" refers to a gardener or someone who takes care of a garden. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who looks after or protects something.
