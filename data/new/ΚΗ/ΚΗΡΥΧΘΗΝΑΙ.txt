Grammar: "κηρυχθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "κηρύσσω" (kerusso). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "κηρυχθηναι" is used to express the idea of being proclaimed or announced in the past passive voice. It is often used in religious or formal contexts to describe the act of making a public declaration or proclamation.
