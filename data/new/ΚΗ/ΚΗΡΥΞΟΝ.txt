Etymology: The word "κηρυξον" (kēryxon) comes from the Greek verb "κηρύσσω" (kēryssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce." 

Grammar: "Κηρυξον" is the imperative form of the verb "κηρύσσω" in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to proclaim or announce something.

Description: "Κηρυξον" is a Greek word that means "proclaim" or "announce." It is often used in a religious or ceremonial context, where someone is called upon to make a public announcement or declaration.
