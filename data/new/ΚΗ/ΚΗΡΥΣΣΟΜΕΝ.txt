Etymology: The word "κηρυσσομεν" comes from the Greek verb "κηρυσσω" (kerusso), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce." It is derived from the noun "κηρυξ" (kerux), meaning "herald" or "messenger."

Grammar: "κηρυσσομεν" is the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice form of the verb "κηρυσσω" in Greek. It is the first-person plural form, meaning "we proclaim" or "we announce."

Description: "κηρυσσομεν" is a Greek verb that signifies the act of proclaiming or announcing something. It implies a public declaration or announcement of a message or news.
