Etymology: The word "κηρυχθηι" (kēruchthēi) comes from the Greek verb "κηρύσσω" (kērýssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce." 

Grammar: "κηρυχθηι" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and subjunctive mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb.

Description: "κηρυχθηι" is used to express an action of proclaiming or announcing something in the past. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action is uncertain or hypothetical.
