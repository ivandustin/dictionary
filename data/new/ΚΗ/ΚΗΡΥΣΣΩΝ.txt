Etymology: The word "κηρυσσών" comes from the Greek verb "κηρύσσω" (kerýssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce."

Grammar: "κηρυσσών" is the present participle form of the verb "κηρύσσω" (kerýssō). It is used to describe someone who is currently proclaiming or announcing something.

Description: "κηρυσσών" refers to a person who is actively proclaiming or announcing something. It can be used to describe someone who is delivering a public speech, making an announcement, or spreading a message.
