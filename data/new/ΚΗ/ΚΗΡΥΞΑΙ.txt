Etymology: The word "κηρυξαι" (kēryxai) comes from the Greek verb "κηρύσσω" (kēryssō), which means "to proclaim" or "to announce." 

Grammar: "κηρυξαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "κηρύσσω" (kēryssō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion.

Description: "κηρυξαι" is used to refer to the act of proclaiming or announcing something. It can be used in various contexts, such as proclaiming a message, announcing news, or making a public declaration.
