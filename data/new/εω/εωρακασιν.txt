In terms of grammar, "εωρακασιν" is a regular conjugation of the verb "ὁράω" in the perfect tense, with the addition of the augment "ε" and the suffix "-κα-". The suffix "-σιν" indicates that the subject is third person plural. 

The etymology of "ὁράω" is uncertain, but it is believed to be related to the Sanskrit word "darśati", meaning "to see".
