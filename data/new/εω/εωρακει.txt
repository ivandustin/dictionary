The word "εωρακει" (eōrakei) is a verb in the Greek language, which is in the present perfect tense and active voice. It is derived from the root word "ὁράω" (horáō), which means "to see" or "to perceive". 

In terms of grammar, "εωρακει" is conjugated in the third person singular, and it indicates that the action of seeing or perceiving has been completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present. 

In English, "εωρακει" can be translated as "he/she/it has seen" or "he/she/it has perceived". 

Overall, "εωρακει" is a useful verb in Greek for expressing completed actions with ongoing relevance in the present.
