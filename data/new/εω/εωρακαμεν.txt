Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "ὁράω" (horaō), which means "to see", and the perfect tense suffix "-κα-" (-ka-), which indicates a completed action in the past. 

Grammar: "εωρακαμεν" is the first person plural form of the perfect tense, which means it refers to an action that was completed in the past and has continuing relevance in the present. 

Description: The word "εωρακαμεν" is commonly used in everyday Greek language to express that the speaker and others have seen something in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as to describe a past event, to report a sighting, or to express surprise or disbelief.
