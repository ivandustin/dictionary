Etymology: The word "πωλούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "πωλέω" (poleo), which means "to sell". It is conjugated in the present tense, third person plural form.

Grammar: "πωλούσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate the person, number, tense, and mood. The "-ούσιν" ending indicates the third person plural form in the present tense.

Description: "πωλούσιν" is used to describe an action of selling that is being performed by a third person plural subject. It can be translated as "they sell" or "they are selling" in English.
