Etymology: The word "πωρωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "πωρόω" (pōróō), which means "to harden" or "to petrify". 

Grammar: "Πωρωσιν" is the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb "πωρόω" (pōróō). It is used to describe an ongoing action of hardening or petrifying that is being performed by a group of people or things.

Description: "Πωρωσιν" refers to the act of hardening or petrifying. It can be used in a literal sense to describe the process of turning something into stone or making it hard and rigid. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of becoming unfeeling, unresponsive, or unyielding.
