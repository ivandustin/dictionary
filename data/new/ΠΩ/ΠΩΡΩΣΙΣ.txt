Etymology: The word "πωρωσις" comes from the Greek verb "πωρόω" (pōróō), which means "to harden" or "to petrify". It is derived from the noun "πέτρα" (pétra), meaning "stone" or "rock".

Grammar: "Πωρωσις" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and has the genitive form "πωρώσεως" (pōrṓseōs).

Description: In Greek, "πωρωσις" refers to the process of hardening or petrification. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the state of being unfeeling, unresponsive, or emotionally hardened.
