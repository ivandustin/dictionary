Etymology: The word "θελησιν" (thelēsin) comes from the Greek verb "θέλω" (thelō), which means "to will" or "to desire". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *télh₂eti, meaning "to wish" or "to aim".

Grammar: "Θελησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case, number, and gender.

Description: "Θελησιν" refers to the act of willing or desiring. It can also be translated as "will" or "volition". In Greek philosophy, "θέλησις" (thelēsis) is often associated with the faculty of the soul that enables a person to make choices and decisions.
