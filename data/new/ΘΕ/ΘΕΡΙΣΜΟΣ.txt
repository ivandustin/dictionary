Etymology: The word "θερισμός" (therismos) comes from the Greek verb "θερίζω" (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". It is derived from the noun "θέρος" (theros), meaning "summer" or "harvest season".

Grammar: "Θερισμός" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "θερισμού" (therismou).

Description: "Θερισμός" refers to the act of harvesting or reaping crops, particularly during the summer season. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of gathering or collecting something, such as information or rewards.
