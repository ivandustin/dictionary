Etymology: The word "θεραπευσον" (therapeuson) comes from the Greek verb "θεραπεύω" (therapeuo), which means "to heal" or "to cure". 

Grammar: "Θεραπευσον" is the imperative form of the verb "θεραπεύω" in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to heal or cure.

Description: "Θεραπευσον" is a Greek word that means "heal" or "cure". It is often used in medical or therapeutic contexts, where it is used to express the idea of treating or curing a disease or ailment.
