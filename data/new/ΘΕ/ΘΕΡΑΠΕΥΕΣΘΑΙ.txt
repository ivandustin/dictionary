Etymology: The word "θεραπεύεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "θεραπεύω" (therapeúo), which means "to heal" or "to cure". 

Grammar: "θεραπεύεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "θεραπεύω". In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive form is used to express the idea of the verb without specifying any tense, mood, or person.

Description: "θεραπεύεσθαι" is a Greek verb that means "to be healed" or "to be cured". It implies the process of receiving or undergoing treatment or therapy in order to recover from an illness or injury.
