Grammar: "Θεωρησαντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "θεωρέω" (theoreo) in Greek. It is in the masculine plural nominative case. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the participle form functions as an adjective.

Description: "Θεωρησαντες" is used to describe a group of people who have observed or contemplated something. It implies that they have carefully examined or considered a particular subject or situation.
