Etymology: The word "θεωδείς" (theoides) comes from the Greek words "θεός" (theos) meaning "god" and "εἶδος" (eidos) meaning "form" or "shape". It is derived from the concept of having a god-like appearance or resemblance.

Grammar: "Θεωδείς" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, plural number, and nominative case. It is used to describe something or someone that has the appearance or characteristics of a god.

Description: "Θεωδείς" is used to describe something or someone that is god-like or divine in appearance, form, or nature. It implies a sense of grandeur, majesty, or awe-inspiring qualities. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person's physical appearance, an artistic representation, or a metaphorical expression of excellence or superiority.
