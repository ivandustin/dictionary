Etymology: The word "θερισον" (therison) comes from the Greek verb "θερίζω" (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". 

Grammar: "Θερισον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Θερισον" refers to the act of harvesting or reaping crops. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of gathering or collecting something.
