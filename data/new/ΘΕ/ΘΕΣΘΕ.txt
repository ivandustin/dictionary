Etymology: The word "θεσθε" comes from the Greek verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰeh₁-.

Grammar: "θεσθε" is the second person plural form of the verb "τίθημι" (tithemi) in the present tense middle voice in Greek. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "θεσθε" is used to express the action of placing or setting something in the present tense. It is specifically used when the subject is the one performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit.
