Etymology: The word "θελης" (thelēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "θέλω" (thelō), which means "to will" or "to desire."

Grammar: In Greek, "θελης" (thelēs) is a noun. It is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative, to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Θελης" (thelēs) refers to a person who has a strong will or desire. It can be translated as "willful," "determined," or "strong-willed" in English. It is often used to describe someone who is resolute and persistent in pursuing their goals or desires.
