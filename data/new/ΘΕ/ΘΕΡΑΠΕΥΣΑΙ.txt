Etymology: The word "θεραπεύσαι" comes from the Greek verb "θεραπεύω" (therapeúo), which means "to heal" or "to cure". 

Grammar: "θεραπεύσαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "θεραπεύω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that is not conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description: "θεραπεύσαι" is a Greek verb that means "to heal" or "to cure". It is used to describe the action of providing medical treatment or restoring someone's health.
