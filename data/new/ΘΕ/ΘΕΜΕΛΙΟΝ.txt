Etymology: The word "θεμελιον" comes from the Greek word "θεμελιος" (themelios), meaning "foundation" or "base".

Grammar: In Greek, "θεμελιον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "θεμελιου" (themeliou).

Description: "Θεμελιον" refers to the concept of a foundation or base, both in a literal and metaphorical sense. It is used to describe the solid structure upon which something is built or established. In a figurative sense, it can also refer to the fundamental principles or beliefs that serve as the basis for something.
