Etymology: The word "θεμελιος" (themiolos) comes from the Greek word "θεμελιον" (themelion), which means "foundation" or "base". 

Grammar: In Greek, "θεμελιος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms depending on the gender and number of the noun it modifies. 

Description: "Θεμελιος" is used to describe something that is foundational, fundamental, or essential. It refers to the basic or underlying principles or elements of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in architecture to describe the foundation of a building, or in a figurative sense to describe the core principles or beliefs of a system or ideology.
