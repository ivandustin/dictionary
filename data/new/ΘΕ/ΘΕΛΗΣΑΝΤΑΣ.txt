Etymology: The word "θελησαντας" (thelēsantas) comes from the Greek verb "θέλω" (thelō), which means "to want" or "to desire." The suffix "-αντας" (-antas) is a participle ending, indicating past tense and active voice.

Grammar: "Θελησαντας" is the accusative plural form of the participle "θελησας" (thelēsas), which is derived from the verb "θέλω" (thelō). In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as verbs.

Description: "Θελησαντας" is used to describe a group of people who have wanted or desired something in the past. It can be translated as "those who wanted" or "those who desired" in English.
