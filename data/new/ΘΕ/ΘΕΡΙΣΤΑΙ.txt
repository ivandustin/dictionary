Etymology: The word "θερισταί" comes from the Greek word "θερίζω" (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". 

Grammar: "θερισταί" is a noun in the plural form. It is the nominative case of the word, which is used to indicate the subject of a sentence.

Description: "θερισταί" refers to a group of people who are involved in the act of harvesting or reaping crops. It can be used to describe farmers, agricultural workers, or anyone who is engaged in the process of gathering crops from the fields.
