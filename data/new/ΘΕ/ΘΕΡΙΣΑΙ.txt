Etymology: The word "θερισαί" comes from the Greek verb "θερίζω" (therízo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". 

Grammar: "Θερισαί" is the plural form of the noun "θερισμός" (therismós), which means "harvest" or "reaping". It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Θερισαί" refers to multiple instances of the act of harvesting or reaping. It can be used to describe the process of gathering crops or cutting down plants for agricultural purposes.
