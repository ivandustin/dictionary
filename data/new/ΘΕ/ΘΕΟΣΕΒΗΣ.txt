Etymology: The word "θεοσέβης" (theosevēs) comes from the combination of two Greek words: "θεός" (theos) meaning "god" and "σέβομαι" (sebomai) meaning "to revere" or "to worship". Therefore, "θεοσέβης" can be translated as "god-fearing" or "devout".

Grammar: In Greek, "θεοσέβης" is a noun that functions as an adjective. It is in the masculine form and can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "θεοσέβης" is a term used to describe someone who has a deep reverence or respect for God. It is often used to refer to individuals who are devoutly religious or pious.
