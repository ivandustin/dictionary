Etymology: The word "θεοτητος" (theotētos) comes from the Greek roots "θεός" (theos) meaning "god" and "τητος" (tētos) meaning "quality" or "state." It is derived from the adjective "θεός" (theos) meaning "divine" or "godly."

Grammar: "Θεότητος" (theotētos) is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Θεότητος" (theotētos) refers to the quality or state of being divine or godlike. It is often used in theological or philosophical contexts to describe the nature or essence of a deity.
