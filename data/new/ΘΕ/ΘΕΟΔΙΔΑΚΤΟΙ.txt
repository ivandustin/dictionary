Etymology: The word "θεοδιδακτοι" comes from the Greek words "θεός" (theos) meaning "god" and "διδάσκω" (didasko) meaning "to teach". It literally translates to "god-taught".

Grammar: "θεοδιδακτοι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description: "θεοδιδακτοι" refers to individuals who are taught or instructed by God. It can be used to describe people who possess divine knowledge or wisdom, or those who are believed to have received direct guidance or inspiration from a higher power.
