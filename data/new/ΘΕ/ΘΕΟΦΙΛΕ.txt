Etymology: The word "θεοφιλε" (theophile) comes from the Greek words "θεός" (theos) meaning "god" and "φίλος" (philos) meaning "friend" or "lover". Therefore, "θεοφιλε" can be translated as "friend of god" or "lover of god".

Grammar: In Greek, "θεοφιλε" is a noun in the vocative case. It is used to directly address someone as a friend or lover of god.

Description: "θεοφιλε" is a term used in Greek to refer to someone who is devoted to or has a strong love for god. It is often used as a form of address or as a term of endearment for someone who is religious or pious.
