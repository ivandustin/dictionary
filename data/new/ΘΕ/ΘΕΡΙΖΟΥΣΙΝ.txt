Etymology: The word "θεριζουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "θερίζω" (therízo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap."

Grammar: "Θεριζουσιν" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "θερίζω" (therízo). It is used to describe an action being performed by a group of people or things.

Description: "Θεριζουσιν" is a Greek word that means "they harvest" or "they reap." It is used to indicate that a group of individuals is engaged in the act of gathering crops or cutting grain during the harvest season.
