Etymology: The word "θερισταίς" comes from the Greek word "θερίζω" (therizo), which means "to harvest" or "to reap". The suffix "-ταις" (-tais) indicates the plural form.

Grammar: "Θερισταίς" is a noun in the feminine gender and in the plural number. In Greek grammar, nouns change their endings to indicate gender, number, and case.

Description: "Θερισταίς" refers to female harvesters or reapers. It is used to describe women who work in the fields during the harvest season, gathering crops such as wheat, barley, or other grains.
