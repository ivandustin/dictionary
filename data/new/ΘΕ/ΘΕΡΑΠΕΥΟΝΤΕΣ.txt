Etymology: The word "θεραπευτής" (therapeftis) comes from the Greek verb "θεραπεύω" (therapevo), which means "to heal" or "to treat". It is derived from the noun "θεράπων" (therapon), which means "attendant" or "servant".

Grammar: In Greek, "θεραπευτής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "θεραπευτές" (therapeftes).

Description: "Θεραπευτής" is a Greek word that translates to "therapist" in English. It refers to a person who provides therapy or treatment to individuals who are experiencing physical, mental, or emotional difficulties. Therapists can work in various fields such as medicine, psychology, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy, among others. They use their knowledge and skills to help improve the well-being and quality of life of their patients.
