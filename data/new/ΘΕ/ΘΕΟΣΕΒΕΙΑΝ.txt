Etymology: The word "θεοσεβείαν" (theosebeian) comes from the Greek words "θεός" (theos) meaning "god" and "σέβομαι" (sevomai) meaning "to revere" or "to worship". 

Grammar: "θεοσεβείαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. It is derived from the root word "θεοσεβής" (theosebes), which is an adjective meaning "god-fearing" or "pious". 

Description: "θεοσεβείαν" refers to the act or quality of being devoutly religious or having a deep reverence for God. It implies a strong commitment to religious beliefs and practices, often involving worship, prayer, and obedience to religious teachings.
