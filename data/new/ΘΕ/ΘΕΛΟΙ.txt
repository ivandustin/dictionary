Etymology: The word "θελοι" (theloi) comes from the Greek verb "θέλω" (thélo), which means "to want" or "to desire."

Grammar: "Θελοι" is the third person singular form of the verb "θέλω" (thélo) in the present subjunctive mood. It is used to express a wish, desire, or possibility.

Description: "Θελοι" is a Greek word that can be translated as "may he/she/it want" or "may he/she/it desire." It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to express a wish or desire.
