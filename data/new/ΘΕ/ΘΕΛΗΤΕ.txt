Etymology: The word "θελητε" (thelēte) comes from the Greek verb "θέλω" (thelō), which means "to want" or "to desire."

Grammar: "Θελητε" is the second person plural form of the verb "θέλω" (thelō) in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all want" or "you all desire."

Description: "Θελητε" is a verb form that expresses the action of wanting or desiring. It is commonly used in Greek to talk about what a group of people wants or desires.
