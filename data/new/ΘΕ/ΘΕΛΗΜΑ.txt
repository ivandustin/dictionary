Etymology: The word "θελημα" (thelēma) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "θέλω" (thelō), which means "to will" or "to desire."

Grammar: In Greek, "θελημα" is a noun of neuter gender. It is singular and can be used in both the nominative and accusative cases.

Description: "Θελημα" refers to a will, desire, or wish. It represents the act of wanting or choosing something. It can also be used to describe a person's intention or purpose.
