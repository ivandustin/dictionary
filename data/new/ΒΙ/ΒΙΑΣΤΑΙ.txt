Etymology: The word "βιασταί" (viastai) comes from the Greek verb "βιάζομαι" (viázomai), which means "to force" or "to violate." It is derived from the noun "βία" (vía), meaning "force" or "violence."

Grammar: "Βιασταί" is the plural form of the noun "βιαστής" (viastís), which means "a violator" or "an oppressor." It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Βιασταί" refers to a group of people who engage in acts of violence, force, or oppression. It can be used to describe individuals who forcefully impose their will on others or violate their rights.
