Etymology: The word "βιβλίο" (vivlio) comes from the Greek word "βίβλος" (biblos), which originally referred to the inner bark of the papyrus plant used for writing. Over time, it came to mean a written document or book.

Grammar: "Βιβλίο" is a neuter noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "βιβλία" (vivlia).

Description: "Βιβλίο" means "book" in Greek. It refers to a written or printed work consisting of pages bound together. It can be used to describe any type of book, such as a novel, textbook, or reference book.
