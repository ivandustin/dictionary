Etymology: The word "βιβλίον" (biblion) comes from the Greek word "βίβλος" (biblos), which originally referred to the inner bark of the papyrus plant used for writing. Over time, it came to mean a written document or a book.

Grammar: In Greek, "βιβλίον" is a neuter noun. It is the singular form, and the plural form is "βιβλία" (biblia).

Description: "Βιβλίον" is the Greek word for "book." It refers to a written or printed work consisting of pages bound together. It can contain various types of information, such as stories, knowledge, or records. Books have been an essential medium for preserving and transmitting knowledge throughout history.
