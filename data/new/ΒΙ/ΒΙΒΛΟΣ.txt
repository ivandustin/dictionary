Etymology: The word "βιβλος" (biblos) comes from the Greek word meaning "book". It is derived from the Phoenician word "byblos", which referred to the papyrus plant used for making paper.

Grammar: In Greek, "βιβλος" (biblos) is a noun of the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The plural form is "βιβλοι" (bibloi).

Description: "Βιβλος" (biblos) is the Greek word for "book". It refers to a written or printed work consisting of pages bound together. It can also be used to describe a literary composition or a collection of writings on a particular subject.
