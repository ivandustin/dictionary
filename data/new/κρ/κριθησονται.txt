Word: κριθησονται

Etymology:
The word κριθησονται is a verb form in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word κρίνω (krinó), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The suffix -σονται (-sontai) is a future tense marker, indicating that the action of the verb will take place in the future.

κριθησονται is a third-person plural future passive indicative form of the verb κρίνω. In this form, the subject of the sentence is the recipient of the action, rather than the doer of the action. The verb tense indicates that the action will take place in the future.

Description:
The word κριθησονται can be translated to mean "they will be judged" or "they will be decided". It is often used in a legal or judicial context, referring to a decision or judgment that will be made in the future.
