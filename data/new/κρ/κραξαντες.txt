Word: κραξάντες

Etymology:
The word κραξάντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κράζω (krázo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The suffix -άντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates past tense and active voice. Therefore, κραξάντες means "having shouted" or "having cried out".

Grammar:
κραξάντες is a past participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe a group of people who have shouted or cried out in the past.

Description:
κραξάντες is a word that describes a specific action that has already taken place. It is often used in historical or narrative contexts to describe a group of people who have shouted or cried out in a particular situation.
