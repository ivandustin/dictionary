Word: κρειττονος

Etymology:
The word κρειττονος (kreittonos) is derived from the Greek word κρείττων (kreitton), which means "better" or "superior". It is a comparative form of the adjective καλός (kalos), which means "good" or "beautiful".

Grammar:
κρειττονος is the genitive singular form of κρείττων (kreitton), which is a first and second declension adjective in Greek. It is used to describe a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship.

Description:
κρειττονος is an adjective that is used to describe something that is better or superior in quality or value. It can be used to describe a wide range of things, such as a person, an object, or an idea.
