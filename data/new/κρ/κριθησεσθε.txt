Word: κριθησεσθε

Etymology:
The word κριθησεσθε is derived from the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The suffix -θησεσθε (-thēsesthe) is a second-person plural passive ending, indicating that the action of judging or deciding is being done to you.

Grammar:
κριθησεσθε is a second-person plural passive verb form in the future tense. It is used to indicate that you (plural) will be judged or decided upon by someone or something else.

Description:
The word κριθησεσθε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 7:1, where Jesus says "Judge not, that ye be not judged." The word is often interpreted as a warning against being judgmental or critical of others, as one's own actions and character will also be judged in turn.
