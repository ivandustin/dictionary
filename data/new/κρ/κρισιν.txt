The word "κρισιν" (krisin) is a Greek noun that means "judgment" or "decision". It is derived from the verb "κρίνω" (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". In terms of grammar, "κρισιν" is a third declension feminine noun in the accusative case. It can also be spelled as "κρίσιν" with an accent on the iota. In Greek mythology, "κρισιν" is often associated with the goddess Themis, who was the personification of divine law and order.
