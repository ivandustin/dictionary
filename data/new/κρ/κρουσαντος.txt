Word: κρουσαντος

Etymology: The word κρουσαντος comes from the Greek verb κρούω (krouo), which means "to strike" or "to hit". The suffix -αντος (-antos) is a participle suffix, which means that κρουσαντος can be translated as "the one who strikes" or "the one who hits".

Grammar: κρουσαντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated into English as "of" or "'s". For example, κρουσαντος could be translated as "of the striker" or "of the hitter".

Description: κρουσαντος is not a commonly used word in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek texts, where it is used to describe someone who strikes or hits something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is forceful or aggressive in their actions or words.
