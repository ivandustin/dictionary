Word: κριματος

Etymology:
The word κριματος (krimatos) is derived from the Greek word κρίμα (krima), which means "judgment" or "verdict". It is related to the verb κρίνω (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide".

Grammar:
κριματος is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. In this case, κριματος indicates possession or origin, and can be translated as "of judgment" or "of verdict".

Description:
κριματος is a word that refers to a judgment or verdict, typically in a legal or moral sense. It can also refer to the consequences or punishment resulting from a judgment. The word is often used in religious contexts, particularly in reference to the final judgment of souls after death.
