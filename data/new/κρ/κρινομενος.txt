Word: κρινομενος

Etymology:
The word κρινομενος comes from the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending, which means that κρινομενος is a present participle of κρίνω. 

Grammar:
κρινομενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -μενος to the stem of the verb. They function as adjectives and describe an ongoing action or state. 

Description:
κρινομενος can be translated as "judging" or "deciding". It describes someone who is currently in the act of judging or deciding. In context, it could refer to a person who is making a decision or passing judgment on a particular matter.
