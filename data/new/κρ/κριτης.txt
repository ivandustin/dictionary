Word: κριτής

Etymology:
The word κριτής (kritēs) comes from the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The noun κριτής refers to a judge or an arbiter.

Grammar:
In Greek, κριτής is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is κριτής, and its genitive singular form is κριτοῦ. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
In ancient Greece, the role of the κριτής was highly respected and important. They were responsible for settling disputes and making decisions in legal cases. The word κριτής is still used in modern Greek to refer to a judge or a referee in sports.
