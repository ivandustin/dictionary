Word: κραυγή

The word κραυγή (kraugé) comes from the Ancient Greek verb κράζω (krázō), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ger- which means "to cry out" or "to shout".

Description:
κραυγή refers to a loud, sharp, and sudden cry or scream. It can be used to describe a variety of sounds, such as the cry of an animal, the sound of a siren, or the scream of a person.
