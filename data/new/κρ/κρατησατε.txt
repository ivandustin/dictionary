Word: κρατησατε

Etymology: κρατησατε is a verb form in the Ancient Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and imperative mood. It is derived from the verb κρατέω (krateo), which means "to hold, to grasp, to possess, to rule, to prevail".

Grammar: κρατησατε is the second person plural form of the aorist tense and imperative mood of the verb κρατέω. In English, it would be translated as "you (all) held" or "you (all) grasp". 

Description: κρατησατε is a command or request to a group of people to hold onto something or to grasp something tightly. It can also be used metaphorically to mean to hold onto power or to rule over something.
