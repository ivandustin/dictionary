The word "κρατει" (kratei) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "κρατ-" (krat-), which means "to rule" or "to have power". 

In terms of grammar, "κρατει" is in the present tense and active voice. It is conjugated in the third person singular form, which means that it refers to a single subject who is currently performing the action of the verb. 

The word "κρατει" can be translated to English as "he/she/it rules" or "he/she/it has power". It is often used in a political or social context to describe someone who holds authority or control over a group of people or a situation. 

Overall, "κρατει" is a versatile and important verb in the Greek language, with a rich history and cultural significance.
