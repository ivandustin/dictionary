Word: κρινοντες

Etymology:
The word κρινοντες is derived from the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently judging or deciding.

Grammar:
κρινοντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is the plural form of the participle, indicating that it refers to a group of people who are currently judging or deciding.

Description:
The word κρινοντες can be translated to "judging" or "deciding". It is often used in a legal or judicial context, referring to people who are making judgments or decisions in a court of law.
