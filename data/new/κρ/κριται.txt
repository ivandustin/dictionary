Word: κριται

Etymology:
The word κριται (kritai) is derived from the Greek word κριτής (kritēs), which means "judge" or "arbiter". It is derived from the verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide".

Grammar:
κριται is the plural form of κριτής (kritēs), which is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
κριται refers to a group of judges or arbiters. In ancient Greece, kritai were appointed to preside over legal cases and make decisions based on the evidence presented. They were highly respected members of society and played an important role in maintaining justice and order. The word κριται can also be used metaphorically to refer to any group of people who are responsible for making important decisions.
