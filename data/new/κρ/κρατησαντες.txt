Word: κρατησαντες

Etymology: κρατησαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb κρατέω (kratéō), which means "to hold, to grasp, to seize". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates past action, so κρατησαντες means "having held, having grasped, having seized".

Grammar: κρατησαντες is the aorist participle of the verb κρατέω (kratéō) in the masculine plural form. The aorist tense in Greek indicates a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the participle form functions as an adjective that describes the subject of the sentence.

Description: κρατησαντες is a word that describes a group of people who have held, grasped, or seized something in the past. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and significance of the action.
