Word: κρατους

Etymology:
The word κρατους (kratous) is derived from the Greek root word κράτος (kratos), which means "power" or "strength". It is a noun in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship.

Grammar:
In Greek, κρατους is a noun in the genitive case. It is a singular noun, meaning it refers to one thing. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship, and in this case, it indicates that κρατους is related to or possessed by something else.

Description:
The word κρατους is not commonly used in modern Greek, but it appears in ancient Greek texts. It is often translated as "power" or "dominion", and is used to describe the strength or authority of a ruler or government. In some contexts, it can also refer to physical strength or force.
