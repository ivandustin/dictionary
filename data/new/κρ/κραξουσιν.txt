The word "κραξουσιν" (kraxousin) is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form, in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the root word "κράζω" (krazo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The suffix "-ουσιν" (-ousin) indicates the future tense, third person plural form.

Grammar: As mentioned, "κραξουσιν" is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is multiple people.

Description: The word "κραξουσιν" can be translated to mean "they will shout" or "they will cry out". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a group of people who will express their opinions or emotions loudly and passionately.
