The word "κρισεως" (krisis) is a Greek noun that means "judgment" or "decision". It comes from the verb "κρίνω" (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". In grammar, it is a third declension feminine noun, meaning that its endings change depending on its function in a sentence.
