Word: κρινομενοι

Etymology:
The word κρινομενοι is a present participle form of the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *krey- which means "to separate" or "to sift".

Grammar:
κρινομενοι is a present participle form of the verb κρίνω (krinō) in the middle voice. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The present participle form indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
The word κρινομενοι can be translated as "judging ourselves" or "deciding for ourselves". It is often used in a moral or ethical context, indicating that individuals are responsible for making their own decisions and judgments. In the New Testament, the word is used in the context of self-examination and self-judgment, as in 1 Corinthians 11:31: "For if we would judge ourselves, we should not be judged."
