Word: κρατων

Etymology:
The word κρατων comes from the Greek verb κρατέω (krateo), which means "to hold, to grasp, to rule, to prevail". The suffix -ων (-on) is a present participle ending, which means that κρατων can be translated as "the one who holds, rules, or prevails".

Grammar:
κρατων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a present participle, which means that it describes an ongoing action or state. In this case, it describes someone who is currently holding, ruling, or prevailing.

Description:
κρατων is a powerful and dynamic word that conveys strength, authority, and dominance. It is often used in Greek mythology to describe gods and heroes who have the power to control and shape the world around them. In modern Greek, it is also used to describe someone who is strong-willed, determined, and successful in their endeavors.
