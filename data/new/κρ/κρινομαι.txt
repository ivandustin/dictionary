The word "κρινομαι" (krinomai) is a Greek verb that means "to judge" or "to decide". It comes from the root word "κρίνω" (krinō), which also means "to judge" or "to separate". 

In terms of grammar, "κρινομαι" is a middle voice verb, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects of judgment or decision-making.

In description, "κρινομαι" can be used in a variety of contexts, from legal proceedings to personal opinions. It can refer to making a judgment about someone's character or actions, or to making a decision about a course of action. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as judging the quality of a piece of art or music.
