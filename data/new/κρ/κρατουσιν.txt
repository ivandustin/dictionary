Word: κρατουσιν

Etymology: The word κρατουσιν is derived from the Greek verb κρατέω (krateo), which means "to rule" or "to have power over". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) is a third person plural present active participle ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of people who are currently ruling or exercising power.

Grammar: κρατουσιν is a third person plural present active participle in Greek. It is declined as follows:

- Nominative: κρατοῦσιν (kratousin)
- Genitive: κρατουσῶν (kratouson)
- Dative: κρατοῦσι(ν) (kratousi(n))
- Accusative: κρατοῦσι(ν) (kratousi(n))

Description: κρατουσιν refers to a group of people who are currently ruling or exercising power. It can be used to describe a political or social elite, or a group of individuals who hold a position of authority or influence. The word is often used in a negative context, implying that the ruling group is oppressive or corrupt.
