Word: κραξας

Etymology: κραξας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κράζω (krázo), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The suffix -ας (-as) is a past tense marker, indicating that the action of shouting or crying out has already taken place.

Grammar: κραξας is the aorist tense of the verb κράζω (krázo), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence (the person doing the shouting or crying out) is performing the action.

Description: κραξας can be translated as "he shouted" or "he cried out". It is a strong and forceful verb that suggests a loud and urgent call. The context in which it is used will determine the exact meaning and tone of the shout or cry.
