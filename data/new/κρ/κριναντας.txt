Word: κριναντας

Etymology:
The word κριναντας is a form of the Greek verb κρίνω (krinó), which means "to judge" or "to decide". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *krei-, which means "to separate" or "to distinguish".

Grammar:
κριναντας is the present active participle of κρίνω in the accusative plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently judging or deciding something. In English, it can be translated as "judging" or "deciding".

Description:
κριναντας is a word that describes a group of people who are actively engaged in the process of judging or deciding something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as legal proceedings, debates, or even everyday conversations. The word carries a sense of authority and responsibility, as those who are judging or deciding are often seen as having an important role in the outcome of a situation.
