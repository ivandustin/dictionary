Word: κρινεις

Etymology:
The word κρινεις (krineis) is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European *krei-, which means "to separate" or "to distinguish".

Grammar:
κρινεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the verb κρίνω. It is used to address one person who is currently judging or deciding something. The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now.

Description:
κρινεις is a word that is often used in Greek literature and conversation to address someone who is currently making a decision or passing judgment. It can be used in a variety of contexts, from legal proceedings to personal relationships. The word carries a sense of authority and responsibility, as the person being addressed is expected to make a fair and just decision.
