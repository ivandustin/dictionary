The word "κραυγης" (kraugēs) is a Greek noun that means "a loud cry or scream." It is derived from the verb "κράζω" (krazō), which means "to cry out or shout." In terms of grammar, "κραυγης" is a third-declension noun, with a stem that ends in -γη- (gē). It is masculine in gender and singular in number. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the sound of battle cries or the screams of animals.
