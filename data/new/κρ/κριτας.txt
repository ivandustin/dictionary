κριτάς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes κριτές (krites). It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns, which means that its stem ends in -τ.

κριτάς refers to a judge or a magistrate in ancient Greece. In Athens, the κριτάς was a public official who presided over the courts and was responsible for interpreting and enforcing the law. The position of κριτάς was highly respected and was often held by wealthy and influential citizens. In other Greek city-states, the role of the κριτάς varied, but it generally involved some form of legal or judicial responsibility.
