Word: κριτικός

Etymology: The word κριτικός (kritikos) comes from the Greek word κριτής (kritis), which means "judge" or "critic". It is derived from the verb κρίνω (krino), which means "to judge" or "to decide".

Grammar: κριτικός is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined in different cases, such as κριτική (feminine) and κριτικό (neuter).

Description: κριτικός is a word that is commonly used in Greek to describe someone who is critical or judgmental. It can also refer to someone who is skilled in the art of criticism, such as a literary or film critic. In modern Greek, the word is often used to describe someone who is overly critical or negative.
