Word: κρατεισθαι

Etymology: κρατεισθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κρατεῖν (kratein), which means "to hold, to rule, to have power over." The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) indicates a reflexive action, so κρατεισθαι means "to hold oneself, to maintain oneself, to keep oneself in power."

Grammar: κρατεισθαι is a present passive infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -σθαι (-sthai) to the present stem of the verb κρατεῖν (kratein). The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: κρατεισθαι is a verb that describes the act of maintaining power or control over oneself or others. It can be used in a political or social context to describe a leader or ruler who is able to maintain their position of power, or in a personal context to describe someone who is able to control their own actions and emotions.
