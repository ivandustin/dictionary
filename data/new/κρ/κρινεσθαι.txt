The word "κρινεσθαι" is a Greek verb that means "to judge" or "to decide". It is derived from the root word "κρίνω" which means "to separate" or "to distinguish". In terms of grammar, "κρινεσθαι" is a middle voice verb, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

In ancient Greek literature, "κρινεσθαι" was often used in legal contexts, such as in trials or in making judgments about right and wrong. It was also used in philosophical discussions about ethics and morality. 

Overall, "κρινεσθαι" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to express the act of making a decision or judgment.
