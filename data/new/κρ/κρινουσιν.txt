Word: κρινουσιν

Etymology: κρινουσιν is the present active indicative third person plural form of the Greek verb κρίνω (krinō), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European *krei-, which means "to separate" or "to distinguish".

Grammar: κρινουσιν is a verb in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. It is in the active voice, meaning that the subject is performing the action. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people or things.

Description: κρινουσιν can be translated to "they judge" or "they decide". It is a common verb in Greek and is used in various contexts, such as legal proceedings, personal decisions, and moral judgments. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of evaluating or assessing something.
