Word: υετούς

Etymology: The word υετούς comes from the Greek word υετός (uetos), which means "rain". The suffix -ούς (-ous) is added to form the adjective form, meaning "rainy".

Grammar: υετούς is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a noun as being rainy or having rain.

Description: υετούς is a word used to describe a place or time that is characterized by rain. It can be used to describe a rainy day, a rainy season, or a region that receives a lot of rain. For example, "Η Βρετανία είναι μια υετούς χώρα" (I Vretanía íne mia uetoús chóra) means "Britain is a rainy country".
