Etymology: The word "οὐρανοῖς" (ouranois) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "οὐρανός" (ouranos), which means "heaven" or "sky."

Grammar: "Οὐρανοῖς" is the dative plural form of the noun "οὐρανός" (ouranos). In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Οὐρανοῖς" refers to the heavens or the skies. It can be used to describe the celestial realm or the expanse above the Earth. In Greek mythology, "Οὐρανοῖς" is often associated with the realm of the gods.
