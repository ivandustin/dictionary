Etymology: The word "ουδαμως" (oudamos) comes from the combination of the negative particle "ου" (ou) and the adverb "δαμως" (damōs), which means "at all" or "in any way". 

Grammar: "Ουδαμως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to express a strong negation or denial, similar to the English phrase "not at all" or "by no means". 

Description: "Ουδαμως" is used to emphasize that something is absolutely not true or does not happen in any way. It is often used to express a strong disagreement or to contradict a statement.
