Etymology: The word "οὑχί" (oukhi) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the negative particle "οὐ" (ou) and the particle "χί" (khi), which is used for emphasis.

Grammar: "Οὑχί" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to express a strong negation or denial. It is often translated as "certainly not," "absolutely not," or "by no means."

Description: "Οὑχί" is used to emphasize a negative statement or to strongly deny something. It is commonly used in Greek to express a firm refusal or disagreement.
