Etymology: The word "οὐραῖος" (ouraios) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "οὐρά" (oura), meaning "tail."

Grammar: In Greek, "οὐραῖος" is an adjective. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: The word "οὐραῖος" is used to describe something that is related to or characteristic of a tail. It can refer to an object, animal, or person that has a tail or tail-like features.
