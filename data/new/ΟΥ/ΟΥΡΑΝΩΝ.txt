Etymology: The word "οὐρανός" (ouranos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂éusōs, meaning "dawn" or "sky". In Greek mythology, Ouranos was the personification of the sky.

Grammar: "Οὐρανός" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "οὐρανός". In the genitive singular, it becomes "οὐρανοῦ" and in the accusative singular, it becomes "οὐρανόν". The plural forms are "οὐρανοί" (nominative), "οὐρανῶν" (genitive), and "οὐρανούς" (accusative).

Description: "Οὐρανός" is the Greek word for "sky" or "heaven". In Greek mythology, it was believed to be a solid dome that covered the Earth. It was the realm of the gods and goddesses, and the place where celestial bodies such as the sun, moon, and stars were believed to reside. The concept of "οὐρανός" also carries a spiritual and divine connotation, representing the realm of the divine and the afterlife.
