Etymology: The word "οὐρα" (oura) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ówr̥ ("tail").

Grammar: In Greek, "οὐρα" (oura) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the first declension and has a singular and plural form. The plural form is "οὐραί" (ourai).

Description: "Οὐρα" (oura) means "tail" in Greek. It refers to the posterior part of an animal's body that extends beyond the trunk or torso. The word can be used to describe the tail of various animals, including mammals, reptiles, and birds.
