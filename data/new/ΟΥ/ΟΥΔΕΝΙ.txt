Etymology: The word "ουδενι" (oudení) comes from the Greek word "οὐδείς" (oudeís), which means "no one" or "nothing." It is a combination of the negative particle "οὐ" (ou) and the word "εἷς" (eîs), meaning "one."

Grammar: "Ουδενι" (oudení) is a pronoun in the Greek language. It is the accusative form of "οὐδείς" (oudeís) and is used to indicate negation or absence. It can be translated as "no one" or "nothing."

Description: "Ουδενι" (oudení) is used to express the absence or non-existence of someone or something. It is often used in negative sentences or questions to indicate that there is no person or thing involved.
