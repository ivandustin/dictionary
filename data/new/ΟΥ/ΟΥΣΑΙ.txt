Etymology: The word "οὐσαί" (ousai) comes from the Greek verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be." It is the present participle form of the verb, indicating "being" or "existing."

Grammar: "Οὐσαί" is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or to predicate a quality of the subject.

Description: "Οὐσαί" is the plural form of the noun "οὐσία" (ousia), which means "essence" or "being." It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to refer to the fundamental nature or substance of something.
