Etymology: The word "οὐρανοῦ" (ouranou) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "οὐρανός" (ouranos), which means "sky" or "heaven."

Grammar: "Οὐρανοῦ" is the genitive form of the noun "οὐρανός" (ouranos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Οὐρανοῦ" is a Greek word that means "of the sky" or "of the heaven." It is often used in religious or poetic contexts to refer to the celestial realm or the dwelling place of the gods.
