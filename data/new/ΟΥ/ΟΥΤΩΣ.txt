Etymology: The word "οὖτως" (oûtos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the particle "οὗτος" (hoûtos), meaning "this," and the adverb "ὡς" (hōs), meaning "thus" or "in this way."

Grammar: "Οὖτως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate manner or way, and it can be translated as "thus," "in this way," or "in such a manner."

Description: "Οὖτως" is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to express various meanings related to manner, method, or way of doing something. It can be used to describe actions, events, or situations.
