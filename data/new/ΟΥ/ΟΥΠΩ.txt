Etymology: The word "ουπω" (oupo) comes from the combination of the Greek words "οὐ" (ou) meaning "not" and "πω" (po) meaning "yet". 

Grammar: "Ουπω" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to express negation or denial of an action that has not yet occurred. It is often translated as "not yet" in English.

Description: "Ουπω" is a word used to indicate that something has not happened or is not the case at the present moment, but it may happen in the future. It is commonly used in Greek to express anticipation or expectation for something to occur.
