Etymology: The word "οὐρανός" (ouranos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂éusōs, meaning "dawn" or "sky". In Greek mythology, Ouranos was the personification of the sky.

Grammar: In Greek, "οὐρανός" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case when used as the subject of a sentence. The genitive form is "οὐρανοῦ" (ouranou), the dative form is "οὐρανῷ" (ouranōi), and the accusative form is "οὐρανόν" (ouranon).

Description: "Οὐρανός" is the Greek word for "sky" or "heaven". It refers to the celestial dome or the expanse that appears above the Earth. In Greek mythology, Ouranos was also considered the father of the Titans and the grandfather of the Olympian gods.
