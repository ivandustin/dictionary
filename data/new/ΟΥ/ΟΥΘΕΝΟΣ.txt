Etymology: The word "οὐθενός" (outhenos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the negative particle "οὐ" (ou) meaning "not" and the word "θενός" (thenos) meaning "something." 

Grammar: "Οὐθενός" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: The word "οὐθενός" is used to describe something that is nonexistent, non-existent, or nothing. It refers to the absence or lack of anything.
