Etymology: The word "ουκετι" (ouketi) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the negative particle "οὐ" (ou) meaning "not" and the adverb "κετι" (keti) meaning "anymore" or "no longer".

Grammar: "Ουκετι" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate that something is no longer the case or that something is no longer happening.

Description: "Ουκετι" is commonly used in Greek to express the idea of "no longer" or "not anymore". It is often used to indicate a change or cessation of a certain state or action.
