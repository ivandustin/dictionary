Etymology: The word "οὐρανοῦς" (ouranous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "οὐρανός" (ouranos), which means "heaven" or "sky."

Grammar: "Οὐρανοῦς" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: "Οὐρανοῦς" refers to the plural form of the noun "οὐρανός" (ouranos). It is used to describe multiple heavens or skies. In Greek mythology, "οὐρανοῦς" is often associated with the realm of the gods and is considered the dwelling place of the divine beings.
