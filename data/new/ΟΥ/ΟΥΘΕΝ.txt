Etymology: The word "οὐθεν" (outhen) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the negative particle "οὐ" (ou) meaning "not" and the word "θεν" (then) meaning "something." Therefore, "οὐθεν" (outhen) can be translated as "nothing."

Grammar: In Greek, "οὐθεν" (outhen) is a pronoun that functions as a neuter singular form. It is used to indicate the absence or lack of something.

Description: "Οὐθεν" (outhen) is a word that expresses the concept of nothingness or nonexistence. It is often used to negate the presence of something or to describe a state of emptiness.
