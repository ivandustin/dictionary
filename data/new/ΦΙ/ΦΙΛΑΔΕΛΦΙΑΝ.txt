Etymology: The word "φιλαδελφίαν" (philadelphia) comes from the Greek words "φίλος" (philos) meaning "love" and "ἀδελφός" (adelphos) meaning "brother". It literally translates to "brotherly love".

Grammar: "Φιλαδελφίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Φιλαδελφίαν" refers to the concept of brotherly love or affection between brothers. It can also be used more broadly to describe a sense of camaraderie, friendship, or goodwill towards others.
