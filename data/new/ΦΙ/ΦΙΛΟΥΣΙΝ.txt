Etymology: The word "φιλοσοφία" (philosophia) comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos), meaning "love," and "σοφία" (sophia), meaning "wisdom." Therefore, "φιλοσοφία" can be translated as "love of wisdom."

Grammar: In Greek, "φιλοσοφία" is a feminine noun. It is written in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "φιλοσοφίες" (philosophies).

Description: "Φιλοσοφία" refers to the study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values, reason, and more. It is a discipline that seeks to understand the nature of reality, human existence, and the universe. Philosophers engage in critical thinking, analysis, and argumentation to explore these questions and develop theories and perspectives. Philosophy encompasses various branches such as metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, logic, and aesthetics.
