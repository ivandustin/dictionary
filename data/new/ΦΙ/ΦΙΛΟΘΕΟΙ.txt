Etymology: The word "φιλοθεοι" (philotheoi) comes from the Greek words "φίλος" (philos) meaning "friend" and "θεοί" (theoi) meaning "gods". It can be translated as "lovers of gods" or "friends of gods".

Grammar: "Φιλοθεοι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is formed by combining the root word "φιλος" (philos) meaning "friend" with the plural form of "θεοί" (theoi) meaning "gods".

Description: "Φιλοθεοι" refers to individuals who have a deep love, admiration, or devotion for gods. It can be used to describe people who are religious, pious, or devoted to the worship and service of gods.
