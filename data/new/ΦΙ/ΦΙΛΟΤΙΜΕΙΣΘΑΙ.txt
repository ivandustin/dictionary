Etymology: The word "φιλοτιμείσθαι" comes from the Greek root words "φίλος" (phílos), meaning "love" or "friend," and "τιμή" (timḗ), meaning "honor" or "respect." 

Grammar: "Φιλοτιμείσθαι" is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

Description: "Φιλοτιμείσθαι" is a Greek word that can be translated as "to strive for honor" or "to seek respect." It conveys the idea of pursuing recognition and esteem from others, often through one's actions or achievements. It is a concept deeply rooted in Greek culture and values.
