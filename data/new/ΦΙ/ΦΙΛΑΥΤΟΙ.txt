Etymology: The word "φιλαυτοι" (pronounced "filauti") comes from the Greek words "φίλος" (philos) meaning "friend" and "αὐτός" (autos) meaning "self". It literally translates to "lovers of self" or "self-lovers".

Grammar: "Φιλαυτοι" is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The plural form indicates that there are multiple individuals who are self-lovers.

Description: "Φιλαυτοι" is a term used to describe individuals who have an excessive love or admiration for themselves. It refers to people who are excessively self-centered, narcissistic, or egotistical.
