Etymology: The word "φιληδόνοι" comes from the Greek words "φίλος" (phílos) meaning "friend" and "ἀηδών" (aēdṓn) meaning "nightingale". 

Grammar: "Φιληδόνοι" is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative form of the word, used to refer to a group of nightingales.

Description: "Φιληδόνοι" is a term used in Greek literature and poetry to describe nightingales. Nightingales are small birds known for their beautiful and melodious songs. They are often associated with love, beauty, and the arrival of spring. In Greek mythology, the nightingale's song was said to be so enchanting that it could even charm the gods.
