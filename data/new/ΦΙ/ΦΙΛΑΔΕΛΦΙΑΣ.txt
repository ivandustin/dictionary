Etymology: The word "φιλαδελφίας" (philadelphias) comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos) meaning "loving" and "ἀδελφός" (adelphos) meaning "brother". It literally translates to "brotherly love".

Grammar: "Φιλαδελφίας" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Φιλαδελφίας" refers to the concept of brotherly love or affection between brothers. It can also be used more broadly to describe a sense of camaraderie, friendship, or goodwill between people.
