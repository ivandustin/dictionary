Etymology: The word "φιλοσοφία" (philosophia) comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos), meaning "love," and "σοφία" (sophia), meaning "wisdom." Therefore, "φιλοσοφία" can be translated as "love of wisdom."

Grammar: In Greek, "φιλοσοφία" is a feminine noun. It is written in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "φιλοσοφίες" (philosophies).

Description: "Φιλοσοφία" refers to the study and pursuit of knowledge, understanding, and wisdom about fundamental questions concerning existence, reality, knowledge, values, reason, and more. It encompasses various branches such as metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, logic, and aesthetics. Philosophers engage in critical thinking, analysis, and reflection to explore these topics and develop theories and frameworks to understand the world and human existence.
