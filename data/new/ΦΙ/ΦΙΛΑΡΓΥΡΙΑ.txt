Etymology: The word "φιλαργυρία" comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (phílos), meaning "love" or "friend", and "ἄργυρος" (árgyros), meaning "silver" or "money". 

Grammar: "Φιλαργυρία" is a noun in the Greek language. It is feminine in gender and singular in number.

Description: "Φιλαργυρία" refers to the excessive love or greed for money. It is the desire to accumulate wealth and possessions, often at the expense of others or without regard for ethical considerations.
