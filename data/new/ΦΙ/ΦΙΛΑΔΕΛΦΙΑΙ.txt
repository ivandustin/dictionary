Etymology: The word "φιλαδελφία" comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (phílos) meaning "friend" and "ἀδελφός" (adelphós) meaning "brother". It literally translates to "brotherly love" or "love for one's brothers".

Grammar: "Φιλαδελφία" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender.

Description: "Φιλαδελφία" is a concept that refers to the love and affection between siblings or brothers. It represents a strong bond and sense of unity among family members. In a broader sense, it can also be used to describe a sense of camaraderie and friendship among people.
