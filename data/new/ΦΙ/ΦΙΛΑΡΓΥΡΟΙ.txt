Etymology: The word "φιλαργυροι" comes from the Greek roots "φιλος" (philos) meaning "love" and "αργυρος" (argyros) meaning "silver". It literally translates to "lovers of silver".

Grammar: "Φιλαργυροι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is in the masculine gender.

Description: "Φιλαργυροι" is used to describe individuals who have a strong love or desire for money or wealth. It refers to people who are excessively greedy or materialistic.
