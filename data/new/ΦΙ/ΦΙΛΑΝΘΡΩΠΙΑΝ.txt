Etymology: The word "φιλανθρωπίαν" (philanthropian) comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos) meaning "love" and "ἄνθρωπος" (anthropos) meaning "human being". 

Grammar: "Φιλανθρωπίαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. 

Description: "Φιλανθρωπίαν" refers to the concept of philanthropy, which is the desire to promote the welfare of others, usually through charitable acts or donations. It encompasses the idea of love and compassion towards humanity and the belief in the importance of helping those in need.
