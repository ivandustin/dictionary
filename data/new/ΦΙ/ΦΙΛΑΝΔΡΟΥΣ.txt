Etymology: The word "φιλανδρος" (philandros) comes from the Greek roots "φιλος" (philos) meaning "loving" or "fond of" and "ανδρος" (andros) meaning "man". 

Grammar: In Greek, "φιλανδρος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can be declined according to the Greek noun declension system.

Description: "Φιλανδρος" is an adjective that describes someone who is loving or fond of men. It can be used to describe a person who has a strong affection or preference for the company of men.
