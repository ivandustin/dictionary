Etymology: The word "φιλανθρωπία" comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos), meaning "love" or "friend," and "άνθρωπος" (anthropos), meaning "human being." Therefore, "φιλανθρωπία" can be translated as "love for humanity" or "friendship towards mankind."

Grammar: In Greek, "φιλανθρωπία" is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows:

- Singular: η φιλανθρωπία (ee filanthropia) - the philanthropy
- Plural: οι φιλανθρωπίες (ee filanthropies) - the philanthropies

Description: "Φιλανθρωπία" refers to the act of showing kindness, compassion, and generosity towards others, particularly towards those in need. It encompasses various forms of charitable actions, such as donating money, providing assistance, or supporting social causes. Philanthropy aims to improve the well-being and quality of life for individuals and communities, often focusing on areas such as education, healthcare, poverty alleviation, and environmental conservation.
