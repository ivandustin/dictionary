Etymology: The word "φιλονικία" comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos), meaning "love" or "friendship," and "νίκη" (nike), meaning "victory." 

Grammar: "Φιλονικία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form. 

Description: "Φιλονικία" refers to the tendency or inclination to engage in or promote quarrels, disputes, or arguments. It can also describe a love for contention or a desire to win at all costs.
