Etymology: The word "φιλοπρωτεύων" comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos) meaning "loving" or "friend" and "πρῶτος" (protos) meaning "first". 

Grammar: "Φιλοπρωτεύων" is a Greek adjective in the masculine form. It is in the present active participle form, which indicates that the subject is currently performing the action of the verb. 

Description: "Φιλοπρωτεύων" describes someone who loves to be first or who seeks to be in a leading position. It can also refer to someone who is ambitious or competitive.
