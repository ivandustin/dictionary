Etymology: The word "φιλονικος" (philonikos) comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos) meaning "friend" and "νίκη" (nike) meaning "victory". 

Grammar: "Φιλονικος" is an adjective in the masculine form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: "Φιλονικος" describes someone who is fond of victory or competition, or someone who is inclined to quarrel or be argumentative. It can also refer to a person who enjoys engaging in disputes or conflicts.
