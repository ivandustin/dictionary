Etymology: The word "φιλοξενίαν" (philozenia) comes from the Greek roots "φίλος" (philos) meaning "friend" or "lover" and "ξένος" (xenos) meaning "stranger" or "guest". It combines these roots to form the concept of "friendliness towards strangers" or "hospitality".

Grammar: "Φιλοξενίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Φιλοξενίαν" refers to the act or practice of showing hospitality or friendliness towards strangers or guests. It encompasses the idea of welcoming and providing care for those who are not familiar or part of one's immediate community. In Greek culture, φιλοξενίαν is highly valued and considered a virtue. It involves offering food, shelter, and kindness to visitors, often going above and beyond to make them feel comfortable and respected.
