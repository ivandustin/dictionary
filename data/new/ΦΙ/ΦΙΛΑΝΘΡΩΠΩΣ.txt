Etymology: The word "φιλανθρωπωπος" (philanthrōpōpos) comes from the Greek roots "φιλος" (philos) meaning "love" and "ανθρωπος" (anthrōpos) meaning "human being". 

Grammar: "Φιλανθρωπωπος" is a Greek adjective in the masculine form. It is declined according to the Greek grammar rules to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

Description: "Φιλανθρωπωπος" is an adjective that describes someone who is philanthropic or benevolent towards humanity. It refers to a person who has a love for humanity and actively seeks to promote the welfare and well-being of others.
