Word: ασπασμούς

Etymology: The word ασπασμούς (as-pas-moos) comes from the Greek verb ασπάζομαι (as-pa-za-mai), which means "to greet" or "to embrace". The suffix -μούς (-moos) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple greetings or embraces.

Grammar: ασπασμούς is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple subjects.

Description: ασπασμούς refers to greetings or embraces, and is often used in the context of social interactions. It can refer to physical embraces, such as hugs or kisses on the cheek, or verbal greetings, such as saying "hello" or "goodbye". The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to expressions of goodwill or affection.
