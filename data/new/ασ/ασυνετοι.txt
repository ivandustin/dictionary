Word: ασυνετοι

Etymology:
The word ασυνετοι (asynetoi) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the adjective "συνετός" (synetos) meaning "intelligent" or "wise". Therefore, ασυνετοι means "not intelligent" or "foolish".

Grammar:
ασυνετοι is a plural adjective in the nominative case, used to describe a group of people who are not intelligent or wise.

Description:
ασυνετοι is a derogatory term used to describe people who lack intelligence or common sense. It can be used to describe someone who is foolish, ignorant, or makes poor decisions. The term is often used in a critical or insulting manner.
