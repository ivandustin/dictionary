Word: ασωτια

Etymology:
The word ασωτια (asotia) comes from the Greek adjective ασωτος (asotos), which means "wasteful" or "prodigal". The suffix -ια (-ia) is added to form a noun, giving the meaning of "wastefulness" or "prodigality".

Grammar:
ασωτια is a feminine noun in Greek, belonging to the first declension. Its nominative singular form is ασωτια, and its genitive singular form is ασωτιας. In the plural, the nominative form is ασωτιες and the genitive form is ασωτιων.

Description:
ασωτια refers to the state or quality of being wasteful or prodigal. It can be used to describe someone who spends money or resources recklessly, without regard for the consequences. It can also be used more broadly to describe any kind of excessive or irresponsible behavior. In Greek literature, the term is often associated with the character of the prodigal son in the Bible, who squandered his inheritance on a life of excess and dissipation.
