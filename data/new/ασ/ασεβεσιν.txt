The word "ασεβεσιν" (asebesin) is a Greek term that can be translated as "impious" or "godless". It is derived from the prefix "a-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the noun "sebeia" (meaning "reverence" or "piety"). In terms of grammar, "ασεβεσιν" is a noun in the accusative plural form. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who lacks respect or reverence for the gods or religious beliefs.
