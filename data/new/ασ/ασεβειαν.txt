The word "ασεβειαν" (asebeian) is a Greek noun that means "impiety" or "irreverence towards the gods." It is derived from the Greek word "asebes," which means "impious" or "godless." In Greek mythology, the gods punished those who showed asebeia, as it was considered a serious offense. 

In terms of grammar, "ασεβειαν" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is also singular, meaning it refers to one instance of impiety. 

Overall, "ασεβειαν" is a word that conveys a sense of disrespect or disregard for the divine, and is often used in a moral or religious context.
