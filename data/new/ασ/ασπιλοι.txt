Word: ασπιλοι

Etymology: The word ασπιλοι comes from the Greek word ασπιλος (aspilos), which means "spotless" or "pure". It is derived from the prefix α- (a-) meaning "not" and the word σπιλος (spilos) meaning "spot" or "blemish".

Grammar: ασπιλοι is a plural adjective in Greek, used to describe a group of people or things that are spotless or pure. It is in the masculine gender and nominative case.

Description: ασπιλοι is a word that is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe people who are morally pure or innocent. It can also be used to describe physical objects that are free from blemishes or imperfections. In modern Greek, it is sometimes used to describe someone who is naive or gullible.
