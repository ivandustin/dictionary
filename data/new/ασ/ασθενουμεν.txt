Word: ασθενουμεν

Etymology: The word ασθενουμεν comes from the Greek verb ασθενέω (asthenéō), which means "to be weak" or "to be sick". The suffix -ουμεν (-oumen) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and describes an ongoing action.

Grammar: ασθενουμεν is a present participle in the active voice, third person singular. It is formed by adding the present participle ending -ουμεν to the verb ασθενέω. The word can function as an adjective, describing a person or thing that is weak or sick.

Description: ασθενουμεν describes someone or something that is currently weak or sick. It can be used to describe a person who is physically or emotionally ill, or a thing that is not functioning properly. The word is often used in medical contexts, such as in discussions of diseases or symptoms.
