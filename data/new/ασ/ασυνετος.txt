Word: ασυνετος

Etymology:
The word ασυνετος comes from the Greek prefix α- (a-), meaning "not," and the adjective συνετος (synetos), meaning "intelligent" or "wise." Therefore, ασυνετος means "unintelligent" or "foolish."

Grammar:
ασυνετος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
ασυνετος is used to describe someone who lacks intelligence or wisdom. It can also be used to describe actions or decisions that are foolish or unwise. In modern Greek, it is often used colloquially to describe someone who is clueless or absent-minded.
