Word: ασχημονειν

Etymology:
The word ασχημονειν (aschemonein) is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and σχημα (schēma) meaning "form" or "shape". The word literally means "to be without form" or "to be shapeless".

Grammar:
ασχημονειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often translated as "to behave unseemly" or "to act indecently".

Description:
ασχημονειν is a Greek word that describes behavior that is inappropriate or indecent. It can refer to actions that are socially unacceptable or morally wrong. The word is often used in a negative context to criticize someone's behavior or actions.
