The word ασπιλον (asplon) is derived from the Greek word ασπιλος (aspilos), which means "unblemished" or "spotless". It is a combination of the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the word σπιλος (spilos), which means "spot" or "blemish".

ασπιλον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive, dative, and vocative.

ασπιλον is used to describe something that is pure, clean, or unblemished.
