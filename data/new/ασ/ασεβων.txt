Word: ασεβων

Etymology:
The word ασεβων (asebōn) is derived from the Greek word ασεβής (asebēs), which means "impious" or "irreverent". It is composed of the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the word σέβομαι (sebomai), which means "to show reverence or respect".

Grammar:
ασεβων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also used as an adjective to describe someone who is impious or irreverent.

Description:
ασεβων is a term used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe someone who is impious or irreverent towards the gods. In ancient Greek society, piety was highly valued, and those who were considered impious were often ostracized or punished. The term is often used in a moral or ethical context, and is associated with the idea of impiety or sacrilege.
