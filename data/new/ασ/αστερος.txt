Word: αστερος

Etymology:
The word αστερος (asteros) comes from the Greek word ἀστήρ (astḗr), which means "star". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂stḗr, which also gave rise to the English word "star".

Grammar:
In Greek, αστερος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also declined in the genitive singular form as αστέρος (asteros) and in the accusative singular form as αστέρα (astera).

Description:
αστερος is a Greek word that means "star". In Greek mythology, the stars were believed to be the souls of the dead, and were often associated with the gods and goddesses. The word αστερος is also used in modern Greek to refer to a celebrity or a star in the entertainment industry.
