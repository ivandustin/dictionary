The word "ασεβειων" (asebeion) is a Greek noun that means "impiety" or "irreverence towards the gods." It is derived from the adjective "ασεβης" (asebes), which means "impious" or "irreverent." In Greek mythology, the gods punished those who showed asebeia, as it was considered a serious offense. In grammar, ασεβειων is a third declension noun, with the genitive form being ασεβειωνος (asebeionos).
