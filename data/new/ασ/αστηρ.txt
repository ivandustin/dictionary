Word: αστηρ

Etymology:
The word αστηρ (astir) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word αστήρ (astér), which means "star". The word is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂stḗr, which also gave rise to the Latin word "stella" and the English word "star".

Grammar:
In Greek, αστηρ is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: ο αστηρ (o astir) - the star
Genitive: του αστηρος (tou astiros) - of the star
Accusative: τον αστηρα (ton astira) - the star

Plural:
Nominative: οι αστερες (oi asteres) - the stars
Genitive: των αστερων (ton asteron) - of the stars
Accusative: τους αστερες (tous asteres) - the stars

Description:
αστηρ is a word that refers to a celestial body that emits light and heat. In Greek mythology, stars were often associated with gods and goddesses, and were believed to have the power to influence human destiny. Today, the word is used in modern Greek to refer to both stars in the sky and to stars in the entertainment industry, such as movie stars and pop stars.
