Word: ασυνετους

Etymology:
The word ασυνετους is derived from the Greek word "ασύνετος" (asynetos), which means "unwise" or "foolish". It is composed of two parts: "α-" which means "not" and "σύνετος" (synetos) which means "wise" or "intelligent".

Grammar:
ασυνετους is an adjective in the masculine accusative plural form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, ασυνετους modifies a masculine noun in the accusative plural form.

Description:
ασυνετους is used to describe someone who is unwise or foolish. It can be used to describe a person's actions or decisions, or to describe their overall character. The word carries a negative connotation and is often used to criticize someone's behavior or choices.
