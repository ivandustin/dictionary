Word: αστρον

Etymology: αστρον (astron) is a Greek word that means "star". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂stḗr, which also gave rise to the English word "star".

Grammar: αστρον is a neuter noun in Greek. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is αστρα (astra).

Description: αστρον is a term used in astronomy to refer to any celestial object that emits or reflects light, such as stars, planets, and galaxies. In ancient Greek mythology, the stars were believed to be the souls of the dead, and the constellations were seen as representations of various gods and heroes. Today, the study of astronomy involves the use of telescopes and other instruments to observe and analyze the properties and behavior of celestial objects.
