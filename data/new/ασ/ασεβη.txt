The word "ασεβη" (asevi) is a Greek adjective that means "impious" or "irreverent". It is derived from the prefix "a-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the noun "σεβη" (sevi), which means "reverence" or "piety". In terms of grammar, it is a feminine singular form of the adjective. It is often used in a religious or moral context to describe someone who lacks respect or reverence for the gods or for moral principles.
