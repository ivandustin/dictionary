In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The imperative mood is used to give a command or instruction.

Description: ασφαλισασθε is a command to a group of people to secure themselves or take precautions. It can be translated as "Be secure!" or "Take precautions!"
