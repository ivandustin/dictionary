Word: αστερας

Etymology:
The word αστερας (asteras) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word αστήρ (aster), which means "star". The suffix -ας (-as) is added to the end of the word to indicate a masculine noun.

Grammar:
In Greek, αστερας is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension, which means that its ending changes depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. For example, in the nominative case, it is αστερας, but in the accusative case, it becomes αστέρα (astera).

Description:
αστερας refers to a star, which is a luminous celestial body that is visible in the night sky. In Greek mythology, stars were often associated with gods and goddesses, and were believed to have special powers and meanings. Today, stars are studied by astronomers to better understand the universe and its origins.
