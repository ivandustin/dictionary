Word: αστειον

Etymology:
The word αστειον (asteion) comes from the Greek adjective αστειος (asteios), which means "funny" or "amusing". It is derived from the noun αστυ (asty), which means "city" or "town".

Grammar:
αστειον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The genitive form of αστειον is αστειου.

Description:
αστειον refers to something that is funny or amusing. It can be used to describe a joke, a story, a situation, or a person's behavior. The word is often used in a positive sense, to express enjoyment or appreciation of something that is humorous or entertaining.
