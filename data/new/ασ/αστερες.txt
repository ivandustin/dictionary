Word: αστέρες

Etymology: The word αστέρες (astéres) comes from the Ancient Greek word ἀστήρ (astḗr), which means "star". 

Grammar: αστέρες is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "stars". It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. 

Description: αστέρες are celestial bodies that emit light and heat due to nuclear reactions in their cores. They are found throughout the universe and are often used for navigation and as symbols in mythology and religion. In Greek mythology, the stars were believed to be the souls of heroes and gods who had been placed in the sky by the gods. Today, stars are studied by astronomers to learn more about the universe and its origins.
