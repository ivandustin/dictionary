Word: ασθενησας

Etymology:
The word ασθενησας (asthenesas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ασθενής (asthenes), which means "weak" or "sick." The verb form means "to become weak" or "to become sick."

Grammar:
ασθενησας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is in the past tense and indicates that the action of becoming weak or sick has already happened.

Description:
ασθενησας is a word that describes a state of weakness or sickness. It can be used to describe a physical illness or a general feeling of weakness. In the context of ancient Greek literature, it is often used to describe the physical or emotional state of a character.
