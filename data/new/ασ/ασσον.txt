The word "ασσον" (asson) is a Greek adverb that means "less" or "not so much". 

Etymology: The origin of the word is uncertain, but it is believed to be derived from the ancient Greek word "ἄσσον" (asson), which means "less" or "smaller". 

Grammar: "Ασσον" is an adverb that modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It does not change form based on gender, number, or case. 

Description: "Ασσον" is a common word in modern Greek and is used in everyday speech. It is often used to express a comparison between two things, indicating that one is less than the other. For example, "Το βιβλίο αυτό είναι ασσον ενδιαφέρον από το προηγούμενο" (This book is less interesting than the previous one).
