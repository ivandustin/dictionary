The word "αστραπαι" (astrapai) is a plural noun in Greek that means "lightning bolts". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "αστραπή" (astrapē), which means "lightning". 

Grammar: "Αστραπαι" is the plural form of the noun, with the singular form being "αστραπή". It is a feminine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Αστραπαι" refers to the bolts of lightning that are seen during a thunderstorm. In Greek mythology, Zeus, the god of thunder and lightning, was often depicted holding a lightning bolt. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is sudden and powerful, like a bolt of lightning.
