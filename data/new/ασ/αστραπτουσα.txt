Word: αστραπτουσα

Etymology: αστραπτουσα is the feminine form of the Greek verb αστράπτω (astrápto), which means "to flash" or "to shine brightly". It comes from the noun αστραπή (astrapí), which means "lightning".

Grammar: αστραπτουσα is a participle in the present tense, active voice, and feminine gender. It is derived from the verb αστράπτω and is used to describe a female subject who is currently flashing or shining brightly.

Description: αστραπτουσα can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a bright star in the sky, a flashing light, or even a person who is radiating with energy or excitement. It is a vivid and descriptive word that conveys a sense of brightness and intensity.
