Word: ασπασμος

Etymology:
The word ασπασμος (as-pas-mos) comes from the Greek verb ασπαζομαι (as-pa-zo-mai), which means "to greet" or "to welcome". The noun ασπασμος refers to the act of greeting or welcoming someone.

ασπασμος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes ασπασμοι (as-pa-zmoi). It is a second declension noun, which means that its stem ends in -ος (os) and its genitive form ends in -ου (ou).

Description:
ασπασμος is a term used to describe the act of greeting or welcoming someone. It can refer to a physical gesture, such as a handshake or a hug, or to verbal expressions of welcome, such as saying "hello" or "welcome". In ancient Greek culture, the act of greeting was considered very important, and it was often accompanied by rituals and ceremonies.
