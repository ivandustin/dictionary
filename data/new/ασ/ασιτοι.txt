Word: ασιτοι

Etymology:
The word ασιτοι (asitoi) is derived from the Greek word "ασιτία" (asitia), which means "fasting" or "abstinence from food". The suffix "-οι" (-oi) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of people.

Grammar:
ασιτοι (asitoi) is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word can be translated to "fasters" or "those who abstain from food".

Description:
ασιτοι (asitoi) refers to people who practice fasting or abstinence from food. In Greek Orthodox Christianity, fasting is a common practice, especially during Lent and other religious holidays. Fasting is also practiced in other religions and cultures as a form of spiritual discipline or as a way to purify the body.
