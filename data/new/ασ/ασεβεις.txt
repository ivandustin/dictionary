Word: ασεβεις

Etymology:
The word ασεβεις (aseveis) is derived from the Greek word ασεβής (asevēs), which means "impious" or "irreverent". It is composed of the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the word σέβομαι (sevomai), which means "to show reverence" or "to honor".

Grammar:
ασεβεις is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe people who are impious or irreverent.

Description:
ασεβεις is a word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe people who do not show proper respect or reverence towards God. It is used to describe those who are disobedient to God's laws and who do not acknowledge His authority.
