The word "αστραπην" (astrapen) is a Greek noun that means "lightning". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek word "αστραπή" (astrapé), which also means "lightning". 

Grammar: "Αστραπην" is the accusative singular form of the noun. 

Description: Lightning is a natural electrical discharge that occurs during thunderstorms. It is characterized by a bright flash of light and a loud thunderclap. Lightning can be dangerous and cause damage to buildings and other structures. In Greek mythology, lightning was associated with the god Zeus, who was said to wield thunderbolts as weapons.
