Etymology: The word "ὁλιγοῦ" (oligou) comes from the Greek adjective "ὀλίγος" (oligos), meaning "few" or "little."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁλιγοῦ" is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is used to indicate a small or limited quantity of something.

Description: "ὁλιγοῦ" is often used in Greek to express a small amount or a scarcity of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a small number of people, a limited amount of time, or a small quantity of a substance.
