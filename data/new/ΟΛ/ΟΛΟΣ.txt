Etymology: The word "ολος" (olos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ólos meaning "whole" or "entire".

Grammar: In Greek, "ολος" (olos) is an adjective. It is used to describe something that is complete, whole, or entire. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ολος" (olos) is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can describe a physical object that is complete or whole, such as "ολο το σπίτι" (olo to spiti) meaning "the whole house". It can also describe a person's character or personality, such as "ολος ο άνθρωπος είναι καλός" (olos o anthropos einai kalos) meaning "the person is entirely good". Additionally, it can be used to express a sense of entirety or completeness, such as "ολοι οι φίλοι μου" (oloi oi filoi mou) meaning "all of my friends".
