Etymology: The word "ολιγοπιστε" comes from the Greek words "ολίγος" (oligos), meaning "few" or "little," and "πιστεύω" (pistevo), meaning "believe." 

Grammar: "Ολιγοπιστε" is a verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 
- Εγώ ολιγοπιστώ (ego oligopisto) - I believe little
- Εσύ ολιγοπιστείς (esi oligopisteis) - You believe little
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό ολιγοπιστεί (aftos/afti/afto oligopistei) - He/She/It believes little
- Εμείς ολιγοπιστούμε (emeis oligopistoume) - We believe little
- Εσείς ολιγοπιστείτε (eseis oligopisteite) - You believe little
- Αυτοί/Αυτές ολιγοπιστούν (aftoi/aftes oligopistoun) - They believe little

Description: "Ολιγοπιστε" is a Greek verb that means "to believe little" or "to have little faith." It describes a state of having a lack of trust or confidence in something or someone.
