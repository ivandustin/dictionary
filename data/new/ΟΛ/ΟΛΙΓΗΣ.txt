Etymology: The word "ὁλιγης" (oligēs) comes from the Greek root "ὀλίγος" (oligos), meaning "few" or "little."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁλιγης" (oligēs) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (ὀλίγου), dative (ὀλίγῳ), and accusative (ὀλίγην).

Description: "ὁλιγης" (oligēs) refers to something or someone that is small in quantity, limited, or scarce. It can be used to describe a small number of people, a small amount of something, or a short duration of time.
