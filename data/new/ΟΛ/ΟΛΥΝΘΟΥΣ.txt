Etymology: The word "ὀλυνθοῦς" (olynthous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ὀλύνω" (olyno), which means "to destroy" or "to ruin."

Grammar: "ὀλυνθοῦς" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word.

Description: "ὀλυνθοῦς" refers to someone who is in a state of ruin or destruction. It can be used to describe a person who has experienced a downfall or has been completely ruined.
