Etymology: The word "ολοκληρον" comes from the Greek word "ολοκληρος" (olokleros), meaning "whole" or "complete".

Grammar: "Ολοκληρον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is the accusative singular form of the word.

Description: "Ολοκληρον" is used to refer to something that is complete or whole. It can be used to describe a finished or fully formed entity.
