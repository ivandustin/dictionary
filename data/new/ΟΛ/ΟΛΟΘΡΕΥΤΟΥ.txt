Etymology: The word "ὁλοθρευτοῦ" (olothreutou) comes from the Greek root words "ὅλος" (holos) meaning "whole" or "entire" and "θρεύτης" (threutes) meaning "destroyer" or "annihilator". 

Grammar: "ὁλοθρευτοῦ" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "ὁλοθρευτοῦ" refers to someone or something that is a complete or total destroyer. It describes a person or entity that causes complete destruction or annihilation.
