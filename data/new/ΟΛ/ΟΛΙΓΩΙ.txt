Etymology: The word "ὁλιγωρία" (oligōria) comes from the Greek roots "ὀλίγος" (oligos), meaning "few" or "little," and "ἔργον" (ergon), meaning "work" or "action." 

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁλιγωρία" is a noun of feminine gender. It is declined as follows: singular - ἡ ὁλιγωρία (hē oligōria), plural - αἱ ὁλιγωρίαι (hai oligōriai).

Description: "ὁλιγωρία" refers to the act of neglecting or disregarding something, often due to a lack of interest or care. It can also imply a dismissive attitude or indifference towards a matter.
