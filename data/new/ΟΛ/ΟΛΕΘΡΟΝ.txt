Etymology: The word "ὁλέθρον" (olethron) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ὄλλυμι" (ollumi), which means "to destroy" or "to ruin."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁλέθρον" is a noun of the neuter gender. It is in the singular form.

Description: "ὁλέθρον" refers to destruction, ruin, or devastation. It can be used to describe the complete and irreversible destruction of something or someone.
