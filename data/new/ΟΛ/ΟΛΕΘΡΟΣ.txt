Etymology: The word "ὁλέθρος" (olethros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ὄλλυμι" (ollumi), which means "to destroy" or "to ruin."

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁλέθρος" is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "ὁλέθρος." The genitive singular form is "ὁλέθρου" (olethrou), the dative singular form is "ὁλέθρῳ" (olethro), and the accusative singular form is "ὁλέθρον" (olethron).

Description: "ὁλέθρος" refers to destruction, ruin, or devastation. It can be used to describe the complete and irreversible destruction of something or someone. The word often carries a negative connotation and is associated with catastrophic events or outcomes.
