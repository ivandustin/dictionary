Etymology: The word "ὁλιγος" (oligos) comes from the Greek language and means "few" or "little". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ol- meaning "small" or "few".

Grammar: In Greek, "ὁλιγος" (oligos) is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "ὁλιγος" (oligos) is used to describe something that is small in quantity, size, or extent. It can refer to a small number of people or things, a short duration of time, or a limited amount of something. It is often used to express the idea of scarcity or insufficiency.
