Etymology: The word "δούλη" (douli) comes from the Greek word "δοῦλος" (doulos), which means "slave" or "servant". 

Grammar: "Δούλη" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its nominative singular form is "δούλη". 

Description: "Δούλη" is a term used to refer to a female slave or servant. In ancient Greece, slavery was a common practice, and slaves were considered the property of their owners.
