Etymology: The word "δουλαγωγώ" comes from the Greek roots "δούλος" (doulos) meaning "slave" and "ἄγω" (ago) meaning "to lead" or "to bring". It literally translates to "to lead as a slave" or "to bring into slavery".

Grammar: "δουλαγωγώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Εγώ δουλαγωγώ (ego doulagogo) - I lead into slavery
- Εσύ δουλαγωγείς (esi doulagogis) - You lead into slavery
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό δουλαγωγεί (aftos/afti/afto doulagogi) - He/She/It leads into slavery
- Εμείς δουλαγωγούμε (emeis doulagogoume) - We lead into slavery
- Εσείς δουλαγωγείτε (eseis doulagogite) - You lead into slavery
- Αυτοί/Αυτές δουλαγωγούν (aftoi/aftes doulagogoun) - They lead into slavery

Description: "δουλαγωγώ" is a verb that describes the action of leading or bringing someone into slavery. It implies the act of subjugating and controlling another person against their will. The word carries a negative connotation and is often used metaphorically to describe situations where someone is being manipulated or oppressed.
