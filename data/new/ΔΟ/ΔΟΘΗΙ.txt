Etymology: The word "δοθηι" (dothēi) comes from the Greek verb "δίδωμι" (dídōmi), which means "to give."

Grammar: "Δοθηι" is the third person singular aorist passive subjunctive form of the verb "δίδωμι" (dídōmi). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The subjunctive mood expresses possibility, potentiality, or doubt.

Description: The word "δοθηι" (dothēi) is used to express a hypothetical or potential action in the past. It suggests that something might have been given or could have been given, but it is uncertain or not confirmed.
