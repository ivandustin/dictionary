Etymology: The word "δογμα" (dogma) comes from the Greek word "δοκέω" (dokeo), which means "to seem" or "to think." It originally referred to a belief or opinion that was considered authoritative or binding.

Grammar: In Greek, "δογμα" is a neuter noun. Its plural form is "δογματα" (dogmata).

Description: "Δογμα" (dogma) refers to a principle or set of principles that are considered to be true or authoritative within a particular system of beliefs or ideology. It is often used to describe a doctrine or tenet that is accepted without question or doubt. In religious contexts, dogmas are often considered to be fundamental truths that are essential to the faith. However, the term can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any firmly established belief or principle.
