Etymology: The word "δουλεύει" comes from the Greek verb "δουλεύω" (doulevo), which means "to work" or "to labor." It is derived from the noun "δοῦλος" (doulos), meaning "slave" or "servant."

Grammar: "Δουλεύει" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "δουλεύω" (doulevo). In Greek, verbs change their endings based on the subject and tense. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time.

Description: "Δουλεύει" means "he/she/it works" in English. It is used to describe the action of someone or something performing work or labor.
