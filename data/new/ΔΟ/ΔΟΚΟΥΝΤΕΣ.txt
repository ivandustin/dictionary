Etymology: The word "δοκούντες" (dokountes) is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It comes from the verb "δοκέω" (dokeo), which means "to think" or "to seem."

Grammar: "Δοκούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "δοκέω" (dokeo) in Greek. It is used to describe ongoing actions or states. In English, it can be translated as "thinking" or "seeming."

Description: "Δοκούντες" is a word that describes the action of thinking or seeming. It can be used to indicate that someone is currently thinking about something or that something appears to be a certain way.
