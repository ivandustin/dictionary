Etymology: The word "δοκείν" (dokein) comes from the Greek verb "δοκέω" (dokeo), which means "to seem" or "to appear."

Grammar: "Δοκείν" is the present infinitive form of the verb "δοκέω" (dokeo). In Greek grammar, the infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb in a general or abstract sense, without specifying a subject or tense.

Description: "Δοκείν" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of something appearing or seeming to be a certain way. It is often used to express opinions, beliefs, or judgments. The verb can be used in various tenses and moods to indicate different nuances of meaning.
