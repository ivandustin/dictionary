Grammar: "δογματων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "δόγμα" (dogma) in Greek. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "δογματων" refers to multiple dogmas or doctrines. A dogma is a principle or belief that is accepted as true and authoritative, especially in a religious or philosophical context. It is a statement or set of statements that are considered to be unquestionably true and binding within a particular system of thought or belief.
