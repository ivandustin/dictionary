Etymology: The word "δουλευουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "δουλεύω" (douleuo), which means "to work" or "to serve." It is derived from the noun "δοῦλος" (doulos), meaning "slave" or "servant."

Grammar: "δουλευουσιν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "δουλεύω" (douleuo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject and tense. The "-ουσιν" ending indicates that the subject is third person plural (they) and the action is happening in the present.

Description: "δουλευουσιν" is a Greek verb that means "they work" or "they serve." It is used to describe the action of multiple people working or serving in the present tense.
