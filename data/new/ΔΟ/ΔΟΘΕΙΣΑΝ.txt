Etymology: The word "δοθεισαν" comes from the Greek verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), which means "to give". It is in the aorist tense, passive voice, and feminine gender.

Grammar: "δοθεισαν" is the third person singular form of the aorist passive participle of the verb "δίδωμι" (didomi). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is receiving the action.

Description: "δοθεισαν" is used to describe a feminine noun or pronoun that has received the action of giving. It can be translated as "given" or "having been given" in English.
