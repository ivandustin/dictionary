Etymology: The word "δουλας" (doulas) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "δουλος" (doulos), which means "slave" or "servant".

Grammar: In Greek, "δουλας" (doulas) is a noun. It is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in different cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Δουλας" (doulas) refers to a person who serves or assists others. In ancient Greece, it specifically referred to a slave or servant. However, in modern usage, it can also refer to a person who provides support and assistance during childbirth.
