Etymology: The word "δουλευειν" (douleuein) comes from the Greek verb "δουλεύω" (douleuo), which means "to work" or "to serve." It is derived from the noun "δοῦλος" (doulos), meaning "slave" or "servant."

Grammar: "δουλευειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb "δουλεύω" in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation class of verbs, which have the ending "-ευω" in the present tense.

Description: "δουλευειν" is a Greek verb that means "to work" or "to serve." It is used to describe the action of engaging in labor or providing service to someone or something. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as referring to employment, performing tasks, or fulfilling obligations.
