Etymology: The word "δοκούσα" (dokousa) comes from the Greek verb "δοκώ" (doko), which means "to think" or "to believe." It is conjugated in the first person singular, past continuous tense.

Grammar: "Δοκούσα" is a verb form in the Greek language. It belongs to the past continuous tense, which is used to describe an ongoing action in the past. In this case, it specifically refers to the first person singular form.

Description: "Δοκούσα" is used to express an ongoing action or state in the past. It indicates that the speaker was thinking or believing something at a specific point in the past.
