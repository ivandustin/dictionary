Etymology: The word "δουλευοντες" comes from the Greek verb "δουλεύω" (douleuo), which means "to work" or "to serve". It is derived from the noun "δοῦλος" (doulos), meaning "slave" or "servant".

Grammar: "δουλευοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "δουλεύω" (douleuo) in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action of working or serving. In this form, it agrees with the subject in gender, number, and case.

Description: "δουλευοντες" is a Greek word that means "working" or "serving". It is often used to describe individuals who are engaged in labor or providing service. The word can be used in various contexts, such as referring to people who work in different professions or those who serve others in a specific capacity.
