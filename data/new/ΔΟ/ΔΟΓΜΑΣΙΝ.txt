Etymology: The word "δογμασιν" comes from the Greek word "δόγμα" (dogma), which means "opinion" or "belief". It is derived from the verb "δοκέω" (dokeo), meaning "to seem" or "to think".

Grammar: "δογμασιν" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple "dogmas" being referred to.

Description: "δογμασιν" refers to a collection or set of dogmas. Dogmas are authoritative beliefs or doctrines that are accepted as true and binding within a particular religious, philosophical, or ideological system.
