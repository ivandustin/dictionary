Etymology: The word "λεπρούς" (leproús) comes from the Greek word "λέπρα" (lépra), which means "leprosy" or "scaly disease." 

Grammar: "Λεπρούς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. 

Description: "Λεπρούς" refers to a person who has leprosy, a chronic infectious disease that affects the skin, nerves, and mucous membranes. In ancient times, leprosy was considered a highly contagious and incurable disease, leading to social isolation and stigmatization of those affected.
