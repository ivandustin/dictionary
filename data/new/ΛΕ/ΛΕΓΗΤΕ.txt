Etymology: The word "λεγητε" (pronounced "legete") comes from the Greek verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak".

Grammar: "Λεγητε" is the second person plural form of the verb "λέγω" (lego) in the present tense. It is used when addressing a group of people and means "you all say" or "you all speak".

Description: "Λεγητε" is a command or imperative form of the verb "λέγω" (lego). It is used to give an instruction or order to a group of people, telling them to say or speak something.
