Etymology: The word "λελαληται" comes from the Greek verb "λαλέω" (laleo), which means "to talk" or "to speak". The prefix "λε-" (le-) indicates a perfect tense, indicating that the action of speaking has been completed.

Grammar: "Λελαληται" is the third person singular form of the verb "λαλέω" in the perfect tense. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance in the present.

Description: "Λελαληται" can be translated as "he/she/it has spoken" or "he/she/it has talked". It implies that someone has spoken or talked in the past, and the effects or consequences of that action continue to be relevant in the present.
