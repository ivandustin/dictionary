Etymology: The word "λελούμενοι" (leloumenoi) comes from the Greek verb "λούω" (louo), which means "to wash." The prefix "λε-" (le-) indicates a completed action, so "λελούμενοι" can be translated as "having been washed."

Grammar: "Λελούμενοι" is the present participle form of the verb "λούω" (louo) in the passive voice. It is used to describe a group of people who have been washed or who are in the state of being washed.

Description: "Λελούμενοι" is a Greek word that refers to individuals who have undergone the action of being washed. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing people who have just taken a bath or individuals who have been cleansed in a symbolic or spiritual sense.
