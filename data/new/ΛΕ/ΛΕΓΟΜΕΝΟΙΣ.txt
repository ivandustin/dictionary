Etymology: The word "λεγομενοις" (legomenois) comes from the Greek verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The suffix "-μενοις" (-menois) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to something currently being said or spoken.

Grammar: "Λεγομενοις" is the plural form of the present participle of the verb "λέγω" (lego). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective, noun, or verb.

Description: "Λεγομενοις" can be translated as "those who are being said" or "those who are being spoken". It refers to a group of people or things that are currently being talked about or mentioned. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in literature, discussions, or general conversations.
