Etymology: The word "λεγειν" (legein) comes from the Greek language and means "to speak" or "to say".

Grammar: "Λεγειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the first conjugation and is classified as a regular verb.

Description: "Λεγειν" is a versatile verb that is used to express the act of speaking or saying something. It can be used in various contexts and tenses to convey different meanings and nuances.
