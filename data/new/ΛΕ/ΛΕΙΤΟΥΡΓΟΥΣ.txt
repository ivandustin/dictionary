Etymology: The word "λειτουργούς" (leitourgous) comes from the Greek root words "λεῖτος" (leitos), meaning "public" or "common," and "ἔργον" (ergon), meaning "work" or "service." 

Grammar: "Λειτουργούς" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. 

Description: "Λειτουργούς" refers to a person who performs a public duty or service. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe someone who served the state or community in a specific role, such as a priest, magistrate, or public official. The term is still used in modern Greek to refer to someone who carries out a public function or service.
