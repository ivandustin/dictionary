Etymology: The word "λειτουργοί" (leitourgoi) comes from the Greek word "λειτουργία" (leitourgia), which means "public service" or "ritual service". It is derived from the combination of "λαός" (laos), meaning "people", and "ἔργον" (ergon), meaning "work".

Grammar: "Λειτουργοί" is the plural form of the noun "λειτουργός" (leitourgos) in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is of masculine gender. In the singular form, it is declined as "ο λειτουργός" (o leitourgos), while in the plural form, it is declined as "οι λειτουργοί" (oi leitourgoi).

Description: "Λειτουργοί" refers to a group of people who perform a public or ritual service. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe individuals who carried out religious ceremonies or public duties on behalf of the community. In modern usage, it can also refer to individuals who serve in specific roles or professions, such as priests, ministers, or government officials.
