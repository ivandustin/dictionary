Etymology: The word "λεγουσης" (legousēs) comes from the Greek verb "λέγω" (legō), which means "to say" or "to speak".

Grammar: "Λεγουσης" is the genitive singular form of the noun "λεγουσα" (legousa), which is the feminine present participle of the verb "λέγω" (legō). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Λεγουσης" can be translated as "of the one speaking" or "of the one saying" in English. It is used to indicate possession or attribution to a female subject who is speaking or saying something.
