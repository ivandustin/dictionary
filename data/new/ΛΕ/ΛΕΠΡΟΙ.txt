Etymology: The word "λεπροί" (leproi) comes from the Greek word "λέπρα" (lepra), which means "leprosy". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *lep- meaning "to peel" or "to scale off".

Grammar: "Λεπροί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. "Λεπροί" is the masculine plural form of the noun "λεπρός" (lepros), which means "leper".

Description: "Λεπροί" refers to a group of people who suffer from leprosy, a chronic infectious disease that affects the skin, nerves, and mucous membranes. Leprosy is characterized by the formation of skin lesions, loss of sensation, and deformities. Historically, lepers were often isolated from society due to the fear of contagion.
