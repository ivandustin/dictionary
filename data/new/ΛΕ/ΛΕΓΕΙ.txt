Etymology: The word "λεγει" (pronounced "legei") comes from the Greek verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak".

Grammar: "Λεγει" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "λέγω" (lego). It is used to indicate that someone or something is saying or speaking in the present.

Description: "Λεγει" is a word in the Greek language that means "he/she/it says" or "he/she/it speaks". It is commonly used in sentences to attribute speech or statements to a third person singular subject.
