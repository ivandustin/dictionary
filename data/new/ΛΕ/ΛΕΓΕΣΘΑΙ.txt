Etymology: The word "λεγεσθαι" (pronounced le-ge-sthai) comes from the Greek verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg-, meaning "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: "λεγεσθαι" is the present passive infinitive form of the verb "λέγω" (lego). In Greek grammar, the infinitive is a non-finite verb form that expresses the action of the verb without specifying any subject or tense. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "λεγεσθαι" can be translated as "to be said" or "to be spoken". It is used to express the passive voice of the verb "λέγω" (lego), indicating that something is being said or spoken by someone or something.
