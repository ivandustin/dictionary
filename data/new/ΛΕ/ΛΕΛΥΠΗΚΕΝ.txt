Etymology: The word "λελυπηκεν" comes from the Greek verb "λυπέω" (lypéo), which means "to grieve" or "to cause sorrow." The word is in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "Λελυπηκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "λυπέω" in the perfect tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The perfect tense is used to describe a past action with present relevance.

Description: "Λελυπηκεν" can be translated as "he/she/it has grieved" or "he/she/it has caused sorrow." It indicates that someone or something has experienced or caused sadness or sorrow in the past, with the effects still being felt in the present.
