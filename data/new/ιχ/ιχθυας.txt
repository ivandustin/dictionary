Word: ιχθυας

Etymology:
The word ιχθυας (ichthuas) is derived from the Greek word ιχθύς (ichthys), which means "fish". The -ας (-as) suffix is a common Greek ending used to form masculine nouns.

Grammar:
Ιχθυας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
Ιχθυας refers to a fish or fishes in general. It can be used to describe any type of fish, whether it is a specific species or not. The word is commonly used in the context of fishing, cooking, and marine biology.
