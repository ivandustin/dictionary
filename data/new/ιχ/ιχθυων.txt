Word: ιχθυων

Etymology:
The word ιχθυων (ichthyon) is derived from the Greek word ιχθύς (ichthys), which means "fish". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns.

Grammar:
Ιχθυων is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
Ιχθυων refers to a group of fish. It can be used to describe a collection of fish in a specific location, such as a school of fish in the ocean. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to fish as a food source or as a group of animals that live in water.
