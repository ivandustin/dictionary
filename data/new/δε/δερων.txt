Word: δερων

Etymology:
The word δερων comes from the Greek verb δέρω (dero), which means "to skin" or "to flay". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *der- meaning "to split" or "to tear".

Grammar:
δερων is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
δερων is not a commonly used word in modern Greek. It appears in the New Testament in the phrase "ὁ δερων τὸν λύκον" (ho deron ton lykon), which means "the one who skins the wolf". It is used metaphorically to refer to someone who is brave and fearless.
