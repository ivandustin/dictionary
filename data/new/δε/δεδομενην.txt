Word: δεδομένην

Etymology:
The word δεδομένην is the feminine form of the Greek verb δίδωμι (didomi), which means "to give". The prefix δε- (de-) indicates that the action is completed or has been done. Therefore, δεδομένην means "having been given" or "granted".

Grammar:
δεδομένην is a participle in the perfect tense, passive voice, and feminine gender. It is declined in the accusative case, singular number, and agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description:
δεδομένην is used to describe a feminine noun that has been given or granted something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in legal documents, contracts, or agreements. For example, "Η δεδομένην άδεια επιτρέπει την είσοδο στο κτίριο" (The granted permission allows entry into the building).
