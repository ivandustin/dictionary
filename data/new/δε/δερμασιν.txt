Word: δερμασιν

Etymology: The word δερμασιν comes from the Greek word δέρμα (derma), which means "skin". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: δερμασιν is an adjective in the feminine gender, singular number, and accusative case. It is used to describe a noun in the accusative case.

Description: δερμασιν means "made of skin" or "skin-like". It is often used to describe objects or materials that have a texture or appearance similar to that of skin. For example, δερμασιν can be used to describe leather, animal hides, or even certain types of fabrics that have a skin-like texture.
