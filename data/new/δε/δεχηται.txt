Word: δεχηται

Etymology:
The word δεχηται (dechetai) is a present tense form of the Greek verb δέχομαι (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". The verb is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deḱ-, which means "to take" or "to accept".

Grammar:
δεχηται is a present tense form of the Greek verb δέχομαι (dechomai), which is a second conjugation verb. It is in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a singular entity that is receiving or accepting something.

Description:
δεχηται is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it receives" or "he/she/it accepts". It is commonly used in everyday conversation and in written texts. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as receiving a gift, accepting an invitation, or welcoming someone.
