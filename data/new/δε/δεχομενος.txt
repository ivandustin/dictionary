Word: δεχόμενος

Etymology:
The word δεχόμενος (dechomenos) is a present participle of the Greek verb δέχομαι (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". The verb is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deḱ- ("to take, accept").

Grammar:
δεχόμενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb δέχομαι (dechomai), which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense.

Description:
The word δεχόμενος (dechomenos) is used to describe someone who is currently receiving or accepting something. It can be translated as "accepting", "receiving", or "taking". The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Ο δεχόμενος ευχαριστεί τον δωρητή" (The recipient thanks the donor).
