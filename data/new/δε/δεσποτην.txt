Word: δεσπότην

Etymology:
The word δεσπότην (despotin) comes from the Greek word δεσπότης (despotes), which means "master" or "lord". The word originally referred to a ruler or monarch, but later came to be used more broadly to refer to any person who had absolute power or control over others.

Grammar:
δεσπότην is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the noun that is directly affected by the action of the verb.

Description:
δεσπότην is a Greek word that refers to a despot or tyrant, someone who has absolute power and control over others. The word has a negative connotation, as it implies a ruler who is cruel, oppressive, and arbitrary in their exercise of power. In modern usage, the word is often used to describe dictators or authoritarian leaders who rule with an iron fist.
