Word: δερβαιος

Etymology:
The word δερβαιος (derbaios) is derived from the Greek word δέρβη (derbe), which means "oak tree". It is believed that the word was used to describe people who lived near or in oak forests.

Grammar:
δερβαιος is an adjective in the masculine gender and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, and accusative, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
δερβαιος is an ancient Greek word that was used to describe people who lived near or in oak forests. It can also be used to describe someone who is strong, sturdy, or resilient, like an oak tree. In modern Greek, the word is rarely used, but it can still be found in some place names or surnames.
