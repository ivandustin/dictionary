Word: δελεαζοντες

Etymology: δελεαζοντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δελεάζω (deleazo), which means "to entice" or "to lure". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the action is ongoing.

Grammar: δελεαζοντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb δελεάζω (deleazo), which means "to entice" or "to lure". As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action, and as an active verb, it indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: δελεαζοντες can be translated to "enticing" or "luring". It describes a group of people who are actively trying to entice or lure someone or something. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning and connotation.
