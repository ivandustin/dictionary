The word "δεησεσιν" (deēsesin) is a noun in the dative plural form in Ancient Greek. It is derived from the verb "δέομαι" (deomai), which means "to lack, need, want, desire, ask for". The noun form refers to a request, need, or supplication. 

In terms of grammar, "δεησεσιν" is a third declension noun, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. The stem of the word is "δεησε-", and the endings "-σιν" indicate that it is in the dative plural form. 

Overall, "δεησεσιν" is a word that refers to requests or needs, and is used in the context of supplication or asking for something.
