Word: δελεαζομενος

Etymology: δελεαζομενος is a present participle of the Greek verb δελεάζω (deleázō), which means "to entice" or "to lure". The word comes from the root δελεασ- (deleas-), which means "bait" or "lure".

Grammar: δελεαζομενος is a present participle in the passive voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb δελεάζω (deleázō), which is in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.

Description: δελεαζομενος describes someone who is being enticed or lured. It can also be translated as "tempted" or "seduced". The word is often used in a negative context, such as being tempted by sin or evil.
