Word: δεκατη

Etymology:
The word δεκατη (dekate) is derived from the Greek word δέκα (deka) which means "ten". It is a feminine noun in the Greek language.

In Greek, δεκατη is a singular noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases such as genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative.

Description:
δεκατη is a term used in Greek to refer to the tenth day of the month. It is commonly used in the context of the Greek Orthodox Church, where it is used to refer to the day of the month on which certain religious observances are held.
