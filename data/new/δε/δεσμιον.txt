Word: δεσμίον

Etymology:
The word δεσμίον (desmion) comes from the Greek word δεσμός (desmos), which means "bond" or "tie." It is derived from the verb δέω (deo), which means "to bind" or "to tie."

δεσμίον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In the nominative case, it is spelled δεσμίον. In the genitive case, it is spelled δεσμίου. In the accusative case, it is spelled δεσμίον.

Description:
δεσμίον refers to a prisoner or captive who is bound or tied up. In ancient Greece, prisoners of war were often taken as δεσμίον and were kept in chains or other forms of restraints.
