Word: δεικνυμι

Etymology:
The word δεικνυμι (deiknumi) is a Greek verb that means "to show" or "to point out". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, which means "to show" or "to point".

Grammar:
δεικνυμι is a present active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ δείχνω (ego deichno) - I show
- Συ δείχνεις (si deichneis) - You show (singular)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό δείχνει (aftos/afti/afto deichnei) - He/She/It shows

Plural:
- Εμείς δείχνουμε (emeis deichnoume) - We show
- Εσείς δείχνετε (eseis deichnete) - You show (plural)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές δείχνουν (aftoi/aftes deichnoun) - They show

Description:
δεικνυμι is a common Greek verb that is used to indicate the act of showing or pointing out something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in everyday conversation, in academic writing, or in literature. The verb is often used in combination with other words to create compound verbs, such as δεικνύω τον δρόμο (deiknyo ton dromo) - to show the way, or δείχνω το δάχτυλό μου (deichno to dachtilo mou) - to point my finger. Overall, δεικνυμι is an important verb in the Greek language that is used frequently in both spoken and written communication.
