Word: δεσμιοι

Etymology:
The word δεσμιοι (desmioi) is derived from the Greek word δεσμός (desmos), which means "bond" or "tie." It is related to the verb δεσμεύω (desmevo), which means "to bind" or "to tie up."

Grammar:
δεσμιοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word is masculine, which means it refers to male individuals or groups.

Description:
δεσμιοι refers to prisoners or captives who are bound or tied up. In ancient Greece, prisoners of war were often taken as slaves and forced to work for their captors. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is trapped or confined in some way, such as being stuck in a job they don't like or a bad relationship.
