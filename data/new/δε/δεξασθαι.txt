Word: δεξασθαι

Etymology: δεξασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δέχομαι (dekhomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept."

Grammar: δεξασθαι is a second aorist middle infinitive form of the verb δέχομαι (dekhomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: δεξασθαι can be translated as "to receive" or "to accept." It is often used in the context of receiving or accepting something from someone else, such as a gift or a message. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the act of receiving the Holy Spirit or the word of God.
