Word: δευτερας

Etymology:
The word δευτερας (deuteras) is derived from the Greek word δεύτερος (deuteros), which means "second" or "next in order". It is a derivative of the word δύο (dyo), which means "two".

Grammar:
δευτερας is a feminine noun in the genitive singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description:
δευτερας is often used in the context of the Christian liturgical calendar, where it refers to the second day of the week, which is Monday. It can also be used more generally to refer to the second day of any given period of time.
