Etymology: δεσμεύω (desmeúō) is a Greek verb meaning "to bind, tie up, or fetter." The -ουσιν (-ousin) ending indicates that the word is in the present tense and third person plural form.

Grammar: δεσμευουσιν is a present tense verb in the active voice, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb δεσμεύω (desmeúō), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description: δεσμευουσιν means "they bind" or "they tie up" in English. It is often used in a literal sense, such as tying up a prisoner or binding a book, but can also be used metaphorically to describe being bound by rules or obligations.
