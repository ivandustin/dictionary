Word: δεδοξασται

Etymology:
δεδοξασται is a Greek word that is derived from the verb δοξάζω (doxazo), which means "to glorify" or "to praise." The prefix δε- (de-) is a perfect tense marker, indicating that the action of glorifying or praising has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
δεδοξασται is a third-person plural form of the verb δοξάζω in the perfect tense. It is in the indicative mood, which means that it is a statement of fact. The word is passive, indicating that the subject of the sentence is receiving the action of glorifying or praising.

Description:
δεδοξασται can be translated as "they have been glorified" or "they have been praised." The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe believers who have been justified by faith in Jesus Christ. It is a reminder that our salvation is not based on our own efforts or merit, but on the grace of God and the work of Christ on the cross.
