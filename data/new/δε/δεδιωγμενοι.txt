Word: δεδιωγμένοι

Etymology:
The word δεδιωγμένοι (dediogmenoi) is a past participle form of the Greek verb διώκω (dioko), which means "to pursue" or "to chase". The prefix δεδ- (ded-) indicates that the action of pursuing has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
δεδιωγμένοι is a masculine plural form of the past participle, which means that it refers to a group of males who have been pursued or chased. It is used in the nominative case, which means that it is the subject of the sentence.

Description:
The word δεδιωγμένοι is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the apostles and early Christians who were persecuted for their faith. It is often translated as "persecuted" or "hunted down". The use of the past participle form emphasizes that the persecution was a completed action in the past.
