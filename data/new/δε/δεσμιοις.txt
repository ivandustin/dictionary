Word: δεσμίοις

Etymology: δεσμίοις comes from the Greek word δεσμός (desmos), meaning "bond" or "fetter". The suffix -οις (-ois) indicates that it is in the dative plural form, meaning "to the bonds" or "for the bonds".

Grammar: δεσμίοις is a noun in the dative plural form. It is often translated as "prisoners" or "captives" in English.

Description: δεσμίοις refers to individuals who are held captive or imprisoned. In ancient Greece, prisoners were often used as laborers or held for ransom. The term can also be used metaphorically to refer to individuals who are trapped or constrained in some way.
