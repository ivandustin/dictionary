Word: δεσμων

Etymology:
The word δεσμων (desmon) comes from the Greek verb δεσμεύω (desmeuo), which means "to bind" or "to tie up." The noun form δεσμων (desmon) refers to something that binds or ties up, such as a chain or a bond.

Grammar:
In Greek, δεσμων (desmon) is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence or clause.

Description:
δεσμων (desmon) can be translated to English as "bond," "fetter," or "chain." It is often used in a metaphorical sense to refer to something that binds or restricts a person or group, such as social or political constraints. In Greek mythology, the god Zeus was said to have bound the Titan Prometheus with δεσμων (desmon) as punishment for stealing fire from the gods.
