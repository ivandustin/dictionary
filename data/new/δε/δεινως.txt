Word: δεινως

Etymology:
The word δεινως (deinōs) is derived from the Greek word δεινός (deinos), which means "terrible" or "fearful". It is related to the word δείδω (deidō), which means "to fear".

Grammar:
δεινως is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs. It is often translated as "terribly" or "fearfully".

Description:
δεινως is a word that is used to describe something that is causing fear or terror. It can be used to describe a person, an event, or a situation. It is often used in a negative context, to describe something that is unpleasant or frightening. For example, one might say "δεινως εφοβήθηκα" (deinōs efobēthēka), which means "I was terribly afraid".
