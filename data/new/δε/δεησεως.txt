The word δεησεως (deēseōs) is a Greek noun that means "supplication" or "prayer." It is derived from the verb δεόμαι (deomai), which means "to ask" or "to beg." In terms of grammar, δεησεως is a genitive singular noun, meaning it is used to indicate possession or relationship to another noun. Overall, δεησεως is a word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to a request or plea made to a higher power.
