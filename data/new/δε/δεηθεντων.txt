Word: δεηθεντων

Etymology: δεηθεντων is a Greek word that comes from the verb δέομαι (deomai), which means "to need" or "to want". The suffix -των (-ton) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word is referring to multiple things that are needed or wanted.

Grammar: δεηθεντων is a genitive plural form of the verb δέομαι (deomai). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple things that are needed or wanted.

Description: δεηθεντων can be translated as "of those who are needed or wanted". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to people who are needed or desired by a deity or higher power. It can also be used more generally to refer to people who are important or necessary for a particular purpose or goal.
