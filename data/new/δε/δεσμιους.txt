Word: δεσμίους

Etymology: δεσμίους comes from the Greek word δεσμός (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie". The suffix -ιους (-ious) is a common ending used to form adjectives indicating possession or relationship.

Grammar: δεσμίους is the accusative plural form of the adjective δεσμίος (desmios), which means "bound" or "captured". It is used to describe multiple people who are bound or imprisoned.

Description: δεσμίους is a word that describes a group of people who are physically restrained or imprisoned. It can be used in a literal sense, such as referring to prisoners or hostages, or in a figurative sense, such as describing someone who feels trapped or constrained in a particular situation. The word carries a connotation of helplessness and lack of freedom.
