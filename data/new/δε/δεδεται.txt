The word "δεδεται" is a Greek verb that is in the perfect tense and passive voice. It comes from the root word "δέω" which means "to bind" or "to tie". In its current form, "δεδεται" means "has been bound" or "has been tied". 

In terms of grammar, "δεδεται" is a third-person singular verb, which means it refers to a single subject that is not the speaker or the listener. The perfect tense indicates that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is the recipient of the action, rather than the doer.

Overall, "δεδεται" is a specific form of the verb "δέω" that indicates something has been bound or tied in the past and continues to be in that state in the present.
