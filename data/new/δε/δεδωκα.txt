Etymology: The word "δίδωμι" (didomi) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-, which means "to give" or "to put". 

Grammar: As mentioned earlier, "δεδωκα" (dedoka) is in the perfect tense and active voice. The perfect tense in Greek indicates that an action was completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present. The active voice means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.
