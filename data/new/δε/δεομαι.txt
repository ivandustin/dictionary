Word: δεομαι

Etymology:
The word δεομαι (deomai) is a Greek verb that means "to ask, to beg, to beseech, to pray." It comes from the root word δεης (dees), which means "need" or "necessity."

Grammar:
δεομαι is a first-person singular present indicative form of the verb δεομαι (deomai). It is a middle voice verb, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In other words, the subject is asking for something for themselves.

Description:
δεομαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to express a simple request or a more urgent plea. It can also be used in a religious context to mean "to pray." Overall, δεομαι is a powerful word that conveys a sense of need and urgency.
