Word: δεκα

Etymology:
The word δεκα (deka) is a Greek word that means "ten". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dekm̥t, which also gave rise to the English word "ten".

Grammar:
In Greek, δεκα is a cardinal number and is used to indicate the quantity of ten. It is an indeclinable word, meaning that it does not change form based on gender, case, or number.

Description:
δεκα is a simple and commonly used word in Greek, representing the number ten. It is used in various contexts, such as counting, telling time, and expressing quantities. In ancient Greece, the number ten was considered a sacred and significant number, and it was often used in religious and cultural practices. Today, δεκα remains an important and fundamental word in the Greek language.
