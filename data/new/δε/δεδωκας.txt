Word: δεδωκας

Etymology: δεδωκας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δίδωμι (didomi), which means "to give." The prefix δε- (de-) indicates that the action of giving has already been completed in the past.

Grammar: δεδωκας is a second-person singular perfect indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb δίδωμι (didomi) and the perfect tense suffix -κα (ka). The verb means "you have given" in English.

Description: δεδωκας is a verb that indicates a completed action of giving in the past. It is commonly used in Greek literature and conversation to express gratitude or to acknowledge a gift or favor that has been given.
