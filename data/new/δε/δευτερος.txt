Word: δευτερος

Etymology:
The word δευτερος (deuteros) is a Greek word that means "second" or "next in line". It is derived from the Greek word δυο (dyo) which means "two".

Grammar:
δευτερος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. 

Description:
δευτερος is a common Greek word that is used to describe something that comes after the first or is the second in a series. It can also be used to describe something that is of secondary importance or rank. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe the second coming of Christ. In modern Greek, the word is used in everyday language to describe the second floor of a building or the second course of a meal.
