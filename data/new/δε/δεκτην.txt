The word "δεκτην" (dekten) is a Greek noun that means "receptacle" or "container". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "δέχομαι" (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". 

Grammar: "Δεκτην" is a feminine noun in the singular form. 

Description: The word can refer to any type of container or receptacle, such as a box, a jar, or a basket. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a person who is receptive or open-minded. For example, one might say "Είναι δεκτή στις ιδέες των άλλων" (Eini dekti stis idees ton allon), which means "She is receptive to other people's ideas".
