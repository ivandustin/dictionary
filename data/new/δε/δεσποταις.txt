Word: δεσποταις

Etymology:
The word δεσπόταις (despotais) is derived from the Greek word δεσπότης (despotes), which means "lord" or "master". It is a noun in the dative plural form.

Grammar:
δεσπόταις (despotais) is a noun in the dative plural form in Greek. It is used to refer to "lords" or "masters" in a formal or respectful manner.

Description:
δεσπόταις (despotais) is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to powerful rulers or masters. It was often used to address or refer to kings, emperors, or other high-ranking officials. In modern Greek, the term is still used to refer to someone who has absolute power or control over a situation or group of people.
