Word: δευτεραν

Etymology: δευτεραν is a Greek word that comes from the root word δεύτερος (deuteros), which means "second" or "next in order". 

Grammar: δευτεραν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Description: δευτεραν can be translated to mean "second" or "the second one". It is often used in reference to the second day of the week, which in Greek is called Δευτέρα (Deutera). It can also be used more generally to refer to something that is second in a series or order.
