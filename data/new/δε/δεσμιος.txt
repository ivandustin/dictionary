Word: δεσμιος

Etymology:
The word δεσμιος (desmios) comes from the Greek word δεσμός (desmos), which means "bond" or "tie." It is derived from the verb δεσμεύω (desmeuo), which means "to bind" or "to tie up."

Grammar:
In Greek, δεσμιος is an adjective that means "bound" or "captured." It is often used to describe a person who is a prisoner or a captive. The word can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
δεσμιος is a word that describes someone who is physically or metaphorically bound or captive. It can be used to describe a prisoner of war, a criminal in custody, or someone who is trapped in a difficult situation. The word carries a sense of confinement and restriction, and it is often used in a negative context.
