The word δεσπότας (despotas) comes from the Greek word δεσπότης (despotes), which means "master" or "lord". It was originally used to refer to a ruler or monarch, but later came to be used more broadly to refer to anyone who had absolute power or authority.

In Greek, δεσπότας is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. It is the plural form of δεσπότης (despotes), which is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form.
