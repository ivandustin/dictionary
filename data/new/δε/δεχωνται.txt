Word: δεχωνται

Etymology: δεχωνται is a present tense form of the Greek verb δέχομαι (dekhomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deḱ- ("to take, accept").

Grammar: δεχωνται is a present tense, third person plural form of the verb δέχομαι. In Greek, verbs are inflected to indicate tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people or things.

Description: δεχωνται is a verb that means "they receive" or "they accept" in Greek. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as when talking about receiving guests, accepting a job offer, or taking in information.
