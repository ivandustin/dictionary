Word: δεικνυοντος

Etymology:
δεικνυοντος is a present participle of the Greek verb δείκνυμι (deiknymi), which means "to show" or "to point out". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, which means "to show" or "to point".

Grammar:
δεικνυοντος is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and genitive case. It is derived from the verb δείκνυμι (deiknymi), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description:
δεικνυοντος can be translated as "showing" or "pointing out". As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action of showing or pointing out something. The genitive case indicates possession or association with another noun in the sentence. For example, in the sentence "Ο δεικνύων το βιβλίο είναι ο φίλος μου" (The one showing the book is my friend), δεικνυοντος is used to describe the subject of the sentence, who is showing the book.
