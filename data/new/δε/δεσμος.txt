Word: δεσμός

Etymology: The word δεσμός (desmos) comes from the Greek verb δεσμεύω (desmeuo), which means "to bind" or "to tie up". It is related to the noun δεσμός (desmos), which means "bond" or "tie".

Grammar: δεσμός is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive (δεσμού), dative (δεσμώ), and accusative (δεσμό).

Description: δεσμός refers to a bond or tie that connects two or more things together. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a rope or chain that binds something, or in a figurative sense, such as a strong emotional or social connection between people. The word can also be used to refer to a constraint or limitation that restricts someone's freedom or ability to act.
