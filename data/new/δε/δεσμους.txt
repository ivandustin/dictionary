Word: δεσμούς

Etymology: δεσμούς comes from the Greek word δεσμός (desmos), which means "bond" or "tie". It is derived from the verb δέω (deo), which means "to bind" or "to tie".

Grammar: δεσμούς is a noun in the plural form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: δεσμούς refers to bonds, ties, or restraints. It can be used in a literal sense, such as physical bonds or chains, or in a figurative sense, such as emotional or social ties. In ancient Greece, δεσμούς was also used to refer to the bonds of marriage or friendship.
