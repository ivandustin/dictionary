Word: δεσμός (desmos)

Etymology:
The word δεσμός (desmos) comes from the Greek verb δεσμεύω (desmeuo), which means "to bind" or "to tie up". The noun form δεσμός (desmos) means "a bond" or "a tie".

Grammar:
δεσμός (desmos) is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes δεσμοί (desmoi).

Description:
δεσμός (desmos) refers to a bond or a tie that connects two or more things together. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a rope or chain that binds something, or in a figurative sense, such as a strong emotional or social connection between people. The word can also be used in a legal context, such as a contract or agreement that binds two parties together.
