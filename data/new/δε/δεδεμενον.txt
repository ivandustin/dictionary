Word: δεδεμένον

Etymology:
The word δεδεμένον (dedemenon) is a past participle of the Greek verb δεμώ (demo), which means "to bind" or "to tie up". The prefix δε- (de-) indicates completion or perfect tense, so δεδεμένον (dedemenon) means "having been bound" or "having been tied up".

Grammar:
δεδεμένον (dedemenon) is a neuter gender noun in the singular form. It is a past participle, which means it functions as an adjective in the sentence. In Greek, past participles are declined like adjectives, so their endings change according to the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description:
δεδεμένον (dedemenon) is a word that describes something that has been bound or tied up. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a person who has been tied up with ropes, or in a figurative sense, such as a situation that is restrictive or limiting. The word can also be used as a metaphor for being trapped or imprisoned.
