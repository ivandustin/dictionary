Word: δεκτός

Etymology:
The word δεκτός (dektos) comes from the Greek verb δέχομαι (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". The suffix -τός (-tos) is a common adjectival suffix in Greek, which is used to form adjectives from verbs.

Grammar:
δεκτός is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as δεκτού (genitive), δεκτώ (dative), δεκτόν (accusative), δεκτέ (vocative).

Description:
The word δεκτός is used to describe something or someone that is accepted, approved, or welcomed. It can also mean "eligible" or "qualified". In modern Greek, it is often used in the context of job applications or university admissions, where it refers to someone who has been accepted or approved for a position or program.
