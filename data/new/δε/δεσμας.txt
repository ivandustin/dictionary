Word: δεσμάς

The word δεσμάς (desmas) comes from the Greek verb δεσμεύω (desmeuo), which means "to bind" or "to tie up". It is derived from the noun δεσμός (desmos), which means "bond" or "fetter".

δεσμάς is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word is also declined in the genitive, dative, and accusative cases, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
δεσμάς refers to a bond or a fetter, which is used to restrain or tie up a person or an animal. In ancient Greece, prisoners were often bound with δεσμάς to prevent them from escaping. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to any kind of restraint or limitation.
