Etymology: The word "οκταημερος" comes from the Greek words "οκτώ" (októ), meaning "eight," and "ημέρα" (iméra), meaning "day." 

Grammar: "Οκταημερος" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and has the genitive form "οκταημέρου" (oktaimérou). 

Description: "Οκταημερος" refers to something that lasts for eight days or occurs every eight days. It can also be used to describe an event or celebration that lasts for eight days.
