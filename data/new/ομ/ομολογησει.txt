The word "ομολογησει" (omologisi) is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology comes from the combination of two words: "ομολογία" (omologia) meaning "confession" or "acknowledgment" and "ειμί" (imi) meaning "to be". 

In terms of grammar, "ομολογησει" is in the third person singular form of the future tense. It can be translated to English as "he/she/it will confess/acknowledge".

The verb "ομολογησει" is often used in legal or religious contexts, where someone is confessing to a wrongdoing or acknowledging a belief. It can also be used in a more general sense to mean admitting or acknowledging something.
