The word "ομνυετε" (omnyete) is a verb in ancient Greek. Its etymology is derived from the root word "ομνυμι" (omnymi), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath." 

In terms of grammar, "ομνυετε" is a present tense, second person plural form of the verb. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all swear" or "you all take an oath." 

In ancient Greek culture, taking oaths was a common practice in legal and religious contexts. The act of swearing an oath was considered a sacred and binding commitment, and failure to uphold an oath was seen as a serious offense. 

Overall, "ομνυετε" is a verb that reflects the importance of oaths and commitments in ancient Greek society.
