The word "ομνυει" (omnyei) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular present indicative form of the verb "ομνύω" (omnyo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath". 

Etymology: The word "ομνύω" comes from the ancient Greek word "ὀμνύω" (omnyo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath". 

Grammar: "Ομνυει" is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person. 

Description: The word "ομνυει" is used to describe the act of swearing or taking an oath. It can be used in various contexts, such as in legal proceedings, religious ceremonies, or personal promises.
