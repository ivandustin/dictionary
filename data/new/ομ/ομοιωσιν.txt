Word: ομοιωσιν

Etymology:
The word ομοιωσιν (omoiōsin) is derived from the Greek verb ομοιόω (omoióō), which means "to make similar" or "to compare." The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the action or result of the verb.

Grammar:
Ομοιωσιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
Ομοιωσιν refers to the act of making something similar or comparing two things. It can also refer to a similarity or resemblance between two things. The word is often used in a philosophical or theological context to describe the concept of likeness or similarity between God and humans.
