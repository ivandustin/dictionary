Word: ομολογω

Etymology: Ομολογώ comes from the Greek words "ομός" (homos) meaning "same" and "λόγος" (logos) meaning "word" or "reason". Therefore, the word ομολογώ means "to say the same thing" or "to agree".

Grammar: Ομολογώ is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is a regular verb. It is used in the present tense, past tense, and future tense, as well as in the imperative mood.

Description: Ομολογώ is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can mean "to confess", "to acknowledge", "to agree", "to admit", or "to concede". It is often used in legal or religious contexts, as well as in everyday conversation. For example, one might say "Ομολογώ ότι έκανα λάθος" (I confess that I made a mistake) or "Ομολογώ ότι έχεις δίκιο" (I admit that you are right).
