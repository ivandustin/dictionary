Word: ομιλησας

Etymology: Ομιλησας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "ομιλία" (omilia), which means "speech" or "conversation". The verb form ομιλησας is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: Ομιλησας is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word "ομιλία" (omilia) and the aorist tense suffix "-σας" (-sas). The verb means "you spoke" or "you talked" in English.

Description: Ομιλησας is a verb that describes a completed action of speaking or talking in the past. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and writing in Greek. The verb can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as giving a speech, having a conversation, or simply talking to someone.
