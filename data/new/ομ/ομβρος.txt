Word: ομβρος

Etymology:
The word ομβρος (ombros) comes from the Ancient Greek ὄμβρος (ombros), which means "rain" or "shower". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂regʷ- (to wet, to rain).

Grammar:
In Greek, ομβρος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is ομβρος, and its genitive singular form is ομβρου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
Ομβρος refers to rain or a shower of rain. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a sudden and heavy downpour of anything, such as emotions or events. In Greek mythology, ομβρος was personified as the god of rain and storms.
