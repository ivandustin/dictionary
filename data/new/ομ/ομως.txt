Word: ομως

Etymology: ομως is a Greek adverb that means "however" or "nevertheless". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ὅμως" (homōs), which means "in like manner" or "similarly".

Grammar: ομως is an adverb in Greek and is used to indicate a contrast or a concession in a sentence. It is often used to introduce a new idea or to show a different perspective on a topic.

Description: ομως is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is commonly used in both spoken and written Greek to express a contrast or a concession. For example, "Δεν μου αρέσει η βροχή, ομως πρέπει να βγω έξω" (I don't like the rain, however, I have to go out). It can also be used to express surprise or disbelief, as in "Ομως, δεν πίστευα ότι θα τα κατάφερνες" (However, I didn't believe you would make it).
