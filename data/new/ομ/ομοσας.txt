The word "ομοσας" (omosas) is a Greek verb that means "to swear" or "to take an oath". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "ομός" (omos), meaning "same" or "equal", and "ὅρκος" (horkos), meaning "oath". 

Grammar: "Ομοσας" is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "ομνύω" (omnyo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath". 

Description: The word "ομοσας" is often used in legal or religious contexts, where a person is required to make a solemn promise or declaration. It can also be used in a figurative sense, to express a strong commitment or conviction.
