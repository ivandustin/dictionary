Etymology: 
The word "ομολογησαντες" is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "ομολογέω" (omologéō), which means "to confess" or "to acknowledge". The suffix "-σαντες" (-santes) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of confessing or acknowledging has already taken place.

Grammar:
"Ομολογησαντες" is the third person plural past participle form of the verb "ομολογέω" (omologéō). It is used to describe a group of people who have already confessed or acknowledged something in the past. 

Description:
The word "ομολογησαντες" can be translated to English as "having confessed" or "having acknowledged". It is often used in religious or legal contexts to describe a group of people who have admitted to a wrongdoing or a belief. For example, in the Christian faith, the phrase "ομολογησαντες τας αμαρτιας" (omologésantes tas amartias) means "having confessed their sins". In a legal context, the phrase "ομολογησαντες την ενοχή τους" (omologésantes tin enochi tous) means "having confessed their guilt".
