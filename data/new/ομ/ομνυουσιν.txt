Word: ομνυουσιν

Etymology: The word ομνυουσιν is derived from the Greek verb ομνύω (omnyo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: ομνυουσιν is a present tense, third person plural form of the Greek verb ομνύω. It is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -ύω (-yo).

Description: ομνυουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they swear" or "they take an oath". It is often used in legal or formal contexts, where people are required to make a solemn promise or declaration. The verb can also be used in a figurative sense, to express a strong commitment or conviction.
