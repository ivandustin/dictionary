Word: σβεννύται

Etymology:
The word σβεννύται is derived from the Greek verb σβέννυμι (sbennymi), which means "to extinguish" or "to put out." The word is formed from the prefix σ- (s-), which indicates a negative or opposite meaning, and the verb βέννυμι (vennymi), which means "to light" or "to kindle."

Grammar:
σβεννύται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the third person singular form. The verb stem is σβεννυ-, and the ending -ται indicates the passive voice.

Description:
The word σβεννύται is used to describe the action of something being extinguished or put out. It can refer to the extinguishing of a fire, a light, or any other source of illumination. The passive voice indicates that the action is being done to the subject of the sentence, rather than the subject performing the action.
