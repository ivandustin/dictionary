Word: σβεσει

Etymology:
The word σβεσει (svesei) is a present tense form of the Greek verb σβέννυμι (svennumi), which means "to extinguish" or "to put out." The verb comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *swen- meaning "to disappear" or "to go out."

Grammar:
σβεσει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb σβέννυμι. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The present tense is used to describe actions that are happening now or are ongoing. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a singular third person, such as "he," "she," or "it."

Description:
σβεσει is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it extinguishes" or "he/she/it puts out." It is often used in the context of extinguishing a fire or a light. For example, "Ο πυροσβέστης σβεννύει τη φωτιά" (O pyrosvestis svennyei ti fotia) means "The firefighter is putting out the fire." The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of calming down or ending a conflict.
