Word: σπευδόντας

Etymology: σπευδόντας is the present participle of the Greek verb σπεύδω (speúdo), which means "to hurry" or "to hasten". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *speh₁- ("to thrive, prosper").

Grammar: σπευδόντας is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb σπεύδω and indicates ongoing action. In English, it can be translated as "hurrying" or "hastening".

Description: σπευδόντας describes a group of people who are currently in the act of hurrying or hastening. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of soldiers rushing into battle or a group of students running to class.
