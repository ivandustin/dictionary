The word "σπιλουσα" (spilousa) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "staining" or "spotting". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "σπιλ-" (spil-), which means "spot" or "stain". 

Grammar: "σπιλουσα" is the present participle form of the verb "σπιλόω" (spiloó), which means "to stain" or "to spot". The present participle form indicates that the action of staining or spotting is ongoing or continuous. 

Description: The word "σπιλουσα" describes the action of staining or spotting something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a garment that has been stained, or a surface that has been spotted with dirt or marks.
