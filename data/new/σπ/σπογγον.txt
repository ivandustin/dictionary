Word: σπογγον

Etymology:
The word σπογγον (spongon) is derived from the Greek word σπόγγος (spongos), which means "sponge". The suffix "-ον" (-on) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns.

Grammar:
In Greek, σπογγον is a masculine noun in the singular form. Its nominative case is σπογγον, and its genitive case is σπογγου (spongou).

Description:
σπογγον refers to a sponge, a porous aquatic animal that is commonly used for cleaning and absorbing liquids. In ancient Greece, sponges were also used for bathing and personal hygiene. The word σπογγον can also be used metaphorically to refer to a person who is easily influenced or absorbs information quickly.
