Word: σπερματος

Etymology:
The word σπερματος (spermatos) is a Greek word that comes from the root word σπερμα (sperma), which means "seed" or "sperm". The suffix -ατος (-atos) is a genitive ending that indicates possession or origin.

Grammar:
σπερματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or origin. It is the genitive singular form of the word σπερμα (sperma).

Description:
σπερματος refers to the seed or sperm of a plant or animal. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the origin or source of something. In biology, the term is often used to refer to the male reproductive cells, or spermatozoa, that fertilize the female reproductive cells, or ova, to create offspring. In botany, it refers to the reproductive cells of plants, such as pollen.
