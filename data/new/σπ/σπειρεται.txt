Word: σπειρεται

Etymology: σπείρω (speírō) is a Greek verb meaning "to sow" or "to scatter". The suffix -ται (-tai) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the action is being performed on oneself. Therefore, σπειρεται (speiretai) means "he/she/it sows/scatters himself/herself/itself".

Grammar: σπειρεται (speiretai) is a third-person singular present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both performing and receiving the action.

Description: σπειρεται (speiretai) is a verb that describes the act of sowing or scattering oneself. It can be used metaphorically to describe someone who is spreading themselves thin or trying to do too many things at once. In Greek mythology, the goddess Demeter was often depicted sowing seeds, so the verb can also have agricultural connotations.
