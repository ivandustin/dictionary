Word: σπαταλώσα (spatalosa)

Etymology: The word σπαταλώσα comes from the Greek verb σπαταλώ (spatalo), which means to waste or squander. The suffix -σα (-sa) is added to the verb stem to indicate that the action was performed by a female subject.

Grammar: σπαταλώσα is the past tense form of the verb σπαταλώ in the first person singular. It is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The suffix -σα indicates that the subject is female.

Description: σπαταλώσα describes a female subject who has wasted or squandered something in the past. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was wasted or squandered.
