Word: σπευσαντες

Etymology:
The word σπευσαντες is derived from the Greek verb σπεύδω (speudo), which means "to hasten" or "to hurry". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the present active participle form of the verb.

Grammar:
σπευσαντες is the present active participle form of the Greek verb σπεύδω (speudo). It is in the masculine plural nominative case, which means it is used to describe a group of people who are currently hastening or hurrying.

Description:
σπευσαντες can be translated to "hurrying" or "hastening". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of people who are rushing to do something, such as the disciples who hurried to the tomb of Jesus after his crucifixion (John 20:4). The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any situation where people are moving quickly or with urgency.
