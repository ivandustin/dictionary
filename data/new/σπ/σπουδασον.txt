Word: σπουδασον

Etymology: σπουδασον is a verb in the imperative mood in Ancient Greek, derived from the noun σπουδή (spoudē), meaning "diligence" or "zeal". 

Grammar: σπουδασον is a second person singular imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "be diligent" or "make an effort". 

Description: σπουδασον is a word that encourages someone to work hard and put in effort towards a particular task or goal. It is often used in a motivational or inspirational context, urging someone to strive for excellence and not give up in the face of challenges.
