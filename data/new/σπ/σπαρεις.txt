Word: σπαρεις

Etymology: σπαρεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σπείρω (speírō), which means "to sow" or "to scatter." 

Grammar: σπαρεις is the second person singular present active indicative form of the verb σπείρω (speírō). It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present and is being performed by the second person singular subject (you). 

Description: σπαρεις can be translated to "you sow" or "you scatter." It is often used in the context of agriculture, referring to the act of planting seeds or scattering them in a field. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of spreading or disseminating something, such as ideas or information.
