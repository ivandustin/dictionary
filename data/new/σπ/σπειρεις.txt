Word: σπειρεις

Etymology:
The word σπειρεις (speireis) is derived from the Greek verb σπείρω (speirō), which means "to sow" or "to scatter." This verb is related to the noun σπόρος (sporos), which means "seed."

Grammar:
σπειρεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the verb σπείρω (speirō). It is translated as "you sow" or "you scatter."

Description:
σπειρεις is a verb that describes the action of sowing or scattering seeds. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when a farmer sows seeds in a field, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone spreads ideas or rumors. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of planting the seeds of an idea or a plan.
