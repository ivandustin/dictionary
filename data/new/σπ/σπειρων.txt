Word: σπειρων

Etymology:
The word σπειρων (speirōn) is derived from the Greek verb σπείρω (speirō), which means "to sow" or "to scatter seed". It is related to the noun σπόρος (sporos), which means "seed".

Grammar:
σπειρων is the present participle of the verb σπείρω (speirō), which means "sowing" or "scattering seed". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
σπειρων is used in the New Testament to refer to a sower or one who scatters seed. It is used in the parable of the sower (Matthew 13:3-9, Mark 4:3-9, Luke 8:5-8) to describe the person who sows the seed that falls on different types of soil. The word emphasizes the action of sowing and the importance of the seed in producing a harvest.
