Word: σπειρουσιν

Etymology: The word σπειρουσιν is derived from the Greek verb σπείρω (speírō), which means "to sow" or "to scatter seed". It is a present active indicative third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: σπειρουσιν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated from the root verb σπείρω (speírō), which means "to sow" or "to scatter seed". The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: σπειρουσιν is a Greek verb that describes the action of sowing or scattering seed. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when a farmer sows seeds in a field, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone spreads ideas or information.
