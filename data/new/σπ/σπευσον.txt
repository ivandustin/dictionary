Word: σπεῦσον (speuson)

Etymology: σπεῦδω (speudō) is a Greek verb meaning "to hurry" or "to hasten." The imperative form of this verb is σπεῦσον (speuson), which means "hurry up" or "make haste."

Grammar: σπεῦσον is the second person singular imperative form of the verb σπεῦδω. It is used to give a command or order to someone to hurry up or make haste.

Description: σπεῦσον is a common word in Greek literature and is often used in the New Testament. It is a strong and urgent command that is used to convey a sense of urgency or importance. The word is often used in situations where time is of the essence, such as in emergencies or when there is a need to act quickly.
