Word: σπουδαιοτερον

Etymology: 
The word σπουδαιοτερον is derived from the Greek word σπουδαῖος (spoudaios), which means "serious" or "earnest". The suffix -τερον (-teron) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "greater". Therefore, σπουδαιοτερον can be translated as "more serious" or "more earnest".

Grammar:
σπουδαιοτερον is an adjective in the comparative form. It is declined in the masculine, neuter, and feminine genders, and in the nominative, accusative, and vocative cases. The declension is as follows:

Masculine: σπουδαιότερος (spoudaioteros)
Feminine: σπουδαιοτέρα (spoudaiotera)
Neuter: σπουδαιότερον (spoudaioteron)

Description:
σπουδαιοτερον is used to compare the degree of seriousness or earnestness between two things. It can be used to describe a person, situation, or action that is more serious or earnest than another. For example, "Η σπουδαιοτερη κατασταση ειναι η υγεια μας" (The most serious situation is our health).
