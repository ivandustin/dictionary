Word: λοιδορος

Etymology: The word λοιδορος (loidoros) comes from the Greek words λοιδορέω (loidoreo) which means "to revile" or "to insult" and όρος (oros) which means "one who does". Therefore, λοιδορος can be translated as "one who reviles" or "one who insults".

Grammar: In Greek, λοιδορος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case λοιδόρου (loidorou) or the accusative case λοιδόρον (loidoron).

Description: λοιδορος is a word that describes someone who habitually insults or reviles others. It can be used to describe someone who is verbally abusive or who uses offensive language towards others. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize someone's behavior.
