Word: λουσάντες

Etymology: λουσάντες is the present participle of the Greek verb λούω (louo), which means "to wash". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leu- which means "to wash" or "to flow".

Grammar: λουσάντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb λούω (louo), which means "to wash". The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action, and in this case, it describes people who are washing.

Description: λουσάντες is a Greek word that means "washing" or "those who are washing". It can be used to describe people who are washing themselves, their clothes, or any other object. The word is often used in religious contexts, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the act of baptism.
