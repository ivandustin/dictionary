The word λογομαχειν (logomachein) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of λόγος (logos) meaning "word" or "speech" and μάχη (mache) meaning "battle" or "fight". Therefore, λογομαχειν means "to engage in a battle of words" or "to argue". 

In terms of grammar, λογομαχειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects and nuances of arguing or engaging in a battle of words.

Overall, λογομαχειν is a word that describes the act of verbal conflict or debate, often used in a negative context to imply a heated or unproductive argument.
