Word: λογος

Etymology: λογος comes from the Greek word λέγω (lego), which means "to speak" or "to say". It is also related to the word λόγος (logos), which means "word" or "speech".

Grammar: λογος is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: λογος has several meanings in Greek, including "word", "speech", "reason", "thought", and "account". It is a very important concept in Greek philosophy, particularly in the works of Plato and Aristotle. In Christian theology, λογος is used to refer to the divine Word or the second person of the Trinity.
