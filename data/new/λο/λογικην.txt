The word "λογικην" (logikēn) is a Greek adjective that means "logical" or "rational". It is derived from the noun "λόγος" (logos), which means "word", "reason", or "logic". In terms of grammar, "λογικην" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

In terms of description, "λογικην" can be used to describe something that is characterized by reason, coherence, and sound judgment. It can also refer to a person who is skilled in logical thinking or reasoning. Overall, the word "λογικην" connotes a sense of intellectual rigor and clarity of thought.
