Word: λουτρό (loutró)

Etymology: The word λουτρό comes from the ancient Greek word λούειν (loúein), which means "to wash". It is related to the English word "lather".

Grammar: λουτρό is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is λουτρά (loutrá).

Description: λουτρό refers to a bath or a place where one can bathe. It can also refer to a ritual cleansing or purification, especially in ancient Greek religion. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to refer to a public bathhouse or a swimming pool.
