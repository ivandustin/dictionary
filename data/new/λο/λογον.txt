The word λογον (logon) is a Greek noun that can be translated as "word," "speech," "reason," or "account." It is derived from the verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak." In Greek philosophy, λογον is often used to refer to the rational principle that governs the universe, or to the divine reason that underlies all things. In grammar, λογον is a masculine noun in the accusative case.
