Word: λοιπών

Etymology: λοιπών is the genitive plural form of the Greek adjective λοιπός (loipós), which means "remaining" or "leftover". It comes from the verb λείπω (leípō), which means "to leave" or "to be missing".

Grammar: λοιπών is a genitive plural form of the adjective λοιπός. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, λοιπών is used to indicate that something belongs to or is related to a group of things that are "remaining" or "leftover".

Description: λοιπών is a Greek word that is often used in the context of lists or groups of things. It indicates that something is part of a group of things that are "remaining" or "leftover" after other items have been accounted for. For example, if someone were to say "τα λοιπά" (ta loipá), they would be referring to the remaining items on a list or the things that were not previously mentioned.
