Word: λογιζομενους

Etymology: λογιζομενους is the present participle of the Greek verb λογίζομαι (logizomai), which means "to count, calculate, reckon, or consider." It comes from the root word λόγος (logos), which means "word, reason, or thought."

Grammar: λογιζομενους is a present participle in the masculine accusative plural form. It is derived from the verb λογίζομαι (logizomai), which is a middle voice verb. In this form, it functions as an adjective modifying a noun in the accusative case.

Description: λογιζομενους is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Hebrews 11:11. It is translated as "considering" or "counting" in English. The verse reads, "By faith Sarah herself received power to conceive, even when she was past the age, since she considered him faithful who had promised." The word implies a deliberate and intentional thought process, suggesting that Sarah actively weighed and evaluated God's promise before accepting it.
