The word λογιος (logios) is a Greek adjective that can be translated as "wise," "learned," or "eloquent." Its etymology comes from the Greek word λόγος (logos), which means "word" or "reason." In terms of grammar, λογιος is a masculine noun that can be declined in various cases and numbers. 

In ancient Greek literature, λογιος was often used to describe individuals who possessed great knowledge and wisdom, particularly in the fields of philosophy, rhetoric, and poetry. It was also used to describe individuals who were skilled in public speaking and could deliver persuasive speeches. 

Overall, λογιος is a word that connotes intelligence, wisdom, and eloquence.
