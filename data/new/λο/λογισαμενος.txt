Word: λογισάμενος

Etymology:
λογισάμενος comes from the Greek verb λογίζομαι (logizomai), which means "to reckon, calculate, or consider." It is derived from the noun λόγος (logos), which means "word, reason, or thought."

Grammar:
λογισάμενος is the aorist participle of the verb λογίζομαι (logizomai). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
λογισάμενος is a Greek word that means "having calculated" or "having considered." It is often used in the New Testament to describe someone who has thought carefully about something before taking action. For example, in Luke 14:28, Jesus uses the word to describe someone who wants to build a tower and first sits down to calculate the cost. In Romans 4:3, the word is used to describe Abraham, who believed God and it was counted to him as righteousness.
