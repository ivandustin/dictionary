Word: λοιπόν

Etymology: λοιπόν is a Greek adverb that is derived from the adjective λοιπός (loipos), meaning "remaining" or "leftover". It is believed to have originated from the ancient Greek phrase "λοιπὸν ἔχειν" (loipon echein), which means "to have remaining".

Grammar: λοιπόν is an adverb that is commonly used in spoken Greek. It is often used as a filler word to signal a change in topic or to indicate that the speaker is about to make a point. It can also be used to express impatience or frustration.
