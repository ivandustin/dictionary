Word: λογιζομενος

Etymology: λογιζομενος is the present participle of the Greek verb λογίζομαι (logizomai), which means "to count, calculate, reckon, or consider." The word comes from the root word λόγος (logos), which means "word, reason, or thought."

Grammar: λογιζομενος is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is derived from the verb λογίζομαι, which is a middle voice verb. In English, it can be translated as "counting," "calculating," or "considering."

Description: λογιζομενος is a word that describes someone who is actively engaged in counting, calculating, or considering something. It can be used to describe someone who is deep in thought, or someone who is carefully analyzing a situation. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the mindset of a believer who is focused on the things of God and is actively seeking to understand and apply His truth to their life.
