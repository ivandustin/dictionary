Word: λογισμούς

Etymology: The word λογισμούς comes from the Greek word λογισμός (logismós), which means "calculation" or "reckoning". It is derived from the verb λογίζομαι (logízomai), which means "to calculate" or "to reckon".

Grammar: λογισμούς is a noun in the accusative case, plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. The plural form indicates that there are multiple objects being referred to.

Description: λογισμούς refers to calculations or computations. It can be used in various contexts, such as in mathematics, accounting, or finance. The word can also be used figuratively to refer to careful consideration or thought.
