Word: λοιποις

Etymology: λοιποις is a Greek word that comes from the root word λοιπός (loipós), which means "remaining" or "leftover."

Grammar: λοιποις is the dative plural form of the adjective λοιπός (loipós), which means "remaining" or "leftover." In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: λοιποις is used to describe a group of people or things that are remaining or leftover after others have been taken away or used up. It can also be used to refer to something that is left over or remaining in a more general sense. For example, in the sentence "Οι λοιποί μαθητές ήρθαν αργότερα" (Oi loipoi mathites irthan argotera), which means "The remaining students came later," λοιποις is used to describe the group of students who came later, after some others had already arrived.
