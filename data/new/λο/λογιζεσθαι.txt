The word λογιζεσθαι (logizesthai) is a Greek verb that means "to calculate," "to reckon," or "to consider." It is derived from the noun λόγος (logos), which means "word," "reason," or "account." In grammar, λογιζεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive, which means that it can be translated as "to be calculated" or "to be reckoned." 

In usage, λογιζεσθαι can refer to both mathematical calculations and more abstract reasoning or consideration. For example, in the New Testament, the word is often used in a theological context to describe the process of reckoning or accounting for one's sins or righteousness before God. 

Overall, λογιζεσθαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe the act of calculating, reckoning, or considering.
