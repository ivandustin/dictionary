The word "λογων" (logon) is a Greek noun in the genitive case, which means "of words" or "of speech". It is derived from the Greek verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to speak" or "to say". In Greek grammar, "λογων" is a second declension noun, and its singular form is "λόγος" (logos). 

In ancient Greek philosophy, "λόγος" (logos) was a central concept, referring to the rational principle that governs the universe and the human mind. In Christian theology, "λόγος" (logos) is used to refer to the divine Word or Son of God. 

Overall, "λογων" (logon) is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts related to language, speech, and communication.
