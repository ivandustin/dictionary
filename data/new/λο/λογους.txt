The word "λογους" (logous) is a Greek noun in the accusative plural form. It is derived from the root word "λόγος" (logos), which means "word" or "speech". 

In terms of grammar, "λογους" is a masculine noun in the accusative case, indicating that it is the direct object of a verb. 

In ancient Greek philosophy, "λόγος" (logos) was used to refer to the principle of reason and order in the universe. It was also used to describe the power of language and communication. 

Overall, "λογους" can be translated to mean "words" or "speeches" in English.
