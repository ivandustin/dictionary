Word: ηπορεί

Etymology: The word ηπορεί comes from the Greek verb ηπορώ (iporó), which means "to be at a loss" or "to be perplexed." It is derived from the combination of the prefix η- (e-), which indicates a state of being, and the verb πορεύομαι (porevómai), which means "to go" or "to proceed."

Grammar: ηπορεί is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb ηπορώ (iporó). It is used to describe the action of being at a loss or being perplexed in the present time, and it agrees with a singular subject.

Description: ηπορεί is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the state of being at a loss or being perplexed. It is commonly used to describe a feeling of confusion or uncertainty.
