Etymology: The word "ωφεληθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "ωφελέω" (opheléo), which means "to benefit" or "to help." It is derived from the noun "οφελος" (ophelos), meaning "advantage" or "benefit."

Grammar: "Ωφεληθησαν" is the third person plural, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "ωφελέω" (opheléo). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Ωφεληθησαν" is a Greek verb form that translates to "they were benefited" or "they were helped" in English. It describes a past action where a group of people received some form of benefit or assistance.
