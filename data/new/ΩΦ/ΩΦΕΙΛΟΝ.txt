Etymology: The word "ωφειλον" (ōpheilon) comes from the Greek verb "οφειλω" (opheilō), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". 

Grammar: "Ωφειλον" is a noun in the neuter gender and singular number. It is in the nominative case.

Description: "Ωφειλον" refers to a debt or an obligation. It is used to describe a financial or moral obligation that one person has towards another.
