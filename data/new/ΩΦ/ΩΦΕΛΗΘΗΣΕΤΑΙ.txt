Etymology: The word "ωφεληθεται" comes from the Greek verb "ωφελέω" (opheléo), which means "to benefit" or "to help." The suffix "-θη-" indicates the passive voice, and the ending "-ται" indicates the third person singular form.

Grammar: "Ωφεληθεται" is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and third person singular form. It is used to describe an action being done to the subject of the sentence.

Description: "Ωφεληθεται" means "he/she/it is being benefited" or "he/she/it is being helped." It implies that someone or something is receiving a benefit or assistance from someone else.
