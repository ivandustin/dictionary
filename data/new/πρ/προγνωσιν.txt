Word: προγνωσιν

Etymology:
The word προγνωσιν (prognōsin) is derived from the Greek words πρό (pro) meaning "before" and γνῶσις (gnōsis) meaning "knowledge". Therefore, the word προγνωσιν means "foreknowledge" or "foreseeing".

Grammar:
In Greek, προγνωσιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. It is the direct object of a sentence and can be used with verbs that require a direct object.

Description:
The word προγνωσιν is often used in a religious or philosophical context to refer to divine foreknowledge or predestination. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any kind of foreknowledge or prediction. In modern Greek, the word is often used to refer to a forecast or prediction of future events.
