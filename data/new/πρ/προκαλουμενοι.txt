Word: προκαλουμενοι

Etymology:
The word προκαλουμενοι is derived from the Greek verb προκαλέω (prokaleo), which means "to call forth" or "to challenge". The suffix -μενοι (-menoi) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing an action that is currently happening.

Grammar:
προκαλουμενοι is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently being challenged or called forth to do something. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives or as part of a verb tense.

Description:
The word προκαλουμενοι can be translated to mean "those who are being challenged" or "those who are being called forth". It is often used in a military context to describe soldiers who are being called to action. It can also be used more broadly to describe anyone who is being challenged or provoked in some way.
