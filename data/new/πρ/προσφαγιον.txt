Word: προσφάγιον

Etymology:
The word προσφάγιον (prosphagion) is derived from the Greek words πρός (pros) meaning "towards" and φαγεῖν (phagein) meaning "to eat". It is a compound word that refers to a place or object where food is offered or consumed.

Grammar:
In Greek, προσφάγιον is a neuter noun. Its singular form is προσφάγιον and its plural form is προσφάγια (prosphagia). It is declined like other neuter nouns in the second declension.

Description:
προσφάγιον is a term used in the Eastern Orthodox Church to refer to the table of preparation where the bread and wine are prepared for the Eucharist. It is also used to refer to the bread and wine themselves that are offered during the Eucharist. The term is also used in a broader sense to refer to any place or object where food is offered or consumed.
