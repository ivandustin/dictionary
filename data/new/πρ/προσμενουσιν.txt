Word: προσμενουσιν

Etymology:
The word προσμενουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root words πρός (pros) meaning "toward" and μένω (meno) meaning "to remain". The verb form is in the present tense and active voice, indicating that the subject is currently performing the action.

Grammar:
προσμενουσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb προσμένω (prosmeno), which means "to wait for" or "to remain with". The verb form indicates that the subject is currently waiting or remaining with someone or something.

Description:
προσμενουσιν is a verb that describes an action of waiting or remaining with someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as waiting for a person, waiting for a decision, or remaining with a group of people. The verb form is in the present tense, indicating that the action is currently happening.
