Word: προαγοντες

Etymology:
The word προαγοντες (proagontes) is a Greek word that comes from the verb προάγω (proago), which means "to lead forward" or "to go before." The suffix -ντες (-ntes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently leading or going before.

Grammar:
προαγοντες is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is the present participle form of the verb προάγω (proago), which means "to lead forward" or "to go before." As a present participle, it describes people who are currently leading or going before.

Description:
προαγοντες refers to people who are leading or going before others. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as a group of people leading a procession or a team of athletes leading a race. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as people who are leading a movement or a cause. The word implies a sense of leadership, initiative, and forward motion.
