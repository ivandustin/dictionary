Word: προιστάσθαι

Etymology:
The word προιστάσθαι comes from the Greek verb προΐστημι (proistēmi), which means "to stand before, to lead, to preside over". It is composed of the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before", and the verb ἵστημι (histēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar:
προιστάσθαι is a middle voice verb in ancient Greek, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
προιστάσθαι means "to preside over, to lead, to be in charge of". It is often used in the context of political or religious leadership, but can also refer to any kind of leadership or management. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe the role of a magistrate or a commander in the army. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to refer to someone who is in charge of a group or organization.
