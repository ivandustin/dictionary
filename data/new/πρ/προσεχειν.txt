Word: προσεχειν

Etymology:
The word προσεχειν (prosechein) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πρός (pros), meaning "toward" or "in addition to," and ἔχειν (echein), meaning "to hold" or "to have." Therefore, the literal meaning of προσεχειν is "to hold toward" or "to have in addition to."

Grammar:
προσεχειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the present stem προσεχ- (prosech-). In English, it can be translated as "to pay attention," "to be careful," or "to take care."

Description:
προσεχειν is a verb that implies a deliberate and focused action. It is often used in the context of paying attention to something or someone, being careful or cautious, or taking care of something. It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as paying attention to one's thoughts or emotions. In Greek philosophy, the concept of προσεχειν is often associated with mindfulness and self-awareness.
