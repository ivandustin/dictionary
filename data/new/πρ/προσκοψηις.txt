The word "προσκοψηις" (proskopsēs) is a Greek noun that means "stumbling" or "offense". It is derived from the verb "προσκόπτω" (proskoptō), which means "to stumble" or "to trip". In grammar, it is a feminine noun of the third declension.

In ancient Greek literature, the word "προσκοψηις" was often used metaphorically to refer to a moral or ethical stumbling block, something that causes one to fall or fail in their beliefs or actions. It was also used in a religious context to refer to a sacrificial offering that was rejected by the gods due to some flaw or imperfection.

Overall, "προσκοψηις" is a word that conveys the idea of stumbling or tripping, both literally and figuratively.
