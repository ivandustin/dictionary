Word: προσενεγκον

Etymology:
The word προσενεγκον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: προς (pros) meaning "towards" or "to" and ενεγκω (enengo) meaning "to bring in" or "to offer". Therefore, the literal meaning of προσενεγκον is "to bring towards" or "to offer to".

Grammar:
προσενεγκον is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The word is in the second person singular form, meaning that it is addressing one person directly.

Description:
προσενεγκον is a word that is often used in religious contexts, particularly in the Bible. It is commonly translated as "offer" or "present", and is used to describe the act of bringing an offering or sacrifice to a deity. In a broader sense, it can also be used to describe the act of presenting something to someone as a gift or tribute.
