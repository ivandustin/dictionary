Word: πρωτου

Etymology: πρωτου is a Greek word that comes from the root word πρώτος (prótos), which means "first" or "foremost."

Grammar: πρωτου is a genitive singular form of the adjective πρώτος (prótos), which means "first" or "foremost." In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description: πρωτου can be translated to mean "of the first" or "of the foremost." It is often used in Greek literature and mythology to describe someone or something that is of great importance or significance. For example, in the Iliad, Achilles is referred to as "πρωτος Αχαιων" (prótos Achaeōn), which means "foremost of the Achaeans."
