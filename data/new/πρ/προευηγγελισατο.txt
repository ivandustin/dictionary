Word: προευηγγελισατο

Etymology: The word προευηγγελισατο is a compound word in Greek, formed by combining the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," with the verb ευαγγελιζω (euangelizo), meaning "to announce good news" or "to proclaim the gospel." Therefore, the word προευηγγελισατο means "he announced good news beforehand" or "he proclaimed the gospel in advance."

Grammar: προευηγγελισατο is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: προευηγγελισατο is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Romans 1:2. The verse reads, "which he promised beforehand through his prophets in the holy Scriptures, concerning his Son, who was descended from David according to the flesh." In this context, προευηγγελισατο is used to describe how God had already announced the good news of his Son's coming through the prophets in the Old Testament.
