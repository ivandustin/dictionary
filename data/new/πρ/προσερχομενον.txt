Word: προσερχομενον

Etymology: 
The word προσερχομενον is a present participle form of the Greek verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai), which means "to come near, approach, or draw near." The word is derived from the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward," and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar:
προσερχομενον is a present participle in the masculine singular form. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντ- (-ont-) to the present stem of the verb. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description:
προσερχομενον can be translated as "coming near" or "approaching." It is often used in the New Testament to describe people who are coming to Jesus or approaching him for healing or guidance. The word conveys a sense of movement and intentionality, as if the person is actively seeking out Jesus. In a broader sense, the word can also be used to describe any situation where someone is drawing near to something or someone else.
