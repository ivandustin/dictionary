Word: προελθοντες

Etymology: προ- (pro-, meaning "before" or "in front of") + ἔρχομαι (erchomai, meaning "to come") + -οντες (-ontes, a present participle suffix). Therefore, προελθόντες means "having come before" or "having gone ahead".

Grammar: προελθόντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is derived from the verb προέρχομαι (proerchomai), which means "to come forth" or "to go before". The participle form indicates that the action of coming before or going ahead is ongoing or continuous.

Description: προελθόντες is a Greek word that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is often translated as "going ahead" or "going before" and is used to describe someone who is leading the way or preparing the path for others. In some contexts, it can also mean "preceding" or "coming before" in a temporal sense.
