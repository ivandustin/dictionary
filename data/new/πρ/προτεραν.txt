Word: προτεραν

Etymology:
The word προτεραν is a Greek word that comes from the root word πρότερος (próteros), which means "former" or "earlier." The suffix -αν (-an) is added to the end of the word to indicate the accusative case, which is used to show the direct object of a sentence.

Grammar:
In Greek, προτεραν is a feminine accusative singular noun. It is used to indicate the direct object of a sentence, meaning that it is the noun that is being acted upon by the verb.

The word προτεραν is not commonly used in modern Greek, but it can be found in ancient texts. It is often used to refer to something that came before or was earlier than something else. For example, it could be used to describe a previous event or a former leader.
