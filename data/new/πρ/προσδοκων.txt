Word: προσδοκών

Etymology: The word προσδοκών comes from the Greek verb προσδοκάω (prosdokao), which means "to expect" or "to anticipate". The suffix -ν (-n) is added to the end of the verb to create the present participle form, which means "expecting" or "anticipating".

Grammar: προσδοκών is the present participle form of the verb προσδοκάω. It is used to describe an ongoing action of expecting or anticipating something. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives, nouns, or verbs.

Description: προσδοκών is a word that describes someone who is in a state of expectation or anticipation. It can be used to describe a person who is eagerly waiting for something to happen, such as a job offer or the arrival of a loved one.
