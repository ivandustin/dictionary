Word: πρεσβυτερου

Etymology: 
The word πρεσβυτερου (presbyterou) is derived from the Greek word πρεσβύτερος (presbyteros), which means "elder" or "senior". In the New Testament, the term was used to refer to the leaders of the early Christian church.

Grammar:
πρεσβυτερου is the genitive singular form of πρεσβύτερος (presbyteros). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. Therefore, πρεσβυτερου can be translated as "of the elder" or "of the senior".
