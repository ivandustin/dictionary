Word: προσμενειν

Etymology:
The word προσμενειν comes from the Greek verb προσμένω (pros-méno), which is a combination of the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward," and the verb μένω (méno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain." Therefore, προσμένω means "to stay toward" or "to remain with."

Grammar:
προσμενειν is the present participle form of the verb προσμένω. It is in the active voice and in the present tense. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: προσμένω (pros-méno) - I stay toward
- Dual: προσμένετον (pros-méneton) - We two stay toward
- Plural: προσμένομεν (pros-ménomen) - We stay toward

Description:
προσμενειν means "to wait for" or "to expect." It implies a sense of anticipation or readiness for something to happen. It can also mean "to remain with" or "to stay close to." The word is often used in a figurative sense, such as waiting for a decision or outcome, or remaining loyal to someone or something.
