Word: προεπηγγελμένην (proepēngelmenēn)

Etymology: The word προεπηγγελμένην comes from the Greek roots προ- (pro-), meaning "before," επηγγέλμην (epēngelmen), meaning "to promise," and the suffix -ην (-ēn), indicating the feminine accusative singular form. 

Grammar: προεπηγγελμένην is a feminine accusative singular form of the past participle of the verb επαγγέλλομαι (epangellomai), meaning "to promise beforehand." It is used to describe a feminine noun that is the object of the verb.

Description: The word προεπηγγελμένην is used in Greek literature to describe something that has been promised or foretold beforehand. It is often used in a religious or prophetic context, such as in the New Testament to describe the promised Messiah.
