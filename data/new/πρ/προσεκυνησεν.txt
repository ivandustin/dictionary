Word: προσεκυνησεν

Etymology: The word προσεκυνησεν comes from the Greek root words προς (pros) meaning "toward" and κυνέω (kyneo) meaning "to kiss". Together, they form the word προσκυνέω (proskuneo) which means "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence".

Grammar: προσεκυνησεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is singular and in the third person.

Description: προσεκυνησεν is a Greek word that means "he/she/it worshiped" or "he/she/it bowed down in reverence". It is often used in the context of religious worship or reverence for a deity. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe the act of worshiping Jesus Christ.
