Word: προσφέρεται

Etymology: The word προσφέρεται comes from the Greek verb προσφέρω (prosfero), which means "to offer" or "to present". The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates that the verb is in the passive voice.

Grammar: προσφέρεται is a third-person singular present passive indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb προσφέρω (prosfero), which is a first-conjugation verb. The verb stem is προσφέρ-, and the ending -εται (-etai) indicates the passive voice.

Description: προσφέρεται is a verb that means "is offered" or "is presented" in Greek. It is often used in the context of offering or presenting something to someone, such as a gift or a service. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is receiving the action of the verb, rather than performing it. For example, "Το φαγητό προσφέρεται στο τραπέζι" (To fagito prosferetai sto trapezi) means "The food is offered on the table".
