Word: προεδωκεν

Etymology:
The word προεδωκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δοκεω (dokeo), which means "to seem" or "to appear." The prefix προ- (pro-) means "before" or "in front of." Therefore, the word προεδωκεν can be translated as "he/she/it appeared beforehand" or "he/she/it seemed beforehand."

Grammar:
προεδωκεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

The word προεδωκεν is found in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark 14:44. In this passage, it is used to describe Judas Iscariot's betrayal of Jesus. The full verse reads: "And he that betrayed him had given them a token, saying, Whomsoever I shall kiss, that same is he; take him, and lead him away safely."
