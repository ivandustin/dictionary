Word: προσερηξεν

Etymology: The word προσερηξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πρός (pros), which means "towards" or "to", and ἐρήμωσις (erēmōsis), which means "desertion" or "abandonment". Therefore, the word προσερηξεν can be translated as "to cling to" or "to attach oneself to".

Grammar: προσερηξεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -εν (-en) to the stem προσερηξ- (proserēx-). The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: προσερήσκει (proserēskei)
- Imperfect tense: προσερήσκεν (proserēsken)
- Aorist tense: προσερηξεν (proserēxen)
- Perfect tense: προσερρώγηκεν (proserrōgēken)
- Pluperfect tense: προσερρώγει (proserrōgei)

Description: The word προσερηξεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark 10:7, where it describes the act of a man leaving his parents and "clinging to" his wife. The word conveys a sense of strong attachment and devotion. In modern Greek, the word is still used to describe a strong emotional connection or bond between people or things.
