Word: προσευχησθε

Etymology:
The word προσευχησθε (proseuchesthe) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words προς (pros) meaning "toward" and ευχη (euche) meaning "prayer". The verb form of the word means "to pray" or "to make a request to a deity".

Grammar:
προσευχησθε is a second person plural present passive imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to pray. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, in this case, by a deity.

Description:
προσευχησθε is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (6:9-13) and the Gospel of Luke (11:2-4). It is part of the Lord's Prayer, which is a prayer that Jesus taught his disciples. The word is a command to pray, and it emphasizes the importance of prayer in the Christian faith.
