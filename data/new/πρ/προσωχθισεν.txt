Word: προσωχθισεν

Etymology: The word προσωχθισεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πρός (pros), which means "towards" or "in addition to," and ώχθησεν (ochthēsen), which means "hated" or "detested." Therefore, the word προσωχθισεν can be translated as "to be hated in addition to" or "to be detested towards."

Grammar: προσωχθισεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -σεν (-sen) to the stem of the verb ώχθεω (ochtheō), which means "to hate" or "to detest." The prefix πρός (pros) is added to indicate the direction or intention of the action.

Description: προσωχθισεν is a strong verb that expresses a strong feeling of hatred or detestation towards someone or something. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the way the Pharisees and the religious leaders of the time felt towards Jesus Christ. The word implies a deep-seated animosity that goes beyond mere dislike or disagreement.
