Word: προσελθουσαι

Etymology: προσελθουσαι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, feminine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai), which means "to come near, approach, or arrive." The prefix προσ- (pros-) means "toward" or "near," and the verb stem is -έλθ- (-elth-).

Grammar: προσελθουσαι is the third person plural form of the aorist tense in the active voice. It is in the nominative case and feminine gender. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating the duration or repetition of the action.

Description: προσελθουσαι can be translated as "they approached" or "they came near." It is often used in the New Testament to describe people coming to Jesus or approaching him for healing or guidance. The word conveys a sense of purposeful movement towards a specific destination or person.
