Word: πρασσόντες

Etymology: πρασσόντες is a present participle of the Greek verb πράσσω (prassō), which means "to do" or "to act". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *per- which means "to bring forth, produce".

Grammar: πρασσόντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb πράσσω and indicates that the subject is currently doing or performing the action of the verb.

Description: πρασσόντες is used in Greek literature to describe people who are actively doing something. It can be translated as "doing", "performing", or "acting". The word is often used in a positive sense to describe people who are actively engaged in a task or activity.
