Word: προχειρισασθαι

Etymology: The word προχειρισασθαι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the verb χειρίζομαι (cheirizomai), meaning "to handle" or "to use." Therefore, the word προχειρισασθαι means "to handle beforehand" or "to prepare in advance."

Grammar: προχειρισασθαι is a verb in the aorist middle voice form in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is completed, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself. Therefore, the word προχειρισασθαι can be translated as "to have prepared oneself beforehand" or "to have handled something in advance."

Description: προχειρισασθαι is a word that implies preparation and foresight. It suggests that someone has taken the time to handle or prepare something in advance, rather than waiting until the last minute. This word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as preparing for a speech, organizing an event, or planning a project. It emphasizes the importance of being proactive and taking action before it is too late.
