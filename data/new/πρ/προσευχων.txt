Word: προσευχων

Etymology:
The word προσευχων (proseuchon) is a Greek word that comes from the verb προσεύχομαι (proseuchomai), which means "to pray" or "to offer prayers." The word is derived from the prefix προς (pros), which means "toward," and the verb εὔχομαι (euchomai), which means "to pray" or "to vow."

προσευχων is a noun in the genitive case, which means it is used to show possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun. In this case, it is the genitive singular form of the noun προσευχή (proseuche), which means "prayer" or "supplication." The word can also be used in the plural form, προσευχαί (proseuchai), to refer to a collection of prayers or a place of prayer.

προσευχων is a word that refers to someone who is engaged in prayer or someone who is devoted to prayer. It can also refer to a specific prayer or supplication that is being offered.
