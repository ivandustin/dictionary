Word: προπετεις

Etymology:
The word προπετεις (propeteis) is derived from the Greek words προ (pro) meaning "before" and πετεινος (peteinos) meaning "flying". The word originally referred to a bird that flew ahead of a flock, but later came to mean "rash" or "reckless".

Grammar:
προπετεις is an adjective in the masculine nominative plural form. It is used to describe someone who is rash or impulsive.

Description:
προπετεις is a word that describes someone who acts without thinking, often in a reckless or impulsive manner. It can be used to describe someone who takes unnecessary risks or acts without considering the consequences of their actions. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize someone's behavior.
