Word: προηλθον

Etymology:
The word προηλθον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come." Therefore, προηλθον means "I came before" or "I went ahead."

Grammar:
προηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb προέρχομαι (proerchomai), which means "to go before" or "to precede." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, without specifying the duration or repetition of the action.

Description:
προηλθον is a verb that describes an action of going ahead or coming before. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a race where one runner goes ahead of the others, or in a meeting where someone arrives before the others. The word emphasizes the idea of being first or leading the way.
