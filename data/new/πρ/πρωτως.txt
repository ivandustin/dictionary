Word: πρωτως

Etymology:
The word πρωτως (protos) is derived from the Greek word πρῶτος (prōtos), which means "first" or "foremost". It is a derivative of the Proto-Indo-European root *per-, which means "forward" or "in front".

Grammar:
πρωτως is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb. It is often translated as "firstly" or "primarily" in English.

Description:
πρωτως is used to introduce the first item in a list or sequence of events. It can also be used to emphasize the importance of a particular point or idea. For example, "πρωτως, πρέπει να κατανοήσουμε τη σημασία αυτού του ζητήματος" (Firstly, we must understand the importance of this issue).
