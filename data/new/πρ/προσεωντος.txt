Word: προσεωντος

Etymology:
The word προσεωντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near." The word is formed by adding the suffix -οντος (-ontos) to the present participle stem προσερχ- (proserch-).

Grammar:
προσεωντος is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai). In Greek, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship to another noun.

Description:
προσεωντος can be translated as "approaching" or "coming near" in English. It is often used in the context of describing someone who is approaching or coming near to someone or something else. For example, in the sentence "ὁ προσεωντος Ἰησοῦς" (ho proseontos Iesous), which means "Jesus who is approaching," the word προσεωντος describes the ongoing action of Jesus approaching.
