Word: προσευχης

Etymology:
The word προσευχης (proseuches) is derived from the Greek word προσευχή (proseuchē), which means "prayer" or "supplication." It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward," and the noun ευχή (euchē), meaning "prayer" or "vow."

Grammar:
προσευχης is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is the genitive form of προσευχή (proseuchē), meaning "of prayer" or "of supplication."

Description:
προσευχης is a Greek word that refers to prayer or supplication. It is often used in the context of religious worship, particularly in the Christian faith. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the act of praying to God, either individually or in a group. The word emphasizes the idea of approaching God with reverence and humility, and seeking His guidance and help.
