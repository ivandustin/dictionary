Word: πριν

Etymology:
The word πριν (prin) is a Greek adverb that means "before" or "previously." It is derived from the ancient Greek word πρό (pro), which means "before" or "in front of."

Grammar:
In Greek, πριν is used as a conjunction or a preposition. As a conjunction, it is used to introduce a subordinate clause that expresses an action that occurred before the main clause. As a preposition, it is used to indicate the time before a certain event or action.

Description:
πριν is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to express the concept of "before." It is used in a variety of contexts, from everyday conversation to formal writing. Its usage as a conjunction or preposition allows speakers and writers to convey the temporal relationship between two events or actions. Overall, πριν is an essential word in the Greek language that helps to clarify the sequence of events and actions.
