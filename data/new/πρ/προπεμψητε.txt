Word: προπεμψητε

Etymology: The word προπεμψητε comes from the Greek verb προπέμπω (propémpo), which means "to send forward" or "to escort." The suffix -τε (-te) indicates the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or orders.

Grammar: προπεμψητε is the second person plural imperative form of the verb προπέμπω. It is used to give a command or order to a group of people, telling them to "send forward" or "escort" someone or something.

Description: προπεμψητε is a Greek verb that is used in a specific context, typically in formal or official situations where someone is being sent off or escorted. It is not commonly used in everyday conversation, but it is an important word to know for those studying the Greek language or working in fields where knowledge of Greek is required.
