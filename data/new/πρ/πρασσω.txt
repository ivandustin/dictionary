Word: πρασσω

Etymology:
The word πρασσω (prassō) is a Greek verb that means "to do" or "to accomplish." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *per- which means "to bring forth, produce."

Grammar:
πρασσω is a present active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Πρασσω (prassō) - I do
- Πραττεις (pratteis) - You do
- Πρασσει (prassei) - He/she/it does

Plural:
- Πρασσομεν (prassomen) - We do
- Πραττετε (prattete) - You (all) do
- Πρασσουσι(ν) (prassousi(n)) - They do

Description:
πρασσω is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe the act of doing or accomplishing something. It can be used to describe physical actions, such as "I am doing my homework," or more abstract actions, such as "I am doing my best." It can also be used to describe the act of achieving a goal or completing a task, such as "I have accomplished my mission." Overall, πρασσω is a fundamental verb in the Greek language that is used frequently in everyday conversation.
