Word: προκριματος

Etymology:
The word προκριματος comes from the Greek prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the noun κριμα (krima), meaning "judgment" or "decision." Together, they form the compound word προκριματος, which means "preliminary judgment" or "preliminary decision."

Grammar:
In Greek, προκριματος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. Its genitive singular form is προκριματου, and its accusative singular form is προκριματον. In the plural form, it is declined as προκριματοι (nominative), προκριματων (genitive), and προκριματους (accusative).

Description:
The word προκριματος refers to a preliminary judgment or decision made before a final decision is reached. It can be used in legal contexts, such as in a court case where a judge may make a preliminary ruling before the final verdict is given. It can also be used in other contexts, such as in sports competitions where preliminary rounds are held before the final round.
