Word: προσευχαις

Etymology:
The word προσευχαις (proseuchais) is a plural form of the Greek noun προσευχή (proseuchē), which means "prayer" or "supplication." The word is derived from the verb προσεύχομαι (proseuchomai), which means "to pray" or "to make a request."

Grammar:
προσευχαις is a plural noun in the dative case in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "to prayers" or "for prayers."

Description:
προσευχαις is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to prayers or supplications made by individuals or groups of people. It is often used in the context of worship or seeking guidance from God. The word emphasizes the act of making a request or petition to a higher power.
