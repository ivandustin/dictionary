Word: προσκυνει

Etymology: The word προσκυνει comes from the Greek verb προσκυνέω (proskynéō), which means "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence." The word is derived from the prefix προσ- (pros-), meaning "toward," and the verb κυνέω (kynéō), meaning "to kiss."

Grammar: προσκυνει is the third person singular present tense form of the verb προσκυνέω (proskynéō). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person of the subject. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person singular form indicates that the subject is he/she/it.

Description: προσκυνει is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it worships" or "he/she/it bows down in reverence." It is often used in a religious context to describe the act of worshiping a deity or showing reverence to a sacred object. The word can also be used more broadly to describe showing respect or deference to someone in authority.
