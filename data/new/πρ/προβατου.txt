Word: προβατου

Etymology:
The word προβατου (pronounced "probatou") is a Greek word that comes from the root word πρόβατον (pronounced "probaton"), which means "sheep". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁éwi, which means "sheep" or "lamb".

Grammar:
προβατου is the genitive singular form of the word πρόβατον. In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns. So, προβατου can be translated as "of the sheep" or "belonging to the sheep".

Description:
προβατου is a specific form of the word πρόβατον, which refers to a domesticated mammal that is commonly raised for its wool, meat, and milk. Sheep have been domesticated for thousands of years and are found all over the world. In Greek mythology, sheep were often associated with the god Apollo and were used in sacrifices and rituals.
