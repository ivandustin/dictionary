Word: προελθων

Etymology:
The word προελθων is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words: προ (pro) meaning "before" or "in front of" and ελθων (elthon) meaning "coming" or "arriving". Therefore, the word προελθων means "having come before" or "having arrived earlier".

Grammar:
προελθων is a participle in the aorist tense, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb προέρχομαι (proerchomai) which means "to come forth" or "to go forward". The participle form προελθων is used to describe a person or thing that has come before or arrived earlier.

Description:
The word προελθων is often used in the context of time, to describe something that has happened or occurred before something else. It can also be used to describe a person who has arrived earlier than expected or someone who has come before others. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo is often referred to as προελθων, as he was believed to have come before other gods and goddesses.
