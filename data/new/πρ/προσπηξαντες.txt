Word: προσπηξαντες

Etymology: The word προσπηξαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προσ- (pros-), meaning "towards" or "in addition to," and the verb πήγνυμι (pēgnymi), meaning "to fasten" or "to fix." Therefore, προσπηξαντες means "having fastened towards" or "having added to the fastening."

Grammar: προσπηξαντες is the aorist participle of the Greek verb προσπήγνυμι (prospēgnymi), which means "to fasten to" or "to attach to." The aorist tense indicates that the action of fastening or attaching has been completed in the past. The participle form is used to describe the subject of the sentence, which in this case is plural.

Description: προσπηξαντες can be translated as "having fastened towards" or "having added to the fastening." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a group of people who have united or joined together for a common purpose. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the disciples of Jesus who were "fastened towards" or united in their faith and commitment to him.
