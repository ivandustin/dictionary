Word: προσοφειλεις

Etymology: The word προσοφειλεις is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix προσο- (meaning "towards" or "in addition to") and the verb ὀφείλω (ophéilō) which means "to owe". Therefore, the word προσοφειλεις can be translated as "to owe in addition to" or "to owe towards".

Grammar: προσοφειλεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the verb προσοφείλω (prosoféilō), which means "to owe in addition to" or "to owe towards". It is a regular verb in Greek and follows the conjugation pattern of the verb ὀφείλω (ophéilō).

Description: The word προσοφειλεις is used to describe a situation where someone owes something in addition to what they already owe. It can also be used to describe a situation where someone owes something to a specific person or entity. In modern Greek, it is commonly used in legal contexts to describe debts or obligations.
