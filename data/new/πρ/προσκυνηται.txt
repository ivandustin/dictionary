Word: προσκυνηται

Etymology:
The word προσκυνηται (proskynetai) is derived from the Greek verb προσκυνέω (proskynéo), which means "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence." The suffix "-ται" (-tai) indicates that the word is in the plural form.

Grammar:
προσκυνηται is a noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or the predicate nominative.

Description:
προσκυνηται refers to a group of people who worship or bow down in reverence. In Greek culture, it was common to prostrate oneself before a deity or a person of high status as a sign of respect and submission. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is a devoted follower or admirer of a particular person or ideology.
