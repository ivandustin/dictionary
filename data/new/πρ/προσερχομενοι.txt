Word: προσερχομενοι

Etymology: προσερχομενοι is a Greek verb that comes from the root words πρός (pros) meaning "toward" and ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". It is a present participle form of the verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai) which means "to approach" or "to come near".

Grammar: προσερχομενοι is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently approaching or coming near to something or someone. In Greek, the present participle is often used to indicate ongoing action or a continuous state.

Description: προσερχομενοι can be translated to "approaching" or "coming near". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe people who are approaching Jesus or coming near to God.
