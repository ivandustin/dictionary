Word: προγινωσκοντες

Etymology: 
The word προγινωσκοντες is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before," and the verb γινώσκω (ginósko), meaning "to know." Therefore, προγινωσκοντες means "knowing beforehand" or "foreseeing."

Grammar:
προγινωσκοντες is the present participle form of the verb προγινώσκω (proginósko), which means "to know beforehand" or "to foresee." The present participle form indicates that the action of knowing beforehand is ongoing or continuous. It is in the active voice and masculine gender, agreeing with the subject of the sentence.

Description:
προγινωσκοντες is a word that describes the action of knowing or foreseeing something before it happens. It implies a sense of anticipation or preparation for future events. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as predicting the outcome of a situation, anticipating the needs of others, or preparing for a future event. It is a useful word for expressing foresight and preparedness.
