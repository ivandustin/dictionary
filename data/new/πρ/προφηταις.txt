Word: προφηταῖς (prophētais)

Etymology: The word προφηταῖς comes from the Greek root word προφήτης (prophētēs), which means "prophet" or "one who speaks for a deity." The suffix -αις (-ais) indicates that the word is in the dative plural form, meaning "to/for the prophets."

Grammar: προφηταῖς is a feminine noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. In this case, the word is used to refer to "the prophets" or "to/for the prophets."

Description: προφηταῖς is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the prophets, specifically in the context of prophesying or speaking on behalf of God. It is used in various passages, such as Acts 2:17, which quotes the prophet Joel: "In the last days, God says, I will pour out my Spirit on all people. Your sons and daughters will prophesy, your young men will see visions, your old men will dream dreams."
