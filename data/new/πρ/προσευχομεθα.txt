Word: προσευχομεθα

Etymology:
The word προσευχομεθα comes from the Greek verb προσεύχομαι (proseúchomai), which means "to pray" or "to offer prayers." The word is derived from the prefix προς- (pros-), which means "toward," and the verb εὔχομαι (eúchomai), which means "to pray" or "to wish."

Grammar:
προσευχομεθα is a first-person plural present indicative passive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -μεθα (-metha) to the stem προσευχο- (proseucho-). The verb is passive, indicating that the subject is being prayed for or is the recipient of prayer.

Description:
προσευχομεθα is a Greek verb that means "we are being prayed for" or "we are the recipients of prayer." It is often used in a religious context to refer to the act of praying for someone or asking for prayers for oneself. The verb emphasizes the idea of being the object of prayer, rather than the act of praying itself.
