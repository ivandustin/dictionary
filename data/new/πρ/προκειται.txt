Word: προκειται

Etymology:
The word προκειται (prokeitai) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: προ (pro), meaning "before" or "in front of," and κειμαι (keimai), meaning "to lie" or "to be situated." Therefore, the literal meaning of προκειται is "to lie before" or "to be situated in front of."

Grammar:
προκειται is a third-person singular present indicative active verb in Greek. It is often used as an impersonal verb, meaning that it does not have a specific subject. In this case, it is translated as "it is" or "there is/are." For example, "προκειται για μια δύσκολη απόφαση" (prokeitai gia mia dyskoli apofasi) means "it is a difficult decision."

Description:
προκειται is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to express the idea of something being situated or located in front of someone or something. It can also be used to introduce a topic or a situation that is about to happen or be discussed. In addition, it is often used in formal or academic writing to express a fact or a statement that is considered to be true or certain.
