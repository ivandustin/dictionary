The word "προσευχην" (proseuchēn) is a Greek noun that means "prayer" or "supplication." It is derived from the verb "προσεύχομαι" (proseuchomai), which means "to pray" or "to make a request." In Greek grammar, "προσευχην" is in the accusative case, indicating that it is the direct object of a verb or preposition. 

In Christian theology, "προσευχην" is often used to refer to the act of communicating with God through prayer. It is considered an important aspect of spiritual life and is often practiced in both private and communal settings. 

Overall, "προσευχην" is a significant word in Greek language and culture, with deep religious and spiritual connotations.
