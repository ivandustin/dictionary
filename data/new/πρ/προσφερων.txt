Word: προσφερων

Etymology: προσφερων is a present participle of the Greek verb προσφέρω (prosfero), which means "to offer" or "to bring forward". The word is derived from the combination of the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward", and the verb φέρω (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bring".

Grammar: προσφερων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently offering or bringing something forward. In English, it can be translated as "offering" or "bringing".

Description: προσφερων is a word that describes someone who is actively offering or bringing something forward. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in religious ceremonies where offerings are made to the gods, or in everyday situations where someone is offering help or assistance to another person.
