Word: προσευχεσθε

Etymology:
The word προσευχεσθε (proseuchesthe) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words πρός (pros) meaning "toward" and εὐχή (euchē) meaning "prayer". Therefore, the word προσευχεσθε means "to pray" or "to make a prayer".

Grammar:
προσευχεσθε is a second-person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to pray. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
προσευχεσθε is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew 6:9-13 and Luke 11:2-4. In these passages, Jesus teaches his disciples how to pray and uses the word προσευχεσθε to instruct them to pray. The word emphasizes the importance of directing one's prayers towards God and seeking His guidance and blessings. Today, the word is still used in Christian contexts as a call to prayer and a reminder of the power of prayer in one's spiritual life.
