Word: πραθηναι

Etymology:
The word πραθηναι (prathenai) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means "to do business" or "to trade." It is derived from the noun πραγμα (pragma), which means "a thing done" or "a transaction."

Grammar:
πραθηναι is a second-person plural aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to perform an action in the past. The verb is conjugated from the root πραγμα (pragma) and the aorist tense ending -ητε (-ete).

Description:
πραθηναι is a verb that is used to command a group of people to engage in business or trade. It is often used in the context of making deals or transactions. The word has a sense of urgency and implies that the action should be taken immediately. In modern Greek, the word has evolved to mean "to negotiate" or "to bargain."
