Word: προσενεχθεις

Etymology:
The word προσενεχθεις is a Greek word that is derived from the verb προσφέρω (prosfero), which means "to offer" or "to bring forward." The prefix προσ- (pros-) means "toward" or "to," while the verb φέρω (fero) means "to carry" or "to bring."

Grammar:
προσενεχθεις is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The participle mood is used to describe a verb as an adjective.

Description:
προσενεχθεις can be translated to mean "having been brought forward" or "having been offered." It is often used in a religious context to describe a sacrifice or offering that has been presented to a deity. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the sacrifice of Jesus Christ on the cross.
