Word: προσαγειν

Etymology:
The word προσαγειν (prosagein) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward," and the verb ἄγειν (agein), meaning "to lead." Therefore, προσαγειν means "to lead towards" or "to bring to."

Grammar:
προσαγειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to a specific tense or person. It is often used in combination with other verbs to form compound verbs, such as προσαγειν εις (prosagein eis), meaning "to bring into."

Description:
προσαγειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physically leading or bringing someone or something towards a specific location or person. It can also be used metaphorically to describe leading or bringing someone towards a specific idea or belief. In addition, it can be used in a legal context to refer to bringing a case or charge against someone.
