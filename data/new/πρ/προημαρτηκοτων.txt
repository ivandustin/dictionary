Word: προημαρτηκοτων

Etymology: The word προημαρτηκοτων is derived from the Greek words προ- (pro-), meaning "before," and ἁμαρτάνω (hamartanō), meaning "to sin." Therefore, the word can be translated to mean "those who have sinned beforehand" or "previously sinning."

Grammar: προημαρτηκοτων is a Greek adjective in the masculine plural form. It is a compound word made up of the prefix προ- and the verb ἁμαρτάνω in the perfect participle form.

Description: The word προημαρτηκοτων is used in Christian theology to refer to those who have sinned before their conversion to Christianity. It is often used in the context of the doctrine of original sin, which teaches that all humans are born with a sinful nature inherited from Adam and Eve. In this context, προημαρτηκοτων refers to the sins committed before a person's conversion to Christianity, which are believed to be forgiven through faith in Jesus Christ.
