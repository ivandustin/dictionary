The word "προσκεκλημαι" (proskeklēmai) is a Greek verb that means "I have been called" or "I have been invited." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to," and the verb "καλέω" (kaleō), meaning "I call." The perfect tense form "προσκεκλημαι" indicates that the action of being called or invited has already been completed in the past.

Grammar: "προσκεκλημαι" is a first-person singular perfect tense verb in the indicative mood. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance in the present.

Description: The word "προσκεκλημαι" is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe being called or invited by God to participate in his kingdom. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe being invited to a social event or gathering.
