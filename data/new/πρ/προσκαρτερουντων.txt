Word: προσκαρτερουντων

Etymology: The word προσκαρτερουντων is derived from the Greek verb προσκαρτερέω (proskartereō), which means "to adhere to" or "to persist in". The suffix -ουντων (-ountōn) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently adhering to or persisting in something.

Grammar: προσκαρτερουντων is a present participle in the genitive case, indicating possession or association. It is the plural form of the present participle, indicating that it refers to multiple people who are currently adhering to or persisting in something.

Description: προσκαρτερουντων can be translated as "of those who are adhering to" or "of those who are persisting in". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to people who are devoted to a particular belief or practice.
