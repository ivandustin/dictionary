Word: προειρηκέν

Etymology:
The word προειρηκέν (proeirēken) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in advance," and the verb εἴρηκα (eirēka), meaning "I have said" or "I have spoken." Therefore, the word προειρηκέν can be translated as "I have said beforehand" or "I have previously stated."

Grammar:
προειρηκέν is a perfect tense verb in Greek, which means that it describes an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The verb is in the third person singular, which means that it refers to a single person or thing that has previously spoken or stated something.

Description:
The word προειρηκέν is used in Greek literature and philosophy to refer to a statement or argument that has been made previously, often in the context of a debate or discussion. It is often used to introduce a quotation or to refer to a previous statement made by the speaker or another person. In some cases, it can also be used to refer to a prophecy or prediction that has been made in the past.
