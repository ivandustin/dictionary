Word: προφητεία (propheteia)

Etymology: The word προφητεία comes from the Greek word προφήτης (prophetes), which means "one who speaks for a god". The suffix -εία (-eia) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the quality or state of being something.

Grammar: προφητεία is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form, but it can also be used in the plural form (προφητείες). It is declined like other feminine nouns in Greek.

Description: προφητεία refers to the act of prophesying or the message that is delivered by a prophet. In ancient Greek culture, prophets were believed to have the ability to communicate with the gods and to predict future events. The word is often used in a religious context, but it can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of prediction or foretelling of the future.
