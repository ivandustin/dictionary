Word: προτερον

Etymology:
The word προτερον comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word πρότερος (próteros), which means "former" or "earlier". The suffix "-ον" is added to the end of the word to make it an adverb, meaning "previously" or "beforehand".

Grammar:
In Greek, προτερον is an adverb. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate that something happened previously or beforehand. It is often used in conjunction with the aorist tense in Greek, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
προτερον is a useful word in Greek for indicating that something happened previously or beforehand. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in storytelling, historical accounts, or personal narratives. Its etymology suggests that it has been in use for a long time, and it remains a common word in modern Greek.
