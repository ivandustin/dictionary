Word: προσκαρτερησει

Etymology: The word προσκαρτερησει comes from the Greek verb προσκαρτερέω (proskarteréo), which means "to adhere to, to persist in, to continue steadfastly." It is derived from the prefix προσ- (pros-), meaning "toward," and the verb καρτερέω (karteréo), meaning "to be strong, to endure."

Grammar: προσκαρτερησει is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb προσκαρτερέω (proskarteréo).

Description: προσκαρτερησει means "he/she/it will persist" or "he/she/it will continue steadfastly" in English. It is often used to describe someone who is determined and unwavering in their pursuit of a goal or objective. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is persistent and does not give up easily.
