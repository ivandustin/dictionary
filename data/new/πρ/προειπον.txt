Word: προειπον

Etymology:
The word προειπον is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the verb εἶπον (eipon), meaning "I said." Therefore, προειπον means "I said beforehand" or "I foretold."

Grammar:
προειπον is the first person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb προεῖπον (proeipon). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice means that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
προειπον is a word that is used in Greek literature to describe a past action of foretelling or predicting something before it happened. It is often used in the context of prophecy or divination. In the New Testament, it is used to describe Jesus' predictions of his own death and resurrection. Overall, προειπον is a word that conveys a sense of foresight and knowledge of future events.
