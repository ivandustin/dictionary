Word: προσελαβετο

Etymology:
The word προσελαβετο is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward" or "in addition to," and the verb ἔλαβεν (elaben), meaning "to take" or "to receive." Therefore, προσελαβετο can be translated as "he/she/it received in addition to" or "he/she/it took on."

Grammar:
προσελαβετο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon itself or is affected by the action. In this case, the subject received or took on something in addition to something else.

προσελαβετο is a verb that implies an action of receiving or taking on something in addition to something else. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly was received or taken on.
