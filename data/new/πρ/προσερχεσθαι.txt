Word: προσερχεσθαι

Etymology: προσερχεσθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πρός (pros), which means "towards" or "to," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come." Therefore, προσερχεσθαι means "to come towards" or "to approach."

Grammar: προσερχεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive verb in Greek. It is often translated as "to come," "to approach," or "to draw near" in English. In its middle voice, it can also mean "to come to oneself" or "to come back."

Description: προσερχεσθαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe people coming to Jesus or approaching God in prayer. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone coming towards or approaching something or someone else. In its middle voice, it can describe someone coming to their senses or returning to a previous state.
