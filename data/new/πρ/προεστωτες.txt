Word: προεστωτες

Etymology:
The word προεστωτες (proestotes) is a Greek word that is derived from the prefix προ- (pro-) meaning "before" or "in front of" and the verb εἰμί (eimi) meaning "to be". The suffix -της (-tes) is a nominal suffix that forms nouns indicating a person who performs a certain action or has a certain quality.

Grammar:
προεστωτες is the plural form of the Greek adjective προεστώς (proestos), which means "present" or "existing". It is used to describe something that is currently happening or existing. The word is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description:
προεστωτες is a term used in Greek grammar to refer to the present tense of verbs. In Greek, the present tense is used to describe actions that are currently happening or ongoing. The term προεστωτες is used to describe the subject of a sentence when it is in the present tense. For example, in the sentence "Οι μαθητές προεστωτες διαβάζουν το βιβλίο" (The students are currently reading the book), the word προεστωτες refers to the students as the subject of the sentence and indicates that they are currently reading the book.
