Word: προσερχομενου

Etymology: 
The word προσερχομενου comes from the Greek verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". It is a compound word made up of the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward", and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar:
προσερχομενου is the present participle form of the verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai). In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. It can function as an adjective, modifying a noun, or as a verb, taking on the tense and voice of the main verb in the sentence.

Description:
προσερχομενου can be translated as "approaching" or "coming near". It is often used in a religious context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, to describe the act of approaching God or a sacred place. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone or something coming closer to a particular location or person.
