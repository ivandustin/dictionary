Word: προσενεγκας

Etymology:
The word προσενεγκας is a Greek verb that comes from the root words πρός (pros) meaning "towards" and φέρω (phero) meaning "to carry". The word is formed by adding the prefix εν (en) which means "in" or "into" to the verb stem.

Grammar:
προσενεγκας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb προσφέρω (prosphero) which means "to offer" or "to bring". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
προσενεγκας can be translated as "you offered" or "you brought". The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of bringing an offering or sacrifice to God. It is often used in the context of worship or religious ceremonies.
