Word: προστεταγμένους

Etymology:
The word προστεταγμένους is a Greek word that comes from the verb προστάττω (prostátto), which means "to command" or "to order." The word is formed by adding the prefix προς (pros), which means "toward," and the perfect participle ending -τεταγμένος (-tetagménos), which means "having been ordered."

Grammar:
προστεταγμένους is a plural masculine accusative participle in Greek. It is declined as follows:

- Case: accusative
- Gender: masculine
- Number: plural
- Part of speech: participle

Description:
The word προστεταγμένους is used to describe a group of people who have been commanded or ordered to do something. It can be translated as "those who have been ordered" or "those who have been commanded." The word is often used in a military context to refer to soldiers who have been given orders to carry out a mission or task.
