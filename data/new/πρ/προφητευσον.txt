Word: προφητεύσον

Etymology: The word προφητεύσον comes from the Greek verb προφητεύω (prophēteuō), which means "to prophesy" or "to predict". The word is derived from the noun προφήτης (prophētēs), which means "prophet".

Grammar: προφητεύσον is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular. It is the aorist imperative form of the verb προφητεύω (prophēteuō).

Description: προφητεύσον is a command to someone to prophesy or predict something. It is often used in religious contexts, where a prophet is believed to receive divine revelations and communicate them to others. In the New Testament, Jesus is often referred to as a prophet, and his followers are encouraged to prophesy as well. The word can also be used more generally to mean "to foretell" or "to predict".
