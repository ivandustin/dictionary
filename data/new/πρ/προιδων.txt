Word: προιδων

Etymology:
The word προιδων (proidon) is a Greek word that comes from the verb προοράω (proorao), which means "to foresee" or "to anticipate." The prefix προ- (pro-) means "before," and οράω (orao) means "to see."

προιδων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is the present participle of the verb προοράω (proorao), which means "foreseeing" or "anticipating." In Greek, present participles are often used as adjectives, and they decline like first and second declension adjectives.

Description:
προιδων is a word that describes someone who has the ability to foresee or anticipate future events. It is often used in a prophetic or divinatory context, and can refer to a seer or prophet.
