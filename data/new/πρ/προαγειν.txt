Word: προαγειν

Etymology:
The word προαγειν comes from the Greek root words προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and ἄγειν (agein), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." Therefore, προαγειν means "to lead forward" or "to bring forward."

Grammar:
προαγειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present active infinitive form, which means that it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. Instead, it is used to express the action of leading forward or bringing forward in a general sense.

Description:
προαγειν is a word that describes the action of leading or bringing something forward. It can be used in a literal sense, such as leading a group of people forward, or in a figurative sense, such as bringing forward an idea or proposal. The word emphasizes the idea of moving something forward, whether it be a physical object or an abstract concept.
