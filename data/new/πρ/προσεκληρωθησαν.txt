Word: προσεκληρωθησαν

Etymology: The word προσεκληρωθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κληρονομέω (klēronomeō), which means "to inherit." The prefix προσ- (pros-) means "towards" or "in addition to." Therefore, the word προσεκληρωθησαν can be translated as "they were added to the inheritance" or "they were included in the inheritance."

Grammar: προσεκληρωθησαν is a third-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact. 

Description: προσεκληρωθησαν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Acts 2:41. The verse reads, "Those who accepted his message were baptized, and about three thousand were added to their number that day." The word προσεκληρωθησαν is used to describe the act of being added to the number of believers who were baptized on that day.
