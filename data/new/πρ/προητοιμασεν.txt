Word: προητοιμασεν

Etymology: προ- (pro-, meaning "before") + ἡτοιμάζω (hētoimázō, meaning "to prepare"). Therefore, προητοιμασεν means "he/she/it prepared beforehand" in Greek.

Grammar: προητοιμασεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb προητοιμάζω (proētoimázō). In Greek, the aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: προητοιμασεν is a Greek verb that describes the act of preparing something beforehand. It is a specific form of the verb προητοιμάζω, which can be used in a variety of contexts. The word can be broken down into its component parts, with προ- indicating that the preparation occurred before something else, and ἡτοιμάζω indicating the act of preparing. Overall, προητοιμασεν is a useful word for describing actions that were taken in advance of a particular event or situation.
