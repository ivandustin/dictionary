Word: προφητεύσουσιν

Etymology: The word προφητεύσουσιν comes from the Greek verb προφητεύω (prophēteuō), which means "to prophesy" or "to foretell." The word is derived from the noun προφήτης (prophētēs), which means "prophet."

Grammar: προφητεύσουσιν is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. It is used to describe an action that will happen in the future, and it refers to a group of people who will prophesy.

Description: προφητεύσουσιν is a word that is often used in religious contexts, particularly in the Bible. It is used to describe the act of prophesying or foretelling future events. In the Bible, prophets were individuals who were believed to have received messages from God and were able to communicate these messages to others.
