The word "προσκεκληται" is a Greek verb that is in the perfect passive participle form. It comes from the root verb "προσκαλέω" which means "to call to oneself" or "to invite". The perfect passive participle form indicates that the action of being called or invited has already been completed and the subject is in a state resulting from that action.

In terms of grammar, "προσκεκληται" is in the third person plural form, indicating that the subject is a group of people who have been called or invited. The word is also in the passive voice, indicating that the subject received the action of being called or invited rather than performing the action themselves.

Overall, "προσκεκληται" can be translated to mean "they have been called" or "they have been invited".
