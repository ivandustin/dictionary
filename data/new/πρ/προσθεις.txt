Word: προσθεις

Etymology:
The word προσθεις (prostheis) is derived from the Greek verb προστίθημι (prostithemi), which means "to add" or "to place upon". The suffix -εις (-eis) is a masculine nominative singular ending, indicating that the word is a noun.

Grammar:
προσθεις is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
προσθεις is a term used in mathematics to refer to an addend, which is a number or quantity that is added to another number or quantity. It is commonly used in the context of addition and arithmetic operations. In Greek, the word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to something that is added or appended to something else.
