Word: προθεσμίας

Etymology: The word προθεσμίας comes from the Greek root words πρό (pro), meaning "before," and θεσμός (thesmos), meaning "law" or "custom." Together, they form the word προθεσμίας, which means "deadline" or "time limit."

Grammar: προθεσμίας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns. For example, "το βιβλίο της προθεσμίας" (to vivlio tis prothesmias) means "the book of the deadline."

Description: προθεσμίας refers to a specific time limit or deadline that must be met. It is commonly used in legal or business contexts, such as filing taxes or submitting a project proposal. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any time limit or deadline, such as a due date for a school assignment.
