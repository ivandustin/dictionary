Word: προβατια

Etymology:
The word προβατια (pronounced "provatia") is derived from the Greek word "πρόβατο" (pronounced "provato"), which means "sheep". The suffix "-ια" is added to indicate a plural form.

Grammar:
προβατια is a feminine noun in Greek and is used in the plural form. It is often used to refer to a group of sheep.

Description:
προβατια is a common word in Greek, especially in rural areas where sheep farming is prevalent. It is often used in everyday conversation to refer to a group of sheep or to describe a sheep-related activity. In Greek mythology, sheep were often sacrificed to the gods, and the word προβατια is also used in this context.
