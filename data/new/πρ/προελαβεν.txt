Word: προελαβεν

Etymology: προ- (pro-, meaning "before") + ἐλαβεν (elaben, meaning "took")

Grammar: προελαβεν is the aorist tense of the Greek verb προλαμβάνω (prolambanō), which means "to take beforehand" or "to anticipate." It is a third-person singular form.

Description: προελαβεν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John (1:15). It is used to describe John the Baptist's testimony about Jesus, saying that Jesus "came after me, but he has surpassed me because he was before me." The word emphasizes the idea that Jesus existed before John the Baptist, and that his coming was anticipated or foreseen.
