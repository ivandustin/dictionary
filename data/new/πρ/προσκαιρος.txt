Word: προσκαιρος

Etymology:
The word προσκαιρος comes from the Greek words πρός (pros) meaning "toward" or "with" and καιρός (kairos) meaning "time" or "opportunity". Therefore, the word προσκαιρος can be translated as "with time" or "temporary".

Grammar:
προσκαιρος is an adjective in Greek language. It is declined in the same way as other adjectives in Greek, according to gender, number and case. For example, in the nominative case, the masculine form is προσκαιρος, the feminine form is προσκαιρα and the neuter form is προσκαιρον.

Description:
προσκαιρος is used to describe something that is temporary or fleeting. It can refer to a temporary situation, a temporary state of mind, or a temporary feeling. For example, if someone is feeling sad, but they know that it is only temporary, they might describe their feelings as προσκαιρος. Similarly, if someone is in a temporary job, they might describe their employment as προσκαιρος. The word can also be used to describe something that is not permanent, such as a temporary solution to a problem.
