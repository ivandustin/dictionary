The word "προθυμίαν" (prothymían) is a noun in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the noun "θυμός" (thymós), meaning "passion" or "temper." Therefore, "προθυμίαν" can be translated as "pre-passion" or "pre-temper."

Grammar: The word is in the accusative case, indicating that it is the direct object of a verb in a sentence. 

Description: The word can be used to describe a state of eagerness or willingness to do something. It can also refer to a predisposition or inclination towards a particular action or attitude.
