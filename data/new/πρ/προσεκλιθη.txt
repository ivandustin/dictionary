Word: προσεκλιθη

Etymology:
The word προσεκλιθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word κλίνω (klinó), which means "to lean" or "to incline". The prefix προσ- (pros-) means "towards" or "in addition to". Therefore, προσεκλιθη can be translated as "leaned towards" or "inclined towards".

Grammar:
προσεκλιθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
προσεκλιθη is a verb that describes an action of leaning or inclining towards something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as leaning towards a person or object, or in a figurative sense, such as leaning towards a particular opinion or belief. The word is often used in a passive voice, indicating that the subject is being influenced or moved by something external.
