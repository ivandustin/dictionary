Word: πρωτευων

Etymology:
The word πρωτευων (prōteuōn) is derived from the Greek words πρῶτος (prōtos) meaning "first" and εὐώνυμος (euōnumos) meaning "having a good name". It is a compound word that means "having a good reputation" or "being of good standing".

Grammar:
πρωτευων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the present participle of the verb πρωτεύω (prōteuō), which means "to be first" or "to hold the first place". The word can be declined in the different cases, numbers, and genders of the Greek language.

Description:
πρωτευων is a word that describes someone who is of high standing or has a good reputation. In ancient Greece, it was used to refer to the leader or ruler of a city-state or a group of people. The word is often used in a positive sense to describe someone who is respected and admired by others. It can also be used in a negative sense to describe someone who is arrogant or self-important.
