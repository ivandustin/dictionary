Word: προσδοκώντες

Etymology: The word προσδοκώντες comes from the Greek verb προσδοκάω (prosdokao), which means "to expect" or "to anticipate". The suffix -ντες (-ntes) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, which in this case means "expecting" or "anticipating".

Grammar: προσδοκώντες is the present participle of the verb προσδοκάω. It is a masculine plural form, which means it refers to a group of people who are expecting or anticipating something. In Greek, the present participle can be used as an adjective, describing the state or action of the subject. For example, προσδοκώντες άνθρωποι (prosdokontes anthropoi) means "people who are expecting".

Description: προσδοκώντες is a word that describes a state of anticipation or expectation. It can be used to describe a group of people who are eagerly waiting for something to happen, such as the arrival of a loved one or the start of an event. The word conveys a sense of excitement and anticipation, as well as a certain level of uncertainty or anxiety about what will happen next.
