Word: προπεμποντων

Etymology: προ- (pro-, meaning "before" or "in front of") + πέμπω (pempo, meaning "to send") + -οντ- (-ont-, a present participle suffix) = προπεμπόντων (propempon-ton), meaning "those who are sending before" or "those who are escorting ahead".

Grammar: προπεμπόντων is the present participle form of the verb προπέμπω (propempo), which means "to send before" or "to escort ahead". It is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship to another noun in the sentence.

Description: προπεμπόντων is a Greek word that describes a group of people who are sending or escorting someone or something ahead of them. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as military operations, diplomatic missions, or personal travel. The word is formed from the combination of the prefix προ- (pro-, meaning "before" or "in front of") and the verb πέμπω (pempo, meaning "to send"), with the addition of the present participle suffix -οντ- (-ont-).
