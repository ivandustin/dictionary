Word: προσκυνησαντες

Etymology:
The word προσκυνησαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word προσκυνέω (proskynéō), which means "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence." The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is in the past tense and that the subject is plural.

Grammar:
προσκυνησαντες is a past participle verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is in the masculine gender and plural number, which means that the subject of the verb is a group of men.

Description:
προσκυνησαντες is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (2:11). It describes the action of the Magi, who came to worship the newborn Jesus and bowed down before him in reverence.
