Word: προσευχεται

Etymology:
The word προσευχεται (proseuchetai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words προς (pros) meaning "toward" and ευχη (euche) meaning "prayer". Therefore, the word προσευχεται means "to pray" or "to offer a prayer".

Grammar:
προσευχεται is a present tense, third person singular verb in Greek. It is often translated as "he/she/it prays" in English. The verb is conjugated from the root word προσευχή (proseuchē), which means "prayer" or "supplication".

προσευχεται is a word that is commonly used in Christian religious contexts to refer to the act of praying or offering a prayer. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the actions of Jesus and his followers.
