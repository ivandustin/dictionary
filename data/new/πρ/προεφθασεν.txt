Word: προεφθάσεν

Etymology:
The word προεφθάσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before," and the verb εφθάσεν (ephthasen), meaning "arrived" or "came." Therefore, the word προεφθάσεν means "arrived beforehand" or "came before."

Grammar:
προεφθάσεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
προεφθάσεν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 1:15. The verse reads: "John testified concerning him. He cried out, saying, 'This is the one I spoke about when I said, "He who comes after me has surpassed me because he was before me."'"

In this context, προεφθάσεν is used to describe the pre-existence of Jesus Christ. John the Baptist is testifying that Jesus existed before him, even though Jesus was born after John. The word emphasizes the idea that Jesus is not just a human being, but a divine being who has always existed.
