Word: προσκόμματος

Etymology:
The word προσκόμματος comes from the Greek root words πρός (pros) meaning "towards" and κόμμα (kómma) meaning "a stumbling block". The word is derived from the verb προσκόπτω (proskóptō) which means "to stumble".

Grammar:
προσκόμματος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
προσκόμματος refers to a stumbling block or obstacle that causes someone to trip or fall. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that impedes progress or causes difficulty. The word is often used in a religious or moral context, referring to something that causes a person to sin or stray from the right path.
