Word: προλαμβάνει (prolambánei)

Etymology: The word προλαμβάνει comes from the Greek verb προλαμβάνω (prolambánō), which is a compound word made up of the prefix προ- (pro-, meaning "before") and the verb λαμβάνω (lambánō, meaning "to take"). Therefore, προλαμβάνω means "to take beforehand" or "to anticipate".

Grammar: προλαμβάνει is the third person singular present tense form of the verb προλαμβάνω. It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present and is being performed by a third person singular subject (he/she/it).

Description: προλαμβάνει is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it anticipates" or "he/she/it takes beforehand". It can be used in various contexts, such as in business, sports, or everyday life, to describe someone who is taking action in advance to prevent a problem or to gain an advantage. For example, "Ο προπονητής προλαμβάνει τις κινήσεις του αντιπάλου" (The coach anticipates the opponent's moves).
