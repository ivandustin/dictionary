Word: προσερρηξεν

Etymology: The word προσερρηξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προς (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb ῥήγνυμι (rhēgnymi), meaning "to break" or "to tear apart." Therefore, the word προσερρηξεν can be translated as "to tear towards" or "to break into."

Grammar: προσερρηξεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is composed of the prefix προς (pros), the verb ῥήγνυμι (rhēgnymi), and the augment ε (e) that is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate past tense.

Description: The word προσερρηξεν describes an action of tearing or breaking something towards a specific direction or target. It can be used in a literal sense, such as tearing a piece of paper towards oneself, or in a figurative sense, such as breaking into a conversation or a group of people.
