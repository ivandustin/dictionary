Word: προφητείαι

Etymology: The word προφητείαι (propheteiai) comes from the Greek word προφήτης (prophetes), which means "one who speaks for a god". The suffix -είαι (-eiai) is a plural ending, so προφητείαι means "prophecies" or "predictions".

Grammar: προφητείαι is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple prophecies being referred to.

Description: προφητείαι is a word that refers to predictions or prophecies made by a prophet. In the context of Greek mythology, there were many prophets who made predictions about the future, often with the help of the gods. In the context of the Bible, the word is often used to refer to the predictions made by the prophets of the Old Testament. Today, the word can be used more broadly to refer to any kind of prediction or prophecy.
