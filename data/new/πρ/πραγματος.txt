Word: πραγματος

Etymology:
The word πραγματος (pragmatos) is derived from the Greek word πρᾶγμα (pragma), which means "thing" or "matter". It is related to the verb πράσσω (prasso), which means "to do" or "to accomplish".

Grammar:
πραγματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence.

Description:
πραγματος can be translated as "thing" or "matter" in English. It is often used in philosophical and legal contexts to refer to a concrete object or abstract concept. In philosophy, it is used to describe the nature of reality and the relationship between ideas and the physical world. In law, it is used to refer to a legal case or matter that is being considered by a court.
