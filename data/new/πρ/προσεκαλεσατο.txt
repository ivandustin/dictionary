Word: προσεκαλεσατο

Etymology: The word προσεκαλεσατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root words πρός (pros) meaning "towards" and καλέω (kaleo) meaning "to call". It is a compound verb formed by adding the prefix προς (pros) to the verb καλέω (kaleo).

Grammar: προσεκαλεσατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon themselves. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: The word προσεκαλεσατο means "he/she/it called to himself/herself/itself" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus calling his disciples to follow him. The word conveys a sense of personal invitation and a desire for a close relationship.
