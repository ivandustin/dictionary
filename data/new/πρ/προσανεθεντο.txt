Etymology:
The word προσανεθεντο is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προσ- (pros-), meaning "toward" or "to," and the verb ἀνατίθημι (anatithemi), meaning "to place upon" or "to dedicate." Therefore, the word προσανεθεντο can be translated as "they dedicated themselves to" or "they committed themselves to."

προσανεθεντο is a third-person plural aorist middle indicative verb in ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a past action that is completed, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon themselves. Therefore, the word προσανεθεντο can be translated as "they dedicated themselves" or "they committed themselves."
