Word: πραγματων

Etymology: πραγματων is the genitive plural form of the Greek word πράγμα (prágmá), which means "thing" or "matter". The word comes from the verb πράσσω (prássō), which means "to do" or "to accomplish".

Grammar: πραγματων is a genitive plural noun in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "of things".

Description: πραγματων is a common word in Greek and is often used in various contexts to refer to things or matters. It can be found in literature, philosophy, and everyday conversation. In English, it is often translated as "things" or "matters".
