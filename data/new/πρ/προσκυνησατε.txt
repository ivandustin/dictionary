Word: προσκυνησατε

Etymology: The word προσκυνησατε comes from the Greek verb προσκυνέω (proskynéō), which means "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence." The word is derived from the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward," and κυνέω (kynéō), meaning "to kiss."

Grammar: προσκυνησατε is the second person plural aorist imperative form of the verb προσκυνέω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: προσκυνησατε is a command to a group of people to worship or bow down in reverence. It is often used in religious contexts, such as in the Bible, where it is used to instruct people to worship God. The word can also be used more generally to mean showing respect or reverence to someone or something.
