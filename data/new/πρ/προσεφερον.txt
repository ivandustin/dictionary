Word: προσεφερον

Etymology:
The word προσεφερον is a Greek verb that comes from the root word προσφέρω (prosphero), which means "to offer" or "to bring to." The word is formed by adding the prefix προς (pros), which means "toward" or "to," to the verb stem φερω (phero), which means "to carry" or "to bear."

Grammar:
προσεφερον is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is the imperfect tense of the verb προσφέρω (prosphero), which means "to offer" or "to bring to." The imperfect tense is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description:
προσεφερον can be translated to "they were offering" or "they used to offer" in English. The word is often used in the context of religious or ceremonial offerings, such as sacrifices or gifts to the gods. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the offerings made by the Jewish people in the temple.
