The word "προσεσχηκεν" (proseschēken) is a Greek verb that means "to pay attention to" or "to be careful about". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "in the direction of", and the verb "εσχηκεν" (eschēken), meaning "to have" or "to hold". 

Grammar: "προσεσχηκεν" is a perfect tense verb, indicating that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. It is in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject is a he/she/it.

Description: The word is often used in a positive sense, indicating that someone is paying close attention to something or being careful about it. It can also be used in a negative sense, indicating that someone is being overly cautious or paranoid.
