Word: προσελθουσα

Etymology: προσελθουσα is a Greek word that comes from the verb προσέρχομαι (prosérchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". The suffix -ουσα (-ousa) indicates that the word is in the feminine singular nominative case, meaning that it refers to a female subject who is approaching or coming near.

Grammar: προσελθουσα is a participle in the present tense, active voice, and feminine gender. It functions as an adjective, modifying a noun or pronoun in the sentence. In this form, it can be translated as "approaching" or "coming near".

Description: προσελθουσα is a word that describes a female subject who is approaching or coming near. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a narrative or in a command. For example, "προσελθουσα η γυνή είπε..." (proselthousa i gyni eipe...) means "the woman who approached said...".
