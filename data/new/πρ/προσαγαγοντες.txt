Word: προσαγαγοντες

Etymology: 
The word προσαγαγοντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root words προς (pros) meaning "toward" or "to" and αγαγειν (agagein) meaning "to lead" or "to bring". Therefore, προσαγαγοντες means "bringing or leading towards".

Grammar:
προσαγαγοντες is the present participle form of the verb προσαγαγειν (prosagagein) which means "to bring or lead towards". The present participle form indicates that the action is ongoing or in progress. It is often used to describe actions that are happening at the same time as another action.

Description:
προσαγαγοντες is a word that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is often translated as "bringing" or "leading" in English. The word is used to describe actions such as bringing someone to a place or leading someone to a person.
