Word: προεπεμπον

Etymology:
The word προεπεμπον is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the verb επεμπω (epempo), meaning "to send." Therefore, προεπεμπον means "they sent before" or "they sent ahead."

Grammar:
προεπεμπον is the third person plural, past tense, indicative mood of the verb προεπεμπω. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person of the subject. The -ον ending indicates that the subject is third person plural, and the past tense.

Description:
προεπεμπον is a verb that describes an action of sending something or someone ahead of time. It can be used in various contexts, such as sending a message, a messenger, or a gift before the arrival of the recipient. The verb can also imply a sense of preparation or anticipation for an event or a journey.
