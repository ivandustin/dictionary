Word: πρωτων

Etymology:
The word πρωτων (prōtōn) is derived from the Greek word πρῶτος (prōtos), which means "first" or "foremost". It is a form of the adjective πρῶτος (prōtos) in the genitive plural.

Grammar:
πρωτων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. The plural form indicates that there are multiple objects or individuals being referred to.

Description:
πρωτων can be translated to mean "of the first" or "of the foremost". It is often used in Greek mythology to refer to the first or most important gods or heroes. In mathematics, it is used to refer to the first term in a sequence or series.
