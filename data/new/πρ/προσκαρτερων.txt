Word: προσκαρτερων

Etymology: The word προσκαρτερων comes from the Greek verb προσκαρτερέω (proskartereo), which means "to adhere to" or "to persist in". It is derived from the prefix προσ- (pros-), meaning "toward", and the verb καρτερέω (kartereo), meaning "to be strong" or "to endure".

Grammar: προσκαρτερων is the present participle form of the verb προσκαρτερέω (proskartereo). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: προσκαρτερων describes someone who is adhering to or persisting in something. It can be translated as "adhering", "persisting", or "continuing". In the context of a sentence, it can be used to describe someone who is persevering through a difficult situation or who is dedicated to a particular task or goal.
