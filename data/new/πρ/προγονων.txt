Word: προγονων

Etymology:
The word προγονων (progonon) is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words: προ (pro) meaning "before" or "in front of" and γονεύς (goneus) meaning "ancestor" or "parent". Therefore, the word προγονων means "forefathers" or "ancestors".

In Greek, προγονων is a noun in the plural form. It is declined as follows:

- Nominative: πρόγονος (progonos)
- Genitive: προγόνου (progonou)
- Accusative: πρόγονον (progonon)
- Vocative: πρόγονε (progone)

Plural:
- Nominative: πρόγονοι (progonoi)
- Genitive: προγόνων (progonon)
- Accusative: προγόνους (progonous)
- Vocative: πρόγονοι (progonoi)

Description:
The word προγονων refers to one's ancestors or forefathers. It is often used to describe a person's lineage or family history. In Greek mythology, the προγονων were the first gods and goddesses who created the world and the human race. The word can also be used in a broader sense to refer to the ancestors of a particular group or nation.
