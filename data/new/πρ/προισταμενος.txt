Word: προισταμένος

Etymology: The word προισταμένος comes from the Greek verb προΐσταμαι (proistamai), which means "to stand before, to preside over, to lead." The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: προισταμένος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a present participle, which means it describes an ongoing action. In this case, it describes someone who is currently presiding over or leading something.

Description: προισταμένος refers to someone who is in a position of leadership or authority. It can be used to describe a variety of leaders, such as a manager, supervisor, or director. The word implies that the person is actively involved in leading and managing others, rather than simply holding a title.
