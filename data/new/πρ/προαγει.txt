Word: προαγει

Etymology:
The word προαγει (proagei) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: προ (pro), which means "before" or "in front of," and αγει (agei), which means "he/she/it leads" or "brings." Therefore, the literal meaning of προαγει is "he/she/it leads before" or "brings forward."

Grammar:
προαγει is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular:
  - Προαγω (proago) - I lead before
  - Προαγεις (proageis) - You lead before
  - Προαγει (proagei) - He/she/it leads before
- Plural:
  - Προαγομεν (proagomen) - We lead before
  - Προαγετε (proagete) - You lead before
  - Προαγουσι (proagousi) - They lead before

Description:
προαγει is a verb that implies movement or action towards a specific direction or goal. It can be used in various contexts, such as in sports, where a player leads the ball forward towards the opponent's goal, or in business, where a company leads the market by introducing innovative products. In general, προαγει conveys the idea of progress, advancement, and leadership.
