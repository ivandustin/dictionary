Word: πρωτοκλισιας

Etymology: The word πρωτοκλισιας comes from the Greek words πρῶτος (prōtos) meaning "first" and κλίσις (klisis) meaning "incline" or "slope". It is a compound word that refers to the first slope or incline of a hill.

Grammar: πρωτοκλισιας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description: πρωτοκλισιας is a term used in geography to describe the first slope or incline of a hill. It refers to the part of the hill that is closest to the base and is often the steepest part of the hill. The term is commonly used in Greece and other countries with hilly terrain.
