Word: προσφατον

Etymology:
The word προσφατον comes from the Greek word πρόσφατος (prósfatos), which means "recent" or "fresh". It is derived from the prefix πρός (prós), meaning "toward", and the adjective φατός (phatós), meaning "spoken".

Grammar:
προσφατον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description:
προσφατον is used to describe something that is recent or fresh. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as food, news, or events. For example, if someone says "Αυτό το ψάρι είναι προσφατον" (Aftó to psári íne prósfaton), it means "This fish is fresh".
