Word: πρεσβυτερων

Etymology: 
The word πρεσβυτερων comes from the Greek word πρεσβύτερος (presbyteros), which means "elder" or "older person". In the New Testament, the word is used to refer to leaders in the early Christian church.

Grammar:
πρεσβυτερων is the genitive plural form of πρεσβύτερος. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. The plural form indicates that there are multiple elders being referred to.

Description:
πρεσβυτερων is a Greek word that refers to a group of elders. In the context of the New Testament, it is often used to refer to leaders in the early Christian church who were responsible for guiding and teaching the community. The word has a rich history and is still used in some Christian denominations today to refer to ordained ministers or leaders within the church.
