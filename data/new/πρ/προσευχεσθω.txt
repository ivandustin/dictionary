Word: προσευχεσθω

Etymology:
The word προσευχεσθω comes from the Greek verb προσεύχομαι (proseúchomai), which means "to pray" or "to offer prayers." The word is derived from the prefix προς (pros), which means "toward," and the verb εὔχομαι (eúchomai), which means "to pray" or "to vow."

Grammar:
προσευχεσθω is a present passive imperative verb in Greek. The verb is in the third person singular form, which means that it is a command directed towards a single person. The passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb, rather than performing it. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing or habitual.

Description:
προσευχεσθω is a command to pray or to offer prayers. It is often used in religious contexts, particularly in Christian and Jewish traditions. The word emphasizes the importance of prayer as a means of communication with a higher power or deity. The passive voice suggests that the act of prayer is not something that can be done by oneself, but rather requires a connection to something greater. The present tense emphasizes the ongoing nature of prayer as a regular practice.
