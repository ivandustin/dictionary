Word: προσκυνησει

Etymology:
The word προσκυνησει (proskynēsei) is derived from the Greek verb προσκυνέω (proskynéō), which means "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence." The word is composed of two parts: προς (pros), which means "toward," and κυνέω (kynéō), which means "to kiss." The word originally referred to the act of kissing the ground in front of someone as a sign of respect or worship.

προσκυνησει is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb προσκυνέω (proskynéō), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek. The verb is used to express an action that will happen in the future, specifically an act of worship or reverence.

Description:
προσκυνησει is a Greek word that means "he/she/it will worship" or "he/she/it will bow down in reverence."
