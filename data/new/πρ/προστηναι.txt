Word: προστηναι

Etymology:
The word προστηναι comes from the Greek verb προστάτης (prostátēs), which means "protector" or "guardian". The verb form προστηναι is derived from this root and means "to protect" or "to guard".

Grammar:
προστηναι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is also in the infinitive form, which means that it is not conjugated to agree with a specific subject or tense.

Description:
προστηναι is a verb that means "to protect" or "to guard". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as protecting someone or something from harm, guarding a location or object, or providing security for a person or group. The verb is often used in a figurative sense as well, such as protecting one's reputation or guarding one's secrets.
