Word: προσδεχονται

Etymology: The word προσδεχονται comes from the Greek verb προσδέχομαι (prosdechomai), which means "to receive" or "to welcome". It is derived from the prefix προσ- (pros-), meaning "toward", and the verb δέχομαι (dechomai), meaning "to receive".

Grammar: προσδεχονται is a present tense, third person plural form of the verb προσδέχομαι (prosdechomai). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, the third person plural indicates that the subject is a group of people, and the -νται (-ntai) ending indicates that the verb is in the passive voice.

Description: προσδεχονται means "they are being welcomed" or "they are being received". It is often used to describe a group of people who are arriving at a place and being greeted by others. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any situation where someone is being welcomed or received, such as a new employee being welcomed to a company or a guest being welcomed into someone's home.
