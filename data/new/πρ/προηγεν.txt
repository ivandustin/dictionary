Word: προηγεν

Etymology: The word προηγεν comes from the Greek root words προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and ἡγέομαι (hēgéomai), meaning "to lead" or "to guide." Together, προηγεν means "having led before" or "having gone before."

Grammar: προηγεν is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb προηγέομαι (proēgéomai), which means "to go before" or "to lead the way."

Description: προηγεν is often used to describe something that has come before or preceded something else. It can also be used to describe a person who has led or guided others in the past. In Greek mythology, the god Hermes was often referred to as προηγεν, as he was believed to have led souls to the underworld.
