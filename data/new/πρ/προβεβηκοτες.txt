The word "προβεβηκοτες" (probebēkotes) is a Greek verb that is in the perfect participle tense. It comes from the root verb "προβαίνω" (probainō), which means "to go forward" or "to advance." The perfect participle tense indicates that the action of the verb has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

In terms of grammar, "προβεβηκοτες" is a masculine plural form, indicating that it refers to a group of people who have gone forward or advanced. It can be translated as "those who have gone forward" or "those who have advanced."

Overall, the word "προβεβηκοτες" emphasizes the idea of progress and moving forward, and can be used to describe individuals or groups who have achieved a certain level of advancement or success.
