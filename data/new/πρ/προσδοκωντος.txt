Word: προσδοκώντος

Etymology: The word προσδοκώντος comes from the Greek verb προσδοκάω (prosdokao), which means "to expect" or "to anticipate". The suffix -ντος (-ntos) is a participle ending, indicating that the word is in the genitive case and is functioning as an adjective.

Grammar: προσδοκώντος is a genitive singular form of the participle προσδοκάω. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the participle is a verbal adjective that describes the action of the verb. Therefore, προσδοκώντος can be translated as "of the one who is expecting" or "belonging to the one who anticipates".

Description: προσδοκώντος is a word that describes someone who is in a state of expectation or anticipation. It can be used to describe a person who is waiting for something to happen, or who is looking forward to a particular event or outcome. The word has a sense of hopefulness and optimism, as it implies that the person is confident that their expectations will be met.
