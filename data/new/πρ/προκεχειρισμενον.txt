Word: προκεχειρισμένον

Etymology:
The word προκεχειρισμένον is a Greek word that is derived from the verb προκεχειρίζω (prokechirizo), which means "to have in hand" or "to have ready". The word is formed by adding the prefix προ- (pro-), which means "before" or "in advance", to the verb κεχειρίζω (kechirizo), which means "to have in hand" or "to hold".

Grammar:
προκεχειρισμένον is a past participle in the neuter gender, singular number, and accusative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -μένον (-menon) to the verb stem προκεχειρισ- (prokechirism-). The word is used to describe a noun that has been prepared or made ready in advance.

Description:
προκεχειρισμένον is an adjective that describes something that has been prepared or made ready in advance. It can be used to describe a wide range of things, from physical objects that have been prepared for use, to abstract concepts that have been thought through and planned out. The word implies a sense of readiness and preparedness, suggesting that the thing being described is well-equipped to handle whatever situation it may encounter.
