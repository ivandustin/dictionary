Word: πρωτης

Etymology: πρωτης comes from the Greek word πρῶτος (prōtos), meaning "first" or "foremost". 

Grammar: πρωτης is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. 

Description: πρωτης can be translated to mean "first" or "chief". It is often used to describe someone who is in a position of authority or leadership. It can also be used to describe something that is the most important or significant in a particular context. For example, in Greek mythology, Zeus was often referred to as the πρωτης θεός (prōtēs theos), meaning "the first god" or "the chief god".
