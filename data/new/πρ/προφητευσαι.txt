Word: προφητεύσαι

Etymology: The word προφητεύσαι comes from the Greek verb προφητεύω (prophēteuō), which means "to prophesy" or "to predict." The word is derived from the noun προφήτης (prophētēs), which means "prophet."

Grammar: προφητεύσαι is the second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb προφητεύω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action, and the imperative mood is used to give a command or instruction.

Description: προφητεύσαι is a command to "prophesy" or "predict." It is a call to speak the truth about future events, often with a religious or spiritual connotation. In the context of the Bible, the word is often used to describe the act of speaking on behalf of God or conveying a divine message.
