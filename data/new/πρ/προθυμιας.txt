Word: προθυμίας

Etymology:
The word προθυμίας (prothymías) is derived from the Greek words πρό (pró), meaning "before" or "in front of," and θυμός (thymós), meaning "passion" or "spirit." Therefore, the word προθυμίας can be translated as "readiness" or "eagerness."

Grammar:
προθυμίας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of eagerness" or "of readiness."

Description:
προθυμίας refers to a state of being eager or ready to do something. It can be used to describe a person's attitude or mindset towards a task or situation. For example, someone who is full of προθυμίας is enthusiastic and motivated to take on a challenge. It can also refer to a willingness to help or cooperate with others.
