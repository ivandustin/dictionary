Word: προσεθετο

Etymology:
The word προσεθετο comes from the Greek verb προστίθημι (prostithēmi), which means "to add" or "to place upon". The word is formed by adding the prefix προς (pros), which means "toward" or "in addition to", to the verb stem θετο (theto), which means "placed" or "set".

Grammar:
προσεθετο is a third-person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb προστίθημι (prostithēmi). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or asking a question about a fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
προσεθετο can be translated as "he/she/it added" or "he/she/it placed upon". It describes a single action that was performed in the past. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as adding an ingredient to a recipe, placing an object on a surface, or adding a clause to a sentence.
