Word: προπεμψατε

Etymology: 
The word προπεμψατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root words προ (pro) meaning "before" and πεμπω (pempo) meaning "to send". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
προπεμψατε is a second person plural aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people to complete an action in the past. The verb is conjugated from the root word πεμπω (pempo) which means "to send" and the prefix προ (pro) which means "before".

Description:
The word προπεμψατε is a command to a group of people to "send before" or "send ahead". It is often used in the context of sending someone ahead to prepare for the arrival of others. In the New Testament, it is used in the context of sending messengers ahead to prepare the way for Jesus.
