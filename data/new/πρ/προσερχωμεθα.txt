Word: προσερχωμεθα

Etymology: προσερχωμεθα is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πρός (pros), which means "toward" or "to," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come." Therefore, προσερχωμεθα means "let us come to" or "let us approach."

Grammar: προσερχωμεθα is a first-person plural present subjunctive active verb in Greek. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible as an exhortation or invitation to approach God or to come closer to Him.

Description: προσερχωμεθα is a verb that implies action and movement towards a specific destination or goal. It is often used in a spiritual or religious context to encourage believers to draw near to God and to seek His presence. In addition, it can also be used in a more general sense to express the idea of approaching or coming closer to someone or something.
