The word "προκεινται" (prokeintai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "πρό" (pro), meaning "before" or "in front of", and "κείμαι" (keimai), meaning "to lie" or "to be situated". 

Grammar: The verb is in the present tense, which indicates that the action is happening now. The third person singular form indicates that the subject is a singular noun or pronoun, such as "he", "she", or "it". 

Description: The word "προκεινται" is often used to indicate that something is about to happen or is imminent. It can also be used to describe a situation or circumstance that is currently present or existing.
