Word: πραγματευσασθαι

Etymology:
The word πραγματευσασθαι comes from the Greek verb πραγματεύομαι (pragmateuomai), which means "to deal with" or "to transact business." The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a reflexive suffix that indicates that the action is being performed on oneself. Therefore, πραγματευσασθαι means "to deal with oneself" or "to transact business with oneself."

Grammar:
πραγματευσασθαι is a verb in the aorist tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The infinitive mood indicates that the verb is being used as a noun, and in this case, it means "dealing with oneself" or "transacting business with oneself."

Description:
πραγματευσασθαι is a Greek verb that means "to deal with oneself" or "to transact business with oneself." It is used to describe a situation where a person is dealing with their own affairs or conducting business with themselves. This could include making decisions about personal finances, managing one's own business, or negotiating with oneself about a particular issue. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe a person who is introspective or self-reflective, and who takes the time to consider their own thoughts and feelings.
