Word: προσελθε (proselthe)

Etymology: προσελθε is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πρός (pros), which means "toward" or "to," and ἔρχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come." Therefore, προσελθε means "to come to" or "to approach."

Grammar: προσελθε is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed to one person, telling them to perform the action of coming to or approaching.

Description: προσελθε is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe people coming to Jesus or approaching him for healing or guidance. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone coming to or approaching someone or something else.
