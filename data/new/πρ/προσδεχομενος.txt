Word: προσδεχομενος

Etymology:
The word προσδεχομενος comes from the Greek verb προσδέχομαι (prosdechomai), which means "to receive" or "to welcome". It is derived from the prefix προσ- (pros-), meaning "toward", and the verb δέχομαι (dechomai), meaning "to receive".

Grammar:
προσδεχομενος is the present participle form of the verb προσδέχομαι (prosdechomai). In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντας (-ontas) to the stem of the verb. The present participle form indicates that the action of receiving or welcoming is ongoing or in progress.

Description:
προσδεχομενος is a Greek word that can be translated to English as "welcoming" or "receiving". It is often used to describe a person who is waiting for someone or something to arrive, and who is ready to receive or welcome them. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe a welcoming or hospitable attitude or environment.
