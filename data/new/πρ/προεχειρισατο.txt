Word: προεχειρισατο

Etymology: The word προεχειρισατο is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the verb εχειρίζομαι (echeirizomai), meaning "to handle" or "to use." Therefore, the word προεχειρισατο means "he/she/it handled beforehand" or "he/she/it used in advance."

Grammar: προεχειρισατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: προεχειρισατο is a word that is not commonly used in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek texts, such as the New Testament of the Bible. The word implies that someone has taken action in advance, perhaps to prepare for a future event or to prevent a problem from occurring.
