Word: προσηλθαν

Etymology:
The word προσηλθαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πρός (pros) meaning "towards" and ἥκω (hēkō) meaning "to come". Therefore, the literal meaning of προσηλθαν is "they came towards".

Grammar:
προσηλθαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb προσέρχομαι (proserchomai), which means "to come towards" or "to approach". The aorist tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact.

Description:
προσηλθαν is a verb that describes an action of a group of people coming towards a certain place or person. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a narrative, a historical account, or a description of an event. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the actions of people coming towards Jesus or his disciples.
