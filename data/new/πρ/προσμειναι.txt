Word: προσμείναι

Etymology:
The word προσμείναι (prosmeínai) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward" or "to," and the verb μένω (méno), meaning "to remain" or "to stay." Therefore, the literal meaning of προσμείναι is "to remain toward" or "to stay with."

Grammar:
προσμείναι is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the present tense. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is conjugated differently depending on the subject and the tense of the sentence.

Description:
προσμείναι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical presence, as in "I will stay with you" or "I will remain by your side." It can also be used to describe emotional or mental presence, as in "I will stay committed to this project" or "I will remain focused on my goals." Overall, προσμείναι conveys a sense of steadfastness and dedication.
