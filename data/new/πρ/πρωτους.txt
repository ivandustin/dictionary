Word: πρωτους

Etymology:
The word πρωτους (protous) is derived from the Greek word πρῶτος (protos), which means "first" or "foremost". It is a combination of the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of", and the adjective ὤν (on), meaning "being" or "existing".

Grammar:
πρωτους is the masculine accusative plural form of the adjective πρῶτος (protos). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, πρωτους is used to describe masculine plural nouns in the accusative case.

Description:
πρωτους is an adjective that can be translated to "first", "foremost", or "leading". It is often used to describe a person or thing that is at the forefront or in a position of importance. In ancient Greek literature, it was used to describe heroes, leaders, and gods. In modern Greek, it is still used in the same way, as well as in everyday language to describe someone who is the best or most skilled at something.
