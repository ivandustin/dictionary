Word: προαγαγειν

Etymology:
The word προαγαγειν comes from the Greek roots προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and αγαγειν (agagein), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." Therefore, προαγαγειν means "to lead or bring forward" or "to promote."

Grammar:
προαγαγειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is an aorist active infinitive form of the verb προαγω (proago), which means "to lead forward" or "to bring forward." The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
προαγαγειν is a word that is often used in a figurative sense to mean "to promote" or "to advance." It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in business, politics, or education. For example, a company might promote an employee by giving them a higher position or salary, a politician might promote a policy by advocating for it, or a teacher might promote a student to the next grade level. In all of these cases, the word προαγαγειν implies a sense of progress or advancement.
