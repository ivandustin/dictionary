Word: προδραμων

Etymology:
The word προδραμων is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: προ- (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and δραμων (dramon), meaning "runner" or "racer." Therefore, the word προδραμων can be translated as "one who runs before" or "forerunner."

Description:
In ancient Greece, a προδραμων was a runner who ran ahead of the chariot races to clear the way and ensure the safety of the racers. The term was also used in a military context to refer to scouts or advance troops who would go ahead of the main army to gather information about the enemy and prepare the way for the rest of the troops. In a broader sense, the term can be used to refer to anyone who goes ahead of others to prepare the way or pave the path for them.
