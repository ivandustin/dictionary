Word: προσφερειν

Etymology:
The word προσφερειν (prosferin) comes from the Greek verb προσφέρω (prosfero), which means "to offer" or "to present". It is derived from the prefix προς (pros), meaning "towards", and the verb φέρω (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bring".

Grammar:
προσφερειν is the present active infinitive form of the verb προσφέρω (prosfero). In Greek, verbs are conjugated according to tense, mood, voice, person, and number. The present tense indicates an action that is happening now, the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action, and the infinitive form indicates that the verb is not being used to describe a specific subject or time.

Description:
προσφερειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe offering a gift, presenting an idea, or providing a service. It can also be used in a religious context to describe offering a sacrifice or prayer. In modern Greek, it is commonly used in the context of offering food or drinks to guests.
