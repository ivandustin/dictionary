Word: προσενεγκε

Etymology:
The word προσενεγκε comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of two words: πρός (pros) meaning "towards" or "to" and ἐνέγκω (enénkō) meaning "to bring in" or "to offer". Therefore, the word προσενεγκε means "to bring or offer something towards someone or something".

Grammar:
προσενεγκε is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests. Therefore, the word προσενεγκε is used to command or request someone to bring or offer something towards someone or something.
