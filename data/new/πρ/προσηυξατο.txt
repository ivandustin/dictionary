Word: προσηυξατο

Etymology: The word προσηυξατο comes from the Greek verb προσευχόμαι (proseuchomai), which means "to pray" or "to make a prayer." The prefix προσ- (pros-) means "toward" or "to," and ευχή (euchē) means "prayer" or "wish."

Grammar: προσηυξατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb προσευχόμαι (proseuchomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: προσηυξατο means "he/she/it prayed" in Greek. It is a specific form of the verb προσευχόμαι (proseuchomai), which is used frequently in the New Testament to describe prayer. The word implies a personal and intentional communication with a higher power, often with a sense of reverence and humility.
