Word: πρωινόν

Etymology:
The word πρωινόν (prōinón) is derived from the Greek word πρωί (prōí), which means "morning". The suffix -νόν (-nón) is added to indicate that it is a noun.

Grammar:
πρωινόν is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The singular form of πρωινόν is used to refer to a single morning.

Description:
πρωινόν refers to the morning meal or breakfast. It is a common word used in Greek to describe the first meal of the day. In Greece, breakfast typically consists of coffee or tea, bread, butter, and jam. Other common breakfast foods include yogurt, cheese, eggs, and pastries.
