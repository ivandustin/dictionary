Word: προθυμως

Etymology: προθυμως is an adverb in Greek that comes from the combination of two words: πρό (pro), meaning "before" or "in front of," and θυμός (thymos), meaning "passion" or "spirit." Therefore, προθυμως can be translated as "with eagerness" or "with enthusiasm."

Grammar: προθυμως is an adverb in Greek, which means that it modifies the verb in a sentence. It is often used to describe how an action is performed, indicating that it is done with eagerness or enthusiasm. For example, "Οι μαθητές προθυμως απάντησαν στις ερωτήσεις του καθηγητή" (The students eagerly answered the teacher's questions).

Description: προθυμως is a word that expresses a positive attitude towards an action or situation. It is often used to describe someone who is willing and eager to do something, or to indicate that an action is performed with enthusiasm. It can be used in various contexts, such as in education, sports, or work, to describe a person's attitude towards a task or goal.
