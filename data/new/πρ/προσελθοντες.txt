Word: προσελθοντες

Etymology: προσελθοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb προσέρχομαι (prosérchomai), which means "to come near" or "to approach." The word is formed by adding the suffix -οντες (-ontes), which is a participle ending that indicates present action.

Grammar: προσελθοντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The present participle indicates ongoing action in the present, and the masculine plural form indicates that the subject is a group of males.

Description: προσελθοντες can be translated as "approaching" or "coming near." It is often used in the New Testament to describe people who come to Jesus or to a place where Jesus is present. For example, in Mark 10:35, the disciples James and John "approach" Jesus to ask him a question. In Luke 24:15, two disciples "approach" Jesus on the road to Emmaus. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of approach or coming near.
