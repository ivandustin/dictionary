Word: προσκαιρον

Etymology:
The word προσκαιρον comes from the Greek words πρός (pros) meaning "toward" or "with" and καιρός (kairos) meaning "time" or "opportunity". Therefore, the word προσκαιρον can be translated as "with time" or "temporary".

Grammar:
προσκαιρον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can also be declined in the genitive, dative, and vocative cases.

Description:
προσκαιρον is a word that describes something that is temporary or fleeting. It can refer to a momentary opportunity or a temporary situation. The word can also be used to describe something that is not permanent or lasting. In modern Greek, the word is often used to describe a temporary job or a short-term contract.
