Word: πρασσουσιν

Grammar: πρασσουσιν is a present tense verb in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action. It is in the third person plural form, indicating that the subject is a group of people or things. The verb is conjugated in the indicative mood, which is used to make statements or ask questions.

Description: πρασσουσιν is a Greek verb that is commonly used in everyday speech and writing. It is often used to describe actions that are currently taking place or actions that are habitual.
