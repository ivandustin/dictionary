Word: πραγματι (pragmati)

Etymology:
The word πραγματι (pragmati) comes from the Greek word πρᾶγμα (pragma), which means "thing" or "matter". The suffix -τι (-ti) is added to the end of the word to form the adverbial form, meaning "in reality" or "in fact".

Grammar:
πραγματι (pragmati) is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate that something is true in reality or in fact.

Description:
πραγματι (pragmati) is a useful word in Greek, as it allows speakers to emphasize that something is true in reality or in fact. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in arguments or discussions where the truth of a statement is being debated.
