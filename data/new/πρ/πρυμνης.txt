Word: πρυμνης

Etymology: πρυμνης is a Greek word that comes from the noun πρυμνα (prumna), which means "stern" or "rear" of a ship. 

Grammar: πρυμνης is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun. It is often translated as "of the stern" or "of the rear" in English.

Description: πρυμνης refers to the back part of a ship, specifically the area behind the main deck. It is an important part of the ship's structure, as it helps to stabilize and steer the vessel. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used metaphorically to refer to the "rear guard" or "last line of defense" in battle.
