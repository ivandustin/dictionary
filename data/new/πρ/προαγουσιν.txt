The word "προαγουσιν" (proagousin) is a Greek verb in the present active participle form. It comes from the root verb "προάγω" (proago), which means "to lead forward" or "to go before." 

In terms of grammar, the present active participle form indicates that the action of leading forward is currently happening. It can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is leading forward or going before.

Overall, the word "προαγουσιν" can be translated as "leading forward" or "going before" depending on the context in which it is used.
