Word: πραυτητι (prautēti)

Etymology:
The word πραυτητι (prautēti) is derived from the Greek adjective πραΰς (praŷs), which means gentle or meek. The suffix -τητι (-tēti) is a nominalizing suffix that is used to form abstract nouns from adjectives.

Grammar:
πραυτητι (prautēti) is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description:
πραυτητι (prautēti) refers to the quality of being gentle or meek. It can also be translated as meekness or mildness. In Christian theology, meekness is considered a virtue and is often associated with humility and patience. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Beatitudes, where Jesus says, "Blessed are the meek, for they shall inherit the earth" (Matthew 5:5).
