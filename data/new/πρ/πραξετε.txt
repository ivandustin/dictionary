The word "πραξετε" (praxete) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "πράξη" (praxi), which means "action" or "deed". The suffix "-ετε" (-ete) is the second person plural ending for the present tense, indicating that the verb is being directed towards a group of people.

Grammar: "Πραξετε" is the second person plural form of the present tense of the verb "πράττω" (pratto), which means "to do" or "to act". It is a regular verb in Greek, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" (-o).

Description: The word "πραξετε" is used to address a group of people and urge them to take action or do something. It can be translated as "do" or "act" in English.
