Word: πρεπει

Etymology:
The word πρεπει (prepei) is a Greek word that comes from the verb πρέπω (prepo), which means "to be fitting" or "to be suitable." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pre-, which means "to go forward" or "to be in front."

Grammar:
πρεπει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is often translated as "it is necessary" or "it is fitting." It is commonly used in Greek to express obligation or duty.

Description:
πρεπει is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to express social norms or expectations, such as "it is necessary to be polite" or "it is fitting to dress appropriately for the occasion." It can also be used to express moral or ethical obligations, such as "it is necessary to tell the truth" or "it is fitting to help those in need."
