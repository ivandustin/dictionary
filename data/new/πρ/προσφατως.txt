Word: προσφατως

Etymology: πρόσφατος (prósfatos) is a Greek word meaning "recent" or "fresh". The suffix -ως (-os) is added to form an adverb, giving us προσφατως (prosphatós), which means "recently" or "lately".

Grammar: προσφατως is an adverb in Greek, used to modify verbs or adjectives to indicate that something has happened recently or in the near past. It does not change form based on gender, number, or case.

Description: προσφατως is a common word in Greek, used in everyday conversation to talk about recent events or activities. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as discussing recent news, events, or personal experiences. It is a useful word to know for anyone learning Greek, as it is frequently used in both spoken and written language.
