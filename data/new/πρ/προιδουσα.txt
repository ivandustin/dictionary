The word "προιδουσα" (proidousa) is a Greek verb in the feminine gender, which means "foreseeing" or "predicting". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in advance", and the verb "οἶδα" (oida), meaning "to know". 

Grammar: "προιδουσα" is the present participle form of the verb "προοράω" (proorao), which means "to foresee" or "to predict". It is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: The word "προιδουσα" is often used in Greek mythology to describe goddesses or other female figures who possess the ability to see into the future and predict events. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who has a keen sense of foresight or intuition.
