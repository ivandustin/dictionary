Word: πρωι

Etymology:
The word πρωι (proi) is derived from the ancient Greek word πρωία (proia), which means "early morning". It is a combination of the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before", and the noun ὥρα (hora), meaning "hour".

Grammar:
πρωι is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender. It is often used with the definite article, το πρωί (to proi), which means "the morning".

Description:
πρωι refers to the early hours of the day, specifically the time between dawn and mid-morning. It is a time when many people wake up and start their day. In Greek culture, breakfast is often eaten during this time, and it is also a common time for people to go to work or school. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to the beginning or start of something.
