Word: πραξαντες

Etymology: 
The word πραξαντες is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word πράσσω (prassō), which means "to do" or "to act". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a common ending for the aorist tense in Greek verbs.

Grammar:
πραξαντες is the aorist tense of the Greek verb πράσσω (prassō), which means "to do" or "to act". It is in the third person plural, which means it refers to a group of people who completed an action in the past. 

Description:
πραξαντες can be translated to "they did" or "they acted" in English. It is often used in historical or narrative contexts to describe a completed action in the past. For example, in the sentence "πραξαντες τα καλα, επεστρεψαν στο σπιτι" (praxantes ta kala, epestrepsan sto spiti), which means "having done good things, they returned home", πραξαντες refers to a group of people who completed some good deeds and then returned home.
