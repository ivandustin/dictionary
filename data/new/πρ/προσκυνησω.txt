Word: προσκυνησω

Etymology:
The word προσκυνησω (proskynēsō) is derived from the Greek words πρός (pros) meaning "toward" and κυνέω (kynéō) meaning "to kiss". In ancient Greece, it was customary to kiss the ground in front of someone as a sign of respect or worship. Over time, the word evolved to mean "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence".

Grammar:
προσκυνησω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the first person singular form of the future tense. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: προσκυνώ (proskynó)
- Future tense: προσκυνήσω (proskynēsō)
- Aorist tense: προσεκύνησα (prosekýnēsa)
- Perfect tense: προσεκύνηκα (prosekýnika)

Description:
προσκυνησω is a Greek verb that means "to worship" or "to bow down in reverence". It is often used in a religious context to describe the act of showing respect or devotion to a deity or higher power. The word can also be used in a secular context to describe showing respect or admiration to a person of authority or someone who is highly respected. In Greek culture, it is customary to bow down or kneel in front of an elder or someone of higher social status as a sign of respect.
