Word: προφητικών

Etymology: The word προφητικών comes from the Greek word προφητικός (prophētikós), which means "prophetic" or "predictive". It is derived from the word προφήτης (prophētēs), which means "prophet".

Grammar: προφητικών is the genitive plural form of the adjective προφητικός. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, προφητικών is used to describe or modify a noun in the genitive case.

Description: προφητικών is an adjective that describes something as prophetic or predictive. It can be used to describe a person, event, or statement that is believed to have prophetic significance. In Greek literature, the term is often used to describe the prophetic books of the Old Testament, such as Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel.
