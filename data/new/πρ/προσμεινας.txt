Word: προσμείνας

Etymology:
The word προσμείνας (prosmeínas) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward," and the verb μένω (méno), meaning "to remain." Therefore, προσμείνας can be translated as "to remain toward" or "to stay with."

Grammar:
προσμείνας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ας (-as) to the stem προσμείν- (prosmeín-). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

προσμείνας is a verb that means "to remain with" or "to stay with." It can be used in various contexts, such as to describe someone who stays with a friend or family member during a difficult time, or to describe someone who remains loyal to a cause or belief.
