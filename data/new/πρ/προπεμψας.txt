Word: προπεμψας

Etymology: προ- (pro-, meaning "before" or "in front of") + πέμπω (pempo, meaning "to send") + -ας (-as, a suffix indicating the second person singular aorist active indicative tense). Therefore, προπεμψας means "you sent before" or "you sent ahead".

Grammar: προπεμψας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb πέμπω (pempo, meaning "to send"). In this form, it indicates that the action of sending occurred in the past and was performed by the second person singular (you).

Description: προπεμψας is a Greek verb that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is often translated as "sent ahead" or "sent before" and is used to describe actions such as sending messengers or preparing the way for someone or something. The word has a sense of urgency and importance, as it implies that the action of sending was done quickly and with purpose.
