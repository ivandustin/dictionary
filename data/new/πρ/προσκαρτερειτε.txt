Word: προσκαρτερείτε

Etymology: The word προσκαρτερείτε comes from the Greek verb προσκαρτερέω (proskarteréo), which means "to persist, persevere, or continue steadfastly." It is derived from the prefix πρός (pros), meaning "toward," and the verb καρτερέω (karteréo), meaning "to be strong, steadfast, or patient."

Grammar: προσκαρτερείτε is a second-person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all persist" or "you all persevere."

Description: προσκαρτερείτε is a motivational word that encourages people to persist and persevere in their endeavors. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to urge believers to remain steadfast in their faith. The word can also be used in a secular context to encourage people to keep working hard and not give up in the face of challenges or obstacles.
