Word: προσκομμα

Etymology:
The word προσκομμα (proskomma) is derived from the Greek verb προσκόπτω (proskopto), which means "to stumble" or "to trip over". The noun form προσκομμα refers to an obstacle or stumbling block.

Grammar:
προσκομμα is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is προσκόμματα (proskommata).

Description:
In Greek, προσκομμα can refer to a physical obstacle or a figurative stumbling block. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a difficulty or challenge that someone faces. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to describe something that causes a person to sin or fall away from their faith.
