Word: προηγηται

Etymology: The word προηγηται comes from the Greek verb προηγέομαι (proēgéomai), which means "to lead, to go before, to precede." It is derived from the prefix προ- (pro-), meaning "before," and the verb ἡγέομαι (hēgéomai), meaning "to lead, to guide."

Grammar: προηγηται is a third-person singular present indicative active form of the verb προηγέομαι (proēgéomai). It is used to describe someone or something that is leading or going before others.

Description: προηγηται can be translated to English as "he/she/it leads" or "he/she/it is leading." It is often used in the context of leadership, where someone is taking charge and guiding others. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone or something that is ahead of others in some way.
