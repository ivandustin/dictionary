Word: προσδεχομενοι

Etymology: The word προσδεχομενοι comes from the Greek verb προσδέχομαι (prosdechomai), which means "to receive, to welcome, to await." It is formed by combining the prefix προς (pros), meaning "toward," and the verb δέχομαι (dechomai), meaning "to receive."

Grammar: προσδεχομενοι is the present participle form of the verb προσδέχομαι (prosdechomai). It is a masculine plural form, which means "those who are receiving, welcoming, or awaiting." In Greek, the present participle is often used to express ongoing or continuous action.

Description: προσδεχομενοι is a word that describes a group of people who are actively receiving, welcoming, or awaiting something or someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as welcoming guests, waiting for a specific event to happen, or receiving a message or news. The word conveys a sense of anticipation and readiness, as well as a willingness to receive or accept what is coming.
