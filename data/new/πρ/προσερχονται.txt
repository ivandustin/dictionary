Word: προσερχονται

Etymology: The word προσερχονται comes from the Greek verb προσέρχομαι (prosérchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". It is derived from the prefix προς- (pros-), meaning "toward", and the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: προσερχονται is a present tense, third person plural form of the verb προσέρχομαι (prosérchomai). In Greek, verbs are inflected to indicate tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person/number. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people or things.

Description: προσερχονται is a Greek verb that means "they approach" or "they come near". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing physical movement towards a location or figuratively describing someone's attitude or behavior towards a situation. The verb is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, such as describing the approach of worshippers to a sacred site or the approach of individuals to a deity.
