Word: προθυμον

Etymology:
The word προθυμον (prothymon) is derived from the Greek words πρό (pro) meaning "before" and θυμός (thymos) meaning "passion" or "spirit". Therefore, the word προθυμον can be translated as "eager" or "enthusiastic".

Grammar:
προθυμον is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
προθυμον is used to describe someone who is eager, enthusiastic, or willing to do something. It can also be used to describe an action or behavior that is done with enthusiasm or eagerness. The word has a positive connotation and is often used to describe someone who is motivated and proactive.
