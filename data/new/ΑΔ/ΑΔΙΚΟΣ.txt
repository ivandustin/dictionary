Etymology: The word "αδικος" (adikos) comes from the Greek root "δικη" (dike), which means "justice" or "righteousness." The prefix "α-" in Greek often indicates negation or absence, so "αδικος" can be understood as "without justice" or "unjust."

Grammar: In Greek, "αδικος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Αδικος" is used to describe something or someone that is unjust, unfair, or not in accordance with what is right or just. It can refer to actions, situations, or individuals who violate principles of justice, morality, or fairness.
