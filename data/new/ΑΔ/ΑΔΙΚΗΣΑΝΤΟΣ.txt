Etymology: The word "αδικησαντος" comes from the Greek verb "αδικέω" (adikéo), which means "to do wrong" or "to treat unjustly". The suffix "-σαντος" (-santos) is the aorist participle form of the verb, indicating past action.

Grammar: "Αδικησαντος" is the masculine form of the aorist participle in Greek. It agrees with a masculine noun in gender, number, and case. It can function as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Description: "Αδικησαντος" describes someone or something that has been wronged or treated unjustly. It implies that an action of injustice has been committed in the past.
