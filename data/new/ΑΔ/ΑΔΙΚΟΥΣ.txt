Etymology: The word "αδικούς" (adikous) comes from the Greek adjective "άδικος" (adikos), which means "unjust" or "unfair". It is derived from the negative prefix "α-" and the noun "δίκη" (diki), meaning "justice" or "right".

Grammar: "Αδικούς" is the plural form of the adjective "άδικος" (adikos) in the masculine gender. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The plural form is used when referring to multiple unjust or unfair things or people.

Description: The word "αδικούς" (adikous) describes multiple things or people that are unjust or unfair. It can be used to refer to actions, situations, or individuals that violate principles of justice or fairness.
