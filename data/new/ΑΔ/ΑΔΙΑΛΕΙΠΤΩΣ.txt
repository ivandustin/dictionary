Etymology: The word "αδιαλείπτως" (adialeíptos) comes from the Greek prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the verb "διαλείπω" (dialeípo), meaning "to interrupt" or "to cease." 

Grammar: "Αδιαλείπτως" is an adverb in Greek. It is the adverbial form of the adjective "αδιάλειπτος" (adiáleiptos), which means "continuous" or "uninterrupted." 

Description: "Αδιαλείπτως" is used to describe an action or state that is continuous, without interruption or cessation. It emphasizes the uninterrupted nature of the action or state being described.
