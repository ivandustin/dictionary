Etymology: The word "αδιαλείπτος" comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "διαλείπω" (dialeípo), meaning "to cease" or "to stop." Therefore, "αδιαλείπτος" can be translated as "unceasing" or "continuous."

Grammar: In Greek, "αδιαλείπτος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Αδιαλείπτος" is used to describe something that does not stop or cease, something that is continuous or uninterrupted. It can be used to describe a state, an action, or a quality that is constant and persistent.
