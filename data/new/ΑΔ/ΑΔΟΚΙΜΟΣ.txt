Etymology: The word "αδοκιμος" (adokimos) comes from the Greek roots "a-" (not) and "dokimos" (approved, tested). It literally means "not approved" or "not tested."

Grammar: In Greek, "αδοκιμος" is an adjective. It can be used to describe a person, thing, or idea that is not approved, not accepted, or not considered valid.

Description: The word "αδοκιμος" is often used to express disapproval or rejection. It implies that something or someone has not met the necessary standards or requirements. It can be used in various contexts, such as in legal, moral, or social situations, to indicate that something is not considered valid or acceptable.
