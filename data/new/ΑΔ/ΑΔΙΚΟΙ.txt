Etymology: The word "αδικοι" (adikoi) comes from the Greek adjective "άδικος" (adikos), which means "unjust" or "unfair". It is derived from the negative prefix "α-" and the noun "δίκη" (diki), meaning "justice" or "righteousness".

Grammar: "Αδικοι" is the plural form of the adjective "άδικος" (adikos). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "αδικοι" is used to describe plural masculine nouns.

Description: The word "αδικοι" is used to describe a group of people or things that are unjust or unfair. It implies that they act in a way that goes against what is morally right or just.
