Etymology: The word "αδικουμένοι" comes from the Greek verb "αδικώ" (adikó), which means "to treat unjustly" or "to do wrong." The suffix "-μένοι" (-menoi) is added to the verb stem to form the present passive participle, indicating that the subject is being acted upon.

Grammar: "Αδικουμένοι" is the plural form of the present passive participle of the verb "αδικώ" (adikó). It is used to describe a group of people who are being treated unjustly or wronged by someone or something.

Description: "Αδικουμένοι" refers to individuals or a collective group who are experiencing injustice or being wronged. It implies that they are victims of unfair treatment or actions.
