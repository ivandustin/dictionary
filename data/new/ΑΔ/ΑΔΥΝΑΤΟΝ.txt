Etymology: The word "αδυνατον" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the adjective "δυνατόν" (dynatón), meaning "possible" or "capable."

Grammar: "Αδυνατον" is a neuter adjective in the singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. It can be declined according to the case and number of the noun it accompanies.

Description: "Αδυνατον" is used to describe something that is impossible or cannot be done. It indicates that a certain action, event, or outcome is not achievable or feasible. It is often used to express a strong negation or to emphasize the impossibility of a situation.
