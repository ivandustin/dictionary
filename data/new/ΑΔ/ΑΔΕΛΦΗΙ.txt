Etymology: The word "αδελφη" (adelfi) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἀδελφή" (adelphē), which means "sister".

Grammar: In Greek, "αδελφη" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a singular form. The plural form of "αδελφη" is "αδελφές" (adelfes).

Description: "Αδελφη" is the Greek word for "sister". It is used to refer to a female sibling, someone who shares at least one parent with another person. In Greek culture, the bond between siblings is considered important and is often celebrated.
