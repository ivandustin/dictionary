Etymology: The word "αδικηθηι" (adikithi) comes from the Greek verb "αδικώ" (adiko), which means "to do wrong" or "to treat unjustly". The suffix "-θηι" (-thi) is added to the verb stem to form the passive voice, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "αδικηθηι" (adikithi) is a verb in the passive voice, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs can be conjugated in different tenses, moods, and voices to indicate the subject, time, and manner of the action.

Description: The word "αδικηθηι" (adikithi) describes the action of being wronged or treated unjustly. It implies that someone has been subjected to unfair treatment or injustice.
