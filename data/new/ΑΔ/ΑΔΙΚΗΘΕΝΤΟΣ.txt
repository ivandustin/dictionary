Etymology: The word "αδικηθεντος" comes from the Greek word "αδικέω" (adikéo), which means "to do wrong" or "to treat unjustly". The suffix "-θεντος" (-thentos) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Grammar: "Αδικηθεντος" is the masculine singular form of the passive participle of the verb "αδικέω" (adikéo). It is used to describe someone or something that has been wronged or treated unjustly.

Description: "Αδικηθεντος" is an adjective that describes someone or something that has been subjected to injustice or unfair treatment. It implies that the person or thing has been treated in a way that goes against what is right or fair.
