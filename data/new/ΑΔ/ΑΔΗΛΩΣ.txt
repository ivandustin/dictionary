Etymology: The word "αδηλως" (adilos) comes from the Greek adjective "αδήλωτος" (adilotos), which means "unrevealed" or "unknown." It is derived from the negative prefix "α-" and the verb "δηλόω" (dilo), which means "to reveal" or "to make known."

Grammar: "Αδηλως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or state that is done or exists without being revealed or known.

Description: "Αδηλως" is used to express the idea of something being done or happening in a way that is unclear, hidden, or not easily understood. It can also imply a lack of transparency or openness.
