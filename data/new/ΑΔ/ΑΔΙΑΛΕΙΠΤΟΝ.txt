Etymology: The word "αδιαλείπτον" (adialeípton) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "διαλείπω" (dialeípo), meaning "to cease" or "to stop." Therefore, "αδιαλείπτον" can be translated as "unceasing" or "continuous."

Grammar: "αδιαλείπτον" is an adjective in the masculine, neuter, or feminine gender, depending on the noun it modifies. It is in the singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they describe.

Description: "αδιαλείπτον" describes something that is uninterrupted, constant, or continuous. It implies that there is no break or interruption in the action or state being described.
