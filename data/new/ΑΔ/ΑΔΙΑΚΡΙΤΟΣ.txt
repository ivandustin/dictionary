Etymology: The word "αδιακριτος" comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "διακριτος" (diakritos), meaning "distinguished" or "separated." 

Grammar: "Αδιακριτος" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αδιακριτος" means "indistinguishable" or "undifferentiated" in Greek. It is used to describe something that cannot be distinguished or separated into different parts or categories.
