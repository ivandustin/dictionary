Etymology: The word "αδελφός" (adelfos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀδελφός" (adelphos), which means "brother".

Grammar: In Greek, "αδελφός" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "αδελφού" (adelfou).

Description: "Αδελφός" is the Greek word for "brother". It refers to a male sibling, someone who shares at least one biological or legal parent with another person. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe a close male friend or a member of a religious or fraternal organization.
