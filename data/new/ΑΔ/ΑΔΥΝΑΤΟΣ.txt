Etymology: The word "αδυνατος" (adynatos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "δυνατος" (dynatos), meaning "able" or "possible." Therefore, "αδυνατος" can be translated as "impossible" or "not able."

Grammar: In Greek, "αδυνατος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "αδυνατος" is used to describe something that is not possible or cannot be done. It indicates that a certain action, event, or outcome is beyond the realm of possibility. It can also be used to express the idea of something being difficult or challenging to achieve.
