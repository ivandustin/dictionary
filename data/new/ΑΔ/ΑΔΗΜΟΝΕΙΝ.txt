Etymology: The word "αδημονειν" comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and "δημονειν" (dēmonein), meaning "to be happy" or "to be content." Therefore, "αδημονειν" can be translated as "to be unhappy" or "to be discontented."

Grammar: "αδημονειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is in the present tense. It is commonly used in the active voice.

Description: "αδημονειν" describes a state of unhappiness, discontent, or restlessness. It can refer to a feeling of dissatisfaction or unease, often related to a specific situation or circumstance. The word implies a longing for something better or a desire for change.
