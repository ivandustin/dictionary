Etymology: The word "χωρίσαι" (chōrisai) comes from the Greek verb "χωρίζω" (chōrizō), which means "to separate" or "to divide." It is derived from the noun "χώρα" (chōra), meaning "space" or "place."

Grammar: "Χωρίσαι" is the second person singular aorist imperative form of the verb "χωρίζω" (chōrizō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: The word "χωρίσαι" is a command form that translates to "separate" or "divide" in English. It is used to instruct someone to split or separate something into different parts or sections.
