Etymology: The word "χωλόν" (chōlón) comes from the Greek adjective "χωλός" (chōlós), meaning "lame" or "limping."

Grammar: In Greek, "χωλόν" is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Description: "Χωλόν" refers to a person or animal that has a physical disability or impairment in one or more limbs, resulting in difficulty or inability to walk or move normally.
