Etymology: The word "χωρις" (chōris) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "χωρίς" (khorís), which means "without" or "apart from."

Grammar: In Greek, "χωρις" is an adverb that is used to indicate separation, exclusion, or absence. It is often translated as "without" or "apart from" in English.

Description: "Χωρις" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used to express the absence or lack of something. For example, "χωρις νερό" (chōris nero) means "without water," and "χωρις χρήματα" (chōris chrīmata) means "without money." It can also be used to indicate separation or exclusion, such as "χωρις εσένα" (chōris esena) meaning "without you." Overall, "χωρις" is a useful word in Greek for expressing the idea of being without or apart from something.
