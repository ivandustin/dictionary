Etymology: The word "χωρίζεσθω" (chōrízesthō) comes from the Greek verb "χωρίζω" (chōrízō), which means "to separate" or "to divide."

Grammar: "Χωρίζεσθω" is a verb in the present imperative form, which is used to give commands or make requests. It is the third person singular form, meaning "let him/her/it separate" or "let there be a separation."

Description: "Χωρίζεσθω" is a command or request for something or someone to be separated or divided. It can be used in various contexts, such as instructing people to separate into different groups or asking for a division of resources or responsibilities.
