Etymology: The word "χωρισθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "χωρίζω" (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". The suffix "-σθηναι" is a passive form ending, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "Χωρισθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "χωρίζω" (chorizo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action.

Description: "Χωρισθηναι" is used to describe the action of being separated or divided. It can be used in various contexts, such as separating objects, dividing groups, or splitting something into parts.
