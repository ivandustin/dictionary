Etymology: The word "χωλούς" (cholous) comes from the Greek adjective "χωλός" (cholos), which means "lame" or "limping."

Grammar: "Χωλούς" is the plural form of the adjective "χωλός" (cholos). It is used to describe multiple people or things that are lame or limping.

Description: "Χωλούς" is an adjective in Greek that describes someone or something that is physically impaired, specifically with a limp or lameness. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is flawed or imperfect.
