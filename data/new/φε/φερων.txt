Word: φερων

Etymology:
The word φερων is derived from the Greek verb φέρω (phero), which means "to carry" or "to bear". It is a present participle form of the verb, which means "carrying" or "bearing".

Grammar:
φερων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe a person or thing that is currently carrying or bearing something.

Description:
φερων can be translated to "the one who carries" or "the bearer". It is often used in Greek mythology to describe various characters who carry or bear important objects or symbols. For example, Atlas is often referred to as φερων τον ουρανού (pheron ton ouranou), which means "the one who carries the sky". In modern Greek, the word φερων can also be used to describe someone who is carrying a heavy load or burden.
