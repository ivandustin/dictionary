Word: φεισομαι

Etymology:
The word φεισομαι (feisomai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φείδομαι (feidomai), which means "to spare" or "to be sparing." The prefix -σο- (-so-) is a future tense marker, so φεισομαι (feisomai) means "I will spare" or "I will be sparing."

Grammar:
φεισομαι (feisomai) is a first-person singular future tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

φεισομαι (feisomai) - I will spare
φεισει (feisei) - You will spare
φεισει (feisei) - He/she/it will spare
φεισομεθα (feisometha) - We will spare
φεισεσθε (feisesthe) - You all will spare
φεισονται (feisontai) - They will spare

Description:
φεισομαι (feisomai) means "to spare" or "to be sparing." It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as sparing someone's life, sparing someone from punishment, or sparing someone from harm. It can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to be frugal" or "to be economical." Overall, φεισομαι (feisomai) is a versatile verb that can be used in many different situations.
