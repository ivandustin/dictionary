Word: φερομενοι

Etymology: φερομενοι is a Greek word that comes from the verb φέρω (fero), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The suffix -μενοι (-menoi) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing an action that is currently happening.

Grammar: φερομενοι is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently carrying or bearing something. In Greek, present participles can also function as adjectives, describing the subject of the sentence.

Description: φερομενοι can be translated to "carrying" or "bearing" in English. It is often used in Greek literature and texts to describe a group of people who are carrying or transporting something, such as a load or a message. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is carrying a burden or responsibility.
