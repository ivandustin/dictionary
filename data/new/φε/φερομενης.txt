Word: φερομενης

Etymology: φερομενης is a Greek word that comes from the verb φέρω (fero), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The suffix -μενης (-menes) is a participle ending that indicates the present passive voice, meaning "being carried" or "being borne".

Grammar: φερομενης is a feminine singular form of the present passive participle of the verb φέρω (fero). It is declined according to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: φερομενης can be translated as "being carried" or "being borne". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe the movement of objects or ideas. In Christian theology, it is used to describe the Virgin Mary as the "bearer" or "carrier" of Jesus Christ.
