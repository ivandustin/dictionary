Word: φειδόμενος

Etymology:
The word φειδόμενος (feidómenos) is a present participle of the verb φείδομαι (feídome), which means "to spare, to be sparing, to refrain from". The verb comes from the ancient Greek noun φειδώ (feidó), which means "sparing, frugal".

Grammar:
φειδόμενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb φείδομαι (feídome), which is a deponent verb, meaning that it has a passive form but an active meaning. The present participle is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the verb stem.

Description:
φειδόμενος is an adjective that describes someone who is sparing or frugal. It can also be used to describe someone who is reluctant to spend money or resources. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe a person who is sparing in their use of words or who refrains from speaking altogether.
