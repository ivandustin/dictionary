Word: φειδομαι

Etymology:
The word φειδομαι (feidomai) is derived from the Greek root word φειδω (feido), which means "to spare" or "to be sparing." It is also related to the Greek word φειδος (feidos), which means "frugality" or "economy."

φειδομαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Φειδομαι (feidomai) - I spare
- Φειδεσαι (feidesai) - You spare
- Φειδεται (feidetai) - He/she/it spares

Plural:
- Φειδομεθα (feidometha) - We spare
- Φειδεσθε (feidesthe) - You all spare
- Φειδονται (feidontai) - They spare

Description:
φειδομαι is a Greek verb that means "to spare" or "to be sparing." It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as sparing someone's life, sparing someone from punishment, or sparing someone from harm. It can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to be frugal" or "to be economical." In this sense, it can refer to being sparing with one's resources, such as money or time. Overall, φειδομαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of situations to convey the idea of sparing or being sparing.
