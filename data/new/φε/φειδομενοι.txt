Word: φειδόμενοι

Etymology:
The word φειδόμενοι (feidómenoi) is a present participle of the Greek verb φείδομαι (feídome), which means "to spare, to be sparing, to refrain from". The verb comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₁- ("to protect, to save").

Grammar:
φειδόμενοι is a present participle in the masculine plural form, which means "those who spare, those who refrain from". It is derived from the verb φείδομαι (feídome), which is in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood.

Description:
φειδόμενοι is a word that describes people who are sparing or refraining from something. It can be used to describe someone who is sparing with their money, time, or resources, or someone who is refraining from doing something that they might otherwise do. The word has a connotation of self-control and discipline, and can be used in a positive sense to describe someone who is responsible and prudent.
