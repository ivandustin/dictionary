Word: φευξονται

Etymology: φευξονται is a verb form in the Ancient Greek language, specifically in the present tense, middle voice, and third person plural. It is derived from the root word φεύγω (pheúgō), which means "to flee" or "to escape."

Grammar: As mentioned, φευξονται is a present tense, middle voice, and third person plural verb form. In Ancient Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit. The present tense indicates that the action is happening currently or habitually.

Description: φευξονται can be translated to mean "they are fleeing" or "they are escaping." It is often used in literature and historical texts to describe individuals or groups who are running away from danger or trying to avoid capture. The word carries a sense of urgency and desperation, as those who are fleeing are often doing so to save their lives or protect themselves from harm.
