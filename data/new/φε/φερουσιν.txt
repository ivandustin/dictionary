Word: φερουσιν

Etymology: φερουσιν is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb φέρω (phero), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰer-.

Grammar: φερουσιν is the third person plural present active indicative form of φέρω. In Greek, verbs are inflected to indicate tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an action that is currently happening or is habitual.

Description: φερουσιν is a verb form that means "they carry" or "they bear". It can be used to describe a variety of actions, such as carrying a burden, bearing a child, or bringing something from one place to another. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is an important part of the language's vocabulary.
