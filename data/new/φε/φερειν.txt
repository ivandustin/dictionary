The word "φερειν" (pronounced "pherein") is a Greek verb that means "to carry" or "to bear". It is derived from the Greek word "φέρω" (pronounced "fero"), which has the same meaning. 

In terms of grammar, "φερειν" is a present active infinitive, which means it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of carrying or bearing in a general sense. 

In description, "φερειν" can be used in a variety of contexts, such as carrying a physical object, bearing a burden or responsibility, or even carrying a message or idea. It is a versatile verb that can be used in both literal and figurative senses.
