Word: φερουσαι

Etymology:
The word φερουσαι is a form of the Greek verb φέρω (phero), which means "to carry" or "to bear". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰer- which means "to carry" or "to bear".

Grammar:
φερουσαι is the present participle, feminine, nominative, plural form of the verb φέρω (phero). As a present participle, it describes an ongoing action of carrying or bearing, and as a feminine nominative plural, it refers to a group of females who are carrying or bearing something.

Description:
φερουσαι can be translated as "carrying" or "bearing" in English. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe women who are carrying or bearing something, such as a basket of fruit or a child. In the context of ancient Greek mythology, the word is often used to describe goddesses who carry or bear important objects or symbols, such as Athena carrying her shield or Demeter carrying a sheaf of wheat.
