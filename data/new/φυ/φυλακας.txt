Word: φυλακάς

Etymology:
The word φυλακάς (phylakas) comes from the Greek word φυλάσσω (phylasso), which means "to guard" or "to protect". The suffix -άς (-as) is a masculine noun ending in Greek.

Grammar:
φυλακάς is a masculine noun in Greek, and it is in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is φυλάκες (phylakes). In Greek, nouns have gender, number, and case, which means that their form changes depending on their function in a sentence.

Description:
φυλακάς refers to a guard or a sentinel, someone who is responsible for protecting a place or a person. In ancient Greece, φυλακάς were often soldiers who were stationed at the gates of a city or a fortress to prevent enemies from entering. Today, the word is still used in Greek to refer to security guards or prison guards.
