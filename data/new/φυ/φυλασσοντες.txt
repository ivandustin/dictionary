Word: φυλάσσοντες

Etymology: 
The word φυλάσσοντες (phylássontes) is a present participle form of the Greek verb φυλάσσω (phylássō), which means "to guard" or "to watch over". The verb is derived from the noun φύλαξ (phýlax), which means "guardian" or "watchman".

Grammar:
φυλάσσοντες is a present participle form of the verb φυλάσσω. It is in the active voice, and it is in the masculine plural form. The present participle form indicates that the action of guarding or watching over is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
φυλάσσοντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Luke 2:8, where it describes the shepherds who were watching over their flocks at night when the angel appeared to them to announce the birth of Jesus. The word conveys the idea of being vigilant and attentive, and it is often used in a military or security context to describe the act of guarding or protecting something or someone.
