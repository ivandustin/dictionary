The word "φυλακην" (phylakēn) is a Greek noun that means "prison" or "jail". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "φυλάσσω" (phylássō), which means "to guard" or "to protect". 

Grammar: "φυλακην" is a singular accusative noun in the Greek language. 

Description: The word is commonly used in Greek literature and history to refer to a place where prisoners are kept. In ancient Greece, prisons were often used to hold political prisoners or those who had committed serious crimes. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a prison or jail.
