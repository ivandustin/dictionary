The word "φυλακαις" (phylakais) is a plural noun in Greek, which means "guards" or "watchmen". It is derived from the Greek word "φυλακή" (phylakē), which means "a place of guarding" or "a prison". The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to prison guards or soldiers who were responsible for guarding prisoners. In modern Greek, the word is still used to refer to guards or security personnel.
