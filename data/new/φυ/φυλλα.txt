Word: φυλλα

Etymology:
The word φυλλα (phulla) is derived from the Greek word φύλλο (phyllo), which means "leaf". It is a noun in the plural form.

Grammar:
In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word φυλλα is a neuter noun in the plural form. It can be declined in different cases, such as nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.

Description:
φυλλα refers to leaves, the thin, flat, and usually green part of a plant that grows from a stem or branch. Leaves are essential for photosynthesis, the process by which plants convert sunlight into energy. They also play a crucial role in respiration, transpiration, and the regulation of water and gas exchange in plants. Leaves come in different shapes, sizes, and colors, and they can be used for various purposes, such as food, medicine, decoration, and art.
