Word: φυλων

Etymology:
The word φυλων comes from the Greek root word φυλή (phylē), which means "tribe" or "clan". The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple tribes or clans.

Grammar:
φυλων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple tribes or clans being referred to.

Description:
φυλων is a word that refers to multiple tribes or clans. It is often used in the context of ancient Greek history and mythology, where different tribes or clans would often be in conflict with one another.
