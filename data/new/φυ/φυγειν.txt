Word: φυγειν

Etymology:
The word φυγειν (fygein) is derived from the Greek verb φεύγω (pheúgō), which means "to flee" or "to escape". It is a common verb in the Greek language and has been used since ancient times.

Grammar:
φυγειν is the infinitive form of the verb φεύγω (pheúgō). It is a first conjugation verb, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω. The verb is in the active voice and the present tense.

φυγειν is a Greek verb that means "to flee" or "to escape". It is often used in the context of running away from danger or avoiding a difficult situation. The verb can be used in various tenses and moods to express different meanings and nuances.
