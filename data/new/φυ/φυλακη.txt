Word: φυλακή

Etymology:
The word φυλακή (fy-lah-KEE) comes from the ancient Greek word φυλάσσω (fy-LAH-sso), which means "to guard" or "to watch over." The noun form φυλακή refers to a place where people are kept under guard, such as a prison or jail.

Grammar:
In Greek, φυλακή is a feminine noun, and its plural form is φυλακές (fy-lah-KEHS). It can be used in various cases, such as nominative, genitive, accusative, and others, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
φυλακή is a word that refers to a place where people are kept under guard, such as a prison or jail. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to any situation or circumstance that restricts a person's freedom or movement. The word has a negative connotation, as it implies confinement and restriction.
