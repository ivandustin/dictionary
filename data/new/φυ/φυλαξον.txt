Word: φυλαξον

Etymology: φυλαξον is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φυλάσσω (phylássō), which means "to guard" or "to protect."

Grammar: φυλαξον is the imperative form of the verb φυλάσσω (phylássō), which means "guard" or "protect." It is the second person singular form, meaning it is used when addressing one person directly.

Description: φυλαξον is a command or request to someone to guard or protect something. It is often used in a military or security context, but can also be used in a more general sense to mean "watch over" or "take care of."
