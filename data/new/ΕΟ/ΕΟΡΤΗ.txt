Etymology: The word "εορτή" (eorti) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἑορτή" (heortē), which means "feast" or "festival".

Grammar: In Greek, "εορτή" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "εορτές" (eortes).

Description: "Εορτή" is a common Greek word that refers to a celebration or a festive occasion. It is often used to describe religious holidays, such as Christmas or Easter, as well as other special events or commemorations. During εορτές, people gather together, engage in festive activities, and often celebrate with food, music, and dancing.
