Word: υψίστου

Etymology: υψίστου is a Greek word that comes from the adjective υψηλός (ypsilos), meaning "high" or "tall". The suffix -ίστου (-istou) is a superlative suffix, meaning "the highest" or "the tallest".

Grammar: υψίστου is the genitive form of the adjective υψηλός (ypsilos). In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, υψίστου is used to describe something that belongs to or is related to the highest or tallest thing.

Description: υψίστου is an adjective that describes something as being the highest or tallest. It can be used to describe physical objects such as mountains or buildings, or abstract concepts such as ideals or goals. In Greek mythology, υψίστου is often used to describe the gods, who are considered to be the highest beings.
