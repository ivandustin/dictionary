Word: υψος

Etymology:
The word υψος (upsos) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the verb ύψοω (ypsoo), which means "to lift up" or "to elevate". It is related to the English word "height".

Grammar:
In Greek, υψος is a noun of the neuter gender. Its plural form is υψη (ypsi).

Description:
υψος refers to the height or elevation of something, such as a mountain or a building. It can also be used figuratively to describe someone's status or rank. In Greek mythology, the god of the sky and thunder, Zeus, was often referred to as υψιστος (ypsistos), which means "the highest".
