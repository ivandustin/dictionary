Word: υψωσεν

Etymology: υψωσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ύψος (hypos), meaning "height" or "elevation". The verb form υψωσεν means "to lift up" or "to elevate".

Grammar: υψωσεν is the aorist tense, third person singular form of the verb υψόω (hysoo), which means "to lift up" or "to raise". In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: υψωσεν is a verb that describes the action of lifting something up or raising it to a higher position. It can be used in a literal sense, such as lifting a heavy object, or in a figurative sense, such as raising someone's spirits or elevating their status.
