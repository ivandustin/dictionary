Word: υψιστος

Etymology: υψιστος is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: ύψος (height) and έστωτος (standing). Therefore, υψιστος means "the highest" or "the most high".

Grammar: υψιστος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, genders, and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: υψιστος is a word used to describe something that is at the highest point or level. It can be used to describe physical objects such as mountains or buildings, or abstract concepts such as power or authority. In religious contexts, υψιστος is often used to refer to God as the highest being.
