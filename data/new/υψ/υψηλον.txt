Word: υψηλόν

Etymology:
The word υψηλόν (ypsilon) comes from the Greek adjective υψηλός (ypsilos), which means "high" or "tall". It is derived from the Greek noun ύψος (ypsos), which means "height" or "elevation".

Grammar:
υψηλόν is a neuter singular form of the adjective υψηλός. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, υψηλόν is used to modify neuter singular nouns.

Description:
υψηλόν is an adjective that describes something that is high or tall. It can be used to describe physical objects such as buildings, mountains, or trees, as well as abstract concepts such as ideals or aspirations. In Greek literature and poetry, υψηλόν is often used to convey a sense of grandeur or majesty.
