Word: υψηλοφρονειν

Etymology:
The word υψηλοφρονειν comes from the Greek words υψηλός (ypsilos) meaning "high" and φρόνηση (fronisi) meaning "mind" or "intelligence". Therefore, υψηλοφρονειν means "to have a high-mindedness" or "to be proud".

Grammar:
υψηλοφρονειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν to the stem υψηλοφρον-. 

Description:
υψηλοφρονειν refers to having a high-mindedness or being proud. It can be used to describe someone who has a sense of superiority or arrogance. It is often used in a negative context to criticize someone for being too proud or haughty.
