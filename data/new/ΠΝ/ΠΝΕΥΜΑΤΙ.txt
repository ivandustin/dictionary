Etymology: The word "πνευματι" (pnematí) comes from the Greek word "πνεῦμα" (pneuma), meaning "breath" or "spirit."

Grammar: "Πνευματι" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the neuter gender and is declined as follows:

- Nominative: το πνευματι (to pnematí) - the breath/spirit
- Genitive: του πνευματος (tou pneumatos) - of the breath/spirit
- Accusative: το πνευματι (to pnematí) - the breath/spirit
- Vocative: πνευματι (pnematí) - O breath/spirit
- Dative: τω πνευματι (to pnevmati) - to/for the breath/spirit

Description: "Πνευματι" refers to the concept of breath or spirit in Greek. It can have various meanings depending on the context. It can refer to the physical act of breathing or the intangible essence of life or vitality. In a spiritual or religious context, it can also refer to the Holy Spirit.
