Etymology: The word "πνικτών" (pniktón) comes from the Greek verb "πνίγω" (pnígo), which means "to choke" or "to suffocate."

Grammar: "Πνικτών" is the plural form of the noun "πνικτής" (pniktís), which means "choker" or "strangler." It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Πνικτών" refers to a group of individuals who are chokers or stranglers. It can be used to describe people who physically choke or strangle others, or metaphorically to refer to individuals who exert control or dominance over others.
