Etymology: The word "πνικτόν" (pnikton) comes from the Greek verb "πνίγω" (pnigo), which means "to choke" or "to suffocate". 

Grammar: "Πνικτόν" is a neuter noun in the singular form. 

Description: "Πνικτόν" refers to something that causes suffocation or choking. It can be used to describe a substance, object, or situation that can block the airways and prevent breathing.
