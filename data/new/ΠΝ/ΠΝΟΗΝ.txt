Etymology: The word "πνοήν" (pnoin) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "πνέω" (pneo), which means "to breathe."

Grammar: "Πνοήν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Πνοήν" refers to the act of breathing or breath. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the essence or spirit of something.
