Etymology: The word "πνευματων" (pnematōn) comes from the Greek word "πνεῦμα" (pneuma), meaning "breath" or "spirit."

Grammar: "πνευματων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "πνεῦμα" (pneuma). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "πνευματων" is a Greek word that can be translated as "of spirits" or "of breaths." It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to supernatural beings or divine entities.
