Etymology: The word "πνευματος" (pnéumatos) comes from the Greek word "πνεῦμα" (pnéuma), meaning "breath" or "spirit."

Grammar: "Πνευματος" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Πνευματος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "of the spirit" or "of the breath." It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to refer to the immaterial essence or life force that animates living beings. In Christian theology, it is also used to refer to the Holy Spirit.
