Etymology: The word "πνευματικοίς" (pnematikoís) comes from the Greek word "πνεῦμα" (pneûma), meaning "spirit" or "breath," and the suffix "-ικός" (-ikós), which denotes "related to" or "pertaining to." 

Grammar: "Πνευματικοίς" is the plural form of the adjective "πνευματικός" (pnematikós) in the dative case. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Πνευματικοίς" is a Greek word that can be translated as "to the spiritual" or "for the spiritual." It is often used to describe things or activities that are related to the spiritual realm, such as spiritual matters, spiritual practices, or spiritual people.
