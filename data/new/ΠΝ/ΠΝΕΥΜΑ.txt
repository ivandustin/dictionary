Etymology: The word "πνεῦμα" (pneuma) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb "πνέω" (pneo), which means "to blow" or "to breathe." In Greek philosophy, "pneuma" refers to the vital breath or life force that animates living beings.

Grammar: In Greek, "πνεῦμα" is a neuter noun. It has a singular form and can be declined in different cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative.

Description: "Πνεῦμα" has multiple meanings and uses in Greek. It can refer to breath, air, wind, or spirit. In the context of human beings, it can denote the breath of life, the soul, or the spirit. In religious and philosophical contexts, "πνεῦμα" can also refer to a divine or supernatural spirit.
