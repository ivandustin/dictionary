The word "ηξουσιν" (exousin) is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root verb "εχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold". In this form, "ηξουσιν" means "they will have" or "they will hold". 

In terms of grammar, "ηξουσιν" is a regular future tense verb in Greek, following the standard conjugation pattern for verbs ending in "-ω". 

Overall, "ηξουσιν" is a simple verb that expresses a future action of possession or holding by a third person plural subject.
