Word: μετεσχηματισα

Etymology: The word μετεσχηματισα is derived from the Greek word μετασχηματίζω (metaschēmatízō), which means "to transform" or "to change form". The prefix μετα- (meta-) means "beyond" or "after", while the root word σχήμα (schēma) means "form" or "shape".

Grammar: μετεσχηματισα is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It is conjugated from the base form μετασχηματίζω (metaschēmatízō), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: μετεσχηματισα describes an action of transformation or change of form that has already taken place in the past. It can be used to describe a physical transformation, such as a change in appearance or shape, or a more abstract transformation, such as a change in attitude or perspective.
