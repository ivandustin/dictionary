Word: μεταβησεται

Etymology:
μεταβησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb μεταβαίνω (metabaino), which means "to go across" or "to pass over". The word is formed from the prefix μετά (meta), which means "beyond" or "across", and the verb βαίνω (baino), which means "to go".

Grammar:
μεταβησεται is a third-person singular future indicative active form of the verb μεταβαίνω (metabaino). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future indicative active form is used to express an action that will happen in the future and is performed by the subject of the sentence.

Description:
μεταβησεται can be translated to English as "he/she/it will go across" or "he/she/it will pass over". The word implies a physical movement from one place to another, usually across a boundary or obstacle. It can also be used metaphorically to express a transition or change from one state to another.
