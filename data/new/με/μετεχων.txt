Word: μετεχων

Etymology:
The word μετεχων comes from the Greek root words μετά (meta) meaning "with" or "after" and ἔχων (echon) meaning "having" or "possessing". Therefore, μετεχων can be translated to mean "having with" or "participating with".

Grammar:
μετεχων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the present participle of the verb μετέχω (metecho), which means "to share" or "to participate". As a participle, it functions as an adjective and describes a person who is currently sharing or participating in something.

Description:
μετεχων is a term used in ancient Greek literature to describe someone who is participating in a particular event or activity. It can refer to someone who is sharing in a meal, a conversation, or a religious ceremony. In some contexts, it can also be used to describe someone who is sharing in a particular emotion or feeling. Overall, μετεχων emphasizes the idea of communal participation and sharing.
