The word μενομεν is a verb in ancient Greek. Its etymology comes from the root μεν-, which means "to remain" or "to stay". The verb form μενομεν means "we remain" or "we stay". 

In terms of grammar, μενομεν is a present tense verb in the first person plural. It is conjugated from the verb μένω (menō), which means "to remain" or "to stay". 

In terms of description, μενομεν is a simple verb that expresses the action of remaining or staying in a particular place or situation. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone's physical location or their emotional state.
