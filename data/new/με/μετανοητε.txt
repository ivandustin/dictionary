The word μετανοητε (metanoeite) is a Greek verb that means "repent" or "change one's mind". It is derived from the Greek words μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond", and νοέω (noeo), meaning "to perceive" or "to understand". In Greek grammar, μετανοητε is the second person plural imperative form of the verb μετανοέω (metanoeo), which is used to command or urge a group of people to repent or change their ways.
