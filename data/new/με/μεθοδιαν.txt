Word: μεθοδιαν

Etymology:
The word μεθοδιαν (methodian) is derived from the Greek word μέθοδος (methodos), which means "method" or "way of proceeding". The suffix -ιαν (-ian) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives or nouns indicating a person or thing related to the root word.

Grammar:
μεθοδιαν is a noun in the masculine gender, singular number, and accusative case in the Greek language.

Description:
μεθοδιαν refers to a person who follows a methodical approach in their work or activities. It can also refer to someone who is skilled in a particular method or technique. The word is often used in academic or professional contexts to describe someone who is highly organized and systematic in their approach to tasks.
