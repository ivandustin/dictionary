Word: μελλούσαν

Etymology: μελλούσαν is the feminine form of the Greek verb μέλλω (méllō), which means "to be about to" or "to intend to". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *mel-, which means "to measure" or "to count".

Grammar: μελλούσαν is the third person plural, past continuous tense of the verb μέλλω (méllō). It is used to describe an action that was ongoing in the past and was expected to continue in the future. In English, it can be translated as "were about to" or "were intending to".

Description: μελλούσαν is a word that describes an action that was in progress in the past and was expected to continue in the future. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to create a sense of anticipation or suspense. For example, "Οι θεοί μελλούσαν να καταστρέψουν την πόλη" (The gods were about to destroy the city).
