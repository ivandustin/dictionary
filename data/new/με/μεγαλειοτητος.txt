Word: μεγαλειότητος

Etymology:
The word μεγαλειότητος is derived from the Greek words μεγαλεῖος (megalios) meaning "great" or "magnificent" and -τητος (-tetos) which is a suffix used to form abstract nouns. Therefore, μεγαλειότητος can be translated as "greatness" or "magnificence".

Grammar:
μεγαλειότητος is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. For example, "the greatness of the king" would be translated as "η μεγαλειότητα του βασιλιά" (i megaliotita tou vasilia).

Description:
μεγαλειότητος is a word that describes something that is grand, impressive, or majestic. It can be used to describe a person, an event, or an object that is considered to be of great importance or significance. For example, "the majesty of the mountains" would be translated as "η μεγαλειότητα των βουνών" (i megaliotita ton vounon).
