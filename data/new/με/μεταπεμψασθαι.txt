The word "μεταπεμψασθαι" is a Greek verb that means "to send back, to recall, to summon again". It is derived from the prefix "μετα-" which means "again" or "back" and the verb "πεμπω" which means "to send". 

In terms of grammar, "μεταπεμψασθαι" is a passive aorist infinitive form of the verb. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is being used as a noun, rather than as a specific action in a sentence.

Overall, "μεταπεμψασθαι" is a complex and specific verb that is used to describe the act of recalling or summoning someone or something back to a previous location or situation.
