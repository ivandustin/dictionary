Word: μελλει

Etymology:
The word μελλει (mellei) is a present tense form of the Greek verb μέλλω (mellō), which means "to be about to" or "to intend to". The verb μέλλω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *mel-, which means "to measure" or "to count".

Grammar:
μελλει (mellei) is a present tense form of the Greek verb μέλλω (mellō), which is a regular verb of the first conjugation. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Εγώ μέλλω (ego mellō) - I am about to
- Εσύ μέλλεις (esu melleis) - You are about to
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό μέλλει (autos/auti/auto mellei) - He/She/It is about to

Plural:
- Εμείς μέλλουμε (emeis melloume) - We are about to
- Εσείς μέλλετε (eseis mellete) - You are about to
- Αυτοί/Αυτές μέλλουν (autoi/autes melloun) - They are about to

Description:
μελλει (mellei) is a present tense form of the Greek verb μέλλω (mellō), which is commonly used to express an action that is about to happen in the near future. It can also be used to express an intention or a plan. For example, "Μελλει να βρέξει" (Mellei na vrexei) means "It is about to rain".
