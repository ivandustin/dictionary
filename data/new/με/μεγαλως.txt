Word: μεγαλως

Etymology: μεγαλως is an adverb in Greek that means "greatly" or "largely". It is derived from the adjective μεγάλος (megálos), which means "big" or "great".

Grammar: μεγαλως is an adverb, which means it modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb. It is often used to indicate the degree or extent of an action or quality. For example, "He greatly admired her talent" or "The project was largely successful".

Description: μεγαλως is a versatile adverb that can be used in a variety of contexts to express the degree or extent of something. It is commonly used in both formal and informal speech and writing.
