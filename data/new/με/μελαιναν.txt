Word: μελαιναν

Etymology:
The word μελαιναν comes from the Greek root word μέλας (melas) which means "black" or "dark". The suffix -αν (-an) is a verb ending that indicates the past tense, so μελαιναν means "they blackened" or "they made dark".

Grammar:
μελαιναν is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and voice.

Description:
μελαιναν can be translated as "they blackened" or "they made dark". It is often used to describe the darkening of a surface or object, such as when something is charred or burned. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a darkening of mood or emotions.
