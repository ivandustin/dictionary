Word: μενουσαν

Etymology: μενουσαν is the feminine form of the Greek verb μένω (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- meaning "to remain".

Grammar: μενουσαν is the present participle of the verb μένω (meno) in the feminine singular form. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently staying or remaining in a particular place or situation.

Description: μενουσαν can be translated as "staying" or "remaining" in English. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a female character who is waiting for someone or something, or who is in a state of contemplation or reflection.
