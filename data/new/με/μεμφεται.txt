Word: μεμφεται

Etymology:
The word μεμφεται (memphetai) is a present tense, third person singular form of the Greek verb μέμφομαι (memphomai), which means "to blame, to censure, to find fault with". The verb is derived from the noun μῶμος (mōmos), which means "blame, reproach, fault".

Grammar:
μεμφεται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb μέμφομαι. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person singular form means that the subject is he/she/it.

Description:
μεμφεται is a verb that expresses the action of blaming or finding fault with someone or something. It is often used in a negative context, to express disapproval or criticism. For example, "Ο πατέρας μεμφεται το γιο του για τη συμπεριφορά του" (The father blames his son for his behavior). The verb can also be used reflexively, as in "Ο ίδιος μεμφεται τον εαυτό του για τα λάθη του" (He blames himself for his mistakes).
