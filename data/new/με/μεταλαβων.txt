Word: μεταλαβων

Etymology: μεταλαβων is a Greek word that comes from the verb μεταλαμβάνω (metalambanō), which means "to take hold of, to grasp, to understand." The word is formed from the prefix μετά (meta), which means "with, after," and the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take, to receive."

Grammar: μεταλαβων is the present participle of the verb μεταλαμβάνω (metalambanō). In Greek, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. It can function as an adjective, modifying a noun, or as a verb, taking on the tense and voice of the main verb in the sentence.

Description: μεταλαβων can be translated as "grasping, understanding, taking hold of." It describes someone who is actively engaged in the process of grasping or understanding something. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from intellectual pursuits to physical actions. In philosophy, for example, it can refer to the process of grasping a concept or idea.
