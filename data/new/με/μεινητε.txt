Word: μεινητε

Etymology:
μεινητε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μένω (menō), which means "to remain" or "to stay". The verb form μεινητε is the second person plural present active imperative form of μένω.

Grammar:
μεινητε is a second person plural present active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards a group of people, telling them to "remain" or "stay". The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the active voice means that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
μεινητε is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospel of John. It is used by Jesus to instruct his disciples to remain with him and follow his teachings. The word carries a sense of steadfastness and commitment, urging the listener to stay true to their beliefs and not be swayed by outside influences.
