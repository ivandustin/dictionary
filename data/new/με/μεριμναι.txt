Word: μεριμναι

Etymology:
The word μεριμναι (merimnai) is derived from the Greek verb μεριμνάω (merimnao), which means "to be anxious or worried." It is a combination of the words μέρος (meros), meaning "part," and μνάομαι (mnaomai), meaning "to remember."

Grammar:
μεριμναι is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the first person singular form, meaning "I am anxious or worried."

Description:
μεριμναι is a Greek word that describes a state of being anxious or worried about something. It is often used in the context of caring for someone or something, such as a family member or a business. The word can also be used to describe a general feeling of unease or concern about a situation. In modern Greek, the word is commonly used to describe the act of worrying excessively or being overly concerned about something.
