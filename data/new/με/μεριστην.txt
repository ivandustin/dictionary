Word: μεριστην

Etymology:
The word μεριστην (meristēn) is derived from the Greek verb μερίζω (merizō), which means "to divide" or "to separate". The suffix -την (-tēn) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form nouns indicating a person or thing that performs a certain action or has a certain quality.

Grammar:
μεριστην is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In this form, it means "a divider" or "a separator". The word can also be used in other cases, such as the nominative case (μεριστής, meristēs) to indicate the subject of a sentence.

Description:
μεριστην is a Greek word that refers to a person or thing that divides or separates. It can be used in a literal sense, such as a tool or device that is used to divide or separate objects, or in a figurative sense, such as a person who separates or divides people or ideas. The word can also be used in a mathematical context to refer to a divisor or factor.
