Word: μενει

Etymology: μενει comes from the Greek verb μένω (menō), which means "to remain" or "to stay".

Grammar: μενει is the third person singular present tense form of the verb μένω (menō). It is often translated as "he/she/it remains" or "he/she/it stays".

Description: μενει is a common verb in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physical staying or remaining in a place, as well as to more abstract concepts such as remaining in a state of mind or emotion. It is often used in combination with other verbs to express more complex ideas, such as μένω εν ειρήνη (menō en eirēnē), which means "to remain in peace".
