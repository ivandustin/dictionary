Word: μετεκαλεσατο

Etymology:
μετεκαλεσατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and καλέω (kaleo) meaning "to call" or "to summon". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
μετεκαλεσατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
μετεκαλεσατο can be translated to mean "he/she/it called upon himself/herself/itself". The verb implies that the subject called upon themselves for a specific purpose or reason. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as calling upon a deity or higher power for assistance or guidance.
