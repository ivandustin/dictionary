Word: μετακαλεσαι

Etymology:
The word μετακαλεσαι is derived from the Greek words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and καλέω (kaleo) meaning "to call". Therefore, the literal meaning of μετακαλεσαι is "to call after" or "to call beyond".

Grammar:
μετακαλεσαι is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the second person singular form. The verb stem is καλεσ- and the prefix μετά (meta) indicates the action of calling after or beyond.

Description:
μετακαλεσαι is a Greek verb that means "to call after" or "to call beyond". It can be used in various contexts, such as calling someone after they have left, calling someone beyond their current location, or calling someone for a specific purpose. The verb can also be used metaphorically to refer to calling upon someone for help or support.
