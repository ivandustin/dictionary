Word: μεριμναν

Etymology:
The word μεριμναν (merimnan) is derived from the Greek verb μεριμνάω (merimnao), which means "to be anxious" or "to worry". It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix μερι- (meri-), which means "part" or "portion", and the verb μνάω (mnao), which means "to remember" or "to be mindful".

Grammar:
μεριμναν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb μεριμνάω (merimnao).

Description:
μεριμναν is a Greek word that means "he/she/it is anxious" or "he/she/it worries". It is often used in the context of expressing concern or worry about something. The word can also be used to describe a state of mind or emotion, such as being anxious or worried.
