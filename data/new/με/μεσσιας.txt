Word: μεσσιας

Etymology:
The word μεσσιας (messias) is derived from the Hebrew word מָשִׁיחַ (māšîaḥ), which means "anointed one" or "chosen one". In Greek, it was transliterated as μεσσίας (messías).

Grammar:
In Greek, μεσσιας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: μεσσιας
- Genitive: μεσσιου
- Accusative: μεσσιαν

Plural:
- Nominative: μεσσιαι
- Genitive: μεσσιων
- Accusative: μεσσιας

Description:
μεσσιας is a term that is commonly used in the Christian religion to refer to Jesus Christ as the Messiah or the Savior. It is also used in the Old Testament to refer to the expected deliverer of the Jewish people. The term has come to be associated with the idea of a divine figure who will bring salvation and redemption to humanity.
