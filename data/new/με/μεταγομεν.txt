Word: μεταγομεν

Etymology:
The word μεταγομεν (metagomen) is a present participle form of the Greek verb μετάγω (metago), which means "to transfer" or "to move from one place to another". The word is derived from the prefix μετά (meta), which means "after" or "beyond", and the verb ἄγω (ago), which means "to lead" or "to bring".

Grammar:
μεταγομεν is a present participle form of the verb μετάγω (metago) in the middle voice. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The present participle form indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
μεταγομεν can be translated as "being transferred" or "being moved from one place to another". It is often used in the context of the transfer of property or ownership, as well as the movement of people or things from one location to another. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a change or transformation in a person's character or behavior.
