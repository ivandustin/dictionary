Word: μετεχειν

Etymology:
μετεχειν is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: μετά (meta) meaning "with" or "after" and ἔχειν (echein) meaning "to have" or "to hold". Therefore, μετεχειν means "to have with" or "to participate in".

Grammar:
μετεχειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present active infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to a specific tense, person, or number. It is used to express the idea of participation or sharing in something.

Description:
μετεχειν is a word that conveys the idea of sharing or participating in something. It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe the participation of individuals in a divine or spiritual reality. In this sense, it can be translated as "to partake" or "to share in". It can also be used in a more general sense to describe participation in any kind of activity or event.
