Word: μενον

Etymology:
The word μενον is derived from the Greek verb μένω (menō), which means "to remain" or "to stay". It is the present participle of the verb, meaning "remaining" or "staying".

Grammar:
In Greek, μενον is a neuter noun in the nominative singular form. It is often used as an adverb, meaning "still" or "yet". It can also be used as an adjective, meaning "remaining" or "left".

Description:
μενον is a versatile word in Greek, used in a variety of contexts to convey the idea of something that remains or stays. It can be used to describe physical objects that are left behind, as well as abstract concepts like time or emotions that persist. As an adverb, it is often used to indicate that something is still ongoing or has not yet been completed. As an adjective, it can be used to describe something that is left over or remaining after other things have been used or consumed.
