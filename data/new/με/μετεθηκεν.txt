Word: μετεθηκεν

Etymology:
μετεθηκεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb μετατίθημι (metatithemi), which means "to transfer" or "to change". The word is formed by combining the prefix μετα- (meta-), which means "beyond" or "after", and the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put".

Grammar:
μετεθηκεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb μετατίθημι (metatithemi). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
μετεθηκεν can be translated as "he/she/it transferred" or "he/she/it changed". The word implies a deliberate action of moving something from one place to another or altering its state or condition. The context in which the word is used will determine the exact meaning.
