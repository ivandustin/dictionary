μεταδιδους (metadidous) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "giving over" or "handing over". 

Etymology: The word μεταδιδους is derived from the Greek words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and δίδωμι (didomi) meaning "to give". 

Grammar: μεταδιδους is a present participle verb in the active voice, which means that it describes an ongoing action of giving or handing over. It is formed by adding the suffix -οντας (-ontas) to the present stem of the verb δίδωμι (didomi). 

Description: μεταδιδους can be used in various contexts, such as in legal or financial transactions, where it refers to the transfer of ownership or possession of something from one person to another. It can also be used in a figurative sense, to describe the act of passing on knowledge or information to someone else.
