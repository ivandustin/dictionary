Word: μεθυουσιν

Etymology: The word μεθυουσιν comes from the Greek verb μεθύω (methyō), which means "to be drunk" or "to become intoxicated". It is derived from the noun μέθη (methē), which means "drunkenness" or "intoxication".

Grammar: μεθυουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is the present active indicative form of the verb μεθύω.

Description: μεθυουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they are getting drunk" or "they are becoming intoxicated". It is often used in the context of excessive drinking or drunkenness. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of being overcome by any kind of passion or emotion.
