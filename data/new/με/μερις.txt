Word: μερις

Etymology:
The word μερις (meris) comes from the Greek root word μερος (meros), which means "part" or "portion". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *mer-, which means "to divide" or "to share".

Grammar:
In Greek, μερις is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. Its genitive form is μεριδος (meridos), and its accusative form is μεριν (merin). It can be declined in all cases and numbers.

Description:
μερις is a term used in ancient Greek philosophy to refer to the concept of "participation" or "sharing". It was used by philosophers such as Plato and Aristotle to describe the relationship between the individual and the universal. In this context, μερις refers to the idea that individuals participate in the universal through their own unique qualities or characteristics. It is also used in other contexts to refer to a portion or share of something, such as a portion of land or a share of profits.
