Word: μεταστησας

Etymology:
μετα- (meta-) + στησας (stasis)
The prefix μετα- means "after" or "beyond," while στησας comes from the verb σταθμός (stathmos), meaning "a standing still." Therefore, μεταστησας can be translated as "to move beyond a standing still."

Grammar:
μεταστησας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb μεταστήσω (metastēsō), which means "to move, shift, or transfer."

Description:
μεταστησας is a Greek verb that describes the act of moving or shifting something from one place to another. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a change in position or perspective. The word is often used in the context of military strategy, where it refers to the movement of troops or resources to a different location.
