Word: μελλοντος

Etymology: μέλλω (méllō) means "to be about to" or "to intend to" in Greek. The suffix -οντος (-ontos) is the genitive singular form of the present participle, which is used to indicate ongoing action. Therefore, μελλοντος (mellontos) means "of one who is about to be" or "of the future."

Grammar: μελλοντος is a masculine noun in the genitive singular form. It is often used as an adjective to describe something that is about to happen or is expected to happen in the future.

Description: μελλοντος is a word that is commonly used in Greek to refer to the future. It can be used to describe events, people, or things that are expected to happen or exist in the future. It is often used in phrases such as "το μέλλον" (to méllon), which means "the future."
