The word "μεμερισται" (memeristai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root word "μερίζω" (merizo), which means "to divide" or "to distribute." 

In its passive form, "μεμερισται" means "to be divided" or "to be distributed." It can also be translated as "to be shared" or "to be apportioned." 

In terms of grammar, "μεμερισται" is a third-person singular verb, meaning it refers to a single subject. The tense, voice, and mood indicate that the action is happening in the present and is being done to the subject rather than by the subject. 

Overall, "μεμερισται" is a word that describes the act of something being divided or shared, and it is commonly used in Greek language and culture.
