Word: μερίδος

Etymology:
The word μερίδος (meridos) is derived from the Greek word μέρος (meros), which means "part" or "portion". The suffix -ιδος (-idos) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns indicating a quality or characteristic.

Grammar:
μερίδος is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description:
μερίδος refers to a portion or share of something, often used in the context of dividing something into parts. It can also refer to a dividend or distribution of profits. The word is commonly used in business and finance contexts.
