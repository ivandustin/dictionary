Word: μεταστρεψαι

Etymology: μετα- (meta-, prefix meaning "change" or "beyond") + στρέφω (stréphō, "to turn"). Therefore, μεταστρέφω (metastrephō) means "to turn around" or "to change direction".

Grammar: μεταστρεψαι is the aorist tense of the verb μεταστρέφω (metastrephō), which means "to turn around" or "to change direction". It is in the active voice and the indicative mood. 

Description: μεταστρεψαι is a Greek verb that describes the action of turning around or changing direction. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone physically turns around, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone changes their mind or attitude. The aorist tense indicates that the action was completed in the past, without specifying the duration or repetition of the action.
