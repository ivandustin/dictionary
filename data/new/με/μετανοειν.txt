Word: μετανοειν

Etymology: μετανοειν comes from the Greek words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and νοεῖν (noein) meaning "to think" or "to perceive". Therefore, μετανοειν means "to think differently" or "to change one's mind".

Grammar: μετανοειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood.

Description: μετανοειν is a term used in Christian theology to describe the act of repentance or turning away from sin. It involves a change of heart and a commitment to live a new life in accordance with God's will. In Greek literature, the term is also used to describe a change of mind or a change of direction in one's life.
