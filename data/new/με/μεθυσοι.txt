Word: μεθυσοι

Etymology:
The word μεθυσοι (methusoi) is derived from the Greek word μέθυ (methu), which means "drunkenness" or "intoxication". The suffix -σοι (-soi) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple individuals who are drunk.

Grammar:
μεθυσοι is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence, and indicates that the word is the one performing the action.

Description:
μεθυσοι is a Greek word that refers to individuals who are drunk or intoxicated. The word can be used to describe a group of people who are drinking together or who have become drunk together. In ancient Greek culture, drinking was often associated with social gatherings and celebrations, and excessive drinking was sometimes seen as a sign of weakness or lack of self-control. Today, the word μεθυσοι is still used in modern Greek to refer to individuals who are drunk or intoxicated.
