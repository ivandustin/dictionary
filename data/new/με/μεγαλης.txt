Word: μεγαλης

Etymology:
μεγαλης is a Greek word that comes from the root word μέγας (megas), which means "great" or "large". The suffix -ης (-es) is a masculine genitive singular ending, which means that μεγαλης is the genitive form of the adjective μεγάλος (megálos), meaning "great" or "large".

Grammar:
μεγαλης is a masculine genitive singular form of the adjective μεγάλος (megálos). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship, and in this case, μεγαλης is modifying a masculine noun.

Description:
μεγαλης is an adjective that describes something as "great" or "large". As a genitive form, it is used to show possession or relationship with a masculine noun. For example, μεγαλης χώρα (megális chóra) means "great country" or "large country", where χώρα (chóra) is a feminine noun.
