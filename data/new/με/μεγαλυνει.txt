Word: μεγαλυνει

Etymology: The word μεγαλυνει comes from the Greek verb μεγαλύνω (megalýno), which means "to enlarge" or "to magnify". It is derived from the adjective μέγας (mégas), meaning "great" or "large".

Grammar: μεγαλυνει is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is in the third person singular form, which means that it refers to a single subject who is performing the action of enlarging or magnifying.

Description: μεγαλυνει is a verb that describes the act of making something larger or more significant. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to the magnification of an object through a microscope, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing the amplification of someone's voice or the exaggeration of a story. The word can also be used to describe the act of glorifying or praising someone or something, as in the phrase "to magnify the Lord".
