Word: μετανοιας

Etymology:
The word μετανοιας comes from the Greek word μετάνοια (metanoia), which means "repentance" or "change of mind". The suffix -ς (-s) indicates the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship.

Grammar:
μετανοιας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is often used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description:
μετανοιας refers to the act of repentance or the state of being repentant. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, but can also be used more broadly to refer to a change of heart or a change of mind. The word carries connotations of remorse, regret, and a desire to make amends for past actions.
