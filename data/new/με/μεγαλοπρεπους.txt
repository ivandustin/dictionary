Word: μεγαλοπρεπους

Etymology: μεγαλοπρεπους comes from the Greek words μέγας (megas) meaning "great" and πρεπής (prepeis) meaning "fitting, proper". 

Grammar: μεγαλοπρεπους is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: μεγαλοπρεπους is used to describe something that is grand, majestic, or impressive. It can be used to describe a person, a building, an event, or anything that is considered to be grand or impressive. For example, one might use μεγαλοπρεπους to describe a magnificent palace or a regal king.
