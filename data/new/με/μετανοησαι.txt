Word: μετανοησαι

Etymology:
The word μετανοησαι comes from the Greek root words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and νοέω (noeo) meaning "to perceive" or "to understand". Together, μετανοέω (metanoeo) means "to change one's mind" or "to repent".

Grammar:
μετανοησαι is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense. It is the second person singular form of the verb μετανοέω (metanoeo), which means "to repent" or "to change one's mind". In this form, it is a command or an imperative, meaning it is used to give a direct order or instruction.

Description:
μετανοησαι is a word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to the act of repentance or turning away from sin. It is a call to change one's ways and to seek forgiveness for past wrongs. The word carries a sense of urgency and importance, emphasizing the need for individuals to take responsibility for their actions and to seek redemption.
