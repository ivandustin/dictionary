Word: μελλομεν

Etymology:
μελλομεν is a present participle of the Greek verb μέλλω (méllō), which means "to be about to" or "to intend to". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *mel-, which means "to measure" or "to count".

Grammar:
μελλομεν is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the present stem of the verb μέλλω (méllō).

Description:
μελλομεν is used to indicate an action that is about to happen or is intended to happen in the future. It can be translated as "about to" or "going to". For example, in the sentence "Μελλομεν να φάμε" (Mellomen na fame), which means "We are about to eat", μελλομεν indicates that the action of eating is going to happen soon.
