Word: μελων

Etymology:
The word μελων (melōn) is derived from the Greek word μέλος (mélos), which means "limb" or "member". It is related to the Greek verb μέλειν (mélein), which means "to be a care or concern".

Grammar:
μελων is a noun in the singular form. It is a masculine noun in the second declension.

Description:
μελων is used in Greek to refer to a limb or member of the body, such as an arm or a leg. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a part of a larger group or organization. For example, in ancient Greek literature, the term was used to refer to a member of a chorus or a musical group.
