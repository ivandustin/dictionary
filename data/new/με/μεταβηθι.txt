μεταβηθι (metabēthi) is a Greek verb in the imperative mood, second person singular. It comes from the verb μεταβαίνω (metabainō), which means "to go across" or "to pass over". The imperative form μεταβηθι is a command to someone to "go across" or "pass over". It is often used in the context of crossing a river or a bridge.
