Word: μετεπειτα

Etymology: μετεπειτα is a Greek adverb that is derived from the combination of two words, μετά (meta) meaning "after" and επειτα (epeita) meaning "then". Therefore, μετεπειτα means "afterwards" or "later".

Grammar: μετεπειτα is an adverb in Greek and is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It does not change form based on the gender, number, or case of the noun it modifies.

Description: μετεπειτα is a common word in Greek and is used to indicate that something happened after a certain point in time. It can be used in both formal and informal contexts. For example, "Μετεπειτα από τη συνάντησή μας, πήγα στο σπίτι" (After our meeting, I went home).
