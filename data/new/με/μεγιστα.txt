Word: μεγιστα

Etymology:
The word μεγιστα (megista) is derived from the Greek word μέγας (megas), which means "great" or "large". The suffix -ιστα (-ista) is a superlative suffix, which means "the greatest" or "the largest". Therefore, μεγιστα means "the greatest" or "the largest".

Grammar:
μεγιστα is a neuter plural adjective in Greek. It is used to describe a noun that is in the neuter gender and in the plural form. For example, μεγιστα βουνά (megista vouna) means "the greatest mountains".

Description:
μεγιστα is an adjective that is used to describe something that is the greatest or the largest in size, quantity, or importance. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as mountains or buildings, or abstract concepts, such as achievements or accomplishments. In Greek literature and mythology, μεγιστα is often used to describe the gods or their attributes, emphasizing their greatness and power.
