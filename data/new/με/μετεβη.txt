Word: μετεβη

Etymology:
μετεβη is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and εβη (ebe) meaning "went". Therefore, the literal meaning of μετεβη is "went beyond" or "went after".

Grammar:
μετεβη is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb μεταβαίνω (metabaino) which means "to go beyond" or "to pass over". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
μετεβη is a verb that describes an action of going beyond or passing over something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as crossing a river or a mountain, or in a figurative sense, such as surpassing a challenge or obstacle. It is a strong and active verb that conveys a sense of determination and accomplishment.
