μετοικεσίας (metoikesías) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb μετοικέω (metoikéo), which means "to change one's residence, to migrate". The noun μετοικεσίας refers to the act of migration or the state of being a migrant. 

In terms of grammar, μετοικεσίας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It can be declined in various cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions in a sentence.

Overall, μετοικεσίας is a word that is often used in discussions about immigration and the movement of people from one place to another.
