Word: μεγαλους

Etymology:
μεγαλους is a Greek word that comes from the root word μέγας (megas), which means "great" or "large". The suffix -ους (-ous) is a masculine plural ending, indicating that the word is referring to a group of great or large things.

Grammar:
μεγαλους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe or modify a noun, indicating that the noun is great or large. In Greek, adjectives must agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, μεγαλους is used to describe masculine plural nouns.

Description:
μεγαλους is a descriptive word that is used to indicate that something is great or large. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as buildings or mountains, or abstract concepts, such as ideas or accomplishments. In Greek literature and mythology, the word is often used to describe powerful gods or heroes.
