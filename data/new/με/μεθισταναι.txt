Word: μεθισταναι

Etymology:
The word μεθισταναι comes from the Greek verb μεθίστημι (methistemi), which means "to transfer" or "to change". It is derived from the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "beyond" or "after", and the verb ἵστημι (histemi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar:
μεθισταναι is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The word is also in the infinitive form, which means that it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
μεθισταναι means "to change" or "to transfer" in Greek. It is often used in a figurative sense, to describe a change in someone's beliefs, opinions, or attitudes. The word can also be used in a more literal sense, to describe the transfer of something from one place to another. Overall, μεθισταναι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe different types of change or transfer.
