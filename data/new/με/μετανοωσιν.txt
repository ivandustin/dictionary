Word: μετανοωσιν

Etymology:
μετανοωσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μετανοέω (metanoeo), which means "to change one's mind" or "to repent." The suffix -σιν (-sin) indicates that it is in the third person plural present active indicative form.

Grammar:
μετανοωσιν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is in the third person plural form, which means that it refers to a group of people who are currently repenting or changing their minds.

Description:
μετανοωσιν is a word that describes the act of repentance or changing one's mind. It is often used in a religious context, particularly in Christianity, where it refers to the act of turning away from sin and turning towards God.
