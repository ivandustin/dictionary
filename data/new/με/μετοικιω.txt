μετοικιω (metoikiō) is a Greek verb that means "to migrate" or "to move to a new place". It is derived from the words μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond", and οἰκία (oikia), meaning "house" or "dwelling". The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Μετοικεῖς (metoikeis) - you migrate
- Μετοικεῖ (metoikei) - he/she/it migrates
- Μετοικοῦμεν (metoikoumen) - we migrate
- Μετοικεῖτε (metoikete) - you (pl.) migrate
- Μετοικοῦσι(ν) (metoikousi(n)) - they migrate

In terms of grammar, μετοικιω is a first-conjugation verb, meaning that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω. It is also a transitive verb, meaning that it takes a direct object. 

Overall, μετοικιω is a useful word for describing the act of moving or migrating to a new place.
