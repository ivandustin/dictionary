Word: μενειν

Etymology:
The word μενειν (menein) is a Greek verb that means "to stay" or "to remain". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- which means "to remain" or "to stay".

Grammar:
μενειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is the infinitive form of the verb μένω (meno), which means "I stay" or "I remain". The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: μένω (meno) - I stay, you stay, he/she/it stays
- Plural: μένουμε (menoume) - we stay, you stay, they stay

Description:
μενειν is a common verb in Greek that is used to express the idea of staying or remaining in a particular place or situation. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as:

- To describe someone staying in a particular location: "Μένω στο Λονδίνο" (Meno sto Londino) - "I stay in London"
- To express the idea of remaining in a particular state or condition: "Μένω αισιόδοξος" (Meno aisiodoxos) - "I remain optimistic"
- To describe someone staying in a particular job or position: "Μένω στη θέση μου" (Meno sti thesi mou) - "I stay in my position"

Overall, μενειν is a versatile verb that is used frequently in Greek to express the idea of staying or remaining in a particular place, state, or situation.
