Word: μεθυουσαν

Etymology: μεθυουσαν is the feminine form of the Greek verb μεθύω (methyō), which means "to be drunk" or "to get drunk". The word comes from the noun μέθη (methē), which means "drunkenness" or "intoxication".

Grammar: μεθυουσαν is a verb in the present participle form, which indicates ongoing or continuous action. It is in the feminine gender, singular number, and accusative case. The verb is derived from the root μεθύω (methyō), which is a first conjugation verb in Ancient Greek.

Description: μεθυουσαν describes a female subject who is currently in a state of drunkenness or intoxication. The word can be used in a literal sense to describe someone who has consumed too much alcohol, or in a figurative sense to describe someone who is acting recklessly or irresponsibly.
