μεταδίδω (metadídō) is a Greek verb that means "to transmit" or "to pass on". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "beyond" or "after", and the verb δίδωμι (dídōmi), meaning "to give". 

Grammar: μεταδίδω is a first conjugation verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood. Its stem is μεταδοτ-, and it follows regular conjugation patterns. 

Description: The verb μεταδίδω is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of transmitting or passing on something, such as information, a disease, or a message. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as "to pass on" a tradition or a legacy.
