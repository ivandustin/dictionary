μεταδω (metadō) is a Greek verb that means "to transmit" or "to pass on." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and the verb δίδωμι (didōmi), meaning "to give." 

Grammar: μεταδω is a first-conjugation verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood. Its stem is μεταδο-, and its endings change depending on the subject of the sentence. 

Description: The verb μεταδω is often used in the context of communication or the transfer of information or disease. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the passing on of something from one person or thing to another.
