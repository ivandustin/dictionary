Word: μεταπεμπομενος

Etymology: μετα- (meta-, prefix meaning "after" or "beyond") + πέμπω (pempo, "to send") + -μενος (-menos, suffix indicating passive voice and present participle). Therefore, μεταπεμπομενος means "being sent after" or "being summoned".

Grammar: μεταπεμπομενος is a present participle in the passive voice, which means it describes an ongoing action that is being done to the subject. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it could be translated as "being sent after" or "being summoned".

Description: μεταπεμπομενος is a Greek word that describes an ongoing action of being sent after or summoned. It is often used in legal or official contexts, such as summoning a witness to court or sending a message to someone. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is being pursued or chased after.
