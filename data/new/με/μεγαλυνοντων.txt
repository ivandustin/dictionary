Word: μεγαλυνοντων

Etymology:
μεγαλυνοντων is a present active participle of the Greek verb μεγαλύνω (megalýno), which means "to magnify" or "to enlarge". The word is derived from the adjective μέγας (mégas), meaning "great" or "large".

Grammar:
μεγαλυνοντων is a present active participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous, and the genitive case indicates possession or association.

Description:
μεγαλυνοντων can be translated as "of those who magnify" or "of those who enlarge". It is often used in a religious context to refer to those who magnify or glorify God. The word can also be used more generally to describe those who make something bigger or more important.
