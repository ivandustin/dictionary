Word: μεταβαίνετε

Etymology:
μεταβαίνετε is a Greek verb that comes from the root words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go" or "to walk". Therefore, μεταβαίνετε means "you go beyond" or "you walk after".

Grammar:
μεταβαίνετε is a second-person plural present indicative active verb in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are currently going beyond or walking after something.

Description:
μεταβαίνετε is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to describe physical movement, such as walking or traveling to a different location. It can also be used metaphorically to describe progress or advancement in a particular field or area of life. Overall, μεταβαίνετε is a useful verb that conveys the idea of moving forward or going beyond.
