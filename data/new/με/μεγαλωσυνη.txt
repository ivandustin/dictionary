Word: μεγαλωσυνη

Etymology:
The word μεγαλωσυνη (megalo̱syni̱) is derived from the Greek words μεγάλος (megálos) meaning "great" and ωσυνη (o̱syni̱) meaning "quality" or "state of being". Therefore, the word μεγαλωσυνη can be translated to mean "greatness" or "magnificence".

Grammar:
In Greek, μεγαλωσυνη is a feminine noun. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence. For example, "Η μεγαλωσυνη του βουνου ειναι εντυπωσιακη" (The greatness of the mountain is impressive).

Description:
μεγαλωσυνη is a word that describes a state of being great or magnificent. It can be used to describe a person, place, or thing that is impressive or awe-inspiring. It is often used in a poetic or literary context to convey a sense of grandeur or majesty.
