Word: μενουσιν

Etymology: The word μενουσιν is a present active participle of the Greek verb μένω (menō), which means "to remain" or "to stay". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates the third person plural form.

Grammar: μενουσιν is a present active participle in the Greek language. It is declined according to gender, number, and case. In this form, it is masculine, plural, and in the nominative case.

Description: The word μενουσιν is used to describe a group of people who are currently remaining or staying in a particular place. It can also be used to describe a group of people who are persevering or enduring through a difficult situation.
