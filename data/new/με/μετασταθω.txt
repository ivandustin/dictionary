Word: μετασταθω

Etymology:
The word μετασταθω comes from the Greek roots μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and σταθμός (stathmos) meaning "station" or "post". The word can be translated to mean "to change station" or "to move beyond".

Grammar:
μετασταθω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated in the first person singular form.

Description:
μετασταθω is a Greek verb that means "to change station" or "to move beyond". It can be used in a literal sense to describe a physical movement from one station to another, or in a figurative sense to describe a change in position or status. The word can also be used to describe a change in mindset or perspective.
