Word: μενετω

Etymology:
The word μενετω comes from the Greek verb μένω (menō), which means "to remain" or "to stay". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- meaning "to remain, stay".

Grammar:
μενετω is a verb in the present active imperative form in Greek. It is the third person singular form of the verb μένω (menō), which means "let him/her/it remain" or "let him/her/it stay".

Description:
μενετω is a command or request for someone or something to remain or stay in a particular place or state. It is often used in the context of a command to persevere or endure through a difficult situation. The word can also be used metaphorically to mean "to continue" or "to persist" in a particular course of action or belief.
