Word: μελλεις

Etymology:
The word μελλεις (melleis) is a present tense verb form in Greek, derived from the root word μελλω (mellō), which means "to be about to" or "to intend to". The word has its origins in the Proto-Indo-European root *mel-, which means "to measure" or "to mark".

Grammar:
μελλεις is a second-person singular present indicative active form of the verb μελλω (mellō). It is used to indicate that the subject (you) are about to do something or intend to do something in the present.

Description:
μελλεις is a common Greek verb that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express future intentions or plans, such as "you are about to go to the store" or "you intend to study tonight". The word can also be used in a more figurative sense, to express a sense of impending action or change, such as "you are on the verge of a breakthrough" or "you are about to experience a major life change".
