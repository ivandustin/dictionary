μετοχούς (metochous) is a Greek word that is derived from the root word μετέχω (metecho), which means "to participate" or "to share". In Greek grammar, μετοχούς is a noun in the plural form, which means "participants" or "sharers". It is often used in the context of business or finance to refer to shareholders or investors who have a stake in a company. In terms of its grammar, μετοχούς is a third declension noun, which means that its endings change depending on its case and gender.
