Word: μελλησετε

Etymology: The word μελλησετε is derived from the Greek verb μέλλω (méllo), which means "to be about to" or "to intend to". The suffix -σετε (-sete) is the second person plural future tense ending.

Grammar: μελλησετε is a second person plural future tense verb in Greek. It is used to indicate an action that will happen in the future and is directed towards a group of people.

Description: μελλησετε is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of "you (plural) will be about to" or "you (plural) will intend to". It is often used in contexts where a group of people are planning or preparing to do something in the near future. For example, "Μελλησετε να φύγετε από εδώ;" (Melletesete na fygete apo edo?) means "Are you (plural) about to leave from here?" or "Do you (plural) intend to leave from here?".
