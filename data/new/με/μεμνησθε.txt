Word: μεμνησθε

Etymology:
The word μεμνησθε comes from the Greek verb μιμνήσκω (mimnisko), which means "to remember" or "to recall". The prefix με- (me-) indicates that the verb is in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests.

Grammar:
μεμνησθε is the second person plural form of the imperative mood of the verb μιμνήσκω. It is used to give a command or make a request to a group of people, and it translates to "remember" or "recall".

Description:
μεμνησθε is a word that is often used in Greek literature and religious texts. It is a call to remember something important, whether it be a past event, a moral principle, or a religious teaching. In the New Testament, for example, Jesus often uses the imperative form of μιμνήσκω to remind his disciples of his teachings and the importance of following them. The word μεμνησθε is a powerful reminder to stay focused on what is important and not to forget the lessons of the past.
