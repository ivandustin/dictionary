Word: μετετεθη

Etymology:
μετετεθη is a Greek word that comes from the verb μετατίθημι (metatithemi), which means "to transfer" or "to change". The word is formed by adding the prefix μετα- (meta-) which means "beyond" or "after" to the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put".

Grammar:
μετετεθη is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
μετετεθη can be translated to English as "was transferred" or "was changed". The word implies that something was moved or altered from one place or state to another. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the transfer of property, the change of a person's status, or the movement of an object from one location to another.
