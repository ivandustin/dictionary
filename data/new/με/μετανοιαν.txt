The word "μετανοιαν" (metanoian) is a Greek noun that means "repentance" or "remorse." It is derived from the Greek verb "μετανοεω" (metanoeo), which means "to change one's mind" or "to repent." In Greek grammar, "μετανοιαν" is in the accusative case, indicating that it is the direct object of a verb or preposition. Overall, "μετανοιαν" is a term that refers to the act of acknowledging and regretting one's wrongdoing, and seeking to make amends or change one's behavior.
