Word: μερισασθαι

Etymology:
The word μερισασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μερίζω (merizo), which means "to divide" or "to share". The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a reflexive suffix that indicates that the action is being done to oneself.

Grammar:
μερισασθαι is a middle voice verb in the aorist tense. In Greek, the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event.

Description:
μερισασθαι means "to divide oneself" or "to share oneself". It implies a voluntary act of dividing or sharing oneself with others. This verb can be used in a literal sense, such as dividing one's possessions or sharing one's time with others. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as sharing one's knowledge or experiences with others.
