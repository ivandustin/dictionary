Word: μενετε

Etymology:
μενετε is a present tense verb in Greek, which comes from the root word μένω (menō) meaning "to remain" or "to stay". 

Grammar:
μενετε is a second person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all remain" or "you all stay". 

Description:
μενετε is a verb that is commonly used in Greek to express the idea of staying or remaining in a particular place or situation. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to encourage someone to stay in a particular location or to express the idea of persistence or endurance. The word is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to express the idea of remaining steadfast in one's beliefs or principles.
