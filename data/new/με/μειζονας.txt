Word: μειζονας

Etymology:
The word μειζονας (meizonas) is derived from the Greek word μείζων (meizon), which means "greater" or "larger". It is a comparative form of the adjective μέγας (megas), which means "big" or "great".

Grammar:
μειζονας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the comparative form of the adjective μείζων (meizon), which means "greater" or "larger". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description:
μειζονας is used to describe something that is greater or larger in size, quantity, or importance than something else. It can be used to compare two or more things, and is often used in a figurative sense to describe abstract concepts such as power, influence, or significance.
