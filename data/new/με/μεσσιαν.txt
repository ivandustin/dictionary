The word "μεσσιαν" (messian) comes from the Greek word "Μεσσίας" (Messias), which means "anointed one" or "savior". In grammar, it is a noun that refers to a person who is believed to be a savior or a deliverer. In description, it is often used in religious contexts to refer to the expected savior or deliverer in various religions, including Judaism and Christianity.
