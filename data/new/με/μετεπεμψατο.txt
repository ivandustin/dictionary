Word: μετεπεμψατο

Etymology: μετεπεμψατο is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and the verb ἐπέμψατο (epémpsato), meaning "he sent." Therefore, μετεπεμψατο means "he sent back" or "he returned."

Grammar: μετεπεμψατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is acting upon himself or herself.

Description: μετεπεμψατο is a verb that describes the act of sending something or someone back to its original location or sender. It can also be used to describe the act of returning to a previous location or state. In Ancient Greek literature, this verb is often used in the context of military campaigns or diplomatic missions, where messengers or envoys are sent back and forth between different parties.
