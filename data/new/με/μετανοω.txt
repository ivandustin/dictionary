Word: μετανοω

Etymology:
The word μετανοω comes from the Greek roots μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and νοέω (noeo) meaning "to perceive" or "to think". Together, μετανοω means "to change one's mind" or "to repent".

Grammar:
μετανοω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: μετανοω (metanoo)
- Plural: μετανοουμεν (metanooumen)

Description:
μετανοω is a term used in Christian theology to describe the act of repentance or turning away from sin. It is often associated with the idea of confessing one's sins and seeking forgiveness. In Greek literature, the word is also used to describe a change of mind or a change of heart.
