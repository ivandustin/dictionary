Word: μεγαλαι

Etymology:
μεγαλαι (megálai) is the feminine plural form of the Greek adjective μεγάλος (megálos), which means "big" or "great". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *meg- which means "great" or "large".

Grammar:
μεγαλαι is a feminine plural form of the adjective μεγάλος. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, μεγαλαι is used to describe feminine plural nouns.

Description:
μεγαλαι is an adjective that describes something as big or great. It can be used to describe physical size, as well as abstract concepts such as importance or significance. As a feminine plural form, it is used to describe multiple feminine nouns that are big or great.
