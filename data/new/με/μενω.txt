Word: μενω

Etymology:
The word μενω (meno) is a Greek verb that means "to stay" or "to remain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- which means "to remain, stay".

Grammar:
μενω (meno) is a first conjugation verb in Greek, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω (o). It is an active verb, which means that it describes an action that the subject performs. It can be conjugated in all tenses, moods, and voices.

Description:
μενω (meno) is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical staying or remaining in a place, as well as metaphorical staying or remaining in a state or condition. For example, μενω μακρια απο το σπιτι (meno makria apo to spiti) means "I stay far from home", while μενω ευχαριστημενος (meno efcharistimenos) means "I remain satisfied". It is a common verb in Greek literature and everyday speech.
