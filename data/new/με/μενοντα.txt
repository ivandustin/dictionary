μενοντα (menonta) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb μένω (meno), which means "to remain" or "to stay". The participle form indicates ongoing action, so μενοντα can be translated as "remaining" or "staying". 

Etymology: μενοντα comes from the verb μένω, which is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- meaning "to remain" or "to stay". 

Grammar: μενοντα is a present active participle in the masculine singular nominative case. 

Example sentence: Οι φίλοι μου είναι στο σπίτι, μενοντα εκεί μέχρι το βράδυ. (My friends are at home, staying there until the evening.)
