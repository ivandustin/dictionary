μετρου (metrou) is a Greek noun that means "measure" or "measurement". 

Etymology: The word μετρου comes from the Greek verb μετρέω (metréo), which means "to measure". 

Grammar: μετρου is a singular noun in the genitive case. 

Description: μετρου is often used in the context of measuring length, weight, or quantity. It can also refer to a standard or unit of measurement. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was often associated with μετρου, as he was considered the god of music and harmony, which require precise measurements.
