Word: μεριμνατε

Etymology:
The word μεριμνατε comes from the Greek verb μεριμνάω (merimnáo), which means "to be anxious, to worry, to care for". It is derived from the noun μέριμνα (merimna), which means "anxiety, care, concern".

Grammar:
μεριμνατε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form, in the active voice. It is the imperative form of the verb μεριμνάω (merimnáo), which means "you (plural) worry, care for, be anxious".

Description:
μεριμνατε is a command or request for a group of people to worry or care for something. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the Bible where Jesus tells his disciples to "take no thought for your life, what ye shall eat, or what ye shall drink; nor yet for your body, what ye shall put on" (Matthew 6:25). The word can also be used in a more general sense to encourage someone to be diligent or attentive to a task or responsibility.
