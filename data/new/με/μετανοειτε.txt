Word: μετανοειτε

Etymology: μετανοειτε is a Greek word that comes from the combination of two words: μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and νοεῖτε (noeite) meaning "you think" or "you understand". Therefore, μετανοειτε means "to think beyond" or "to change one's mind".

Grammar: μετανοειτε is a verb in the imperative mood, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb μετανοέω (metanoeo) which means "to repent" or "to change one's mind". The verb μετανοειτε is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people to change their ways or behavior.

Description: μετανοειτε is a word that is often used in religious contexts, particularly in Christianity. It is a call to repentance and a change of heart and mind. The word emphasizes the need for individuals to turn away from their sinful ways and turn towards a more righteous path. In a broader sense, μετανοειτε can be applied to any situation where a change of mind or behavior is necessary for personal growth or improvement.
