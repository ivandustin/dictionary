Word: μεταβα

Etymology: μεταβα comes from the Greek words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to go" or "to walk". 

Grammar: μεταβα is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description: μεταβα means "to pass over" or "to cross over" in Greek. It can be used in a literal sense, such as crossing a river or a bridge, or in a figurative sense, such as crossing over from one state of being to another. It is often used in the context of spiritual or philosophical journeys.
