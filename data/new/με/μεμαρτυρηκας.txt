Word: μεμαρτυρηκας

Etymology: The word μεμαρτυρηκας is derived from the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The prefix με- (me-) is a perfective prefix, indicating that the action of bearing witness has been completed in the past.

Grammar: μεμαρτυρηκας is the second person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb μαρτυρέω. In English, it translates to "you have testified" or "you bore witness". The perfect tense indicates that the action was completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present.

Description: μεμαρτυρηκας is a word that conveys the idea of having given testimony or bearing witness to something in the past. It is often used in legal or religious contexts, where the act of testifying is important. The word emphasizes the completion of the action and the ongoing relevance of the testimony.
