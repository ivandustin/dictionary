Word: μετεστησεν

Etymology: μετεστησεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb μεταστήσειν (metastḗsein), which means "to transfer" or "to move from one place to another."

Grammar: μετεστησεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb μεταστήσειν (metastḗsein). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: μετεστησεν can be translated as "he/she/it transferred" or "he/she/it moved from one place to another." The word implies a deliberate action of moving something or someone from one location to another. It can be used in a literal sense, such as moving a physical object, or in a figurative sense, such as transferring power or authority from one person to another.
