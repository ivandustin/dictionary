Word: μεμφομενος

Etymology:
The word μεμφομενος comes from the Greek verb μέμφομαι (mémphomai), which means "to blame, to reproach". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous. Therefore, μεμφομενος means "blaming, reproaching".

Grammar:
μεμφομενος is a masculine singular participle in the present tense, middle voice, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb μέμφομαι (mémphomai), which is a middle voice verb that means "to blame oneself, to reproach oneself". The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
μεμφομενος is an adjective that describes someone who is constantly blaming or reproaching themselves. It can also be used to describe someone who is constantly blaming or reproaching others. The word has a negative connotation and implies a sense of self-criticism or criticism towards others.
