Word: μετρουντες

Etymology: The word μετρουντες is derived from the Greek verb μετρώ (metró), which means "to measure". The suffix -ουντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently measuring something.

Grammar: μετρουντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -ουντες to the stem μετρώ.

Description: μετρουντες refers to people who are currently measuring something. The context in which the word is used will determine what is being measured. For example, in the sentence "Οι μετρούντες μετρούν το μήκος του δρόμου" (The people measuring are measuring the length of the road), μετρουντες refers to people who are measuring the length of a road.
