Word: μετακαλεσομαι

Etymology:
The word μετακαλεσομαι is derived from the Greek words μετά (meta) meaning "after" or "beyond" and καλέω (kaleo) meaning "to call". Therefore, the word μετακαλεσομαι means "to call after" or "to call beyond".

Grammar:
μετακαλεσομαι is a Greek verb in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is in the present tense and the first person singular form. The verb is conjugated as follows:

μετακαλεσομαι (present tense, first person singular)

Description:
μετακαλεσομαι is a Greek verb that means "to call after" or "to call beyond". It can be used in various contexts, such as calling someone after they have left, calling someone to come to a different location, or calling someone to a higher purpose or goal. The verb is often used in a figurative sense, such as calling someone to a higher level of morality or spirituality.
