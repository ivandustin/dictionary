Word: μεταβας

Etymology:
μεταβας is a Greek word that comes from the verb μεταβαίνω (metabaino), which means "to pass over" or "to cross over". The word is composed of two parts: μετά (meta), which means "after" or "beyond", and βαίνω (baino), which means "to go" or "to walk".

μεταβας is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is in the second person singular form, which means that it is addressing one person.

μεταβας can be translated to English as "you crossed over" or "you passed over".
