Word: μετελάμβανον

Etymology:
μετελάμβανον is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and the verb ἐλαμβάνω (elambanō), meaning "to take" or "to receive." Therefore, μετελάμβανον can be translated as "to take after" or "to receive beyond."

Grammar:
μετελάμβανον is a verb in the imperfect tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person plural form of the verb, meaning "we were taking after" or "we were receiving beyond."

Description:
μετελάμβανον is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark (14:33) and the Acts of the Apostles (20:9). In both instances, it is used to describe a state of being or an action that was ongoing in the past. The word conveys a sense of continuation or repetition, suggesting that the action of taking after or receiving beyond was happening over a period of time.
