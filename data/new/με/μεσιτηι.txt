The word "μεσιτηι" (mesitēi) is a Greek noun that means "mediator" or "intermediary". It is derived from the Greek word "μέσος" (mesos), which means "middle" or "between", and "ἵστημι" (histēmi), which means "to stand". In grammar, it is a masculine noun in the singular form. 

In ancient Greek society, a "μεσιτηι" was a person who acted as a go-between or intermediary in business or legal transactions. They were often hired to negotiate deals or settle disputes between two parties. In modern usage, the word can also refer to a real estate agent or broker who acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers.
