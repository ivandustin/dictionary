Word: μελλειν

Etymology:
The word μελλειν (mellein) is a Greek verb that means "to be about to" or "to intend to". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *mel- which means "to crush" or "to grind". The word has been used in Greek literature since ancient times and is still used in modern Greek.

Grammar:
μελλειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is often used with the auxiliary verb εἶναι (eínai) to form the periphrastic future tense, which is used to express an action that will take place in the future. For example, "Μέλλει να έρθει" (Méllei na érthei) means "He is about to come".

Description:
μελλειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to express an imminent action or intention, as in "Μέλλω να φύγω" (Mélo na fýgo) which means "I am about to leave". It can also be used to express a future action, as in "Μέλλει να βρέξει αύριο" (Méllei na vréxei ávrio) which means "It is going to rain tomorrow".
