Word: μενοντος

Etymology: μενοντος is the genitive form of the Greek verb μένω (meno), which means "to remain" or "to stay". 

Grammar: μενοντος is a masculine singular genitive form of the verb μένω (meno). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. 

Description: μενοντος can be translated as "of the one remaining" or "of the one staying". It is often used in Greek literature to describe someone who is steadfast or resolute in their actions or beliefs.
