Word: μεγαλων

Etymology: μεγαλων is the present participle of the Greek verb μεγαλόω (megalóo), which means "to make great" or "to magnify". It comes from the adjective μέγας (mégas), meaning "great" or "large".

Grammar: μεγαλων is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb μεγαλόω and functions as an adjective, describing a noun or pronoun.

Description: μεγαλων can be translated as "magnifying" or "making great". It is often used in the context of praising or glorifying someone or something. For example, in the New Testament, it is used to describe God's power and greatness.
