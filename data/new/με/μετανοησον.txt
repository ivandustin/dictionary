Word: μετανοησον

Etymology:
The word μετανοησον comes from the Greek verb μετανοέω (metanoeo), which means "to change one's mind" or "to repent." It is composed of two parts: μετά (meta), which means "after" or "beyond," and νοέω (noeo), which means "to perceive" or "to think."

Grammar:
μετανοησον is the second person singular aorist imperative form of the verb μετανοέω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. Therefore, μετανοησον can be translated as "repent!" or "change your mind!"

Description:
μετανοησον is a powerful word that calls for a change of heart and mind. It is often used in religious contexts to urge people to turn away from their sins and seek forgiveness. The word emphasizes the need for personal responsibility and transformation. It is a reminder that we have the power to change our thoughts and actions, and that true repentance can lead to a better life.
