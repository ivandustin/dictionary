Word: μεγας

Etymology:
μεγας (megas) is a Greek word that means "great" or "large". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *meg- which means "great" or "powerful".

Grammar:
μεγας is an adjective in Greek and has three genders: masculine, feminine, and neuter. It declines according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, in the nominative case, the masculine form is μέγας (megas), the feminine form is μεγάλη (megali), and the neuter form is μέγα (mega).

Description:
μεγας is a common Greek word that is used to describe something that is large, great, or powerful. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a large building or a powerful animal, or abstract concepts, such as a great idea or a powerful emotion.
