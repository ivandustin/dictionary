Word: μεριμνων

Etymology:
The word μεριμνων (merimnōn) is derived from the Greek verb μεριμνάω (merimnáō), which means "to be anxious or worried." It is formed from the combination of the prefix μερι- (meri-), which means "part" or "portion," and the verb μνάω (mnáō), which means "to remember" or "to be mindful."

Grammar:
μεριμνων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a present participle of the verb μεριμνάω (merimnáō), which means "being anxious" or "worrying." In Greek, present participles are used to describe an ongoing action or state of being.

Description:
μεριμνων is a word that describes someone who is anxious or worried. It can also refer to someone who is careful or diligent in their actions. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the state of mind of a person who is concerned about their spiritual well-being or the well-being of others.
