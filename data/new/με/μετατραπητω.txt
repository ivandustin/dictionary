μετατραπητω (metatrapētō) is a Greek verb that means "to be transformed" or "to be changed". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond", and the verb τρέπω (trépō), meaning "to turn" or "to change". 

Grammar: μετατραπητω is a passive verb in the aorist tense, indicating a completed action in the past. It is also in the third person singular form. 

Description: The word μετατραπητω is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a significant change or transformation, such as a change in one's beliefs, personality, or circumstances. It can also be used in a more literal sense to describe a physical transformation, such as the transformation of a caterpillar into a butterfly.
