Word: μετεχω

Etymology:
The word μετεχω (metechō) comes from the Greek roots μετά (meta) meaning "with" or "after" and ἔχω (echō) meaning "to have" or "to hold". Therefore, μετεχω can be translated as "to have with" or "to share in".

Grammar:
μετεχω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: μετέχω (metechō)
- Plural: μετέχομεν (metechomen)

Description:
μετεχω is a Greek verb that means "to participate" or "to share in". It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe one's participation in a divine or spiritual reality. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the believer's participation in the life of Christ and in the blessings of salvation.
