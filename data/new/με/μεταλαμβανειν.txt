Word: μεταλαμβάνειν

Etymology: μεταλαμβάνειν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix μετά (meta), meaning "after" or "beyond," and the verb λαμβάνειν (lambanein), meaning "to take" or "to receive." Therefore, μεταλαμβάνειν means "to take over" or "to assume."

Grammar: μεταλαμβάνειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is λαμβαν- and it follows the regular pattern of conjugation. The verb can be conjugated in all tenses, moods, and voices.

Description: μεταλαμβάνειν is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to taking over a position, responsibility, or ownership of something. It can also mean to assume a role or a duty. In addition, it can be used in a figurative sense, such as taking on a challenge or a task. Overall, μεταλαμβάνειν is a useful verb that conveys the idea of taking over or assuming something.
