Word: μεταλαμβάνει

Etymology: μεταλαμβάνει comes from the Greek word μεταλαμβάνω (metalambanō), which means "to take up, to receive, to assume". It is composed of the prefix μετά (meta), which means "after, beyond, with", and the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take, to receive".

Grammar: μεταλαμβάνει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb μεταλαμβάνω (metalambanō). It is a regular verb, which means that it follows the conjugation pattern of the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō).

Description: μεταλαμβάνει is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it takes up, receives, assumes". It is often used in a figurative sense, to indicate the assumption of a role, responsibility, or duty. For example, one might say "Ο πρόεδρος μεταλαμβάνει τα καθήκοντά του" (The president assumes his duties).
