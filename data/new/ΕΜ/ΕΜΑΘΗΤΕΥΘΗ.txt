Etymology: The word "εμαθητευθη" comes from the Greek verb "μαθητεύω" (mathitevo), which means "to be an apprentice" or "to be a disciple." It is derived from the noun "μαθητής" (mathitis), meaning "learner" or "disciple."

Grammar: "Εμαθητευθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "μαθητεύω" (mathitevo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is receiving the action.

Description: "Εμαθητευθη" describes the action of someone being apprenticed or becoming a disciple. It implies that the person has undergone a period of learning or training under the guidance of a teacher or mentor.
