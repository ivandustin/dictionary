Etymology: The word "εμνησθη" comes from the Greek verb "μνάομαι" (mnáomai), which means "to remember" or "to recall". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, resulting in "εμνησθη" (emnēsthē), meaning "he/she/it remembered" or "he/she/it recalled".

Grammar: "εμνησθη" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. It is often used for a single event or a specific moment in the past.

Description: "εμνησθη" is a verb in Greek that indicates the act of remembering or recalling something. It is used to describe the action of someone remembering or recalling a past event or information.
