Etymology: The word "εμου" (emou) comes from the Greek language. It is a possessive pronoun that means "my" or "mine".

Grammar: "Εμου" is a first-person singular possessive pronoun in Greek. It is used to indicate ownership or possession of something by the speaker.

Description: "Εμου" is a pronoun that is used to express possession in Greek. It is equivalent to the English pronoun "my" or "mine". It is commonly used in sentences to indicate that something belongs to the speaker. For example, "Αυτό είναι το βιβλίο εμου" (This is my book).
