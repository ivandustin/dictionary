Etymology: The word "εμης" (emis) comes from the Greek language. It is the genitive form of the pronoun "εγώ" (ego), which means "I" or "me" in English.

Grammar: "Εμης" is a pronoun in the Greek language. It is used to refer to oneself in the first person singular. In English, it corresponds to the pronoun "me" or "my" in the genitive case.

Description: "Εμης" is a word that indicates possession or ownership. It is used to show that something belongs to the speaker. For example, in the sentence "Αυτό είναι το βιβλίο εμης" (This is my book), "εμης" is used to indicate that the book belongs to the speaker.
