Etymology: The word "εμελετησαν" comes from the Greek verb "μελετώ" (meletó), which means "to study" or "to examine". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, making it "εμελετησαν" (emeletísan), which translates to "they studied" or "they examined".

Grammar: "Εμελετησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "μελετώ" in the past tense. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate different tenses, moods, and persons. The "-σαν" ending is used for the past tense of regular verbs in the third person plural.

Description: "Εμελετησαν" is a verb in the Greek language that describes an action that was performed in the past by a group of people. It can be translated as "they studied" or "they examined" in English.
