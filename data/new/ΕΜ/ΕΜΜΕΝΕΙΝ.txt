Etymology: The word "εμμενείν" comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". The prefix "εμ-" adds emphasis to the verb, giving it the meaning of "to persist" or "to endure".

Grammar: "Εμμενείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive form. It can be conjugated to match different subjects and tenses.

Description: "Εμμενείν" means "to persist" or "to endure" in English. It implies the act of staying steadfast, not giving up, or remaining committed to a particular course of action or belief. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's determination, resilience, or perseverance.
