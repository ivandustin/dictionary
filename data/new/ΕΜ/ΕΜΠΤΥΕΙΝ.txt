Etymology: The word "εμπτυείν" (emptyein) comes from the Greek verb "πτύειν" (ptyein), which means "to spit". The prefix "εμ-" (em-) adds the meaning of "out" or "away". Therefore, "εμπτυείν" (emptyein) literally means "to spit out" or "to expel".

Grammar: "Εμπτυείν" (emptyein) is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ εμπτύω (emptyo) - I spit out
- Εσύ εμπτύεις (emptyeis) - You spit out
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό εμπτύει (emptyei) - He/She/It spits out
- Εμείς εμπτύουμε (emptyoume) - We spit out
- Εσείς εμπτύετε (emptyete) - You spit out
- Αυτοί/Αυτές εμπτύουν (emptyoun) - They spit out

Description: "Εμπτυείν" (emptyein) is a verb that describes the action of forcefully expelling saliva or any other substance from the mouth. It can also be used metaphorically to express the act of rejecting or expressing strong disapproval towards something or someone.
