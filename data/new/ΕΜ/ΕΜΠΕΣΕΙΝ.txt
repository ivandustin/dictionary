The word "εμπεσείν" (empesein) is a Greek verb that means "to fall into" or "to get caught in." Here is the breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "εμπεσείν" comes from the combination of the prefix "εμ-" (em-), which indicates "into," and the verb "πίπτω" (pipto), which means "to fall."

Grammar: "Εμπεσείν" is an infinitive verb form in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation class and follows regular conjugation patterns.

Description: The verb "εμπεσείν" is used to describe the action of falling into or getting caught in something. It can be used both literally and figuratively. In a literal sense, it can refer to physically falling into a hole, trap, or any other kind of enclosure. In a figurative sense, it can describe getting involved in a difficult or undesirable situation.

Please note that the provided information is based on the Greek language.
