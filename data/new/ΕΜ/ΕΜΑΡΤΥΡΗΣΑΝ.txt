Etymology: The word "εμαρτυρησαν" (emartyrisan) comes from the Greek verb "μαρτυρέω" (martyreo), which means "to testify" or "to bear witness". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to the verb to indicate the past tense, making it "εμαρτυρησαν" (emartyrisan), which translates to "they testified" or "they bore witness".

Grammar: "Εμαρτυρησαν" (emartyrisan) is the third person plural form of the verb "μαρτυρέω" (martyreo) in the past tense. It is used to refer to a group of people who testified or bore witness to something in the past.

Description: "Εμαρτυρησαν" (emartyrisan) is a Greek word that describes an action of testifying or bearing witness. It implies that a group of individuals provided evidence or gave testimony about a particular event or situation.
