Etymology: The word "εμπορευσονται" comes from the Greek verb "εμπορεύομαι" (emporevomai), which means "to trade" or "to engage in commerce." It is derived from the noun "εμπόριο" (emporio), meaning "trade" or "commerce."

Grammar: "Εμπορεύονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense in Greek. It is a passive voice verb, indicating that the subject is being acted upon. In this case, it means "they are being traded" or "they are being engaged in commerce."

Description: "Εμπορεύονται" is a Greek verb that describes the action of trading or engaging in commerce. It can refer to the buying, selling, or exchanging of goods or services for profit. The word implies a commercial transaction or business activity.
