Etymology: The word "εμμενει" comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". The prefix "εμ-" (em-) is added to indicate emphasis or intensity.

Grammar: "Εμμενει" is the third person singular form of the verb "μένω" (meno) in the present tense. It is used to describe someone or something that stays or remains in a particular state or place.

Description: "Εμμενει" can be translated as "he/she/it stays" or "he/she/it remains" in English. It is commonly used in Greek to express the action of staying or remaining in a specific location, condition, or situation.
