Etymology: The word "εμπλεκεται" comes from the Greek verb "εμπλέκω" (em-ple-ko), which means "to entangle" or "to involve". 

Grammar: "Εμπλέκεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "εμπλέκω" in Greek. It is used to describe an action or state of being that is happening in the present and is being performed or experienced by a third person.

Description: "Εμπλέκεται" is a Greek word that can be translated to English as "he/she/it is involved" or "he/she/it is entangled". It is used to indicate that someone or something is caught up in a situation, problem, or conflict.
