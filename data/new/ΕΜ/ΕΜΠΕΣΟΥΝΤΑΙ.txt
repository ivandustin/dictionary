Etymology: The word "εμπεσούνται" comes from the Greek verb "εμπίπτω" (empípto), which means "to fall into" or "to be involved in." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in," and the verb "πίπτω" (pípto), meaning "to fall."

Grammar: "Εμπεσούνται" is the third person plural form of the verb "εμπίπτω" (empípto) in the present tense. It is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of falling into or being involved in something.

Description: "Εμπεσούνται" is a Greek verb that describes the action of falling into or being involved in something. It can be used to express the idea of being caught up in a situation, becoming entangled in a problem, or being affected by an event or circumstance.
