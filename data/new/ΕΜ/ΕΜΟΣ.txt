Etymology: The word "εμος" (emos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἐμός" (emos), meaning "mine" or "my own".

Grammar: In Greek, "εμος" (emos) is a pronoun that means "mine" or "my own". It is used to indicate possession or ownership. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it refers to.

Description: "Εμος" (emos) is a first-person possessive pronoun in Greek. It is used to show that something belongs to the speaker. For example, if someone says "Αυτός είναι ο φίλος μου" (Aftos einai o filos mou), it means "He is my friend". In this case, "μου" (mou) is the possessive form of "εμος" (emos) and indicates that the friend belongs to the speaker.
