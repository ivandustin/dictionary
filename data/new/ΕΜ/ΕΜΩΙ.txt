Etymology: The word "εμωι" (emōi) comes from the Greek language. It is a personal pronoun in the dative case, meaning "to me" or "for me".

Grammar: "Εμωι" (emōi) is the first person singular pronoun in the dative case in Greek. It is used to indicate the recipient or beneficiary of an action.

Description: "Εμωι" (emōi) is a Greek pronoun that is used to refer to oneself in the dative case. It is equivalent to the English pronoun "to me" or "for me". It is commonly used in sentences to indicate the person or entity that something is directed towards or intended for.
