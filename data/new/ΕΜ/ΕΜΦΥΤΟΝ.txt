Etymology: The word "εμφυτον" (emphyton) comes from the Greek word "εμφυτος" (emphytos), which means "inborn" or "innate". 

Grammar: "Εμφυτον" is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "εμφυτου" (emphytou). 

Description: "Εμφυτον" refers to something that is inherent, natural, or innate. It can be used to describe qualities, characteristics, or traits that are present from birth or deeply ingrained in someone or something.
