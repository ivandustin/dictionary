Etymology: The word "εμους" (emous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "μοῦς" (mous), meaning "mouse".

Grammar: "Εμους" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: "Εμους" refers to a mouse, which is a small mammal belonging to the rodent family. Mice are known for their small size, pointed snouts, and long tails. They are found worldwide and are often considered pests due to their ability to damage crops and spread diseases.
