Etymology: The word "εμπαίζειν" (empaízein) comes from the Greek verb "παίζω" (paízō), which means "to play". The prefix "εμ-" (em-) adds the sense of "in" or "into", so "εμπαίζειν" can be translated as "to play in" or "to make fun of".

Grammar: "Εμπαίζειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time. The active voice means that the subject of the sentence performs the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that is not conjugated.

Description: "Εμπαίζειν" is a Greek verb that means "to make fun of" or "to mock". It is used to describe the act of teasing or ridiculing someone or something in a playful or mocking manner.
