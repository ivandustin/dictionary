Etymology: The word "εμενον" (emenon) comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men-.

Grammar: "Εμενον" is the neuter form of the adjective "εμενος" (emenos), which means "remaining" or "enduring". In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, "εμενον" is used to describe neuter nouns.

Description: "Εμενον" is an adjective that describes something that remains or endures. It can be used to describe physical objects, abstract concepts, or qualities of a person or thing.
