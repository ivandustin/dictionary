Etymology: The word "εμεινεν" comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". It is conjugated in the aorist tense, third person singular form.

Grammar: "εμεινεν" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, person, and number. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "εμεινεν" is used to describe an action that took place and was completed in the past. It indicates that someone or something stayed or remained in a certain state or location.
