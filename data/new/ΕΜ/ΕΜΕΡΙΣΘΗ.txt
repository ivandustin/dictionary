Etymology: The word "εμερισθη" comes from the Greek verb "μερίζω" (merízo), which means "to divide" or "to distribute". It is derived from the noun "μέρος" (méros), meaning "part" or "portion".

Grammar: "εμερισθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive form of the verb "μερίζω" (merízo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "εμερισθη" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it was divided" or "he/she/it was distributed". It describes a past action where something was divided or distributed among different parts or individuals.
