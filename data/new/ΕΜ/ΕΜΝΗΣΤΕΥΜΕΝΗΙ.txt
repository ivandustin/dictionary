Etymology: The word "εμνηστευμένη" comes from the Greek verb "μνηστεύομαι" (mnisteúomai), which means "to be engaged" or "to be betrothed." The prefix "εμ-" (em-) is added to indicate the feminine form.

Grammar: "Εμνηστευμένη" is the feminine form of the adjective "εμνηστευμένος" (emnisteúmenos), which means "engaged" or "betrothed." It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Εμνηστευμένη" is used to describe a woman who is engaged or betrothed to be married. It indicates that she has made a formal commitment to marry someone but has not yet entered into the marriage itself.
