Etymology: The word "εχοι" (echi) comes from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess".

Grammar: "Εχοι" is the third person singular form of the verb "έχω" (echo) in the present tense. It is used to indicate that someone or something possesses or has something.

Description: "Εχοι" is a Greek word that is used to express possession or ownership. It is commonly used in sentences to indicate that someone or something has something. For example, "Ο άνθρωπος έχει ένα αυτοκίνητο" (O anthropos echei ena aftokinito) means "The man has a car".
