Etymology: The word "εχουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess". It is the third person plural form of the present tense in the indicative mood.

Grammar: "Εχουσιν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the third person plural form of the present tense in the indicative mood. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice.

Description: "Εχουσιν" is used to indicate that a group of people or things possess or have something. It is commonly translated as "they have" in English. The verb can be used in various contexts to express ownership, possession, or existence of something.
