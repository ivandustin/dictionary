Etymology: The word "εχθρού" (echthrou) comes from the Greek word "εχθρός" (echthros), which means "enemy" or "foe". 

Grammar: "Εχθρού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. 

Description: "Εχθρού" refers to someone or something that is an enemy or adversary. It can be used to describe a person, a group, or even an abstract concept that is opposed to someone or something else.
