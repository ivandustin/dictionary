Etymology: The word "εχθρα" (echthra) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ἔχθρα" (ekhthra), which means "enmity" or "hostility".

Grammar: In Greek, "εχθρα" (echthra) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a singular and plural form. The plural form is "εχθρες" (echthres).

Description: "Εχθρα" (echthra) refers to enmity, hostility, or animosity between individuals or groups. It represents a deep-seated dislike or opposition towards someone or something. It can be used to describe a personal or collective feeling of antagonism or hatred.
