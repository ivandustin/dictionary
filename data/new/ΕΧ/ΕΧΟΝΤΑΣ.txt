Etymology: The word "εχοντας" (echontas) comes from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess". It is the present participle form of the verb.

Grammar: "Εχοντας" is a present participle in the Greek language. Present participles are formed by adding the suffix "-οντας" to the verb stem. They function as adjectives and can be used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description: "Εχοντας" is used to describe someone or something that is currently having or possessing something. It can be used to indicate an ongoing action or state of possession.
