Etymology: The word "εχεις" (echis) comes from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess". It is conjugated in the second person singular form of the present tense.

Grammar: "Εχεις" is a verb in the indicative mood, present tense, second person singular form. It is used to express possession or ownership. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice.

Description: "Εχεις" is commonly used in everyday Greek conversations to ask someone if they have something or to state that someone possesses something. It can also be used to express various other meanings depending on the context.
