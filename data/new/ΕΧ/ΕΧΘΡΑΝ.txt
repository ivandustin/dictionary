Etymology: The word "εχθραν" (echthran) comes from the Greek word "εχθρός" (echthros), which means "enemy" or "foe". 

Grammar: "Εχθραν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Εχθραν" refers to the concept of "enmity" or "hostility". It represents a state of being an enemy or having hostile feelings towards someone or something.
