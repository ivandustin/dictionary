Etymology: The word "εχουσι" (echousi) comes from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess". It is conjugated in the third person plural present tense.

Grammar: "Εχουσι" is a verb form in the Greek language. It is the third person plural present tense of the verb "έχω" (echo). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice.

Description: "Εχουσι" is used to express the action of having or possessing something in the present tense. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing to describe the possession of objects, qualities, or attributes by a group of people or entities.
