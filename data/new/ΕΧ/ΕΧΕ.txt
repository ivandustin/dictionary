Etymology: The word "εχε" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess."

Grammar: "Εχε" is a verb in the imperative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb "έχω" (echo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood.

Description: "Εχε" is used as a command or request, similar to the English imperative form "have." It is often used to tell someone to possess or take something. For example, if someone says "Εχε ένα μήλο" (Eche ena milo), it means "Have an apple."
