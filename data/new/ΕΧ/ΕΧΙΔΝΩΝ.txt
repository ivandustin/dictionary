Etymology: The word "εχιδνων" (echidnon) comes from the Greek word "εχιδνα" (echidna), which means "viper" or "snake." In Greek mythology, Echidna was a half-woman, half-serpent creature who gave birth to many famous monsters.

Grammar: "Εχιδνων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "εχιδνα" (echidna) in Greek. It is used to indicate possession or association with multiple vipers or snakes.

Description: "Εχιδνων" refers to a group of vipers or snakes. It can be used to describe a collection of these reptiles or to indicate ownership or affiliation with them.
