Etymology: The word "εχθρός" (echthros) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἐχθρός" (ekhthros), which means "enemy" or "hostile". It is derived from the verb "ἔχθω" (ekhtho), meaning "to hate" or "to be hostile".

Grammar: In Greek, "εχθρός" (echthros) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "εχθροί" (echthroi). It can also be used as an adjective to describe something or someone as "hostile" or "enemy-like".

Description: "Εχθρός" (echthros) refers to an enemy or someone who is hostile towards another person or group. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is harmful or detrimental.
