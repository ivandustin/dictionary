Etymology: The word "εχωρισθη" comes from the Greek verb "χωρίζω" (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". The suffix "-σθη" is a passive voice ending, indicating that the action of separating or dividing has been done to the subject.

Grammar: "Εχωρισθη" is the third person singular form of the aorist passive tense of the verb "χωρίζω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Εχωρισθη" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was separated" or "he/she/it was divided". It is used to describe a situation where something or someone has been separated or divided from something else.
