Etymology: The word "εχουσαι" (echousai) comes from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess." It is conjugated in the present tense, third person, singular, feminine form.

Grammar: "Εχουσαι" (echousai) is a verb form in the Greek language. It belongs to the present tense, which indicates an action happening in the current moment. The third person singular form is used when referring to a female subject.

Description: "Εχουσαι" (echousai) is used to describe a female subject who possesses or has something. It can be translated as "she has" or "she possesses" in English.
