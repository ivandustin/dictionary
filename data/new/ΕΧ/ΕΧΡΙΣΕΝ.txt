Etymology: The word "εχρισεν" comes from the Greek verb "χρίζω" (chrizo), which means "to anoint" or "to consecrate." 

Grammar: "Εχρισεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "χρίζω" (chrizo). It is used to indicate an action that occurred in the past.

Description: "Εχρισεν" is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it anointed" or "he/she/it consecrated" in English. It is commonly used in religious contexts to refer to the act of anointing someone with oil as a sign of consecration or blessing.
