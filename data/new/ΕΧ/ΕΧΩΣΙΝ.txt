Etymology: The word "εχωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "εχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess". It is conjugated in the third person plural present tense.

Grammar: "Εχωσιν" is a verb form in the Greek language. It belongs to the present tense and is used to express an action or state of possession in the third person plural.

Description: "Εχωσιν" is used to describe an ongoing action or state of possession that is being performed or experienced by a group of people or things. It indicates that the subject of the sentence has something or is in a certain state.
