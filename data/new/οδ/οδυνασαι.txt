Word: οδυνασαι

Etymology:
The word οδυνασαι (odunásai) is a verb in the present tense, second person singular, active voice, indicative mood, in the Greek language. It comes from the root word οδύνη (odýni), which means "pain" or "suffering". 

Grammar:
As mentioned above, οδυνασαι is a verb in the present tense, second person singular, active voice, indicative mood. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, person, and number of the subject. The present tense is used to describe actions that are happening now or are ongoing. The second person singular refers to the subject "you". The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action, and the indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
The word οδυνασαι can be translated to English as "you are in pain" or "you are suffering". It is a verb that describes a physical or emotional state of discomfort or distress. The word can be used in a medical context to describe a patient's symptoms or in a personal context to express one's own pain or suffering.
