Word: οδοντα (odontá)

Etymology:
The word οδοντα (odontá) comes from the Greek word οδούς (odoús), which means "tooth". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃dónts.

Grammar:
Οδοντα (odontá) is a noun in the singular form. It is masculine in gender and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description:
Οδοντα (odontá) refers to a tooth, which is a hard, calcified structure found in the jaws of many vertebrates. Teeth are used for biting, chewing, and grinding food, as well as for other functions such as communication and defense. In humans, teeth are an important part of the digestive system and play a crucial role in maintaining overall health.
