Word: οδοντος

Etymology:
The word οδοντος (odontos) is derived from the Greek word ὀδούς (odous), which means "tooth". It is a common root word in many medical and dental terms.

Grammar:
Οδοντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
Οδοντος refers to a tooth, which is a hard, calcified structure found in the jaws of many vertebrates. Teeth are used for biting and chewing food, as well as for other functions such as communication and defense. In humans, there are four types of teeth: incisors, canines, premolars, and molars. Each type of tooth has a specific shape and function. Dental health is important for overall health, and regular brushing and flossing can help prevent tooth decay and gum disease.
