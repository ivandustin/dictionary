Word: οδοιποριαις

Etymology: The word οδοιποριαις comes from the Greek words οδος (odos) meaning "road" and πορεια (poreia) meaning "journey". The suffix -αις (-ais) indicates the plural feminine form.

Grammar: οδοιποριαις is a noun in the plural feminine form. It is declined according to the second declension of Greek nouns.

Description: οδοιποριαις refers to journeys or travels on roads. It can also be used to describe the act of traveling or the experience of being on a journey. The word has a sense of adventure and exploration, and can be used to describe both physical and metaphorical journeys.
