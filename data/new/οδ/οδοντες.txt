Word: οδοντες

Etymology: The word οδοντες (odontes) comes from the Greek word ὀδούς (odous), which means "tooth". 

Grammar: οδοντες is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and is used to refer to teeth. 

Description: οδοντες is a term used to describe the hard, calcified structures that are found in the mouth of humans and many animals. Teeth are used for biting, chewing, and grinding food, and they play an important role in the digestive process. In humans, there are four types of teeth: incisors, canines, premolars, and molars. Each type of tooth has a specific shape and function. Teeth are also important for speech and for maintaining the structure of the face. Dental hygiene is important for keeping teeth healthy and preventing tooth decay and gum disease.
