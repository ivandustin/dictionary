Word: οδηγειν

Etymology:
The word οδηγειν (odigín) comes from the Greek verb οδηγώ (odigó), which means "to lead" or "to guide". It is derived from the noun οδός (odós), which means "road" or "way".

Grammar:
Οδηγειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem ends in -α or -ε.

Description:
Οδηγειν means "to guide" or "to lead" in Greek. It is often used in the context of driving or navigating, but it can also refer to guiding someone in a more general sense. For example, one might say "Οδηγώ τον φίλο μου στο μουσείο" (odigó ton fílo mou sto mouseío), which means "I am guiding my friend to the museum".
