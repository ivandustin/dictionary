Word: οδοντας

Etymology:
The word οδοντας (odontas) is derived from the Greek word "ὀδούς" (odous) which means "tooth". It is a noun in the Greek language.

Grammar:
In Greek, οδοντας (odontas) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. The genitive form is οδόντα (odontas) and the accusative form is οδόντα (odontas).

Description:
Οδοντας (odontas) refers to a tooth, which is a hard, calcified structure found in the jaws of many vertebrates. Teeth are used for biting and chewing food, as well as for other functions such as defense and communication. In humans, there are four types of teeth: incisors, canines, premolars, and molars. Each type of tooth has a specific shape and function. Teeth are an important part of the digestive system and play a crucial role in maintaining overall health.
