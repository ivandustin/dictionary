Word: οδυναις

Etymology:
The word οδυναις (odunais) is derived from the Greek word οδύνη (odunē), which means "pain" or "sorrow". It is related to the Greek word οδύνα (oduna), which means "grief" or "anguish".

Grammar:
Οδυναις is a noun in the plural form. It is a feminine noun and belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description:
Οδυναις refers to pains or aches in the body. It can also be used to describe emotional pain or distress. The word is often used in medical contexts to describe physical pain or discomfort. In literature, it is used to describe the emotional pain of characters in a story.
