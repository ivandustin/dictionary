Word: οδηγοί

Etymology:
The word οδηγοί (odigoi) is derived from the Greek verb οδηγώ (odigo), which means "to guide" or "to lead". The suffix -οί (-oi) is a plural ending in Greek, so οδηγοί means "guides" or "leaders".

Grammar:
Οδηγοί is a masculine plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative case form, which means it is used as the subject of a sentence. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description:
Οδηγοί can refer to a group of people who guide or lead others, such as tour guides, teachers, or mentors. It can also refer to the guides or instructions that come with a product or service, such as a user manual or a set of driving directions. In Greek mythology, the word οδηγός (odigos) is used to describe a guide or leader, such as the god Hermes who was known as the "guide of souls".
