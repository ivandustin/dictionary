Word: υποδραμόντες

Etymology:
The word υποδραμόντες is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: υπό (under) and δραμών (runner). The suffix -τες is added to form the plural. Therefore, the word υποδραμόντες means "those who run underneath" or "under-runners".

Grammar:
υποδραμόντες is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: υποδραμώντας (under-runner)
- Genitive: υποδραμόντα (of under-runner)
- Accusative: υποδραμόντα (under-runner)

Plural:
- Nominative: υποδραμόντες (under-runners)
- Genitive: υποδραμόντων (of under-runners)
- Accusative: υποδραμόντες (under-runners)

Description:
The word υποδραμόντες is not commonly used in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek texts, where it refers to soldiers who run underneath the enemy's shields to attack them from below. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is sneaky or deceitful.
