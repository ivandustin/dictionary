Word: υπαντησιν

Etymology: The word υπαντησιν comes from the Greek verb υπαντάω (ypantáo), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a noun ending that indicates the action or result of the verb.

Grammar: υπαντησιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: υπαντησιν refers to the act of meeting or encountering someone or something. It can also be used to describe a meeting or encounter itself. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe unexpected or chance encounters, as well as planned meetings.
