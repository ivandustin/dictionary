Word: υπερεπλεονάσεν

Etymology: The word υπερεπλεονάσεν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix υπέρ (meaning "over" or "beyond") and the verb επλεονάσεν (meaning "to abound" or "to be in excess"). The word is found in the New Testament of the Bible, in 2 Corinthians 10:15.

Grammar: υπερεπλεονάσεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: The word υπερεπλεονάσεν can be translated as "he abounded exceedingly" or "he went beyond measure." It is used in 2 Corinthians 10:15 to describe the apostle Paul's ministry, which he says has extended beyond the region of Corinth to other areas. The word conveys the idea of abundance and excess, suggesting that Paul's ministry was not just successful, but overwhelmingly so.
