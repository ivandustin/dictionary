Word: υπηρετειν

Etymology:
The word υπηρετειν (yperetein) comes from the Greek verb υπηρετέω (yperetéo), which means "to serve" or "to attend to". It is derived from the noun υπηρέτης (yperetis), which means "servant" or "attendant".

Grammar:
υπηρετειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often used in the context of serving or attending to someone or something.

Description:
υπηρετειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to serving in a formal capacity, such as serving as a waiter or a butler. It can also refer to serving in a more general sense, such as serving one's country or community. Additionally, it can refer to attending to someone's needs or desires, such as attending to a patient's medical needs or attending to a customer's requests. Overall, υπηρετειν is a useful verb that conveys the idea of serving or attending to someone or something.
