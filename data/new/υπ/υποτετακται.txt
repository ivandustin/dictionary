Word: υποτετακται

Etymology:
The word υποτετακται (hypotetaktai) is a Greek word that comes from the verb υποτάσσω (hypotassō), which means "to subject, to subordinate". The word is formed by adding the prefix υπο- (hypo-), which means "under" or "beneath", to the verb τάσσω (tassō), which means "to arrange, to put in order".

Grammar:
The word υποτετακται is a third-person plural present passive indicative form of the verb υποτάσσω (hypotassō). In Greek grammar, the present tense is used to describe actions that are happening now or are ongoing. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
The word υποτετακται describes a group of people or things that are being subordinated or subjected to someone or something else. It can be translated as "they are being subjected" or "they are being subordinated". The word is often used in a military or hierarchical context, where one group is being placed under the authority of another.
