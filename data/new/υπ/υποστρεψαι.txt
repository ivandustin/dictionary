Etymology:
The word "υποστρεψαι" (ypostrepsei) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: "υπό" (ypo) meaning "under" or "beneath" and "στρέφω" (strefó) meaning "to turn". Therefore, "υποστρεψαι" means "to turn back" or "to return".

Grammar:
"Υποστρεψαι" is a first-person singular aorist active subjunctive verb in Greek. It belongs to the second aorist conjugation and is formed by adding the suffix "-σ-" to the stem of the verb "στρέφω" (strefó) and the personal ending "-αι" (-ai) for the first-person singular. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the subjunctive mood expresses doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations.

Description:
"Υποστρεψαι" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to express the idea of turning back or returning. It can be used literally, as in "I turned back to pick up my phone" or figuratively, as in "I returned to my hometown after many years". It can also be used in a metaphorical sense, as in "I changed my mind and turned back on my decision". Overall, "υποστρεψαι" is a useful verb that can convey different shades of meaning depending on the context in which it is used.
