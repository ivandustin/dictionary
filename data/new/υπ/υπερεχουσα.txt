Word: υπερεχουσα

Etymology: The word υπερεχουσα comes from the Greek verb υπερέχω (yperécho), which means "to excel" or "to surpass". The suffix -ούσα (-oúsa) is a feminine present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing a female subject who is currently excelling or surpassing.

Grammar: υπερεχουσα is a feminine singular form of the present participle of the verb υπερέχω. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of the sentence. The word can be translated to English as "surpassing" or "excelling".

Description: υπερεχουσα is a word that describes a female subject who is currently excelling or surpassing in a particular area. It can be used to describe a woman who is excelling in her career, surpassing her peers in a competition, or simply doing exceptionally well in any given task.
