Word: υπηρετησας

Etymology:
The word υπηρετησας comes from the Greek verb υπηρετέω (yperetéo), which means "to serve" or "to attend to". It is derived from the noun υπηρέτης (yperetis), which means "servant" or "attendant".

Grammar:
υπηρετησας is the aorist tense of the Greek verb υπηρετέω (yperetéo), which means "to serve" or "to attend to". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, υπηρετησας means "you served" or "you attended to".

Description:
υπηρετησας is a Greek verb that describes an action of serving or attending to someone or something. It is often used in a religious context to describe serving God or attending to the needs of a religious community. In a secular context, it can be used to describe serving or attending to someone in a professional or personal capacity.
