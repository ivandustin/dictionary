Word: υπακοης

Etymology:
The word υπακοης (ypakois) is derived from the Greek word υπακοή (ypakoí), which means obedience or compliance. It is formed from the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under," and the verb ακούω (akoúo), meaning "to hear."

Grammar:
υπακοης is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of υπακοή (ypakoí).

Description:
υπακοης refers to the quality or state of being obedient or compliant. It can also refer to an act of obedience or compliance. The word is often used in a religious or moral context, but can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who follows rules or instructions.
