Word: υποτασσεσθε

Etymology:
The word υποτασσεσθε comes from the Greek verb υποτάσσω (hypotássō), which means "to submit" or "to obey." It is derived from the prefix υπο- (hypo-), meaning "under," and the verb τάσσω (tássō), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order."

Grammar:
υποτασσεσθε is a second-person plural present passive imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to submit or obey. The passive voice indicates that the action is being done to the subject, rather than the subject doing the action.

Description:
υποτασσεσθε is a strong and direct command, often used in a military or authoritative context. It implies a hierarchy or power dynamic, where the person giving the command has authority over the people being commanded. The word emphasizes the importance of obedience and submission to authority.
