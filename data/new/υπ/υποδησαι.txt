The word "υποδησαι" (ypodisai) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "υπο-" (ypo-), meaning "under" or "beneath," and the verb "δένω" (deno), meaning "to tie" or "to fasten." 

Grammar: "Υποδησαι" is in the aorist tense, imperative mood, and second person singular form. This means that it is a command directed towards one person to perform a specific action in the past. 

Description: The word "υποδησαι" means "to tie under" or "to fasten beneath." It can be used in various contexts, such as tying shoelaces or fastening a belt.
