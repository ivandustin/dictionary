Word: υπακοην

Etymology: The word υπακοην (ypakoēn) is derived from the Greek verb υπακούω (ypakoúo), which means "to obey" or "to comply with". The suffix -ην (-ēn) is added to the verb to form a noun, indicating the act of obedience.

Grammar: υπακοην is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: υπακοην refers to the act of obedience or compliance. It can be used to describe someone who follows rules or instructions without question or hesitation. The word is often associated with religious or moral obedience, but can also be used in a more general sense.
