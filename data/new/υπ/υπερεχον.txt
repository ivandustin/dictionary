Word: υπερεχον

Etymology:
The word υπερεχον (yperexon) is a Greek word that comes from the verb υπερέχω (yperécho), which means "to excel" or "to surpass". The word is formed by combining the prefix υπέρ (yper), which means "above" or "beyond", and the verb έχω (écho), which means "to have" or "to hold".

The word υπερεχον is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb υπερέχω, which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense. The present participle is formed by adding the suffix -ων (-on) to the stem of the verb.

Description:
The word υπερεχον can be translated as "surpassing" or "excelling". It is often used to describe someone or something that is superior or outstanding in some way. For example, one might say "ο υπερεχον αθλητής" (o yperexon athlitis), which means "the outstanding athlete".
