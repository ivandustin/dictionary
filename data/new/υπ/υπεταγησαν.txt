The word υπεταγησαν is derived from the Greek verb υποτάσσω (hypotassō), which means "to submit" or "to obey". The verb is formed from the prefix υπο- (hypo-), meaning "under", and the verb τάσσω (tassō), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order".

In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.
