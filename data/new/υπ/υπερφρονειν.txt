Word: υπερφρονειν

Etymology:
The word υπερφρονειν (hyperphronein) is derived from the Greek words υπέρ (hyper) meaning "above" or "beyond" and φρονέω (phroneo) meaning "to think" or "to have an opinion". Therefore, the word υπερφρονειν can be translated as "to think too highly of oneself" or "to be arrogant".

Grammar:
υπερφρονειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is υπερφρονε-. The verb is composed of the prefix υπέρ (hyper) and the verb φρονέω (phroneo).

Description:
υπερφρονειν is a Greek verb that describes the act of having an excessive or exaggerated opinion of oneself. It is often used to describe someone who is arrogant, conceited, or full of themselves. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, politics, or business. It is often used in a negative sense, as it implies that the person in question is not humble or modest.
