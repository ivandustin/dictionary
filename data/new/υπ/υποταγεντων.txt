Word: υποταγεντων

Etymology: The word υποταγεντων is derived from the Greek verb υποτάσσω (hypotássō), which means "to submit" or "to obey". The suffix -των (-ton) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives from verbs.

Grammar: υποταγεντων is the genitive plural form of the adjective υποταγής (hypotagḗs), which means "submissive" or "obedient". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, υποταγεντων is used to describe plural nouns in the genitive case that are submissive or obedient.

Description: υποταγεντων is a Greek adjective that describes a group of people or things that are submissive or obedient.
