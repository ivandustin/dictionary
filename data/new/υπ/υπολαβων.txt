Word: υπολαβων

Etymology: The word υπολαβων comes from the Greek verb υπολαμβάνω (ypolamváno), which means "to take up, to receive, to assume". The suffix -ων (-on) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently taking up or assuming something.

Grammar: υπολαβων is a masculine noun in the nominative case, singular form. It is a present participle, which means it describes someone who is currently taking up or assuming something. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ων (-on) to the stem of the verb.

Description: υπολαβων refers to someone who is currently taking up or assuming something. It can be translated as "the one who assumes" or "the one who takes up". The word is often used in legal contexts, where it refers to someone who takes on a responsibility or obligation.
