Word: υπεραιρωμαι

Etymology:
The word υπεραιρωμαι (hyperairōmai) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: υπερ (hyper), meaning "above" or "beyond," and αιρω (airō), meaning "to lift up" or "to take away." The word is derived from the verb υπεραιρεω (hyperaireō), which means "to take away, to remove, to surpass."

υπεραιρωμαι is a first-person singular present passive indicative form of the verb υπεραιρεω (hyperaireō). It is a passive form, indicating that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now.

The word υπεραιρωμαι (hyperairōmai) can be translated as "I am being lifted up" or "I am being taken away." It can also be translated as "I am being surpassed" or "I am being exceeded."
