Word: υπηρεται

Etymology:
The word υπηρεται (yperetai) is derived from the Greek verb υπηρετώ (yperetó), which means "to serve" or "to attend to". It is formed from the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under", and the verb ερετεύω (eretevo), meaning "to work for hire".

Grammar:
υπηρεται is a third-person singular present indicative form of the verb υπηρετώ (yperetó). It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Description:
The word υπηρεται (yperetai) refers to someone who serves or attends to someone or something. It can also refer to someone who works for hire or is employed in a service-oriented job. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in the military, in a restaurant, or in a household.
