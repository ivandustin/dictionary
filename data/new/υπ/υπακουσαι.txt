Word: υπακουσαι

Etymology:
The word υπακουσαι (ypakousai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word υπακούω (ypakouo), which means "to obey" or "to listen attentively". The word is formed by adding the suffix -σαι (-sai) to the root verb.

Grammar:
υπακουσαι (ypakousai) is a second-person singular aorist imperative active verb in the Greek language. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to obey or listen attentively. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: υπακούω (ypakouo) - I obey, you obey, he/she/it obeys, we obey, you (plural) obey, they obey
- Aorist tense: υπήκουσα (ypikousa) - I obeyed, you obeyed, he/she/it obeyed, we obeyed, you (plural) obeyed, they obeyed
- Imperative mood: υπακούσαι (ypakousai) - obey, listen attentively

Description:
The word υπακουσαι (ypakousai) is a command or instruction to someone to obey or listen attentively. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the Bible, where it is used to instruct believers to obey God's commands. The word emphasizes the importance of obedience and attentiveness in following instructions and living a righteous life.
