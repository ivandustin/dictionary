The word "υποκρισεως" (hypokriseos) is a Greek noun that means "hypocrisy" or "pretense". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "υποκρινομαι" (hypokrinomai), which means "to pretend" or "to act". 

Grammar: "Υποκρισεως" is a genitive singular noun in Greek. 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is pretending to be something they are not, or who is acting in a way that is inconsistent with their true beliefs or values. It can also refer to the practice of pretending to have virtues or qualities that one does not actually possess. The concept of hypocrisy is often associated with moral or religious contexts, but it can also be applied to other areas of life, such as politics or business.
