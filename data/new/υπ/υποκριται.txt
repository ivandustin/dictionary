Word: υποκριται

Etymology:
The word υποκριται (ypokritai) comes from the Greek verb υποκρίνομαι (ypokrinomai), which means "to play a part" or "to pretend". The noun form υποκριτής (ypokrites) means "actor" or "hypocrite".

Grammar:
υποκριται is the plural form of υποκριτής (ypokrites), which is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its nominative singular form is υποκριτής (ypokrites).

Description:
υποκριται is a Greek word that refers to hypocrites or actors. In modern Greek, it is commonly used to describe someone who is insincere or deceitful. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize someone's behavior or character.
