Word: υπομεινας

Etymology:
The word υπομεινας is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: υπό (hypo) meaning "under" and μένω (meno) meaning "to remain". Therefore, υπομεινας means "to endure" or "to persevere".

Grammar:
υπομεινας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb υπομένω (ypoméno), which means "to remain under" or "to endure". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
υπομεινας is a verb that describes the act of enduring or persevering through a difficult situation. It implies a sense of patience and resilience in the face of adversity. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, work, or sports. It is often associated with a sense of strength and determination.
