Word: υπηρεταις

Etymology:
The word υπηρεταις (yperetais) is derived from the Greek word υπηρετης (yperetes), which means "servant" or "attendant". It is a noun in the feminine gender and plural number.

Grammar:
In Greek, nouns have gender, number, and case. The word υπηρεταις is in the feminine gender and plural number. It is declined in the first declension and its nominative case form is υπηρεταις.

Description:
The word υπηρεταις refers to female servants or attendants. It can be used to describe women who work in various service industries, such as hospitality, healthcare, or domestic work. In ancient Greece, υπηρεταις were often slaves or servants who worked in the households of wealthy families. Today, the word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to women who work in service-oriented jobs.
