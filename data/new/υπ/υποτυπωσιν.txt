Word: υποτυπωσιν

Etymology: The word υποτυπωσιν comes from the Greek word υπότυπος (hypótypos), which means "under impression" or "copy". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a nominalizing suffix that turns the verb υποτυπόω (hypotypóo), which means "to sketch" or "to outline", into a noun.

Grammar: υποτυπωσιν is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb in a sentence.

Description: υποτυπωσιν refers to a sketch, outline, or rough draft of something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as art, writing, or planning. It implies that the final product is not yet complete or polished, but rather in the early stages of development.
