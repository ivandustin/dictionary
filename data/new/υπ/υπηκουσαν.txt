Word: υπηκουσαν

Etymology:
The word υπηκουσαν (ypikousan) is a verb in the past tense form of the Greek word υπακούω (ypakouo), which means "to obey" or "to comply with". The word is derived from the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under", and the verb ακούω (akouo), meaning "to hear".

Grammar:
υπηκουσαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb υπακούω (ypakouo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened. The third person plural refers to a group of people or things, and indicates that the action was performed by more than one person.

Description:
The word υπηκουσαν (ypikousan) describes an action that has already taken place, where a group of people obeyed or complied with something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a military setting, where soldiers obey orders from their superiors, or in a workplace, where employees comply with company policies. The word implies a sense of submission and respect for authority.
