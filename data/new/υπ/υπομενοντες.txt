Word: υπομενοντες

Etymology:
The word υπομενοντες is a present participle of the Greek verb υπομένω (hypoméno), which means "to endure" or "to persevere". It is derived from the prefix υπο- (hypo-), meaning "under", and the verb μένω (méno), meaning "to remain" or "to stay".

Grammar:
υπομενοντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently enduring or persevering through a difficult situation. In Greek, present participles are often used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description:
The word υπομενοντες can be translated to English as "enduring" or "persevering". It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe the act of patiently enduring hardship or suffering. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the perseverance of early Christians in the face of persecution.
