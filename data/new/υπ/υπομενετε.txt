Word: υπομενετε

Etymology: υπομενετε is a Greek word that comes from the verb υπομένω (ypoméno), which means "to endure" or "to persevere". It is formed by combining the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb μένω (méno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar: υπομενετε is a second-person plural present active imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to endure or persevere in a particular situation.

Description: υπομενετε is a powerful word that encourages perseverance and endurance in the face of difficulty or hardship. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, urging believers to remain steadfast in their faith and trust in God.
