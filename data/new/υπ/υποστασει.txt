The word "υποστασει" (hypostasei) is a Greek noun that can be translated to English as "substance" or "essence". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek roots "υπό" (hypo), meaning "under" or "beneath", and "στάσις" (stasis), meaning "standing" or "position". 

Grammar: "Υποστασει" is a feminine noun in the singular form. 

Description: In philosophy and theology, "υποστασει" refers to the underlying reality or essence of a thing, as opposed to its outward appearance or attributes. It is often used in the context of the Christian doctrine of the Trinity, where it refers to the three distinct persons (Father, Son, and Holy Spirit) who share a single divine essence or substance. In everyday language, the word can also be used to refer to the essential nature or character of a person or thing.
