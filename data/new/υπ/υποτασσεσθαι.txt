Word: υποτασσεσθαι

Etymology:
The word υποτασσεσθαι comes from the Greek verb υποτάσσω (hypotássō), which means "to submit" or "to obey." It is derived from the prefix υπο- (hypo-), meaning "under," and the verb τάσσω (tássō), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order."

Grammar:
υποτασσεσθαι is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -σθαι (-sthai) to the stem of the verb υποτάσσω (hypotássō).

Description:
υποτασσεσθαι means "to be subjected" or "to be subordinated." It is often used in the context of military or political subordination, but can also refer to any situation where one person or group is under the authority or control of another. The word implies a certain level of obedience or compliance, and can have both positive and negative connotations depending on the context in which it is used.
