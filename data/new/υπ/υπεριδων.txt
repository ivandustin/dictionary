Word: υπεριδων

Etymology:
The word υπεριδων (yperidon) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix υπερ- (hyper-), meaning "above" or "beyond," and the noun ἴδων (idon), meaning "seeing" or "sight." Therefore, the word υπεριδων can be translated as "overseeing" or "supervising."

Grammar:
In Greek, υπεριδων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It belongs to the second declension, which means that its stem ends in -ον (on). The genitive singular form of υπεριδων is υπεριδοντος (yperidontos), the dative singular form is υπεριδοντι (yperidonti), and the accusative singular form is υπεριδοντα (yperidonta).

Description:
The word υπεριδων is not commonly used in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek texts, such as the works of Homer and Hesiod. In these texts, υπεριδων is often used to describe gods or heroes who have the ability to see and oversee everything. It can also be used to describe someone who has great insight or foresight.
