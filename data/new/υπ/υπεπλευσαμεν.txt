Word: υπεπλευσαμεν

Etymology:
The word υπεπλευσαμεν is a compound verb in the Ancient Greek language. It is formed by combining the prefix υπερ- (hyper-), meaning "over" or "beyond," with the verb πλέω (pleo), meaning "to sail." The prefix υπερ- emphasizes the idea of sailing over or beyond something, such as a body of water.

Grammar:
υπεπλευσαμεν is a first-person plural aorist indicative active verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb (we) performed the action. 

Description:
The word υπεπλευσαμεν describes the act of sailing over or beyond something. It can be translated as "we sailed over" or "we sailed beyond." The context in which the word is used would determine what exactly was sailed over or beyond.
