Word: υπερηφανους

Etymology:
The word υπερηφανους (yperifanoús) is derived from the Greek words υπέρ (yper), meaning "above" or "beyond," and ηφανία (ifanía), meaning "pride" or "arrogance." Therefore, the word υπερηφανους can be translated to mean "overly proud" or "arrogant."

Grammar:
υπερηφανους is the plural form of the adjective υπερήφανος (yperífanos), which means "proud" or "arrogant." It is in the masculine gender and accusative case, and it agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description:
υπερηφανους is an adjective that describes someone who is excessively proud or arrogant. It can be used to describe a person's behavior, attitude, or demeanor. The word carries a negative connotation and is often used to criticize someone for their excessive pride or arrogance.
