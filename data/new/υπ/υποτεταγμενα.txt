Word: υποτεταγμένα

Etymology:
The word υποτεταγμένα comes from the Greek verb υποτάσσω (hypotássō), which means "to submit" or "to subordinate". The word is formed from the prefix υπο- (hypo-), meaning "under", and the verb τάσσω (tássō), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order".

Grammar:
υποτεταγμένα is the plural neuter form of the past participle of the verb υποτάσσω (hypotássō). It is used as an adjective to describe something that has been subordinated or placed under the authority of something else.

Description:
υποτεταγμένα is an adjective that describes something that has been placed under the authority or control of something else. It can be used to describe a subordinate position in a hierarchy, such as a subordinate employee in a company, or a subordinate clause in a sentence. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any situation where one thing is subordinated to another, such as a country being subordinated to a more powerful nation.
