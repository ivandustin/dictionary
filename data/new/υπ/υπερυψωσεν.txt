Etymology:
The word "υπερυψωσεν" (hyperypsōsen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words "υπέρ" (hyper) meaning "above" or "beyond" and "ύψωση" (ypsōsē) meaning "height" or "elevation". The verb form means "to lift up" or "to elevate".

Grammar:
"υπερυψωσεν" is a third-person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

The word "υπερυψωσεν" is used to describe the action of lifting up the humble.
