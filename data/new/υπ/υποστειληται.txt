The word "υποστειληται" (yposteilētai) is a Greek verb in the third person singular form of the passive voice. It comes from the root word "υποστηρίζω" (ypostirizō), which means "to support" or "to sustain". The suffix "-ται" (-tai) indicates that the verb is in the passive voice, meaning that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

In terms of grammar, "υποστειληται" is a present tense verb, indicating that the action is happening currently. It is also in the indicative mood, which means that it is a statement of fact rather than a command or a question.

Overall, "υποστειληται" can be translated to mean "is being supported" or "is being sustained".
