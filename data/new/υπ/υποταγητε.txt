The word "υποταγητε" is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "υποτάσσω" (hypotássō), which means "to submit" or "to obey". It is in the second person plural, aorist tense, imperative mood. 

Etymology: The word "υποτάσσω" comes from the prefix "υπο-" (hypo-), meaning "under" or "beneath", and the verb "τάσσω" (tássō), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order". Therefore, "υποτάσσω" literally means "to put under order" or "to arrange under".

Grammar: As mentioned, "υποταγητε" is in the second person plural, aorist tense, imperative mood. The aorist tense in Greek generally refers to a completed action in the past, while the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "υποταγητε" is a command form of the verb "υποτάσσω", telling a group of people to submit or obey. It can be used in various contexts, such as in military orders, religious teachings, or everyday conversations.
