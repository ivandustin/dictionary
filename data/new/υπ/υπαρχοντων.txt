Word: υπαρχοντων

Etymology: The word υπαρχοντων is derived from the Greek verb υπάρχω (ypárcho), which means "to exist" or "to be present". The suffix -οντων (-onton) is the present participle form of the verb.

Grammar: υπαρχοντων is the genitive plural form of the present participle of the verb υπάρχω. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. The plural form indicates that there are multiple things or people that exist or are present.

Description: υπαρχοντων can be translated to "of those who exist" or "of those who are present". It is often used in Greek to refer to the existence or presence of something or someone. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in philosophy, science, or everyday conversation.
