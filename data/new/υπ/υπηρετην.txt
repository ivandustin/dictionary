Word: υπηρέτην

Etymology:
The word υπηρέτην (yperetin) comes from the ancient Greek word υπηρέτης (yperetes), which means "servant" or "attendant". It is derived from the verb υπηρετέω (ypereteo), which means "to serve" or "to attend to".

υπηρέτην is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb, which means that υπηρέτην is the noun that receives the action of the verb.

Description:
υπηρέτην refers to a servant or attendant who performs various tasks for someone else. In ancient Greece, yperetes were often employed by wealthy families to perform household tasks such as cooking, cleaning, and running errands. They were also used in military contexts to perform tasks such as carrying weapons and supplies.
