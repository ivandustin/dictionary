Word: υπηρετησαν

Etymology: The word υπηρετησαν comes from the Greek verb υπηρετέω (yperetéo), which means "to serve" or "to attend to". It is derived from the noun υπηρέτης (yperetis), which means "servant" or "attendant".

Grammar: υπηρετησαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb υπηρετέω (yperetéo). It is a regular verb in Greek, and its conjugation follows the pattern of verbs ending in -ω (o).

Description: υπηρετησαν means "they served" or "they attended to" in Greek. It is a past tense verb, indicating that the action of serving or attending to something or someone has already been completed. The word can be used in various contexts, such as referring to someone who served in the military or someone who served as a waiter in a restaurant.
