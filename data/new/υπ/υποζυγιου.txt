Word: υποζυγιου

Etymology: The word υποζυγιου comes from the Greek words υπό (hypo) meaning "under" and ζυγός (zygos) meaning "yoke". It literally means "under the yoke".

Grammar: υποζυγιου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun. It is the genitive form of the word υποζυγία (hypozygia), which means "subjugation" or "submission".

Description: υποζυγιου refers to the state of being under someone's control or authority, or being in a subordinate position. It can also refer to the act of being subjugated or subdued. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe the condition of slaves who were under the yoke of their masters. Today, it can be used to describe any situation where one person or group is dominated by another.
