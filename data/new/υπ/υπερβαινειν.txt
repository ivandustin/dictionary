Word: υπερβαινειν

Etymology:
The word υπερβαινειν (yperbainein) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: υπερ (hyper) meaning "above" or "beyond" and βαινειν (bainein) meaning "to go" or "to walk". Therefore, the literal meaning of υπερβαινειν is "to go beyond" or "to surpass".

Grammar:
υπερβαινειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is υπερβαιν- and it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs of this type.

Description:
υπερβαινειν is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to express the idea of surpassing or going beyond something. It can be used both literally and figuratively, and it is often used in a positive sense to describe achievements or accomplishments that exceed expectations. For example, one might say "Ο αθλητής υπερέβη τα όρια του σώματός του και κατάφερε να κερδίσει τον αγώνα" (The athlete went beyond the limits of his body and managed to win the race). However, it can also be used in a negative sense to describe actions or behaviors that are excessive or inappropriate. For example, one might say "Οι πολιτικοί υπερβαίνουν τα όρια της ανοχής με τις δηλώσεις τους" (Politicians exceed the limits of tolerance with their statements).
