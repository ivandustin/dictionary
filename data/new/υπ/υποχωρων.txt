Word: υποχωρών

Etymology: The word υποχωρών comes from the Greek verb υποχωρώ (ypochoró), which means "to retreat" or "to withdraw". It is formed by the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb χωρώ (choró), meaning "to make room" or "to give way".

Grammar: υποχωρών is the present participle of the verb υποχωρώ. It is a masculine noun in the nominative case, singular form. In Greek, present participles are often used as adjectives to describe a noun or pronoun.

Description: υποχωρών refers to someone or something that is retreating or withdrawing. It can be used to describe a military force that is pulling back from a battle, or a person who is stepping back from a situation. It can also be used figuratively to describe a person who is giving up or conceding in an argument or debate.
