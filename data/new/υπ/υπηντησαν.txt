Word: υπηντησαν

Etymology:
The word υπηντησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: υπό (hypo) meaning "under" and ένταξη (entaksi) meaning "encounter". Therefore, the literal meaning of υπηντησαν is "they encountered under".

Grammar:
υπηντησαν is a third-person plural aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb υπαντάω (ipantao) which means "to meet, to encounter". The aorist tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the indicative mood shows that the action is a statement of fact.

Description:
υπηντησαν is a verb that describes an encounter or meeting that happened in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as meeting someone unexpectedly, encountering a problem or obstacle, or coming across a new idea or concept. The word has a sense of surprise or unexpectedness, as it implies that the encounter was not planned or anticipated.
