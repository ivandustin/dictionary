Word: υπερεκεινα

Etymology:
The word υπερεκεινα is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: υπέρ (hyper) meaning "above" or "beyond" and εκείνα (ekeina) meaning "those" or "that". Therefore, the word υπερεκεινα can be translated as "beyond those" or "above that".

Grammar:
In Greek, υπερεκεινα is an adverb that modifies a verb or an adjective. It is in the neuter gender and the plural number, agreeing with the noun it modifies. For example, in the sentence "Τα αστέρια είναι υπερεκείνα", which means "The stars are beyond that", υπερεκεινα modifies the noun αστέρια (stars) and agrees with it in gender and number.

Description:
The word υπερεκεινα is used to describe something that is beyond or above a certain point or limit. It can be used in a literal sense, such as referring to something that is physically beyond a certain point, or in a figurative sense, such as referring to something that is beyond a certain level of understanding or comprehension. It is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to convey a sense of transcendence or superiority.
