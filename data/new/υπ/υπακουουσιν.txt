Word: υπακουουσιν

Etymology: The word υπακουουσιν comes from the Greek verb υπακούω (hypakoúo), which means "to obey" or "to listen attentively". The word is formed from the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under", and the verb ακούω (akoúo), meaning "to hear".

- υπακούω (hypakoúo) - I obey, I listen attentively
- υπακούεις (hypakoúeis) - you obey, you listen attentively
- υπακούει (hypakoúei) - he/she/it obeys, he/she/it listens attentively
- υπακουουμεν (hypakoúoumen) - we obey, we listen attentively
- υπακουουτε (hypakoúoute) - you (plural) obey, you (plural) listen attentively
- υπακουουσιν (hypakoúousin) - they obey, they listen attentively

Description: υπακουουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they obey" or "they listen attentively". It is often used in the context of religious or moral obedience, but can also be used in a more general sense of following instructions or heeding advice.
