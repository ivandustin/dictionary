The word "υποστρεφοντι" (ypostrefonti) is a present participle in Greek, which means "turning back" or "returning". 

Etymology: 
The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "υπο" (ypo) meaning "under" or "below" and "στρέφω" (strefo) meaning "to turn". 

Grammar: 
As mentioned earlier, "υποστρεφοντι" is a present participle, which is a verb form that functions as an adjective. It is formed by adding the suffix "-οντ-" to the present stem of the verb, which in this case is "στρέφω". The ending "-ι" indicates that the participle is in the masculine nominative singular form. 

Description: 
The word "υποστρεφοντι" describes someone or something that is currently turning back or returning. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person who is physically turning back or returning to a place, or a situation that is reverting back to a previous state.
