Word: υποστρέφειν

Etymology:
The word υποστρέφειν comes from the combination of two Greek words: υπό (hypo) meaning "under" or "beneath" and στρέφειν (strepho) meaning "to turn". Therefore, the literal meaning of υποστρέφειν is "to turn under" or "to turn beneath".

Grammar:
υποστρέφειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is υποστρεφ- and it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs of this type.

Description:
υποστρέφειν is a Greek verb that means "to turn back" or "to return". It is often used in the context of physical movement, such as when someone turns back to go in the opposite direction or returns to a previous location. It can also be used in a metaphorical sense, such as when someone "turns back" to a previous topic of conversation or "returns" to a previous state of mind.
