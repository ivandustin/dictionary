Word: υπηκουσεν

Etymology:
The word υπηκουσεν (ypēkousen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word υπακούω (ypakouō), which means "to obey" or "to comply with." The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
υπηκουσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb υπακούω (ypakouō), which means "to obey" or "to comply with." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
The word υπηκουσεν (ypēkousen) means "he/she/it obeyed" in Greek. It is a verb that describes an action of compliance or obedience in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a historical narrative or in a religious text to describe someone's obedience to a higher power.
