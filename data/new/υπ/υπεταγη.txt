Word: υπεταγη

Etymology:
The word υπεταγη (ipetagi) is a past tense form of the Greek verb υποτάσσω (ipotasso), which means "to submit" or "to obey". The verb is derived from the prefix υπο- (ipo-), meaning "under", and the verb τάσσω (tasso), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order".

Grammar:
υπεταγη is a third person singular past tense form of the verb υποτάσσω (ipotasso). In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action of submitting or obeying has already taken place.

Description:
υπεταγη describes an action that has already happened in the past, where someone submitted or obeyed someone else. The context of the sentence would determine who submitted to whom and why. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a military setting, in a workplace, or in a personal relationship.
