Word: υπομιμνηίσκειν

Etymology:
The word υπομιμνηίσκειν comes from the Greek roots υπο- (hypo-), meaning "under" or "beneath," and μιμνήσκω (mimnisko), meaning "to remind." Therefore, υπομιμνηίσκειν means "to remind someone of something."

Grammar:
υπομιμνηίσκειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν to the stem υπομιμνηίσκ-.

Description:
υπομιμνηίσκειν is a Greek verb that means "to remind someone of something." It is often used in a formal or academic context, such as reminding someone of a rule or policy. The verb can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to match the subject and context of the sentence.
