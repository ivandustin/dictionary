Word: υπεχωρησεν

Etymology: The word υπεχωρησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: υπό (hypo) meaning "under" and χωρέω (choreo) meaning "to withdraw" or "to retire". Therefore, υπεχωρησεν means "he/she/it withdrew" or "he/she/it retreated".

Grammar: υπεχωρησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb υποχωρέω (ypochoréo), which means "to withdraw" or "to retreat". The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, completed at a specific point in time. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact or reality.

Description: υπεχωρησεν is a Greek verb that describes an action of withdrawing or retreating. It can be used in various contexts, such as in military or political situations, or in personal relationships. The word implies a deliberate action of moving away from a certain place or situation.
