Word: υποτιθέμενος

Etymology:
The word υποτιθέμενος comes from the Greek verb υποτίθημι (hypotithemi), which means "to place under, to suppose, to assume". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending, which means "being, having been, or about to be".

Grammar:
υποτιθέμενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb υποτίθημι (hypotithemi), which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense.

Description:
The word υποτιθέμενος is used to describe something that is being assumed or supposed. It can also be used to describe something that is hypothetical or conjectural.
