Word: υποτασσόμενος

Etymology:
The word υποτασσόμενος comes from the Greek verb υποτάσσω (hypotassō), which means "to submit" or "to subordinate". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word describes an ongoing action or state.

Grammar:
υποτασσόμενος is a present participle in the passive voice, which means that the subject is receiving the action of the verb. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it could be translated as "being subjected" or "being subordinated".

Description:
υποτασσόμενος describes a state of being under someone else's authority or control. It can be used to describe a person who is subordinate to someone else, or a group that is under the control of another group. The word can also be used in a more abstract sense, to describe a situation or condition that is subject to external forces or influences.
