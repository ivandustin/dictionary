Word: υπεστρεψαν

Etymology: υπεστρεψαν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: υπο (hypo), meaning "under" or "beneath," and εστρεψαν (estrepsan), meaning "turned." Therefore, υπεστρεψαν means "they turned back" or "they returned."

Grammar: υπεστρεψαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb υποστρέφω (hypostrefó), which means "to turn back" or "to return." In this form, the verb indicates that the action was completed in the past and that it was performed by a group of people.

Description: υπεστρεψαν is a verb that describes an action of returning or turning back. It can be used in various contexts, such as when referring to a group of people who returned from a trip, or when describing a situation where someone changed their mind and went back on their decision. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday speech.
