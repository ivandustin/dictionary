Word: υποδημάτων

Etymology:
The word υποδημάτων (ypodimáton) is a plural form of the Greek word υπόδημα (ypódima), which means "footwear" or "shoe". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: υπό (ypó), meaning "under" or "beneath", and δήμος (dímos), meaning "people" or "crowd". Therefore, υπόδημα originally meant "something worn under the feet by the people".

Grammar:
υποδημάτων is the genitive plural form of υπόδημα. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. In this case, υποδημάτων indicates that the noun it modifies (e.g. παπούτσια, shoes) belongs to or is related to the noun υπόδημα.

Description:
υποδημάτων is a Greek word that refers to footwear or shoes. It is commonly used in everyday language to describe any type of shoes, from sandals to boots. The word is often used in the context of fashion and style, as well as in discussions about comfort and functionality.
