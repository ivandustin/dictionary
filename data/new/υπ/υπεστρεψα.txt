The word "υπεστρεψα" (ypestrepsa) is a Greek verb in the past tense, which means "I returned" in English. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistrefo), which means "to turn back" or "to return". The prefix "υπερ-" (yper-) is added to the verb stem to indicate completion or intensity, giving the meaning of "I returned completely" or "I returned with great intensity".

Grammar: "Υπεστρεψα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "επιστρέφω". It belongs to the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description: The word "υπεστρεψα" is commonly used in everyday Greek language to express the action of returning to a place or situation after being away for a period of time. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a change in attitude or behavior, such as "I returned to my old self".
