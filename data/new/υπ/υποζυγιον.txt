Word: υποζυγιον

Etymology: The word υποζυγιον comes from the Greek words υπο (hypo) meaning "under" and ζυγος (zygos) meaning "yoke". It literally means "under the yoke" and was used to refer to a pack animal or beast of burden.

Grammar: υποζυγιον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is υποζυγια (hypozygia).

Description: υποζυγιον refers to any animal that is used for carrying heavy loads, such as a donkey, mule, or horse. In ancient times, it was commonly used for transportation of goods and people. Today, it is still used in some parts of the world for agricultural and transportation purposes.
