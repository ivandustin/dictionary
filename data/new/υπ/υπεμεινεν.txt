Word: υπεμείνεν (upemeinen)

Etymology:
The word υπεμείνεν comes from the Greek verb υπομένω (upomeno), which means "to endure" or "to bear up under." It is formed from the prefix υπο- (upo-), meaning "under," and the verb μένω (meno), meaning "to remain" or "to stay."

Grammar:
υπεμείνεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb υπομένω (upomeno). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact.

Description:
υπεμείνεν can be translated as "he/she/it endured" or "he/she/it bore up under." The word implies a sense of perseverance and resilience in the face of difficulty or hardship. It is often used in a moral or spiritual sense, to describe someone who has endured suffering or persecution with patience and fortitude.
