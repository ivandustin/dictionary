Word: υπερβαλλουσης

Etymology: The word υπερβαλλουσης comes from the Greek verb υπερβάλλω (hyperballo), which means "to exceed, to surpass, to exaggerate". The suffix -ουσα (-ousa) indicates the feminine present participle form of the verb.

Grammar: υπερβαλλουσης is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb υπερβάλλω in the nominative singular case. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently exceeding or surpassing something.

Description: υπερβαλλουσης can be translated to English as "exceeding, surpassing, exaggerating". It is often used to describe someone who is going beyond what is expected or necessary, or who is exaggerating a situation. In Greek literature, it is also used to describe heroic or epic actions.
