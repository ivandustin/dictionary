Word: υπαγε (ypage)

Etymology:
The word υπαγε (ypage) comes from the Greek verb υπάγω (ypago), which means "to go under" or "to go away". It is derived from the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under", and the verb αγω (ago), meaning "to lead" or "to bring".

Grammar:
υπαγε (ypage) is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular. It is the singular form of the verb υπάγετε (ypagete), which is the plural form of the same imperative verb.

Description:
υπαγε (ypage) is a command form of the verb υπάγω (ypago), which means "go away" or "depart". It is often used in a formal or commanding context, such as in a military or religious setting. It can also be used in a more casual context, such as telling someone to leave or go away.
