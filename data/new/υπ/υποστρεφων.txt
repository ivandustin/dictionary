Word: υποστρέφων

Etymology: The word υποστρέφων comes from the Greek roots υπό (hypo) meaning "under" or "beneath" and στρέφω (strefó) meaning "to turn" or "to twist". Therefore, υποστρέφων means "turning under" or "twisting beneath".

Grammar: υποστρέφων is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and singular number. It is derived from the verb υποστρέφω (hypostrefó) which means "to turn back" or "to return".

Description: υποστρέφων describes someone or something that is currently turning back or returning. It can be used to describe a physical action, such as a person turning around to go back the way they came, or a metaphorical action, such as a person changing their mind and returning to a previous decision.
