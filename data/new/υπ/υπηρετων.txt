Word: υπηρετων

Etymology:
The word υπηρετων (yperetōn) is derived from the Greek verb υπηρετέω (yperetéō), which means "to serve" or "to attend to." It is formed from the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under," and the verb ερετέω (eretéō), meaning "to row" or "to serve."

Grammar:
υπηρετων is the genitive plural form of υπηρετής (yperetēs), which means "servant" or "attendant." In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple servants or attendants.

Description:
υπηρετων is a word that refers to a group of servants or attendants. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to describe the staff of a household, the attendants of a king or queen, or the servants of a temple. The word connotes a sense of duty and service, and is often used in a respectful or formal manner.
