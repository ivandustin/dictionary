Word: υπαντησαι

Etymology:
The word υπαντησαι comes from the Greek verb υπαντάω (ipantáo), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". It is derived from the prefix υπο- (ypo-), meaning "under" or "below", and the verb ανταω (antáo), meaning "to meet".

Grammar:
υπαντησαι is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense and the active voice. It is the second person singular form of the verb υπαντάω (ipantáo), which means "you met" or "you encountered".

Description:
υπαντησαι is a Greek verb that describes the act of meeting or encountering someone or something. It is often used in everyday conversation to describe a chance meeting with someone, or to describe a planned meeting or appointment. The word can also be used in a more figurative sense, to describe a meeting of ideas or concepts.
