Word: υπακουειν

The word υπακουειν (ypakouein) is derived from the Greek verb υπακούω (ypakoúo), which means "to obey" or "to listen attentively". The word is composed of two parts: υπό (ypó), which means "under" or "beneath", and ακούω (akoúo), which means "to hear" or "to listen".

υπακουειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific tense, person, or number. It is often used in combination with auxiliary verbs to form compound tenses, such as the present continuous (e.g. "I am obeying" = υπακούω) or the past perfect (e.g. "I had obeyed" = είχα υπακουσει).

The word υπακουειν is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of obeying or following instructions.
