Word: πωλοῦντες (pōloûntes)

Etymology:
The word πωλοῦντες comes from the Greek verb πωλέω (pōléō), which means "to sell". The suffix -οῦντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently selling something.

Grammar:
πωλοῦντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is declined like a regular first-declension noun, with the stem πωλού- (pōloú-) and the ending -ντες (-ntes). The word functions as a noun, and can be used as the subject or object of a sentence.

Description:
πωλοῦντες refers to people who are currently selling something. It can be used to describe merchants, salespeople, or anyone engaged in the act of selling goods or services. The word has a neutral connotation and does not imply anything about the quality or legality of the sales being made.
