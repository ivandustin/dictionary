Word: πωρωσις

Etymology:
The word πωρωσις (pōrōsis) comes from the Greek verb πωρόω (pōróō), which means "to harden" or "to petrify". It is derived from the noun πέτρα (pétra), which means "rock" or "stone".

Grammar:
πωρωσις is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word πωρωσις is declined according to the first declension, which means that its stem ends in -οσις (-osis).

Description:
πωρωσις refers to the medical condition of osteoporosis, which is a disease that weakens bones and makes them more prone to fractures. The word is used in medical terminology to describe the process of bone loss and the resulting decrease in bone density. Osteoporosis is a common condition, especially among older adults, and can lead to serious health problems if left untreated. Treatment options include medication, exercise, and dietary changes.
