The word "πωλουσιν" (pōlousin) is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form, in the Greek language. It comes from the root word "πωλέω" (pōleō), which means "to sell". Therefore, "πωλουσιν" means "they are selling" or "they sell". 

In terms of grammar, "πωλουσιν" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs. It is formed by adding the present tense suffix "-ουσιν" to the stem "πωλε-". 

Overall, "πωλουσιν" is a simple and common verb in Greek, used to describe the action of selling.
