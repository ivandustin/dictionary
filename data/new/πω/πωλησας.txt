Word: πωλησας

Etymology:
The word πωλησας (polēsas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πωλέω (poleō), which means "to sell". The suffix -σας (-sas) is the aorist tense ending for the second person singular in the active voice.

Grammar:
πωλησας is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is used to describe a completed action in the past, specifically the act of selling. The verb is conjugated as follows:

πωλέω (poleō) - to sell
πωλησας (polēsas) - you sold

Description:
πωλησας is a verb that describes the act of selling something in the past. It is commonly used in Greek literature and conversation to refer to a completed transaction. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as selling goods or services, or even selling oneself in a metaphorical sense.
