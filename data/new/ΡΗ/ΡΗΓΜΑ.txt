Etymology: The word "ρηγμα" (rigma) comes from the Greek verb "ρήγνυμι" (rignymi), which means "to break" or "to rupture."

Grammar: In Greek, "ρηγμα" is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "ρήγματα" (rigmata).

Description: "Ρηγμα" refers to a rupture, a break, or a crack. It is often used to describe a physical or structural breakage, such as a fracture in a bone or a crack in a wall. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a division or a rupture in a relationship, society, or any other aspect of life.
