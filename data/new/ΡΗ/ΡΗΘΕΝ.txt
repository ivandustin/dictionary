Etymology: The word "ρηθεν" (rēthen) comes from the Greek verb "ρητός" (rētos), which means "spoken" or "uttered."

Grammar: "Ρηθεν" is a noun in the singular form. It is a neuter noun in the nominative case.

Description: "Ρηθεν" refers to something that has been spoken or uttered. It can be used to describe a statement, a piece of information, or a message that has been communicated verbally.
