Etymology: The word "ρηξον" comes from the Greek verb "ρήγνυμι" (rēgnymi), which means "to break" or "to tear apart".

Grammar: "Ρηξον" is the imperative form of the verb "ρήγνυμι" (rēgnymi) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "break" or "tear apart" something.

Description: "Ρηξον" is a Greek word that specifically instructs someone to break or tear something apart. It is a direct and forceful command, often used in situations where immediate action is required.
