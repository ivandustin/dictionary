Etymology: The word "ρηγνυνται" comes from the Greek verb "ρήγνυμι" (rēgnymi), which means "to break" or "to shatter."

Grammar: "Ρηγνυνται" is the third person plural form of the present tense in the Greek language. It is a passive voice verb form, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of breaking or shattering.

Description: "Ρηγνυνται" describes the action of something being broken or shattered. It can be used to refer to physical objects or abstract concepts.
