Word: θηλαζουσαις

Etymology: θηλαζουσαις is a Greek word derived from the verb θηλάζω (thelazo), which means "to suckle" or "to nurse." The suffix -ουσας (-ousas) indicates the present participle form, which means "suckling" or "nursing."

Grammar: θηλαζουσαις is a feminine noun in the present participle form, which means "suckling" or "nursing." It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: θηλαζουσαις is a word that describes a female who is currently breastfeeding or nursing a child. It is often used in medical or scientific contexts to describe the act of breastfeeding.
