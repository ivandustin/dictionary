Word: θησαυρούς

Etymology: θησαυρούς is a Greek word that comes from the root word θησαυρός (thēsaurós), which means "treasure" or "storehouse". The suffix -ούς (-oús) indicates the plural form.

Grammar: θησαυρούς is a masculine noun in the accusative plural form in Greek. It is often used as a direct object in a sentence.

Description: θησαυρούς refers to treasures or valuable possessions that are stored or kept in a safe place. In Greek mythology, the god Hades was said to have a treasury of riches in the underworld. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to anything that is highly valued or cherished.
