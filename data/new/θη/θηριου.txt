The word "θηρίου" (theriou) is a Greek noun that means "beast" or "wild animal". It is derived from the Greek word "θηράω" (therao), which means "to hunt". 

In terms of grammar, "θηρίου" is a singular genitive form of the noun. It can be used to indicate possession or association with the noun it modifies. For example, "τα δόντια του θηρίου" (ta dontia tou theriou) means "the teeth of the beast".

Overall, "θηρίου" is a descriptive word that is often used in literature and mythology to describe fierce or dangerous creatures.
