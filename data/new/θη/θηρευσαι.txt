Word: θηρεύσαι

Etymology: θηρεύσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the noun θήρα (théra), meaning "hunt" or "prey". The verb form means "to hunt" or "to chase after".

Grammar: θηρεύσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in ancient Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to hunt or chase after something.

Description: θηρεύσαι is a verb that describes the act of hunting or chasing after prey. It is often used in ancient Greek literature to describe the actions of hunters or warriors. The imperative form of the verb is used to give a direct command to someone to hunt or chase after something.
