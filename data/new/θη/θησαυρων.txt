Word: θησαυρων

Etymology: θησαυρων is a Greek word that comes from the root word θησαυρός (thesauros), which means "treasure" or "storehouse."

Grammar: θησαυρων is a plural noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive plural form of θησαυρός (thesauros).

Description: θησαυρων refers to treasures or riches that are stored or accumulated. In Greek mythology, the god Hades was said to have a treasury of riches in the underworld. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to anything that is valuable or precious.
