Word: θησαυρός (thēsaurós)

Grammar: θησαυρός is a masculine noun in Greek, and its nominative singular form is θησαυρός. In the plural, it becomes θησαυροί (thēsaurói). It is declined like other second declension masculine nouns, with the genitive singular form being θησαυρού (thēsauroú).

Description: θησαυρός refers to a collection of valuable objects or wealth that is stored or hidden away. It can also refer to a treasury or a storehouse where such items are kept. In Greek mythology, the god Hades was said to have a θησαυρός in the underworld, where he kept the riches of the earth.
