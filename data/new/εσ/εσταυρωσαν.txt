Word: εσταυρωσαν

Etymology:
The word εσταυρωσαν comes from the Greek verb σταυρόω (stauróō), which means "to crucify". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense, making εσταυρωσαν (estaurōsan) mean "they crucified".

Grammar:
εσταυρωσαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb σταυρόω (stauróō). It is in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question. In this case, it is a statement indicating that "they" (an unspecified group of people) crucified someone.

Description:
εσταυρωσαν is a powerful and emotive word that describes a brutal and painful form of execution. It is often associated with the crucifixion of Jesus Christ, which is a central event in Christian theology. The word conveys a sense of violence, suffering, and sacrifice, and is often used in a religious or spiritual context.
