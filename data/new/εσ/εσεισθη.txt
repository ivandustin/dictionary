Word: εσεισθη

Etymology:
The word εσεισθη comes from the Greek verb σείω (seio), which means "to shake" or "to tremble". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a past tense form, so εσεισθη means "was shaken" or "trembled".

Grammar:
εσεισθη is a third person singular past tense form of the verb σείω (seio). It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is passive, meaning that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
εσεισθη is a Greek word that describes a state of being shaken or trembling. It can be used to describe physical shaking or trembling, as well as emotional or mental agitation. The word is often used in the context of earthquakes or other natural disasters, but can also be used metaphorically to describe a person's emotional state.
