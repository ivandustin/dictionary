The word "εστηκως" (estekos) is a Greek verb in the perfect tense, which means that the action it describes has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "στέκω" (steko), which means "to stand". The prefix "ε-" (e-) indicates the perfect tense, which adds the idea of completion to the verb.

Grammar: "Εστηκως" is the second person singular form of the perfect tense of "στέκω". It can be translated as "you have stood" or "you have been standing". 

Description: The word describes a completed action of standing or being in a standing position, with the implication that the person is still standing or has remained in a standing position. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has taken a firm or unwavering stance on a particular issue.
