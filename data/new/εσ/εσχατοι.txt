Word: εσχατοι

Etymology: The word εσχατοι (eschatos) comes from the Greek word ἔσχατος (eskhatos), which means "last" or "final". It is derived from the root word σχέδω (skhedo), which means "to hold" or "to have".

Grammar: εσχατοι is the plural form of the adjective εσχατος (eschatos), which means "last" or "final". It is used to describe the end or conclusion of something.

Description: εσχατοι is often used in a religious or philosophical context to refer to the end of the world or the final judgment. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the last or final stage of something, such as the last chapter of a book or the final moments of a game.
