Word: εστρωμένον

Etymology:
The word εστρωμένον comes from the Greek verb στρώνω (stróno), which means "to spread out" or "to lay down". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past participle form of the verb, so εστρωμένον means "spread out" or "laid down".

Grammar:
εστρωμένον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
εστρωμένον describes something that has been spread out or laid down. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a tablecloth that has been spread out on a table, or a bed that has been made with fresh sheets. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation that has been prepared or arranged in advance.
