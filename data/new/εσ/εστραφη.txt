Word: εστραφη

Etymology: The word εστραφη comes from the Greek verb στρέφω (strefó), which means "to turn" or "to twist". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a change of direction or a reversal of action.

Grammar: εστραφη is the third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb στρέφω (strefó). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: εστραφη can be translated as "he/she/it was turned" or "he/she/it turned around". It implies a sudden change of direction or a reversal of movement. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as when describing the movement of a person or object, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing a change of opinion or attitude.
