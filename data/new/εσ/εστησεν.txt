Word: εστησεν

Etymology:
The word εστησεν (estesen) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb στήνω (steno), which means "to stand" or "to set up". The verb στήνω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂-, which means "to stand" or "to be firm".

Grammar:
εστησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb στήνω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
εστησεν can be translated as "he/she/it stood" or "he/she/it set up". The context of the sentence will determine the exact meaning. For example, εστησεν το τραπεζι (estesen to trapezi) means "he/she/it set up the table", while εστησεν εκει (estesen ekei) means "he/she/it stood there". The verb στήνω is a common and versatile verb in Greek, used in a variety of contexts to describe standing, setting up, establishing, and more.
