The word "εσπαρμενον" (esparmenon) is a Greek verb in the passive participle form. It comes from the verb "σπείρω" (speiro), which means "to sow" or "to scatter." The passive participle form indicates that the action of sowing or scattering has been done to the subject of the sentence.

In terms of grammar, "εσπαρμενον" is in the neuter gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can function as a subject or a predicate nominative in a sentence.

Overall, "εσπαρμενον" describes something that has been sown or scattered, and it can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing seeds, crops, or even ideas.
