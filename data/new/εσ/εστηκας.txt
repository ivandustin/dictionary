Word: εστηκας

Etymology: The word εστηκας comes from the Greek verb "στέκω" (stéko), which means "to stand". The prefix "ε-" is added to the verb to indicate the past tense, making εστηκας the past tense form of "στέκω".

Grammar: εστηκας is a second person singular past tense form of the verb "στέκω". It is used to indicate that the subject (you) stood at a specific point in the past.

Description: εστηκας is a Greek word that means "you stood" in English. It is a past tense form of the verb "στέκω" and is used to describe an action that took place in the past.
