Word: εσχηκεν

Etymology:
The word εσχηκεν is a perfect tense form of the Greek verb σχεῖν (schein), which means "to hold" or "to have". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the perfect tense, which denotes a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present.

Grammar:
εσχηκεν is a third-person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb σχεῖν. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe a past action that has present relevance or ongoing effects. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact or reality.

Description:
εσχηκεν can be translated as "he/she/it has held" or "he/she/it has had". The word implies that the action of holding or having has been completed in the past, but the effects of that action are still present in the present. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the verb.
