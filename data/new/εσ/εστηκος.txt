Word: εστηκος

Etymology:
The word εστηκος (estekos) is derived from the Greek verb "ίστημι" (istemi), which means "to stand". The suffix "-κος" (-kos) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
εστηκος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined in different cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
εστηκος describes something or someone that is standing or upright. It can also be used to describe something that is fixed or stable. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a person who is reliable or trustworthy.
