Word: εστωτες

Etymology:
The word εστωτες (estotes) is a Greek word that comes from the verb εἰμί (eimi), which means "to be." It is the present participle of the verb, which means "being" or "existing."

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, εστωτες is a masculine plural noun in the nominative case. It is often used as a predicate adjective, meaning it describes the subject of the sentence. For example, "The men are being brave" would be translated as "οι άνδρες είναι εστωτες."

Description:
The word εστωτες is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the concept of existence or being. It can also be used to describe someone who is present or existing in a particular situation. In modern Greek, it is not commonly used in everyday language.
