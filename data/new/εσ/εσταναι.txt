Word: εσταναι

Etymology: The word εσταναι is derived from the Greek verb "είμαι" (eimai), which means "to be". It is a conjugated form of the verb in the third person singular, present tense, indicative mood.

Grammar: εσταναι is a verb in the Greek language. It is a present tense verb in the indicative mood, which means that it is used to express a fact or a statement. It is also in the third person singular, which means that it refers to a single person or thing.

Description: εσταναι is a common verb in the Greek language, used to express the state of being or existence of a person or thing. It is often used in sentences to describe someone or something, such as "Ο άνθρωπος είναι ψηλός" (The man is tall) or "Η καρέκλα είναι κόκκινη" (The chair is red). It is an important verb to know when learning Greek, as it is used frequently in everyday conversation.
