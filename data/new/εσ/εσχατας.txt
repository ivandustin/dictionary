Word: εσχατας

Etymology:
The word εσχατας (eschátas) is derived from the Greek word εσχατος (eschátos), which means "last" or "final". It is a combination of the prefix ες- (es-), meaning "into" or "towards", and the root word χατος (chatos), meaning "end" or "extreme".

Grammar:
In Greek, εσχατας is a masculine noun in the accusative case. It is the plural form of εσχατος (eschátos), which means "last" or "final". The word can be used as an adjective, as in εσχατος αιων (eschátos aión), meaning "end of the world".

Description:
The word εσχατας is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to refer to the end of the world or the final judgment. It can also be used more generally to refer to the end or conclusion of something.
