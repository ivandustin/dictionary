Word: εσχατον

Etymology:
The word εσχατον (eschaton) is derived from the Greek word εσχατος (eschatos), which means "last" or "final." It is often used in reference to the end times or the final judgment.

Grammar:
εσχατον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe a noun as being the "last" or "final" one.

Description:
εσχατον is a word that is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to refer to the end times or the final judgment. It can also be used more generally to describe something as being the "last" or "final" one in a series.
