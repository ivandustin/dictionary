Word: εσθιουσιν

Etymology: The word εσθιουσιν is derived from the Greek verb "εσθίω" (esthio), which means "to eat". The suffix "-ουσιν" (-ousin) indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: εσθιουσιν is a present tense, third person plural form of the Greek verb "εσθίω" (esthio), which means "to eat". It is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible.

Description: εσθιουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they eat" in English. It is used to describe the action of a group of people consuming food. In the New Testament, it is often used in reference to the Last Supper, where Jesus and his disciples are said to have eaten together.
