Word: εσχατων

Etymology: The word εσχατων comes from the Greek word εσχατος (eschatos), which means "last" or "final". The suffix -ων (-on) is added to make it plural.

Grammar: εσχατων is a plural noun in the genitive case. It is often used in the phrase "των εσχατων ημερων" (ton eschaton hemeron), which means "the last days".

Description: εσχατων is a biblical term that refers to the end times or the last days. It is often used in the context of eschatology, which is the study of the end of the world and the ultimate destiny of humanity. In Christian theology, the term is associated with the Second Coming of Christ and the final judgment.
