Word: εσεβασθησαν

Etymology: 
The word εσεβασθησαν comes from the Greek verb εὐσεβέω (eusebeo), which means "to be pious" or "to show reverence." The verb is derived from the adjective εὐσεβής (eusebes), which means "pious" or "reverent." 

Grammar:
εσεβασθησαν is a third-person plural aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. 

Description:
εσεβασθησαν is a verb that means "they showed reverence" or "they were pious." It is often used in a religious context to describe acts of worship or devotion. The word appears in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the book of Acts, where it is used to describe the actions of devout Jews and Gentiles who converted to Christianity.
