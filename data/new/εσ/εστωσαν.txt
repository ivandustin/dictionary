Word: εστωσαν

Etymology: The word εστωσαν is derived from the Greek verb "είμαι" (eimai), which means "to be". It is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb.

Grammar: εστωσαν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: εστωσαν is a verb that is used to indicate that a group of people or things existed or were present in the past. It can be translated as "they were" or "they existed". It is often used in historical or narrative contexts to describe events that took place in the past.
