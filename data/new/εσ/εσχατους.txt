The word "εσχατους" (eschátous) is a Greek adjective that means "last" or "final". It is derived from the Greek word "έσχατος" (éskhatos), which also means "last" or "final". In terms of grammar, "εσχατους" is in the masculine plural form, and it is used to describe a group of people or things that are the last or final in a series or sequence. For example, "οι εσχατοί μαθητές" (oi eschátoi mathítes) means "the last students" or "the final students".
