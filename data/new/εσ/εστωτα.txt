Word: εστωτα

Etymology:
The word εστωτα (estota) is derived from the Greek verb εἰμί (eimí), which means "to be" in the present tense. The suffix -ωτα (-ota) is added to the stem of the verb to form the present participle, which means "being" or "existing."

Grammar:
εστωτα is the present participle of the verb εἰμί (eimí), which means "being" or "existing." In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes a continuous action or state of being. It can be used as an adjective, adverb, or noun in a sentence.

Description:
εστωτα is a Greek word that describes a continuous state of being or existence. It can be used to describe a person, thing, or situation that is currently happening or existing. For example, in the sentence "Ο άνθρωπος είναι εστωτα στο δωμάτιο" (The man is being in the room), εστωτα describes the continuous state of being of the man in the room.
