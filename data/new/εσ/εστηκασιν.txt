The word "εστηκασιν" is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "στέκω" (stéko), which means "to stand". It is in the third person plural, aorist active indicative tense, indicating that the action of standing was completed in the past. 

In terms of grammar, "εστηκασιν" is a regular conjugation of the verb "στέκω" in the aorist tense. The prefix "ε-" indicates the aorist tense, while the ending "-ασιν" indicates the third person plural. 

Overall, "εστηκασιν" means "they stood" in English.
