Word: εσαλευθη

Etymology:
The word εσαλευθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σαλεύω (salevo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to indicate a past tense form, making εσαλευθη (esalevthi) mean "was shaken" or "was moved back and forth".

Grammar:
εσαλευθη is a third person singular past tense form of the verb σαλεύω (salevo). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is passive, indicating that the subject of the sentence was acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
εσαλευθη is a word that describes a state of being shaken or moved back and forth. It can be used to describe physical movements, such as the shaking of an earthquake or the swaying of a boat on rough waters. It can also be used metaphorically to describe emotional or mental turmoil, such as feeling shaken by a traumatic event or being emotionally moved by a powerful experience.
