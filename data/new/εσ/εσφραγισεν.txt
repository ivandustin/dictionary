Word: εσφραγισεν

Etymology:
The word εσφραγισεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb σφραγίζω (sphragizo), which means "to seal" or "to stamp." The prefix ε- (e-) is a common Greek prefix that can indicate a completed action or a past tense.

Grammar:
εσφραγισεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb σφραγίζω (sphragizo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
εσφραγισεν can be translated as "he/she/it sealed" or "he/she/it stamped." The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the sealing of a tomb (Matthew 27:66) and the sealing of the servants of God (Revelation 7:3). The word conveys a sense of finality and security, as if the thing being sealed is protected and cannot be tampered with.
