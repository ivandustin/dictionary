Word: εσπερας

Etymology: The word εσπερας (esperas) comes from the Ancient Greek word ἑσπέρα (hespera), which means "evening" or "dusk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁es- ("to become dark").

Grammar: εσπερας is a masculine noun in Greek, with the nominative singular form being εσπερας. It is declined like the second declension, with the genitive singular form being εσπερου (esperou).

Description: εσπερας refers to the time of day when the sun has set and the sky is getting dark. It can also refer to the evening star, Venus. In Greek mythology, Hesperus was the personification of the evening star and the son of the dawn goddess Eos. The word εσπερας is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to evoke a sense of melancholy or nostalgia.
