Word: εσθων

Etymology:
The word εσθων (esthon) is a Greek word that comes from the verb εσθίω (esthio), which means "to eat" or "to consume." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ed- ("to eat").

Grammar:
In Greek, εσθων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also the present participle of the verb εσθίω (esthio), which means "eating" or "consuming."

Description:
εσθων is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to a person who is eating or consuming food. It is often used in the context of the Last Supper, where Jesus is described as breaking bread and giving it to his disciples, saying "Take, eat; this is my body" (Matthew 26:26). The word is also used in other passages of the New Testament to describe people eating together or sharing a meal.
