Word: εσπουδασα

Etymology: The word εσπουδασα comes from the Greek verb "σπουδάζω" (spoudazo), which means "to study" or "to be diligent". The prefix "ε-" is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action was completed in the past tense.

Grammar: εσπουδασα is a first-person singular past tense form of the verb "σπουδάζω" (spoudazo). It is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb stem is "σπουδασ-", and the suffix "-α" indicates that the subject of the sentence is first-person singular.

Description: εσπουδασα is a Greek verb that means "I studied" or "I was diligent". It is used to describe a completed action in the past, specifically related to studying or being diligent. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing academic or personal pursuits.
