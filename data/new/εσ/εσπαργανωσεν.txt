Word: εσπαργανώσεν

Etymology: The word εσπαργανώσεν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb εσπαργανώνω (esparaganóno), which means "to wrap in swaddling clothes". The word is derived from the noun σπάργανον (spárganon), which means "swaddling clothes".

Grammar: εσπαργανώσεν is the aorist tense, third person singular form of the verb εσπαργανώνω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the sentence is he/she/it.

Description: εσπαργανώσεν is a word that describes the act of wrapping someone in swaddling clothes. In ancient times, swaddling clothes were used to wrap newborn babies tightly to help them feel secure and prevent them from moving around too much. The word is often used in the context of the birth of Jesus Christ, as it is mentioned in the Bible that he was wrapped in swaddling clothes after his birth.
