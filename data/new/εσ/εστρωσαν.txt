Word: εστρωσαν

Etymology: The word εστρωσαν comes from the Greek verb στρώνω (stróno), which means "to spread out" or "to lay down". The prefix ε- indicates the past tense, so εστρωσαν means "they spread out" or "they laid down".

Grammar: εστρωσαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb στρώνω. It is a regular verb in the active voice and belongs to the first conjugation group in Greek.

Description: εστρωσαν is a verb that describes an action of spreading out or laying down something. It can be used in various contexts, such as spreading out a tablecloth, laying down a carpet, or preparing a bed. In the past tense form, it refers to a completed action that happened in the past.
