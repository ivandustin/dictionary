Word: εσεισθησαν

Etymology: 
The word εσεισθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σείω (seio), which means "to shake" or "to tremble". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a third person plural form, making the word mean "they were shaken" or "they trembled".

Grammar:
εσεισθησαν is a third person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
εσεισθησαν is a verb that describes a physical shaking or trembling. It can be used to describe earthquakes, tremors, or any other kind of shaking or movement. In the context of a sentence, it would typically be used to describe a group of people or objects that were shaken or trembled.
