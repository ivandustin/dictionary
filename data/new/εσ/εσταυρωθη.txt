Word: εσταυρωθη

Etymology: The word εσταυρωθη comes from the Greek verb σταυρόω (stauróō), which means "to crucify". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the verb to indicate that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar: εσταυρωθη is a third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb σταυρόω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon.

Description: εσταυρωθη is a Greek word that is commonly translated as "he was crucified" in English. It is a key term in Christian theology, referring to the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the manner in which Jesus was put to death by the Romans. The crucifixion of Jesus is considered by Christians to be a central event in the history of salvation, representing the ultimate sacrifice of love and redemption.
