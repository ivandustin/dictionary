Word: εστηκα

Etymology: The word εστηκα comes from the Greek verb στέκω (stéko), which means "to stand". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to indicate the first person singular present tense.

Grammar: εστηκα is a first person singular present tense form of the verb στέκω (stéko). It is used to indicate that the speaker is currently standing or has just stood up.

Description: εστηκα is a simple and common Greek verb that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to describe the act of standing up from a seated or lying position. The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is standing firm in their beliefs or position.
