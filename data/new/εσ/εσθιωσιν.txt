Word: εσθιωσιν

Etymology: 
The word εσθιωσιν is a present active indicative third person plural form of the Greek verb εσθίω (esthio), which means "to eat". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁ed- meaning "to eat".

Grammar:
εσθιωσιν is a present tense verb in the active voice. It is in the indicative mood and the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person.

Description:
εσθιωσιν is a verb that means "they eat" in English. It is used to describe the action of eating in the present tense. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels, to describe the act of eating food.
