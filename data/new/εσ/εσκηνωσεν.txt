Word: εσκηνωσεν

Etymology: The word εσκηνωσεν comes from the Greek verb σκηνόω (skēnóō), which means "to pitch a tent" or "to dwell in a tent". The prefix ε- (e-) is a common Greek prefix that can indicate a variety of meanings, including "out" or "away". In this case, it likely emphasizes the action of pitching a tent as a temporary dwelling.

In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating the duration or repetition of the action. The verb stem is σκηνόω, and the suffix -σεν (-sen) indicates the third person singular aorist tense.

Description: εσκηνωσεν can be translated as "he pitched a tent" or "he dwelt in a tent". The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of various characters, including Abraham and the Israelites during their journey in the wilderness.
