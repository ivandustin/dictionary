Word: εστωτος

Etymology:
The word εστωτος (estōtos) is derived from the Greek verb εἰμί (eimí), which means "to be" or "to exist." It is the present participle of the verb, which means "being" or "existing."

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, εστωτος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is often used as an adjective to describe a person or thing that is present or existing. It can also be used as a noun to refer to someone who is present or in attendance.

Description:
The word εστωτος is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the concept of being or existence. It is also used in everyday language to refer to someone who is present or in attendance at a particular event or gathering. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the presence of a god or goddess.
