Word: εσαλευσεν

Etymology: εσαλευσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σαλεύω (salevo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εσαλευσεν means "he/she/it shook" or "he/she/it moved back and forth".

Grammar: εσαλευσεν is a third-person singular past tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated from the aorist tense of the verb σαλεύω (salevo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person/number of the subject.

Description: εσαλευσεν describes an action of shaking or moving back and forth that was completed in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing an earthquake, a person shaking something, or a feeling of being shaken emotionally.
