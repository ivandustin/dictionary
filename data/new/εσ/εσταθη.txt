Word: εσταθη

Etymology:
The word εσταθη comes from the Greek verb "ίστημι" (ístimi), which means "to stand". The prefix "ε-" is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar:
εσταθη is a third person singular past tense form of the verb "ίστημι" (ístimi). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence performed the action.

Description:
εσταθη is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it stood". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and other biblical figures. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone standing in a particular place or position.
