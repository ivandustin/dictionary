Word: εστωτας

Etymology:
The word εστωτας (estotas) is derived from the Greek verb "είμαι" (eimai), which means "to be" in the present tense. The suffix "-ωτας" (-otas) is added to the stem of the verb to form the present participle, which means "being" or "existing" in English.

Grammar:
εστωτας is the present participle of the verb είμαι (eimai), which means "to be" in Greek. As a present participle, it functions as an adjective and describes a state of being or existence. It is declined like a regular adjective, agreeing in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
εστωτας is a Greek word that describes a state of being or existence. It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the nature of being or the existence of God. In everyday language, it can be used to describe a person or thing that is currently present or existing.
