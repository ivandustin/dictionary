Word: εστησαν

Etymology: The word εστησαν comes from the Greek verb στήνω (stino), which means "to stand" or "to set up". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to indicate the past tense, making εστησαν (estisan) mean "they stood" or "they set up".

Grammar: εστησαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb στήνω (stino). It is a regular verb in the Greek language, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -ω (-o). The verb stem is στησ- (stis-), and the past tense suffix for the third person plural is -αν (-an).

Description: εστησαν is a Greek verb that means "they stood" or "they set up". It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people standing in a line or setting up equipment for an event. The verb is commonly used in both spoken and written Greek, and is an important part of the language's vocabulary.
