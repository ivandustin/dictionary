Word: εσταυρωμένος

Etymology: The word εσταυρωμένος (estavroménos) comes from the Greek verb σταυρόω (stavróo), which means "to crucify". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the verb to indicate the past participle form, which means "crucified".

Grammar: εσταυρωμένος is a past participle adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe someone who has been crucified.

Description: εσταυρωμένος is a term used in the New Testament to describe the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. It is also used to describe the crucifixion of other individuals in ancient times. The word carries a strong connotation of suffering and sacrifice. In modern times, the word is often used metaphorically to describe someone who is enduring great suffering or hardship.
