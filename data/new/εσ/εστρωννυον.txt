The word "εστρωννυον" (estronnyon) is a verb in ancient Greek that means "to spread out" or "to lay down". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "stornyno" which means "to spread out" or "to stretch". The prefix "e-" is added to indicate the present tense.

Grammar: "εστρωννυον" is the present participle form of the verb "stornyno". It is in the active voice, indicative mood, and present tense. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of preparing a bed or a couch by spreading out blankets or sheets. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the spreading out of a plan or idea.
