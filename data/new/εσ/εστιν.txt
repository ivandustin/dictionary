Word: εστιν

Etymology: εστιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb εἰμί (eimí), which means "to be" or "to exist". It is a present indicative third person singular form of the verb.

Grammar: εστιν is a present indicative third person singular form of the verb εἰμί (eimí). In Greek, the present indicative is used to describe actions or states that are currently happening or exist. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the sentence is singular and in the third person.

Description: εστιν is a simple Greek verb that means "is" or "exists". It is commonly used in Greek sentences to describe the existence or presence of something or someone. For example, "Η καφετέρια εστιν εδώ" (I kafetería estin edó) means "The café is here". It is a fundamental verb in the Greek language and is used in many different contexts.
