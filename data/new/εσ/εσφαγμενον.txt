Word: εσφαγμένον

Etymology:
The word εσφαγμένον (esphagmenon) is a past participle of the Greek verb σφάζω (sfazo), which means "to slaughter" or "to sacrifice." It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *sp(h)eg-, which means "to cut" or "to pierce."

Grammar:
εσφαγμένον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
εσφαγμένον is an adjective that describes something that has been slaughtered or sacrificed. It can be used to describe an animal that has been killed for food or a person who has been killed in a violent manner. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe Jesus Christ as the sacrificial lamb who was slain for the sins of humanity.
