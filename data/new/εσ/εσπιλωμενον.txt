The word "εσπιλωμενον" (espilomenon) is a Greek verb in the passive participle form. It comes from the root verb "σπίλω" (spilo), which means "to stain" or "to defile". The passive participle form indicates that the action of staining or defiling has been done to the subject of the verb.

In terms of grammar, "εσπιλωμενον" is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender. It can be translated to English as "stained" or "defiled".

Overall, "εσπιλωμενον" is a word that describes something that has been stained or defiled, and it is used in a passive sense to indicate that the action was done to the subject.
