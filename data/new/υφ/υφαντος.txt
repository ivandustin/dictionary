Word: υφαντος

The word υφαντος (yfantos) comes from the Greek verb υφαίνω (yfaino), which means "to weave". The suffix -τος (-tos) is added to the verb stem to form an adjective, which means "woven" or "fabricated".

υφαντος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is often used as an adjective to describe something that has been woven or fabricated. In Greek, adjectives must agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, υφαντος χαλί (yfantos chali) means "woven rug", where χαλί (chali) is a neuter noun.
