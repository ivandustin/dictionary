Word: λελυμένα

Etymology: λελυμένα is the neuter plural form of the Greek verb λύω (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". The prefix λε- (le-) indicates that the action is completed or finished.

Grammar: λελυμένα is a neuter plural adjective in the nominative case. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. In this form, it is used to describe a group of things that have been loosened or untied.

Description: λελυμένα can be translated as "loosened", "untied", or "released". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe something that has been freed from a constraint or burden. For example, it can be used to describe a person who has been released from a difficult situation or a burden that they were carrying. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the "loosened" or "untied" state of a donkey that Jesus rode into Jerusalem (Matthew 21:2).
