Word: λεγωσιν

Etymology: λεγωσιν is a present active subjunctive third person plural form of the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The word λέγω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: λεγωσιν is a verb form in the present active subjunctive mood, indicating that the action is uncertain or hypothetical. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people. 

Description: λεγωσιν is used in Greek to express a hypothetical or uncertain action in the present tense. It can be translated as "they may say" or "they might be saying". The subjunctive mood is often used in Greek to express doubt, possibility, or potentiality.
