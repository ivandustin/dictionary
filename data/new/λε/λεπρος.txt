Word: λεπρός

Etymology:
The word λεπρός (lepros) comes from the Greek root word λέπω (lepo), which means "to peel" or "to scale off." It is related to the word λεπίς (lepis), which means "scale" or "flake."

Grammar:
λεπρός is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It is declined like this:

Singular:
- ο λεπρός (o lepros) - masculine
- η λεπρή (i lepri) - feminine
- το λεπρό (to lepro) - neuter

Plural:
- οι λεπροί (oi leproi) - masculine
- οι λεπρές (oi lepres) - feminine
- τα λεπρά (ta lepra) - neuter

Description:
λεπρός is an adjective that describes someone or something that has leprosy, a chronic infectious disease that affects the skin, nerves, and mucous membranes. In ancient Greece, people with leprosy were often ostracized and forced to live outside of the city walls. Today, leprosy is treatable with antibiotics, but it still affects people in some parts of the world.
