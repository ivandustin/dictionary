The word λεγεσθαι (legesthai) is a Greek verb in the passive voice, which means "to be said" or "to be spoken". It is derived from the root word λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". In Greek grammar, λεγεσθαι is a present passive infinitive, which means that it describes an ongoing action that is being done to the subject of the sentence. 

For example, in the sentence "The story is said to be true", λεγεσθαι is used to indicate that the act of saying or speaking about the story is being done to the story itself. 

Overall, λεγεσθαι is a common verb in Greek literature and is often used to convey information or to report what someone has said.
