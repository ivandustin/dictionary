The word "λεγοντα" (legonta) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The present participle form indicates that the action of speaking is currently happening or ongoing. 

In terms of grammar, "λεγοντα" is in the masculine singular nominative case. It can function as a subject or a predicate nominative in a sentence. 

Etymologically, "λέγω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg̑- which means "to collect" or "to gather". This root also gave rise to words such as "legend" and "lecture" in English. 

Overall, "λεγοντα" is a word that describes someone who is currently speaking or saying something.
