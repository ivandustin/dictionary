Word: λεγωμεν

Etymology:
λεγωμεν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The verb λέγω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg-, which means "to collect" or "to gather". 

Grammar:
λεγωμεν is the first person plural present active subjunctive form of λέγω (lego). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the active voice means that the subject is performing the action. The first person plural form indicates that the subject is "we". 

Description:
λεγωμεν can be translated to "let us say" or "we may say". It is often used to introduce a hypothetical or uncertain statement. For example, "λεγωμεν ότι ο καιρός θα είναι καλός αύριο" (legomen oti o kairos tha einai kalos aurio) means "let us say that the weather will be good tomorrow".
