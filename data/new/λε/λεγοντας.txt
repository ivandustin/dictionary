Word: λεγοντας

Etymology: λεγοντας is the present participle of the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: λεγοντας is a masculine accusative plural form of the present participle of the verb λέγω (lego). It is used to describe a group of people who are currently speaking or saying something. In Greek, the present participle is often used to express ongoing or continuous action.

Description: λεγοντας can be translated to English as "saying" or "speaking". It is commonly used in Greek literature and speech to describe a group of people who are currently speaking or expressing their thoughts. For example, in the sentence "Οι λεγόντες είναι φίλοι μου" (Oi legontes einai filoi mou), which means "The ones who are speaking are my friends", λεγοντας refers to the group of people who are currently speaking.
