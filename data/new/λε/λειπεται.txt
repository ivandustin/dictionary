Word: λειπεται

Etymology: λειπεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λείπω (leípō), which means "to leave" or "to be missing."

Grammar: λειπεται is a third-person singular present indicative passive form of the verb λείπω (leípō). It is used to indicate that something is missing or absent.

Description: λειπεται is a common verb in Greek that is used to describe the absence or lack of something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as when referring to a missing person or object, or when describing a situation where something is lacking. The verb is often used in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action themselves.
