The word "λεγουσαν" (legousan) is a form of the Greek verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". It is in the present participle form, feminine gender, and singular number. 

Etymology: The word "λέγω" (lego) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg̑- which means "to collect" or "to gather". 

Grammar: As mentioned earlier, "λεγουσαν" is in the present participle form, which is used to describe an ongoing action. The feminine gender and singular number indicate that the subject of the sentence is a female who is currently speaking. 

Description: "λεγουσαν" can be translated to "she is saying" or "she is speaking". It is commonly used in Greek literature and conversation to describe a female character who is currently speaking or expressing her thoughts.
