The word λεγον (legon) is a present participle in Greek, meaning "saying" or "speaking". It comes from the verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospel of John, where it is used to introduce Jesus' teachings and actions. The word can also be used as a noun, meaning "a saying" or "a statement".
