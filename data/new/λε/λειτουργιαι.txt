Word: λειτουργίαι

Etymology: The word λειτουργίαι comes from the Greek word λειτουργία (leitourgia), which means "public service" or "ministry". It is derived from the words λαός (laos), meaning "people", and ἔργον (ergon), meaning "work".

Grammar: λειτουργίαι is a plural noun in Greek. It is pronounced "leitourgi-ai" and is spelled with the Greek letters λειτουργίες.

Description: λειτουργίαι refers to the liturgical services or rituals performed in the Eastern Orthodox Church. These services include the Divine Liturgy, Vespers, Matins, and other services that are performed throughout the liturgical year. The word can also refer to any public service or ministry performed for the benefit of others.
