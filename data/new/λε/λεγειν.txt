Word: λεγειν

Etymology:
The word λεγειν (legein) is derived from the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". It is a common verb in the Greek language and is used in a variety of contexts.

Grammar:
λεγειν is the present active infinitive form of the verb λέγω (lego). In Greek, the infinitive form is used to express the idea of an action without specifying who is performing the action. It is often translated into English as "to" followed by the base form of the verb.

Description:
λεγειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to express the idea of speaking or saying something, but it can also be used to convey the idea of reading, reciting, or even thinking. In the New Testament, it is frequently used to describe the words and teachings of Jesus and other biblical figures.
