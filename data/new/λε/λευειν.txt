Word: λευειν

Etymology:
The word λευειν (leuein) is derived from the Greek verb λεύω (leúō), which means "to loosen" or "to untie". It is also related to the Greek word λύω (lýō), which means "to release" or "to dissolve".

Grammar:
λευειν (leuein) is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the pattern of verbs that end in -ω (ō) in the present tense.

Description:
λευειν (leuein) means "to loosen" or "to untie" in Greek. It can be used in a literal sense, such as untying a knot, or in a figurative sense, such as loosening one's grip on something. It is a common verb in Greek literature and is used in various contexts.
