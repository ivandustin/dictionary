Word: λεγουσιν

Etymology: λεγουσιν is a present tense verb form in Greek, derived from the root word λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak."

Grammar: λεγουσιν is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb λέγω (lego). It is used to indicate that a group of people are currently saying or speaking something.

Description: λεγουσιν is a common verb form in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe people speaking in a conversation, reciting a poem or story, or making a statement. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the words and actions of Jesus and his followers.
