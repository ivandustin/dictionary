Word: λεοντων

Etymology: λεοντων is the genitive plural form of the Greek word λέων (leōn), which means "lion". The word λέων is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁l̥h₁u-.

Grammar: λεοντων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: λεοντων refers to a group of lions. In ancient Greek mythology, lions were often associated with strength, courage, and royalty. The Nemean lion, for example, was a legendary creature that was said to be invulnerable to weapons and was eventually slain by the hero Heracles as one of his twelve labors. Lions are also a common symbol in heraldry and are often used to represent bravery and power.
