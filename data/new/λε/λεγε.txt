Word: λεγε

Etymology:
λεγε (lege) is a Greek verb that means "to say" or "to speak". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar:
λεγε (lege) is a present tense verb in the second person singular form. It is often used as an imperative, meaning it is a command or request to someone to say or speak something.

Description:
λεγε (lege) is a common verb in the Greek language and is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to ask someone to speak or to express oneself. It can also be used to refer to the act of reading or reciting something aloud. In addition, it can be used in a more figurative sense to mean "to tell" or "to relate" a story or information.
