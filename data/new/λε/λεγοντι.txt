The word "λεγοντι" (legonti) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The present participle form indicates that the action of speaking is currently happening or ongoing. 

Etymology: The verb "λέγω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect" or "to gather". 

Grammar: "λεγοντι" is in the present tense and active voice. It is also in the masculine gender and singular number, agreeing with the subject of the sentence. 

Description: "λεγοντι" can be translated as "saying" or "speaking" in English. It is often used as a participial phrase in Greek sentences to describe an ongoing action or to introduce a direct quote.
