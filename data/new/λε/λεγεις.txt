Word: λεγεις

Etymology:
λεγεις (legeis) is a present tense verb in Greek, which means "you say" or "you speak". It is derived from the root word λεγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The word λεγεις has been used in Greek literature since ancient times.

Grammar:
λεγεις is a second person singular present tense verb in Greek. It is used to address one person directly. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: λεγεις (you say)
- Imperfect tense: έλεγες (you were saying)
- Future tense: θα λες (you will say)

Description:
λεγεις is a common verb in Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to ask someone what they think or to express agreement or disagreement. The verb can also be used to make a statement or to give an opinion. Overall, λεγεις is a versatile verb that is essential for communication in Greek.
