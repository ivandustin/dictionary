Word: λελυπηκεν

Etymology: λελυπηκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λυπη (lypē), which means "grief" or "sorrow". The prefix λε- (le-) indicates that the action of the verb is completed or perfect.

Grammar: λελυπηκεν is the third person singular perfect active indicative form of the verb λυπέω (lypéō), which means "to cause grief" or "to sadden". The perfect tense indicates that the action of the verb was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description: λελυπηκεν describes a state of being where someone or something has been caused grief or sadness in the past and continues to feel the effects of that grief in the present. It can be used to describe a person who is still grieving the loss of a loved one, or a situation that continues to cause sadness or distress.
