Word: λεγομενος

Etymology:
λεγομενος is a Greek word that comes from the verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action is ongoing or continuous. Therefore, λεγομενος means "being said" or "being spoken".

Grammar:
λεγομενος is a present passive participle in the masculine singular form. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or verbs. The present tense indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon.

Description:
λεγομενος is a word that is often used in Greek texts to refer to someone or something that is being talked about or mentioned. It can also be used to describe a person or thing that is known by a certain name or title.
