Word: λεγομενα

Etymology:
λεγομενα is the present participle of the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". The word λέγω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg-, which means "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar:
λεγομενα is a neuter gender noun in the plural form. It is the present participle of the verb λέγω (lego), which means "being said" or "being spoken". In Greek, present participles are often used as adjectives to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description:
λεγομενα refers to things that are being said or spoken. It can be translated as "the things being said" or "the things being spoken". The word is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to refer to ideas or concepts that are being discussed or debated.
