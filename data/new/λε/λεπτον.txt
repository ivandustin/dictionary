Word: λεπτόν

Etymology:
The word λεπτόν (lepton) comes from the Greek word λεπτός (leptos), which means "thin" or "fine". In ancient Greece, the word was used to describe a small, thin coin.

Grammar:
λεπτόν is a neuter noun in the singular form. Its nominative and accusative forms are the same. In the plural form, it becomes λεπτά.

Description:
λεπτόν is a term used in physics to describe a subatomic particle with a very small mass and no electric charge. It is one of the six types of leptons, which also include the electron, muon, and tau. The discovery of the λεπτόν was a significant milestone in the development of the Standard Model of particle physics.
