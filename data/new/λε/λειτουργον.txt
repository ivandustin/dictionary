Word: λειτουργον

Etymology: The word λειτουργον comes from the Greek words λειτουργία (leitourgia) meaning "public service" and ἔργον (ergon) meaning "work". It originally referred to a person who performed a public duty or service, such as a priest or government official.

Grammar: λειτουργον is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is masculine in gender.

Description: λειτουργον refers to a person who performs a public duty or service. In modern Greek, it is often used to refer to a civil servant or government official. In ancient Greece, it was used to refer to a priest who performed religious ceremonies or a wealthy citizen who financed public works. The word has also been used in Christian liturgy to refer to a minister or priest who performs religious services.
