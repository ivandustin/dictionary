Word: λεγομενοις

Etymology: λεγομενοις is the present participle of the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: λεγομενοις is a masculine, plural, dative case form of the present participle of the verb λέγω (lego). In Greek, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action, and the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of the verb.

Description: λεγομενοις can be translated to mean "those who are being said" or "those who are being spoken of". It is often used in Greek literature to refer to characters or groups of people who are being discussed or mentioned in a narrative.
