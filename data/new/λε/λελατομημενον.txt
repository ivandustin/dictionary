Word: λελατομημένον

Etymology:
The word λελατομημένον comes from the Greek verb λατομέω (latomeo), which means "to quarry" or "to cut stones". The word is formed from the perfect passive participle of the verb, which means "having been quarried" or "having been cut".

Grammar:
λελατομημένον is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is in the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, the stone) is the recipient of the action.

Description:
λελατομημένον describes a stone that has been quarried or cut. It implies that the stone has been shaped or prepared for a specific purpose, such as building or sculpture. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe something that has been carefully crafted or refined.
