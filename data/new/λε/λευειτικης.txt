λευειτικης (leveitikēs) is a Greek adjective that means "pertaining to salvation" or "saving". 

Etymology: The word λευειτικης comes from the Greek word λευτήριον (leutērion), which means "ransom" or "redemption". The suffix -ικης (-ikēs) is added to form an adjective meaning "related to" or "pertaining to".

Grammar: λευειτικης is a feminine singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: λευειτικης is a word that is often used in religious contexts to describe something that is related to salvation or redemption. It can be used to describe a person, an action, or an object that is believed to have a saving or redemptive quality.
