Word: λεγοντων

Etymology: λεγοντων is the present participle of the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: λεγοντων is a masculine plural genitive form of the present participle of the verb λέγω (lego). In Greek, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship.

Description: λεγοντων can be translated as "of those who are saying" or "of those who are speaking". It is often used in the New Testament to refer to the words or teachings of Jesus or other speakers. For example, in Matthew 5:2, it is used to describe the crowd that gathered to hear Jesus speak: "And he opened his mouth and taught them, saying (λεγοντων)".
