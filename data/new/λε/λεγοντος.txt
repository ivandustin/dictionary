Word: λεγοντος

Etymology: λεγοντος is the genitive form of the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect" or "to gather".

Grammar: λεγοντος is a participle in the present active voice, masculine gender, and genitive case. It is derived from the verb λέγω (lego) and functions as an adjective, describing the subject of the sentence.

Description: λεγοντος is used to describe someone who is currently speaking or saying something. It can also be used to describe something that is being said or spoken about. In English, it can be translated as "speaking" or "saying". For example, in the sentence "Ο λόγος του λεγοντος ήταν πολύ σημαντικός" (O logos tou legontos itan poly simantikos), which means "The words of the speaker were very important", λεγοντος describes the subject of the sentence, "the speaker".
