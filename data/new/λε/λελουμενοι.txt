Word: λελουμενοι

Etymology: λελουμενοι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λούω (louo), which means "to wash." The prefix λε- (le-) indicates a completed action, so λελουμενοι (leloumenoi) means "having been washed."

Grammar: λελουμενοι is a participle in the perfect tense, passive voice, and masculine gender. It is used to describe a group of people who have been washed or cleansed.

Description: λελουμενοι is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in 1 Corinthians 6:11. In this passage, the apostle Paul is addressing the Corinthian church and reminding them that they have been washed, sanctified, and justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ. The word λελουμενοι is used to emphasize the idea of being cleansed or purified.
