The word "λειπει" (leipei) is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense and the third person singular form. 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "λείπω" (leipō), which means "to leave" or "to be missing". 

Grammar: "Λειπει" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation group. It is conjugated as follows: 

- Λείπω (leipō) - I leave 
- Λείπεις (leipeis) - you leave 
- Λείπει (leipei) - he/she/it leaves 
- Λείπουμε (leipoume) - we leave 
- Λείπετε (leipete) - you (plural) leave 
- Λείπουν (leipoun) - they leave 

Description: The word "λειπει" can be translated to English as "he/she/it is missing" or "he/she/it is absent". It is often used to describe the absence of a person or an object. For example, "Ο Πέτρος λείπει από το σπίτι" (O Petros leipei apo to spiti) means "Peter is missing from the house".
