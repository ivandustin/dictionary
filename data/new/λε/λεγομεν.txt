Word: λεγομεν (legomen)

Etymology: λεγομεν comes from the Greek verb λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak." The suffix -μεν (-men) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to an ongoing action.

Grammar: λεγομεν is a present participle in the active voice, third person plural form. It is often translated as "they are saying" or "they are speaking." In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: λεγομεν is a common word in Greek, used in both spoken and written language. It is often used to introduce reported speech or to describe ongoing actions. For example, in the sentence "Οι φίλοι μου λεγομεν ότι θα έρθουν αύριο" (My friends are saying that they will come tomorrow), λεγομεν introduces the reported speech. In the sentence "Οι μαθητές λεγομεν το μάθημα" (The students are studying the lesson), λεγομεν describes an ongoing action.
