Word: λελυσαι

Etymology: λελυσαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λυω (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to release". The prefix λε- (le-) indicates that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar: λελυσαι is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in the active voice. It is translated as "you have loosened" or "you have released". The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: λυω (I loosen), λυεις (you loosen), λυει (he/she/it loosens), λυομεν (we loosen), λυετε (you all loosen), λυουσι(they loosen)
- Aorist tense: ελυσα (I loosened), λελυκα (I have loosened), ελυσας (you loosened), λελυκας (you have loosened), ελυσε (he/she/it loosened), λελυκε (he/she/it has loosened), ελυσαμεν (we loosened), λελυκαμεν (we have loosened), ελυσατε (you all loosened), λελυκατε (you all have loosened), ελυσαν (they loosened), λελυκασι (they have loosened)

Description: λελυσαι is a verb that describes an action of loosening or releasing something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as untying a knot, releasing a prisoner, or letting go of something. The aorist tense indicates that the action has been completed in the past, and the second person singular form indicates that the action was performed by the person being addressed.
