Word: σεβομενη

Etymology:
The word σεβομενη (sevomeni) is the present participle of the Greek verb σέβομαι (sevomai), which means "to show respect" or "to revere." The verb comes from the noun σέβας (sevas), which means "reverence" or "awe."

Grammar:
σεβομενη is a feminine singular form of the present participle in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence.

Description:
σεβομενη can be translated as "respecting" or "showing respect." It is often used to describe a person who is showing respect to someone or something. For example, "Η σεβομενη κυρία μίλησε με σεβασμό στον διευθυντή" (The respectful lady spoke with respect to the director).
