Word: σεληνη

Etymology:
The word σεληνη (selene) comes from the Ancient Greek word σελάννα (selanna), which means "moon". In Greek mythology, Selene was the goddess of the moon.

Grammar:
σεληνη is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
σεληνη refers to the moon, the natural satellite of the Earth. In Greek mythology, Selene was often depicted as a beautiful woman with a crescent moon on her forehead, riding a chariot pulled by two horses. The moon has been an important symbol in many cultures throughout history, representing femininity, fertility, and the passage of time. In modern times, the moon is also of scientific interest, as it affects tides, animal behavior, and even human mood.
