Word: σεβαστόν

Etymology:
The word σεβαστόν (sebaston) comes from the Greek verb σέβομαι (sevomai), which means "to show respect" or "to revere." The suffix -τόν (-ton) is a neuter adjective ending, which means "worthy of respect" or "venerable."

Grammar:
σεβαστόν (sebaston) is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
σεβαστόν (sebaston) is an adjective that describes something or someone as worthy of respect or veneration. It can be used to describe a person, a place, an object, or an idea. In ancient Greece, it was often used to describe gods, emperors, and other important figures. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to describe someone or something that is highly respected or esteemed.
