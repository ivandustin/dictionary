The word "σεμεειν" (semein) is a Greek verb that means "to signify" or "to indicate". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "semeion" which means "sign" or "mark". 

Grammar: "σεμεειν" is a first-conjugation verb in Greek, meaning that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in "-ω" (omega). In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows: 

- Σημαίνω (semaino) - I signify 
- Σημαίνεις (semaineis) - You signify 
- Σημαίνει (semainei) - He/she/it signifies 
- Σημαίνουμε (semainoume) - We signify 
- Σημαίνετε (semainete) - You all signify 
- Σημαίνουν (semainoun) - They signify 

Description: "σεμεειν" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to indicate or signify something. It can be used to express a wide range of meanings, from indicating a physical sign or symbol, to conveying a deeper meaning or significance.
