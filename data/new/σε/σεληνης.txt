Word: σεληνης

Etymology:
The word σεληνης (selēnēs) is derived from the Greek word σελήνη (selḗnē), which means "moon". In Greek mythology, Selene was the goddess of the moon.

Grammar:
σεληνης is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive form of σελήνη (selḗnē), which means "moon".

Description:
σεληνης is a word that refers to the moon in Greek. It is often used in literature and mythology to describe the moon and its various phases. In Greek mythology, Selene was often depicted as a beautiful goddess who rode a chariot across the sky, pulling the moon behind her. The word σεληνης is still used in modern Greek to refer to the moon.
