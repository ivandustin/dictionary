Word: ρηγνυνται

Etymology: ρηγνυνται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "ρήγνυμι" (rēgnymi), which means "to break, shatter, or destroy." The verb is in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.

Grammar: ρηγνυνται is a third-person plural verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, voice, and mood. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: ρηγνυνται means "they are being broken/shattered/destroyed" in English. The verb can be used to describe physical objects that are being broken or destroyed, or it can be used metaphorically to describe relationships, institutions, or other intangible things that are falling apart.
