In Greek, ρηγμα is a neuter noun. Its plural form is ρήγματα (rigmata). It is often used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural to refer to multiple fractures or breaks.

ρηγμα (rigma) refers to a fracture or a break in a solid object, such as a rock or a bone. It can also refer to a fault line in the earth's crust.
