Word: ρηξωσιν

Etymology: ρηξωσιν is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form of the word ρήγνυμι (rēgnymi), which means "to break, shatter, or destroy." The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁reg- which means "to break, tear."

Grammar: ρηξωσιν is a future tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: ρηξωσιν is a verb that describes an action of breaking, shattering, or destroying that will happen in the future and will be done by a group of people or things. It is a strong and forceful word that conveys a sense of power and destruction.
