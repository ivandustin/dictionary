Word: ρημάτων

Etymology: ρήμα (ríma) is a Greek word meaning "verb". The suffix -των (-ton) is a plural ending, so ρημάτων (rímaton) means "verbs" in Greek.

Grammar: ρημάτων is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. It is used to refer to multiple verbs.

Description: ρημάτων is a word that is commonly used in Greek grammar to refer to verbs. It is often used in language textbooks and courses to teach students about verb conjugation and usage. In Greek, verbs are an important part of speech and are used to express actions, states, and events. The study of verbs is essential for anyone who wants to learn the Greek language.
