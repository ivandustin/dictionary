Word: παροντος

Etymology: παροντος is a present participle of the Greek verb πάρειμι (pareimi), which means "to be present" or "to be in existence". The word is derived from the prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb εἰμί (eimi), meaning "to be".

Grammar: παροντος is a masculine singular genitive form of the present participle πάρων (paron), which means "being present" or "existing". In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action or state of being. The genitive case indicates possession or relationship to another noun.

Description: παροντος can be translated as "of the present" or "of the existing". It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to refer to the present moment or the current state of existence. In grammar, it can be used as an attributive adjective to modify a noun, such as "the present situation" or "the existing conditions".
