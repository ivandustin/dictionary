Word: παιδεία

Etymology: The word παιδεία (paideia) comes from the Greek root παις (pais), meaning "child." It originally referred to the education and upbringing of children in ancient Greece.

Grammar: παιδεία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is παιδείες (paideies).

Description: παιδεία is a term that encompasses the education, training, and cultural development of individuals. In ancient Greece, it referred to the education of young boys in areas such as music, literature, and physical training. Today, it is often used to refer to the broader concept of education and learning, including formal schooling, personal development, and cultural enrichment. The term is also associated with the idea of cultivating virtues such as wisdom, morality, and civic responsibility.
