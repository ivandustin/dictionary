Word: παρηλθον

Etymology:
The word παρηλθον (parēlthon) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, παρηλθον can be translated as "I passed by" or "I went past".

Grammar:
παρηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb παρέρχομαι (parerchomai), which means "to pass by" or "to go past". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any indication of the duration or repetition of the action.

Description:
παρηλθον is a Greek verb that describes the action of passing by or going past something or someone. It is often used in a literal sense, such as when describing someone walking past a building or a person passing by another person on the street. However, it can also be used in a figurative sense, such as when describing the passing of time or the passing of an opportunity.
