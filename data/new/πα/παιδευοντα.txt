Word: παιδευοντα

Etymology: παιδεύω (paideuō) is a Greek verb meaning "to educate" or "to train". The word παιδευοντα (paideuonta) is the present participle form of the verb, meaning "educating" or "training".

Grammar: παιδευοντα is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and accusative case. It is modifying a noun or pronoun as a descriptive phrase.

Description: παιδευοντα can be translated as "educating" or "training" in English. It is often used in the context of teaching or instructing someone. For example, "Ο δάσκαλος παιδεύει τους μαθητές του" (O daskalos paideuei tous mathites tou) means "The teacher is educating his students".
