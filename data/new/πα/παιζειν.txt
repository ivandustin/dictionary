Word: παιζειν

Etymology: The word παιζειν (paizein) comes from the ancient Greek verb παίζω (paizō), which means "to play" or "to sport". It is derived from the noun παῖς (pais), which means "child" or "boy".

Grammar: παιζειν is the present active infinitive form of the verb παίζω (paizō). In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on the tense, mood, voice, and person of the subject. The present tense indicates an action that is currently happening, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The infinitive form is used to express the idea of the verb without specifying a subject or tense.

Description: παιζειν is a versatile verb that can be used to describe a wide range of playful activities, such as playing games, sports, or musical instruments. It can also be used in a figurative sense to describe someone who is "playing" with an idea or concept. In modern Greek, the verb is often used in the context of playing video games or other electronic games.
