Word: παροικον

Etymology:
The word παροικον (paroikon) is derived from the Greek words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and οἶκος (oikos) meaning "house" or "dwelling". It is a compound word that means "dwelling beside" or "sojourner".

Grammar:
In Greek, παροικον is a neuter noun in the singular form. Its nominative case is παροικον and its genitive case is παροικου.

Description:
παροικον is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to foreigners or strangers who reside in a foreign land. It is often translated as "sojourner" or "alien". In a broader sense, it can also refer to anyone who is living away from their homeland or place of origin. The term is used to emphasize the temporary nature of their stay and their lack of permanent citizenship in the land where they reside.
