Word: παρθενών

Etymology:
The word παρθενών (parthenón) is derived from the Greek word παρθένος (parthénos), which means "virgin" or "maiden". The Parthenon is a temple dedicated to the goddess Athena, who was considered a virgin goddess.

Grammar:
παρθενών is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender, and παρθενών is a masculine noun. It is also in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun.

Description:
παρθενών refers specifically to the Parthenon temple located on the Acropolis in Athens, Greece. It was built in the 5th century BCE and is considered one of the greatest examples of ancient Greek architecture. The temple was dedicated to the goddess Athena, who was the patron goddess of Athens. The Parthenon is known for its impressive size, intricate design, and use of the Doric order of architecture. It has been a symbol of Greek culture and civilization for centuries and is now a popular tourist attraction.
