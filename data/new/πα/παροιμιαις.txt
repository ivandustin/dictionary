Word: παροιμιαίς

Etymology: The word παροιμία (paroimía) comes from the Greek words παρά (pará), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and οἶμος (oîmos), meaning "way" or "path." Therefore, παροιμία means "a saying that runs alongside the main path of speech."

Grammar: παροιμιαίς is the plural form of παροιμία (paroimía) in the dative case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: παροιμιαίς refers to "proverbs" or "sayings" in Greek. These are short, pithy statements that express a general truth or piece of advice. Proverbs are often used to convey wisdom or to teach a moral lesson. In Greek literature, proverbs are found in a variety of genres, including poetry, drama, and philosophy.
