Word: παρειμένας

Etymology:
The word παρειμένας is a Greek word that comes from the verb παραίνεω (paraineo), which means "to exhort" or "to encourage". The word is formed by adding the suffix -μένος (-menos) to the verb stem παραιν- (parain-).

Grammar:
παρειμένας is a masculine noun in the nominative case, singular form. It is the participle form of the verb παραίνεω (paraineo), which means "encouraging" or "exhorting". The word can be used as an adjective to describe a person who is encouraging or exhorting others.

Description:
παρειμένας is a word that describes someone who is actively encouraging or exhorting others. It can be used to describe a person who is motivating others to take action or to do something that they might not otherwise do.
