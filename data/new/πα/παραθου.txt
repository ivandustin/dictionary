The word "παραθου" (parathou) is a Greek verb that means "to place beside" or "to set aside." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "παρα-" (para-), which means "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set."

Grammar: "παραθου" is a second-person singular imperative verb in the present tense. It is often used in commands or instructions, such as "set aside" or "put aside."

Description: The word "παραθου" is commonly used in everyday Greek language, especially in cooking and food preparation, where it is used to instruct someone to set aside a certain ingredient or dish. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as setting aside time for a specific task or setting aside one's personal feelings in order to make an objective decision.
