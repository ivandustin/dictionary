Word: παρρησιαν

Etymology: The word παρρησιαν comes from the Greek word παρρησία (parrhēsía), which means "boldness, frankness, outspokenness". The suffix -αν (-an) is added to form the accusative case.

Grammar: παρρησιαν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: παρρησιαν refers to the state or quality of being bold, frank, or outspoken. It can also refer to an instance of speaking boldly or openly. The word is often used in the context of politics or public speaking, where the ability to speak one's mind freely is highly valued.
