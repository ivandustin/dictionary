Word: παρακαλούντες

Etymology: The word παρακαλούντες comes from the Greek verb παρακαλώ (parakaló), which means "to call to one's side" or "to appeal to." The suffix -ούντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently doing the action of calling or appealing.

Grammar: παρακαλούντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe people who are currently calling or appealing to someone.

Description: παρακαλούντες can be translated to English as "those who are calling/appealing." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to people who are praying or making requests to a higher power. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe people who are making requests or appeals to others.
