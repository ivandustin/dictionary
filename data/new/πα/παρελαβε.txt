Word: παρελαβε (parelave)

Etymology:
The word παρελαβε (parelave) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "alongside" and the verb λαμβανω (lambano) meaning "to take" or "to receive". Therefore, παρελαβε (parelave) means "to receive alongside" or "to take in addition to".

Grammar:
παρελαβε (parelave) is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb παραλαμβανω (paralambano) which means "to take along with" or "to receive".

παρελαβε (parelave) is a verb that is used to describe the act of receiving or taking something in addition to what is already present.
