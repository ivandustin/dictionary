Word: παραδεδωκοσι

Etymology: The word παραδεδωκοσι is a conjugated form of the Greek verb παραδίδωμι (paradídōmi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver." It is formed by adding the suffix -κοσι (-kosi) to the stem παραδεδω (paradedō).

Grammar: παραδεδωκοσι is the third person plural, perfect active indicative form of the verb παραδίδωμι. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: The word παραδεδωκοσι can be translated as "they have handed over" or "they have delivered." It is often used in the context of betrayal or surrender, as in the phrase "they have handed over the city to the enemy." The word has a sense of finality and irrevocability, as the action of handing over is complete and cannot be undone.
