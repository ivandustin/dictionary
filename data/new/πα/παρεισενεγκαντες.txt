Word: παρεισενεγκαντες

Etymology:
The word παρεισενεγκαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of three words: παρά (para), εἰσέρχομαι (eiserchomai), and φέρω (phero). The prefix παρά (para) means "beside" or "near," εἰσέρχομαι (eiserchomai) means "to enter," and φέρω (phero) means "to carry." Therefore, the word παρεισενεγκαντες can be translated as "to carry in beside" or "to bring in."

Grammar:
παρεισενεγκαντες is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. The aorist tense indicates that the action happened in the past, and the active voice means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The participle mood is used to describe the action of the verb in a more general sense.

The verse reads, "and with his wife's knowledge he kept back some of the proceeds, and brought only a part of it and laid it at the apostles' feet."
