Word: παρακαλείτε

Etymology: παρακαλείτε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word παρακαλέω (parakaleo), which means "to call to one's side" or "to appeal to." It is formed by combining the prefix παρα- (para-), which means "beside" or "alongside," and the verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call."

Grammar: παρακαλείτε is the second person plural form of the present tense of the verb παρακαλέω. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all are appealing to" or "you all are calling to one's side."

Description: παρακαλείτε is a formal and polite way of asking a group of people to do something. It is often used in official or professional settings, such as in business meetings or in legal proceedings. It can also be used in everyday conversation to make a request in a respectful manner.
