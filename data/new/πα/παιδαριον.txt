Word: παιδαριον

Etymology:
The word παιδαριον (paidarion) is derived from the Greek word παῖς (pais), which means "child" or "boy". The suffix -αριον (-arion) is a diminutive suffix, which means "little" or "small". Therefore, παιδαριον (paidarion) can be translated as "little child" or "little boy".

Grammar:
In Greek, παιδαριον (paidarion) is a neuter noun. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: το παιδαριον (to paidarion) - the little child/boy
Genitive: του παιδαριου (tou paidariou) - of the little child/boy
Accusative: το παιδαριον (to paidarion) - the little child/boy
Vocative: παιδαριον (paidarion) - little child/boy

Plural:
Nominative: τα παιδαρια (ta paidaria) - the little children/boys
Genitive: των παιδαριων (ton paidariwn) - of the little children/boys
Accusative: τα παιδαρια (ta paidaria) - the little children/boys
Vocative: παιδαρια (paidaria) - little children/boys

Description:
παιδαριον (paidarion) is a term of endearment used to refer to a young child or boy. It is often used in a playful or affectionate manner. The word can also be used to describe something small or cute, similar to the English word "little".
