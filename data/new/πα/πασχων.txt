Word: πασχων

Etymology:
The word πασχων (paschon) is the present participle of the Greek verb πάσχω (pascho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." The root of the word is πάθος (pathos), which means "suffering" or "passion."

Grammar:
πασχων is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb πάσχω, which is a second conjugation verb in the present tense. The present participle is formed by adding the suffix -ων to the stem of the verb.

Description:
πασχων is used to describe someone who is currently suffering or experiencing something. It can also be used to describe someone who is enduring a difficult situation or hardship. In Greek literature, the word is often used in the context of religious or philosophical discussions, where suffering is seen as a necessary part of the human experience.
