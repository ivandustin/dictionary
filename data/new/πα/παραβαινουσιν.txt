The word παραβαινουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word παραβαίνω (parabaínō), which means "to transgress" or "to go beyond." The word is formed by adding the suffix -ουσιν (-ousin), which is the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb.

παραβαινουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. It is an active voice verb, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is also indicative, which means that it is a statement of fact.

The word παραβαινουσιν is used to describe a group of people who are transgressing or going beyond a certain limit or boundary.
