Word: παρεκαλεσας

Etymology:
The word παρεκαλεσας (parekalesas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call." The prefix παρα (para) means "beside" or "near," so the word παρεκαλεσας (parekalesas) can be translated as "to call near" or "to call beside."

Grammar:
παρεκαλεσας (parekalesas) is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is the past tense form of the verb παρακαλέω (parakaleo), which means "to call to one's side" or "to encourage." In this form, it means "you called" or "you encouraged."

Description:
παρεκαλεσας (parekalesas) is a verb that implies a personal and direct action of calling or encouraging someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as calling someone to come closer, calling someone to help, or encouraging someone to do something.
