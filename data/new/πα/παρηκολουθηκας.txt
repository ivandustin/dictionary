Word: παρηκολουθηκας

Etymology: The word παρηκολουθηκας is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix παρα- (meaning "beside" or "alongside") and the verb εκολούθησα (meaning "I followed"). Therefore, the word παρηκολουθηκας means "you followed alongside" or "you accompanied".

Grammar: παρηκολουθηκας is a second-person singular verb in the aorist tense, active voice, indicative mood. It is derived from the verb κολουθέω (kolouthéo), which means "to follow". The prefix παρα- adds the meaning of "alongside" or "beside".

Description: παρηκολουθηκας is a Greek verb that describes the act of following someone or something alongside or beside them. It can be used in a literal sense, such as following someone on a walk, or in a figurative sense, such as following someone's ideas or beliefs. The word is commonly used in religious contexts, such as following the teachings of a particular faith.
