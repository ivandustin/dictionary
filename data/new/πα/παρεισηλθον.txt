Etymology:
The word παρεισηλθον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of three words: παρά (para), εἰς (eis), and ἔρχομαι (erchomai). The prefix παρά means "beside" or "alongside," εἰς means "into," and ἔρχομαι means "to come." Therefore, the word παρεισηλθον means "to come in beside or alongside."

παρεισηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb παρεισέρχομαι (pareiserchomai), which means "to enter in beside or alongside." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past. The word is in the first person singular form, which means "I entered in beside or alongside."

Description:
παρεισηλθον is a word that describes an action of entering in beside or alongside something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as entering a room or a conversation.
