Word: παραπτωμάτων

Etymology:
The word παραπτωμάτων is derived from the Greek word παράπτωμα (paraptoma), which means "a falling aside" or "a lapse." The suffix -ων (-on) is added to the end of the word to indicate that it is plural.

Grammar:
παραπτωμάτων is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "sins" or "transgressions." It is often used in a religious or moral context.

Description:
παραπτωμάτων refers to a wrongdoing or a moral failing. It is often used in the context of Christianity to refer to sins or transgressions against God. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the sins of humanity and the need for redemption through Jesus Christ.
