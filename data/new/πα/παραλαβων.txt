Word: παραλαβων

Etymology:
The word παραλαβων comes from the Greek verb παραλαμβάνω (paralambanō), which means "to take along, to receive, to accept." It is formed from the prefix παρα- (para-), which means "beside" or "alongside," and the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take."

Grammar:
παραλαβων is the present participle form of the verb παραλαμβάνω (paralambanō). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action, and in this case, it means "taking along" or "receiving."

Description:
παραλαβων can be translated as "taking along" or "receiving." It is often used in the context of receiving or accepting something from someone else. In the New Testament, it is used to describe Jesus "taking along" or "receiving" his disciples, as well as his followers receiving his teachings.
