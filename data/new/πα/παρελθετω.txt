Word: παρελθετω

Etymology:
The word παρελθετω comes from the Greek verb παρέρχομαι (parerchomai), which means "to pass by" or "to go past". It is formed from the prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go".

Grammar:
παρελθετω is a verb in the present imperative active form. It is the third person singular form of the verb παρέρχομαι (parerchomai), which means "let him/her/it pass by" or "let him/her/it go past". The present imperative active form is used to give commands or instructions in the present tense.

Description:
παρελθετω is a Greek verb that is used to give a command or instruction to let something pass by or go past. It is often used in a figurative sense, such as letting go of a past mistake or moving on from a difficult situation.
