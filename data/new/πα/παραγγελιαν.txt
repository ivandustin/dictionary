Word: παραγγελιαν

Etymology: The word παραγγελιαν comes from the Greek verb παραγγέλλω (parangello), which means "to order" or "to command". The suffix -αν (-an) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form nouns from verbs.

Grammar: παραγγελιαν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In this case, it is the object of the verb παραγγέλλω (parangello), meaning "to order" or "to command".

Description: παραγγελιαν refers to an order or a command given to someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as placing an order at a restaurant or giving instructions to an employee. The word emphasizes the authoritative nature of the order or command, implying that it must be followed without question.
