Word: παραβολευσαμενος

Etymology: The word παραβολευσαμενος is derived from the Greek verb παραβολεύω (paraboleuo), which means "to compare" or "to illustrate with a parable". The suffix -σαμενος (-samenos) is a participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and passive voice.

Grammar: παραβολευσαμενος is a past participle in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the suffix -σαμενος to the stem of the verb παραβολεύω. The word is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: The word παραβολευσαμενος describes someone who has been compared or illustrated with a parable. It can also be translated as "having been parabolized" or "having been made into a parable". The word is often used in a religious context, referring to the parables told by Jesus in the New Testament.
