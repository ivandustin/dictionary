Word: παγίδα

Etymology:
The word παγίδα (pagida) comes from the ancient Greek word παγίς (pagis), which means "trap" or "snare". It is derived from the verb πήγνυμι (pēgnymi), which means "to fix" or "to fasten".

Grammar:
παγίδα is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is παγίδες (pagides). In the nominative case, it is spelled παγίδα, but in the accusative case, it is spelled παγίδαν (pagidan).

Description:
παγίδα refers to a device or mechanism used to trap or ensnare something, such as an animal or a person. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or circumstance that is difficult to escape from. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday language.
