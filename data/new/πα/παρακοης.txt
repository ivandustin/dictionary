Word: παρακοής

Etymology: The word παρακοής comes from the Greek root words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and κούω (kouo) meaning "to hear". Together, they form the word παρακοής which means "eavesdropping" or "listening in".

Grammar: παρακοής is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns. For example, "το βιβλίο της παρακοής" (to vivlio tis parakois) means "the book of eavesdropping".

Description: παρακοής refers to the act of secretly listening in on a conversation or spying on someone's private communication. It can also refer to the information obtained through eavesdropping. The word has a negative connotation and is often associated with unethical or illegal behavior.
