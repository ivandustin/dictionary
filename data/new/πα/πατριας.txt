Word: πατριας

Etymology: The word πατριας comes from the Greek word πατρίς (patris), which means "fatherland" or "homeland". It is derived from the word πατήρ (pater), which means "father".

Grammar: πατριας is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the first declension and has the genitive form πατρίδος (patridos).

Description: πατριας refers to one's homeland or native country. It is often used in a patriotic or nationalistic sense, and can also refer to the land of one's ancestors. In Greek mythology, the concept of πατριας was closely tied to the idea of the polis, or city-state, and the duty of citizens to defend and protect their homeland.
