Word: πατρωιοις

Etymology: The word πατρωιοις comes from the Greek word πατρῷος (patrōios), which means "of one's father, paternal". It is derived from the word πατήρ (patēr), which means "father".

Grammar: πατρωιοις is a plural dative form of the adjective πατρῷος (patrōios). In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: πατρωιοις is an adjective that describes something as being related to or belonging to one's father or paternal ancestors. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as customs, traditions, or property that has been passed down through the male line of a family.
