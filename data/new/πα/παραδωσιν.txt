Word: παραδωσιν

Etymology: The word παραδωσιν comes from the Greek verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver." The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a variant of the suffix -σις (-sis), which is used to form abstract nouns.

Grammar: παραδωσιν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender.

Description: παραδωσιν refers to the act of handing over or delivering something, often used in a legal or contractual context. It can also refer to a tradition or custom that is passed down from generation to generation. In Christian theology, it is used to refer to the handing down of the teachings and practices of the faith from the apostles to the present day.
