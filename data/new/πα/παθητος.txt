Word: παθητός

Etymology:
The word παθητός comes from the Greek root word πάθος (pathos), which means "suffering" or "passion". The suffix -τός (-tos) is a common adjectival suffix in Greek, which means "having the quality of" or "able to be".

Grammar:
παθητός is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined in various cases to agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
παθητός is an adjective that describes something or someone that is capable of experiencing or undergoing suffering or passion. It can also be used to describe something that is affected by an external force or influence. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a person who is sensitive or emotional.
