Word: παυσάτω (pau-sa-toh)

Etymology: παυσάτω comes from the Greek verb παύω (pauo), which means "to stop" or "to cease". The suffix -σάτω (-sato) is the third person singular imperative form, meaning "let him/her/it stop".

Grammar: παυσάτω is a verb in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests. It is in the third person singular form, meaning it is a command directed at one person or thing.

Description: παυσάτω is a command to stop or cease something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as telling someone to stop talking, stop moving, or stop doing something specific. It is a strong and direct command, often used in situations where immediate action is required.
