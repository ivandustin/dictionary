Word: παραγγέλλων (parangelon)

Etymology: The word παραγγέλλων comes from the Greek verb παραγγέλλω (parangellō), which means "to order" or "to command". It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb ἀγγέλλω (angellō), meaning "to announce" or "to report".

Grammar: παραγγέλλων is the present participle of the verb παραγγέλλω. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The present participle is formed by adding the suffix -ων (-ōn) to the present stem of the verb.

Description: παραγγέλλων can be translated as "ordering" or "commanding". It refers to someone who is currently giving orders or commands. In context, it could be used to describe a military commander, a boss giving instructions to employees, or a parent directing their children.
