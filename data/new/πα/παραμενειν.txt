Word: παραμενειν

Etymology:
The word παραμενειν comes from the Greek roots παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and μένειν (menein) meaning "to remain" or "to stay". Therefore, the word παραμενειν means "to stay beside" or "to remain near".

Grammar:
παραμενειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν to the stem παραμεν-.

Description:
παραμενειν is a Greek verb that means "to stay beside" or "to remain near". It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone who stays by the side of a sick friend or family member, or someone who remains near a loved one during a difficult time. It can also be used in a more literal sense, such as describing an object that remains in a certain place or position.
