Word: παγίδος

Etymology:
The word παγίδος (pagidos) is derived from the Greek word παγίς (pagis), which means "trap" or "snare". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *pag-, which means "to trap" or "to catch".

Grammar:
παγίδος is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. The singular form of the word is παγίς (pagis), while the plural form is παγίδες (pagides).

Description:
παγίδος refers to a trap or snare that is used to catch animals. It can be made of various materials, such as wood, metal, or rope, and is designed to capture the prey by holding it in place or causing it to become entangled. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or circumstance that is difficult to escape from or overcome.
