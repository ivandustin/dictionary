Word: πατριάρχου

Etymology: The word πατριάρχου comes from the Greek words πατήρ (patēr), meaning "father," and ἄρχων (archōn), meaning "ruler" or "leader." It is a compound word that refers to the male head of a family or tribe.

Grammar: πατριάρχου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive form of the word πατριάρχης (patriarchēs), which means "patriarch" or "father figure."

Description: πατριάρχου is a term used in Greek Orthodox Christianity to refer to the highest-ranking bishop in a particular region or country. The patriarch is responsible for overseeing the spiritual and administrative affairs of the church in his jurisdiction. In addition to his religious duties, the patriarch also has a role in the political and social life of his community.
