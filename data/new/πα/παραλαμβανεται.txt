Word: παραλαμβάνεται

Etymology: The word παραλαμβάνεται comes from the Greek verb παραλαμβάνω (paralamvano), which means "to receive" or "to take". It is formed by combining the prefix παρα- (para-), which means "beside" or "alongside", and the verb λαμβάνω (lamvano), which means "to take".

Grammar: παραλαμβάνεται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is the third person singular form of the verb παραλαμβάνω. In English, it can be translated as "is received" or "is taken".

Description: παραλαμβάνεται is a verb that describes the action of receiving or taking something. It is often used in formal or official contexts, such as in legal documents or business transactions. The passive voice form of the verb emphasizes that the subject is the recipient of the action, rather than the one performing the action.
