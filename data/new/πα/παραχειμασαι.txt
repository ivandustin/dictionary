Word: παραχειμασαι

Etymology: The word παραχειμασαι is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb χειμάζω (cheimazo) meaning "to winter" or "to spend the winter". Therefore, παραχειμασαι means "to spend the winter near or beside something".

Grammar: παραχειμασαι is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb χειμάζω (cheimazo) in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The second person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is "you".

Description: παραχειμασαι is a rare word in Greek and is only found in the New Testament in the Bible (2 Timothy 4:21). In this context, it is used as a personal request from Paul to Timothy to come to him before winter. The word conveys a sense of urgency and importance, as Paul is asking Timothy to come to him before the harsh winter weather sets in.
