Word: παραβάτης

Etymology:
The word παραβάτης (paravates) comes from the Greek verb παραβαίνω (paravaino), which means "to transgress" or "to violate". The suffix -της (-tes) is added to the verb stem to form a noun indicating the agent or doer of the action. Therefore, παραβάτης means "transgressor" or "violator".

Grammar:
παραβάτης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or the predicate nominative, which renames the subject. The plural form of παραβάτης is παραβάτες (paravates).

Description:
παραβάτης is a word that refers to someone who transgresses or violates a law, rule, or moral principle. It can be used in a legal context to describe someone who breaks the law, or in a moral context to describe someone who behaves in an unethical or immoral way. The word has a negative connotation and implies that the person is acting against what is considered right or just.
