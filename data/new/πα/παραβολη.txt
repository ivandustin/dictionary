Word: παραβολή

Etymology: The word παραβολή comes from the Greek word παραβολή (parabolē), which means "comparison" or "analogy". It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb βάλλω (ballō), meaning "to throw".

Grammar: παραβολή is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is παραβολές (parabolēs).

Description: παραβολή is a term used in literature and rhetoric to refer to a figure of speech that involves a comparison between two things, often using the words "like" or "as". It is a common device used in storytelling, where a story or situation is used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson. In the New Testament, Jesus often used parables to teach his followers about the kingdom of God. Some famous examples of parables include "The Good Samaritan" and "The Prodigal Son".
