Word: παρεστιν

Etymology: παρεστιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb πάρειμι (pareimi), which means "to be present" or "to be at hand." It is a combination of the preposition παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "near," and the verb εἰμί (eimi), meaning "to be."

Grammar: παρεστιν is a third-person singular present indicative active form of the verb πάρειμι (pareimi). It is used to indicate that something or someone is present or available.

Description: παρεστιν is a simple yet important Greek verb that is used to express the presence or availability of something or someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to describe the presence of a person at a particular location or the availability of a resource. Its conjugation is relatively straightforward, making it easy to use in conversation or writing.
