Word: παροιμίαν

Etymology: The word παροιμίαν comes from the Greek word παροιμία (paroimía), which means "proverb" or "saying". It is derived from the words παρά (pará), meaning "beside" or "near", and οἶμος (oîmos), meaning "way" or "path".

Grammar: παροιμίαν is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: παροιμίαν refers to a proverb or saying that expresses a general truth or piece of wisdom. In Greek literature, proverbs were often used to convey moral lessons or to provide guidance on how to live a good life. Some famous Greek proverbs include "know thyself" (γνῶθι σεαυτόν) and "actions speak louder than words" (ἔργα δηλοῦσι μᾶλλον ἢ λόγοι). Proverbs are still commonly used in modern Greek language and culture.
