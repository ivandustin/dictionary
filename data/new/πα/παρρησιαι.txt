Word: παρρησιαι

Etymology: The word παρρησιαι (parrhesiai) comes from the Greek word παν (pan) meaning "all" and ῥησις (rhesi) meaning "speech". Together, they form the word παρρησιαι (parrhesiai) which means "to speak everything" or "to speak freely".

Grammar: παρρησιαι (parrhesiai) is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: παρρησιαι (parrhesiai) is a Greek word that refers to the act of speaking freely or openly, without fear of consequences. It is often used to describe a person who speaks their mind honestly and boldly, even in the face of opposition or danger. In ancient Greece, parrhesia was considered a virtue and was highly valued in democratic societies. Today, the word is still used to describe the act of speaking truthfully and fearlessly, particularly in political or social contexts.
