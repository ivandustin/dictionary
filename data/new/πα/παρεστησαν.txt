Word: παρεστησαν

Etymology: The word παρεστησαν comes from the Greek verb παρίστημι (paristemi), which means "to present" or "to stand beside". It is formed from the prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb ἵστημι (histemi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: παρεστησαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb παρίστημι (paristemi). In this form, it indicates that the action of presenting or standing beside has been completed in the past by a group of people.

Description: παρεστησαν is a Greek verb that means "they presented" or "they stood beside". It is often used in the context of presenting someone or something to an audience or authority figure. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the actions of people who brought others before Jesus or the Jewish authorities.
