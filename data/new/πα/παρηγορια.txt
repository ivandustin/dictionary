Word: παρηγορια

Etymology: The word παρηγορια (parēgoria) comes from the Greek words παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "near," and ἠγορά (ēgora), meaning "marketplace" or "assembly." Together, they form the meaning "to speak beside or near someone," which evolved to mean "to console" or "to comfort."

Grammar: In Greek, παρηγορια is a feminine noun. Its plural form is παρηγορίες (parēgories).

Description: παρηγορια refers to the act of comforting or consoling someone who is experiencing grief, sadness, or distress. It can also refer to the words or actions used to provide comfort. The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context, as a form of divine comfort or solace.
