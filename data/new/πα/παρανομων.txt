Word: παρανομων

Etymology: The word παρανομων comes from the Greek prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and the Greek noun νόμος (nomos), meaning "law." Together, they form the word παρανομων, which means "lawless" or "transgressing the law."

Grammar: In Greek, παρανομων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is παρανόμοι (paranómoi).

Description: παρανομων is a word that describes someone who breaks the law or acts in a lawless manner. It can refer to someone who violates a specific law or someone who habitually disregards the law. The word can also be used to describe actions or behaviors that are illegal or against the law. In modern Greek, the word is often used to describe someone who is a criminal or a lawbreaker.
