Word: παραβασις

Etymology: παραβασις comes from the Greek word παράβασις (parábasis), which means "stepping over" or "transgression". It is derived from the verb παραβαίνω (parabaínō), which means "to step over" or "to transgress".

Grammar: παραβασις is a feminine noun in Greek. Its genitive form is παραβάσεως (parabáseōs) and its accusative form is παράβασιν (parábasis).

Description: παραβασις refers to a violation or breach of a law, rule, or agreement. It can also refer to a deviation from the norm or expected behavior. In literature, παραβασις is used to describe a type of Greek comedy in which the chorus steps out of character and addresses the audience directly.
