Word: παραδιδοναι

Etymology:
The word παραδιδοναι (paradidonai) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "alongside" and the verb διδοναι (didonai) meaning "to give". Therefore, παραδιδοναι can be translated as "to give alongside" or "to hand over".

Grammar:
παραδιδοναι is a present tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: παραδιδω (paradido)
- Plural: παραδιδομεν (paradidomen)

Description:
παραδιδοναι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can mean "to deliver", "to hand over", "to betray", "to surrender", or "to transmit". In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the act of delivering someone over to authorities or enemies, as in the case of Judas Iscariot betraying Jesus to the Roman authorities. In other contexts, it can refer to the act of passing on knowledge or information, as in the case of a teacher imparting wisdom to a student.
