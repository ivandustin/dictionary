Word: παραγενώμαι

Etymology: The word παραγενώμαι comes from the Greek prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "near," and the verb γίνομαι (ginomai), meaning "to become" or "to happen." Therefore, παραγενώμαι can be translated as "to come near" or "to arrive."

Grammar: παραγενώμαι is a reflexive verb in the Greek language, which means that the subject of the verb is also the object. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

Παραγίνομαι (paragínomai) - I come near
Παραγίνεσαι (paragínesai) - You come near
Παραγίνεται (paragínetai) - He/she/it comes near
Παραγινόμαστε (paraginómaste) - We come near
Παραγίνεστε (paragíneste) - You (plural) come near
Παραγίνονται (paragínondai) - They come near

Description: παραγενώμαι is a verb that describes the act of arriving or coming near to a place or person. It can also be used to describe the act of appearing or showing up. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is often used to describe the arrival of Jesus or his disciples to a particular location.
