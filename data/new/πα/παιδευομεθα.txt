Word: παιδευομεθα

Etymology:
The word παιδευομεθα (paideuometha) is derived from the Greek verb παιδεύω (paideuo), which means "to educate" or "to train". The suffix -μεθα (-metha) indicates that the verb is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Grammar:
παιδευομεθα is a present tense, indicative mood, middle voice verb in Greek. It is conjugated in the first person plural, which means "we are being educated" or "we are being trained". The verb stem is παιδευ-, and the ending -ομεθα indicates the middle voice.

Description:
παιδευομεθα is a verb that describes the process of being educated or trained. It implies that the subject is actively engaged in the learning process and is receptive to instruction. The middle voice emphasizes that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, which suggests a sense of personal responsibility and agency in the learning process. Overall, παιδευομεθα conveys a sense of ongoing growth and development through education and training.
