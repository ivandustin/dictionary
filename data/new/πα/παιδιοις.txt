Word: παιδιοις

Etymology: παιδιοις is the dative plural form of the Greek word παιδίον (paidíon), which means "little child" or "young child". The word is derived from the Greek word παῖς (pais), which means "child" or "boy".

Grammar: παιδιοις is a dative plural noun in Greek. It is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. In English, it can be translated as "to/for the children".

Description: παιδιοις is a word that refers to young children in Greek. It is often used in the context of education or child-rearing. In ancient Greece, education was highly valued and children were taught from a young age. The word παιδιοις reflects this cultural emphasis on the importance of children and their education.
