Word: παροινον

Etymology:
The word παροινον (paroinon) is derived from the Greek words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and οἶνος (oinos) meaning "wine". Therefore, the word παροινον can be translated as "drinking beside" or "drinking companion".

Grammar:
παροινον is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in the Greek language.

Description:
παροινον is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a drinking companion or someone who drinks with another person. In ancient Greek society, drinking was often a social activity and having a παροινον was considered a sign of friendship and camaraderie. The term is often used in the context of symposia, which were drinking parties held by the ancient Greeks. The παροινον was expected to engage in conversation, recite poetry, and participate in other forms of entertainment during the symposium.
