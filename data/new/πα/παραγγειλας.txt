Word: παραγγειλας

Etymology:
The word παραγγειλας (parangeilas) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and ἀγγέλλω (angello) meaning "to announce" or "to report". Therefore, the literal meaning of παραγγειλας is "to announce near" or "to report beside".

Grammar:
παραγγειλας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb παραγγέλλω (parangello), which means "to order" or "to command". The aorist tense indicates that the action of ordering or commanding was completed in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject (you) performed the action.

Description:
παραγγειλας is a verb that is commonly used in Greek to express the act of ordering or commanding someone to do something. It can also be used to describe the act of reporting or announcing something that is near or beside the speaker. The word is often used in a formal or authoritative context, such as in military or business settings.
