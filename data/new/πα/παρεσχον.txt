Word: παρεσχον

Etymology:
The word παρεσχον (pareschon) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word παρέχω (parecho), which means "to provide" or "to furnish." The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
παρεσχον is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the aorist tense suffix -ον to the stem παρεσχ-, which is derived from the present tense stem παρέχ-. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: παρέχω (parecho) - "I provide"
- Aorist tense: παρεσχον (pareschon) - "he/she/it provided"
- Perfect tense: πεπαρεχωκα (peparechoka) - "I have provided"

Description:
παρεσχον is a verb that means "he/she/it provided" in Greek. It is often used in the context of giving or providing something to someone. For example, "Ο πατέρας μου παρέσχε τη βοήθειά του" (O pateras mou paresche ti voitheia tou) means "My father provided his help." The verb can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as "Η εμπειρία που παρεσχον μου βοήθησε να λύσω το πρόβλημα" (I empeiria pou pareschon mou voithise na lysso to provlima), which means "The experience that I was provided helped me solve the problem."
