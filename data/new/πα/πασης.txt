Word: πασης

Etymology: πασης is a Greek word that comes from the root word πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". The suffix -ης (-es) is a feminine genitive singular ending, indicating possession or relationship to a feminine noun.

Grammar: πασης is a feminine genitive singular form of the adjective πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". It is used to modify a feminine noun and indicate possession or relationship.

Description: πασης is often used in Greek literature and language to describe something that belongs to or is related to a feminine noun. For example, in the phrase "της πασης αρετης" (tis pases aretes), which means "of all virtues", πασης modifies the feminine noun αρετης (aretes), indicating that the virtues being referred to are all feminine in nature.
