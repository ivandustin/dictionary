Word: παροικίας

Etymology: The word παροικίας comes from the Greek word πάροικος (paroikos), which means "resident foreigner" or "sojourner". The suffix -ίας (-ias) is added to form the noun παροικίας, which means "a community of resident foreigners".

Grammar: παροικίας is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word παροικίας can be declined in different cases, such as nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.

Description: παροικίας refers to a community of resident foreigners who live in a particular area or city. In ancient Greece, many cities had παροικίες, which were often made up of merchants, artisans, and other non-citizens who were allowed to live and work in the city.
