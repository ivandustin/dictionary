Word: παραβατην

Etymology:
The word παραβατην comes from the Greek word παραβάτης (parabátēs), which means "transgressor" or "violator". It is derived from the verb παραβαίνω (parabaínō), which means "to transgress" or "to violate".

Grammar:
παραβατην is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
παραβατην refers to a transgressor or violator, someone who breaks the law or goes against established rules or norms. It can be used to describe someone who has committed a crime or violated a social norm. The word has a negative connotation and implies that the person in question has done something wrong or immoral.
