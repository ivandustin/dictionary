Word: παρατιθεμενον

Etymology:
The word παρατιθεμενον comes from the Greek verb παρατιθημι (paratithemi), which means "to place beside" or "to set before". The word is derived from the prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb τιθημι (tithemi), meaning "to place" or "to set".

παρατιθεμενον is a Greek participle in the present passive voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb παρατιθημι (paratithemi), which means "to place beside" or "to set before". The participle form indicates that the action of placing or setting is ongoing or continuous.

παρατιθεμενον can be translated to English as "being placed beside" or "being set before". It is often used in Greek literature to describe something that is being presented or offered to someone.
