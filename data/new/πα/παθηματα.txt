Word: παθήματα

Etymology: The word παθήματα comes from the Greek verb πάσχω (paskho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience". The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a plural noun ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple instances of suffering or experiences.

Grammar: παθήματα is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is the nominative form of the word, which means it is used as the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive παθημάτων (pathimaton) or the accusative παθήματα (pathimata).

Description: παθήματα refers to experiences of suffering or hardship. It can be used to describe physical or emotional pain, as well as difficult or challenging situations. The word is often used in a philosophical or spiritual context, to refer to the trials and tribulations that one must endure in order to grow and develop as a person.
