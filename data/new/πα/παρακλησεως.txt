Word: παρακλησεως

Etymology: The word παρακλησεως comes from the Greek root words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "alongside" and κλῆσις (klēsis) meaning "a calling" or "summons". Together, they form the word παρακλησεως which means "a calling or summons to one's side" or "an appeal for help or comfort".

Grammar: παρακλησεως is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of the word παράκλησις (paraklēsis).

Description: παρακλησεως is a word that is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to a call for comfort or help from a higher power. It can also be used more generally to refer to any kind of appeal for assistance or support. In some contexts, it may be translated as "consolation" or "encouragement".
