Word: παραβολην

Etymology: The word παραβολην comes from the Greek word παραβολή (parabolē), which means "comparison" or "analogy". It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb βάλλω (ballō), meaning "to throw".

Grammar: παραβολην is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: παραβολην is a word that refers to a parable or a comparison. In the Bible, Jesus often used parables to teach moral or spiritual lessons. The word can also be used more generally to refer to any kind of analogy or comparison between two things.
