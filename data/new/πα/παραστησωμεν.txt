Word: παραστησωμεν

Etymology:
The word παραστησωμεν is derived from the Greek verb παραστήσω (parastéso), which means "to present" or "to set before". It is formed by adding the suffix -ωμεν (-omen) to the stem παραστησ- (parastés-).

Grammar:
παραστησωμεν is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive active form of the verb παραστήσω (parastéso). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
παραστησωμεν can be translated as "let us present" or "let us set before". It is a call to action, inviting the listener or reader to participate in presenting or setting something before someone else. The subjunctive mood suggests that this action is not certain or definite, but rather a possibility or suggestion.
