Word: παρεθηκεν

Etymology:
The word παρεθηκεν (parethēken) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τίθημι (tithēmi), which means "to place" or "to set." The prefix παρα- (para-) means "beside" or "near," so παρεθηκεν can be translated as "he placed beside" or "he set near."

Grammar:
παρεθηκεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject (he) performed the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
παρεθηκεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Luke (23:33), where it describes the act of the Roman soldiers placing Jesus on the cross. The full verse reads: "And when they came to the place that is called The Skull, there they crucified him, and the criminals, one on his right and one on his left."
