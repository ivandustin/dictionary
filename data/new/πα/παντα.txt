Word: παντα

Etymology: παντα is a Greek word that means "everything" or "all". It is derived from the Greek word πᾶς (pas), which means "all" or "every".

Grammar: παντα is a neuter plural form of the adjective πᾶς (pas), which means "all" or "every". It is often used as a pronoun to mean "everything" or "all things".

Description: παντα is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to refer to all things in general, or to emphasize the completeness or totality of something. For example, one might say "παντα ενταξει" (panta entaxei) to mean "everything is okay", or "παντα τα παιδια" (panta ta paidia) to mean "all the children". It can also be used in philosophical or religious contexts to refer to the ultimate reality or divine essence that underlies all things.
