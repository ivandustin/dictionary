Word: παραδοθείς

Etymology:
The word παραδοθείς comes from the Greek verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver." The word is formed from the prefix παρά (para), which means "beside" or "alongside," and the verb δίδωμι (didomi), which means "to give."

Grammar:
παραδοθείς is a past participle of the verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
παραδοθείς is often translated as "delivered" or "handed over." It is used in the New Testament to describe Jesus being handed over to the authorities for crucifixion (Matthew 27:2, Mark 15:1, Luke 23:1, John 18:30). It can also be used more generally to describe something being given or delivered to someone else.
