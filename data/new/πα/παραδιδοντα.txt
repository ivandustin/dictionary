Word: παραδιδοντα

Etymology: παραδιδοντα is a present participle form of the Greek verb παραδίδωμι (paradídōmi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver". The word is derived from the prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb δίδωμι (dídōmi), meaning "to give".

Grammar: παραδιδοντα is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and accusative case. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently in the process of being handed over or delivered.

Description: παραδιδοντα can be translated as "handing over" or "delivering". It is often used in the context of betrayal or surrender, as in the phrase "παραδιδοντα τον εχθρον" (paradídonta ton échthron), which means "handing over the enemy". The word can also be used in a more general sense, such as "παραδιδοντα το βιβλιο στον φιλο μου" (paradídonta to vivlio ston filo mou), which means "delivering the book to my friend".
