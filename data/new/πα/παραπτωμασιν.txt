Word: παραπτωμασιν

Etymology: The word παραπτωμασιν is derived from the Greek word παράπτωμα (paraptoma), which means "a falling aside" or "a lapse." The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a plural ending, so παραπτωμασιν means "lapses" or "mistakes."

Grammar: παραπτωμασιν is a noun in the dative plural case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: παραπτωμασιν is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Romans 11:11. It is translated as "stumbling" or "falling" in English versions of the Bible.
