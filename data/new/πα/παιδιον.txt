Word: παιδιον

Etymology:
The word παιδιον (paidion) is a diminutive form of the Greek word παις (pais), which means "child". The suffix -ιον (-ion) is a common diminutive suffix in Greek, indicating smallness or endearment. Therefore, παιδιον (paidion) can be translated as "little child" or "little one".

Grammar:
In Greek, παιδιον (paidion) is a neuter noun. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: το παιδιον (to paidion) - the little child
Genitive: του παιδιου (tou paidiou) - of the little child
Accusative: το παιδιον (to paidion) - the little child
Vocative: παιδιον (paidion) - little child

Plural:
Nominative: τα παιδια (ta paidia) - the little children
Genitive: των παιδιων (ton paidion) - of the little children
Accusative: τα παιδια (ta paidia) - the little children
Vocative: παιδια (paidia) - little children

Description:
παιδιον (paidion) is a term of endearment used to refer to a young child. It can be used to describe a child's physical size or age, or to express affection or fondness for a child. In Greek literature, the word is often used in a metaphorical sense to represent innocence, vulnerability, or purity.
