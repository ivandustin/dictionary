Word: παραβασεων

Etymology:
The word παραβασεων is derived from the Greek word παράβασις (parabasis), which means "transgression" or "violation". The suffix -εων (-eon) is added to the end of the word to indicate the plural form.

Grammar:
παραβασεων is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "violations" or "transgressions". 

Description:
παραβασεων refers to multiple instances of breaking a law, rule, or agreement. It can be used in a legal context, such as referring to traffic violations or criminal offenses, or in a more general sense, such as violating social norms or ethical principles. The word implies a sense of wrongdoing or disobedience.
