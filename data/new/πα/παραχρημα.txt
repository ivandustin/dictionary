Word: παραχρημα

Etymology:
The word παραχρημα comes from the Greek words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and χρηματισμός (chrematismos) meaning "money-making" or "commerce". Therefore, the literal meaning of παραχρημα is "beside or near money-making".

Grammar:
παραχρημα is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action that is done immediately or without delay. It is often translated as "immediately", "instantly", or "without delay" in English.

Description:
παραχρημα is a common word in modern Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express urgency or to emphasize the need for immediate action. For example, if someone asks for help, the response might be "Θα έρθω παραχρημα" (I will come immediately). It can also be used in a business context to indicate that a payment needs to be made immediately, as in "Παρακαλώ κάντε την πληρωμή παραχρημα" (Please make the payment without delay).
