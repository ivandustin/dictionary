Word: παρεσκευασται

Etymology: The word παρεσκευασται is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb εσκευασται (eskeuastai) meaning "to prepare" or "to make ready". Together, παρεσκευασται means "to be prepared beside" or "to be made ready near".

Grammar: παρεσκευασται is a third-person plural passive perfect indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The perfect tense indicates that the action was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description: παρεσκευασται can be translated to mean "they have been prepared" or "they have been made ready". The word implies that something or someone has been prepared or made ready by someone else, rather than by their own actions. The context of the sentence would determine what has been prepared or made ready.
