Word: παροξυσμός

Etymology: The word παροξυσμός comes from the Greek verb παροξύνω (paroxýno), which means "to sharpen, to stimulate, to provoke". The suffix -ισμός (-ismós) is used to form abstract nouns from verbs, indicating the action or state of the verb.

Grammar: παροξυσμός is a masculine noun in Greek, belonging to the second declension. Its genitive form is παροξυσμού and its plural form is παροξυσμοί.

Description: παροξυσμός refers to the act of intensifying or exaggerating something, often for rhetorical effect. It can also refer to a sudden outburst of emotion or a sudden increase in symptoms of a medical condition. In English, the term "paroxysm" is often used to translate παροξυσμός.
