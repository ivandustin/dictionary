Word: παραγγελλει

Etymology:
The word παραγγελλει (parangellei) is derived from the Greek verb παραγγέλλω (parangellō), which means "to order" or "to command". It is composed of two parts: παρά (para), which means "beside" or "alongside", and ἀγγέλλω (angellō), which means "to announce" or "to report".

Grammar:
παραγγελλει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the base form παραγγέλλω (parangellō), which is a regular verb in Greek. The verb is active, indicating that the subject is performing the action of ordering or commanding.

Description:
παραγγελλει is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it orders" or "he/she/it commands". It is often used in the context of giving instructions or directions, such as in a military or business setting. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to indicate someone who is in a position of authority or control.
