Word: παιδεύσας

Etymology: 
The word παιδεύσας comes from the Greek verb παιδεύω (paideuō), which means "to educate" or "to train". It is derived from the noun παῖς (pais), which means "child" or "boy".

Grammar:
παιδεύσας is the aorist active participle of the verb παιδεύω. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
παιδεύσας means "having educated" or "having trained" in English. It is often used in the context of teaching or instructing someone. For example, in the New Testament, it is used to describe Jesus as a teacher who educated his disciples.
