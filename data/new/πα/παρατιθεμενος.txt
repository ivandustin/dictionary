Word: παρατιθεμενος

Etymology:
The word παρατιθεμενος comes from the Greek verb παρατιθημι (paratithemi), which means "to place beside" or "to set before". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending, indicating that the word is in the passive voice and describes someone or something that is being placed or set before.

παρατιθεμενος is a participle in the passive voice, masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb παρατιθημι, which is a compound verb made up of the prefix παρα- (para-, meaning "beside" or "alongside") and the verb τιθημι (tithemi, meaning "to place" or "to set").

παρατιθεμενος can be translated as "being placed beside" or "being set before". It is often used in the New Testament to describe someone who is appointed or designated for a particular task or role.
