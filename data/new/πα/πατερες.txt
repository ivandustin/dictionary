Word: πατέρες

Etymology: πατέρας (patéras) is the Greek word for "father". The plural form, πατέρες (patéres), means "fathers".

Grammar: πατέρες is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. It is the plural form of πατήρ (patír), which is the singular form of "father".

Description: πατέρες is a term used to refer to multiple fathers. It can be used in a literal sense to refer to a group of biological fathers, or in a figurative sense to refer to a group of male ancestors or forefathers. In Greek culture, the term πατέρες is often used to refer to the founding fathers of a city or nation.
