Word: παθειν

Etymology:
The word παθειν (pathein) is derived from the Greek verb πάσχω (pascho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." It is a combination of the prefix παθ- (path-) meaning "suffering" and the suffix -ειν (-ein) indicating the infinitive form of the verb.

Grammar:
παθειν is the infinitive form of the verb πάσχω (pascho). In Greek, the infinitive form is used to express the idea of an action without specifying the subject or tense. It can be translated into English as "to suffer" or "to experience."

Description:
παθειν is a Greek verb that is commonly used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the experience of suffering or undergoing a particular emotion or state. It is often used in the context of discussing the nature of human experience and the role of suffering in human life. The word has a strong emotional connotation and is often used to describe intense or difficult experiences.
