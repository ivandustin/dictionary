Word: παραπτώματα

Etymology: The word παραπτώματα comes from the Greek verb παραπίπτω (parapípto), which means "to fall beside" or "to fall away from." The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a plural noun ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple instances of falling away or making a mistake.

Grammar: παραπτώματα is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "mistakes" or "transgressions." It is often used in a religious or moral context to refer to sins or offenses against God or society. In English, it can be translated as "transgressions," "trespasses," or "sins."

Description: παραπτώματα is a word that carries a strong moral connotation. It refers to actions or behaviors that are considered wrong or sinful, and implies a sense of guilt or shame. In Greek Orthodox Christianity, the word is often used in the context of confession and repentance, as believers seek to acknowledge and atone for their mistakes. In secular contexts, the word can be used more broadly to refer to any kind of mistake or error, but it still carries a sense of moral weight and responsibility.
