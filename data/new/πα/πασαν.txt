Word: πασαν

Etymology: πασαν is a Greek word that comes from the root word πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". 

Grammar: πασαν is the accusative singular form of the feminine adjective πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". It is used to modify a feminine noun in the accusative case. 

Description: πασαν is often used in Greek literature to emphasize the completeness or totality of something. For example, in the New Testament, it is used in the phrase "πασαν την γραφην" (pasan tēn graphēn), meaning "all of scripture". It can also be used to describe a specific group of things, such as "πασαν την νυκτα" (pasan tēn nykta), meaning "all night long".
