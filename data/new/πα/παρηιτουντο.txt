Word: παρηιτουντο

Etymology:
The word παρηιτουντο is a conjugated form of the Greek verb παραιτέομαι (paraitéomai), which means "to ask for, to request, to beg". The verb is derived from the prefix παρά (pará), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb αἰτέω (aitéō), meaning "to ask, to request".

Grammar:
παρηιτουντο is the third person plural, imperfect middle/passive indicative form of the verb παραιτέομαι. This form indicates that the action of requesting or begging was ongoing or repeated in the past, and that the subject of the verb was either receiving the action (middle voice) or being acted upon (passive voice).

Description:
παρηιτουντο can be translated as "they were being requested/begged" or "they were requesting/begging for themselves". The context in which the word is used would determine the exact meaning.
