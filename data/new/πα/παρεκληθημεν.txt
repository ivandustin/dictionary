Word: παρεκληθημεν

Etymology:
The word παρεκληθημεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word παρακαλέω (parakaleo), which means "to call to one's side" or "to encourage." The verb is in the passive voice and past tense, indicating that the subject received encouragement or comfort from someone else.

Grammar:
παρεκληθημεν is a second-person plural verb in the passive voice and past tense. It is formed by adding the suffix -θη- to the stem παρακλη- and then adding the personal ending -μεν, which indicates that the subject is plural and the action is completed.

Description:
The word παρεκληθημεν can be translated as "you all were comforted" or "you all received encouragement." It implies that the subject was in a state of distress or discomfort and that someone else provided comfort or encouragement to them. The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the comfort that believers receive from God or from other members of their community.
