Word: παραθήκην

Etymology:
The word παραθήκην comes from the Greek word παραθήκη (parathēkē), which means "deposit" or "trust". It is derived from the verb παρατίθημι (paratithēmi), which means "to place beside" or "to deposit".

Grammar:
παραθήκην is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description:
παραθήκην refers to a deposit or trust that is placed in someone's care. It can also refer to a will or testament, as it is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the "deposit" of faith that has been entrusted to the apostles. In modern Greek, the word can also refer to a safe or a safety deposit box.
