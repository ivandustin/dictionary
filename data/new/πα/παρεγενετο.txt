Word: παρεγενετο

Etymology:
The word παρεγενετο (paregeneto) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb εγενετο (egeneto) meaning "to come into being" or "to happen". Therefore, παρεγενετο can be translated as "he/she/it came near" or "he/she/it arrived".

παρεγενετο is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

παρεγενετο is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to a physical arrival or approach, as in "he arrived at the airport" or "she came near the house". It can also be used metaphorically to describe the occurrence of an event or the appearance of a person or thing, as in "a solution to the problem came up" or "a new opportunity presented itself".
