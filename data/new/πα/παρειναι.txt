Word: παρειναι

Etymology:
The word παρειναι (pareinai) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb εἶναι (einai) meaning "to be". Therefore, παρειναι means "to be beside" or "to be near".

Grammar:
παρειναι is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειναι (-einai) to the present stem of the verb εἶναι (einai).

Description:
παρειναι is a verb that can be translated as "to be present", "to be nearby", or "to be at hand". It is often used in the context of physical presence, but can also be used in a more abstract sense to describe a feeling of being close to someone or something. For example, one might say "Ο Θεός είναι πάντα παρών" (O Theos einai panta paron) which means "God is always present".
