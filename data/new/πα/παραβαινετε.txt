Word: παραβαινετε

Etymology:
The word παραβαινετε comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of two words: παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "beyond" and βαίνω (baino) meaning "to walk" or "to step". Therefore, the literal meaning of παραβαινετε is "to step beyond" or "to transgress".

Grammar:
παραβαινετε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb παραβαίνω (paravaino) which means "to transgress" or "to violate". The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Παραβαίνω (paravaino) - I transgress
- Παραβαίνεις (paravainis) - You transgress (singular)
- Παραβαίνει (paravaini) - He/She/It transgresses
- Παραβαίνουμε (paravainoume) - We transgress
- Παραβαίνετε (paravainete) - You transgress (plural)
- Παραβαίνουν (paravainoun) - They transgress

Description:
παραβαινετε is a strong and forceful verb that implies a deliberate and conscious act of transgression or violation. It is often used in a moral or ethical context to describe actions that go against established norms or rules. The word can also be used in a legal context to describe actions that violate laws or regulations. Overall, παραβαινετε is a powerful and impactful word that conveys a sense of defiance and rebellion.
