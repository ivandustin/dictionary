Word: πατάξας

Etymology: 
The word πατάξας is a conjugated form of the Greek verb πατάσσω (patássō), which means "to strike" or "to hit". The verb comes from the ancient Greek word πάταγος (pátagos), which means "blow" or "stroke".

Grammar:
πατάξας is the aorist active participle of the verb πατάσσω. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of the verb.

Description:
πατάξας can be translated to English as "having struck" or "having hit". It is a past tense form that describes a completed action of striking or hitting. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Πατάξας τον εχθρό μου" (Patáxas ton echthró mou), which means "You struck my enemy".
