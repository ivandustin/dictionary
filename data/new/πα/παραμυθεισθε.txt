The word παραμυθεισθε comes from the Greek verb παραμυθέομαι (paramytheomai), which means "to console, to comfort". It is derived from the prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb μυθέομαι (mytheomai), meaning "to speak, to tell a story".

παραμυθεισθε is a second person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Ancient Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -εισθε (-eisthe) to the stem παραμυθε- (paramythe-). The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, or that the action is being done to the subject. παραμυθεισθε is a verb that means "be comforted" or "take comfort". It is a command or request to find solace or consolation in a difficult situation.
