Word: παρακλητον

Etymology:
The word παρακλητον comes from the Greek word παράκλητος (paráklētos), which means "called to one's aid" or "helper". It is derived from the prefix παρά (pará), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb καλέω (kaléō), meaning "to call".

Grammar:
παρακλητον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word παρακλητον is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
παρακλητον is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the Holy Spirit. It is often translated as "Comforter", "Advocate", or "Helper". The word is used to describe the role of the Holy Spirit in providing guidance, comfort, and support to believers. In Christian theology, the Holy Spirit is considered to be the third person of the Trinity, along with God the Father and Jesus Christ.
