Word: πασχετε

Etymology: The word πασχετε comes from the Greek verb πάσχω (paskho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peḱ- ("to be affected by") and is related to the English words "pathos" and "pathetic."

Grammar: πασχετε is a second-person plural present active indicative form of the verb πάσχω (paskho). It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they are currently experiencing or suffering from something.

Description: πασχετε is a verb that expresses the idea of suffering or experiencing something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as physical or emotional pain, hardship, or difficulty. In the New Testament, it is often used in reference to the suffering of Jesus Christ.
