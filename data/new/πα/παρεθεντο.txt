Word: παρεθεντο

Etymology:
The word παρεθεντο comes from the Greek verb παρατίθημι (paratithemi), which means "to place beside" or "to set before". The prefix παρα- (para-) means "beside" or "alongside", while the root θε- (the-) means "to place" or "to set". The suffix -ντο (-nto) is a past tense ending in Greek.

Grammar:
παρεθεντο is the third person plural past tense form of the verb παρατίθημι (paratithemi). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action took place in the past, and the third person plural indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description:
παρεθεντο can be translated as "they placed beside" or "they set before" in English. The word implies an action of placing or setting something next to or in front of something else. The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning and significance of the action.
