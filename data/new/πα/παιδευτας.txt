Word: παιδευτάς

Etymology:
The word παιδευτάς comes from the Greek verb παιδεύω (paideuō), which means "to educate" or "to train". The suffix -τάς (-tas) is a common suffix in Greek that denotes a person who performs a certain action or has a certain quality.

Grammar:
παιδευτάς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence. The plural form of παιδευτάς is παιδευτές (paideutes).

Description:
παιδευτάς refers to a person who educates or trains others. It can be used to describe a teacher, instructor, coach, or mentor. The word implies a sense of responsibility and authority in guiding and shaping the development of others. In ancient Greece, the role of the παιδευτάς was highly valued, as education was considered essential for the development of a well-rounded individual.
