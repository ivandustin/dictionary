Word: παρακαλει

Etymology: παρακαλει comes from the Greek verb παρακαλέω (parakaleo), which means "to call to one's side" or "to encourage." It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside," and the verb καλέω (kaleo), meaning "to call."

Grammar: παρακαλει is the third person singular present indicative active form of the verb παρακαλέω (parakaleo). It is used to express a present action or state of being in which the subject is calling to someone's side or encouraging them.

Description: παρακαλει is a Greek verb that is often translated as "he/she/it calls" or "he/she/it encourages." It is a common verb in Greek literature and is used in a variety of contexts, from personal interactions to religious and philosophical discussions. The verb conveys a sense of urging or inviting someone to come closer or to take action.
