Word: πατρωιου

Etymology: The word πατρωιου comes from the Greek word πατρῷος (patrōios), which means "of one's father" or "paternal". It is derived from the word πατήρ (patēr), which means "father".

Grammar: πατρωιου is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is used to describe something that is related to or belongs to one's father. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: πατρωιου is a word that describes something that is related to or belongs to one's father. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a father's property, inheritance, or lineage. For example, one might say "Οι πατρωιοί μου κτήσεις" (Oi patrōioi mou ktēseis) which means "My paternal possessions". The word can also be used to describe a person's paternal ancestry or lineage.
