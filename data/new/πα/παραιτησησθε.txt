Word: παραιτησησθε

Etymology:
The word παραιτησησθε is derived from the Greek verb παραιτέομαι (paraitéomai), which means "to decline" or "to refuse." The suffix -σθε (-sthe) is added to the end of the verb to indicate the second person plural imperative form, which is used to give a command or instruction.

Grammar:
παραιτησησθε is a second person plural imperative verb in the Greek language. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to decline or refuse something.

Description:
The word παραιτησησθε is often used in legal or formal contexts, where someone is being asked to decline or refuse a position or responsibility. It can also be used in everyday conversation to tell someone to decline an offer or invitation. The word is commonly used in Greek-speaking countries and is an important part of the Greek language.
