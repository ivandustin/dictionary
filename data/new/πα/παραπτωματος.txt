Word: παραπτώματος

Etymology: The word παραπτώματος comes from the Greek root words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "beyond" and πτώμα (ptoma) meaning "fall" or "stumble". Together, they form the word παραπτώματος which means "a fault" or "a mistake".

Grammar: παραπτώματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of the word παράπτωμα (paraptoma).

Description: παραπτώματος is a Greek word that is often used in a religious context to refer to a sin or transgression. It can also be used more generally to refer to any kind of mistake or fault. In English, it is often translated as "trespass" or "offense".
