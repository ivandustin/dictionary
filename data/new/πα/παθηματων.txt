Word: παθημάτων

Etymology:
The word παθημάτων is derived from the Greek word πάθος (pathos), which means "suffering" or "passion". The suffix -ματων (-maton) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to "sufferings" or "passions" in the plural form.

παθημάτων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, nouns decline according to their case, number, and gender. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of the noun.

Description:
παθημάτων refers to "sufferings" or "passions" in the plural form. It can be used to describe a range of emotional or physical experiences, such as pain, grief, or desire. In Greek literature, the concept of pathos is often associated with tragedy and the human experience of suffering.
