Word: παντοτε

Etymology: παντοτε is a Greek adverb that is derived from the words παν (pan) meaning "all" and τοτε (tote) meaning "then" or "at that time". Therefore, παντοτε means "always" or "at all times".

Grammar: παντοτε is an adverb in Greek and is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It does not change form based on the gender or number of the subject.

Description: παντοτε is a commonly used word in Greek and is often used to express the idea of something happening continuously or without interruption. It can also be used to express the idea of something happening repeatedly or habitually. For example, "Παντοτε πηγαίνω στη δουλειά με το αυτοκίνητο" (Pantote pigaino sti douleia me to aftokinito) means "I always go to work by car".
