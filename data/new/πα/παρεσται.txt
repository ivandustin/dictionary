Word: παρεσται

Etymology: παρεσται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word παρίστημι (paristēmi), which means "to present" or "to stand beside". The prefix παρα- (para-) adds the meaning of "beside" or "near".

Grammar: παρεσται is a third-person plural present indicative active form of the verb παρίστημι. It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present moment and is being performed by a group of people.

Description: παρεσται can be translated to "they are present" or "they stand beside". It is often used in a literal sense to describe a group of people who are physically present in a particular location. However, it can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a group of people who are present in a more abstract sense, such as being present in a conversation or in someone's thoughts.
