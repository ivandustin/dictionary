Word: παρεγινοντο

Etymology:
The word παρεγινοντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen." The prefix παρε- (pare-) means "near" or "beside." Therefore, παρεγινοντο can be translated as "they came near" or "they approached."

Grammar:
παρεγινοντο is a third-person plural imperfect indicative active verb in Greek. The imperfect tense indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action of the verb.

Description:
παρεγινοντο is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Luke 24:28, which describes the encounter between two disciples and Jesus on the road to Emmaus after his resurrection. The verb is translated as "they drew near" in the English Standard Version of the Bible. The word conveys a sense of movement and proximity, indicating that the disciples were approaching Jesus as they walked together.
