Word: παρακουσάς

Etymology: παρακουσάς is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "beyond" and the verb κούω (kouo) meaning "to hear". Therefore, παρακουσάς means "to overhear" or "to listen in".

Grammar: παρακουσάς is a second person singular aorist active participle of the verb παρακούω (parakouo). It is declined as follows:

- Masculine: παρακουσάς
- Feminine: παρακουσασα
- Neuter: παρακουσαν

Description: παρακουσάς is a word that describes the act of listening in or overhearing a conversation or information that was not intended for the listener. It can have a negative connotation, implying that the listener is eavesdropping or being nosy. However, it can also be used in a neutral or positive context, such as when someone accidentally overhears something important or helpful.
