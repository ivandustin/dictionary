Word: παραλαμβανουσιν

Etymology:
The word παραλαμβανουσιν comes from the Greek verb παραλαμβάνω (paralambanō), which means "to take along, to receive, to accept." It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), meaning "to take."

Grammar:
παραλαμβανουσιν is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is the third person plural form of the verb παραλαμβάνω (paralambanō).

παραλαμβανουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they receive" or "they accept." It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus' disciples or followers. For example, in John 14:3, Jesus says, "And if I go and prepare a place for you, I will come back and take you to be with me that you also may be where I am."
