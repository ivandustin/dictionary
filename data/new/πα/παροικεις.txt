Word: παροικείς

Etymology: The word παροικείς comes from the Greek root words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and οἰκέω (oikeo) meaning "to dwell" or "to inhabit". Together, they form the word παροικείς which means "sojourners" or "resident aliens".

Grammar: παροικείς is a noun in the Greek language and is in the plural form. It is declined as a third declension noun and has a stem of παροικει-. The nominative plural form is παροικείς.

Description: In ancient Greece, παροικείς referred to resident aliens who lived in a city-state but were not citizens. They were allowed to live and work in the city-state but did not have the same rights and privileges as citizens. They were often merchants, artisans, or laborers who had migrated from other parts of Greece or from other countries. The term was also used in the New Testament to refer to Christians who were living in a foreign land.
