Word: παρηκολουθησας

Etymology: The word παρηκολουθησας comes from the Greek roots παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and ακολουθώ (akoloutho), meaning "to follow." Together, they form the verb παρηκολούθησας (parēkolouthēsas), which means "you followed closely" or "you accompanied."

Grammar: παρηκολουθησας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb παρακολουθέω (parakoloutheo), which means "to follow closely" or "to accompany." The aorist tense indicates that the action of following closely or accompanying has been completed in the past.

Description: παρηκολουθησας is a Greek verb that describes the act of following closely or accompanying someone. It implies a sense of attentiveness and dedication to the person being followed. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as following someone on a journey, or in a figurative sense, such as following someone's instructions or advice.
