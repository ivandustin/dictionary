Word: παραιτούμαι

Etymology: The word παραιτούμαι comes from the Greek prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and the verb αἰτέω (aiteo), meaning "to ask" or "to request." Together, παραιτούμαι means "to ask to be beside or beyond," or more commonly, "to decline" or "to refuse."

Grammar: παραιτούμαι is a first-person present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It can be translated to English as "I decline" or "I refuse." The verb can also be conjugated in other tenses and moods, such as the aorist tense (παρεῖπον, "I refused") or the subjunctive mood (παραιτοῦμαι, "that I may refuse").

Description: παραιτούμαι is a common Greek verb used to express the act of declining or refusing something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as declining an invitation, refusing a request, or rejecting an offer. The verb is often used in formal situations, such as in business or legal settings, but can also be used in everyday conversation.
