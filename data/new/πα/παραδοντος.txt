Word: παραδοντος

Etymology: παραδοντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver."

Grammar: παραδοντος is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi). In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship.

Description: παραδοντος can be translated as "handing over" or "delivering." It is often used in the context of delivering or handing over something to someone else, such as a message, a package, or a person. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe the act of Judas Iscariot handing over Jesus to the authorities for his crucifixion.
