Word: πασχω

Etymology:
The word πασχω (paschō) is derived from the Greek root πάθος (pathos), which means "suffering" or "passion". It is related to the English words "pathos" and "passion".

Grammar:
πασχω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: πάσχω (paskhō)
- Plural: πάσχομεν (paskhomen)

Description:
πασχω is a Greek verb that means "to suffer" or "to experience pain". It is often used in the context of physical or emotional suffering, but can also refer to enduring hardship or difficulty. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the suffering of Jesus Christ.
