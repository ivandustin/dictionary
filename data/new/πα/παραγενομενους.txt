Word: παραγενομενους

Etymology: 
The word παραγενομενους is a Greek verb form that comes from the root word παραγίνομαι (paragínomai), which means "to come near" or "to arrive." The verb form παραγενομενους is in the present participle passive voice, which means "having come near" or "having arrived."

Grammar:
παραγενομενους is a present participle passive voice form of the Greek verb παραγίνομαι. It is declined in the masculine accusative plural form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verbal adjective that describes an ongoing action or state. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
παραγενομενους can be translated to English as "having come near" or "having arrived." It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the arrival of people or events. For example, in Luke 2:38, the prophetess Anna is described as "coming up" (παραγενομενη) to Mary and Joseph when they bring Jesus to the temple.
