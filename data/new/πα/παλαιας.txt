Word: παλαιάς

Etymology: The word παλαιάς comes from the Greek word παλαιός (palaios), which means "old" or "ancient."

Grammar: παλαιάς is a feminine noun in the genitive case, which means it is used to show possession or relationship to another noun in a sentence.

Description: παλαιάς can be translated to mean "of the old" or "of the ancient." It is often used to describe something that is old or ancient, such as a building or artifact. In Greek mythology, it is also used to describe the goddess of the underworld, Hades' wife, Persephone, as the "ancient one."
