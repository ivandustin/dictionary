Word: παντων

Etymology: παντων is a Greek word that comes from the root word πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". It is a combination of the prefix παν- (pan-), meaning "all" or "every", and the suffix -των (-ton), which is a genitive plural ending.

Grammar: παντων is a genitive plural form of the Greek word πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple things being possessed or related to.

Description: παντων can be translated to mean "of all" or "of every". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to refer to the universal or all-encompassing nature of something. For example, in the famous quote from Heraclitus, "πάντα ῥεῖ" (panta rhei), meaning "everything flows", the word παντα (panta) is used to convey the idea that all things are in a constant state of change.
