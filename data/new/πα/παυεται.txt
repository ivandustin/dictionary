Word: παυεται

Etymology: The word παυεται (pauetai) comes from the Greek verb παύω (pauo), which means "to stop" or "to cease."

Grammar: παυεται is a third-person singular present middle/passive indicative form of the verb παύω (pauo). It is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb.

Description: παυεται is a Greek verb that can be translated as "he/she/it stops" or "he/she/it ceases." It is often used in the context of someone or something coming to a halt or ending. For example, "Η βροχή παύεται" (I vrochi pauetai) means "The rain stops." The verb can also be used in a reflexive sense, as in "Ο Γιάννης παύεται να καπνίζει" (O Giannis pauetai na kapnizei), which means "John stops smoking."
