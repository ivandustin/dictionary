The word "παραδιδως" (paradidōs) is a Greek adverb that means "betrayingly" or "treacherously". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidōmi), which means "to hand over" or "to betray". 

Grammar: "παραδιδως" is an adverb, which means it modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. 

Description: The word is often used to describe actions or behaviors that are deceitful or disloyal. It can also be used to describe a situation where someone is betrayed or handed over to an enemy.
