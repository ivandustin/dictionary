Word: παρεκτος

Etymology:
The word παρεκτος (parektos) is derived from the Greek prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "beyond" and the adjective εκτος (ektos) meaning "outside" or "external". Therefore, the word παρεκτος can be translated as "beside the external" or "beyond the outside".

Grammar:
In Greek, παρεκτος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). The declension of the word depends on the gender and case of the noun it is describing.

Description:
The word παρεκτος can be used to describe something that is outside or beyond the usual or expected boundaries. It can also be used to describe something that is beside or adjacent to something else. In modern Greek, the word is often used to describe something that is irrelevant or unrelated to the topic at hand.
