Word: παρελαβες

Etymology:
The word παρελαβες (parelavas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and λαμβάνω (lamvano) meaning "to take" or "to receive". Therefore, the word παρελαβες means "you received" or "you took beside".

Grammar:
παρελαβες is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in the indicative mood of the Greek verb παραλαμβάνω (paralamvano), which means "to receive" or "to take". The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
παρελαβες is a word that describes an action of receiving or taking something beside or near oneself. It can be used in various contexts, such as receiving a package, taking over a task from someone else, or accepting a gift. The word is commonly used in everyday Greek language and is an important part of Greek vocabulary.
