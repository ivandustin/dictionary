Word: παρεισακτους

Etymology: The word παρεισακτους comes from the Greek words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "alongside" and εἰσάγω (eisagō) meaning "to bring in" or "to introduce". 

Grammar: παρεισακτους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is derived from the verb παρεισάγω (pareisagō) which means "to introduce secretly" or "to smuggle in". 

Description: παρεισακτους is used to describe something that has been brought in secretly or smuggled in. It can also be used to describe someone who has been introduced into a group or organization without proper authorization or approval.
