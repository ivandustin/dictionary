Word: παιδευω

Etymology:
The word παιδευω (paideuo) comes from the Greek root word παις (pais), which means "child". The word παιδευω (paideuo) means "to educate" or "to train", and it is derived from the idea of teaching and guiding children.

Grammar:
παιδευω (paideuo) is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: παιδευω (paideuo)
- Plural: παιδευομεν (paideuomen)

Description:
παιδευω (paideuo) is a Greek word that means "to educate" or "to train". It is often used in the context of teaching and guiding children, but it can also be used in a broader sense to refer to any kind of education or training.
