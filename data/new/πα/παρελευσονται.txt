The word παρελευσονται is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is derived from the root word "παρέρχομαι" (parerchomai), which means "to pass by" or "to go past."

παρελευσονται is a third-person plural verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, voice, and mood. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
παρελευσονται means "they are being passed by" or "they are going past" in English. It is often used to describe something that is happening in the present moment, such as a group of people walking by or a car driving past.
