Word: παιδευουσα

Etymology: παιδευουσα is a present participle form of the Greek verb παιδεύω (paideuō), which means "to educate" or "to train". The word comes from the root παιδ- (paid-), which means "child".

Grammar: παιδευουσα is a feminine singular form of the present participle of the verb παιδεύω. It is in the active voice and the present tense. The word functions as an adjective, modifying a feminine singular noun.

Description: παιδευουσα describes a female subject who is currently educating or training someone or something. It can also be used to describe a female subject who is being educated or trained. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in education, sports, or personal development.
