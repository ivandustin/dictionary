The word "παρακαλειν" (parakalein) is a Greek verb that means "to call to one's side," "to invite," "to urge," or "to encourage." It is derived from the prefix "παρα-" (para-), which means "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "καλειν" (kalein), which means "to call." 

In terms of grammar, "παρακαλειν" is a present active infinitive verb form, which means that it expresses the action of urging or encouraging in a general sense, without specifying who is doing the urging or who is being urged. It can be conjugated into various tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different nuances of meaning and context.

Overall, "παρακαλειν" is a versatile and commonly used verb in Greek, with a range of applications in both formal and informal settings.
