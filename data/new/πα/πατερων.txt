Word: πατερων

Etymology: πατερων is the genitive plural form of the Greek word πατήρ (patēr), which means "father". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pəter-.

Grammar: πατερων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple fathers.

Description: πατερων refers to "fathers" in Greek. It can be used to refer to biological fathers, as well as to ancestors or forefathers. In the context of Greek mythology, it can also refer to the gods who were considered the fathers of various heroes and demigods.
