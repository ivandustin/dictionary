Word: παρακαλων

Etymology: παρακαλων is the present participle of the Greek verb παρακαλέω (parakaleo), which means "to call to one's side," "to invite," "to exhort," or "to encourage." The word is derived from the prefix παρα- (para-), which means "beside" or "alongside," and the verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call."

Grammar: παρακαλων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently in the act of calling to one's side, inviting, exhorting, or encouraging.

Description: παρακαλων can be translated as "calling," "inviting," "exhorting," or "encouraging." It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus or other religious figures who are calling people to follow them or encouraging them to live a certain way. In modern Greek, the word is also used in a more general sense to describe someone who is urging or persuading someone else to do something.
