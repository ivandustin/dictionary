Word: παρεδωκας

Etymology: παρα- (para-, prefix meaning "beside" or "alongside") + δίδωμι (didōmi, "to give") → παραδίδωμι (paradidōmi, "to give over, to hand over"). The verb form παρεδωκας (paredōkas) is the second person singular aorist indicative active form of παραδίδωμι.

Grammar: παρεδωκας is a verb in the Greek language, specifically the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (you) performed the action.

Description: παρεδωκας means "you handed over" or "you delivered" in English. It is a verb that implies the act of giving something to someone else, often with the connotation of giving something up or surrendering it. The context in which this word is used can vary widely, from a simple act of passing an object to someone else, to a more complex situation involving betrayal or surrender.
