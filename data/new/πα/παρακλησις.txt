Word: παρακλησις

Etymology:
The word παρακλησις comes from the Greek verb παρακαλέω (parakaleo), which means "to call to one's side" or "to encourage." The noun form παρακλησις (paraklesis) means "encouragement" or "comfort."

Grammar:
παρακλησις is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. In the genitive singular form, it is παρακλήσεως (parakleseos). In the plural form, it is παρακλήσεις (parakleseis).

Description:
παρακλησις is a word that is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to comfort or encouragement. It can also refer to a formal request or appeal, such as a petition or plea. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the Holy Spirit as a comforter or advocate. Overall, παρακλησις conveys a sense of support and encouragement in difficult times.
