Word: παρατηρησεως

Etymology: The word παρατηρησεως comes from the Greek verb παρατηρέω (paratereo), which means "to observe" or "to watch closely". The suffix -σις (-sis) is added to the verb stem to form the noun παρατηρησις (parateresis), which means "observation" or "watching".

Grammar: παρατηρησεως is the genitive singular form of the noun παρατηρησις (parateresis). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated into English as "of" or "'s". Therefore, παρατηρησεως can be translated as "of observation" or "of watching".

Description: παρατηρησεως refers to the act of observing or watching closely. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as scientific observation, surveillance, or simply paying close attention to something. The word emphasizes the importance of careful observation and attention to detail.
