Word: παρηγγειλαν

Etymology:
The word παρηγγειλαν (parēngeilan) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb ἐντέλλω (entellō) meaning "to command" or "to order". Therefore, παρηγγειλαν means "they ordered" or "they commanded".

Grammar:
παρηγγειλαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb παραγγέλλω (parangellō), which means "to order" or "to command". In this form, it indicates that the action of ordering or commanding was completed in the past.

Description:
παρηγγειλαν is a verb that describes an action of ordering or commanding that was completed in the past. It is often used in historical or narrative contexts to describe events that have already taken place. The word can be used in various tenses and forms to indicate different aspects of the action, such as whether it was ongoing or completed, or whether it was active or passive.
