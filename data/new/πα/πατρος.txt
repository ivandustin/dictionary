Word: πατρός

Etymology: πατρός is a genitive form of the Greek noun πατήρ (patēr), which means "father". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European language, where the word *pəter- meant "father".

Grammar: πατρός is a genitive singular form of the noun πατήρ. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. Therefore, πατρός can be translated as "of the father" or "father's".

Description: πατρός is a word that is commonly used in Greek to refer to a person's father or to indicate a relationship with one's father. It can also be used in a broader sense to refer to a male ancestor or a male parent in general. The word is often used in Greek mythology to refer to the father of a god or goddess.
