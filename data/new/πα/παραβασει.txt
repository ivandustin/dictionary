Etymology: The word παραβασει comes from the Greek verb παραβαίνω (paravaíno), which means "to transgress" or "to violate". It is derived from the prefix παρά (pará), meaning "beside" or "beyond", and the verb βαίνω (vaíno), meaning "to go".

Grammar: παραβασει is a noun in the singular form. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: παραβασει refers to a violation or transgression of a law, rule, or agreement. It can also refer to a breach of trust or a failure to fulfill a duty or obligation. The word is often used in legal contexts, such as in discussions of traffic violations or criminal offenses.
