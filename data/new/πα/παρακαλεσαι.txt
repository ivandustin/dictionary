Word: παρακαλεσαι

Etymology:
The word παρακαλεσαι (parakalesai) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "alongside" and the verb καλεω (kaleo) meaning "to call". Therefore, the literal meaning of παρακαλεσαι is "to call alongside" or "to call to one's side".

Grammar:
παρακαλεσαι is a second-person singular present active imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or request to someone in the present tense. The verb is conjugated from the root word καλεω (kaleo) and the prefix παρα- (para-).

Description:
παρακαλεσαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be translated as "to ask", "to request", "to urge", "to encourage", "to invite", "to entreat", "to implore", "to appeal", "to exhort", or "to beseech". The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is translated as "to pray" or "to beseech". In modern Greek, it is commonly used to mean "to ask" or "to request".
