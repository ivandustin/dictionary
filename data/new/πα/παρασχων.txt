Word: παρασχων

Etymology:
The word παρασχων is a present participle of the Greek verb παρέχω (parechō), which means "to provide" or "to furnish". The prefix παρα- (para-) means "beside" or "alongside", and the root verb ἔχω (echō) means "to have" or "to hold". Therefore, παρέχω (parechō) can be translated as "to hold alongside" or "to provide alongside".

Grammar:
παρασχων is the present participle of the verb παρέχω (parechō), which means that it describes an ongoing action. In this case, it describes someone who is currently providing or furnishing something. The word is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
παρασχων can be translated as "the one who is providing" or "the one who is furnishing". It is often used in Greek literature to describe someone who is giving aid or assistance to another person.
