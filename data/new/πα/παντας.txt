Word: παντας

Etymology: παντας is a Greek word that comes from the root word πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". It is commonly used in Greek literature and language.

Grammar: παντας is the accusative plural form of the Greek word πᾶς (pas). It is used to refer to all or every person or thing in a group. In Greek grammar, the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: παντας is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to refer to all people or things in a group, or to emphasize the completeness or totality of something. For example, παντας οι άνθρωποι (pantas oi anthropoi) means "all people", while παντας οι λόγοι (pantas oi logoi) means "all the words". It is a common word in Greek literature and language, and is used in a variety of contexts.
