Word: πατρια

Etymology:
The word πατρια (patria) comes from the Greek word πατήρ (pater), which means "father." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pəter-.

Grammar:
πατρια is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural, and its nominative and genitive forms are as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: η πατρια (i patria)
Genitive: της πατριας (tis patrias)

Plural:
Nominative: οι πατριες (oi patries)
Genitive: των πατριων (ton patrion)

Description:
πατρια is a Greek word that means "homeland" or "fatherland." It refers to the country or region where a person was born or where their ancestors came from. The word is often used in a patriotic or nationalistic context, and it can evoke feelings of pride and loyalty towards one's homeland. In ancient Greece, the concept of πατρια was closely tied to the idea of citizenship and the duties and responsibilities that came with it. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to one's homeland or country.
