Word: παιδειαι

Etymology:
The word παιδειαι (paideiai) is derived from the Greek word παιδεία (paideia), which means education or upbringing. The suffix -αι (-ai) is a plural ending, so παιδειαι refers to multiple educations or upbringings.

Grammar:
παιδειαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
παιδειαι is a term that refers to the various forms of education or upbringing that a person may receive. In ancient Greece, paideia was highly valued and considered essential for the development of a well-rounded individual. It encompassed not only academic subjects such as mathematics and philosophy, but also physical education, music, and art. The goal of paideia was to produce individuals who were not only knowledgeable but also virtuous and capable of contributing to society. Today, the term παιδειαι is still used in Greek to refer to education, but it can also be used more broadly to refer to the process of personal development and growth.
