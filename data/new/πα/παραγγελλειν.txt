Word: παραγγέλλειν

Etymology:
The word παραγγέλλειν (parangellein) comes from the Greek roots παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and ἀγγέλλειν (angellein), meaning "to announce" or "to proclaim." Together, the word means "to give orders" or "to command."

Grammar:
παραγγέλλειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It is a compound verb, made up of the prefix παρά and the verb ἀγγέλλειν. In Greek, verbs are highly inflected, meaning that they change form depending on tense, mood, voice, and person. For example, in the present indicative active form, the verb would be conjugated as follows: παραγγέλλω (I give orders), παραγγέλλεις (you give orders), παραγγέλλει (he/she/it gives orders), παραγγέλλομεν (we give orders), παραγγέλλετε (you all give orders), παραγγέλλουσι(ν) (they give orders).

Description:
παραγγέλλειν is a verb that means "to give orders" or "to command." It is often used in a military or hierarchical context, but can also be used in everyday situations to indicate someone giving instructions or directions. The word emphasizes the authority and power of the person giving the orders.
