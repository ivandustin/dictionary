Word: παντος

Etymology: παντος is a Greek word that comes from the root word πᾶς (pas), meaning "all" or "every". It is often used in compound words to convey the idea of "all-encompassing" or "universal".

Grammar: παντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is often used as an adjective to modify other nouns, such as in the phrase παντοδύναμος θεός (pantodýnamos theós), meaning "almighty God".

Description: παντος can be translated to mean "all", "every", or "whole". It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to convey the idea of universality or completeness. In Greek mythology, it was used to describe the god Pan, who was believed to be the god of all things.
