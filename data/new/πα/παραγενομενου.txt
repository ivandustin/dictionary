Word: παραγενομενου

Etymology: 
The word παραγενομενου is a Greek verb that comes from the root words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and γίνομαι (ginomai) meaning "to become" or "to happen". The word is in the present participle form, which means "coming" or "arriving".

Grammar:
παραγενομενου is a present participle in the genitive case, masculine gender, and singular number. It is derived from the verb παραγίνομαι (paragínomai), which means "to arrive" or "to come near". The genitive case indicates possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun.

Description:
παραγενομενου can be translated to mean "of the one who is coming" or "of the one who has arrived". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the arrival of Jesus Christ or other important figures.
