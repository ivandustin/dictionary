Word: παιδιων

Etymology:
The word παιδιων is derived from the Greek word παιδίον (paidíon), which means "little child" or "young child". The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple children.

Grammar:
παιδιων is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple children being referred to.

Description:
παιδιων is a Greek word that refers to multiple young children. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in literature, poetry, or everyday conversation. The word carries connotations of innocence, playfulness, and youthfulness.
