Word: παραδοθηναι

Etymology: παραδοθηναι is a Greek word that comes from the verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver."

Grammar: παραδοθηναι is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: παραδοθηναι can be translated as "to be handed over" or "to be delivered." It is often used in the context of legal or judicial proceedings, where someone is being handed over to authorities or being delivered to a particular fate. In the New Testament, for example, the word is used to describe Jesus being handed over to the authorities for crucifixion.
