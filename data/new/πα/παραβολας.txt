Word: παραβολας

Etymology:
The word παραβολας comes from the Greek word παραβολή (parabolē), which means "comparison" or "analogy". It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb βάλλω (ballō), meaning "to throw".

Grammar:
παραβολας is the genitive singular form of the Greek noun παραβολή (parabolē), which is a feminine noun of the first declension. In Greek, nouns decline according to gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated in English as "of" or "'s".

Description:
παραβολας is a Greek word that can be translated as "of the parables" or "of the comparisons". It is often used in reference to the parables of Jesus in the New Testament, which are stories or analogies that illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson. The use of parables was a common teaching method in ancient Jewish and Greek literature, and Jesus used them to convey complex theological concepts in a way that was accessible to his audience.
