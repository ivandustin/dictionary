Word: παρακείται

Etymology:
The word παρακείται (parakeitai) is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb κεῖμαι (keimai) meaning "to lie down" or "to be situated". Therefore, the literal meaning of παρακείται is "to lie beside" or "to be situated near".

Grammar:
παρακείται is a third-person singular present indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb κεῖμαι (keimai) which is a deponent verb, meaning that it has a passive form but an active meaning. The verb παρακείται is often used in the present tense to describe a current state or situation.

Description:
The word παρακείται is often used in Greek to describe something that is located or situated near something else. It can also be used to describe a person who is lying down or reclining next to someone else. In addition, it can be used metaphorically to describe a situation or circumstance that is closely related to another.
