Word: παρακλητος

Etymology:
The word παρακλητος comes from the Greek word παράκλησις (paraklēsis), which means "comfort" or "encouragement". It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb καλέω (kaleō), meaning "to call".

Grammar:
In Greek, παρακλητος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person or thing that provides comfort or encouragement. It is often used in the New Testament to refer to the Holy Spirit, who is described as the "Comforter" or "Advocate" (John 14:16, 26; 15:26; 16:7).

Description:
παρακλητος is a word that conveys the idea of someone who comes alongside to provide comfort, encouragement, or support. It is often used in a legal context to refer to an advocate or counselor who provides assistance and guidance to someone in need. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the Holy Spirit, who is seen as a helper and guide for believers. Overall, παρακλητος is a word that emphasizes the importance of support and encouragement in difficult times.
