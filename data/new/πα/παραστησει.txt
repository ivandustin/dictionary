Word: παραστησει

Etymology:
The word παραστησει (parastēsei) is derived from the Greek verb παραστήσω (parastēsō), which means "to present" or "to exhibit". It is formed from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb στήσω (stēsō), meaning "to stand".

Grammar:
παραστησει is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs are highly inflected, meaning that their form changes depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description:
παραστησει can be translated to English as "he/she/it will present" or "he/she/it will exhibit". It is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts, such as presenting an argument, exhibiting artwork, or introducing someone. The word is commonly used in formal settings, such as academic or professional presentations.
