Word: παραθήσω

Etymology:
The word παραθήσω (parathēsō) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "near," and the verb τίθημι (tithēmi), meaning "to place" or "to set." Therefore, παραθήσω means "to place beside" or "to set near."

Grammar:
παραθήσω is a first-person singular future active indicative form of the verb παρατίθημι (paratithēmi), which means "to place beside" or "to set near." The verb is conjugated in the future tense, indicating that the action will take place at a later time. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
παραθήσω is a Greek verb that means "I will place beside" or "I will set near." It is often used in a figurative sense, meaning "to present" or "to offer." The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as offering a gift, presenting an argument, or introducing a new idea.
