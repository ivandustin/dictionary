Word: παρηλθεν

Etymology:
The word παρηλθεν (parēlthen) is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρά (pará), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), meaning "to come." Therefore, παρηλθεν can be translated as "to pass by" or "to go beyond."

Grammar:
παρηλθεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
παρηλθεν is a verb that is used to describe the action of passing by or going beyond something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone passes by a building or a person, or in a figurative sense, such as when time passes by or an opportunity is missed. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus passing by or going beyond a certain place or person.
