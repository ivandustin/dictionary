Word: πανουργίαι

Etymology:
The word πανουργίαι (panourgiai) is derived from the Greek word πανοῦργος (panourgos), which means "crafty" or "cunning". The suffix -ία (-ia) is added to form a noun, which means "the quality of being crafty or cunning".

Grammar:
πανουργίαι is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of the quality of being crafty or cunning.

Description:
πανουργίαι refers to the quality of being crafty, cunning, or sly. It can be used to describe someone who is skilled at manipulating situations or people to their advantage. The word can have both positive and negative connotations, depending on the context in which it is used. In some cases, it can be seen as a desirable trait, such as in politics or business, where being able to outmaneuver one's opponents can be an advantage.
