Word: παρενοχλειν

Etymology:
The word παρενοχλειν (parenokhlein) is a Greek verb that is derived from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "near" and the verb ἐνοχλέω (enokhleō) meaning "to annoy" or "to trouble". Therefore, παρενοχλειν means "to bother" or "to disturb".

Grammar:
παρενοχλειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem παρενοχλε-. The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: παρενοχλώ (parenokhló) - I bother
- Dual: παρενοχλοῦμεν (parenokholoumen) - we bother
- Plural: παρενοχλοῦμεν (parenokholoumen) - we bother

Description:
παρενοχλειν is a verb that describes the act of bothering or disturbing someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, work environments, or public spaces. The verb can also be used in a figurative sense, such as in the phrase "παρενοχλειν την ησυχία" (parenokhlein tin esykhía) which means "to disturb the peace".
