Word: παρρησιαζομενος

Etymology: The word παρρησιαζομενος comes from the Greek verb παρρησιαζομαι (parrhēsiazomai), which means "to speak freely, to speak boldly, to speak openly". The word is derived from the Greek words παρρησία (parrhēsia), meaning "free speech, frankness, openness", and the verb form of the suffix -ιζω (-izō), which indicates the action of the verb.

Grammar: παρρησιαζομενος is the present participle form of the verb παρρησιαζομαι. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The present participle form indicates that the action of speaking freely, boldly, and openly is currently happening.

Description: παρρησιαζομενος describes someone who is speaking freely, boldly, and openly. It can be used to describe someone who is expressing their opinions or ideas without fear of consequences or retribution. The word implies a sense of courage and confidence in one's speech.
