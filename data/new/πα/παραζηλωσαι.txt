Word: παραζηλώσαι

Etymology: The word παραζηλώσαι comes from the Greek verb παραζηλόω (parazeloo), which means "to provoke to jealousy" or "to envy". It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb ζηλόω (zeloo), meaning "to be jealous" or "to envy".

Grammar: παραζηλώσαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and subjunctive mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb παραζηλόω (parazeloo).

Description: παραζηλώσαι means "that you may provoke to jealousy" or "that you may make jealous" in English. It is often used in the context of relationships, where one person may intentionally or unintentionally make another person jealous. In the New Testament, the word is used in Romans 10:19 to describe how God used the Gentiles to provoke the Jews to jealousy.
