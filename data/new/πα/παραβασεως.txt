Word: παραβασεως

Etymology:
The word παραβασεως comes from the Greek verb παραβαίνω (parabaínō), which means "to transgress" or "to violate". The suffix -εως (-eos) is a genitive singular ending that indicates possession or origin. Therefore, παραβασεως can be translated as "of transgression" or "of violation".

Grammar:
παραβασεως is a noun in the genitive singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship between two nouns. In this case, παραβασεως is used to describe the nature or quality of something that is related to transgression or violation.

Description:
παραβασεως is a Greek word that refers to the act of transgressing or violating a law, rule, or boundary. It can be used to describe any type of wrongdoing or misconduct, such as breaking a contract, disobeying a command, or crossing a physical or moral boundary. The word has a negative connotation and implies that the action is wrongful or unjustified. In modern Greek, παραβασεως is commonly used in legal contexts to refer to a violation of the law.
