Word: παρερχεσθε

Etymology: παρερχεσθε is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix παρα- (para-) meaning "beside" or "alongside" and the verb ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, παρερχεσθε means "to pass by" or "to go past".

Grammar: παρερχεσθε is a second person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to "pass by" or "go past" something.

Description: παρερχεσθε is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to convey the idea of passing by or going past something or someone. It can be used literally, as in passing by a physical location, or figuratively, as in passing by an opportunity or ignoring someone in need. The word emphasizes the idea of moving forward without stopping or getting distracted.
