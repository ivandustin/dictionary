The word "παρουσιαν" (parousian) is a Greek noun that means "presence" or "coming". It is derived from the Greek word "παρουσία" (parousia), which means "presence" or "arrival". In terms of grammar, it is a feminine noun in the accusative case. It can be used to describe the physical presence of someone or something, or it can refer to a spiritual or religious concept, such as the second coming of Christ.
