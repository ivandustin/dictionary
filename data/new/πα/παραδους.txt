Word: παραδους

Etymology: The word παραδους comes from the Greek verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver". It is derived from the prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb δίδωμι (didomi), meaning "to give".

Grammar: παραδους is a noun in the nominative singular form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form of the word is παραδόντος (paradontos).

Description: παραδους refers to someone who hands over or delivers something to another person. It can also be used to describe someone who betrays or gives up information or secrets to someone else. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used in the context of military or political situations, where one side hands over a city or a prisoner to the other side.
