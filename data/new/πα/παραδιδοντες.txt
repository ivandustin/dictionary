Word: παραδιδόντες

Etymology:
The word παραδιδόντες (paradidontes) is a present participle form of the Greek verb παραδίδωμι (paradidomi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver." The word is derived from the prefix παρά (para), which means "beside" or "alongside," and the verb δίδωμι (didomi), which means "to give."

Grammar:
παραδιδόντες is a present participle form of the verb παραδίδωμι. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -οντας (-ontas) to the stem of the verb. The present participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous. In this case, παραδιδόντες means "those who are handing over" or "those who are delivering."

Description:
παραδιδόντες is a Greek word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to describe the actions of those who betrayed or handed over Jesus to the authorities. The word can also be used more generally to describe anyone who is handing over or delivering something to someone else.
