The word "παραδω" (parado) is a Greek verb that means "to hand over" or "to deliver." 

Etymology: The word comes from the combination of two Greek words: "παρά" (para), which means "beside" or "near," and "δίδωμι" (didomi), which means "to give." 

Grammar: "Παραδω" is a first-conjugation verb in Greek, meaning that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in "-ω." It is also a transitive verb, meaning that it requires a direct object. 

Description: The verb "παραδω" is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of handing over or delivering something to someone else. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as delivering a package, handing over a document, or surrendering in battle. It is often used in the passive voice, as in "Μου παραδόθηκε ένα δώρο" (Mou paradotike ena doro), which means "A gift was handed over to me."
