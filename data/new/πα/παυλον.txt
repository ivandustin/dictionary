Word: παυλον

Etymology:
The word παυλον (paulon) is a Greek word that means "small coin" or "denarius". It is derived from the Latin word "denarius" which was a silver coin used in ancient Rome.

Grammar:
In Greek, παυλον is a neuter noun in the singular form. Its genitive form is παυλου (paulou) and its accusative form is παυλον (paulon).

Description:
παυλον was a small silver coin used in ancient Greece and Rome. It was equivalent to the Roman denarius and was used as a standard currency in the Mediterranean region. The word παυλον is also used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the amount of money paid to Judas Iscariot for betraying Jesus.
