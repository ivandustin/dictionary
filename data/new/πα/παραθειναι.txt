Word: παραθείναι

Etymology:
The word παραθείναι comes from the Greek verb παρατίθημι (paratithemi), which means "to place beside" or "to set before". The prefix παρα- (para-) means "beside" or "alongside", while the root θείναι (theinai) comes from the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put".

Grammar:
παραθείναι is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. The verb stem is παραθειν-, and the ending -αι indicates that it is an infinitive form.

Description:
παραθείναι means "to present" or "to offer" in Greek. It is often used in the context of presenting an argument or evidence in a discussion or debate. The verb can also be used in a more general sense of offering something to someone, such as a gift or a service.
