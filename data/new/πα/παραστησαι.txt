Word: παραστησαι

Etymology:
The word παραστησαι comes from the Greek verb παρίστημι (paristēmi), which means "to present" or "to place beside". It is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb ἵστημι (histēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar:
παραστησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the infinitive mood is used to express the idea of an action without specifying who is performing it.

Description:
παραστησαι can be translated to English as "to present oneself" or "to appear". It is often used in the context of appearing before a judge or authority figure. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the appearance of Jesus before Pontius Pilate.
