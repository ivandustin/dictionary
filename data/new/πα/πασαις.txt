Word: πασαις

Etymology: πασαις is a form of the Greek adjective πᾶς (pas), which means "all" or "every". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₂- ("to protect, guard").

Grammar: πασαις is the feminine dative plural form of πᾶς (pas). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, πασαις modifies a feminine plural noun in the dative case.

Description: πασαις can be translated as "to all" or "to every" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the universal scope of God's love and salvation. For example, in Romans 10:12, it is written: "For there is no distinction between Jew and Greek; for the same Lord is Lord of all, bestowing his riches on all who call on him." Here, πασαις is used to emphasize that God's love and salvation are available to everyone, regardless of their background or ethnicity.
