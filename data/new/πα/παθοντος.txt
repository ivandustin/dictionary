Word: παθόντος

Etymology: The word παθόντος is derived from the Greek verb πάσχω (paskho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience". The suffix -οντος (-ontos) is the present participle form of the verb, indicating that the word refers to something or someone that is currently experiencing or undergoing something.

Grammar: παθόντος is the genitive singular form of the word, which means it is used to indicate possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in a sentence. It is often translated as "of the one who is suffering" or "of the one who is experiencing".

Description: παθόντος is a Greek word that is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the concept of suffering or experiencing. It can be used to refer to physical or emotional pain, as well as to the broader idea of undergoing a difficult or challenging experience. In literature and poetry, it is often used to convey a sense of pathos or emotional depth.
