Word: παραζηλώνω

Etymology: The word παραζηλώνω comes from the Greek prefix παρα- (para-), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and the verb ζηλόω (zeloo), meaning "to be jealous" or "to envy." Together, παραζηλώνω means "to provoke to jealousy" or "to make jealous."

Grammar: παραζηλώνω is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Παραζηλώνω (I provoke to jealousy)
- Παραζηλώνεις (you provoke to jealousy)
- Παραζηλώνει (he/she/it provokes to jealousy)
- Παραζηλώνουμε (we provoke to jealousy)
- Παραζηλώνετε (you all provoke to jealousy)
- Παραζηλώνουν (they provoke to jealousy)

Description: παραζηλώνω is a verb that describes the act of intentionally making someone jealous or envious. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in relationships, competition, or social situations. The word carries a negative connotation, as it implies a desire to harm or hurt someone emotionally.
