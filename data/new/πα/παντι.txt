Word: παντι

Etymology: παντι (panti) is a Greek word that means "to all" or "to everyone". It is derived from the Greek word πᾶς (pas), which means "all" or "every".

Grammar: παντι (panti) is a dative singular form of the Greek word πᾶς (pas), which means "all" or "every". In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: παντι (panti) is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to indicate that something applies to everyone or everything, or that something is done for the benefit of everyone. For example, παντι ανθρώπων (panti anthropon) means "for all people", while παντι τρόπω (panti tropo) means "in every way". It can also be used to indicate that something is done repeatedly or continuously, as in παντι χρόνω (panti chronō), which means "every year".
