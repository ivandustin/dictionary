Word: πατηρ

Etymology:
The word πατηρ (pater) is a Greek word that means "father". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pəter- which means "father" or "first". The word has been used in Greek literature since ancient times and has been adopted by many other languages.

Nominative: ὁ πατήρ (ho patēr) - the father
Genitive: τοῦ πατρός (tou patros) - of the father
Dative: τῷ πατρί (tō patri) - to the father
Accusative: τὸν πατέρα (ton patera) - the father

Accusative: τοὺς πατέρας (tous pateras) - the fathers

The word πατηρ is a term used to refer to a male parent or a father figure. It is a word that is deeply rooted in Greek culture and has been used in literature, religion, and everyday life.
