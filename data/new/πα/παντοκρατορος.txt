Word: παντοκρατορος

Etymology: The word παντοκρατορος comes from the Greek words παντο- (panto-), meaning "all" or "every," and κράτος (kratos), meaning "power" or "rule." Therefore, παντοκρατορος can be translated as "all-ruling" or "almighty."

Grammar: In Greek, παντοκρατορος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is a compound word made up of two elements: παντο- and κράτος. The word can be declined according to the rules of Greek grammar to indicate its function in a sentence.

Description: παντοκρατορος is a term used in Christian theology to refer to God as the "Almighty" or "All-Ruling" one. It is often used in hymns and prayers as a title for God. In the Bible, the term is used in the book of Revelation to describe God's power and authority over all things.
