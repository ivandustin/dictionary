Word: παρεστωσιν

Etymology:
The word παρεστωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb παρίστημι (paristēmi), which means "to present" or "to stand beside". The word is formed from the prefix παρα- (para-), which means "beside" or "near", and the verb ἵστημι (histēmi), which means "to stand".

Grammar:
παρεστωσιν is a third-person plural present active subjunctive form of the verb παρίστημι (paristēmi). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
παρεστωσιν can be translated as "they may be present" or "let them be present". The word is often used in legal or formal contexts to indicate that someone or something should be present or available. It can also be used in a more general sense to express the idea of being present or standing beside someone or something.
