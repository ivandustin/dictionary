Word: παρεστηκως

Etymology:
The word παρεστηκως (parestekos) is a Greek word that is derived from the verb παρίστημι (paristemi), which means "to stand beside" or "to present oneself." The suffix -ως (-os) is a common adverbial suffix in Greek that is used to form adverbs from adjectives.

Grammar:
παρεστηκως is an adverb in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the adverbial suffix -ως to the verb παρίστημι in the participle form παρεστηκώς.

Description:
The word παρεστηκως is used to describe someone who is standing beside or present at a particular place or event. It can also be used to describe someone who is ready or prepared for something. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the presence of angels or other divine beings.
