Word: παραπλεύσαι

Etymology: The word παραπλεύσαι comes from the Greek verb παραπλέω (parapléo), which means "to sail past, to sail alongside". It is composed of the prefix παρα- (para-), which means "beside, alongside", and the verb πλέω (pléo), which means "to sail".

Grammar: παραπλεύσαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action, and the infinitive mood indicates that the verb is being used as a verbal noun.

Description: παραπλεύσαι can be translated as "to sail past, to sail alongside". It is often used in the context of ships or boats sailing past each other, or sailing alongside each other. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is passing by or moving on from a situation or problem.
