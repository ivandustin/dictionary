Word: παραστητε

Etymology:
The word παραστητε (parastete) is derived from the Greek verb παρίστημι (paristemi), which means "to present" or "to stand beside". The prefix παρα- (para-) means "beside" or "near", while the root word στη- (stē-) means "to stand".

Grammar:
παραστητε is the second person plural imperative form of the verb παρίστημι (paristemi). Imperative forms are used to give commands or make requests. In this case, παραστητε is telling a group of people to "present" or "stand beside" something.

Description:
The word παραστητε is a command to a group of people to present or stand beside something. It is often used in a theatrical context, where actors are instructed to take their places on stage. It can also be used more generally to mean "to present" or "to introduce" something or someone.
