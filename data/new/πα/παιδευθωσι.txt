Word: παιδευθωσι

Etymology: The word παιδευθωσι comes from the Greek verb παιδεύω (paideuō), which means "to educate" or "to train". The suffix -θωσι (-thōsi) is a third person plural present passive subjunctive ending, indicating that the action of educating or training is being done to them.

Grammar: παιδευθωσι is a third person plural present passive subjunctive form of the verb παιδεύω. In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it.

Description: παιδευθωσι can be translated as "they may be educated" or "they may be trained". The word implies that the subject is not currently educated or trained, but that there is a possibility that they could be. The subjunctive mood suggests that this is a hypothetical situation or a desired outcome.
