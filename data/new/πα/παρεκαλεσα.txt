Word: παρεκαλεσα

Etymology:
The word παρεκαλεσα (parekalesa) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words παρά (para) meaning "beside" or "near" and καλέω (kaleo) meaning "to call". The prefix παρά (para) in this word implies a sense of urgency or proximity, while the root καλέω (kaleo) means to call or summon.

Grammar:
παρεκαλεσα (parekalesa) is a first-person singular aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb παρακαλέω (parakaleo), which means "to call to one's side", "to invite", "to exhort", "to encourage", or "to comfort". The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, and the indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact.

Description:
παρεκαλεσα (parekalesa) is a Greek verb that expresses the act of calling someone to one's side or inviting them to do something. It can also mean to exhort, encourage, or comfort someone. The word implies a sense of urgency or proximity, suggesting that the person being called is needed immediately or that the situation is pressing. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus calling his disciples to follow him or to comfort his followers in times of distress.
