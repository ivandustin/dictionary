The word "παρακυψαι" (parakypsai) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "near", and the verb "κύπτω" (kypto), meaning "to bend" or "to stoop". 

Grammar: "παρακυψαι" is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, meaning the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The verb is in the third person singular form. 

Description: The word "παρακυψαι" means "he/she/it bent down" or "he/she/it stooped". It is often used to describe a physical action of bending down or stooping, but can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is humble or submissive.
