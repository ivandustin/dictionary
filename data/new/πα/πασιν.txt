Word: πασιν

Etymology:
The word πασιν (pasin) is a Greek word that means "to all" or "to everyone." It is derived from the Greek word πᾶς (pas), which means "all" or "every."

Grammar:
πασιν (pasin) is a dative plural form of the Greek word πᾶς (pas). In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
πασιν (pasin) is a word that is often used in Greek literature and the New Testament to indicate that something applies to everyone or to all people. It is often used in phrases such as "to all nations" or "to all people."
