Word: παρεχομενος

Etymology:
The word παρεχομενος is a present participle form of the Greek verb παρέχω (parechō), which means "to provide" or "to furnish". The verb is derived from the prefix παρά (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb ἔχω (echō), meaning "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar:
παρεχομενος is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενος (-menos) to the present stem παρεχ- (parech-). The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description:
παρεχομενος can be translated as "providing" or "furnishing". It describes someone who is currently in the act of providing or furnishing something. For example, in the sentence "Ο παρεχόμενος βοήθησε τον πελάτη" (The provider helped the customer), παρεχομενος refers to the person who is currently providing assistance to the customer.
