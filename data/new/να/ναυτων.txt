Word: ναυτων

Etymology:
The word "ναυτων" is derived from the Greek word "ναύτης" (nautes), which means "sailor" or "seaman". It is a noun in the genitive plural form.

Grammar:
ναυτων is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "of sailors" or "of seamen". It is often used in Greek mythology to refer to the sailors who sailed with heroes such as Odysseus and Jason.

Description:
ναυτων is a word that is used to describe a group of sailors or seamen. It is often used in the context of Greek mythology, where it refers to the sailors who accompanied heroes on their journeys. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any group of sailors or seamen.
