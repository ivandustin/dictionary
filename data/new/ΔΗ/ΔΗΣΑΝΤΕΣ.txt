Etymology: The word "δησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "δέω" (deo), which means "to bind" or "to tie". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₁-.

Grammar: "δησαντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "δέω" (deo) in Greek. It is in the masculine plural nominative case. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the participle form functions as an adjective.

Description: "δησαντες" describes a group of people who have bound or tied something in the past. It can be translated as "having bound" or "having tied" in English.
