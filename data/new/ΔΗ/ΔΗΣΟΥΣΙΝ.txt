Etymology: The word "δησούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "δησοῦν" (desoun), which means "to bind" or "to tie up." It is derived from the noun "δεσμός" (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie."

Grammar: "Δησούσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, it is classified as a second conjugation verb.

Description: "Δησούσιν" is used to express the action of binding or tying up in the present tense. It can refer to physically restraining someone or something, or metaphorically representing a figurative binding or constraint.
