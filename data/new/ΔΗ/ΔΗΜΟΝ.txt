Etymology: The word "δημον" (demon) comes from the Greek word "δαίμων" (daimōn), which originally referred to a divine being or spirit. In ancient Greek mythology, daimones were considered intermediate beings between gods and humans.

Grammar: In Greek, "δημον" (demon) is a noun. It is in the masculine gender and singular number.

Description: The word "δημον" (demon) has different meanings depending on the context. In ancient Greek mythology, demons were supernatural beings, often associated with specific places or activities. They could be benevolent or malevolent, and they were believed to have influence over various aspects of human life. In modern usage, the term "demon" is often associated with evil spirits or malevolent entities.
