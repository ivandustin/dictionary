Etymology: The word "δησαί" (dēsai) comes from the Greek verb "δέω" (déō), which means "to bind" or "to tie." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₁-.

Grammar: "Δησαί" is the 2nd person singular imperative form of the verb "δέω" (déō) in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, specifically telling them to bind or tie something.

Description: "Δησαί" is a Greek word that means "bind" or "tie." It is used as a command to instruct someone to physically secure or fasten something by tying it.
