Etymology: The word "ιησού" (pronounced "ee-soo") comes from the Greek name Ἰησοῦς (Iēsous), which is derived from the Hebrew name יֵשׁוּעַ (Yeshua). It is a transliteration of the Hebrew name meaning "Yahweh is salvation."

Grammar: "Ιησού" is a noun in the Greek language. It is the nominative form of the word and is used to refer to Jesus, the central figure of Christianity.

Description: "Ιησού" is the Greek name for Jesus. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible and is widely recognized as the name of the founder of Christianity. Jesus is considered the Son of God and the Messiah by Christians. He is believed to have lived in the 1st century AD and his teachings and life story form the basis of the Christian faith.
