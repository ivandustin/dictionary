Word: ιωσιας

Etymology: Ιωσιας (Iōsias) is a Greek name derived from the Hebrew name יֹאשִׁיָּהוּ (Yoshiyahu), meaning "Yahweh supports" or "Yahweh heals". It appears in the Old Testament as the name of a king of Judah.

Grammar: ιωσιας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the Greek form of the name Josiah.

Description: ιωσιας is a name that has biblical origins and is still used today. It is a popular name in Greece and other countries with a Greek Orthodox tradition. The name is associated with strength, support, and healing, reflecting its Hebrew roots.
