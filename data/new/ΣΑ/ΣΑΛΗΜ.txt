Etymology: The word "σάλημ" (sálim) comes from the Turkish word "shalim" which means "loose trousers". It was borrowed into Greek during the Ottoman period.

Grammar: In Greek, "σάλημ" is a noun of neuter gender. It is declined as follows:

- Singular: το σάλημ (to sálim) - the trousers
- Plural: τα σάλιμ (ta sálim) - the trousers

Description: "Σάλημ" refers to a type of traditional Greek trousers that are loose-fitting and usually made of wool. They are typically worn by men and are often associated with traditional Greek folk costumes.
