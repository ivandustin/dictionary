Etymology: The word "σαρκινης" (sarkines) comes from the Greek word "σαρξ" (sarks), meaning "flesh" or "meat". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁sḱer-.

Grammar: In Greek, "σαρκινης" is a noun in the feminine gender. It is declined in the singular form as "η σαρκινης" (i sarkines) and in the plural form as "οι σαρκινες" (oi sarkines).

Description: "Σαρκινης" is an adjective that means "fleshy" or "meaty" in Greek. It is used to describe something that is related to or resembles flesh or meat.
