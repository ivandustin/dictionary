Etymology: The word "σαρρας" (sarras) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Hebrew word "שַׂרָס" (saras), which means "leprosy" or "skin disease."

Grammar: In Greek, "σαρρας" (sarras) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form of "σαρρα" (sarra).

Description: "Σαρρας" (sarras) refers to a type of skin disease, specifically leprosy. In ancient times, leprosy was a feared and stigmatized condition characterized by the progressive and disfiguring deterioration of the skin, nerves, and other tissues.
