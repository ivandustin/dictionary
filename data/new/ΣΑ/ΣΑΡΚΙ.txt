Etymology: The word "σαρκι" (sarki) comes from the Greek word "σάρξ" (sárx), which means "flesh". 

Grammar: "Σαρκι" (sarki) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is classified as a neuter noun. 

Description: "Σαρκι" (sarki) refers to the flesh of animals, particularly meat that is used for consumption. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the physical body or the human nature.
