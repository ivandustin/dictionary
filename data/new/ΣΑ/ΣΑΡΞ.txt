Etymology: The word "σαρξ" (sarx) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁sérk̑s, meaning "flesh" or "body."

Grammar: In Greek, "σαρξ" (sarx) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form of "σαρκός" (sarkos).

Description: "Σαρξ" (sarx) refers to the physical flesh or body of an organism, particularly in the context of human or animal bodies. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the physical nature or material aspect of something.
