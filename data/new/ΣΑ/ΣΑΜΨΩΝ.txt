Etymology: The word "σαμψών" (sampsṓn) comes from the Greek verb "σαμψάω" (sampsáō), which means "to collect" or "to gather."

Grammar: "Σαμψών" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: In Greek mythology, "Σαμψών" refers to Samson, a biblical figure known for his incredible strength. He is mentioned in the Book of Judges in the Old Testament. Samson's story includes his battles against the Philistines and his eventual downfall due to his love for Delilah.
