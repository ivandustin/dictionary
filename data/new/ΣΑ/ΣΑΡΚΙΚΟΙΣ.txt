Etymology: The word "σαρκικοίς" (sarkikois) comes from the Greek word "σάρξ" (sark) meaning "flesh" and the suffix "-ικος" (-ikos) which denotes "related to" or "pertaining to". 

Grammar: "Σαρκικοίς" is the plural form of the adjective "σαρκικός" (sarkikos) in the dative case. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Σαρκικοίς" is an adjective in Greek that means "fleshy" or "carnal". It is used to describe something that is related to or characteristic of the flesh or physical body.
