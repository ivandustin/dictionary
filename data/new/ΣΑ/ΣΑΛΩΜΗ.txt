Etymology: The word "σαλωμη" (salome) comes from the Hebrew name "שָׁלוֹם" (shalom), meaning "peace". It was later adopted into Greek as "σαλώμη" (salome).

Grammar: In Greek, "σαλώμη" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is declined as follows:

- Singular: η σαλώμη (i salomi) 
- Plural: οι σαλώμες (i salomes)

Description: "Σαλώμη" is a Greek name that is commonly associated with the biblical figure of Salome. In the New Testament, Salome is mentioned as one of the women who witnessed the crucifixion and burial of Jesus Christ. The name has also been used in various literary and artistic works throughout history.
