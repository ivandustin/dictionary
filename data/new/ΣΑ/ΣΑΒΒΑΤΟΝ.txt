Etymology: The word "σαββατον" (sabbaton) comes from the Hebrew word "שַׁבָּת" (shabbat), which means "rest" or "sabbath". It was borrowed into Greek and later into other languages.

Grammar: In Greek, "σαββατον" is a neuter noun of the second declension. It has the plural form "σαββατα" (sabbata). It can be used in the nominative, accusative, and vocative cases.

Description: "Σαββατον" refers to the seventh day of the week, which is considered a day of rest and worship in Judaism and Christianity. It is commonly known as the Sabbath. In Greek, it can also refer to a week or a period of seven days.
