Etymology: The word "σαλευθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "σαλεύω" (salevo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". The suffix "-θηναι" (-thenai) is a passive form ending, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "σαλευθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "σαλεύω" (salevo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "σαλευθηναι" can be translated as "to be shaken" or "to be moved back and forth". It describes the action of something or someone being subjected to shaking or movement.
