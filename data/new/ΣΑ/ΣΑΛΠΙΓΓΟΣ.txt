Etymology: The word "σαλπιγγος" (salpingos) comes from the Greek word "σαλπιγξ" (salpinx), which means "trumpet". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *swel- meaning "to sound, resound".

Grammar: In Greek, "σαλπιγγος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case, singular number. The genitive form is "σαλπιγγου" (salpingou).

Description: "Σαλπιγγος" refers to a musical instrument known as a trumpet. It is a brass instrument with a flared bell and three valves. The trumpet is played by blowing air through the mouthpiece and using the valves to change the pitch. It is commonly used in orchestras, bands, and military ceremonies.
