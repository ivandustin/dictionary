Etymology: The word "σακκων" (sakkōn) comes from the Latin word "saccus," meaning "bag" or "sack." It was borrowed into Greek and later evolved into "σακκων" (sakkōn).

Grammar: In Greek, "σακκων" (sakkōn) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension, and its genitive form is "σακκωνος" (sakkōnos).

Description: "Σακκων" (sakkōn) refers to a bag or sack used for carrying or storing various items. It can be made of cloth, leather, or other materials. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe a person's character or behavior as "baggage" or "burden."
