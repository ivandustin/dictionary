Etymology: The word "σαγηνηι" (sagēnēi) comes from the Greek verb "σαγηνεύω" (sagēneúō), which means "to enchant" or "to bewitch."

Grammar: "Σαγηνηι" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Σαγηνηι" refers to a state of being enchanted or bewitched. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that captivates or fascinates someone.
