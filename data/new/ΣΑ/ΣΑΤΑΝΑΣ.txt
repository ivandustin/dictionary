Etymology: The word "σατανας" (satanas) comes from the Hebrew word "שָׂטָן" (satan), which means "adversary" or "accuser". It was later adopted into Greek and other languages.

Grammar: In Greek, "σατανας" (satanas) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it can be declined to match different cases, numbers, and genders.

Description: In Greek mythology and Christian theology, "σατανας" (satanas) refers to the devil or Satan. It is often used to describe a malevolent supernatural being who opposes God and tempts humans to sin. In Christian belief, Satan is seen as the embodiment of evil and the leader of fallen angels.
