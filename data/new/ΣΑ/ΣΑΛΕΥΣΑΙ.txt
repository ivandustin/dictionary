Etymology: The word "σαλευσαι" (pronounced sa-lef-say) comes from the Greek verb "σαλεύω" (pronounced sa-le-vo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". 

Grammar: "σαλευσαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "σαλεύω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description: "σαλευσαι" can be translated as "to shake" or "to move back and forth". It is used to describe the action of physically shaking or moving something in a back and forth motion.
