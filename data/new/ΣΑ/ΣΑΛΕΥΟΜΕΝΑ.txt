Etymology: The word "σαλευομενα" comes from the Greek verb "σαλεύω" (salevo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". The suffix "-μενα" (-mena) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing something that is currently happening.

Grammar: "Σαλευομενα" is the plural form of the word, indicating that there are multiple things shaking or moving back and forth. It is in the neuter gender, which is used for inanimate objects or concepts.

Description: "Σαλευομενα" can be translated as "shaking" or "moving back and forth". It is often used to describe objects or things that are in motion or experiencing some kind of movement.
