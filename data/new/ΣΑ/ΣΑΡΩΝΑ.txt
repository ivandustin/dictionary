Etymology: The word "σαρωνα" (sarona) comes from the Greek verb "σαρώνω" (sarono), which means "to sweep" or "to clean". 

Grammar: "Σαρωνα" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the first declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Σαρωνα" refers to a broom or a brush used for sweeping or cleaning purposes. It is typically made of bristles or fibers attached to a handle.
