Etymology: The word "σαπρόν" (sapron) comes from the Greek adjective "σαπρός" (sapros), meaning "rotten" or "decayed."

Grammar: In Greek, "σαπρόν" is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Description: "Σαπρόν" is used to describe something that is rotten, decayed, or spoiled. It can refer to organic matter that has decomposed or to something that has gone bad or become unusable.
