The word "αξιουμεν" (axioumen) is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root word "αξιόω" (axioo), which means "to deem worthy" or "to consider deserving." 

In its present tense, passive voice form, "αξιουμεν" means "we are considered worthy" or "we are deemed deserving." It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to express the idea of being deemed worthy by a higher power or authority. 

In terms of grammar, "αξιουμεν" is a first-person plural verb, meaning it refers to a group of people (in this case, "we") who are being considered worthy. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is receiving the action, rather than performing it. 

Overall, "αξιουμεν" is a word that expresses the idea of being deemed worthy or deserving, often in a religious or philosophical context.
