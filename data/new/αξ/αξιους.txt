Word: αξιους

Etymology: The word αξιους (axious) comes from the Greek adjective αξιος (axios), which means "worthy" or "deserving". 

Grammar: αξιους is the masculine accusative form of the adjective αξιος. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: αξιους is an adjective that describes something or someone as worthy or deserving. It can be used to express admiration, respect, or approval. For example, one might say "Ο αθλητής είναι αξιούς επαίνου" (o athlitis einai axious epanou), which means "The athlete is worthy of praise".
