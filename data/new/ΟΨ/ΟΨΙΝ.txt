Etymology: The word "οψιν" (opsin) comes from the Greek word "οψις" (opsis), meaning "sight" or "vision". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *okw- meaning "to see".

Grammar: "Οψιν" (opsin) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the third declension.

Description: "Οψιν" (opsin) refers to the act or process of seeing or perceiving with the eyes. It can also be used to describe the sense of sight itself.
