Etymology: The word "οψις" (opsis) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "ὄψ" (ops), meaning "eye" or "sight".

Grammar: In Greek, "οψις" (opsis) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has a genitive form of "οψεως" (opseos).

Description: "Οψις" (opsis) refers to the act of seeing or the sense of sight. It can also be used to describe the appearance or visual aspect of something.
