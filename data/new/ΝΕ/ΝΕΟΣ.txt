Etymology: The word "νεος" (neos) comes from the Greek language and means "new".

Grammar: "Νεος" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative form "νεος" (neos), the genitive form "νεου" (neou), the accusative form "νεον" (neon), the vocative form "νεε" (nee), and the dative form "νεῳ" (neō).

Description: "Νεος" is used to describe something or someone that is new, fresh, or recent. It can refer to a new person, a new thing, or a new situation. In Greek mythology, "Νεος" is also the name of a river god.
