Etymology: The word "νεκρος" (nekros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nek- meaning "death" or "corpse."

Grammar: In Greek, "νεκρος" (nekros) is an adjective. It is in the masculine gender, singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Νεκρος" (nekros) means "dead" or "lifeless" in Greek. It is used to describe something that has ceased to live or is no longer functioning. It can refer to both animate and inanimate objects.
