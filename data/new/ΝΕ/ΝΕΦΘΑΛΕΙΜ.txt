Etymology: The word "νεφθαλειμ" (nefthaleim) comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by combining the words "νεφρός" (nephros) meaning "kidney" and "θάλαμος" (thalamos) meaning "chamber" or "room".

Grammar: "Νεφθαλειμ" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Νεφθαλειμ" refers to the kidney chamber or renal pelvis, which is a part of the kidney where urine collects before it is transported to the bladder. It is an anatomical term used in medical and biological contexts.
