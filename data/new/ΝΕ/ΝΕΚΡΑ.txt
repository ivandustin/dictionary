Etymology: The word "νεκρα" (nekrá) comes from the Greek word "νεκρός" (nekros), meaning "dead" or "lifeless".

Grammar: "Νεκρα" (nekrá) is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Νεκρα" (nekrá) refers to something that is dead or lifeless. It can be used to describe a deceased person, an inanimate object, or a lifeless state.
