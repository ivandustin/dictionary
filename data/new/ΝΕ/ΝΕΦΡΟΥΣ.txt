Etymology: The word "νεφρούς" (nefroús) comes from the Greek word "νεφρός" (nefrós), which means "kidney".

Grammar: "Νεφρούς" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case and is the plural form of "νεφρός".

Description: "Νεφρούς" refers to the kidneys, which are a pair of organs located in the back of the abdomen. The kidneys play a crucial role in filtering waste products and excess fluids from the blood, producing urine, and maintaining the body's electrolyte balance.
