Etymology: The word "νεκρον" (nekrón) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nek- meaning "death" or "corpse."

Grammar: In Greek, "νεκρον" (nekrón) is a noun. It is in the singular form and is classified as a neuter noun.

Description: "Νεκρον" (nekrón) means "corpse" or "dead body" in Greek. It refers to the lifeless remains of a deceased person or animal.
