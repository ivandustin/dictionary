Etymology: The word "νεκρος" (nekros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nek- meaning "death" or "corpse."

Grammar: In Greek, "νεκρος" (nekros) is an adjective. It is in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Νεκρος" (nekros) means "dead" or "lifeless" in Greek. It is used to describe something that has ceased to live or is no longer functioning. It can refer to a deceased person, an inanimate object, or even a metaphorical state of being.
