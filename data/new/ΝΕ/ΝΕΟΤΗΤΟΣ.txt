Etymology: The word "νεοτητος" (neotetos) comes from the Greek root "νέος" (neos), meaning "new," and the suffix "-τητος" (-tetos), which denotes a state or quality. 

Grammar: "Νεοτητος" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Νεοτητος" refers to the state or quality of being young or youthful. It can also be translated as "youthfulness" or "youth."
