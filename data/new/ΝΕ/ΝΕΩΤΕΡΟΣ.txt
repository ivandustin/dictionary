Etymology: The word "νεωτερος" (neōteros) comes from the Greek root "νέος" (neos), meaning "new" or "young."

Grammar: In Greek, "νεωτερος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. For example, "νεωτερος" can be used to describe a new book (νεωτερος βιβλίος), a new car (νεωτερο αυτοκίνητο), or a new idea (νεωτερη ιδέα).

Description: "Νεωτερος" is used to describe something that is new or recent. It can refer to objects, concepts, or ideas that have been recently introduced or created. It is often used to contrast with something older or more traditional.
