Etymology: The word "νεκραν" (nekran) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "νεκρός" (nekros), meaning "dead" or "corpse".

Grammar: "Νεκραν" (nekran) is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.
