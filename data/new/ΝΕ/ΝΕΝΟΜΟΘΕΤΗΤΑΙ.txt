Etymology: The word "νενομοθετηται" comes from the Greek verb "νομοθετώ" (nomothetó), which means "to legislate" or "to enact laws". The prefix "νενομο-" indicates the perfect tense, indicating that the action of legislating has been completed.

Grammar: "Νενομοθετηται" is the third person singular form of the verb "νομοθετώ" in the perfect tense. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The perfect tense indicates a completed action with ongoing results.

Description: "Νενομοθετηται" is a Greek word that means "has been legislated" or "has been enacted". It is used to describe a situation where laws or regulations have been officially established or put into effect.
