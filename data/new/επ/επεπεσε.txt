Word: επεπεσε

Etymology: The word επεπεσε comes from the Greek verb πίπτω (pípto), which means "to fall". The prefix επ- (ep-) is added to the verb to indicate that the action happened in the past.

Grammar: επεπεσε is a third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb πίπτω (pípto). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: επεπεσε can be translated to English as "he/she/it fell". It is a simple past tense form that describes a single event in the past. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a physical fall or a metaphorical fall from grace.
