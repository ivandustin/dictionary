The word "επελυεν" (epelyen) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "λυ-" (ly-) which means "to loosen" or "to untie". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to indicate a completed action, so "επελυεν" can be translated as "he/she/it untied" or "he/she/it loosened".

Grammar: "Επελυεν" is in the past tense, third person singular form of the verb. It can be conjugated in other tenses and persons as well, depending on the context of the sentence.

Description: The word "επελυεν" describes an action of untying or loosening something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as untying a knot, or in a figurative sense, such as loosening a grip on power or control.
