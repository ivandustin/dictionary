Word: επειθετό (epithetó)

Etymology:
The word επειθετό (epithetó) comes from the Greek verb επείθομαι (epéithomai), which means "to obey" or "to be persuaded". The suffix -τό (-tó) is added to the verb stem to form an adjective, which means "obedient" or "compliant".

Grammar:
επειθετό (epithetó) is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
επειθετό (epithetó) is used to describe a person or thing that is obedient, compliant, or submissive. It can be used in a positive or negative sense, depending on the context. For example, it can be used to describe a student who is obedient and follows the rules, or it can be used to describe a person who is too compliant and lacks initiative.
