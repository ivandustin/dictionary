Word: επισυντρέχει (episynthréchei)

Etymology: The word επισυντρέχει comes from the combination of the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb συντρέχω (syntrécho), meaning "to run together" or "to converge". Therefore, επισυντρέχει means "to converge upon" or "to come together".

Grammar: επισυντρέχει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb συντρέχω (syntrécho), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: επισυντρέχει is used to describe a situation where multiple things or people are coming together or converging upon a certain point or location. It can also be used to describe a person who is rushing to join a group or gathering.
