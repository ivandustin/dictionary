Word: επισυναξει

Etymology: The word επισυναξει comes from the Greek verb επισυνάγω (episynagō), which means "to gather together" or "to assemble". The suffix -ει (-ei) indicates the future tense, so επισυναξει means "it will gather together" or "it will assemble".

Grammar: επισυναξει is a third-person singular future active indicative form of the verb επισυνάγω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: επισυναξει is a Greek verb that means "it will gather together" or "it will assemble". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people coming together for a meeting or a collection of objects being gathered in one place.
