Word: επικαλεσαμενος

Etymology:
The word επικαλεσαμενος comes from the Greek verb επικαλέομαι (epikaleomai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb καλέω (kaleo), meaning "to call".

Grammar:
επικαλεσαμενος is the aorist participle of the verb επικαλέομαι (epikaleomai). As a participle, it functions as an adjective and describes a noun or pronoun. In this case, it describes a masculine singular noun or pronoun. The aorist tense indicates that the action of calling upon or invoking has been completed in the past.

Description:
επικαλεσαμενος can be translated as "having called upon" or "having invoked". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the act of calling upon a deity or higher power for assistance or guidance. It can also be used more generally to describe the act of calling upon someone or something for help or support.
