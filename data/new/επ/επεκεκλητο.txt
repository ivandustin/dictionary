Word: επεκεκλητο

Etymology:
The word επεκεκλητο is derived from the Greek verb επικαλέομαι (epikaleomai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". The prefix επι- (epi-) means "upon" or "on", while the root καλέω (kaleo) means "to call". The suffix -το (-to) indicates that the verb is in the middle or passive voice.

Grammar:
επεκεκλητο is a third-person singular aorist middle/passive indicative form of the verb επικαλέομαι (epikaleomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description:
The word επεκεκλητο can be translated as "he/she/it called upon himself/herself/itself" or "he/she/it was called upon". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, to describe the act of calling upon a deity or higher power for help or guidance. In ancient Greek literature, it is frequently used in the context of prayer or supplication.
