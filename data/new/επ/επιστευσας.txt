Word: επιστευσας

The word επιστευσας comes from the Greek verb επιστεύω (epistevo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith in". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb στεύω (stevo), meaning "to believe" or "to trust".

επιστευσας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb επιστεύω (epistevo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.
