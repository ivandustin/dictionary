Word: επενεγκειν

Etymology:
The word επενεγκειν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and φέρω (phero) meaning "to carry" or "to bear". Therefore, επενεγκειν means "to carry upon" or "to bring over".

Grammar:
επενεγκειν is an infinitive verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense, which indicates a past action that is completed. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
επενεγκειν is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to describe physically carrying something over or upon something else, such as carrying a box over a fence. It can also be used metaphorically to describe bringing something over or upon someone, such as bringing a message or a burden to someone. In addition, it can be used in a religious context to describe offering a sacrifice or a prayer to a deity.
