The word "επιπεπτωκος" (epipeptokos) is a Greek adjective that means "fallen upon" or "fallen down upon". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pipto), which means "to fall", and the prefix "επι-" (epi-), which means "upon" or "on".

Grammar: "Επιπεπτωκος" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe something that has fallen upon or affected someone or something in a negative way. It can also be used in a literal sense to describe something that has physically fallen upon something else.
