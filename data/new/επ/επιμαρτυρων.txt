Word: επιμαρτυρων

Etymology: The word επιμαρτυρων comes from the Greek words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and μαρτυρέω (martyreo) meaning "to testify" or "to bear witness". 

Grammar: επιμαρτυρων is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb μαρτυρέω (martyreo) which means "to testify" or "to bear witness". The prefix επί (epi) adds the meaning of "upon" or "over" to the verb, making the participle επιμαρτυρων mean "testifying upon" or "bearing witness over".

Description: επιμαρτυρων is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Acts 14:3. It is used to describe Paul and Barnabas as they were preaching the gospel and bearing witness to the truth of Jesus Christ. The word emphasizes the idea of testifying or bearing witness to something important and true.
