Word: επεστησαν

Etymology: The word επεστησαν comes from the Greek verb επιστατεω (epistateo), which means "to stand over, to preside over, to be in charge of". The prefix επι- (epi-) means "over" or "upon", and the root word στατεω (stateo) means "to stand".

Grammar: επεστησαν is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is the aorist tense of the verb επιστατεω (epistateo), which means that it refers to a completed action in the past.

Description: επεστησαν can be translated to English as "they stood over" or "they presided over". It implies a sense of authority or control, as if the subject was in charge of a situation or group of people. The context in which the word is used would determine the exact meaning and connotation.
