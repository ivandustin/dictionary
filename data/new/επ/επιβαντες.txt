Word: επιβάντες

Etymology:
The word επιβάντες (epivántes) is derived from the Greek verb επιβαίνω (epivaíno), which means "to embark" or "to board". The suffix -της (-tes) is added to the verb stem to form a noun indicating the agent or doer of the action, hence επιβάντες means "those who embark" or "those who board".

Grammar:
επιβάντες is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence, i.e. the person or thing that performs the action of the verb. 

Description:
επιβάντες refers to people who are boarding a ship or other means of transportation. It can also be used more broadly to refer to passengers or travelers in general. The word has a connotation of movement and departure, as it implies that the επιβάντες are leaving one place and heading to another.
