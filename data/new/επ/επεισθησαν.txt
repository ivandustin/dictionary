Word: επεισθησαν

Etymology:
The word επεισθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) in this word means "upon" or "to", and the suffix "-σαν" (-san) indicates the past tense, third person plural form of the verb. Therefore, επεισθησαν means "they were persuaded" or "they have been convinced".

Grammar:
επεισθησαν is a past tense, third person plural form of the Greek verb πείθω (peitho). It is a passive voice verb, which means that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon. In this case, the subject is not mentioned, but it is implied that "they" were persuaded or convinced by someone or something.

Description:
επεισθησαν is a verb that describes the act of being persuaded or convinced. It implies that someone or something has influenced the subject to change their mind or opinion about something. The context in which this word is used will determine who or what is doing the persuading and what the subject is being persuaded about.
