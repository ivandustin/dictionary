Word: επιβαλλουσιν

Etymology: The word επιβαλλουσιν comes from the Greek verb επιβάλλω (epibállō), which means "to impose" or "to inflict". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb βάλλω (bállō), meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

επιβάλλω (epibállō) - I impose/inflict
επιβάλλεις (epibálleis) - you impose/inflict
επιβάλλει (epibállei) - he/she/it imposes/inflicts
επιβάλλουμεν (epibálloumen) - we impose/inflict
επιβάλλετε (epibállete) - you (plural) impose/inflict
επιβάλλουσιν (epibállousin) - they impose/inflict

Description: επιβαλλουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they impose" or "they inflict". It is often used in the context of laws, rules, or regulations that are imposed on people or situations.
