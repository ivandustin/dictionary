Word: επροφητευον

Etymology:
The word επροφητευον is derived from the Greek verb προφητεύω (prophēteuō), which means "to prophesy" or "to foretell." The prefix ε- (e-) in επροφητευον indicates the past tense, making the word mean "they prophesied" or "they foretold."

επροφητευον is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is conjugated from the verb προφητεύω (prophēteuō), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek. The verb stem is προφητευ-, and the suffix -ον (-on) indicates the third person plural form in the past tense.

Description:
επροφητευον is a Greek word that means "they prophesied" or "they foretold." It is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of prophesying or foretelling future events.
