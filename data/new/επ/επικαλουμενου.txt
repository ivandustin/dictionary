Word: επικαλουμενου

Etymology:
The word επικαλουμενου comes from the Greek verb επικαλούμαι (epikaloumai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on", and the verb καλούμαι (kaloumai), meaning "to call" or "to name".

Grammar:
επικαλουμενου is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb επικαλούμαι. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions.

Description:
επικαλουμενου can be translated to English as "calling upon" or "invoking". It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, where people call upon a deity or higher power for help or guidance. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any situation where someone is calling upon or invoking something, such as a law or a principle.
