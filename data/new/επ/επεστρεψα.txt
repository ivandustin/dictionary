The word "επεστρεψα" (epéstrapsa) is a Greek verb in the past tense, which means "I returned" in English. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistréfo), which means "to turn back" or "to return". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) means "upon" or "on", and the root "στρέφω" (stréfo) means "to turn".

Grammar: "Επεστρεψα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "επιστρέφω". In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The "-α" (-a) ending in this form indicates that the subject is singular and in the past tense.

Description: The word "επεστρεψα" is commonly used in everyday Greek language to express the action of returning to a place or situation. It can be used in various contexts, such as returning home, returning to work, or returning to a previous state of mind.
