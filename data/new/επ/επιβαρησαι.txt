Word: επιβαρησαι

Etymology: The word επιβαρησαι comes from the Greek verb επιβαρέω (epivaréo), which means "to burden" or "to weigh down". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb βαρέω (varéo), meaning "to weigh down" or "to burden".

Grammar: επιβαρησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -σαι (-sai) to the stem επιβαρη-, which is derived from the verb επιβαρέω.

Description: επιβαρησαι means "to burden oneself" or "to take on a burden". It can also mean "to impose a burden on someone else". The word is often used in a figurative sense, to describe taking on a responsibility or obligation that is difficult or challenging.
