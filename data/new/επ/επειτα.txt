Word: επειτα

Etymology:
The word επειτα (epita) is a Greek adverb that means "then" or "afterwards". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words: επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "after" and είτα (eita) meaning "then" or "next".

Grammar:
In Greek, επειτα is an adverb that is used to indicate a sequence of events. It is often used to connect two clauses or sentences, indicating that the action in the second clause or sentence follows the action in the first. It can also be used to indicate a temporal or logical relationship between two events.

Description:
επειτα is a common word in Greek and is used in both spoken and written language. It is often used in storytelling or recounting events, as it helps to create a sense of chronological order. It is also used in everyday conversation to indicate what happened next or what will happen after a certain event.
