The word επιποθησιν (epipothēsin) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb επιποθέω (epipotheō), which means "to long for" or "to desire eagerly". The word is composed of two parts: επι (epi), which means "upon" or "toward", and ποθέω (potheō), which means "to desire" or "to long for".

επιποθησιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. επιποθησιν refers to a strong desire or longing for something. It can be used to describe a deep yearning for a person, object, or experience. The word conveys a sense of intense emotion and passion. It is often used in a poetic or literary context to express a powerful feeling of longing or desire.
