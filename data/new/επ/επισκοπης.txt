Word: επισκοπης

Etymology:
The word επισκοπης comes from the Greek word επίσκοπος (episkopos), which means "overseer" or "bishop". The word is derived from the prefix επί (epi), meaning "over" or "above", and the verb σκοπέω (skopeo), meaning "to look at" or "to examine".

Grammar:
επισκοπης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. The word is feminine in gender and singular in number.

Description:
επισκοπης refers to the office or position of a bishop in the Greek Orthodox Church. It can also refer to the diocese or territory over which a bishop has jurisdiction. In modern Greek, the word can also be used to refer to a bishopric or bishop's residence.
