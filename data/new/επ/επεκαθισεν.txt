Word: επεκαθισεν

Etymology:
The word επεκαθισεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and καθίζω (kathizó) meaning "to sit". Therefore, επεκαθισεν means "he/she/it sat down upon".

Grammar:
επεκαθισεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. In English, this means that it refers to a single person or thing (he/she/it) who performed the action in the past (aorist tense) and did so actively (indicative mood).

Description:
επεκαθισεν is a verb that is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark (Mark 14:32). In this passage, Jesus and his disciples go to the Garden of Gethsemane and Jesus tells them to sit while he goes off to pray. The word επεκαθισεν is used to describe Jesus sitting down upon a rock or a spot in the garden. The word is also used in other contexts to describe someone sitting down upon a throne or a seat.
