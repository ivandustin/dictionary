Word: επιγινωσκει

Etymology:
The word επιγινωσκει (epiginōskei) is derived from the Greek words επι (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and γινωσκω (ginōskō) meaning "to know". Therefore, the word επιγινωσκει can be translated to mean "to know upon" or "to recognize".

Grammar:
επιγινωσκει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of recognizing or acknowledging something or someone.

Description:
επιγινωσκει is a powerful word that conveys the idea of truly knowing or recognizing something or someone. It implies a deep understanding and acknowledgement of the truth or reality of a situation. In the context of the Bible, it is often used to describe the act of recognizing Jesus as the Son of God and the Savior of the world.
