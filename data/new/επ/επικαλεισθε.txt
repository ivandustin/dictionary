The word "επικαλεισθε" (epikaleisthe) is a Greek verb that means "to call upon" or "to invoke." It is derived from the prefix "epi-" meaning "upon" and the verb "kaleo" meaning "to call." 

In terms of grammar, "επικαλεισθε" is a second person plural present middle/passive imperative verb. This means that it is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people (second person plural), and that the action of the verb is either being done to or by the subject (middle/passive voice).

In usage, "επικαλεισθε" can be used in a variety of contexts, such as calling upon a deity or higher power for help or guidance, invoking a law or rule in a legal context, or even calling upon someone's name in conversation.
