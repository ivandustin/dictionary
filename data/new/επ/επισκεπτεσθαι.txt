Word: επισκεπτεσθαι

Etymology: 
The word επισκεπτεσθαι comes from the Greek verb επισκέπτομαι (episkeptomai), which means "to visit" or "to inspect". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb σκέπτομαι (skeptomai), meaning "to look at" or "to consider".

Grammar:
επισκεπτεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb επισκέπτομαι (episkeptomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an action that is happening now, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, and the infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated to match a specific subject.

Description:
επισκεπτεσθαι is a Greek verb that means "to visit" or "to inspect". It can be used in various contexts, such as visiting a place, a person, or a website, or inspecting something for quality or accuracy. The present middle/passive infinitive form επισκεπτεσθαι does not specify who is doing the visiting or inspecting, but rather indicates that the action is ongoing and involves both the doer and the receiver of the action.
