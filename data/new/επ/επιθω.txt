The word "επιθω" (epitho) is a Greek verb that means "to desire" or "to long for". 

Etymology: The word comes from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), which means "on" or "upon", and the verb "θεω" (theo), which means "to run". 

Grammar: "Επιθω" is a first conjugation verb in the present tense, active voice. Its conjugation in the present tense is as follows: 

- Εγώ επιθώ (ego epitho) - I desire 
- Εσύ επιθείς (esi epithis) - You desire 
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιθεί (aftos/afti/afto epithi) - He/She/It desires 
- Εμείς επιθούμε (emeis epithoume) - We desire 
- Εσείς επιθείτε (eseis epithete) - You (plural) desire 
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά επιθούν (aftoi/aftes/afta epithoun) - They desire 

Description: "Επιθω" is a common verb in Greek and is used to express a strong desire or longing for something. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing a desire for food, a desire for success, or a desire for love.
