Word: επιλανθανεσθε

Etymology:
The word επιλανθανεσθε is derived from the Greek words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and λανθάνω (lanthano) meaning "to forget" or "to be hidden". The suffix -σθε (-sthe) is added to form the imperative mood, making the word a command to "forget" or "overlook".

Grammar:
επιλανθανεσθε is a second person plural imperative verb in Ancient Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to forget or overlook something.

Description:
The word επιλανθανεσθε is often used in a figurative sense, meaning to forget or overlook something important or significant. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, business dealings, or political situations. The word emphasizes the importance of being mindful and not forgetting important details or events.
