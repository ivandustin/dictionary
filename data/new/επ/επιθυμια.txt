Word: επιθυμία

Etymology: The word επιθυμία (epithymía) comes from the Greek root words επί (epí), meaning "upon" or "over," and θυμός (thymós), meaning "passion" or "desire." Together, they form the word επιθυμία, which means "desire" or "longing."

Grammar: In Greek, επιθυμία is a feminine noun. It is declined like other first-declension nouns, with the nominative singular form being επιθυμία and the genitive singular form being επιθυμίας.

Description: επιθυμία is a Greek word that refers to a strong desire or longing for something. It can be used to describe a wide range of desires, from physical cravings to emotional yearnings. The word is often used in a positive sense, to express a healthy desire for something good or beneficial. However, it can also be used in a negative sense, to describe an unhealthy or excessive desire for something that is harmful or destructive. Overall, επιθυμία is a powerful word that captures the intensity and complexity of human desire.
