Word: επορευθησαν

Etymology: 
The word επορευθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πορεύομαι (poreúomai), which means "to go, to travel." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a past tense, so επορευθησαν means "they went" or "they traveled."

Grammar:
επορευθησαν is a third-person plural aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, "they") performed the action.

Description:
επορευθησαν is a verb that describes an action of going or traveling that was completed in the past. It is often used in historical or narrative contexts to describe the movements of people or groups. In the New Testament, for example, it is used to describe the travels of Jesus and his disciples.
