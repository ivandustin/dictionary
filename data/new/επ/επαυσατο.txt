Word: επαυσατο

Etymology:
The word επαυσατο comes from the Greek verb "παύω" (pauo), which means "to stop" or "to cease". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is completed or finished.

Grammar:
επαυσατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb "παύω" (pauo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description:
επαυσατο can be translated to mean "he/she/it stopped" or "he/she/it ceased". It is a past tense form of the verb "παύω" (pauo), which can be used in a variety of contexts. For example, it could be used to describe someone stopping an activity, or to describe the end of a particular event or situation.
