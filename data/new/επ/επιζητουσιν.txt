Word: επιζητουσιν

Etymology: 
The word επιζητουσιν (epizētousin) is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb επιζητέω (epizēteō), which means "to seek after, to search for, to desire eagerly". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb ζητέω (zēteō), meaning "to seek, to search for".

Grammar:
επιζητουσιν is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb επιζητέω (epizēteō). It is in the third person plural, indicating that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
επιζητουσιν is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of actively seeking or searching for something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as searching for knowledge, seeking after a goal, or desiring something eagerly. The word has a sense of urgency and determination, suggesting that the subject is actively pursuing their objective.
