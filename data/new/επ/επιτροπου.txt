Word: επιτροπου

Etymology: The word επιτροπου comes from the Greek word επιτροπή (epitropi), which means "commission" or "delegation". The suffix -ου (-ou) is a genitive ending, indicating possession or association.

Grammar: επιτροπου is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or association. It is the genitive form of the word επιτροπή (epitropi).

Description: επιτροπου refers to someone who has been entrusted with a commission or delegation. It can also refer to a guardian or trustee who has been appointed to manage the affairs of another person or organization. In modern Greek, the word is often used in the context of business or politics, to refer to someone who has been appointed to oversee a particular project or task.
