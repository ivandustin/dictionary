Word: επαυριον

Etymology: 
The word επαυριον (epaurion) is derived from the Greek words επί (epi) meaning "on" or "upon" and αύριον (aurion) meaning "tomorrow". Therefore, επαυριον means "on the next day" or "tomorrow".

Grammar:
In Greek, επαυριον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is declined as follows:

Nominative: επαυριον
Genitive: επαυριου
Dative: επαυριω
Accusative: επαυριον

Description:
επαυριον is a word used to refer to the day after the current day. It is often used in a similar way to the English word "tomorrow". For example, if today is Monday, then επαυριον would refer to Tuesday. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and writing in Greek.
