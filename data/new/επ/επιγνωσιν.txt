Word: επιγνωσιν

Etymology:
The word επιγνωσιν comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and γνώσις (gnosis) meaning "knowledge" or "understanding". Therefore, επιγνωσιν can be translated as "full knowledge" or "complete understanding".

Grammar:
In Greek, επιγνωσιν is a noun in the accusative case. It is a feminine noun, and its singular form is επίγνωσις (epignosis). The word can be declined according to the case, number, and gender of the sentence.

Description:
επιγνωσιν is a term used in Christian theology to describe a deeper, more intimate knowledge of God. It is often associated with spiritual growth and maturity, and is considered to be a key component of the Christian faith. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the letters of Paul, to describe the knowledge of God that comes through faith in Jesus Christ.
