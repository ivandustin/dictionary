The word "επικεκλησαι" (epikeklēsai) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is the second person singular form of the verb "επικαλέω" (epikaleō), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke." 

The word is composed of two parts: "επι-" (epi-), which means "upon" or "over," and "καλέω" (kaleō), which means "to call." Therefore, "επικαλέω" can be translated as "to call upon" or "to invoke."

In ancient Greek, this verb was often used in religious contexts to refer to calling upon the gods or goddesses for help or protection. It could also be used in a more general sense to mean "to call upon" someone for assistance or support.

Overall, "επικεκλησαι" is a specific form of the verb "επικαλέω" that refers to a completed action of calling upon or invoking someone or something in the past.
