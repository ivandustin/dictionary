The word "επιπρασκον" (epipraskon) is a Greek verb that means "to bargain" or "to negotiate". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), which means "on" or "upon", and the verb "πρασκω" (prasko), which means "to do" or "to act". 

Grammar: "Επιπρασκον" is the present participle form of the verb, which means it describes an ongoing action. It is in the active voice and can be conjugated to match the subject of the sentence. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of business or commerce, where negotiations and bargaining are common. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe any situation where two parties are trying to come to an agreement.
