Word: επεπεσαν

Etymology:
The word επεπεσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πίπτω (pípto), which means "to fall." The prefix επ- (ep-) is added to the root word to indicate a completed action in the past tense.

Grammar:
επεπεσαν is a third-person plural past tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated from the verb πίπτω (pípto) and is used to describe an action that was completed in the past. In this case, it means "they fell."

Description:
επεπεσαν is a verb that describes an action of falling that has already happened. It can be used to describe a physical fall, such as falling off a bike, or a metaphorical fall, such as a decline in popularity or status. The word is often used in literature and poetry to create vivid imagery and convey a sense of movement or change.
