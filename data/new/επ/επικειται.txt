Word: επικείται

Etymology: The word επικείται comes from the Greek verb επικείμαι (epikeimai), which means "to be imminent" or "to be about to happen". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on", and the verb κείμαι (keimai), meaning "to lie" or "to be situated".

Grammar: επικείται is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is a passive verb, meaning that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it is implied that something is about to happen.

Description: επικείται is a word that describes a state of imminent or impending action. It is often used to indicate that something is about to happen, but has not yet occurred. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in weather reports to describe an approaching storm, or in political discussions to describe an upcoming election. The word conveys a sense of urgency or importance, as if the event in question is significant and requires attention.
