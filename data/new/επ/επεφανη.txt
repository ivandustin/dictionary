Word: επεφάνη (epiphane)

Etymology: The word επεφάνη comes from the Greek verb επιφαίνω (epiphaino), which means "to appear" or "to manifest". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb φαίνω (phaino), meaning "to shine" or "to appear".

Grammar: επεφάνη is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is used to describe a sudden appearance or manifestation of something or someone.

Description: επεφάνη is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the appearance of a divine being or a revelation. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the sudden appearance of something or someone, such as a new idea or a long-lost friend.
