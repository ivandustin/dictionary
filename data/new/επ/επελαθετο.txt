Word: επελαθετο

Etymology:
The word επελαθετο (epelatheto) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and λανθάνω (lanthano) meaning "to forget" or "to be hidden". Therefore, επελαθετο can be translated as "he forgot" or "he overlooked".

Grammar:
επελαθετο is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word λανθάνω (lanthano) and the prefix επί (epi). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
επελαθετο is a verb that describes the act of forgetting or overlooking something. It can be used in various contexts, such as forgetting a task, overlooking a detail, or neglecting a responsibility. The word carries a sense of negligence or carelessness, and it is often used in a negative context.
