Word: εποιησαν

Etymology:
The word εποιησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εποιησαν (epoiésan) means "they made" or "they did".

Grammar:
εποιησαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb ποιέω (poiéo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The -αν (-an) ending indicates the past tense, and the ε- (e-) prefix indicates the third person plural. 

Description:
εποιησαν is a verb that describes an action that was completed in the past. It can be translated as "they made" or "they did". The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning.
