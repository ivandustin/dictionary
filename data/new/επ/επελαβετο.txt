Word: επελαβετο

Etymology:
The word επελαβετο comes from the Greek verb επιλαμβάνομαι (epilambanomai), which means "to take hold of" or "to seize". The prefix επι- (epi-) means "upon" or "on top of", while the root λαμβάνω (lambano) means "to take" or "to receive". The verb επελαβετο is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
επελαβετο is a third-person singular form of the aorist tense in the middle voice. In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The verb is also in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
επελαβετο can be translated to English as "he/she/it took hold of" or "he/she/it seized". The word implies a sudden and forceful action, as if the subject grabbed something quickly and decisively. The middle voice suggests that the subject was both the initiator and the recipient of the action, perhaps indicating a sense of agency or control. Overall, επελαβετο is a strong and dynamic verb that conveys a sense of action and power.
