Word: επιλαβεσθαι

Etymology:
The word επιλαβεσθαι comes from the Greek verb επιλαμβάνω (epilambanō), which means "to take hold of" or "to seize". It is formed from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), meaning "to take".

Grammar:
επιλαβεσθαι is a verb in the aorist tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit. The infinitive mood is used to express the idea of an action without specifying the subject or tense.

Description:
The word επιλαβεσθαι is often translated as "to take hold of" or "to seize". It can also mean "to grasp", "to catch", or "to lay hold of". In context, it can refer to physically taking hold of something, as well as figuratively taking control of a situation or assuming responsibility for a task.
