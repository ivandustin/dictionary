Word: επιβαλλει

Etymology: The word επιβαλλει comes from the Greek verb επιβάλλω (epivállo), which means "to impose" or "to enforce". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb βάλλω (vállo), meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar: επιβαλλει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb επιβάλλω (epivállo), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: επιβαλλει means "imposes" or "enforces" in English. It is often used in the context of laws, rules, or regulations that are being enforced or imposed upon someone or something. For example, "Ο νόμος επιβάλλει πρόστιμα σε όσους παραβιάζουν τους κανονισμούς" (The law imposes fines on those who violate the regulations).
