Word: επεβαλλεν

The word επεβαλλεν comes from the Greek verb "ἐπιβάλλω" (epibállō), which means "to impose" or "to inflict". It is derived from the prefix "ἐπι-" (epi-), which means "on" or "upon", and the verb "βάλλω" (bállō), which means "to throw" or "to cast".

επεβαλλεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "ἐπιβάλλω" (epibállō) in Ancient Greek. It is a regular verb that follows the first conjugation pattern. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
επεβαλλεν means "he/she/it imposed" or "he/she/it inflicted" in English. It is a verb that describes the act of forcing something upon someone or something else.
