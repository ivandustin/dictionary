Word: επαγγελίας

Etymology:
The word επαγγελίας (epangelías) comes from the Greek verb επαγγέλλομαι (epangéllomai), which means "to promise" or "to pledge". The noun form επαγγελία (epangelía) means "promise" or "pledge".

Grammar:
επαγγελίας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
επαγγελίας is a word that refers to a promise or pledge. It can be used in various contexts, such as a promise made by a person to another person, a promise made by a company to its customers, or a promise made by a government to its citizens. The word implies a commitment to fulfill a certain obligation or expectation.
