Word: εποιησεν

Etymology: εποιησεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix επι- (epi-) adds the meaning of "completely" or "thoroughly", so εποιησεν can be translated as "he made completely" or "he did thoroughly".

Grammar: εποιησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb ποιέω (poiéo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: εποιησεν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 2:11. It is used to describe the miracle of Jesus turning water into wine at the wedding in Cana. The word emphasizes the completeness and thoroughness of the miracle, indicating that Jesus did not just change the water into wine, but did so completely and thoroughly.
