Word: επιταγης

Etymology:
The word επιταγης comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "on" or "upon" and τάγη (tagi) meaning "order" or "command". Therefore, επιταγης literally means "upon order" or "commanded".

Grammar:
επιταγης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the feminine gender and is in the genitive case. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
επιταγης refers to a written order or command, usually in the form of a financial document such as a check or a bill. It is commonly used in the context of banking and finance. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any type of written order or command.
