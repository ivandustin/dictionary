Word: επεμενον

Etymology:
The word επεμενον is derived from the Greek verb επιμένω (epiméno), which means "to persist" or "to persevere". It is formed from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb μένω (méno), meaning "to remain" or "to stay".

Grammar:
επεμενον is the past tense form of the verb επιμένω in the third person plural. It is used to describe an action that was persistently carried out in the past.

Description:
επεμενον can be translated to English as "they persisted" or "they persevered". It implies that a group of people continued to do something despite difficulties or obstacles. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in sports, business, or personal relationships.
