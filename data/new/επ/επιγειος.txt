Word: επιγειος

Etymology:
The word επιγειος (epigeios) comes from the Greek words επί (epi) meaning "on" or "upon" and γη (ge) meaning "earth" or "land". Therefore, the word επιγειος means "on the earth" or "pertaining to the earth".

επιγειος is an adjective in the Greek language. It is declined according to gender, number, and case. For example, in the nominative case, the masculine form is επιγειος, the feminine form is επιγεια, and the neuter form is επιγειο.

Description:
The word επιγειος is used to describe things that are related to or exist on the earth. It can refer to physical objects such as plants, animals, and rocks, as well as abstract concepts such as culture, society, and politics. In modern usage, the word is often used to describe environmental issues and concerns, such as the impact of human activity on the earth's ecosystems.
