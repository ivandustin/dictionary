The word "επιστας" (epistas) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "knowing" or "having knowledge". It is derived from the Greek verb "επίσταμαι" (epistamai), which means "to know" or "to understand". 

In terms of grammar, "επιστας" is a present participle, which is a verb form that describes an ongoing action or state. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ντ-" to the stem of the verb and then adding the appropriate endings. 

Example: "Επιστας την αλήθεια" (Epistas tin alithia) means "knowing the truth".

Overall, "επιστας" is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe someone who has knowledge or understanding of a particular subject.
