Word: επιτιμησαι

Etymology: The word επιτιμησαι comes from the Greek verb επιτιμαω (epitimao), which means "to rebuke, to reprimand, to censure". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), which means "upon" or "over", and the verb τιμαω (timao), which means "to honor, to value, to esteem".

Grammar: επιτιμησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb επιτιμαω (epitimao), which means "you rebuke" or "you reprimand".

Description: επιτιμησαι is a strong and forceful word that conveys a sense of rebuke or reprimand. It is often used in the context of correcting someone's behavior or actions, and can be translated as "to rebuke", "to reprimand", or "to censure".
