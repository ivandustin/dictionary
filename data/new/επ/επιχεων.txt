The word "επιχεων" (epicheon) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb "επιχεω" (epicheo), which means "to pour upon" or "to overflow." It is a compound word, consisting of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon," and the verb "χεω" (cheo), meaning "to pour." 

In terms of grammar, "επιχεων" is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive "επιχεωνος" (epicheonos) or the accusative "επιχεωνα" (epicheona).

As for its description, "επιχεων" can refer to a person or thing that pours or overflows onto something else. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is abundant or overflowing with a particular quality or emotion.
