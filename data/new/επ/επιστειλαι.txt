The word "επιστειλαι" (episteilai) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "επιστέλλω" (epistello), which means "to command" or "to order". 

In terms of grammar, "επιστειλαι" is a second person singular aorist imperative form of the verb "επιστέλλω". This means that it is a command directed towards one person, and it is in the past tense. 

Overall, the word "επιστειλαι" can be translated to mean "you commanded" or "you ordered" in English.
