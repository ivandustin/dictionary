The word "επιλελησμένον" is a Greek adjective that means "forgiven" or "pardoned". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the verb "λελησμένος" (lelēsmenos), which means "released" or "freed". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) adds the meaning of "completely" or "thoroughly", so "επιλελησμένον" (epilelēsmenon) can be translated as "thoroughly released" or "completely freed".

Grammar: "Επιλελησμένον" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: The word "επιλελησμένον" is often used in a religious context to describe the forgiveness of sins. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe being pardoned or forgiven for any wrongdoing.
