The word "επετρεψεν" (epetrepsen) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." 

The verb "επετρεψεν" is often translated as "allowed" or "permitted," and it is used to indicate that someone gave permission for something to happen. It can also be translated as "granted" or "authorized." 

In terms of grammar, "επετρεψεν" is a third-person singular verb, which means it refers to a single person or thing that performed the action. It is also in the indicative mood, which means it is a statement of fact. 

Overall, "επετρεψεν" is a versatile verb that is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday conversation to express permission or authorization.
