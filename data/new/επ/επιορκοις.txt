The word "επιορκοις" (epiorkois) is a Greek term that means "perjurers" or "those who swear falsely." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "epi-" meaning "upon" or "over," and the noun "orkos" meaning "oath" or "sworn statement." In Greek grammar, "επιορκοις" is a plural noun in the dative case. It is often used in legal contexts to refer to individuals who have violated their oaths or sworn statements.
