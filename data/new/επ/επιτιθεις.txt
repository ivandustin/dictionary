Word: επιτιθεις

Etymology:
The word επιτιθεις (epititheis) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "on" and τίθημι (tithemi) meaning "to place" or "to put". Therefore, επιτιθεις can be translated as "to place upon" or "to put on".

Grammar:
επιτιθεις is a second person singular present active participle of the Greek verb επιτίθημι (epitithemi). It is used to describe someone who is currently attacking or assaulting someone or something.

Description:
επιτιθεις is a strong and forceful word that is often used in situations where someone is physically or verbally attacking another person or group. It can also be used in a more figurative sense to describe someone who is aggressively pursuing a goal or objective. In either case, the word conveys a sense of intensity and urgency, and is often associated with conflict and confrontation.
