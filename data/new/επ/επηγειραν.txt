Word: επηγείραν

Etymology: The word επηγείραν comes from the Greek verb επανεγείρω (epanegéiro), which means "to raise up again" or "to revive". It is formed by combining the prefix επι- (epi-), which means "upon" or "over", and the verb ανεγείρω (anegéiro), which means "to raise up" or "to lift".

Grammar: επηγείραν is the third person plural, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb επανεγείρω (epanegéiro). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: επηγείραν means "they raised up again" or "they revived" in English. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the restoration of something that was lost or forgotten. For example, it can be used to describe the revival of a tradition or the resurrection of a person from the dead.
