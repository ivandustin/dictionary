Word: επιστευσα

Etymology: 
The word επιστευσα (episteusa) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The prefix επι- (epi-) means "upon" or "on", so επιστευσα can be translated as "I believed upon" or "I put my faith in".

Grammar:
επιστευσα is the first person singular aorist tense of the Greek verb πιστεύω (pisteuo). The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. Therefore, επιστευσα can be translated as "I believed" or "I had faith".

Description:
επιστευσα is a word that expresses an act of faith or belief. It is often used in a religious context to describe a person's belief in God or a higher power. In the New Testament of the Bible, επιστευσα is used to describe the act of believing in Jesus Christ as the Son of God and the Savior of the world. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe a person's trust or confidence in someone or something.
