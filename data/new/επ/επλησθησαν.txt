Word: επλησθησαν

Etymology:
The word επλησθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to be filled." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a completed action, so επλησθησαν means "they were filled" or "they became full."

Grammar:
επλησθησαν is a third-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
επλησθησαν is a verb that describes a state of being filled or becoming full. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to a container being filled with a substance, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing a person being filled with emotion or a group of people being filled with a sense of satisfaction or contentment. The word is often used in religious contexts, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the filling of the Holy Spirit.
