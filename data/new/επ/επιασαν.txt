Word: επιασαν

Etymology: The word επιασαν comes from the Greek verb "πιάνω" (piáno), which means "to catch" or "to seize". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) adds emphasis to the action of catching or seizing.

Grammar: επιασαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "πιάνω" (piáno). It is a regular verb in Greek, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-άνω" (-áno).

Description: επιασαν is a verb that describes the action of catching or seizing something. It can be used in various contexts, such as catching a ball, seizing an opportunity, or apprehending a criminal. In Greek language, it is a common verb used in everyday conversation and writing.
