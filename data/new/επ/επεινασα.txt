Word: επεινασα

Etymology: The word επεινασα comes from the Greek verb πεινάω (peináo), which means "to be hungry". The prefix επ- (ep-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense, making επεινασα (epína̱sa) mean "I was hungry".

Grammar: επεινασα is a first-person singular past tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb πεινάω (peináo) in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: επεινασα is a Greek verb that describes the state of being hungry in the past. It can be used to indicate that someone was hungry at a specific point in time, or for a period of time in the past. For example, "Επεινασα πολύ χθες το βράδυ" (Epína̱sa polý chthes to vrádi) means "I was very hungry last night".
