The word επεμενεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "μενω" (meno), which means "to remain" or "to stay". The prefix "επι" (epi) means "upon" or "on", so επεμενεν can be translated as "to remain upon" or "to persist".

επεμενεν is a third-person singular past tense verb in the Greek language. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated from the root word "μενω" (meno) with the augment "επι" (epi) added to the beginning of the word.

The word επεμενεν describes the act of persisting or remaining steadfast in a particular course of action or belief. It can also refer to someone who is determined to continue doing something despite obstacles or opposition. In English, it can be translated as "persisted" or "persevered".
