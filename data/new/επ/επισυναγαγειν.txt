Word: επισυναγαγειν

Etymology:
The word επισυναγαγειν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb συναγαγειν (synagagein), meaning "to gather together". Therefore, επισυναγαγειν means "to gather together upon" or "to collect together over".

Grammar:
επισυναγαγειν is a verb in the Greek language, in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is derived from the root verb συναγαγειν (synagagein), which means "to gather together". The prefix επι- (epi-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "over" to the verb.

Description:
επισυναγαγειν is a Greek verb that means "to gather together upon" or "to collect together over". It is often used in a figurative sense, to describe the gathering or collection of ideas, thoughts, or people. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in religious or political gatherings, or in the collection of data or information.
