Word: επιτιμησον

Etymology: The word επιτιμησον comes from the Greek verb επιτιμαω (epitimao), which means "to rebuke" or "to reprimand". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb τιμαω (timao), meaning "to honor" or "to value".

Grammar: επιτιμησον is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular, active voice. It is the aorist imperative form of the verb επιτιμαω (epitimao).

Description: The word επιτιμησον is a command or request to someone to rebuke or reprimand someone else. It is often used in a religious or moral context, such as in the Bible, where it is used to urge people to correct the behavior of others who are straying from the path of righteousness. The word can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to admonish" or "to warn".
