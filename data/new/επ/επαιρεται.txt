The word "επαιρεται" (epairetai) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the root word "αιρω" (airo), which means "to lift" or "to take away". The prefix "επι" (epi) means "upon" or "over", so "επαιρεται" can be translated as "lifted up" or "taken upon oneself".

Grammar: "Επαιρεται" is a third person singular present passive indicative form of the verb "αιρω" (airo). It is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of the verb. 

Description: The word "επαιρεται" can be translated as "is lifted up" or "is taken upon oneself". It can be used in a literal sense, such as lifting a heavy object, or in a figurative sense, such as taking on a responsibility or burden.
