Word: επεισαν

Etymology:
The word επεισαν (epeisan) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb επείθω (epeitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The word has its roots in the ancient Greek language and has been used in various forms throughout history.

Grammar:
As mentioned earlier, επεισαν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a past action that is completed. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
The word επεισαν is used to describe the act of persuading or convincing someone to do something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, business negotiations, or political discussions. The word implies that the person doing the persuading has successfully convinced the other person to take a certain action or adopt a certain belief.
