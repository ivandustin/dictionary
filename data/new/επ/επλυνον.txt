Word: επλυνον

Etymology: The word επλυνον is derived from the Greek verb πλύνω (plýno), which means "to wash". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: επλυνον is the past tense form of the verb πλύνω (plýno) in the third person plural. It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past. In Greek, verbs change their form depending on the tense, person, and number of the subject.

Description: επλυνον can be translated to English as "they washed". It is a simple past tense verb that describes an action that was completed in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people washing dishes, clothes, or even themselves.
