The word "επιγειων" is a Greek adjective that means "terrestrial" or "of the earth". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon", and "γη" (ge), meaning "earth".

Grammar: "Επιγειων" is in the masculine, feminine, and neuter gender, and can be used as a singular or plural noun. It is declined like a regular first-declension adjective in Greek.

Description: The word "επιγειων" is often used to describe things that are related to or found on the earth's surface, such as plants, animals, and geological formations. It can also be used metaphorically to describe things that are grounded or practical, as opposed to abstract or theoretical.
