Word: επλεονάσεν

Etymology:
The word επλεονάσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πλεονάζω (pleonazo), which means "to be more abundant" or "to increase." The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate the past tense, making επλεονάσεν (epleonasen) mean "he/she/it increased" or "he/she/it became more abundant."

Grammar:
επλεονάσεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
επλεονάσεν is a verb that describes an increase or abundance of something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the growth of a plant, the accumulation of wealth, or the expansion of a business. The word has a positive connotation, suggesting that the increase is desirable or beneficial.
