The word "επιστευθη" is a Greek verb that means "believed" in English. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "πιστεύω" (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) in this case means "upon" or "in addition to".

Grammar: "Επιστευθη" is the third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb "πιστεύω" (pisteuo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: "Επιστευθη" is a word that expresses the idea of belief or faith in a past context. It can be used to describe someone who was believed or trusted by others, or to express one's own belief in something that has already happened.
