Word: επάνω

Etymology: The word επάνω (epáno) comes from the ancient Greek word ἐπάνω (epánō), which means "above" or "on top of". It is derived from the prefix ἐπί (epí), meaning "on" or "upon", and the adverb ἄνω (ánō), meaning "up" or "above".

Grammar: επάνω is an adverb in Greek, which means it modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is often used to indicate location or direction, and can be translated as "above", "on top of", or "up".

Description: επάνω is a versatile word in Greek, used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical location, as in "the book is on top of the table" (το βιβλίο είναι επάνω στο τραπέζι). It can also be used to describe direction, as in "go up the stairs" (πηγαίνεις επάνω στις σκάλες). Additionally, it can be used in idiomatic expressions, such as "on top of everything" (επάνω σε όλα), which means "in addition to everything else".
