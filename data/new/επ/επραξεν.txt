Word: επραξεν

Etymology: επραξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πράττω (pratto), which means "to do" or "to act". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so επραξεν means "he/she/it did" or "he/she/it acted".

Grammar: επραξεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb πράττω (pratto). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The -εν (-en) ending indicates the third person singular past tense.

Description: επραξεν is a verb that describes an action that was completed in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it did" or "he/she/it acted". The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning.
