Word: επαγγελίαν

Etymology:
The word επαγγελίαν (epangelían) comes from the Greek verb επαγγέλλω (epangéllo), which means "to promise" or "to pledge". The word is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb αγγέλλω (angéllo), meaning "to announce" or "to proclaim".

Grammar:
επαγγελίαν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the noun that is directly affected by the action of the verb.

Description:
επαγγελίαν refers to a promise or a pledge. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as a promise made by a person to another person, a promise made by a company to its customers, or a promise made by a government to its citizens. The word implies a commitment to fulfill a certain obligation or to achieve a certain goal.
