The word "επιγνοντες" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "knowing" or "recognizing". It is derived from the root word "γινώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "in addition to", giving the word a sense of deeper understanding or recognition. 

In terms of grammar, "επιγνοντες" is a present participle verb, which means it describes an ongoing action or state of being. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ντ" (-nt) to the stem of the verb, and then adding the appropriate ending for the subject. 

For example, in the sentence "Επιγνωντες την αλήθεια, οι μαθητές έγιναν ελεύθεροι" (Epignontes tin alitheia, oi mathites eginan eleftheroi), which means "Knowing the truth, the disciples became free", "επιγνωντες" is the present participle form of the verb "γινώσκω" (ginosko), and it describes the ongoing action of the disciples knowing the truth. 

Overall, "επιγνοντες" is a versatile and important word in Greek, conveying the idea of deep understanding and recognition.
