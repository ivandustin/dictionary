The word επιθυμων (epithymon) is derived from the Greek verb επιθυμεω (epithymeō), which means "to desire" or "to long for". It is formed by combining the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb θυμος (thymos), meaning "passion" or "desire".

επιθυμων is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb επιθυμεω (epithymeō), which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense. The participle form is often used to describe a continuous action or state.
