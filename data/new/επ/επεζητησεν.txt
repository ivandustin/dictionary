Word: επεζητησεν

Etymology: The word επεζητησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix επι- (epi-) meaning "upon" or "over" and the verb ζητεω (zeteo) meaning "to seek" or "to search". Therefore, επεζητησεν means "he/she/it searched for" or "he/she/it sought after".

Grammar: επεζητησεν is a third person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: επεζητησεν is a verb that describes the action of searching or seeking after something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as searching for a lost item, seeking knowledge or information, or pursuing a goal or objective. The word has a sense of purpose and determination, as the subject is actively seeking something out.
