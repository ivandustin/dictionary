Word: επιμενειν

Etymology:
The word επιμενειν comes from the Greek verb επιμένω (epiméno), which means "to persist" or "to persevere". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb μένω (meno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar:
επιμενειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, verbs are inflected to indicate tense, voice, mood, person, and number. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action, and the infinitive mood indicates that the verb is not conjugated to agree with a specific subject.

Description:
επιμενειν means "to persist" or "to persevere" in Greek. It is often used to describe someone who continues to pursue a goal or objective despite obstacles or difficulties. The word can also be used to describe someone who is stubborn or unyielding in their beliefs or opinions.
