Word: επερισσευσαν

Etymology: The word επερισσευσαν comes from the Greek verb επερισσεύω (eperissevo), which means "to abound, to be in excess". It is formed by adding the prefix επι- (epi-) which means "over, above" to the verb περισσεύω (perissevo) which means "to be abundant, to be in excess".

Grammar: επερισσευσαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb επερισσεύω (eperissevo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: επερισσευσαν means "they abounded, they were in excess" in English. It describes a past action where the subject was in a state of abundance or excess. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a surplus of food, money, or other resources.
