Word: επιτρέπω

Etymology:
The word επιτρέπω (epitrepó) comes from the Greek roots επί (epí), meaning "on" or "upon," and τρέπω (trépo), meaning "to turn" or "to direct." Together, επιτρέπω means "to allow" or "to permit."

Grammar:
επιτρέπω is a verb in the Greek language. It is a first-conjugation verb, meaning that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs that end in -ω. In the present tense, it is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ επιτρέπω (egó epitrepó) - I allow
- Εσύ επιτρέπεις (esý epitrepís) - You allow
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιτρέπει (aftós/aftí/aftó epitrepí) - He/She/It allows
- Εμείς επιτρέπουμε (emeís epitrepoume) - We allow
- Εσείς επιτρέπετε (eseís epitrepéte) - You allow
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά επιτρέπουν (aftoí/aftés/aftá epitrepoun) - They allow

Description:
επιτρέπω is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to indicate permission or authorization, as in "Μου επέτρεψε να φύγω" (Mou epétrepse na fýgo) - "He allowed me to leave." It can also be used to indicate the allowance of a certain action or behavior, as in "Δεν επιτρέπεται ο καπνός στο εσωτερικό του κτιρίου" (Den epitrepéte o kapnós sto esoterikó tou ktiríou) - "Smoking is not allowed inside the building." Additionally, επιτρέπω can be used in a more figurative sense to indicate the acceptance or approval of something, as in "Η κατάσταση επιτρέπει τη χρήση αυτής της λύσης" (I katástasi epitrepí ti chrísi aftís tis lýsis) - "The situation allows for the use of this solution."
