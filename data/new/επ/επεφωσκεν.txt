The word "επεφωσκεν" is a Greek verb that means "to dawn" or "to become light". It is derived from the root word "φως" (phos), which means "light". In terms of grammar, it is a perfect tense verb in the third person singular form. The perfect tense indicates that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The verb is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe the beginning of a new era or the start of a new day.
