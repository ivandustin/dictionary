Word: επνευσαν

Etymology: The word επνευσαν comes from the Greek verb πνέω (pnéo), which means "to breathe". The prefix επ- (ep-) means "upon" or "on", so επνευσαν can be translated as "they breathed upon" or "they breathed on".

Grammar: επνευσαν is a verb in the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood, and active voice. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: επνευσαν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in John 20:22. In this passage, Jesus appears to his disciples after his resurrection and breathes on them, saying "Receive the Holy Spirit". The verb επνευσαν is used to describe this action of Jesus breathing on his disciples.
