The word "επιγραφην" (epigraphen) is a Greek noun that means "inscription" or "writing on a surface". It is derived from the verb "επιγράφω" (epigrafo), which means "to inscribe" or "to write on". 

In terms of grammar, "επιγραφην" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Examples of usage include: "Η επιγραφή στο μνημείο ήταν σε αρχαία ελληνικά" (The inscription on the monument was in ancient Greek) and "Η επιγραφή στην πόρτα έλεγε 'Μην ενοχλείτε'" (The writing on the door said 'Do not disturb').
