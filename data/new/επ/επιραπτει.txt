The word "επιραπτει" is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be broken down into two parts: "επι-" which means "on" or "upon" and "ράπτω" which means "to sew". Therefore, "επιραπτει" can be translated to "sew on" or "attach".

In terms of grammar, "επιραπτει" is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It can be conjugated to match the subject of the sentence, for example:

- Εγώ επιράβω (I sew on)
- Εσύ επιράβεις (You sew on)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιράβει (He/She/It sews on)
- Εμείς επιράβουμε (We sew on)
- Εσείς επιράβετε (You all sew on)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επιράβουν (They sew on)

In terms of description, "επιραπτει" is a verb that describes the action of attaching or sewing something onto another object. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as sewing on a button, attaching a patch to a jacket, or even figuratively, such as attaching blame to someone.
