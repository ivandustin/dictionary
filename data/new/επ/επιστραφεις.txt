The word "επιστραφεις" is a Greek verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It comes from the root word "επιστρέφω" which means "to turn back" or "to return". The verb form "επιστραφεις" specifically means "you are returning" or "you are coming back". 

In terms of grammar, "επιστραφεις" is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω". It is also in the active voice and indicative mood, indicating a statement of fact.

Overall, "επιστραφεις" is a simple and straightforward verb that describes the action of returning or coming back.
