Word: επωλησεν

Etymology: The word επωλησεν comes from the Greek verb πωλέω (poleo), which means "to sell". The prefix επι (epi) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate completion or accomplishment of the action. Therefore, επωλησεν means "he/she/it sold".

Grammar: επωλησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb πωλέω (poleo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: επωλησεν is a verb that describes the action of selling something. It is in the past tense, indicating that the action has already been completed. The subject of the sentence is the one who performed the action of selling. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Ο πωλητής επωλησεν τα προϊόντα του" (The salesman sold his products).
