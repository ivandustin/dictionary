Word: επετυχον

Etymology:
The word επετυχον (epetychon) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τυγχάνω (tygchano), which means "to happen" or "to occur". The prefix επ- (ep-) is added to the root word to indicate a successful or positive outcome. Therefore, επετυχον (epetychon) means "I succeeded" or "I achieved".

Grammar:
επετυχον (epetychon) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb επιτυγχάνω (epitygchano), which means "to succeed" or "to achieve". The verb is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description:
επετυχον (epetychon) is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of success or achievement. It is often used to describe a positive outcome or accomplishment. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal achievements, academic or professional accomplishments, or even in sports or other competitive activities.
