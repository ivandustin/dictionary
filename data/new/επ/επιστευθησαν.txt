Word: επιστευθησαν

Etymology: 
The word επιστευθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The prefix επι- (epi-) means "upon" or "on", so επιστευθησαν can be translated as "they believed upon" or "they had faith in".

Grammar:
επιστευθησαν is the third person plural aorist passive indicative form of the verb πιστεύω (pisteuo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
επιστευθησαν means "they were believed" or "they had faith in" in English. The word is often used in a religious context to describe the act of believing in God or a higher power.
