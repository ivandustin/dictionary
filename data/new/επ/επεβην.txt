Word: επεβην

Etymology:
The word επεβην (epévēn) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βαίνω (váino), which means "to walk" or "to step." The prefix επι- (epi-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "on top of," so επεβην (epévēn) can be translated as "I stepped upon" or "I mounted."

Grammar:
επεβην (epévēn) is a first-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (in this case, "I") is performing the action.

Description:
επεβην (epévēn) is a verb that describes the action of stepping upon or mounting something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as stepping onto a platform or mounting a horse, or in a figurative sense, such as taking on a new responsibility or challenge. As a first-person singular verb, it specifically refers to the action of the speaker.
