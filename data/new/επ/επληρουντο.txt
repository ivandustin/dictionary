Word: επληρουντο

Etymology: επληρουντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a past tense form.

Grammar: επληρουντο is a third-person plural past tense form of the verb πληρόω (plēróō). It is in the passive voice, indicating that the action was done to the subject rather than by the subject. The tense indicates that the action was completed in the past.

Description: επληρουντο can be translated to English as "they were being filled" or "they were being completed". The context of the sentence would determine the exact meaning. The passive voice suggests that the subject was not actively filling or completing something, but rather that something was being done to them.
