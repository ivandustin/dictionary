The word "επενδυτην" (ependytin) is a Greek noun that means "investor" in English. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the verb "επενδύω" (ependyo), which means "to invest" or "to put money into something". The suffix "-την" (-tin) is added to the verb stem to form the noun.

Grammar: "Επενδυτην" is a masculine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and number (singular or plural). 

Description: An "επενδυτην" is a person who invests money in a business or a financial venture with the expectation of making a profit. They may invest in stocks, bonds, real estate, or other assets. The role of an investor is important in the economy as they provide the necessary capital for businesses to grow and create jobs.
