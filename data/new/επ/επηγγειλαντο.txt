Word: επηγγειλαντο

Etymology:
The word επηγγειλαντο (epēngeilanto) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and αγγέλλω (angellō) meaning "to announce" or "to declare". The prefix επί (epi) adds emphasis to the action of announcing or declaring.

Grammar:
επηγγειλαντο (epēngeilanto) is a third-person plural aorist middle voice verb in ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, the subject is plural, meaning that the action was performed by more than one person.

Description:
The word επηγγειλαντο (epēngeilanto) means "they promised" in English. It is a verb that describes an action of making a commitment or assurance to do something in the future.
