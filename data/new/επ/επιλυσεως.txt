Word: επιλυσεως

Etymology: The word επιλυσεως comes from the Greek verb επιλύω (epilýo), which means "to solve" or "to resolve". The suffix -σεως (-seos) is a genitive singular ending that indicates possession or relation.

Grammar: επιλυσεως is a noun in the genitive singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relation, and it usually answers the question "of what?" or "whose?".

Description: επιλυσεως refers to the act of solving or resolving a problem or issue. It can also refer to the result of this action, i.e. the solution or resolution itself. The word is often used in technical or academic contexts, such as mathematics, physics, or engineering, where problem-solving is a key skill.
