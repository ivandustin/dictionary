The word "επισκεψεται" (episkeptetai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "επίσκεψη" (episkepsi), which means "visit" or "inspection". 

In terms of grammar, "επισκεψεται" is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" in the present tense. It is also a transitive verb, meaning it requires a direct object to complete its meaning. 

As for its meaning, "επισκεψεται" refers to the act of visiting or inspecting someone or something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as visiting a friend, inspecting a building, or checking on the progress of a project. 

Overall, "επισκεψεται" is a versatile verb that is commonly used in everyday Greek conversation.
