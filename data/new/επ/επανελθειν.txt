Word: επανελθειν

Etymology:
The word επανελθειν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of the prefix επαν- (meaning "again" or "re-") and the verb ελθειν (meaning "to come"). Therefore, επανελθειν means "to come again" or "to return".

Grammar:
επανελθειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific tense, person, or number. It is often used in combination with auxiliary verbs to form compound tenses, such as the perfect or future perfect.

Description:
επανελθειν is a common verb in Greek, used to express the idea of returning or coming back to a place or situation. It can be used in a literal sense, such as returning to a physical location, or in a figurative sense, such as returning to a previous state of mind or emotion. It is often used in everyday conversation and writing.
