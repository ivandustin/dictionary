Word: επεχειρουν

Etymology:
The word επεχειρουν (epecheiroun) is a Greek verb that comes from the root words επί (epi) meaning "on" or "upon" and χείρ (cheir) meaning "hand". The verb form επεχειρουν is in the present tense and active voice, and it means "they undertake" or "they attempt".

Grammar:
επεχειρουν is a third-person plural verb in the present tense and active voice. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, voice, and mood. The present tense is used to describe actions that are happening now or are ongoing. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
επεχειρουν is a verb that describes the action of undertaking or attempting something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in business, politics, or personal endeavors. The word implies a sense of initiative and determination, as those who undertake something are actively working towards a goal.
