Word: επικαλεσασθαι

Etymology: The word επικαλεσασθαι comes from the Greek roots επί (epi), meaning "upon" or "on", and καλέω (kaleo), meaning "to call". Together, επικαλεσασθαι means "to call upon" or "to invoke".

Grammar: επικαλεσασθαι is a verb in the aorist middle infinitive form. It is derived from the verb καλέω (kaleo), which means "to call". The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: επικαλεσασθαι is a Greek verb that means "to call upon" or "to invoke". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, where it refers to calling upon a deity or supernatural power for help or guidance. The word can also be used in a more general sense, to refer to calling upon someone or something for assistance or support.
