Word: επιλαθεσθαι

Etymology:
The word επιλαθεσθαι comes from the Greek roots επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and λαμβανω (lambano), meaning "to take". The prefix επι- adds emphasis to the verb λαμβανω, creating the meaning "to take upon oneself" or "to take over".

Grammar:
επιλαθεσθαι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. In English, this verb is often translated as "to forget" or "to neglect".

Description:
επιλαθεσθαι is a verb that describes the act of forgetting or neglecting something. It can be used to describe forgetting something important, such as a task or responsibility, or neglecting something that requires attention, such as a relationship or a duty. The word carries a sense of culpability, suggesting that the subject should have remembered or attended to the matter in question.
