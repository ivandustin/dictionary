The word "επισταται" (epistatai) is a Greek noun in the plural form. It comes from the verb "επιστατεω" (epistateo), which means "to oversee" or "to preside over." 

In ancient Greece, an "epistates" was a magistrate or official who presided over a particular area or group of people. The term was also used in the context of religious ceremonies, where an "epistates" would oversee the proceedings.

In terms of grammar, "επισταται" is a third-person plural noun in the nominative case. It can be translated as "overseers" or "presiders."

Overall, "επισταται" is a word that conveys a sense of authority and leadership, particularly in the context of ancient Greek society.
