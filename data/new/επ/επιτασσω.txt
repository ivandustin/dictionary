Word: επιτασσω

Etymology:
The word επιτασσω (epitasso) comes from the Greek roots επί (epi), meaning "upon" or "over," and τάσσω (tasso), meaning "to arrange" or "to put in order." Together, επιτασσω means "to command" or "to order."

Grammar:
επιτασσω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The conjugation of επιτασσω in the present tense is as follows:

- Εγώ επιτάσσω (ego epitasso) - I command
- Εσύ επιτάσσεις (esi epitasseis) - You command
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιτάσσει (aftos/afti/afto epitassei) - He/She/It commands
- Εμείς επιτάσσουμε (emeis epitassoume) - We command
- Εσείς επιτάσσετε (eseis epitassete) - You (plural) command
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επιτάσσουν (aftoi/aftes epitassoun) - They command

Description:
επιτασσω is a strong and authoritative word that is used to express a command or an order. It is often used in military or hierarchical contexts, but can also be used in everyday situations to express a strong and direct request. The word carries a sense of urgency and importance, and is often used to convey a sense of power or authority.
