Word: επιπεσων

Etymology:
The word επιπεσων comes from the Greek verb επιπίπτω (epipípto), which means "to fall upon" or "to attack". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on top of", and the verb πίπτω (pípto), meaning "to fall".

Grammar:
επιπεσων is the present participle of the verb επιπίπτω (epipípto), which means "falling upon" or "attacking". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
επιπεσων is a word that describes someone or something that is currently in the act of falling upon or attacking. It can be used to describe a physical attack, such as a person falling upon another person, or a metaphorical attack, such as a sudden onset of illness or a financial crisis. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any sudden or unexpected event that has a significant impact.
