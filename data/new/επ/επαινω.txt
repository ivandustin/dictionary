The word "επαινω" (epaino) is a Greek verb that means "to praise" or "to commend". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "επαινεω" (epaineo), which means "to praise" or "to extol". 

Grammar: "Επαινω" is a first-conjugation verb in Greek, meaning that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in "-ω". It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Εγώ επαινώ (ego epaino) - I praise 
- Εσύ επαινείς (esi epaineis) - You praise 
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επαινεί (aftos/afti/afto epainei) - He/She/It praises 
- Εμείς επαινούμε (emeis epainoume) - We praise 
- Εσείς επαινείτε (eseis epaineite) - You (plural) praise 
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά επαινούν (aftoi/aftes/afta epainoun) - They praise 

Description: "Επαινω" is a positive verb that is used to express admiration or approval for someone or something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as praising a person for their accomplishments, complimenting someone on their appearance, or expressing gratitude for a kind gesture. It is a common word in Greek and is often used in everyday conversation.
