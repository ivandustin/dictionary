Word: επεμείνα (epeména)

Etymology:
The word επεμείνα (epeména) is derived from the Greek verb επιμένω (epiméno), which means "to persist" or "to persevere". The prefix επι- (epi-) means "on" or "upon", while the root word μένω (méno) means "to remain" or "to stay". Therefore, επιμένω (epiméno) literally means "to stay on" or "to remain upon".

Grammar:
επεμείνα (epeména) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb επιμένω (epiméno). It is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb stem is επιμείν-, and the suffix -α indicates the first person singular form.

Description:
επεμείνα (epeména) can be translated to English as "I persisted" or "I persevered". It describes an action that was completed in the past, where the speaker continued to do something despite difficulties or obstacles. The word implies a sense of determination and perseverance, and is often used to describe someone who does not give up easily.
