Word: επηκολουθησεν

Etymology: 
The word επηκολουθησεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of the prefix επι- (epi-), which means "upon" or "following," and the verb ακολουθώ (akoloutho), which means "to follow." Therefore, επηκολουθησεν means "he/she/it followed upon" or "he/she/it pursued."

Grammar:
επηκολουθησεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in the Greek language. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
επηκολουθησεν is a verb that describes the act of following or pursuing. It can be used in a literal sense, such as following someone on a path, or in a figurative sense, such as following a plan or a course of action. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples.
