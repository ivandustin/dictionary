Word: επεβαλαν

Etymology:
The word επεβαλαν is a Greek verb that comes from the prefix επι- (epi-) meaning "on" or "upon" and the verb βάλλω (ballo) meaning "to throw" or "to put". Therefore, επεβαλαν means "they put on" or "they imposed".

Grammar:
επεβαλαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb επιβάλλω (epiballo), which means "to impose" or "to put on". In this form, it indicates that the action was completed in the past by a group of people.

Description:
επεβαλαν is a verb that is often used in legal or formal contexts to describe the act of imposing something on someone or something. It can refer to the imposition of a law, a tax, a penalty, or a duty. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of putting on or applying something, such as a coat or makeup.
