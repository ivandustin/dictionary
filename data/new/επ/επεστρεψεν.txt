Word: επεστρεψεν

Etymology: 
The word επεστρεψεν (epistrepsen) is a Greek verb that means "returned" or "came back". It is derived from the Greek word "επιστροφή" (epistrofi), which means "return" or "turning back".

Grammar:
επεστρεψεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "he/she/it returned" or "he/she/it came back". The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
επεστρεψεν is a common verb in Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to describe someone returning from a trip or coming back to a place they had previously been. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone returning to a previous state of mind or behavior.
