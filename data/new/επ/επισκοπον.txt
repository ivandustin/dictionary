Word: επισκοπον

Etymology:
The word επισκοπον comes from the Greek word επίσκοπος (episkopos), which means "overseer" or "bishop". The word is derived from the prefix επί (epi), meaning "over" or "above", and the verb σκοπέω (skopeo), meaning "to look at" or "to examine".

Grammar:
επισκοπον is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender in the Greek language.

Description:
επισκοπον refers to a bishop or overseer in the Christian church. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe leaders who are responsible for the spiritual well-being of a congregation. The term is still used in modern Greek to refer to a bishop or archbishop.
