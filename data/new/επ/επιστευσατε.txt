Word: επιστευσατε

Etymology: 
The word επιστευσατε (episteusate) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The prefix επι- (epi-) adds emphasis to the verb, indicating a strong belief or faith.

Grammar:
επιστευσατε is the second person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb πιστεύω (pisteuo). In Greek, the aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject (you) performed the action of believing. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
επιστευσατε translates to "you believed" in English. It is a form of the verb πιστεύω (pisteuo), which is a common word in the New Testament of the Bible. The word is often used in the context of having faith in God or Jesus Christ. In the Bible, it is used to describe the act of believing in the message of salvation and trusting in God's promises.
