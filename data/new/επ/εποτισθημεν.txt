Word: εποτισθημεν

Etymology: The word εποτισθημεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ποτίζω (potizo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate". The prefix επο- (epo-) is a form of the verb επί (epi), which means "upon" or "on".

Grammar: εποτισθημεν is a passive voice verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: The word εποτισθημεν means "they were watered" or "they were irrigated". It is often used in the context of agriculture or gardening, to describe the process of watering plants or crops. The passive voice suggests that the plants or crops are the recipients of the action, rather than the ones performing it.
