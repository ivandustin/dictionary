Word: επηρωτων

Etymology:
The word επηρωτων (epērōtōn) is a present participle form of the Greek verb επερωταω (eperōtaō), which means "to ask, inquire, question". The verb is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), which means "upon" or "over", and the verb ρωταω (rōtaō), which means "to ask".

Grammar:
επηρωτων is a present participle form of the verb επερωταω (eperōtaō), which is in the present tense and active voice. The participle form indicates that the action of asking or inquiring is ongoing or continuous. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives, describing the subject of the sentence.

επηρωτων can be translated as "asking" or "inquiring". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe individuals who are questioning or seeking information from Jesus or other religious figures.
