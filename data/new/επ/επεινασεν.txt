In terms of grammar, "επεινασεν" is a third person singular form of the aorist tense. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any indication of the duration or repetition of the action. 

Etymologically, "πεινάω" comes from the Greek word "πείνα" (peina), which means "hunger". 

Overall, "επεινασεν" can be translated as "he/she/it hungered" or "he/she/it was hungry" in English.
