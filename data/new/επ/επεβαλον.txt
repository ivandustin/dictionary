Word: επεβαλον

Etymology:
The word επεβαλον comes from the Greek verb επιβάλλω (epibállō), which means "to impose" or "to inflict". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb βάλλω (bállō), meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar:
επεβαλον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb επιβάλλω (epibállō), which means "to impose" or "to inflict". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, επεβαλον describes an action that was imposed or inflicted in the past.

Description:
επεβαλον is a Greek verb that describes an action of imposing or inflicting something. It is often used in a negative context, such as imposing a punishment or inflicting harm. The aorist tense of the verb indicates that the action was completed in the past.
