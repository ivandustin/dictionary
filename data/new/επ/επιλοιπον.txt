Word: επιλοιπον

Etymology: The word επιλοιπον comes from the Greek roots επί (epi), meaning "upon" or "over", and λοιπός (loipos), meaning "remaining" or "leftover". Together, they form the word επιλοιπον, which means "remaining upon" or "leftover on".

Grammar: επιλοιπον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is often used as an adjective to describe something that is remaining or leftover. In this case, it would agree in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: επιλοιπον refers to something that is left over or remaining after everything else has been used or taken away. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as food or materials, or abstract concepts, such as time or resources. It can also be used to describe a person or group of people who are left behind or excluded from a larger group or activity.
