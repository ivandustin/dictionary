The word "επαρας" (eparas) is a Greek adjective that means "excessive" or "superfluous". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "over" or "above", and the adjective "αρας" (aras), meaning "enough" or "sufficient". 

Grammar: "Επαρας" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word "επαρας" is often used to describe something that is more than what is necessary or appropriate. It can also be used to describe someone who is overly talkative or boastful.
