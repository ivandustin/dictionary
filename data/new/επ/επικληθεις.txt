Word: επικληθεις

Etymology:
The word επικληθεις comes from the Greek verb επικαλέω (epikaleo), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". The suffix -θεις (-theis) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and refers to someone who has been called upon or invoked.

Grammar:
επικληθεις is a past participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe someone who has been called upon or invoked.

Description:
The word επικληθεις is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe someone who has been called upon or invoked by a higher power or deity. It can also be used more generally to describe someone who has been called upon or summoned for a specific purpose.
