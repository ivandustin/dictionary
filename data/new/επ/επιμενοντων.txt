Word: επιμενοντων

Etymology: The word επιμενοντων is derived from the Greek verb επιμένω (epiméno), which means "to persist" or "to persevere". The suffix -οντων (-onton) is the present participle form of the verb, indicating that the word refers to people or things that are currently persisting or persevering.

Grammar: επιμενοντων is the genitive plural form of the word, which means it is used to indicate possession or association with a group of people or things that are persisting or persevering. In Greek, the genitive case is often used to show possession, origin, or relationship between nouns.

Description: επιμενοντων can be translated to mean "of those who persist" or "of those who persevere". It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe individuals or groups who are steadfast in their beliefs or practices. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe people who are determined to achieve a goal or overcome an obstacle.
