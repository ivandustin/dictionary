Word: επειρασεν

Etymology: επειρασεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πειράω (peiráō), which means "to try" or "to attempt". The prefix επ- (ep-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "against", giving the verb the sense of "to try against" or "to attempt to overcome".

Grammar: επειρασεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb πειράω (peiráō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: επειρασεν means "he tried" or "he attempted" in English. It is often used in a heroic or epic context, to describe a character's brave or daring actions. In the New Testament, it is used to describe Jesus' temptation in the wilderness (Matthew 4:1).
