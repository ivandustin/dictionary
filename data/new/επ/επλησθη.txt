Word: επλησθη

Etymology: επλησθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληθω (plitho), which means "to fill". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a completed action, so επλησθη means "was filled" or "became full".

Grammar: επλησθη is a third person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: επλησθη is a verb that describes a state of being filled or becoming full. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when a container is filled with a substance, or in a figurative sense, such as when a person is filled with emotion or a place is filled with people. The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the filling of the Holy Spirit.
