Word: επαρρησιαζετο

Etymology: The word επαρρησιαζετο comes from the Greek verb παρρησιάζομαι (parrhēsiazomai), which means "to speak freely, to speak boldly". The prefix επα- (epa-) adds emphasis to the verb, meaning "to speak out even more boldly".

Grammar: επαρρησιαζετο is a verb in the imperfect tense, third person singular, active voice. It is derived from the verb παρρησιάζομαι (parrhēsiazomai), which is a deponent verb, meaning that it has a passive form but an active meaning. The imperfect tense indicates that the action was ongoing in the past.

Description: επαρρησιαζετο describes someone who was speaking out boldly or freely. It implies that the person was not holding back or being cautious in their speech. The word can be used to describe someone who is being honest and direct, even if their words may be controversial or unpopular.
