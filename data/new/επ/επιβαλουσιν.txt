Word: επιβαλουσιν

Etymology:
The word επιβαλουσιν is derived from the Greek verb επιβάλλω (epibállō), which means "to impose" or "to lay upon". It is formed from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb βάλλω (bállō), meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar:
επιβαλουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is a conjugation of the verb επιβάλλω (epibállō), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description:
The word επιβαλουσιν is used to describe the act of imposing or laying something upon someone or something else. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when placing a burden or responsibility on someone, or in a figurative sense, such as when imposing a rule or regulation. The word is often used in legal or bureaucratic contexts, where rules and regulations are frequently imposed on individuals or organizations.
