Word: επιλησμονης

Etymology:
The word επιλησμονης comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and λήθη (lethe) meaning "forgetfulness" or "oblivion". Therefore, επιλησμονης can be translated to mean "upon forgetfulness" or "over oblivion".

Grammar:
επιλησμονης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender. 

Description:
επιλησμονης refers to forgetfulness or oblivion. It can be used to describe a state of mind where someone forgets something or is forgetful in general. It can also be used to describe a situation where something is forgotten or overlooked. In Greek mythology, the river Lethe was said to cause forgetfulness, and επιλησμονης can be seen as a derivative of this concept.
