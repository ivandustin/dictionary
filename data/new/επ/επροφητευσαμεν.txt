The word "επροφητευσαμεν" is a Greek verb that is in the aorist tense and first person plural form. It comes from the root word "προφητεύω" which means "to prophesy" or "to predict". The prefix "ε-" indicates that the action was completed in the past. 

In terms of grammar, the aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any indication of the duration or repetition of the action. The first person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is "we".

Overall, "επροφητευσαμεν" can be translated to mean "we prophesied" or "we predicted".
