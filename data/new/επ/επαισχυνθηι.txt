The word "επαισχυνθηι" (epaischunthēi) is a Greek verb that means "to be ashamed" or "to feel shame." It is derived from the root word "αισχυνη" (aischunē), which means "shame" or "disgrace." In terms of grammar, it is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. Overall, the word describes a feeling of shame or embarrassment that someone experiences.
