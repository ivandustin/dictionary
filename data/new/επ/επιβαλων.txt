Word: επιβαλων

Etymology:
The word επιβαλων comes from the Greek verb επιβάλλω (epibállō), which means "to impose" or "to inflict". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb βάλλω (bállō), meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar:
επιβαλων is the present participle form of the verb επιβάλλω (epibállō), which means "imposing" or "inflicting". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
επιβαλων is used to describe someone who is currently imposing or inflicting something. It can refer to a person who is forcing their will on others, or to a situation where something is being imposed or inflicted upon someone or something else. The word can have negative connotations, suggesting that the imposition or infliction is unwanted or unfair.
