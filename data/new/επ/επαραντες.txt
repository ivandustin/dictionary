Word: επαραντες

Etymology: The word επαραντες comes from the Greek verb επαίρω (epaíro), which means "to lift up" or "to raise". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates a past action, so επαραντες can be translated as "having lifted up" or "having raised".

Grammar: επαραντες is a past participle in the masculine plural form. It is used in the nominative case and can function as a subject or a predicate in a sentence. For example, "Οι επαραντες άνδρες ήταν δυνατοί" (Oi epárantes ándres ítan dynatói) means "The men who had lifted up were strong".

Description: επαραντες is a word that describes a group of men who have lifted up or raised something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a military or construction setting. The word emphasizes the action of lifting or raising, and implies that the men are strong and capable.
