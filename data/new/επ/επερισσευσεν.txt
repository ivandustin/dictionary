Word: επερισσευσεν

Etymology: The word επερισσευσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word περισσεύω (perisseúō), which means "to be in abundance" or "to have more than enough." The prefix επι- (epi-) adds emphasis to the root word, giving the verb a sense of overflowing abundance.

Grammar: επερισσευσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word περισσεύω, which is a first-conjugation verb. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb happened at a specific point in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performed the action.

Description: επερισσευσεν means "he/she/it had more than enough" or "he/she/it overflowed with abundance." It is a strong verb that conveys a sense of excess or surplus. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a person's wealth, a harvest that produced an abundance of crops, or a situation where there is more than enough of something.
