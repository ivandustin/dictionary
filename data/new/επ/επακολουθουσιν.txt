Word: επακολουθουσιν

Etymology: The word επακολουθουσιν comes from the Greek roots επί (epi), meaning "upon" or "following," and ακολουθέω (akolouthéo), meaning "to follow." Therefore, επακολουθουσιν means "they follow upon" or "they come after."

Grammar: επακολουθουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb ακολουθέω (akolouthéo), which means "to follow." The prefix επί (epi) adds the meaning of "upon" or "following."

Description: επακολουθουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they follow upon" or "they come after." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the Bible, to describe the disciples following Jesus. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe something that follows or comes after something else.
