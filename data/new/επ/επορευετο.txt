Word: επορευετο

Etymology: The word επορευετο comes from the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreúomai), which means "to go, to travel". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the imperfect tense, which suggests that the action of going or traveling was ongoing or repeated in the past.

Grammar: επορευετο is the third person singular form of the imperfect tense of the verb πορεύομαι. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The verb is in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description: επορευετο can be translated as "he/she/it was going/traveling". The word suggests a continuous or repeated action of traveling in the past. It can be used to describe someone's journey or movement from one place to another. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone's progress or development in a particular area.
