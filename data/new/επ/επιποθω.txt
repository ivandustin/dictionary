Word: επιποθώ

Etymology: 
The word επιποθώ (epipotho) comes from the combination of two Greek words: επί (epi) meaning "on" or "upon" and ποθώ (potho) meaning "to desire" or "to long for". Therefore, επιποθώ can be translated as "to long for intensely" or "to yearn for".

Grammar:
επιποθώ is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs ending in -ω. The verb stem is ποθ-, and the prefix επι- adds the meaning of intensity or emphasis.

Description:
επιποθώ is a strong and intense verb that expresses a deep longing or desire for something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing a strong desire for a person, a place, an object, or an experience. It is often used in literature and poetry to convey strong emotions and feelings.
