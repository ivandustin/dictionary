Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek word "επίσκοπος" (episkopos), which means "bishop" or "overseer". The suffix "-ην" (-in) is added to form the noun.

Grammar: "Επισκοπην" is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender and number, and their endings change depending on their function in a sentence.

It is commonly used in the context of the Eastern Orthodox Church, where bishops are responsible for overseeing the spiritual life of their diocese.
