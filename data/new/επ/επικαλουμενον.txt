Word: επικαλουμενον

Etymology:
The word επικαλουμενον (epikaloumenon) is a present participle form of the Greek verb επικαλεω (epikaleo), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". The word is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb καλεω (kaleo), meaning "to call".

Grammar:
επικαλουμενον is a present participle form of the verb επικαλεω (epikaleo), which means "calling upon" or "invoking". It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
The word επικαλουμενον (epikaloumenon) is used to describe someone or something that is currently being called upon or invoked. It can be used in a religious or spiritual context, such as calling upon a deity or higher power, or in a more general sense, such as calling upon someone for help or support. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone is relying on or seeking the assistance of something or someone else.
