The word "επιδυετω" (epiduetō) is a Greek verb that means "to seek after eagerly" or "to strive for." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), which means "upon" or "over," and the verb "δυναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able to" or "to have power." 

Grammar: "Επιδυετω" is a present active imperative verb in the third person singular. 

Description: The word is often used in a positive sense, indicating a strong desire or ambition to achieve something. It can also be used in a negative sense, indicating an excessive or obsessive pursuit of something.
