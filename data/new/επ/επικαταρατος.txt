Word: επικατάρατος

Etymology: The word επικατάρατος comes from the Greek words επί (epi), meaning "upon" or "over", and κατάρα (katara), meaning "curse" or "malediction". Therefore, επικατάρατος means "cursed" or "accursed".

Grammar: επικατάρατος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description: επικατάρατος is a strong and negative adjective used to describe someone or something that is under a curse or has been cursed. It can also be used to describe a situation or event that is considered to be cursed or unlucky. The word has a dark and ominous connotation and is often used in a dramatic or poetic context.
