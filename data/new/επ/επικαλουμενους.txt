Word: επικαλουμενους

Etymology:
The word επικαλουμενους comes from the Greek verb επικαλέομαι (epikaléomai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". The word is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on", and the verb καλέω (kaléo), meaning "to call".

Grammar:
επικαλουμενους is the plural form of the present participle of the verb επικαλέομαι (epikaléomai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is a verb form that is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The plural form of the present participle is used to describe multiple people or things that are performing the action.

Description:
επικαλουμενους can be translated to English as "those who are calling upon" or "those who are invoking". The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context, to describe people who are calling upon a deity or higher power for help or guidance. It can also be used more generally to describe people who are seeking assistance or support from others.
