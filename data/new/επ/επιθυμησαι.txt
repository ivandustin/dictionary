Word: επιθυμησαι

Etymology: The word επιθυμησαι comes from the Greek verb επιθυμέω (epithyméō), which means "to desire" or "to long for". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb θυμός (thymós), meaning "passion" or "desire".

Grammar: επιθυμησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and second person singular form. It is a regular verb and follows the conjugation pattern of the first conjugation in Ancient Greek.

Description: επιθυμησαι means "you desired" or "you longed for" in Ancient Greek. It is a past tense verb and implies that the desire or longing has already been fulfilled or has passed. The word can be used in various contexts, such as expressing a desire for material possessions, a person, or a particular experience.
