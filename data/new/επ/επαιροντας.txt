The word "επαιροντας" (epairontas) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "lifting up" or "raising". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "αιρω" (airo), which means "to lift" or "to raise". The prefix "επι" (epi) is added to the verb to indicate the continuous action of lifting or raising.

Grammar: "Επαιροντας" is the present participle form of the verb "αιρω" (airo), which means that it describes an ongoing action of lifting or raising. It is used to form the present continuous tense in Greek, and can also be used as an adjective to describe a person or thing that is lifting or raising something.

Description: The word "επαιροντας" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical actions, such as lifting weights or raising one's arms. It can also be used metaphorically to describe emotional or mental actions, such as lifting one's spirits or raising one's awareness. Overall, the word conveys a sense of upward movement or progress.
