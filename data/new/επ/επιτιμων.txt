The word "επιτιμων" (epitimon) is a Greek verb that means "to rebuke" or "to reprimand". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb "τιμων" (timon), meaning "to honor" or "to esteem". Therefore, "επιτιμων" can be understood as "to place honor upon" or "to esteem with rebuke".

Grammar: "Επιτιμων" is a present participle form of the verb, which means it describes an ongoing action. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: The word "επιτιμων" is often used in the context of correcting or disciplining someone for their behavior or actions. It can also be used to express disapproval or dissatisfaction with someone's conduct.
