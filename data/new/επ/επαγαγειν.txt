Word: επαγαγειν

Etymology:
The word επαγαγειν comes from the Greek roots επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "on" and αγαγειν (agagein) meaning "to lead" or "to bring". 

Grammar:
επαγαγειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates that the action it describes happened at a specific point in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. 

Description:
The word επαγαγειν means "to bring upon" or "to introduce". It is often used in a legal context to refer to the introduction of evidence or witnesses in a trial. It can also be used more generally to describe the act of bringing something or someone into a particular situation or context.
