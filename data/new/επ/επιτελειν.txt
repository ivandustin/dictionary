The word "επιτελειν" (epitelin) is a Greek verb that means "to accomplish" or "to complete". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon", and "τελείν" (telein), meaning "to complete" or "to finish". 

Grammar: "Επιτελειν" is a regular verb in Greek, belonging to the first conjugation. It is usually conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Εγώ επιτελώ (ego epitelo) - I accomplish 
- Εσύ επιτελείς (esi epitelis) - You accomplish 
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιτελεί (aftos/afti/afto epiteli) - He/She/It accomplishes 
- Εμείς επιτελούμε (emeis epiteloume) - We accomplish 
- Εσείς επιτελείτε (eseis epitelite) - You (plural) accomplish 
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά επιτελούν (aftoi/aftes/afta epiteloun) - They accomplish 

Description: "Επιτελειν" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to express the idea of completing or achieving something. It can be used to describe the completion of a task, the fulfillment of a goal, or the realization of a plan. It can also be used in a figurative sense to describe the attainment of a desired state or condition, such as "επιτελείται η ειρήνη" (epiteleitai i irini) - peace is achieved.
