The word "επιθυμει" (epithymei) is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the combination of two words: "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "θυμός" (thymos), meaning "passion" or "desire." Therefore, "επιθυμει" can be translated as "to desire" or "to long for."

In terms of grammar, "επιθυμει" is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb. It can be conjugated in various tenses and persons to indicate different subjects and time frames.

In terms of description, "επιθυμει" is a strong verb that conveys a sense of intense desire or longing. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing a person's wishes, aspirations, or cravings.
