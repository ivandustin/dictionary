Word: επεδωκαν

Etymology:
The word επεδωκαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "επιδίδωμι" (epididomi), which means "to give over, to deliver, to surrender". The verb is composed of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), which means "over" or "upon", and the verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), which means "to give".

Grammar:
επεδωκαν is the third person plural, past tense, indicative mood, active voice form of the verb "επιδίδωμι" (epididomi). This means that it refers to a group of people who gave over, delivered, or surrendered something in the past.

Description:
The word επεδωκαν can be translated as "they gave over" or "they delivered". It is a verb that describes an action of giving or surrendering something to someone else. The context in which the word is used will determine what was given or delivered.
