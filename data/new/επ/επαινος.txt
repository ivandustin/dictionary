Word: επαινος

Etymology:
The word επαινος (epainos) comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "above" and αίνος (ainos) meaning "praise". Therefore, επαινος means "praise upon" or "praise above".

Grammar:
In Greek, επαινος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is επαινοι (epainoi).

Description:
επαινος is a word that refers to praise or commendation. It is often used to express admiration or approval for someone's actions or accomplishments. In ancient Greek literature, επαινος was often used to describe the praise given to heroes or gods. Today, it is still commonly used in modern Greek to express praise or appreciation for someone's work or achievements.
