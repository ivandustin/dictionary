Word: επερωτηθείς

Etymology:
The word επερωτηθείς comes from the Greek verb επερωτάω (eperotao), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". The suffix -θείς (-theis) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and that the subject has been asked or inquired about.

Grammar:
επερωτηθείς is a past participle in the passive voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence has received the action of being asked or inquired about. It is often used in conjunction with the auxiliary verb έχω (echo) to form the perfect tense, as in έχω επερωτηθείς (echo eperotithis), which means "I have been asked".

Description:
επερωτηθείς is a word that describes someone who has been asked or inquired about. It can be used to indicate that someone has been questioned or interrogated, or simply that someone has been asked a question. The word is often used in legal or formal contexts, such as in court proceedings or official investigations.
