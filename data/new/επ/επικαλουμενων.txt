Word: επικαλουμενων

Etymology:
The word επικαλουμενων is derived from the Greek verb επικαλέω (epikaleo), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". The suffix -ων (-on) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to something or someone that is currently being called upon or invoked.

Grammar:
επικαλουμενων is a present participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, the word is indicating that something or someone is being called upon or invoked by a group of people.

Description:
επικαλουμενων can be translated to mean "of those who are being called upon" or "of those who are being invoked". It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to the people who are calling upon a deity or higher power for help or guidance. The word can also be used more generally to refer to any group of people who are calling upon someone or something for assistance.
