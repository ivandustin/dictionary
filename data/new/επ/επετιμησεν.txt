Word: επετιμησεν

Etymology: The word επετιμησεν comes from the Greek verb "ἐπιτιμάω" (epitimao), which means "to rebuke, to censure, to reprimand". It is derived from the prefix "ἐπί" (epi), which means "upon" or "over", and the verb "τιμάω" (timao), which means "to honor, to value, to esteem".

Grammar: επετιμησεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "ἐπιτιμάω" (epitimao) in Ancient Greek. It is translated as "he/she/it rebuked" or "he/she/it censured" in English.

Description: επετιμησεν is a verb that expresses a strong disapproval or criticism of someone's behavior or actions. It is often used in the context of a rebuke or a reprimand, and implies a sense of authority or power over the person being rebuked. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe any kind of strong criticism or condemnation.
