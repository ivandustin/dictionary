Word: επαγγελμα (epangelma)

Etymology:
The word επαγγελμα (epangelma) comes from the Greek verb επαγγέλλομαι (epangelomai), which means "to promise" or "to declare". The noun form επαγγελμα (epangelma) means "promise" or "pledge".

Grammar:
In Greek, επαγγελμα (epangelma) is a neuter noun. Its plural form is επαγγελματα (epangelmata).

Description:
επαγγελμα (epangelma) is a word that refers to a promise or a pledge. It can be used in various contexts, such as in business, politics, or personal relationships. For example, someone might make an επαγγελμα (epangelma) to pay back a loan, to fulfill a contract, or to keep a secret. The word emphasizes the importance of keeping one's word and following through on commitments.
