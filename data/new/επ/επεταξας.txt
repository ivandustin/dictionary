Word: επεταξας

Etymology: The word επεταξας comes from the Greek verb επιτάσσω (epitasso), which means "to command" or "to order".

Grammar: επεταξας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb επιτάσσω (epitasso). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: επεταξας can be translated to English as "you commanded" or "you ordered". It is a strong and authoritative word that implies a sense of power and control. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in military or political situations, or in everyday life when giving orders or instructions.
