Word: επουρανιον

Etymology:
The word επουρανιον (epouranion) is derived from the Greek words επί (epi) meaning "above" and ουρανός (ouranos) meaning "sky" or "heaven". Therefore, επουρανιον literally means "above the sky" or "heavenly".

Grammar:
In Greek, επουρανιον is a neuter noun. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: επουρανιον
Genitive: επουρανίου (epouraniou)
Accusative: επουρανιον
Vocative: επουρανιον

Plural:
Nominative: επουρανια (epourania)
Genitive: επουρανίων (epouraniōn)
Accusative: επουρανια (epourania)
Vocative: επουρανια (epourania)

Description:
In Greek mythology, επουρανιον refers to the realm of the gods, the heavens or the sky. It is often used to describe something divine or celestial. In the New Testament, επουρανιον is used to refer to the kingdom of God or heaven. It is also used in the Lord's Prayer as "Our Father who art in heaven" (Πάτερ ἡμῶν ὁ ἐν τοῖς οὐρανοῖς).
