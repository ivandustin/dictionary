The word "επισυναγωγην" (episynagogēn) is a Greek noun that means "a gathering together" or "a collection." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek roots "epi" meaning "upon" or "together with," and "synagōgē" meaning "a gathering" or "a synagogue."

Grammar: "επισυναγωγην" is a singular accusative noun in the Greek language.

Description: The word can be used to refer to a physical gathering of people, such as a meeting or assembly, or to a collection of things, such as a group of books or artifacts. It is also used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the gathering of believers in Jesus Christ.
