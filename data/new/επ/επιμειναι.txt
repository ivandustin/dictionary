Word: επιμειναι

Etymology:
The word επιμειναι comes from the Greek verb "επιμένω" (epiméno), which means "to persist" or "to persevere". It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb "μένω" (meno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain".

Grammar:
επιμειναι is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. The verb stem is "επιμειν-", and the ending "-αι" indicates that it is in the infinitive form.

Description:
The word επιμειναι refers to the act of persisting or persevering in something, despite difficulties or obstacles. It can also mean to insist on something or to continue to do something despite opposition or resistance. The word is often used in a positive sense, to describe someone who is determined and persistent in achieving their goals. However, it can also have negative connotations, suggesting stubbornness or an unwillingness to change one's mind.
