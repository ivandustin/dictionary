Word: επεθυμησαν

Etymology:
The word επεθυμησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θυμός (thymos), which means "passion" or "desire". The prefix επε- (epe-) means "to intensify", so επεθυμησαν can be translated as "they intensely desired" or "they passionately wanted".

Grammar:
επεθυμησαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb επιθυμέω (epithumeo), which means "to desire" or "to long for". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice means that the subject (in this case, "they") performed the action.

Description:
επεθυμησαν is a strong and passionate word that conveys a deep desire or longing for something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, from expressing a strong craving for food or drink to describing a burning desire for success or love. The word carries a sense of urgency and intensity, suggesting that the desire is not easily quenched or satisfied.
