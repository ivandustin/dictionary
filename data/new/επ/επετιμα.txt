The word "επετιμα" (epetima) is a Greek verb that means "to rebuke" or "to reprimand". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "τιμάω" (timao), which means "to honor" or "to value". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) means "upon" or "over", so "επετιμα" can be understood as "to place value upon" or "to hold accountable".

Grammar: "Επετιμα" is a first-person singular past tense form of the verb. The verb can be conjugated in various tenses and persons to indicate different actions and subjects.

Description: The word is often used in the context of expressing disapproval or criticism towards someone's behavior or actions. It can also be used in a more formal or authoritative setting, such as a parent rebuking a child or a boss reprimanding an employee.
