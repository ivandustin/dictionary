The word "επορθει" is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "πορθέω" (portheo), which means "to ravage" or "to destroy." 

In terms of grammar, "επορθει" is in the present tense and active voice. It is conjugated in the third person singular form, which means that it refers to a single subject performing the action. 

The word "επορθει" can be translated to English as "he/she/it ravages" or "he/she/it destroys." It is often used in the context of warfare or destruction, and can also be used metaphorically to describe the devastation of something non-physical, such as a reputation or relationship.
