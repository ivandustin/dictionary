Word: επικρανθησαν

Etymology: The word επικρανθησαν is derived from the Greek verb "επικραίνω" (epikraino), which means "to prevail" or "to overcome". The suffix "-θησαν" (-thēsan) is a past tense marker, indicating that the action of prevailing or overcoming has already taken place.

Grammar: επικρανθησαν is a third-person plural past tense verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is passive, meaning that the subject of the sentence is the recipient of the action rather than the doer.

Description: επικρανθησαν can be translated to English as "they were prevailed upon" or "they were overcome". The word implies that a group of people or a situation was dominated or defeated by another force. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a battle or a political struggle.
