The word "επηκουσα" (epikousa) is a Greek verb in the past tense, which means "I listened attentively" or "I obeyed". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) in this case adds the meaning of "attentively" or "carefully".

Grammar: "Επηκουσα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "ακούω". The verb belongs to the first conjugation group in Greek, which means that it follows a regular pattern of conjugation.

Description: The word "επηκουσα" implies a sense of attentiveness and obedience. It suggests that the person who used this verb was actively listening and paying attention to what was being said, and that they followed through with the instructions or requests given to them.
