Word: επικαλεσώνται

Etymology: 
The word επικαλεσώνται comes from the Greek verb επικαλούμαι (epikaloumai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on", and the verb καλώ (kalo), meaning "to call".

Grammar:
επικαλεσώνται is the third person plural, present tense, middle/passive voice form of the verb επικαλούμαι. In this form, the subject is receiving the action of the verb. It can be translated as "they are being called upon" or "they are invoking".

Description:
επικαλεσώνται is a verb that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, where someone is calling upon a deity or higher power for help or guidance. It can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to call upon" or "to appeal to" someone or something for assistance.
