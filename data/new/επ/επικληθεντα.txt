Word: επικληθεντα

Etymology: The word επικληθεντα comes from the Greek verb επικαλώμαι (epikalómai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". The suffix -τα (-ta) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and passive voice.

Grammar: επικληθεντα is a past participle in the neuter gender, nominative case, and plural number. It is used to describe or modify a noun, and can function as an adjective in a sentence.

Description: The word επικληθεντα is often used in Greek Orthodox liturgy to refer to the saints who are invoked during prayer. It can also be used more generally to describe things that have been called upon or invoked in the past.
