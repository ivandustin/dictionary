Word: επεπεσεν

Etymology:
The word επεπεσεν (epepesen) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πίπτω (pipto), which means "to fall." The prefix επ- (ep-) is added to the root word to indicate that the action of falling has already taken place.

Grammar:
επεπεσεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates that the action of falling occurred at a specific point in time in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence (he/she/it) performed the action of falling. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement of fact.

Description:
επεπεσεν is a verb that means "he/she/it fell" in Greek. It is often used in narratives or descriptions of events that have already taken place. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe the fall of the walls of Jericho (Joshua 6:20) and the fall of a man from a window (Acts 20:9).
