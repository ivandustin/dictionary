The word "επερωτωσιν" (eperōtōsin) is a Greek noun that means "questioning" or "interrogation". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the verb "επερωταω" (eperōtaō), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". The suffix "-σιν" (-sin) is a nominalizing suffix that turns the verb into a noun.

Grammar: "Επερωτωσιν" is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: The word "επερωτωσιν" is often used in legal or formal contexts to refer to questioning or interrogation. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any kind of inquiry or investigation.
