Etymology: The word comes from the verb "επίστημι" (epistimi), which means "to stand upon" or "to be present".

The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. 

Description: The word "επεστη" means "he/she/it stood upon" or "he/she/it was present". It can be used to describe a physical presence or a figurative presence, such as being present in someone's thoughts or emotions.
