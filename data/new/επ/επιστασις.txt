Word: επιστασις

Etymology: The word επιστασις comes from the Greek verb επισταμαι (epistamai), which means "to know" or "to understand". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb ιστημι (histemi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: επιστασις is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: επιστασις is a Greek word that can be translated as "knowledge", "understanding", or "comprehension". It refers to a deep and thorough understanding of a subject or topic. In philosophy, επιστασις is often contrasted with δοξα (doxa), which means "opinion" or "belief". In this context, επιστασις represents true knowledge, while δοξα represents mere belief or conjecture.
