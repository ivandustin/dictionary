Word: επιεικεις

Etymology:
The word επιεικεις (epieikeis) comes from the Greek word επιεικής (epieikēs), which means "gentle, mild, equitable, fair, reasonable". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the adjective εικής (eikēs), meaning "just, right, fitting".

Grammar:
επιεικεις is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The feminine form is επιεικής (epieikēs), and the neuter form is επιεικές (epieikés).

Description:
The word επιεικεις is used to describe someone or something that is gentle, mild, equitable, fair, or reasonable. It can be used to describe a person's character, behavior, or attitude, as well as a situation or circumstance. In Greek philosophy, the concept of επιείκεια (epieikeia) refers to the virtue of fairness, justice, and leniency, which is often contrasted with strict adherence to the letter of the law.
