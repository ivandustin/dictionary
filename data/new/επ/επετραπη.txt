Word: επετράπη

Etymology: 
The word επετράπη (epetrápi) is a past tense form of the Greek verb πιστεύω (pistévo), which means "to believe" or "to trust". The verb is derived from the noun πίστη (písti), which means "faith" or "belief". 

Grammar: 
επετράπη is a third person singular past tense form of the verb πιστεύω (pistévo). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is passive, indicating that the subject received the action of the verb. 

Description: 
επετράπη is a Greek word that means "was entrusted" or "was believed". It is often used in a religious context to describe the act of entrusting oneself to God or believing in God's promises. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of trusting or believing in someone or something.
