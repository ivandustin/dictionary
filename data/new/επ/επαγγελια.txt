Word: επαγγελία (epangelía)

Etymology:
The word επαγγελία (epangelía) comes from the Greek verb επαγγέλλομαι (epangéllomai), which means "to promise" or "to pledge". The word is composed of two parts: επί (epí), which means "upon" or "over", and αγγέλλω (angéllō), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim".

Grammar:
επαγγελία (epangelía) is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural, and its declension follows the second declension of Greek nouns. The genitive singular form is επαγγελίας (epangelías), and the nominative plural form is επαγγελίες (epangelíes).

Description:
επαγγελία (epangelía) means "promise" or "pledge" in Greek. It refers to a commitment or assurance given by one person to another, usually to do something or to refrain from doing something. The word can also be used in a legal context to refer to a contract or agreement between two parties. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to refer to God's promises to his people.
