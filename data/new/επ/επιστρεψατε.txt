Word: επιστρεψατε

Etymology: 
The word επιστρεψατε is a verb in the Greek language, which is derived from the root word "στρέφω" (strefó), meaning "to turn". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) in the word επιστρεψατε adds the meaning of "back" or "again", making the word mean "to turn back" or "to return".

Grammar:
επιστρεψατε is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in the Greek language. It is used to indicate a completed action in the past, specifically the action of returning or turning back. 

Description:
επιστρεψατε is a verb that is commonly used in everyday conversation in Greece. It is often used to express the act of returning to a place or situation that one has previously left. For example, "Επιστρέψατε στο σπίτι;" (Epistrépsate sto spíti?) means "Did you return home?" or "Have you come back home?". It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as "Επιστρέψατε στη δουλειά μετά τις διακοπές;" (Epistrépsate sti douleía metá tis diakopés?) which means "Did you return to work after the holidays?".
