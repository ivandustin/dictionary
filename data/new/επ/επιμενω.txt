Word: επιμενω

Etymology:
The word επιμενω comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "on" or "upon" and μένω (meno) meaning "to remain" or "to stay". Therefore, επιμενω can be translated to mean "to persist" or "to continue".

Grammar:
επιμενω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense and active voice. The conjugation of the verb in the present tense is as follows:

- Εγώ επιμένω (ego epimenō) - I persist
- Εσύ επιμένεις (esy epimeneis) - You persist
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιμένει (autos/auti/auto epimenei) - He/She/It persists
- Εμείς επιμένουμε (emeis epimenoume) - We persist
- Εσείς επιμένετε (eseis epimenete) - You persist
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά επιμένουν (autoi/autes/auto epimenoun) - They persist

Description:
επιμενω is a word that describes the act of continuing to do something despite difficulties or opposition. It can also refer to the act of insisting on something or refusing to give up on a particular goal or belief. The word is often used in a positive sense to describe someone who is determined and persistent in achieving their goals. However, it can also be used in a negative sense to describe someone who is stubborn and refuses to change their mind or behavior.
