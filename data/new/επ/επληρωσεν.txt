Word: επληρωσεν

Etymology: επληρωσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the action is completed or finished.

Grammar: επληρωσεν is the aorist tense form of the verb πληρόω (plēróō) in the third person singular. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, επληρωσεν means "he/she/it filled" or "he/she/it completed".

Description: επληρωσεν is a verb that describes the act of filling or completing something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as filling a container with water, completing a task or project, or fulfilling a promise or obligation. The word has a sense of finality or accomplishment, indicating that something has been brought to completion.
