Word: επληρουτο

Etymology: επληρουτο is a verb in the imperfect tense form of the Greek word πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the imperfect tense, which describes an ongoing or incomplete action in the past.

Grammar: επληρουτο is a third-person singular verb in the imperfect tense form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and voice. The imperfect tense describes an ongoing or incomplete action in the past.

Description: επληρουτο can be translated as "was being filled" or "was being completed". It describes an ongoing action in the past, indicating that something was in the process of being filled or completed. The context of the sentence would determine what was being filled or completed.
