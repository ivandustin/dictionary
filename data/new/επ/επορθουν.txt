Word: επορθουν

Etymology:
The word επορθουν is derived from the Greek verb "πορθέω" (portheo), which means "to ravage" or "to destroy". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar:
επορθουν is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb πορθέω. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εγώ πορθώ
εσύ πορθείς
αυτός/αυτή/αυτό πορθεί
εμείς πορθούμε
εσείς πορθείτε
αυτοί/αυτές/αυτά πορθούν

Description:
The word επορθουν describes an ongoing action of ravaging or destroying. It can be used to describe a military campaign, a natural disaster, or any other situation where destruction is taking place. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to describe violent or destructive behavior.
