Word: επιτιμαν

Etymology: The word επιτιμαν comes from the Greek verb επιτιμαω (epitimao), which means "to rebuke" or "to reprimand". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb τιμαω (timao), meaning "to honor" or "to value".

Grammar: επιτιμαν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb επιτιμαω.

Description: επιτιμαν means "he/she/it rebukes" or "he/she/it reprimands" in Greek. It is often used in a disciplinary context, such as a parent rebuking a child or a teacher reprimanding a student. The word carries a sense of authority and seriousness, and is often used to convey a strong message of disapproval or correction.
