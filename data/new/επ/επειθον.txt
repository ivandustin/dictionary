The word "επειθον" (epithon) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". 

In terms of grammar, "επειθον" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it can be conjugated to match the subject of the sentence. For example, "εγώ πείθομαι" (ego peithomai) means "I am persuaded", while "αυτοί πείθονται" (autoi peithontai) means "they are persuaded".

In terms of usage, "επειθον" can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in persuasive speeches or arguments. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is convinced or persuaded by something.

Overall, "επειθον" is a versatile verb that is commonly used in Greek language and culture.
