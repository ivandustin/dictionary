Word: επιταγην

Etymology:
The word επιταγην (epitagen) is derived from the Greek word επιταγή (epitagi), which means "order" or "command". It is formed from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the noun ταγή (tagi), meaning "order" or "command".

Grammar:
επιταγην is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description:
επιταγην refers to a written order or command, typically used in a financial context. It can be translated to English as "bill of exchange" or "promissory note". In modern Greek, it is commonly used to refer to a check or a bank draft.
