The word "επεσπειρεν" is a Greek verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "σπείρω" (speíro), which means "to sow" or "to scatter." The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "over," so "επεσπείρεν" can be translated as "he/she/it scattered upon" or "he/she/it sowed over." 

In terms of grammar, "επεσπείρεν" is a regular verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject (he/she/it) performed the action.

Overall, "επεσπείρεν" is a descriptive word that can be used to convey the idea of scattering or sowing something over a surface.
