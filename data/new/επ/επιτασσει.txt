Word: επιτάσσει (epitássei)

Etymology: The word επιτάσσει (epitássei) comes from the Greek verb επιτάσσω (epitássō), which means "to command" or "to order". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), which means "upon" or "over", and the verb τάσσω (tássō), which means "to arrange" or "to put in order".

Grammar: επιτάσσει (epitássei) is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb επιτάσσω (epitássō). It is a regular verb in Greek, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -ω (-ō).

Description: επιτάσσει (epitássei) means "he/she/it commands" or "he/she/it orders" in Greek. It is often used in a military or authoritative context, but can also be used in everyday situations to indicate someone giving orders or instructions.
