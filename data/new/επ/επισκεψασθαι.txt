The word "επισκεψασθαι" (episkepsasthai) is a Greek verb that means "to visit" or "to inspect". It is derived from the root word "επισκεπτομαι" (episkeptomai), which means "to look at" or "to examine". 

In terms of grammar, "επισκεψασθαι" is a second aorist middle/passive infinitive. The second aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

Overall, "επισκεψασθαι" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts, from visiting a friend to inspecting a building or site.
