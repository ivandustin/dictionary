The word "επαραι" (eparai) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the verb "επαίρω" (epairo), which means "to lift up" or "to raise." 

In terms of grammar, "επαραι" is a second person plural form, meaning it is used when addressing a group of people. It can be translated to "you all lifted" or "you all raised."

Overall, the word "επαραι" is a specific form of the verb "επαίρω" and is used to describe a completed action of lifting or raising that was performed by a group of people.
