Word: επιθεντος

Etymology:
The word επιθεντος comes from the Greek word επίθετον (epítheton), which means "attributive" or "adjective". It is derived from the prefix επί (epí), meaning "on" or "upon", and the noun θέτος (thétos), meaning "placed" or "put".

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, επιθεντος is an adjective, which means it modifies a noun or a pronoun. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. It can be used attributively, predicatively, or substantively.

Description:
επιθεντος is a word that describes a quality or characteristic of a noun or a pronoun. It can be used to describe a person, place, thing, or idea. For example, in the phrase "ο κόκκινος καρπός" (o kókkinos karpós), which means "the red fruit", επιθεντος is the adjective that describes the color of the fruit. In Greek, adjectives usually come after the noun they modify.
