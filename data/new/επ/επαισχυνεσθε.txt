The word "επαισχυνεσθε" (epaischunesthe) is a Greek verb that means "to be ashamed" or "to feel shame." It is derived from the prefix "epi-" meaning "upon" or "over" and the root "aischunomai" meaning "to feel shame." 

In terms of grammar, "επαισχυνεσθε" is a second person plural present middle/passive indicative verb. This means that it is used to address a group of people (you all) and indicates that the subject is both performing the action and receiving the action at the same time. 

In the New Testament, the word "επαισχυνεσθε" is often used in the context of moral or ethical behavior, urging individuals to feel shame for their wrongdoing and to repent.
