Word: επαγοντες

Etymology:
The word επαγοντες comes from the Greek verb επάγω (epago), which means "to bring upon" or "to impose". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently doing the action of bringing upon or imposing something.

Grammar:
επαγοντες is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is derived from the present participle of the verb επάγω, which is επαγοντας (epagontas). The -ας (-as) ending of the participle changes to -οντες (-ontes) to form the plural.

Description:
επαγοντες refers to people who are currently imposing or bringing upon something. The context in which the word is used will determine what exactly is being imposed or brought upon. It could refer to people who are imposing a law or regulation, bringing upon a punishment or consequence, or even imposing their will or influence on others.
