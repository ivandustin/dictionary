Word: επινοια

Etymology:
The word επινοια (epinoia) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of two words: επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and νόος (noos) meaning "mind" or "intellect". Therefore, επινοια can be translated as "overthinking" or "excessive thinking".

Grammar:
In Greek, επινοια is a feminine noun. Its plural form is επινοιες (epinoies).

Description:
επινοια is a term used to describe the state of overthinking or excessive thinking. It refers to the tendency to dwell on a particular thought or idea for an extended period of time, often to the point of obsession. This can lead to anxiety, stress, and even mental health issues if left unchecked. In some cases, επινοια can be a positive trait, as it can lead to creative problem-solving and innovation. However, when it becomes excessive, it can be detrimental to one's well-being.
