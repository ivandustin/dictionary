Word: επερωτησαι

Etymology:
The word επερωτησαι comes from the Greek verb επερωτάω (eperotao), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb ρωτάω (rotaw), meaning "to ask".

Grammar:
επερωτησαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb επερωτάω (eperotao). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give a command or make a request. Therefore, επερωτησαι can be translated as "ask!" or "inquire!".

Description:
επερωτησαι is a verb that is commonly used in Greek to ask a question or make an inquiry. It is often used in formal settings, such as in academic or professional contexts, but can also be used in everyday conversation.
