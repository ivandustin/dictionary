Word: εποιησας

Etymology:
The word εποιησας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate the past tense, making εποιησας (epoiisas) mean "you made" or "you did".

Grammar:
εποιησας is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. This means that it is used to describe an action that was completed in the past by the person being addressed (you). The aorist tense is used to describe a single, completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject (you) performed the action.

Description:
εποιησας is a powerful word that conveys the idea of taking action and making something happen. It is often used in the context of creation or accomplishment, such as in the phrase "εποιησας τον κόσμο" (epoiisas ton kosmo), which means "you created the world". In everyday conversation, it can be used to describe any action that was completed in the past, such as "εποιησας τη δουλειά σου;" (epoiisas ti douleia sou), which means "did you finish your work?".
