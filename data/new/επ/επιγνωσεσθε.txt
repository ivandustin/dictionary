Word: επιγνωσεσθε

Etymology:
The word επιγνωσεσθε comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and γνώση (gnósi) meaning "knowledge" or "understanding". The suffix -σθε (-sthe) is a second person plural ending, indicating that the word is in the imperative mood.

Grammar:
επιγνωσεσθε is a verb in the second person plural form of the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The word επιγνωσεσθε can be translated to mean "you will recognize" or "you will understand". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, where it may refer to a deeper understanding of God or a higher power. The word implies a sense of enlightenment or revelation, suggesting that the speaker is urging the listener to gain a new level of knowledge or insight.
