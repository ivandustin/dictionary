Word: επιθυμησετε

Etymology: The word επιθυμησετε comes from the Greek verb επιθυμέω (epithyméō), which means "to desire" or "to long for". The suffix -σετε (-sete) is the second person plural ending for the future tense.

Grammar: επιθυμησετε is a verb in the future tense, second person plural form. It is used to express a desire or longing for something that will happen in the future.

Description: επιθυμησετε is a formal way of saying "you will desire" or "you will long for" in Greek. It is often used in formal or written contexts, such as in legal documents or official announcements. The word emphasizes the idea of a strong desire or longing for something that will happen in the future.
