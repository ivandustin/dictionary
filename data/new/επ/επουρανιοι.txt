Word: επουρανιοι

Etymology: The word επουρανιοι (epouranioi) comes from the Greek words επί (epi) meaning "above" and ουρανός (ouranos) meaning "sky" or "heavenly". Therefore, επουρανιοι can be translated as "heavenly" or "celestial".

Grammar: επουρανιοι is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe heavenly beings or things related to heaven.

Description: επουρανιοι is a word that refers to things that are of or related to heaven. It can be used to describe heavenly beings, such as angels, or heavenly places, such as the kingdom of God. In the New Testament, it is often used in the context of describing the spiritual realm and the things that are beyond the physical world.
