Word: επιπίπτειν (epipíptein)

Etymology: The word επιπίπτειν comes from the Greek roots επί (epí), meaning "on" or "upon," and πίπτειν (píptein), meaning "to fall." Therefore, επιπίπτειν literally means "to fall upon" or "to happen upon."

Grammar: επιπίπτειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem is formed by removing the -ειν (ein) ending from the infinitive form.

Description: επιπίπτειν is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to express the idea of something happening unexpectedly or suddenly. It can be translated as "to fall upon," "to come upon," "to happen upon," "to occur," or "to chance upon." It is often used in the context of accidents, incidents, or unexpected events. For example, "Επιπίπτειν βροχή" (Epipíptein vrochí) means "It's raining suddenly."
