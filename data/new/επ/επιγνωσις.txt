Word: επιγνωσις

Etymology: 
The word επιγνωσις comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and γνώσις (gnosis) meaning "knowledge". Therefore, επιγνωσις can be translated as "knowledge upon knowledge" or "full knowledge".

Grammar:
In Greek, επιγνωσις is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is spelled επιγνωσις and pronounced "ep-ig'-no-sis".

Description:
επιγνωσις is a term used in Christian theology to describe a deeper, more intimate knowledge of God. It is often translated as "knowledge" or "understanding", but it carries a connotation of a personal, experiential knowledge that goes beyond mere intellectual understanding. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the knowledge of God that comes through faith in Jesus Christ. It is considered a key component of Christian spirituality and is often associated with the concept of salvation.
