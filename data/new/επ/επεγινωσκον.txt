Word: επεγινωσκον

Etymology:
The word επεγινωσκον is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know" or "to understand." The prefix επε- (epe-) adds the meaning of "fully" or "completely," so επεγινωσκον can be translated as "they fully knew" or "they completely understood."

Grammar:
επεγινωσκον is the third person plural imperfect indicative active form of the verb γινώσκω. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
επεγινωσκον is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John. It is used to describe the actions of the disciples after Jesus performed a miracle of feeding a large crowd with only a few loaves of bread and fish. The word emphasizes that the disciples fully understood the significance of the miracle and the power of Jesus.
