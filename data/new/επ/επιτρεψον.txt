The word "επιτρεψον" (epitrepson) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "επιτρέπω" (epitrepō), which means "to allow" or "to permit". 

Grammar: "Επιτρεψον" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "επιτρέπω". It is used to give a command or request someone to allow or permit something. 

Description: The word "επιτρεψον" is commonly used in everyday Greek language. It is often used in situations where someone is asking for permission to do something or requesting someone to allow them to do something. For example, "Επιτρέψον μου να μιλήσω" (Epitrepson mou na miliso) means "Allow me to speak".
