Word: επυθετο

Etymology:
The word επυθετο (epytheto) is derived from the Greek verb "πυνθάνομαι" (pythánomai), which means "to inquire" or "to ask". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) is added to the verb to indicate a completed action, resulting in the meaning "inquired" or "asked".

Grammar:
επυθετο is a past tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb "πυνθάνομαι" (pythánomai), which is a deponent verb in Greek. This means that although it is in the passive voice, it has an active meaning. The verb is formed by adding the suffix "-το" (-to) to the stem of the verb.

Description:
The word επυθετο (epytheto) is used to describe an action that has already taken place in the past. It indicates that someone has inquired or asked about something. The word can be used in various contexts, such as inquiring about a person's health, asking for information, or seeking advice.
