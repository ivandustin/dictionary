The word "επιφαναι" (epiphanei) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "επιφαίνω" (epiphaino), which means "to appear" or "to become visible". 

In terms of grammar, "επιφαναι" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" in Greek. 

As for its meaning, "επιφαναι" can be translated as "he/she/it appears" or "he/she/it becomes visible". It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to an object or person that suddenly comes into view, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing a sudden realization or understanding. 

Overall, "επιφαναι" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe the act of something becoming visible or apparent.
