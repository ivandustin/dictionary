Word: επηρθη

Etymology:
The word επηρθη comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pípto), which means "to fall". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) is added to the verb to indicate that the action is sudden or unexpected. Therefore, επηρθη means "he/she/it fell suddenly" or "he/she/it was struck down".

Grammar:
επηρθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. Therefore, επηρθη means "he/she/it was struck down" or "he/she/it fell suddenly".

Description:
επηρθη is a strong and dramatic verb that is often used in Greek literature to describe sudden and unexpected events. It can be used to describe physical falls or metaphorical falls, such as a sudden loss of power or status. The word carries a sense of shock and surprise, and is often used to create a sense of urgency or tension in a narrative.
