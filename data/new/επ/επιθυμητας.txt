Word: επιθυμητάς

Etymology: The word επιθυμητάς comes from the Greek verb επιθυμώ (epithymó), which means "to desire" or "to long for". The suffix -τάς (-tas) is added to the verb stem to form the noun επιθυμητάς, which means "one who desires" or "one who longs for".

Grammar: επιθυμητάς is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or clause.

Description: επιθυμητάς refers to someone who desires or longs for something. It can be used to describe a person who has a strong craving or yearning for something, whether it be a material object, a person, or an abstract concept. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe someone who is ambitious or driven to achieve their goals.
