The word "επελθοντος" is a Greek adjective in the genitive case, which means "coming" or "approaching". Its etymology can be traced back to the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". In terms of grammar, it is a masculine singular form of the adjective, and it agrees with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

In usage, "επελθοντος" can be used to describe a future event or a person who is approaching. For example, "ο επελθών χειμώνας" (o epelethon cheimonas) means "the upcoming winter". 

Overall, "επελθοντος" is a versatile adjective that can be used in various contexts to describe something that is coming or approaching.
