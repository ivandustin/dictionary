Word: επετιμων

Etymology:
The word επετιμων (epetimon) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τιμη (time), which means "honor" or "respect." The prefix επι (epi) means "upon" or "over," so επετιμων can be translated as "he was honoring" or "he was showing respect."

Grammar:
επετιμων is the third person singular, imperfect tense form of the verb επιτιμαω (epitimao), which means "to honor" or "to show respect." In this form, it refers to a male subject who was honoring or showing respect in the past.

Description:
επετιμων is a word that describes an action of showing honor or respect. It can be used to describe a person who is paying tribute to someone else, or who is showing reverence for a particular tradition or custom. The word has a positive connotation and is often used in a religious or ceremonial context.
