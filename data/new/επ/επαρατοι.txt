Word: επαρατοι

Etymology:
The word επαρατοι (eparatoi) is derived from the Greek word επαράτης (eparates), which means "reserve" or "reinforcement". In ancient Greece, the eparatoi were a group of soldiers who were kept in reserve and called upon when needed.

επαρατοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative form of the word, which means it is used as the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive, dative, and accusative, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
επαρατοι refers to a group of soldiers who were kept in reserve in ancient Greece. They were called upon when needed to reinforce the main army. The eparatoi were typically older men who had already served in the army and had gained experience in battle.
