Word: επιστρέφειν

Etymology: The word επιστρέφειν comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and στρέφω (strepho) meaning "to turn". Therefore, επιστρέφειν means "to turn upon" or "to turn over".

Grammar: επιστρέφειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often used in Greek to express the action of returning or going back to a previous state or location.

Description: επιστρέφειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical actions such as returning to a place or turning around, as well as more abstract actions such as changing one's mind or returning to a previous state of being. It is a common verb in Greek and is used in both formal and informal settings.
