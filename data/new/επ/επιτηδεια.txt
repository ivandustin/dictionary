Word: επιτηδεια

Etymology: The word επιτηδεια comes from the Greek root words επί (epi) meaning "upon" or "over" and τηνδε (tinde) meaning "purpose" or "aim". Together, they form the word επιτηδεια which means "skill" or "expertise".

Grammar: επιτηδεια is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is επιτηδείες (epitidies).

Description: επιτηδεια refers to a high level of skill or expertise in a particular area. It can also refer to a craft or trade that requires specialized knowledge and training. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe someone who is resourceful or clever in solving problems.
