Word: επιλαβου

Etymology:
The word επιλαβου comes from the Greek verb επιλαμβάνομαι (epilambanomai), which means "to take hold of" or "to seize". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb λαμβάνω (lambano), meaning "to take".

Grammar:
επιλαβου is a second person singular imperative verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -ου (-ou) to the stem επιλαβ- (epilab-).

Description:
The word επιλαβου is a command or instruction to someone to "take hold of" or "seize" something. It is often used in a figurative sense, such as "take hold of an opportunity" or "seize the day". The word can also be used in a more literal sense, such as "take hold of a tool" or "seize a weapon".
