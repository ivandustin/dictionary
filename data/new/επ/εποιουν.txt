Word: εποίησαν (epoíisan)

Etymology: The word εποίησαν comes from the Greek verb ποιώ (poiṓ), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix επι- (epi-) adds the meaning of "completing" or "finishing", so εποίησαν means "they completed/made/did".

Grammar: εποίησαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb ποιώ. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject (they) performed the action.

Description: εποίησαν is a verb that describes a completed action in the past. It can be translated as "they completed/made/did". It is commonly used in narratives or historical accounts to describe events that have already happened.
