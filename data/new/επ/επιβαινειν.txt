Word: επιβαινειν

Etymology: The word επιβαινειν comes from the Greek roots επί (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and βαίνω (baino), meaning "to walk" or "to step." Therefore, επιβαινειν means "to step on" or "to mount."

Grammar: επιβαινειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν to the stem επιβαιν-.

It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to stepping onto a horse or a vehicle, or in a figurative sense, such as when referring to taking control of a situation. The verb can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects and nuances of the action.
