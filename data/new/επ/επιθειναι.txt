The word "επιθειναι" (epitheinai) is a Greek verb that means "to add on" or "to place upon". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), which means "on" or "upon", and the verb "τιθεναι" (tithenai), which means "to place" or "to put".

Grammar: "Επιθειναι" is a second person singular present active imperative verb. It is often used in commands or instructions, such as "επιθείναι το βάρος στο πόδι" (epitheinai to varos sto podi), which means "place the weight on the foot".

Description: The verb "επιθειναι" is commonly used in everyday Greek language, especially in the context of adding or placing something on top of something else. It can also be used metaphorically, such as in the phrase "επιθείναι βάρος στην καρδιά" (epitheinai varos stin kardia), which means "to burden the heart".
