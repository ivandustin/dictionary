Word: επιστολην

Etymology: The word επιστολην comes from the Greek word επιστολή (epistolē), which means "letter" or "missive". It is derived from the verb επιστέλλω (epistellō), which means "to send a message".

Grammar: επιστολην is the accusative singular form of the noun επιστολή (epistolē). In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: επιστολην refers to a single letter or missive. It can be used to describe a physical letter or a written message sent electronically. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is still used in modern Greek language.
