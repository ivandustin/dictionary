Word: επιποθουντων

Etymology:
The word επιποθουντων is derived from the Greek verb επιποθέω (epipotheo), which means "to long for" or "to desire eagerly". The suffix -ουντων (-ounton) is the present participle form of the verb, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently longing for or desiring something.

Grammar:
επιποθουντων is the genitive plural form of the present participle of the verb επιποθέω. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple people who are currently longing for or desiring something.

Description:
επιποθουντων can be translated to "of those who are longing for" or "of those who desire eagerly". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a group of people who share a common desire or longing for something, such as love, freedom, or success. The word carries a sense of intensity and passion, emphasizing the strong emotions and desires of the people it describes.
