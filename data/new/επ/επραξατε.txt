Word: επραξατε

Etymology: επραξατε is a verb in the Greek language, which is derived from the root word πράττω (pratto), meaning "to do" or "to act".

Grammar: επραξατε is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in the indicative mood. It is used to describe a completed action in the past, specifically an action that was performed by a group of people.

Description: επραξατε can be translated to English as "you (all) did" or "you (all) acted". It is a formal way of addressing a group of people and is commonly used in written or formal speech. The word implies that the action being described has already been completed and is not ongoing.
