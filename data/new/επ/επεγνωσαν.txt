Word: επεγνωσαν

Etymology:
The word επεγνωσαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know." The prefix επε- (epe-) adds the meaning of "fully" or "completely," so επεγνωσαν (epegnosan) means "they fully knew" or "they completely understood."

Grammar:
επεγνωσαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb επιγινώσκω (epiginósko), which means "to recognize," "to acknowledge," or "to understand fully." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice means that the subject (they) performed the action.

Description:
επεγνωσαν is a word that describes a state of complete understanding or recognition. It implies that the subject has gained a deep knowledge or insight into something, and has fully acknowledged its significance or importance. This word can be used in a variety of contexts, from personal relationships to intellectual pursuits, to describe a moment of clarity or realization.
