Word: επερισσευον

Etymology:
The word επερισσευον (eperisseuon) is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It comes from the root word περισσεύω (perisseuo), which means "to be in excess, to abound, to be left over." The prefix επι- (epi-) adds emphasis to the meaning of the root word.

Grammar:
επερισσευον is a present participle verb in the active voice. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The present participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
The word επερισσευον describes something that is in excess or abundance. It can be used to describe a surplus of something, such as food or money. It can also be used to describe a situation where there is more of something than is needed or desired. The word can be used in a positive or negative context, depending on the situation.
