Word: επινον

Etymology:
The word επινον comes from the Greek verb επινοέω (epinoeo), which means "to devise" or "to invent". It is derived from the prefix επι- (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb νοέω (noeo), meaning "to think" or "to perceive".

Grammar:
επινον is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb επινοέω.

Description:
επινον means "he/she/it devises" or "he/she/it invents". It is used to describe the act of coming up with a new idea or creating something new. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from scientific discoveries to artistic creations.
