The word ιματιον (imatíon) comes from the Greek word ἱμάς (himás), which means "strap" or "belt." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁eym- ("to tie, bind").

In Greek, ιματιον is a neuter noun. Its nominative singular form is ιματιον, and its genitive singular form is ιματιου. The word can also be declined in the plural.
