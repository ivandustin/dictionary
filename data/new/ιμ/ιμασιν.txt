The word "ιμασιν" (imasin) is a Greek noun that means "bridles" or "reins". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "ιμάομαι" (imaomai), which means "to put on a bridle" or "to harness". 

Grammar: "Ιμασιν" is a plural noun in the nominative case. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of horse riding or chariot racing, where the reins are used to control the horse or chariot. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to any means of control or restraint.
