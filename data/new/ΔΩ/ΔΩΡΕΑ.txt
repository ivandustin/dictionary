Etymology: The word "δωρεά" (dorea) comes from the Ancient Greek word "δωρεά" (dōrea), which means "gift" or "donation".

Grammar: In Greek, "δωρεά" is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows:

- Singular: η δωρεά (i dorea) - the gift
- Plural: οι δωρεές (i dorees) - the gifts

Description: "Δωρεά" refers to a gift or donation that is given voluntarily, without expecting anything in return. It can be used to describe a physical object or an act of giving. In a broader sense, it can also refer to a talent or ability that someone possesses and freely shares with others.
