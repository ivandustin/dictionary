Word: νοημα (noima)

Etymology:
The word νοημα (noima) comes from the Greek verb νοέω (noeo), which means "to perceive" or "to understand." The noun form, νόημα (noema), means "perception" or "understanding," and the modern Greek form, νοημα (noima), has a similar meaning.

Grammar:
In Greek, νοημα (noima) is a neuter noun. Its plural form is νοήματα (noemata).

Description:
νοημα (noima) refers to the meaning or significance of something. It can also refer to an idea or concept. In philosophy, noema is used to describe the object of thought or perception. The word is often used in the context of language and communication, where it refers to the intended meaning of a message.
