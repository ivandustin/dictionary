It is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "νους" meaning "mind" and "θετειν" meaning "to place". Therefore, the word can be literally translated as "placing the mind" or "advising the mind". 

In terms of grammar, "νουθετουντες" is a present participle verb, which means it is used to describe an ongoing action or state. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of admonishing or warning.
