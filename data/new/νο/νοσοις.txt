Word: νοσοις

Etymology:
The word νοσοις (nosois) is a noun in the dative plural form in Ancient Greek. It is derived from the root word νόσος (nosos), which means "disease" or "illness". The suffix -οις (-ois) indicates the dative plural case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Grammar:
As mentioned above, νοσοις is a noun in the dative plural form. In Ancient Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. For example, in the sentence "Δίδωσι τοις νοσοις φάρμακα" (Didosi tois nosois pharmaka), which means "He gives medicine to the sick", νοσοις is in the dative case and indicates the indirect object of the verb δίδωσι (didosi).

Description:
νοσοις refers to a group of people who are sick or suffering from a disease. It is often used in medical contexts or in descriptions of illnesses or epidemics. In Ancient Greek literature, the word is used in various contexts, such as in the works of Hippocrates, who is known as the father of medicine. Overall, νοσοις is a word that is closely associated with the concept of illness and disease in Ancient Greek.
