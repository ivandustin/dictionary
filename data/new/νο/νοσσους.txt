Word: νοσσούς

Etymology: The word νοσσούς (nossous) comes from the Greek noun νοσσός (nossos), which means "nestling" or "young bird". 

Grammar: νοσσούς is a plural noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a sentence. 

Description: νοσσούς refers to a group of young birds that are still in the nest and have not yet learned to fly. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to young children who are still dependent on their parents or caregivers.
