Word: νομισαντες

Etymology:
The word νομισαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word νομίζω (nomizo), which means "to consider" or "to think." The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates past tense and active voice. Therefore, νομισαντες means "having considered" or "having thought."

Grammar:
νομισαντες is a past participle in the active voice, which means it describes an action that was completed in the past. It is also in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
νομισαντες is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of individuals who have considered or thought about something. It is commonly translated as "having thought" or "having decided." For example, in Acts 15:22, it is used to describe the actions of the apostles and elders who had considered and decided on a course of action.
