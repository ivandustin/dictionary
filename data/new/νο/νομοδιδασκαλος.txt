Word: νομοδιδάσκαλος

Etymology: The word νομοδιδάσκαλος comes from the Greek words νόμος (nomos) meaning "law" and διδάσκαλος (didaskalos) meaning "teacher". Therefore, νομοδιδάσκαλος literally means "law teacher".

Grammar: νομοδιδάσκαλος is a masculine noun in Greek. Its nominative singular form is νομοδιδάσκαλος and its genitive singular form is νομοδιδασκάλου.

Description: νομοδιδάσκαλος was a term used in ancient Greece to refer to a teacher or instructor of law. These individuals were responsible for teaching the laws and legal procedures of the city-state to the citizens. They were highly respected members of society and played an important role in maintaining order and justice.
