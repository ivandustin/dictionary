Word: νοος

Etymology:
The word νοος (noos) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the verb νοέω (noeo), which means "to perceive" or "to understand." It is related to the Latin word "nous," which has a similar meaning.

Grammar:
In Greek, νοος is a masculine noun of the third declension. It is singular and can be used as the subject or object of a sentence.

Description:
νοος is a philosophical term that refers to the intellect or the mind. It is often used in the context of ancient Greek philosophy, particularly in the works of Plato and Aristotle. In these works, νοος is often contrasted with other aspects of the human psyche, such as the emotions or the senses. It is also used in the context of theology, where it can refer to the divine intellect or the mind of God.
