Word: νουθετω

Etymology:
The word νουθετω comes from the Greek words νους (nous) meaning "mind" and θετος (thetos) meaning "placed" or "set". Therefore, νουθετω literally means "to set the mind".

Grammar:
νουθετω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the first person singular as νουθετω, and in the third person singular as νουθετει.

Description:
νουθετω is a Greek verb that means "to admonish", "to warn", or "to advise". It is often used in a moral or ethical context, and implies a sense of correction or guidance towards a better course of action. The word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the teachings of Jesus and the apostles.
