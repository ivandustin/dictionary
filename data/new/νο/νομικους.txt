Word: νομικούς

Etymology: The word νομικούς comes from the Greek word νόμος (nomos), meaning "law" or "custom", and the suffix -ικός (-ikos), which denotes "related to" or "pertaining to". Therefore, νομικούς means "related to law" or "pertaining to law".

Grammar: νομικούς is the plural form of the adjective νομικός (nomikos), which means "legal" or "juridical". It agrees in gender and number with the noun it modifies.

Description: νομικούς is an adjective used to describe things related to law or legal matters. It can be used to describe people, such as lawyers or judges, or things, such as legal documents or procedures. In Greek, it is often used in the context of the legal system and the administration of justice.
