Word: νομικας

Etymology:
The word "νομικας" is derived from the Greek word "νόμος" (nomos), which means "law". The suffix "-ικας" (-ikas) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form adjectives indicating a profession or occupation. Therefore, "νομικας" can be translated as "related to law" or "pertaining to law".

Grammar:
In Greek, "νομικας" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case "νομικού" (nomikou) or the accusative case "νομικό" (nomiko).
