The word "νοι" (noi) is a Greek noun that means "minds" or "intellects". It is the plural form of "νοῦς" (nous), which is a philosophical term used to refer to the rational or intellectual part of the soul. 

Etymology: The word "νοῦς" (nous) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *men-, which means "to think". 

Grammar: "Νοι" (noi) is a third-declension noun in Greek, and its nominative singular form is "νοῦς" (nous). 

Description: In ancient Greek philosophy, "nous" was considered to be the highest part of the soul, responsible for rational thought and understanding.
