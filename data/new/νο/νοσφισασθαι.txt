Word: νοσφισασθαι

Etymology:
The word νοσφισασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word νόσφις (nóphis), which means "to separate" or "to keep apart." The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a middle voice ending that indicates the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Grammar:
νοσφισασθαι is a middle voice aorist infinitive verb in Ancient Greek. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves, and the aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past.

Description:
The word νοσφισασθαι means "to separate oneself" or "to withdraw oneself." It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe the act of separating oneself from worldly desires or distractions in order to focus on spiritual or intellectual pursuits.
