Word: νομικων

Etymology: The word νομικων comes from the Greek word νομικός (nomikos), which means "pertaining to law" or "legal". The suffix -ων (-on) is a masculine plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple things that are related to law.

Grammar: νομικων is a Greek noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive plural form of the noun νομικός (nomikos).

Description: The word νομικων refers to things that are related to law or legal matters. It can be used to describe people, institutions, or objects that are involved in the legal system.
