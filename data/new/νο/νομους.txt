The word "νομους" (nomous) is a Greek noun in the plural form, which means "laws" or "rules". It is derived from the Greek word "νόμος" (nomos), which means "law" or "custom". In Greek mythology, Nomos was the personification of law and order.

In terms of grammar, "νομους" is a second declension masculine noun in the nominative or accusative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, and vocative.

In description, "νομους" refers to a set of regulations or principles that govern a particular society or community. These laws can be written or unwritten and are enforced by a governing authority. In ancient Greece, the nomoi were the laws that governed the city-states and were often attributed to legendary lawgivers such as Solon and Lycurgus.
