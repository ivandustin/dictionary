Word: νομικός

Etymology: The word νομικός comes from the Greek word νόμος (nomos), meaning "law". The suffix -ικός (-ikos) is used to form adjectives, indicating "related to" or "pertaining to" the root word.

Grammar: νομικός is a masculine singular adjective in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: The word νομικός is commonly used in Greek to describe someone or something related to the law. It can refer to a lawyer, a legal expert, or anything related to the legal system. In English, it is often translated as "legal" or "juridical".
