The word "νοειτω" (noeito) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "νοεω" (noeo), which means "to perceive" or "to understand". 

In terms of grammar, "νοειτω" is in the present tense and is in the third person singular form. It can be translated to mean "let him/her/it understand" or "let him/her/it perceive". 

In terms of description, "νοειτω" is often used in a command or imperative sense, as in "let him/her/it understand" or "may he/she/it perceive". It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts, where understanding or perception is seen as a desirable or necessary quality.
