The word "νοειτε" (noeite) is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form, in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "νοε-" (noe-), which means "to perceive" or "to understand". 

Grammar: "νοειτε" is a conjugated form of the verb "νοέω" (noeo), which means "to perceive", "to understand", or "to think". It is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. The second person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people being addressed. 

Description: The word "νοειτε" is used to address a group of people and instruct them to perceive, understand, or think about something. It can be translated to English as "you all perceive", "you all understand", or "you all think".
