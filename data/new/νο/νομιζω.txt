Word: νομίζω

Etymology:
The word νομίζω (nomizo) comes from the Greek root word νόμος (nomos), which means "law" or "custom". The suffix -ίζω (-izo) is a common Greek verb ending that indicates the act of making or doing something.

Grammar:
νομίζω is a Greek verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows a regular pattern of inflection. The verb stem is νομιζ-, and the present tense ending for the first person singular is -ω.

Description:
The verb νομίζω means "to think" or "to believe" in Greek. It is often used to express an opinion or a judgment about something. For example, "Νομίζω ότι έχει δίκιο" (Nomizo oti exei dikio) means "I think he is right". The verb can also be used to indicate a custom or a tradition, as in "Νομίζουν ότι ήταν σωστό" (Nomizoun oti itan sostο), which means "They believed it was the right thing to do".
