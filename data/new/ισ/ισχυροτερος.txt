Word: ισχυροτερος

Etymology: 
The word ισχυροτερος comes from the Greek root word ισχυρός (ischyrós), which means strong or powerful. The suffix -τερος (-teros) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "stronger". Therefore, ισχυροτερος means "stronger" or "more powerful".

Grammar:
ισχυροτερος is an adjective in the comparative form. It is used to compare the strength or power of two things. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, ισχυροτερος will change its form depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
ισχυροτερος is a word that describes something that is stronger or more powerful than something else. It can be used to describe physical strength, as well as strength of character or willpower. For example, one might say "Ο αθλητής είναι ισχυροτερος από τον αντίπαλό του" (The athlete is stronger than his opponent) or "Η δύναμη της θέλησής του τον έκανε ισχυροτερο από τις δυσκολίες" (The strength of his will made him stronger than the difficulties).
