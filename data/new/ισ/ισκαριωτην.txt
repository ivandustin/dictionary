Word: ισκαριωτην

Etymology: The word ισκαριωτην (iskarioten) is derived from the Greek word Ισκαριώθ (Iskarióth), which is the name of a town in Judea. The word means "man from Iskarióth".

Grammar: ισκαριωτην is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, masculine gender. It is the accusative form of the noun ισκαριωτης (iskariotis), which means "a man from Iskarióth".

Description: ισκαριωτην is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve apostles of Jesus Christ who betrayed him for thirty pieces of silver. The term is used to distinguish Judas from the other apostles.
