Word: ισοτιμον

Etymology: The word ισοτιμον comes from the Greek words ισος (isos) meaning "equal" and τιμη (timi) meaning "value". Therefore, ισοτιμον means "of equal value".

Grammar: ισοτιμον is a neuter noun in Greek. Its singular form is ισοτιμον and its plural form is ισοτιμα.

Description: ισοτιμον is a term used in economics to describe two or more currencies that have the same value. It is often used in the context of exchange rates and international trade. When two currencies are of equal value, they can be exchanged without any loss or gain in value. This is important for businesses and individuals who engage in international trade and need to exchange currencies.
