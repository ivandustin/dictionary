The word "ισχυραν" (ischyran) is a form of the Greek verb "ισχύω" (ischyō), which means "to be strong" or "to have power". It is in the third person singular, aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "ισχύς" (ischys), which means "strength" or "power".

Grammar: As mentioned above, "ισχυραν" is in the third person singular, aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating its duration or repetition.

Description: "Ισχυραν" is used to describe someone or something that was strong or had power in the past. It can also be used to describe a sudden burst of strength or power.
