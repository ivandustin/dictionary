Word: ισχυω

Etymology:
The word ισχυω (ischyo) comes from the Greek root word ισχυς (ischys), which means "strength" or "power". The suffix -ω (-o) is a verb ending that indicates the present tense.

Grammar:
Ισχυω is a Greek verb in the present tense, which means "I am strong" or "I have strength". It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω (-o).

Description:
Ισχυω is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physical strength, as in "I am strong enough to lift this heavy object". It can also be used to describe mental or emotional strength, as in "I have the strength to overcome this difficult situation". Additionally, it can be used in a more abstract sense to describe the power or influence of something, as in "The government's policies have a strong impact on the economy".
