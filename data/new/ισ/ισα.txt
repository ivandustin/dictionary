The word "ισα" (isa) is a Greek adverb that means "equally" or "in the same way". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek adjective "ισος" (isos), which means "equal". 

Grammar: "Ισα" is an adverb and does not change form based on gender or number. 

Description: "Ισα" is commonly used in Greek to express that something is done in the same way or to the same extent as something else. For example, "Τα παιδιά έπαιζαν ισα" (Ta paidia epaizan isa) means "The children were playing equally". It can also be used to indicate agreement or confirmation, as in "Ισα ισα" (Isa isa), which means "Exactly" or "That's right".
