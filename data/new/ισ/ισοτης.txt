Word: ισοτης

Etymology: The word ισοτης comes from the Greek words ισος (isos) meaning "equal" and της (tēs) meaning "ness" or "state of being". Therefore, ισοτης means "equality" or "equalness".

Grammar: ισοτης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is of feminine gender.

Description: ισοτης refers to the state or quality of being equal. It is often used in the context of social, political, or economic equality. In mathematics, ισοτης is used to denote an equation or a statement of equality between two expressions. The concept of ισοτης is fundamental to many philosophical and ethical theories, as it is seen as a basic human right to be treated equally and fairly.
