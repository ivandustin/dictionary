The word "ιστανομεν" (istánomen) is a verb in the present tense, active voice, first person plural form in Greek. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "ίστημι" (ístimi), which means "to stand". The suffix "-νομεν" (-nomen) is a common ending for present tense, first person plural verbs in Greek.

Grammar: As mentioned, "ιστανομεν" is a present tense verb, which means that it refers to an action that is currently happening. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The first person plural form means that the subject is "we".

Description: "ιστανομεν" can be translated to "we are standing" or "we stand". It is a simple and straightforward verb that describes the action of standing in the present moment.
