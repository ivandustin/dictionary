Word: ισκαριωθ

Etymology: The word ισκαριωθ (iskariōth) is derived from the Hebrew word "Ish Kerioth" which means "man of Kerioth". Kerioth was a town in the southern part of Judea.

Grammar: ισκαριωθ is a proper noun and is used as a name in the New Testament. It is always written in Greek letters and is not declined.

Description: ισκαριωθ is most commonly known as the surname of Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve apostles of Jesus Christ who betrayed him for thirty pieces of silver. The name is mentioned in all four gospels of the New Testament.
