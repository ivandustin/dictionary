The word "σκυλλεις" (skulleis) is a Greek verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It comes from the root word "σκυλλω" (skullō), which means "to strip off, to plunder, to rob". 

In terms of grammar, "σκυλλεις" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: σκυλλεις (skulleis) - you strip off/plunder/rob
- Plural: σκυλλετε (skulletε) - you all strip off/plunder/rob

As for its description, "σκυλλεις" is a verb that describes an action of forcefully taking something away from someone or something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when talking about robbing or plundering, or in a figurative sense, such as when talking about taking away someone's dignity or rights.
