Word: σκληρότητα

Etymology:
The word σκληρότητα (sklirotita) comes from the Greek adjective σκληρός (skliros), meaning "hard" or "tough". The suffix -τητα (-tita) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form abstract nouns from adjectives.

Grammar:
σκληρότητα is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and is used to describe the quality of being hard or tough.

Description:
σκληρότητα is a word that describes the quality of being hard or tough. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as rocks or metal, as well as abstract concepts, such as a person's personality or a situation. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe emotional or mental toughness.
