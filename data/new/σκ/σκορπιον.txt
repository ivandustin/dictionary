Word: σκορπιόν (skorpión)

Etymology:
The word σκορπιόν (skorpión) comes from the Greek verb σκορπίζω (skorpízo), which means "to scatter" or "to disperse". This is likely due to the scorpion's ability to scatter its venom when attacking.

Grammar:
In Greek, σκορπιόν (skorpión) is a masculine noun. Its plural form is σκορπιοί (skorpioí).

Description:
A σκορπιόν (skorpión) is a type of arachnid with a long, segmented body and a pair of pincers at the front. It also has a curved tail with a stinger at the end, which it uses to inject venom into its prey or attackers. Scorpions are found in many parts of the world, particularly in dry and arid regions. Some species of scorpions are venomous and can be dangerous to humans.
