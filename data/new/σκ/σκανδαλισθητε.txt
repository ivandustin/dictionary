Word: σκανδαλισθητε

Etymology:
The word σκανδαλισθητε comes from the Greek verb σκανδαλίζω (skandalízo), which means "to cause to stumble" or "to cause offense." The suffix -θητε (-thete) is a second-person plural passive ending, indicating that the action of stumbling or causing offense is being done to you.

Grammar:
σκανδαλισθητε is a second-person plural passive imperative verb form in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to be caused to stumble or be offended.

Description:
The word σκανδαλισθητε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 18:6 and Luke 17:2. In both instances, it is used in the context of warning against causing others to stumble or be led into sin. The word carries a strong connotation of moral offense and wrongdoing, and is used to emphasize the seriousness of the sin of causing others to stumble.
