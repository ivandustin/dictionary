Etymology:
The word σκανδαλισθησονται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σκάνδαλον (skandalon), which means "stumbling block" or "offense." The verb form σκανδαλισθησονται is in the future passive indicative tense, meaning "they will be scandalized" or "they will be offended."

Grammar:
σκανδαλισθησονται is a third-person plural verb in the future passive indicative tense. In Greek, verbs change their form to indicate tense, voice, mood, person, and number. The future tense indicates that the action will happen in the future, the passive voice indicates that the subject will receive the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact. The third-person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
The word σκανδαλισθησονται is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 13:41 and 24:10, to describe the fate of those who do not follow God's teachings.
