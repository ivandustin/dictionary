Word: σκυθρωποι

Etymology:
The word σκυθρωποι comes from the Greek words σκυθρωπός (skuthrōpos) meaning "gloomy" or "sullen" and -οι (-oi) which is a plural suffix. Therefore, σκυθρωποι means "gloomy ones" or "sullen ones".

Grammar:
σκυθρωποι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
σκυθρωποι is an adjective used to describe someone who is gloomy or sullen. It can also be used as a noun to refer to a group of people who are gloomy or sullen. The word is often used to describe someone's facial expression or demeanor.
