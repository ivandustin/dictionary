Word: σκληρων

Etymology: σκληρων is the genitive form of the Greek adjective σκληρός (sklēros), which means hard, harsh, or severe. The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *sker- which means to cut or to scrape.

Grammar: σκληρων is a genitive plural form of the adjective σκληρός. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship between nouns.

Description: σκληρων can be translated as "of the hard ones" or "of the harsh ones" in English. It is often used in Greek mythology to describe the tough and unyielding nature of heroes or gods. The word can also be used in a more literal sense to describe something that is physically hard or difficult to break.
