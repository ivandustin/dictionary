Word: σκορπίζει (skorpízei)

Etymology: The word σκορπίζει comes from the Greek verb σκορπίζω (skorpízo), which means "to scatter" or "to disperse". It is derived from the noun σκορπός (skorpós), which means "scorpion".

Grammar: σκορπίζει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb σκορπίζω. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ σκορπίζω (egó skorpízo) - I scatter
- Εσύ σκορπίζεις (esý skorpízeis) - You scatter
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό σκορπίζει (afTós/afTí/afTó skorpízei) - He/She/It scatters
- Εμείς σκορπίζουμε (emeís skorpízoyme) - We scatter
- Εσείς σκορπίζετε (eseís skorpízete) - You (plural) scatter
- Αυτοί/Αυτές σκορπίζουν (afToí/afTés skorpízoyn) - They scatter

Description: σκορπίζει is a verb that describes the action of scattering or dispersing something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as scattering seeds in a garden, or in a figurative sense, such as dispersing a crowd or spreading rumors.
