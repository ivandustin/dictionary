Word: σκανδαλισθησεσθε

Etymology: The word σκανδαλισθησεσθε comes from the Greek word σκάνδαλον (skándalon) which means "stumbling block" or "offense". The suffix -θησεσθε (-thēsesthe) is a passive voice ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Grammar: σκανδαλισθησεσθε is a second person plural passive form of the verb σκανδαλίζω (skandalízo), which means "to cause to stumble" or "to offend". The tense is future passive, indicating that the action will happen to the subject in the future.

Description: σκανδαλισθησεσθε is a warning or a command, telling the listener that they will be offended or caused to stumble in the future.
