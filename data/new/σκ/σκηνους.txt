Word: σκηνους

Etymology:
The word σκηνους (skēnous) is derived from the Greek word σκηνή (skēnē), which means "tent" or "booth". It is related to the verb σκηνόω (skēnoō), which means "to dwell in a tent".

Grammar:
σκηνους is a noun in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender in the Greek language.

Description:
σκηνους is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the tabernacle or tent of meeting used by the Israelites during their wilderness wanderings. It is also used metaphorically to refer to the human body as a temporary dwelling place for the soul.
