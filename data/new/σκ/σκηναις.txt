Word: σκηναις

Etymology:
The word σκηναις (skēnais) is derived from the Greek word σκηνή (skēnē), which means "tent" or "booth". It is related to the Latin word "scena", which means "stage" or "scene".

Grammar:
σκηναις is a noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
σκηναις is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Acts 18:3, where it refers to the tents or booths that were used by Paul and his companions as they traveled and preached. The word can also be used more generally to refer to any type of temporary shelter or dwelling.
