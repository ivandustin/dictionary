Word: σκοπόν

Etymology:
The word σκοπόν (skopón) is derived from the Greek verb σκοπέω (skopéo), which means "to look at, to observe, to aim at". The noun σκοπόν refers to the object or goal that one aims at or looks for.

Grammar:
σκοπόν is a masculine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
σκοπόν is a word that is often used in Greek to refer to a goal, aim, or objective. It can also refer to a target, purpose, or intention.
