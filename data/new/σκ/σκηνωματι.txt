The word "σκηνωματι" (skēnōmati) is a Greek noun that means "tent" or "tabernacle". It is derived from the word "σκηνή" (skēnē), which means "tent" or "dwelling". In grammar, it is a neuter noun in the singular form. The word is often used in the context of the Old Testament, where it refers to the portable tent used by the Israelites during their journey in the wilderness. It is also used in the New Testament to refer to the tabernacle of God.
