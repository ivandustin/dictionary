Word: σκανδαλων

Etymology:
The word σκανδαλων comes from the Greek word σκάνδαλον (skándalon), which means "stumbling block" or "trap". It is derived from the verb σκανδαλίζω (skandalízo), which means "to cause to stumble" or "to offend".

Grammar:
σκανδαλων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is masculine in gender. The word is declined in the genitive case as σκανδάλου (skandálou).

Description:
σκανδαλων is a term used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to a cause of offense or stumbling. It is often used metaphorically to describe something that causes someone to sin or fall away from their faith. In modern Greek, the word is also used to refer to a scandal or controversy.
