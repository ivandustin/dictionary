Word: σκηνοπηγια

Etymology:
The word σκηνοπηγια comes from the Greek words σκηνή (skēnḗ), meaning "tent," and πηγνύω (pēgnýō), meaning "to fix" or "to fasten." Therefore, σκηνοπηγια literally means "tent fixing" or "tent fastening."

Grammar:
σκηνοπηγια is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is written in the Greek alphabet as σκηνοπηγία. The word is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in the nominative, genitive, accusative, and vocative cases.

Description:
σκηνοπηγια refers to the act of setting up or pitching a tent. It can also refer to the equipment or tools used for this purpose. In ancient times, σκηνοπηγια was an important skill for travelers, soldiers, and nomads who needed to set up temporary shelters. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe camping or outdoor activities that involve setting up tents.
