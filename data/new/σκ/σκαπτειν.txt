Word: σκαπτειν

Etymology:
The word σκαπτειν comes from the Greek verb σκάπτω (skáptō), which means "to dig". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *skabh-, which means "to scrape, dig".

σκαπτειν is the present active infinitive form of the verb σκάπτω. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood of the subject. The present tense conjugation of σκάπτω is as follows:

- Σκάπτω (skápto) - I dig
- Σκάπτεις (skápteis) - You dig
- Σκάπτει (skáptei) - He/she/it digs
- Σκαπτούμε (skaptoúme) - We dig
- Σκάπτετε (skáptete) - You all dig
- Σκάπτουν (skáptoun) - They dig

Description:
σκαπτειν is a verb that refers to the act of digging. It can be used in a literal sense, such as digging a hole in the ground, or in a figurative sense, such as digging into a topic or problem to gain a deeper understanding. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is still used in modern Greek.
