Word: σκηνοῦντες (skēnoúntes)

Etymology: The word σκηνοῦντες comes from the Greek verb σκηνόω (skēnóō), which means "to dwell in a tent" or "to live in a temporary shelter." The -οῦντες (-ountes) ending is a present participle suffix, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently dwelling in tents or temporary shelters.

Grammar: σκηνοῦντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe people who are currently dwelling in tents or temporary shelters.

Description: σκηνοῦντες is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Acts 18:3. In this passage, it is used to describe the occupation of Aquila and Priscilla, who were tentmakers by trade. The word can also be used more generally to describe people who are living in tents or temporary shelters, such as nomadic tribes or refugees.
