Word: σκηνη

Etymology:
The word σκηνη (skēnē) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *skēi-, which means "to cut, split". In Ancient Greek, σκηνη originally referred to a tent or a temporary shelter made of skins or cloth. Later, it also came to mean a stage or a theatrical backdrop.

In Ancient Greek, σκηνη is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is σκηνη, and its genitive singular form is σκηνης. The word can also be declined in the plural, with the nominative plural form being σκηναι and the genitive plural form being σκηνων.

In Ancient Greek, σκηνη had multiple meanings depending on the context. It could refer to a tent or a temporary shelter, a stage or a theatrical backdrop, or even a military camp.
