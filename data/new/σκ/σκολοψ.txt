The word "σκολοψ" (skolops) is a Greek noun that means "splinter" or "thorn". It is derived from the Greek words "skolos" (meaning "pointed object") and "ops" (meaning "eye"). In terms of grammar, it is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can be used to describe a small, sharp piece of wood or other material that can cause pain or discomfort if it becomes embedded in the skin.
