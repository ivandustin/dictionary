Word: σκευεσιν

Etymology:
The word σκευεσιν is derived from the Greek verb σκευάζω (skeuázō), which means "to equip" or "to furnish." It is a present active participle, which means that it describes an ongoing action in the present tense.

Grammar:
In Greek, σκευεσιν is a present active participle in the dative plural form. It is often used as an adjective to describe a noun or pronoun in the dative case. The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
The word σκευεσιν can be translated as "to those who are equipping" or "to those who are furnishing." It is often used in the context of military or athletic training, where individuals are being equipped with the necessary tools or skills to succeed. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the act of preparing or providing for someone or something.
