Word: σκορπιοις

Etymology: The word σκορπιοις comes from the Greek word σκορπίος (skorpios), which means "scorpion". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *sker-, which means "to cut" or "to scratch".

Grammar: σκορπιοις is a noun in the dative plural form in Greek. It is often translated as "scorpions" in English.

Description: σκορπιοις refers to a group of arachnids that are characterized by their elongated bodies and venomous stingers. They are found in many parts of the world and are known for their ability to deliver painful and sometimes deadly stings. In Greek mythology, the scorpion is often associated with the goddess Artemis and is sometimes depicted as a symbol of death and destruction.
