Word: σκανδαλα

Etymology:
The word σκανδαλα (skandala) comes from the Greek verb σκανδαλίζω (skandalizo), which means "to cause to stumble" or "to cause offense." The noun form σκανδαλα refers to the stumbling block or obstacle that causes offense.

Grammar:
In Greek, σκανδαλα is a neuter noun in the plural form. Its singular form is σκάνδαλον (skandalon).

Description:
σκανδαλα is a word that refers to something that causes offense or scandal. It can be used to describe a situation or action that is considered morally or socially unacceptable. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the stumbling block that causes someone to sin or fall away from their faith. Today, the word is still used in Greek to describe scandalous behavior or events.
