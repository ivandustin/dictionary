Word: σκηνην

Etymology:
The word σκηνην (skēnēn) is a Greek word that means "tent" or "tabernacle". It is derived from the root word σκηνή (skēnē), which means "a covering" or "a shelter". The word σκηνή is also the root of the English word "scene".

Grammar:
σκηνην is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. So, σκηνην would be used to indicate the object of an action, such as "I set up the tent" (στήνω τη σκηνην).

σκηνην is a word that refers to a tent or tabernacle. In ancient times, tents were used as temporary shelters for nomadic people, and tabernacles were used as portable places of worship.
