Word: γλωσσαν

Etymology:
The word γλωσσαν (glōssan) is derived from the Greek word γλῶσσα (glōssa), which means "tongue" or "language". It is related to the Latin word lingua, which has a similar meaning.

Grammar:
In Greek, γλωσσαν is the accusative singular form of γλῶσσα (glōssa). It is used to indicate the direct object of a sentence. For example, "Ο μαθητής μαθαίνει τη γλώσσαν" (O mathitis mathaínei ti glóssan) means "The student is learning the language".

Description:
γλωσσαν is a Greek word that refers to the tongue or a language. It can be used to describe the physical organ in the mouth that is used for tasting and speaking, as well as the language or dialect spoken by a particular group of people. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday conversation.
