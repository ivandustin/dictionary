Ζαχαρία (Zachariah) is a male given name of Hebrew origin, meaning "God remembers". It is derived from the Hebrew name זְכַרְיָה (Zekharyah), which is composed of two elements: זָכַר (zakhar), meaning "to remember", and יָה (yah), a shortened form of the name of God. In the Bible, Zachariah was a prophet and the father of John the Baptist. The name is also spelled as Zacharias or Zechariah in English. In terms of grammar, ζαχαρία is a masculine noun in the nominative case.
