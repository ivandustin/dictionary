Ζακχαιε (Zacchaeus) is a Greek name that appears in the New Testament. Its etymology is uncertain, but it is believed to be a variant of the Hebrew name זַכַּי (Zakkai), which means "pure" or "innocent". 

In terms of grammar, ζακχαιε is a proper noun and is typically capitalized in writing. It is also a masculine name. 

Description-wise, ζακχαιε is most famously known as the name of a tax collector who climbed a tree to see Jesus in the Gospel of Luke. He is described as a short man who was despised by the people for his profession, but who was ultimately redeemed by his encounter with Jesus.
