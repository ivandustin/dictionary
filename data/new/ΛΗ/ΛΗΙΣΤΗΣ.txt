Etymology: The word "ληστης" (pronounced "leestis") comes from the Greek word "ληστηρ" (pronounced "leestir"), which means "robber" or "bandit". It is derived from the verb "ληστεύω" (pronounced "leestefo"), meaning "to rob" or "to plunder".

Grammar: In Greek, "ληστης" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "ληστες" (pronounced "leestes").
