Etymology: The word "ληιστων" (lēistōn) comes from the Greek verb "ληίζω" (lēizō), which means "to forget" or "to neglect."

Grammar: "Ληιστων" is a noun in the genitive case, singular number, masculine gender. It is the genitive form of the noun "ληιστής" (lēistēs), which means "one who forgets" or "one who neglects."

Description: "Ληιστων" refers to someone who forgets or neglects something. It can be used to describe a person who fails to remember or pay attention to important details or responsibilities.
