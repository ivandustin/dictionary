Etymology: The word "ληνού" (lēnoú) comes from the Greek word "ληνός" (lēnós), which means "millstone" or "mill." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *lendh- meaning "to grind."

Grammar: "Ληνού" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender.

Description: "Ληνού" refers to a miller, someone who operates or works in a mill. In ancient times, mills were used to grind grains into flour. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is hardworking or diligent.
