Etymology: The word "ληνος" (lēnos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *lēh₁- meaning "to grind" or "to crush."

Grammar: In Greek, "ληνος" (lēnos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case, and its genitive form is "ληνου" (lēnou).

Description: "Ληνος" (lēnos) refers to a millstone or a grinding stone used for grinding grain or other materials. It was an essential tool in ancient times for processing food and other substances.
