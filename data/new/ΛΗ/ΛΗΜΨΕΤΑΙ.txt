Etymology: The word "λημψεται" (lēmpsetai) comes from the Greek verb "λαμβάνω" (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to receive". It is conjugated in the future tense, third person singular form.

Grammar: "λημψεται" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "λημψεται" is used to describe an action that will be taken or received by a third person in the future. It can be translated as "he/she/it will take" or "he/she/it will receive" in English.
