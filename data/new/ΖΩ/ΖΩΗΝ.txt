Etymology: The word "ζωην" (zōēn) comes from the Greek verb "ζῶ" (zō), which means "to live" or "to be alive."

Grammar: "ζωην" (zōēn) is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence, and the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "ζωην" (zōēn) means "life" in Greek. It refers to the state of being alive or the period of time during which a living organism exists. It can also be used metaphorically to describe vitality, energy, or the essence of something.
