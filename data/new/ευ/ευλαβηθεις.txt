Word: ευλαβηθεις

Etymology:
The word ευλαβηθεις comes from the Greek root words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and λαβη (labē) meaning "to take". The suffix θεις (theis) is added to form the verb form, which means "to take care" or "to be cautious".

Grammar:
ευλαβηθεις is a verb in the aorist tense, second person singular form. It is derived from the verb ευλαβεομαι (eu-lab-e-o-mai), which means "to be cautious" or "to take care". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description:
The word ευλαβηθεις is used to describe someone who is cautious, careful, or takes care in their actions. It can also be used to describe someone who is respectful or reverent towards something or someone. In the context of religion, it can refer to someone who is devout or pious.
