Word: ευφρανθηναι

Etymology: The word ευφρανθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ευφραινω (euphraino), which means "to make happy" or "to cheer up". The -θηναι (-thenai) ending indicates that it is in the aorist passive voice, meaning that the action was done to the subject.

Grammar: ευφρανθηναι is a second person singular aorist passive form of the verb ευφραινω (euphraino). It is translated as "you were made happy" or "you were cheered up". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject received the action rather than performed it.

Description: ευφρανθηναι is a verb that describes the state of being made happy or cheered up. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as being made happy by the grace of God.
