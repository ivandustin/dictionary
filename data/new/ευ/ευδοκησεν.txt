The word "ευδοκησεν" (eudokesen) is a Greek verb that means "to be well pleased" or "to take pleasure in." It is derived from the root words "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well," and "δοκεω" (dokeo), meaning "to think" or "to seem." In terms of grammar, "ευδοκησεν" is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the third person singular form, indicating that the subject is a singular entity.
