Word: ευαγγελιζομενοι

Etymology: The word ευαγγελιζομενοι comes from the Greek root words ευαγγέλιον (euangelion) meaning "good news" or "gospel" and ιζω (izo) meaning "to make" or "to do". Therefore, ευαγγελιζομενοι means "those who proclaim the good news" or "those who preach the gospel".

Grammar: ευαγγελιζομενοι is a present participle in the Greek language, which means it is a verb form that describes an ongoing action. In this case, it describes the action of proclaiming or preaching the good news. It is in the masculine plural form, which means it refers to a group of people who are preaching the gospel.

Description: ευαγγελιζομενοι is a word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the apostles and other early Christian missionaries who spread the message of Jesus Christ. It emphasizes the importance of sharing the good news of salvation with others and is a reminder of the mission of the Christian church to spread the gospel to all nations.
