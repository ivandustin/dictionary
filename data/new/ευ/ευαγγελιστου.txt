The word "ευαγγελιστου" (evangelistou) is a Greek noun that means "evangelist" or "bearer of good news". It is derived from the Greek word "ευαγγέλιο" (evangelio), which means "good news" or "gospel". 

In terms of grammar, "ευαγγελιστου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It can be translated as "of the evangelist" or "belonging to the evangelist". 

As for its description, "ευαγγελιστου" is a word that is often used in the context of Christianity, specifically in reference to the four evangelists (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John) who wrote the four Gospels of the New Testament. It can also refer to anyone who spreads the message of Christianity or any other message of good news.
