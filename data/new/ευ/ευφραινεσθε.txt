Word: ευφραινεσθε

Etymology:
The word ευφραινεσθε comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of two words: ευ (eu) meaning "good" and φραινω (phraino) meaning "to make happy". Therefore, the literal meaning of ευφραινεσθε is "be happy" or "rejoice".

Grammar:
ευφραινεσθε is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is used as a command or an imperative, meaning that it is a direct request or instruction to the listener to be happy or to rejoice.

Description:
ευφραινεσθε is a word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, particularly in Christian liturgy and hymns. It is a call to celebrate and give thanks for the blessings of life, and to find joy in the present moment.
