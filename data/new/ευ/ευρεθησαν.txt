The word ευρεθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εὑρίσκω (heurisko), which means "to find" or "to discover". The verb is in the passive voice and the aorist tense, indicating that the action of finding or discovering has already taken place.

The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, an unspecified group of people) received the action of the verb rather than performing it themselves. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

The word ευρεθησαν can be translated to English as "they were found" or "they were discovered". The context in which the word is used will determine what or who was found or discovered.
