Word: ευπροσδεκτος

Etymology: The word ευπροσδεκτος comes from the Greek roots ευ- (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and προσδεκτος (prosdektos), meaning "acceptable" or "welcomed." Therefore, ευπροσδεκτος can be translated as "well-received" or "welcomed."

Grammar: ευπροσδεκτος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, ευπροσδεκτη (feminine singular), ευπροσδεκτο (neuter singular), and ευπροσδεκτοι (masculine plural) are all possible forms.

Description: ευπροσδεκτος is a word used to describe something or someone that is well-received or welcomed. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as welcoming guests, accepting an invitation, or receiving good news. It conveys a positive connotation and is often used to express gratitude or appreciation.
