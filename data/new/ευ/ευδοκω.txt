The word "ευδοκω" (eudoko) is a Greek verb that means "to be well pleased" or "to take pleasure in." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek roots "eu" meaning "good" and "dokos" meaning "opinion" or "judgment."

Grammar: "Ευδοκω" is a first-person singular present indicative active verb in Greek. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and persons.

Description: The word is often used in a positive context to express satisfaction or approval. It can be used to describe one's own feelings or the feelings of others. For example, "Ευδοκω που σε βλέπω" (eudoko pou se blepo) means "I am pleased to see you."
