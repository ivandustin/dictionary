The word "ευδοκησα" (eudokesa) is a Greek verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It comes from the root word "ευδοκέω" (eudokeo), which means "to be well pleased" or "to approve." 

In terms of grammar, "ευδοκησα" is a regular verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The "-σα" ending indicates that it is in the first person singular form.

Overall, the word "ευδοκησα" can be translated to mean "I was well pleased" or "I approved." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to express a sense of satisfaction or approval from a higher power.
