Word: ευαγγελιζομαι

Etymology: The word ευαγγελιζομαι comes from the Greek root words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and αγγελος (angelos) meaning "messenger". Together, they form the verb ευαγγελιζομαι which means "to bring good news" or "to proclaim the gospel".

Grammar: ευαγγελιζομαι is a first-person singular present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

ευαγγελιζομαι (present tense)
ευαγγελιζομην (imperfect tense)
ευαγγελισθην (aorist tense)
ευαγγελισομαι (future tense)

Description: ευαγγελιζομαι is a verb that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of preaching or proclaiming the gospel. It is often translated as "to evangelize" or "to preach the good news". The word emphasizes the importance of spreading the message of salvation and hope to others.
