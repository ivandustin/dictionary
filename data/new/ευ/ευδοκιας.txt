Word: ευδοκιας

Etymology:
The word ευδοκιας (eudokia) is derived from the Greek words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and δοκιμή (dokime) meaning "approval" or "judgment". It is a compound word that means "good judgment" or "good will".

Grammar:
ευδοκιας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
ευδοκιας is a term that refers to the concept of having good judgment or approval. It can be used to describe a person who is wise and discerning, or a decision that is well thought out and beneficial. In the context of Christianity, ευδοκιας is often used to describe God's good will or pleasure towards humanity.
