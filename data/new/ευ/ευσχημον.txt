Word: ευσχημον

Etymology: The word ευσχημον comes from the Greek roots ευ- (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and σχημα (schēma), meaning "form" or "shape." Together, ευσχημον means "well-formed" or "well-behaved."

Grammar: ευσχημον is an adjective in Greek, and it can be used to describe a person or their behavior. It is declined like other Greek adjectives, with different forms for masculine, feminine, and neuter genders, as well as for singular and plural numbers.

Description: ευσχημον is a word that is often used to describe someone who behaves in a polite, respectful, and appropriate manner. It can also be used to describe something that is aesthetically pleasing or well-proportioned. In modern Greek, it is often used in the context of social etiquette and manners.
