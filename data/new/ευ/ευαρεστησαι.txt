Word: ευαρεστησαι

Etymology:
The word ευαρεστησαι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: ευ (eu) meaning "good" and αρεστός (arestos) meaning "pleasing". Therefore, ευαρεστησαι means "to please" or "to be pleased".

Grammar:
ευαρεστησαι is a first-person singular present tense verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

ευαρεστώ (I please)
ευαρεστείς (you please)
ευαρεστεί (he/she/it pleases)
ευαρεστούμε (we please)
ευαρεστείτε (you please)
ευαρεστούν (they please)

Description:
The word ευαρεστησαι is commonly used in Greek to express the act of pleasing someone or being pleased. It can be used in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, business, or social situations. The verb can also be used in a figurative sense, such as pleasing oneself by achieving a goal or fulfilling a desire.
