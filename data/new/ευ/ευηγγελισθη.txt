Word: ευηγγελισθη

Etymology: 
The word ευηγγελισθη (euangelisthē) is a Greek verb that comes from the noun εὐαγγέλιον (euangelion), which means "good news" or "gospel." The verb form means "to proclaim good news" or "to evangelize."

Grammar:
ευηγγελισθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action. 

Description:
ευηγγελισθη is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark (1:15). It is used to describe the preaching of Jesus, who proclaimed the good news of the kingdom of God. The word emphasizes the idea of spreading the message of salvation and hope to others.
