The word "ευραν" (euran) is a Greek noun that means "sky" or "heaven". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ουρανός" (ouranos), which also means "sky" or "heaven". In Greek mythology, Ouranos was the god of the sky and the father of the Titans. 

In terms of grammar, "ευραν" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is declined in the first declension and has the genitive form "ευρανού" (euranou) and the accusative form "ευρανό" (eurano). 

In description, "ευραν" is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the vastness and beauty of the sky. It is also used in religious contexts to refer to the abode of the gods or the afterlife.
