Word: ευκοπωτερον

Etymology:
The word ευκοπωτερον is derived from the Greek words ευκοπος (eukopos) meaning "easy" and πιπτω (pipto) meaning "to fall". The suffix -τερον (-teron) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "rather".

Grammar:
ευκοπωτερον is a comparative adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to compare the ease of falling between two things.

Description:
The word ευκοπωτερον describes something that is more easily fallen into or more prone to falling. It can be used in a literal sense, such as describing a slippery surface, or in a figurative sense, such as describing a situation that is more likely to lead to failure.
