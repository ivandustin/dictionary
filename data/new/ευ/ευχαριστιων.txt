The word "ευχαριστιων" is a Greek verb that means "to give thanks" or "to express gratitude". It is derived from the words "ευχαριστία" (eucharistia), which means "thanksgiving" or "gratitude", and "χαρις" (charis), which means "grace" or "favor". 

In terms of grammar, "ευχαριστιων" is a present participle, which means it describes an ongoing action of giving thanks or expressing gratitude. It can be used as an adjective to describe a person who is thankful or grateful, or as a noun to refer to the act of giving thanks.

Overall, "ευχαριστιων" is a word that emphasizes the importance of gratitude and appreciation in Greek culture.
