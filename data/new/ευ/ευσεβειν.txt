Word: ευσεβειν

Etymology:
The word ευσεβειν comes from the Greek word εὐσεβής (eusebēs), which means "pious" or "devout". It is derived from the prefix εὖ (eu), meaning "well" or "good", and the root σέβω (sebō), meaning "to worship" or "to revere".

Grammar:
ευσεβειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often translated as "to be pious" or "to be devout". The verb is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem ευσεβ- (euseb-).

Description:
ευσεβειν is a term used in ancient Greek literature to describe a person who is deeply religious and devoted to the gods. It is often used in a positive sense to describe someone who is morally upright and virtuous. In Christian literature, the term is used to describe a person who is devout and faithful to God. The concept of ευσέβεια (eusebeia), or piety, was highly valued in ancient Greek and Roman society, and was considered a key virtue in leading a good life.
