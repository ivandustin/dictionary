The word "ευλογηκεν" is a Greek verb that means "blessed" or "praised." It is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "ευλογέω" (eulogeó), which comes from the combination of the words "ευ" (eu), meaning "good," and "λογος" (logos), meaning "word" or "reason." Therefore, "ευλογηκεν" can be translated as "he/she/it has been blessed" or "he/she/it has been praised." In terms of grammar, it is a past tense verb in the active voice.
