The word "ευλογουμεν" (eulogoumen) is a Greek verb in the present passive participle form. It comes from the root word "ευλογέω" (eulogeo), which means "to bless" or "to praise." The present passive participle form indicates that the action of blessing or praising is being done to the subject of the sentence, rather than by the subject. 

In terms of grammar, "ευλογουμεν" is in the third person plural form, meaning that it refers to a group of people or things being blessed or praised. 

Overall, "ευλογουμεν" is a word that conveys the idea of being blessed or praised, and is often used in religious or spiritual contexts.
