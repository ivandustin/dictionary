Word: ευχαριστησεν

Etymology: The word ευχαριστησεν comes from the Greek root words ευχαριστία (eucharistia) meaning "gratitude" and ευχαριστώ (eucharisto) meaning "to give thanks". The suffix -σεν (-sen) indicates the past tense.

Grammar: ευχαριστησεν is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb ευχαριστώ (eucharisto) which means "to give thanks" or "to express gratitude". 

Description: ευχαριστησεν means "he/she/it thanked" in Greek. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and in formal settings to express gratitude or appreciation. The word is often used in religious contexts, particularly in reference to the Christian sacrament of the Eucharist, which is a ritual of thanksgiving.
