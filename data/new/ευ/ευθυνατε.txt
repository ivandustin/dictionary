The word "ευθυνατε" is a verb in Greek, which means "to take responsibility" or "to be accountable". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root words "ευθύς" (euthys) meaning "straight" or "direct" and "νομίζω" (nomizo) meaning "to consider" or "to think". 

Grammar: "Ευθυνατε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "ευθυνώ" (euthyno), which means "I take responsibility". 

Description: The word is commonly used in formal and informal settings to express the idea of taking responsibility for one's actions or decisions. It can be used in various contexts, such as in the workplace, in personal relationships, or in public service.
