Word: ευθυμειν

Etymology:
The word ευθυμειν comes from the Greek words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and θυμος (thymos) meaning "spiritedness" or "passion". Therefore, ευθυμειν literally means "to have a good spirit" or "to be in good spirits".

Grammar:
ευθυμειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem ends in -ει. The verb is composed of the prefix ευ- and the stem θυμειν.

Description:
ευθυμειν is a Greek verb that describes the state of being in good spirits or having a positive attitude. It can be used to describe a person's emotional state or as an encouragement to someone to maintain a positive outlook. The verb is often used in literature and poetry to convey a sense of optimism and hope.
