The word "ευνουχοι" (eunouchoi) is a Greek term that refers to castrated men. The word is derived from the Greek words "eu" meaning "well" and "nouchos" meaning "guardian" or "keeper". In ancient times, eunuchs were often employed as servants or officials in royal courts and harems, as they were considered to be trustworthy and loyal due to their inability to have children. In terms of grammar, "ευνουχοι" is a plural noun in the nominative case.
