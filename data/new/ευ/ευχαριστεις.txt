The word "ευχαριστεις" (efcharisteis) is a verb in the Greek language, which means "you thank" in English. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words, "ευ" (ef) meaning "good" and "χαρις" (charis) meaning "grace" or "favor". 

Grammar: "Ευχαριστεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "ευχαριστώ" (efcharisto), which means "to thank". 

Description: The word is commonly used in everyday conversations and is considered a polite way to express gratitude or appreciation towards someone. It is often used in the context of saying "thank you" or expressing thanks for a favor or a kind gesture.
