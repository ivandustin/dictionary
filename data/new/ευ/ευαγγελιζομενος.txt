Word: ευαγγελιζομενος

Etymology: The word ευαγγελιζομενος comes from the Greek root words ευαγγέλιον (euangelion) meaning "good news" or "gospel" and ιζω (izo) meaning "to make" or "to become". Therefore, ευαγγελιζομενος means "one who proclaims or spreads the good news".

Grammar: ευαγγελιζομενος is a present participle in the Greek language, which means it is a verb form that describes an ongoing action. It is derived from the verb ευαγγελιζω (euangelizo) which means "to proclaim or announce good news". The -ομενος (-omenos) ending indicates that the subject is masculine and singular.

Description: ευαγγελιζομενος is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe those who proclaim or spread the good news of Jesus Christ. It is often translated as "preaching" or "evangelizing" in English. The word emphasizes the importance of sharing the message of salvation with others and is a key concept in Christian theology.
