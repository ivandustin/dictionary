Word: ευτυχος

Etymology:
The word ευτυχος comes from the Greek roots ευ- (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and τυχη (tyche), meaning "luck" or "fortune." Therefore, ευτυχος literally means "having good fortune" or "fortunate."

Grammar:
In Greek, ευτυχος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person or thing that is fortunate or lucky. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, ευτυχος can become ευτυχης (masculine singular), ευτυχη (feminine singular), ευτυχοι (masculine plural), or ευτυχες (neuter plural).

Description:
ευτυχος is a word that describes a state of being fortunate or lucky. It can be used to describe a person who has good luck or a situation that has a positive outcome. The word is often used in Greek culture to express congratulations or well wishes for someone who has achieved success or good fortune.
