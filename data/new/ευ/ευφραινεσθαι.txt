The word "ευφραινεσθαι" (euphrainesthai) is a Greek verb that means "to be glad, to rejoice, to be happy." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "ευ" (eu) meaning "good" and "φρην" (phrēn) meaning "mind." Therefore, "ευφραινεσθαι" literally means "to have a good mind."

Grammar: "ευφραινεσθαι" is a present middle/passive infinitive verb form. It can be conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to match the subject and context of the sentence.

Description: The word "ευφραινεσθαι" is often used in Greek literature and everyday language to express joy, happiness, and contentment. It can be used to describe a person's emotional state or as an exclamation of celebration.
