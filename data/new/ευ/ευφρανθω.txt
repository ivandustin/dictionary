Word: ευφρανθω

The word ευφρανθω comes from the Greek roots ευ- (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and φρην (phren), meaning "mind" or "spirit." Together, ευφρανθω means "to make glad" or "to cheer up."

ευφρανθω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the passive voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
ευφρανθω is a word that describes the act of bringing joy or happiness to someone.
