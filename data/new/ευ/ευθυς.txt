Word: ευθυς

Etymology: The word ευθυς comes from the Greek root words ευ (eu) meaning "good" or "well" and θυω (thuo) meaning "to sacrifice" or "to offer". Together, ευθυς means "straight" or "direct", as if offering a good sacrifice in a straight line.

Grammar: ευθυς is an adjective in Greek, meaning it describes a noun as being straight or direct. It can be used in the masculine, feminine, or neuter gender, and in the nominative, genitive, dative, or accusative case.

Description: ευθυς is a word that describes something that is not crooked or bent, but rather goes in a straight line. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a straight road or a straight line, or it can be used to describe a person's character, as being honest and straightforward. In Greek philosophy, ευθυς was considered a virtue, as it represented a person who was truthful and just in their actions.
