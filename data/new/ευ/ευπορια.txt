Word: ευπορία

Etymology:
The word ευπορία (euphoria) comes from the Greek words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and πόρος (poros) meaning "passage" or "way out". The term was first used in ancient Greek literature to describe a state of well-being and happiness.

Grammar:
In Greek, ευπορία is a feminine noun. Its plural form is ευπορίες (euphoríes). It is often used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural to refer to multiple instances of euphoria.

Description:
Euphoria is a feeling of intense happiness and well-being. It is often described as a state of elation or ecstasy. Euphoria can be triggered by a variety of experiences, such as achieving a goal, falling in love, or using drugs. It is a temporary state that can last from a few minutes to several hours. Euphoria is often accompanied by physical sensations such as increased heart rate, sweating, and a feeling of lightness. While euphoria can be a positive experience, it can also be dangerous if it is caused by drug use or other risky behaviors.
