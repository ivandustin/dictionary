Word: ευφραινομενος

Etymology: 
The word ευφραινομενος comes from the Greek root words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and φραινω (phraino) meaning "to make happy". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing someone who is currently experiencing the action of the verb.

Grammar:
ευφραινομενος is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb ευφραινω (eufraino), which means "to make happy" or "to cheer up". The present participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
The word ευφραινομενος describes someone who is currently experiencing happiness or joy. It can also be translated as "cheerful" or "merry".
