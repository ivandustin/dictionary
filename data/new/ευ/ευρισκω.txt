Word: ευρισκω

The word ευρισκω (eurisko) comes from the Greek root εὑρίσκω (heurisko), which means "to find" or "to discover". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *wer- ("to perceive, watch out for").

ευρισκω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that its stem ends in -α- and its endings follow a regular pattern.

ευρισκω is a common Greek verb that means "to find" or "to discover". It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as finding a lost object, discovering a new idea, or uncovering a hidden truth. It is often used in combination with other verbs to express different shades of meaning, such as ευρίσκω τον χρόνο (eurisko ton chronon), which means "to find the time" or "to make time".
