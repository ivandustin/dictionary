The word "ευσεβειαις" (eusebeiais) is a Greek noun that is derived from the adjective "ευσεβής" (eusebēs), which means "pious" or "devout." The noun form can be translated as "piety" or "devotion." 

In terms of grammar, "ευσεβειαις" is in the dative case, which indicates the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. 

Overall, "ευσεβειαις" refers to a state of being devout or showing reverence towards a deity or religious beliefs. It can also be used to describe acts of piety or devotion.
