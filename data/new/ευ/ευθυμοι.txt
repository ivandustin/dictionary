Word: ευθυμοι

Etymology:
The word ευθυμοι (efthymoi) comes from the Greek words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and θυμος (thymos) meaning "spiritedness" or "passion". Therefore, ευθυμοι can be translated as "good-spirited" or "cheerful".

Grammar:
ευθυμοι is the plural form of the adjective ευθυμος (efthymos), which means "good-spirited" or "cheerful". It is used to describe a group of people who are in a good mood or have a positive attitude.

Description:
ευθυμοι is a Greek adjective that describes a group of people who are cheerful, optimistic, and have a positive outlook on life. It is often used to describe a group of friends or colleagues who are in a good mood and enjoying each other's company. The word can also be used to describe a person who is generally cheerful and optimistic.
