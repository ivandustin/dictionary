In terms of grammar, "ευοδουσθαι" is a second aorist passive infinitive. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not being used as a specific tense or mood, but rather as a verbal noun.

Overall, "ευοδουσθαι" can be translated to mean "to be prospered" or "to be successful". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to being blessed or favored by a higher power.
