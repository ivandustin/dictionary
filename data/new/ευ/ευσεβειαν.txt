The word "ευσεβειαν" (eusebeian) is a Greek noun that means "piety" or "devotion." It is derived from the adjective "ευσεβής" (eusebes), which means "pious" or "god-fearing." In Greek mythology, the term was used to describe individuals who showed reverence and respect towards the gods. 

In terms of grammar, "ευσεβειαν" is a third-declension feminine noun in the accusative case. It can also be spelled as "εὐσέβειαν" in the original Greek alphabet.

Overall, "ευσεβειαν" is a word that emphasizes the importance of showing respect and devotion towards a higher power or authority.
