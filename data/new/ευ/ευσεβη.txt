Etymology: The word comes from the Greek roots "ευ-" (eu-), meaning "good" or "well", and "σεβη" (sebi), meaning "reverence" or "piety". 

Grammar: "Ευσεβη" is the feminine form of the adjective, with the masculine form being "ευσεβής" (eusebis) and the neuter form being "ευσεβές" (eusebes). 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is deeply religious or devoted to their faith. It can also be used to describe actions or behaviors that are considered morally upright or righteous.
