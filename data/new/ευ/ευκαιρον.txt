The word "ευκαιρον" (eukairon) is a Greek adjective that means "opportune" or "timely". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "ευ" (eu) meaning "good" or "well" and "καιρός" (kairos) meaning "time" or "opportunity". 

Grammar: "Ευκαιρον" is a neuter singular form of the adjective. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word is often used to describe a situation or event that occurs at the right time or is advantageous. It can also be used to describe a person who is available or ready to take advantage of an opportunity.
