The word "ευαγγελιωι" (euangelioi) is a Greek word that means "evangelists" or "gospel writers". It is derived from the Greek words "eu" meaning "good" and "angelos" meaning "messenger". In Greek grammar, "ευαγγελιωι" is a plural noun in the dative case. It is used to refer to the authors of the four Gospels in the New Testament: Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John. These writers are considered to be messengers of the good news of Jesus Christ.
