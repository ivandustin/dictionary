Word: ευπειθης

Etymology:
The word ευπειθης (eupethēs) is derived from the Greek words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and πειθω (peithō) meaning "to persuade". Therefore, the literal meaning of ευπειθης is "easily persuaded" or "compliant".

Grammar:
ευπειθης is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine, feminine, and neuter genders and declines in the same way as other adjectives in Greek.

Description:
ευπειθης is a word that describes a person who is easily persuaded or compliant. It can be used to describe someone who is willing to follow orders or instructions without question. It can also be used to describe someone who is easily influenced by others or who is easily swayed by arguments or opinions. In a positive sense, ευπειθης can be used to describe someone who is cooperative and willing to work with others.
