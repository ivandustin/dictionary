Word: ευνοων

Etymology:
The word ευνοων comes from the Greek root words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and νοος (noos) meaning "mind" or "intelligence". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common ending for masculine nouns in Greek.

Grammar:
ευνοων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is singular and can be translated to mean "one who favors" or "one who is well-disposed towards".

Description:
In ancient Greek society, ευνοων referred to someone who was well-disposed towards another person or group. This could be in the form of political support, financial assistance, or simply a friendly attitude. The term was often used in the context of patronage, where a wealthy or influential person would support a less powerful individual or group. In modern Greek, the word has taken on a more general meaning of "supporter" or "advocate".
