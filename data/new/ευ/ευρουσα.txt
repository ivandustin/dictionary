The word "ευρουσα" (euroosa) is a Greek verb in the present tense, which means "I find" or "I discover". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "ευρ-" (eur-), which means "to find" or "to discover". 

Grammar: "Ευρουσα" is the first person singular form of the present tense of the verb "ευρίσκω" (eurisko), which means "to find" or "to discover". 

Description: The word "ευρουσα" is commonly used in everyday Greek language to express the act of finding or discovering something. It can be used in various contexts, such as finding a lost item, discovering a new idea, or uncovering a hidden truth.
