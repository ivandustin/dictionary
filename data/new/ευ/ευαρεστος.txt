Word: ευαρεστος

Etymology:
The word ευαρεστος comes from the Greek roots ευ- (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and αρεστος (arestos), meaning "pleasing" or "acceptable." Therefore, ευαρεστος means "well-pleasing" or "agreeable."

Grammar:
In Greek, ευαρεστος is an adjective that agrees with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a masculine singular noun, it would be ευαρεστος. If it modifies a feminine singular noun, it would be ευαρεστη. If it modifies a neuter singular noun, it would be ευαρεστον. If it modifies a plural noun, the ending would change accordingly.

Description:
The word ευαρεστος is often used to describe something that is pleasing or agreeable to someone. It can be used to describe a person's behavior, a situation, or even a taste or smell. It is a positive adjective that conveys a sense of satisfaction or contentment.
