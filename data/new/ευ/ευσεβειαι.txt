The word "ευσεβειαι" (eusebeiai) is a Greek noun that can be translated as "piety" or "devotion." It is derived from the adjective "ευσεβής" (eusebēs), which means "pious" or "devout." In Greek mythology, the goddess Eusebia was the personification of piety and devotion.

In terms of grammar, "ευσεβειαι" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive ("ευσεβειων"), dative ("ευσεβειαις"), and accusative ("ευσεβειας").

Overall, "ευσεβειαι" refers to the quality of being devout or showing reverence towards a higher power or authority. It can also be used to describe acts of piety or religious devotion.
