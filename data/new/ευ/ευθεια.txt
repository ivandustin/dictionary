The word "ευθεία" (eftheia) is a Greek term that can be translated to mean "straight" or "straight line". It is derived from the Greek words "ευ" (eu), meaning "good" or "well", and "θεία" (theia), meaning "divine" or "godly". 

In terms of grammar, "ευθεία" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case "ευθείας" (eftheias) or the accusative case "ευθείαν" (eftheian).

In geometry, "ευθεία" refers to a straight line that extends infinitely in both directions. It is often used in mathematical equations and formulas to represent a linear relationship between two variables.

Overall, "ευθεία" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts, from mathematics to everyday language.
