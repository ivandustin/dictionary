Word: ευθυμειτε

Etymology: The word ευθυμειτε comes from the Greek words ευθύμως (euthymos) meaning "cheerfully" or "optimistically" and εἶτε (eite) meaning "either" or "or". 

Grammar: ευθυμειτε is a verb in the imperative mood, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb ευθυμέω (euthymeō) which means "to be in good spirits" or "to be cheerful". 

Description: ευθυμειτε is a command to a group of people to be cheerful or optimistic. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to encourage a group of friends to enjoy themselves or to motivate a team to stay positive during a difficult task.
