Word: ευσχημονας

Etymology:
The word ευσχημονας comes from the Greek words ευ (eu) meaning "good" and σχημα (schēma) meaning "form" or "shape". Therefore, ευσχημονας can be translated as "having a good form" or "well-formed".

Grammar:
ευσχημονας is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
ευσχημονας is used to describe someone or something that is well-behaved, proper, or decorous. It can also refer to something that is aesthetically pleasing or well-proportioned. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe someone who is polite or courteous.
