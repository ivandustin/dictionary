Word: ευχρηστος

Etymology: The word ευχρηστος comes from the Greek roots ευ- (eu-), meaning "good" or "well," and χρηστος (chrēstos), meaning "useful" or "serviceable." Therefore, ευχρηστος means "well-suited" or "convenient."

Grammar: ευχρηστος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: ευχρηστος is used to describe something that is easy to use, convenient, or well-suited for a particular purpose. It can be used to describe a tool, a piece of technology, or even a person who is helpful and accommodating. It is a positive and useful word that conveys a sense of efficiency and practicality.
