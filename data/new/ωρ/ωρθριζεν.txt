The word "ωρθριζεν" (orthrizen) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek word "ωρθρος" (orthros), which means "dawn" or "daybreak". 

Grammar: The word is in the present tense, third person singular form. 

Description: The word means "to dawn" or "to become light". It is often used to describe the moment when the sun rises and the sky begins to lighten. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the beginning of a new era or the start of a new day.
