Word: ωρυξεν

Etymology: ωρυξεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb ωρύομαι (ōrýomai), which means "to dig" or "to excavate". The verb is derived from the noun ωρυγή (ōrygḗ), which means "a digging" or "an excavation".

Grammar: ωρυξεν is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the indicative mood, which is used to make statements or ask questions. The verb is in the active voice, which means that the subject is performing the action.

Description: ωρυξεν describes the action of digging or excavating that was completed in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing archaeological excavations, mining activities, or even digging a hole in the ground. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of uncovering or discovering something hidden or unknown.
