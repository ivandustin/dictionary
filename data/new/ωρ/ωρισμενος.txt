Word: ωρισμένος

Etymology:
The word ωρισμένος (orismenos) is derived from the Greek verb ωρίζω (orizo), which means "to define" or "to determine". The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates a state or condition.

Grammar:
ωρισμένος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description:
The word ωρισμένος is used to describe something that has been defined, determined, or established. It can also mean "appointed" or "designated". In modern Greek, it is often used in legal contexts to refer to a person who has been appointed to a position or role.
