Word: ωρυομενος

Etymology: ωρυομενος is a present participle of the Greek verb ωρύομαι (ōrúomai), which means "to dig, excavate". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *wer- meaning "to turn, bend".

Grammar: ωρυομενος is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is declined as follows:

Singular: ωρυομενος (masculine), ωρυομενη (feminine), ωρυομενον (neuter)

Plural: ωρυομενοι (masculine), ωρυομεναι (feminine), ωρυομενα (neuter)

Description: ωρυομενος describes someone who is currently digging or excavating. It can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is delving deeply into a subject or investigating something thoroughly.
