The suffix "-μένοι" ("-menoi") is added to the verb stem to indicate the past participle form, resulting in "ητοιμασμένοι" (etimasmenoi), which translates to "prepared" or "ready".

It is used to describe a group of people or things that have been prepared or made ready for a certain purpose. In Greek grammar, the past participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.
