Etymology: The word "ητοιμασίας" (etimassias) comes from the Greek verb "ετοιμάζω" (etimazo), which means "to prepare" or "to get ready".

Grammar: "ητοιμασίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "ητοιμασίας" refers to the act or process of preparing or getting ready for something. It can be used in various contexts, such as preparing for an event, organizing materials, or making arrangements.
