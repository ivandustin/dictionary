Etymology: The word "ητιμασαν" (etimasan) comes from the Greek verb "τιμάω" (timao), which means "to honor" or "to esteem". The suffix "-σαν" (-san) indicates the past tense, so "ητιμασαν" (etimasan) translates to "they honored" or "they esteemed".

Grammar: "Ητιμασαν" (etimasan) is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "τιμάω" (timao). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The "-ασαν" (-asan) ending is used for the past tense of regular verbs in the third person plural.

Description: "Ητιμασαν" (etimasan) is a Greek verb that describes an action that happened in the past. It specifically refers to the act of honoring or esteeming someone or something.
