Etymology: The word "ητοιμασμένην" comes from the Greek verb "ετοιμάζω" (etoimazo), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready." The suffix "-μένην" is added to the verb to indicate the past participle form, making it "ητοιμασμένην" (etimasmenin), which translates to "prepared" or "made ready."

Grammar: "Ητοιμασμένην" is the feminine singular form of the past participle of the verb "ετοιμάζω" (etoimazo). In Greek grammar, the past participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this case, "ητοιμασμένην" is used to modify a feminine singular noun.

Description: "Ητοιμασμένην" is an adjective in Greek that describes something or someone that has been prepared or made ready. It can be used to indicate that something is ready for use or that someone is prepared for a specific task or situation.
