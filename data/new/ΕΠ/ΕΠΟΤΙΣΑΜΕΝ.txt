Etymology: The word "εποτισαμεν" comes from the Greek verb "ποτίζω" (potízo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to the verb to indicate the past tense, resulting in "εποτίσαμεν" (epotísamen).

Grammar: "Εποτίσαμεν" is the first person plural form of the verb "ποτίζω" in the past tense. It is used to indicate that "we" (a group of people) performed the action of watering or irrigating in the past.

Description: "Εποτίσαμεν" is a word in the Greek language that specifically refers to the past action of watering or irrigating. It is used to describe the act of providing water to plants, crops, or any other objects that require hydration.
