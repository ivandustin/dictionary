Etymology: The word "επεπεσε" comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pípto), which means "to fall". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to intensify the action, so "επεπεσε" can be translated as "to fall heavily" or "to fall with force".

Grammar: "επεπεσε" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, it indicates that someone or something fell heavily or with force at a specific point in the past.

Description: "επεπεσε" is a verb in Greek that describes the action of falling heavily or with force. It can be used to describe a physical fall, as well as metaphorical falls or failures.
