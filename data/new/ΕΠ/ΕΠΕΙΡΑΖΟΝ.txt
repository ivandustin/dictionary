Etymology: The word "επειραζον" comes from the Greek verb "πειράζω" (peirazo), which means "to test" or "to tempt". It is derived from the noun "πείρα" (peira), meaning "trial" or "attempt".

Grammar: "Επειραζον" is the present participle form of the verb "πειράζω" (peirazo) in the Greek language. It is used to describe an ongoing action of testing or tempting.
