Etymology: The word "επιστρέψει" comes from the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistréfo), which means "to return" or "to come back". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb "στρέφω" (stréfo), meaning "to turn".

Grammar: "Επιστρέψει" is the third person singular future tense form of the verb "επιστρέφω" (epistréfo). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated according to person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "Επιστρέψει" is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it will return" or "he/she/it will come back" in English. It expresses the idea of someone or something going back to a previous location or state.
