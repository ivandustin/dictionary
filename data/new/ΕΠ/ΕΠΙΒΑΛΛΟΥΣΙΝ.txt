Etymology: The word "επιβαλλουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "επιβάλλω" (epiválo), which means "to impose" or "to enforce". The suffix "-ουσιν" indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "Επιβαλλουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The present tense indicates an action happening in the current moment. The third person plural form refers to multiple individuals or entities performing the action.

Description: "Επιβαλλουσιν" is a Greek verb that means "they impose" or "they enforce". It is used to describe the action of compelling or forcing something upon others.
