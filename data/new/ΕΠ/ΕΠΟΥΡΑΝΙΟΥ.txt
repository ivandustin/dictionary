Etymology: The word "επουρανίου" (epouraniou) comes from the Greek words "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "ουρανίου" (ouraniou), meaning "heavenly" or "celestial." 

Grammar: "Επουρανίου" is an adjective in the genitive case, masculine gender, and singular number. It is used to describe something that is related to or belongs to the heavens or the celestial realm.

Description: "Επουρανίου" is often used in Greek mythology and literature to describe gods, goddesses, or other divine beings who reside in the heavens. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is elevated, sublime, or of great importance.
