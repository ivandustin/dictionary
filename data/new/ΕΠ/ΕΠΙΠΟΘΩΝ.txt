Etymology: The word "επιποθων" (epipothon) comes from the Greek verb "επιποθώ" (epipotho), which means "to yearn for" or "to long for". 

Grammar: "Επιποθών" is the masculine singular form of the present participle of the verb "επιποθώ". In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. 

Description: "Επιποθών" is an adjective that describes someone or something that is yearning or longing for something. It can be used to express a strong desire or craving for someone or something.
