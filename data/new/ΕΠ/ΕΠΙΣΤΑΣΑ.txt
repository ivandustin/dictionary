Etymology: The word "επιστασία" (epistasia) comes from the Greek word "επί" (epi), meaning "upon" or "over," and "στάσις" (stasis), meaning "standing" or "position." 

Grammar: "Επιστασία" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "επιστασίες" (epistasies). 

Description: "Επιστασία" refers to the act or state of standing upon or overseeing something. It can be used to describe a position of authority, supervision, or control. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the act of being knowledgeable or well-informed about a particular subject.
