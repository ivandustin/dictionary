Etymology: The word "επισκοπούντες" comes from the Greek verb "επισκοπώ" (episkopó), which means "to oversee" or "to inspect." The suffix "-ούντες" is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Επισκοπούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "επισκοπώ" in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently overseeing or inspecting something. In English, it can be translated as "overseeing" or "inspecting."

Description: "Επισκοπούντες" refers to a group of people who are currently engaged in the act of overseeing or inspecting something. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a professional setting where supervisors or managers are monitoring the work of their subordinates, or in a broader sense where individuals are observing or examining a particular situation or event.
