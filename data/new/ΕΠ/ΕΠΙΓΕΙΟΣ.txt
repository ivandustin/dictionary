Etymology: The word "επιγειος" (epigeios) comes from the Greek roots "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon," and "γη" (ge), meaning "earth." Therefore, "επιγειος" can be translated as "on the earth" or "earthly."

Grammar: In Greek, "επιγειος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms depending on the context.

Description: "Επιγειος" is used to describe something that is related to or happening on the earth's surface. It can refer to earthly or terrestrial things, as opposed to celestial or heavenly ones.
