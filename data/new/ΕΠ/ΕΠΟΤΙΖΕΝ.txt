Etymology: The word "εποτιζεν" comes from the Greek verb "ποτίζω" (potízo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate." The prefix "επ-" (ep-) indicates the action of doing something thoroughly or intensively.

Grammar: "Εποτίζεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "ποτίζω" in the imperfect tense. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "Εποτίζεν" describes the action of watering or irrigating. It indicates that someone or something was regularly or repeatedly performing this action in the past.
