Etymology: The word "επλησθη" (eplēsthē) comes from the Greek verb "πληθω" (plēthō), which means "to fill" or "to be filled". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pelh₁- ("to fill").

Grammar: "Επλησθη" is the third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb "πληθω" (plēthō) in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Επλησθη" is a verb form that means "he/she/it was filled" in Greek. It is used to describe a state of being filled or becoming full.
