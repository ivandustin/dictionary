Etymology: The word "επεθυμεί" comes from the Greek verb "επιθυμώ" (epithymó), which means "to desire" or "to long for". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb "θυμός" (thymós), meaning "desire" or "passion".

Grammar: "Επεθυμεί" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "επιθυμώ" (epithymó). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Επεθυμεί" is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it desires" or "he/she/it longs for" in English. It expresses a strong feeling of wanting or craving something.
