Etymology: The word "επιθεις" (epithēis) comes from the Greek verb "ἐπιτίθημι" (epitithēmi), which means "to place upon" or "to add to". In grammar, an epithet is a word or phrase that describes or characterizes a noun.

Grammar: In Greek grammar, an epithet is a word or phrase that is used to describe or qualify a noun. It is often used to provide additional information or to emphasize a particular quality or characteristic of the noun. Epithets can be adjectives, participles, or noun phrases.

Description: Epithets are commonly used in literature and poetry to create vivid and memorable descriptions. They can be used to enhance the imagery, evoke emotions, or highlight certain aspects of a person, object, or concept. Epithets can also be used in everyday language to provide more specific or detailed descriptions.
