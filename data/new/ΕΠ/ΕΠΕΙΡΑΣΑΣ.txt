Etymology: The word "επειρασας" (epairasas) comes from the Greek verb "πειράω" (peirao), which means "to try" or "to attempt". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "on" or "upon", giving the word the sense of "trying on" or "attempting".

Grammar: "Επειρασας" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is used to address someone directly and indicates that the action of trying or attempting was performed by that person.

Description: "Επειρασας" is a verb in Greek that means "you tried" or "you attempted". It implies that the person being addressed made an effort to do something, but it does not specify the outcome or success of that attempt.
