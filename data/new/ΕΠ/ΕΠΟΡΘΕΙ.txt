Etymology: The word "επορθει" comes from the Greek verb "επορθεω" (eportheo), which means "to destroy" or "to ravage". 

Grammar: "Επορθει" is the third person singular form of the verb "επορθεω" in the present tense. It is used to describe an action being done by a singular subject.

Description: "Επορθει" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it destroys" or "he/she/it ravages". It is commonly used to describe the act of causing destruction or devastation.
