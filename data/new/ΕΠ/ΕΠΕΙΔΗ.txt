Etymology: The word "επειδή" (epidí) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epí), meaning "upon" or "because of," and "είδος" (eídos), meaning "form" or "kind." 

Grammar: "Επειδή" is a conjunction in Greek that is used to introduce a cause or reason. It is similar to the English word "because" or "since." It is often followed by a clause that explains the reason for something.

Description: "Επειδή" is a common word in Greek that is used to express causality or provide a reason for something. It is used to connect two clauses, with the first clause stating the cause or reason and the second clause stating the effect or result. It is an important word in Greek grammar and is frequently used in everyday conversation and writing.
