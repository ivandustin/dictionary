Etymology: The word "επεστρεψαν" (epestrepsan) is derived from the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistrefo), which means "to return" or "to come back". It is formed by combining the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "back", and the verb "στρέφω" (strefo), meaning "to turn".

Grammar: "Επεστρεψαν" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "επιστρέφω" (epistrefo). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action of returning or coming back happened in the past, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: "Επεστρεψαν" is a Greek word that translates to "they returned" or "they came back" in English. It describes an action where a group of people or things went back to a previous location or state.
