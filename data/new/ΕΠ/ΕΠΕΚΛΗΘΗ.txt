Etymology: The word "επεκληθη" (epiklithi) comes from the Greek verb "επικαλούμαι" (epikaloumai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke." It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on," and the verb "καλώ" (kalo), meaning "to call."

Grammar: "Επεκληθη" is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "επικαλούμαι" (epikaloumai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Επεκληθη" is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it was called upon" or "he/she/it was invoked" in English. It is used to describe an action in the past where someone or something was called upon or invoked by someone else.
