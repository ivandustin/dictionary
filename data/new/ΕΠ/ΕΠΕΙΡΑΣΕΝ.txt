Etymology: The word "επειρασεν" comes from the Greek verb "πειράζω" (peirazo), which means "to try" or "to test". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "on". Therefore, "επειρασεν" can be translated as "he/she/it tried" or "he/she/it tested".

Grammar: "επειρασεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "πειράζω" (peirazo) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The "-εν" ending indicates the third person singular form.

Description: "επειρασεν" is a verb form that describes an action of trying or testing that was completed in the past. It can be used to talk about someone's attempt or experiment.
