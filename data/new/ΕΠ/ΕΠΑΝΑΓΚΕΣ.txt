Etymology: The word "επαναγκες" (epanankes) comes from the Greek roots "επανά" (epana), meaning "again" or "re-", and "ανάγκη" (ananki), meaning "necessity" or "compulsion". 

Grammar: "Επαναγκες" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of the noun.

Description: "Επαναγκες" refers to "necessities" or "compulsions" in Greek. It can be used to describe things that are essential or indispensable.
