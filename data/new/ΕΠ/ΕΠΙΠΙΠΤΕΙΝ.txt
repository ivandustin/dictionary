Etymology: The word "επιπτείν" comes from the Greek verb "επιπίπτω" (epípito), which means "to fall upon" or "to attack". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on", and the verb "πίπτω" (pípto), meaning "to fall".

Grammar: "Επιπτείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ επιπτεύω (Egó epiptévo) - I fall upon
- Εσύ επιπτεύεις (Esý epiptévis) - You fall upon
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιπτεύει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó epiptévi) - He/She/It falls upon
- Εμείς επιπτεύουμε (Emeís epiptévoume) - We fall upon
- Εσείς επιπτεύετε (Eseís epiptévete) - You fall upon
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επιπτεύουν (Aftoí/Aftés epiptévoun) - They fall upon

Description: "Επιπτείν" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It typically conveys the action of falling upon or attacking something or someone. It can be used both literally and metaphorically, depending on the context.
