Etymology: The word "επιπεσοντες" comes from the Greek verb "επιπίπτω" (epipípto), which means "to fall upon" or "to attack". It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on", and the verb "πίπτω" (pípto), meaning "to fall".

Grammar: "Επιπεσόντες" is the plural form of the verb "επιπίπτω" in the present participle tense. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Επιπεσόντες" describes a group of people or things that are currently falling upon or attacking something. It implies an active and dynamic action.
