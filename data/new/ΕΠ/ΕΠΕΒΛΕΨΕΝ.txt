Etymology: The word "επεβλέψεν" comes from the Greek language. It is a verb form derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over," and the verb "βλέπω" (blepo), meaning "to see."

Grammar: "Επεβλέψεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "βλέπω" (blepo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action.

Description: "Επεβλέψεν" translates to "he/she/it looked upon" or "he/she/it saw" in English. It describes a single completed action of looking or seeing that occurred in the past.
