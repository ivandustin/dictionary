Etymology: The word "επορευετο" comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreúomai), which means "to go" or "to travel." The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "on" or "upon," giving the word the sense of "to go upon" or "to proceed."

Grammar: "Επορευετο" is the third person singular form of the verb "πορεύομαι" in the imperfect tense. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood. The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: "Επορευετο" can be translated as "he/she/it was going" or "he/she/it used to go." It indicates an action that was happening in the past but was not completed. The verb "πορεύομαι" implies movement or travel, suggesting that the subject was in the process of going somewhere.
