Etymology: The word "επεστρεψα" (epestrepsa) is derived from the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistrefo), which means "to return" or "to come back". It is formed by combining the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb "στρέφω" (strefo), meaning "to turn".

Grammar: "Επέστρεψα" (epestrepsa) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "επιστρέφω" (epistrefo). In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The past tense indicates an action that has already happened in the past.

Description: "Επέστρεψα" (epestrepsa) translates to "I returned" or "I came back" in English. It is used to express the action of going back to a previous location or state.
