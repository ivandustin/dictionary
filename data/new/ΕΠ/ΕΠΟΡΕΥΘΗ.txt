Etymology: The word "επορευθη" (eporeuthe) comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreuomai), which means "to go" or "to travel." The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the sense of "away" or "out." 

Grammar: "Επορεύθη" is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "πορεύομαι" (poreuomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact, and the active voice shows that the subject performs the action.

Description: "Επορεύθη" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it went" or "he/she/it traveled." It is used to describe a completed action of someone or something moving from one place to another.
