Etymology: The word "επραξαν" (epraxan) comes from the Greek verb "πράττω" (pratto), which means "to do" or "to act". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) indicates the past tense, so "επραξαν" translates to "they did" or "they acted".

Grammar: "Επραξαν" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "πράττω" (pratto). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The "-αν" ending in "επραξαν" is specific to the past tense, third person plural form.

Description: "Επραξαν" is a verb form used to describe an action that was done or performed by a group of people in the past. It can be translated as "they did" or "they acted" in English.
