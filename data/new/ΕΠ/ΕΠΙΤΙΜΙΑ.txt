Etymology: The word "επιτιμία" comes from the Greek word "επιτίμιος" (epitimios), which means "punishment" or "penalty". It is derived from the verb "επιτιμάω" (epitimao), meaning "to punish" or "to reprimand".

Grammar: "Επιτιμία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "επιτιμίες" (epitimies).

Description: "Επιτιμία" refers to a punishment or penalty imposed on someone as a result of their actions or behavior. It can also be used to describe a reprimand or a severe criticism. The term is often used in legal contexts or in situations where disciplinary action is taken.
