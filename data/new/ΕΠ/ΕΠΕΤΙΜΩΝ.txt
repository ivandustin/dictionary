Etymology: The word "επετιμών" comes from the Greek verb "επιτιμάω" (epitimáo), which means "to reprimand" or "to rebuke". 

Grammar: "Επετιμών" is the present participle form of the verb "επιτιμάω" in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe someone who is currently reprimanding or rebuking.

Description: "Επετιμών" refers to a person who is actively reprimanding or rebuking someone. It can be used as an adjective to describe someone's behavior or as a noun to refer to someone who frequently engages in reprimanding others.
