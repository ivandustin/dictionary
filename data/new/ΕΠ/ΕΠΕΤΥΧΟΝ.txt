Etymology: The word "επετυχον" (epetychon) comes from the Greek verb "επιτυγχάνω" (epitygchano), which means "to succeed" or "to achieve". 

Grammar: "Επετυχον" is the first person singular, aorist tense of the verb "επιτυγχάνω" (epitygchano). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe completed actions in the past.

Description: "Επετυχον" is a Greek word that means "I succeeded" or "I achieved". It is used to express the completion of a successful action in the past.
