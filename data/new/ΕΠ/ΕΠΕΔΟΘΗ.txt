Grammar: "Επεδόθη" (epedothi) is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "επιδίδωμι" (epididomi). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is receiving the action.

Description: "Επεδόθη" (epedothi) can be translated as "it was given" or "it was granted." It is used to describe a past event or action in which something was given or bestowed upon someone or something.
