The word "επικαλείσθαι" is a Greek verb that means "to invoke" or "to call upon." Here is a breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "επικαλείσθαι" comes from the combination of two Greek words: "επί" (epi), meaning "upon" or "on," and "καλείσθαι" (kaleisthai), meaning "to call." Therefore, the literal translation of "επικαλείσθαι" is "to call upon" or "to invoke."

Grammar: "Επικαλείσθαι" is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In English, it can be translated as "to be calling upon oneself" or "to be invoking."

Description: The verb "επικαλείσθαι" is used to express the act of invoking or calling upon someone or something for assistance, support, or guidance. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, where individuals call upon deities, saints, or higher powers for help or protection. It can also be used in a more general sense to indicate the act of seeking help or support from someone or something.
