Etymology: The word "επιζητησης" (epizitisis) comes from the Greek verb "επιζητώ" (epizito), which means "to seek" or "to desire". The suffix "-σης" (-sis) is added to form the noun form.

Grammar: "Επιζητησης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Επιζητησης" refers to the act of seeking or desiring something. It can be translated as "seeking" or "desire" in English.
