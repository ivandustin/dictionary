Etymology: The word "επιτροπούς" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" meaning "on" or "over" and the noun "τροπούς" meaning "turn" or "direction".

Grammar: "Επιτροπούς" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes "επιτρόποι".

Description: "Επιτροπούς" refers to a person who is appointed or authorized to act on behalf of someone else, usually in a position of authority or responsibility. It can be translated as "delegate", "representative", or "commissioner" in English.
