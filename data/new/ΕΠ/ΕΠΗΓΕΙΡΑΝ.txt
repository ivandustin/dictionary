Etymology: The word "επηγειραν" comes from the Greek verb "επαγειρω" (epageiro), which means "to raise up" or "to awaken". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "up", and the verb "αγειρω" (ageiro), meaning "to raise" or "to lift".

Grammar: "Επηγείραν" is the third person plural, past tense form of the verb "επαγειρω" (epageiro) in Greek. It is used to indicate that a group of people raised up or awakened something or someone in the past.

Description: "Επηγείραν" is a Greek verb that describes the action of raising up or awakening. It can be used in various contexts, such as waking someone up from sleep, lifting something from a lower position to a higher one, or figuratively, to describe the act of arousing or stirring up emotions or thoughts.
