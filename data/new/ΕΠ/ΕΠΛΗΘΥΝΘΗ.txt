Etymology: The word "επληθυνθη" comes from the Greek verb "πληθύνω" (plithyno), which means "to multiply" or "to increase in number". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) indicates the action of "increasing" or "making more".

Grammar: "Επληθυνθη" is the third person singular form of the aorist passive indicative tense of the verb "πληθύνω" (plithyno). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Επληθυνθη" describes the action of something or someone being multiplied or increased in number. It implies that the subject has undergone a process of becoming more numerous or abundant.
