Etymology: The word "επιταγήν" (epitagin) comes from the Greek word "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "τάγη" (tagi), meaning "order" or "command." 

Grammar: "Επιταγήν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Επιταγήν" refers to a written order or command, typically issued by a person or an authority, instructing someone to do something or to make a payment. It is commonly used in the context of financial transactions, where it represents a formal request for payment to be made from one party to another.
