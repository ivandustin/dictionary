Etymology: The word "επιγραφήν" (epigraphēn) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "γράφω" (graphō), meaning "to write." It refers to the act of writing or inscribing something on a surface.

Grammar: "Επιγραφήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Επιγραφήν" refers to an inscription or a written message that is usually engraved or written on a surface such as stone, metal, or wood. It can be found on various objects, such as monuments, buildings, tombstones, or plaques, and it often serves to provide information, commemorate an event, or mark a specific location.
