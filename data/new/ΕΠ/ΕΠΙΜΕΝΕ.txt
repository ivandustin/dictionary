Etymology: The word "επιμένειν" (epiménein) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epí), meaning "on" or "upon," and "μένειν" (ménein), meaning "to remain" or "to stay." 

Grammar: "Επιμένειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "Επιμένειν" means "to persist" or "to insist" in English. It refers to the act of continuing to do or believe in something despite opposition, difficulty, or discouragement.
