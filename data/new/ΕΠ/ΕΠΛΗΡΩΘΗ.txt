Etymology: The word "επληρωθη" (eplērōthē) comes from the Greek verb "πληρόω" (plēroō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". It is derived from the noun "πλήρης" (plērēs), meaning "full" or "complete".

Grammar: "Επληρωθη" (eplērōthē) is the past tense form of the verb "πληρόω" (plēroō) in the passive voice. It is used to indicate that something was filled or completed in the past.

Description: "Επληρωθη" (eplērōthē) is a Greek word that can be translated as "was filled" or "was completed". It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe the fulfillment of prophecies or the completion of a task or mission.
