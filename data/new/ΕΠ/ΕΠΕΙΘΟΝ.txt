Etymology: The word "επειθον" (epithon) comes from the Greek verb "επείθω" (epitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince."

Grammar: "Επειθον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the neuter gender and singular number.

Description: "Επειθον" refers to the act of persuading or convincing someone. It can also be used to describe the power or ability to persuade or convince others.
