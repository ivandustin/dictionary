Etymology: The word "επιπληξης" (epiplixis) comes from the Greek verb "επιπλήσσω" (epiplisso), which means "to strike upon" or "to attack". 

Grammar: "Επιπληξης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case and is used to describe the act of striking or attacking.

Description: "Επιπληξης" refers to the act of striking or attacking someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as physical attacks, verbal attacks, or even metaphorical attacks. The word implies a forceful and aggressive action towards a target.
