Etymology: The word "επαναγων" (epanagon) comes from the Greek roots "επανά" (epana), meaning "again" or "re-", and "αγων" (agon), meaning "contest" or "struggle". 

Grammar: "Επαναγων" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. 

Description: "Επαναγων" refers to someone who participates in a contest or struggle again, or someone who returns to compete after a previous attempt or absence. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who faces challenges or obstacles with determination and perseverance.
