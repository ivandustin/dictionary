Etymology: The word "επιγραφη" (epigrafi) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "γράφω" (grapho), meaning "to write." It refers to the act of inscribing or writing on a surface.

Grammar: "Επιγραφη" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Επιγραφη" refers to an inscription or a written message that is usually engraved or written on a surface such as a stone, metal, or wood. It can be found on various objects, such as monuments, buildings, tombstones, or plaques, and serves to provide information, commemorate an event or person, or convey a message. Epigraphy is the study of these inscriptions and their historical, linguistic, and cultural significance.
