Etymology: The word "επικεκληται" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by the prefix "επι-" meaning "upon" or "over" and the verb "κεκληται" meaning "called" or "summoned". 

Grammar: "επικεκληται" is a verb in the perfect tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "καλέω" (to call) in the perfect tense. The perfect tense indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood is used to state a fact or make a statement.

Description: "επικεκληται" means "he/she/it has been called" or "he/she/it has been summoned" in English. It refers to someone or something that has received a call or summons.
