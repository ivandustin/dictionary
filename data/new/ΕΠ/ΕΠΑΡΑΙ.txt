Etymology: The word "επαραι" (eparai) comes from the Greek verb "επαραινω" (eparaino), which means "to lift up" or "to raise." It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "above," and the verb "αιρω" (airo), meaning "to lift" or "to take away."

Grammar: "Επαραι" is a verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ επαραίνω (ego eparaino) - I lift up
- Εσύ επαραίνεις (esi eparainis) - You lift up
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επαραίνει (aftos/afti/afto eparaini) - He/She/It lifts up
- Εμείς επαραίνουμε (emeis eparainoume) - We lift up
- Εσείς επαραίνετε (eseis eparainete) - You (plural) lift up
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επαραίνουν (aftoi/aftes eparainoun) - They lift up

Description: "Επαραι" is an action verb that describes the act of lifting or raising something. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically lifting an object, raising one's hand, or figuratively lifting someone's spirits.
