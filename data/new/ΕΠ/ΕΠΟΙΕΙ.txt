Etymology: The word "εποιει" (epoiei) comes from the Greek verb "ποιώ" (poió), which means "to do" or "to make". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "on". Therefore, "εποιει" can be translated as "he/she/it was doing" or "he/she/it was making".

Grammar: "Εποιει" is the third person singular form of the verb "ποιώ" in the past continuous tense. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, and mood. The past continuous tense is used to describe an action that was ongoing in the past.

Description: "Εποιει" is a verb form that indicates an ongoing action in the past. It is commonly used in storytelling or to describe past events that were happening at a specific time.
