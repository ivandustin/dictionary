Etymology: The word "επιστευσα" (episteusa) comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pistevo), which means "to believe" or "to trust". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) in this word adds the meaning of "upon" or "to" the action of believing.

Grammar: "Επιστεύσα" is the first person singular aorist tense of the verb "πιστεύω" (pistevo). The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this case, it indicates that the speaker believed or trusted at a specific point in the past.

Description: "Επιστεύσα" is a Greek word that means "I believed" or "I trusted". It is used to express an act of believing or trusting that occurred in the past.
