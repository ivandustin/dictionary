Etymology: The word "επιστρέψας" comes from the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistréfo), which means "to return" or "to come back". The suffix "-ας" (-as) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle.

Grammar: "Επιστρέψας" is the masculine singular form of the past participle of the verb "επιστρέφω". In Greek grammar, past participles are used to form compound tenses and passive voice constructions.

Description: "Επιστρέψας" means "having returned" or "having come back" in English. It is used to describe someone or something that has completed the action of returning or coming back in the past.
