Etymology: The word "επερωτώντα" comes from the Greek verb "επερωτώ" (eperoto), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". The suffix "-ντα" (-nta) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating ongoing or continuous action.

Grammar: "Επερωτώντα" is the present participle form of the verb "επερωτώ" (eperoto) in Greek. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently asking or inquiring.

Description: "Επερωτώντα" can be translated as "asking" or "inquiring" in English. It is commonly used as an adjective to describe a person who is actively asking questions or seeking information.
