Etymology: The word "επιθυμία" (epithymia) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "upon" or "over," and "θυμός" (thymos), meaning "desire" or "passion." 

Grammar: In Greek, "επιθυμία" is a feminine noun. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can take different cases depending on its role in a sentence.

Description: "Επιθυμία" refers to a strong desire or longing for something. It can be used to describe both physical and emotional desires. It can also be translated as "craving," "yearning," or "lust." The word is often used in the context of desires or passions that are intense and difficult to control.
