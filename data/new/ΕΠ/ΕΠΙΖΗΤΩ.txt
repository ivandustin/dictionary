Etymology: The word "επιζητώ" (epizito) comes from the Greek verb "ζητώ" (zito), which means "to seek" or "to desire." The prefix "επι-" (epi-) adds the meaning of "on" or "upon," giving the word the sense of seeking or desiring something strongly or persistently.

Grammar: "Επιζητώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ επιζητώ (ego epizito) - I seek
- Εσύ επιζητείς (esi epizitis) - You seek
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιζητεί (aftos/afti/afto epiziti) - He/She/It seeks
- Εμείς επιζητούμε (emeis epizitoume) - We seek
- Εσείς επιζητείτε (esies epizitite) - You seek
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επιζητούν (aftoi/aftes epizitoun) - They seek

Description: "Επιζητώ" is a verb that expresses the act of seeking or desiring something strongly or persistently. It can be used in various contexts, such as seeking knowledge, seeking success, or seeking a particular outcome. The word implies a strong motivation or determination to achieve the desired goal.
