Etymology: The word "επιτασσω" (epitasso) comes from the Greek verb "τασσω" (tasso), which means "to arrange" or "to command." The prefix "επι-" (epi-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "over," giving the word "επιτασσω" the sense of "to command upon" or "to order."

Grammar: "Επιτασσω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ επιτάσσω (ego epitasso) - I command
- Εσύ επιτάσσεις (esi epitasseis) - You command
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιτάσσει (aftos/afti/afto epitassei) - He/She/It commands
- Εμείς επιτάσσουμε (emeis epitassoume) - We command
- Εσείς επιτάσσετε (eseis epitassete) - You (plural) command
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επιτάσσουν (aftoi/aftes epitassoun) - They command

Description: "Επιτασσω" is a Greek verb that means "to command" or "to order." It is used to express the act of giving authoritative instructions or directives to someone. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in military commands, instructions from a superior, or even in everyday situations where someone is giving orders or directions.
