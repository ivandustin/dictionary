Etymology: The word "επιβαρώ" (epivaro) comes from the Greek verb "βαρύνω" (varino), which means "to burden" or "to weigh down." The prefix "επι-" (epi-) adds the meaning of "on" or "upon," indicating that something is being placed or added on top of a burden.

Grammar: "Επιβαρώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ επιβαρώ (ego epivaro) - I burden
- Εσύ επιβαρείς (esi epivaris) - You burden
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιβαρεί (aftos/afti/afto epivari) - He/She/It burdens
- Εμείς επιβαρούμε (emeis epivaroyme) - We burden
- Εσείς επιβαρείτε (eseis epivareite) - You burden
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επιβαρούν (aftoi/aftes epivaroyn) - They burden

Description: "Επιβαρώ" is a verb that describes the action of burdening or weighing down something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the act of adding extra weight to an object, placing a heavy load on someone's shoulders, or figuratively referring to the act of imposing additional responsibilities or difficulties on someone.
