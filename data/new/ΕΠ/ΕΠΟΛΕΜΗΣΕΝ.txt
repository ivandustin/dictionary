Etymology: The word "επολεμησεν" comes from the Greek verb "πολεμέω" (polemeo), which means "to wage war" or "to fight". The prefix "επι-" (epi-) in this word adds the meaning of "upon" or "against". 

Grammar: "επολεμησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "πολεμέω" (polemeo). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description: "επολεμησεν" means "he/she/it fought" in English. It is used to describe an action of fighting or waging war that has already been completed in the past.
