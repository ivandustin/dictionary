Etymology: The word "επιποθει" (epipothei) comes from the Greek verb "επιποθώ" (epipotho), which means "to yearn for" or "to long for". 

Grammar: "Επιποθει" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "επιποθώ" (epipotho) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: επιποθεί (epipothei) - he/she/it yearns for.

Description: "Επιποθει" is a Greek word that expresses the act of yearning or longing for something or someone. It conveys a strong desire or craving for something that is deeply desired or missed.
