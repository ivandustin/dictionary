Etymology: The word "επιστας" (epistas) comes from the Greek verb "επισταμαι" (epistamai), which means "to know" or "to understand". It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb "ιστημι" (histemi), meaning "to stand". Therefore, "επιστας" can be translated as "one who stands upon knowledge" or "one who understands".

Grammar: In Greek, "επιστας" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence or for predicate nouns.

Description: "Επιστας" is a term used in ancient Greek philosophy to refer to a person who possesses knowledge or understanding in a particular field. It is often translated as "expert" or "sage". In philosophical discussions, an "επιστας" is someone who has acquired wisdom and expertise through study and experience. They are considered to have a deep understanding of a specific subject and are highly respected for their knowledge.
