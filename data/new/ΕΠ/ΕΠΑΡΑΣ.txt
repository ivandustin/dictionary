Etymology: The word "επαρας" (eparas) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "επαίρω" (epairo), which means "to lift up" or "to raise."

Grammar: "Επαρας" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, singular number.

Description: In Greek, "επαρας" refers to a person who is arrogant, proud, or haughty. It describes someone who has an inflated sense of self-importance and looks down upon others.
