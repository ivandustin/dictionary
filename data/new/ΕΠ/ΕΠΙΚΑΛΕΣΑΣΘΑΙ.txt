Etymology: The word "επικαλεσασθαι" comes from the Greek verb "επικαλέομαι" (epikaleomai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on", and the verb "καλέω" (kaleo), meaning "to call".

Grammar: "Επικαλεσασθαι" is the infinitive form of the verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense and middle voice. The middle voice indicates that the subject of the verb is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Επικαλεσασθαι" is a Greek verb that expresses the act of calling upon or invoking someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as calling upon a deity, summoning assistance, or appealing to a higher power.
