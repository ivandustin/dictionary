Etymology: The word "επειτα" (epita) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the preposition "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and the adverb "είτα" (eita), meaning "then" or "afterwards."

Grammar: "Επειτα" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate a sequence of events or actions, expressing the idea of "then" or "afterwards." It is commonly used to connect two or more actions or events in a chronological order.

Description: "Επειτα" is a versatile word that is frequently used in both spoken and written Greek. It helps to establish a clear sequence of events or actions, providing a logical flow to the narrative. It is an essential word in Greek grammar and is often used in storytelling, descriptions, and explanations.
