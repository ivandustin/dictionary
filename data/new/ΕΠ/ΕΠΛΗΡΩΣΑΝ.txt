Etymology: The word "επληρώσαν" comes from the Greek verb "πληρώνω" (plirono), which means "to fill" or "to complete." The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, making it "επληρώσαν" (eplirosan), which translates to "they filled" or "they completed."

Grammar: "Επληρώσαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "πληρώνω" in the past tense. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate different tenses, moods, and persons. The "-σαν" ending in "επληρώσαν" is used for the third person plural in the past tense.

Description: "Επληρώσαν" is a verb form that describes an action of filling or completing that was done by a group of people in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as completing a task, filling a container, or fulfilling a requirement.
