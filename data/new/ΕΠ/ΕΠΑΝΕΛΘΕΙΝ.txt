Etymology: The word "επανελθείν" (epanelthein) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "upon" or "again," and "ανελθείν" (anelthein), meaning "to come" or "to go." 

Grammar: "Επανελθείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to show tense, mood, or person. 

Description: "Επανελθείν" means "to return" or "to come back" in English. It is used to describe the action of going back to a previous place, state, or condition.
