Etymology: The word "επιστολήν" (epistolēn) comes from the Greek word "ἐπιστολή" (epistolē), which means "letter" or "missive". It is derived from the verb "ἐπιστέλλω" (epistellō), meaning "to send a letter".

Grammar: "Επιστολήν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Επιστολήν" refers to a letter or a written message that is sent from one person to another. It can be a personal letter, a formal correspondence, or any written communication intended to convey information, thoughts, or emotions.
