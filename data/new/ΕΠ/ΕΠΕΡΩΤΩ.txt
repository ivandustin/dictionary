Etymology: The word "επερωτώ" (eperotó) comes from the Greek verb "επερωτάω" (eperotáo), which means "to ask" or "to inquire". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon", and the verb "ρωτάω" (rotáo), meaning "to ask".

Grammar: "Επερωτώ" is a first-person singular present tense form of the verb "επερωτάω". In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action happening in the present time. The first-person singular form refers to the speaker as the subject of the verb.

Description: "Επερωτώ" is a Greek verb that means "I ask" or "I inquire". It is used to express the act of asking a question or seeking information.
