Etymology: The word "επορευοντο" comes from the Greek verb "πορεύομαι" (poreúomai), which means "to go" or "to travel." The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "on" or "upon," giving the word the sense of "going upon" or "traveling on."

Grammar: "Επορευοντο" is the third person plural form of the verb "πορεύομαι" in the imperfect tense. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the subject and the tense. The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: "Επορευοντο" is a Greek word that means "they were going" or "they were traveling." It describes a group of people or things in the past engaged in the action of going or traveling.
