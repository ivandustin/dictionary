Etymology: The word "επειθεν" (epithen) comes from the Greek verb "επειθω" (epitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince."

Grammar: "Επειθεν" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "επειθω" (epitho), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: "Επειθεν" is used to describe the action of persuading or convincing someone. It implies the act of influencing someone's thoughts, beliefs, or actions through reasoning or argumentation.
