Etymology: The word "εποιησεν" comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to indicate the past tense, resulting in "εποιησεν" (epoiēsen), which means "he/she/it made" or "he/she/it did".

Grammar: "εποιησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe completed actions in the past. In this form, the verb is conjugated to agree with the subject "he/she/it".

Description: "εποιησεν" is a verb in Greek that indicates a completed action in the past. It is commonly used in narratives or historical contexts to describe events that have already happened.
