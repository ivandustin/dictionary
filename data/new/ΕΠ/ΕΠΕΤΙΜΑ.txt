Etymology: The word "επετιμα" (epetima) comes from the Greek verb "επιτιμαω" (epitimao), which means "to rebuke" or "to reprimand". 

Grammar: "Επετιμα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the neuter gender. 

Description: "Επετιμα" refers to a rebuke, reprimand, or a form of disciplinary action. It is used to describe the act of expressing disapproval or criticism towards someone's behavior or actions.
