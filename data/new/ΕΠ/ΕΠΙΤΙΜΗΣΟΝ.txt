Etymology: The word "επιτημησον" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "επιτιμαω" (epitimao), which means "to reprimand" or "to rebuke."

Grammar: "Επιτημησον" is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular form. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to reprimand or rebuke someone or something.

Description: "Επιτημησον" is a strong and direct word that is used to express a strong desire or command to reprimand or rebuke someone or something. It is often used in situations where someone has done something wrong or unacceptable and needs to be corrected or disciplined.
