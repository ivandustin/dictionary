Etymology: The word "επαιτείν" (epaitéin) comes from the Greek verb "αιτέω" (aitéo), which means "to ask" or "to request". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "on" or "upon", indicating that the action of asking is directed towards someone or something.

Grammar: "Επαιτείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ επαιτώ (ego epaitó) - I ask
- Εσύ επαιτείς (esý epaiteís) - You ask
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επαιτεί (aftós/aftí/aftó epaiteí) - He/She/It asks
- Εμείς επαιτούμε (emeís epaitoúme) - We ask
- Εσείς επαιτείτε (eseís epaiteíte) - You ask
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επαιτούν (aftoí/aftés epaitoún) - They ask

Description: "Επαιτείν" refers to the act of asking or requesting something from someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as asking for help, asking for information, or asking for a favor. The verb implies a direct appeal or solicitation to another person or entity.
