Etymology: The word "επιτελείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "επιτελώ" (epiteló), which means "to accomplish" or "to complete". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb "τελώ" (teló), meaning "to bring to an end" or "to fulfill".

Grammar: "Επιτελείσθαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, passive voice, and infinitive form. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon or affected by the action. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description: "Επιτελείσθαι" is a formal and somewhat archaic verb that is used to express the idea of accomplishing or completing something. It implies the fulfillment of a task, duty, or purpose. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the completion of a project, the fulfillment of a goal, or the execution of a plan.
