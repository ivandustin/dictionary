Etymology: The word "επικείται" comes from the Greek verb "επικείμαι" which means "to be imminent" or "to be approaching". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" meaning "on" or "upon" and the verb "κείμαι" meaning "to lie" or "to be situated".

Grammar: "Επικείται" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is used to describe an action or state that is imminent or approaching. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood.

Description: "Επικείται" is often used to express the idea that something is about to happen or is on the verge of happening. It implies a sense of anticipation or expectation. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing an upcoming event, a pending decision, or a looming threat.
