Etymology: The word "επιστωθης" (epistothēs) comes from the Greek verb "επιστω" (epistō), which means "to know" or "to understand." The suffix "-θης" (-thēs) indicates the agent or doer of the verb, so "επιστωθης" can be translated as "one who knows" or "one who understands."

Grammar: In Greek, "επιστωθης" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is declined as a second-declension noun, with the genitive form being "επιστωθου" (epistōthou) and the accusative form being "επιστωθη" (epistōthē).

Description: "Επιστωθης" is a Greek word that refers to someone who possesses knowledge or understanding. It can be used to describe a person who is knowledgeable in a particular field or someone who has a deep understanding of a subject.
