Etymology: The word "επιβλέπω" (epiblepo) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "βλέπω" (blepo), meaning "to see." Therefore, "επιβλέπω" can be translated as "to look upon" or "to oversee."

Grammar: "Επιβλέπω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ επιβλέπω (ego epiblepo) - I look upon
- Εσύ επιβλέπεις (esi epiblepeis) - You look upon
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επιβλέπει (aftos/afti/afto epiblepei) - He/She/It looks upon
- Εμείς επιβλέπουμε (emeis epiblepoume) - We look upon
- Εσείς επιβλέπετε (eseis epiblepete) - You look upon
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επιβλέπουν (aftoi/aftes epiblepoun) - They look upon

Description: "Επιβλέπω" is a versatile verb that can have different meanings depending on the context. It generally refers to the act of overseeing, supervising, or watching over something or someone. It can also imply the act of observing, monitoring, or keeping an eye on a situation.
