Etymology: The word "επανερχεσθαι" (epanerchesthai) comes from the Greek roots "επι-" (epi-), meaning "again" or "upon," and "ανερχομαι" (anerchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go." 

Grammar: "Επανερχεσθαι" is a verb in the present middle/passive participle form. It is derived from the verb "επανερχομαι" (epanerchomai), which means "to return" or "to come back." The middle/passive form indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Επανερχεσθαι" is used to describe the act of returning or coming back to a previous place, state, or condition. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the act of resuming or starting again after a pause or interruption.
