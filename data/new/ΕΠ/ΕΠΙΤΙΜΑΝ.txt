Etymology: The word "επιτιμαν" comes from the Greek word "επιτιμάω" (epitimáo), which means "to rebuke" or "to reprimand". 

Grammar: "Επιτιμαν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: "Επιτιμαν" means "I rebuke" or "I reprimand" in English. It is used to express the act of scolding or reproaching someone for their actions or behavior.
