Etymology: The word "επαιροντας" (epairontas) comes from the Greek verb "επαιρω" (epairo), which means "to lift up" or "to raise". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "above", and the verb "αιρω" (airo), meaning "to lift" or "to take away".

Grammar: "Επαιροντας" (epairontas) is the present participle form of the verb "επαιρω" (epairo) in the active voice. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Επαιροντας" (epairontas) describes someone or something that is currently lifting up or raising. It can refer to a person physically lifting an object, or it can be used metaphorically to describe someone who is uplifting or inspiring others.
