Etymology: The word "επικαταρατος" (epikataratos) comes from the Greek roots "επι" (epi), meaning "upon" or "over", and "καταρατος" (kataratos), meaning "cursed" or "doomed". 

Grammar: "Επικαταρατος" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Επικαταρατος" is used to describe something or someone that is cursed, doomed, or under a curse. It implies a state of being afflicted or condemned.
