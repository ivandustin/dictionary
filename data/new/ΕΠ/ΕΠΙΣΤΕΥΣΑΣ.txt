Etymology: The word "επιστεύσας" comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pistevo), which means "to believe" or "to trust." The prefix "επι-" (epi-) in this word adds the meaning of "upon" or "to" the action of believing.

Grammar: "Επιστεύσας" is the aorist tense form of the verb "πιστεύω" (pistevo) in the second person singular. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this form, it means "you believed" or "you trusted."

Description: "Επιστεύσας" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of having faith or trust in something or someone. It implies a strong conviction or belief in the truth or reliability of a person, idea, or concept.
