Etymology: The word "επαίνω" (epaino) comes from the Greek verb "αίνω" (aino), which means "to praise". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) adds the meaning of "upon" or "to a higher degree", so "επαίνω" can be translated as "to praise highly" or "to commend".

Grammar: "Επαίνω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Εγώ επαινώ (ego epaino) - I praise
- Εσύ επαινείς (esi epaineis) - You praise
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό επαινεί (aftos/afti/afto epainei) - He/She/It praises
- Εμείς επαινούμε (emeis epainoume) - We praise
- Εσείς επαινείτε (eseis epainiete) - You (plural) praise
- Αυτοί/Αυτές επαινούν (aftoi/aftes epainoun) - They praise

Description: "Επαίνω" is a positive action of expressing admiration or approval for someone or something. It is often used to acknowledge and appreciate someone's achievements, qualities, or efforts. It can also be used to compliment or give credit to someone for their accomplishments.
