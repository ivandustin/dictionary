Etymology: The word "επεστη" (epistē) comes from the Greek verb "επισταμαι" (epistamai), which means "to know" or "to understand". It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over", and the verb "ιστημι" (histēmi), meaning "to stand". Therefore, "επεστη" can be translated as "to stand upon" or "to have knowledge upon".

Grammar: "επεστη" is the aorist tense of the Greek verb "επισταμαι" (epistamai), which is a middle voice verb. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, it implies that the subject has knowledge or understanding.

Description: "επεστη" is a Greek verb that is used to express the act of knowing or understanding something. It can be used to describe a state of knowledge or a moment of realization.
