Etymology: The word "επικαλυμμα" (epikalymma) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "upon" or "over", and "κάλυμμα" (kalymma), meaning "cover" or "veil". 

Grammar: "Επικαλυμμα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a neuter noun, and its plural form is "επικαλύμματα" (epikalymmata).

Description: "Επικαλυμμα" refers to a cover or a protective layer that is placed on top of something. It can be used to describe various types of covers, such as a lid, a cap, a case, or a wrapping. The purpose of an "επικαλυμμα" is to provide protection, concealment, or containment for the object it covers.
