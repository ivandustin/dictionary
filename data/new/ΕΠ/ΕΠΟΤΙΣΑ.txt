Etymology: The word "εποτισα" (epotisa) comes from the Greek verb "ποτίζω" (potizo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate". It is derived from the noun "ποτός" (potos), meaning "drink" or "beverage".

Grammar: "Εποτίσα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "ποτίζω" (potizo) in Greek. It is used to indicate that the speaker performed the action of watering or irrigating in the past.

Description: "Εποτίσα" is a verb in the Greek language that specifically refers to the act of watering or irrigating something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the action of watering plants, crops, or gardens.
