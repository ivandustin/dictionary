Etymology: The word "επισκοπος" (episkopos) comes from the Greek roots "epi" meaning "over" or "above" and "skopos" meaning "watcher" or "guardian". It originally referred to a person who had the role of overseeing or supervising.

Grammar: In Greek, "επισκοπος" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is declined in the nominative case as "επισκοπος" and in the genitive case as "επισκοπου".

Description: "Επισκοπος" is the Greek word for "bishop". In the Christian context, a bishop is a high-ranking clergyman who is responsible for the spiritual and administrative oversight of a diocese or a group of churches. Bishops are considered to be successors of the apostles and have the authority to ordain priests and deacons.
