Etymology: The word "επτακισχιλιος" (eptakischilios) comes from the Greek words "επτά" (epta) meaning "seven" and "χίλιοι" (chilioi) meaning "thousand". It is a compound word that literally translates to "seven thousand".

Grammar: In Greek, "επτακισχιλιος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. For example, if used to describe a feminine noun, it would change to "επτακισχιλιες" (eptakischilies).

Description: "Επτακισχιλιος" is a numerical adjective used to indicate the quantity of seven thousand. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as counting large numbers, expressing distances, or referring to historical events.
