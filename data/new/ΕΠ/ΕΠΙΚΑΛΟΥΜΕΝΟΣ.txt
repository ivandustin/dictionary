Etymology: The word "επικαλούμενος" comes from the Greek verb "επικαλούμαι" (epikaloumai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke." It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on," and the verb "καλώ" (kalo), meaning "to call."

Grammar: "Επικαλούμενος" is the masculine singular form of the present participle of the verb "επικαλούμαι" (epikaloumai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to form verbal adjectives. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Επικαλούμενος" can be translated as "calling upon" or "invoking." It is used to describe someone or something that is currently in the act of calling upon or invoking someone or something else.
