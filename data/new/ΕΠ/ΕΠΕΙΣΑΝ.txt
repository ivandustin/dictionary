Etymology: The word "επεισαν" comes from the Greek verb "επείθω" (epeítho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince."

Grammar: "Επεισαν" is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb "επείθω" (epeítho). It is used to express past actions or events that were completed.

Description: "Επεισαν" is a Greek word that means "they persuaded" or "they convinced." It is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of convincing or persuading someone to do something.
