Etymology: The word "εποιησα" (epoiisa) comes from the Greek verb "ποιώ" (poió), which means "to do" or "to make". The prefix "επο-" (epo-) is added to the verb to indicate the past tense, resulting in "εποιησα" (epoiisa), which means "I did" or "I made".

Grammar: "Εποιησα" (epoiisa) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "ποιώ" (poió). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The past tense indicates an action that has already been completed in the past.

Description: "Εποιησα" (epoiisa) is a word in the Greek language that expresses the action of doing or making something in the past. It can be used to describe a specific action or event that occurred in the past.
