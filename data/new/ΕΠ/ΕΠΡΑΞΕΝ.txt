Etymology: The word "επραξεν" comes from the Greek verb "πράττω" (pratto), which means "to do" or "to act". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to indicate the past tense, resulting in "επραξεν" (epraxen), which means "he/she/it did" or "he/she/it acted".

Grammar: "Επραξεν" is the third person singular past tense form of the verb "πράττω" (pratto). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The past tense indicates an action that has already been completed in the past.

Description: "Επραξεν" is a word used in Greek to describe an action that someone or something did in the past. It can be used to talk about a specific event or a series of actions that took place in the past.
