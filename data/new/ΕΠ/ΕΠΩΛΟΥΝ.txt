Etymology: The word "επωλούν" comes from the Greek verb "πωλώ" (polo), which means "to sell". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "πόλις" (polis), meaning "city". In ancient Greece, selling goods was often done in the city marketplace, hence the connection between selling and the word for city.

Grammar: "Επωλούν" is the third person plural form of the verb "πωλώ" (polo) in the present tense. It is used to describe the action of selling performed by a group of people or things.
