Etymology: The word "εποτισεν" comes from the Greek verb "ποτίζω" (potízo), which means "to water" or "to irrigate". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) in this word indicates the completion or intensification of the action.

Grammar: "Εποτίσεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "ποτίζω" (potízo) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence performs the action.

Description: "Εποτίσεν" means "he/she/it watered" or "he/she/it irrigated" in English. It refers to the act of providing water to plants or crops.
