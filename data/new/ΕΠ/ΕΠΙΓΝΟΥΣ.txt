Etymology: The word "επιγνους" (epignous) comes from the Greek roots "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over," and "γνωσις" (gnosis), meaning "knowledge" or "understanding." Therefore, "επιγνους" can be translated as "having knowledge" or "having understanding."

Grammar: In Greek, "επιγνους" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Επιγνους" is used to describe someone who possesses deep knowledge or understanding of a particular subject or situation. It implies a level of expertise and insight beyond basic knowledge.
