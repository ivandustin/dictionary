Etymology: The word "επεισθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "επειθομαι" (epeithomai), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon," and the verb "πειθω" (peitho), meaning "to persuade."

Grammar: "Επεισθησαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense, passive voice form of the verb "επειθομαι" (epeithomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Επεισθησαν" is a Greek verb that means "they were persuaded" or "they were convinced." It implies that a group of people were influenced or convinced by someone or something.
