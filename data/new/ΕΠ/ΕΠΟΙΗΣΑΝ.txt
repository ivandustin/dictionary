Etymology: The word "εποιησαν" (epoiesan) comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poieo), which means "to make" or "to do". It is conjugated in the third person plural, aorist tense, active voice.

Grammar: "Εποιησαν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the third person plural form, indicating that the action was performed by multiple people. The aorist tense suggests that the action happened in the past, without specifying its duration or completion. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: "Εποιησαν" is used to describe an action that was done or made by a group of people in the past. It can be translated as "they made" or "they did" in English, depending on the context.
