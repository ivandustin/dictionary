Etymology: The word "επιστρεφουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistrepho), which means "to turn back" or "to return". The suffix "-ουσιν" (-ousin) indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "Επιστρεφουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate different tenses, moods, and persons. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Επιστρεφουσιν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the Greek verb "επιστρέφω" (epistrepho). It is used to describe an action of turning back or returning that is being performed by a group of people or things.
