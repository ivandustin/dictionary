Etymology: The word "επουρανιων" (epouraniōn) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "ουρανός" (ouranos), meaning "heaven" or "sky." 

Grammar: "επουρανιων" is the genitive plural form of the adjective "επουρανιος" (epouranios), which means "heavenly" or "celestial." In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "επουρανιων" is used to describe something that is related to or belongs to the heavens or the celestial realm. It can refer to heavenly beings, divine qualities, or anything associated with the spiritual or supernatural realm.
