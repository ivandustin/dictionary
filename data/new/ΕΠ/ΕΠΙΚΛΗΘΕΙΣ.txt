Etymology: The word "επικληθείς" (epiklithis) comes from the Greek verb "επικαλούμαι" (epikaloumai), which means "to call upon" or "to invoke." It is derived from the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on," and the verb "καλώ" (kalo), meaning "to call."

Grammar: "Επικληθείς" is the masculine singular form of the past participle of the verb "επικαλούμαι" (epikaloumai). In Greek grammar, the past participle is used to form the perfect tense and passive voice. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Επικληθείς" is an adjective that means "called upon" or "invoked." It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe someone who has been called upon or invoked for help or guidance.
