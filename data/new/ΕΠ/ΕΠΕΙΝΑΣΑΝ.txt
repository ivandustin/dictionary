Etymology: The word "επεινασαν" (epinasan) comes from the Greek verb "πεινάω" (peinao), which means "to be hungry". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to indicate the past tense, so "επεινασαν" translates to "they were hungry".

Grammar: "Επεινασαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "πεινάω" in the past tense. It is used to describe an action that happened in the past and was performed by a group of people.
