Etymology: The word "επενέγκειν" (epenénkein) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epí), meaning "on" or "upon," and "έγκειν" (énkein), meaning "to bring" or "to carry." 

Grammar: "Επενέγκειν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to show tense, mood, or person. 

Description: "Επενέγκειν" means "to bring upon" or "to inflict" in Greek. It is often used to describe the act of causing harm, damage, or suffering to someone or something.
