Etymology: The word "επαταξεν" (epataxen) comes from the Greek verb "πατάσσω" (patasso), which means "to strike" or "to hit". The prefix "επα-" (epa-) intensifies the action, giving the word a sense of strong or forceful striking.

Grammar: "Επαταξεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "πατάσσω" (patasso) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Επαταξεν" is a verb form that describes a specific action of striking or hitting that was completed in the past. It can be used to talk about someone or something striking or hitting another person or object forcefully.
