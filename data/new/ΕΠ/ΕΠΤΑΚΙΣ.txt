Etymology: The word "επτακις" (eptakis) comes from the Greek word "επτά" (epta), meaning "seven". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *septḿ̥, which also gave rise to the Latin word "septem" and the English word "seven".

Grammar: "Επτακις" is a Greek adverb that means "seven times". It is formed by adding the suffix "-κις" (-kis) to the cardinal number "επτά" (epta).

Description: "Επτακις" is used to indicate repetition or multiplication by seven. It is commonly used in mathematical or numerical contexts, such as when expressing the number of times something occurs or the result of a multiplication.
