Etymology: The word "εποπτεία" (epopteia) comes from the Greek word "ἐποπτεία" (epopteia), which means "supervision" or "inspection". It is derived from the verb "ἐποπτεύω" (epopteuo), meaning "to oversee" or "to watch over".

Grammar: "Εποπτεία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "εποπτεία". In the plural form, it becomes "εποπτείες" (epopteies).

Description: "Εποπτεία" refers to the act or process of supervising or inspecting something. It can also be used to describe the authority or role of a supervisor or inspector. In a broader sense, it can refer to the overall concept of supervision or oversight.
