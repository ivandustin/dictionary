Etymology: The word "επρισθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "επρισθημι" (epristhēmi), which means "to put on" or "to place upon." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon," and the verb "ριπτω" (riptō), meaning "to throw" or "to place."

Grammar: "Επρισθησαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense, passive voice form of the verb "επρισθημι" (epristhēmi). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.
