Etymology: The word "επιφερων" comes from the Greek verb "επιφέρω" (epiphéro), which means "to bring upon" or "to impose." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "on," and the verb "φέρω" (fero), meaning "to bring" or "to carry."

Grammar: "Επιφερων" is the present participle form of the verb "επιφέρω" (epiphéro) in the masculine gender. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Επιφερων" describes someone or something that is currently bringing upon or imposing something. It can refer to a person who is actively causing or introducing something, or to a situation where something is being imposed or brought upon.
