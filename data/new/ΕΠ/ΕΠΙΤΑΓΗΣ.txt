Etymology: The word "επιταγής" (epitagis) comes from the Greek word "επί" (epi), meaning "on" or "upon," and "ταγή" (tagi), meaning "order" or "command." 

Grammar: "Επιταγής" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive. The genitive case is often used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Επιταγής" refers to a written order or command, typically used in the context of financial transactions. It can be a written document issued by a bank or other financial institution, instructing the payment of a specific amount of money to a designated recipient.
