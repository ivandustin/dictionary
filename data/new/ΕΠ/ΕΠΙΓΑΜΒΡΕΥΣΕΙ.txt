Etymology: The word "επιγαμβρευσει" comes from the Greek verb "επιγαμβρεύω" (epigambrevo), which means "to marry into" or "to take as a husband." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "on" or "upon," and the verb "γαμβρεύω" (gambrevo), meaning "to marry."

Grammar: "Επιγαμβρεύσει" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person singular form refers to a single subject.

Description: "Επιγαμβρεύσει" describes the action of someone (third person singular) marrying into a family or taking someone as a husband in the future. It implies a future event of marriage or union.
