Etymology: The word "επεινασα" (epinasa) comes from the Greek verb "πεινάω" (peinao), which means "to be hungry". The prefix "επ-" (ep-) is added to indicate the past tense, so "επεινασα" (epinasa) translates to "I was hungry".

Grammar: "Επεινασα" (epinasa) is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "πεινάω" (peinao). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The "-ασα" (-asa) ending in "επεινασα" (epinasa) indicates the past tense and the first person singular.

Description: "Επεινασα" (epinasa) is a word used to express that someone was hungry in the past. It is commonly used in everyday conversations to talk about a previous state of hunger.
