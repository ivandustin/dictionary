Etymology: The word "επιτελείν" (epitelein) comes from the Greek roots "επί" (epi), meaning "upon" or "over," and "τελείν" (telein), meaning "to complete" or "to accomplish." 

Grammar: "Επιτελείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different aspects of completion or accomplishment.

Description: "Επιτελείν" is a versatile verb that can be used in different contexts to express the idea of completing or accomplishing something. It can refer to finishing a task, achieving a goal, or carrying out a duty. The word implies a sense of fulfillment or achievement.
