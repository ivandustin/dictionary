Etymology: The word "επεχειρούν" comes from the Greek verb "επεχειρέω" (epecheiréo), which means "to undertake" or "to attempt." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "επι-" (epi-), meaning "upon" or "over," and the verb "χειρόω" (cheiróo), meaning "to handle" or "to deal with."

Grammar: "Επεχειρούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "επεχειρέω" (epecheiréo). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time. The third person plural form refers to a group of people or things. 

Description: "Επεχειρούν" is a verb in the Greek language that means "they undertake" or "they attempt."
