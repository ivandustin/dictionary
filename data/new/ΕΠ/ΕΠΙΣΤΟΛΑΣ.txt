Etymology: The word "επιστολή" (epistolē) comes from the Greek word "ἐπιστολή" (epistolē), which means "letter" or "missive". It is derived from the verb "ἐπιστέλλω" (epistellō), meaning "to send a message" or "to dispatch".

Grammar: "Επιστολή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "επιστολές" (epistolēs). In Greek grammar, nouns are declined according to gender, number, and case.

Description: "Επιστολή" refers to a letter or a written message that is sent from one person to another. It can be a personal letter, a formal correspondence, or any written communication intended to convey information, thoughts, or emotions. In modern usage, it can also refer to an email or any electronic message sent through digital means.
