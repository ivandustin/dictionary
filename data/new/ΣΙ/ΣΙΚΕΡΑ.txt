Etymology: The word "σικερα" (sikera) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Hebrew word "שֵׁכָר" (shekar), which means "strong drink" or "intoxicating beverage".

Grammar: In Greek, "σικερα" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "σικερας" (sikeras).

Description: "Σικερα" refers to an alcoholic beverage, specifically a strong drink or intoxicating beverage. It is often used to describe fermented drinks made from grains or fruits.
