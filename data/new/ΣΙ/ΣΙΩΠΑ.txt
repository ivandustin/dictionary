Etymology: The word "σιωπή" (siopi) comes from the Greek verb "σιωπάω" (siopao), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet."

Grammar: "Σιωπή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and belongs to the first declension. In the plural form, it becomes "σιωπές" (siopes).

Description: "Σιωπή" refers to the state or act of being silent or quiet. It can also imply the absence of speech or noise. It is often used to describe a calm or peaceful atmosphere.
