Etymology: The word "σιγησαί" (sigēsai) comes from the Greek verb "σιγάω" (sigáō), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet."

Grammar: "Σιγησαί" is the imperative form of the verb "σιγάω" in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to be silent or keep quiet.

Description: "Σιγησαί" is a word in the Greek language that is used to tell someone to be silent or keep quiet. It is a command form of the verb "σιγάω" and is used in various contexts where silence or quietness is desired or necessary.
