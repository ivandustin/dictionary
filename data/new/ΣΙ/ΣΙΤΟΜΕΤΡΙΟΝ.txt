Etymology: The word "σιτομετρία" comes from the Greek roots "σίτος" (sitos), meaning "grain" or "wheat," and "μέτρον" (metron), meaning "measure." 

Grammar: "Σιτομετρία" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun, and its plural form is "σιτομετρίες" (sitometries).

Description: "Σιτομετρία" refers to the science or practice of measuring and analyzing grains, particularly in the context of agriculture and food production. It involves the measurement of various parameters such as grain size, weight, moisture content, and quality. This field is important for ensuring accurate and standardized measurements in the grain industry.
