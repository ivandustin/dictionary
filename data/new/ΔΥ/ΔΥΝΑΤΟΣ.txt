Etymology: The word "δυνατός" (dunatos) comes from the Greek root "δύναμις" (dynamis), meaning "power" or "ability". It is related to the English word "dynamic".

Grammar: "Δυνατός" is an adjective in Greek. It is used to describe something or someone as "strong", "powerful", or "capable".

Description: "Δυνατός" is a versatile word that can be used to describe various aspects of strength or power. It can refer to physical strength, such as a strong person or a powerful animal. It can also describe mental or intellectual strength, such as a person with strong problem-solving abilities or a powerful argument. Additionally, it can be used to describe something as "possible" or "feasible", indicating that it has the potential to be achieved or accomplished.
