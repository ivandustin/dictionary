Etymology: The word "δυναμενος" (dunamenos) comes from the Greek verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power." It is derived from the noun "δύναμις" (dunamis), meaning "power" or "ability."

Grammar: "Δυναμενος" is the masculine form of the present participle of the verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to express ongoing or continuous action. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Δυναμενος" is an adjective that means "able" or "capable" in Greek. It describes someone or something that has the power or ability to do something. It can also imply potential or possibility.
