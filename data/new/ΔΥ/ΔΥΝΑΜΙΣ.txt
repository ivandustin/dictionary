Etymology: The word "δυναμις" (dynamis) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root "δυναμ-" (dynam-), which means "power" or "ability."

Grammar: In Greek, "δυναμις" (dynamis) is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "δυναμεως" (dynameōs).

Description: "Δυναμις" (dynamis) refers to power, strength, or ability. It can also denote potential or capacity. In philosophy, it is often used to describe the inherent power or potentiality of something. The term is also used in various scientific and technical fields, such as physics, where it refers to the capacity to do work or produce an effect.
