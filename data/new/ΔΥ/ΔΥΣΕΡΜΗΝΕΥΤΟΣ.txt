Etymology: The word "δυσερμηνευτος" comes from the Greek roots "δυς" (dys), meaning "difficult" or "bad," and "ερμηνευτος" (hermeneutos), meaning "interpretable" or "explainable." 

Grammar: "Δυσερμηνευτος" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe something that is difficult to interpret or explain.
