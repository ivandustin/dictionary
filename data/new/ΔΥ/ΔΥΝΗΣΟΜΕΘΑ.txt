The suffix "-σομεθα" (-sometha) indicates the first person plural middle voice form of the verb, indicating that the action is being performed by the subject upon themselves.

Grammar: "δυνησομεθα" is a verb in the present tense, first person plural middle voice form. In Greek grammar, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is performing the action upon themselves or is affected by the action in some way.

Description: "δυνησομεθα" expresses the idea of "we are able" or "we have power" in Greek. It indicates that the subject (we) has the ability or capacity to do something.
