Etymology: The word "δυσμων" (dysmon) comes from the Greek roots "δυσ-" (dys-), meaning "bad" or "difficult," and "μων" (mon), meaning "sound" or "voice." 

Grammar: "Δυσμων" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. 

Description: "Δυσμων" refers to a person with a bad or unpleasant voice. It can also be used to describe a sound or voice that is harsh or discordant.
