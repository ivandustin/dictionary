Etymology: The word "δυσφημίας" comes from the Greek roots "δυσ-" meaning "bad" or "difficult" and "φήμη" meaning "reputation" or "fame". 

Grammar: "δυσφημίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "δυσφημίας" refers to the act or state of speaking ill or disrespectfully about someone or something. It can also be translated as "defamation" or "slander".
