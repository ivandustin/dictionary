Etymology: The word "δυναμεως" (dunameos) comes from the Greek root "δυναμις" (dunamis), meaning "power" or "ability."

Grammar: "Δυναμεως" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Δυναμεως" refers to the power or ability of someone or something. It can also be translated as "strength" or "force."
