Etymology: The word "δυναμενων" comes from the Greek verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". It is derived from the noun "δύναμις" (dynamis), meaning "power" or "ability".

Grammar: "δυναμενων" is the present participle form of the verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai) in Greek. It is in the masculine, plural, genitive case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action.

Description: "δυναμενων" can be translated as "of those who are able" or "of those who have power". It is used to describe a group of people who possess the ability or power to do something.
