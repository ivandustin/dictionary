Etymology: The word "δυσβαστακτα" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of "δυσ" (dys), meaning "difficult" or "bad," and "βαστάζω" (vastazo), meaning "to bear" or "to endure."

Grammar: "Δυσβαστακτα" is a plural noun in the neuter gender. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Δυσβαστακτα" is used to describe things or situations that are difficult to bear, endure, or tolerate. It implies a sense of discomfort, annoyance, or frustration.
