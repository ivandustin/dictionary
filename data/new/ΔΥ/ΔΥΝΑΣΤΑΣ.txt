Grammar: "Δυναστας" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (δυναστου), dative (δυναστη), accusative (δυναστη), and vocative (δυναστα).

Description: "Δυναστας" refers to a ruler or a person who holds power and authority. It can be used to describe a monarch, a dictator, or any individual who exercises control over others. The term is often associated with political leaders or influential figures in history.
