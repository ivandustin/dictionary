Etymology: The word "δυναμενον" comes from the Greek verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power." It is derived from the noun "δύναμις" (dynamis), meaning "power" or "ability."

Grammar: "Δυναμενον" is the neuter singular form of the present participle of the verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to express ongoing or continuous action. The neuter form is used when referring to things or concepts that are neither masculine nor feminine.

Description: "Δυναμενον" can be translated as "being able" or "having power." It describes something or someone that is currently capable of doing or achieving something. It implies an ongoing state of ability or power.
