Etymology: The word "δυναμενους" comes from the Greek verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power." It is derived from the noun "δύναμις" (dynamis), meaning "power" or "ability."

Grammar: "Δυναμενους" is the accusative plural form of the Greek adjective "δυναμενος" (dynamenos), which means "able" or "capable." In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the nouns they modify. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Δυναμενους" is used to describe multiple individuals who possess the ability or capability to do something. It indicates that they have the power or capacity to perform a certain action or possess a certain quality.
