Etymology: The word "δυσκολως" comes from the Greek roots "δυσ-" meaning "difficult" and "κολος" meaning "labor, toil." 

Grammar: "Δυσκολως" is an adverb in Greek. It is the adverbial form of the adjective "δύσκολος" (difficult) and is used to describe how an action is done.

Description: "Δυσκολως" is used to indicate that something is done with difficulty or in a challenging manner. It expresses the idea of struggling or finding something hard to accomplish.
