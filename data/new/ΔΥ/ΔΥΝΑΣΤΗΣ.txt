Etymology: The word "δυναστης" (dynastes) comes from the Greek word "δυναστεία" (dynasteia), which means "power" or "authority". It is derived from the verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), meaning "to be able" or "to have power".

Grammar: "Δυναστης" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "δυναστης". In the plural form, it becomes "δυναστεις".

Description: "Δυναστης" refers to a ruler or a person who holds great power and authority. It is often used to describe a monarch, a dictator, or someone who exercises absolute control over a country or a region.
