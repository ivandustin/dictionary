Etymology: The word "δυναμενου" (dynamenou) comes from the Greek verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai), which means "to be able" or "to have power". It is derived from the noun "δύναμις" (dynamis), meaning "power" or "ability".

Grammar: "Δυναμενου" is the present participle form of the verb "δύναμαι" (dynamai) in the masculine singular genitive case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate an ongoing action or state. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship.

Description: "Δυναμενου" can be translated as "of being able" or "of having power". It describes someone or something that possesses the ability or power to do something.
