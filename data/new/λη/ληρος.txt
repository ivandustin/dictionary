ληρός (leros) is a Greek adjective that means "senseless", "foolish", or "mad". It comes from the Greek noun λήρης (leres), which means "a madman" or "a fool". In grammar, ληρός is a masculine singular nominative form of the adjective. It can be used to describe a person or an action that is irrational or nonsensical. For example, "Ο Τζον είναι ληρός" (O John einai leros) means "John is senseless" or "John is a fool".
