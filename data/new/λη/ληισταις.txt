The word is derived from the verb λείω (leió), which means "to smooth" or "to polish". In ancient Greece, a leistēs was a person who distributed or dispensed goods, such as food or wine, to guests at a banquet or symposium. In grammar, ληισταις is a noun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.
