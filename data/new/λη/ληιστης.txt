Word: ληιστης

Etymology: The word ληιστης (leistes) comes from the Greek verb ληιζω (leizo), which means "to plunder" or "to rob". The suffix -της (-tes) is a common Greek suffix used to form agent nouns, indicating a person who performs a certain action. Therefore, ληιστης can be translated as "plunderer" or "robber".

Grammar: ληιστης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, and accusative, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description: ληιστης is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a pirate or a robber. It was often used to describe the actions of bandits who would attack and plunder ships or coastal towns. The word has also been used metaphorically to describe someone who takes advantage of others or exploits a situation for personal gain.
