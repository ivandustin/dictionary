Word: ληνός

Etymology: The word ληνός comes from the Ancient Greek λήνη (lḗnē), which means "a trough or vat for washing or kneading." It is derived from the verb λούω (loúō), which means "to wash."

Grammar: In Ancient Greek, ληνός is a masculine noun of the third declension. It has a nominative singular form of ληνός and a genitive singular form of ληνοῦ. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description: ληνός refers to a trough or vat used for washing or kneading. In Ancient Greece, it was commonly used for washing clothes or kneading dough. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to a difficult or laborious task.
