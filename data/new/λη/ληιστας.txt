ληιστας (leistas) is a Greek word that means "negligent" or "careless". 

Etymology: The word ληιστας comes from the Greek verb λείπω (leipo), which means "to leave" or "to abandon". The suffix -ιστας (-istas) is a noun-forming suffix that denotes a person who performs the action of the verb. Therefore, ληιστας literally means "one who leaves" or "one who abandons".

Grammar: ληιστας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is ληισταί (leistai).

Description: The word ληιστας is often used to describe someone who is careless or negligent in their duties or responsibilities. It can also be used to describe someone who is absent-minded or forgetful. In ancient Greek literature, the word was often used to describe soldiers who deserted their posts or failed to carry out their duties in battle.
