Word: ληθην

Etymology: ληθην (lethen) is a Greek word that means "forgetfulness" or "oblivion". It comes from the Greek goddess of forgetfulness, Lethe.

Grammar: ληθην is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: ληθην is a concept that has been present in Greek mythology since ancient times. It refers to the state of forgetfulness or oblivion, where memories and knowledge are lost. In Greek mythology, the river Lethe was believed to flow through the underworld, and those who drank from it would forget their past lives. The concept of ληθην has been used in literature and philosophy to explore the nature of memory, identity, and the human condition.
