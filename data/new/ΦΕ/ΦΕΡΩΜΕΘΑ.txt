Etymology: The word "φερωμεθα" (pronounced "pherometha") comes from the Greek verb "φέρω" (pronounced "fero"), which means "to carry" or "to bear." The suffix "-μεθα" ("-metha") indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning "we carry ourselves" or "we bear ourselves."

Grammar: "Φερωμεθα" is the present tense, middle voice, indicative mood form of the verb "φέρω" in Greek. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, it means "we carry ourselves" or "we bear ourselves."

Description: "Φερωμεθα" is a Greek verb that expresses the action of carrying or bearing oneself. It can be used to describe the act of physically carrying oneself or metaphorically carrying oneself in a certain manner or behavior.
