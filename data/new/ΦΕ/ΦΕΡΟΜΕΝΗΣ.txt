Etymology: The word "φερομένης" (pronounced: feroménis) comes from the Greek verb "φέρω" (pronounced: féro), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The suffix "-μένης" (-ménis) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating an ongoing action or state.

Grammar: "Φερομένης" is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb "φέρω". In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Φερομένης" can be translated as "being carried" or "being borne". It describes something or someone that is currently in the state of being carried or borne. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person carrying a heavy load or a vehicle transporting goods.
