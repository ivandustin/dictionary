Etymology: The word "φερεσθαι" (pronounced "phereisthai") comes from the Greek verb "φέρω" (pronounced "phero"), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The suffix "-σθαι" (-sthai) is a middle/passive voice ending in Greek.

Grammar: "Φερεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "φέρω". In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the voice (active, middle, or passive) and the tense (present, past, future, etc.). The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is either performing the action on itself (middle voice) or receiving the action (passive voice).

Description: "Φερεσθαι" is used to express the action of carrying or bearing in the present tense, middle/passive voice. It can be translated as "to be carried" or "to be borne". The exact meaning and usage of the word may vary depending on the context in which it is used.
