Etymology: The word "φερων" (pronounced "pheron") comes from the Greek verb "φέρω" (pronounced "fero"), which means "to carry" or "to bear". 

Grammar: "Φερων" is the present participle form of the verb "φέρω" in the masculine gender. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently carrying or bearing something.

Description: "Φερων" can be translated as "carrying" or "bearing" in English. It is often used in Greek mythology to describe various characters who carry or bear important objects or symbols.
