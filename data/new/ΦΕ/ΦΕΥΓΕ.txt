Etymology: The word "φευγε" (pronounced "fev-ge") comes from the Greek verb "φεύγω" (pronounced "fev-go"), which means "to flee" or "to escape". 

Grammar: "Φευγε" is the imperative form of the verb "φεύγω" in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to flee or escape.

Description: "Φευγε" is a word in the Greek language that is used to command someone to flee or escape. It is often used in situations where there is danger or a need to quickly get away from a certain place or situation.
