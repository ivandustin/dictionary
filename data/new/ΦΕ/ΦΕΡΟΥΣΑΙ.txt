Etymology: The word "φερουσαι" (pronounced "pherousai") is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "φέρω" (pronounced "fero"), which means "to carry" or "to bear" in English.

Grammar: "φερουσαι" is a verb form that indicates an action being performed by a third person singular subject. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate different grammatical aspects such as tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: The verb "φερουσαι" is used to describe the action of carrying or bearing something by a third person singular subject. It can be used in various contexts and conjugated into different tenses, moods, and voices to convey different meanings and nuances.
