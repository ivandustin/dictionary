Etymology: The word "φερουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "φέρω" (fero), which means "to carry" or "to bring". It is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb.

Grammar: "φερουσιν" is a verb form in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: "φερουσιν" is used to express the action of carrying or bringing something in the present time. It can be translated as "they carry" or "they bring" in English.
