Etymology: The word "φεισομαι" (pronounced "feisomai") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "φείδομαι" (feidomai), which means "to spare" or "to have mercy."

Grammar: "Φεισομαι" is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: The word "φεισομαι" is used to express the act of sparing or showing mercy towards someone or something. It implies a sense of compassion, kindness, or forgiveness.
