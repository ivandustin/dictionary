Word: ακουσαντες

Etymology:
The word ακουσαντες is derived from the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates past tense and active voice. Therefore, ακουσαντες means "having heard" or "having listened".

Grammar:
ακουσαντες is a past participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who have heard or listened to something in the past. In Greek, past participles are often used as adjectives to modify nouns or pronouns.

Description:
ακουσαντες is a word that describes a group of people who have heard or listened to something in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a historical account, a news report, or a personal narrative. For example, "Οι ακουσαντες των νέων ειδήσεων έμειναν έκπληκτοι" (The listeners of the news were surprised).
