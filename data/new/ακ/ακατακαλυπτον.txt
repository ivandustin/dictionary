Word: ακατακαλυπτον

Etymology: The word ακατακαλυπτον comes from the Greek roots α- (a-), meaning "not," κατα- (kata-), meaning "down," and καλυπτω (kalypto), meaning "to cover." Therefore, ακατακαλυπτον means "uncovered" or "unhidden."

Grammar: ακατακαλυπτον is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is not covered or hidden.

Description: ακατακαλυπτον is a word that is often used in a spiritual or religious context to describe something that is not hidden or concealed from God. It can also be used more generally to describe something that is not hidden or covered up.
