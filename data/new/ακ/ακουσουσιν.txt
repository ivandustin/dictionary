Word: ακουσουσιν

Etymology:
The word ακουσουσιν (akousousin) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen." The suffix -σουσιν (-sousin) is the third person plural future indicative active ending, which means "they will hear" or "they will listen."

Grammar:
ακουσουσιν (akousousin) is a third person plural future indicative active verb in Greek. It is used to indicate that "they will hear" or "they will listen" in the future tense. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -σουσιν (-sousin) to the root word ακούω (akouo).

Description:
ακουσουσιν (akousousin) is a Greek verb that is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is often used to describe the act of listening to God's word or to the teachings of Jesus. The word emphasizes the importance of actively listening and paying attention to what is being said. In modern Greek, the word is still used to mean "to hear" or "to listen."
