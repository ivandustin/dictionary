The word "ακων" (akon) is a Greek adjective that means "unwilling" or "unwillingly". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek prefix "a-" which means "not" or "without", and the Greek noun "κωνος" (konos) which means "will" or "volition". 

Grammar: "ακων" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is doing something against their will or without enthusiasm. It can also be used to describe a situation or action that is done reluctantly or without desire.
