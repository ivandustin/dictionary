Word: ακουσομεθα

Etymology: ακουσομεθα is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix -μεθα (-meta) indicates that the verb is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Grammar: ακουσομεθα is a present tense, middle voice, first person plural form of the verb ακούω (akouo). It can be translated to "we are being heard" or "we are listening".

Description: ακουσομεθα is a verb that describes the act of listening or being heard. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a conversation, a lecture, or a musical performance. The middle voice indicates that the subject is actively participating in the act of listening or being heard.
