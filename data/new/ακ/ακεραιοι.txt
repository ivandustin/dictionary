Word: ακεραιοι

Etymology: The word ακεραιοι comes from the Greek word ακέραιος (akeraios), which means "unmixed" or "unadulterated". It is derived from the prefix α- (a-), meaning "not", and the word κεραία (keraia), meaning "mixture".

Grammar: ακεραιοι is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: ακεραιοι refers to something that is pure or unadulterated. It can be used to describe a substance that is not mixed with anything else, or a concept or idea that is not influenced by outside factors. In Greek philosophy, the term was used to describe the pure, unchanging essence of things. Today, it is often used in a more general sense to describe something that is genuine or authentic.
