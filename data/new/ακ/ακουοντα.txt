The word "ακουοντα" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The present participle form indicates that the action of hearing or listening is currently happening or ongoing. 

In terms of grammar, "ακουοντα" is in the masculine singular nominative case. It can be used as a subject or as a predicate nominative in a sentence. 

Example sentence: Ο ακούοντας κατάλαβε τι συνέβη. (O akouontas katalave ti synevi) - The one who is listening understood what happened.
