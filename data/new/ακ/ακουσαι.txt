Word: ακουσαι

Etymology:
The word ακουσαι (akousai) is a verb in the Greek language, which is derived from the root word ακούω (akouo), meaning "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix -σαι (-sai) indicates the second person singular aorist tense, which means "you heard" or "you listened".

Grammar:
ακουσαι (akousai) is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated from the root verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
ακουσαι (akousai) means "you heard" or "you listened" in Greek. It is a verb that describes an action that was completed in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a conversation, a story, or a historical account. It is a common word in the Greek language and is used in everyday communication.
