Word: ακανθαι

Etymology:
The word ακανθαι (akanthai) is derived from the Greek word ακανθα (akantha), which means "thorn" or "spine". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂éḱā (sharp).

Grammar:
ακανθαι is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the first declension and is feminine in gender. The singular form of the word is ακανθα (akantha).

Description:
ακανθαι refers to thorns or spines, typically those found on plants. In Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was often depicted wearing a helmet adorned with a pair of sharp, curved ακανθαι. The word is also used in modern Greek to describe the spines on a hedgehog or porcupine.
