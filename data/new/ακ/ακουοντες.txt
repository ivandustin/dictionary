Word: ακουοντες

Etymology: ακουοντες is a present participle form of the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ḱous-.

Grammar: ακουοντες is a masculine plural nominative form of the present participle of the verb ακούω. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently hearing or listening to something.

Description: ακουοντες can be translated to "those who are hearing" or "those who are listening". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the disciples of Jesus who were listening to his teachings. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe anyone who is currently listening to something.
