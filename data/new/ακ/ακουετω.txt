Word: ακουετω

Etymology:
The word ακουετω (akoueto) is derived from the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix -ετω (-eto) is added to the verb stem to form the third person singular present tense.

Grammar:
ακουετω (akoueto) is a third person singular present tense verb in Greek. It is used to describe an action that is currently happening or is habitual. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Present tense:
εγώ ακούω (ego akouo) - I hear
εσύ ακούς (esy akous) - you hear
αυτός/αυτή/αυτό ακούει (autos/afti/afto akouei) - he/she/it hears
εμείς ακούμε (emeis akoume) - we hear
εσείς ακούτε (eseis akoute) - you hear
αυτοί/αυτές/αυτά ακούνε (autoi/aftes/afta akoune) - they hear

Description:
ακουετω (akoueto) is a simple and common verb in Greek that is used to describe the act of hearing or listening. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as listening to music, hearing someone speak, or paying attention to instructions. The verb is often used in combination with other verbs to describe more complex actions, such as "I hear you talking" or "I am listening to the radio".
