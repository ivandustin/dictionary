Word: ακαιρως

Etymology:
The word ακαιρως (akairos) is derived from the Greek words "a-" meaning "not" and "kairos" meaning "time". Therefore, the literal meaning of ακαιρως is "not at the right time" or "untimely".

Grammar:
ακαιρως is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or event that occurs at an inappropriate or inconvenient time.

Description:
ακαιρως is a word that is often used to describe situations where something happens at the wrong time. It can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as a missed opportunity, an untimely death, or an inconvenient interruption. The word is often used in a negative context, as it implies that something has gone wrong or that a mistake has been made. However, it can also be used in a more neutral context, such as when describing a situation that is simply not ideal or convenient.
