The word "ακοης" (akoes) is a Greek noun that means "hearing" or "sense of hearing". It is derived from the Greek verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear". In terms of grammar, "ακοης" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It can be used in various contexts, such as in medical terminology or in literature. For example, in Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was said to have had exceptional hearing, which was referred to as her "ακοης".
