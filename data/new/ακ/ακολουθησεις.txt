Word: ακολουθήσεις

Etymology:
The word ακολουθήσεις (akolouthísis) is derived from the Greek verb ακολουθώ (akolouthó), which means "to follow". The suffix -σεις (-seis) is added to the verb stem to form the noun ακολουθήσεις, which means "followings" or "followers".

Grammar:
ακολουθήσεις is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple followings or followers.

Description:
ακολουθήσεις can refer to a group of people who follow a particular person, idea, or movement. It can also refer to the act of following someone or something. In modern Greek, the word is often used in the context of social media, where it refers to the number of followers a person or page has.
