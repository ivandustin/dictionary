Word: ακουσονται

Etymology: ακουσονται is a conjugated form of the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The verb comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ḱous-.

Grammar: ακουσονται is the third person plural present passive indicative form of ακούω. This means that it is used to describe an action that is being done to the subject of the sentence by someone or something else. In this case, it means "they are being heard" or "they are being listened to".

Description: ακουσονται is a verb that is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of being heard or listened to. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in conversations, speeches, or music performances. The passive voice of the verb emphasizes that the subject is not the one doing the action, but rather is the recipient of it.
