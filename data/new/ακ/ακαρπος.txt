Word: ακαρπος

Etymology:
The word ακαρπος comes from the Greek roots α- (a-) meaning "not" and καρπος (karpos) meaning "fruit". Therefore, ακαρπος literally means "not fruitful" or "unproductive".

Grammar:
ακαρπος is an adjective in Greek, and it can be declined according to gender, number, and case. For example, in the masculine singular nominative case, it is ακαρπος (akarpos), while in the feminine singular nominative case, it is ακαρπη (akarpē).

Description:
ακαρπος is used to describe something that is unproductive, barren, or fruitless. It can refer to a tree that does not bear fruit, a field that does not yield crops, or a person who does not produce results or achievements. In a figurative sense, it can also describe a situation or endeavor that does not lead to any positive outcome or benefit.
