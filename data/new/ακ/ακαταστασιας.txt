Word: ακαταστασίας

Etymology:
The word ακαταστασίας (akatastasías) is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and κατάσταση (katástasi) meaning "state" or "condition". Therefore, the literal meaning of ακαταστασίας is "not in a state of order or stability".

Grammar:
ακαταστασίας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
ακαταστασίας is a word that describes a state of disorder, chaos, or instability. It can refer to a political or social situation that is characterized by unrest, rebellion, or revolution. It can also be used to describe a personal state of disarray or confusion. The word is often used in a negative context, implying a lack of control or organization.
