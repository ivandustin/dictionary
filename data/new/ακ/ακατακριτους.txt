Word: ακατακρίτους

Etymology:
The word ακατακρίτους is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and κατακρίνω (katakrino) meaning "to condemn". Therefore, the word ακατακρίτους means "not condemned" or "uncondemned".

Grammar:
ακατακρίτους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who have not been condemned or judged.

Description:
The word ακατακρίτους is often used in a religious context to describe those who are considered righteous or blameless in the eyes of God. It can also be used more generally to describe individuals or groups who have not been found guilty of any wrongdoing.
