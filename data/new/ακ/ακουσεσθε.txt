Word: ακουσεσθε

Etymology:
The word ακουσεσθε (akousesethe) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen." The suffix -σεσθε (-sethe) is a second-person plural ending that indicates the verb is in the imperative mood, meaning it is a command or request.

Grammar:
ακουσεσθε is a second-person plural verb in the imperative mood. It is used to give a command or request to a group of people to listen or hear something.

Description:
ακουσεσθε is often used in the Bible, particularly in the New Testament, to introduce a message or teaching that the speaker wants the audience to pay attention to. It is often translated as "listen" or "hear" in English. In context, it can be seen as a call to action, urging the audience to not only hear the words being spoken but to also take them to heart and act on them.
