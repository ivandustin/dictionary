Word: ακουόντας

Etymology: ακούω (akouo) means "to hear" in Greek, and the suffix -οντας (-ontas) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently hearing or listening.

Grammar: ακουόντας is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen." As a present participle, it can function as an adjective or a noun, describing someone who is currently hearing or listening.

Description: ακουόντας describes someone who is currently hearing or listening. It can be used to refer to a person who is actively listening to a conversation, music, or any other sound. In Greek, it can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is paying attention or being attentive to something.
