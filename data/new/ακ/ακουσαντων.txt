Word: ακουσαντων

Etymology: ακουσαντων is the genitive plural form of the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". 

Grammar: ακουσαντων is a third-person plural genitive active participle, which means it describes a group of people who have heard or listened to something. 

Description: ακουσαντων can be translated as "of those who have heard" or "of those who have listened". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the disciples or followers of Jesus who have heard his teachings.
