Word: ακουσασιν

Etymology: ακουσασιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is in the aorist tense and the third person plural form.

Grammar: ακουσασιν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description: ακουσασιν can be translated to "they heard" or "they listened" in English. It is often used in the context of storytelling or recounting events that have already taken place.
