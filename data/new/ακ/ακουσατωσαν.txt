Word: ακουσατωσαν

Etymology: The word ακουσατωσαν is a compound word in Greek, formed by combining the verb ακούω (akouo) which means "to hear" and the verb σατω (sato) which means "to cause to happen". The suffix -σαν (-san) indicates the third person plural aorist active indicative tense.

Grammar: ακουσατωσαν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the third person plural aorist active indicative tense. This tense indicates that the action of hearing and causing to happen has already been completed in the past.

Description: ακουσατωσαν can be translated to English as "they heard and caused to happen". The context in which this word is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action being described.
