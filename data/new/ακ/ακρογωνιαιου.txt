The word "ακρογωνιαιου" (akrogoniaiou) is a Greek adjective that means "of the cornerstones" or "pertaining to the cornerstones". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "ακρο-" (akro-), meaning "top" or "extreme", and "γωνία" (gonia), meaning "corner". 

In terms of grammar, "ακρογωνιαιου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is often used to describe the cornerstones of a building or structure, which are the stones that form the foundation and support the weight of the entire structure. 

Overall, "ακρογωνιαιου" is a specific and technical term that is mainly used in the context of architecture and construction.
