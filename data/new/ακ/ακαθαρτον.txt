Word: ακαθαρτον

Etymology: ακαθαρτον is a Greek word that comes from the root words α- (a-) meaning "not" and καθαρός (katharos) meaning "clean" or "pure". Therefore, ακαθαρτον means "unclean" or "impure".

Grammar: ακαθαρτον is a neuter noun in Greek. It is in the nominative singular case. In other cases, it would change its ending accordingly.

Description: ακαθαρτον is a word that is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to something that is impure or unclean. It can also be used more generally to describe something that is dirty or contaminated. In some contexts, it can also be used metaphorically to refer to something that is morally or spiritually corrupt.
