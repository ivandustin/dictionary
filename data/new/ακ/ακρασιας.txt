The word "ακρασίας" (akrasías) is a Greek noun that refers to the state or quality of being without self-control or temperance. It is derived from the Greek words "α-" (a-), meaning "without," and "κράτος" (krátos), meaning "power" or "control." 

In terms of grammar, "ακρασίας" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It can be used in various contexts, such as in philosophy, ethics, and psychology, to describe a lack of self-discipline or restraint.

Overall, "ακρασίας" is a word that describes a negative quality or behavior, and is often contrasted with the concept of "σωφροσύνη" (sophrosýni), which means self-control or moderation.
