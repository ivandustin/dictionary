Word: ακολουθησαι

Etymology:
The word ακολουθησαι (akolouthesai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ακολουθία (akolouthia), which means "following" or "accompaniment". The verb form ακολουθησαι means "to follow" or "to accompany".

Grammar:
ακολουθησαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to follow or accompany someone or something.

Description:
ακολουθησαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when instructing someone to follow a person or a path. It can also be used in a figurative sense, such as when instructing someone to follow a set of rules or principles. In the New Testament, the word is often used in the context of following Jesus or his teachings.
