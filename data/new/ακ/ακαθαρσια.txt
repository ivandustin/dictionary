Word: ακαθαρσία (akatharsía)

Etymology:
The word ακαθαρσία (akatharsía) comes from the Greek root words α- (a-) meaning "not" and καθαρός (katharós) meaning "clean" or "pure". Therefore, ακαθαρσία (akatharsía) means "impurity" or "uncleanness".

Grammar:
ακαθαρσία (akatharsía) is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and can be used as the subject or object of a sentence.

Description:
ακαθαρσία (akatharsía) is a term used in Greek to describe something that is impure or unclean. It can refer to physical impurities, such as dirt or filth, or to moral impurities, such as sin or corruption. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to describe spiritual impurities or sins that separate people from God.
