The word "ακρατεις" (akrateis) is a Greek adjective that means "unrestrained" or "uncontrolled". It is derived from the Greek word "ακρατης" (akrates), which means "lacking self-control" or "intemperate". In terms of grammar, "ακρατεις" is a masculine plural nominative form of the adjective. It can be used to describe people who are unable to control their desires or impulses, or who lack discipline or self-restraint.
