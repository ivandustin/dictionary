Word: ακαθαρσίας

Etymology:
The word ακαθαρσίας (akatharsías) is derived from the Greek word ακάθαρτος (akáthartos), which means "unclean" or "impure". The suffix -ίας (-ías) is added to form a noun, indicating the state or quality of being unclean or impure.

Grammar:
ακαθαρσίας is a feminine noun in the genitive case, which means it is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, it is used to indicate the possession of the quality of being unclean or impure.

Description:
ακαθαρσίας is a term used in Greek to describe a state of impurity or uncleanness. It can refer to physical impurity, such as dirt or filth, or moral impurity, such as sin or corruption. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe spiritual impurity or defilement.
