Word: ακαθαρσιαν

Etymology:
The word ακαθαρσιαν (akatharsian) is derived from the Greek word ακάθαρτος (akathartos), which means "unclean" or "impure". The suffix -ιαν (-ian) is added to form a noun, indicating the state or quality of being unclean.

Grammar:
ακαθαρσιαν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

Description:
ακαθαρσιαν refers to the state or quality of being unclean or impure. It can be used to describe physical impurities, such as dirt or filth, as well as moral impurities, such as sin or corruption. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to describe spiritual impurities or defilement.
