Word: ακουοντος

Etymology:
The word ακουοντος is derived from the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix -οντος (-ontos) is the present participle form of the verb, indicating that the action of hearing or listening is currently taking place.

Grammar:
ακουοντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. In this case, ακουοντος is used to describe a noun that possesses the quality of hearing or listening.

Description:
ακουοντος can be translated to mean "of the one who is hearing" or "of the one who is listening". It is often used in Greek literature and language to describe a person who is actively engaged in listening or paying attention to something. The word can also be used to describe a situation or environment where there is a lot of listening or hearing taking place.
