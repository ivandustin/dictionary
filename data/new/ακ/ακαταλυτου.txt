The word "ακαταλυτου" (akatalytou) is a Greek adjective that means "indestructible" or "unbreakable". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the verb "καταλύω" (katalyō), which means "to dissolve" or "to destroy". 

Grammar: "ακαταλυτου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is often used in phrases such as "του ακαταλύτου" (tou akatalytou), meaning "of the indestructible" or "belonging to the unbreakable". 

Description: The word "ακαταλυτου" is often used to describe things that are extremely strong, durable, or resistant to damage. It can be used to describe physical objects such as buildings, materials, or machinery, as well as abstract concepts such as relationships, ideas, or beliefs.
