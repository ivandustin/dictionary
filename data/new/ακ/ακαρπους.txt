Word: ακαρπους

Etymology:
The word ακαρπους (akarpous) is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and καρπός (karpos) meaning "fruit". Therefore, the literal meaning of ακαρπους is "not fruitful" or "unproductive".

Grammar:
ακαρπους is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
ακαρπους is used to describe something that is unproductive, barren, or fruitless. It can be used to describe a person, a plant, or a situation. For example, a barren land can be described as ακαρπους, or a person who is not achieving anything in life can be described as ακαρπους. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a lack of progress or success in a particular endeavor.
