The word "ακουουσιν" (akouousin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The double "ου" in the word is a result of the Greek language's phonetic rules.

In terms of grammar, "ακουουσιν" is a regular verb in the present tense, and it is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: ακούω (akouo) - I hear, you hear, he/she/it hears
- Plural: ακούουμε (akououme) - we hear, ακούετε (akouete) - you hear, ακουουσιν (akouousin) - they hear

Overall, "ακουουσιν" means "they hear" in English.
