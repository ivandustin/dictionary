Word: ακαταστατος

Etymology: The word ακαταστατος comes from the Greek prefix α- (a-) meaning "not" and the Greek word κατάσταση (katastasi) meaning "state" or "condition". Therefore, ακαταστατος means "unstable" or "unsettled".

Grammar: ακαταστατος is an adjective in Greek, and it has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). In the nominative case, it is spelled ακατάστατος. In the genitive case, it is spelled ακαταστάτου. In the accusative case, it is spelled ακατάστατο.

Description: ακαταστατος is an adjective that describes something that is not stable or settled. It can be used to describe a person's behavior, a situation, or a physical object. For example, "Η κατάσταση στη χώρα είναι ακατάστατη" (The situation in the country is unstable) or "Ο πελάτης ήταν ακατάστατος και δυσκολεύτηκα να τον εξυπηρετήσω" (The customer was unstable and I had difficulty serving him).
