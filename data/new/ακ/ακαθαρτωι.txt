The word "ακαθαρτωι" (akathartoi) is a Greek adjective that means "unclean" or "impure". It is derived from the root word "καθαρός" (katharos), which means "clean" or "pure". In grammar, it is a masculine plural form in the nominative case. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe people who are spiritually unclean or impure.
