The word "ακουεις" (akoueis) is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form, in the Greek language. It comes from the root verb "ακούω" (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". 

In terms of grammar, "ακουεις" is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω". It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Singular: ακούω (akouo) - I hear, ακους (akous) - you hear, ακούει (akouei) - he/she/it hears 
- Plural: ακούμε (akoume) - we hear, ακούετε (akouete) - you hear (plural), ακούνε (akoune) - they hear 

In terms of usage, "ακουεις" is often used in everyday conversation to ask someone if they are listening or paying attention. It can also be used to express surprise or disbelief, similar to the English phrase "you're kidding".
