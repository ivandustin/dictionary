The word ακανθων comes from the Greek word ακανθα (akantha), which means "thorn" or "spine". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common ending in Greek that denotes a place or location, so ακανθων can be translated as "a place with thorns" or "a thorny place".

ακανθων is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative singular form, it is spelled ακανθων. In the genitive singular form, it is spelled ακανθωνος. In the accusative singular form, it is spelled ακανθωνα.
