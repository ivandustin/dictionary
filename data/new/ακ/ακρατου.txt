The word "ακρατου" (akratou) is a Greek adjective that means "unrestrained" or "uncontrolled". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" or "without", and the adjective "κρατούς" (kratoús) meaning "powerful" or "strong".

Grammar: "ακρατου" is in the masculine genitive singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "ακρατου" is often used to describe someone who lacks self-control or discipline. It can also refer to something that is unbridled or unchecked. The word is commonly used in philosophical and ethical discussions, particularly in the context of discussing the virtues of self-control and moderation.
