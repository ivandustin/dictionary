Word: ακαταπαυστους

Etymology:
The word ακαταπαυστους (akatapaystous) is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and κατάπαυση (katapafsi) meaning "rest". Therefore, the word means "restless" or "unrelenting".

Grammar:
ακαταπαυστους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, if the noun is feminine, the adjective would be ακατάπαυστες (akatapafstees) in the plural form.

Description:
The word ακαταπαυστους is used to describe something or someone that is constantly active, never resting, or unrelenting. It can be used to describe a person's behavior, a situation, or a feeling. For example, "Οι ακαταπαυστοι ήχοι της πόλης με κούρασαν" (The restless sounds of the city tired me out).
