Word: ακολουθειν

Etymology:
The word ακολουθειν (akolouthein) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: ακολουθος (akolouthos) meaning "follower" and ειναι (einai) meaning "to be". Therefore, ακολουθειν means "to follow" or "to accompany".

Grammar:
ακολουθειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used in the present tense, but can also be used in the past or future tense by adding the appropriate auxiliary verb.

Description:
ακολουθειν is a common verb in Greek that is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe physically following someone or something, as well as metaphorically following a plan, idea, or belief. It is often used in religious contexts to describe following the teachings of a particular faith.
