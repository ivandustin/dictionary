The word ακουσθησεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The verb form ακουσθησεται is in the future passive indicative tense, which means "it will be heard" or "it will be listened to".

ακουσθησεται is a third-person singular verb in the future passive indicative tense. In Greek grammar, the passive voice is used to indicate that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.
