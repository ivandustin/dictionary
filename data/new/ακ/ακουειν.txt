Word: ακουειν

Etymology:
The word ακουειν (akouein) is derived from the Greek verb ἀκούω (akouō), which means "to hear" or "to listen". It is a combination of the prefix ἀ- (a-), which means "not" or "without", and the verb κούω (kouō), which means "to hear".

Grammar:
ακουειν (akouein) is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the pattern of verbs that end in -ω (ō) in the present tense. The verb stem is ακου- (akou-), and the ending -ειν (-ein) indicates that it is an infinitive.

Description:
ακουειν (akouein) means "to hear" or "to listen" in Greek. It is a common verb that is used in everyday conversation and in various contexts, such as in music, language learning, and communication. The verb can be used in different tenses and moods to express different meanings and nuances. For example, in the present tense, it can be used to describe an ongoing action, such as "I am hearing the music".
