Word: ακουοντων

Etymology:
The word ακουοντων is derived from the Greek verb ακούω (akouo), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The suffix -ων (-on) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to a present action or state. Therefore, ακουοντων can be translated as "those who are hearing" or "those who are listening".

Grammar:
ακουοντων is a present participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, ακουοντων indicates that the noun it modifies is in a possessive or relational relationship with those who are hearing or listening.

Description:
ακουοντων is a word that describes a group of people who are currently hearing or listening to something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a lecture hall, a concert, or a conversation. The word emphasizes the active and ongoing nature of the act of listening, and suggests that the people described by the word are engaged and attentive.
