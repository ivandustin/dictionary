Word: ακολουθων

Etymology: ακολουθων is a present participle of the Greek verb ακολουθέω (akolouthéō), which means "to follow". It is derived from the prefix α- (a-), which means "not" or "without", and the verb κολουθέω (kolouthéō), which means "to follow".

Grammar: ακολουθων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently following or accompanying someone else.

Description: ακολουθων can be translated as "follower" or "accompanying". In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the disciples of Jesus Christ, who were his followers and companions. The word can also be used more generally to describe anyone who is following or accompanying someone else.
