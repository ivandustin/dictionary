Word: ακαθαρτος

Etymology:
The word ακαθαρτος (akathartos) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the adjective "katharos" meaning "clean" or "pure". Therefore, ακαθαρτος means "unclean" or "impure".

Grammar:
ακαθαρτος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. 

Description:
ακαθαρτος is used to describe something that is dirty, impure, or unclean. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as dirty clothes or impure substances, as well as abstract concepts, such as impure thoughts or motives. In the New Testament of the Bible, ακαθαρτος is often used to describe demons or evil spirits.
