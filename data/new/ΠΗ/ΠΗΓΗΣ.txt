Etymology: The word "πηγής" (pēgēs) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πηγή" (pēgē), meaning "spring" or "source."

Grammar: In Greek, "πηγής" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "πηγῆς" (pēgēs).

Description: "Πηγής" is a Greek word that refers to a source or spring of water. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the origin or beginning of something, such as ideas, knowledge, or inspiration.
