Etymology: The word "πηλού" (pilou) comes from the Greek word "πηλός" (pilos), which means "clay" or "mud". 

Grammar: "Πηλού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Πηλού" refers to the clay or mud used for pottery or sculpting. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is shapeless or malleable.
