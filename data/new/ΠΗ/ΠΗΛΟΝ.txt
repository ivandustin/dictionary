Etymology: The word "πηλον" (pēlon) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πηλός" (pēlos), meaning "clay" or "mud."

Grammar: In Greek, "πηλον" is a noun. It is a neuter noun of the second declension, and its nominative singular form is "πηλον."

Description: "Πηλον" refers to clay or mud, which is a type of soil composed of fine particles of minerals and organic matter. It is often used in pottery and sculpture due to its malleability and ability to be shaped when wet, and its ability to harden when dried or fired. Clay is also used in construction, agriculture, and various other industries.
