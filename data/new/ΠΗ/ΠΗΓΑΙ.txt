Etymology: The word "πηγαίνω" (pronounced "pee-ghé-no") comes from the Greek verb "πήγνυμι" (pígnymi), which means "to set in motion" or "to go." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peḱ- ("to go, to walk").

Grammar: "Πηγαίνω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ πηγαίνω (Egó pee-ghé-no) - I go
- Εσύ πηγαίνεις (Esý pee-ghé-neis) - You go (singular)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό πηγαίνει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó pee-ghé-nee) - He/She/It goes
- Εμείς πηγαίνουμε (Emís pee-ghé-noo-me) - We go
- Εσείς πηγαίνετε (Esís pee-ghé-ne-te) - You go (plural)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές πηγαίνουν (Aftí/Aftés pee-ghé-noon) - They go

Description: "Πηγαίνω" is a versatile verb that is used to express the action of going or moving from one place to another. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate the act of progressing or advancing in a certain situation or context.
