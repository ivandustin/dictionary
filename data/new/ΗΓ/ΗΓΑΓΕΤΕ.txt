Etymology: The word "ηγαγετε" (pronounced "igagete") comes from the Greek verb "αγω" (pronounced "ago"), which means "to lead" or "to bring". The prefix "η-" (pronounced "ee") is added to the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: "ηγαγετε" is the past tense form of the verb "αγω" in the second person plural. It is used to address a group of people and means "you led" or "you brought".

Description: "ηγαγετε" is a verb form that indicates an action of leading or bringing that was done by a group of people in the past. It is commonly used in Greek language to refer to a collective action or accomplishment.
