Etymology: The word "ηγεμονος" (hēgemonos) comes from the Greek root "ἡγεμών" (hēgemōn), which means "leader" or "ruler". It is derived from the verb "ἡγέομαι" (hēgeomai), meaning "to lead" or "to guide".

Grammar: "Ηγεμονος" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number. In its nominative form, it is spelled as "ηγεμονος". It can also be declined in different cases, such as genitive "ηγεμονος" (ēgemonos), dative "ηγεμονι" (ēgemoni), and accusative "ηγεμονα" (ēgemona).

Description: "Ηγεμονος" refers to a leader or ruler, someone who holds authority or exercises control over others. It can be used to describe a political leader, a military commander, or any person in a position of power and influence.
