Etymology: The word "ηγεμοσύνη" (igemosíni) comes from the Greek root words "ηγέομαι" (igéomai), meaning "to lead" or "to govern," and "σύνη" (síni), meaning "state" or "condition." 

Grammar: "Ηγεμοσύνη" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "ηγεμοσύνης" (igemosínis). 

Description: "Ηγεμοσύνη" refers to the state or condition of being a leader or governor. It can also be used to describe the authority, power, or control exercised by a leader or ruler.
