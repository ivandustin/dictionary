The word "ηγεμονευτος" (pronounced: ee-ge-mo-nef-tos) is a Greek word that can be translated as "ruler" or "leader". Here is a breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "ηγεμονευτος" is derived from the Greek root "ηγεμον" (pronounced: ee-ge-mon), which means "leader" or "ruler". The suffix "-ευτος" (-ef-tos) is added to indicate the quality or state of being a leader.

Grammar: In Greek, "ηγεμονευτος" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the singular form.

Description: "ηγεμονευτος" refers to someone who holds a position of authority or leadership. It can be used to describe a ruler, governor, or leader of any kind.
