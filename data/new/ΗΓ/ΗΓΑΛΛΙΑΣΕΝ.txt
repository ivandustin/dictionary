Etymology: The word "ηγαλλιάσεν" comes from the Greek verb "αγαλλιάζω" (agalliazo), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad". The prefix "η-" is a form of the definite article "ἡ" (he), which means "the". Therefore, "ηγαλλιάσεν" can be translated as "the rejoiced" or "the glad".

Grammar: "ηγαλλιάσεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "αγαλλιάζω" (agalliazo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The indicative mood is used to state a fact or make a statement.

Description: "ηγαλλιάσεν" describes a third person singular subject that rejoiced or was glad in the past. It can be used to talk about someone who experienced joy or happiness at a specific point in time.
