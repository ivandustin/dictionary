The word "πλανηθητε" is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "πλανάω" (planáo), which means "to wander" or "to roam". It is in the second person plural, aorist tense, passive voice, and imperative mood. 

In terms of grammar, the aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Overall, "πλανηθητε" can be translated as "you all were/were being/were to be wandered" or simply "wander!" depending on the context.
