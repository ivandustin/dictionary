Word: πλανησουσιν

Etymology:
The word πλανησουσιν is a verb form in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word πλανάω (planáō), which means "to wander" or "to go astray." The verb form πλανησουσιν is in the future tense and active voice, and it means "they will wander" or "they will go astray."

Grammar:
πλανησουσιν is a third-person plural future tense verb in the active voice. In Greek, verbs change their form to indicate tense, voice, and mood. The future tense indicates that the action will happen in the future, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
πλανησουσιν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 24:24 and Mark 13:22. In both instances, it is used in the context of false prophets and false messiahs who will deceive people in the end times. The word conveys the idea of wandering or going astray from the truth, and it suggests a sense of danger or deception.
