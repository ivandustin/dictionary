The word πλανη comes from the Greek verb πλανάω (planáo), which means "to wander" or "to roam". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pel-, which means "to move" or "to drive".

πλανη is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative singular form. The genitive form is πλάνης (planis), the accusative form is πλάνην (planin), and the dative form is πλάνῃ (planē).

Description:
πλανη is a Greek word that can be translated as "error", "deception", or "illusion". It is often used in a negative context to describe something that is misleading or false.
