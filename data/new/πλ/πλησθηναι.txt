Word: πλησθηναι

Etymology:
The word πλησθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πλήθω (pléthō), which means "to fill" or "to be full." The suffix -θηναι (-thēnai) is a passive aorist infinitive ending, indicating that the action of the verb is completed in the past and was done to the subject of the sentence.

Grammar:
πλησθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -θηναι to the root verb πλήθω. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb is completed in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is the recipient of the action.

Description:
πλησθηναι means "to be filled" or "to have been filled" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe being filled with the Holy Spirit or with joy. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe being filled with any substance or emotion.
