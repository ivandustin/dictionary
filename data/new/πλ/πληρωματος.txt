Word: πληρωματος

Etymology:
The word πληρωματος comes from the Greek word πλήρωμα (plērōma), which means "fullness" or "completion". It is derived from the verb πληρόω (plēroō), which means "to fill" or "to complete".

Grammar:
πληρωματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is often used to indicate possession or to show the relationship between two nouns.

Description:
πληρωματος refers to something that is full or complete. It can be used to describe a physical object that is filled to capacity, or it can be used in a more abstract sense to describe a state of completeness or fulfillment. In Christian theology, the word is often used to refer to the fullness of God's grace or the completeness of Christ's sacrifice.
