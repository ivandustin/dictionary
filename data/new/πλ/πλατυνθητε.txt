Word: πλατυνθητε

Etymology: The word πλατυνθητε is a conjugated form of the Greek verb πλατύνω (platyno), which means "to widen" or "to broaden". The verb is derived from the adjective πλατύς (platys), which means "wide" or "broad".

Grammar: πλατυνθητε is the second person plural, aorist passive imperative form of the verb πλατύνω (platyno). This form is used to give a command or instruction in the past tense, and it indicates that the action of widening or broadening is being done to the subject of the sentence.

Description: πλατυνθητε is a Greek verb form that instructs a group of people to widen or broaden something in the past. The context of the sentence would determine what exactly is being widened or broadened.
