Word: πληρεις

Etymology: πληρεις comes from the Greek word πλήρης (pléris), which means "full" or "complete".

Grammar: πληρεις is an adjective in the masculine, plural form. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are full or complete.

Description: πληρεις is often used to describe a group of people who are full of energy, enthusiasm, or passion. It can also be used to describe a group of things that are complete or fully stocked. In Greek mythology, the term is often used to describe the gods who are full of power and strength. Overall, πληρεις is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe fullness or completeness.
