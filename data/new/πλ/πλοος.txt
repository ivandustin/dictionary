Word: πλοος

Etymology:
The word πλοος (ploos) comes from the Greek verb πλέω (pleo), which means "to sail." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pleu-, which means "to flow" or "to swim."

Grammar:
πλοος is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative singular form. The genitive singular form is πλου, the accusative singular form is πλοον, and the nominative plural form is πλοοι.

Description:
πλοος refers to a voyage or a journey by sea. It can also refer to a ship or a boat used for sailing. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used metaphorically to describe the journey of life or the journey of the soul.
