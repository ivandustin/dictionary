Word: πλειν

Etymology:
The word πλειν (plein) is a Greek verb that means "to sail" or "to navigate". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pleu-, which means "to flow" or "to float". The word has been used in Greek literature since ancient times, and it is still used in modern Greek.

πλειν is a first conjugation verb in Greek, which means that it belongs to the group of verbs that have the ending -ω in the present tense. The conjugation of the verb is as follows:

Πλέω (I sail)
Πλέεις (you sail)

Πλέουμε (we sail)
Πλέετε (you all sail)
Πλέουν (they sail)

Description:
πλειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts related to sailing or navigation. It can refer to the physical act of sailing a ship or boat, as well as to the metaphorical idea of navigating through life or a difficult situation. The word is often used in Greek mythology and literature to describe the journeys of heroes and gods, such as Odysseus and Jason.
