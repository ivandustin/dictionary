Word: πλουτων

Etymology:
The word πλουτων (Ploutōn) is derived from the Greek word πλουτος (ploutos), which means "wealth" or "riches". In Greek mythology, Ploutōn was the god of the underworld and wealth.

Grammar:
In Greek, πλουτων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is spelled πλούτων in modern Greek.

Description:
πλουτων is a term that refers to the Greek god of the underworld, Ploutōn. He was one of the twelve Olympian gods and was associated with wealth and riches. In Roman mythology, he was known as Pluto. The name Pluto is also used to refer to the dwarf planet in our solar system.
