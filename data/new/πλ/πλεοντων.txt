Word: πλεοντων

Etymology: πλεοντων is the present participle of the Greek verb πλέω (pleo), which means "to sail" or "to navigate". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *pleu-, which means "to flow" or "to swim".

Grammar: πλεοντων is a masculine noun in the genitive case, which is the form used to show possession or relationship to another noun. It is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb πλέω (pleo).

Description: πλεοντων can be translated as "of those who are sailing" or "of those who are navigating". It is often used in Greek literature to describe sailors or seafarers. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is "on a journey" or "making progress".
