Word: πληρωθησεται

Etymology:
The word πληρωθησεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete." The suffix -θησεται (-thēsetai) is a future passive form, indicating that the action of filling or completing will be done to the subject of the sentence.

Grammar:
πληρωθησεται is a third-person singular future passive indicative verb in Greek. It is often translated as "will be fulfilled" or "will be completed." The verb is composed of the root πληρόω and the future passive suffix -θησεται.

Description:
πληρωθησεται is a verb that expresses the idea of something being filled or completed in the future. It is often used in a religious or prophetic context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the fulfillment of prophecies. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the completion of any task or goal.
