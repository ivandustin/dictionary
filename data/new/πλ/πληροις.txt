Word: πληροις

Etymology:
The word πληροις (plerois) is a Greek word that comes from the root word πληρόω (pleroo), which means "to fill" or "to complete." It is derived from the adjective πλήρης (pleres), which means "full" or "complete."

Grammar:
πληροις is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It is the second person singular present indicative active form of the verb πληρόω (pleroo).

Description:
πληροις is translated as "you fill" or "you complete" in English. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospel of John, to describe the act of filling or completing something. In John 2:7, for example, it is used to describe the filling of water jars with wine at the wedding in Cana. In John 15:11, it is used to describe the joy that comes from being filled with the love of Christ.
