Word: πλειους

Etymology:
The word πλειους (pleious) is derived from the Greek word πλέον (pleon), which means "more" or "greater." It is a comparative form of the adjective πολύς (polys), which means "many" or "much."

Grammar:
πλειους is an adjective in the masculine, nominative, and plural form. It is used to describe a noun in a comparative sense, indicating that there are more of something than another.

Description:
πλειους is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe a group of people or things that are more numerous than another. For example, in Matthew 7:13, Jesus says, "Enter through the narrow gate. For wide is the gate and broad is the road that leads to destruction, and many (πλειους) enter through it." The word is also used in other contexts to describe a greater quantity or number of something.
