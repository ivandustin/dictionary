Word: πλησάς

Etymology: πλησάς is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πλήθω (pléthō), which means "to fill" or "to be full." The suffix -άς (-ás) is a past tense marker, indicating that the action of filling has already been completed.

Grammar: πλησάς is a past participle of the verb πλήθω (pléthō), which means "to fill." As a past participle, it can function as an adjective or as part of a compound verb. In this form, it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun or pronoun it modifies.

Description: πλησάς means "having filled" or "having made full." It is often used in the context of filling something up, such as a container or a space. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is filled with a particular emotion or quality, such as joy or courage.
