The word "πληρωματι" (plērōmati) is a Greek noun that means "fullness" or "completion." It is derived from the verb "πληρόω" (plēroō), which means "to fill" or "to complete." In grammar, it is a neuter noun in the singular form. 

In the New Testament of the Bible, the word "πληρωματι" is used to describe the fullness of God's grace and the completeness of Christ's sacrifice. It is also used to describe the fullness of knowledge and wisdom in Christ. 

Overall, "πληρωματι" conveys a sense of wholeness and completeness, suggesting that something is fully realized or fully developed.
