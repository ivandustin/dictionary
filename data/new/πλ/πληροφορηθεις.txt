Word: πληροφορηθείς

Etymology:
The word πληροφορηθείς comes from the Greek verb πληροφορέω (pliroforeo), which means "to inform" or "to give information". The suffix -θείς (-theis) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of informing has already taken place.

Grammar:
πληροφορηθείς is a past participle in the passive voice, meaning "having been informed" or "having received information". It is often used in combination with the auxiliary verb έχω (echo) to form the perfect tense, as in έχω πληροφορηθείς (echo pliroforeis), which means "I have been informed".

Description:
πληροφορηθείς is a word that describes someone who has received information or has been informed about something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a legal or business setting, where someone might say "I have been informed of the terms of the contract" or "I have been informed of the decision". It can also be used in everyday conversation, such as when someone says "I have been informed about the party tonight".
