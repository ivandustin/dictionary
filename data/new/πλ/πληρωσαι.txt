Word: πληρώσαι

Etymology: 
The word πληρώσαι comes from the Greek verb πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". It is derived from the noun πλήρης (plērēs), which means "full" or "complete".

Grammar:
πληρώσαι is the aorist tense, active voice, second person singular form of the verb πληρόω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The second person singular form indicates that the subject of the sentence is "you".

Description:
πληρώσαι means "you filled" or "you completed" in English. It is a verb that describes a past action that was performed by the subject of the sentence. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as completing a task, filling a container, or fulfilling a promise.
