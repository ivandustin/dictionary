Word: πληρωθωσιν

Etymology:
The word πληρωθωσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete." The suffix -θωσιν (-thōsin) is a third-person plural aorist passive subjunctive ending, indicating that the action of the verb is completed by someone else in the past.

Grammar:
πληρωθωσιν is a third-person plural aorist passive subjunctive verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb is completed in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The subjunctive mood suggests that the action of the verb is uncertain or hypothetical.

Description:
πληρωθωσιν can be translated as "they may be filled" or "let them be filled." The verb is often used in a religious context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the filling of the Holy Spirit.
