Word: πληρωθητε

Etymology: πληρωθητε is a Greek word that comes from the verb πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The suffix -θητε (-thēte) is a passive voice ending, indicating that the action of filling or completing is being done to the subject of the verb.

Grammar: πληρωθητε is the second person plural form of the aorist passive imperative of πληρόω. In other words, it is a command directed at a group of people to be filled or completed by someone or something else.

Description: πληρωθητε can be translated as "be filled" or "be completed". It is often used in a religious context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to urge believers to be filled with the Holy Spirit. It can also be used more generally to mean "be satisfied" or "be fulfilled".
