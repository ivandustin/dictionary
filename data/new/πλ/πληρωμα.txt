Word: πληρωμα

Etymology:
The word πληρωμα (plērōma) is a Greek word that means "fullness" or "completion". It comes from the verb πληρόω (plēroō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the fullness of God's grace and the completeness of Christ's work.

Grammar:
In Greek, πληρωμα is a neuter noun. It is declined like this:

Singular:
Nominative: τὸ πλήρωμα (to plērōma) - the fullness
Genitive: τοῦ πληρώματος (tou plērōmatos) - of the fullness
Dative: τῷ πληρώματι (tō plērōmati) - to/for the fullness
Accusative: τὸ πλήρωμα (to plērōma) - the fullness

Plural:
Nominative: τὰ πληρώματα (ta plērōmata) - the fullnesses
Genitive: τῶν πληρωμάτων (tōn plērōmatōn) - of the fullnesses
Dative: τοῖς πληρώμασι (tois plērōmasi) - to/for the fullnesses
Accusative: τὰ πληρώματα (ta plērōmata) - the fullnesses

Description:
πληρωμα is a word that conveys the idea of completeness and fullness. It is often used in a theological context to describe the fullness of God's grace and the completeness of Christ's work. In Colossians 2:9-10, for example, it says, "For in him the whole fullness of deity dwells bodily, and you have been filled in him, who is the head of all rule and authority." Here, πληρωμα is used to describe the fullness of Christ's deity and the completeness of our salvation in him.
