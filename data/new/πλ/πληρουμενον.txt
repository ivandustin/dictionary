Word: πληρουμενον

Etymology:
The word πληρουμενον is a present participle form of the Greek verb πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The word is derived from the root word πλήρης (plērēs), which means "full" or "complete".

Grammar:
πληρουμενον is a present participle in the passive voice, masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενον (-menon) to the present stem of the verb πληρόω (plēróō).

Description:
πληρουμενον is used to describe something that is being filled or completed at the present time. It can also be used to describe something that is continuously being filled or completed. In English, it can be translated as "being filled" or "being completed".
