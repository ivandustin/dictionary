Word: πληρωματα

Etymology: The word πληρωματα comes from the Greek word πλήρωμα (pléroma), which means "fullness" or "completion". The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a pluralizing suffix, so πληρωματα means "completions" or "fullnesses".

Grammar: πληρωματα is a plural noun in Greek. It is the plural form of πλήρωμα (pléroma), which is a neuter noun. In English, it can be translated as "completions" or "fullnesses".

Description: πληρωματα is a term used in Christian theology to refer to the fullness of God's grace and power. It is often used in the context of the doctrine of the Trinity, where it refers to the fullness of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. In the New Testament, the word is used in Ephesians 1:23 to describe the church as the "fullness" of Christ.
