Word: πλεονέκται

Etymology:
The word πλεονέκται (pleonektai) is derived from the Greek words πλέον (pleon) meaning "more" and ἔχω (echo) meaning "to have". The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates that it is a third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar:
πλεονέκται is a third person plural present active indicative form of the verb πλεονεκτέω (pleonekteo), which means "to take advantage of, to exploit". It is a regular verb in Greek and follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -έω (-eo).

Description:
πλεονέκται refers to a group of people who are taking advantage of a situation or exploiting others for their own benefit. It can be used in a negative context to describe someone who is selfish and greedy, or in a neutral context to describe a group of people who are simply making the most of a situation.
