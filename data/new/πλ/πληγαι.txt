Word: πληγαι

Etymology:
The word πληγαι (plēgai) is a Greek noun that means "wounds" or "strikes." It is derived from the verb πλήσσω (plēssō), which means "to strike" or "to hit."

Grammar:
πληγαι is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

πληγαι is a word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe physical wounds or injuries.
