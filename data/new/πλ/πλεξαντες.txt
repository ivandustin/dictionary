Word: πλεξαντες

Etymology: 
The word πλεξαντες is a form of the Greek verb πλέκω (plekó), which means "to weave" or "to braid". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb has been completed. Therefore, πλεξαντες means "having woven" or "having braided".

Grammar:
πλεξαντες is a participle in the aorist tense, active voice, and masculine gender. It is derived from the verb πλέκω (plekó), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description:
πλεξαντες is used in Greek literature to describe someone who has completed the action of weaving or braiding. It can be used as an adjective to describe a person or thing that has been woven or braided, or as a verb to describe the act of weaving or braiding. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the crown of thorns that was woven and placed on Jesus' head before his crucifixion (Matthew 27:29).
