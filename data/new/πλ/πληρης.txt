Word: πληρης

Etymology:
The word πληρης (plērēs) comes from the Greek root word πληρόω (plēroō), which means "to fill" or "to complete." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pelh₁- ("to fill"), which is also the source of the English word "full."

πληρης is an adjective in Greek, meaning "full" or "complete." It is declined like this:

- Feminine: πλήρης (plērēs)
- Neuter: πλήρες (plēres)

- Feminine: πλήρεις (plēreis)
- Neuter: πλήρη (plērē)

Description:
πληρης is a word that describes something that is full or complete. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a full glass of water, or abstract concepts, such as a complete understanding of a subject. It can also be used to describe a person who is full of a particular quality or characteristic, such as someone who is full of joy or wisdom. In Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was often described as πληρης, meaning she was full of wisdom and knowledge.
