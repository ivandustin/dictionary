Word: πλουτησαντες

Etymology: The word πλουτησαντες is derived from the Greek verb πλουτέω (ploutéo), which means "to become rich" or "to prosper". The suffix -σαντες (-santes) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and refers to a group of people who have become rich.

Grammar: πλουτησαντες is a past participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is formed by adding the suffix -σαντες to the stem πλουτέω. The word functions as an adjective, modifying a noun or pronoun.

Description: πλουτησαντες describes a group of people who have become rich or prosperous. The word can be used to refer to individuals or a collective group. It connotes a sense of success and abundance, and is often used in a positive context.
