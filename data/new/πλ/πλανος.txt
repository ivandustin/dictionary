Word: πλανος

Etymology:
The word πλανος comes from the Greek root word πλανάω (planáo), which means "to wander" or "to roam." In ancient Greek mythology, the word was often used to describe the wandering of the planets in the sky.

Grammar:
πλανος is a masculine noun in Greek, and it is declined in the first declension. The nominative singular form is πλανός, and the genitive singular form is πλανοῦ. In the plural, the nominative form is πλανοί, and the genitive form is πλανῶν.

Description:
πλανος can be translated to mean "wandering," "roaming," or "straying." In ancient Greek literature, the word was often used to describe someone who was lost or wandering aimlessly. In modern Greek, the word can also be used to describe someone who is deceitful or untrustworthy.
