Word: πληροφορίαν

Etymology:
The word πληροφορίαν (plērophorian) is derived from the Greek word πληροφορέω (plērophoreō), which means "to inform" or "to give information". It is composed of two parts: πλήρης (plērēs), meaning "full" or "complete", and φέρω (pherō), meaning "to carry" or "to bear". 

Grammar:
πληροφορίαν is a noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. 

Description:
πληροφορίαν refers to information or knowledge that is communicated or conveyed to someone. It can also refer to a report or a piece of news. The word is commonly used in modern Greek and is often used in the context of technology and communication.
