Word: πλαστοις

Etymology:
The word πλαστοις comes from the Greek verb πλάσσω (plássō), which means "to mold" or "to shape." The suffix -οις (-ois) indicates that the word is in the dative plural form.

Grammar:
πλαστοις is a noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
πλαστοις can be translated to mean "molds" or "shapes." It is often used in the context of sculpture or pottery, where molds are used to shape the material into a desired form. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to something that is artificial or fake, as if it has been molded or shaped to appear a certain way.
