Word: πληγη

Etymology:
The word πληγη (plēgē) is derived from the Greek verb πλήσσω (plēssō), which means "to strike" or "to hit". It is related to the English word "plague", which originally referred to a contagious disease that struck many people.

Grammar:
πληγη is a feminine noun in Greek, and its nominative singular form is πληγή. It is declined like other feminine nouns in the first declension, and its genitive singular form is πληγῆς (plēgēs).

Description:
πληγη can be translated to mean "blow", "wound", or "injury". In ancient Greek literature, it was often used to describe physical wounds inflicted in battle or punishment. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the wounds inflicted on Jesus during his crucifixion. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to refer to physical injuries or wounds.
