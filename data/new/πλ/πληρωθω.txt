The word πληρωθω (plērōthō) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληροω (plēroō), which means "to fill" or "to complete." It is derived from the adjective πληρης (plērēs), which means "full" or "complete."

πληρωθω is a passive verb in the aorist tense, which means that the action of the verb happened in the past and was completed at a specific point in time. It is also in the indicative mood, which indicates a statement of fact.

πληρωθω means "to be filled" or "to be completed." It is often used in the New Testament to describe the fulfillment of prophecy or the completion of a task.
