Word: πλεονεξία

Etymology: The word πλεονεξία comes from the Greek words πλέον (pleon) meaning "more" and ἕξις (hexis) meaning "possession" or "habit". It is a compound word that refers to the desire to have more than what is necessary or what one deserves.

Grammar: πλεονεξία is a feminine noun in Greek. Its genitive form is πλεονεξίας (pleonexias) and its accusative form is πλεονεξίαν (pleonexian).

Description: πλεονεξία is a term used to describe the excessive desire for wealth or material possessions. It is often associated with greed and a lack of contentment with what one already has. In ancient Greek philosophy, πλεονεξία was considered a vice and a hindrance to living a virtuous life. It is still used today to describe the negative effects of consumerism and materialism on society.
