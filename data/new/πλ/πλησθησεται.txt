Word: πλησθησεται

Etymology:
The word πλησθησεται is a Greek verb in the future tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is derived from the root word πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to be filled." The suffix -σεται (-setai) indicates the future tense, while the prefix πλησθ- (plēsth-) indicates the passive voice.

Grammar:
As mentioned above, πλησθησεται is a Greek verb in the future tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. In this form, it means "it will be filled." The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Future tense: The suffix -σεται (-setai) indicates the future tense.
- Passive voice: The prefix πλησθ- (plēsth-) indicates the passive voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.
- Indicative mood: This indicates a statement of fact or reality.

Description:
πλησθησεται is a verb that describes the state of being filled or becoming full. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe a sense of satisfaction or completion.
