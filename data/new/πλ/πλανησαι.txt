Word: πλανησαι

Etymology:
The word πλανησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word πλανάω (planáō), which means "to wander" or "to stray." 

Grammar:
πλανησαι is a second-person singular aorist active infinitive verb in Greek. It is used to express an action that occurred in the past and is completed. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The infinitive mood indicates that the verb is not being used to show tense, person, or number, but rather to express the action in a general sense.

Description:
πλανησαι can be translated to mean "to wander" or "to stray" in English. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone who has gone astray from the right path or has lost their way. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the act of being led astray by false teachings or beliefs. Overall, πλανησαι is a word that conveys the idea of being lost or misguided.
