Word: πλην

Etymology:
The word πλην (plēn) is a Greek adverb that means "except" or "besides". It is derived from the Greek word πλέον (pleon), which means "more" or "greater".

Grammar:
πλην is a conjunction that is used to introduce an exception or a limitation to a statement. It is often translated as "except" or "besides" in English. It is usually followed by a noun or a pronoun in the genitive case.

Description:
πλην is a useful word in Greek that allows speakers to introduce exceptions or limitations to their statements. It is commonly used in legal or formal contexts, but can also be used in everyday speech. For example, one might say "πάντες είναι καλοί, πλην αυτού" (everyone is good, except for him) to indicate that there is one person who is not good.
