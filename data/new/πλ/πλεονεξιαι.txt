Word: πλεονεξιαι

Etymology: The word πλεονεξιαι comes from the Greek words πλεονέκτης (pleonéktis) meaning "greedy" and αἰσθάνομαι (aisthánomai) meaning "to feel". 

Grammar: πλεονεξιαι is a feminine noun in the plural form. 

Description: πλεονεξιαι refers to the act of being greedy or covetous. It can also refer to the desire to have more than what is necessary or what is rightfully one's own. In ancient Greece, πλεονεξιαι was considered a vice and was often associated with dishonesty and corruption.
