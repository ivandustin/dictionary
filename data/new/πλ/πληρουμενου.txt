Word: πληρουμενου

Etymology: 
The word πληρουμενου comes from the Greek verb πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The suffix -μενου (-menou) is the present passive participle form of the verb, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar:
πληρουμενου is the present passive participle form of the verb πληρόω (plēróō). It is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in the sentence. 

Description:
πληρουμενου can be translated to mean "being filled" or "being completed". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the state of being filled with the Holy Spirit. For example, in Ephesians 5:18, it says "And do not get drunk with wine, for that is debauchery, but be filled with the Spirit". In this context, πληρουμενου is used to describe the ongoing process of being filled with the Holy Spirit.
