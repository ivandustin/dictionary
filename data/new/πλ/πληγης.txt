The word "πληγης" (plēgēs) is a Greek noun that means "a blow, a wound, a stroke, or a plague." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "πλήσσω" (plēssō), which means "to strike, to hit." 

Grammar: "Πληγης" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. Its singular form is "πληγή" (plēgē), and its plural form is "πληγαί" (plēgai). 

Description: The word "πληγης" can refer to a physical injury or a disease that affects a large number of people. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe the plagues that God sent upon Egypt in the book of Exodus. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a severe setback or a calamity.
