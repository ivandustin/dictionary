Word: πληρωσαντες

Etymology: πληρωσαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates past tense and active voice, so πληρωσαντες means "having filled" or "having completed".

Grammar: πληρωσαντες is a past participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is used as an adjective to modify a noun or pronoun, indicating that the noun or pronoun has been filled or completed by the action of the verb.

Description: πληρωσαντες is a word that describes a completed action in the past. It is often used in Greek literature and the New Testament to describe the actions of individuals who have fulfilled a task or completed a mission. In English, it can be translated as "having filled" or "having completed".
