The word "πλησθηις" (plēsthēis) is a Greek verb that means "to be filled" or "to be satisfied." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "πληθω" (plēthō), which means "to fill" or "to be full." The suffix "-θηις" (-thēis) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the action of being filled or satisfied is being done to the subject of the verb.

Grammar: "πλησθηις" is a second-person singular passive aorist indicative verb. In other words, it is used to address one person (you), and it describes a completed action in the past (passive voice means the subject received the action). 

Description: The word "πλησθηις" is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe being filled with the Holy Spirit or being satisfied with God's blessings. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe being filled or satisfied with food, drink, or other physical needs.
