Word: πλουτος

Etymology:
The word πλουτος (ploutos) comes from the Greek root word πλουτ- (plout-), which means "wealth" or "riches". In Greek mythology, Ploutos was the god of wealth and abundance.

Grammar:
In Greek, πλουτος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its genitive form is πλουτου (ploutou), and its accusative form is πλουτον (plouton).

Description:
πλουτος refers to wealth, riches, or abundance. It can also be used to describe a person who is wealthy or prosperous. In ancient Greece, wealth was highly valued and often seen as a sign of success and power. The concept of πλουτος was also important in Greek philosophy, with some philosophers arguing that true happiness and fulfillment could only be achieved through a life of simplicity and detachment from material possessions.
