Word: πληροφορησον

Etymology: The word πληροφορησον is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular, of the Greek verb πληροφορέω (pliroforeo), which means "to inform" or "to give information". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: πλήρης (pliris), meaning "full" or "complete", and φέρω (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bring". Therefore, πληροφορέω literally means "to carry or bring full information".

Grammar: πληροφορησον is a second person singular imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is used to give a command or request to someone to inform or give information. The verb is formed by adding the suffix -σον (-son) to the stem πληροφορη- (plirofore-).

Description: πληροφορησον is a command or request to someone to inform or give information. It is often used in situations where someone needs to know something urgently or when someone is seeking clarification on a particular matter. The word is commonly used in formal settings, such as in business or academic contexts.
