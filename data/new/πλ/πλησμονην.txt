Word: πλησμονην

Etymology:
The word πλησμονην (plēsmonēn) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb πλησμονεῖν (plēsmonēin), which means "to fill up" or "to be full." It is derived from the adjective πλήρης (plērēs), which means "full" or "complete."

Grammar:
πλησμονην is a singular feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
πλησμονην can be translated as "fullness" or "abundance." It is often used in a spiritual or religious context to refer to the fullness of God's grace or the abundance of blessings. In the New Testament, it is used in Ephesians 3:19 to describe the "fullness of God" that believers can experience through faith.
