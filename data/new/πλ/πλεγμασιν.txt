Word: πλεγμασιν

Etymology:
The word πλεγμασιν is derived from the Greek word πλέγμα (pléghma), which means "a net" or "a mesh". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
πλεγμασιν is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. πλεγμασιν is a feminine noun in the dative case.

Description:
πλεγμασιν refers to a net or a mesh, typically used for catching fish or trapping animals. It can also refer to a web or network of interconnected things. The word is often used metaphorically to describe complex systems or structures.
