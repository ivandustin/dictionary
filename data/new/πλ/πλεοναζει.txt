Word: πλεονάζει (pleonázei)

Etymology: The word πλεονάζει comes from the Greek verb πλεονάζω (pleonázo), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound". It is derived from the adjective πλέον (pléon), meaning "more" or "excessive".

Grammar: πλεονάζει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb πλεονάζω. It is a regular verb in Greek, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -άζω.

Description: πλεονάζει is used to describe a situation where there is an excess or abundance of something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to an overabundance of food or money, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing someone who talks too much or is overly dramatic. The word can also be used to express a negative connotation, implying that the excess is unnecessary or unwanted.
