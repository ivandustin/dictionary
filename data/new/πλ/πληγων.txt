The word "πληγων" (plēgōn) is a Greek noun that means "wound" or "blow". It is derived from the verb "πλήσσω" (plēssō), which means "to strike" or "to hit". In terms of grammar, "πληγων" is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive "πληγῶν" (plēgōn) or the accusative "πληγῶνα" (plēgōna). Overall, "πληγων" is a word that describes physical injury or harm caused by a blow or wound.
