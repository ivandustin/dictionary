Word: πληθυνθηναι

Etymology:
The word πληθυνθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πληθύνω (plēthýnō), which means "to multiply" or "to increase in number." The suffix -θηναι (-thēnai) is a passive form that indicates that the action is being done to the subject of the verb.

Grammar:
πληθυνθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not being used to describe a specific subject, but rather as a general concept.

Description:
πληθυνθηναι can be translated to mean "to be multiplied" or "to be increased in number." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the growth or expansion of something, such as a population or an idea. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the multiplication of loaves and fishes by Jesus, as well as the growth of the early Christian church.
