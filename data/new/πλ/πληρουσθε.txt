The word "πληρουσθε" is a verb in the Greek language, which is derived from the root word "πληρόω" (plēroō). It is a present passive imperative form of the verb, which means "to be filled" or "to be made complete". 

In terms of grammar, "πληρουσθε" is a second-person plural verb, which means it is used to address a group of people. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action themselves. The imperative mood indicates that the verb is a command or instruction.

Overall, "πληρουσθε" is a word that is used to instruct a group of people to be filled or made complete in some way. It is often used in a spiritual or religious context, such as in the Bible, where it is used to encourage believers to be filled with the Holy Spirit.
