Word: πλεονέκταις

Etymology:
The word πλεονέκταις (pleonektēs) is derived from the Greek words πλέον (pleon) meaning "more" and ἔχω (ekho) meaning "to have". It literally means "one who has more" or "one who desires more".

Grammar:
πλεονέκταις is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. 

Description:
πλεονέκταις is a term used to describe someone who is greedy or selfish, who seeks to gain an advantage over others. It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is willing to exploit others for their own gain. The word can also be used to describe someone who is overly ambitious or who seeks to accumulate wealth or power at the expense of others.
