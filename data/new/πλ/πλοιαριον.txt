Word: πλοιάριον

Etymology:
The word πλοιάριον (ploiárion) is derived from the Greek word πλοῖον (ploîon), which means "ship" or "vessel". The suffix -άριον (-árion) is a diminutive suffix, which means "little" or "small". Therefore, πλοιάριον can be translated as "little ship" or "small vessel".

Grammar:
In Greek, πλοιάριον is a neuter noun. Its nominative singular form is πλοιάριον, and its genitive singular form is πλοιαρίου (ploiariou). The word can also be declined in the plural form, which is πλοιάρια (ploiaria).

Description:
πλοιάριον refers to a small ship or vessel. It can be used to describe any type of small watercraft, such as a rowboat, sailboat, or motorboat. The word is often used in the context of fishing or transportation, as small boats were commonly used for these purposes in ancient Greece. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to small boats or yachts.
