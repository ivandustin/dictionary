The word "πληθυνθειη" is a Greek verb in the aorist passive subjunctive mood. It comes from the root word "πληθυνω" which means "to multiply" or "to increase in number". The suffix "-θειη" indicates the subjunctive mood and the passive voice. 

In terms of grammar, the aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action themselves.

Overall, "πληθυνθειη" can be translated as "may it be multiplied" or "let it be increased in number" in English.
