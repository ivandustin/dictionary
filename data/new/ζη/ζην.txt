ζην (zen) is a Greek verb that means "to live" or "to be alive". It is derived from the ancient Greek word ζάω (zao), which also means "to live". In terms of grammar, ζην is a present active infinitive, which means it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of living in a general sense. 

In ancient Greek philosophy, ζην was often used to refer to the concept of "the good life" or "living well". This idea was central to the teachings of philosophers such as Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle, who believed that the purpose of human existence was to live a virtuous and fulfilling life. 

Overall, ζην is a fundamental word in the Greek language that expresses the concept of life and living, both in a literal and philosophical sense.
