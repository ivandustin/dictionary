Word: ζηλουσιν

Etymology:
ζηλουσιν is a present active indicative verb form in Greek. It is derived from the root word ζηλος (zelos), which means "zeal" or "envy". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates the third person plural present tense.

Grammar:
ζηλουσιν is a verb in the present tense, indicating that the action is currently happening. It is in the active voice, meaning that the subject is performing the action. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
ζηλουσιν can be translated to mean "they are jealous" or "they envy". It is often used in a negative context to describe someone who is envious or covetous of someone else's possessions, status, or achievements. The word can also be used in a positive context to describe someone who is passionate or enthusiastic about something.
