Word: ζητειν

Etymology:
ζητειν (zetein) is a Greek verb that means "to seek" or "to search for". It comes from the ancient Greek word ζητέω (zēteō), which has the same meaning.

Grammar:
ζητειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific tense, person, or number. It is often used in combination with other verbs to form compound verbs, such as αναζητείν (anazēteīn) which means "to search thoroughly".

Description:
ζητειν is a common verb in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe the act of searching for something, whether it be a physical object or an abstract concept. It can also be used to describe the act of seeking out information or knowledge. In addition, it can be used in a more metaphorical sense, such as seeking out a solution to a problem or seeking out a new opportunity.
