Word: ζητούντες

Etymology: ζητούντες is the present participle of the Greek verb ζητέω (zētéō), which means "to seek" or "to search for". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁gʰes-.

Grammar: ζητούντες is a masculine plural form of the present participle in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently seeking or searching for something.

Description: ζητούντες can be translated to "seekers" or "searchers" in English. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe people who are seeking knowledge, truth, or enlightenment. In a religious context, it can refer to those who are seeking God or a higher power. In everyday language, it can refer to people who are searching for a solution to a problem or trying to find something that is lost.
