Word: ζητων

Etymology:
ζητων is the present participle of the Greek verb ζητέω (zeteo), which means "to seek" or "to search for". The word ζητων is derived from the root word ζητ- (zet-), which means "to seek" or "to desire".

Grammar:
ζητων is a masculine noun in the nominative case, singular number, and present participle tense. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ων (-on) to the present stem of the verb. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state.

Description:
ζητων can be translated as "seeking" or "searching". It is often used in the context of seeking or searching for something specific, such as knowledge, truth, or answers. In Greek philosophy, the concept of ζητων is closely related to the idea of inquiry and the pursuit of wisdom.
