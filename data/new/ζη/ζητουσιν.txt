Word: ζητούσιν

Etymology: ζητούσιν is a present participle form of the Greek verb ζητέω (zēteō), which means "to seek" or "to search for". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁gʰes-.

Grammar: ζητούσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative form of ζητέω. In this form, it indicates that the subject (which is not specified) is currently seeking or searching for something.

Description: ζητούσιν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 7:7 and Luke 11:9. In both instances, it is part of the phrase "ζητεῖτε καὶ εὑρήσετε" (zēteite kai heurēsete), which means "seek and you will find". This phrase is often used to encourage people to actively search for knowledge, truth, or spiritual enlightenment.
