Word: ζητούν

Etymology: ζητούν is a present tense verb in Greek, derived from the root word ζητώ (zito), which means "to seek" or "to search for". It is a common verb in modern Greek and is used in various contexts.

Grammar: ζητούν is a third-person plural present tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated as follows:

- ζητούν (zitoún) - present tense, indicative mood, active voice, third-person plural

Description: ζητούν is a verb that is commonly used in everyday speech in Greek. It can be used to express the act of searching for something or someone, or to describe the act of seeking information or advice. It can also be used in a figurative sense, to describe the act of pursuing a goal or objective. Overall, ζητούν is a versatile verb that is used in a variety of contexts in modern Greek.
