Word: ζητηματα

Etymology:
ζητηματα (zitimata) is a Greek word that comes from the verb ζητώ (zito), which means "to seek" or "to search for". The word ζητηματα refers to issues, problems, or questions that require investigation or resolution.

Grammar:
ζητηματα is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has a masculine gender. The singular form of the word is ζήτημα (zitima).

Description:
ζητηματα is a common word in Greek that is used to refer to various types of issues or problems. It can be used in a formal or informal context and can refer to anything from personal problems to political issues. The word is often used in discussions or debates to refer to a point of contention or disagreement that needs to be resolved.
