Word: ζηλωσαντες

Etymology: ζηλωσαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb ζηλόω (zēlóō), which means "to be jealous" or "to envy". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the past tense and active voice, so ζηλωσαντες means "they were jealous" or "they envied".

Grammar: ζηλωσαντες is a past tense participle in the active voice, which means it describes an action that was completed in the past and was performed by the subject of the sentence. In Greek, participles can function as adjectives, nouns, or verbs, depending on the context. In this case, ζηλωσαντες is used as an adjective to describe the subject of the sentence.

Description: ζηλωσαντες is a word that describes a state of mind or emotion, specifically jealousy or envy. It is often used in the context of interpersonal relationships, where one person feels envious of another's possessions, achievements, or relationships. The word can also be used in a broader sense to describe a general feeling of discontent or resentment towards someone or something. In Greek literature, ζηλωσαντες is often used to describe the behavior of gods and mortals alike, highlighting the universal nature of jealousy and envy.
