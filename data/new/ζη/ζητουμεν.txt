Word: ζητούμεν (zitoumen)

Etymology: The word ζητούμεν comes from the Greek verb ζητώ (zito), which means "to seek" or "to search for". The suffix -ούμεν (-oumen) is a passive participle ending, which means that the word ζητούμεν can be translated as "that which is sought" or "that which is being searched for".

Grammar: ζητούμεν is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is in the nominative case, which means that it is the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive, dative, and accusative.

Description: ζητούμεν refers to something that is being sought or searched for. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a job search, a quest for knowledge, or a search for a lost item. The word implies a sense of urgency or importance, as the thing being sought is considered valuable or necessary.
