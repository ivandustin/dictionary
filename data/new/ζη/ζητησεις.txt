Word: ζητήσεις

Etymology: ζητήσεις comes from the Greek verb ζητώ (zito), which means "to seek" or "to ask for". The suffix -εις (-eis) is added to the verb stem to form the plural noun ζητήσεις (zitiseis), which means "requests" or "demands".

Grammar: ζητήσεις is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: η ζήτηση (i zitisi) - the request
- Genitive: της ζήτησης (tis zitisis) - of the request
- Accusative: τη ζήτηση (ti zitisi) - the request

Plural:
- Nominative: οι ζητήσεις (i zitiseis) - the requests
- Genitive: των ζητήσεων (ton zitiseon) - of the requests
- Accusative: τις ζητήσεις (tis zitiseis) - the requests

Description: ζητήσεις refers to requests or demands made by someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as in business, politics, or personal relationships. For example, a customer may make a request for a refund, a politician may make a demand for a change in policy, or a friend may make a request for help. ζητήσεις can also be used in a legal context, where it refers to a formal request made to a court or other legal authority.
