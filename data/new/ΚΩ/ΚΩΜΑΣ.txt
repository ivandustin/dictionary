Etymology: The word "κώμας" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "κωμάω" (kōmáō), which means "to revel" or "to celebrate." 

Grammar: In Greek, "κώμας" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (κώμου), dative (κώμῳ), accusative (κῶμα), and vocative (κώμα). 

Description: "Κώμας" refers to a festive gathering or celebration, often involving music, dancing, and revelry. It can also be used to describe a party or a social event where people come together to enjoy themselves.
