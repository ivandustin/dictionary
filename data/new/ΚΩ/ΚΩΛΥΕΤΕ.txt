Etymology: The word "κωλυετε" comes from the Greek verb "κωλύω" (kōlýō), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent."

Grammar: "κωλυετε" is the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice form of the verb "κωλύω" in Greek. It is the third person plural form, meaning "they hinder" or "they prevent."

Description: "κωλυετε" is a verb in Greek that expresses the action of hindering or preventing something. It is used to describe the act of stopping or obstructing someone or something from proceeding or achieving a certain goal.
