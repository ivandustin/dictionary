Etymology: The word "κωμοις" (kōmois) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "κῶμος" (kōmos), which means "revelry" or "festivity."

Grammar: "Κωμοις" (kōmois) is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Κωμοις" (kōmois) refers to "revelers" or "merrymakers." It is used to describe a group of people who engage in festive celebrations or parties.
