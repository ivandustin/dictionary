Etymology: The word "κωλυεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "κωλύω" (kōlýō), which means "to hinder" or "to prevent."

Grammar: "κωλυεσθαι" is a verb in the present middle/passive infinitive form. It belongs to the second conjugation class in Greek verbs.

Description: The word "κωλυεσθαι" means "to be hindered" or "to be prevented." It indicates the passive or middle voice of the verb "κωλύω" and implies that the subject is being hindered or prevented from doing something.
