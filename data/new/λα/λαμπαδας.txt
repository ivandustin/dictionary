Word: λαμπαδας

Etymology:
The word λαμπαδας (lampadas) is derived from the Greek word λαμπάς (lampas), which means "torch" or "lamp". The suffix -αδας (-adas) is a common Greek suffix used to form nouns denoting a person or thing that is associated with the root word.

Grammar:
λαμπαδας is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word is also declined in the genitive, dative, and accusative cases, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
λαμπαδας refers to a torch or a lamp, which is a portable source of light. In ancient Greece, torches were used in religious ceremonies, processions, and athletic events.
