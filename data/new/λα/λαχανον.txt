Word: λαχανον

Etymology: The word λαχανον comes from the Greek word λάχανον (láchanon), which means "vegetable" or "greens". It is derived from the verb λαχάνειν (lachánein), which means "to dig up vegetables".

Grammar: λαχανον is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender, and λαχανον is neuter. Its nominative case is λαχανον, and its genitive case is λαχανου.

Description: λαχανον refers to any type of vegetable or greens, such as lettuce, spinach, or cabbage. It is a common ingredient in Greek cuisine and is often used in salads, soups, and stews. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to something that is unimportant or insignificant.
