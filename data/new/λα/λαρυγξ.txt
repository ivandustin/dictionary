Word: λαρυγξ

Etymology: The word λαρυγξ (larynx) comes from the Greek word λάρυγξ (larunx), which means "throat" or "windpipe".

Grammar: In Greek, λαρυγξ is a feminine noun. Its plural form is λάρυγγες (larynges).

Description: The larynx is a part of the respiratory system located in the neck. It is also known as the voice box, as it contains the vocal cords that produce sound when air passes through them. The larynx also plays a crucial role in protecting the airway during swallowing and preventing food or liquid from entering the lungs. Various conditions can affect the larynx, including infections, tumors, and vocal cord dysfunction. Treatment options depend on the underlying cause and may include medication, surgery, or voice therapy.
