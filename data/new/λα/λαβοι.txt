Word: λαβοι

Etymology:
The word λαβοι (laboi) is a plural form of the Greek noun λαβή (labē), which means "handle" or "grip". The word is derived from the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to seize".

Grammar:
In Greek, λαβοι is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is the plural form of λαβή (labē), which is a feminine noun in the singular form. The word λαβοι is declined according to the second declension, which means that its stem ends in -οι.

Description:
The word λαβοι refers to handles or grips, typically on tools or weapons. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a means of control or influence. The word is commonly used in the context of ancient Greek art and architecture, where it refers to the handles on vases, urns, and other decorative objects.
