Word: λαληθησομενων

Etymology:
The word λαληθησομενων is a present participle form of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The word is derived from the root λαλ- (lal-), which is related to the sound of speech.

Grammar:
λαληθησομενων is a present participle form of the verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "speaking" or "talking". It is in the passive voice and the plural number, indicating that the action of speaking is being done to or about a group of people. The word is also in the genitive case, indicating possession or association with another noun.

Description:
λαληθησομενων can be translated as "of those who are speaking" or "of those who are being spoken about". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the words spoken by prophets or apostles. The word emphasizes the ongoing nature of the speaking or the message being conveyed.
