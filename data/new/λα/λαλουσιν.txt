Word: λαλουσιν

Etymology: λαλουσιν is the present active participle of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- ("to speak").

Grammar: λαλουσιν is a third-person plural present active participle in the Greek language. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently speaking or talking.

Description: λαλουσιν can be translated to "they are speaking" or "they are talking" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe groups of people who are discussing or preaching about religious topics.
