Word: λαλησομεν

Etymology:
λαλησομεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The suffix -σομεν (-somen) is a future tense ending, indicating that the verb is in the first person plural form, meaning "we will speak" or "we will talk".

Grammar:
λαλησομεν is a first person plural future tense verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σομεν to the root verb λαλέω. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: λαλούμεν (laloumen) - "we speak"
- Future tense: λαλησομεν (lalesomen) - "we will speak"

Description:
λαλησομεν is a Greek verb that means "we will speak" or "we will talk". It is a future tense verb, indicating that the action of speaking or talking will take place at some point in the future. The verb is commonly used in everyday conversation and can be used in a variety of contexts, such as making plans, discussing ideas, or expressing opinions.
