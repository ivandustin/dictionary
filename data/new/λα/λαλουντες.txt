Word: λαλοῦντες (lalountes)

Etymology: 
The word λαλοῦντες comes from the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The suffix -οῦντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently speaking or talking.

Grammar:
λαλοῦντες is a present participle in the nominative plural form. It is derived from the verb λαλέω (laleo), which is in the present tense. The word can function as a noun or an adjective, depending on the context.

Description:
λαλοῦντες refers to people who are currently speaking or talking. It can be used to describe a group of people who are engaged in conversation or to refer to individuals who are speaking at a particular moment. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe people who are spreading ideas or information, such as in the phrase "the talking heads on TV".
