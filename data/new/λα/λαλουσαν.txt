Word: λαλοῦσαν (lalousan)

Etymology: λαλοῦσαν is the present participle of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- ("to speak").

Grammar: λαλοῦσαν is a feminine singular form of the present participle in the active voice. It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of the sentence. The verb λαλέω is an irregular verb, and its present participle form is formed by adding -οῦσαν (-ousan) to the stem.

Description: λαλοῦσαν describes a female subject who is currently speaking or talking. It can be translated as "she who is speaking" or "she who talks". The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Η γυναίκα λαλοῦσαν μεταξύ των φίλων της" (The woman who is speaking among her friends).
