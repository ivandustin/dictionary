Word: λανθανειν

Etymology: λανθανειν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λανθάνω (lanthánō), which means "to escape notice" or "to be hidden". 

Grammar: λανθανειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is often translated as "to hide" or "to conceal". 

Description: λανθανειν is a verb that describes the act of hiding or concealing something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as hiding an object or concealing a secret, or in a figurative sense, such as hiding one's emotions or intentions. In Greek mythology, the goddess of deception and trickery, Apate, was often associated with the verb λανθανειν.
