Word: λαβων

Etymology: λαβων is the aorist participle of the Greek verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to receive".

Grammar: λαβων is a masculine singular nominative participle in the aorist tense. It is declined as follows: λαβών, λαβόντος, λαβόντι, λαβόντα, λαβών.

Description: λαβων is used to describe someone who has taken or received something in the past. It can also be used as an adjective to describe someone who is taking or receiving something at the present moment. In Greek literature, λαβων is often used to describe heroes who have taken up arms or who have received a divine gift or blessing.
