Word: λαλοῦντων

Etymology: λαλοῦντων is the present active participle of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleō), which means "to speak" or "to talk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- ("to speak").

Grammar: λαλοῦντων is a masculine, plural, genitive form of the present active participle of λαλέω. In Greek, the present active participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state. The genitive case indicates possession or relationship.

Description: λαλοῦντων can be translated as "of those who are speaking" or "of those who talk". It is often used in the New Testament to refer to people who are speaking or preaching about the gospel. For example, in Acts 2:11, it is used to describe the people who were speaking in tongues on the day of Pentecost: "we hear them speaking in our own tongues of the mighty works of God."
