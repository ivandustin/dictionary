Word: λαλούσης

Etymology: λαλούσης is the present participle of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- ("to speak").

Grammar: λαλούσης is a masculine singular present participle in the active voice. It is declined like a regular first-declension noun, with the stem λαλουσ-, and the ending -ης. It is used to describe someone who is currently speaking or talking.

Description: λαλούσης is a Greek word that means "speaking" or "talking". It is often used in the context of describing someone who is currently engaged in conversation or giving a speech. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any kind of verbal communication.
