The word λαβουσα (labousa) is a feminine participle form of the Greek verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to receive". It is in the nominative singular case and translates to "she who takes" or "she who receives". In terms of grammar, it is a present active participle, indicating ongoing action in the present tense. 

Etymologically, λαβουσα comes from the root λαβ- (lab-), which means "to take" or "to seize". The suffix -ουσα (-ousa) is a feminine present active participle ending, indicating the subject is a woman who is currently taking or receiving something. 

Overall, λαβουσα is a specific form of the verb λαμβάνω that describes a woman who is actively taking or receiving something in the present moment.
