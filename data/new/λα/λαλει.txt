Word: λαλει

Etymology: λαλει comes from the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to talk" or "to speak". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- which means "to speak".

Grammar: λαλει is the third person singular present active indicative form of the verb λαλέω. It is used to describe someone who is currently speaking or talking.

Description: λαλει is a Greek word that refers to the act of speaking or talking. It can be used to describe any type of verbal communication, from casual conversation to formal speeches. The word is often used in religious contexts to describe the act of preaching or proclaiming the word of God.
