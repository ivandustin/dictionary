Word: λαβοντα

Etymology: λαβοντα is the present active participle of the Greek verb λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to receive".

Grammar: λαβοντα is in the masculine gender, singular number, and accusative case. It is used to describe the direct object of the verb in a sentence.

Description: λαβοντα can be translated as "taking" or "receiving" in English. It is commonly used in Greek literature and language to describe an action that is currently happening or has just happened. For example, "ο λαβώντας το βιβλίο" (o labóntas to vivlío) means "the one who is taking the book" or "the one who has taken the book".
