Word: λαληθεντων

Etymology: λαληθεντων is the genitive plural form of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- ("to speak").

Grammar: λαληθεντων is a third declension noun in Greek, with a masculine gender and a plural number. It is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: λαληθεντων can be translated as "of those who have spoken" or "of those who have talked". It is often used in the context of religious or philosophical texts to refer to those who have spoken or written about a particular topic. In the New Testament, for example, it is used to refer to the prophets who spoke about the coming of Jesus Christ.
