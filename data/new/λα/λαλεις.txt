Word: λαλεις

Etymology: λαλεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαλέω (laleo), which means "to talk" or "to speak."

Grammar: λαλεις is the second person singular present indicative form of the verb λαλέω. It is translated to "you speak" or "you are speaking" in English.

Description: λαλεις is a common verb in the Greek language and is used to describe the act of speaking or talking. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in everyday conversation, public speaking, or storytelling. The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the teachings of Jesus and the apostles.
