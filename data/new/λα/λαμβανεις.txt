Word: λαμβάνεις

Etymology:
The word λαμβάνεις (lamvaneis) is a present tense verb form in Greek, derived from the root word λαμβάνω (lamvano), which means "to take" or "to receive". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leb- which means "to take" or "to seize".

Grammar:
λαμβάνεις is the second person singular present indicative form of the verb λαμβάνω (lamvano). It is used to indicate that the subject (you) is currently taking or receiving something. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
Εγώ λαμβάνω (I take/receive)
Εσύ λαμβάνεις (You take/receive)
Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό λαμβάνει (He/She/It takes/receives)

Plural:
Εμείς λαμβάνουμε (We take/receive)
Εσείς λαμβάνετε (You take/receive)
Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά λαμβάνουν (They take/receive)

Description:
λαμβάνεις is a common verb in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to indicate the act of physically taking something, such as a book or a piece of food. It can also be used to indicate the act of receiving something, such as a gift or a message.
