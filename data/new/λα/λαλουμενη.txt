Word: λαλουμενη

Etymology:
The word λαλουμενη is derived from the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The suffix -μενη (-meni) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is describing an action that is currently happening.

Grammar:
λαλουμενη is a feminine singular form of the present participle of the verb λαλέω (laleo). It is in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence. In English, it would be translated as "the speaking (woman)" or "the woman who is speaking".

Description:
λαλουμενη describes a woman who is currently speaking or talking. It can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is characterized by speech or conversation. For example, one might say "η λαλουμενη αυτή ημέρα" (i la loumeni afti imera), which means "this talkative day".
