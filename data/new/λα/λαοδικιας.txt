λαοδικιας (Laodikias) is a Greek word that is derived from the combination of two words: λαός (laos) meaning "people" and δίκη (dike) meaning "justice". It is the name of a city in ancient Phrygia, located in modern-day Turkey. In grammar, λαοδικιας is a proper noun and is typically capitalized. It is often used in reference to the biblical book of Colossians, where it is mentioned as the location of one of the seven churches of Asia.
