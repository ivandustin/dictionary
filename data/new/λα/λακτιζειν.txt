Word: λακτιζειν

Etymology: The word λακτιζειν comes from the Greek word λακτίζω (laktízo), which means "to suckle" or "to nurse". It is derived from the noun λακτίς (laktís), which means "milk".

Grammar: λακτιζειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem λακτιζ- (laktiz-).

Description: λακτιζειν refers to the act of breastfeeding or nursing a baby or young animal. It is a natural and important process for the growth and development of infants. In Greek mythology, the goddess Hera was often depicted breastfeeding her son, Hercules. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe nurturing or caring for someone or something.
