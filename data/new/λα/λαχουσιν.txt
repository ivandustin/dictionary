The word "λαχουσιν" (lachousin) is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically in the present tense, third person plural. It is derived from the root word "λαγχάνω" (lanchano), which means "to obtain by lot" or "to win by chance." 

In terms of grammar, "λαχουσιν" is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" in Greek. It is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action of obtaining or winning by chance. 

Overall, "λαχουσιν" can be translated to mean "they obtain by lot" or "they win by chance." It is often used in the context of lotteries or raffles, where the outcome is determined by chance rather than skill or effort.
