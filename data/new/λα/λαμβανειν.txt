Word: λαμβάνειν

The word λαμβάνειν (lamvanein) comes from the ancient Greek verb λαμβάνω (lamvano), which means "to take" or "to receive". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leb- which means "to take" or "to seize".

λαμβάνειν is a present active infinitive verb in ancient Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation of verbs, which means that it has the stem λαμβαν- and the ending -ειν. The verb is used to express the action of taking or receiving something.

λαμβάνειν is a common verb in ancient Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe the act of taking something physically, such as taking a book from a shelf. It can also be used to describe the act of receiving something, such as receiving a gift or a message.
