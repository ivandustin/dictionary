Word: λαβουσαι

Etymology: λαβουσαι is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the Greek verb λαμβάνω (lamvano), which means "to take" or "to receive".

Grammar: λαβουσαι is a participle, which is a verbal adjective that describes the action of the verb. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: λαβουσαι can be translated as "having taken" or "having received". It is often used in Greek literature to describe a past action that has already been completed. For example, in the New Testament, it is used to describe the women who had taken spices to anoint Jesus' body after his crucifixion (Mark 16:1).
