Word: λαληθεντος

Etymology: λαληθεντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The suffix -θεντος (-thentos) is a passive participle ending, which means that the word λαληθεντος can be translated as "spoken" or "said".

Grammar: λαληθεντος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In Greek, adjectives usually follow the noun they modify.

Description: λαληθεντος is an adjective that describes something that has been spoken or said. It can be used to refer to a specific statement or to the act of speaking in general. The word is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to describe the nature of truth and knowledge.
