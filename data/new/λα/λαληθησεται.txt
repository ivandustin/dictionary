Word: λαληθησεται

Etymology:
λαληθησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The word is in the future passive indicative tense, which means "it will be spoken" or "it will be said".

Grammar:
λαληθησεται is a third-person singular verb in the future passive indicative tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates that the action will happen in the future, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description:
λαληθησεται is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Matthew 10:26 and Luke 12:2. In both instances, it is used in the context of Jesus telling his disciples that the truth will eventually be revealed, even if it is initially hidden or suppressed. The word emphasizes the idea that the truth will be spoken or declared, regardless of attempts to keep it hidden.
