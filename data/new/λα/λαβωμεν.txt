Word: λαβωμεν

Etymology: λαβωμεν is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word λαμβάνω (lambanō), which means "to take" or "to receive."

Grammar: λαβωμεν is the first person plural aorist active subjunctive form of the verb λαμβάνω (lambanō). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or a hypothetical situation. The aorist tense indicates that the action was completed in the past.

Description: λαβωμεν can be translated as "let us take" or "let us receive." It is often used in the context of a command or suggestion, where the speaker is urging others to take action. For example, in the New Testament, Jesus uses this verb when he says, "Let us go across to the other side" (Mark 4:35).
