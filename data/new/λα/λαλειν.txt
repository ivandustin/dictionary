Word: λαλειν

Etymology:
The word λαλειν (lalein) is a Greek verb that means "to speak" or "to talk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- which means "to speak" or "to tell".

Grammar:
λαλειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation and is classified as a thematic verb. The conjugation of this verb in the present tense is as follows:

Singular:
- Λαλῶ (lalō) - I speak
- Λαλεῖς (laleis) - You speak
- Λαλεῖ (lalei) - He/she/it speaks

Plural:
- Λαλοῦμεν (laloumen) - We speak
- Λαλεῖτε (laleite) - You speak
- Λαλοῦσι(ν) (lalousi(n)) - They speak

Description:
λαλειν is a common Greek verb that is used to express the act of speaking or talking. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in everyday conversation, public speaking, or storytelling. The verb is often used in combination with other words to create compound verbs that convey more specific meanings, such as διαλέγομαι (dialěgomai) which means "to converse" or "to discuss". Overall, λαλειν is an important verb in the Greek language that is used frequently in both spoken and written communication.
