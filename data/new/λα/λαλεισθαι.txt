Word: λαλεῖσθαι (laleisthai)

Etymology: The word λαλεῖσθαι comes from the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) is a reflexive suffix, indicating that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar: λαλεῖσθαι is a passive form of the verb λαλέω. It is in the present tense and the infinitive mood. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: λαλεῖσθαι means "to be spoken" or "to be talked about". It is often used in a passive sense, indicating that something is being discussed or talked about by others. For example, "Οἱ πολίτες λαλοῦσιν περὶ τῆς πολιτικῆς καταστάσεως" (Hoi polites lalousin peri tes politikes katastaseos) means "The citizens are talking about the political situation".
