Word: λατρευουσιν

Etymology: λατρευουσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λατρεία (latreia), which means "worship" or "service." The verb form λατρεύω (latreuó) means "to serve" or "to worship," and the -ουσιν (-ousin) ending indicates the third person plural present tense.

Grammar: λατρευουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject is performing the action of worship or service. In Greek, verbs are conjugated to indicate the tense, mood, voice, and person of the subject.

Description: λατρευουσιν is a verb that describes the act of worship or service. It can be used in a religious context to refer to the worship of a deity or in a secular context to refer to the act of serving or helping others.
