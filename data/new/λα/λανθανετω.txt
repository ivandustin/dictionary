Word: λανθανετω

Etymology: λανθανετω is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λανθάνω (lanthano), which means "to escape notice," "to be hidden," or "to be concealed."

Grammar: λανθανετω is a present active imperative verb in the third person singular. It is often translated as "let it be hidden" or "let it remain concealed."

Description: λανθανετω is a command or request for something to remain hidden or concealed. It can be used in a literal sense, such as asking someone to hide an object, or in a figurative sense, such as asking someone to keep a secret. The word is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to refer to the hidden or mysterious aspects of the divine or the universe.
