Word: λατρεύσουσιν

Etymology: λατρεύω (latreuo) is a Greek verb meaning "to serve, worship, or adore." The suffix -σουσιν (-sousin) is the third person plural future active indicative form of the verb, indicating that the subject will serve, worship, or adore in the future.

Grammar: λατρεύσουσιν is a third person plural future active indicative form of the verb λατρεύω. In Greek, verbs change form to indicate tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates that the action will take place in the future, the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action, and the indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: λατρεύσουσιν is a Greek verb meaning "they will serve, worship, or adore." It is often used in religious contexts to describe the act of worshiping or serving a deity. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any act of service or devotion.
