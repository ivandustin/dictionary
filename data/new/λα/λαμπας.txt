Word: λαμπας

Etymology:
The word λαμπας (lampas) comes from the Greek verb λάμπω (lambo), which means "to shine" or "to give light". It is related to the English word "lamp".

Grammar:
λαμπας is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. In the genitive singular form, it is λαμπαδός (lampados). In the plural form, it is λαμπάδες (lampades).

Description:
λαμπας is a word that refers to a torch or a lamp. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe a torch that was used in religious ceremonies or as a symbol of victory. In modern Greek, it is used to describe a lamp or a light bulb.
