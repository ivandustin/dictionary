Word: λαλοῦν (laloun)

Etymology:
The word λαλοῦν comes from the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- ("to speak").

Grammar:
λαλοῦν is the present participle of λαλέω (laleo), which means "speaking" or "talking". It is in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case.

Description:
λαλοῦν is a word that describes someone who is currently speaking or talking. It can be used to refer to a specific person or group of people who are engaged in conversation or giving a speech. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of speaking or talking in general.
