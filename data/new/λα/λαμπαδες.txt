Word: λαμπάδες

Etymology:
The word λαμπάδες (lampades) is derived from the Greek word λαμπάς (lampras), which means "torch" or "lamp". In Greek mythology, the Lampades were nymphs of the underworld who carried torches and accompanied Hecate, the goddess of witchcraft and magic.

Grammar:
λαμπάδες is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case and is feminine in gender. The singular form of the word is λαμπάς.

Description:
λαμπάδες refers to torches or lamps that are used for lighting. In modern Greek, the word is often used to refer to the torches that are carried during religious processions, especially during Easter. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to something that illuminates or enlightens, such as knowledge or wisdom.
