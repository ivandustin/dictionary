Word: λαλουμενον

Etymology: λαλουμενον is a present participle of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- ("to speak").

Grammar: λαλουμενον is a masculine singular nominative present participle in the Greek language. It is declined according to the first declension, and its stem is λαλουμενον-.

Description: λαλουμενον is a word that describes someone or something that is currently speaking or talking. It can be translated as "speaking", "talking", or "uttering". The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus or other characters who are speaking.
