Word: λατρεύουσα (latreuousa)

Etymology: The word λατρεύουσα comes from the Greek verb λατρεύω (latreuo), which means "to serve" or "to worship." The suffix -ουσα (-ousa) indicates the present participle form, meaning "she who is serving/worshipping."

Grammar: λατρεύουσα is a feminine singular present participle in the active voice. It is derived from the verb λατρεύω, which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description: λατρεύουσα is used to describe a female who is currently serving or worshiping. It can be used in a religious context to describe a woman who is actively engaged in worshiping a deity, or in a secular context to describe a woman who is serving in some capacity, such as a waitress or a maid.
