Word: λατρείας

Etymology:
The word λατρείας (latreias) is derived from the Greek word λατρεία (latreia), which means "worship" or "service." It is derived from the verb λατρεύω (latreuō), which means "to serve" or "to worship."

Grammar:
λατρείας is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of worship" or "of service."

Description:
λατρείας is a term used in Greek Orthodox Christianity to refer to the act of worship or service to God. It is often used in the context of liturgical worship, such as the Divine Liturgy, where the faithful offer their worship and service to God through prayer, hymns, and the partaking of the Eucharist. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to any act of service or devotion to God, such as acts of charity or personal prayer.
