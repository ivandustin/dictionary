Word: λατρευωμεν

Etymology: λατρευωμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λατρεύω (latreuo), which means "to serve" or "to worship". The suffix -μεν (-men) is added to indicate that the verb is in the present tense and is being performed by multiple people.

Grammar: λατρευωμεν is a present tense, active voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word λατρεύω (latreuo) and the present tense suffix -μεν (-men). The verb is in the first person plural form, meaning "we serve" or "we worship".

Description: λατρευωμεν is a verb that expresses the act of serving or worshiping. It is often used in a religious context to describe the act of worshiping a deity or performing religious rituals. The word emphasizes the idea of serving or worshiping with devotion and reverence.
