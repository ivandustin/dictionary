Word: λαμπει

Etymology: λαμπει comes from the Greek verb λάμπω (lampo), which means "to shine" or "to radiate light". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *lemp-, which means "to shine".

Grammar: λαμπει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb λάμπω (lampo), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: λαμπει is a Greek verb that means "it shines" or "it radiates light". It can be used to describe the shining of a light source, such as the sun or a lamp, or the radiance of a person or object. It is often used metaphorically to describe something that is bright, brilliant, or outstanding.
