Word: λαμβανων

Etymology: λαμβανων is the present participle of the Greek verb λαμβάνω (lamváno), which means "to take" or "to receive". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leb- which means "to take" or "to seize".

Grammar: λαμβανων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently taking or receiving something. In English, it can be translated as "taking" or "receiving".

Description: λαμβανων is a word that describes an action of taking or receiving something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Ο λαμβανων το δωρο ευχαριστηθηκε" (The one receiving the gift was pleased). The word is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday speech.
