Word: λαλοῦντα (lalounta)

Etymology: λαλοῦντα is the present active participle of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". 

Grammar: λαλοῦντα is in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender. It is used to describe a person who is speaking or talking.

Description: λαλοῦντα is a Greek word that describes someone who is currently speaking or talking. It can be used to refer to a specific person or to people in general. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe Jesus or his disciples as they preach and teach.
