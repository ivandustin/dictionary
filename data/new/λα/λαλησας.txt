Word: λαλησας

Etymology: λαλησας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk."

Grammar: λαλησας is the aorist tense of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "I spoke" or "I talked." It is in the second person singular form, meaning that it refers to the action of one person speaking or talking.

Description: λαλησας is a word that describes the action of speaking or talking in the past. It is often used in the context of storytelling or recounting events that have already happened. The word can also be used to describe the act of giving a speech or making a statement. In Greek literature, λαλησας is often used to convey a sense of authority or power, as it implies that the speaker has the ability to communicate effectively and persuasively.
