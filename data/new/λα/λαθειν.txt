Word: λαθειν

Etymology:
The word λαθειν (lathain) is derived from the Greek verb λανθάνω (lanthano), which means "to hide" or "to be concealed". The verb λαθειν is the present active infinitive form of λανθάνω.

Grammar:
λαθειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present active infinitive form, which means that it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of hiding or concealing.

Description:
λαθειν is a Greek verb that means "to hide" or "to be concealed". It is often used in the context of secrets or things that are not meant to be revealed. The verb can be used in various tenses and forms to express different aspects of the action of hiding or concealing. In Greek literature and mythology, the concept of hiding or concealing is often associated with deception, trickery, and the manipulation of truth.
