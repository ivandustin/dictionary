Word: εβαπτισθη

Etymology:
The word εβαπτισθη is a Greek word that comes from the verb βαπτίζω (baptizó), which means "to dip, to immerse, to wash". The word εβαπτισθη is the aorist passive indicative form of the verb, which means "he/she/it was baptized".

Grammar:
εβαπτισθη is a third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb βαπτίζω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
εβαπτισθη is a word that is commonly used in Christian contexts to refer to the act of baptism, which is a religious rite that involves the immersion of a person in water as a symbol of purification and initiation into the Christian faith. The word εβαπτισθη specifically refers to the past tense of this act, indicating that someone has already been baptized.
