Word: εβαλλον

Etymology: 
The word εβαλλον (eballon) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". The verb βάλλω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷʰel- which means "to throw, to hit".

Grammar:
εβαλλον is the imperfect tense, indicative mood, and active voice form of the verb βάλλω. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
εβαλλον can be translated to English as "I was throwing" or "I used to throw". It describes an ongoing or repeated action of throwing in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in sports, games, or everyday activities.
