Word: εβαλον

Etymology:
The word εβαλον (ebalon) is a past tense form of the Greek verb βάλλω (ballo), which means "to throw" or "to cast". The verb βάλλω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷʰel- which means "to throw, to hit".

Grammar:
εβαλον is the aorist tense form of βάλλω in the first person plural. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description:
εβαλον is a verb that describes an action of throwing or casting something. It is a past tense form, indicating that the action has already taken place. The context in which the word is used will determine what was thrown or cast. For example, it could be used to describe throwing a ball, casting a fishing line, or launching a missile.
