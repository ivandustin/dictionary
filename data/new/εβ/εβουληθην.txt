Word: εβουληθην

Etymology:
The word εβουληθην is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βουλή (boulé), which means "will" or "intention". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the first person singular aorist passive form of the verb, which means "I was advised" or "I was counseled".

Grammar:
εβουληθην is a first person singular aorist passive form of the Greek verb βουλεύω (bouleúō), which means "to advise" or "to counsel". The aorist tense indicates a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb.

Description:
εβουληθην can be translated as "I was advised" or "I was counseled". It is a verb that describes an action that was done to the subject, rather than an action that the subject did. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as receiving advice from a friend or being counseled by a professional.
