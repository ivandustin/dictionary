Word: εβλεπον

Etymology: εβλεπον is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βλέπω (blepo), which means "to see". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εβλεπον means "I saw" or "I was seeing".

Grammar: εβλεπον is a first-person singular past tense verb in Greek. It belongs to the imperfect tense, which is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εβλεπον (I saw)
εβλεπες (you saw)
εβλεπε (he/she/it saw)
εβλεπομεν (we saw)
εβλεπετε (you all saw)
εβλεπον (they saw)

Description: εβλεπον is a common verb in Greek and is used to describe past actions of seeing or observing. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a scene, recounting a memory, or expressing an opinion. The imperfect tense suggests that the action was ongoing or repeated in the past, rather than a one-time event.
