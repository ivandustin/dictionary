Word: εβαλεν

Etymology: εβαλεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast."

Grammar: εβαλεν is the aorist tense of the Greek verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "he/she/it threw" or "he/she/it cast."

Description: εβαλεν is a past tense verb that describes an action of throwing or casting that has already happened. It is commonly used in Greek literature and conversation to describe a variety of actions, such as throwing a ball, casting a fishing line, or launching a missile.
