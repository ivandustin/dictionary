Word: εβασιλευσατε

Etymology: 
The word εβασιλευσατε (ebasileusate) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βασιλεύω (basileuo), which means "to reign" or "to rule." The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the second person plural form, making the word mean "you all reign" or "you all rule."

Grammar:
εβασιλευσατε is a second person plural aorist active indicative verb in Greek. This means that it is used to address a group of people and is in the past tense. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any indication of the duration of the action. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
εβασιλευσατε is a command or exhortation to a group of people to reign or rule. It is often used in a religious or political context, where a leader or authority figure is calling on their followers to take charge and exercise their power. The word can also be used metaphorically, to describe taking control or being in charge of a situation.
