Word: εβουλετο

Etymology:
The word εβουλετο (ebouleto) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek verb βουλεύω (bouleuo), which means "to deliberate, to plan, to counsel". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the aorist tense, which refers to a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
εβουλετο (ebouleto) is a third-person singular form of the aorist tense in the middle voice. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it could be inferred from the context.

Description:
εβουλετο (ebouleto) can be translated as "he/she/it deliberated, planned, counseled". The verb implies a process of careful consideration and decision-making. It could be used in various contexts, such as politics, military strategy, or personal decision-making.
