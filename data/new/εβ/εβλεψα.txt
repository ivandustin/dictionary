Word: εβλεψα

Etymology: The word εβλεψα (eblepsa) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb βλέπω (blepo), which means "to see" or "to look". The verb βλέπω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰel- ("to shine, flash, gleam").

Grammar: εβλεψα is the first person singular past tense form of the verb βλέπω (blepo) in the aorist tense. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating the duration or repetition of the action.

Description: εβλεψα (eblepsa) means "I saw" or "I looked" in Greek. It is a specific instance of seeing or looking, and it implies that the action is completed. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a past event or narrating a story.
