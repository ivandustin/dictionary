Word: εβλασφημησαν

The word εβλασφημησαν is derived from the Greek word βλασφημέω (blasphemeo), which means to speak evil of, to slander, or to revile. The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate that the action was done in the past tense.

Grammar:
εβλασφημησαν is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is conjugated from the verb βλασφημέω (blasphemeo), which is a regular verb in the Greek language.

Description:
The word εβλασφημησαν is used to describe an action of blasphemy or slander. It is often used in religious contexts to describe the act of speaking against God or sacred things. In modern usage, it can also refer to speaking ill of someone or something in a disrespectful or offensive manner.
