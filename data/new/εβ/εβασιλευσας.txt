Word: εβασιλευσας

Etymology: 
The word εβασιλευσας (ebasileusas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βασιλεύω (basileuo), which means "to reign" or "to rule." The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar:
εβασιλευσας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. This means that it is used to describe a completed action in the past, and it is directed towards the second person (you). The active voice indicates that the subject (you) is performing the action of reigning or ruling.

Description:
εβασιλευσας can be translated to English as "you reigned" or "you ruled." It is often used in the context of describing a king or ruler who has completed their reign. The word carries a sense of authority and power, as it describes someone who has held a position of leadership and control.
