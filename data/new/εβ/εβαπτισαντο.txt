Word: εβαπτισαντο

Etymology:
The word εβαπτισαντο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βαπτίζω (baptizo), which means "to dip, to immerse, to wash." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the third person plural form of the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
εβαπτισαντο is a third person plural form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is a passive voice verb, which means that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it is implied that they were baptized by someone or something else.

Description:
εβαπτισαντο can be translated to English as "they were baptized." The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the baptism of Jesus by John the Baptist in the Jordan River. The act of baptism is a symbolic representation of a person's spiritual rebirth and commitment to their faith. In Greek Orthodox tradition, baptism is one of the seven sacraments and is considered a necessary step in a person's journey towards salvation.
