The word εβληθη is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a passive voice, so εβληθη means "was thrown" or "was cast".

εβληθη is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb.

εβληθη is a verb that describes an action of being thrown or cast upon.
