Word: εχορτασθησαν

Etymology: 
The word εχορτασθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word χορταζω (chortazo), which means "to feed" or "to satisfy". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a completed action, so εχορτασθησαν (echortasthesan) means "they were satisfied" or "they had their fill".

Grammar:
εχορτασθησαν is a third-person plural passive aorist verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The third-person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
εχορτασθησαν is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Matthew (14:20) and the Gospel of Mark (6:42). In both instances, it is used to describe the feeding of a large group of people with a small amount of food. The word conveys the idea that the people were completely satisfied and had enough to eat.
