The word "εχαιρον" (echairon) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the imperfect tense. It is derived from the root word "χαιρω" (chairō), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad". 

In its imperfect form, "εχαιρον" indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past. It can be translated to English as "I was rejoicing" or "I used to rejoice". 

In terms of grammar, "εχαιρον" is conjugated in the first person singular, which means that it refers to the speaker or writer of the sentence. 

Overall, the word "εχαιρον" conveys a sense of joy or happiness in the past, and is often used in religious or spiritual contexts.
