Word: εχρησαμην

Etymology: εχρησαμην is a Greek verb that comes from the root word χράομαι (chráomai), which means "to use" or "to make use of." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the first person singular aorist middle voice, which means "I used myself."

Grammar: εχρησαμην is a first person singular aorist middle voice verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action.

Description: εχρησαμην can be translated to English as "I used myself" or "I made use of myself." It implies that the subject actively used their own abilities or resources to accomplish something. This verb is often used in a positive sense, indicating that the subject was resourceful and effective in achieving their goals.
