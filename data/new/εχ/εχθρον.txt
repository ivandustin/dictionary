Word: εχθρον

Etymology:
The word εχθρον (echthron) comes from the Greek root word εχθρός (echthros), which means "enemy" or "hostile". It is derived from the verb εχθίζω (echthizo), which means "to hate" or "to be hostile towards".

Grammar:
εχθρον is a noun in the singular form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form of εχθρον is εχθρού (echthrou), the dative form is εχθρώ (echthro), and the accusative form is εχθρόν (echthron).

Description:
εχθρον refers to an enemy or a hostile person. It can also be used to describe something that is harmful or detrimental. In Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was often referred to as the "enemy of Ares" (εχθρός του Άρη) because she represented wisdom and strategy, while Ares represented war and violence. The word εχθρον is still commonly used in modern Greek to refer to an enemy or adversary.
