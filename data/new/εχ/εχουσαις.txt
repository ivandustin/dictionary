The word "εχουσαις" (echousais) is a verb form in the Greek language. It is the second person singular, present tense, indicative mood, and active voice of the verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold". 

Etymology: The verb "έχω" (echo) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *seǵh-, which means "to hold" or "to have". 

Grammar: As mentioned above, "εχουσαις" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "έχω" (echo). The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact or reality. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. 

Description: "εχουσαις" can be translated to "you have" or "you hold" in English. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and writing in Greek.
