Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "echthros" which means "enemy" or "foe". 

Grammar: "Εχθραν" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Description: The word is often used to describe a state of animosity or hostility between individuals or groups. It can also refer to a feeling of hatred or ill-will towards someone or something.
