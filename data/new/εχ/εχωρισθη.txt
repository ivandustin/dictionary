The word εχωρισθη is derived from the Greek verb χωρίζω (chorizo), which means "to separate" or "to divide". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, and the suffix -θη (-the) indicates the passive voice.

εχωρισθη is a third-person singular past passive indicative form of the verb χωρίζω (chorizo). It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. Description:
εχωρισθη can be translated as "was separated" or "was divided". It is often used in a religious context to describe a separation or division between people or groups.
