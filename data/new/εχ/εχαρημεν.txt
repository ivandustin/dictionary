The word "εχαρημεν" is a past participle form of the Greek verb "χαίρω" (kháiro), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad". It is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels and the Epistles. 

In terms of grammar, "εχαρημεν" is in the perfect tense and passive voice, indicating that the action of rejoicing has been completed in the past and the subject has received the action rather than performed it. 

Overall, "εχαρημεν" can be translated to mean "I have been made glad" or "I have rejoiced".
