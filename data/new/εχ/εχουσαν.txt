Word: εχουσαν

Etymology: The word εχουσαν is derived from the Greek verb έχω (echo), which means "to have" or "to possess". It is conjugated in the third person plural of the past tense.

Grammar: εχουσαν is a verb in the past tense, specifically the imperfect tense. It is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past. In Greek, the imperfect tense is formed by adding the appropriate endings to the stem of the verb.

Description: εχουσαν is a Greek verb that means "they had" or "they were having". It is commonly used in narratives to describe a past event or situation. For example, "Εχουσαν μια ωραία σπίτι στην παραλία" (They had a beautiful house by the beach).
