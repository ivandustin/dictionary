Word: εχωσιν

Etymology: The word εχωσιν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb εχω (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar: εχωσιν is the third person plural present active subjunctive form of the verb εχω (echo). In Greek grammar, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, possibility, or hypothetical situations.

Description: εχωσιν is a verb form that indicates that a group of people (third person plural) may or may not have something. It is often used in conditional or hypothetical statements, such as "If they have the money, they can buy the car" (Αν εχωσιν τα χρηματα, μπορουν να αγορασουν το αυτοκινητο).
