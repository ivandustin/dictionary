Word: εχουσα

Etymology: The word εχουσα is derived from the Greek verb έχω (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold". The suffix -ούσα (-ousa) is a feminine participle ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to a female subject.

Grammar: εχουσα is a present participle in the feminine singular form. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently holding or possessing something. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix -ούσα to the stem of the verb.

Description: εχουσα can be translated to English as "having" or "holding". It is often used in Greek to describe a woman who is currently in possession of something, such as an object or a quality. For example, "Η γυναίκα εχουσα ένα βιβλίο" (The woman having a book) or "Η κοπέλα εχουσα όμορφα μάτια" (The girl having beautiful eyes).
