Word: εχοντας

Etymology: εχοντας is the present participle of the Greek verb έχω (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold". It is derived from the ancient Greek verb ἔχω (échō).

Grammar: εχοντας is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe a person or thing that is currently having or holding something.

Description: εχοντας can be translated to English as "having" or "holding". It is often used in Greek to describe a person or thing that possesses or holds something at the present moment. For example, "Εχοντας μια κούπα καφέ" (Echo̱ntas mia koúpa kafé) means "Having a cup of coffee".
