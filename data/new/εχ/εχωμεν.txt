Word: εχωμεν

Etymology: The word εχωμεν is derived from the Greek verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold". The suffix "-μεν" is added to the verb stem to indicate the first person plural present tense.

Grammar: εχωμεν is a first person plural present tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb "έχω" (echo) and is used to indicate that "we have" or "we hold" something.

Description: εχωμεν is a Greek verb that is commonly used in everyday conversation. It is often used to express possession or ownership of something. For example, "Εχουμεν ένα σπίτι" (echoumen ena spiti) means "We have a house". It can also be used to express the idea of holding onto something, such as "Εχουμεν την ελπίδα" (echoumen tin elpida) which means "We hold onto hope".
