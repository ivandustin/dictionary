Word: εχοντος

Etymology: εχοντος is the genitive form of the Greek verb "εχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *seǵh-, which means "to hold" or "to have".

Grammar: εχοντος is a masculine singular genitive form of the verb "εχω" (echo). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, εχοντος indicates that something belongs to or is related to a masculine singular subject that is performing the action of "εχω" (echo).

Description: εχοντος can be translated to English as "of having" or "of holding". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe possession or ownership of something. For example, "του εχοντος την δυναμην" (tou echontos tin dynamin) can be translated to "the power of the one who has it".
