Word: εχθρους

Etymology:
The word εχθρους (echthrous) is derived from the Greek word εχθρός (echthros), which means "enemy" or "hostile". It is a combination of the prefix εχ- (ech-), which means "out of" or "away from", and the noun θρόος (thros), which means "rush" or "attack".

Grammar:
εχθρους is a plural form of the adjective εχθρός (echthros), which means "hostile" or "enemy". It is in the masculine gender and accusative case. 

Description:
The word εχθρους is used to describe a group of people who are considered enemies or hostile towards someone or something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as in a military context, or in a figurative sense, such as in a personal or political conflict. The word carries a negative connotation and implies a sense of danger or threat.
