Word: εχουσι

Etymology: εχουσι is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "έχω" (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar: εχουσι is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person. The present tense is used to describe actions that are happening now or are ongoing.

Description: εχουσι is a verb that indicates possession or ownership. It is often used in sentences to describe what someone has or holds. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου έχουν ένα σπίτι στην παραλία" (My friends have a house on the beach). It can also be used in a more abstract sense, such as "Οι σκέψεις μου έχουν αλλάξει" (My thoughts have changed).
