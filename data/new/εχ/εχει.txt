Word: εχει

Etymology: The word εχει comes from the Greek verb έχω (echo), which means "to have" or "to hold".

Grammar: εχει is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb έχω (echo). It is used to indicate that someone or something has or holds something at the present moment. 

Description: εχει is a common Greek word that is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to describe possession or ownership of something. For example, "Ο Πέτρος έχει ένα αυτοκίνητο" (O Petros echei ena aftokinito) means "Peter has a car". It can also be used to describe physical or emotional states, such as "Η Μαρία εχει πονοκέφαλο" (I Maria echei ponokefalo) which means "Maria has a headache".
