Word: βουλόμενος

Etymology:
βουλόμενος comes from the Greek verb βούλομαι (boulomai), which means "to wish" or "to want". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending, which means that βουλόμενος is a present participle of βούλομαι.

Grammar:
βουλόμενος is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb βούλομαι, which is in the present tense, and the participle ending -μενος. As a participle, βουλόμενος can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description:
βουλόμενος can be translated as "wishing", "wanting", or "desiring". It is often used to describe someone who has a strong desire or intention to do something. For example, in the sentence "ὁ βουλόμενος πορεύεται" (ho boulomenos poreuetai), which means "he who wishes to go", βουλόμενος describes someone who has a strong desire or intention to go somewhere.
