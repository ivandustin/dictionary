Word: βουλομενους

Etymology:
βουλομενους is a Greek word that comes from the verb βούλομαι (boulomai), which means "to will" or "to wish". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the present tense and active voice.

Grammar:
βουλομενους is the present participle form of the verb βούλομαι (boulomai), which means "willing" or "wanting". It is in the masculine, plural, accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb or preposition.

Description:
βουλομενους can be translated as "those who are willing" or "those who want". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe people who are willing to do something, such as follow Jesus or serve God. The word implies a sense of determination and purpose, as those who are willing are actively seeking to accomplish something.
