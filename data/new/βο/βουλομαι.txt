Word: βουλομαι

Etymology:
The word βουλομαι (boulomai) is a Greek verb that means "to wish" or "to want". It comes from the Greek noun βουλή (boulē), which means "will" or "purpose".

Grammar:
βουλομαι is a middle deponent verb in Greek, which means that it has a passive form but an active meaning. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

Singular:
- Βούλομαι (boulomai) - I wish
- Βούλεσαι (boulesai) - You wish
- Βούλεται (bouletai) - He/she/it wishes

Plural:
- Βουλόμαστε (boulomaste) - We wish
- Βουλεσθε (boulesthe) - You wish
- Βούλονται (boulontai) - They wish

Description:
βουλομαι is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can express a desire or a wish, as in "I wish I could go to Greece". It can also express a willingness or a readiness to do something, as in "I am willing to help you with your project". Additionally, it can express a decision or an intention, as in "I have decided to study Greek". Overall, βουλομαι is a useful verb that can help convey a range of meanings in Greek.
