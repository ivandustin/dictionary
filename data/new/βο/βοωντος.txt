Word: βοωντος

Etymology: βοωντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb βοάω (boao), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". 

Grammar: βοωντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns. 

Description: βοωντος can be translated as "of the one who shouts" or "of the one who cries out". It is often used in Greek mythology to describe characters who are known for their loud voices or who are associated with shouting or crying out.
