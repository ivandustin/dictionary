Word: βουληται

Etymology:
βουληται is a verb form in the Ancient Greek language, specifically in the present middle or passive voice, third person singular. It comes from the root word βουλή (boulḗ), which means "will" or "wish". 

Grammar:
βουληται is a present middle or passive voice, third person singular form of the verb βούλομαι (boúlomai), which means "to will" or "to wish". In this form, it can be translated as "he/she/it is willing" or "he/she/it is being willed". 

Description:
βουληται is a verb form that expresses the idea of someone or something having a will or desire. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as expressing someone's willingness to do something or the fact that something is being willed or desired by someone or something else. In Ancient Greek literature, this verb form is often used to describe the actions and motivations of gods and other supernatural beings.
