Word: βοωντων

Etymology: βοωντων is the present participle of the Greek verb βοάω (boáō), which means "to shout" or "to cry out". The word comes from the root βοή (boé), which means "a shout" or "a cry".

Grammar: βοωντων is a masculine plural present participle in the genitive case. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently shouting or crying out.

Description: βοωντων can be translated as "of those who are shouting" or "of those who are crying out". It is often used in the context of battle or protest, where a group of people are raising their voices to be heard. In Greek literature, the word is used to describe the sound of animals, such as the roar of a lion or the bellow of a bull.
