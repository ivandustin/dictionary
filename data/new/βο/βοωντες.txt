The word βοωντες (boontes) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb βοάω (boao), which means "to shout" or "to cry out." The participle form indicates that the action of shouting is ongoing or continuous. It can be translated as "shouting" or "crying out." In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the battle cries of warriors or the shouts of crowds.
