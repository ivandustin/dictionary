Word: βοηθος

Etymology:
The word βοηθος (boethos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of two words, βοη (boe) meaning "help" and θος (thos) meaning "swift". Therefore, the literal meaning of βοηθος is "swift help".

Grammar:
In Greek, βοηθος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases such as genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative. The plural form of βοηθος is βοηθοι (boethoi).

Description:
βοηθος is a term used to describe someone who provides assistance or help. In ancient Greece, βοηθος was used to refer to a helper or assistant in various fields such as agriculture, military, and politics. In modern times, the term βοηθος is still used in Greek to refer to an assistant or helper in various professions such as healthcare, education, and administration.
