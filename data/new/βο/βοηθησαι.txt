Word: βοηθησαι

Etymology: βοηθησαι is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: βοή (boé) meaning "shout" or "cry" and θέω (théo) meaning "to run". Therefore, βοηθησαι literally means "to run to the cry" or "to come to the aid".

Grammar: βοηθησαι is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the Greek verb βοηθέω (boétheo), which means "to help" or "to assist". It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows a regular pattern of inflection.

Description: βοηθησαι is a verb that expresses the action of helping or assisting someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as in everyday conversations, formal speeches, or written texts. Its meaning is closely related to the concept of support, aid, or assistance, and it implies a willingness to lend a hand to someone in need.
