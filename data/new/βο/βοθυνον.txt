The word βοθυνον (bothynon) is a Greek noun that refers to a pit or a hole in the ground. It is derived from the verb βόθυνεω (bothyneō), which means to dig a pit or to excavate. In terms of grammar, βοθυνον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It can be declined in various cases, such as βοθυνου (bothynou) in the genitive case, βοθυνῷ (bothynō) in the dative case, and βοθυνον (bothynon) in the accusative case.
