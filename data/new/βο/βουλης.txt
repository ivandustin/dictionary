Word: βουλης

Etymology:
The word βουλης (boulēs) comes from the Greek verb βούλομαι (boulomai), which means "to will" or "to wish". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bheu-, which means "to be, exist, grow".

Description:
βουλης is a Greek word that means "will" or "wish". It is often used in the context of decision-making, as in the phrase βουλη και διανοια (boulē kai dianoia), which means "will and understanding". In ancient Greece, the βουλη (boulē) was a council of citizens who advised the city-state's leaders on matters of policy and governance. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a council or assembly.
