Etymology: The word "αντι" (anti) comes from the Greek preposition meaning "against" or "opposite". 

Grammar: "Αντι" is a preposition in Greek. It is used to indicate opposition, contrast, or substitution. It is often followed by the genitive case.

Description: "Αντι" is a versatile preposition that can be used in various contexts. It can indicate opposition or contrast between two things, such as "αντίθεση" (opposition) or "αντίθετος" (opposite). It can also indicate substitution or replacement, as in "αντικαθιστώ" (replace) or "αντικαταστατικός" (substitute). Overall, "αντι" is a common and important preposition in the Greek language.
