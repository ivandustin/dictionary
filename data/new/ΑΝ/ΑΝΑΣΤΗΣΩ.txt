Etymology: The word "αναστησω" (anastiso) comes from the Greek verb "αναστηση" (anastisi), which means "to raise up" or "to resurrect." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again," and the verb "στηση" (stisi), meaning "to stand."

Grammar: "Αναστησω" is the first person singular form of the future tense of the Greek verb "αναστηση" (anastisi). It is used to express an action that will happen in the future, specifically when the subject "I" will raise up or resurrect something or someone.

Description: "Αναστησω" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of raising up or resurrecting. It can be used in various contexts, such as referring to the physical act of lifting something or someone, or metaphorically, to indicate the revival or restoration of something.
