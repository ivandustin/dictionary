Etymology: The word "αναγκαίος" (anankaíos) comes from the Greek root "αναγκή" (anankí), meaning necessity or compulsion.

Grammar: In Greek, "αναγκαίος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter.

Description: "Αναγκαίος" is translated as "necessary" or "essential" in English. It is used to describe something that is required, indispensable, or unavoidable. It can also refer to something that is urgent or pressing.
