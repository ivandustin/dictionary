Etymology: The word "ανακλιναι" comes from the Greek verb "ανακλίνω" (anaklíno), which means "to lean back" or "to recline." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "back" or "again," and the verb "κλίνω" (klíno), meaning "to lean" or "to incline."

Grammar: "Ανακλιναι" is the third person singular form of the verb "ανακλίνω" in the present tense. It is used to describe an action performed by a singular subject.

Description: "Ανακλιναι" is a Greek verb that describes the act of leaning back or reclining. It can be used in various contexts, such as reclining on a chair or bed, leaning back in a relaxed position, or tilting something backward.
