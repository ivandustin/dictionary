Etymology: The word "αναίτιοι" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "αν-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the adjective "αίτιοι" (meaning "causal" or "responsible"). 

Grammar: "Αναίτιοι" is the plural form of the adjective "αναίτιος" in Greek. It is used to describe multiple entities or individuals who are without cause or responsibility for a certain action or event. 

Description: "Αναίτιοι" refers to a group of people or things that are not responsible or accountable for a particular action or event. It implies that they are innocent or blameless in relation to the matter being discussed.
