Etymology: The word "ανοητους" (anoitos) comes from the Greek adjective "ανόητος" (anoitos), which means "foolish" or "senseless". It is derived from the negative prefix "αν-" (an-) and the adjective "νόητος" (noitos), meaning "intelligent" or "perceptive".

Grammar: "Ανοητους" is the plural form of the adjective "ανόητος" (anoitos) in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ανοητους" is used to describe multiple individuals who are foolish, lacking intelligence, or lacking common sense. It is often used to criticize or mock someone's actions or behavior.
