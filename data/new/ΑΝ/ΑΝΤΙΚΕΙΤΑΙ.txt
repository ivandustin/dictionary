Etymology: The word "αντικείμενο" (antikeímeno) comes from the Greek roots "αντί" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "κείμενο" (keímeno), meaning "that which is placed" or "that which is set." 

Grammar: "Αντικείμενο" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. 

Description: "Αντικείμενο" is a Greek word that translates to "object" or "thing" in English. It refers to something that is perceived or observed and can be the subject of an action or thought. In philosophy, it is often used to describe the object of knowledge or perception.
