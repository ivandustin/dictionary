Etymology: The word "αναγκαζεις" (anagkazeis) comes from the Greek verb "αναγκάζω" (anagkazo), which means "to force" or "to compel". It is derived from the noun "ανάγκη" (anagki), meaning "necessity" or "constraint".

Grammar: "Αναγκάζεις" is the second person singular form of the verb "αναγκάζω" (anagkazo) in the present tense. It is used when addressing someone directly and means "you force" or "you compel".

Description: "Αναγκάζεις" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of forcing or compelling someone to do something against their will. It implies a sense of coercion or necessity.
