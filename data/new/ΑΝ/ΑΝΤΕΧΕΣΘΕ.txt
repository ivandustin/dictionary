Grammar: "Αντεχεσθε" is the second person plural present middle/passive imperative form of the verb "αντέχομαι" (antéchomai). In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.
