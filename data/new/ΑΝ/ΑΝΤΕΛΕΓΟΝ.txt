Etymology: The word "αντελεγον" comes from the Greek roots "αντί" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "λέγω" (lego), meaning "to speak" or "to say." 

Grammar: "Αντελεγον" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: "Αντελεγον" means "to contradict" or "to argue against" in Greek. It refers to the act of expressing disagreement or opposing someone's statement or argument.
