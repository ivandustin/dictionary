Etymology: The word "ανομους" (anomous) comes from the Greek word "ανομος" (anomos), which means "lawless" or "without law". It is derived from the prefix "αν-" (a-) meaning "without" and the noun "νομος" (nomos) meaning "law".

Grammar: "Ανομους" is the plural form of the adjective "ανομος" (anomos) in Greek. It is used to describe multiple things or people that are lawless or without law.

Description: The word "ανομους" is used to describe things or people that do not conform to or follow established laws or rules. It can refer to actions, behaviors, or situations that are considered illegal, immoral, or against societal norms.
