Etymology: The word "ανθρωπινης" (anthropines) comes from the Greek word "ανθρωπος" (anthropos), meaning "human" or "man", and the suffix "-ινης" (-ines), which denotes belonging or relating to something.

Grammar: "Ανθρωπινης" (anthropines) is the feminine form of the adjective "ανθρωπινος" (anthropinos), which means "human" or "human-related". It is used to describe something that is characteristic of or pertaining to humans.

Description: "Ανθρωπινης" (anthropines) is an adjective that describes something as being human or related to humans. It can be used to describe human qualities, characteristics, behaviors, or anything that is associated with humanity.
