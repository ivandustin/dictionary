Etymology: The word "αντιχριστοι" (antichristoi) comes from the Greek words "αντι" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "Χριστός" (Christos), meaning "Christ." It refers to those who are opposed to or against Christ.

Grammar: "Αντιχριστοι" is a plural noun in Greek. It is the plural form of "αντιχριστος" (antichristos), which means "antichrist" in English.

Description: The term "αντιχριστοι" is used in Christian theology to refer to individuals or forces that are seen as opposing or working against Christ and his teachings. It is often associated with the concept of a figure or figures who will appear in the end times to deceive and lead people away from the true faith. The concept of the antichrist has been interpreted differently throughout history and in different religious traditions.
