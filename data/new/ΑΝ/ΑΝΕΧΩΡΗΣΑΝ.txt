Etymology: The word "ανεχώρησαν" (anechóṟisan) is derived from the Greek verb "ανεχωρώ" (anechóro̱), which means "to withdraw" or "to retreat". It is formed by combining the prefix "αν-" (an-), meaning "not" or "away", with the verb "εχωρώ" (echóro̱), meaning "to make room" or "to give way".

Grammar: "Ανεχώρησαν" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "ανεχωρώ" (anechóro̱). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, and person. The past tense indicates that the action of withdrawing or retreating happened in the past, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: "Ανεχώρησαν" is a Greek verb that means "they withdrew" or "they retreated". It describes the action of a group of people or things moving away or leaving a particular place. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing the retreat of an army, the withdrawal of a group of individuals, or the departure of objects from a location.
