Etymology: The word "αναιρείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "αναιρέω" (anaireo), which means "to take away" or "to remove". 

Grammar: "Αναιρείσθαι" is the present passive infinitive form of the verb "αναιρέω". In Greek grammar, the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon or affected by the action.

Description: "Αναιρείσθαι" is a verb in the Greek language that means "to be removed" or "to be taken away". It is used to express the action of something being eliminated, cancelled, or revoked.
