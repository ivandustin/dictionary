Etymology: The word "αντεστην" (antestin) comes from the Greek verb "αντιστέκομαι" (antistekomai), which means "to resist" or "to oppose". 

Grammar: "Αντεστην" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. It is conjugated in the first person singular form. 

Description: "Αντεστην" is used to express the action of resisting or opposing something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as resisting an idea, a person, or a situation.
