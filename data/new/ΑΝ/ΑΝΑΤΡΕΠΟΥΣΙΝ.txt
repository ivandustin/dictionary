Etymology: The word "ανατρεπούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "ανατρέπω" (anatrépo), which means "to overturn" or "to upset." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again," and the verb "τρέπω" (trépo), meaning "to turn."

Grammar: "Ανατρεπούσιν" is the present participle form of the verb "ανατρέπω" (anatrépo) in the third person plural. It is used to describe an ongoing action of overturning or upsetting.

Description: "Ανατρεπούσιν" is a Greek word that describes the act of overturning or upsetting. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a political or social upheaval, a disruption of plans or expectations, or a physical overturning of objects.
