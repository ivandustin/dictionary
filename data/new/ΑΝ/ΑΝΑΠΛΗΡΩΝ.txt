Etymology: The word "αναπληρών" comes from the Greek verb "αναπληρόω" (anapleróo), which means "to replenish" or "to fill up again". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "re-", and the verb "πληρόω" (pleróo), meaning "to fill" or "to complete".

Grammar: "Αναπληρών" is the present participle form of the verb "αναπληρόω" (anapleróo) in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action of replenishing or filling up again. In Greek grammar, the present participle is formed by adding the suffix "-ντ-" (-nt-) to the verb stem.
