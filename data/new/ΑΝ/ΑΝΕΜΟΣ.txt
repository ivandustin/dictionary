Etymology: The word "ανεμος" (anemos) comes from the Ancient Greek word ἄνεμος (ánemos), meaning "wind".

Grammar: In Greek, "ανεμος" (anemos) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "ανεμος" (anemos), the genitive singular form "ανεμου" (anemou), and the accusative singular form "ανεμο" (anemo).

Description: "Ανεμος" (anemos) means "wind" in Greek. It refers to the natural movement of air in the atmosphere. The wind can vary in intensity and direction and plays an important role in weather patterns and climate.
