Etymology: The word "αναιρουμένων" comes from the Greek verb "αναιρώ" (anaíro), which means "to cancel" or "to annul". The suffix "-μένων" (-menon) is added to the verb stem to indicate the present participle form.

Grammar: "Αναιρουμένων" is the present participle form of the verb "αναιρώ" in the plural masculine genitive case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The plural masculine genitive case indicates possession or association with a group of males.

Description: "Αναιρουμένων" can be translated as "of the ones being canceled" or "of the ones being annulled" in English. It refers to a group of males who are currently undergoing the action of being canceled or annulled.
