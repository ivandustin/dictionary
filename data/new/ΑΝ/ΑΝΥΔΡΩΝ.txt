Etymology: The word "ανυδρων" (anudron) comes from the Greek roots "αν" (an), meaning "without," and "υδρων" (udron), meaning "water." 

Grammar: "Ανυδρων" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. 

Description: "Ανυδρων" is a Greek word that translates to "waterless" or "without water" in English. It is often used to describe a place or a region that lacks water or is arid.
