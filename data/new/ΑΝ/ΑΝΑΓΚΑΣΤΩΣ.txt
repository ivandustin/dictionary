Etymology: The word "αναγκαστως" (anagkastos) comes from the Greek root "αναγκη" (anagkē), meaning "necessity" or "compulsion."

Grammar: "Αναγκαστως" is an adverb in the Greek language. It is the adverbial form of the adjective "αναγκαστος" (anagkastos), which means "forced" or "compelled."

Description: "Αναγκαστως" is used to describe an action or situation that is done out of necessity or compulsion. It implies that there is no other choice or option but to do something.
