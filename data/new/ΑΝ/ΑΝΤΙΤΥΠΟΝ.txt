Etymology: The word "αντιτυπον" comes from the Greek roots "αντι-" (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "τυπος" (typos), meaning "type" or "impression." 

Grammar: "Αντιτυπον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the neuter gender.

Description: "Αντιτυπον" refers to a counterpart or an opposite. It can be used to describe something that is the reverse or contrary of another thing.
