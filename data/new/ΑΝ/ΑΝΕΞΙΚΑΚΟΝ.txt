Etymology: The word "ανεξικακον" (anexikakon) comes from the Greek roots "ανεξί" (anexi), meaning "without" or "free from," and "κακόν" (kakon), meaning "evil" or "bad." Therefore, "ανεξικακον" can be translated as "without evil" or "free from bad."

Grammar: In Greek, "ανεξικακον" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ανεξικακον" is a word used to describe something or someone that is free from evil or bad qualities. It implies a state of being morally upright, virtuous, or without malice. It can be used to describe a person's character, behavior, or intentions.
