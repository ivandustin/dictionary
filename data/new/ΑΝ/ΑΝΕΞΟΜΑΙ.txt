Etymology: The word "ανεξομαι" (anexomai) comes from the Greek roots "αν-" (an-), meaning "not," and "εξομαι" (exomai), meaning "to yield" or "to give in." Therefore, "ανεξομαι" can be translated as "not yielding" or "not giving in."

Grammar: "Ανεξομαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and persons to express different meanings and nuances.

Description: "Ανεξομαι" is a strong and powerful word that conveys the idea of resistance, defiance, and refusal to yield or give in. It implies a strong determination and a refusal to be controlled or influenced by external forces. It can be used in various contexts, such as personal willpower, political resistance, or standing up against oppression.
