Etymology: The word "ανεμοι" (anemoi) comes from the Greek word "ἄνεμος" (ánemos), meaning "wind".

Grammar: "Ανεμοι" (anemoi) is a noun in the Greek language. It is the plural form of "ανεμος" (ánemos), which means "wind".

Description: "Ανεμοι" (anemoi) refers to the winds in Greek mythology. In ancient Greek religion and mythology, the anemoi were the gods of the winds, each associated with a specific direction. They were often depicted as winged men, each holding a different symbol to represent their respective wind. The anemoi played a significant role in Greek literature and were believed to have control over the weather and the seas.
