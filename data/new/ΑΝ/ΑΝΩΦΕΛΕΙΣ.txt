Etymology: The word "ανωφελεις" (anōpheleis) comes from the Greek roots "ανω" (anō), meaning "above" or "higher," and "φελειν" (phelein), meaning "to benefit" or "to profit." Therefore, the literal meaning of "ανωφελεις" is "above benefiting" or "not benefiting."

Grammar: "Ανωφελεις" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe nouns that are not beneficial or do not bring any advantage.

Description: "Ανωφελεις" is used to describe something or someone that is useless, futile, or unprofitable. It implies that the subject does not provide any benefit or advantage.
