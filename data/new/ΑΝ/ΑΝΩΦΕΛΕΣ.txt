Etymology: The word "ανωφελες" (anōpheles) comes from the Greek roots "ανω" (anō), meaning "above" or "higher," and "φελλω" (phellō), meaning "to benefit" or "to profit." Therefore, the literal meaning of "ανωφελες" is "above benefit" or "not beneficial."

Grammar: "Ανωφελες" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine, singular, and nominative case form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Ανωφελες" is used to describe something that is useless, pointless, or without any practical value. It indicates that the subject does not bring any benefit or advantage.
