Etymology: The word "αναστροφην" (anastrophen) comes from the Greek verb "αναστρέφω" (anastrefo), which means "to turn back" or "to reverse." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "back" or "again," and the verb "στρέφω" (strefo), meaning "to turn."

Grammar: "Αναστροφην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Αναστροφην" refers to the act of turning back or reversing. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a change in direction, a reversal of a decision or situation, or a return to a previous state.
