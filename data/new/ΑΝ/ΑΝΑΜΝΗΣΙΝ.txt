Etymology: The word "αναμνησιν" (anamnēsin) comes from the Greek verb "αναμιμνήσκω" (anamimnēskō), which means "to remember" or "to recall". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back", and the verb "μιμνήσκω" (mimnēskō), meaning "to remember".

Grammar: "Αναμνησιν" (anamnēsin) is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.
