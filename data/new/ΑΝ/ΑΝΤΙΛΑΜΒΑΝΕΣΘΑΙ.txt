Etymology: The word "αντιλαμβάνεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "αντιλαμβάνομαι" (antilambanomai), which is a compound word formed by the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and the verb "λαμβάνω" (lambano), meaning "to take" or "to receive."

Grammar: "Αντιλαμβάνεσθαι" is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: The word "αντιλαμβάνεσθαι" means "to understand" or "to comprehend" in Greek. It implies the act of grasping or perceiving something intellectually or mentally.
