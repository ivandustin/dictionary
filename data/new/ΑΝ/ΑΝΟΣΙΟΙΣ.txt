Etymology: The word "ανοσιος" (anósios) comes from the Greek prefix "αν-" (an-), meaning "not," and the adjective "οσιος" (osios), meaning "pious" or "holy." Therefore, "ανοσιος" can be translated as "impious" or "unholy."

Grammar: "Ανοσιος" is an adjective in the masculine form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, "ανοσιος" can be used to describe a masculine singular noun, such as "άνθρωπος" (ánthropos), meaning "man," as in "ο ανοσιος άνθρωπος" (o anósios ánthropos) - "the impious man."

Description: The word "ανοσιος" is used to describe someone or something that is not pious, lacking in religious devotion, or disrespectful towards religious beliefs or practices. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is morally corrupt or wicked.
