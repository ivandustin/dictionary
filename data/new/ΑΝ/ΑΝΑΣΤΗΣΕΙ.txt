Etymology: The word "αναστησει" (anastesei) comes from the Greek verb "ανασταίνω" (anastaino), which means "to raise up" or "to resurrect." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again," and the verb "σταίνω" (staino), meaning "to stand."

Grammar: "Αναστησει" is the third person singular future tense form of the verb "ανασταίνω" (anastaino). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "Αναστησει" is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it will raise up" or "he/she/it will resurrect" in English. It is commonly used in religious contexts to refer to the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
