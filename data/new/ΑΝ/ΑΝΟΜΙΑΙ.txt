Etymology: The word "ανομιαί" comes from the Greek word "ανομία" (anomia), which means "lawlessness" or "lack of law". It is derived from the prefix "αν-" (a negative prefix) and the noun "νόμος" (nomos), meaning "law".

Grammar: "Ανομιαί" is the plural form of the noun "ανομία" (anomia) in Greek. It is used to refer to multiple instances of lawlessness or lack of law.

Description: "Ανομιαί" is a term used to describe situations or conditions where there is a disregard for laws or a lack of legal order. It can refer to a state of lawlessness, chaos, or disorder.
