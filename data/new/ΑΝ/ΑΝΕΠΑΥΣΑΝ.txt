Etymology: The word "ανεπαυσαν" (anepausan) comes from the Greek verb "αναπαύω" (anapauo), which means "to rest" or "to take a break." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back," and the verb "παύω" (pauo), meaning "to stop" or "to cease."

Grammar: "Ανεπαυσαν" is the third person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "αναπαύω" (anapauo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action.

Description: "Ανεπαυσαν" is a Greek verb that means "they rested" or "they took a break." It describes a past action where a group of people or things stopped their activities and found rest or relaxation.
