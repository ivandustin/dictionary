Etymology: The word "αντιπας" (antipas) comes from the Greek words "αντι" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "πας" (pas), meaning "all" or "everyone." 

Grammar: "Αντιπας" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in the nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases.

Description: "Αντιπας" refers to someone who is against or opposed to everyone or everything. It can be used to describe a person who holds strong opposing views or acts in opposition to the majority.
