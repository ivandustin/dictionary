Etymology: The word "ανιεντες" comes from the Greek verb "ανίενται" (aníentai), which means "they go up" or "they ascend." It is derived from the prefix "αν-" (an-), meaning "up" or "on," and the verb "ιέναι" (iénai), meaning "to go."

Grammar: "Ανιεντες" is the present participle form of the verb "ανίενται" (aníentai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Ανιεντες" describes a group of people or objects that are currently going up or ascending. It implies a continuous action or state of ascent.
