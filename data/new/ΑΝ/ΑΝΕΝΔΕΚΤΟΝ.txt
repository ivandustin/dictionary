Etymology: The word "ανενδεκτον" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "αν-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "ενδεκτον" (meaning "acceptable" or "worthy").

Grammar: "ανενδεκτον" is a neuter adjective in the singular form. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, if used to describe a feminine or masculine noun, it would change accordingly.

Description: The word "ανενδεκτον" is used to describe something that is not acceptable or not worthy. It implies that the thing being described does not meet the required standards or criteria.
