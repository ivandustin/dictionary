Etymology: The word "αντιλεγόμενον" (antilegómenon) comes from the Greek roots "αντι-" (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "λέγω" (legó), meaning "to speak" or "to say." Therefore, "αντιλεγόμενον" can be translated as "that which is spoken against" or "that which is contradicted."

Grammar: "Αντιλεγόμενον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" in the nominative case.

Description: "Αντιλεγόμενον" refers to something that is being contradicted or disputed. It can be used to describe an idea, statement, or belief that is being challenged or argued against.
