Etymology: The word "αντιμετρηθησεται" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and the verb "μετρώ" (metró), meaning "to measure." 

Grammar: "αντιμετρηθησεται" is a verb in the future passive indicative form in Greek. It is the third person singular form of the verb "αντιμετρώ" (antimetró), which means "to be measured against" or "to be compared."

Description: "αντιμετρηθησεται" describes an action or state of being that will be measured against or compared to something else in the future. It implies a comparison or evaluation of two or more things.
