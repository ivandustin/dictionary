Etymology: The word "αναδεξαμενος" comes from the Greek verb "αναδέχομαι" (anadéchomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". The suffix "-μενος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice.

Grammar: "Αναδεξαμενος" is the masculine singular form of the participle, in the nominative case. It is used to describe a noun that receives or accepts something. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as predicates.

Description: "Αναδεξαμενος" describes someone or something that has been received or accepted. It implies a passive action of receiving or accepting, rather than actively doing something.
