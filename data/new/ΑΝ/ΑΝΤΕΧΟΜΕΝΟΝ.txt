Etymology: The word "αντεχομενον" comes from the Greek verb "αντεχομαι" (antechomai), which means "to endure" or "to withstand". The suffix "-μενον" (-menon) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and active voice.

Grammar: "Αντεχομενον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The neuter gender is used for objects or concepts that are neither masculine nor feminine. The singular form indicates that there is only one of the noun.

Description: "Αντεχομενον" can be translated as "endurance" or "resistance". It refers to the ability to withstand or endure something, whether it is physical, emotional, or mental. It can also imply perseverance or the ability to persist in the face of challenges or difficulties.
