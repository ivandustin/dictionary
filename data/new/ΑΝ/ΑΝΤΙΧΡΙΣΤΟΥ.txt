Etymology: The word "αντιχριστου" (antichristou) comes from the Greek words "αντι" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "Χριστού" (Christou), meaning "of Christ." 

Grammar: "Αντιχριστου" is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of the Antichrist" or "belonging to the Antichrist."

Description: "Αντιχριστου" refers to the Antichrist, a figure mentioned in Christian eschatology who is believed to oppose and mimic Christ. The concept of the Antichrist varies across different Christian denominations and interpretations. In general, the Antichrist is seen as a powerful and deceptive figure who will appear before the Second Coming of Christ.
