Etymology: The word "ανθρωπινη" (anthropini) comes from the Greek word "άνθρωπος" (anthropos), which means "human" or "person". The suffix "-ινη" (-ini) is added to indicate that something is related to or characteristic of humans.

Grammar: "Ανθρωπινη" (anthropini) is an adjective in the feminine form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. It can be used to describe something that is human, pertaining to humans, or characteristic of humans.
