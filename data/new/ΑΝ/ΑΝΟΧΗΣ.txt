Etymology: The word "ανοχής" (anochis) comes from the Greek verb "ανέχομαι" (anechomai), which means "to endure" or "to tolerate". 

Grammar: "Ανοχής" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Ανοχής" refers to the act of tolerating or enduring something. It can also be translated as "tolerance" or "forbearance". It is the ability to accept or allow something that is considered undesirable or unpleasant.
