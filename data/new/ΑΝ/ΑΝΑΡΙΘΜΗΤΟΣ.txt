Etymology: The word "αναριθμητος" (anarithmitos) comes from the Greek roots "ανα" (ana), meaning "without" or "not," and "αριθμητος" (arithmitos), meaning "countable" or "enumerable." Therefore, "αναριθμητος" can be translated as "uncountable" or "innumerable."

Grammar: In Greek, "αναριθμητος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Αναριθμητος" is used to describe something that cannot be counted or enumerated. It implies a large or indefinite quantity, suggesting that the thing being described is too numerous or vast to be accurately measured or counted.
