Etymology: The word "αναπολογητος" comes from the Greek roots "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "without" or "not," and "πολογητος" (pologetos), meaning "defensible" or "justifiable." 

Grammar: "Αναπολογητος" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αναπολογητος" is a Greek word that means "indefensible" or "unjustifiable." It is used to describe something that cannot be defended or justified due to its nature or actions.
