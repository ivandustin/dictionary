Etymology: The word "ανθρωπινον" (anthrōpinon) comes from the Greek word "ἄνθρωπος" (anthrōpos), which means "human" or "man."

Grammar: "Ανθρωπινον" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: "Ανθρωπινον" is used to describe something that is related to or characteristic of humans. It can be translated as "human" or "human-related."
