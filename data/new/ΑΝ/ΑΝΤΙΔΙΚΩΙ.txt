Etymology: The word "αντιδικοι" comes from the Greek words "αντι" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "δικοι" (dikoi), meaning "lawsuits" or "legal disputes." 

Grammar: "Αντιδικοι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is declined as follows: αντιδικοι (nominative), αντιδικων (genitive), αντιδικοις (dative), αντιδικους (accusative), αντιδικοι (vocative).

Description: "Αντιδικοι" refers to people who are involved in a legal dispute or lawsuit. It can be used to describe both the plaintiff and the defendant in a legal case.
