Etymology: The word "ανεωιξεν" (aneōixen) comes from the Greek verb "ανοίγω" (anoigō), which means "to open". It is formed by adding the prefix "αν-" (an-) meaning "up" or "open" and the verb "οίγω" (oigō) meaning "to open".

Grammar: "Ανεωιξεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "ανοίγω" (anoigō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Ανεωιξεν" means "he/she/it opened" in English. It refers to the action of opening something.
