Etymology: The word "αναστας" (anastas) comes from the Greek verb "αναστημι" (anastēmi), which means "to rise" or "to stand up". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again", and the verb "ιστημι" (histēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: "Αναστας" is the aorist participle form of the verb "αναστημι" (anastēmi). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The participle form indicates that the action of rising or standing up has been completed.

Description: "Αναστας" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having risen" or "having stood up". It is often used in religious or biblical contexts to refer to the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
