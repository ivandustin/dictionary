Etymology: The word "ανθρωπον" (anthropon) comes from the Greek word "ἄνθρωπος" (anthrōpos), which means "human being" or "person".

Grammar: "Ανθρωπον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ανθρωπον" refers to a human being or a person. It is a generic term used to describe individuals of the human species.
