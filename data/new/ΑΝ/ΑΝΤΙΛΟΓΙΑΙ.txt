Etymology: The word "αντιλογία" comes from the Greek roots "αντί" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "λογία" (logia), meaning "speech" or "discourse." Therefore, "αντιλογία" can be translated as "counter-argument" or "rebuttal."

Grammar: In Greek, "αντιλογία" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular in number and can be declined according to the case and gender of the sentence.

Description: "Αντιλογία" refers to the act of presenting arguments or evidence that contradict or oppose a particular statement or claim. It is commonly used in debates, discussions, or legal proceedings to challenge or refute an argument.
