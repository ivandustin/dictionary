Etymology: The word "αναγκην" (anagkēn) comes from the Greek word "αναγκη" (anagkē), which means "necessity" or "compulsion".

Grammar: "Αναγκην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence.

Description: "Αναγκην" refers to the concept of necessity or compulsion. It can be used to describe a situation or condition that is unavoidable or obligatory.
