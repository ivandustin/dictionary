Etymology: The word "ανεγκλητους" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "αν-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "εγκλητος" (meaning "accused" or "charged"). 

Grammar: "Ανεγκλητους" is the plural form of the adjective "ανεγκλητος" in Greek. It is used to describe multiple individuals who have not been accused or charged with a crime. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.
