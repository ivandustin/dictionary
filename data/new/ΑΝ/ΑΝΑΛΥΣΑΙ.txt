Etymology: The word "αναλυσαι" comes from the Greek verb "αναλύω" (analyo), which means "to analyze" or "to break down." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again," and the verb "λύω" (lyo), meaning "to loosen" or "to untie."

Grammar: "Αναλύσαι" is the second person singular aorist imperative form of the verb "αναλύω" (analyo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "Αναλύσαι" is a command form that translates to "analyze" or "break down" in English. It is used to instruct someone to carefully examine or dissect something in order to understand its components or structure.
