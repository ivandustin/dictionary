Etymology: The word "ανδριζεσθε" comes from the Greek verb "ανδρίζομαι" (andrizomai), which means "to act like a man" or "to be courageous." It is derived from the noun "άνδρας" (andras), meaning "man" or "male."

Grammar: "Ανδριζεσθε" is the second person plural form of the present middle/passive imperative of the verb "ανδρίζομαι." In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Ανδριζεσθε" is a command in Greek, specifically addressing a group of people. It can be translated as "Be courageous!" or "Act like men!" It is often used to encourage bravery, strength, or determination in the face of challenges.
