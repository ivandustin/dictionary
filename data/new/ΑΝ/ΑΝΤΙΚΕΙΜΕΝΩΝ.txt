Etymology: The word "αντικειμενων" (antikeimenon) comes from the Greek roots "αντι" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "κειμενον" (keimenon), meaning "that which is being discussed" or "that which is being talked about." 

Grammar: "Αντικειμενων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "αντικειμενο" (antikeimeno), which means "object" or "subject matter" in Greek. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Αντικειμενων" refers to multiple objects or subject matters. It is often used in philosophical or academic contexts to discuss the objects or topics under consideration.
