Etymology: The word "αντιπατρίδα" comes from the Greek words "αντί" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "πατρίδα" (patrida), meaning "homeland" or "country." 

Grammar: "Αντιπατρίδα" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used with definite and indefinite articles.

Description: "Αντιπατρίδα" refers to the feeling or sentiment of being against or opposed to one's homeland or country. It can be used to describe a person's negative or critical attitude towards their own nation, government, or cultural identity.
