Grammar: "Αναστησας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "αναστησαν." In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Αναστησας" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having raised up" or "having resurrected." It is often used in the context of the resurrection of Jesus Christ in Christian theology. The word emphasizes the act of raising someone or something up from a state of death or inactivity.
