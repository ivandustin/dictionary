Etymology: The word "αναζητησαι" (anazitisi) comes from the Greek verb "αναζητώ" (anazito), which means "to search" or "to seek". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back", and the verb "ζητώ" (zito), meaning "to seek" or "to look for".

Grammar: "Αναζητήσαι" is the aorist tense, second person singular form of the verb "αναζητώ" (anazito). The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. In this form, it is used when addressing someone directly and asking them to search or seek something.
