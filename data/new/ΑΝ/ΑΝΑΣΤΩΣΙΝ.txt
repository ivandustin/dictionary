Etymology: The word "αναστώσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αναστέλλω" (anastello), which means "to raise up" or "to stand up". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again", and the verb "στέλλω" (stello), meaning "to set" or "to place".

Grammar: "αναστώσιν" is a Greek noun in the accusative case. It is the plural form of the noun "ανάσταση" (anastasi), which means "resurrection" or "rising up". In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive.

Description: "αναστώσιν" refers to the act of resurrection or rising up. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe the resurrection of Jesus Christ or the general resurrection of the dead. The word carries a sense of renewal, rebirth, and triumph over death.
