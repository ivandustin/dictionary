Etymology: The word "αντλειν" (antlein) comes from the Greek verb "αντλέω" (antleo), which means "to draw out" or "to pump". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ent-.

Grammar: "Αντλείν" is the present tense form of the verb in the third person singular. It is used to describe the action of drawing out or pumping.

Description: "Αντλείν" is a Greek verb that refers to the act of drawing out or pumping a liquid, usually from a well, a container, or a source. It is commonly used in contexts related to water supply, irrigation, or fluid mechanics.
