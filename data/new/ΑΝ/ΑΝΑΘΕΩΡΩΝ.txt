Etymology: The word "αναθεωρων" comes from the Greek roots "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back," and "θεωρων" (theoron), meaning "to observe" or "to contemplate." 

Grammar: "Αναθεωρων" is a present participle in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb "αναθεωρεω" (anatheoreo), which means "to observe" or "to contemplate." The present participle form indicates that the action of observing or contemplating is ongoing or continuous.

Description: "Αναθεωρων" refers to someone who is observing or contemplating something repeatedly or continuously. It can also be translated as "one who constantly observes" or "a perpetual observer."
