Etymology: The word "ανθυπατος" (anthypatos) comes from the Greek roots "ανθρωπος" (anthropos) meaning "man" and "υπατος" (hypatos) meaning "highest" or "supreme." It originally referred to a high-ranking official in ancient Greece.

Grammar: "Ανθυπατος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Ανθυπατος" is an ancient Greek term that referred to a high-ranking official or magistrate. In the Roman Empire, it was used to denote a governor or deputy governor of a province. The term is no longer in use in modern Greek.
