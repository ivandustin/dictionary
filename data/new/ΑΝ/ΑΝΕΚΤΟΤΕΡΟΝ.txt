Etymology: The word "ανεκτοτερον" comes from the Greek word "ανεκτος" (anektos), meaning "tolerable" or "endurable", and the suffix "-τερον" (-teron), which is a comparative suffix indicating "more". 

Grammar: "Ανεκτοτερον" is an adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is used to describe something as being more tolerable or more endurable compared to something else.

Description: "Ανεκτοτερον" is a word that expresses the idea of something being more tolerable or more endurable. It is often used to compare the level of tolerance or endurance of different things or situations.
