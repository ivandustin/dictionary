Etymology: The word "ανθρακας" (anthrakas) comes from the Greek word "ανθραξ" (anthrax), which means "coal" or "charcoal". 

Grammar: "Ανθρακας" is a masculine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its genitive form is "ανθρακα" (anthraka). 

Description: "Ανθρακας" refers to coal, which is a black or brownish-black sedimentary rock that is primarily composed of carbon. It is formed from the remains of plants that lived and died millions of years ago. Coal is a valuable energy source and is used for various purposes, including electricity generation, heating, and industrial processes.
