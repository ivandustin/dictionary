Etymology: The word "ανθρωπινος" (anthropinos) comes from the Greek word "άνθρωπος" (anthropos), which means "human" or "man". The suffix "-ινος" (-inos) is added to indicate possession or belonging.

Grammar: "Ανθρωπινος" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, if the noun is feminine or plural, the adjective would change accordingly.

Description: "Ανθρωπινος" means "human" or "human-like" in Greek. It is used to describe something that is related to or characteristic of humans.
