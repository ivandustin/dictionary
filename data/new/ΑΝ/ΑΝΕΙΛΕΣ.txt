Etymology: The word "ανείλες" (aneiles) comes from the Greek verb "αναιρώ" (anairó), which means "to take away" or "to remove". 

Grammar: "Ανείλες" is the second person singular, aorist tense of the verb "αναιρώ" (anairó). It is used to address someone directly and refers to an action that was completed in the past.

Description: "Ανείλες" is a Greek word that means "you took away" or "you removed". It is used to express the act of taking something away or removing it from a certain place or person.
