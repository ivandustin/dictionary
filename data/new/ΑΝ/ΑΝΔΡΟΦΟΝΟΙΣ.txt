Etymology: The word "ανδροφονοις" comes from the Greek words "ανδρος" (andros) meaning "man" and "φονος" (phonos) meaning "murder". 

Grammar: "ανδροφονοις" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "ανδροφονοις" refers to individuals who commit murder or are involved in acts of killing men.
