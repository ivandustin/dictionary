Etymology: The word "αντιτυπα" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), meaning "against" or "opposite," and the noun "τυπος" (typos), meaning "type" or "pattern."

Grammar: "Αντιτυπα" is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the neuter gender and is declined according to the second declension. In the singular form, the word would be "αντιτυπο" (antitypo).

Description: "Αντιτυπα" refers to "copies" or "duplicates" in Greek. It is commonly used to describe multiple instances or reproductions of a particular document, book, or other printed material.
