Etymology: The word "αναπαυού" (anapavou) comes from the Greek verb "αναπαύω" (anapavo), which means "to rest" or "to relax". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back", and the verb "παύω" (pavo), meaning "to stop" or "to cease".

Grammar: "Αναπαυού" is the second person singular form of the verb "αναπαύω" (anapavo) in the present tense. It is used when addressing one person and means "you rest" or "you relax".

Description: "Αναπαυού" is a command or an imperative form of the verb "αναπαύω" (anapavo). It is used to instruct or encourage someone to take a break, rest, or relax.
