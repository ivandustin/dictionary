Etymology: The word "αναστρέφω" (anastrefo) comes from the Greek prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back," and the verb "στρέφω" (strefo), meaning "to turn." Therefore, "αναστρέφω" can be translated as "to turn back" or "to reverse."

Grammar: "Αναστρέφω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and persons to indicate different actions and states of turning back or reversing.

Description: "Αναστρέφω" is a versatile verb that can be used in different contexts. It can refer to physically turning back or reversing direction, as well as metaphorically reversing a decision, opinion, or situation. It can also imply a change in behavior or attitude. The exact meaning and usage of "αναστρέφω" depend on the context in which it is used.
