Etymology: The word "αντιλεγόντα" comes from the Greek words "αντί" (anti), meaning "against" or "opposite," and "λεγόμενα" (legomena), meaning "things said" or "utterances." 

Grammar: "Αντιλεγόντα" is a noun in the plural form. It is derived from the verb "αντιλέγω" (antilégo), which means "to contradict" or "to argue against." 

Description: "Αντιλεγόντα" refers to statements or arguments that contradict or oppose each other. It is often used in the context of debates, discussions, or conflicting opinions.
