Etymology: The word "ανορθωσω" (anorthoso) comes from the Greek verb "ανορθόω" (anortho), which means "to straighten" or "to correct." It is derived from the prefix "αν-" (an-), meaning "up" or "back," and the root "ορθός" (orthos), meaning "straight" or "correct."

Grammar: "Ανορθώσω" is the first person singular future tense of the verb "ανορθόω" (anortho). In Greek grammar, the future tense is used to express actions or states that will happen in the future.

Description: "Ανορθώσω" is a Greek word that translates to "I will straighten" or "I will correct" in English. It is used to indicate the action of making something straight or correcting a mistake or error.
