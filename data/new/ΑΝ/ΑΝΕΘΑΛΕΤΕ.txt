Etymology: The word "ανεθαλετε" comes from the Greek language. It is a verb derived from the combination of the prefix "αν-" (meaning "not") and the verb "εθαλετε" (meaning "to forget"). 

Grammar: "ανεθαλετε" is the second person plural, present tense, indicative mood form of the verb "εθαλετε". It is used to address a group of people and indicates an action that is happening in the present.

Description: "ανεθαλετε" means "you all do not forget" or "you all are not forgetting" in English. It is a command or a statement urging a group of people to remember something or not to forget something.
