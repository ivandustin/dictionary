Etymology: The word "αναίδειαν" comes from the Greek word "αναιδεία" (anaideia), which means shamelessness or impudence. It is derived from the prefix "αν-" (an-), meaning without, and the noun "αἰδώς" (aidos), which means shame or modesty.

Grammar: "αναίδειαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "αναίδειαν" refers to the state or quality of being shameless or impudent. It describes a lack of shame or modesty in one's behavior or actions.
