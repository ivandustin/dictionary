Etymology: The word "ανωμως" comes from the Greek word "ανωμα" (anoma), which means "irregular" or "abnormal". It is derived from the prefix "αν-" (an-), meaning "not", and the root "ωμος" (omos), meaning "regular" or "normal".

Grammar: "Ανωμως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or state that is done in an irregular or abnormal manner.

Description: "Ανωμως" is used to express the way something is done in a manner that deviates from the norm or is considered unusual. It implies that the action or state being described is not in accordance with the usual or expected way of doing things.
