Etymology: The word "αναστασα" comes from the Greek verb "ανασταίνω" (anastaino), which means "to rise" or "to stand up". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again", and the verb "σταίνω" (staino), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: "αναστασα" is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "ανασταίνω" (anastaino), which means "he/she/it rose" or "he/she/it stood up".

Description: "αναστασα" is a Greek word that describes the action of someone or something rising or standing up. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the physical act of getting up from a seated or lying position, or metaphorically referring to a person or thing becoming active or taking a stand.
