Etymology: The word "ανοιγειν" (anoigein) comes from the Greek verb "ανοίγω" (anoigo), which means "to open".

Grammar: "Ανοιγειν" is the present tense form of the verb "ανοίγω" (anoigo) in the Greek language. It is the third person singular form.

Description: "Ανοιγειν" is used to describe the action of opening something. It can refer to physically opening a door, a window, or any other object that can be opened. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of opening up or revealing something, such as opening up one's heart or mind.
