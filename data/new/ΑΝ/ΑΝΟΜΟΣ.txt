Etymology: The word "ανομος" (anomos) comes from the Greek roots "α-" (a-), meaning "without" or "not," and "νομος" (nomos), meaning "law." Therefore, "ανομος" (anomos) can be translated as "lawless" or "without law."

Grammar: In Greek, "ανομος" (anomos) is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Ανομος" (anomos) is used to describe something or someone that is not subject to or does not conform to the law. It can refer to actions, behaviors, or individuals that are considered unlawful, illegal, or outside the boundaries of established rules and regulations.
