Etymology: The word "αντιπαρηλθεν" comes from the Greek roots "αντι-" meaning "against" or "opposite" and "παρηλθεν" meaning "passed by" or "gone by". 

Grammar: "αντιπαρηλθεν" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "παρέρχομαι" (parérchomai) which means "to pass by" or "to go by". The prefix "αντι-" adds the meaning of "against" or "opposite" to the verb.

Description: "αντιπαρηλθεν" describes an action of passing by or going by in an opposing or contrary manner. It implies a movement or action that goes against the usual or expected direction.
