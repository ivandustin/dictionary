Etymology: The word "αντεστη" (antesti) comes from the Greek verb "αντιστέκομαι" (antistekomai), which means "to resist" or "to oppose". 

Grammar: "Αντεστη" is a verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in various tenses and forms to indicate different aspects of resistance or opposition.

Description: "Αντεστη" is used to describe the act of resisting or opposing something or someone. It can refer to physical resistance, such as in a physical confrontation, or it can also refer to non-physical resistance, such as resisting an idea or a policy. The word carries a sense of defiance and standing up against something.
