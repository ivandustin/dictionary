Etymology: The word "ανασταντες" comes from the Greek verb "ανασταίνω" (anastaino), which means "to rise" or "to stand up". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "up" or "again", and the verb "σταίνω" (staino), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: "ανασταντες" is the plural form of the verb "ανασταίνω" (anastaino) in the aorist tense. It is used to describe multiple individuals who have risen or stood up.

Description: "ανασταντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having risen" or "having stood up". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe individuals who have taken a stand or risen against something.
