Etymology: The word "αναγκης" (anagkis) comes from the Greek word "αναγκη" (anagki), which means "necessity" or "compulsion."

Grammar: "Αναγκης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship.

Description: "Αναγκης" refers to the concept of necessity or compulsion. It can be used to describe a situation or condition that is unavoidable or obligatory.
