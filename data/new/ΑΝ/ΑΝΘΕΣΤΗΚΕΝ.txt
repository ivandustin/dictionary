Etymology: The word "ανθεστηκεν" comes from the Greek verb "ανθίστημι" (anthistēmi), which means "to stand against" or "to resist". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "αντι-" (anti-), meaning "against", and the verb "ίστημι" (istēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: "ανθεστηκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "ανθίστημι" (anthistēmi) in the perfect tense. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance or consequences in the present.

Description: "ανθεστηκεν" can be translated as "he/she/it has stood against" or "he/she/it has resisted". It implies that someone or something has taken a stand or resisted something in the past, and the effects of this action are still relevant in the present.
