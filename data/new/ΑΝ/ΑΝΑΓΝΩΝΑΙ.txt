Etymology: The word "αναγνωναι" comes from the Greek verb "αναγινώσκω" (anaginósko), which means "to read." It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back," and the verb "γινώσκω" (ginósko), meaning "to know" or "to recognize."

Grammar: "Αναγνωναι" is the present tense, indicative mood, active voice, third person singular form of the verb "αναγινώσκω" (anaginósko). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an action happening in the present time, the indicative mood expresses a statement or a fact, the active voice indicates that the subject performs the action, and the third person singular form refers to a singular subject.

Description: "Αναγνωναι" means "he/she/it reads" in Greek. It is used to describe the action of reading performed by a third person singular subject.
