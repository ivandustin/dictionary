Etymology: The word "ανοητοι" (anoiti) comes from the Greek adjective "ανόητος" (anoitos), which means "foolish" or "senseless". It is derived from the negative prefix "αν-" (an-) and the adjective "οητός" (oitos), which means "intelligent" or "perceptive".

Grammar: "Ανοητοι" is the plural form of the adjective "ανόητος" (anoitos). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, if the noun is plural and masculine, the adjective also takes the plural masculine form.

Description: "Ανοητοι" is used to describe a group of people who are foolish, senseless, or lacking intelligence. It can be used in a derogatory manner to criticize someone's actions, behavior, or decisions.
