Etymology: The word "αντιτασσομενων" comes from the Greek verb "αντιτάσσομαι" (antitássomai), which means "to oppose" or "to resist". The suffix "-ων" (-on) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating ongoing action.

Grammar: "Αντιτασσομενων" is the present participle of the verb "αντιτάσσομαι" in the masculine plural form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Αντιτασσομενων" describes a group of people or things that are currently opposing or resisting something. It implies an ongoing action or state of opposition.
