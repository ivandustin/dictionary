Etymology: The word "αναβαινουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "αναβαίνω" (anabaino), which means "to go up" or "to ascend". The suffix "-ουσιν" (-ousin) indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "Αναβαινουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Αναβαινουσιν" is used to describe the action of going up or ascending. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically going up a hill or stairs, or metaphorically referring to progress or improvement.
