Etymology: The word "ανεωιχθη" (aneōichthē) comes from the Greek roots "ανε" (ane), meaning "up" or "back", and "ωιχθη" (ōichthē), meaning "to shout" or "to cry out". 

Grammar: "Ανεωιχθη" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb.

Description: "Ανεωιχθη" means "he/she/it shouted" or "he/she/it cried out" in Greek. It is used to describe the action of someone raising their voice or making a loud sound to express something.
