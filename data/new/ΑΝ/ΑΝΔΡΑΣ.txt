Etymology: The word "ανδρας" (andras) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀνήρ" (anēr), which means "man" or "male".

Grammar: In Greek, "ανδρας" is a noun of the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Ανδρας" is the Greek word for "man" or "male". It refers to an adult human male. In Greek mythology, "Ανδρας" is often used to describe heroes or warriors.
