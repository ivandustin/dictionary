Etymology: The word "ανυποκριτον" (anypokriton) comes from the Greek roots "ανυπο" (any-po), meaning "without" or "not," and "κριτον" (kriton), meaning "judge" or "critic." 

Grammar: "Ανυποκριτον" is a Greek adjective in the neuter gender. It is used to describe something or someone that is sincere, genuine, or without hypocrisy. 

Description: "Ανυποκριτον" is a term used to describe a person or behavior that is honest, straightforward, and genuine. It refers to someone who does not pretend or put on a false appearance. It is often used to praise individuals who are sincere in their actions and words, without any hidden motives or hypocrisy.
