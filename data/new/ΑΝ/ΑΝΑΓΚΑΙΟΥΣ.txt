Etymology: The word "αναγκαίος" (anankaíos) comes from the Greek root "ανάγκη" (anánki), meaning "necessity" or "compulsion."

Grammar: In Greek, "αναγκαίος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Αναγκαίος" is an adjective in Greek that means "necessary" or "essential." It is used to describe something that is required or indispensable. It can also convey the idea of something that is inevitable or unavoidable.
