Etymology: The word "αναβολήν" comes from the Greek verb "αναβάλλω" (anaválo), which means "to postpone" or "to defer."

Grammar: "Αναβολήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. It is derived from the verb "αναβάλλω" and is used to refer to the act of postponing or deferring something.

Description: "Αναβολήν" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the act of postponing or deferring something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in personal or professional situations, to indicate the delay or rescheduling of an event, task, or decision.
