Grammar: In Greek, "αντιχριστος" is a noun. It is masculine and singular. It can be declined in different cases and forms depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: The term "αντιχριστος" is primarily used in Christian theology to describe a figure who opposes or denies the teachings and authority of Christ. In Christian eschatology, the Antichrist is often associated with the end times and is believed to be a powerful and deceptive figure who will lead many astray.
