Etymology: The word "ανοιξαντες" comes from the Greek verb "ανοίγω" (anoígo), which means "to open". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "αν-" (an-), meaning "up" or "open", and the verb "οίγω" (oígo), meaning "to open".

Grammar: "Ανοιξαντες" is the plural form of the verb "ανοίγω" in the past tense. It is used to refer to a group of people who opened something in the past. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, and number of the subject.

Description: "Ανοιξαντες" is a word in the Greek language that describes a group of people who have opened something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of individuals who opened a door, a package, or an event.
