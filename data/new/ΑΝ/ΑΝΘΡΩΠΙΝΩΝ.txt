Etymology: The word "ανθρωπινων" (anthrōpinōn) comes from the Greek word "άνθρωπος" (ánthrōpos), meaning "human" or "person". The suffix "-ινων" (-inōn) is added to indicate the plural form.

Grammar: "ανθρωπινων" is the genitive plural form of the adjective "άνθρωπινος" (ánthrōpinos), which means "human" or "human-related". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "ανθρωπινων" is used to describe or refer to things that are related to or associated with humans. It can be used to describe human qualities, characteristics, or attributes.
