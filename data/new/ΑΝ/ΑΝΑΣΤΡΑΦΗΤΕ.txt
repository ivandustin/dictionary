Etymology: The word "αναστραφητε" comes from the Greek verb "αναστρέφω" (anastrefo), which means "to turn around" or "to change direction". The suffix "-ητε" is added to the verb stem to form the imperative mood, indicating a command or request.

Grammar: "αναστραφητε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "αναστρέφω" (anastrefo). It is used to give a command or request to a group of people, telling them to turn around or change direction.

Description: "αναστραφητε" is a Greek verb form that instructs a group of people to turn around or change direction. It is commonly used in situations where a group needs to change their course or face a different direction.
