Etymology: The word "αναπειθει" comes from the Greek verb "αναπείθω" (anapeítho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince."

Grammar: "Αναπείθει" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "αναπείθω" (anapeítho). It is used to describe an action of persuading or convincing someone.

Description: "Αναπείθει" is a Greek word that refers to the act of persuading or convincing someone. It implies the use of arguments, reasoning, or evidence to change someone's opinion or behavior.
