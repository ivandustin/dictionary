Grammar: "Ανωρθωθη" (anorthothi) is the third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb "ανορθόω" (anortho). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Ανωρθωθη" (anorthothi) can be translated as "he/she/it was corrected" or "he/she/it was straightened." It describes a past action where someone or something was made right or brought back to the correct position or state.
