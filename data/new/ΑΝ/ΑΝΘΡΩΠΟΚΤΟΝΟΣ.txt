Etymology: The word "ανθρωποκτονος" comes from the Greek words "ανθρωπος" (anthropos) meaning "human" and "κτονος" (ktonos) meaning "killer" or "murderer". 

Grammar: "ανθρωποκτονος" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine form and singular number. 

Description: "ανθρωποκτονος" is used to describe someone or something that kills or murders humans. It can be used to refer to a person who has committed murder or to describe a situation or act that results in the death of a human being.
