Etymology: The word "αντιλυτρον" comes from the Greek roots "αντι-" meaning "against" or "opposite" and "λυτρον" meaning "ransom" or "redemption". 

Grammar: "Αντιλυτρον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their case, number, and gender. 

Description: "Αντιλυτρον" refers to something that is used as a substitute or alternative for a ransom or redemption. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a means of compensation or reparation.
