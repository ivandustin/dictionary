Etymology: The word "αναγγελλων" comes from the Greek verb "αναγγέλλω" (anangello), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim". It is derived from the prefix "ανα-" (ana-), meaning "again" or "back", and the verb "γγέλλω" (gello), meaning "to tell" or "to report".

Grammar: "Αναγγελλων" is the present participle form of the verb "αναγγέλλω" (anangello) in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. It can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description: "Αναγγελλων" refers to a person who is currently announcing or proclaiming something. It can be translated as "announcer" or "proclaimer" in English. The word implies an active role in delivering news, information, or messages to others.
