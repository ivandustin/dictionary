Grammar: "ανεσείσαν" is the third person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "ανεσείω" (anesio). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "ανεσείσαν" describes an action of shaking off or removing something. It implies a forceful or vigorous movement to get rid of something.
