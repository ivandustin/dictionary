Etymology: The word "ανθρωπαρεσκοι" comes from the Greek words "ανθρωπος" (anthropos) meaning "human" and "αρεσκω" (aresko) meaning "to please". It literally translates to "those who please humans" or "people-pleasers".

Grammar: "ανθρωπαρεσκοι" is the plural form of the adjective "ανθρωπαρεσκος" (anthropareskos). It is used to describe people who seek to please others or gain their approval.

Description: "ανθρωπαρεσκοι" refers to individuals who are overly concerned with pleasing others and gaining their approval. They often prioritize the opinions and desires of others over their own. This term can have both positive and negative connotations, depending on the context.
