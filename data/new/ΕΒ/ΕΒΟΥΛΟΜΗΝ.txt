Etymology: The word "εβουλομην" comes from the Greek verb "βούλομαι" (voulomai), which means "to wish" or "to want". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰel- ("to want, wish for").

Grammar: "εβουλομην" is the first person singular form of the verb "βούλομαι" in the imperfect tense. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: "εβουλομην" can be translated as "I was wishing" or "I wanted" in English. It indicates a desire or wish that was ongoing or repeated in the past.
