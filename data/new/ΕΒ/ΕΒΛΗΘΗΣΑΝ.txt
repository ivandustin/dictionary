Etymology: The word "εβληθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "βληθησαν" (blithisan), which means "they were thrown" or "they were cast". It is derived from the verb "βάλλω" (ballo), meaning "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar: "εβληθησαν" is the third person plural, past tense, passive voice form of the verb "βάλλω" (ballo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and person. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon.

Description: "εβληθησαν" is a Greek verb form that describes an action in the past where the subject (they) was thrown or cast. It is commonly used in narratives or descriptions to indicate that something was thrown or cast by an unspecified subject.
