Grammar: "εβοησεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "βοάω" (boao) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "εβοησεν" translates to "he/she/it shouted" or "he/she/it cried out" in English. It is used to describe a specific instance of someone shouting or crying out in the past.
