Etymology: The word "εβασιλευσαν" comes from the Greek verb "βασιλεύω" (vasilevo), which means "to reign" or "to rule." The suffix "-σαν" (-san) indicates the past tense, so "εβασιλευσαν" (evasilefsan) translates to "they reigned" or "they ruled."

Grammar: "Εβασιλευσαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "βασιλεύω" in the past tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings based on the subject and tense. The "-σαν" ending indicates that the subject is plural and the action took place in the past.

Description: "Εβασιλευσαν" is a Greek word that describes the action of multiple individuals reigning or ruling in the past. It can be used to refer to historical figures, such as kings or queens, who held power and authority over a certain period of time.
