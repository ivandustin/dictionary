Etymology: The word "εβληθη" (ebli̱thi̱) comes from the Greek verb "βλήθη" (bli̱thi̱), which means "to be thrown" or "to be cast." It is derived from the ancient Greek verb "βάλλω" (ballō), meaning "to throw."

Grammar: "Εβληθη" (ebli̱thi̱) is the third person singular, aorist passive indicative form of the verb "βλήθη" (bli̱thi̱). The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Εβληθη" (ebli̱thi̱) is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was thrown" or "he/she/it was cast." It describes an action of being thrown or cast in the past.
