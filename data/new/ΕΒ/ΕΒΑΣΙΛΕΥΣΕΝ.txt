Etymology: The word "εβασιλευσεν" comes from the Greek verb "βασιλεύω" (basileúo), which means "to reign" or "to rule." The suffix "-σεν" (-sen) indicates the past tense, so "εβασιλευσεν" (ebasileusen) translates to "he/she/it reigned" or "he/she/it ruled."

Grammar: "εβασιλευσεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "βασιλεύω" in the aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a singular third person pronoun (he, she, or it).

Description: "εβασιλευσεν" is a word in the Greek language that describes the action of someone reigning or ruling in the past. It can be used to refer to a historical figure or a fictional character who held power or authority.
