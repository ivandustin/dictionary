Etymology: The word "εβουληθην" comes from the Greek verb "βουλεύομαι" (bouleúomai), which means "to deliberate" or "to plan." It is derived from the noun "βουλή" (boulḗ), meaning "counsel" or "advice."

Grammar: "εβουληθην" is the aorist passive form of the verb "βουλεύομαι" (bouleúomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms to indicate tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense generally refers to a past action, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "εβουληθην" is the first person singular form of the aorist passive tense of the verb "βουλεύομαι" (bouleúomai). It can be translated as "I was deliberated" or "I was planned."
