Word: φρονημα

Etymology:
The word φρονημα (phronema) comes from the Greek verb φρονέω (phroneo), which means "to think" or "to have an opinion". The noun φρόνησις (phronesis) is related to φρονημα and means "practical wisdom" or "good judgment".

Grammar:
In Greek, φρονημα is a neuter noun of the first declension. Its nominative singular form is φρονημα, and its genitive singular form is φρονηματος (phronematos). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
φρονημα refers to a way of thinking or a mindset. It can also mean an attitude, a disposition, or a frame of mind. In Christian theology, φρονημα is often used to describe a spiritual mindset or a way of thinking that is focused on God and his will.
