Word: φρεναπαται

Etymology: The word φρεναπαται comes from the Greek words φρήν (phrēn) meaning "mind" and πατάω (patáō) meaning "to deceive". Therefore, φρεναπαται literally means "to deceive the mind".

Grammar: φρεναπαται is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb φρεναπατάω (phrenapatáō), which means "to deceive the mind" or "to delude".

Description: φρεναπαται is a Greek verb that refers to the act of deceiving or deluding someone's mind. It can be used in various contexts, such as in psychology, philosophy, or literature. The word implies that the deception is aimed at the mind, rather than the senses or emotions. It is often used to describe situations where someone is misled or tricked into believing something that is not true.
