Word: φρονουσιν

Etymology:
The word φρονουσιν (phronousin) is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb φρονέω (phroneo), which means "to think, to have understanding, to be of a certain opinion or disposition." The word comes from the root φρον- (phron-), which means "mind, thought, understanding."

Grammar:
φρονουσιν (phronousin) is a third-person plural present active indicative form of the verb φρονέω (phroneo). In this form, it indicates that the subject (which is not specified) is currently thinking, having understanding, or holding a certain opinion or disposition.

Description:
φρονουσιν (phronousin) is a verb form that expresses the idea of thinking or having understanding. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the mindset or attitude of believers. For example, in Romans 8:5, it is written, "For those who live according to the flesh set their minds on the things of the flesh, but those who live according to the Spirit set their minds on the things of the Spirit." Here, the verb form φρονουσιν (phronousin) is used to describe the mindset of those who live according to the Spirit.
