Word: φραγμού (phragmou)

Etymology: The word φραγμού comes from the Greek word φραγμός (phragmos), which means "fence" or "barrier".

Grammar: φραγμού is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship to another noun.

Description: φραγμού refers to a barrier or obstruction that prevents movement or access. It can be used to describe physical barriers such as walls or fences, as well as metaphorical barriers such as social or cultural barriers. The word is often used in the context of military fortifications or defensive structures.
