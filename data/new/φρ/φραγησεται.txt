Word: φραγησεται

Etymology:
The word φραγησεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φράσσω (phrássō), which means "to fence in" or "to block off." The verb form φραγησεται is in the passive voice and means "to be blocked off" or "to be obstructed."

Grammar:
φραγησεται is a third-person singular passive verb in the future tense. It is conjugated from the root verb φράσσω (phrássō) and the passive ending -ησεται (-ēsetai).

Description:
φραγησεται is a Greek verb that describes a state of being blocked off or obstructed. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to a road or path that is blocked, or in a figurative sense, such as when referring to a person's progress being hindered. The verb is in the future tense, indicating that the blocking or obstruction will occur at some point in the future.
