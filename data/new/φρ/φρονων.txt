Word: φρονων

Etymology:
The word φρονων (phronōn) is derived from the Greek verb φρονέω (phroneō), which means "to think, to have understanding, to be of sound mind." The suffix -ων (-ōn) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently thinking or having understanding.

Grammar:
φρονων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a present participle, which means it describes someone who is currently thinking or having understanding. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives, describing the subject of the sentence.

Description:
φρονων is often translated as "wise" or "prudent" in English. It describes someone who has good judgment, is thoughtful, and has a sound mind. In ancient Greek philosophy, the term was used to describe someone who had achieved a state of wisdom or enlightenment. The word is often used in the New Testament to describe the character of a virtuous person.
