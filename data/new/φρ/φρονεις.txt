Word: φρονεις

Etymology:
The word φρονεις (pronounced "froneis") is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φρονέω (pronounced "fronéo"), which means "to think" or "to have an opinion". It is derived from the Greek word φρήν (pronounced "frin"), which means "mind" or "intellect".

Grammar:
φρονεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the verb φρονέω. It is translated to "you think" or "you have an opinion" in English.

Description:
φρονεις is a word that describes the act of thinking or having an opinion. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in philosophical discussions, debates, or everyday conversations. The word implies a certain level of thoughtfulness and consideration, as it suggests that the person is actively engaging with their own thoughts and ideas.
