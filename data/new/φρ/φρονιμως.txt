Word: φρονιμως

Etymology: 
The word φρονιμως (phronimos) is derived from the Greek word φρόνησις (phronesis), which means "practical wisdom" or "prudence". It is a combination of the words φρόνησις (phronesis) and the suffix -μως (-mos), which is used to form adverbs.

Grammar:
φρονιμως (phronimos) is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe the manner in which an action is performed. It is often translated as "wisely" or "prudently" in English.

Description:
φρονιμως (phronimos) is a word that describes the quality of being wise or prudent. It is often used to describe someone who is able to make good decisions and act in a way that is beneficial to themselves and others. In Greek philosophy, the concept of φρόνησις (phronesis) is considered to be one of the highest virtues, as it involves the ability to make practical and ethical decisions in complex situations.
