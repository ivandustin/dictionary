Word: φρισσουσιν

Etymology: The word φρισσουσιν is derived from the Greek verb φρίσσω (phríssō), which means "to shiver" or "to tremble". It is a present active indicative third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: φρισσουσιν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated from the root verb φρίσσω (phríssō) and is in the third person plural form.

Description: The word φρισσουσιν describes an action of shivering or trembling. It is often used to describe a physical reaction to cold or fear. In Greek literature, it is also used metaphorically to describe a feeling of intense emotion or excitement.
