The word "φραγμον" (phragmon) is a Greek noun that means "a barrier, a fence, or a wall." It is derived from the verb "φράζω" (phrazo), which means "to fence in, to enclose, or to block off." In terms of grammar, "φραγμον" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative. In ancient Greek, "φραγμον" was often used to refer to a physical barrier or obstacle, but it could also be used metaphorically to describe a mental or emotional blockage.
