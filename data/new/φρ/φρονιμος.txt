Word: φρονιμος

Etymology: 
The word φρονιμος (phronimos) comes from the Greek root word φρόνησις (phronesis), which means "practical wisdom" or "prudence". It is derived from the verb φρονέω (phroneo), which means "to think" or "to have an opinion".

Grammar:
φρονιμος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: φρόνιμος (phronimos)
- Genitive: φρονίμου (phronimou)
- Dative: φρονίμῳ (phronimo)
- Accusative: φρόνιμον (phronimon)
- Vocative: φρόνιμε (phronime)

Plural:
- Nominative: φρόνιμοι (phronimoi)
- Genitive: φρονίμων (phronimon)
- Dative: φρονίμοις (phronimois)
- Accusative: φρόνιμους (phronimous)
- Vocative: φρόνιμοι (phronimoi)

Description:
φρονιμος is an adjective that describes someone who is wise, prudent, or sensible. It is often used to describe someone who has good judgment and makes wise decisions. In Greek philosophy, φρόνησις (phronesis) was considered one of the four cardinal virtues, along with σωφροσύνη (sophrosyne) or self-control, δικαιοσύνη (dikaiosyne) or justice, and ἀνδρεία (andreia) or courage.
