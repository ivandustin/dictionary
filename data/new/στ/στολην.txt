Word: στολην

Etymology: The word στολην comes from the Greek word στολή (stolé), which means "garment" or "dress". It is derived from the verb στέλλω (stéllō), which means "to set in order" or "to arrange".

Grammar: στολην is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: στολην refers to a garment or dress that is worn by someone. It can be used to describe any type of clothing, from a simple tunic to a more elaborate gown. The word is often used in a poetic or literary context, and can be associated with elegance, beauty, and grace.
