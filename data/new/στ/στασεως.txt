The word "στασεως" (staseos) is a Greek noun in the genitive case, which means "of standing" or "of stopping". It is derived from the verb "ἵστημι" (histemi), which means "to stand" or "to stop". In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension and is feminine in gender. 

The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing the act of standing still or stopping, or referring to a state of rest or stability. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of emotional or mental stability. 

Example sentence: Η στάση του αυτοκινήτου στο φανάρι ήταν πολύ μακριά. (I stasi tou aftokinitou sto fanari itan poly makria.) - The car's stop at the traffic light was very far away.
