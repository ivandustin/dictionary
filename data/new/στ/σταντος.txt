Word: σταντος

Etymology:
The word σταντος is derived from the Greek word σταντήρ, which means "a stand or support". It is also related to the Greek verb σταίνω, which means "to stand".

Grammar:
In Greek, σταντος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive σταντού, the accusative σταντό, and the vocative σταντέ.

Description:
σταντος refers to a stand or support, typically used to hold something up. It can refer to a variety of objects, such as a stand for a musical instrument, a stand for a microphone, or a stand for a plant. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to a person who provides support or assistance to someone else.
