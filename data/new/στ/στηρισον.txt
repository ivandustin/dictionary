Word: στηρισον

Etymology: στηρισον is a verb in the imperative mood in Ancient Greek, derived from the root word στηρίζω (stērízō), which means "to support" or "to prop up". The word is formed by adding the suffix -σον (-son) to the stem στηρι- (stēri-).

Grammar: στηρισον is a second person singular imperative verb in the aorist tense in Ancient Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "support" or "prop up" something.

Description: στηρισον is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Revelation (3:2).
