Word: στοιχωμεν

Etymology:
The word στοιχωμεν comes from the Greek verb στοιχέω (stoicheo), which means "to line up" or "to arrange in order". The suffix -μεν (-men) is a first person plural ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to "we" or "us".

Grammar:
στοιχωμεν is a present tense verb in Greek, specifically in the first person plural form. It is conjugated from the root verb στοιχέω (stoicheo), which means "to line up" or "to arrange in order". The verb is active, indicating that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
στοιχωμεν is a Greek verb that means "we line up" or "we arrange in order". It is used to describe the action of putting things in a specific order or sequence. The verb can be used in a literal sense, such as lining up objects or arranging them in a specific order, or in a figurative sense, such as organizing thoughts or ideas. The present tense form of the verb indicates that the action is currently happening or ongoing.
