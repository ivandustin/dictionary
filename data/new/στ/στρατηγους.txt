Word: στρατηγούς

Etymology: The word στρατηγούς comes from the Greek word στρατηγός (stratēgos), which means "general" or "commander". It is derived from the words στρατός (stratos), meaning "army", and ἡγέομαι (hēgeomai), meaning "to lead".

Grammar: στρατηγούς is the plural form of στρατηγός in the nominative case. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: στρατηγούς refers to a group of generals or commanders in an army. In ancient Greece, the position of stratēgos was highly respected and was often held by the most prominent citizens. The stratēgoi were responsible for leading the army in battle and making strategic decisions. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to military generals.
