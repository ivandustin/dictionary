Word: στενοχωριαις

Etymology:
The word στενοχωριαις is derived from the Greek word στενοχωρία (stenochoria), which means "narrowness, confinement, or distress." The suffix -αις (-ais) is added to the end of the word to indicate the dative plural form.

Grammar:
στενοχωριαις is a noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
στενοχωριαις refers to a state of distress or confinement. It can be used to describe a physical space that is narrow or cramped, or a psychological state of feeling trapped or overwhelmed. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe emotional or mental distress.
