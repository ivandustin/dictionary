Word: στρουθια

The word στρουθια (strouthia) is derived from the Greek word στρουθος (strouthos), which means "sparrow". The suffix -ια (-ia) is a common Greek suffix used to form feminine nouns.

στρουθια (strouthia) is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is the plural form of στρουθος (strouthos), which is a masculine noun. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description:
στρουθια (strouthia) refers to sparrows, which are small, common birds found throughout the world. They are known for their distinctive chirping and are often seen in urban areas.
