Word: σταυρώσον

Etymology: σταυρώσον is a Greek verb that comes from the word σταυρός (stauros), meaning "cross". The verb form σταυρώνω (stauro̱nó̱) means "to crucify", and σταυρώσον (stauro̱són) is the imperative form, meaning "crucify!".

Grammar: σταυρώσον is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. It is used as a command or request to someone to perform the action of crucifying.

Description: σταυρώσον is a powerful and emotive word that is often associated with the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. It is a command that was given by the Roman authorities to execute criminals by nailing them to a cross. The word is still used in modern Greek to mean "crucify", but it is also used metaphorically to mean "to torment" or "to cause suffering".
