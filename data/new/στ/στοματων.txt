Word: στομάτων

Etymology: The word στόμα (stoma) means "mouth" in Greek. The suffix -ατων (-aton) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to "of the mouths" or "belonging to the mouths".

Grammar: στομάτων is a genitive plural form of the noun στόμα (stoma). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple mouths being referred to.

Description: στομάτων is a Greek word that means "of the mouths" or "belonging to the mouths". It is often used in medical or anatomical contexts to refer to the various parts of the mouth, such as the teeth, tongue, and gums. It can also be used more broadly to refer to the act of speaking or eating, or to the overall function of the mouth in the human body.
