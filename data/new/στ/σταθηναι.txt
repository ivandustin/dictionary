Word: σταθηναι

Etymology:
The word σταθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word στάθμη (stathmi), which means "to stand". The suffix -ναι (-nai) is a common ending for verbs in the aorist tense in Ancient Greek.

Grammar:
σταθηναι is the aorist tense of the Greek verb σταθμένος (stathmenos), which means "to stand". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, σταθηναι means "stood" or "has stood".

Description:
σταθηναι is a verb that describes the action of standing or having stood in the past. It is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a person or object that has stood still or remained in a fixed position. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who has remained steadfast or resolute in the face of adversity.
