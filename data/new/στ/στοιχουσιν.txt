The word "στοιχουσιν" (stoichousin) is a verb in Greek, which means "they line up" or "they arrange in rows". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "stoichos", which means "row" or "line".

Grammar: "στοιχουσιν" is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb "στοιχέω" (stoicheo), which means "to line up" or "to arrange in rows". 

Description: The word is often used in a literal sense to describe the act of arranging objects or people in a straight line or row. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the organization or order of ideas or concepts.
