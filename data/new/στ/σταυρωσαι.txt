Word: σταυρώσαι

Etymology: The word σταυρώσαι comes from the Greek verb σταυρόω (stauróō), which means "to crucify". It is derived from the noun σταυρός (stauros), which means "cross".

Grammar: σταυρώσαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb σταυρόω.

Description: σταυρώσαι means "to crucify yourself" or "to be crucified". It is often used in a religious context to refer to the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the act of crucifying Jesus (Matthew 20:19, Mark 15:13, Luke 23:21, John 19:6) and also to describe the act of taking up one's own cross and following Jesus (Matthew 16:24, Mark 8:34, Luke 9:23).
