The word "στρατιαι" (stratai) is a plural noun in Greek, which means "armies" or "troops". It is derived from the Greek word "στρατός" (stratos), which means "army". In terms of grammar, it is a third declension noun, with the nominative plural form being "στρατιαι". 

The word "στρατιαι" is often used in ancient Greek literature to refer to the armies of various city-states or empires, such as the Persian army or the Spartan army. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a large group of people or things that are organized or working together towards a common goal. 

Overall, "στρατιαι" is a versatile and important word in ancient Greek, with a rich history and cultural significance.
