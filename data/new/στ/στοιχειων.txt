Word: στοιχειων

Etymology: The word στοιχειων comes from the Greek word στοιχεῖον (stoicheion), which means "element" or "fundamental principle". It is derived from the word στοίχος (stoichos), which means "row" or "line".

Grammar: στοιχειων is the genitive plural form of the noun στοιχεῖον. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns.

Description: στοιχειων refers to the elements or fundamental principles of something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in chemistry to refer to the chemical elements, in mathematics to refer to the basic principles of a subject, or in philosophy to refer to the fundamental principles of reality. The genitive plural form στοιχειων indicates that there are multiple elements or principles being referred to.
