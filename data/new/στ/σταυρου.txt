The word "σταυρου" (stavrou) is a Greek noun that means "cross" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "σταυρόω" (stavroo), which means "to crucify". 

Grammar: "Σταυρου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it means "of the cross". 

Description: The cross is a symbol of Christianity and is often used in religious ceremonies and art. It is made up of two intersecting lines, with the vertical line representing the divine and the horizontal line representing the earthly.
