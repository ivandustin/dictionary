Word: στηρίξαι

Etymology:
The word στηρίξαι (stiríxe) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It comes from the root word στήριγμα (stírigma), which means "support" or "prop". The suffix -ξαι (-xe) is a conjugation ending that indicates the aorist tense and the active voice.

Grammar:
στηρίξαι is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to "support" or "prop" something. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
The word στηρίξαι is a verb that means "to support" or "to prop up". It is often used in a figurative sense to mean "to encourage" or "to strengthen". For example, one might say "στήριξε με" (stírixé me) to mean "support me" or "encourage me". The word can also be used in a literal sense, such as "στήριξε τον τοίχο" (stírixé ton tóicho) which means "prop up the wall".
