The word "στρατιωτην" (stratioten) is a Greek noun that means "soldier" or "warrior". It is derived from the Greek word "στρατιώτης" (stratiotes), which also means "soldier". 

In terms of grammar, "στρατιωτην" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. For example, in the sentence "Ο στρατιώτης είδε τον εχθρό" (O stratiotes ide ton echthro), which means "The soldier saw the enemy", "στρατιωτην" is the direct object of the verb "είδε" (ide).

Overall, "στρατιωτην" is a common Greek word that is used to refer to a member of the military or someone who fights in a war.
