Word: στεφανουται

Etymology: The word στεφανουται is derived from the Greek word στέφανος (stéfanos), which means "crown" or "wreath". The suffix -ουται (-outai) is a reflexive suffix, indicating that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar: στεφανουται is a third-person singular present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb στεφανόω (stefanóo), which means "to crown" or "to wreath". The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on themselves or is being acted upon.

Description: στεφανουται can be translated to mean "he/she/it is crowning/wreathing themselves" or "he/she/it is being crowned/wreathed". It is often used in the context of awarding someone a crown or wreath as a symbol of victory or honor.
