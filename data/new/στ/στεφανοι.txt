Word: στεφανοι

Etymology:
The word στεφανοι (stephanoi) is derived from the Greek word στέφανος (stephanos), which means "crown" or "wreath". In ancient Greece, wreaths were used to crown victorious athletes, soldiers, and poets. The word στεφανοι is the plural form of στέφανος.

Grammar:
In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word στεφανοι is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. It is used to refer to multiple crowns or wreaths.

Description:
The word στεφανοι is used in various contexts, such as in ancient Greek literature, art, and mythology. In Christianity, the crown of thorns worn by Jesus Christ is sometimes referred to as a στέφανος. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to a symbol of victory or achievement.
