Word: στυλον

Etymology:
The word στυλον (stylus) comes from the Greek word στύλος (stulos), which means a pointed instrument used for writing or engraving. The word στύλος is derived from the verb στύλλω (styllō), which means to prick or puncture.

Grammar:
In Greek, στυλον is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is στυλος, and its genitive singular form is στυλου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
στυλον refers to a pointed instrument used for writing or engraving. In ancient Greece, it was typically made of metal, bone, or ivory and was used to write on wax tablets or to engrave on stone or metal. The word στυλον is also used in modern Greek to refer to a pen or pencil.
