Word: στρατηγοί

Etymology: The word στρατηγοί comes from the Greek words στρατός (army) and ἡγέομαι (to lead). It literally means "army leader" or "general".

Grammar: στρατηγοί is the plural form of στρατηγός (strategos) in Greek. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative case, it is spelled as στρατηγοί.

Description: στρατηγοί is a term used in ancient Greece to refer to military leaders or generals. They were responsible for leading the army in battles and making strategic decisions. In Athens, the position of strategos was an elected office and held significant political power. The term is still used in modern Greek to refer to military officers of high rank.
