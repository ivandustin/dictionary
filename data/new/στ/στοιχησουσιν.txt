Word: στοιχησουσιν

Etymology:
The word στοιχησουσιν is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form of the word στοιχέω (stoicheo), which means "to line up" or "to march in order". The word is derived from the Greek noun στοῖχος (stoichos), which means "a row" or "a line".

Grammar:
The word στοιχησουσιν is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people.
