Word: στραφεντες

Etymology:
The word στραφεντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb στρέφω (strefó), which means "to turn" or "to twist". The suffix -εντες (-entes) is a participle suffix that indicates a past action, so στραφεντες means "having turned" or "having twisted".

Grammar:
στραφεντες is a participle in the aorist tense, masculine gender, and plural number. It is derived from the verb στρέφω (strefó), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

στραφεντες is used in Greek to describe a group of people who have turned or twisted in a certain direction.
