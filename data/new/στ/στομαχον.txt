Word: στομαχον

Etymology:
The word στομαχον (stomakhon) is derived from the Greek word στόμαχος (stomakhos), which means "stomach". The suffix -ον (-on) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form nouns.

Grammar:
στομαχον (stomakhon) is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word στομαχον (stomakhon) is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
στομαχον (stomakhon) refers to the stomach, which is an organ in the digestive system that is responsible for breaking down food.
