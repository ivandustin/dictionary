Word: στασις

Etymology:
The word στασις (stasis) comes from the Greek verb σταίνω (staino), which means "to stand" or "to stop." It was originally used in ancient Greece to refer to a political uprising or rebellion, but it later came to mean any kind of conflict or disagreement.

Grammar:
In Greek, στασις is a feminine noun of the third declension. Its nominative singular form is στασις, and its genitive singular form is στάσεως (staseos). The word can be declined in all cases and numbers.

στασις is a Greek word that can be translated as "uprising," "rebellion," "conflict," or "disagreement." It was commonly used in ancient Greece to describe political unrest or civil war, but it can also refer to any kind of conflict or disagreement.
