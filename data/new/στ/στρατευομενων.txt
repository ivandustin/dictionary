Word: στρατευομένων

Etymology: The word στρατευομένων comes from the Greek verb στρατεύομαι (strateuomai), which means "to go on a military expedition" or "to serve in the army." The suffix -μένων (-menon) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently engaged in military service.

Grammar: στρατευομένων is a present participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple people involved. The present participle form indicates that the action of going on a military expedition or serving in the army is ongoing or continuous.

Description: στρατευομένων is a word that refers to people who are currently serving in the military or engaged in a military expedition. It can be used to describe soldiers, warriors, or any other individuals who are actively involved in military service.
