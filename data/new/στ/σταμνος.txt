Word: σταμνος

Etymology:
The word σταμνος (stamnos) is derived from the Greek word σταμνη (stamnē), which means a jar or a large earthenware vessel. The word is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *steb(h)-, which means to support or to stiffen.

Description:
σταμνος is a Greek word that refers to a large earthenware vessel or jar used for storing liquids or grains. These jars were commonly used in ancient Greece for storing and transporting wine, olive oil, and other commodities. They were often decorated with intricate designs and patterns, and some were even used as burial urns. The word σταμνος is still used in modern Greek to refer to a jar or a large container.
