Word: σταθητε

Etymology:
The word σταθητε is a conjugated form of the Greek verb σταθῆναι (stathenai), which means "to stand". The verb is derived from the noun στάσις (stasis), which means "standing" or "position". 

Grammar:
σταθητε is the second person plural aorist passive imperative form of the verb σταθῆναι (stathenai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The word σταθητε is a command to a group of people to "stand" or "be still". It is often used in the context of military commands or in situations where a group of people need to be organized or prepared for something.
