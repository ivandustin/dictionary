The word σταυρός (stavros) comes from the Greek verb σταυρόω (stavroo), which means "to crucify." The noun σταυρός refers to the cross on which Jesus Christ was crucified.

In Greek, σταυρός is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is σταυρός, and its genitive singular form is σταυρού. The word can also be declined in the plural.

σταυρός is a significant symbol in Christianity, representing the crucifixion and death of Jesus Christ.
