Word: σταθεντες

Etymology:
The word σταθεντες is derived from the Greek verb σταθείς (stathis), which means "to stand firm" or "to be steadfast." The suffix -εντες (-entes) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar:
σταθεντες is a participle in the nominative plural form. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The nominative case indicates that the participle is the subject of the sentence.

Description:
σταθεντες can be translated as "those who are standing firm" or "those who are steadfast." It is often used in a religious or moral context to describe individuals who are unwavering in their beliefs or principles. The word can also be used to describe physical objects that are firmly fixed in place.
