Word: στρατηγός

Etymology: The word στρατηγός comes from the Greek words στρατός (army) and ἡγέομαι (to lead). Therefore, στρατηγός means "army leader" or "general".

Grammar: In Greek, στρατηγός is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is στρατηγοί. It can be used as a subject, object, or possessive noun in a sentence.

Description: στρατηγός is a high-ranking military officer who is responsible for commanding an army or a group of soldiers. In ancient Greece, the position of στρατηγός was highly respected and was often held by aristocrats. Today, the term is still used in Greece and other countries to refer to military leaders.
