Word: στηκει

Etymology: The word στηκει comes from the Greek verb στέκω (stéko), which means "to stand" or "to be upright."

Grammar: στηκει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb στέκω (stéko) in the present tense.

Description: στηκει is a Greek verb that means "stands" or "is standing." It is often used to describe a person or object that is in an upright position. For example, "Η κολόνα στηκει ψηλά" (I kolóna stékei psilá) means "The column stands tall." It can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is steadfast or resolute in their beliefs or actions.
