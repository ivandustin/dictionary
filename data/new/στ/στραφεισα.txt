Word: στραφεισα

Etymology: στραφεισα is a Greek word that comes from the verb στρέφω (stréfo), which means "to turn" or "to twist."

Grammar: στραφεισα is the feminine form of the past participle of the verb στρέφω (stréfo). It is in the singular form and in the nominative case.

Description: στραφεισα means "turned" or "twisted" in English. It is used to describe something that has been turned or twisted in a particular direction. For example, if someone says "η κοπέλα στράφηκε προς τα αριστερά" (i kopéla stráfike pros ta aristerá), it means "the girl turned to the left."
