Word: στυλος

Etymology:
The word στυλος (stylus) comes from the Greek word στύλος (stulos), which means a pointed instrument used for writing or engraving. The word is derived from the verb στύλλω (styllō), which means to prick or puncture.

Grammar:
In Greek, στυλος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is στυλος, and its genitive singular form is στυλου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
στυλος is a versatile word that can refer to a variety of objects. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe a pointed instrument used for writing on wax tablets. Later, it was used to describe a pen or pencil used for writing on paper. Today, it can refer to any type of writing instrument, including pens, pencils, markers, and styluses used for touchscreens. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a person's writing style or artistic style.
