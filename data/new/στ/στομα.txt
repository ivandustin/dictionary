Word: στομα

Etymology:
The word στομα (stoma) comes from the Ancient Greek στόμα (stóma), which means "mouth". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *stómh₂, which means "mouth, opening".

Grammar:
In Greek, στομα is a neuter noun. Its nominative singular form is στόμα (stóma), and its genitive singular form is στόματος (stómatos). The word can also be declined in the plural, with the nominative plural form being στόματα (stómata) and the genitive plural form being στομάτων (stomáton).

Description:
στομα (stoma) refers to the anatomical structure that is used for eating, drinking, speaking, and breathing in humans and animals. It is the opening through which food and drink enter the body, and air is taken in and expelled. The mouth is also an important part of communication, as it is used for speaking and expressing emotions.
