Word: στραφεις

Etymology:
The word στραφεις (strafeis) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word στρέφω (strefó), which means "to turn" or "to twist". The suffix -εις (-eis) is added to the root word to form the second person singular present tense of the verb.

Grammar:
στραφεις (strafeis) is a second person singular present tense verb in the Greek language. It is used to address one person directly and indicates that the action of turning or twisting is being performed by that person. The verb belongs to the first conjugation class of Greek verbs, which means that it follows a regular pattern of inflection.
