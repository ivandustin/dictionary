Word: στρατευματος

Etymology: The word στρατευματος comes from the Greek word στρατεύματα (strateumata), which means "army" or "military expedition". It is derived from the verb στρατεύω (strateuo), which means "to march" or "to go to war".

Grammar: στρατευματος is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is the genitive singular form of στρατεύματα. 

Description: στρατευματος refers to a military unit or an army. It can also refer to a military expedition or campaign. In ancient Greece, the term was used to describe a group of soldiers who were organized and led by a commander. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a military force or unit.
