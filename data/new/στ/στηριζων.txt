Word: στηριζων

Etymology: στηριζων is the present participle of the Greek verb στηρίζω (stirizo), which means "to support" or "to prop up". The word comes from the root στηρ- (stir-), which means "to stand firm" or "to be stable".

Grammar: στηριζων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe a person or thing that is currently supporting or propping up something else. In English, it would be translated as "supporting" or "propping up".

Description: στηριζων is a word that describes an action of support or propping up. It can be used in a literal sense, such as supporting a building or structure, or in a figurative sense, such as supporting a person or idea. The word implies strength and stability, as the act of supporting something requires a firm and steady foundation.
