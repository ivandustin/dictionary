Word: σταθεις

Etymology:
The word σταθεις comes from the Greek verb σταθμεύω (stathmeuo), which means "to stand firm" or "to be fixed in place." It is derived from the noun στάθμη (stathme), which means "a fixed point" or "a standard of measurement."

Grammar:
σταθεις is the aorist participle of the verb σταθμεύω (stathmeuo), which means "having stood firm" or "having been fixed in place." It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
σταθεις is a word that describes something that has stood firm or been fixed in place. It can be used to describe a physical object that has been anchored or secured, or it can be used metaphorically to describe a person who has remained steadfast in their beliefs or actions. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe heroes or warriors who have stood their ground in battle.
