Word: στραφωσιν

Etymology: στραφωσιν is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically in the present tense, third person plural. It is derived from the root word στρέφω (stréphō), which means "to turn" or "to twist."

Grammar: στραφωσιν is a present tense, third person plural form of the verb στρέφω (stréphō). In Greek grammar, this form indicates that the action of turning or twisting is being performed by a group of people or things.

Description: στραφωσιν can be translated to mean "they turn" or "they twist." It is a verb form that is often used in Greek literature and conversation to describe physical movements or changes in direction.
