Word: στησηις

Etymology: The word στησηις (stasis) comes from the Greek verb σταίνω (staino), which means "to stand" or "to stop." In ancient Greece, stasis referred to a political situation where two opposing factions were at a standstill, unable to reach a resolution.

Grammar: στησηις is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the third declension and has the following forms:

Singular:
- Nominative: στησηις
- Genitive: στησεως
- Dative: στησει
- Accusative: στησην
- Vocative: στηση

Plural:
- Nominative: στησεις
- Genitive: στησεων
- Dative: στησεσι(ν)
- Accusative: στησεις
- Vocative: στησεις

Description: στησηις refers to a state of standing still or a state of equilibrium. In ancient Greece, it was used to describe a political situation where two opposing factions were at a standstill, unable to reach a resolution. In modern Greek, it is used more broadly to refer to any situation where there is a stalemate or impasse.
