Word: στρατηγοις

Etymology:
The word στρατηγοις comes from the Greek word στρατηγός (stratēgos), which means "general" or "commander". The suffix -οις (-ois) indicates the dative plural form, which means "to the generals" or "for the generals".

Grammar:
στρατηγοις is a noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
στρατηγοις refers to a group of generals or commanders. It is often used in historical or military contexts to refer to a specific group of leaders.
