The word "στοικων" (stoikon) is a Greek noun that means "pillar" or "support". It is derived from the Greek verb "στοιχεῖν" (stoichein), which means "to line up" or "to arrange in rows". In ancient Greek architecture, stoikons were used as vertical supports for buildings and temples. 

In terms of grammar, "στοικων" is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek, which means that its stem ends in -ον. 

Overall, "στοικων" is a word that describes a structural element that provides support and stability.
