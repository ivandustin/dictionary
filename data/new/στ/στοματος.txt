Word: στοματος

Etymology: The word στοματος (stomatos) is derived from the Greek word στόμα (stoma), which means "mouth".

Grammar: στοματος is a genitive singular form of the noun στόμα (stoma), which is a neuter noun in Greek. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and in this case, it means "of the mouth".

Description: στοματος refers to something that is related to or belongs to the mouth. It can be used to describe various things such as the teeth, tongue, gums, or any other part of the mouth. In medical terminology, it is often used to describe conditions or diseases that affect the mouth, such as stomatitis (inflammation of the mouth) or stomatognathic system (the system of structures involved in chewing and speaking).
