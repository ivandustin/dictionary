The word στησονται is a conjugated form of the Greek verb στήνω (steno), which means "to stand up" or "to set up." The verb comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂-, which means "to stand."

στησονται is a third-person plural present indicative active form of the verb στήνω. It is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening now. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement about a fact or reality.
