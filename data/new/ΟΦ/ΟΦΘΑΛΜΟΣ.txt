Etymology: The word "οφθαλμος" (ophthalmos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *okw- meaning "eye."

Grammar: In Greek, "οφθαλμος" is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "οφθαλμος." The genitive singular form is "οφθαλμου" and the accusative singular form is "οφθαλμον."

Description: "Οφθαλμος" is the Greek word for "eye." It refers to the organ responsible for vision in humans and many other animals. The eye is a complex structure that allows light to enter and be converted into electrical signals that are sent to the brain for interpretation. It plays a crucial role in our perception of the world around us.
