Etymology: The word "οφεσιν" comes from the Greek word "οφις" (ophis), meaning "snake" or "serpent".

Grammar: "Οφεσιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender in Greek.

Description: "Οφεσιν" refers to a snake or serpent in Greek. It is used to describe a reptile with a long, cylindrical body and no legs. Snakes are found in various habitats around the world and are known for their ability to slither and their venomous bites in some species.
