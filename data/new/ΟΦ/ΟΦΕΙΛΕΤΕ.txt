Etymology: The word "οφειλετε" comes from the Greek verb "οφείλω" (opheílo), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". 

Grammar: "Οφειλετε" is the second person plural form of the verb "οφείλω" in the present tense. It is used when addressing a group of people and means "you owe" or "you are indebted".

Description: "Οφειλετε" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the concept of owing or being indebted. It is used to indicate a financial or moral obligation that someone has towards another person or entity.
