Etymology: The word "οφειλεται" comes from the Greek verb "οφείλω" (opheílo), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". 

Grammar: "Οφειλεται" is the third person singular form of the verb "οφείλω" in the present tense. It is used to describe an action or state of owing or being indebted that is attributed to a third person.

Description: "Οφειλεται" is a Greek word that expresses the concept of owing or being indebted. It is used to indicate that someone or something is obligated to pay or repay a debt, whether it is financial, moral, or otherwise.
