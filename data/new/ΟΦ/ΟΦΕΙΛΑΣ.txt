Etymology: The word "οφειλας" comes from the Greek verb "οφειλω" (opheilō), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". 

Grammar: "Οφειλας" is the second person singular form of the verb "οφειλω" in the present tense. It is used when addressing one person directly and means "you owe" or "you are indebted".

Description: "Οφειλας" is a Greek word that is used to express the concept of owing or being indebted. It is commonly used in financial or moral contexts to indicate that someone has a debt or obligation to fulfill.
