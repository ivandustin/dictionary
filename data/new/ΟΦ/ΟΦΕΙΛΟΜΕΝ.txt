Etymology: The word "οφειλομεν" comes from the Greek verb "οφειλω" (opheilō), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". It is derived from the noun "οφειλη" (opheilē), meaning "debt" or "obligation".

Grammar: "Οφειλομεν" is the present tense, first person plural form of the verb "οφειλω" (opheilō). It is used to express that "we owe" or "we are indebted".

Description: "Οφειλομεν" is a Greek verb that indicates a state of owing or being indebted. It is used to describe a situation where there is a financial or moral obligation to repay or fulfill a debt.
