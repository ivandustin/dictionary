Etymology: The word "οφθεις" (ophtheis) comes from the Greek verb "οράω" (orao), which means "to see". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₃ekʷ-.

Grammar: "Οφθεις" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and singular number. It belongs to the third declension, which means its endings change depending on the case and number.
