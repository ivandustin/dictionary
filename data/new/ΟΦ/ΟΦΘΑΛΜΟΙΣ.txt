Etymology: The word "οφθαλμοίς" comes from the Greek word "ὀφθαλμός" (ophthalmós), which means "eye".

Grammar: "Οφθαλμοίς" is the plural form of the noun "οφθαλμός" (ophthalmós), which means "eyes" in Greek. It is in the dative case, indicating the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Οφθαλμοίς" refers to the eyes in Greek. It is used to describe the organs of vision in humans and animals. The eyes play a crucial role in perceiving the world around us and are responsible for our sense of sight.
