Etymology: The word "οφεων" (ophéon) comes from the Greek word "όφις" (óphis), which means "snake" or "serpent".

Grammar: "Οφεων" (ophéon) is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Οφεων" (ophéon) refers to "snakes" or "serpents" in Greek. It is used to describe a group of reptiles that are elongated, legless, and carnivorous. Snakes are found in various habitats around the world and are known for their ability to slither and swallow their prey whole. They play important roles in ecosystems but can also be venomous and pose a threat to humans.
