Etymology: The word "οφιν" (ophin) comes from the Greek word "όφις" (ophis), which means "snake" or "serpent". 

Grammar: "Οφιν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is of masculine gender. 

Description: "Οφιν" refers to a snake or serpent. In Greek mythology, snakes are often associated with various gods and goddesses, and they symbolize different concepts such as wisdom, healing, and transformation.
