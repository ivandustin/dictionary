Etymology: The word "οφθαλμων" (ophthalmōn) comes from the Greek word "οφθαλμός" (ophthalmos), meaning "eye". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *okw-, which means "to see".

Grammar: "Οφθαλμων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "οφθαλμός" (ophthalmos). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. The plural form indicates that there are multiple eyes.

Description: "Οφθαλμων" refers to the eyes in Greek. It is used to describe the organs of vision in humans and many other animals. The eyes play a crucial role in perceiving the world around us and are responsible for our sense of sight.
