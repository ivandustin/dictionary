Etymology: The word "οφειλεις" comes from the Greek verb "οφειλω" (opheilō), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". 

Grammar: "Οφειλεις" is the second person singular form of the verb "οφειλω" in the present tense. It is used when addressing one person and means "you owe" or "you are indebted".

Description: "Οφειλεις" is a Greek word that is commonly used to express the concept of owing or being indebted. It is often used in financial or legal contexts to refer to debts or obligations.
