Etymology: The word "οφειλέτης" (opheilétis) comes from the Greek verb "οφείλω" (opheílo), which means "to owe". The suffix "-της" (-tis) is added to form the noun, indicating a person who owes something.

Grammar: "Οφειλέτης" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and its plural form is "οφειλέτες" (opheilétes).

Description: "Οφειλέτης" refers to a debtor or someone who owes a debt. It is commonly used in legal and financial contexts to describe a person who has an outstanding obligation to pay money or fulfill a financial obligation.
