Etymology: The word "οφειλην" comes from the Greek verb "οφείλω" (opheílo), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted". 

Grammar: "Οφειλην" is the first person singular form of the verb "οφείλω" in the aorist tense. It is used to express an action that occurred in the past and is completed.

Description: "Οφειλην" is used to indicate that the speaker owed or was indebted to someone or something in the past. It implies that the debt has been paid or the obligation has been fulfilled.
