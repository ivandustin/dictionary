Etymology: The word "οφθαλμοδουλεια" comes from the Greek words "οφθαλμός" (ophthalmos) meaning "eye" and "δουλειά" (douleia) meaning "work" or "labor". 

Grammar: "Οφθαλμοδουλεια" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by combining the word for "eye" with the word for "work". 

Description: "Οφθαλμοδουλεια" refers to the act of straining or overworking one's eyes, often due to excessive reading, studying, or staring at screens for long periods of time. It can result in eye fatigue, dryness, and discomfort.
