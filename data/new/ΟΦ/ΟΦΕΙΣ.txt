Etymology: The word "οφεις" (ophis) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "ὄφις" (ophis), which means "snake" or "serpent".

Grammar: In Greek, "οφεις" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Οφεις" refers to snakes in Greek. It is a general term used to describe various species of snakes. Snakes are elongated, legless reptiles that are typically covered in scales. They are found in different habitats worldwide and play important roles in various ecosystems.
