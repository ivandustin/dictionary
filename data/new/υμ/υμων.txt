Word: υμων

Etymology: υμων is a Greek word that is derived from the pronoun "υμεις" (you). It is a possessive form of "υμεις" and means "your" in English.

Grammar: υμων is a second person plural possessive pronoun in Greek. It is used to indicate possession or ownership of something by a group of people. In English, it is equivalent to "your" when addressing a group of people.

Description: υμων is a common word in Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used in formal and informal settings to indicate possession or ownership of something by a group of people. For example, "τα βιβλία υμών" (your books) or "τα παιδιά υμών" (your children).
