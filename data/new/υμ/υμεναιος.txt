Word: υμεναιος

Etymology:
The word υμεναιος (hymenaios) comes from the Greek god of marriage, Hymenaeus. In ancient Greek mythology, Hymenaeus was the god of weddings and marriage ceremonies. The word υμεναιος was used to describe anything related to marriage or weddings.

Grammar:
In Greek, υμεναιος is an adjective that is used to describe something related to marriage or weddings. It is often used to describe the wedding ceremony itself, as well as the bride and groom.
