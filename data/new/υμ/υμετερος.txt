Word: υμετερος

Etymology: υμετερος is a Greek word that comes from the pronoun υμεις (you) and the suffix -τερος (-teros), which is used to indicate comparison or preference. Therefore, υμετερος means "belonging to you" or "yours".

Grammar: υμετερος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to modify a noun and agree with it in gender, number, and case. For example, υμετερος φίλος (your friend).

Description: υμετερος is a possessive adjective that indicates ownership or possession. It is used to refer to something that belongs to the person or people being addressed. In English, it can be translated as "your" or "yours".
