Word: υμετερας

Etymology: υμετερας is a Greek word that comes from the possessive pronoun υμετερος (your) and the suffix -ας, which is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: υμετερας is a feminine singular adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe a feminine singular noun.

Description: υμετερας means "your" in Greek. It is used to indicate possession or ownership of something by the person or people being addressed. For example, if someone says "υμετερας φίλη" (your friend, feminine singular), they are referring to a friend that belongs to the person or people they are speaking to.
