Word: πιστευοντα

Etymology:
The word πιστευοντα (pisteuonta) is the present participle form of the Greek verb πιστεύω (pisteuō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The verb is derived from the noun πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "belief".

Grammar:
πιστευοντα is the present participle form of the verb πιστεύω (pisteuō), which means "to believe". The present participle form indicates that the action of believing is ongoing or continuous. In Greek, the present participle form can also function as an adjective, describing a person or thing that is currently in the act of believing.

Description:
πιστευοντα is a Greek word that describes a person who is currently in the act of believing or having faith. It can be used as an adjective to describe a person who is faithful or trustworthy, or as a verb to describe the act of believing. The word is often used in religious contexts, particularly in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the disciples and followers of Jesus who believed in his teachings.
