Word: πικριας

Etymology:
The word πικριας (pikrias) is derived from the Greek word πικρός (pikros), which means "bitter". It is a noun form of the adjective πικρός.

Grammar:
πικριας is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description:
πικριας refers to bitterness or a bitter taste. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a feeling of bitterness or resentment towards someone or something. The word is often used in the context of food and drink, as well as in emotional and psychological contexts.
