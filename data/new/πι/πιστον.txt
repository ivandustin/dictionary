Word: πιστόν

Etymology:
The word πιστόν comes from the Greek word πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "trust". The suffix -όν (-on) is a neuter noun ending that is often used to form nouns from adjectives or verbs.

πιστόν is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is the accusative case of the word πιστός (pistos), which means "faithful" or "trustworthy". In Greek, the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

πιστόν is a word that is often used in the context of Christianity to refer to the communion bread or the Eucharist. It is also used more generally to refer to any type of bread or loaf. In ancient Greece, the word πιστόν was used to refer to a type of musical instrument that was similar to a lyre.
