Word: πιστεύεις

Etymology: πιστεύεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πίστη (pistis), which means "faith" or "belief". The verb form πιστεύω (pisteuo) means "I believe" or "I have faith", and πιστεύεις (pisteveis) is the second person singular form, meaning "you believe" or "you have faith".

Grammar: πιστεύεις is a present tense verb in the second person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb πιστεύω (pisteuo), which is a regular verb in Greek. The verb is in the indicative mood, which means it is a statement of fact or reality.

Description: πιστεύεις is a word that expresses belief or faith in something. It can be used in a religious context, as in "Do you believe in God?" (Πιστεύεις στον Θεό;), or in a more general sense, as in "Do you believe in love?" (Πιστεύεις στην αγάπη;). It can also be used to express trust or confidence in someone or something, as in "I believe in you" (Πιστεύω σε σένα).
