Word: πιστευοντων

Etymology: πιστευοντων is the present participle form of the Greek verb πιστεύω (pisteuó), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The word comes from the root πιστ- (pist-), which means "faith" or "trust".

Grammar: πιστευοντων is a present participle in the genitive case, which means it functions as an adjective modifying a noun. In this form, it indicates that the noun it modifies is in the genitive case and that the action of believing or having faith is ongoing or continuous.

Description: πιστευοντων can be translated as "believing" or "having faith" in English. It is often used in religious contexts to describe those who have faith in a particular deity or belief system. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe the disciples of Jesus Christ as πιστευοντων εις αυτον (pisteuónton eis auton), meaning "believing in him".
