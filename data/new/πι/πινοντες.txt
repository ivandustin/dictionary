Word: πινοντες

Etymology: πινοντες is the present participle form of the Greek verb πίνω (pino), which means "to drink". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃- ("to drink").

Grammar: πινοντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe the subject of the sentence as currently drinking.

Description: πινοντες can be translated to "drinking" or "those who are drinking". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a scene or setting where people are drinking. For example, "οι πινοντες" (oi pinontes) can be translated to "the drinkers" or "those who are drinking".
