Word: πιστων

Etymology:
The word πιστων is derived from the Greek word πιστός (pistos), which means "faithful" or "trustworthy". It is a form of the adjective πιστός in the genitive case, which indicates possession or association.

Grammar:
πιστων is the genitive singular form of the adjective πιστός. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or association. In this case, πιστων indicates possession or association with something that is faithful or trustworthy.

Description:
πιστων is a Greek word that is often used in religious contexts to describe someone who is faithful or trustworthy. It can also be used more generally to describe someone who is reliable or dependable. The word has a positive connotation and is often used to praise someone for their loyalty or commitment.
