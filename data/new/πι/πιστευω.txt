Word: πιστεύω

Etymology:
The word πιστεύω (pisteuō) is a Greek verb that means "to believe" or "to have faith." It comes from the root word πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "trust."

Grammar:
πιστεύω is a first-person singular present active indicative verb in Greek. It can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects of belief or faith.

Description:
πιστεύω is a fundamental word in Christian theology, as it is used in the New Testament to describe the act of believing in Jesus Christ as the Son of God. It is also used more broadly to describe any act of faith or trust in someone or something. In Greek philosophy, the concept of πίστις (pistis) was also important, as it referred to the idea of trust or confidence in one's beliefs or knowledge.
