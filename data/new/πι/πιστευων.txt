Word: πιστευων

Etymology: πιστευων is the present participle of the Greek verb πιστεύω (pisteuō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The word comes from the root πιστός (pistos), which means "faithful" or "trustworthy".

Grammar: πιστευων is a masculine singular nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently believing or having faith in something.

Description: πιστευων can be translated as "believing" or "having faith" in English. It is often used in religious contexts to describe someone who has faith in God or a particular religious doctrine. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the disciples of Jesus who believed in him and his teachings.
