Word: πιστας

Etymology:
The word πιστας (pistas) is a Greek word that comes from the verb πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The noun form πίστη (pistis) means "faith" or "belief".

Grammar:
πιστας is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender.

Description:
πιστας is a word that is often used in the context of religion or faith. It can refer to a person who is faithful or loyal, or it can refer to the act of having faith or belief in something. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to describe the faith of Christians in God.
