Word: πιασας

Etymology: The word πιασας comes from the Greek verb πιάνω (piáno), which means "to grasp" or "to catch". It is a conjugated form of the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: πιασας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb πιάνω. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: πιασας can be translated to English as "you caught" or "you grasped". It is a verb form that describes a completed action of catching or grasping something in the past. The context of the sentence will determine what was caught or grasped.
