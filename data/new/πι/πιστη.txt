Word: πιστη

Etymology: πιστη (pistē) is a Greek word that means "faith" or "trust". It is derived from the Greek verb πείθω (peithō), which means "to persuade" or "to convince".

Grammar: πιστη is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description: πιστη is a word that is often used in religious contexts to refer to faith or trust in God. It can also be used more broadly to refer to trust or confidence in someone or something. In Greek philosophy, πιστη was considered one of the three main virtues, along with hope (ἐλπίς) and love (ἀγάπη).
