Word: πινω

The word πινω (pinō) is derived from the Ancient Greek verb πίνω (pínō), which means "to drink". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃-, which means "to drink".

πινω is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the verb πίνω (pínō). It is used to indicate that the speaker is currently drinking. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: πίνω (pínō) - I drink
- Imperfect tense: έπινα (épina) - I was drinking
- Future tense: θα πιω (tha piō) - I will drink
- Aorist tense: ήπια (ípia) - I drank
- Perfect tense: έχω πιεί (échō pieí) - I have drunk

πινω is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of drinking. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as when referring to drinking water, wine, or other beverages.
