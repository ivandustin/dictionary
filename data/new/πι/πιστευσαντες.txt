Word: πιστευσαντες

Etymology: πιστευσαντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb πιστεύω (pisteuó), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a participle ending that indicates the action of believing has been completed.

Grammar: πιστευσαντες is the plural form of the aorist active participle of the verb πιστεύω. It is used to describe a group of people who have completed the action of believing. In this form, it functions as an adjective and can be used to modify a noun.

Description: πιστευσαντες is a word that describes a group of people who have believed or had faith in something. It is often used in religious contexts to describe those who have accepted a particular doctrine or belief system. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the disciples of Jesus who believed in his teachings and followed him.
