Word: πιπτοντων

Etymology: πιπτοντων is the present active participle of the Greek verb πίπτω (pipto), which means "to fall". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, which means "to fly, rush, fall".

Grammar: πιπτοντων is a masculine plural nominative present active participle in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently falling.

Description: πιπτοντων can be translated to "falling" or "those who are falling". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a scene of chaos or destruction, where people or objects are falling down.
