Word: πινουσιν

Etymology: πινουσιν is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb πίνω (pino), which means "to drink". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃- ("to drink").

Grammar: πινουσιν is the third person plural form of the present tense in the active voice of the Greek verb πίνω. It is used to describe an action that is currently happening or is habitual.

Description: πινουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they drink". It is often used in everyday conversation to describe the act of drinking. The word can be used to describe any type of liquid consumption, including water, wine, or other beverages.
