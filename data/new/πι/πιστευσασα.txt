Word: πιστευσασα

Etymology: πιστευσασα is a Greek word that comes from the verb πιστεύω (pisteuó), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The suffix -σασα (-sasa) is the aorist tense form of the verb, indicating that the action of believing has already taken place.

Grammar: πιστευσασα is the aorist tense form of the Greek verb πιστεύω (pisteuó), which means "to believe". The aorist tense in Greek indicates a past action that has been completed. The word is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: πιστευσασα is a Greek word that means "having believed" or "having faith". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of believing in Jesus Christ as the Son of God.
