Word: πιστος

Etymology:
The word πιστος (pistos) is derived from the Greek word πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". It is related to the Greek word πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "trust".

Grammar:
πιστος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, genders, and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
πιστος is a Greek word that means "faithful", "trustworthy", or "loyal". It is often used to describe a person who is reliable and can be trusted to keep their word. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe God's faithfulness and the faithfulness of believers.
