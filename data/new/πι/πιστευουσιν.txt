Word: πιστευουσιν

Etymology:
The word πιστευουσιν (pisteuousin) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith." It is derived from the noun πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "belief."

Grammar:
πιστευουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. It is the present active indicative form of the verb πιστεύω (pisteuō), which means "they believe" or "they have faith."

Description:
πιστευουσιν is a word that expresses the act of believing or having faith. It is often used in religious contexts to describe a person's belief in a higher power or in a particular religious doctrine. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is frequently used to describe the belief of the disciples in Jesus Christ. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe a person's trust or confidence in something or someone.
