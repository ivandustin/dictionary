Word: πιασαι

Etymology: The word πιασαι is derived from the Greek verb πιάνω (piáno), which means "to catch" or "to seize".

Grammar: πιασαι is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in the active voice of the Greek verb πιάνω. It is used to address one person and indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: πιασαι is a verb that means "you caught" or "you seized" in English. It is often used in the context of physical actions, such as catching a ball or seizing an object. It can also be used metaphorically to describe capturing an idea or understanding a concept.
