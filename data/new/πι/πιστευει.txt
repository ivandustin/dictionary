Word: πιστεύει (pisteúei)

Etymology: πιστεύει comes from the Greek verb πιστεύω (pisteúō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". It is derived from the noun πίστις (pístis), which means "faith" or "belief".

Grammar: πιστεύει is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb πιστεύω. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person of the subject. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person singular form means that the subject is he/she/it.

Description: πιστεύει is a verb that expresses the act of believing or having faith. It can be used in a religious or spiritual context, as well as in a more general sense of trusting or having confidence in something or someone. For example, one might say "Πιστεύω σε εσένα" (Pisteúō se eséna), which means "I believe in you".
