Word: πιστευσον

Etymology:
The word πιστευσον is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist imperative active tense. It is derived from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuō), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The addition of the suffix -σον (-son) indicates the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or orders.

Grammar:
As mentioned, πιστευσον is a verb form in the aorist imperative active tense. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The imperative mood is used to give commands or orders, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
The word πιστευσον can be translated to English as "believe" or "have faith". It is a command or order, urging the listener or reader to believe in something. In the context of Greek literature, it is often used in religious or philosophical texts to encourage faith in a particular deity or belief system.
