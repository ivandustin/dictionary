Word: πιστεύειν

Etymology: 
The word πιστεύειν (pisteuein) is a Greek verb that means "to believe" or "to have faith". It comes from the Greek word πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "trust".

Grammar:
πιστεύειν is a present tense verb in Greek. It is in the active voice and is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: πιστεύω (pisteuo) - I believe
- Dual: πιστεύομεν (pisteuomen) - We two believe
- Plural: πιστεύομεν (pisteuomen) - We believe

Description:
πιστεύειν is a fundamental concept in Christianity, as it refers to the act of having faith in God. It is also used in a more general sense to refer to believing in something or someone. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the act of believing in Jesus Christ as the Son of God. In Greek philosophy, the word is used to describe the act of trusting in reason and logic.
