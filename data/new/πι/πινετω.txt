Word: πινετω

Etymology: πινετω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and imperative mood in Greek. It comes from the root word πίνω (pino), which means "to drink."

Grammar: πινετω is a second-person singular imperative verb, which means it is a command directed at one person. It is used to tell someone to drink.

Description: πινετω is a simple and straightforward verb that means "drink." It is often used in everyday conversation and is a common command given at meals or when offering someone a drink. It is a regular verb and follows the standard conjugation pattern for Greek verbs.
