Word: πιστευσας

Etymology: πιστευσας is a Greek word that comes from the verb πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". It is a past tense form of the verb, meaning "you believed".

Grammar: πιστευσας is a second person singular past tense form of the verb πιστεύω. It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The word is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject (you) performed the action of believing.

Description: πιστευσας is a word that expresses the act of believing or having faith in something. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, but can also be used in a more general sense to describe belief in something or someone. The word carries a sense of conviction and trust, and implies a willingness to accept something as true or real.
