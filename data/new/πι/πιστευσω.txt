Word: πιστεύω

Etymology: The word πιστεύω (pisteuō) comes from the Greek verb πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "belief". The verb πιστεύω means "to believe" or "to have faith".

Grammar: πιστεύω is a first-person singular present active indicative verb in Greek. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and persons, such as πιστεύεις (pisteueis) for second-person singular, πιστεύει (pisteuei) for third-person singular, πιστεύομεν (pisteuomen) for first-person plural, πιστεύετε (pisteuete) for second-person plural, and πιστεύουσι(ν) (pisteuousi(n)) for third-person plural.

Description: πιστεύω is a common Greek verb that is used to express belief or faith in something or someone. It can be used in a religious context, such as believing in God, or in a more general sense, such as believing in a person's abilities or trusting in a particular idea or concept. The verb is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the act of believing in Jesus Christ as the Son of God.
