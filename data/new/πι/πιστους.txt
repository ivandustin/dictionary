Word: πιστους

Etymology:
The word πιστους (pistous) is a Greek adjective that means "faithful" or "loyal". It is derived from the Greek noun πίστις (pistis), which means "faith" or "trust".

Grammar:
πιστους is the masculine accusative plural form of the adjective πιστός (pistos), which means "faithful" or "loyal". It is used to describe a group of people who are faithful or loyal to someone or something.

πιστους is a word that is often used in the context of religion or faith. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used to describe the disciples of Jesus who were faithful to him.
