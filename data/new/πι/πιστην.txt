The word "πιστην" (pistin) is a Greek noun that means "faith" or "belief". It is derived from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". In terms of grammar, "πιστην" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

In Christian theology, "πιστην" is often used to refer to the act of believing in God or in the teachings of Jesus Christ. It is a central concept in the New Testament, where it is often translated as "faith" or "belief". 

Overall, "πιστην" is a word that conveys the idea of trust, confidence, and conviction in something or someone. It is a fundamental concept in many religious and philosophical traditions, and is often associated with the idea of hope and salvation.
