Word: πιστικης

Etymology: The word πιστικης comes from the Greek word πίστη (písti), which means "faith" or "trust". The suffix -ικης (-ikēs) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives, indicating "related to" or "pertaining to".

Grammar: πιστικης is an adjective in the feminine gender, singular form. It is declined according to the first declension in Greek grammar.

Description: πιστικης is an adjective that describes something as "faithful" or "trustworthy". It can be used to describe a person, an object, or an action that is reliable and dependable. In Greek Orthodox Christianity, the term is often used to describe the faithful members of the church.
