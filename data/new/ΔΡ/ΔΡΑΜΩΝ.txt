Etymology: The word "δραμων" (dramon) comes from the Greek verb "δραμεῖν" (dramein), which means "to run."

Grammar: In Greek, "δραμων" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "δραμονος" (dramonos).

Description: "Δραμων" is an ancient Greek word that refers to a runner or a racer. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is quick or swift in their actions or movements.
