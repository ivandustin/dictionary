Word: τηρησαι

Etymology:
The word τηρησαι (tērēsai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, or observe." The suffix -σαι (-sai) is the aorist tense ending, indicating that the action of the verb is completed in the past.

Grammar:
τηρησαι (tērēsai) is a second-person singular aorist active imperative verb in the Greek language. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to keep, guard, or observe something. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb is completed in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
τηρησαι (tērēsai) is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible. It is used to instruct or command someone to keep or guard something, such as a commandment or a teaching. For example, in John 14:15, Jesus says, "If you love me, you will keep my commandments" (τὰς ἐντολὰς μου τηρήσετε, tas entolas mou tērēsete). In this context, τηρησαι (tērēsai) is used to emphasize the importance of obeying Jesus' teachings as a sign of love for him.
