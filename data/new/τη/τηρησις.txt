Word: τηρησις

Etymology:
The word τηρησις (tērēsis) is derived from the Greek verb τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, observe, or maintain." It is related to the noun τέρας (téras), which means "a wonder, marvel, or portent."

Grammar:
τηρησις is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a third declension noun in Greek, which means that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description:
τηρησις refers to the act of keeping or guarding something, or the state of being kept or guarded. It can also refer to the observance or maintenance of a particular practice or tradition. In a broader sense, it can refer to the preservation or protection of something valuable or important. The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as the "keeping" of God's commandments or the "guarding" of one's soul.
