Word: τηρησουσιν

Etymology:
The word τηρησουσιν is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form of the verb τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, observe, or maintain." The root of the word is τηρ- (tēr-), which means "to watch over, guard, or preserve."

Grammar:
τηρησουσιν is a future tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, voice, and person of the subject. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
τηρησουσιν is a verb that means "they will keep, guard, observe, or maintain." It is often used in the context of keeping or observing a law, commandment, or tradition. The word can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to watch over, protect, or preserve."
