The word "τηρουμεν" is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "τηρέω" which means "to keep, guard, or observe." 

In terms of grammar, "τηρουμεν" is conjugated in the third person plural, indicating that the subject is a group of people or things. The passive voice suggests that the subject is receiving the action rather than performing it. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact or reality.

Overall, "τηρουμεν" can be translated to mean "they are being kept/guarded/observed."
