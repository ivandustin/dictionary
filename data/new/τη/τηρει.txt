τηρει (tērei) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word τηρέω (tēreō), which means "to guard, keep, or observe." 

In terms of grammar, τηρει is a regular verb in the present tense, following the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in -ει. It is also an active verb, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of guarding, keeping, or observing.

Example sentence: Ο πατέρας μου τηρεί πάντα την υπόσχεσή του. (O patéras mou tērei pánta tin ypóschesi tou.) - "My father always keeps his promise."
