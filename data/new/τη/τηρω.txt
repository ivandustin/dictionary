Word: τηρω

Etymology:
The word τηρω (tērō) is a Greek verb that means "to keep, to guard, to observe, to maintain, to preserve." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter- which means "to watch, to guard."

Grammar:
τηρω is a first conjugation verb in Greek, which means that it follows the regular pattern of verbs that end in -ω. It is an active verb, which means that the subject of the sentence performs the action of the verb. It can be conjugated in all tenses, moods, and voices.

Description:
τηρω is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physical actions such as guarding or preserving something, as well as more abstract actions such as observing or maintaining a certain behavior or attitude.
