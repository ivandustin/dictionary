Word: τηρουμενους

Etymology: τηρουμενους is a Greek word that comes from the verb τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, or observe." The word is in the present participle form, which means "being kept" or "being guarded."

Grammar: τηρουμενους is a present participle in the masculine gender, accusative case, and plural number. It is used as an adjective to modify a noun or pronoun.

Description: τηρουμενους is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in 1 Peter 1:5, where it describes believers who are "kept by the power of God through faith." The word implies a sense of protection and preservation, as if the believers are being guarded by God.
