Word: τηρηθηναι

Etymology:
The word τηρηθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to guard, keep, or observe." The suffix -θηναι (-thēnai) is a passive form that indicates the action of being kept or guarded.

Grammar:
τηρηθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -θηναι to the stem τηρη-, which is the aorist tense stem of the verb τηρέω. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description:
The word τηρηθηναι means "to be kept, guarded, or observed." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to the observance of laws, commandments, or rituals. In the New Testament, for example, it is used to describe the keeping of the Sabbath day or the observance of the Mosaic law. The word can also be used more generally to refer to the act of preserving or protecting something, such as a tradition, a language, or a cultural practice.
