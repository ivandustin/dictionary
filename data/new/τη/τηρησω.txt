Word: τηρησω

Etymology:
The word τηρησω (tērēsō) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, or observe." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter- meaning "to watch over, guard, or preserve."

Grammar:
τηρησω is a first-person singular future active indicative form of the verb τηρέω (tēréō). It is translated as "I will keep" or "I will guard." The verb is a regular verb in the Greek language and follows the conjugation pattern of the first conjugation.

Description:
τηρησω is a verb that expresses the action of keeping or guarding something. It can be used in various contexts, such as keeping a promise, guarding a secret, or observing a law. The word emphasizes the importance of being vigilant and careful in preserving something valuable.
