Word: τηρησει

Etymology:
The word τηρησει (tērēsei) is a Greek verb in the future tense, which means "to keep, to guard, to observe, to maintain." It comes from the root word τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to watch, to guard, to keep safe."

Grammar:
τηρησει (tērēsei) is a third-person singular future active indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense ending -σει (-sei) to the stem τηρη- (tērē-). The verb is conjugated as follows:

τηρησει (tērēsei) - he/she/it will keep/guard/observe/maintain

Description:
τηρησει (tērēsei) is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to keeping or guarding physical objects, such as a treasure or a city. It can also refer to observing or following rules, laws, or customs. Additionally, it can be used to describe maintaining a certain state or condition, such as good health or a clean environment. Overall, τηρησει (tērēsei) is a useful verb that conveys the idea of preserving or protecting something important.
