τηρησηις (tērēsis) is a Greek noun that means "keeping, guarding, preservation, observance, or custody." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, or observe." 

Grammar: τηρησηις is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. 

Description: The word is often used in a religious or legal context to refer to the act of keeping or observing laws, rules, or traditions. It can also refer to the act of preserving or guarding something valuable or important.
