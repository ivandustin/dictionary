τηρειν (tērein) is a Greek verb that means "to keep, to guard, to observe, to maintain, to preserve". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter- which means "to watch, to guard". It is related to the Latin word "terere" which means "to rub, to wear down" and the English word "terrace" which originally referred to a raised platform for watching or guarding.

Grammar: τηρειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation and has a stem of τηρε-. Its conjugation in the present tense is:

- Singular: τηρω (tērō) - I keep, you keep, he/she/it keeps
- Plural: τηρουμεν (tēroumen) - we keep, you keep, they keep

Description: τηρειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can refer to physical guarding or keeping, such as guarding a city or keeping a watchful eye on something. It can also refer to observing rules or customs, such as keeping a promise or observing a religious ritual. Additionally, it can refer to maintaining or preserving something, such as keeping a garden or preserving a historical artifact.
