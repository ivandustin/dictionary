τηρησιν (tērēsin) is a Greek noun that means "keeping", "guarding", or "observance". 

Etymology: The word comes from the verb τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep", "to guard", or "to observe". 

Grammar: τηρησιν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. 

Description: The word is often used in a religious or moral context, referring to the act of keeping or observing commandments, laws, or traditions. It can also refer to the act of guarding or protecting something.
