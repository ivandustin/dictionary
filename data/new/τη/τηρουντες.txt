τηρουντες (tērountes) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "keeping" or "guarding". It is derived from the root word τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep", "to guard", or "to observe". In grammar, it is a present active participle, which means it describes an ongoing action in the present tense. 

Example sentence: Οι μαθητές τηρούντες τους κανόνες του σχολείου. (Oi mathites tērountes tous kanones tou scholeiou) - The students who are keeping the school rules.
