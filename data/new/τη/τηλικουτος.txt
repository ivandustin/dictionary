Word: τηλικουτος

Etymology:
The word τηλικουτος is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: τηλικος (telikos) meaning "so great" or "so much" and ουτος (outos) meaning "this". Therefore, τηλικουτος can be translated as "so much this" or "such a great thing".

Grammar:
In Greek, τηλικουτος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
The word τηλικουτος is used to describe something that is so great or so much that it is difficult to express in words. It can be used to describe a person, an object, or an event that is of great significance or importance. For example, τηλικουτος can be used to describe a great achievement, a momentous occasion, or a remarkable person.
