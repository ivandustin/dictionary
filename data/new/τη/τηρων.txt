Word: τηρων

Etymology:
The word τηρων (tērōn) is the present participle of the Greek verb τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, observe, maintain, preserve". The verb is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter-, which means "to watch, guard, observe".

Description:
τηρων is a present participle that describes someone or something that is currently keeping, guarding, observing, maintaining, or preserving. It can be used as an adjective to modify a noun, or as a noun itself to refer to the person or thing doing the action. For example, in the sentence "Ο τηρων την πόλιν είναι πολύ σημαντικός" (The one who guards the city is very important), τηρων is used as a noun to refer to the person who is guarding the city.
