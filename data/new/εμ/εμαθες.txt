Word: εμαθες

Etymology: εμαθες is a verb in the Greek language, which comes from the root word μαθαίνω (mathaino), meaning "to learn."

Grammar: εμαθες is the second person singular past tense of the verb μαθαίνω (mathaino), which means "you learned" in English. It is conjugated in the aorist tense.

Description: εμαθες is a verb that indicates that the subject (you) has learned something in the past. It is commonly used in everyday conversation to express the idea of acquiring knowledge or information.
