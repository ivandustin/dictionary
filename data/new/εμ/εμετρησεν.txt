Word: εμετρησεν

Etymology: The word εμετρησεν comes from the Greek verb μετρέω (metréo), which means "to measure" or "to count". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so εμετρησεν means "he/she/it measured" or "he/she/it counted".

Grammar: εμετρησεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb (he/she/it) performed the action.

Description: εμετρησεν is a verb that describes the act of measuring or counting. It is often used in a literal sense, such as measuring a distance or counting objects, but it can also be used figuratively, such as measuring someone's worth or counting one's blessings. In the context of Greek literature, εμετρησεν appears in various works, including the New Testament, where it is used to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples.
