Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root words "moichos" (adulterer) and "moicheia" (adultery).

Grammar: "εμοιχευσεν" is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek.

Description: The word is used in Greek literature and mythology to describe instances of infidelity and betrayal. It is often associated with negative connotations and is considered a serious offense in Greek culture.
