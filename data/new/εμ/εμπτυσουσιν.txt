Word: εμπτυσουσιν

Etymology:
The word εμπτυσουσιν is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the root word "πτύω" (ptýo), which means "to spit". The prefix "εμ-" (em-) is added to the root word to indicate intensity or emphasis.

Grammar:
εμπτυσουσιν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people or things.

Description:
The word εμπτυσουσιν means "they spit intensely" or "they spit with force". It is a strong and vivid verb that conveys a sense of forceful expulsion of saliva or other substances. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as when someone spits on the ground, or in a figurative sense, such as when someone expresses strong disapproval or contempt.
