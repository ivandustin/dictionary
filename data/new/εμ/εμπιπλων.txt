The word "εμπιπλων" is a Greek verb that means "to furnish" or "to equip". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "εμπίπλημι" (empíplēmi), which means "to fill up" or "to supply". 

Grammar: "Εμπιπλών" is the present participle form of the verb, which means it describes an ongoing action. It is often used in combination with the auxiliary verb "είμαι" (eimai) to form the present continuous tense, such as "Είμαι εμπιπλώνοντας το δωμάτιο" (I am furnishing the room).

Description: The word "εμπιπλών" is commonly used in everyday Greek language to describe the act of furnishing or equipping a space, such as a room or a house. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of providing someone with the necessary tools or resources to accomplish a task.
