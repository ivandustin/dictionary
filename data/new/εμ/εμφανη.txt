Word: εμφανη

Etymology: The word εμφανη comes from the Greek word "εμφαίνω" (emfaino), which means "to show" or "to reveal". It is derived from the prefix "εμ-" (em-), which means "in" or "into", and the verb "φαίνω" (faino), which means "to appear" or "to shine".

Grammar: εμφανη is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The feminine form of the word is εμφανής (emfanis), and the neuter form is εμφανές (emfanes).

Description: The word εμφανη is used to describe something that is visible, apparent, or evident. It can also be used to describe someone who is well-known or famous. For example, "Η εμφανής αλλαγή στον καιρό έφερε βροχή" (The apparent change in the weather brought rain) or "Ο εμφανής ηθοποιός έπαιξε σε πολλές ταινίες" (The well-known actor played in many movies).
