Word: εμπλησθω

Etymology:
The word εμπλησθω comes from the Greek verb "πληθω" (plitho), which means "to fill". The prefix "εμ-" (em-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate a sense of "being filled with" or "being full of".

Grammar:
εμπλησθω is a passive verb in the aorist tense, which means that the action of being filled is happening to the subject of the sentence. It is also in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the recipient and the agent of the action.

Description:
The word εμπλησθω means "to be filled with" or "to be full of". It can be used to describe a physical state of being filled, such as being full of food or drink, or a more abstract state of being filled with emotion or knowledge. The word can also be used in a metaphorical sense, such as being filled with joy or hope.
