Word: εμος

Etymology: εμος is a Greek word that means "mine" or "my own". It is derived from the Greek word "εγώ" (ego), which means "I".

Grammar: εμος is a pronoun in Greek, which means it is used to replace a noun. It is in the first person singular, meaning it refers to the speaker or writer.

Description: εμος is a possessive pronoun that is used to indicate ownership or possession. It is commonly used in Greek to express possession of an object or a person. For example, "Αυτό είναι το βιβλίο μου" (This is my book) or "Αυτός είναι ο γιος μου" (He is my son).
