Word: εμνησθη

Etymology:
The word εμνησθη comes from the Greek verb μιμνήσκω (mimnisko), which means "to remember" or "to recall". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate a past tense form.

Grammar:
εμνησθη is a third person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb μιμνήσκω (mimnisko). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. Therefore, εμνησθη can be translated as "he/she/it was remembered/recalled".

Description:
εμνησθη is a word that describes an action that has already taken place in the past. It is often used in narratives or historical accounts to indicate that something or someone was remembered or recalled at a particular point in time. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of remembering or recalling something from the past.
