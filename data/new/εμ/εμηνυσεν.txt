Word: εμηνυσεν

Etymology:
The word εμηνυσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μηνύω (mēnúō), which means "to announce" or "to declare". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate the past tense, making εμηνυσεν (emēnysen) mean "he/she/it announced" or "he/she/it declared".

Grammar:
εμηνυσεν is a third-person singular past tense verb in the active voice. In Greek, verbs are inflected to indicate tense, voice, mood, person, and number. The past tense indicates that the action took place in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. 

Description:
εμηνυσεν is a verb that is used to describe an action of announcing or declaring something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in legal proceedings, political speeches, or personal conversations. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is an important part of the Greek language.
