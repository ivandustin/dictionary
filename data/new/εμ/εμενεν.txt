The word "εμενεν" (emenen) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular, past tense, indicative mood of the verb "μένω" (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain". 

Etymology: The word "μένω" (meno) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- which means "to remain, stay". 

Grammar: "εμενεν" (emenen) is a regular verb in the past tense, indicative mood. In Greek, verbs change their endings depending on the tense, mood, and person. 

Description: "εμενεν" (emenen) indicates that someone or something stayed or remained in a certain place or condition in the past. For example, "Ο Πέτρος εμενεν στο σπίτι του χθες το βράδυ" (O Petros emenen sto spiti tou hthes to vradi) means "Peter stayed at his house last night".
