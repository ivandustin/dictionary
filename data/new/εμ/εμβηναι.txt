Word: εμβηναι

Etymology:
The word εμβηναι comes from the Greek verb "εμβαίνω" (embaíno), which means "to step into" or "to board". It is derived from the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into", and the verb "βαίνω" (baíno), meaning "to step" or "to go".

Grammar:
εμβηναι is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
The word εμβηναι refers to the act of stepping into or boarding something, such as a vehicle or a ship. It can also be used metaphorically to describe entering into a situation or taking on a new role or responsibility.
