The word "εμπτυειν" (emptyein) is a Greek verb that means "to empty out" or "to pour out." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "πτυ-" (pty-), which means "to spit" or "to eject." The prefix "εμ-" (em-) adds the meaning of "out" or "away," giving the word its sense of "emptying out."

Grammar: "Εμπτυειν" is a present active infinitive verb, meaning it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of emptying out. It can be conjugated to match the tense, mood, and voice of the sentence.

Description: The word "εμπτυειν" is often used in a literal sense to describe the act of pouring out a liquid or emptying a container. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of releasing or letting go of something, such as emotions or thoughts.
