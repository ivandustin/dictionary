Word: εμβαπτομενος

Etymology: 
The word εμβαπτομενος comes from the Greek verb εμβαπτω (embapto) which means "to dip in, to immerse". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action is ongoing or continuous. Therefore, εμβαπτομενος means "being continuously immersed or dipped in".

Grammar:
εμβαπτομενος is a present participle in the passive voice. It is formed by adding the suffix -μενος to the present stem of the verb εμβαπτω. The word agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
εμβαπτομενος is an adjective that describes something or someone that is continuously immersed or dipped in a liquid. It can be used to describe a person who is being baptized or a material that is being soaked in a solution. The word emphasizes the ongoing nature of the action, suggesting that it is not a one-time event but a continuous process.
