Word: εμμαινομενος

Etymology: 
The word εμμαινομενος comes from the Greek verb μαίνομαι (mainomai), which means "to rage" or "to be mad". The prefix εμ- (em-) is an intensifier, so εμμαινομενος means "very mad" or "furious".

Grammar:
εμμαινομενος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the present participle of the verb μαίνομαι (mainomai), which means "to rage" or "to be mad". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is added to the stem of the verb to form the present participle.

Description:
εμμαινομενος describes someone who is extremely angry or furious. It can also be used to describe someone who is acting in a frenzied or irrational manner. The word is often used in a dramatic or poetic context to convey intense emotion.
