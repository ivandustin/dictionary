Word: εμαρτυρησεν

Etymology: The word εμαρτυρησεν comes from the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the third person singular aorist active indicative tense.

Grammar: εμαρτυρησεν is a third person singular aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: εμαρτυρησεν is a Greek verb form that means "he/she/it bore witness" or "he/she/it testified". It is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of bearing witness or testifying about something, particularly in a legal or religious context.
