Word: εμαθητευθη

Etymology:
The word εμαθητευθη comes from the Greek verb μαθητεύω (mathitevo), which means "to teach, instruct, train". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a passive voice, so εμαθητευθη means "was taught, was instructed, was trained".

Grammar:
εμαθητευθη is a third person singular aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description:
εμαθητευθη is a verb that describes the action of being taught or instructed. It is often used in a religious context to describe the disciples of Jesus being trained by him. The word emphasizes the passive nature of the disciples' learning, as they were being taught by someone else rather than actively seeking knowledge on their own.
