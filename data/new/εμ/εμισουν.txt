Word: εμισουν

Etymology: The word εμισουν comes from the Greek verb μισέω (miseo), which means "to hate" or "to detest". It is derived from the ancient Greek word μῖσος (misos), which means "hatred".

Grammar: εμισουν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb μισέω (miseo) in the active voice. It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past by a group of people who hated or detested something or someone.

Description: εμισουν is a Greek verb that expresses a strong feeling of hatred or detestation towards something or someone. It is often used in a negative context to describe a past action or event that was disliked or despised by a group of people. For example, "Οι Έλληνες εμίσουν τους Πέρσες" (The Greeks hated the Persians) or "Οι φίλοι μου εμίσουν την ιδέα μου να φύγω" (My friends detested my idea of leaving).
