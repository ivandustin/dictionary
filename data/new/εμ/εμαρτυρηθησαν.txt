Word: εμαρτυρηθησαν

Etymology: The word εμαρτυρηθησαν comes from the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is in the passive voice, meaning "they were testified" or "they were witnessed".

Grammar: εμαρτυρηθησαν is a third-person plural passive form of the verb μαρτυρέω (martureo). It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence (in this case, an unspecified group of people) received the action of being testified or witnessed.

Description: εμαρτυρηθησαν is a Greek word that means "they were testified" or "they were witnessed". It is often used in a legal or judicial context to describe the act of giving testimony or bearing witness to something. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the testimony of the apostles and other witnesses to the life and teachings of Jesus Christ.
