Word: εμφανιζειν

Etymology: 
The word εμφανιζειν (emphanizein) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εμφανής (emphanis), which means "visible" or "apparent". The suffix -ιζειν (-izein) is a common Greek verb ending that indicates the act of making something or someone do or be something.

Grammar:
εμφανιζειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem εμφανιζ- (emphaniz-). The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

εμφανίζω (emphanizo) - I appear
εμφανίζεις (emphanizeis) - you appear
εμφανίζει (emphanizei) - he/she/it appears
εμφανίζουμε (emphanizoume) - we appear
εμφανίζετε (emphanizete) - you (plural) appear
εμφανίζουν (emphanizoun) - they appear

Description:
The verb εμφανιζειν means "to make visible" or "to reveal". It is often used in the context of making something or someone visible or apparent, such as revealing a hidden object or making a person's emotions or intentions clear. The verb can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to show" or "to display".
