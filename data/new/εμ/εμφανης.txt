Word: εμφανής

Etymology:
The word εμφανής (emfanis) comes from the Greek root words εμφαίνω (emfaino) which means "to show" or "to appear" and the suffix -ής (-is) which is used to form adjectives. Therefore, εμφανής means "visible" or "apparent".

Grammar:
εμφανής is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
εμφανής is used to describe something that is clearly visible or apparent. It can be used to describe a physical object or a concept. For example, "Ο ήλιος είναι εμφανής στον ουρανό" (The sun is visible in the sky) or "Η ανησυχία ήταν εμφανής στο πρόσωπό της" (The anxiety was apparent on her face).
