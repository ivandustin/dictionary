The word "εμπαιγμων" (empaigmon) is a Greek adjective that means "mocking" or "ridiculing". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "παίζω" (paizo), which means "to play". The prefix "εμ-" (em-) is added to the verb to create the adjective form, which means "playing with" or "mocking".

Grammar: "Εμπαιγμων" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: The word "εμπαιγμων" is often used to describe someone who is making fun of or mocking another person. It can also be used to describe a situation or event that is meant to be humorous or satirical.
