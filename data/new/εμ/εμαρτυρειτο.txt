Word: εμαρτυρειτο

Etymology: 
The word εμαρτυρειτο comes from the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo), which means "to bear witness" or "to testify". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the third person singular form of the imperfect tense.

Grammar:
εμαρτυρειτο is the third person singular form of the imperfect tense of the Greek verb μαρτυρέω (martureo). The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. In this case, εμαρτυρειτο means "he/she/it was testifying" or "he/she/it used to testify".

Description:
εμαρτυρειτο is a Greek verb that describes the act of bearing witness or testifying. The word is in the imperfect tense, indicating that the action of testifying was ongoing or repeated in the past.
