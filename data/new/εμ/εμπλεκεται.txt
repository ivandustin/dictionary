Word: εμπλέκεται

Etymology: The word εμπλέκεται comes from the Greek verb "εμπλέκω" (em-ple-ko), which means "to entangle" or "to involve". It is derived from the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into", and the verb "πλέκω" (ple-ko), meaning "to weave" or "to braid".

Grammar: εμπλέκεται is a present tense, third person singular form of the verb εμπλέκω. It is in the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εμπλέκω (em-ple-ko) - I entangle
εμπλέκεις (em-ple-keis) - you entangle
εμπλέκει (em-ple-ki) - he/she/it entangles
εμπλέκουμε (em-ple-koume) - we entangle
εμπλέκετε (em-ple-kete) - you entangle
εμπλέκουν (em-ple-koun) - they entangle

Description: εμπλέκεται means "is involved" or "is entangled" in Greek. It is often used to describe a person or situation that is caught up in a complicated or difficult situation. For example, "Ο πολιτικός εμπλέκεται σε ένα σκάνδαλο διαφθοράς" (The politician is involved in a corruption scandal). The verb can also be used in a more literal sense, such as "Η κλωστή εμπλέκεται στον αργαλειό" (The thread is entangled in the loom).
