Word: εμνησθημεν

Etymology:
The word εμνησθημεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μνημονεύω (mnēmoneuō), which means "to remember." The prefix εμ- (em-) is a form of the Greek preposition εν (en), which means "in" or "within." Therefore, εμνησθημεν can be translated as "we remembered" or "we were reminded."

Grammar:
εμνησθημεν is a first-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Ancient Greek. The aorist tense indicates a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description:
εμνησθημεν is a word that expresses the act of remembering or being reminded of something. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used to describe the disciples' remembrance of Jesus' teachings. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of recalling something from memory.
