Word: εμμενειν

Etymology:
The word εμμενειν comes from the Greek verb μένω (meno), which means "to remain" or "to stay". The prefix εμ- (em-) adds emphasis to the verb, giving it the meaning of "to remain steadfast" or "to persevere".

Grammar:
εμμενειν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the present active infinitive form. It is often used in the context of endurance or perseverance, and is commonly translated as "to persevere" or "to endure".

Description:
εμμενειν is a word that describes the act of remaining steadfast and persevering through difficult circumstances. It is often used in a moral or ethical context, to describe the determination and resilience required to overcome challenges and achieve one's goals. The word emphasizes the importance of persistence and endurance, even in the face of adversity.
