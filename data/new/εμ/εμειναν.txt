Word: εμειναν

Etymology: εμειναν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μένω (meno), which means "to stay" or "to remain."

Grammar: εμειναν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb μένω (meno). It is used to describe an action that happened in the past and was completed by a group of people.

Description: εμειναν can be translated to English as "they stayed" or "they remained." It is often used to describe a group of people who stayed in a particular place for a period of time. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου εμείναν στο σπίτι μου για το Σαββατοκύριακο" (My friends stayed at my house for the weekend).
