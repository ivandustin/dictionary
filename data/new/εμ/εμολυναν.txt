Word: εμολυναν

Etymology: The word εμολυναν comes from the Greek verb μολύνω (molyno), which means "to pollute" or "to defile". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is in the past tense and that the subject is plural.

Grammar: εμολυναν is a third-person plural past tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated from the verb μολύνω (molyno) and is used to describe an action that was performed by a group of people in the past.

Description: εμολυναν can be translated to mean "they polluted" or "they defiled". It is often used in a negative context to describe an action that has caused harm or damage to something or someone.
