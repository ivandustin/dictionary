The word "εμβαντες" (embantes) is a Greek noun that means "soldiers who embark on a ship." 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "εμβαίνω" (emvaino), which means "to embark" or "to board a ship." The suffix "-της" (-tes) is added to the verb stem to form a noun indicating a person who performs the action of the verb.

Grammar: "Εμβαντες" is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: The word "εμβαντες" refers to soldiers who board a ship for military purposes. It was commonly used in ancient Greece, especially during the time of the Persian Wars and the Peloponnesian War. The soldiers who embarked on a ship were usually part of a naval fleet or an expeditionary force. The term can also be used metaphorically to refer to people who are ready to embark on a new venture or mission.
