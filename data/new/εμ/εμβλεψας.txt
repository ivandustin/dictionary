Word: εμβλεψας

Etymology:
The word εμβλεψας comes from the Greek verb "εμβλέπω" (emblepō), which means "to look into" or "to gaze at". It is formed by combining the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into", and the verb "βλέπω" (blepō), meaning "to see" or "to look".

Grammar:
εμβλεψας is a second person singular aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb "εμβλέπω" (emblepō). In this form, the verb indicates that the action was completed in the past and was performed by the subject (you). 

Description:
εμβλεψας can be translated to English as "you looked into" or "you gazed at". It implies a deliberate and focused act of looking, rather than a casual glance. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as looking into a mirror or gazing at a beautiful view, or in a figurative sense, such as looking into someone's eyes to understand their emotions or gazing at a work of art to appreciate its beauty.
