Word: εμπεσειν

Etymology:
The word εμπεσειν comes from the Greek verb "εμπίπτω" (empípto), which means "to fall into" or "to come upon". It is derived from the prefix "εν-" (en-), meaning "in" or "into", and the verb "πίπτω" (pípto), meaning "to fall".

Grammar:
εμπεσειν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense. It is conjugated from the root verb "εμπίπτω" (empípto), which means "to fall into" or "to come upon". The verb is often used in the passive voice, as in "εμπέσω" (empéso), which means "I fall into" or "I come upon".

Description:
The word εμπεσειν describes the act of falling into or coming upon something unexpectedly. It can be used in a literal sense, such as falling into a hole or stumbling upon an object, or in a figurative sense, such as stumbling upon a solution to a problem or coming across an unexpected opportunity.
