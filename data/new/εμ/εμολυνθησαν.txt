The word εμολυνθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μολύνω (molyno), which means "to pollute" or "to defile". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the action is done to oneself, so εμολυνθησαν means "they polluted themselves" or "they defiled themselves".

εμολυνθησαν is a third-person plural aorist passive indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

εμολυνθησαν is a strong word that conveys a sense of moral or spiritual pollution.
