Word: ζωσαν

Etymology:
ζωσαν is the third person plural aorist active indicative form of the Greek verb ζώννυμι (zónnumi), which means "to gird" or "to fasten with a belt". The word is derived from the noun ζώνη (zónē), which means "a belt" or "a girdle".

Grammar:
ζωσαν is a verb form in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description:
ζωσαν can be translated as "they girded" or "they fastened with a belt". It is often used in the context of preparing for battle or physical activity, as soldiers and athletes would often wear belts to support their bodies. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe mental or emotional preparation for a task or challenge.
