The word ζωνην (zonēn) is a Greek noun that means "belt" or "girdle". It is derived from the verb ζώννυμι (zōnnumi), which means "to gird" or "to fasten with a belt". In terms of grammar, ζωνην is a singular accusative form of the noun, which means it is used to indicate the direct object of a sentence. The word can be used to describe a variety of belts, including those worn for fashion or for practical purposes such as holding up clothing or weapons.
