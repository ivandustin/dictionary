ζωης (zoēs) is a Greek word that means "life" or "living". It is derived from the Greek verb ζῶ (zō), which means "to live". In terms of grammar, ζωης is a noun in the genitive case, which is often used to indicate possession or relationship. 

In Greek philosophy, ζωης was often used to refer to the essence or quality of life, as opposed to mere existence. It was also used in religious contexts to refer to eternal life or the afterlife. 

Overall, ζωης is a versatile and important word in Greek language and culture, with a rich history and meaning.
