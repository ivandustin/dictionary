Etymology: The word "υγιαινοντες" (pronounced "ygeiandes") comes from the Greek verb "υγιαινω" (pronounced "ygeaino"), which means "to be healthy" or "to be in good health". The suffix "-ντες" (-ntes) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action of being healthy is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Υγιαινοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "υγιαινω" in Greek. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Υγιαινοντες" is the plural form of the present participle, and it translates to "those who are healthy" or "the healthy ones" in English. It refers to a group of people who are currently in a state of good health.
