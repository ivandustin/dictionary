Word: εδεξατο

Etymology: The word εδεξατο comes from the Greek verb δέχομαι (dekhomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar: εδεξατο is a third-person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb δέχομαι. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εδεξατο can be translated to English as "he/she/it received" or "he/she/it accepted". It is a simple past tense form of the verb δέχομαι, which is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of receiving or accepting something. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as receiving a gift, accepting an invitation, or welcoming someone into a home or community.
