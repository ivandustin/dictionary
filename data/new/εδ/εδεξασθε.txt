Word: εδεξασθε

Etymology: 
The word εδεξασθε comes from the Greek verb δέχομαι (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is in the aorist tense, which refers to a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
εδεξασθε is the second person plural aorist passive imperative form of the verb δέχομαι (dechomai). This means that it is a command directed at a group of people to receive or accept something. The passive voice indicates that the action is being done to the subject of the verb, rather than the subject performing the action themselves.

Description:
εδεξασθε is a word that is used in Greek to give a command to a group of people to receive or accept something. It is a formal and polite way of making a request, and is often used in official or professional settings. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in business transactions, hospitality, or social situations.
