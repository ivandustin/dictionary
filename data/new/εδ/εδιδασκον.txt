Word: εδιδασκον

Etymology:
The word εδιδασκον comes from the Greek verb διδασκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The prefix ε- (e-) is a common augment used in ancient Greek to indicate past tense.

Grammar:
εδιδασκον is the third person plural, imperfect indicative active form of the verb διδασκω (didasko). This form indicates an ongoing action in the past, meaning "they were teaching" or "they used to teach".

Description:
εδιδασκον is a verb that describes the action of teaching or instructing. In the context of ancient Greek literature, it is often used to describe the actions of teachers, philosophers, or other learned individuals. The imperfect tense suggests that the action of teaching was ongoing or repeated in the past.
