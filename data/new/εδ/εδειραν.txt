Word: εδειραν

Etymology: The word εδειραν is derived from the Greek verb δέρνω (dérno), which means "to beat" or "to strike". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate past tense, making εδειραν (ediran) the past tense form of δέρνω.

Grammar: εδειραν is a past tense verb in the third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The -αν (-an) ending in εδειραν indicates that it is in the past tense and the third person plural form.

Description: εδειραν is a Greek verb that means "they beat" or "they struck". It is used to describe an action that happened in the past, specifically an action where someone or something was beaten or struck.
