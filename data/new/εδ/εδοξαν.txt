Word: εδοξαν

Etymology: The word εδοξαν comes from the Greek verb δοξάζω (doxazo), which means "to glorify" or "to praise". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense, making εδοξαν (edoxan) mean "they glorified" or "they praised".

Grammar: εδοξαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb δοξάζω (doxazo). In Greek, verbs change form depending on the tense, person, and number of the subject. The -αν (-an) ending indicates that the subject is third person plural (they).

Description: εδοξαν is a word that describes an action that has already taken place in the past. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe the act of glorifying or praising a deity or higher power. In everyday language, it can also be used to describe any act of praise or admiration towards someone or something.
