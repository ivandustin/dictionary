Word: εδοξε

Etymology: The word εδοξε (edokse) is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek verb δοκέω (dokéo), which means "to seem" or "to appear". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: εδοξε is a third person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. It is used to indicate a completed action in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εδοξε (edokse) - he/she/it seemed

Description: The word εδοξε is often used in Greek to express an opinion or a belief that someone had in the past. It can also be used to indicate a decision that was made in the past. For example, "Εδοξε να πάμε στην παραλία" (Edokse na pame stin paralia) means "It was decided to go to the beach". 

Overall, εδοξε is a useful verb in Greek for expressing past opinions, beliefs, and decisions.
