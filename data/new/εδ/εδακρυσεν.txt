Word: εδακρυσεν

Etymology: The word εδακρυσεν comes from the Greek verb δακρύω (dakryó), which means "to shed tears". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar: εδακρυσεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb δακρύω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εδακρυσεν means "he/she/it shed tears" in English. It is a simple past tense form of the verb δακρύω. The word can be used to describe someone crying or shedding tears in the past.
