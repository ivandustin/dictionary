Word: εδηλωσεν

Etymology: The word εδηλωσεν is derived from the Greek verb δηλόω (delóo), which means "to make clear" or "to declare". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: εδηλωσεν is a third person singular past tense form of the verb δηλόω (delóo) in the active voice. It is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: εδηλωσεν can be translated to English as "he/she/it declared" or "he/she/it made clear". It is a verb that indicates an action of making something known or revealing something that was previously hidden. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning and implications of the action being described.
