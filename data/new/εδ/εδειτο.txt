The word "εδειτο" (edeito) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "δείκνυμι" (deiknymi), which means "to show" or "to point out". 

Etymology: The word "δείκνυμι" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deik-, which means "to show" or "to point". 

Grammar: "εδειτο" is the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of "δείκνυμι". The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

Description: "εδειτο" can be translated as "he/she/it showed (himself/herself/itself)". It is a past tense form, indicating that the action of showing has already taken place. The middle voice suggests that the subject showed something to himself/herself/itself, or that the subject was both the one who showed and the one who was shown something.
