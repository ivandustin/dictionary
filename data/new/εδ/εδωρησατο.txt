Word: εδωρησατο

Etymology: The word εδωρησατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δωρέομαι (doreomai), which means "to give as a gift." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates that the action is in the past tense, and the suffix -σατο (-sato) indicates that the subject is in the third person singular.

Grammar: εδωρησατο is a third person singular aorist middle indicative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves. The indicative mood indicates that the verb is a statement of fact.

Description: εδωρησατο means "he/she/it gave as a gift to himself/herself/itself" in Greek. It is often used in the context of someone giving themselves a gift or a reward for something they have done.
