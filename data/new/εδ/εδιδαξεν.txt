Word: εδιδαξεν

Etymology: εδιδαξεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διδασκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: εδιδαξεν is the third person singular past tense form of the verb διδασκω (didasko). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject (he/she/it) performed the action.

Description: εδιδαξεν means "he/she/it taught" or "he/she/it instructed" in English. It is a verb that describes the act of imparting knowledge or skills to someone else. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in education, training, or mentoring.
