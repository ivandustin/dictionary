Word: εδιψησα

Etymology: The word εδιψησα is a conjugated form of the Greek verb διψάω (dipsao), which means "to thirst". The verb is derived from the noun δίψος (dipsos), which means "thirst".

Grammar: εδιψησα is the first person singular past tense form of the verb διψάω (dipsao) in the aorist tense. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: εδιψησα can be translated to "I thirsted" or "I was thirsty". It is a common verb in Greek and is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, where Jesus says "I thirst" on the cross (John 19:28).
