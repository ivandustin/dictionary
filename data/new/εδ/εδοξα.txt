The word "εδοξα" (edoxa) is a Greek verb in the past tense, which means "I thought" or "I believed". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "doxa", which means "opinion" or "belief". The prefix "e-" in this case indicates the past tense.

Grammar: "εδοξα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "δοξάζω" (doxazo), which means "to think" or "to believe". 

Description: The word "εδοξα" is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday speech to express a past belief or opinion. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as expressing a past thought or belief about a situation, person, or idea.
