Word: εδουλευσεν

Etymology: The word εδουλευσεν comes from the Greek verb δουλεύω (doulevo), which means "to work as a slave or servant". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: εδουλευσεν is a third person singular past tense form of the verb δουλεύω. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The -σεν (-sen) ending indicates the past tense and the third person singular.

Description: εδουλευσεν means "he/she/it worked as a slave or servant" in Greek. It is a past tense form of the verb δουλεύω, which is commonly used to describe someone who works for someone else, often without pay or under harsh conditions. The word carries a negative connotation, as it implies a lack of freedom and autonomy.
