Word: εδιστασαν

Etymology: The word εδιστασαν comes from the Greek verb διστάζω (distazo), which means "to hesitate" or "to doubt". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense, making εδιστασαν (edistasan) the third person plural past tense form of the verb.

Grammar: εδιστασαν is a verb in the third person plural past tense in the Greek language. It is conjugated from the verb διστάζω (distazo) and indicates that a group of people hesitated or doubted in the past.

Description: εδιστασαν is a word that describes a past action of hesitation or doubt by a group of people. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group's reluctance to make a decision or take action. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday conversation.
