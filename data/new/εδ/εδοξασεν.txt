Word: εδοξασεν

Etymology: εδοξασεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word δοξαζω (doxazo), which means "to glorify" or "to praise." The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate that the action is in the past tense.

Grammar: εδοξασεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: εδοξασεν can be translated to English as "he/she/it glorified" or "he/she/it praised." The word is often used in a religious context to describe the act of glorifying or praising God. It can also be used more generally to describe the act of giving honor or recognition to someone or something.
