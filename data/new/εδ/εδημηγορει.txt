Word: εδημηγορει

Etymology: The word εδημηγορει comes from the Greek words εδημος (edimos) meaning "public" and αγορευω (agoreuo) meaning "to speak in public". Therefore, εδημηγορει means "to speak in public".

Grammar: εδημηγορει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the ancient Greek language and is commonly used in modern Greek.

Description: εδημηγορει refers to the act of speaking in public, addressing a group of people or an audience. It can be used in various contexts, such as political speeches, lectures, or public debates. The word implies a certain level of authority and expertise in the subject matter being discussed.
