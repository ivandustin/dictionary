Word: εδιδαξα

Etymology: The word εδιδαξα comes from the Greek verb διδάσκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate that the action was completed in the past tense.

Grammar: εδιδαξα is the first person singular past tense form of the verb διδάσκω (didasko). It is used to indicate that the speaker taught or instructed someone or something in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εγώ εδίδαξα (ego edidaxa) - I taught
εσύ εδίδαξες (esi edidaxes) - you taught
αυτός/αυτή/αυτό εδίδαξε (aftos/afti/afto edidaxe) - he/she/it taught
εμείς εδιδάξαμε (emeis edidaxame) - we taught
εσείς εδιδάξατε (eseis edidaxate) - you taught
αυτοί/αυτές εδίδαξαν (aftoi/aftes edidaxan) - they taught

Description: εδιδαξα is a verb that describes the action of teaching or instructing in the past tense. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as teaching a subject in school, instructing someone on how to do a task, or sharing knowledge with others. The verb is commonly used in everyday conversation and writing in the Greek language.
