The word "εδιδου" (edidou) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), which means "to give". It is in the imperfect tense, third person singular, indicative mood. 

Etymology: The word "δίδωμι" (didomi) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃- ("to give"), which is also the origin of the English word "donate".

Grammar: As mentioned, "εδιδου" is in the imperfect tense, third person singular, indicative mood. The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The third person singular indicates that the subject of the verb is he/she/it.

Description: "εδιδου" can be translated to English as "he/she/it was giving" or "he/she/it used to give". It is a common verb in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts, such as giving gifts, offering help, or providing information.
