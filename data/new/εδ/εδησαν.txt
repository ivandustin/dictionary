Word: εδησαν

Etymology: The word εδησαν comes from the Greek verb δέω (deo), which means "to bind" or "to tie". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates a completed action, so εδησαν means "they bound" or "they tied".

Grammar: εδησαν is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb δέω (deo). In this form, it indicates a completed action in the past. The verb is in the active voice, which means that the subject (they) performed the action of binding or tying.

Description: εδησαν is a verb that describes the action of binding or tying. It is often used in a literal sense, such as tying up an animal or binding a prisoner. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe being bound by a promise or obligation. In the context of ancient Greek literature, εδησαν is often used to describe the binding of a sacrifice or the tying up of a prisoner of war.
