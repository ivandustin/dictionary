Word: εδυνατο

Etymology: 

The word εδυνατο (edynato) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb δύναμαι (dynamai), which means "to be able to" or "to have power". The verb δύναμαι comes from the Greek noun δύναμις (dynamis), which means "power" or "ability". The root of the word δύναμις is δυνα-, which means "able" or "capable".

Grammar:

εδυνατο is the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form of the verb δύναμαι. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In this case, εδυνατο means "he/she/it was able to" or "he/she/it had the power to".
