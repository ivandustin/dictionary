Word: εδιδασκεν

Etymology: εδιδασκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word διδασκω (didasko), which means "to teach" or "to instruct". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate the third person singular form of the verb in the imperfect tense.

Grammar: εδιδασκεν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the imperfect tense. In this tense, it indicates an ongoing or repeated action in the past. The verb is conjugated in the third person singular form, which means that it refers to a single subject who is doing the action of teaching.

Description: εδιδασκεν means "he/she/it was teaching" in English. It is a verb that describes the action of instructing or educating someone.
