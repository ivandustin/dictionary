Word: ησθιον

Etymology:
The word ησθιον (esthion) is derived from the Greek verb ἐσθίω (esthiō), which means "to eat". It is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Ησθιον (esthion) refers to food or nourishment. It is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the food that John the Baptist ate in the wilderness (Matthew 3:4, Mark 1:6).
