Word: ησυχαζειν

Etymology: ησυχαζειν is a Greek verb that comes from the adjective ήσυχος (hēsychos), meaning "quiet" or "still". The verb form means "to be quiet" or "to be still".

Grammar: ησυχαζειν is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is in the active voice and the infinitive form. The verb is often used with the auxiliary verb "να" (na) to form the phrase "να ησυχάζεις" (na isycházis), which means "to be quiet" or "to calm down".

Description: ησυχαζειν is a verb that describes the act of being quiet or still. It can be used to describe a person who is calm and peaceful, or a situation that is quiet and peaceful. The verb is often used in the context of meditation or prayer, where the goal is to achieve a state of inner peace and stillness.
