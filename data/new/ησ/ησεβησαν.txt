Word: ησεβησαν

Etymology: ησεβησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word "ασεβής" (asevís), which means "impious" or "irreverent". The prefix "η-" (i-) in this case indicates the past tense, so ησεβησαν means "they acted impiously" or "they were irreverent".

Grammar: ησεβησαν is the third person plural past tense form of the verb ησεβέω (asevéo), which means "to act impiously" or "to be irreverent". It is a regular verb in the Greek language and follows the conjugation pattern of the verb stem.

Description: ησεβησαν is a verb that describes an action of acting impiously or irreverently. It is often used in a religious context to describe behavior that goes against the beliefs or practices of a particular faith. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the actions of those who opposed or persecuted the early Christian church.
