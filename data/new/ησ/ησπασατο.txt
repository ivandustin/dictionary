Word: ησπασατο

Etymology: The word ησπασατο is a conjugated form of the Ancient Greek verb "σπάω" (spao), which means "to break" or "to snap". The prefix "η-" indicates that the verb is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar: ησπασατο is a third-person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb "σπάω". The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit. In this case, the verb form means "he/she/it broke (something) for himself/herself/itself".

Description: ησπασατο is a specific form of the verb "σπάω" that describes a completed action of breaking or snapping something for oneself. The context in which this verb form is used would determine what exactly was broken or snapped.
