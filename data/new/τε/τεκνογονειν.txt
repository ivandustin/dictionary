Word: τεκνογονειν

The word τεκνογονειν is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: τέκνον (teknon) meaning "child" and γεννάω (gennao) meaning "to give birth". Therefore, τεκνογονειν means "to give birth to children".

τεκνογονειν is a verb in the present active infinitive form. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, and voice. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time, while the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

τεκνογονειν is a word that refers to the act of giving birth to children. It is a common word in Greek literature and is often used to describe the role of women in society.
