Word: τεκνων

Etymology:
The word τεκνων (teknon) is derived from the Greek word τέκνον (teknon), which means "child" or "offspring". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *tek-, which means "to beget" or "to give birth".

Grammar:
τεκνων is the genitive plural form of τέκνον (teknon). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. Therefore, τεκνων means "of the children" or "of the offspring".

Description:
τεκνων is a Greek word that refers to the children or offspring of a person or animal. It is often used in the context of family relationships, such as in the phrase "τεκνων του θεου" (teknon tou theou), which means "child of God". The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to the products or creations of a person or group, such as in the phrase "τεκνων της τεχνης" (teknon tis technis), which means "offspring of art" or "products of art".
