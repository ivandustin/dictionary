In terms of grammar, τεθυμενα is a participle, which is a type of verb form that can function as an adjective or adverb. The perfect tense indicates that the action of sacrificing has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence (the thing being sacrificed) is receiving the action of the verb rather than performing it.

Overall, τεθυμενα is a word that is often used in the context of ancient Greek religion and mythology, where sacrifices were a common practice. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that has been given up or offered as a sacrifice for a greater cause.
