Word: τεθνηκως

Etymology:
The word τεθνηκως (tethnēkos) is a form of the Greek verb θνῄσκω (thnēskō), which means "to die." The prefix τε- (te-) indicates that the action is completed or finished, so τεθνηκως means "having died" or "dead."

Grammar:
τεθνηκως is a participle, which is a type of verb form that can function as an adjective or a noun. In this case, it is an adjective that describes a noun as being dead. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case, which means it is the subject of a sentence.

Description:
τεθνηκως is a word that describes something or someone as being dead. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a deceased person or animal, or referring to something that is no longer functioning or active.
