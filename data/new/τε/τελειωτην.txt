The word "τελειωτην" (teleiōtēn) is a Greek noun that means "finisher" or "completer". It is derived from the verb "τελειόω" (teleioō), which means "to complete" or "to perfect". In terms of grammar, "τελειωτην" is in the accusative case, indicating that it is the direct object of a verb. Overall, "τελειωτην" is a word that describes someone or something that brings a task or process to completion.
