The word "τεταγμέναι" is a Greek verb in the perfect passive participle form. It comes from the root verb "τάσσω" which means "to arrange" or "to put in order". The perfect passive participle form indicates that the action of arranging or putting in order has been completed in the past and the subject of the verb has received the action.

In terms of grammar, "τεταγμέναι" is a feminine plural form, indicating that the subject of the verb is a group of female individuals who have been arranged or put in order.

Overall, "τεταγμέναι" can be translated to mean "having been arranged" or "having been put in order".
