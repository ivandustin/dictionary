The word τεσσαρεσκαιδεκατη is a Greek word that means "fourteenth". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words, "τέσσερα" (téssera) meaning "four" and "καίδεκα" (kaídeka) meaning "ten".

In Greek, τεσσαρεσκαιδεκατη is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is spelled as "τεσσαρεσκαιδεκατη" and pronounced as "tess-ah-res-keh-thee-tee". The word is used to indicate the ordinal number "fourteenth" in a series.

τεσσαρεσκαιδεκατη is a specific term used to indicate the ordinal number "fourteenth" in a series. It is commonly used in various contexts such as dates, rankings, and lists. For example, "τεσσαρεσκαιδεκατη Ιουλίου" (tess-ah-res-keh-thee-tee Iouliou) means "fourteenth of July" in Greek.
