Word: τεθωσιν

Etymology:
The word τεθωσιν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The prefix τε (te) adds emphasis to the action of placing or setting.

Grammar:
τεθωσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative verb in Greek. It is in the indicative mood, which indicates a statement of fact. The present tense indicates that the action is happening currently, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
τεθωσιν can be translated to mean "they have placed" or "they are setting". It is a verb that describes an action of placing or setting something. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning.
