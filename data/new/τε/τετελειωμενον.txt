Word: τετελειωμένον

Etymology:
The word τετελειωμένον is a Greek word that comes from the verb τελειόω (teleioo), which means "to complete" or "to finish." The word is in the perfect passive participle form, indicating that the action of completing or finishing has already been done to the subject.

Grammar:
In Greek, the perfect passive participle form of a verb is used to describe a state resulting from a completed action. In this case, τετελειωμένον describes a state of being that results from the action of completing or finishing something. The word is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

τετελειωμένον can be translated to English as "completed" or "finished." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the completion of a task or the attainment of a goal.
