τετρακοσιοις (tetra-kosiois) is a Greek word that means "four hundred". 

Etymology: 
The word is derived from the Greek words "tetra" meaning "four" and "kosi" meaning "hundred". The suffix "-ois" is added to indicate the dative plural form.

Grammar:
τετρακοσιοις is a masculine noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
The word τετρακοσιοις is used to describe a quantity of four hundred. It can be used in various contexts, such as counting money, measuring distance, or describing a group of people. For example, "Έχω τετρακόσια ευρώ στην τσέπη μου" (I have four hundred euros in my pocket).
