In ancient Greek philosophy, the concept of "telos" referred to the ultimate purpose or goal of something. For example, the "telos" of a plant is to grow and reproduce, while the "telos" of a human being is to live a virtuous life. The adjective "τελειων" was often used to describe something that had achieved its ultimate purpose or was fully developed and mature.
