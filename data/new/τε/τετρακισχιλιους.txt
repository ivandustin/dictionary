Word: τετρακισχίλιους

Etymology: The word τετρακισχίλιους (tetrakis-khílious) is a compound word in Greek, formed by the combination of the words τέσσερα (téssera) meaning "four" and χίλιοι (khílioi) meaning "thousand". Therefore, the word τετρακισχίλιους means "four thousand".

Grammar: τετρακισχίλιους is an adjective in the masculine gender, nominative case, and plural number. It agrees with a noun in gender, case, and number.

Description: τετρακισχίλιους is a numerical adjective used to describe a quantity of four thousand. It can be used in various contexts, such as in counting or measuring large numbers of objects or people. For example, τετρακισχίλιους άνθρωποι (tetrakis-khílioi ánthropoi) means "four thousand people".
