Word: τελεσθηναι

Etymology:
The word τελεσθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τέλος (telos), which means "end" or "goal". The suffix -θηναι (-thenai) is a passive form of the verb, indicating that the action is being done to the subject rather than by the subject.

Grammar:
τελεσθηναι is a passive aorist infinitive verb in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "to be completed" or "to be finished". The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, without specifying the duration of the action.

Description:
τελεσθηναι is a verb that describes the completion or finishing of a task or goal. It implies that the action has been done to the subject, rather than by the subject. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in business, education, or personal goals.
