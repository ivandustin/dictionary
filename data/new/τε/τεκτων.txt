Word: τεκτων

Etymology:
The word τεκτων (tekton) is derived from the Greek verb τεκτω (tektō), which means "to build" or "to construct". It is related to the English word "architect".

Grammar:
τεκτων is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
τεκτων is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to a craftsman or artisan who works with his hands to build or construct things. The word is often used in the context of carpentry or woodworking, but it can also refer to other types of skilled labor, such as masonry or metalworking. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe Joseph, the earthly father of Jesus, who was a carpenter by trade.
