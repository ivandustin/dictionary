τετελευτηκοτος (tetelēutēkotos) is a Greek word that is the perfect participle of the verb τελευτάω (teleutáō), which means "to come to an end" or "to die." The word τετελευτηκοτος, therefore, means "having come to an end" or "having died." 

In terms of grammar, τετελευτηκοτος is a masculine singular adjective that agrees with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Etymologically, τετελευτηκοτος is derived from the verb τελευτάω, which comes from the root τελε-, meaning "to complete" or "to finish." The suffix -υτηκοτος (-utēkotos) is the perfect participle suffix, indicating that the action of the verb has been completed in the past and has ongoing relevance to the present.

Overall, τετελευτηκοτος is a word that describes something that has come to an end or has died, and it is used in various contexts, including obituaries, epitaphs, and historical accounts.
