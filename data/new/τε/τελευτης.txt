Word: τελευτης

Etymology:
The word τελευτης (teleutes) comes from the Greek verb τελευτάω (teleutao), which means "to come to an end" or "to die". It is derived from the root word τελευτή (teleute), which means "end" or "completion".

Grammar:
τελευτης is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive, dative, and accusative.

Description:
τελευτης refers to the end or conclusion of something, such as the end of a life or the end of an event. It can also refer to death or the act of dying. In Greek mythology, the god of death was named Thanatos, and he was often depicted as a companion of τελευτης. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to the end of a period of time or the completion of a task.
