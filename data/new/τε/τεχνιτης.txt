Word: τεχνίτης

Etymology:
The word τεχνίτης (technites) comes from the Greek word τέχνη (techne), which means "art" or "craft". The suffix -ίτης (-ites) is a common suffix in Greek that denotes a person who practices or is skilled in a particular craft or profession.

Grammar:
τεχνίτης is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word is singular, meaning it refers to one person or thing.

Description:
τεχνίτης refers to a skilled craftsman or artisan who is proficient in a particular trade or craft. This could include professions such as a carpenter, blacksmith, or potter. The word implies a high level of skill and expertise in a particular craft, and is often used to describe someone who has spent years perfecting their trade.
