The word "τεθνηκοτος" is a Greek adjective that means "dead" or "having died". It is derived from the verb "θνῄσκω" (thnēskō), which means "to die". In terms of grammar, "τεθνηκοτος" is in the perfect tense, indicating that the action of dying has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. It is also in the masculine gender and singular number, agreeing with the gender and number of the noun it modifies.
