Word: τετηρημένην

Etymology: τετηρημένην is the feminine form of the past participle of the Greek verb τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, observe, maintain." The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ter- which means "to watch, observe."

Grammar: τετηρημένην is a feminine singular accusative form of the past participle of the verb τηρέω. It is used to describe a feminine singular noun that is the direct object of the verb.

Description: τετηρημένην describes something that has been kept, guarded, observed, or maintained. It can be used to describe a physical object that has been preserved, such as a document or artifact, or a concept or tradition that has been passed down through generations. In a religious context, it can refer to a sacred text or teaching that has been faithfully preserved.
