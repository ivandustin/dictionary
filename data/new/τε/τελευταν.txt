Word: τελευταν

Etymology:
The word τελευταν (teleutan) is derived from the Greek verb τελευτάω (teleutao), which means "to come to an end" or "to die". It is formed from the prefix τελευτ- (teleut-) meaning "end" and the verb suffix -αω (-ao) indicating action or process.

Grammar:
τελευταν is the present participle of the verb τελευτάω (teleutao), which means "dying" or "coming to an end". It is in the masculine singular nominative case, and can be used as a noun or an adjective.

Description:
τελευταν refers to someone or something that is in the process of dying or coming to an end. It can be used to describe a person who is on their deathbed, or a situation that is coming to a close. In Greek mythology, the word is often used to describe the death of a hero or a god.
