Word: τεκνου

Etymology:
The word τεκνου (teknoú) is derived from the Greek word τέκνον (téknon), which means "child" or "offspring". It is a noun in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship.

Grammar:
τεκνου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it indicates that something belongs to or is related to a child or offspring.

Description:
τεκνου is a Greek word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to children or offspring. It is a term of endearment and affection, and is often used to describe the relationship between a parent and child. In Greek culture, children were highly valued and considered a blessing from the gods. The word τεκνου reflects this cultural value and the importance placed on family and children.
