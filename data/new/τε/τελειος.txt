τελειος (teleios) is a Greek adjective that means "perfect" or "complete". It comes from the root word τελος (telos), which means "end" or "goal". In Greek philosophy, τελος refers to the ultimate purpose or aim of something.

In terms of grammar, τελειος is a third declension adjective, meaning its endings change depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Examples:
- Η δουλειά μου δεν είναι τελεία ακόμα. (My work is not perfect yet.)
- Οι μαθητές έχουν πετύχει την τελειότητα στις εξετάσεις. (The students have achieved perfection in their exams.)
- Η τελειότητα του έργου του Μιχαήλ Άγγελου είναι αξεπέραστη. (The perfection of Michaelangelo's work is unparalleled.)
