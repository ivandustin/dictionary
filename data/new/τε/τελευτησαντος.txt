τελευτησαντος (teleutēsantos) is a Greek word that is the genitive form of the verb τελευτάω (teleutáō), which means "to come to an end" or "to die." The word is derived from the root word τελευτή (teleutē), which means "end" or "completion." 

In terms of grammar, τελευτησαντος is a participle, which is a verbal adjective that describes the action of the verb. The -σαντος (-santos) ending indicates that it is in the aorist tense and masculine gender, agreeing with the subject of the sentence. 

Overall, τελευτησαντος can be translated as "of the one who has come to an end" or "of the one who has died."
