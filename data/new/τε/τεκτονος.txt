τεκτονος (tektonos) is a Greek word that means "carpenter" or "builder". It is derived from the Greek word "τέκτων" (tekton), which means "craftsman" or "artisan". In Greek mythology, the god Hephaestus was often referred to as a tekton, as he was the god of metalworking and craftsmanship. 

In terms of grammar, τεκτονος is a noun in the genitive case, which is often used to indicate possession or origin. 

Overall, τεκτονος is a word that is associated with skilled labor and craftsmanship, particularly in the construction and building trades.
