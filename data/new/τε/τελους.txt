Word: τελους

Etymology:
The word τελους (telous) is derived from the Greek word τέλος (telos), which means "end" or "goal". It is related to the verb τελέω (teleo), which means "to complete" or "to finish".

Grammar:
τελους is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. 

Description:
τελους is often used in the context of financial transactions or payments. It can refer to a payment that is due or a debt that needs to be paid. It can also refer to the completion or fulfillment of a task or goal. In philosophy, τελος is used to refer to the ultimate purpose or end goal of human life.
