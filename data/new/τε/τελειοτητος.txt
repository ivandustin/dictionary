τελειότητος (teleiotētos) is a Greek noun that comes from the adjective τέλειος (teleios), meaning "perfect" or "complete". The suffix -τητος (-tetos) is used to form abstract nouns from adjectives, so τελειότητος means "perfection" or "completeness". 

In terms of grammar, τελειότητος is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It can be declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: η τελειότητα (i teleiotita) 
- Genitive: της τελειότητος (tis teleiotitos)
- Accusative: την τελειότητα (tin teleiotita)
- Vocative: ω τελειότητα (o teleiotita)

Plural:
- Nominative: οι τελειότητες (oi teleiotites)
- Genitive: των τελειοτήτων (ton teleiotiton)
- Accusative: τις τελειότητες (tis teleiotites)
- Vocative: ω τελειότητες (o teleiotites)

Overall, τελειότητος is a word that emphasizes the idea of perfection and completeness.
