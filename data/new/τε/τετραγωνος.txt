Word: τετράγωνος

Etymology:
The word τετράγωνος comes from the combination of two Greek words: τέσσερα (téssera) meaning "four" and γωνία (gonía) meaning "angle". Therefore, τετράγωνος literally means "four-angled" or "four-cornered".

Grammar:
τετράγωνος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases depending on the gender and number of the noun it modifies.

Description:
τετράγωνος is used to describe a shape or object that has four equal sides and four right angles, such as a square. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is rigid, inflexible, or unyielding.
