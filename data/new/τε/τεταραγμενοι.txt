Word: τεταραγμένοι

Etymology:
The word τεταραγμένοι (tetaragmenoi) is a Greek word that comes from the verb ταράσσω (tarasso), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". The prefix τετρα- (tetra-) means "four", so the word τεταραγμένοι (tetaragmenoi) literally means "four disturbed ones". 

Grammar:
τεταραγμένοι (tetaragmenoi) is the plural form of the adjective τεταραγμένος (tetaragmenos), which means "disturbed" or "agitated". It is a masculine adjective, and it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. 

Description:
τεταραγμένοι (tetaragmenoi) is an adjective that describes a state of being disturbed or agitated. It can be used to describe people who are upset, anxious, or unsettled. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe situations or events that are chaotic or turbulent.
