Word: τελειωσαι

Etymology:
The word τελειωσαι comes from the Greek verb τελειόω (teleioo), which means "to complete" or "to perfect". The suffix -σαι (-sai) indicates that the verb is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
τελειωσαι is a verb in the aorist tense, second person singular form. It is derived from the verb τελειόω, which is a first conjugation verb in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the second person singular form indicates that the action was performed by the person being addressed.

Description:
τελειωσαι means "you completed" or "you perfected" in Greek.
