τεχνιταις (technitais) is a Greek word that is derived from the noun τέχνη (techne), which means "art" or "craft". The suffix -ταις (-tais) is a plural ending that indicates a group of people who practice a particular craft or art. Therefore, τεχνιταις refers to a group of artisans or craftsmen.

In terms of grammar, τεχνιταις is a feminine noun in the dative plural case. It can be translated as "to the artisans" or "for the craftsmen".

Overall, τεχνιταις is a word that highlights the importance of skilled labor and craftsmanship in ancient Greek society.
