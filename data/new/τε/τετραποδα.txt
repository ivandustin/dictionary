τετραποδα (tetrapoda) is a Greek word that literally means "four-footed". It is commonly used in biology to refer to a group of animals that have four limbs or legs, such as mammals, reptiles, and amphibians. The word is derived from the Greek words "tetra" meaning "four" and "pous" meaning "foot". In terms of grammar, τετραποδα is a neuter noun in the plural form.
