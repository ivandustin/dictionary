Word: τεθνηκοτα

Etymology:
The word τεθνηκοτα is the perfect participle of the Greek verb θνῄσκω (thnḗskō), which means "to die". The prefix τε- (te-) indicates that the action is completed, and the suffix -κοτα (-kota) indicates the masculine accusative singular form.

Grammar:
τεθνηκοτα is a perfect participle in the masculine accusative singular form. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The perfect tense indicates that the action is completed in the past and has continuing effects in the present.

Description:
τεθνηκοτα is used to describe someone or something that has died. It can be translated as "the one who has died" or "the deceased". In Greek literature, it is often used to refer to heroes or important figures who have passed away.
