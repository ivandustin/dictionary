Word: τετηρηκεν

Etymology: τετηρηκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τηρέω (tēréō), which means "to keep, guard, or observe." The prefix τε- (te-) indicates that the action is completed or perfect.

Grammar: τετηρηκεν is a third-person singular perfect active indicative verb in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the prefix τε- to the stem τηρέω and the appropriate endings for the perfect tense.

Description: τετηρηκεν means "he/she/it has kept, guarded, or observed" in Greek. It is a verb that indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone who has kept a promise or guarded a secret.
