Word: τελεσθησεται

Etymology:
The word τελεσθησεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τελέω (teleo), which means "to complete" or "to finish." The suffix -σεται (-setai) is a future tense marker, indicating that the action of the verb will take place in the future.

τελεσθησεται is a third-person singular future passive indicative verb in Greek. The verb is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject will receive the action of the verb. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement about a fact or reality. The future tense indicates that the action of the verb will take place in the future.

The word τελεσθησεται means "it will be completed" or "it will be finished."
