Word: τεταρτος

Etymology:
The word τεταρτος (tetartos) is derived from the Greek word τέσσαρες (téssares), which means "four". The suffix "-τος" (-tos) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
τεταρτος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a noun as "fourth" in a series or sequence.

Description:
τεταρτος is a Greek word that means "fourth". It is commonly used to describe the position of something in a series or sequence. For example, if there are four people in a line, the person in the fourth position would be described as "ο τέταρτος" (o téttaros) in Greek. The word can also be used to describe the fourth day of the week, which is Thursday in the Greek calendar.
