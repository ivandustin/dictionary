Word: τεθεικως

Etymology:
The word τεθεικως is a Greek word that comes from the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The suffix -ως (-os) is a common adverbial suffix in Greek that is used to form adverbs from adjectives.

Grammar:
τεθεικως is an adverb in Greek. It is formed from the perfect participle of the verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "having been placed" or "having been set". The suffix -ως (-os) is added to the end of the participle to form the adverb.

Description:
τεθεικως is used to describe something that has been placed or set in a particular way. It can also be used to describe something that has been established or arranged in a certain manner. In English, it can be translated as "placed", "set", "established", or "arranged".
