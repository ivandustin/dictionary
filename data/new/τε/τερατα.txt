Word: τερατα

Etymology:
The word τερατα (terata) is derived from the Greek word τέρας (teras), which means "monster" or "prodigy". It is related to the Greek word τερατόω (teratoō), which means "to show signs or wonders".

Grammar:
τερατα is a plural noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
τερατα refers to monsters or prodigies in Greek mythology. These creatures were often depicted as having strange or terrifying features, such as multiple heads, wings, or serpentine bodies. They were believed to be omens of disaster or divine punishment, and were often associated with the gods and goddesses of the Greek pantheon. The term τερατα can also be used more broadly to refer to any strange or unusual phenomenon.
