τελειως (teleios) is an adverb in Greek that means "completely" or "perfectly". 

Etymology: τελειως comes from the root word τελεία (teleia), which means "complete" or "perfect". 

Grammar: τελειως is an adverb and does not change form based on gender, number, or case. 

Description: τελειως is often used to emphasize the completeness or perfection of an action or state. It can also be used to indicate that something is done thoroughly or with great attention to detail. For example, "Οι εξετάσεις πήγαν τελείως καλά" (The exams went perfectly well).
