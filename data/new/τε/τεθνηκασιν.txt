The word "τεθνηκασιν" is a form of the Greek verb "θνῄσκω" (thnēskō), which means "to die". It is in the perfect tense and the third person plural, indicating that "they have died". In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to describe a completed action with ongoing relevance to the present. Therefore, "τεθνηκασιν" means "they have died and remain dead".
