The word "τεχνιται" (technitai) is a Greek noun in the plural form. It comes from the root word "τέχνη" (techne), which means "art" or "craft". 

In Greek grammar, "τεχνιται" is a third declension noun, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. 

As for its description, "τεχνιται" refers to skilled craftsmen or artisans who have expertise in a particular craft or trade. In ancient Greece, these craftsmen were highly respected and played an important role in society.
