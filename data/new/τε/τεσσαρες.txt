Word: τεσσαρες

Etymology: τεσσαρες is a Greek word that means "four". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷetwóres.

Grammar: τεσσαρες is a plural form of the Greek numeral τέσσερα (téssera), which means "four". It is declined like a regular first-declension noun, with the nominative singular form being τέσσαρες (téssares).

Description: τεσσαρες is a word used to refer to the number four in Greek. It can be used to count objects or people, or to indicate a quantity. In Greek mythology, there were four seasons, four winds, and four elements. The number four is also considered to be a lucky number in some cultures.
