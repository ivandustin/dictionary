Word: τετελεκα

Etymology:
The word τετελεκα is a perfect tense form of the Greek verb τελεω (teleō), which means "to complete" or "to finish." The root of the word is τελε-, which is related to the concept of completion or fulfillment.

Grammar:
τετελεκα is a first-person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb τελεω. The perfect tense in Greek indicates a completed action with ongoing results or effects. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact or reality, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
The word τετελεκα can be translated as "I have completed" or "I have finished." It is a statement of a completed action with ongoing results or effects. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to describe the completion of a task or project, the fulfillment of a promise or obligation, or the attainment of a goal.
