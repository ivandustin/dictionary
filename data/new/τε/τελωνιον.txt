τελωνιον (telōnion) is a Greek word that refers to a tax collection station or customs house. It is derived from the word τελωνης (telōnēs), which means tax collector. In terms of grammar, τελωνιον is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the tax collection stations where Matthew and Zacchaeus worked before becoming disciples of Jesus.
