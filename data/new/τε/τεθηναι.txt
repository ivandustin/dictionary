Word: τεθηναι

Etymology:
The word τεθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put." The prefix "τε-" (te-) is a perfect tense marker, indicating that the action of placing or putting has been completed in the past.

τεθηναι is a perfect passive participle in Greek. It is formed by adding the prefix "τε-" to the stem of the verb τίθημι, and then adding the passive participle ending "-μένος" (-menos). The word τεθηναι is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender.

Description:
The word τεθηναι can be translated as "having been placed" or "having been put." It is often used in Greek literature to describe something that has been set in place or established. For example, in the Odyssey, the hero Odysseus is described as τεθηναι (having been placed) on the island of Calypso by the gods.
