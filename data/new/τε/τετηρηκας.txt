Word: τετηρηκας

Etymology:
The word τετηρηκας (tetērēkas) is a perfect tense form of the Greek verb τηρέω (tēreō), which means "to keep, guard, observe, or maintain." The root of the word is τηρ- (tēr-), which is related to the concept of "watching over" or "protecting."

Grammar:
τετηρηκας is a second-person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb τηρέω. The perfect tense indicates that the action of "keeping" or "guarding" has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject (you) is performing the action.

Description:
τετηρηκας can be translated as "you have kept" or "you have guarded." It implies that the subject has taken care to protect or maintain something, whether it be a physical object, a relationship, or a set of beliefs or values. The perfect tense emphasizes that the action has been completed and has ongoing effects, suggesting that the subject has been successful in their efforts to keep or guard whatever it is they value.
