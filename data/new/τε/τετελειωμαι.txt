Word: τετελειωμαι

Etymology:
The word τετελειωμαι (teteleiōmai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word τελείωσις (teleiōsis), which means "completion" or "perfection." The prefix "te-" indicates that the action is complete or finished.

Grammar:
τετελειωμαι is a passive verb in the perfect tense. It is conjugated from the verb τελειόω (teleioō), which means "to complete" or "to perfect." The perfect tense indicates that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description:
τετελειωμαι means "I am finished" or "I am completed." It can also mean "I have been perfected" or "I have been brought to completion." The word is often used in a spiritual or religious context to describe the process of becoming fully mature or reaching a state of perfection. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the completion of Christ's work on the cross and the salvation of believers.
