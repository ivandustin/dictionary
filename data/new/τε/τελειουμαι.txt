Word: τελειουμαι

Etymology:
The word τελειουμαι (teleioumai) is derived from the Greek word τέλειος (teleios), which means "perfect" or "complete". The suffix -ουμαι (-oumai) is a reflexive suffix that indicates that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar:
τελειουμαι (teleioumai) is a passive verb in the present tense, indicative mood, and middle voice. It is conjugated as follows:

Singular:
- Τελειούμαι (teleioúmai) - I am being perfected/completed
- Τελειούσαι (teleioúsai) - You are being perfected/completed
- Τελειούται (teleioútai) - He/She/It is being perfected/completed

Plural:
- Τελειούμεθα (teleioúmetha) - We are being perfected/completed
- Τελειούσθε (teleioústhe) - You (plural) are being perfected/completed
- Τελειούνται (teleioúntai) - They are being perfected/completed

Description:
τελειουμαι (teleioumai) is a verb that describes the process of becoming perfect or complete. It is often used in a spiritual or moral sense, referring to the process of becoming more virtuous or achieving a higher level of enlightenment. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the process of sanctification, or becoming more like Christ. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe the process of reaching a state of perfection or completion in any area of life.
