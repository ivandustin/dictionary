Etymology: The word "προσκαλεσασθω" comes from the Greek verb "προσκαλέομαι" (proskaleomai), which means "to call upon" or "to invite." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "καλέω" (kaleo), meaning "to call."

Grammar: "Προσκαλεσασθω" is a verb in the imperative mood, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The third person singular form indicates that the command or request is directed towards a single person.

Description: The word "προσκαλεσασθω" is an instruction or command for someone to call upon or invite someone else. It is used to express the action of inviting or calling upon someone to come or join in a particular activity or event.
