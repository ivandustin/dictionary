Etymology: The word "προσκυνώ" (proskynó) comes from the Greek prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kynéo), meaning "to kiss." In ancient Greece, it referred to the act of kissing the ground in front of someone as a sign of respect or worship.

Grammar: "Προσκυνώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ προσκυνώ (proskynó), εσύ προσκυνάς (proskynás), αυτός/αυτή/αυτό προσκυνάει (proskynáei), εμείς προσκυνούμε (proskynoúme), εσείς προσκυνάτε (proskynáte), αυτοί/αυτές προσκυνούν (proskynoún).

Description: "Προσκυνώ" is a verb that means "to worship" or "to show reverence" in Greek.
