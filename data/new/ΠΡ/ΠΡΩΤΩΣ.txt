Etymology: The word "πρωτος" (prōtos) comes from the Greek language and means "first" or "foremost".

Grammar: In Greek, "πρωτος" (prōtos) is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe the first or foremost in a series or hierarchy.

Description: "Πρωτος" (prōtos) is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to the first in a sequence, the most important or prominent, or the leader in a group. It can also be used to indicate priority or precedence.
