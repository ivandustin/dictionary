Etymology: The word "πραξετε" (praxete) comes from the Greek verb "πράσσω" (prasso), which means "to do" or "to act".

Grammar: "Πραξετε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "πράσσω" (prasso). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people.

Description: "Πραξετε" is a verb in the Greek language that is used to tell a group of people to take action or do something. It is commonly used in commands or instructions to encourage people to act or perform a specific task.
