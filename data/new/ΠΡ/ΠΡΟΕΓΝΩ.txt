Etymology: The word "προεγνώ" (pronounced "proegno") comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the verb "εγνώ" (egno), meaning "to know." 

Grammar: "Προεγνώ" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the tense, person, and number. The past tense indicates that the action took place in the past, and the third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a singular third person.

Description: "Προεγνώ" means "he/she/it knew beforehand" or "he/she/it had foreknowledge" in English. It implies that the subject had knowledge or awareness of something before it happened.
