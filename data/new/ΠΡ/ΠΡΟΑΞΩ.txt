Etymology: The word "προαξω" (proaxo) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἄξω" (axo), meaning "I lead" or "I guide." 

Grammar: "Προαξω" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the first person singular form of the present tense. 

Description: "Προαξω" means "I lead before" or "I guide in front of" in English. It implies taking the lead or guiding someone or something ahead of others.
