Etymology: The word "προσκομματος" (proskommatos) comes from the Greek verb "προσκοπτω" (proskopto), which means "to stumble" or "to trip over". 

Grammar: "Προσκομματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Προσκομματος" refers to an obstacle or stumbling block. It can be used metaphorically to describe anything that hinders or obstructs progress or success.
