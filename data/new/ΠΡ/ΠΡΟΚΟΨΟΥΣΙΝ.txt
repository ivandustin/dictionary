Etymology: The word "προκοψούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "προκόπτω" (prokópto), which means "to advance" or "to make progress."

Grammar: "Προκοψούσιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "προκόπτω" in the present tense. It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: "Προκοψούσιν" is used to describe a group of people or entities who are currently making progress or advancing in some way. It indicates ongoing action in the present tense.
