Etymology: The word "προεχειρισατο" comes from the Greek verb "προεχειρίζομαι" (proecherízomai), which means "to handle beforehand" or "to take care of in advance." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before," and the verb "εχειρίζομαι" (echerízomai), meaning "to handle" or "to take care of."

Grammar: "προεχειρισατο" is the third person singular, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "προεχειρίζομαι" (proecherízomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The middle voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description: "προεχειρισατο" is a Greek verb that describes the action of handling or taking care of something beforehand or in advance. It implies a proactive approach to dealing with a situation or task.
