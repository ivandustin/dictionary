Etymology: The word "προδοτης" (prodotis) comes from the Greek root "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and the verb "διδωμι" (didomi), meaning "to give." Therefore, "προδοτης" can be translated as "one who gives before" or "one who gives in advance."

Grammar: In Greek, "προδοτης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension, and its genitive form is "προδοτου" (prodotou).

Description: "Προδοτης" is a Greek word that means "traitor" or "betrayer." It refers to someone who betrays the trust or loyalty of others, often by revealing confidential information or acting against a group or cause.
