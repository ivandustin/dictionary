Etymology: The word "προσπείνος" (prospeínos) comes from the Greek roots "πρός" (prós), meaning "towards," and "πεῖνα" (peîna), meaning "hunger." It literally translates to "towards hunger" or "hunger-stricken."

Grammar: "Προσπείνος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. It can also be declined in other cases such as genitive, dative, accusative, etc.

Description: "Προσπείνος" refers to someone who is extremely hungry or starving. It describes a person who is suffering from intense hunger or experiencing a state of extreme hunger.
