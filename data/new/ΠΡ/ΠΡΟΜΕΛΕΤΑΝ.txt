Etymology: The word "προμελέταν" (pronounced "promelétan") comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of "προ" (pro), meaning "before" or "pre-", and "μελέτη" (meléti), meaning "study" or "practice". 

Grammar: "Προμελέταν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. It is conjugated in the third person singular form. 

Description: The word "προμελέταν" refers to the act of studying or practicing something in advance or beforehand. It implies preparing or familiarizing oneself with a subject or activity before actually engaging in it.
