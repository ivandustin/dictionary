Etymology: The word "προσευχήσθε" comes from the Greek root words "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to," and "εὔχομαι" (euchomai), meaning "to pray" or "to wish." When combined, they form the verb "προσευχήσθε," which means "to pray."

Grammar: "Προσευχήσθε" is the second person plural, aorist middle imperative form of the verb "προσεύχομαι" (proseúchomai), which means "to pray." The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The imperative form is used to give a command or instruction.

Description: "Προσευχήσθε" is a verb that instructs a group of people to pray. It is used in a religious or spiritual context to encourage individuals to communicate with a higher power, express their desires, or seek guidance.
