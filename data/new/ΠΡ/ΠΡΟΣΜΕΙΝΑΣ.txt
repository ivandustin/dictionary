Etymology: The word "προσμεινάς" (prosmeinás) comes from the Greek verb "προσμένω" (prosméno), which means "to wait for" or "to endure." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (prós), meaning "towards" or "for," and the verb "μένω" (méno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain."

Grammar: "Προσμεινάς" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and second person singular form. It is used to address someone directly and instruct them to wait or endure.

Description: "Προσμεινάς" is a command form of the verb "προσμένω" and is often used in a context where someone is being asked to wait patiently or endure a difficult situation. It implies a sense of perseverance and resilience.
