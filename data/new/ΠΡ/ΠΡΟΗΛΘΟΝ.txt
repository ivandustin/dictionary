Etymology: The word "προηλθον" (proēlthon) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "prior to," and "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come." 

Grammar: "Προηλθον" is the aorist tense of the verb "προέρχομαι" (proerchomai), which means "to come forth" or "to go before." It is the first person singular form of the verb, indicating that the action was performed by the speaker in the past.

Description: "Προηλθον" is a Greek word that describes an action of going before or coming forth in the past. It can be used to indicate that someone or something preceded or came before another person or thing.
