Etymology: The word "προφητών" (pronounced "pro-fē-tōn") comes from the Greek language. It is the plural form of the noun "προφήτης" (pronounced "pro-fē-tēs"), which means "prophet" in English.

Grammar: In Greek, "προφητών" is the genitive plural form of the noun "προφήτης." The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. In this case, "προφητών" means "of the prophets" or "belonging to the prophets."

Description: "προφητών" is a word used to refer to a group of prophets. In Greek mythology and religious texts, prophets were individuals who claimed to have the ability to communicate with the divine and foretell future events. They played an important role in ancient Greek society and were often consulted for guidance and predictions.
