Etymology: The word "προεστώτες" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προ-" (meaning "before" or "in front of") and the verb "εστώτες" (meaning "standing" or "being"). 

Grammar: "προεστώτες" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension and is declined as follows:

- Nominative: προεστώτες
- Genitive: προεστώτων
- Accusative: προεστώτας
- Vocative: προεστῶτες
- Dative: προεστώσι(ν)
- Instrumental: προεστῶσι(ν)
- Locative: προεστῶσι(ν)

Description: "προεστώτες" is a Greek word that translates to "leaders" or "rulers" in English. It refers to individuals who hold positions of authority or power, typically in a political or administrative context.
