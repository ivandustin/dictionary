Etymology: The word "προτεραν" (pronounced "proteran") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "prior to," and the noun "τεραν" (teran), meaning "time" or "era."

Grammar: In Greek, "προτεραν" is a noun in the singular form. It does not have any specific grammatical gender.

Description: "Προτεραν" refers to a time or era that occurred before the present or a specific point in time. It can be used to describe a historical period, an age, or a time period that is no longer current.
