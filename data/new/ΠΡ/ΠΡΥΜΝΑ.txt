Etymology: The word "πρυμνα" (pronounced "prymna") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "πρυμνη" (pronounced "prymnē"), which means "stern" or "rear" of a ship.

Grammar: In Greek, "πρυμνα" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the first declension and has the nominative singular form "πρυμνα" and the genitive singular form "πρυμνης".

Description: "Πρυμνα" refers to the stern or rear part of a ship. It is the backmost section of a vessel, opposite to the bow or front. The stern typically houses the steering mechanism and other important equipment.
