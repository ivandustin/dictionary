Etymology: The word "προφητεύσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "προφητεύω" (prophēteuō), which means "to prophesy" or "to predict." It is derived from the noun "προφήτης" (prophētēs), meaning "prophet."

Grammar: "Προφητεύσουσιν" is a verb form in the future tense, third person plural, active voice, and indicative mood. It is used to express the action of prophesying or predicting performed by a group of people.

Description: "Προφητεύσουσιν" is a Greek word that means "they will prophesy" or "they will predict." It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to refer to the act of foretelling future events or conveying divine messages.
