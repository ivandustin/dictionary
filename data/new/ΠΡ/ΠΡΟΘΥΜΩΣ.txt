Etymology: The word "προθυμως" (prothymos) comes from the Greek root words "προ" (pro), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "θυμος" (thymos), meaning "passion" or "enthusiasm." 

Grammar: "Προθυμως" is an adverb in Greek. It is derived from the adjective "προθυμος" (prothymos), which means "eager" or "enthusiastic." 

Description: "Προθυμως" is used to describe an action or behavior that is done willingly, eagerly, or with enthusiasm. It implies a positive attitude and a readiness to engage in a particular activity.
