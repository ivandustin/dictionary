Etymology: The word "προσεπίπτον" comes from the Greek roots "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to," and "επίπτω" (epípto), meaning "fall upon" or "happen." 

Grammar: "Προσεπίπτον" is a present participle in the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "προσέπιπτω" (prosépipto), which means "to happen" or "to occur." The present participle form indicates an ongoing action or state.

Description: "Προσεπίπτον" is used to describe something that is happening or occurring at the present moment. It can be translated as "happening" or "occurring" in English.
