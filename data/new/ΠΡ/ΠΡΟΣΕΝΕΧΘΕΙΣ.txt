Etymology: The word "προσενέχθεις" (prosenéchtheis) comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (prosérchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near." The verb is formed by combining the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Προσενέχθεις" is the aorist passive participle form of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (prosérchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Προσενέχθεις" can be translated as "having been approached" or "having come near." It describes a past action of being approached or coming near to someone or something.
