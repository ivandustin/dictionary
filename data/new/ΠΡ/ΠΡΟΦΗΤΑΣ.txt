Etymology: The word "προφητας" (pronounced "prophitas") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "προφητεύω" (pronounced "prophitevo"), which means "to prophesy" or "to foretell." 

Grammar: In Greek, "προφητας" is the plural form of the noun "προφητης" (pronounced "prophitis"). It belongs to the second declension and is of masculine gender. 

Description: The word "προφητας" translates to "prophets" in English. In Greek mythology and religion, prophets were individuals who claimed to have the ability to communicate with the gods and foretell future events. They played a significant role in ancient Greek society and were often consulted for guidance and predictions.
