Etymology: The word "προσκυνείτε" (proskynite) comes from the Greek verb "προσκυνώ" (proskyno), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kuneo), meaning "to kiss" or "to adore."

Grammar: "Προσκυνείτε" is the second person plural form of the verb "προσκυνώ" (proskyno) in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all worship" or "you all bow down before."

Description: "Προσκυνείτε" is a Greek word that is often used in religious contexts to express reverence, adoration, or worship towards a deity or a sacred object. It can also be used metaphorically to describe extreme respect or admiration towards someone or something.
