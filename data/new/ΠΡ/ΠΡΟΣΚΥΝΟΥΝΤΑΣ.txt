Etymology: The word "προσκυνούντας" comes from the Greek verb "προσκυνώ" (proskynó), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (prós), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kynéo), meaning "to kiss" or "to bow down."

Grammar: "Προσκυνούντας" is the present participle form of the verb "προσκυνώ" (proskynó), which means "worshipping" or "bowing down before." In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description: "Προσκυνούντας" is a Greek word that refers to the act of worshiping or bowing down before someone or something. It can be used to describe the action of showing reverence, respect, or adoration towards a deity, a religious figure, or a person of authority.
