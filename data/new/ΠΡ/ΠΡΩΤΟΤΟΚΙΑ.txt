Etymology: The word "πρωτοτοκία" comes from the Greek words "πρῶτος" (prōtos), meaning "first," and "τοκεῖν" (tokein), meaning "to give birth." 

Grammar: "Πρωτοτοκία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined as follows: 
- Singular: η πρωτοτοκία (i prototokía)
- Plural: οι πρωτοτοκίες (i prototokíes)

Description: "Πρωτοτοκία" refers to the concept of being the firstborn or the first in a series. It can be used in various contexts, such as in the context of birth order or in a metaphorical sense to describe something that is the first or most important in a particular category.
