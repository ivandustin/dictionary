Etymology: The word "προσκαρτερήσει" comes from the Greek verb "προσκαρτερώ" (proskarteró), which means "to wait eagerly" or "to persevere". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "for", and the verb "καρτερώ" (karteró), meaning "to be steadfast" or "to endure".

Grammar: "Προσκαρτερήσει" is the third person singular future tense form of the verb "προσκαρτερώ" (proskarteró). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "Προσκαρτερήσει" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it will wait eagerly" or "he/she/it will persevere". It implies a sense of anticipation and determination in waiting for something or persisting in a task or goal.
