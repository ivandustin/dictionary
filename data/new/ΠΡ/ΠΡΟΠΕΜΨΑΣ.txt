Etymology: The word "προπεμψας" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "προπέμπω" (propempo), which means "to send forth" or "to escort."

Grammar: "Προπεμψας" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. It is the masculine singular form.

Description: "Προπεμψας" is a word used in Greek to describe someone who has sent forth or escorted someone or something. It implies the action of accompanying or seeing someone off.
