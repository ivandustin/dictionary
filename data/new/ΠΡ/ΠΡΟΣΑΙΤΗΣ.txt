Etymology: The word "προσαίτης" (prosaitēs) comes from the Greek roots "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to," and "αἴτησις" (aitēsis), meaning "request" or "petition." 

Grammar: In Greek, "προσαίτης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "προσαίτου" (prosaitou). 

Description: "Προσαίτης" refers to a person who makes a request or petition, typically to someone in authority or power. It can also be used to describe a supplicant or a beggar who asks for assistance or favors.
