Etymology: The word "προσερχόμεν" (proserchomen) comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "προσερχόμεν" is the present participle form of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate an ongoing action or state. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "προσερχόμεν" is a Greek word that describes the act of approaching or coming near to something or someone. It can be used to express physical proximity or figurative closeness in a variety of contexts.
