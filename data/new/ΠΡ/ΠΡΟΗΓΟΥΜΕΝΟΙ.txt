Etymology: The word "προηγούμενοι" (proēgoúmenoi) is derived from the Greek verb "προηγούμαι" (proēgoúmai), which means "to go before" or "to precede." It is formed by combining the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before," and the verb "ἡγέομαι" (hēgéomai), meaning "to lead" or "to go."

Grammar: "Προηγούμενοι" is the plural form of the adjective "προηγούμενος" (proēgoúmenos), which means "previous" or "preceding." It is used to describe multiple things or people that come before or precede something else.

Description: The word "προηγούμενοι" is often used in various contexts to refer to individuals or groups that come before or precede others in a particular sequence or order. It can be used to describe previous generations, predecessors, or those who hold a higher rank or position.
