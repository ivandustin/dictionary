Etymology: The word "προσκεκλημένος" comes from the Greek verb "προσκαλώ" (proskaló), which means "to invite" or "to call upon." The suffix "-μένος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates a passive voice, so "προσκεκλημένος" can be translated as "being invited" or "having been called upon."

Grammar: "Προσκεκλημένος" is a masculine singular participle in the nominative case. In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives. They can modify nouns or act as predicates in a sentence. The participle "προσκεκλημένος" agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Προσκεκλημένος" describes someone who has been invited or called upon. It can be used to refer to a person who has received an invitation to an event or a gathering. The word implies that the person has accepted the invitation and is expected to attend.
