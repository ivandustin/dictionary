Etymology: The word "προλέγω" (prolego) comes from the combination of the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before," and the verb "λέγω" (lego), meaning "to speak." Therefore, "προλέγω" can be translated as "to speak before" or "to preface."

Grammar: In Greek, "προλέγω" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ προλέγω (ego prolego) - I speak before
- Εσύ προλέγεις (esi prolegeis) - You speak before
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό προλέγει (autos/auti/auto prolegei) - He/She/It speaks before
- Εμείς προλέγουμε (emeis prolegoume) - We speak before
- Εσείς προλέγετε (eseis prolegete) - You speak before
- Αυτοί/Αυτές προλέγουν (autoi/autes prolegoun) - They speak before

Description: "Προλέγω" is a Greek verb that means "to speak before" or "to preface." It refers to the act of introducing or stating something before the main topic or discussion. It can also imply giving a preliminary explanation or setting the stage for a subsequent event or argument.
