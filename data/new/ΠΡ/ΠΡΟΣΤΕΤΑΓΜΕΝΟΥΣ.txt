Etymology: The word "προστεταγμένους" comes from the Greek verb "προστάσσω" (prostássō), which means "to command" or "to order." The suffix "-μένους" (-ménous) is added to the verb stem to form the participle form, indicating that the action is completed or ongoing.

Grammar: "Προστεταγμένους" is the accusative plural form of the participle "προσταγμένος" (prostagménos). In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as nouns themselves. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Προστεταγμένους" describes a group of people who have been commanded or ordered to do something. It implies that they are under someone's authority or have been given specific instructions.
