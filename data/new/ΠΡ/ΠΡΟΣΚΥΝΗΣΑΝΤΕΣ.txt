The word "προσκυνησαντες" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, active voice, participle form. Here is a breakdown of its components:

- Etymology: The word "προσκυνησαντες" is derived from the Greek root "προσκυνέω" (proskynéo), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is added to the root to form the aorist participle.

- Grammar: The word is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the active voice, suggesting that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The participle form indicates that the verb is functioning as an adjective.

- Description: "προσκυνησαντες" describes an action of worship or reverence that has already taken place. It can be translated as "having worshiped" or "having bowed down before."
