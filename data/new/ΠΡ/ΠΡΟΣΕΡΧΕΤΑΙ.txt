Etymology: The word "προσερχεται" (proserchetai) comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "προσερχεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, and tense. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.
