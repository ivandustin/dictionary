Etymology: The word "προσκυνείν" comes from the Greek roots "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and "κυνέω" (kyneo), meaning "to kiss." In ancient Greece, it referred to the act of kissing someone's hand or touching their feet as a sign of respect or worship.

Grammar: "Προσκυνείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. It is conjugated in the third person singular form.

Description: "Προσκυνείν" is a Greek word that means "to worship" or "to show reverence." It is often used to describe the act of bowing down, kneeling, or showing deep respect towards a deity, a religious figure, or someone of higher authority. It can also be used metaphorically to express admiration or devotion towards someone or something.
