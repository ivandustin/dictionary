Etymology: The word "προσευχή" (proseuchē) comes from the Greek verb "προσεύχομαι" (proseuchomai), which means "to pray" or "to make a request." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "εὔχομαι" (euchomai), meaning "to wish" or "to pray."

Grammar: In Greek, "προσευχή" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: η προσευχή (i proseuchē) 
- Genitive: της προσευχής (tēs proseuchēs)
- Accusative: την προσευχή (tēn proseuchē)
- Vocative: προσευχή (proseuchē)

Plural:
- Nominative: οι προσευχές (oi proseuches)
- Genitive: των προσευχών (tōn proseuchōn)
- Accusative: τις προσευχές (tis proseuches)
- Vocative: προσευχές (proseuches)

Description: "Προσευχή" is the Greek word for "prayer." It refers to the act of communicating with a higher power, expressing reverence, supplication, or thanksgiving. In various religious traditions, prayer plays a significant role as a means of connecting with the divine, seeking guidance, and offering devotion. It is a fundamental practice in many faiths and is often accompanied by specific rituals, gestures, or recitations.
