Etymology: The word "προσελήλυθατε" comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to come near" or "to approach." It is formed by combining the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Προσελήλυθατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "προσέρχομαι" in the perfect tense. The perfect tense indicates a completed action with ongoing results. In this form, it is used to address a group of people and means "you all have come" or "you all have approached."

Description: "Προσελήλυθατε" is a Greek word that is used to address a group of people and acknowledge their arrival or approach. It can be used in various contexts, such as welcoming guests, acknowledging the presence of a group, or indicating that someone has reached a certain destination.
