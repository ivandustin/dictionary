Etymology: The word "προφητεύων" (pronounced "prophetévon") comes from the Greek verb "προφητεύω" (pronounced "prophetévo"), which means "to prophesy" or "to predict." It is derived from the noun "προφήτης" (pronounced "prophítis"), meaning "prophet."

Grammar: "Προφητεύων" is the present participle form of the verb "προφητεύω" in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently prophesying or predicting events.
