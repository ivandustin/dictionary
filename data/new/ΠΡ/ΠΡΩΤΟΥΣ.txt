Etymology: The word "πρωτούς" (pronounced "protous") comes from the Greek language. It is the plural form of the adjective "πρῶτος" (pronounced "protos"), which means "first" or "foremost".

Grammar: In Greek, "πρωτούς" is the masculine plural form of the adjective "πρῶτος". It is used to describe multiple nouns that are in the masculine gender and in the nominative or accusative case. The form of the adjective changes depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πρωτούς" is an adjective that is used to indicate that something or someone is the first or foremost in a certain context. It can be used to describe a group of people or things that are considered to be the most important or significant.
