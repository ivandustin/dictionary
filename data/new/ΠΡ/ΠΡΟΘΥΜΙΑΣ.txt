Etymology: The word "προθυμίας" (prothymías) comes from the Greek root words "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "θυμός" (thymós), meaning "passion" or "desire." 

Grammar: "Προθυμίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Προθυμίας" refers to the state or quality of being eager, enthusiastic, or willing to do something. It can also imply readiness or preparedness.
