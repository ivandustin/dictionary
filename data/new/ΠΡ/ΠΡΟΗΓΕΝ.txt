Etymology: The word "προηγεν" (proegen) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "prior to," and "ἡγέομαι" (hegeomai), meaning "to lead" or "to go before." 

Grammar: "προηγεν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, indicative mood, and active voice. 

Description: The word "προηγεν" means "he/she/it led" or "he/she/it went before" in English. It is used to describe an action of leading or going before someone or something.
