Etymology: The word "προσειχον" (proseichon) comes from the Greek verb "προσέχω" (prosecho), which means "to pay attention" or "to be attentive." 

Grammar: "Προσειχον" is the present participle form of the verb "προσέχω" (prosecho). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Προσειχον" is a Greek word that can be translated as "paying attention" or "being attentive." It describes the action of focusing one's attention on something or someone. It implies being alert, careful, and mindful.
