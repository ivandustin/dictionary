Etymology: The word "προεπεμπον" (pronounced "proepempon") comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἐπεμβαίνω" (epembaino), meaning "to embark" or "to board." 

Grammar: "Προεπεμπον" is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It is derived from the verb "προεπέμπω" (proepempo), which means "I sent before" or "I dispatched ahead." 

Description: "Προεπεμπον" is used to describe an action of sending or dispatching something or someone ahead of time or in advance. It implies that the action took place in the past.
