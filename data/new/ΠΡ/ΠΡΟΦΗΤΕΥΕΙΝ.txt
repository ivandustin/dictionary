Etymology: The word "προφητεύειν" (pronounced "prophēteúein") comes from the Greek verb "προφητεύω" (pronounced "prophēteúō"), which means "to prophesy" or "to foretell." It is derived from the noun "προφήτης" (pronounced "prophētēs"), meaning "prophet."

Grammar: "Προφητεύειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, voice, mood, and person. The present tense indicates an ongoing action, the active voice means the subject performs the action, and the infinitive form expresses the verb in its most basic form.

Description: "Προφητεύειν" is a Greek verb that means "to prophesy" or "to foretell." It is often used in the context of predicting or revealing future events or divine messages.
