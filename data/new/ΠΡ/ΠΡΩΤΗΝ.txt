Etymology: The word "πρωτην" (pronounced "protin") comes from the Greek word "πρῶτος" (prōtos), meaning "first" or "foremost".

Grammar: "Πρωτην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Πρωτην" is a Greek word that means "first" or "foremost". It can be used to describe something that is the initial or primary in a sequence or ranking. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is of utmost importance or significance.
