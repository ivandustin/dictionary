Etymology: The word "προσέλθε" (proselthe) comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "Προσέλθε" is the imperative form of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to approach or come near.

Description: "Προσέλθε" is a word used in Greek to invite or instruct someone to come closer or approach a specific location or person. It is often used in formal or ceremonial contexts, such as in religious services or official gatherings.
