Etymology: The word "προσελθούσαι" comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near." It is formed by combining the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Προσελθούσαι" is the aorist participle form of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai) in the feminine gender, plural number, and nominative case. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The participle form functions as an adjective and can be used to describe the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Προσελθούσαι" describes a group of female subjects who have approached or come near to a certain location or person. The context of the sentence will determine the specific meaning and usage of the word.
