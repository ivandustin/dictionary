Etymology: The word "προσεφώνησεν" comes from the Greek verb "προσεφωνέω" (prosephōnéō), which is a compound word consisting of "πρός" (pros) meaning "towards" or "to" and "φωνέω" (phōnéō) meaning "to speak" or "to call out". 

Grammar: "προσεφώνησεν" is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb "προσεφωνέω" in Greek. It is used to indicate that someone called out or spoke to someone else in the past.

Description: "προσεφώνησεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it called out" or "he/she/it spoke to". It is commonly used in Greek literature and conversation to describe an action of calling out or speaking to someone.
