Etymology: The word "προσδοκώντες" comes from the Greek verb "προσδοκάω" (prosdokáo), which means "to expect" or "to anticipate." The suffix "-ντες" (-ntes) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or in progress.

Grammar: "Προσδοκώντες" is the present participle form of the verb "προσδοκάω" in the plural, masculine, nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Προσδοκώντες" describes a group of people who are currently expecting or anticipating something. It implies that they have a certain level of anticipation or hope for a particular outcome or event.
