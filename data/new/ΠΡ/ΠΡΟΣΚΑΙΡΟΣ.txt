Etymology: The word "προσκαιρος" (proskairos) comes from the Greek roots "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "for," and "καιρος" (kairos), meaning "time." Together, they form the meaning of "temporary" or "transient."

Grammar: In Greek, "προσκαιρος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Προσκαιρος" is used to describe something that is temporary, fleeting, or not lasting. It refers to things that are not permanent or enduring, but rather exist for a limited period of time.
