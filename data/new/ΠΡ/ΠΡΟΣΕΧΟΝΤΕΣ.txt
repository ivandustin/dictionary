Etymology: The word "προσεχοντες" (prosechontes) comes from the Greek verb "προσέχω" (prosechō), which means "to pay attention to" or "to be careful of." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "ἔχω" (echō), meaning "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar: "Προσεχοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "προσέχω" (prosechō), which means "paying attention" or "being careful." In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states.

Description: "Προσεχοντες" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of being attentive, watchful, or cautious. It implies actively focusing one's attention on something or someone, and being mindful of potential dangers or risks.
