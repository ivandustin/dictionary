It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before," and the noun "γνῶσις" (gnosis), meaning "knowledge."

Grammar: "Προγνωσιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Προγνωσιν" refers to the act or ability of predicting or foretelling future events or outcomes. It can also refer to a forecast or prognosis made by someone with specialized knowledge or expertise in a particular field.
