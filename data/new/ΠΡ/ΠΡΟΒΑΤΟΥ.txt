Etymology: The word "προβατού" (pronounced "provatou") comes from the Greek word "πρόβατο" (pronounced "provato"), which means "sheep". 

Grammar: "Προβατού" is the genitive form of the noun "πρόβατο". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Προβατού" refers to something that belongs to or is related to a sheep. It can be used to describe various things associated with sheep, such as sheep's milk, sheep's wool, or sheep's cheese.
