Etymology: The word "προφητικόν" (prophetikon) comes from the Greek word "προφήτης" (prophetes), meaning "prophet". It is derived from the verb "προφητεύω" (propheteuo), which means "to prophesy" or "to foretell".

Grammar: In Greek, "προφητικόν" is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and follows the regular pattern for neuter nouns.

Description: "Προφητικόν" is a Greek word that translates to "prophetic" in English. It is used to describe something that is related to or characteristic of a prophet or prophecy. It can refer to predictions, revelations, or messages believed to be inspired by a divine source.
