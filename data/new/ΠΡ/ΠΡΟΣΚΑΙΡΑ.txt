Etymology: The word "προσκαίρα" comes from the Greek words "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "for," and "καιρός" (kairos), meaning "time" or "opportunity." 

Grammar: "Προσκαίρα" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in various grammatical cases depending on its function in a sentence.

Description: "Προσκαίρα" refers to a temporary or transient situation, event, or circumstance. It implies that something is not permanent or lasting, but rather exists for a limited period of time. It can also be used to describe an opportune or favorable moment or occasion.
