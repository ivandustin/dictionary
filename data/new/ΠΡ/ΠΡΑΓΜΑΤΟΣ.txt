Etymology: The word "πραγματος" (pragmatos) comes from the Greek word "πρᾶγμα" (pragma), meaning "thing" or "matter". 

Grammar: "Πραγματος" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Πραγματος" refers to a thing or matter. It can be used to describe an object, an event, or a concept. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case, number, and gender. "Πραγματος" is the genitive singular form of the noun.
