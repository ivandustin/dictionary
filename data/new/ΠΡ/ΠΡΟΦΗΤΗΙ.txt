Etymology: The word "προφητης" (prophetes) comes from the Greek verb "προφημι" (prophemi), which means "to speak forth" or "to proclaim." In ancient Greece, a prophet was someone who spoke on behalf of the gods and delivered messages or predictions.

Grammar: In Greek, "προφητης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has a stem ending in -ητ-. The plural form is "προφηται" (prophetai).

Description: The word "προφητης" refers to a prophet, someone who is believed to have the ability to communicate with the divine and receive revelations or predictions about the future. In various religious traditions, prophets are seen as intermediaries between humans and the divine, delivering messages, teachings, or warnings. Prophets have played significant roles in many cultures throughout history, including ancient Greece, Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.
