Etymology: The word "πράσσεις" (prássis) comes from the Greek verb "πράσσω" (prássō), which means "to do" or "to act".

Grammar: "Πράσσεις" is the plural form of the noun "πράξη" (práxi), which means "action" or "deed". It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Πράσσεις" refers to actions or deeds that someone performs. It can also be used to describe the behavior or conduct of a person.
