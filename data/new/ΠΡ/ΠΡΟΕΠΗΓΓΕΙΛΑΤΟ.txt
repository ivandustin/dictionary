Etymology: The word "προεπηγγείλατο" comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "prior to," and "επηγγέλλομαι" (epengéllomai), meaning "to promise" or "to announce." 

Grammar: "Προεπηγγείλατο" is a verb in the third person singular, past tense, middle voice form. It is derived from the verb "προεπαγγέλλομαι" (proepangéllomai), which means "to foretell" or "to announce beforehand." 

Description: "Προεπηγγείλατο" is used to describe an action that was promised or announced in advance. It implies that the action was foretold or announced before it actually happened.
