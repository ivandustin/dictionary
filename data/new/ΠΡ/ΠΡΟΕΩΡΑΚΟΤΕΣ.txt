Etymology: The word "προεωρακότες" comes from the Greek verb "προοράω" (proorao), which means "to foresee" or "to anticipate." The suffix "-κότες" (-kotes) is added to the verb stem to form a noun that denotes people who have foreseen or anticipated something.

Grammar: "Προεωρακότες" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is declined as follows:

- Nominative: οι προεωρακότες (oi proeōrakótes)
- Genitive: των προεωρακότων (tōn proeōrakóton)
- Accusative: τους προεωρακότες (tous proeōrakótes)
- Vocative: προεωρακότες (proeōrakótes)

Description: "Προεωρακότες" refers to people who have foreseen or anticipated something. It can be used to describe individuals who have predicted future events or outcomes based on their knowledge, experience, or intuition.
