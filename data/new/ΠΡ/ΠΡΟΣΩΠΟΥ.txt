Etymology: The word "προσωπού" (prosopou) comes from the Greek word "πρόσωπο" (prosopo), which means "face" or "countenance".

Grammar: "Προσωπού" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Προσωπού" refers to the genitive form of the word "πρόσωπο" (prosopo), which means "face" or "countenance". In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or origin. So, "προσωπού" can be translated as "of the face" or "of the countenance".
