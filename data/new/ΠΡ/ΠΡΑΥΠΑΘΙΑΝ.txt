It is derived from the combination of the Greek words "πραΰς" (praus), meaning "gentle" or "meek," and "πάθος" (pathos), meaning "suffering" or "passion."

Grammar: "πραυπαθιαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.
