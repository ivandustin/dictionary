Etymology: The word "πραξαντες" (praxantes) comes from the Greek verb "πράσσω" (prasso), which means "to do" or "to act". The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who have performed the action.

Grammar: "Πραξαντες" is the plural form of the word, referring to multiple people who have performed an action. It is a masculine noun in the nominative case.

Description: "Πραξαντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "those who have acted" or "those who have done". It is often used to refer to individuals who have taken specific actions or carried out certain tasks.
