Etymology: The word "προισταμένους" comes from the Greek verb "προΐστημι" (proistēmi), which means "to stand before" or "to preside over." The suffix "-ους" (-ous) indicates the plural form.

Grammar: "Προισταμένους" is the accusative plural form of the noun "προιστάμενος" (proistamenos), which is a participle of the verb "προΐστημι" (proistēmi). In Greek grammar, the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Προισταμένους" refers to a group of people who are in a position of authority or leadership. It can be translated as "superiors" or "those in charge."
