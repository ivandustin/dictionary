Etymology: The word "προφητεία" comes from the Greek word "προφήτης" (prophetes), which means "prophet". It is derived from the verb "φημί" (phemi), meaning "to speak" or "to declare".

Grammar: "Προφητεία" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "προφητείες" (propheteies).

Description: "Προφητεία" refers to the act or practice of prophesying or predicting the future. It can also refer to a prediction or a statement about the future, often believed to be inspired by a divine source. In religious contexts, it is often associated with the messages or revelations delivered by prophets.
