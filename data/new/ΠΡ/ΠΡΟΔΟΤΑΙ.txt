Etymology: The word "προδοται" (pronounced "prodote") comes from the Greek verb "προδίδωμι" (pronounced "prodídomee"), which means "to betray" or "to hand over." 

Grammar: "Προδοται" is the plural form of the noun "προδότης" (pronounced "prodotis"), which means "traitor" in Greek. It is used to refer to multiple individuals who betray or deceive others.

Description: "Προδοται" is a term used to describe people who betray the trust or loyalty of others. It is often used in a negative context to refer to individuals who betray their friends, family, or country.
