Etymology: The word "προσαίτων" comes from the Greek word "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to," and "αἴτησις" (aitēsis), meaning "request" or "petition." 

Grammar: "προσαίτων" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the plural form of the word "πρόσαιτος" (prosaitos), which means "suppliant" or "petitioner." 

Description: "προσαίτων" refers to a group of people who make requests or petitions. In ancient Greece, they were individuals who sought help or assistance from a higher authority, such as a deity or a powerful person. They would approach the person or entity with humility and present their request or plea.
