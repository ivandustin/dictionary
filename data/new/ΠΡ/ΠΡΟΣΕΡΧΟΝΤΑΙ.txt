Etymology: The word "προσερχονται" (proserchontai) is derived from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". It is formed by combining the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to", with the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "προσερχονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening in the present time.

Description: "προσερχονται" is a Greek word that translates to "they approach" or "they come near" in English. It describes the action of a group of people or things moving towards a specific location or person.
