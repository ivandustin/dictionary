Etymology: The word "προσεκαλεσατο" comes from the Greek verb "προσκαλέω" (proskaleo), which means "to call to" or "to invite." The prefix "προς" (pros) indicates direction or movement towards, and "καλέω" (kaleo) means "to call."

Grammar: "Προσεκαλεσατο" is the third person singular aorist middle indicative form of the verb "προσκαλέω" (proskaleo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on or for themselves.

Description: "Προσεκαλεσατο" can be translated as "he/she/it called to himself/herself/itself" or "he/she/it invited himself/herself/itself." It implies that the subject initiated the action of calling or inviting.
