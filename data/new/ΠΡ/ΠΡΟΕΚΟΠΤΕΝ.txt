Etymology: The word "προεκπτεν" (proekpten) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἐκπτεν" (ekpten), meaning "to extend" or "to stretch out." 

Grammar: "Προεκπτεν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, indicative mood, active voice, and third person singular form. 

Description: The word "προεκπτεν" is used to describe the action of extending or stretching something out in front of or before something else. It implies the act of elongating or lengthening an object or a concept.
