Etymology: The word "προσκαιρον" comes from the Greek words "προς" (pros), meaning "towards" or "for", and "καιρός" (kairos), meaning "time". It literally translates to "for a time" or "temporary".

Grammar: In Greek, "προσκαιρον" is an adjective in the neuter gender. It is used to describe something that is temporary or transient.

Description: "προσκαιρον" is used to describe something that is not permanent or lasting. It refers to things that are temporary in nature, existing or occurring for a limited period of time.
