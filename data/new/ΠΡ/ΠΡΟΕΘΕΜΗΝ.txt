Etymology: The word "προεθεμην" (pronounced "proethemen") comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἔθεμην" (ethemen), meaning "I placed" or "I set." 

Grammar: "Προεθεμην" is the first person singular, aorist tense form of the verb "προτίθημι" (protithemi), which means "I propose" or "I suggest" in Greek. It is used to express an action that occurred in the past.

Description: "Προεθεμην" is a Greek verb that indicates an action of proposing or suggesting something in the past. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as in discussions, debates, or decision-making processes.
