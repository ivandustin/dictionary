Etymology: The word "προημαρτηκοσιν" (proēmartēkosin) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before," and "ἡμαρτηκόσιν" (hēmartēkosin), meaning "having sinned." 

Grammar: "Προημαρτηκοσιν" is a verb form in the Greek language. It is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood, active voice form of the verb "προαμαρτάνω" (proamartanō), which means "to sin beforehand" or "to have sinned previously."

Description: "Προημαρτηκοσιν" is used to describe an action of sinning that occurred before a certain point in time. It indicates that the action of sinning took place in the past, prior to another event or time frame.
