Etymology: The word "προσεχείν" (prosechein) comes from the Greek roots "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and "ἔχειν" (echein), meaning "to have." It literally means "to have towards" or "to pay attention to."

Grammar: "Προσεχείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ προσέχω (prosecho) - I pay attention, εσύ προσέχεις (prosecheis) - you pay attention, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό προσέχει (prosechei) - he/she/it pays attention, εμείς προσέχουμε (prosechoume) - we pay attention, εσείς προσέχετε (prosechete) - you pay attention, αυτοί/αυτές προσέχουν (prosechoun) - they pay attention.

Description: "Προσεχείν" is a Greek verb that means "to pay attention," "to be careful," or "to take care." It implies focusing one's mind and senses on something or someone, being cautious, and giving consideration to details or potential dangers. It is often used in contexts where vigilance, attentiveness, or mindfulness is required.
