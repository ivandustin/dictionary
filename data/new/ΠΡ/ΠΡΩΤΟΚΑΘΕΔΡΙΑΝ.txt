Etymology: The word "πρωτοκαθεδρίαν" comes from the Greek words "πρώτος" (protos) meaning "first" and "καθέδρα" (kathedra) meaning "chair" or "seat". 

Grammar: "Πρωτοκαθεδρίαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. 

Description: "Πρωτοκαθεδρίαν" refers to the position or role of being the first or chief chairperson or chairwoman. It is often used in academic or institutional contexts to denote the highest-ranking or most prestigious chair or professorship.
