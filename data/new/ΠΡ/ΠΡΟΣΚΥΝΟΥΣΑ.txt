Etymology: The word "προσκυνούσα" comes from the Greek verb "προσκυνώ" (proskynó), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kynéo), meaning "to kiss" or "to bow down."

Grammar: "Προσκυνούσα" is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb "προσκυνώ" (proskynó). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The feminine form indicates that the subject of the verb is a female.

Description: "Προσκυνούσα" is a Greek word that can be translated as "worshipping" or "bowing down before." It describes the action of showing reverence, respect, or adoration towards a deity, a person of higher status, or an object of veneration. It implies a gesture of submission or devotion.
