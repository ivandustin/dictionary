Etymology: The word "πραυτητος" (prautētos) comes from the Greek root "πραΰς" (praus), meaning gentle or meek.

Grammar: In Greek, "πραυτητος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πραυτητος" describes someone or something that is gentle, meek, or mild in nature. It can refer to a person's character or behavior, as well as to the quality of an object or situation.
