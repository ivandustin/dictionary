Etymology: The word "προστατης" (prostatis) comes from the Greek verb "προστατεω" (prostateo), which means "to stand before" or "to protect." It is derived from the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "ιστημι" (istemi), meaning "to stand."

Grammar: In Greek, "προστατης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "προστατεις" (prostateis).

Description: "Προστατης" is a Greek word that translates to "protector" or "guardian" in English. It refers to someone who stands before or protects others, often used to describe a person who defends or supports someone or something.
