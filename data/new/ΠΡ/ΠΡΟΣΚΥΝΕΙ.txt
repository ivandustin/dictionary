Etymology: The word "προσκυνεί" comes from the Greek verb "προσκυνώ" (proskynó), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (prós), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kynéo), meaning "to kiss" or "to adore."

Grammar: "Προσκυνεί" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "προσκυνώ" (proskynó). It is used to describe the action of worshiping or showing reverence towards someone or something.

Description: "Προσκυνεί" is a Greek word that refers to the act of worshiping, bowing down, or showing reverence towards a deity, a religious figure, or a person of higher authority. It can also be used metaphorically to describe extreme admiration or respect towards someone or something.
