Etymology: The word "πρηνης" (prēnēs) comes from the Greek word "πρῆν" (prēn), which means "burning" or "fiery."

Grammar: In Greek, "πρηνης" is an adjective that is used to describe something or someone as "burning" or "fiery." It can be used to describe both literal and figurative qualities.

Description: "Πρηνης" is often used to describe something that is intensely hot or burning, such as a fire or a burning sensation. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is passionate, intense, or full of energy.
