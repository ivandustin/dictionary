Etymology: The word "προσλαλούντες" comes from the Greek verb "προσλαλέω" (proslaléo), which means "to speak to" or "to address." The suffix "-ούντες" (-ountes) is added to the verb stem to indicate the present participle form, which means "speaking to" or "addressing."

Grammar: "Προσλαλούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "προσλαλέω" (proslaléo) in Greek. It is used to describe people who are currently speaking to or addressing someone or something.

Description: "Προσλαλούντες" is a Greek word that refers to individuals who are speaking to or addressing someone or something. It can be used to describe people engaged in conversation, giving speeches, or simply talking to others.
