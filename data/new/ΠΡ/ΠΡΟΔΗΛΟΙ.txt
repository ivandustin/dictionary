Etymology: The word "προδηλοι" (prodēloi) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "δηλος" (dēlos), meaning "clear" or "evident." 

Grammar: "Προδηλοι" is the plural form of the adjective "προδηλος" (prodēlos) in Greek. It is used to describe something that is clearly or obviously evident or apparent.

Description: "Προδηλοι" is an adjective that is used to emphasize the clarity or obviousness of something. It is often used to describe situations, facts, or evidence that are so clear and evident that they cannot be disputed or ignored.
