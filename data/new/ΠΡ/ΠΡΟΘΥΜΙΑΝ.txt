Etymology: The word "προθυμίαν" (prothymían) comes from the Greek root words "προ" (pro), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "θυμός" (thymós), meaning "mind" or "soul." Together, they form the word "προθυμίαν," which can be translated as "readiness" or "eagerness."

Grammar: In Greek, "προθυμίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative form of the word "προθυμία" (prothymía), which is a feminine noun. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence, such as subject, object, or possessive.

Description: "Προθυμίαν" refers to a state of being ready or eager to do something. It implies a positive attitude and willingness to take action. The word can be used in various contexts, such as expressing enthusiasm, motivation, or preparedness.
