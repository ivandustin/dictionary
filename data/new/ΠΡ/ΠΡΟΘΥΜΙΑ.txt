Etymology: The word "προθυμία" comes from the Greek roots "προ-" meaning "before" and "θυμός" meaning "mind" or "soul". It can be translated as "readiness" or "eagerness".

Grammar: In Greek, "προθυμία" is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: η προθυμία (i prothymía) 
- Genitive: της προθυμίας (tis prothymías)
- Accusative: την προθυμία (tin prothymía)
- Vocative: προθυμία (prothymía)

Plural:
- Nominative: οι προθυμίες (i prothymíes)
- Genitive: των προθυμιών (ton prothymión)
- Accusative: τις προθυμίες (tis prothymíes)
- Vocative: προθυμίες (prothymíes)

Description: "Προθυμία" refers to a positive attitude or willingness to do something. It implies enthusiasm, eagerness, or readiness to take action. It can be used to describe someone's motivation, interest, or proactive approach towards a task or goal.
