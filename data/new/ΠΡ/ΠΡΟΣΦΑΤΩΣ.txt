Etymology: The word "προσφατως" (prosphatōs) is derived from the Greek words "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "in addition to," and "φαίνω" (phainō), meaning "to show" or "to appear." 

Grammar: "Προσφατως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or event that has occurred recently or in the near past. It is often translated as "recently," "lately," or "of late" in English.

Description: "Προσφατως" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to indicate the recency of an action or event. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts to express the idea of something happening not long ago.
