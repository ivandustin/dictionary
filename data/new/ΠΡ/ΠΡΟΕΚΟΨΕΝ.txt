Etymology: The word "προεκψεν" (proekpsen) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἔκψημα" (ekpsēma), meaning "projection" or "ejection."

Grammar: "Προεκψεν" is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, indicative mood, and third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "προεκπίπτω" (proekpiptō), which means "to project forward" or "to throw out."

Description: "Προεκψεν" is a Greek word that describes an action of projecting or throwing something forward. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically throwing an object or metaphorically projecting an idea or concept.
