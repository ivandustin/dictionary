Etymology: The word "προσκυνήσωσιν" (proskynēsōsin) comes from the Greek verb "προσκυνέω" (proskynéō), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (prós), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kynéō), meaning "to kiss" or "to bow down."

Grammar: "Προσκυνήσωσιν" is a verb in the third person plural, aorist tense, subjunctive mood, and active voice. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, the subjunctive mood expresses possibility or potentiality, and the active voice indicates that the subject performs the action.

Description: "Προσκυνήσωσιν" is a Greek word that means "they may worship" or "they may bow down." It is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts to describe the act of showing reverence, respect, or adoration towards a deity, a sacred object, or a person of higher authority.
