Etymology: The word "προαγαγείν" (proagagein) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "forward," and "ἄγω" (ago), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." 

Grammar: "Προαγαγείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, suggesting that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Προαγαγείν" means "to promote" or "to advance" in English. It refers to the act of moving something or someone forward or ahead, often in a hierarchical or professional context.
