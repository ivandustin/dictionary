Etymology: The word "προηγαγον" (proēgagon) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἡγέομαι" (hēgéomai), meaning "to lead" or "to go before." 

Grammar: "Προηγαγον" is the first person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "προηγάγω" (proēgagō), which means "I led" or "I went before." It is used to express an action that occurred in the past.

Description: "Προηγαγον" is a Greek word that describes the action of leading or going before something or someone. It implies taking the initiative or being in a position of leadership.
