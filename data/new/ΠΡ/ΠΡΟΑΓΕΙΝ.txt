Etymology: The word "προαγείν" (proageín) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἄγειν" (ágein), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." 

Grammar: "προαγείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "προαγείν" means "to lead forward" or "to bring forward" in Greek. It implies the action of guiding or taking someone or something ahead or in advance.
