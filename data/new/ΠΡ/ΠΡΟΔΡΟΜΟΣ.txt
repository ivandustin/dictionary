Etymology: The word "πρόδρομος" (pronounced "pro-dro-mos") comes from the Greek roots "πρό" (pro), meaning "before," and "δρόμος" (dromos), meaning "course" or "path." Therefore, "πρόδρομος" can be translated as "forerunner" or "precursor."

Grammar: In Greek, "πρόδρομος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe a person, thing, or event that comes before or prepares the way for something else.

Description: The word "πρόδρομος" is often used metaphorically to describe someone or something that precedes or prepares the way for another person, thing, or event. It can refer to a pioneer, a trailblazer, or a herald. In religious contexts, it is commonly used to refer to John the Baptist, who is considered the forerunner of Jesus Christ in Christian tradition.
