Etymology: The word "προσκυνήσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "προσκυνέω" (proskuneo), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kuneo), meaning "to kiss."

Grammar: "Προσκυνήσουσιν" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb "προσκυνέω" (proskuneo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The "-σουσιν" ending indicates the future tense and the third person plural.

Description: "Προσκυνήσουσιν" is a Greek word that means "they will worship" or "they will bow down before." It is used to express an action that will happen in the future, where a group of people will show reverence, respect, or adoration towards someone or something.
