Etymology: The word "προφητεία" (pronounced "pro-fē-teí-a") comes from the Greek word "προφήτης" (prophētēs), meaning "prophet". It is derived from the verb "προφητεύω" (prophēteúō), which means "to prophesy" or "to foretell".

Grammar: In Greek, "προφητεία" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular and can be used in both the nominative and accusative cases. The plural form is "προφητείες" (prophēteíes).

Description: "Προφητεία" refers to the act or practice of prophesying or foretelling future events. It can also refer to a prediction or a message believed to be inspired by a divine source. In various religious and spiritual contexts, "προφητεία" is often associated with the ability to receive and communicate messages from a higher power.
