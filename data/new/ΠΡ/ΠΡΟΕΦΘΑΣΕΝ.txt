Etymology: The word "προεφθασεν" (pronounced "proefthasen") comes from the Greek verb "προεφθάνω" (pronounced "proefthano"), which means "to anticipate" or "to precede." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προ-" (pronounced "pro-"), meaning "before," and the verb "εφθάνω" (pronounced "efthano"), meaning "to arrive" or "to reach."

Grammar: "προεφθασεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "προεφθάνω." In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "προεφθασεν" can be translated as "he/she/it anticipated" or "he/she/it preceded" in English. It describes an action that happened in the past, where someone or something arrived or reached a destination before another person or thing.
