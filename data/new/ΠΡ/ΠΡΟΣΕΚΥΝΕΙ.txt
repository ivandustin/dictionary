Etymology: The word "προσεκυνεῖ" (prosekynei) comes from the Greek verb "προσκυνέω" (proskuneo), which means "to worship" or "to bow down before." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "κυνέω" (kuneo), meaning "to kiss" or "to adore."

Grammar: "Προσεκυνεῖ" is the third person singular present active indicative form of the verb "προσκυνέω" (proskuneo). In Greek grammar, this form indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of worshiping or bowing down before someone or something.

Description: "Προσεκυνεῖ" is a Greek word that describes the act of worshiping or showing reverence to a deity, a person of higher status, or an object of veneration. It implies a deep respect, submission, and adoration towards the entity being worshiped. This word is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts to express devotion and honor.
