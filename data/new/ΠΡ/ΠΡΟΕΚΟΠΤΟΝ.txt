Etymology: The word "προεκπτον" (proekpton) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "εκπτον" (ekpton), meaning "to throw out" or "to project." 

Grammar: "Προεκπτον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" (-on) in the nominative case.

Description: "Προεκπτον" refers to a projection or a protrusion that extends forward or outward. It can be used to describe physical objects or abstract concepts.
