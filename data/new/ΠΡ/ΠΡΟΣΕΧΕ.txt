Etymology: The word "προσεχε" (proseche) comes from the Greek verb "προσέχω" (prosecho), which means "to pay attention" or "to be careful." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "ἔχω" (echo), meaning "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar: "Προσεχε" is the imperative form of the verb "προσέχω" (prosecho) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to pay attention or be careful.

Description: The word "προσεχε" is used to urge someone to be attentive or cautious. It is often used as a warning or reminder to stay focused and mindful of one's surroundings or actions.
