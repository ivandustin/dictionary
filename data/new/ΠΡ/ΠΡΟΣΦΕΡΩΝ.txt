Etymology: The word "προσφέρων" (prosferon) comes from the Greek verb "προσφέρω" (prosfero), which means "to offer" or "to present". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to", and the verb "φέρω" (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bring".

Grammar: "Προσφέρων" is the present participle form of the verb "προσφέρω" (prosfero). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Προσφέρων" is a Greek word that can be translated as "offering" or "presenting". It refers to someone or something that is currently offering or presenting something.
