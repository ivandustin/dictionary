Etymology: The word "προσεκψαν" (prosepsan) comes from the Greek verb "προσέχω" (prosecho), which means "to pay attention" or "to be careful." The suffix "-ψαν" (-psan) is a past tense ending.

Grammar: "Προσεκψαν" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb "προσέχω" (prosecho). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action of paying attention or being careful happened in the past, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is multiple people.

Description: "Προσεκψαν" is a Greek verb that means "they paid attention" or "they were careful." It describes an action that multiple people performed in the past.
