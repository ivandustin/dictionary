Etymology: The word "πρωτον" (prōton) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word "πρῶτος" (prōtos), meaning "first."

Grammar: In Greek, "πρωτον" (prōton) is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the first declension and has the nominative case ending "-ον" (-on).

Description: "Πρωτον" (prōton) is the Greek word for "first." It is often used to refer to the first element or the primary component of something. In physics, it specifically refers to the proton, which is a subatomic particle with a positive charge found in the nucleus of an atom.
