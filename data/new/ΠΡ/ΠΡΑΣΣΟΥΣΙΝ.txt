Etymology: The word "πρασσουσιν" (prassousin) comes from the Greek verb "πράσσω" (prasso), which means "to do" or "to act". It is conjugated in the present tense, third person plural form.

Grammar: "Πρασσουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject and tense. The "-ουσιν" (-ousin) ending indicates the third person plural form.

Description: "Πρασσουσιν" is used to describe an action being performed by a third person plural subject in the present tense. It can be translated as "they do" or "they act" in English.
