Etymology: The word "προσελθών" comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near." It is formed by combining the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Προσελθών" is the aorist participle form of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The participle form indicates that the action of approaching or coming near has been completed.

Description: "Προσελθών" is a Greek word that can be translated as "having approached" or "having come near." It is often used in a narrative context to describe someone who has come close to someone or something.
