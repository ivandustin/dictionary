Etymology: The word "προσοφείλεις" comes from the Greek verb "προσοφείλω" (prosofeílo), which means "to owe" or "to be indebted to." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "οφείλω" (ofeílo), meaning "to owe."

Grammar: "Προσοφείλεις" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "προσοφείλω" (prosofeílo). It is used when addressing one person and means "you owe" or "you are indebted to."

Description: "Προσοφείλεις" is a Greek word that expresses the concept of owing or being indebted to someone. It is used to indicate a financial or moral obligation towards another person or entity.
