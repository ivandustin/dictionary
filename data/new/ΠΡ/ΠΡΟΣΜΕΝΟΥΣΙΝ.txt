Etymology: The word "προσμενουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "προσμένω" (prosméno), which means "to wait for" or "to expect." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "μένω" (méno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain."

Grammar: "προσμενουσιν" is the present participle form of the verb "προσμένω" (prosméno) in the third person plural. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "προσμενουσιν" describes a group of people or things that are currently waiting or expecting something. It implies that the action of waiting or expecting is ongoing or continuous.
