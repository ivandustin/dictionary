Etymology: The word "προσωπον" (prosopon) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the preposition "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the noun "ωπον" (opon), meaning "face." Together, they form the word "προσωπον" (prosopon), which literally translates to "towards the face."

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "προσωπον" (prosopon) is a neuter noun of the third declension. It can be used in both the singular and plural forms. The plural form is "προσωπα" (prosopa).

Description: "Προσωπον" (prosopon) refers to the face or the front part of the head. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person's identity, presence, or character. In ancient Greek drama, actors would wear masks called "προσωπα" (prosopa) to represent different characters. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to the face.
