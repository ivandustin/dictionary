Etymology: The word "προετειναν" comes from the Greek verb "προτείνω" (proteíno), which means "to propose" or "to suggest". It is formed by combining the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of", and the verb "τείνω" (teíno), meaning "to stretch" or "to extend".

Grammar: "προετειναν" is the third person plural, past tense form of the verb "προτείνω" (proteíno). In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The past tense indicates that the action of proposing or suggesting occurred in the past, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people.

Description: "προετειναν" is a Greek word that means "they proposed" or "they suggested". It refers to an action performed by a group of people in the past, where they put forward an idea or recommendation.
