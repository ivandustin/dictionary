Etymology: The word "προσεταγμένη" comes from the Greek verb "προστάσσω" (prostássō), which means "to command" or "to order." The suffix "-μένη" (-méni) is added to the verb stem to form the passive participle, indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "Προσεταγμένη" is the feminine singular form of the passive participle of the verb "προστάσσω" (prostássō). It is used to describe a noun that is receiving the action of being commanded or ordered.

Description: "Προσεταγμένη" is an adjective that describes something or someone that has been commanded or ordered. It implies that the subject is under an obligation to follow the given command or order.
