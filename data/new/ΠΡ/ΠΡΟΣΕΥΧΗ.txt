Etymology: The word "προσευχή" (proseuchē) comes from the Greek verb "προσεύχομαι" (proseuchomai), which means "to pray" or "to make a request." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "εὔχομαι" (euchomai), meaning "to wish" or "to pray."

Grammar: In Greek, "προσευχή" is a feminine noun. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can take different cases depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Προσευχή" is the Greek word for "prayer." It refers to the act of communicating with a higher power, expressing reverence, supplication, or thanksgiving. In various religious traditions, prayer plays a significant role as a means of connecting with the divine, seeking guidance, and offering devotion.
