Etymology: The word "προεθετο" (proetheto) comes from the Greek roots "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ἐθέτο" (etheto), meaning "placed" or "set." 

Grammar: "προεθετο" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "προτίθημι" (protithemi), which means "to propose" or "to set forth."

Description: "προεθετο" is used to describe an action that was proposed or set forth in the past. It indicates that something was suggested or put forward before a certain point in time.
