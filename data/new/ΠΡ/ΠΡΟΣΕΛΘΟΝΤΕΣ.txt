Etymology: The word "προσελθοντες" comes from the Greek verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai), which means "to approach" or "to come near". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "πρός" (pros), meaning "towards" or "to", and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come".

Grammar: "προσελθοντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "προσέρχομαι" (proserchomai) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the participle form functions as an adjective. In this case, "προσελθοντες" means "having approached" or "having come near".

Description: "προσελθοντες" is a Greek word that describes the action of approaching or coming near to something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a physical sense of physically moving towards an object or person, or in a metaphorical sense of approaching a problem or situation.
