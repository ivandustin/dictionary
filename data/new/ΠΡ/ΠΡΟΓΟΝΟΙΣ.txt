Etymology: The word "προγονοις" (pronounced "progonis") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "prior to," and the noun "γονείς" (goneis), meaning "parents" or "ancestors."

Grammar: In Greek, "προγονοις" is a noun in the dative case. It is the plural form of the word "προγονός" (progonos), which means "ancestor" or "forefather." The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Προγονοις" refers to a group of ancestors or forefathers. It can be used to describe a collective group of people who came before and are connected through a lineage or family tree. The word emphasizes the importance of one's ancestry and the influence that previous generations have on the present.
