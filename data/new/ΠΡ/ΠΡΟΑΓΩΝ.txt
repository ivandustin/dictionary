Etymology: The word "προαγων" (proagon) comes from the Greek words "προ" (pro), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "αγων" (agon), meaning "contest" or "competition." 

Grammar: "προαγων" is a noun in the singular form. 

Description: In Greek, "προαγων" refers to a preliminary or qualifying round in a competition or contest. It is used to describe an event or stage that takes place before the main competition, where participants compete to qualify for the next round.
