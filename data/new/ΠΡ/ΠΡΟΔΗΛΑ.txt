Etymology: The word "προδηλα" (pronounced prodila) comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word consisting of "προ" (pro), meaning "before" or "in front of," and "δηλα" (dila), meaning "clear" or "evident." Therefore, "προδηλα" can be translated as "before clear" or "evident beforehand."

Grammar: In Greek, "προδηλα" is an adverb. It is used to describe something that is evident or clear before a certain event or situation.

Description: "προδηλα" is used to indicate that something is obvious or apparent before it actually happens or becomes clear. It implies that the outcome or result is already known or predictable based on existing evidence or circumstances.
