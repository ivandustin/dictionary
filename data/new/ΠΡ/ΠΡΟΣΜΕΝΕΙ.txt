Etymology: The word "προσμενεί" comes from the Greek verb "προσμένω" (prosméno), which means "to wait for" or "to expect." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "μένω" (meno), meaning "to stay" or "to remain."

Grammar: "Προσμενεί" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "προσμένω" (prosméno). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated according to person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "Προσμενεί" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it waits" or "he/she/it expects." It is used to describe the action of waiting for something or someone, or having an expectation about a future event or outcome.
