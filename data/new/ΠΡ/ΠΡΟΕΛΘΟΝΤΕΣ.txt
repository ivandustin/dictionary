Etymology: The word "προελθόντες" comes from the Greek verb "προέρχομαι" (proérchomai), which means "to come forth" or "to originate from." It is formed by combining the prefix "προ-" (pro-), meaning "before" or "prior to," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "προελθόντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "προέρχομαι" (proérchomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The participle form indicates that the action of "coming forth" has been completed.

Description: "προελθόντες" can be translated as "having come forth" or "having originated from." It is used to describe something or someone that has emerged or appeared from a previous state or location.
