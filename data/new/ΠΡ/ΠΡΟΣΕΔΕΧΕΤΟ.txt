Etymology: The word "προσεδέχετο" comes from the Greek verb "προσδέχομαι" (prosdechomai), which means "to receive" or "to welcome." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "προς" (pros), meaning "towards," and the verb "δέχομαι" (dechomai), meaning "to receive."

Grammar: "Προσεδέχετο" is the third person singular, imperfect tense form of the verb "προσδέχομαι" (prosdechomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a third person singular pronoun, such as "he," "she," or "it."

Description: "Προσεδέχετο" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was receiving" or "he/she/it was welcoming." It describes an action that was happening in the past, with a focus on the ongoing or repeated nature of the action.
