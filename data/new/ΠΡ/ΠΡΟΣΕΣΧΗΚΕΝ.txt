Etymology: The word "προσεσχημέν" (proseschimen) comes from the Greek verb "προσέχω" (prosecho), which means "to pay attention" or "to be careful." The suffix "-μέν" (-men) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle.

Grammar: "Προσεσχημέν" is the past participle of the verb "προσέχω" (prosecho) in the active voice. It is used to describe something or someone that has received attention or has been careful in the past.

Description: "Προσεσχημέν" is an adjective that describes someone or something that has been attentive or careful. It implies that the person or thing has paid close attention to details or has been cautious in their actions.
