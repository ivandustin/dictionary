Word: θλιβεσθαι

Etymology: θλιβεσθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word θλίβω (thlívo), which means "to press, squeeze, or oppress." The suffix -εσθαι (-esthai) indicates that it is a middle/passive voice verb, meaning that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Grammar: θλιβεσθαι is a present tense, middle/passive voice verb in Greek. It is often translated as "to be afflicted, distressed, or troubled." The conjugation of this verb in the present tense is as follows:

- Singular: θλίβομαι (thlívomai) - I am afflicted/distressed/troubled
- θλίβεσαι (thlívesai) - You are afflicted/distressed/troubled
- θλίβεται (thlívetai) - He/she/it is afflicted/distressed/troubled

- Plural: θλιβόμαστε (thlívomaste) - We are afflicted/distressed/troubled
- θλίβεσθε (thlívesthe) - You (plural) are afflicted/distressed/troubled
- θλίβονται (thlívontai) - They are afflicted/distressed/troubled

Description: θλιβεσθαι is a verb that describes a state of being afflicted, distressed, or troubled. It can be used to describe a range of emotions, from sadness and grief to anxiety and fear. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the emotional state of characters who are facing difficult or challenging situations.
