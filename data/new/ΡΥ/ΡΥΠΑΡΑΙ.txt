Etymology: The word "ρυπαραί" (rhyparai) comes from the Greek word "ρυπαρός" (ryparos), which means "dirty" or "unclean".

Grammar: "Ρυπαραί" is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple dirty or unclean things.

Description: "Ρυπαραί" refers to dirty or unclean things. It can be used to describe physical objects that are soiled or contaminated, as well as metaphorical concepts or situations that are morally impure or morally corrupt.
