Word: βλεποντων

Etymology: βλεποντων is the present participle of the Greek verb βλέπω (blepō), which means "to see" or "to look". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰel- ("to shine, flash, gleam").

Grammar: βλεποντων is a present participle in the genitive case, which means it is used to describe a noun and indicates possession or relationship. In this case, it is describing a group of people who are seeing or looking at something.

Description: βλεποντων can be translated to "of those who are seeing" or "of those who are looking". It is often used in Greek literature and texts to describe a group of people who are observing something, such as a scene or an event. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is perceptive or insightful.
