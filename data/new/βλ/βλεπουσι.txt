The word βλεπουσι (blepousi) is a conjugated form of the Greek verb βλέπω (blepo), which means "to see" or "to look". It is in the present tense, third person plural, indicative mood. 

In terms of grammar, βλεπουσι is a regular verb in the present tense, and its conjugation follows the pattern of the verb βλέπω. 

Etymologically, βλέπω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰel- ("to shine, flash, gleam"), which is also the origin of the English words "bright" and "blaze". 

Overall, βλεπουσι means "they see" or "they are looking" in Greek.
