Word: βλασφημιαι

Etymology:
The word βλασφημιαι (blasphemiai) is derived from the Greek words βλασφημέω (blasphemeo) which means "to speak evil of" or "to slander" and αι (ai) which is a plural suffix. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to blasphemy or speaking against God.

Grammar:
βλασφημιαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "blasphemies" or "slanders".

Description:
βλασφημιαι refers to speech or actions that show disrespect or contempt towards God or sacred things. It is considered a serious sin in many religions and is often punished by excommunication or other forms of discipline. In the New Testament, Jesus warns against blasphemy of the Holy Spirit, which is considered an unforgivable sin. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any speech or action that is disrespectful or offensive.
