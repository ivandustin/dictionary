Word: βλεπομενον

Etymology: βλεπομενον is a present participle of the Greek verb βλέπω (blepō), which means "to see" or "to look". The word is formed by adding the suffix -μενον (-menon) to the stem βλεπ- (blep-).

Grammar: βλεπομενον is a neuter singular form of the present participle in the passive voice. It is used to describe something that is currently being seen or looked at, or something that is capable of being seen or looked at.

Description: βλεπομενον can be translated as "being seen" or "that which is seen". It is often used in philosophical and theological contexts to refer to the visible world or the realm of appearances, as opposed to the invisible world of ideas or the divine. In grammar, it is used to form the present passive participle, which is a common verb form in Greek.
