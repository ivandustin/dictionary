Word: βλασφημησωσιν

Etymology: βλασφημησωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the root words βλασφημία (blasphemia) meaning "blasphemy" and the verb suffix -σωσιν (-sōsin) indicating the third person plural future active indicative tense.

Grammar: βλασφημησωσιν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the future tense. It is in the third person plural, meaning that it refers to a group of people who will commit blasphemy in the future.

Description: βλασφημησωσιν is a strong word that refers to the act of speaking or acting in a way that shows disrespect or contempt towards God or sacred things.
