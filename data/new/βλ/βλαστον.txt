Word: βλαστον

Etymology:
The word βλαστον comes from the Greek word βλαστάνω (blastanō), which means "to sprout" or "to bud". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰleh₃- ("to bloom, blossom").

Grammar:
βλαστον is a neuter noun in Greek. It is in the nominative singular form. In the plural form, it becomes βλαστά.

Description:
βλαστον refers to a sprout or a shoot, especially of a plant. It can also refer to a bud or a germ. In biology, it is used to describe the early stages of growth in an organism. The word is often used in the context of agriculture and horticulture, where it refers to the young shoots of plants that are used for propagation. In Greek mythology, the word is associated with the goddess Persephone, who was the goddess of spring and the growth of plants.
