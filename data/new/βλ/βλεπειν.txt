Word: βλεπειν

Etymology:
βλεπειν is a Greek verb that means "to see". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰel- which means "to shine, flash, gleam". The word has been used in Greek literature since ancient times and is still used in modern Greek.

Grammar:
βλεπειν is an irregular verb in Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation and is conjugated as follows:

Present tense:
εγώ βλέπω (I see)
εσύ βλέπεις (you see)
αυτός/αυτή/αυτό βλέπει (he/she/it sees)
εμείς βλέπουμε (we see)
εσείς βλέπετε (you see)
αυτοί/αυτές βλέπουν (they see)

Description:
βλεπειν is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can be used to describe the act of physically seeing something, as well as the act of understanding or perceiving something. It can also be used in a metaphorical sense, such as "seeing" the truth or "seeing" the future. Overall, βλεπειν is a fundamental verb in the Greek language that is used frequently in everyday conversation and literature.
