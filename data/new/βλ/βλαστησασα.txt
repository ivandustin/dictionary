Word: βλαστησασα

Etymology: βλαστησασα is a Greek word that comes from the verb βλαστάνω (blastanō), which means "to sprout" or "to bud". The suffix -σασα (-sasa) is a feminine form of the aorist tense, indicating that the action of sprouting or budding has been completed.

Grammar: βλαστησασα is a feminine singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. It is a participle, which means it functions as an adjective or a verb in a sentence. In this case, it describes a female subject who has completed the action of sprouting or budding.

Description: βλαστησασα can be translated as "having sprouted" or "having budded" in English. It is often used in botanical contexts to describe the growth of plants or trees. In literature, it can be used metaphorically to describe the growth or development of a character or an idea.
