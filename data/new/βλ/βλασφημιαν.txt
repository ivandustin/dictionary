Word: βλασφημίαν

Etymology:
The word βλασφημίαν comes from the Greek word βλασφημία (blasphemia), which means "slander" or "defamation". It is derived from the words βλάπτω (blapto), meaning "to harm", and φήμη (pheme), meaning "rumor" or "report".

Grammar:
βλασφημίαν is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb, and the singular number indicates that the noun refers to one thing. The feminine gender indicates that the noun refers to a female person, animal, or object.

Description:
βλασφημίαν refers to the act of speaking or writing something that is disrespectful or insulting towards a deity, religion, or sacred object. It is often translated as "blasphemy" in English. In Greek culture, blasphemy was considered a serious offense and was punishable by law. The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the act of speaking against God or Jesus Christ.
