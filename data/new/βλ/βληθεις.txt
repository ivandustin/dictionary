Word: βληθεις

Etymology:
βληθεις is a Greek word that comes from the verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw". The word βληθεις is the aorist passive participle of βάλλω, which means "having been thrown".

Grammar:
βληθεις is a masculine singular nominative form of the aorist passive participle of βάλλω. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description:
βληθεις can be translated as "the one who has been thrown" or "the one who was thrown". It is often used in Greek literature to describe someone who has been cast out or rejected, either physically or metaphorically. The word can also be used in a more literal sense to describe something that has been thrown or hurled, such as a spear or a stone.
