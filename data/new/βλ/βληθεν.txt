Word: βληθεν

Etymology:
βληθεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". βληθεν is the aorist passive participle of βάλλω, which means "having been thrown" or "having been cast".

Grammar:
βληθεν is a participle in the aorist tense, passive voice, and masculine gender. As a participle, it functions as an adjective and describes a noun or pronoun. In this case, it describes a masculine noun or pronoun that has been thrown or cast.

Description:
βληθεν is a word that describes something that has been thrown or cast. It is often used in Greek literature to describe objects that have been thrown or cast, such as stones, spears, or arrows. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has been thrown or cast out of a group or society.
