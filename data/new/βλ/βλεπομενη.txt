Word: βλεπομενη

Etymology: βλεπομενη is a Greek word that comes from the verb βλέπω (blepo), which means "to see" or "to look". The suffix -μένη (-meni) is a feminine present participle ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to a female subject.

Grammar: βλεπομενη is a present participle in the feminine singular form. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently seeing or looking at something. In Greek, present participles are often used to form compound tenses and express ongoing actions.

Description: βλεπομενη can be translated to "the one who is seeing" or "the one who is looking". It is a descriptive word that can be used to refer to a female who is currently engaged in the act of seeing or looking at something. It can also be used as an adjective to describe a noun, such as "the seeing woman" or "the looking girl".
