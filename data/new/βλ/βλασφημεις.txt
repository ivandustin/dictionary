Word: βλασφημεις

Etymology:
The word βλασφημεις (blasphemeis) is derived from the Greek word βλασφημέω (blasphemeo), which means to speak evil of, to slander, or to revile. The word is composed of two parts: βλασφημία (blasphemia), which means blasphemy, and the verb εἶναι (einai), which means to be.

Grammar:
βλασφημεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the verb βλασφημέω (blasphemeo). It is used to address someone directly and means "you are blaspheming" or "you are speaking evil of."

Description:
βλασφημεις is a strong word that is used to express disapproval or condemnation of someone who is speaking or acting in a way that is disrespectful or offensive to a deity or religion. It is often used in a religious context to describe someone who is speaking against God or the teachings of a particular religion. The word is also used more broadly to describe any speech or action that is considered to be disrespectful or offensive.
