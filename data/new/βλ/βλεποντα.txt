The word βλεποντα (bleponta) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb βλέπω (blepo), which means "to see". The present participle form indicates that the action of seeing is currently happening or ongoing. 

In terms of grammar, βλεποντα is in the masculine, nominative, singular form. It can be used as a subject or as a predicate nominative in a sentence. 

Etymologically, βλέπω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰel- meaning "to shine, flash, gleam". The word has cognates in other Indo-European languages, such as the Latin verb videre and the English word "vision". 

Overall, βλεποντα is a word that describes the act of seeing or looking, and is commonly used in Greek language and literature.
