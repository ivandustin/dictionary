Word: βλεπομενα

Etymology: βλεπομενα is a Greek word that comes from the verb βλέπω (blepo), which means "to see" or "to look". The suffix -μενα (-mena) is a participle ending that indicates the present passive voice, meaning "being seen" or "being looked at".

Grammar: βλεπομενα is a present passive participle in the neuter gender, nominative case, and plural number. It is derived from the verb βλέπω (blepo), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description: βλεπομενα can be translated as "things being seen" or "things being looked at". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to refer to the objects of perception or contemplation. In modern Greek, it can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is visible or apparent.
