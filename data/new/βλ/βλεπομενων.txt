Word: βλεπομενων

Etymology: βλεπομενων is the present participle of the Greek verb βλέπω (blepō), which means "to see" or "to look". The suffix -μενων (-menōn) indicates that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: βλεπομενων is a masculine, plural, genitive form of the present participle. It is used to describe a noun in the genitive case, indicating that the noun is in a state of continuous seeing or looking.

Description: βλεπομενων can be translated as "of those who are seeing" or "of those who are looking". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe a group of people who are observing or contemplating something. In Christian theology, it is used to describe the vision of God that is granted to the blessed in heaven.
