Word: βλεπουσιν

Etymology: βλεπουσιν is a present active indicative form of the Greek verb βλέπω (blepō), which means "to see" or "to look". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- ("to shine, flash, gleam").

Grammar: βλεπουσιν is the third person plural present active indicative form of βλέπω. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, and person. The present tense indicates an action that is happening now, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action. The third person plural indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description: βλεπουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they see" or "they look". It is used to describe an action that is happening in the present, and it is conjugated to match the subject of the sentence. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is an important part of the Greek language.
