Word: βλασφημουμαι

Etymology:
The word βλασφημουμαι comes from the Greek root words βλασφημία (blasphemia) which means "blasphemy" and μαι (mai) which is a reflexive suffix. Therefore, βλασφημουμαι means "to blaspheme oneself".

Grammar:
βλασφημουμαι is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and first person singular form. It is derived from the verb βλασφημέω (blasphemeo) which means "to speak impiously or irreverently about God or sacred things".

Description:
βλασφημουμαι is a strong word that describes the act of blaspheming oneself. It is often used in a religious context to describe someone who speaks or acts in a way that is disrespectful or irreverent towards God or sacred things. The word carries a negative connotation and is generally considered to be a sin in many religions.
