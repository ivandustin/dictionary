Word: βλασται

Etymology:
The word βλασται (blastai) is derived from the Greek word βλαστάνω (blastanō), which means "to sprout" or "to bud". It is related to the word βλαστός (blastos), which means "shoot" or "sprout".

Grammar:
βλασται is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is the plural form of βλαστός (blastos), which means "shoot" or "sprout". In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence.

Description:
βλασται refers to shoots or sprouts, typically of plants. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the growth or development of something. The word is often used in the context of agriculture or gardening, where the growth of plants is of particular importance.
