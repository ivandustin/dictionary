Etymology: The word "θυραῖος" (thuraîos) comes from the Greek word "θύρα" (thýra), meaning "door". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰwer-.

Grammar: "Θυραῖος" is an adjective in the masculine form. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. It can be declined in different cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative.

Description: "Θυραῖος" is an ancient Greek word that means "pertaining to a door" or "door-related". It can be used to describe something or someone that is connected to or associated with doors.
