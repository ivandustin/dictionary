Etymology: The word "θυείν" (thyein) comes from the Greek verb θύω (thyo), which means "to sacrifice." 

Grammar: θυείν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. 

Description: θυείν means "to sacrifice" in Greek. It refers to the act of offering something, usually an animal, to a deity as an act of worship or devotion. In ancient Greek religion, sacrifices were an important part of religious rituals and were believed to establish a connection between humans and the gods.
