Etymology: The word "θυσίαις" (thysiais) comes from the Greek word "θυσία" (thysia), which means "sacrifice" or "offering". 

Grammar: "Θυσίαις" is the dative plural form of the noun "θυσία" (thysia). In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Θυσίαις" refers to sacrifices or offerings in the plural form. In ancient Greek culture, sacrifices were a common religious practice where animals or other items were offered to the gods as a way of showing devotion or seeking favor. These sacrifices were often performed in temples or at altars.
