In ancient Greek, "θυμος" originally referred to the smoke or vapor that rises from a burning sacrifice, but it later evolved to mean "soul" or "spirit."

Grammar: In Greek, "θυμος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has a singular form (θυμος) and a plural form (θυμοι). It can function as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Θυμος" has multiple meanings and connotations in Greek. It can refer to the physical organ of the thymus gland, which is associated with the immune system. It can also refer to the emotional state of anger, rage, or wrath. In ancient Greek literature, "θυμος" often represents the passionate and impulsive aspect of human nature.
