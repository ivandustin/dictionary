Etymology: The word "θυγατήρ" (thugatēr) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰugh₂tḗr, which means "daughter."

Grammar: In Greek, "θυγατήρ" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has a stem ending in -τηρ (-tēr). The word can be declined to indicate different cases, numbers, and genders.

Description: "Θυγατήρ" means "daughter" in Greek. It is used to refer to a female child in relation to her parents. The word can also be used metaphorically to denote a female descendant or a female person in a figurative sense.
