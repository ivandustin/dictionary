Etymology: The word "θυμιατήριον" comes from the Greek word "θυμιάω" (thymiaō), meaning "to burn incense". It is derived from the noun "θυμίαμα" (thymíama), which means "incense".

Grammar: "Θυμιατήριον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a neuter noun of the second declension.

Description: "Θυμιατήριον" refers to a censer or a thurible, which is a vessel used for burning incense in religious ceremonies. It is typically made of metal and has a handle and a lid with holes to allow the smoke to escape. The censer is swung or carried by a priest or religious officiant during rituals to create a fragrant smoke as an offering to the gods or as a symbol of purification.
