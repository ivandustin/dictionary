Etymology: The word "θυγατριον" comes from the Greek word "θυγάτηρ" (thugatēr), meaning "daughter".

Grammar: "Θυγατριον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the neuter gender and singular number.

Description: "Θυγατριον" refers to a daughter, a female child in relation to her parents. It is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday language to denote a girl or young woman who is the offspring of someone.
