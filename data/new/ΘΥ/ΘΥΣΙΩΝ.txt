Etymology: The word "θυσιων" (thysion) comes from the Greek verb "θύω" (thyo), which means "to sacrifice." 

Grammar: "Θυσιων" is a noun in the genitive case, singular number, masculine gender. 

Description: "Θυσιων" refers to a sacrifice or an offering made to a deity in ancient Greek religious practices. It can also be used metaphorically to describe any act of offering or surrendering something valuable or important.
