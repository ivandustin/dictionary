Etymology: The word "θυσιαστηρία" comes from the Greek word "θυσία" (thysia), meaning "sacrifice," and the suffix "-στηρία" (-stiria), which denotes a place or object related to the root word.

Grammar: "Θυσιαστηρία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "θυσιαστηρίες" (thysiastiries).

Description: "Θυσιαστηρία" refers to an altar or a place where sacrifices are offered in religious rituals. It is commonly used in the context of ancient Greek religion and mythology.
