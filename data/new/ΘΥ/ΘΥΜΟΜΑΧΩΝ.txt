Etymology: The word "θυμομαχών" comes from the Greek words "θυμός" (thymos) meaning "anger" and "μάχη" (machi) meaning "battle". 

Grammar: "θυμομαχών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case. 

Description: "θυμομαχών" refers to someone who fights or battles with anger. It can be used to describe a person who engages in conflicts or disputes fueled by anger or rage.
