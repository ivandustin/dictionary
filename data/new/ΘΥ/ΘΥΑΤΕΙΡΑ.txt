Etymology: The word "θυατείρα" (pronounced "thuateira") comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "θυάω" (thuao), which means "to sacrifice" or "to offer a sacrifice."

Grammar: "Θυατείρα" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "θυατείρα." In the plural, it becomes "θυατείρες" (thuateires).

Description: In ancient Greece, a "θυατείρα" referred to a female priestess or attendant who participated in religious rituals and sacrifices. These priestesses were often associated with specific temples or cults and played an important role in the worship of various gods and goddesses. They would assist in the preparation and performance of sacrifices, as well as in the maintenance of sacred spaces.
