Etymology: The word "συνηλθε" (synēlthe) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come." 

Grammar: "Συνηλθε" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "συνέρχομαι" (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble."

Description: "Συνηλθε" is used to describe the action of people coming together or assembling in a specific place or for a specific purpose. It implies a gathering or meeting of individuals.
