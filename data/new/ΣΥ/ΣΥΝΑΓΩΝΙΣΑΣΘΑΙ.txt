Etymology: The word "συναγωνισασθαι" comes from the Greek verb "συναγωνίζομαι" (synagonizomai), which is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "αγωνίζομαι" (agonizomai), meaning "to struggle" or "to compete." 

Grammar: "Συναγωνισασθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "συναγωνίζομαι" (synagonizomai). The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past.

Description: "Συναγωνισασθαι" means "to compete together" or "to struggle together." It implies engaging in a joint effort or competition with others.
