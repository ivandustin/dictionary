Etymology: The word "συνπαραλαβείν" (synparalaveín) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "παραλαμβάνω" (paralambáno), meaning "to receive" or "to take." 

Grammar: "Συνπαραλαβείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "Συνπαραλαβείν" means "to receive together" or "to take along with." It implies the action of receiving or taking something or someone in the company of others.
