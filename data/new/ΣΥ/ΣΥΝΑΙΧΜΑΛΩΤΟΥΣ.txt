Etymology: The word "συναιχμαλωτούς" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "αἰχμάλωτος" (aichmálotos), meaning "prisoner" or "captive." 

Grammar: "Συναιχμαλωτούς" is a Greek noun in the plural form. It is declined in the accusative case and can be translated as "prisoners" or "captives" in English.

Description: "Συναιχμαλωτούς" refers to a group of people who are held captive or imprisoned together. It can be used to describe prisoners of war, hostages, or any group of individuals who are confined against their will.
