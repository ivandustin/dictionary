Etymology: The word "συνηγμένα" (synēgmena) comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synagō), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." The suffix "-μένα" (-mena) is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, indicating that the action of gathering has been completed.

Grammar: "Συνηγμένα" is the neuter plural form of the past participle of the verb "συνάγω" (synagō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. In this case, "συνηγμένα" is used to modify neuter plural nouns.

Description: "Συνηγμένα" is an adjective that means "gathered" or "collected" in Greek. It is used to describe things that have been brought together or assembled in one place.
