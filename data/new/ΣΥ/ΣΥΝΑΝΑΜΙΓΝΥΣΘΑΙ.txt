Etymology: The word "συναναμιγνύσθαι" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἀναμιγνύναι" (anamignynai), meaning "to mix." 

Grammar: "Συναναμιγνύσθαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

Description: "Συναναμιγνύσθαι" means "to mix together" or "to mingle." It implies the act of combining or blending different elements or substances into a unified whole.
