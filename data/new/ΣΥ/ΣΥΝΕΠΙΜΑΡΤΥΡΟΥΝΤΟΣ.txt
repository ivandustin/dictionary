Etymology: The word "συνεπιμαρτυροῦντος" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," "ἐπί" (epi), meaning "upon," and "μαρτυρέω" (martyreo), meaning "to testify." 

Grammar: "Συνεπιμαρτυροῦντος" is a verb form in the present active participle tense in Greek. It is derived from the verb "συνεπιμαρτυρέω" (synepimartyreo), which means "to bear witness together with."

Description: "Συνεπιμαρτυροῦντος" is used to describe someone or something that is testifying or bearing witness together with someone else. It implies a joint or collaborative testimony.
