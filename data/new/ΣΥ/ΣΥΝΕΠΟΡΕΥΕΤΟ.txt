Etymology: The word "συνεπορεύετο" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "ἐπορεύετο" meaning "to go, to proceed". 

Grammar: "Συνεπορεύετο" is a verb in the third person singular form of the imperfect tense in the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "πορεύομαι" (to go, to proceed) with the prefix "συν-" added to indicate the idea of doing something together or in company with others.

Description: "Συνεπορεύετο" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it was going together" or "he/she/it was proceeding together". It describes the action of someone or something moving or progressing in the company of others.
