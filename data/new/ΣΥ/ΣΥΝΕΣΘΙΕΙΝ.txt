Etymology: The word "συνεσθιείν" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἐσθίειν" (esthiein), meaning "to eat." 

Grammar: "Συνεσθιείν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is derived from the ancient Greek verb "ἐσθίειν" (esthiein), which means "to eat."

Description: "Συνεσθιείν" is a Greek word that translates to "to eat together" or "to share a meal." It implies the act of eating in the company of others, emphasizing the social aspect of dining.
