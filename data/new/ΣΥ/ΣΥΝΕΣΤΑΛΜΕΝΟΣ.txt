Etymology: The word "συνεσταλμένος" comes from the Greek verb "συστέλλω" (synstéllo), which means "to contract" or "to shrink." The prefix "συν-" (syn-) indicates "together" or "with," and the root "στέλλω" (stéllo) means "to send" or "to bring." Therefore, "συνεσταλμένος" can be translated as "contracted" or "shrunk together."

Grammar: "Συνεσταλμένος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Συνεσταλμένος" describes something that has been contracted or shrunk together. It can be used to describe physical objects that have reduced in size or volume, as well as abstract concepts that have been condensed or compressed.
