Etymology: The word "συροντες" (syrontes) comes from the Greek verb "συρω" (syro), which means "to drag" or "to pull". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *swer-.

Grammar: "Συροντες" is the present participle form of the verb "συρω" (syro) in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action of dragging or pulling. In this form, it agrees with the subject in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Συροντες" is a Greek word that refers to individuals or things that are currently dragging or pulling something. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing people pulling a heavy object or animals dragging a load.
