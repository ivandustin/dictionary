Etymology: The word "συνεργος" (synergos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εργον" (ergon), meaning "work" or "action." Therefore, "συνεργος" can be translated as "working together" or "cooperative."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνεργος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (συνεργου), dative (συνεργω), and accusative (συνεργο).

Description: "Συνεργος" is used to describe someone who collaborates or works together with others towards a common goal. It implies cooperation, teamwork, and synergy. This term can be applied to various contexts, such as business, projects, or personal relationships, to emphasize the importance of working together for mutual benefit.
