Etymology: The word "συνεργών" comes from the Greek verb "συνεργέω" (synergeó), which means "to work together" or "to cooperate." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "έργο" (ergo), meaning "work."

Grammar: "Συνεργών" is the present participle form of the verb "συνεργέω" (synergeó) in the masculine singular nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Description: "Συνεργών" refers to a person who is currently working together or cooperating with others. It implies active participation and collaboration in a joint effort or project. The word can be used to describe someone who is actively contributing to a team, partnership, or any form of collective work.
