Etymology: The word "συνηθροισμένοι" comes from the Greek verb "συναθροίζω" (synathroízo), which means "to gather together" or "to assemble." The suffix "-μένοι" (-menoí) is added to the verb stem to indicate a passive voice and present participle form.

Grammar: "Συνηθροισμένοι" is the masculine plural form of the adjective, used to describe a group of people who have gathered together. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "συνηθροισμένοι" describes a group of people who have come together or assembled in one place. It implies a sense of collective gathering or congregation.
