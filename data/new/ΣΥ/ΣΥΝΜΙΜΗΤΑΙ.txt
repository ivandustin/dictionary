Etymology: The word "συνμιμηταί" (synmimitai) comes from the Greek root words "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "μιμητής" (mimitis), meaning "imitator." 

Grammar: "Συνμιμηταί" is a noun in the plural form. It is the nominative case of the word, which is used to indicate the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Συνμιμηταί" refers to a group of people who imitate or mimic each other. It can be used to describe individuals who copy or imitate the behavior, actions, or style of others.
