Etymology: The word "συναθροίζω" (synathroízo) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "αθροίζω" (athroízo), meaning "to gather." 

Grammar: "Συναθροίζω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ συναθροίζω (I gather), εσύ συναθροίζεις (you gather), αυτός/αυτή/αυτό συναθροίζει (he/she/it gathers), εμείς συναθροίζουμε (we gather), εσείς συναθροίζετε (you gather), αυτοί/αυτές συναθροίζουν (they gather).

Description: "Συναθροίζω" means "to gather" or "to assemble" in English. It refers to the action of bringing people or things together in one place.
