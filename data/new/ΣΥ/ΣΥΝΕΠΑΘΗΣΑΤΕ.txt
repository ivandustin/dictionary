Etymology: The word "συνεπαθήσατε" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "επαθήσατε" (epathisate), meaning "to suffer." 

Grammar: "Συνεπαθήσατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "συνεπαθέω" (synepatheo), which is in the aorist tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all suffered together."

Description: "Συνεπαθήσατε" is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of experiencing or sharing in someone else's suffering or hardship. It implies empathy and solidarity with others in their difficult circumstances.
