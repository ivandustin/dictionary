Etymology: The word "συνκακουχείσθαι" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κακουχείσθαι" (kakoucheisthai), meaning "to suffer." 

Grammar: "Συνκακουχείσθαι" is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. It is derived from the verb "κακουχέομαι" (kakoucheomai), which means "to suffer together."

Description: "Συνκακουχείσθαι" is a Greek word that describes the act of suffering or enduring hardship together with someone else. It implies a sense of solidarity and shared experience in facing difficulties.
