Etymology: The word "συνυπουργούντων" comes from the Greek language. It is a compound word formed by combining the prefix "συν-" meaning "together" or "with," the root "υπουργούντων" which is the present participle of the verb "υπουργώ" meaning "to serve" or "to minister," and the suffix "-ντων" indicating the plural form.

Grammar: "Συνυπουργούντων" is the present participle form of the verb "υπουργώ" in the third person plural. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently serving or ministering together.

Description: "Συνυπουργούντων" refers to a group of individuals who are working together as ministers or serving in a ministerial capacity. It implies a collaborative effort and shared responsibility in the execution of their duties.
