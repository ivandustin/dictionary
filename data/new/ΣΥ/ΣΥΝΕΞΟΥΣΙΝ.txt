Etymology: The word "συνεξουσιν" (syneksousin) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εξουσία" (exousia), meaning "authority" or "power." 

Grammar: "Συνεξουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb "συνέχω" (synecho), which means "to hold together" or "to keep together." 

Description: "Συνεξουσιν" refers to a collective or joint exercise of authority or power. It implies a shared or united effort in exercising control or influence over a situation or a group of people.
