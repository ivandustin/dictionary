Etymology: The word "συνηγμένοι" (synēgmenoi) comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synagō), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." The suffix "-μένοι" (-menoi) is a participle ending that indicates a passive voice and a plural subject.

Grammar: "Συνηγμένοι" is the masculine plural form of the verb "συνάγω" in the passive voice. It is used to describe a group of people who have been gathered or brought together.

Description: "Συνηγμένοι" is an adjective that describes a collective group of individuals who have been assembled or united for a common purpose. It implies a sense of unity and collaboration among the members of the group.
