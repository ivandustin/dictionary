Etymology: The word "συνμαρτυρούσης" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "μαρτυρούσης" (marturousis), meaning "testifying." 

Grammar: "Συνμαρτυρούσης" is a Greek verb in the present active participle form. It is derived from the verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureo), meaning "to testify" or "to bear witness." The prefix "συν" (syn) adds the meaning of "together" or "with." The ending "-ούσης" (-ousis) indicates the feminine gender and the present tense.

Description: "Συνμαρτυρούσης" is used to describe a female subject who is currently testifying or bearing witness together with someone else. It implies a joint or collaborative act of testifying.
