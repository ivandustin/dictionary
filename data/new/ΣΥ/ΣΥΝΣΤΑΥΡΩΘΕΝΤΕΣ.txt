Grammar: "Συνσταυρώθεντες" is the plural form of the verb "συνσταυρόω" in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It is used to describe a group of people who were crucified together.

Description: "Συνσταυρώθεντες" is a word used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Greek translation of the Gospel accounts of the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. It refers to the two criminals who were crucified alongside Jesus. The word emphasizes the shared experience of being crucified together with Jesus.
