Etymology: The word "συνειδησιν" (syneidēsin) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εἶδος" (eidos), meaning "form" or "shape." It literally translates to "together with form" or "jointly formed."

Grammar: "Συνειδησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in Greek grammar.

Description: In Greek, "συνειδησιν" refers to the concept of conscience or moral consciousness. It represents the inner sense of right and wrong, and the awareness of one's own actions and their moral implications. It is often associated with the ability to make ethical judgments and decisions.
