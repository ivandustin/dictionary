Etymology: The word "συνησαν" (synēsan) comes from the Greek verb "συνίημι" (syniēmi), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend."

Grammar: "Συνησαν" is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb "συνίημι" (syniēmi). It is used to indicate that a group of people understood or comprehended something in the past.

Description: "Συνησαν" is a Greek word that means "they understood" or "they comprehended." It is often used in the context of gaining knowledge or insight into something.
