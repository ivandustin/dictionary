Etymology: The word "συνηλθαν" (synēlthan) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come." 

Grammar: "Συνηλθαν" is the third person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb "συνέρχομαι" (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is used to describe a group of people coming together or gathering in a specific place.

Description: "Συνηλθαν" is a Greek verb that indicates the action of a group of people coming together or assembling. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a meeting, a gathering, or a congregation. The word implies a collective action or a shared purpose among the individuals involved.
