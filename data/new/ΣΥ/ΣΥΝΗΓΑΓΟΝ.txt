Etymology: The word "συνηγαγον" (synēgagon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἄγω" (agō), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." 

Grammar: "Συνηγαγον" is the aorist tense of the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synagō), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." It is conjugated in the first person singular form. 

Description: "Συνηγαγον" is a Greek word that describes an action of gathering or bringing together. It can be used in various contexts, such as gathering people, objects, or ideas.
