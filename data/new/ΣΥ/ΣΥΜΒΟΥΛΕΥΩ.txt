Etymology: The word "συμβουλεύω" (sympouleúo) comes from the Greek roots "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "βουλεύω" (bouleúo), meaning "to advise." It literally means "to advise together" or "to give counsel."

Grammar: "Συμβουλεύω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ συμβουλεύω (ego sympouleúo) - I advise
- Εσύ συμβουλεύεις (esý sympouleúeis) - You advise (singular)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό συμβουλεύει (aftós/aftí/aftó sympouleúei) - He/She/It advises
- Εμείς συμβουλεύουμε (emeís sympouleúoume) - We advise
- Εσείς συμβουλεύετε (eseís sympouleúete) - You advise (plural)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές συμβουλεύουν (aftoí/aftés sympouleúoun) - They advise

Description: "Συμβουλεύω" is a versatile verb that means "to advise," "to counsel," or "to give guidance." It is used when someone offers suggestions, recommendations, or opinions to help others make decisions or solve problems. It can be used in various contexts, such as personal relationships, professional settings, or even in a broader sense, like advising on a course of action or giving general advice.
