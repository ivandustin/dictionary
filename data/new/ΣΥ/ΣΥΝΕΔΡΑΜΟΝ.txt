Etymology: The word "συνεδραμον" (synedramon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εδραμον" (edramon), meaning "I ran." 

Grammar: "Συνεδραμον" is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It is derived from the verb "τρέχω" (trecho), meaning "I run," with the prefix "συν" (syn) added to indicate the action of running together with someone or something.

Description: "Συνεδραμον" describes the action of running together with someone or something. It implies a sense of collaboration or joint effort in the act of running.
