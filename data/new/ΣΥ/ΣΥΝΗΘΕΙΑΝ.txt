Etymology: The word "συνηθειαν" (synētheian) comes from the Greek root words "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ηθος" (ēthos), meaning "habit" or "custom." 

Grammar: "Συνηθειαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. It is derived from the Greek adjective "συνηθης" (synēthēs), meaning "habitual" or "customary." 

Description: "Συνηθειαν" refers to the act or state of having a habit or custom. It can also be used to describe a habitual practice or behavior.
