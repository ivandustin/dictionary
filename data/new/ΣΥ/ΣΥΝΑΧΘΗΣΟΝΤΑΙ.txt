Etymology: The word "συναχθεί" comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synago), which means "to gather" or "to bring together". The suffix "-θη-" is added to form the aorist passive tense, and the ending "-σο" indicates the future tense.

Grammar: "Συναχθεί" is the third person singular form of the future passive tense of the verb "συνάγω" (synago). In Greek grammar, the future tense is used to express actions that will happen in the future. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Συναχθεί" can be translated as "he/she/it will be gathered" or "he/she/it will be brought together". It describes a future event where someone or something will be collected or assembled.
