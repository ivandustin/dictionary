Etymology: The word "συνηλθεν" (synēlthen) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come." 

Grammar: "Συνηλθεν" is the aorist tense of the Greek verb "συνέρχομαι" (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is conjugated in the third person singular.

Description: "Συνηλθεν" is used to describe the action of coming together or assembling. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people gathering for a meeting or a crowd gathering for an event.
