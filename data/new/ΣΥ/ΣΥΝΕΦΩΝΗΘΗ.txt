Etymology: The word "συνεφωνήθη" (synefoníthi) comes from the Greek roots "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and "εφωνήθη" (efoníthi), meaning "to sound." 

Grammar: "Συνεφωνήθη" is a verb in the past tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "συνεφωνήθηκα" (synefoníthike), which means "to be agreed upon" or "to be in harmony."

Description: "Συνεφωνήθη" is used to describe a state of agreement or harmony between multiple parties. It implies that there is a consensus or mutual understanding among individuals or groups.
