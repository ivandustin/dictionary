Etymology: The word "συνιστάσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "συνίστημι" (synístēmi), which means "to set together" or "to establish." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἵστημι" (hístēmi), meaning "to stand" or "to place."

Grammar: "Συνιστάσθαι" is a verb in the present middle/passive infinitive form. In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description: "Συνιστάσθαι" is a Greek verb that can be translated as "to be established," "to be constituted," or "to be recommended." It is often used to describe the process of something being set up, formed, or recommended.
