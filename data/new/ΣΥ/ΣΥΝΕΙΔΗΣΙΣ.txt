Etymology: The word "συνείδησης" (syneídisis) comes from the Greek roots "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εἶδος" (eîdos), meaning "form" or "appearance." It can be translated as "conscience" or "consciousness."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνείδησης" is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of the word "συνείδηση" (syneídisi), which is the nominative form.

Description: "Συνείδησης" refers to the faculty of the mind that enables one to be aware of their own thoughts, actions, and moral judgments. It is often associated with the ability to distinguish right from wrong and to act accordingly. Conscience plays a significant role in ethical decision-making and personal responsibility.
