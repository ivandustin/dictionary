Etymology: The word "συνκυριαν" comes from the Greek words "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κυριαν" (kyrian), meaning "lady" or "mistress." 

Grammar: "Συνκυριαν" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it is declined according to the second declension, feminine gender. 

Description: "Συνκυριαν" refers to a woman who is the mistress or lady of a household. It can also be used to describe a woman who holds a position of authority or influence.
