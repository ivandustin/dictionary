Etymology: The word "συμμορφον" (symmorfon) comes from the Greek roots "συμ-" (sym-), meaning "together" or "with," and "μορφή" (morphē), meaning "form" or "shape." 

Grammar: "Συμμορφον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is the nominative singular form of the word. 

Description: "Συμμορφον" refers to something that is in accordance with a particular form or shape. It can also be used to describe something that is conforming to a standard or following a set of rules.
