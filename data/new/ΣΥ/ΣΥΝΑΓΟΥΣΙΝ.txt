Etymology: The word "συναγουσιν" (pronounced "synagousin") comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (pronounced "synago"), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (pronounced "syn-"), meaning "together," and the verb "άγω" (pronounced "ago"), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar: "Συναγουσιν" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "συνάγω." In Greek grammar, it belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:
- First person singular: συνάγω (synago)
- Second person singular: συνάγεις (synageis)
- Third person singular: συνάγει (synagei)
- First person plural: συνάγομεν (synagomen)
- Second person plural: συνάγετε (synagete)
- Third person plural: συνάγουσιν (synagousin)

Description: "Συναγουσιν" is used to describe an action performed by a group of people who gather or come together. It can also imply a sense of unity or collaboration among the individuals involved.
