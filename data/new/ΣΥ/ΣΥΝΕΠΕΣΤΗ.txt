Etymology: The word "συνεπέστη" (synepesti) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἐπέστη" (epesti), meaning "to happen" or "to occur." 

Grammar: "Συνεπέστη" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "συνέπιπτω" (synepipto), which means "to coincide" or "to happen at the same time." 

Description: "Συνεπέστη" is used to describe a situation where two or more events or circumstances occur simultaneously or coincide with each other. It implies that these events or circumstances happened together or at the same time.
