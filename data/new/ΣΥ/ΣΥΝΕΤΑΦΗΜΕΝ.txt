Etymology: The word "συνεταφήμεν" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εταφήμεν" (etaphímen), meaning "buried." 

Grammar: "συνεταφήμεν" is a verb in the past tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person plural form of the verb "συνετάφη" (synetáfi), which means "to be buried together."

Description: "συνεταφήμεν" describes a group of people or things that were buried together. It implies that multiple individuals or objects were interred in the same location or grave.
