Etymology: The word "συνελαβον" (synelevon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ελαβον" (eleavon), meaning "I took." 

Grammar: "Συνελαβον" is the first person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "συλλαμβάνω" (sylambano), which means "to take together" or "to comprehend." It is used to express an action that happened in the past.

Description: "Συνελαβον" is a Greek word that describes the act of taking or comprehending something together. It implies a collective or shared understanding or perception.
