Etymology: The word "συνηγαγετε" comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synagō), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." It is formed by combining the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "άγω" (agō), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar: "Συνηγαγετε" is the second person plural, aorist tense, active voice form of the verb "συνάγω" (synagō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The second person plural indicates that the action is being performed by a group of people.

Description: "Συνηγαγετε" translates to "you all gathered" or "you all brought together" in English. It is used to refer to a group of people who have gathered or brought something together.
