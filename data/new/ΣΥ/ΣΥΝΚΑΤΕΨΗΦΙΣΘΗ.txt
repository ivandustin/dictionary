Etymology: The word "συνκατεψηφισθη" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "καταψηφίζω" (katapsefízo), meaning "to vote against." 

Grammar: "Συνκατεψηφισθη" is a verb in the past tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb.

Description: "Συνκατεψηφισθη" means "he/she/it was voted against" in Greek. It indicates that someone or something received negative votes or was opposed by a group of people.
