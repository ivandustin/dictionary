Etymology: The word "συνανακειμένοις" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," "ανα" (ana), meaning "up," and "κείμενος" (keimenos), meaning "lying." 

Grammar: "Συνανακειμένοις" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which is used to describe an ongoing action or state. It is in the masculine, plural, dative case, indicating that the action is being done to a group of people.

Description: "Συνανακειμένοις" can be translated as "together lying" or "lying together." It describes a group of people who are reclining or lying down together, often used in the context of a social gathering or a meal.
