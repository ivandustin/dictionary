Etymology: The word "συνδεσμος" (syndesmos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "δεσμος" (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie." Therefore, "συνδεσμος" can be translated as "connection" or "link."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνδεσμος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "συνδεσμοι" (syndesmoi).

Description: "Συνδεσμος" refers to a connection or link between two or more things. It can be used in various contexts, such as in the fields of technology, linguistics, or social sciences. In technology, it can refer to a physical or virtual connection between devices or systems. In linguistics, it can refer to words or phrases that connect different parts of a sentence or discourse. In social sciences, it can refer to the relationships or associations between individuals or groups.
