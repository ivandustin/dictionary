Etymology: The word "συνεπιομέν" (synepiomen) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἐπιομέν" (epiomen), meaning "to go." 

Grammar: "Συνεπιομέν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person plural form of the verb "συνεπιομένω" (synepiomeno), which means "we go together" or "we accompany."

Description: "Συνεπιομέν" is used to express the action of going together or accompanying someone or something. It implies a sense of unity or togetherness in movement.
