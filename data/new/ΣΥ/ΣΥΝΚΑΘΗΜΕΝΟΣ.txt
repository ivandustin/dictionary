Etymology: The word "συνκαθημενος" (syngkathimenos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "καθημαι" (kathemai), meaning "to sit." 

Grammar: "Συνκαθημενος" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb "καθημαι" (kathemai), which means "to sit," and the prefix "συν" (syn), which means "together." 

Description: "Συνκαθημενος" is an adjective that describes someone or something that is sitting together with others. It can be used to describe a person who is sitting in a group or a thing that is placed or positioned together with other things.
