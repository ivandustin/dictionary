Etymology: The word "συνεδρία" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἑδρία" (hedria), meaning "seat" or "assembly." It literally translates to "sitting together" or "meeting."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνεδρία" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the first declension and has the genitive form "συνεδρίας" (synedrias).

Description: "Συνεδρία" refers to a meeting or session where people gather together to discuss and make decisions on a specific topic. It is commonly used to describe formal assemblies, such as conferences, congresses, or council meetings.
