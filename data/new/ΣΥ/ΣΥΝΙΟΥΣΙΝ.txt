Etymology: The word "συνιούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "συνίημι" (syníēmi), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἵημι" (híēmi), meaning "to send" or "to go."

Grammar: "Συνιούσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is the active voice of the verb "συνίημι" (syníēmi), which means "they understand" or "they comprehend."

Description: "Συνιούσιν" is used to describe the action of a group of people understanding or comprehending something together. It implies a collective understanding or shared knowledge among the individuals referred to.
