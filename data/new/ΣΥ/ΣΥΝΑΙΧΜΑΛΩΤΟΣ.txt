Etymology: The word "συναιχμαλωτος" (pronounced: sin-eh-mah-LOH-tos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together" or "with," and "αιχμαλωτος" (aichmalotos), meaning "captive" or "prisoner." 

Grammar: In Greek, "συναιχμαλωτος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Συναιχμαλωτος" is used to describe someone who is captured or held captive together with others. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is trapped or confined in a situation or circumstance.
