Etymology: The word "συνερχέσθε" (synerchesthe) comes from the Greek verb "συνέρχομαι" (synerchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Συνερχέσθε" is the second person plural present middle/passive imperative form of the verb "συνέρχομαι" (synerchomai). In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Συνερχέσθε" is a command or request for a group of people to come together or assemble. It is often used in a religious or communal context, such as in the Bible or during gatherings or meetings.
