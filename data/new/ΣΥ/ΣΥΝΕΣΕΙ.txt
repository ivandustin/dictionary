Etymology: The word "συνεσεῖ" (synesei) comes from the Greek verb "συνίημι" (syniemi), which means "to understand" or "to comprehend." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἵημι" (hiemi), meaning "to send" or "to put in motion."

Grammar: "Συνεσεῖ" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on tense, person, number, voice, and mood.

Description: The word "συνεσεῖ" is used to express an action that will happen in the future and will be understood or comprehended by someone. It implies the act of gaining knowledge or insight into something.
