Etymology: The word "συντομως" (syntomos) comes from the Greek root words "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "τομος" (tomos), meaning "cut." It literally means "cut together" or "concisely."

Grammar: "Συντομως" is an adverb in Greek. It is the adverbial form of the adjective "συντομος" (syntomos), which means "concise" or "brief."

Description: "Συντομως" is used to describe something that is done or said in a concise or brief manner. It indicates that an action is performed quickly or without unnecessary details. It can be used to express efficiency, brevity, or a sense of urgency.
