Etymology: The word "συνεκαλεσαν" (synekalesan) comes from the Greek verb "συνκαλέω" (synkaleo), which is a compound of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "καλέω" (kaleo), meaning "to call." 

Grammar: "Συνεκαλεσαν" is the third person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "συνκαλέω" (synkaleo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Συνεκαλεσαν" is a Greek verb that means "they called together" or "they summoned." It implies that multiple people or entities came together to call or summon someone.
