Etymology: The word "συνδουλος" (syndoulos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "δουλος" (doulos), meaning "slave." Therefore, the literal meaning of "συνδουλος" is "together with a slave."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνδουλος" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Συνδουλος" refers to a person who is enslaved or in bondage together with someone else. It implies a shared experience of servitude or subjugation. The term can be used metaphorically to describe a person who is oppressed or controlled by another person or entity.
