Etymology: The word "συνηρχετο" (synērkheto) comes from the Greek verb "συνέρχομαι" (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (érchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Συνηρχετο" is the third person singular, imperfect tense form of the verb "συνέρχομαι" (synérchomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a singular entity.

Description: "Συνηρχετο" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it was coming together" or "he/she/it was assembling." It describes an action of coming together or gathering in the past.
