Etymology: The word "συνεσταυρωθη" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "σταυρός" (stauros), meaning "cross." 

Grammar: "συνεσταυρωθη" is a verb in the aorist passive tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "συνεσταυρόω" (synestauróo), which means "to be crucified together with."

Description: "συνεσταυρωθη" is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. It signifies that Jesus was crucified alongside others, emphasizing the shared suffering and sacrifice.
