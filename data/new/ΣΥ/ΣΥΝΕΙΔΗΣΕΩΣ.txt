Grammar: In Greek, "συνειδήσεως" is the genitive form of the noun "συνείδηση" (suneidēsē), which means "conscience" or "consciousness." The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "Συνειδήσεως" refers to the state or faculty of being aware of one's own thoughts, feelings, and actions. It encompasses the moral and ethical judgments that guide a person's behavior. It is often associated with a sense of right and wrong and the ability to distinguish between them.
