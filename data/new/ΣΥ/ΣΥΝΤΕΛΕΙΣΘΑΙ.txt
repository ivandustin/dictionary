Etymology: The word "συντελείσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "τελέω" (teleo), which means "to complete" or "to accomplish," and the prefix "συν-" (syn-), which denotes "together" or "with." Therefore, "συντελείσθαι" can be translated as "to be completed together" or "to be accomplished jointly."

Grammar: "Συντελείσθαι" is a verb in the present tense, passive voice, and infinitive mood. It is used to express an ongoing or continuous action that is being completed or accomplished by multiple parties.

Description: "Συντελείσθαι" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of collaboration or joint effort in completing a task or achieving a goal. It emphasizes the collective nature of the action, suggesting that it is not done by a single individual but rather by a group of people working together.
