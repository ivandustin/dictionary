Etymology: The word "συνκαλούσιν" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "καλούσιν" (kalousin), meaning "they call." 

Grammar: "Συνκαλούσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb "καλώ" (kalo), meaning "to call," and the prefix "συν" (syn), which indicates a collective or joint action. 

Description: "Συνκαλούσιν" means "they call together" or "they are calling together" in Greek. It implies a group of people calling or summoning someone or something collectively.
