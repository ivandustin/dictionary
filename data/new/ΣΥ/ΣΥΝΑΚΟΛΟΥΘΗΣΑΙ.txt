Etymology: The word "συνακολουθησαί" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "ακολουθέω" meaning "to follow". 

Grammar: "Συνακολουθησαί" is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb.

Description: "Συνακολουθησαί" means "to follow along" or "to accompany" in Greek. It implies going along with someone or something, usually in a physical sense.
