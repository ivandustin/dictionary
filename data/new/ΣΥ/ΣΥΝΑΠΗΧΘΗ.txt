Etymology: The word "συναπηχθη" (synapēchthē) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "απηχθη" (apēchthē), meaning "to echo." 

Grammar: "Συναπηχθη" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "συναπηχνύω" (synapēchnýō), which means "to echo together" or "to resound together."

Description: "Συναπηχθη" describes the action of multiple sounds or voices echoing or resounding together. It implies a harmonious or synchronized effect of the echoes.
