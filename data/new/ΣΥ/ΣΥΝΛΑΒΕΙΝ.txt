Etymology: The word "συνλαβείν" (synlaveín) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "λαμβάνειν" (lambánein), meaning "to take." 

Grammar: "Συνλαβείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "Συνλαβείν" means "to understand" or "to comprehend" in Greek. It refers to the act of grasping or comprehending something intellectually.
