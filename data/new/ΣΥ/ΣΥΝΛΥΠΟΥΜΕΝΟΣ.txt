Etymology: The word "συνλυπούμενος" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "λυπούμενος" meaning "sorrowful". 

Grammar: "Συνλυπούμενος" is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It is derived from the verb "λυπούμαι" meaning "to be sorrowful" and the prefix "συν-" which adds the meaning of "together". The verb is conjugated in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood.

Description: "Συνλυπούμενος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "being sorrowful together" or "sharing in sorrow". It describes the state of feeling empathy or compassion towards someone who is experiencing sorrow or grief.
