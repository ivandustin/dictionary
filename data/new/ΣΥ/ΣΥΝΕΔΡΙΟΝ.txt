Etymology: The word "συνεδρίον" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἑδρίον" (hedrion), meaning "seat" or "chair." It originally referred to a council or assembly of people sitting together.

Grammar: "Συνεδρίον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their case, number, and gender. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative (συνεδρίον), genitive (συνεδρίου), dative (συνεδρίῳ), accusative (συνέδριον), and vocative (συνέδριε).

Description: "Συνεδρίον" refers to a council, assembly, or meeting where people gather to discuss and make decisions. It can be used to describe a formal gathering of representatives or experts in a particular field, such as a conference or symposium. In ancient Greece, it was often used to refer to the assembly of citizens in a city-state.
