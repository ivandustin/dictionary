Etymology: The word "συνευδοκεῖ" (syneudokei) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εὐδοκέω" (eudokeo), meaning "to think well of" or "to approve." 

Grammar: "Συνευδοκεῖ" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "εὐδοκέω" (eudokeo), which means "to think well of" or "to approve." The prefix "συν" (syn) adds the meaning of "together" or "in agreement."

Description: "Συνευδοκεῖ" is used to express the idea of "to be pleased with" or "to approve of something together with someone else." It implies a shared agreement or approval.
