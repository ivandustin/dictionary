Etymology: The word "συρακούσας" (sy-rak-oo-sas) comes from the Greek city of Syracuse, which was founded by ancient Greeks in Sicily. 

Grammar: "Συρακούσας" is the genitive form of the noun "Συρακούσα" (Syracuse) in Greek. It is used to indicate possession or origin. 

Description: "Συρακούσας" refers to something that belongs to or is related to Syracuse. It can be used to describe a person, object, or concept that is associated with the city.
