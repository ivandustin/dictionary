Etymology: The word "συνερχεται" comes from the Greek verb "συνέρχομαι" (synerchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Συνερχεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "συνέρχομαι" (synerchomai) in Greek. It is used to describe an action or state that is happening in the present and is performed by a single subject.

Description: "Συνερχεται" is a Greek word that can be translated as "he/she/it comes together" or "he/she/it assembles." It is used to express the idea of people or things gathering or meeting in one place.
