Etymology: The word "συνκαθημενοι" (synkathēmenoi) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "καθημενοι" (kathēmenoi), meaning "sitting." 

Grammar: "Συνκαθημενοι" is a Greek verb in the present participle form. It is derived from the verb "καθημαι" (kathēmai), meaning "to sit." The prefix "συν" (syn) adds the meaning of "together" or "with." The participle form indicates an ongoing action or state.

Description: "Συνκαθημενοι" is used to describe a group of people who are sitting together or sitting in a collective manner. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people sitting in a meeting, gathering, or assembly.
