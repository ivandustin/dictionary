Etymology: The word "συμβουλευσας" (pronounced: sym-vou-lef-sas) comes from the Greek verb "συμβουλεύω" (pronounced: sym-vou-levo), which means "to advise" or "to counsel". The suffix "-ας" (-as) indicates the past tense, so "συμβουλευσας" translates to "having advised" or "having counseled".

Grammar: "Συμβουλευσας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "συμβουλεύω" in the third person singular masculine. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Συμβουλευσας" is a Greek word that describes someone who has advised or counseled. It can refer to someone who has given guidance, suggestions, or recommendations to others.
