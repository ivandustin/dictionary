Etymology: The word "συνκληρονομοι" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κληρονομος" (klēronomos), meaning "heir." 

Grammar: "Συνκληρονομοι" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their endings to indicate number, case, and gender. 

Description: "Συνκληρονομοι" refers to a group of people who inherit something together, typically used in the context of legal inheritance. It can also be used metaphorically to describe people who share a common heritage or legacy.
