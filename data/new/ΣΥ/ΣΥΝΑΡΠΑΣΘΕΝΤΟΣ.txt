Etymology: The word "συναρπασθεντος" comes from the Greek verb "συναρπάζω" (synarpazo), which means "to seize, to snatch away". The suffix "-τος" (-tos) is added to the verb stem to form the adjective form.

Grammar: "Συναρπασθεντος" is the masculine singular form of the adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: "Συναρπασθεντος" describes something or someone that has been seized or snatched away. It can also convey a sense of being captivated or enthralled by something.
