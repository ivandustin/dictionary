Etymology: The word "συνοχής" (synochis) comes from the Greek verb "συνέχω" (synecho), which means "to hold together" or "to unite". It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb "έχω" (echo), meaning "to hold".

Grammar: "Συνοχής" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns decline according to gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Συνοχής" refers to the state or quality of being coherent, connected, or united. It can be used to describe the cohesion or unity within a group, organization, or system. It can also refer to the logical or meaningful connection between ideas or parts of a text.
