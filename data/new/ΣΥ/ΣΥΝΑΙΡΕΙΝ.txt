Etymology: The word "συναιρείν" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "αἱρεῖν" meaning "to take, to choose." 

Grammar: "Συναιρείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is in the present tense. It is commonly used in the active voice.

Description: "Συναιρείν" means "to deduct" or "to subtract" in English. It refers to the mathematical operation of taking away one quantity from another.
