Etymology: The word "συμβούλιον" (symvoulion) comes from the Greek roots "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "βουλή" (voulē), meaning "counsel" or "advice." It refers to a group of people who come together to provide advice or make decisions.

Grammar: In Greek, "συμβούλιον" is a neuter noun. It is declined as follows: singular (nominative) συμβούλιον, plural (nominative) συμβούλια.

Description: "Συμβούλιον" is commonly translated as "council" or "committee" in English. It refers to a group of individuals who are appointed or elected to discuss, deliberate, and make decisions on specific matters. These matters can vary depending on the context, such as government councils, advisory boards, or professional committees. The purpose of a "συμβούλιον" is to provide expertise, guidance, and recommendations to assist in decision-making processes.
