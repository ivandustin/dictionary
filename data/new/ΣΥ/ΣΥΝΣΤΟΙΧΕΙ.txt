Etymology: The word "συνστοιχεί" (systoicheí) comes from the Greek roots "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "στοιχεῖον" (stoicheíon), meaning "element" or "component." 

Grammar: "Συνστοιχεί" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "συνστοιχέω" (systoichéo), which means "to correspond" or "to match."

Description: "Συνστοιχεί" is used to describe the act of two or more elements or components being in correspondence or alignment with each other. It implies a harmonious or coordinated relationship between these elements.
