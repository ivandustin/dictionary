Etymology: The word "συνεχοντες" (synechontes) comes from the Greek verb "συνεχω" (synecho), which means "to hold together" or "to connect." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "εχω" (echo), meaning "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar: "Συνεχοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "συνεχω" (synecho) in the active voice. It is used to describe ongoing or continuous action. In Greek grammar, the present participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Συνεχοντες" can be translated as "those who hold together" or "those who connect." It refers to individuals or groups who actively engage in the act of holding things together or connecting different elements. The word can be used in various contexts, both literal and metaphorical, to describe people who bring unity, coherence, or continuity to a situation or relationship.
