Etymology: The word "συνεσεως" (synēseōs) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εσεως" (eseōs), meaning "perception" or "understanding." 

Grammar: "Συνεσεως" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Συνεσεως" refers to the act or state of understanding or perceiving together. It can also imply shared knowledge or comprehension.
