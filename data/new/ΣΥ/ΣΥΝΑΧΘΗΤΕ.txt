Etymology: The word "συναχθητε" (pronounced: synachthite) comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (pronounced: synago), which means "to gather" or "to bring together". The suffix "-τε" (-te) is added to the verb stem to form the imperative mood, indicating a command or request.

Grammar: "Συναχθητε" is the second person plural form of the verb "συνάγω" in the aorist tense. It is used to address a group of people and instruct them to gather or come together.

Description: "Συναχθητε" is a verb form that is used in Greek to give a command or request for a group of people to gather or assemble. It is commonly used in contexts where people need to come together for a specific purpose or event.
