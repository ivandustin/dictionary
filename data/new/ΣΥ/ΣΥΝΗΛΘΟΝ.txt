Etymology: The word "συνηλθον" (synēlthon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come." 

Grammar: "Συνηλθον" is the aorist tense of the Greek verb "συνέρχομαι" (synérchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is the first person singular form of the verb, indicating that the action was performed by the speaker.

Description: "Συνηλθον" is a Greek word that describes the act of coming together or assembling. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people gathering for a meeting or a crowd gathering for an event.
