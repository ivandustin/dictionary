Etymology: The word "συναγεται" (pronounced "synagetai") comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (pronounced "synago"), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." The suffix "-ται" indicates the third person singular form of the verb in the present tense, indicating that the subject is performing the action.

Grammar: "Συναγεται" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the present tense and the third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "Συναγεται" is used to describe the action of gathering or bringing together in the present tense. It can be used to express the idea of someone or something gathering or coming together with others.
