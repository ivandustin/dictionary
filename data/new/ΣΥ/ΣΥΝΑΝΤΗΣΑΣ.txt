Etymology: The word "συναντήσας" comes from the Greek verb "συναντάω" (synantáo), which means "to meet" or "to encounter." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "αντάω" (antáo), meaning "to meet" or "to come face to face."

Grammar: "Συναντήσας" is the aorist active participle form of the verb "συναντάω" (synantáo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Συναντήσας" is used to describe someone who has met or encountered someone or something in the past. It can be translated as "having met" or "having encountered." The word is commonly used in narratives or descriptions to indicate a previous meeting or encounter.
