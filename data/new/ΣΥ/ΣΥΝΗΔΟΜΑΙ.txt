Etymology: The word "συνηδομαι" (synēdomai) comes from the combination of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ηδομαι" (ēdomai), meaning "to enjoy" or "to be pleased." Therefore, "συνηδομαι" can be translated as "to enjoy together" or "to be pleased together."

Grammar: "Συνηδομαι" is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In Greek grammar, verbs can have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person.

Description: "Συνηδομαι" is a Greek verb that expresses the concept of experiencing pleasure or satisfaction together with someone else. It implies a shared enjoyment or pleasure. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as describing the enjoyment of a meal, the pleasure of spending time with loved ones, or the satisfaction of achieving a common goal.
