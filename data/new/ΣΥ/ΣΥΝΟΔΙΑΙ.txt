Etymology: The word "συνοδιαί" (synodiai) comes from the Greek root words "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ὁδός" (hodos), meaning "way" or "path." It can be translated as "companionship" or "fellowship."

Grammar: "Συνοδιαί" is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of the noun.

Description: "Συνοδιαί" refers to companionship or fellowship. It implies a sense of being together with others, sharing experiences, and supporting each other. It can be used to describe a group of people who are united by a common purpose or goal.
