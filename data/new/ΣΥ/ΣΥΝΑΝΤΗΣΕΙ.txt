Etymology: The word "συναντήσει" comes from the Greek verb "συναντώ" (synantó), which means "to meet" or "to encounter". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together" or "with", and the verb "αντι-" (antí), meaning "against" or "opposite".

Grammar: "Συναντήσει" is the third person singular future tense form of the verb "συναντώ" (synantó). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated according to person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "Συναντήσει" is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it will meet" or "he/she/it will encounter" in English. It expresses the future action of meeting or encountering someone or something.
