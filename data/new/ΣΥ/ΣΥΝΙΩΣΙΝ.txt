Etymology: The word "συνιωσίν" (syniōsin) comes from the Greek verb "συνιόω" (synióō), which means "to understand together" or "to comprehend jointly." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἴω" (ió), meaning "to go" or "to come."

Grammar: "Συνιωσίν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. It is the accusative form of the noun "συνίημι" (syníēmi), which means "understanding" or "comprehension."

Description: "Συνιωσίν" refers to the act or state of understanding or comprehending something jointly or together with others. It implies a shared understanding or a collective comprehension of a concept or idea.
