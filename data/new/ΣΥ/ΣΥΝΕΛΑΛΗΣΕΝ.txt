Etymology: The word "συνελαλήσεν" comes from the Greek verb "συνελαλέω" (sy-nel-a-LEO), which is a compound word formed by the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ελαλέω" (el-a-LEO), meaning "to speak." 

Grammar: "Συνελαλήσεν" is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "συνελαλέω" in Greek. It is used to indicate that someone spoke together with someone else in the past.

Description: "Συνελαλήσεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it spoke together." It implies that someone spoke in collaboration or conversation with another person or group.
