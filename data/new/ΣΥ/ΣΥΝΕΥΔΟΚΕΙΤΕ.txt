Etymology: The word "συνευδοκεῖτε" (syneudokeite) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εὐδοκέω" (eudokeo), meaning "to think well of" or "to approve." 

Grammar: "Συνευδοκεῖτε" is a verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It is derived from the verb "εὐδοκέω" (eudokeo), which means "to think well of," "to approve," or "to be pleased with." The prefix "συν" (syn) adds the meaning of "together" or "in agreement." 

Description: "Συνευδοκεῖτε" is used to express the idea of being in agreement or having a shared approval of something. It can be translated as "you all approve," "you all are pleased with," or "you all think well of." It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to convey the idea of being in agreement with God's will or plan.
