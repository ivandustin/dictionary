Etymology: The word "συνηλικιώτας" comes from the Greek roots "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἡλικία" (hēlikia), meaning "age." It literally translates to "one of the same age" or "peer."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνηλικιώτας" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Συνηλικιώτας" refers to someone who is of the same age as another person. It is commonly used to describe a peer or a person who is in the same age group as oneself.
