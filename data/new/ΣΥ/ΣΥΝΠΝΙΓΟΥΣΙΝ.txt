Etymology: The word "συνπνευγουσιν" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "πνευγουσιν" (pneugin), meaning "to breathe." 

Grammar: "Συνπνευγουσιν" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the verb "συνπνέω" (synpneo), which means "to breathe together" or "to co-breathe."

Description: "Συνπνευγουσιν" describes the action of multiple individuals or entities breathing together or in unison. It implies a sense of unity or synchronization in the act of breathing.
