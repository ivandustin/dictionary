Etymology: The word "συναπολετο" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "απολετο" (apoletos), meaning "destroyed" or "ruined." 

Grammar: "συναπολετο" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "απολείπω" (apoleipo), which means "to leave behind" or "to abandon." The prefix "συν" adds the meaning of "together" or "with."

Description: "συναπολετο" describes an action of leaving or abandoning something together with someone else. It implies a joint decision or action of abandonment.
