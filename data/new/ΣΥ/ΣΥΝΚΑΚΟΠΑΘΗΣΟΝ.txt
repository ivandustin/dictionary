Etymology: The word "συνκακοπαθησον" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κακοπαθησον" (kakopatheson), meaning "to suffer." 

Grammar: "Συνκακοπαθησον" is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular form. It is derived from the aorist tense of the verb "κακοπαθέω" (kakopatheo), which means "to suffer together."

Description: "Συνκακοπαθησον" is a command that means "suffer together" or "share in suffering." It is often used in a figurative sense to encourage empathy and solidarity with someone who is going through a difficult time.
