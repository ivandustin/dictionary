Etymology: The word "συγκληρονομος" (syngklēronomos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κληρονομος" (klēronomos), meaning "heir." 

Grammar: In Greek, "συγκληρονομος" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is declined in the nominative case as "ο συγκληρονομος" (o syngklēronomos), in the genitive case as "του συγκληρονομου" (tou syngklēronomou), and in the accusative case as "τον συγκληρονομο" (ton syngklēronomo).

Description: The word "συγκληρονομος" refers to someone who inherits together with others, usually in the context of shared property or assets. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who shares in or participates in something inherited or passed down.
