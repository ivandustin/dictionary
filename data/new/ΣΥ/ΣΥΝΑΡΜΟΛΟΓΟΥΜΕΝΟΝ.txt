Etymology: The word "συναρμολογούμενον" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "ἁρμολογέω" meaning "to fit together". 

Grammar: "Συναρμολογούμενον" is a Greek adjective in the neuter gender, singular number, and present participle form. It is derived from the verb "συναρμολογώ" which means "to assemble" or "to put together". 

Description: "Συναρμολογούμενον" describes something that is being assembled or put together. It can be used to refer to a physical object that is being constructed or a concept that is being formed.
