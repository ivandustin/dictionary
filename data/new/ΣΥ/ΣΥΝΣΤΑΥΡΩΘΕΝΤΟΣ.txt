Etymology: The word "συνσταυρωθεντος" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "σταυρός" (stauros), meaning "cross." 

Grammar: "Συνσταυρωθεντος" is a verb form in the passive voice, aorist tense, and participle mood. It is the masculine singular form.

Description: "Συνσταυρωθεντος" is the aorist passive participle of the Greek verb "συνσταυρόω" (synstauróo), which means "to crucify together with." It is used to describe someone who has been crucified alongside others.
