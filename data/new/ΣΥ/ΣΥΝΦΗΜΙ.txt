Etymology: The word "συνφημι" (synphēmi) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "φημι" (phēmi), meaning "to speak." 

Grammar: "Συνφημι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is classified as a regular verb. It is commonly used in the present tense, but it can also be conjugated in other tenses such as the past and future.

Description: "Συνφημι" means "to agree" or "to speak together" in Greek. It implies a consensus or harmony in communication or opinion. It can also refer to the act of making a joint statement or expressing a shared viewpoint.
