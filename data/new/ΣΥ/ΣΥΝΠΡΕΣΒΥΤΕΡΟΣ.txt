Etymology: The word "συνπρεσβυτερος" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "πρεσβυτερος" (presbyteros), meaning "elder." 

Grammar: "Συνπρεσβυτερος" is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. 

Description: In Greek, "συνπρεσβυτερος" refers to a fellow elder or co-elder. It is often used in the context of the Christian church to describe someone who holds a position of leadership or authority among the elders.
