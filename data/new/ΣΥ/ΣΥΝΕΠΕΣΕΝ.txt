Etymology: The word "συνεπεσεν" (synepesen) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "επεσεν" (epesen), meaning "to fall." 

Grammar: "Συνεπεσεν" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "συνίπτω" (synipto), which means "to coincide" or "to happen at the same time." 

Description: "Συνεπεσεν" is used to describe an event or situation where two or more things or people fall or happen together or at the same time. It implies a sense of coincidence or simultaneous occurrence.
