Etymology: The word "συνοντων" (syndonton) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "οντων" (onton), the present participle of the verb "εἰμί" (eimi), meaning "to be." Therefore, "συνοντων" can be translated as "being together" or "existing together."

Grammar: "Συνοντων" is the present active participle of the verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be." As a participle, it functions as an adjective and can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Συνοντων" is a Greek word that describes the state of being together or existing together. It can be used to refer to people, objects, or concepts that are in a state of coexistence or unity.
