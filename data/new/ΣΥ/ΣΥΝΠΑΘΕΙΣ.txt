Etymology: The word "συνπαθείς" (synpatheis) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "πάθος" (pathos), meaning "suffering" or "feeling." It is derived from the verb "συνπάσχω" (synpascho), which means "to suffer together."

Grammar: "Συνπαθείς" is the plural form of the adjective "συνπαθής" (synpathis), which means "sympathetic" or "compassionate." It is used to describe someone who shares or understands the feelings or experiences of others.

Description: "Συνπαθείς" refers to individuals who have the ability to empathize with others and understand their emotions or experiences. They are compassionate and show understanding towards others' suffering or difficulties.
