Etymology: The word "συνμεριζονται" comes from the Greek verb "συνμερίζομαι" (synmerízomai), which means "to share together" or "to divide among." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "μερίζω" (merízo), meaning "to divide" or "to share."

Grammar: "Συνμεριζονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "συνμερίζομαι" (synmerízomai). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an ongoing action in the present time. The third person plural form refers to a group of people or things.

Description: "Συνμεριζονται" describes the action of multiple individuals or entities sharing or dividing something together. It implies a collective or collaborative effort in distributing or apportioning something among a group.
