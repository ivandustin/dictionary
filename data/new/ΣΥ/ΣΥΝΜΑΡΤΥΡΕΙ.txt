Etymology: The word "συνμαρτυρεῖ" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "μαρτυρεῖν" meaning "to testify." 

Grammar: "Συνμαρτυρεῖ" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "μαρτυρέω" (martureo), which means "to testify" or "to bear witness." The prefix "συν-" adds the meaning of "together" or "with."

Description: "Συνμαρτυρεῖ" is used to express the idea of testifying or bearing witness together with someone else. It implies a joint or collective testimony.
