Etymology: The word "συνκεκαλυμμένον" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κεκαλυμμένον" (kekalymmenon), meaning "covered." 

Grammar: "Συνκεκαλυμμένον" is a Greek adjective in the neuter gender, singular form. It is in the perfect passive participle form, indicating that the action of being covered has been completed in the past and the subject is the recipient of the action.

Description: "Συνκεκαλυμμένον" describes something that has been covered or concealed together with something else. It implies that the covering or concealment has been done intentionally or deliberately.
