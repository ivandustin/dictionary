Etymology: The word "συναγωγήν" (synagogin) comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synago), which means "to gather together" or "to bring together." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "άγω" (ago), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar: "Συναγωγήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Συναγωγήν" refers to a gathering or assembly of people. In particular, it is commonly used to refer to a Jewish place of worship and community center, known as a synagogue.
