Etymology: The word "συνεκλεκτη" (syneklekti) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εκλεκτη" (eklekti), meaning "chosen" or "selected." 

Grammar: "Συνεκλεκτη" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Συνεκλεκτη" is a term used in Greek philosophy to refer to a group of individuals who are chosen or selected to work together towards a common goal or purpose. It implies a sense of unity and collaboration among the members of the group.
