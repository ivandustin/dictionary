Etymology: The word "συνεργούς" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "έργο" (ergo), meaning "work." Therefore, "συνεργούς" can be translated as "working together" or "collaborator."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνεργούς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it can be declined to match different cases, numbers, and genders.

Description: "Συνεργούς" refers to someone who collaborates or works together with others towards a common goal. It can be used to describe a person who actively participates in a joint effort or partnership.
