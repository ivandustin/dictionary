Etymology: The word "συνλημφθεντα" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "λημφθεντα" (lēmphthenta), meaning "taken." 

Grammar: "Συνλημφθεντα" is a noun in the neuter gender and plural number. It is the past participle of the verb "συλλαμβάνω" (sylambanō), meaning "to take together" or "to comprehend."

Description: "Συνλημφθεντα" refers to things that have been taken or comprehended together. It can be used to describe a collection of ideas, concepts, or objects that are considered as a whole.
