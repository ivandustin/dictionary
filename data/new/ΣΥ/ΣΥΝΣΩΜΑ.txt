Etymology: The word "συνσωμα" (sýnsōma) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "σῶμα" (sōma), meaning "body." 

Grammar: "Συνσωμα" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their case, number, and gender. 

Description: "Συνσωμα" refers to the concept of a collective body or a unified entity. It can be used metaphorically to describe a group of people or things that are closely connected or working together towards a common goal.
