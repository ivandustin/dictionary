Etymology: The word "συνδουλον" (syndoulon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "δουλος" (doulos), meaning "slave." It literally translates to "together slave" or "fellow slave."

Grammar: "Συνδουλον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative, to indicate its role in a sentence.

Description: "Συνδουλον" refers to a person who is a fellow slave or a companion in slavery. It is used to describe someone who shares the same condition of being enslaved or oppressed.
