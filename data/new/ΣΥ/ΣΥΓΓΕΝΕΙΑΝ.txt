Etymology: The word "συγγενείαν" (syngeneían) comes from the Greek root words "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "γένος" (genos), meaning "race" or "family." It refers to the concept of being related or having a common ancestry.

Grammar: "Συγγενείαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in Greek grammar.

Description: "Συγγενείαν" is a Greek word that means "kinship" or "relationship." It is used to describe the connection or bond between individuals who share a common ancestry or are part of the same family.
