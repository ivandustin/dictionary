Etymology: The word "συνετοί" (syne̱toí) comes from the Greek root "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the adjective "ετοίμος" (etoímos), meaning "ready" or "prepared." 

Grammar: "Συνετοί" is the plural form of the adjective "συνετός" (syne̱tós), which means "wise" or "intelligent" in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who possess wisdom or intelligence collectively.

Description: "Συνετοί" refers to a group of individuals who are wise, intelligent, or possess good judgment. It implies that these individuals have the ability to think critically, make sound decisions, and understand complex concepts.
