Etymology: The word "συντροφος" (syntrophos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "τροφος" (trophos), meaning "nourisher" or "feeder." It can be translated as "companion" or "fellow traveler."

Grammar: In Greek, "συντροφος" is a noun of the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Συντροφος" refers to a person who accompanies or travels together with someone else. It implies a close relationship or friendship between individuals who share experiences, adventures, or a common purpose. The term can also be used more broadly to describe a colleague, partner, or associate in various contexts.
