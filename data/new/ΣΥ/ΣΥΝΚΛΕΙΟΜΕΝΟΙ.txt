Etymology: The word "συνκλειομένοι" (synkleioménoi) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κλείω" (kleió), meaning "to close." 

Grammar: "Συνκλειομένοι" is the plural form of the verb "συνκλείω" (synkleió), which means "to close together" or "to be enclosed." It is in the passive voice and present tense. 

Description: "Συνκλειομένοι" is used to describe a group of people or objects that are being closed together or enclosed. It implies that they are in a confined or restricted space.
