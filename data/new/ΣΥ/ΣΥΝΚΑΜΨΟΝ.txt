Etymology: The word "συνκαμψον" (sunkamson) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "καμψον" (kamson), meaning "bend." 

Grammar: "Συνκαμψον" is a verb in the imperative mood, second person singular form. It is derived from the verb "συνκάμπτω" (sunkampto), which means "to bend together" or "to flex." 

Description: "Συνκαμψον" is a command or instruction to someone to bend or flex something together. It is used to direct someone to perform a bending action in a collaborative or collective manner.
