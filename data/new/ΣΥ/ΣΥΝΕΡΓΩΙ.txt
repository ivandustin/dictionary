Etymology: The word "συνεργώ" (synergo) comes from the Greek roots "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and "ἔργον" (ergon), meaning "work." Therefore, "συνεργώ" can be translated as "to work together" or "to cooperate."

Grammar: "Συνεργώ" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ συνεργώ (ego synergo) - I work together
- Εσύ συνεργείς (esi synergeis) - You work together
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό συνεργεί (aftos/afti/afto synergei) - He/She/It works together
- Εμείς συνεργούμε (emeis synergoume) - We work together
- Εσείς συνεργείτε (eseis synergeite) - You work together
- Αυτοί/Αυτές συνεργούν (aftoi/aftes synergoun) - They work together

Description: "Συνεργώ" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It generally refers to the act of working together or cooperating with others towards a common goal. It can be used in both professional and personal settings to describe collaboration, teamwork, or joint efforts.
