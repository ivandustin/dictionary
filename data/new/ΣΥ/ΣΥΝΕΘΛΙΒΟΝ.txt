Etymology: The word "συνεθλιβον" (syneethlivon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εθλιβω" (ethlivó), meaning "to press" or "to squeeze." 

Grammar: "Συνεθλιβον" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb.

Description: "Συνεθλιβον" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it is pressing together" or "he/she/it is squeezing together." It describes the action of applying pressure or force to bring two or more things closer or to compress them.
