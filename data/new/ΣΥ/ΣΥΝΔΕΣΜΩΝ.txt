Etymology: The word "συνδεσμων" (syndesmon) comes from the Greek root words "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "δεσμος" (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie." 

Grammar: "Συνδεσμων" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. 

Description: "Συνδεσμων" is a Greek word that translates to "link" or "connection" in English. It refers to something that joins or connects different elements together. In a broader sense, it can also refer to a bond, tie, or relationship between people or things.
