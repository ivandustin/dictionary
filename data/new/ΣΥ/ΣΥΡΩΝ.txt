Etymology: The word "συρων" (syron) comes from the Greek verb "σύρω" (syro), which means "to drag" or "to pull". 

Grammar: "Συρων" is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. 

Description: "Συρων" refers to a person who drags or pulls something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is influential or persuasive in leading others.
