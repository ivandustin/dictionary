Etymology: The word "συνδεσμον" (syndesmon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "δεσμός" (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie." 

Grammar: "Συνδεσμον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension and is classified as a neuter noun. 

Description: "Συνδεσμον" refers to a bond, connection, or link between two or more things. It can also be used to describe a tie or relationship between people or groups.
