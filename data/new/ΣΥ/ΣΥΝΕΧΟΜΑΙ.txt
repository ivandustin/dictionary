Etymology: The word "συνεχομαι" (synechomai) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εχω" (echo), meaning "to have." 

Grammar: "Συνεχομαι" is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the first person singular form. 

Description: The word "συνεχομαι" means "I continue" or "I persevere" in English. It implies the act of persisting or enduring in a certain action or state.
