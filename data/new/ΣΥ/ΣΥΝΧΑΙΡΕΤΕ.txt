Etymology: The word "συνχαιρετε" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the prefix "συν-" meaning "together" or "with," and the verb "χαιρετε" meaning "to greet" or "to rejoice." Therefore, "συνχαιρετε" can be translated as "to greet together" or "to rejoice together."

Grammar: "Συνχαιρετε" is the second person plural form of the verb "χαιρετε" in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all greet" or "you all rejoice." In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the subject, tense, and mood.

Description: "Συνχαιρετε" is a common Greek expression used to greet a group of people or to express joy and happiness together. It is often used in formal or informal settings to address multiple individuals.
