Etymology: The word "συνπασχει" (synpaschei) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "πάσχει" (paschei), meaning "to suffer." 

Grammar: "Συνπασχει" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "πάσχω" (pascho), meaning "to suffer," and the prefix "συν" (syn), which indicates joint or shared action. 

Description: "Συνπασχει" is used to describe the act of suffering together or sharing in someone else's suffering. It implies empathy, compassion, and solidarity with others who are going through a difficult or painful experience.
