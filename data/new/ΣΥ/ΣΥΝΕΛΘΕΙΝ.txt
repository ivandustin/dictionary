Etymology: The word "συνελθείν" (synelethein) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἔλθειν" (elthein), meaning "to come." 

Grammar: "Συνελθείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is also in the active voice, suggesting that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Συνελθείν" means "to come together" or "to assemble" in Greek. It implies the gathering or meeting of people or things in one place. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the convergence of ideas, opinions, or forces.
