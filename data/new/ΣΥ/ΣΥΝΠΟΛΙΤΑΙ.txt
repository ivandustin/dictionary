Etymology: The word "συνπολιται" (synpolite) comes from the Greek words "συν" (syn), meaning "together" or "with," and "πολίται" (polite), meaning "citizens." 

Grammar: "Συνπολιται" is a noun in the plural form. It is used to refer to a group of people who are fellow citizens or co-citizens.

Description: The term "συνπολιται" is often used in a political or social context to describe individuals who share the same citizenship or belong to the same community. It emphasizes the idea of unity and commonality among citizens.
