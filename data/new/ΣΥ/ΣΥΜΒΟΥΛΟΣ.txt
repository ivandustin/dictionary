Etymology: The word "συμβούλος" (symvoulos) comes from the Greek roots "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "βουλή" (vouli), meaning "counsel" or "advice." It literally translates to "one who gives advice together" or "counselor."

Grammar: In Greek, "συμβούλος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "σύμβουλοι" (symvouloi).

Description: "Συμβούλος" is a Greek word that refers to a person who provides advice or counsel. It can be used to describe a professional advisor, consultant, or counselor in various fields such as business, politics, or personal matters.
