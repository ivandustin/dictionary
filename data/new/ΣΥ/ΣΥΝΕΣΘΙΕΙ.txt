Etymology: The word "συνεσθιεῖ" comes from the Greek verb "συνεσθίω" (synesthiō), which is a combination of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἐσθίω" (esthiō), meaning "to eat." Therefore, "συνεσθιεῖ" can be translated as "he/she/it will eat together" or "he/she/it will consume together."

Grammar: "Συνεσθιεῖ" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person singular form refers to a singular subject (he/she/it).

Description: "Συνεσθιεῖ" is a Greek verb that describes the action of eating or consuming together. It implies a shared meal or the act of eating in company with others.
