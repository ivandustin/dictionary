Etymology: The word "συναγαγείν" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἄγω" (agō), meaning "to lead" or "to bring." 

Grammar: "Συναγαγείν" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "συνάγω" (synagō), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." It is used in Greek grammar to express an action that is completed in the past.

Description: "Συναγαγείν" is a Greek verb that describes the act of gathering or bringing things or people together. It can be used in various contexts, such as gathering a group of people for a meeting or collecting objects in one place.
