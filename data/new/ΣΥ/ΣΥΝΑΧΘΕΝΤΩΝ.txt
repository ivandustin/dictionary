Grammar: "Συναχθέντων" is the genitive plural form of the verb "συνάγω" (synagō). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin. The plural form indicates that there are multiple subjects or objects being gathered or brought together.

Description: "Συναχθέντων" is a word used in Greek to describe a group of subjects or objects that have been gathered or brought together. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a gathering of people, a collection of items, or a compilation of information.
