Etymology: The word "συνεκαθισεν" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "καθίζω" (kathizō), meaning "to sit." 

Grammar: "Συνεκαθισεν" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "καθίζω" (kathizō), which means "to sit." The prefix "συν" (syn) adds the meaning of "together" or "with."

Description: "Συνεκαθισεν" is a Greek word that translates to "he/she/it sat down together" in English. It is used to describe the action of sitting down in a collective or communal manner.
