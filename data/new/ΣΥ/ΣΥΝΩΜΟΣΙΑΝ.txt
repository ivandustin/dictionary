Etymology: The word "συνωμοσίαν" (synomosian) comes from the Greek roots "σύν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ὄμνυμι" (omnumi), meaning "to swear." It originally referred to a group of people who swore an oath together.

Grammar: "Συνωμοσίαν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Συνωμοσίαν" means "conspiracy" in Greek. It refers to a secret plan or agreement between a group of people to commit an illegal or harmful act.
