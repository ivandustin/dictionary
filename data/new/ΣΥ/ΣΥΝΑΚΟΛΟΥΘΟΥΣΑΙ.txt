Etymology: The word "συνακολουθούσαι" comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the prefix "συν-" meaning "together" or "with," and the verb "ακολουθώ" meaning "to follow." 

Grammar: "Συνακολουθούσαι" is the third person singular, present tense, active voice form of the verb "ακολουθώ" in Greek. It is used to describe an action that is happening in the present and is being performed by a single subject. 

Description: "Συνακολουθούσαι" is a Greek verb that means "she/he/it follows together" or "she/he/it is following together." It implies that the subject is following or accompanying someone or something else.
