Etymology: The word "συνωσίν" (synōsin) comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synagō), which means "to gather" or "to bring together." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "άγω" (agō), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar: "Συνωσίν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Συνωσίν" refers to a gathering or assembly of people. It can also be used to describe a meeting or a congregation.
