Etymology: The word "συναγωγών" (synagogon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἀγωγή" (agogé), meaning "leading" or "bringing." It originally referred to a place of assembly or gathering.

Grammar: "Συναγωγών" is the plural form of the noun "συναγωγή" (synagogi), which means "synagogue" in Greek. It is a second declension feminine noun.

Description: "Συναγωγών" refers to multiple synagogues. In Judaism, a synagogue is a place of worship and community gathering for Jews. It is used for prayer, study, and communal events. Synagogues play a central role in Jewish religious and cultural life.
