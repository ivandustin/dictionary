Etymology: The word "συνεκλείσαν" comes from the Greek verb "συνεκλείω" (synekleio), which is a compound of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "εκλείω" (ekleio), meaning "to close." 

Grammar: "Συνεκλείσαν" is the third person plural, aorist active indicative form of the verb "συνεκλείω" in Greek. It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past by a group of people.

Description: "Συνεκλείσαν" means "they closed together" or "they closed collectively" in English. It implies that a group of individuals closed or shut something in unison or cooperation.
