Etymology: The word "συνεργον" (synergon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εργον" (ergon), meaning "work" or "action." It literally translates to "working together" or "cooperation."

Grammar: "Συνεργον" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. It can be declined in various cases, such as nominative (συνεργον), genitive (συνεργου), dative (συνεργῳ), accusative (συνεργον), and vocative (συνεργε).

Description: "Συνεργον" refers to someone or something that works together or cooperates with others. It can be used to describe a person who collaborates with others to achieve a common goal or a thing that contributes to a joint effort. The term emphasizes the idea of synergy and the combined effort of multiple individuals or elements working in harmony.
