Etymology: The word "συνεχαίρων" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εχαίρων" (echairon), meaning "rejoicing." 

Grammar: "Συνεχαίρων" is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb "χαίρω" (chairō), meaning "to rejoice," and the prefix "συν" (syn), which indicates a sense of togetherness or unity. 

Description: "Συνεχαίρων" is used to describe someone who is rejoicing or expressing joy together with others. It implies a sense of shared happiness or celebration.
