Etymology: The word "συνεπληρούντο" comes from the Greek verb "συνεπληρόω" (syneplēroō), which is a compound word formed by the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "επληρόω" (eplēroō), meaning "to fulfill" or "to complete."

Grammar: "Συνεπληρούντο" is the third person plural, past tense, passive voice form of the verb "συνεπληρόω" (syneplēroō). In Greek grammar, the passive voice is used to indicate that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon. The past tense indicates that the action of fulfilling or completing took place in the past.

Description: "Συνεπληρούντο" is a Greek verb that means "they were being fulfilled" or "they were being completed." It describes an ongoing action in the past where something was being fulfilled or completed by a group of people.
