Etymology: The word "συνδουλος" (syndoulos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together" or "with," and "δουλος" (doulos), meaning "slave." Therefore, the literal meaning of "συνδουλος" is "together with a slave" or "co-slave."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνδουλος" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used as a subject, object, or possessive noun in a sentence.

Description: "Συνδουλος" refers to a person who is a fellow or co-slave. In ancient Greece, slavery was a common practice, and this term was used to describe individuals who were enslaved together or shared the same status as slaves. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe individuals who are in a similar situation or share a common fate.
