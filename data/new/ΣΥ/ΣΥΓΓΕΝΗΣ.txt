Etymology: The word "συγγενής" (syngenis) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together" or "with," and "γένος" (genos), meaning "race" or "family." Therefore, "συγγενής" can be translated as "related" or "of the same family."

Grammar: In Greek, "συγγενής" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe familial relationships or connections between people.

Description: "Συγγενής" is commonly used to describe someone who is related to another person by blood or marriage. It can also be used more broadly to indicate a connection or similarity between two things.
