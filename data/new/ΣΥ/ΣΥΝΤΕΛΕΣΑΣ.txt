Etymology: The word "συντελέσας" comes from the Greek verb "συντελέω" (synteleo), which means "to complete" or "to accomplish". It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb "τελέω" (teleo), meaning "to bring to an end" or "to fulfill".

Grammar: "Συντελέσας" is the aorist active participle of the verb "συντελέω" (synteleo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Συντελέσας" can be translated as "having completed" or "having accomplished". It is used to describe someone who has finished or fulfilled a task or action.
