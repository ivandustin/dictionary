Etymology: The word "συνηθεια" (synētheia) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ηθος" (ēthos), meaning "habit" or "custom." It can be translated as "habitual association" or "familiarity."

Grammar: "Συνηθεια" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the first declension and has the nominative singular form "συνηθεια." In the plural, it becomes "συνηθειες" (synētheies).

Description: "Συνηθεια" refers to the habitual or customary behavior of individuals or groups. It can also denote a familiar or regular practice. In a broader sense, it can encompass traditions, routines, or patterns of behavior that are commonly followed.
