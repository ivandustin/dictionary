Etymology: The word "συνεκινησαν" comes from the Greek verb "συνεκινέω" (synekinéo), which is a compound of the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "εκινέω" (ekinéo), meaning "to move." 

Grammar: "συνεκινησαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood form of the verb "συνεκινέω" (synekinéo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "συνεκινησαν" means "they moved together" or "they were moved together" in English. It describes a past action where multiple subjects moved or were moved in unison.
