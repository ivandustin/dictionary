Etymology: The word "συνεστηκεν" comes from the Greek verb "συνίστημι" (synístēmi), which means "to establish" or "to set up." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "ἵστημι" (hístēmi), meaning "to stand" or "to place."

Grammar: "Συνεστηκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "συνίστημι" in the perfect tense. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate completed actions or states that have ongoing relevance to the present.

Description: "Συνεστηκεν" can be translated as "he/she/it has established" or "he/she/it has set up." It implies that someone or something has been established or created in the past and continues to exist or have an effect in the present.
