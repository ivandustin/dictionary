Etymology: The word "συνελθούσαις" comes from the Greek verb "συνέρχομαι" (synerchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." The verb is formed by combining the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Συνελθούσαις" is the feminine plural form of the verb "συνέρχομαι" in the aorist tense. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The feminine plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of female individuals.

Description: "Συνελθούσαις" can be translated as "having come together" or "having assembled" in English. It describes a group of female individuals who have gathered or met at a specific time in the past.
