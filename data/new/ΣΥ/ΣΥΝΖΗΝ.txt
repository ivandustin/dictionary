Etymology: The word "συνζήν" (syzín) comes from the Greek verb "ζῆν" (zēn), which means "to live," and the prefix "συν-" (syn-), which means "together." Therefore, "συνζήν" can be translated as "to live together" or "to coexist."

Grammar: In Greek, "συνζήν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: συνζώ (syzó) - I live together, you live together, he/she/it lives together
- Plural: συνζοῦμεν (syzoúmen) - we live together, you live together, they live together

Description: "Συνζήν" is a word that describes the act of living together or coexisting with others. It implies a sense of shared existence and mutual support. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the relationships between individuals, communities, or even different species.
