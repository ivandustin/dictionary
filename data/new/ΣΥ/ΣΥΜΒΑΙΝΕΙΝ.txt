Etymology: The word "συμβαίνειν" (sympaínein) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "βαίνειν" (baínein), meaning "to go." It literally means "to go together" or "to happen."

Grammar: "Συμβαίνειν" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject and tense. The verb "συμβαίνειν" is commonly used in the present tense to describe ongoing or habitual actions.

Description: "Συμβαίνειν" is a versatile verb that can be translated as "to happen," "to occur," "to take place," or "to happen to." It is used to describe events, situations, or circumstances that occur or come about. It can be used in various contexts, such as discussing daily occurrences, accidents, or unexpected events.
