Etymology: The word "συναρμολογούμενη" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "αρμολογώ" meaning "to assemble" or "to put together". 

Grammar: "Συναρμολογούμενη" is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb "συναρμολογώ", which means "to assemble" or "to put together". In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. 

Description: "Συναρμολογούμενη" is an adjective that describes something or someone that is being assembled or put together. It can be used to describe physical objects that are being constructed or figurative concepts that are being developed or organized.
