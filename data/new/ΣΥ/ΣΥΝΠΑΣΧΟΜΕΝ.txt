Etymology: The word "συνπασχομένος" (synpaschomenos) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "πάσχω" (pascho), meaning "to suffer." 

Grammar: "Συνπασχομένος" is a Greek adjective in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb "πάσχω" (pascho), which means "to suffer," and the prefix "συν" (syn), which means "together." The adjective form indicates that someone is suffering together with someone else.

Description: "Συνπασχομένος" is an adjective that describes someone who is suffering together with someone else. It implies a shared experience of suffering or hardship.
