Etymology: The word "συναγωγή" (synagogi) comes from the Greek verb "συνάγω" (synago), which means "to gather together" or "to bring together." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "άγω" (ago), meaning "to lead" or "to bring."

Grammar: "Συναγωγή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "συναγωγές" (synagoges). In Greek grammar, nouns are declined according to gender, number, and case.

Description: "Συναγωγή" primarily refers to a place of assembly or gathering for Jewish worship and study. It is commonly used to describe a synagogue, which is a Jewish house of prayer and community center. Synagogues serve as places for communal worship, religious education, and social gatherings for Jewish communities.
