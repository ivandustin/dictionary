Etymology: The word "συνερχονται" (synerchontai) is derived from the Greek verb "συνέρχομαι" (synerchomai), which means "to come together" or "to assemble." It is formed by combining the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come."

Grammar: "Συνερχονται" is the third person plural form of the present tense of the verb "συνέρχομαι" (synerchomai). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject and tense. The "-ονται" (-ontai) ending indicates that the subject is in the third person plural. The verb is in the present tense, indicating an ongoing action.

Description: "Συνερχονται" is a Greek verb that describes the action of multiple individuals coming together or assembling. It can be used to express the idea of people gathering or meeting in a specific location or for a particular purpose.
