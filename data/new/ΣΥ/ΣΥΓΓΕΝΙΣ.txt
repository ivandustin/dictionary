Etymology: The word "συγγενις" (syngenis) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together" or "with," and "γένος" (genos), meaning "race" or "family." Therefore, "συγγενις" can be translated as "related" or "of the same family."

Grammar: In Greek, "συγγενις" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in various forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Συγγενις" is used to describe a relationship or connection between people who are related by blood or marriage. It indicates that two or more individuals share a common ancestry or belong to the same family.
