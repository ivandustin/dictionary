Etymology: The word "συνζητείν" comes from the Greek roots "συν-" meaning "together" and "ζητείν" meaning "to seek". It can be translated as "to discuss" or "to converse".

Grammar: "Συνζητείν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the third person singular form.

Description: "Συνζητείν" is a Greek word that means "to discuss" or "to converse". It refers to the act of engaging in a conversation or dialogue with others, usually to exchange ideas, opinions, or information. It implies a mutual exchange of thoughts and perspectives between two or more individuals.
