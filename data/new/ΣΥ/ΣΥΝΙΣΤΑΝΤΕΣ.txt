Etymology: The word "συνιστάντες" comes from the Greek verb "συνίστημι" (synístēmi), which means "to set together" or "to establish." The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Συνιστάντες" is the present participle form of the verb "συνίστημι" in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently "setting together" or "establishing" something. In terms of grammar, it functions as an adjective or a noun, depending on the context.

Description: "Συνιστάντες" refers to a group of people who are actively "setting together" or "establishing" something. The exact meaning and context can vary depending on the specific usage.
