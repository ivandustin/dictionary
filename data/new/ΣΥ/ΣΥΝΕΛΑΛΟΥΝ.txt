Etymology: The word "συνελαλοῦν" (syne-laloun) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ἐλαλέω" (elaleo), meaning "to speak." 

Grammar: "Συνελαλοῦν" is a verb in the present active participle form of the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "λαλέω" (laleo), which means "to speak." The prefix "συν" (syn) adds the meaning of "together" or "with." 

Description: "Συνελαλοῦν" is used to describe the action of speaking together or conversing. It implies a collaborative or interactive form of communication where multiple individuals are engaged in a conversation or dialogue.
