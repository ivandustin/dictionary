Etymology: The word "συνκαταθέσεις" (synkatatheseis) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "κατάθεση" (katathesi), meaning "deposit" or "placing." 

Grammar: "Συνκαταθέσεις" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Συνκαταθέσεις" refers to "deposits" or "contributions" made by multiple individuals or entities. It can be used in various contexts, such as financial or political discussions, where multiple parties contribute to a common fund or cause.
