Etymology: The word "συνταφέντες" comes from the Greek verb "συντάσσω" (syntássō), which means "to bury together" or "to inter together". The word is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together", and the verb "τάσσω" (tássō), meaning "to arrange" or "to place".

Grammar: "Συνταφέντες" is the plural form of the past participle of the verb "συντάσσω". It is used to describe a group of people who have been buried together or interred together.

Description: "Συνταφέντες" refers to a group of individuals who have been buried together in the same grave or burial site. It can be used to describe a collective burial of people who share a common fate or event, such as soldiers who died in battle or victims of a natural disaster.
