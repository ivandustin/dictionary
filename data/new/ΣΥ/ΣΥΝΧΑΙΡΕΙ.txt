Etymology: The word "συνχαιρει" (synchairei) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "χαιρει" (chairei), meaning "to rejoice." 

Grammar: "Συνχαιρει" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "χαιρω" (chairo), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad." The prefix "συν" (syn) adds the meaning of "together" or "with."

Description: "Συνχαιρει" is used to express the act of rejoicing or being glad together with someone. It implies a sense of shared joy or happiness.
