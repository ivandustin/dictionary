Etymology: The word "συνκατατεθειμένος" (synkatatetheimenos) is derived from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," "κατά" (kata), meaning "down," and "τίθημι" (tithemi), meaning "to place." 

Grammar: "Συνκατατεθειμένος" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe someone or something that has been agreed upon or settled together.

Description: "Συνκατατεθειμένος" refers to a person or thing that has reached a mutual agreement or consensus. It implies that all parties involved have come to a decision or understanding together.
