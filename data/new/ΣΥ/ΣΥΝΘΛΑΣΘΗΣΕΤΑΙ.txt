Etymology: The word "συνθλάσθησεται" comes from the Greek verb "συνθλάω" (synthlao), which means "to crush" or "to shatter." The suffix "-σθησεται" is a passive form indicating that the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar: "Συνθλάσθησεται" is a third-person singular, future passive indicative form of the verb "συνθλάω" (synthlao). It is used to express an action that will be done to the subject in the future.

Description: "Συνθλάσθησεται" describes an action of being crushed or shattered that will happen to a third-person singular subject in the future.
