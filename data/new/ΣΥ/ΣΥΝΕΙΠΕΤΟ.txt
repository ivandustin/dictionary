Etymology: The word "συνειπετο" comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "ειπετο" (eipeto), meaning "he/she/it said." 

Grammar: "Συνειπετο" is the third person singular, past tense form of the verb "συνειπω" (suneipo), which means "to say together" or "to speak in unison." It is a compound verb formed by adding the prefix "συν-" to the verb "ειπω" (eipo), meaning "to say."

Description: "Συνειπετο" is used to describe an action in the past where multiple individuals or groups spoke or said something together. It implies a sense of unity or agreement in speech.
