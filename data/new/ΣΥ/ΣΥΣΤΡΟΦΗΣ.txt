Etymology: The word "συστροφής" comes from the Greek verb "στρέφω" (strefó), which means "to turn" or "to twist". The prefix "συν-" (syn-) means "together" or "with", so "συστροφής" can be translated as "turning together" or "twisting together".

Grammar: In Greek, "συστροφής" is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of the noun "συστροφή" (systrofí), which means "turning" or "twisting". The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "Συστροφής" refers to the act or process of turning or twisting. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the rotation of an object, the winding of a path, or the twisting of a rope.
