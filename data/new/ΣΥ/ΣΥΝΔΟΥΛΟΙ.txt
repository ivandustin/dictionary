Etymology: The word "συνδουλοι" (syndouloi) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "δουλος" (doulos), meaning "slave." It literally translates to "together slaves" or "fellow slaves."

Grammar: "Συνδουλοι" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word "συνδουλοι" is masculine, plural, and in the nominative case.

Description: "Συνδουλοι" refers to a group of people who are slaves or servants together. It implies a sense of shared servitude or common subjugation. The term can be used metaphorically to describe people who are oppressed or exploited together.
