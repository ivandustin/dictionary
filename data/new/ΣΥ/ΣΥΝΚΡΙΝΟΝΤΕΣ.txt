Etymology: The word "συνκρινοντες" (syngkrinontes) comes from the Greek verb "συνκρίνω" (syngkrino), which means "to compare" or "to judge together." It is derived from the prefix "συν-" (syn-), meaning "together," and the verb "κρίνω" (krino), meaning "to judge" or "to separate."

Grammar: "Συνκρινοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "συνκρίνω" (syngkrino) in the plural form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Συνκρινοντες" is a Greek word that translates to "comparing" or "judging together" in English. It refers to the act of comparing or judging multiple things or ideas simultaneously. This word can be used in various contexts, such as in discussions, debates, or academic research, where different elements are being compared or evaluated.
