Etymology: The word "συνεσχον" (synechon) comes from the Greek roots "συν" (syn), meaning "together," and "εσχον" (echon), meaning "to hold." 

Grammar: "Συνεσχον" is the aorist tense of the verb "συνέχω" (synecho), which means "to hold together" or "to contain." It is conjugated in the first person singular.

Description: "Συνεσχον" is a Greek word that describes an action of holding something together or containing it. It can be used in various contexts, both literal and figurative, to express the idea of keeping things united or connected.
