The word "εγερσιν" (egerin) is a Greek noun that means "resurrection" or "rising up". It is derived from the verb "εγείρω" (egero), which means "to wake up" or "to raise up". In terms of grammar, "εγερσιν" is a third declension noun, meaning that its endings change depending on its grammatical case (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, etc.). Overall, "εγερσιν" is a powerful and significant word in Greek, often used in religious and spiritual contexts to refer to the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
