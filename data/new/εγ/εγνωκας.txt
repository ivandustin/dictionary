Word: εγνωκας

Etymology: εγνωκας is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the perfect tense, which means that the action of knowing has been completed in the past.

Grammar: εγνωκας is the second person singular form of the perfect tense of the verb γινώσκω (ginosko). It is translated as "you have known" or "you have understood" in English. The verb is conjugated as follows:

εγνώκα (I have known)
εγνώκας (you have known)
εγνώκει (he/she/it has known)
εγνώκαμεν (we have known)
εγνώκατε (you all have known)
εγνώκασι (they have known)

Description: εγνωκας is a verb that indicates that the action of knowing or understanding has been completed in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as to express knowledge of a fact, understanding of a concept, or familiarity with a person or place. The perfect tense emphasizes the result of the action, rather than the action itself.
