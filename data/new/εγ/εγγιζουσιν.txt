The word "εγγιζουσιν" (engizousin) is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form, in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "εγγίζω" (engizo), which means "to approach" or "to draw near."

Grammar: The word is a regular verb in the present tense, and it is conjugated as follows: 

- εγγιζω (engizo) - I approach
- εγγιζεις (engizeis) - you approach
- εγγιζει (engizei) - he/she/it approaches
- εγγιζομεν (engizomen) - we approach
- εγγιζετε (engizete) - you approach (plural)
- εγγιζουσι(ν) (engizousi(n)) - they approach

Description: The word "εγγιζουσιν" is used to describe an action of approaching or drawing near to something or someone. It can be used in a literal sense, such as approaching a physical object or location, or in a figurative sense, such as approaching a goal or objective.
