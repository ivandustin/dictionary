Word: εγεγονει

Etymology: The word εγεγονει comes from the Greek verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the present tense, while the reduplication of the initial consonant γ- (g-) emphasizes the action of the verb.

Grammar: εγεγονει is a third-person singular present indicative active form of the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai). It is used to describe an ongoing action or state of being in the present.

Description: εγεγονει can be translated as "he/she/it is becoming" or "he/she/it is being born". It implies a continuous process of transformation or development. In a broader sense, it can also refer to any kind of change or evolution.
