Word: εγειρονται

Etymology: The word εγειρονται comes from the Greek verb εγείρω (egeíro), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁wegʷ- ("to be strong, lively").

Grammar: εγειρονται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is the third person plural form of the verb εγείρω. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description: εγειρονται is a verb that describes the action of waking up or rising. It can be used to describe the physical act of getting out of bed in the morning, or the figurative act of becoming alert or active. The passive voice form suggests that the subject is being awakened or raised by an external force or agent.
