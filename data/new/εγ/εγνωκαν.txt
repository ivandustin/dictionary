Word: εγνωκαν

Etymology: εγνωκαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the perfect tense, which means the action has been completed in the past.

Grammar: εγνωκαν is the third person plural of the perfect tense of the verb γινώσκω (ginósko). In English, it translates to "they have known."

Description: εγνωκαν is a verb that indicates a completed action in the past. It is often used to express knowledge or understanding of something. In context, it can mean "they have come to know" or "they have learned."
