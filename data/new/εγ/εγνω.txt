Word: εγνω

Etymology:
The word εγνω (egno) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginosko), which means "to know." The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate the past tense, making εγνω (egno) mean "he/she/it knew."

Grammar:
εγνω (egno) is a third-person singular past tense verb in the Greek language. It can be used to describe an action that was completed in the past, such as "he knew" or "she knew."

Description:
εγνω (egno) is a simple past tense verb that is used to describe knowledge or understanding that was gained in the past. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as to describe a person's knowledge of a particular subject or to indicate that someone had prior knowledge of a situation. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is an important part of the Greek language.
