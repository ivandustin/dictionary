The word "εγειρειν" (egeirein) is a Greek verb that means "to wake up" or "to rise". It comes from the ancient Greek word "εγειρω" (egeirō), which has the same meaning. 

In terms of grammar, "εγειρειν" is an infinitive verb form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used in combination with auxiliary verbs to form compound tenses, such as "Έχω ξυπνήσει" (Echō xypnēsei), which means "I have woken up".

In terms of description, "εγειρειν" is a common and important verb in Greek, as it is used to describe the act of waking up or rising from sleep or a lying position. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person or group becoming active or alert, or a situation or idea gaining attention or momentum.
