Word: εγεννηθης

Etymology:
The word εγεννηθης is a Greek word that comes from the verb γεννάω (gennao), which means "to beget" or "to give birth." The prefix ε- (e-) in this word is a common Greek prefix that indicates a sense of "out of" or "from."

Grammar:
εγεννηθης is a second-person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb γεννάω (gennao). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
The word εγεννηθης can be translated to English as "you were born" or "you have been born." It is a form of the verb γεννάω (gennao), which is used to describe the act of giving birth or begetting.
