Word: εγενομην

The word εγενομην (egenomēn) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." It is a compound word made up of the prefix ε- (e-), which means "out of" or "from," and the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai).

εγενομην (egenomēn) is a first-person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

εγενομην (egenomēn) can be translated to English as "I became" or "I was born."
