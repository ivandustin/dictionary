Word: εγνωσαν

Etymology: εγνωσαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know" or "to understand". The verb comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃- which means "to know".

Grammar: εγνωσαν is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood of the verb γινώσκω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: εγνωσαν means "they knew" or "they understood" in English. It is a past tense verb that indicates that a group of people had knowledge or understanding of something in the past. The context of the sentence will determine what exactly they knew or understood.
