Word: εγενεσθε

Etymology:
The word εγενεσθε is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to come into existence". The word εγενεσθε is the second person plural aorist indicative active form of the verb, which means "you became" or "you came into existence".

Grammar:
εγενεσθε is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a past action that is completed. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

εγενεσθε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Ephesians 5:8, where it is translated as "you were once darkness, but now you are light in the Lord".
