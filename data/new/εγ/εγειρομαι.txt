The word "εγειρομαι" (egiromai) is a Greek verb that means "to wake up" or "to rise". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "εγείρω" (egiro), which means "to lift up" or "to raise". 

Grammar: "εγειρομαι" is a reflexive verb, which means that the subject of the verb is also the object. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Εγειρομαι (egiromai) - I wake up 
- Εγειρεσαι (egiresai) - You wake up 
- Εγειρεται (egiretai) - He/She/It wakes up 
- Εγειρομαστε (egiromaste) - We wake up 
- Εγειρεστε (egireste) - You all wake up 
- Εγειρονται (egirontai) - They wake up 

Description: "εγειρομαι" is a common verb in Greek and is used in everyday conversation. It is often used to describe the act of waking up in the morning or after a nap. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person or situation that is coming to life or becoming active.
