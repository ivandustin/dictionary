Word: εγενηθησαν

Etymology:
The word εγενηθησαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate a past tense form, making εγενηθησαν the third person plural past tense form of γίνομαι.

Grammar:
εγενηθησαν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the past tense. It is the third person plural form, meaning it refers to a group of people or things that became or happened in the past. In Greek, verbs change form depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person of the subject.

Description:
εγενηθησαν is a word that describes an action that occurred in the past. It can be translated to English as "they became" or "they happened". The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning. It is a common word in Greek literature and is used in a variety of contexts, from describing the creation of the world in Greek mythology to the birth of Jesus in the New Testament.
