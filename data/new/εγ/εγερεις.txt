Word: εγερεις

Etymology: The word εγερεις comes from the Greek verb εγείρω (egero), which means "to wake up" or "to raise up."

Grammar: εγερεις is a second-person singular present active indicative form of the verb εγείρω (egero). It is used to address one person who is currently waking up or being raised up.

Description: εγερεις is a Greek word that is commonly used in everyday conversation to ask someone if they are awake or to tell them to wake up. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "to stir up" or "to rouse."
