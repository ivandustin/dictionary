Word: εγινωσκεν

Etymology: εγινωσκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know" or "to understand."

Grammar: εγινωσκεν is the third person singular, imperfect indicative active form of the verb γινώσκω. In Greek, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Description: εγινωσκεν means "he/she/it knew" or "he/she/it was knowing" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus' knowledge or understanding of a situation or person.
