The word "εγκρατη" (engkrati) is a Greek term that can be translated to mean "self-control" or "self-restraint." It is derived from the Greek words "εν" (en), meaning "in," and "κρατος" (kratos), meaning "power" or "strength." 

In terms of grammar, "εγκρατη" is a noun in the singular form. It can be used as a subject or object in a sentence, and can be modified by adjectives or other nouns.

In description, "εγκρατη" refers to the ability to control one's own desires and impulses, particularly in the context of avoiding excess or indulgence. It is often associated with virtues such as temperance, moderation, and self-discipline. In ancient Greek philosophy, the concept of "εγκρατη" was considered a key component of moral character and a necessary trait for achieving happiness and fulfillment.
