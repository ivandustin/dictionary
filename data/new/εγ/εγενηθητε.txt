Word: εγενηθητε

Etymology: εγενηθητε is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". The word is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: εγενηθητε is the second person plural form of the aorist passive indicative of γίνομαι. In English, it would be translated as "you were made" or "you became".

Description: εγενηθητε is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of 1 Corinthians 6:11. In this passage, the apostle Paul is addressing the Corinthian church and reminding them that they were once sinful and unrighteous, but they have been washed, sanctified, and justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ. The word εγενηθητε is used to emphasize the transformation that has taken place in the lives of the believers.
