The word "εγερω" (egero) is a Greek verb that means "to wake up" or "to arise". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "εγείρω" (egeiro), which has the same meaning. 

Grammar: "Εγερω" is a first-conjugation verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. Its conjugation in the present tense is as follows:

- Εγείρομαι (egeromai) - I wake up
- Εγείρεσαι (egeresai) - You wake up (singular)
- Εγείρεται (egeretai) - He/she/it wakes up
- Εγειρόμαστε (egeromaste) - We wake up
- Εγείρεστε (egereste) - You wake up (plural)
- Εγείρονται (egerontai) - They wake up

Description: "Εγερω" is a common verb in Greek and is used in various contexts, such as waking up from sleep, waking up from a coma, or waking up from a dream. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a sudden realization or awareness of something.
