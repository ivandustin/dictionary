Word: εγνωρισεν

Etymology: εγνωρισεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb γνωρίζω (gnorizo), which means "to make known" or "to inform."

Grammar: εγνωρισεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb γνωρίζω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: εγνωρισεν means "he/she/it made known" or "he/she/it informed" in English. It is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples, as they spread the message of Christianity. The word implies an act of communication, where information is shared or revealed to someone else.
