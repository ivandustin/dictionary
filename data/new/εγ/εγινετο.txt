Word: εγινετο

Etymology: εγινετο is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". It is a past tense form of the verb, which means "it happened" or "it became".

Grammar: εγινετο is a third person singular past tense form of the verb γίνομαι (ginomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, mood, voice, and person. The past tense is used to describe actions or events that happened in the past.

Description: εγινετο is a common Greek word that is used to describe something that happened or became in the past. It is often used in narratives, stories, or historical accounts to indicate a change or an event that occurred. The word can be translated into English as "it happened" or "it became".
