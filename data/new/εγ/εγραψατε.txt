Word: εγραψατε

Etymology: εγραψατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γράφω (grapho), which means "to write." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the second person plural form, making εγραψατε mean "you all wrote."

Grammar: εγραψατε is the second person plural aorist active indicative form of the verb γράφω (grapho). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject (in this case, "you all") performed the action of writing.

Description: εγραψατε is a verb that means "you all wrote" in Greek. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and writing. The word can be used to describe any type of writing, from a simple note to a lengthy essay. As a second person plural verb, it is often used in group settings or when addressing multiple people.
