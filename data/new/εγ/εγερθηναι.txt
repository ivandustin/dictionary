The word "εγερθηναι" is a Greek verb in the aorist passive infinitive form. Its etymology can be traced back to the root word "εγείρω" which means "to raise up" or "to awaken". 

In terms of grammar, "εγερθηναι" is a passive form of the verb, indicating that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The aorist tense suggests that the action was completed in the past, without specifying the duration or repetition of the action. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not being used to describe a specific subject or time, but rather as a general concept or idea.

In terms of description, "εγερθηναι" can be translated to mean "to be raised up" or "to be awakened". It can be used in a literal sense, such as referring to someone being physically lifted or woken up from sleep, or in a figurative sense, such as referring to someone being inspired or motivated.
