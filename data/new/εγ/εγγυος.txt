The word "εγγυος" (engyos) is a Greek adjective that means "guaranteed" or "pledged". It comes from the Greek verb "εγγυάω" (engyao), which means "to guarantee" or "to pledge". 

In terms of grammar, "εγγυος" is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be used as an adjective to describe a noun, in which case it agrees with the gender and number of the noun it modifies. 

In Greek culture, the concept of "εγγυος" was important in legal and financial matters. It referred to a person who acted as a guarantor or surety for someone else's debt or obligation. The "εγγυος" would be responsible for fulfilling the debt or obligation if the original debtor or obligor failed to do so. 

Overall, "εγγυος" is a word that conveys the idea of security and reliability, and is still used in modern Greek in legal and financial contexts.
