Word: εγειρεται

Etymology:
The word εγειρεται (egiretai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word εγείρω (egiro), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵ- (to rise, to lift up).

Grammar:
εγειρεται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb εγείρω (egiro) and the present tense suffix -ται (-tai) for the third person singular. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
εγειρεται means "he/she/it is being raised" or "he/she/it is waking up" in English. It can be used to describe the physical act of waking up from sleep or the figurative act of being raised or lifted up.
