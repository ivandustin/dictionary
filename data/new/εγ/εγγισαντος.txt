The word "εγγισαντος" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is the third person singular form of the verb "εγγίζω" (engizo), which means "to approach" or "to draw near". 

Etymology: The word "εγγίζω" comes from the Greek root "εγγύς" (engys), which means "near" or "close". 

Grammar: "Εγγισαντος" is a participle, which means it functions as an adjective or adverb in a sentence. In this form, it agrees with the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

Description: "Εγγισαντος" can be translated as "having approached" or "having drawn near". It is often used in the context of physical proximity, but can also be used metaphorically to describe emotional or spiritual closeness.
