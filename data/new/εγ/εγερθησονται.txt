Word: εγερθησονται

Etymology:
The word εγερθησονται is a Greek verb in the future passive indicative tense. It is derived from the root word εγείρω (egero), which means "to raise up" or "to awaken". The suffix -σονται (-sontai) indicates the future tense and the passive voice, meaning that the action will be done to the subject of the sentence.

Grammar:
εγερθησονται is a third-person plural verb in the future passive indicative tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, voice, and mood. The future tense indicates that the action will take place in the future, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence will receive the action. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
εγερθησονται can be translated to English as "they will be raised up" or "they will be awakened". The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to the resurrection of the dead.
