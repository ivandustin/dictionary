Etymology: The word comes from the root "εγείρω" (egiero), which means "to wake up" or "to rise". 

Grammar: "εγειραι" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "εγείρω". 

Description: The word "εγειραι" means "to wake up" or "to rise" in English. It is often used in the context of waking up from sleep or rising from a seated or lying position. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "to awaken" or "to stir up" something, such as emotions or memories.
