Word: εγενηθημεν

Etymology: εγενηθημεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". The word is in the passive voice and past tense, indicating that something has happened to the subject.

Grammar: εγενηθημεν is a third-person plural form of the verb γίνομαι in the aorist tense. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb rather than performing it.

Description: εγενηθημεν can be translated to English as "they were made" or "they were created". The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the creation of the world or the birth of Jesus Christ. It is a powerful word that conveys the idea of something coming into existence or being brought into being by a higher power.
