Word: εγραφη

Etymology: The word εγραφη comes from the Greek verb γράφω (grapho), which means "to write". The prefix ε- (e-) is a past tense marker, indicating that the action of writing has already taken place.

Grammar: εγραφη is a third person singular past tense form of the verb γράφω (grapho). It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The word is also passive, indicating that the subject of the sentence received the action of the verb.

Description: εγραφη is a Greek word that means "was written" or "has been written". It is often used in reference to written documents or inscriptions. For example, one might say "Εγραφη η ιστορία στα βιβλία" (egrafii i istoria sta vivlia), which means "History was written in books". The word can also be used in a more literal sense, such as "Εγραφη το όνομά μου στο βιβλίο επισκεπτών" (egrafii to onoma mou sto vivlio episkepton), which means "My name was written in the guestbook".
