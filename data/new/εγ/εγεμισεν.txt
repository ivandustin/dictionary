Word: εγεμίσεν

Etymology:
The word εγεμίσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γεμίζω (gemizo), which means "to fill" or "to fill up". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the word to indicate the past tense, making εγεμίσεν (egemisen) mean "filled" or "was filled".

Grammar:
εγεμίσεν is a third-person singular past tense verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. The verb is also in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
εγεμίσεν is a verb that describes the action of filling something up in the past. It can be used to describe a physical object being filled, such as a cup or a container, or it can be used in a more abstract sense to describe a person or situation being filled with a particular emotion or feeling.
