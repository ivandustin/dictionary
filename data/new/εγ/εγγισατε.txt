Word: εγγισατε

Etymology:
The word εγγισατε comes from the Greek verb εγγίζω (engizo), which means "to approach" or "to draw near". It is derived from the prefix εν- (en-), meaning "in" or "within", and the verb γίζω (gizo), meaning "to bring near" or "to bring close".

Grammar:
εγγισατε is the second person plural aorist tense of the verb εγγίζω (engizo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The second person plural form indicates that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description:
εγγισατε can be translated to English as "you approached" or "you drew near". It is a verb that describes an action of moving closer to something or someone. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning of the action.
