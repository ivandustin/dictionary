Etymology: The word "σπουδής" (spoudēs) comes from the Ancient Greek word "σπουδή" (spoudē), which means "diligence" or "zeal."

Grammar: In Greek, "σπουδής" is a noun of the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in the nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases.

Description: "Σπουδής" is a Greek word that is often translated as "diligence," "zeal," or "earnestness." It refers to a strong and focused effort or dedication towards a particular goal or task. It can also imply a sense of seriousness, commitment, and enthusiasm in one's actions or pursuits.
