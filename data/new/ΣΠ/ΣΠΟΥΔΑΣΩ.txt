Etymology: The word "σπουδάζω" (spoudázo) comes from the Greek verb "σπουδάζω" (spoudázo), which means "to study" or "to be diligent". It is derived from the noun "σπουδή" (spoudé), meaning "diligence" or "zeal".

Grammar: "Σπουδάζω" is a first-conjugation verb in Greek, which means it follows a regular pattern of conjugation. It is used to express the action of studying or being diligent. In its present tense, it conjugates as follows:

- Εγώ σπουδάζω (ego spoudázo) - I study
- Εσύ σπουδάζεις (esý spoudázeis) - You study (singular)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό σπουδάζει (aftós/aftí/aftó spoudázei) - He/She/It studies
- Εμείς σπουδάζουμε (emeís spoudázoume) - We study
- Εσείς σπουδάζετε (eseís spoudázete) - You study (plural)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά σπουδάζουν (aftoí/aftés/aftá spoudázoun) - They study

Description: "Σπουδάζω" is a versatile verb that is commonly used in Greek to describe the act of studying or being diligent in any field or subject. It can refer to formal education, such as studying at school or university, as well as self-study or personal development.
