Etymology: The word "σπαταλώσα" comes from the Greek verb "σπαταλώ" (spataló), which means "to waste" or "to squander". 

Grammar: "Σπαταλώσα" is the aorist tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form of the verb "σπαταλώ" (spataló). It is used to describe an action that occurred in the past and was performed by the speaker.

Description: "Σπαταλώσα" is a Greek word that translates to "I wasted" or "I squandered" in English. It is used to express the act of spending or using something wastefully or extravagantly.
