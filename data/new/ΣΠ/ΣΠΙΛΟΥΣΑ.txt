Etymology: The word "σπιλούσα" (spilousa) comes from the Greek verb "σπίλοω" (spiloo), which means "to stain" or "to spot". 

Grammar: "Σπιλούσα" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the first declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Σπιλούσα" refers to a woman who has been stained or spotted, usually in a negative or derogatory sense. It can be used metaphorically to describe someone who has a tarnished reputation or is considered morally impure.
