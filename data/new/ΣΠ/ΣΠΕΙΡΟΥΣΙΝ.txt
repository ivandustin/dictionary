Etymology: The word "σπειρούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "σπείρω" (speíro), which means "to sow" or "to scatter." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *sper-, meaning "to strew" or "to scatter."

Grammar: "Σπειρούσιν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb "σπείρω" (speíro) in Greek. It is used to indicate that a group of people or things are sowing or scattering something.

Description: "Σπειρούσιν" is a verb in Greek that describes the action of sowing or scattering. It can be used in various contexts, such as referring to the act of planting seeds in a field or spreading something in a dispersed manner.
