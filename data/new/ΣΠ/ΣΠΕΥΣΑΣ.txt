Etymology: The word "σπευσας" (spheusas) comes from the Greek verb "σπεύδω" (speudo), which means "to hurry" or "to hasten."

Grammar: "Σπευσας" is the aorist active participle of the verb "σπεύδω" (speudo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Σπευσας" can be translated as "having hurried" or "having hastened." It describes someone who has performed the action of hurrying or hastening in the past.
