Etymology: The word "σπερμασίν" (spermasin) comes from the Greek word "σπέρμα" (sperma), meaning "seed" or "sperm."

Grammar: "Σπερμασίν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun of the second declension.

Description: "Σπερμασίν" refers to the act of sowing or planting seeds. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of spreading or disseminating something, such as ideas or beliefs.
