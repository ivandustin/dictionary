Etymology: The word "σπερματος" (spermatos) comes from the Greek word "σπέρμα" (sperma), meaning "seed" or "sperm."

Grammar: In Greek, "σπερματος" is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of "σπέρμα" (sperma), which is a neuter noun.

Description: "Σπερματος" is used to refer to the seed or sperm of a living organism. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the origin or source of something.
