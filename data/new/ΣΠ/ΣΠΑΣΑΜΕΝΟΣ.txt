Etymology: The word "σπασαμένος" (spasamenos) comes from the Greek verb "σπάω" (spao), which means "to break" or "to shatter." The suffix "-μένος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice, so "σπασαμένος" can be translated as "broken" or "shattered."

Grammar: "Σπασαμένος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Σπασαμένος" describes something or someone that has been broken or shattered. It can be used to refer to physical objects that are damaged or to describe a person who is emotionally or mentally broken.
