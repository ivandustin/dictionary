Etymology: The word "σπηλαίον" (spēlaíon) comes from the Ancient Greek word "σπήλαιον" (spḗlaion), which means "cave".

Grammar: In Greek, "σπηλαίον" is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "σπήλαια" (spḗlaia).

Description: "Σπηλαίον" refers to a cave or a cavern. It is a natural underground hollow or passage, typically formed by the erosion of rock. Caves can be found in various geological formations and are often explored by speleologists or cave enthusiasts.
