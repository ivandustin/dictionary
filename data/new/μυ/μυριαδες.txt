Word: μυριάδες

Etymology:
The word μυριάδες (myriades) comes from the Greek word μύριοι (myrioi), meaning "ten thousand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *meu̯r-, meaning "number".

Grammar:
μυριάδες is a feminine noun in Greek, and its nominative plural form is μυριάδες. It is often used in the plural form to refer to a large number of things or people.

Description:
μυριάδες is a Greek word that means "countless" or "innumerable". It is often used to describe a large number of things or people, and can be used in both a literal and figurative sense. For example, one might say "μυριάδες αστέρια" (myriades asteria) to describe a sky full of stars, or "μυριάδες ανθρώπων" (myriades anthropon) to describe a large crowd of people.
