Word: μυρον

Etymology:
The word μυρον (myron) comes from the Greek μύρον (mýron), which means "ointment" or "perfume". It is derived from the verb μύρω (mýro), which means "to anoint" or "to smear with oil".

Grammar:
μυρον is a neuter noun in Greek. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: τὸ μῦρον (to mýron) - the ointment/perfume
Genitive: τοῦ μύρου (toú mýrou) - of the ointment/perfume
Accusative: τὸ μῦρον (to mýron) - the ointment/perfume

Plural:
Nominative: τὰ μύρα (tà mýra) - the ointments/perfumes
Genitive: τῶν μύρων (tôn mýrōn) - of the ointments/perfumes
Accusative: τὰ μύρα (tà mýra) - the ointments/perfumes

Description:
μυρον is a word that refers to a fragrant substance, such as an ointment or perfume, that is used for anointing or perfuming. In ancient Greece, it was often used in religious ceremonies and as a symbol of luxury and wealth. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to perfumes and fragrances.
