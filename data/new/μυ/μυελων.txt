Word: μυελων

Etymology:
The word μυελων (myelon) is derived from the Greek word μυελός (myelos), which means "marrow" or "spinal cord". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common Greek ending used to form nouns.

Grammar:
μυελων is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. μυελων is a masculine noun in the singular form.

Description:
μυελων refers to the spinal cord, which is a long, thin, tubular bundle of nervous tissue that extends from the brainstem to the lower back. It is a vital part of the central nervous system and is responsible for transmitting signals between the brain and the rest of the body. The spinal cord is protected by the vertebral column and is surrounded by cerebrospinal fluid. Damage to the spinal cord can result in paralysis or other serious neurological conditions.
