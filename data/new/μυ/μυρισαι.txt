Word: μυρίσαι

Etymology:
The word μυρίσαι (myrisai) is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood in Ancient Greek. It is derived from the root word μύρον (myron), which means "perfume" or "ointment." 

Grammar:
μυρίσαι is a second-person singular aorist active infinitive verb in Ancient Greek. It is used to express the action of "smelling" or "perceiving a scent." 

Description:
μυρίσαι is a verb that is used to describe the act of smelling or perceiving a scent. It is often used in the context of perfumes, fragrances, and aromas. In Ancient Greek literature, it is used to describe the act of smelling flowers, incense, and other fragrant substances.
