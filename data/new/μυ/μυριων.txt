Word: μυριων

Etymology: μυριων is a Greek word that means "ten thousand". It is derived from the Greek word μύριος (myrios), which means "countless" or "innumerable".

Grammar: μυριων is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word μυριάς (myrias), which means "ten thousand".

Description: μυριων is a term used to describe a large number or quantity. In ancient Greece, it was often used to describe the size of armies or the number of people attending an event. Today, it is still used in modern Greek to describe large numbers or quantities.
