The word μυστηριου (mysteriou) is a Greek noun in the genitive case, which means "of mystery" or "of secret". It is derived from the Greek word μυστήριον (mysterion), which means "mystery" or "secret". In Greek mythology, the term was used to refer to secret rites or ceremonies that were only known to a select few. In the New Testament of the Bible, the term is used to refer to the hidden wisdom of God that is revealed to believers. 

In terms of grammar, μυστηριου is a second declension noun in the genitive case. It is masculine in gender and singular in number. 

Overall, μυστηριου is a word that connotes secrecy, hidden knowledge, and mystery.
