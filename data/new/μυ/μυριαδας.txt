The word μυριάδας (myriadas) comes from the Greek word μύριοι (myrioi), meaning "ten thousand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *meu̯r- ("number, measure"), which also gave rise to the English word "myriad".

μυριάδας is a feminine noun in Greek, with the nominative singular form being μυριάδα. It is declined like other first-declension nouns, with the genitive singular form being μυριάδας and the accusative singular form being μυριάδα.

Description:
μυριάδας is a Greek word that refers to a very large number, specifically ten thousand. It can be used to describe a multitude of things, such as a crowd of people or a vast number of stars in the sky. In modern Greek, it is also used to describe a large sum of money.
