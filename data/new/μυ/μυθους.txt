The word μυθους (mythous) is a Greek noun that means "myths" or "legends". It is derived from the Greek word μυθος (mythos), which means "story" or "tale". In Greek mythology, myths were stories that explained the origins of the world, natural phenomena, and the actions of gods and heroes. 

In terms of grammar, μυθους is the plural form of the noun μυθος. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Overall, μυθους is a significant word in Greek culture and literature, representing the rich tradition of storytelling and mythology in ancient Greece.
