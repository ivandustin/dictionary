Word: ααρων

Etymology:
The word ααρων (Aaron) is a Greek transliteration of the Hebrew name אַהֲרֹן (Aharon). The name is of uncertain origin, but it is commonly believed to mean "mountain of strength" or "enlightened one".

Grammar:
In Greek, ααρων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It does not have any declensions or conjugations, as it is a proper noun.

Description:
In the Bible, Aaron was the older brother of Moses and the first high priest of the Israelites. He played a significant role in the Exodus from Egypt and the establishment of the Israelite priesthood. In Christianity, Aaron is considered a saint and is venerated in various traditions. The name Aaron is still commonly used today.
