Etymology: The word "πεσόντες" (pesontes) comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pipto), which means "to fall". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-.

Grammar: "Πεσόντες" is the plural form of the Greek noun "πεσών" (peson), which means "fallen ones". It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: "Πεσόντες" is used to refer to a group of people or things that have fallen or have been defeated. It can be used in a literal sense, such as fallen soldiers, or in a metaphorical sense, such as fallen empires or fallen heroes.
