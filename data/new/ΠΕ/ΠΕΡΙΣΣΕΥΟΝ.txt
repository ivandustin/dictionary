Etymology: The word "περισσεύων" comes from the Greek verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúō), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess."

Grammar: In Greek, "περισσεύων" is the present participle form of the verb "περισσεύω." It is used as an adjective to describe something that is abundant or in excess.

Description: "Περισσεύων" is an adjective that describes something that is surplus, abundant, or in excess. It is often used to indicate an excess or surplus of something, such as food, money, or resources.
