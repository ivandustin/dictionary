Etymology: The word "πεισάντες" (peisantes) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰeydʰ-.

Grammar: "πεισάντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "πείθω" (peitho) in Greek. It is used to indicate a completed action in the past. The participle form is commonly used to modify nouns or act as adjectives in Greek sentences.

Description: "πεισάντες" is a Greek word that translates to "having persuaded" or "having convinced" in English. It is often used in Greek literature and rhetoric to describe someone who has successfully convinced or persuaded others.
