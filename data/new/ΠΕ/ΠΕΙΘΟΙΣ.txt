Etymology: The word "πειθοις" (peithois) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." 

Grammar: "Πειθοις" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: "Πειθοις" refers to "persuasions" or "arguments." It is often used in the context of rhetoric or debate, where different arguments are presented to convince or persuade someone.
