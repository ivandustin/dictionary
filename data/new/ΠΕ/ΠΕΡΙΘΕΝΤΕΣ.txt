Etymology: The word "περιθέντες" comes from the Greek verb "περιτίθημι" (peritithemi), which means "to place around" or "to surround". The suffix "-ες" is added to form the plural form of the word.

Grammar: "περιθέντες" is a masculine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or for predicate nouns.

Description: "περιθέντες" refers to a group of people who are surrounding or encircling something or someone. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people who are protecting or guarding someone or something.
