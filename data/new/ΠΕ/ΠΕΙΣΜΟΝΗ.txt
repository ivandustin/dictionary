Etymology: The word "πεισμονή" (peismoni) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰeydʰ-.

Grammar: "Πεισμονή" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and has the genitive form "πεισμονής" (peismonis).

Description: "Πεισμονή" refers to the quality or state of being persuasive or convincing. It can also mean persistence or determination in persuading someone. It is often used to describe someone's strong conviction or determination to achieve a goal or convince others of their viewpoint.
