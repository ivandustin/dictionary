Etymology: The word "περιπατούμεν" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατώ" (pató), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατούμεν" is the present participle form of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató) in the first person plural. It is used to describe an ongoing action of walking around in the present tense.

Description: "περιπατούμεν" is a Greek word that describes the act of walking around. It can be used to refer to physically walking around in a specific location or to metaphorically explore or investigate something.
