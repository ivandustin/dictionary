Etymology: The word "πετομένοις" (petomenois) comes from the Greek verb "πετάομαι" (petáomai), which means "to fly". It is derived from the noun "πτέρυξ" (ptéryx), meaning "wing".

Grammar: "Πετομένοις" is the dative plural form of the adjective "πετόμενος" (petómenos), which means "flying" or "winged". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "Πετομένοις" is used to describe multiple objects or beings that are in the state of flying or having wings. It can be used to refer to birds, insects, or any other creatures that are capable of flight.
