Etymology: The word "πεπυρωμένης" comes from the Greek verb "πυρόω" (pyroo), which means "to set on fire" or "to burn". The prefix "πε-" (pe-) indicates a completed action, so "πεπυρωμένης" can be translated as "burned" or "set on fire".

Grammar: "πεπυρωμένης" is the feminine form of the adjective in the nominative singular. It agrees with a feminine noun in gender, number, and case. In Greek, adjectives change their form to match the noun they modify.

Description: "πεπυρωμένης" describes something that has been burned or set on fire. It can be used to describe a physical object that has been charred or a metaphorical situation that has been ruined or destroyed.
