Etymology: The word "πεπληρωμένος" (pepleromenos) comes from the Greek verb "πληρόω" (pleroo), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The prefix "πε-" (pe-) indicates a perfective aspect, indicating that the action of filling or completing has been fully accomplished.

Grammar: "Πεπληρωμένος" is the masculine form of the past participle of the verb "πληρόω" (pleroo). It is used to describe something or someone that has been filled or completed in the past. In Greek grammar, the past participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πεπληρωμένος" describes something or someone that has been filled or completed. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a task that has been finished, a container that has been filled, or a person who has achieved a state of fulfillment or completion.
