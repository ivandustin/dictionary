Etymology: The word "πεποιθέν" (pepoithén) comes from the Greek verb "πεποιθέω" (pepoithéo), which means "to trust" or "to have confidence in". 

Grammar: "Πεποιθέν" is the third person singular form of the verb "πεποιθέω" in the perfect tense. It is used to express an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. 

Description: "Πεποιθέν" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of trust or confidence. It implies a sense of assurance or belief in something or someone.
