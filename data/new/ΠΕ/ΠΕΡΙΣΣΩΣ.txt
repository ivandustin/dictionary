Etymology: The word "περισσώς" comes from the Greek word "περισσός" (perissós), which means "abundant" or "excessive".

Grammar: "Περισσώς" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe an action or state in an excessive or abundant manner.

Description: "Περισσώς" is used to emphasize the degree or intensity of an action or state. It indicates that something is done or exists to a great extent or in excess. It can be used to describe various aspects such as quantity, quality, or intensity.
