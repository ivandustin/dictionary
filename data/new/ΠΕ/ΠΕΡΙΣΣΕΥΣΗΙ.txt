Etymology: The word "περισσεύση" comes from the Greek verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúo), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess."

Grammar: "Περισσεύση" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third person singular of the aorist subjunctive mood in Greek.

Description: "Περισσεύση" refers to an abundance or surplus of something. It implies that there is more than what is needed or expected.
