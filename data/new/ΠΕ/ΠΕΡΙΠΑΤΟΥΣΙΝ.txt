Etymology: The word "περιπατούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατέω" (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατέω" (pateo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατούσιν" is the present participle form of the verb "περιπατέω" (peripateo) in the third person plural. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "περιπατούσιν" describes a group of people who are currently walking or strolling around. It indicates that the action of walking is happening in the present and that it involves multiple individuals.
