Etymology: The word "πεποιθότες" comes from the Greek verb "πεποιθέω" (pepoithéo), which means "to trust" or "to have confidence in". The suffix "-τες" (-tes) is added to form the plural noun.

Grammar: "Πεποιθότες" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is in the nominative case. The word is used to refer to a group of people who have confidence or trust in something.

Description: "Πεποιθότες" is a Greek word that describes individuals or a group of people who have a strong belief or trust in something. It implies a sense of confidence and assurance in their beliefs or convictions.
