Etymology: The word "πεπιστευκατε" comes from the Greek verb "πιστεύω" (pisteúo), which means "to believe" or "to trust". The verb is conjugated in the second person plural, perfect tense, indicative mood.

Grammar: "πεπιστευκατε" is the second person plural form of the verb "πιστεύω" in the perfect tense. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance to the present. The second person plural form indicates that the action is being performed by a group of people.

Description: "πεπιστευκατε" is a Greek verb form that translates to "you have believed" in English. It is used to express the belief or trust that a group of people has placed in something or someone.
