Etymology: The word "περγης" (pergēs) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb "πέργω" (pergō), which means "to accomplish" or "to achieve."

Grammar: In Greek, "περγης" is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "περγητος" (pergētos).

Description: "Περγης" refers to a person who is skillful, accomplished, or successful in a particular field or activity. It can be translated as "expert," "master," or "adept."
