Etymology: The word "πενθος" (penthos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pent-, meaning "to suffer" or "to mourn."

Grammar: In Greek, "πενθος" (penthos) is a noun. It is a neuter noun of the third declension. In the singular, it can be declined as follows: nominative and accusative case - πενθος, genitive case - πενθους, dative case - πενθει, and vocative case - πενθε.

Description: "Πενθος" (penthos) refers to deep sorrow, grief, or mourning. It is often used to describe intense emotional pain or sadness, particularly in response to the loss of a loved one or a tragic event.
