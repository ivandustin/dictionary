Etymology: The word "πειρασμός" comes from the Greek verb "πειράω" (peiráō), which means "to try" or "to test". It is derived from the noun "πεῖρα" (peîra), meaning "trial" or "attempt".

Grammar: In Greek, "πειρασμός" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "πειρασμοί" (peirasmói).

Description: "Πειρασμός" refers to the act of tempting or enticing someone to do something that is morally wrong or against their better judgment. It can also refer to a trial or test of one's faith or willpower. In a religious context, it is often associated with the concept of temptation by the devil or evil forces.
