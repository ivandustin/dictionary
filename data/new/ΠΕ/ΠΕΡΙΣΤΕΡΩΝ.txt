Etymology: The word "περιστερών" comes from the Greek word "περιστέρι" (peristeri), which means "pigeon" or "dove".

Grammar: "περιστερών" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "περιστερών" refers to pigeons or doves. These birds are known for their gentle nature and are often associated with peace and love. They have been domesticated for thousands of years and are found in various parts of the world. Pigeons are known for their ability to navigate and find their way back home over long distances.
