Etymology: The word "πειραζετε" (peirazete) comes from the Greek verb "πειράζω" (peirazo), which means "to test" or "to try". 

Grammar: "πειραζετε" is the present tense, second person plural form of the verb "πειράζω" (peirazo). It is used to address a group of people and means "you test" or "you try".

Description: "πειραζετε" is a verb in the Greek language that is used to express the action of testing or trying something. It can be used in various contexts, such as testing someone's patience, trying out a new idea, or experimenting with a new method.
