Etymology: The word "περιπατείς" (peripateis) comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripato), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb "πατώ" (pato), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατείς" is the second person singular form of the present tense of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripato). It is used to address someone directly and indicates that the subject is currently walking or strolling.

Description: "περιπατείς" is a Greek word that describes the action of walking or strolling around. It can be used to refer to physical movement or to metaphorical exploration or contemplation.
