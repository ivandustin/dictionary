Etymology: The word "περιρηξαντες" comes from the Greek verb "περιρήσσω" (periríssō), which means "to tear apart" or "to rip up". It is derived from the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around" or "through", and the verb "ρήσσω" (ríssō), meaning "to tear" or "to rend".

Grammar: "περιρηξαντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "περιρήσσω" (periríssō) in the masculine plural nominative case. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to modify nouns or function as independent verbs. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: "περιρηξαντες" describes a group of people who have torn something apart or ripped it up. It can be used to refer to individuals who have forcefully or violently torn or ripped something, such as a document, fabric, or object.
