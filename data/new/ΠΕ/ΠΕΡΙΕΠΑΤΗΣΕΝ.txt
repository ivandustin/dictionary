Etymology: The word "περιεπατησεν" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατέω" (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to wander". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατέω" (pateo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιεπατησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "περιπατέω" (peripateo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a singular third person (he/she/it).

Description: "περιεπατησεν" describes an action of walking around or wandering that was completed in the past. It can be used to refer to someone walking aimlessly or exploring their surroundings.
