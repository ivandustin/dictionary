Etymology: The word "πεποίθα" (pepoitha) comes from the Greek verb "πεποιθέω" (pepoitheo), which means "to trust" or "to have confidence in". 

Grammar: "Πεποίθα" is the first person singular form of the verb "πεποιθέω" in the perfect tense. It is used to express that the speaker has trusted or had confidence in something in the past.

Description: "Πεποίθα" is a Greek word that conveys the idea of trust or confidence. It implies that the speaker has placed trust in someone or something and has a sense of assurance or belief in its reliability.
