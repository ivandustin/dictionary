Etymology: The word "περιπατησούσιν" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατέω" (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατέω" (pateo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατησούσιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "περιπατέω" in the future tense. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The suffix "-σούσιν" indicates the future tense and the third person plural.

Description: "περιπατησούσιν" is a Greek verb form that means "they will walk around" or "they will stroll". It refers to a future action of multiple individuals walking or strolling around.
