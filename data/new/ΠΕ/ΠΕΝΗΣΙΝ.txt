Etymology: The word "πενησιν" (penēsin) comes from the Greek word "πένης" (penēs), meaning "poor" or "needy". 

Grammar: "Πενησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and masculine gender. 

Description: "Πενησιν" refers to a poor or needy person. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who lacks resources or is in a disadvantaged position.
