Etymology: The word "πειθομεθα" comes from the Greek verb "πείθομαι" (peithomai), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." 

Grammar: "πειθομεθα" is the present tense, middle voice form of the verb "πείθομαι" (peithomai). In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. Therefore, "πειθομεθα" can be translated as "we persuade ourselves" or "we convince ourselves."

Description: "πειθομεθα" is a verb form that expresses the action of persuading or convincing oneself. It implies that the subject is actively involved in the process of persuasion or conviction.
