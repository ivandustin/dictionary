Etymology: The word "περισσεύητε" comes from the Greek verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúo), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess." The suffix "-τε" (-te) is added to the verb to form the imperative mood, making it a command or request.

Grammar: "Περισσεύητε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúo) in Modern Greek. It is used to give a command or request to a group of people, telling them to abound or have an excess of something.

Description: The word "περισσεύητε" is used in Greek to instruct or encourage a group of people to have an abundance or surplus of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as telling a group to have plenty of food, resources, or positive qualities.
