Etymology: The word "πεπεισμένος" comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peítho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The suffix "-μένος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates a passive voice and a state of being. Therefore, "πεπεισμένος" can be translated as "convinced" or "persuaded".

Grammar: "Πεπεισμένος" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Πεπεισμένος" describes someone who has been convinced or persuaded about something. It implies that the person has a strong belief or conviction based on reasoning or evidence.
