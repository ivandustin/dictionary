Etymology: The word "πεπονθασιν" comes from the Greek verb "πάσχω" (paskho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." It is in the perfect tense form, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "πεπονθασιν" is a verb in the perfect tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their form based on tense, voice, mood, and person.

Description: "πεπονθασιν" is used to express that the speaker has suffered or experienced something in the past. It implies that the action has been completed but may still have ongoing effects.
