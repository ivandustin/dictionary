Etymology: The word "πειθείς" (peitheis) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince."

Grammar: "Πειθείς" is the plural form of the adjective "πειθής" (peithis), which means "persuasive" or "convincing." It is used to describe people or things that have the ability to persuade or convince others.

Description: "Πειθείς" is an adjective that is often used to describe individuals who have a persuasive or convincing manner. It can also be used to describe arguments, evidence, or reasoning that is compelling and convincing.
