Etymology: The word "πεπλανημένοις" comes from the Greek verb "πλανάω" (planáo), which means "to wander" or "to roam". The prefix "πε-" (pe-) indicates completion or intensification, so "πεπλανημένοις" can be translated as "those who have wandered" or "those who have roamed".

Grammar: "Πεπλανημένοις" is the dative plural form of the adjective "πεπλανημένος" (peplaniménos), which agrees with a noun in the dative plural case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: "Πεπλανημένοις" describes a group of people who have wandered or roamed. It implies a sense of aimlessness or lack of direction.
