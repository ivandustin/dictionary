Etymology: The word "πεποιηκότες" comes from the Greek verb "ποιέω" (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do." The word is formed by adding the prefix "πε-" (pe-) to the verb stem "ποιη-" (poiē-), which indicates completion or perfect tense.

Grammar: "Πεποιηκότες" is the plural form of the verb "πεποιηκώς" (pepoiēkōs), which is the perfect participle of "ποιέω" (poiéo). In Greek grammar, the perfect participle is used to express completed actions or states. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πεποιηκότες" can be translated as "those who have made" or "those who have done." It refers to a group of people who have completed an action or achieved a certain state. The specific meaning of the word depends on the context in which it is used.
