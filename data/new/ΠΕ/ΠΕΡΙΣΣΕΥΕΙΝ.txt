Etymology: The word "περισσεύειν" comes from the Greek verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúō), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess."

Grammar: "περισσεύειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. It belongs to the first conjugation class in Greek verbs.

Description: "περισσεύειν" is a Greek verb that means "to abound," "to be in excess," or "to be surplus." It is often used to describe a situation where there is more than enough of something.
