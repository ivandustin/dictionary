Etymology: The word "πεπεισμαι" (pepeismai) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The prefix "πεπ-" indicates the perfect tense, indicating a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present. The suffix "-μαι" indicates the reflexive form, meaning "I am persuaded" or "I have been convinced".

Grammar: "πεπεισμαι" is a first-person singular verb form in the perfect tense and the middle voice. It is used to express that the subject (in this case, "I") has been persuaded or convinced by someone or something.

Description: "πεπεισμαι" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of being persuaded or convinced. It implies that the subject has come to a certain belief or opinion as a result of being convinced by someone or something.
