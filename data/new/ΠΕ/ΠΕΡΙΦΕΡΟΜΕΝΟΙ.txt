Etymology: The word "περιφερομενοι" comes from the Greek verb "περιφέρω" (perifero), which means "to carry around" or "to bear." The suffix "-μενοι" (-menoi) is a participle ending that indicates the present passive voice.

Grammar: "Περιφερόμενοι" is the present passive participle form of the verb "περιφέρω" (perifero). It is used to describe a subject that is being carried around or borne by something or someone else. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as independent verbs.

Description: "Περιφερόμενοι" describes a subject that is in the process of being carried around or borne by something or someone else. It implies a passive action, indicating that the subject is not actively carrying itself but is being carried by an external force.
