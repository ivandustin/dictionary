Etymology: The word "πεντακοσιοι" (pentakosioi) comes from the Greek words "πεντα-" (penta-), meaning "five," and "κοσιοι" (kosioi), meaning "hundred." It is a compound word that literally translates to "five hundred."

Grammar: In Greek, "πεντακοσιοι" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It agrees with a noun in gender, number, and case. For example, "πεντακόσιοι άνδρες" (pentakosioi andres) means "five hundred men."

Description: "πεντακοσιοι" is a numerical adjective used to express the quantity of five hundred. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as counting, measurements, or describing a large number of something.
