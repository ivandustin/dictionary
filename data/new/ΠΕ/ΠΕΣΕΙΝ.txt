Etymology: The word "πεσείν" (pesin) comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pipto), which means "to fall". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-.

Grammar: "Πεσείν" is the infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated according to tense, mood, voice, and person. The verb "πεσείν" belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the aorist tense.

Description: "Πεσείν" means "to fall" in Greek. It is a common verb used to describe the action of descending or dropping from a higher position to a lower one. It can be used both literally and metaphorically.
