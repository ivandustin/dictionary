Etymology: The word "πενθείν" (penthéin) comes from the Greek verb "πένθος" (pénthos), which means "grief" or "sorrow". 

Grammar: "Πενθείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to indicate different aspects of mourning or grieving.

Description: "Πενθείν" refers to the act of mourning or grieving, expressing deep sorrow or sadness over a loss or tragedy. It can be used to describe the emotional response to the death of a loved one, the loss of a relationship, or any other significant loss or hardship.
