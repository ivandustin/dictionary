Etymology: The word "πειραζομαι" (peirazomai) comes from the Greek verb "πειράζω" (peirazo), which means "to test" or "to try". It is derived from the noun "πείρα" (peira), meaning "trial" or "attempt".

Grammar: "Πειράζομαι" is a reflexive verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In English, it can be translated as "I am tempted" or "I am being tested".

Description: "Πειράζομαι" is used to express the state of being tempted or tested. It can refer to both external temptations or internal struggles. It is often used in a moral or spiritual context, indicating the inner conflict between doing what is right and giving in to temptation.
