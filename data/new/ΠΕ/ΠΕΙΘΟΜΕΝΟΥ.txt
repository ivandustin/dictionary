Etymology: The word "πειθομένου" (peithomenou) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The suffix "-μένου" (-menou) is a present participle form, indicating the action of being persuaded or convinced.

Grammar: "Πειθόμενου" (peithomenou) is the present participle form of the verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "being persuaded" or "being convinced". It is used to describe an ongoing action or state of being.

Description: "Πειθόμενου" (peithomenou) is a Greek word that describes the act of being persuaded or convinced. It implies that someone is being influenced or swayed by someone else's arguments, reasoning, or evidence.
