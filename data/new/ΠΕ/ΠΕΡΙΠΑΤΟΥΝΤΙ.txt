Etymology: The word "περιπατούντι" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατώ" (pató), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατούντι" is the present participle form of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató) in the third person plural. It is used to describe an ongoing action of walking or strolling that is currently being performed by a group of people.

Description: "περιπατούντι" is a Greek word that specifically refers to a group of people walking or strolling around. It emphasizes the collective nature of the action and implies that the individuals in the group are engaged in a leisurely or casual walk.
