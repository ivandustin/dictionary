Etymology: The word "πειθομενοις" comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The suffix "-μενοις" (-menois) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to those who are currently being persuaded or convinced.

Grammar: "πειθομενοις" is the dative plural form of the present participle of the verb "πείθω" (peitho). In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the recipient of an action. The plural form indicates that the word refers to multiple individuals.

Description: "πειθομενοις" is a Greek word that can be translated as "to those who are being persuaded" or "to those who are being convinced". It is often used in a rhetorical or persuasive context to refer to the audience or target group that someone is trying to persuade or convince.
