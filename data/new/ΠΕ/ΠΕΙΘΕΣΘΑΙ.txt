Etymology: The word "πειθεσθαι" (peithesthai) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince".

Grammar: "πειθεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "πείθω" (peitho). In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is being persuaded or convinced by someone or something else.

Description: "πειθεσθαι" is a Greek verb that means "to be persuaded" or "to be convinced". It implies that the subject is influenced or convinced by someone or something else. This verb is commonly used in philosophical and persuasive contexts.
