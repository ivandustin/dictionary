Etymology: The word "περιτρέπει" comes from the Greek verb "περιτρέπω" (peritrepó), which means "to turn around" or "to revolve". It is derived from the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb "τρέπω" (trepó), meaning "to turn".

Grammar: "περιτρέπει" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "περιτρέπω" (peritrepó). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "περιτρέπει" is a Greek word that can be translated as "he/she/it turns around" or "he/she/it revolves". It describes the action of something or someone rotating or moving in a circular motion.
