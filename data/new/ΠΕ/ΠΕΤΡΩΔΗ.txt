Etymology: The word "πετρωδης" (petrodes) comes from the Greek words "πέτρα" (petra) meaning "rock" and "εἶδος" (eidos) meaning "form" or "shape". It refers to something that has the characteristics or appearance of a rock.

Grammar: In Greek, "πετρωδης" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined to match the noun it describes.

Description: "Πετρωδης" is used to describe something that is rocky or has the qualities of a rock. It can refer to the physical appearance of something, such as a rocky landscape or terrain. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is solid, unyielding, or unchanging.
