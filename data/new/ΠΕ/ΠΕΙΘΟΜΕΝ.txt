Etymology: The word "πειθομεν" (peithomen) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince."

Grammar: "πειθομεν" is the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice form of the verb "πείθω" (peitho) in the first person plural. It is used to express that "we persuade" or "we convince."

Description: "πειθομεν" is a Greek word that emphasizes the act of persuading or convincing. It implies the ability to influence someone's beliefs, opinions, or actions through reasoning or argumentation.
