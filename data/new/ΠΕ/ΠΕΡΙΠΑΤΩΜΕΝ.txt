Etymology: The word "περιπατώμεν" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατάω" (patáo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατώμεν" is the present tense, indicative mood, first person plural form of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The form "περιπατώμεν" indicates that the subject is "we" and the action is happening in the present.

Description: "περιπατώμεν" is a Greek verb form that translates to "we walk" or "we are walking" in English. It is used to express the action of walking or strolling in the present tense, indicating that the subject (we) is currently engaged in this activity.
