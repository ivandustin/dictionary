Grammar: "Πεμψάντα" is the past participle of the verb "πέμπω" (pempo) in the masculine singular accusative case. In Greek grammar, the past participle is used to form compound tenses and passive voice constructions.

Description: "Πεμψάντα" refers to a singular masculine noun or pronoun that has been sent or dispatched. It can be used to describe someone or something that has been sent to a specific destination or on a specific mission.
