Etymology: The word "περισσεύσαι" comes from the Greek verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúo), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess."

Grammar: "περισσεύσαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action.

Description: "περισσεύσαι" is used to express the action of abounding or being in excess. It can be translated as "to abound" or "to be in surplus." It is often used in contexts where there is an excess or surplus of something, such as resources, money, or time.
