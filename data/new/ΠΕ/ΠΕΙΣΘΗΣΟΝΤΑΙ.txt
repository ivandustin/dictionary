Etymology: The word "πεισθησονται" comes from the Greek verb "πείθομαι" (peithomai), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". It is conjugated in the future tense, third person plural form.

Grammar: "πεισθησονται" is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description: "πεισθησονται" is a Greek verb that means "they will be persuaded" or "they will be convinced". It implies that a group of people or things will be convinced or persuaded to do something in the future.
