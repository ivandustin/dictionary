Etymology: The word "περιθείς" (peritheis) comes from the Greek verb "περιτίθημι" (peritithemi), which means "to place around" or "to surround". It is derived from the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), meaning "to place".

Grammar: "Περιθείς" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of the noun.

Description: "Περιθείς" refers to "surroundings" or "environment" in Greek. It is used to describe the things or conditions that are present around a particular place or object. It can also refer to the circumstances or context in which something occurs.
