Etymology: The word "περιεπατησαμεν" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατέω" (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατέω" (pateo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιεπατησαμεν" is the first person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood form of the verb "περιπατέω" (peripateo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, and person. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood is used to state a fact or make a statement.

Description: "περιεπατησαμεν" describes the action of walking around or strolling in the past. It indicates that the action of walking took place and was completed by a group of people.
