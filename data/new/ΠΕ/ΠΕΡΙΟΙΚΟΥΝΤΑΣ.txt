Etymology: The word "περιοικούντας" comes from the Greek verb "περιοικέω" (perioikeo), which means "to dwell around" or "to live in the vicinity of." The suffix "-ας" (-as) is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating the action of dwelling or living around something.

Grammar: "περιοικούντας" is the present participle form of the verb "περιοικέω" (perioikeo) in Greek. It is used to describe an ongoing action of dwelling or living around something in the present tense. In Greek grammar, the present participle can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "περιοικούντας" describes the act of dwelling or living around something. It can refer to people or things that are located in the vicinity of a particular place or object. The word implies a close proximity or association with the subject being described.
