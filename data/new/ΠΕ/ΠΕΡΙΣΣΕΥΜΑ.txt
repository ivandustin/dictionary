Etymology: The word "περισσευμα" comes from the Greek word "περισσεύω" (perisseúo), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess".

Grammar: In Greek, "περισσευμα" is a noun. It is a neuter noun of the first declension, and its plural form is "περισσεύματα" (perisseúmata).

Description: "Περισσευμα" refers to something that is in excess or surplus. It can be used to describe an abundance of something, such as food, money, or resources. It can also be used metaphorically to describe an excess of emotions or thoughts.
