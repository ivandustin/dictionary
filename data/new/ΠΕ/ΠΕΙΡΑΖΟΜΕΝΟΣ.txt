Etymology: The word "πειραζομενος" comes from the Greek verb "πειράζω" (peirazo), which means "to test" or "to try". The suffix "-μενος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice, so "πειραζομενος" can be translated as "being tested" or "being tried".

Grammar: "πειραζομενος" is a present passive participle in the masculine singular form. In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives. The present tense indicates that the action is happening in the present, and the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action.

Description: "πειραζομενος" describes someone or something that is currently being tested or tried. It implies that the subject is undergoing a process of examination or evaluation.
