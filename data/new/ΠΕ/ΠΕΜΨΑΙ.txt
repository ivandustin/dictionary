Etymology: The word "πεμψαί" (pempsai) comes from the Greek verb "πέμπω" (pempo), which means "to send". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pempe, meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Πεμψαί" is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb "πέμπω" (pempo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Πεμψαί" is a verb form that indicates that a group of people (third person plural) sent or were sent. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of individuals sending a message, a package, or any other object.
