Etymology: The word "πεμψαντος" (pempsantos) comes from the Greek verb "πέμπω" (pempo), which means "to send". The suffix "-αντος" (-antos) is a past participle ending, indicating that the action of sending has already taken place.

Grammar: "Πεμψαντος" is the genitive form of the word, which is used to indicate possession or origin. It is commonly used in Greek grammar to show the relationship between nouns.

Description: "Πεμψαντος" is a Greek word that means "of the one who sent" or "belonging to the one who sent". It is often used in Greek literature and texts to refer to someone who has sent a message, a letter, or a gift.
