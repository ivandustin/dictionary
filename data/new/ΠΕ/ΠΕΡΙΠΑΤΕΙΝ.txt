Etymology: The word "περιπατείν" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb "πατώ" (pató), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατείν" is the present tense, third person singular form of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening in the present time.

Description: "περιπατείν" is a verb in Greek that means "he/she/it walks around" or "he/she/it strolls". It describes the action of someone walking or strolling around in a leisurely manner.
