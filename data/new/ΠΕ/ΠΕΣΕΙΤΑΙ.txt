Etymology: The word "πεσείται" comes from the Greek verb "πίπτω" (pípto), which means "to fall". It is conjugated in the third person singular, present tense, passive voice.

Grammar: "Πεσείται" is a verb form in Greek. It is in the present tense, indicating an action that is happening now. It is in the passive voice, suggesting that the subject of the verb is receiving the action rather than performing it.

Description: "Πεσείται" is used to describe an action of falling that is happening to a third person singular subject. It can be used to talk about physical falls or metaphorical falls, depending on the context.
