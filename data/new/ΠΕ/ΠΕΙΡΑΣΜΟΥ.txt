Etymology: The word "πειρασμός" (peirasmós) comes from the Greek verb "πειράω" (peiráō), which means "to try" or "to test". It is derived from the noun "πεῖρα" (peîra), meaning "trial" or "attempt".

Grammar: In Greek, "πειρασμός" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "πειρασμοί" (peirasmói).

Description: "Πειρασμός" refers to the act of tempting or testing someone's willpower or moral character. It can also mean a trial or a challenge that one faces, often in a moral or spiritual sense. In religious contexts, it is often used to describe the temptation of sin or the testing of faith.
