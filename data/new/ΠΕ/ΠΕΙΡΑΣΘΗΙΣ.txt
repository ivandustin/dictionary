Etymology: The word "πειρασθηις" (peirastheis) comes from the Greek verb "πειράομαι" (peiráomai), which means "to try" or "to attempt". It is derived from the noun "πείρα" (peíra), meaning "trial" or "attempt".

Grammar: "πειρασθηις" is a verb form in the aorist passive tense, second person singular, middle voice, indicative mood. It is used to express an action that was done to you (second person) in the past (aorist tense) and in which you were the recipient or target of the action (passive voice).

Description: "πειρασθηις" is a word in the Greek language that means "you were tried" or "you were tested". It implies that someone or something subjected you to a trial or test.
