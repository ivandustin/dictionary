Etymology: The word "περιεπατεί" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb "πατώ" (pató), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιεπατεί" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "περιεπατεί" describes the action of someone walking around or strolling. It can be used to express the physical act of walking or to convey a metaphorical sense of exploring or wandering.
