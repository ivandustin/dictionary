Etymology: The word "πεισας" (peisas) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". 

Grammar: "Πείσας" is the aorist active participle of the verb "πείθω" (peitho). It is used to describe someone who has persuaded or convinced someone else.

Description: "Πείσας" is a Greek word that refers to someone who has successfully persuaded or convinced someone else. It is often used to describe someone who has used their powers of persuasion to change someone's opinion or behavior.
