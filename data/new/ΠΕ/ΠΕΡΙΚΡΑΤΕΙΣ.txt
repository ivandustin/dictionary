Etymology: The word "περικρατείς" comes from the Greek word "περικρατέω" (perikrateo), which means "to have power over" or "to prevail over". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around" or "over", and the verb "κρατέω" (krateo), meaning "to have power" or "to prevail".

Grammar: "περικρατείς" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple entities or individuals being referred to.

Description: "περικρατείς" refers to individuals or entities that have power or control over something. It can be used to describe people who have authority, influence, or dominance in a particular area or situation.
