Etymology: The word "πεμψάντες" (pempsantes) comes from the Greek verb "πέμπω" (pempo), which means "to send". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pempe, meaning "to send".

Grammar: "Πεμψάντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "πέμπω" (pempo) in Greek. It is used to indicate an action that has been completed in the past. In this form, it agrees with the subject in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Πεμψάντες" is a Greek word that means "having sent" or "after sending". It is often used in Greek literature and texts to describe a group of people who have sent or dispatched someone or something.
