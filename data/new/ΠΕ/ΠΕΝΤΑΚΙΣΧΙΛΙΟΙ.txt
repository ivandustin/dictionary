Etymology: The word "πεντακισχίλιοι" (pentakischílioi) comes from the Greek words "πέντε" (pénte) meaning "five" and "χίλιοι" (chílioi) meaning "thousand". It is a compound word that literally translates to "five thousand".

Grammar: "Πεντακισχίλιοι" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. It can be used to describe a plural noun, indicating that there are five thousand of something.

Description: "Πεντακισχίλιοι" is a numerical adjective used to express the quantity of five thousand. It is commonly used in Greek to indicate a large number or a significant amount of something.
