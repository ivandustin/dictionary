Etymology: The word "πειθων" (peithon) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". 

Grammar: "Πειθων" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. 

Description: "Πειθων" refers to a person who persuades or convinces others. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is influential or persuasive in their actions or words.
