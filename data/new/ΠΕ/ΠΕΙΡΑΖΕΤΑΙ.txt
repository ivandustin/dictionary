Etymology: The word "πειραζεται" (peirazetai) comes from the Greek verb "πειράζω" (peirazo), which means "to test" or "to try". 

Grammar: "πειραζεται" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "πειράζω" (peirazo). It is used to describe an action or state that is happening in the present and is being performed by a third person.

Description: "πειραζεται" is a Greek word that can be translated as "he/she/it is being tested" or "he/she/it is being tried". It implies that someone or something is undergoing a test or trial, either in a literal or figurative sense.
