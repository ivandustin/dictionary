Etymology: The word "περιεργαζόμενος" comes from the Greek verb "περιεργάζομαι" (periergázomai), which means "to be curious" or "to be inquisitive". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around" or "about", and the verb "εργάζομαι" (ergázomai), meaning "to work" or "to do".

Grammar: "Περιεργαζόμενος" is the masculine form of the present participle in Greek. It is used to describe someone who is currently engaged in the act of being curious or inquisitive. In Greek, participles can function as adjectives, and they agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description: "Περιεργαζόμενος" describes someone who is actively showing curiosity or interest in something. It implies a sense of being engaged, observant, and eager to learn or discover.
