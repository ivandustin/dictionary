Etymology: The word "πεπυρωμένα" comes from the Greek verb "πεπυρόω" (pepyróo), which means "to set on fire" or "to burn". It is derived from the noun "πῦρ" (pyr), meaning "fire".

Grammar: "πεπυρωμένα" is the plural form of the adjective "πεπυρωμένος" (pepyrómenos), which means "burned" or "charred". It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "πεπυρωμένα" is used to describe objects or substances that have been burned or charred. It indicates that something has undergone the process of being set on fire and has been left with a blackened or charred appearance.
