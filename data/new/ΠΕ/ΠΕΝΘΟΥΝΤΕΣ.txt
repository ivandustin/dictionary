Etymology: The word "πενθούντες" comes from the Greek verb "πενθέω" (penthéo), which means "to mourn" or "to grieve."

Grammar: "Πενθούντες" is the plural form of the verb "πενθέω" in the present active participle. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently mourning or grieving.

Description: "Πενθούντες" refers to individuals who are in a state of mourning or grief. It can be used to describe a group of people who are collectively experiencing sadness and sorrow due to the loss of a loved one or a tragic event.
