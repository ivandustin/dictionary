Etymology: The word "πεμφθεντες" (pemphthentes) comes from the Greek verb "πεμπω" (pempo), which means "to send." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pem(p)-, meaning "to send" or "to drive."

Grammar: "Πεμφθεντες" is the plural form of the verb "πεμπω" (pempo) in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It is used to describe a group of people or things that have been sent or dispatched.

Description: "Πεμφθεντες" is a Greek word that can be translated as "those who have been sent" or "the ones who were sent." It is often used in a narrative context to refer to messengers, envoys, or individuals who have been sent on a mission or task.
