Etymology: The word "περισσευματος" comes from the Greek word "περισσεύω" (perisseúo), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess".

Grammar: "Περισσευματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Περισσευματος" refers to something that is in excess or surplus. It can be used to describe an abundance of something or the leftover or extra amount of something.
