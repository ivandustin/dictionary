Etymology: The word "πεπεισμέθα" comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peítho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The suffix "-σμεθα" indicates the passive voice, so "πεπεισμέθα" can be translated as "we have been persuaded" or "we have been convinced".

Grammar: "πεπεισμέθα" is the first person plural form of the verb "πείθω" in the perfect tense. The perfect tense indicates an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "πεπεισμέθα" is a word used in Greek to express the idea of being persuaded or convinced. It implies that someone or something has influenced or convinced the subject of the verb.
