Etymology: The word "περιπατεί" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb "πατώ" (pató), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατεί" is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató). In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the person, number, tense, and mood. The present tense indicates an action that is happening at the current moment.

Description: "περιπατεί" is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it walks" or "he/she/it is walking". It is used to describe the action of someone moving on foot, taking steps, or strolling around.
