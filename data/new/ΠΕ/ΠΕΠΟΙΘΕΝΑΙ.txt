Etymology: The word "πεποιθεναι" comes from the Greek verb "πεποιθέναι" (pepoithénai), which means "to trust" or "to have confidence in". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "πε-" (pe-), indicating completion or intensity, and the verb "ποιθέναι" (poithénai), meaning "to trust".

In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to express completed actions or states that have a continuing effect in the present. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action, and the indicative mood is used to state a fact or make a statement.

Description: "Πεποιθεναι" is often translated as "to trust", "to have confidence in", or "to rely on". It conveys a sense of firm belief or assurance in someone or something. It can be used to express trust in a person's abilities, trust in the outcome of a situation, or trust in one's own judgment.
