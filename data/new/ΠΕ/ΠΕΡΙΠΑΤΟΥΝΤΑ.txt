Etymology: The word "περιπατούντα" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατώ" (pató), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατούντα" is the present participle form of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató) in the masculine gender, accusative case, and singular number. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. The accusative case indicates the direct object of the verb.

Description: "περιπατούντα" refers to a singular masculine noun or pronoun that is currently walking or strolling around. It can be used to describe a person or an object in motion.
