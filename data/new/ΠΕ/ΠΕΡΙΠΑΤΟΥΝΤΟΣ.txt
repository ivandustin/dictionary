Etymology: The word "περιπατούντος" comes from the Greek verb "περιπατώ" (peripató), which means "to walk around". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "περί" (peri), meaning "around", and the verb "πατέω" (patéo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: "περιπατούντος" is the genitive form of the present participle of the verb "περιπατώ" (peripató). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. The present participle is a verb form that expresses ongoing action.

Description: "περιπατούντος" can be translated as "of walking around" or "while walking around". It is used to describe someone or something that is in the act of walking around or moving about.
