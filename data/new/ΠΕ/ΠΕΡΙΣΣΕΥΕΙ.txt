Etymology: The word "περισσεύει" comes from the Greek verb "περισσεύω" (perisseúo), which means "to abound" or "to be in excess." It is derived from the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around" or "excessive," and the verb "εὑρίσκω" (heurísko), meaning "to find" or "to discover."

Grammar: "περισσεύει" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It belongs to the active voice and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, it is classified as a regular verb of the first conjugation.

Description: The word "περισσεύει" is used to express the action of something being in excess or surplus. It indicates that there is more of something than is needed or expected. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing an abundance of resources, an overflow of emotions, or an excess of information.
