Etymology: The word "πειθαρχείν" comes from the Greek roots "πείθω" (peitho), meaning "to persuade" or "to convince," and "ἀρχή" (arche), meaning "rule" or "authority." 

Grammar: "πειθαρχείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "πειθαρχείν" means "to obey" or "to be disciplined" in Greek. It refers to the act of following rules, orders, or instructions in a disciplined manner.
