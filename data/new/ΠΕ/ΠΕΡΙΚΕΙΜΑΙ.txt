Etymology: The word "περικείμαι" comes from the Greek verb "περικείμαι" (perikeimai), which means "to surround" or "to encircle". It is derived from the prefix "περι-" (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb "κείμαι" (keimai), meaning "to lie" or "to be situated".

Grammar: "περικείμαι" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, indicative mood, and first person singular form. It is conjugated as follows:

- Present: περικαίομαι (perikaíomai) - I surround, I encircle
- Future: θα περικαλύπτομαι (tha perikalýptomai) - I will surround, I will encircle
- Past: περικαλύφθηκα (perikalýfthika) - I surrounded, I encircled

Description: "περικείμαι" is a Greek verb that describes the action of surrounding or encircling something. It can be used in a literal sense, referring to physically enclosing or encompassing an object or area. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of surrounding or enveloping something with thoughts, emotions, or ideas.
