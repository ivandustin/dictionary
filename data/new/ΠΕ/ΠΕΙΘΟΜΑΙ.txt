Etymology: The word "πειθομαι" (peithomai) comes from the Greek verb "πείθω" (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." 

Grammar: "Πειθομαι" is a verb in the present tense, first person singular form. It belongs to the middle voice, indicating that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. 

Description: "Πειθομαι" is used to express the act of being persuaded or convinced. It implies a change in one's belief or opinion due to the influence of someone or something else.
