Etymology: The word "περισσότερον" comes from the Greek word "περισσός" (perissós), meaning "excessive" or "abundant".

Grammar: "Περισσότερον" is the neuter form of the adjective "περισσός" (perissós), which means "more" or "additional". It is used to compare two or more things, indicating that one thing has a greater quantity or degree than another.

Description: "Περισσότερον" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used to express comparative degrees, indicating that something is more or less than something else. It can also be used to emphasize excess or abundance.
