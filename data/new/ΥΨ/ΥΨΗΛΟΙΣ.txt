Etymology: The word "υψηλοίς" (ypsilois) comes from the Greek adjective "υψηλός" (ypsilos), which means "high" or "tall".

Grammar: "Υψηλοίς" is the plural form of the adjective "υψηλός" (ypsilos) in the dative case. It is used to describe or modify nouns in the dative case, indicating that something is high or tall in relation to something else.

Description: "Υψηλοίς" is an adjective that is used to describe objects or people that are high or tall. It can be used to describe physical height, such as tall buildings or mountains, or metaphorical height, such as high ideals or aspirations.
