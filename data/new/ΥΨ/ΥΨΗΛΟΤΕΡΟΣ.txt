Etymology: The word "υψηλότερος" (ypsiloteros) comes from the Greek root "υψηλός" (ypsilos), meaning "high" or "tall," and the comparative suffix "-τερος" (-teros), which indicates a higher degree or comparison.

Grammar: "Υψηλότερος" is the comparative form of the adjective "υψηλός" (ypsilos), meaning "higher" or "taller." It is used to compare two or more things, indicating that one is higher or taller than the other(s).

Description: "Υψηλότερος" is an adjective in the Greek language that describes something as being higher or taller in comparison to something else. It is commonly used to describe physical height or elevation, but can also be used metaphorically to describe a higher level or degree of something.
