Etymology: The word "γημας" (gēmas) comes from the Greek verb "γαμέω" (gaméō), which means "to marry" or "to wed."

Grammar: "Γημας" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Γημας" refers to a man who is married or has been married. It specifically denotes the husband in a marriage.
