Etymology: The word "ναυκληρωί" comes from the Greek words "ναύς" (naus), meaning "ship," and "κληρονόμος" (klironomos), meaning "heir." It refers to the practice of leasing or chartering a ship.

Grammar: "Ναυκληρωί" is a noun in the singular form.

Description: "Ναυκληρωί" is a term used in maritime law and commerce to describe the act of leasing or chartering a ship. It involves the temporary transfer of a ship from its owner to another party, who pays a fee for its use. This practice has been common throughout history and is still used today in the shipping industry.
