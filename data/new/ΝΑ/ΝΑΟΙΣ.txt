Etymology: The word "ναοίς" (naois) comes from the Greek word "ναός" (naos), which means "temple" or "sacred place". 

Grammar: "Ναοίς" is the plural form of the noun "ναός" (naos) in the dative case. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Ναοίς" refers to multiple temples or sacred places. In ancient Greece, temples were important religious structures dedicated to various gods and goddesses. They were used for worship, rituals, and offerings. The temples were often adorned with intricate architectural designs and sculptures.
