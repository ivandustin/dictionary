Etymology: The word "ναος" (naos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nes-, meaning "to dwell" or "to be at home."

Grammar: In Greek, "ναος" (naos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The plural form is "ναοι" (naoi).

Description: "Ναος" (naos) refers to a temple or a sacred place in ancient Greek religion. It specifically denotes the innermost part of a temple, where the cult statue of a deity was housed. The naos was considered the most sacred area of the temple and was accessible only to priests and priestesses.
