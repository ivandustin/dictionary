Etymology: The word "ταραχή" comes from the Greek verb "ταράσσω" (tarássō), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *terə-, meaning "to shake" or "to tremble".

Grammar: In Greek, "ταραχή" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "ταραχές". It can be used in both the singular and plural forms depending on the context.

Description: "Ταραχή" refers to a state of disturbance, agitation, or turmoil. It can be used to describe a chaotic situation, a state of unrest, or a disruption of peace and order. It can also refer to a state of emotional or mental turmoil.
