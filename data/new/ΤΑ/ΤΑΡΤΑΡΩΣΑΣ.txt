Etymology: The word "ταρταρώσας" comes from the Greek verb "ταρταρόω" (tartaróo), which means "to throw into Tartarus." Tartarus is a deep abyss in Greek mythology, serving as a prison for the Titans.

Grammar: "Ταρταρώσας" is the present participle form of the verb "ταρταρόω" in the active voice. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently in the act of throwing into Tartarus.

Description: "Ταρταρώσας" is a Greek word that refers to the action of throwing someone or something into Tartarus. It is often used in a metaphorical sense to describe severe punishment or condemnation.
