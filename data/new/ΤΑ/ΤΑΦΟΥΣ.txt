Etymology: The word "ταφος" (tafos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "τάφος" (taphos), meaning "grave" or "tomb".

Grammar: In Greek, "ταφος" (tafos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (τάφου), dative (τάφῳ), accusative (τάφον), and vocative (τάφε).

Description: "Ταφος" (tafos) refers to a grave or a tomb, which is a place where a deceased person is buried. It can also be used metaphorically to represent death or the final resting place of someone.
