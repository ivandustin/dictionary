Etymology: The word "ταρασσοντες" comes from the Greek verb "ταράσσω" (tarássō), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". 

Grammar: "ταρασσοντες" is the present participle form of the verb "ταράσσω" in the active voice. It is used to describe a continuous action of disturbing or agitating.

Description: "ταρασσοντες" refers to a group of people or things that are currently disturbing or agitating. It can be used to describe individuals who are causing unrest or turmoil, or objects that are causing disruption or chaos.
