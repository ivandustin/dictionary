Etymology: The word "ταπεινούς" (tapinoús) comes from the Greek adjective "ταπεινός" (tapeinós), which means "humble" or "lowly". It is derived from the verb "ταπεινόω" (tapeinóo), meaning "to humble" or "to lower".

Grammar: "Ταπεινούς" is the masculine form of the adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The feminine form is "ταπεινή" (tapeiní) and the neuter form is "ταπεινό" (tapeinó).

Description: "Ταπεινούς" is an adjective that describes someone or something as humble, modest, or lowly. It can be used to describe a person's character, behavior, or social status.
