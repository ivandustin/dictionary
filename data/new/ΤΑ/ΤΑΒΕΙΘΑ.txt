Etymology: The word "ταβείθα" comes from the Arabic word "طابا" (tabba), which means "to cook slowly over low heat". It was borrowed into Greek during the Ottoman period.

Grammar: "Ταβείθα" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and its plural form is "ταβείθες".

Description: In Greek cuisine, "ταβείθα" refers to a traditional dish made with lamb or goat meat, cooked slowly in a clay pot with various vegetables, herbs, and spices. It is a hearty and flavorful dish that is often enjoyed during festive occasions or family gatherings.
