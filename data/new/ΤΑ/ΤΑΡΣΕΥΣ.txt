Etymology: The word "ταρσευς" (tarseus) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ταρσεύω" (tarseuo), which means "to mend" or "to repair."

Grammar: In Greek, "ταρσευς" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be used as both a subject and a predicate noun.

Description: "Ταρσευς" refers to a shoemaker or a cobbler. It is a term used to describe a person who makes or repairs shoes.
