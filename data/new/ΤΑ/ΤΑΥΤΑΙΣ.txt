Etymology: The word "ταυταίς" comes from the Greek language. It is the feminine plural form of the demonstrative pronoun "οὗτος" (houtos), meaning "this" or "these".

Grammar: In Greek grammar, "ταυταίς" is the dative case form of the noun. It is used to indicate the indirect object or the recipient of an action.

Description: "ταυταίς" is a Greek word that refers to the feminine plural form of "this" or "these". It is used to point out or refer to a group of feminine objects or entities that are nearby or have been previously mentioned.
