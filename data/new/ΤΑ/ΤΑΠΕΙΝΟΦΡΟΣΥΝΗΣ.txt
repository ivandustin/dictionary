Etymology: The word "ταπεινοφροσύνης" comes from the Greek roots "ταπεινός" (tapeinos) meaning "humble" and "φρόνησις" (phronesis) meaning "mind" or "wisdom". 

Grammar: "ταπεινοφροσύνης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. It is derived from the adjective "ταπεινός" (tapeinos) meaning "humble" and the noun "φρόνησις" (phronesis) meaning "mind" or "wisdom". 

Description: "ταπεινοφροσύνης" refers to the quality or state of being humble. It is the Greek word for humility. Humility is the virtue of recognizing one's own limitations, weaknesses, and insignificance in relation to others. It involves modesty, selflessness, and a lack of arrogance or pride.
