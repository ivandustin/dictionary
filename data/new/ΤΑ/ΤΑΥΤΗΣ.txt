Etymology: The word "ταυτης" (tautēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek pronoun "αυτη" (autē), meaning "this" or "she/her," with the addition of the possessive suffix "-ς" (-s).

Grammar: In Greek, "ταυτης" (tautēs) is a feminine singular genitive form of the pronoun "αυτη" (autē). It is used to indicate possession or association with a feminine noun.

Description: "ταυτης" (tautēs) is a Greek word that means "of this" or "belonging to this." It is used to express possession or ownership in relation to a feminine noun.
