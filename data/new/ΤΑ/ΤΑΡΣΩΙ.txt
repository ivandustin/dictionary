Etymology: The word "ταρσοι" (tarsos) comes from the Greek word "ταρσός" (tarsos), meaning "ankle". 

Grammar: In Greek, "ταρσοι" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the second declension and is classified as a masculine noun. 

Description: "Ταρσοι" refers to the ankles, which are the joints connecting the lower leg to the foot. They play a crucial role in supporting the body's weight and facilitating movement.
