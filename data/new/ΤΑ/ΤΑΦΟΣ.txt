Etymology: The word "ταφος" (tafos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "τάφος" (taphos), which means "grave" or "tomb".

Grammar: In Greek, "ταφος" (tafos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "τάφοι" (tafoi).

Description: "Ταφος" (tafos) refers to a grave or a tomb, which is a place where a deceased person is buried. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a place of burial or a memorial for someone who has passed away.
