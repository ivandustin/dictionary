Etymology: The word "ταρσεα" (tarsea) comes from the Greek word "ταρσός" (tarsos), which means "ankle" or "instep."

Grammar: In Greek, "ταρσεα" is a noun in the plural form. It belongs to the third declension and is classified as a neuter noun.

Description: "Ταρσεα" refers to the ankles or insteps of a person. It is the plural form of the word "ταρσος" (tarsos), which is used to describe the bony structure that connects the foot to the leg.
