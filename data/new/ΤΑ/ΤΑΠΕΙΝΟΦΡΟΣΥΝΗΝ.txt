Etymology: The word "ταπεινοφροσύνην" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of two words: "ταπεινός" (tapeinos) meaning "humble" and "φρόνησις" (phronesis) meaning "mind" or "wisdom".

Grammar: "ταπεινοφροσύνην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "ταπεινοφροσύνην" refers to the quality or state of being humble. It is the Greek word for humility. Humility is the virtue of recognizing one's own limitations, weaknesses, and insignificance in relation to others. It involves having a modest opinion of oneself and showing respect and deference towards others. Humility is often seen as a positive trait in many cultures and religions, as it promotes empathy, gratitude, and a willingness to learn from others.
