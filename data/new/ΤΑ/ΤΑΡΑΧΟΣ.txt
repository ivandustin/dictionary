Etymology: The word "ταραχος" (tarachos) comes from the Greek verb "ταράσσω" (tarasso), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". 

Grammar: In Greek, "ταραχος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. 

Description: "Ταραχος" refers to a state of disturbance, agitation, or turmoil. It can be used to describe a chaotic situation, a state of unrest, or a feeling of unease.
