Etymology: The word "θιγηις" (thigēis) comes from the Greek verb "θίγω" (thigō), which means "to touch" or "to come into contact with."

Grammar: "Θιγηις" is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Θιγηις" refers to the act of touching or contact. It can also be used metaphorically to describe an emotional or intellectual impact or influence.
