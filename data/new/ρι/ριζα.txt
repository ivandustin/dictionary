Word: ριζα

Etymology: ριζα comes from the Greek word "rhiza" which means "root".

Grammar: ριζα is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and its nominative case is ριζα.

Description: ριζα refers to the underground part of a plant that anchors it to the ground and absorbs water and nutrients. It is an essential part of the plant's structure and survival. In Greek cuisine, ριζα is also used to refer to root vegetables such as carrots, potatoes, and beets.
