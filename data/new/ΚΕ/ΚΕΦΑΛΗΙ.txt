Etymology: The word "κεφαλή" (kephalē) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ghebh- meaning "head."

Grammar: In Greek, "κεφαλή" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has the nominative singular form "κεφαλή." The genitive singular form is "κεφαλῆς" (kephalēs), the accusative singular form is "κεφαλήν" (kephalēn), and the vocative singular form is "κεφαλή" (kephalē).

Description: "Κεφαλή" is the Greek word for "head." It refers to the uppermost part of the human body that contains the brain, eyes, nose, mouth, and other sensory organs. The head plays a crucial role in various functions, including perception, cognition, and communication. It is also used metaphorically to represent leadership, authority, or importance in different contexts.
