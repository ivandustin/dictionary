Etymology: The word "κερδαίνω" (kerdaino) comes from the Greek verb "κερδίζω" (kerdizo), which means "to gain" or "to profit". It is derived from the noun "κέρδος" (kerdos), meaning "gain" or "profit".

Grammar: "Κερδαίνω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ κερδαίνω (ego kerdaino) - I gain
- Εσύ κερδαίνεις (esi kerdainis) - You gain (singular, informal)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό κερδαίνει (aftos/afti/afto kerdaini) - He/She/It gains
- Εμείς κερδαίνουμε (emeis kerdainoume) - We gain
- Εσείς κερδαίνετε (eseis kerdainete) - You gain (plural, formal)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές κερδαίνουν (aftoi/aftes kerdainoun) - They gain

Description: "Κερδαίνω" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It generally refers to the act of gaining or acquiring something, whether it be material possessions, money, knowledge, or skills. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the attainment of success, advantage, or benefit in different aspects of life.
