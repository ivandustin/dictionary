Etymology: The word "κελευσάντες" comes from the Greek verb "κελεύω" (keleúo), which means "to command" or "to order." The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending, indicating that the word refers to those who have commanded or ordered.

Grammar: "Κελευσάντες" is a noun in the masculine gender and plural number. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence or the predicate nominative.

Description: "Κελευσάντες" refers to a group of people who have given commands or orders. It can be used to describe military commanders, leaders, or anyone who has authority to give instructions.
