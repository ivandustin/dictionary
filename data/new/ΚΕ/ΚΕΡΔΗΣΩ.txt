Etymology: The word "κερδίζω" (kerdízo) comes from the Greek root "κερδος" (kerdos), meaning "gain" or "profit". 

Grammar: "Κερδίζω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: 

- Εγώ κερδίζω (ego kerdízo) - I gain
- Εσύ κερδίζεις (esý kerdízeis) - You gain (singular)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό κερδίζει (aftós/aftí/aftó kerdízei) - He/She/It gains
- Εμείς κερδίζουμε (emeís kerdízoume) - We gain
- Εσείς κερδίζετε (eseís kerdízete) - You gain (plural)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά κερδίζουν (aftoí/aftés/aftá kerdízoun) - They gain

Description: "Κερδίζω" means "to gain" or "to profit" in Greek. It refers to the act of acquiring something valuable or beneficial, whether it be material possessions, money, knowledge, or success.
