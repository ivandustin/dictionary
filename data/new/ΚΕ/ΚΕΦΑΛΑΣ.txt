Etymology: The word "κεφαλας" (kephalas) comes from the Greek word "κεφαλή" (kephalē), which means "head". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kaput-.

Grammar: "Κεφαλας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case.

Description: "Κεφαλας" refers to the head of a person or an animal. It is a body part located at the top of the body, containing the brain, eyes, nose, mouth, and other sensory organs. The head plays a crucial role in various functions such as perception, communication, and cognition.
