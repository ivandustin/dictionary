Etymology: The word "κενης" (kenes) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "κενός" (kenos), which means "empty" or "void".

Grammar: In Greek, "κενης" is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is used to indicate possession or association.

Description: "Κενης" is a Greek word that translates to "of emptiness" or "of void". It is often used in philosophical or metaphysical contexts to describe the concept of emptiness or nothingness.
