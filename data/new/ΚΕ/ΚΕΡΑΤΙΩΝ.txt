Etymology: The word "κερατιών" comes from the Greek word "κέρας" (keras), meaning "horn". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ḱer- which also gave rise to words like "horn" in English.

Grammar: "κερατιών" is the plural form of the noun "κεράτι" (kerati), which means "horn" in Greek. It belongs to the second declension class of Greek nouns.

Description: "κερατιών" refers to multiple horns. In Greek mythology, horns are often associated with various gods and creatures, symbolizing power, strength, and divinity. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe the bony protrusions found on the heads of certain animals.
