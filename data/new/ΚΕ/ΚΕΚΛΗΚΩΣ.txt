Etymology: The word "κεκληκως" comes from the Greek verb "καλέω" (kaleo), which means "to call" or "to name." The prefix "κε-" indicates a perfect tense, and the suffix "-ως" denotes the participle form.

Grammar: "Κεκληκως" is the perfect participle form of the verb "καλέω" (kaleo). In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The participle form functions as an adjective, describing a noun or pronoun.

Description: "Κεκληκως" can be translated as "having called" or "having named." It is used to describe someone or something that has been called or named in the past and continues to bear that name or title.
