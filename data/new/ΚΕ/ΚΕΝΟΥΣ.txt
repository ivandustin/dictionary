Etymology: The word "κενούς" (kenous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "κενόν" (kenon), meaning "empty" or "void".

Grammar: In Greek, "κενούς" is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It is declined according to the grammatical rules of the Greek language.

Description: The word "κενούς" is used to describe something that is empty, vacant, or devoid of content. It can be used to refer to physical spaces or objects that are empty, as well as metaphorical or abstract concepts that lack substance or meaning.
