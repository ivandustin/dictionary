Etymology: The word "κεκριται" comes from the Greek verb "κρίνω" (kríno), which means "to judge" or "to decide". The prefix "κε-" indicates a perfect tense, so "κεκριται" can be translated as "has been judged" or "has been decided".

Grammar: "κεκριται" is a third-person singular form of the verb "κρίνω" in the perfect tense. It is in the indicative mood and the active voice. The perfect tense indicates a completed action with ongoing results.

Description: "κεκριται" is used to describe something that has been judged or decided in the past and continues to have an impact or relevance in the present. It implies a sense of finality or certainty in the judgment or decision that has been made.
