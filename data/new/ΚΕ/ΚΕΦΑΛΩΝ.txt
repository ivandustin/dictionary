Etymology: The word "κεφαλων" comes from the Greek word "κεφαλή" (kephalē), which means "head". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kaput-.

Grammar: "κεφαλων" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes "κεφαλοι" (kephaloi).

Description: "κεφαλων" refers to "heads" in Greek. It can be used to describe the anatomical part of the body or metaphorically to refer to leaders or important individuals.
