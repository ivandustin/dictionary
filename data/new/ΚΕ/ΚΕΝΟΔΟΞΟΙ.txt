Etymology: The word "κενοδοξοι" comes from the Greek words "κενός" (kenos), meaning "empty" or "void," and "δόξα" (doxa), meaning "glory" or "opinion." 

Grammar: "κενοδοξοι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is in the nominative case. 

Description: "κενοδοξοι" refers to people who have empty or vain opinions about themselves. It describes individuals who have an exaggerated sense of self-importance or who hold inflated beliefs about their abilities or achievements.
