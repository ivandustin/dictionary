Etymology: The word "κενή" (keni) comes from the Greek word "κενός" (kenos), which means "empty" or "void".

Grammar: In Greek, "κενή" is an adjective in the feminine form. It is used to describe something that is empty or devoid of something.

Description: "Κενή" is used to describe a state of emptiness or lack. It can refer to physical emptiness, such as an empty space or container, or it can be used metaphorically to describe a lack of substance or meaning.
