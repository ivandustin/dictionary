Etymology: The word "κεκαθικέν" comes from the Greek verb "καθίζω" (kathízo), which means "to sit down" or "to be seated." The prefix "κε-" indicates a perfect tense, indicating a completed action in the past.

Grammar: "Κεκαθικέν" is the third person singular form of the perfect tense of the verb "καθίζω" (kathízo). It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description: "Κεκαθικέν" can be translated as "he/she/it has sat down" or "he/she/it is seated." It indicates that someone or something has taken a seated position and remains in that state.
