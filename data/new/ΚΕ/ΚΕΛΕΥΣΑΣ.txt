Etymology: The word "κελευσας" comes from the Greek verb "κελεύω" (keleúo), which means "to command" or "to order". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kelh₁-.

Grammar: "κελευσας" is the aorist active participle of the verb "κελεύω" (keleúo), which means "having commanded" or "having ordered". It is in the masculine singular form.

Description: "κελευσας" is used to describe someone who has given a command or an order. It is often used in historical or narrative contexts to indicate that someone has issued a directive.
