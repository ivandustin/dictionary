Etymology: The word "κενοις" (kenois) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "κενός" (kenos), meaning "empty" or "void."

Grammar: "Κενοις" is the plural form of the noun "κενός" (kenos). In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension and is classified as a masculine noun.

Description: "Κενοις" is used to refer to multiple empty or void spaces. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing empty rooms, vacant places, or void areas.
