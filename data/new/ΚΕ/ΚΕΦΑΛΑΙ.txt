Etymology: The word "κεφαλαί" (kephalai) comes from the Greek word "κεφαλή" (kephalē), which means "head". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kaput-.

Grammar: "Κεφαλαί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension and is classified as a feminine noun.

Description: "Κεφαλαί" is the plural form of the noun "κεφαλή" (kephalē), which means "head" in Greek. It refers to the anatomical part of the body that contains the brain, eyes, ears, nose, and mouth. In a figurative sense, it can also refer to the top or highest part of something, or the leader or chief of a group.
