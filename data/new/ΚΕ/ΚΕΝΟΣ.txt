Etymology: The word "κενός" (kenos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ken- meaning "empty" or "void."

Grammar: In Greek, "κενός" is an adjective. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and declines according to the case, number, and gender of the noun it modifies.

Description: The word "κενός" means "empty" or "void" in Greek. It is used to describe something that lacks substance, content, or presence. It can also refer to an empty space or a void.
