Etymology: The word "κερδος" (kerdos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "κέρδος" (kérdos), meaning "gain" or "profit".

Grammar: In Greek, "κερδος" (kerdos) is a noun of neuter gender. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: το κέρδος (to kérdos) - the gain/profit
- Genitive: του κέρδους (tou kérdous) - of the gain/profit
- Accusative: το κέρδος (to kérdos) - the gain/profit
- Vocative: κέρδος (kérdos) - O gain/profit!

Plural:
- Nominative: τα κέρδη (ta kérdi) - the gains/profits
- Genitive: των κερδών (ton kerdon) - of the gains/profits
- Accusative: τα κέρδη (ta kérdi) - the gains/profits
- Vocative: κέρδη (kérdi) - O gains/profits!

Description: "Κερδος" (kerdos) refers to the concept of gain or profit. It can be used to describe financial or material gain, as well as any kind of advantage or benefit.
