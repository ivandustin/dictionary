Etymology: The word "κεφαλή" (kephalē) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ghebh- meaning "head" or "skull".

Grammar: In Greek, "κεφαλή" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the third declension and has the nominative singular form "κεφαλή". The genitive singular form is "κεφαλῆς" (kephalēs), the accusative singular form is "κεφαλήν" (kephalēn), and the vocative singular form is also "κεφαλή" (kephalē).

Description: "Κεφαλή" means "head" in Greek. It refers to the uppermost part of the human body that contains the brain, eyes, nose, mouth, and other sensory organs. It is also used metaphorically to refer to the leader or main part of something.
