Etymology: The word "κεκοσμηται" comes from the Greek verb "κοσμέω" (kosmeo), which means "to arrange" or "to adorn". The prefix "κε-" indicates that the action is completed or perfect.

Grammar: "κεκοσμηται" is the third person singular, perfect tense, middle voice form of the verb "κοσμέω" (kosmeo). In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action with ongoing results. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description: "κεκοσμηται" can be translated as "he/she/it has been arranged" or "he/she/it has adorned". It implies that someone or something has been carefully arranged or decorated in the past, and the effects of that action are still present.
