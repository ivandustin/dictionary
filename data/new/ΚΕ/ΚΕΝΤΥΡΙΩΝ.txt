Etymology: The word "κεντυριών" comes from the Greek word "κένταυρος" (kentauros), which means "centaur". In Greek mythology, centaurs were creatures with the upper body of a human and the lower body of a horse.

Grammar: "κεντυριών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and the nominative case.

Description: "κεντυριών" refers to a centaur, a mythical creature from Greek mythology. Centaurs were known for their strength and wild nature. They were often depicted as half-human and half-horse, with the upper body of a human and the lower body of a horse. In Greek mythology, centaurs were associated with both positive and negative traits, representing both wisdom and savagery. They were often depicted as skilled warriors and hunters.
