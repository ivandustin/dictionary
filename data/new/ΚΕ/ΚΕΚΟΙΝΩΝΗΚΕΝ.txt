Etymology: The word "κεκοινωνηκεν" comes from the Greek verb "κοινωνέω" (koinonéo), which means "to share" or "to participate". The verb is in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "κεκοινωνηκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "κοινωνέω" in the perfect tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The perfect tense is used to describe a past action with present relevance.

Description: "κεκοινωνηκεν" can be translated as "he/she/it has shared" or "he/she/it has participated". It implies that the subject has taken part in an activity or has shared something with others in the past, and the effects of this action are still ongoing.
