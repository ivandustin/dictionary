Etymology: The word "κελευεις" (keleueis) comes from the Greek verb "κελευω" (keleuo), which means "to command" or "to order".

Grammar: "Κελευεις" is a verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It is derived from the verb "κελευω" and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: κελευεις (keleueis) - you command/order
- Plural: κελευετε (keleuete) - you all command/order

Description: "Κελευεις" is a Greek word that means "you command" or "you order". It is used to address someone directly and tell them to give a command or order.
