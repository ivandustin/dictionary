Etymology: The word "κεραμευς" (kerameus) comes from the Greek word "κέραμος" (keramos), which means "potter" or "ceramic artist". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ker- meaning "to burn" or "to heat".

Grammar: In Greek, "κεραμευς" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "κεραμέως" (kerameos).

Description: "Κεραμευς" refers to a potter or a person who works with ceramics. It is someone who shapes clay into various forms and then fires them in a kiln to create pottery or ceramic objects. The term can also be used more broadly to describe someone who works with clay or other materials to create artistic or functional objects.
