Etymology: The word "κεραμιον" (keramion) comes from the Greek word "κεραμος" (keramos), meaning "potter's clay" or "pottery". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ker- meaning "to burn" or "to heat".

Grammar: "Κεραμιον" is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "κεραμιον". In the plural, it becomes "κεραμια" (keramia).

Description: "Κεραμιον" refers to a ceramic object or pottery. It can be used to describe various types of pottery, such as vases, bowls, plates, or other ceramic artifacts.
