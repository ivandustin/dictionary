Word: βρωμα

Etymology:
The word βρωμα (broma) is derived from the Greek verb βρωσκω (brosko), which means "to eat". It is related to the English word "bromatology", which is the scientific study of food.

Grammar:
βρωμα is a neuter noun in Greek. Its plural form is βρωματα (bromata). In English, it can be translated as "food" or "nourishment".

Description:
βρωμα refers to any kind of food or nourishment, whether it is plant-based or animal-based. It can also refer to the act of eating or consuming food. In ancient Greek culture, food was considered a vital part of daily life and was often shared in communal settings. The word βρωμα is still commonly used in modern Greek to refer to food or meals.
