Word: βρωμασιν

Etymology: βρωμασιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb βρωμάω (bromao), which means "to eat greedily" or "to devour". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a common ending for nouns in the Greek language.

Grammar: βρωμασιν is a noun in the feminine gender. It is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb in a sentence.

Description: βρωμασιν refers to a food or a meal that is eaten greedily or voraciously. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is consumed or devoured quickly and eagerly, such as a book or a piece of information.
