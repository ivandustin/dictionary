Word: βραδυτητα

Etymology: βραδυτητα is a Greek word that comes from the root word βραδύς (bradys) meaning "slow" or "sluggish". The suffix -τητα (-tita) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates a state or quality.

Grammar: βραδυτητα is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it is used to describe a state or quality of being slow or sluggish.

Description: βραδυτητα refers to the state or quality of being slow or sluggish. It can be used to describe physical movement, mental processes, or even the passage of time. In Greek, it is often used in scientific or technical contexts, such as in physics or engineering, to describe the rate of change or the speed of a process.
