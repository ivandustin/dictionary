Word: βρωματων

Etymology: βρωματων is a Greek word that comes from the verb βρωματίζω (bromatizo), which means "to feed" or "to nourish". The suffix -ων (-on) is a plural ending, so βρωματων means "foods" or "nourishments".

Grammar: βρωματων is a plural noun in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. So, βρωματων can be translated as "of foods" or "of nourishments".

Description: βρωματων is a word that refers to various types of foods or nourishments. It can be used to describe any type of food or substance that is consumed for sustenance or nourishment. In ancient Greek literature, βρωματων was often used to refer to the food offerings made to the gods in religious ceremonies.
