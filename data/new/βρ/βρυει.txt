Word: βρυει

Etymology: The word βρυει comes from the Greek verb βρύω (bryō), which means "to bubble up" or "to boil". 

Grammar: βρυει is a present tense verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular form of the verb βρύω (bryō). 

Description: βρυει is a verb that describes the action of boiling or bubbling up. It can be used to describe the movement of water or other liquids, as well as the action of boiling something on a stove or in a pot. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or emotion that is intense or boiling over.
