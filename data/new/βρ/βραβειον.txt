Word: βραβεῖον (brabeion)

Etymology: From the Greek word βραβεύς (brabeus), meaning "judge" or "umpire". The suffix -εῖον (-eion) denotes a place or object associated with the root word.

Grammar: βραβεῖον is a neuter noun in the singular form.

Description: βραβεῖον refers to a prize or award given to a winner in a competition or contest. In ancient Greece, it was often used to refer to the prize given to the winner of athletic or artistic competitions, such as the Olympic Games. Today, it can refer to any type of award or recognition given for achievement in a particular field.
