Word: βραδυνω

Etymology:
The word βραδυνω (bradyno) comes from the Greek root βραδύς (bradys), which means "slow" or "sluggish". The suffix -νω (-no) is a verb ending that indicates the action of slowing down or delaying.

Grammar:
βραδυνω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: βραδύνω (bradyno)
- Plural: βραδύνουμε (bradynoume)

Description:
βραδυνω means "to slow down" or "to delay" in Greek. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the slowing down of a vehicle or the delay of a project. It is a common verb in everyday speech and writing.
