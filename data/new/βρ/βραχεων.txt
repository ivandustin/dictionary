Word: βραχεων

Etymology: βραχεων comes from the Greek word βραχύς (brachys) which means "short".

Grammar: βραχεων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also the genitive singular form of the adjective βραχύς.

Description: βραχεων is used to describe something that is short or brief. It can also be used to describe a person who is of short stature. In Greek mythology, the god Hermes was often depicted as being βραχεων, which is why he was known as the "short one".
