μοι (moi) is a Greek pronoun that means "to me" or "for me". It is in the dative case and is often used as an indirect object. 

Etymology: μοι comes from the ancient Greek word "ἐμοί" (emoi), which is the dative singular form of the pronoun "ἐγώ" (ego), meaning "I". 

Grammar: μοι is a pronoun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. It is often translated as "to me" or "for me" in English. 

Description: μοι is a common word in Greek, used in both formal and informal contexts. It is often used in combination with verbs to indicate the recipient of an action, such as "δίνω μοι" (dino moi), which means "give to me". It can also be used with prepositions, such as "προς μοι" (pros moi), which means "towards me".
