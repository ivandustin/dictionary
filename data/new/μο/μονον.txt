Word: μονον

Etymology:
The word μονον (monon) is a Greek adverb that means "only" or "alone". It is derived from the Greek word μόνος (monos), which means "alone" or "solitary".

Grammar:
μονον is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs. It is often translated as "only" or "alone" in English.

Description:
μονον is a common word in Greek and is used in a variety of contexts. It is often used to emphasize the exclusivity of something, such as "I only have one apple" or "He alone can solve this problem". It can also be used to express a limitation or restriction, such as "I can only stay for an hour". In addition, it can be used to express a sense of certainty or emphasis, such as "I am absolutely certain that he is the one".
