μοιχεύειν (moikheúein) is a Greek verb that means "to commit adultery". It is derived from the noun μοιχός (moikhós), which means "adulterer". In terms of grammar, μοιχεύειν is a present active infinitive, meaning it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of committing adultery in the present tense.
