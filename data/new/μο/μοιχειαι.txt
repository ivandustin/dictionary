The word "μοιχειαι" (moicheiai) is a Greek noun that means "adulteries" or "acts of adultery". It is derived from the verb "μοιχεύω" (moicheuo), which means "to commit adultery". In Greek grammar, "μοιχειαι" is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. It is often used in the context of moral or religious discussions, as adultery was considered a serious sin in ancient Greek society.
