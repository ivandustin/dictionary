The word "μοιχευσηις" (moicheusis) is a Greek noun that means "adultery" or "infidelity". It is derived from the verb "μοιχεύω" (moicheuō), which means "to commit adultery". In terms of grammar, it is a feminine noun in the nominative case. The word is used in both secular and religious contexts, and is often associated with moral transgression and betrayal.
