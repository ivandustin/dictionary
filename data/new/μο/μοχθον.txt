The word "μοχθον" (mochthon) is a Greek noun that means "native soil" or "land of one's birth". It is derived from the combination of the words "mochos" (toil) and "thonos" (soil). In terms of grammar, it is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is often used in literature and poetry to describe a deep connection to one's homeland or a sense of nostalgia for a place of origin.
