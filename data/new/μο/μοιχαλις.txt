Word: μοιχαλίς

Etymology:
The word μοιχαλίς (moichalis) is derived from the Greek word μοιχός (moichos), which means "adulterer" or "adulteress". The suffix -αλίς (-alis) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form feminine nouns.

Grammar:
μοιχαλίς is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined like other first-declension nouns, with the genitive form being μοιχαλίδος (moichalidos).

Description:
μοιχαλίς is a Greek word that refers to a woman who commits adultery. In ancient Greece, adultery was considered a serious crime, and women who were found guilty of it could face severe punishment, including death. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a woman who cheats on her husband or partner.
