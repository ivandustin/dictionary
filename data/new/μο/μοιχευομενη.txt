Word: μοιχευομενη

Etymology:
The word μοιχευομενη comes from the Greek verb μοιχεύω (moicheuō), which means "to commit adultery". The suffix -ομενη (-omenē) is a present participle form, indicating that the action of committing adultery is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar:
μοιχευομενη is a present participle form of the verb μοιχεύω. It is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
μοιχευομενη is a Greek word that refers to a woman who is committing adultery. The word is often used in a negative context, as adultery was considered a serious offense in ancient Greek society. The present participle form suggests that the woman is currently engaged in the act of adultery.
