Word: μογις

Etymology: The word μογις (mogis) is a Greek adverb that means "with difficulty" or "hardly". It is derived from the Greek word μόγος (mogos), which means "toil" or "hardship".

Grammar: μογις is an adverb in Greek and is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is often used in negative sentences to express that something is difficult or barely possible.

Description: μογις is a word that conveys the idea of struggle and hardship. It is often used to describe situations where something is difficult to achieve or accomplish.
