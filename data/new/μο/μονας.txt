The word "μονας" (monas) is a Greek noun that means "unit" or "single entity". It is derived from the Greek word "μόνος" (monos), which means "alone" or "single". In grammar, "μονας" is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases and numbers, such as "μονάδα" (monada) in the nominative plural form. 

In ancient Greek philosophy, "μονας" was used to refer to the concept of a single, indivisible entity that was the source of all existence. This concept was later adopted by Christian theology to refer to the unity of God. 

Overall, "μονας" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts, from mathematics to philosophy and theology.
