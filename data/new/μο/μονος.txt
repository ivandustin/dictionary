Word: μονος

Etymology:
The word μονος (monos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- meaning "small, isolated, weak". In Greek, it means "alone, solitary, single".

In Greek, μονος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). It can also be used as an adverb to modify a verb.

Description:
μονος is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can describe a person who is alone or solitary, or it can describe something that is unique or singular. It can also be used to express emphasis or exclusivity. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was often referred to as μονος because he was seen as a solitary figure who stood apart from the other gods.
