Word: μουσικών

Etymology: The word μουσικών comes from the Greek word μουσικός (mousikos), which means "musical" or "of the Muses". The Muses were the nine goddesses of the arts and sciences in Greek mythology.

Grammar: μουσικών is the genitive plural form of μουσικός. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, μουσικών means "of the musicians" or "belonging to the musicians".

Description: μουσικών is a Greek word that refers to musicians or people who are involved in music. It can be used to describe a group of musicians, such as a band or orchestra, or to refer to individuals who play musical instruments or sing. The word can also be used in a broader sense to refer to people who are knowledgeable about music or who appreciate it as an art form.
