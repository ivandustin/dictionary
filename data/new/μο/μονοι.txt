The word "μονοι" (monoi) is a Greek adjective that means "alone" or "solitary". It is derived from the Greek word "μόνος" (monos), which also means "alone" or "single". In terms of grammar, "μονοι" is the masculine plural form of the adjective, while the feminine plural form is "μόνες" (mones) and the neuter plural form is "μόνα" (mona). 

In Greek literature and philosophy, the concept of "monoi" or "monos" is often associated with the idea of self-sufficiency and independence. It is also used to describe individuals who prefer to be alone or who are isolated from society. 

Overall, "μονοι" is a versatile adjective that can be used to describe a variety of situations and individuals who are alone or solitary.
