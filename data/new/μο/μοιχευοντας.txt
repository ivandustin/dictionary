Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "μοιχός" (moichos), which means "adulterer" or "unfaithful". 

Grammar: "μοιχευοντας" is the present participle form of the verb, which means it describes an ongoing action of committing adultery. It is in the masculine accusative plural form, indicating that the subject of the verb is a group of men who are committing adultery. 

Description: The word "μοιχευοντας" is a strong and negative term in Greek, as adultery was considered a serious offense in ancient Greek society. The present participle form suggests that the action of committing adultery is ongoing and continuous.
