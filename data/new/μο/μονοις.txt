Word: μονοις

Etymology: μονοις is the dative plural form of the Greek adjective μόνος (monos), which means "alone" or "solitary". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *men- meaning "small, isolated".

Grammar: μονοις is a dative plural form of the adjective μόνος. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: μονοις is used to describe a group of people who are alone or solitary. It can also be used to describe something that is unique or singular. In Greek literature, the word is often used to describe heroes who are alone in their struggles or who stand out from the crowd.
