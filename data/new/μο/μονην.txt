The word μονην (monēn) is a Greek noun that means "a dwelling place" or "a place of abode." It is derived from the Greek word μονή (monē), which means "a solitary place" or "a place of seclusion." In grammar, μονην is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. 

Example sentence: Πήγα στη μονην του φίλου μου για να μείνω εκεί για λίγες μέρες. (Piga sti monēn tou filou mou gia na meinō ekei gia liges meres.) - I went to my friend's dwelling place to stay there for a few days.
