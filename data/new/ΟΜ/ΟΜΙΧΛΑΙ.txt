Etymology: The word "ομιχλαι" (omichlai) comes from the Greek word "ομίχλη" (omíchli), which means "fog" or "mist".

Grammar: "Ομιχλαί" is the plural form of the noun "ομίχλη" (omíchli). It is a feminine noun in Greek and belongs to the second declension. In the plural form, it takes the ending "-αι" (-ai).

Description: "Ομιχλαί" refers to multiple instances of fog or mist. Fog is a weather phenomenon characterized by a thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the air near the Earth's surface, reducing visibility. Mist is similar to fog but less dense. Both fog and mist are commonly found in humid or cool environments, often in the early morning or evening.
