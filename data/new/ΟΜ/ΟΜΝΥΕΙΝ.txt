Etymology: The word "ομνυειν" (omnyein) comes from the Greek verb "ομνυω" (omnyo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath". 

Grammar: "Ομνυειν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an action happening in the present time. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that is not conjugated to a specific tense, mood, voice, or person.

Description: "Ομνυειν" is a Greek verb that means "to swear" or "to take an oath". It is used to describe the act of making a solemn declaration or promise, often invoking a higher power or authority as a witness. Swearing an oath is a common practice in legal, religious, and ceremonial contexts.
