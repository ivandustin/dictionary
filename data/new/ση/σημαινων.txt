Word: σημαινων

Etymology:
The word σημαινων comes from the Greek verb σημαίνω (sēmaínō), which means "to signify" or "to indicate". It is derived from the noun σῆμα (sêma), which means "sign" or "mark".

Grammar:
σημαινων is the present participle of the verb σημαίνω (sēmaínō). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description:
σημαινων is used to describe someone or something that is signifying or indicating something. It can also be used to describe someone who is giving a signal or making a gesture. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the actions of gods or other supernatural beings who are communicating with mortals.
