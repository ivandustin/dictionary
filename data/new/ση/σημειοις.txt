Word: σημειοις

Etymology:
The word σημειοις (sēmeiois) is a plural form of the Greek noun σημεῖον (sēmeion), which means "sign" or "mark". It is derived from the verb σημαίνω (sēmainō), which means "to signify" or "to indicate".

Grammar:
σημειοις is a plural form of the noun σημεῖον (sēmeion) in the dative case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
σημειοις is a Greek word that refers to signs or marks. It can be used to describe physical signs, such as road signs or symbols, or more abstract signs, such as indications or omens. In the New Testament, the word is often used to refer to miraculous signs performed by Jesus or his disciples.
