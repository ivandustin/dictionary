Word: σημειον

Etymology:
The word σημειον (sēmeion) comes from the Greek root word σημαίνω (sēmainō), which means "to signify" or "to indicate". It is derived from the noun σῆμα (sēma), which means "sign" or "mark".

Genitive: τοῦ σημείου (tou sēmeiou) - of the sign
Dative: τῷ σημείῳ (tō sēmeiō) - to/for the sign
Accusative: τὸ σημεῖον (to sēmeion) - the sign

Dative: τοῖς σημείοις (tois sēmeiois) - to/for the signs
Accusative: τὰ σημεῖα (ta sēmeia) - the signs

σημειον can refer to a sign, mark, or signal that indicates or represents something. It can also refer to a miracle or wonder that serves as a sign of divine power or intervention.
