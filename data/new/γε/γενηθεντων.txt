Word: γενηθεντων

Etymology: 
The word γενηθεντων is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". The word is in the passive participle form, which means "having been born" or "having been created".

Grammar:
γενηθεντων is a passive participle in the genitive plural form. In Greek, the passive participle is used to describe an action that has been done to the subject of the sentence. The genitive plural form indicates that the word is describing a group of people or things that have been born or created.

Description:
The word γενηθεντων is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to describe the creation of the world or the birth of humanity. It can also be used more generally to describe any group of things that have been created or born.
