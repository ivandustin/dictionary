Word: γενεσις

Etymology:
The word γενεσις comes from the Greek word γένεσις (genesis), which means "origin" or "birth". It is derived from the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to be born" or "to come into being".

Grammar:
In Greek, γενεσις is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is spelled with the Greek letters γενέσεως in the genitive case, which is commonly used to indicate possession or origin.

Description:
γενεσις is a term that is often used in religious contexts to refer to the creation or origin of the world. It is most commonly associated with the Book of Genesis in the Bible, which describes the creation of the world in seven days. However, the term can also be used more broadly to refer to the origin or beginning of anything, such as the genesis of a new idea or the genesis of a scientific theory.
