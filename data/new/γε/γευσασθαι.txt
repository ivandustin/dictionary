Word: γευσασθαι

Etymology:
γευσασθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γεύομαι (geúomai), which means "to taste" or "to try." The suffix -σασθαι (-sasthai) is a reflexive suffix that indicates that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar:
γευσασθαι is a middle voice verb in the aorist tense. In Greek, the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
γευσασθαι means "to taste" or "to try" in Greek. It is often used in the context of food or drink, but can also be used more broadly to describe trying or experiencing something new. The middle voice form of the verb emphasizes that the subject is both the one doing the tasting and the one being affected by the taste.
