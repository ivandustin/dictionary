Word: γενησθε

Etymology:
The word γενησθε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." The verb form γενησθε is the second person plural aorist passive imperative form of γίνομαι.

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, γενησθε is a verb in the aorist tense, which refers to a completed action in the past. It is also in the passive voice, which means that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The imperative mood indicates that the verb is a command or request.

Description:
The word γενησθε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 3:7, where Jesus tells Nicodemus, "You must be born again." The verb form γενησθε is translated as "be born" or "become" in English. The phrase "born again" has become a common expression in Christian theology, referring to a spiritual rebirth or conversion.
