Word: γενηματα

Etymology: The word γενηματα comes from the Greek word γέννημα (gennēma), which means "offspring" or "product". The suffix -ατα (-ata) is a pluralizing suffix in Greek, so γενηματα means "offspring" or "products" in the plural form.

Grammar: γενηματα is a noun in the plural form in Greek. It is a neuter noun, which means that it does not have a specific gender. In English, it can be translated as "offspring" or "products".

Description: γενηματα refers to the offspring or products of something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as the offspring of animals or the products of a factory, or in a figurative sense, such as the results of a project or the fruits of one's labor. The word emphasizes the idea of something being produced or created, and the plural form suggests that there are multiple offspring or products.
