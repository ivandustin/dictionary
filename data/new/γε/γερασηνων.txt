Word: γερασηνων

Etymology:
The word γερασηνων is a Greek word that is derived from the root word γέρων (geron), which means "old man" or "elder". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common Greek suffix that is used to form nouns.

Grammar:
γερασηνων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and is in the plural form.

Description:
γερασηνων refers to a group of old men or elders. In ancient Greek society, elders were highly respected and played an important role in decision-making and governance. The word can also be used to refer to a council of elders or a group of wise men.
