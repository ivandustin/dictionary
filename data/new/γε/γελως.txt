Word: γελως

Etymology:
The word γελως (gelōs) is derived from the Greek verb γελάω (geláō), which means "to laugh." The noun γελως refers to laughter or a laughing fit.

Grammar:
In Greek, γελως is a masculine noun of the third declension. Its nominative singular form is γελως, and its genitive singular form is γελωτός (gelōtós). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
γελως is a term used to describe laughter or a laughing fit. It can refer to both the physical act of laughing and the emotional state of being amused or entertained. In ancient Greek culture, laughter was often associated with joy and happiness, and was considered a desirable trait. The word γελως is still used in modern Greek to refer to laughter.
