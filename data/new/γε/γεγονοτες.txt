Word: γεγονότες

Etymology: The word γεγονότες comes from the Greek verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". The word is in the perfect participle form, which indicates that the action has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Grammar: γεγονότες is a masculine plural noun in the nominative case. It is often used as an adjective to describe a group of people or things that have already happened or occurred.

Description: γεγονότες can be translated to English as "those who have become" or "those who have happened". It is often used in historical or philosophical contexts to refer to events or people who have already existed or occurred. In Greek mythology, for example, the γεγονότες were the gods and heroes who had already lived and died. In modern Greek, the word is also used to refer to graduates or alumni of a school or university.
