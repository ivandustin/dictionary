Word: γενησομενον

Etymology: 
The word γενησομενον is a Greek verb in the future tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is derived from the root word γίγνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born."

Grammar:
In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and mood. γενησομενον is in the future tense, which indicates that the action will happen at some point in the future. It is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. Finally, it is in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
γενησομενον can be translated to English as "will be born" or "will become." It is often used in the context of birth or creation, such as in the phrase "a new world will be born" or "a new idea will be created." The middle voice emphasizes that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, which can convey a sense of personal responsibility or agency.
