Word: γερων

Etymology:
The word γερων (geron) comes from the Greek root word γερας (geras), which means "old age" or "old man." It is related to the English word "geriatric."

Grammar:
In Greek, γερων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is γεροντες (gerontes).

Description:
γερων is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to an older man, typically one who is respected for his wisdom and experience. In Greek mythology, the god Zeus was sometimes referred to as γερων, emphasizing his status as the wise and powerful ruler of the gods. The term is also used in modern Greek to refer to an elderly man.
