The word "γεωργοις" (georgois) is a Greek noun in the dative plural form, which means "to farmers" or "for farmers". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "γεωργός" (georgos) meaning "farmer" and "-οις" (-ois) which is a suffix used to indicate the dative plural form.

Grammar: In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The dative plural form is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Γεωργοις" is a specific form of the noun "γεωργός" (georgos) and is used to refer to a group of farmers. It is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the agricultural way of life.
