Word: γενηθωμεν

Etymology: γενηθωμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." The suffix -θωμεν (-thomen) is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive ending, indicating that the verb is in the subjunctive mood and refers to a completed action in the past.

Grammar: γενηθωμεν is a first-person plural aorist subjunctive verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, while the subjunctive mood indicates that the action is uncertain or hypothetical. The first-person plural form indicates that the subject is "we."

Description: γενηθωμεν can be translated as "let us be born" or "let us become." It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to express a desire for spiritual rebirth or transformation. In the New Testament, it appears in John 3:3, where Jesus tells Nicodemus that "unless one is born again (γεννηθῇ ἄνωθεν), he cannot see the kingdom of God."
