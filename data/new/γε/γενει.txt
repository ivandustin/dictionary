Word: γενει

Etymology:
The word γενει (genei) is derived from the Greek word γένος (genos), which means "race" or "kind". It is related to the English word "genealogy".

Grammar:
In Greek, γενει is a noun in the dative case. It can be translated as "to a race" or "to a kind".

Description:
The word γενει is used in Greek to refer to a particular race or kind of people. It can also be used to describe a particular characteristic or quality that is common to a group of people. For example, one might say "Οι Έλληνες είναι ένα γένος ανθρώπων με μακρά ιστορία και πολιτισμό" (The Greeks are a race of people with a long history and culture).
