Etymology:
The word γεγραμμένας is a Greek word that comes from the verb γράφω (grapho), which means "to write." The word is in the perfect participle form, which means "having been written."

γεγραμμένας is a feminine noun in the accusative case. It is the accusative singular form of the perfect participle of the verb γράφω. In Greek, the perfect participle is used to describe an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description:
γεγραμμένας is used to describe something that has been written or inscribed. It can refer to a written document, a message, or any other form of written communication. The word is often used in a legal context to refer to a written record or document that has been officially recorded.
