Word: γεννηθεντων

Etymology:
The word γεννηθεντων is derived from the Greek verb γεννάω (gennáo), which means "to beget" or "to give birth". The suffix -των (-tōn) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word is referring to multiple things that have been begotten or born.

Grammar:
γεννηθεντων is a genitive plural form of the verb γεννάω (gennáo). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple things that possess or are related to the action of being begotten or born.

Description:
γεννηθεντων can be translated to mean "of those who have been begotten" or "of those who have been born". It is often used in a religious context to refer to the descendants of a particular person or group, such as the "sons of Abraham" in the Bible. The word can also be used more generally to refer to any group of people who share a common ancestry or origin.
