Word: γενομενης

Etymology: γενομενης is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born."

Grammar: γενομενης is the genitive singular form of the feminine noun γενομένη (genoméni), which means "becoming" or "coming into being." In Greek, the genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between nouns.

Description: γενομενης is a word that describes the state of something that is in the process of becoming or coming into being. It can be used to describe a wide range of things, from physical objects to abstract concepts. In Greek philosophy, the concept of γενομενης was central to the idea of change and the constant flux of the universe.
