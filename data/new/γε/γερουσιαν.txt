Word: γερουσιαν

Etymology: The word γερουσιαν comes from the Greek word γερουσία (gerousía), which means "senate" or "council of elders". The suffix -αν (-an) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form nouns.

Grammar: γερουσιαν is a noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) and declension (changes in form depending on their grammatical function in a sentence). γερουσιαν is a masculine noun in the first declension.

Description: γερουσιαν refers to a member of the gerousía, the council of elders in ancient Greek city-states. The gerousía was a group of respected and experienced men who advised the city's leaders and made important decisions. The term γερουσιαν can also be used more broadly to refer to an elder or a wise person.
