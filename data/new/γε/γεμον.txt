The word "γεμον" (gemon) is a Greek noun that means "ruler" or "governor". It is derived from the verb "γεμίζω" (gemizo), which means "to fill" or "to complete". In ancient Greece, a gemon was a high-ranking official who had the power to govern a city-state or a region. In modern Greek, the word "gemon" is still used to refer to a governor or a leader. 

In terms of grammar, "γεμον" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its plural form is "γεμονες" (gemones). 

Overall, "γεμον" is a word that has been used for centuries to describe those in positions of power and authority.
