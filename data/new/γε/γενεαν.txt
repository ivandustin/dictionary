Word: γενεαν

Etymology:
The word γενεαν is derived from the Greek word γενεά (genea), which means "generation" or "race". It is related to the Greek word γίγνομαι (ginomai), which means "to be born" or "to become".

Grammar:
γενεαν is a noun in the accusative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
γενεαν refers to a generation or a group of people who are born around the same time and share common characteristics or experiences. It can also refer to a lineage or a family tree. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is often used to refer to the genealogy of Jesus Christ.
