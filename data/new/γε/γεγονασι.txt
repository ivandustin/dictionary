Word: γεγονασι

Etymology: γεγονασι is the third person plural perfect active indicative form of the Greek verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁- which means "to give birth, to beget".

Grammar: γεγονασι is a verb form in the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing relevance to the present. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates that the speaker is making a statement or asking a question.

Description: γεγονασι can be translated as "they have become" or "they have happened". It is often used to describe a change or transformation that has taken place. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου γεγόνασι πιο ώριμοι" (Oi filoi mou gegonasi pio orimoi) means "My friends have become more mature".
