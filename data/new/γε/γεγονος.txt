Word: γεγονος

Etymology:
The word γεγονος (gegonos) is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίγνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." The prefix γε- (ge-) is an intensifier, so γεγονος (gegonos) means "become completely" or "fully born."

Grammar:
γεγονος (gegonos) is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is a perfect participle of the verb γίγνομαι (ginomai), which means that it describes a completed action. In this case, it describes someone or something that has been fully born or has fully become something.

Description:
γεγονος (gegonos) can be translated as "one who has been born" or "one who has become." It can refer to a person who has been born, or to something that has come into existence or has been created. In Greek mythology, the term is often used to describe the offspring of gods and mortals, such as Hercules or Perseus. In philosophy, the term is used to describe the process of becoming, or the transition from potentiality to actuality.
