Word: γεωργων

Etymology:
The word γεωργων comes from the Greek word γεωργός (georgos), which means "farmer" or "tiller of the soil". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common ending for Greek nouns in the genitive case.

Grammar:
γεωργων is the genitive singular form of γεωργός. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship. So, γεωργων can be translated as "of the farmer" or "belonging to the farmer".

Description:
γεωργων is a word that refers to the farmer or someone who works the land. It is a common term in Greek literature and culture, as agriculture has been an important part of Greek society for thousands of years. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is hardworking and diligent, like a farmer tending to his crops.
