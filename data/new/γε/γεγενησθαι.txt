Word: γεγενησθαι

Etymology:
The word γεγενησθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." The prefix γε- (ge-) adds emphasis to the verb, indicating that the action has already taken place in the past.

Grammar:
γεγενησθαι is a perfect passive participle in Greek, which means that it describes an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. It is often translated as "having been born" or "having come into existence." In terms of its grammatical form, it is a third-person singular verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and indicative mood.

Description:
γεγενησθαι is a word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the birth or creation of something. For example, it is used to describe the birth of Jesus in Matthew 1:18: "Now the birth of Jesus Christ took place in this way. When his mother Mary had been betrothed to Joseph, before they came together she was found to be with child from the Holy Spirit." The word is also used in other contexts to describe the creation of the world or the beginning of a new era.
