Word: γενησεται

Etymology: γενησεται is a Greek word that comes from the verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". It is a future tense form of the verb.

Grammar: γενησεται is a third person singular future indicative active form of the verb γίνομαι (ginomai). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, aspect, voice, mood, and person. The future tense indicates that the action will happen in the future.

Description: γενησεται is a Greek verb that means "it will happen" or "it will become". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe future events or actions. For example, in Matthew 24:6, Jesus says, "You will hear of wars and rumors of wars, but see to it that you are not alarmed. Such things must happen (γενησεται), but the end is still to come."
