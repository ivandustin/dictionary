Word: γενομενοις

Etymology:
The word γενομενοις is a form of the Greek verb γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to happen". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁- which means "to give birth" or "to become".

Grammar:
γενομενοις is the dative plural form of the aorist participle of γίνομαι. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can be used to modify nouns or act as independent verbs. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration.

Description:
The word γενομενοις can be translated as "having become" or "having happened". It is often used in Greek literature to describe events or situations that have already taken place. For example, in the New Testament, it is used to describe the disciples "having become" afraid during a storm at sea (Mark 4:41). In classical Greek, it can also be used to describe a person's status or condition, such as "having become" wealthy or "having happened" to be in a certain place at a certain time.
