Word: γεωργιον

Etymology:
The word γεωργιον (georgion) is derived from the Greek words γεωργός (georgos) meaning "farmer" and γῆ (ge) meaning "earth" or "land". Therefore, γεωργιον literally means "land of the farmer" or "farmland".

Grammar:
γεωργιον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In the nominative case, it is spelled as γεωργιον. In the genitive case, it is spelled as γεωργίου (georgiou). In the accusative case, it is spelled as γεωργιον.

Description:
γεωργιον refers to a piece of land that is used for farming or agriculture. It can also refer to a farm or a plantation. In ancient Greece, farming was an important occupation and the word γεωργιον was commonly used to describe farmland. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to agricultural land.
