The word "γεωργον" (georgon) is a Greek noun that means "farmer" or "agriculturist". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek words "γῆ" (ge) meaning "earth" and "ἔργον" (ergon) meaning "work" or "task". 

Grammar: "Γεωργον" is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "γεωργοί" (georgoi). 

Description: The word "γεωργον" refers to a person who cultivates the land and raises crops or livestock. In ancient Greece, agriculture was a vital part of the economy and society, and farmers were highly respected. Today, farming remains an important industry in Greece, and the word "γεωργον" is still used to describe those who work in this field.
