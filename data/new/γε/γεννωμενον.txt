Word: γεννωμενον

Etymology:
The word γεννωμενον is a present participle form of the Greek verb γεννάω (gennáo), which means "to beget" or "to give birth to". The root of the word is γενν- (genn-), which means "birth" or "origin". The suffix -ωμενον (-omenon) is a present participle ending that indicates ongoing action.

Grammar:
γεννωμενον is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a noun or pronoun as currently undergoing the action of being born or being begotten.

Description:
The word γεννωμενον is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus Christ as the "begotten" or "born" Son of God. It emphasizes the idea that Jesus was not created, but rather was born of God the Father. The word can also be used more generally to describe any person or thing that is currently being born or begotten.
