Word: γενέσθαι

Etymology:
The word γενέσθαι comes from the Greek verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁- which means "to give birth" or "to produce".

Grammar:
γενέσθαι is a verb in the aorist tense, middle voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description:
γενέσθαι is a versatile verb that can be translated in a variety of ways depending on the context. It can mean "to come into being", "to arise", "to be born", "to become", "to happen", or "to occur". It is often used in philosophical and theological discussions to describe the process of creation or the origin of the universe. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the birth of Jesus Christ and the beginning of his ministry.
