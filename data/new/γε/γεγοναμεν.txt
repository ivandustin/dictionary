Word: γεγοναμεν

Etymology: γεγοναμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." The prefix γε- (ge-) adds emphasis to the verb, indicating that the action has already taken place.

Grammar: γεγοναμεν is the perfect tense form of the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai). In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The verb is conjugated in the first person plural, indicating that "we have become" or "we were born."

Description: γεγοναμεν is a powerful and significant word in Greek, as it describes a fundamental aspect of human existence: the process of becoming. The perfect tense form emphasizes that this process has already taken place, and that the effects of this transformation continue to shape our lives. In Christian theology, γεγοναμεν is often used to describe the process of spiritual rebirth or regeneration, which is seen as a transformative event that brings new life and purpose to believers.
