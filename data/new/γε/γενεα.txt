Word: γενεα

Etymology:
The word γενεα comes from the Greek root word γένος (genos), which means "race" or "kind". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁- which means "to beget" or "to give birth".

In Greek, γενεα is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
Nominative: η γενεά (i genea) 
Genitive: της γενεάς (tis geneas) 
Accusative: τη γενεά (ti genea) 
Vocative: γενεά (genea)

Plural:
Nominative: οι γενεές (i genees) 
Genitive: των γενεών (ton geneon) 
Accusative: τις γενεές (tis genees) 
Vocative: γενεές (genees)

Description:
The word γενεα refers to a generation or a group of people who are born around the same time and share similar characteristics or experiences. It can also refer to a lineage or a family tree. In Greek mythology, the term is often used to describe the genealogy of the gods and heroes.
