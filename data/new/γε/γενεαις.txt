The word γενεαις (geneais) is derived from the Greek word γενεά (genea), which means "generation" or "race". It is a plural form of the word γενεά.

γενεαις is a noun in the nominative case, plural number, and feminine gender in the Greek language.

γενεαις is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to generations or descendants of a particular person or group of people.
