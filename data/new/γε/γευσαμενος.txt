Word: γευσαμένος

Etymology:
The word γευσαμένος (geusamenos) is a participle form of the Greek verb γεύομαι (geuomai), which means "to taste" or "to experience." The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰewgʰ- which means "to enjoy" or "to please."

Grammar:
γευσαμένος is a participle form of the verb γεύομαι (geuomai), which means "to taste" or "to experience." It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
The word γευσαμένος (geusamenos) is used to describe someone who has tasted or experienced something. It can be translated as "having tasted" or "having experienced." The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone who has gained knowledge or understanding through personal experience. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe Jesus as "having tasted death for everyone" (Hebrews 2:9).
