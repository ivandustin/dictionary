Word: γενηθητω

Etymology:
The word γενηθητω is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." The verb form γενηθητω is in the aorist passive imperative tense, which means it is a command to someone to let something happen to them.

Grammar:
As mentioned above, γενηθητω is a verb in the aorist passive imperative tense. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The word γενηθητω can be translated to mean "let it be done" or "let it happen." It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts as a way of expressing submission to the will of a higher power. In the New Testament of the Bible, it is used by Jesus in the Garden of Gethsemane when he prays to God, "Father, if you are willing, take this cup from me; yet not my will, but yours be done" (Luke 22:42).
