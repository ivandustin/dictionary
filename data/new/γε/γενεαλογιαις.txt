Word: γενεαλογιαις

Etymology: The word γενεαλογιαις comes from the Greek words γενεαλογία (genealogy) and the suffix -αις (-ais), which is a dative plural ending. 

Grammar: γενεαλογιαις is a feminine noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: γενεαλογιαις refers to genealogies or family trees. It is often used in the context of tracing one's ancestry or lineage. In ancient Greece, genealogies were important for determining one's social status and legitimacy. Today, genealogy is a popular hobby and field of study for many people.
