The word "γελωντες" (gelontes) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "γελαω" (gelao), which means "to laugh". The participle form "γελωντες" can be translated as "laughing" or "those who are laughing". It is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a group of people who are laughing or having a good time. In terms of grammar, it is a masculine plural nominative participle.
