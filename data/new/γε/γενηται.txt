Word: γενηται

Etymology: γενηται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born."

Grammar: γενηται is a third-person singular aorist passive indicative form of the verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: γενηται can be translated as "was made" or "was created." It is often used in the New Testament to describe the creation of the world or the birth of Jesus Christ. For example, in John 1:3, it is written, "All things were made through him, and without him was not anything made that was made" (ESV).
