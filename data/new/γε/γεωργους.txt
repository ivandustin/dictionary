Word: γεωργους

Etymology: The word γεωργους comes from the Greek words γῆ (gē) meaning "earth" and ἔργον (érgon) meaning "work". Therefore, γεωργους means "earth-worker" or "farmer".

Grammar: γεωργους is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in the Greek language.

Description: γεωργους refers to a person who cultivates the land, raises crops, and/or tends to livestock. In ancient Greece, farming was a crucial occupation and farmers were highly respected members of society. Today, the term γεωργους is still used in modern Greek to refer to farmers or people who work in agriculture.
