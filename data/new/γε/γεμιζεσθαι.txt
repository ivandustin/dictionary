Word: γεμιζεσθαι

Etymology:
The word γεμιζεσθαι comes from the Greek verb γεμίζω (gemízo), which means "to fill" or "to be full". The suffix -σθαι (-sthai) is a reflexive suffix, indicating that the action is being done to oneself. Therefore, γεμιζεσθαι means "to fill oneself" or "to be filled".

Grammar:
γεμιζεσθαι is a reflexive verb in the present tense, middle or passive voice, and infinitive mood. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and mood. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, the middle or passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, and the infinitive mood indicates that the verb is not conjugated to agree with a specific subject.

Description:
γεμιζεσθαι is a Greek verb that means "to fill oneself" or "to be filled". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe being filled with emotions, thoughts, or ideas. For example, one might say "Γεμίζομαι με χαρά" (Gemízome me chará), which means "I am filled with joy". The verb can also be used in a literal sense to describe filling oneself with food or drink.
