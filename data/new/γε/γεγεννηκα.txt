Word: γεγεννηκα

Etymology: γεγεννηκα is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γεννάω (gennáo), which means "to beget" or "to give birth to". The prefix γε- (ge-) indicates completion or perfect tense, so γεγεννηκα means "I have been born" or "I have been begotten".

Grammar: γεγεννηκα is the first person singular perfect indicative active form of the verb γεννάω. In Greek, the perfect tense indicates a completed action with ongoing results. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: γεγεννηκα is a word that expresses the idea of being born or begotten. It is often used in a religious context to refer to the birth of Jesus Christ. In Greek literature, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the birth or creation of something new.
