Word: γεγονας

Etymology:
The word γεγονας (gegonas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γίγνομαι (ginomai), which means "to become" or "to be born." The prefix γε- (ge-) is a perfective prefix that indicates completion or a finished action. Therefore, γεγονας (gegonas) means "you have become" or "you were born."

Grammar:
γεγονας (gegonas) is the second person singular form of the perfect tense in Greek. The perfect tense is used to describe a completed action that has present relevance. In this case, γεγονας (gegonas) means "you have become" or "you were born."
