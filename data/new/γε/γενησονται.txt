Word: γενησονται

Etymology:
The word γενησονται is a verb form in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". The verb form γενησονται is in the future tense and passive voice, meaning "they will be born" or "they will come into being".

Grammar:
As mentioned, γενησονται is a verb form in the Greek language. It is in the future tense and passive voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence will be born or come into being. The word is also conjugated in the third person plural, meaning that it refers to multiple individuals or things.

Description:
The word γενησονται is a verb that describes the act of coming into being or being born. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the creation or emergence of something new, such as an idea or a movement.
