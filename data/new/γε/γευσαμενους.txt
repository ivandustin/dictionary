Word: γευσαμένους

Etymology:
The word γευσαμένους is a Greek verb in the aorist participle form of the verb γεύομαι (geuomai), which means "to taste" or "to experience." The word is derived from the Greek root word γεύω (geuo), which means "to taste" or "to try."

Grammar:
γευσαμένους is a participle in the aorist tense, which indicates that the action of tasting or experiencing has been completed in the past. It is in the masculine, plural form, which means that it refers to a group of people who have tasted or experienced something.

Description:
The word γευσαμένους is often used in the context of food or drink, indicating that a group of people have tasted or sampled a particular dish or beverage. It can also be used more broadly to describe any kind of experience or sensation that has been tasted or tried.
