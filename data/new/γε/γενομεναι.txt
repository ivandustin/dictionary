Word: γενομεναι

Etymology: γενομεναι is the feminine form of the Greek verb γίγνομαι (gígnomai), which means "to become" or "to be born". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵenh₁- which means "to give birth" or "to produce".

Grammar: γενομεναι is the present participle of the verb γίγνομαι in the feminine nominative plural form. In Greek, the present participle is used to indicate an ongoing action or state. The feminine nominative plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of female entities.

Description: γενομεναι can be translated as "being born" or "becoming". It is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe the process of creation or the emergence of something new.
