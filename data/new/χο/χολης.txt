The word "χολης" (cholēs) is a Greek noun that means "bile" or "gall". It is derived from the Greek word "χολή" (cholē), which also means "bile". In ancient Greek medicine, bile was believed to be one of the four humors that regulated the body's health and temperament. 

In terms of grammar, "χολης" is a singular noun in the genitive case. It can be used to indicate possession or association with bile, as in "the color of the bile" (χρώμα της χολής). 

Overall, "χολης" is a word that is primarily used in medical or scientific contexts to refer to the digestive fluid produced by the liver and stored in the gallbladder.
