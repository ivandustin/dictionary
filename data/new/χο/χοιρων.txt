The word "χοιρων" (choiron) is a Greek noun that means "pig" or "swine". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "χοῖρος" (choiros), which also means "pig". In terms of grammar, it is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case "χοίρου" (choirou) or the accusative case "χοίρον" (choiron). In Greek mythology, the goddess Demeter was often depicted holding a piglet, which was considered a sacred animal.
