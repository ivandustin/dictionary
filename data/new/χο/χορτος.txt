Word: χορτος

Etymology:
The word χορτος (chortos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵʰreh₁- meaning "to grow". In Ancient Greek, χορτος originally referred to any kind of vegetation or plant, but later came to specifically mean grass or fodder.

Grammar:
In Greek, χορτος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is χορτος, and its genitive singular form is χορτου. In the plural, the nominative form is χορτοι, and the genitive form is χορτων.

Description:
χορτος refers to any kind of grass or vegetation that is used as food for animals. It can also refer to any kind of plant that grows wild or is cultivated for human consumption. In modern Greek, the word χορτος is often used to refer to a vegetarian diet or lifestyle.
