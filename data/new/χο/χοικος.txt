The word "χοικος" (choikos) is a Greek adjective that means "muddy" or "muddy-colored". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek word "χοινός" (choinos), which means "mud". 

Grammar: "χοικος" is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. 

Description: The word is often used to describe the color of something that is brownish or yellowish, like muddy water or soil. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is unclear or confusing.
