Word: χορτον

The word χορτον (chorton) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word χόρτος (chórtos), which means "grass" or "herb".

χορτον is a noun in the Greek language. It is a neuter noun, which means that it does not have a gender. In the singular form, it is χορτον, and in the plural form, it is χόρτα (chórta).

Description:
χορτον refers to any type of grass or herb. It can be used to describe the vegetation in a field or garden, or the food that animals eat. In modern Greek, it is often used to refer to vegetarian or vegan food, as it is associated with plant-based diets.
