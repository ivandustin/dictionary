The word "χορταζεσθε" (chortazesthe) is a Greek verb in the present tense, second person plural form. It comes from the root word "χορταζω" (chortazo), which means "to be filled, satisfied, or satiated." 

In terms of grammar, "χορταζεσθε" is a reflexive verb, meaning that the subject (you) is performing the action on themselves. The "-σθε" ending indicates that it is in the present tense, second person plural form.

Overall, the word "χορταζεσθε" can be translated to mean "you (plural) are being filled or satisfied." It is often used in a spiritual or metaphorical sense, such as in the biblical phrase "Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they shall be filled" (Matthew 5:6).
