Etymology: The word "λατρευείν" (latreuein) comes from the Greek verb "λατρεύω" (latreuo), which means "to serve" or "to worship." It is derived from the noun "λάτρις" (latris), meaning "servant" or "worshipper."

Grammar: "Λατρευείν" is the present active infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an ongoing action, the active voice shows that the subject performs the action, and the infinitive form expresses the verb in its most basic form.

Description: "Λατρευείν" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of serving or worshiping. It is often used in religious contexts to describe the act of worshiping a deity or showing reverence. The word implies a deep devotion and commitment to the object of worship.
