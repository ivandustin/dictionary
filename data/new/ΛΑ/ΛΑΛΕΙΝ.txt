Etymology: The word "λαλεῖν" (lalein) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- meaning "to talk, to speak."

Grammar: "Λαλεῖν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation and is in the present tense. It is commonly used in the active voice.

Description: "Λαλεῖν" means "to speak" or "to talk" in Greek. It refers to the act of using words to communicate or express thoughts, ideas, or information.
