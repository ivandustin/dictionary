Etymology: The word "λατρευσουσιν" comes from the Greek verb "λατρεύω" (latreuo), which means "to worship" or "to serve." It is derived from the noun "λάτρις" (latris), meaning "servant" or "worshipper."

Grammar: "Λατρευσουσιν" is the third person plural future active indicative form of the verb "λατρεύω" (latreuo). In Greek, verbs change their form based on tense, voice, mood, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, the active voice shows that the subject performs the action, and the indicative mood states a fact or a statement. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people.

Description: "Λατρευσουσιν" is a Greek word that translates to "they will worship" or "they will serve" in English. It expresses the future action of a group of people worshipping or serving someone or something.
