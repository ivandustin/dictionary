Etymology: The word "λαζαρον" (lazaros) comes from the Hebrew name "Elazar" which means "God has helped". It is also associated with the biblical character Lazarus, who was raised from the dead by Jesus.

Grammar: "Λαζαρον" (lazaros) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, singular form.

Description: "Λαζαρον" (lazaros) refers to Lazarus, a biblical figure mentioned in the New Testament. According to the Gospel of John, Lazarus was a friend of Jesus who died and was later resurrected by Jesus. The name "Lazarus" is often used to symbolize resurrection or new life.
