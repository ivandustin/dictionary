Etymology: The word "λαβέτω" (laveto) comes from the Greek verb "λαμβάνω" (lamvano), which means "to take" or "to receive".

Grammar: "Λαβέτω" is the third person singular imperative form of the verb "λαμβάνω" (lamvano). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone in the third person.

Description: "Λαβέτω" is a Greek word that is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts. It is commonly seen in the phrase "Λαβέτω ο θεός" (Laveto o theos), which translates to "Let God take" or "Let God receive". This phrase is often used to express surrender or submission to a higher power.
