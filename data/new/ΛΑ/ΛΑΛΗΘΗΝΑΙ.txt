Etymology: The word "λαληθηναί" comes from the Greek verb "λαλέω" (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂-.

Grammar: "λαληθηναί" is the third person singular, present tense, passive voice form of the verb "λαλέω" (laleo). In Greek grammar, it belongs to the class of verbs that end in "-ω" and have a stem ending in "-λαληθ-". The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "λαληθηναί" means "to be spoken" or "to be talked about" in Greek. It is used to describe something that is the subject of conversation or discussion.
