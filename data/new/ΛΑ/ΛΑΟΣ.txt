Etymology: The word "λαος" (laos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leH₂w-, meaning "people" or "folk".

Grammar: In Greek, "λαος" (laos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case, singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "λαοι" (laoi).

Description: "Λαος" (laos) refers to the people or the population of a community, nation, or ethnic group. It can also be used to describe a crowd or a group of individuals. In a broader sense, it can represent the common people or the masses.
