Etymology: The word "λαλησαί" (lalēsai) comes from the Greek verb "λαλέω" (laleō), which means "to speak" or "to talk."

Grammar: "Λαλησαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "λαλέω" (laleō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Λαλησαί" is the infinitive form of the verb "λαλέω" (laleō), which means "to speak" or "to talk." It is used to describe the action of speaking or talking in a general sense.
