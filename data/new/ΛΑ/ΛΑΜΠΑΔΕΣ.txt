Etymology: The word "λαμπάδες" (lam-pa-des) comes from the Greek word "λαμπάς" (lam-pas), which means "torch" or "lamp". 

Grammar: "Λαμπάδες" is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is feminine in gender. 

Description: "Λαμπάδες" refers to torches or lamps that are used for lighting purposes. In Greek culture, "λαμπάδες" are often associated with celebrations and religious ceremonies, where they are lit and carried as a symbol of light and hope.
