Etymology: The word "λαλούντων" comes from the Greek verb "λαλέω" (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂-.

Grammar: "Λαλούντων" is the present participle form of the verb "λαλέω" in the genitive plural form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action. The genitive plural form indicates that the action is being performed by multiple individuals.

Description: "Λαλούντων" can be translated as "of those who are speaking" or "of those who are talking". It refers to a group of people who are currently engaged in the act of speaking or talking.
