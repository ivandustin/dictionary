Etymology: The word "λατρεία" (latreia) comes from the Greek root "λατρεύω" (latreuō), which means "to serve" or "to worship." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *le- ("to let go, to slacken") and the suffix -τρεία (-treia), which denotes an action or practice.

Grammar: "Λατρεία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the first declension and has the nominative singular form "λατρεία." In the plural, it becomes "λατρείες" (latreies).

Description: "Λατρεία" refers to the act of worship or the service rendered to a deity or a higher power. It can also denote devotion, adoration, or reverence towards a particular religious belief or practice. In ancient Greek culture, "λατρεία" was an essential part of religious rituals and ceremonies, involving sacrifices, prayers, and other forms of worship.
