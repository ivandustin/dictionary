Etymology: The word "λαμβάνω" (lamváno) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "λαμβάνειν" (lambánein), which means "to take" or "to receive".

Grammar: "Λαμβάνω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ λαμβάνω (Egó lamváno) - I take/receive
- Εσύ λαμβάνεις (Esý lambáneis) - You take/receive
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό λαμβάνει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó lambánei) - He/She/It takes/receives
- Εμείς λαμβάνουμε (Emeís lamvánoume) - We take/receive
- Εσείς λαμβάνετε (Eseís lambánete) - You take/receive
- Αυτοί/Αυτές λαμβάνουν (Aftoí/Aftés lamvánoun) - They take/receive

Description: "Λαμβάνω" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It generally means "to take" or "to receive", but it can also be translated as "to get", "to acquire", or "to obtain". It is commonly used to express the action of physically taking or receiving something, as well as the concept of understanding or comprehending something.
