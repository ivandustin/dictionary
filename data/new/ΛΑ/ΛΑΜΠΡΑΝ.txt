Etymology: The word "λαμπραν" comes from the Greek word "λαμπρός" (lampros), meaning "bright" or "shining".

Grammar: "λαμπραν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "λαμπραν" refers to something or someone that is bright, shining, or radiant. It can be used to describe a physical object, such as a bright light or a shining star, or it can be used metaphorically to describe a person who is distinguished, brilliant, or outstanding.
