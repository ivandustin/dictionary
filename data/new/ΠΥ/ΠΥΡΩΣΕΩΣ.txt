Etymology: The word "πυρωσέως" (pyroseos) comes from the Greek verb "πυρόω" (pyroo), which means "to set on fire" or "to burn". 

Grammar: "Πυρωσέως" is the genitive form of the noun "πυρωσίς" (pyrosis), which means "burning" or "inflammation". In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: "Πυρωσέως" refers to the state or condition of being burned or inflamed. It is often used in medical contexts to describe the inflammation of a body part or tissue.
