Etymology: The word "πυργος" (pyrgos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πύργος" (purgos), meaning "tower". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰergʰ- which means "high" or "elevated".

Grammar: In Greek, "πυργος" (pyrgos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be declined as follows:

- Singular: ο πύργος (o pyrgos) - the tower
- Plural: οι πύργοι (oi pyrgoi) - the towers

Description: "Πυργος" (pyrgos) refers to a tower, a tall structure typically used for defensive or observation purposes. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something tall or imposing.
