Etymology: The word "πυγμή" (pygmē) comes from the Greek word "πυγμή" meaning "fist" or "punch". It is derived from the verb "πύσσω" (pyssō) meaning "to punch" or "to strike with the fist".

Grammar: In Greek, "πυγμή" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular and can be used in the nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative cases.

Description: "Πυγμή" refers to a punch or a blow with the fist. It is commonly used to describe a physical strike or attack using the fist. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a strong or forceful action or impact.
