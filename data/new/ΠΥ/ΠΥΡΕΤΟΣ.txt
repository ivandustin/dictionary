Etymology: The word "πυρετός" (pyretos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πῦρ" (pyr), meaning "fire". It is related to the concept of fever, which is characterized by an increase in body temperature.

Grammar: In Greek, "πυρετός" is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "πυρετοῦ" (pyretou).

Description: "Πυρετός" is the Greek word for "fever". It refers to an elevated body temperature that is often a symptom of an underlying illness or infection. Fever is a natural response of the body's immune system to fight off infections and other diseases. It can be accompanied by symptoms such as sweating, chills, headache, and muscle aches.
