Etymology: The word "πυκνότερον" comes from the Greek adjective "πυκνός" (pyknos), meaning "dense" or "thick". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dheu-, meaning "to thicken" or "to become solid".

Grammar: "Πυκνότερον" is the comparative form of the adjective "πυκνός" (pyknos) in Greek. It is used to compare the density or thickness of two or more things. In English, it can be translated as "denser" or "thicker".

Description: "Πυκνότερον" is an adjective that describes something that has a higher density or thickness compared to something else. It is often used to compare the concentration of substances, the closeness of objects, or the thickness of materials.
