Etymology: The word "πυθώνα" (pythōna) comes from the Greek word "πύθων" (pythōn), which means "a large snake" or "a dragon." In Greek mythology, the Python was a monstrous serpent or dragon that was slain by the god Apollo.

Grammar: "Πυθώνα" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Πυθώνα" refers to a python, which is a large non-venomous snake found in tropical regions. Pythons are known for their ability to constrict their prey and are often considered powerful and dangerous creatures. In Greek mythology, the Python was associated with the Oracle of Delphi and was believed to guard the sacred site.
