Etymology: The word "πυρινούς" (pyrinous) comes from the Greek word "πῦρ" (pyr), meaning "fire". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *péh₂wr̥, which also gave rise to the Latin word "pyra" and the English word "pyre".

Grammar: "Πυρινούς" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and accusative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The form "πυρινούς" indicates that it is modifying a masculine noun in the accusative case.

Description: The adjective "πυρινούς" describes something that is related to or made of fire. It can be used to describe objects, substances, or qualities that are associated with fire or have characteristics similar to fire.
