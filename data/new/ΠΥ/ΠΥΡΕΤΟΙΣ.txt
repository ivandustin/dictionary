Etymology: The word "πυρετός" (pyretos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πυρέσσω" (pyresso), meaning "to be on fire" or "to be feverish."

Grammar: In Greek, "πυρετός" is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "πυρετοῦ" (pyretou).

Description: "Πυρετός" is the Greek word for "fever." It refers to an elevated body temperature, often accompanied by symptoms such as sweating, chills, and general discomfort. Fever is a common response to infections, inflammation, or other medical conditions.
