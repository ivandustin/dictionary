Etymology: The word "πυρος" (pyros) comes from the Greek language and means "fire".

Grammar: In Greek, "πυρος" (pyros) is a noun of the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Πυρος" (pyros) refers to fire in the Greek language. It is a fundamental element and has been of great significance in various cultures throughout history. Fire is associated with heat, light, energy, and transformation. It plays a crucial role in cooking, providing warmth, and enabling various industrial processes. In Greek mythology, fire was considered a divine element and was associated with the god Hephaestus.
