Etymology: The word "πυρ" (pyr) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "péh₂wr" meaning "fire".

Grammar: In Greek, "πυρ" (pyr) is a noun of neuter gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "πῦρ" (pyr).

Description: "Πυρ" (pyr) means "fire" in Greek. It is a fundamental element and has been of great significance in various cultures throughout history. In Greek mythology, the god Hephaestus was associated with fire and was believed to have the power to control it. Fire has been used for cooking, heating, and providing light since ancient times. It is also a symbol of transformation and purification.
