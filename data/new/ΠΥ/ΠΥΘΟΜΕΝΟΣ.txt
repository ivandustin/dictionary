Etymology: The word "πυθομενος" (pythomenos) comes from the Greek verb "πυθομαι" (pythomai), which means "to inquire" or "to ask". It is derived from the name of the Greek god Apollo, who was also known as Pythios, the god of prophecy and oracles.

Grammar: "Πυθομενος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (πυθομενου), dative (πυθομενῳ), accusative (πυθομενον), and vocative (πυθομενε).

Description: "Πυθομενος" refers to someone who is inquiring or asking questions. It can be used to describe a person who is seeking knowledge or information, or someone who is curious and inquisitive.
