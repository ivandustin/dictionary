Etymology: The word "πυρετών" (pyreton) comes from the Greek word "πυρέτης" (pyretes), which means "fever". 

Grammar: "Πυρετών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. 

Description: "Πυρετών" refers to a person who has a fever. It is commonly used in medical contexts to describe someone who is experiencing an elevated body temperature as a result of an illness or infection.
