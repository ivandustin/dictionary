Etymology: The word "νυμφωνος" (nymphōnos) comes from the Greek word "νυμφη" (nymphē), meaning "bride" or "nymph."

Grammar: "Νυμφωνος" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, singular form.

Description: "Νυμφωνος" refers to something that is related to or associated with a bride or a nymph. It can be used to describe various aspects related to weddings, marriage, or nymphs in Greek mythology.
