Etymology: The word "νυμφαν" (nymphan) comes from the Greek word "νύμφη" (nymphi), meaning "bride" or "nymph". 

Grammar: "Νυμφαν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Νυμφαν" refers to a bride or a nymph in Greek mythology. In ancient Greek culture, nymphs were female nature spirits associated with various natural features such as trees, mountains, and bodies of water. They were often depicted as beautiful young women. In the context of a bride, "νυμφαν" refers to a woman who is about to get married or has recently been married.
