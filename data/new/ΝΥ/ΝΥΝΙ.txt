Etymology: The word "νυνί" (nyni) comes from the Ancient Greek word "νῦν" (nyn), meaning "now" or "at present."

Grammar: "Νυνί" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to indicate the present time or the current state of affairs.

Description: "Νυνί" is commonly used in Greek to express the current moment or to refer to something happening at the present time. It can be used in various contexts, such as in everyday conversations, news reports, or written texts.
