Etymology: The word "ζευγος" (zeugos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ζεύγνυμι" (zeugnumi), which means "to yoke" or "to join together."

Grammar: In Greek, "ζευγος" (zeugos) is a noun of neuter gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "ζευγη" (zeugē).

Description: "ζευγος" (zeugos) refers to a pair or a couple. It is often used to describe a pair of animals that are yoked together, such as oxen or horses. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a pair or a couple of people who are closely associated or connected.
