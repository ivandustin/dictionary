Etymology: The word "πασχετε" (pronounced "paschete") comes from the Greek verb "πάσχω" (paskho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." 

Grammar: "Πασχετε" is the second person plural form of the verb "πάσχω" in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all suffer" or "you all experience."

Description: "Πασχετε" is a verb in the Greek language that expresses the action of suffering or experiencing something. It can be used in various contexts, such as physical or emotional suffering, or going through a particular experience.
