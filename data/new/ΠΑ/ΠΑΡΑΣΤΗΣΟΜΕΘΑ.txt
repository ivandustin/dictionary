Etymology: The word "παραστησομεθα" comes from the Greek verb "παρίστημι" (paristēmi), which means "to present" or "to exhibit". It is formed by combining the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb "ἵστημι" (histēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: "παραστησομεθα" is a first-person plural future middle indicative form of the verb "παρίστημι" (paristēmi). In Greek grammar, the future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on or for themselves, and the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "παραστησομεθα" can be translated as "we will present ourselves" or "we will exhibit ourselves". It implies that the subject will actively present or exhibit themselves in a certain way or in a certain situation.
