Etymology: The word "παρεσκευασταί" comes from the Greek verb "παρεσκευάζω" (pareskevazo), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready." The suffix "-σταί" (-stai) indicates a plural form.

Grammar: "Παρεσκευασταί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word "παρεσκευασταί" is in the nominative case and refers to a group of people who are prepared or ready.

Description: "Παρεσκευασταί" can be translated as "those who are prepared" or "those who are ready." It is used to describe a group of people who have made the necessary preparations or are ready for a particular task or event.
