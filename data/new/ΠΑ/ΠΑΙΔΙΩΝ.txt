Etymology: The word "παίδιον" (paídion) comes from the Greek word "παῖς" (paîs), meaning "child" or "young person."

Grammar: "Παίδιον" is a neuter noun in Greek. It is the diminutive form of "παῖς" (paîs), which means it is a smaller or more affectionate version of the word "child."

Description: "Παίδιον" refers to a young child or a little one. It is often used to express endearment or affection towards a child.
