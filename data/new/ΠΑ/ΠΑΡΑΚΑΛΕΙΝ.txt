Etymology: The word "παρακαλείν" (parakalein) comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "καλεῖν" (kalein), meaning "to call." It can be translated as "to call to one's side" or "to appeal to."

Grammar: "Παρακαλεῖν" (parakalein) is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is often used in the imperative form to express a request or plea.

Description: "Παρακαλεῖν" (parakalein) is a versatile word that can have various meanings depending on the context. It can mean "to ask," "to request," "to beg," "to implore," or "to beseech." It is often used to express a polite or formal request for assistance, favor, or advice.
