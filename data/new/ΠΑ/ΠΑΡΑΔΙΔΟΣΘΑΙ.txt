Etymology: The word "παραδιδοσθαι" (paradidothai) comes from the Greek verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver."

Grammar: "Παραδιδοσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi). In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Παραδιδοσθαι" is an ancient Greek verb form that expresses the action of handing over or delivering something. It can be used in various contexts, such as delivering a message, transferring ownership, or surrendering.
