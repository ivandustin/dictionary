Etymology: The word "πανουργίαν" (panourgían) comes from the Greek word "πανοῦργος" (panoûrgos), which means "crafty" or "cunning". It is derived from the combination of "πᾶν" (pân), meaning "all" or "every", and "ἔργον" (érgon), meaning "work" or "deed".

Grammar: "Πανουργίαν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Πανουργίαν" refers to the quality or characteristic of being crafty, cunning, or sly. It describes someone who is skilled at using deceit or trickery to achieve their goals.
