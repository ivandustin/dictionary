Etymology: The word "παροικούς" (paroikoús) comes from the Greek root words "παρά" (pará), meaning "beside" or "near," and "οἰκέω" (oikéō), meaning "to dwell" or "to inhabit." It refers to someone who lives near or beside a particular place.

Grammar: In Greek, "παροικούς" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the plural form of the word "παροικός" (paroikós), which means "neighbor" or "resident."

Description: "Παροικούς" is used to describe people who live near or beside a specific location. It can refer to neighbors or residents of a particular area.
