Etymology: The word "πασαίς" (pasaís) comes from the Greek word "πᾶς" (pâs), meaning "all" or "every." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pant-.

Grammar: "Πασαίς" (pasaís) is the feminine form of the adjective "πᾶς" (pâs) in the dative plural case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. In this case, "πασαίς" is used to describe or modify a feminine plural noun in the dative case.

Description: "Πασαίς" (pasaís) means "to all" or "to every" in Greek. It is used to indicate that something is directed or applies to all or every member of a group.
