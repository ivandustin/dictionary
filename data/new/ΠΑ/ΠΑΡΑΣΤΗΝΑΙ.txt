Etymology: The word "παραστηναι" comes from the Greek verb "παρίστημι" (paristēmi), which means "to present" or "to stand before". 

Grammar: "παραστηναι" is the aorist infinitive form of the verb "παρίστημι" (paristēmi). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not conjugated for a specific subject or tense.
