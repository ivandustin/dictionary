Etymology: The word "παραβατης" (paravatis) comes from the Greek word "παραβαίνω" (paravaino), which means "to transgress" or "to violate". 

Grammar: In Greek, "παραβατης" is a noun of masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "παραβατης". 

Description: "Παραβατης" is a Greek word that translates to "transgressor" or "violator" in English. It refers to someone who breaks or violates rules, laws, or boundaries.
