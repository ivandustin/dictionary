Etymology: The word "παροντες" (parontes) comes from the Greek verb "παρέρχομαι" (parerchomai), which means "to pass by" or "to go past." It is derived from the prefix "παρ-" (par-), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go."

Grammar: "Παροντες" is the present participle form of the verb "παρέρχομαι" (parerchomai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective, noun, or part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Παροντες" can be translated as "those who are passing by" or "those who are going past." It refers to individuals who are currently in the act of passing by or going past a certain location or situation. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing people walking by a street, individuals moving through a crowd, or participants in a procession.
