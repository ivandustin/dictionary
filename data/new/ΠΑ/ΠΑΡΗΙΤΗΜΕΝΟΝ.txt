Etymology: The word "παρηγορητήμενον" (parēgorētēmenon) comes from the Greek verb "παρηγορέω" (parēgoreō), which means "to console" or "to comfort." The suffix "-μενον" (-menon) is a passive participle ending, indicating that the word is in the passive voice.

Grammar: "Παρηγορητήμενον" is a Greek word in the neuter gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is a passive participle, indicating that the subject of the verb is receiving the action.

Description: "Παρηγορητήμενον" is an adjective that describes something or someone that has been consoled or comforted. It can be used to describe a person who has received comfort or a situation that has been alleviated or made less distressing.
