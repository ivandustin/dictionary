Etymology: The word "πατείν" (pronounced "patin") comes from the Greek verb "πατέω" (pateo), which means "to tread" or "to walk." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pent-.

Grammar: "Πατείν" is the present tense, active voice, indicative mood form of the verb "πατέω" (pateo) in Greek. It is the third person singular form, used to describe an action performed by a singular subject in the present time.

Description: "Πατείν" is a verb in Greek that means "he/she/it treads" or "he/she/it walks." It is used to describe the action of stepping or walking on a surface.
