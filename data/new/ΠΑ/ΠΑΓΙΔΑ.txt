Etymology: The word "παγίδα" (págida) comes from the Greek verb "παγιδεύω" (pagidévo), which means "to trap" or "to ensnare". It is derived from the noun "παγίς" (págis), meaning "snare" or "trap".

Grammar: In Greek, "παγίδα" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a singular and plural form. The plural form is "παγίδες" (págides).

Description: "Παγίδα" refers to a trap or snare that is used to catch or capture something, typically animals. It can be a physical device or a metaphorical concept used to describe a situation or circumstance that is designed to deceive or entrap someone.
