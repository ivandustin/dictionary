Etymology: The word "παρακοήν" (parakoín) comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "κοῖνος" (koinos), meaning "common" or "shared." 

Grammar: "Παρακοήν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. It is derived from the verb "παρακούω" (parakoúo), which means "to overhear" or "to listen secretly." 

Description: "Παρακοήν" refers to the act of eavesdropping or listening in on a conversation without the knowledge or consent of the speakers. It implies a secretive or hidden form of listening.
