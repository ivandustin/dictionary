Etymology: The word "παραπλησίων" comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "near," and "πλησίος" (plēsios), meaning "close" or "near." 

Grammar: "Παραπλησίων" is a noun in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it refers to something or someone that is close or near.

Description: "Παραπλησίων" is used to describe something or someone that is in close proximity or similarity to another thing or person. It implies a nearness or resemblance in terms of characteristics, qualities, or attributes.
