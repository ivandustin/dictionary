Etymology: The word "παρακαλείσθε" (parakaleisthe) comes from the Greek verb "παρακαλώ" (parakalo), which means "to ask, to request, to beg." It is derived from the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "καλώ" (kalo), meaning "to call."

Grammar: "Παρακαλείσθε" is the second person plural form of the verb "παρακαλώ" (parakalo) in the present tense, passive voice, and imperative mood. It is used to address a group of people and means "you are being asked" or "please."

Description: "Παρακαλείσθε" is a polite and formal way of asking or requesting something in Greek. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as when making a polite request, inviting someone, or asking for assistance.
