Etymology: The word "παρρησία" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παρ-" meaning "beside" or "alongside" and the noun "ῥῆσις" meaning "speech" or "utterance".

Grammar: In Greek, "παρρησία" is a feminine noun. It is declined in various cases and numbers to indicate different grammatical functions within a sentence.

Description: "παρρησία" is a Greek word that can be translated as "frankness", "boldness", "candor", or "freedom of speech". It refers to the act of speaking openly and honestly, without fear or hesitation. In ancient Greece, "παρρησία" was highly valued as a virtue and was associated with democratic ideals. It was considered essential for citizens to express their opinions freely and participate in public discourse.
