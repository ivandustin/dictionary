Etymology: The word "παίδαριον" (pronounced "paidarion") comes from the Greek word "παῖς" (pronounced "pais"), meaning "child" or "boy." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₂- ("to protect, feed, guard"), which also gave rise to words like "pedagogue" and "pediatrician."

Grammar: In Greek, "παίδαριον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "παιδαρίου" (pronounced "paidariou").

Description: "Παίδαριον" refers to a young boy or child, typically between the ages of 7 and 12. It is often used affectionately or playfully to refer to a young boy.
