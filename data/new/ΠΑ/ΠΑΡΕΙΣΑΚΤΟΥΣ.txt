Etymology: The word "παρεισακτος" (pareisaktos) comes from the Greek words "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "εἰσάγω" (eisago), meaning "to bring in" or "to introduce." 

Grammar: "Παρεισακτος" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It is used to describe something or someone that has been brought in or introduced alongside something else.

Description: The word "παρεισακτος" is often used in Greek to describe something or someone that has been added or included in a situation or group. It implies that the thing or person was not originally part of the situation, but has been brought in or introduced later.
