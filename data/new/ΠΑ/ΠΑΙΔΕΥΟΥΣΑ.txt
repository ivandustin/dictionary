Etymology: The word "παιδευούσα" (paideuousa) comes from the Greek verb "παιδεύω" (paideuo), which means "to educate" or "to teach." It is derived from the noun "παῖς" (pais), meaning "child" or "student."

Grammar: "Παιδευούσα" is the present participle form of the verb "παιδεύω" (paideuo) in the feminine gender. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently educating or teaching.

Description: "Παιδευούσα" refers to a woman who is actively engaged in the act of educating or teaching. It implies that she is actively involved in the process of imparting knowledge, skills, or values to others, particularly to children or students.
