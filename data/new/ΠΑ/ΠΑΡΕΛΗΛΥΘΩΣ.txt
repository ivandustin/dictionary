Etymology: The word "παρεληλυθως" (parelēlythōs) comes from the Greek verb "παρέρχομαι" (parerchomai), which means "to pass by" or "to go past." The word is formed by adding the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "beyond," to the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), meaning "to come" or "to go."

Grammar: "Παρεληλυθως" is an adverb in the Greek language. It is derived from the perfect tense of the verb "παρέρχομαι" (parerchomai), indicating that the action of passing by or going past has been completed in the past.

Description: "Παρεληλυθως" is used to describe an action that has already taken place, specifically referring to something that has passed by or gone past. It can be used to indicate the passage of time or the occurrence of an event in the past.
