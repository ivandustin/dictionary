Etymology: The word "παιδιοθέν" comes from the Greek words "παιδίον" (paidion), meaning "child," and "θέν" (then), meaning "from." 

Grammar: "παιδιοθέν" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe something that has been done or acquired since childhood.

Description: "παιδιοθέν" refers to something that has been present or ingrained since childhood. It is often used to emphasize the long-lasting or deeply rooted nature of a characteristic, habit, or skill that has been developed or acquired from an early age.
