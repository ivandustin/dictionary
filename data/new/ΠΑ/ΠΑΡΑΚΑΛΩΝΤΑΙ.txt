Etymology: The word "παρακαλώνται" comes from the Greek verb "παρακαλώ" (parakaló), which means "to ask" or "to request." The suffix "-νται" (-ntai) is added to the verb stem to indicate the passive voice, making the word mean "they are asked" or "they are requested."

Grammar: "Παρακαλώνται" is the third person plural form of the verb "παρακαλώ" in the passive voice. It is used to refer to a group of people who are being asked or requested to do something.

Description: "Παρακαλώνται" is a Greek word that is commonly used in polite requests or invitations. It is often used in signs, announcements, or formal situations to ask people to do something or follow certain rules.
