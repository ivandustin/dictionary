Etymology: The word "παραδοθέσθε" comes from the Greek verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi), which means "to deliver" or "to hand over." It is formed by combining the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), meaning "to give."

Grammar: "Παραδοθέσθε" is a verb in the 2nd person plural, aorist tense, middle voice, and imperative mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "Παραδοθέσθε" is a Greek word that translates to "deliver yourselves" or "hand yourselves over." It is often used in a religious or moral context, urging individuals to surrender or submit themselves to a higher authority or to a specific cause.
