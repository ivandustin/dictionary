Etymology: The word "παρανομων" (paranomon) comes from the Greek words "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "against," and "νόμος" (nomos), meaning "law." Therefore, "παρανομων" can be translated as "against the law" or "unlawful."

Grammar: In Greek, "παρανομων" is a noun in the plural form. It is declined as a third declension noun, with the genitive form being "παρανόμων" (paranomon).

Description: "παρανομων" is used to describe something or someone that is in violation of the law or is considered illegal. It can refer to actions, behaviors, or objects that are not permitted by the legal system.
