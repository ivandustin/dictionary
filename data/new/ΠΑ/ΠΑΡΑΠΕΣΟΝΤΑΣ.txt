Etymology: The word "παραπεσοντας" (parapesontas) comes from the Greek verb "παραπίπτω" (parapipto), which means "to fall beside" or "to deviate from the right path." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and the verb "πίπτω" (pipto), meaning "to fall."

Grammar: "Παραπεσοντας" is the present participle form of the verb "παραπίπτω" (parapipto) in the masculine singular form. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Παραπεσοντας" can be translated as "falling beside" or "deviating." It describes someone or something that is currently in the act of falling beside or deviating from the right path or course of action.
