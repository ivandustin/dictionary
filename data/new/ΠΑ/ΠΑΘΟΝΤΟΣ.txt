Grammar: "Παθοντος" is the genitive form of the noun "πάθος" (pathos), which means "suffering" or "passion" in Greek. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Παθοντος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "of suffering" or "of passion" in English. It is often used in philosophical and literary contexts to refer to intense emotions, experiences, or states of being.
