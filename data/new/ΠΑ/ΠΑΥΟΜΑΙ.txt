Etymology: The word "παυομαι" (pronounced "pauomai") comes from the Greek verb "παύω" (pronounced "pao"), which means "to stop" or "to cease." 

Grammar: "Παυομαι" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. It is conjugated in various tenses, moods, and voices to express different meanings and nuances.

Description: "Παυομαι" means "to stop" or "to cease" in Greek. It can be used in various contexts, such as stopping an activity, ceasing an action, or taking a break. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate the end or cessation of something.
