Etymology: The word "παραφερομέναι" comes from the Greek verb "παραφέρω" (paraphero), which means "to carry along" or "to bring with." The suffix "-μέναι" (-menai) is a present participle ending, indicating ongoing action.

Grammar: "Παραφερομέναι" is the present participle form of the verb "παραφέρω" (paraphero) in the feminine plural. It is used to describe multiple female subjects who are currently carrying or bringing something along.

Description: "Παραφερομέναι" is a Greek word that can be translated as "carrying" or "bringing along" in English. It refers to multiple female subjects who are actively engaged in the action of carrying or bringing something with them.
