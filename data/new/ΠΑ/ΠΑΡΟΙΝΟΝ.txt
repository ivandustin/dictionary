Etymology: The word "παροινον" (paroinon) comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "οἶνος" (oinos), meaning "wine." It literally translates to "beside wine" or "drinking alongside wine."

Grammar: "Παροινον" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is classified as a neuter noun. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "παροινον."

Description: "Παροινον" refers to a person who drinks alongside others, often in a festive or social setting. It can also be used to describe someone who is a companion or a drinking buddy.
