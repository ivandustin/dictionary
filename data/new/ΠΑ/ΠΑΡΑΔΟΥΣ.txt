Etymology: The word "παραδούς" (paradous) comes from the Greek verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi), which means "to give over" or "to hand down." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), meaning "to give."

Grammar: "Παραδούς" is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word "παραδούς" is masculine, singular, and in the nominative case.

Description: "Παραδούς" refers to someone who hands over or delivers something to another person. It can also be used to describe someone who is a messenger or a bearer of news. The word can have various connotations depending on the context in which it is used.
