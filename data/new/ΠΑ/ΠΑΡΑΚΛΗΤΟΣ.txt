Etymology: The word "παρακλητος" (paraklētos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "παρακαλέω" (parakaleō), which means "to call to one's side" or "to comfort." In ancient Greek, the term was used to refer to someone who was called to assist or advocate for another person.

Grammar: In Greek, "παρακλητος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. The word can also be declined in different cases, such as genitive, dative, and accusative, depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Παρακλητος" is a Greek word that has several meanings. It can be translated as "comforter," "advocate," "helper," or "counselor." In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the Holy Spirit, who is seen as a helper and advocate for believers.
