Etymology: The word "πατέρες" (pateres) comes from the Greek language. It is the plural form of the noun "πατήρ" (pater), which means "father".

Grammar: In Greek, "πατέρες" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the plural form of "πατήρ". The word belongs to the second declension, which means that its ending changes depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: "Πατέρες" is the Greek word for "fathers". It refers to multiple male parents or ancestors. In Greek culture, fathers hold an important role in the family and are often seen as the head of the household.
