Etymology: The word "παροδων" (parodon) comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "ὁδός" (hodos), meaning "road" or "way." 

Grammar: "Παροδων" is a noun in the genitive case, singular number, masculine gender. It is the genitive form of the noun "παροδος" (parodos), which means "passage" or "way through." 

Description: "Παροδων" refers to a specific passage or way through something, often used in the context of theater to describe the entrance or exit of actors on stage. It can also be used more generally to refer to a path or route.
