Etymology: The word "πατροιος" (patroios) comes from the Greek roots "πατήρ" (patēr), meaning "father," and "οἶκος" (oikos), meaning "house." It refers to something that is related to or characteristic of one's father or ancestors.

Grammar: In Greek, "πατροιος" is an adjective in the masculine form. It can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πατροιος" is used to describe something that is inherited or passed down from one's father or ancestors. It can refer to traits, characteristics, customs, traditions, or possessions that are associated with one's paternal lineage.
