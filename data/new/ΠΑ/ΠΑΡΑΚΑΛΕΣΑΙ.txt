Etymology: The word "παρακαλέσαι" comes from the Greek verb "παρακαλώ" (parakaló), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is derived from the prefix "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "καλώ" (kaló), meaning "to call" or "to summon."

Grammar: "παρακαλέσαι" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "παρακαλώ" (parakaló). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb and does not indicate tense, person, or number.

Description: "παρακαλέσαι" is a Greek verb that means "to ask" or "to request." It is used to express the act of making a plea or appeal to someone for something. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as asking for help, making a request, or seeking assistance.
