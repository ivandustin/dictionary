Etymology: The word "παραθήκην" (parathēkēn) comes from the Greek verb "παρατίθημι" (paratithēmi), which means "to place beside" or "to deposit." It is derived from the prefix "παρά" (para), meaning "beside," and the verb "τίθημι" (tithēmi), meaning "to place."

Grammar: "Παραθήκην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek grammar, the accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Παραθήκην" refers to a deposit, a trust, or something that is entrusted to someone for safekeeping. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is passed down or handed over from one generation to another.
