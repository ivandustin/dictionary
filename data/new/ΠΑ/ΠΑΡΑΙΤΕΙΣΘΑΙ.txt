Etymology: The word "παραίτεισθαι" comes from the Greek verb "παραιτέομαι" (paraitéomai), which means "to excuse oneself" or "to ask for forgiveness." It is derived from the prefix "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "αἰτέω" (aitéō), meaning "to ask" or "to request."

Grammar: "παραίτεισθαι" is a verb in the middle voice, which indicates that the subject is both performing and receiving the action. It is conjugated in the present tense, third person singular form.

Description: "παραίτεισθαι" is an ancient Greek verb that is used to express the act of excusing oneself or asking for forgiveness. It implies a sense of humility and acknowledging one's wrongdoing or mistake.
