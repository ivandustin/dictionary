Etymology: The word "παραδούναι" (paradounai) comes from the Greek verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi), which means "to give over" or "to hand over". It is derived from the combination of the preposition "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), meaning "to give".

Grammar: "παραδούναι" is the infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, and voice. The infinitive form is used to express the action of the verb in a general or abstract sense, without specifying the subject or the time of the action.

Description: "παραδούναι" is a Greek verb that conveys the idea of giving something over or handing something over to someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as giving a gift, transferring ownership, or delivering a message. The verb can also be used metaphorically to express the act of surrendering or yielding something, both in a physical and abstract sense.
