Etymology: The word "πανουργία" comes from the Greek roots "παν" (pan), meaning "all" or "every," and "οὐργία" (ourgia), meaning "work" or "action." 

Grammar: In Greek, "πανουργία" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular and can be used in the nominative, accusative, or vocative case.

Description: "Πανουργία" refers to cunning, craftiness, or deceitfulness. It describes the ability to use clever and often deceptive tactics to achieve one's goals. It can also imply a sense of shrewdness or resourcefulness in dealing with difficult situations.
