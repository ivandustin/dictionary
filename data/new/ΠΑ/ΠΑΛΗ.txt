Etymology: The word "παλη" (pali) comes from the Greek word "παλαιός" (palaíos), meaning "old" or "ancient".

Grammar: "Παλη" (pali) is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun and belongs to the first declension. In the singular, it is declined as follows: η παλη (i pali) - the old woman, την παλη (tin pali) - the old woman (accusative case), της παλης (tis palis) - of the old woman (genitive case), την παλη (tin pali) - to the old woman (dative case).

Description: "Παλη" (pali) refers to an old woman in Greek. It is used to describe a female person who is advanced in age.
