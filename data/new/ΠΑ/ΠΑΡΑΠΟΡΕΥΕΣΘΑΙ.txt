Etymology: The word "παραπορευεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "παραπορεύομαι" (paraporeúomai), which means "to pass by, to go past". It is derived from the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb "πορεύομαι" (poreúomai), meaning "to go, to travel".

Grammar: "παραπορευεσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense indicates an ongoing action, and the middle/passive voice suggests that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "παραπορευεσθαι" describes the act of passing by or going past something. It implies movement or travel alongside or beside something.
