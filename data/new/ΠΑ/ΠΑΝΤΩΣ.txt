Etymology: The word "παντως" (pantos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παν-" (pan-), meaning "all" or "every," and the word "τος" (tos), which is a form of the definite article "ο" (o) meaning "the." 

Grammar: "Παντως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to express certainty, agreement, or emphasis. It can be translated as "definitely," "certainly," "for sure," or "in any case."

Description: "Παντως" is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday Greek conversation. It is often used to confirm or emphasize a statement, to express agreement, or to indicate that something is true regardless of other factors. It can also be used to transition between topics or to conclude a conversation.
