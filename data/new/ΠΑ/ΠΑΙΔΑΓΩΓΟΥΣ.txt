Etymology: The word "παιδαγωγούς" comes from the Greek roots "παιδί" (paidi), meaning "child," and "ἀγωγός" (agogos), meaning "leader" or "guide." 

Grammar: "Παιδαγωγούς" is a noun in the plural form. It is the accusative case of the noun "παιδαγωγός" (paidagogos), which means "teacher" or "instructor" in Greek. 

Description: "Παιδαγωγούς" refers to a group of teachers or instructors who guide and educate children. It can be used to describe educators in various settings, such as schools, tutoring centers, or even parents who take on the role of teaching their children.
