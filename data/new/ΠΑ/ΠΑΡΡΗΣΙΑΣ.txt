Etymology: The word "παρρησίας" comes from the Greek word "παρρησία" (parrhēsía), which means "frankness" or "boldness". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "beyond", and the noun "ῥῆσις" (rhēsis), meaning "speech" or "utterance".

Grammar: "Παρρησίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular number, masculine gender. In Greek grammar, nouns decline according to case, number, and gender. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Παρρησίας" refers to the quality or state of being frank, outspoken, or bold in speech. It conveys the idea of speaking freely and openly, without fear or hesitation. The term is often associated with the concept of free speech and the courage to express one's opinions or beliefs, even in the face of opposition or potential consequences.
