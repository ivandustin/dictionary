Etymology: The word "παταξομεν" (pataxomen) comes from the Greek verb "πατάσσω" (patasso), which means "to strike" or "to hit". The suffix "-μεν" (-men) indicates the future tense, so "παταξομεν" (pataxomen) translates to "we will strike" or "we will hit".

Grammar: "παταξομεν" (pataxomen) is a first-person plural form of the future tense in Greek. It is used to express an action that will happen in the future, specifically when the subject is "we".
