Etymology: The word "παραγγειλαντες" comes from the Greek verb "παραγγέλλω" (parangéllō), which means "to order" or "to command". The suffix "-αντες" (-antes) is a participle ending, indicating the past tense and plural form.

Grammar: "Παραγγειλαντες" is the past participle form of the verb "παραγγέλλω" (parangéllō) in the masculine plural. It is used to describe a group of people who have ordered or commanded something in the past.

Description: "Παραγγειλαντες" refers to a group of individuals who have given orders or commands. It can be used to describe a specific group of people or to refer to a general concept of individuals who have engaged in ordering or commanding actions.
