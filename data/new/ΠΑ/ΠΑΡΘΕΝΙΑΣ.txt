Etymology: The word "παρθενιας" (parthenias) comes from the Greek word "παρθένος" (parthenos), which means "virgin" or "maiden".

Grammar: "Παρθενιας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender.

Description: "Παρθενιας" refers to a male virgin or a young man who has not yet engaged in sexual activity. It is often used in the context of discussing purity or chastity.
