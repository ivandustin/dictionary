Etymology: The word "παραγγελλω" (parangello) comes from the Greek roots "παρα" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "αγγελλω" (angello), meaning "to announce" or "to declare." 

Grammar: "παραγγελλω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: εγώ παραγγέλνω (ego parangello) - I order, εσύ παραγγέλνεις (esi parangellis) - you order, αυτός/αυτή/αυτό παραγγέλνει (aftos/afti/afto parangelli) - he/she/it orders, εμείς παραγγέλνουμε (emeis parangelloume) - we order, εσείς παραγγέλνετε (esies parangellnete) - you order, αυτοί/αυτές παραγγέλνουν (aftoi/aftes parangellnun) - they order.

Description: "παραγγελλω" means "to order" or "to command" in Greek. It is used to express the act of giving instructions or directives to someone.
