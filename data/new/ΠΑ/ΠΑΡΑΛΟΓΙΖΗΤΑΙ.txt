Etymology: The word "παραλογισμός" (paralogismos) comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and "λογισμός" (logismos), meaning "reasoning" or "thought." 

Grammar: "παραλογισμός" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the second declension.

Description: "παραλογισμός" refers to a fallacious or illogical argument or reasoning. It is used to describe a flawed or misleading line of thinking that does not follow proper logic or reasoning principles.
