Etymology: The word "παραπλησιως" (paraplisios) comes from the Greek roots "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "near," and "πλησιος" (plisios), meaning "close" or "near." 

Grammar: "παραπλησιως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe actions or states that are done or occur in a similar or close manner.

Description: "παραπλησιως" is used to express similarity or closeness in Greek. It is often used to compare two actions or states that are similar or close in nature. It can also be used to indicate approximation or nearness in degree or quantity.
