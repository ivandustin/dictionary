Etymology: The word "παραπτώμα" (paraptoma) comes from the Greek verb "παραπίπτω" (parapipto), which means "to fall beside" or "to stumble." It is derived from the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "πίπτω" (pipto), meaning "to fall."

Grammar: "Παραπτώμα" is a noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the neuter gender. It can be declined according to the case and number, such as "το παράπτωμα" (the mistake) in the nominative case, "του παραπτώματος" (of the mistake) in the genitive case, and so on.

Description: "Παραπτώμα" refers to a mistake, error, or fault. It is used to describe an action or decision that deviates from what is considered correct or acceptable. It can also refer to a moral or ethical transgression.
