Etymology: The word "πασχω" (pascho) comes from the Greek verb "πάσχω" (paskho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *peh₃- ("to hurt," "to suffer").

- Πάσχω (pascho) - I suffer
- Πάσχεις (pascheis) - You suffer (singular)
- Πάσχει (paschei) - He/she/it suffers
- Πάσχομεν (paschomen) - We suffer
- Πάσχετε (paschete) - You suffer (plural)
- Πάσχουσι(ν) (paschousi(n)) - They suffer
