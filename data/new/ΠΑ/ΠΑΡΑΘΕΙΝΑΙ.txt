Etymology: The word "παραθείναι" (paratheínai) comes from the Greek verb "παρατίθημι" (paratíthēmi), which means "to place beside" or "to present."

Grammar: "Παραθείναι" is the infinitive form of the verb and belongs to the first conjugation class in Greek. It is an active verb, meaning that it describes an action performed by the subject.

Description: "Παραθείναι" is a Greek verb that is commonly translated as "to offer" or "to present." It is used to express the act of giving or presenting something to someone. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as offering a gift, presenting an idea, or extending an invitation.
