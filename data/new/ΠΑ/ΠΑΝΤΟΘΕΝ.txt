Etymology: The word "παντοθεν" comes from the Greek roots "παν" (pan), meaning "all" or "every," and "θεν" (then), meaning "from" or "origin." Therefore, "παντοθεν" can be translated as "from all sides" or "from everywhere."

Grammar: "παντοθεν" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe the origin or source of something, indicating that it comes from all directions or from various places.

Description: "παντοθεν" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It emphasizes the idea of something coming from all sides or from multiple sources. It can be used to describe the origin of an idea, a phenomenon, or a situation.
