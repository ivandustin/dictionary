Etymology: The word "παίδαγωγος" comes from the Greek words "παῖς" (païs) meaning "child" and "ἄγω" (agō) meaning "to lead". It literally translates to "one who leads children".

Grammar: In Greek, "παίδαγωγος" is a noun. It is a masculine noun in the singular form.

Description: "παίδαγωγος" refers to a person who educates, guides, and takes care of children. It is commonly used to describe a teacher or an educator who is responsible for the overall development and well-being of children.
