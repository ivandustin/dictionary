Etymology: The word "παθοντας" (pathontas) comes from the Greek verb "πάσχω" (pascho), which means "to suffer" or "to experience." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pent-, meaning "to suffer" or "to endure."

Grammar: "Παθοντας" is the present participle form of the verb "πάσχω" (pascho) in the Greek language. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently experiencing or undergoing an action or state of suffering.

Description: "Παθοντας" is a Greek word that can be translated as "suffering" or "experiencing." It is often used to describe someone who is going through a difficult or painful situation. In Greek literature and philosophy, the concept of "pathos" (suffering) is often explored as a fundamental aspect of human existence and a source of wisdom and understanding.
