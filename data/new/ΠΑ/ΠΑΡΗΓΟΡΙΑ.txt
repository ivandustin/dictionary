Etymology: The word "παρηγορία" comes from the Greek word "παρηγορέω" (parēgoreō), which means "to console" or "to comfort". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb "ἀγορεύω" (agoreuō), meaning "to speak" or "to address".

Grammar: In Greek, "παρηγορία" is a feminine noun. It belongs to the first declension and has the plural form "παρηγορίες" (parēgories). It can be used in both the singular and plural forms depending on the context.

Description: "Παρηγορία" refers to the act or process of providing comfort, solace, or consolation to someone who is experiencing grief, sadness, or distress. It can also refer to the feeling of relief or consolation that one receives from someone or something. The word is often used in the context of emotional support and empathy.
