Etymology: The word "παρεκαλούν" comes from the Greek verb "παρακαλώ" (parakaló), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is derived from the combination of the preposition "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "near," and the verb "καλώ" (kaló), meaning "to call" or "to invite."

Grammar: "Παρεκαλούν" is the third person plural form of the verb "παρακαλώ" in the present tense. It is used to indicate that multiple people are asking or requesting something.

Description: "Παρεκαλούν" is a Greek word that translates to "they ask" or "they request" in English. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and formal situations to express a polite request or invitation made by a group of people.
