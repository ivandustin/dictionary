Etymology: The word "παρεδεχθησαν" (paredechthēsan) comes from the Greek verb "παραδέχομαι" (paradechomai), which means "to accept" or "to receive."

Grammar: "παρεδεχθησαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense, passive voice form of the verb "παραδέχομαι" (paradechomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe past actions that are viewed as a whole, without specifying their duration or completion. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is receiving the action.

Description: "παρεδεχθησαν" is a Greek word that means "they accepted" or "they received." It is used to describe an action in the past where a group of people accepted or received something.
