Etymology: The word "παρακοής" (parakoēs) comes from the Greek root "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and "κοή" (koē), meaning "hearing." It literally translates to "beyond hearing" or "overhearing."

Grammar: "Παρακοής" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Παρακοής" refers to the act of overhearing or eavesdropping. It describes the unintentional act of hearing something that was not intended for one's ears.
