Etymology: The word "παθήματων" (pathimaton) comes from the Greek root "πάθος" (pathos), meaning "suffering" or "passion," and the suffix "-ματων" (-maton), which denotes a plural form. 

Grammar: "Παθήματων" is the genitive plural form of the noun "πάθημα" (pathima), which means "suffering" or "affliction" in Greek. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Παθήματων" refers to multiple instances or types of suffering or affliction. It can be used to describe various experiences of hardship, pain, or emotional distress.
