Etymology: The word "παρρησιαζόμενος" comes from the Greek verb "παρρησιάζομαι" (parrēsiazomai), which means "to speak freely" or "to speak openly." It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παρρησία" (parrēsia), meaning "frankness" or "boldness," and the verb "αἰσιάζομαι" (aisiazomai), meaning "to speak."

Grammar: "Παρρησιαζόμενος" is the present participle form of the verb "παρρησιάζομαι" (parrēsiazomai). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Παρρησιαζόμενος" describes someone who is speaking freely or openly, without hesitation or fear of consequences. It implies a sense of boldness and frankness in expressing one's thoughts or opinions.
