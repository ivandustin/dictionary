Etymology: The word "παρακαλούντες" comes from the Greek verb "παρακαλώ" (parakaló), which means "to ask" or "to request." The suffix "-ούντες" is added to the verb stem to form the present participle, indicating that the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: "Παρακαλούντες" is the present participle form of the verb "παρακαλώ" in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently asking or requesting something. In English, it can be translated as "those who ask" or "those who are asking."

Description: "Παρακαλούντες" is a word used to refer to a group of people who are currently making requests or asking for something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Οι παρακαλούντες ζητούν βοήθεια" (The ones who are asking are seeking help).
