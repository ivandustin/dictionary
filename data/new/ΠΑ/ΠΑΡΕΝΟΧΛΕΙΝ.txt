Etymology: The word "παρενοχλείν" (parenochlein) comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "ἐνοχλεῖν" (enochlein), meaning "to annoy" or "to disturb."

Grammar: "Παρενοχλείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood.

Description: "Παρενοχλείν" means "to harass" or "to bother" in English. It refers to the act of causing annoyance or disturbance to someone.
