Etymology: The word "παίδευσας" (pronounced "paideusas") comes from the Greek verb "παιδεύω" (paideuo), which means "to educate" or "to teach." It is derived from the noun "παῖς" (pais), meaning "child" or "student."

Grammar: "Παίδευσας" is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in the active voice of the verb "παιδεύω." In Greek, verbs change their form based on the person, number, tense, voice, and mood. The aorist tense generally refers to a completed action in the past.

Description: "Παίδευσας" is a Greek word that translates to "you educated" or "you taught" in English. It is used to address someone directly and indicate that they have educated or taught someone or something in the past.
