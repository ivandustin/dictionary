Etymology: The word "παρακοή" (parakoí) comes from the Greek roots "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and "κούω" (koúo), meaning "to hear." It literally translates to "hearing beside" or "overhearing."

Grammar: "Παρακοή" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Παρακοή" refers to the act of listening or hearing something unintentionally or without being the intended recipient. It can also imply eavesdropping or overhearing a conversation.
