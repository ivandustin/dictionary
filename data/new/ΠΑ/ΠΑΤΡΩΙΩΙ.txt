Etymology: The word "πατρωιοι" (patrōioi) comes from the Greek word "πατρικός" (patrikos), meaning "paternal" or "ancestral."

Grammar: "Πατρωιοι" is a plural noun in the nominative case. It is used to refer to people who are related by blood or descent, specifically on the paternal side.

Description: "Πατρωιοι" is a term used in Greek to describe individuals who are part of the same paternal lineage or family. It emphasizes the importance of the father's side of the family and the shared ancestry among its members.
