Etymology: The word "παραδοσιν" (paradosin) comes from the Greek word "παράδοση" (paradosi), which means "tradition" or "handing down." It is derived from the verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi), meaning "to hand over" or "to transmit."

Grammar: "Παραδοσιν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Παραδοσιν" refers to the act of handing down or transmitting something, usually referring to customs, beliefs, or practices from one generation to another. It can also refer to a tradition or a body of knowledge that is passed on through generations.
