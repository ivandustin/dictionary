Etymology: The word "παροψίδος" (paropsidos) comes from the Greek word "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and "ὄψις" (opsis), meaning "sight" or "view." It refers to something that is seen or observed from the side.

Grammar: "Παροψίδος" is a noun in the feminine gender in the singular form.

Description: "Παροψίδος" is a Greek word that can be translated as "sidelong glance" or "sideways look." It refers to a quick or casual glance that is made from the corner of the eye, without turning the head. It is often used to describe a look that is furtive, sly, or secretive.
