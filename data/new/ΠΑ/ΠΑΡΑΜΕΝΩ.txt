Etymology: The word "παραμένω" (paraméno) comes from the Greek verb "μένω" (méno), which means "to stay" or "to remain." The prefix "παρα-" (para-) adds the meaning of "beside" or "near," giving the word "παραμένω" the sense of "to stay beside" or "to remain near."

Grammar: "Παραμένω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ παραμένω (Egó paraméno) - I stay
- Εσύ παραμένεις (Esý paraméneis) - You stay
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό παραμένει (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó paraménei) - He/She/It stays
- Εμείς παραμένουμε (Emeís paraménoyme) - We stay
- Εσείς παραμένετε (Eseís paraménete) - You stay
- Αυτοί/Αυτές παραμένουν (Aftoí/Aftés paraménoyn) - They stay

Description: "Παραμένω" is a versatile verb that can be used to express the action of staying, remaining, or continuing in a certain place, state, or condition. It can also convey the idea of persisting, enduring, or lasting. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone staying at a location, maintaining a particular state or position, or continuing a certain activity or behavior.
