Etymology: The word "πανούργος" (panourgos) comes from the Greek roots "πᾶν" (pan), meaning "all," and "ἔργον" (ergon), meaning "work." It can be translated as "crafty" or "cunning."

Grammar: In Greek, "πανούργος" is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, if used to describe a masculine singular noun, it would be "πανούργος," while for a feminine singular noun it would be "πανούργη," and for a neuter singular noun it would be "πανούργο."

Description: "Πανούργος" is an adjective used to describe someone who is cunning, crafty, or sly. It refers to a person who is skilled at deceiving others and achieving their goals through clever and often dishonest means.
