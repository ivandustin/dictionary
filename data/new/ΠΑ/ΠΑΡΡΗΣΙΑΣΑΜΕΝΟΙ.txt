It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παρρησία" (parrhēsía), meaning "frankness" or "boldness," and the verb "αἰσιάζομαι" (aisiázomai), meaning "to speak."

The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the participle mood functions as an adjective.
