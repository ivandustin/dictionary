Etymology: The word "παρακούσας" comes from the Greek verb "παρακούω" (parakoúo), which means "to overhear" or "to listen secretly." The suffix "-ας" (-as) is added to the verb stem to form the participle form, which means "one who overhears" or "one who listens secretly."

Grammar: "Παρακούσας" is the masculine singular form of the participle. In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. They have different forms depending on gender, number, and case.

Description: "Παρακούσας" refers to a person who overhears or listens secretly. It can be used to describe someone who eavesdrops on a conversation or secretly listens to someone's private discussions.
