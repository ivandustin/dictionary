Etymology: The word "παρεδίδου" (paredidou) comes from the Greek verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi), which means "to give over" or "to hand over". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "alongside", and the verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), meaning "to give".

Grammar: "Παρεδίδου" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradidomi) in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone in the singular form.

Description: "Παρεδίδου" is a Greek word that means "give over" or "hand over". It is often used in a commanding or instructive context, where someone is being told to give or hand over something to someone else.
