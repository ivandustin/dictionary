Etymology: The word "πασιν" (pasin) comes from the Greek word "πᾶς" (pas), meaning "all" or "every". 

Grammar: "Πασιν" is a form of the Greek adjective "πᾶς" (pas), which means "all" or "every". It is in the dative case, indicating that it is used to show the indirect object or the recipient of an action. 

Description: "Πασιν" is used to refer to "all" or "every" in a general sense. It is often used to indicate inclusiveness or totality.
