Etymology: The word "παίδευω" (paideuo) comes from the Greek root "παιδ-" (paid-), which means "child" or "education." It is related to the word "παιδεία" (paideia), which means "education" or "upbringing."

Grammar: "Παίδευω" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ παιδεύω (ego paideuo) - I educate
- Εσύ παιδεύεις (esi paideveis) - You educate
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό παιδεύει (autos/afti/afto paidevei) - He/She/It educates
- Εμείς παιδεύουμε (emeis paidevoume) - We educate
- Εσείς παιδεύετε (eseis paidevete) - You educate
- Αυτοί/Αυτές παιδεύουν (autoi/aftes paidevoun) - They educate

Description: "Παίδευω" means "to educate" or "to teach" in Greek. It refers to the act of imparting knowledge, skills, or values to someone, usually in a formal or structured manner. It can also imply the process of disciplining or training someone to develop their character or abilities.
