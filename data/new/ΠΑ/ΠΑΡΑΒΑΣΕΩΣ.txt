Etymology: The word "παραβασεως" (parabaseos) comes from the Greek verb "παραβαίνω" (parabaino), which means "to transgress" or "to go beyond." It is derived from the prefix "παρα-" (para-), meaning "beside" or "beyond," and the verb "βαίνω" (baino), meaning "to go."

Grammar: "Παραβασεως" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Παραβασεως" refers to the act of transgressing or going beyond a limit or boundary. It can be used to describe a violation, infringement, or breach of a rule, law, or moral code.
