Etymology: The word "παταξαί" (pataxai) comes from the Greek verb "πατάσσω" (patasso), which means "to strike" or "to hit". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet- meaning "to rush" or "to fly".

Grammar: "παταξαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, subjunctive mood, second person singular form of the verb "πατάσσω" (patasso). The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject performs the action. The subjunctive mood is used to express possibility, doubt, or a hypothetical situation. The second person singular form is used when addressing one person.

Description: "παταξαί" is a verb form that can be translated as "you might strike" or "you could hit" in English. It suggests a hypothetical or potential action of striking or hitting someone or something.
