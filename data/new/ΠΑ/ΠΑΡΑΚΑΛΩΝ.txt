Etymology: The word "παρακαλών" (parakalōn) comes from the Greek verb "παρακαλώ" (parakalō), which means "to call to one's side" or "to appeal to." It is derived from the prefix "παρά" (para), meaning "beside" or "alongside," and the verb "καλέω" (kaleō), meaning "to call."

Grammar: "Παρακαλών" is the present participle form of the verb "παρακαλώ" (parakalō). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing actions or states. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Παρακαλών" can be translated as "calling," "appealing," or "pleading." It describes someone who is currently in the act of calling or appealing to someone else. It can also be used as an adjective to describe someone who is generally polite or courteous.
