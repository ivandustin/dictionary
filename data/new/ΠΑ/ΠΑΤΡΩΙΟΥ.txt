Etymology: The word "πατρώιος" (patrōios) comes from the Greek word "πατήρ" (patēr), meaning "father." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root "*pəter-," which also gave rise to similar words in other Indo-European languages.

Grammar: "Πατρώιος" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine form and can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πατρώιος" is used to describe something that is related to or characteristic of one's father or paternal side. It can refer to things such as paternal lineage, family traditions, or inherited traits.
