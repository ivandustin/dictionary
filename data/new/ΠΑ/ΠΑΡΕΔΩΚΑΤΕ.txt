Etymology: The word "παρεδωκατε" (pare̱dōkate) comes from the Greek verb "παραδίδωμι" (paradídōmi), which means "to hand over" or "to deliver."

Grammar: "Παρεδωκατε" is the second person plural form of the aorist tense in the indicative mood of the verb "παραδίδωμι." It is used to address a group of people and indicates that they have handed over or delivered something in the past.

Description: "Παρεδωκατε" is a Greek verb form that expresses an action of handing over or delivering something in the past. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as in narratives, instructions, or commands.
