Etymology: The word "παυσονται" (pronounced "pausontai") comes from the Greek verb "παύω" (pronounced "pao"), which means "to stop" or "to cease." The suffix "-νται" ("-ntai") indicates the third person plural form of the verb, making it "they stop" or "they cease."

Grammar: "Παυσονται" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the subject and tense. The "-ονται" ("-ontai") ending is used for the present tense, third person plural.

Description: "Παυσονται" is used to describe an action of stopping or ceasing that is performed by a group of people or things. It can be translated as "they stop" or "they cease" in English.
