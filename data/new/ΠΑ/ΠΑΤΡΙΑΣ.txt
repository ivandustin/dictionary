Etymology: The word "πατριας" (patrias) comes from the Greek word "πατήρ" (pater), meaning "father." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pəter-.

Grammar: In Greek, "πατριας" (patrias) is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension and is classified as a feminine noun.

Description: "Πατριας" (patrias) is a Greek word that means "fatherland" or "homeland." It refers to the country or region where a person or a group of people have ancestral or cultural ties. It is often used to express a sense of belonging, loyalty, and love for one's native land.
