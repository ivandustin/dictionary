Grammar: "παρεπορευόμεντο" is the third person plural, imperfect, middle/passive voice form of the verb "παρεπορεύομαι" (pareporevomai). In Greek grammar, the imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing or repeated actions in the past. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "παρεπορευόμεντο" describes a group of people or things that were passing by or going past in the past. It implies that the action was ongoing or repeated at that time.
