Etymology: The word "παρθένων" (parthenon) comes from the Greek word "παρθένος" (parthenos), which means "virgin" or "maiden". 

Grammar: "Παρθένων" is a noun in the genitive case, singular number, masculine gender. It is the genitive form of "παρθένος" (parthenos).

Description: "Παρθένων" is the name of a famous ancient Greek temple located on the Acropolis of Athens. It is also known as the Parthenon. The Parthenon was built in the 5th century BC and is considered one of the greatest achievements of classical Greek architecture. It was dedicated to the goddess Athena Parthenos, the patron deity of Athens. The Parthenon is renowned for its architectural beauty, harmonious proportions, and intricate sculptures. It is a symbol of ancient Greek civilization and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
