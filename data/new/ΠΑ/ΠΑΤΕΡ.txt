Etymology: The word "πατερ" (pater) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pəter, meaning "father."

Grammar: In Greek, "πατερ" (pater) is a noun of the masculine gender. It is declined in various cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative, to indicate its role in a sentence.

Description: "Πατερ" (pater) means "father" in Greek. It is a term used to refer to a male parent or a figure of authority and protection within a family. The word can also be used metaphorically to denote a founder or originator of something.
