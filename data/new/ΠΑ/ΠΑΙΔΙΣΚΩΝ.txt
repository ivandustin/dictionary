Etymology: The word "παίδισκων" (pronounced "paidiskon") comes from the Greek word "παῖς" (pronounced "pais"), meaning "child" or "servant," and the suffix "-ισκων" (-iskon), which denotes a diminutive form. 

Grammar: "Παίδισκων" is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. It is the genitive plural of the noun "παίδισκος" (pronounced "paidiskos"), which means "young girl" or "maid."

Description: "Παίδισκων" refers to multiple young girls or maids. It is used to describe a group of young female servants or children.
