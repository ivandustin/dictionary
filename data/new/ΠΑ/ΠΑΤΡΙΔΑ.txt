Etymology: The word "πατρίδα" (patrida) comes from the Greek word "πατήρ" (pater), meaning "father." It is derived from the concept of the fatherland or homeland.

Grammar: In Greek, "πατρίδα" is a feminine noun. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used with definite and indefinite articles.

Description: "Πατρίδα" is a Greek word that refers to one's homeland or native country. It carries a strong emotional and cultural significance, representing the place where one's roots, heritage, and sense of belonging are found. It encompasses not only the physical land but also the people, traditions, and history associated with it. The concept of "πατρίδα" is deeply ingrained in Greek culture and is often associated with feelings of love, pride, and nostalgia.
