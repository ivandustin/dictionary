Etymology: The word "πανταχοῦ" (pantachou) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the words "παν" (pan), meaning "all" or "every," and "ταχύς" (tachys), meaning "quick" or "fast." 

Grammar: "Πανταχοῦ" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to describe the location or manner in which something happens. It can be translated as "everywhere" or "in every way."

Description: "Πανταχοῦ" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It emphasizes the idea of something happening or being present in all places or in every aspect. It can be used to express the concept of ubiquity or universality.
