Etymology: The word "παυσασθαι" (pausasthai) comes from the Greek verb "παύω" (pauo), which means "to stop" or "to cease." The suffix "-σθαι" (-sthai) indicates the middle voice, suggesting that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar: "Παυσασθαι" is an infinitive verb form in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense, which expresses a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on oneself.

Description: "Παυσασθαι" means "to stop" or "to cease" in Greek. It implies the act of putting an end to an action or process.
