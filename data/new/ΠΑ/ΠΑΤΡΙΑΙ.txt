Etymology: The word "πατριαί" (patrai) comes from the Greek word "πατρίς" (patris), which means "fatherland" or "homeland". It is derived from the word "πατήρ" (pater), meaning "father".

Grammar: "Πατριαί" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the first declension and is feminine in gender.

Description: "Πατριαί" refers to the ancestral lands or territories that are considered the homeland or native country of a person or a group of people. It can also be used to describe the customs, traditions, and cultural heritage associated with a particular homeland.
