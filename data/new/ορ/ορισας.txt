Word: ορισας

Etymology: ορισας is the aorist tense of the Greek verb "ορίζω" (orizo), which means "to define", "to determine", "to set boundaries". The word comes from the noun "ορος" (oros), which means "mountain" or "boundary".

Grammar: ορισας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb "ορίζω" (orizo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: ορισας can be translated as "you defined", "you determined", or "you set boundaries". It implies that the action of defining or determining something has been completed in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in mathematics, law, or philosophy, where the concept of setting boundaries or defining terms is important.
