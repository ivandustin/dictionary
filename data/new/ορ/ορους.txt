Word: ορους

Etymology:
The word ορους (oros) is derived from the Greek word ὄρος (oros), which means "mountain" or "hill". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂er- meaning "to rise, lift, or be high".

Grammar:
In Greek, ορους is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form ορου (orou).

Description:
Ορους is a term used in Greek mythology to refer to the mountains or hills. It is often used to describe the dwelling places of the gods and goddesses, such as Mount Olympus, the home of the Olympian gods. The word is also used in modern Greek to refer to mountains or hills in general.
