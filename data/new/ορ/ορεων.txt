Word: ορεων

Etymology:
The word ορεων (oreon) is derived from the Greek word ορος (oros), which means "mountain". The suffix -ων (-on) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to "mountains" in the plural form.

Grammar:
Ορεων (oreon) is a noun in the genitive plural form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple mountains being referred to.

Description:
Ορεων (oreon) refers to "mountains" in the plural form. It is often used in Greek mythology to describe the dwelling places of the gods, such as Mount Olympus, the home of Zeus and the other Olympian gods. In modern Greek, the word is used to refer to any mountain range or group of mountains.
