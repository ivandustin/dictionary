Word: ορνις

Etymology: The word ορνις comes from the Greek word ὄρνις (ornis), which means "bird".

Grammar: ορνις is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun and belongs to the second declension. The genitive form of ορνις is ορνιθός (ornithos).

Description: ορνις refers to a bird in general. It does not specify any particular species of bird. The word is commonly used in scientific and academic contexts to refer to birds.
