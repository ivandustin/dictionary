Word: ορχησαμένης

Etymology:
The word ορχησαμένης is derived from the Greek verb ορχέομαι (orchéomai), which means "to dance". The suffix -μένης (-menis) is a participle ending that indicates the action of the verb is ongoing or completed.

Grammar:
Ορχησαμένης is a feminine singular participle in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description:
Ορχησαμένης describes a female subject who has danced or is dancing. It can be used as an adjective to modify a noun, such as "the dancing woman" or "the woman who danced". It can also be used as a standalone noun to refer to a female dancer.
