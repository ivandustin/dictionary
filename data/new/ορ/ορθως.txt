Word: ορθως

Etymology:
The word ορθως (orthos) is derived from the Greek word ὀρθός (orthos), which means "straight" or "upright". It is related to the Greek word ὀρθοδοξία (orthodoxia), which means "right belief" or "correct opinion".

Grammar:
Ορθως is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate that something is done correctly, accurately, or in a straight manner. It is often translated as "correctly", "rightly", or "properly" in English.

Description:
Ορθως is a common word in Greek, used in everyday conversation and writing. It is often used to express approval or agreement with something that has been said or done. For example, if someone gives a correct answer to a question, another person might say "Ορθώς!" to indicate that they agree with the answer and think it is correct. Similarly, if someone does something in a proper or correct manner, another person might say "Ορθώς!" to express approval.
