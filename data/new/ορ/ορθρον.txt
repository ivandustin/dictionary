Word: ορθρον

Etymology:
The word ορθρον (orthron) is derived from the Greek word ορθρος (orthros), which means "dawn" or "daybreak". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂erḱ- ("to become light").

Grammar:
Ορθρον is a noun in the singular form in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. The genitive form of the word is ορθρου (orthrou).

Description:
Ορθρον refers to the time of day just before sunrise, when the sky begins to lighten and the first light of day appears. It is often used in poetry and literature to describe the beauty and tranquility of this time of day. In Greek mythology, the goddess Eos, who was associated with the dawn, was often depicted as rising from the ορθρον.
