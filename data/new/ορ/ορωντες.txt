Word: ορωντες

Etymology: ορωντες is a present participle of the Greek verb οραω (orao), which means "to see" or "to perceive". The suffix -ντ- (-nt-) is a common ending for present participles in Greek.

Grammar: ορωντες is a masculine plural nominative present participle in Greek. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently seeing or perceiving something.

Description: ορωντες can be translated to "those who see" or "those who are seeing". It is often used in Greek literature and mythology to describe characters who have the ability to see things beyond the physical realm, such as gods, prophets, or seers. In modern Greek, it can also be used to describe someone who has a keen sense of observation or perception.
