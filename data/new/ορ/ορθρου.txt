The word "ορθρου" (orthrou) is a Greek noun that means "dawn" or "daybreak". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "ορθρος" (orthros), which means "morning". 

Grammar: "Ορθρου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. In this case, it is used to indicate that something belongs to or is related to the dawn or daybreak. 

Description: The word "ορθρου" is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe the beginning of a new day, the first light of dawn, and the beauty of the sunrise. It is also used metaphorically to represent new beginnings, hope, and renewal.
