Word: ορθος

Etymology:
The word ορθος (orthos) comes from the Greek root word ὀρθός (orthos), which means "straight" or "upright". It is related to other Greek words such as ὀρθοδοξία (orthodoxia) meaning "right belief" and ὀρθοπεδικός (orthopedikos) meaning "straightening of bones".

Grammar:
In Greek, ορθος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person, object, or idea as "straight", "upright", "correct", or "right". It can be declined in all cases, genders, and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
Ορθος is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It can describe physical objects that are straight or upright, such as a straight line or an upright pole. It can also describe people who are morally upright or correct in their beliefs and actions. In medical terminology, it is used to describe conditions that involve the straightening of bones or joints. Overall, ορθος conveys a sense of correctness, uprightness, and alignment.
