Word: ορκος

Etymology:
The word ορκος (orkos) comes from the ancient Greek language. It is derived from the verb ὀρκίζω (orkizo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath". The noun ορκος (orkos) refers to the oath itself or the person who takes the oath.

Description:
Ορκος (orkos) is a word that refers to a solemn promise or a vow that is made by an individual. It is often used in legal or religious contexts, where people are required to swear an oath to tell the truth or to uphold certain values and principles. The word can also be used to describe a person who has taken an oath, such as a witness in a court of law or a member of a religious order.
