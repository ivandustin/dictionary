The word "ορνεα" (ornea) is a Greek noun that means "birds" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "όρνις" (ornis), which means "bird". 

Grammar: "Ορνεα" is a plural noun in Greek, and it is used to refer to more than one bird. The singular form of the word is "όρνις" (ornis). 

Description: Birds are warm-blooded, feathered animals that have wings and lay eggs. They are found all over the world and come in a wide variety of shapes, sizes, and colors.
