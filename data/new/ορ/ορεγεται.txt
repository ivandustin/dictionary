Word: ορεγεται

The word ορεγεται (oregetai) is derived from the Greek verb ορεγομαι (oregomai), which means "to desire" or "to long for". It is formed from the prefix ορεγ- (oreg-), which means "to reach out for" or "to stretch towards", and the suffix -ομαι (-omai), which is a middle voice ending that indicates the subject is acting upon itself.

Grammar:
Ορεγεται (oregetai) is a third-person singular present indicative form of the verb ορεγομαι (oregomai). It is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. In this case, the subject is the third-person singular pronoun, which could refer to he, she, or it.

Ορεγεται (oregetai) describes a state of desire or longing. It indicates that the subject is actively reaching out for something that they want or need. The context of the sentence would determine what the subject is desiring or longing for.
