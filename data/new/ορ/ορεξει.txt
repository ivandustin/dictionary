The word "ορεξει" (orexei) is a Greek noun that means "appetite" or "desire". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "ορεγομαι" (oregomai), which means "to desire" or "to long for". 

Grammar: "Ορεξει" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: The word "ορεξει" is commonly used in Greek to refer to the physical appetite for food, but it can also be used to describe a strong desire or craving for something. For example, someone might say "Έχω ορεξη για μια πίτσα" (Eho orexei gia mia pitsa) which means "I have an appetite for pizza", or "Έχω ορεξη για ταξίδι" (Eho orexei gia taxidi) which means "I have a desire for travel".
