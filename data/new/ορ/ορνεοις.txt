The word "ορνεοις" (orneois) is a Greek noun that means "birds" or "fowls". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "ornis" which means "bird". The suffix "-eis" is added to indicate the plural form.

Grammar: "Ορνεοις" is a third declension noun in Greek, which means that its endings change depending on its grammatical function in a sentence. For example, in the nominative case, it is "ορνεοις", but in the accusative case, it becomes "ορνεους".

Description: "Ορνεοις" is a common word in Greek and is used to refer to any type of bird or fowl. It can be used in both a literal and figurative sense, such as in the phrase "ορνεοις του ουρανου" (birds of the sky) which is a metaphor for freedom.
