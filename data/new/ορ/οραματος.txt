Word: οραματος

Etymology:
The word οραματος (oramatos) is derived from the Greek word οραμα (orama), which means "vision" or "sight". The suffix -τος (-tos) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
Οραματος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is declined in the second declension and has the genitive form οραματου (oramatos).

Description:
Οραματος is an adjective that describes something that is visible or can be seen. It can also refer to something that is visionary or imaginative. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a dream or a vision.
