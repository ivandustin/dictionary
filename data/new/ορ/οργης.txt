The word "οργης" (orges) is a Greek noun that means "anger" or "wrath". It is derived from the Greek verb "οργιζω" (orgizo), which means "to provoke to anger". In terms of grammar, "οργης" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It can be used to describe a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility towards someone or something.
