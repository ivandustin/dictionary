Word: ορκον

Etymology: The word ορκον (orkon) is derived from the Greek word ορκος (orkos), which means "oath" or "sworn statement". 

Grammar: ορκον is a noun in the singular form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. 

Description: ορκον refers to an oath or a sworn statement. It is often used in legal or formal contexts, where someone makes a promise or a declaration under oath. In ancient Greece, oaths were considered sacred and binding, and breaking an oath was seen as a serious offense. Today, the word ορκον is still used in legal proceedings and in other formal contexts where an oath is required.
