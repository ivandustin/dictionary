The word "ορθας" (orthas) is a Greek adjective that means "straight" or "upright". It is derived from the Greek word "ορθός" (orthos), which also means "straight" or "upright". In terms of grammar, "ορθας" is a masculine singular form of the adjective. It can be used to describe a person, object, or idea that is physically or morally straight or upright.
