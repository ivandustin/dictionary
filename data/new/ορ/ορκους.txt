Word: ορκους

Etymology:
The word ορκους (orkous) is derived from the Greek word ορκος (orkos), which means "oath" or "sworn statement". It is related to the verb ορκιζω (orkizo), which means "to swear" or "to take an oath".

Grammar:
Ορκους is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. Ορκους is a masculine noun in the nominative case.

Description:
Ορκους refers to oaths or sworn statements. It can be used to describe a formal promise or pledge made by an individual, often in a legal or religious context. The plural form suggests that there are multiple oaths or sworn statements being referred to. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a solemn or binding commitment to a particular course of action.
