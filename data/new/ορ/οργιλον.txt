Word: οργιλον

Etymology:
The word οργιλον (orgilon) is derived from the Greek word οργή (orgē), which means anger or wrath. The suffix -ιλον (-ilon) is a common suffix used to form adjectives in Greek, indicating a quality or characteristic.

Grammar:
In Greek, οργιλον is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can be used to describe a person or thing that is angry, irritable, or easily provoked.

Description:
Οργιλον is a word that describes a state of anger or irritability. It can be used to describe a person who is easily angered or provoked, or a situation that is causing frustration or annoyance. The word can also be used to describe a feeling of intense anger or rage.
