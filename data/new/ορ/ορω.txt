Word: ορω

Etymology:
The word ορω (orō) comes from the Ancient Greek verb ὁράω (horáō), which means "to see" or "to perceive". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *wer- ("to perceive, watch out for").

Grammar:
Ορω (orō) is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the verb ὁράω (horáō). It is used to indicate that the speaker is currently seeing or perceiving something.

Description:
Ορω (orō) is a Greek verb that means "I see" or "I perceive". It is a common verb used in everyday conversation and is often used to describe the act of seeing something with one's eyes or perceiving something with one's senses. It can also be used metaphorically to describe understanding or comprehending something.
