Word: ηνεωιξεν

Etymology: ηνεωιξεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb ανοίγω (anoigo), which means "to open". The prefix ενε- (ene-) is added to the verb to indicate that the action is completed, resulting in the meaning "he/she/it opened".

Grammar: ηνεωιξεν is the third person singular aorist indicative active form of the verb ανοίγω (anoigo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates that the action is a statement of fact, and the active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action.

Description: ηνεωιξεν means "he/she/it opened" in Greek. It is used to describe a single past action of opening something. The context of the sentence will determine what was opened.
