Word: ην

Etymology: ην is the third person singular past tense form of the Greek verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁es-.

Grammar: ην is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically the aorist tense of the verb "εἰμί" (eimi). It is used to indicate a completed action in the past. In this form, it is conjugated for the third person singular.

Description: ην is a word that is used in the Greek language to indicate that something or someone existed or was present in the past. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to Jesus Christ, as in the phrase "ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ ὤν" (ego eimi ho on), which means "I am the one who is".
