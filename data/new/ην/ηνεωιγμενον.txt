The word "ηνεωιγμενον" (eneōigmenon) is a Greek participle that means "opened" or "unsealed". It comes from the verb "ἀνοίγω" (anoigō), which means "to open". In terms of grammar, it is a neuter singular form in the perfect tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It is often used in the New Testament to describe the opening of seals in the book of Revelation.
