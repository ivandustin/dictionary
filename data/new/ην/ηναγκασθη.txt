Word: ηναγκασθη

Etymology:
The word ηναγκασθη is a past tense form of the Greek verb αναγκάζω (anagkazo), which means "to force" or "to compel". The verb is derived from the noun ανάγκη (anagke), which means "necessity" or "compulsion".

Grammar:
ηναγκασθη is a third-person singular past tense form of the verb αναγκάζω (anagkazo) in the passive voice. The verb is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
ηναγκασθη is a Greek word that means "was forced" or "was compelled". It is often used to describe a situation where someone was made to do something against their will or without their consent. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in legal or political situations, or in personal relationships.
