Word: ηναγκασεν

Etymology: ηναγκασεν is a Greek word that comes from the verb αναγκάζω (anagkazo), which means "to force" or "to compel."

Grammar: ηναγκασεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of αναγκάζω (anagkazo). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration or repetition.

Description: ηναγκασεν means "he/she/it forced" in English. It is a verb that describes an action of compelling or coercing someone to do something against their will. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Ηναγκασεν τον αδελφό του να πάει στο γιατρό" (He forced his brother to go to the doctor).
