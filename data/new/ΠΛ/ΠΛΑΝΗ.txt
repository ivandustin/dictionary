Etymology: The word "πλάνη" (plani) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "πλανάω" (planao), which means "to wander" or "to stray." It is derived from the noun "πλάνης" (planes), meaning "wanderer" or "stray."

Grammar: "Πλάνη" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "πλάνης" (planes). In the plural form, it becomes "πλάνες" (planes).

Description: "Πλάνη" refers to the act of wandering or straying from a path or course. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of confusion, uncertainty, or deception. In Greek mythology, the term is often associated with the wandering of celestial bodies, such as planets, which move across the sky in a seemingly erratic manner.
