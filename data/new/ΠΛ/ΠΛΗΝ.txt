Etymology: The word "πλην" (plin) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πλήν" (plḗn), meaning "except" or "besides".

Grammar: In Greek, "πλην" is a preposition that is used to indicate an exception or exclusion. It is often translated as "except for" or "besides".

Description: "Πλην" is a versatile word that is commonly used in Greek to introduce an exception or exclusion in a sentence. It is often used to indicate that something is true or applicable in all cases except for one specific case. It can also be used to introduce an additional point or consideration that is not directly related to the main topic.
