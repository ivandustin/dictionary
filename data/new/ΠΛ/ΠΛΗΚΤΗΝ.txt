Etymology: The word "πληκτήν" (pliktín) comes from the Greek verb "πλήσσω" (plísso), which means "to strike" or "to hit". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pleh₁k-, meaning "to strike" or "to beat".

Grammar: "Πληκτήν" is a noun in the singular form. It belongs to the third declension in Greek grammar. The word has a feminine gender and is declined as follows:

- Nominative: πληκτήν
- Genitive: πληκτῆς
- Dative: πληκτῇ
- Accusative: πληκτήν
- Vocative: πληκτήν

Description: "Πληκτήν" refers to a whip or lash used for striking or beating. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a harsh or severe criticism.
