Etymology: The word "πλεονεξίας" (pleonexias) comes from the Greek word "πλεονεξία" (pleonexia), which means "greed" or "covetousness". It is derived from the combination of the words "πλέον" (pleon), meaning "more" or "excess", and "ἔχω" (echo), meaning "to have".

Grammar: "πλεονεξίας" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "πλεονεξίας" refers to the concept of greed or covetousness. It describes an excessive desire for wealth, possessions, or power, often at the expense of others. It is a negative trait associated with selfishness and a lack of contentment.
