Etymology: The word "πλανητης" (planētēs) comes from the Greek word meaning "wanderer" or "wandering star." In ancient times, the planets were observed to move differently from the fixed stars, hence the name.

Grammar: In Greek, "πλανητης" (planētēs) is a masculine noun of the third declension. It can be declined in various cases, numbers, and genders to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Πλανητης" (planētēs) refers to a celestial body that orbits around a star, is not a satellite of another planet, and shines by reflecting the light of its star. In our solar system, there are eight recognized planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. These planets have various characteristics, such as size, composition, and atmosphere, and they play a significant role in the dynamics of the solar system.
