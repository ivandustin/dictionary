Etymology: The word "πλεονέκτης" (pleonéktis) comes from the Greek roots "πλέον" (pleon), meaning "more" or "excess," and "ἔχω" (écho), meaning "to have." 

Grammar: In Greek, "πλεονέκτης" is a noun of the masculine gender. It is declined in the singular and plural forms, and it can be used in the nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases.

Description: "πλεονέκτης" is a Greek word that refers to a person who seeks to gain an advantage or benefit at the expense of others. It can be translated as "advantage-seeker," "opportunist," or "selfish person." This term is often used to describe someone who is willing to exploit or take advantage of others in order to achieve their own goals.
