Etymology: The word "πλησθησεται" comes from the Greek verb "πλησθέναι" (plēsthénai), which means "to be filled" or "to be satisfied". It is derived from the root word "πλήθω" (plēthō), meaning "to fill" or "to abound".

Grammar: "πλησθησεται" is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb "πλησθέναι" (plēsthénai), which is an aorist passive infinitive.

Description: The word "πλησθησεται" is used to express the action of being filled or satisfied in the future. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a person or thing being filled with a substance or reaching a state of satisfaction.
