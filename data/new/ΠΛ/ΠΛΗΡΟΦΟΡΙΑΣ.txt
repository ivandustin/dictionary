Etymology: The word "πληροφορίας" (pliroforias) comes from the Greek word "πλήρης" (pliris), meaning "full" or "complete," and the noun "φέρω" (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bear." Therefore, "πληροφορίας" can be translated as "carrying or bearing fullness or completeness."

Grammar: "πληροφορίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "πληροφορίας" refers to the concept of information or knowledge. It represents the collection, organization, and communication of facts, data, or details about a particular subject. In modern usage, it is commonly associated with the field of information technology and the digital age.
