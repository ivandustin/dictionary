Etymology: The word "πληγήν" (plēgēn) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πληγή" (plēgē), meaning "a blow" or "a stroke".

Grammar: In Greek, "πληγήν" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it refers to a specific instance of a blow or a wound.

Description: "Πληγήν" is used to describe a physical injury or wound caused by a blow or a strike. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a figurative or emotional injury.
