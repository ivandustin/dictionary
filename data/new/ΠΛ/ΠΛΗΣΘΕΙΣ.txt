Etymology: The word "πλησθεῖς" (plēstheis) comes from the Greek verb "πλήθω" (plēthō), which means "to fill" or "to be full." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pelh₁- ("to fill") and is related to words like "plenty" and "plentiful."

Grammar: "Πλησθεῖς" is the second person singular aorist passive imperative form of the verb "πλήθω" (plēthō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "Πλησθεῖς" is a command form that can be translated as "be filled" or "get filled." It is used to instruct or urge someone to become full or to have their desires or needs satisfied.
