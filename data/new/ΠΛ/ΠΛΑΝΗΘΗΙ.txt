Etymology: The word "πλανητης" (planētēs) comes from the Greek word meaning "wanderer" or "wandering star." In ancient times, the planets were observed to move across the night sky in a way that distinguished them from the fixed stars.

Grammar: In Greek, "πλανητης" (planētēs) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension, and its genitive form is "πλανητου" (planētou).

Description: "πλανητης" (planētēs) refers to a celestial body that orbits around a star, is not a satellite of another planet, and shines by reflecting the light of its star. In our solar system, there are eight recognized planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. These planets have been studied extensively and have various characteristics, such as size, composition, and atmosphere, that distinguish them from one another.
