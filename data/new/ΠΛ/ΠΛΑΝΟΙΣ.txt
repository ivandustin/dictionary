Etymology: The word "πλανος" (planos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "πλανάω" (planao), which means "to wander" or "to roam."

Grammar: In Greek, "πλανοις" (planos) is the nominative form of the noun, while "πλανου" (planou) is the genitive form. The word belongs to the second declension, masculine gender.

Description: "Πλανοις" (planos) refers to a wanderer or a person who roams around without a fixed destination. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is deceitful or misleading.
