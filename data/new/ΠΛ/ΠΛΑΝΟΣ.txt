Etymology: The word "πλανος" (planos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "πλανάω" (planao), which means "to wander" or "to roam."

Grammar: In Greek, "πλανος" (planos) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "πλανου" (planou).

Description: "Πλανος" (planos) refers to someone who wanders or roams around aimlessly. It can also be used to describe someone who is deceitful or cunning. In Greek mythology, the term is often associated with the god Hermes, who was known as the "trickster" and the "wanderer."
