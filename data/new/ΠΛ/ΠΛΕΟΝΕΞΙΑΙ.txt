Etymology: The word "πλεονεξία" comes from the Greek roots "πλέον" (pleon), meaning "more" or "excess," and "ἔχω" (echo), meaning "to have." 

Grammar: "Πλεονεξία" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun of the first declension.

Description: "Πλεονεξία" refers to the concept of greed or avarice. It denotes an excessive desire for wealth or possessions, often at the expense of others. It is associated with the idea of wanting more than what is necessary or fair.
