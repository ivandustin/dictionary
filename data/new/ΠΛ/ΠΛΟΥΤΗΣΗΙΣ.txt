Etymology: The word "πλουτησης" (ploutēsis) comes from the Greek verb "πλουτέω" (plouteō), which means "to be rich" or "to have abundance". 

Grammar: "Πλουτησης" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Πλουτησης" refers to the act or state of being rich or having abundance. It can also be translated as "wealth" or "prosperity".
