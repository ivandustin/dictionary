Etymology: The word "πλησθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "πληθω" (plēthō), which means "to be full" or "to be filled". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pelh₁- ("to fill").

Grammar: "πλησθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "πληθω" (plēthō) in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "πλησθηναι" is used to express the idea of being filled or becoming full in Greek. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the filling of a container, the satisfaction of a desire, or the completion of a task.
