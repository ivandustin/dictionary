Etymology: The word "πληθούς" (plēthoús) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "πληθύνω" (plēthýnō), which means "to multiply" or "to increase in number."

Grammar: In Greek, "πληθούς" is a noun in the genitive case. It is the genitive form of the word "πλῆθος" (plēthos), which means "a multitude" or "a great number."

Description: "Πληθούς" refers to the genitive form of the noun "πλῆθος" (plēthos). It is used to indicate possession or association. For example, "το πλήθος των ανθρώπων" (to plēthos tōn anthrōpōn) means "the multitude of people."
