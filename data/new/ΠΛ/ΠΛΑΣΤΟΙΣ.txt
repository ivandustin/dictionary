Etymology: The word "πλαστοίς" (plastoís) comes from the Greek verb "πλάσσω" (plássō), which means "to mold" or "to shape." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pelh₁- ("to shape, to form").

Grammar: "Πλαστοίς" is the plural form of the adjective "πλαστός" (plastós), which means "molded" or "shaped." It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πλαστοίς" is used to describe something that has been molded or shaped into a specific form. It can refer to physical objects that have been crafted or manufactured, as well as abstract concepts that have been created or constructed.
