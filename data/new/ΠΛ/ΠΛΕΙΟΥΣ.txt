Etymology: The word "πλειούς" (pleious) comes from the Greek word "πλέον" (pleon), meaning "more" or "greater."

Grammar: "Πλειούς" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Πλειούς" is used to describe something that is more or greater in quantity, size, or degree. It can be translated as "more," "greater," or "larger" in English.
