Etymology: The word "πλανοι" (planoi) comes from the Greek word "πλανάω" (planao), which means "to wander" or "to roam". In ancient Greek, "πλανοι" referred to wandering stars or planets.

Grammar: "Πλανοι" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word "πλανοι" is in the masculine gender and nominative case.

Description: "Πλανοι" refers to wandering stars or planets. In ancient times, people observed that certain celestial bodies moved across the night sky, unlike the fixed stars. These moving objects were called "πλανοι" or planets. Today, we know that planets are celestial bodies that orbit around the sun and do not emit their own light but reflect the light of the sun.
