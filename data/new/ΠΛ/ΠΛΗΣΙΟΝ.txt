Etymology: The word "πλησίον" comes from the Greek word "πλησίος" (plēsios), meaning "near" or "close".

Grammar: In Greek, "πλησίον" is an adverb that means "nearby" or "close by". It is used to describe the proximity or closeness of something or someone.

Description: "Πλησίον" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is often used to indicate physical proximity, such as the distance between two objects or the nearness of a location. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the closeness of relationships or the immediacy of a situation. Overall, "πλησίον" is a useful word in Greek for expressing the concept of being near or close.
