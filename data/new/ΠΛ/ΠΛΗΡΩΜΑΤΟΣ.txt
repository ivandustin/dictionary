Etymology: The word "πληρωματος" (plērōmatos) comes from the Greek verb "πληρόω" (plēroō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". It is derived from the noun "πλήρης" (plērēs), meaning "full" or "complete".

Grammar: "Πληρωματος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship. It is often translated as "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Description: "Πληρωματος" refers to the act of filling or completing something. It can also be used to describe a fullness or completeness. In a religious context, it can refer to the fullness of God's grace or the completion of a spiritual journey.
