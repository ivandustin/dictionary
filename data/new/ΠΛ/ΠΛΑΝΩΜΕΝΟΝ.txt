Etymology: The word "πλανώμενον" (planómenon) comes from the Greek verb "πλανάω" (planáo), which means "to wander" or "to roam". It is derived from the noun "πλάνη" (plánē), meaning "wandering" or "error".

Grammar: "Πλανώμενον" is the present participle form of the verb "πλανάω" (planáo) in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: "Πλανώμενον" is used to describe something or someone that is currently wandering or roaming. It indicates ongoing action in the present tense.
