Etymology: The word "πληροφορία" (pliroforiai) comes from the Greek roots "πλήρης" (pliris), meaning "full" or "complete," and "φέρω" (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bear." Therefore, "πληροφορία" can be translated as "that which carries or conveys fullness or completeness."

Grammar: In Greek, "πληροφορία" is a feminine noun of the first declension. It is singular and can be used in both the nominative and accusative cases. The plural form is "πληροφορίες" (plirofories).

Description: "πληροφορία" refers to information or knowledge that is obtained or communicated about a particular subject. It can also denote the act of informing or providing information. In modern usage, it is commonly used to refer to data, facts, or details that are used to understand or explain something.
