Etymology: The word "πληροφορησον" (pliroforison) comes from the Greek verb "πληροφορέω" (pliroforeo), which means "to inform" or "to give information". It is derived from the combination of the words "πλήρης" (pliris), meaning "full" or "complete", and "φέρω" (fero), meaning "to carry" or "to bring".

Grammar: "πληροφορησον" is the imperative form of the verb "πληροφορέω" (pliroforeo) in the second person singular. It is used to give a command or request someone to inform or provide information.
