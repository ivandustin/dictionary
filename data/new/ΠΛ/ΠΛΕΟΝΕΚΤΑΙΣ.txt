Etymology: The word "πλεονέκτης" (pleonéktis) comes from the Greek roots "πλέον" (pleon), meaning "more" or "excess," and "ἔχω" (écho), meaning "to have." 

Grammar: In Greek, "πλεονέκτης" is a noun of the masculine gender. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative singular form "πλεονέκτης." 

Description: "Πλεονέκτης" is a Greek word that translates to "advantage-seeker" or "opportunist" in English. It refers to someone who seeks to gain an advantage or exploit a situation for personal gain.
