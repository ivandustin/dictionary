Etymology: The word "πλάσμα" (plasma) comes from the Greek verb "πλάσσω" (plasso), which means "to mold" or "to shape." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pelh₁-, meaning "to shape" or "to form."

Grammar: In Greek, "πλάσμα" is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "πλάσματα" (plasmata). It can function as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Πλάσμα" refers to something that has been formed or shaped. It can be used to describe various things, such as a creation, a product of artistic expression, or an organism. In scientific contexts, it often refers to a substance that consists of charged particles and is considered the fourth state of matter, alongside solid, liquid, and gas.
