Etymology: The word "πληγής" (plēgēs) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "πλήσσω" (plēssō), which means "to strike" or "to hit". It is related to the noun "πληγή" (plēgē), meaning "a blow" or "a wound".

Grammar: "Πληγής" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender. In the plural form, it becomes "πληγές" (plēgés).

Description: "Πληγής" refers to a wound or an injury caused by a blow or a strike. It can be used to describe physical injuries or metaphorical wounds, such as emotional or psychological pain.
