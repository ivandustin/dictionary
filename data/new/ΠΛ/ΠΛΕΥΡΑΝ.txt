Etymology: The word "πλευράν" (pleuran) comes from the Ancient Greek word "πλευρή" (pleurē), meaning "side" or "rib".

Grammar: In Greek, "πλευράν" is the accusative form of the noun "πλευρά" (pleura), which means "side" or "rib". It is a feminine noun and belongs to the second declension.

Description: "πλευράν" is used to refer to the side or rib of something, both in a literal and figurative sense. It can be used to describe the physical sides of an object or the sides of a person's body. In a figurative sense, it can refer to someone's perspective or point of view.
