Etymology: The word "τελευταί" (teleutai) comes from the Greek verb "τελευτάω" (teleutao), which means "to end" or "to finish."

Grammar: "Τελευταί" is the plural form of the adjective "τελευταίος" (teleutaios), which means "last" or "final" in Greek. It is used to describe multiple objects or individuals that are the last in a sequence or series.

Description: "Τελευταί" is an adjective that is commonly used in Greek to refer to the last or final item in a list, the last occurrence of an event, or the ultimate result of a process. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something as the ultimate or most extreme in a certain category.
