Etymology: The word "τετραμηνος" comes from the Greek roots "τετρα-" meaning "four" and "μηνος" meaning "month". It literally translates to "four months".

Grammar: In Greek, "τετραμηνος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: τετραμηνος
- Genitive: τετραμηνου
- Accusative: τετραμηνο
- Vocative: τετραμηνε

Plural:
- Nominative: τετραμηνοι
- Genitive: τετραμηνων
- Accusative: τετραμηνους
- Vocative: τετραμηνοι

Description: "τετραμηνος" refers to a period of four months. It can be used to describe a duration or a specific time frame that spans four months.
