Etymology: The word "τερατών" comes from the Greek word "τέρας" (teras), which means "monster" or "prodigy".

Grammar: "Τερατών" is a noun in the genitive case, plural form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Τερατών" refers to the genitive plural form of the word "τέρας" (teras), which means "monster" or "prodigy". It is used to describe multiple monsters or prodigies.
