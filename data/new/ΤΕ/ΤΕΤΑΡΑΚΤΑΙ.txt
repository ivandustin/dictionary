Etymology: The word "τεταρακταί" comes from the Greek verb "ταράσσω" (tarássō), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". The suffix "-αί" is added to form the plural form of the noun.

Grammar: "τεταρακταί" is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form is used when referring to multiple instances of the noun.

Description: "τεταρακταί" refers to disturbances or agitations. It can be used to describe a state of turmoil, unrest, or disruption.
