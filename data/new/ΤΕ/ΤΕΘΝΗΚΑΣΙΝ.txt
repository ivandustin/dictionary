Etymology: The word "τεθνηκασιν" comes from the Greek verb "θνῄσκω" (thnēskō), which means "to die." The prefix "τε-" (te-) indicates a perfect tense, indicating that the action of dying has been completed.

Grammar: "τεθνηκασιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "θνῄσκω" in the perfect tense. It is used to describe multiple individuals who have died.

Description: "τεθνηκασιν" is a Greek word that means "they have died" or "they are dead." It is commonly used in various contexts to indicate the state of death for multiple individuals.
