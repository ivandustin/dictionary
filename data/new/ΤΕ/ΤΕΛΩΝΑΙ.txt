Etymology: The word "τελωναί" (telonai) comes from the Greek word "τέλος" (telos), meaning "tax" or "duty". In ancient Greece, "τελωναί" referred to the tax collectors or customs officers.

Grammar: "τελωναί" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple tax collectors or customs officers.

Description: "τελωναί" refers to tax collectors or customs officers. In ancient times, they were responsible for collecting taxes and duties on goods and merchandise that were imported or exported. They would often work at border crossings or ports, inspecting goods and collecting the appropriate taxes.
