Etymology: The word "τεθνηκεν" comes from the Greek verb "θνῄσκω" (thnēskō), which means "to die." It is in the perfect tense form, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "Τεθνηκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "θνῄσκω" (thnēskō) in the perfect tense. In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "Τεθνηκεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it has died" in English. It is used to describe the completed action of someone's death.
