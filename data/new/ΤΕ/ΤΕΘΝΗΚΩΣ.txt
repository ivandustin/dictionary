Etymology: The word "τεθνηκως" (pronounced "teh-THNEE-kohs") comes from the Greek verb "θνησκω" (pronounced "THNEES-koh"), which means "to die." The prefix "τε-" (pronounced "teh-") indicates the perfect tense, indicating that the action of dying has been completed.

Grammar: "Τεθνηκως" is the masculine singular form of the perfect participle of the verb "θνησκω." In Greek grammar, the perfect participle is used to indicate an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing relevance to the present. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Τεθνηκως" means "having died" or "having been dead." It is used to describe someone or something that has already passed away. It can be used in various contexts, such as in obituaries, historical accounts, or discussions about the deceased.
