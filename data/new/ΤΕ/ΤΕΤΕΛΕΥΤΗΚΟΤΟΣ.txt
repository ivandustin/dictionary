Etymology: The word "τετελευτηκοτος" comes from the Greek verb "τελευτάω" (teleutáo), which means "to come to an end" or "to die." The suffix "-κοτος" (-kotos) is added to the verb stem to form an adjective indicating completion or past action.

Grammar: "Τετελευτηκοτος" is the masculine singular form of the adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The word can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it describes.

Description: "Τετελευτηκοτος" is an adjective that means "having come to an end" or "having died." It is used to describe something or someone that has completed a certain action or has reached the end of their life.
