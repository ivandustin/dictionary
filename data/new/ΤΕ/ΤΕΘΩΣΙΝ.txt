Etymology: The word "τεθωσιν" comes from the Greek verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to put." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰeh₁-.

Grammar: "Τεθωσιν" is the third person plural form of the verb "τίθημι" in the aorist tense. In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Τεθωσιν" is used to describe an action that was completed in the past by a group of people or things. It indicates that the action has already taken place and is no longer ongoing.
