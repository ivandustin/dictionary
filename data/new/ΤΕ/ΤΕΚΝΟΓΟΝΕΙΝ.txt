Etymology: The word "τεκνογονείν" comes from the Greek roots "τέκνον" (teknon), meaning "child" or "offspring," and "γεννάω" (gennao), meaning "to beget" or "to give birth." 

Grammar: "Τεκνογονείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. 

Description: "Τεκνογονείν" means "to beget children" or "to procreate" in Greek. It refers to the act of giving birth to or producing offspring.
