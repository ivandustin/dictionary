Etymology: The word "τεσσαρες" (tessares) comes from the Greek numeral "τέσσερα" (téssera), meaning "four".

Grammar: In Greek, "τεσσαρες" is a noun in the plural form. It is the nominative case of the word, which is used to indicate the subject of a sentence.

Description: "Τεσσαρες" is the Greek word for "four". It is used to represent the number 4 in counting and numerical expressions.
