Etymology: The word "τελειότητος" comes from the Greek word "τέλειος" (teleios), meaning "perfect" or "complete", and the suffix "-τητα" (-tita), which is used to form abstract nouns indicating a state or quality.

Grammar: "Τελειότητος" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or description.

Description: "Τελειότητος" refers to the state or quality of being perfect or complete. It can be used to describe something that is flawless, without any faults or deficiencies.
