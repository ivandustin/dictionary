Etymology: The word "τετηρηκαν" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to keep" or "to guard." It is in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "τετηρηκαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "τηρέω" in the perfect tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "τετηρηκαν" means "they have kept" or "they have guarded" in English. It implies that a group of people has performed an action of keeping or guarding something in the past, and the effects of that action continue in the present.
