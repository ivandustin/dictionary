Etymology: The word "τεθεαται" comes from the Greek verb "θεάομαι" (theaomai), which means "to see" or "to behold". The prefix "τε-" (te-) is an intensifier, adding emphasis to the verb. 

Grammar: "Τεθεαται" is the third person plural form of the verb "θεάομαι" in the perfect tense. The perfect tense is used to indicate an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present. 

Description: "Τεθεαται" can be translated as "they have seen" or "they have beheld". It implies that the subject has completed the action of seeing or beholding something in the past, and that this action has some significance or impact on the present.
