Etymology: The word "τεσσερακονταετης" comes from the Greek words "τεσσεράκοντα" (forty) and "έτη" (years), meaning "forty years".

Grammar: In Greek, "τεσσερακονταετης" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is declined in the nominative case as "τεσσερακονταετης" and in the genitive case as "τεσσερακονταετη".

Description: "τεσσερακονταετης" refers to a person or thing that is forty years old or has a duration of forty years.
