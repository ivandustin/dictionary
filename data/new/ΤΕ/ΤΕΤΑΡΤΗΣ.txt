Etymology: The word "τεταρτης" (tetartēs) comes from the Greek word "τέταρτος" (tétartos), meaning "fourth".

Grammar: In Greek, "τεταρτης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: τεταρτης (tetartēs)
- Genitive: τεταρτου (tetartou)
- Accusative: τεταρτη (tetartē)
- Vocative: τεταρτη (tetartē)

Plural:
- Nominative: τεταρτοι (tetartoi)
- Genitive: τεταρτων (tetartōn)
- Accusative: τεταρτους (tetartous)
- Vocative: τεταρτοι (tetartoi)

Description: "Τεταρτης" is the Greek word for "fourth". It is used to indicate the ordinal number four in a series or sequence.
