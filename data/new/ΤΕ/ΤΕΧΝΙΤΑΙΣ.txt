Etymology: The word "τεχνίτης" (tekhnítis) comes from the Greek word "τέχνη" (tékhni), meaning "art" or "craft". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *teks-, which means "to weave" or "to fabricate".

Grammar: In Greek, "τεχνίτης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "τεχνίτες" (tekhnítes). It can be used to refer to a male artisan or craftsman.

Description: "Τεχνίτης" is a term used to describe someone who is skilled in a particular craft or trade. It can refer to various types of artisans, such as carpenters, blacksmiths, painters, or any other skilled worker who creates or repairs things by hand.
