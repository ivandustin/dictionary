Etymology: The word "τελείως" (teleios) comes from the Greek root "τέλος" (telos), meaning "end" or "completion". It is derived from the verb "τελέω" (teleo), which means "to complete" or "to finish".

Grammar: "Τελείως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate the degree or extent of an action or quality. It is often translated as "completely", "totally", or "entirely" in English.

Description: "Τελείως" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It emphasizes the completeness or thoroughness of an action or state. It can be used to express a high degree or intensity, indicating that something is done to its fullest extent. It can also be used to convey a sense of certainty or finality.
