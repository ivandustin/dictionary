Etymology: The word "τελειώσας" comes from the Greek verb "τελειόω" (teleioo), which means "to complete" or "to finish." The suffix "-σας" is the aorist tense ending in Greek, indicating past tense.

Grammar: "Τελειώσας" is the masculine singular form of the aorist participle of the verb "τελειόω." In Greek grammar, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past.

Description: "Τελειώσας" can be translated as "having completed" or "having finished." It describes someone or something that has finished an action in the past.
