Etymology: The word "τετηρηκέν" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tēréō), which means "to keep" or "to guard." It is in the perfect tense form, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "τετηρηκέν" is the third person singular form of the verb "τηρέω" in the perfect tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The perfect tense is used to express an action that was completed in the past but has ongoing effects in the present.

Description: "τετηρηκέν" means "he/she/it has kept" or "he/she/it has guarded" in English. It implies that someone or something has performed an action of keeping or guarding in the past, and the effects of that action continue in the present.
