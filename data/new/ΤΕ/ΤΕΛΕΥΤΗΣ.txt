Etymology: The word "τελευτης" (teleutes) comes from the Greek verb "τελευτάω" (teleutao), which means "to come to an end" or "to die."

Grammar: In Greek, "τελευτης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "τελευτου" (teleutou).

Description: "Τελευτης" is a Greek word that means "end" or "death." It is often used to refer to the final stage or conclusion of something, such as the end of a journey, the end of a life, or the end of an event.
