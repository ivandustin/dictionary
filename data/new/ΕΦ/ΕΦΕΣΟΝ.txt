Etymology: The word "εφεσον" comes from the Greek word "Ἔφεσος" (Ephesos), which was the name of an ancient Greek city in Asia Minor (modern-day Turkey).

Grammar: "Εφεσον" is a noun in the accusative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it is used to refer to the city of Ephesus.

Description: Ephesus was an important city in ancient times, known for its rich history and cultural significance. It was a major center of trade and commerce, as well as a hub for religious and philosophical activities. The city was home to the Temple of Artemis, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Today, the ruins of Ephesus are a popular tourist attraction, showcasing the remnants of its ancient glory.
