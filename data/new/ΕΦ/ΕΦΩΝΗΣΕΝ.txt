Etymology: The word "εφωνησεν" comes from the Greek verb "φωνέω" (phonéo), which means "to sound" or "to speak out". The prefix "ε-" (e-) in this word indicates the past tense, so "εφωνησεν" translates to "he/she/it sounded" or "he/she/it spoke out".

Grammar: "Εφωνησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. In this case, it indicates that someone or something "sounded" or "spoke out" at a specific point in the past.

Description: "Εφωνησεν" is a verb in Greek that describes the action of making a sound or speaking out. It can be used to refer to any type of sound or speech, depending on the context.
