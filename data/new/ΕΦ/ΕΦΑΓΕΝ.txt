Etymology: The word "εφαγεν" (efagen) comes from the Greek verb "φάγω" (fago), which means "to eat". It is in the aorist tense, indicating a completed action in the past.

Grammar: "Εφαγεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "φάγω" (fago) in the aorist tense. In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "Εφαγεν" is a verb form that indicates that someone or something ate in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it ate" in English.
