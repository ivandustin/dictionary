Etymology: The word "εφυγον" (efygon) comes from the Greek verb "φεύγω" (fevgo), which means "to flee" or "to escape".

Grammar: "Εφυγον" is the past tense form of the verb "φεύγω" (fevgo) in the first person singular. It is used to indicate that the speaker or subject has fled or escaped in the past.

Description: "Εφυγον" is a Greek word that specifically refers to the act of fleeing or escaping in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone running away from danger or escaping from a difficult situation.
