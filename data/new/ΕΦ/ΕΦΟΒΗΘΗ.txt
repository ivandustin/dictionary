Etymology: The word "εφοβηθη" (efovithi) comes from the Greek verb "φοβάμαι" (fovame), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid". The prefix "ε-" (e-) is added to the verb to indicate a passive voice, so "εφοβηθη" (efovithi) can be translated as "was feared" or "was afraid".

Grammar: "Εφοβηθη" (efovithi) is the third person singular form of the verb "φοβάμαι" (fovame) in the past tense. It is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject of the verb is receiving the action rather than performing it.

Description: "Εφοβηθη" (efovithi) describes a state of being feared or afraid. It can be used to talk about someone or something that experienced fear or was feared by others in the past.
