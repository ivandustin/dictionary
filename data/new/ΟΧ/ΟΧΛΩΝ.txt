Etymology: The word "οχλων" (ochlon) comes from the Greek verb "οχλεω" (ochleo), which means "to crowd" or "to throng". 

Grammar: "Οχλων" is the present participle form of the verb "οχλεω" in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description: "Οχλων" is used to describe a person who is crowding or thronging. It can also refer to a person who is causing a disturbance or creating chaos in a crowded place.
