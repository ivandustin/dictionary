The word "αφιησιν" (aphiēsin) is a Greek noun that means "forgiveness" or "remission." 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "αφίημι" (aphiēmi), which means "to let go," "to release," or "to forgive." 

Grammar: "Αφιησιν" is a feminine noun in the accusative case. 

Description: The word is often used in a religious or spiritual context to refer to the act of forgiving sins or granting pardon. It can also be used more generally to refer to the act of letting go or releasing something.
