Word: αφες

Etymology:
The word αφες (aphes) is a Greek word that comes from the verb αφίημι (aphiemi), which means "to let go" or "to release". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb ἵημι (himi), meaning "to send".

Grammar:
αφες is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, singular number, and masculine gender.

The word αφες has several meanings in Greek, depending on the context in which it is used. Some of its common meanings include "forgiveness", "remission", "release", "discharge", and "liberty".
