Word: αφησω

Etymology:
The word αφησω (afiso) is a verb in the Greek language, which means "to let go" or "to release". It is derived from the Greek word "αφίημι" (afí̱ēmi), which means "to send away" or "to let go".

Grammar:
αφησω is a first-person singular present tense verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it follows the regular pattern of conjugation for verbs that end in -ω. The verb stem is αφήσ-, and the ending -ω indicates the present tense, first-person singular form.

Description:
The verb αφησω is commonly used in everyday Greek language to express the act of letting go or releasing something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as letting go of a physical object, releasing a person from a responsibility, or even letting go of a thought or emotion. The verb can also be used figuratively to express the act of forgiving or pardoning someone.
