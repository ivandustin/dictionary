Word: αφορμήν (aphormin)

Etymology:
The word αφορμήν (aphormin) comes from the Greek verb αφορμάω (aphormao), which means "to set in motion" or "to give impetus". The noun αφορμήν (aphormin) is derived from this verb and means "impetus", "stimulus", or "motivation".

Grammar:
αφορμήν (aphormin) is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
αφορμήν (aphormin) refers to a force or influence that sets something in motion or gives it impetus. It can be used in a literal sense, such as the force that propels a moving object, or in a figurative sense, such as the motivation that drives a person to take action. The word is often used in the context of starting or initiating something, such as a project or a conversation.
