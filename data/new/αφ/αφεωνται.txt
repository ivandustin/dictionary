Word: αφεωνται

Etymology: The word αφεωνται is derived from the Greek verb αφίημι (aphiemi), which means "to let go" or "to release". The suffix -ονται (-ontai) is a third person plural present passive indicative ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Grammar: αφεωνται is a third person plural present passive indicative form of the verb αφίημι (aphiemi). In this form, the subject is being acted upon and is being released or let go by someone or something else.

Description: αφεωνται is a Greek verb form that means "they are being released" or "they are being let go". It is often used in a passive sense, indicating that the subject is not actively doing the releasing or letting go, but is instead being acted upon. This verb form can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in reference to prisoners being released from jail or to debts being forgiven.
