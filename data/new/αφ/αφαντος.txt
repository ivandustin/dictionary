Word: αφαντος

Etymology:
The word αφαντος comes from the Greek word "α-" which means "not" and "φαντός" which means "visible" or "apparent". Therefore, αφαντος means "invisible" or "unseen".

Grammar:
αφαντος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined in different cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
αφαντος is used to describe something that cannot be seen or is not apparent. It can refer to physical objects that are invisible, such as gases or particles, or to abstract concepts that are not easily perceived, such as emotions or thoughts. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is hidden or unknown.
