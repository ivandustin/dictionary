Word: αφαιρειν

Etymology:
The word αφαιρειν (aphairein) comes from the Greek root words από (apo) meaning "away from" and αίρω (airō) meaning "to lift up" or "to take away". Therefore, αφαιρειν means "to take away" or "to remove".

Grammar:
αφαιρειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Present tense:
Αφαιρώ (aphairó) - I take away
Αφαιρείς (aphaireís) - You take away
Αφαιρεί (aphaireí) - He/She/It takes away
Αφαιρούμε (aphairoúme) - We take away
Αφαιρείτε (aphaireíte) - You (plural) take away
Αφαιρούν (aphairoún) - They take away

Description:
αφαιρειν is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of taking something away or removing something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as removing an object from a location, taking away someone's rights or privileges, or subtracting a number from another number. The verb is often used in combination with other words to create compound verbs, such as αποαφαιρώ (apoaphairó) which means "to completely remove" or "to eliminate".
