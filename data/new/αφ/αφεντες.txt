Word: αφεντες

Etymology:
The word αφεντες (afentes) is derived from the Turkish word "efendi" which means "master" or "lord". It was adopted into the Greek language during the Ottoman Empire's rule over Greece.

Grammar:
αφεντες is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "masters" or "lords". It is used to refer to a group of people who have authority or power over others.

Description:
In modern Greek, αφεντες is often used to refer to wealthy or powerful individuals, or to describe a group of people who are in charge of a particular organization or institution. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to anyone who has a position of authority or influence.
