Word: αφωνος

Etymology: The word αφωνος comes from the Greek prefix α- (meaning "not") and the word φωνή (meaning "voice" or "sound").

Grammar: αφωνος is an adjective in Greek, and it is declined like this:

Singular:
- Masculine: αφώνος
- Feminine: αφώνη
- Neuter: αφώνο

Plural:
- Masculine: αφώνοι
- Feminine: αφώνες
- Neuter: αφώνα

Description: αφωνος means "voiceless" or "silent" in Greek. It can be used to describe a person who is unable to speak or make sounds, or it can be used to describe a situation or environment that is quiet or without noise. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is not speaking up or expressing their opinions.
