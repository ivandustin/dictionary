The word αφανής (aphanis) comes from the Greek word αφανής (aphanes), which means "invisible" or "unseen". It is derived from the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the word φανής (phanis), which means "visible" or "apparent".

αφανής is an adjective in Greek, which means it describes a noun. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). It can also be declined in the singular and plural forms.

αφανής is used to describe something that is invisible, unseen, or hidden. It can also be used to describe something that is obscure or unknown. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a person who is missing or has disappeared.
