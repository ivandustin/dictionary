The word "αφορισωσιν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the third person plural, aorist tense, subjunctive mood, and active voice. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "αφορίζω" (aphorizo), which means "to separate" or "to set apart."

Grammar: As mentioned above, "αφορισωσιν" is a verb in the Greek language. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, while the subjunctive mood suggests that the action is uncertain or hypothetical. The active voice means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: The word "αφορισωσιν" can be translated to mean "they might separate" or "they may set apart." It is often used in a religious or legal context to refer to the act of separating or excluding someone or something from a group or community.
