The word "αφρονες" (afrones) is a Greek noun in the plural form. Its etymology is derived from the Greek word "αφρός" (afrós), which means "foam". In ancient Greek mythology, the Afrones were sea deities who were associated with the foam of the sea.

In terms of grammar, "αφρονες" is a third-declension noun in the plural form. It is masculine in gender and has a nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative case.

Description-wise, "αφρονες" is not a commonly used word in modern Greek. It is mostly found in ancient Greek texts and mythology.
