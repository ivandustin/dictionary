Word: αφαιρεθησεται

Etymology: The word αφαιρεθησεται is derived from the Greek verb αφαιρέω (aphaireo), which means "to take away" or "to remove". The suffix -θησεται (-thesetai) is a passive voice ending, indicating that the action of taking away is being done to the subject of the verb.

Grammar: αφαιρεθησεται is a third person singular passive form of the verb αφαιρέω (aphaireo). It is in the future tense and indicative mood. The verb is conjugated in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action of the verb rather than performing it.

Description: αφαιρεθησεται can be translated to English as "it will be taken away" or "it will be removed". The word is often used in legal or formal contexts to describe the process of something being taken away or removed from someone or something else.
