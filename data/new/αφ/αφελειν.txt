Word: αφελειν

Etymology:
The word αφελειν comes from the Greek verb αφαιρέω (aphaireo), which means "to take away" or "to remove". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb αίρω (airo), meaning "to lift up" or "to take away".

Grammar:
αφελειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. The verb stem is αφελ-, and the ending -ειν indicates the infinitive form.

Description:
αφελειν means "to remove" or "to take away" in Greek. It is a common verb used in various contexts, such as in medicine, where it refers to the removal of a body part or tissue, or in mathematics, where it refers to the process of subtracting one number from another. In a figurative sense, it can also mean to eliminate or get rid of something undesirable or harmful.
