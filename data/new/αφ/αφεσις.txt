Word: αφεσις

Etymology:
The word αφεσις (aphesis) comes from the Greek verb αφίημι (aphiemi), which means "to let go" or "to release". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb ίημι (iemi), meaning "to send".

Grammar:
αφεσις is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
αφεσις is a Greek word that can be translated as "release", "forgiveness", or "remission". In the New Testament, it is often used to refer to the forgiveness of sins. For example, in Matthew 26:28, Jesus says, "For this is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many for the forgiveness (αφεσις) of sins." The word can also be used more generally to refer to the release or liberation of someone or something.
