Word: αφροσυνη

Etymology: The word αφροσυνη (aphrosyne) comes from the Greek words αφρος (aphros) meaning "foam" and συνη (syne) meaning "union" or "association". In Greek mythology, Aphrodite, the goddess of love and beauty, was born from the foam of the sea, which may have influenced the use of the word αφρος in this context.

Grammar: αφροσυνη is a feminine noun in Greek. It is declined like other feminine nouns ending in -η. The plural form is αφροσυνες (aphrosynes).

Description: αφροσυνη refers to frivolity, light-heartedness, or lack of seriousness. It can also refer to a lack of self-control or discipline. The word is often used in a negative context to describe behavior that is considered inappropriate or irresponsible.
