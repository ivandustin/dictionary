Word: αφορίσας

Etymology:
The word αφορίσας comes from the Greek verb αφορίζω (aphorizo), which means "to separate" or "to set apart". It is derived from the prefix από (apo), meaning "away from", and the verb χωρίζω (chorizo), meaning "to divide" or "to separate".

Grammar:
αφορίσας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and second person singular form. It is the aorist tense of the verb αφορίζω (aphorizo), which means "you separated" or "you set apart".

Description:
αφορίσας is a Greek verb that means "to separate" or "to set apart". It is often used in a religious context to refer to the act of excommunication or expulsion from a community. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the act of separating oneself from sin or from those who practice sin.
