Word: αφρων

Etymology:
The word αφρων (aphron) is derived from the Greek word α- (a-) which means "not" and φρονέω (phroneo) which means "to think". Therefore, αφρων means "not thinking" or "foolish".

Grammar:
αφρων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also used as an adjective to describe someone who is foolish or lacking in common sense.

Description:
αφρων is a term used to describe someone who is foolish, lacking in common sense, or acting in a reckless manner. It is often used in a derogatory way to criticize someone's behavior or decisions. In ancient Greek literature, the term was often used to describe characters who were arrogant and made poor decisions, leading to their downfall.
