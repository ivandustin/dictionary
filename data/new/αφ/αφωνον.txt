Word: αφωνον

Etymology:
The word αφωνον is derived from the Greek word "αφωνος" (aphonos), which means "voiceless" or "mute". The suffix "-ον" (-on) is a neuter singular ending in Greek.

Grammar:
αφωνον is a neuter singular adjective in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
αφωνον is an adjective that describes something as voiceless or mute. It can be used to describe a person who is unable to speak, or an animal that does not make any sounds. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is silent or uncommunicative.
