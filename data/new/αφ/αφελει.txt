The word "αφελει" (apheli) is a verb in Greek that means "to remove, to take away, to withdraw". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek prefix "από" (apo) which means "away from" and the verb "έχω" (echo) which means "to have". 

Grammar: "αφελει" is a third person singular present indicative active verb. 

Description: The word is commonly used in everyday Greek language to express the action of taking something away or removing it from a certain place or situation. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of withdrawing from a conversation or a relationship.
