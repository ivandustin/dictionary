The word "αφετε" (aphete) is a verb in Greek, which means "to let go" or "to release". It is derived from the Greek word "αφίημι" (aphiemi), which also means "to let go" or "to leave behind". 

In terms of grammar, "αφετε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb. This means that it is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. For example, "Αφήστε τον σκύλο να περπατήσει" (Aphiste ton skilo na perpatisei) means "Let the dog walk".

Overall, "αφετε" is a versatile verb that can be used in a variety of contexts to express the idea of letting go or releasing something.
