Word: αφωριζεν

Etymology: αφωριζεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word αφορίζω (aphorizo), which means "to separate" or "to set apart". The suffix -εν (-en) indicates that it is in the present tense.

Grammar: αφωριζεν is a present tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated in the third person singular form.

Description: αφωριζεν means "he/she/it separates" or "he/she/it sets apart" in Greek. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as when referring to the separation of the righteous from the unrighteous. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe any act of separation or distinction.
