Word: αφιλαργυρον

Etymology:
The word αφιλαργυρον is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and φιλαργυρος (philargyros) meaning "avaricious" or "greedy for money". 

Grammar:
αφιλαργυρον is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
The word αφιλαργυρον describes someone or something that is not greedy for money, or someone who is not materialistic. It can also be used to describe a person who is generous or unselfish.
