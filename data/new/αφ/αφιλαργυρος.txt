Word: αφιλαργυρος

Etymology: The word αφιλαργυρος comes from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" and φιλαργυρος (philargyros) meaning "loving money". 

Grammar: αφιλαργυρος is an adjective in Greek. It is in the masculine form, singular, and nominative case. 

Description: αφιλαργυρος is used to describe someone who is not interested in money or material possessions. It can also be used to describe something that is not motivated by financial gain. The word is often used in a positive sense, to describe someone who is selfless or generous.
