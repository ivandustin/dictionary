Word: αφελωμαι

Etymology:
The word αφελωμαι comes from the Greek verb αφαιρέω (aphaireo), which means "to take away" or "to remove". The prefix αφ- (aph-) means "away from" or "off", while the root word ελω (elo) means "to take". The suffix -μαι (-mai) indicates that the verb is in the middle or passive voice.

Grammar:
αφελωμαι is a first-person singular middle/passive form of the verb αφαιρέω (aphaireo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action.

Description:
αφελωμαι can be translated as "I am taken away" or "I am removed". It can also mean "I am separated" or "I am withdrawn". The word is often used in a metaphorical sense, to describe a person who is detached or removed from a situation or emotion. It can also be used to describe a physical removal or separation, such as the removal of a tumor or the separation of two objects.
