Word: αφης

Etymology:
The word αφης (aphes) is derived from the Greek verb αφίημι (aphiemi), which means "to let go" or "to release". It is related to the English word "aphesis", which refers to the release or forgiveness of sins.

Grammar:
αφης is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, singular number, and feminine gender.

Description:
αφης is a Greek word that can be translated to mean "forgiveness" or "pardon". It is often used in a religious context, referring to the forgiveness of sins. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the forgiveness of sins through Jesus Christ. The concept of αφης is also important in Greek philosophy, where it is seen as a virtue that involves letting go of anger and resentment towards others.
