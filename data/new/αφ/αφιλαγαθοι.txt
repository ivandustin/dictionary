Word: αφιλαγαθοι

Etymology:
The word αφιλαγαθοι is derived from the Greek words α- (a-) meaning "not" or "without", φιλ- (phil-) meaning "love" or "friendship", and αγαθος (agathos) meaning "good" or "virtuous". Therefore, the literal meaning of αφιλαγαθοι is "without love for good".

Grammar:
αφιλαγαθοι is an adjective in the masculine plural form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, αφιλαγαθοι can modify masculine plural nouns in the nominative, accusative, and vocative cases.

Description:
αφιλαγαθοι is a term used to describe people who lack a love for goodness or virtue. It can refer to individuals who are selfish, greedy, or immoral. The term is often used in a negative context to criticize someone's behavior or character.
