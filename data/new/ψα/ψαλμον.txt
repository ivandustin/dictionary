Word: ψαλμόν (psalmon)

Etymology: The word ψαλμόν comes from the Greek word ψαλμός (psalmos), which means "song sung to a harp." It is derived from the verb ψάλλω (psallo), which means "to play a stringed instrument."

Grammar: ψαλμόν is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb.

Description: ψαλμόν refers to a psalm or a sacred song that is sung in religious services. In the Christian tradition, psalms are often sung during worship services and are considered an important part of the liturgy. The Book of Psalms in the Bible contains 150 psalms, which are attributed to King David and other authors. Psalms are also an important part of Jewish worship and are recited during daily prayers.
