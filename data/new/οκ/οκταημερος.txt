Word: οκταημερος

Etymology: The word οκταημερος comes from the Greek words οκτώ (októ), meaning "eight," and ημέρα (iméra), meaning "day." Therefore, οκταημερος literally means "eight-day."

Grammar: οκταημερος is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: οκταημερος is used to describe something that lasts for eight days or occurs every eight days. It can refer to a period of time, such as an eight-day festival or celebration, or to a recurring event that happens every eight days.
