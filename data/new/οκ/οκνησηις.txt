The word "οκνησηις" (oknesis) is a Greek noun that means "hesitation" or "reluctance". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek verb "οκνέω" (okneo), which means "to hesitate" or "to be reluctant". 

Grammar: "οκνησηις" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. 

Description: The word is often used to describe a feeling of hesitation or reluctance to do something, such as taking action or making a decision. It can also refer to a general lack of motivation or enthusiasm. In philosophy, the term "oknesis" is used to describe the process of deliberation and decision-making.
