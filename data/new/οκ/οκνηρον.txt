Word: οκνηρον

Etymology:
The word οκνηρον (okneron) is derived from the Greek word οκνέω (okneo), which means "to be idle" or "to be lazy". The suffix -ρον (-ron) is added to the stem to form an adjective, which means "lazy" or "indolent".

Grammar:
In Greek, οκνηρον is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
Οκνηρον is a Greek adjective that describes someone who is lazy or indolent. It can be used to describe a person who is unwilling to work or make an effort, or someone who is slow to act or respond. The word can also be used figuratively to describe a situation or process that is slow or sluggish.
