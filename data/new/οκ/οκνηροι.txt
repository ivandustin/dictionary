Word: οκνηροι

Etymology:
The word οκνηροι (okneroi) is derived from the Greek word οκνέω (okneo), which means "to be idle" or "to be lazy". The suffix -ρος (-ros) is added to the root word to form the adjective οκνηρος (okneros), which means "lazy" or "idle". The plural form of οκνηρος is οκνηροι (okneroi).

Grammar:
In Greek, οκνηροι is a masculine plural adjective that agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. It can be used to describe a group of lazy or idle men.

Description:
Οκνηροι is a negative adjective that describes people who are lazy, idle, or unwilling to work. It can be used to criticize someone's work ethic or to describe a group of people who are not productive. The word can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is slow to act or hesitant to make decisions.
