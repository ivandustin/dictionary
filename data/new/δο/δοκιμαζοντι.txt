Word: δοκιμαζοντι

Etymology: δοκιμαζοντι is the present active participle of the Greek verb δοκιμάζω (dokimazo), which means "to test" or "to examine". The word comes from the root word δοκιμή (dokime), which means "trial" or "test".

Grammar: δοκιμαζοντι is a participle in the present tense, active voice, and masculine gender. It is declined in the dative case, which indicates the indirect object of the verb.

Description: δοκιμαζοντι can be translated as "to the one who is testing" or "to the one who is examining". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to God or Jesus as the one who tests or examines the faith of believers. The word implies a process of scrutiny or evaluation, and suggests that the subject is being put to the test in order to determine its worth or validity.
