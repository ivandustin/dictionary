Word: δογμάτων

Etymology:
The word δογμάτων is derived from the Greek word δόγμα (dogma), which means "opinion" or "belief". The suffix -ων (-on) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple opinions or beliefs.

Grammar:
δογμάτων is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "dogmas" or "beliefs".

Description:
δογμάτων refers to a set of established beliefs or doctrines, often associated with a particular religion or ideology. These beliefs are considered to be authoritative and are often not open to debate or questioning. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to any set of established principles or beliefs.
