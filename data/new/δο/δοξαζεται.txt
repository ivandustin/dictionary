Word: δοξάζεται

Etymology: δοξάζεται comes from the Greek verb δοξάζω (doxazo), which means "to glorify" or "to praise". The suffix -ται (-tai) indicates that the verb is in the passive voice, meaning "is glorified" or "is praised".

Grammar: δοξάζεται is a third person singular present passive indicative verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the verb δοξάζω (doxazo), which is a first conjugation verb in Greek.

Description: δοξάζεται is a Greek verb that means "is glorified" or "is praised". It is often used in religious contexts to describe the glorification of God or other divine beings. The passive voice emphasizes that the subject is receiving the action of the verb, rather than performing it.
