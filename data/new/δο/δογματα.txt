Word: δογματα

Etymology:
The word δογματα (dogmata) is derived from the Greek word δόγμα (dogma), which means "opinion" or "belief." It is related to the verb δοκεῖν (dokein), which means "to seem" or "to think."

Grammar:
δογματα is a plural noun in Greek. It is the plural form of δόγμα (dogma). In English, it can be translated as "dogmas" or "doctrines."

Description:
δογματα refers to a set of principles or beliefs that are considered to be true or authoritative. It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to describe a system of beliefs or teachings. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any set of established beliefs or principles.
