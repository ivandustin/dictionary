Word: δογματιζεσθε

Etymology: The word δογματιζεσθε comes from the Greek word δόγμα (dogma), which means "a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true." The suffix -ιζεσθε (-ize) is added to form the verb, which means "to make dogmatic."

Grammar: δογματιζεσθε is a second-person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. It is derived from the verb δογματίζω (dogmatizo), which means "to lay down a principle or set of principles as incontrovertibly true."

Description: δογματιζεσθε is a command to a group of people to make dogmatic statements or to assert their beliefs as incontrovertibly true. It is often used in a negative sense to criticize someone for being too rigid or inflexible in their beliefs. The word is commonly used in religious contexts, where it refers to the act of imposing a set of beliefs on others without allowing for any dissent or questioning.
