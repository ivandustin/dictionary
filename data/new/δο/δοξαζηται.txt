The word "δοξαζηται" (doxazetai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root word "δοξάζω" (doxazo), which means "to glorify" or "to praise." 

In its present tense, passive voice, and indicative mood form, "δοξαζηται" means "he/she/it is being glorified" or "he/she/it is being praised." 

In terms of grammar, "δοξαζηται" is a third-person singular verb, which means it refers to a single subject (he/she/it) performing the action of being glorified or praised. 

Overall, "δοξαζηται" is a word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe the act of giving glory or praise to a higher power.
