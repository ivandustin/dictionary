Word: δοκοῦντες

Etymology: δοκοῦντες is a present active participle of the Greek verb δοκέω (dokéō), which means "to seem" or "to appear". The word comes from the root δοκ- (dok-), which means "to think" or "to suppose".

Grammar: δοκοῦντες is a masculine plural nominative present active participle. It is used to describe the subject of a sentence as "seeming" or "appearing" to do something. In this form, it is often translated as "those who seem" or "those who appear".

Description: δοκοῦντες is a word that is used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the book of Acts. It is used to describe a group of people who are considering or attempting to do something. For example, in Acts 5:36-37, it is used to describe a group of men who were trying to start a rebellion against the Roman authorities. The word emphasizes the appearance or perception of their actions, rather than their actual intentions or success.
