The word δοκουμεν (dokoumen) is a Greek verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It comes from the root word δοκέω (dokéō), which means "to seem" or "to appear." 

In its present tense form, δοκουμεν means "we seem" or "we appear." The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, so it could also be translated as "we seem to ourselves" or "we appear to ourselves." 

In terms of grammar, δοκουμεν is a first-person plural verb, meaning it refers to a group of people (in this case, "we"). It is in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening currently. 

Overall, δοκουμεν is a word that expresses the idea of appearance or perception, and it is used to describe how something seems or appears to a group of people.
