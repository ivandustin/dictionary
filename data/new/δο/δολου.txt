The word δολου (dolou) is a noun in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the Greek word δόλος (dolos), which means deceit or trickery. In terms of grammar, δολου is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in the sentence. 

As for its description, δολου can be translated to mean "of deceit" or "of trickery." It is often used in the context of describing someone or something that is deceptive or dishonest. For example, one might say "του δολου" (tou dolou) to refer to someone who is known for their deceitful behavior.
