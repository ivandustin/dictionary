The word "δουλοις" (doulois) is a Greek noun in the dative plural form, which means "to the slaves" or "for the slaves". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root "δοῦλος" (doulos), which means "slave" or "servant". 

Grammar: In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. In this case, "δουλοις" is the dative plural form of "δοῦλος". 

Description: The word "δουλοις" is commonly used in ancient Greek literature to refer to slaves or servants. It is often used in the context of describing the treatment of slaves or their role in society.
