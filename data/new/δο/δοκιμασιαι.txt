Word: δοκιμασιαι

Etymology: The word δοκιμασιαι comes from the Greek verb δοκιμάζω (dokimazo), which means "to test" or "to examine". The suffix -αι (-ai) indicates that the word is plural.

Grammar: δοκιμασιαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: δοκιμασιαι refers to tests or trials that are used to examine or evaluate something. In the New Testament, the word is often used to describe the testing of one's faith or character. For example, in James 1:2-3, it says "Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds, because you know that the testing of your faith produces perseverance."
