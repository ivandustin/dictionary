Word: δουλευοντες

Etymology:
δουλευοντες is a present participle form of the Greek verb δουλεύω (douleuo), which means "to serve" or "to work as a slave". The word is derived from the noun δοῦλος (doulos), which means "slave" or "servant".

Grammar:
δουλευοντες is a present participle form of the verb δουλεύω (douleuo), which is in the present tense and active voice. The participle form indicates that the action of serving or working as a slave is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
δουλευοντες is a Greek word that describes people who are serving or working as slaves. The word has a negative connotation, as it implies that the individuals are being forced to work against their will and are not free to make their own choices. In ancient Greece, slavery was a common practice, and many people were forced into servitude for various reasons, such as debt, war, or being born into a slave family. Today, the word δουλευοντες is not commonly used in modern Greek, as slavery has been abolished in most parts of the world.
