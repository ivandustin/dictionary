Word: δουλεύω

Etymology:
The word δουλεύω (douleuo) comes from the Greek root word δοῦλος (doulos), which means "slave" or "servant". The verb form δουλεύω (douleuo) means "to serve" or "to work as a slave".

Grammar:
δουλεύω (douleuo) is a regular Greek verb that belongs to the first conjugation. It is a present active indicative verb that can be conjugated in all tenses and moods. The verb stem is δουλευ-, and the endings change depending on the tense and mood.

Description:
δουλεύω (douleuo) is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of serving or working as a slave. It can also be used more broadly to describe any kind of work or service. The verb is often used in the New Testament to describe the service of God or the work of the apostles.
