Word: δουλεύειν

Etymology: δουλεύειν comes from the Greek word δοῦλος (doulos), meaning "slave" or "servant". The verb form δουλεύειν means "to serve" or "to work as a slave".

Grammar: δουλεύειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν to the stem δουλευ-. The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular: δουλεύω (douleúo) - I serve/work
- Plural: δουλεύουμε (douleúoume) - We serve/work

Description: δουλεύειν is a common Greek verb that is used to describe the act of serving or working. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in the workplace, in the home, or in a religious setting. The word carries connotations of obedience and submission, as it is often used to describe the work of slaves or servants. However, it can also be used in a positive sense to describe the act of serving others out of love or respect.
