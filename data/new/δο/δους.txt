Etymology:
δους is a Greek word that comes from the verb δίδωμι (didomi), which means "to give". It is the aorist participle of the verb, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. 

Grammar:
δους is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also the aorist participle of the verb δίδωμι (didomi), which means "having given" or "having offered". 

Description:
δους is a word that refers to someone who has given or offered something in the past. It can also be translated as "donor" or "giver". In Greek mythology, there are several figures who are referred to as δους, including the god Zeus, who was known for his generosity and gift-giving.
