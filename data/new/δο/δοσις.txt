Word: δοσις

Etymology:
The word δοσις (dosis) comes from the Greek verb δίδωμι (didōmi), which means "to give". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-, which means "to give" or "to put". The word δοσις originally referred to the act of giving or administering a dose of medicine.

In Greek, δοσις is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its genitive form is δόσεως (dóseōs), its dative form is δόσει (dósei), and its accusative form is δόσιν (dósin). The word can be used in both the singular and plural forms.

Description:
The word δοσις has several meanings in Greek, including "dose", "portion", "measure", and "gift". In medicine, it refers specifically to the amount of a drug or medicine that is prescribed or administered to a patient. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any amount or portion of something that is given or received.
