Word: δοχην

Etymology:
The word δοχην (dochēn) is derived from the Greek word δοχεῖον (docheion), which means "a container for holding liquids." It is derived from the verb δέχομαι (dechomai), which means "to receive."

Grammar:
δοχην is a noun in the singular form. It is a feminine noun in the first declension, and its nominative singular form is δοχη.

Description:
δοχην refers to a container or vessel used for holding liquids. It can be made of various materials such as clay, metal, or glass. In ancient Greece, it was commonly used for storing and serving wine or water. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a pitcher or jug.
