Word: δουλευουσιν

Etymology: δουλευουσιν is a present active indicative verb form in Greek, derived from the root word δουλεύω (douleuo), which means "to serve" or "to work as a slave". The word is derived from the noun δοῦλος (doulos), which means "slave" or "servant".

Grammar: δουλευουσιν is a third-person plural present active indicative verb form in Greek. It is used to describe an action that is currently happening in the present, and the subject of the verb is a group of people.

Description: δουλευουσιν can be translated to "they are serving" or "they are working as slaves". The word carries a connotation of servitude or subjugation, as it is derived from the word for "slave". In ancient Greek society, slavery was a common practice, and many people were forced to work as slaves. Today, the word can be used to describe any situation where someone is serving or working for someone else, whether voluntarily or involuntarily.
