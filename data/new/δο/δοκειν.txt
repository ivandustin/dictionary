Word: δοκειν

Etymology:
δοκειν (dokein) is a Greek verb that means "to seem" or "to appear". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *deḱ-, which means "to show" or "to appear".

Grammar:
δοκειν is an infinitive verb in Greek, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense. It is often used with auxiliary verbs to form compound tenses.

Description:
δοκειν is a common verb in Greek and is used to express the idea of something appearing or seeming a certain way. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as expressing an opinion or making a judgment about something. For example, "It seems that the weather will be nice today" could be translated as "Δοκεί ότι ο καιρός θα είναι ωραίος σήμερα" in Greek.
