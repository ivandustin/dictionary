Word: δοθεντος

Etymology:
δοθεντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb δίδωμι (didomi), which means "to give". The word is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or origin. Therefore, δοθεντος means "given" or "granted".

Grammar:
δοθεντος is a masculine singular adjective in the genitive case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this case, it could modify a masculine singular noun, such as ἄνθρωπος (anthropos), meaning "man".

Description:
δοθεντος is a word that expresses the idea of something being given or granted. It can be used to describe a gift, a privilege, or a blessing. In Greek literature, it is often used in the context of divine gifts or blessings. For example, in the New Testament, it is used to describe the gift of salvation that comes from God.
