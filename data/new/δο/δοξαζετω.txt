Word: δοξάζετω

Etymology:
δοξάζετω comes from the Greek word δόξα (doxa) which means "glory" or "praise". The suffix -ζω (-zo) is added to the end of the word to form the imperative form of the verb, which means "let him/her/it glorify".

Grammar:
δοξάζετω is a verb in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests. It is in the third person singular form, which means that the subject of the sentence is he/she/it.

Description:
δοξάζετω is a Greek verb that means "let him/her/it glorify". It is often used in religious contexts, such as in hymns or prayers, to encourage people to praise or worship God. It can also be used more generally to encourage people to give praise or recognition to someone or something.
