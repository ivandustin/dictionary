Word: σιωπων

Etymology:
The word σιωπων (siōpōn) is derived from the Greek verb σιωπάω (siōpáō), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet." It is formed from the prefix σι- (si-), which means "together," and the verb ὀπάω (opáō), which means "to speak."

Description:
The word σιωπων (siōpōn) refers to someone who is silent or quiet. It can also be used to describe someone who is keeping a secret or not revealing their thoughts or feelings. In Greek literature, the word is often used to describe characters who are stoic or reserved in their demeanor.
