Word: σιδηρου

The word σιδηρου (sidirou) comes from the Greek word σίδηρος (sidēros), which means "iron". The word σίδηρος is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁sh₂id- meaning "to split, cleave".

σιδηρου is a genitive singular form of the noun σίδηρος (sidēros). In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description:
σιδηρου is a Greek word that means "of iron" or "made of iron". It is often used in scientific or technical contexts to describe objects or materials that are composed of iron.
