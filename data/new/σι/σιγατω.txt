Word: σιγατω

Etymology: σιγατω comes from the Greek verb σιγάω (sigáo), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet."

Grammar: σιγατω is a verb in the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or make requests. It is in the third person singular form, meaning "let him/her/it be silent" or "let him/her/it keep quiet."

Description: σιγατω is a command to be quiet or silent. It is often used in situations where someone is being too loud or disruptive, and someone else wants them to stop. It can also be used as a way to tell someone to keep a secret or not reveal information.
