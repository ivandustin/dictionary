Word: σιτευτον

Etymology:
The word σιτευτον comes from the Greek word σιτευτός (siteutos), which means "cultivated" or "planted". It is derived from the verb σιτεύω (siteuo), which means "to sow" or "to plant".

σιτευτον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can be declined as follows:

Nominative/Accusative: σιτευτον
Genitive: σιτευτου
Dative: σιτευτῳ
Vocative: σιτευτον

Description:
σιτευτον refers to a cultivated or planted area of land. It can also refer to a crop or harvest that has been grown on such land. The word is often used in the context of agriculture and farming.
