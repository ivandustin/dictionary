Word: σιτον

The word σιτον (siton) is derived from the Greek word σῖτος (sitos), which means "grain" or "wheat". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₁i- ("to sow, plant").

Grammar:
In Greek, σιτον is a neuter noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is σιτον, and its genitive singular form is σιτου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
σιτον refers to grain or wheat, which is a staple food in many cultures. In ancient Greece, it was a major crop and was used to make bread, porridge, and other food items. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to wheat or other grains.
