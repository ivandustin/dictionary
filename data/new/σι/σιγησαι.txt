Word: σιγησαι

Etymology: σιγησαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and infinitive mood in Ancient Greek. It comes from the root word σιγάω (sigao), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet."

Grammar: σιγησαι is a second aorist active infinitive verb in Ancient Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -σαι (-sai) to the root σιγάω (sigao). The second aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: σιγησαι means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet" in Ancient Greek. It is often used in the context of someone being told to be quiet or to stop talking. It can also be used to describe a state of silence or quietness.
