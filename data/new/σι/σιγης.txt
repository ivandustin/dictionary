Word: σιγης

Etymology: The word σιγης (siges) comes from the Greek verb σιγάω (sigao), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet". The noun σιγης refers to the state of being silent or quiet.

Grammar: σιγης is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to show possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description: σιγης is a word that describes the state of being silent or quiet. It can refer to a physical absence of sound, or to a figurative silence, such as a lack of communication or expression. The word can also be used to describe a peaceful or tranquil atmosphere. In Greek literature, σιγης is often used to convey a sense of mystery or suspense.
