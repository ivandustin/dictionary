Word: γυναικες

Etymology:
The word γυναικες (gynaikes) is derived from the ancient Greek word γυνή (gynē), which means "woman" or "wife". The plural form γυναικες (gynaikes) means "women".

Grammar:
In Greek, γυναικες (gynaikes) is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is the plural form of γυνή (gynē), which is also a feminine noun.

Description:
γυναικες (gynaikes) is a common Greek word that refers to women. It can be used to describe a group of women or to refer to women in general. The word is often used in everyday conversation and in literature.
