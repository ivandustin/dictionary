Word: γυναικας

Etymology: The word γυναικας (gynaikas) is derived from the ancient Greek word γυνή (gynē), which means "woman" or "wife". The suffix -ας (-as) is a genitive ending, indicating possession or relationship.

Grammar: γυναικας is the genitive singular form of the noun γυνή (gynē). In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case (nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.) and number (singular or plural). The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated in English as "of" or "'s".

It is often used in Greek literature and language to refer to women in general, or to a specific woman in a possessive or relational sense.
