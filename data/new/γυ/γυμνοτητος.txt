Word: γυμνότητος

Etymology:
The word γυμνότητος (gymnotitos) is derived from the Greek words γυμνός (gymnos), meaning "naked," and τητος (titos), meaning "state of being." Therefore, γυμνότητος means "state of being naked" or "nudity."

Grammar:
γυμνότητος is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word can be declined in different cases, such as the genitive case (γυμνότητου) or the accusative case (γυμνότητη).

Description:
γυμνότητος refers to the state of being naked or nudity. It can be used to describe a person who is not wearing any clothes or to refer to the act of being naked. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or action that is exposed or without any cover or protection.
