The word "γυμνάζε" (gymnaze) is a verb in Greek that means "to exercise" or "to train". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "γυμνός" (gymnos), which means "naked". In ancient Greece, athletes would exercise and train in the nude, hence the connection between the word "gymnaze" and physical exercise.

Grammar: "γυμνάζε" is a present tense, singular, second person imperative verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to exercise or train.

Description: The word "γυμνάζε" is commonly used in modern Greek to refer to physical exercise or training, whether it be in a gym or outdoors. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to mental or intellectual exercise, such as studying or practicing a skill.
