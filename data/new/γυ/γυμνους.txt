Word: γυμνοὺς

Etymology: The word γυμνοὺς comes from the Greek word γυμνός (gymnos), which means "naked" or "bare". The suffix -οὺς (-ous) is a masculine plural ending.

Grammar: γυμνοὺς is a masculine plural adjective in the nominative case. It modifies a noun in the same case and number.

Description: γυμνοὺς describes a group of people who are naked or partially clothed. In ancient Greece, athletes would compete in the nude, and the word γυμνοὺς was often used to describe them. The word can also be used more generally to describe someone who is not wearing clothes or is underdressed.
