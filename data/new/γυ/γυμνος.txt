Word: γυμνος

Etymology:
The word γυμνος (gumnos) is derived from the Greek root word γυμνός (gymnos), which means "naked" or "bare". The word is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷm̥-nós, which means "naked" or "bare".

Grammar:
In Greek, γυμνος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person or object that is naked or bare. It can be declined in various cases, genders, and numbers to agree with the noun it modifies. For example, in the nominative singular, γυμνος becomes γυμνός (gumnos) when modifying a masculine noun, γυμνή (gymnē) when modifying a feminine noun, and γυμνόν (gymnon) when modifying a neuter noun.

Description:
The word γυμνος is commonly used in Greek to describe a person or object that is naked or bare. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is exposed or vulnerable. In ancient Greece, the word was often used to describe athletes who competed in the nude during the Olympic games. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe nudity or bareness.
