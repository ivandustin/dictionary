Word: κιθαραις

Etymology: κιθάρα (kithara) is a Greek word for a type of stringed musical instrument. The suffix -αις (-ais) is a Greek feminine plural ending, indicating that κιθαραις is the plural form of κιθάρα.

Grammar: κιθαραις is a noun in the feminine gender and plural number in the Greek language.

Description: κιθαραις refers to multiple kitharas, which were ancient Greek musical instruments similar to lyres or harps. They were played by plucking the strings with the fingers or a plectrum. Kitharas were often used in ancient Greek music and were associated with the god Apollo.
