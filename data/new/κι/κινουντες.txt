Word: κινούντες

Etymology: κινούντες is the present participle of the Greek verb κινέω (kineo), which means "to move" or "to stir". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *kewH- which means "to move" or "to shake".

Grammar: κινούντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and plural number. It is formed by adding the suffix -ούντ- to the stem κιν-, which is the present tense stem of the verb κινέω. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description: κινούντες can be translated as "moving" or "stirring". It is often used to describe people or things that are in motion or actively doing something. For example, in the sentence "Οι κινούντες χορεύουν στο πάρτι" (The moving ones are dancing at the party), κινούντες refers to the people who are dancing.
