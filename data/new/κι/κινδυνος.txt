Word: κινδυνος

Etymology:
The word κινδυνος (kindunos) is derived from the Greek root κινδυν- (kindyn-), which means "danger" or "peril". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵʰen- ("to strike, kill").

Grammar:
In Greek, κινδυνος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is κινδυνος, and its genitive singular form is κινδύνου (kindynou). The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
κινδυνος refers to danger, peril, or risk. It can be used to describe a hazardous situation or a potential threat. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the dangers faced by heroes on their quests or adventures. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe dangerous situations or risks.
