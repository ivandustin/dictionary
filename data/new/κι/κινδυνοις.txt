Word: κινδυνοις

Etymology:
The word κινδυνοις (kindunois) is a plural form of the Greek noun κίνδυνος (kindunos), which means "danger" or "peril". The word is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ḱen- ("to sharpen, to be sharp"), which is also the source of the English words "kin" and "kindred".

Grammar:
In Greek, κινδυνοις is a noun in the dative plural case. It is often used in the context of expressing danger or peril that is faced by a group of people. For example, "οἱ στρατιῶται ἐν κινδυνοῖς εἰσιν" (hoi stratiōtai en kindunois eisin) means "the soldiers are in danger".

Description:
κινδυνοις is a word that describes a state of being in danger or peril. It is often used in the context of war, battle, or other dangerous situations. The word conveys a sense of urgency and seriousness, and is often used to emphasize the gravity of a situation.
