Word: κινησαι

Etymology:
The word κινησαι (kinēsai) is a verb in the Greek language. It is derived from the root word κινέω (kinéō), which means "to move" or "to stir". The suffix -σαι (-sai) is the second person singular aorist imperative ending, which means that the word is a command to someone to perform an action in the past.

Grammar:
As mentioned above, κινησαι is a second person singular aorist imperative verb. This means that it is a command directed towards one person to perform an action in the past. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any reference to its duration. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

The word κινησαι can be translated to English as "move" or "stir". It is a command directed towards one person to perform an action in the past.
