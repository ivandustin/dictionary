Word: αγνοτητος

Etymology:
The word αγνοτητος (agnotitos) is derived from the Greek word αγνός (agnos), which means "pure" or "chaste". The suffix -τητος (-tetos) is used to form abstract nouns, so αγνοτητος can be translated as "purity" or "chastity".

Grammar:
αγνοτητος is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive αγνοτητου (agnotitou) or accusative αγνότητο (agnotito).

Description:
αγνοτητος refers to the state or quality of being pure or chaste. It can be used to describe a person's moral character or behavior, as well as the condition of an object or substance that is free from impurities. The word is often associated with religious or spiritual purity, but can also be used in a more general sense.
