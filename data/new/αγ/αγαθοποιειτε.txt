The word "αγαθοποιειτε" (agathopoieite) is a Greek verb that means "to do good" or "to perform good deeds". It is derived from the Greek words "αγαθός" (agathos) meaning "good" and "ποιέω" (poieo) meaning "to do" or "to make". 

In terms of grammar, "αγαθοποιειτε" is a second person plural present active imperative verb, which means it is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. 

Overall, the word "αγαθοποιειτε" encourages individuals to engage in acts of kindness and goodness towards others.
