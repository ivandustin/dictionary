Word: αγιαζων

Etymology: The word αγιαζων comes from the Greek verb αγιάζω (agiázo), which means "to make holy" or "to sanctify". It is derived from the adjective αγιος (agios), which means "holy" or "sacred".

Grammar: αγιαζων is the present participle of the verb αγιάζω (agiázo), which means "sanctifying" or "making holy". It is a masculine noun in the nominative case.

Description: αγιαζων is a word that describes someone or something that is in the process of being sanctified or made holy. It can refer to a person who is engaged in religious activities or to an object that is being consecrated for religious use. In Christian theology, the term is often used to describe the process of sanctification, which is the gradual transformation of a person's character to become more like Christ.
