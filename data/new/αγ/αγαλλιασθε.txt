Word: αγαλλιασθε

Etymology:
The word αγαλλιασθε is derived from the Greek verb αγαλλιάω (agalliaó), which means to exult, rejoice, or be glad. It is formed from the prefix αγαλλίασ- (agallias-) and the suffix -θε (-the), which is the second person plural ending for the present imperative tense.

Grammar:
αγαλλιασθε is a verb in the present imperative tense, second person plural form. In Greek, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now or is ongoing. The second person plural form means that the command is directed at a group of people.

αγαλλιασθε is a command to a group of people to rejoice or be glad. It is often used in a religious context, such as in the New Testament of the Bible, where it appears in several verses, including Philippians 4:4: "Rejoice in the Lord always.
