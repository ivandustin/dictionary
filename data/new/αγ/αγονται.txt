Word: αγονται

Etymology: The word αγονται comes from the Greek verb αγωνίζομαι (agonizomai), which means "to struggle" or "to fight". It is derived from the noun αγών (agon), which means "contest" or "competition".

Grammar: αγονται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is the third person plural form of the verb αγωνίζομαι (agonizomai).

Description: αγονται is a verb that describes an action of struggling or fighting, but in the passive voice, which means that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in sports, politics, or personal struggles.
