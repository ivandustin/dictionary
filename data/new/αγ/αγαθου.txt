The word "αγαθου" (agathou) is a Greek adjective in the genitive case, meaning "of good". Its etymology can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "ἀγαθός" (agathos), which also means "good". In terms of grammar, it is a second declension adjective, with the stem "αγαθ-" and the ending "-ου" indicating the genitive case. 

In usage, "αγαθου" can be used to describe a noun in the genitive case, indicating that the noun possesses a quality of goodness. For example, "του αγαθου ανθρώπου" (tou agathou anthropou) means "of the good person". 

Overall, "αγαθου" is a versatile adjective that can be used to describe a wide range of nouns in Greek, indicating a quality of goodness or excellence.
