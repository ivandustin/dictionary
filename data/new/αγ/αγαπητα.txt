Word: αγαπητα

Etymology:
The word αγαπητα (agapita) is the feminine form of the adjective αγαπητός (agapitos), which comes from the verb αγαπάω (agapao) meaning "to love". It is derived from the ancient Greek word ἀγάπη (agape), which refers to a selfless, unconditional love.

Grammar:
αγαπητα is the plural form of the adjective αγαπητός (agapitos), which means "beloved" or "dear". It is used to describe people or things that are loved or cherished. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, αγαπητα is used to describe plural feminine nouns.

Description:
αγαπητα is a Greek adjective that is commonly used to express affection or endearment towards a group of people or things. It can be used in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, business, or even in addressing a group of people.
