The word "αγειν" (agein) is a Greek verb that means "to lead" or "to bring". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "αγω" (ago), which has the same meaning. 

In terms of grammar, "αγειν" is a present active infinitive verb. It can be conjugated in various tenses and moods to indicate different aspects and nuances of leading or bringing. 

In ancient Greek literature, "αγειν" is often used in the context of military or political leadership, as well as in the sense of guiding or directing someone or something. It is also used in a more general sense of leading or bringing something to a certain place or state.
