Word: αγορασαντα

Etymology: αγορασαντα is the aorist participle of the Greek verb αγοράζω (agorazo), which means "to buy" or "to purchase". The word comes from the noun αγορά (agora), which means "marketplace" or "assembly".

Grammar: αγορασαντα is a masculine singular aorist participle in the nominative case. In Greek, participles are verb forms that can function as adjectives or nouns. The aorist tense indicates that the action of buying was completed in the past.

Description: αγορασαντα can be translated as "the one who bought" or "having bought". It refers to a person who has completed the action of buying something in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Ο αγορασαντας το αυτοκίνητο ήταν πολύ ευχαριστημένος" (The one who bought the car was very satisfied).
