Word: αγρυπνουσιν

Etymology: The word αγρυπνουσιν comes from the Greek root words αγρυπνέω (agrypneō) which means "to stay awake" and the suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) which indicates the present tense third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: αγρυπνουσιν is a present tense verb in Greek, specifically the third person plural form. It is often translated to English as "they are staying awake" or "they are vigilant". The verb is formed by adding the suffix -ουσιν to the root word αγρυπνέω.

Description: αγρυπνουσιν is a Greek verb that describes the action of staying awake or being vigilant. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone who is watchful or alert, such as a guard or a sentry. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe someone who is unable to sleep or who chooses to stay awake for a period of time.
