Word: αγαθοεργειν

Etymology:
The word αγαθοεργειν is derived from the Greek words αγαθός (agathos) meaning "good" and εργεία (ergeia) meaning "work" or "deed". Therefore, the word αγαθοεργειν means "to do good deeds" or "to perform acts of kindness".

Grammar:
αγαθοεργειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. The verb stem is αγαθοεργ-, and the ending -ειν indicates the infinitive form.

Description:
αγαθοεργειν is a term that refers to the act of doing good deeds or performing acts of kindness. It is a concept that is highly valued in many cultures and religions, as it is seen as a way to improve the world and make it a better place. The word is often used in the context of moral and ethical behavior, and it is associated with virtues such as compassion, generosity, and altruism. In Greek philosophy, the concept of αγαθοεργειν is closely linked to the idea of eudaimonia, or human flourishing, which is achieved through the cultivation of virtues and the pursuit of excellence.
