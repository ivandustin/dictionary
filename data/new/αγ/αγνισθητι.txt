Word: αγνισθητι

Etymology: The word αγνισθητι is derived from the Greek verb αγνίζω (agnizo), which means "to purify" or "to make clean". The suffix -θητι (-thiti) is a second person singular aorist passive imperative ending, which means that the word is a command to someone to be purified or cleansed.

Grammar: αγνισθητι is a second person singular aorist passive imperative verb form in Greek. It is used as a command to someone to be purified or cleansed. The aorist tense indicates that the action is completed in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description: αγνισθητι is a command to someone to be purified or cleansed. It is often used in religious contexts, such as in the Greek Orthodox Church during the sacrament of confession. The word emphasizes the importance of purity and cleanliness in both a physical and spiritual sense.
