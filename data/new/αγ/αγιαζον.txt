Word: αγιαζον

Etymology: The word αγιαζον comes from the Greek verb αγιάζω (agiázo), which means "to make holy" or "to sanctify". It is derived from the adjective αγιος (agios), which means "holy" or "sacred".

Grammar: αγιαζον is the present participle of the verb αγιάζω (agiázo), which means "sanctifying" or "making holy". It is in the neuter gender, singular number, and nominative case.

Description: αγιαζον is a word that describes the act of making something holy or sacred. It can refer to a religious ceremony or ritual, or to the process of purifying or consecrating an object or place. In Christian theology, the term is often used to describe the process of sanctification, or the gradual transformation of a person's character to become more like Christ.
