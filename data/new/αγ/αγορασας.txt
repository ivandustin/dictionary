Word: αγοράσας

Etymology:
The word αγοράσας is a conjugated form of the Greek verb αγοράζω (agorazo), which means "to buy" or "to purchase". The verb is derived from the noun αγορά (agora), which means "marketplace" or "assembly".

Grammar:
αγοράσας is the second person singular past tense of the verb αγοράζω (agorazo). It is in the indicative mood and active voice. The verb is conjugated in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
αγοράσας is a Greek verb that means "you bought" or "you purchased". It is used to describe a completed action of buying or purchasing something in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a conversation about shopping, business transactions, or personal finances.
