Word: αγαθης

Etymology:
The word αγαθης (agathēs) is derived from the Greek word αγαθός (agathos), which means "good" or "virtuous". It is a common Greek name that has been used since ancient times.

αγαθης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it means "good man" or "virtuous man". In Greek, nouns have gender, number, and case, which determine their form and function in a sentence.

αγαθης is a word that is often used to describe a person who is good, virtuous, or morally upright. It is a positive attribute that is highly valued in Greek culture.
