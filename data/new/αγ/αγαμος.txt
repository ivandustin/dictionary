Word: αγαμος

Etymology:
The word αγαμος (agamos) is derived from the Greek prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the word "γαμος" (gamos) meaning "marriage". Therefore, αγαμος means "unmarried" or "celibate".

Grammar:
In Greek, αγαμος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person who is not married or a state of being unmarried. It can also be used to describe things that are not joined or united.

Description:
αγαμος is a term used to describe someone who has not entered into the state of marriage. It can be used to describe someone who has never been married, or someone who is currently not married. In ancient Greece, celibacy was often associated with religious devotion and was considered a virtue.
