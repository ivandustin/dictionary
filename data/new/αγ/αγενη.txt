Word: αγενη

Etymology:
The word αγενη (agene) is a Greek word that comes from the prefix "a-" meaning "not" and the word "gene" meaning "born" or "produced". Therefore, αγενη means "not born" or "not produced".

Grammar:
In Greek, αγενη is an adjective that is used to describe something that is not born or produced. It is in the feminine gender, singular form.

Description:
The word αγενη is often used in Greek mythology to describe creatures or beings that were not born in the traditional sense. For example, the goddess Athena was said to have been born fully grown and armored from the head of her father Zeus, making her αγενη. The word can also be used to describe things that are not naturally occurring, such as man-made objects or artificial constructs.
