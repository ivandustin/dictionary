The word "αγορας" (agoras) is a Greek noun that means "marketplace" or "assembly." It comes from the ancient Greek word "αγορά" (agora), which referred to the central gathering place in ancient Greek cities where people would come to buy and sell goods, exchange ideas, and participate in political discussions. 

In terms of grammar, "αγορας" is a singular noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It can be translated as "of the marketplace" or "of the assembly."

Overall, "αγορας" is a word that is deeply rooted in Greek history and culture, and it continues to be used today to refer to both physical marketplaces and metaphorical gathering places for discussion and debate.
