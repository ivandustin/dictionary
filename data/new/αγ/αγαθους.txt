The word "αγαθους" (agathous) is an adjective in Greek that means "good" or "virtuous". It is derived from the Greek word "αγαθός" (agathos), which also means "good" or "virtuous". In terms of grammar, "αγαθους" is in the masculine accusative plural form. It is commonly used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe a person's moral character or actions.
