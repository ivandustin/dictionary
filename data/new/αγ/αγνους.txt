Word: αγνους

Etymology:
The word αγνους (agnous) comes from the Greek adjective αγνός (agnos), which means "pure" or "chaste". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eng- which means "narrow" or "tight".

Grammar:
αγνους is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word. In the plural form, it becomes αγνοί (agnoi).

Description:
αγνους is a Greek word that is often used to describe someone who is pure or innocent. It can also be used to describe something that is clean or unspoiled. In Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was often referred to as αγνή (agni), which is the feminine form of the word. The word is still commonly used in modern Greek to describe someone who is morally pure or chaste.
