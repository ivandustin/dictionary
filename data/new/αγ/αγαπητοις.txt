Word: αγαπητοις

Etymology: αγαπητοις is a Greek word that comes from the root word αγαπη (agape), which means love. The suffix -τοις (-tois) is a dative plural ending, indicating that the word is being used in the dative case and referring to multiple people.

Grammar: αγαπητοις is a dative plural form of the adjective αγαπητός (agapetos), which means beloved or dear. In Greek, adjectives must agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, αγαπητοις is used to describe multiple people who are beloved or dear.

Description: αγαπητοις is a term of endearment used to address a group of people in a respectful and affectionate way. It is often translated as "beloved" or "dear ones" in English. This word is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is used by the apostles to address their fellow Christians.
