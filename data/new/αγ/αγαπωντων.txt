The word "αγαπωντων" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "αγαπάω" (agapao), which means "to love". The present participle form indicates ongoing action, so "αγαπωντων" would translate to "loving" or "those who are loving". In terms of grammar, it is in the genitive case and masculine gender, indicating that it is modifying a masculine noun.
