Word: αγιαζω

Etymology: The word αγιαζω (agiázō) comes from the Greek root word αγιος (agios), which means "holy" or "sacred". The suffix -άζω (-ázō) is a verb ending that indicates the action of making something holy or sacred.

Grammar: αγιαζω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ αγιάζω (egó agiázo) - I sanctify
- Εσύ αγιάζεις (esý agiázeis) - You sanctify
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό αγιάζει (afTós/afTí/afTó agiázei) - He/She/It sanctifies
- Εμείς αγιάζουμε (emeís agiázoume) - We sanctify
- Εσείς αγιάζετε (eseís agiázete) - You (plural) sanctify
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά αγιάζουν (afTí/afTés/afTá agiázoun) - They sanctify

Description: αγιαζω is a Greek verb that means "to sanctify" or "to make holy". It is often used in religious contexts to describe the act of consecrating something or someone to a higher purpose or to God. It can also be used in a more general sense to describe the act of making something pure or sacred.
