Word: αγαπησαντος

Etymology: αγαπησαντος is a Greek word that comes from the verb αγαπάω (agapao), which means "to love". The suffix -τος (-tos) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and passive voice.

Grammar: αγαπησαντος is a past participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a male subject who has performed the action of loving in the past.

Description: αγαπησαντος can be translated to English as "having been loved" or "having been beloved". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus Christ as the beloved son of God.
