Word: αγιασμός

Etymology: The word αγιασμός comes from the Greek verb αγιάζω (agiázo), which means "to make holy" or "to sanctify". The suffix -μός (-mos) is a common noun-forming suffix in Greek.

Grammar: αγιασμός is a masculine noun in Greek. Its plural form is αγιασμοί (agiásmoi).

Description: αγιασμός is a religious term in Greek, referring to the act of blessing or consecrating something or someone. It is commonly used in the Orthodox Christian Church, where it is performed by a priest or bishop using holy water and prayers. The αγιασμός is often performed on important occasions such as the opening of a new church, the blessing of a new home, or the beginning of a new school year. It is also performed on individuals, such as newborn babies or those about to embark on a journey. The αγιασμός is seen as a way of invoking God's protection and blessings on the person or object being blessed.
