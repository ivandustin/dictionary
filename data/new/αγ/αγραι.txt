The word "αγραι" (agrai) is a plural noun in Greek that means "fields" or "pastures". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek word "ἀγρός" (agros) which means "field" or "land".

Grammar: "Αγραι" is the plural form of the noun and is feminine in gender. It is declined in the first declension and has the same form in both the nominative and accusative cases.

Description: The word "αγραι" is commonly used in Greek to refer to fields or pastures where animals graze. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a wide open space or a vast expanse.
