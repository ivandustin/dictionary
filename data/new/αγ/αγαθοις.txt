Word: αγαθοις

Etymology: αγαθοις is the dative plural form of the Greek adjective αγαθός (agathos), which means "good" or "virtuous". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ag- which means "to drive, lead, move".

Grammar: αγαθοις is a dative plural form of the adjective αγαθός. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: αγαθοις is used to describe a group of people who are good or virtuous. It can be translated as "to the good" or "for the good". The word is often used in a moral or ethical context, to describe people who are morally upright or who have a strong sense of virtue.
