Word: αγαθος

Etymology:
The word αγαθος (agathos) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ag- which means "good" or "to be good". In Greek, the word αγαθος is used to describe something that is good, virtuous, or noble.

Grammar:
In Greek, αγαθος is an adjective that can be used to describe both masculine and feminine nouns. It has three different forms depending on the gender of the noun it modifies: αγαθος (masculine), αγαθη (feminine), and αγαθον (neuter). It is also declined according to the case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
The word αγαθος is often used in Greek literature and philosophy to describe a person who is morally good, virtuous, and noble. It is also used to describe actions, thoughts, and ideas that are considered to be good and beneficial. In modern Greek, the word αγαθος is still commonly used to describe someone who is kind, generous, and helpful.
