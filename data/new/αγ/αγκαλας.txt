Word: αγκαλας

Etymology:
The word αγκαλας (angalas) is derived from the Greek verb αγκαλίζω (angalizo), which means "to embrace" or "to hug". The noun αγκαλας refers to the act of embracing or hugging someone.

Grammar:
In Greek, αγκαλας is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. Its plural form is αγκαλες (angales). It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns, which means that its stem ends in -ας (as) and its genitive singular form ends in -ου (ou).

Description:
The word αγκαλας is often used to express affection or comfort towards someone. It can also be used to describe the physical act of embracing or hugging someone. In Greek culture, hugging and physical touch are often seen as a way to express warmth and closeness towards others.
