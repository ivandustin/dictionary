The word "αγει" (agei) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person singular form of the present tense of the verb "αγω" (ago), which means "to lead" or "to bring". 

Etymology: The word "αγω" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eǵ-, which means "to drive" or "to lead". 

Grammar: "αγει" is a present tense verb in the indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows: 

- Singular: αγει (agei) 
- Plural: αγουν (agoun) 

Description: The verb "αγει" is used to indicate that someone is leading or bringing something. For example, "Ο πατέρας αγει το παιδί στο σχολείο" (O pateras agei to paidi sto scholeio) means "The father is bringing the child to school". It can also be used metaphorically to indicate leading or guiding someone in a non-physical sense.
