Word: αγορασον

Grammar: αγορασον is a second person singular verb in the aorist imperative mood. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: αγορασον is a command to buy or purchase something. It is often used in everyday conversation when someone wants to tell another person to buy something.
