The word "αγαθοποιουσαι" is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "doing good" or "benefiting others". It is derived from the combination of two Greek words: "αγαθός" meaning "good" and "ποιέω" meaning "to do/make". In terms of grammar, it is a feminine plural form, indicating that the subject is a group of females who are doing good deeds.
