The word "αγοραζοντες" is a present participle form of the Greek verb "αγοράζω" (agorazo), which means "to buy" or "to purchase". The word is formed by adding the present participle suffix "-οντ-" to the stem "αγοραζ-". 

In terms of grammar, "αγοραζοντες" is a masculine plural form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a group of male individuals who are currently buying or purchasing something. 

Etymologically, the word "αγοράζω" comes from the noun "αγορά" (agora), which means "marketplace" or "assembly". The verb originally referred to the act of buying and selling goods in a marketplace, but it has since taken on a broader meaning to encompass any kind of purchase or acquisition. 

In summary, "αγοραζοντες" is a present participle form of the Greek verb "αγοράζω", meaning "to buy" or "to purchase". It is a masculine plural form and has its roots in the Greek word for "marketplace".
