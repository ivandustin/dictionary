The word "αγουσιν" (agousin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "αγω" (ago), which means "to lead" or "to bring". The suffix "-ουσιν" (-ousin) indicates that the subject of the verb is plural and in the present tense. 

In English, "αγουσιν" can be translated as "they lead" or "they bring". It is commonly used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels, to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples. For example, in Mark 10:32, it is written: "And they were on the road, going up to Jerusalem, and Jesus was walking ahead of them. And they were amazed, and those who followed were afraid. In terms of grammar, "αγουσιν" is a third person plural present indicative verb.
