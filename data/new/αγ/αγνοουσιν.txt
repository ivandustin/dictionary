Word: αγνοουσιν

Etymology: The word αγνοουσιν comes from the Greek verb αγνοέω (agnoeo), which means "to not know" or "to be ignorant". The suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) indicates the present tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: αγνοουσιν is a present tense, third person plural form of the Greek verb αγνοέω (agnoeo). It is in the active voice and indicative mood.

Description: αγνοουσιν is a verb that means "they do not know" or "they are ignorant" in Greek. It is often used to describe a lack of knowledge or understanding about a particular subject or situation. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from everyday conversation to academic writing.
