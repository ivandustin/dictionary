The word "αγαθοποιητε" is a Greek verb in the imperative mood, which means "do good" or "be a doer of good". It is derived from the Greek words "αγαθός" (agathos), meaning "good", and "ποιέω" (poieo), meaning "to do" or "to make". In essence, the word encourages the listener to perform acts of kindness and generosity towards others. In terms of grammar, it is a second-person plural verb in the present tense, which means it is used to address a group of people.
