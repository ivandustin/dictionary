Word: αγνας

Etymology:
The word αγνας (agnas) comes from the Greek adjective αγνός (agnos), which means "pure" or "chaste". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂negʷʰ- which means "clean" or "pure".

Grammar:
αγνας is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word αγνοί (agni), which means "pure ones". In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case.

Description:
αγνας is a word that is often used to describe someone who is pure, chaste, or innocent. It can also be used to describe something that is clean or free from impurities. In Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was often referred to as αγνή (agni), which means "pure" or "virgin". The word αγνας is still commonly used in modern Greek to describe someone who is morally upright or pure-hearted.
