Word: αγωσιν

Etymology:
The word αγωσιν (agōsin) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person plural form of the verb αγω (agō), which means "to lead" or "to bring". The word is derived from the Greek root word αγω (agō), which is related to the Latin word "ago" and the English word "act".

αγωσιν (agōsin) is a present tense, third person plural form of the verb αγω (agō). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and voice of the action. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now, and the third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people or things.

Description:
αγωσιν (agōsin) can be translated to English as "they lead" or "they bring". The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples, who are said to lead or bring people to salvation.
