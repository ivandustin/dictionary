Word: αγαπω

Etymology: The word αγαπω (agapo) comes from the ancient Greek verb ἀγαπάω (agapao), which means "to love". It is derived from the noun ἀγάπη (agape), which means "love" or "affection".

Grammar: αγαπω is a first-person singular present indicative active form of the verb ἀγαπάω (agapao). It is used to express the action of loving or having affection for someone or something in the present tense.

Description: αγαπω is a common Greek verb that is used to express love or affection towards someone or something. It is often used in everyday conversation and in literature, poetry, and songs.
