Word: αγιοις

Etymology: αγιοις is a Greek word that comes from the adjective αγιος (holy) and the dative plural ending -οις. It is commonly used in the Greek Orthodox Church to refer to saints.

Grammar: αγιοις is a dative plural form of the adjective αγιος. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: αγιοις is a word that refers to holy or sacred things or people, particularly saints in the Greek Orthodox Church. It is often used in religious contexts, such as in prayers or hymns.
