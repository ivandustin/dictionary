Word: αγνος

Etymology:
The word αγνος (agnos) comes from the Ancient Greek word ἁγνός (hagnós), which means "pure" or "chaste". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂egʰ- which means "to purify".

Grammar:
In Greek, αγνος is an adjective that can be used to describe a person, object, or action. It has three genders (masculine, feminine, and neuter) and three cases (nominative, genitive, and accusative). The declension of the word depends on the gender and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
The word αγνος is often used to describe someone who is pure, innocent, or morally upright. It can also be used to describe something that is clean, unspoiled, or free from contamination. In a religious context, it can refer to a state of spiritual purity or holiness. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to convey a sense of purity and goodness.
