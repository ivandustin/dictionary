Word: αγνοειν

Etymology:
The word αγνοειν (agnoein) comes from the Greek verb αγνοέω (agnoeo), which means "to not know" or "to be ignorant of". It is derived from the prefix α- (a-), which means "not", and the verb γινώσκω (ginosko), which means "to know".

Grammar:
αγνοειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the infinitive ending -ειν (-ein) to the stem αγνο- (agno-).

Description:
αγνοειν is a Greek verb that means "to ignore" or "to be unaware of". It is often used in the context of not knowing or being ignorant of something. For example, one might say "Αγνοώ το πού βρίσκεται η βιβλιοθήκη" (Agnoo to pou vrisketai i vivliothiki), which means "I don't know where the library is". The verb can also be used in a negative sense, such as "Δεν αγνοώ τις συνέπειες των πράξεών μου" (Den agnoo tis synepeies ton praxeon mou), which means "I am not ignorant of the consequences of my actions".
