Word: αγνοων

Etymology:
The word αγνοων (agnōon) is a present participle form of the Greek verb αγνοεω (agnoeō), which means "to be ignorant of" or "to not know". The verb is derived from the adjective αγνος (agnos), which means "ignorant" or "unknown".

Grammar:
αγνοων is a present participle form of the verb αγνοεω (agnoeō), which is in the present tense and active voice. The participle form indicates that the action of the verb is ongoing or continuous.

Description:
αγνοων can be translated as "ignoring" or "not knowing". It is often used to describe someone who is unaware of something or who is intentionally ignoring something. The word can also be used in a more general sense to describe a lack of knowledge or understanding.
