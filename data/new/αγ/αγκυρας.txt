Word: αγκυρας

Etymology:
The word αγκυρας (ankyras) comes from the ancient Greek word ἄγκυρα (ankura), which means "anchor". The word is derived from the verb ἀγκύρωσις (ankurōsis), which means "to anchor".

Grammar:
In Greek, αγκυρας is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. Its genitive singular form is αγκυρας, and its accusative singular form is αγκυραν. In the plural form, it is αγκυρες in the nominative and accusative cases, and αγκυρων in the genitive case.

Description:
αγκυρας refers to an anchor, which is a device used to keep a ship or boat from drifting away. It is typically made of heavy metal and is attached to a chain or rope that is lowered into the water to hold the vessel in place. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to something that provides stability or security in a situation.
