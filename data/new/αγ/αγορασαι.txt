Word: αγοράσαι

The word αγοράσαι (agorasai) is a verb in the Greek language, which is derived from the noun αγορά (agora), meaning "marketplace" or "assembly." The verb form αγοράζω (agorazo) means "to buy" or "to purchase" in modern Greek.

Grammar:
αγοράσαι is the second person singular form of the aorist tense in the active voice. It is formed by adding the suffix -σαι to the stem αγορά-. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description:
αγοράσαι means "you bought" or "you purchased" in English. It is a past tense verb form and is used to describe a completed action of buying or purchasing something. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in a conversation about shopping or business transactions.
