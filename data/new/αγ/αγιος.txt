Word: αγιος

Etymology:
The word αγιος (agios) comes from the Greek adjective ἅγιος (hagios), which means "holy" or "sacred". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *sak- ("to sanctify").

Grammar:
In Greek, αγιος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be used as an adjective to describe a masculine noun. The feminine form is αγία (agia) and the neuter form is αγίο (agio).

Description:
The word αγιος is commonly used in Greek Orthodox Christianity to refer to saints or holy persons. It is also used to describe holy places, objects, and events. In everyday language, it can be used to describe something that is pure, sacred, or revered.
