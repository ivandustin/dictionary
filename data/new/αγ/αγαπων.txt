The word "αγαπων" (agapon) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "αγαπάω" (agapao), which means "to love". The word can be translated as "loving" or "one who loves". It is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a person who is deeply in love or has a strong affection for someone or something. In grammar, it is a masculine singular nominative form.
