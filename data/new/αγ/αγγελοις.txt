Word: αγγελοις

Etymology:
The word αγγελοις (angelois) is derived from the Greek word αγγελος (angelos), which means "messenger" or "envoy". In Greek mythology, angels were considered to be divine messengers who acted as intermediaries between the gods and humans.

Grammar:
αγγελοις is a noun in the dative case, which is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. In this case, it is the dative plural form of αγγελος.

Description:
αγγελοις refers to angels in Greek. In Christian theology, angels are believed to be spiritual beings who serve as messengers of God and are often depicted as having wings. They are also believed to be guardians and protectors of humans. The word αγγελοις is used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to angels.
