Word: αγραν

Etymology: The word αγραν comes from the Greek word αγράνα, which means "wilderness" or "desert". It is derived from the word αγρός, which means "field" or "land".

Grammar: αγραν is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is masculine. It is often used in the phrase "εις το αγραν", which means "into the wilderness".

Description: αγραν refers to a wild and uninhabited area, such as a desert or wilderness. It can also refer to a place that is uncultivated or unused. In Greek mythology, the god Apollo was said to have been born in the αγραν, which was a sacred place. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe remote and isolated areas.
