Word: αγιους

Etymology: The word αγιους (agious) is derived from the Greek word ἅγιος (hagios), which means "holy" or "saintly". 

Grammar: αγιους is the plural form of the adjective αγιος (agios), which means "holy" or "saintly". It is used to describe people or things that are considered sacred or blessed. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: αγιους is a Greek word that is commonly used in religious contexts to refer to saints or holy people. It can also be used to describe places or objects that are considered sacred or blessed. The word has a strong connotation of reverence and respect, and is often used in prayers and religious ceremonies.
