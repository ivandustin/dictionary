The word "αγνιαι" (agniai) is a plural noun in Greek that means "lambs". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek word "αγνός" (agnos), which means "pure" or "innocent". 

Grammar: "αγνιαι" is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. 

Description: The word refers to young sheep, specifically those that are less than a year old. In Greek culture, lambs were often used for sacrifices and were considered a symbol of purity and innocence.
