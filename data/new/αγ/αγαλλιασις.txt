Word: αγαλλιασις

Etymology: The word αγαλλιασις comes from the Greek verb αγαλλιάω (agalliaō), which means "to exult, to rejoice, to be glad." The suffix -σις (-sis) is a noun-forming suffix that indicates the action or process of the verb.

Grammar: αγαλλιασις is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence. The word is also in the singular form.

Description: αγαλλιασις refers to a feeling of great joy and exultation. It is often used in a religious context to describe the joy and celebration that comes from a deep spiritual experience. The word is also used in a more general sense to describe any kind of intense happiness or jubilation.
