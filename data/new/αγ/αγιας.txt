The word "αγιας" (agias) is a feminine noun in Greek, which means "saint" or "holy woman". It is derived from the adjective "αγιος" (agios), which means "holy" or "sacred". In Greek Orthodox Christianity, the term "αγιας" is often used to refer to female saints or martyrs who are venerated for their piety and devotion to God. 

In terms of grammar, "αγιας" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. For example, "η αγιας του Χριστού" (i agias tou Christou) means "the saint of Christ" or "Christ's saint". 

Overall, "αγιας" is a word that carries a strong religious and cultural significance in Greek language and society.
