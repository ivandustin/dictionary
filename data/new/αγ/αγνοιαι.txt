Word: αγνοιαι

Etymology:
The word αγνοιαι (agniai) is derived from the Greek word αγνός (agnos), which means "pure" or "clean". The suffix -αι (-ai) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple instances of purity or cleanliness.

Grammar:
αγνοιαι is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple subjects.

Description:
αγνοιαι refers to the state of being pure or clean. It can be used to describe physical cleanliness, such as the cleanliness of a room or a person's body, or it can refer to moral purity, such as the purity of one's intentions or actions.
