Word: αγαθας

Etymology:
The word αγαθας (agathas) is derived from the Greek word αγαθός (agathos), which means "good" or "virtuous". It is a masculine noun in the nominative case.

In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. αγαθας is a masculine noun in the nominative case, which means it is the subject of the sentence. It is also singular, which means it refers to one person or thing.

αγαθας is a Greek word that means "good" or "virtuous". It is often used to describe a person who is morally upright or has good character.
