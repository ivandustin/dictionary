The word "αγαθουργων" (agathourgon) is a Greek term that is derived from the combination of two words: "αγαθός" (agathos) meaning "good" and "έργο" (ergo) meaning "work". Therefore, "αγαθουργων" can be translated as "one who does good deeds" or "a doer of good works".

In terms of grammar, "αγαθουργων" is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case. It is also a compound word, which is common in the Greek language.

Overall, "αγαθουργων" is a positive term that describes someone who is actively engaged in doing good deeds and making a positive impact in their community.
