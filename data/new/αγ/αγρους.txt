Word: αγρους

Etymology:
The word αγρους (agrous) is derived from the Greek word αγρός (agros), which means "field" or "farm". It is a noun in the genitive case, which indicates possession or origin.

Grammar:
αγρους is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or origin. In Greek, the genitive case is often used to show possession, as in "the field of the farmer" (ο αγρός του αγρότη).

Description:
αγρους is a Greek word that refers to a field or farm. It is often used in the context of agriculture and farming, and can also be used metaphorically to refer to a wide open space or a place of opportunity. The word has a long history in Greek culture, and is still commonly used today.
