In terms of grammar, αγω is a first-conjugation verb in the present tense. Its principal parts are αγω, ηγαγον (ēgagon), and ηχα (ēcha). It is a transitive verb, meaning it takes a direct object.

In usage, αγω can refer to physically leading or bringing someone or something, as well as leading or bringing in a more abstract sense, such as leading a discussion or bringing up a topic. It can also be used in a metaphorical sense, such as leading a life or leading a movement.
