Word: αγνοουντες

Etymology:
The word αγνοουντες is derived from the Greek verb αγνοέω (agnoeo), which means "to be ignorant of" or "to not know". The suffix -ουντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently in the act of not knowing or being ignorant of something.

Grammar:
αγνοουντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe people who are currently in the act of not knowing or being ignorant of something. In Greek, present participles are often used to form adjectives or to describe ongoing actions.

Description:
αγνοουντες can be translated to English as "ignorant" or "unaware". It is often used to describe people who are unaware of a particular fact or situation. For example, in the sentence "Οι αγνοούντες δεν γνωρίζουν την αλήθεια" (The ignorant are not aware of the truth), αγνοουντες refers to people who are ignorant or unaware of the truth.
