Word: αγαπατω

Etymology: The word αγαπατω (agapato) is derived from the Greek word αγαπη (agape), which means love. The suffix -τω (-to) is a verb ending that indicates the present tense.

Grammar: αγαπατω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated in the third person singular form.

Description: αγαπατω means "he/she/it loves" in Greek. It is a common verb used to express affection or love towards someone or something. The word is often used in the context of romantic love, but it can also be used to express love for family, friends, or even objects.
