Word: αγαπη

Etymology: The word αγαπη (agape) comes from the ancient Greek verb αγαπαω (agapao), which means "to love." It is believed that the word was first used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the unconditional love of God.

Grammar: αγαπη is a feminine noun in Greek. It is in the singular form and is often used with the definite article, την αγαπη (tin agapi), which means "the love."

Description: αγαπη is a word that is commonly used in Greek to describe love. It is often used to express a deep, unconditional love, such as the love between family members or the love of God. The word can also be used to describe a strong affection or attachment to something, such as a hobby or a place. In Greek culture, love is highly valued and celebrated, and the word αγαπη is often used to express this sentiment.
