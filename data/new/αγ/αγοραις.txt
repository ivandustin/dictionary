The word "αγοραις" (agorais) is a plural form of the Greek noun "αγορά" (agora), which means "marketplace" or "assembly." The word has its roots in ancient Greek, where it referred to a public gathering place where people would come to buy and sell goods, exchange ideas, and participate in political discussions. 

In terms of grammar, "αγοραις" is a feminine noun in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of a verb or preposition. It can also be used as the object of certain verbs, such as "πηγαίνω" (pigaíno), which means "to go to."

Overall, "αγοραις" is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts, from describing a physical marketplace to referring to a figurative gathering place for ideas and discussions.
