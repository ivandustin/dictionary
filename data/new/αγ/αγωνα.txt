Word: αγωνα

Etymology: The word αγωνα (agona) comes from the ancient Greek word ἀγών (agón), which means "contest" or "competition". It is derived from the verb ἀγωνίζομαι (agonizomai), which means "to struggle" or "to compete".

Grammar: αγωνα is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender.

Description: αγωνα refers to a contest or competition, typically in sports or athletics. It can also refer to a struggle or conflict in a broader sense. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a protest or demonstration.
