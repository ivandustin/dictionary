Word: αγαγοντες

Etymology: αγαγοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb αγαγω (agago), which means "to lead" or "to bring". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the action of leading or bringing is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: αγαγοντες is the present participle form of the verb αγαγω (agago) in the active voice. It is masculine, plural, and nominative, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a group of men who are currently leading or bringing something.

Description: αγαγοντες can be translated as "leading" or "bringing" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of Jesus and his disciples, who are frequently depicted as leading or bringing people to God. In a broader sense, αγαγοντες can refer to anyone who is leading or bringing others towards a particular goal or destination.
