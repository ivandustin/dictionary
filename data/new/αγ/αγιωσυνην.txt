Word: αγιωσύνην

Etymology: 
The word αγιωσύνην (agiōsynēn) is derived from the Greek root words αγιος (agios) meaning "holy" and σύνη (synē) meaning "union" or "communion". The suffix -νην (-nēn) is added to form the accusative singular feminine form of the noun.

Grammar:
αγιωσύνην is a feminine noun in the accusative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Description:
αγιωσύνην refers to holiness or sanctity. It is often used in a religious context to describe the state of being pure or sacred. The word can also be used to describe a holy object or place. In Christianity, the term is often used to describe the process of becoming more like God through spiritual growth and transformation.
