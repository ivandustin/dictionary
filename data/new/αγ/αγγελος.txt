Word: αγγελος

Etymology:
The word αγγελος (angelos) comes from the Greek verb αγγελλω (angello), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim". In ancient Greek, an αγγελος was a messenger or envoy who carried important news or messages between people or gods.

Grammar:
In Greek, αγγελος is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, such as:

- Nominative: αγγελος (angelos) - "messenger"
- Genitive: αγγελου (angelou) - "of the messenger"
- Dative: αγγελω (angelo) - "to/for the messenger"
- Accusative: αγγελον (angelon) - "messenger"

Description:
αγγελος is a word that refers to a messenger or envoy who carries important news or messages. In religious contexts, it is often used to refer to a divine messenger or angel. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to someone who delivers a message or communicates important information.
