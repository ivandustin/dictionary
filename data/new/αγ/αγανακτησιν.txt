Word: αγανακτησιν

Etymology: The word αγανακτησιν comes from the Greek verb αγανακτώ (aganaktó), which means "to be indignant" or "to be angry". The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a nominalizing suffix that turns the verb into a noun, so αγανακτησιν (aganaktisin) means "indignation" or "anger".

Grammar: αγανακτησιν is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can be declined in different cases, such as the nominative, genitive, accusative, etc., depending on its function in a sentence.

Description: αγανακτησιν refers to a strong feeling of anger or indignation. It can be used to describe a person's emotional state or a situation that causes anger or frustration. The word is often used in political or social contexts to express dissatisfaction with a particular policy or situation.
