The word "αγαθωσυνης" (agathosynēs) is a Greek noun that means "goodness" or "virtue." It is derived from the Greek words "αγαθος" (agathos), meaning "good," and "συνη" (synē), meaning "together with" or "union." In Greek philosophy, agathosynēs is often associated with the concept of eudaimonia, or human flourishing, and is considered a key component of a virtuous life. In terms of grammar, αγαθωσυνης is a feminine noun in the genitive case.
