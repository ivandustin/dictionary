Word: αγγελους

Etymology:
The word αγγελους (angelous) is derived from the Greek word ἄγγελος (angelos), which means "messenger" or "envoy". In Greek mythology, angels were considered to be divine messengers who acted as intermediaries between the gods and humans.

Grammar:
αγγελους is the plural form of the Greek noun αγγελος (angelos). It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative case, the singular form is αγγελος and the plural form is αγγελοι. In the accusative case, the singular form is αγγελον and the plural form is αγγελους.

Description:
αγγελους is a Greek word that refers to angels or messengers. In Christianity, angels are believed to be spiritual beings who act as messengers of God and are often depicted as having wings. The word is also used in a broader sense to refer to any messenger or envoy, whether divine or human.
