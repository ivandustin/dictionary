Word: αγαπητος

Etymology:
The word αγαπητος (agapitos) is derived from the Greek word αγαπη (agape), which means love. It is a combination of the prefix αγαπη- (agape-) and the suffix -τος (-tos), which is used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
In Greek, αγαπητος is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be used to describe a person, thing, or idea that is beloved, dear, or cherished.

Description:
The word αγαπητος is often used to express affection or endearment towards someone or something. It can be used to describe a beloved family member, a cherished possession, or a beloved pet. It is also commonly used in religious contexts to describe God's love for humanity.
