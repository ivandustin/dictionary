The word "αγγελου" (angelou) is a Greek noun that means "angel" in English. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "angelos" which means "messenger" or "envoy". In Greek mythology, angels were considered messengers of the gods.

Grammar: "αγγελου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of "αγγελος" (angelos).

Description: Angels are spiritual beings in many religions, including Christianity, Judaism, and Islam. They are often depicted as messengers of God who carry out his will and protect humans.
