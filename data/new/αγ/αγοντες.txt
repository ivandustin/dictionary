Word: αγοντες

Etymology: αγοντες is the present participle of the Greek verb αγω (ago), which means "to lead" or "to bring". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eǵ- (to lead, drive).

Grammar: αγοντες is a masculine plural noun in the nominative case. It is the present participle of the verb αγω (ago), which means "leading" or "bringing". In Greek, present participles are often used as adjectives to describe the subject of the sentence.

Description: αγοντες can be translated as "those who lead" or "those who bring". It is often used in the context of leading or bringing someone or something to a particular place or situation. In ancient Greek literature, it is often used to describe military leaders or commanders who lead their troops into battle.
