Word: αγνισατε

Etymology: The word αγνισατε comes from the Greek verb αγνίζω (agnizo), which means "to purify" or "to cleanse". It is derived from the adjective αγνός (agnos), which means "pure" or "clean".

Grammar: αγνισατε is the second person plural imperative form of the verb αγνίζω (agnizo). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to purify or cleanse something.

Description: αγνισατε is a word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, where purification or cleansing is an important part of the practice. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to the act of cleaning or purifying something, such as a physical object or a space. The word has a strong connotation of removing impurities or negative influences, and is often associated with the idea of spiritual or moral purity.
