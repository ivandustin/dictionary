The word "ελθουσα" (elthousa) is a feminine form of the Greek verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". It is in the past participle form, indicating that the action of coming has already taken place. 

Etymology: The word "έρχομαι" (erchomai) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁regʷ- which means "to move in a straight line". 

Grammar: "Ελθουσα" is in the feminine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be used as a standalone word or as part of a sentence. 

Description: "Ελθουσα" is a word that describes a female who has come or arrived at a certain place. It can be used to refer to a person, an object, or an idea. For example, "Η ελθούσα του Πάσχα ήταν μια όμορφη εμπειρία" (The coming of Easter was a beautiful experience).
