Word: εληλυθας

Etymology:
The word εληλυθας (elēlythas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ἔρχομαι (érchomai), which means "to come" or "to go." The prefix ελ- (el-) is a perfect tense marker, indicating that the action of the verb has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
εληλυθας is a second-person singular form of the perfect tense in the indicative mood. It is the past tense of the verb ἔρχομαι (érchomai), which means "you have come" or "you have gone." The verb is conjugated as follows:

εληλυθας (elēlythas) - you have come/gone (singular)

Description:
The word εληλυθας (elēlythas) is a past tense verb that indicates that the action of coming or going has been completed in the past. It is often used in the context of someone arriving at a destination or completing a journey. The word can also be used metaphorically to indicate that someone has reached a certain point in their life or achieved a particular goal.
