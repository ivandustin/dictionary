Word: ελεγον

Etymology:
The word ελεγον (el-eg-on) is a Greek verb in the past tense, which means "I said" or "I spoke". It comes from the root word λέγω (lego), which means "to say" or "to speak". 

Grammar:
ελεγον is a first-person singular past tense verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description:
ελεγον is a common verb in the Greek language, used to express past actions of speaking or saying. It is often used in narratives or storytelling to describe what someone said in the past. The verb can be conjugated to match the subject of the sentence, and can also be used in different tenses to describe actions in the present or future.
