Word: ελπίζω (elpizo)

Etymology: The word ελπίζω (elpizo) comes from the Greek root word ελπίς (elpis), which means "hope" or "expectation". The suffix -ζω (-zo) is a verb ending that indicates the action of hoping or expecting.

Grammar: ελπίζω (elpizo) is a first-person singular present tense verb in Greek. It is often translated to "I hope" or "I expect". The verb can be conjugated to match the tense, mood, and voice of the sentence.

Description: ελπίζω (elpizo) is a common Greek verb that expresses the act of hoping or expecting. It is often used in everyday conversation and can be used in a variety of contexts, such as expressing optimism for the future or expressing a desire for something to happen. The word is also used in religious contexts, such as expressing hope in God or in the afterlife.
