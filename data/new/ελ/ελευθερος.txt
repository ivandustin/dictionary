Word: ελευθερος

Etymology:
The word ελευθερος (eleutherós) comes from the Greek root word ελευθερία (eleuthería), which means "freedom" or "liberty". The suffix -ος (-os) is a common ending for Greek adjectives.

Grammar:
ελευθερος is a masculine singular adjective in Greek. It is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence. The word can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
ελευθερος means "free" or "liberated" in Greek. It can be used to describe a person who is free from physical or mental constraints, or a society that values freedom and democracy. The word has been used throughout Greek history, from the ancient city-states to the modern Greek state.
