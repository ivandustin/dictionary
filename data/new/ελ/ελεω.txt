The word "ελεω" (eleo) is a Greek verb that means "to have mercy" or "to show compassion." It comes from the Greek word "ελεημοσύνη" (eleemosyne), which means "mercy" or "compassion." 

In terms of grammar, "ελεω" is a first-person singular present active indicative verb. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and moods, such as the aorist tense ("ελεησα") or the subjunctive mood ("να ελεω").

In Greek mythology, the goddess Eleos was the personification of mercy and compassion. She was often depicted holding a horn of plenty, symbolizing her ability to provide for those in need.

Overall, "ελεω" is a word that emphasizes the importance of showing kindness and compassion towards others.
