The word "ελευσομαι" (eleusomai) is a Greek verb that means "I will come" or "I will arrive". It is a future tense form of the verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to arrive". 

Etymology: The word "ελευσομαι" comes from the Greek root "ελευθερία" (eleutheria), which means "freedom" or "liberty". This root is also found in the name of the ancient Greek goddess Eleutheria, who was associated with freedom and independence.

Grammar: "ελευσομαι" is a first-person singular future tense form of the verb "έρχομαι". In Greek, verbs are conjugated to indicate the tense, person, and number of the subject. The future tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future.

Description: "ελευσομαι" is a common Greek verb that is used in everyday conversation. It can be used to express the speaker's intention to come or arrive at a certain place or time in the future. For example, "Θα ελευσομαι στις 8 το βράδυ" (Tha eleusomai stis 8 to vradi) means "I will come at 8 o'clock in the evening".
