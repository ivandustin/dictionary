Word: ελαλησαμεν

Etymology: ελαλησαμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the past tense, so ελαλησαμεν means "we spoke" or "we talked".

Grammar: ελαλησαμεν is a first-person plural past tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root word λαλέω (laleo) and the past tense prefix ε- (e-). The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: ελαλησαμεν means "we spoke" or "we talked" in Greek. It is a past tense verb that indicates a completed action in the past. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as in a conversation, a speech, or a narrative.
