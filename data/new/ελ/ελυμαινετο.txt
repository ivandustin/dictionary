In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, mood, and person. The past tense indicates that the action happened in the past, the passive voice indicates that the subject received the action, and the third-person singular indicates that the subject is he/she/it.

Description: ελυμαινετο is a word that describes a state of distress or trouble. It can be used to describe a person who is anxious, worried, or upset about something.
