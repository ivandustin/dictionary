Word: ελαμβανον

Etymology: 
The word ελαμβανον comes from the Greek verb λαμβάνω (lamváno), which means "to take" or "to receive". The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the present tense, while the suffix -ον (-on) indicates the first person singular.

Grammar:
ελαμβανον is the first person singular of the present tense of the Greek verb λαμβάνω. It is an active verb, meaning that the subject is performing the action of taking or receiving. The verb is in the indicative mood, which is used to make statements or ask questions.

Description:
ελαμβανον is a Greek verb that means "I take" or "I receive". It is used to indicate that the subject is actively taking or receiving something. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in everyday conversation, in literature, or in formal writing. It is an important verb in the Greek language and is used frequently in both spoken and written communication.
