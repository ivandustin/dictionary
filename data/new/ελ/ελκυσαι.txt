Word: ελκυσαι

Etymology: The word ελκυσαι comes from the Greek verb ελκύω (elkýo), which means "to draw" or "to pull". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁elǵʰ-.

Grammar: ελκυσαι is the second person singular, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb ελκύω (elkýo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself.

Description: ελκυσαι can be translated as "you pulled yourself" or "you drew yourself". It implies a voluntary action, as the subject is performing the action on themselves. The context in which the word is used would determine the exact meaning and connotation.
