Word: ελαττουσθαι

Etymology:
The word ελαττουσθαι comes from the Greek verb ελαττόω (elattoo), which means "to diminish" or "to make less." The suffix -ουσθαι (-ousthai) is a passive form that indicates the action is being done to the subject.

Grammar:
ελαττουσθαι is a passive verb in the present tense, third person singular. It is derived from the verb ελαττόω (elattoo), which means "to diminish" or "to make less." The passive form indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action.

Description:
ελαττουσθαι is a Greek verb that means "to be diminished" or "to be made less." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a decrease in something, such as a decrease in power, influence, or importance. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe a decrease in size or quantity.
