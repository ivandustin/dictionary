Word: ελπιζουσαι

Etymology: 
The word ελπιζουσαι (elpizoosai) is a verb form in the Greek language, specifically in the present participle form of the verb ελπίζω (elpizo), which means "to hope". The word comes from the ancient Greek word ἐλπίς (elpis), which means "hope" or "expectation".

Grammar:
ελπιζουσαι is the present participle form of the verb ελπίζω (elpizo), which means "to hope". The present participle form indicates that the action of hoping is ongoing or continuous. In Greek, the present participle form is often used to form compound tenses, such as the present continuous tense.

Description:
ελπιζουσαι is a Greek word that means "hoping" or "having hope". It is often used in the context of expressing optimism or positive expectations for the future. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, career aspirations, or political movements. The present participle form of the verb ελπίζω (elpizo) is commonly used in Greek literature and poetry to convey a sense of ongoing hope or expectation.
