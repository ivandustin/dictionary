Word: Έλληνας

Etymology: The word Έλληνας (Ellinas) comes from the ancient Greek word Ἕλληνες (Hellenes), which referred to the people who lived in Greece during the classical period. The origin of the word is uncertain, but it is believed to have been derived from the name of a tribe that lived in Thessaly.

Grammar: Έλληνας is a masculine noun in Greek, and it is declined according to the rules of the second declension. The nominative singular form is Έλληνας, and the genitive singular form is Έλληνα. The plural forms are Έλληνες (nominative) and Ελλήνων (genitive).

Description: Έλληνας is the Greek word for "Greek" or "a Greek person". It is used to refer to someone who is a citizen of Greece or who has Greek ancestry. The word is also used to describe the Greek language, culture, and traditions. In modern Greek, the word Έλληνας is often used interchangeably with the word Έλλην, which is a shortened form of the same word.
