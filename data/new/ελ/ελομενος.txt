The word "ελομενος" (elomenos) is a Greek verb in the passive participle form. It comes from the root verb "ελεινω" (eleino), which means "to have mercy" or "to pity". The passive participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is receiving the action, rather than performing it.

In terms of grammar, "ελομενος" is in the masculine singular nominative case. This means that it is describing a single male subject who is receiving the action of the verb.

Overall, the word "ελομενος" can be translated to mean "having been pitied" or "having received mercy". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe someone who has been shown compassion or forgiveness.
