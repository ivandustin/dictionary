Word: ελαχιστον

Etymology: The word ελαχιστον (elachiston) comes from the Greek root words ελαχύς (elachys) meaning "small" and -ιστος (-istos) meaning "superlative suffix". Therefore, ελαχιστον means "smallest" or "least".

Grammar: ελαχιστον is an adverb in Greek, which means it modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. It is the superlative form of the adjective ελάχιστος (elachistos), which means "smallest" or "least". 

Description: ελαχιστον is a word used to describe the smallest or least amount of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in measurements, quantities, or degrees. For example, "Πήρα ελαχιστον χρήματα από τον τραπεζικό λογαριασμό μου" (I withdrew the smallest amount of money from my bank account).
