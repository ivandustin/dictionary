The word "ελισσομενον" is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "ελίσσω" (elisso), which means "to roll" or "to turn around". The participle form indicates an ongoing action of rolling or turning. In English, it can be translated as "rolling" or "turning". 

In terms of grammar, "ελισσομενον" is in the present tense, active voice, and masculine gender. It agrees with the subject it modifies in gender, number, and case. 

Overall, "ελισσομενον" is a descriptive word that describes something that is currently rolling or turning.
