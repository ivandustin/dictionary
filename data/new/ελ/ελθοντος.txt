Word: ελθοντος

Etymology: 
The word ελθοντος is derived from the Greek verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come". It is the genitive form of the participle "ερχόμενος" (erchomenos), which means "coming".

Grammar:
ελθοντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. It can also be used to indicate the source or origin of something.

Description:
ελθοντος can be translated to English as "of the one who is coming" or "of the coming one". It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to refer to a person who is about to arrive or who is expected to arrive. In Christian theology, it is used to refer to the second coming of Jesus Christ.
