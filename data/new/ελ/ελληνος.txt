Word: Ελληνός

Etymology: The word Ελληνός (Ellenos) comes from the ancient Greek word Ἕλλην (Hellen), which was the name of the mythical ancestor of the Greeks. The word was later used to refer to the people of Greece.

Grammar: Ελληνός is a masculine noun in Greek. It is declined in the singular and plural, and can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence. For example, "Ο Έλληνας μιλάει ελληνικά" (The Greek man speaks Greek).

Description: Ελληνός is the Greek word for "Greek" and is used to refer to a person of Greek nationality or ethnicity. It can also be used as an adjective to describe things that are related to Greece or Greek culture. For example, "Η ελληνική κουζίνα είναι πολύ γνωστή σε όλο τον κόσμο" (Greek cuisine is well-known all over the world).
