The word "ελεων" (eleon) is a Greek noun that means "olive tree" or "olive grove". It is derived from the Greek word "ελαία" (elaia), which also means "olive tree". In Greek mythology, the olive tree was considered a symbol of peace, wisdom, and victory. 

In terms of grammar, "ελεων" is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases and numbers, such as the genitive singular "ελαίου" (elaioy) or the accusative plural "ελαίους" (elaious).

Overall, "ελεων" is a significant word in Greek culture and language, representing the importance of the olive tree in Mediterranean cuisine, agriculture, and mythology.
