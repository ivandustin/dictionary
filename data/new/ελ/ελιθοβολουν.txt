The word "ελιθοβολουν" (elithovoloun) is a Greek verb that means "to stone" or "to throw stones at". 

Etymology: The word is derived from two Greek words: "λίθος" (lithos) meaning "stone" and "βάλλω" (ballo) meaning "to throw". 

Grammar: "Ελιθοβολούν" is the present tense, third person plural form of the verb. 

Description: The act of "ελιθοβολία" (elithovolia) was a common form of punishment in ancient Greece, where a person would be pelted with stones until they died. It was also used as a form of execution for certain crimes. In modern times, the word is used more generally to describe the act of throwing stones at someone or something.
