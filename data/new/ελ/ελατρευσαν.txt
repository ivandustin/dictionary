Word: ελατρεύσαν (elatreusan)

Etymology: The word ελατρεύσαν comes from the Greek verb ελατρεύω (elatreuo), which means "to worship" or "to serve as a priest". The -σαν (-san) ending indicates that it is in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Grammar: ελατρεύσαν is the third person plural form of the aorist tense of the verb ελατρεύω. In this form, it is used to describe an action that was completed by a group of people in the past. 

Description: ελατρεύσαν is a Greek word that means "they worshiped" or "they served as priests". It is often used in the context of religious worship or rituals. The word has a strong connotation of devotion and reverence towards a deity or higher power.
