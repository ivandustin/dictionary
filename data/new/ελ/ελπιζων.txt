Word: ελπιζων

The word ελπιζων (elpizon) is the present participle of the Greek verb ελπιζω (elpizo), which means "to hope". The word comes from the Greek noun ελπις (elpis), which means "hope" or "expectation".

ελπιζων is a present participle in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb ελπιζω, which is in the present tense, indicative mood, and active voice. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state of being, and in this case, it describes someone who is currently hoping.

Description:
ελπιζων can be translated to "hoping" or "one who hopes". It describes a person who has a positive outlook and is looking forward to a positive outcome.
