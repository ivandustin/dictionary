Word: ελεησω

Etymology:
The word ελεησω (eleēsō) is derived from the Greek root word ελεειν (elein), which means "to have pity" or "to show mercy". The suffix -σω (-sō) is a first-person singular future tense ending, indicating that the word means "I will have pity" or "I will show mercy".

Grammar:
ελεησω (eleēsō) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the future tense. It is conjugated in the first-person singular form, meaning "I will have pity" or "I will show mercy". The verb is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σω (-sō) to the root word ελεειν (elein).

Description:
The word ελεησω (eleēsō) is a powerful expression of compassion and mercy. It is often used in religious contexts, such as in the Bible, to describe God's mercy towards humanity. In everyday language, it can be used to express a desire to show kindness and compassion towards others. The word emphasizes the importance of empathy and understanding towards those who are suffering or in need.
