Word: ελεγχος

Etymology:
The word ελεγχος comes from the Greek verb ελέγχω (eléncho), which means "to test, examine, or prove." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *leg- which means "to collect, gather, or select."

Grammar:
In Greek, ελεγχος is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and its plural form is ελέγχοι (elénchoi). The word can be used as both a countable and uncountable noun.

Description:
The word ελεγχος refers to the act of testing, examining, or verifying something. It can also mean proof or evidence. In modern Greek, it is commonly used in the context of quality control, scientific research, and auditing. The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe the process of scrutinizing or criticizing someone's actions or beliefs.
