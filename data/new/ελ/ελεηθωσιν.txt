Word: ελεηθωσιν

Etymology: The word ελεηθωσιν comes from the Greek verb ελεεω (eleéō), which means "to have mercy" or "to pity". The suffix -θωσιν (-thōsin) is a nominalizing suffix that turns the verb into a noun, indicating the act of having mercy or showing pity.

Grammar: ελεηθωσιν is a noun in the accusative case, which means it is the direct object of a verb. It is also a third declension noun, which means it has a variety of possible endings depending on its grammatical function in a sentence.

Description: ελεηθωσιν refers to an act of showing mercy or pity towards someone. It can be used in a religious context to refer to God's mercy towards humanity, or in a more general sense to describe any act of compassion or kindness towards others.
