Word: ελληνιστων

Etymology: The word ελληνιστων comes from the Greek word ελληνιστής (ellinistis), which means "a Greek speaker" or "a Hellenist". It is derived from the word Έλλην (Ellin), which means "Greek".

Grammar: ελληνιστων is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive plural form of ελληνιστής (ellinistis).

Description: ελληνιστων refers to a group of people who speak or study the Greek language. It can also refer to people who are of Greek descent or have a strong connection to Greek culture. The word is often used in academic or scholarly contexts, such as in the study of ancient Greek literature or in the field of linguistics.
