Word: ελεησον

Etymology: The word ελεησον comes from the Greek verb ελεέω (eleéō), which means "to have mercy" or "to pity". It is derived from the noun ελεος (eleos), which means "mercy" or "pity".

Grammar: ελεησον is a verb in the aorist imperative mood, second person singular. It is often used as a plea for mercy or compassion, and is commonly translated as "have mercy" or "show pity".

Description: ελεησον is a powerful and emotive word that is often used in religious or spiritual contexts. It is a call for compassion and mercy, and is often used in prayers or supplications. The word carries a sense of urgency and desperation, and is often used in times of great need or distress.
