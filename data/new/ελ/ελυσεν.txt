Word: ελυσεν

Etymology: ελυσεν is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the aorist tense. It comes from the root word λύω (lyo), which means "to loosen" or "to untie."

Grammar: ελυσεν is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb λύω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past.

Description: ελυσεν can be translated to English as "he/she/it loosened" or "he/she/it untied." It is a verb that describes an action that has been completed in the past. The context in which it is used will determine the specific meaning and significance of the action.
