The word "ελκη" (elki) is a Greek noun that means "attraction" or "pull". 

Etymology: The word comes from the ancient Greek verb "ελκύω" (elkyo), which means "to draw" or "to pull". 

Grammar: "Ελκη" is a feminine noun in the singular form. 

Description: The word is often used in the context of physics to describe the force of attraction between two objects, such as the gravitational pull between the Earth and the Moon. It can also be used in a metaphorical sense to describe a strong emotional or intellectual attraction between people or ideas.
