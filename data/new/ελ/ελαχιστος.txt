Word: ελαχίστος

Etymology: The word ελαχίστος (elachistos) comes from the Greek root word έλαχυν (elachyn), which means "least" or "smallest". The suffix -τος (-tos) is added to the root word to form the superlative degree, meaning "the smallest" or "the least".

Grammar: ελαχίστος is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It can also be used as an adverb, in which case it is spelled ελάχιστα (elachista).

Description: ελαχίστος is used to describe something that is the smallest or least in a group or category. It can be used to describe physical size, quantity, or importance. For example, "Αυτός είναι ο ελαχίστος αριθμός που μπορείς να χρησιμοποιήσεις" (This is the smallest number you can use). It can also be used to describe someone's abilities or achievements, as in "Είναι ο ελαχίστος από όλους τους μαθητές" (He is the least capable of all the students).
