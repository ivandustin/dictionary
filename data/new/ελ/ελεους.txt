Word: ελεους

Etymology:
The word ελεους (eleous) comes from the Greek root word ἔλεος (éleos), which means "mercy" or "pity". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁el- ("to be kind, show mercy").

Grammar:
In Greek, ελεους is a noun of the masculine gender. It is in the singular form and is declined as follows:

Nominative: ελεους
Genitive: ελέους
Dative: ελέει
Accusative: ελεον

Description:
The word ελεους is used in Greek to describe a feeling of compassion or mercy towards someone who is suffering or in need. It is often used in religious contexts to describe the mercy of God towards humanity. The word can also be used to describe a feeling of pity or sympathy towards someone who is experiencing a difficult situation.
