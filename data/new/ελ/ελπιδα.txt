Word: ελπίδα (elpida)

The word ελπίδα (elpida) comes from the ancient Greek word ἐλπίς (elpis), which means "hope" or "expectation". The word ἐλπίς is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *wel-, which means "to wish" or "to will".

Grammar:
In Greek, ελπίδα (elpida) is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is declined in the nominative case as ελπίδα and in the genitive case as ελπίδας.

Description:
ελπίδα (elpida) is a word that represents a feeling of optimism and expectation for something positive to happen in the future. It is a powerful emotion that can motivate people to work towards their goals and dreams. In Greek culture, ελπίδα is often associated with the concept of Ελπίς (Elpis), the personification of hope in Greek mythology.
