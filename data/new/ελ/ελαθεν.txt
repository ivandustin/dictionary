Word: ελαθεν

Etymology: The word ελαθεν comes from the Greek verb "λανθάνω" (lanthano), which means "to hide" or "to be concealed". The prefix "ε" indicates the past tense, so ελαθεν means "he/she/it hid" or "was hidden".

Grammar: ελαθεν is a third person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: ελαθεν is a verb that describes an action of hiding or being hidden. It can be used in various contexts, such as hiding an object or concealing a truth.
