Word: ελογιζομην

Etymology: The word ελογιζομην comes from the Greek verb λογίζομαι (logizomai), which means "to count, calculate, reckon, or consider." The prefix ε- (e-) indicates the first person singular form of the verb in the aorist tense, which means that it refers to a completed action in the past.

Grammar: ελογιζομην is a first person singular form of the aorist tense in the Greek language. It is a middle voice verb, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In English, it can be translated as "I counted," "I calculated," "I reckoned," or "I considered."

Description: ελογιζομην is a verb that expresses the idea of counting, calculating, reckoning, or considering something. It is often used in a figurative sense to indicate a mental process of evaluating or assessing a situation or a person's actions.
