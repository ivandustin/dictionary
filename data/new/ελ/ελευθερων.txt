Word: ελευθερων

Etymology: The word ελευθερων comes from the Greek word ελευθερία (eleftheria), which means "freedom" or "liberty". The suffix -ων (-on) is a common ending in Greek that is used to form adjectives.

Grammar: ελευθερων is the genitive singular form of the adjective ελεύθερος (eleftheros), which means "free" or "liberated". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: ελευθερων is an adjective that describes something or someone as "free" or "liberated". It can be used to describe a person who is free from slavery or oppression, or a country that is free from foreign rule. It can also be used in a more abstract sense to describe a person who is free from mental or emotional constraints.
