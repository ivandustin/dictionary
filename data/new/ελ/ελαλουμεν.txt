Word: ελαλουμεν

Etymology: The word ελαλουμεν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The verb λαλέω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- which means "to speak".

Grammar: ελαλουμεν is a first-person plural form of the verb λαλέω in the aorist tense. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The first-person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is "we".

Description: ελαλουμεν can be translated to "we spoke" or "we talked". It is a past tense form of the verb λαλέω and indicates that a group of people spoke or talked about something in the past.
