Word: ελαθον

Etymology:
The word ελαθον (elathon) is a Greek verb that means "I forgot" or "I have forgotten". It comes from the Greek root word λανθάνω (lanthano), which means "to escape notice" or "to be hidden". 

Grammar:
ελαθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb λανθάνω (lanthano), which means "to forget". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. 

Description:
ελαθον is a Greek verb that expresses the act of forgetting or having forgotten something. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and literature. The word can be used in various contexts, such as forgetting a name, a task, or a memory. The aorist tense of the verb emphasizes that the forgetting has already taken place and is a completed action.
