Word: ελαλησα

Etymology: The word ελαλησα comes from the Greek verb λαλέω (laléo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The prefix ε- (e-) is added to the beginning of the verb to indicate the past tense.

Grammar: ελαλησα is the first person singular past tense form of the verb λαλέω (laléo). It is used to indicate that the speaker talked or spoke in the past. The verb is conjugated as follows:

ελάλησα (elálisa) - I talked/spoke
ελάλησες (elálises) - you talked/spoke
ελάλησε (elálise) - he/she/it talked/spoke
ελαλήσαμε (elalísame) - we talked/spoke
ελαλήσατε (elalísate) - you (plural) talked/spoke
ελάλησαν (elálisan) - they talked/spoke

Description: ελαλησα is a Greek verb that is used to indicate that the speaker talked or spoke in the past. It is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of the verb λαλέω (laléo). The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in storytelling, recounting events, or simply describing a conversation that took place in the past.
