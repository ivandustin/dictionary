Word: ελθοντι

Etymology: ελθοντι is a Greek word that comes from the verb ερχομαι (erchomai), which means "to come". It is in the aorist tense and the active voice, indicating that the action of coming has already taken place.

Grammar: ελθοντι is a masculine singular form of the aorist participle of the verb ερχομαι. It is in the dative case, indicating that it is the indirect object of the sentence. In English, it would be translated as "to the one who came".

Description: ελθοντι is a word used in Greek to refer to someone who has come or arrived at a certain place. It is often used in religious or poetic contexts to refer to the coming of a deity or a hero. In the New Testament, it is used to refer to the coming of Jesus Christ.
