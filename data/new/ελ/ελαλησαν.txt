Word: ελαλησαν

Etymology: The word ελαλησαν is a conjugated form of the Greek verb λαλέω (laleo), which means "to speak" or "to talk". The verb λαλέω comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *leh₂- which means "to speak".

Grammar: ελαλησαν is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood of the verb λαλέω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description: ελαλησαν means "they spoke" or "they talked" in Greek. It is a past tense verb form, indicating that the action of speaking or talking has already been completed. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a narrative or in a conversation.
