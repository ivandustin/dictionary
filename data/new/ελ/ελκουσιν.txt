The word "ελκουσιν" (elkousin) is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb "ελκύω" (elkyo), which means "to draw" or "to pull". 

Etymology: The word "ελκύω" comes from the ancient Greek root "ἕλκω" (helko), which also means "to draw" or "to pull". 

Grammar: "Ελκουσιν" is a present tense verb in the indicative mood, which means it is used to express a fact or a statement. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the sentence is a group of people or things, and they are currently performing the action of drawing or pulling. 

Description: The word "ελκουσιν" can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people pulling a heavy object or drawing a picture together. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a group of people being drawn towards a particular idea or goal.
