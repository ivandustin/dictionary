The word "ελλην" (Ellin) is a Greek noun that refers to a person of Greek nationality or ethnicity. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "Hellas," which was the name of the country in ancient times. The word "ελλην" is used in modern Greek to refer to a Greek person, regardless of their location or citizenship. 

In terms of grammar, "ελλην" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It can be used as a subject, object, or possessive noun in a sentence. For example, "Ο ελληνας μου φίλος" (O ellinas mou filos) means "My Greek friend."

Overall, "ελλην" is a simple and straightforward word that is widely used in the Greek language to refer to Greek people.
