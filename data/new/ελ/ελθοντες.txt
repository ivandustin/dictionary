Word: ελθοντες

Etymology: 
The word ελθοντες (elthontes) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "coming" or "arriving". It is derived from the Greek verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to arrive".

Grammar:
ελθοντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. In Greek, present participles are formed by adding the suffix "-οντας" (-ontas) to the stem of the verb. The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action or state that is happening at the same time as the main verb.

Description:
ελθοντες is a word that describes a group of people who are currently arriving or coming to a certain place. It can be used in various contexts, such as in a sentence like "Οι ελθόντες φίλοι μου θα μείνουν στο σπίτι μου για λίγες μέρες" (My arriving friends will stay at my house for a few days). The word can also be used in a more general sense, such as in the phrase "οι ελθόντες του χειμώνα" (the arrivals of winter), which refers to the beginning of the winter season.
