Word: ελουσεν

Etymology: The word ελουσεν comes from the Greek verb λούω (louo), which means "to wash". It is in the third person singular, aorist tense, indicative mood, and active voice.

Grammar: ελουσεν is a verb in the Greek language. As mentioned above, it is in the third person singular, aorist tense, indicative mood, and active voice. The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating its duration. The indicative mood is used to make a statement or ask a question. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description: ελουσεν means "he/she/it washed" in Greek. It is a simple past tense verb that describes an action that was completed in the past. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone washing their hands, washing clothes, or washing dishes.
