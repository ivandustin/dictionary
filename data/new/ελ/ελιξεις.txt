The word "ελιξεις" (elixeis) is a Greek verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "ελικ-", which means "to twist" or "to wind". 

In terms of grammar, "ελιξεις" is a regular verb that follows the conjugation pattern of verbs ending in "-ω" in the present tense. It is also a transitive verb, meaning it requires a direct object to complete its meaning. 

As for its meaning, "ελιξεις" can be translated to "twists" or "winds". It is often used in the context of twisting or winding something physically, such as a rope or a wire. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or a person's actions that are convoluted or complicated. 

Overall, "ελιξεις" is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts to describe twisting or winding actions.
