Etymology: The Greek letter "ω" (omega) is derived from the Phoenician letter "waw" and is the last letter of the Greek alphabet. It is also the final letter in the Greek word "Ωμέγα" (Omega), which means "great O."

Grammar: In Greek, the letter "ω" represents the long "o" sound. It is often used as a vowel and can be found in various words and names.

Description: The letter "ω" is written in lowercase as "ω" and in uppercase as "Ω." It has a rounded shape and is similar to the English letter "o." In mathematics, "ω" is commonly used to represent angular velocity or the last element in an ordered set. In physics, it is used to denote the resistance in electrical circuits.
