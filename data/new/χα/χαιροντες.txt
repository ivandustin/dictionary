The participle form can be translated as "rejoicing" or "being glad". It is often used in the New Testament to describe people who are joyful or happy, particularly in the context of religious or spiritual matters. 

In terms of grammar, "χαιροντες" is a masculine nominative plural form, indicating that it refers to a group of people who are rejoicing. The word can also be declined in other cases and genders to indicate different grammatical functions.

Overall, "χαιροντες" is a word that conveys a sense of joy and happiness, particularly in a religious or spiritual context. Its etymology and grammar reflect its origins as a Greek verb and its use as a participle in various contexts.
