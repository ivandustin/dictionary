Word: χαρησεται

Etymology: The word χαρησεται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word χαίρω (chairō), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad". The suffix -σεται (-setai) is a third person singular future tense ending, which means "he/she/it will rejoice".

Grammar: χαρησεται is a third person singular future tense verb in Greek. It is conjugated from the root verb χαίρω (chairō) and the future tense ending -σεται (-setai). The verb χαρησεται means "he/she/it will rejoice".

Description: χαρησεται is a Greek verb that expresses the future tense of the action of rejoicing or being glad. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and literature. The verb can be used in various contexts, such as expressing happiness for a future event or expressing gratitude for something that has happened.
