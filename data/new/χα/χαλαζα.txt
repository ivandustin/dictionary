Word: χαλαζα

Etymology: The word χαλαζα (chalaza) comes from the Greek word χάλαζα (chálaza), which means "hailstone". 

Grammar: χαλαζα is a feminine noun in Greek. Its plural form is χαλάζια (chalázia). 

Description: χαλαζα refers to hailstones, which are small balls of ice that fall from the sky during thunderstorms. Hailstones can vary in size, from small pellets to large chunks of ice. They can cause damage to crops, buildings, and vehicles. In Greek mythology, Zeus was said to have used hailstones as weapons against his enemies.
