Word: χαλασαντες

Etymology: The word χαλασαντες is derived from the Greek verb χαλάω (chalaó), which means "to loosen" or "to relax". The suffix -αντες (-antes) is a common Greek suffix used to form the present participle of verbs.

Grammar: χαλασαντες is the present participle of the verb χαλάω (chalaó) in the masculine plural form. It is often used as an adjective to describe a group of people who are relaxing or taking a break.

Description: χαλασαντες can be translated to "relaxing" or "loosening". It is often used to describe a group of people who are taking a break or resting. For example, "Οι χαλασαντες στο πάρκο απολαμβάνουν τον ήλιο" (The people relaxing in the park are enjoying the sun).
