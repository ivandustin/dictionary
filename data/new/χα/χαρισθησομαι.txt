Word: χαρισθησομαι

Etymology:
The word χαρισθησομαι comes from the Greek verb χαρίζομαι (charizomai), which means "to show favor, to grant, to forgive." It is derived from the noun χάρις (charis), which means "grace, favor, kindness."

Grammar:
χαρισθησομαι is a first-person singular future passive indicative form of the verb χαρίζομαι. The verb is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject receives the action of the verb. The future tense indicates that the action will take place in the future.

Description:
χαρισθησομαι can be translated as "I will be granted favor" or "I will be forgiven." It is a form of the verb χαρίζομαι, which is used in the New Testament to describe God's grace and forgiveness towards humanity. The word emphasizes the idea of receiving something as a gift or favor, rather than earning it through one's own efforts.
