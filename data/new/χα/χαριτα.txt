The word "χαριτα" (charita) is a Greek noun that means "grace" or "favor." It is derived from the Greek word "χαρις" (charis), which means "grace" or "kindness." In Greek mythology, the Charites (also known as the Graces) were goddesses of charm, beauty, nature, creativity, and fertility. 

In terms of grammar, "χαριτα" is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases, such as the genitive case "χαριτων" (chariton) or the accusative case "χαριταν" (charitan).

Overall, "χαριτα" is a word that conveys a sense of kindness, beauty, and gracefulness. It is often used in religious contexts to refer to the grace of God or in everyday language to express gratitude or appreciation.
