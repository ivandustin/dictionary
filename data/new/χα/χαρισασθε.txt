Word: χαρισασθε

Etymology:
The word χαρισασθε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word χαρίζομαι (charizomai), which means "to show favor, to grant, to forgive." It is derived from the noun χάρις (charis), which means "grace, favor, kindness."

Grammar:
χαρισασθε is a second-person plural aorist middle imperative verb in Greek. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
χαρισασθε is a command that means "be gracious" or "show favor." It is often used in a religious context to ask for forgiveness or to express gratitude for God's grace. In everyday conversation, it can be used to ask someone to be kind or forgiving.
