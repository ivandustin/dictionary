Word: χαραν

Etymology:
The word χαραν comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Greek word χαρά (chara), which means "joy" or "happiness".

Grammar:
χαραν is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, which means that it is the direct object of a verb. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their function in a sentence.

Description:
χαραν is a word that expresses joy or happiness. It is often used in Greek literature and poetry to describe a feeling of contentment or pleasure. The word can also be used to express gratitude or appreciation for something that brings joy.
