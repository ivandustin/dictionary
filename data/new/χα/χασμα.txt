Word: χασμα

Etymology:
The word χασμα (chasma) comes from the Greek word χάσμα (chasma), which means "a gaping opening" or "a wide gap". It is derived from the verb χαίνω (chaino), which means "to gape" or "to open wide".

Grammar:
In Greek, χασμα is a neuter noun. Its plural form is χάσματα (chasmata).

Description:
χασμα refers to a wide gap or chasm, often used to describe a deep and narrow opening in the ground or a cliff. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a significant division or separation between two things or ideas. The word is often used in scientific or technical contexts, such as in geology or astronomy.
