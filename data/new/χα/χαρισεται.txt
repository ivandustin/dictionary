Word: χαρισεται

Etymology: The word χαρισεται comes from the Greek verb χαρίζομαι (charizomai), which means "to show favor, to grant, to forgive, to bestow a gift". It is derived from the noun χάρις (charis), which means "grace, favor, kindness".

Grammar: χαρισεται is a third person singular present middle/passive indicative form of the verb χαρίζομαι (charizomai). The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the recipient and the agent of the action. In this case, χαρισεται can be translated as "he/she/it is being granted/forgiven/bestowed a gift".

Description: χαρισεται is a Greek verb that expresses the idea of receiving a gift or favor. It can also be used to describe the act of forgiving someone or granting them a pardon. The word has a positive connotation and is often associated with acts of kindness and generosity.
