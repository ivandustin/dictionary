Word: χαριν

Etymology:
The word χαριν (charin) is a Greek word that means "grace" or "favor". It is derived from the Greek word χαρις (charis), which means "grace" or "kindness". The word χαριν is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the grace of God.

Grammar:
In Greek, χαριν is a noun that is typically used in the accusative case. It can be used as a direct object or as the object of a preposition. The word is often used in phrases such as "χαριν Χριστού" (charin Christou), which means "for the sake of Christ".

χαριν is a word that is often used in religious contexts to refer to the grace of God.
