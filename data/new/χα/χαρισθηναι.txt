The word χαρισθηναι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word χάρις (charis), which means "grace" or "favor." The verb form χαρισθηναι is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb χαρίζομαι (charizomai), which means "to show favor" or "to grant."

Grammar:
χαρισθηναι is a passive infinitive verb form, which means that it is not conjugated to show tense, person, or number. Instead, it is used to express the idea of the action of the verb without specifying who is performing it. In this case, the verb form χαρισθηναι means "to be granted" or "to be shown favor."

Description:
χαρισθηναι is a verb that expresses the idea of receiving favor or grace from someone or something.
