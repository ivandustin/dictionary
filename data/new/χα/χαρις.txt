Word: χαρις

Etymology:
The word χαρις (charis) is a Greek word that means "grace" or "favor." It is derived from the root word χαιρω (chairō), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad."

In Greek, χαρις is a feminine noun. It is declined in the first declension and has the following forms:

Singular:
- Nominative: χαρις (charis)
- Genitive: χαριτος (charitos)
- Dative: χαριτι (chariti)
- Accusative: χαριν (charin)

Plural:
- Nominative: χαριτες (charites)
- Genitive: χαριτων (charitōn)
- Dative: χαρισι (charisi)
- Accusative: χαριτας (charitas)

Description:
χαρις is a word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the unmerited favor or kindness of God towards humanity. It is also used to describe the gratitude or thankfulness that one feels towards God or others. In Greek literature, χαρις is often associated with beauty, charm, and elegance.
