Word: χαριζεσθε

Etymology: χαριζεσθε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word χάρις (charis), which means "grace" or "favor." The verb form χαριζεσθε means "to show grace" or "to be gracious."

Grammar: χαριζεσθε is a second-person plural present middle/passive imperative verb in Greek. It is often translated as "be gracious" or "show favor." The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the recipient and the agent of the action.

Description: χαριζεσθε is a term that is often used in Christian contexts, particularly in the New Testament. It is a call to be gracious and kind to others, to show them favor and to forgive them. It is a reminder that we have received grace and forgiveness from God, and that we should extend that same grace to others.
