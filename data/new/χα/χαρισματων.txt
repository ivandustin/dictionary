Word: χαρισμάτων

Etymology:
The word χαρισμάτων comes from the Greek word χάρισμα (charisma), which means "gift" or "grace". The suffix -ων (-on) is added to the end of the word to indicate the plural form.

Grammar:
χαρισμάτων is a noun in the genitive plural form in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "gifts" or "graces". It is often used in a religious context to refer to spiritual gifts or blessings.

Description:
χαρισμάτων is a word that refers to gifts or graces that are given to someone. These gifts can be physical, spiritual, or emotional in nature. In a religious context, χαρισμάτων can refer to the gifts of the Holy Spirit, such as wisdom, knowledge, faith, healing, and prophecy. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any kind of gift or blessing that someone receives.
