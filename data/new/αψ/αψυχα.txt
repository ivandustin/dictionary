Word: αψυχα

Etymology:
The word αψυχα is derived from the Greek word "άψυχος" (apsychos) which means "lifeless" or "soulless". The prefix "α-" means "not" or "without", and "ψυχή" (psyche) means "soul" or "life".

Grammar:
αψυχα is a feminine noun in the nominative plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word αψυχα is used to describe things that are lifeless or soulless.

Description:
αψυχα is a word that describes things that lack life or soul. It can be used to describe inanimate objects, such as rocks or machines, or it can be used to describe people who are seen as lacking emotion or vitality. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize or insult someone or something.
