Word: αψαμενος

Etymology: The word αψαμενος comes from the Greek verb αψαομαι (apsaomai), which means "to touch lightly" or "to graze." The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar: αψαμενος is an adjective in the masculine nominative singular form. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description: αψαμενος describes something that has been lightly touched or grazed. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is superficial or incomplete. For example, one might describe a superficial wound as αψαμενος.
