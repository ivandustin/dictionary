The word "αψωνται" is a Greek verb in the present tense, middle or passive voice, third person plural form. It comes from the root word "αγοράζω" which means "to buy" in Greek. The prefix "α-" indicates negation or absence, so "αψωνται" means "they are not buying" or "they are not being bought". 

In terms of grammar, "αψωνται" is a present tense verb in the middle or passive voice, which means that the subject is either performing the action on themselves (middle voice) or the action is being done to them (passive voice). 

Overall, "αψωνται" is a negative verb that indicates a lack of buying or being bought.
