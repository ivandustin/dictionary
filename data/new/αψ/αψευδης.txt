Word: αψευδης

Etymology: The word αψευδης comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of two words: α (a) meaning "not" and ψευδής (pseudis) meaning "false". Therefore, αψευδης means "not false" or "true".

Grammar: αψευδης is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: αψευδης is a word that describes something that is true, genuine, or authentic. It is often used to describe a person who is honest, sincere, and trustworthy. In Greek mythology, the goddess Athena was often referred to as αψευδης because she was known for her wisdom, honesty, and integrity.
