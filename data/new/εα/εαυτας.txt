Word: εαυτας

Etymology:
The word εαυτας (eautas) is a Greek word that comes from the root word εαυτός (eautos), which means "self" or "oneself". The suffix -ας (-as) is added to the end of the word to indicate the plural form.

Grammar:
εαυτας (eautas) is a plural noun in the accusative case in Greek. It is used to refer to "selves" or "oneselves" in the third person.

Description:
εαυτας (eautas) is a word that is used to refer to the selves or identities of individuals. It is often used in the context of self-reflection or introspection, and can be used to describe the different aspects of a person's personality or character. In Greek literature and philosophy, the concept of εαυτας (eautas) is often explored as a means of understanding the human condition and the nature of the self.
