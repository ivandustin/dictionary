Word: εαυτης

Etymology:
The word εαυτης (eautēs) is a Greek word that comes from the root word εαυτός (eautós), which means "self" or "oneself". It is derived from the reflexive pronoun αυτός (autós), which means "himself", "herself", or "itself".

Grammar:
In Greek, εαυτης (eautēs) is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It is often used to refer to one's own self or identity.

Description:
The word εαυτης (eautēs) is commonly used in Greek to refer to oneself or one's own identity. It can also be used to refer to one's own body or physical being. In literature and philosophy, it is often used to explore the concept of self-awareness and self-knowledge.
