The word "εασαντες" (easantes) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. It comes from the root verb "εάω" (eaō), which means "to allow" or "to let go". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

In terms of grammar, "εασαντες" is a participle, which is a verbal adjective that describes the subject of the sentence. In this case, it means "having let go" or "having allowed".

Overall, "εασαντες" is a specific form of the verb "εάω" that describes a completed action of allowing or letting go.
