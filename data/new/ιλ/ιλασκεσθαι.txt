Word: ιλασκεσθαι

Etymology: ιλασκεσθαι is a Greek verb that comes from the noun ιλασμος (ilasmos), which means "propitiation" or "atonement." The verb form means "to make propitiation" or "to atone for."

Grammar: ιλασκεσθαι is a middle/passive voice verb in Greek, which means that the subject is both performing and receiving the action of the verb. It is in the present tense and the infinitive form.

Description: ιλασκεσθαι is a term used in Greek mythology and religion to describe the act of making amends or appeasing a deity. It is often associated with sacrifice or offerings made to the gods in order to gain their favor or forgiveness. In a broader sense, it can also refer to any act of reconciliation or making up for a wrong that has been committed.
