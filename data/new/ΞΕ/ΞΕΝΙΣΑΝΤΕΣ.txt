Etymology: The word "ξενισαντες" comes from the Greek verb "ξενίζω" (xenízo), which means "to host" or "to entertain as a guest." It is derived from the noun "ξένος" (xénos), meaning "stranger" or "guest."

Grammar: "ξενισαντες" is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the verb "ξενίζω" (xenízo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "ξενισαντες" can be translated as "having hosted" or "having entertained as guests." It describes a group of people who have performed the action of hosting or entertaining guests in the past.
