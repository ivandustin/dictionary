In Greek, the word "ξενος" evolved to also mean "guest" or "host", and it is related to the concept of hospitality.

Grammar: In Greek, "ξενος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It has various forms depending on its grammatical role in a sentence. For example, in the nominative case, it is "ξενος" (xenos), in the genitive case, it is "ξενου" (xenou), and in the accusative case, it is "ξενον" (xenon).

Description: "ξενος" is a versatile word in Greek that can have different meanings depending on the context. It can refer to a stranger, a foreigner, a guest, or a host. It is often used to describe someone who is not from the same place or culture as the speaker. In ancient Greece, hospitality towards strangers was highly valued, and being a good host or guest was considered a virtue. The concept of "ξενος" reflects this cultural importance of hospitality.
