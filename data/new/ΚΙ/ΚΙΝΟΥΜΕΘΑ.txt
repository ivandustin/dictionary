Etymology: The word "κινουμεθα" comes from the Greek verb "κινέω" (kineo), which means "to move" or "to be in motion". The suffix "-μεθα" (-metha) indicates the reflexive form, meaning "we move ourselves" or "we are in motion".

Grammar: "κινουμεθα" is the present tense, middle voice, indicative mood form of the verb "κινέω" (kineo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the subject, tense, voice, and mood. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "κινουμεθα" is the first person plural form, meaning "we move ourselves" or "we are in motion". It implies that the subject is actively involved in the action of moving or being in motion.
