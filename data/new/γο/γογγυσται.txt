Word: γογγυσται

Etymology:
The word γογγυσται is derived from the Greek verb γογγύζω (gongyzō), which means to grumble or murmur. The suffix -σται (-stai) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to a group of people who are grumbling or murmuring.

Grammar:
γογγυσται is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or the predicate nominative.

Description:
γογγυσται refers to a group of people who are grumbling or murmuring. The word has a negative connotation, implying that the people are complaining or expressing dissatisfaction. It can be used to describe a group of dissatisfied customers, disgruntled employees, or unhappy protesters.
