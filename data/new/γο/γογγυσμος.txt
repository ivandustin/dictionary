Word: γογγυσμος

Etymology:
The word γογγυσμος comes from the Greek verb γογγύζω (gongyzō), which means to grumble or murmur. The suffix -σμος (-smos) is a common suffix in Greek that is used to form abstract nouns.

Grammar:
γογγυσμος is a masculine noun in Greek. Its plural form is γογγυσμοί (gongysmoi). It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns, which means that its stem ends in -ος (-os).

Description:
γογγυσμος refers to the act of grumbling or murmuring in a low voice. It can also refer to a low, continuous sound, such as the hum of a machine or the buzz of insects. In Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the murmuring of crowds or the grumbling of unhappy people.
