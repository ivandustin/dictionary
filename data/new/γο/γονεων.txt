Word: γονεων

Etymology:
The word γονεων (goneon) is derived from the Greek word γονεύς (goneus), which means "parent". The suffix -ων (-on) is a genitive plural ending, indicating that the word refers to "parents" in the plural form.

Grammar:
In Greek, γονεων is a noun in the genitive plural form. It is used to refer to "parents" or "ancestors". The word can be used as a subject or object in a sentence, and can be modified by adjectives or other nouns.

Description:
The word γονεων is a common term used in Greek to refer to one's parents or ancestors. It is often used in genealogy or family history research, as well as in everyday conversation. The word carries a sense of respect and reverence for one's family and heritage.
