Word: γονυπετησαντες

Etymology:
The word γονυπετησαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: γόνυ (gonu) meaning "knee" and πετάομαι (petáomai) meaning "to fall, to prostrate oneself". Therefore, the word γονυπετησαντες means "to kneel down, to prostrate oneself".

Grammar:
γονυπετησαντες is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. It is the third person plural form of the verb γονυπετέω (gonupeteó), which means "to kneel down, to prostrate oneself". The verb is formed by adding the suffix -σαντες (-santes) to the stem γονυπετη-, which indicates the past action of the verb.

Description:
γονυπετησαντες is a word that describes an action of kneeling down or prostrating oneself. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in prayer or worship. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a deep sense of respect or submission to someone or something.
