Word: γονυπετων

Etymology:
The word γονυπετων is derived from the Greek words γόνυ (gonu) meaning "knee" and πετάω (petao) meaning "to fall, to prostrate oneself". Therefore, the word γονυπετων literally means "one who falls on their knees".

Grammar:
γονυπετων is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description:
γονυπετων is a term used in ancient Greek literature to describe a person who falls on their knees in supplication or worship. It is often used to describe a person who is begging for mercy or forgiveness from a deity or a powerful ruler. In Greek mythology, many characters are described as γονυπετων, including Odysseus, who falls on his knees before the goddess Athena, and Priam, who falls on his knees before Achilles to beg for the return of his son's body.
