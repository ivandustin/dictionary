Word: γογγυσμων

Etymology:
The word γογγυσμων comes from the Greek verb γογγύζω (gongyzō), which means "to grumble" or "to murmur". The suffix -σμων (-smōn) is a plural ending, indicating that γογγυσμων refers to multiple instances of grumbling or murmuring.

Grammar:
γογγυσμων is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. γογγυσμων is a masculine noun in the nominative plural case.

Description:
γογγυσμων refers to the act of grumbling or murmuring. It can be used to describe a group of people who are complaining or expressing dissatisfaction in a low, muttering voice. The word has a negative connotation and is often associated with discontent or dissatisfaction.
