Word: γονυπετησας

Etymology:
The word γονυπετησας is derived from the Greek words γόνυ (gonu) meaning "knee" and πετάομαι (petáomai) meaning "to fall down, prostrate oneself". Therefore, the word γονυπετησας means "to kneel down, to prostrate oneself".

Grammar:
γονυπετησας is a verb in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. It is in the second person singular form, which means it is addressing one person who performed the action of kneeling down or prostrating oneself.

Description:
γονυπετησας is a Greek word that describes an act of humility and reverence. It is often used in religious contexts to describe the act of kneeling down in prayer or worship. The word can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a person who is showing great respect or deference to someone else.
