The word "γονευσιν" (gon-eu-sin) is a noun in Greek language. 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek root word "γονεύς" (gon-eus) which means "parent" or "father". The suffix "-σιν" (-sin) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple parents or fathers.

Grammar: The word is in the nominative case and is plural. It can be translated as "parents" or "fathers".

Description: The word "γονευσιν" is used in Greek literature to refer to a group of parents or fathers. It can be used in a literal sense to refer to biological parents or in a metaphorical sense to refer to a group of people who act as parents or mentors.
