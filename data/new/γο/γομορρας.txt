The word "γομορρας" (gomorras) is a Greek noun that refers to the biblical city of Sodom and Gomorrah, which were destroyed by God for their wickedness. The word is derived from the Hebrew word "Amorah," which means "a heap of ruins." In terms of grammar, "γομορρας" is a feminine noun in the genitive case. It is often used in the New Testament to refer to the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah as a warning against sin and immorality.
