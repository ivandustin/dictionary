Etymology: The word "λογιζόμενος" (logizomenos) comes from the Greek verb "λογίζομαι" (logizomai), which means "to calculate" or "to reckon." It is derived from the noun "λόγος" (logos), meaning "word" or "reason."

Grammar: "Λογιζόμενος" is the present participle form of the verb "λογίζομαι" in the masculine singular nominative case. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to describe ongoing or continuous actions.

Description: "Λογιζόμενος" can be translated as "calculating" or "reckoning." It refers to someone who is actively engaged in the process of calculating or reasoning.
