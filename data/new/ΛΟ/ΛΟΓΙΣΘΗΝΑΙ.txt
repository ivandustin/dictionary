Etymology: The word "λογισθηναι" comes from the Greek verb "λογίζομαι" (logízomai), which means "to calculate" or "to reckon." It is derived from the noun "λόγος" (lógos), meaning "word" or "reason."

Grammar: "λογισθηναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "λογίζομαι" (logízomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "λογισθηναι" is used to express the action of being calculated or reckoned in the past passive voice. It can be translated as "to have been calculated" or "to have been reckoned."
