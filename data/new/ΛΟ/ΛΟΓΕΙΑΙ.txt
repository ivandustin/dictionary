Etymology: The word "λογείαι" (logeiai) comes from the Greek word "λόγος" (logos), which means "word" or "speech". It is derived from the verb "λέγω" (lego), meaning "to speak" or "to say".

Grammar: "Λογείαι" is a plural noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the first declension and is feminine in gender. In the singular form, it is "λογεία" (logeia).

Description: "Λογείαι" refers to the study or science of words or language. It can be translated as "etymology" or "grammar" in English. It encompasses the analysis and understanding of the origins, development, and structure of words, as well as their meanings and usage.
