Etymology: The word "λοιδορούμενος" comes from the Greek verb "λοιδορέω" (loidoreo), which means "to revile" or "to insult". The suffix "-μενος" (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates the passive voice, so "λοιδορούμενος" can be translated as "being reviled" or "being insulted".

Grammar: "Λοιδορούμενος" is a present passive participle in the masculine singular form. In Greek grammar, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as independent verbs. The present tense indicates that the action is happening currently, and the passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action.

Description: "Λοιδορούμενος" describes someone who is currently being reviled or insulted by others. It implies that the person is the target of negative remarks or verbal abuse.
