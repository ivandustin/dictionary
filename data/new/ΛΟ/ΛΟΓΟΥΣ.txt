Etymology: The word "λογος" (logos) comes from the Greek language and has its roots in ancient Greek philosophy. It is derived from the verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to speak" or "to say."

Grammar: In Greek, "λογος" (logos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, numbers, and genders to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: The word "λογος" (logos) has multiple meanings and interpretations. In ancient Greek philosophy, it was often used to refer to reason, logic, or rationality. It was associated with the power of human intellect and the ability to understand and explain the world. In Christian theology, "λογος" (logos) is used to describe the divine Word or the second person of the Holy Trinity. It represents the creative and ordering principle of the universe.
