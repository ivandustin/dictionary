Etymology: The word "λογικόν" (logikon) comes from the Greek word "λόγος" (logos), which means "word" or "reason". It is derived from the root "λεγ-" (leg-), which means "to speak" or "to reason".

Grammar: "Λογικόν" is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the nominative form "λογικόν". In the plural, it becomes "λογικά".

Description: "Λογικόν" is the Greek word for "logical". It is used to describe something that is based on reason, rationality, or logical thinking. It can refer to logical reasoning, logical arguments, or logical principles. In philosophy, "λογικόν" is often associated with the study of logic and the principles of valid reasoning.
