Etymology: The word "λοιδορίας" (loidorias) comes from the Greek root "λοιδορέω" (loidoreo), which means "to revile" or "to insult". 

Grammar: "Λοιδορίας" is a noun in the genitive case, singular form. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Λοιδορίας" refers to the act of reviling, insulting, or verbally abusing someone. It can also be used to describe offensive or derogatory language.
