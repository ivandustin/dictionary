Etymology: The word "λογος" (logos) comes from the Greek language and has its roots in ancient Greek philosophy. It is derived from the verb "λέγω" (lego), which means "to speak" or "to say."

Grammar: In Greek, "λογος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It can be declined in various cases, numbers, and genders to indicate different grammatical functions.

Description: "Λογος" has multiple meanings and can be translated as "word," "speech," "reason," "logic," or "discourse." It is a fundamental concept in Greek philosophy, particularly in the works of philosophers like Heraclitus and Aristotle. It refers to the power of language and rational thought, as well as the ability to communicate and express ideas. In different contexts, "λογος" can also refer to a narrative, a story, or a philosophical argument.
