Etymology: The word "αχαϊκού" comes from the Greek word "αχαϊκός" (achaiikós), which means "Achaean" or "related to Achaea." Achaea was an ancient region in Greece.

Grammar: "Αχαϊκού" is the genitive form of the word "αχαϊκός" (achaiikós). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αχαϊκού" is an adjective in Greek that describes something as being related to or originating from Achaea. It can be used to refer to people, things, or concepts that have a connection to the ancient region of Achaea.
