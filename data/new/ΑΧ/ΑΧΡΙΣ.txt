Etymology: The word "αχρις" (achris) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "without," and the noun "χρόνος" (chronos), meaning "time." 

Grammar: In Greek, "αχρις" (achris) is a preposition that is used to indicate the absence or lack of something. It is often translated as "without" or "until" in English. It is followed by a noun or a verb in the genitive case.

Description: "αχρις" (achris) is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used to express the absence or lack of something, such as "αχρις χρήματα" (achris hrimata) meaning "without money." It can also be used to indicate a duration of time until a certain event or point, such as "αχρις αύριο" (achris avrio) meaning "until tomorrow."
