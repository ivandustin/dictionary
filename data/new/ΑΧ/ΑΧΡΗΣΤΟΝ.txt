Etymology: The word "αχρηστον" (achrēston) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the prefix "α-" (a-), meaning "not," and the noun "χρηστον" (chrēston), meaning "useful" or "good." Therefore, "αχρηστον" can be translated as "not useful" or "useless."

Grammar: In Greek, "αχρηστον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. It is used to describe something that lacks utility or practical value.

Description: "Αχρηστον" is an adjective that is commonly used in Greek to describe objects, actions, or situations that are deemed useless, ineffective, or of no practical use. It implies that something is not beneficial or does not serve a purpose.
