Etymology: The word "αχρείοι" (achreioi) comes from the Greek adjective "αχρείος" (achreios), which means "useless" or "unprofitable". It is derived from the negative prefix "α-" and the noun "χρεία" (chreia), meaning "need" or "necessity".

Grammar: "Αχρείοι" is the plural form of the adjective "αχρείος" in Greek. It is used to describe multiple things or people as being useless or unprofitable.

Description: The word "αχρείοι" is used to describe things or people that have no practical value or benefit. It implies that they are not useful or productive in any way.
