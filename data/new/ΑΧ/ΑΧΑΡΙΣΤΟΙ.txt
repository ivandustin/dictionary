Etymology: The word "αχαριστοι" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "α-" (a-) meaning "not" or "without," and the noun "χαρις" (charis) meaning "grace" or "kindness."

Grammar: "Αχαριστοι" is the plural form of the adjective "αχαριστος" (acharistos) in Greek. It is used to describe multiple people who are ungrateful or unappreciative.

Description: "Αχαριστοι" is a Greek word that refers to individuals who do not show gratitude or appreciation for acts of kindness or favors done for them. It describes people who fail to acknowledge or express thanks for the help or support they receive from others.
