Etymology: The word "αχειροποίητον" comes from the Greek roots "αχείρ" (acheir), meaning "without hands," and "ποίητον" (poieton), meaning "made." It literally translates to "handmade."

Grammar: "Αχειροποίητον" is a Greek adjective in the neuter gender. It is used to describe something that is made without the use of hands, typically referring to objects or crafts that are created manually.

Description: "Αχειροποίητον" is used to emphasize the manual craftsmanship and the absence of mechanical or industrial processes in the creation of an object. It implies uniqueness, individuality, and attention to detail.
