Word: υστερηθεις

Etymology:
The word υστερηθεις comes from the Greek verb υστερέω (hystereó), which means "to be late" or "to lag behind." The suffix -θεις (-theis) is a past participle ending, indicating that the word is in the past tense and passive voice. Therefore, υστερηθεις means "having been made late" or "having been delayed."

Grammar:
υστερηθεις is a past participle in the passive voice, which means that it describes an action that was done to the subject of the sentence. It is often used in combination with the auxiliary verb "to be" to form the passive voice. For example, "The package was delayed" can be translated as "Το πακέτο υστερήθηκε" (To paketo hystereethike) in Greek.

Description:
υστερηθεις is an adjective that describes something that has been delayed or made late. It can be used to describe a person, an event, or an object. For example, "The flight was delayed" can be translated as "Το πτήση υστερήθηκε" (To ptisi hystereethike) in Greek.
