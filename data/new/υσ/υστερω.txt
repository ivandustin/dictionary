Word: υστερω

Etymology:
The word υστερω (hystero) comes from the Greek verb υστερεω (hystereo), which means "to be behind, to lag behind, to be late." It is derived from the adjective υστερος (hysteros), which means "later, latter, last."

Grammar:
υστερω is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: υστερω (hystero) - I am late
- Plural: υστερουμεν (hysteroumen) - we are late

Description:
The word υστερω is used to describe being late or behind schedule. It can be used in a literal sense, such as being late for an appointment, or in a figurative sense, such as being behind in one's work or studies. The verb can also be used to describe a delay or a lag in progress.
