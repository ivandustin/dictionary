Word: υστερησαντος

Etymology:
The word υστερησαντος is derived from the Greek verb υστερέω (hystereó), which means "to be late" or "to lag behind." The suffix -σαντος (-santos) is the aorist participle form of the verb, indicating past action.

Grammar:
υστερησαντος is a masculine noun in the genitive case. It is the aorist participle form of the verb υστερέω (hystereó), which means "to be late" or "to lag behind." In this form, it functions as an adjective modifying a noun.

Description:
The word υστερησαντος describes someone or something that has been late or has lagged behind. It can be used to describe a person who is tardy or a situation that is delayed. In the context of Greek grammar, it is often used as an adjective modifying a noun to indicate that the noun is late or delayed.
