Word: υστερεισθαι

Etymology:
The word υστερεισθαι comes from the Greek verb υστερεω (hystereo), which means "to be behind, to lag behind, to be late." The suffix -ιζω (-izo) is added to the verb to form the passive voice, giving the meaning "to be left behind, to be delayed."

Grammar:
υστερεισθαι is a passive verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb υστερεω (hystereo), which is a regular verb of the first conjugation in Greek.

Description:
υστερεισθαι is a Greek verb that means "to be left behind, to be delayed." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone who is falling behind in their work or studies, or who is not keeping up with the pace of others.
