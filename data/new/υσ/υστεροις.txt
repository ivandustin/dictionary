Word: υστεροις

Etymology:
The word υστεροις comes from the Greek word "υστερος" (hysteros), which means "late" or "behind". The suffix "-οις" (-ois) is a dative plural ending, which indicates that the word is being used in the dative case.

Grammar:
In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb, the object of certain prepositions, and to show possession. In the case of υστεροις, it is being used as an indirect object, indicating to whom something is being given or done.

Description:
υστεροις is a Greek word that means "to the late ones" or "to those who are behind".
