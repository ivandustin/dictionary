The word "υστερει" (ysteri) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "υστερώ" (ysteró), which means "to be late" or "to lag behind". 

Grammar: "υστερει" is in the present tense, third person singular form. It can be translated to "he/she/it is late" or "he/she/it lags behind". 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone or something that is behind schedule or not keeping up with others. It can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is intellectually or socially behind their peers.
