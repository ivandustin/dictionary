Etymology: The word "βουληθείς" comes from the Greek verb "βούλομαι" (voulomai), which means "to want" or "to wish". The suffix "-θείς" (-theis) is added to the verb stem to form the aorist tense, indicating a completed action in the past.

Grammar: "Βουληθείς" is the aorist participle of the verb "βούλομαι" (voulomai). It is used to express a completed action in the past and is declined according to gender, number, and case. In this form, it is masculine, singular, and nominative.

Description: "Βουληθείς" can be translated as "having wanted" or "having wished". It is often used in Greek to introduce a condition or a purpose, indicating that something was done with a specific intention or desire.
