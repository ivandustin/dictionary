It is derived from the combination of the words "βοῦς" (boûs), meaning "ox", and "θεῖν" (theîn), meaning "to place". This etymology suggests the idea of "placing an ox" to help with heavy tasks.

Grammar: "Βοηθειαίς" is the plural form of the noun "βοηθεία" (boētheia) in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is declined as a feminine noun. In the plural form, it indicates multiple instances of help or assistance.
