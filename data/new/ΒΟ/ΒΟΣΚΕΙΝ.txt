Etymology: The word "βοσκείν" (boskein) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "βόσκειν" (boskein), which means "to feed" or "to graze." 

Grammar: "Βοσκείν" is the present tense form of the verb, which means "to feed" or "to graze." It is used in the third person singular form. 

Description: "Βοσκείν" is a Greek verb that is commonly used to describe the action of feeding or grazing animals, particularly livestock such as sheep or cattle. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of nourishing or taking care of something.
