Etymology: The word "βουνος" (vounos) comes from the Ancient Greek word "βουνός" (bounos), which means "hill" or "mountain."

Grammar: In Greek, "βουνος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The plural form is "βουνοι" (vounoi).

Description: "Βουνος" is the Greek word for "mountain" or "hill." It refers to a large natural elevation of the earth's surface, typically with steep sides and a peak. Mountains are often associated with beauty, majesty, and a sense of awe. They can be found in various parts of the world and are important for their ecological, geological, and recreational value.
