Etymology: The word "βουλομένος" (vouloménos) comes from the Greek verb "βούλομαι" (voúlomai), which means "to want" or "to wish."

Grammar: "Βουλομένος" is the masculine singular form of the present participle of the verb "βούλομαι." In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to express ongoing or continuous action. It can function as an adjective or as part of a verb phrase.

Description: "Βουλομένος" can be translated as "wanting" or "wishing." It describes someone who is currently in the state of wanting or desiring something. It can also be used to express intention or purpose.
