Etymology: The word "βοος" comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷṓws, meaning "cow" or "ox".

Grammar: In Greek, "βοος" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It belongs to the nominative case and can be used as the subject of a sentence or as a predicate nominative.

Description: "Βοος" refers to a bovine animal, specifically a cow or an ox. It is commonly used in Greek to describe domesticated cattle.
