Etymology: The word "βουλή" (vouli) comes from the ancient Greek verb "βούλομαι" (voulomai), which means "to wish" or "to want." It originally referred to a group of people who gathered to discuss and make decisions.

Grammar: In Greek, "βουλή" is a feminine noun. It is declined as follows: singular (nominative) "η βουλή" (i vouli), plural (nominative) "οι βουλές" (i voules).

Description: "Βουλή" is a Greek word that means "council" or "assembly." It refers to a group of people who come together to discuss and make decisions, especially in a political context. In modern Greek, it specifically refers to the Greek Parliament, which is the supreme legislative body of the country.
