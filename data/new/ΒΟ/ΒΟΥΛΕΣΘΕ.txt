Etymology: The word "βουλεσθε" (pronounced "voulesthé") comes from the Greek verb "βούλομαι" (pronounced "voulomai"), which means "to want" or "to wish."

Grammar: "Βουλεσθε" is the second person plural form of the verb "βούλομαι" in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people and means "you all want" or "you all wish."

Description: "Βουλεσθε" is a verb form that expresses the action of wanting or wishing. It is commonly used in Greek to make requests, express desires, or ask for opinions.
