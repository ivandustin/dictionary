Etymology: The word "ερχεσθω" (erchestho) comes from the Greek verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to go". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁erǵʰ-.

Grammar: "Ερχεσθω" is the third person singular present middle/passive imperative form of the verb "ἔρχομαι" (erchomai). In Greek grammar, the middle/passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is either performing the action on itself or is being acted upon. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "Ερχεσθω" is a verb form that is used to give a command or make a request in the third person singular. It can be translated as "let him/her/it come" or "let him/her/it go" depending on the context.
