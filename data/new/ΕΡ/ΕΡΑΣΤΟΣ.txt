Etymology: The word "εραστός" (erastos) comes from the Greek verb "εράω" (erao), which means "to love". It is derived from the noun "έρως" (eros), which refers to passionate love or desire.

Grammar: "Εραστός" is a masculine noun in Greek. It is in the nominative case, singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "εραστοί" (erastoi).

Description: "Εραστός" is a term used in Greek to describe a person who is in love or has a romantic interest in someone. It can also refer to a lover or a suitor. The word carries a connotation of passionate and intense love.
