Etymology: The word "ερχηθ" (ercheth) comes from the Greek verb "έρχομαι" (erchomai), which means "to come" or "to go". 

Grammar: "Ερχηθ" is the third person singular form of the verb "έρχομαι" in the aorist tense. It is used to indicate that someone or something came or went in the past.

Description: "Ερχηθ" is a word used in the Greek language to describe an action of coming or going that occurred in the past. It is commonly used in narratives or to talk about past events.
