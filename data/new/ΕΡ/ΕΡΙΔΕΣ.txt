Etymology: The word "εριδες" (erides) comes from the Greek word "ερις" (eris), which means "strife" or "discord". It is derived from the Greek mythology, where Eris was the goddess of discord.

Grammar: "Εριδες" (erides) is a noun in the plural form in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is feminine in gender.

Description: "Εριδες" (erides) refers to "strifes" or "quarrels" in Greek. It is used to describe conflicts or disputes between individuals or groups.
