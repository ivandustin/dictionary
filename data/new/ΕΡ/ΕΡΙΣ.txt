Etymology: The word "ερις" (eris) comes from Ancient Greek and is derived from the verb "εριζω" (erizo), which means "to strive" or "to quarrel". 

Grammar: In Greek, "ερις" is a feminine noun of the third declension. It is in the nominative case and can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence. 

Description: "Ερις" is a Greek word that means "strife" or "discord". It refers to a state of conflict, disagreement, or rivalry between individuals or groups. In Greek mythology, Eris was the goddess of strife and discord.
