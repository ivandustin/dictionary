The word "ερμογενης" (ermogenis) is a Greek adjective that can be translated as "industrious" or "hardworking" in English. Here is the breakdown of the requested information:

Etymology: The word "ερμογενης" comes from the combination of two Greek words: "εργο" (ergo), meaning "work," and "γενναω" (gennao), meaning "to generate" or "to produce." Therefore, "ερμογενης" can be understood as someone who generates or produces work.

Grammar: "Ερμογενης" is an adjective in the masculine form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. So, if used to describe a feminine noun, it would change to "ερμογενη" (ermogeni), and if used to describe a plural noun, it would change to "ερμογενεις" (ermogenis).

Description: "Ερμογενης" describes someone who is diligent, hardworking, and industrious. It implies a person who is dedicated to their work and puts in a lot of effort to achieve their goals.
