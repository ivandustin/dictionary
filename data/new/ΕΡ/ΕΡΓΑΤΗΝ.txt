Etymology: The word "εργατην" (ergaten) comes from the Greek word "εργάτης" (ergatis), which means "worker" or "laborer". It is derived from the verb "εργάζομαι" (ergazomai), meaning "to work".

Grammar: "Εργατην" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Εργατην" refers to a worker or laborer. It is used to describe someone who is engaged in physical or manual labor. The word can be used to refer to various types of workers, such as factory workers, construction workers, or agricultural workers.
