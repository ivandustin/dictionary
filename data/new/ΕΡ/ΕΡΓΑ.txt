Etymology: The word "εργα" (erga) comes from the Greek verb "εργάζομαι" (ergázomai), which means "to work" or "to labor".

Grammar: In Greek, "εργα" is a noun in the plural form. It is a neuter noun and belongs to the second declension.

Description: "Εργα" is the plural form of the noun "εργο" (ergo), which means "work" or "task" in Greek. It refers to the physical or mental effort that is exerted to achieve a specific goal or result. The word can also be used to describe a piece of work, a job, or a task that needs to be completed.
