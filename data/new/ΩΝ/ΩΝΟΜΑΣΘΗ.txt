Etymology: The word "ωνομασθή" (onomasthē) comes from the Greek roots "ὄνομα" (onoma) meaning "name" and "σθή" (sthē) meaning "to place" or "to set". 

Grammar: "Ωνομασθή" is a verb in the passive voice, aorist tense, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form. 

Description: The word "ωνομασθή" means "he/she/it was named" or "he/she/it was called" in Greek. It is used to indicate the act of giving a name to someone or something.
