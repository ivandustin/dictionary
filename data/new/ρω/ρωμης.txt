Word: ρωμης

Etymology:
The word ρωμης (romēs) is derived from the Greek word ῥώμη (rhōmē), which means "strength" or "power." It is believed that the word was originally used to describe the strength and power of the city of Rome, which was the capital of the Roman Empire.

Grammar:
In Greek, ρωμης is a feminine noun in the nominative case. Its genitive form is ρώμης (rhōmēs), and its accusative form is ρώμην (rhōmēn). The word is declined like other first-declension nouns in Greek.

Description:
ρωμης is a Greek word that refers to the city of Rome. It is often used in historical and literary contexts to describe the power and influence of the Roman Empire. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to the culture and civilization of ancient Rome.
