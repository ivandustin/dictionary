Etymology: The word "σεμνούς" (semnous) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "σεμνός" (semnos), which means "reverent" or "dignified."

Grammar: "Σεμνούς" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. In Greek grammar, adjectives agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify.

Description: "Σεμνούς" is used to describe someone or something that is characterized by seriousness, dignity, or reverence. It can refer to a person's behavior, appearance, or attitude.
