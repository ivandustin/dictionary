Etymology: The word "σεαυτού" (seautou) is derived from the Greek words "σε" (se), meaning "you," and "αυτού" (autou), meaning "of yourself." 

Grammar: "Σεαυτού" is a reflexive pronoun in the genitive case. It is used to refer back to the subject of the sentence and indicates that the action of the verb is directed towards the subject itself.

Description: "Σεαυτού" is used to express the possessive form of the reflexive pronoun "συ" (you). It is used when referring to something that belongs to or is associated with the second person singular (you) in a reflexive manner.
