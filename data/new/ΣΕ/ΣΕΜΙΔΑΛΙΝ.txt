Etymology: The word "σεμιδαλιν" comes from the Greek word "σεμίδαλις" (semidalis), which means "fine flour" or "semolina."

Grammar: "Σεμιδαλιν" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the neuter gender.

Description: "Σεμιδαλιν" refers to semolina, which is a coarse flour made from durum wheat. It is commonly used in cooking and baking, especially in the preparation of pasta, couscous, and desserts. Semolina has a slightly yellow color and a gritty texture.
