Etymology: The word "σεισμοί" (seismoi) comes from the Greek word "σείω" (seio), meaning "to shake" or "to tremble". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *tewH- which also gave rise to words like "tremor" and "tremble" in English.

Grammar: "Σεισμοί" is a plural noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is masculine in gender. In the singular form, the word is "σεισμός" (seismos).

Description: "Σεισμοί" refers to earthquakes, which are the shaking or trembling of the Earth's surface caused by the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust. Earthquakes can range in intensity from minor tremors to major destructive events. They are often associated with tectonic plate movements and can result in significant damage to buildings, infrastructure, and loss of life.
