Etymology: The word "σεισμός" (seismós) comes from the Greek verb "σείω" (seíō), meaning "I shake" or "I tremble". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *tewH- which also gave rise to words like "tremor" and "tremble" in English.

Grammar: In Greek, "σεισμός" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The plural form is "σεισμοί" (seismói).

Description: "Σεισμός" refers to an earthquake, which is a sudden and violent shaking of the ground caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. Earthquakes can vary in intensity and can cause significant damage and loss of life.
