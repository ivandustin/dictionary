Etymology: The word "σεβαστόν" (sevaston) comes from the Greek verb "σέβομαι" (sevomai), which means "to respect" or "to honor." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₂g-.

Grammar: "Σεβαστόν" is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The neuter form is used when the noun being described is neuter.

Description: "Σεβαστόν" means "respectable" or "honorable" in Greek. It is used to describe something or someone that is worthy of respect or admiration.
