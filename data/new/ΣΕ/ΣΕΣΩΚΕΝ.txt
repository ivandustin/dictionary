Etymology: The word "σεσωκέν" (sesōken) comes from the Greek verb "σῴζω" (sōzō), which means "to save" or "to rescue."

Grammar: "Σεσωκέν" is the perfect tense form of the verb "σῴζω" (sōzō) in the third person singular. The perfect tense indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present.

Description: "Σεσωκέν" is used to describe someone or something that has been saved or rescued. It implies that the action of saving or rescuing has already taken place and the person or thing is now in a state of safety or security.
