Etymology: The word "σεαυτοί" (seauti) comes from the Greek language. It is a combination of the reflexive pronoun "σε" (se) meaning "yourself" and the plural form of the personal pronoun "εσείς" (eseis) meaning "you". 

Grammar: "Σεαυτοί" is the plural form of the reflexive pronoun in Greek. It is used to refer to a group of people performing an action on themselves. It is commonly used in the third person plural.

Description: "Σεαυτοί" is a pronoun that emphasizes self-action or self-reference. It is used when the subject and the object of a sentence refer to the same group of people. For example, "Οι φίλοι σας πρέπει να φροντίζουν τους εαυτούς τους" (Your friends should take care of themselves).
