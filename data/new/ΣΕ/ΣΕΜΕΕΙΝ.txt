Etymology: The word "σεμείν" (semein) comes from the Greek verb "σημαίνω" (semaino), which means "to signify" or "to indicate."

Grammar: "Σεμείν" is a verb in the Greek language. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Εγώ σημαίνω (ego simeeno) - I signify
- Εσύ σημαίνεις (esi simeenis) - You signify
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό σημαίνει (aftos/afti/afto simeeni) - He/She/It signifies
- Εμείς σημαίνουμε (emeis simeenoome) - We signify
- Εσείς σημαίνετε (eseis simeinete) - You signify
- Αυτοί/Αυτές σημαίνουν (aftoi/aftes simeinoon) - They signify

Description: "Σεμείν" is used to express the action of signifying or indicating something. It can be used in various contexts, such as indicating a meaning, symbolizing, or representing something.
