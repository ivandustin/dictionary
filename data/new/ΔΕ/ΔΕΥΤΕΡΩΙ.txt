Etymology: The word "δευτερωί" (deuterōi) comes from the Greek word "δεύτερος" (deuteros), meaning "second".

Grammar: "Δευτερωί" (deuterōi) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and is in the singular form.

Description: "Δευτερωί" (deuterōi) refers to the second day of the month in the Greek calendar. It is used to indicate the specific day in a month, following the first day.
