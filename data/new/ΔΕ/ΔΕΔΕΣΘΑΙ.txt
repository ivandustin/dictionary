Etymology: The word "δεδεσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "δέω" (deo), which means "to bind" or "to tie". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₁- ("to tie, to fasten").

Grammar: "δεδεσθαι" is the perfect passive participle form of the verb "δέω" (deo). In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past with ongoing results in the present. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Description: "δεδεσθαι" can be translated as "having been bound" or "having been tied". It describes a state of being bound or tied that was completed in the past but still has relevance in the present.
