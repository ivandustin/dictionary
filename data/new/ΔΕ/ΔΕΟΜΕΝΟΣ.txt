Etymology: The word "δεομένος" (deomenos) comes from the Greek verb "δέομαι" (deomai), which means "to lack" or "to need".

Grammar: "Δεομένος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the present participle of the verb "δέομαι" (deomai), which is in the middle voice. In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing or continuous action.

Description: "Δεομένος" refers to someone who is lacking or in need of something. It can also be used to describe someone who is dependent on someone or something else.
