Etymology: The word "δεσποτης" (despotis) comes from the Greek word "δεσπότης" (despotes), which means "master" or "lord". It is derived from the combination of the prefix "δεσ-" (des-), meaning "binding" or "tying", and the root "πότης" (potes), meaning "ruler" or "master".

Grammar: In Greek, "δεσποτης" (despotis) is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension, and its genitive form is "δεσπότου" (despotou).

Description: "Δεσποτης" (despotis) is a term used in Greek to refer to a person who has absolute power and control over others. It is often used to describe a ruler or a master who exercises authority and dominance. The term can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has complete control or influence over a particular situation or domain.
