Etymology: The word "δεδουλωμένας" comes from the Greek verb "δουλώνω" (doulóno), which means "to enslave" or "to subjugate". The prefix "δε-" (de-) indicates a completed action, so "δεδουλωμένας" can be translated as "enslaved" or "subjugated".

Grammar: "Δεδουλωμένας" is the feminine form of the past participle of the verb "δουλώνω". In Greek, verbs have different forms based on gender, number, and case. The "-μένας" ending indicates the past participle form for a feminine singular subject.

Description: "Δεδουλωμένας" is an adjective that describes something or someone that has been enslaved or subjugated. It can be used to refer to a person, a group of people, or even abstract concepts.
