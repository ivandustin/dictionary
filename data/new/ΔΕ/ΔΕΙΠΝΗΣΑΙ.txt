Etymology: The word "δειπνησαί" comes from the Greek verb "δειπνέω" (deipneo), which means "to dine" or "to have dinner". 

Grammar: "δειπνησαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "δειπνέω". In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that is not conjugated to show tense, mood, or person.

Description: "δειπνησαί" is used to refer to the act of dining or having dinner. It can be used as a standalone verb or as part of a sentence to describe the action of having a meal in the past.
