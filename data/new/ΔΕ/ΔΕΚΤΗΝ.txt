Etymology: The word "δεκτην" (dekten) comes from the Greek verb "δέχομαι" (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept".

Grammar: "Δεκτην" is a noun in the accusative case, singular form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Δεκτην" refers to the receiver or the recipient of something. It can be used to describe a person or an object that receives or accepts something.
