Etymology: The word "δερείς" (pronounced "deris") comes from the Greek verb "δέρνω" (pronounced "derno"), which means "to beat" or "to strike." 

Grammar: "Δερείς" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. "Δερείς" is the plural form of the noun "δέρη" (pronounced "deri"), which means "a blow" or "a strike." 

Description: "Δερείς" refers to blows or strikes, usually in the context of physical violence. It can be used to describe punches, hits, or any kind of forceful impact.
