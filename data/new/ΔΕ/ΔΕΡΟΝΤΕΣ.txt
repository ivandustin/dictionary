Etymology: The word "δεροντες" comes from the Greek word "δέρνω" (derno), which means "to beat" or "to strike". 

Grammar: "Δεροντες" is the plural form of the noun "δεροντης" (derontis) in Greek. It belongs to the third declension and is a masculine noun. 

Description: "Δεροντες" refers to a group of people who are beating or striking someone or something. It can be used to describe individuals who engage in physical violence or aggression.
