Etymology: The word "δεδουλωται" comes from the Greek verb "δουλόω" (douloo), which means "to enslave" or "to make someone a slave." The prefix "δε-" (de-) indicates the perfect tense, indicating that the action of enslavement has been completed in the past.

Grammar: "δεδουλωται" is a verb in the perfect tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb "δουλόω" (douloo), meaning "he/she/it has been enslaved."

Description: "δεδουλωται" is used to describe the state of being enslaved or made a slave. It indicates that someone or something has been subjected to the condition of slavery.
