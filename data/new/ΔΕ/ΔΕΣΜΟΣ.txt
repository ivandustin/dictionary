Etymology: The word "δεσμος" (desmos) comes from the Greek verb "δεσμεύω" (desmevo), which means "to bind" or "to tie". It is derived from the noun "δεσμός" (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie".

Grammar: In Greek, "δεσμός" (desmos) is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case, singular form. The plural form is "δεσμοί" (desmoi).

Description: "Δεσμός" (desmos) refers to a bond, tie, or connection between two or more things. It can be used in both literal and figurative contexts. In a literal sense, it can refer to a physical bond or tie, such as a rope, chain, or strap. In a figurative sense, it can refer to a connection or relationship between people, ideas, or concepts.
