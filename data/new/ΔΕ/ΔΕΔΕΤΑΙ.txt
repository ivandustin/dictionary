Etymology: The word "δεδεται" comes from the Greek verb "δένω" (deno), which means "to tie" or "to bind". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *déh₁- ("to tie, to fasten").

Grammar: "Δεδεται" is the third person singular form of the verb "δένω" in the present tense, passive voice, indicative mood. In Greek grammar, verbs are conjugated based on person, number, tense, voice, and mood. The form "δεδεται" indicates that the subject is a third person singular entity, the action is happening in the present, the voice is passive, and the mood is indicative.

Description: "Δεδεται" means "he/she/it is tied" in English. It describes the state of being bound or fastened.
