Etymology: The word "δεξασθαι" (dexasthai) comes from the Greek verb "δέχομαι" (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept."

Grammar: "Δεξασθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "δέχομαι" (dechomai). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action without specifying its duration or completion. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description: "Δεξασθαι" is an infinitive form of the verb that expresses the action of receiving or accepting something. It can be used in various contexts to indicate the act of taking in or welcoming something or someone.
