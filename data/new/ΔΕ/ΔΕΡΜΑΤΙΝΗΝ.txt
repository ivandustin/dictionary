Etymology: The word "δερματινην" comes from the Greek word "δέρμα" (derma), meaning "skin". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *der- meaning "to split" or "to flay".

Grammar: "δερματινην" is a feminine noun in the accusative case in Greek. It is declined as follows: 

Singular: δερματινην (dermatinēn)
Plural: δερματινηνς (dermatinēns)

Description: "δερματινην" refers to something that is made of or resembles leather. It can be used to describe various objects or materials that are made from or have the appearance of leather, such as leather jackets, leather bags, or leather furniture.
