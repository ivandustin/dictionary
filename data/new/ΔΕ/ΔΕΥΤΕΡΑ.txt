Etymology: The word "δευτερα" comes from the Greek word "δεύτερος" (deuteros), which means "second".

Grammar: "Δευτέρα" is a feminine noun in Greek. It is the nominative form of the word, used to refer to the second day of the week, Monday.

Description: "Δευτέρα" is the Greek word for Monday. It is the second day of the week, following Sunday. In Greece and many other countries, Monday is considered the first working day of the week after the weekend.
