Etymology: The word "δεσμιος" (desmios) comes from the Greek word "δεσμός" (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie". It is derived from the verb "δέω" (deo), which means "to bind" or "to tie".

Grammar: In Greek, "δεσμιος" is an adjective. It is used to describe someone or something that is bound, captive, or imprisoned.
