Etymology: The word "δεδωκα" (dedoka) comes from the Greek verb "δίδωμι" (didomi), which means "to give". It is in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action with ongoing effects.

Grammar: "Δεδωκα" is the first person singular form of the verb "δίδωμι" in the perfect tense. In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The perfect tense is used to express a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present.

Description: "Δεδωκα" is a Greek word that means "I have given" in English. It indicates that the action of giving has been completed in the past, but its effects continue in the present.
