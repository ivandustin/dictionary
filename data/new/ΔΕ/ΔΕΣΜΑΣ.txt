Etymology: The word "δεσμας" (desmas) comes from the Greek verb "δεσμεύω" (desmevo), which means "to bind" or "to tie". It is derived from the noun "δεσμός" (desmos), meaning "bond" or "tie".

Grammar: "Δεσμας" is a masculine noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "δεσμού" (desmou) and the plural form "δεσμοί" (desmoi).

Description: "Δεσμας" refers to a bond, tie, or connection between two or more things. It can be used in a literal sense to describe physical bonds or ties, as well as in a figurative sense to describe emotional, social, or intellectual connections.
