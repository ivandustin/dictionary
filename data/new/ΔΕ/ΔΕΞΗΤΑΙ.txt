Etymology: The word "δεξηταί" (dexetai) comes from the Greek verb "δέχομαι" (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept".

Grammar: "Δεξηταί" is the plural form of the noun "δεξητής" (dexetis), which means "receiver" or "acceptor". It is a masculine noun of the second declension in Greek.

Description: "Δεξηταί" refers to a group of people who receive or accept something. It can be used to describe individuals or a collective group who are open to receiving or accepting ideas, opinions, gifts, or any other form of offering.
