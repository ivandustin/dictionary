Etymology: The word "δεχηταί" comes from the Greek verb "δέχομαι" (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept".

Grammar: "Δεχηταί" is the plural form of the noun "δεχητής" (dechitis), which means "receiver" or "acceptor". It is used to refer to a group of people who receive or accept something.

Description: "Δεχηταί" is a Greek word that is often used in a formal or legal context. It is used to describe a group of people who have the authority or responsibility to receive or accept something, such as documents, payments, or requests.
