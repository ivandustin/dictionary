Etymology: The word "δεδαμασται" comes from the Greek verb "δαμάζω" (damazo), which means "to tame" or "to subdue". The prefix "δε-" (de-) indicates a perfect tense, suggesting that the action of taming or subduing has been completed.

Grammar: "Δεδαμασται" is a verb in the perfect tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The perfect tense indicates a completed action in the past. The third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a singular third person.

Description: "Δεδαμασται" means "he/she/it has been tamed" or "he/she/it has been subdued" in English. It describes an action that has been completed in the past, where someone or something has been brought under control or made submissive.
