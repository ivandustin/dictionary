Etymology: The word "δεησιν" comes from the Greek verb "δεομαι" (deomai), which means "to ask" or "to beg."

Grammar: "Δεησιν" is a noun in the accusative case, singular number, and feminine gender in Greek.

Description: "Δεησιν" refers to a request, plea, or supplication made by someone. It implies a sense of urgency or desperation in asking for something.
