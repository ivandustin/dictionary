Etymology: The word "δεδοκεῖ" (dedokei) comes from the Greek verb "δοκέω" (dokeo), which means "to seem" or "to appear." The prefix "δε-" (de-) adds the meaning of "already" or "indeed" to the verb.

Grammar: "Δεδοκεῖ" is a verb in the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action with ongoing effects. It is in the third person singular form.

Description: "Δεδοκεῖ" is used to express the idea of something that has already seemed or appeared to be a certain way. It implies a sense of certainty or conviction about the appearance or perception of something.
