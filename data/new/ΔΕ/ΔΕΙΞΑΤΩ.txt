Etymology: The word "δείξατω" (pronounced "deixato") comes from the Greek verb "δείκνυμι" (pronounced "deiknymi"), which means "to show" or "to demonstrate."

Grammar: "Δείξατω" is the third person singular imperative form of the verb "δείκνυμι." In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "Δείξατω" is a Greek word that translates to "let him/her/it show" or "let him/her/it demonstrate" in English. It is used to give a command or request someone to show or demonstrate something.
