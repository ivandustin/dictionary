Grammar: "Δεδουλευκαμεν" is the first person plural perfect tense form of the verb "δουλεύω" (doulevo). In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate a completed action in the past that has ongoing relevance to the present. The first person plural form indicates that the action was performed by a group of people.

Description: "Δεδουλευκαμεν" can be translated to English as "we have worked" or "we have labored." It implies that a group of people has completed a task or job in the past, and the result of their work is still relevant or noticeable in the present.
