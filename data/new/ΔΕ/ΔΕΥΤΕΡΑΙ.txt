Etymology: The word "δευτεραί" (deuteraí) comes from the Greek word "δεύτερος" (deúteros), meaning "second".

Grammar: "Δευτεραί" is the plural form of the noun "δευτέρα" (deutéra), which means "second" in Greek. It is used to refer to the second day of the week, Monday.

Description: In the Greek language, "δευτεραί" is used to describe the second day of the week, which follows Sunday. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and in calendars to indicate the day after Sunday.
