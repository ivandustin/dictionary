Etymology: The word "δεκτος" (dektos) comes from the Greek verb "δεχομαι" (dechomai), which means "to receive" or "to accept".

Grammar: In Greek, "δεκτος" (dektos) is an adjective that agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. It can be declined in different forms to match the noun it describes.

Description: The word "δεκτος" (dektos) means "acceptable" or "admissible" in Greek. It is used to describe something that is considered suitable, fitting, or worthy of acceptance. It can also refer to something that is received or welcomed by others.
