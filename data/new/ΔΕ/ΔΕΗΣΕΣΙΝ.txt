Grammar: "Δεησεσιν" is the plural form of the noun "δέησις" (deesis) in the Greek language. It belongs to the third declension and is classified as a feminine noun. In the plural form, it is declined as a second declension noun.

Description: "Δεησεσιν" refers to "prayers" or "supplications" in Greek. It is often used to describe the act of making a request or plea to a higher power, such as a god or deity.
