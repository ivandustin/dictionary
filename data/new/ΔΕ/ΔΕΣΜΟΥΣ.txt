Etymology: The word "δεσμούς" (desmous) comes from the Greek verb "δέω" (deo), which means "to bind" or "to tie". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *deh₃-.

Grammar: "Δεσμούς" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and belongs to the second declension. In the singular form, the word is "δεσμός" (desmos).

Description: "Δεσμούς" refers to "bonds" or "ties" in Greek. It can be used to describe physical or metaphorical connections or relationships between people, objects, or ideas.
