Etymology: The word "ξυρασθαι" (xyrasthai) comes from the Greek verb "ξυρίζω" (xyrizo), which means "to shave". 

Grammar: "ξυρασθαι" is the aorist middle infinitive form of the verb "ξυρίζω" (xyrizo). The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying the duration or repetition of the action. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: "ξυρασθαι" is used to express the action of shaving oneself. It is commonly used in Greek to refer to the act of removing hair from the body using a razor or other shaving tools.
