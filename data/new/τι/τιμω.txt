τιμω (timō) is a Greek verb that means "to honor" or "to value." It comes from the root word τιμή (timē), which means "honor" or "value." 

In terms of grammar, τιμω is a first-person singular present active indicative verb. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and moods, such as the aorist tense (ἐτίμησα, etimēsa) and the imperative mood (τίμα, tima).

In ancient Greek culture, τιμή was a highly valued concept, and τιμω was used to describe actions that showed respect and admiration for others. It was also used in religious contexts to describe the act of offering sacrifices to the gods as a sign of honor and reverence.

Overall, τιμω is a versatile verb that conveys the idea of honoring and valuing something or someone, whether it be a person, an object, or a deity.
