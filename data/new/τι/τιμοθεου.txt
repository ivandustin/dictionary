τιμοθεου (Timothéou) is a Greek name that is derived from the words τιμή (timi), meaning "honor" or "respect," and θεός (theos), meaning "god." Therefore, the name τιμοθεου can be translated to mean "honoring God" or "respected by God."

In terms of grammar, τιμοθεου is a genitive form of the name Τιμόθεος (Timótheos), which is a compound of the words τιμή and θεός. The genitive form is often used to indicate possession or association.

Overall, τιμοθεου is a meaningful and significant name in Greek culture, reflecting the importance of honoring and respecting the divine.
