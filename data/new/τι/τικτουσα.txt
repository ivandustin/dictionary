τικτουσα (tiktousa) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "giving birth" or "bearing offspring". It comes from the root word τίκτω (tikto), which means "to give birth" or "to produce". In Greek mythology, the goddess Hera was often referred to as τικτουσα because she was the goddess of childbirth and protector of women in labor. 

In terms of grammar, τικτουσα is in the feminine gender and singular number, agreeing with the subject of the sentence. It is also in the present tense, indicating that the action is currently happening. 

Overall, τικτουσα is a powerful and significant word in Greek culture, representing the strength and importance of women in the process of giving birth and bringing new life into the world.
