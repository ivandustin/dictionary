Word: τιτον

Etymology:
The word τιτον (titon) is a Greek word that means "titan" or "giant". In Greek mythology, the Titans were a race of powerful gods who ruled the world before the Olympian gods.

Grammar:
τιτον is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form of τιτον is τιτανος (titanos).

Description:
τιτον is a word that refers to a giant or a titan in Greek mythology. The Titans were known for their immense strength and power, and they were often depicted as towering figures with incredible abilities. In Greek literature, the Titans were often portrayed as the enemies of the Olympian gods, and their battles were the stuff of legend. Today, the word τιτον is still used to refer to anything that is large, powerful, or imposing.
