Word: τινος

Etymology: τινος is a Greek word that comes from the pronoun τις (tis), meaning "someone" or "something." It is a genitive form of the pronoun, indicating possession or relationship to something else.

Grammar: τινος is a genitive singular form of the pronoun τις (tis). In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship to something else. It is often translated in English as "of" or "belonging to."

Description: τινος is a word that is used to indicate possession or relationship to something else. It is often used in Greek literature and language to describe the ownership or origin of something.
