τιμωρων (timōrōn) is a Greek word that is the genitive plural form of the noun τιμωρός (timōros), which means "avenger" or "punisher". The word is derived from the verb τιμωρέω (timōreō), which means "to exact punishment" or "to avenge". In Greek mythology, the Furies were often referred to as τιμωροί (timōroi), which emphasizes their role as punishers of those who committed crimes or violated social norms. In terms of grammar, τιμωρων is a second declension masculine noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship.
