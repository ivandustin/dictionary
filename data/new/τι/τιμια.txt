τιμια (timia) is a Greek adjective that means "honorable" or "respectable". It is derived from the noun τιμή (timi), which means "honor" or "respect". In terms of grammar, τιμια is a feminine singular form of the adjective. It can be used to describe a person, an action, or an object that is considered honorable or respectable. For example, one might say "Η Μαρία είναι μια τιμια γυναίκα" (I Maria ine mia timia gynaika), which means "Maria is an honorable woman".
