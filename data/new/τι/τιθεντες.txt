Word: τιθέντες

Etymology:
The word τιθέντες is a present participle form of the Greek verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The verb is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dhe- which means "to put, place, set".

Grammar:
τιθέντες is a present participle form of the verb τίθημι. It is in the masculine gender, plural number, and nominative case. The present participle form indicates an ongoing action, and in this case, it means "placing" or "setting".

Description:
τιθέντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Mark 11:7, where it describes the actions of the people who spread their cloaks on the road for Jesus to ride on during his triumphal entry into Jerusalem. The word can be translated as "those who were placing" or "those who were setting".
