Word: τι

Etymology:
The word τι (ti) is a Greek interrogative pronoun that means "what". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *kʷis, which also gave rise to the Latin word "quis" and the English word "who".

Grammar:
In Greek, τι is a neuter singular pronoun that can function as a subject, object, or predicate nominative. It is often used in questions to ask for information or clarification.

Description:
τι is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is commonly used in everyday conversation to ask questions such as "τι κάνεις;" (ti kanis?) which means "what are you doing?" or "τι ώρα είναι;" (ti ora einai?) which means "what time is it?". It can also be used in more formal settings, such as in academic writing or legal documents, to ask for specific information or to clarify a point.
