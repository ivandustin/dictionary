The word "τιθεασιν" is a verb in the Greek language. It is the third person plural present indicative active form of the verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". 

Etymology: The word "τίθημι" comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰeh₁- ("to put, place, set"). 

Grammar: "τιθεασιν" is a present tense verb in the indicative mood, which means it indicates a fact or a reality. It is in the active voice, which means the subject is performing the action. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people or things. 

Description: The word "τιθεασιν" is used to describe the act of placing or setting something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as placing an object on a table, setting up a tent, or establishing a rule or law.
