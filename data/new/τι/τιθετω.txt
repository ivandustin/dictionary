Word: τιθέτω

The word τιθέτω comes from the Greek verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dhe- which means "to put, place, set".

τιθέτω is a present active imperative verb in Greek. It is in the third person singular form and is used to give a command or instruction. The verb is conjugated as follows:

τιθέτω (present active imperative)
τίθημι (present active indicative)
