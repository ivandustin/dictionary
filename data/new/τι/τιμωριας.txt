τιμωρίας (timōrías) is a Greek noun that means "punishment" or "retribution". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb τιμωρέω (timōréō), which means "to exact punishment" or "to avenge". 

Grammar: τιμωρίας is a genitive singular form of the noun τιμωρία (timōría). 

Description: The word is often used in legal or judicial contexts to refer to the punishment or penalty imposed on someone who has committed a crime or offense. It can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of retribution or revenge.
