τιθημι (tithemi) is a Greek verb that means "to place, to set, to establish". It comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰeh₁- ("to put, to place"). In Greek grammar, it is a second conjugation verb, meaning that it has a stem that ends in -θε- and forms its present tense by adding -ω. It is an irregular verb, with some forms that do not follow the regular pattern. 

Examples of τιθημι in use include:

- Τίθησιν αὐτοὺς ἐν τοῖς κλίμαξιν. (Titheisin autous en tois klimaxin.) - "He sets them on the stairs."
- Τίθει δὲ αὐτοὺς καὶ ἐπὶ τῆς κεφαλῆς αὐτοῦ. (Tithei de autous kai epi tes kephales autou.) - "He also sets them on his head."
- Τίθεμεν τὰς χεῖρας ἐπὶ τὸν σταυρόν. (Tithemen tas cheiras epi ton stauro.) - "We place our hands on the cross."

Overall, τιθημι is a versatile and important verb in Greek, used in a variety of contexts to describe the act of placing or setting something in a particular location or position.
