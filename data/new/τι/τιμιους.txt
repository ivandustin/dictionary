τιμιους (timious) is an adjective in Greek that means "honorable" or "respectable". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root word "timē" which means "honor" or "respect". 

Grammar: τιμιους is in the masculine accusative plural form. 

Description: The word is often used to describe someone who is worthy of respect or honor due to their character or actions. It can also be used to describe something that is valuable or precious.
