The word "τιμα" (pronounced "tee-ma") is a verb in the Greek language. Its etymology can be traced back to the ancient Greek word "τιμάω" (timáo), which means "to honor" or "to value." 

In terms of grammar, "τιμα" is the present tense, second person singular form of the verb "τιμάω." It is used to address someone directly and can be translated as "you honor" or "you value." 

In general, the word "τιμα" is used to express respect or admiration for someone or something. It can also be used to indicate the importance or worth of something. For example, one might say "Τιμάω πολύ τη δουλειά σου" (Timáo polý ti douleía sou), which means "I value your work very much."
