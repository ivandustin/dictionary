Word: τιθεις

Etymology:
The word τιθεις (titheis) is a present participle of the Greek verb τίθημι (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The verb τίθημι comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *dʰeh₁- ("to put, place").

Grammar:
τιθεις is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb τίθημι and means "placing" or "setting".

Description:
τιθεις is a word that describes the action of placing or setting something. It can be used in various contexts, such as placing an object in a specific location or setting a rule or standard. The word is commonly used in Greek literature and is an important part of the Greek language.
