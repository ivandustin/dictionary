τιμιος (timios) is an adjective in the Greek language that means "honorable", "respectable", or "valuable". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "tim-" which means "honor" or "respect". 

Grammar: τιμιος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can also be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. 

Description: The word is often used to describe a person who is highly respected or esteemed, or to describe something that is valuable or worthy of honor. It can also be used in a moral sense to describe someone who is virtuous or righteous.
