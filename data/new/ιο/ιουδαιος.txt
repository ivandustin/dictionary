Word: Ιουδαίος

Etymology:
The word Ιουδαίος (Ioudaios) comes from the Greek word Ιουδαία (Ioudaia), which means "Judea". It refers to a person who is a member of the Jewish community or religion.

Ιουδαίος is a masculine noun in Greek. It is declined in the nominative case as Ιουδαίος (Ioudaios), in the genitive case as Ιουδαίου (Ioudaiou), in the dative case as Ιουδαίῳ (Ioudaio), and in the accusative case as Ιουδαῖον (Ioudaion).

Description:
Ιουδαίος is a term used to describe a person who is Jewish. It can refer to someone who is ethnically Jewish or someone who practices the Jewish religion. In the New Testament, the term is often used to distinguish between Jews and Gentiles.
