Word: ιουδαιον

Etymology:
The word ιουδαιον (pronounced "ioudaion") comes from the Greek word Ιουδαίος (pronounced "Ioudaios"), which means "Jewish" or "of Judea". It is derived from the name of the tribe of Judah, one of the twelve tribes of Israel.

Grammar:
Ιουδαιον is a noun in the singular form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar. The genitive form of ιουδαιον is ιουδαιου (pronounced "ioudaiou").

Description:
Ιουδαιον is a term used in the New Testament to refer to Jewish people. It is often translated as "Jew" in English. In some contexts, it can also refer to the Jewish religion or culture. The term is used frequently in the Gospel of John, where it is often contrasted with the term "Gentile" (εθνικος, pronounced "ethnikos").
