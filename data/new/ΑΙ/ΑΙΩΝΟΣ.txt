Etymology: The word "αἰώνος" (aiōnos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root "αἰών" (aiōn), meaning "age" or "lifetime."

Grammar: "Αἰώνος" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession, origin, or relationship.

Description: "Αἰώνος" refers to the genitive form of the Greek word "αἰών" (aiōn), which means "age" or "lifetime." It can also be translated as "eternity" or "forever." In Greek philosophy and theology, "αἰών" is often used to describe an eternal or infinite period of time.
