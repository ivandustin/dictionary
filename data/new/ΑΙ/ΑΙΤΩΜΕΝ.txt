Etymology: The word "αἴτωμεν" (aitōmen) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aiteō), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid-.

Grammar: "Αἴτωμεν" is the first person plural present active subjunctive form of the verb "αἰτέω" (aiteō). It is used to express a wish, desire, or request in a hypothetical or uncertain manner.

Description: "Αἴτωμεν" is a Greek verb form that is often translated as "let us ask" or "let us request." It is commonly used in prayers, invocations, or when making a collective request.
