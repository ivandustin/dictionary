Etymology: The word "αἰδοῖος" (aidoios) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the noun "αἰδώς" (aidos), which means "shame" or "modesty."

Grammar: In Greek, "αἰδοῖος" is an adjective. It belongs to the third declension and has both masculine and feminine forms. The neuter form is "αἰδόιον" (aidoion). It can be used in the nominative, accusative, genitive, dative, and vocative cases.

Description: The word "αἰδοῖος" is often translated as "shameful," "modest," or "bashful." It is used to describe someone who is characterized by a sense of shame or modesty. It can also refer to something that is considered shameful or immodest.
