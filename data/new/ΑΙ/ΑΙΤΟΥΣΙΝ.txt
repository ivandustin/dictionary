Etymology: The word "αἴτουσιν" (aitousin) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid-.

Grammar: "Αἴτουσιν" is the third person plural present active indicative form of the verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo). In Greek grammar, it belongs to the present tense, indicating an action happening in the present time. The active voice indicates that the subject is performing the action, and the indicative mood expresses a statement of fact.

Description: "Αἴτουσιν" is used to describe the action of a group of people asking or requesting something. It can be translated as "they ask" or "they request" in English.
