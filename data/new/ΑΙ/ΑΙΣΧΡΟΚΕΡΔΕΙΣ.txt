Etymology: The word "αισχροκερδείς" comes from the Greek words "αισχρός" (aischros) meaning "shameful" or "disgraceful" and "κέρδος" (kerdos) meaning "gain" or "profit". 

Grammar: "Αισχροκερδείς" is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe people or actions that are characterized by shameful or disgraceful greed for profit.

Description: "Αισχροκερδείς" refers to individuals who prioritize their own financial gain above ethical considerations. It describes those who engage in dishonest or exploitative practices in order to achieve personal profit, often at the expense of others.
