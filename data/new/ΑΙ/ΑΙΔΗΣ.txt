Etymology: The word "αιδης" (aidēs) comes from the Greek mythology, specifically from the name of the Greek god of the underworld, Hades. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eidʰ- meaning "to be hidden" or "unseen."

Grammar: "Αιδης" (aidēs) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "αιδης" (aidēs), and in the genitive case, it is spelled as "αιδου" (aidou).

Description: In Greek mythology, "Αιδης" (aidēs) refers to the realm of the dead, the underworld. It is the domain ruled by Hades, where the souls of the deceased go after death. It is often associated with darkness, gloom, and the afterlife.
