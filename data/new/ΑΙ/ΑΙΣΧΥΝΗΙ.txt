Etymology: The word "αἰσχύνη" (aischynē) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "αἰσχύνω" (aischynō), which means "to feel shame" or "to disgrace." 

Grammar: "Αἰσχύνη" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun of the first declension. In its nominative form, it is spelled as "αἰσχύνη." 

Description: "Αἰσχύνη" refers to the feeling of shame, disgrace, or embarrassment. It is the emotional response to a perceived violation of social norms or moral standards. It can also be used to describe a sense of modesty or a desire to avoid dishonor.
