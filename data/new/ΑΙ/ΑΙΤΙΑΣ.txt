Etymology: The word "αἴτιος" (aitios) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the noun "αἰτία" (aitia), meaning "cause" or "reason."

Grammar: "Αἴτιος" is an adjective in the Greek language. It has three forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter, and it declines according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αἴτιος" is often translated as "responsible," "culpable," or "guilty" in English. It is used to describe someone or something that is the cause or reason for a particular event or situation.
