Etymology: The word "αἴτουσα" (aitousa) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid-.

Grammar: "Αἴτουσα" is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo). In Greek grammar, the present participle is used to form verbal adjectives. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αἴτουσα" is a Greek word that can be translated as "asking" or "requesting" in English. It is used to describe a female subject who is currently in the act of asking or requesting something.
