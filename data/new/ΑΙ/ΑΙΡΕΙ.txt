Etymology: The word "αἰρεῖ" (airei) comes from the Greek verb "αἴρω" (airō), which means "to lift" or "to take up." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ey-.

Grammar: "Αἰρεῖ" is the third person singular present active indicative form of the verb "αἴρω" (airō) in Ancient Greek. It belongs to the first conjugation class of verbs, which have the stem ending in -αιρ-.

Description: "Αἰρεῖ" means "he/she/it lifts" or "he/she/it takes up" in English. It is used to express the action of lifting or taking something up in the present tense.
