Etymology: The word "αἴνων" (ainōn) comes from the Greek verb "αἰνέω" (aineō), which means "to praise" or "to commend."

Grammar: "Αἴνων" is the present active participle form of the verb "αἰνέω" (aineō). It is used to describe someone who is currently praising or commending.

Description: "Αἴνων" refers to a person who is actively praising or commending someone or something. It can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is praiseworthy or commendable.
