Etymology: The word "αἰτεῖσθαι" (aiteisthai) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid-.

Grammar: "αἰτεῖσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on itself (middle voice) or receiving the action (passive voice).

Description: "αἰτεῖσθαι" is a verb in the Greek language that means "to ask" or "to request." It is used to express the act of seeking or soliciting something from someone. The middle/passive form suggests that the subject is either asking for something on their own behalf or being asked for something by someone else.
