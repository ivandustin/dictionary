Etymology: The word "αισχροκερδής" comes from the Greek words "αισχρός" (aischros) meaning "shameful" or "disgraceful" and "κέρδος" (kerdos) meaning "profit" or "gain". 

Grammar: "Αισχροκερδής" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine form and can be declined to match the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αισχροκερδής" is used to describe someone who is motivated by shameful or dishonorable gain. It refers to a person who is willing to do unethical or immoral things in order to make a profit.
