Etymology: The word "αιρετικόν" (airetikon) comes from the Greek word "αἵρεσις" (hairesis), meaning "choice" or "sect." It is derived from the verb "αἱρέω" (haireo), which means "to choose" or "to take."

Grammar: "Αιρετικόν" is a Greek adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It is used to describe something or someone as heretical or dissenting from established beliefs or doctrines.

Description: "Αιρετικόν" refers to something or someone that deviates from accepted or orthodox beliefs, especially in the context of religion or ideology.
