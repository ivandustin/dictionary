Etymology: The word "αιτίνες" (aitines) comes from the Greek language. It is the plural form of "αίτινα" (aitina), which means "which" or "who". 

Grammar: "Αιτίνες" is a pronoun in Greek. It is used to refer to a group of people or things, and it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it refers to. 

Description: "Αιτίνες" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is often used to introduce a relative clause, providing additional information about the noun it refers to. For example, "Οι φίλοι μου, οι αιτίνες με στήριξαν, είναι πολύ σημαντικοί" (My friends, who supported me, are very important).
