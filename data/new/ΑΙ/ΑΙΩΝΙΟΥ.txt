Etymology: The word "αἰώνιος" (aiōnios) comes from the Greek root "αἰών" (aiōn), meaning "age" or "eternity." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *aiw-, which means "vital force" or "life."

Grammar: "Αἰώνιος" is an adjective in the Greek language. It is in the masculine, feminine, and neuter genders, and it can be declined to match the case, number, and gender of the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αἰώνιος" is often translated as "eternal" or "everlasting" in English. It describes something that is without end or that lasts for an infinite amount of time. It can be used to refer to concepts such as eternity, immortality, or the divine.
