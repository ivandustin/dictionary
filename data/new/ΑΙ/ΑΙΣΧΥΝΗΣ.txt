Etymology: The word "αἰσχύνης" (aischynēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "αἰσχύνω" (aischynō), which means "to shame" or "to disgrace."

Grammar: "Αἰσχύνης" is a noun in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or origin.

Description: "Αἰσχύνης" refers to the concept of shame or disgrace. It is the genitive form of the noun, indicating possession or association with shame. In Greek culture, shame was considered a negative emotion associated with dishonor or moral wrongdoing.
