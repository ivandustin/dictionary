Grammar: "Αἴτιος" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. It can also be declined in other cases such as genitive, dative, and accusative.

Description: "Αἴτιος" is often translated as "cause" or "reason" in English. It refers to the person or thing that is responsible for an action, event, or outcome. It can also be used to describe someone who is guilty or at fault for something.
