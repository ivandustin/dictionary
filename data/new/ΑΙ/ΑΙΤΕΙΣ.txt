Etymology: The word "αἴτεις" (aitēs) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aiteō), which means "to ask" or "to request."

Grammar: "Αἴτεις" is the second person singular form of the verb "αἰτέω" in the present tense. It is used when addressing one person and means "you ask" or "you request."

Description: "Αἴτεις" is a word in the Greek language that is used to address someone directly and ask them to do something or request something from them. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and can be used in various contexts.
