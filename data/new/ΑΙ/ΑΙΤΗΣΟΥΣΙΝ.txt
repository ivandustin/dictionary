Etymology: The word "αἰτησοῦσιν" (aitēsoūsin) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aitéō), which means "to ask" or "to request." The suffix "-σοῦσιν" (-soūsin) indicates the third person plural form of the verb in the future tense.

Grammar: "Αἰτησοῦσιν" is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "Αἰτησοῦσιν" is the third person plural form of the future tense of the verb "αἰτέω" (aitéō), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is used to describe an action that multiple people will ask or request in the future.
