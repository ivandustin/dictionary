Etymology: The word "αἰρήσομαι" (airēsomai) comes from the Greek verb "αἴρω" (airō), which means "to lift" or "to take up." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ey- ("to lift, take up").

Grammar: "αἰρήσομαι" is a future middle indicative form of the verb "αἴρω" (airō). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future, and the middle voice suggests that the subject is performing the action on themselves.

Description: "αἰρήσομαι" means "I will lift myself" or "I will take myself up" in English. It implies an action of self-elevation or self-raising.
