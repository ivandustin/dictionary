Etymology: The word "αἰδοῦς" (aidous) comes from the Ancient Greek language. It is derived from the root "αἰδώς" (aidos), which means "shame" or "modesty."

Grammar: "Αἰδοῦς" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship.

Description: "Αἰδοῦς" refers to the concept of "shame" or "modesty" in Ancient Greek culture. It is often associated with a sense of honor, respect, and propriety. The word can also be used to describe a feeling of embarrassment or a sense of moral consciousness.
