Etymology: The word "αἰσχυνας" (aischynas) comes from the Greek verb "αἰσχύνω" (aischyno), which means "to shame" or "to disgrace." It is derived from the noun "αἰσχύνη" (aischyne), meaning "shame" or "disgrace."

Grammar: "Αἰσχυνας" is a noun in the nominative case, singular form. It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description: "Αἰσχυνας" refers to a person who feels shame or disgrace. It can also be used to describe someone who is shameful or disgraceful in their actions or behavior.
