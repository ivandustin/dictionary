Etymology: The word "αἰώνιον" (aiōnion) comes from the Greek adjective "αἰώνιος" (aiōnios), which means "eternal" or "everlasting." It is derived from the noun "αἰών" (aiōn), meaning "age" or "lifetime."

Grammar: "Αἰώνιον" is the accusative singular form of the adjective "αἰώνιος" in Greek. It agrees in case, number, and gender with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Αἰώνιον" is used to describe something that is eternal, everlasting, or without end. It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to refer to the concept of eternity or the eternal nature of God.
