Etymology: The word "αιτησασθε" comes from the Greek verb "αιτέω" (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request". The suffix "-σασθε" (-sasthe) is the second person plural middle voice ending, indicating that the action is being performed by the subject upon themselves.

Grammar: "αιτησασθε" is the second person plural form of the verb "αιτέω" in the aorist tense. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action upon themselves.

Description: "αιτησασθε" is a verb form that means "you (plural) asked" or "you (plural) requested" in Greek. It is used when addressing a group of people and referring to an action of asking or requesting that they performed in the past.
