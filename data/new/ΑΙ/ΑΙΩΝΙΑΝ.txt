Etymology: The word "αἰώνιαν" (aiōnian) comes from the Greek adjective "αἰώνιος" (aiōnios), which means "eternal" or "everlasting." It is derived from the noun "αἰών" (aiōn), meaning "age" or "lifetime."

Grammar: "Αἰώνιαν" is the accusative singular form of the adjective "αἰώνιος" in Greek. It is used to modify a noun in the accusative case.

Description: "Αἰώνιαν" is used to describe something that is eternal or everlasting. It implies a sense of permanence and timelessness. It can be used to describe concepts, qualities, or states that are unending or infinite in nature.
