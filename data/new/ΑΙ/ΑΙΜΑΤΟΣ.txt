Etymology: The word "αἴματος" (aimatos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁éh₃mos, meaning "blood."

Grammar: "Αἴματος" is a noun in the Greek language. It belongs to the second declension and is in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, nouns decline based on their gender, number, and case.

Description: "Αἴματος" means "of blood" in English. It is used to indicate possession or association with blood.
