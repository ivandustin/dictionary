Etymology: The word "αιτωμέθα" (aitometha) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request." The suffix "-μέθα" (-metha) indicates the reflexive form, meaning "we ask ourselves" or "we request for ourselves."

Grammar: "Αιτωμέθα" is the first person plural present middle/passive indicative form of the verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo). It is used when the subject "we" is performing the action of asking or requesting for ourselves.

Description: "Αιτωμέθα" is a Greek verb form that expresses the act of asking or requesting. It specifically refers to the action being performed by the first person plural (we) in the present tense. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.
