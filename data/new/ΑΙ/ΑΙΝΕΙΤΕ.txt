Etymology: The word "αινείτε" (aineíte) comes from the Greek verb "αινώ" (ainó), which means "to praise" or "to commend."

Grammar: "Αινείτε" is the second person plural form of the verb "αινώ" (ainó) in the present tense imperative mood. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to praise or commend someone or something.

Description: "Αινείτε" is a Greek verb that is used to express the act of praising or commending. It is often used in a religious or poetic context to express admiration or reverence for a deity or a person of high status.
