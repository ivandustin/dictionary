Etymology: The word "αἰτεῖσθε" (aiteisthe) comes from the Greek verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo), which means "to ask" or "to request." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eid-.

Grammar: "αἰτεῖσθε" is the second person plural present middle/passive indicative form of the verb "αἰτέω" (aiteo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the person, number, tense, mood, and voice. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is either performing the action on themselves (middle voice) or receiving the action (passive voice).

Description: "αἰτεῖσθε" is used to address a group of people and means "you all ask" or "you all request" in English. It is commonly used in formal or polite contexts when addressing multiple individuals.
