Etymology: The word "αἰωνία" (aiōnia) comes from the Greek adjective "αἰώνιος" (aiōnios), which means "eternal" or "everlasting." It is derived from the noun "αἰών" (aiōn), meaning "age" or "lifetime."

Grammar: "Αἰωνία" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is declined in the singular form and can take different cases, such as nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative, depending on its role in a sentence.

Description: "Αἰωνία" refers to the concept of eternity or everlastingness. It is often used in philosophical, religious, or theological contexts to describe a state or quality that is without end or beginning.
