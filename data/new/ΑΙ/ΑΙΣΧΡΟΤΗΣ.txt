Etymology: The word "αισχρότης" (aischrotis) comes from the Greek root "αἰσχρός" (aischros), meaning "shameful" or "disgraceful."

Grammar: "Αισχρότης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender.

Description: "Αισχρότης" refers to the quality or state of being shameful, disgraceful, or indecent. It is used to describe actions, behaviors, or words that are morally offensive or socially unacceptable.
