Etymology: The word "αιρομενον" comes from the Greek verb "αιρω" (airo), which means "to lift" or "to take away". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ey-.

Grammar: "αιρομενον" is the present participle form of the verb "αιρω" (airo) in the middle voice. In Greek grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit.

Description: "αιρομενον" is a Greek word that can be translated as "being lifted" or "being taken away". It describes an ongoing action of lifting or taking away something.
