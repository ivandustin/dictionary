Etymology: The word "αιτησωμεν" comes from the Greek verb "αιτέω" (aitéo), which means "to ask" or "to request". It is formed by adding the suffix "-σωμεν" (-somen) to the verb stem.

Grammar: "αιτησωμεν" is the first person plural, aorist subjunctive active form of the verb "αιτέω" (aitéo). The aorist tense refers to a past action, and the subjunctive mood indicates possibility or potentiality. The active voice suggests that the subject is performing the action.

Description: "αιτησωμεν" is used to express a request or a desire in the first person plural form. It can be translated as "let us ask" or "let us request".
