Etymology: The word "αἰώσιν" (aiōsin) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "αἴω" (aiō), which means "to go" or "to move."

Grammar: "Αἰώσιν" is a noun in the accusative case, plural form. In Greek grammar, nouns change their form depending on their role in the sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Αἰώσιν" refers to a group of people or things that are going or moving. It can be translated as "those who go" or "those who are moving." The specific context of its usage will determine the exact meaning.
