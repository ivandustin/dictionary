Etymology: The word "αἰνέως" (ainéōs) comes from the Greek verb "αἰνέω" (ainéō), which means "to praise" or "to commend." It is derived from the noun "αἶνος" (ainos), meaning "praise" or "commendation."

Grammar: "Αἰνέως" is an adjective in the masculine form, singular number, and nominative case. It is used to describe a noun or pronoun as "praiseworthy" or "commendable."

Description: "Αἰνέως" is used to express admiration or approval for someone or something. It implies that the person or thing being described is deserving of praise or commendation.
