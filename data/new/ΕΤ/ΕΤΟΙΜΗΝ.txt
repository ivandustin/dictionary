Etymology: The word "etymology" comes from the Greek word "etymologia," which means "study of the true sense or origin of a word." It is derived from the combination of "etymon" (true sense) and "logia" (study).

Grammar: Grammar refers to the set of rules and principles that govern the structure and usage of a language. It includes the study of syntax, morphology, phonetics, and semantics. Grammar helps us understand how words are formed, how they are organized in sentences, and how they convey meaning.

Description: "Etymology" is the study of the origin and history of words, including their changes in form and meaning over time. It involves tracing the roots and development of words, exploring their connections to other languages, and understanding the cultural and historical contexts in which they emerged. Etymology provides insights into the evolution of language and helps us understand the connections between different languages and cultures.
