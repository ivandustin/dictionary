Etymology: The word "ετεραίς" (eterais) comes from the Greek word "ἕτερος" (heteros), meaning "other" or "different".

Grammar: "Ετεραίς" is a noun in the feminine gender and in the nominative case. It is the plural form of the word "ετεραί" (eterai).

Description: "Ετεραίς" is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a group of women who were not citizens of Athens. They were typically foreign-born or slaves and were not granted the same rights and privileges as Athenian women. The term is often used in contrast to "ἀθηναίαι" (athenai), which refers to Athenian women.
