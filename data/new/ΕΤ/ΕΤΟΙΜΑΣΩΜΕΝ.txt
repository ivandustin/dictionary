Etymology: The word "ετοιμασώμεν" comes from the Greek verb "ετοιμάζω" (etimazo), which means "to prepare" or "to get ready". The suffix "-μεν" is added to the verb stem to indicate the first person plural form.

Grammar: "Ετοιμασώμεν" is the first person plural form of the verb "ετοιμάζω" in the present tense. It is used when the subject is "we" and indicates that the action of preparing or getting ready is being done by the speaker and others.

Description: "Ετοιμασώμεν" is a Greek verb form that expresses the action of preparing or getting ready. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing to indicate that the speaker and others are actively engaged in preparing for something.
