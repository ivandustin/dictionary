Etymology: The word "εταραξαν" (etaraxan) comes from the Greek verb "ταράσσω" (tarasso), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, making it "εταράσσω" (etarasso), which means "they disturbed" or "they agitated".

Grammar: "Εταράξαν" (etaraxan) is the third person plural form of the verb "εταράσσω" (etarasso) in the past tense. In Greek, verbs change their endings based on the tense, mood, and person. The "-αν" ending indicates the past tense and the third person plural.

Description: "Εταράξαν" (etaraxan) is a verb in Greek that means "they disturbed" or "they agitated". It describes an action performed by a group of people in the past.
