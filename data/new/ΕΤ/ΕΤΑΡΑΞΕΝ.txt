Etymology: The word "εταραξεν" comes from the Greek language.

Grammar: "εταραξεν" is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb "ταράσσω" (tarasso), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate".

Description: "εταραξεν" describes an action that occurred in the past, specifically the third person singular form. It indicates that someone or something caused disturbance or agitation in the past.
