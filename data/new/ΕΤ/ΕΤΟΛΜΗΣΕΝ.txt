Etymology: The word "ετολμησεν" comes from the Greek verb "ετολμαω" (etolmao), which means "to dare" or "to have the courage". 

Grammar: "ετολμησεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense of the verb "ετολμαω" (etolmao). In Greek, verbs change their form based on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "ετολμησεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it dared" or "he/she/it had the courage". It is used to describe an action of daring or having the courage to do something in the past.
