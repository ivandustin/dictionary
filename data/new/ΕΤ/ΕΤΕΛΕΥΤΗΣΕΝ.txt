Etymology: The word "ετελευτησεν" comes from the Greek verb "τελευτάω" (teleutáo), which means "to come to an end" or "to finish." The prefix "ε-" indicates the past tense, and the suffix "-εν" indicates the third person singular form.

Grammar: "ετελευτησεν" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened.

Description: "ετελευτησεν" means "he/she/it finished" or "he/she/it came to an end" in Greek. It is used to describe an action or event that has been completed in the past.
