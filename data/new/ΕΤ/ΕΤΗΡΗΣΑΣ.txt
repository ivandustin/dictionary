Etymology: The word "ετηρησας" comes from the Greek verb "τηρέω" (tereo), which means "to keep, to guard, to observe." The suffix "-ας" is added to form the past tense, making it "ετηρησας" (etirisas), which translates to "you kept" or "you observed."

Grammar: "Ετηρησας" is the second person singular form of the verb "τηρέω" in the past tense. It is used to address one person who performed the action of keeping or observing in the past.

Description: "Ετηρησας" is a Greek verb that indicates an action of keeping, guarding, or observing that was performed by the second person singular. It is commonly used in various contexts to describe actions or behaviors of individuals.
