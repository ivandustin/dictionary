Etymology: The word "ετηρησαν" comes from the Greek verb "τηρώ" (tiro), which means "to keep" or "to observe". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "τερός" (teros), meaning "guard" or "watcher".

Grammar: "ετηρησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "τηρώ" (tiro) in the past tense. It is in the indicative mood and active voice. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, and person.

Description: "ετηρησαν" is a Greek word that translates to "they kept" or "they observed" in English. It is used to describe an action that was performed by a group of people in the past.
