Etymology: The word "εταπεινώσεν" comes from the Greek verb "ταπεινόω" (tapeinoó), which means "to humble" or "to lower." The prefix "ε-" indicates the past tense, and the suffix "-σεν" indicates the third person singular form.

Grammar: "εταπεινώσεν" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on tense, mood, voice, and person. The past tense indicates that the action occurred in the past, and the third person singular form indicates that the subject of the verb is a singular third person.

Description: "εταπεινώσεν" is a word that describes an action of humbling or lowering. It can be used to express the act of making someone or something less important, significant, or proud.
