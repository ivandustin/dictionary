Etymology: The word "ετεθησαν" comes from the Greek verb "τίθημι" (tithemi), which means "to place" or "to set". The prefix "ε-" indicates the past tense, and the suffix "-αν" indicates the plural form.

Grammar: "ετεθησαν" is the third person plural form of the verb "τίθημι" in the past tense. It is used to indicate that a group of people or things placed or set something in the past.

Description: "ετεθησαν" is a Greek verb that describes an action of placing or setting something in the past. It is commonly used in narratives or descriptions to indicate that multiple individuals or objects performed this action.
