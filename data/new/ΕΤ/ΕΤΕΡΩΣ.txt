Etymology: The word "ετερως" (eteros) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the combination of the prefix "ετερ-" (eter-), meaning "other" or "different," and the suffix "-ως" (-os), which is used to form adverbs. 

Grammar: "Ετερως" is an adverb in Greek. It is used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate manner, way, or condition. 

Description: "Ετερως" is used to describe something that is done or happening in a different or alternative way. It implies a contrast or deviation from the usual or expected manner.
