The word "ρυομενον" (ryomenon) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "ρύομαι" (ryomai), which means "to flow, to stream, to pour." The participle form indicates an ongoing action, so "ρυομενον" can be translated as "flowing" or "streaming." 

In terms of grammar, "ρυομενον" is a neuter singular form, which means it can be used to describe a singular neuter noun. For example, "το ρυομενον νερό" (to ryomenon nero) means "the flowing water."

Overall, "ρυομενον" is a descriptive word that can be used to convey the idea of something in motion or in a state of flow.
