Grammar:
The word "ρυσθεντας" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It is the masculine plural form of the verb "ρύομαι" (ryomai), which means "to flow, to stream, to rush". The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb occurred in the past, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb (in this case, the masculine plural) received the action of the verb. The participle mood indicates that the word is functioning as an adjective, describing the subject of the sentence.

Description:
The word "ρυσθεντας" can be translated to English as "having been flowed, streamed, or rushed". It is often used in the context of water or other liquids flowing or rushing, such as in the phrase "ρυσθέντας ποταμούς" (rysthentas potamoús), which means "rushing rivers".
