Word: ρυπαρος

Etymology:
The word ρυπαρος (ryparos) comes from the Greek root word ρυπος (rypos), which means "dirt" or "filth". The suffix -αρος (-aros) is a common Greek suffix that is used to form adjectives, and in this case, it means "full of" or "characterized by".

Grammar:
In Greek, ρυπαρος is an adjective that can be used to describe a noun. It is declined like other first and second declension adjectives, and its form changes depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it is describing.

Description:
The word ρυπαρος is often used to describe something that is dirty, filthy, or contaminated. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a dirty room or a polluted river, as well as abstract concepts, such as a corrupt government or a morally compromised individual. In some contexts, it can also be used to describe something that is impure or unclean in a religious or spiritual sense.
