Word: ρυσθωμεν

Etymology:
The word ρυσθωμεν is derived from the Greek verb ρύομαι (ryomai), which means "to rescue" or "to save". The suffix -θωμεν (-thomen) is a first-person plural present passive subjunctive ending, indicating that the action of rescuing or saving is being done to us.

Grammar:
ρυσθωμεν is a first-person plural present passive subjunctive form of the verb ρύομαι (ryomai). In Greek grammar, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The passive voice indicates that the subject is receiving the action of the verb, rather than performing it. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now.

Description:
The word ρυσθωμεν can be translated as "let us be saved" or "may we be rescued". It is a call to action, urging the listener to take steps to ensure their safety or well-being. The subjunctive mood adds a sense of urgency or importance to the message, emphasizing the need for action. Overall, ρυσθωμεν is a powerful and motivating word that encourages people to take control of their lives and work towards a better future.
