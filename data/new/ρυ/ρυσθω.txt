Word: ρυσθω

The word ρυσθω comes from the Greek verb ρύομαι (ryomai), which means "to draw, to pull, to drag." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁rewdʰ-.

Grammar:
ρυσθω is a passive verb in the aorist tense. It is conjugated in the third person singular and is often translated as "was drawn" or "was pulled."

Description:
ρυσθω is a verb that describes the action of being drawn or pulled. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing the movement of a person or object.
