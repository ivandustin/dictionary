Word: ρυεσθαι

Etymology:
The word ρυεσθαι is derived from the Greek verb ρύω (ryō), which means "to flow" or "to stream". The suffix -εσθαι (-esthai) is a middle voice ending, indicating that the action is being performed by the subject upon itself.

Grammar:
ρυεσθαι is a present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb ρύω (ryō). In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The passive voice is used to indicate that the subject is being acted upon. The infinitive form indicates that the verb is not being conjugated for tense, person, or number.

Description:
The word ρυεσθαι can be translated to mean "to flow" or "to stream" in English. It is often used in the context of water or other liquids moving in a continuous, unbroken manner. The middle/passive voice form of the verb indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action, or that the subject is being acted upon.
