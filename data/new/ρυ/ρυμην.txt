Word: ρυμην

Etymology:
The word ρυμην (rhumēn) is derived from the Greek word ρύμος (rhumos), which means "stream" or "flowing water". It is also related to the Greek word ρέω (reō), which means "to flow".

Grammar:
In Greek, ρυμην is a feminine noun in the singular form. Its nominative case is ρυμην, and its genitive case is ρύμηνος (rhumēnos).

Description:
ρυμην refers to a stream or a current of water, typically in a river or a canal. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a flow or a current of something abstract, such as ideas or emotions.
