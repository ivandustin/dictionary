The word "ρυομενος" (ryomenos) is a Greek verb in the present participle form, which means "flowing" or "streaming". It is derived from the root word "ρεω" (reo), which means "to flow". In terms of grammar, it is a present participle, which is a verb form that describes an ongoing action. 

In Greek mythology, the river god Achelous was often depicted as a ryomenos figure, symbolizing the flow of water in rivers and streams.
