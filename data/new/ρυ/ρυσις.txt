Word: ρυσις

Etymology:
The word ρυσις (rhy̆sis) is derived from the Greek verb ρύειν (ry̆ein), which means "to flow" or "to stream." It is related to the Greek noun ρύαξ (ry̆ax), which means "stream" or "flow."

Grammar:
In Greek, ρυσις is a feminine noun of the first declension. Its genitive form is ρύσεως (ry̆seōs) and its accusative form is ρύσιν (ry̆sin).

Description:
ρυσις is a term used in ancient Greek medicine to refer to the discharge or flow of bodily fluids, such as blood, mucus, or pus. It was believed that the balance of bodily fluids, or humors, was essential to maintaining good health, and an excess or deficiency of any one humor could lead to illness. The concept of ρυσις was also used in other contexts, such as the flow of water or the movement of celestial bodies.
