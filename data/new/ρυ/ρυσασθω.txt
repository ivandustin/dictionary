Word: ρυσασθω

Etymology:
The word ρυσασθω is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ρυσις (rusis), which means "a flowing" or "a stream." The verb form ρυσασθω means "to flow" or "to stream."

ρυσασθω is a second-person singular aorist middle imperative verb in Greek. It is in the middle voice, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The word ρυσασθω is a command or request to "flow" or "stream."
