Etymology: The word "βεβαιοτερον" comes from the Greek word "βεβαίως" (bebaios), which means "certainly" or "surely." It is derived from the verb "βεβαιόω" (bebaioo), meaning "to make firm" or "to confirm."

Grammar: "Βεβαιοτερον" is the comparative form of the adjective "βεβαίος" (bebaios), which means "firm," "stable," or "reliable." In Greek grammar, the comparative form is used to compare two things, indicating that one has a higher degree of the quality expressed by the adjective.

Description: "Βεβαιοτερον" is used to describe something that is more certain, more reliable, or more stable compared to something else. It implies a higher level of confidence or assurance.
