Etymology: The word "βεβηλους" comes from the Greek word "βεβηλος" (bebēlos), which means "profane" or "unholy". It is derived from the prefix "βε-" (be-), meaning "thoroughly" or "completely", and the root "βηλος" (bēlos), meaning "sacred" or "holy".

Grammar: In Greek, "βεβηλους" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "βεβηλου" (bebēlou).

Description: The word "βεβηλους" is used to describe something or someone as profane, unholy, or disrespectful towards sacred things. It implies a lack of reverence or proper respect for religious or sacred matters.
