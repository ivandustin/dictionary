Etymology: The word "βεβληται" comes from the Greek verb "βάλλω" (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷʰel-.

Grammar: "Βεβληται" is the third person singular, aorist tense, middle voice form of the verb "βάλλω" (ballō) in Greek. The aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past, and the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself.

Description: "Βεβληται" can be translated as "he/she/it was thrown" or "he/she/it has thrown (himself/herself/itself)". It describes an action of throwing or casting that was completed in the past, and the subject of the verb is the one who performed the action on themselves.
