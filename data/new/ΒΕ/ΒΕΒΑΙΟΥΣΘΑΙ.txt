Etymology: The word "βεβαιούσθαι" comes from the Greek verb "βεβαιοῦν" (bebaioûn), which means "to confirm" or "to make certain." It is derived from the adjective "βέβαιος" (bébaios), meaning "firm" or "stable."

Grammar: "Βεβαιούσθαι" is the present middle/passive infinitive form of the verb "βεβαιοῦν" in Greek. The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. In English, it can be translated as "to be confirmed" or "to be made certain."

Description: "Βεβαιούσθαι" is a verb that expresses the idea of being confirmed or made certain. It implies a state of stability, assurance, or certainty. It can be used in various contexts, such as confirming a fact, validating a claim, or establishing the truth of something.
