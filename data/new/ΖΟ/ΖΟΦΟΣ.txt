Etymology: The word "ζοφος" (zophos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *dheubh- meaning "darkness" or "gloom".

Grammar: In Greek, "ζοφος" (zophos) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "ζοφου" (zophou).

Description: "ζοφος" (zophos) refers to darkness, obscurity, or gloom. It can be used both literally and metaphorically to describe a lack of light or clarity. In Greek mythology, it is often associated with the underworld or the realm of the dead.
