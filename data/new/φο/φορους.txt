Word: φορους

Etymology:
The word φορους (phorous) is derived from the Greek word φόρος (phoros), which means "tribute" or "tax." The word has been used in Greek language since ancient times.

Grammar:
φορους (phorous) is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and is used to refer to taxes or tributes.

Description:
φορους (phorous) is a term used to describe the taxes or tributes that are paid by individuals or businesses to the government. In ancient Greece, these taxes were often paid in the form of goods or services, such as crops or labor. Today, taxes are typically paid in the form of money. The term φορους (phorous) is still used in modern Greek to refer to taxes.
