Word: φορεσωμεν

Etymology: The word φορεσωμεν is derived from the Greek verb φορέω (foreo), which means "to wear" or "to carry". The suffix -σωμεν (-somen) is a first person plural present subjunctive ending, indicating that the word is in the subjunctive mood and refers to an action that is uncertain or hypothetical.

Grammar: φορεσωμεν is a first person plural present subjunctive form of the verb φορέω (foreo). In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now or is ongoing. The first person plural form indicates that the subject is "we".

Description: φορεσωμεν can be translated to mean "let us wear" or "let us carry". It is a call to action, suggesting that the speaker is urging themselves and others to put on or carry something.
