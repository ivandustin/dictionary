The word "φοιβην" (phoibēn) is a feminine accusative singular form of the Greek adjective "φοίβος" (phoibos), which means "bright", "shining", or "radiant". It is derived from the name of the Greek god Apollo, who was also known as Phoebus. In Greek mythology, Apollo was associated with the sun and light, and was often depicted as a radiant and beautiful young man. 

In terms of grammar, "φοιβην" is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. It is also singular, indicating that it refers to one feminine noun. 

Overall, "φοιβην" can be translated as "bright" or "radiant" in English, and is often used to describe something that is shining or glowing.
