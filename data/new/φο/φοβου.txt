The word "φοβου" (phobou) is a Greek verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It comes from the root word "φοβέω" (phobeo), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid of". 

In terms of grammar, "φοβου" is a present active imperative verb, which means it is a command or request for someone to do something in the present moment. The verb is directed towards the second person singular, which means it is addressing one person specifically.

Overall, the word "φοβου" is a command to "fear" or "be afraid of" something, and is used in a present tense, second person singular form.
