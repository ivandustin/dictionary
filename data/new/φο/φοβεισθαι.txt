The word "φοβεισθαι" (phobeisthai) is a Greek verb that means "to fear" or "to be afraid". It is derived from the noun "φόβος" (phobos), which means "fear" or "terror". 

In terms of grammar, "φοβεισθαι" is a present middle/passive infinitive, which means that it is the unconjugated form of the verb that expresses the action of fearing in the present tense, and that it can be used in either the middle or passive voice. 

In usage, "φοβεισθαι" can be used to describe a range of fears or anxieties, from everyday worries to more intense phobias. It is often used in the context of Greek philosophy and literature to explore the nature of fear and its effects on human behavior.
