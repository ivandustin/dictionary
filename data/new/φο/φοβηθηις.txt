The word "φοβηθηις" (phobēthēis) is a Greek verb that is in the aorist passive participle form. It comes from the root word "φοβέω" (phobeō), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid." The suffix "-θηις" (-thēis) indicates that the action of the verb was done to the subject, making it a passive participle.

In terms of grammar, the aorist tense in Greek refers to a past action that was completed at a specific point in time. The passive voice indicates that the subject received the action of the verb rather than performing it themselves.

Overall, "φοβηθηις" can be translated as "having been feared" or "having been afraid." It is often used in the New Testament to describe individuals who were afraid or intimidated by something or someone.
