Word: φορον

Etymology:
The word φορον (phoron) is derived from the Greek word φορός (phoros), which means "carrying" or "bearing". It is related to the verb φέρω (phero), which means "to carry" or "to bear".

Grammar:
φορον is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
φορον is a Greek word that refers to a tax or tribute that is paid to a government or ruler. In ancient Greece, it was common for city-states to collect taxes from their citizens in order to fund public works and other projects. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to various types of taxes and fees.
