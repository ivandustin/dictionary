Word: φορτιοις

Etymology: 
The word φορτιοις (phortiois) is a Greek word that comes from the root word φορτίον (phortion), which means "a burden" or "a load". The suffix -οις (-ois) is a dative plural ending, indicating that the word is in the dative case and refers to multiple objects.

Grammar:
φορτιοις is a noun in the dative plural case. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions. 

Description:
φορτιοις refers to multiple burdens or loads. It is often used in the context of commerce or trade, where it can refer to the goods being transported or the weight of those goods. The word can also be used metaphorically to refer to emotional or mental burdens.
