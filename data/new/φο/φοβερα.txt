Word: φοβερα

Etymology:
The word φοβερα (phobera) is derived from the Greek word φόβος (phobos), which means fear or terror. It is related to the English word "phobia."

Grammar:
φοβερα is the feminine form of the adjective φοβερός (phoberos), which means terrible, dreadful, or awe-inspiring. It is used to describe something that inspires fear or respect.

Description:
φοβερα can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a scary movie, a powerful storm, or a majestic mountain. It can also be used to describe a person who is intimidating or impressive. In modern Greek, it is often used colloquially to mean "awesome" or "amazing."
