Word: φοβος

Etymology:
The word φοβος (phobos) comes from the Greek root word φοβέω (phobeo), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid." It is also related to the Greek word φόβητρον (phobetron), which means "object of fear."

Grammar:
In Greek, φοβος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is also a second declension noun, meaning that its stem changes in different cases. For example, in the genitive singular form, it becomes φόβου (phobou).

Description:
φοβος is a Greek word that means "fear" or "terror." It is often used in Greek mythology to describe the fear that mortals feel when encountering the gods or other supernatural beings. The word has also been used in modern psychology to describe various types of phobias, such as arachnophobia (fear of spiders) or acrophobia (fear of heights).
