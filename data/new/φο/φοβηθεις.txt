Word: φοβηθεις

Etymology:
The word φοβηθεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φόβος (phobos), which means fear or terror. The suffix -θεις (-theis) is a second person singular aorist active indicative ending, which means that the verb is in the past tense and the subject is the second person singular (you).

Grammar:
φοβηθεις is a second person singular aorist active indicative verb in Greek. It is translated to "you were afraid" in English. The verb is in the past tense, indicating that the action happened in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject (you) performed the action of being afraid.
