Word: βαπτιστης

Etymology:
The word βαπτιστης (baptistes) is derived from the Greek verb βαπτίζω (baptizo), which means "to dip" or "to immerse". In the New Testament, the word is used to refer to John the Baptist, who baptized Jesus in the Jordan River.

Grammar:
βαπτιστης is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive (βαπτιστου), dative (βαπτιστη), and accusative (βαπτιστην).

Description:
βαπτιστης refers to a person who baptizes others. In the Christian tradition, baptism is a sacrament that involves the immersion or sprinkling of water on a person's head as a symbol of purification and initiation into the faith. John the Baptist is considered the first baptist and is revered as a prophet and saint in Christianity. The word can also be used more generally to refer to someone who advocates or promotes a particular cause or belief.
