Word: βασανίσαι

Etymology: βασανίζω (basanizo) is a Greek verb that means "to torture" or "to torment". The word βασανίσαι (basanisai) is the aorist tense of this verb, which means it refers to a completed action in the past.

Grammar: βασανίσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed at one person (you), in the past tense, and in the active voice (meaning the subject is performing the action).

Description: βασανίσαι is a strong and forceful word that conveys a sense of urgency and severity. It is often used in contexts where someone is being urged to take action or to do something difficult or painful. In the New Testament, for example, it is used to describe the suffering of Jesus on the cross, and to urge believers to endure persecution and hardship. Overall, βασανίσαι is a powerful and evocative word that captures the intensity of human experience.
