Word: βασανοις

Etymology:
The word βασανοις (basanois) is derived from the Greek word βάσανος (basanos), which means "touchstone" or "test." In ancient times, a basanos was a black stone used to test the purity of gold or silver. Later, the word came to be used metaphorically to refer to any kind of test or trial.

Grammar:
βασανοις (basanois) is a noun in the dative plural form in Greek. It is often translated as "torments" or "tortures" in English.

Description:
βασανοις (basanois) is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the punishment of the wicked in hell. It is also used in other ancient Greek literature to describe various forms of torture or torment.
