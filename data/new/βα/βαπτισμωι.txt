The word "βαπτισμωι" (baptismoi) is a Greek noun that refers to the act of baptism or the state of being baptized. It comes from the verb "βαπτίζω" (baptizo), which means "to dip" or "to immerse." In Christian theology, baptism is a sacrament that symbolizes the washing away of sins and the initiation into the Christian faith. The word "baptismoi" is plural, indicating that it refers to multiple instances of baptism. In terms of grammar, "baptismoi" is a second declension noun in the nominative case.
