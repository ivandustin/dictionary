Word: βαπτισμων

Etymology:
The word βαπτισμων (baptismos) is derived from the Greek verb βαπτίζω (baptizo), which means "to dip" or "to immerse". In Christian theology, baptism is a sacrament that involves the immersion of a person in water, symbolizing their purification and rebirth.

Grammar:
βαπτισμων is a noun in the Greek language, belonging to the masculine gender. It is in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in the sentence.

Description:
βαπτισμων refers to the act of baptism or the state of being baptized. It is a term commonly used in Christian theology to describe the sacrament of baptism, which is considered a fundamental rite of initiation into the Christian faith. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any act of immersion or purification, whether religious or secular.
