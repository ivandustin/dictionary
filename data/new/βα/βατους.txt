Etymology: The word βατους comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word βατός (batós), which means "deep, bottomless".

Description: βατους is a term used in ancient Greek literature to describe a type of fish. It is often translated as "bream" or "sea bream". The fish was highly valued in ancient Greece and was often served at banquets and feasts. It is still a popular food in Mediterranean cuisine today.
