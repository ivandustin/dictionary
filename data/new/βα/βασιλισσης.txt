Word: βασιλίσσης

Etymology: βασιλίσσης (basilíssis) is a Greek word that means "queen". It is derived from the word βασιλεύς (basileús), which means "king".

Grammar: βασιλίσσης is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship.

Description: βασιλίσσης is a word that refers to a female monarch or queen. In ancient Greece, queens were often the wives of kings or the mothers of kings. They had significant power and influence in their societies, and were often involved in political and diplomatic affairs. Today, the word βασιλίσσης is still used in modern Greek to refer to a queen or female monarch.
