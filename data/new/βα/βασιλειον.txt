Word: βασιλειον

Etymology:
The word βασιλειον (basileion) is derived from the Greek word βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". It is a noun in the neuter gender.

Grammar:
In Greek, βασιλειον is a second declension neuter noun. Its nominative singular form is βασιλειον, and its genitive singular form is βασιλείου (basileiou). The word can also be declined in the plural form.

Description:
βασιλειον is a term used in ancient Greek to refer to a royal palace or a royal residence. It was often used to describe the grandeur and magnificence of the king's dwelling place. The word is also used in the New Testament to refer to the kingdom of God or the kingdom of heaven.
