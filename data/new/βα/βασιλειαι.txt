The word "βασιλειαι" (basileiai) is a plural form of the Greek noun "βασιλεία" (basileia), which means "kingdom" or "realm". 

Etymology: The word "βασιλεία" comes from the Greek root "βασιλεύς" (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". 

Grammar: "βασιλειαι" is a feminine plural form of the noun "βασιλεία". In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: The word "βασιλειαι" refers to multiple kingdoms or realms. It can be used in a historical or mythological context, such as referring to the kingdoms of ancient Greece or the realms of the gods in Greek mythology.
