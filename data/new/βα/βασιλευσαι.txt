Word: βασιλεύσαι

Etymology: βασιλεύσαι is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means "to reign" or "to rule". It is derived from the noun βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler".

Grammar: βασιλεύσαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. This means that it is a command directed towards one person to perform an action in the past. In this case, the action is to reign or rule.

Description: βασιλεύσαι is a verb that is often used in the context of ancient Greek history and mythology. It is commonly used to describe the actions of kings and rulers, and is often associated with power and authority. In modern Greek, it is still used to describe the act of ruling or reigning, but is less commonly used in everyday language.
