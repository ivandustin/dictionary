Word: βαλλομεν

Etymology:
βαλλομεν is a present active participle of the Greek verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". The word has its roots in the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷʰel- which means "to throw, to hit".

Grammar:
βαλλομεν is a present active participle in the Greek language. It is declined in the present tense, and it is used to describe an ongoing action. The word is in the first person plural, which means "we are throwing" or "we are casting".

Description:
βαλλομεν is a Greek verb that describes the act of throwing or casting. It is often used in the context of sports or warfare, where throwing objects such as javelins or stones is a common practice.
