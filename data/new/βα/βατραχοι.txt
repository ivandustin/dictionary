Word: βατραχοι

Etymology:
The word βατραχοι (batrachoi) is derived from the Greek word βάτραχος (batrachos), which means "frog". The suffix -οι (-oi) is a plural ending in Greek.

Grammar:
βατραχοι (batrachoi) is a plural noun in Greek. It is the plural form of βάτραχος (batrachos), which is a masculine noun.

Description:
βατραχοι (batrachoi) refers to a group of frogs. In Greek mythology, frogs were associated with the god Dionysus and were believed to have healing powers. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to frogs.
