Word: βαρναβαν

Etymology: The word βαρναβαν is a Greek name that is derived from the Aramaic word "bar-nabba" which means "son of encouragement" or "son of consolation". In the New Testament, Barnabas was a companion of the apostle Paul.

Grammar: βαρναβαν is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: βαρναβαν is a proper noun that refers to a person. As mentioned earlier, it is the Greek name for Barnabas, a companion of the apostle Paul in the New Testament. Barnabas was known for his encouragement and support of Paul, and for his missionary work in spreading the Christian faith.
