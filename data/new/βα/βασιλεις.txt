Word: βασιλεις

Etymology: βασιλεις is a Greek word that comes from the root word βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". In ancient Greece, βασιλεις referred to the kings of the various city-states.

Grammar: βασιλεις is a plural noun in Greek, meaning "kings". It is declined in the second declension, and its nominative singular form is βασιλεύς (basileus).

Description: βασιλεις is a term used to refer to kings or rulers in ancient Greece. It was a title of great importance and was often associated with power, wealth, and prestige. In Greek mythology, many of the gods were also referred to as βασιλεις, such as Zeus, who was known as the king of the gods. The term is still used in modern Greek to refer to kings or monarchs.
