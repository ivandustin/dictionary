Word: βασιλειαν

Grammar: βασιλειαν is a noun in the accusative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: βασιλειαν refers to a kingdom or reign, typically ruled by a king or queen. In ancient Greece, βασιλειαν was used to refer to the kingdom of the gods, as well as the kingdoms of mortal rulers. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a kingdom or monarchy.
