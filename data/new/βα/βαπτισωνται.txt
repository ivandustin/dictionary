Word: βαπτισωνται

Etymology: βαπτισωνται is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βαπτίζω (baptizo), which means "to dip, immerse, or wash." In the New Testament, it is often used in reference to the Christian rite of baptism.

Grammar: βαπτισωνται is a third-person plural present middle/passive indicative form of the verb βαπτίζω. This form indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves (middle voice) or receiving the action (passive voice) in the present tense.

Description: βαπτισωνται is a Greek verb that means "they are baptizing themselves" or "they are being baptized." It is often used in the context of Christian baptism, which involves the immersion or sprinkling of water as a symbol of purification and initiation into the faith. The middle/passive voice of the verb suggests that the act of baptism is both a personal choice and a communal experience.
