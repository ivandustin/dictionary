Word: βαπτισθεις

Etymology: βαπτισθεις is a Greek word that comes from the verb βαπτίζω (baptizō), which means "to dip, immerse, or wash." In Christian theology, it refers to the act of baptism, which symbolizes the washing away of sins and the rebirth of the baptized person.

Grammar: βαπτισθεις is the aorist passive participle of βαπτίζω (baptizō). In Greek, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The participle form is used to modify a noun or pronoun, and in this case, it is used to describe someone who has been baptized.

Description: βαπτισθεις is a word that describes someone who has undergone the Christian rite of baptism. It is often used in the New Testament to refer to individuals who have been baptized by John the Baptist or by Jesus' disciples. The act of baptism is seen as a symbol of spiritual cleansing and rebirth, and it is considered an important step in the Christian faith.
