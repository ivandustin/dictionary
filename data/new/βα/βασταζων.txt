Word: βασταζων

Etymology: βαστάζων is the present participle of the Greek verb βαστάζω (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰer- meaning "to carry, bear".

Grammar: βασταζων is a masculine noun in the nominative case, singular form. It is the present participle of the verb βαστάζω (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear". In Greek, present participles are often used as adjectives to describe a noun or pronoun.

Description: βασταζων refers to someone who is carrying or bearing something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is enduring or putting up with something difficult. The word is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe Jesus carrying the cross.
