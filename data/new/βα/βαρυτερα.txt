Word: βαρυτερα

Etymology: βαρυτερα is a Greek word that comes from the root word βαρύς (barýs), meaning "heavy" or "weighty". The suffix -τερα (-tera) is a comparative suffix, meaning "more" or "greater". Therefore, βαρυτερα can be translated as "heavier" or "more weighty".

Grammar: βαρυτερα is the comparative form of the adjective βαρύς (barýs), which means "heavy". In Greek, adjectives have different forms depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. βαρυτερα is the feminine singular nominative form of the comparative adjective.

Description: βαρυτερα is used to compare the weight or importance of two things. For example, if someone says "Αυτό το βιβλίο είναι βαρύ, αλλά το άλλο είναι βαρυτερο" (This book is heavy, but the other one is heavier), they are comparing the weight of two books. Similarly, if someone says "Η υγεία είναι βαρύτερη από τα χρήματα" (Health is more important than money), they are comparing the importance of health and money.
