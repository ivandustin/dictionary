Word: βαλλειν

Etymology:
βαλλειν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast." It is related to the English words "ballistic" and "ball."

Description:
βαλλειν means "to throw" or "to cast" in Greek. It is a common verb in Greek literature and is used in a variety of contexts, from describing the throwing of a spear or a stone to the casting of lots or votes.
