Word: βασιλεύειν

Etymology: βασιλεύειν is a Greek verb that comes from the noun βασιλεύς (basileus), meaning "king" or "ruler". The verb βασιλεύειν means "to rule" or "to reign".

Grammar: βασιλεύειν is a present active infinitive verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the present tense ending -ειν to the stem βασιλευ-. The verb is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Βασιλεύω (basileuo) - I rule
- Βασιλεύεις (basileueis) - You rule
- Βασιλεύει (basileuei) - He/she/it rules
- Βασιλεύομεν (basileuomen) - We rule
- Βασιλεύετε (basileuete) - You all rule
- Βασιλεύουσι(ν) (basileuousi(n)) - They rule

Description: βασιλεύειν is a powerful and important verb in Greek, as it refers to the act of ruling or reigning over others. It is often used in historical and political contexts, as well as in literature and mythology. The verb can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has a great deal of influence or power over others.
