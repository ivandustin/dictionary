Word: βασανισμόν

Etymology:
The word βασανισμόν (basanismón) comes from the Greek verb βασανίζω (basanízo), which means "to torture" or "to torment". The suffix -ισμός (-ismós) is used to form nouns from verbs, indicating the action or result of the verb.

Grammar:
βασανισμόν is a masculine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
βασανισμόν refers to the act of torturing or tormenting someone. It can also be used to describe a state of extreme suffering or distress. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe emotional or psychological pain. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the punishment of the wicked in hell.
