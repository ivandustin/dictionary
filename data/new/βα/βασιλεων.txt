Word: βασιλεων

Etymology: βασιλεων is a Greek word that comes from the root word βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". The suffix -ων (-on) is a masculine plural ending, so βασιλεων means "kings" or "rulers".

Grammar: βασιλεων is a noun in the masculine plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. βασιλεων is in the nominative case, which is used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: βασιλεων is a word that refers to multiple kings or rulers. In ancient Greece, there were many city-states that were ruled by different kings, so this word would have been used frequently. In the New Testament, βασιλεων is often used to refer to the kings of the earth or the rulers of the nations.
