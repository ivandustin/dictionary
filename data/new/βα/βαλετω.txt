Word: βαλετω

Etymology:
βαλετω (baletō) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". 

Grammar:
βαλετω is a present active imperative verb in Greek. It is the second person singular form of the verb, which means that it is used when addressing one person directly and giving them a command. 

Description:
βαλετω is a command that means "throw" or "cast". It is often used in the context of throwing or casting something away or towards someone or something. It can also be used figuratively to mean "to launch" or "to initiate".
