Word: βασταζοντος

Etymology: βαστάζω (bastazo) is a Greek verb meaning "to carry" or "to bear". The suffix -οντος (-ontos) is the present participle form, indicating that the word refers to someone or something that is currently carrying or bearing something.

Grammar: βασταζοντος is the present participle form of the verb βαστάζω (bastazo) in the genitive case. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between nouns. In this case, βασταζοντος indicates that the noun it modifies possesses the quality of carrying or bearing.

Description: βασταζοντος can be translated to English as "of the one carrying" or "of the one bearing". It is often used in Greek literature and scripture to describe someone who is carrying a burden or responsibility. For example, in the New Testament, Jesus is described as the one who carries the sins of the world (βασταζων την αμαρτιαν του κοσμου).
