Word: βασιλευετω

Etymology: βασιλευετω is a Greek verb that comes from the root word βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". The verb form means "let him/her/it rule" or "let him/her/it be king".

Grammar: βασιλευετω is a third person singular present active imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction, specifically to tell someone to let someone or something rule or be king.

Description: βασιλευετω is a powerful and commanding word that is often used in religious or political contexts. It is a call to action, urging someone to allow a particular person or entity to take charge and lead. In ancient Greece, it was often used in reference to the ruling class or the gods. Today, it can be used in a variety of contexts, from political speeches to religious ceremonies.
