Word: βασιλικης

Etymology: The word βασιλικης (basilikēs) is derived from the Greek word βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". βασιλικης is the feminine form of βασιλικός (basilikos), which means "royal" or "belonging to the king".

Grammar: βασιλικης is a Greek adjective in the feminine form. It is declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, if it modifies a feminine singular noun in the nominative case, it would be βασιλική (basilikē).

Description: βασιλικης can be translated as "royal" or "belonging to the king". It is often used to describe things that are associated with royalty or the monarchy, such as a royal palace or a royal decree. In the context of the Greek Orthodox Church, βασιλικης is used to describe a type of liturgical music that is sung during certain religious services.
