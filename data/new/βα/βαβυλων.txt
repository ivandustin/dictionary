Word: βαβυλων

Etymology:
The word βαβυλων (Babylon) comes from the Akkadian word "Bābili" which means "Gate of God". The Greeks adopted the name from the Semitic-speaking people of Mesopotamia, who founded the city of Babylon in the 3rd millennium BCE.

Grammar:
βαβυλων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative singular form. The word is masculine in gender.

Description:
βαβυλων refers to the ancient city of Babylon, which was located in Mesopotamia (present-day Iraq). It was one of the most important cities in the ancient world and was known for its impressive architecture, including the Hanging Gardens of Babylon. The city was also an important center of trade and commerce. Today, the ruins of Babylon are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
