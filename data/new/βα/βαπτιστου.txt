Word: βαπτιστου

Etymology: βαπτιστου is the genitive form of the Greek word βαπτιστής (baptistēs), which means "one who baptizes" or "baptizer". The word is derived from the verb βαπτίζω (baptizō), which means "to dip" or "to immerse".

Grammar: βαπτιστου is a Greek noun in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated into English using the preposition "of". So, βαπτιστου can be translated as "of the baptizer" or "of the one who baptizes".

Description: βαπτιστου is a specific form of the Greek word βαπτιστής (baptistēs), which refers to someone who performs baptisms. In the New Testament, John the Baptist is referred to as ὁ βαπτιστής (ho baptistēs) or "the baptizer". The word βαπτιστής is also used to refer to Jesus Christ, who was baptized by John.
