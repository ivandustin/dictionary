Word: βαλετε

Etymology: βαλετε is a conjugated form of the Greek verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast".

Grammar: βαλετε is the second person plural imperative form of βάλλω (ballō), which is used to give a command or order to a group of people. It is translated as "throw" or "cast" in English.

Description: βαλετε is a verb form that instructs a group of people to throw or cast something. It is commonly used in sports, such as basketball or volleyball, where players are instructed to throw or pass the ball to their teammates. It can also be used in other contexts, such as giving directions to a group of people to throw away trash or to cast a fishing line.
