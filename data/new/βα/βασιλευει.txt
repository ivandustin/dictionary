Word: βασιλεύει (basileuei)

Etymology: βασιλεύει comes from the Greek verb βασιλεύω (basileuo), which means "to reign" or "to rule". It is derived from the noun βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler".

Grammar: βασιλεύει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb βασιλεύω (basileuo) and means "he/she/it reigns" or "he/she/it rules".

Description: βασιλεύει is a common Greek verb used to describe the act of ruling or reigning. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a king or queen who is currently in power, or referring to a person who is in charge of a particular organization or group. The verb is often used in political or historical contexts, but can also be used in everyday conversation.
