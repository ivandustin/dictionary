Word: βαπτιστην

Etymology:
The word βαπτιστην (baptisten) is a Greek word that comes from the verb βαπτίζω (baptizo), which means "to dip, to immerse, to baptize." The suffix -της (-tes) is added to the verb to form a noun that means "one who baptizes."

Grammar:
βαπτιστην is a masculine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. 

Description:
βαπτιστην refers to a person who baptizes others. In Christian tradition, a baptist is someone who performs the sacrament of baptism, which involves immersing a person in water or pouring water over their head as a symbol of purification and initiation into the Christian faith. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to anyone who practices baptism, regardless of their religious affiliation.
