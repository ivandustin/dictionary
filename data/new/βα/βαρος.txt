Word: βαρος

Etymology:
βαρος (baros) is a Greek word that means "weight" or "burden". It is derived from the Greek word βαρύς (varus), which means "heavy".

Grammar:
βαρος is a noun in the Greek language. It is a neuter noun, which means that it does not have a gender. In the singular form, it is written as βάρος (varos) and in the plural form, it is written as βάρη (vari).

Description:
βαρος is a term used to describe the amount of weight or mass that an object has. It is commonly used in physics and engineering to measure the force of gravity on an object. In everyday language, it can also be used to describe a heavy burden or responsibility that someone has to bear.
