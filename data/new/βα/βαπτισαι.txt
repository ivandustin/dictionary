Word: βαπτίσαι

Etymology:
The word βαπτίσαι comes from the Greek verb βαπτίζω (baptizō), which means "to dip, to immerse, to wash". It is derived from the root word βάπτω (baptō), which means "to dip, to dye". The word βαπτίζω was commonly used in ancient Greece to refer to the act of immersing or dipping something in water or other liquids.

βαπτίσαι is the second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb βαπτίζω (baptizō). In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.
