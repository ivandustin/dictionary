Word: βαρβαροις

Etymology: The word βαρβαροις comes from the Greek word βάρβαρος (barbaros), which means "foreign" or "strange". In ancient Greece, the term was used to refer to people who did not speak Greek and were considered uncivilized.

Grammar: βαρβαροις is a noun in the dative plural form. It is often used in Greek literature to refer to foreign or barbarian people.

Description: βαρβαροις is a term used in ancient Greek literature to describe foreign or barbarian people. It was often used to distinguish between Greeks and non-Greeks. The term has also been used in modern times to describe people who are perceived as uncivilized or primitive.
