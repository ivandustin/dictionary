Word: βαρναβαι

Etymology:
The word βαρναβαι is a plural form of the Greek word βαρνάβας (barnabas), which means "son of encouragement" or "son of consolation". The name is derived from the Aramaic word בר נחמה (bar nakhama), which has a similar meaning.

Grammar:
βαρναβαι is a plural noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence or for a predicate nominative that renames the subject.

Description:
βαρναβαι is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in Acts 11:22-30 and Acts 13:1. In these passages, it refers to a group of people who were sent by the church in Jerusalem to Antioch to encourage and strengthen the new believers there. Among the group was Barnabas, who is described as a "good man, full of the Holy Spirit and of faith" (Acts 11:24). Barnabas later became a prominent figure in the early Christian church, working closely with the apostle Paul on several missionary journeys.
