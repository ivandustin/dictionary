Word: βαθος

Etymology:
βαθος (bathos) is a Greek word that means "depth" or "profoundness". It is derived from the Greek word βαθύς (bathys), which means "deep".

Grammar:
βαθος is a noun in the Greek language. It is a masculine noun and is in the nominative case. In the plural form, it becomes βάθη (bathi).

Description:
βαθος is a word that is often used to describe the depth of something, such as water or a hole. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the depth of a person's emotions or thoughts. In Greek mythology, the god of the sea, Poseidon, was often referred to as "βαθύς θαλάσσης" (bathys thalassis), which means "the deep sea".
