The word βαλλοντων is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb βάλλω (ballō), which means "to throw" or "to cast". The present participle form indicates ongoing action, so βαλλοντων would translate to "those who are throwing" or "those who are casting". 

In terms of grammar, βαλλοντων is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship between nouns. It can be translated as "of those who are throwing" or "of those who are casting". 

Overall, βαλλοντων is a specific form of the verb βάλλω that describes ongoing action and possession or relationship.
