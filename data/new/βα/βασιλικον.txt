Word: βασιλικον

Etymology: 
The word βασιλικον (basilikón) is derived from the Greek word βασιλεύς (basileús), which means "king" or "ruler". βασιλικον is the neuter form of the adjective βασιλικός (basilikós), which means "royal" or "belonging to a king".

Grammar:
βασιλικον is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, and vocative.

Description:
βασιλικον can be translated to English as "royal" or "imperial". It is often used to describe things that are associated with royalty or the ruling class, such as palaces, crowns, and thrones. In modern Greek, the word is also used to describe a type of cheese that is made from sheep's milk.
