Word: βασιλεια

Etymology: βασιλεια comes from the Greek word βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". βασιλεια is the feminine form of βασιλεύς.

Grammar: βασιλεια is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case, which is the case used for the subject of a sentence.

Description: βασιλεια is a Greek word that means "kingdom" or "realm". It can refer to a physical kingdom or a metaphorical one, such as a realm of ideas or a domain of influence. In ancient Greece, βασιλεια was often used to refer to the kingdom of the gods. In the New Testament, it is used to refer to the kingdom of God.
