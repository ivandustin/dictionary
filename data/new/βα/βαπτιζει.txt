Word: βαπτίζει (baptizei)

Etymology: The word βαπτίζει comes from the Greek verb βαπτίζω (baptizo), which means "to dip" or "to immerse". It is derived from the noun βάπτω (bapto), which means "to dip" or "to dye".

Grammar: βαπτίζει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb βαπτίζω (baptizo), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: βαπτίζει means "he/she/it baptizes" in English. It is commonly used in the context of Christian baptism, where a person is immersed in water as a symbol of their faith. The word can also be used in a more general sense to mean "to immerse" or "to dip".
