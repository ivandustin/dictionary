Word: βαπτισμοις

Etymology:
The word βαπτισμοις comes from the Greek word βάπτισμα (baptisma), which means "immersion" or "baptism". The suffix -οις (-ois) is a plural ending in the dative case, indicating that the word is being used to refer to something that is being done to or for someone.

Grammar:
βαπτισμοις is a noun in the plural dative case in Greek. In English, it would be translated as "baptisms" or "immersions". The word can be used to refer to the act of baptizing someone, or to the ritual of baptism itself.

Description:
βαπτισμοις is a word that is closely associated with the Christian religion, particularly with the practice of baptism. In Christianity, baptism is a sacrament that involves the immersion of a person in water, symbolizing their spiritual rebirth and cleansing from sin. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of immersion or submersion, such as in a swimming pool or bathtub.
