Word: βαστασαι

Etymology:
The word βαστασαι (bastasai) is a Greek verb in the present tense, second person singular form. It comes from the root word βαστάζω (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear." The verb is derived from the noun βάσταγμα (bastagma), which means "burden" or "load."

Grammar:
βαστασαι is a present tense, second person singular form of the verb βαστάζω (bastazo). In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, mood, voice, and person. The present tense is used to describe actions that are happening now or are ongoing. The second person singular form is used when addressing one person directly.

Description:
βαστασαι is a command form of the verb βαστάζω (bastazo), which means "to carry" or "to bear." The command form is used to give an order or instruction to someone. In this case, the command is directed at one person and is telling them to carry or bear something. The exact context of the command would depend on the situation in which it is used.
