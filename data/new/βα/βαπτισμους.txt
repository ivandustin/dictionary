Word: βαπτισμούς

Etymology: The word βαπτισμούς comes from the Greek verb βαπτίζω (baptizō), which means "to dip, to immerse, to baptize." The suffix -μός (-mos) is a common Greek suffix that forms abstract nouns.

Grammar: βαπτισμούς is a plural noun in the genitive case. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns.

Description: βαπτισμούς refers to the act of baptizing or the rite of baptism in the Christian faith. It is a sacrament that involves the immersion or sprinkling of water on a person's head as a symbol of purification and initiation into the Christian community. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any kind of ritual washing or purification.
