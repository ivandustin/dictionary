Word: βασιλικην

Etymology: βασιλικην is a Greek word that comes from the root word βασιλεύς (basileus), which means "king" or "ruler". βασιλικην is the accusative singular form of the adjective βασιλικός (basilikos), which means "royal" or "belonging to a king".

Grammar: βασιλικην is an adjective in the accusative singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: βασιλικην can be translated as "royal" or "belonging to a king". It is often used to describe things that are associated with royalty or have a regal quality. For example, βασιλικην τέχνη (basilikin techni) means "royal art" or "art fit for a king".
