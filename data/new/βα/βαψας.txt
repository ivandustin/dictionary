Word: βαψας

Etymology: βαψας is a Greek word that comes from the verb βάπτω (bápto), which means "to dip" or "to immerse". 

Grammar: βαψας is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and participle mood. It is the masculine singular form. 

Description: βαψας can be translated as "having dipped" or "having immersed". It is often used in the context of baptism or dyeing fabrics. In the New Testament, it is used to describe the baptism of Jesus by John the Baptist.
