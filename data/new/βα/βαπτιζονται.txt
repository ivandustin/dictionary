Word: βαπτίζονται

Etymology: βαπτίζονται is a Greek verb that comes from the word βάπτω (báptō), which means "to dip" or "to immerse". In Christian theology, the word is often associated with the act of baptism, which involves the immersion of a person in water.

Grammar: βαπτίζονται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is the third person plural form of the verb βαπτίζω (baptízō), which means "to baptize" or "to immerse in water".

Description: βαπτίζονται is a verb that describes the act of being baptized or immersed in water. It is often used in the context of Christian baptism, but can also be used more generally to describe any act of immersion or submersion in water. The passive voice form of the verb emphasizes that the action is being done to the subject, rather than the subject doing the action.
