Word: βασιλευοντων

Etymology: βασιλευοντων is a present participle form of the Greek verb βασιλεύω (basileúō), which means "to reign" or "to rule". The word comes from the noun βασιλεύς (basileús), which means "king" or "ruler".

Grammar: βασιλευοντων is a present participle form of the verb βασιλεύω (basileúō), which is in the present tense and active voice. The word is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun.

Description: βασιλευοντων can be translated as "of those who are reigning" or "of those who are ruling". It is often used in historical or political contexts to refer to the rulers or kings of a particular time period or region.
