Word: βαρεις

Etymology: βαρεις comes from the Greek verb βαρύνω (varyno), which means "to make heavy" or "to weigh down". 

Grammar: βαρεις is the plural form of the adjective βαρύς (varis), which means "heavy". It is used to describe objects or people that have a significant weight or mass. 

Description: βαρεις is an adjective that is commonly used in Greek to describe heavy objects or people. For example, one might use βαρεις to describe a large piece of furniture or a person who is physically heavy. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is emotionally or mentally burdensome.
