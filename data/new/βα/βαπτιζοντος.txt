Word: βαπτιζοντος

Etymology: βαπτιζοντος is the present participle form of the Greek verb βαπτίζω (baptizō), which means "to dip, immerse, or wash." The word has its roots in the Greek word βάπτω (baptō), which means "to dip or immerse."

Grammar: βαπτιζοντος is a present participle in the genitive case, which means it is used to describe the action of baptizing in a possessive or attributive sense. In this form, it can function as an adjective modifying a noun or pronoun.

Description: βαπτιζοντος is a word that is commonly used in Christian theology to refer to the act of baptism, which is the ritual of immersing a person in water as a symbol of their spiritual cleansing and rebirth. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the baptism of Jesus by John the Baptist, as well as the baptism of new converts to Christianity.
