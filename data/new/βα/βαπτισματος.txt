Word: βαπτισματος

Etymology:
The word βαπτισματος comes from the Greek word βάπτισμα (baptisma), which means "immersion" or "baptism". The suffix -ματος (-matos) is a genitive singular ending, indicating possession or association.

Grammar:
βαπτισματος is a noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or association. It is the genitive singular form of βάπτισμα (baptisma).

Description:
βαπτισματος refers to something that is related to or associated with baptism. It can be used to describe the act of baptism itself, or the various elements and rituals associated with the practice of baptism in the Christian faith. In a broader sense, it can also be used to describe any kind of initiation or purification ritual.
