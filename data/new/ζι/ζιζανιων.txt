Ζιζάνια (ζιζανιών in genitive case) is a Greek word that refers to weeds or wild plants that grow among crops and are considered undesirable. The word comes from the Greek verb ζιζάνιζειν, which means to weed out or to remove weeds. In terms of grammar, ζιζάνια is a plural noun in the genitive case, indicating possession or relationship to something else.
