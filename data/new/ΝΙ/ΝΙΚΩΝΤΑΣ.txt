Etymology: The word "νικώντας" comes from the Greek verb "νικάω" (nikao), which means "to conquer" or "to overcome". It is derived from the noun "νίκη" (nike), meaning "victory".

Grammar: "Νικώντας" is the present participle form of the verb "νικάω" (nikao) in Greek. It is used to indicate an ongoing action of conquering or overcoming in the present tense. In English, it can be translated as "conquering" or "overcoming".

Description: "Νικώντας" is a word that describes the act of conquering or overcoming. It implies a sense of victory or success in overcoming obstacles or challenges. It can be used in various contexts, such as in sports, personal achievements, or overcoming difficulties in life.
