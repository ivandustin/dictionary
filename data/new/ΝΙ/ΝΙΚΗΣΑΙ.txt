Etymology: The word "νικησαί" comes from the Greek verb "νικάω" (nikao), which means "to conquer" or "to overcome". It is derived from the noun "νίκη" (nike), meaning "victory".

Grammar: "νικησαί" is the aorist tense, active voice, infinitive form of the verb "νικάω" (nikao). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The infinitive form is the base form of the verb that does not indicate tense, mood, or person.

Description: "νικησαί" is used to express the idea of conquering or overcoming something. It can be used in various contexts, such as winning a battle, defeating an opponent, or overcoming a challenge.
