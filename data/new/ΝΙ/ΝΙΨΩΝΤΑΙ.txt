Etymology: The word "νιψωνται" comes from the Greek verb "νίπτω" (nipto), which means "to wash". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nei̯- meaning "to wash" or "to clean".

Grammar: "Νιψωνται" is the third person plural form of the verb "νίπτω" (nipto) in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. In Greek grammar, the middle voice indicates that the subject is performing the action on themselves or for their own benefit.

Description: "Νιψωνται" is a Greek verb that means "they wash" or "they are washing". It is used to describe the action of multiple people washing themselves or something else.
