Etymology: The word "νικος" (nikos) comes from the Greek language and is derived from the Greek goddess Nike, who was the personification of victory.

Grammar: In Greek, "νικος" (nikos) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "νικου" (nikou).

Description: "Νικος" (nikos) means "victory" in Greek. It is often used to refer to a triumph or success in various contexts, such as sports, battles, or personal achievements. The concept of victory is highly valued in Greek culture and has been celebrated since ancient times.
