Etymology: The word "νιπτείν" (niptein) comes from the Ancient Greek verb "νίπτω" (nipto), which means "to wash". 

Grammar: "Νιπτείν" is the present tense infinitive form of the verb. In Greek, verbs are conjugated based on tense, mood, voice, and person. 

Description: "Νιπτείν" is a verb in the Greek language that means "to wash". It is commonly used to refer to the action of cleaning or cleansing something using water or other liquids.
