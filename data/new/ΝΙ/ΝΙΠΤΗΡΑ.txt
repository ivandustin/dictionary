Etymology: The word "νιπτήρα" comes from the Greek verb "νίπτω" (nipto), which means "to wash". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *nei̯- meaning "to wash" or "to clean".

Grammar: "Νιπτήρα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the masculine gender. In the plural form, it becomes "νιπτήρες" (nipteres).

Description: "Νιπτήρα" refers to a washbasin or a sink used for washing hands or face. It is commonly found in bathrooms or public restrooms.
