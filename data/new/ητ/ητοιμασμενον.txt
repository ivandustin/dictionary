Word: ητοιμασμενον

Etymology: ητοιμασμενον is a Greek word that comes from the verb "ετοιμάζω" (etoimazo), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready". The word is in the past participle form, which means "prepared" or "made ready".

Grammar: ητοιμασμενον is a masculine singular form of the past participle in Greek. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. In this form, it can be used as an adjective or a predicate.

Description: ητοιμασμενον is a word that describes something that has been prepared or made ready. It can be used to describe a person, an object, or a situation that is ready for a particular purpose. For example, "Το φαγητό είναι ητοιμασμένο" (To fagito einai etoimasméno) means "The food is ready".
