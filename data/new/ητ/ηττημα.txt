Word: ηττημα

Etymology: The word ηττημα (ittima) comes from the ancient Greek verb ητταομαι (ittaomai), which means "to be defeated" or "to suffer a loss". The noun form ηττημα (ittima) means "defeat" or "loss".

Grammar: ηττημα (ittima) is a neuter noun in Greek. It is declined like other neuter nouns, with the nominative singular form being ηττημα (ittima) and the genitive singular form being ηττηματος (ittimatos).

Description: ηττημα (ittima) refers to a defeat or loss, usually in a competition or battle. It can also refer to a setback or failure in general. The word is commonly used in sports, politics, and business contexts.
