Word: ψευσασθαι

Etymology:
The word ψευσασθαι comes from the Greek verb ψεύδομαι (pseudomai), which means "to lie" or "to deceive". The verb ψευσασθαι is the aorist middle infinitive form of ψεύδομαι.

Grammar:
ψευσασθαι is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist middle infinitive form, which means that it is in the past tense and the middle voice. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action.

Description:
ψευσασθαι means "to deceive oneself" or "to lie to oneself". It is a reflexive verb, which means that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The verb is often used in a negative context, indicating that the subject is not being truthful with themselves. It can also be used in a positive context, indicating that the subject is being honest with themselves and acknowledging their faults or mistakes.
