Word: ψευστην

Etymology:
The word ψευστην (pseustēn) is derived from the Greek verb ψεύδομαι (pseudomai), which means "to lie" or "to deceive". The suffix -την (-tēn) is added to the verb to form a noun, indicating a person who lies or deceives.

Grammar:
ψευστην is a masculine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. For example, in the sentence "Ο άντρας είπε ψέματα" (O ántras eípe psémata), meaning "The man told lies", ψέματα (psémata) is in the accusative case.

Description:
ψευστην refers to a person who lies or deceives. It can be used to describe someone who intentionally misleads others or gives false information. The word has a negative connotation and is often used to criticize someone's behavior. In Greek literature, ψευστην is used to describe characters who are deceitful or dishonest.
