Word: ψευδονται

Etymology:
The word ψευδονται is derived from the Greek word ψεύδομαι (pseudomai), which means "to lie" or "to deceive". The suffix -νται (-ntai) is a passive voice ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is being acted upon.

Grammar:
ψευδονται is a third-person plural present passive indicative form of the verb ψεύδομαι (pseudomai). In this form, the subject is being lied to or deceived by someone or something else. 

Description:
ψευδονται is a Greek verb that means "they are being deceived" or "they are being lied to". It is often used in the context of someone being misled or tricked by another person or entity. The word can be used in a variety of situations, from personal relationships to politics and business.
