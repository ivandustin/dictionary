Word: ψευδοδιδάσκαλοι

Etymology:
The word ψευδοδιδάσκαλοι is a compound word in Greek, consisting of two parts: ψευδο- (pseud-) meaning "false" or "lying" and διδάσκαλοι (didaskaloi) meaning "teachers". Therefore, the word ψευδοδιδάσκαλοι means "false teachers" or "lying teachers".

ψευδοδιδάσκαλοι is a plural noun in Greek. The word is declined as follows:

Genitive: ψευδοδιδασκάλων
Dative: ψευδοδιδασκάλοις

Description:
The word ψευδοδιδάσκαλοι is used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe false teachers who spread false teachings and lead people astray from the true teachings of Jesus Christ. The word is used in 2 Peter 2:1, where it says, "But there were also false prophets among the people, just as there will be false teachers among you.
