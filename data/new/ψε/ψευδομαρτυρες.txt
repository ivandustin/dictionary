Word: ψευδομαρτυρες

Etymology:
The word ψευδομαρτυρες is derived from two Greek words: ψευδο (pseudos) meaning false or lying, and μαρτυρες (martures) meaning witnesses. Therefore, the word ψευδομαρτυρες means false witnesses.

Grammar:
ψευδομαρτυρες is a noun in the plural form. In Greek, the word is spelled as ψευδομάρτυρες.

Description:
The word ψευδομαρτυρες is used to describe people who give false testimony or bear false witness. In legal contexts, it refers to individuals who provide untrue information under oath or affirmation. The term can also be used more broadly to describe anyone who lies or deceives others. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe false witnesses who testified against Jesus during his trial.
