Word: ψευδομεθα

Etymology:
The word ψευδομεθα is derived from the Greek word ψεύδομαι (pseudomai) which means "to lie" or "to deceive". The suffix -μεθα (-meta) is a reflexive suffix that indicates that the action is being performed on oneself.

Grammar:
ψευδομεθα is a first-person plural present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It is formed by combining the stem ψευδο- (psevdo-) with the reflexive suffix -μεθα (-meta). The verb ψευδομεθα means "we are deceiving ourselves" or "we are lying to ourselves".

Description:
The word ψευδομεθα describes the act of deceiving oneself or lying to oneself. It implies a deliberate act of self-deception or self-delusion. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as psychology, philosophy, or everyday conversation, to describe situations where individuals are not being honest with themselves or are avoiding facing the truth.
