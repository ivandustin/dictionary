Word: ψευδος

Etymology:
The word ψευδος (pseudos) is a Greek word that means "falsehood" or "lie". It is derived from the Greek verb ψεύδομαι (pseúdomai), which means "to lie" or "to deceive".

Grammar:
In Greek, ψευδος is a neuter noun. Its nominative singular form is ψευδος, and its genitive singular form is ψευδους. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as the accusative, dative, and vocative.

Description:
ψευδος is a word that refers to something that is not true or is intentionally misleading. It can be used to describe a false statement, a deceptive action, or a misleading appearance. The word is often used in philosophical and ethical discussions to describe the nature of truth and falsehood.
