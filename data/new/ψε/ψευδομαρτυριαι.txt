Word: ψευδομαρτυρίαι (pseudomartyriai)

Etymology: The word ψευδομαρτυρίαι comes from the Greek words ψευδής (pseudēs) meaning false and μαρτυρία (martyria) meaning testimony. Therefore, the word ψευδομαρτυρίαι means false testimony.

Grammar: ψευδομαρτυρίαι is a plural noun in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -αι to the stem ψευδομαρτυρία. In English, it can be translated as "false testimonies".

Description: ψευδομαρτυρίαι refers to false or untrue statements made under oath or in a legal setting. It is a term commonly used in legal contexts to describe perjury or false testimony. The word can also be used more broadly to refer to any false or misleading statements made with the intention of deceiving others.
