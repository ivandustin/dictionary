Word: ψευδομαι

Etymology:
The word ψευδομαι (pseudomai) is derived from the Greek word ψευδής (pseudēs), which means false or deceitful. The word ψευδομαι is a verb that means to lie or to deceive.

Grammar:
ψευδομαι is a first-person singular present middle/passive indicative verb in Greek. It belongs to the second conjugation of Greek verbs, which means that it has a stem that ends in -δο. The verb is conjugated as follows:

Present Indicative:
ψεύδομαι (I lie/deceive)
ψεύδῃ (you lie/deceive)
ψεύδεται (he/she/it lies/deceives)
ψευδόμεθα (we lie/deceive)
ψεύδεσθε (you all lie/deceive)
ψεύδονται (they lie/deceive)

Description:
ψευδομαι is a Greek verb that is commonly used to describe the act of lying or deceiving. It is a strong word that conveys a sense of intentional deceit or falsehood. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from everyday conversations to more formal settings.
