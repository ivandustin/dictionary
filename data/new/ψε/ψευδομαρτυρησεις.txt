Word: ψευδομαρτυρησεις

Etymology:
The word ψευδομαρτυρησεις is derived from two Greek words: ψευδο (pseudos) meaning false or lying, and μαρτυρία (martyria) meaning testimony or witness. Therefore, the word ψευδομαρτυρησεις means false testimony or false witness.

Grammar:
ψευδομαρτυρησεις is a plural noun in Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -εις to the stem ψευδομαρτυρ-. The word is declined according to the second declension in Greek.

Description:
ψευδομαρτυρησεις refers to false testimony or false witness. It is often used in legal contexts to describe someone who gives false testimony in court. The word can also be used more broadly to describe any situation where someone is lying or giving false information.
