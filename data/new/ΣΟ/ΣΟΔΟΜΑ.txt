Etymology: The word "σοδομα" (sodoma) comes from the Greek Σόδομα, which in turn is derived from the Hebrew סְדוֹם (Sedom). It is believed to be the name of a city mentioned in the Bible.

Grammar: "Σοδομα" is a noun in the Greek language. It is a feminine noun and belongs to the second declension. In the nominative case, it is spelled as "Σοδομα".

Description: "Σοδομα" refers to the biblical city of Sodom, which is mentioned in the Old Testament. It is known for its wickedness and was ultimately destroyed by God. The story of Sodom and its destruction is often used as a cautionary tale.
