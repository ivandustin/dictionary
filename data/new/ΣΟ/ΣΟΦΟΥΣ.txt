Etymology: The word "σοφούς" (sophous) comes from the Greek adjective "σοφός" (sophos), which means "wise" or "clever."

Grammar: "Σοφούς" is the masculine accusative plural form of the adjective "σοφός" (sophos). In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: "Σοφούς" is used to describe multiple wise or clever individuals in the masculine gender. It is commonly used in Greek literature and philosophy to refer to wise men or philosophers.
