Etymology: The word "σος" (sos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *swésōr, meaning "self" or "one's own".

Grammar: In Greek, "σος" (sos) is a pronoun that means "your" or "yours". It is the second person singular possessive pronoun in the nominative case.

Description: "Σος" (sos) is used to indicate possession or ownership. It is used when referring to something that belongs to the person being addressed. For example, "Πού είναι το βιβλίο σου;" (Pou einai to vivlio sou?) means "Where is your book?"
