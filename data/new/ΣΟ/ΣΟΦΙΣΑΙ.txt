Etymology: The word "σοφίσαι" (sophísai) comes from the Greek verb "σοφίζω" (sophízo), which means "to make wise" or "to teach wisdom." It is derived from the adjective "σοφός" (sophós), meaning "wise" or "skilled."

Grammar: "Σοφίσαι" is the aorist active infinitive form of the verb "σοφίζω" in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb performs the action. The infinitive form expresses the verb's action without specifying the subject or tense.

Description: "Σοφίσαι" is used to express the action of making someone wise or teaching wisdom. It can also imply the act of imparting knowledge or expertise in a particular subject.
