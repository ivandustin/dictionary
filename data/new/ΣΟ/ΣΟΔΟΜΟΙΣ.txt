Etymology: The word "σοδομοις" (sodomos) comes from the Greek mythological city of Sodom, which was known for its wickedness and immorality.

Grammar: "Σοδομοις" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and belongs to the second declension.

Description: "Σοδομοις" refers to the people of Sodom, the inhabitants of the city mentioned in the Bible and various ancient texts. The city of Sodom is infamous for its sinful behavior, which ultimately led to its destruction. The term is often used metaphorically to describe a place or group of people characterized by immorality or depravity.
