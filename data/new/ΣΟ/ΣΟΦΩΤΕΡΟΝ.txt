Etymology: The word "σοφωτερον" (sophōteron) comes from the Greek adjective "σοφός" (sophos), meaning "wise" or "clever," and the comparative suffix "-τερον" (-teron), indicating "more" or "comparatively." 

Grammar: "Σοφωτερον" is the neuter form of the comparative degree of the adjective "σοφός." In Greek, adjectives change their form based on gender, number, and case. The neuter form is used when referring to things or concepts that are neither masculine nor feminine.

Description: "Σοφωτερον" means "wiser" or "more wise" in English. It is used to compare the level of wisdom or intelligence between two or more things or individuals.
