Etymology: The word "στασις" (stasis) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "ἵστημι" (histēmi), which means "to stand" or "to cause to stand."

Grammar: In Greek, "στασις" is a feminine noun of the third declension. It can be declined in various cases, numbers, and genders to indicate different grammatical functions in a sentence.

Description: "Στασις" has multiple meanings depending on the context. It can refer to a state of standing or being stationary, as well as a state of balance or equilibrium. In a broader sense, it can also mean a state of unrest, discord, or conflict, particularly in political or social contexts.
