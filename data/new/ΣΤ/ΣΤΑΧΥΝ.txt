Etymology: The word "σταχυν" (stachyn) comes from the Greek word "στάχυς" (stachys), meaning "ear of grain" or "stalk of wheat." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂- ("to stand").

Grammar: "Σταχυν" is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical role in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: "Σταχυν" refers to a single ear of grain or a stalk of wheat. It is commonly used in the context of agriculture, farming, or discussing the growth and harvesting of crops.
