Etymology: The word "σταυρώσαι" (stavrosai) comes from the Greek verb "σταυρόω" (stavroo), which means "to crucify." It is derived from the noun "σταυρός" (stavros), meaning "cross."

Grammar: "Σταυρώσαι" is the aorist tense, imperative mood, and second person singular form of the verb "σταυρόω" (stavroo). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to crucify.

Description: "Σταυρώσαι" is a Greek word that means "crucify" in English. It is a verb used to describe the act of nailing or affixing someone to a cross as a form of execution. This word is commonly associated with the crucifixion of Jesus Christ in Christian theology.
