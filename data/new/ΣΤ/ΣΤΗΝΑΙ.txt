Etymology: The word "στηναι" (stēnai) comes from the Greek verb "σταίνω" (stainō), which means "to stand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂-.

Grammar: "Στηναι" is a verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Εγώ στέκομαι (Egō stekomai) - I stand
- Εσύ στέκεσαι (Esý stékesai) - You stand
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό στέκεται (Aftós/Aftí/Aftó stéketai) - He/She/It stands
- Εμείς στεκόμαστε (Emeís stekómaste) - We stand
- Εσείς στέκεστε (Eseís stékeste) - You stand
- Αυτοί/Αυτές στέκονται (Aftoí/Aftés stékontai) - They stand

Description: "Στηναι" refers to the action of standing or being in an upright position. It can be used both literally and figuratively. In its literal sense, it describes the physical act of standing on one's feet. In a figurative sense, it can imply being firm, steadfast, or maintaining a certain position or belief.
