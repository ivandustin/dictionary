Etymology: The word "στομαχον" (stomachon) comes from the Greek word "στόμαχος" (stomachos), which means "stomach". 

Grammar: "Στομαχον" is a noun in the neuter gender. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. 

Description: "Στομαχον" refers to the stomach, which is an organ in the body responsible for digestion. It is located between the esophagus and the small intestine. The stomach plays a crucial role in breaking down food and absorbing nutrients.
