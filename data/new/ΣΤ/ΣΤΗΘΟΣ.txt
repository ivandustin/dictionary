Etymology: The word "στηθος" (stēthos) comes from the Ancient Greek word meaning "chest" or "breast".

Grammar: In Greek, "στηθος" (stēthos) is a noun of neuter gender. It is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: το στηθος (to stēthos) - the chest
- Genitive: του στηθους (tou stēthous) - of the chest
- Accusative: το στηθος (to stēthos) - the chest
- Vocative: στηθος (stēthos) - O chest!

Plural:
- Nominative: τα στηθη (ta stēthē) - the chests
- Genitive: των στηθων (tōn stēthōn) - of the chests
- Accusative: τα στηθη (ta stēthē) - the chests
- Vocative: στηθη (stēthē) - O chests!

Description: "Στηθος" (stēthos) refers to the chest or breast area of the human body. It can also be used metaphorically to represent the heart or the seat of emotions.
