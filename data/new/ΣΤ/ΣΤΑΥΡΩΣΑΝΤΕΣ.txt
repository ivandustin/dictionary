Etymology: The word "σταυρώσαντες" comes from the Greek verb "σταυρόω" (stavroo), which means "to crucify." It is derived from the noun "σταυρός" (stavros), meaning "cross."

Grammar: "Σταυρώσαντες" is the third person plural, aorist active participle of the verb "σταυρόω" (stavroo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or completion. The active participle form indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description: "Σταυρώσαντες" is a Greek word that translates to "having crucified" or "having nailed to the cross" in English. It is often used in reference to the crucifixion of Jesus Christ.
