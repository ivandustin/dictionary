Etymology: The word "στησαντες" comes from the Greek verb "στήνω" (stíno), which means "to stand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂-.

Grammar: "στησαντες" is the aorist participle form of the verb "στήνω" (stíno) in Greek. It is in the masculine plural nominative case. The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, and the participle form functions as an adjective.

Description: "στησαντες" describes a group of people who have stood or who stood in the past. It can be used to refer to a specific group of individuals or to a general group of people.
