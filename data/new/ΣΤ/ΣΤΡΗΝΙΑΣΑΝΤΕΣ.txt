Etymology: The word "στρηνιασάντες" comes from the Greek verb "στρηνιάω" (strēniáō), which means "to revel" or "to carouse". The suffix "-σάντες" is added to the verb stem to form the past participle, indicating that the action of reveling or carousing has been completed.

Grammar: "Στρηνιασάντες" is the past participle of the verb "στρηνιάω" in the masculine plural form. In Greek grammar, past participles are used to form compound tenses and passive voice constructions.

Description: "Στρηνιασάντες" describes a group of people who have engaged in revelry or carousing. It implies that they have actively participated in lively and often excessive celebrations or parties.
