Etymology: The word "σταχυας" (stachyas) comes from the Greek word "στάχυς" (stachys), meaning "ear of grain" or "spike". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂- ("to stand").

Grammar: In Greek, "σταχυας" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the nominative case and singular number.

Description: "Σταχυας" refers to an ear of grain or a spike, typically found on cereal plants such as wheat or barley. It is the part of the plant that contains the seeds and is harvested for food or used in agricultural processes.
