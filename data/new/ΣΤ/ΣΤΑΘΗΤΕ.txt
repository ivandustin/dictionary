Etymology: The word "σταθητε" (stathete) comes from the Greek verb "σταθείς" (stathis), which means "to stand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂-.

Grammar: "σταθητε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "σταθώ" (statho), which means "to stand". In Greek grammar, the imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description: "σταθητε" is a command in Greek, instructing a group of people to stand. It is used when addressing multiple individuals and is commonly used in situations where people need to rise from a seated or lying position.
