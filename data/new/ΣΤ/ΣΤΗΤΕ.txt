Etymology: The word "στητε" (stete) comes from the Greek verb "στέω" (steo), which means "to stand" or "to be firm". 

Grammar: "στητε" is the second person plural imperative form of the verb "στέω" (steo). It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. 

Description: "στητε" is a Greek word that is used to tell a group of people to stand or be firm. It is often used in situations where a group needs to be organized or prepared for something.
