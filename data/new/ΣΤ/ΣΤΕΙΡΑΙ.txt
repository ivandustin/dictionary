Etymology: The word "στείραι" (steirai) comes from the Greek verb "στείρω" (steiro), which means "to make barren" or "to render infertile." 

Grammar: "Στείραι" is the plural form of the noun "στείρα" (steira), which is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and is declined as follows:

Singular:
- Nominative: η στείρα (i steira) 
- Genitive: της στείρας (tis steiras)
- Accusative: τη(ν) στείρα (ti(n) steira)
- Vocative: στείρα (steira)

Plural:
- Nominative: οι στείραι (i steire)
- Genitive: των στειρών (ton steiron)
- Accusative: τις στείρες (tis steires)
- Vocative: στείρες (steires)

Description: "Στείραι" is a Greek noun that means "barren women" or "infertile women." It refers to women who are unable to conceive or bear children.
