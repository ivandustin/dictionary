Etymology: The word "στησει" (stesei) comes from the Greek verb "στήνω" (stíno), which means "to stand" or "to erect."

Grammar: "Στησει" (stesei) is the future tense, third person singular form of the verb "στήνω" (stíno). It is used to indicate an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "Στησει" (stesei) is a Greek word that means "he/she/it will stand" or "he/she/it will erect." It is commonly used to describe the act of physically standing or erecting something.
