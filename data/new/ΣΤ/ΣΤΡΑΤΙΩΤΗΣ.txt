Etymology: The word "στρατιωτης" (stratiotes) comes from the Greek word "στρατος" (stratos), meaning "army" or "military," combined with the suffix "-ωτης" (-otes), which denotes a person or agent. 

Grammar: "Στρατιωτης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence (nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.). 

Description: "Στρατιωτης" means "soldier" in Greek. It refers to a person who serves in the military or is a member of an army.
