Etymology: The word "στοματι" (stomati) comes from the Greek word "στόμα" (stoma), which means "mouth".

Grammar: "Στοματι" is a noun in the singular form. It is a neuter noun, and its grammatical gender does not change in different cases.

Description: "Στοματι" refers to the mouth, the opening through which food enters the body and words are spoken. It is an essential part of the human anatomy and plays a crucial role in various functions such as eating, drinking, breathing, and communication.
