Etymology: The word "στείρα" (stira) comes from the Greek word "στείρος" (steiros), meaning "barren" or "sterile". 

Grammar: In Greek, "στείρα" is a feminine noun in the singular form. 

Description: "Στείρα" is used to describe a female animal that is unable to reproduce or bear offspring. It is often used in the context of veterinary medicine or animal breeding.
