Etymology: The word "στένης" (stenēs) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the verb "στένω" (stenō), which means "to narrow" or "to constrict."

Grammar: In Greek, "στένης" is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It belongs to the second declension and has the genitive form "στένου" (stenou).

Description: "Στένης" is a Greek word that translates to "narrow" or "tight" in English. It is used to describe something that has a small width or limited space. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or circumstance that is restrictive or constricting.
