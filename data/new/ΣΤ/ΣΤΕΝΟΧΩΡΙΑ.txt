Etymology: The word "στενοχωρία" (stenochoria) comes from the Greek words "στενός" (stenos), meaning "narrow" or "tight," and "χώρα" (chora), meaning "space" or "place." It can be translated as "narrow space" or "tight place."

Grammar: "Στενοχωρία" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the second declension and has the plural form "στενοχωρίες" (stenochories).

Description: "Στενοχωρία" refers to a feeling of sadness, sorrow, or distress. It is often used to describe a state of being emotionally or mentally constricted, as if one is trapped in a tight or narrow space. It can be associated with feelings of discomfort, anxiety, or unease.
