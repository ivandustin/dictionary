Etymology: The word "στομα" (stoma) comes from the Ancient Greek word "στόμα" (stóma), which means "mouth".

Grammar: In Greek, "στομα" is a neuter noun. It belongs to the second declension and its nominative singular form is "το στομα" (to stoma).

Description: "Στομα" is the Greek word for "mouth". It refers to the opening through which food enters the body and through which sounds are produced for speech. The mouth is also involved in various functions such as tasting, chewing, and swallowing.
