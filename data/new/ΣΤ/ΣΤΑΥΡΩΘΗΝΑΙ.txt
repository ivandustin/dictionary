Grammar: "Σταυρωθείναι" is the aorist passive infinitive form of the verb "σταυρόω". In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on tense, voice, mood, and person. The aorist tense refers to a completed action in the past, and the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The infinitive form expresses the action of the verb without specifying the subject or tense.

Description: "Σταυρωθείναι" is a Greek word that means "to be crucified". It is often used in the context of the crucifixion of Jesus Christ, as described in the New Testament of the Bible.
