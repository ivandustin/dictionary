Etymology: The word "στησαί" (stesai) comes from the Greek verb "στήνω" (stíno), which means "to stand" or "to erect". 

Grammar: "Στησαί" is the second person singular imperative form of the verb "στήνω" (stíno). It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to stand or erect something.

Description: "Στησαί" is a Greek word that is used to command someone to stand or erect something. It is a direct and authoritative way of telling someone to take a standing position or to set up something in an upright position.
