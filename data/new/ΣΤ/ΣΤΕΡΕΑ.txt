Etymology: The word "στερεά" (sterea) comes from the Greek word "στερεός" (stereos), meaning "solid" or "firm".

Grammar: "Στερεά" is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the nominative case and can function as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "Στερεά" is used to refer to the mainland of Greece, as opposed to the islands. It is often used in geographical and political contexts to distinguish between the mainland and the surrounding islands.
