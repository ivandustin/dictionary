Etymology: The word "στοαί" (stoai) comes from the Greek word "στοά" (stoa), which means "colonnade" or "portico". It is derived from the verb "ἵστημι" (histēmi), meaning "to stand".

Grammar: "Στοαί" is the plural form of the noun "στοά" (stoa) in Greek. It is a feminine noun and belongs to the second declension.

Description: "Στοαί" refers to colonnades or porticoes, which are covered walkways supported by columns. In ancient Greece, stoas were commonly found in public spaces, such as marketplaces or temples, and served as gathering places or sheltered walkways.
