Etymology: The word "σταδίους" (stadios) comes from the Greek word "στάδιον" (stádion), which originally referred to a unit of length used in ancient Greece. Over time, the word evolved to also mean a racecourse or a stadium.

Grammar: "Σταδίους" is the plural form of the noun "στάδιον" (stádion) in Greek. It is a second declension noun, and its genitive form is "σταδίου" (stádiou).

Description: "Σταδίους" is a Greek word that can be translated as "stadiums" in English. It refers to large sports arenas or racecourses where athletic events, such as track and field competitions, take place. In modern usage, it can also refer to any large venue used for sporting events or concerts.
