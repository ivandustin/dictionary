Etymology: The word "στρεφειν" (stréphein) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *streb(h)-, meaning "to turn" or "to twist."

Grammar: In Greek, "στρεφειν" is an infinitive verb form. It belongs to the first conjugation class and is classified as a regular verb. It is commonly used in the active voice.

Description: "Στρεφειν" means "to turn" or "to rotate" in Greek. It is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts, such as describing physical movements, changing directions, or shifting positions.
