Word: μνημονευουσιν

Etymology: μνημονευουσιν is a present active participle of the Greek verb μνημονεύω (mnēmoneúō), which means "to remember" or "to bear in mind". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: μνήμη (mnēmē), meaning "memory", and εὖ (eu), meaning "well" or "good".

Grammar: μνημονευουσιν is a third-person plural present active participle in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the suffix -ουσιν (-ousin) to the present stem of the verb μνημονεύω (mnēmoneúō). The present active participle indicates that the action of remembering is ongoing and currently taking place.

Description: μνημονευουσιν is a Greek word that describes the act of remembering or bearing something in mind. It is often used in the context of historical or cultural memory, as well as personal memory. The word emphasizes the ongoing nature of memory and the importance of keeping things in mind.
