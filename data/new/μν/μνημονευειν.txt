Word: μνημονευειν

Etymology:
The word μνημονευειν comes from the Greek root words μνήμη (mnēmē), meaning "memory," and εὖ (eu), meaning "well" or "good." The word is a compound verb formed from the combination of the prefix μνημονε- (mnemone-), meaning "to remember," and the verb εἶναι (einai), meaning "to be."

Grammar:
μνημονευειν is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is a compound verb, as mentioned above, and is formed from the combination of the prefix μνημονε- and the verb εἶναι.

Description:
μνημονευειν means "to remember" or "to keep in mind" in Greek. It is often used in the context of remembering something important or significant, such as a person, event, or idea. The word is commonly used in both formal and informal settings, and is an important part of the Greek language and culture.
