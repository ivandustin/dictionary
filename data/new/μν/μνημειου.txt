Word: μνημείου

Etymology: μνημείου comes from the Greek word μνημεῖον (mnēmeîon), which means "a monument or memorial." It is derived from the word μνήμη (mnēmē), which means "memory."

Grammar: μνημείου is a noun in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. It is the genitive singular form of μνημεῖον.

Description: μνημείου refers to a monument or memorial, typically one that is erected in memory of a person or event. It can also refer to a tomb or grave marker. In modern Greek, it can also refer to a museum or exhibition space.
