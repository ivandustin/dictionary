Word: μνησθω

Etymology:
The word μνησθω (mnēsthō) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μνημονεύω (mnēmoneuō), which means "to remember." The verb μνησθω is in the aorist tense, which indicates a past action that is completed.

Grammar:
μνησθω is a second-person singular aorist middle imperative verb in Greek. The middle voice indicates that the subject is both the doer and the receiver of the action. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The verb μνησθω is used to command someone to remember something. It is often translated as "remember" or "be mindful of." In the New Testament, it is used in various contexts, such as to remember God's promises, to remember the teachings of Jesus, or to remember the poor. The imperative form of the verb is often used in prayers or exhortations.
