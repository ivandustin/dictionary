Word: μνημονευετε

Etymology: μνημονευετε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word μνημονεύω (mnēmoneúō), which means "to remember" or "to bear in mind". The verb form μνημονευετε is in the present tense and second person plural, meaning "you all remember" or "you all bear in mind".

Grammar: μνημονευετε is a present tense verb in the second person plural form. It is a regular verb in Greek, meaning that it follows the standard conjugation patterns for verbs in this tense and person. The verb stem is μνημονευ-, and the ending -ετε indicates the second person plural form.

Description: μνημονευετε is a command or imperative form of the verb μνημονεύω, which means "to remember" or "to bear in mind". It is often used in Greek texts as a call to action, urging the reader or listener to remember something important. In English, it can be translated as "remember" or "keep in mind".
