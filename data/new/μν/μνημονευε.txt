Word: μνημονευε

Etymology: The word μνημονευε comes from the Greek verb μνημονεύω (mnēmoneúō), which means "to remember" or "to bear in mind". It is derived from the words μνήμη (mnēmē), meaning "memory", and νεύω (neúō), meaning "to nod" or "to beckon".

Grammar: μνημονευε is the second person singular imperative form of the verb μνημονεύω. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to remember something.

Description: μνημονευε is a Greek word that means "remember". It is often used in the context of reminding someone of something important or significant. The word can be used in a variety of situations, such as reminding someone of a deadline, a promise, or an event. The imperative form of the word, μνημονευε, is often used in a commanding or urgent tone, emphasizing the importance of the thing being remembered.
