Word: μνησθητε

Etymology:
The word μνησθητε is a verb in the aorist tense, second person plural form of the Greek verb μιμνήσκω (mimnēskō), which means "to remember" or "to recall". The root of the word is μνη- (mnē-), which means "memory" or "remembrance".

Grammar:
μνησθητε is a second person plural form of the aorist tense in Greek. The aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is completed or viewed as a whole. The second person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people being addressed.

Description:
μνησθητε is a command form of the verb μιμνήσκω (mimnēskō), which means "to remember" or "to recall". The command form is used to give an order or instruction to someone. In this case, the speaker is instructing a group of people to remember or recall something. The context of the sentence would determine what it is that the speaker wants the group to remember.
