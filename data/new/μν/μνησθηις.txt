The word "μνησθηις" (mnēsthēis) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means "to remember" or "to recall". Its etymology comes from the Greek word "μνήμη" (mnēmē), which means "memory". In terms of grammar, it is a second-person singular aorist indicative active verb. 

The word "μνησθηις" is often used in Greek literature and mythology, such as in Homer's epic poems, to describe the act of remembering important events or people. It is also used in philosophical and religious contexts to refer to the importance of memory and remembrance in human life.
