Word: οξους

Etymology:
The word οξους (oxous) is derived from the Greek word οξύς (oxys), which means "sharp" or "acidic". It is related to the English word "oxy-" as in "oxygen" or "oxidize".

Grammar:
Οξους is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It is declined as follows: οξους (nominative), οξου (genitive), οξου (accusative), οξον (vocative).

Description:
Οξους is a term used in chemistry to refer to an acid or acidic substance. It is often used in the context of pH levels, where a substance with a pH less than 7 is considered acidic. In ancient Greek, the word οξύς was also used to describe things that were sharp or pointed, such as a sword or a thorn.
