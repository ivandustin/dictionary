Word: οξος

Etymology:
The word οξος (oxos) comes from the Ancient Greek ὀξύς (oxús), which means "sharp" or "sour". It is related to the word ὀξύτης (oxýtēs), which means "acidity".

Grammar:
In Greek, οξος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is οξος, and its genitive singular form is οξεος. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
Οξος is a Greek word that refers to a sour or acidic taste. It is commonly used to describe sour foods or drinks, such as vinegar or lemon juice. In addition to its culinary use, οξος can also be used metaphorically to describe a person's attitude or behavior as sour or bitter.
