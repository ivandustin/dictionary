Word: υαλινη

Etymology:
The word υαλινη (yalini) comes from the Greek word υάλινος (yalinos), which means "made of glass". The root of the word is υάλος (yálos), which means "glass".

Grammar:
υαλινη is an adjective in the feminine gender, singular form. It is used to describe something that is made of glass or has the appearance of glass.

Description:
υαλινη is a word that describes something that is transparent, shiny, and smooth like glass. It can be used to describe objects such as vases, windows, or mirrors, as well as figuratively to describe a person's demeanor or personality as being clear and transparent.
