Word: πεπεισμένος

Etymology:
The word πεπεισμένος (pepeismenos) is derived from the Greek verb πείθω (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The suffix -μένος (-menos) is a participle ending that indicates a state or condition. Therefore, πεπεισμένος (pepeismenos) can be translated as "convinced" or "persuaded".

Grammar:
πεπεισμένος (pepeismenos) is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case. For example, if we want to say "the convinced man", we would use the masculine singular nominative form πεπεισμένος άντρας (pepeismenos antras).

Description:
πεπεισμένος (pepeismenos) describes someone who has been convinced or persuaded to believe or do something. It can also be used to describe a person who is confident or certain about something. For example, if someone says "είμαι πεπεισμένος ότι έχω δίκιο" (ime pepeismenos oti eho dikio), it means "I am convinced that I am right".
