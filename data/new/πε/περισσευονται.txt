Word: περισσευονται

Etymology: The word περισσευονται comes from the Greek verb περισσεύω (perisseúō), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound". It is derived from the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around" or "excessively", and the verb σεύω (seúō), meaning "to pour".

Grammar: περισσευονται is a present tense, passive voice, indicative mood verb in Greek. It is the third person plural form of the verb περισσεύω. In English, it can be translated as "they are in excess" or "they abound".

Description: περισσευονται is a verb that describes a state of abundance or excess. It can be used to describe a variety of situations, such as a surplus of food or resources, an excess of emotions or feelings, or an abundance of opportunities or options. The word is often used in a positive context, suggesting that there is more than enough of something to go around.
