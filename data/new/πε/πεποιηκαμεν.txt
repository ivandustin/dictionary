Word: πεποιηκαμεν

Etymology: πεποιηκαμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix πεπο- (pepo-) indicates that the action has been completed in the past.

Grammar: πεποιηκαμεν is the first person plural, perfect active indicative form of the verb ποιέω. This means that the subject (we) has completed the action of making or doing something in the past.

Description: πεποιηκαμεν can be translated to "we have made" or "we have done". It is a strong statement of completion, indicating that the action has been fully accomplished in the past. This verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a completed project or task, or expressing satisfaction with a job well done.
