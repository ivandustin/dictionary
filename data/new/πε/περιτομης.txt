Word: περιτομής

Etymology:
The word περιτομής comes from the Greek verb περιτέμνω (peritemnō), which means "to cut around." It is derived from the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around," and the verb τέμνω (temnō), meaning "to cut."

περιτομής is a feminine noun in the genitive case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated in English as "of" or "'s."

Description:
περιτομής is a Greek word that refers to the act of circumcision. It is used in both a literal and figurative sense in the New Testament of the Bible, where it is often used to refer to the Jewish practice of circumcision as a symbol of their covenant with God.
