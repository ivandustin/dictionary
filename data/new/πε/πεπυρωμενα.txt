Word: πεπυρωμένα

Etymology: The word πεπυρωμένα comes from the Greek verb πυρόω (pyroo), which means "to set on fire" or "to burn". The prefix πεπ- (pep-) indicates that the action of burning has been completed in the past.

Grammar: πεπυρωμένα is the plural neuter form of the past participle of the verb πυρόω. It is used to describe objects or things that have been burned or set on fire.

Description: πεπυρωμένα is an adjective that describes something that has been burned or charred. It can be used to describe food that has been cooked over an open flame, or objects that have been damaged by fire. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a person or situation that has been through a difficult or traumatic experience.
