Word: πεινασουσιν

Etymology:
The word πεινασουσιν is a verb form in the future tense of the Greek language. It is derived from the root word πεινάω (peináō), which means "to hunger" or "to be hungry". The suffix -σουσιν (-sousin) is added to the root word to indicate the future tense, meaning "they will hunger" or "they will be hungry".

Grammar:
πεινασουσιν is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, voice, and person. The future tense is used to describe actions that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people.

Description:
πεινασουσιν is a word that describes a future action of hunger or being hungry. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a group of people who will be hungry in the future, or as a warning of a future famine or food shortage.
