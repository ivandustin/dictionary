The word "πετρου" (petrou) is a Greek noun that means "of Peter" or "belonging to Peter". It is derived from the Greek name "Πέτρος" (Petros), which means "rock" or "stone". In grammar, "πετρου" is in the genitive case, which is used to show possession or relationship. 

Example sentence: Το βιβλίο είναι του Πέτρου. (To vivlio einai tou Petrou.) - The book belongs to Peter.
