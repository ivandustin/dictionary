Word: περιπατούμεν (peripatoumen)

Etymology: The word περιπατούμεν comes from the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The suffix -μεν (-men) is a present participle ending, indicating that the action is ongoing.

Grammar: περιπατούμεν is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is the present participle form of the verb περιπατέω (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The present participle is used to describe an ongoing action, and can function as an adjective or a noun.

Description: περιπατούμεν describes someone who is currently walking around or strolling. It can be used as an adjective to describe a person or group of people who are walking, or as a noun to refer to the act of walking. For example, "Οι περιπατούμενοι" (oi peripatoumenoi) means "the walkers" or "those who are walking", while "Το περιπάτημα" (to peripatima) means "the act of walking" or "the stroll".
