Word: περιζωσαμένος

Etymology: 
The word περιζωσαμένος comes from the Greek verb περιζώννυμι (perizónnumi), which means "to gird around" or "to encircle with a belt". The word is formed from the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb ζώννυμι (zónnumi), meaning "to gird" or "to fasten with a belt". 

Grammar:
περιζωσαμένος is the past participle form of the verb περιζώννυμι (perizónnumi). It is a compound word consisting of the prefix περι- (peri-) and the verb ζώννυμι (zónnumi). The word is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. 

Description:
περιζωσαμένος describes someone who has girded themselves with a belt or something similar. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is prepared or ready for something. The word is often used in the context of battle or warfare, where soldiers would gird themselves with a belt to hold their weapons and armor in place.
