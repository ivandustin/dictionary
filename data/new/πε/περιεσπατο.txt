Word: περιεσπατο

Etymology:
The word περιεσπατο is a Greek verb that comes from the root word σπαω (spao), which means "to draw, pull, or tear apart." The prefix περι- (peri-) means "around" or "about." Therefore, περιεσπατο can be translated as "he was distracted" or "he was pulled in different directions."

Grammar:
περιεσπατο is a third-person singular, past tense, middle/passive voice form of the verb περισπάω (perispao). The middle/passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon or is acting on themselves. In this case, the subject is being distracted or pulled in different directions.

Description:
περιεσπατο is a verb that describes a state of being distracted or pulled in different directions. It can be used to describe a person who is unable to focus on a task or is easily swayed by different influences. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where there are many competing interests or distractions.
