Word: πεποιθησιν

Etymology:
The word πεποιθησιν (pepoithēsin) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb πείθω (peithō), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." The prefix πεπο- (pepo-) indicates that the action of the verb is completed or perfect, so πεποιθησιν (pepoithēsin) means "complete persuasion" or "full conviction."

πεποιθησιν (pepoithēsin) is a feminine noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word πεποιθησιν (pepoithēsin) is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
πεποιθησιν (pepoithēsin) refers to a state of complete confidence or trust in something or someone. It implies a strong conviction that something is true or reliable.
