Word: περικρατεις

Etymology:
The word περικρατεις is derived from the Greek words περί (peri) meaning "around" and κρατέω (krateo) meaning "to hold, to rule". Therefore, the word περικρατεις means "to hold power around" or "to rule over".

Grammar:
περικρατεις is a noun in the masculine gender and the nominative case in Greek. It is the plural form of περικρατής (perikrates), which means "ruler, governor, or master". In Greek, the word can be declined in different cases, such as genitive, dative, accusative, and vocative, depending on its function in a sentence.

Description:
περικρατεις is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to the rulers or governors of a city-state or a region. These rulers were often aristocrats or wealthy individuals who held power over the people and the land. In some cases, they were elected by the citizens, while in others, they were appointed by a higher authority. The term can also be used metaphorically to refer to anyone who holds power or influence over others.
