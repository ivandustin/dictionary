Word: περισσεύσαι

Etymology: The word περισσεύσαι comes from the Greek verb περισσεύω (perisseuō), which means "to be in excess, to abound, to be left over." It is derived from the adjective περισσός (perissos), which means "abundant, excessive, superfluous."

Grammar: περισσεύσαι is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb περισσεύω (perisseuō), which means "to be in excess, to abound, to be left over."

Description: περισσεύσαι is a Greek verb that describes the action of having an excess or abundance of something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a surplus of food or money, or an excess of emotions or thoughts. The verb is often used in a positive sense, indicating that there is more than enough of something, but it can also be used in a negative sense, indicating that there is too much of something.
