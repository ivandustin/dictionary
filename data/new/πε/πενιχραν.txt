The word "πενιχραν" (penichran) is a Greek adjective that means "meager" or "scanty". 

Etymology: The word is derived from the Greek word "πένης" (penes), which means "poor" or "needy". 

Grammar: "πενιχραν" is in the feminine accusative singular form. 

Description: The word is often used to describe something that is lacking in quantity or quality, such as a meager meal or a scanty amount of money. It can also be used to describe a person who is thin or emaciated.
