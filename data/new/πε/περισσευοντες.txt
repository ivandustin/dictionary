Word: περισσευοντες

Etymology: περισσευοντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb περισσεύω (perisseuō), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people or things that are currently in a state of excess or abundance.

Grammar: περισσευοντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are currently in a state of excess or abundance. In Greek, present participles can function as adjectives, nouns, or verbs depending on the context.

Description: περισσευοντες can be translated as "those who are in excess" or "those who abound". It is often used in a positive sense to describe people or things that are plentiful or abundant. For example, in the New Testament, the word is used to describe the abundance of grace that God has given to believers (Romans 5:17). However, it can also be used in a negative sense to describe something that is excessive or unnecessary.
