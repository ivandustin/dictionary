Word: πεποιθως

Etymology:
The word πεποιθως (pepoithōs) is derived from the Greek verb πείθω (peithō), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." The prefix πεπο- (pepo-) is the perfect tense form of the verb, indicating that the action of persuasion has been completed in the past and has resulted in a state of confidence or trust.

Grammar:
πεποιθως is an adverb in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the suffix -ως (-ōs) to the perfect tense form of the verb πείθω (peithō). The adverb πεποιθως (pepoithōs) means "confidently" or "with trust."

Description:
πεποιθως (pepoithōs) is a word that conveys a sense of assurance and trust. It is often used to describe someone who is confident in their abilities or beliefs. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the faith and trust that Christians have in God.
