Word: πεσωσιν

Etymology:
The word πεσωσιν is a Greek word that comes from the verb πίπτω (pípto), which means "to fall." The suffix -σιν (-sin) is a third-person plural ending in the aorist tense, indicating that the action of falling has already taken place.

Grammar:
πεσωσιν is a verb in the aorist tense, third-person plural form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third-person plural form indicates that the action was performed by multiple people.

Description:
πεσωσιν can be translated to "they fell" in English. It is a simple past tense verb that describes an action that has already taken place. The context in which this word is used will determine the specific meaning and significance of the action of falling.
