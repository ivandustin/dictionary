Word: πεμφθεντες

Etymology:
The word πεμφθεντες is a Greek word that comes from the verb πέμπω (pempo), which means "to send." The word is in the passive participle form, which means "having been sent."

πεμφθεντες is a plural masculine nominative participle in Greek. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past. The participle form indicates that the subject of the sentence has received the action of being sent.

Description:
πεμφθεντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Acts. It is used to describe a group of people who have been sent on a mission or task.
