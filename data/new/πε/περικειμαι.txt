Word: περικείμαι

Etymology: περι- (peri-, meaning "around") + κείμαι (keimai, meaning "to lie down"). The word comes from ancient Greek.

Grammar: περικείμαι is a verb in the present tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is conjugated as follows:

- Singular: περικέομαι (perikéomai, I lie around), περικείσαι (perikeísai, you lie around), περικείται (perikeítai, he/she/it lies around)
- Plural: περικειμέθα (perikeimétha, we lie around), περικείσθε (perikeísthe, you all lie around), περικείνται (perikeíntai, they lie around)

Description: περικείμαι means "to lie around" or "to recline." It is often used in reference to someone lying down or resting in a relaxed position. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is idle or inactive.
