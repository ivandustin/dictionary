The word "πειρασθηις" (peirastheis) is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and participle mood. It comes from the root word "πειράω" (peirao), which means "to try, attempt, or test." The suffix "-σθηις" (-stheis) indicates that the action was done to the subject, making it a passive participle.

In English, "πειρασθηις" can be translated as "having been tested" or "having been tried." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone who has undergone a difficult or challenging experience and has come out stronger as a result.
