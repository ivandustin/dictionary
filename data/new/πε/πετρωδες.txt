Word: πετρωδες

Etymology: The word πετρωδες comes from the Greek word πέτρα (petra) which means "rock" or "stone". The suffix -ωδης (-odes) is used to form adjectives that describe the nature or quality of something. Therefore, πετρωδες means "rocky" or "stony".

Grammar: πετρωδες is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It agrees with the noun it modifies in gender, number, and case.

Description: πετρωδες is used to describe something that is rocky or stony. It can be used to describe a landscape, a building material, or even a person's complexion. For example, "Η περιοχή είναι πετρώδης" (I periochi einai petrodes) means "The area is rocky".
