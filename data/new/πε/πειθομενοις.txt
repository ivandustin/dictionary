Word: πειθομενοις

Etymology: πειθομενοις is the present participle of the Greek verb πείθομαι (peithomai), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰeydʰ- ("to persuade, convince").

Grammar: πειθομενοις is a masculine, plural, dative case form of the present participle of the verb πείθομαι. In Greek, the present participle is used to indicate ongoing action or a state of being. The dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of the verb, or the person or thing to or for whom the action is being done.

Description: πειθομενοις can be translated as "to those being persuaded" or "to those being convinced". It is often used in the context of persuasion or convincing someone to do something. In Greek literature, it is commonly used in the context of political speeches or philosophical arguments.
