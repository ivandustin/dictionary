Word: πειραζομενοις

Etymology: 
The word πειραζομενοις is a present participle form of the Greek verb πειράζω (peirázo), which means "to test" or "to try". The word is derived from the noun πεῖρα (peîra), which means "trial" or "attempt".

Grammar:
πειραζομενοις is a present participle form of the verb πειράζω (peirázo) in the passive voice. It is in the dative case and masculine gender, indicating that it is referring to a group of people who are being tested or tried.

Description:
The word πειραζομενοις can be translated as "being tested" or "being tried". It is often used in a religious or moral context, referring to individuals who are being tested or tempted by external forces. In the New Testament, the word is used to describe the testing of Jesus in the wilderness by Satan. The word can also be used more generally to describe any situation in which someone is being put to the test or challenged.
