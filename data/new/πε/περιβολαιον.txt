Word: περιβολαιον

Etymology:
The word περιβολαιον comes from the Greek word περιβόλαιος (peribólaios), which means "enclosing, surrounding." It is derived from the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around," and the verb βάλλω (ballō), meaning "to throw."

Grammar:
περιβολαιον is a neuter noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and its nominative case is περιβολαιον. In the genitive case, it is περιβολαιου (peribolaiou).

Description:
περιβολαιον refers to a fence or an enclosure that surrounds a particular area. It can also refer to a boundary or a limit. In ancient Greece, περιβολαιον was used to refer to the walls that surrounded a city or a fortress. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a fence or a boundary.
