Word: πεπορευμένους

Etymology: The word πεπορευμένους is a past participle form of the Greek verb πορεύομαι (poreúomai), which means "to go, to travel". The prefix πε- (pe-) indicates a completed action, so πεπορευμένους means "having gone, having traveled".

Grammar: πεπορευμένους is a masculine accusative plural form of the past participle. It is used to describe a group of people who have completed a journey or traveled to a certain place. In Greek, the past participle is often used as an adjective to describe the state or condition of a noun.

Description: πεπορευμένους can be translated as "those who have traveled" or "those who have gone". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the disciples of Jesus who traveled with him during his ministry. The word implies a sense of purpose and determination, as those who have traveled have done so with a specific goal in mind.
