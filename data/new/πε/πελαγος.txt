Word: πελαγος

Etymology:
The word πελαγος (pelagos) comes from the ancient Greek language. It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pelǵ-, meaning "to pour, to flow". In ancient Greek, the word originally referred to the open sea or the high seas.

Grammar:
In Greek, πελαγος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is πελαγος, and its genitive singular form is πελαγου. The word can also be declined in the plural.

Description:
πελαγος is a term used to describe the open sea or the high seas. It is often used in literature and poetry to evoke a sense of vastness and mystery. In ancient Greek mythology, the god of the sea, Poseidon, was often associated with the πελαγος. The word is still used in modern Greek to refer to the sea.
