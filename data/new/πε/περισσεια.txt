Word: περισσεία

Etymology:
The word περισσεία (perisseia) comes from the Greek root word περισσός (perissos), which means "abundant" or "excessive." The suffix -εία (-eia) is a common Greek suffix used to form abstract nouns.

Grammar:
περισσεία is a feminine noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form, and its nominative case ending is -εία (-eia).

Description:
περισσεία refers to an excess or surplus of something. It can be used to describe an abundance of material possessions, wealth, or resources. It can also refer to an excess of emotions or feelings, such as joy or anger. In a more general sense, περισσεία can be used to describe anything that is in excess or surplus.
