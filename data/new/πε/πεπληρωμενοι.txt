Word: πεπληρωμένοι

Etymology: The word πεπληρωμένοι comes from the Greek verb πληρόω (plēróō), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The prefix πεπ- (pep-) indicates that the action of filling or completing has already been done in the past.

Grammar: πεπληρωμένοι is the plural form of the past participle of the verb πληρόω. It is used to describe a group of people who have been filled or completed with something. In Greek, the past participle is often used as an adjective, and agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: πεπληρωμένοι can be translated as "filled", "completed", or "fulfilled". It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe people who have been filled with the Holy Spirit or who have fulfilled a particular prophecy or mission. It can also be used more generally to describe people who have achieved a sense of fullness or completion in their lives.
