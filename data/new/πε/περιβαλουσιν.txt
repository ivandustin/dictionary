Word: περιβαλουσιν

Etymology: περι- (peri-, around) + βάλλω (ballō, to throw) + -ουσιν (-ousin, third person plural present active indicative suffix). The word comes from Ancient Greek.

Grammar: περιβαλουσιν is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is an active voice verb, indicating that the subject is performing the action. The verb is conjugated from the root word περιβάλλω (periballō), which means "to surround" or "to encircle".

Description: περιβαλουσιν is a Greek verb that means "they surround" or "they encircle". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a group of people or things surrounding or enclosing something. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe physical objects or structures that surround or encircle something.
