Word: πειρασθεις

Etymology:
The word πειρασθεις is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πειράω (peiráō), which means "to try, attempt, or test." The suffix -σθεις (-stheis) is a passive participle ending, which means that the word can be translated as "having been tried" or "having been tested."

Grammar:
πειρασθεις is a second-person singular passive aorist participle of the verb πειράω (peiráō). In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, without any reference to its duration. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon, rather than performing the action. The participle form indicates that the word is functioning as an adjective, describing the subject of the sentence.

Description:
πειρασθεις can be translated as "having been tried" or "having been tested." It is often used in a metaphorical sense, to describe someone who has undergone a difficult experience or has been put to the test. The word can also be used in a more literal sense, to describe something that has been tested or experimented with. Overall, πειρασθεις conveys a sense of endurance, resilience, and strength in the face of adversity.
