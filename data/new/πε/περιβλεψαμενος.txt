Word: περιβλεψαμενος

Etymology: 
The word περιβλεψαμενος is a Greek verb that comes from the root word περιβλέπω (periblepo), which means "to look around" or "to survey". The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
περιβλεψαμενος is a participle form of the verb περιβλέπω (periblepo). It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. The participle form indicates that the action of looking around or surveying has been completed in the past. 

Description:
περιβλεψαμενος can be translated to English as "having looked around" or "having surveyed". It is often used in Greek literature to describe someone who has taken a careful look at their surroundings or who has surveyed a particular area. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who has carefully considered a situation or who has taken a broad view of a problem.
