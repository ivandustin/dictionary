The word "πεμψουσιν" (pempsousin) is a verb in the Greek language, specifically in the future tense. It is the third person plural form of the verb "πέμπω" (pempo), which means "to send" or "to dispatch". 

Etymology: The word "πέμπω" (pempo) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *pem(p)-, which means "to send". 

Grammar: As mentioned, "πεμψουσιν" is the third person plural form of the future tense of "πέμπω". In Greek, the future tense is formed by adding the appropriate endings to the stem of the verb. 

Description: "πεμψουσιν" is a verb that indicates an action that will happen in the future, specifically the action of sending or dispatching. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in a sentence like "They will send the package tomorrow."
