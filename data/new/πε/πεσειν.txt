Word: πεσειν

Etymology:
The word πεσειν (pesin) is derived from the Greek verb πίπτω (pipto), which means "to fall." The word is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem πεσ- (pes-), which is the aorist tense of πίπτω.

Grammar:
πεσειν is a verb in the aorist tense, which is used to describe a completed action in the past. It is also in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to match a specific subject or tense.

Description:
πεσειν is often used in Greek literature to describe the act of falling or descending. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a decline or downfall in a person's status or reputation.
