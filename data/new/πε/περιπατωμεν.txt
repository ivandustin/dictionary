Word: περιπατώμεν (peripatomen)

Etymology: The word περιπατώμεν comes from the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The suffix -μεν (-men) indicates that the word is in the present tense and is used for the first person plural (we).

Grammar: περιπατώμεν is a present tense verb in the first person plural form. It is conjugated from the verb περιπατέω (peripateo), which is a regular verb in Greek. The verb stem is περιπατ-, and the present tense endings are -ω, -εις, -ει, -ομεν, -ετε, -ουν.

Description: περιπατώμεν is a Greek verb that means "we walk around" or "we stroll". It is often used to describe leisurely walking or wandering around without a specific destination. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe a mental or emotional state of wandering or aimlessness.
