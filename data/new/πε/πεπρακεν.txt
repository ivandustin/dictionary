The word "πεπρακεν" (pepraken) is a Greek verb that is in the perfect tense and active voice. It is derived from the root verb "πράσσω" (prasso), which means "to do" or "to accomplish." The prefix "πε-" (pe-) indicates that the action has been completed in the past and the suffix "-κεν" (-ken) indicates the third person singular.

In terms of grammar, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing relevance in the present. The active voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

As for the meaning of "πεπρακεν," it can be translated as "he/she/it has done" or "he/she/it accomplished." The context in which the word is used will determine the specific meaning.
