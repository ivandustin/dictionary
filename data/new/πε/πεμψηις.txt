Etymology: 
The word "πεμψηις" (pempsēis) is a Greek noun that comes from the verb "πέμπω" (pempō), which means "to send". The suffix "-ηις" (-ēis) is added to the verb stem to form a noun that means "the act of sending".

"πεμψηις" is a feminine noun in the nominative singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The word can also be declined in other cases, such as genitive, dative, and accusative, depending on its function in a sentence.

"πεμψηις" refers to the act of sending someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as sending a message, sending a gift, or sending a person on a mission.
