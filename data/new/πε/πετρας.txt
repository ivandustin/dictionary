Word: πετρας

Etymology: πετρας is a Greek word that comes from the noun πέτρα (petra), meaning "rock" or "stone". 

Grammar: πετρας is the genitive singular form of πέτρα (petra). In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and is often translated into English as "of" or "'s". 

Description: πετρας is a word that refers to a specific rock or stone, often used in the context of construction or geology. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to something that is solid, unchanging, or enduring.
