Word: περιεπατησεν

Etymology: περι- (peri-, around) + επατησεν (epatesen, walked) = περιεπατησεν (periepatesen, walked around)

Grammar: περιεπατησεν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person singular, indicative mood, active voice in Ancient Greek.

Description: περιεπατησεν means "walked around" or "walked about" in English. It is derived from the combination of the prefix περι- (peri-, around) and the verb επατησεν (epatesen, walked). The word is commonly used in Ancient Greek literature to describe someone walking around or exploring a particular area.
