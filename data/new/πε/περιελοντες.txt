Word: περιελόντες

Etymology:
The word περιελόντες is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: περί (peri), which means "around" or "about," and ἐλαύνω (elauno), which means "to drive" or "to push." Therefore, περιελόντες can be translated as "driving around" or "pushing around."

Grammar:
περιελόντες is the aorist participle of the verb περιέρχομαι (perierchomai), which means "to go around" or "to surround." The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb happened in the past, and the participle form indicates that the word is used as an adjective. In this case, περιελόντες is used to describe a group of people who were driving or pushing something around.

Description:
περιελόντες can be used to describe a group of people who are moving something around, such as a cart or a heavy object. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is trying to manipulate or control a situation by pushing people or ideas around. Overall, the word conveys a sense of movement and force.
