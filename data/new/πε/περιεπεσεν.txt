Word: περιεπεσεν

Etymology:
The word περιεπεσεν is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: περι (peri) meaning "around" or "about" and επεσεν (epesen) meaning "fell". Therefore, the literal meaning of περιεπεσεν is "fell around" or "fell about".

Grammar:
περιεπεσεν is a third-person singular aorist indicative active verb in Greek. The aorist tense refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the indicative mood indicates a statement of fact. The active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

περιεπεσεν is used in the New Testament of the Bible in Luke 10:30 to describe the actions of a man who "fell among thieves" on the road from Jerusalem to Jericho. The word is used to convey the idea that the man fell into the hands of the thieves, or that he was surrounded by them.
