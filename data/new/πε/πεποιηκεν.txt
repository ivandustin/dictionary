Word: πεποιηκεν

Etymology:
The word πεποιηκεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix πεπο- (pepo-) is a perfect tense marker, indicating that the action of making or doing has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
πεποιηκεν is a third-person singular verb in the perfect tense. In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. The verb is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

Description:
πεποιηκεν can be translated to English as "has made" or "has done". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the actions of God or Jesus, such as in John 5:17 where Jesus says, "My Father is working until now, and I am working." The use of the perfect tense in this context emphasizes the ongoing nature of God's work and the continuous effects it has in the present.
