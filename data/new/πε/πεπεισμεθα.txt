Word: πεπεισμέθα (pepeismétha)

Etymology: πεπεισμέθα is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πείθω (peíthō), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." The prefix πεπ- (pep-) indicates that the action of the verb is completed or perfect.

In other words, it is the past tense of the verb πείθω (to persuade) in the passive voice, indicating that the subject (we) received the action of the verb. The perfect tense indicates that the action was completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Description: πεπεισμέθα means "we have been persuaded" or "we have been convinced."
