Word: περιπατών

Etymology: περιπατών comes from the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripatéo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the words περί (peri), meaning "around", and πατέω (pateo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: περιπατών is the present participle form of the verb περιπατέω. It is in the masculine gender, singular number, and nominative case. It can be translated to English as "walking" or "strolling".

Description: περιπατών is a word that describes the action of walking or strolling. It can be used to describe someone who is walking around leisurely or someone who is taking a walk for exercise. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is moving forward in life or making progress towards a goal.
