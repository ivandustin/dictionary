Word: περιπατησωμεν

Etymology: περιπατησωμεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word περιπατέω (peripatéo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The suffix -σωμεν (-somen) is the first person plural present subjunctive ending, which indicates a suggestion or a hypothetical action.

Grammar: περιπατησωμεν is a first person plural present subjunctive form of the verb περιπατέω. In Greek, the subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or hypothetical situations. The present tense indicates that the action is happening now or in the near future. The first person plural form indicates that the subject is "we".

Description: περιπατησωμεν means "let's walk around" or "let's take a stroll". It is a suggestion or a hypothetical action that is expressed in the present tense. The verb implies a sense of leisure or relaxation, as if the speaker is suggesting a casual walk with friends or loved ones.
