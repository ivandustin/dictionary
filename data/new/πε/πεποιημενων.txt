Word: πεποιημένων

Etymology:
The word πεποιημένων is a form of the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix πεποιημέν- (pepoiimén-) is the perfect participle form of the verb, indicating that the action of making or doing has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
πεποιημένων is the genitive plural form of the perfect participle of the verb ποιέω. In Greek, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship, and the plural form indicates that there are multiple things that have been made or done.

Description:
πεποιημένων can be translated as "of the things that have been made" or "of the things that have been done". It is often used in philosophical or theological contexts to refer to the created world or the works of God. In grammar, it can be used as an adjective to modify a noun, or as a noun itself in certain contexts.
