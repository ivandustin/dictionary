Word: πενθουσιν

Etymology:
The word πενθουσιν is derived from the Greek verb πενθέω (pentheo), which means "to mourn" or "to grieve". It is a present active participle, indicating ongoing action.

Grammar:
πενθουσιν is a third-person plural present active participle in the Greek language. It is often translated as "they mourn" or "they grieve". The word is used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the book of Matthew, to describe the mourning of those who are grieving.

Description:
πενθουσιν is a word that describes the ongoing action of mourning or grieving. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context to describe the mourning of those who have lost a loved one or who are experiencing a difficult time. The word conveys a sense of deep sadness and sorrow, and is often associated with the process of healing and coming to terms with loss.
