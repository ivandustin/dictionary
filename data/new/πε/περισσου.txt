The word "περισσου" (perissou) is a Greek adverb that means "abundantly" or "excessively". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "περι-" (peri-), which means "around" or "excessive", and the suffix "-σσου" (-ssou), which is used to form adverbs indicating abundance or excess.

Grammar: "περισσου" is an adverb in the Greek language, which means it modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to indicate the manner, degree, or frequency of an action or state.

Description: The word "περισσου" is commonly used in Greek to express the idea of something being done or present in excess or abundance. For example, "Πίνω περισσότερο νερό από ό,τι χρειάζομαι" (I drink more water than I need). It can also be used to indicate a surplus or excess of something, such as "Έχουμε περισσότερα αυγά από ό,τι χρειαζόμαστε" (We have more eggs than we need).
