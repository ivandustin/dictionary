Word: περιπατείτε

Etymology: The word περιπατείτε comes from the Greek verb περιπατώ (peripató), which means "to walk around". The suffix -είτε (-eíte) indicates that the word is in the present tense and second person plural form.

Grammar: περιπατείτε is a verb in the present tense and second person plural form. It is derived from the verb περιπατώ, which is a regular verb in the Greek language. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: περιπατείτε (peripatíte)
- Imperfect tense: περιπατούσατε (peripatoúsate)
- Future tense: θα περιπατείτε (tha peripatíte)

Description: περιπατείτε is a command form of the verb περιπατώ, which means "to walk around". It is used to address a group of people and tell them to walk around or take a walk. The word can be used in various contexts, such as giving directions, suggesting a leisure activity, or encouraging physical exercise.
