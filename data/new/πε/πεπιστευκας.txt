Word: πεπιστευκας

Etymology:
The word πεπιστευκας (pepisteukas) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith." The prefix πεπι- (pepi-) is a perfect tense marker, indicating that the action of believing has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
πεπιστευκας is the second person singular form of the perfect tense of the Greek verb πιστεύω. It is translated as "you have believed" in English. The verb is in the active voice, indicating that the subject (you) is performing the action of believing.

Description:
πεπιστευκας is a word that expresses the idea of having faith or trust in something or someone. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, but can also be used in a more general sense to indicate trust or confidence in a person, idea, or concept. The perfect tense form of the verb suggests that the act of believing has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.
