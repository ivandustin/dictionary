Word: πεπιστευμαι

Etymology:
The word πεπιστευμαι (pepisteumai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith." The prefix πεπι- (pepi-) is a perfect tense marker, indicating that the action of believing has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present.

Grammar:
πεπιστευμαι is a first-person singular verb in the perfect tense, indicating that the action of believing has been completed in the past and has ongoing effects in the present. It is in the middle voice, indicating that the subject is both the agent and the recipient of the action. In English, it would be translated as "I have believed" or "I am convinced."

Description:
πεπιστευμαι is a strong word that conveys a deep sense of conviction and trust. It is often used in religious contexts to express faith in God or in a particular doctrine or belief system.
