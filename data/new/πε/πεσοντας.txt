Word: πεσόντας

Etymology: πεσόντας is the present participle of the Greek verb πίπτω (pípto), which means "to fall". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, which means "to fly, rush, fall".

Grammar: πεσόντας is a masculine noun in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. As a present participle, it is used to describe an action that is currently happening or has just happened. In this case, it means "falling".

Description: πεσόντας is a word that describes the action of falling. It can be used to describe a person, object, or even an idea that is falling. For example, "Οι φύλακες έπιασαν τον κακοποιό πεσόντας από τον τοίχο" (The guards caught the criminal falling from the wall).
