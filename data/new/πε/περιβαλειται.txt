Word: περιβαλείται

Etymology: περι- (peri-, around) + βάλλω (vállo, to throw) + -είται (-eítai, reflexive suffix) = περιβάλλω (perivállo, to surround)

Grammar: περιβαλείται is a verb in the present tense, third person singular, passive voice, indicative mood. It is derived from the verb περιβάλλω (perivállo, to surround) and means "is surrounded".

Description: περιβαλείται is a Greek verb that describes the action of something being surrounded by something else. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a physical object being surrounded by other objects, or a person being surrounded by a group of people. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon, rather than performing the action themselves.
