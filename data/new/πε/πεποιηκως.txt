Word: πεποιηκως

Etymology: πεποιηκως is a Greek word that comes from the verb ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do". The prefix πεπο- (pepo-) indicates that the action has been completed in the past.

Grammar: πεποιηκως is a participle in the perfect tense, active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb ποιέω and means "having made" or "having done".

Description: πεποιηκως is used to describe someone or something that has completed an action in the past. It is often used in the New Testament to describe Jesus Christ as the one who has accomplished salvation for humanity. In Greek literature, it can be used to describe any completed action, such as a work of art or a successful business venture.
