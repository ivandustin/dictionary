Word: περιπατούντας

Etymology: The word περιπατούντας comes from the Greek verb περιπατώ (peripató), which means "to walk around". The suffix -ούντας (-ountas) is the present participle form of the verb.

Grammar: περιπατούντας is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is derived from the verb περιπατώ, which is a first conjugation verb in the present tense.

Description: περιπατούντας describes someone who is walking around or strolling. It can be used as an adjective to modify a noun, or as a noun itself to refer to a person who is walking. For example, "Ο περιπατούντας" (o peripatountas) means "the person who is walking around".
