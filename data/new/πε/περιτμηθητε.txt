Word: περιτμηθητε

Etymology: The word περιτμηθητε is derived from the Greek verb περιτέμνω (peritémno), which means "to circumcise". The suffix -θητε (-thete) is a second person plural passive imperative ending, which means "you all should be circumcised".

Grammar: περιτμηθητε is a second person plural passive imperative verb in the Greek language. It is formed by adding the passive voice suffix -θητε to the stem of the verb περιτέμνω. The verb is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: περιτμηθητε is a command in Greek, instructing a group of people to be circumcised. It is found in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Galatians 5:2. The verse reads: "Behold, I Paul say unto you, that if ye be circumcised, Christ shall profit you nothing."
