Word: πεφιμωσο

Etymology: The word πεφιμωσο comes from the Greek verb φιμώνω (fimóno), which means "to muzzle" or "to silence". The prefix πε- (pe-) indicates a completed action, so πεφιμωσο can be translated as "I have silenced" or "I have been silenced".

Grammar: πεφιμωσο is a verb in the first person singular of the aorist tense in Greek. It is a compound verb formed by adding the prefix πε- to the verb φιμώνω. The aorist tense in Greek is used to describe a completed action in the past, without indicating the duration or repetition of the action.
