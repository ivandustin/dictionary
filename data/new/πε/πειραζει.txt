The word "πειράζει" (peirazei) is a verb in the Greek language. 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek verb "πειράω" (peirao), which means "to try" or "to attempt". 

Grammar: "Πειράζει" is a present tense, indicative mood, third person singular form of the verb "πειράζω" (peirazo). It can be translated to English as "he/she/it tries" or "he/she/it tempts".

Description: The word "πειράζει" can have different meanings depending on the context. It can mean "to try" or "to attempt", as in "Πειράζει να λύσει το πρόβλημα" (Peirazei na lysei to provlima) which means "He/she/it is trying to solve the problem". It can also mean "to tempt" or "to provoke", as in "Μην τον πειράζεις" (Min ton peirazeis) which means "Don't provoke him".
