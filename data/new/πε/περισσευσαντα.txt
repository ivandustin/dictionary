Word: περισσευσαντα

Etymology: The word περισσευσαντα is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, which means it refers to a completed action in the past. It is derived from the root word περισσεύω (perisseuō), which means "to be in excess, to abound, to be left over." 

Grammar: περισσευσαντα is the aorist active participle of the verb περισσεύω. In Greek, participles are verbal adjectives that can modify nouns or function as independent clauses. The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb is completed in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. 

Description: περισσευσαντα can be translated as "having abounded" or "having been left over." It describes a completed action in the past where something was in excess or surplus. The word can be used in various contexts, such as in economics, where it can refer to a surplus of goods or resources, or in a social context, where it can refer to an excess of food or drink at a feast.
