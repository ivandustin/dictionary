Word: περιεδεδετο

Etymology: The word περιεδεδετο comes from the Greek verb περιδέω (peridéō), which means "to bind around" or "to tie up". The prefix περι- (peri-) means "around" or "about", and the root word δέω (deō) means "to bind" or "to tie".

Grammar: περιεδεδετο is a verb in the past tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb περιδέω (peridéō).

Description: περιεδεδετο means "he/she/it was bound around" or "he/she/it was tied up". It is a descriptive word that can be used to describe someone or something that has been physically restrained or tied up. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is emotionally or mentally bound or constrained.
