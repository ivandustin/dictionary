Word: περιεπατούν

Etymology: The word περιεπατούν comes from the Greek verb περιπατώ (peripató), which means "to walk around". The prefix περι- (peri-) means "around" and the root πατ- (pat-) means "to walk".

Grammar: περιεπατούν is a present tense verb in the third person plural form. It is derived from the verb περιπατώ (peripató) and the present tense suffix -ούν (-oun). The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Εγώ περιπατώ (I walk)
- Εσύ περιπατείς (You walk)
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό περιπατεί (He/She/It walks)
- Εμείς περιπατούμε (We walk)
- Εσείς περιπατείτε (You all walk)
- Αυτοί/Αυτές περιπατούν (They walk)

Description: περιεπατούν describes the action of walking around or strolling. It can be used to describe a leisurely walk or a more purposeful one. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is indecisive or unsure, as if they are "walking around" a problem or issue.
