Word: περιεποιησατο

Etymology: The word περιεποιησατο comes from the Greek root words περι- (peri-), meaning "around" or "surrounding," and εποιησατο (epoiēsato), meaning "made" or "created." Together, the word means "made around" or "surrounded."

Grammar: περιεποιησατο is a verb in the aorist tense, middle voice, and indicative mood. It is the third person singular form of the verb περιεποιεομαι (peri-epoieomai), which means "to acquire," "to obtain," or "to possess."

Description: περιεποιησατο is a Greek word that describes the act of acquiring or obtaining something. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as acquiring property, obtaining knowledge, or possessing a skill. The word emphasizes the idea of actively seeking and obtaining something, rather than simply receiving it passively.
