Word: περιεπατησατε

Etymology:
The word περιεπατησατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root words περι (peri) meaning "around" and επατησατε (epatēsate) meaning "you walked". The word is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar:
περιεπατησατε is a second-person plural verb in the aorist tense. It is the past tense of the verb περιπατώ (peripató), which means "to walk around". The prefix περι (peri) adds the meaning of "around" to the verb.

Description:
περιεπατησατε means "you walked around" or "you took a walk around". It is a verb that describes the action of walking around a place or area. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a leisurely stroll or a more purposeful walk around a specific location.
