Word: περιθεις

Etymology:
The word περιθεις comes from the Greek verb περιτίθημι (peritithemi), which means "to place around" or "to put on". The word is derived from the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb τίθημι (tithemi), meaning "to place" or "to put".

Grammar:
περιθεις is a noun in the masculine gender and singular number. It is declined in the first declension, with the genitive form being περιθέως (peritheos).

Description:
περιθεις is a term used in ancient Greek literature to refer to a type of headband or wreath worn by athletes during competitions. It was typically made of leaves or branches and was worn as a symbol of victory. The word is also used in a metaphorical sense to refer to any type of crown or honor bestowed upon someone for their achievements.
