Word: περισσότερον

Etymology: περισσότερον is a Greek adverb that means "more". It is derived from the adjective περισσός (perissos), which means "abundant" or "excessive".

Grammar: περισσότερον is the comparative form of the adverb πολύ (polý), which means "much" or "very". It is used to compare two things, indicating that one is more than the other. It can be used with adjectives, adverbs, and verbs.

Description: περισσότερον is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts. It is commonly used in everyday speech and writing to express comparisons between two things. For example, "Αυτό το βιβλίο είναι περισσότερο ενδιαφέρον από το άλλο" (This book is more interesting than the other one). It can also be used to express a degree of intensity or quantity, such as "Πίνω περισσότερο νερό από ό,τι καφέ" (I drink more water than coffee). Overall, περισσότερον is a useful and commonly used word in the Greek language.
