Word: πεποιηκα

Etymology:
The word πεποιηκα (pepoiēka) is a perfect tense form of the Greek verb ποιέω (poiéō), which means "to make" or "to do". The root of the word is ποι- (poi-), which is related to the English word "poem" and the Latin word "poēma", both of which mean "a work of art".

Grammar:
πεποιηκα (pepoiēka) is a first-person singular perfect tense form of the verb ποιέω (poiéō). In Greek, the perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed in the past and has continuing relevance in the present. The first-person singular form indicates that the action was performed by the speaker.

Description:
πεποιηκα (pepoiēka) can be translated to English as "I have made" or "I have done". It is a statement of completion, indicating that the speaker has finished a task or created something. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from describing a physical object that has been made to expressing a sense of accomplishment or achievement.
