Word: πειθαρχησαντας

Etymology: The word πειθαρχησαντας is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: πείθω (peíthō) meaning "to persuade" and ἀρχή (archḗ) meaning "rule" or "authority". The suffix -σαντας (-santas) indicates the past participle form, meaning "having obeyed".

Grammar: πειθαρχησαντας is the past participle form of the verb πειθαρχέω (peitharchéō), which means "to obey" or "to submit to authority". It is in the masculine accusative plural form, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a group of people who have obeyed or submitted to authority.

Description: πειθαρχησαντας is a word that describes a group of people who have obeyed or submitted to authority. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in military or political settings, to describe soldiers or citizens who have followed orders or laws.
