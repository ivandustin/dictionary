Word: περισσευουσα

Etymology: περισσευουσα is a Greek word that comes from the verb περισσεύω (perisseúō), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound".

Grammar: περισσευουσα is the feminine form of the present participle of the verb περισσεύω (perisseúō), which means "abounding" or "excess". It is in the nominative case, singular number, and agrees with a feminine noun.

Description: περισσευουσα is used to describe something that is abundant or in excess. It can be used to describe a physical quantity, such as an abundance of food or water, or a non-physical quantity, such as an abundance of love or happiness. In Greek literature, it is often used to describe the abundance of blessings or good fortune.
