Word: πεμψαι

Etymology: πεμψαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πέμπω (pempo), which means "to send". It is in the aorist tense, which indicates a completed action in the past.

Grammar: πεμψαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative form of the verb πέμπω (pempo). This means that it is a command directed towards one person to "send" or "dispatch" something.

Description: πεμψαι is a verb that is often used in Greek literature and language to express the act of sending or dispatching something or someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as sending a message, sending a gift, or sending someone on a mission. The imperative form of the verb, as in πεμψαι, is often used in commands or requests to someone to send or dispatch something.
