Word: περισσείαν

Etymology: The word περισσείαν comes from the Greek word περίσσευμα (perisseuma), which means "surplus" or "excess". The suffix -αν (-an) is added to the end of the word to indicate the accusative case.

Grammar: περισσείαν is a feminine noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description: περισσείαν refers to something that is in excess or surplus. It can be used to describe an abundance of something, such as food or money. In ancient Greece, the concept of περισσείαν was important in the economy, as it referred to the surplus of goods that could be traded or sold. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to describe excess or surplus in various contexts.
