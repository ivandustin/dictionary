Word: πεπληρωμένη (pepliroméni)

Etymology: The word πεπληρωμένη comes from the Greek verb πληρόω (pliróo), which means "to fill" or "to complete". The prefix πεπ- (pep-) indicates that the action of filling or completing has already been done in the past.

Grammar: πεπληρωμένη is the feminine form of the past participle of the verb πληρόω. It is used to describe a feminine noun that has been filled or completed in the past. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: πεπληρωμένη is an adjective that describes something that has been filled or completed in the past. It can be used to describe a variety of things, such as a container that has been filled with a liquid, a task that has been completed, or a person who has been fulfilled or satisfied. The word has a sense of finality and completeness, indicating that the action of filling or completing has been done thoroughly and completely.
