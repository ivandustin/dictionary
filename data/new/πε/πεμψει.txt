Etymology: The word "πέμπω" (pempo) comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *pem(p)-, which means "to send". 

Grammar: "πεμψει" (pemsei) is a future tense verb, indicating that the action of sending will take place in the future. It is also in the active voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence is performing the action of sending. 

Description: "πεμψει" (pemsei) can be used in a variety of contexts, such as when someone is talking about sending a message, a package, or a person to a specific location. For example, "Θα πεμψει το γράμμα στον φίλο του." (Tha pemsei to gramma ston filo tou.) translates to "He will send the letter to his friend."
