Word: πεπληρωμαι

Etymology:
The word πεπληρωμαι comes from the Greek root words πληρόω (plēróō) meaning "to fill" and the prefix πε- (pe-) which indicates a completed action. Therefore, πεπληρωμαι means "I have been filled" or "I am full".

Grammar:
πεπληρωμαι is a verb in the perfect tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is the first person singular form of the verb πληρόω (plēróō) which means "to fill". In this form, it indicates that the subject (I) has been filled or completed an action in the past and is currently in a state of being filled.

Description:
πεπληρωμαι is a word that describes a state of being filled or completed. It can be used in a literal sense, such as being filled with food or drink, or in a figurative sense, such as being filled with joy or satisfaction. In the New Testament, it is often used to describe being filled with the Holy Spirit.
