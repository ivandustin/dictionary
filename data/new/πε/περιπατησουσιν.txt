Word: περιπατησουσιν

Etymology: περιπατησουσιν is a Greek verb in the future tense, third person plural form of the word περιπατέω (peripatéō), which means "to walk around, to stroll". The word is derived from the combination of two Greek words: περί (peri), meaning "around" and πατέω (pateō), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: περιπατησουσιν is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the tense, mood, and person. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people.

Description: περιπατησουσιν is a word that describes a future action of a group of people walking around or strolling. It is commonly used in Greek literature and everyday language to describe a group of people taking a leisurely walk or wandering around.
