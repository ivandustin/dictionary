Word: περιηιρειτο

Etymology: The word περιηιρειτο is derived from the Greek verb περιαιρέω (periaireo), which means "to take away, to remove". The prefix περι- (peri-) means "around" or "about", while the verb αιρέω (aireo) means "to take, to lift up".

Grammar: περιηιρειτο is a third-person singular, middle voice, imperfect tense form of the verb περιαιρέω (periaireo). In Greek, the middle voice is used to indicate that the subject is performing the action on itself or for its own benefit. The imperfect tense is used to describe an ongoing or repeated action in the past.

Description: περιηιρειτο can be translated as "he/she/it was removing/taking away (for himself/herself/itself)". It describes an ongoing or repeated action of removing or taking away something for one's own benefit. The context in which the word is used will determine what is being removed or taken away.
