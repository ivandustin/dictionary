Word: πειθομεθα

Etymology: πειθομεθα is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πείθω (peíthō), which means "to persuade" or "to convince." The suffix -ομαι (-omai) indicates that the verb is in the middle or passive voice, meaning that the subject is being acted upon or is acting on itself.

Grammar: πειθομεθα is a present tense, middle or passive voice, indicative mood verb. It is conjugated in the first person plural, meaning "we are persuaded" or "we are convincing ourselves."

Description: πειθομεθα is a verb that describes the act of being persuaded or convincing oneself. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal decision-making or in interpersonal relationships.
