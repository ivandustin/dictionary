Word: περιπατεί (peripatei)

Etymology: The word περιπατεί comes from the Greek verb περιπατώ (peripato), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb πατέω (pateo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: περιπατεί is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb περιπατώ (peripato), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: περιπατεί means "he/she/it walks" in Greek. It is a common verb used to describe the action of walking or strolling. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone's behavior or way of life, such as "he walks the path of righteousness".
