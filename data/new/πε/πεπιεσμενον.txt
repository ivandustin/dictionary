Word: πεπιεσμένον

Etymology:
The word πεπιεσμένον is derived from the Greek verb πιέζω (piezo), which means "to press" or "to squeeze". The prefix πεπι- (pepi-) is a perfect tense form of the verb, indicating that the action of pressing or squeezing has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
πεπιεσμένον is a neuter singular adjective in the nominative case. It is used to describe a noun that is being pressed or squeezed. In Greek, adjectives must agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify.

Description:
πεπιεσμένον describes something that has been pressed or squeezed. It can be used to describe physical objects that have been compressed, such as a squeezed fruit or a compressed piece of paper. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who is under pressure or stress, or a situation that is tense or difficult.
