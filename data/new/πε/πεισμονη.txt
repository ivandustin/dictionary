Word: πεισμονη

The word πεισμονη (peismonē) is derived from the Greek verb πείθω (peithō), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The suffix -μονη (-monē) is a noun-forming suffix that denotes a state or condition. Therefore, πεισμονη can be translated as "persuasion" or "conviction".

Description:
πεισμονη refers to the state of being convinced or persuaded. It can also mean stubbornness or obstinacy, especially when someone is unwilling to change their beliefs or opinions.
