Word: πετεινων

The word πετεινων (peteinon) is derived from the Greek word πετεινός (peteinós), which means "bird". The word is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, which means "to fly".

Grammar:
πετεινων is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, which indicates possession or relationship to another noun or pronoun in the sentence. In this form, it means "of birds".

Description:
πετεινων is a word that refers to birds in general. It is often used in scientific or technical contexts, such as in the study of ornithology. The word can also be used in a more general sense to refer to any type of bird, whether wild or domesticated.
