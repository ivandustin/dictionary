Word: περιελειν

Etymology:
The word περιελειν comes from the Greek root words περι- (peri-), meaning "around" or "about," and ελείν (elein), meaning "to take away" or "to remove." 

Grammar:
περιελειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the aorist tense, which indicates that the action it describes occurred in the past. The verb is also in the active voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is performing the action. 

Description:
περιελειν means "to remove completely" or "to take away entirely." It is often used in a figurative sense to describe the complete removal of something, such as a problem or obstacle. The word can also be used in a literal sense to describe the physical removal of something, such as a tumor or growth.
