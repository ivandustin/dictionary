Word: πειραζομενος

Etymology: 
The word πειραζομενος comes from the Greek verb πειράζω (peirázo), which means "to test" or "to try". The suffix -μενος (-menos) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to someone who is currently being tested or tried.

Grammar:
πειραζομενος is a present participle in the passive voice, meaning that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. In this case, the subject is not specified, but it could be translated as "being tested" or "being tried".

Description:
πειραζομενος can be translated as "being tested", "being tried", or "being tempted". It implies a sense of struggle or challenge, as if the subject is being put to the test. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in religious or moral contexts where someone is being tempted to do something wrong, or in a scientific context where a hypothesis is being tested.
