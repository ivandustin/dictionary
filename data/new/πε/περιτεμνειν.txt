Word: περιτέμνειν

Etymology:
The word περιτέμνειν comes from the Greek root words περί (peri) meaning "around" and τέμνειν (temnein) meaning "to cut". It is derived from the ancient Greek practice of circumcision, which involved cutting around the foreskin of the penis.

Grammar:
περιτέμνειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν to the stem περιτεμν-.

Description:
περιτέμνειν means "to circumcise" in Greek. It refers to the surgical removal of the foreskin of the penis, which is a common practice in many cultures and religions. The word can also be used metaphorically to mean "to cut around" or "to trim".
