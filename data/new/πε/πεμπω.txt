Word: πεμπω

Etymology:
The word πεμπω (pempo) is derived from the Greek root word πεμπ-, which means "to send". It is related to other Greek words such as πεμπτος (fifth), πεμπτη (fifth), and πεμπομαι (to be sent).

Grammar:
πεμπω is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense, active voice, and indicative mood. The conjugation of the verb is as follows:

- Πεμπω (I send)
- Πεμπεις (You send)
- Πεμπει (He/She/It sends)
- Πεμπομεν (We send)
- Πεμπετε (You all send)
- Πεμπουσι(They send)

Description:
The word πεμπω is commonly used in Greek to refer to the act of sending something or someone. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as sending a message, sending a package, or sending a person on a mission. The word can also be used metaphorically, such as in the phrase "πεμπω τις ευχες μου" (I send my wishes), which means to express one's good wishes to someone. Overall, πεμπω is a versatile verb that is used frequently in everyday Greek conversation.
