Word: περιεσχεν

Etymology:
The word περιεσχεν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word περι-, which means "around" or "surrounding," and the verb εἶχον, which means "to have" or "to hold." Therefore, περιεσχεν can be translated as "to have surrounded" or "to have enclosed."

Grammar:
περιεσχεν is the third person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb περιέχω (periechō), which means "to surround" or "to enclose." In this form, it indicates that the action of surrounding or enclosing was completed in the past.

Description:
περιεσχεν is a verb that describes an action of enclosing or surrounding something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as enclosing a garden with a fence, or in a figurative sense, such as surrounding oneself with friends and family for support. The word is often used in the context of military tactics, where soldiers may surround an enemy to gain an advantage in battle.
