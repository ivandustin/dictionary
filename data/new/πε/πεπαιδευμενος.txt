Word: πεπαιδευμένος

Etymology:
The word πεπαιδευμένος (pepaideumenos) is a past participle of the Greek verb παιδεύω (paideuo), which means "to educate" or "to train". The prefix πεπα- (pepa-) indicates that the action of the verb has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
πεπαιδευμένος is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. The word can be translated as "educated" or "trained".

Description:
πεπαιδευμένος is a word that describes someone who has received a formal education or training. It can refer to someone who has been educated in a specific field or discipline, or someone who has received a general education. The word carries a positive connotation, suggesting that the person is knowledgeable and well-informed.
