Word: πειράζεται

Etymology: The word πειράζεται comes from the Greek verb πειράζω (peirázo), which means "to try", "to test", or "to tempt". The suffix -ται (-tai) is a reflexive ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is also the object of the verb.

Grammar: πειράζεται is a third-person singular present tense form of the verb πειράζω. It is in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- Present tense: πειράζεται
- Imperfect tense: πειραζόταν (peirazótan)
- Future tense: θα πειραχθεί (tha peirachthí)
- Aorist tense: πειράχτηκε (peiráchti̱ke)

Description: The word πειράζεται is often used to describe someone who is being tempted or tested. It can also be used to describe someone who is being bothered or annoyed. In some contexts, it can mean "to be in trouble" or "to be at risk". The word is commonly used in everyday conversation in Greece.
