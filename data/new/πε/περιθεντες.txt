Word: περιθεντες

Etymology:
The word περιθεντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word περιτιθημι (peritithemi), which means "to place around" or "to put on". The prefix περι- (peri-) means "around" or "about", while the verb ending -εντες (-entes) is a participle ending that indicates the action is ongoing or continuous.

Grammar:
περιθεντες is a present participle in the active voice, which means it describes an ongoing action that is being performed by the subject of the sentence. In Greek, participles can function as adjectives, adverbs, or even nouns, depending on the context in which they are used.

Description:
περιθεντες can be translated as "putting on" or "placing around". It is often used in the context of clothing or armor, as in the phrase "περιθεντες την πανοπλιαν" (putting on the armor). However, it can also be used more broadly to describe any action of placing or putting something around or on something else.
