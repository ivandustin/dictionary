Word: περιπατεις

Etymology:
The word περιπατεις comes from the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripatéō), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The word is derived from the combination of the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb πατέω (patéō), meaning "to walk".

Grammar:
περιπατεις is a second-person singular present indicative active form of the Greek verb περιπατέω. It is used to address one person who is currently walking or strolling around.

Description:
περιπατεις is a Greek verb that describes the act of walking or strolling around. It can be used in a literal sense to describe physical movement, or in a figurative sense to describe someone who is aimlessly wandering or not making progress towards a goal. The word can also be used to describe a leisurely or relaxed pace of walking.
