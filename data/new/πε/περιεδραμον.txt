Word: περιεδραμον

Etymology:
The word περιεδραμον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: περι (peri) meaning "around" and εδραμον (edramon) meaning "I ran". Therefore, the literal translation of περιεδραμον is "I ran around".

Grammar:
περιεδραμον is a first-person singular, aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb εδραμον (edramon). The aorist tense in Greek refers to a past action that is viewed as a single event, completed at a specific point in time. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact or reality.

Description:
περιεδραμον is a verb that describes the action of running around something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as running around a track or a building, or in a figurative sense, such as running around in circles trying to solve a problem. The word is not commonly used in modern Greek, but it can be found in ancient Greek literature.
