Word: περιβεβλημένους

Etymology:
The word περιβεβλημένους is derived from the Greek verb περιβάλλω (periballō), which means "to wrap around" or "to cover". The word is formed by adding the suffix -μένος (-menos), which is a participle suffix indicating a passive action. Therefore, περιβεβλημένους means "those who have been wrapped around" or "those who have been covered".

Grammar:
περιβεβλημένους is a plural masculine accusative participle in the Greek language. It is declined as follows:

Case: Accusative
Gender: Masculine
Number: Plural
Part of Speech: Participle

Description:
περιβεβλημένους is used in the New Testament of the Bible in the book of Hebrews 12:1. The verse reads, "Therefore, since we are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses, let us throw off everything that hinders and the sin that so easily entangles. And let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us". In this context, περιβεβλημένους refers to the believers who are surrounded by a great cloud of witnesses, which are the faithful people who have gone before them. The word emphasizes the idea of being encompassed or surrounded by something.
