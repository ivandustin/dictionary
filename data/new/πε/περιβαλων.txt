Word: περιβαλων

Etymology: περι- (peri-, "around") + βάλλω (ballō, "to throw") → περιβάλλω (peribállō, "to throw around, encircle"). The word περιβαλων (peribalōn) is the present participle of περιβάλλω, meaning "encircling" or "surrounding".

Grammar: In Greek, περιβαλων is a masculine noun in the nominative case. It is the present participle of the verb περιβάλλω, which means "to encircle" or "to surround". As a participle, it can function as an adjective, describing a noun or pronoun.

Description: περιβαλων is a word that describes something that is encircling or surrounding. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as a wall or a fence that encircles a property, or it can be used in a more abstract sense, such as describing the way that a person's thoughts or emotions can surround them. The word can also be used metaphorically, to describe the way that a person or group can encircle or surround another person or group, either physically or figuratively.
