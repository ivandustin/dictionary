Word: πειρασθητε

Etymology:
The word πειρασθητε is a verb in the aorist passive imperative form of the Greek verb πειράζω (peirázo), which means "to test" or "to try". The verb is derived from the noun πεῖρα (peîra), which means "trial" or "test".

Grammar:
πειρασθητε is a second-person plural verb in the aorist passive imperative form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a past action that is viewed as a single event, while the passive voice indicates that the subject of the verb is being acted upon. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests.

Description:
The word πειρασθητε is a command or request for a group of people to be tested or tried. It can be interpreted in different ways depending on the context in which it is used. It could be a call to action, urging people to face challenges and overcome obstacles, or it could be a warning to be prepared for difficult times ahead. Overall, the word conveys a sense of resilience and determination in the face of adversity.
