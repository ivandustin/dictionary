Word: περιβλεψαμενοι

Etymology: περι- (peri-, around) + βλέπω (blépo, to see) + -μενοι (-menoi, suffix indicating passive voice and plural subject) = περιβλεψάμενοι (periblepsámenoi, having been seen around)

Grammar: περιβλεψαμενοι is the past participle form of the verb περιβλέπω (periblépo, to look around). It is in the masculine plural nominative case, agreeing with the subject of the sentence.

Description: περιβλεψαμενοι means "having looked around" or "having observed from all sides". It is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone who has thoroughly examined a situation or issue.
