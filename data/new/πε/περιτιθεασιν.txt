Word: περιτιθεασιν

Etymology: 
The word περιτιθεασιν is derived from the Greek verb περιτιθημι (peritithemi), which means "to place around" or "to put on". The suffix -ασιν (-asin) is a third person plural present indicative active ending, indicating that the word is in the present tense and refers to multiple people performing the action.

Grammar:
περιτιθεασιν is a third person plural present indicative active form of the Greek verb περιτιθημι. In this form, the verb is conjugated to agree with the subject, which is plural and in the present tense. The verb is also in the active voice, indicating that the subject is performing the action.

Description:
περιτιθεασιν is a Greek verb that means "they are placing around" or "they are putting on". It is a present tense verb, indicating that the action is currently happening. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing someone putting on clothes or placing decorations around a room.
