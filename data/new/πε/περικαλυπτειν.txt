Word: περικαλύπτειν

Etymology: 
The word περικαλύπτειν comes from the Greek prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around" or "surrounding," and the verb καλύπτω (kalýpto), meaning "to cover" or "to conceal." Therefore, περικαλύπτειν means "to cover or conceal completely."

Grammar:
περικαλύπτειν is a Greek verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem περικαλυπτ- (perikalýpt-).

Description:
περικαλύπτειν is a verb that describes the action of completely covering or concealing something. It can be used in a literal sense, such as covering an object with a cloth or hiding something from view, or in a figurative sense, such as concealing one's emotions or intentions. The word can also be used in a passive sense, as in "to be covered or concealed completely."
