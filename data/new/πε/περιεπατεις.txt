Word: περιεπατείς

Etymology:
The word περιεπατείς (periepateis) is derived from the Greek words περί (peri) meaning "around" and επατέω (epateo) meaning "to walk". It is a present active indicative second person singular form of the verb περιπατέω (peripateo) which means "to walk around, to stroll".

Grammar:
περιεπατείς is a second person singular form of the present tense in Greek. It is used to address one person who is currently walking around or strolling.

Description:
περιεπατείς is a Greek verb that describes the action of walking around or strolling. It can be used to describe someone who is taking a leisurely walk or someone who is walking around with a purpose. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is indecisive or unsure of what to do, as if they are "walking around in circles".
