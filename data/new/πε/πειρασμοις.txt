Word: πειρασμοις

Etymology: πειρασμοις is a Greek word derived from the root word πειράζω (peirázo), which means "to test" or "to tempt". The suffix -οις (-ois) indicates that the word is in the dative plural form.

Grammar: πειρασμοις is a noun in the dative plural form. In Greek, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: πειρασμοις refers to temptations or trials that test one's faith or character. It is often used in a religious or moral context, and can refer to both external and internal struggles. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the temptations faced by Jesus in the wilderness.
