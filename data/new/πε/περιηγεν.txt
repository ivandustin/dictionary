Word: περιηγεν

Etymology:
The word περιηγεν (periegen) is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: περί (peri) meaning "around" and ἡγέομαι (hegeomai) meaning "to lead, guide, or direct". Therefore, the word περιηγεν can be translated as "to lead or guide around".

Grammar:
περιηγεν is a third-person singular aorist active indicative verb in Ancient Greek. It is formed by adding the suffix -εν (-en) to the stem περιηγ- (periege-). The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action.

Description:
περιηγεν is a verb that describes the act of leading or guiding someone or something around a particular place or area. It can be used in a literal sense, such as leading someone on a tour or guiding a group of people through a city, or in a figurative sense, such as guiding someone through a difficult situation or helping them navigate a complex problem. The word has a connotation of expertise and authority, suggesting that the person doing the guiding is knowledgeable and experienced in the area they are leading others through.
