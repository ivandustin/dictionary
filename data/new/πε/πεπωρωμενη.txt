Word: πεπωρωμένη

Etymology: The word πεπωρωμένη comes from the Greek verb πωρόω (pōróō), which means "to make hard, to petrify". The prefix πεπ- (pep-) indicates that the action is completed or perfect.

Grammar: πεπωρωμένη is the feminine form of the past participle of the verb πωρόω. It is in the nominative singular form and agrees in gender and number with the noun it modifies.

Description: πεπωρωμένη describes something that has become hard or petrified. It can be used to describe physical objects, such as rocks or wood, or figuratively to describe a person's heart or emotions that have become hardened or unfeeling.
