The word "πειλατου" (peilatou) is a Greek word that is derived from the name of the Roman governor Pontius Pilate, who is famously known for presiding over the trial and crucifixion of Jesus Christ. 

In terms of grammar, "πειλατου" is a genitive form of the name Pilate, which is used to indicate possession or association. 

In terms of description, "πειλατου" is not a commonly used word in modern Greek language. It is mostly used in religious contexts or historical discussions related to the Roman Empire.
