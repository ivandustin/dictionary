Word: περιπατούντος

Etymology: The word περιπατούντος comes from the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The suffix -ούντος (-ountos) indicates the present participle form, which means "walking around" or "strolling".

Grammar: περιπατούντος is the genitive singular form of the present participle of the verb περιπατέω. In Greek grammar, the genitive case is used to indicate possession or relationship between two nouns. In this case, περιπατούντος can be translated as "of the one who is walking around" or "of the one who is strolling".

Description: περιπατούντος is a Greek word that describes someone who is currently walking around or strolling. It is often used in literature and poetry to create vivid imagery and convey a sense of movement or activity. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is aimlessly wandering or going through life without a clear purpose.
