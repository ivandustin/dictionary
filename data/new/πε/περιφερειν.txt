Word: περιφερειν

Etymology: περι- (peri-, meaning "around") + φέρειν (pherein, meaning "to carry") = περιφέρειν (peripherin, meaning "to carry around")

Grammar: περιφερειν is a verb in the present tense, active voice, infinitive mood. It is formed by adding the suffix -ειν (-ein) to the stem περιφερ- (peripher-).

Description: περιφερειν means "to carry around" or "to transport". It can also mean "to circulate" or "to spread". This verb is often used in a figurative sense, such as "to spread rumors" or "to circulate news".
