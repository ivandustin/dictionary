Word: πεμπομενοις

Etymology: πεμπομενοις is a Greek word that comes from the verb πέμπω (pempo), which means "to send". The suffix -μενοις (-menois) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to something that is currently happening.

Grammar: πεμπομενοις is a present participle in the dative plural form. It is used to describe the recipients of the action of sending. In English, it would be translated as "to those who are being sent".

Description: πεμπομενοις is a word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to refer to the disciples of Jesus who were sent out to preach and spread the gospel. It can also be used more generally to refer to anyone who is being sent somewhere.
