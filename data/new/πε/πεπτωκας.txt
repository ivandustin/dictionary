Word: πεπτωκας

Etymology: πεπτωκας is the second person singular perfect active indicative form of the Greek verb πίπτω (pipto), which means "to fall". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *pet-, which means "to fly, rush, fall".

Grammar: πεπτωκας is a verb in the perfect tense, which indicates a completed action in the past with ongoing effects in the present. It is in the active voice, which means that the subject (you) is performing the action. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: πεπτωκας means "you have fallen" in Greek. It is a common verb in Greek and is used in various contexts, such as describing a physical fall or a moral failure. The perfect tense emphasizes the completed action of falling and the ongoing effects of that action in the present.
