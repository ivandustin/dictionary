Word: πεπιστευκοσιν

Etymology: πεπιστευκοσιν is the present perfect tense, third person plural form of the Greek verb πιστεύω (pisteuó), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The word comes from the root πιστός (pistos), which means "faithful" or "trustworthy".

Grammar: πεπιστευκοσιν is a verb form in the present perfect tense, which indicates an action that began in the past and continues up to the present. The third person plural form indicates that the subject is a group of people who have believed or have faith in something.

Description: πεπιστευκοσιν can be translated as "they have believed" or "they have faith". It is a word that expresses a strong conviction or trust in something, often used in a religious or spiritual context. The present perfect tense suggests that the belief or faith is ongoing and has not yet been completed.
