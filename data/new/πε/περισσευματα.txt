Word: περισσεύματα

Etymology: The word περισσεύματα comes from the Greek verb περισσεύω (perissevo), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound". The suffix -ματα (-mata) is a plural noun ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple instances of something.

Grammar: περισσεύματα is a neuter plural noun in Greek. In English, it can be translated as "surplus", "excess", or "leftovers". It is often used in the context of food or resources that are left over after a particular use or consumption.

Description: περισσεύματα refers to something that is left over or in excess. It can be used to describe food that is leftover after a meal, resources that are unused or surplus, or even ideas or thoughts that are not fully developed or utilized. The word has a connotation of abundance or excess, but can also imply waste or inefficiency if the surplus is not put to good use.
