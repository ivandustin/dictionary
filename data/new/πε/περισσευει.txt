Word: περισσεύει

Etymology: The word περισσεύει comes from the Greek verb περισσεύω (perisseúo), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound". It is derived from the adjective περισσός (perissós), which means "abundant" or "excessive".

Grammar: περισσεύει is a present tense verb in the third person singular form. It is conjugated from the verb περισσεύω (perisseúo), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description: περισσεύει is used to describe a situation where there is an excess or abundance of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as in economics, where it refers to a surplus of goods or services, or in everyday language, where it can describe an excess of anything. For example, "Περισσεύουν τα χρήματα" (Perissevoyn ta chrimata) means "There is an excess of money".
