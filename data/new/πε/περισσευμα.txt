Word: περισσευμα

Etymology:
The word περισσευμα (perisseuma) comes from the Greek verb περισσεύω (perisseuo), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound". The suffix -μα (-ma) is added to the verb stem to form a noun, indicating the result or product of the action.

Grammar:
περισσευμα is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases. The genitive form of the word is περισσεύματος (perisseumatos), the dative form is περισσεύματι (perisseumati), and the accusative form is also περισσευμα.

Description:
περισσευμα refers to something that is in excess or surplus, beyond what is needed or required. It can be used to describe a surplus of food, money, or other resources. In a broader sense, it can also refer to an abundance of anything, such as ideas, emotions, or experiences. The word can have both positive and negative connotations, depending on the context in which it is used.
