Word: περιαγειν

Etymology:
The word περιαγειν comes from the Greek root words περί (peri) meaning "around" and αγειν (agein) meaning "to lead". Therefore, περιαγειν means "to lead around" or "to go around".

Grammar:
περιαγειν is a verb in the Greek language. It is in the present tense and active voice. The conjugation of the verb is as follows:

- Εγώ περιάγω (ego periago) - I lead around
- Εσύ περιάγεις (esi periageis) - You lead around
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό περιάγει (aftos/afti/afto periagei) - He/She/It leads around
- Εμείς περιαγούμε (emeis periagoume) - We lead around
- Εσείς περιαγείτε (eseis periageite) - You (plural) lead around
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά περιαγουν (aftoi/aftes/afta periagoun) - They lead around

Description:
περιαγειν is a verb that describes the action of leading or going around something. It can be used in various contexts, such as leading someone around a city or showing them a particular place. It can also be used metaphorically to describe going around a problem or obstacle. Overall, περιαγειν is a versatile verb that can be used in many different situations.
