Word: περιπατητε

Etymology: περιπατητε comes from the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripatéo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". It is derived from the words περί (peri), meaning "around", and πατέω (pateo), meaning "to walk".

Grammar: περιπατητε is the second person plural imperative form of the verb περιπατέω (peripatéo). Imperative forms are used to give commands or make requests. In this case, περιπατητε is telling a group of people to "walk around" or "stroll".

Description: περιπατητε is a command in Greek, telling a group of people to walk around or stroll. It is often used in a casual or leisurely context, such as when suggesting a group take a walk together.
