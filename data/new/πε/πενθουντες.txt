Word: πενθοῦντες (pen-thoo-oon-tes)

Etymology: The word πενθοῦντες comes from the Greek verb πενθέω (pentheo), which means "to mourn" or "to grieve". The suffix -οῦντες (-ountes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently mourning or grieving.

Grammar: πενθοῦντες is a present participle in the masculine plural form. It is derived from the verb πενθέω, which is in the present tense. The word functions as an adjective, describing people who are currently mourning or grieving.

Description: πενθοῦντες is a Greek word that refers to people who are currently mourning or grieving. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, such as in the Bible or in hymns. The word emphasizes the emotional state of those who are mourning, and can be used to describe a group of people who are united in their grief.
