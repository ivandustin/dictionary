Word: περισσεύειν

Etymology: The word περισσεύειν comes from the Greek verb περισσεύω (perisseúō), which means "to be in excess" or "to abound". It is derived from the adjective περισσός (perissós), which means "abundant" or "excessive".

- Singular: περισσεύω (perisseúō) - I am in excess/abound
- Dual: περισσεύομεν (perisseúomen) - We two are in excess/abound
- Plural: περισσεύομεν (perisseúomen) - We are in excess/abound

Description: περισσεύειν is a verb that describes a state of abundance or excess. It can be used to describe a surplus of something, such as food or money, or a surplus of emotions, such as joy or love. The verb can also be used in a negative sense to describe an excess of something undesirable, such as stress or anxiety.
