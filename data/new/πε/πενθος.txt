Word: πενθος

Etymology:
The word πενθος (penthos) is derived from the Greek word πένθος (penthos), which means "grief" or "sorrow". It is believed to have originated from the Proto-Indo-European root *pent-, which means "to suffer".

Grammar:
In Greek, πενθος is a neuter noun. Its singular form is πένθος (penthos) and its plural form is πένθη (penthē). It is declined like other neuter nouns in Greek.

Description:
πενθος is a Greek word that refers to deep sorrow or grief. It is often used in the context of mourning or lamentation. In ancient Greek literature, it was a common theme in tragedies and epic poetry. The word is still used in modern Greek to describe intense feelings of sadness or mourning.
