Word: περιεπατει

Etymology:
The word περιεπατει comes from the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripateo), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The prefix περι- (peri-) means "around" or "about", while πατέω (pateo) means "to walk".

Grammar:
περιεπατει is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It is derived from the verb περιπατέω (peripateo), which is a regular verb in Greek.

Description:
περιεπατει means "he/she/it walks around" or "he/she/it strolls". It is often used to describe someone who is leisurely walking or wandering around without a specific destination in mind. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is indecisive or uncertain, as if they are "walking around" in their thoughts or actions.
