Word: πετρον

The word πετρον (petron) is derived from the Greek word πέτρα (petra), which means "rock" or "stone". It is a masculine noun in the Greek language.

In Greek, πετρον is a second declension noun. It is in the accusative case, which is used to indicate the direct object of a verb. The singular form of the word is πέτρος (petros), while the plural form is πέτροι (petroi).

πετρον is a Greek word that refers to a small stone or pebble. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the context of Jesus Christ's teachings.
