Word: πεντακισχίλιοι

Etymology: The word πεντακισχίλιοι (pentakischilioi) is a compound word in Greek, formed by the combination of πέντε (pente) meaning "five" and χίλιοι (chilioi) meaning "thousand". Therefore, the literal meaning of πεντακισχίλιοι is "five thousand".

Grammar: πεντακισχίλιοι is an adjective in Greek, used to describe a noun in the plural form. It agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, πεντακισχίλιοι άνθρωποι (pentakischilioi anthropoi) means "five thousand people", while πεντακισχίλιες γυναίκες (pentakischilies gynaikes) means "five thousand women".

Description: πεντακισχίλιοι is a numerical adjective used to express the quantity of something. It is commonly used in Greek to refer to a large number of people, objects, or events. In ancient Greece, it was often used to describe the size of armies or crowds.
