Word: περιεπατησαμεν

Etymology: περι- (peri-, around) + επατησαμεν (epatēsamen, walked)

Grammar: περιεπατησαμεν is the first person plural aorist indicative active form of the Greek verb περιπατέω (peripatéō), which means "to walk around" or "to stroll". The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past.

Description: περιεπατησαμεν describes a group of people walking around or strolling in the past. It is a specific moment in time and implies that the action was completed. The word can be used in various contexts, such as describing a leisurely walk in a park or a group of people exploring a new city.
