Word: πειραζοντες

Etymology: The word πειραζοντες comes from the Greek verb πειράζω (peirázo), which means "to test" or "to try". The suffix -οντες (-ontes) is a present participle ending, indicating that the word refers to people who are currently testing or trying something.

Grammar: πειραζοντες is a present participle in the active voice, masculine gender, and nominative case. It is used to describe people who are currently testing or trying something.

Description: πειραζοντες can be translated to "testing" or "trying" in English. It is often used in a figurative sense, to describe people who are testing or trying someone's patience, faith, or loyalty. In the New Testament of the Bible, the word is used to describe the actions of Satan, who is said to be constantly testing and tempting people.
