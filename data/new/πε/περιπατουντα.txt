The word "περιπατούντα" (peripatounta) is a present participle in Greek, derived from the verb "περιπατώ" (peripato), which means "to walk". The word can be translated as "walking" or "the one who walks". 

In terms of grammar, "περιπατούντα" is in the present tense and active voice, and it agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies. For example, "ο περιπατούντα άνθρωπος" (o peripatounta anthropos) means "the walking man", while "η περιπατούσα γυναίκα" (i peripatousa gynaika) means "the walking woman".

Overall, "περιπατούντα" is a versatile word that can be used in a variety of contexts to describe someone who is walking or in motion.
