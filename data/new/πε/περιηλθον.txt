Word: περιηλθον

Etymology:
The word περιηλθον is a Greek verb that comes from the combination of two words: περι (peri) meaning "around" or "about" and ἔρχομαι (erchomai) meaning "to come". Therefore, the literal meaning of περιηλθον is "to come around" or "to come about".

Grammar:
περιηλθον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb περιέρχομαι (perierchomai), which means "to go around" or "to pass around". The aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, without any indication of the duration or repetition of the action.

Description:
περιηλθον is a word that appears in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of Luke (22:43). In this passage, it is used to describe an angel who appeared to Jesus in the Garden of Gethsemane, after he had prayed to God. The word suggests that the angel came around or approached Jesus, possibly to comfort him or deliver a message. Overall, περιηλθον is a word that conveys movement and action, specifically the idea of coming around or passing around something or someone.
