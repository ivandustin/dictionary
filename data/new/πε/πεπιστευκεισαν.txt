Word: πεπιστευκεισαν

Etymology: The word πεπιστευκεισαν is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πιστεύω (pisteuo), which means "to believe" or "to have faith". The prefix πεπιστευ- (pepisteu-) is the perfect tense form of the verb, indicating that the action of believing has been completed in the past.

Grammar: πεπιστευκεισαν is the third person plural form of the perfect tense of the Greek verb πιστεύω. It is a transitive verb, meaning it takes a direct object. In this form, it means "they have believed" or "they had faith".

Description: πεπιστευκεισαν is a word that expresses a completed action of belief or faith in the past. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts, such as in the New Testament of the Bible. The word emphasizes the certainty and completeness of the belief or faith that has been expressed.
