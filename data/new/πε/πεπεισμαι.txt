Word: πεπεισμαι

Etymology:
The word πεπεισμαι (pepeismai) is a Greek verb that comes from the root word πείθω (peitho), which means "to persuade" or "to convince". The prefix πεπ- (pep-) indicates that the action of persuasion has already taken place, and the suffix -μαι (-mai) indicates that the subject is in a passive voice.

Grammar:
πεπεισμαι (pepeismai) is a first-person singular present passive indicative form of the verb πείθω (peitho). It can be translated to "I have been persuaded" or "I am convinced". The verb is in the present tense, indicating that the persuasion or conviction is ongoing, and in the passive voice, indicating that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
πεπεισμαι (pepeismai) is a word that describes a state of being convinced or persuaded. It implies that the subject has been influenced by someone or something else and has come to a certain belief or conclusion. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in personal relationships, politics, or religion. It is often used to express a sense of certainty or confidence in one's beliefs or opinions.
