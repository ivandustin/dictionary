Word: περιεβάλον

Etymology:
The word περιεβάλον comes from the Greek verb περιβάλλω (periballō), which means "to surround" or "to encircle". It is composed of the prefix περι- (peri-), meaning "around", and the verb βάλλω (ballō), meaning "to throw".

Grammar:
περιεβάλον is the aorist tense of the Greek verb περιβάλλω (periballō), which means "they surrounded" or "they encircled". It is in the third person plural.

Description:
περιεβάλον is a verb that describes an action of surrounding or encircling something or someone. It can be used in a literal sense, such as when describing a group of people surrounding a building or a person, or in a figurative sense, such as when describing a situation where someone is surrounded by problems or difficulties.
