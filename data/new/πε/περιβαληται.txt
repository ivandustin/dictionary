Word: περιβαληται

Etymology: περι- (peri-, around) + βάλλω (ballō, to throw) + -ται (-tai, third person plural middle/passive ending) = περιβαληται (they are surrounded)

Grammar: περιβαληται is a third person plural middle/passive form of the verb περιβάλλω (peribállō), which means "to surround" or "to encircle". In this form, the subject is being acted upon or is acting upon themselves.

Description: περιβαληται is a Greek verb form that describes a group of people or things being surrounded or encircled. It can also be translated as "they are enveloped" or "they are encompassed". This word is commonly used in literature and poetry to describe the setting or atmosphere of a scene.
