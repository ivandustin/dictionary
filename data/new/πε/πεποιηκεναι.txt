Word: πεποιηκεναι

Etymology:
The word πεποιηκεναι is a Greek verb in the perfect tense, which means "to have made" or "to have done". It is derived from the root word ποιέω (poiéo), which means "to make" or "to do".

Grammar:
πεποιηκεναι is a perfect tense verb in the Greek language. It is conjugated in the third person singular, which means "he/she/it has made/done". The verb is formed by adding the prefix πε- (pe-) to the root word ποιέω (poiéo), and then adding the appropriate ending for the third person singular.

Description:
πεποιηκεναι is a verb that describes an action that has already been completed in the past. It is often used to indicate that something has been made or done, and that the action is now finished.
