Word: γνωθι (gnōthi)

Etymology:
The word γνωθι (gnōthi) is derived from the Greek verb γιγνώσκω (gignōskō), which means "to know" or "to recognize." The imperative form of this verb is γνῶθι (gnōthi), which means "know" or "be aware."

Grammar:
γνωθι (gnōthi) is a second-person singular imperative verb in ancient Greek. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone, telling them to "know" or "be aware" of something.

Description:
The word γνωθι (gnōthi) is famous for its appearance on the front of the Temple of Apollo at Delphi in ancient Greece. The phrase "γνῶθι σεαυτόν" (gnōthi seauton) was inscribed on the temple, which means "know thyself." This phrase was a reminder to those who visited the temple to understand their own limitations and to strive for self-knowledge. Today, the phrase is still used as a philosophical and psychological concept, encouraging individuals to understand themselves and their place in the world.
