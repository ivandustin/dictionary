Word: γνοντες

Etymology:
The word γνοντες is a form of the Greek verb γιγνώσκω (gignósko), which means "to know" or "to understand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃-, which means "to know".

Grammar:
γνοντες is the present participle form of the verb γιγνώσκω (gignósko) in the masculine plural nominative case. It is used to describe a group of people who are currently knowing or understanding something.

Description:
The word γνοντες can be translated to "knowing" or "understanding" in English. It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe the disciples of Jesus who had knowledge and understanding of his teachings. In modern Greek, the word γνώση (gnósi) is used to mean "knowledge".
