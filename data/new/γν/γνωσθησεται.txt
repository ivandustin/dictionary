Word: γνωσθησεται

Etymology:
The word γνωσθησεται is a Greek verb in the future tense, passive voice, and indicative mood. It is derived from the root word γιγνώσκω (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand."

Grammar:
In Greek, verbs have different forms depending on the tense, voice, and mood. γνωσθησεται is in the future tense, which indicates that the action will happen at some point in the future. It is in the passive voice, which means that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action. Finally, it is in the indicative mood, which is used to make a statement or ask a question.

Description:
The word γνωσθησεται can be translated to mean "it will be known" or "it will be understood." It is often used in a religious or philosophical context to suggest that some truth or knowledge will eventually be revealed or discovered.
