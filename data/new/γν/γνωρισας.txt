Word: γνωρισας

Etymology: γνωρισας is a verb in the aorist tense of the Greek language. It comes from the root word γνωρίζω (gnorízo), which means "to make known" or "to introduce." 

Grammar: γνωρισας is a second-person singular aorist active indicative form of the verb γνωρίζω. In this form, it means "you made known" or "you introduced." 

Description: γνωρισας is a past tense verb that describes an action of making something known or introducing someone. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and can be used in various contexts, such as meeting someone for the first time or introducing a new idea.
