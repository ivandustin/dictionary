Word: γνωσθεντες

Etymology: 
The word γνωσθεντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know" or "to understand". The verb form γνωσθεντες is the aorist passive participle of γινώσκω, which means "having been known" or "having been understood".

Grammar:
γνωσθεντες is a participle in the aorist tense, passive voice, and masculine gender. It is used as an adjective to modify a noun or pronoun. In this form, it indicates that the subject has been known or understood by someone else.

Description:
The word γνωσθεντες is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the book of Acts (17:31). In this context, it is translated as "having given assurance" or "having provided proof". The passage reads, "Because he has fixed a day on which he will judge the world in righteousness by a man whom he has appointed; and of this he has given assurance to all by raising him from the dead." Here, γνωσθεντες refers to the fact that God has provided proof of his judgment by raising Jesus from the dead.
