Word: γνωρισθηι

Etymology:
The word γνωρισθηι is a Greek verb in the aorist passive tense. It is derived from the root word γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know" or "to recognize". The addition of the suffix -θηι (-thii) indicates that the action of the verb is in the passive voice, meaning that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Grammar:
As mentioned, γνωρισθηι is a Greek verb in the aorist passive tense. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The passive voice indicates that the subject of the sentence is being acted upon. 

Description:
The word γνωρισθηι can be translated to English as "was known" or "was recognized". It is often used in the context of introducing someone to another person or group of people. For example, "Τον Πέτρο τον γνώρισαν όλοι στο πάρτι" (Ton Petro ton gnorisan oli sto parti) means "Everyone at the party recognized Peter". 

Overall, γνωρισθηι is a useful verb in Greek for describing past actions in the passive voice.
