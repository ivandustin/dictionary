Word: γνωστοις

Etymology:
The word γνωστοις (gnōstois) is a Greek word that comes from the root word γιγνώσκω (gignōskō), which means "to know" or "to recognize." The suffix -τοις (-tois) is a dative plural ending, indicating that the word is referring to multiple people.

Grammar:
In Greek, γνωστοις is a dative plural noun. It is often used as the indirect object of a verb, indicating to whom or for whom an action is being done. For example, in the sentence "Δίδωσι τοις γνωστοις τας βίβλους" (Dídōsi tois gnōstois tas bívlous), which means "He gives the books to his acquaintances," γνωστοις is the indirect object of the verb δίδωσι (dídōsi).

Description:
The word γνωστοις refers to acquaintances or people who are known to someone. It is often used in Greek literature and everyday conversation to describe people who are familiar to the speaker. The word can also be used to describe a group of people who are known to each other, such as a group of friends or colleagues.
