Word: γνωσεσθε

Etymology:
The word γνωσεσθε is a Greek verb in the future tense, which means "you will know" or "you will understand". It is derived from the root word γιγνώσκω (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand".

Grammar:
γνωσεσθε is a second-person plural future indicative active verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the future tense suffix -σθε (-sthe) to the root word γιγνώσκω (ginosko). The verb is conjugated as follows:

γνωσεσθε (gnosese) - you will know/understand (plural)

Description:
The word γνωσεσθε is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 8:32, where Jesus says "And you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free." The word emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in achieving freedom. It is also used in other contexts to express the idea of gaining knowledge or understanding about something.
