Word: γνωστα

Etymology: 
The word γνωστα comes from the Greek verb γινώσκω (ginósko), which means "to know". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵneh₃- ("to know").

Grammar:
γνωστα is the neuter plural form of the adjective γνωστός (gnostós), which means "known" or "famous". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. Therefore, γνωστα is used to modify neuter plural nouns.

Description:
γνωστα is a Greek adjective that is commonly used to describe things that are well-known or famous. It can be used to describe people, places, events, or things. For example, one might say "Τα γνωστά αξιοθέατα της Αθήνας" (Ta gnosta axiotheata tis Athinas), which means "The famous landmarks of Athens".
