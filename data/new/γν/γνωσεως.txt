The word "γνωσεως" (gnoseos) is a Greek noun that means "knowledge" or "understanding". It is derived from the verb "γιγνώσκω" (ginosko), which means "to know" or "to understand". In terms of grammar, "γνωσεως" is in the genitive case, which is used to indicate possession or relationship. 

In Greek philosophy, "gnosis" (knowledge) was often associated with spiritual enlightenment and the attainment of wisdom. The term was also used in early Christian literature to refer to a mystical or intuitive knowledge of God. 

Overall, "γνωσεως" is a word that conveys the idea of knowledge and understanding, particularly in a deeper or more profound sense.
