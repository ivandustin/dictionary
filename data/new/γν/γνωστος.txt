Word: γνωστός

Etymology:
The word γνωστός (gnostos) comes from the Greek verb γιγνώσκω (gignosko), which means "to know". The suffix -τός (-tos) is added to the verb stem to form an adjective, which means "known" or "famous".

Grammar:
γνωστός is a masculine singular adjective in the nominative case. It can also be declined in other cases and genders to agree with the noun it modifies.

Description:
γνωστός is an adjective that describes something or someone that is known or famous. It can be used to describe a person, a place, an event, or an object. For example, in the sentence "Ο Σωκράτης ήταν γνωστός φιλόσοφος" (O Sokratis itan gnostos filosofos), γνωστός describes Sokratis as a famous philosopher.
