Etymology: The word "χειρον" (cheiron) comes from the Greek word "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵʰes-.

Grammar: In Greek, "χειρον" is a noun in the neuter gender. It is the accusative singular form of the word "χείρ" (cheir), which means "hand". 

Description: "Χειρον" is used to refer to the hand or hands. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate power, control, or agency.
