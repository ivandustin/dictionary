Etymology: The word "χειμάρρου" (χειμάρρου in Greek) comes from the combination of the words "χείμαρρος" (cheímarros) and "ουρά" (ourá), which mean "torrent" and "tail" respectively. It refers to a fast-flowing stream or river.

Grammar: In Greek, "χειμάρρου" is a noun in the genitive case. It is used to describe the tail or the flow of a torrent or a river.

Description: "Χειμάρρου" is a term used to describe the tail or the flow of a fast-flowing stream or river. It emphasizes the dynamic and powerful nature of the water as it rushes downstream.
