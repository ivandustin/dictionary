Etymology: The word "χειραγωγούς" comes from the Greek words "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand," and "ἀγωγός" (agogos), meaning "leader" or "guide." 

Grammar: "Χειραγωγούς" is a noun in the plural form. In Greek grammar, it belongs to the third declension and is classified as a masculine noun. 

Description: "Χειραγωγούς" refers to "handcuffs" in Greek. It is used to describe a device or tool used to restrain a person's hands by locking them together. Handcuffs are commonly used by law enforcement officers to detain individuals and prevent them from escaping or causing harm.
