Etymology: The word "χειρογραφον" comes from the Greek words "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand," and "γράφω" (grapho), meaning "to write." It literally translates to "handwritten."

Grammar: "Χειρογραφον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek grammar, neuter nouns have the same form for both the nominative and accusative cases.

Description: "Χειρογραφον" refers to a handwritten document or manuscript. It is used to describe any text or writing that is produced by hand, rather than being printed or typed.
