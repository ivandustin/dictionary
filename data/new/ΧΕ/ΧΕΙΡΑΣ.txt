Etymology: The word "χειρας" (cheiras) comes from the Greek word "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand". 

Grammar: "Χειρας" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the genitive case, singular form. 

Description: "Χειρας" refers to the hand in Greek. It can be used to describe the physical body part or metaphorically to represent power, action, or control.
