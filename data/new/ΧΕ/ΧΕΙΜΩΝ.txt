Etymology: The word "χειμών" (cheimón) comes from the Ancient Greek word "χεῖμα" (cheîma), meaning "winter" or "storm."

Grammar: In Greek, "χειμών" is a masculine noun of the second declension. It is in the nominative case and singular form. The plural form is "χειμῶνες" (cheimônes).

Description: "Χειμών" refers to the season of winter. It is characterized by cold temperatures, shorter days, and often includes snow, ice, and storms. In Greek mythology, "χειμών" was personified as the god of winter.
