Etymology: The word "χειροποίητος" (cheiropoíitos) comes from the Greek words "χείρ" (cheír), meaning "hand," and "ποιητός" (poiitós), meaning "made." Therefore, "χειροποίητος" literally translates to "handmade."

Grammar: "Χειροποίητος" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. For example, if the noun is feminine, the adjective would be "χειροποίητη" (cheiropoíiti) in the singular form.

Description: "Χειροποίητος" is used to describe something that is made by hand, rather than by a machine or mass-produced. It emphasizes the craftsmanship and individuality of the item. This term is commonly used to describe handmade crafts, artwork, clothing, and other products.
