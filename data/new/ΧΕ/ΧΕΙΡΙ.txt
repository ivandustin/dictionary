Etymology: The word "χειρι" (cheiri) comes from the Greek word "χείρ" (cheir), which means "hand". 

Grammar: "Χειρι" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the singular form and belongs to the feminine gender. 

Description: "Χειρι" refers to the hand, which is the part of the human body located at the end of the arm. It is used for grasping, holding, and manipulating objects. The hand is composed of fingers, a palm, and a wrist. It plays a crucial role in various activities such as writing, eating, and gesturing.
