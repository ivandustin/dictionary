Etymology: The word "χειλεών" comes from the Greek word "χεῖλος" (cheilos), meaning "lip".

Grammar: "Χειλεών" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the plural form and belongs to the third declension.

Description: "Χειλεών" refers to the lips in Greek. It is used to describe the fleshy, movable, and sensitive parts that surround the mouth opening. Lips play a crucial role in speech, eating, and expressing emotions.
