Etymology: The word "χειρα" (cheira) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the ancient Greek word "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand."

Grammar: In Greek, "χειρα" (cheira) is a noun. It is in the feminine gender and is in the plural form.

Description: "Χειρα" (cheira) refers to hands in Greek. It is used to describe the body part that is located at the end of the arm, consisting of the palm, fingers, and thumb. Hands are essential for various activities, such as grasping, touching, and manipulating objects.
