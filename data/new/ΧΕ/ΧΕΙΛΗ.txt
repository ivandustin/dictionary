Etymology: The word "χειλή" (cheilē) comes from the Greek word "χεῖλος" (cheilos), which means "lip".

Grammar: In Greek, "χειλή" is a noun in the plural form. It is the plural of "χεῖλος" (cheilos).

Description: "Χειλή" refers to the lips, which are the soft, movable parts that surround the mouth opening. Lips are important for various functions, such as speaking, eating, and expressing emotions. They are also associated with beauty and attractiveness.
