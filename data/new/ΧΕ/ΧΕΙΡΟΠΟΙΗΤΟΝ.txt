Etymology: The word "χειροποίητον" comes from the Greek words "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand," and "ποιητός" (poietos), meaning "made." Therefore, "χειροποίητον" literally translates to "handmade."

Grammar: "Χειροποίητον" is a neuter singular adjective in the Greek language. It agrees with a neuter singular noun in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Χειροποίητον" is used to describe something that is made by hand, rather than by a machine or mass-produced. It emphasizes the craftsmanship and individuality of the item.
