Etymology: The word "χειλεσίν" comes from the Greek word "χεῖλος" (cheilos), meaning "lip."

Grammar: "Χειλεσίν" is a noun in the accusative plural form. It is the accusative plural of "χεῖλος" (cheilos).

Description: "Χειλεσίν" refers to "lips" in Greek. It is used to describe the pair of fleshy, movable parts forming the opening of the mouth in humans and many animals. Lips are important for speech, eating, and expressing emotions.
