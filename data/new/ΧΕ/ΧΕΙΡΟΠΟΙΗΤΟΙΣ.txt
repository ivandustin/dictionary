Etymology: The word "χειροποίητος" comes from the Greek words "χείρ" (cheir), meaning "hand," and "ποίητος" (poietos), meaning "made." Therefore, "χειροποίητος" literally translates to "handmade."

Grammar: "Χειροποίητος" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. So, if the noun is feminine, plural, or in a different case, the adjective will change accordingly.

Description: "Χειροποίητος" is used to describe something that is made by hand, rather than by a machine or through mass production. It emphasizes the craftsmanship and individuality of the item. This term is commonly used to describe handmade crafts, artwork, clothing, and other products.
