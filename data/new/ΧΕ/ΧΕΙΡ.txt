Etymology: The word "χειρ" (cheir) comes from the Ancient Greek word "χείρ" (cheír), meaning "hand".

Grammar: In Greek, "χειρ" is a feminine noun in the singular form.

Description: "Χειρ" refers to the anatomical part of the body that is attached to the arm, consisting of the palm, fingers, and thumb. It is used for various activities such as grasping, holding, and manipulating objects.
