Word: ταπεινωθητε

Etymology:
The word ταπεινωθητε comes from the Greek verb ταπεινόω (tapeinóō), which means "to humble" or "to lower oneself". The verb is derived from the adjective ταπεινός (tapeinós), which means "humble" or "lowly".

Grammar:
ταπεινωθητε is a second person plural passive imperative verb in Greek. It is formed by adding the passive ending -θητε to the stem ταπεινω-. The imperative mood is used to give commands or make requests, and the passive voice indicates that the action is being done to the subject.

Description:
ταπεινωθητε is a command to be humble or to lower oneself. It is often used in a religious or spiritual context, as a call to submit oneself to a higher power or to show humility towards others. The word can also be used more generally to encourage modesty or selflessness.
