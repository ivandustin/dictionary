Etymology: The word ταρσον comes from the Greek verb τέμνω (témno), which means "to cut." 

Grammar: ταρσον is a neuter noun in the singular form. 

Description: In ancient Greece, ταρσον was a common household item used for cutting and chopping food. It was typically made of wood or stone and had a flat surface. Today, the word ταρσον is rarely used in modern Greek, and the more common word for cutting board is κοπάνα (kopána).
