Word: ταπεινοις

Etymology:
The word ταπεινοις (tapeinois) is derived from the Greek word ταπεινός (tapeinos), which means "humble" or "lowly". It is derived from the root word ταπεινόω (tapeinoó), which means "to make low" or "to humble".

Grammar:
ταπεινοις is the dative plural form of ταπεινός. In Greek grammar, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description:
ταπεινοις is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in James 4:6, which reads: "God opposes the proud but shows favor to the humble (ταπεινοις)". The word is used to describe those who are humble, lowly, and meek. It is often associated with the virtues of modesty, humility, and selflessness.
