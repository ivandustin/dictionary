ταφην (tafēn) is a Greek word that means "burial" or "funeral". 

Etymology: The word ταφην comes from the Greek verb τάφω (táphō), which means "to bury". 

Grammar: ταφην is a noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes ταφέντα (tafénta). 

Description: ταφην refers to the act of burying a dead body or the ceremony that accompanies it. In ancient Greece, funerals were an important part of the culture and were often accompanied by rituals and ceremonies. The word ταφην can also refer to the place where a dead body is buried, such as a cemetery or a tomb.
