ταχα (takha) is a Greek adverb that means "supposedly" or "apparently". It is derived from the ancient Greek word "τάχα" (tacha), which means "quickly" or "swiftly". In terms of grammar, ταχα is an adverb that modifies verbs or adjectives. It is often used to express uncertainty or doubt about a statement or situation. For example, "Ταχα θα έρθει αύριο" (Takha tha erthei avrio) means "He/she will supposedly come tomorrow".
