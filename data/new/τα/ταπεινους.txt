Word: ταπεινους

Etymology:
The word ταπεινους (tapeinous) is derived from the Greek word ταπεινός (tapeinós), which means "humble" or "lowly". It is derived from the verb ταπεινόω (tapeinóō), which means "to make low" or "to humble".

Grammar:
ταπεινους is an adjective in the masculine plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are humble or lowly.

Description:
ταπεινους is a word that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe those who are humble and meek. It is a word that is associated with a lack of pride and a willingness to serve others. In modern Greek, the word is still used to describe someone who is humble or modest.
