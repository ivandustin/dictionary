ταμείον (taméion) is a Greek word that means "cash register" or "till". 

Etymology: The word ταμείον comes from the ancient Greek word τάμιον (támion), which means "treasury" or "storehouse". 

Grammar: ταμείον is a neuter noun in Greek, and its plural form is ταμεῖα (taméia). 

Description: ταμείον is a common word in modern Greek, used to refer to a cash register or till in a store or business. It can also refer to a financial institution or treasury. In ancient times, τάμιον was used to refer to a storehouse or treasury where valuables were kept.
