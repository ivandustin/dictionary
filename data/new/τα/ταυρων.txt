ταυρων (taurōn) is a Greek noun that means "bull" or "steer". It is derived from the Greek word ταῦρος (tauros), which also means "bull". In terms of grammar, ταυρων is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. It can also be declined in other cases and numbers, such as ταύρου (taurou) in the genitive singular and ταύροις (taurois) in the dative plural. In Greek mythology, the bull was often associated with the god Zeus and was a symbol of strength and fertility.
