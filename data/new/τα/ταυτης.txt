ταυτης (tautēs) is a Greek word that is the genitive singular form of the pronoun "this". It is derived from the demonstrative pronoun "οὗτος" (houtos), which means "this" or "this one". In Greek grammar, ταυτης is used to indicate possession or ownership, and it can be translated as "of this" or "of this one". 

In terms of grammar, ταυτης is a second declension feminine noun, and its stem is ταυτη-. It is declined like other second declension nouns, with the genitive singular form being ταυτης. 

Overall, ταυτης is a useful word in Greek for indicating possession or ownership, and it is commonly used in both written and spoken Greek.
