Word: ταραχος

Etymology:
The word ταραχος (tarachos) is derived from the Greek verb ταράσσω (tarasso), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate." The suffix -ος (-os) is a common Greek suffix used to form adjectives.

Grammar:
ταραχος is a masculine noun in the nominative singular form. In the plural form, it becomes ταραχοι (tarachoi). It belongs to the second declension of Greek nouns.

Description:
ταραχος refers to a state of disturbance, agitation, or confusion. It can be used to describe a chaotic situation or a state of unrest. It can also refer to a person who is agitated or disturbed. In modern Greek, it is often used to describe a state of anxiety or nervousness.
