Word: ταλαιπωρία

Etymology:
The word ταλαιπωρία (talai-pōría) comes from the Greek words ταλαίπωρος (talai-pōros), meaning "suffering" or "enduring hardship," and -ία (-ía), a suffix used to form abstract nouns. The word has been in use in Greek since ancient times.

Grammar:
ταλαιπωρία is a feminine noun in Greek, and its nominative singular form is ταλαιπωρία. Its genitive singular form is ταλαιπωρίας (talai-pōrías), and its accusative singular form is ταλαιπωρίαν (talai-pōrían). In the plural, the word is ταλαιπωρίες (talai-pōríes) in the nominative and accusative cases, and ταλαιπωριών (talai-pōrión) in the genitive case.

Description:
ταλαιπωρία is a Greek word that refers to a state of suffering or hardship. It can be used to describe physical or emotional pain, as well as difficult or trying circumstances. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe the struggles and challenges of life. It is a common word in Greek literature and is often used in philosophical and religious texts to explore the nature of suffering and the human condition.
