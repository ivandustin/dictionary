Word: ταυταις

Etymology: ταυταις is a form of the Greek word ταῦτα (tauta), which means "these" or "these things". It is a feminine dative plural form of the pronoun.

Grammar: In Greek, ταυταις is a feminine dative plural form of the pronoun ταῦτα (tauta). It is used to indicate the indirect object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Description: ταυταις is a word that is used in Greek to refer to a group of feminine objects or things. It is often used in conjunction with other words to describe or identify a specific group of objects. For example, ταυταις ταῖς ἡμέραις (tautais tais hemerais) means "in these days" or "during this time period".
