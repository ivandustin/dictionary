Word: ταλαιπωρίαις

Etymology:
The word ταλαιπωρίαις (talai-pōríais) is derived from the Greek words ταλαιπωρία (talai-pōría) meaning "hardship" or "suffering" and the suffix -αις (-ais) which is used to form the plural of feminine nouns.

Grammar:
ταλαιπωρίαις is a feminine noun in the plural form. It is declined according to the second declension of Greek nouns. The nominative singular form is ταλαιπωρία (talai-pōría).

Description:
ταλαιπωρίαις refers to a state of hardship, suffering, or distress. It can be used to describe physical or emotional suffering, as well as difficult or trying circumstances. The word is often used in a figurative sense to describe the struggles and challenges of life.
