ταρσεύς (tarseus) is a Greek adjective that means "of or pertaining to the tarsus," which is the ankle bone in humans and other animals. The word comes from the Greek word ταρσός (tarsos), which means "flat surface" or "sole of the foot." In grammar, ταρσεύς is a third-declension adjective, meaning that its endings change depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.
