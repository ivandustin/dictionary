ταπεινός (tapeinos) is a Greek adjective that means humble, lowly, or meek. It comes from the Greek word ταπείνωσις (tapeinosis), which means humiliation or abasement. In grammar, ταπεινός is a first/second declension adjective, meaning its form changes depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies. For example, ταπεινός becomes ταπεινή (tapeinē) in the feminine singular nominative case, and ταπεινοί (tapeinoi) in the masculine plural nominative case. In description, ταπεινός is often used to describe someone who is modest, unassuming, or not arrogant.
