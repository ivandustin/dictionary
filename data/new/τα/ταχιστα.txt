ταχιστα (takhista) is an adverb in Greek that means "very quickly" or "at great speed". 

Etymology: The word ταχιστα comes from the Greek adjective ταχύς (takhys), which means "fast" or "quick". The suffix -ιστα (-ista) is added to the end of the adjective to form the adverb, indicating that the action is done quickly or at great speed.

Grammar: ταχιστα is an adverb, which means it modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb to indicate the speed or manner of the action. It is an invariable word, meaning it does not change form based on gender, number, or case.

Description: ταχιστα is a useful word in Greek for expressing the idea of doing something quickly or at great speed. It can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing how fast someone is running, how quickly a task is being completed, or how rapidly a situation is changing.
