Word: ταφος

Etymology:
The word ταφος (tafos) comes from the Ancient Greek word τάφος (táphos), which means "grave" or "tomb". It is derived from the verb τάπτω (táptō), which means "to bury".

Grammar:
In Greek, ταφος is a masculine noun of the second declension. Its nominative singular form is ταφός (tafós), and its genitive singular form is τάφου (táfou).

Description:
ταφος refers to a burial place or tomb where a dead body is laid to rest. In Ancient Greece, tombs were often elaborate structures built to honor the deceased and were sometimes decorated with sculptures or inscriptions. Today, the word is still used in modern Greek to refer to a grave or burial site.
