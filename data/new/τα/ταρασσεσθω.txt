Word: ταρασσεσθω

Etymology:
The word ταρασσεσθω comes from the Greek verb ταράσσω (tarássō), which means "to disturb" or "to agitate". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *terə-, which means "to shake" or "to tremble".

Grammar:
ταρασσεσθω is a present passive imperative verb in Greek. It is in the third person singular form and is used to give a command or instruction. The passive voice indicates that the subject is being acted upon rather than performing the action.

Description:
ταρασσεσθω is a strong and forceful word that conveys a sense of disturbance or agitation. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe emotional or mental turmoil, as well as physical shaking or trembling. The word can be used in a variety of contexts, from describing the effects of an earthquake to expressing the inner turmoil of a person's thoughts and feelings.
