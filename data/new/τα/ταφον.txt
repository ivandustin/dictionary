ταφον (tafon) is a Greek noun that means "tomb" or "grave". 

Etymology: The word ταφον comes from the Greek verb τάφω (tafo), which means "to bury". 

Grammar: ταφον is a masculine noun in the singular form. 

Description: In ancient Greece, tombs were often elaborate structures built to honor the dead. They were decorated with sculptures, paintings, and inscriptions that told the story of the deceased. The word ταφον is still used today to refer to burial sites, both ancient and modern.
