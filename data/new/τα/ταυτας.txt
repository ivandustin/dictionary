Word: ταυτας

Etymology:
The word ταυτας is derived from the Greek word ταῦτα (tauta), which means "these" or "those". It is a demonstrative pronoun in the feminine accusative plural form.

Grammar:
In Greek grammar, ταυτας is a feminine accusative plural form of the demonstrative pronoun ταῦτα (tauta). It is used to refer to a group of feminine nouns that are the object of the sentence.

Description:
ταυτας is a Greek word that is used to refer to a group of feminine nouns. It is commonly used in sentences to indicate the object of the sentence. For example, "Πήρε ταυτας τις βιβλίες" (Pire tautas tis vivlies) means "She took these books".
