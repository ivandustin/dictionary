Word: ικανά

Etymology: ικανά comes from the Greek word "ικανός" (ikanós), which means "able" or "capable".

Grammar: ικανά is the plural form of the adjective ικανός (ikanós), which means "able" or "capable". It is used to describe a group of people or things that are capable of doing something.

Description: ικανά is an adjective that is used to describe a group of people or things that are capable of doing something. For example, if you say "Οι μαθητές είναι ικανά να λύσουν αυτό το πρόβλημα" (Oi mathites einai ikana na lysoun auto to provlima), it means "The students are capable of solving this problem".
