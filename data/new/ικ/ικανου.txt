The word "ικανου" (ikanou) is a Greek adjective that means "capable" or "able". 

Etymology: The word comes from the Greek root "ικανός" (ikanós), which means "able" or "capable". 

Grammar: "Ικανου" is the genitive singular form of the adjective "ικανός". In Greek, adjectives agree in gender, number, and case with the noun they modify. 

Description: The word "ικανου" is often used to describe someone who has the ability or skill to do something. It can also be used to describe something that is sufficient or satisfactory for a particular purpose. For example, "Ο άνθρωπος είναι ικανός να επιλύσει το πρόβλημα" (O ánthropos eínai ikanós na epilýsei to próvlima) means "The man is capable of solving the problem".
