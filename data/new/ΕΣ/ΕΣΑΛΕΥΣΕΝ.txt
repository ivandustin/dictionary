Etymology: The word "εσαλευσεν" comes from the Greek verb "σαλεύω" (salevo), which means "to shake" or "to move back and forth". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, and the suffix "-εν" indicates the third person singular.

Grammar: "εσαλευσεν" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened.

Description: "εσαλευσεν" describes an action of shaking or moving back and forth that was performed by a third person singular subject in the past. It can be used in various contexts to describe physical or metaphorical shaking or movement.
