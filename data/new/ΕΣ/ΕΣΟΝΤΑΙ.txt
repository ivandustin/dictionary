Etymology: The word "εσονται" (esontai) comes from the Greek verb "εἰμί" (eimi), which means "to be." It is the future tense, third person plural form of the verb.

Grammar: "Εσονται" (esontai) is a verb in the future tense, third person plural form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form refers to multiple individuals or objects.

Description: "Εσονται" (esontai) is used to express future actions or states of being that will occur by multiple individuals or objects. It can be translated as "they will be" in English.
