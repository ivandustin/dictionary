Etymology: The word "εσπερα" (espera) comes from the Greek word "ἑσπέρα" (hespera), which means "evening" or "dusk". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂éspus, meaning "evening" or "night".

Grammar: "Εσπερα" is a feminine noun in Greek. It belongs to the first declension and has the nominative singular form "εσπερα". In the plural form, it becomes "εσπερες" (esperes).

Description: "Εσπερα" refers to the period of time between sunset and nightfall, commonly known as evening or dusk. It is often associated with the end of the day and the beginning of the night. In Greek culture, "εσπερα" holds symbolic significance and is often used in literature and poetry to evoke a sense of tranquility, beauty, and the transition from day to night.
