Etymology: The word "εστηκοτες" (estekotes) comes from the Greek verb "στήκω" (stiko), which means "to stand". The suffix "-τες" (-tes) is added to indicate the plural form.

Grammar: "Εστηκοτες" is a noun in the nominative case and plural number. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their case, number, and gender.

Description: "Εστηκοτες" refers to a group of people or objects that are standing. It can be used to describe a static position or a temporary state of standing.
