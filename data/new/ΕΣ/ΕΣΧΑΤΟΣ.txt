Etymology: The word "εσχατος" (eschatos) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the root word "εξω" (exo), meaning "outside," and the suffix "-τος" (-tos), which denotes a state or condition. Therefore, "εσχατος" (eschatos) can be translated as "outermost" or "extreme."

Grammar: In Greek, "εσχατος" (eschatos) is an adjective. It can be used to describe the farthest or most distant position or condition of something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe something as the ultimate or final state.

Description: "Εσχατος" (eschatos) is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to the physical location of something, such as the outermost point of a boundary or the farthest distance from a center.
