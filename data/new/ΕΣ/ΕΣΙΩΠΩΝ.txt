Etymology: The word "εσιωπων" (esio̱pōn) comes from the Greek verb "σιωπάω" (siōpáō), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet."

Grammar: "Εσιωπων" is the present participle form of the verb "σιωπάω" (siōpáō) in the third person singular. It is used to describe someone or something that is currently silent or keeping quiet.

Description: "Εσιωπων" refers to a person or thing that is silent or not speaking at the present moment. It can be used to describe someone who is intentionally quiet, someone who is not speaking due to shyness or fear, or something that is silent or not making any noise.
