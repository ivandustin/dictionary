Etymology: The word "εσφαγης" (esphagis) comes from the Greek verb "σφάζω" (sfazo), which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher". 

Grammar: "Εσφαγης" is a noun in the Greek language. It is in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. 

Description: "Εσφαγης" refers to a person who slaughters or butchers animals for food. It is commonly used to describe someone who works in a slaughterhouse or is involved in the meat industry.
