Etymology: The word "εσθιετε" comes from the Greek verb "εσθίω" (esthio), which means "to eat" or "to consume".

Grammar: "Εσθιετε" is the second person plural form of the present tense in Greek. It is used when addressing a group of people and means "you all eat" or "you all consume".

Description: "Εσθιετε" is a command or an imperative form of the verb "εσθίω". It is used to give instructions or orders to a group of people, telling them to eat or consume something.
