The word "εσιγησεν" is a Greek verb in the aorist tense, third person singular form. It comes from the root word "σιγάω" (sigao), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet." The aorist tense indicates a completed action in the past. Therefore, "εσιγησεν" translates to "he/she/it was silent" or "he/she/it kept quiet."
