Etymology: The word "εσχεν" (eschēn) comes from the Greek verb "ἔχω" (echō), which means "to have" or "to possess". It is the aorist tense form of the verb.

Grammar: "Εσχεν" is the third person singular form of the aorist tense in the indicative mood of the verb "ἔχω" (echō). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a completed action in the past.

Description: "Εσχεν" is used to describe an action that was completed in the past. It indicates that someone or something had or possessed something at a specific point in time.
