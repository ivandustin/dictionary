Etymology: The word "εσφαγμένου" comes from the Greek verb "σφάζω" (sfázo), which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher". The suffix "-μένου" (-menou) is added to the verb stem to indicate the past participle form, resulting in "εσφαγμένου" (esfagménoy), which means "slaughtered" or "butchered".

Grammar: "Εσφαγμένου" is the masculine singular form of the past participle in Greek. It agrees with a masculine singular noun in gender, number, and case.

Description: "Εσφαγμένου" is an adjective that describes something or someone that has been slaughtered or butchered. It is often used in the context of describing animals that have been killed for food.
