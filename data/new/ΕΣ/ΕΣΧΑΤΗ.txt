Etymology: The word "εσχάτη" (escháti) comes from the Greek adjective "ἔσχατος" (éskhatos), which means "last" or "final".

Grammar: "Εσχάτη" is a feminine noun in the nominative case. It is the singular form of the word, and it means "last" or "final" in Greek.

Description: "Εσχάτη" is used to refer to the last or final thing in a series or sequence. It can be used to describe the last person, the last event, or the last item in a list. It is often used to emphasize the finality or ultimate nature of something.
