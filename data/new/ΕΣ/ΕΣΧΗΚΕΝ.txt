Etymology: The word "εσχηκεν" comes from the Greek verb "εσχηκα" (eschēka), which is the perfect tense form of the verb "εχω" (echō), meaning "to have" or "to hold."

Grammar: "Εσχηκεν" is the third person singular form of the verb "εσχηκα" in the perfect tense. In Greek grammar, the perfect tense is used to indicate an action that has been completed in the past and has ongoing relevance to the present.

Description: "Εσχηκεν" is a Greek word that means "he/she/it has/had." It is used to express possession or the completion of an action in the past.
