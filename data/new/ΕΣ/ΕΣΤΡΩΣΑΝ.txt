Etymology: The word "εστρωσαν" (estrōsan) comes from the Greek verb "στρώνω" (strōnō), which means "to spread" or "to lay down". The prefix "ε-" indicates the past tense, and the suffix "-αν" indicates the third person plural.

Grammar: "Εστρωσαν" is the past tense, third person plural form of the verb "στρώνω" (strōnō). In Greek grammar, verbs change their form based on tense, person, and number. The past tense indicates that the action occurred in the past, and the third person plural indicates that the subject of the verb is multiple people or things.

Description: "Εστρωσαν" is a verb in the Greek language that means "they spread" or "they laid down". It is used to describe an action performed by a group of people or things in the past.
