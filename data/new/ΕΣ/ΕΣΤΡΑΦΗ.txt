Etymology: The word "εστραφη" (estravi) comes from the Greek verb "στρέφω" (strefó), which means "to turn" or "to change direction". The prefix "ε-" (e-) indicates a change or transformation.

Grammar: "Εστραφη" is the past tense form of the verb "στρέφω" (strefó) in the third person singular. It is used to indicate that someone or something turned or changed direction in the past.

Description: "Εστραφη" is a Greek word that describes an action of turning or changing direction. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the movement of a person, an object, or even an abstract concept.
