Etymology: The word "εσθιετω" (esthiētō) comes from the Greek verb "εσθιω" (esthiō), which means "to eat" or "to consume."

Grammar: "Εσθιετω" is a verb in the imperative mood and the third person singular form. It is derived from the aorist tense of the verb "εσθιω" (esthiō), which means "let him/her/it eat" or "let him/her/it consume."

Description: "Εσθιετω" is a command or an instruction in Greek, telling someone to eat or consume something. It is used to give a directive or an order to someone to perform the action of eating.
