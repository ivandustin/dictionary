Etymology: The word "εσχηκότα" (eschēkota) is the past participle of the Greek verb "έχω" (ekho), which means "to have". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₁- ("to hold, possess").

Grammar: "Εσχηκότα" is the neuter plural form of the past participle, used to describe plural nouns of neuter gender. In Greek grammar, the past participle agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies.

Description: "Εσχηκότα" is an adjective that means "having" or "having possessed". It is used to describe a state of possession or having experienced something in the past.
