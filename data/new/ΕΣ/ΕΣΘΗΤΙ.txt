Etymology: The word "εσθητι" (esthēti) comes from the Greek verb "εσθημι" (esthēmi), which means "to perceive" or "to sense." It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₁es- ("to be, exist").

Grammar: "Εσθητι" (esthēti) is the third person singular form of the verb "εσθημι" (esthēmi) in the present tense. It is used to describe someone or something that perceives or senses.

Description: "Εσθητι" (esthēti) refers to the act of perceiving or sensing. It can be used to describe any form of perception, whether it is through the five senses (sight, hearing, taste, smell, touch) or through other means of sensing.
