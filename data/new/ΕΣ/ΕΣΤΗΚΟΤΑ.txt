Etymology: The word "εστηκοτα" (estikota) comes from the Greek verb "στέκω" (steko), which means "to stand". The suffix "-τα" (-ta) is added to form the noun form.

Grammar: "Εστηκοτα" (estikota) is a neuter noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The neuter gender is used for objects and concepts that are neither masculine nor feminine. The plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of the noun.

Description: "Εστηκοτα" (estikota) refers to things that are standing or in a standing position. It can be used to describe objects, people, or any other entities that are upright or not in a sitting or lying position.
