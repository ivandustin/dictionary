Etymology: The word "εσωθή" (esothi) comes from the Greek verb "σώζω" (sozo), which means "to save" or "to rescue". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *seh₂- ("to hold, protect").

Grammar: "Εσωθή" is a verb in the aorist tense, passive voice, and third person singular form. It is used to describe an action of being saved or rescued.

Description: "Εσωθή" is a Greek word that refers to the act of being saved or rescued. It implies being protected from harm or danger.
