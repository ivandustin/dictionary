Etymology: The word "εσχατον" (eschaton) comes from the Greek language. It is derived from the word "εσχατος" (eschatos), meaning "last" or "final."

Grammar: "Εσχατον" is a neuter noun in the singular form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. Neuter nouns typically end in "-ον" in the singular form.

Description: "Εσχατον" refers to the concept of the "end" or "final" state of something. It is often used in religious or philosophical contexts to describe the ultimate destiny or outcome of the world or humanity.
