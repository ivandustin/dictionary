Etymology: The word "εσταυρώσαν" (estavrosan) comes from the Greek verb "σταυρόω" (stavroo), which means "to crucify". It is derived from the noun "σταυρός" (stavros), meaning "cross".

Grammar: "Εσταυρώσαν" is the third person plural, aorist tense, indicative mood form of the verb "σταυρόω" (stavroo). In Greek grammar, the aorist tense is used to express a past action that is viewed as a whole, without specifying its duration or repetition. The indicative mood indicates a statement of fact.

Description: "Εσταυρώσαν" is a Greek verb that means "they crucified" in English. It refers to the act of executing someone by nailing or binding them to a cross, a form of punishment used in ancient times.
