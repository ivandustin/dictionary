Etymology: The word "εσθιητε" comes from the Greek verb "εσθίω" (esthío), which means "to eat" or "to consume".

Grammar: "Εσθιητε" is the second person plural form of the verb "εσθίω" in the present tense, imperative mood. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to eat or consume something.

Description: "Εσθιητε" is a word that is used in Greek to command or instruct a group of people to eat or consume something. It is commonly used in situations where food is being served or offered.
