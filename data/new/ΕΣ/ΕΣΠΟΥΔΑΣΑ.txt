Etymology: The word "εσπουδασα" (espoudasa) comes from the Greek verb "σπουδάζω" (spoudazo), which means "to study" or "to make an effort." The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, so "εσπουδασα" translates to "I studied" or "I made an effort."

Grammar: "Εσπουδασα" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "σπουδάζω" (spoudazo). In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate different tenses, moods, and persons. The "-ασα" ending in "εσπουδασα" indicates the past tense and the first person singular.

Description: "Εσπουδασα" is a Greek word that means "I studied" or "I made an effort." It is used to describe an action that was completed in the past. This word is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts to express the act of studying or making an effort in the past.
