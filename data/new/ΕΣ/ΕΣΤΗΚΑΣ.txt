Etymology: The word "εστηκας" (estekas) comes from the Greek verb "στέκω" (steko), which means "to stand". It is derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *steh₂-.

Grammar: "Εστηκας" is the second person singular form of the verb "στέκω" (steko) in the present tense. It is used to address someone directly and means "you stand" or "you are standing".

Description: "Εστηκας" is a verb in the Greek language that describes the action of standing or being in a standing position. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and can be used to ask someone if they are standing or to give a command to stand.
