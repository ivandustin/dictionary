Etymology: The word "εστρηνιασεν" comes from the Greek verb "στρηνιάω" (strēniáō), which means "to revel" or "to carouse". The prefix "ε-" is added to indicate the past tense, and the suffix "-εν" indicates the third person singular form.

Grammar: "Εστρηνιασεν" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. In Greek grammar, verbs change their endings to indicate tense, person, and number. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened.

Description: "Εστρηνιασεν" describes an action that someone or something did in the past. It can be translated as "he/she/it reveled" or "he/she/it caroused" in English.
