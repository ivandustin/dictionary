Etymology: The word "μνησθω" (mnēsthō) comes from the Greek verb "μνάομαι" (mnaomai), which means "to remember" or "to recall."

Grammar: "Μνησθω" is a verb in the aorist tense, imperative mood, and active voice. It is the second person singular form of the verb.

Description: "Μνησθω" is a command or request in Greek, meaning "remember" or "be mindful." It is used to urge someone to recall or keep something in mind.
