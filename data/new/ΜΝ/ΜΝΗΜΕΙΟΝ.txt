Etymology: The word "μνημείον" (mnemeion) comes from the Greek word "μνήμη" (mneme), meaning "memory" or "remembrance". It is derived from the verb "μνάομαι" (mnaomai), which means "to remember".

Grammar: In Greek, "μνημείον" is a neuter noun of the second declension. It has the plural form "μνημεῖα" (mnemeia).

Description: "Μνημείον" refers to a monument or memorial that is built to commemorate a person, event, or significant historical site. It is often used to honor and remember individuals who have passed away or to commemorate important historical events.
