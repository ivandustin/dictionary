Etymology: The word "μνημονευειν" (mnēmoneuein) comes from the Greek roots "μνημον" (mnēmon), meaning "memory," and "ευειν" (euein), meaning "to have." 

Grammar: "μνημονευειν" is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and infinitive mood. It belongs to the first conjugation class in Greek verbs.

Description: "μνημονευειν" means "to remember" or "to recall" in Greek. It refers to the act of bringing something back to one's memory or being able to retrieve information from one's mind.
