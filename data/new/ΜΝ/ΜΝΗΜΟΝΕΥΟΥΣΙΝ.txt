μνημονευούσιν (mnēmoneuousin) is a Greek verb that means "they remember" or "they are remembering." Here is the breakdown of the word:

- Etymology: The word μνημονευούσιν (mnēmoneuousin) comes from the root word μνημονεύω (mnēmoneuō), which means "to remember" or "to bear in mind." The suffix -ούσιν (-ousin) is the third person plural present active indicative ending, indicating that the subject of the verb is "they."

- Grammar: μνημονευούσιν (mnēmoneuousin) is a present tense verb in the active voice. It is conjugated for the third person plural, indicating that the subject is "they." In Greek, verbs change their endings to indicate the person, number, tense, and mood of the subject.

- Description: The verb μνημονευούσιν (mnēmoneuousin) describes the action of remembering or bearing something in mind. It indicates that a group of people (the subject) is currently engaged in the act of remembering something.
