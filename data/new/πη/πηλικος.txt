Word: πηλικός

Etymology:
The word πηλικός (pēlikós) comes from the Greek word πηλός (pēlós), which means "clay" or "mud". The suffix -ικός (-ikós) is a common Greek suffix that denotes "related to" or "pertaining to".

Grammar:
πηλικός is an adjective in the masculine gender, singular form. It can be declined according to the gender, number, and case of the noun it modifies.

Description:
πηλικός is used to describe something that is made of or related to clay or mud. It can also be used to describe something that is earthy or muddy in color or texture. For example, πηλικός χώμας (pēlikós chómas) means "clay soil", and πηλικός χρώμα (pēlikós chróma) means "earthy color".
