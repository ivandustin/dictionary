Word: πηγανον

Etymology:
The word πηγανον (pēganon) is a Greek word that means "a net" or "a fishing net". It is derived from the Greek word πηγνυμι (pēgnymi), which means "to fasten" or "to fix".

Grammar:
πηγανον is a masculine noun in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes πηγανα (pēgana). It belongs to the second declension in Greek grammar.

Description:
πηγανον is a type of net used for fishing. It is made of woven threads or cords and is designed to catch fish by entangling them in its mesh. The use of fishing nets dates back to ancient times, and they are still used today in many parts of the world. In Greek mythology, the god Poseidon was often depicted holding a trident and a fishing net, symbolizing his power over the sea and its creatures.
