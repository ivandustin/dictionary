Etymology: The word "αηρ" (aer) comes from the Ancient Greek word "ἀήρ" (aēr), meaning "air".

Grammar: In Greek, "αηρ" (aer) is a masculine noun. It belongs to the second declension and has a genitive form of "αέρος" (aeros).

Description: "Αηρ" (aer) refers to the substance that surrounds the Earth and is composed of a mixture of gases, primarily nitrogen and oxygen. It is essential for the survival of living organisms and plays a crucial role in various natural processes.
