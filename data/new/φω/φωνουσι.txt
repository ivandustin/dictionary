Word: φωνουσι

Etymology: φωνουσι is a conjugated form of the Greek verb φωνέω (phonéo), which means "to sound" or "to speak". It comes from the Greek noun φωνή (phoné), which means "voice" or "sound".

Grammar: φωνουσι is the third person plural present indicative active form of φωνέω. In Greek, verbs are conjugated to show the person, number, tense, voice, and mood of the action. The present indicative active form indicates that the action is happening in the present and that the subject is performing the action.

Description: φωνουσι is a Greek verb that means "they sound" or "they speak". It is used to describe the action of a group of people making noise or speaking. The verb can be used in a variety of contexts, such as describing a group of people singing, talking, or shouting.
