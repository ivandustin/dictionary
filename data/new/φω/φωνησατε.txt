Word: φωνησατε

Etymology: φωνησατε is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φωνή (phoné), meaning "voice" or "sound". The suffix -σατε (-sate) is a second person plural imperative ending, which means "you all speak" or "you all call out".

Grammar: φωνησατε is a second person plural imperative verb in the aorist tense. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people, telling them to speak or call out.

Description: φωνησατε is a verb that is often used in the Bible, particularly in the New Testament. It is usually translated as "speak" or "call out" and is often used in the context of prayer or worship.
