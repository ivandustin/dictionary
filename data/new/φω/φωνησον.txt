Word: φωνησον

Etymology: φωνησον is a verb in the aorist imperative active form of the Greek word φωνέω (phonéo), which means "to sound, to speak, to call out". The word comes from the root word φωνή (phoné), which means "voice, sound".

Grammar: φωνησον is a second person singular aorist imperative active form of the verb φωνέω. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past, and the imperative mood is used to give a command or request.

Description: φωνησον is a command to someone to "sound" or "call out". It is often used in the context of calling out to someone or making a loud noise. In the New Testament, it is used in the imperative form by Jesus to command a dead man to rise from the dead (Mark 5:41).
