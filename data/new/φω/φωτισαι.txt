Word: φωτισαι

Etymology: 
The word φωτισαι comes from the Greek verb φωτίζω (photízo), which means "to illuminate" or "to enlighten". It is derived from the noun φῶς (phos), which means "light".

Grammar:
φωτισαι is a verb in the aorist tense, active voice, and imperative mood. It is the second person singular form of the verb φωτίζω (photízo).

Description:
φωτισαι is a command form of the verb φωτίζω (photízo), which means "to illuminate" or "to enlighten". It is used to instruct someone to illuminate or enlighten something or someone. The word can be used in a literal sense, such as turning on a light or lighting a candle, or in a figurative sense, such as enlightening someone with knowledge or understanding.
