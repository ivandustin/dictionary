Word: φωνησαν

Etymology:
The word φωνησαν (phonēsan) is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form of the Greek word φωνέω (phonéō), which means "to sound, to make a noise, to speak." The word comes from the root word φωνή (phonē), which means "sound, voice."

Grammar:
φωνησαν is a verb in the aorist tense, third person plural form. In Greek, the aorist tense is used to describe a completed action in the past. The third person plural form indicates that the subject of the verb is a group of people or things.

Description:
φωνησαν is used in the New Testament of the Bible, specifically in the Gospel of John 18:40, where it is translated as "they shouted." The context of the passage is the trial of Jesus before Pilate, and the word is used to describe the crowd's reaction to Pilate's question about what to do with Jesus. The word conveys a sense of loud, forceful speech, indicating the intensity of the crowd's response.
