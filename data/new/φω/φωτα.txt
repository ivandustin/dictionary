The word φωτα (fota) is a plural form of the Greek noun φως (fos), which means "light". The word comes from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰā-, meaning "to shine, to gleam".

φωτα is a plural noun in Greek. It is the nominative and accusative form of the noun. The genitive form is φωτων (foton) and the dative form is φωτες (fotes).

φωτα is a common word in Greek, used to refer to lights or lamps. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to enlightenment or understanding. In modern Greek, the word is often used in the context of photography, where it refers to the flash or lighting used to illuminate a subject.
