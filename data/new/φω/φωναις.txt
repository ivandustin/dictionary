Word: φωναις

Etymology:
The word φωναις (phonais) is derived from the Greek word φωνή (phonē), which means "voice" or "sound". The suffix -αις (-ais) is a plural ending, indicating that the word refers to multiple voices or sounds.

Grammar:
φωναις is a feminine noun in the plural form. In Greek, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and case. The plural form indicates that there are multiple voices or sounds.

Description:
φωναις can be translated to mean "voices" or "sounds". It is often used in the context of music or singing, referring to the different voices or instruments that make up a piece of music. It can also be used more generally to refer to any kind of sound or noise.
