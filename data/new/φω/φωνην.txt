Word: φωνην

Etymology:
The word φωνην (phonēn) is derived from the Greek word φωνή (phonē), which means "voice" or "sound". It is related to the English word "phone" and the Latin word "phonēma".

Grammar:
φωνην is a noun in the accusative case. In Greek, nouns change their form depending on their grammatical function in a sentence. The accusative case is used to indicate the direct object of a verb.

Description:
φωνην refers to a sound or a voice. It can also be used to refer to a musical tone or note. In ancient Greek literature, the word is often used to describe the sound of a trumpet or other musical instrument. In modern Greek, the word is commonly used to refer to a telephone or other communication device.
