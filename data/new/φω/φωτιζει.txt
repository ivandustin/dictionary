Word: φωτιζει

Etymology: The word φωτιζει (pronounced "fotizi") comes from the Greek verb φωτίζω (fotizo), which means "to illuminate" or "to enlighten". It is derived from the noun φως (fos), which means "light".

- Εγώ φωτίζω (ego fotizo) - I illuminate
- Εσύ φωτίζεις (esi fotizeis) - You illuminate
- Αυτός/Αυτή/Αυτό φωτίζει (aftos/afti/afto fotizei) - He/She/It illuminates
- Εμείς φωτίζουμε (emeis fotizoume) - We illuminate
- Εσείς φωτίζετε (eseis fotizete) - You illuminate
- Αυτοί/Αυτές/Αυτά φωτίζουν (aftoi/aftes/afta fotizoun) - They illuminate

Description: φωτιζει is a Greek verb that means "he/she/it illuminates" or "he/she/it enlightens". It can be used in a literal sense, such as when referring to a light source, or in a figurative sense, such as when referring to someone who enlightens others with knowledge or wisdom.
