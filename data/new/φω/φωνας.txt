Word: φωνας

Etymology:
The word φωνας (phonas) is derived from the Greek word φωνή (phone), which means "voice" or "sound". It is related to the English word "phone" and the word "phonetics", which is the study of speech sounds.

Grammar:
In Greek, φωνας is a noun in the plural form. It is a masculine noun and belongs to the second declension. The singular form of the word is φωνή (phone).

Description:
φωνας refers to "voices" or "sounds" in Greek. It can be used to describe the sound of a person's voice or any other type of sound. The word is often used in the context of music, where it can refer to the different voices or instruments in a piece of music.
