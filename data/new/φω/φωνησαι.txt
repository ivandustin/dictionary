Word: φωνησαι

Etymology:
The word φωνησαι is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φωνή (phoné), which means "voice" or "sound". The suffix -σαι (-sai) is the aorist tense ending for the second person singular in the active voice.

Grammar:
φωνησαι is a second person singular aorist active imperative verb in Greek. It is used to give a command or order to someone to "speak" or "call out".

Description:
φωνησαι is a verb that is often used in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels and the Book of Acts. It is used in various contexts, such as when Jesus commands someone to speak or when someone is calling out to Jesus for help. The word emphasizes the importance of using one's voice to communicate and connect with others.
