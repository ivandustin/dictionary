Word: φωσφόρος

Etymology: The word φωσφόρος comes from the Greek words φῶς (phos) meaning "light" and φέρω (phero) meaning "to bear" or "to carry". Therefore, φωσφόρος literally means "light-bearer" or "bringer of light".

Grammar: In Greek, φωσφόρος is a masculine noun in the nominative case. Its genitive form is φωσφόρου and its accusative form is φωσφόρο.

Description: φωσφόρος is a term used in Greek mythology to refer to the planet Venus, which is the brightest object in the sky after the sun and the moon. In Christian tradition, the term is also used to refer to Lucifer, the fallen angel who rebelled against God and was cast out of heaven. The term is also used in chemistry to refer to elements that are capable of emitting light, such as phosphorus.
