Word: φωνησαντες

Etymology:
The word φωνησαντες is a Greek verb that comes from the root word φωνή (phoné), which means "voice" or "sound". The suffix -σαντες (-santes) is a past participle ending that indicates the action of the verb has been completed in the past.

Grammar:
φωνησαντες is the third person plural aorist active participle of the Greek verb φωνέω (phonéo), which means "to speak" or "to sound". The aorist tense indicates that the action of the verb was completed in the past, while the active voice indicates that the subject of the verb is performing the action. The participle form indicates that the verb is being used as an adjective to describe a noun.

Description:
φωνησαντες can be translated to mean "having spoken" or "having sounded". It is often used in the New Testament of the Bible to describe a group of people who have spoken or made a sound. For example, in Acts 4:31, it is written, "And when they had prayed, the place where they were assembled together was shaken; and they were all filled with the Holy Spirit, and they spoke the word of God with boldness."
