Word: φωλεους

Etymology:
The word φωλεους comes from the Greek word φωλεός (pholeós), which means "a hole or cavity in the ground, a burrow". It is derived from the verb φωλέω (pholéō), which means "to dig a hole or burrow".

Grammar:
φωλεους is a noun in the masculine gender and in the nominative case. It is the plural form of φωλεύς (pholeús), which means "one who lives in a hole or burrow".

Description:
φωλεους refers to animals that live in burrows or holes in the ground, such as rabbits, foxes, and badgers. It can also be used metaphorically to describe people who are reclusive or introverted.
