Etymology: The word "שוקת" (shoket) comes from the Hebrew root שׁ.ק.ט (sh.k.t), which means "to pour out" or "to spill". 

Grammar: "שוקת" is a noun in the feminine singular form. It is used to describe a container or vessel used for pouring or spilling liquids.

Description: "שוקת" refers to a pitcher, jug, or any other type of vessel that is used for pouring or spilling liquids. It is commonly used in the context of serving drinks or pouring water.
