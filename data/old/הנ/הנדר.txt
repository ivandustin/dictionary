Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַנְדֵּר" (HaNeder) comes from the root נדר (N-D-R), which means "to vow" or "to make a promise." 

Grammar: "הַנְדֵּר" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the definite form (הַ) and singular number. 

Description: "הַנְדֵּר" refers to a vow or a promise made by an individual to abstain from or perform a specific action. In Jewish tradition, making a vow is considered a serious commitment and is often done in a religious or spiritual context. Fulfilling a vow is seen as a way to demonstrate devotion and dedication.
