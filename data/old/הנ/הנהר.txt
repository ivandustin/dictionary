Etymology: The Hebrew word "הנהר" (HaNahar) comes from the root word "נהר" (nahar), which means "river" or "stream". 

Grammar: "הנהר" (HaNahar) is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is in the definite form, indicated by the prefix "ה" (ha-), which means "the". 

Description: "הנהר" (HaNahar) translates to "the river" in English. It refers to a large flowing body of water that typically flows towards the sea. In Hebrew, it can be used to describe specific rivers or used more generally to refer to any river.
