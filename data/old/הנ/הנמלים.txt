Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַנְמָלִים" (Ha'namalim) is derived from the root word "נָמַל" (namal), which means "to ant" or "to crawl." 

Grammar: "הַנְמָלִים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ים" (-im) to the singular form "הַנְמָל" (Ha'namal).

Description: "הַנְמָלִים" refers to ants in Hebrew. Ants are small insects that live in colonies and are known for their organized social structure and hardworking nature. They are found in various habitats around the world and play an important role in ecosystems by scavenging and decomposing organic matter.
