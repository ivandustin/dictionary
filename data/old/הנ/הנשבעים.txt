Etymology: The word "הַנְשָׁבִים" (HaNeshabim) is a Hebrew word that means "the survivors" or "the remaining ones." It is derived from the root נ-ש-ב (N-SH-B), which means "to remain" or "to survive."

Grammar: "הַנְשָׁבִים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the definite article "הַ" (Ha) at the beginning of the word and the plural suffix "-ים" (-im) at the end. The word is used to refer to a group of people who have survived or remained after a particular event or situation.

Description: "הַנְשָׁבִים" can be used in various contexts, such as referring to survivors of a natural disaster, survivors of a war, or survivors of a disease outbreak. It emphasizes the resilience and endurance of the individuals who have managed to overcome difficult circumstances and continue to exist.
