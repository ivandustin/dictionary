Etymology: The word "הנבאים" (HaNevi'im) is a Hebrew word that translates to "The Prophets" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root נ-ב-א (n-b-a), which means "to prophesy" or "to speak on behalf of God."

Grammar: "הנבאים" (HaNevi'im) is a plural noun in Hebrew. The definite article "ה" (Ha) indicates that it refers to a specific group of prophets. The suffix "-ים" (-im) is the masculine plural ending in Hebrew.

Description: "הנבאים" (HaNevi'im) is a term used in Judaism to refer to a section of the Hebrew Bible, specifically the books of the prophets. It includes the books of Joshua, Judges, Samuel, Kings, Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, and the twelve minor prophets. These books contain the writings and prophecies of various prophets who played significant roles in the history and religious teachings of the Jewish people. The prophets are considered to be messengers of God, delivering divine messages, warnings, and guidance to the people of Israel.
