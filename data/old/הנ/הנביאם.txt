Etymology: The word "הנביא" (HaNavi) is a Hebrew word that translates to "the prophet" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root נ-ב-א (N-B-A), which means "to prophesy" or "to speak on behalf of God."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "הנביא" (HaNavi) is a masculine noun that is used to refer to a prophet. It is preceded by the definite article "ה" (Ha), which means "the" in English. The word can be modified to indicate different grammatical forms, such as plural or possessive.

Description: "הנביא" (HaNavi) is a significant term in Jewish religious and cultural contexts. It refers to individuals who were believed to have been chosen by God to deliver divine messages and guidance to the people. Prophets played a crucial role in ancient Israelite society and are mentioned extensively in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh). They were seen as intermediaries between God and humanity, conveying God's will and teachings. The prophets' messages often included warnings, exhortations, and predictions of future events.
