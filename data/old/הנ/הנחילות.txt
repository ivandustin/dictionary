Etymology: The word "הַנְחִילוּת" (Ha-n'khilut) is a Hebrew noun derived from the root נ.ח.ל (N.Kh.L), which means "to inherit" or "to bequeath." 

Grammar: "הַנְחִילוּת" is a feminine singular noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ת (-ut) to the root נ.ח.ל (N.Kh.L). The definite article הַ (Ha) is added at the beginning to indicate "the."

Description: "הַנְחִילוּת" refers to the act of inheriting or bequeathing something, typically property or possessions, from one person to another. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the passing down of traditions, values, or characteristics from one generation to the next.
