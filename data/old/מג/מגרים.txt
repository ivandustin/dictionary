Etymology: The word "מגרים" (megarim) is derived from the Hebrew root גרם (g.r.m), which means "to cause" or "to bring about". The prefix מ (m) indicates the causative form, so מגרים (megarim) can be translated as "causing" or "bringing about".

- אנחנו מגרים (anachnu megarim) - We cause/bring about
- אתם מגרים (atem megarim) - You (masculine plural) cause/bring about
- אתן מגרות (aten megarot) - You (feminine plural) cause/bring about
- הם מגרים (hem megarim) - They cause/bring about
- הן מגרות (hen megarot) - They cause/bring about

Description: מגרים (megarim) is a Hebrew verb that describes the action of causing or bringing about something.
