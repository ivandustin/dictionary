Etymology: The word "דמיתי" (pronounced "d'miti") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ד-מ-ה (D-M-H), which means "to resemble" or "to be similar." 

Grammar: "דמיתי" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb "דמה" (dama), which means "to resemble" or "to be similar." It is used when referring to oneself and expressing that one resembled or was similar to someone or something in the past.

Description: "דמיתי" is a word that is used to describe a past action of resembling or being similar to someone or something. It is commonly used in storytelling or when recounting past experiences.
