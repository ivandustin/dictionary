Etymology: The word "צרח" (tzarach) comes from the Hebrew root צ-ר-ח (ts-r-ch), which means "to cry out" or "to shout". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, צרח is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and forms to match different subjects and contexts.

Description: צרח means "he shouted" or "he cried out" in Hebrew. It is used to describe a loud and forceful vocal expression, often indicating distress, excitement, or urgency.
