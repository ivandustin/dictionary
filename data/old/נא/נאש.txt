Etymology: The word "נאש" (pronounced "na'ash") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root נ-א-ש (N-A-Sh), which means "to forget" or "to neglect."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "נאש" is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and forms to match different subjects and contexts.

Description: The word "נאש" is used to describe the act of forgetting or neglecting something or someone. It implies a lack of attention or care towards a particular matter.
