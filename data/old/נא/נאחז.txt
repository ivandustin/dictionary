Etymology: The word "נאחז" (pronounced "na'achaz" in Hebrew) is derived from the root נ-א-ח-ז, which means "to hold" or "to grasp" in Hebrew.

Grammar: נאחז is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני נאחז (ani na'achaz) - I hold
- Masculine singular: אתה נאחז (ata na'achaz) - You (masculine) hold
- Feminine singular: את נאחזת (at na'achazet) - You (feminine) hold
- Masculine plural: אתם נאחזים (atem na'achazim) - You (masculine plural) hold
- Feminine plural: אתן נאחזות (atan na'achazot) - You (feminine plural) hold

Description: נאחז is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It generally refers to the action of physically holding or grasping something. However, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of capturing, seizing, or taking hold of something figuratively.
