Etymology: The word "בלוא" (belo) is derived from the Hebrew root ב-ל-א (b-l-a), which means "without" or "lacking".

Grammar: "בלוא" (belo) is a preposition in Hebrew. It is used to indicate exclusion or absence. It is often translated as "without" or "except for" in English.

Description: "בלוא" (belo) is used to express the absence or exclusion of something. It is commonly used in phrases such as "בלוא ידיעה" (belo yedi'a), meaning "without knowledge", or "בלוא תנאי" (belo t'nai), meaning "without condition". It is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to indicate the absence or exclusion of something.
