Etymology: The word "בלבושו" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the words "בלבוש" (balbus) meaning "clothing" and "ו" (vav) meaning "his". 

Grammar: "בלבושו" is a Hebrew noun phrase. It consists of the preposition "ב" (be) meaning "in" or "with", the noun "לבוש" (levush) meaning "clothing", and the possessive suffix "ו" (o) meaning "his". Together, they form the phrase "בלבושו" which translates to "in his clothing" or "with his clothing".

Description: "בלבושו" is used to describe someone or something that is wearing or adorned with clothing. It indicates possession or association with the clothing.
