Etymology: The word "בְּלִי" (beli) comes from the Hebrew root ב־ל־י (b-l-y), which means "without" or "lacking." 

Grammar: "בְּלִי" is a preposition in Hebrew that is used to indicate the absence or lack of something. It is often translated as "without" or "not having." It is followed by a noun or a verb in the infinitive form.

Description: "בְּלִי" is a versatile word that is used in various contexts to express negation or absence. It can be used to indicate the lack of possession, the absence of a certain quality or condition, or the negation of an action. It is commonly used in biblical and modern Hebrew.
