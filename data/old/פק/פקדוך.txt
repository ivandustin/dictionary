Etymology: The word "פקדון" (pronounced "pekudon") comes from the Hebrew root פ-ק-ד, which means "to appoint" or "to entrust." It is derived from the noun פְּקֻדָּה (pekuddah), which means "a charge" or "a deposit."

Grammar: "פקדון" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the singular and plural forms. In the plural form, it becomes "פקדונות" (pekudonot).

Description: "פקדון" refers to a deposit or a pledge that is given as a security or guarantee for a debt or obligation. It is commonly used in financial and legal contexts, such as when someone deposits money as collateral or when a person is entrusted with a responsibility.
