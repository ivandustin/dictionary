Etymology: The word "פקדת" (pronounced "pekudat") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root פ-ק-ד (P-K-D). This root carries the meaning of "to appoint," "to command," or "to count." 

Grammar: "פקדת" is a noun in the feminine form. It is in the singular form and can be used as a subject or an object in a sentence. 

Description: "פקדת" is often translated as "commandment," "charge," or "order." It refers to a specific instruction or directive given by someone in authority. In Jewish religious context, it is commonly used to refer to the commandments given by God to the Jewish people, as recorded in the Torah. These commandments are seen as obligations and guidelines for living a righteous and moral life.
