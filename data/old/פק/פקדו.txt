Etymology: The word "פקדו" (pronounced "pakadu") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root פ-ק-ד, which means "to visit" or "to appoint." 

Grammar: "פקדו" is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is commonly used in the context of God's actions or divine intervention.

Description: The word "פקדו" is often translated as "He visited" or "He appointed." It implies a sense of divine intervention or oversight. In biblical texts, it is used to describe God's actions in watching over or taking care of His people.
