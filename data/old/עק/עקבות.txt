Etymology: The word "עקבות" (akavot) is derived from the Hebrew root עקב (akav), which means "heel" or "footstep". It is related to the idea of following or tracing someone's footsteps.

Grammar: "עקבות" (akavot) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to footprints, tracks, or traces left behind by someone or something.

Description: "עקבות" (akavot) is often used metaphorically to describe the lasting impact or consequences of someone's actions. It can also refer to the evidence or signs that indicate the presence or passage of someone or something.
