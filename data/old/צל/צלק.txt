Etymology: The word "צלק" (tsalak) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root צ-ל-ק (ts-l-k), which means "to bind" or "to tie."

- אֲנַחְנוּ צוֹלְקִים (anachnu tzol'kim) - We tie

- אַתֶּן צוֹלְקוֹת (aten tzol'kot) - You tie (feminine plural)

- הֵן צוֹלְקוֹת (hen tzol'kot) - They tie (feminine)

Description: The verb "צלק" (tsalak) means "to tie" or "to bind" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of fastening or securing something with a knot or other means.
