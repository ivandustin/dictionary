Etymology: The word "צלק" (pronounced "tsalak") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root צ-ל-ק, which means "to connect" or "to link."

- אֲנִי צוֹלֵק (ani tzolek) - I connect

- אַתְּ צוֹלֶקֶת (at tzoleket) - You (feminine singular) connect
- הוּא צוֹלֵק (hu tzolek) - He connects

- אֲנַחְנוּ צוֹלְקִים (anachnu tzol'kim) - We connect

- אַתֶּן צוֹלְקוֹת (aten tzol'kot) - You (feminine plural) connect
- הֵם צוֹלְקִים (hem tzol'kim) - They (masculine) connect
- הֵן צוֹלְקוֹת (hen tzol'kot) - They (feminine) connect

Description: The word "צלק" means "to connect" or "to link" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of joining or bringing together separate entities or elements.
