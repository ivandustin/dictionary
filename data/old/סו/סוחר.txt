Etymology: The word "סוחר" (socher) comes from the Hebrew root ס.ח.ר (s.ch.r), which means "to trade" or "to deal". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, סוחר is a noun that means "merchant" or "trader". It is a masculine singular noun, and its plural form is סוחרים (sochrim).

Description: סוחר refers to a person who engages in buying and selling goods or services for profit. It can also refer to someone who is involved in trade or commerce. In ancient times, merchants played a crucial role in the economy, facilitating the exchange of goods between different regions. Today, the term can be used to describe individuals involved in various types of trade, such as retail, wholesale, or international commerce.
