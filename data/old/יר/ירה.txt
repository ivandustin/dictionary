Etymology: The word "ירה" (Yara) is derived from the Hebrew root י-ר-ה (Y-R-H), which means "to shoot" or "to throw". 

Grammar: "ירה" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the Qal (simple) conjugation. It is in the masculine singular form, which indicates the third person. 

Description: The word "ירה" is often translated as "he shoots" or "he throws" in English. It can refer to the act of shooting an arrow, throwing an object, or even casting lots. The context in which it is used determines its specific meaning.
