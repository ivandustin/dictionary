Etymology: The word "יראיו" (Yerayu) is a Hebrew word derived from the root י-ר-א (Y-R-A), which means "to fear" or "to revere." It is formed by adding the suffix יו (-yu) to the root.

Grammar: "יראיו" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the plural form, indicating multiple individuals who fear or revere something or someone.

Description: "יראיו" refers to individuals who have fear or reverence towards a specific object, concept, or deity. It can be used to describe people who have a deep respect or awe for something or someone.
