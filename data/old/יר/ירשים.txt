Etymology: The word "ירשים" (Yarshim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root י-ר-שׁ (Y-R-Sh), which means "to inherit" or "to possess." 

Grammar: "ירשים" is a plural noun in Hebrew, derived from the singular form "ירש" (yarash). It is used to refer to individuals who inherit or possess something.

Description: "ירשים" refers to heirs or inheritors. It is commonly used in legal and biblical contexts to describe individuals who receive an inheritance or possess something that has been passed down to them.
