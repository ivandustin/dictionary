Etymology: The word "ירצו" (pronounced "yirtzu") is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root רצה (pronounced "ratza"), which means "to want" or "to desire."

Grammar: "ירצו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb רצה. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: The word "ירצו" can be translated to English as "they will want" or "they will desire." It expresses the future action of a group of people wanting or desiring something.
