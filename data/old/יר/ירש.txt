Etymology: The word "ירש" (Yarash) comes from the Hebrew root י-ר-ש (Y-R-Sh), which means "to inherit" or "to possess." 

Grammar: "ירש" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אֲנִי יוֹרֵשׁ (ani yoresh) - I inherit, אַתָּה יוֹרֵשׁ (atah yoresh) - you (masculine) inherit, אַתְּ יוֹרֶשֶׁת (at yoreshet) - you (feminine) inherit, הוּא יוֹרֵשׁ (hu yoresh) - he inherits, הִיא יוֹרֶשֶׁת (hi yoreshet) - she inherits, אֲנַחְנוּ יוֹרְשִׁים (anachnu yorshim) - we inherit, אַתֶּם יוֹרְשִׁים (atem yorshim) - you (plural) inherit, הֵם יוֹרְשִׁים (hem yorshim) - they (masculine) inherit, הֵן יוֹרְשִׁים (hen yorshim) - they (feminine) inherit.

Description: The word "ירש" (Yarash) means "to inherit" or "to possess" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of receiving or acquiring something, typically property or possessions, from a previous owner, usually through a legal or familial process. In a broader sense, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the acquisition or adoption of traits, characteristics, or qualities.
