Etymology: The Hebrew word "הָרִימִי" (HaRimi) is derived from the root רמה (R.M.H), which means "to lift up" or "to exalt." 

Grammar: "הָרִימִי" is a verb in the imperative form, second person, singular, feminine. It is addressed to a female and translates to "lift up" or "exalt." 

Description: "הָרִימִי" is a command or request to a female to lift up or exalt something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as encouraging someone to raise their voice, elevate their status, or praise and honor someone.
