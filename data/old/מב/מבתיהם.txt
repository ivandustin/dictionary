Etymology: The word "מבתיהם" is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "מבת" (mevate) meaning "from the house of" and "הם" (hem) meaning "them". 

Grammar: "מבתיהם" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-יהם" (-eihem) to the word "מבת" (mevate). This suffix indicates possession, meaning "their houses".

Description: "מבתיהם" is used to refer to houses or dwellings that belong to a group of people. It implies that the houses are collectively owned or shared by the group.
