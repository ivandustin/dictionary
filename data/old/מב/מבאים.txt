Etymology: The word "מבאים" (pronounced "meva'im") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root בא (pronounced "ba"), which means "to come" or "to arrive". The prefix מ (pronounced "me") indicates plural form, so "מבאים" translates to "they come" or "they arrive".

Grammar: "מבאים" is a verb in the present tense, third person plural form. It is used to describe a group of people or things that are coming or arriving at a certain place.

Description: "מבאים" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to people coming to a specific location, such as "הם מבאים לבית" (they are coming home), or objects being delivered or brought to a place, such as "החבילה מבאה מהחנות" (the package is coming from the store). It can also be used metaphorically to describe the arrival or occurrence of events or situations, such as "הקיץ מבא" (summer is coming) or "הסוף מבא" (the end is coming).
