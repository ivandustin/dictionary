Grammar: In Hebrew, "מבית" (mebet) is a prepositional phrase that functions as an adverbial phrase. It is used to indicate the origin or source of something. It is commonly used to describe the place where someone or something comes from.

For example, it can be used to describe the origin of a person, such as "אני מבית ירושלים" (I am from Jerusalem).
