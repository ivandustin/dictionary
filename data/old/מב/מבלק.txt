Etymology: The word "מבלק" (mavlek) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ב-ל-ק (B-L-K), which means "to waste" or "to squander". 

Grammar: "מבלק" is a noun in Hebrew and it is in the masculine form. It can also be used as a verb in the present tense, such as "אני מבלק" (ani mavlek) which means "I waste" or "I squander".

Description: "מבלק" is used to describe someone who wastes or squanders their resources, whether it be money, time, or opportunities. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is careless or reckless in their actions.
