Etymology: The word "יעור" (pronounced "ya'or") comes from the Hebrew root ע.ו.ר (ayin-vav-resh), which means "to awaken" or "to stir up". 

Grammar: "יעור" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני עוֹר (ani or), אתה עוֹר (ata or), הוא/היא עוֹר (hu/hi or), אנחנו עוֹרים (anachnu orim), אתם עוֹרים (atem orim), הם/הן עוֹרים (hem/hen orim).

Description: The word "יעור" means "to awaken" or "to stir up" in Hebrew. It can be used both in a literal sense, referring to waking up from sleep, and in a figurative sense, referring to arousing or stimulating something or someone.
