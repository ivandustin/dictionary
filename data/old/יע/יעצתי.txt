Etymology: The word "יעצתי" (pronounced "ye'atzti") is a Hebrew verb in the past tense, derived from the root י-ע-צ (y-ayin-tsade). 

Grammar: "יעצתי" is the first person singular form of the verb יעץ (ya'atz), which means "to advise" or "to counsel" in Hebrew. The suffix תי (ti) indicates that the action was performed by the speaker in the past.

Description: "יעצתי" translates to "I advised" or "I counseled" in English. It is used to express that the speaker gave advice or guidance to someone in the past.
