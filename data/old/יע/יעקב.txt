Etymology: The word "יעקב" (Yaakov) is a Hebrew name derived from the Hebrew root עקב (ayin-kuf-bet), which means "heel" or "supplanter". In the Bible, Yaakov is the name of one of the patriarchs, also known as Jacob in English.

Grammar: "יעקב" (Yaakov) is a masculine proper noun in Hebrew. It is typically used as a given name for boys.

Description: Yaakov is a popular Hebrew name with significant biblical and cultural significance. In the Bible, Yaakov is known for his role as one of the patriarchs of the Israelite people. He is the son of Isaac and Rebecca, and the twin brother of Esau. Yaakov's story includes his famous wrestling match with an angel, his twelve sons who became the heads of the twelve tribes of Israel, and his journey from Canaan to Egypt. The name Yaakov is still commonly used today in Jewish communities and holds a strong connection to Jewish heritage and tradition.
