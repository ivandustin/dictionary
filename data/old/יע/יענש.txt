Etymology: The word "יענש" (pronounced "ya'anesh" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root ע.נ.ש (ayin.nun.shin), which means "to punish" or "to chastise."

- Singular: אני יענש (ani ya'anesh) - I punish
- Singular feminine: אני יענשת (ani ya'anisht) - I punish

- Singular feminine: את יענשי (at ya'aneshi) - You (feminine) punish

- Plural: אתם יענשו (atem ya'aneshu) - You (plural) punish
- Plural feminine: אתן יענשנה (aten ya'aneshna) - You (plural feminine) punish
- Plural masculine: הם יענשו (hem ya'aneshu) - They (masculine) punish
- Plural feminine: הן יענשנה (hen ya'aneshna) - They (feminine) punish

Description: The word "יענש" means "to punish" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of imposing a penalty or retribution for wrongdoing or disobedience.
