Etymology: The word "בחבלים" (b'chavlim) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in ropes" or "in chains." It is derived from the Hebrew root חבל (chav-lamed-bet), which means "to bind" or "to tie."

Grammar: "בחבלים" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "ב" (b) means "in" or "with," and "חבלים" (chavlim) is the plural form of the noun חבל (chavel), which means "rope" or "chain." The phrase is used to describe a state of being bound or restrained.

Description: The phrase "בחבלים" is often used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone feels trapped, restricted, or unable to act freely. It can refer to physical or metaphorical restraints, symbolizing a lack of freedom or autonomy.
