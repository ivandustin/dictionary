Etymology: The word "בחלום" (b'chalom) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "ב" (b) meaning "in" or "with," and "חלום" (chalom) meaning "dream." 

Grammar: "בחלום" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (b) means "in" or "with," and "חלום" (chalom) is a noun meaning "dream." Together, they form the phrase "in a dream" or "with a dream."

Description: "בחלום" is used to describe something that happens or is experienced in a dream. It is often used in storytelling or when recounting a dream sequence.
