Etymology: The word "בחוצותינו" (b'chutzotaynu) is a Hebrew term that translates to "in our streets" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew words "ב" (b') meaning "in" or "at," "חוץ" (chutz) meaning "outside" or "out," and "ינו" (otaynu) meaning "our."

Grammar: "בחוצותינו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition "ב" (b') followed by the noun "חוצותינו" (chutzotaynu), which means "our streets." The noun is in the plural form, indicating multiple streets.

Description: "בחוצותינו" is used to refer to something happening or existing in the streets of a specific location. It can be used to describe various activities, events, or situations taking place in public spaces.
