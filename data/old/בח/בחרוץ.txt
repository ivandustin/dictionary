Etymology: The word "בחרוץ" (b'harutz) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ב.ח.ר.ץ (B.Ch.R.Tz), which means "to dig" or "to excavate". 

Grammar: "בחרוץ" is a noun in the singular masculine form. It is used to describe a specific type of digging or excavation. 

Description: "בחרוץ" refers to a deep and thorough digging or excavation. It implies a significant effort and precision in the process of digging. This term can be used in various contexts, such as archaeological excavations, construction projects, or even metaphorically to describe a thorough investigation or research.
