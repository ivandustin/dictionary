Etymology: The word "בחדש" (b'chadesh) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root "חדש" (chadesh), which means "new" or "renew." 

Grammar: "בחדש" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition "ב" (b) meaning "in" or "during," and the noun "חדש" (chodesh) meaning "month." Together, it means "in the month" or "during the month."

Description: "בחדש" is used in Hebrew to refer to something happening or occurring during a specific month. It is commonly used in the context of the Jewish calendar to indicate a specific month in which an event or holiday takes place. For example, "בחדש ניסן" (b'chodesh Nissan) means "in the month of Nissan," which is the first month of the Jewish calendar and is associated with the holiday of Passover.
