Etymology: The word "בחרבי" (b'chirbi) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root letters ב-ח-ר, which means "to choose" or "to select". The suffix בי (bi) is added to indicate possession or belonging.

Grammar: "בחרבי" is a possessive phrase in Hebrew. The word "ב" (b) means "in" or "with", and "חרבי" (chirbi) means "my sword". So, "בחרבי" literally translates to "in my sword" or "with my sword".

Description: "בחרבי" is often used metaphorically to express determination, strength, or reliance on one's own abilities. It can imply a sense of readiness to face challenges or to defend oneself.
