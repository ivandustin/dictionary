Etymology: The word "בחי" (bachi) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ב.ח.י (b.ch.y), which means "to live" or "to be alive". 

Grammar: "בחי" is a noun in Hebrew and it is in the singular form. It is commonly used in the phrase "בחי הפה" (bachi hapé), which means "by word of mouth" or "oral tradition".

Description: "בחי" refers to information or knowledge that is passed down orally from one person to another, rather than being written down or recorded. It emphasizes the importance of personal communication and the transmission of knowledge through spoken words.
