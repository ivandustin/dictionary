Etymology: The word "חללו" (pronounced "challo") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ח-ל-ל (ch-l-l), which means "to pierce" or "to hollow out."

Grammar: "חללו" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is commonly used in the context of creating a hole or hollowing out something.

Description: "חללו" describes the action of making a hole or creating a hollow space in an object. It can refer to physically piercing or drilling through something, or metaphorically creating an empty space or void.
