Etymology: The word "וָאַמְרְתִי" is a Hebrew word that comes from the root אמר (amar), which means "to say" or "to speak". The prefix וָ (va) means "and", and the suffix תִי (ti) indicates the first person singular past tense. Therefore, וָאַמְרְתִי (va'amarti) translates to "and I said" or "and I spoke".

Grammar: וָאַמְרְתִי (va'amarti) is a verb form in the past tense, specifically the first person singular. It is used when the speaker wants to express that they said or spoke something in the past. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated based on the subject, tense, and gender.
