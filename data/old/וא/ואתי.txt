Etymology: The word "וָאֵתִי" (pronounced "v'eti") is a Hebrew word that means "and I came." It is derived from the root word "בוא" (pronounced "bo"), which means "to come."

Grammar: "וָאֵתִי" is a first-person singular verb form in the past tense. It is composed of the prefix "וָ" (pronounced "va"), which means "and," and the verb "אָתָה" (pronounced "atah"), which means "to come."

Description: "וָאֵתִי" is used to express the action of coming in the past tense. It is commonly used in narratives or personal accounts to describe the speaker's arrival at a specific location.
