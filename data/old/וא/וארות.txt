Etymology: The word "וָאֹת" (pronounced "vav-aleph-tav") is a Hebrew word that is used as a direct object marker in the Hebrew language. It is often translated as "and" or "with" in English.

Grammar: In Hebrew grammar, "וָאֹת" is known as the direct object marker. It is used to mark the direct object of a verb, indicating that the action of the verb is being performed on that object. It is placed before the direct object in a sentence.

Description: "וָאֹת" is a small but important word in the Hebrew language. It helps to clarify the relationship between the subject and the direct object in a sentence. Without it, the meaning of a sentence could be ambiguous. It is commonly used in both biblical and modern Hebrew.
