Etymology: The word "וָאֵל" (pronounced "v'el") is a Hebrew word that means "and God." It is a combination of the Hebrew conjunction "וְ" (pronounced "v'") meaning "and," and the Hebrew noun "אֵל" (pronounced "el") meaning "God."

Grammar: "וָאֵל" is a conjunction that is used to connect two words or phrases together. It is commonly used in biblical Hebrew to join the subject and predicate of a sentence. For example, in the sentence "וָאֵל יֹאמֶר," which means "and God said," "וָאֵל" connects the subject "God" with the verb "said."

Description: "וָאֵל" is a significant word in Hebrew as it is used to refer to God in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh). It is often used to describe the actions, attributes, and interactions of God in various biblical narratives. The word carries a sense of authority, power, and divinity.
