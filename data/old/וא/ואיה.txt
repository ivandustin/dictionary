Etymology: The word "וָאִיה" (pronounced "vaiha") is a Hebrew word that means "and where." It is a combination of the conjunction "וְ" (pronounced "ve") meaning "and" and the interrogative pronoun "אֵיה" (pronounced "eifo") meaning "where."

Grammar: "וָאִיה" is a compound word formed by combining the conjunction "וְ" (and) with the interrogative pronoun "אֵיה" (where). It is used to ask about the location or whereabouts of something or someone.

Description: "וָאִיה" is a word commonly used in Hebrew to ask about the location of something or someone. It is often used in questions such as "וָאִיה הספר?" (Where is the book?) or "וָאִיה האוטו?" (Where is the car?). It is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to inquire about the whereabouts of objects, people, or places.
