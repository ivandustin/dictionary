Etymology: The word "וָאֵיה" (pronounced "v'ayeha") is a Hebrew word that means "and where." It is a combination of the conjunction "וְ" (pronounced "v'") meaning "and," and the interrogative pronoun "אֵיה" (pronounced "ayeh") meaning "where."

Grammar: "וָאֵיה" is a compound word consisting of the conjunction "וְ" and the interrogative pronoun "אֵיה." In Hebrew grammar, the conjunction "וְ" is used to connect words or phrases, while the interrogative pronoun "אֵיה" is used to ask about the location or whereabouts of something or someone.

Description: "וָאֵיה" is commonly used in Hebrew to ask about the location of something or someone. It is often used in questions such as "וָאֵיה הַסֵּפֶר?" (Where is the book?) or "וָאֵיה הַמַּפְתֵּחַ?" (And where is the key?). It is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to inquire about the whereabouts of objects, people, or places.
