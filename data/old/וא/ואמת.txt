Etymology: The word "וָאַמְתָּ" (pronounced "v'amat") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root אמת (pronounced "emet"), which means "truth" or "faithfulness". The prefix וָ (pronounced "va") means "and". 

Grammar: "וָאַמְתָּ" is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It is derived from the root אמת and is conjugated with the prefix וָ and the suffix תָּ. The verb form indicates that the action was performed by the speaker in the past.

Description: "וָאַמְתָּ" can be translated to English as "and I said" or "and I spoke". It is used to express that the speaker made a statement or communicated something in the past. The word is commonly used in biblical texts and can be found in various contexts.
