Etymology: The word "וָאַחִי" (pronounced "va'achi") is a Hebrew word that means "and my brother." It is derived from the Hebrew root א.ח.י (A.CHI), which means "brother."

Grammar: "וָאַחִי" is a phrase composed of two parts: "וָ" (pronounced "va") meaning "and," and "אַחִי" (pronounced "achi") meaning "my brother." In Hebrew, possessive pronouns like "my" are attached to the noun they modify.

Description: "וָאַחִי" is a phrase used to indicate the speaker's relationship with their brother. It can be used in various contexts, such as introducing or referring to one's brother in a conversation.
