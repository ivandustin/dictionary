Etymology: The word "וָאֵילַם" (pronounced "va'eilam") is a Hebrew word that means "and I said." It is derived from the Hebrew root אָמַר (amar), which means "to say."

Grammar: "וָאֵילַם" is a verb form in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense, first person singular, and prefixed with the conjunction "וַ" (va), which means "and." The suffix "ַאֵילַם" (-eilam) indicates the first person singular pronoun "I."

Description: "וָאֵילַם" is used in biblical Hebrew texts to indicate that the speaker is recounting or reporting something they said in the past. It is often translated as "and I said" or "so I said" in English. This verb form is commonly found in narratives and dialogues in the Hebrew Bible.
