Etymology: The word "וָאֹמֶר" (pronounced "va'omer") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root אמר (amar), which means "to say" or "to speak." The prefix וְ (ve) means "and," so וָאֹמֶר can be translated as "and I said."

Grammar: וָאֹמֶר is a verb form in the past tense, first person singular. It is a conjugation of the verb אמר (amar), which means "to say" or "to speak." The prefix וְ (ve) indicates the past tense, and the suffix תִי (ti) indicates the first person singular.

Description: וָאֹמֶר is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is often translated as "and I said" or "and I spoke." It is commonly used to introduce direct speech or reported speech. The word is used to indicate that the speaker is recounting something they said in the past.
