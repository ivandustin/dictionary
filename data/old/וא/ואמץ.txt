Etymology: The word "וָאִם־" (pronounced "v'im") is a Hebrew conjunction composed of the letter "ו" (pronounced "vav") meaning "and" and the word "אִם" (pronounced "im") meaning "if". 

Grammar: "וָאִם־" is used in Hebrew to introduce a conditional clause or a hypothetical situation. It is often translated as "and if" or "but if" in English. It is commonly used in biblical texts and in modern Hebrew.

Description: "וָאִם־" is a versatile conjunction that allows for the expression of various conditions or possibilities. It is used to connect two clauses, with the first clause stating a condition or a hypothetical situation, and the second clause presenting the consequence or result. It is an important tool in Hebrew grammar for expressing conditional statements and hypothetical scenarios.
