Etymology: The word "וָאֶשְׁלַח" (pronounced "va'eshlach") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root שָׁלַח (shalach), which means "to send." The prefix וָ (va) means "and," indicating the past tense. Therefore, וָאֶשְׁלַח translates to "and I sent."

Grammar: וָאֶשְׁלַח is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It is used to describe an action of sending that was performed by the speaker.

Description: וָאֶשְׁלַח is found in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament) in the book of Genesis, specifically in Genesis 32:4. It is part of the narrative of Jacob sending messengers ahead of him to his brother Esau. The word is used to indicate that Jacob personally sent the messengers.
