Grammar: "וָאֹמוֹ" is a verb form in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense, first person singular, and prefixed with the conjunction "וְ" (pronounced "ve"), which means "and." The suffix "ֹ" (pronounced "o") indicates the first person singular pronoun "I."

Description: "וָאֹמוֹ" is used in biblical Hebrew to express the action of speaking or saying something in the past tense. It is often used to introduce direct speech or to report something that was said.
