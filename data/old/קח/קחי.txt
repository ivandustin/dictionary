Etymology: The word "קחי" (pronounced "kachi") is a Hebrew verb in the imperative form. It comes from the root ק.ח.ה (k.ch.h), which means "to take" or "to grab."

Grammar: "קחי" is the second person singular feminine form of the imperative verb. It is used when addressing a female and instructing her to take or grab something.

Description: "קחי" is a command that tells someone to take or grab something. It is commonly used in everyday speech and can be used in various contexts, such as asking someone to take an object, seize an opportunity, or grasp a concept.
