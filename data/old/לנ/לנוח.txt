Etymology: The word "לנוח" (lenoch) comes from the Hebrew root "נ.ו.ח" (n.w.h), which means "to rest" or "to relax".

Grammar: "לנוח" (lenoch) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular:
  - אֲנִי מְנוּחָה (ani menucha) - I rest
  - אַתְּ מְנוּחָה (at menucha) - You (feminine) rest
  - אַתָּה מְנוּחָה (ata menucha) - You (masculine) rest
  - הוּא מְנוּחָה (hu menucha) - He rests
  - הִיא מְנוּחָה (hi menucha) - She rests
- Plural:
  - אֲנַחְנוּ מְנוּחִים (anachnu menuchim) - We rest
  - אַתֶּם מְנוּחִים (atem menuchim) - You (masculine plural) rest
  - אַתֶּן מְנוּחוֹת (aten menuchot) - You (feminine plural) rest
  - הֵם מְנוּחִים (hem menuchim) - They (masculine) rest
  - הֵן מְנוּחוֹת (hen menuchot) - They (feminine) rest

Description: The word "לנוח" (lenoch) means "to rest" or "to relax" in Hebrew. It refers to taking a break, finding peace, or finding comfort. It can be used in various contexts, such as physical rest, mental relaxation, or finding tranquility.
