Etymology: The word "לנוע" (leno'a) is derived from the Hebrew root "נ.ו.ע" (n.w.a), which means "to move" or "to sway."

Grammar: "לנוע" (leno'a) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular:
  - 1st person: אֲנִי נוֹעָה (ani no'a) - I move
  - 2nd person: אַתְּ נוֹעָה (at no'a) - You (feminine) move
  - 3rd person: הוּא/הִיא נוֹעָה (hu/hi no'a) - He/She moves
- Plural:
  - 1st person: אֲנַחְנוּ נוֹעוֹת (anachnu no'ot) - We move
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם נוֹעִים (atem no'im) - You (masculine) move
  - 3rd person: הֵם/הֵן נוֹעוֹת (hem/hen no'ot) - They move

Description: "לנוע" (leno'a) means "to move" or "to sway" in Hebrew. It can refer to physical movement, such as walking, running, or dancing, as well as metaphorical movement, such as changing one's opinion or position. The verb can be used in various contexts, expressing different types of movement.
