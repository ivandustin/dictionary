Etymology: The word "לנפשו" (lenafsho) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "for himself" or "for his own sake". It is composed of the preposition "ל" (le), meaning "to" or "for", and the noun "נפשו" (nafsho), meaning "his soul" or "himself".

Grammar: "לנפשו" (lenafsho) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" (le) indicates the direction or purpose of an action, and it is followed by the possessive pronoun "נפשו" (nafsho), which means "his soul" or "himself". This phrase is used to express an action or decision made for one's own benefit or well-being.

Description: "לנפשו" (lenafsho) is a Hebrew phrase that emphasizes the importance of self-care and self-preservation. It suggests that an action or decision is motivated by personal needs, desires, or interests.
