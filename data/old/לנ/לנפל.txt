Etymology: The word "לנפל" (lenafel) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root נ.פ.ל (n.p.l), which means "to fall" or "to collapse".

Grammar: "לנפל" (lenafel) is an infinitive verb form in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני נוֹפֵל (ani nofel) - I fall
- Plural: אנחנו נוֹפְלִים (anachnu noflim) - We fall

Description: The verb "לנפל" (lenafel) is used to describe the action of falling or collapsing physically or metaphorically. It can refer to a person or an object falling down, as well as to a decline or failure in a situation or condition.
