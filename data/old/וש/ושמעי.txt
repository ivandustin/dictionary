Etymology: The word "וְשָׂמְעִי" (pronounced "veshame'i") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root שמע (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen." The prefix וְ (ve) means "and," and the suffix י (i) indicates the first person singular possessive form. Therefore, the word can be translated as "and my hearing" or "and my listening."

Grammar: In Hebrew, the word "וְשָׂמְעִי" is a verb in the imperative form. It is conjugated in the first person singular, indicating that the speaker is commanding themselves to listen or hear.

Description: The word "וְשָׂמְעִי" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is often translated as "listen" or "hear." It is a call to pay attention and give heed to what is being said. It can also imply obedience and submission to the speaker's words or instructions.
