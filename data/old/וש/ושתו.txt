Grammar: "וְשֵׁתוֹ" is a noun phrase composed of the conjunction "וְ" (v') meaning "and" and the noun "שֵׁתוֹ" (sheto) meaning "his wife." In Hebrew, nouns have gender and number, and they can be modified by possessive pronouns or other determiners.

Description: "וְשֵׁתוֹ" is used in the Hebrew Bible to refer to the wife of Adam, the first man according to the biblical creation story. It appears in the Book of Genesis, specifically in Genesis 4:1, where it says, "וְהָאָדָם יָדַע אֶת־חַוָּה אִשְׁתּוֹ" (v'ha'adam yada et-chava ishto), which translates to "And Adam knew Eve his wife."
