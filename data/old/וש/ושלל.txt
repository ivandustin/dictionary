Etymology: The word "וֶשֶׁלֶל" (veshelel) is a Hebrew noun that comes from the root שׁ.ל.ל (sh.l.l), which means "to plunder" or "to spoil."

Grammar: "וֶשֶׁלֶל" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is used to describe the act of plundering or spoiling.

Description: "וֶשֶׁלֶל" refers to the act of taking goods or possessions by force or through conquest. It can also refer to the spoils or booty obtained from such an act. In biblical contexts, it is often used to describe the spoils of war or the plunder taken from defeated enemies.
