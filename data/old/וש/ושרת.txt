Etymology: The word "וֶשֶׁרֶת" (vesheret) is derived from the Hebrew root שׁרת (sh-r-t), which means "to serve" or "to minister." 

Grammar: "וֶשֶׁרֶת" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the singular form and is feminine in gender. 

Description: "וֶשֶׁרֶת" refers to a female servant or a female minister. In biblical times, women who served in the Tabernacle or the Temple were called "וֶשֶׁרֶת." They were responsible for various tasks related to the religious service, such as preparing the sacrifices, maintaining the sacred vessels, and assisting the priests.
