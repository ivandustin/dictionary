Etymology: The word "וְשִׁימוּ" (v'shimu) is a Hebrew verb in the imperative form. It comes from the root שׁ.מ.ע (sh.m.a), which means "to hear" or "to listen."

Grammar: "וְשִׁימוּ" is the plural imperative form of the verb. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. The prefix וְ (v') means "and" and is used to connect the imperative form to the subject of the sentence. The root שׁ.מ.ע (sh.m.a) is conjugated in the third person plural form, indicating that the command is directed at multiple individuals.

Description: The word "וְשִׁימוּ" is often translated as "listen" or "hear." It is used to command or urge someone to pay attention and give heed to what is being said. In Hebrew, listening is not just about hearing sounds, but also about understanding and responding to the message being conveyed.
