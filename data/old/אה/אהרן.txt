Etymology: The word "אהרן" (Aharon) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Hebrew root א-ה-ר (A-H-R), which means "to shine" or "to enlighten." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אהרן" (Aharon) is a masculine proper noun. It is typically used as a given name for boys. 

Description: Aharon is a biblical name that holds significant religious and cultural importance in Judaism. In the Hebrew Bible, Aharon is the older brother of Moses and the first High Priest of the Israelites. He played a crucial role in the liberation of the Israelites from slavery in Egypt and in the establishment of the priesthood. Aharon is revered as a symbol of leadership, loyalty, and devotion in Jewish tradition. The name Aharon is still commonly used today in Jewish communities around the world.
