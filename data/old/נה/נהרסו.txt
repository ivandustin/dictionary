Etymology: The word "נהרסו" (Neharso) is a Hebrew verb in the past tense, derived from the root נ-ה-ר-ס (N-H-R-S). 

Grammar: "נהרסו" is the third person plural form of the verb, indicating that the subject is plural and the action of being ruined or destroyed has already happened. 

Description: "נהרסו" means "they were ruined" or "they were destroyed" in Hebrew. It describes a state of destruction or collapse that has occurred in the past.
