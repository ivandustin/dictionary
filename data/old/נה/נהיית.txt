Etymology: The word "נהיה" (pronounced "nehiya") is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root נ.ה.י (N.H.Y), which means "to become" or "to be." It is derived from the Hebrew noun הַיָּה (hayah), which means "being" or "existence."

- Singular:
  - 1st person: אֲנִי נוֹהֵיה (ani noheh)
  - 2nd person: אַתָּה נוֹהֶה (ata noheh)
  - 3rd person: הוּא/הִיא נוֹהֶה (hu/hi noheh)

- Plural:
  - 1st person: אֲנַחְנוּ נוֹהִים (anachnu nohim)
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם נוֹהִים (atem nohim)
  - 3rd person: הֵם/הֵן נוֹהִים (hem/hen nohim)

Description: The word "נהיה" is used to express the act of becoming or being. It is often used to describe a change or transformation in a person or thing. It can also be used to indicate a state of existence or presence.
