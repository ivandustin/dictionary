Etymology: The word "יהודע" (Yehuda) comes from the Hebrew root י-ה-ו-ד, which means "to know" or "to make known." It is derived from the Hebrew name יהודה (Yehuda), which means "praise" or "thanksgiving."

Grammar: "יהודע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular:
  - אֲנִי יוֹדֵעַ (ani yodea) - I know
  - אַתָּה יוֹדֵעַ (atah yodea) - You know (masculine singular)
  - אַתְּ יוֹדֶעָה (at yode'ah) - You know (feminine singular)
  - הוּא יוֹדֵעַ (hu yodea) - He knows
  - הִיא יוֹדֶעָה (hi yode'ah) - She knows

- Plural:
  - אֲנַחְנוּ יוֹדְעִים (anachnu yod'im) - We know
  - אַתֶּם יוֹדְעִים (atem yod'im) - You know (masculine plural)
  - אַתֶּן יוֹדְעוֹת (aten yod'ot) - You know (feminine plural)
  - הֵם יוֹדְעִים (hem yod'im) - They know (masculine)
  - הֵן יוֹדְעוֹת (hen yod'ot) - They know (feminine)

Description: The word "יהודע" is used to express the action of knowing or being aware of something. It can refer to having knowledge or understanding about a particular subject or being familiar with someone or something.
