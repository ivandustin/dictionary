Etymology: The word "יהלכו" (Yehalku) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root ה-ל-ך (H-L-K), which means "to walk" or "to go".

Grammar: "יהלכו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb הלך (Halakh), which means "to walk" or "to go". In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to tense, person, and number.

Description: "יהלכו" is used to describe an action that will happen in the future, specifically when referring to a group of people. It can be translated as "they will walk" or "they will go" in English.
