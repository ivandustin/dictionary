Etymology: The word "יהרוס" (Yeharos) is a Hebrew word derived from the root ח.ר.ס (Ch.R.S), which means "to destroy" or "to demolish".

Grammar: "יהרוס" (Yeharos) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני יהרוס (Ani Yeharos) - I destroy
- Plural: אנחנו יהרוסים (Anachnu Yeharosim) - We destroy

Description: The word "יהרוס" (Yeharos) describes the action of destroying or demolishing something. It can be used in various contexts, such as destroying a building, breaking an object, or ruining a plan.
