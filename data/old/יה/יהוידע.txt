Etymology: The word "יהודע" (pronounced yehudá) comes from the Hebrew root י-ה-ד, which means "to know" or "to inform." It is derived from the Hebrew word ידע (yada), which means "knowledge" or "awareness."

Grammar: "יהודע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני יודע (ani yodea) - I know
- Singular feminine: אני יודעת (ani yoda'at) - I know
- Plural masculine: אנחנו יודעים (anachnu yod'im) - We know
- Plural feminine: אנחנו יודעות (anachnu yod'ot) - We know

Description: The word "יהודע" means "to know" or "to be aware" in Hebrew. It is used to express knowledge or understanding of something. It can also be used to indicate informing or notifying someone about something.
