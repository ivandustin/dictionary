Etymology: The word "יהקים" (Yahkim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root קום (kum), which means "to rise" or "to stand up." The prefix יה (Yah) is a shortened form of the divine name יהוה (Yahweh), indicating a connection to God. Therefore, יהקים can be translated as "God will establish" or "God will raise up."

Grammar: יהקים is a verb in the future tense, third person singular form. It is commonly used in biblical Hebrew.

Description: יהקים is a word that conveys the idea of God's action of establishing or raising up something or someone. It is often used in a prophetic or poetic context to describe God's intervention or divine plan.
