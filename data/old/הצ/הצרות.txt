Etymology: The word "הַצְרוּת" (HaTzrut) is a Hebrew noun derived from the root צ-ר-ה (ts-r-h), which means "to narrow" or "to constrict." 

Grammar: "הַצְרוּת" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is commonly used with the definite article "הַ" (ha) to mean "the narrowness" or "the constriction."

Description: "הַצְרוּת" refers to a state or condition of being narrow or constricted. It can be used both literally, to describe a physical narrowness or tightness, and metaphorically, to describe a feeling of constraint or limitation.
