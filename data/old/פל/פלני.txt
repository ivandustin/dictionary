Etymology: The word "פלני" (pronounced "ploni") comes from Hebrew and is used as a placeholder name, similar to the English term "John Doe" or "Jane Doe". It is commonly used when referring to an anonymous or unidentified person.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "פלני" is a masculine singular noun. It is often used with a definite article, as in "הפלני" (ha-ploni), meaning "the anonymous person" or "the unidentified person".

Description: "פלני" is a versatile term that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used in legal documents, official forms, or when referring to someone whose identity is unknown or irrelevant. It is also used in storytelling or anecdotes when the specific details of a person's identity are not important.
