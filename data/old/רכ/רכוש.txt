Etymology: The word "רְכוּשׁ" (rekush) comes from the Hebrew root ר-כ-שׁ (r-k-sh), which means "to possess" or "to acquire."

Grammar: In Hebrew, רְכוּשׁ is a noun that is used to refer to possessions, property, or wealth. It is typically used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form רְכוּשִׁים (rekushim).

Description: רְכוּשׁ is a term that encompasses various forms of material possessions, including money, land, buildings, and other valuable assets. It is often used in the context of ownership and wealth accumulation.
