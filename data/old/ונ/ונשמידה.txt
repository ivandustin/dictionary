Etymology: The word "ונשמידה" (pronounced "venashmidah") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root נ.ש.מ (n.sh.m), which means "to destroy" or "to annihilate." The prefix ו (vav) means "and," and the suffix ה (he) indicates a feminine noun. Therefore, ונשמידה translates to "and we will destroy" or "and we will annihilate."

Grammar: ונשמידה is a verb form in the future tense, first person plural, and imperative mood. It is used to express a command or a strong desire for the action of destruction or annihilation to occur.

Description: ונשמידה is a powerful and forceful word that conveys the intention to completely eliminate or eradicate something. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing determination to destroy an enemy, eradicate a problem, or eliminate a threat.
