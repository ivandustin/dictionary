Etymology: The word "לטמון" comes from the Hebrew root "ט.מ.נ" which means "to bury" or "to hide". 

Grammar: "לטמון" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני מטמין (ani matmin) - I bury, אתה מטמין (ata matmin) - you bury (masculine singular), את מטמינה (at matmina) - you bury (feminine singular), הוא/היא מטמין (hu/hi matmin) - he/she buries, אנחנו מטמינים (anachnu matminim) - we bury, אתם מטמינים (atem matminim) - you bury (masculine plural), אתן מטמינות (atan matminot) - you bury (feminine plural), הם/הן מטמינים (hem/hen matminim) - they bury.

Description: "לטמון" means "to bury" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of placing a dead body or an object underground or in a grave. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "to hide" or "to conceal" something.
