Etymology: The word "לטובים" (letovim) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root word "טוב" (tov), which means "good" or "beneficial". The prefix "ל" (le) in this word indicates "to" or "for". Therefore, "לטובים" can be translated as "for the good" or "for the benefit".

Grammar: "לטובים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ים" (-im) to the root word "טוב" (tov). The plural form indicates that it refers to multiple good things or benefits.

Description: "לטובים" is often used in Hebrew to express good wishes or blessings. It can be used as a standalone phrase or as part of a longer sentence. For example, "לטובים!" (letovim) can be translated as "To good things!" or "For the best!". It is commonly used to wish someone well or to express hope for positive outcomes.
