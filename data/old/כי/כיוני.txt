Etymology: The word "כִּיוּנִי" (kiyuni) comes from the Hebrew root כ.ו.נ (k.w.n), which means "to direct" or "to aim." It is derived from the noun כֵּן (ken), meaning "direction" or "intention."

Grammar: "כִּיוּנִי" is a Hebrew adjective that means "intentional" or "purposeful." It is used to describe actions or behaviors that are done with a specific aim or goal in mind.

Description: The word "כִּיוּנִי" is often used to describe someone who is focused, determined, and deliberate in their actions. It implies that the person has a clear intention or purpose behind what they do.
