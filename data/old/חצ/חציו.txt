Etymology: The word "חֲצִיו" (pronounced "chatziyu") comes from the Hebrew root חֶצְיָה (chatziyah), which means "half" or "middle."

Grammar: "חֲצִיו" is a noun in Hebrew. It is the construct form of the word חֲצִי (chatzi), which means "half." The suffix וֹ (o) indicates possession, so חֲצִיו means "his half" or "its half."

Description: חֲצִיו is used to describe the possession or ownership of something. It indicates that something belongs to someone or something in terms of a half or a portion.
