Etymology: The word "רמס" (pronounced "ramas" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root ר.מ.ס (r.m.s), which means "to crawl" or "to creep". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, רמס is a verb in the third person singular form. It can also be conjugated in other forms, such as רָמַסְתִּי (ramasti) for first person singular, רָמַסְתָּ (ramasta) for second person singular, and so on.

Description: רמס is used to describe the action of crawling or creeping on the ground, typically done by animals or insects. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is behaving in a sneaky or deceitful manner.
