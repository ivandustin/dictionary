Etymology: The word "בגזל" (begazal) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root גזל (gazal), which means "to steal" or "to rob".

Grammar: "בגזל" (begazal) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני בוגזל (ani bogezel) - I steal
- Plural: אנחנו בוגזלים (anachnu bogezelim) - We steal

Description: "בגזל" (begazal) is a word that describes the act of stealing or robbing. It can refer to taking someone else's property without permission or unlawfully.
