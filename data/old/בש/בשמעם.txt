Etymology: The word "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is a Hebrew word that means "in the name of." It is derived from the root שם (shem), which means "name."

Grammar: "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition בְּ (b') meaning "in" or "with," and the noun שֵׁם (shem) meaning "name." It is used to indicate that an action or statement is done or made on behalf of someone or something.

Description: "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is a common phrase used in Jewish and Hebrew religious contexts. It is often used to introduce a prayer, blessing, or dedication, indicating that the action or words are being done or said in the name of God or a specific religious figure.
