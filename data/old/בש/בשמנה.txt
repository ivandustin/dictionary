Etymology: The word "בְּשֵׁמֶן" (b'shemen) comes from the Hebrew root שמן (sh-m-n), which means "oil." 

Grammar: "בְּשֵׁמֶן" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "בְּ" (b') means "in" or "with," and "שֵׁמֶן" (shemen) means "oil." Together, it means "in oil" or "with oil."

Description: "בְּשֵׁמֶן" is used in various contexts in the Hebrew language. It can refer to cooking or frying food in oil, anointing with oil for religious or ceremonial purposes, or using oil for medicinal or cosmetic purposes. The word is commonly found in biblical texts and is still used in modern Hebrew.
