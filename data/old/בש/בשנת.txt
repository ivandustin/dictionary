Etymology: The word "בְּשֵׁנֶת" (beshenat) is a Hebrew word that means "in the year." It is derived from the root word "שָׁנָה" (shanah), which means "year."

Grammar: "בְּשֵׁנֶת" (beshenat) is a prepositional phrase that consists of the preposition "בְּ" (be), meaning "in," and the noun "שָׁנָה" (shanah), meaning "year." It is used to indicate a specific year or time period.

Description: "בְּשֵׁנֶת" (beshenat) is commonly used in Hebrew to specify a particular year. It is often followed by a number or a specific event to indicate the year in which something happened. For example, "בְּשֵׁנֶת אֶלֶף תְּשַׁעְיָה וְעֶשְׂרִים" (beshenat elef teshayyah ve'esrim) means "in the year 1920."
