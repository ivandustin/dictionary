Etymology: The word "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is a Hebrew word that means "in the name of." It is derived from the Hebrew root שם (shem), which means "name."

Grammar: "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition בְּ (b') meaning "in" or "with," and the noun שֵׁם (shem) meaning "name." It is used to indicate that an action or statement is done or made in the name of someone or something.

Description: The phrase "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is commonly used in Jewish and Hebrew religious contexts. It is often used to introduce a prayer, a blessing, or a dedication, indicating that the action or words are being done or said in the name of God or a specific deity. It is also used in legal documents or contracts to indicate that an agreement is made in the name of a person or an organization.
