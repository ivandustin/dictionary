Etymology: The word "בְּשָׁלוֹשָׁה" (b'shaloshah) is a Hebrew word that means "in three." It is derived from the root שׁ.ל.שׁ (sh.l.sh), which means "three."

Grammar: "בְּשָׁלוֹשָׁה" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition בְּ (b') means "in" or "with," and the noun שָׁלוֹשָׁה (shaloshah) means "three." Together, they form the phrase "in three."

Description: The phrase "בְּשָׁלוֹשָׁה" is used to indicate the number three or to describe something that is done or occurs in a group of three. It can be used in various contexts, such as counting, measurements, or describing a specific quantity.
