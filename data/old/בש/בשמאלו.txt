Etymology: The word "בְּשֶׁמְאֵלוֹ" (beshem'elo) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in his name." It is composed of the preposition "בְּ" (be), meaning "in," and the noun "שֵׁם" (shem), meaning "name," followed by the possessive pronoun "אֵלוֹ" (elo), meaning "his."

Grammar: "בְּשֶׁמְאֵלוֹ" (beshem'elo) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "בְּ" (be) indicates the location or state of being "in," and the noun "שֵׁם" (shem) functions as the object of the preposition, meaning "name." The possessive pronoun "אֵלוֹ" (elo) indicates possession, meaning "his."

Description: The phrase "בְּשֶׁמְאֵלוֹ" (beshem'elo) is commonly used in Hebrew to indicate that something is done or said in the name of someone. It is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts to express dedication or attribution to a higher power.
