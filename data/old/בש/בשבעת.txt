Etymology: The word "בְּשֶׁבַעְתָּ" (b'shevat) comes from the Hebrew root "שבע" (sh-v-a), which means "seven". The suffix "-תָּ" (-ta) indicates the feminine form.

Grammar: "בְּשֶׁבַעְתָּ" (b'shevat) is a noun in the singular form. It is used to describe the seventh day of the week in the Hebrew calendar, which is Saturday.

Description: In Jewish tradition, the seventh day of the week, Saturday, is considered a day of rest and is known as the Sabbath. It is a holy day and is observed by refraining from work and engaging in prayer, study, and spending time with family. The Sabbath begins at sundown on Friday and ends at sundown on Saturday.
