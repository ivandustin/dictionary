Description: "בֶּשֶׁר" (baser) refers to the edible flesh of animals, particularly mammals. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to the physical body or human nature. In Jewish dietary laws (kashrut), there are specific rules and restrictions regarding the consumption of meat.
