Etymology: The word "בְּשֶׁמִי" (b'shemi) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in my name." It is derived from the Hebrew root word "שֵׁם" (shem), which means "name."

Grammar: "בְּשֶׁמִי" (b'shemi) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "בְּ" (b') means "in" or "with," and "שֵׁמִי" (shemi) means "my name." Together, they form the phrase "in my name."

Description: The phrase "בְּשֶׁמִי" (b'shemi) is used in Hebrew to indicate that something is done or said on behalf of someone or in their name. It is often used in religious or formal contexts, such as when making a declaration or taking an oath.
