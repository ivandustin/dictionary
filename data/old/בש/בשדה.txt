Etymology: The word "בְּשֵׁדֶה" (b'sheda) comes from the Hebrew root שׁדה (sh.d.h), which means "field" or "open country."

Grammar: "בְּשֵׁדֶה" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "בְּ" (b') means "in" or "at," and "שֵׁדֶה" (sheda) means "field." Together, they form the phrase "in the field" or "at the field."

Description: "בְּשֵׁדֶה" is used to describe a location or situation that takes place in a field. It can refer to various contexts, such as agricultural activities, outdoor events, or simply being in an open area.
