Grammar: "בֶּשֶׁת" is a noun in Hebrew and is used to describe the feeling of shame or embarrassment. It is typically used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form "בָּשְׁתוֹת" (bashot).

Description: "בֶּשֶׁת" refers to the emotional state of feeling ashamed or embarrassed. It is often associated with a sense of discomfort or humiliation due to a perceived failure, mistake, or social judgment. The concept of "בֶּשֶׁת" is significant in Jewish and Hebrew culture, as it is considered a moral and ethical value to avoid actions that may lead to shame or embarrassment.
