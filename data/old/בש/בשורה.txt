Etymology: The word "בשורה" (b'shura) comes from the Hebrew root "ב-ש-ר" (b-sh-r), which means "to announce" or "to proclaim." It is derived from the noun "בשור" (b'shor), which means "good news" or "tidings."

Grammar: "בשורה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. In the plural form, it becomes "בשורות" (b'shorot).

Description: "בשורה" refers to "good news" or "glad tidings." It is often used to describe positive or joyful information that is shared or announced. The word can be used in various contexts, such as receiving good news about a personal achievement, hearing about a happy event, or learning about positive developments in the world.
