Etymology: The word "בְּשָׁעֵר" (b'sha'er) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root שער (sha'ar), which means "gate" or "entrance". 

Grammar: "בְּשָׁעֵר" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition בְּ (b') means "in" or "at", and שָׁעֵר (sha'er) means "gate" or "entrance". Together, it means "at the gate" or "in the entrance".

Description: "בְּשָׁעֵר" is used to describe a location or position at the gate or entrance of a place. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone waiting at the gate, standing at the entrance, or being positioned near a gate.
