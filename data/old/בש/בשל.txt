Etymology: The word "בֵּשֶׁל" (beshel) comes from the Hebrew root ב-ש-ל (b-sh-l), which means "to cook" or "to boil." 

Grammar: "בֵּשֶׁל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the past tense as "בִּשֵּׁלְתִי" (bishel'ti) for the first person singular, "בִּשַּׁלְתָּ" (bishalta) for the second person singular masculine, "בִּשַּׁלְתְּ" (bishalt) for the second person singular feminine, and so on.

Description: "בֵּשֶׁל" means "to cook" or "to boil" in Hebrew. It refers to the process of preparing food by heating it until it is fully cooked or softened. This verb is commonly used in the context of cooking meals or ingredients.
