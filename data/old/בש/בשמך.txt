Etymology: The word "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is a Hebrew word that means "in the name of." It is derived from the Hebrew root שם (shem), which means "name."

Grammar: "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition בְּ (b') meaning "in" or "with," and the noun שֵׁם (shem) meaning "name."

Description: The phrase "בְּשֵׁם" (b'shem) is commonly used in Hebrew to indicate that something is done or said in the name of someone or something. It is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts, such as when reciting prayers or performing rituals. It signifies that the action or statement is being done as a representation or on behalf of the person or entity whose name is mentioned.
