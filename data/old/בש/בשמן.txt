Etymology: The word "בְּשֶׁם" (b'shem) is a Hebrew word that means "in the name of." It is derived from the Hebrew root שם (shem), which means "name."

Grammar: "בְּשֶׁם" (b'shem) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It is composed of the preposition בְּ (b') meaning "in" or "with," and the noun שֵׁם (shem) meaning "name." It is used to indicate that an action or statement is done or made in the name of someone or something.

Description: "בְּשֶׁם" (b'shem) is a common phrase used in Hebrew to express that something is done or said on behalf of someone or something else. It is often used in religious or formal contexts, such as when making a dedication or giving credit to someone.
