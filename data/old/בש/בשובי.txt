Etymology: The word "בְּשׁוֹבִי" (b'shovi) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root שׁ.וֹ.ב (sh.o.v), which means "to return" or "to come back."

Grammar: "בְּשׁוֹבִי" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition בְּ (b') means "in" or "with," and the noun שׁוֹבִי (shovi) means "return" or "coming back." Together, the phrase can be translated as "in my return" or "with my coming back."

Description: The word "בְּשׁוֹבִי" is used to express the idea of returning or coming back. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's physical return to a place or their return to a previous state or condition.
