Etymology: The word "אנה" (pronounced "anah") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root אנה, which means "to answer" or "to respond."

Grammar: "אנה" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני עונה (ani oneh) - I answer
- Masculine singular: אתה עונה (atah oneh) - You answer (masculine)
- Feminine singular: את עונה (at oneh) - You answer (feminine)
- Masculine plural: אתם עונים (atem onim) - You answer (masculine plural)
- Feminine plural: אתן עונות (aten onot) - You answer (feminine plural)

Description: The word "אנה" is used to describe the action of responding or answering to a question, request, or statement. It can also be used metaphorically to indicate a reaction or response to a situation or circumstance.
