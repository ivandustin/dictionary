Etymology: The word "אמור" (amor) comes from the Hebrew root א-מ-ר (aleph-mem-resh), which means "to say" or "to speak". 

Grammar: "אמור" is a verb in the imperative form, which is used to give commands or instructions. It is the second person singular masculine form, meaning "you say" or "you should say". 

Description: "אמור" is a common word in Hebrew and is used in various contexts. It is often used in the Bible to indicate a command or instruction given by God to the Israelites. In modern Hebrew, it is used to give instructions or to express a request for someone to say something.
