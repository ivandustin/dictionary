Etymology: The word "אמטיר" (amteer) is derived from the Hebrew root א-מ-ט-ר (aleph-mem-tet-resh), which means "to rain" or "to shower". 

Grammar: "אמטיר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני ממטיר (ani memteer) - I rain, אתה ממטיר (atah memteer) - you rain (masculine singular), את ממטירת (at memteeret) - you rain (feminine singular), הוא ממטיר (hu memteer) - he rains, היא ממטירה (hi memteerah) - she rains, אנחנו ממטירים (anachnu memteerim) - we rain, אתם ממטירים (atem memteerim) - you rain (masculine plural), אתן ממטירות (atan memteerot) - you rain (feminine plural), הם ממטירים (hem memteerim) - they rain (masculine), הן ממטירות (hen memteerot) - they rain (feminine).

Description: "אמטיר" is used to describe the action of raining or showering. It is often used in the context of weather or precipitation.
