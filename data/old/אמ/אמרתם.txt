Grammar: "אמרתם" is a verb in the past tense, second person plural form. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated based on the subject, tense, and number. The suffix "-תם" (-tem) indicates that the subject is second person plural (you all). The root א-מ-ר (a-m-r) remains unchanged.

Description: "אמרתם" is used to address a group of people in the past tense, indicating that they said or spoke something. It is commonly used in conversation or storytelling to refer to the collective speech of a group.
