Etymology: The word "אמתינו" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the words "אמת" (emet) meaning "truth" and "ינו" (inu) which is a possessive suffix meaning "our". Therefore, "אמתינו" translates to "our truth" or "the truth of us".

Grammar: "אמתינו" is a noun in Hebrew. It is in the singular form and is used to refer to the truth that belongs to a group of people. In this case, it signifies the truth that is shared by a specific group or community.

Description: "אמתינו" is a powerful and meaningful word that emphasizes the importance of truth within a collective context. It implies a sense of unity and shared values among a group of individuals. The word can be used to express the idea that there is a common truth that binds people together and serves as a guiding principle for their actions and beliefs.
