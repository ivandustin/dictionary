Etymology: The word "וְכִזְאָת" (pronounced "v'kiz'at" in Hebrew) is a combination of two words: "וְ" (pronounced "v'") meaning "and" and "כִּזֵּאת" (pronounced "kiz'at") meaning "like this". 

Grammar: "וְכִזְאָת" is a conjunction in Hebrew, used to connect two phrases or clauses. It is commonly translated as "and thus" or "and in this manner". 

Description: "וְכִזְאָת" is a versatile phrase that is often used to introduce a new idea or to summarize a previous statement. It is commonly used in storytelling, explanations, or when presenting examples.
