Etymology: The word "וְכָל־ם" (pronounced "v'cholam") is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "וְ" (pronounced "v'") meaning "and" and "כָּל־ם" (pronounced "cholam") meaning "all of them". 

Grammar: "וְכָל־ם" is a conjunction phrase in Hebrew, combining the conjunction "וְ" (meaning "and") with the noun phrase "כָּל־ם" (meaning "all of them"). It is used to connect multiple elements or ideas together.

Description: "וְכָל־ם" is a versatile phrase that can be used in various contexts. It is often used to express inclusiveness or totality, indicating that something applies to all the mentioned elements or individuals. It can be used in both formal and informal speech and writing.
