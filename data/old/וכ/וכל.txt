Etymology: The word "וּכָל" (pronounced "ukol" in Hebrew) is a conjunction composed of the prefix "וּ" (meaning "and") and the word "כֹּל" (meaning "all"). 

Grammar: "וּכָל" is used to connect two or more elements in a sentence, indicating that all of them are included. It is commonly translated as "and all" or "and every" in English.

Description: "וּכָל" is a versatile word that is frequently used in Hebrew texts to express inclusiveness or totality. It is often used to emphasize that something applies to every item or person in a group.
