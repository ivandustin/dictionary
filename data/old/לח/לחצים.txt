Etymology: The word "לחצים" (lachatzim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root "חצץ" (chatzatz), which means "to squeeze" or "to press."

Grammar: "לחצים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ים" (-im) to the singular form "לחץ" (lachatz).

Description: "לחצים" refers to the act of squeezing or pressing something tightly. It can be used to describe physical pressure or figurative pressure in various contexts.
