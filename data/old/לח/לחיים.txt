Etymology: The word "לחיים" is a Hebrew term that translates to "to life" in English. It is commonly used as a toast or a celebratory expression in Jewish culture.

Grammar: "לחיים" is a noun phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition "ל" (meaning "to" or "for") and the noun "חיים" (meaning "life"). The preposition "ל" indicates the direction or purpose of the noun, in this case, expressing a wish or a toast for life.

Description: "לחיים" is a versatile phrase that is often used to celebrate or toast to good health, happiness, and longevity. It is commonly used during festive occasions, such as weddings, birthdays, or other joyous events. The phrase carries a positive and optimistic connotation, symbolizing the importance of cherishing and embracing life's blessings.
