Etymology: The word "לחול" (lachol) comes from the Hebrew root "ח.ו.ל" (ch.w.l), which means "to profane" or "to desecrate". 

Grammar: "לחול" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is the base form of the verb and does not indicate tense or subject. It can be conjugated to match different subjects and tenses.

Description: "לחול" is often translated as "to profane" or "to desecrate" in English. It refers to the act of treating something sacred or holy with disrespect or irreverence. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of violating or disregarding a rule, tradition, or value.
