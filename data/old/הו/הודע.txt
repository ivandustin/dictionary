Etymology: The word "הודע" (pronounced "hoda'a") comes from the Hebrew root ה.ו.ד (h.w.d), which means "to inform" or "to announce." It is derived from the noun הֹודָעָה (hoda'ah), which means "message" or "announcement."

Grammar: "הודע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in various forms to match the subject and tense of the sentence. For example, in the present tense, it can be conjugated as אֲנִי מוֹדִיע (ani modi'a) for "I inform" or אַתָּה מוֹדִיע (atah modi'a) for "you inform."

Description: The word "הודע" means "to inform" or "to notify" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of conveying information or making someone aware of something. It can be used in various contexts, such as informing someone about an event, sharing news, or giving instructions.
