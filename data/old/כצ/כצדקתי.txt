Etymology: The word "כְּצַדְקָתִי" (k'tzadkati) is a Hebrew word that translates to "according to my righteousness" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root צדק (tz-d-k), which means "to be righteous" or "to be just."

Grammar: "כְּצַדְקָתִי" is a prepositional phrase composed of the preposition כְּ (k') meaning "according to" and the noun צַדְקָה (tz'dakah) meaning "righteousness." The suffix י (i) indicates the possessive form, meaning "my."

Description: This phrase is often used in religious or moral contexts to express the idea of acting or being judged based on one's own righteousness or moral standing. It can be found in various religious texts and prayers.
