Etymology: The word "תראה" (tar'eh) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ראה (ra'ah), which means "to see" or "to perceive". The prefix ת (ta) in this word indicates the second person singular form, making it mean "you will see" or "you shall see".

Grammar: "תראה" (tar'eh) is a verb in the future tense, second person singular form. It is used when addressing someone informally and telling them that they will see something in the future.

Description: "תראה" (tar'eh) is a command form in Hebrew, instructing someone to look or pay attention to something that will happen in the future. It is often used to express confidence or assurance that the person being addressed will witness or experience something specific.
