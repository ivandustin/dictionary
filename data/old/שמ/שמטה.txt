Etymology: The word "שמטה" (shematah) comes from the Hebrew root שמט (shamat), which means "to release" or "to let go". 

Grammar: "שמטה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is the singular form of the word, and its plural form is "שמטות" (shemtot). 

Description: In Hebrew, "שמטה" refers to a release or a letting go. It can be used in various contexts, such as releasing someone from a duty or obligation, letting go of something physically or metaphorically, or even referring to a dismissal or termination.
