Etymology: The word "וַחֲרֻמִים" (vacharumim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root חָרַם (charam), which means "to devote" or "to consecrate." 

Grammar: "וַחֲרֻמִים" is a plural noun in the masculine form. It is formed by adding the prefix וַ- (va-) to the root חָרַם (charam), and the suffix יִם (-im) to indicate the plural form.

Description: "וַחֲרֻמִים" refers to something or someone that is devoted or consecrated. In biblical context, it often refers to objects or people that are set apart for religious purposes or that are under a ban of destruction.
