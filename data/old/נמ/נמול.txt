Etymology: The word "נמול" (pronounced "namol") comes from the Hebrew root נ.מ.ל (N.M.L), which means "to crawl" or "to creep."

It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני נומל (ani nomel) - I crawl, אתה נומל (ata nomel) - you crawl (masculine singular), את נומלת (at nomlet) - you crawl (feminine singular), הוא נומל (hu nomel) - he crawls, היא נומלת (hi nomlet) - she crawls, אנחנו נומלים (anachnu nomlim) - we crawl, אתם נומלים (atem nomlim) - you crawl (masculine plural), אתן נומלות (atan nomlot) - you crawl (feminine plural), הם נומלים (hem nomlim) - they crawl (masculine), הן נומלות (hen nomlot) - they crawl (feminine).

Description: The word "נמול" refers to the action of crawling or creeping on the ground, typically done by animals or young children. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who is moving slowly or cautiously.
