Etymology: The word "חֳדֵל" (chodel) comes from the Hebrew root חדל (ch.d.l), which means "to cease" or "to stop."

Grammar: "חֳדֵל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי חוֹדֵל (ani chodel) - I cease
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה חוֹדֵל (ata chodel) - You (masculine) cease
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ חוֹדֶלֶת (at chodelet) - You (feminine) cease
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם חוֹדְלִים (atem chodlim) - You (masculine plural) cease
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן חוֹדְלוֹת (aten chodlot) - You (feminine plural) cease

Description: "חֳדֵל" means "to cease" or "to stop" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of ending or discontinuing something.
