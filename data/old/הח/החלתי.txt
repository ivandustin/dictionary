Etymology: The word "החלתי" (HaChalti) is a Hebrew verb in the past tense, derived from the root ח.ל.ת (Ch.L.T), which means "to begin" or "to start."

Grammar: "החלתי" is the first-person singular form of the verb in the past tense. In Hebrew, verbs change their form based on the subject and tense of the sentence. The prefix "ה" (Ha) indicates the past tense, and the suffix "תי" (ti) indicates the first-person singular subject.

Description: "החלתי" is used to express that the speaker has started or begun an action or process in the past. It can be translated as "I started" or "I began" in English.
