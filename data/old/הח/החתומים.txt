Etymology: The word "החתומים" (HaChatumim) is a Hebrew word that translates to "the signatures" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root ח.ת.מ (chet-tet-mem), which means "to seal" or "to sign."

Grammar: "החתומים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the definite article "ה" (ha) at the beginning, which means "the," and the suffix "-ים" (-im) at the end, which indicates plural masculine nouns.

Description: "החתומים" refers to the signatures or signed documents. It is commonly used to describe official documents, contracts, or agreements that have been signed by multiple parties. The word emphasizes the importance and validity of the signatures on these documents.
