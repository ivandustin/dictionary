Etymology: The Hebrew word "החדשים" (HaChadashim) is derived from the root חדש (chadash), which means "new" or "fresh" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "החדשים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root word חדש (chadash). The definite article "ה" (ha) is added at the beginning to indicate "the".

Description: "החדשים" refers to "the new ones" or "the fresh ones" in Hebrew. It can be used to describe a group of people, objects, or ideas that are new or recently introduced.
