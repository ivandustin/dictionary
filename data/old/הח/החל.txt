Etymology: The Hebrew word "החל" (ha'hal) comes from the root ח-ל-ל (ch-l-l), which means "to begin" or "to initiate."

Grammar: "החל" (ha'hal) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the past tense as הֵחִיל (hechil), in the present tense as מַחְלִיל (machil), and in the future tense as יַחְלִיל (yachil).

Description: "החל" (ha'hal) means "to begin" or "to initiate" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the start of an action, process, or event.
