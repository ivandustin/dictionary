Etymology: The word "בריחים" (pronounced "brihim") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ב-ר-ח, which means "to flee" or "to escape." It is derived from the noun בְּרִיחַ (b'riach), which means "a flight" or "an escape."

Grammar: "בריחים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (-im) to the singular form בריח (briach). The plural form is used to refer to multiple instances of flights or escapes.

Description: "בריחים" refers to flights or escapes, typically from a dangerous or undesirable situation. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the escape of prisoners, refugees, or animals. The word implies a sense of urgency and the act of quickly getting away from a threatening or confining environment.
