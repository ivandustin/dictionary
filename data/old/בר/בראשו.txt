Etymology: The word "בראשו" (pronounced "b'rosho") is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in his head" or "in his mind." It is composed of the preposition "ב" (pronounced "b") meaning "in" or "at," and the noun "ראש" (pronounced "rosh") meaning "head."

Grammar: "בראשו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" indicates location or possession, and the noun "ראש" functions as the object of the preposition. The suffix "ו" indicates possession, meaning "his head" or "his mind."

Description: The phrase "בראשו" is used to describe something that is primarily or solely in someone's mind or thoughts. It can refer to an idea, concept, plan, or belief that exists only in one's head and has not been expressed or put into action.
