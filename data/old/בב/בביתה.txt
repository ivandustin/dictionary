Etymology: The word "בביתה" (betiha) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "ב" (bet), meaning "in" or "at," and "ביתה" (baita), meaning "her house." 

Grammar: "בביתה" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (bet) indicates location or position, and "ביתה" (baita) is the possessive form of the noun "בית" (bayit), meaning "house." Together, "בביתה" means "in her house" or "at her house."

Description: "בביתה" is used to describe a location or position, specifically referring to being inside or at someone's house. It is commonly used in Hebrew to indicate where someone is or where an action takes place.
