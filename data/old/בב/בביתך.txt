Etymology: The word "בביתך" is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "ב" (bet) meaning "in" or "at" and "ביתך" (beitecha) meaning "your house". 

Grammar: "בביתך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (bet) means "in" or "at", and "ביתך" (beitecha) is the possessive form of the noun "בית" (bayit) meaning "house". The suffix "-ך" (-cha) indicates the second person singular possessive form, meaning "your".

Description: "בביתך" is used to indicate the location of an action or event, specifically referring to someone's house. It can be translated as "in your house" or "at your house" in English.
