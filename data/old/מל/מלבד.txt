Etymology: The word "מלבד" (melavd) comes from the Hebrew root לבד (l-b-d), which means "alone" or "separate". 

Grammar: "מלבד" is a preposition in Hebrew. It is used to indicate exclusion or exception. It is often translated as "except" or "besides" in English. 

Description: "מלבד" is used to specify that something or someone is excluded from a general statement or category. It is commonly used to express exceptions or exclusions. For example, "אני אוהב את כל הפירות, מלבד התפוחים" (I love all fruits, except apples).
