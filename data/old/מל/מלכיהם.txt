Etymology: The word "מלכיהם" (Melkhiham) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "מלכים" (Melkhim) meaning "kings" and "הם" (ham) meaning "their". Therefore, "מלכיהם" can be translated as "their kings".

Grammar: "מלכיהם" is a noun in Hebrew. It is in the plural form, indicating multiple kings. The suffix "הם" (ham) is added to the noun "מלכים" (Melkhim) to indicate possession, meaning "their kings".

Description: "מלכיהם" is used to refer to a group of kings who belong to someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as historical accounts, biblical stories, or figurative expressions.
