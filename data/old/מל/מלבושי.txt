Etymology: The word "מלבושי" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the words "מלבוש" (malbush) meaning "clothing" and "י" (i) which is a possessive suffix indicating "my". Therefore, "מלבושי" translates to "my clothing" or "my garments".

Grammar: "מלבושי" is a possessive noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the possessive suffix "י" (i) to the noun "מלבוש" (malbush). The possessive suffix indicates ownership or possession, in this case, "my".

Description: "מלבושי" is a Hebrew word that means "my clothing" or "my garments". It is used to refer to the clothes or attire that belongs to the speaker.
