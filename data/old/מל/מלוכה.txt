Etymology: The word "מִלּוּכָה" (melukha) comes from the Hebrew root ל.כ.ה (l.k.h), which means "to work" or "to labor." It is derived from the noun מֶלֶךְ (melekh), meaning "king," and carries the connotation of work done by a king or ruler.

Grammar: "מִלּוּכָה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. In the plural form, it becomes "מְלָאכוֹת" (melakhot).

Description: "מִלּוּכָה" refers to a task or work that requires effort and skill. It can also refer to a specific type of work or occupation. In Jewish law, it is often used to describe activities that are prohibited on the Sabbath, as the Sabbath is a day of rest and refraining from work.
