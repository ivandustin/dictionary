Etymology: The word "מלחשים" (melachshim) is derived from the Hebrew root לחש (lamed-chet-shin), which means "to whisper" or "to speak softly."

Grammar: "מלחשים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root word מלחש (melachash).

Description: "מלחשים" refers to people who whisper or speak softly. It can also be used metaphorically to describe individuals who engage in secretive or confidential conversations.
