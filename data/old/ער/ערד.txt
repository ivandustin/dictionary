Etymology: The word "ערד" (pronounced "er-d") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the Hebrew root ע-ר-ד, which means "to flee" or "to escape."

- Singular:
  - 1st person: אָנִי עוֹרֵד (ani ored) - I flee
  - 2nd person: אַתָּה עוֹרֵד (ata ored) - You (masculine) flee
  - 3rd person: הוּא עוֹרֵד (hu ored) - He flees, הִיא עוֹרֶדֶת (hi oredet) - She flees

- Plural:
  - 1st person: אֲנַחְנוּ עוֹרְדִים (anachnu ordim) - We flee
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם עוֹרְדִים (atem ordim) - You (masculine plural) flee, אַתֶּן עוֹרְדוֹת (aten ordot) - You (feminine plural) flee
  - 3rd person: הֵם עוֹרְדִים (hem ordim) - They (masculine) flee, הֵן עוֹרְדוֹת (hen ordot) - They (feminine) flee

Description: The verb "ערד" is used to describe the action of fleeing or escaping from a place or situation. It can be used in various contexts, such as escaping danger, running away from a person or a situation, or avoiding something unpleasant.
