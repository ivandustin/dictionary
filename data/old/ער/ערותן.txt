Etymology: The word "ערותן" (pronounced "arutan") is derived from the Hebrew root ע.ר.ה (ayin, resh, hey), which means "nakedness" or "exposure."

Grammar: "ערותן" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the masculine form and is singular. In plural form, it becomes "ערותנים" (arutanim).

Description: "ערותן" refers to the state of being naked or exposed. It can be used to describe physical nakedness or metaphorical exposure, such as revealing one's secrets or vulnerabilities. The word is often used in the context of modesty and privacy.
