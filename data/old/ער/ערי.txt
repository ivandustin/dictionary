Etymology: The word "ערי" (pronounced "eri") is a Hebrew word that means "awake" or "arise". It is derived from the Hebrew root ע-ר-ה (ayin-resh-hei), which conveys the idea of waking up or becoming alert.

Grammar: "ערי" is a verb in the imperative form, which is used to give commands or instructions. In this form, it is addressing someone in the second person singular, instructing them to wake up or arise.

Description: The word "ערי" is often used in Hebrew as a call to action or encouragement to be alert and active. It can be used in various contexts, such as waking up in the morning, getting ready for a new day, or metaphorically, to encourage someone to be proactive and engaged.
