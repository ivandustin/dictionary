Etymology: The word "ערכתי" (erkati) comes from the Hebrew root ע-ר-כ (ayin-resh-kaf), which means "to arrange" or "to set in order." It is derived from the noun עֵרֶךְ (erekh), meaning "order" or "arrangement."

Grammar: "ערכתי" (erkati) is the first-person singular past tense form of the verb עָרַךְ (arakh), which means "to arrange" or "to set in order." It is conjugated in the past tense, masculine, and singular form.

Description: "ערכתי" (erkati) is used to describe an action that was performed in the past by the speaker. It indicates that the speaker arranged or set something in order at some point in the past.
