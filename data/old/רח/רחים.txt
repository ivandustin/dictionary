Etymology: The word "רחים" comes from the Hebrew root ר-ח-ם, which means "to have compassion" or "to show mercy". It is derived from the noun רַחֲמִים (rachamim), which means "mercy" or "compassion".

Grammar: "רחים" is a masculine plural noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to the internal organs, specifically the kidneys. In biblical Hebrew, it can also be used metaphorically to represent the seat of emotions and feelings.

Description: In Hebrew, "רחים" refers to the kidneys, which are vital organs responsible for filtering waste products from the blood and producing urine. In biblical and poetic contexts, it can also symbolize the seat of emotions, compassion, and mercy. The word is often used metaphorically to describe deep feelings or emotions.
