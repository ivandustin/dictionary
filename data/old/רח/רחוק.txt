Etymology: The word "רחוק" (pronounced "rachok") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ר-ח-ק, which means "to be far" or "to distance oneself."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "רחוק" is an adjective that means "far" or "distant." It can be used to describe physical distance or metaphorical distance, such as emotional or relational distance.

Description: "רחוק" is used to indicate a significant distance between two objects or concepts. It can refer to a physical distance between two locations or the emotional distance between people. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a long period of time or a remote possibility.
