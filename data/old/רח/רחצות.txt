Etymology: The word "רחצות" (rachatzot) comes from the Hebrew root ר.ח.צ (r.ch.ts), which means "to wash" or "to bathe". 

Grammar: "רחצות" is a noun in the plural form. In Hebrew, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and function in a sentence. The plural form of "רחצות" indicates that it refers to multiple instances of washing or bathing.

Description: "רחצות" refers to the act of washing or bathing. It can be used to describe the action of cleaning oneself or an object with water or other liquids. In a broader sense, it can also refer to the ritual washing performed in Jewish religious practices, such as before prayer or meals.
