Etymology: The word "רחצו" (rachatzu) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ר.ח.צ (r.ch.ts), which means "to wash" or "to bathe". 

Grammar: "רחצו" is the imperative form of the verb, used to give a command or instruction. It is the plural form, addressing a group of people. 

Description: "רחצו" is a command in Hebrew, instructing a group of people to wash or bathe. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as in personal hygiene, ritual purification, or cleaning.
