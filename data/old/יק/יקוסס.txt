Etymology: The word "יקוסס" (pronounced "yikusas") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ק.ו.ס (k.w.s), which means "to be proud" or "to boast."

Grammar: "יקוסס" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני יקוסס (ani yikusas) - I boast
- Plural: אנחנו יקוססים (anachnu yikusasim) - We boast

Description: The word "יקוסס" is used to describe the act of boasting or being proud of one's achievements, qualities, or possessions. It can also imply arrogance or excessive pride.
