Etymology: The word "להכות" (lehakot) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root כ.ו.ת (k.w.t), which means "to strike" or "to hit".

Grammar: "להכות" is an infinitive verb form in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מַכֶּה (ani maka), אתה מַכֶּה (ata maka), הוא/היא מַכֶּה (hu/hi maka)
- Plural: אנחנו מַכְּאִים (anachnu mak'im), אתם מַכְּאִים (atem mak'im), הם/הן מַכְּאוֹת (hem/hen mak'ot)

Description: The word "להכות" means "to strike" or "to hit" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of physically hitting or striking someone or something.
