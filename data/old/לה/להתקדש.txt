Etymology: The word "להתקדש" (lehiskadesh) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root ק.ד.ש (k.d.sh), which means "to be holy" or "to sanctify."

Grammar: "להתקדש" is an infinitive verb form in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the reflexive verb group, indicating that the subject is performing the action on itself. In this case, it means "to sanctify oneself" or "to become holy."

Description: "להתקדש" is a verb that conveys the idea of dedicating oneself to a higher purpose or becoming spiritually pure. It is often used in religious contexts to describe the act of sanctifying oneself or seeking holiness.
