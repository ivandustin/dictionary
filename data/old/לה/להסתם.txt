Etymology: The word "להסתם" (lehastem) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root ס.ת.מ (s.t.m), which means "to close" or "to seal." The prefix ל (le) indicates the infinitive form of the verb.

Grammar: "להסתם" is the reflexive form of the verb, indicating that the action is being performed on oneself. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני מתסתם (ani mitstam) - I close myself
- אתה מתסתם (atah mitstam) - You (masculine singular) close yourself
- את מתסתמת (at mitstamet) - You (feminine singular) close yourself
- הוא/היא מתסתם/מתסתמת (hu/hi mitstam/mitstamet) - He/She closes himself/herself
- אנחנו מתסתמים (anachnu mitstamim) - We close ourselves
- אתם מתסתמים (atem mitstamim) - You (masculine plural) close yourselves
- אתן מתסתמות (atan mitstamot) - You (feminine plural) close yourselves
- הם/הן מתסתמים/מתסתמות (hem/hen mitstamim/mitstamot) - They close themselves

Description: The verb "להסתם" means "to close oneself" or "to seal oneself." It is used when someone intentionally closes or seals themselves off from others or from the outside world. This can be done physically or metaphorically, indicating a desire for privacy, solitude, or protection.
