Etymology: The word "ינחילך" (pronounced "yanchilcha") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root נ.ח.ל (n.ch.l), which means "to inherit" or "to bequeath." 

Grammar: "ינחילך" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the future tense. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form. The prefix י (yod) indicates the future tense, and the suffix ך (kaf) indicates the second person singular object pronoun, meaning "you" in this case.

Description: The word "ינחילך" translates to "he will inherit you" or "he will bequeath you" in English. It implies the action of passing down or transferring something to you as an inheritance.
