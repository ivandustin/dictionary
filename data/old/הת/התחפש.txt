Etymology: The Hebrew word "התחפש" (hitchapes) comes from the root ח.פ.ש (ch.p.sh), which means "to disguise" or "to dress up." It is derived from the noun חפוש (chafush), meaning "disguise" or "costume."

Grammar: "התחפש" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the reflexive verb form, which indicates that the subject is performing the action on itself. In this case, it means "to dress up" or "to disguise oneself."

Description: "התחפש" is a common word used in Hebrew to describe the act of dressing up or disguising oneself, especially during holidays like Purim. It is often used when talking about children or adults who put on costumes for fun or as part of a celebration.
