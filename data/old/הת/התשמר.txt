Etymology: The word "התשמר" (hithshamer) comes from the Hebrew root "שמר" (shamar), which means "to guard" or "to keep". The prefix "ה" (ha) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, so "התשמר" (hithshamer) can be translated as "to guard oneself" or "to take care".

Grammar: "התשמר" (hithshamer) is a reflexive verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מתשמר (ani mitshamer) - I guard myself
- Plural: אנחנו מתשמרים (anachnu mitshamerim) - We guard ourselves

Description: The word "התשמר" (hithshamer) is used to describe the act of guarding oneself or taking care of oneself. It can refer to physical, emotional, or spiritual self-care. It implies being cautious, watchful, and protective of one's well-being.
