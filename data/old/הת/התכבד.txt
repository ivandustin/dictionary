Etymology: The word "התקבד" (hithkaved) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ק.ב.ד (k.b.d), which means "to honor" or "to glorify." The prefix ה (he) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning "to honor oneself" or "to take pride in oneself."

Grammar: "התקבד" is a reflexive verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מתקבד (ani mitkaved) - I honor myself
- Plural: אנחנו מתקבדים (anachnu mitkavedim) - We honor ourselves

Description: The word "התקבד" is used to describe the act of honoring oneself or taking pride in one's achievements or qualities. It implies a sense of self-respect and recognition of one's worth.
