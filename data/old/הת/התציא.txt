Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַתְּצִיא" (hat-tetzi) comes from the root יצא (yatzah), which means "to go out" or "to exit". The prefix הַ (ha) is the definite article "the".

Grammar: הַתְּצִיא is a verb in the imperative form. It is conjugated in the second person singular, addressing one person.

Description: הַתְּצִיא is a command that means "bring out" or "take out". It is used when instructing someone to remove or extract something from a certain place or situation.
