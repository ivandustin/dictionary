Etymology: The Hebrew word "התימני" (HaTeimani) comes from the Hebrew root ת.מ.נ (T.M.N), which means "Yemenite." It refers to the Jewish community that lived in Yemen for centuries.

Grammar: "התימני" is a masculine singular noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the prefix ה- (Ha-) to the word תימני (Teimani), which means "Yemenite."

Description: "התימני" is used to describe someone or something that is related to Yemen or the Yemenite Jewish community. It can refer to the language, culture, traditions, or people from Yemen.
