Etymology: The word "התורה" (HaTorah) comes from the Hebrew root י-ר-ה (Y-R-H), which means "to teach" or "to instruct." It is derived from the verb תּוֹרֶה (torah), which means "instruction" or "teaching."

Grammar: "התורה" (HaTorah) is a noun in Hebrew and is written with the definite article "ה" (Ha) at the beginning, which means "the." It is singular and feminine in gender.

Description: "התורה" (HaTorah) refers to the Torah, which is the central religious text of Judaism. It is composed of the Five Books of Moses: Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy. The Torah contains the foundational laws, commandments, and narratives of the Jewish faith. It is considered sacred and is studied and revered by Jewish communities worldwide.
