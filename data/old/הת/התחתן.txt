Etymology: The word "התחתן" (hitchaten) is derived from the Hebrew root ח.ת.נ (ch.t.n), which means "to marry" or "to wed."

Grammar: "התחתן" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular masculine: אני מִתְחַתֵּן (ani mit'chaten)
- Singular feminine: אני מִתְחַתֶּנֶת (ani mit'chatenet)
- Plural masculine: אנחנו מִתְחַתְּנִים (anachnu mit'chatenim)
- Plural feminine: אנחנו מִתְחַתְּנוֹת (anachnu mit'chatenot)

Description: "התחתן" means "to get married" or "to marry" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of entering into a marital union or the process of getting married.
