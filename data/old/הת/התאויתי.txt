Etymology: The word "התאויתי" is derived from the Hebrew root א.ו.ה (aleph-vav-hei), which means "to desire" or "to tempt." The prefix ה (hei) indicates the reflexive form, meaning "to be tempted" or "to desire oneself."

Grammar: "התאויתי" is a verb in the past tense, masculine singular form. It is conjugated in the reflexive form, indicating that the subject is both the one who desires and the one who is being desired.

Description: "התאויתי" can be translated as "I desired" or "I was tempted." It expresses the action of having a strong desire or being enticed by something.
