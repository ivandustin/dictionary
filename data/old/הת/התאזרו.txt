Etymology: The word "התאזרו" (hita'zeru) is a Hebrew verb in the imperative form. It is derived from the root ת-א-ז-ר (aleph-zayin-resh), which means "to equip" or "to arm oneself."

Grammar: "התאזרו" is the plural form of the imperative verb, used when addressing a group of people. It is commonly translated as "Equip yourselves!" or "Arm yourselves!"

Description: "התאזרו" is a command or instruction to a group of people to prepare themselves or get ready for something. It can be used in various contexts, such as military instructions, sports coaching, or general preparation for a task or event.
