Etymology: The word "לאבי" (pronounced "la'avi") is a Hebrew word that means "to my father." It is a possessive form of the word "אב" (pronounced "av"), which means "father."

Grammar: "לאבי" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition "ל" (pronounced "le"), which means "to," and the possessive form of the noun "אב" (pronounced "av"), which means "father." Together, they form the phrase "לאבי," which translates to "to my father."

Description: "לאבי" is used in Hebrew to indicate possession or ownership. It is commonly used to express the relationship between a person and their father, similar to the English phrase "to my father."
