Etymology: The word "לאחינו" (le'achinu) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "to our brothers" in English. It is composed of the preposition "ל" (le), meaning "to" or "for," and the noun "אחינו" (achinu), meaning "our brothers."

Grammar: "לאחינו" (le'achinu) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" (le) indicates direction or purpose, and it is followed by the possessive pronoun "אחינו" (achinu), meaning "our brothers." The phrase functions as an indirect object, indicating that something is done for or directed towards our brothers.

Description: "לאחינו" (le'achinu) is a phrase commonly used in Hebrew to express solidarity or unity with a group of people, specifically with one's fellow Jewish people. It is often used in prayers, speeches, or other contexts to emphasize the importance of unity and support among Jewish individuals.
