Etymology: The word "לאדינו" (ladino) comes from the Spanish word "ladino," which means "crafty" or "sly." It refers to the language spoken by Sephardic Jews, who were expelled from Spain in 1492.

Grammar: Ladino is a Romance language that developed from Old Spanish. It is heavily influenced by Hebrew, Arabic, Turkish, and other languages spoken in the regions where Sephardic Jews settled. Ladino uses the Hebrew alphabet for writing.

Description: Ladino is primarily spoken by Sephardic Jews, who trace their ancestry back to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a language that has evolved over centuries and has different dialects depending on the region. Ladino is considered an endangered language, as its usage has declined over time. However, efforts are being made to preserve and revitalize the language.
