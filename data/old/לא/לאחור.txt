Etymology: The word "לאחור" (pronounced "le'achor" in Hebrew) is derived from the root א.ח.ר (A.Kh.R), which means "behind" or "back". 

Grammar: "לאחור" is a preposition in Hebrew, which means "behind" or "in the back of". It is used to indicate the location or position of something that is situated at the rear or posterior side of another object or person.

Description: "לאחור" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to describe the physical location of something, such as "הכיסא נמצא לאחור השולחן" (The chair is behind the table). It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation or action that is considered to be in the past or no longer relevant, such as "היא השאירה את העבודה לאחור" (She left the job behind).
