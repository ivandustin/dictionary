Etymology: The word "לאחת" (pronounced "le'achat") is a Hebrew word that means "to one" or "together." It is derived from the Hebrew root א.ח.ת (A.Ch.T), which means "one" or "unity."

Grammar: "לאחת" is a preposition in Hebrew that is used to indicate unity or togetherness. It is often used to express the idea of doing something together or in unison with others.

Description: "לאחת" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to describe actions done collectively, such as "לאחת עם החברים" (together with friends) or "לאחת במשפחה" (together as a family). It can also be used to express agreement or consensus, as in "אנחנו חייבים לעבוד לאחת" (we need to work together). Overall, "לאחת" conveys the idea of unity and cooperation.
