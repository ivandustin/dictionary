Etymology: The word "לאנשי" (pronounced "le'anshi") is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "to the people" in English. It is composed of two parts: "ל" (pronounced "le") meaning "to" or "for" and "אנשי" (pronounced "anshi") meaning "people".

Grammar: "לאנשי" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" indicates direction or purpose, and when combined with the noun "אנשי", it forms a phrase that means "to the people" or "for the people".

Description: "לאנשי" is commonly used in Hebrew to express the idea of something being directed towards or intended for a specific group of people. It can be used in various contexts, such as in advertising, politics, or social issues, to emphasize that a certain product, message, or action is meant for the benefit or interest of the general public.
