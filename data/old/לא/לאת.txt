Etymology: The word "לאת" (pronounced "le'et") is a Hebrew word that means "to you" or "for you". It is a combination of the preposition "ל" (pronounced "le") meaning "to" or "for", and the pronoun "את" (pronounced "et") meaning "you".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "לאת" is used as a prepositional phrase to indicate that something is directed towards or intended for the person being addressed. It is commonly used in formal or poetic language.

Description: "לאת" is a versatile phrase that can be used in various contexts. It can express a sense of dedication, offering, or a personal connection. It is often used to convey a sense of respect or honor towards the person being addressed.
