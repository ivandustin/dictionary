Etymology: The word "אקף" (pronounced "akaf" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root קף (kuf-peh), which means "to encircle" or "to surround."

Grammar: "אקף" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אֲקוֹף (a'kof) - I encircle
- תַּקוֹף (ta'kof) - You (singular) encircle
- יַקוֹף (ya'kof) - He/She encircles
- נַקוֹף (na'kof) - We encircle
- תַּקוֹפוּ (ta'ko'fu) - You (plural) encircle
- יַקוֹפוּ (ya'ko'fu) - They encircle

Description: The word "אקף" is used to describe the action of encircling or surrounding something or someone. It can be used in a literal sense, such as encircling a physical object, or in a figurative sense, such as encircling a concept or idea.
