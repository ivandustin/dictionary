Etymology: The word "אקלנו" (aklenu) is a Hebrew word that means "our food" or "our sustenance." It is derived from the Hebrew root כ-ל-ה (k-l-h), which means "to eat" or "to consume."

Grammar: "אקלנו" (aklenu) is a first-person plural possessive pronoun in Hebrew. It is formed by combining the prefix א- (a-) meaning "our" with the noun קליה (kliya) meaning "food" or "sustenance."

Description: "אקלנו" (aklenu) is used to express possession or ownership of food or sustenance by a group of people. It is commonly used in Hebrew to refer to a shared meal or a communal source of nourishment.
