Etymology: The word "וּבְכָל" (pronounced "u'v'chol" in Hebrew) is a combination of two words: "וּ" (pronounced "u") meaning "and" and "בְכָל" (pronounced "b'chol") meaning "in all" or "with all". 

Grammar: "וּבְכָל" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "וּ" serves as a conjunction, connecting the phrase to the previous clause or sentence. "בְכָל" is a preposition meaning "in" or "with" and is followed by the noun it modifies.

Description: "וּבְכָל" is used in Hebrew to express the idea of "in all" or "with all". It is often used to emphasize the completeness or totality of something. For example, "וּבְכָל לְבָבְךָ" (u'v'chol levavecha) means "with all your heart" or "with all your being".
