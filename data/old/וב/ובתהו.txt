Etymology: The word "וַבְתַהוּ" (vav-tav-hei-vav) is a Hebrew word that translates to "and chaos" or "and void" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew Bible, specifically from the book of Genesis, where it is used to describe the state of the earth before God's creation.

Grammar: "וַבְתַהוּ" is a conjunction composed of the letter "ו" (vav) meaning "and," and the word "תֹּהוּ" (tohu) meaning "chaos" or "void." In Hebrew grammar, the vav is used to connect words or phrases, and in this case, it connects the previous clause to the following clause.

Description: "וַבְתַהוּ" is used in the Hebrew Bible to describe the initial state of the earth before God's creation. It signifies a formless and empty condition, suggesting a lack of order or structure. This word is significant in the biblical narrative as it sets the stage for God's act of creation, bringing order and purpose to the chaos.
