Etymology: The word "וּבְמִשְׁה" (u'veMoshé) is a Hebrew phrase composed of two parts. "וּ" (u) means "and" and "בְּמֹשֶׁה" (bemoshe) means "in Moses". 

Grammar: "וּבְמִשְׁה" (u'veMoshé) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "וּ" (u) means "and" and the preposition "בְּ" (be) means "in". The noun "מֹשֶׁה" (Moshé) means "Moses". Together, the phrase means "and in Moses".

Description: The phrase "וּבְמִשְׁה" (u'veMoshé) is used in Hebrew to indicate that something is connected to or associated with Moses. It can be used to introduce a story, a law, or a teaching that is attributed to Moses.
