Description: In the Hebrew calendar, "וּבָעְשׂוֹר" (uva'asor) refers to the tenth day of the month. It is often used in religious contexts to specify important dates, such as the tenth day of the Jewish month of Tishrei, which is the Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur).
