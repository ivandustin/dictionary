Etymology: The word "וּבְצָלוֹ" (u'vatzalo') is a Hebrew word that means "and in his shadow." It is derived from the root בצל (b-tz-l), which means "shadow."

Grammar: "וּבְצָלוֹ" is a prepositional phrase composed of the conjunction "וּ" (u) meaning "and," the preposition "בְ" (b') meaning "in," and the noun "צֵל" (tzel) meaning "shadow." The suffix "וֹ" (o) indicates possession, meaning "his."

Description: The phrase "וּבְצָלוֹ" is used in biblical Hebrew to describe being in someone's shadow or under someone's protection. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to being in someone's influence or authority.
