Grammar: "וַבְהַרוֹת" (vaharot) is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the feminine plural form. It is conjugated in the past tense and can be translated as "and they shone" or "and they were radiant."

Description: "וַבְהַרוֹת" (vaharot) is used in the Hebrew Bible, specifically in the book of Exodus (34:29), to describe the radiant face of Moses after he descended from Mount Sinai. It signifies the divine presence and the transformative experience Moses had while receiving the Ten Commandments.
