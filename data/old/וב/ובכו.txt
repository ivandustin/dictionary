Etymology: The word "וּבְכוֹ" (pronounced "u'v'cho") is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "וּ" (pronounced "u") meaning "and" and "בְכוֹ" (pronounced "v'cho") meaning "in you". 

Grammar: "וּבְכוֹ" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "וּ" functions as a conjunction meaning "and", while "בְכוֹ" is a combination of the preposition "בְ" meaning "in" and the pronoun "כוֹ" meaning "you". Together, the phrase means "and in you".

Description: "וּבְכוֹ" is a phrase commonly used in Hebrew texts, particularly in religious and poetic contexts. It is often used to express a sense of unity, connection, or inclusion.
