Etymology: The word "וּבָעֵת" (uva'et) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "וּ" (u) meaning "and" and "בָעֵת" (ba'et) meaning "at the time." 

Grammar: "וּבָעֵת" (uva'et) is a conjunction in Hebrew, used to connect two phrases or clauses. It is commonly translated as "and at the time" or "and when."

Description: "וּבָעֵת" (uva'et) is used to indicate a specific time or condition in which an action or event takes place. It is often used in biblical texts to introduce a narrative or describe a sequence of events.
