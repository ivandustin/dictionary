Etymology: The word "ובהם" (pronounced "uvahem" in Hebrew) is a combination of two words: "ו" (pronounced "vav") meaning "and" and "בהם" (pronounced "bahem") meaning "in them". 

Grammar: "ובהם" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "ובהם" functions as a conjunction, connecting two elements in a sentence. It is used to indicate that something is happening or existing in multiple places or objects.

Description: "ובהם" is a versatile phrase that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used in biblical and modern Hebrew texts to express the idea of something being present or happening in multiple locations or objects. It is often translated as "and in them" or "and among them" in English.
