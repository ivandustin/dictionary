Etymology: The word "וּבְרֵאשִׁית" (u'v'reishit) is a Hebrew word that translates to "and in the beginning" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root ב-ר-א (b-r-a), which means "to create" or "to shape."

Grammar: "וּבְרֵאשִׁית" is a prepositional phrase composed of the conjunction "וּ" (u) meaning "and," and the preposition "בְּ" (b') meaning "in" or "at." The noun "רֵאשִׁית" (reishit) means "beginning" or "start."

Description: "וּבְרֵאשִׁית" is the opening phrase of the Hebrew Bible, also known as the Old Testament. It appears in the Book of Genesis, specifically in Genesis 1:1, and introduces the creation narrative. The phrase signifies the beginning of time and the start of God's creation of the universe.
