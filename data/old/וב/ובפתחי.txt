Etymology: The word "וּבְפָתְחִי" is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "וּ" meaning "and" and "בְפָתַחִי" meaning "at my door". 

Grammar: "וּבְפָתְחִי" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "וּ" functions as a conjunction meaning "and", and "בְפָתַחִי" is a compound word consisting of the preposition "בְ" meaning "at" and the noun "פֶּתַח" meaning "door". The suffix "י" indicates possession, so "פָתְחִי" means "my door".

Description: The phrase "וּבְפָתְחִי" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament) in Proverbs 8:34. It is part of a verse that encourages wisdom and understanding, stating "Blessed is the man who listens to me, watching daily at my doors, waiting at the posts of my entrance." The phrase emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and being attentive to its teachings.
