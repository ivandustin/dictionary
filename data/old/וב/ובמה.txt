Etymology: The word "וּבְמָה" (uv'mah) is a Hebrew phrase composed of the conjunction "וּ" (u) meaning "and" and the noun "מָה" (mah) meaning "what." 

Grammar: "וּבְמָה" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "בְ" (b') means "in" or "with," and the noun "מָה" (mah) means "what." Together, they form the phrase "in what" or "with what."

Description: "וּבְמָה" is used in Hebrew to ask about the means or method by which something is done. It is often translated as "how" in English. For example, if someone asks "וּבְמָה אתה עושה את זה?" (uv'mah ata oseh et ze?), it means "How are you doing this?" or "By what means are you doing this?"
