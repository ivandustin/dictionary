Etymology: The word "וּבְתוֹ" (u'veto') is a Hebrew word that means "and his daughter."

Grammar: "וּבְתוֹ" is a noun phrase composed of the conjunction "וּ" (u') meaning "and" and the noun "בַּת" (bat) meaning "daughter." The suffix "וֹ" (to) indicates possession, meaning "his daughter."

Description: "וּבְתוֹ" is used in Hebrew to refer to someone's daughter. It is commonly used in biblical texts and can be found in various contexts and stories.
