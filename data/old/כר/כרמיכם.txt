Etymology: The word "כרמיך" (pronounced "karmikh" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root כ-ר-מ, which means "vineyard" or "vine." The suffix -יך (-ikh) indicates possession, so כרמיך literally means "your vineyard."

Grammar: כרמיך is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the masculine gender and is in the plural form. The suffix -יך (-ikh) indicates second-person possessive, meaning "your."

Description: כרמיך is used to refer to someone's vineyard or vineyards. It can be used in a literal sense to describe an actual vineyard or in a metaphorical sense to symbolize abundance, fertility, or prosperity.
