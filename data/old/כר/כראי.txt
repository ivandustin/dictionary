Etymology: The word "כראי" (pronounced "kar'ai") is a Hebrew word that means "read" or "to read." It is derived from the Hebrew root ק.ר.א (k.r.a), which means "to read" or "to call out."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "כראי" is a verb in the imperative form. It is used to give a command or instruction to someone to read or call out something.

Description: "כראי" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to instruct someone to read a specific text or passage, to call out a name or announcement, or to recite something aloud. It is commonly used in educational settings, religious ceremonies, and public speaking engagements.
