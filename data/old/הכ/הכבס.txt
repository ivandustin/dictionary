Etymology: The word "הכבס" (hakbess) is derived from the Hebrew root כ-ב-ס (k-b-s), which means "to wash" or "to cleanse."

Grammar: "הכבס" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מכבס (ani mekabes) - I wash
- Plural: אנחנו מכבסים (anachnu mekabesim) - We wash

Description: "הכבס" is the Hebrew word for "to wash" or "to launder." It is commonly used to refer to the act of cleaning clothes or other items by using water and detergent.
