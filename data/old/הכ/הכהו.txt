Etymology: The Hebrew word "הכהו" (pronounced "hakahu") is derived from the root כָּהָה (kaha), which means "to dim" or "to darken." 

Grammar: הכהו is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the past tense. It is conjugated in the third person plural masculine form. 

Description: הכהו is translated as "they dimmed" or "they darkened" in English. It is used to describe an action of making something less bright or reducing its light intensity.
