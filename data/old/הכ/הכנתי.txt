Etymology: The word "הכנתי" (hakanti) is a Hebrew verb in the past tense, derived from the root כ-נ-ה (k-n-h), which means "to prepare" or "to make ready."

Grammar: "הכנתי" is the first person singular form of the verb, used when the subject is "I" and the action is in the past tense. It is conjugated in the perfect tense, indicating a completed action.

Description: "הכנתי" translates to "I prepared" or "I made ready" in English. It is used to express that the speaker has completed the action of preparing or making something ready in the past.
