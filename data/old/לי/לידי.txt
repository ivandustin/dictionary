Etymology: The word "לידי" (lidi) is a Hebrew word that means "to me" or "for me". It is derived from the Hebrew root ל.ד.ה (l.d.h), which conveys the idea of possession or belonging.

Grammar: "לידי" (lidi) is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition "ל" (l) meaning "to" or "for", and the pronoun "ידי" (yadi) meaning "me" or "my". It is used to indicate possession or ownership, similar to the English phrase "to me" or "for me".

Description: The phrase "לידי" (lidi) is commonly used in Hebrew to express possession or ownership. It is often used to indicate that something belongs to or is intended for the speaker. For example, "הספר שלי לידי" (ha-sefer sheli lidi) means "my book" or "the book that belongs to me".
