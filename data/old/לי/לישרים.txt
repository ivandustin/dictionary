Etymology: The word "לישרים" (Lisharim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root word "שר" (shar), which means "to be straight" or "to be upright". It is related to the concept of honesty, integrity, and righteousness.

Grammar: "לישרים" (Lisharim) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ים" (-im) to the root word "לישר" (lishar). The plural form indicates that it refers to multiple individuals who possess the quality of being upright or righteous.

Description: "לישרים" (Lisharim) is a term used to describe people who are honest, upright, and morally righteous. It is often used to refer to individuals who have high ethical standards and conduct themselves with integrity in their actions and interactions with others.
