Etymology: The word "לישון" (lishon) comes from the Hebrew root "ל.ש.נ" (l.sh.n), which means "to sleep" or "to rest". It is derived from the noun "לֵישָׁן" (leishan), which means "tongue". The connection between "sleep" and "tongue" is that when a person is asleep, their tongue is at rest.

Grammar: "לישון" is a verb in the infinitive form. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to tense, gender, and number. The infinitive form is used to express the basic action or concept of the verb.

Description: "לישון" means "to sleep" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of resting or being in a state of sleep.
