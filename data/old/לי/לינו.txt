Etymology: The word "לינו" (pronounced "lino") is a Hebrew word that means "to sleep" or "to rest". It is derived from the Hebrew root ל.נ.ה (l.n.h), which conveys the idea of resting or sleeping.

- Singular:
  - 1st person: אֲנִי לוֹנֶה (ani loneh) - I sleep

  - 2nd person: אַתְּ לוֹנָה (at loneh) - You sleep (feminine)
  - 3rd person: הוּא לוֹנֶה (hu loneh) - He sleeps
  - 3rd person: הִיא לוֹנָה (hi loneh) - She sleeps
- Plural:
  - 1st person: אֲנַחְנוּ לוֹנִים (anachnu lonim) - We sleep
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם לוֹנִים (atem lonim) - You sleep (masculine/plural)
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּן לוֹנוֹת (aten lonot) - You sleep (feminine/plural)
  - 3rd person: הֵם לוֹנִים (hem lonim) - They sleep (masculine)
  - 3rd person: הֵן לוֹנוֹת (hen lonot) - They sleep (feminine)

Description: "לינו" is a versatile verb that is commonly used in everyday Hebrew conversations. It refers to the act of sleeping or resting. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of tranquility or calmness.
