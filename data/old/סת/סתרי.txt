Etymology: The word "סתרי" comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ס.ת.ר (s.t.r), which means "to hide" or "to conceal."

Grammar: "סתרי" is a Hebrew adjective that is used to describe something as hidden or concealed. It is in the masculine singular form. In Hebrew, adjectives agree in gender, number, and definiteness with the noun they modify.

Description: "סתרי" is used to describe something that is not easily seen or known, something that is hidden or kept secret. It can refer to physical objects that are concealed from view or abstract concepts that are not readily apparent.
