Etymology: The word "קלע" (pronounced "kla") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ק.ל.ע, which means "to shoot" or "to strike."

- אֲנִי קוֹלֵעַ (ani kolea) - I shoot/strike
- אַתָּה קוֹלֵעַ (ata kolea) - You (masculine singular) shoot/strike
- אַתְּ קוֹלֶעָה (at kolea) - You (feminine singular) shoot/strike
- הוּא קוֹלֵעַ (hu kolea) - He shoots/strikes
- הִיא קוֹלֶעָה (hi kolea) - She shoots/strikes
- אֲנַחְנוּ קוֹלְעִים (anachnu kole'im) - We shoot/strike
- אַתֶּם קוֹלְעִים (atem kole'im) - You (masculine plural) shoot/strike
- אַתֶּן קוֹלְעוֹת (aten kole'ot) - You (feminine plural) shoot/strike
- הֵם קוֹלְעִים (hem kole'im) - They (masculine) shoot/strike
- הֵן קוֹלְעוֹת (hen kole'ot) - They (feminine) shoot/strike

Description: The word "קלע" generally means "to shoot" or "to strike" in Hebrew. It can refer to shooting a weapon, such as a gun or a bow and arrow, or striking an object forcefully. The context in which it is used determines the specific meaning.
