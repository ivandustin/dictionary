Etymology: The word "סמוך" comes from the Hebrew root ס.מ.כ (samekh.mem.kaf), which means "to be close" or "to be near."

Grammar: סמוך is an adjective in Hebrew, and it is used to describe something or someone that is close or near to another thing or person. It can also be used as a preposition to indicate proximity or closeness.

Description: סמוך is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday Hebrew. It can be used to describe physical proximity, such as "הבנק סמוך לבית" (The bank is close to the house), or it can be used to describe a close relationship or connection between people, such as "האחים סמוכים זה לזה" (The brothers are close to each other).
