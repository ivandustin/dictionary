Etymology: The word "הארני" (pronounced ha'arni) is a Hebrew word that means "the lion." It is derived from the Hebrew root א-ר-י (aleph-resh-yod), which is associated with the concept of strength and power.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "הארני" is a definite noun, meaning that it refers to a specific lion. The word is masculine and singular. It is formed by adding the definite article "ה" (ha) at the beginning of the noun, followed by the root word "ארני" (arni).

Description: The lion is a large and powerful carnivorous mammal that belongs to the cat family. It is known for its majestic appearance, with a muscular body, a large head, and a mane around its neck in the case of male lions. Lions are often associated with strength, courage, and leadership.
