Etymology: The Hebrew word "האגמים" (Ha'agamim) is derived from the root א.ג.מ (A.G.M), which means "lake" or "pond" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "האגמים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the singular form האגם (Ha'agam), meaning "the lake" or "the pond."

Description: "האגמים" refers to multiple lakes or ponds. It is commonly used to describe natural bodies of water that are larger than a typical pond but smaller than a sea or an ocean.
