Etymology: The Hebrew word "האי" (pronounced "ha-ai") is derived from the root א-י-ה (aleph-yod-hei), which means "to be" or "to exist." It is often used as a definite article, similar to the English word "the."

Grammar: "האי" is a masculine singular definite article in Hebrew. It is used before nouns to indicate that the noun is specific or known to both the speaker and the listener.

Description: In Hebrew, "האי" is used to specify a particular noun or to refer to something that has already been mentioned or is known in the context of the conversation. It is an essential part of Hebrew grammar and is used extensively in everyday speech and writing.
