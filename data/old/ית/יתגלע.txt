Etymology: The word "יתגלע" (Yitgalea) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root גלע (g-l-'a), which means "to reveal" or "to disclose". The prefix ית (yit) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, meaning that the action is performed on oneself.

Grammar: "יתגלע" is a reflexive verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני נתגלע (ani nitgalea) - I reveal myself
- Plural: אנחנו נתגלעים (anachnu nitgaleim) - We reveal ourselves

Description: The word "יתגלע" is used to express the act of revealing or disclosing oneself. It can be used in various contexts, such as revealing one's true feelings, thoughts, or identity.
