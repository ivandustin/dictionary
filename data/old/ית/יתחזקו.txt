Etymology: The word "יתחזקו" (pronounced "yit'hazeku") is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root חזק (pronounced "hazak"), which means "to strengthen" or "to fortify."

Grammar: "יתחזקו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb חזק. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and the tense of the sentence. The prefix ית- (pronounced "yit-") indicates the future tense, and the suffix -ו (pronounced "-u") indicates the third person plural.

Description: "יתחזקו" is used to express an action that will happen in the future and is performed by a group of people or objects. It can be translated as "they will strengthen" or "they will fortify" in English.
