Etymology: The word "מחיל" (makhil) comes from the Hebrew root ח.י.ל (ch.y.l), which means "to forgive" or "to pardon."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מחיל" (makhil) is a verb in the present tense, masculine singular form. It can also be conjugated to match different genders, numbers, and tenses.

Description: The word "מחיל" (makhil) means "to forgive" or "to pardon" in Hebrew. It is often used in the context of granting forgiveness or showing mercy to someone who has wronged you. It implies letting go of resentment or anger and choosing not to hold a grudge.
