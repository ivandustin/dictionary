Etymology: The word "מחקק" (makhkek) is derived from the Hebrew root חקק (kh.q.k), which means "to engrave" or "to carve." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מחקק" is a noun that is used to describe someone who carves or engraves. It is in the masculine form, and its plural form is "מחקקים" (makhkekim).

Description: "מחקק" refers to a person who specializes in carving or engraving various materials, such as wood, stone, or metal. They use tools like chisels, knives, or drills to create intricate designs or patterns on the surface of the material. Carving and engraving can be used for artistic purposes, as well as for practical applications like creating sculptures, inscriptions, or decorative elements.
