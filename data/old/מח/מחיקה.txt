Etymology: The word "מחיקה" (makhikha) comes from the Hebrew root ח.ק.ה (ch.q.h), which means "to erase" or "to wipe away."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מחיקה" is a feminine noun. It is used to describe the act of erasing or wiping away something.

Description: "מחיקה" refers to the action of removing or eliminating something by rubbing or wiping it away. It can be used in various contexts, such as erasing pencil marks from paper, wiping away a stain, or deleting information from a computer.
