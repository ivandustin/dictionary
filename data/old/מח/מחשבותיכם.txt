Etymology: The word "מחשבותיך" (pronounced "machshavotayich" in Hebrew) is a combination of two Hebrew words: "מחשבות" (machshavot) meaning "thoughts" and "יך" (ayich) meaning "your". 

Grammar: "מחשבותיך" is a noun in the plural form. It is composed of the root word "מחשבות" (machshavot) and the possessive suffix "יך" (ayich) which indicates possession or ownership. The plural form suggests that it refers to multiple thoughts belonging to the second person feminine singular.

Description: "מחשבותיך" translates to "your thoughts" in English. It is used to refer to the thoughts or ideas that belong to someone.
