Etymology: The word "מחמת" (makhmat) is derived from the Hebrew root ח.מ.ת (ch.m.t), which means "cause" or "reason". 

Grammar: "מחמת" is a preposition in Hebrew. It is used to indicate the cause or reason for something. It is often translated as "because of" or "due to" in English.

Description: "מחמת" is a versatile word that is commonly used in Hebrew to explain the cause or reason behind an action, event, or situation. It helps to provide context and explain the motivation or circumstances that led to a particular outcome.
