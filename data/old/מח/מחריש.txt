Etymology: The word "מחריש" (pronounced "makh-rish" in English) comes from the Hebrew root ח-ר-שׁ (ch-r-sh), which means "to be silent" or "to quiet down."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מחריש" is a verb in the present tense, masculine singular form. It can also be conjugated to match different genders, numbers, and tenses.

Description: The word "מחריש" means "to silence" or "to quiet down" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of making someone or something silent or reducing noise. It can be used in various contexts, such as silencing a person, calming a situation, or reducing the volume of a sound.
