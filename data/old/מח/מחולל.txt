Etymology: The word "מחולל" (makholel) comes from the Hebrew root ח.ו.ל (ch.w.l), which means "to mix" or "to stir". The prefix מ (m) indicates the causative form, so מחולל (makholel) can be translated as "one who causes to mix" or "one who stirs".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מחולל" (makholel) is a noun that belongs to the masculine gender. It is in the singular form. The plural form is מחוללים (makholelim).

Description: The word "מחולל" (makholel) is used to describe someone who mixes or stirs ingredients together, typically in the context of cooking or preparing food. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who stirs up trouble or causes discord.
