Etymology: The word "מחקקים" (makhkakim) is derived from the Hebrew root חקק (kh-q-k), which means "to engrave" or "to carve". 

Grammar: "מחקקים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is in the present tense and masculine gender. 

Description: "מחקקים" refers to people who engage in engraving or carving activities. It can be used to describe artisans, craftsmen, or artists who specialize in creating intricate designs or patterns by carving or engraving on various materials such as wood, stone, metal, or glass.
