Etymology: The word "הקרואים" (HaKeru'im) is a Hebrew word that means "the readers" or "those who are called." It is derived from the Hebrew root ק.ר.א (k.r.a), which means "to read" or "to call."

Grammar: "הקרואים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (-im) to the root word קרא (kara), which means "to read" or "to call." The definite article ה (ha) is added at the beginning of the word to indicate "the."

Description: "הקרואים" refers to a group of people who are readers or those who are called to read. In Hebrew, it can be used to describe individuals who are appointed to read from the Torah during religious services or those who are engaged in reading activities in general.
