Etymology: The word "הקשיב" (pronounced "hakshiv" in Hebrew) comes from the root ק.ש.ב (k.sh.b), which means "to listen" or "to pay attention" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "הקשיב" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the past tense as הִקְשִׁיב (hikshiv), in the present tense as מַקְשִׁיב (makshiv), and in the future tense as יַקְשִׁיב (yakshiv). It can be used in different grammatical forms to match the subject and tense of the sentence.

Description: "הקשיב" means "to listen" or "to pay attention" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of actively listening or giving attention to someone or something. It implies focusing one's ears and mind on what is being said or heard. It can also be used metaphorically to describe paying attention to instructions, advice, or warnings.
