Grammar: "הקדשות" is a plural noun in Hebrew, derived from the singular form "הקדשה" (HaKedusha). It is used to refer to acts or objects that are considered holy or sacred.

Description: "הקדשות" is a term used in Jewish religious context to refer to various acts or objects that are set apart or consecrated for religious purposes. It can include rituals, prayers, blessings, sacred texts, and holy places. The concept of "הקדשות" is central to Jewish religious practice and is often associated with the idea of sanctification and separation from the mundane.
