Etymology: The word "הקנאה" (HaKina'ah) comes from the Hebrew root קנא (kana), which means "to be jealous" or "to be zealous". 

Grammar: "הקנאה" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is written with the definite article "ה" (ha) at the beginning, which means "the". The word is feminine and singular.

Description: "הקנאה" refers to the emotion of jealousy or envy. It is the feeling of discontent or resentment aroused by someone else's possessions, qualities, or achievements. Jealousy can often lead to negative thoughts and actions.
