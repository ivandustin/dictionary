Etymology: The word "הקהתי" (HaKhati) is derived from the Hebrew root ק.ה.ת (k.h.t), which means "to gather" or "to assemble". The prefix ה- (ha) indicates "the" and the suffix -תי (-ti) indicates "my" or "belonging to me". Therefore, "הקהתי" can be translated as "the one who gathers" or "belonging to me, the gatherer".

Grammar: "הקהתי" is a Hebrew adjective in the masculine singular form. It follows the pattern of "ה- + root + י" to indicate possession or belonging. In this case, it is used to describe a person or thing that gathers or assembles.
