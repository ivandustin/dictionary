Description: In Jewish tradition, a "הקרבן" (HaKorban) refers to a sacrificial offering that was brought to the Temple in Jerusalem as an act of worship and atonement. These offerings could be animals, such as sheep, goats, or cattle, or other items like grain or wine. The purpose of the offering was to draw closer to God and seek forgiveness for sins or express gratitude.

The concept of "הקרבן" (HaKorban) is deeply rooted in the ancient Israelite religious practices described in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh).
