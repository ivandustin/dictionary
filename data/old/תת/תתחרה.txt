Etymology: The word "תתחרה" (pronounced "titḥareh") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root חרה (ḥ-r-h), which means "to burn" or "to be angry." The prefix תת (tit) indicates the reflexive form of the verb, giving it the meaning of "to burn oneself" or "to be angry with oneself."

Grammar: "תתחרה" is a reflexive verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תתחרה (ani titḥareh) - I burn myself / I am angry with myself
- Plural: אנחנו נתחרים (anachnu nitḥarim) - We burn ourselves / We are angry with ourselves

Description: The word "תתחרה" is used to express the act of being angry with oneself or feeling intense internal anger. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a strong desire or passion for something.
