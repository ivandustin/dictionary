Etymology: The word "תתהפך" (pronounced "tit'hapech" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root ה.פ.ך (H.P.Kh), which means "to turn over" or "to flip." The prefix תת (tit) indicates a reflexive action, so תתהפך literally means "to turn oneself over" or "to flip oneself."

Grammar: תתהפך is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תתהפך (ani tit'hapech) - I flip myself
- Plural: אנחנו תתהפכים (anachnu tit'hapechim) - We flip ourselves

Description: תתהפך is a reflexive verb that describes the action of turning oneself over or flipping oneself. It can be used both literally, to describe a physical action, and figuratively, to describe a change in one's attitude or perspective.
