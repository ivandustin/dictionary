Grammar: In Hebrew, "רְדִי" is a noun that functions as a possessive form of the word "רֹעִי" (ro'i), which means "shepherd" or "guide." It is used to indicate ownership or possession, specifically in the first person singular form.

Description: "רְדִי" is a term of endearment used to refer to God as a shepherd or guide. It is often used in religious and poetic contexts to express a personal and intimate relationship with God, emphasizing His care, protection, and guidance.
