Etymology: The word "תחלץ" (tehalech) comes from the Hebrew root ח-ל-ץ (ch-l-ts), which means "to rescue" or "to deliver." It is derived from the noun חֵלֶץ (cheletz), which means "rescue" or "deliverance."

Grammar: "תחלץ" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מתחלץ (ani mit'halech) - I rescue
- Plural: אנחנו מתחלצים (anachnu mit'haletzim) - We rescue

Description: "תחלץ" is an action word that describes the act of rescuing or delivering someone or something from a difficult or dangerous situation. It can be used in various contexts, such as rescuing a person from danger, delivering a message, or saving someone from a problem.
