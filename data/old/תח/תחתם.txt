Etymology: The word "תחת" comes from the Hebrew language and is derived from the root ת-ח-ת (T-Ch-T), which means "under" or "beneath".

Grammar: "תחת" is a preposition in Hebrew. It is used to indicate a position or location that is below or underneath something else. It can also be used to express subordination or being under someone's authority.

Description: "תחת" is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday Hebrew conversations. It is used to describe physical locations, such as being under a table or beneath a tree. It can also be used metaphorically to describe being in a subordinate position or under someone's control.
