Etymology: The word "תחסר" (tachsar) comes from the Hebrew root חסר (ch.s.r), which means "to lack" or "to be deficient". 

Grammar: "תחסר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני תחסר (ani tachsar) - I lack, אתה תחסר (atah tachsar) - you lack (masculine singular), את תחסר (at tachser) - you lack (feminine singular), הוא תחסר (hu tachsar) - he lacks, היא תחסר (hi tachser) - she lacks, אנחנו נחסר (anachnu nechser) - we lack, אתם תחסרו (atem tachseru) - you lack (masculine plural), אתן תחסרנה (atan tachsernah) - you lack (feminine plural), הם תחסרו (hem tachseru) - they lack (masculine plural), הן תחסרנה (hen tachsernah) - they lack (feminine plural).

Description: The word "תחסר" is used to express the state of lacking or being deficient in something. It can refer to a physical or abstract absence or insufficiency.
