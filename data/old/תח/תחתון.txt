Etymology: The word "תחתון" comes from the Hebrew root ח.ת.נ (ch.t.n), which means "below" or "underneath". It is derived from the noun תחת (tachat), meaning "under" or "beneath".

Grammar: "תחתון" is an adjective in Hebrew. It is the masculine singular form. The feminine singular form is "תחתונה" (tachatona), and the plural form is "תחתונים" (tachatanim) for masculine and "תחתונות" (tachatot) for feminine.

Description: "תחתון" means "lower" or "bottom" in Hebrew. It is used to describe something that is situated or located below or underneath something else. It can refer to the lower part of an object, the lower level of a building, or the lower position in a hierarchy.
