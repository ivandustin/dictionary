Etymology: The word "תחריש" (tacharish) comes from the Hebrew root ח-ר-שׁ (ch-r-sh), which means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet". It is derived from the noun חֶרֶשׁ (cheresh), which means "deafness" or "silence".

Grammar: "תחריש" (tacharish) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תחריש (ani tacharish) - I am silent
- Masculine singular: אתה תחריש (atah tacharish) - You (masculine) are silent
- Feminine singular: את תחריש (at tacharish) - You (feminine) are silent
- Masculine plural: אתם תחרישו (atem tacharishu) - You (masculine plural) are silent
- Feminine plural: אתן תחרישנה (atan tacharishnah) - You (feminine plural) are silent

Description: "תחריש" (tacharish) means "to be silent" or "to keep quiet" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the act of not speaking or refraining from making noise. It can also be used metaphorically to convey the idea of keeping one's thoughts or opinions to oneself.
