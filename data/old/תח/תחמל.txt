Etymology: The word "תחמל" (Tachmol) is derived from the Hebrew root חמל (ch-m-l), which means "to have compassion" or "to show mercy."

Grammar: "תחמל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תחמל (Ani tachmol) - I have compassion
- Plural: אנחנו תחמל (Anachnu tachmol) - We have compassion

Description: "תחמל" is a word that describes the act of showing compassion or mercy towards someone. It implies a feeling of empathy and a desire to alleviate someone's suffering or distress.
