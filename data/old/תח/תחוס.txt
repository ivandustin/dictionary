Etymology: The word "תחוס" (pronounced "tachos" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root ח-ו-ס (ch-w-s), which means "to search" or "to seek." 

Grammar: "תחוס" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני תחוס (ani tachos) - I search, אתה תחוס (atah tachos) - you search (masculine), את תחוסי (at tachosi) - you search (feminine), הוא/היא תחוס (hu/hi tachos) - he/she searches, אנחנו תחוסים (anachnu tachosim) - we search, אתם תחוסים (atem tachosim) - you search (plural masculine), אתן תחוסות (aten tachosot) - you search (plural feminine), הם/הן תחוסים (hem/hen tachosim) - they search.

Description: The word "תחוס" means "to search" or "to seek" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of looking for something or trying to find information. It can be used in various contexts, such as searching for a lost item, searching for answers, or searching for a solution to a problem.
