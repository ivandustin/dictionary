Etymology: The word "תחשבו" (pronounced "tachshavu") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ח-ש-ב (ch-sh-b), which means "to think" or "to consider."

Grammar: "תחשבו" is the second-person plural form of the verb in the present tense. It is used when addressing a group of people and means "you (all) think" or "you (all) consider."

Description: "תחשבו" is a command form of the verb, instructing a group of people to think or consider something. It implies an action of reflecting, pondering, or giving careful thought to a particular matter.
