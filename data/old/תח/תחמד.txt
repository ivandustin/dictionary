Etymology: The word "תחמד" (Tachmod) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ח-מ-ד (ch-m-d), which means "to desire" or "to covet". 

Grammar: "תחמד" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the future tense. It is conjugated in the second person singular form. 

Description: The word "תחמד" is often translated as "you shall not covet" in English. It is one of the Ten Commandments given in the Hebrew Bible (Exodus 20:17) and is a prohibition against desiring or coveting someone else's possessions or relationships.
