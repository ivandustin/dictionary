Etymology: The word "לזבוח" comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root word "ז.ב.ח" which means "to slaughter" or "to sacrifice".

Grammar: "לזבוח" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי זוֹבֵחַ (ani zoveach) - I am slaughtering
- Plural: אֲנַחְנוּ זוֹבְחִים (anachnu zovchim) - We are slaughtering

Description: "לזבוח" means "to slaughter" or "to sacrifice" in Hebrew. It is often used in the context of religious rituals or the preparation of meat for consumption.
