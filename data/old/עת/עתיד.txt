Etymology: The word "עֵתִיד" (etid) comes from the Hebrew root ע.ת.ד (Ayin-Tet-Dalet), which means "to be destined" or "to be future." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, עֵתִיד is a noun that means "future" or "destiny." It is used to refer to events or circumstances that are yet to come.

Description: עֵתִיד is a concept that relates to the future and what is yet to happen. It can refer to both specific events or general expectations. In Hebrew, it is often used in phrases or expressions to discuss plans, predictions, or aspirations for the future.
