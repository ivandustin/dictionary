Etymology: The word "עֵטֶתִי" (eteti) is a Hebrew word derived from the root ע.ט.ת (ayin-tet-tet), which means "to write" or "to inscribe."

Grammar: "עֵטֶתִי" is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It is conjugated from the root ע.ט.ת (ayin-tet-tet) with the addition of the first person singular suffix י (i).

Description: The word "עֵטֶתִי" means "I wrote" or "I inscribed" in Hebrew. It is used to indicate an action of writing or inscribing that was performed by the speaker in the past.
