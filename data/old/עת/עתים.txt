Etymology: The word "עֵתִים" (etim) comes from the Hebrew root ע.ת.מ (ayin.tet.mem), which means "time" or "season". 

Grammar: "עֵתִים" is a noun in Hebrew and is usually used in the plural form. It can also be used in the singular form as "עֵת" (et), meaning "time" or "occasion". 

Description: "עֵתִים" refers to specific periods or moments in time. It can be used to describe a particular season, a specific time of day, or a specific event or occasion. It is often used in the context of biblical or poetic language.
