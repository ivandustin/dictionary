Etymology: The word "עֵתִיק" (etik) comes from the Hebrew root ע.ת.ק (ayin.tet.kuf), which means "ancient" or "old." It is derived from the noun עֵת (et), meaning "time" or "era."

Grammar: עֵתִיק is a masculine singular adjective in Hebrew. It is used to describe something or someone as ancient or old.

Description: עֵתִיק is used to refer to something that is very old or ancient. It can be used to describe historical artifacts, buildings, traditions, or even people. It carries the connotation of something that has been around for a long time and has a sense of historical significance.
