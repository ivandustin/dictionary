Etymology: The word "שחטו" comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ש-ח-ט, which means "to slaughter" or "to butcher."

Grammar: "שחטו" is a verb in the imperative form. It is the second person plural masculine command form of the verb "שחט" (to slaughter).

Description: "שחטו" is used as a command to instruct a group of males to slaughter or butcher something. It is commonly used in the context of religious rituals, specifically in the practice of kosher slaughter in Judaism.
