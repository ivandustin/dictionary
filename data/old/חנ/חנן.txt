Etymology: The word "חַנָּן" (channan) comes from the Hebrew root חָנַן (chanan), which means "to be gracious" or "to show favor." 

Grammar: "חַנָּן" (channan) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in various forms to indicate different tenses, persons, and numbers. 

Description: "חַנָּן" (channan) is often translated as "to be gracious," "to show favor," or "to be merciful." It is used to describe an act of kindness, compassion, or favor shown towards someone. In the Hebrew Bible, this word is frequently used to describe God's graciousness and mercy towards humanity.
