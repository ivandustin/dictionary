Etymology: The word "חֲנִים" (pronounced "chanim") comes from the Hebrew root חָנַן (chanan), which means "to be gracious" or "to show favor." 

Grammar: "חֲנִים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to "grace" or "favor" in a general sense. 

Description: "חֲנִים" is a term that is often used in religious contexts to describe the grace or favor that God bestows upon individuals or the Jewish people as a whole. It can also be used more broadly to refer to acts of kindness or favor shown by one person to another.
