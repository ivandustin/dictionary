Etymology: The word "קבור" (pronounced "kavor") comes from the Hebrew root ק.ב.ר (k.b.r), which means "to bury" or "to inter." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, קבור is a verb in the masculine singular form. It can be conjugated in different tenses and forms to match the subject and the tense of the sentence.

Description: קבור means "to bury" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of placing a deceased person in a grave or tomb. It is a common word used in the context of funeral rituals and burial ceremonies.
