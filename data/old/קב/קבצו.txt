Etymology: The word "קבצו" (pronounced "kavtsu") is a Hebrew verb that means "they gathered" or "they collected." It is derived from the root ק.ב.צ (k.b.ts), which conveys the idea of bringing things together or assembling.

Grammar: "קבצו" is the third person plural past tense form of the verb קָבַץ (kavats), which belongs to the binyan (verb structure) called "קָטַל" (katal). This verb form indicates that a group of people or objects gathered or collected something in the past.

Description: The word "קבצו" is used to describe an action of gathering or collecting. It can refer to people gathering in one place, collecting objects or resources, or bringing together scattered items. The context in which it is used determines the specific meaning.
