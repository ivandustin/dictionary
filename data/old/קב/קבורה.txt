Etymology: The word "קבורה" (pronounced "kevurah") comes from the Hebrew root ק.ב.ר (k.b.r), which means "to bury" or "to inter." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, קבורה is a feminine noun. It is used to refer to the act of burying someone or the place where someone is buried. 

Description: קבורה is an important concept in Jewish culture and religion. It is the act of burying a deceased person in accordance with Jewish customs and traditions. The burial process includes preparing the body, placing it in a casket, and interring it in a designated burial site. Jewish law places great importance on treating the deceased with respect and dignity, and burial is considered a sacred duty. The burial site, known as a קבר (kever), is often marked with a tombstone or monument to honor the memory of the deceased.
