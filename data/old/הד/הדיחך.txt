Etymology: The word "הַדִּיחַ" (Ha-dichah) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ד.ח.ה (D.Ch.H), which means "to laugh" or "to mock".

Grammar: "הַדִּיחַ" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the past tense as הִדְּחַתִּי (hid-dach-ti), meaning "I mocked" or "I laughed at".

Description: "הַדִּיחַ" is used to describe the act of mocking or laughing at someone or something. It implies a sense of ridicule or scorn towards the subject.
