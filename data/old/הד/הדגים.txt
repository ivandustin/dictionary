Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַדְגִים" (ha-dagim) comes from the root ד.ג.ם (d.g.m), which means "to fish" in Hebrew. 

Grammar: "הַדְגִים" is a masculine plural noun in Hebrew. It is the plural form of the singular noun "הַדָּג" (ha-dag), which means "the fish" in Hebrew. 

Description: "הַדְגִים" refers to multiple fish in Hebrew. It is commonly used to describe a group of fish or to refer to fish in general.
