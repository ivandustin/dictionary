Etymology: The word "בעתהו" (pronounced "be'etahu") is a Hebrew phrase that consists of two parts: "בעת" (pronounced "be'et") meaning "at the time of" or "during," and "הו" (pronounced "hu") which is a pronoun meaning "him" or "it." Together, "בעתהו" can be translated as "at the time of him" or "during it."

Grammar: "בעתהו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (pronounced "be") means "in" or "at," and it is followed by the noun "עתהו" (pronounced "etahu") which means "his time" or "its time." The pronoun "הו" (pronounced "hu") is a possessive pronoun indicating possession or association.

Description: The phrase "בעתהו" is used to refer to a specific time or moment in relation to someone or something. It implies that something happened or will happen during that particular time.
