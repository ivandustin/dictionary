Etymology: The word "בעיו" (pronounced "ba'ayu") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ע.ו.י (Ayin-Vav-Yod), which means "to desire" or "to want."

Grammar: "בעיו" is a noun in the singular form. It is commonly used in the phrase "בעיו של מישהו" (ba'ayu shel mishehu), which means "someone's desire" or "someone's wish."

Description: "בעיו" refers to a person's desire or wish. It can be used to express what someone wants or longs for. It is often used in a figurative sense to describe a strong desire or craving for something.
