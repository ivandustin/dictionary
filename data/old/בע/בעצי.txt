Etymology: The word "בעצי" (pronounced "ba'atsi") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root עצה (pronounced "etza"), which means "advice" or "counsel". The prefix ב (pronounced "ba") means "in" or "with". Therefore, "בעצי" can be translated as "with my advice" or "in my counsel".

Grammar: "בעצי" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The prefix ב (pronounced "ba") means "in" or "with", and עצי (pronounced "atsi") is the possessive form of the noun עצה (pronounced "etza"), which means "advice" or "counsel". The possessive form indicates that the advice or counsel belongs to the speaker.

Description: "בעצי" is a phrase that is often used to express the speaker's opinion or advice on a certain matter.
