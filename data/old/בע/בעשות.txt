Etymology: The word "בְּעָשׂוֹת" (be'asot) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ע.ש.ה (ayin, shin, hey), which means "to do" or "to make."

Grammar: "בְּעָשׂוֹת" is the infinitive construct form of the verb, which means it is the basic form of the verb that expresses the action of "doing" or "making." It is often used in Hebrew sentences to indicate an action that is being performed.

Description: "בְּעָשׂוֹת" is a versatile verb in Hebrew that can be used in various contexts and conjugated to match different subjects and tenses. It is commonly used to describe actions, activities, or tasks that someone is doing or making.
