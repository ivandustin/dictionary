Etymology: The word "בעצת" (betzat) is derived from the Hebrew root עצה (etza), which means "advice" or "counsel". The prefix ב (bet) indicates "in" or "with". Therefore, "בעצת" can be translated as "with advice" or "in counsel".

Grammar: "בעצת" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The prefix ב (bet) is a preposition that means "in" or "with". The noun עצת (etza) is in the construct state, indicating a possessive relationship. It is often followed by a noun or pronoun to indicate the possessor or the object of the advice.

Description: "בעצת" is used in Hebrew to express the idea of seeking advice or counsel. It implies that a decision or action is made with the guidance or input of others. It can be used in various contexts, such as seeking advice from a friend, consulting with experts, or considering different opinions before making a decision.
