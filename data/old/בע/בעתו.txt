Etymology: The word "בעתו" (be'etav) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ע.ת.ו (ayin.tav.vav), which means "to be at the right time" or "to be appropriate." 

Grammar: "בעתו" is a prepositional phrase that consists of the preposition "ב" (be), meaning "in" or "at," and the noun "עתו" (etav), meaning "his time" or "the right time." It is used to indicate that something happens or is done at the appropriate or expected time.

Description: "בעתו" is a Hebrew phrase that emphasizes the importance of timing and doing things at the right moment. It suggests that there is a specific time or season for everything and that it is crucial to act or make decisions at the appropriate time. This phrase is often used to convey the idea of patience and waiting for the right moment to take action.
