Etymology: The word "בעמקים" (ba'amakim) is a Hebrew word that translates to "in the valleys" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root ע.מ.ק (A.M.Q), which means "valley" or "lowland."

Grammar: "בעמקים" (ba'amakim) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root word עמק (amak), which means "valley." The prefix ב (ba) means "in" or "at."

Description: "בעמקים" (ba'amakim) is used to describe something that is located or happening in the valleys. It can refer to physical locations in lowland areas or metaphorically represent a state of being or a situation that is low or downcast.
