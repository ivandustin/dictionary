Etymology: The word "חיש" (chayesh) comes from the Hebrew root ח.י.ש (ch.y.sh), which means "to live" or "to be alive." It is derived from the noun חַי (chai), meaning "life."

Grammar: "חיש" (chayesh) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אֲנִי חוֹיֵשׁ (ani choyesh) - I am living
- אַתָּה חוֹיֵשׁ (ata choyesh) - You are living (masculine singular)
- אַתְּ חוֹיֶשֶׁת (at choyeshet) - You are living (feminine singular)
- הוּא חוֹיֵשׁ (hu choyesh) - He is living
- הִיא חוֹיֶשֶׁת (hi choyeshet) - She is living
- אֲנַחְנוּ חוֹיְשִׁים (anachnu choyeshim) - We are living
- אַתֶּם חוֹיְשִׁים (atem choyeshim) - You are living (masculine plural)
- אַתֶּן חוֹיְשֹׁת (aten choyeshot) - You are living (feminine plural)
- הֵם חוֹיְשִׁים (hem choyeshim) - They are living (masculine)
- הֵן חוֹיְשֹׁת (hen choyeshot) - They are living (feminine)

Description: The word "חיש" (chayesh) is used to describe the act of living or being alive. It can refer to both physical and spiritual existence. In a broader sense, it can also convey the idea of vitality, energy, or liveliness.
