Etymology: The word "חיי" (pronounced "chai") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the Hebrew root ח-י-י (chet-yod-yod), which means "to live" or "to be alive."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "חיי" is a noun that means "life." It is in the plural form, indicating multiple lives. The singular form of the word is "חי" (chai).

Description: "חיי" is a significant word in Hebrew and Jewish culture. It is often used as a symbol of vitality, energy, and the importance of living life to the fullest. It is commonly associated with the phrase "לחיים" (l'chaim), which means "to life" and is used as a toast or a celebratory expression.
