Etymology: The word "הביאי" (pronounced "habiyai") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ב.א.ה (B.A.H), which means "to bring" or "to bring forth."

Grammar: "הביאי" is a verb in the imperative form, second person singular, in the Hebrew language. It is used to command or instruct someone to bring or bring forth something.

Description: "הביאי" is a command form of the verb "to bring" in Hebrew. It is used when you want to tell someone to bring something to you or to a specific location. For example, if you want to ask someone to bring you a book, you can say "הביאי לי ספר" (bring me a book).
