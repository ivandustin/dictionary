Etymology: The word "והכפות" (v'hakafot) is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "וה" (v'h) meaning "and the" and "כפות" (kafot) meaning "palms" or "hands".

Description: "והכפות" is a phrase used in Jewish religious context, specifically during the holiday of Sukkot. It refers to the ritual of circling the synagogue or the Torah scrolls while holding the Four Species (etrog, lulav, hadassim, and aravot) in the palms of the hands. This ritual is performed during the holiday's prayer service and symbolizes unity, joy, and gratitude.
