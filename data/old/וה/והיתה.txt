Etymology: The word "וְהִיא" (v'hi'ta) is a Hebrew phrase composed of two parts: "וְ" (v') meaning "and" and "הִיא" (hi'ta) meaning "she." 

Grammar: "וְהִיא" is a pronoun in the Hebrew language, specifically the third person singular feminine form. It is used to refer to a female subject or object in a sentence.

Description: "וְהִיא" is commonly translated as "and she" or "and it" in English. It is often used in biblical texts to introduce a new subject or to refer back to a previously mentioned female subject.
