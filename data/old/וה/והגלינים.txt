Etymology: The word "והגלינים" is a Hebrew word that consists of the following components: ו (vav) meaning "and", ה (he) meaning "the", גלין (galin) meaning "chicken", and ים (yim) indicating plural. Therefore, the literal translation of והגלינים is "and the chickens".

Grammar: והגלינים is a noun phrase in Hebrew. It is composed of a definite article "ה" (the) followed by the noun "גלינים" (chickens), and the conjunction "ו" (and) connects it to another element in the sentence.

Description: והגלינים refers to a specific group of chickens. It can be used to describe a flock of chickens or to refer to chickens in a general sense.
