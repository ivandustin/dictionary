Etymology: The word "והרבית" (vaharbit) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root רבה (ravah), which means "to increase" or "to multiply". The prefix ו (vav) in Hebrew is often used to indicate "and" or "together with". So, והרבית (vaharbit) can be translated as "and you shall increase" or "and you shall multiply".

Grammar: והרבית (vaharbit) is a verb in the second person singular form in the future tense. It is used in the context of a command or instruction, indicating that the speaker is telling the listener to increase or multiply something.

Description: והרבית (vaharbit) is a word that appears in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh), specifically in the book of Genesis (Bereishit) 1:28. In this verse, God blesses Adam and Eve and tells them to be fruitful and multiply, filling the earth and subduing it. The word is used to convey the idea of procreation and population growth. It is often interpreted as a commandment to have children and continue the human race.
