Etymology: The word "והגה" (v'hagah) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ה.ג.ה (h.g.h), which means "to meditate" or "to ponder." It is derived from the Hebrew verb הָגָה (hagah), which means "to speak" or "to utter."

- Singular: אֲנִי וְהָגָה (ani v'hagah) - I meditate
- Plural: אֲנַחְנוּ וְהָגִים (anachnu v'hagim) - We meditate

Description: The word "והגה" (v'hagah) refers to the act of meditating, pondering, or contemplating. It implies deep thought and reflection. In Jewish tradition, "והגה בו יומם ולילה" (v'hagita bo yomam valaylah) is a commandment to meditate on the Torah day and night.
