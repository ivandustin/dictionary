Etymology: The word "והחשכתי" is a Hebrew word that comes from the root חשך (chashak), which means "darkness" or "to be dark." The prefix וה (v'ha) means "and the." So, והחשכתי can be translated as "and I will darken" or "and I will make dark."

Grammar: והחשכתי is a verb form in the Hebrew language. It is in the future tense and first person singular. The prefix וה (v'ha) indicates the future tense, and the root חשך (chashak) is conjugated to match the first person singular subject.

Description: והחשכתי is a word used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh), specifically in the book of Exodus (Shemot) 10:21. It is part of the narrative of the ten plagues that were inflicted upon Egypt. In this verse, it is God speaking to Moses, foretelling the ninth plague, which is darkness. The word signifies the action of God causing darkness to cover the land of Egypt for three days.
