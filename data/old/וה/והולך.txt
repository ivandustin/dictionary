Etymology: The word "וַהוּא" (vahul) is a Hebrew phrase composed of the conjunction "וַ" (va) meaning "and" and the pronoun "הוּא" (hu) meaning "he" or "it".

Grammar: "וַהוּא" is a masculine singular form in Hebrew. It is used to refer to a male person or object.

Description: "וַהוּא" is commonly translated as "and he" or "and it" in English. It is often used in biblical texts to introduce a new subject or to continue a narrative.
