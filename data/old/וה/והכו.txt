Etymology: The word "והכו" (v'haku) is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "וה" (v'h) meaning "and the" and "כו" (ku) which is an abbreviation for "כאומר" (ka'omer) meaning "as it says" or "like saying". 

Grammar: "והכו" is a conjunction phrase in Hebrew, combining the word "וה" (v'h) meaning "and the" with the abbreviation "כו" (ku) meaning "as it says". It is commonly used to introduce a quote or a reference to a source.

Description: "והכו" is often used in Hebrew texts, especially in religious and legal contexts, to indicate that a statement or a concept is being quoted or referenced from a specific source. It is similar to the English phrase "as it says" or "like saying".
