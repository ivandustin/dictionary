Etymology: The word "יבואו" (pronounced "yavo'u") is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root word "בוא" (pronounced "bo"), which means "to come" or "to arrive".

Grammar: "יבואו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb "בוא" (to come). In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense. The future tense is used to express actions that will happen in the future.

Description: "יבואו" is used to describe an action that multiple people or objects will perform in the future. It can be translated as "they will come" or "they will arrive" in English.
