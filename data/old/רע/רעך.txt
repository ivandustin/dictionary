Etymology: The word "רעד" (pronounced "ra'ad") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ר-ע-ד (R-A-D), which means "to speak" or "to talk."

Grammar: In Hebrew, רעד is a verb that belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני רועד (ani ro'ed) - I speak
- אתה רועד (ata ro'ed) - You speak (masculine singular)
- את רועדת (at ro'edet) - You speak (feminine singular)
- הוא רועד (hu ro'ed) - He speaks
- היא רועדת (hi ro'edet) - She speaks
- אנחנו רועדים (anachnu ro'edim) - We speak
- אתם רועדים (atem ro'edim) - You speak (masculine plural)
- אתן רועדות (aten ro'edot) - You speak (feminine plural)
- הם רועדים (hem ro'edim) - They speak (masculine)
- הן רועדות (hen ro'edot) - They speak (feminine)

Description: רעד is a versatile word that can refer to various forms of speaking or talking. It can be used to describe a conversation, a speech, or any form of verbal communication.
