Etymology: The word "רעיה" (re'iyah) comes from the Hebrew root רעה (ra'ah), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to." It is related to the concept of caring for and guiding a flock of sheep.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "רעיה" is a feminine noun. It is used to refer to a female shepherd or a female caretaker of animals.

Description: "רעיה" is a term used in Hebrew to describe a woman who takes care of and tends to animals, particularly sheep. She is responsible for guiding and protecting the flock, ensuring they have food and water, and keeping them safe from harm. The role of a "רעיה" requires patience, skill, and a deep understanding of the animals she cares for.
