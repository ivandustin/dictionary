Etymology: The word "רעים" (pronounced "ra'im") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root רעה (pronounced "ra'ah"), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to." In Hebrew, the word רעים specifically refers to a group of shepherds or a flock of sheep.

Grammar: רעים is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (pronounced "im") to the root רעה. The plural form indicates that it refers to multiple shepherds or a group of sheep.

Description: רעים is a term used in Hebrew to describe a group of shepherds or a flock of sheep. It can also be used metaphorically to refer to a group of people who are being guided or led by someone.
