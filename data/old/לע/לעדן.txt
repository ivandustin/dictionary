Etymology: The word "לעדן" (ladan) comes from the Hebrew root עדן (ayin-dalet-nun), which means "to delight" or "to enjoy." It is derived from the noun עֵדֶן (eden), which means "paradise" or "delight."

Grammar: "לעדן" (ladan) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי לוֹעֵדֵן (ani lo'eden) - I delight
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה לוֹעֵדֵן (atah lo'eden) - You delight
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ לוֹעֶדֶת (at lo'edet) - You delight
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם לוֹעֲדִים (atem lo'adim) - You (plural) delight
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן לוֹעֲדוֹת (aten lo'adot) - You (plural) delight

Description: The word "לעדן" (ladan) is used to express the act of delighting, enjoying, or taking pleasure in something. It can refer to physical enjoyment, such as enjoying a delicious meal or a beautiful view, as well as to emotional or spiritual delight.
