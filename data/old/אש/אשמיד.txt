Etymology: The word "אַשְמִיד" (Ashmid) comes from the Hebrew root שׁ.מ.ד (sh.m.d), which means "to destroy" or "to annihilate". 

Grammar: אַשְמִיד is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the Piel stem, which is an intensive form of the verb. In this form, the verb conveys the idea of causing destruction or annihilation.

Description: אַשְמִיד is used to describe the act of destroying or annihilating something or someone completely. It implies a complete and thorough destruction, leaving nothing behind. This word is often used in the context of war, punishment, or divine judgment.
