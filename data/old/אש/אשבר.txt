Etymology: The word "אשבר" (Ashbar) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root א-ש-ב-ר (A-Sh-B-R), which means "to break" or "to shatter."

Grammar: In Hebrew, אשבר is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and forms to match different subjects and contexts.

Description: The word אשבר can be translated as "I will break" or "I am breaking" in English. It is used to express the action of breaking or shattering something physically or metaphorically.
