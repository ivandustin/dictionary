Etymology: The word "מתלאה" (Matlaha) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root "לאה" (lah), which means "to tire" or "to weary". The prefix "מ" (m) indicates the causative form, so "מתלאה" (Matlaha) can be translated as "to cause to tire" or "to cause to weary".

Grammar: "מתלאה" (Matlaha) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מתלאה (Ani matlaha) - I tire/weary
- Plural: אנחנו מתלאים (Anachnu matla'im) - We tire/weary

Description: "מתלאה" (Matlaha) is used to describe the act of becoming tired or weary. It can refer to physical exhaustion or mental fatigue. It is often used in the context of hard work, exertion, or stress.
