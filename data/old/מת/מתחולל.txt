Etymology: The word "מתחולל" (Mat'chulal) is derived from the Hebrew root ח-ו-ל (ch-w-l), which means "to roll" or "to revolve". The prefix מ- (m-) indicates the causative form, so מתחולל can be translated as "to cause to roll" or "to cause to revolve".

Grammar: In Hebrew, מתחולל is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It can be conjugated to match different subjects and tenses.

Description: מתחולל is used to describe a process or action of something rolling or revolving. It can be used in a literal sense, such as the rolling of a ball, or in a figurative sense, such as the development or unfolding of events.
