Etymology: The word "מתלחשים" (metlachshim) is derived from the Hebrew root לחש (lachash), which means "to whisper" or "to murmur."

Grammar: "מתלחשים" is a plural verb form in the present tense, masculine gender, and third person. It is used to describe a group of people who are whispering or murmuring.

Description: "מתלחשים" refers to individuals who are speaking in a low, soft voice, often in a secretive or confidential manner. It can also imply a sense of gossip or speaking in hushed tones.
