Etymology: The word "מתחת" (matḥat) comes from the Hebrew root תחת (taḥat), which means "under" or "beneath". 

Grammar: "מתחת" is an adverb in Hebrew. It is used to indicate a location or position that is below or underneath something else. It can also be used metaphorically to express being in a lower or inferior position.

Description: "מתחת" is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday Hebrew. It is used to describe physical locations, such as "under the table" (תחת השולחן) or "under the bed" (תחת המיטה). It can also be used to describe abstract concepts, such as "under pressure" (תחת לחץ) or "under someone's control" (תחת שליטה של מישהו).
