Etymology: The word "מתבנא" (Matbena) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root בנה (b-n-h), which means "to build" or "to construct". The prefix מ (m) indicates the causative form, so מתבנא (Matbena) can be translated as "to cause to build" or "to cause to construct".

Grammar: In Hebrew, מתבנא (Matbena) is a verb in the causative form. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- Singular:
  - 1st person: אני מתבנה (Ani matbene)
  - 2nd person: אתה מתבנה (Ata matbene)
  - 3rd person: הוא/היא מתבנה (Hu/Hi matbene)

- Plural:
  - 1st person: אנחנו מתבנים (Anachnu matbenim)
  - 2nd person: אתם מתבנים (Atem matbenim)
  - 3rd person: הם/הן מתבנים (Hem/Hen matbenim)

Description: מתבנא (Matbena) is a verb that describes the action of causing someone or something to build or construct. It implies that the subject is actively involved in the process of building or construction.
