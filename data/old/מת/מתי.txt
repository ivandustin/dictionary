Etymology: The word "מתי" (pronounced "matay") is a Hebrew word that means "when" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root ת.י.ה (T.Y.H), which means "to be" or "to exist."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מתי" is an interrogative adverb used to ask about the time or duration of an action or event. It is commonly used in questions such as "מתי אתה הולך?" (When are you going?) or "מתי האירוע יתקיים?" (When will the event take place?).

Description: "מתי" is a versatile word that is essential for asking questions about time in Hebrew. It allows speakers to inquire about specific moments or periods, and it is commonly used in everyday conversations.
