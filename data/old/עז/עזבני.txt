Etymology: The word "עזבני" (azavani) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ע-ז-ב (ayin-zayin-bet), which means "to leave" or "to abandon."

Grammar: "עזבני" is a verb in the imperative form, second person singular. It is used when addressing someone directly and commanding them to leave or abandon.

Description: "עזבני" is a strong and direct command in Hebrew, telling someone to leave or abandon. It can be used in various contexts, such as asking someone to leave a place, stop bothering you, or end a relationship.
