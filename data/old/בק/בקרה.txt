Etymology: The word "בקרה" (bakarah) is derived from the Hebrew root "בקר" (bakar), which means "to examine" or "to inspect."

Grammar: "בקרה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of examining or inspecting something.

Description: "בקרה" refers to the process of checking or inspecting something in order to ensure its quality, accuracy, or compliance with certain standards. It can be used in various contexts, such as quality control in manufacturing, auditing financial records, or monitoring systems and processes.
