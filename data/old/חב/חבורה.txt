Etymology: The word "חבורה" (chavurah) comes from the Hebrew root ח.ב.ר (ch.b.r), which means "to join" or "to connect." It is derived from the noun חֶבְרָה (chevrah), meaning "a group" or "a company."

Grammar: "חבורה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is usually used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form חֲבָרוֹת (chavurot). It can be modified by adjectives or determiners to specify the type or characteristics of the group.

Description: "חבורה" refers to a group of people who come together for a common purpose or shared interest. It can be used to describe various types of groups, such as a social group, a study group, or a team.
