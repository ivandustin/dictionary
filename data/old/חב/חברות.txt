Etymology: The word "חברות" (chavrutot) comes from the Hebrew root ח.ב.ר (ch.b.r), which means "to join" or "to connect." It is derived from the noun חֶבֶר (chever), meaning "friend" or "companion."

Grammar: "חברות" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ות (-ot) to the singular form חברה (chavera), which means "friendship" or "companionship."

Description: "חברות" refers to a group of friends or companions who study together. In Jewish tradition, it is common for individuals to form chavrutot to study religious texts, such as the Talmud, together. Chavrutot provide a supportive and collaborative environment for learning and discussing complex topics.
