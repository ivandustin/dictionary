Etymology: The word "אינך" (pronounced "einkha") is a Hebrew word that means "you are not." It is derived from the Hebrew root א.נ.ה (A.N.H), which means "to be" or "to exist," and the negative particle אַיִן (ayin), which means "no" or "not."

Grammar: "אינך" is the second person singular form of the verb "להיות" (to be) in the present tense, combined with the negative particle "אַיִן" (no/not). It is used to express the negation of existence or the absence of something.

Description: "אינך" is a formal and literary way to say "you are not" in Hebrew. It is often used in religious or poetic contexts.
