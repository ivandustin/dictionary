Etymology: The word "איבינו" (pronounced "Ivinu") is a Hebrew word that means "our father." It is derived from the Hebrew root אב (av), which means "father."

Grammar: "איבינו" is a possessive noun in Hebrew, specifically in the first person plural form. It is used to refer to "our father" in a collective sense, indicating that the speaker is referring to their father as a shared figure among a group of people.

Description: "איבינו" is a term of endearment and respect used to refer to one's father in Hebrew. It is often used in prayers and religious contexts, such as in the Jewish prayer known as the "Avot" (Fathers), where it is used to address God as the father of the Jewish people.
