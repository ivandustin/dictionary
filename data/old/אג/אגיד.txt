Etymology: The word "אגיד" (Agid) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ג-ו-ד (G-W-D), which means "to tell" or "to narrate" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "אגיד" (Agid) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the first person singular form of the future tense. The verb is conjugated as follows:

- אני אגיד (Ani Agid) - I will tell

Description: "אגיד" (Agid) means "I will tell" in Hebrew. It is used to express the action of narrating or recounting something in the future tense.
