Etymology: The word "תמהר" (tamhar) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root מ-ה-ר (M-H-R), which means "to hasten" or "to hurry."

Grammar: "תמהר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני ממהר (ani memhar) - I hurry
- Singular feminine: אני ממהרת (ani memharet) - I hurry
- Plural masculine: אנחנו ממהרים (anachnu memharim) - We hurry
- Plural feminine: אנחנו ממהרות (anachnu memharot) - We hurry

Description: "תמהר" means "to hurry" or "to hasten" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of moving or acting quickly, often in response to a time constraint or urgency.
