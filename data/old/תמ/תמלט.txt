Etymology: The word "תמלט" (temelt) comes from the Hebrew root מ.ל.ט (m.l.t), which means "to escape" or "to flee". It is derived from the noun מַלְטֵף (maltef), which means "fugitive" or "refugee".

Grammar: "תמלט" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מַתְמִילֵט (ani matmilét) - I escape
- Singular feminine: אני מַתְמִילָה (ani matmilá) - I escape
- Plural: אנחנו מַתְמִילִים (anachnu matmilim) - We escape

Description: "תמלט" means "to escape" or "to flee" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of running away or seeking refuge from a dangerous or threatening situation.
