Etymology: The word "תמלח" (tamlech) comes from the Hebrew root מ-ל-ח (m-l-ch), which means "to salt" or "to season." 

Grammar: "תמלח" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני ממליח (ani memalich) - I salt, אתה ממליח (ata memalich) - you salt (masculine singular), את ממליכה (at memalicha) - you salt (feminine singular), הוא ממליח (hu memalich) - he salts, היא ממליחה (hi memalicha) - she salts, אנחנו ממליחים (anachnu memalichim) - we salt, אתם ממליחים (atem memalichim) - you salt (masculine plural), אתן ממליחות (atan memalichot) - you salt (feminine plural), הם ממליחים (hem memalichim) - they salt (masculine), הן ממליחות (hen memalichot) - they salt (feminine).

Description: "תמלח" refers to the action of salting or seasoning food. It is commonly used in cooking to enhance the flavor of dishes.
