- Singular:
  - 1st person: אֲנִי מַמְשִׁיל (ani mamshil) - I rule
  - 2nd person: אַתָּה מַמְשִׁיל (atah mamshil) - You rule (masculine)

  - 3rd person: הוּא/הִיא מַמְשִׁיל/ה (hu/hi mamshil/a) - He/She rules
- Plural:
  - 1st person: אֲנַחְנוּ מַמְשִׁילִים (anachnu mamshilim) - We rule
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם מַמְשִׁילִים (atem mamshilim) - You rule (masculine/plural)
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּן מַמְשִׁילוֹת (aten mamshilot) - You rule (feminine/plural)
  - 3rd person: הֵם/הֵן מַמְשִׁילִים/הַן (hem/hen mamshilim/hen) - They rule

Description: The verb "תמשל" (tamshel) means "to rule" or "to govern" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of having authority or control over something or someone.
