Etymology: The word "תבהל" (teval) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root בהל (bahal), which means "to be alarmed" or "to be disturbed". The prefix ת (tav) indicates the causative form, so תבהל (teval) means "to cause to be alarmed" or "to cause to be disturbed".

Grammar: תבהל (teval) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מבהיל (ani mevahel) - I cause to be alarmed
- Plural: אנחנו מבהילים (anachnu mevahelim) - We cause to be alarmed

Description: תבהל (teval) is used to describe the action of causing someone to be alarmed or disturbed. It can refer to a sudden or unexpected event that causes anxiety or fear.
