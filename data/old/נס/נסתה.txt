Etymology: The word "נֶסֶתָה" (nesetah) comes from the Hebrew root נסה (n-s-h), which means "to test" or "to try." It is derived from the noun נֵס (nes), meaning "miracle" or "sign."

Grammar: נֶסֶתָה (nesetah) is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is used to describe a test, trial, or experiment.

Description: נֶסֶתָה (nesetah) refers to a process of testing or trying something in order to determine its quality, effectiveness, or suitability. It can be used in various contexts, such as scientific experiments, product testing, or even personal trials and challenges.
