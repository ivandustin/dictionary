Etymology: The word "נֶסֶה" (nesheh) comes from the Hebrew root נסה (n-s-h), which means "to test" or "to try." 

Grammar: נֶסֶה (nesheh) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the Qal form, which is the basic form of the verb. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form. 

Description: נֶסֶה (nesheh) means "he tested" or "he tried" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of someone attempting or experimenting with something to see its outcome or effectiveness.
