Etymology: The word "אודיע" (pronounced "odi'a") comes from the Hebrew root י-ד-ע (yod-dalet-ayin), which means "to inform" or "to make known." It is derived from the noun יְדִיעָה (yedi'ah), meaning "knowledge" or "information."

Grammar: אודיע is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אָנִי אוֹדִיע (ani odi'a) - I inform
- אַתָּה אוֹדֵעַ (atah odea) - You inform (masculine singular)
- אַתְּ אוֹדַעְיִי (at oda'i) - You inform (feminine singular)
- הוּא/הִיא אוֹדֵעַ (hu/hi odea) - He/She informs
- אֲנַחְנוּ אוֹדְעָה (anachnu ode'ah) - We inform
- אַתֶּם אוֹדְעִים (atem ode'im) - You inform (masculine plural)
- אַתֶּן אוֹדְעוֹת (aten ode'ot) - You inform (feminine plural)
- הֵם/הֵן אוֹדְעִים (hem/hen ode'im) - They inform

Description: אודיע is an active verb that means "to inform" or "to make known." It is used when someone wants to communicate or convey information to others. This verb can be used in various contexts, such as sharing news, giving updates, or providing knowledge about a particular subject.
