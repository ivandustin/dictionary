Etymology: The word "אוליך" (pronounced "olikh") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root א.ל.ך, which means "to lead" or "to guide."

- אני מוליך (ani molekh) - I lead
- אתה מוליך (atah molekh) - You lead (masculine singular)
- את מוליכה (at molekhet) - You lead (feminine singular)
- הוא מוליך (hu molekh) - He leads
- היא מוליכה (hi molekhet) - She leads
- אנחנו מוליכים (anakhnu molekhim) - We lead
- אתם מוליכים (atem molekhim) - You lead (masculine plural)
- אתן מוליכות (atan molekhot) - You lead (feminine plural)
- הם מוליכים (hem molekhim) - They lead (masculine)
- הן מוליכות (hen molekhot) - They lead (feminine)

Description: "אוליך" means "to lead" or "to guide" in Hebrew. It refers to the action of showing the way or directing someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as leading a group, guiding someone through a process, or being in charge of a project.
