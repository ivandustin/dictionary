Etymology: The word "יטיב" (pronounced "yitiv" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root טב (tav-bet), which means "good" or "pleasant." It is related to the word טוב (tov), which also means "good."

- Singular:
  - אֲנִי יוֹטֵב (ani yotev) - I am good
  - אַתָּה יוֹטֵב (atah yotev) - You (masculine singular) are good
  - אַתְּ יוֹטֶבֶת (at yotevet) - You (feminine singular) are good
  - הוּא יוֹטֵב (hu yotev) - He is good
  - הִיא יוֹטֶבֶת (hi yotevet) - She is good

- Plural:
  - אֲנַחְנוּ יוֹטְבִים (anachnu yotvim) - We are good
  - אַתֶּם יוֹטְבִים (atem yotvim) - You (masculine plural) are good
  - אַתֶּן יוֹטְבוֹת (aten yotvot) - You (feminine plural) are good
  - הֵם יוֹטְבִים (hem yotvim) - They (masculine) are good
  - הֵן יוֹטְבוֹת (hen yotvot) - They (feminine) are good

Description: The word "יטיב" is used to describe someone or something as good, pleasant, or well-behaved. It can also be used to express approval or satisfaction.
