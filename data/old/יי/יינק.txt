Etymology: The word "ינק" (Yanak) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root י-נ-ק (Y-N-K), which means "to suckle" or "to nurse."

Grammar: "ינק" (Yanak) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי יוֹנֵק (ani yonek) - I suckle
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה יוֹנֵק (atah yonek) - You (masculine) suckle
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ יוֹנֶקֶת (at yoneket) - You (feminine) suckle
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם יוֹנְקִים (atem yonkim) - You (masculine plural) suckle
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן יוֹנְקוֹת (aten yonkot) - You (feminine plural) suckle

Description: The verb "ינק" (Yanak) refers to the act of breastfeeding or nursing a baby. It is used to describe the action of a mother providing nourishment to her infant by allowing them to suckle milk from her breast.
