Etymology: The word "יטיבו" (pronounced "yitivu") is a Hebrew verb in the third person plural form. It is derived from the root ט.ו.ב (T.W.B), which means "to be good" or "to do good."

Grammar: "יטיבו" is a verb in the present tense, indicating that the action is happening in the current time. The third person plural form suggests that the subject of the verb is a group of people or multiple entities.

Description: The word "יטיבו" is often translated as "they will do good" or "they will be good." It implies that the subject is engaged in acts of kindness, goodness, or positive actions.
