Etymology: The word "ירש" (Yarash) comes from the Hebrew root י-ר-ש (Y-R-Sh), which means "to inherit" or "to possess." 

Grammar: "ירש" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in various tenses and forms to indicate different grammatical aspects such as past, present, and future, as well as singular and plural subjects.

Description: "ירש" means "to inherit" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of receiving or acquiring something, typically property or possessions, from a previous owner, usually through legal or familial means. In a broader sense, it can also be used metaphorically to describe the acquisition or adoption of traits, qualities, or characteristics.
