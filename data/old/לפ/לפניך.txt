Etymology: The word "לפניך" (pronounced "lifnecha" in Hebrew) is a combination of two Hebrew words: "לפני" (pronounced "lifnei") meaning "before" or "in front of," and "ך" (pronounced "cha") which is a second-person singular pronoun meaning "you." Therefore, "לפניך" can be translated as "before you" or "in front of you."

Grammar: "לפניך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It is composed of the preposition "ל" (pronounced "l") meaning "to" or "for," and the noun "פניך" (pronounced "panecha") meaning "your face" or "in front of you." The phrase is used to indicate direction or location, expressing that something is positioned or directed towards the person being addressed.

Description: "לפניך" is a versatile phrase used in various contexts in the Hebrew language. It can be used to convey physical proximity, as in "הספר נמצא לפניך" (The book is in front of you). It can also be used metaphorically to express a figurative presence or attention, as in "אני עומד לפניך" (I am standing before you) meaning "I am at your service" or "I am ready to listen to you." Overall, "לפניך" is a common phrase in Hebrew that is used to indicate a position or direction towards the person being addressed.
