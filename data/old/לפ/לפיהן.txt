Etymology: The word "לפיהן" (pronounced "lefi-hen") is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "ל" (pronounced "le") meaning "to" or "for", and "פיהן" (pronounced "fi-hen") meaning "their mouth". 

Grammar: "לפיהן" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" indicates direction or purpose, and "פיהן" is a possessive pronoun meaning "their mouth". Together, the phrase can be translated as "to their mouth" or "for their mouth".

Description: "לפיהן" is used to refer to something that is done or given specifically for the benefit or satisfaction of someone. It implies a sense of catering to someone's desires or preferences.
