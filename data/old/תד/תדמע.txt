Etymology: The word "תדמע" (pronounced "tadma") comes from the Hebrew root דמע (DM'A), which means "to cry" or "to weep". It is derived from the noun דמעה (dema'a), which means "tear".

Grammar: "תדמע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תודמע (ani todma)
- Plural: אנחנו תודמעים (anachnu todma'im)
- Masculine: אתה תודמע (atah todma)
- Feminine: את תודמעי (at todma'i)
- Masculine plural: אתם תודמעים (atem todma'im)
- Feminine plural: אתן תודמענה (aten todma'nah)

Description: The word "תדמע" means "to cry" or "to weep" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of shedding tears as an expression of sadness, pain, or strong emotion.
