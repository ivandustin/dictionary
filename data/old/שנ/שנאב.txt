Etymology: The word "שנאב" (pronounced "shenav" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root "שנא" (shin-nun-aleph), which means "to hate" or "to detest."

Grammar: "שנאב" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אֲנִי שׁוֹנֵא (ani shone)
- אַתָּה שׁוֹנֵא (ata shone)
- הוּא/הִיא שׁוֹנֵא (hu/hi shone)
- אֲנַחְנוּ שׁוֹנְאִים (anachnu shon'im)
- אַתֶּם שׁוֹנְאִים (atem shon'im)
- הֵם/הֵן שׁוֹנְאוֹת (hem/hen shon'ot)

Description: The word "שנאב" means "to hate" or "to detest" in Hebrew. It is used to express a strong feeling of dislike or aversion towards someone or something.
