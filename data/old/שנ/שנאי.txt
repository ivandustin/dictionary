Etymology: The word "שנאי" (pronounced "shin-ay") comes from the Hebrew root שנא (shin-ayin-aleph), which means "to hate" or "to detest." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, "שנאי" is a noun that means "hater" or "enemy." It is a masculine singular noun, and its plural form is "שנאים" (shin-ayin-yod-mem). 

Description: "שנאי" is used to describe someone who hates or detests another person or thing. It can refer to an individual who holds strong negative feelings towards someone or something, often resulting in animosity or hostility.
