Etymology: The word "ענית" (pronounced "anit") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ענה (pronounced "ana"), which means "to answer" or "to respond."

Grammar: "ענית" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is used to describe an action that someone or something did in the past.

Description: The word "ענית" is often translated as "answered" or "responded" in English. It indicates that someone provided a response or reply to a question, statement, or request.
