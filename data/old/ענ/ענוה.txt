Etymology: The word "עִבְרִית" (Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root עבר (A-B-R), which means "to cross over" or "to pass through". It is believed to refer to the Hebrew language as the language of the Hebrew people who crossed over the Jordan River into the land of Canaan.

Grammar: Hebrew is a Semitic language and has a unique grammatical structure. It is written from right to left and consists of consonants and vowel points. Hebrew nouns have gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural), and verbs are conjugated according to tense, person, and number.

Description: Hebrew is an ancient language that has been spoken for thousands of years. It is the language of the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is considered sacred by Jews. Modern Hebrew, also known as Israeli Hebrew, is the official language of the State of Israel and is spoken by millions of people worldwide. It is used in various domains, including literature, media, education, and daily life.
