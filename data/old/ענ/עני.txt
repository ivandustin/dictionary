Etymology: The word "עני" (ani) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ענה (anah), which means "to answer" or "to respond."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "עני" (ani) is a first-person singular pronoun. It is used to refer to oneself, similar to the English pronoun "I."

Description: "עני" (ani) is a personal pronoun that is commonly used in everyday speech and writing in Hebrew. It is used to indicate the speaker or writer as the subject of a sentence. For example, "עני אוהב לקרוא" (ani ohev likro) means "I love to read."
