Etymology: The word "סחף" (sachaf) comes from the Hebrew root ס.ח.פ (s.ch.p), which means "to sweep away" or "to carry off." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, סחף is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and forms to match different subjects and contexts.

Description: סחף is used to describe the action of sweeping or carrying something away forcefully. It can be used both literally, to refer to physically sweeping or carrying something away, and figuratively, to describe the act of overwhelming or engulfing someone or something.
