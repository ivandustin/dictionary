Etymology: The word "פתי" (pronounced "p'ti") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root word "פתה" (pronounced "patah"), which means "to open" or "to entice."

Grammar: "פתי" is an adjective in Hebrew. It is used to describe someone who is naive, gullible, or easily persuaded.

Description: When used to describe a person, "פתי" implies that they are easily influenced or deceived. It suggests a lack of critical thinking or skepticism.
