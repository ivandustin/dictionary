Etymology: The word "פתחות" (pronounced "petaḥot") is a Hebrew noun derived from the root פתח (pronounced "pataḥ"), which means "to open" or "to uncover."

Grammar: "פתחות" is a plural noun in Hebrew, indicating multiple openings or entrances. It is formed by adding the suffix ות (pronounced "ot") to the singular form פתח (pronounced "petaḥ").

Description: "פתחות" refers to openings or entrances, both literal and metaphorical. It can describe physical openings such as doors, gates, or windows, as well as figurative openings like opportunities or possibilities.
