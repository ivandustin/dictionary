Etymology: The word "סקלות" comes from the Hebrew root ס.ק.ל (s.k.l), which means "to stone". It is derived from the noun סֶקֶל (sekel), meaning "a stone".

Grammar: "סקלות" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the plural form, indicating multiple instances of stoning.

Description: "סקלות" refers to the act of stoning, which is a form of punishment where a person is pelted with stones until death. In ancient times, stoning was a common method of execution for various offenses, such as adultery or blasphemy. The practice of stoning is mentioned in religious texts, including the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament).
