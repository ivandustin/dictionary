Etymology: The word "אליהם" (pronounced "elihem") is a Hebrew word. It is a combination of the preposition "אל" (meaning "to" or "towards") and the pronoun "הם" (meaning "them"). 

Grammar: "אליהם" is a third-person plural pronoun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to a group of people or objects that are not present in the conversation. It is commonly used in sentences where the action is directed towards a group of individuals.

Description: "אליהם" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is often used in storytelling, descriptions, or instructions to refer to a group of people or objects. It helps to specify that the action or direction is towards them.
