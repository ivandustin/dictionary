Etymology: The word "אלו" (pronounced "elu") is a Hebrew word that means "these" or "these are." It is derived from the Hebrew root אלה, which means "to be" or "to exist."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אלו" is a demonstrative pronoun used to indicate a group of objects or people that are near the speaker. It is used in the plural form and agrees in gender and number with the noun it modifies.

Description: "אלו" is commonly used in Hebrew to point out or identify a specific group of things or people. It is often used to introduce a list or to refer to a group of items that have been previously mentioned. For example, "אלו הספרים הם חדשים" (elu hasfarim hem chadashim) means "these books are new."
