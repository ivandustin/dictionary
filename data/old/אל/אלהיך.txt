Etymology: The word "אלהיך" (Elohecha) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root אלה (Elah), which means "god" or "deity." The suffix ך (cha) indicates possession, so אלהיך (Elohecha) can be translated as "your god" or "your deity."

Grammar: אלהיך (Elohecha) is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the singular form and is used to refer to a single god or deity. The suffix ך (cha) indicates the second person possessive pronoun "your."

Description: אלהיך (Elohecha) is a term used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) to refer to God. It is often translated as "your God" or "your deity" in English. The word emphasizes the personal relationship between an individual and their god or deity.
