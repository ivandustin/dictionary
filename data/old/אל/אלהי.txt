Etymology: The word "אלהי" (pronounced "Elohai") is a Hebrew word that means "my God." It is derived from the Hebrew word "אל" (pronounced "El"), which means "God," and the possessive suffix "י" (pronounced "ai"), which means "my."

Grammar: "אלהי" is a possessive form of the word "אל" (God) in Hebrew. The suffix "י" (my) indicates possession, making it mean "my God."

Description: "אלהי" is a term used in Hebrew to express a personal relationship with God. It is often used in prayers and religious texts to address God as a personal deity.
