Etymology: The word "אליהו" (pronounced "Eliyahu" in English) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Hebrew name אֵלִיָּהוּ (Eliyahu), which means "my God is Yahweh" or "Yahweh is my God". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אליהו" is a masculine proper noun. It is commonly used as a given name for boys. 

Description: "אליהו" is a name that holds significant religious and cultural importance in Judaism. It is the Hebrew equivalent of the English name "Elijah". In the Hebrew Bible, Elijah was a prophet who performed miracles and played a crucial role in the history of Israel. The name is still widely used today in Jewish communities and holds a positive connotation.
