Etymology: The word "אלוהי" (pronounced "Elohai") is a Hebrew word that means "my God." It is derived from the Hebrew word "אלוה" (pronounced "Eloah"), which means "God."

Grammar: "אלוהי" is a possessive form of the word "אלוה" (Eloah), indicating possession or ownership. The suffix "-י" (pronounced "-ai") is added to the word to indicate "my" or "mine."

Description: "אלוהי" is a term used in Hebrew to express a personal relationship with God. It is often used in prayers and religious texts to address God as a personal deity. The word emphasizes a sense of closeness and intimacy with the divine.
