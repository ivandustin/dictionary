Etymology: The word "אלהכם" (pronounced "al-ha-khem") is a Hebrew word. It is a combination of two words: "אלה" (pronounced "el-ha") meaning "these" and "כם" (pronounced "khem") meaning "you". Together, "אלהכם" means "these you" or "these are you".

Grammar: "אלהכם" is a pronoun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to a group of people, specifically the second person plural. It is similar to the English pronoun "you all" or "you guys".

Description: "אלהכם" is used to address or refer to a group of people. It is commonly used in informal or colloquial speech in Hebrew-speaking communities.
