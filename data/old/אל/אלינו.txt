Etymology: The word "אלינו" (pronounced "alaynu") is a Hebrew word that translates to "upon us" or "our responsibility." It is derived from the Hebrew root א-ל-י, which means "upon" or "on."

Grammar: "אלינו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition "על" (al), meaning "on" or "upon," and the pronoun "נו" (nu), meaning "us" or "our." It is used to express a sense of obligation or responsibility.

Description: "אלינו" is a phrase commonly used in Jewish prayers and liturgy. It is often used to express the idea that certain responsibilities or duties are upon the Jewish people. It can also be used to convey a sense of gratitude or acknowledgment of one's obligations.
