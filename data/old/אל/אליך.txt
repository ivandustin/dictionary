Etymology: The word "אליך" (pronounced "elecha" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root א-ל-ך, which means "to you" or "towards you". It is a combination of the preposition אֶל (el), meaning "to" or "towards", and the second person singular pronoun ךָ (cha), meaning "you".

Grammar: "אליך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition אֶל (el) and the second person singular pronoun ךָ (cha). It is used to indicate direction or movement towards the second person singular, similar to the English preposition "to" or "towards".

Description: "אליך" is a word in Hebrew that is used to address or refer to someone in the second person singular. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts to express a sense of direction or movement towards the person being addressed.
