Etymology: The word "אלילים" (pronounced "elilim") is a Hebrew word that appears in the Hebrew Bible. It is derived from the root אלל (pronounced "alal"), which means "to be weak" or "to be worthless." In the context of the Bible, it refers to idols or false gods that are considered to be powerless and insignificant.

Grammar: "אלילים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ים" to the singular form "אליל" (pronounced "elil"). The word is used to refer to multiple idols or false gods.

Description: In Hebrew, "אלילים" is used to describe idols or false gods that are worshipped by people who have turned away from the true God.
