Etymology: The word "אל" (pronounced "el") is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Hebrew root אלף-למד (aleph-lamed), which means "to learn" or "to teach."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אל" is a preposition that is used to indicate motion or direction towards something or someone. It is often translated as "to" or "towards" in English.

Description: "אל" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can indicate physical movement towards a location or person, as well as metaphorical movement towards a goal or purpose. It is commonly used in phrases such as "אל הבית" (to the house), "אל הים" (to the sea), or "אל האהבה" (towards love).
