Etymology: The word "אלהיו" (pronounced "elohav") is a Hebrew word that means "his God" or "his deity." It is a combination of the word "אל" (pronounced "el"), which means "God," and the suffix "היו" (pronounced "hav"), which indicates possession.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אלהיו" is a possessive form of the word "אלהים" (pronounced "elohim"), which means "God" or "deity." The suffix "היו" is added to indicate possession, similar to the English possessive pronoun "his."

Description: "אלהיו" is used in Hebrew to refer to someone's God or deity. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to indicate a personal relationship between an individual and their God.
