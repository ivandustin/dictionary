Etymology: The word "ילקדו" (Yalkadu) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root י-ל-ק-ד (Y-L-K-D).

Grammar: "ילקדו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb ילקד (Yalkad), which means "they will burn" in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ו (u) to the root י-ל-ק-ד.

Description: "ילקדו" is a verb that describes an action that will happen in the future, specifically the action of burning. It is used to indicate that a group of people or objects will be set on fire.
