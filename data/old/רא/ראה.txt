Etymology: The word "ראה" (ra'ah) comes from the Hebrew root ראה (r-'-h), which means "to see" or "to perceive." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, ראה is a verb in the Qal (simple) conjugation. It is used in the masculine singular form. 

Description: ראה is the Hebrew word for "see" or "look." It is commonly used to refer to the act of perceiving something visually with the eyes. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "understand" or "comprehend."
