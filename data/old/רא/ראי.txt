Etymology: The word "ראי" (pronounced "rai") is a Hebrew word that means "shepherd" or "pastor." It is derived from the Hebrew root ראה (ra'ah), which means "to see" or "to look after." In ancient times, shepherds were responsible for tending to and protecting their flocks, and the term "ראי" came to represent this role.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "ראי" is a noun that is used to refer to a male shepherd or pastor. It is typically used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form "רעים" (ra'im) to refer to a group of shepherds or pastors.

Description: The term "ראי" carries a sense of care, guidance, and protection. In biblical and religious contexts, it is often used metaphorically to describe God as the shepherd of his people, guiding and watching over them. The role of a shepherd or pastor is also associated with leadership, responsibility, and the nurturing of a community.
