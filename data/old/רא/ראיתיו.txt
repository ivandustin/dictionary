Etymology: The word "רָאִיתִיו" (ra'itiyu) is a Hebrew word derived from the root ראה (ra'ah), which means "to see". The suffix יו (yu) is added to indicate the first person singular possessive form.

Grammar: "רָאִיתִיו" is a verb form in the past tense, first person singular, with a possessive suffix. It translates to "I saw him" or "I saw it" in English.

Description: "רָאִיתִיו" is used to express the action of seeing something or someone in the past. It is commonly used in everyday conversations and written texts in Hebrew.
