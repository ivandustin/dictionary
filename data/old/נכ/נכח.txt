Etymology: The word "נכח" (nakhakh) comes from the Hebrew root נ-כ-ח (n-kh-kh), which means "to appear" or "to be present". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, נכח is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and forms to match different subjects and contexts.

Description: נכח is used to describe the act of appearing or being present in a certain place or situation. It can refer to physically being present or to the act of showing up or making oneself known.
