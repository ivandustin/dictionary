Etymology: The word "ישָׁה" (Yeshah) comes from the Hebrew root י-ש-ע (Y-SH-A), which means "to save" or "to deliver." It is derived from the noun יֵשׁוּעַ (Yeshua), which means "salvation" or "deliverance."

Grammar: ישָׁה is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the past tense. It is commonly used in the third person masculine singular form.

Description: ישָׁה means "he saved" or "he delivered" in Hebrew. It is often used in the context of God's saving acts or deliverance in the Hebrew Bible. The word carries a sense of rescue or liberation from danger or oppression.
