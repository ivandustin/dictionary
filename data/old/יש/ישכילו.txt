Etymology: The word "ישכילו" (Yaskilu) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root word "שכל" (sakal), which means "to understand" or "to have intelligence".

Grammar: "ישכילו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb "שכל" (sakal). In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and the tense of the sentence. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "ישכילו" is used to describe the action of a group of people understanding or having intelligence. It can be translated as "they will understand" or "they will have intelligence" in English.
