Etymology: The word "ישטטו" (pronounced "yishtetu") is a Hebrew verb in the third person plural form. It is derived from the root שטט (sh-t-t), which means "to wander" or "to roam".

Grammar: "ישטטו" is a verb in the present tense, indicating an ongoing action performed by a group of people. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense. The suffix "ו" (u) indicates the third person plural, referring to multiple individuals performing the action.

Description: The word "ישטטו" can be translated as "they wander" or "they roam" in English. It describes the action of a group of people moving around aimlessly or without a specific destination.
