Etymology: The word "ישלטו" (Yishl'tu) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root ש-ל-ט (sh-l-t), which means "to rule" or "to govern."

Grammar: "ישלטו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "ישלטו" means "they will rule" or "they will govern" in English. It implies that a group of people or entities will have authority or control over something or someone in the future.
