Etymology: The word "ישעיהו" (pronounced "Yeshayahu" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew name יְשַׁעְיָהוּ (Yeshayahu), which means "Yahweh is salvation" or "salvation of Yahweh". It is a combination of the Hebrew words יָשַׁע (yasha) meaning "to save" and יָהוּ (Yahu) which is a shortened form of the divine name Yahweh.

Grammar: "ישעיהו" is a proper noun in Hebrew and is the name of the biblical prophet Isaiah. In Hebrew, proper nouns are usually not declined or modified for gender or number.

Description: Isaiah is one of the major prophets in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament). The Book of Isaiah contains prophecies and messages of judgment, comfort, and hope. Isaiah is known for his poetic style and his messages of repentance, faith, and the coming of the Messiah. The book is divided into two main sections: chapters 1-39 focus on the judgment and exile of Israel, while chapters 40-66 offer hope and restoration. Isaiah's prophecies have had a significant impact on Jewish and Christian theology and have been interpreted in various ways throughout history.
