Etymology: The word "ישימו" (Yeshimu) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root ש.מ.ע (sh.m.a), which means "to put" or "to place."

Grammar: "ישימו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "ישימו" can be translated as "they will put" or "they will place" in English. It describes an action that will be performed by a third person plural subject in the future.
