Etymology: The word "ישמע" (Yishma) is derived from the Hebrew root שמע (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen". 

Grammar: "ישמע" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the future tense. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form. 

Description: The word "ישמע" is used to express the action of hearing or listening in the future. It can also be used metaphorically to convey the idea of paying attention or taking something into consideration.
