Etymology: The word "ישקלו" (Yishkalo) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root שקל (sh.q.l), which means "to weigh" or "to balance."

Grammar: "ישקלו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and the tense of the sentence. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "ישקלו" is used to describe an action that will be performed by a third person plural subject in the future. It can be translated as "they will weigh" or "they will balance" depending on the context.
