Etymology: The word "ישקרו" (Yishkoru) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root word "שקר" (sheker), which means "lie" or "falsehood".

Grammar: "ישקרו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb "שקר" (sheker), which means "to lie" or "to deceive". In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to tense, person, and number.

Description: "ישקרו" is used to describe an action that will be performed in the future by a group of people. It indicates that they will lie or deceive at some point in time that is yet to come.
