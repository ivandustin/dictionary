Etymology: The word "ישביעו" (Yeshbi'u) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root שבע (sh-b-a), which means "to be satisfied" or "to be full." 

Grammar: "ישביעו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb, which means "they will be satisfied" or "they will be full." In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense of the sentence.

Description: "ישביעו" is used to express the future action of being satisfied or full. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a physical state of being full after eating or a metaphorical state of being content or fulfilled.
