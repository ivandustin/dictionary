Etymology: The word "ישאון" (Yeshuon) is a Hebrew noun derived from the root word "ישע" (yasha), which means "to save" or "to deliver." It is related to the Hebrew name "ישוע" (Yeshua), which is the equivalent of the name Jesus in English.

Grammar: "ישאון" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. In the plural form, it becomes "ישאונים" (yeshuonim).

Description: "ישאון" refers to salvation, deliverance, or rescue. It is often used in religious or spiritual contexts to describe the act of being saved or delivered from danger, sin, or suffering. The word can also be used metaphorically to express a sense of relief, liberation, or redemption.
