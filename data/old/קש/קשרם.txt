Grammar: קֶשֶׁר is a noun in Hebrew and is used to refer to a knot or a bond. It is a masculine singular noun.

Description: קֶשֶׁר is a term used to describe the act of tying or binding something together. It can refer to a physical knot, such as tying a shoelace or a rope, or it can be used metaphorically to describe a connection or bond between people or things.
