Etymology: The word "קֶשֶׁרִים" (kesharim) is derived from the Hebrew root קֶשֶׁר (k-sh-r), which means "to tie" or "to bind." 

Grammar: קֶשֶׁרִים is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (-im) to the singular form קֶשֶׁר (keshar). 

Description: קֶשֶׁרִים refers to "knots" in Hebrew. It can be used to describe physical knots, such as those used in tying ropes or cords, as well as metaphorical knots, such as complex or difficult situations.
