Etymology: The word "עַמָּר" (ammar) comes from the Hebrew root ע־מ־ר (ayin-mem-resh), which means "to say" or "to speak."

Grammar: "עַמָּר" (ammar) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense, third person, singular form. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and forms to match different subjects and contexts.

Description: "עַמָּר" (ammar) means "he said" or "he spoke" in Hebrew. It is used to describe an action of speaking or expressing words in the past tense.
