Etymology: The word "עמיתי" (amiti) is derived from the Hebrew root ע-מ-ת (ayin-mem-tet), which means "to be with" or "to accompany." It is formed by adding the suffix י (yod) to the root, which indicates possession or belonging.

Grammar: "עמיתי" (amiti) is a noun in Hebrew and is used to refer to a companion, friend, or someone who is with you. It is a masculine singular form, and its plural form is "עמיתים" (amitim).

Description: The word "עמיתי" (amiti) carries a sense of closeness and camaraderie. It is often used to describe a person who shares similar interests, values, or goals and is someone you can rely on and trust.
