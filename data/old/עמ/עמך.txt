Etymology: The word "עַמְדָּה" (amda) comes from the Hebrew root ע.מ.ד (ayin.mem.dalet), which means "to stand" or "to take a stand."

Grammar: "עַמְדָּה" (amda) is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is used to describe a standing position or a stance.

Description: "עַמְדָּה" (amda) refers to the act of standing or taking a stand. It can be used in a literal sense to describe a physical position of standing, or in a figurative sense to describe a firm or resolute position on a particular issue or belief.
