Etymology: The word "עַמְלִים" (amalim) is derived from the Hebrew root עמל (amal), which means "to labor" or "to toil". 

Grammar: "עַמְלִים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root עמל (amal). The plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of labor or toil.

Description: "עַמְלִים" refers to labors or toils. It can be used to describe physical or mental efforts, hard work, or exertion. The word can also carry a connotation of hardship or difficulty associated with the labor or toil.
