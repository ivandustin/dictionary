Etymology: The word "עמוס" (Amos) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Hebrew name עָמוֹס (Amos), which means "burden" or "burden-bearer".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "עמוס" (Amos) is a masculine proper noun. It is typically used as a given name for boys.

Description: Amos is a biblical name that appears in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament). Amos was a prophet who lived during the 8th century BCE. The Book of Amos, attributed to him, contains a collection of his prophecies and teachings. Amos is known for his messages of social justice and his criticism of the wealthy and powerful. His prophecies often focused on the impending judgment and punishment of Israel for its sins.
