Etymology: The word "עמשים" (Amashim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root ע.מ.ש (Ayin.Mem.Shin), which means "to do" or "to make."

Grammar: "עמשים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is the masculine plural form of the word "עמש" (Amash), which means "worker" or "laborer."

Description: "עמשים" refers to a group of workers or laborers. It can be used to describe people who are engaged in physical or manual work, such as construction workers, factory workers, or agricultural laborers.
