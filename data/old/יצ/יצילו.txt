Etymology: The word "יצילו" (pronounced "yatzilu") is a Hebrew verb in the past tense, derived from the root י.צ.ל (y.t.z.l), which means "to save" or "to rescue."

Grammar: "יצילו" is the third person plural form of the verb יציל (yatzil), which is conjugated in the past tense. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense of the sentence. The suffix "ו" (u) indicates that the subject of the sentence is plural.

Description: The word "יצילו" is used to describe an action of saving or rescuing that was performed by a group of people or entities. It can be used in various contexts, such as saving someone from danger, rescuing someone from a difficult situation, or delivering someone from harm.
