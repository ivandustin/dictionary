Etymology: The word "עלהם" (pronounced "al-hem") is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "על" (al) meaning "on" or "upon" and "הם" (hem) meaning "them". 

Grammar: "עלהם" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "על" (al) indicates the relationship of being "on" or "upon", and "הם" (hem) is the plural pronoun meaning "them". Together, "עלהם" is used to express the idea of something being on or upon a group of people or objects.

Description: "עלהם" is commonly used in Hebrew to indicate possession or responsibility. It can be translated as "on them" or "upon them" in English. It is often used to describe a situation where something is the responsibility or burden of a specific group of people.
