Etymology: The word "עִלִּיָּה" (Aliyah) comes from the Hebrew root ע־ל־ה (Ayin-Lamed-Hei), which means "to go up" or "to ascend." It is derived from the verb עָלָה (Alah), which has the same meaning.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "עִלִּיָּה" (Aliyah) is a feminine noun. It is usually used with the definite article "הַ" (Ha) to form "הַעִלִּיָּה" (HaAliyah), which means "the Aliyah." It can also be used in its basic form without the definite article.

Description: Aliyah is a term used in Jewish culture to refer to the act of immigrating to Israel. It specifically refers to the immigration of Jews from the diaspora (Jewish communities outside of Israel) to the land of Israel. Aliyah is considered a significant event in Jewish history and is often associated with the Zionist movement, which aimed to establish a Jewish homeland in Israel. The concept of Aliyah has deep historical and religious roots and holds great importance in Jewish identity and tradition.
