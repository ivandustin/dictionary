Etymology: The word "עלכם" (pronounced "al-khem") is a Hebrew word that is derived from the root ע-ל-כ (ayin-lamed-kaf). It is a combination of the preposition עַל (al), meaning "on" or "upon," and the pronoun כֶּם (khem), meaning "you" in the plural form.

Grammar: In Hebrew, עלכם is a second-person plural pronoun that is used to address a group of people. It is commonly used in formal or respectful contexts. It can be translated as "upon you" or "on you all" in English.

Description: עלכם is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts, such as blessings or prayers. It is a way of expressing good wishes or invoking blessings upon a group of people. It can also be used to convey a sense of responsibility or obligation, as in the phrase "עלכם הוא" (al-khem hu), meaning "it is upon you" or "it is your responsibility."
