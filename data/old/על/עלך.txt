Etymology: The word "עלך" (pronounced "alakh" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root ע-ל-ך (ayin-lamed-kaf), which means "to you" or "upon you". 

Grammar: "עלך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition עַל (al) meaning "on" or "upon", and the second person singular pronoun ךָ (kha) meaning "you". It is used to indicate possession or responsibility, similar to the English phrase "on you" or "your responsibility".

Description: The word "עלך" is commonly used in Hebrew to express the idea of something being someone's responsibility or under their control. It can also be used to indicate that something is directed towards a specific person.
