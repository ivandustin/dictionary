Etymology: The word "משה" (Moses) comes from the Hebrew root "מָשָׁה" (mashah), which means "to draw out" or "to pull out." It is derived from the story of Moses being drawn out of the Nile River by Pharaoh's daughter in the Bible.

Grammar: "משה" is a masculine proper noun in Hebrew. It is typically used as a given name for boys.

Description: "משה" is a significant name in Jewish and biblical history. Moses is considered one of the most important figures in Judaism, known for leading the Israelites out of slavery in Egypt and receiving the Ten Commandments from God on Mount Sinai. He is also recognized as a prophet and the author of the Torah, the first five books of the Hebrew Bible. The name "משה" is still commonly used today as a given name in Jewish communities.
