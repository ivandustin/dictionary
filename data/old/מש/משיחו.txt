Etymology: The word "משיחו" (Mashiach) comes from the Hebrew root "משח" (mashach), which means "to anoint." In Jewish tradition, the term refers to the anointed one, the expected savior or messiah.

Grammar: "משיחו" is a Hebrew noun that means "his anointed one" or "his messiah." It is formed by combining the root "משח" (mashach) with the possessive suffix "ו" (his).

Description: "משיחו" is a term used in Jewish religious texts to refer to the expected messiah, a future leader who will bring redemption and peace to the world. The concept of the messiah holds significant importance in Jewish theology and has been interpreted in various ways throughout history.
