Etymology: The word "מַשְׂמִיעַ" (mashmi'a) is derived from the Hebrew root שמע (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix מַ (ma) indicates the causative form, so מַשְׂמִיעַ literally means "causing to hear" or "making someone listen".

Grammar: In Hebrew, מַשְׂמִיעַ is a verb in the present tense, active voice, and masculine singular form. It can be conjugated to match different subjects, tenses, and genders.

Description: מַשְׂמִיעַ is used to describe the action of making someone hear or listen to something. It implies the act of transmitting sound or information to another person's ears. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing the act of playing music, broadcasting a message, or simply getting someone's attention.
