Etymology: The word "מְשִׁיחִי" (M'shichi) comes from the Hebrew root שׁ.י.ח (sh.y.ch), which means "to anoint" or "to smear with oil." In Jewish tradition, the term refers to the anointed one, the Messiah.

Grammar: "מְשִׁיחִי" is an adjective in Hebrew. It is derived from the noun "מָשִׁיחַ" (Mashiach), which means "Messiah." The suffix "-י" (i) indicates possession or attribution, making it mean "my Messiah" or "of the Messiah."

Description: "מְשִׁיחִי" is used to describe something or someone that is related to or associated with the Messiah. It can be used to express a personal connection or devotion to the Messiah in Jewish religious context.
