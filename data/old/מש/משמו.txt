Etymology: The word "משמו" (mashmo) is a Hebrew word that means "from his name" or "from its name". It is derived from the root שמ"ע (sh-m-a), which means "name".

Grammar: "משמו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition "מ" (m), meaning "from", and the possessive pronoun "שמו" (shmo), meaning "his name" or "its name". It is used to indicate the source or origin of something.

Description: The phrase "משמו" is commonly used in Hebrew to attribute something to a specific person or thing. It is often used in legal or formal contexts to indicate the source of information or the authority behind a statement.
