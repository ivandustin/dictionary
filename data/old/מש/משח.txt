Etymology: The word "משח" (mashach) comes from the Hebrew root שׁ.ח.ה (sh.ch.h), which means "to anoint" or "to smear". It is derived from the ancient practice of anointing with oil as a sign of consecration or appointment to a special role or position.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "משח" (mashach) is a verb in the Qal stem, which is the basic active stem. It is conjugated in different tenses, moods, and persons to indicate the subject, object, and time of the action. The word can also be used as a noun, referring to the act of anointing or the anointing oil itself.

Description: "משח" (mashach) is a significant word in Hebrew, often associated with religious and ceremonial practices. It is used in the Bible to describe the anointing of kings, priests, and prophets, symbolizing their divine appointment and authority.
