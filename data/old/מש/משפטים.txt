Etymology: The word "מִשְׁפָּטִים" (mishpatim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root שפט (sh-p-t), which means "to judge" or "to govern." It is plural in form, indicating multiple judgments or laws.

Grammar: "מִשְׁפָּטִים" (mishpatim) is a noun in the masculine plural form. In Hebrew, nouns have gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural). The plural form is used to refer to a collection of laws or judgments.

Description: "מִשְׁפָּטִים" (mishpatim) is a term used in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament) to refer to a specific section of laws and commandments given by God to the Israelites after the Ten Commandments. These laws cover various aspects of civil and criminal justice, social ethics, and religious practices. They provide guidelines for resolving disputes, establishing fair treatment, and maintaining a just society. The Mishpatim are considered an important part of Jewish law and serve as a foundation for legal and ethical principles in Judaism.
