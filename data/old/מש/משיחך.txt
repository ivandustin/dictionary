Etymology: The word "מְשִׁיחַ" (Mashiach) comes from the Hebrew root "מָשַׁח" (mashach), which means "to anoint." In Jewish tradition, the term refers to the anointed one, the expected savior or deliverer.

Grammar: "מְשִׁיחַ" (Mashiach) is a noun in Hebrew. It is masculine and singular. It can also be used as an adjective to describe someone or something that is anointed or chosen.

Description: In Jewish belief, the term "מְשִׁיחַ" (Mashiach) refers to the Messiah, a future Jewish king from the Davidic line who will be anointed with holy anointing oil. The Messiah is expected to bring peace, redemption, and the ultimate fulfillment of God's plan for humanity. The concept of the Messiah is central to Jewish eschatology and is also significant in Christianity.
