Etymology: The word "נתון" (pronounced "netun") comes from the Hebrew root נ.ת.נ (n.t.n), which means "to give" or "to place". 

Grammar: "נתון" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the singular and plural forms. In the plural form, it becomes "נתונים" (netunim). 

Description: In Hebrew, "נתון" is used to describe something that is given, placed, or put in a certain state or condition. It can also refer to data or information that is given or provided. Additionally, "נתון" can be used to describe a person who is determined, resolute, or steadfast.
