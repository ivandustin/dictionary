Etymology: The word "נְתוּשׁ" (Netush) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ת.שׁ (N.T.Sh), which means "to uproot" or "to pluck out." 

Grammar: נְתוּשׁ is a noun in the singular masculine form. It is derived from the verb נָתַשׁ (natash), which means "to uproot" or "to pluck out." 

Description: נְתוּשׁ refers to something that has been uprooted or plucked out. It can be used metaphorically to describe a person or thing that has been removed or taken away from its original place or context.
