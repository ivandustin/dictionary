Etymology: The word "נְטִיה" (Neti'ah) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ט.ה (N.T.H), which means "to lean" or "to incline."

Grammar: In Hebrew, נְטִיה is a feminine noun. It is used to describe the act of leaning or inclining.

Description: נְטִיה refers to the action of leaning or inclining towards something. It can be used both in a physical sense, such as leaning on a wall, and in a metaphorical sense, such as leaning towards a particular opinion or belief.
