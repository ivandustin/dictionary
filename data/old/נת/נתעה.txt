Etymology: The word "נְתַעֲה" (neta'ah) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ת.ע (n.t.a), which means "to wander" or "to stray." 

Grammar: נְתַעֲה is a verb in the Qal stem, which is the basic form of the verb. It is conjugated in the present tense, third person, singular form. 

Description: נְתַעֲה means "he/she wanders" or "he/she strays" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe someone who deviates from the right path or goes astray.
