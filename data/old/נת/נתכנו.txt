Etymology: The word "נתקנו" (Netkenu) is a Hebrew verb in the past tense, derived from the root תקן (T.K.N), which means "to fix" or "to repair".

Grammar: נתקנו is the third person plural form of the verb תקן in the past tense. It is used to describe an action that was done by a group of people or objects in the past.

Description: נתקנו is used to indicate that something has been fixed or repaired. It implies that an action has been taken to restore something to its proper or functional state.
