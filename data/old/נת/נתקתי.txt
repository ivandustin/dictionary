Grammar: נְתַקְתִּי (Netakhti) is the past tense form of the verb נִתְקוֹת (Nitkot), which means "to tear" or "to rip." The suffix תִּי (ti) indicates the first person singular form of the past tense.

Description: נְתַקְתִּי (Netakhti) is used to express an action of tearing or ripping that was performed by the speaker in the past. It is commonly used in everyday conversation and written texts in Hebrew.
