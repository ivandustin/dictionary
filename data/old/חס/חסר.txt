Etymology: The word "חסר" (chaser) comes from the Hebrew root חסר (ch-s-r), which means "to lack" or "to be missing."

Grammar: In Hebrew, חסר is an adjective that means "missing" or "lacking." It is used to describe something that is not present or is deficient in some way.

Description: חסר can be used to describe various things that are missing or lacking. For example, it can be used to describe a missing item, a deficiency in a person's abilities or qualities, or a lack of something in a particular situation.
