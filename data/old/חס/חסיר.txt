Etymology: The word "חסיר" (chaser) comes from the Hebrew root חסר, which means "to lack" or "to be missing."

Grammar: In Hebrew grammar, "חסיר" is an adjective that means "missing" or "lacking." It is used to describe something that is not present or is deficient in some way.

Description: "חסיר" is used to describe something that is absent or deficient. It can refer to a physical object that is missing or to a quality or attribute that is lacking in a person or thing. For example, if a book is missing a page, you can say "הספר חסר עמוד" (ha-sefer chaser amud). Similarly, if someone lacks patience, you can say "הוא חסר סבלנות" (hu chaser savlanut).
