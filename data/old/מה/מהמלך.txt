Etymology: The word "מהמלך" (pronounced "mah-melech" in Hebrew) is a combination of two Hebrew words: "מה" (mah) meaning "what" and "מלך" (melech) meaning "king". 

Grammar: "מהמלך" is a noun phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the interrogative pronoun "מה" (mah) meaning "what" and the noun "מלך" (melech) meaning "king". The word order is subject-verb, with "מה" functioning as the subject and "מלך" as the verb.

Description: "מהמלך" is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "what king" in English. It is used to ask a question about the identity or characteristics of a king.
