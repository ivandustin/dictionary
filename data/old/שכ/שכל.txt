Etymology: The word "שְׁכֵל" (shakel) comes from the Hebrew root שׁ-כ-ל (sh-k-l), which means "to consider" or "to understand."

Grammar: "שְׁכֵל" is a noun in Hebrew, and it is used to describe someone who is intelligent, wise, or has good judgment.

Description: In Hebrew, "שְׁכֵל" is often used to refer to someone who possesses intellectual abilities and is capable of making sound decisions. It is a positive attribute and is highly valued in Jewish culture.
