Etymology: The word "שכול" (shakol) comes from the Hebrew root שכל (sh.k.l), which means "to weigh" or "to consider." It is derived from the noun שקל (shekel), which was a unit of weight in ancient times.

Grammar: "שכול" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is used to describe a state of mourning or grief, specifically for the loss of a close relative. It is often used in the phrase "בשכול" (b'shakol), which means "in mourning."

Description: "שכול" refers to the period of mourning observed by Jewish individuals after the death of a close family member. During this time, mourners may refrain from certain activities, such as attending celebrations or shaving. The length of the mourning period varies depending on the relationship to the deceased.
