Etymology: The word "שכבר" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of two words: "שכב" (shakhav) meaning "to lie down" and "ר" (resh) which is a suffix indicating past tense. Therefore, "שכבר" can be translated as "already lay down" or "already slept".

Grammar: "שכבר" is an adverb in Hebrew. It is used to express that something has already happened or been done in the past. It is often used to convey surprise or disbelief that something has happened sooner than expected.

Description: "שכבר" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can be used to express surprise or disbelief, as mentioned earlier. For example, if someone says "אני שכבר עשיתי את זה" (ani shakhavr asiti et ze), it means "I already did it!" with a sense of surprise or frustration. It can also be used to indicate that something has already happened before a certain point in time. For example, "שכבר הגעת?" (shakhavr higata) means "Have you already arrived?" or "Did you already get here?".

Overall, "שכבר" is a useful word in Hebrew for expressing past actions or events that have already taken place.
