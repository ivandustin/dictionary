Etymology: The word "יפאר" (pronounced "yafar") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root פאר, which means "to glorify" or "to adorn."

- Singular:
  - 1st person: אֲנִי מְפַאֵר (ani m'fa'er) - I glorify
  - 2nd person: אַתָּה מְפַאֵר (atah m'fa'er) - You (masculine) glorify
  - 3rd person: הוּא/הִיא מְפַאֵר (hu/hi m'fa'er) - He/She glorifies
- Plural:
  - 1st person: אֲנַחְנוּ מְפַאְרִים (anachnu m'fa'rim) - We glorify
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם מְפַאֲרִים (atem m'fa'rim) - You (masculine plural) glorify
  - 3rd person: הֵם/הֵן מְפַאֲרִים (hem/hen m'fa'rim) - They glorify

Description: The word "יפאר" means "to glorify" or "to adorn" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the act of praising or honoring someone or something. It can also refer to the act of beautifying or enhancing something.
