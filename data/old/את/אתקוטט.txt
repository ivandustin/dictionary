Etymology: The word "אתקוטט" comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ק.ו.ט (k.w.t), which means "to argue" or "to dispute."

- אני אתקוטט (ani etkotet) - I argue
- אתה אתקוטט (ata etkotet) - You argue (masculine singular)
- את אתקוטט (at etkotet) - You argue (feminine singular)
- הוא/היא אתקוטט (hu/hi etkotet) - He/She argues
- אנחנו אתקוטטים (anachnu etkotetim) - We argue
- אתם אתקוטטים (atem etkotetim) - You argue (masculine plural)
- אתן אתקוטטות (aten etkotetot) - You argue (feminine plural)
- הם/הן אתקוטטים/אתקוטטות (hem/hen etkotetim/etkotetot) - They argue

Description: "אתקוטט" means "to argue" or "to dispute" in Hebrew. It refers to engaging in a verbal or written disagreement or debate with someone, expressing differing opinions or points of view.
