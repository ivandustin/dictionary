Etymology: The word "אתחבר" (et'chaber) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root ח-ב-ר (ch-b-r), which means "to connect" or "to join."

- אני מתחבר (ani mit'chaber) - I connect
- אתה מתחבר (atah mit'chaber) - You (masculine singular) connect
- את מתחברת (at mit'chaberet) - You (feminine singular) connect
- הוא מתחבר (hu mit'chaber) - He connects
- היא מתחברת (hi mit'chaberet) - She connects
- אנחנו מתחברים (anachnu mit'chaberim) - We connect
- אתם מתחברים (atem mit'chaberim) - You (masculine plural) connect
- אתן מתחברות (aten mit'chaberot) - You (feminine plural) connect
- הם מתחברים (hem mit'chaberim) - They (masculine) connect
- הן מתחברות (hen mit'chaberot) - They (feminine) connect

Description: אתחבר means "to connect" or "to join" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of bringing together separate entities or individuals into a unified whole. It can be used in various contexts, such as connecting people, ideas, or objects.
