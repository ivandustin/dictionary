Etymology: The word "אתפאר" (etpar) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root פאר (par), which means "to boast" or "to glorify." The prefix את (et) is a preposition that can indicate the direct object of a verb.

Grammar: "אתפאר" (etpar) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני אתפאר (ani etpar) - I boast
- אתה אתפאר (ata etpar) - You (singular, masculine) boast
- את אתפארת (at etpart) - You (singular, feminine) boast
- הוא/היא אתפאר (hu/hi etpar) - He/She boasts
- אנחנו אתפארים (anachnu etparim) - We boast
- אתם אתפארים (atem etparim) - You (plural) boast
- הם/הן אתפארים (hem/hen etparim) - They boast

Description: The word "אתפאר" (etpar) is used to express the act of boasting or glorifying oneself. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing someone's pride or self-promotion.
