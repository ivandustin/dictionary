Etymology: The word "אתמול" (etmol) is derived from the Hebrew root א.מ.ל (a.m.l), which means "yesterday". 

Grammar: אתמול is an adverb in Hebrew, used to describe an action or event that occurred in the past, specifically the day before the current day. It is often used in conjunction with the verb "היה" (hayah), meaning "was" or "were", to indicate that something happened in the past.

Description: אתמול is a commonly used word in Hebrew to refer to the previous day. It is used in everyday conversations and is an essential part of the Hebrew language.
