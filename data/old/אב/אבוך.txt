Etymology: The word "אבוך" (pronounced "avukh") is a Hebrew word that means "your father." It is derived from the Hebrew root א-ב-ה (aleph-bet-heh), which means "father."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אבוך" is a possessive form of the word "אב" (av), which means "father." The suffix "-ך" (-kh) indicates the second person singular possessive form, meaning "your." Therefore, "אבוך" translates to "your father."

Description: "אבוך" is a term used to refer to someone's father in Hebrew. It is commonly used in informal conversations and is a respectful way to address or talk about someone's father.
