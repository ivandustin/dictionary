Etymology: The word "אבתי" (pronounced "avti") is a Hebrew word that means "my father." It is derived from the Hebrew root א-ב-ה (aleph-bet-heh), which means "father."

Grammar: "אבתי" is a possessive form of the word "אב" (av), which means "father." The suffix "-י" (yod) indicates possession, making it mean "my father."

Description: "אבתי" is a term used to refer to one's own father in Hebrew. It is a personal and intimate way to address or talk about one's father.
