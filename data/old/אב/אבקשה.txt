Etymology: The word "אבקשה" (pronounced "avakesha") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root בקש (bakash), which means "to seek" or "to request." The prefix א (a) in front of the root indicates the first person singular form of the verb, making it "I will seek" or "I will request."

Grammar: "אבקשה" is a verb in the future tense, first person singular form. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated based on the subject and the tense of the sentence. The future tense is used to express actions that will happen in the future. The first person singular form indicates that the subject of the sentence is "I."
