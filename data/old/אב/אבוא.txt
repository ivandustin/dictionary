Etymology: The word "אבוא" (pronounced "avoa") is a Hebrew verb that means "I will come" or "I will enter." It is derived from the root א-ב-ו, which conveys the idea of coming or entering.

Grammar: "אבוא" is a first-person singular future tense verb in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the prefix א- (meaning "I") to the root ב-ו-א. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future.

Description: "אבוא" is a verb used to express the act of coming or entering. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically entering a place or figuratively entering a new phase or situation. In Hebrew grammar, verbs are conjugated based on the subject, tense, and other factors.
