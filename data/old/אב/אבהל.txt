Etymology: The word "אבהל" (pronounced "avhal" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root א-ב-ה-ל, which means "to be confused" or "to be bewildered."

Grammar: "אבהל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אֲבַהֵל (I confuse)
- תַּבְהֵל (you confuse)
- יַבְהֵל (he/she confuses)
- נַבְהֵל (we confuse)
- תַּבְהֵלוּ (you confuse)
- יַבְהֵלוּ (they confuse)

Description: The word "אבהל" is used to describe a state of confusion, bewilderment, or being flustered. It can refer to a mental or emotional state of being overwhelmed or disoriented.
