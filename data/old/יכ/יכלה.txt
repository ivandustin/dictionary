Etymology: The word "יכלה" (Yekhaleh) is derived from the Hebrew root כ-ל-ה (k-l-h), which means "to be able" or "to have the power". 

Grammar: יכלה is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the past tense, third person, singular form. It is used to express the ability or capability of someone or something to perform an action.

Description: יכלה is often translated as "he/she/it was able" or "he/she/it could" in English. It is used to indicate that someone or something had the capacity or capability to do something in the past.
