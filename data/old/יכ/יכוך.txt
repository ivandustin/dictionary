Etymology: The word "יכול" (pronounced "yachol") comes from the Hebrew root י-כ-ל (y-k-l), which means "to be able" or "to have the power." 

Grammar: "יכול" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the masculine singular form. It is in the present tense, indicating the ability or capability of the subject to perform an action. 

Description: "יכול" means "can" or "able" in English. It is used to express the ability or possibility of doing something. For example, "אני יכול לעזור" (pronounced "ani yachol la'azor") means "I can help."
