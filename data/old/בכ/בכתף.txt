Etymology: The word "בכתף" (baktav) is derived from the Hebrew root "כתף" (k-t-f), which means "shoulder". 

Grammar: "בכתף" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition "ב" (b) meaning "in" or "on", and the noun "כתף" (katef) meaning "shoulder". It is used to indicate the location or position of something on or in the shoulder.

Description: "בכתף" is used to describe something that is located or positioned on the shoulder. It can refer to a physical object or a metaphorical concept. For example, "התינוק ישן בכתף של אמו" (hatinok yashen baktav shel imo) means "the baby is sleeping on his mother's shoulder".
