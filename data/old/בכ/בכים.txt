Etymology: The word "בְּכִים" (bekhim) is derived from the Hebrew root "בָּכָה" (bakha), which means "to weep" or "to cry."

Grammar: "בְּכִים" (bekhim) is a noun in the plural form. It is used to describe a group of people who are weeping or crying.

Description: "בְּכִים" (bekhim) refers to a collective group of individuals who are engaged in the act of weeping or crying. It can be used to describe a scene of mourning, sadness, or emotional distress.
