Etymology: The word "בכפך" (b'kafcha) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in your palm." It is derived from the Hebrew words "ב" (b') meaning "in" or "at," "כף" (kaf) meaning "palm," and "ך" (cha) indicating the possessive form of "you."

Grammar: "בכפך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition "ב" (b') meaning "in" or "at," and the noun "כף" (kaf) meaning "palm." The possessive form of "you" is indicated by the final letter "ך" (cha).

Description: The phrase "בכפך" is used to express the idea of something being in someone's palm or under their control. It can be used both literally and figuratively. In a literal sense, it refers to something physically held in the palm of one's hand. Figuratively, it can imply having control or power over something or someone.
