Etymology: The word "תפלה" (tefillah) comes from the Hebrew root פ.ל.ל (p.l.l), which means "to judge" or "to pray." It is derived from the noun פֶּלֶל (pelel), which means "judge" or "intercessor."

Grammar: "תפלה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the singular and plural forms. In the plural form, it becomes "תפילות" (tefillot).

Description: "תפלה" is the Hebrew word for "prayer." It refers to the act of communicating with God or a higher power, expressing gratitude, seeking guidance, or making requests. In Jewish tradition, prayer plays a central role in religious observance and is performed multiple times a day. It is considered a way to connect with the divine and to express one's faith and devotion.
