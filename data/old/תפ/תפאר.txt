Etymology: The word "תפאר" (pronounced "ta'ar") comes from the Hebrew root פאר, which means "to glorify" or "to adorn." It is derived from the noun תפארת (pronounced "tiferet"), which means "beauty" or "glory."

Grammar: "תפאר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני מְתַפֵּאֵר (ani metape'er) - I am glorifying
- אתה מְתַפֵּאֵר (ata metape'er) - You (masculine singular) are glorifying
- את מְתַפֶּאֶרֶת (at metape'eret) - You (feminine singular) are glorifying
- הוא/היא מְתַפֵּאֵר (hu/hi metape'er) - He/She is glorifying
- אנחנו מְתַפְּאִירִים (anachnu metap'irim) - We are glorifying
- אתם מְתַפְּאִירִים (atem metap'irim) - You (masculine plural) are glorifying
- אתן מְתַפְּאִירוֹת (atan metap'irot) - You (feminine plural) are glorifying
- הם/הן מְתַפְּאִירִים (hem/hen metap'irim) - They are glorifying

Description: The word "תפאר" is used to describe the act of glorifying or adorning something or someone. It can refer to enhancing the beauty or splendor of a person, object, or situation.
