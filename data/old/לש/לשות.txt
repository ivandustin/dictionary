Grammar: "לשון" (leshon) is a noun in Hebrew and is used to refer to language or tongue. It is typically used to describe a specific language or the ability to speak and understand languages.

Description: "לשון" (leshon) is a fundamental concept in Hebrew, representing the power of communication and expression through language. It encompasses both the physical organ of speech (the tongue) and the abstract concept of language itself. In Jewish tradition, the importance of "לשון" (leshon) is emphasized, as it is believed to have the power to create and destroy, to heal and to harm.
