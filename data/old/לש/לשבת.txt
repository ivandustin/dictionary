Etymology: The word "לשבת" (leshavet) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root שבת (shavat), which means "to rest" or "to cease." It is derived from the Hebrew noun שבת (Shabbat), which refers to the Jewish day of rest and is observed from Friday evening to Saturday evening.

Grammar: "לשבת" (leshavet) is an infinitive verb form in Hebrew. It is composed of the preposition ל (le), which means "to," and the verb שבת (shavat), which means "to rest." When conjugated, it can be used in different tenses, such as past, present, or future, to indicate the action of resting or ceasing.

Description: The word "לשבת" (leshavet) is often used in the context of observing the Jewish Sabbath. It can refer to the act of resting or ceasing from work and engaging in spiritual and communal activities.
