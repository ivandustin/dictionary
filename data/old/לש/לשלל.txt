Etymology: The word "לשלל" comes from the Hebrew root "ש.ל.ל" which means "to plunder" or "to loot". It is derived from the noun "שלל" which means "spoils" or "booty".

- אֲנִי שׁוֹלֵל (ani sholel) - I plunder
- אַתָּה שׁוֹלֵל (ata sholel) - You (masculine singular) plunder
- אַתְּ שׁוֹלֶלֶת (at sholelet) - You (feminine singular) plunder
- הוּא/הִיא שׁוֹלֵל (hu/hi sholel) - He/She plunders
- אֲנַחְנוּ שׁוֹלְלִים (anachnu shol'lim) - We plunder
- אַתֶּם שׁוֹלְלִים (atem shol'lim) - You (masculine plural) plunder
- אַתֶּן שׁוֹלְלוֹת (aten shol'lot) - You (feminine plural) plunder
- הֵם/הֵן שׁוֹלְלִים (hem/hen shol'lim) - They plunder

Description: "לשלל" means "to plunder" or "to loot" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of taking goods or valuables by force, typically during a war or conflict. The word can also be used metaphorically to describe taking or acquiring something in a forceful or aggressive manner.
