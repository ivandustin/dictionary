Etymology: The word "לשלוט" (leshlot) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root ש-ל-ט (sh-l-t), which means "to rule" or "to govern."

Grammar: "לשלוט" is an infinitive verb form in Hebrew. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני שולט (ani sholeṭ) - I rule
- אתה שולט (atah sholeṭ) - You rule (masculine singular)
- את שולטת (at sholeṭet) - You rule (feminine singular)
- הוא/היא שולט (hu/hi sholeṭ) - He/She rules
- אנחנו שולטים (anachnu sholṭim) - We rule
- אתם/אתן שולטים/שולטות (atem/atn sholṭim/sholṭot) - You rule (masculine plural/feminine plural)
- הם/הן שולטים/שולטות (hem/hen sholṭim/sholṭot) - They rule

Description: "לשלוט" means "to rule" or "to govern" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of having authority or control over something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as ruling a country, governing a company, or having control over a situation.
