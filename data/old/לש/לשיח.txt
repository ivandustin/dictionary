Etymology: The word "לשיח" (leshich) comes from the Hebrew root "ש.י.ח" (sh.y.ch), which means "to converse" or "to talk". 

Grammar: "לשיח" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the infinitive form, which means it is not conjugated to a specific tense, gender, or number. It can be used in various grammatical constructions, such as in the present tense ("אני משיח", "I am talking") or in the future tense ("הוא ישיח", "He will talk").
