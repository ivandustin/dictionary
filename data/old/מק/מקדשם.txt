Etymology: The word "מקדש" (Mikdash) comes from the Hebrew root ק.ד.ש (k.d.sh), which means "to sanctify" or "to set apart." It is derived from the noun קֹדֶשׁ (kodesh), meaning "holiness" or "sacredness."

Grammar: "מקדש" (Mikdash) is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. In the plural form, it becomes "מקדשים" (Mikdashim).

Description: "מקדש" (Mikdash) refers to a sanctuary or a holy place in Judaism. It is most commonly associated with the ancient Jewish temples in Jerusalem, such as the First Temple (built by King Solomon) and the Second Temple (rebuilt after the Babylonian exile). The term can also be used more broadly to describe any place or structure that is dedicated to worship or considered sacred in Judaism.
