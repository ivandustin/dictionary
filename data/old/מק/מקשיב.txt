Etymology: The word "מקשיב" (pronounced "makshiv" in Hebrew) comes from the root ק.ש.ב, which means "to listen" or "to pay attention" in Hebrew. The prefix מ- (pronounced "me-") indicates the causative form, so מקשיב literally means "causing to listen" or "making someone listen."

Grammar: מקשיב is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated based on the subject and tense of the sentence. The present tense form of מקשיב is used when referring to a third person singular subject (he, she, or it) in the present time.

Description: מקשיב is an active verb that describes the action of making someone listen or pay attention. It implies that the subject is actively engaging in the act of listening and trying to understand what is being said.
