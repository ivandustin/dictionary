Etymology: The word "מקטירות" (Maktirot) is derived from the Hebrew root ק.ט.ר (k.t.r), which means "to burn" or "to smoke." It is related to the word "קטורת" (ketoret), which refers to the incense offering in ancient Jewish rituals.

Grammar: "מקטירות" is a plural noun in the feminine form. It is used to describe a group of objects or places where burning or smoking takes place.

Description: "מקטירות" refers to places or objects used for burning or smoking, such as incense burners, smoking rooms, or areas where sacrifices were offered in ancient times. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a place or situation that is filled with smoke or has a strong smell.
