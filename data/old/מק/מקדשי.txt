Etymology: The word "מקדשי" (Mikdashai) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root word "קדש" (kadosh), which means "holy" or "sanctified." The prefix "מ" (m) indicates possession, so "מקדשי" (Mikdashai) can be translated as "my holy" or "my sanctified."

Grammar: "מקדשי" (Mikdashai) is a possessive adjective in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the prefix "מ" (m) to the root word "קדש" (kadosh) and the suffix "י" (ai) to indicate possession. In this case, it means "my holy" or "my sanctified."

Description: "מקדשי" (Mikdashai) is used to describe something that belongs to or is associated with the speaker and is considered holy or sanctified. It can refer to a place, object, or concept that is set apart for religious or sacred purposes.
