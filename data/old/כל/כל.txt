Etymology: The word "כל" (pronounced "kol" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root כ.ל.ל (k.l.l), which means "to contain" or "to include". 

Grammar: "כל" is a Hebrew word that functions as a determiner or pronoun. It is used to express the concept of "all" or "every" in a general sense. It can be used with both singular and plural nouns.

Description: "כל" is a versatile word in Hebrew that is used to indicate inclusiveness or totality. It is commonly used to express the idea of "all" or "every" in various contexts. For example, "כל האנשים" (kol ha'anashim) means "all the people" and "כל הספרים" (kol hasfarim) means "all the books". It can also be used to emphasize the entirety of something, such as "כל היום" (kol hayom) meaning "all day" or "the whole day".
