Grammar: "כְּלוּב" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. In the plural form, it becomes "כְּלָבִים" (kelavim).

Description: "כְּלוּב" refers to a dog in Hebrew. Dogs have been domesticated for thousands of years and are known for their loyalty and companionship. They are often kept as pets or used for various purposes such as guarding, herding, or hunting.
