Etymology: The word "כלמד" (klamad) comes from the Hebrew root כ-ל-מ-ד (k-l-m-d), which means "to learn" or "to study."

Grammar: "כלמד" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני לומד (ani lomed) - I learn
- Singular feminine: אני לומדת (ani lomedet) - I learn
- Plural masculine: אנחנו לומדים (anachnu lomedim) - We learn
- Plural feminine: אנחנו לומדות (anachnu lomedot) - We learn

Description: "כלמד" is used to describe the act of acquiring knowledge or skills through study or instruction. It can refer to formal education or self-learning.
