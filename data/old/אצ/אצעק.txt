Grammar: אצעק is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני אצעק (ani etza'ak) - I cry out
- אתה אצעק (ata etza'ak) - You (singular) cry out
- הוא/היא אצעק (hu/hi etza'ak) - He/She cries out
- אנחנו אצעק (anachnu etza'ak) - We cry out
- אתם אצעקו (atem etza'aku) - You (plural) cry out
- הם/הן אצעקו (hem/hen etza'aku) - They cry out

Description: אצעק is an action verb that describes the act of crying out or shouting loudly. It is often used to express distress, fear, or a call for help.
