Etymology: The word "מאפקם" (ma'apakem) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root פקם (p.k.m), which means "to judge" or "to decide." The prefix מ (ma) indicates a causative form, so מאפקם can be translated as "to cause to judge" or "to cause to decide."

Grammar: In Hebrew, מאפקם is a verb in the causative form, which means it is used to express causing someone to judge or decide. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מאפקם (ani ma'apakem) - I cause to judge/decide
- Plural: אנחנו מאפקמים (anachnu ma'apakemim) - We cause to judge/decide

Description: מאפקם is a Hebrew word that describes the action of causing someone to judge or decide. It can be used in various contexts, such as influencing someone's decision-making process or persuading someone to make a judgment.
