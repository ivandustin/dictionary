Etymology: The word "מאן" (ma'an) is a Hebrew word that means "who" or "whom". It is derived from the Hebrew root אנה (ana), which means "to answer" or "to respond".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מאן" (ma'an) is an interrogative pronoun used to ask questions about people. It is similar to the English word "who" or "whom". It can be used in both singular and plural forms, and can also be used as a relative pronoun.

Description: "מאן" (ma'an) is a versatile word in Hebrew that is commonly used in everyday conversations. It is used to inquire about the identity of a person or a group of people. For example, "מאן הוא?" (ma'an hu?) means "Who is he?" and "מאן הם?" (ma'an hem?) means "Who are they?". It is an essential word in Hebrew for asking questions and gathering information about people.
