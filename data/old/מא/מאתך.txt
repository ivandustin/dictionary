Etymology: The word "מאתך" (me'itcha) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "מ" (me) meaning "from" and "אתך" (itcha) meaning "you". 

Grammar: "מאתך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "מ" (me) indicates the source or origin, and the pronoun "אתך" (itcha) means "you" in the singular form. Together, "מאתך" means "from you" or "by you" depending on the context.

Description: The word "מאתך" is used in Hebrew to express the idea of something coming from or being done by a specific person or entity. It is often used to indicate possession, origin, or agency.
