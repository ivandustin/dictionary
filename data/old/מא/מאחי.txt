Etymology: The word "מאחי" (pronounced "ma'ahi") is a Hebrew word that means "from my brothers." It is derived from the Hebrew root א.ח.י (aleph, chet, yod), which means "brother."

Grammar: "מאחי" is a possessive phrase in Hebrew, composed of the preposition "מ" (from) and the noun "אחי" (my brothers). It is used to indicate possession or origin.
