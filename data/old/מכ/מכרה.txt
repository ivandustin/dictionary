Grammar: "מכרה" (mikra) is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to the act of reading or calling out, particularly in the context of reading from the Torah or other sacred texts.

Description: "מכרה" (mikra) is a term used in Judaism to describe the public reading of the Torah during synagogue services. It is an important ritual that takes place on Shabbat (Sabbath) and holidays. The Torah is divided into weekly portions, and each portion is read aloud in Hebrew by a designated reader. The reading is often accompanied by blessings and prayers. The purpose of the מכרה is to fulfill the commandment of hearing the Torah being read and to engage the community in the study and understanding of the sacred text.
