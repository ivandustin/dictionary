Etymology: The word "מכי" (pronounced "maki") is a Hebrew word that means "from where" or "since when." It is derived from the Hebrew root כ.י.י (k.y.y), which means "to be" or "to exist."

Grammar: "מכי" is an interrogative adverb in Hebrew. It is used to ask questions about the origin, source, or duration of something. It is often used in combination with other words to form longer questions.

Description: "מכי" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is commonly used in everyday conversations to inquire about the beginning or duration of an event, action, or state. For example, one might ask "מכי אתה כאן?" (maki ata kan?), which means "Since when have you been here?" or "מכי אתה מכיר אותו?" (maki ata mekhir oto?), which means "Since when do you know him?"
