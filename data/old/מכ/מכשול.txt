Etymology: The word "מכשול" (makhshol) comes from the Hebrew root כ-ש-ל (k-sh-l), which means "to stumble" or "to trip." It is derived from the noun כִּשְׁלוּל (kishlul), meaning "stumbling" or "obstacle."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מכשול" is a masculine noun. It is usually used in the singular form, but it can also be used in the plural form, "מכשולים" (makhsholim).

Description: "מכשול" refers to an obstacle, hindrance, or difficulty that hinders progress or success. It can be a physical, mental, or emotional barrier that one needs to overcome. The term is often used metaphorically to describe challenges or setbacks in various aspects of life, such as relationships, work, or personal growth.
