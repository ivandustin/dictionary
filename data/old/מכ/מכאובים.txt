Etymology: The word "מקאובים" (Makabim) is derived from the Hebrew word "מקבים" (Makavim), which means "hammers". It refers to the Jewish rebel group known as the Maccabees, who fought against the Seleucid Empire in the 2nd century BCE.

Grammar: "מקאובים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is the plural form of "מקבים" (Makavim).

Description: "מקאובים" is a term used to refer to the Maccabees, a group of Jewish fighters who led a successful rebellion against the Seleucid Empire. They are known for their bravery and determination in defending Jewish religious and cultural practices. The story of the Maccabees is commemorated during the Jewish holiday of Hanukkah.
