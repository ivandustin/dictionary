Etymology: The word "וָעוֹדִי" (pronounced "va'odi") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root עוד (OD), which means "again" or "more." The prefix וְ (VE) means "and." So, וָעוֹדִי literally means "and more."

Grammar: וָעוֹדִי is a masculine singular noun in Hebrew. It is in the construct state, which means it is used to indicate possession or connection to another noun. It can be translated as "and more of" or "and additional."

Description: וָעוֹדִי is used to express the idea of having more of something or adding to what already exists. It is often used in contexts where there is a desire for more or an emphasis on abundance.
