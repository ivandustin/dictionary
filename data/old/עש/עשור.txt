Etymology: The word "עשור" (pronounced asher) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ע-ש-ר, which means "ten" in Hebrew.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "עשור" is a noun that means "decade" or "ten years." It is used to refer to a period of ten years.

Description: "עשור" is a term used to describe a specific period of time consisting of ten years. It is commonly used in various contexts, such as history, economics, and personal milestones. For example, "the 1990s" in Hebrew would be translated as "שנות התשעים" or "עשור התשעים," referring to the decade from 1990 to 1999.
