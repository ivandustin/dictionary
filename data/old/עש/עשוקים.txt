Etymology: The word "עשוקים" (Ashukim) is derived from the Hebrew root ע.ש.ק (Ayin, Shin, Kuf), which means "oppression" or "exploitation." 

Grammar: "עשוקים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It belongs to the masculine gender and is used to refer to a group of people who are oppressed or exploited.

Description: "עשוקים" is a term used in Hebrew to describe individuals or groups who are subjected to oppression or exploitation. It can refer to various forms of mistreatment, such as economic exploitation, social injustice, or abuse of power.
