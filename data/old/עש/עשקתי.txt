Etymology: The word "עשקתי" (pronounced ashekti) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ע-ש-ק (ayin-shin-kuf), which means "to oppress" or "to exploit."

Grammar: "עשקתי" is the first-person singular past tense form of the verb עשק (asak), which means "I oppressed" or "I exploited." It is conjugated in the past tense, indicating that the action of oppressing or exploiting has already occurred.

Description: The word "עשקתי" describes an action of oppressing or exploiting someone or something. It implies the use of power or authority to take advantage of others or to treat them unjustly.
