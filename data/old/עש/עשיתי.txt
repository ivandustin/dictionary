Etymology: The word "עשיתי" (pronounced as "asiti" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root ע.ש.ה (pronounced as "a.s.h") which means "to do" or "to make". The suffix תי (pronounced as "ti") is added to the root to indicate the first person singular past tense form of the verb.

Grammar: "עשיתי" is a verb in the first person singular past tense form in Hebrew. It is used to express an action that was done or made by the speaker in the past.

Description: The word "עשיתי" is commonly used in everyday Hebrew conversations. It is used to talk about actions that the speaker has performed in the past. For example, "עשיתי את השיעורים" (pronounced as "asiti et hashiurim") means "I did the homework".
