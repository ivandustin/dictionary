Etymology: The word "יגד" (pronounced "yagad" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root י-ג-ד (y-g-d), which means "to tell" or "to recount."

Grammar: "יגד" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אֲנִי מְגַד (ani megad) - I tell
- אַתָּה מְגַד (atah megad) - You tell (masculine singular)
- אַתְּ מְגַדֶּה (at megadah) - You tell (feminine singular)
- הוּא מְגַד (hu megad) - He tells
- הִיא מְגַדֶּה (hi megadah) - She tells
- אֲנַחְנוּ מְגַדִּים (anachnu megadim) - We tell
- אַתֶּם מְגַדִּים (atem megadim) - You tell (masculine plural)
- אַתֶּן מְגַדּוֹת (aten megadot) - You tell (feminine plural)
- הֵם מְגַדִּים (hem megadim) - They tell (masculine)
- הֵן מְגַדּוֹת (hen megadot) - They tell (feminine)

Description: The word "יגד" means "to tell" or "to recount" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of conveying information or sharing a story.
