Etymology: The word "יגיד" (pronounced "yagid" in English) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root י-ג-ד (y-g-d). 

Grammar: "יגיד" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the present tense, third person singular form. The verb is conjugated as follows: אני יוגד (ani yoged) - I tell, אתה יוגד (atah yoged) - you tell (masculine singular), את יוגדת (at yogedet) - you tell (feminine singular), הוא/היא יוגד (hu/hi yoged) - he/she tells, אנחנו יוגדים (anachnu yogdim) - we tell, אתם יוגדים (atem yogdim) - you tell (masculine plural), אתן יוגדות (aten yogdot) - you tell (feminine plural), הם/הן יוגדים (hem/hen yogdim) - they tell.

Description: The verb "יגיד" means "to tell" or "to say" in Hebrew. It is used to express the action of conveying information or expressing thoughts or ideas through speech.
