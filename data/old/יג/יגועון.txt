Etymology: The word "יגועון" (Yeguon) is derived from the Hebrew root י-ג-ע (Y-G-A), which means "to tire" or "to become weary". The suffix -ון (-on) is added to form a noun, indicating the state or condition of being tired or weary.

Grammar: "יגועון" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. In the plural form, it becomes "יגועונים" (Yeguonim).

Description: "יגועון" refers to the state of being tired or weary. It can describe physical exhaustion or mental fatigue. This word is often used to express a feeling of being worn out or drained after exertion or prolonged effort.
