Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמֵר" (hashemar) comes from the root שָׁמַר (shamar), which means "to guard" or "to keep". 

Grammar: "הַשְׁמֵר" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is preceded by the definite article "הַ" (ha), which means "the". 

Description: "הַשְׁמֵר" is translated as "the guard" or "the keeper" in English. It refers to someone who is responsible for protecting or watching over something or someone.
