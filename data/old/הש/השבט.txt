Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשָּׁבַת" (HaShabbat) comes from the root שׁ-ב-ת (sh-b-t), which means "to cease" or "to rest." It is derived from the biblical account of the creation, where God rested on the seventh day.

Grammar: "הַשָּׁבַת" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is usually preceded by the definite article "הַ" (ha), which means "the." In Hebrew grammar, nouns have gender and number, and they can be declined to match the grammatical context.

Description: "הַשָּׁבַת" refers to the Jewish Sabbath, which is observed from Friday evening to Saturday evening. It is a day of rest and worship in Judaism, commemorating the biblical account of God resting on the seventh day after creating the world. The Sabbath is considered a holy day and is marked by special prayers, rituals, and restrictions on work and certain activities. It is a time for Jews to gather with family and community, attend synagogue services, and engage in spiritual reflection and study.
