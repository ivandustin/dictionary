Etymology: The Hebrew word "השוערים" (hashoarim) is derived from the root ש-ע-ר (sh-a-r), which means "to guard" or "to watch over." The word is formed by adding the prefix ה- (ha), which means "the," and the plural suffix ים (im), indicating multiple individuals.

Grammar: "השוערים" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is in the plural form, indicating that it refers to multiple individuals. In Hebrew, nouns have gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural), and they can also be declined for case (nominative, accusative, genitive, etc.).

Description: "השוערים" literally translates to "the guards" or "the gatekeepers" in English. In the Hebrew Bible, the term is often used to refer to the Levites who were responsible for guarding the entrances of the Tabernacle or the Temple. They were tasked with maintaining order, ensuring the sanctity of the sacred space, and preventing unauthorized access. The role of the gatekeepers was highly esteemed and considered essential for the proper functioning of the religious rituals and ceremonies.
