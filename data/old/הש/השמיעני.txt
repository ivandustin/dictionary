Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמִיעַנִי" (hashmi'eni) comes from the root שָׁמַע (shama'), which means "to hear" or "to listen." The word is formed by adding the prefix הַ (ha) which means "the" and the suffix נִי (ni) which indicates the first person singular object form.

Grammar: "הַשְׁמִיעַנִי" is a verb in the imperative form. It is conjugated in the second person masculine singular, meaning "you (masculine) make me hear" or "cause me to listen."

Description: "הַשְׁמִיעַנִי" is a request or command to someone to make the speaker hear or listen to something. It implies a desire for the person being addressed to communicate or share information with the speaker.
