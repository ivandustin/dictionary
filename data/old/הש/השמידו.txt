Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמִידוּ" (hashmidu) comes from the root שָׁמַד (shamad), which means "to destroy" or "to annihilate". 

Grammar: הַשְׁמִידוּ is a verb in the imperative form, which is used to give commands or instructions. It is in the plural form, addressing a group of people. 

Description: הַשְׁמִידוּ is a strong and forceful word that means "destroy" or "annihilate". It is often used in a context of warfare or the complete eradication of something or someone.
