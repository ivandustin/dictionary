Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמוּעָה" (hashmu'ah) comes from the root שָׁמַע (shama'), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix הַ (ha) is the definite article "the".

Grammar: "הַשְׁמוּעָה" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is preceded by the definite article הַ (ha), which indicates that it refers to a specific "hearing" or "news".

Description: "הַשְׁמוּעָה" is commonly translated as "the news" or "the report" in English. It refers to information or tidings that are heard or received. It can also refer to a rumor or a piece of information that is spread among people.
