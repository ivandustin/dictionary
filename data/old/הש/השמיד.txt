Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמִיד" (hashmid) comes from the root שָׁמַד (shamad), which means "to destroy" or "to annihilate." 

Grammar: הַשְׁמִיד is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense, masculine singular form. 

Description: הַשְׁמִיד is a strong and powerful word that conveys the idea of complete destruction or annihilation. It is often used in contexts related to war, violence, or the eradication of something or someone.
