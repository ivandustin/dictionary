Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁלֵלִים" (hashlelim) comes from the root שׁ.ל.ל (sh.l.l), which means "to plunder" or "to loot". It is derived from the noun שָׁלָל (shalal), which means "spoils" or "booty".

Grammar: "הַשְׁלֵלִים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (-im) to the root word שָׁלָל (shalal). The definite article הַ (ha) is added at the beginning to indicate "the".

Description: "הַשְׁלֵלִים" refers to the spoils or booty that are taken as plunder during a military conquest or raid. It can include valuable items such as money, jewelry, weapons, or other possessions that are seized from the enemy. The term is often used in the context of ancient warfare or biblical narratives.
