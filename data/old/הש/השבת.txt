Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשֵּׁבֶת" (HaShabbat) comes from the root שׁבת (Sh-B-T), which means "to rest" or "to cease." It is derived from the biblical concept of the Sabbath, a day of rest and worship.

Grammar: "הַשֵּׁבֶת" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is preceded by the definite article "הַ" (Ha), which means "the." In Hebrew, nouns are gendered, and the feminine form is used to refer to the Sabbath day.

Description: "הַשֵּׁבֶת" refers to the Jewish Sabbath, which is observed from Friday evening to Saturday evening. It is a day of rest and worship, as commanded in the Ten Commandments. During this time, Jewish individuals and communities refrain from work, engage in prayer and study, and spend time with family and friends. The Sabbath is considered a holy day and is an important aspect of Jewish religious and cultural life.
