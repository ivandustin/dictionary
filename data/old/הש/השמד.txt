Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמַד" (hashmad) comes from the root שָׁמַד (shamad), which means "to destroy" or "to annihilate." 

Grammar: "הַשְׁמַד" is a noun in the masculine form. It is used to describe the act of destruction or annihilation. 

Description: "הַשְׁמַד" refers to the act of completely destroying or annihilating something or someone. It implies a total eradication or elimination. This word is often used in the context of war, disaster, or divine punishment.
