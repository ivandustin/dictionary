Etymology: The Hebrew word "השוער" (HaSho'er) comes from the root ש-ע-ר (sh-'-r), which means "to guard" or "to watch over." It is derived from the noun שוער (sho'er), which means "gatekeeper" or "watchman."

Grammar: "השוער" (HaSho'er) is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is in the singular form and can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence.

Description: "השוער" (HaSho'er) refers to a gatekeeper or a watchman. In ancient times, a sho'er was responsible for guarding the entrance of a city, a building, or a property. They would control access, monitor who enters or exits, and ensure the security and safety of the premises. The role of a sho'er was crucial in maintaining order and protecting against potential threats.
