Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמִיעֵנוּ" (hashmi'enu) is derived from the root שָׁמַע (shama'), which means "to hear" or "to listen." The word is formed by adding the prefix הַ (ha), which means "the," and the suffix נוּ (nu), which indicates the first person plural possessive form.

Grammar: "הַשְׁמִיעֵנוּ" is a verb in the imperative form. It is conjugated in the plural form and is used when addressing a group of people. The word is often translated as "make us hear" or "let us hear."

Description: "הַשְׁמִיעֵנוּ" is a request or a plea for someone to make us hear or listen. It can be used in various contexts, such as asking someone to share information, give a speech, or convey a message. The word emphasizes the desire to receive and understand what is being communicated.
