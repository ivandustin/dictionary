Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמֵרוּ" (hashme'ru) is derived from the root שָׁמַר (shamar), which means "to keep" or "to guard."

Grammar: הַשְׁמֵרוּ (hashme'ru) is a verb in the imperative form, which is used to give commands or instructions. It is in the plural form, addressing a group of people.

Description: הַשְׁמֵרוּ (hashme'ru) translates to "Keep" or "Guard" in English. It is a command that instructs someone or a group of people to be vigilant, watchful, or cautious. It can be used in various contexts, such as instructing someone to protect something, to be mindful of their actions, or to stay alert.
