Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמֵעָה" (hashme'ah) comes from the root שָׁמַע (shama), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix הַ (ha) is the definite article "the".

Grammar: הַשְׁמֵעָה (hashme'ah) is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is preceded by the definite article הַ (ha), which indicates that it refers to a specific hearing or listening.

Description: הַשְׁמֵעָה (hashme'ah) refers to the act of hearing or listening. It can also be used to describe a report, news, or information that is heard or listened to. In a broader sense, it can refer to the process of receiving and understanding auditory stimuli.
