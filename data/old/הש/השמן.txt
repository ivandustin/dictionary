Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמָן" (hashemen) comes from the root שָׁמַן (shaman), which means "to anoint" or "to smear with oil." 

Grammar: "הַשְׁמָן" is a masculine noun in the singular form. It is preceded by the definite article "הַ" (ha), which means "the" in English. 

Description: "הַשְׁמָן" refers to "the oil" in Hebrew. It is commonly used to describe various types of oil, such as olive oil or cooking oil. In religious contexts, it is often associated with anointing oil used for ceremonial purposes.
