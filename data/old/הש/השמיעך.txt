Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמִיעַ" (hashmi'a) comes from the root שָׁמַע (shama), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The word is derived from the noun שֵׁם (shem), meaning "name". In Hebrew, the act of hearing is often associated with paying attention and giving importance to something or someone.

Grammar: הַשְׁמִיעַ is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the hif'il (causative) stem, which indicates that the subject causes someone else to hear or listen. The word can be conjugated in different tenses, persons, and numbers to match the subject and the context of the sentence.

Description: הַשְׁמִיעַ means "to make someone hear" or "to cause someone to listen" in Hebrew. It implies an action of actively making someone aware of something by transmitting sound or information to their ears. The word can be used in various contexts, such as instructing someone to listen, announcing news, or sharing information.
