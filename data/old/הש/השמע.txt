Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמֵעַ" (hashmea) comes from the root שָׁמַע (shama), which means "to hear" or "to listen." 

Grammar: הַשְׁמֵעַ is a noun in the masculine form. It is in the singular form and can be used as both a subject and an object in a sentence. 

Description: הַשְׁמֵעַ is often translated as "the hearing" or "the act of listening" in English. It refers to the sense or ability to perceive sounds through the ears. In a broader sense, it can also refer to paying attention or giving heed to something.
