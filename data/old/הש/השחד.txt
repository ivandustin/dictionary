Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁחֵד" (HaShachad) comes from the root שׁ.ח.ד (sh.ch.d), which means "to bribe" or "to corrupt." 

Grammar: "הַשְׁחֵד" is a noun in the masculine gender. It is in the definite form (הַ) and singular number. 

Description: "הַשְׁחֵד" refers to the act of bribery or corruption. It is used to describe the act of giving or receiving money, gifts, or favors in order to influence someone's behavior or decision-making. In Hebrew society, bribery is considered a serious offense and is condemned.
