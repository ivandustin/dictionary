Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁמִיחִים" (hashmichim) is derived from the root שָׁמַח (shamach), which means "to rejoice" or "to be glad."

Grammar: "הַשְׁמִיחִים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root word שְׁמִיחִים (shmichim).

Description: "הַשְׁמִיחִים" refers to people who bring joy or happiness. It can be translated as "rejoicers" or "those who make others glad." This word is often used to describe entertainers, performers, or musicians who bring joy to others through their talents.
