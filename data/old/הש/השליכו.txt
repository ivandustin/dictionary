Etymology: The Hebrew word "הַשְׁלִיךְ" (hashlikh) comes from the root שׁ.ל.כ (sh.l.kh), which means "to throw" or "to cast away."

Grammar: הַשְׁלִיךְ (hashlikh) is a verb in the masculine singular form. It is in the past tense and imperative mood.

Description: הַשְׁלִיךְ (hashlikh) means "throw away" or "cast off" in Hebrew. It is used to express the action of discarding or getting rid of something.
