Etymology: The word "כשה" (k'sheh) is a Hebrew word that means "when" or "whenever". It is derived from the root כ-ש-ה (k-sh-h), which means "to be" or "to exist".

Grammar: "כשה" is a conjunction in Hebrew that is used to introduce a subordinate clause expressing a condition or a time frame. It is often translated as "when" or "whenever" in English.

Description: "כשה" is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday speech and writing in Hebrew. It is used to indicate a specific time or condition that triggers an action or event. For example, "כשהאור ייכבה, אני הולך לישון" (When the light goes off, I go to sleep). It can also be used to express a general truth or habitual action, such as "כשהוא רואה כוס פנויה, הוא ממלא אותה" (Whenever he sees an empty cup, he fills it).
