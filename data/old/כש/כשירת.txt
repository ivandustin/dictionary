Etymology: The word "כשירת" (kashira) is a Hebrew word derived from the root כ-ש-ר (k-sh-r), which means "to be fit" or "to be suitable."

Grammar: "כשירת" is a feminine noun in the singular form. It is used to describe something or someone that is fit, suitable, or qualified for a specific purpose or task.

Description: "כשירת" is often used to describe objects, materials, or individuals that meet certain requirements or standards. It implies that the subject is capable, competent, or appropriate for a particular function or role.
