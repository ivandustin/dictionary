Etymology: The word "כשהיה" (k'shehaya) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "when it was." It is composed of the preposition "כ" (k), meaning "like" or "as," and the verb "היה" (haya), meaning "was."

Grammar: "כשהיה" is a conjunction phrase in Hebrew that is used to introduce a subordinate clause expressing a past event or condition. It is commonly used to describe a situation or event that occurred in the past.

Description: "כשהיה" is a versatile phrase that can be used in various contexts to provide background information or set the stage for a story or narrative. It is often used in storytelling, historical accounts, or when recounting past experiences.
