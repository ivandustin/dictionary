Etymology: The word "כשול" (keshol) comes from the Hebrew root כ-ש-ל (k-sh-l), which means "to stumble" or "to trip." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, כשול is a noun that means "stumbling" or "trip." It is used to describe a physical stumble or trip, as well as a metaphorical stumble or setback.

Description: כשול is often used in a figurative sense to describe obstacles or difficulties that one encounters in life. It can refer to both literal stumbling blocks and metaphorical challenges that hinder progress or success.
