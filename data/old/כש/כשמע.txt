Etymology: The word "כשמע" (kashma) comes from the Hebrew root "שמע" (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix "כ" (k) in this case is used to form a question, so "כשמע" (kashma) can be translated as "what do you hear?" or "what's the news?"

Grammar: "כשמע" (kashma) is an interrogative phrase in Hebrew. It is used to ask someone about the latest news or information.

Description: "כשמע" (kashma) is a colloquial expression in Hebrew that is commonly used to ask someone if they have any news or information to share. It is often used in informal conversations or as a greeting.
