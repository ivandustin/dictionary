Etymology: The word "כשמעי" (k'sh'ma'i) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root שמע (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix כ (k) is added to indicate the imperative form, making it a command to "listen" or "hear".

Grammar: "כשמעי" is a verb in the imperative form, addressing someone in the second person singular. It is used to command or request someone to listen or pay attention.

Description: "כשמעי" is a powerful and direct command in Hebrew, urging someone to listen attentively. It is often used in religious or poetic contexts, such as in prayers or biblical verses, to emphasize the importance of actively listening and understanding.
