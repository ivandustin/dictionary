Etymology: The word "כשל" (kashal) comes from the Hebrew root כ-ש-ל (k-sh-l), which means "to stumble" or "to trip." 

Grammar: "כשל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in various tenses and forms to indicate different grammatical aspects such as past, present, and future, as well as singular and plural.

Description: "כשל" is used to describe the act of stumbling or tripping, both literally and metaphorically. It can refer to physical stumbling or to encountering difficulties or obstacles in life.
