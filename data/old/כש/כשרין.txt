Therefore, when referring to multiple objects or people that are fit or proper, the plural form כשרין is used.

Description: The word "כשרין" (kashrin) is used in Hebrew to describe objects or people that meet certain religious or dietary requirements. It is often used in the context of kosher food, which refers to food that is prepared according to Jewish dietary laws.
