Etymology: The word "עבדתי" (pronounced "avduti") is a Hebrew word derived from the root עבד (pronounced "avad"), which means "to serve" or "to work." The suffix תי (pronounced "ti") indicates the first person singular past tense, so עבדתי translates to "I served" or "I worked."

Grammar: עבדתי is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. In Hebrew, verbs change their form based on the tense, person, and number of the subject. The past tense is used to describe actions that have already happened.

Description: עבדתי is a word that describes an action of serving or working that was done in the past. It can be used to talk about any kind of service or work that someone has performed.
