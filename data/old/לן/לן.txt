Etymology: The word "לְן" (pronounced "len") is a Hebrew word that means "to sleep" or "to spend the night." It is derived from the root ל-ו-ן (l-w-n), which conveys the idea of resting or dwelling.

Grammar: "לְן" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי לוֹנֵן (ani lonen) - I sleep
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה לוֹנֵן (atah lonen) - You sleep (masculine)
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ לוֹנֶנֶת (at lonenet) - You sleep (feminine)
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם לוֹנְנִים (atem lonenim) - You sleep (masculine plural)
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן לוֹנְנוֹת (aten lonenot) - You sleep (feminine plural)

Description: The word "לְן" refers to the act of sleeping or spending the night in a specific location. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a state of rest or tranquility.
