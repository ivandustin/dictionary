Etymology: The word "תנד" (pronounced "tand") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root נדה (n-d-h), which means "to wander" or "to roam."

Grammar: "תנד" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תונד (ani tond) - I wander
- Plural: אנחנו תונדים (anachnu tondim) - We wander

Description: "תנד" refers to the act of wandering or roaming aimlessly. It can be used to describe physical wandering or to express a state of mind or behavior characterized by aimlessness or lack of direction.
