Etymology: The word "תנקש" (pronounced "tan-kesh") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root נ.ק.ש (N.K.Sh), which means "to plot" or "to conspire" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "תנקש" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תנקש (ani tan-kesh) - I plot
- Masculine singular: אתה תנקש (atah tan-kesh) - You (masculine) plot
- Feminine singular: את תנקש (at tan-kesh) - You (feminine) plot
- Masculine plural: אתם תנקשו (atem tan-keshu) - You (masculine plural) plot
- Feminine plural: אתן תנקשנה (atan tan-keshna) - You (feminine plural) plot

Description: The word "תנקש" means "to plot" or "to conspire" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of planning or scheming to carry out a secret or harmful action.
