Etymology: The word "תנאץ" (pronounced "tena'atz") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root נ.א.ץ (N.A.Tz), which means "to provoke" or "to taunt."

- Singular:
  - 1st person: אֲנִי מְתַנֵּאֵץ (ani metana'etz) - I provoke
  - 2nd person: אַתָּה מְתַנֵּאֵץ (atah metana'etz) - You provoke (masculine)
  - 2nd person: אַתְּ מְתַנֵּאָצִית (at metana'etzit) - You provoke (feminine)
  - 3rd person: הוּא/הִיא מְתַנֵּאֵץ (hu/hi metana'etz) - He/She provokes
- Plural:
  - 1st person: אֲנַחְנוּ מְתַנְּאִיצִים (anachnu metanne'itzim) - We provoke
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם מְתַנְּאִיצִים (atem metanne'itzim) - You provoke (masculine/plural)
  - 2nd person: אַתֶּן מְתַנְּאִיצוֹת (aten metanne'itzot) - You provoke (feminine/plural)
  - 3rd person: הֵם/הֵן מְתַנְּאִיצִים (hem/hen metanne'itzim) - They provoke

Description: The word "תנאץ" means "to provoke" or "to taunt" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of intentionally irritating or inciting someone, often with the intention of eliciting a reaction or response.
