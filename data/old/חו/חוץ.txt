Etymology: The word "חוץ" (chutz) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the Hebrew root ח.ו.צ (chet-vav-tsade), which means "to go out" or "to exit."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "חוץ" is a noun that means "outside" or "outdoors." It can also be used as a preposition to indicate "except" or "besides."

Description: "חוץ" is a versatile word that is commonly used in everyday Hebrew conversations. It is used to refer to the area or space that is outside of a particular place or location. For example, "אני יושב בחוץ" (Ani yoshev bachutz) means "I am sitting outside." It can also be used to describe something that is external or unrelated to a specific situation. For instance, "אני אוהב את הטבע והחוץ" (Ani ohev et hateva ve'hachutz) means "I love nature and the outdoors." Additionally, "חוץ" can be used to express an exception or exclusion. For example, "כולם אכלו פיצה, חוץ ממני" (Kulam achlu pitza, chutz mimeni) means "Everyone ate pizza, except for me."
