Etymology: The word "חוקק" (pronounced "hokkek") comes from the Hebrew root ח.ק.ק (h.k.k), which means "to carve" or "to sculpt".

- אֲנִי חוֹקֵק (ani hokkek) - I carve
- אַתָּה חוֹקֵק (atah hokkek) - You (masculine singular) carve
- אַתְּ חוֹקֶקֶת (at hokket) - You (feminine singular) carve
- הוּא חוֹקֵק (hu hokkek) - He carves
- הִיא חוֹקֶקֶת (hi hokket) - She carves
- אֲנַחְנוּ חוֹקְקִים (anachnu hokkekim) - We carve
- אַתֶּם חוֹקְקִים (atem hokkekim) - You (masculine plural) carve
- אַתֶּן חוֹקְקוֹת (aten hokkot) - You (feminine plural) carve
- הֵם חוֹקְקִים (hem hokkekim) - They (masculine) carve
- הֵן חוֹקְקוֹת (hen hokkot) - They (feminine) carve

Description: "חוקק" refers to the act of carving or sculpting, typically using tools to shape or form a material such as wood, stone, or metal. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the process of creating or shaping something intangible, such as an idea or a plan.
