Etymology: The word "חומות" (chomot) comes from the Hebrew root ח.מ.ה (ch.m.h), which means "to surround" or "to enclose." It is derived from the noun חומה (choma), meaning "wall."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "חומות" is a plural noun. It is formed by adding the suffix ות (-ot) to the singular form חומה (choma). The plural form is used to refer to multiple walls.

Description: "חומות" is the Hebrew word for "walls." It refers to the structures that are built to enclose or protect an area. Walls can be made of various materials such as stone, brick, or concrete, and they serve as a physical barrier or boundary. In a figurative sense, "חומות" can also represent protection, security, or separation.
