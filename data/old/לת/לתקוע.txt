Etymology: The word "לתקוע" comes from the Hebrew root ת-ק-ע, which means "to fix" or "to fasten". It is derived from the noun תִּקּוּן (tikkun), meaning "repair" or "correction".

- אֲנִי תוֹקֵעַ (ani toke'a) - I fix
- אַתָּה תוֹקֵעַ (atah toke'a) - You (masculine singular) fix
- אַתְּ תוֹקַעְיִינָה (at toka'inah) - You (feminine singular) fix
- הוּא תוֹקֵעַ (hu toke'a) - He fixes
- הִיא תוֹקַעַת (hi tok'at) - She fixes
- אֲנַחְנוּ תוֹקְעִים (anachnu tok'im) - We fix
- אַתֶּם תוֹקְעִים (atem tok'im) - You (masculine plural) fix
- אַתֶּן תוֹקְעוֹת (aten tok'ot) - You (feminine plural) fix
- הֵם תוֹקְעִים (hem tok'im) - They fix
- הֵן תוֹקְעוֹת (hen tok'ot) - They (feminine) fix

Description: "לתקוע" means "to fix" or "to fasten" in Hebrew. It can refer to physically repairing or securing something, as well as metaphorically fixing or correcting a situation or problem.
