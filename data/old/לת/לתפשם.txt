Etymology: The word "לתפשם" (letafshem) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ת.פ.ש (T.P.Sh), which means "to catch" or "to seize". 

Grammar: "לתפשם" is a verb in the infinitive form. It is composed of the preposition "ל" (meaning "to" or "for") and the root ת.פ.ש (T.P.Sh). The suffix "ם" (em) indicates the third person plural object pronoun, meaning "them".

Description: "לתפשם" is used to express the action of catching or seizing something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as catching a ball, seizing an opportunity, or apprehending a criminal.
