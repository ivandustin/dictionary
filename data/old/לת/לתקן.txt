Etymology: The word "לתקן" (letaken) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root תקן (t.k.n). 

Grammar: "לתקן" is an infinitive verb form in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני מתקן (ani mitaken) - I fix, אתה מתקן (atah mitaken) - you fix (masculine), את מתקנת (at mitakenet) - you fix (feminine), הוא/היא מתקן (hu/hi mitaken) - he/she fixes, אנחנו מתקנים (anachnu mitakenim) - we fix, אתם מתקנים (atem mitakenim) - you fix (plural), הם/הן מתקנים (hem/hen mitakenim) - they fix.

Description: The word "לתקן" means "to fix" or "to repair" in English. It is used to describe the action of restoring something to its proper or functional state.
