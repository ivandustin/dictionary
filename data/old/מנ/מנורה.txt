Etymology: The word "מנורה" (Menorah) comes from the Hebrew root "נור" (NWR), which means "to shine" or "to give light." It is derived from the noun "נר" (ner), which means "candle" or "lamp."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מנורה" (Menorah) is a feminine noun. It is usually used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form "מנורות" (Menorot).

Description: The Menorah is a seven-branched candelabrum that has been a symbol of Judaism for centuries. It is most commonly associated with the Jewish holiday of Hanukkah, during which a special nine-branched Menorah, called a Hanukkiah, is used.
