Etymology: The word "מנעורי" (pronounced "min'orai") is a Hebrew word that translates to "from my youth" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root נער (na'ar), which means "youth" or "young person."

Grammar: "מנעורי" is a possessive phrase in Hebrew, composed of the preposition "מ" (pronounced "mi"), meaning "from," and the noun "נעורי" (pronounced "no'orai"), meaning "my youth." The word "נעורי" is in the construct state, indicating possession or association.

Description: The phrase "מנעורי" is used to express a connection or attachment to something that has been present or experienced since one's youth. It conveys a sense of familiarity, nostalgia, or long-lasting involvement with a particular person, place, or activity.
