Etymology: The word "מנדח" (mandach) comes from the Hebrew root נדח (n.d.ch), which means "to push away" or "to reject."

Grammar: "מנדח" (mandach) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מנדח (ani mandach) - I reject
- Plural: אנחנו מנדחים (anachnu mandachim) - We reject

Description: The word "מנדח" (mandach) is used to describe the act of pushing away or rejecting something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as rejecting an idea, a proposal, or a person.
