Etymology: The word "מנרה" (Menorah) comes from the Hebrew root "נר" (ner), which means "lamp" or "candle". It is derived from the verb "להאיר" (leha'ir), meaning "to illuminate" or "to light up".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מנרה" (Menorah) is a feminine noun. It is usually used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form "מנורות" (Menorot).

Description: The Menorah is a seven-branched candelabrum that has been a symbol of Judaism for centuries. It is most commonly associated with the Jewish holiday of Hanukkah, during which a special nine-branched Menorah, called a Hanukkiah, is used. The Menorah has deep religious and historical significance and is often used as a symbol of light, knowledge, and divine presence.
