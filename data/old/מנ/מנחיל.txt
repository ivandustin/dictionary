Etymology: The word "מַנְחִיל" (manchil) is derived from the Hebrew root נָחַל (nachal), which means "to inherit" or "to possess." 

Grammar: "מַנְחִיל" (manchil) is a masculine noun in the singular form. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is in the active participle form. 

Description: In Hebrew, "מַנְחִיל" (manchil) refers to someone who inherits or possesses something. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who acquires or gains knowledge, skills, or qualities.
