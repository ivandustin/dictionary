Etymology: The word "ארש" (Arsh) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root א-ר-ש (A-R-Sh). It is derived from the Aramaic word "אַרְשָׁא" (Arsha) which means "to betroth" or "to engage in marriage."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "ארש" (Arsh) is a verb in the Qal (simple) stem, which means it is in its basic form. It is conjugated in different tenses, moods, and persons to indicate different aspects of the action of betrothing or engaging in marriage.

Description: The word "ארש" (Arsh) refers to the act of betrothing or engaging in marriage. It is used to describe the formal agreement between a man and a woman to become husband and wife. In Jewish tradition, betrothal is considered a binding commitment, similar to marriage, although the couple is not yet living together. Betrothal is followed by the wedding ceremony, which completes the marriage.
