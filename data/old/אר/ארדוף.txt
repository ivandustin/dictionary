Etymology: The word "ארדוף" (arduf) comes from the Hebrew root רדף (radaf), which means "to chase" or "to pursue".

Grammar: ארדוף is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני ארדוף (ani arduf) - I chase
- אתה רודף (atah rodaf) - You chase (masculine singular)
- את רודפת (at rodafet) - You chase (feminine singular)
- הוא רודף (hu rodaf) - He chases
- היא רודפת (hi rodafet) - She chases
- אנחנו רודפים (anachnu rodafim) - We chase
- אתם רודפים (atem rodafim) - You chase (masculine plural)
- אתן רודפות (aten rodafot) - You chase (feminine plural)
- הם רודפים (hem rodafim) - They chase (masculine)
- הן רודפות (hen rodafot) - They chase (feminine)

Description: ארדוף is an action verb that describes the act of chasing or pursuing someone or something. It can be used in various contexts, such as chasing after a person, an animal, or an idea.
