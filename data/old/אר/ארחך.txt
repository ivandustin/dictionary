Etymology: The word "ארחץ" (aruchat) is derived from the Hebrew root רחץ (rachatz), which means "to wash" or "to cleanse."

Grammar: ארחץ is a verb in the first person singular form of the future tense in Hebrew. It is conjugated from the root רחץ (rachatz) by adding the prefix א (a) and the suffix תי (ti).

Description: ארחץ is the Hebrew word for "I will wash" or "I will cleanse." It is used to express an action that will happen in the future.
