Etymology: The word "לדש" (Ladash) is a Hebrew verb that means "to study" or "to learn." It is derived from the Hebrew root "ל.ד.ש" (L.D.SH), which conveys the idea of acquiring knowledge or understanding.

Grammar: "לדש" (Ladash) is an infinitive verb form in the Hebrew language. It is commonly used in the present tense to express the act of studying or learning. In Hebrew grammar, verbs are conjugated according to tense, person, and number.

Description: "לדש" (Ladash) is a versatile verb that can be used in various contexts. It is often used to describe the process of acquiring knowledge or engaging in educational activities.
