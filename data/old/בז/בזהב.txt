Etymology: The word "בזהב" (b'zahav) is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "ב" (b) meaning "in" or "with" and "זהב" (zahav) meaning "gold". 

Grammar: "בזהב" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (b) indicates a location or means, and "זהב" (zahav) is the noun meaning "gold". Together, the phrase means "in gold" or "with gold".

Description: "בזהב" is used to describe something that is made of gold or covered in gold. It can refer to objects, jewelry, or even figuratively to describe something of great value or beauty.
