Etymology: The word "נעניתי" (pronounced "na'aniti") is derived from the Hebrew root נ.ע.נ (n.a.n), which means "to answer" or "to respond". The suffix יתי (-iti) indicates the first person singular past tense, so נעניתי translates to "I answered" or "I responded".

Grammar: נעניתי is a verb in the first person singular past tense in Hebrew. It is conjugated in the passive voice, indicating that the subject received the action of answering or responding. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated based on the subject, tense, and other grammatical factors.

Description: נעניתי is a word used to express that the speaker answered or responded to something in the past. It can be used in various contexts, such as answering a question, responding to a request, or replying to a message.
