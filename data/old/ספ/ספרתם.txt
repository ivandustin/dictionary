Etymology: The word "ספרתם" comes from the Hebrew root ס.פ.ר (s.p.r), which means "to count" or "to enumerate". The suffix תם (-tem) is a plural form indicating "you all" or "they". Therefore, ספרתם means "you all counted" or "they counted".

Grammar: ספרתם is the second person plural masculine form of the verb ספר (safar), which means "to count". It is conjugated in the past tense and includes the suffix תם (-tem) to indicate the subject as "you all" or "they".

Description: ספרתם is a Hebrew word that specifically refers to the act of counting or enumerating done by a group of people. It is commonly used in contexts where multiple individuals are involved in counting or keeping track of something.
