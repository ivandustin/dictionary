Etymology: The word "ספחת" (pronounced "safachat") comes from the Hebrew root ס.פ.ח (s.p.h), which means "to adhere" or "to stick". 

Grammar: "ספחת" is a noun in the feminine form. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. 

Description: In Hebrew, "ספחת" refers to a skin infection or a rash. It is commonly used to describe a contagious skin condition characterized by redness, itching, and inflammation.
