Etymology: The word "למועדי" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the preposition "ל" (meaning "to" or "for") and the noun "מועדים" (meaning "appointed times" or "festivals").

Grammar: "למועדי" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" indicates direction or purpose, and it is followed by the noun "מועדים" in the plural form. The word "למועדי" can be translated as "to the appointed times" or "for the festivals" in English.

Description: "למועדי" is used in Hebrew to refer to the various religious festivals and appointed times in the Jewish calendar. These include holidays such as Passover, Shavuot, Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, and Sukkot. The phrase emphasizes the significance and purpose of these special occasions in the Jewish tradition.
