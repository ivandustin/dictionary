Etymology: The word "למואל" (Lemuel) is a Hebrew name that appears in the Bible. It is derived from the Hebrew root "למד" (lamad), which means "to learn" or "to teach."

Grammar: "למואל" is a proper noun in Hebrew and is typically used as a personal name. It is masculine and singular.

Description: In the Bible, "Lemuel" is mentioned in Proverbs 31:1 as the name of the author of a collection of wise sayings. The identity of Lemuel is uncertain, and some scholars believe it may be a pseudonym for King Solomon.
