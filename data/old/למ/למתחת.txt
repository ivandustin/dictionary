Etymology: The word "למתחת" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the preposition "ל" (meaning "to" or "for") and the noun "מתחת" (meaning "underneath" or "below").

Grammar: "למתחת" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It is commonly used to indicate a location or position that is underneath or below something else. It is often followed by a noun or pronoun to specify what is underneath.

Description: "למתחת" is a versatile Hebrew phrase that is used in various contexts. It can be used to describe physical locations, such as "הכוס נמצאת למתחת לשולחן" (The cup is underneath the table). It can also be used metaphorically to describe a subordinate or inferior position, such as "הילד נמצא למתחת לאחיו" (The child is below his brother).
