Etymology: The word "למי" (pronounced "le-mi") is a Hebrew word that means "to whom" or "for whom". It is derived from the Hebrew root "מ.י.י" (pronounced "m.y.y"), which means "who" or "whom".

Grammar: "למי" is a preposition in Hebrew that is used to indicate the recipient or beneficiary of an action. It is commonly used in questions to ask "to whom" or "for whom" something is being done.

Description: "למי" is a versatile word that is used in various contexts in the Hebrew language. It is often used in everyday conversations, formal writing, and literature. It helps to specify the intended recipient or beneficiary of an action or to inquire about the recipient of an action.
