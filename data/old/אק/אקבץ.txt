Etymology: The word "אקבץ" (akbats) is derived from the Hebrew root קבץ (k-b-ts), which means "to gather" or "to collect."

Grammar: אקבץ is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אֲקַבֵּץ (a-kavets) - I gather
- תְּקַבֵּץ (te-kavets) - you gather (singular)
- יְקַבֵּץ (ye-kavets) - he gathers
- תְּקַבְּצִי (te-kavetsi) - you gather (feminine singular)
- נְקַבֵּץ (ne-kavets) - we gather
- תְּקַבְּצוּ (te-kavetsu) - you gather (plural)
- יְקַבְּצוּ (ye-kavetsu) - they gather

Description: אקבץ is used to describe the action of gathering or collecting things together. It can be used in various contexts, such as gathering people, objects, or ideas.
