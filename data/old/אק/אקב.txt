Etymology: The word "אקב" (pronounced "akav" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root עקב (ayin-kuf-bet), which means "heel" or "to follow closely". 

Grammar: "אקב" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני אקב (ani akav) - I follow closely, אתה אקב (ata akav) - you (singular) follow closely, הוא אקב (hu akav) - he follows closely, היא אקבה (hi akava) - she follows closely, אנחנו אקבים (anachnu akavim) - we follow closely, אתם אקבים (atem akavim) - you (plural) follow closely, הם אקבים (hem akavim) - they (masculine) follow closely, הן אקבות (hen akavot) - they (feminine) follow closely.

Description: The verb "אקב" is used to describe the action of closely following or pursuing someone or something. It can also be used metaphorically to mean "to pursue" or "to chase after" in a non-literal sense.
