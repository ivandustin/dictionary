Etymology: The word "אקום" (akum) is derived from the Hebrew root קום (kum), which means "to rise" or "to stand up."

Grammar: "אקום" (akum) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני אקום (ani akum) - I rise
- אתה קם (atah kam) - You rise (masculine singular)
- את קמה (at kama) - You rise (feminine singular)
- הוא קם (hu kam) - He rises
- היא קמה (hi kama) - She rises
- אנחנו קמים (anachnu kamim) - We rise
- אתם קמים (atem kamim) - You rise (masculine plural)
- אתן קמות (aten kamot) - You rise (feminine plural)
- הם קמים (hem kamim) - They rise (masculine)
- הן קמות (hen kamot) - They rise (feminine)

Description: The word "אקום" (akum) refers to the action of rising or standing up physically or metaphorically. It can be used to describe getting out of bed, standing up from a sitting position, or even rising to a higher level or position.
