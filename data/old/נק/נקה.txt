Etymology: The word "נקה" (naka) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ק.ה (n.k.h), which means "to clean" or "to purify".

Grammar: In Hebrew, נקה is a verb in the Qal (simple) conjugation. It is usually used in the past tense, present tense, and future tense to describe the act of cleaning or purifying.

Description: נקה is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It can refer to physically cleaning or purifying something, such as cleaning a house or purifying water. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the act of cleansing or purifying oneself spiritually or morally. Additionally, נקה can be used in legal contexts to refer to the act of acquitting or declaring someone innocent.
