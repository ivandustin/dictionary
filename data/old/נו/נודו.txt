Etymology: The word "נודו" (nodo) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root נ.ו.ד (n.w.d), which means "to wander" or "to roam."

Grammar: "נודו" (nodo) is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is commonly used in the context of describing a group of people or animals wandering or roaming.

Description: "נודו" (nodo) describes the action of a group of people or animals wandering or roaming aimlessly. It implies a lack of direction or purpose in their movement.
