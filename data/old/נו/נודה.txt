Etymology: The word "נודה" (nudah) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ו.ד (n.w.d), which means "to wander" or "to roam". It is derived from the noun נֹד (nod), which means "a wandering" or "a roaming".

Grammar: "נודה" (nudah) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי נוֹדֶה (ani nodé) - I wander
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה נוֹדֶה (atah nodé) - You (masculine) wander
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ נוֹדֶה (at nodé) - You (feminine) wander
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם נוֹדִים (atem nodim) - You (masculine plural) wander
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן נוֹדוֹת (aten nodot) - You (feminine plural) wander

Description: The word "נודה" (nudah) describes the action of wandering or roaming. It can be used in various contexts, such as physically wandering in a place or figuratively wandering in thoughts or ideas.
