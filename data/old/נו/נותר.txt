Etymology: The word "נותר" (notar) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ת.ר (n.t.r), which means "to remain" or "to be left over."

Grammar: "נותר" (notar) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי נוֹתֵר (ani noter) - I remain
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה נוֹתֵר (atah noter) - You (masculine) remain
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ נוֹתֶרֶת (at noteret) - You (feminine) remain
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם נוֹתְרִים (atem notrim) - You (masculine plural) remain
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן נוֹתְרוֹת (aten notrot) - You (feminine plural) remain

Description: The word "נותר" (notar) is used to express the idea of something or someone remaining or being left over after a certain event or action. It can also convey the sense of being leftover or remaining unused.
