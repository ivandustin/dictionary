Etymology: The word "נושע" (nosa) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ש.ע (n.sh.a), which means "to save" or "to deliver."

Grammar: "נושע" (nosa) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני נושע (ani nosa) - I save
- Masculine singular: אתה נושע (ata nosa) - You save (masculine)
- Feminine singular: את נושעת (at nosa'at) - You save (feminine)
- Plural: אנחנו נושעים (anachnu nosa'im) - We save
- Masculine plural: אתם נושעים (atem nosa'im) - You save (masculine)
- Feminine plural: אתן נושעות (aten nosa'ot) - You save (feminine)

Description: "נושע" (nosa) means "to save" or "to deliver" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of rescuing someone or something from danger, harm, or a difficult situation. The word can also be used metaphorically to express the idea of being saved or redeemed spiritually or emotionally.
