Etymology: The word "לקדישי" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the preposition "ל" (meaning "to" or "for") and the word "קדישי" (meaning "holy" or "sacred").

Grammar: "לקדישי" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" indicates direction or purpose, and it is followed by the noun "קדישי" which functions as the object of the preposition. In this case, "לקדישי" can be translated as "to the holy" or "for the sacred".

Description: "לקדישי" is used in Hebrew to express a connection or dedication to something holy or sacred. It can be used in various contexts, such as referring to holy places, sacred rituals, or religious objects.
