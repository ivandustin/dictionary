Etymology: The word "דַּלְתִּי" (dalati) comes from the Hebrew root ד.ל.ת (D.L.T), which means "door" or "gate". 

Grammar: "דַּלְתִּי" is a noun in the singular form. It is masculine and has a definite article "ה" (ha) attached to it, making it "הַדַּלְתִּי" (hadalati), which means "the door" in Hebrew.

Description: "דַּלְתִּי" refers to a door or a gate in Hebrew. It is used to describe the physical entrance or exit point of a building or a room. In a broader sense, it can also symbolize opportunities, possibilities, or transitions.
