Etymology: The word "אדורים" (Adurim) is a Hebrew word that is derived from the root ד.ו.ר (D.W.R), which means "to dwell" or "to abide". It is related to the word "אדר" (Adar), which means "to settle" or "to inhabit".

Grammar: "אדורים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root word אדור (Adur). The plural form indicates that there are multiple instances of the noun.

Description: "אדורים" refers to "dwellings" or "abodes" in Hebrew. It can be used to describe houses, residences, or any place where people live or dwell.
