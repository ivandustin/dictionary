Etymology: The word "אדין" (adin) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Hebrew root "עדן" (ayin-dalet-nun), which means "to judge" or "to rule."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אדין" (adin) is a noun that means "justice" or "judgment." It is commonly used to refer to the concept of fairness, righteousness, or the act of making a legal decision.

Description: "אדין" (adin) is a significant term in Jewish law and ethics. It represents the ideal of justice and the fair treatment of individuals. It is often associated with the concept of divine judgment and the belief that God is the ultimate judge who ensures justice is served. In Jewish tradition, the pursuit of justice and the establishment of a just society are considered fundamental values.
