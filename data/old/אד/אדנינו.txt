Etymology: The word "אדנינו" (pronounced "adonenu") is a Hebrew word that translates to "our Lord" or "our Master" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew word "אדון" (pronounced "adon"), which means "lord" or "master."

Grammar: "אדנינו" is a possessive form of the word "אדון" (adon), which means "lord" or "master." The suffix "-נו" (-nu) indicates possession, meaning "our" in this case. It is used to refer to God as the Lord or Master of the speaker or the Jewish people.

Description: "אדנינו" is a term of reverence and respect used in Jewish prayers and religious texts to refer to God. It acknowledges God as the ultimate authority and ruler. It is often used to express devotion and submission to God's will.
