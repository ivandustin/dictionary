Grammar: "אדננו" is a possessive form of the word "אדון" (adon), which means "lord" or "master." The suffix "-נו" (nu) indicates possession, meaning "our" in this case. It is used to refer to God as the Lord or Master of the speaker or the Jewish people.

Description: "אדננו" is a term of reverence and respect used in Jewish prayers and religious texts to refer to God. It acknowledges God as the ultimate authority and ruler. It is often used in the context of expressing devotion and submission to God.
