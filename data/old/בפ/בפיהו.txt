Etymology: The word "בפיהו" (pronounced "b'fihu") is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in his mouth" or "on his lips." It is derived from the Hebrew words "ב" (pronounced "b") meaning "in" or "on," "פה" (pronounced "peh") meaning "mouth," and "הוא" (pronounced "hu") meaning "he" or "his."

Grammar: "בפיהו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition "ב" (in/on) followed by the noun "פה" (mouth) and the possessive pronoun "הוא" (his). In Hebrew grammar, prepositional phrases are commonly used to indicate location, possession, or other relationships between words.

Description: The phrase "בפיהו" is often used in Hebrew to express the idea that something is widely known or frequently mentioned by someone. It can also imply that someone is speaking about something with great emphasis or authority.
