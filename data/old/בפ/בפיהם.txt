Etymology: The word "בפיהם" (pronounced "b'fiham") is a Hebrew phrase that consists of two parts: "ב" (pronounced "b") meaning "in" or "with," and "פיהם" (pronounced "fiham") meaning "their mouths." 

Grammar: "בפיהם" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" indicates a location or means, and "פיהם" is the plural possessive form of the noun "פה" (pronounced "peh"), meaning "mouth." Together, the phrase means "in their mouths" or "with their mouths."

Description: "בפיהם" is often used in Hebrew to express the idea that something is spoken or said by a group of people. It can refer to words, opinions, or statements that are collectively expressed by a specific group.
