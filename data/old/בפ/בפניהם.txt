Etymology: The word "בפניהם" (pronounced "b'fanayhem") is a Hebrew phrase composed of two parts: "ב" (pronounced "b") meaning "in" or "with," and "פניהם" (pronounced "fanayhem") meaning "their faces." 

Grammar: "בפניהם" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" indicates a location or manner, and "פניהם" is a noun meaning "their faces." Together, the phrase means "in their faces" or "with their faces."

Description: "בפניהם" is used to describe a situation where something is done openly or directly to someone's face. It implies a confrontational or direct interaction with someone, often involving a verbal or physical action.
