Etymology: The word "בפרים" (beparim) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root word "פר" (par), which means "bull" or "ox". 

Grammar: "בפרים" (beparim) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the prefix "ב" (be-) meaning "in" or "with" to the singular form "פר" (par). The plural ending "-ים" (-im) is added to indicate multiple bulls or oxen.

Description: "בפרים" (beparim) refers to a group of bulls or oxen. It can be used to describe a herd of these animals or to refer to them in a general sense.
