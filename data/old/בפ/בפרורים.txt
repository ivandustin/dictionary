Etymology: The word "בפרורים" (b'frurim) is a Hebrew word that means "in pieces" or "fragmented". It is derived from the root word "פרר" (parar), which means "to break" or "to shatter".

Grammar: "בפרורים" (b'frurim) is an adverbial phrase in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the preposition "ב" (b) meaning "in" or "with" to the noun "פרורים" (frurim) meaning "pieces" or "fragments".

Description: The phrase "בפרורים" (b'frurim) is used to describe something that is broken or fragmented into pieces. It can be used both literally and figuratively to describe physical objects or abstract concepts.
