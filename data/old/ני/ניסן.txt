Etymology: The word "ניסן" (Nisan) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the Akkadian word "nīsānu," which means "first fruits."

Grammar: "ניסן" (Nisan) is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is used as the name of the first month of the Hebrew calendar.

Description: In the Hebrew calendar, Nisan is the first month of the year, occurring in the spring. It is a significant month in Jewish tradition, as it marks the beginning of the religious year and is associated with the holiday of Passover. Nisan is also known as the month of redemption, as it commemorates the liberation of the Israelites from slavery in Egypt.
