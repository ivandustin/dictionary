Etymology: The word "יחשכו" (pronounced "yachshichu") is a Hebrew verb in the future tense. It is derived from the root ח-ש-כ, which means "to darken" or "to obscure."

Grammar: "יחשכו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb חשך (yachshichu). In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to tense, person, and number. The future tense indicates an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural form refers to a group of people or objects.

Description: The word "יחשכו" can be translated to English as "they will darken" or "they will obscure." It describes an action that will be performed by a group of people or objects in the future, causing something to become darker or less visible.
