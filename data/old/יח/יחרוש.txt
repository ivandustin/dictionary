Etymology: The word "יחרוש" (Yacharosh) is derived from the Hebrew root ח.ר.ש (Ch.R.Sh), which means "to plow" or "to till the soil." 

Grammar: "יחרוש" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני חורש (Ani chorash) - I plow, אתה חורש (Ata chorash) - You (masculine singular) plow, את חורשת (At chorasht) - You (feminine singular) plow, הוא חורש (Hu chorash) - He plows, היא חורשת (Hi chorasht) - She plows, אנחנו חורשים (Anachnu chorshim) - We plow, אתם חורשים (Atem chorshim) - You (masculine plural) plow, אתן חורשות (Aten chorshot) - You (feminine plural) plow, הם חורשים (Hem chorshim) - They (masculine) plow, הן חורשות (Hen chorshot) - They (feminine) plow.

Description: "יחרוש" is a Hebrew verb that specifically refers to the act of plowing or tilling the soil. It is commonly used in agricultural contexts to describe the process of preparing the ground for planting seeds or crops. Plowing is an essential step in farming, as it helps loosen the soil, remove weeds, and create furrows for planting.
