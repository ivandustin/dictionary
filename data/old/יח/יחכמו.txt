Etymology: The word "יחקמו" (Yachkemo) is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root ח-ק-מ (Ch-Q-M). 

Grammar: "יחקמו" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb חקם (Chakam), which means "to be wise" or "to become wise" in Hebrew. The future tense indicates that the action of becoming wise will happen in the future.

Description: The word "יחקמו" is used to describe the action of a group of people becoming wise or acquiring wisdom. It implies a process of gaining knowledge, understanding, and insight.
