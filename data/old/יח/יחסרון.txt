Etymology: The word "יחסרון" (Yechasron) is derived from the Hebrew root חסר (chasar), which means "to lack" or "to be deficient". The suffix ון (-on) is added to indicate a noun form.

Grammar: "יחסרון" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is in the singular form. In the plural form, it becomes "יחסרונים" (Yechasronim).

Description: "יחסרון" refers to a lack or deficiency in something. It can be used to describe a shortage, a deficit, or an insufficiency in various contexts.
