Etymology: The word "יחליץ" (pronounced yachlitz) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ח-ל-ץ (ch-l-tz). 

Grammar: "יחליץ" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני יחליץ (ani yachlitz) - I extract, אתה יחליץ (atah yachlitz) - you extract (masculine singular), את תחליצי (at tachlizi) - you extract (feminine singular), הוא יחליץ (hu yachlitz) - he extracts, היא תחליץ (hi tachlitz) - she extracts, אנחנו נחליצ (anachnu nechlitzeem) - we extract, אתם תחליצו (atem tachlitzeem) - you extract (masculine plural), אתן תחליצנה (atan tachlitzena) - you extract (feminine plural), הם יחליצו (hem yachlitzeem) - they extract (masculine), הן יחליצנה (hen yachlitzena) - they extract (feminine).

Description: The word "יחליץ" means "to extract" or "to free" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the act of removing or liberating something from a particular state or condition.
