Etymology: The word "יחמד" (Yachmad) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ח-מ-ד (ch-m-d), which means "to desire" or "to covet". 

Grammar: "יחמד" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the third person singular masculine form. It is in the present tense, indicating an ongoing action or state. 

Description: The word "יחמד" is often translated as "he desires" or "he covets" in English. It expresses a strong longing or craving for something.
