Etymology: The word "כחי" (pronounced "kachi") is a Hebrew word that means "my strength" or "my power." It is derived from the Hebrew root כח (pronounced "koach"), which means "strength" or "power."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "כחי" is a possessive form of the noun כח (pronounced "koach"). The suffix י (pronounced "i") indicates possession, meaning "my." So, "כחי" translates to "my strength" or "my power."

Description: "כחי" is a word that expresses personal strength or power. It can be used to describe physical, mental, or emotional strength. It is often used in a positive and empowering context, emphasizing one's own abilities and capabilities.
