Etymology: The word "מעשתי" (ma'ashti) is derived from the Hebrew root ע.ש.ת (ayin, shin, tav), which means "to do" or "to make." It is formed by adding the possessive suffix י (yod) to the root, indicating "my" or "mine."

Grammar: "מעשתי" (ma'ashti) is a first-person singular possessive form of the noun "מעשה" (ma'aseh), which means "action" or "deed." The possessive form indicates that the action or deed belongs to the speaker.

Description: "מעשתי" (ma'ashti) can be translated as "my action" or "my deed" in English. It is used to express ownership or possession of a specific action or deed.
