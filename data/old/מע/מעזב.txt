Etymology: The word "מעזב" (me'azev) comes from the Hebrew root עזב (azav), which means "to leave" or "to abandon".

Grammar: "מעזב" (me'azev) is a verb in the present tense, third person singular form. It can also be conjugated in other tenses and forms to match different subjects and time frames.

Description: The word "מעזב" (me'azev) is used to describe the act of leaving or abandoning something or someone. It can refer to physically leaving a place, ending a relationship, or neglecting a responsibility.
