Etymology: The word "מעשקות" (ma'asakot) is derived from the Hebrew root ע.ש.ק (ayin, shin, kuf), which means "to play" or "to amuse oneself." It is a plural noun form, indicating multiple instances of play or amusement.

Grammar: "מעשקות" is a feminine plural noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to various forms of play, amusement, or entertainment.

Description: "מעשקות" is a versatile word that can encompass a wide range of activities or objects related to play or amusement. It can refer to games, toys, recreational activities, or any form of entertainment that brings joy or amusement.
