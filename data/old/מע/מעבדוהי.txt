Etymology: The word "מעבדוהי" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the words "מעבד" (ma'avid) meaning "worker" or "laborer" and "הי" (hi) which is a possessive pronoun meaning "my". Therefore, "מעבדוהי" can be translated as "my worker" or "my laborer".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מעבדוהי" is a noun phrase consisting of the possessive pronoun "הי" (hi) and the noun "מעבד" (ma'avid). The word order is subject-possessive pronoun-noun.

Description: "מעבדוהי" is a term used in Hebrew to refer to someone's personal worker or laborer. It implies a sense of ownership or possession over the worker.
