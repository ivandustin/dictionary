Etymology: The word "מעבדי" (me'abdi) is derived from the Hebrew root עבד (avad), which means "to serve" or "to work." The prefix מ (me) indicates the causative form, so מעבדי (me'abdi) can be translated as "those who cause to serve" or "those who cause to work."

Grammar: In Hebrew, מעבדי (me'abdi) is a plural noun. The suffix י (i) indicates the possessive form, so מעבדי (me'abdi) can be translated as "my servants" or "my workers."

Description: מעבדי (me'abdi) is a term used in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament) to refer to servants or workers. It is often used to describe individuals who serve or work for someone else, such as slaves or employees.
