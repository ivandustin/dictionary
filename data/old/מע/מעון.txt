Etymology: The word "מעון" (ma'on) comes from the Hebrew root עון (ayin-vav-nun), which means "to dwell" or "to reside". It is related to the word "מושב" (moshav), which means "settlement" or "residence".

Grammar: "מעון" (ma'on) is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in both the singular and plural forms. In the plural form, it becomes "מעונות" (ma'onot).

Description: "מעון" (ma'on) refers to a dwelling place or a residence. It can be used to describe a physical structure such as a house, apartment, or any other place where someone lives. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a place of refuge, sanctuary, or a shelter.
