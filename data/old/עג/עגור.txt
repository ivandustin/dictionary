Etymology: The word "עגור" (agur) comes from the Hebrew root ע.ג.ר (ayin.gimel.reish), which means "to fly" or "to soar".

It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אָנֹכִי עוֹגֵר (anokhi oger) - I fly, אַתָּה עוֹגֵר (ata oger) - you fly (masculine singular), אַתְּ עוֹגֶרֶת (at ogeret) - you fly (feminine singular), הוּא עוֹגֵר (hu oger) - he flies, הִיא עוֹגֶרֶת (hi ogeret) - she flies, אֲנַחְנוּ עוֹגְרִים (anakhnu ogerim) - we fly, אַתֶּם עוֹגְרִים (atem ogerim) - you fly (masculine plural), אַתֶּן עוֹגְרוֹת (aten ogerot) - you fly (feminine plural), הֵם עוֹגְרִים (hem ogerim) - they fly (masculine), הֵן עוֹגְרוֹת (hen ogerot) - they fly (feminine).

Description: The word "עגור" is used to describe the action of flying or soaring through the air. It can be used both literally, to refer to the physical act of flying, and metaphorically, to describe a feeling of freedom or elevation.
