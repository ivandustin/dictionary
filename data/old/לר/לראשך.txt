Etymology: The word "לראשך" is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "ל" (meaning "to" or "for") and "ראשך" (meaning "your head"). 

Grammar: "לראשך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" indicates direction or purpose, and "ראשך" is the possessive form of the noun "ראש" (meaning "head"). Together, the phrase means "to your head" or "for your head".

Description: "לראשך" is often used in Hebrew to express the idea of something being done or happening specifically for someone's benefit or advantage. It can be used in various contexts, such as giving advice or expressing good wishes.
