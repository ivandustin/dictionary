Etymology: The word "נחמיה" (Nehemiah) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Hebrew root נָחַם (nacham), which means "to comfort" or "to console."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "נחמיה" (Nehemiah) is a masculine proper noun. It is typically used as a personal name.

Description: Nehemiah is a biblical figure who served as a governor of the Persian province of Judah in the 5th century BCE. He is known for his efforts to rebuild the walls of Jerusalem and restore the religious practices of the Jewish people. The Book of Nehemiah in the Hebrew Bible recounts his story and leadership. The name Nehemiah is still used today as a given name in various cultures.
