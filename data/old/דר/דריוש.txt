Etymology: The word "דריוש" (Darius) is of Persian origin and is derived from the Old Persian name "Dārayavahush," meaning "possessing goodness" or "holding firm."

Grammar: "דריוש" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is usually used as a proper name, referring to the historical figure Darius the Great, who was the king of Persia from 522 to 486 BCE.

Description: Darius the Great was one of the most influential rulers of the Achaemenid Empire. He expanded the empire to its greatest extent, implementing administrative reforms and promoting cultural and economic development. He is known for his construction projects, including the building of the Royal Road and the palace complex at Persepolis. Darius the Great is also mentioned in the Hebrew Bible in the book of Daniel.
