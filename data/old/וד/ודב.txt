Etymology: The word "וֹדֵב" (vodav) comes from the Hebrew root ו־ד־ב (v-d-b), which means "to speak" or "to talk."

Grammar: "וֹדֵב" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי מוֹדֵב (ani modav) - I speak
- Plural: אֲנַחְנוּ מוֹדְבִים (anachnu modvim) - We speak

Description: "וֹדֵב" is used to describe the act of speaking or talking. It can refer to any form of communication using words or language.
