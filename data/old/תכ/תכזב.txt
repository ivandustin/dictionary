Etymology: The word "תקזב" (pronounced "tekhezav" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root ק-ז-ב, which means "to lie down" or "to recline."

- הֵן מִתְקַזְּבוֹת (hen mitkazvot) - They (feminine) lie down

Description: "תקזב" is a Hebrew verb that means "to lie down" or "to recline." It is used to describe the action of resting or reclining in a horizontal position. This verb can be used in various contexts, such as lying down to sleep, relaxing on a couch, or reclining at a table during a meal.
