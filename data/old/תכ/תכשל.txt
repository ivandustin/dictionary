Etymology: The word "תקשל" (pronounced "tikshel") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ק.ש.ל, which means "to bind" or "to tie." It is derived from the noun קֶשֶׁל (keshel), which means "a knot" or "a tie."

Grammar: "תקשל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני תקשל (ani tikshel) - I tie
- Plural: אנחנו תקשלים (anachnu tikshelim) - We tie

Description: The word "תקשל" is used to describe the action of tying or binding something together. It can refer to physically tying objects together or metaphorically connecting ideas or concepts.
