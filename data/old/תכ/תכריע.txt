Etymology: The word "תכריע" (pronounced "takri'a") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root כרע (k-r-'a), which means "to bend" or "to bow down." 

Grammar: "תכריע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני מתכרע (ani mitkare'a) - I bend down, אתה מתכרע (atah mitkare'a) - you (masculine) bend down, את מתכרעת (at mitkare'a) - you (feminine) bend down, הוא/היא מתכרע (hu/hi mitkare'a) - he/she bends down, אנחנו מתכרעים (anachnu mitkare'im) - we bend down, אתם מתכרעים (atem mitkare'im) - you (plural) bend down, הם/הן מתכרעים (hem/hen mitkare'im) - they bend down.

Description: The word "תכריע" is used to describe the action of bending down or bowing down physically. It can also be used metaphorically to describe submitting or yielding to someone or something.
