Etymology: The word "הפכפך" comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root פ-כ-פ-ך (P-K-P-Kh), which means "to stumble" or "to falter."

- Singular masculine: הֵפֵך (hefekh)
- Singular feminine: הֵפִיכִי (hefikhi)
- Plural masculine: הֵפְכוּ (hefekhu)
- Plural feminine: הֵפִיכְנָה (hefikhna)

Description: "הפכפך" means "to stumble" or "to falter" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe someone's physical or metaphorical stumbling or hesitation.
