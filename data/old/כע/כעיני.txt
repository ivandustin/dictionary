Etymology: The word "כְּעִינִי" (pronounced "ke'ini") is a Hebrew word that translates to "in my eyes" or "in my opinion." It is derived from the root ע.נ.ה (ayin.nun.hei), which means "to see" or "to perceive."

Grammar: "כְּעִינִי" is a prepositional phrase composed of the preposition כְּ (ke), meaning "like" or "as," and the noun עַיִן (ayin), meaning "eye." The suffix י (i) indicates possession, making it "my eyes" or "my opinion."

Description: This phrase is commonly used in Hebrew to express one's personal perspective or subjective opinion about something. It emphasizes that the following statement is based on the speaker's own perception or viewpoint.
