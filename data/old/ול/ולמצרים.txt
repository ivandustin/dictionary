Etymology: The word "וְלַמְצָרִים" (velamtsarim) is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "וְ" (ve) meaning "and" and "לַמְצָרִים" (lamtsarim) meaning "to the Egyptians". 

Grammar: "וְ" (ve) is a conjunction that connects words or phrases. "לַמְצָרִים" (lamtsarim) is a prepositional phrase consisting of the preposition "לְ" (le) meaning "to" and the noun "מִצְרַיִם" (Mitzrayim) meaning "Egypt" in the plural form.

Description: The phrase "וְלַמְצָרִים" (velamtsarim) is found in the Hebrew Bible, specifically in the book of Exodus (Shemot) 7:3. It is part of a sentence spoken by God to Moses and Aaron, instructing them to go to Pharaoh and perform miracles in order to convince him to release the Israelites from slavery in Egypt. The phrase emphasizes that Moses and Aaron were sent specifically to the Egyptians, indicating the target audience of their message and actions.
