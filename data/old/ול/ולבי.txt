Etymology: The word "וֹלְבִי" (pronounced "volvi") is a Hebrew word that means "and my heart." It is derived from the Hebrew root word "לב" (lev), which means "heart."

Grammar: "וֹלְבִי" is a possessive phrase composed of the conjunction "וֹ" (pronounced "o") meaning "and" and the possessive pronoun "לִי" (pronounced "li") meaning "my." It is used to express ownership or possession of the heart.

Description: "וֹלְבִי" is a poetic phrase commonly used in Hebrew literature and songs to express deep emotions, particularly love and longing. It is often used to convey the idea of someone being deeply connected to another person or something they are passionate about.
