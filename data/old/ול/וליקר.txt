Etymology: The word "וֹלִיקֵר" (voliker) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ו-ל-ק-ר (v-l-k-r), which means "to roll" or "to turn."

Grammar: "וֹלִיקֵר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי מוֹלִיקֵר (ani moliker) - I roll/turn
- Plural: אֲנַחְנוּ מוֹלְקְרִים (anachnu molkerim) - We roll/turn

Description: "וֹלִיקֵר" is used to describe the action of rolling or turning something. It can refer to physically rolling an object or metaphorically turning a situation or circumstance.
