Etymology: The word "וּלְשֵׁם־הַמִּצְוָה" (ul'shem hamitzvah) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "and for the sake of the commandment." It is commonly used in Jewish law to indicate that a specific action is being performed solely for the purpose of fulfilling a religious commandment.

The phrase is used as a prepositional phrase to indicate the purpose or intention behind an action.

It is often used to emphasize the intentionality and religious significance of performing a specific action.
