Etymology: The word "וּלְשֵׁם" (pronounced "ul'shem") is a Hebrew word that translates to "and for the name of." It is composed of the conjunction "וּ" (pronounced "oo") meaning "and," and the preposition "לְ" (pronounced "le") meaning "for" or "to," followed by the noun "שֵׁם" (pronounced "shem") meaning "name."

Grammar: "וּלְשֵׁם" is a phrase that functions as a prepositional phrase in Hebrew grammar. It is used to indicate the purpose or intention behind an action or the reason for doing something.

Description: The phrase "וּלְשֵׁם" is often used in religious or ceremonial contexts in Hebrew. It is commonly found in prayers, blessings, and dedications, where it signifies that an action or offering is being done for the sake of honoring or glorifying a specific name or deity.
