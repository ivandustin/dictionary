Etymology: The word "וּלְשַׁלְמָה" (pronounced "ul'shalma") is a Hebrew phrase composed of two words: "וּלְ" (pronounced "ul") meaning "and to" and "שַׁלְמָה" (pronounced "shalma") meaning "peace". 

Grammar: "וּלְשַׁלְמָה" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "וּלְ" functions as a conjunction meaning "and to", and "שַׁלְמָה" is a noun meaning "peace". Together, they form the phrase "and to peace".

Description: The phrase "וּלְשַׁלְמָה" is often used in Hebrew as a greeting or farewell, similar to the English phrase "peace be with you". It is a wish for peace and well-being to the person being addressed.
