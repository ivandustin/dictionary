Etymology: The word "וּלְצָאֵת" (ul'tza'et) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root יצא (yatzah), which means "to go out" or "to exit."

Grammar: "וּלְצָאֵת" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, composed of the conjunction "וּ" (u) meaning "and," the preposition "לְ" (le) meaning "to," and the verb "צָאֵת" (tza'et) meaning "to go out" or "to exit."

Description: "וּלְצָאֵת" is used to express the purpose or goal of an action. It indicates that something is done in order to go out or exit from a certain place or situation.
