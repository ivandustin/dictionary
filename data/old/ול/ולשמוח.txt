Etymology: The word "וּלְשֵׁמוֹ" (pronounced "ulshemo") is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "and for His name." It is composed of the conjunction "וּ" (pronounced "oo") meaning "and," the preposition "לְ" (pronounced "le") meaning "for," and the noun "שֵׁם" (pronounced "shem") meaning "name."

Grammar: "וּלְשֵׁמוֹ" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The conjunction "וּ" connects it to the previous clause or phrase, indicating a continuation or addition. The preposition "לְ" introduces the object of the phrase, which is "שֵׁם" (name). The suffix "וֹ" (pronounced "o") indicates possession, meaning "His name."

Description: "וּלְשֵׁמוֹ" is a phrase commonly used in Hebrew prayers and blessings. It is often added at the end of a sentence or phrase to express that an action or statement is done for the sake of honoring or glorifying God's name. It signifies the intention to dedicate an action or attribute its purpose to God.
