Etymology: The word "וֹלְטָת" (voltaat) is a Hebrew word derived from the root ו-ל-ט (v-l-t), which means "to wander" or "to roam."

Grammar: "וֹלְטָת" is a noun in the singular feminine form. It is used to describe the act of wandering or roaming.

Description: "וֹלְטָת" refers to the action of moving around aimlessly or without a specific destination. It can be used to describe physical wandering or metaphorical wandering of the mind or thoughts.
