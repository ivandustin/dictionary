Etymology: The word "דברו" (pronounced "davaroo") comes from the Hebrew root ד-ב-ר (D-B-R), which means "to speak" or "to say". It is derived from the noun דָּבָר (davar), which means "word" or "thing".

Grammar: "דברו" is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is commonly used in Modern Hebrew to mean "they spoke" or "they said".

Description: "דברו" is a verb that indicates an action of speaking or saying performed by a group of people in the past. It can be used in various contexts and conjugated according to the subject and tense of the sentence.
