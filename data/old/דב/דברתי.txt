Etymology: The word "דברתי" (pronounced "dibarti") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ד-ב-ר (D-B-R), which means "to speak" or "to talk".

Grammar: "דברתי" is the first person singular past tense form of the verb דבר (diber), which means "I spoke" or "I talked". It is used when referring to an action that was completed in the past.

Description: "דברתי" is a verb that indicates the act of speaking or talking in the past. It is commonly used in everyday conversations to express that the speaker has communicated or expressed something in the past.
