Etymology: The word "דבריהם" (pronounced "dvarihem") is a Hebrew word. It is a combination of two Hebrew words: "דבר" (pronounced "davar") meaning "word" or "thing," and "הם" (pronounced "hem") meaning "their." So, "דבריהם" can be translated as "their words" or "their things."

Grammar: "דבריהם" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-יהם" to the word "דבר." The suffix "-יהם" indicates possession, meaning "their" in this case.

Description: "דבריהם" is used to refer to the words or things that belong to a group of people. It can be used in various contexts, such as discussing someone's opinions, statements, or possessions.
