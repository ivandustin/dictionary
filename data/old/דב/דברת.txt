Etymology: The word "דברת" (dibarta) comes from the Hebrew root ד-ב-ר (D-B-R), which means "to speak" or "to say".

Grammar: "דברת" (dibarta) is the second person singular feminine form of the verb "לדבר" (ledaber), which means "to speak" or "to talk". It is in the past tense, indicating that the action of speaking has already occurred.

Description: "דברת" (dibarta) is used to address a female person directly, indicating that she has spoken or said something in the past.
