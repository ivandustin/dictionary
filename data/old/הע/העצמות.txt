Etymology: The word "העצמות" (Ha'atzamot) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root עצם (atzam), meaning "bone" or "skeleton".

Grammar: "העצמות" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ות (ot) to the singular form העצם (ha'etzem).

Description: העצמות (Ha'atzamot) refers to the bones or skeletal structure of a living organism. In human anatomy, the skeletal system provides support, protection, and movement for the body. Bones are made up of living cells and various minerals, and they play a crucial role in maintaining the body's structure and functioning.
