Etymology: The word "העמלק" (HaAmalek) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root עמלק (Amalek), which means "to labor" or "to toil."

Grammar: "העמלק" (HaAmalek) is a noun in Hebrew. It is a proper noun, referring to a specific group or nation mentioned in the Hebrew Bible.

Description: In Jewish tradition, Amalek refers to an ancient enemy of the Israelites. According to the Hebrew Bible, the Amalekites were a nomadic people who attacked the Israelites during their journey from Egypt to the Promised Land. The Amalekites are often seen as a symbol of evil and cruelty. The commandment to remember and eradicate Amalek is mentioned several times in the Hebrew Bible.
