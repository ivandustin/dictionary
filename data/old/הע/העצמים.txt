Etymology: The word "העצמים" (Ha'atzmim) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root עצם (atzam), which means "bone" or "essence." The prefix ה- (ha-) indicates "the" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "העצמים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix -ים (-im) to the root עצם (atzam). The definite article ה- (ha-) is added at the beginning to indicate "the bones" or "the essences."

Description: "העצמים" can have different meanings depending on the context. Literally, it means "the bones." It can refer to the physical bones in the human body or be used metaphorically to represent the core or essence of something. In a figurative sense, it can refer to the fundamental principles or values that form the foundation of a concept or belief.
