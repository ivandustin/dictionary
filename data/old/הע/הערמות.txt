Etymology: The word "הערמות" (Ha'arumot) is a Hebrew noun derived from the root ע.ר.מ (A.R.M), which means "to pile up" or "to heap." 

Grammar: "הערמות" is a plural noun in Hebrew, indicating multiple piles or heaps. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ות" (-ot) to the root word. 

Description: "הערמות" refers to piles or heaps of objects that are stacked or accumulated together. It can be used to describe physical piles of items, such as a pile of books or a heap of clothes. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a large quantity or a multitude of things.
