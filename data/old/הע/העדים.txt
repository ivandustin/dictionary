Etymology: The word "העדים" (Ha'edim) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root עד (ed), which means "witness" or "testimony". The prefix ה (ha) is the definite article "the" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "העדים" (Ha'edim) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root עד (ed). The definite article ה (ha) indicates that it is referring to a specific group of witnesses.

Description: "העדים" (Ha'edim) refers to "the witnesses" in Hebrew. It is commonly used to describe individuals who provide testimony or evidence in a legal or formal setting. In Jewish law, witnesses play a crucial role in validating contracts, marriages, and other legal matters.
