Grammar: "בְּדִי" is a possessive pronoun in Hebrew. It is formed by combining the preposition "בְּ" (pronounced "be") meaning "in" or "with," and the possessive pronoun "דִּי" (pronounced "di") meaning "my."

Description: The word "בְּדִי" is used to express ownership or possession. It is commonly used to indicate that something belongs to the speaker or is associated with them. For example, "בְּדִי סֵפֶר" (pronounced "b'di sefer") means "my book" and "בְּדִי כֶּלֶב" (pronounced "b'di kelev") means "my dog."
