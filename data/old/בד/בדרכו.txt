It consists of the preposition בְּ (be), which means "in" or "according to," and the noun דֶּרֶךְ (derech), which means "way" or "path." The suffix וֹ (o) indicates possession, making the phrase mean "in his way" or "according to his way."

Description: "בְּדַרְכוֹ" (bedarko) is used to describe someone's behavior or actions, indicating that they are acting in a manner consistent with their own way or style. It implies that the person is following their own path or approach, rather than conforming to others' expectations or norms.
