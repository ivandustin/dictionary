Etymology: The word "בְּדַק" (bedak) comes from the Hebrew root ב־ד־ק, which means "to examine" or "to investigate."

Grammar: "בְּדַק" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the binyan Pa'al (active voice) and in the present tense. The word can be used in different grammatical forms to match the subject and tense of the sentence.

Description: "בְּדַק" means "to examine" or "to investigate" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the act of carefully inspecting or scrutinizing something in order to gain knowledge or understanding. The word can be used in various contexts, such as examining a document, investigating a crime, or analyzing a problem.
