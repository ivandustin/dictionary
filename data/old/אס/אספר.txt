Etymology: The word "אספר" (Aspar) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root אספר, which means "to tell" or "to recount."

Grammar: "אספר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אֲסַפֵּר (I tell)
- תְּסַפֵּר (you tell)
- יְסַפֵּר (he/she tells)
- נְסַפֵּר (we tell)
- תְּסַפְּרוּ (you tell)
- יְסַפְּרוּ (they tell)

Description: "אספר" is a verb that means "to tell" or "to recount" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of narrating or sharing information or stories.
