Etymology: The word "אסתר" (Esther) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Persian name "Esther" which means "star".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אסתר" (Esther) is a feminine noun. It is used as a proper name for individuals, particularly referring to the biblical character Queen Esther.

Description: Esther is a significant figure in the Hebrew Bible, specifically in the Book of Esther. She was a Jewish queen of the Persian king Ahasuerus (Xerxes I) and played a crucial role in saving the Jewish people from a plot to annihilate them. The story of Esther is celebrated during the Jewish holiday of Purim.
