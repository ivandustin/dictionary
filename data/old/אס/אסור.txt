Etymology: The word "אסור" (asur) comes from the Hebrew root אסר, which means "to prohibit" or "to forbid."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אסור" is an adjective that means "forbidden" or "prohibited." It is used to describe something that is not allowed or is against the rules.

Description: "אסור" is commonly used in Hebrew to indicate that something is not permitted or is prohibited. It can refer to actions, behaviors, or objects that are forbidden by law, religious rules, or social norms.
