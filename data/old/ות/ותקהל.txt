Etymology: The word "וְתַקְהֵל" (v'takheil) is a Hebrew word derived from the root קהל (k-h-l), which means "to gather" or "to assemble." The prefix וְ (v') means "and."

Grammar: "וְתַקְהֵל" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the qal stem and imperfect tense. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form. The verb form indicates an action that is ongoing or incomplete.

Description: "וְתַקְהֵל" is the Hebrew word for "and she gathered" or "and she assembled." It is used in the context of the Torah, specifically in the book of Exodus (Exodus 35:1), where it describes the action of Moses gathering the Israelites together to instruct them regarding the construction of the Tabernacle.
