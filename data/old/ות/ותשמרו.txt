Etymology: The word "וְתַשְׁמֵרוּ" is a Hebrew verb form derived from the root שמר (shamar), which means "to keep" or "to guard."

Grammar: "וְתַשְׁמֵרוּ" is the plural, masculine, and imperative form of the verb. It is used to address a group of people and instructs them to "keep" or "guard" something.

Description: The word "וְתַשְׁמֵרוּ" is found in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is often translated as "you shall keep" or "you shall guard." It is used in various contexts to convey the idea of protecting, observing, or obeying something.
