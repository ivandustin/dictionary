Grammar: "וְתַשְׁכִּיבֵהוּ" is conjugated in the future tense, second person, singular, feminine form. It is used when addressing a female subject and instructing her to lay down a male object or person.

Description: The phrase "וְתַשְׁכִּיבֵהוּ" is found in the Hebrew Bible, specifically in the book of Exodus (Exodus 2:3). It is part of the story of Moses' birth and his mother's attempt to hide him from Pharaoh's decree to kill all Hebrew male infants.
