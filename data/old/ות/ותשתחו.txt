The prefix וְ (v') indicates the conjunction "and," and the verb form תֵּשְׁתָּחוּ (tishtachu) is the third person plural, masculine, future tense of the verb.

Grammar: The verb וְתֵשְׁתָּחוּ (v'tishtachu) is conjugated in the future tense, indicating an action that will happen in the future. The third person plural, masculine form suggests that the subject of the verb is a group of males. The verb is also in the imperative mood, which expresses a command or request.

Description: The word וְתֵשְׁתָּחוּ (v'tishtachu) is found in the Hebrew Bible and is often translated as "and they shall bow down" or "and they shall prostrate themselves." It is used in the context of worship or reverence towards a higher power. The verb conveys the act of physically bowing down or prostrating oneself as a sign of respect, submission, or adoration.
