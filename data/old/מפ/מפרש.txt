Etymology: The word "מפרש" (pronounced "mefarash" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root פרש, which means "to explain" or "to interpret." It is derived from the noun פרשה (parashah), which means "portion" or "section." In Jewish tradition, פרשה refers to a weekly portion of the Torah that is read and studied.

Grammar: "מפרש" is a noun in Hebrew. It is a masculine singular form, and its plural form is "מפרשים" (mefarashim). It can also be used as a verb in the form of "להפריש" (lehafreesh), which means "to explain" or "to interpret."

Description: "מפרש" refers to a commentator or an interpreter. In Jewish religious texts, it specifically refers to a scholar who provides explanations and interpretations of biblical texts, such as the Torah, Talmud, or other religious writings. These commentators analyze the text, provide historical context, and offer insights into the meaning and application of the teachings. Their interpretations often serve as a guide for understanding and applying religious laws and principles.
