Etymology: The word "מפרש׳" (mefarsh) comes from the Hebrew root פרש׳ (p-r-sh), which means "to explain" or "to interpret."

Grammar: "מפרש׳" is a noun in Hebrew, and it is in the masculine singular form. It is derived from the verb "לפרש׳" (lifrosh), which means "to explain" or "to interpret."

Description: "מפרש׳" refers to a commentator or an interpreter. In Jewish tradition, it is commonly used to describe scholars who provide explanations and interpretations of religious texts, such as the Bible or Talmud. These commentators analyze the text, provide insights, and offer explanations to help understand the meaning and context of the original text. Their interpretations often contribute to the development of Jewish law and thought.
