Etymology: The word "פשרה" (pronounced "peshara") comes from the Hebrew root פ-ש-ר, which means "to interpret" or "to explain." It is derived from the noun פֶּשֶׁר (peshar), which means "interpretation" or "explanation."

Grammar: In Hebrew, פשרה is a feminine noun. It is usually used in the singular form, but it can also be used in the plural form פְּשָׁרוֹת (pesharot). It can be modified by adjectives or used in different grammatical constructions.

Description: פשרה refers to the act of interpreting or explaining something, especially a text or a dream. It can also refer to the interpretation or explanation itself. In Jewish tradition, פשרה is often associated with the interpretation of biblical texts, particularly the Torah. It is a significant aspect of Jewish hermeneutics and is used to uncover deeper meanings and insights within the text.
