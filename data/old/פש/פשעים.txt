Etymology: The word "פשעים" (pronounced "psha'im") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root פ-ש-ע, which means "to transgress" or "to sin" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "פשעים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (-im) to the root פשע (psha), indicating that there are multiple transgressions or sins.

Description: "פשעים" refers to transgressions or sins. It is used to describe actions or behaviors that go against moral or religious laws. The word can be used in various contexts, such as discussing personal sins, societal transgressions, or religious offenses.
