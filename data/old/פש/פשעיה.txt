Etymology: The word "פְּשָׁעִים" (pronounced "peshaim") comes from the Hebrew root פָּשַׁע (pasha), which means "to transgress" or "to rebel." 

Grammar: פְּשָׁעִים is a noun in the plural form. In Hebrew, nouns have gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural). 

Description: פְּשָׁעִים refers to "transgressions" or "sins" in Hebrew. It is used to describe acts of disobedience or rebellion against religious or moral laws.
