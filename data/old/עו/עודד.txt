Etymology: The word "עודד" (pronounced "odad" in English) is a Hebrew word that comes from the Hebrew root ע.ו.ד (Ayin-Vav-Dalet). 

Grammar: "עודד" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the Pa'al (active) binyan (verb form). It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אוֹדֵד (I encourage), תּוֹדֵד (you encourage), יוֹדֵד (he/she encourages), נוֹדֵד (we encourage), תּוֹדְדוּ (you all encourage), יוֹדְדוּ (they encourage).

Description: The word "עודד" means "to encourage" or "to strengthen" in Hebrew. It can refer to providing support, motivation, or inspiration to someone. It is often used in contexts related to boosting someone's morale or giving them confidence.
