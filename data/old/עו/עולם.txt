Etymology: The word "עולם" (olam) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root word "עלם" (alam), which means "to hide" or "to conceal." The word "עולם" (olam) can be translated as "world" or "universe."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "עולם" (olam) is a masculine noun. It is usually used with definite articles such as "העולם" (ha'olam) meaning "the world" or "the universe." It can also be used in possessive constructions, such as "עולם שלי" (olam sheli) meaning "my world."

Description: "עולם" (olam) is a versatile word that can have different meanings depending on the context. It can refer to the physical world, the universe, or the concept of time. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a long period of time or an everlasting existence. In religious contexts, it can refer to the world as created by God or the spiritual realm.
