Etymology: The word "עוקר" (pronounced "okher") comes from the Hebrew root ע-ק-ר (Ayin-Kuf-Resh), which means "to uproot" or "to remove." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, עוקר is a verb in the present tense, masculine singular form. It can also be conjugated to match different genders, numbers, and tenses.

Description: עוקר is used to describe the action of uprooting or removing something from its place. It can be used both literally, to refer to physically uprooting a plant or removing something from the ground, as well as metaphorically, to describe removing or eliminating something from a situation or context.
