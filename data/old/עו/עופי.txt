Etymology: The word "עופי" (pronounced "ofi") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ע.ו.פ (Ayin-Vav-Pe), which means "to fly" or "to soar."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "עופי" is a noun that means "bird" or "fowl." It is used to refer to any type of bird, whether domestic or wild.

Description: Birds are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by feathers, beaks, and the ability to fly. They are found in various habitats around the world and come in a wide range of sizes, shapes, and colors. Birds play important roles in ecosystems, such as pollination, seed dispersal, and insect control. They are also admired for their beauty and are often kept as pets or studied by ornithologists.
