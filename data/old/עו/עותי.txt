Etymology: The word "עותי" (pronounced "oti") is a Hebrew word that means "my time" or "my season." It is derived from the Hebrew root ע.ת.ה (ayin-tet-hei), which means "to be timely" or "to be at the right time."

Grammar: "עותי" is a possessive form of the noun עת (et), which means "time" or "season." The suffix י (i) indicates possession, so "עותי" translates to "my time" or "my season."

Description: The word "עותי" is used to express ownership or possession of a specific time or season. It can be used in various contexts, such as referring to a personal period of time or a specific season that belongs to someone.
