Etymology: The word "עומד" (oméd) comes from the Hebrew root ע.מ.ד (ayin.mem.dalet), which means "to stand" or "to be upright".

Grammar: In Hebrew, עומד is a verb in the present tense, masculine singular form. It can also be conjugated to match different genders, numbers, and tenses.

Description: עומד is used to describe the action of standing or being upright physically. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone's position, stability, or commitment to a certain belief or cause.
