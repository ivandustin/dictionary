Etymology: The word "כפרה" (kaparah) comes from the Hebrew root כפר (k-p-r), which means "to cover" or "to atone." It is derived from the ancient practice of covering or smearing blood on an altar as a form of atonement for sins.

Grammar: "כפרה" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is a feminine singular noun, and its plural form is "כפרות" (kaparot).

Description: In Jewish tradition, "כפרה" refers to the act of atonement or expiation for sins. It is often associated with the rituals performed on Yom Kippur, the Day of Atonement, where individuals seek forgiveness for their transgressions. The concept of "כפרה" emphasizes the idea of cleansing and purifying oneself from wrongdoing.
