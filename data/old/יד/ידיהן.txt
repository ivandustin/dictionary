Etymology: The word "ידיהן" (Yedihen) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root word "יד" (yad), which means "hand". The suffix "-יהן" (-hen) is a possessive suffix that means "their". So, "ידיהן" (Yedihen) literally translates to "their hand".

Grammar: "ידיהן" (Yedihen) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-יהן" (-hen) to the root word "יד" (yad). The suffix "-יהן" (-hen) indicates possession, specifically "their" in this case.

Description: "ידיהן" (Yedihen) refers to the hands of a group of people. It can be used to describe the physical hands of individuals or symbolically represent their actions, abilities, or power.
