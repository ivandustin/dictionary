Etymology: The word "ידר" (pronounced "yadir") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root י-ד-ר (y-d-r), which means "to arrange" or "to organize."

- אֲנִי יוֹדֵר (ani yoder) - I arrange
- אַתָּה יוֹדֵר (atah yoder) - You (masculine singular) arrange
- אַתְּ יוֹדֶרֶת (at yoderet) - You (feminine singular) arrange
- הוּא יוֹדֵר (hu yoder) - He arranges
- הִיא יוֹדֶרֶת (hi yoderet) - She arranges
- אֲנַחְנוּ יוֹדְרִים (anachnu yodrim) - We arrange
- אַתֶּם יוֹדְרִים (atem yodrim) - You (masculine plural) arrange
- אַתֶּן יוֹדְרוֹת (aten yodrot) - You (feminine plural) arrange
- הֵם יוֹדְרִים (hem yodrim) - They (masculine) arrange
- הֵן יוֹדְרוֹת (hen yodrot) - They (feminine) arrange

Description: The verb "ידר" means "to arrange" or "to organize" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of putting things in order or planning something systematically. The word can be used in various contexts, such as organizing a schedule, arranging objects in a specific order, or planning an event.
