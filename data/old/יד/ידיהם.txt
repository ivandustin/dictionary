Etymology: The word "ידיהם" (Yadihem) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "יד" (Yad) meaning "hand" and "הם" (hem) meaning "their". When combined, it means "their hands".

Grammar: "ידיהם" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix "-ים" (-im) to the word "יד" (Yad), which changes the word from singular ("hand") to plural ("hands"). The prefix "ה" (ha) is added to indicate possession, making it "their hands".

Description: "ידיהם" refers to the hands of a group of people. It can be used to describe the physical hands of individuals or symbolically represent their actions, abilities, or power.
