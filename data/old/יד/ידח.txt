Etymology: The word "ידח" (pronounced "yadach" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root דחה (pronounced "dacha"), which means "to push away" or "to reject."

Grammar: ידח is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני יודח (ani yodach) - I am rejected
- Plural: אנחנו יודחים (anachnu yodachim) - We are rejected

Description: ידח is used to describe the act of pushing away or rejecting something or someone. It can be used in various contexts, such as rejecting a proposal, dismissing an idea, or refusing an offer.
