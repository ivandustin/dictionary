Etymology: The word "תאחר" (ta'acher) comes from the Hebrew root א-ח-ר (A-Ch-R), which means "to delay" or "to postpone". 

Grammar: "תאחר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני מְאַחֵר (ani me'acher) - I delay, אתה מְאַחֵר (ata me'acher) - you delay (masculine singular), את מְאַחֶרֶת (at me'acheret) - you delay (feminine singular), הוא מְאַחֵר (hu me'acher) - he delays, היא מְאַחֶרֶת (hi me'acheret) - she delays, אנחנו מְאַחֲרִים (anachnu me'acharim) - we delay, אתם מְאַחֲרִים (atem me'acharim) - you delay (masculine plural), אתן מְאַחֲרוֹת (atan me'acharot) - you delay (feminine plural), הם מְאַחֲרִים (hem me'acharim) - they delay (masculine), הן מְאַחֲרוֹת (hen me'acharot) - they delay (feminine).

Description: The word "תאחר" means "to delay" or "to postpone" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of intentionally causing something to happen later than originally planned or expected.
