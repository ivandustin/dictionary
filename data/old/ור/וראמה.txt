Etymology: The word "וֹרָאִים" (voraim) is derived from the Hebrew root ראה (ra'ah), which means "to see" or "to perceive." The prefix וֹ (vo) indicates the plural form.

Grammar: "וֹרָאִים" is a masculine plural noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to a group of people who see or perceive something.

Description: The word "וֹרָאִים" is found in the Hebrew Bible and is often translated as "seers" or "prophets." It is used to describe individuals who have the ability to see or perceive divine messages or visions. In biblical times, prophets played a significant role in communicating God's will to the people of Israel. They were considered to be intermediaries between God and humanity.
