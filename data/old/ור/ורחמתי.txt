Etymology: The word "וֹרְחַמְתִּי" (vor'chamti) is a Hebrew word derived from the root רחם (racham), which means "to have compassion" or "to show mercy."

Grammar: "וֹרְחַמְתִּי" is a verb in the past tense, first person singular form. It is conjugated from the root רחם (racham) with the prefix וֹ (vav) and the suffix תִּי (ti), indicating the first person singular pronoun "I."

Description: The word "וֹרְחַמְתִּי" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is often translated as "I have had compassion" or "I have shown mercy." It expresses an act of showing kindness, empathy, or pity towards someone or something.
