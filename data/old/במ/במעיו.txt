Etymology: The word "במעיו" (pronounced "b'ma'ayo") is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in his belly" or "in his stomach." It is derived from the Hebrew root word "מעה" (ma'ah), which means "belly" or "stomach."

Grammar: "במעיו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition "ב" (pronounced "b"), meaning "in," and the possessive pronoun "מעיו" (pronounced "ma'ayo"), meaning "his belly" or "his stomach." The phrase is used to indicate location or possession.

Description: "במעיו" is used in Hebrew to describe something that is located or contained within someone's belly or stomach. It can be used literally to refer to something physically inside the body, or figuratively to describe a feeling or emotion deeply felt within oneself.
