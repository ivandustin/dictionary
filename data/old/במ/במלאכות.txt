Etymology: The word "במלאכות" (bemelakhot) is a Hebrew term that comes from the root word "מלאכה" (melakhah), which means "work" or "occupation". 

Grammar: "במלאכות" is a noun in the plural form. It is used to refer to various types of work or labor.

Description: "במלאכות" is a term used in Jewish law to describe activities that are prohibited on the Sabbath (Shabbat) and certain holidays. These activities are traditionally associated with the work that was done in the construction of the Tabernacle (Mishkan) during the time of Moses. The specific types of work included in the category of "במלאכות" can vary depending on different interpretations and traditions. Generally, they encompass actions such as cooking, lighting a fire, sewing, writing, and other forms of creative or productive work. Observant Jews refrain from engaging in these activities during the designated holy days.
