Etymology: The word "במטהו" is a Hebrew phrase that consists of two words: "ב" (pronounced "be") meaning "in" or "at" and "מטהו" (pronounced "matto") meaning "his bed". 

Grammar: "במטהו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" means "in" or "at", and "מטהו" is a possessive noun phrase meaning "his bed". Together, they form the phrase "in his bed" or "at his bed".

Description: "במטהו" is used to describe the location of someone or something in relation to a bed. It indicates that someone or something is in or at a specific bed.
