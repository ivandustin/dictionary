Etymology: The word "בְּמִקְוֵה" (b'mikveh) comes from the Hebrew root ק.ו.ה (q.w.h), which means "to gather" or "to collect." It is derived from the noun מִקְוֶה (mikveh), which means "a gathering" or "a collection."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "בְּמִקְוֵה" (b'mikveh) is a prepositional phrase composed of the preposition בְּ (b') meaning "in" or "at," and the noun מִקְוֶה (mikveh) meaning "a gathering" or "a collection." It is used to refer to a ritual bath used for purification in Jewish tradition.

Description: A mikveh is a Jewish ritual bath used for various purposes, including purification before certain religious activities or after certain events. It is typically a pool of water that meets specific requirements according to Jewish law. The immersion in the mikveh is seen as a symbolic act of spiritual cleansing and renewal. Mikvehs are used by both men and women for various religious and life events, such as conversion to Judaism, marriage, and the end of menstruation.
