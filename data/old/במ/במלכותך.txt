The suffix "-ך" ("-cha") indicates the possessive form, indicating that the kingdom belongs to the second person singular.

Description: The phrase "במלכותך" is often used in Jewish prayers and religious texts to express the belief in God's sovereignty and the desire for His kingdom to be established. It acknowledges God as the ultimate ruler and expresses the hope for His reign to be recognized and realized.
