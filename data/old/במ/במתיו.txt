Etymology: The word "בְּמִתְיוֹ" (bemito) is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "in his death." It is composed of the preposition "בְּ" (be), meaning "in," and the noun "מִתְיוֹ" (mito), meaning "his death."

Description: The phrase "בְּמִתְיוֹ" (bemito) is used in Hebrew to refer to someone or something being in a state of death. It can be used to describe the condition of a person or an object after their death.
