Etymology: The word "בהצותו" (behatzoto) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "ב" (be) which means "in" or "at", and "הצותו" (hatzoto) which is the verb form of the noun "צו" (tzeva) meaning "command" or "order". Therefore, "בהצותו" can be translated as "in his command" or "at his order".

Grammar: "בהצותו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, composed of the preposition "ב" (be) meaning "in" or "at", and the noun "הצותו" (hatzoto) which is the construct form of the noun "צו" (tzeva) meaning "command" or "order". The construct form is used to indicate possession or association. In this case, it indicates that the command or order belongs to someone.

Description: "בהצותו" is used in Hebrew to express that something is done or happening according to someone's command or order. It implies obedience or compliance with the instructions given by someone.
