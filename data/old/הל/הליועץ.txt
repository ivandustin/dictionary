Etymology: The word "הליעץ" (halioz) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root word "לעץ" (la'atz), which means "to advise" or "to counsel".

Grammar: "הליעץ" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני הוליעץ (ani holeitz) - I advise
- Plural: אנחנו הוליעצים (anachnu holeitzim) - We advise

Description: "הליעץ" means "to advise" or "to counsel" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of giving guidance, suggestions, or recommendations to someone in order to help them make a decision or solve a problem.
