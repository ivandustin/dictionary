Etymology: The word "סרתי" (pronounced "sarti") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ס-ר-ת (s-r-t). This root is related to the concept of turning or deviating from a path.

Grammar: "סרתי" is the first-person singular past tense form of the verb סר (sar), which means "to turn" or "to deviate." In this form, it indicates that the speaker (or the subject) turned or deviated from a path in the past.

Description: "סרתי" is a specific form of the verb סר (sar) that indicates a past action of turning or deviating. It is used when the speaker wants to express that they turned or deviated from a path at some point in the past.
