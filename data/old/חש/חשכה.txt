Etymology: The word "חשכה" (chashkha) comes from the Hebrew root ח־ש־כ־ה, which means "to be dark" or "to become dark." It is derived from the noun חשך (choshech), meaning "darkness."

Grammar: "חשכה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is used to describe the state or condition of darkness.

Description: "חשכה" refers to the absence or lack of light, resulting in a dark or dim environment. It can be used both literally, to describe the absence of light in a physical space, and metaphorically, to describe a state of ignorance, confusion, or despair.
