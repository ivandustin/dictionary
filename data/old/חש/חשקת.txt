Etymology: The word "חשקת" (pronounced "cheshkat") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ח-ש-ק (ch-sh-q). This root is related to the concept of desire or longing.

Grammar: "חשקת" is the past tense, second person singular form of the verb חָשַק (cha-shak), which means "to desire" or "to long for" in Hebrew. It is conjugated in the past tense, indicating an action that has already happened.

Description: "חשקת" is used to describe an action of desiring or longing for something in the past. It is specifically used when addressing a single person, in the second person singular form.
