Etymology: The word "נבער" (pronounced "nabar") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root נ.ב.ע (n.b.a), which means "to prophesy" or "to speak forth." 

Grammar: "נבער" is a verb in the Qal (simple) conjugation, third person masculine singular form. It is also used in other conjugations and forms to indicate different tenses, genders, and numbers.

Description: "נבער" means "to prophesy" or "to speak forth as a prophet" in Hebrew. It is often used in the context of receiving divine messages or insights and communicating them to others. In the Hebrew Bible, prophets were individuals who were believed to have been chosen by God to deliver His messages to the people.
