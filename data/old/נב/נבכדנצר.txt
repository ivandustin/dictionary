Etymology: The word "נבקדנצר" (Nebuchadnezzar) is derived from the Hebrew name "נְבוּכַדְנֶאצַּר" (Nəḇūḵaḏneṣṣar), which in turn comes from the Akkadian name "Nabû-kudurri-uṣur". The Akkadian name means "O Nabu, protect the boundary".

Grammar: "נבקדנצר" is a proper noun in Hebrew and does not have a specific grammatical form. It is used to refer to the Babylonian king Nebuchadnezzar II.

Description: Nebuchadnezzar II was a prominent ruler of the Neo-Babylonian Empire from 605 BC to 562 BC. He is known for his military campaigns, including the conquest of Jerusalem and the destruction of the First Temple. Nebuchadnezzar is also mentioned in the Bible, particularly in the Book of Daniel. He is often depicted as a powerful and ambitious king in historical and religious texts.
