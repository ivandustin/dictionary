Etymology: The word "נבוזראדן" (Nebuzaradan) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the name of a Babylonian military officer mentioned in the Hebrew Bible.

Grammar: "נבוזראדן" (Nebuzaradan) is a noun in Hebrew. It is a proper noun, referring to a specific person.

Description: Nebuzaradan was a Babylonian military officer who served under King Nebuchadnezzar II. According to the Hebrew Bible, he played a significant role in the destruction of Jerusalem and the Babylonian exile of the Jewish people. Nebuzaradan is mentioned in the books of Jeremiah and 2 Kings.
