Etymology: The word "נבוכדנאצר" (Nebuchadnezzar) is of Babylonian origin. It is derived from the Akkadian name "Nabû-kudurri-uṣur," which means "O Nabu, protect the boundary."

Grammar: "נבוכדנאצר" is a proper noun in Hebrew. It is typically used to refer to the Babylonian king Nebuchadnezzar II.

Description: Nebuchadnezzar II was a prominent ruler of the Neo-Babylonian Empire from 605 BC to 562 BC. He is known for his military campaigns, including the conquest of Jerusalem and the destruction of the First Temple. Nebuchadnezzar is also mentioned in the Bible, particularly in the Book of Daniel. He is often depicted as a powerful and ambitious king who played a significant role in the ancient Near East.
