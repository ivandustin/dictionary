Grammar: "נביאיך" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix -ים (-im) to the singular form נביא (navi), which means "prophet." The suffix -יך (-echa) indicates the possessive form, meaning "your" or "of yours."

Description: "נביאיך" is used in Hebrew to refer to "your prophets" or "prophets of yours." It is often used in religious or biblical contexts to denote prophets who were sent by God to deliver messages or prophecies to the people.
