Etymology: The word "נביא" (pronounced "navi") comes from the Hebrew root נ-ב-א (n-b-a), which means "to speak" or "to announce." In Hebrew, a prophet is someone who speaks on behalf of God, delivering messages or prophecies.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "נביא" is a masculine noun. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. The plural form of "נביא" is "נביאים" (navi'im).

Description: "נביא" is a term used in Judaism to refer to a prophet. Prophets played a significant role in ancient Israelite society, serving as intermediaries between God and the people. They were believed to receive divine revelations and communicate God's will to the community. Prophets were often called upon to deliver messages of warning, guidance, or encouragement. Some well-known prophets in the Hebrew Bible include Moses, Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel.
