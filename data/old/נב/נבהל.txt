Etymology: The word "נבהל" (Nevhal) comes from the Hebrew root "בהל" (bahal), which means "to be alarmed" or "to be disturbed."

Grammar: "נבהל" (Nevhal) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני נבהל (ani nevhal) - I am alarmed
- Plural: אנחנו נבהלים (anachnu nevhalim) - We are alarmed

Description: The word "נבהל" (Nevhal) is used to describe a state of being alarmed, disturbed, or agitated. It can refer to both physical and emotional states of distress.
