Etymology: The word "נביא" (pronounced "navi") comes from the Hebrew root נ-ב-א, which means "to speak" or "to prophesy." In Hebrew, a prophet is someone who speaks on behalf of God, delivering messages, warnings, or teachings to the people.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "נביא" is a masculine noun. It is singular and can be used in both the definite and indefinite forms. The plural form of "נביא" is "נביאים" (navi'im).

Description: "נביא" refers to a prophet in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh). Prophets played a significant role in ancient Israelite society, acting as intermediaries between God and the people. They were chosen by God to deliver divine messages, provide guidance, and sometimes predict future events. Prophets were known for their spiritual insight, moral teachings, and their ability to inspire and lead the community. Some well-known prophets in the Hebrew Bible include Moses, Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel.
