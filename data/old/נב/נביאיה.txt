Etymology: The word "נביאה" (Nevi'ah) comes from the Hebrew root נ-ב-א (n-b-a), which means "to prophesy" or "to speak on behalf of God." In Hebrew, the suffix ה (ah) is added to indicate the feminine form.

Grammar: "נביאה" is a noun in the feminine form. It is used to refer to a female prophet or a woman who speaks on behalf of God.

Description: In Jewish tradition, a "נביאה" is a woman who is believed to have been chosen by God to receive divine messages and communicate them to others. These messages can include prophecies, teachings, or guidance. In the Hebrew Bible, there are several notable female prophets, such as Miriam, Deborah, and Huldah. The role of a "נביאה" is highly respected and carries great significance in Jewish religious and cultural contexts.
