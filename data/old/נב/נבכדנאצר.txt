Grammar: "נבקדנאצר" is a proper noun in Hebrew. It is typically used to refer to the Babylonian king Nebuchadnezzar II, who ruled from 605 to 562 BCE.

Description: Nebuchadnezzar II was a prominent figure in ancient history, known for his military campaigns and the conquest of Jerusalem. He is mentioned in various biblical texts, including the Book of Daniel. Nebuchadnezzar is often associated with the Babylonian exile of the Jewish people and the destruction of the First Temple in Jerusalem.
