Etymology: The word "נבוא" (pronounced "navua") comes from the Hebrew root נ-ב-א (n-b-a), which means "to prophesy" or "to speak on behalf of God." It is derived from the noun נָבִיא (navi), which means "prophet."

Grammar: "נבוא" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי נָבוֹא (ani navo) - I prophesy
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה נוֹבֵא (atah nove) - You (masculine) prophesy
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ נוֹבַעַת (at nova'at) - You (feminine) prophesy
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם נוֹבְאִים (atem nove'im) - You (plural, masculine) prophesy
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן נוֹבְאוֹת (aten nove'ot) - You (plural, feminine) prophesy

Description: The word "נבוא" refers to the act of prophesying or speaking on behalf of God. In Jewish tradition, a prophet is someone who receives divine messages and communicates them to the people. Prophets played a significant role in ancient Israelite society and continue to hold a revered position in Jewish religious texts and teachings.
