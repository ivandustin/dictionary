Etymology: The word "נבואה" (Nevuah) comes from the Hebrew root נ-ב-א (N-B-A), which means "to speak" or "to prophesy." It is derived from the noun נָבִיא (navi), which means "prophet."

Grammar: "נבואה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is usually used in the singular form, but it can also be used in the plural form "נְבוּאוֹת" (nevuot).

Description: "נבואה" refers to the concept of prophecy in Judaism. It is the ability to receive divine messages or revelations and communicate them to others. Prophets in the Hebrew Bible, such as Moses, Isaiah, and Jeremiah, are considered to have had the gift of prophecy.
