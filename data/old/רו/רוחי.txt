Etymology: The word "רוחי" (pronounced "ruchi") is a Hebrew word that means "my spirit" or "my breath." It is derived from the Hebrew root ר-ו-ח (r-w-ḥ), which means "to breathe" or "to blow."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "רוחי" is a possessive form of the noun "רוח" (ruach), which means "spirit" or "breath." The suffix "-י" (i) indicates that it is the possessive form, meaning "my spirit" or "my breath."

Description: "רוחי" is a personal and possessive term used to express ownership or possession of one's own spirit or breath. It can be used in various contexts, such as expressing emotions, desires, or personal experiences.
