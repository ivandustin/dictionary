Etymology: The word "רועי" (pronounced "ro'i") is a Hebrew name that means "shepherd" or "pastoral". It is derived from the Hebrew root ר.ע.ה (r.a.h), which means "to shepherd" or "to tend to a flock".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "רועי" is a masculine noun. It can be used as a name for a person, or to refer to a shepherd or someone who tends to a flock of animals.

Description: "רועי" is a name that has biblical origins and is commonly used in Hebrew-speaking communities. It carries connotations of care, protection, and guidance, as a shepherd is responsible for the well-being and safety of their flock.
