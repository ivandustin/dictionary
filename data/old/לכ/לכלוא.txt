Etymology: The word "לקלוא" (lekloa) is derived from the Hebrew root "ק.ל.א" (k.l.a), which means "to receive" or "to accept."

- אֲנִי לוֹקֵל (ani lokeil) - I receive
- אַתָּה לוֹקֵל (atah lokeil) - you (masculine singular) receive
- אַתְּ לוֹקֶלֶת (at lokelet) - you (feminine singular) receive
- הוּא לוֹקֵל (hu lokeil) - he receives
- הִיא לוֹקֶלֶת (hi lokelet) - she receives
- אֲנַחְנוּ לוֹקְלִים (anachnu lokelim) - we receive
- אַתֶּם לוֹקְלִים (atem lokelim) - you (masculine plural) receive
- אַתֶּן לוֹקְלוֹת (aten lokelot) - you (feminine plural) receive
- הֵם לוֹקְלִים (hem lokelim) - they (masculine) receive
- הֵן לוֹקְלוֹת (hen lokelot) - they (feminine) receive

Description: The verb "לקלוא" (lekloa) means "to receive" or "to accept" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of taking or accepting something that is given or offered.
