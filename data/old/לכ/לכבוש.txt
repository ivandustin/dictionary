Etymology: The word "לכבוש" (lekabosh) is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root כ.ב.ש (k.b.sh). 

Grammar: "לכבוש" is an infinitive verb form in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני מכביש (ani mekabesh) - I conquer, אתה מכביש (atah mekabesh) - you conquer (masculine singular), את מכבישה (at mekabeshah) - you conquer (feminine singular), הוא/היא מכביש (hu/hi mekabesh) - he/she conquers, אנחנו מכבישים (anachnu mekabeshim) - we conquer, אתם מכבישים (atem mekabeshim) - you conquer (masculine plural), אתן מכבישות (atan mekabeshot) - you conquer (feminine plural), הם/הן מכבישים (hem/hen mekabeshim) - they conquer.

Description: The word "לכבוש" means "to conquer" in English. It refers to the act of gaining control or dominance over something or someone through force or power.
