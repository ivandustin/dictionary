Etymology: The word "אפרע" (pronounced "afra") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root פרע, which means "to scatter" or "to disperse."

Grammar: אפרע is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני אפרע (ani afra) - I scatter
- Plural: אנחנו אפרעים (anachnu afra'im) - We scatter

Description: אפרע is an action word that describes the act of scattering or dispersing something. It can be used in various contexts, such as scattering seeds, dispersing a crowd, or spreading information.
