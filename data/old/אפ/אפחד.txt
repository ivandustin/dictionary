Etymology: The word "אפחד" (pronounced "afhad" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root פ-ח-ד (P-Kh-D), which means "to fear" or "to be afraid." 

Grammar: "אפחד" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the present tense. It is conjugated in the first person singular form. 

Description: The word "אפחד" translates to "I fear" or "I am afraid" in English. It expresses the feeling of being scared or having a sense of fear.
