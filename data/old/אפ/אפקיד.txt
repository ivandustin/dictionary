Etymology: The word "אפקיד" (pronounced "apkid" in Hebrew) is derived from the root פ.ק.ד (P.K.D) which means "to entrust" or "to commit". 

Grammar: אפקיד is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני אפקיד (ani apkid) - I entrust, אתה אפקיד (ata apkid) - you entrust (masculine singular), את אפקיד (at apkid) - you entrust (feminine singular), הוא אפקיד (hu apkid) - he entrusts, היא אפקידה (hi apkidah) - she entrusts, אנחנו אפקידים (anachnu apkidim) - we entrust, אתם אפקידים (atem apkidim) - you entrust (masculine plural), אתן אפקידות (atan apkidot) - you entrust (feminine plural), הם אפקידים (hem apkidim) - they entrust (masculine), הן אפקידות (hen apkidot) - they entrust (feminine).

Description: אפקיד is a verb that means "to entrust" or "to commit" in Hebrew. It is used to express the act of giving someone or something into the care or responsibility of another person. It can be used in various contexts, such as entrusting a task, responsibility, or possession to someone.
