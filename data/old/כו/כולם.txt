Etymology: The word "כולם" (kolam) is a Hebrew word that means "everyone" or "all of them". It is derived from the Hebrew root כ.ל.ה (k.l.h), which means "to complete" or "to include".

Grammar: "כולם" (kolam) is a plural form of the word "כל" (kol), which means "all" or "every". It is used to refer to a group of people or things, indicating that every individual in the group is included.

Description: "כולם" (kolam) is a versatile word that is commonly used in Hebrew to express the idea of inclusiveness or totality. It can be used in various contexts, such as when referring to a group of people, objects, or concepts. For example, "כולם אהבו את הסרט" (kolam ahavu et haseret) means "everyone loved the movie".
