Etymology: The word "כושל" (koshel) comes from the Hebrew root כ-ש-ל (k-sh-l), which means "to stumble" or "to falter."

Grammar: "כושל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני כושל (ani koshel) - I stumble
- Plural: אנחנו כושלים (anachnu koshelim) - We stumble

Description: "כושל" is used to describe the act of stumbling or faltering physically, mentally, or emotionally. It can refer to tripping or losing balance while walking, making a mistake or error in judgment, or experiencing difficulties or setbacks in life.
