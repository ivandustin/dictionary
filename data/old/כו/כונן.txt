- Singular: אֲנִי כוֹנֵן (ani konen) - I establish
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה כוֹנֵן (ata konen) - You (masculine) establish
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ כוֹנֶנֶת (at konenet) - You (feminine) establish
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם כוֹנְנִים (atem konnim) - You (masculine plural) establish
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן כוֹנְנוֹת (aten konnot) - You (feminine plural) establish
- Masculine singular: הוּא כוֹנֵן (hu konen) - He establishes
- Feminine singular: הִיא כוֹנֶנֶת (hi konenet) - She establishes
- Masculine plural: הֵם כוֹנְנִים (hem konnim) - They (masculine) establish
- Feminine plural: הֵן כוֹנְנוֹת (hen konnot) - They (feminine) establish

Description: The verb "כונן" means "to establish" or "to prepare" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the act of setting up or arranging something in a proper or organized manner. It can also refer to the process of planning or getting ready for a specific purpose or goal.
