Etymology: The word "כוסי" (kosi) comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root word "כוס" (kos), which means "cup" or "goblet".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "כוסי" (kosi) is a possessive adjective that means "my cup" or "my goblet". It is formed by adding the possessive suffix "-י" (i) to the noun "כוס" (kos).

Description: "כוסי" (kosi) is a word used to indicate possession or ownership of a cup or goblet. It is commonly used in Hebrew to express the idea of "my cup" or "my goblet" in a possessive sense.
