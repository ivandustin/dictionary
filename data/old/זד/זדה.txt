Etymology: The word "זְדָה" (z'dah) comes from the Hebrew root ז־ד־ה (z-d-h), which means "to be insolent" or "to act presumptuously."

Grammar: זְדָה is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the feminine form and is singular.

Description: זְדָה refers to someone who acts with insolence, arrogance, or presumption. It describes a person who behaves in a way that disregards the rights or feelings of others and shows a lack of respect or humility.
