Etymology: The word "דניאל" (Daniel) is of Hebrew origin. It is derived from the Hebrew name דָּן (Dan) meaning "God is my judge" and אֵל (El) meaning "God". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, "דניאל" (Daniel) is a masculine proper noun. It is typically used as a given name for boys.

Description: Daniel is a biblical name that appears in the Old Testament. In the Bible, Daniel was a prophet and a wise man who served in the court of King Nebuchadnezzar in Babylon. He is known for his interpretation of dreams and his unwavering faith in God. The name Daniel is still commonly used today in many cultures and has variations in different languages.
