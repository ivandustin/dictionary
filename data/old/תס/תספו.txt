Etymology: The word "ספו" (pronounced "sefu") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ס.פ.ר (s.p.r), which means "to count" or "to tell."

Grammar: "ספו" is a verb in the imperative form. It is the second person plural masculine command form of the verb "לספר" (lispor), which means "to count" or "to tell."

Description: "ספו" is used to command a group of people (masculine) to count or tell something. It is often used in a context where there is a need to count or recount items, or to tell a story or information.
