Etymology: The word "לצרוף" (litzrof) is derived from the Hebrew root צ.ר.פ (ts.r.p), which means "to join" or "to connect."

Grammar: "לצרוף" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- אני מצריף (ani m'tzarif) - I join
- אתה מצריף (atah m'tzarif) - You join (masculine singular)
- את מצריפה (at m'tzarifah) - You join (feminine singular)
- הוא/היא מצריף/ה (hu/hi m'tzarif/a) - He/She joins
- אנחנו מצריפים (anachnu m'tzarifim) - We join
- אתם מצריפים (atem m'tzarifim) - You join (masculine plural)
- אתן מצריפות (atan m'tzarifot) - You join (feminine plural)
- הם/הן מצריפים/ות (hem/hen m'tzarifim/ot) - They join

Description: "לצרוף" means "to join" or "to connect" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of bringing together separate parts or elements to form a unified whole. This verb can be used in various contexts, such as joining pieces of a puzzle, connecting wires, or combining ingredients in cooking.
