Grammar: "חרבה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used with definite or indefinite articles. In plural form, it becomes "חרבות" (charbot).

Description: "חרבה" refers to a desert or a barren land. It is often used to describe arid regions with little or no vegetation. In a broader sense, it can also refer to a desolate or uninhabited place. The term is commonly used in the context of the Negev Desert in Israel.
