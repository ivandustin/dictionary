Etymology: The word "חרשי" (pronounced "charshi") comes from the Hebrew root ח-ר-שׁ, which means "to engrave" or "to carve." It is derived from the noun חֵרֶשׁ (cheresh), which means "potter" or "craftsman."

Grammar: "חרשי" is an adjective in Hebrew. It is used to describe something that is made or crafted by a potter or craftsman. It can also refer to the art of pottery or craftsmanship.
