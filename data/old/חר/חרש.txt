Etymology: The word "חרש" (pronounced "charash" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root ח-ר-ש (ch-r-sh), which means "to engrave" or "to carve". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, חרש is a noun that means "craftsman" or "artisan". It is also used as a verb, meaning "to work" or "to create". 

Description: חרש refers to someone who is skilled in a specific craft or trade, such as a carpenter, blacksmith, or potter. It can also be used more broadly to describe any skilled worker or artist. The term emphasizes the craftsmanship and expertise of the individual in their chosen field.
