Etymology: The word "חרצים" (pronounced "charatzim") comes from the Hebrew root ח.ר.צ (ch.r.ts), which means "to dig" or "to carve". 

Grammar: "חרצים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is the plural form of the singular noun "חרץ" (charatz), which means "a carving" or "an engraving". 

Description: "חרצים" refers to carvings or engravings made on various materials, such as wood, stone, or metal. It can also be used metaphorically to describe intricate or detailed workmanship.
