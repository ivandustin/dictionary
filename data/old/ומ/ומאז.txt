Etymology: The word "וּמָאַז" (pronounced "u-ma'az" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root אזז (azaz), which means "to be strong" or "to prevail." The prefix וּ (u) means "and," indicating a conjunction.

Grammar: "וּמָאַז" is a conjunction in Hebrew, used to connect two clauses or phrases. It is often translated as "and since" or "and from then on."

Description: "וּמָאַז" is used to express a cause-and-effect relationship between two events or actions. It implies that the second event or action occurred as a result of the first one. It is commonly used in biblical and classical Hebrew texts.
