Etymology: The word "וְמִפְי" is a Hebrew word that means "and from the mouth of".

Grammar: "וְמִפְי" is a prepositional phrase composed of the conjunction "וְ" (meaning "and") and the preposition "מִפְי" (meaning "from the mouth of"). It is used to indicate the source or origin of something.

Description: "וְמִפְי" is commonly used in Hebrew to introduce a quotation or to attribute something to a specific speaker. It is often used in religious texts to indicate that a statement or commandment is coming directly from God.
