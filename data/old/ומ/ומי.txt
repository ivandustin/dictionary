Etymology: The word "וְמִי" (pronounced "u-mi") is a Hebrew word that means "and who." It is a combination of the conjunction "וְ" (pronounced "ve") meaning "and" and the interrogative pronoun "מִי" (pronounced "mi") meaning "who."

Grammar: "וְמִי" is a conjunction used in Hebrew to connect two clauses or phrases. It is commonly used to introduce a question or inquiry about a person or identity.

Description: "וְמִי" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is often used in biblical texts and modern Hebrew to ask questions about someone's identity, such as "וְמִי אַתָּה?" (Who are you?). It can also be used to express curiosity or uncertainty about a person or thing.
