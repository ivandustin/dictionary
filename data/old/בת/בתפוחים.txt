Grammar: "בתפוחים" (betapuchim) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the plural suffix "-ים" (-im) to the singular noun "תפוח" (tapuach). The preposition "ב" (bet) indicates location or being "in" something.

Description: The word "בתפוחים" (betapuchim) is used to describe a location or state of being "in apples." It can be used literally to refer to being physically inside a group of apples or metaphorically to describe being immersed or surrounded by something.
