Grammar: "בְּתוֹרֶת" (betoret) is a preposition in Hebrew. It is composed of the preposition "בְּ" (be), which means "in" or "with," and the noun "תוֹרָה" (torah), which means "law" or "instruction." Together, they form the phrase "in the law" or "with the law."

Description: "בְּתוֹרֶת" (betoret) is used to indicate that something is done in accordance with the law or is based on the principles of the law. It is often used in religious or legal contexts to refer to actions or decisions that are in line with the teachings of the Torah or Jewish law.
