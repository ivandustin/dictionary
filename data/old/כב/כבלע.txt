Etymology: The word "כבלע" (pronounced "kavla") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root כ-ב-ל, which means "to swallow" or "to gulp down."

Grammar: "כבלע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני כובלע (ani kavla) - I swallow
- Plural: אנחנו כובלעים (anachnu kavla'im) - We swallow

Description: "כבלע" is an action verb that describes the act of swallowing or gulping down something. It is often used metaphorically to express the idea of accepting or internalizing information or ideas.
