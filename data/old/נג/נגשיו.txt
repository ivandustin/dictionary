Etymology: The word "נגשיו" (nagshav) is derived from the Hebrew root נ.ג.ש (n.g.sh), which means "to approach" or "to draw near."

Grammar: "נגשיו" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is the plural form of the word "נגש" (nagash), which means "messenger" or "emissary." The suffix "יו" (av) indicates possession, so "נגשיו" can be translated as "his messengers" or "their messengers."

Description: "נגשיו" refers to a group of messengers or emissaries. In biblical times, messengers were often sent to deliver important messages or to represent someone in a specific mission or task. They played a crucial role in communication and diplomacy.
