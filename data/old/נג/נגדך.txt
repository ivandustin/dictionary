Etymology: The word "נגד" (nagad) comes from the Hebrew root נ.ג.ד (n.g.d), which means "to tell" or "to inform." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, נגד is a verb that belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי נוֹגֵד (ani noged) - I tell
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה נוֹגֵד (ata noged) - You tell
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ נוֹגֶדֶת (at nogedet) - You tell
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם נוֹגְדִים (atem nogdim) - You tell
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן נוֹגְדוֹת (aten nogdot) - You tell

Description: נגד is a Hebrew verb that means "to tell" or "to inform." It is often used to express the act of conveying information or sharing news with someone.
