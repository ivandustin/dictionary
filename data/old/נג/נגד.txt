Etymology: The word "נגד" (nagad) comes from the Hebrew root נ-ג-ד (n-g-d), which means "to oppose" or "to confront."

Grammar: In Hebrew, נגד is a verb that belongs to the binyan (verb structure) called "piel." It is conjugated in various tenses and forms to indicate different grammatical aspects.

Description: נגד is a versatile word that can have multiple meanings depending on the context. It can mean "to oppose," "to confront," "to contradict," or "to resist." It is often used to describe a situation where someone or something is standing against or challenging another person or thing.
