Etymology: The word "נגעים" (nega'im) is of Hebrew origin.

Grammar: "נגעים" (nega'im) is a noun in the plural form. It is derived from the root word "נגע" (naga), which means "to touch" or "to strike."

Description: In Hebrew, "נגעים" (nega'im) refers to "plagues" or "afflictions." It is often used in the context of the biblical narrative, specifically in relation to the ten plagues that were inflicted upon Egypt in the book of Exodus. These plagues were seen as divine punishments for the oppression of the Israelites.
