Etymology: The word "פסק" (pronounced "pasak" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root פ-ס-ק, which means "to cut" or "to divide." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, פסק is a noun that means "a break" or "a pause." It is commonly used in the context of punctuation marks, such as the period (סימן פסיק) or the comma (סימן פסיק קטן), which indicate a pause or separation between words or phrases in a sentence. 

Description: פסק is an important element in Hebrew grammar and punctuation. It helps to clarify the structure and meaning of sentences by indicating where breaks or pauses should occur. The use of פסק can greatly affect the interpretation of a text, as it determines the flow and rhythm of the language.
