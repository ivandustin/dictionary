Etymology: The word "גוער" (pronounced "go'er") comes from the Hebrew root ג.ע.ר, which means "to rebuke" or "to scold". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, גוער is a verb in the present tense, masculine singular form. It can also be conjugated to match different genders, numbers, and tenses.

Description: גוער is used to describe the act of rebuking or scolding someone. It implies a strong and forceful expression of disapproval or criticism towards another person's behavior or actions.
