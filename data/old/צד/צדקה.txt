Etymology: The word "צדקה" (Tzedakah) comes from the Hebrew root צ.ד.ק (Tz.D.K), which means "justice" or "righteousness". It is derived from the Hebrew word צֶדֶק (Tzedek), meaning "justice" or "righteousness".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "צדקה" (Tzedakah) is a feminine noun. It is usually used in the singular form, but can also be used in the plural form "צדקות" (Tzedakot).

Description: Tzedakah is a Hebrew term that refers to the act of giving charity or performing acts of righteousness and justice. In Jewish tradition, Tzedakah is considered a moral obligation and a fundamental part of living a just and ethical life. It involves giving to those in need, supporting the poor, and contributing to the welfare of the community. Tzedakah can take various forms, including donating money, providing food or clothing, volunteering time, or offering assistance to those in need. It is seen as a way to promote social justice, alleviate suffering, and create a more equitable society.
