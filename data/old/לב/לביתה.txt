Etymology: The word "לביתה" (lebeitah) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "ל" (le) meaning "to" or "towards" and "ביתה" (beitah) meaning "her house". 

Grammar: "לביתה" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ל" (le) indicates direction or movement towards something, and "ביתה" (beitah) is the possessive form of the noun "בית" (beit) meaning "house". Together, "לביתה" means "to her house" or "towards her house".

Description: "לביתה" is used to indicate movement or direction towards a female's house. It can be used in various contexts, such as when someone is going to visit a female friend or family member at her house.
