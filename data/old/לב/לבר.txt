Etymology: The word "לבר" (pronounced "levar") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root word "לברר" which means "to clarify" or "to purify."

Grammar: "לבר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) which is the most common verb conjugation pattern in Hebrew. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:

- אני לבר (ani levar) - I clarify/purify
- אתה לבר (ata levar) - You clarify/purify (masculine singular)
- את לברי (at levari) - You clarify/purify (feminine singular)
- הוא לבר (hu levar) - He clarifies/purifies
- היא לברה (hi levra) - She clarifies/purifies
- אנחנו לברים (anachnu levarim) - We clarify/purify
- אתם לברים (atem levarim) - You clarify/purify (masculine plural)
- אתן לברות (aten levarot) - You clarify/purify (feminine plural)
- הם לברים (hem levarim) - They clarify/purify (masculine)
- הן לברות (hen levarot) - They clarify/purify (feminine)

Description: The word "לבר" is used to describe the act of clarifying or purifying something. It can be used in various contexts, such as purifying water, clarifying a concept, or refining a process.
