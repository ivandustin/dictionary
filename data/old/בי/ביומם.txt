Etymology: The word "בְּיוֹם" (pronounced "b'yom") is a Hebrew word that means "in the day" or "on the day." It is derived from the Hebrew root word "יום" (pronounced "yom"), which means "day."

Grammar: "בְּיוֹם" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. It consists of the preposition "בְּ" (pronounced "b'") meaning "in" or "on," and the noun "יוֹם" meaning "day." It is commonly used to indicate a specific time or event that occurs during a particular day.

Description: "בְּיוֹם" is a versatile phrase that is used in various contexts in the Hebrew language. It can be used to express the concept of "on the day" or "during the day" when referring to a specific day or time period. For example, "בְּיוֹם הַשַּׁבָּת" (pronounced "b'yom ha-shabbat") means "on the Sabbath day." It can also be used to indicate a general time frame, such as "בְּיוֹם חַג" (pronounced "b'yom chag") meaning "during the holiday." Overall, "בְּיוֹם" is an essential phrase in Hebrew for expressing temporal relationships.
