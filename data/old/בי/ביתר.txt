Etymology: The word "ביתר" (pronounced "beytar") is a Hebrew word that means "to excel" or "to surpass." It is derived from the Hebrew root ב-ת-ר (B-T-R), which conveys the idea of being better or superior.

Grammar: In Hebrew, "ביתר" is a verb in the infinitive form. It can be conjugated to match different subjects and tenses. For example, in the present tense, "I excel" would be אני מַתְבִּיר (ani matbir), while "you excel" would be אתה מַתְבִּיר (atah matbir).

Description: The word "ביתר" is often used to describe someone or something that excels or surpasses others in a particular area. It can refer to surpassing in skills, abilities, achievements, or any other aspect. It implies being better or superior compared to others.
