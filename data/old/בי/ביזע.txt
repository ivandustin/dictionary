Etymology: The word "ביזע" (bizea) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root בזע, which means "to despise" or "to scorn". 

Grammar: "ביזע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני בוזע (ani bozea) - I despise, אתה בוזע (ata bozea) - you despise (masculine singular), את בוזעת (at bozea) - you despise (feminine singular), הוא בוזע (hu bozea) - he despises, היא בוזעת (hi bozea) - she despises, אנחנו בוזעים (anachnu bozeim) - we despise, אתם בוזעים (atem bozeim) - you despise (masculine plural), אתן בוזעות (atan bozeot) - you despise (feminine plural), הם בוזעים (hem bozeim) - they despise (masculine), הן בוזעות (hen bozeot) - they despise (feminine).

Description: The word "ביזע" is used to describe the act of despising or scorning someone or something. It conveys a strong negative feeling towards the subject.
