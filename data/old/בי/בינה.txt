Etymology: The word "בינה" (Binah) comes from the Hebrew root בנה (B-N-H), which means "to understand" or "to discern." It is derived from the noun בין (bin), meaning "between" or "among."

Grammar: "בינה" is a feminine noun in Hebrew. It is usually used as a singular noun, but it can also be used in the plural form "בינות" (binot).

Description: In Hebrew, "בינה" refers to the intellectual ability to comprehend, understand, and discern. It is often associated with wisdom, insight, and deep understanding. "בינה" is considered one of the ten sefirot (divine emanations) in Jewish mysticism, representing understanding and knowledge. It is also used in various contexts to describe intelligence, perception, and discernment.
