Etymology: The word "בידך" (pronounced "beyadcha") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root word "יד" (pronounced "yad"), which means "hand". The suffix "ך" (pronounced "cha") indicates possession, so "בידך" literally translates to "in your hand" or "in your possession".

Grammar: "בידך" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition "ב" (pronounced "be") meaning "in" or "at", and the pronoun "ידך" meaning "your hand". It is used to indicate possession or control over something.

It can be used to express ownership or control over something, as in "הכסף בידך" (pronounced "hakesef beyadcha"), meaning "the money is in your hand".
