Etymology: The word "שָׁעוּ" (sha'u) is a Hebrew verb that means "to ask" or "to inquire." It is derived from the root שׁ.א.ל (sh.a.l), which conveys the idea of seeking or requesting information.

Grammar: "שָׁעוּ" is the plural form of the verb, used when referring to multiple individuals or a group of people. It is conjugated in the past tense and is commonly used in the third person.

Description: "שָׁעוּ" is a verb that signifies the act of asking or inquiring. It is often used to express the action of seeking information, advice, or clarification from someone else. In Hebrew, asking questions is highly valued, as it promotes learning, understanding, and communication.
