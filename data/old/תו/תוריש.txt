- Singular:

  - 2nd person: אַתָּה תוֹרֵשׁ (atah toresh) - You (masculine singular) inherit
  - 3rd person: הוּא תוֹרֵשׁ (hu toresh) - He inherits, הִיא תוֹרֶשֶׁת (hi torasht) - She inherits

- Plural:

  - 2nd person: אַתֶּם תוֹרְשִׁים (atem torshim) - You (masculine plural) inherit
  - 3rd person: הֵם תוֹרְשִׁים (hem torshim) - They (masculine) inherit, הֵן תוֹרְשִׁים (hen torshim) - They (feminine) inherit

Description: "תוריש" (Torish) means "to inherit" in Hebrew. It refers to the act of receiving or obtaining something, typically property or possessions, from a predecessor or ancestor. It can also be used metaphorically to describe the passing down of traits, characteristics, or traditions from one generation to another.
