Etymology: The word "תושיע" (toshi'a) comes from the Hebrew root י-ש-ע (y-sh-a), which means "to save" or "to deliver." It is derived from the noun תְּשׁוּעָה (teshu'ah), meaning "salvation" or "deliverance."

Grammar: "תושיע" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the future tense. It is conjugated in the second person singular masculine form. The verb is used to address someone and means "you will save" or "you will deliver."

Description: "תושיע" is a powerful word that conveys the act of saving or delivering someone from harm, danger, or distress. It implies a sense of rescue and protection. In Hebrew, it is often used in a religious or spiritual context, referring to God's ability to save and deliver His people.
