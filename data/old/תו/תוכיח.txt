Etymology: The word "תוכיח" (Tukhiach) is derived from the Hebrew root י-כ-ח (Y-Kh-Kh), which means "to prove" or "to demonstrate".

Grammar: "תוכיח" (Tukhiach) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מוכיח (Ani mokhiach) - I prove
- Singular feminine: אני מוכיחה (Ani mokhiacha) - I prove
- Plural masculine: אנחנו מוכיחים (Anakhnu mokhiachim) - We prove
- Plural feminine: אנחנו מוכיחות (Anakhnu mokhiachot) - We prove

Description: "תוכיח" (Tukhiach) means "to prove" or "to demonstrate" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of providing evidence or arguments to support a claim or statement.
