Etymology: The word "צואתיו" comes from the Hebrew root צ.ו.א (ts.w.a), which means "to excrete" or "to evacuate." The suffix תִיו (-tiyu) indicates possession or belonging, so צואתיו literally means "his excrement" or "his waste."

Grammar: צואתיו is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the masculine gender and singular number. The suffix יו (-yu) indicates possession, making it a possessive noun.

Description: צואתיו refers to the waste or excrement of a person. It is commonly used to describe feces or bodily waste material.
