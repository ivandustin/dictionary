Etymology: The word "תועה" (to'eha) comes from the Hebrew root ת.ע.ה (T.A.H), which means "to wander" or "to stray." It is derived from the noun תֵּעָה (te'ah), which means "error" or "mistake."

Grammar: "תועה" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the singular form and can be used to refer to a person who wanders or strays from the right path, both literally and metaphorically.

Description: "תועה" is often used to describe someone who is lost, confused, or misguided. It can refer to someone who is physically lost in a place or someone who is mentally or morally lost in life. The word carries a sense of aimlessness and lack of direction.
