Etymology: The word "תוקל" (Tukel) is derived from the Hebrew root ת-ק-ל (T-Q-L), which means "to stumble" or "to trip." 

Grammar: "תוקל" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the past tense form. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form. 

Description: "תוקל" is used to describe the action of stumbling or tripping, both literally and metaphorically. It can refer to physically stumbling over an obstacle or experiencing difficulties or setbacks in life.
