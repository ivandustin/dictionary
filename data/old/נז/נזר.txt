Grammar: In Hebrew, "נזר" (nazar) is a noun that is used to describe a person who is consecrated or dedicated to a specific religious or spiritual purpose. It can also be used as a verb, meaning "to consecrate" or "to set apart."

Description: "נזר" (nazar) is a term used in the Hebrew Bible to refer to individuals who take a vow of consecration to God. These individuals voluntarily separate themselves from certain activities or behaviors for a specific period of time. During this time, they are considered holy and are expected to adhere to certain rules and restrictions.
