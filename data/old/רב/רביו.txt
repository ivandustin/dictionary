Etymology: The word "רביע" (pronounced "ravi'a" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew root ר-ב-ע, which means "to be full" or "to be complete." It is derived from the noun רֶבַע (reva), meaning "quarter" or "fourth."

Grammar: In Hebrew, רביע is a masculine noun that is used to describe the number "fourth" or "quarter." It can be used as a standalone noun or as an ordinal number.

Description: רביע is used to indicate the position of something in a sequence or to divide something into four equal parts. For example, it can be used to describe the fourth day of the week (יום רביעי, yom ravi'i) or to refer to a quarter of a year (רבעון, reva'on).
