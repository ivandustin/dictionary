Etymology: The word "יסיר" (pronounced "yaseer") is derived from the Hebrew root ס.ו.ר (s.w.r), which means "to remove" or "to take away."

- אֲנִי מוֹסִיר (ani mosir) - I remove
- אַתָּה מוֹסִיר (atah mosir) - You (masculine singular) remove
- אַתְּ מוֹסֶרֶת (at moseret) - You (feminine singular) remove
- הוּא מוֹסִיר (hu mosir) - He removes
- הִיא מוֹסֶרֶת (hi moseret) - She removes
- אֲנַחְנוּ מוֹסְרִים (anachnu mosrim) - We remove
- אַתֶּם מוֹסְרִים (atem mosrim) - You (masculine plural) remove
- אַתֶּן מוֹסְרוֹת (aten mosrot) - You (feminine plural) remove
- הֵם מוֹסְרִים (hem mosrim) - They (masculine) remove
- הֵן מוֹסְרוֹת (hen mosrot) - They (feminine) remove

Description: The word "יסיר" means "to remove" or "to take away" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of physically or metaphorically taking something away from a person, place, or situation.
