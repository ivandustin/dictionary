Etymology: The word "יסרני" (pronounced "yisreni") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root י-ס-ר (y-s-r), which means "to discipline" or "to correct."

Grammar: "יסרני" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the causative form, indicating that the subject is causing someone else to be disciplined or corrected. The word is conjugated in the first person singular, present tense, and imperative mood.

Description: The word "יסרני" is a command that can be translated as "discipline me" or "correct me." It is used when someone wants to ask for guidance, correction, or discipline from another person. It implies a desire for personal growth and improvement.
