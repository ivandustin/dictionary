Etymology: The word "נפוצה" (Nefutzah) comes from the Hebrew root נ.פ.צ (N.P.Tz), which means "to scatter" or "to disperse." It is derived from the noun נֵפֶץ (nefetz), meaning "fragment" or "splinter."

Grammar: נפוצה is the feminine singular form of the adjective נְפוּצָה (nefutzah), which means "scattered" or "dispersed." It is used to describe something that is spread out or scattered in various places.

Description: נפוצה is often used to describe things that are widely spread or scattered, either physically or metaphorically. It can refer to objects or ideas that are found in different locations or are not concentrated in one place.
