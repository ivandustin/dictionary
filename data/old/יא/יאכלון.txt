Etymology: The word "יאכלון" (pronounced "ya'akhlun") is a Hebrew verb in the future tense, derived from the root י-א-כ-ל (y-a-kh-l), which means "to eat."

Grammar: "יאכלון" is the third person plural future tense form of the verb יאכל (ya'akhel), which means "they will eat." In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject and tense of the sentence.

Description: "יאכלון" is used to describe an action that will be performed by a group of people or things in the future. It indicates that "they" (the subject) will eat.
