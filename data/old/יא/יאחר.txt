Etymology: The word "יאחר" (pronounced "ya'acher") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root י-ח-ר (Y-Ch-R), which means "to delay" or "to be late."

Grammar: "יאחר" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני מְאַחֵר (ani me'acher) - I am delaying
- Masculine singular: אתה מְאַחֵר (atah me'acher) - You (masculine) are delaying
- Feminine singular: את מְאַחֶרֶת (at me'acheret) - You (feminine) are delaying
- Masculine plural: אתם מְאַחְרִים (atem me'achrim) - You (plural, masculine) are delaying
- Feminine plural: אתן מְאַחֲרוֹת (atan me'acharot) - You (plural, feminine) are delaying

Description: The word "יאחר" means "to delay" or "to be late" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the action of postponing or not being on time for something.
