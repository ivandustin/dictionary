Etymology: The word "כתובים" (Ketuvim) comes from the Hebrew root כתב (k-t-v), which means "to write." It is derived from the Hebrew word כתוב (katuv), which means "written."

Grammar: "כתובים" is the plural form of the Hebrew word כתוב (katuv), which means "written." In Hebrew grammar, it is a masculine plural noun.

Description: "כתובים" is a term used in Judaism to refer to the third and final section of the Hebrew Bible, known as the Tanakh. The Ketuvim consists of various books, including Psalms, Proverbs, Job, Song of Songs, Ruth, Lamentations, Ecclesiastes, Esther, Daniel, Ezra, Nehemiah, and Chronicles. These books are considered sacred scripture and are often referred to as the "Writings" or "Hagiographa" in English.
