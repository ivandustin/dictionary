Etymology: The word "כתיבים" (pronounced "ktavim") is a Hebrew word that means "writings" or "scriptures." It is derived from the Hebrew root כתב (pronounced "k-t-v"), which means "to write."

Grammar: "כתיבים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to the collection of sacred writings in Judaism, including the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings (such as Psalms, Proverbs, and Job).

Description: The term "כתיבים" is commonly used in Jewish religious and scholarly contexts to refer to the third section of the Hebrew Bible, also known as the Tanakh. The כתיבים section contains various books that provide wisdom, poetry, and historical accounts. These writings are considered sacred and authoritative in Judaism.
