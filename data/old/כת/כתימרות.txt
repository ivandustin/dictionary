Etymology: The word "כתימרות" (pronounced "k'timarot") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root כ-ת-מ-ר, which means "to hide" or "to conceal." It is derived from the noun תמרה (pronounced "timrah"), which means "a hiding place" or "a secret."

Grammar: "כתימרות" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ות (-ot) to the singular form כתימרה (k'timrah). The word is feminine and is used to refer to multiple instances of hiding or concealing.

Description: "כתימרות" refers to the act of hiding or concealing something. It can be used in various contexts, such as hiding a secret, concealing information, or keeping something hidden from others. The word carries the connotation of secrecy and can imply that the hidden information or object is not easily accessible or known to others.
