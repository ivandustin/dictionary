Etymology: The word "ללין" (lalin) is derived from the Hebrew root "ל.ל.נ" (l.l.n), which means "to stay" or "to spend the night."

Grammar: "ללין" (lalin) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני לולן (ani lolen) - I stay
- Singular feminine: אני לולנת (ani lolenet) - I stay
- Plural masculine: אנחנו לולנים (anachnu lolanim) - We stay
- Plural feminine: אנחנו לולנות (anachnu lolanot) - We stay

Description: The word "ללין" (lalin) refers to the act of staying or spending the night in a certain place. It can be used in various contexts, such as staying at a hotel, a friend's house, or any other location.
