Etymology: The word "ממהר" (mamher) is derived from the Hebrew root מ-ה-ר (m-h-r), which means "to hasten" or "to hurry."

Grammar: "ממהר" (mamher) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני ממהר (ani mamher) - I hurry
- Plural: אנחנו ממהרים (anachnu mamherim) - We hurry

Description: "ממהר" (mamher) is used to describe the action of moving or acting quickly, without delay. It can refer to physical movement or to completing a task promptly.
