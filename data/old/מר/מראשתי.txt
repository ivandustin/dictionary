Etymology: The word "מראשתי" (marashti) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ראש (rosh), which means "head" or "beginning". The prefix מ (me) indicates "from" or "out of". Therefore, "מראשתי" can be translated as "from my head" or "from the beginning".

Grammar: "מראשתי" is a first-person singular verb in the past tense. It is derived from the root ראש (rosh) and the prefix מ (me). The suffix תי (ti) indicates that the action is performed by the speaker.

Description: "מראשתי" is used to express that something was done or decided by the speaker from the very beginning or without any external influence. It implies a sense of personal initiative and responsibility.
