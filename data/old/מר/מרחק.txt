Etymology: The word "מרחק" (marhak) comes from the Hebrew root ר-ח-ק (r-ch-q), which means "to distance" or "to be far". 

Grammar: In Hebrew, "מרחק" is a noun that means "distance" or "gap". It is a masculine noun and can be used in both singular and plural forms. 

Description: "מרחק" refers to the physical or metaphorical space between two objects or concepts. It can describe the physical distance between two locations, the emotional or relational distance between people, or the gap between two ideas or perspectives.
