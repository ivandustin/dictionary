Etymology: The word "מרגלים" (meraglim) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root רגל (ragal), which means "to spy" or "to scout". The prefix מ (mem) indicates plural form, so "מרגלים" literally means "spies" or "scouts".

Grammar: "מרגלים" is a masculine plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the plural suffix ים (im) to the root רגל (ragal). In Hebrew, nouns have different forms depending on their gender, number, and function in a sentence.

Description: "מרגלים" refers to spies or scouts. In the context of the Hebrew Bible, it specifically refers to the twelve spies sent by Moses to scout the land of Canaan before the Israelites entered it. These spies were known as the "מרגלים" or "spies" of the Israelites.
