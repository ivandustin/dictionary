Etymology: The word "תַּעַשׁ" (ta'ash) comes from the Hebrew root עָשָׂה (asah), which means "to do" or "to make."

Grammar: "תַּעַשׁ" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the second person masculine singular form of the imperative mood, which is used to give commands or instructions.

Description: "תַּעַשׁ" is translated as "do" or "make" in English. It is used to command or instruct someone to perform an action.
