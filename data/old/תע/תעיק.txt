Etymology: The word "תַּעֲקֵיף" (ta'akif) comes from the Hebrew root עקף (ayin-kuf-peh), which means "to surround" or "to encircle."

Grammar: "תַּעֲקֵיף" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי תַּעֲקֵיף (ani ta'akif) - I surround
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה תַּעֲקֵיף (atah ta'akif) - You (masculine) surround
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ תַּעֲקְפִי (at ta'akfi) - You (feminine) surround
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם תַּעֲקְפוּ (atem ta'akfu) - You (masculine plural) surround
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן תַּעֲקְפֵנָה (aten ta'akfenah) - You (feminine plural) surround

Description: The word "תַּעֲקֵיף" means "to surround" or "to encircle" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the action of encircling or surrounding something or someone.
