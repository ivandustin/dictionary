Etymology: The word "תַּעֲנִיק" (ta'anik) comes from the Hebrew root ענק (ayin-nun-kuf), which means "to grant" or "to bestow."

Grammar: "תַּעֲנִיק" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי תַּעֲנִיק (ani ta'anik) - I grant
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה תַּעֲנִיק (atah ta'anik) - You (masculine) grant
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ תַּעֲנִיק (at ta'anik) - You (feminine) grant
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם תַּעֲנִיקוּ (atem ta'aniku) - You (masculine plural) grant
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן תַּעֲנִיקְנָה (aten ta'anikna) - You (feminine plural) grant

Description: The word "תַּעֲנִיק" means "to grant" or "to bestow" in Hebrew. It is used to express the act of giving or providing something to someone.
