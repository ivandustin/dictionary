Etymology: The word "אחרי" (pronounced "achari") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root א-ח-ר, which means "after" or "behind."

Grammar: "אחרי" is a preposition in Hebrew. It is used to indicate the concept of "after" or "following" in relation to time, place, or order. It is often used to express the idea of something happening or being located after or behind something else.

Description: "אחרי" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. For example, it can be used to describe the sequence of events, such as "אחרי הארוחה אני הולך לישון" (After dinner, I go to sleep). It can also be used to describe the location of something, such as "הבית שלי נמצא אחרי הגן" (My house is located behind the garden). Overall, "אחרי" is an important word in Hebrew that helps convey the concept of "after" or "behind" in different contexts.
