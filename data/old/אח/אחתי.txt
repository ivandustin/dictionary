Etymology: The word "אחתי" (pronounced "achoti") is a Hebrew word that means "my sister". It is derived from the Hebrew root א.ח.ת (A.Ch.T), which means "sister".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אחתי" is a possessive form of the word "אחות" (achot), which means "sister". The suffix "-י" (i) indicates possession, similar to the English possessive pronoun "my". So, "אחתי" literally translates to "my sister".

Description: "אחתי" is a term of endearment used to refer to one's sister in Hebrew. It is commonly used in informal and familial contexts to express affection and closeness.
