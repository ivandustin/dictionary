Etymology: The word "אחקר" (pronounced "ahkar") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root חקר, which means "to investigate" or "to examine."

- אני אחקר (ani ahkar) - I investigate
- אתה אחקר (atah ahkar) - You investigate (masculine singular)
- את אחקרת (at ahkarat) - You investigate (feminine singular)
- הוא אחקר (hu ahkar) - He investigates
- היא אחקרת (hi ahkarat) - She investigates
- אנחנו אחקרים (anachnu ahkarim) - We investigate
- אתם אחקרים (atem ahkarim) - You investigate (masculine plural)
- אתן אחקרות (aten ahkarot) - You investigate (feminine plural)
- הם אחקרים (hem ahkarim) - They investigate (masculine)
- הן אחקרות (hen ahkarot) - They investigate (feminine)

Description: אחקר means "to investigate" or "to examine" in Hebrew. It is used to describe the act of thoroughly examining or researching a subject or topic. It can be used in various contexts, such as scientific research, detective work, or simply exploring and learning about something in depth.
