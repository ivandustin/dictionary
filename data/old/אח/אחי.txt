Etymology: The word "אחי" (pronounced "achi") is a Hebrew word that means "my brother" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root א.ח.י (aleph, chet, yod), which means "brother" or "kinsman."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אחי" is a possessive pronoun that indicates ownership or possession. It is used to express the possessive relationship between the speaker and their brother. It is also used as a term of endearment or familiarity.

Description: "אחי" is a simple and commonly used word in Hebrew. It is often used in informal conversations or when addressing someone affectionately. It can be used to refer to a biological brother, a close friend, or even as a term of camaraderie among peers.
