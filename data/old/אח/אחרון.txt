Etymology: The word "אחרון" (acharon) comes from the Hebrew root א-ח-ר (a-ch-r), which means "after" or "last". It is derived from the Hebrew word אַחֲרִית (acharit), meaning "end" or "final".

Grammar: In Hebrew, "אחרון" is an adjective that means "last" or "final". It is used to describe the position of something in relation to others in a sequence or series.

Description: "אחרון" is commonly used to refer to the last or final item in a list, the last person in a line, or the last event in a series. It can also be used metaphorically to describe someone or something as being the ultimate or most extreme in a certain quality or characteristic.
