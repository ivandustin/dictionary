Etymology: The word "אחוד" (pronounced "achud") comes from the Hebrew root א-ח-ד, which means "to unite" or "to join together." It is derived from the noun אֶחָד (echad), which means "one" or "unity."

Grammar: In Hebrew, אחוד is a verb in the Qal (simple) conjugation. It is usually used in the masculine singular form, but can also be conjugated in other forms to match the subject of the sentence.

Description: אחוד means "to unite" or "to join together" in Hebrew. It is often used to describe the act of bringing different elements or individuals together to form a cohesive whole. It can also be used metaphorically to express the idea of harmony, cooperation, or solidarity.
