Etymology: The word "אחישנה" (Ahishena) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root א.ח.ש.ן (A.Ch.Sh.N), which means "to delay" or "to postpone."

Grammar: אחישנה is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the third person singular, past tense, and masculine gender. The verb is used to describe the action of delaying or postponing something.

Description: אחישנה is used to express the act of delaying or postponing an action or event. It can be used in various contexts, such as delaying a meeting, postponing a decision, or putting off a task. The word implies a deliberate action of delaying something for a later time.
