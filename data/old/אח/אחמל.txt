Etymology: The word "אחמל" (ahmal) is derived from the Hebrew root ח-מ-ל (ch-m-l), which means "to have compassion" or "to show mercy."

- אֲחַמֵּל (ahamél) - I have compassion
- תַּחְמִיל (tachmíl) - You have compassion (masculine singular)
- תַּחְמִילִי (tachmíli) - You have compassion (feminine singular)
- יַחְמֵּל (yachmél) - He has compassion
- תַּחְמֵּל (tachmél) - She has compassion
- נַחְמֵּל (nachmél) - We have compassion
- תַּחְמִילוּ (tachmílu) - You have compassion (masculine plural)
- תַּחְמִילְנָה (tachmílna) - You have compassion (feminine plural)
- יַחְמֵּלוּ (yachmélu) - They have compassion

Description: The word "אחמל" (ahmal) describes the act of having compassion or showing mercy towards someone. It implies a feeling of empathy and a desire to alleviate the suffering or distress of others.
