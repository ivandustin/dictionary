Etymology: The word "בנהר" (pronounced "benehar") is a Hebrew word that means "in the river". It is composed of two parts: "ב" (pronounced "be") meaning "in" or "at", and "נהר" (pronounced "nahar") meaning "river".

Grammar: "בנהר" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" indicates location or direction, and "נהר" is the noun meaning "river". Together, they form the phrase "in the river".

Description: "בנהר" is used to describe a location or direction that is within or towards a river. It can be used in various contexts, such as describing a place near a river or indicating a movement towards a river.
