Etymology: The word "בני" (pronounced "b'ni") is a Hebrew word that means "my son" or "sons of." It is derived from the Hebrew root בנה (pronounced "bana"), which means "to build" or "to establish."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "בני" is a possessive form of the word "בן" (pronounced "ben"), which means "son." The suffix "י" (pronounced "i") indicates possession, so "בני" translates to "my son" or "sons of me."

Description: "בני" is a term of endearment used by a parent to refer to their son or sons. It can also be used in a broader sense to refer to a group of people as "sons" in a metaphorical or symbolic way.
