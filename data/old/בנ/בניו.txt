Etymology: The word "בניו" (pronounced "b'niu") is a Hebrew word that means "his sons" or "his children." It is derived from the root word "בֵּן" (pronounced "ben"), which means "son" or "child."

Grammar: "בניו" is a possessive form of the word "בן" (pronounced "ben"), which means "son" or "child." The suffix "-יו" (pronounced "-av") indicates possession, meaning "his" or "of him." In this case, "בניו" specifically means "his sons" or "his children."

Description: "בניו" is used in Hebrew to refer to the sons or children of a male individual. It is commonly used in family contexts to indicate the offspring of a specific person.
