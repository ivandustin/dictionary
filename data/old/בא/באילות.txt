Etymology: The word "בָּיִת" (bayit) comes from the Hebrew root ב־י־ת (B-Y-T), which means "house" or "dwelling." It is derived from the ancient Semitic word "bayt," which has a similar meaning.

Grammar: "בָּיִת" (bayit) is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used in different grammatical forms, such as the definite form "הַבַּיִת" (habayit) meaning "the house," or the plural form "בָּתִּים" (batim) meaning "houses."

Description: "בָּיִת" (bayit) refers to a physical structure or building that serves as a place of residence or habitation. It can also be used metaphorically to represent a family or household. In Jewish tradition, the term "בֵּית הַמִּקְדָּשׁ" (Beit HaMikdash) refers to the Holy Temple in Jerusalem.
