Etymology: The word "באשם" (pronounced "ba'ashem") is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "ב" (pronounced "ba") meaning "in" or "with," and "אשם" (pronounced "ashem") meaning "guilt" or "sin." Therefore, "באשם" can be translated as "in guilt" or "with guilt."

Grammar: "באשם" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, composed of the preposition "ב" (ba) meaning "in" or "with," and the noun "אשם" (ashem) meaning "guilt" or "sin." It is commonly used to express the state of being guilty or responsible for something.
