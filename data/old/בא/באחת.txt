Etymology: The word "באחת" (ba'achat) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root ב-א-ח (B-A-Ch), which means "to unite" or "to join together."

Grammar: "באחת" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew, consisting of the preposition "ב" (ba), meaning "in" or "at," and the noun "אחת" (achat), meaning "one" or "a single one." Together, it means "in one" or "at the same time."

Description: "באחת" is used to describe a situation where multiple actions or events occur simultaneously or at the same time. It emphasizes the unity or synchronization of these actions or events.
