Etymology: The word "בארצם" (ba'artzam) is a Hebrew word that consists of two parts: "ב" (ba), meaning "in" or "at," and "ארצם" (artzam), meaning "their land." 

Grammar: "בארצם" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (ba) indicates location or possession, and "ארצם" (artzam) is a possessive pronoun meaning "their land." Together, they form the phrase "in their land" or "at their land."

Description: The phrase "בארצם" is used to refer to a specific location or possession. It is commonly used in Hebrew to indicate ownership or belonging to a particular land or territory.
