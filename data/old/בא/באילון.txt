Etymology: The word "בַּיָּלוֹן" (Babylon) comes from the Akkadian word "bāb-ilim," which means "gate of the gods." It was later adapted into Hebrew as "בַּיָּלוֹן."

Grammar: "בַּיָּלוֹן" is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is singular and can be used with definite or indefinite articles.

Description: "בַּיָּלוֹן" refers to the ancient city of Babylon, which was located in present-day Iraq. It was a major cultural and political center in ancient Mesopotamia. The city is mentioned in various ancient texts, including the Bible, and is known for its famous Hanging Gardens, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
