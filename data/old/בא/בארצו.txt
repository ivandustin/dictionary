Etymology: The word "בארצו" (ba'artzo) is a Hebrew phrase composed of two parts: "ב" (ba), meaning "in" or "at," and "ארצו" (artzo), meaning "his land" or "the land." 

Grammar: "בארצו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (ba) indicates location or possession, and "ארצו" (artzo) is a possessive pronoun meaning "his land" or "the land." 

Description: "בארצו" is used to describe someone or something being in their own land or country. It implies a sense of belonging or being rooted in a specific place.
