Etymology: The word "באחיהו" (pronounced "ba'achihu") is a Hebrew phrase that consists of two parts: "בא" (ba) meaning "in" or "with," and "אחיהו" (achihu) meaning "his brother." 

Grammar: "באחיהו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The preposition "ב" (ba) means "in" or "with," and "אחיהו" (achihu) is a possessive pronoun meaning "his brother." Together, the phrase means "in his brother" or "with his brother."

Description: "באחיהו" is used to describe a situation where someone is involved or associated with their brother. It can refer to a physical location or a relationship.
