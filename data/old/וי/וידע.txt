Etymology: The word "וידע" (pronounced "vayda") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root י-ד-ע (y-d-a), which means "to know" or "to understand" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "וידע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense, third person, singular form. It is commonly used in biblical texts and can be translated as "he knew" or "he understood."

Description: The word "וידע" carries the meaning of knowledge or understanding. It implies having awareness or familiarity with something or someone. In biblical contexts, it often refers to a deep or intimate knowledge, particularly in relation to God or divine matters.
