Etymology: The word "ויגאל" (pronounced "vigal" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root גאל (g.a.l), which means "to redeem" or "to ransom." 

Grammar: "ויגאל" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the past tense form. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form, which indicates that the subject of the verb is a male and the action took place in the past. 

Description: The word "ויגאל" translates to "and he redeemed" or "and he ransomed" in English. It is commonly used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) to describe the act of redemption or deliverance, often referring to God's redemption of the Israelites from slavery in Egypt.
