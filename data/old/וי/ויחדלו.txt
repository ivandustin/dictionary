Etymology: The word "ויחדלו" (pronounced "viyachdilu") is a Hebrew verb in the past tense. It is derived from the root חדל (pronounced "chadal"), which means "to cease" or "to stop."

Grammar: "ויחדלו" is a third-person plural verb in the past tense. It is formed by adding the prefix וַ (pronounced "va") to the root חדל and the suffix וּ (pronounced "u") to indicate the past tense and the plural subject.

Description: The word "ויחדלו" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and translates to "they ceased" or "they stopped" in English. It indicates that a group of people or things came to an end or discontinued a certain action or state.
