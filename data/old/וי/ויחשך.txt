Etymology: The word "ויחשך" (pronounced "vayechashech" in Hebrew) comes from the Hebrew Bible, specifically from the book of Exodus (Shemot) 10:21. It is derived from the root חשך (chashak), which means "darkness" or "to be dark."

Grammar: "ויחשך" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense and is conjugated in the third person singular. The prefix "ו" (vav) indicates the past tense, and the suffix "יחשך" (yechashech) indicates the action of darkness happening.

Description: "ויחשך" is used in the context of the biblical story of the ten plagues of Egypt. In Exodus 10:21, it describes the ninth plague, where darkness covered the land of Egypt for three days. The word signifies the physical absence of light and the resulting darkness that engulfed the land.
