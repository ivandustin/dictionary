Etymology: The word "וינבאו" (pronounced "vin'bau") is a Hebrew verb in the past tense, derived from the root נ.ב.א (n.b.a), which means "to prophesy" or "to speak on behalf of God." 

Grammar: "וינבאו" is the third person plural form of the verb, indicating that a group of people prophesied or spoke on behalf of God. The prefix "ו" (vav) indicates the past tense, and the suffix "ו" (vav) indicates the third person plural.

Description: "וינבאו" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) to describe instances where individuals or groups received divine inspiration and spoke messages from God. It is often translated as "they prophesied" or "they spoke as prophets."
