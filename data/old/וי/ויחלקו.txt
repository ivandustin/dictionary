Etymology: The word "ויחלקו" (pronounced "vayachalku" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root חלק (ch-l-k), which means "to divide" or "to share." The prefix ו (vav) indicates the past tense, and the suffix ו (vav) indicates the plural form.

Grammar: "ויחלקו" is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is commonly used in biblical Hebrew.

Description: "ויחלקו" is translated as "they divided" or "they shared" in English. It is often used to describe the act of dividing or sharing something among multiple people.
