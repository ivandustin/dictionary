Etymology: The word "וישא" (pronounced "vayisa" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root נ.ש.א (n.sh.a), which means "to lift up" or "to carry." It is conjugated in the past tense, third person, singular form.

Grammar: "וישא" is a verb in the past tense, indicating an action that has already occurred. In Hebrew, verbs are conjugated according to the subject, tense, and gender of the sentence. The prefix "ו" (vav) indicates the past tense, and the suffix "י" (yod) indicates the third person, singular form.

Description: The word "וישא" can be translated as "he lifted up" or "he carried." It is commonly used in biblical texts to describe someone physically lifting or carrying something.
