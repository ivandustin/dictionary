Etymology: The word "וישלח" (pronounced "Vayishlach" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root שלח (sh-l-ch), which means "to send". The prefix ו (vav) means "and". Therefore, "וישלח" translates to "And he sent".

Grammar: "וישלח" is a verb form in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense, third person, singular, and masculine. It is commonly used in biblical texts.

Description: "וישלח" is a word that appears in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh), specifically in the book of Genesis (Bereishit) 32:4. It refers to the action of Jacob sending messengers ahead of him to his brother Esau. The word is part of a larger narrative and is used to describe an event in the biblical story.
