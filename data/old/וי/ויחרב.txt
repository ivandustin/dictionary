Etymology: The word "ויחרב" (pronounced "vayicharav" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root חרב (ch-r-b), which means "to destroy" or "to devastate." The prefix ו (vav) indicates the past tense, making it "and it was destroyed" or "and it was devastated."

Grammar: "ויחרב" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is commonly used in biblical Hebrew to describe the destruction or devastation of something.

Description: "ויחרב" is often used in the Hebrew Bible to describe the destruction of cities, temples, or other structures. It conveys a sense of complete devastation and ruin.
