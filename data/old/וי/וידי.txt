Etymology: The word "וידי" (pronounced "vaydi") is a Hebrew word that means "and my hand." It is derived from the Hebrew root יד (yud-dalet), which means "hand."

Grammar: "וידי" is a possessive phrase composed of the conjunction "ו" (pronounced "vav") meaning "and" and the possessive pronoun "ידי" (pronounced "yadi") meaning "my hand." In Hebrew, possessive phrases are formed by combining the possessive pronoun with the noun it possesses.

Description: "וידי" is used to express ownership or possession of something by the speaker. It indicates that the hand being referred to belongs to the speaker.
