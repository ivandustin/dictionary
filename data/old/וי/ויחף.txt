Etymology: The word "ויחף" (pronounced "vayachef" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root י-ח-פ, which means "to hover" or "to flutter". 

Grammar: "ויחף" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is used in the Bible in the book of Genesis, specifically in Genesis 1:2, to describe the state of the earth before God began creating. The verb form "ויחף" indicates that the action of hovering or fluttering was performed by a singular masculine subject.

Description: In the context of Genesis 1:2, the word "ויחף" is used to describe the state of the earth as being formless and empty, with darkness covering the surface of the deep. It suggests a sense of chaos and lack of order before God's creative work began.
