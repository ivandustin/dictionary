Etymology: The word "ויקהלו" (pronounced "vayakhel") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root קהל (k-h-l), which means "to gather" or "to assemble." The prefix ו (vav) means "and," so ויקהלו literally translates to "and they gathered."

Grammar: ויקהלו is a verb form in the Hebrew language, specifically the third person plural past tense. It is used to describe a group of people coming together or assembling for a specific purpose.

Description: ויקהלו is a word that appears in the Hebrew Bible, specifically in the book of Exodus (Shemot) 35:1. It refers to the Israelites gathering together to hear the commandments and instructions from Moses regarding the construction of the Tabernacle. The word emphasizes the unity and collective action of the Israelites in carrying out this important task.
