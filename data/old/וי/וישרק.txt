Etymology: The word "וישרק" (pronounced "vayishrek") is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root ש-ר-ק (sh-r-q). 

Grammar: "וישרק" is a conjugated form of the verb in the past tense, third person, singular, masculine. It is commonly used in narrative texts in the Hebrew language.

Description: The word "וישרק" means "and he whistled" in English. It describes the action of producing a high-pitched sound by blowing air through pursed lips.
