Etymology: The word "ויעטוהי" is a Hebrew word. It is a combination of two words: "ויעטו" (vay'etu) which means "they wrapped" and "הי" (hay) which means "it was." 

Grammar: "ויעטוהי" is a verb form in the past tense, third person plural, combined with the pronoun "הי" to indicate that the action was completed. 

Description: "ויעטוהי" is a biblical Hebrew phrase that appears in the book of Genesis, specifically in Genesis 3:7. It describes how Adam and Eve realized that they were naked after eating the forbidden fruit and they made themselves loincloths by wrapping fig leaves around their waists.
