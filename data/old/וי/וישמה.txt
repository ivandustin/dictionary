Etymology: The word "וישמה" (pronounced "vayishmah" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root שמע (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The prefix ו (vav) in this word indicates the past tense, making it mean "and he heard" or "and he listened".

Grammar: "וישמה" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular masculine form. It is conjugated from the root שמע (sh-m-a) and is used to describe an action of hearing or listening that occurred in the past.

Description: "וישמה" is a word found in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh), specifically in the book of Genesis (Bereshit) 18:10. It is part of the narrative describing the visit of three angels to Abraham and Sarah, where they inform Sarah that she will have a son. The word "וישמה" is used to indicate that Sarah overheard the conversation between Abraham and the angels.
