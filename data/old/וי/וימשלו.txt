Etymology: The word "וימשלו" comes from the Hebrew language. It is a combination of the prefix "ו" (meaning "and") and the verb "ימשלו" (meaning "they ruled" or "they governed").

Grammar: "וימשלו" is a verb in the past tense, third person plural form. It is derived from the root "מ.ש.ל" (m.sh.l), which means "to rule" or "to govern". The prefix "ו" indicates the conjunction "and", connecting the verb to a previous action or subject.

Description: "וימשלו" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is often translated as "and they ruled" or "and they governed". It is commonly used to describe the actions of kings, leaders, or rulers who exercise authority over a certain territory or people.
