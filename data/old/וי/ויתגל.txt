Etymology: The word "ויתגל" (pronounced "vayitgal") is a Hebrew verb that comes from the root גלה (galah), which means "to reveal" or "to uncover." The prefix ו (vav) indicates the past tense and the subject "he" or "it."

Grammar: "ויתגל" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is commonly used in biblical Hebrew.

Description: The word "ויתגל" means "and he revealed" or "and it was revealed." It is often used in the context of something being uncovered or made known.
