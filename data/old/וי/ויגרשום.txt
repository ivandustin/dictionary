Etymology: The word "ויגרשום" is a Hebrew word that comes from the root גרש (g-r-sh), which means "to drive out" or "to expel." The prefix ו (vav) in front of the root indicates the past tense, making it "ויגרשום" (vayigreshem), which translates to "and he drove them out."

Grammar: "ויגרשום" is a verb in the past tense, third person masculine singular form. The suffix ם (mem) at the end of the word indicates the direct object pronoun "them," referring to a group of people or objects that were driven out.

Description: "ויגרשום" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is often translated as "he drove them out" or "he expelled them." It is commonly used to describe the act of forcefully removing or expelling a group of people or objects from a particular place.
