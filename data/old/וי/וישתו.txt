Grammar: "וישתו" is the third person plural form of the verb, indicating that a group of people drank. It is conjugated in the past tense and is used to describe an action that has already happened.

Description: "וישתו" is used in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh) and is found in various passages. It is often translated as "and they drank" or "and they were drinking" in English. The context of the passage determines the specific meaning and significance of the action of drinking.
