Etymology: The word "וישרצו" (pronounced "vayishretzu") is a Hebrew verb in the past tense. It is derived from the root ש-ר-צ (sh-r-ts), which means "to swarm" or "to teem."

Grammar: "וישרצו" is the third person plural form of the verb, indicating that the subject of the sentence is a group of people or things. It is in the past tense, suggesting that the action described has already occurred.

Description: "וישרצו" is often translated as "and they swarmed" or "and they teemed." It is used in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament) to describe the rapid multiplication or increase of living creatures, such as animals or insects. The word conveys a sense of abundance, proliferation, or rapid growth.
