Etymology: The word "וישקד" (pronounced "vayishkad" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root י-ש-ק-ד (yod-shin-kuf-dalet), which means "to watch" or "to be vigilant." 

Grammar: "וישקד" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the past tense form. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form, which is commonly used in biblical texts.

Description: The word "וישקד" appears in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament) in the book of Genesis, specifically in Genesis 22:24. It is used to describe the actions of Abraham's servant, Eliezer, who was sent to find a wife for Abraham's son, Isaac. In this context, "וישקד" means that Eliezer diligently carried out his task and watched over it with care and attention.
