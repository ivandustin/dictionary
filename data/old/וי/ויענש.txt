Etymology: The word "ויענש" (pronounced "vee'anash" in Hebrew) is derived from the Hebrew root ע.נ.ש (Ayin-Nun-Shin), which means "to punish" or "to chastise."

Grammar: "ויענש" is a verb in the Hebrew language, specifically in the past tense form. It is conjugated in the third person singular masculine form, which indicates that the subject of the sentence is a male and the action of punishing has already taken place.

Description: "ויענש" translates to "and he was punished" in English. It is commonly used in Hebrew texts, such as the Bible, to describe instances where someone receives punishment or retribution for their actions.
