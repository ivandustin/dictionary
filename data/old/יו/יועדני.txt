Etymology: The word "יועדני" comes from the Hebrew root י-ע-ד (y-ayin-dalet), which means "to aim" or "to intend". The suffix ני (ni) is added to indicate the adjective form, meaning "intentional" or "purposeful".

Grammar: "יועדני" is an adjective in the masculine singular form. In Hebrew, adjectives agree in gender, number, and definiteness with the noun they modify.

Description: The word "יועדני" describes something or someone that is intentional, purposeful, or deliberate. It implies that the subject has a clear goal or objective in mind and is acting with intention.
