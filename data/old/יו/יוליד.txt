Etymology: The word "יוליד" (Yolid) comes from the Hebrew root י-ל-ד (Y-L-D), which means "to give birth" or "to beget". 

Grammar: "יוליד" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אני יוליד (ani yolid) - I give birth, אתה יוליד (ata yolid) - you give birth (masculine), את יולידה (at yolidah) - you give birth (feminine), הוא/היא יוליד (hu/hi yolid) - he/she gives birth, אנחנו יולידים (anachnu yolidim) - we give birth, אתם יולידים (atem yolidim) - you give birth (plural masculine), אתן יולידות (aten yolidot) - you give birth (plural feminine), הם/הן יולידים (hem/hen yolidim) - they give birth.

Description: "יוליד" is a Hebrew verb that specifically refers to the act of giving birth. It is used to describe the process of a woman bringing a child into the world.
