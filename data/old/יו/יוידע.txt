Etymology: The word "יוידע" (pronounced "yodea") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root י-ד-ע (y-d-a), which means "to know" or "to be aware."

Grammar: "יוידע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני יוידע (ani yodea) - I know
- Masculine singular: אתה יוידע (atah yodea) - You know (informal, addressing a male)
- Feminine singular: את יוידעת (at yodea) - You know (informal, addressing a female)
- Masculine plural: אתם יוידעים (atem yodeim) - You know (plural, addressing males or mixed group)
- Feminine plural: אתן יוידעות (aten yodeot) - You know (plural, addressing females)

Description: The word "יוידע" means "to know" or "to be aware" in Hebrew. It is used to express knowledge or understanding of something. It can also be used to indicate familiarity with a person or a place.
