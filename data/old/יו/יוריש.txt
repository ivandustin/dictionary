Etymology: The word "יוריש" (Yorish) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root י.ר.ש (Y.R.Sh), which means "to inherit" or "to bequeath" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "יוריש" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני יוריש (ani yorish) - I inherit
- Plural: אנחנו יורשים (anachnu yorshim) - We inherit

Description: The word "יוריש" is used to describe the act of inheriting or passing down something from one generation to another. It can refer to inheriting physical possessions, such as property or money, as well as inheriting traits, characteristics, or traditions.
