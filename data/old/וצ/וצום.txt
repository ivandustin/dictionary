Etymology: The word "וֹצוּם" (votzum) is derived from the Hebrew root צ.ו.מ (ts.w.m), which means "to fast" or "to abstain." 

Grammar: "וֹצוּם" is a noun in the masculine singular form. It is also the infinitive construct form of the verb צָמַם (tzamam), which means "to fast" or "to abstain."

Description: "וֹצוּם" refers to the act of fasting or abstaining from food and drink for a specific period of time, usually for religious or spiritual purposes. Fasting is a common practice in many religions and is often observed as a form of self-discipline, purification, or penance. It is particularly significant in Judaism, where fasting is observed on certain holy days, such as Yom Kippur, the Day of Atonement.
