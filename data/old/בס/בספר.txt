Etymology: The word "בספר" (b'sefer) comes from the Hebrew root "ס.פ.ר" (s.p.r), which means "to count" or "to enumerate." It is derived from the noun "סֵפֶר" (sefer), which means "book" or "scroll."

Grammar: "בספר" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "ב" (b) means "in" or "at," and "ספר" (sefer) means "book." Together, "בספר" means "in a book" or "at a book."

Description: "בספר" is used to indicate that something is written or recorded in a book. It can refer to a specific book or books in general. It is commonly used in phrases like "בספר הקודש" (b'sefer hakodesh), which means "in the Holy Scriptures" or "in the Bible."
