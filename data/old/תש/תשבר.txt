Etymology: The word "תשבר" (teshaver) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root שבר (shavar), which means "to break" or "to shatter". 

Grammar: "תשבר" is the second person singular, future tense, feminine form of the verb. It is used when addressing a female subject and expressing an action that will happen in the future.

Description: The word "תשבר" is commonly used in Hebrew to convey the idea of breaking or shattering something. It can be used both literally, to describe the physical act of breaking an object, and figuratively, to express the idea of emotional or psychological breaking.
