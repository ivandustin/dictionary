Etymology: The word "תְּשׁוּבָה" (teshuvah) comes from the Hebrew root שׁ.וּ.ב (sh.u.v), which means "to return" or "to repent." It is derived from the verb שָׁב (shav), which means "to turn back" or "to come back."

Grammar: "תְּשׁוּבָה" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the feminine form and is singular. It is commonly used in religious and spiritual contexts to refer to the act of repentance or returning to a righteous path.

Description: "תְּשׁוּבָה" is a concept deeply rooted in Jewish tradition and is often associated with the High Holy Days, such as Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur. It refers to the process of introspection, self-reflection, and turning away from wrongdoing or negative behavior. Teshuvah involves acknowledging one's mistakes, expressing remorse, and making amends. It is seen as a way to seek forgiveness from both God and other individuals, and to strive for personal growth and spiritual renewal.
