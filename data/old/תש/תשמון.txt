Etymology: The word "תְּשֻׁמָּע" (teshuma) comes from the Hebrew root שמע (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen." The prefix תְּ (te) indicates the causative form, so תְּשֻׁמָּע can be translated as "you will cause to hear" or "you will make someone listen."

Grammar: תְּשֻׁמָּע is the second person singular future tense form of the verb שמע (sh-m-a). It is used when addressing a single person and expressing an action that will happen in the future. The verb form is causative, indicating that the subject will cause someone else to hear or listen.

Description: תְּשֻׁמָּע is a Hebrew verb that conveys the idea of making someone listen or causing someone to hear. It implies an active role in ensuring that someone pays attention or hears what is being said. The word can be used in various contexts, such as instructing someone to listen or commanding someone to make others listen.
