Etymology: The word "תֵּשְׁקֵל" (teshkel) is derived from the Hebrew root שקל (shkl), which means "to weigh." It is related to the noun מִשְׁקָל (mishkal), meaning "weight."

Grammar: "תֵּשְׁקֵל" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אֲנִי מֵשְׁקֵל (ani meshkel) - I weigh
- Plural: אֲנַחְנוּ מְשַׁקְּלִים (anachnu meshakelim) - We weigh

Description: The word "תֵּשְׁקֵל" is used to express the action of weighing something. It can refer to measuring the weight of an object or evaluating the importance or value of something.
