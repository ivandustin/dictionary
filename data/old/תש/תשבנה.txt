Etymology: The word "תְּשֻׁבָּה" (teshuvah) comes from the Hebrew root שׁ.ו.ב (sh.w.b), which means "to return" or "to turn back." It is derived from the noun שׁוּב (shuv), which means "return" or "repentance."

Grammar: "תְּשֻׁבָּה" is a feminine noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the singular form. The word is also used as a verb in the imperative form, meaning "return" or "repent."

Description: "תְּשֻׁבָּה" is a significant concept in Judaism, referring to the act of repentance or returning to God. It involves acknowledging one's mistakes, regretting them, and committing to change one's behavior. Teshuvah is considered a central theme during the High Holy Days, particularly on Yom Kippur, the Day of Atonement. It is believed that sincere teshuvah can lead to forgiveness and spiritual renewal.
