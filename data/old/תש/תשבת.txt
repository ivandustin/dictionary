Etymology: The word "תשבת" (Shabbat) comes from the Hebrew root "שבת" (Sh-B-T), which means "to rest" or "to cease." It is derived from the biblical account of the creation of the world, where God rested on the seventh day.

Grammar: "תשבת" is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the feminine singular form.

Description: "תשבת" refers to the Jewish day of rest and worship, which begins at sundown on Friday and ends at sundown on Saturday. It is considered the holiest day of the week in Judaism. During Shabbat, Jewish individuals and communities engage in prayer, study, and spend time with family and friends. It is a time of spiritual reflection, rest, and rejuvenation. Observance of Shabbat includes refraining from work, lighting candles, reciting blessings, attending synagogue services, and sharing festive meals.
