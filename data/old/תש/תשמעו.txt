Etymology: The word "תְּשֻׁמָּעוּ" (teshuma'u) is a Hebrew verb derived from the root שמע (sh-m-a), which means "to hear" or "to listen." The prefix תְּ (te) indicates the imperative form, commanding the listener to "hear" or "listen."

Grammar: "תְּשֻׁמָּעוּ" is the plural imperative form of the verb, addressing a group of people and commanding them to "hear" or "listen." The plural form is used when addressing multiple individuals.

Description: The word "תְּשֻׁמָּעוּ" is a command to actively listen or pay attention. It is often used in religious or instructional contexts, urging individuals or a group to listen attentively to a message, instruction, or teaching.
