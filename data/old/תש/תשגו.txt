Etymology: The word "תֵּשְׁגוּ" (teshgu) is a Hebrew verb that means "to err" or "to make a mistake." It is derived from the root שגה (sh-g-h), which conveys the idea of going astray or deviating from the correct path.

Grammar: "תֵּשְׁגוּ" is the plural form of the verb in the present tense. It is used to address a group of people or to refer to multiple individuals who are making mistakes or errors.

Description: The word "תֵּשְׁגוּ" is often used in a figurative sense to describe someone's incorrect or mistaken actions, decisions, or beliefs. It implies that the individuals in question are deviating from what is considered right or proper.
