Etymology: The word "קרי" (pronounced "kari") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ק.ר.א (k.r.a), which means "to read" or "to call out."

Grammar: In Hebrew, "קרי" is a noun that means "reading" or "calling out." It is used to refer to the act of reading aloud from a text, especially in a religious or ceremonial context.

Description: "קרי" is a term used in Jewish religious practice to denote the reading of biblical texts, particularly during public worship services. It is often associated with the public reading of the Torah, the first five books of the Hebrew Bible. The person who performs the reading is called a "קורא" (koreh) or "reader." The practice of "קרי" is an important part of Jewish tradition and is considered a sacred act of connecting with the divine word.
