Etymology: The word "קרועי" (pronounced "karu'i") is a Hebrew adjective derived from the verb "קרע" (pronounced "kara"), which means "to tear" or "to rip." 

Grammar: "קרועי" is the masculine plural form of the adjective, used to describe multiple nouns in a torn or ripped state. In Hebrew, adjectives agree in gender, number, and definiteness with the nouns they modify.

Description: "קרועי" describes something that is torn or ripped. It can be used to describe torn clothes, ripped paper, or any other object that has been damaged by tearing.
