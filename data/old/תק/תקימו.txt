Etymology: The word "תקימו" (pronounced "takimu") is a Hebrew verb derived from the root ק.ו.מ (k.w.m), which means "to establish" or "to set up."

Grammar: "תקימו" is the plural form of the verb in the imperative mood. It is used to give a command or instruction to a group of people. The verb is conjugated in the second person, addressing multiple individuals.

Description: The word "תקימו" is often used in a figurative sense to mean "establish" or "set up." It can be used to instruct a group of people to establish or create something, whether it be a physical structure, an organization, or a system.
