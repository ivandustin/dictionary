Etymology: The word "נְשֵׁתָה" (neshetah) comes from the Hebrew root נ-ש-ת (n-sh-t), which means "to forget" or "to neglect."

Grammar: נְשֵׁתָה is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the Qal stem, which is the basic form of the verb. It is conjugated in the third person singular, past tense.

Description: נְשֵׁתָה is used to describe the act of forgetting or neglecting something or someone. It implies a lack of attention or care towards a particular matter.
