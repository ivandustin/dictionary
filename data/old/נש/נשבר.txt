Etymology: The word "נשבר" (nashbar) comes from the Hebrew root "שבר" (sh.b.r), which means "to break" or "to shatter". 

Grammar: "נשבר" is a verb in the past tense, third person singular form. It is commonly used in Hebrew to describe something that has been broken or shattered.

Description: "נשבר" is used to express the state of something that has been physically or metaphorically broken. It can refer to objects, relationships, plans, or emotions that have been damaged or destroyed.
