Etymology: The word "נשארה" (neshara) is derived from the Hebrew root נ-ש-אר (n-sh-a-r), which means "to remain" or "to stay."

Grammar: "נשארה" (neshara) is a verb in the feminine singular form. It is in the past tense and indicates that the subject (feminine singular) remained or stayed.

Description: "נשארה" (neshara) is used to describe a situation where something or someone stayed or remained in a particular state or place. It can be used to express the idea of something being left behind or not changing.
