Etymology: The word "נשברה" (neshbera) is derived from the Hebrew root "שבר" (shavar), which means "to break." The prefix "נ" (n) indicates the past tense and the feminine gender, so "נשברה" (neshbera) translates to "she broke" or "it broke."

Grammar: "נשברה" (neshbera) is a verb in the past tense, third person singular, feminine form. In Hebrew, verbs change their form based on the gender, number, and tense of the subject.

Description: "נשברה" (neshbera) is used to describe an action that happened in the past, specifically when something broke. It can refer to a physical object breaking or to a situation or relationship falling apart.
