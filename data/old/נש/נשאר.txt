Etymology: The word "נשאר" (nashar) comes from the Hebrew root נ-ש-אר (n-sh-a-r), which means "to remain" or "to stay."

Grammar: "נשאר" (nashar) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It belongs to the binyan Pa'al (פָּעַל) and is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular masculine: אני נשאר (ani noshar) - I remain
- Singular feminine: אני נשארת (ani nosharet) - I remain
- Plural masculine: אנחנו נשארים (anachnu nosharim) - We remain
- Plural feminine: אנחנו נשארות (anachnu nosharot) - We remain

Description: The word "נשאר" (nashar) is used to express the action of staying or remaining in a certain place or condition. It can also convey the idea of being left or remaining after something else has been removed or taken away.
