Etymology: The word "נשביר" (Neshbir) comes from the Hebrew root "שבר" (shavar), which means "to break" or "to shatter". It is derived from the noun "שבירה" (shevirah), which means "a break" or "a fracture".

Grammar: "נשביר" (Neshbir) is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני נשביר (ani neshbir) - I break
- Plural: אנחנו נשבירים (anachnu neshbirim) - We break

Description: The word "נשביר" (Neshbir) is used to describe the action of breaking or shattering something into pieces. It can be used in a literal sense, such as breaking a glass or shattering a mirror, or in a figurative sense, such as breaking a promise or shattering someone's dreams.
