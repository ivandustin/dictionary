Etymology: The Hebrew word "המשפטים" (HaMishpatim) is derived from the root שפט (sh-p-t), which means "to judge" or "to govern." The prefix ה (ha) means "the." Therefore, "המשפטים" can be translated as "the judgments" or "the laws."

Grammar: "המשפטים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root word משפט (mishpat). The plural form indicates that it refers to multiple judgments or laws.

Description: "המשפטים" is the name of a biblical portion in the book of Exodus (Shemot) in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh). It is also known as Parashat Mishpatim. This portion contains a collection of laws and commandments given by God to the Israelites after the revelation at Mount Sinai. These laws cover various aspects of civil and criminal justice, social ethics, and religious observances. The laws in "המשפטים" provide guidelines for living a just and righteous life according to the Hebrew tradition.
