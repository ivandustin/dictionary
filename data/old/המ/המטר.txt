Etymology: The Hebrew word "המטר" (hamatar) comes from the root "מ.ט.ר" (m.t.r), which means "to rain" in Hebrew. 

Grammar: "המטר" is a noun in the masculine form. It is in the definite form, indicated by the prefix "ה" (ha), which means "the" in Hebrew. 

Description: "המטר" means "the rain" in Hebrew. It refers to the water that falls from the sky in the form of droplets. Rain is an essential part of the water cycle and plays a crucial role in the growth of plants and the overall ecosystem.
