Grammar: "המתאבלים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the definite article "ה" (ha) at the beginning, followed by the root אבל (avel), and the plural suffix ים (im).

Description: "המתאבלים" refers to a group of people who are mourning the loss of a loved one. In Jewish tradition, mourners often gather together during a designated mourning period to offer support and comfort to each other. They may engage in specific mourning rituals and practices, such as sitting shiva (a seven-day mourning period) or reciting special prayers. The term can also be used more broadly to refer to anyone who is grieving the loss of someone close to them.
