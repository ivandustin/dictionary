Etymology: The Hebrew word "המשתחוים" (hamishtachavim) is derived from the root שָׁחָה (shachah), which means "to bow down" or "to worship." The prefix ה- (ha) indicates the definite article "the," and the suffix ים (im) denotes the plural form.

Grammar: "המשתחוים" (hamishtachavim) is a masculine plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the plural suffix ים (im) to the singular form המשתחוה (hamishtachaveh).

Description: "המשתחוים" (hamishtachavim) translates to "the worshipers" or "those who bow down" in English. It refers to individuals or a group of people who engage in acts of worship or reverence, particularly in a religious context.
