Etymology: The word "המקות" (HaMakot) is a Hebrew word that translates to "the plagues" in English. It is derived from the Hebrew root נ.ג.ע (n.g.a), which means "to strike" or "to smite."

Grammar: "המקות" is a noun in the plural form. In Hebrew, nouns have gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural). The word "המקות" is masculine and plural.

Description: "המקות" refers to the ten plagues that were inflicted upon the Egyptians in the biblical story of the Exodus. According to the Book of Exodus, these plagues were sent by God to convince Pharaoh to release the Israelites from slavery. The ten plagues include blood, frogs, lice, wild animals, pestilence, boils, hail, locusts, darkness, and the death of the firstborn. Each plague was a demonstration of God's power and a punishment for Pharaoh's refusal to let the Israelites go.
