Etymology: The Hebrew word "המתיחשים" (hamatikhshim) is derived from the root ת-ח-שׁ (T-Ch-Sh), which means "to calculate" or "to reckon." The prefix ה- (ha) indicates the definite article "the."

Grammar: "המתיחשים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the plural suffix ים (-im) to the root ת-ח-שׁ (T-Ch-Sh). The definite article ה- (ha) is also added to indicate "the."

Description: "המתיחשים" refers to people who are involved in calculations or reckoning. It can be translated as "the calculators" or "the reckoners" in English.
