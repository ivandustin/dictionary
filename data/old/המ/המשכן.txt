Etymology: The Hebrew word "המשכן" (hamishkan) comes from the root שכן (sh-k-n), which means "to dwell" or "to reside". It is derived from the noun שכינה (shekhinah), which refers to the divine presence or dwelling of God.

Grammar: "המשכן" (hamishkan) is a masculine noun in Hebrew. It is in the singular form and can be used with definite or indefinite articles. In the plural form, it becomes "המשכנות" (hamishkanot).

Description: "המשכן" (hamishkan) is a biblical term that refers to the Tabernacle, a portable sanctuary used by the Israelites during their journey in the wilderness. It was a sacred place where the Israelites worshiped and offered sacrifices to God. The Tabernacle was constructed according to specific instructions given by God to Moses and served as a central place of worship until the construction of the First Temple in Jerusalem.
