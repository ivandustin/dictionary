Etymology: The Hebrew word "המשררים" (hamashrerim) is derived from the root ש-ר-ר (sh-r-r), which means "to sing" or "to chant". The prefix ה- (ha) indicates "the" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "המשררים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (im) to the root word משרר (mashrer), which means "singer" or "chanting".

Description: "המשררים" refers to "the singers" or "the chanters" in Hebrew. It is commonly used to describe individuals or groups who sing or chant in various contexts, such as religious ceremonies, concerts, or performances.
