Etymology: The Hebrew word "המשלחים" (hamashlachim) is derived from the root ש-ל-ח (sh-l-ch), which means "to send" or "to dispatch". The prefix ה- (ha) indicates the definite article "the".

Grammar: "המשלחים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is formed by adding the suffix ים (-im) to the root word. The definite article ה- (ha) is also added to indicate "the".

Description: "המשלחים" refers to "the messengers" or "the dispatchers" in Hebrew. It is used to describe individuals who are sent or dispatched to deliver a message or perform a specific task.
