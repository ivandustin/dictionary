Etymology: The Hebrew word "המנדים" (hamandim) comes from the root נדה (n-d-h), which means "to wander" or "to stray." The prefix ה- (ha) indicates "the," so המנדים literally means "the wanderers" or "the strayers."

Grammar: המנדים is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is in the masculine form and is used to refer to a group of people who wander or stray.

Description: המנדים is a term that can be used metaphorically to describe people who are lost, confused, or unsure of their path in life. It can also refer to individuals who deviate from societal norms or expectations.
