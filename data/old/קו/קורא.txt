Etymology: The word "קורא" (pronounced "koreh") comes from the Hebrew root ק.ר.א, which means "to read" or "to call out." 

Grammar: In Hebrew, קורא is a noun that means "reader" or "caller." It is derived from the present participle form of the verb קרא (kara), which means "to read" or "to call out." 

Description: קורא is used to refer to someone who reads or calls out, particularly in the context of reading from a text or reciting aloud. It can be used to describe someone who reads books, newspapers, or any written material, as well as someone who reads or recites religious texts or prayers.
