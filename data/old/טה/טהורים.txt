Etymology: The word "טהורים" (Tahorim) is a Hebrew word derived from the root ט-ה-ר (T-H-R), which means "to be pure" or "to be clean."

Grammar: "טהורים" (Tahorim) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is in the masculine gender and is used to refer to a group of people or objects that are pure or clean.

Description: "טהורים" (Tahorim) is a term used in Jewish religious context to refer to individuals or objects that are ritually pure or clean according to Jewish laws and customs.
