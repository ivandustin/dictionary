Etymology: The word "קימו" (pronounced "kay-moo") comes from the Hebrew language. It is derived from the root ק.ו.מ (q.w.m), which means "to stand" or "to arise."

Grammar: "קימו" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is in the past tense, third person plural form. It can be translated as "they stood" or "they arose."

Description: "קימו" is used to describe an action of standing or arising that was performed by a group of people or objects. It indicates that multiple individuals or things stood up or arose at a certain point in time.
