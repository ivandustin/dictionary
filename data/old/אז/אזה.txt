Etymology: The word "אזה" (azeh) is a Hebrew word that means "which" or "what." It is derived from the Hebrew root אזה, which means "to be" or "to exist."

Grammar: In Hebrew grammar, "אזה" is an interrogative pronoun used to ask questions about the identity or nature of something. It is similar to the English word "which" or "what."

Description: "אזה" is commonly used in Hebrew to ask questions such as "איזה ספר אתה קורא?" (Which book are you reading?) or "איזה צבע אתה מעדיף?" (What color do you prefer?). It is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts to inquire about specific details or choices.
