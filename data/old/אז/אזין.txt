Etymology: The word "אזין" (azin) comes from the Hebrew root אזן, which means "to listen" or "to hear". 

Grammar: אזין is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows: אָנֹכִי אוֹזֵן (ani ozen) - I listen, אַתָּה אוֹזֵן (atah ozen) - you listen (masculine singular), אַתְּ אוֹזֶנֶת (at ozenet) - you listen (feminine singular), הוּא אוֹזֵן (hu ozen) - he listens, הִיא אוֹזֶנֶת (hi ozenet) - she listens, אֲנַחְנוּ אוֹזְנִים (anachnu oznim) - we listen, אַתֶּם אוֹזְנִים (atem oznim) - you listen (masculine plural), אַתֶּן אוֹזְנוֹת (aten oznot) - you listen (feminine plural), הֵם אוֹזְנִים (hem oznim) - they listen (masculine), הֵן אוֹזְנוֹת (hen oznot) - they listen (feminine).

Description: אזין is a verb that describes the act of listening or hearing. It is used to indicate the action of paying attention to sounds or voices.
