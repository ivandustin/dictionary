Etymology: The word "אזנות" (aznot) is derived from the Hebrew root זנה (zanah), which means "to hear" or "to listen". The plural form אזנות (aznot) literally means "ears".

Grammar: אזנות (aznot) is a noun in the Hebrew language. It is in the plural form, indicating more than one ear.

Description: אזנות (aznot) refers to the anatomical organs that humans and many animals use for hearing. Ears play a crucial role in the perception of sound and maintaining balance. They consist of the outer ear, middle ear, and inner ear. The outer ear collects sound waves, the middle ear amplifies and transmits them, and the inner ear converts them into electrical signals that are sent to the brain for interpretation.
