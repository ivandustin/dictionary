Etymology: The word "נדמה" (pronounced "niddmah" in Hebrew) comes from the root דמה (pronounced "d.m.h") which means "to resemble" or "to be similar". The prefix נ (pronounced "n") indicates the passive form, so נדמה means "to be resembled" or "to be similar to".

Grammar: נדמה is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני נדמה (ani niddmah) - I resemble
- Masculine singular: אתה נדמה (atah niddmah) - You (masculine) resemble
- Feminine singular: את נדמה (at niddmah) - You (feminine) resemble
- Masculine plural: אתם נדמים (atem niddmim) - You (masculine plural) resemble
- Feminine plural: אתן נדמות (aten niddmot) - You (feminine plural) resemble
- Masculine singular: הוא נדמה (hu niddmah) - He resembles
- Feminine singular: היא נדמה (hi niddmah) - She resembles
- Masculine plural: הם נדמים (hem niddmim) - They (masculine) resemble
- Feminine plural: הן נדמות (hen niddmot) - They (feminine) resemble

Description: נדמה is used to describe a situation where something or someone resembles or appears similar to something else. It can be used to express a physical resemblance, as well as a similarity in behavior, characteristics, or qualities.
