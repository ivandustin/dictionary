Etymology: The word "נדדים" (Nadadim) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root נ.ד.ד (N.D.D), which means "to wander" or "to roam". 

Grammar: "נדדים" is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is derived from the singular noun "נדד" (Nadad), which means "wanderer" or "roamer". The plural form indicates that there are multiple wanderers or roamers.

Description: "נדדים" refers to people who wander or roam around without a specific destination or purpose. It can be used to describe nomads, travelers, or individuals who are constantly on the move. The word carries a sense of restlessness and a lack of settledness.
