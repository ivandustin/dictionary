Etymology: The word "נדע" (pronounced "nada") is a Hebrew word that comes from the root י-ד-ע (y-d-a), which means "to know" or "to understand" in Hebrew.

Grammar: "נדע" is a verb in the Hebrew language. It is conjugated in the present tense as follows:
- Singular: אני יודע (ani yodea) - I know
- Masculine singular: אתה יודע (atah yodea) - You know (masculine)
- Feminine singular: את יודעת (at yoda'at) - You know (feminine)
- Masculine plural: אתם יודעים (atem yod'im) - You know (masculine plural)
- Feminine plural: אתן יודעות (atan yoda'ot) - You know (feminine plural)

Description: The word "נדע" is used to express knowledge or understanding of something. It can refer to factual knowledge, awareness, or comprehension of a subject or situation. It is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts and can be conjugated to match the subject and tense of the sentence.
