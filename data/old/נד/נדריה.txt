Etymology: The word "נדריה" (Nedariha) is a Hebrew word that comes from the root נדר (n-d-r), which means "to vow" or "to promise." 

Grammar: "נדריה" is a noun in the feminine form. It is the plural form of "נדר" (neder), which means "vow" or "pledge." The suffix "יה" (-yeh) indicates possession, so "נדריה" means "her vows" or "her pledges."

Description: "נדריה" refers to the vows or pledges made by a woman. In Jewish tradition, individuals can make vows to dedicate themselves to a specific action or refrain from certain behaviors. These vows are considered binding and must be fulfilled. "נדריה" specifically refers to the vows made by a woman.
