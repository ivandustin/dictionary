Etymology: The word "נדרים" (Nedarim) is of Hebrew origin.

Grammar: "נדרים" (Nedarim) is a plural noun in Hebrew. It is derived from the root word "נדר" (Neder), which means "vow" or "pledge." The plural form indicates multiple vows or pledges.

Description: In Jewish law, "נדרים" (Nedarim) refers to vows or pledges made by individuals to abstain from certain actions or to perform specific actions. These vows are considered binding and have legal implications. The tractate of the Talmud called "נדרים" (Nedarim) discusses the laws and regulations surrounding vows and their annulment.
