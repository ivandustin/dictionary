Etymology: The word "כָּכֵל" (pronounced "kakel") is a Hebrew word that means "to gather" or "to collect." It is derived from the root כ־כ־ל, which conveys the idea of bringing things together.

- Singular: אֲנִי כוֹכֵל (ani kochel) - I gather
- Masculine singular: אַתָּה כוֹכֵל (atah kochel) - You gather
- Feminine singular: אַתְּ כוֹכֶלֶת (at kochelat) - You gather
- Masculine plural: אַתֶּם כוֹכְלִים (atem kochelim) - You (plural) gather
- Feminine plural: אַתֶּן כוֹכְלוֹת (aten kochelot) - You (plural) gather

Description: The word "כָּכֵל" is used to describe the action of gathering or collecting things together. It can be used in various contexts, such as gathering objects, people, or ideas.
