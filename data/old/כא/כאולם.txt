Etymology: The word "כאולם" (ka'ulam) is a Hebrew word that means "everyone" or "all." It is derived from the root כ-ו-ל (k-w-l), which means "to complete" or "to include."

Grammar: "כאולם" is a masculine singular noun in Hebrew. It is used to refer to a group of people or things collectively, indicating that every individual in the group is included.

Description: "כאולם" is a versatile word that can be used in various contexts. It is often used to emphasize the inclusiveness or universality of a statement or situation. For example, "כאולם אהבו את הסרט" (ka'ulam ahavu et haseret) means "everyone loved the movie." It can also be used to express the idea of "all" or "every" in a general sense, such as "כאולם יום" (ka'ulam yom), meaning "every day."
