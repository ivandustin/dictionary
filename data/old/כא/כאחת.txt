Etymology: The word "כאחת" (pronounced "ka'achat") is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "at once" or "simultaneously." It is composed of two words: "כ" (pronounced "ka"), meaning "like" or "as," and "אחת" (pronounced "achat"), meaning "one."

Grammar: "כאחת" is an adverbial phrase in Hebrew. It is used to describe actions or events that occur simultaneously or in a synchronized manner. It is often used to express the idea of doing multiple things at the same time.

Description: "כאחת" is a versatile phrase that can be used in various contexts. For example, you can say "אני רוצה לעשות שתי דברים כאחת" (pronounced "Ani rotze la'asot shtei dvarim ka'achat"), which means "I want to do two things at once." It emphasizes the idea of efficiency and multitasking.
