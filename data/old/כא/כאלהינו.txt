Etymology: The word "כאלהינו" (pronounced "k'elehenu") is a Hebrew phrase that translates to "like our God" in English. It is composed of the Hebrew words "כ" (pronounced "k") meaning "like" or "as," "אלהינו" (pronounced "elohenu") meaning "our God."

Grammar: "כאלהינו" is a prepositional phrase in Hebrew. The word "כ" functions as a preposition meaning "like" or "as," and "אלהינו" is a possessive pronoun meaning "our God." Together, they form a phrase that expresses a comparison or similarity to God.

Description: The phrase "כאלהינו" is often used in Jewish prayers and religious texts to express a desire to emulate or resemble the qualities and attributes of God. It conveys the idea of striving to be like God in terms of righteousness, compassion, and moral behavior.
