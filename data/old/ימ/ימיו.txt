Etymology: The word "ימיו" (Yamav) is a Hebrew word that means "his days" or "his lifetime". It is derived from the root word "ימים" (yamim), which means "days".

Grammar: "ימיו" is a possessive form of the word "ימים" (yamim), which is the plural form of "יום" (yom), meaning "day". The suffix "-יו" (-av) indicates possession, specifically "his" or "its".

Description: "ימיו" is used to refer to someone's days or lifetime. It is commonly used in Hebrew to describe the duration or span of time that someone has lived or will live.
