Grammar: "וֶזֶת" is a compound word formed by combining the conjunction "וְ" with the pronoun "זֶה." In Hebrew grammar, it is used to connect two nouns or phrases, indicating that both are being referred to or included. It functions as a coordinating conjunction.

Description: "וֶזֶת" is commonly used in Hebrew to introduce a new item or concept in a list or series. It is often used in biblical texts and modern Hebrew literature to connect and emphasize different elements.
